10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Melissa officinalis (Lemon Balm)

Melissa officinalis, also known as lemon balm, is a herb that has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries due to its calming and soothing properties.

Two common forms of melissa officinalis preparations are tisanes and tinctures. A tisane, or herbal tea, is made by steeping dried lemon balm leaves in hot water, creating a warm and comforting beverage that may help alleviate symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues.

On the other hand, a tincture is a concentrated extract of lemon balm that is made by soaking the leaves in alcohol or glycerin. Tinctures are typically taken in small doses, and they may provide relief for various ailments, such as cold sores, menstrual cramps, and indigestion.

Both tisanes and tinctures of melissa officinalis are easy to make at home and can be a natural and effective way to treat common health issues.

1. Infusion

Melissa Officinalis infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the leaves and flowers of the lemon balm plant in hot water, which extracts the beneficial compounds.

The main health benefits of this preparation include reducing anxiety, promoting sleep, and alleviating digestive issues such as bloating and indigestion. The leaves and flowers of the Melissa Officinalis plant are used to make this infusion.

To prepare, pour boiling water over 1-2 teaspoons of dried lemon balm and let it steep for 5-10 minutes. Some precautions to take when using this preparation include avoiding it during pregnancy and breastfeeding, and being mindful of potential interactions with sedatives and other medications.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal remedy.

2. Tincture

Melissa Officinalis tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking the leaves and flowers of the lemon balm plant in a solution of alcohol and water.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its ability to reduce anxiety, promote sleep, and alleviate digestive issues such as bloating and indigestion. The leaves and flowers of the Melissa officinalis plant are used to make the tincture, and they are harvested when the plant is in full bloom.

To make the tincture, the plant material is steeped in alcohol and water for several weeks, then strained. The resulting liquid can be taken orally, often in small doses of 2-3 drops at a time. When using Melissa Officinalis tincture, it is important to follow the recommended dosage, as taking too much may cause increased sedation and dizziness.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using this tincture if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking any medications.

3. Dry extract

Melissa Officinalis dry extract is a concentrated form of the lemon balm plant, created by drying and extracting the beneficial compounds from the leaves and flowers.

The main health benefits of this preparation include stress reduction, improved sleep quality, and relief from anxiety and gastrointestinal issues. The primary plant parts used are the leaves and flowers, which are harvested and then undergo a process of drying and extraction to create the final product.

To make the dry extract, the plant material is typically soaked in a solvent, such as ethanol or water, to extract the desired compounds, which are then dried to create a concentrated powder. When using Melissa Officinalis dry extract, it is important to follow the recommended dosage, as excessive use may cause adverse effects such as increased heart rate and sedation.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using this preparation, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

4. Fluid extract

Its main health benefits include reducing anxiety, promoting sleep, alleviating digestive issues such as bloating and indigestion, and boosting cognitive function. The leaves and flowers of the Melissa officinalis plant are used to make the fluid extract.

To prepare the extract, the plant material is soaked in a solvent, such as alcohol or glycerin, and then strained, leaving behind a potent liquid. When using Melissa Officinalis fluid extract, it is important to follow the recommended dosage, as excessive use can cause side effects such as increased heart rate, dizziness, and nausea.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using lemon balm extract if you are taking any medications, as it can interact with certain drugs.

5. Capsules

Melissa Officinalis capsules are dietary supplements that contain the dried leaves or extract of the Melissa officinalis plant, also known as lemon balm.

The main health benefits of this preparation include reducing anxiety, promoting sleep, and alleviating gastrointestinal symptoms such as bloating and indigestion. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the leaves, which are harvested when the plant is in full bloom.

To make Melissa Officinalis capsules, the leaves are dried and then ground into a fine powder, which is then encapsulated. When using Melissa Officinalis capsules, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and not to exceed it, as excessive consumption may lead to increased drowsiness and interactions with other medications.

Additionally, pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using this preparation, as its safety during pregnancy and lactation has not been established.

6. Compress

Melissa Officinalis compress is a medicinal preparation that involves soaking lemon balm leaves in hot water to extract their beneficial compounds, which are then applied to the skin in the form of a compress.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its ability to soothe skin irritations, reduce inflammation, and promote relaxation. The leaves of the Melissa officinalis plant are used to make this preparation.

To make a Melissa Officinalis compress, fresh lemon balm leaves can be steeped in hot water for several minutes, and then the resulting liquid can be used to soak a cloth, which can be applied to the affected area. When using a Melissa Officinalis compress, it is important to avoid applying it to open wounds or broken skin, and to discontinue use if any irritation occurs.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using lemon balm compresses if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking any medications.

7. Poultice

Melissa Officinalis poultice is a medicinal preparation made by crushing and applying fresh or dried leaves of the lemon balm plant directly to the skin.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its ability to soothe skin irritations, reduce inflammation, and promote relaxation. The leaves and flowers of the Melissa officinalis plant are used for this preparation.

To make a Melissa Officinalis poultice, crush fresh or dried lemon balm leaves and apply the mashed plant material directly to the affected area. Main precautions to take when using this Melissa Officinalis preparation include performing a patch test to check for skin sensitivity and avoiding use on broken or damaged skin.

Additionally, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using lemon balm poultices for medicinal purposes, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women, and those taking medications that may interact with lemon balm.

8. Essential oil

Melissa Officinalis essential oil is a concentrated hydrophobic liquid extracted from the leaves and flowers of the lemon balm plant, Melissa officinalis, through a steam distillation process.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its ability to reduce anxiety, promote sleep, alleviate gastrointestinal issues such as indigestion and nausea, and boost cognitive function. The primary plant parts used are the leaves and flowers, which are harvested when in full bloom and then undergo steam distillation.

To make Melissa Officinalis essential oil, fresh plant material is steeped in water and then subjected to steam, which releases the oil-soluble compounds from the plant material. The resulting steam, now containing the essential oil, is then condensed and collected. When using Melissa Officinalis essential oil, it is important to dilute it with a carrier oil before applying it to the skin, as it can cause irritation if used undiluted.

Additionally, it should be stored in a cool, dark place and used within a year of opening to ensure maximum potency and effectiveness.

9. Ointment

Melissa Officinalis ointment is a topical preparation made from the leaves and flowers of the lemon balm plant, Melissa officinalis, which is known for its calming and soothing properties.

The main health benefits of this ointment include providing relief from skin irritations, such as insect bites, rashes, and minor burns, and its potential to reduce anxiety and promote sleep. The leaves and flowers of the lemon balm plant are harvested, dried, and then steeped in a carrier oil to extract their active compounds before being combined with beeswax to create the ointment. To make Melissa Officinalis ointment, first, infuse the dried lemon balm in a carrier oil, such as olive or sweet almond oil, by gently heating it for several hours.

After straining the oil, melt beeswax in a double boiler and mix in the infused oil. Pour the mixture into a sterilized container and let it cool before use. When using Melissa Officinalis ointment, it is important to perform a patch test first to check for skin sensitivity, avoid using it on broken or irritated skin, and keep it out of reach of children.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using lemon balm ointment if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking any medications.

10. Cream

Melissa Officinalis cream is a topical preparation made from the extracts of the Melissa officinalis plant combined with a soothing cream base.

Its main health benefits include its ability to reduce inflammation, alleviate pain, and promote relaxation due to its calming and mildly sedative properties. The aerial parts of the plant, including the leaves and flowers, are typically used to make this preparation.

To make Melissa Officinalis cream, dried lemon balm leaves are steeped in a carrier oil, such as sweet almond or olive oil, and then combined with beeswax and shea butter to create a smooth, easy-to-spread cream. When using Melissa Officinalis cream, it is important to perform a patch test first to ensure that you are not allergic to any of the ingredients, and to avoid applying it to broken or irritated skin.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using this preparation if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking any medications, as it may interact with certain drugs.
