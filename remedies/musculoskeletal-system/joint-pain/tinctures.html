The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic arnica flower tincture for joint pain.

  1. Harvest 2 ounces of fresh arnica flowers, carefully avoiding the toxic leaves and stems, during their full bloom in late spring or early summer.
  2. Clean the flowers gently by rinsing under cold running water, then let them air dry on a paper towel.
  3. Fill a clean glass jar with 1 cup of 80-proof vodka or rum, then add the dry arnica flowers, ensuring they are completely submerged.

Tinctures made from turmeric, ginger, and green-lipped mussel can help reduce inflammation and provide pain relief. Willow bark tincture, a natural source of salicin, is used to alleviate muscle and joint pain.

Devil's claw tincture is another popular option for treating arthritis-related pain and inflammation. Additionally, valerian root tincture can help with muscle tension and discomfort.

It is important to consult a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal treatment.
