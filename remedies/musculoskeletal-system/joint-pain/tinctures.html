Licorice tinctures helps with joint pain because of its anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and alleviate discomfort in the joints.

The glycyrrhizin present in licorice root has been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory enzymes, thereby providing relief from arthritis, osteoarthritis, and other degenerative joint disorders.   • Flavonoids: Licorice tincture contains flavonoids like glabridin and isoliquiritigenin, which have potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, helping to alleviate joint pain and stiffness.
  • Saponins: Saponins in licorice tincture help reduce inflammation by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2, which contribute to joint pain and swelling.

The list below shows the primary parts of licorice used to make tinctures for joint pain.

  • Roots: The roots of the licorice plant contain a high concentration of glycyrrhizin, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce joint pain and inflammation.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhyzomes, or underground stems, of the licorice plant have been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat joint pain and arthritis due to their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice for joint pain.

  1. Harvest fresh or dried glycyrrhiza glabra roots in late summer or early fall.
  2. Chop the harvested roots into small pieces and mix them with vodka at a 1:5 ratio by weight.
  3. Allow the mixture to steep for 6-8 weeks in a clean glass jar with a lid in a cool dark place.
  4. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar discarding the solids and retaining the liquid extract.
  5. Transfer the tincture to amber glass bottles with tight-fitting lids and store them in a cool dark place for up to 2 years.
