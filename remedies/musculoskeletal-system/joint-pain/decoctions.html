1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Licorice decoctions helps with joint pain because it contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling and alleviate discomfort.

The decoction also contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have antioxidant effects that help protect joints from further damage caused by free radicals.

Additionally, licorice root has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, leading to a decrease in joint pain and stiffness.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in licorice decoctions that aid with joint pain.

  • Flavonoids: Flavonoids, such as liquiritin and isoliquiritigenin, have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in joints, alleviating joint pain.
  • Glycyrrhizin: Glycyrrhizin, a saponin found in licorice root, has been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory mediators and enzymes, thereby reducing joint inflammation and pain.
  • Polysaccharides: Licorice decoctions contain polysaccharides that have been found to stimulate the immune system's natural anti-inflammatory response, helping to reduce joint swelling and pain.

The list below shows the primary parts of licorice used to make decoctions for joint pain.

  • Roots: The roots of the licorice plant contain glycyrrhizin, a compound that reduces inflammation and alleviates joint pain.
  • Barks: The barks of the licorice plant have anti-inflammatory properties, making them effective in soothing joint pain and reducing swelling.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice for joint pain.

  1. Measure one teaspoon of dried glycyrrhiza glabra root powder into a small saucepan.
  2. Add four cups of water to the saucepan and bring the mixture to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce heat to low and simmer the mixture for 10-15 minutes or until the liquid has reduced by half.
  4. Strain the decoction through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a large bowl, discarding solids.
  5. Allow the decoction to cool to room temperature before refrigerating it for up to 24 hours or freezing for later use.
