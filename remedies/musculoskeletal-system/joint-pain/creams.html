1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra, also known as licorice, creams helps with joint pain because of its anti-inflammatory and anti-arthritic properties.

Medicinal Constituents

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Glycyrrhiza glabra creams that help with joint pain.

  • Saponins: Saponins, particularly glycyrrhizin, help with joint pain by inhibiting the enzyme 11β-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase, which leads to an increase in cortisol levels, thereby reducing inflammation and pain.
  • Liquiritin: Liquiritin, a phenolic compound, helps with joint pain by reducing inflammation and oxidative stress through its antioxidant properties, which in turn alleviate pain and discomfort.
  • Glycyrrhetic acid: Glycyrrhetic acid, a triterpenoid saponin, helps with joint pain by suppressing the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, such as TNF-α and IL-1β, which contribute to joint inflammation and pain.

Parts Used

The list below shows the primary parts of licorice used to make creams for joint pain.

  • Roots: They are the primary source of glycyrrhizin, a compound responsible for the anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties in creams for joint pain.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice for joint pain.

  1. Weigh 20 grams of dried glycyrrhiza glabra roots and grind them into a fine powder.
  2. Steep the powder in 100 milliliters of boiling water for 10 to 15 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid through a cheesecloth into a clean container and discard the solids.
  4. Mix 10 grams of beeswax with 20 grams of coconut oil in a double boiler.
  5. Combine the infused liquid with the beeswax mixture and stir until it thickens into a smooth cream.
