1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Licorice creams helps with joint pain because they contain compounds that have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling and ease discomfort in affected joints.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in licorice creams that aid with joint pain.

  • Glycyrrhizin: A compound found in licorice root, glycyrrhizin has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which can help reduce joint inflammation and alleviate pain.
  • Flavonoids: Licorice creams contain flavonoids, such as luteolin and apigenin, that have potent anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce joint swelling and pain.
  • Phytochemicals: Herbal licorice creams often contain phytochemicals like isoflavones and triterpenes, which have been shown to exhibit analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, helping to alleviate joint pain.

The list below shows the primary parts of licorice used to make creams for joint pain.

  • Rhyzomes: Rhyzomes contain a high concentration of licoricidin, which helps to reduce inflammation and pain associated with joint conditions.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice for joint pain.

  1. Harvest dried glycyrrhiza glabra roots from a reputable source and grind them into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle.
  2. Combine 10 grams of powdered glycyrrhiza glabra with 20 milliliters of distilled water in a heat-resistant mixing bowl to create an infusion.
  3. Heat the mixture over low heat for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the liquid has reduced by half and the extract has reached a temperature of 60 degrees celsius.
  4. Allow the extract to cool slightly before adding 100 grams of petroleum jelly and blending until a smooth, creamy consistency is achieved.
  5. Transfer the cream to sterilized glass jars and store them in an airtight container in a dark place for at least two weeks to allow the herbal properties to mature before use.
