Sage essential oils helps with joint pain because of its anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling and alleviate discomfort.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in sage essential oils that aid with joint pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic sage essential oil for joint pain.

  1. Harvest fresh salvia sclarea leaves in the morning after dew has evaporated for optimal potency.
  2. Rinse the harvested leaves gently with distilled water to remove any impurities and debris.
  3. Steam distill the washed leaves using a clevenger apparatus at 100°c for approximately 4-6 hours.
  4. Collect the resulting essential oil in a sterile glass container and store it in an airtight bottle at room temperature.
  5. Filter the essential oil through a coffee filter or cheesecloth to remove any impurities or sediment before use.
