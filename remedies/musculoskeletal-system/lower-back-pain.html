Lower Back Pain: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Lower back pain, characterized by discomfort and inflammation in the lumbar region, is a common condition affecting millions worldwide.

Its causes range from physical strain, poor posture, and muscle imbalances to underlying medical conditions like herniated discs, spinal stenosis, and osteoporosis. If left untreated, lower back pain can lead to chronic fatigue, digestive issues, and even mental health problems like anxiety and depression.

Fortunately, herbal remedies offer a natural solution. Turmeric, ginger, and willow bark contain anti-inflammatory compounds that reduce pain and swelling. Capsaicin from chili peppers can also relieve discomfort.

However, when using medicinal herbs, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional and follow recommended dosages to avoid interactions and adverse reactions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of lower back pain, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of lower back pain?

The main causes of lower back pain are multifaceted, and include muscle strain (accounting for 30-40% of cases), often resulting from heavy lifting, bending, or sudden movements.

Degenerative disc disease, where spinal discs lose their cushioning properties due to wear and tear over time, is another common cause (25-30% of cases). Herniated discs, where the soft tissue inside a spinal disc bulges out through a tear, can also lead to lower back pain (15-20% of cases). Additionally, conditions such as spondylolisthesis (slipping of a vertebra) and spinal stenosis (narrowing of the spinal canal) can compress nerves and cause pain.

Other contributing factors include poor posture, sedentary lifestyle, and underlying medical conditions such as osteoporosis, arthritis, and scoliosis.

  • Herniated Disk: A herniated disk, also known as a bulging or ruptured disk, occurs when the soft tissue between the vertebrae in the lower back breaks through a crack in the outer, harder tissue, putting pressure on the surrounding nerves and causing pain.
  • Degenerative Joint Disease: Degenerative joint disease, also known as osteoarthritis, is a condition in which the cartilage that cushions the joints in the lower back wears down, leading to bone-on-bone contact and pain.
  • Strain: A strain in the muscles or ligaments of the lower back occurs when they are stretched or torn, often due to overuse, lifting heavy objects, or sudden movements, leading to pain and inflammation.
  • Scoliosis: Scoliosis is a condition in which the spine curves abnormally, putting pressure on the surrounding muscles, nerves, and joints in the lower back and causing pain.
  • Kidney Stones: Kidney stones can cause lower back pain due to inflammation and irritation of the nerves in the lower back, as well as the pain caused by the stones themselves.
  • Osteoporosis: Osteoporosis is a condition in which the bones in the lower back become brittle and porous, leading to pain and increased risk of fractures.
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis: Rheumatoid arthritis is an autoimmune disease that causes inflammation and pain in the joints of the lower back, including the sacroiliac joint.
  • Spinal Stenosis: Spinal stenosis is a condition in which the spinal canal in the lower back narrows, putting pressure on the surrounding nerves and causing pain.
  • Spondylolisthesis: Spondylolisthesis is a condition in which a vertebra in the lower back slips out of place, putting pressure on the surrounding nerves and causing pain.
