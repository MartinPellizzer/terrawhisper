10 best herbal teas for gout

Herbal teas for gout are infusions or decoctions made from various plants and herbs that can help alleviate the symptoms of gout, a type of arthritis characterized by sudden, severe attacks of pain, swelling, redness, and tenderness in the joints, often the big toe.

Some herbs, such as birch leaf, devil's claw, and nettle, have anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties that can help reduce uric acid levels in the body and alleviate pain and inflammation. For example, drinking birch leaf tea can help eliminate uric acid through urination, while devil's claw and nettle can reduce inflammation and swelling in the affected joints.

By incorporating these herbal teas into their daily routine, individuals with gout can experience a significant improvement in their symptoms, enabling them to lead a more comfortable and active life.

1. Cat's claw tea

Cat's claw tea helps with gout because it contains several active compounds, such as pentacyclic oxindole alkaloids, that have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

By reducing inflammation and pain, cat's claw tea can help alleviate the symptoms of gout and improve joint function. Additionally, cat's claw tea has antioxidant properties that can help protect cells from damage caused by free radicals, which are produced in excess during gout flares.

Overall, cat's claw tea can be a useful natural remedy for managing gout and promoting joint health.

  • Analgesic: The tea can help alleviate gout pain by acting as a natural analgesic, thanks to its alkaloid and flavonoid content.
  • Antioxidant: Cat's claw tea contains powerful antioxidants that help protect joints from oxidative stress and damage, which are often associated with gout, due to its phenolic and terpene content.
  • Immune-boosting: The tea can help strengthen the immune system, which is crucial for managing gout, as it can reduce the frequency and severity of gout attacks, due to its alkaloids, glycosides, and sterols.
  • Diuretic: Cat's claw tea has diuretic properties that help remove uric acid from the body, which is the primary cause of gout, due to its various plant sterols and tannins.

The list below shows the primary parts of cat's claw tea used to make teas for gout.

  • Roots: Contain high levels of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that help reduce uric acid levels and alleviate gout symptoms.
  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids and other bioactive compounds, leaves help reduce inflammation and provide pain relief for gout sufferers.

  • Seeds: Packed with antioxidants and essential fatty acids, seeds help reduce inflammation and protect joints affected by gout.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic cat's claw tea for gout.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water and 2 teaspoons of dried cat's claw bark, then bring the mixture to a boil.

  3. Strain the tea to remove the cat's claw bark, then let it cool to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Optionally, add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of cat's claw tea daily, as needed, to help manage gout symptoms.
