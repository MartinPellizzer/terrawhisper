What is bunion and how it affects your life?

A bunion is a bony growth that forms on the joint at the base of the big toe, causing pain and discomfort.

It is a deformity of the metatarsophalangeal (MTP) joint, characterized by a large, swollen lump that can press against shoes and rub against other toes. Living with a bunion can be challenging, as it can limit mobility and make everyday activities painful. For instance, going for a walk or hike can become excruciating due to the constant pressure on the affected toe.

Even simple tasks like tying shoelaces or getting dressed can become daunting tasks. Furthermore, engaging in sports or activities that require quick movements, such as dancing or running, can be severely hampered by a bunion. Even sleeping at night can be uncomfortable due to the pain and swelling, leading to a poor night's sleep and subsequent fatigue. Additionally, the bunion can cause skin irritation, redness, and inflammation, making it difficult to wear certain shoes or participate in activities that involve standing or walking for long periods.

Overall, a bunion can significantly impact daily life, making it difficult to enjoy normal activities and maintain a quality of life.
