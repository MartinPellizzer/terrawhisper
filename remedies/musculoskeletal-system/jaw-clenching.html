Jaw Clenching: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Jaw clenching, also known as bruxism, is a habit of grinding or clenching the teeth, often during sleep, due to anxiety, stress, or an abnormal bite.

This can lead to facial pain, headaches, and temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders. Using herbal remedies can help alleviate jaw clenching.

Herbs like lavender, chamomile, and valerian root can be used to calm the nervous system, reducing stress and promoting relaxation. Ginseng and passionflower have anti-inflammatory properties that can ease TMJ pain.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, especially if you have underlying health conditions or take medications.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of jaw clenching, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of jaw clenching?

The main causes of jaw clenching are multifaceted and can be attributed to a combination of physiological, psychological, and environmental factors.

Research suggests that stress and anxiety, which can be triggered by work-related pressures, financial concerns, or relationship issues, are primary culprits in jaw clenching (1). Additionally, individuals with temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders, sleep disorders such as sleep apnea, and bruxism are more likely to experience jaw clenching (2, 3). Other contributing factors include habits like nail biting, teeth grinding, and tight jaw or facial muscles, which can be exacerbated by poor posture or ergonomic issues (4).

Furthermore, certain medications like selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) and stimulants can also contribute to jaw clenching as a side effect (5).

  • Stress: Stress is considered the most common cause of jaw clenching due to the body's natural response to anxiety and tension, which often manifests as involuntary teeth grinding and jaw clenching.
  • Anxiety: Anxiety is another frequent cause of jaw clenching as individuals may unconsciously clench their jaw in an attempt to cope with feelings of worry or apprehension.
  • Sleep Disorders: Sleep disorders such as sleep apnea and bruxism can cause jaw clenching due to the irregular sleep patterns and the body's inability to relax properly.
  • Misaligned Teeth: Misaligned teeth can cause jaw clenching as the teeth are not properly aligned, leading to strain on the jaw muscles and joints, which can result in involuntary clenching.
  • Depression: Depression can contribute to jaw clenching due to the emotional distress and anxiety associated with the condition, which can lead to unconscious jaw clenching.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes during pregnancy and menstruation can cause jaw clenching due to the fluctuations in hormone levels, which can lead to anxiety and tension.
  • Medications: Certain medications such as antidepressants and stimulants can cause jaw clenching as a side effect, leading to involuntary teeth grinding and jaw clenching.
  • Nerve Disorders: Nerve disorders such as trigeminal neuralgia can cause jaw clenching due to the abnormal electrical signals sent to the muscles and joints, which can lead to involuntary clenching.
  • Nutritional Deficiencies: Nutritional deficiencies such as a lack of magnesium and calcium can contribute to jaw clenching due to the role these minerals play in muscle relaxation and function.
  • Neurological Disorders: Neurological disorders such as Parkinson's disease and multiple sclerosis can cause jaw clenching due to the abnormal brain activity and muscle control.
