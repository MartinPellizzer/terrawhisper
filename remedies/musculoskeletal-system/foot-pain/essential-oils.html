1. Copaifera officinalis

Balsam fir essential oils helps with foot pain because of its unique blend of terpenes, particularly beta-pinene and limonene.

These compounds work to reduce inflammation and ease muscle tension in the feet, providing relief from conditions such as plantar fasciitis and achilles tendonitis. The anti-inflammatory properties also help to alleviate pain and discomfort caused by overuse or strain.

Additionally, balsam fir essential oils can be easily absorbed through the skin, making it a convenient and effective natural remedy for foot pain.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in balsam fir essential oils that aid with foot pain.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Balsam fir essential oil's anti-inflammatory properties can help reduce swelling and alleviate pain associated with foot injuries or conditions such as plantar fasciitis.
  • Pain-relieving: The analgesic and anti-nociceptive properties of balsam fir essential oil can help numb the affected area, providing quick relief from sharp pains caused by foot problems like heel spurs or Morton's neuroma.
  • Antibacterial: Balsam fir essential oil's antibacterial properties can help prevent infection in open wounds or cuts on the feet, reducing the risk of complications and promoting faster healing.

The list below shows the primary parts of balsam fir essential oil used to make essential oils for foot pain.

  • Roots: The roots of the balsam fir plant are rich in sesquiterpenes, which help reduce inflammation and alleviate pain associated with foot conditions.
  • Barks: The barks of the balsam fir plant contain alpha-pinene, a compound that helps to relax muscles and relieve tension in the feet and ankles.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic balsam fir essential oil for foot pain.

  1. Harvest fresh copaifera officinalis leaves and stems in the morning after dew has evaporated.
  2. Dry the harvested plant material in a warm dark place for at least two weeks.
  3. Steam distill 250 grams of dried copaifera officinalis plant material in 1 liter of water using a clevenger apparatus for three hours.
  4. Filter the resulting essential oil through a coffee filter or cheesecloth to remove any impurities.
  5. Store the finished copaifera officinalis essential oil in dark glass bottles with tight-fitting lids at room temperature.
