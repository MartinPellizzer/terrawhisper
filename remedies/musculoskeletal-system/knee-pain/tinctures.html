1. Arnica flower tincture

Arnica flower tincture helps with knee pain because it contains anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and inflammation in the knee joint.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in arnica flower tincture that aid with knee pain.

  • Analgesic: Arnica flower tincture alleviates pain by acting as a natural analgesic, containing helenalin and dihydrohelenalin that inhibit pain perception.
  • Circulation-boosting: Arnica flower tincture improves blood flow to the knee, promoting healing and reducing pain, due to the presence of volatile oils like thymol and carvacrol.
  • Antioxidant: Arnica flower tincture neutralizes free radicals, preventing further damage and promoting tissue repair, owing to its high antioxidant content, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.
  • Antispasmodic: Arnica flower tincture relaxes muscles and reduces spasms, which can contribute to knee pain, by containing lactones and essential oils that have antispasmodic properties.

The list below shows the primary parts of arnica flower tincture used to make tinctures for knee pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic arnica flower tincture for knee pain.

  1. Gather 2-3 handfuls of fresh Arnica montana flowers and gently rinse them to remove any debris.
  2. Coarsely chop flowers and place them in a clean glass jar, filling it three-quarters full, then pour enough 100-proof vodka to cover the flowers and seal the jar.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 2-4 weeks in a cool, dark place, shaking it gently every few days.
  4. After the steeping period, strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out the liquid from the flowers.
  5. Store the final tincture in a clean, dark glass bottle and keep it in a cool, dark place; use 1-2 mL up to three times a day for knee pain.
