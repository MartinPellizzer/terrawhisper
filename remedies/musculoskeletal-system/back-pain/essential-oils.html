1. Cymbopogon citratus

Lemongrass essential oils helps with back pain because of its unique properties that promote relaxation, reduce inflammation, and ease muscle tension.

The citral content in lemongrass oil has a natural analgesic effect, which helps to numb the pain receptors in the back, providing relief from discomfort and stiffness. Additionally, the antispasmodic properties help to relax the muscles and joints, reducing spasms and cramps that can exacerbate back pain.

As a result, lemongrass essential oil is an effective natural remedy for alleviating back pain and promoting overall well-being.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in lemongrass essential oils that aid with back pain.

  • Antispasmodic property: Lemongrass essential oil helps relax muscle spasms and cramps, reducing tension and pain in the back muscles.
  • Anti-inflammatory property: Lemongrass essential oil reduces inflammation, which is a common underlying cause of back pain, by blocking the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes.
  • Pain-relieving property: The citral content in lemongrass essential oil has analgesic and anti-pyretic effects, making it effective in reducing pain and discomfort associated with back problems.

The list below shows the primary parts of lemongrass used to make essential oils for back pain.

  • Roots: Rich in sesquiterpenes, which provide analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties to help soothe back pain.
  • Rhyzomes: Containing high levels of citral and geraniol, these oils have a sedative effect that can relieve muscle tension and spasms associated with back pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lemongrass for back pain.

  1. Harvest fresh leaves of cymbopogon citratus in the morning when they are still slightly dewy.
  2. Wash the harvested leaves thoroughly with distilled water to remove dirt and impurities.
  3. Steam distill the washed leaves using a steam distillation apparatus at atmospheric pressure.
  4. Collect the resulting essential oil and filter it through a coffee filter paper or cheesecloth.
  5. Store the filtered essential oil in dark glass bottles with tight-fitting lids for up to 6 months.
