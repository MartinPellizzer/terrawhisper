1. Salix alba

White willow tinctures helps with back pain because it contains salicin, a natural pain reliever similar to aspirin.

This compound is absorbed quickly into the bloodstream and works to reduce inflammation and ease muscle tension in the lower back, neck, and spine.

As a result, white willow tincture can provide effective relief from chronic pain, acute flare-ups, and stiffness, making it a popular natural remedy for back pain sufferers seeking an alternative or complementary treatment to traditional methods.

Medicinal Constituents

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Salix alba tinctures that help with back pain.

  • Salicin: A phenolic glycoside that acts as a natural anti-inflammatory agent, helping to reduce pain and inflammation associated with back pain.
  • Salicylic acid: A phenolic acid that has analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate back pain by reducing swelling and inflammation in the affected area.
  • Quercetin: A flavonoid that has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping to reduce pain and inflammation associated with back pain by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes.

Parts Used

The list below shows the primary parts of white willow used to make tinctures for back pain.

  • Leaves: Rich in salicylic acid, a compound similar to aspirin, which helps to reduce pain and inflammation.
  • Buds: Contain flavonoids and phenolic acids with anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, helping to alleviate back pain.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic white willow for back pain.

  1. Harvest fresh salix alba leaves and twigs when the plant is in full leaf in late spring or early summer.
  2. Clean and dry the harvested plant material at room temperature away from direct sunlight for 2-3 weeks.
  3. Chop the dried plant material into smaller pieces using a sharp knife or a herb cutter.
  4. Combine 1 part dried salix alba material with 2 parts 80% vodka in a clean glass jar to create a 1:2 ratio.
  5. Store the mixture in a cool dark place for 2-6 weeks shaking the jar every few days to facilitate extraction.
