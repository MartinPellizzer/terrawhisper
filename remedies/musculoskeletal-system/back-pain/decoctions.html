1. Salix alba

White willow decoctions helps with back pain because they contain salicin, a compound similar to aspirin that has anti-inflammatory properties.

When consumed as a tea or infusion, white willow can help reduce inflammation and ease stiffness in the muscles and joints, providing relief from chronic back pain.

Additionally, white willow's natural analgesic properties can also help alleviate muscle spasms and soreness, making it a popular herbal remedy for managing back discomfort.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in white willow decoctions that aid with back pain.

  • Salicin: Salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, helps reduce inflammation and alleviate pain by blocking the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that cause pain and swelling.
  • Flavonoids and phenolic acids: These bioactive compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body, contributing to relief from back pain.
  • Glycosides: White willow glycosides have been shown to exhibit analgesic and antipyretic (fever-reducing) properties, helping to relieve muscle and joint pain associated with back pain.

The list below shows the primary parts of white willow used to make decoctions for back pain.

  • Roots: The roots of the white willow contain salicin, a natural analgesic and anti-inflammatory compound that helps reduce pain and inflammation associated with back pain.
  • Barks: The barks of the white willow are rich in salicin and other bioactive compounds that help relieve pain, reduce inflammation, and promote healing in the muscles and joints affected by back pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic white willow for back pain.

  1. Harvest fresh salix alba bark and clean it thoroughly to remove any impurities or debris.
  2. Measure out 20 grams of dried salix alba bark or 30 grams of fresh bark for every liter of water used.
  3. Combine the measured bark with one liter of boiling water in a large pot and reduce heat to simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean container, discarding solids.
  5. Allow the decoction to cool before storing it in an amber glass bottle at room temperature for up to two weeks.
