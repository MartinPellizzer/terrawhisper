What is plantar fasciitis and how it affects your life?

Plantar fasciitis is a painful condition that affects the heel and bottom of the foot, causing inflammation in the plantar fascia - a band of tissue that runs along the bottom of the foot.

This debilitating condition can have a significant impact on daily life, making even simple activities excruciatingly uncomfortable. For instance, taking a morning walk around the block can turn into a painful ordeal, as every step seems to trigger shooting pains in the heel and arch. Going to work or school becomes a struggle, as standing for extended periods or walking between classes is agony.

Even doing household chores like laundry or cooking can become unbearable, as bending, twisting, or standing on your feet for long stretches of time exacerbates the pain. Additionally, engaging in recreational activities such as hiking, cycling, or running can be impossible due to the intense discomfort and stiffness in the foot. In extreme cases, plantar fasciitis can even disrupt sleep patterns, leading to fatigue, mood disturbances, and a general decline in overall well-being.

As a result, individuals with plantar fasciitis often find themselves altering their lifestyle, avoiding activities they once enjoyed, and struggling to maintain their usual daily routine due to the constant discomfort and pain.

What are the main causes of plantar fasciitis?

The main causes of plantar fasciitis are a combination of biomechanical, lifestyle, and environmental factors.

One of the most common causes is overpronation or flat feet, which can lead to excessive stretching and strain on the plantar fascia ligament. Another significant factor is repetitive stress from activities that involve running, jumping, or walking, particularly if done frequently or with poor foot mechanics.

Additionally, being overweight or obese can increase the risk of developing plantar fasciitis due to added pressure and strain on the feet. Poor footwear choices, such as shoes with inadequate arch support or cushioning, can also contribute to the development of plantar fasciitis by not providing sufficient protection for the heel and foot.

Finally, age-related wear and tear on the plantar fascia ligament, combined with a lack of flexibility and strength in the feet and ankles, can also play a role in the development of this condition.

The most common causes of plantar fasciitis are listed below.

  • Overuse or Overstretching: Repeatedly putting stress on the plantar fascia, especially during activities that involve running, jumping, or climbing stairs, can lead to inflammation and irritation.
  • Foot Mechanics: Abnormal foot mechanics, such as flat feet or high arches, can put additional strain on the plantar fascia, increasing the risk of plantar fasciitis.
  • Age-Related Wear and Tear: As we age, the plantar fascia and its surrounding tissues can become weaker and more prone to inflammation.
  • Obesity: Carrying excess weight puts additional stress on the plantar fascia, making it more susceptible to irritation and inflammation.
  • Wearing Shoes with Poor Arch Support: Wearing shoes that don't provide adequate arch support or cushioning can cause the plantar fascia to become inflamed and irritated.
  • High-Impact Activities: Participating in high-impact activities such as running, jumping, or dancing without proper training or warm-up can lead to overuse injuries like plantar fasciitis.
  • Standing for Long Periods: Standing for extended periods, especially on hard surfaces, can cause strain and inflammation on the plantar fascia.
  • Poor Posture: Maintaining poor posture, such as having a tendency to roll your feet inward or outward, can put additional stress on the plantar fascia.
  • Previous Injury or Trauma: Previous injuries or trauma to the heel or surrounding tissues can lead to scarring and adhesions that can cause plantar fasciitis.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal fluctuations during pregnancy or menopause can cause changes in foot mechanics, leading to increased stress on the plantar fascia and a higher risk of developing plantar fasciitis.
