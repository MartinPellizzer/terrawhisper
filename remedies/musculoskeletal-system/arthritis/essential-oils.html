1. Cannabis sativa

Marijuana essential oils helps with arthritis because they contain powerful anti-inflammatory compounds that can reduce joint pain and swelling.

The terpenes and cannabinoids present in these oils interact with the body's endocannabinoid system, helping to modulate pain perception and inflammation. By applying topical oils directly to affected joints, users can experience fast-acting relief from arthritic symptoms, including reduced stiffness and improved mobility.

Additionally, some herbal marijuana essential oils may also help to reduce joint damage and promote cartilage repair.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in marijuana essential oils that aid with arthritis.

  • Anti-inflammatory effects: CBD has been shown to reduce inflammation, which is a common symptom of arthritis, by suppressing the production of pro-inflammatory molecules.
  • Pain relief: CBD has been found to have analgesic and anxiolytic properties, helping to alleviate chronic pain associated with arthritis without producing psychoactive effects.
  • Immune system modulation: CBD may help regulate the immune response in cases of arthritis, reducing joint inflammation and preventing further damage by modulating cytokine production.

The list below shows the primary parts of marijuana essential oil used to make essential oils for arthritis.

  • Roots: The roots of the marijuana plant are rich in sesquiterpenes, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate joint pain and swelling associated with arthritis.
  • Rhyzomes: Marijuana rhizomes contain a high concentration of beta-caryophyllene, a sesquiterpene known for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, making it effective in reducing arthritis symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marijuana essential oil for arthritis.

  1. Harvest 30g of dried cannabis sativa leaves and flowers from organic sources.
  2. Dry the plant material in a warm dark place for at least 7 days to reduce moisture.
  3. Fill a 100ml glass bottle 2/3 full with the dried plant material and add 10ml of carrier oil such as coconut or jojoba.
  4. Heat the mixture in a double boiler or a slow cooker set on low heat for 6 hours to extract essential oils.
  5. Strain the liquid through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another container leaving behind the plant material.

Turmeric essential oils helps with arthritis because of its potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in turmeric essential oils that aid with arthritis.

  • Cyclooxygenase (cox) inhibition: The oil's curcuminoids inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes called COX-1 and COX-2, thereby reducing the production of prostaglandins, which contribute to pain and swelling in arthritic joints.

The list below shows the primary parts of turmeric essential oil used to make essential oils for arthritis.

  • Rhyzomes: The rhizomes of turmeric contain a high concentration of curcuminoids, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it effective in reducing joint pain and swelling associated with arthritis.
  • Roots: Turmeric roots have been traditionally used to treat various inflammatory conditions, including arthritis, due to their rich content of volatile oils and flavonoids, which help reduce inflammation and improve joint mobility.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric essential oil for arthritis.

  1. Harvest fresh turmeric rhizomes carefully selecting healthy ones with no signs of rot or mold.
  2. Clean the harvested rhizomes thoroughly by rinsing them under running water and gently scrubbing off any dirt.
  3. Dry the cleaned rhizomes in a well-ventilated area for at least 7 days or until they reach a moisture level of 10%.
  4. Steam distill the dried turmeric rhizomes using a steam distillation apparatus with 1 part turmeric to 2 parts water for 30 minutes.
  5. Collect and filter the resulting essential oil through cheesecloth or coffee filters to remove any impurities and store it in dark glass bottles.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger essential oil for arthritis.

  1. Harvest fresh ginger root, select pieces free of mold or mildew, cut into small cubes and weigh out 500 grams for the distillation process.
  2. Combine the cubed ginger root with 500 milliliters of pure water in a stainless steel or enameled pot, cover with a lid and bring to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer the mixture for 30-40 minutes or until the liquid has reduced by half, straining the solids from the resulting decoction.
  4. Use a steam distillation apparatus to extract the essential oil from the decoction, collecting 100 milliliters of zingiber officinale essential oil over a 2-hour period.
  5. Filter the essential oil through a coffee filter or cheesecloth into a dark glass bottle, store in a cool dark place for up to one year, using within six months for optimal therapeutic benefits.
