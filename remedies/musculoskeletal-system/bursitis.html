What is bursitis and how it affects your life?

Bursitis is a painful condition that occurs when the fluid-filled sacs, called bursae, that cushion and reduce friction between bones, tendons, and muscles become inflamed.

This inflammation can lead to swelling, redness, and warmth in the affected area, causing significant discomfort and limiting mobility. Bursitis can affect various parts of the body, including the elbows, shoulders, hips, knees, and ankles. For instance, if you have bursitis in your knee, it can make it difficult to walk or climb stairs, forcing you to rely on others for assistance.

Similarly, if you have bursitis in your shoulder, it can make everyday activities like lifting groceries, cooking, or even changing clothes excruciatingly painful. In severe cases, bursitis can even limit your ability to work or participate in hobbies, leading to feelings of frustration, anxiety, and isolation. Moreover, the constant pain and discomfort can also disrupt sleep patterns, making it challenging to get a good night's rest.

Overall, bursitis can have a significant impact on a person's quality of life, making even the simplest tasks a daunting challenge.
