What is bursitis and how it affects your life?

Bursitis is a painful condition that occurs when the fluid-filled sacs, called bursae, surrounding joints become inflamed and swollen.

This inflammation can cause significant discomfort, stiffness, and limited mobility in the affected joint, making everyday activities a challenge. For example, someone with shoulder bursitis may find it difficult to lift their arm or perform tasks that require overhead movement, such as brushing their hair or reaching for items on high shelves.

Similarly, hip bursitis can make walking or climbing stairs painful and cumbersome. Knee bursitis can impede simple activities like squatting or kneeling, while elbow bursitis may prevent individuals from holding objects or doing tasks that require repetitive hand movements. Additionally, bursitis in the ankle can make it difficult to walk or wear shoes comfortably, affecting even the most basic daily routines.

Overall, bursitis can significantly impact quality of life, leaving individuals feeling restricted and uncomfortable in their own bodies.

What are the main causes of bursitis?

The main causes of bursitis are repetitive strain, trauma, and overuse.

Bursitis occurs when a bursa, which is a small fluid-filled sac that cushions joints and reduces friction between moving parts, becomes inflamed or irritated. This inflammation can occur due to repeated stress and pressure on the joint, such as from activities like running or cycling, or from poor posture or movement patterns.

Additionally, trauma to the area, such as a direct blow or fall, can also cause bursitis. Furthermore, overuse of a particular joint or muscle group, such as from frequent lifting or bending, can lead to inflammation and swelling in the surrounding tissues.

Other factors that may contribute to the development of bursitis include age-related wear and tear, arthritis, and certain medical conditions, such as rheumatoid arthritis or gout.

The most common causes of bursitis are listed below.

  • Repetitive Motion: Performing repetitive movements or actions that rub against the bursae, such as typing, lifting heavy objects, or running, can cause inflammation and irritation.
  • Trauma: A direct blow to the affected area, such as a fall or a car accident, can cause bruising and inflammation of the bursa.
  • Overuse: Engaging in activities that involve repeated strain on the joint or surrounding muscles, such as throwing a baseball or playing tennis, can lead to inflammation and irritation.
  • Poor Posture: Maintaining poor posture for extended periods can put pressure on the joints and surrounding tissues, leading to inflammation and bursitis.
  • Obesity: Carrying excess weight can put additional strain on the joints and muscles, increasing the risk of developing bursitis.
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis: This chronic autoimmune disorder can cause inflammation in the joints, including the bursae.
  • Gout: A condition characterized by high uric acid levels in the blood, gout can cause inflammation and swelling in the joints, including the bursae.
  • Infection: Bacterial or fungal infections can cause inflammation and irritation of the bursa, leading to bursitis.
  • Hypothyroidism: This hormonal disorder can cause joint stiffness and pain, including bursitis.
  • Aging: As people age, the connective tissue around the joints can become thinner and more prone to inflammation, increasing the risk of developing bursitis.
