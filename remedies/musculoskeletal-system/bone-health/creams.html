Maidenhair tree creams helps with bone health because they contain a unique compound called pterostilbene, which has been shown to stimulate bone growth and density.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in maidenhair tree creams that aid with bone health.

  • Calcitonin: Some herbs like turmeric (Curcuma longa) contain compounds that mimic the action of calcitonin, a hormone that helps regulate calcium levels in the blood and promotes bone density.
  • Anti-inflammatory effects: Herbs like ginger (Zingiber officinale), ashwagandha (Withania somnifera), and ginseng (Panax spp.) have anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce inflammation in joints and bones, which can contribute to osteoporosis and other bone-related disorders.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic maidenhair tree for bone health.

  1. Harvest and dry ginkgo biloba leaves weighing 20 grams then grind into fine powder using mortar and pestle.
  2. Mix the powdered ginkgo biloba with 50 grams of beeswax and 30 grams of coconut oil in a heat-resistant container.
  3. Place the mixture in a double boiler or a microwave-safe bowl and melt for 10 minutes at low heat stirring occasionally.
  4. Remove from heat and add 20 drops of vitamin d oil and 15 drops of lavender essential oil then stir well to combine.
  5. Allow the mixture to cool and solidify completely before transferring it to an airtight container for storage.
