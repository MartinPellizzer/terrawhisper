1. Camellia sinensis

Tea teas helps with bone health because of its unique combination of flavonoids, polyphenols, and other bioactive compounds that promote osteoblast activity and inhibit osteoclast activity.

This results in improved bone density, reduced risk of fractures, and enhanced bone mineralization.

Many herbal tea varieties, such as rosehip, sage, and nettle, are particularly rich in these beneficial compounds, making them a natural and effective way to support overall bone health and reduce the risk of osteoporosis.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in tea teas that aid with bone health.

  • Flavonoids: Flavonoids, particularly in herbs like peppermint, chamomile, and rosehip tea, help to increase calcium absorption and reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to osteoporosis.
  • Silica: Silica-rich herbs like oatstraw, dandelion root, and alfalfa leaf tea contain minerals that help to strengthen bones and improve bone density by promoting collagen production and reducing the excretion of calcium in urine.
  • Polyphenols: Polyphenol-rich herbs like green tea, ginkgo biloba, and turmeric tea contain antioxidants that reduce inflammation, which is a key risk factor for osteoporosis, and may also help to slow down bone loss by inhibiting the activity of enzymes that break down bone tissue.

The list below shows the primary parts of tea used to make teas for bone health.

  • Roots: Rich in calcium and other minerals, roots are a natural source of support for bone health and can help reduce the risk of osteoporosis.
  • Barks: Containing flavonoids and phenolic acids, barks have been traditionally used to promote bone density and strength.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic tea for bone health.

  1. Harvest high-quality camellia sinensis leaves and stems in the early morning or late afternoon.
  2. Rinse 1-2 teaspoons of dried or fresh leaves and stems with filtered water to remove impurities.
  3. Heat 1 cup of filtered water to a temperature between 195°f and 205°f and steep the herbal mixture for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean teapot or mug, discarding solids.
  5. Consume the tea immediately or let it cool to room temperature before refrigerating for up to 24 hours.
