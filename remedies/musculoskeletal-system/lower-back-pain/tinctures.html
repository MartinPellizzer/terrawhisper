1. Corydalis yanhusuo

Yanhusuo tinctures helps with lower back pain because of its potent analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The herb's active compounds, such as corydaline and allocryptopine, effectively reduce muscle spasms and inflammation in the back muscles, thereby alleviating discomfort and pain. By targeting both the physical and nervous sources of pain, Yanhusuo tinctures provide effective relief for those suffering from lower back strain.

Medicinal Constituents

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Corydalis yanhusuo tinctures that help with lower back pain.

  • Corydaline: This alkaloid has analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help reduce pain and inflammation associated with lower back pain.
  • Corynoline: This alkaloid has a similar analgesic effect to corydaline, and may also help to reduce muscle spasms and promote relaxation, which can contribute to lower back pain relief.
  • Dehydrocorybulbine: This alkaloid has been shown to have analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which may help to reduce pain, inflammation, and oxidative stress associated with lower back pain.

Parts Used

The list below shows the primary parts of yanhusuo used to make tinctures for lower back pain.

  • Roots: They are rich in alkaloids, specifically dehydrocorybulbine, which is responsible for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Rhyzomes: They contain a higher concentration of dehydrocorybulbine and other bioactive compounds, making them a preferred choice for pain relief.
  • Leaves: They also contain alkaloids, although in lower concentrations than roots and rhyzomes, but are still valued for their analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yanhusuo for lower back pain.

  1. Harvest the corydalis yanhusuo roots in late fall when the plant is dormant after the first frost.
  2. Dry the roots in a single layer at 30 degrees celsius for 2 days to preserve the medicinal properties.
  3. Chop the dried roots into small pieces and mix with 80 proof vodka at a 1:5 ratio.
  4. Store the mixture in a clean glass jar with a tight lid and steep for 2 weeks in a cool dark place.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle and discard the solids.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric for lower back pain.
