1. Cymbopogon citratus

Lemongrass essential oils helps with lower back pain because of its potent anti-inflammatory properties, which effectively reduce muscle tension and relieve strain on the spine.

The citrusy scent also has a calming effect, easing spasms and cramps that can exacerbate discomfort.

Additionally, lemongrass oil's natural analgesic properties help to dull persistent aches and pains, promoting relaxation and rejuvenation of the entire body.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in lemongrass essential oils that aid with lower back pain.

  • Antispasmodic property: Lemongrass essential oil helps to relax muscles and relieve spasms, which can provide relief from lower back pain caused by muscle tension or inflammation.
  • Anti-inflammatory property: The anti-inflammatory compounds in lemongrass essential oil can help reduce swelling and pain associated with lower back pain conditions such as sciatica or herniated discs.
  • Analgesic property: Lemongrass essential oil has been shown to have analgesic properties, which means it can help numb the area affected by lower back pain, providing temporary relief from discomfort and pain.

The list below shows the primary parts of lemongrass used to make essential oils for lower back pain.

  • Roots: Rich in sesquiterpenes, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce muscle and joint pain.
  • Rhyzomes: Contains compounds like citral and geraniol, which have analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, relieving back pain and stiffness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lemongrass for lower back pain.

  1. Harvest fresh cymbopogon citratus leaves in the early morning, collecting at least one pound for processing.
  2. Dry the harvested leaves in a warm, well-ventilated area, away from direct sunlight, for 7-10 days.
  3. Steam distill the dried cymbopogon citratus leaves using a distillation apparatus with water and steam, collecting the essential oil for 4-6 hours.
  4. Filter the collected essential oil through a coffee filter or cheesecloth to remove impurities and sediment, resulting in a clear yellowish liquid.
  5. Store the obtained cymbopogon citratus essential oil in dark glass bottles with tight-fitting lids, kept away from light and heat, for future use.
