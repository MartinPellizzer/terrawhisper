Ankylosing Spondylitis: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Ankylosing spondylitis is a chronic inflammatory disease characterized by inflammation and stiffness of the joints in the spine and other areas of the body, leading to severe pain and limited mobility.

It is caused by a combination of genetic and environmental factors, with a family history and certain genetic markers increasing the risk of developing the condition. If left untreated, ankylosing spondylitis can lead to serious health complications, including bowel problems, eye inflammation, and increased risk of cardiovascular disease. Herbal remedies, such as turmeric containing curcumin, ginger, and boswellia, have anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate symptoms and reduce pain.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as they can interact with other medications and exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of ankylosing spondylitis, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of ankylosing spondylitis?

The main causes of ankylosing spondylitis are still not fully understood, but research suggests that it is a complex interplay of genetic and environmental factors.

The condition is associated with the presence of HLA-B27 gene, which is found in about 90% of people with ankylosing spondylitis. However, having the gene does not guarantee the development of the disease, and other genetic and environmental factors are also involved. Inflammation in the body, particularly in the spinal joints, is a key component of ankylosing spondylitis, and it is thought that the immune system mistakenly attacks the body's own tissue, leading to inflammation and damage.

Additionally, some research suggests that certain infections, such as gastroenteritis, may trigger the onset of ankylosing spondylitis in genetically predisposed individuals.

  • HLA-B27: HLA-B27 is a specific protein marker that significantly increases the risk of developing ankylosing spondylitis, particularly in individuals of Caucasian and European descent.
  • Genetics: Genetics play a crucial role in the development of ankylosing spondylitis, with family history and specific genetic mutations, such as those in the ERAP1 and IL23R genes, contributing to the risk.
  • Infection: Infection, particularly with bacteria such as Klebsiella pneumoniae, has been suggested to trigger the onset of ankylosing spondylitis in genetically predisposed individuals.
  • Tobacco use: Tobacco use has been identified as a potential trigger for ankylosing spondylitis, possibly due to its ability to induce inflammation and oxidative stress.
  • Environmental factors: Environmental factors, such as exposure to certain chemicals and physical stress, may contribute to the development of ankylosing spondylitis, although the exact mechanisms are not fully understood.
  • Autoimmune response: An autoimmune response, in which the immune system mistakenly attacks healthy tissues, is thought to play a role in the development of ankylosing spondylitis, particularly in the inflammation and damage of spinal and sacroiliac joints.
