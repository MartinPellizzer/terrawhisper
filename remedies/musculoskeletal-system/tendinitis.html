What is tendinitis and how it affects your life?

Tendinitis is a painful condition that occurs when the tendons, which are the fibers that connect muscles to bones, become inflamed or irritated.

This debilitating condition can have a significant impact on one's daily life, making even simple activities a struggle. For instance, individuals with tendinitis in their Achilles tendon may find it difficult to walk or stand for long periods, making everyday tasks like grocery shopping or running errands a daunting task.

Those affected by elbow tendinitis may experience difficulty lifting objects or performing daily chores, such as cooking or cleaning, while hand tendinitis can make simple tasks like typing or holding objects a painful and frustrating experience. In addition, tendinitis in the knee or hip joints can cause limited mobility, making it challenging to engage in activities that were once enjoyed, such as playing sports or hiking.

Furthermore, the chronic pain and stiffness associated with tendinitis can also lead to sleep disturbances, mood changes, and decreased overall quality of life.

What are the main causes of tendinitis?

The main causes of tendinitis are repetitive strain, overuse, and improper movement or technique.

Tendons are connective tissue that connects muscles to bones, and when they are subjected to repeated stress or trauma, inflammation can occur, leading to pain and stiffness. This is often the case in athletes who participate in activities that involve repetitive movements, such as tennis players experiencing wrist tendinitis due to frequent serving and forehand swings.

Similarly, individuals who work jobs that require heavy lifting, bending, or twisting, such as construction workers, are also at risk of developing tendinitis. Additionally, poor posture or movement patterns can put unnecessary stress on certain tendons, leading to inflammation and pain.

In some cases, a sudden injury or trauma, such as a fall or direct blow, can also cause tendinitis.

The most common causes of tendinitis are listed below.

  • Overuse: Tendinitis can occur when you repeatedly use a joint or muscle, leading to inflammation and irritation of the tendon.
  • Age-related wear and tear: As we age, our tendons naturally degenerate and become more prone to injury, increasing the risk of tendinitis.
  • Improper technique: Using incorrect posture, movement patterns, or exercise form can put excessive stress on tendons, leading to inflammation and tendinitis.
  • Repetitive strain: Repeatedly performing tasks that involve bending, twisting, lifting, or carrying heavy objects can cause tendon irritation and tendinitis.
  • Sports injuries: Participating in high-impact sports like tennis, running, or jumping can put excessive stress on joints and lead to tendinitis.
  • Muscle imbalances: Weak or tight muscles surrounding a joint can pull unevenly on the tendons, leading to inflammation and tendinitis.
  • Poor footwear: Wearing shoes that don't provide adequate support or cushioning can increase the risk of tendinitis in feet and ankles.
  • Medical conditions: Certain medical conditions like diabetes, rheumatoid arthritis, or thyroid disorders can increase the risk of developing tendinitis.
  • Trauma: A direct blow to a joint or muscle can cause inflammation and tendinitis.
  • Biomechanical issues: Abnormal movement patterns or foot mechanics can put excessive stress on joints and tendons, leading to tendinitis.
