What is tendinitis and how it affects your life?

Tendinitis is a painful condition that occurs when the tendons, which connect muscles to bones, become inflamed.

This inflammation can lead to severe pain, stiffness, and limited mobility in the affected area, making everyday activities a challenge. For instance, a person suffering from tendinitis in their elbow may find it difficult to perform simple tasks like tying their shoelaces or holding a pencil.

Similarly, tendinitis in the Achilles tendon can make it hard to walk or run, while inflammation in the rotator cuff can make it painful to lift or move the arm. Tendinitis in the fingers or thumb can make it difficult to perform fine motor tasks, such as typing or playing musical instruments. Even simple actions like opening jars or doing household chores can become daunting tasks when suffering from tendinitis.

The pain and discomfort can also affect sleep and mood, making it a debilitating condition that impacts daily life significantly.

What are the main causes of tendinitis?

Tendinitis is a common condition characterized by inflammation or irritation of the tendons, which are fibrous tissues that connect muscles to bones.

Similarly, tendinitis in the elbow (tennis elbow) can make it difficult to grip or twist objects, affecting work or hobbies that require manual dexterity.

Other examples of tendinitis affecting daily life include: chronic knee pain making it hard to climb stairs or engage in sports, persistent wrist pain hindering ability to cook or type, and shoulder pain making it uncomfortable to carry groceries or lift heavy objects.
