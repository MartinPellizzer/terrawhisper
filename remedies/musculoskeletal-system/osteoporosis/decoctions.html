Figwort decoctions helps with osteoporosis because it is rich in phytochemicals, particularly saponins and flavonoids, which have been shown to promote bone density and strength.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic figwort for osteoporosis.

  1. Gather dried scrophularia nodosa root material in sufficient quantity for the desired amount of decoction.
  2. Measure and combine one tablespoon of dried scrophularia nodosa roots with four cups of pure water.
  3. Bring the water mixture to a boil in a pot then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
  4. Strain the decoction through a fine-mesh filter or cheesecloth into a clean glass container discarding solids.
  5. Cool and store the decoction in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to five days.
