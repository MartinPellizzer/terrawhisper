Maidenhair tree essential oils helps with osteoporosis because of its unique blend of chemical compounds that promote bone health.

The oil's rich antioxidant properties help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body, which can contribute to bone degradation.

Additionally, maidenhair tree oil has been shown to stimulate collagen production, improve calcium absorption, and increase bone density, all of which are essential for maintaining strong bones and preventing osteoporosis-related fractures.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in maidenhair tree essential oils that aid with osteoporosis.

  • Antioxidant activity: Ginkgo biloba essential oil has antioxidant properties that can help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation, which can contribute to bone loss in osteoporosis.
  • Vasodilatory effect: The essential oil's ability to dilate blood vessels may improve circulation, which is important for delivering calcium and other minerals to bones, potentially reducing the risk of osteoporosis.
  • Inhibitory activity on osteoclasts: Ginkgo biloba extract has been shown to inhibit the activity of osteoclasts, which are cells that break down bone tissue, suggesting a potential role in preventing bone loss.

  • Roots: The roots of the maidenhair tree contain high amounts of saponins, which have been shown to promote bone growth and density, making it an effective ingredient in treating osteoporosis.
  • Barks: The barks of the maidenhair tree are rich in flavonoids, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce bone loss and promote bone health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic maidenhair tree essential oil for osteoporosis.

  1. Harvest fresh ginkgo biloba leaves in late summer or early fall and dry them immediately using a dehydrator set at 60°f for 24 hours.
  2. Crush the dried leaves into small pieces using a mortar and pestle to release their aromatic compounds and oil-rich content.
  3. Steep the crushed ginkgo biloba leaves in a carrier oil such as sweet almond oil or jojoba oil at a ratio of 1:5 for 4 weeks with occasional shaking.
  4. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a glass bottle and discard the solids, leaving approximately 10 milliliters of infused oil.
  5. Store the herbal essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid at room temperature for up to six months, shaking occasionally before use.
