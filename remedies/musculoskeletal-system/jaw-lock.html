What is jaw lock and how it affects your life?

Jaw lock, also known as temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorder, is a painful condition that affects the jaw joint and surrounding muscles, causing limited mobility and discomfort.

When someone has a jaw lock, they may experience difficulty opening or closing their mouth, chewing, or speaking, which can be frustrating and affect their daily activities. For instance, eating becomes a chore as they struggle to bite and chew food, making mealtimes a source of stress and anxiety.

Even simple tasks like talking on the phone, eating an apple, or even yawning can become excruciatingly painful. Social interactions become strained as they may avoid speaking or eating in front of others, affecting their relationships and overall quality of life. Moreover, jaw lock can also lead to headaches, earaches, and facial pain, making everyday activities like working, studying, or even sleeping uncomfortable.

It can even affect their self-esteem and confidence, as they may feel embarrassed or self-conscious about their condition, impacting their mental well-being and overall happiness.
