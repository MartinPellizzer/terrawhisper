Jaw Pain: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Jaw pain, also known as temporomandibular joint (TMJ) pain, refers to discomfort or pain in the jaw joint and surrounding muscles, often caused by inflammation, teeth grinding, or misaligned teeth.

The causes of jaw pain can range from stress and anxiety to dental issues and even certain medical conditions. If left untreated, jaw pain can lead to headaches, ear problems, and difficulties eating and speaking.

To alleviate jaw pain, herbal remedies such as chamomile tea, ginger root, and willow bark can be used. These herbs can be consumed as teas or applied topically as salves or oils.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, especially if you are pregnant or have allergies, to avoid potential interactions or adverse reactions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of jaw pain, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of jaw pain?

The main causes of jaw pain are multifaceted and can be attributed to various factors.

Temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders, which are the leading cause of jaw pain, are often the result of misalignment or malocclusion of the teeth, trauma, or repetitive strain from habits such as teeth grinding or clenching. Other causes include gum recession, impacted wisdom teeth, and dental abscesses, which can be caused by poor oral hygiene, diet, or underlying medical conditions such as rheumatoid arthritis. Additionally, jaw pain can be a symptom of a larger issue, such as sinusitis, migraines, or fibromyalgia.

According to the American Dental Association, approximately 10% of the population experiences jaw pain, with women being more likely to experience TMJ disorders than men.

  • Temporomandibular Disorder: Temporomandibular disorder is a condition characterized by pain and stiffness in the jaw joint and surrounding muscles, often caused by a misaligned bite, teeth grinding, or jaw clenching.
  • Bruxism: Bruxism is a condition where people involuntarily grind or clench their teeth, putting excessive strain on the jaw muscles and joint, leading to pain and discomfort.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis, an infection or inflammation of the sinuses, can cause jaw pain due to the proximity of the sinuses to the jaw joint and the potential for referred pain from the sinuses to the jaw.
  • Dental Abscess: A dental abscess, a bacterial infection of the gum or tooth pulp, can cause jaw pain due to the spread of infection to the jawbone and surrounding tissues.
  • Gum Disease: Gum disease, a bacterial infection of the gums, can cause jaw pain due to inflammation and infection of the gum tissue and bone that supports the jaw.
  • Jaw Injury: A jaw injury, such as a fracture or dislocation, can cause jaw pain due to the trauma and inflammation caused by the injury.
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis: Rheumatoid arthritis, an autoimmune disease that causes inflammation and pain in the joints, can cause jaw pain due to the involvement of the temporomandibular joint and surrounding tissues.
  • Gout: Gout, a type of arthritis caused by the buildup of uric acid crystals in the joints, can cause jaw pain due to the involvement of the temporomandibular joint and surrounding tissues.
  • Tumors: Tumors, abnormal growths of tissue, can cause jaw pain due to the pressure and inflammation caused by the tumor on the surrounding tissues and bone.
