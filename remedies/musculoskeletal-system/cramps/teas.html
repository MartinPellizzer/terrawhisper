  • Emmenagogue: Mugwort tea stimulates menstrual flow and regulates menstrual cycles, which can help reduce cramping and discomfort. The emmenagogue effect is primarily due to the presence of the chemical compound, thujone.
  • Nervine: Mugwort tea has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help alleviate stress and anxiety associated with menstrual cramps. The nervine property is attributed to the presence of flavonoids and essential oils in mugwort.

The list below shows the primary parts of mugwort tea used to make teas for cramps.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the mugwort plant are commonly used to make medicinal tea for cramps due to their antispasmodic properties, which help to relax the muscles and alleviate discomfort.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhyzomes of the mugwort plant contain high levels of volatile oils and other compounds that have been shown to have analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, making them useful for reducing cramp-related pain and inflammation.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the mugwort plant are rich in flavonoids and other polyphenolic compounds that have been shown to have antioxidant and muscle-relaxing effects, making them useful for treating menstrual cramps and other types of muscle spasms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic mugwort tea for cramps.

  1. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool down for a minute or two, so it's not boiling hot when you add the mugwort.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried mugwort leaves to a tea infuser or a reusable tea bag.
  3. Pour the hot water over the mugwort leaves and let it steep for 5-10 minutes, depending on how strong you like your tea.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag and discard the used mugwort leaves.
  5. Add honey or lemon to taste (optional) and enjoy your homemade mugwort tea for cramps.
