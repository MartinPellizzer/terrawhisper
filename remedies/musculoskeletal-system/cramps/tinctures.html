1. Mugwort tincture

Mugwort tincture helps with cramps because it contains antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory compounds that can relax the smooth muscles of the uterus and reduce inflammation.

These properties can help alleviate menstrual cramps and discomfort. The list below shows the primary active constituents in mugwort tincture that aid with cramps.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Its anti-inflammatory properties, attributed to compounds like chamazulene and alpha-thujone, can help reduce inflammation and swelling, thereby alleviating cramps.
  • Analgesic: Mugwort tincture has pain-relieving properties, which can help manage the discomfort and pain associated with cramps, thanks to its active constituents like camphor, borneol, and thujone.
  • Nervine: Mugwort tincture acts as a nervine, calming the nervous system and reducing anxiety, which can help alleviate tension-related cramps, due to its active constituents like flavonoids and essential oils.
  • Circulatory stimulant: Mugwort tincture can improve blood flow and circulation, helping to relieve cramps by ensuring adequate oxygen and nutrient supply to muscles, due to its active constituents like volatile oils and lactones.

The list below shows the primary parts of mugwort tincture used to make tinctures for cramps.

  • Leaves: Contain volatile oils and flavonoids that have antispasmodic properties, making them effective in alleviating menstrual cramps.
  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, helping to reduce inflammation and alleviate muscle spasms.
  • Rhyzomes: Packed with essential oils and therapeutic compounds, rhyzomes have been traditionally used to treat menstrual cramps and regulate menstrual cycles.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic mugwort tincture for cramps.

  1. Chop 1 ounce of dried mugwort root into small pieces and place in a clean jar.
  2. Pour 2 cups of 100-proof vodka or grain alcohol over the mugwort, ensuring it is fully submerged.
  3. Seal the jar tightly and store in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking daily.
  4. Strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out any excess liquid. Store the tincture in a tightly sealed glass bottle.
  5. Take 1-2 mL of the mugwort tincture up to three times a day for cramps, as needed.
