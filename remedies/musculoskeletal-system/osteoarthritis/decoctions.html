1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Licorice decoctions helps with osteoarthritis because they contain compounds that reduce inflammation and alleviate joint pain.

Glycyrrhizin, a primary component of licorice root, inhibits the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which contribute to joint damage. Additionally, licorice decoctions have been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in cartilage breakdown, thereby slowing down the progression of osteoarthritis.

By reducing inflammation and protecting cartilage health, herbal licorice decoctions provide a natural relief for osteoarthritis sufferers.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in licorice decoctions that aid with osteoarthritis.

  • Glycyrrhizin: Helps reduce inflammation and alleviate pain by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, thereby reducing joint swelling and discomfort.
  • Licoricidin: Has anti-catabolic effects, meaning it helps prevent the breakdown of cartilage and bone tissue, which can worsen osteoarthritis symptoms over time.
  • Isomaltose: Exhibits antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals that can damage joint tissues and exacerbate osteoarthritis.

The list below shows the primary parts of licorice used to make decoctions for osteoarthritis.

  • Roots: The roots of the licorice plant are used due to their high content of glycyrrhizin, which helps reduce inflammation and alleviate pain associated with osteoarthritis.
  • Rhyzomes: Licorice rhyzomes are rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that help reduce joint inflammation and improve joint mobility.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice for osteoarthritis.

  1. Harvest high-quality dried roots of glycyrrhiza glabra and store them in an airtight container for future use.
  2. Measure 10 grams of the dried root material and add it to a heat-resistant glass bowl.
  3. Boil 500 milliliters of distilled water in a separate pot and pour it over the root material, allowing it to steep for 20 minutes.
  4. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass container, discarding the solids and reserving the liquid.
  5. Allow the decoction to cool to room temperature before refrigerating or freezing it for up to three days for future use.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric for osteoarthritis.

  1. Wash 2-3 teaspoons of dried turmeric root powder in cold water then drain well.
  2. Boil one liter of distilled water in a large saucepan for exactly 10 minutes.
  3. Add the washed turmeric root powder to the boiling water and stir well immediately.
  4. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the liquid reduces by half.
  5. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass container.
