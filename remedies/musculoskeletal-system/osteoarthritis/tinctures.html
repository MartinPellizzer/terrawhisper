1. Black cohosh tincture

Black cohosh tincture helps with osteoarthritis because it contains phytoestrogens, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

These properties can help alleviate the pain and inflammation associated with osteoarthritis. Additionally, black cohosh has been found to inhibit the degradation of cartilage, a key factor in the progression of osteoarthritis. The tincture form of black cohosh allows for quick absorption and increased bioavailability of its active compounds, making it a potentially effective option for managing osteoarthritis symptoms.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in black cohosh tincture that aid with osteoarthritis.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Black cohosh tincture helps reduce inflammation in osteoarthritis by containing triterpene glycosides, which inhibit the release of pro-inflammatory cytokines.
  • Analgesic: Its pain-relieving properties come from isoferulic acid and salicylic acid, which help alleviate osteoarthritis pain by inhibiting the production of pain-inducing prostaglandins.
  • Antioxidant: Black cohosh contains phenolic acids and flavonoids, which exhibit antioxidant properties, helping protect cartilage from oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals in osteoarthritis.
  • Estrogenic: Black cohosh tincture may help regulate estrogen levels due to the presence of isoflavones and formononetin, which can alleviate symptoms of osteoarthritis, particularly in postmenopausal women.
  • Chondroprotective: The herb's chondroprotective effects, attributed to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, may help preserve cartilage integrity in osteoarthritis by reducing degradation and promoting cartilage repair.

The list below shows the primary parts of black cohosh tincture used to make tinctures for osteoarthritis.

  • Roots: The roots of black cohosh contain active compounds that have been shown to help reduce inflammation and relieve joint pain associated with osteoarthritis.
  • Rhizomes: The rhizomes of black cohosh contain triterpene glycosides, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory properties, making them useful for treating osteoarthritis symptoms.
  • Flowers: While not as potent as the roots or rhizomes, the flowers of black cohosh do contain some active compounds that can help reduce inflammation and provide relief from osteoarthritis symptoms.
  • Leaves: The leaves of black cohosh contain small amounts of the same active compounds found in the roots and rhizomes, making them a suitable addition to medicinal teas for osteoarthritis.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic black cohosh tincture for osteoarthritis.

  1. Chop 1 ounce of dried black cohosh root into small pieces and place in a clean glass jar.
  2. Pour 2 cups of 100-proof vodka or grain alcohol over the chopped root, ensuring it is fully submerged.
  3. Seal the jar and store in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks, shaking daily.
  4. Strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out the excess liquid.
  5. Store the final tincture in a tightly sealed glass bottle, discarding the spent plant material. Take 30-40 drops, 3 times daily, as needed for osteoarthritis symptoms.
