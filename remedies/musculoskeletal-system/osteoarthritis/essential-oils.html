The list below shows the primary parts of turmeric used to make essential oils for osteoarthritis.

  • Rhyzomes: The thick, underground stems of turmeric contain a high concentration of curcuminoids, which have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help alleviate symptoms of osteoarthritis.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric for osteoarthritis.

  1. Harvest 50g of fresh turmeric root and rinse it thoroughly under cold running water.
  2. Dry the harvested turmeric in a warm place for 3-4 days or until it becomes brittle.
  3. Blend 20g of dried turmeric powder with 100ml of carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.
  4. Place the blended mixture in a glass container and leave it to infuse for 14 days with occasional shaking.
  5. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container and discard solids.
