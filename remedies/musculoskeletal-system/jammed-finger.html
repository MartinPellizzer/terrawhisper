Jammed Finger: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

A jammed finger, also known as a mallet finger, is a type of injury where the extensor tendon of the finger is stretched or torn, causing pain, swelling, and difficulty in bending the finger.

This condition often occurs when an object lands on the fingertip, causing the finger to be jammed. The causes of jammed fingers include sports injuries, accidents, and improper lifting techniques.

If left untreated, a jammed finger can lead to chronic pain, limited mobility, and even permanent damage to the tendon. To alleviate the pain and promote healing, herbal remedies such as arnica oil, aloe vera gel, and turmeric paste can be applied topically.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as some may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of jammed finger, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of jammed finger?

The main causes of jammed finger are direct impact or sudden force applied to the finger, often resulting from sports injuries, falls, or accidents, with the most common causes being finger bending (45-55 degrees) and subsequent stretching of the ligaments, tendons, and joint capsule, which can lead to partial or complete disruption of the joint and surrounding tissues.

A jammed finger can also occur when the finger is forced into an abnormal position, such as hyperextension or hyperflexion, causing the joint to be stretched beyond its normal range of motion. Additionally, repetitive strain or overuse injuries can also lead to jammed fingers, particularly in athletes participating in activities that involve quick changes of direction or repetitive finger movements. The most common injury to the finger is the volar plate injury, which occurs when the ligaments that support the finger joint are stretched or torn.

According to the American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons, jammed fingers are most common in sports such as basketball, football, and soccer.

  • Sports: Sports injuries, particularly those involving contact sports such as football, basketball, and hockey, are a leading cause of jammed fingers due to the high-speed impact and sudden bending of the finger.
  • Falls: Falling objects or falling from a height can cause a jammed finger as the finger gets caught between the object and the ground, resulting in sudden and forceful bending.
  • Accidents: Accidents involving machinery, tools, and other equipment can cause jammed fingers due to the finger getting caught or compressed between moving parts.
  • Overexertion: Overexertion during activities such as lifting heavy objects or bending can cause a jammed finger due to the excessive force applied to the finger joint.
  • Bicycle Crashes: Bicycle crashes, particularly those involving handlebars and the ground, can cause a jammed finger due to the sudden bending and twisting of the finger.
  • Workplace Injuries: Workplace injuries, such as those involving machinery, tools, and other equipment, can cause jammed fingers due to the finger getting caught or compressed between moving parts.
  • Home Accidents: Home accidents, such as those involving furniture, toys, and other objects, can cause jammed fingers due to the finger getting caught or compressed between objects.
  • Playground Injuries: Playground injuries, particularly those involving climbing structures and slides, can cause jammed fingers due to the sudden bending and twisting of the finger.
  • Slip and Falls: Slip and fall accidents can cause jammed fingers due to the finger getting caught between the ground and an object.
  • Animal-Related Injuries: Animal-related injuries, such as those involving pets and wild animals, can cause jammed fingers due to the sudden biting or pouncing of the animal.
