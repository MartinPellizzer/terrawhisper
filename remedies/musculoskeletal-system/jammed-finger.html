What is jammed finger and how it affects your life?

A jammed finger is a common injury that occurs when the fingers are bent or forced beyond their normal range of motion, causing the tendons, ligaments, or joints to become strained or torn.

This can lead to pain, swelling, and stiffness in the affected finger, making everyday activities challenging. A jammed finger can affect various aspects of life, such as work, where it may hinder one's ability to perform tasks that require fine motor skills, like typing or operating machinery.

It can also impact sports and recreational activities, making it difficult to participate in activities that require grip strength or dexterity. Furthermore, a jammed finger can make it hard to cook, clean, or even dress oneself, as simple tasks become painful and frustrating. Additionally, it can cause emotional distress, as it can be a source of anxiety and frustration, especially when trying to perform daily routines that were once effortless.
