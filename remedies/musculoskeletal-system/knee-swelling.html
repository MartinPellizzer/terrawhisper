Knee Swelling: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Knee swelling, also known as edema, is the abnormal accumulation of fluid in the soft tissues surrounding the knee joint, causing it to become inflamed and enlarged.

This condition can be caused by various factors, including injury, overuse, arthritis, and poor circulation. If left untreated, knee swelling can lead to chronic pain, limited mobility, and increased risk of falls and injuries.

To alleviate knee swelling, herbal remedies such as turmeric, ginger, and willow bark can be used to reduce inflammation and relieve pain. These herbs can be consumed as teas, capsules, or applied topically as creams or ointments.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as they can interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of knee swelling, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of knee swelling?

The main causes of knee swelling are injury to the ligaments or tendons, inflammation of the bursae (inframensical and suprapatellar bursitis) or synovium (synovitis), fluid accumulation due to fluid imbalance or liver cirrhosis, infection by bacteria or viruses (septic arthritis), and inflammatory conditions such as gout, pseudogout, and rheumatoid arthritis.

Injury to the knee can cause bleeding into the joint space and swelling. Inflammation of the bursae or synovium can cause swelling due to increased fluid production and impaired drainage. Fluid imbalance, often due to heart failure or liver cirrhosis, can lead to fluid accumulation in the knee joint.

Infections and inflammatory conditions can cause swelling through the activation of immune cells and release of chemical mediators.

  • Gout: Gout is a condition where uric acid crystals accumulate in the joint, causing inflammation and swelling, often due to genetic predisposition, diet rich in purines, or kidney dysfunction.
  • RA: Rheumatoid arthritis is a chronic autoimmune disorder that causes the immune system to attack the lining of the joints, leading to inflammation, swelling, and pain in the knee and other joints.
  • Tendinitis: Tendinitis is an inflammation of the tendons, usually due to overuse or repetitive strain, which can cause swelling and pain in the knee.
  • Meniscus Tear: A meniscus tear is a common injury that occurs when the cartilage in the knee joint is torn, often due to a sudden twisting motion or a direct blow to the knee, causing swelling and pain.
  • Bursitis: Bursitis is an inflammation of the fluid-filled sacs, known as bursae, that cushion joints and reduce friction, often due to repetitive motion or direct trauma, causing swelling and pain in the knee.
  • Osteoarthritis: Osteoarthritis is a degenerative joint disease that causes the cartilage in the knee joint to wear down, leading to bone-on-bone contact, inflammation, and swelling.
  • Ligament Sprain: A ligament sprain is a stretching or tearing of the ligaments that connect bones to each other, often due to a sudden twisting motion or a direct blow to the knee, causing swelling and pain.
  • Knee Fracture: A knee fracture is a break in one or more of the bones in the knee joint, often due to a direct blow or a fall, causing swelling, pain, and instability in the knee.
  • Infection: A knee infection, such as cellulitis or osteomyelitis, is a bacterial or fungal infection that can cause inflammation, swelling, and pain in the knee.
  • Pregnancy: Pregnancy can cause knee swelling due to increased fluid retention, weight gain, and changes in the body's hormonal balance.
  • Kidney Disease: Kidney disease can cause knee swelling due to the buildup of waste products in the body, which can cause fluid retention and swelling in the knee and other joints.
