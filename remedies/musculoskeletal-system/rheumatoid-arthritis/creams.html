1. Curcuma longa

Turmeric creams helps with rheumatoid arthritis because they contain curcumin, a potent anti-inflammatory compound that targets the root cause of the condition.

Curcumin binds to inflammatory molecules in the body, reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms such as joint pain and stiffness.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in turmeric creams that aid with rheumatoid arthritis.

  • Curcuminoids: These bioactive compounds, particularly curcumin, have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which help reduce joint inflammation and oxidative stress in RA patients.
  • Cyclooxygenase-2 (cox-2) inhibition: Turmeric creams containing curcumin have been shown to inhibit COX-2 enzyme activity, thereby reducing the production of prostaglandins and other inflammatory mediators that contribute to RA symptoms.
  • Anti-cytokine effects: Curcumin in turmeric creams has been found to suppress pro-inflammatory cytokines, such as TNF-alpha and IL-1beta, which play a key role in RA pathology.

The list below shows the primary parts of turmeric used to make creams for rheumatoid arthritis.

  • Rhyzomes: Rich in curcuminoids, which have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making them effective in reducing joint pain and inflammation associated with rheumatoid arthritis.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric for rheumatoid arthritis.

  1. Harvest 50 grams of fresh turmeric root curcuma longa and clean it thoroughly with distilled water.
  2. Dry the turmeric root in a low-temperature oven at 60°c for 4 hours or until completely dry.
  3. Grind 20 grams of dried turmeric powder into a fine paste using a mortar and pestle for 5 minutes.
  4. Mix the turmeric paste with 100ml of coconut oil and 50ml of aloe vera gel in a double boiler at 70°c for 10 minutes.
  5. Pour the mixture into sterile glass jars and store it in the refrigerator for 24 hours before use.
