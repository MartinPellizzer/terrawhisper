Turmeric essential oils helps with rheumatoid arthritis because of its potent anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce joint swelling and pain.

The curcuminoids in turmeric oil inhibit pro-inflammatory enzymes, thus decreasing inflammation and oxidative stress. Additionally, it also has natural analgesic and anti-pyretic effects, providing relief from chronic pain and stiffness associated with rheumatoid arthritis.

  • Cineole: Cineole, a constituent of turmeric essential oil, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, thereby reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms of RA.

The list below shows the primary parts of turmeric essential oil used to make essential oils for rheumatoid arthritis.

  • Roots: Contain a high concentration of curcumin, a potent anti-inflammatory compound that helps reduce joint pain and swelling associated with rheumatoid arthritis.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric essential oil for rheumatoid arthritis.

  1. Harvest high-quality turmeric roots at peak ripeness for optimal curcumin content.
  2. Dry the harvested turmeric roots in shade or using a food dehydrator on low heat for 6-8 hours.
  3. Grind the dried turmeric roots into fine powder using a coffee grinder or spice mill and sift through a 100-mesh sieve.
  4. Combine 1 part ground turmeric with 2 parts carrier oil such as coconut oil or olive oil in a clean glass jar and store in a cool dark place for 7-10 days.
  5. Filter the mixture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a separate container to extract the essential oil from the curcuma longa.
