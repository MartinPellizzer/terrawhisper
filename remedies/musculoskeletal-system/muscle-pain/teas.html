1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce muscle pain and swelling.

When consumed, these compounds interact with pain receptors in the body, blocking the production of pain-causing chemicals like prostaglandins. This results in reduced muscle inflammation and pain. If you're experiencing muscle pain, try making a warm Zingiber officinale tea by steeping a teaspoon of dried ginger root in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. To enhance the effectiveness, try combining Zingiber officinale tea with other anti-inflammatory herbs like turmeric or willow bark. From taking Zingiber officinale tea, you may experience a reduction in muscle pain and inflammation within 30 minutes to an hour. Some people may also notice an improvement in their range of motion and ability to move without discomfort. However, the effects may vary depending on the severity of the muscle pain and individual tolerance.

To get the most out of Zingiber officinale tea, make sure to drink it regularly and combine it with a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet and regular exercise.

2. Curcuma longa

To use Curcuma longa teas for muscle pain, steep a teaspoon of dried turmeric root in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain and drink 1-2 cups a day.

You can also add a pinch of black pepper, which enhances curcumin's bioavailability by 2,000 times. Start with a small dose and gradually increase as needed. If you have sensitive stomachs, consider taking it after meals to minimize potential digestive discomfort. When you drink Curcuma longa teas regularly, you may experience a reduction in muscle pain and inflammation within a few days. 3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains licorice root, which is rich in compounds like glycyrrhizin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These properties help reduce muscle pain by inhibiting the production of inflammatory enzymes, such as COX-2, and by blocking the action of pain-producing chemicals like bradykinin. Glycyrrhizin, in particular, has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, which can help relax tense muscles and reduce muscle spasms. If you're experiencing muscle pain, you can try drinking Glycyrrhiza glabra teas 2-3 times a day. You can steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried licorice root in boiling water for 5-7 minutes to make a tea. Strain the tea and drink it warm or at room temperature.

You can also add a pinch of salt or honey to taste. To get the most out of the tea, it's best to drink it before bedtime and on an empty stomach. When you start drinking Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, you may notice a decrease in muscle pain and inflammation within a few hours. You may also feel a sense of relaxation and calmness, which can help you sleep better. In some cases, you may experience a reduction in muscle spasms and cramps, making it easier to move and perform daily activities.
