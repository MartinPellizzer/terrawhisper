1. Astragalus membranaceus teas

Astragalus membranaceus teas have been traditionally used to treat hyperthyroidism due to their adaptogenic properties, which help the body to adapt to stress and balance the thyroid function.

The bioactive constituents of Astragalus membranaceus, including polysaccharides, flavonoids, and saponins, have been found to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to regulate the thyroid hormones and alleviate symptoms of hyperthyroidism.

By reducing inflammation and oxidative stress, Astragalus membranaceus teas can help to slow down the production of thyroid hormones and promote a more balanced thyroid function.

The benefits of using Astragalus membranaceus teas to treat hyperthyroidism include reduced symptoms, improved thyroid function, and enhanced overall well-being.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Astragalus membranaceus root, 1 cup of water, and a tea infuser.
  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried Astragalus membranaceus root and put it in the tea infuser.
  3. Heat the water in a pot until it reaches 212°F (100°C).
  4. Steep the Astragalus membranaceus root in the hot water for 5-7 minutes, then strain the tea.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea 2-3 times a day, as needed, under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Astragalus membranaceus teas can cause interactions with medications such as blood thinners, thyroid medications, and diabetes medications when used to treat hyperthyroidism, potentially leading to adverse effects such as irregular heartbeat, excessive bleeding, or blood sugar fluctuations.

Precautions should be taken when using Astragalus membranaceus teas for hyperthyroidism, including monitoring thyroid hormone levels, blood sugar levels, and blood pressure, and being cautious with its use in pregnant or breastfeeding women.

Recipe:
