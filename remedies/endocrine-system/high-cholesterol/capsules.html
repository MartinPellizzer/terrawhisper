  • Roots: The roots of Commiphora mukul are another commonly used part in capsules for high cholesterol, as they contain triterpenoid saponins that may help in lowering cholesterol levels and improving overall cardiovascular health.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic guggul for high cholesterol.

  1. Weigh 500 grams of dried commiphora mukul resin and grind it into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle.
  2. Mix 500 grams of powdered resin with 500 grams of excipients such as vegetable cellulose and silicon dioxide.
  3. Use a capsule machine to fill 500 capsules with the powder mixture at a dosage of 500 milligrams per capsule.
  4. Place the filled capsules in a desiccator to maintain a relative humidity of 30% for 24 hours.
  5. Store the commiphora mukul capsules in a cool dark place at a temperature below 25 degrees celsius for up to two years.
