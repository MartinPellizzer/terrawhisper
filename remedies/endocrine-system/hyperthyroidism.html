Hyperthyroidism: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Hyperthyroidism is a medical condition characterized by an overproduction of thyroid hormones, which can lead to an accelerated metabolic rate, weight loss, tremors, and an enlarged thyroid gland.

The causes of hyperthyroidism include Graves' disease, thyroid nodules, thyroiditis, and excessive iodine intake. If left untreated, hyperthyroidism can lead to negative impacts on health, such as brittle bones, cardiac problems, and reproductive issues. To alleviate symptoms, herbal remedies like ashwagandha, licorice root, and bladderwrack can be used in medicinal preparations, such as teas or supplements.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies, especially if taking medications, as some herbs may interact with them or exacerbate underlying conditions.

What are the main causes of hyperthyroidism?

The main causes of hyperthyroidism are usually an autoimmune response, where the body's immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys the thyroid gland, or an overproduction of thyroid hormones due to a benign tumor or hyperplasia of the gland.

The most common cause is Graves' disease, an autoimmune disorder in which the immune system produces antibodies that stimulate the thyroid gland to produce excessive amounts of thyroid hormones. Other causes include thyroiditis, where inflammation of the thyroid gland leads to the release of stored thyroid hormones into the bloodstream, and iodine-induced hyperthyroidism, which occurs when excessive iodine intake stimulates the thyroid gland to produce too many hormones. Additionally, rare causes include solitary toxic nodules, which are benign tumors that produce excessive amounts of thyroid hormones, and pituitary gland disorders, such as a pituitary tumor that stimulates the thyroid gland to produce excessive hormones.

These causes can lead to an overproduction of thyroid hormones, resulting in symptoms such as weight loss, palpitations, and tremors.

  • Autoimmune: Autoimmune thyroiditis, also known as Graves' disease, is the most common cause of hyperthyroidism, in which the immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys the thyroid gland's ability to regulate thyroid hormone production, leading to an overproduction of thyroid hormones.
  • Toxic Adenoma: Toxic adenoma, also known as solitary toxic nodule, is the second most common cause of hyperthyroidism, in which a single thyroid nodule becomes overactive and starts producing excessive amounts of thyroid hormones.
  • Thyroiditis: Thyroiditis, also known as subacute thyroiditis, is a third common cause of hyperthyroidism, in which inflammation of the thyroid gland causes the release of stored thyroid hormones into the bloodstream, leading to a temporary overproduction of thyroid hormones.
  • Exogenous: Exogenous thyroid hormone, which is thyroid hormone taken as medication, can cause hyperthyroidism if taken in excessive amounts, leading to an overproduction of thyroid hormones in the body.
  • Pituitary: Pituitary gland disorders, such as pituitary tumors, can cause hyperthyroidism by secreting excessive amounts of thyroid-stimulating hormone, which stimulates the thyroid gland to produce excessive amounts of thyroid hormones.
  • Iodine Excess: Excessive iodine intake, often due to iodine supplements or iodine-rich foods, can cause hyperthyroidism by overstimulating the thyroid gland and increasing thyroid hormone production.
  • Pheochromocytoma: Pheochromocytoma, a rare tumor of the adrenal gland, can cause hyperthyroidism due to the secretion of excessive amounts of epinephrine, which stimulates the thyroid gland to produce excessive amounts of thyroid hormones.
