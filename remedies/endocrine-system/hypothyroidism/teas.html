1. Zingiber officinale teas

Zingiber officinale teas, derived from the ginger root, have been traditionally used to treat hypothyroidism, a condition characterized by an underactive thyroid gland.

The bioactive constituents of Zingiber officinale teas, including gingerols and shogaols, possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to stimulate thyroid function and improve overall thyroid health.

These bioactive compounds, particularly 6-gingerol and 8-gingerol, have been shown to increase the production of thyroid hormones, thereby alleviating symptoms of hypothyroidism, such as fatigue and weight gain.

The use of Zingiber officinale teas to treat hypothyroidism has been associated with benefits, including improved thyroid function, reduced inflammation, and enhanced overall well-being.

Related Study

According to "Evidence-based complementary and alternative medicine : eCAM", Zingiber officinale teas for hypothyroidism may be beneficial in relieving persistent symptoms, including weight gain, cold intolerance, constipation, and dry skin, as well as having beneficial effects on weight reduction and regulation of blood sugar and lipid levels.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh ginger roots (about 2-3 inches) and 2 cups of water.
  2. Peel the ginger roots and slice them into thin pieces.
  3. Boil the 2 cups of water in a saucepan and add the sliced ginger.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, then strain the tea.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the ginger tea, 3-4 times a day, with honey or lemon to taste.

Zingiber officinale teas can be used to treat hypothyroidism, but its usage may lead to side effects such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea and diarrhea, due to its high ginger content.

Precautions include taking the herbal preparation in moderation, as excessive consumption can lead to heartburn, and also monitor for interactions with medications, especially blood thinners and thyroid medications.

2. Panax ginseng teas

Panax ginseng teas have been studied for their potential in treating hypothyroidism, a condition characterized by an underactive thyroid gland.

The bioactive constituents present in Panax ginseng, such as ginsenosides and saponins, have been found to have adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties that help to regulate thyroid function and alleviate symptoms of hypothyroidism.

The herbal preparation helps to treat this ailment by stimulating the thyroid gland, improving its function, and enhancing the production of thyroid hormones.

The benefits of using Panax ginseng teas to treat hypothyroidism include improved thyroid function, increased energy levels, and enhanced overall well-being, making it a promising complementary therapy for this condition.

Related Study

According to the study, Panax ginseng teas, specifically as part of the Yiqi Huayu Recipe, Liqi Xiaoying decoction, and Shugan Sanjie therapy, may be effective in reducing thyroid antibody levels, including TPOAb, in patients with hypothyroidism, specifically Hashimoto's thyroiditis (HT).

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 2-3 slices of dried Panax ginseng root.
  2. Add the ginseng slices to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup using a tea strainer or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey or sugar to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea 1-2 times a day, ideally in the morning or afternoon, as directed by your healthcare provider.

Panax ginseng teas can be used as a complementary treatment for hypothyroidism, but it may cause potential side effects such as insomnia, anxiety, and stomach upset due to its stimulatory properties and potential interaction with thyroid medications.

To minimize side effects, it is recommended to consume Panax ginseng teas in moderation, ideally in small amounts and for limited periods, as excessive consumption can exacerbate hypothyroidism symptoms and lead to adverse reactions.

Withania somnifera teas have gained attention for their potential in treating hypothyroidism due to their adaptogenic and immunomodulatory properties.
