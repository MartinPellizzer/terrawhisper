Valerian tinctures helps with hot flashes because it has a calming effect on the body, which can help to reduce feelings of heat and anxiety associated with menopause.

The herb has been traditionally used as a natural relaxant, and its compounds have been shown to interact with the brain's neurotransmitters to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian tinctures that aid with hot flashes.

  • Adaptogenic and stress-reducing: Valerian's adaptogenic properties help to calm the nervous system, reducing stress and anxiety that can exacerbate hot flashes.
  • Muscle relaxant: Valerian's muscle-relaxing properties can help alleviate physical symptoms of hot flashes such as sweating, palpitations, and body temperature changes by relaxing the muscles and blood vessels.
  • Hormone-regulating: Valerian contains compounds that may help regulate hormone production and balance, which can help reduce the frequency and severity of hot flashes associated with menopause.

The list below shows the primary parts of valerian used to make tinctures for hot flashes.

  • Roots: The roots of valerian are rich in compounds like valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, which have been shown to help reduce symptoms of hot flashes.
  • Rhizomes: Valerian rhizomes contain a higher concentration of essential oils, particularly valeric acid, which has a sedative effect that can help alleviate hot flash symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian for hot flashes.

  1. Harvest fresh valeriana officinalis roots in the morning after dew has dried, ensuring at least 2 feet of root material per pound of dried root.
  2. Wash the harvested roots thoroughly under running water to remove dirt and debris, then dry them immediately using a clean towel or drying rack.
  3. Chop the dried roots into small pieces, approximately 1/4 inch in size, and combine with 80 proof vodka or a neutral grain spirit at a ratio of 1 part root material to 2 parts liquid.
  4. Store the mixture in a clean glass jar with a tight-fitting lid, allowing it to steep for 6-8 weeks, shaking the jar daily to ensure optimal extraction, before straining and discarding the solids.
  5. Filter the resulting tincture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into dark glass bottles, labeling them clearly and storing them in a cool, dark place at room temperature, ready for use as needed.
