1. Yarrow tea

Yarrow tea helps with dry scalp because it has anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties that can soothe an itchy, dry scalp and reduce redness and irritation.

It also contains flavonoids, which are powerful antioxidants that can improve circulation and promote hair growth. Additionally, yarrow tea has been shown to regulate sebum production, which can help to balance the natural oils in the scalp and prevent dryness. Its anti-fungal and anti-bacterial properties can also help to eliminate any potential scalp infections that may be contributing to dryness.

Drinking yarrow tea or using it as a rinse can provide relief and help to maintain a healthy scalp.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in yarrow tea that aid with dry scalp.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Yarrow tea's anti-inflammatory properties, due to the presence of flavonoids, help reduce redness and irritation associated with dry scalp.
  • Astringent: The astringent properties of yarrow tea, caused by tannins, tighten skin and reduce flakiness, providing relief for dry scalp.
  • Antimicrobial: Yarrow tea's antimicrobial properties, from volatile oils like azulene, help maintain a healthy scalp by preventing the growth of harmful bacteria and fungi.
  • Antioxidant: The antioxidant properties of yarrow tea, due to phenolic acids, protect skin cells from damage, promoting overall scalp health and reducing dryness.
  • Circulation-boosting: Yarrow tea helps improve blood circulation, thanks to lactones, ensuring nutrients reach the scalp and promoting the growth of healthy hair follicles, alleviating dry scalp.

The list below shows the primary parts of yarrow tea used to make teas for dry scalp.

  • Leaves: Rich in vitamins and antioxidants, leaves help nourish and soothe dry, itchy scalp.
  • Flowers: Containing anti-inflammatory properties, flowers help reduce scalp irritation and flakiness.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential fatty acids, seeds promote scalp moisture retention and hair strength.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yarrow tea for dry scalp.

  1. In a pot, boil 2 cups of water and add 2 tablespoons of dried yarrow flowers, simmering for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat and let the mixture steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid to remove the yarrow flowers, leaving you with the tea.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Consume the herbal yarrow tea daily to help alleviate dry scalp.
