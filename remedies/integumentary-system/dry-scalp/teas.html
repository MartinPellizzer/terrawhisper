9 Best Herbal Teas For Dry Scalp

Herbal teas for dry scalp are a natural remedy that involves brewing and consuming teas made from various herbs to alleviate dry scalp symptoms.

These teas can treat dry scalp by soothing inflammation, reducing flaking, and promoting a healthy scalp environment due to their antifungal, antibacterial, and anti-inflammatory properties.

Some examples of herbal teas that can help treat dry scalp include peppermint tea, which helps to stimulate blood flow and reduce itching; chamomile tea, which soothes and calms the scalp; tea tree oil tea, which has antifungal properties to combat dandruff-causing yeast; licorice root tea, which reduces inflammation and promotes a healthy scalp; and rosemary tea, which helps to improve circulation and reduce flaking.

Additionally, other herbs like ginger, turmeric, and sage can also be used to make teas that aid in treating dry scalp by reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy scalp environment.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis teas

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, also known as rosemary tea, have been used to treat the dry scalp ailment due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The herbal preparation helps to treat this ailment by reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy scalp environment, which in turn reduces flaking and itchiness.

The bioactive constituents of this herbal preparation, including carnosic acid and rosmarinic acid, have been found to exhibit antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help to combat scalp infections and promote a balanced scalp microbiome.

The benefits of using Rosmarinus officinalis teas to treat the dry scalp ailment include reduced inflammation, improved scalp health, and a decrease in the severity of symptoms such as flaking and itchiness.

Featured Study

According to "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Rosmarinus officinalis teas for dry scalp may be beneficial as the essential oils from Rosmarinus officinalis have been found to be active against Malassezia species, which can cause common infections or exacerbate multiple skin disorders such as dandruff.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 2 tablespoons of fresh or dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves.
  2. Let the leaves steep in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a cup.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey or sugar to the tea, if desired, and mix well.
  5. Drink the tea once a day to help soothe and calm the scalp.

Usage:

  1. Wash hair with mild shampoo 2-3 times a week.
  2. Steep 1 tablespoon of Rosmarinus officinalis tea in 1 cup of boiling water.
  3. Strain the tea and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Use the cooled tea as a final rinse after shampooing, 2-3 times a week.
  5. Repeat the process for 4-6 weeks to see noticeable improvements.

Rosmarinus officinalis teas can be used as a potential natural remedy to treat dry scalp ailments, but it may also cause side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal issues in some individuals.

Precautions should be taken when using Rosmarinus officinalis teas to treat dry scalp, including monitoring for excessive consumption which may lead to over-stimulation and potential interactions with medications, as well as being cautious with individuals with sensitive skin or those who are pregnant or breastfeeding.

2. Lavandula angustifolia teas

Lavandula angustifolia teas, derived from the flowers of the lavender plant, are often used to treat the dry scalp ailment due to their soothing and antiseptic properties.

The herbal preparation helps to treat this ailment by reducing inflammation and promoting a healthy scalp environment, thus alleviating symptoms such as itchiness and flakiness.

The bioactive constituents of Lavandula angustifolia teas, including linalool and linalyl acetate, possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that contribute to their therapeutic effects.

The benefits of using Lavandula angustifolia teas to treat a dry scalp include reduced symptoms, improved scalp health, and a natural and non-invasive treatment approach.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water, 1 tablespoon of dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers, and a tea infuser.
  2. Put the tea infuser in a cup and add the dried Lavandula angustifolia flowers.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the flowers in the tea infuser.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, then remove the tea infuser.
  5. Strain the tea and use it as a final rinse after shampooing to help soothe a dry scalp.

Usage:

  1. Wash your scalp with a gentle shampoo.
  2. Mix 1-2 tablespoons of Lavandula angustifolia tea with 1 cup of water.
  3. Soak a clean cloth in the tea mixture and apply it to your scalp.
  4. Leave the tea on for 30 minutes to an hour, then rinse with water.
  5. Repeat once or twice a week, as needed, for best results.

Lavandula angustifolia teas can be a soothing and effective remedy for a dry scalp ailment, but its use may cause side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, or interaction with medications that increase the risk of bleeding.

To minimize potential side effects, it is recommended to start with a low dose, patch test the tea on a small area of skin, and avoid using it in conjunction with other products that contain salicylates or essential oils, as this may enhance their potency and increase the risk of adverse reactions.

3. Aloe barbadensis teas

Aloe barbadensis teas have been traditionally used to treat the dry scalp ailment due to their soothing, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties.

This herbal preparation helps to treat the ailment by reducing inflammation, calming irritation, and promoting a healthy scalp environment.

The bioactive constituents of Aloe barbadensis teas, including aloin, aloe-emodin, and acemannan, work together to reduce itching, scaling, and flaking of the scalp, promoting a balanced scalp pH and hydration.

Regular consumption of Aloe barbadensis teas has been found to provide numerous benefits, including reducing dandruff, promoting hair growth, and improving overall scalp health.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of water, 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel from Aloe barbadensis, and a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Boil the water and let it cool for 1 minute.
  3. Add the aloe vera gel to the tea infuser or strainer and place it in the cooled water.
  4. Steep for 5-7 minutes, then remove the infuser or strainer.
  5. Strain the liquid into a cup and drink immediately as a tea, or use as a scalp treatment by massaging it into your scalp and leaving it on for 30 minutes before rinsing.

Usage:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo as usual.
  2. Apply 1-2 tablespoons of Aloe barbadensis tea to your scalp.
  3. Massage the tea gently into your scalp for 5 minutes.
  4. Leave the tea on for 30 minutes to an hour before washing.
  5. Use this treatment 2-3 times a week for best results.

Aloe barbadensis teas can be used to treat the dry scalp ailment, but possible side effects include skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive issues such as diarrhea or stomach upset in some individuals.

To use Aloe barbadensis teas safely, it is recommended to start with a small dose and gradually increase the amount to minimize the risk of skin irritation, and to avoid consuming it if you have any open wounds or sensitive areas on the scalp.

4. Urtica dioica teas

Urtica dioica teas, also known as nettle leaf tea, have been traditionally used to treat dry scalp ailments due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

This herbal preparation helps to treat dry scalp by reducing itching and flaking, and promoting a healthy scalp environment.

  4. Discard the solids and let the tea cool to room temperature.
  5. Use the cooled tea as a final rinse after shampooing, focusing on the scalp to help soothe and calm dryness.

Usage:

  1. Drink 1 cup of Urtica dioica tea 2-3 times a day
  2. Use 2 tablespoons of dried Urtica dioica leaves for each cup
  3. Steep in hot water for 5-7 minutes, then strain
  4. Continue for 2-4 weeks for noticeable improvement
  5. Monitor scalp condition and adjust usage as needed

Urtica dioica teas can be used to treat dry scalp ailments, but it may cause side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased sensitivity to the sun, especially if not used in moderation.

To minimize these risks, it's essential to start with a small dose, gradually increase the amount, and avoid applying the tea directly to the scalp, instead using it as a rinse or adding it to a shampoo.
