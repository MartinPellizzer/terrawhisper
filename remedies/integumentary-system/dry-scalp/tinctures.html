8 Herbal Tinctures For Dry Scalp

1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and linalool, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to reduce inflammation and combat fungal and bacterial infections that can cause dry scalp. The terpenoids present in the tincture, such as pinene and bornyl acetate, have antiseptic and antifungal properties, which help to soothe and calm the scalp. To use Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures for dry scalp, mix a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and gently massage it into the scalp.

Leave it on for 30 minutes to an hour before shampooing your hair as usual. You can also add a few drops of the tincture to your shampoo or conditioner for an extra boost. When you start using Melaleuca alternifolia tinctures, you may notice a reduction in flakiness and itchiness on your scalp within a few days. The tincture's antifungal properties may help to control fungal infections like dandruff and psoriasis, while its anti-inflammatory properties may reduce redness and swelling.

As you continue to use the tincture, you may notice an improvement in the overall health and appearance of your scalp.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dry scalp?

Many herbal teas, such as peppermint and ginger, have natural oils that can help moisturize and soothe a dry scalp, making it a great way to start your day.
