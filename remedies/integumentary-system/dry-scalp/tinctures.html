  • Aloe-vera-polysaccharides: These complex sugars have been shown to stimulate the production of ceramides, an essential component of the skin's barrier function, which can help lock in moisture and reduce dryness and flakiness on the scalp.

  • Leaves: Aloe vera leaf extract has been shown to have anti-fungal and antibacterial properties, helping to control scalp infections and promote a healthy scalp environment.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic aloe for dry scalp.

  1. Harvest mature aloe vera leaves from your garden or purchase from a local supplier weighing approximately 500g.
  2. Wash the leaves thoroughly with lukewarm water to remove any dirt or debris and pat dry with paper towels.
  3. Chop the leaves into small pieces and macerate them in a 1:2 ratio with 95% ethanol for 2 weeks.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle to separate the liquid from solids.
  5. Store the aloe vera tincture in a cool dark place for at least 2 weeks to mature and age properly.
