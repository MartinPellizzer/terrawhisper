1. Aloe vera

Aloe tinctures helps with dry scalp because of its soothing and moisturizing properties.

The aloe vera gel extracted from the plant contains vitamins, minerals, and amino acids that deeply nourish the scalp, calming irritation and flakiness. As it penetrates the skin, it hydrates the scalp, reducing dryness and itchiness, while also promoting healthy hair growth.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in aloe tinctures that aid with dry scalp.

  • Anti-inflammatory properties: Aloe vera's anti-inflammatory compounds, such as aloin and aloe-emodin, help to reduce inflammation in the scalp, which can contribute to dryness, itchiness, and flaking.
  • Antioxidant properties: The antioxidants present in aloe tinctures, like vitamin C and E, help to protect the scalp from damage caused by free radicals, promoting healthy skin cell growth and reducing oxidative stress that can lead to dry scalp issues.
  • Moisturizing properties: Aloe's natural moisturizers, including glycoproteins and polysaccharides, help to lock in moisture and soothe the scalp, alleviating dryness, itchiness, and irritation while promoting healthy skin hydration.

The list below shows the primary parts of aloe used to make tinctures for dry scalp.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, aloe vera leaves help soothe and calm dry scalp irritations.
  • Roots: Containing bioactive compounds like saponins and flavonoids, aloe vera roots aid in reducing inflammation and promoting healthy scalp tissue.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic aloe for dry scalp.

  1. Harvest fresh aloe vera leaves from healthy plants with no signs of disease or pests.
  2. Clean the harvested leaves thoroughly under running water to remove dirt and debris.
  3. Chop the clean leaves into small pieces and soak them in 80 proof vodka for 2 weeks.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass jar, discarding solids and reserving liquid tincture.
  5. Label the finished tincture with the date of preparation and store it in a cool dark place for up to 6 months before use.
