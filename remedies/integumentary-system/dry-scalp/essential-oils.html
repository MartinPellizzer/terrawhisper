1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Tea tree essential oils helps with dry scalp because of its natural antifungal and antibacterial properties.

When applied topically, it can help to reduce irritation and inflammation caused by fungal infections, such as dandruff and psoriasis. The oil's antimicrobial agents also work to soothe and calm the scalp, promoting a healthy environment for hair growth.

Additionally, tea tree essential oils can help to balance the natural pH of the scalp, reducing dryness and itchiness, leading to a healthier and more balanced scalp overall.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in tea tree essential oils that aid with dry scalp.

  • Antifungal properties: Tea tree oil's antifungal properties can help combat fungal infections such as malassezia, which can contribute to a dry and flaky scalp.
  • Antibacterial properties: Tea tree oil's antibacterial properties can help reduce the growth of bacteria on the scalp, reducing inflammation and irritation that can lead to a dry and itchy scalp.
  • Anti-inflammatory properties: Tea tree oil's anti-inflammatory properties can help soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing redness and swelling associated with a dry scalp.

The list below shows the primary parts of tea tree used to make essential oils for dry scalp.

  • Leaves: Rich in cineole, an active compound that helps to soothe and calm dry scalp irritations.
  • Buds: Contains alpha-terpinen-7-ol, a natural antifungal agent that combats dry scalp infections.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic tea tree for dry scalp.

  1. Harvest fresh leaves of melaleuca alternifolia plant, selecting only healthy and aromatic ones for processing.
  2. Dry the harvested leaves using air circulation method, taking approximately 7-10 days to achieve optimal dryness.
  3. Steam-distill dried melaleuca alternifolia leaves using a copper still, collecting essential oil in a separate container.
  4. Monitor temperature and pressure during distillation process, aiming for optimal conditions between 100°c and 150°c.
  5. Filter and store the resulting essential oil in dark glass bottles with tight-fitting lids, ensuring optimal shelf life of 12-18 months.

How to best treat dry scalp with herbal essential oils?

The best way to treat dry scalp with herbal essential oils is by combining a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil with potent herbs like tea tree, lavender, and rosemary.

Mix 2-3 drops of each essential oil with 1 tablespoon of carrier oil and gently massage onto the scalp. Leave on for 30 minutes before shampooing. Repeat once or twice a week to moisturize and soothe the scalp.

This treatment reduces flaking, itchiness, and inflammation while promoting healthy hair growth.
