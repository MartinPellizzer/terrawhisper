1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia teas contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and terpinen-4-ol, which are known for their antifungal properties.

These compounds can help combat fungal infections, such as nail fungus, by preventing the growth of fungal cells and promoting a healthy environment for fungal-free growth. To use Melaleuca alternifolia teas for nail fungus, steep a teaspoon of dried leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and let it cool. Soak your affected nail in the tea for 15-20 minutes, twice a day, for best results.

You can also apply a few drops of tea directly to the affected nail, and gently massage it in. As you continue this treatment, you may notice a decrease in nail thickness, a reduction in fungal growth, and an improvement in nail color. The antifungal properties in the tea can also help to prevent the spread of the infection to surrounding nails.

However, keep in mind that nail fungus can be stubborn, and it may take several weeks or even months of consistent treatment to see significant improvement.
