Aloe essential oils helps with burns because of its unique properties that promote rapid healing, reduce inflammation, and soothe pain.

The oil's anti-inflammatory compounds quickly alleviate redness and swelling, while its moisturizing properties help to hydrate the affected area, promoting faster recovery. Additionally, aloe's antiseptic properties prevent bacterial growth, reducing the risk of infection.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in aloe essential oils that aid with burns.

The list below shows the primary parts of aloe essential oil used to make essential oils for burns.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic aloe essential oil for burns.

  1. Harvest fresh aloe vera leaves and wash them thoroughly with distilled water.
  2. Cut the leaves into small pieces and extract the gel using a cheesecloth or fine-mesh strainer.
  3. Combine 250ml of extracted aloe vera gel with 20 drops of lavender essential oil and stir well for 10 minutes.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a dark glass bottle and store it in the refrigerator at 40°f for 72 hours to allow for infusion and settling.
  5. After 72 hours, strain the mixture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean dark glass bottle and discard the solids.
