7 Best Herbal Teas For Burns

Herbal teas for burns are a type of traditional remedy that involves steeping herbs in hot water to create a soothing, topical treatment for burn wounds.

These teas can help treat burns because of their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can reduce pain, prevent infection, and promote healing.

Some examples of herbal teas used to treat burns include aloe vera tea, which helps to cool and moisturize the skin; chamomile tea, which has anti-inflammatory properties to reduce swelling and promote relaxation; calendula tea, which has antimicrobial properties to prevent infection; lavender tea, which promotes wound healing and reduces scarring; and plantain tea, which helps to reduce pain and inflammation.

Additionally, other herbal teas such as peppermint, tea tree, and eucalyptus can also be used to treat burns due to their cooling, antibacterial, and anti-inflammatory properties.

1. Calendula officinalis teas

Calendula officinalis teas have been traditionally used to treat burns due to their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

The bioactive constituents of Calendula officinalis, such as triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids, help to soothe and protect the skin from further damage, promoting faster healing and reducing the risk of infection.

By applying Calendula officinalis teas topically, the tea's antiseptic properties help to prevent bacterial and fungal growth, while its anti-inflammatory properties reduce redness and swelling, facilitating the healing process.

The benefits of using Calendula officinalis teas to treat burns include reduced pain, faster wound closure, and a lower risk of scarring.

Featured Study

According to "Life (Basel, Switzerland)", Calendula officinalis teas for burns can be beneficial in wound treatment and infection prevention due to its considerable pharmacological properties that facilitate the treatment of wounds.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of dried flowers and place in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the dried flowers and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a separate cup and discard the flowers.
  5. Let the tea cool down before applying it to the burned area with a clean cloth.

Usage:

  1. Wash the burn with cool water to stop the burning process.
  2. Steep 2-3 teaspoons of Calendula officinalis tea in 1 cup of boiling water.
  3. Allow the tea to cool, then apply it to the affected area.
  4. Use 2-3 times a day, as needed, for up to 7 days.
  5. Cover the area with a non-stick dressing or bandage to protect it.

Calendula officinalis teas can be beneficial in treating burns due to their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, but possible side effects include skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive issues such as diarrhea or stomach upset.

To minimize potential side effects, users should start with small amounts, monitor their skin's reaction, and avoid using Calendula officinalis teas on open wounds or broken skin.

2. Hypericum perforatum teas

Hypericum perforatum teas, also known as St. John's Wort teas, have been traditionally used to treat burns and accelerate the healing process.

The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of this herbal preparation help to soothe and protect the affected area, reducing the risk of infection and promoting tissue repair.

The bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, work synergistically to reduce pain, inflammation, and oxidative stress associated with burns.

Regular consumption of Hypericum perforatum teas has been shown to promote faster wound healing, reduce scarring, and alleviate discomfort, making it a valuable natural remedy for treating burns.

Featured Study

According to the study, Hypericum perforatum teas for burns are supported by 5 laboratory studies and 3 clinical trials, confirming their potential benefits in wound healing.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Hypericum perforatum leaves.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of the leaves and place them in a cup.
  3. Add 8 ounces of boiling water to the cup.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-10 minutes, then strain it.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe burns.

Usage:

  1. Wash the burn area with cool water for 10 minutes.
  2. Prepare a cup of Hypericum perforatum tea by following the preparation instructions.
  3. Take 2-3 tablespoons of the tea 3-4 times a day.
  4. Soak a clean cloth in the tea and apply it to the burn area.
  5. Replace the cloth every 2 hours or as the tea cools down.

Hypericum perforatum teas can be used to treat burns, but possible side effects may include allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal issues such as nausea and diarrhea.

Precautions should be taken when using Hypericum perforatum teas for burns, including avoiding prolonged use, monitoring for signs of overdose, and keeping the affected area clean to prevent infection.

3. Echinacea purpurea teas

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to treat burns, leveraging their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties to soothe and heal damaged skin.

The bioactive constituents, including alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, work together to reduce inflammation, promote tissue repair, and protect against oxidative stress.

By reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair, echinacea purpurea teas help to alleviate pain, promote wound healing, and prevent infection in burn wounds.

The benefits of using echinacea purpurea teas to treat burns include reduced recovery time, improved wound healing, and minimized risk of complications, making it a popular natural remedy for this condition.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea flowers.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of the flowers and place them in a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Boil 1 cup of water and pour it over the flowers in the cup.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain it using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  5. Drink the tea warm or at room temperature, 2-3 times a day, for relief from burns.

Usage:

  1. Soak a clean cloth in cooled Echinacea purpurea tea.
  2. Gently apply the tea-soaked cloth to the burn area.
  3. Leave the cloth on for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  4. Repeat the process 2-3 times a day for 3-5 days.
  5. Use 1 cup of cooled Echinacea purpurea tea per application.

Echinacea purpurea teas can be used to treat burns, but some possible side effects include allergic reactions, increased heart rate, and interactions with certain medications.

Precautions to take include using it in moderation, avoiding it if you have any allergies or sensitivities, and being cautious when combining it with other herbal remedies or medications to treat the burns ailment.

4. Aloe vera teas

Aloe vera teas have been traditionally used to treat burns and offer relief from pain and discomfort.

This herbal preparation helps to treat burns by reducing inflammation, promoting wound healing, and soothing the skin with its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Aloe vera teas contain bioactive constituents such as aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E, which help to reduce oxidative stress, promote collagen synthesis, and enhance tissue repair.

The benefits of using aloe vera teas to treat burns include reduced risk of infection, accelerated healing time, and minimized scarring, making it a popular natural remedy for burn victims.

Featured Study

According to "Brazilian journal of biology = Revista brasleira de biologia", Aloe vera teas for burns have been found to accelerate the healing of wounds by promoting proliferation and cell migration of human skin fibroblasts and keratinocytes, and have also been shown to have antioxidant effects and protect against oxidative stress.

Recipe:

  1. Cut 1 aloe vera leaf from a healthy plant. Remove the outer green skin to expose the gel inside.
  2. Scoop 2 tablespoons of aloe vera gel into a blender or food processor.
  3. Add 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to the blender.
  4. Blend the mixture on low speed for 10-15 seconds until smooth. Strain the liquid through a fine-mesh sieve.
  5. Pour the aloe vera tea into a cup and drink immediately. You can store any leftover tea in the fridge for up to 24 hours.

Usage:

  1. Cool burn with cold water for 10 minutes
  2. Mix 2 tablespoons of Aloe vera tea with 1 cup of water
  3. Soak a clean cloth in the tea mixture for 5 minutes
  4. Gently apply the cloth to the burn for 15-20 minutes, 3 times a day
  5. Replace the cloth with a new one every 24 hours, for up to 3 days

Aloe vera teas can be an effective remedy for treating burns, but it may cause side effects such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation in some individuals.

To use Aloe vera teas safely, individuals should be cautious when consuming it, especially if they have sensitive stomachs, and monitor their body's response to the tea, reducing the frequency or amount if any adverse reactions occur.

5. Arnica montana teas

Arnica montana teas have been traditionally used to treat burns, thanks to their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which help to soothe and protect the affected skin.

The bioactive constituents present in Arnica montana, including flavonoids, sesquiterpenes, and phenolic acids, contribute to its therapeutic effects by reducing inflammation, promoting wound healing, and preventing infection.

The benefits of using Arnica montana teas to treat burns include reduced risk of infection, faster recovery time, and improved skin texture and appearance.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Arnica montana flowers and 2 cups of boiling water.
  2. Pour the boiling water over the Arnica flowers in a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes, then strain the liquid.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the tea, if desired, for a soothing flavor.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe burns and promote healing.

Usage:

  1. Soak a clean cloth in cooled Arnica montana tea.
  2. Gently apply the cloth to the burned area for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Repeat application 2-3 times a day for 3-5 days.
  4. Drink 1/4 cup of cooled Arnica montana tea, 2-3 times a day.
  5. Continue for 5-7 days or until the burn heals completely.

Arnica montana teas can be used to treat burns, but possible side effects may include skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased sensitivity to sunlight, which can worsen the burn or cause new skin damage.

To minimize risks, individuals should avoid applying Arnica montana teas directly to open wounds, use a diluted solution, and monitor their skin for any adverse reactions, as well as avoid using it on children and pregnant women.

6. Symphytum officinale teas

Symphytum officinale teas have been traditionally used to treat burns and promote wound healing due to their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

The mucilages present in these teas help to soothe the skin, reduce pain and inflammation, and protect the wound from further infection.

Bioactive constituents such as allantoin, mucilage, and saponins in Symphytum officinale teas promote tissue repair, reduce scarring, and promote the growth of new skin cells, thereby aiding in the healing process.

The benefits of using Symphytum officinale teas to treat burns include reduced risk of infection, faster wound healing, and minimal scarring, making it a valuable herbal remedy for burn treatment.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Symphytum officinale leaves.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the dried leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the liquid and let it cool before applying it to the burned area.

Usage:

  1. Soak a clean cloth in 1 cup of cooled Symphytum officinale tea.
  2. Gently place the tea-soaked cloth on the burn area for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Repeat the process every 2-3 hours as needed, for 3-5 days.
  4. Use 2-3 tablespoons of dried Symphytum officinale herb per 1 cup of boiling water.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day, for 3-5 days, to aid healing.

Symphytum officinale teas can be used to treat burns, but possible side effects include stomach upset, diarrhea, and allergic reactions, which may be severe in some individuals.

Precautions include using Symphytum officinale teas in moderation and avoiding their use on open wounds or skin conditions, as well as keeping them out of reach of children to prevent accidental ingestion.
