Sage decoctions helps with gum pain because of its natural anti-inflammatory properties, which reduce swelling and ease discomfort in the gums.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic sage for gum pain.

  1. Gather 10-15 grams of dried salvia officinalis leaves and flowers for the decoction.
  2. Combine the herb mixture with 250 milliliters of boiling water in a heat-resistant glass or ceramic container.
  3. Simmer the mixture for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to infuse and the liquid to reduce slightly.
  4. Strain the decoction through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass bottle, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the prepared decoction in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours or freeze it for later use.
