The list below shows the primary active constituents in sage tea that aid with gum pain.

The list below shows the primary parts of sage tea used to make teas for gum pain.

  • Leaves: Rich in anti-inflammatory compounds, sage leaves help reduce gum inflammation and soothe pain.
  • Buds: Packed with antimicrobial properties, sage buds help control oral bacteria, promoting gum health.

  • Roots: Contain tannins that have astringent properties, aiding in tissue repair and reducing gum pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic sage tea for gum pain.

  1. Boil 2 cups of water for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of dried sage leaves to the boiling water.
  3. Let the sage leaves steep in the hot water for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid to remove the sage leaves.
  5. Sip the sage tea slowly, allowing it to reach the gums.

How to best treat gum pain with herbal teas?

The best way to treat gum pain with herbal teas is by using chamomile or clove tea.

Chamomile has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce gum inflammation and pain. Steep a chamomile tea bag in hot water for 5-10 minutes, then let it cool down before applying the tea bag directly to the affected area.

Clove tea contains eugenol, a natural anesthetic that helps alleviate gum pain. To use clove tea, steep cloves in boiling water for 10-15 minutes, then strain and rinse your mouth with the tea. Avoid swallowing the tea, as it can cause stomach upset.

Remember to consult with a dental professional if your gum pain persists.
