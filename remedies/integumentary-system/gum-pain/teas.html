1. Melissa officinalis

Melissa officinalis teas contains a unique blend of compounds that can help alleviate gum pain.

The tea is rich in flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. These properties can help reduce swelling and fight off infections that may be causing the gum pain. The tea also contains rosmarinic acid, which has a calming effect on the body and can help soothe irritated gums. To use Melissa officinalis tea for gum pain, start by making a strong brew of the tea by steeping one teaspoon of dried leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and let it cool before rinsing your mouth with it. You can do this 2-3 times a day as needed. It's also essential to practice good oral hygiene, including brushing your teeth regularly and flossing to remove any food particles that may be causing irritation. The likely outcomes of using Melissa officinalis tea for gum pain include reduced inflammation, less pain and discomfort, and a decrease in bacterial growth. You may also notice an improvement in your overall oral health and a reduction in bad breath.

However, if your gum pain persists or worsens, it's essential to consult with a dentist or healthcare professional to rule out any underlying conditions that may require medical attention.
