1. Mentha x piperita

Peppermint essential oils helps with gum pain because it has natural analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties that can effectively soothe and calm the affected area.

When applied topically to the gums, peppermint oil's menthol content numbs the pain, reducing swelling and discomfort caused by gum inflammation or irritation.

Additionally, peppermint oil's cooling sensation helps to desensitize the gums, making it an effective natural remedy for relieving gum pain and promoting overall oral health.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint essential oils that aid with gum pain.

  • Antiseptic properties: Peppermint essential oil's antiseptic properties help to reduce inflammation and prevent infection in the gums, which can exacerbate gum pain.
  • Anesthetic properties: The anesthetic properties of peppermint oil numb the affected area, reducing sensitivity and discomfort associated with gum pain.
  • Anti-inflammatory properties: Peppermint oil's anti-inflammatory compounds help to reduce swelling and ease pain in the gums by blocking the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that promote inflammation.

The list below shows the primary parts of peppermint essential oil used to make essential oils for gum pain.

  • Roots: Containing menthol and other bioactive compounds, peppermint roots are effective in reducing inflammation and pain associated with gum disease.
  • Buds: The young buds of the peppermint plant contain concentrated amounts of menthone, which helps to numb and soothe gum pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint essential oil for gum pain.

  1. Harvest fresh peppermint leaves from a healthy plant with no signs of pests or diseases for optimal quality.
  2. Dry the harvested peppermint leaves using a food dehydrator on the lowest setting for 24 hours to prevent spoilage.
  3. Combine 50 grams of dried peppermint leaves with 500 milliliters of carrier oil such as coconut or olive oil in a clean glass jar.
  4. Allow the mixture to steep at room temperature for 72 hours to allow for maximum extraction of beneficial compounds.
  5. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth and transfer the resulting essential oil to dark glass bottles for storage and use as needed.
