Bleeding Spots In Mouth: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Bleeding spots in the mouth, also known as petechiae, are small, pinpoint hemorrhages that appear as red or purple spots on the mucous membranes, often in the cheeks, gums, or tongue.

Caused by minor injuries, infections, or underlying health conditions such as vitamin deficiencies, bleeding disorders, or autoimmune diseases, these spots can be a sign of more severe health issues, including gastrointestinal problems or blood clotting disorders. To alleviate bleeding spots in the mouth, herbal remedies like aloe vera gel, calendula oil, and myrrh can be used to soothe and protect the mucous membranes.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as some may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of bleeding spots in mouth, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of bleeding spots in mouth?

The main causes of bleeding spots in mouth are primarily related to trauma, with minor injuries or mouth trauma causing 50-60% of cases, while 10-15% are associated with infections, such as gingivitis or periodontitis, leading to inflammation and bleeding.

Other causes include poor oral hygiene, resulting in inflammation and bleeding gums. Nutritional deficiencies, specifically vitamin C deficiency (scurvy), can also cause bleeding spots in the mouth. In addition, certain medical conditions, including bleeding disorders, such as hemophilia, and autoimmune disorders, such as systemic lupus erythematosus, can cause bleeding spots.

Furthermore, smoking and tobacco use can also contribute to bleeding spots in the mouth due to gum recession and inflammation.

  • Gingivitis: Gingivitis is the inflammation of the gums, usually caused by poor oral hygiene, which leads to bleeding spots in the mouth due to the irritation and inflammation of the gum tissue.
  • Oral Ulcers: Oral ulcers, such as canker sores or cold sores, are a common cause of bleeding spots in the mouth due to the lesions and ulcers that form on the mucous membranes, causing pain and bleeding.
  • Mucositis: Mucositis is an inflammation of the mucous membranes in the mouth, often caused by chemotherapy, radiation therapy, or infection, which leads to bleeding spots in the mouth due to the damage and inflammation of the mucous membranes.
  • Periodontitis: Periodontitis is a bacterial infection of the gums and bone that support the teeth, which can lead to bleeding spots in the mouth due to the destruction of the gum tissue and bone.
  • Leukemia: Leukemia, a type of blood cancer, can cause bleeding spots in the mouth due to the abnormal production of blood cells and the resulting anemia, thrombocytopenia, or platelet dysfunction.
  • Thrombocytopenia: Thrombocytopenia, a condition characterized by low platelet count, can cause bleeding spots in the mouth due to the inadequate formation of blood clots and the resulting bleeding.
  • Hematological Disorders: Hematological disorders, such as hemophilia or von Willebrand disease, can cause bleeding spots in the mouth due to the abnormal production of clotting factors and the resulting bleeding.
  • Vitamin Deficiency: Vitamin deficiency, particularly a deficiency in vitamin C or vitamin K, can cause bleeding spots in the mouth due to the inadequate production of collagen and clotting factors.
  • Oral Infection: Oral infection, such as a bacterial or fungal infection, can cause bleeding spots in the mouth due to the inflammation and damage caused by the infection.
  • Mouth Trauma: Mouth trauma, such as a fall or a blow to the mouth, can cause bleeding spots in the mouth due to the injury and damage to the mucous membranes and underlying tissues.
