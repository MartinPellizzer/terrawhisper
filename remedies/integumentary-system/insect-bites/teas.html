Aloe teas helps with insect bites because of its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The gel from the aloe plant contains natural compounds that reduce swelling, redness, and itching caused by insect stings or bites. Drinking aloe tea can also help to calm down the allergic response and ease the discomfort associated with bites and stings.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in aloe teas that aid with insect bites.

  • Anti-inflammatory properties: Aloe vera's anti-inflammatory compounds, such as aloin and aloe-emodin, help reduce swelling, redness, and pain caused by insect bites.
  • Antibacterial properties: The antimicrobial agents in aloe vera, including salicylic acid and cinnamic acid, combat bacterial infections that can occur due to insect bites, preventing the development of abscesses or other complications.
  • Anti-pruritic properties: Aloe vera's anti-itch compounds, such as lupeol and beta-carotene, help alleviate itching, scratching, and discomfort associated with insect bites, promoting a sense of calm and relief.

The list below shows the primary parts of aloe tea used to make teas for insect bites.

  • Roots: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, aloe roots can help soothe and calm irritated skin caused by insect bites.
  • Leaves: Aloe leaves contain mucilages that help reduce inflammation, itching, and swelling associated with insect bites, making them an effective ingredient for medicinal teas.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic aloe tea for insect bites.

  1. Harvest fresh aloe vera leaves from your garden or purchase them from a local health food store measuring about six inches long and one inch thick.
  2. Wash the harvested aloe leaves thoroughly with cold running water to remove any dirt or impurities allowing excess moisture to drip off.
  3. Cut off the top third of each leaf to expose the clear gel inside then scoop out the gel using a spoon and transfer it to a blender or food processor.
  4. Add one cup of boiling water to the blended aloe gel in a large mug let it steep for 10 to 15 minutes before straining and discarding the solids.
  5. Strain the tea into a separate cup and add one teaspoon of honey and a pinch of salt stir well and drink immediately.
