Cracked Heels: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Cracked heels, also known as heel fissures, are deep cracks in the skin on the heels that can cause pain, discomfort, and bleeding.

They are often caused by dry skin, excessive foot sweating, or wearing ill-fitting shoes. If left untreated, cracked heels can lead to infections, nerve damage, and even diabetes complications. To heal cracked heels, herbal remedies such as aloe vera, tea tree oil, and vitamin E oil can be applied topically.

However, it's essential to note that these remedies should be used with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional to avoid any adverse reactions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of cracked heels, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of cracked heels?

The main causes of cracked heels are due to a combination of factors, including excessive dryness of the skin, often caused by over-washing, harsh soaps, or exposure to cold weather (1).

Poor footwear, such as tight shoes or high heels, can also contribute to the development of cracked heels by causing friction and pressure on the heels (2). Other contributing factors include obesity, age-related skin changes, and medical conditions such as eczema, diabetes, and thyroid disorders (3). Furthermore, a diet lacking essential nutrients like vitamin C, vitamin E, and omega-3 fatty acids can also lead to dry, cracked heels (4).

Additionally, genetic predisposition and certain medications, such as diuretics and beta-blockers, may also increase the risk of developing cracked heels (5).

  • Dry Skin: Dry skin is the most common cause of cracked heels due to a lack of moisture and natural oils in the skin, often caused by harsh soaps, hot showers, and cold weather.
  • Poor Hygiene: Poor hygiene is a common cause of cracked heels due to inadequate foot washing and drying, allowing bacteria and fungi to thrive and cause skin damage.
  • Footwear: Wearing shoes that are too tight or don't fit properly can cause friction and pressure on the heels, leading to cracks and fissures.
  • Nutritional Deficiencies: Nutritional deficiencies, such as a lack of vitamins A, C, and E, can cause skin problems, including cracked heels, due to the essential role these nutrients play in maintaining skin health.
  • Medical Conditions: Certain medical conditions, such as eczema, psoriasis, and diabetes, can cause cracked heels due to underlying skin issues or nerve damage.
  • Age: Cracked heels can occur with age due to a decrease in skin elasticity and the natural oils that help to moisturize and protect the skin.
  • Genetics: Genetics can play a role in the development of cracked heels, as some people may be more prone to dry skin and skin problems due to their genetic makeup.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes during pregnancy, menopause, or puberty can cause skin changes, including dryness and cracked heels, due to the fluctuations in hormone levels.
  • Environmental Factors: Exposure to environmental factors, such as cold temperatures, wind, and dry air, can cause dry skin and cracked heels due to the stripping of natural oils from the skin.
