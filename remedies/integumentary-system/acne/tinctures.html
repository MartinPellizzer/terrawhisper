  • Wound healing: Yarrow tincture promotes tissue repair and reduces redness, which is beneficial for treating acne-related skin damage. The wound-healing properties are attributed to flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and alkaloids found in yarrow.

  • Seeds: Yarrow seeds are a great source of essential fatty acids, which can help regulate skin oil production and reduce acne breakouts.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yarrow tincture for acne.

  1. Harvest 2 ounces of fresh yarrow flowers and leaves, ensuring they are fully grown and free from pesticides.
  2. Chop the yarrow into small pieces and place them in a clean, dry glass jar.
  3. Pour 10 ounces of 80-proof vodka or rum over the yarrow, ensuring the plant material is fully submerged.
  4. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking it gently every few days.
  5. Strain the mixture using cheesecloth or a fine-mesh strainer, squeezing out any excess liquid, and store the tincture in a dark glass bottle.
