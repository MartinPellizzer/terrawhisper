Germander tinctures helps with acne because of its potent antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties.

The tannins present in germander help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, which are often underlying causes of acne. The list below shows the primary active constituents in germander tinctures that aid with acne.

  • Antioxidant properties: Germander tincture's antioxidant flavonoids and phenolic acids may help neutralize free radicals that cause oxidative stress, which can lead to skin damage and inflammation, ultimately helping to prevent or alleviate acne.

The list below shows the primary parts of germander used to make tinctures for acne.

  • Roots: Rich in antibacterial and anti-inflammatory compounds, the roots of germander plant have been traditionally used to treat skin conditions like acne due to their ability to reduce inflammation and combat bacterial infections.
  • Leaves: With a high concentration of flavonoids and phenolic acids, the leaves of germander plant exhibit potent antioxidant properties that help soothe and calm inflamed skin, reducing redness and irritation associated with acne.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic germander for acne.

  1. Harvest 100 grams of fresh or dried teucrium chamaedrys leaves and flowers in the early morning for maximum potency.
  2. Cleanse the herb by gently washing it with cold water and then drying it with a clean towel to remove any debris.
  3. Combine the cleaned herb with 500 milliliters of high-proof vodka in a clean glass jar, ensuring all plant material is completely submerged.
  4. Allow the mixture to macerate for six weeks, shaking the jar every other day to facilitate extraction, before straining and discarding solids.
