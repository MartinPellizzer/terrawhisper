1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Tea tree essential oils helps with acne because of its powerful antimicrobial properties, which target the bacteria that cause breakouts.

When applied topically to the skin, tea tree oil penetrates deep into pores, killing Propionibacterium acnes (P. acnes) bacteria that can clog pores and lead to inflammation. This natural antibacterial agent also reduces redness and swelling associated with acne, promoting a clearer complexion.

Additionally, its antifungal properties help control the growth of yeast on the skin, further contributing to its acne-fighting benefits.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in tea tree essential oils that aid with acne.

  • Antibacterial property: Tea Tree Essential Oil has antibacterial properties that can help to reduce the growth of bacteria on the skin, which is a common cause of acne.
  • Anti-inflammatory property: Tea Tree Essential Oil has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce redness, swelling, and irritation associated with acne.
  • Antifungal property: Tea Tree Essential Oil has antifungal properties that can help to combat fungal infections that can cause acne-like symptoms, such as fungal acne or pityrosporum folliculitis.

The list below shows the primary parts of tea tree used to make essential oils for acne.

  • Leaves: The leaves are rich in cineole, a compound that has potent antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, making them effective in treating acne-prone skin.
  • Stems: The stems contain a high concentration of terpinen-4-ol, an important compound with antimicrobial activity that helps combat acne-causing bacteria.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic tea tree for acne.

  1. Harvest high-quality melaleuca alternifolia leaves at dawn when essential oil content is highest using pruning shears for precise cutting.
  2. Dry the harvested leaves in a single layer on paper towels or clean cloths for 7-10 days, monitoring humidity levels below 60%.
  3. Steam distill 500g of dried melaleuca alternifolia leaves with 1l of high-quality water at 100°c for 4-6 hours to extract essential oil.
  4. Filter the resulting essential oil mixture through a coffee filter or paper towel to remove impurities, allowing clear liquid to pass while retaining particles.
  5. Bottle and store the pure melaleuca alternifolia essential oil in airtight glass containers away from direct sunlight and heat sources for up to 2 years.
