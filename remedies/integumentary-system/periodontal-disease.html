What is periodontal disease and how it affects your life?

Periodontal disease, also known as gum disease, is a serious infection of the gums and other tissues that surround and support the teeth.

It is typically caused by plaque, a sticky film of bacteria that constantly forms on our teeth. When left untreated, periodontal disease can lead to a number of negative consequences for an individual's life.

For example, it can cause teeth to loosen or fall out, resulting in the need for dentures or dental implants. Additionally, the infection can spread to other parts of the body, increasing the risk of developing heart disease, stroke, and other systemic conditions. Periodontal disease can also cause bad breath, painful chewing, and receding gums, all of which can negatively impact an individual's self-esteem and quality of life.

Furthermore, it can lead to difficulty in speaking, swallowing and even malnutrition.
