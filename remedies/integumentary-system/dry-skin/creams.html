1. Aloe vera

Aloe creams helps with dry skin because they provide an intense moisturizing experience, locking in moisture to soothe and calm parched skin.

The aloe vera gel in these creams contains natural anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce redness and irritation, while its humectant properties attract and retain moisture from the air.

This combination of nourishment and hydration helps to repair and restore dry skin's natural barrier function, leaving it soft, supple, and feeling refreshed.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in aloe creams that aid with dry skin.

  • Moisturizing properties: Herbal aloe creams contain polysaccharides, which help to lock in moisture and soothe dry, itchy skin by forming a protective barrier on the skin's surface.
  • Anti-inflammatory effects: The bioactive compounds present in herbal aloe creams, such as aloin and aloe-emodin, have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce redness, swelling, and discomfort associated with dry skin.
  • Antioxidant properties: Herbal aloe creams contain antioxidants like vitamin E and beta-carotene, which help to neutralize free radicals that can cause oxidative stress and damage to the skin, leading to dryness and premature aging.

The list below shows the primary parts of aloe used to make creams for dry skin.

  • Leaves: Rich in vitamins, minerals, and amino acids, making them an excellent source of natural moisturizers and emollients.
  • Buds: Containing high amounts of anthraquinones, which help soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing inflammation and promoting healing.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic aloe for dry skin.

  1. Harvest high-quality aloe vera leaves and rinse them gently under cool running water.
  2. Remove the thick green rind from the aloe vera leaves using a sharp knife or vegetable peeler.
  3. Extract the clear gel from the aloe vera leaves by cutting them into small pieces and blending with 1 cup of distilled water for 5 minutes.
  4. Mix the extracted aloe vera gel with 1/2 cup of coconut oil, 1 tablespoon of beeswax, and 1 teaspoon of vitamin e oil in a double boiler.
  5. Heat the mixture under low heat stirring constantly until it reaches a temperature of 120°f to 140°f for about 10 minutes.
