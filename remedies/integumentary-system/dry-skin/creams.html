1. Aloe vera

Aloe vera, also known as aloe, creams helps with dry skin because of its rich moisturizing properties.

The gel extracted from the aloe plant contains vitamins A, C, and E, as well as beta-carotene and other essential nutrients that help to hydrate and soothe dry skin. It has anti-inflammatory properties that calm irritation and redness, while its natural humectants retain moisture and prevent water loss, leaving skin feeling soft, smooth, and refreshed.

Regular use can provide long-lasting relief from dry skin.

Medicinal Constituents

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Aloe vera creams that help with dry skin.

  • Champhene: This terpene helps to reduce inflammation and promote skin hydration, which can help alleviate dry skin symptoms.
  • Aloe-emodin: This anthraquinone derivative has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help soothe and protect dry skin from further damage.
  • Glucomannan: This polysaccharide has moisturizing and soothing properties, which can help to lock in moisture and reduce dryness and irritation.

Parts Used

The list below shows the primary parts of aloe used to make creams for dry skin.

  • Leaves: They contain the highest concentration of aloe vera's active compounds, such as glycoproteins and vitamins, that provide moisturizing and soothing properties.
  • Gel (from leaves): The clear, jelly-like substance inside the leaves is extracted and used in creams to provide intense hydration and skin repair.
  • Aloe vera extract (from leaves): This is a concentrated form of the gel that is often used in creams to provide long-lasting moisturization and skin protection.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic aloe for dry skin.

  1. Gather 1 cup of aloe vera gel from the leaves of the aloe vera plant and let it sit for 30 minutes.
  2. Melt 1/4 cup of beeswax in a double boiler over low heat for 10 minutes.
  3. Combine 1/2 cup of coconut oil and 1/4 cup of shea butter in a separate bowl and whisk until smooth.
  4. Add the aloe vera gel and the melted beeswax mixture to the coconut oil mixture and stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a clean container and let it cool and thicken for 30 minutes before use.

The list below shows the primary parts of pot marigold used to make creams for dry skin.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic pot marigold for dry skin.

  1. Harvest 2 cups of dried calendula officinalis flowers at peak bloom for optimal potency.
  2. Steep the flowers in 2 cups of carrier oil such as sweet almond oil at 100°f for 2 hours.
  3. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth into a clean container to remove solids after 2 hours.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of beeswax and 2 tablespoons of distilled water to the oil mixture and heat until melted.
  5. Pour the mixture into small containers and let cool at room temperature for 30 minutes.
