Aloe tinctures helps with dry skin because it possesses anti-inflammatory and moisturizing properties that soothe and hydrate the skin.

The aloe vera plant contains compounds such as aloin and aloe emodin, which work together to lock in moisture and reduce redness and irritation. The list below shows the primary active constituents in aloe tinctures that aid with dry skin.

  • Anti-inflammatory properties: Aloe vera's anti-inflammatory compounds, such as aloin and aloe-emodin, help reduce redness and swelling associated with dry skin, promoting comfort and relief.
  • Moisturizing and emollient properties: Aloe tincture's moisturizing agents, including vitamins A, C, and E, as well as amino acids, help lock in moisture and soothe dry, irritated skin, leaving it feeling soft and supple.
  • Antioxidant properties: The antioxidants present in aloe tincture, such as beta-carotene, help protect the skin from environmental stressors and damage caused by free radicals, which can exacerbate dryness and premature aging.

The list below shows the primary parts of aloe used to make tinctures for dry skin.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the aloe plant are rich in antioxidants and contain compounds like aloin, which help to soothe and moisturize dry skin.
  • Barks: Aloe bark contains a high concentration of saponins, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce redness and irritation associated with dry skin.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic aloe for dry skin.

  1. Harvest fresh aloe vera leaves selecting healthy, mature ones with thick, fleshy skin for optimal potency.
  2. Clean the harvested leaves by rinsing them gently under cold running water to remove dirt and debris.
  3. Cut the cleaned leaves into small pieces and blend them in a food processor or blender until they become a smooth paste.
  4. Combine 1 part of the aloe vera paste with 2 parts of high-proof vodka or grain alcohol in a glass jar, ensuring the mixture is well-covered.
  5. Store the jar in a cool, dark place for 6-8 weeks, shaking it daily to facilitate extraction and infusion of aloe's active compounds.
