10 best herbal teas for dry skin

1. High mallow tea

High mallow tea helps with dry skin because it contains mucilage, a substance that creates a protective barrier on the skin, helping it retain moisture.

Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties, it soothes irritation and reduces redness. High mallow tea also has essential fatty acids that nourish the skin, promoting elasticity and suppleness.

Drinking high mallow tea or using it as a compress can provide relief from dry, itchy skin, leaving it feeling soft and hydrated.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in high mallow tea that aid with dry skin.

  • Moisturizing: High mallow tea is rich in mucilage, a slippery substance that helps to lock in moisture and soothe dry, irritated skin.
  • Anti-inflammatory: The flavonoids and phenolic acids in high mallow tea have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce redness and irritation associated with dry skin.
  • Antioxidant: High mallow tea contains high levels of antioxidants, such as vitamin C and E, which can help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals and environmental stressors, and promote skin health.
  • Soothing: The demulcent properties of high mallow tea help to soothe and protect dry, cracked skin by forming a protective barrier on the skin's surface.
  • Emollient: High mallow tea is rich in emollients, such as omega-3 fatty acids, that help to soften and smooth dry, rough skin.

The list below shows the primary parts of high mallow tea used to make teas for dry skin.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants, the leaves help soothe dry skin and reduce inflammation.
  • Flowers: Contain anti-inflammatory properties, making them ideal for alleviating dry, irritated skin.
  • Seeds: Packed with essential fatty acids, seeds help to restore the skin's natural barrier and lock in moisture.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic high mallow tea for dry skin.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of dried high mallow flowers and leaves with 4 cups of water.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove solid plant material, reserving the liquid.
  4. Compost the solid plant material and let the tea cool to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the high mallow tea, up to three times daily, for moisturizing benefits to dry skin.

How to best treat dry skin with herbal teas?

The best way to treat dry skin with herbal teas is by using them as a compress or in the bath.

Steep a strong tea from herbs known to soothe and moisturize, such as chamomile, calendula, or lavender, and let it cool. For a compress, soak a clean cloth in the tea and apply to the affected areas. For a bath, add several tea bags or loose tea to the running water.

These herbal teas help restore the skin's natural barrier, reducing dryness and leaving skin soft and supple.
