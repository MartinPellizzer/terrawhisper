What is burns and how it affects your life?

Burns are a type of injury that can have a profound impact on one's daily life.

A burn can leave you feeling like a shadow of your former self, affecting every aspect of your existence. You may struggle to perform even the simplest tasks, such as getting dressed or taking a shower, due to chronic pain and discomfort. Social gatherings and intimate relationships become increasingly difficult as you're forced to navigate the world with limited mobility and confidence.

Your career may suffer as well, as burns can make it challenging to concentrate and focus, leading to decreased productivity and missed deadlines. Even simple pleasures like cooking or enjoying a favorite hobby are no longer possible, leaving you feeling isolated and withdrawn. Sleep is often disrupted by pain, causing fatigue and irritability, which can strain relationships with loved ones.

As the physical healing process drags on, the emotional toll of burn trauma can leave a lasting scar, affecting not just your daily routine but also your overall well-being and sense of identity.

What are the main causes of burns?

The main causes of burns are a combination of factors that involve heat, electricity, chemicals, and other substances interacting with the skin.

Thermal burns occur when the skin comes into contact with flames, hot liquids or solids, steam, or electrical currents, causing damage to the skin tissues. Chemical burns result from exposure to corrosive substances, such as acids or bases, which can break down the skin's natural barrier and cause tissue damage.

Scalds occur when the skin is exposed to hot water or other liquids, often resulting in third-degree burns. Electrocution burns happen when an electrical current passes through the body, causing thermal and chemical reactions that damage the skin and underlying tissues.

Other causes of burns include radiation therapy, lightning strikes, and even friction or abrasion from repetitive motion or activities.

The most common causes of burns are listed below.

  • Scalds: Scalds occur when hot liquids or steam come into contact with the skin, causing damage to the epidermis and dermis layers.
  • Flames: Open flames from fires, candles, or lighters can cause burns by direct heat transfer, charring the skin and underlying tissues.
  • Electrical Burns: Electrical shock can cause burns by generating heat through resistance in the body, often resulting in deep tissue damage.
  • Chemical Burns: Exposure to corrosive chemicals, such as acid or alkali, can cause burns by breaking down skin tissues and causing inflammation.
  • Hot Surfaces: Touching hot surfaces, like stovetops, oven racks, or radiators, can cause thermal injuries due to direct heat transfer.
  • Steam Inhalation: Inhaling hot steam can cause burns in the lungs and throat, particularly in cases of steam inhalation therapy gone wrong.
  • Sunburn: Prolonged exposure to UV radiation from the sun or tanning beds can cause burns, also known as erythema, due to damage to skin cells' DNA.
  • Fireworks and Explosions: Traumatic injuries from fireworks or explosions can result in burns, lacerations, and other physical harm.
  • Cooking Accidents: Accidental splashing of hot food or liquids while cooking can cause scalds or burns, especially when working with high-temperature appliances.
  • Lightning Strikes: Direct strikes by lightning can cause severe electrical burns, often resulting in life-threatening injuries.

What precautions to take when using herbal remedies for burns?

When using herbal remedies for burns, it's essential to exercise caution and follow proper guidelines to avoid exacerbating the injury.

Before applying any herbal remedy, clean and disinfect the burn wound thoroughly to prevent infection. Choose herbs that have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, such as aloe vera, tea tree oil, or chamomile, but consult with a healthcare professional first to ensure you're using them safely and effectively.

Avoid using herbs that may irritate the skin, such as turmeric or cinnamon, on open wounds. Additionally, don't apply excessive pressure or rub the herb into the wound, as this can cause further damage.

Finally, monitor the burn's progress closely and seek medical attention if it shows signs of infection, such as increased redness, swelling, or pus, or if you experience severe pain or difficulty breathing.

The most important precautions to take when using herbal remedies for burns are listed below.

  • Apply cool

  • Consult with a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies

  • Keep the affected area moist to promote healing
  • Monitor for signs of infection such as increased redness, swelling, or pus
