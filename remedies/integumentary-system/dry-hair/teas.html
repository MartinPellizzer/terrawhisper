10 best herbal teas for dry hair

Herbal teas for dry hair are infusions made from various dried herbs, flowers, and seeds that are known to moisturize and nourish dry hair.

By incorporating these herbal teas into their hair care routine, individuals with dry hair can experience softer, more manageable hair that is less prone to breakage and damage.

1. Yarrow tea

Yarrow tea helps with dry hair because it contains inflammation-reducing and moisture-retaining properties that can improve the condition of dry and damaged hair.

The plant's anti-inflammatory compounds can help soothe a dry, itchy scalp, while its emollient properties can lock in moisture and protect the hair shaft from further damage. Additionally, yarrow tea is rich in vitamins and minerals, including vitamin E, which can help promote hair growth and prevent breakage.

By drinking yarrow tea or using it as a rinse, individuals can nourish their hair and scalp, leaving them with softer, shinier, and more manageable hair.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in yarrow tea that aid with dry hair.

  • Moisturizing: Yarrow tea contains several hydrating compounds that help to lock in moisture and alleviate dryness in hair, preventing brittleness and breakage. Active constituents include flavonoids, tannins, and saponins.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Yarrow tea has potent anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe an irritated scalp and reduce inflammation, promoting healthy hair growth and reducing dryness-related irritation. The primary active constituents responsible for this property are sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids.
  • Antioxidant: Yarrow tea contains powerful antioxidants that help protect the hair from environmental damage and oxidative stress, which can lead to dryness and hair aging. The main active constituents responsible for this property are flavonoids and phenolic acids.
  • Improved blood circulation: Yarrow tea helps improve blood flow to the scalp, promoting nutrient absorption and stimulating healthy hair growth, which can help reduce dryness and brittleness. The active constituents responsible for this property are volatile oils, such as azulene and chamazulene.
  • Balanced sebum production: Yarrow tea has a balancing effect on sebum production, reducing excess oiliness and promoting a healthy scalp environment, which helps prevent dryness and flakiness. The active constituents responsible for this property are sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids.

The list below shows the primary parts of yarrow tea used to make teas for dry hair.

  • Leaves: Rich in nutrients and antioxidants, yarrow leaves promote hair growth and help moisturize dry hair.
  • Flowers: Containing anti-inflammatory properties, yarrow flowers soothe the scalp, reducing dryness and irritation.
  • Seeds: High in essential fatty acids, yarrow seeds nourish the hair follicles, promoting healthy hair growth and reducing dryness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yarrow tea for dry hair.

  1. In a pot, boil 2 cups of water and add 2 tablespoons of dried yarrow herb, simmering for 15-20 minutes.
  2. After straining the mixture, let it cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  3. In a separate container, mix the cooled yarrow tea with 1 cup of warm water.
  4. Before shampooing, thoroughly wet your hair and pour the yarrow tea mixture over it, massaging gently into the scalp.
  5. Leave the mixture on for 10-15 minutes, then rinse it out and proceed with your usual hair care routine.

  • Emollient property: The high mucilage content in marshmallow tea smoothes the hair cuticle, promoting shine and reducing breakage caused by dry, brittle hair.

The list below shows the primary parts of marshmallow tea used to make teas for dry hair.

  • Seeds: High in fatty acids, seeds provide essential nourishment to dry hair, promoting shine and softness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for dry hair.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water, 1 tablespoon of dried nettle leaves, and 1 tablespoon of dried horsetail herb; bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. Remove from heat, add 1 tablespoon of dried marshmallow root, and let the mixture steep for 20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the herbs, then compost the solids.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.

How to best treat dry hair with herbal teas?

The best way to treat dry hair with herbal teas is by rinsing your hair with cooled, steeped tea.

Choose teas rich in nutrients, like chamomile, calendula, or rosemary. These herbs can help moisturize and strengthen dry hair.

After shampooing, pour the cooled tea over your hair, massaging it into your scalp. Allow the tea to sit for a few minutes before rinsing with water.

Do this regularly to see an improvement in your hair's texture and appearance.

What ailments similar to dry hair are treated with herbal teas?

Ailments similar to dry hair that are treated with herbal teas include dandruff, itchy scalp, and hair loss.

Chamomile tea is known for its anti-inflammatory properties, making it an effective treatment for a dry, itchy scalp. Meanwhile, nettle tea can help stimulate hair growth and prevent hair loss due to its high mineral content.

For dandruff, tea tree oil herbal tea can be beneficial as it has antifungal and antiseptic properties that can help alleviate the symptoms. However, it is important to note that while herbal teas can help treat these ailments, they should not replace medical treatment, especially for severe cases.

Always consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.
