Rosemary decoctions helps with dry hair because it stimulates blood flow to the scalp, promoting healthy circulation of oxygen and nutrients to the follicles.

The antioxidants present in rosemary also help protect the hair shaft from damage caused by free radicals, leaving strands soft, shiny, and resilient.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in rosemary decoctions that aid with dry hair.

  • Antioxidant properties: Rosemary's antioxidants help protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals, reducing oxidative stress and promoting healthy hair growth.
  • Anti-inflammatory properties: Rosemary's anti-inflammatory compounds reduce redness and irritation in the scalp, soothing itchy or flaky skin and promoting a healthier environment for hair growth.

The list below shows the primary parts of rosemary used to make decoctions for dry hair.

  • Roots: Rich in antioxidants and essential oils, rosemary roots help nourish and moisturize dry hair, promoting a healthy scalp and reducing dandruff.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic rosemary for dry hair.

  1. Rinse dried rosmarinus officinalis leaves in cold water then measure one tablespoon of leaves for every cup of boiling water.
  2. Boil the leaf-water mixture for ten minutes to release the active ingredients and essential oils.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and let it steep for 30 minutes to allow the mixture to cool and infuse.
  4. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean container discarding the solids.
  5. Use the cooled decoction as a pre-shampoo treatment by applying it to dry hair for 15-20 minutes before shampooing.
