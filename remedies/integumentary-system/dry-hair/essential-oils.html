1. Lavandula angustifolia

English lavender essential oils helps with dry hair because of its incredible moisturizing properties.

The oil is rich in antioxidants and fatty acids that deeply nourish and hydrate the hair, leaving it soft, silky, and manageable. Additionally, lavender's anti-inflammatory properties help to calm and soothe the scalp, reducing irritation and flaking caused by dryness.

As a result, English lavender essential oils can provide long-lasting hydration and leave your hair feeling smooth, supple, and luscious.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in english lavender essential oils that aid with dry hair.

  • Antioxidant: English Lavender essential oil's antioxidant properties help to protect the hair from damage caused by free radicals, which can lead to dryness and breakage.
  • Moisturizing: The fatty acid composition of English Lavender essential oil helps to moisturize and nourish the hair, leaving it soft and supple, while also reducing frizz and flyaways.
  • Anti-inflammatory: English Lavender essential oil's anti-inflammatory properties can help to soothe and calm irritated scalps and dry ends, reducing inflammation and promoting healthy hair growth.

The list below shows the primary parts of english lavender used to make essential oils for dry hair.

  • Flowers: Lavender flowers are a popular choice for making essential oils due to their sweet, calming aroma and ability to soothe dry, itchy scalps.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the lavender plant contain a high concentration of camphor, which helps to nourish and moisturize dry hair while reducing dandruff and flaking.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic english lavender for dry hair.

  1. Harvest high-quality fresh or dried lavandula angustifolia flowers in quantities of approximately one pound for small-scale production.
  2. Dry the harvested flowers using shade-drying techniques or low-temperature drying machines to preserve their essential oil content within 24 hours.
  3. Steam distill the dried flowers using a traditional steam distillation apparatus or a commercial still at atmospheric pressure with water temperatures around 100 degrees celsius for four to six hours.
  4. Collect and filter the obtained essential oil by decanting it from the distillate and separating it from any impurities within two days of distillation.
  5. Store the extracted lavandula angustifolia essential oil in dark glass containers with tight-fitting lids at room temperature away from direct sunlight and heat sources for up to six months.

This blend helps to nourish, moisturize, and add shine to dry hair.
