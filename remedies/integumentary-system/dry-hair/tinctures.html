10 best herbal tinctures for dry hair

1. Yarrow tincture

Yarrow tincture helps with dry hair because it contains anti-inflammatory and moisturizing properties that can improve the health of the scalp and hair.

The plant's active compounds, including flavonoids and sesquiterpene lactones, can help stimulate blood flow to the scalp, promoting hair growth and reducing dryness. Additionally, yarrow tincture can help regulate sebum production, providing natural moisture to the hair and preventing further dryness. Its emollient and nourishing properties can also help smooth the hair cuticle, adding shine and softness to dry, brittle hair.

Overall, yarrow tincture can be a valuable natural remedy for addressing dry hair and improving hair health.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in yarrow tincture that aid with dry hair.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Yarrow tincture's anti-inflammatory properties, due to the presence of flavonoids, help reduce scalp inflammation, promoting healthy hair growth and reducing dryness.
  • Astringent: The astringent properties of yarrow tincture, from tannins, help to tighten the scalp's pores, reducing excess oil production and improving the hair's overall moisture balance.
  • Antimicrobial: Yarrow tincture's antimicrobial properties, owing to the presence of lactones and sesquiterpene, help maintain a clean and healthy scalp, preventing dandruff and dryness.
  • Circulation-boosting: The circulation-boosting properties of yarrow tincture, due to the presence of lactones, promote blood flow to the scalp, encouraging hair growth and reducing dryness.
  • Moisturizing: Yarrow tincture's moisturizing properties, from plant acids, help to hydrate the hair and scalp, reducing dryness and improving hair health.

The list below shows the primary parts of yarrow tincture used to make tinctures for dry hair.

  • Flowers: Rich in vitamins and minerals, yarrow flowers help nourish and moisturize dry hair.
  • Leaves: Containing essential oils, yarrow leaves can help stimulate hair growth and soothe a dry scalp.
  • Seeds: High in fatty acids, yarrow seeds can help condition and strengthen dry, brittle hair.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yarrow tincture for dry hair.

  1. Harvest 2 ounces of fresh yarrow flowers and leaves, carefully cleaning them to remove any debris.
  2. Chop the yarrow into small pieces and place in a clean jar, filling it 3/4 full.
  3. Pour 10 ounces of vodka or grain alcohol (80-100 proof) over the yarrow, ensuring all plant material is submerged.
  4. Seal the jar and store in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks, shaking it gently every few days.
  5. Strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out the excess liquid, and store the resulting tincture in a dark glass bottle.
