10 best herbal teas for dry eyes

1. Eyebright tea

Eyebright tea helps with dry eyes because it contains flavonoids, phenolic acids, and iridoid glycosides, which have anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.

Drinking eyebright tea or using it as a compress can provide relief from dry eyes and improve overall eye health. However, it is important to note that eyebright tea should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment for dry eyes.

Always consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

  • Antimicrobial: Eyebright tea inhibits the growth of bacteria and viruses that can cause eye infections, promoting overall eye health and alleviating dry eye symptoms, thanks to its active constituents like phenolic acids and essential oils.
  • Antioxidant: The antioxidant properties of eyebright tea help protect the eyes from oxidative stress and damage, supporting eye health and reducing the risk of dry eye complications, due to the high levels of flavonoids and phenolic acids.
  • Diuretic: Eyebright tea promotes the elimination of excess fluids from the body, which can help reduce eye puffiness and improve overall eye comfort, thanks to the active constituents like flavonoids and saponins.

The list below shows the primary parts of eyebright tea used to make teas for dry eyes.

  • Flowers: Rich in antioxidants, the flowers of eyebright tea plant help soothe and relieve dry eye symptoms.
  • Leaves: Containing anti-inflammatory properties, the leaves of the plant aid in reducing irritation caused by dry eyes.
  • Seeds: High in essential fatty acids, the seeds of the eyebright tea plant help lubricate and moisturize dry eyes.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic eyebright tea for dry eyes.

  1. In a stovepot, boil 1 cup of water and add 1 teaspoon of dried Eyebright herb.
  2. Reduce heat, cover the pot and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the herb particles.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of Eyebright tea daily for dry eye relief.

How to best treat dry eyes with herbal teas?

The best way to treat dry eyes with herbal teas is by using chamomile or fennel tea.

Steep one tea bag in hot water for several minutes, then let it cool down. Once warm, gently apply the tea bag to the affected eye for 5-10 minutes.

Both chamomile and fennel have anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe irritation and reduce dryness. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day for best results.

Remember to always use clean hands and a fresh tea bag for each application to avoid infection.

What ailments similar to dry eyes are treated with herbal teas?

Ailments similar to dry eyes that are treated with herbal teas include blepharitis and conjunctivitis.

Blepharitis is an inflammation of the eyelids, often caused by bacteria or skin conditions like dandruff. Chamomile tea, known for its anti-inflammatory properties, can help soothe the irritation.

Conjunctivitis, or pink eye, is an inflammation of the conjunctiva, the membrane that covers the eye and inner eyelid. Calendula tea, with its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, can be used as a compress to alleviate symptoms.

However, it's important to consult a healthcare professional for accurate diagnosis and treatment.
