9 Best Herbal Teas For Eczema

1. Calendula officinalis teas

Calendula officinalis teas have been traditionally used to treat eczema due to their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm the skin, reducing redness and itching associated with eczema.

The bioactive constituents of Calendula officinalis teas, including triterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to the development and progression of eczema.

By using Calendula officinalis teas topically, individuals with eczema may experience improved skin health, reduced symptoms, and enhanced overall well-being.

Featured Study

According to "Forschende Komplementarmedizin", Calendula officinalis teas for eczema may be of value in selected dermatological indications.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of dried Calendula officinalis flowers and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of dried Calendula officinalis flowers and place it in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the Calendula officinalis flowers. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into another cup and discard the flowers. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 1 cup of Calendula officinalis tea 2-3 times a day, or as directed by a healthcare professional.

Usage:

  1. Steep 1 cup of dried Calendula flowers in boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea and let it cool to room temperature.
  3. Soak a clean cloth in the tea and apply to affected skin areas.
  4. Use this tea compress 2-3 times a day for best results.
  5. Combine with a gentle skincare routine for optimal eczema relief.

Calendula officinalis teas can be used as a potential natural remedy for eczema, but some possible side effects include skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased sensitivity to sunlight.

To minimize potential risks, it is recommended to start with a small dose, monitor your skin condition closely, and avoid using Calendula officinalis teas on broken or sensitive skin, as well as in areas exposed to direct sunlight.
