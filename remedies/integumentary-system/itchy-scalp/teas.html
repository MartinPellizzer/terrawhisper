German chamomile tea helps with itchy scalp because it has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can soothe and heal an irritated scalp.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in german chamomile tea that aid with itchy scalp.

  • Anti-inflammatory: German chamomile tea can reduce inflammation, which is important for itchy scalp as it helps alleviate symptoms such as redness, swelling, and irritation. This property is attributed to the active constituent, chamazulene.
  • Antimicrobial: It can help inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi, which is crucial for itchy scalp as it may prevent infections that can worsen the condition. The antimicrobial properties come from the compound matricin, which is converted to chamazulene.
  • Antioxidant: German chamomile tea has antioxidant properties that help protect the skin from damage caused by free radicals, promoting overall skin health and reducing itchiness. The antioxidant effects are primarily due to the presence of flavonoids and terpenoids.
  • Soothing: The tea has a calming effect on the skin, which can help alleviate itchiness and discomfort. This soothing property is attributed to the flavonoid apigenin, which has anti-pruritic (anti-itch) effects.
  • Wound healing: German chamomile tea can help speed up wound healing, which is important for itchy scalp as it may reduce the risk of developing scabs or sores from excessive scratching. The wound-healing properties are attributed to the flavonoids and essential oils found in the plant.

The list below shows the primary parts of german chamomile tea used to make teas for itchy scalp.

  • Flowers: The flowers of German chamomile are rich in flavonoids and essential oils, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help soothe an itchy scalp.
  • Leaves: The leaves of German chamomile contain tannins that can help reduce inflammation and irritation, providing relief for an itchy scalp.
  • Seeds: German chamomile seeds are a good source of essential fatty acids, which can help moisturize and soothe a dry, itchy scalp.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic german chamomile tea for itchy scalp.

  1. Measure out 2 teaspoons of dried German chamomile flowers and place them in a tea infuser or loose leaf tea bag.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the chamomile flowers, allowing them to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the tea infuser or tea bag from the water.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  5. Slowly pour the tea over your scalp, massaging it in gently, and letting it sit for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out.
