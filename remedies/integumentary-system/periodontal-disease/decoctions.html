The decoction's flavonoids and polyphenols help reduce the growth of harmful bacteria in the mouth, thereby preventing the development of plaque and tartar. Additionally, elder's anti-inflammatory compounds ease swelling and pain associated with gum disease, promoting a healthy oral environment that allows gums to reattach to teeth, reversing early stages of periodontal disease.

This natural remedy also stimulates saliva production, which helps neutralize acids and remineralize tooth enamel.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in elder decoctions that aid with periodontal disease.

  • Antimicrobial properties: Elderberry's antimicrobial properties help combat bacterial infections, such as periodontitis, by inhibiting the growth of bacteria and reducing inflammation.
  • Anti-inflammatory properties: Elderberry's anti-inflammatory compounds reduce swelling, redness, and pain associated with periodontal disease, promoting a healthy oral environment.
  • Antioxidant properties: Elderberry's high antioxidant content helps protect against oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to chronic inflammation and tissue destruction in the mouth.

The list below shows the primary parts of elder used to make decoctions for periodontal disease.

  • Roots: The roots of the Elder plant are used to treat gum inflammation and reduce swelling, making them effective in soothing periodontal disease.
  • Barks: The barks of the Elder plant contain anti-inflammatory compounds that help alleviate pain and discomfort associated with periodontal disease.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic elder for periodontal disease.

  1. Measure one teaspoon of dried sambucus nigra flowers and leaves and place them in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add two cups of boiling water to the jar and steep for 10 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid through a fine-mesh sieve into another container and discard the solids.
  4. Combine the decoction with one cup of cold water and allow it to cool to room temperature.
  5. Use the cooled decoction as an oral rinse by swishing it around your mouth for 30 seconds and then spitting it out.
