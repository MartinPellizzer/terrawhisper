1. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis teas contains compounds like carnosic acid, rosmarinic acid, and salvianolic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

To use Salvia officinalis teas to help with periodontal disease, start by drinking 1-2 cups a day. You can steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried Salvia officinalis leaves in boiling water for 5-10 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it warm or at room temperature.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. To enhance the effectiveness, consider combining Salvia officinalis teas with other treatments, such as brushing and flossing regularly, and visiting your dentist for regular check-ups. If you take action and drink Salvia officinalis teas regularly, you may notice a reduction in gum inflammation and bleeding, and an improvement in the overall health of your gums and teeth. Your breath may also become fresher, and you may experience less pain when eating or drinking. However, it's essential to note that Salvia officinalis teas are not a replacement for professional dental care.

Consult your dentist before using Salvia officinalis teas to treat periodontal disease, especially if you have severe symptoms or other underlying health conditions.
