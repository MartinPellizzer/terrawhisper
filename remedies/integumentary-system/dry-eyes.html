What is dry eyes and how it affects your life?

Dry eyes is a frustrating and uncomfortable condition that can have a significant impact on daily life.

Characterized by a lack of adequate moisture in the eyes, it can cause a range of symptoms including blurry vision, gritty or sandy feeling in the eyes, excessive tearing, and sensitivity to light. For those suffering from dry eyes, even simple tasks like reading, watching TV, or using a computer can become daunting challenges. Imagine being unable to enjoy your favorite hobbies, such as fishing or golfing, due to the constant irritation and discomfort in your eyes.

Or picture trying to sleep at night, only to be kept awake by the burning sensation in your eyes. Dry eyes can even affect one's professional life, making it difficult to focus during long meetings or presentations. In extreme cases, dry eyes can lead to chronic eye fatigue, headaches, and even blurred vision that makes everyday activities like driving or cooking a hazard.

Overall, dry eyes can be a debilitating condition that takes a significant toll on both physical and mental well-being.

What are the main causes of dry eyes?

The main causes of dry eyes are a combination of factors that disrupt the normal functioning of the tear glands and the surface of the eye.

One of the most common causes is inadequate tear production, which can be due to hormonal changes during menopause or pregnancy, certain medications such as antihistamines and decongestants, or medical conditions like diabetes and rheumatoid arthritis. Other factors that contribute to dry eyes include increased evaporation of tears due to windy weather, air conditioning or heating, or staring at digital screens for extended periods, which can reduce tear production and increase tear evaporation.

Additionally, age-related decline in tear quality and quantity, as well as certain eye diseases such as blepharitis and meibomian gland dysfunction, can also lead to dry eyes.

The most common causes of dry eyes are listed below.

  • Aging: As people age, they are more likely to experience dry eye syndrome due to decreased tear production and changes in tear composition.
  • Vitamin Deficiency: Lack of essential vitamins such as omega-3 fatty acids, vitamin A, and vitamin D can contribute to dry eye symptoms.
  • Smoking: Smoking can reduce tear production and increase the risk of developing dry eye syndrome by damaging the tear glands and ducts.
  • Environmental Factors: Exposure to wind, dust, and dry air can evaporate tears more quickly, leading to dry eyes.
  • Computer Use: Prolonged screen time can cause decreased tear production and increased tear evaporation due to staring at a fixed point for extended periods.
  • Contact Lens Wear: Wearing contact lenses, especially if not cleaned and cared for properly, can irritate the eyes and lead to dryness.
  • Allergies: Seasonal allergies or other allergic reactions can cause conjunctival inflammation and increased tear evaporation, leading to dry eye symptoms.
  • Medications: Certain medications such as antihistamines, decongestants, and blood pressure medications can reduce tear production or increase tear evaporation.
  • Autoimmune Disorders: Conditions like rheumatoid arthritis, lupus, and Sjögren's syndrome can cause inflammation and damage to the tear glands and ducts, leading to dry eyes.
  • Blockages of Tear Ducts: Blockages in the tear ducts can prevent tears from draining properly, leading to excessive tearing and dry eye symptoms.

What precautions to take when using herbal remedies for dry eyes?

When considering the use of herbal remedies for dry eye relief, it's essential to exercise caution and follow certain precautions.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before trying any new herbal remedy, as some may interact with medications or have adverse effects on certain individuals. Additionally, choose high-quality, pure herbal products from reputable sources to minimize the risk of contamination or adulteration. When using herbal remedies for dry eyes, start with small doses and gradually increase as needed, monitoring your symptoms closely.

Also, be aware that herbs can affect blood thinning, so if you're taking anticoagulant medications, consult with a healthcare professional before combining them with herbal supplements.

The most important precautions to take when using herbal remedies for dry eyes are listed below.

  • Research carefully the recommended dosages and potential interactions with other medications
  • Consult with a healthcare professional before trying any new herbal remedy
  • Start with small amounts and gradually increase as needed and tolerated

  • Stop immediately if you experience any irritation, redness or discomfort in the eyes

  • Store herbal remedies safely out of reach of children and pets

  • Disclose your use of herbal remedies to your healthcare provider when receiving medical treatment
