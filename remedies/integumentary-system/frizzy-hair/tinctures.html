1. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures contains compounds like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to soothe and calm the scalp, reducing irritation and inflammation that can contribute to frizzy hair. The tannins in Rosmarinus officinalis also help to tighten hair cuticles, reducing frizz and adding shine to your hair.

To use Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures for frizzy hair, start by mixing a few drops with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil, and apply it to the ends of your hair to help smooth and tame frizz. Alternatively, you can add a few drops to your shampoo or conditioner for an intense dose of frizz-fighting properties. When you use Rosmarinus officinalis tinctures, you can expect to see a reduction in frizz and flyaways, and your hair may appear smoother, shinier, and more manageable.

As the tannins in Rosmarinus officinalis work to tighten hair cuticles, you may notice that your hair holds its style better, and you may require less styling product to achieve the look you want.

To use Urtica dioica tinctures for frizzy hair, try mixing a few drops of the tincture with a leave-in conditioner or hair serum, and apply it to the ends of your hair.

You can also add a few drops to your shampoo or conditioner for an extra dose of smoothing power. Taking action with Urtica dioica tinctures may lead to a decrease in frizz and flyaways, making hair appear smoother and more manageable.
