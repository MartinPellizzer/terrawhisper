1. Cymbopogon citratus teas

Cymbopogon citratus teas, also known as lemongrass tea, have been traditionally used to treat frizzy hair due to its properties that help to smooth and tame unruly locks.

The herbal preparation's ability to soothe and calm the scalp helps to reduce frizz by promoting a healthy scalp environment, while its moisturizing properties hydrate the hair strands, leaving them soft and manageable.

The bioactive constituents of Cymbopogon citratus, including citral and geraniol, possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to repair damaged hair and prevent breakage, further contributing to a smooth and frizz-free hair texture.

Regular consumption of lemongrass tea has been found to provide numerous benefits, including reduced frizz, improved hair elasticity, and enhanced overall hair health, making it a popular natural remedy for frizzy hair.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Cymbopogon citratus leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the liquid into a separate container and let it cool.
  4. Once cooled, add 1 tablespoon of lemon juice to the tea and mix well.
  5. Use the tea as a final rinse after shampooing your hair to help reduce frizz.

Usage:

  1. Wash hair with shampoo and conditioner as usual
  2. Steep 1 tablespoon of Cymbopogon citratus tea in 1 cup of hot water for 5 minutes
  3. Let the tea cool down to room temperature
  4. Use the tea as a final rinse after shampooing 2-3 times a week
  5. Leave the tea on hair for 5-10 minutes before rinsing with cold water

Cymbopogon citratus teas can help to treat frizzy hair by controlling oil production and balancing the scalp's pH levels, but it may cause side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased sensitivity to sunlight.

To use Cymbopogon citratus teas safely, it is recommended to start with small amounts, patch test on a small area of skin, and avoid using it on sensitive areas or if you have a history of skin conditions.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis teas

Rosmarinus officinalis teas have been traditionally used as a natural remedy to treat frizzy hair, a common issue caused by dryness and damage to the hair shaft.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

  4. Add 1 tablespoon of olive oil to the tea and mix well.
  5. Use the tea as a final rinse after shampooing to help reduce frizz and add shine to your hair.

Usage:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and towel dry.
  2. Steep 2 tablespoons of dried Rosmarinus officinalis in 1 cup of boiling water.
  3. Allow the tea to cool for 30 minutes before use.
  4. Use the cooled tea as a final rinse after shampooing, 2-3 times a week.
  5. Leave the tea on for 5-10 minutes, then shampoo and condition as usual.

Rosmarinus officinalis teas can be an effective treatment for frizzy hair, but potential side effects may include digestive issues due to the high concentration of essential oils, as well as skin irritation or allergic reactions in some individuals.

To use Rosmarinus officinalis teas safely, take a small amount to start, mix with a carrier oil before applying to the hair, and avoid using it on damaged or sensitive scalp areas.
