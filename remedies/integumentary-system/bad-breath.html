Bad Breath: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Bad breath, medically known as halitosis, is a condition characterized by an unpleasant odor emanating from the mouth.

It is often caused by poor oral hygiene, gum disease, dry mouth, and consumption of certain foods or drinks. If left unaddressed, bad breath can lead to social isolation, low self-esteem, and even affect one's mental health. Fortunately, various herbal remedies can provide relief, including peppermint, eucalyptus, and sage, which can be consumed as teas or used in mouthwashes.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs and to follow proper preparation and dosing guidelines to avoid adverse reactions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of bad breath, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of bad breath?

The main causes of bad breath are primarily related to oral health, with poor dental hygiene being a significant contributor.

Bacteria in the mouth, particularly on the tongue and between teeth, break down food particles and produce volatile sulfur compounds (VSCs) as a byproduct, resulting in bad breath. Additionally, gum disease, periodontitis, and tooth decay can also cause bad breath due to the presence of bacteria and inflammation in the mouth. Other factors such as dry mouth, a condition known as xerostomia, can also contribute to bad breath due to the lack of saliva, which helps to neutralize acids and wash away bacteria.

Furthermore, medical conditions such as diabetes, gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), and respiratory tract infections can also cause bad breath.

  • Poor Oral Hygiene: Poor oral hygiene is the most common cause of bad breath due to the accumulation of bacteria and food particles in the mouth, which can lead to the production of volatile sulfur compounds and other odorous compounds.
  • Dry Mouth: Dry mouth, also known as xerostomia, is a common cause of bad breath due to the reduced saliva production, which can lead to the accumulation of bacteria and food particles in the mouth.
  • Gum Disease: Gum disease, also known as periodontal disease, is a common cause of bad breath due to the inflammation and infection of the gums, which can lead to the production of volatile sulfur compounds and other odorous compounds.
  • Tobacco Use: Tobacco use is a common cause of bad breath due to the chemicals and tar present in tobacco smoke, which can lead to the production of volatile sulfur compounds and other odorous compounds.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes is a less common cause of bad breath due to the high glucose levels in the saliva, which can lead to the growth of bacteria and the production of volatile sulfur compounds.
  • Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease: Gastroesophageal reflux disease is a less common cause of bad breath due to the regurgitation of stomach acid into the mouth, which can lead to the production of volatile sulfur compounds and other odorous compounds.
  • Kidney Disease: Kidney disease is a less common cause of bad breath due to the buildup of waste products in the body, which can lead to the production of volatile sulfur compounds and other odorous compounds.
  • Liver Disease: Liver disease is a less common cause of bad breath due to the buildup of toxins in the body, which can lead to the production of volatile sulfur compounds and other odorous compounds.
  • Medications: Certain medications, such as antidepressants and decongestants, can be a less common cause of bad breath due to their side effects, such as dry mouth or changes in saliva composition.
  • Diet: A diet high in sulfur-containing foods, such as garlic and onions, can be a less common cause of bad breath due to the production of volatile sulfur compounds.
