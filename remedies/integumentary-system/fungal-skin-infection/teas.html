Melaleuca alternifolia teas, also known as tea tree oil tea, have been traditionally used to treat fungal skin infections, such as athlete's foot and ringworm, due to their antimicrobial and antifungal properties.

The bioactive constituents of this herbal preparation, including cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and limonene, help to inhibit the growth of fungi and bacteria, thereby reducing the severity of the infection.

The antifungal properties of Melaleuca alternifolia teas help to treat fungal skin infections by reducing inflammation, preventing the spread of the infection, and promoting the healing of affected skin.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 2 cups of water and 2 tablespoons of dried Melaleuca alternifolia leaves.

  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day for fungal skin infection relief.
