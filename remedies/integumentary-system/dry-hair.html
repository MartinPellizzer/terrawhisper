Dry Hair: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Dry hair is characterized by its brittleness, frizziness, and lack of moisture, leading to breakage and split ends.

It occurs due to factors such as over-shampooing, harsh chemical treatments, cold weather, and poor hair care routines. Dry hair can cause scalp irritation, itchiness, and even infections, negatively impacting overall health.

To alleviate dry hair, consider using herbal remedies like aloe vera, coconut oil, and olive oil, which can be massaged into the scalp and left on overnight. Additionally, medicinal preparations like rosemary essential oil and chamomile tea can help hydrate and soothe dry hair.

However, it's essential to note that some herbs may cause allergic reactions or interact with medications, so consult a healthcare professional before using them.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of dry hair, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of dry hair?

The main causes of dry hair are excessive heat styling, which damages the hair cuticle and strips it of its natural oils, with temperatures above 150°C causing the most damage.

Over-washing and using hot water, which can strip the hair of its natural moisture barrier, is also a contributing factor. Chemical treatments, such as hair dye, perms, and relaxers, can cause damage to the hair shaft and lead to dryness. Genetics play a role, with people with naturally curly or wavy hair more prone to dryness due to their hair's natural tendency to lose moisture.

Additionally, environmental factors such as humidity, wind, and sun exposure can also contribute to dry hair, especially in individuals with compromised hair barriers.

  • Overwashing: Overwashing hair strips it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and damage.
  • Heat Styling: Excessive use of heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons damages hair cuticles, causing dryness and brittleness.
  • Dry Climate: Living in a dry climate with low humidity causes hair to lose moisture, leading to dryness and frizz.
  • Genetics: Hair texture can be influenced by genetics, with some people naturally having drier hair due to their hair's structure and porosity.
  • Chemical Treatments: Using chemical treatments like hair dye, perms, and relaxers can damage hair cuticles and lead to dryness and breakage.
  • Poor Diet: A diet lacking essential nutrients like omega-3 fatty acids, vitamin E, and zinc can lead to dry, damaged hair.
  • Aging: As people age, their hair's natural oil production decreases, leading to dryness and brittleness.
  • Hard Water: Using hard water to wash hair can strip it of its natural oils and cause dryness due to the high mineral content.
  • Environmental Pollution: Exposure to environmental pollution like air pollution and smoke can damage hair and lead to dryness.
