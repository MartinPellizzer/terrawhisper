1. Arnica flower tincture

Arnica flower tincture helps with dark circles because it contains anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce swelling and puffiness under the eyes.

By improving circulation, arnica flower tincture can help eliminate toxins and reduce the appearance of dark circles.

Additionally, the tincture's anti-oxidant properties can help protect the delicate skin around the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, further reducing the appearance of dark circles.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in arnica flower tincture that aid with dark circles.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Arnica flower tincture helps reduce inflammation, which can alleviate dark circles caused by swollen blood vessels and puffiness around the eyes. The main active constituents responsible for this property are sesquiterpene lactones, such as helenalin and dihydrohelenalin.
  • Vasoconstrictor: Arnica flower tincture constricts blood vessels, which can diminish the appearance of dark circles by decreasing the visibility of dilated blood vessels under the delicate skin around the eyes. The flavonoids and tannins in arnica contribute to its vasoconstrictive properties.
  • Antioxidant: Arnica flower tincture contains antioxidants that help protect the skin from oxidative stress and damage, which can contribute to the development of dark circles. The phenolic acids, flavonoids, and volatile oils in arnica exhibit strong antioxidant activities.
  • Improves microcirculation: Arnica flower tincture enhances local microcirculation, promoting better blood flow and oxygenation in the affected area, which can help diminish the appearance of dark circles. The saponins and essential oils in arnica are believed to be responsible for its microcirculatory effects.
  • Skin-regenerating: Arnica flower tincture supports skin regeneration and healing, which can help improve the overall appearance of the skin around the eyes and reduce the visibility of dark circles. The helenalin derivatives, flavonoids, and essential oils in arnica contribute to its skin-regenerating properties.

The list below shows the primary parts of arnica flower tincture used to make tinctures for dark circles.

  • Flowers: The arnica flower contains flavonoids and volatile oils that can help reduce inflammation and improve circulation, making it effective for reducing the appearance of dark circles.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the arnica plant have similar properties to the flowers and can be used as a gentle astringent to tone and brighten the skin around the eyes.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhyzomes of the arnica plant contain helenalin, a compound that has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it useful for reducing puffiness and discoloration associated with dark circles.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic arnica flower tincture for dark circles.

  1. Gather 2 ounces of dried arnica flowers and place them in a clean glass jar.
  2. Pour 10 ounces of vodka or rum (80 proof) over the arnica flowers, ensuring they are fully submerged.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for two to four weeks, shaking it daily.
  4. After the designated time, strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out the excess liquid.
  5. Store the resulting arnica flower tincture in a clean, dark glass bottle and keep it in a cool, dark place; it will last for up to a year.
