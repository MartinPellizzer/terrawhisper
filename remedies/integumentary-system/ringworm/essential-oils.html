1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds like cineole, terpinen-4-ol, and limonene, which are known for their antifungal and antibacterial properties.

These compounds help to combat the fungus that causes ringworm, Trichophyton, by disrupting its cell membrane and preventing it from spreading. This antifungal activity is attributed to the oil's ability to inhibit the growth of fungal spores, making it difficult for the infection to progress. If you're looking to use Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils for ringworm, apply a few drops of the oil to the affected area using a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil.

You can also add a few drops to a warm bath or use a diffuser to inhale the oil's vapors. However, it's essential to note that ringworm is a fungal infection that requires treatment with antifungal medications, so consult a doctor before using essential oils as a replacement for medical treatment. When used correctly, you can expect the oil's antifungal properties to help reduce the severity of ringworm symptoms, such as itching, redness, and skin lesions. It may also help to prevent the spread of the infection.

However, the oil may not completely eliminate the infection, and it's crucial to follow a proper treatment plan under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
