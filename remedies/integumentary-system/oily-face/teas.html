1. Rosmarinus officinalis teas

Rosmarinus officinalis teas have been used traditionally to treat oily face ailments due to their antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and astringent properties.

These properties help to reduce the production of sebum, balance the skin's pH, and tighten the pores, thereby controlling oiliness and acne.

The bioactive constituents of Rosmarinus officinalis teas, including rosmarinic acid, carnosic acid, and camphor, contribute to their therapeutic effects by reducing inflammation, fighting bacterial and fungal infections, and promoting antioxidant activity.

Regular consumption of Rosmarinus officinalis teas has been shown to have benefits in treating oily face ailments, including reduced oil production, improved skin texture, and a decrease in the occurrence of acne and other skin problems.

Related Study

According to "RSC advances", Rosmarinus officinalis teas for oily face may be beneficial as they contain potent antioxidants and anti-aging properties due to the high total phenolics content in Rosemary, which can help delay skin aging caused by reactive oxygen species.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture into a cup using a tea strainer or cheesecloth.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) to the tea for taste and to help balance the skin.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a week to help reduce oiliness on the face.

Usage:

  1. Wash your face with a gentle cleanser to remove dirt and oils.
  2. Steep 1 teaspoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis in 1 cup of boiling water.
  3. Drink the tea once a day, ideally in the morning.
  4. Use a toner to balance your skin pH after drinking the tea.
  5. Apply a lightweight moisturizer to hydrate and control oil production.

Rosmarinus officinalis teas can be beneficial in treating oily face ailments, but potential side effects may include allergic reactions, skin irritation, and increased heart rate due to its stimulating properties.

To use Rosmarinus officinalis teas safely, take precautions such as starting with small amounts, avoiding use if you have high blood pressure, and being cautious when combining it with other medications or herbal supplements.

2. Aloe barbadensis teas

Aloe barbadensis teas have become a popular herbal remedy for treating oily face ailments due to their soothing and purifying properties.

The anti-inflammatory and astringent properties of this herbal preparation help to reduce oil production and minimize pores, thereby treating oily skin.

The bioactive constituents present in Aloe barbadensis teas, such as aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E, work together to balance the skin's pH, reduce sebum production, and protect the skin from environmental stressors.

The benefits of using Aloe barbadensis teas to treat oily face ailments include a reduction in acne, improved skin texture, and a more balanced skin tone, making it an effective and natural solution for those suffering from oily skin.

Recipe:

  1. Gather ingredients: 1 cup of Aloe barbadensis gel, 1 cup of water, and 1 tablespoon of honey.
  2. Cut the Aloe barbadensis leaf and extract the gel. Strain the gel through a cheesecloth to remove any impurities.
  3. Combine the Aloe gel and water in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and add the honey. Stir well until the honey dissolves.
  5. Let the mixture cool, then strain it into a cup. Drink the tea 2-3 times a week to balance your skin's oil production.

Usage:

  1. Wash your face with mild soap and lukewarm water
  2. Steep 1 teaspoon of Aloe barbadensis in 1 cup of boiling water
  3. Drink the tea once a day, preferably in the morning
  4. Apply aloe vera gel topically to the affected area
  5. Leave the gel on for 30 minutes to an hour before rinsing

Aloe barbadensis teas can be used to treat oily skin, but possible side effects include mild stomach upset and allergic reactions such as redness or itching of the skin.

When using Aloe barbadensis teas to treat oily face, it is essential to start with a small dose and gradually increase as needed, and also to avoid consuming it if you have sensitive stomach or any open wounds on the skin.

Melissa officinalis teas, also known as lemon balm tea, have been traditionally used to treat oily facial skin due to their astringent and antiseptic properties.

Melissa officinalis teas can be used to help alleviate oily facial skin, but it may cause side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, or headaches in some individuals.
