1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Tea tree essential oils helps with oily face because of its natural antibacterial and antifungal properties, which work to balance the skin's pH levels and reduce sebum production.

When used in a toner or serum, tea tree oil can help dissolve excess oil and unclog pores, leaving the skin feeling refreshed and matte.

Additionally, its ability to regulate the skin's natural moisture barrier helps to prevent future oil buildup, keeping the face looking healthy and radiant.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in tea tree essential oils that aid with oily face.

  • Antimicrobial property: Kills bacteria and other microorganisms that can cause acne and inflammation, reducing the risk of breakouts and clogged pores.
  • Anti-inflammatory property: Reduces redness and swelling associated with acne, rosacea, and other skin conditions, helping to calm and soothe the skin.
  • Antioxidant property: Neutralizes free radicals and prevents damage caused by environmental stressors and pollution, which can contribute to oily skin and premature aging.

The list below shows the primary parts of tea tree used to make essential oils for oily face.

  • Leaves: Known for their high concentration of cineole, which helps to reduce sebum production and combat acne.
  • Buds: Contain a higher percentage of cineole than leaves, making them effective in treating mild to moderate acne and reducing inflammation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic tea tree for oily face.

  1. Harvest fresh melaleuca alternifolia leaves at dawn when dew is present for maximum oil content and quality.
  2. Dry the harvested leaves in a warm shade for 7-10 days or use a food dehydrator on low setting for 24 hours.
  3. Steam distill 100g of dried melaleuca alternifolia leaves with 500ml of distilled water at 60°c for 1 hour.
  4. Collect the resulting essential oil and separate it from the distillation water using a separator funnel or filter paper.
  5. Bottle and store the melaleuca alternifolia essential oil in an amber glass bottle, away from direct sunlight and heat sources.
