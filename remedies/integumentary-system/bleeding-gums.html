Bleeding Gums: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Bleeding gums, also known as gingivitis, is a condition characterized by inflammation and bleeding of the gums due to poor oral hygiene or plaque buildup.

This condition can lead to serious health issues such as heart disease, diabetes, and even pregnancy complications. The causes of bleeding gums include poor oral hygiene, smoking, and certain medications.

To alleviate bleeding gums, herbal remedies like aloe vera, chamomile, and tea tree oil can be used to reduce inflammation and kill bacteria. Aloe vera gel can be applied directly to the gums, while chamomile tea can be used as a mouthwash.

However, it's essential to consult a dentist before using medicinal herbs and to follow proper precautions to avoid further irritation.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of bleeding gums, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of bleeding gums?

The main causes of bleeding gums are poor oral hygiene, plaque and tartar buildup, which lead to inflammation and irritation of the gums (gingivitis).

This condition is often caused by inadequate brushing and flossing, resulting in the accumulation of bacteria on the teeth and gumline. Furthermore, gum recession and periodontitis can also lead to bleeding gums due to the destruction of the gum tissue and underlying bone. Other contributing factors include smoking and tobacco use, which reduce blood flow to the gums and increase the risk of gum disease.

Additionally, nutritional deficiencies, such as a lack of vitamin C, can also cause bleeding gums due to the essential role of vitamin C in collagen synthesis and gum health.

  • Poor Oral Hygiene: Poor oral hygiene, including infrequent brushing and flossing, leads to the accumulation of plaque and tartar on the teeth, causing inflammation and bleeding of the gums due to the body's natural response to infection and irritation.
  • Gingivitis: Gingivitis, an inflammation of the gums caused by the bacterial accumulation in plaque, leads to bleeding gums as the body's natural response to infection and irritation, which can be caused by poor oral hygiene or other factors.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes during pregnancy, menstruation, or menopause can cause increased blood flow and sensitivity in the gums, leading to bleeding and inflammation.
  • Smoking: Smoking damages the gums and reduces blood flow to the gums, leading to inflammation and bleeding due to the reduction in the body's ability to fight infection.
  • Genetics: Genetic predisposition can increase the risk of developing gum disease and bleeding gums, as some people may be more susceptible to the effects of plaque and tartar on the teeth.
  • Nutritional Deficiencies: Nutritional deficiencies, particularly a lack of vitamin C, can impair the production of collagen, a protein essential for maintaining healthy gums, leading to bleeding and inflammation.
  • Medications: Certain medications, such as anticoagulants and antidepressants, can cause bleeding gums as a side effect due to their effects on the body's ability to form blood clots and fight infection.
  • Dental Work: Dental work, such as dental surgery or orthodontic appliances, can cause bleeding gums due to the irritation and inflammation caused by the procedure.
  • Systemic Diseases: Systemic diseases, such as diabetes, leukemia, and heart disease, can cause bleeding gums as a symptom of the underlying condition, due to the body's impaired ability to fight infection and maintain healthy blood vessels.
