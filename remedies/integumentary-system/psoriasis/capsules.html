The list below shows the primary active constituents in turmeric capsules that aid with psoriasis.

  • Cyclooxygenase (cox) inhibition: Turmeric's curcumin content inhibits COX-2 enzymes, which are involved in the production of pro-inflammatory molecules that exacerbate psoriatic symptoms like redness, swelling, and pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric for psoriasis.

  1. Harvest fresh turmeric roots from your garden or local market weighing approximately 200 grams.
  2. Dry the harvested turmeric roots in a warm oven at 150 degrees fahrenheit for eight hours.
  3. Grind the dried turmeric powder into a fine texture using a coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
  4. Mix one part of ground turmeric powder with two parts of organic rice flour and add a pinch of sea salt to enhance bioavailability.
  5. Fill empty gelatin capsules with the mixture and seal them immediately to preserve the herbal properties.
