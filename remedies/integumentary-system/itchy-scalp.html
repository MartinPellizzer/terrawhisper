Itchy Scalp: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

An itchy scalp, medically known as pruritus capitis, is a common condition characterized by an unpleasant sensation of itching on the scalp.

It can be caused by various factors such as dandruff, eczema, psoriasis, fungal infections, and allergic reactions to hair care products. If left untreated, an itchy scalp can lead to hair loss, skin irritation, and even mental health issues like anxiety and depression. To alleviate this condition, herbal remedies like neem, tea tree oil, and aloe vera can be used to create medicated shampoos and oils.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as they may interact with other medications or exacerbate underlying conditions, requiring proper guidance on usage and dosages.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of itchy scalp, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of itchy scalp?

The main causes of itchy scalp are typically associated with skin conditions such as dandruff, seborrheic dermatitis, and psoriasis, which are often linked to an overgrowth of yeast-like fungus called Malassezia.

Other common causes include dry scalp, which can result from excessive shampooing, use of harsh chemicals, or cold weather. Additionally, skin conditions like eczema, contact dermatitis, and ringworm can also cause an itchy scalp. Hormonal changes, stress, and certain medical conditions such as thyroid disease and Parkinson's disease can also contribute to itchy scalp.

Furthermore, certain hair care products, especially those containing artificial fragrances and dyes, can irritate the scalp and cause itchiness.

  • Dandruff: Dandruff is a common scalp condition characterized by flakes of dead skin caused by a fungal infection, specifically Malassezia, which feeds on the oils produced by the scalp, leading to irritation and itchiness.
  • Psoriasis: Psoriasis is an autoimmune condition that causes the skin cells to grow at an abnormal rate, leading to thick, scaly patches on the scalp and intense itchiness.
  • Eczema: Eczema, also known as atopic dermatitis, is a chronic inflammatory skin condition that can cause redness, itchiness, and flaking on the scalp, particularly in individuals with a history of allergies or asthma.
  • Seborrheic dermatitis: Seborrheic dermatitis is a skin condition that causes a red, itchy, and flaky rash on the scalp, face, and other oily areas of the body, often triggered by a combination of genetic and environmental factors.
  • Contact dermatitis: Contact dermatitis is a skin reaction to an allergen or irritant, such as shampoos, hair dyes, or styling products, which can cause redness, itchiness, and inflammation on the scalp.
  • Fungal infections: Fungal infections, such as ringworm, can cause a scaly, itchy rash on the scalp, particularly in individuals with compromised immune systems or who engage in activities that increase their risk of fungal exposure.
  • Lice infestation: Lice infestation, also known as pediculosis capitis, is a common scalp condition caused by the presence of parasitic lice, which can cause intense itchiness, redness, and inflammation on the scalp.
  • Tinea capitis: Tinea capitis, also known as ringworm of the scalp, is a fungal infection that causes a circular, itchy patch on the scalp, often accompanied by hair loss and redness.
  • Thyroid issues: Thyroid issues, such as hypothyroidism, can cause an itchy scalp due to changes in hormone levels, which can lead to dryness, flakiness, and irritation on the scalp.
  • Nutritional deficiencies: Nutritional deficiencies, such as a lack of essential fatty acids, zinc, or iron, can cause an itchy scalp due to impaired skin health and function.
