Dry Lips: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Dry lips, also known as chapped lips, refer to the condition where the lips become cracked, flaky, and rough due to a lack of moisture and natural lip oils.

The causes of dry lips include exposure to cold weather, dry air, and harsh lip products. If left untreated, dry lips can lead to discomfort, pain, and even infection. To alleviate dry lips, herbal remedies such as aloe vera, vitamin E oil, and honey can be applied topically.

However, it's essential to note that individuals with sensitive skin or allergies should exercise caution when using medicinal herbs and perform a patch test before applying them to their lips.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of dry lips, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of dry lips?

The main causes of dry lips are due to a combination of external and internal factors, with the primary culprit being the loss of moisture from the skin's surface.

This dehydration is often caused by low humidity levels in the air, leading to a decrease in the skin's natural barrier function and causing the lips to dry out. Internal factors such as hormonal imbalances, particularly the fluctuations in estrogen levels during menopause or menstruation, can also contribute to dry lips. Additionally, certain medical conditions like hypothyroidism, diabetes, and autoimmune disorders can increase the risk of developing dry lips.

Furthermore, the overuse of harsh or fragrant lip products, as well as certain medications like diuretics and beta-blockers, can also strip the skin of its natural oils, exacerbating the issue.

  • Cold Weather: Cold weather is a common cause of dry lips due to the low humidity levels in the air that strip the skin of its natural moisture and cause the lips to dry out.
  • Dry Air: Dry air is another prevalent cause of dry lips as it leads to moisture loss from the skin, causing the lips to become chapped and dry.
  • Dehydration: Dehydration is a significant cause of dry lips as a lack of water in the body affects the skin's ability to retain moisture, leading to dryness and chapping of the lips.
  • Over-Washing: Over-washing the lips with harsh soaps or exfoliants can strip the skin of its natural oils, leading to dryness and irritation.
  • Nutritional Deficiencies: Nutritional deficiencies, particularly a lack of essential fatty acids and vitamins, can impair the skin's ability to retain moisture, contributing to dry lips.
  • Medications: Certain medications, such as diuretics and beta-blockers, can cause dryness in the mouth and lips as a side effect due to their ability to alter the body's water balance.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes during pregnancy, menopause, or other hormonal imbalances can lead to changes in the skin's moisture levels, causing dryness and irritation of the lips.
  • Aging: Aging skin naturally loses its ability to retain moisture, leading to dryness and fine lines, including on the lips.
  • Genetics: Genetic predisposition can contribute to dry lips due to an individual's unique skin characteristics and moisture retention abilities.
  • Allergies: Allergies to certain substances, such as lip balms or soaps, can cause irritation and dryness of the lips.
