What is chickenpox and how it affects your life?

Chickenpox is a highly contagious and potentially debilitating viral illness characterized by an itchy, blistering rash, fever, and fatigue.

It typically affects children, but adults can also contract the disease. The symptoms of chickenpox can be miserable, making it difficult to carry out daily activities. For instance, the intense itching can disrupt sleep patterns and make it challenging to focus on work or school responsibilities.

The fever can lead to dehydration, especially in young children, and may necessitate medical attention. In addition, the rash can leave behind scars and discoloration, affecting self-esteem and body image. Furthermore, chickenpox can also cause secondary infections, such as pneumonia or encephalitis, which can have long-term consequences on overall health and quality of life.

In severe cases, chickenpox can lead to complications like bacterial infections, meningitis, or even brain damage.
