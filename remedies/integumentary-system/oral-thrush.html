What is oral thrush and how it affects your life?

Oral thrush is a fungal infection that occurs on the mucous membranes inside the mouth, causing white patches or lesions to form.

It can affect anyone, but it's more common in babies, older adults, and people with weakened immune systems. Oral thrush can have a significant impact on one's daily life, causing discomfort, pain, and difficulty eating, drinking, and speaking. For instance, it can make it hard to swallow or speak clearly, making everyday tasks like eating and communicating with others challenging.

Additionally, oral thrush can lead to bad breath, dry mouth, and a sore throat, making social interactions and work or school attendance difficult. Furthermore, the discomfort and pain associated with oral thrush can lead to anxiety and stress, affecting one's overall mental health and well-being. In severe cases, oral thrush can even lead to weight loss and malnutrition if it's severe enough to limit food intake.

Overall, oral thrush can have a significant negative impact on one's quality of life, affecting not just physical health but also emotional and social well-being.
