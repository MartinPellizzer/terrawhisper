1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, limonene, and cineole, which have antifungal and antibacterial properties.

These properties help to combat the fungus and bacteria that cause athlete's foot. The tea's antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties can also soothe the skin and reduce irritation. To use Eucalyptus globulus teas for athlete's foot, start by making a tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the tea and soak your feet in it for 15-20 minutes, 2-3 times a day. You can also add the cooled tea to a spray bottle and spray it on the affected area. As you take these actions, you can expect to see a reduction in the fungal growth and symptoms of athlete's foot, such as itching, burning, and cracking.
