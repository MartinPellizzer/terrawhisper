The list below shows the primary parts of lemon verbena tea used to make teas for grey hair.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lemon verbena tea for grey hair.

  1. In a pot, combine 2 cups of water and 1 tablespoon of dried lemon verbena leaves, bringing them to a boil.

How to best treat grey hair with herbal teas?

The best way to treat grey hair with herbal teas is by rinsing your hair with strong brews of herbal infusions.

Chamomile tea brings out natural highlights and adds shine, while sage tea darkens hair and promotes growth. Rooibos tea, rich in antioxidants, helps maintain hair health and can slow down the graying process.

Regular use of these herbal teas as a final rinse after washing your hair can result in softer, stronger, and more vibrant locks.

What ailments similar to grey hair are treated with herbal teas?

Ailments similar to grey hair that are treated with herbal teas include hair loss and damaged hair.

For hair loss, teas made from nettle, rosemary, and ginkgo biloba are often recommended due to their high nutrient content and stimulating effects on the scalp. Damaged hair can be treated with tea rinses made from chamomile, sage, and rooibos, which are rich in antioxidants and help to smooth the hair cuticle, add shine, and promote hair growth. These herbal teas can be used as a rinse after shampooing or as a leave-in treatment, depending on the desired result.

However, it is important to note that while herbal teas can support hair health, they should not be used as a substitute for medical treatment for serious hair conditions.
