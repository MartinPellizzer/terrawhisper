What to know about mouth ulcers before treating it with medicinal herbs

Mouth ulcers, also known as aphthous ulcers, are small, shallow lesions that develop on the soft tissues inside your mouth or on the surface of your lips.

They can cause significant discomfort, making it difficult to eat, drink, and even speak. Negative impacts of mouth ulcers include impaired nutrition, reduced quality of life, and potential psychological distress. Common causes of mouth ulcers include injury, stress, hormonal changes, nutritional deficiencies, and certain medications.

Medicinal herbs that can help alleviate the symptoms of mouth ulcers include licorice root, chamomile, calendula, and echinacea. These herbs can be prepared as teas, tinctures, or topical applications such as gels or ointments. However, it is essential to exercise caution when using herbs for medicinal purposes. What is mouth ulcers and how it affects your life?

Mouth ulcers, also known as canker sores, are small, shallow lesions that develop on the soft tissues inside your mouth or gums.

They can be white, gray, or yellow in color, surrounded by a red border. Mouth ulcers can cause significant discomfort, making it painful to talk, eat, drink, or brush your teeth.

In severe cases, they may even interfere with your ability to sleep or concentrate. The constant pain and discomfort can lead to a loss of appetite, weight loss, and fatigue.

Additionally, mouth ulcers can affect your social life, causing embarrassment and self-consciousness about your appearance, which can impact your confidence and overall quality of life.
