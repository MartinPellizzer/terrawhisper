What is mouth ulcers and how it affects your life?

Mouth ulcers, also known as canker sores or aphthous ulcers, are painful open sores that form on the mucous membranes inside the mouth.

These unsightly and uncomfortable lesions can significantly disrupt daily life, causing embarrassment and self-consciousness. For instance, a person with a mouth ulcer may avoid social interactions or public speaking, fearing others will notice their condition. They may also struggle to eat certain foods, especially spicy or acidic ones, which can exacerbate the discomfort and make meals a chore.

Simple activities like brushing teeth or flossing can become agonizing experiences, leading to avoidance of oral hygiene practices altogether. Furthermore, mouth ulcers can affect one's ability to focus on work or school due to the constant distraction and discomfort they cause. Additionally, the pain and sensitivity can lead to difficulty sleeping, fatigue, and irritability, further impacting daily life and overall well-being.

As a result, individuals with mouth ulcers often feel like their lives are being taken over by this debilitating condition.

What are the main causes of mouth ulcers?

The main causes of mouth ulcers are a combination of factors that can lead to the development of painful sores on the mucous membranes inside the mouth.

Trauma to the mouth, such as biting or chewing on the tongue or cheek, is a common cause, as is irritation from sharp teeth or dentures. Allergic reactions to certain foods, medicines, or other substances can also trigger mouth ulcers.

Additionally, nutritional deficiencies, such as a lack of vitamin B12 or iron, can contribute to their development. Hormonal changes, stress, and infections, including herpes simplex virus and coxsackievirus, are also known triggers.

Furthermore, some people may experience recurring mouth ulcers due to conditions like Crohn's disease, Behçet's syndrome, or other autoimmune disorders.

The most common causes of mouth ulcers are listed below.

  • Apthous Ulcer: A common, non-contagious condition characterized by recurring small sores or ulcers on the lips, tongue, and inner cheeks.
  • Trauma: Bites or scratches to the mouth can cause bleeding and lead to ulcer formation.
  • Food Irritation: Consuming sharp or rough foods like acidic fruits, spicy dishes, or hard candies can cause irritation and ulceration.
  • Dental Work: Dental procedures such as teeth whitening, fillings, or extractions can cause ulcers in the mouth.
  • Viral Infections: Certain viruses like herpes simplex (cold sores), chickenpox, or shingles can cause mouth ulcers.
  • Bacterial and Fungal Infections: Infections like strep throat, tonsillitis, or oral thrush can lead to mouth ulcers.
  • Nutritional Deficiencies: Lack of essential vitamins and minerals like iron, zinc, or B12 can contribute to mouth ulcer development.
  • Gastrointestinal Issues: Conditions such as Crohn's disease, celiac disease, or gastrointestinal infections can cause mouth ulcers.
  • Medications: Certain medications like antibiotics, nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), and beta-blockers can cause mouth ulcers as a side effect.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal fluctuations during menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause can lead to mouth ulcer formation.
