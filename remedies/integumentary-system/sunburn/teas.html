1. Aloe vera tea

Aloe vera tea helps with sunburn because it contains powerful anti-inflammatory compounds called polyphenols, which can help reduce inflammation and redness in sunburned skin.

Additionally, aloe vera has antioxidant properties that can help repair damage caused by UV radiation and promote skin healing. The tea is also hydrating, which can help soothe and moisturize sunburned skin, preventing further dryness and peeling.

Drinking aloe vera tea can also help from the inside out, as it can help boost the immune system and promote overall skin health.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in aloe vera tea that aid with sunburn.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Aloe vera tea contains compounds like bradykinase, which helps reduce inflammation, providing relief from sunburn pain and redness.
  • Moisturizing: Aloe vera tea is rich in polysaccharides and water, making it an effective moisturizer that helps soothe and hydrate sunburnt skin.
  • Antioxidant: Aloe vera tea has antioxidant properties due to its content of vitamins A, C, and E, which help protect skin from free radical damage caused by sun exposure.
  • Wound healing: Aloe vera tea contains compounds like gibberellins and auxins, which promote wound healing and tissue regeneration, aiding in sunburn recovery.
  • Pain relief: Aloe vera tea's analgesic properties, derived from compounds like salicylic acid and magnesium lactate, help alleviate sunburn-related pain and discomfort.

The list below shows the primary parts of aloe vera tea used to make teas for sunburn.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the aloe vera plant contain a gel-like substance that is rich in compounds with anti-inflammatory and moisturizing properties, making it effective for soothing sunburned skin.
  • Stems: The stems of the aloe vera plant contain a sap that can be used to make medicinal tea, and this sap has been shown to have skin-healing and sunburn-relieving properties.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the aloe vera plant contain nectar that can be used to make a medicinal tea, and this tea has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving effects, which can help to alleviate the discomfort of sunburn.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic aloe vera tea for sunburn.

  1. Slice two leaves from an aloe vera plant, then use a knife to scrape the gel into a pot, adding 2 cups of water and 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes to allow the chamomile to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea into a teapot or container, discarding the used aloe vera gel and chamomile flowers.
  4. Add honey or lemon juice to taste, if desired, and let the tea cool down to a warm temperature that's comfortable for sunburned skin.
  5. Soak a clean cloth in the tea and apply it to the sunburned areas for 10-15 minutes, repeating as needed every few hours.
