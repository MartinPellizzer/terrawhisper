9 Best Herbal Teas For Sunburn

Herbal teas for sunburn are a natural remedy that involves brewing various herbs to create a soothing and cooling liquid that can help alleviate the symptoms of sunburn, such as redness, itching, and pain.

These herbal teas can treat sunburn effectively due to their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce the damage caused by UV radiation and promote the healing process.

Examples of herbal teas used to treat sunburn include chamomile, which calms the skin and reduces inflammation; peppermint, which cools and refreshes the skin; aloe vera, which soothes and hydrates the skin; and calendula, which promotes wound healing and reduces pain; as well as licorice root, green tea, and lavender, which also possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

By drinking these herbal teas or applying them topically as a compress, individuals can find relief from the discomfort and pain associated with sunburn.

1. Aloe barbadensis teas

Aloe barbadensis teas have been traditionally used to treat sunburn ailments due to their soothing, anti-inflammatory, and moisturizing properties.

This herbal preparation helps to treat sunburn by reducing redness, swelling, and pain, promoting skin hydration and accelerating the healing process.

The bioactive constituents of Aloe barbadensis teas, such as aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A and E, work together to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects that help to alleviate sunburn symptoms.

The benefits of using Aloe barbadensis teas to treat sunburn include faster recovery, reduced risk of infection, and minimal scarring, making it a popular natural remedy for sunburn relief.

Featured Study

According to the study, Aloe barbadensis teas for sunburn may potentially benefit treatment due to its diverse mechanism of action, antibacterial activity, safety of use, and cost-effectiveness.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of water, 1 cup of fresh Aloe barbadensis leaves, and a pot.
  2. Cut the Aloe leaves into small pieces and add them to the pot.
  3. Pour in the cup of water and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat, let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, then strain the liquid.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the Aloe tea, 2-3 times a day, to help soothe sunburned skin.

Usage:

  1. Soak a cloth in cooled Aloe barbadensis tea.
  2. Gently apply the cloth to sunburned skin for 15-30 minutes.
  3. Repeat application 2-3 times a day as needed.
  4. Drink 1 cup of cooled Aloe barbadensis tea 2-3 times a day.
  5. Consume 1 tablespoon of Aloe barbadensis gel 2-3 times a day.

Aloe barbadensis teas can be an effective remedy for treating sunburn, but it may cause minor side effects such as stomach upset, diarrhea, or allergic reactions in some individuals, especially when consumed in excess or without proper preparation.

To use Aloe barbadensis teas safely, it is recommended to start with small doses, avoid consuming it if you have sensitive stomach or allergies, and be cautious when combining it with other medications or herbal supplements.

2. Hypericum perforatum teas

Hypericum perforatum teas, also known as St. John's Wort, have been traditionally used to treat sunburn and its associated symptoms.

This herbal preparation helps to treat sunburn by reducing inflammation, soothing skin irritation, and promoting healing due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The bioactive constituents of Hypericum perforatum teas, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, contribute to its therapeutic effects.

The benefits of using Hypericum perforatum teas to treat sunburn include reduced redness and swelling, accelerated wound healing, and improved skin health.

Featured Study

According to "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Hypericum perforatum teas for sunburn have been shown to have beneficial effects, supported by 5 laboratory studies and 3 clinical trials.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Hypericum perforatum leaves or 2 tablespoons of dried leaves.
  2. Measure 1 cup of boiling water and pour it over the leaves in a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Steep the leaves for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid into another cup.
  4. Discard the leaves and let the tea cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe sunburned skin.

Usage:

  1. Soak a clean cloth in cooled tea, wring out excess liquid.
  2. Apply the cloth to the sunburned area for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Repeat application 2-3 times a day, as needed.
  4. Drink 1/2 cup of Hypericum perforatum tea, 2-3 times a day.
  5. Continue use for 3-5 days, or until sunburn subsides.

Hypericum perforatum teas can be used to treat sunburn, but its use may cause side effects such as increased sensitivity to sunlight, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal issues like nausea and diarrhea.

To use Hypericum perforatum teas safely, take them in moderation, avoid prolonged exposure to the sun after consumption, and start with small doses to monitor any adverse reactions.

Recipe:

  4. Let the tea cool down to a comfortable temperature for applying to sunburned skin.
  5. Soak a clean cloth in the cooled tea and gently apply it to the affected area, repeating as needed.

Usage:

  2. Strain and let the tea cool to room temperature.
  3. Apply the cooled tea to affected sunburn areas with a clean cloth.
  4. Repeat application 2-3 times a day, for up to 5 days.
  5. Increase frequency if needed, but not exceeding 4 cups per day.

Calendula officinalis teas can be used to treat sunburn, but possible side effects may include skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased sensitivity to sunlight, which may worsen the condition.

To avoid these side effects, it's recommended to patch test the tea on a small area of skin before using it on a larger area, and to avoid using it on broken skin or applying it too frequently, as this may lead to prolonged irritation.

  5. Repeat tea preparation as directed for best results

Silybum marianum teas can be used to treat sunburn, but possible side effects include gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications such as blood thinners and diabetes medications.

When using Silybum marianum teas to treat sunburn, precautions include starting with a low dose and gradually increasing it, avoiding consumption if you have a known allergy to plants in the Asteraceae family, and monitoring your body's response to potential allergic reactions.

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, derived from the leaves of the rosemary plant, have been traditionally used to treat sunburn due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

  5. Strain the tea into another cup and let it cool. You can drink it warm or cold, sweetened with honey if desired.

Usage:

Rosmarinus officinalis teas can be beneficial in treating sunburn due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, but it may cause side effects such as stomach upset, diarrhea, and allergic reactions in some individuals.

Usage:

Taraxacum officinale teas can be used to treat sunburn, but it may cause side effects such as mild allergic reactions, increased heart rate, and gastrointestinal upset in some individuals.

To avoid potential issues, individuals should be cautious when consuming Taraxacum officinale teas to treat sunburn, as excessive use can exacerbate dehydration and interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications.
