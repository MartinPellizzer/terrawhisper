The plant's leaves contain a unique combination of flavonoids, terpenes, and saponins that work together to reduce redness, itching, and swelling in the eyes. When used as an eyewash or added to a warm compress, mullein decoctions can calm irritated conjunctiva and relieve dry, itchy eyes caused by allergies, wind, or exposure to environmental irritants.

Its natural anti-inflammatory agents also help to alleviate puffiness and promote healthy tear production.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in mullein decoctions that aid with eye irritation.

  • Anti-inflammatory compounds: Mullein decoctions contain anti-inflammatory compounds such as rosmarinic acid and caffeic acid, which help reduce swelling and redness in the eyes, providing relief from irritation.
  • Antioxidant activity: The antioxidants present in mullein decoctions, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, neutralize free radicals that can cause oxidative stress and damage to the delicate tissues of the eye, helping to soothe and protect the eyes.
  • Soothing and moisturizing mucilage: Mullein's mucilage (a thick, gel-like substance) provides a protective coating for the eyes, soothing dryness, irritation, and itchiness while also helping to lubricate the ocular surface.

The list below shows the primary parts of mullein used to make decoctions for eye irritation.

  • Roots: Mullein roots are rich in mucilage, which helps soothe and calm irritated eyes.
  • Flowers: The flowers contain flavonoids, antioxidants that have anti-inflammatory properties, reducing redness and swelling in the eyes.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic mullein for eye irritation.

  1. Harvest fresh or dried verbascum thapsus leaves and flowers for preparation.
  2. Rinse the harvested plant material under cold running water.
  3. Chop the fresh or dried verbascum thapsus leaves and flowers into smaller pieces.
  4. Combine the chopped plant material with boiling water in a heat-resistant container.
  5. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve and discard solids.

  • Astringent and soothing properties: Lemongrass decoctions contain astringent compounds like citral and geraniol that help shrink swollen tissues, reduce puffiness, and provide a calming effect on the eyes, relieving itchiness, irritation, and burning sensations.

The list below shows the primary parts of lemongrass used to make decoctions for eye irritation.

  • Roots: The roots are used due to their high concentration of citral, a compound that helps to reduce inflammation and soothe eye irritations.
  • Buds: Lemongrass buds are rich in essential oils, which provide anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate eye discomfort and redness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lemongrass for eye irritation.

  1. Harvest high-quality fresh leaves of cymbopogon citratus and rinse them thoroughly under cold running water.
  2. Combine one teaspoon of the cleaned leaves with two cups of boiling distilled water in a heat-resistant glass container.
  3. Steep the mixture for 15-20 minutes in a warm location away from direct sunlight and heat sources.
  4. Strain the decoction through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle discarding the solids.
  5. Allow the decoction to cool to room temperature before refrigerating it for up to 3 days for later use.
