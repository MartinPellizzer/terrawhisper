Aloe tinctures helps with eye irritation because of its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

Medicinal Constituents

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Aloe vera tinctures that help with eye irritation.

  • Chromones: Chromones, specifically aloin and aloe-emodin, have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that help to reduce redness and swelling associated with eye irritation.
  • Flavonoids: Flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects that help to protect the eyes from oxidative stress and inflammation caused by eye irritation.

Parts Used

The list below shows the primary parts of aloe used to make tinctures for eye irritation.

  • Buds: Contain aloin, a compound that has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help to soothe and protect the eyes from irritation and infection.
  • Stems: Contain aloe vera gel, a soothing and moisturizing compound that helps to calm and protect the eyes from irritation and dryness.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic aloe for eye irritation.

  1. Harvest 50-100 mature aloe vera leaves with thick green skin for tincture making.
  2. Cut the leaves into small pieces and soak them in 80 proof vodka for 4 weeks.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth into a clean glass jar 3 times a day.
  4. Discard the solids and store the clear liquid in a dark glass bottle in the refrigerator.
  5. Use the tincture within 2 years and take 20-30 drops in water 2-3 times a day.
