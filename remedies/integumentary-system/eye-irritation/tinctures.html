1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis tinctures contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help soothe and calm eye irritation.

The main constituents, such as triterpenoids and flavonoids, work together to reduce swelling and fight off infections that can cause redness and discomfort. When you experience eye irritation, try applying a few drops of Calendula officinalis tincture to a clean cloth and gently press it onto your closed eyes for 5-10 minutes.

This can help to reduce the inflammation and promote healing. As the tincture works, you may notice a decrease in redness and swelling, and your eyes may feel more comfortable and refreshed. It's essential to dilute the tincture with water first, as undiluted tinctures can be too strong for the delicate skin around your eyes.

Also, be sure to perform a patch test before using it to ensure you don't have any sensitivity or allergic reactions.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea tinctures contains properties that can help with eye irritation, such as flavonoids, alkylamides, and glycosides.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which can help soothe and calm the irritated eyes. The flavonoids, specifically, have been shown to reduce inflammation and protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

When using Echinacea purpurea tinctures for eye irritation, it's recommended to dilute the tincture with water and apply it to a clean cloth or cotton pad, then gently apply it to the affected eye. If you experience eye irritation, try applying the tincture 2-3 times a day, and monitor the symptoms for improvement.
