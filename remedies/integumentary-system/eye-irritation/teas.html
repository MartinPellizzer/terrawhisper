1. Eyebright tea

Eyebright tea helps with eye irritation because it contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, iridoid glycosides, and tannins that have anti-inflammatory, astringent, and antioxidant properties.

These compounds can help reduce redness, swelling, and inflammation in the eyes, as well as soothe irritation and itchiness. Additionally, eyebright tea has been traditionally used as a natural remedy for various eye conditions, including conjunctivitis, styes, and blepharitis.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in eyebright tea that aid with eye irritation.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Eyebright tea's anti-inflammatory property, due to its tannins and flavonoids, helps reduce eye irritation and inflammation.
  • Astringent: Eyebright tea's astringent effect, caused by its tannins, can help alleviate eye irritation by tightening and soothing swollen tissue.
  • Antihistamine: Eyebright tea's antihistamine properties, from its bioflavonoids, can help relieve eye irritation by reducing allergic reactions.
  • Antimicrobial: Eyebright tea's antimicrobial property, attributed to its tannins and volatile oils, can help alleviate eye irritation by fighting bacterial and viral infections.
  • Analgesic: Eyebright tea's analgesic effect, due to its phenolic compounds, can help reduce eye irritation by alleviating pain and discomfort.

The list below shows the primary parts of eyebright tea used to make teas for eye irritation.

  • Flowers: Rich in anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, eyebright flowers are excellent for soothing eye irritation.
  • Leaves: Containing active compounds that reduce redness and irritation, eyebright leaves are commonly used in medicinal tea.
  • Seeds: Packed with therapeutic benefits, eyebright seeds can help alleviate symptoms of eye irritation when used in tea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic eyebright tea for eye irritation.

  1. In a small pot, boil 1 cup of water for 3-5 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried eyebright herb to the boiling water.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid to remove the herb particles.
  5. Allow the tea to cool down before using it as a compress on irritated eyes.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic plantain tea for eye irritation.

  1. Harvest 2-3 large plantain leaves ( Plantago major), rinse, and chop coarsely.
  2. In a saucepan, combine 2 cups of water and chopped leaves, bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat, simmer for 15 minutes, then strain and discard leaves.
  4. Allow tea to cool down to a warm temperature.
  5. Soak a clean cloth in the tea, wring out excess, and apply as a compress to irritated eyes for 10-15 minutes.
