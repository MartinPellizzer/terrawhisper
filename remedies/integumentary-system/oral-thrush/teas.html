8 Herbal Teas For Oral Thrush

If you're dealing with oral thrush, a fungal infection that causes white patches on your tongue and mouth, herbal teas can be a helpful solution.

Oral thrush occurs when the balance of bacteria in your mouth gets disrupted, allowing the fungus to overgrow. Certain herbs have antifungal and antibacterial properties that can help restore this balance and alleviate symptoms. One example is ginger (Zingiber officinale), which has been traditionally used to soothe digestive issues and reduce inflammation. Drinking ginger tea may help ease oral thrush by reducing inflammation and fighting the fungus. Licorice root (Glycyrrhiza glabra) is another herb that can be beneficial for oral thrush. It contains compounds that have antifungal and antibacterial properties, which can help combat the infection and promote healing.

Licorice root tea may also help reduce inflammation and soothe irritated tissues in the mouth. Eucalyptus (Eucalyptus globulus) is a popular herb used in many herbal teas, and it has antifungal properties that can help combat oral thrush. Drinking eucalyptus tea may help reduce inflammation and ease congestion in the mouth, making it easier to manage symptoms and promote healing. When you drink herbal teas for oral thrush, you can expect several benefits. You may experience reduced inflammation and swelling, making it more comfortable to eat and speak. Your mouth may feel cleaner and fresher, and you may be able to manage symptoms more effectively.

Additionally, some herbs like licorice root have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce stress and promote overall well-being.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contain compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have potent antifungal properties.

The anti-inflammatory properties of Zingiber officinale teas may also reduce swelling and discomfort in the mouth and throat. To use Zingiber officinale teas for oral thrush, mix 1 teaspoon of dried ginger root in a cup of boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and use it as a mouthwash 2-3 times a day.

You can also drink it as a warm tea, 1-2 cups a day. However, if you're pregnant or breastfeeding, consult with your doctor before consuming it. When using Zingiber officinale teas for oral thrush, you may experience a reduction in fungal growth and inflammation within a few days.
