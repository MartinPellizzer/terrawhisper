1. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas contains flavonoids, triterpenoids, and carotenoids, which provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help soothe and heal chapped lips.

The tea's high concentration of flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, helps reduce inflammation and irritation, while its antioxidant properties combat free radicals that can damage lip tissue. The tea's carotenoids, including beta-carotene, also promote collagen production, which can help repair and regenerate damaged lip cells.

To use Calendula officinalis tea for chapped lips, you can steep 1 teaspoon of dried flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then let it cool. Soak a cotton swab in the tea and gently apply it to your chapped lips 2-3 times a day. As the tea's active compounds are absorbed into your skin, you can expect to see a reduction in inflammation and discomfort, followed by an improvement in lip texture and appearance.

The tea's moisturizing properties can help lock in moisture, leaving your lips feeling soft and supple.
