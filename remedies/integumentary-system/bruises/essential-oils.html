  • Roots: Mountain arnica roots contain bioactive compounds that help reduce inflammation and alleviate pain, making them ideal for treating bruise-related discomfort.
  • Buds: Arnica buds are rich in sesquiterpenes, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can effectively soothe and calm irritated skin, promoting healing from bruises.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic mountain arnica for bruises.

  1. Harvest high-quality fresh arnica montana flowers with no signs of mold or mildew within the morning dew hours.
  2. Dry the harvested flowers in a warm, dry place for at least seven days or until they become brittle and crumbly.
  3. Mix 100 grams of dried arnica montana flowers with 500 milliliters of carrier oil such as sweet almond or grapeseed oil.
  4. Steep the mixture in a double boiler set over low heat for two hours, allowing the essential oils to infuse into the carrier oil.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean glass container and store it in a dark place for up to six months after preparation time.
