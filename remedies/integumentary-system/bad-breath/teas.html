1. Peppermint tea

Peppermint tea helps with bad breath because it contains menthol, a compound that has natural antibacterial and antiseptic properties.

These properties can help kill the bacteria in your mouth that cause bad breath. Additionally, peppermint tea can freshen your breath by leaving a cool, minty taste in your mouth. Therefore, drinking peppermint tea can not only help treat bad breath, but also prevent it from occurring in the first place.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that aid with bad breath.

  • Antimicrobial: Peppermint tea contains antimicrobial compounds, such as menthol and limonene, that help reduce the growth of bacteria responsible for bad breath in the mouth.
  • Anti-inflammatory: The anti-inflammatory properties of peppermint tea, due to rosmarinic acid and flavonoids, can help decrease gum inflammation and redness, which can contribute to bad breath.
  • Fresh breath: Peppermint tea's refreshing aroma, primarily from menthol, immediately masks bad breath odors and leaves a pleasant, long-lasting minty scent.
  • Salivation stimulation: Peppermint tea promotes saliva production, which helps wash away bacteria and food particles that cause bad breath.
  • Digestive aid: Peppermint tea can alleviate digestive issues like bloating, gas, and indigestion, which can indirectly improve bad breath by reducing the production of gases that contribute to halitosis.

The list below shows the primary parts of peppermint tea used to make teas for bad breath.

  • Leaves: Rich in menthol and menthone, which have antibacterial properties that can help freshen breath.
  • Buds: High in essential oils, which have antimicrobial properties that can help eliminate the cause of bad breath.
  • Flowers: Contain flavonoids that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help promote oral health and freshen breath.
  • Seeds: Contain essential oils that have antiseptic properties, which can help kill the bacteria that cause bad breath.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for bad breath.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 cup of boiling water with 1 tablespoon of dried peppermint leaves.
  2. Steep the mixture for 5-10 minutes to allow the active compounds to be released.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the spent peppermint leaves.
  4. Optionally, add a small amount of honey or lemon juice to improve the taste.
  5. Consume the tea slowly, allowing it to coat your mouth and freshen your breath.
