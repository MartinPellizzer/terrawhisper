  • Antioxidant: Eyebright tea contains antioxidants that help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to eye infections, making it a valuable ally for maintaining eye health.
  • Analgesic: Eyebright tea has pain-relieving properties that can help alleviate discomfort associated with eye infections, thanks to its active constituents like salicylic acid derivatives and flavonoids.

The list below shows the primary parts of eyebright tea used to make teas for eye infection.

  • Seeds: Packed with therapeutic compounds, the seeds of the eyebright tea plant are used to make tea that helps alleviate symptoms of eye infections such as redness, itching, and discharge.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic eyebright tea for eye infection.

  1. In a saucepan, combine 1 cup of water and 2 teaspoons of dried eyebright herb, then bring the mixture to a boil.
  2. Reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solid herb material.
  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable drinking temperature.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, sipping it slowly to let it fully coat the mouth and throat.
