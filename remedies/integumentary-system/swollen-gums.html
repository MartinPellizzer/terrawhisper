Swollen Gums: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Swollen gums, also known as gingivitis or gum inflammation, are inflamed and swollen tissues surrounding the teeth due to infection, poor dental hygiene, or other underlying health conditions.

This condition can be caused by factors such as poor oral hygiene, smoking, or nutritional deficiencies, and can lead to severe health issues like heart disease, diabetes, and respiratory infections. To alleviate swollen gums, herbal remedies like aloe vera, tea tree oil, and eucalyptus oil can be used in the form of mouthwashes or topical applications.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as some may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions, and to use them in moderation to avoid adverse reactions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of swollen gums, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of swollen gums?

The main causes of swollen gums are bacterial infections, such as periodontitis, which occurs when plaque builds up and leads to inflammation of the gums.

Poor oral hygiene, including inadequate brushing and flossing, can contribute to swollen gums by allowing bacteria to accumulate. Hormonal changes during pregnancy or menstruation can also cause swollen gums due to increased blood flow and sensitivity. Smoking and tobacco use can exacerbate gum inflammation and lead to swollen gums.

Additionally, certain medical conditions, such as diabetes, heart disease, and autoimmune disorders, can increase the risk of developing swollen gums.

  • Poor Oral Hygiene: Poor oral hygiene is the most common cause of swollen gums due to the accumulation of plaque and tartar on teeth, leading to inflammation and irritation of the gum tissues.
  • Gingivitis: Gingivitis is a bacterial infection of the gums that causes inflammation and swelling, often resulting from poor oral hygiene or other underlying conditions.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes during puberty, menopause, or pregnancy can cause fluctuations in estrogen levels, leading to inflammation and swelling of the gums.
  • Nutritional Deficiencies: Nutritional deficiencies, particularly a lack of vitamin C or other essential nutrients, can cause weakened immune function and lead to gum inflammation and swelling.
  • Smoking: Smoking is a significant risk factor for gum disease and swollen gums due to its damaging effects on blood flow and immune function, making it more challenging for the body to fight off infections.
  • Medications: Certain medications, such as anticonvulsants, antidepressants, and immunosuppressants, can cause swollen gums as a side effect due to their effects on the body's immune system and blood vessels.
  • Genetic Predisposition: Genetic predisposition can increase the risk of developing gum disease and swollen gums due to inherited traits that may affect the body's ability to fight off infections.
  • Systemic Diseases: Systemic diseases, such as diabetes, heart disease, and kidney disease, can cause gum inflammation and swelling due to the body's decreased ability to fight off infections and heal wounds.
  • Infections: Infections, such as bacterial or viral infections, can cause swollen gums due to the body's immune response to the invading pathogens.
  • Tumors or Cysts: Tumors or cysts in the mouth can cause swollen gums due to the abnormal growth and pressure on surrounding tissues.
