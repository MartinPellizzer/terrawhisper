What is swollen gums and how it affects your life?

Swollen gums, also known as gingivitis, is a common oral health condition characterized by inflammation and swelling of the gums.

This condition can cause discomfort, pain, and tenderness in the gums, making everyday activities challenging. For instance, eating spicy or acidic foods can be unbearable, and simple actions like brushing or flossing teeth can be excruciating.

Swollen gums can also lead to bad breath, affecting one's self-esteem and social relationships. Furthermore, this condition can cause fatigue and irritability, making it difficult to concentrate at work or school. In addition, swollen gums can make it hard to eat or speak properly, which can be embarrassing in public situations.

Moreover, if left untreated, swollen gums can lead to more severe health issues, such as tooth loss, heart disease, and diabetes, negatively impacting one's overall quality of life and increasing the risk of complications.
