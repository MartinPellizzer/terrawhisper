1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains compounds like alkylamides, caffeic acid, and rosmarinic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling and calm the skin, which can ease itchy eyes caused by allergies or irritants. The rosmarinic acid in Echinacea purpurea teas also has a soothing effect on the eyes, reducing redness and inflammation. To try Echinacea purpurea teas for itchy eyes, steep 1 teaspoon of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers in 1 cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Let the tea cool, then use it as an eye wash or apply a soaked cotton ball to the affected area. It's essential to note that Echinacea purpurea teas may not completely eliminate itchy eyes but can provide relief from symptoms. Some people may experience reduced redness and itchiness after using Echinacea purpurea teas, while others may need to try it multiple times to see results.

It's also crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Echinacea purpurea teas, especially if you have a pre-existing medical condition or take medications.

2. Achillea millefolium

Achillea millefolium teas contains anti-inflammatory compounds like sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids, which are known to soothe and calm irritated eyes.

These compounds help reduce swelling and ease pain, making it a natural remedy for itchy eyes. When you apply the cooled tea to your itchy eyes, you can expect to experience a reduction in itching and redness within a few minutes. 3. Calendula officinalis

Calendula officinalis teas contains a wealth of properties that can help alleviate itchy eyes.
