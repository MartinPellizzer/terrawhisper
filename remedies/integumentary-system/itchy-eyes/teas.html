Recipe:

  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of Melissa officinalis leaves and flowers and place them in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.

  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the leaves. You can add honey to taste if needed.
  5. Cool the tea to room temperature before applying it to your itchy eyes using a clean cloth or cotton ball.

Usage:

  3. Steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the tea.
  4. Soak a cotton pad or cloth in the cooled tea.
  5. Apply the soaked pad to itchy eyes 2-3 times a day.

Melissa officinalis teas can be used to treat itchy eyes, but possible side effects may include dizziness, nausea, and allergic reactions in some individuals, especially those with sensitivities to plants in the Lamiaceae family.
