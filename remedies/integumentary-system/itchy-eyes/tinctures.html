1. Eyebright tincture

Eyebright tincture helps with itchy eyes because it contains anti-inflammatory and astringent properties that can reduce redness, swelling, and irritation.

This herbal remedy has been used for centuries to treat various eye ailments, including itchy eyes. The active compounds in eyebright, such as tannins and flavonoids, can help soothe irritated eyes and alleviate discomfort.

Additionally, eyebright has natural antimicrobial and antioxidant properties that can protect the eyes from environmental stressors and infections, further contributing to its ability to relieve itchy eyes.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in eyebright tincture that aid with itchy eyes.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Eyebright tincture reduces inflammation, which is important for itchy eyes as it helps alleviate redness, swelling, and discomfort caused by inflammation. Active constituents include flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids.
  • Astringent: Eyebright tincture tightens and strengthens tissues, which is important for itchy eyes as it can help reduce eye watering and irritation. The astringent property is attributed to tannins present in eyebright.
  • Antimicrobial: Eyebright tincture inhibits bacterial and fungal growth, which is important for itchy eyes as it helps prevent and treat eye infections that can cause itching. The antimicrobial properties are due to the presence of phenolic acids and flavonoids.
  • Antihistamine: Eyebright tincture reduces histamine release, which is important for itchy eyes as it helps alleviate itching, redness, and watering caused by allergies. The antihistamine effect is attributed to the flavonoids and alkaloids found in eyebright.
  • Analgesic: Eyebright tincture has pain-relieving properties, which is important for itchy eyes as it helps reduce discomfort and pain. The analgesic effect is due to the presence of alkaloids and tannins in eyebright.

The list below shows the primary parts of eyebright tincture used to make tinctures for itchy eyes.

  • Flowers: Rich in active compounds, eyebright flowers are commonly used in tinctures for their anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, providing relief for itchy eyes.
  • Leaves: High in therapeutic constituents, eyebright leaves contribute to tinctures with their soothing, anti-pruritic (anti-itch) properties, helping to alleviate eye irritation.
  • Stems: Though less potent, eyebright stems still contain beneficial compounds that can aid in the production of tinctures, providing additional support for reducing eye inflammation and itchiness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic eyebright tincture for itchy eyes.

  1. Chop 2 ounces of dried eyebright herb and place it in a clean glass jar.
  2. Pour 1 pint of vodka or grain alcohol (80-100 proof) over the herb, ensuring it is fully submerged.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking it gently every few days.
  4. Strain the tincture using cheesecloth or a fine mesh strainer, squeezing out any excess liquid.
  5. Store the final tincture in a clean, dark glass bottle and use a few drops in each eye as needed for itchy eyes.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic plantain tincture for itchy eyes.
