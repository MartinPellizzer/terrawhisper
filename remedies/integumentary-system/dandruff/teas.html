1. Rosmarinus officinalis teas

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, derived from the leaves of the rosemary plant, have been used to treat dandruff due to their anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.

The herbal preparation helps to treat this ailment by soothing the scalp, reducing flaking and itching, and promoting a healthy scalp environment.

The bioactive constituents of Rosmarinus officinalis teas, including camphor, bornyl acetate, and 1,8-cineole, possess antifungal and antimicrobial activities that inhibit the growth of fungi and bacteria that contribute to dandruff.

Regular consumption of Rosmarinus officinalis teas may help to alleviate dandruff symptoms, promote a balanced scalp microbiome, and improve overall scalp health.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and flowers.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of the leaves and flowers, and place them in a heat-resistant cup or teapot.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves and flowers.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-10 minutes, then strain the liquid into another cup.
  5. Drink the tea 1-2 times a day, after shampooing, to help control dandruff and soothe the scalp.

Usage:

  1. Wash hair with shampoo before using Rosmarinus officinalis tea.
  2. Use 1 tablespoon of dried Rosmarinus officinalis leaves per cup of boiling water.
  3. Steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid.
  4. Use the tea as a final rinse after shampooing, 2-3 times a week.
  5. Leave the tea on hair for 1-2 minutes before rinsing with cold water.

Rosmarinus officinalis teas can be used to treat dandruff, but it may cause gastrointestinal side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea in some individuals, especially when consumed in large quantities.

When using Rosmarinus officinalis teas to treat dandruff, it's essential to start with a low dose and gradually increase as needed, and also be cautious when consuming it with other medications or substances that may interact with its active compounds.

Recipe:
