1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Tea tree essential oils helps with dandruff because of its powerful antifungal and antibacterial properties.

The oil's active compound, terpinen-4-ol, has been shown to reduce the growth of Malassezia fungi, a common cause of dandruff. Additionally, tea tree oil's antiseptic properties help soothe itchy scalps and calm inflammation, reducing flaking and scaling.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in tea tree essential oils that aid with dandruff.

  • Antifungal properties: Tea Tree Essential Oil has been shown to be effective against fungal infections, including those that can cause dandruff and other scalp conditions, by preventing the growth of fungi and promoting a healthy scalp environment.
  • Anti-inflammatory properties: The oil's anti-inflammatory compounds help reduce redness, irritation, and swelling associated with dandruff, allowing for a more comfortable and manageable condition.
  • Antioxidant properties: Tea Tree Essential Oil's antioxidant properties help protect the scalp from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to inflammation and exacerbate dandruff symptoms, promoting a healthy and balanced scalp ecosystem.

The list below shows the primary parts of tea tree used to make essential oils for dandruff.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the tea tree plant are rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, making them effective in treating scalp irritation and reducing flaking associated with dandruff.
  • Buds: Tea tree buds contain a high concentration of cineole, an active compound that has natural antifungal and antibacterial properties, helping to combat fungal infections and soothe itchy scalps.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic tea tree for dandruff.

  1. Harvest fresh leaves from the melaleuca alternifolia plant at dawn or just before sunrise for maximum potency.
  2. Dry the harvested leaves in a single layer on paper towels or clean cloth for 24 to 48 hours.
  3. Steam distill the dried melaleuca leaves with 100ml of distilled water for 1 hour and 30 minutes to extract essential oil.
  4. Collect and filter the extracted essential oil through coffee filters or paper towels to remove impurities and sediment.
  5. Store the resulting melaleuca alternifolia essential oil in dark glass bottles with tight-fitting lids at room temperature for up to 6 months.
