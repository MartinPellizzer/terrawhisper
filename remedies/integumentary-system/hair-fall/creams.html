Aloe creams helps with hair fall because they promote healthy scalp conditions, reducing inflammation and itchiness that can lead to excessive shedding.

  • Antioxidant properties: Aloe vera's antioxidant compounds, including vitamins C and E, beta-carotene, and other phenolic acids, neutralize free radicals that can damage hair follicles and cause hair fall, promoting healthy scalp and hair growth.
  • Wound-healing and soothing properties: Aloe vera's gel contains acemannan, a complex sugar that promotes wound healing by stimulating collagen synthesis and improving tissue elasticity, which can help soothe an itchy or irritated scalp and promote healthy hair growth.

The list below shows the primary parts of aloe used to make creams for hair fall.

  • Roots: Rich in antioxidants and minerals that help to strengthen hair follicles and reduce dandruff.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic aloe for hair fall.

  1. Collect pure aloe vera gel from 2-3 ripe aloe vera leaves and extract its juice using a cheese cloth or fine mesh strainer.
  2. Mix the extracted aloe vera juice with 20 grams of coconut oil and 10 grams of olive oil in a small saucepan over medium heat.
  3. Add 5 grams of glycerin, 2 grams of vitamin e oil, and 1 gram of biotin powder to the mixture while stirring constantly for 10 minutes.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool down completely before transferring the mixture to a sterilized glass jar for storage.
  5. Allow the mixture to infuse for at least 2 weeks by storing it in a cool dark place, shaking the jar every day before using as hair cream.
