1. Rosemary tea

Rosemary tea helps with hair fall because it is rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that promote hair growth.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in rosemary tea that aid with hair fall.

  • Prevents premature graying: Rosemary tea contains antioxidants that protect hair follicles from oxidative stress, which can cause premature graying and hair fall. The antioxidant properties come from rosmarinic acid and caffeic acid present in rosemary.
  • Reduces scalp inflammation: Rosemary tea has anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe an irritated scalp, reducing inflammation and dandruff, which can lead to hair fall. The anti-inflammatory effects are due to the presence of compounds like carnosol and carnosic acid.
  • Strengthens hair: Rosemary tea contains nutrients that help to strengthen hair, reducing breakage and promoting healthy hair growth. The strengthening properties can be attributed to the presence of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants in rosemary.
  • Balances hormones: Rosemary tea has been shown to balance hormones, particularly androgens, which can contribute to hair fall. By maintaining hormonal balance, rosemary tea helps promote healthy hair growth and reduces hair fall. The hormone-balancing effects are attributed to the presence of rosmarinic acid and other polyphenolic compounds in rosemary.

The list below shows the primary parts of rosemary tea used to make teas for hair fall.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, rosemary leaves help promote hair growth and reduce hair fall.
  • Flowers: Containing essential oils and antioxidants, rosemary flowers can improve blood circulation in the scalp, thereby reducing hair fall.
  • Seeds: Packed with nutrients, rosemary seeds can help nourish the hair follicles, promoting hair growth and reducing hair fall.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic rosemary tea for hair fall.

  1. In a pot, boil 2 cups of water for 5 minutes.
  2. Add 1-2 teaspoons of dried rosemary leaves to the boiling water, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. After simmering, remove the pot from heat and let it cool down for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the rosemary leaves.
  5. Drink the rosemary tea daily, preferably before or after meals, for best results in reducing hair fall.
