  • Analgesic: Garden orach tea contains analgesic compounds, such as rosmarinic acid, that help alleviate pain and discomfort associated with red eyes.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic garden orach tea for red eyes.

  1. Plant organic orach seeds in well-drained soil, allowing 10 inches of space between each plant, and wait 4-6 weeks for the plants to reach maturity.
  2. Harvest the orach leaves in the morning, choosing the younger, tender leaves for a milder flavor, and rinse them thoroughly to remove any dirt or debris.
  3. Boil 2 cups of water and add 1 tablespoon of fresh orach leaves, reducing heat to a simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent leaves, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 1-2 cups of orach tea per day, as needed, to relieve red eyes and soothe irritation.

How to best treat red eyes with herbal teas?

The best way to treat red eyes with herbal teas is by using chamomile or green tea bags.

Steep the tea bags in hot water for several minutes, then let them cool down until they're comfortably warm. Gently place the tea bags over your closed eyes for 10-15 minutes.

Both chamomile and green tea contain anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce redness and irritation. Remember to always test the temperature of the tea bags before applying them to your eyes to avoid burns.

If redness persists, consult a healthcare professional.

What ailments similar to red eyes are treated with herbal teas?

Ailments similar to red eyes that are treated with herbal teas include dry eye, eye strain, and conjunctivitis.

Chamomile tea is a popular choice for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, which can help alleviate redness and irritation. Green tea, rich in antioxidants, can reduce eye puffiness and dark circles.

For dry eye, fennel or eyebright tea can provide relief by increasing tear production and reducing inflammation. When using herbal teas for eye ailments, it is important to ensure the tea is properly cooled and to avoid using tea bags that have been previously steeped, as they can harbor bacteria.

Always consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.
