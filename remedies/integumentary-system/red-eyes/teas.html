8 Herbal Teas For Red Eyes

Herbal teas are a great way to soothe red eyes.

When our eyes get irritated, it's usually because of dryness, allergies, or overusing contact lenses. These teas work by reducing inflammation and soothing the delicate skin around our eyes. One example is Echinacea purpurea, which is known for its anti-inflammatory properties. It helps calm down the immune system's response to irritants, so you can relax and let your eyes recover. Euphrasia officinalis, also known as eyebright, is another herb that's been used for centuries to treat eye problems.

It reduces redness and swelling, making it a popular choice for people who work on computers or spend a lot of time outdoors. Ginkgo biloba, on the other hand, improves blood flow to the eyes, which helps to flush out irritants and reduce puffiness. Drinking herbal teas for red eyes can bring many benefits. For one, it allows you to rest and avoid rubbing your eyes, which can make the problem worse. It also gives you a break from the discomfort and stress of dealing with irritated eyes. When you're not stuck with red, itchy eyes, you can focus on the things you love - reading, watching movies, or even just working on your computer without feeling like your eyes are screaming for mercy.

Plus, herbal teas are a natural and gentle way to treat your eyes, so you don't have to worry about putting harsh chemicals in your body.

1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains active compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its potential benefits for red eyes.

The tea may help reduce inflammation and swelling in the eyes, possibly due to the anti-inflammatory properties of compounds like cichoric acid and kaempferol. These constituents may also have antioxidant effects, which can help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals.

To use Echinacea purpurea tea for red eyes, steep 1-2 teaspoons of dried flowers in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5-7 minutes, then strain and let it cool. You can apply a cooled compress soaked in the tea to your closed eyes for 5-10 minutes. After using the tea, you may notice a reduction in redness and swelling, and your eyes may feel more comfortable.

However, if your red eyes are a symptom of an underlying condition, you should consult a doctor for proper diagnosis and treatment.

2. Euphrasia officinalis

Euphrasia officinalis teas contains flavonoids, iridoids, and phenolic acids that help reduce redness and inflammation in the eyes.

The iridoids, particularly aucubin and harpagide, have anti-inflammatory properties that soothe the blood vessels and reduce the amount of blood flowing to the eyes, which in turn reduces redness. The flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, also have antioxidant properties that help protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals. If you're experiencing red eyes, try making a tea by infusing 1-2 teaspoons of dried Euphrasia officinalis leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the liquid and let it cool before applying it as a compress to your closed eyes. You can also drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help reduce inflammation and soothe your eyes. By using Euphrasia officinalis teas, you may start to notice a reduction in redness and puffiness within a few hours. The anti-inflammatory properties of the iridoids may help alleviate itchiness and discomfort, making it easier to relax and focus on your daily activities.

However, if your red eyes are caused by an underlying medical condition, such as conjunctivitis or dry eye syndrome, you should consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

3. Ginkgo biloba

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for red eyes?

If you're dealing with red eyes, it's best to drink herbal tea in the morning.

Morning is a great time to give your eyes a boost.
