Itchy Eyes: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Itchy eyes, medically known as conjunctival itch or ocular pruritus, is a condition characterized by an intense urge to rub or scratch the eyes due to irritation or inflammation of the conjunctiva, the thin membrane covering the white part of the eyes.

Itchy eyes can be caused by allergies, dry eyes, conjunctivitis, or exposure to irritants such as pollen, dust, or chemicals. Prolonged itchy eyes can lead to eye infections, vision problems, and even mental health issues due to discomfort and stress.

Fortunately, herbal remedies can provide relief. Aloe vera gel, chamomile tea, and tea tree oil have anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can calm itchy eyes. Calendula and eyebright have antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties that can help treat conjunctivitis.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies, especially if you have sensitive eyes or are allergic to any ingredients.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of itchy eyes, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of itchy eyes?

The main causes of itchy eyes are allergic reactions to environmental factors such as pollen, dust, and pet dander, which stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical that increases blood flow and leads to itching.

Irritants like smoke, chemicals, and fragrances can also cause itchy eyes by damaging the ocular surface and triggering a response. Infections such as conjunctivitis, caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi, can lead to itchy eyes due to inflammation and the release of chemical mediators. Dry eye syndrome, which occurs when the tear glands don't produce enough tears, can cause itchy eyes by irritating the ocular surface.

Certain medical conditions, such as blepharitis, thyroid eye disease, and rosacea, can also cause itchy eyes due to inflammation and the release of chemical mediators.

  • Allergies: Itchy eyes are commonly caused by allergies, particularly to pollen, dust, and pet dander, which trigger the release of histamine, leading to increased mucus production and inflammation in the eyes.
  • Dry eyes: Dry eyes are a common cause of itchy eyes due to inadequate tear production or poor tear quality, resulting in irritation and inflammation of the ocular surface.
  • Contact lenses: Itchy eyes can be caused by contact lenses, especially if they are not properly fitted, cleaned, or maintained, leading to irritation and inflammation of the cornea and conjunctiva.
  • Blepharitis: Blepharitis, an inflammation of the eyelids, is a common cause of itchy eyes due to the presence of demodex mites, meibomian gland dysfunction, or other factors that lead to inflammation and irritation of the eyelids.
  • Rosacea: Rosacea, a chronic skin condition, can cause itchy eyes due to the presence of rosacea-like lesions on the eyelids, which can lead to inflammation and irritation of the eyes.
  • Conjunctivitis: Conjunctivitis, also known as pink eye, is a common cause of itchy eyes due to viral or bacterial infections, which lead to inflammation and irritation of the conjunctiva.
  • Iritis: Iritis, an inflammation of the iris, can cause itchy eyes due to the presence of inflammatory cells and the release of chemical mediators, leading to pain and irritation of the eyes.
  • Uveitis: Uveitis, an inflammation of the uvea, can cause itchy eyes due to the presence of inflammatory cells and the release of chemical mediators, leading to pain and irritation of the eyes.
  • Glaucoma: Glaucoma, a group of eye conditions that can lead to vision loss, can cause itchy eyes due to increased intraocular pressure, which can lead to irritation and pain in the eyes.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis, an inflammation of the sinuses, can cause itchy eyes due to the presence of bacterial or viral infections, which can lead to inflammation and irritation of the eyes.
  • Sjögren's syndrome: Sjögren's syndrome, an autoimmune disorder, can cause itchy eyes due to the presence of autoantibodies that target the lacrimal and salivary glands, leading to dry eyes and irritation.
