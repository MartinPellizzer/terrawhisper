Frizzy Hair: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Frizzy hair is characterized by unruly, unmanageable locks with a rough, disheveled texture, often caused by humidity, heat styling, and genetics.

This condition can lead to scalp irritation, itchiness, and dandruff, which may further exacerbate the issue. To alleviate frizzy hair, individuals can turn to herbal remedies. Aloe vera, chamomile, and rosemary extracts have been shown to soothe and calm the scalp, while neem oil and coconut oil can help to nourish and moisturize the hair.

However, when using medicinal herbs, it's essential to conduct a patch test and consult with a healthcare professional to avoid any adverse reactions or interactions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of frizzy hair, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of frizzy hair?

The main causes of frizzy hair are excessive humidity, which disrupts the cuticle's compact layer allowing moisture to penetrate and causing it to swell, leading to frizz.

High temperatures, particularly above 85°F (29°C), accelerate the evaporation of moisture from the hair shaft, causing it to lose its natural moisture balance. Use of heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can damage the hair's cuticle layer, making it prone to frizz. Presence of minerals such as sodium, calcium, and magnesium in hard water can also cause frizz by disrupting the hair's cuticle layer.

Furthermore, genetics play a significant role in determining an individual's hair texture, with people having tightly coiled hair being more prone to frizz due to the hair's natural tendency to curl and lift.

  • Humidity: Humidity in the air causes water molecules to penetrate the hair shaft, leading to swelling and a loss of moisture balance, resulting in frizzy hair.
  • Damaged Cuticle: Damaged cuticle layer of the hair shaft allows moisture to penetrate and escape, causing the hair to swell and become frizzy.
  • Protein Imbalance: An imbalance of protein and moisture in the hair shaft can cause the hair to become weak, brittle, and prone to frizz.
  • Hair Porosity: Poor hair porosity, either too high or too low, can cause the hair to absorb and retain too much moisture, leading to frizz.
  • Chemical Processing: Chemical processing, such as dyeing or perming, can damage the hair shaft and alter its structure, leading to frizzy hair.
  • Heat Styling: Excessive heat styling can damage the hair shaft and cause it to lose its natural moisture, leading to frizzy hair.
  • Hard Water: Hard water can strip the hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and prone to frizz.
  • Sulfates: Sulfates in shampoos can strip the hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and prone to frizz.
  • Towel-Drying: Roughly towel-drying hair can cause friction and lead to frizz, especially when hair is still damp.
  • Split Ends: Split ends can cause the hair to fray and become prone to frizz, especially when hair is not regularly trimmed.
