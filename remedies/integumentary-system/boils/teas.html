1. Calendula officinalis teas

Calendula officinalis teas have been traditionally used to treat boils, a skin infection characterized by pus-filled lesions, due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Featured Study

According to "Life (Basel, Switzerland)", Calendula officinalis teas for boils may be beneficial due to its use as a natural phytomedicine that possesses considerable pharmacological properties to facilitate wound treatment and infection prevention.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Calendula officinalis flowers or 1 teaspoon of dried flowers.
  2. Combine the Calendula flowers with 1 cup of boiling water in a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Steep the mixture for 5-10 minutes, then strain it using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the tea to make it sweeter and more palatable.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe and calm the boil, and reduce inflammation.

Usage:

  1. Soak a clean cloth in 1 cup of Calendula officinalis tea
  2. Apply the soaked cloth to the boil 3-4 times a day
  3. Leave the cloth on for 30 minutes each application
  4. Repeat this process for 3-5 days to see improvement
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of Calendula officinalis tea per day for support

Calendula officinalis teas can be effective in treating boils, but possible side effects may include skin irritation, allergic reactions, or increased sensitivity to sunlight.

When using Calendula officinalis teas to treat boils, it is recommended to start with small doses and gradually increase as needed, and to avoid using it on broken skin or open wounds, and to discontinue use if any adverse reactions occur.
