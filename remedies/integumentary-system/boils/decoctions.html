The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yarrow for boils.

  1. Gather fresh or dried leaves of achillea millefolium, measuring 2-3 teaspoons for every 8 ounces water.
  2. Combine the herb material with boiling water in a pot, steeping for 15 minutes to allow for optimal extraction.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  4. Allow the decoction to cool, then refrigerate it at least 2 hours before use to enhance its antibacterial properties.
  5. Use the cooled decoction as a topical application, applying a few drops directly to the boil area and gently massaging in for relief.
