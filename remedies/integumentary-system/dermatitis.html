Dermatitis: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Dermatitis, also known as eczema, is a chronic inflammatory skin condition characterized by itchy, red, and scaly patches, often caused by irritants, allergies, or skin conditions such as psoriasis or seborrheic dermatitis.

The causes of dermatitis can be varied, including genetics, environmental factors, stress, and certain foods. If left untreated, dermatitis can lead to skin infections, anxiety, and even depression. Fortunately, several herbal remedies, such as aloe vera, chamomile, and calendula, have shown promise in relieving symptoms when applied topically in the form of creams, ointments, or infused oils.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies, as some may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions, and to patch test any new products to avoid further irritation.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of dermatitis, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of dermatitis?

The main causes of dermatitis are multifactorial, with genetic predisposition, environmental factors, and immune system dysfunction playing significant roles.

Irritants such as soaps, detergents, and fragrances can cause allergic contact dermatitis, while physical agents like friction, heat, and cold can lead to irritant contact dermatitis. Insect bites and stings, as well as plant dermatitis, can also trigger reactions. Certain medical conditions, such as eczema, psoriasis, and allergies, can increase the risk of developing dermatitis.

Additionally, exposure to metals like nickel, chromium, and cobalt can cause contact dermatitis due to their allergenic properties.

  • Irritants: Irritants such as soaps, detergents, and other chemicals can cause dermatitis by damaging the skin's natural barrier and triggering an allergic or irritant reaction.
  • Allergens: Allergens like nickel, latex, and certain metals can cause dermatitis by triggering an allergic immune response and leading to inflammation and skin damage.
  • Fragrances: Fragrances in personal care products, such as perfumes, shampoos, and lotions, can cause dermatitis by irritating the skin and triggering an allergic reaction.
  • Soaps: Soaps can cause dermatitis by stripping the skin of its natural oils and causing dryness, irritation, and inflammation.
  • Moisture: Excess moisture, such as sweating or exposure to water, can cause dermatitis by creating an environment conducive to bacterial and fungal growth and leading to skin irritation and inflammation.
  • Infections: Infections, such as bacterial or fungal infections, can cause dermatitis by triggering an immune response and leading to inflammation and skin damage.
  • Stress: Stress can cause dermatitis by weakening the immune system and making the skin more susceptible to irritation and inflammation.
  • Hormonal changes: Hormonal changes, such as those experienced during pregnancy or menopause, can cause dermatitis by altering the skin's natural balance and leading to inflammation and skin damage.
  • Genetics: Genetics can play a role in dermatitis by predisposing individuals to certain types of dermatitis, such as atopic dermatitis.
  • Vitamin deficiencies: Vitamin deficiencies, such as a lack of vitamin B12 or omega-3 fatty acids, can cause dermatitis by impairing the skin's natural barrier function and leading to inflammation and skin damage.
