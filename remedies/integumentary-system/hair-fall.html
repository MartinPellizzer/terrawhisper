What to know about hair fall before treating it with medicinal herbs

Hair fall is a common condition characterized by excessive shedding of hair, leading to thinning or balding areas on the scalp.

It can have a significant impact on an individual's self-esteem and confidence, causing feelings of embarrassment and frustration. Hair loss can be caused by factors such as genetics, hormonal imbalances, poor diet, and stress.

To address this issue, certain medicinal herbs like Bhringaraj (Eclipta alba), Amla (Emblica officinalis), and Coconut oil extract (Cocos nucifera) have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to promote hair growth and reduce fall. Preparations include applying a paste made from Bhringaraj leaves or consuming Amla juice as supplements.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using herbs medicinally, as some may interact with medications or have allergic reactions.

What is hair fall and how it affects your life?

Hair fall, a common yet distressing phenomenon, can have a profound impact on one's daily life.

When excessive shedding occurs, it can lead to emotional distress, affecting not only physical appearance but also overall well-being. For instance, individuals with severe hair loss may feel self-conscious and embarrassed, leading to social anxiety and avoidance of social gatherings or public events where they might be scrutinized.

This can further lead to decreased confidence, impacting personal relationships and work performance. Hair fall can also have a significant impact on one's career, particularly for those in industries where appearance is crucial, such as modeling, acting, or entertainment. Moreover, it can affect mental health, causing feelings of depression, anxiety, and low self-esteem.

Furthermore, hair loss can lead to difficulties in everyday activities like grooming, styling, and even simply enjoying leisure time, as one may feel embarrassed to go out without a hat or wig.

What are the main causes of hair fall?

The main causes of hair fall are a combination of genetic, hormonal, and environmental factors.

Genetic predisposition can play a significant role, where hair loss is inherited from one's parents. Hormonal imbalance, particularly in men, can lead to hair thinning and fall due to the conversion of testosterone into dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone that contributes to hair loss. Environmental factors such as excessive heat styling, chemical treatments, and exposure to pollutants like smoke, dust, and UV radiation can also cause damage to the hair follicles leading to hair fall.

Additionally, poor diet, stress, and certain medical conditions like thyroid disorders, autoimmune diseases, and skin infections can all contribute to hair loss.

The most common causes of hair fall are listed below.

  • Genetics: Inheriting hair loss from parents can be a significant factor in hair fall, especially as one ages.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Changes in hormone levels, such as those experienced during menopause or pregnancy, can lead to hair fall due to increased sensitivity to androgens.
  • Stress: Physical or emotional stress can cause the body to shift into a state of "fight or flight," leading to an increase in cortisol production, which can contribute to hair loss.
  • Nutritional Deficiencies: Lack of essential nutrients such as iron, zinc, or biotin can impair hair growth and lead to hair fall.
  • Over-Processing: Frequent heat styling, dyeing, and chemical treatments can weaken the hair shaft and cause breakage.
  • Dandruff and Scalp Conditions: Fungal infections like ringworm, psoriasis, or eczema on the scalp can cause inflammation, irritation, and hair fall.
  • Traction Alopecia: Prolonged use of tight hairstyles such as braids, ponytails, or cornrows can lead to hair loss due to constant pulling and tension on the hair follicles.
  • Medical Conditions: Certain conditions like thyroid disorders, autoimmune diseases (e.g., alopecia areata), and skin conditions (e.g., dermatitis) can cause hair fall.
  • Medications: Using medications such as blood thinners, antidepressants, or antipsychotics can lead to hair fall as a side effect.
  • Aging: As people age, the growth cycle of hair slows down, leading to thinner and shorter hair, which may appear like hair fall.

What precautions to take when using herbal remedies for hair fall?

When considering the use of herbal remedies for hair fall, it is essential to take certain precautions to ensure their safe and effective use.

First, consult with a healthcare professional or an experienced practitioner before starting any new treatment regimen. Additionally, choose reputable sources for purchasing herbal products, as low-quality ingredients can lead to adverse reactions or contamination.

Start with small amounts and gradually increase the dose as needed, as some herbs can be potent and cause allergic reactions or interactions with medications. Furthermore, be aware of potential interactions with other treatments you may be using, such as medications, shampoos, or conditioners. Finally, monitor your body's response to the herbal remedy and discontinue use if you experience any adverse effects, such as itching, redness, or discomfort.

By taking these precautions, you can minimize risks and maximize the benefits of herbal remedies for hair fall.
