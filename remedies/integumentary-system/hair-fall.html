Hair Fall: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Hair fall, or alopecia, is a condition characterized by excessive and accelerated hair shedding from the scalp, often resulting in noticeable thinning or baldness.

It is typically caused by hormonal imbalances, genetics, stress, poor diet, and inadequate hair care. If left unaddressed, hair fall can lead to emotional distress, low self-esteem, and even exacerbate existing health conditions. To alleviate hair fall, herbal remedies such as amla, neem, and bhringaraj are effective, often in the form of oil-based preparations or supplements.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as they can interact with other medications or exacerbate underlying conditions, and to follow proper dosage and application guidelines to avoid adverse reactions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of hair fall, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of hair fall?

The main causes of hair fall are primarily attributed to hormonal imbalances, with dihydrotestosterone (DHT) being a key contributor, as it causes the hair follicle to shrink, leading to thinner and shorter hair.

Genetic predisposition, particularly androgenetic alopecia, also plays a significant role, with over 80% of men and 40% of women experiencing hair loss by the age of 50. Nutritional deficiencies, such as iron, zinc, and biotin deficiencies, can also lead to hair fall, as these essential nutrients are crucial for hair growth and maintenance. Environmental factors, such as excessive heat styling, chemical treatments, and exposure to pollution, can cause hair damage and lead to hair fall.

Furthermore, certain medical conditions, such as thyroid disorders, autoimmune diseases, and skin conditions, can also contribute to hair loss.

  • Hormonal Imbalance: Hormonal imbalances, particularly androgenetic alopecia, can lead to hair thinning and fall due to an overactive hormone dihydrotestosterone (DHT), which causes hair follicles to shrink and eventually stop producing hair.
  • Genetics: Genetics play a significant role in hair loss, with individuals inheriting traits that increase their susceptibility to androgenetic alopecia, the most common cause of hair loss in both men and women.
  • Stress: Chronic stress can cause hair loss due to the increased production of cortisol, a hormone that can disrupt the normal hair growth cycle and lead to hair thinning and fall.
  • Poor Diet: A diet lacking essential nutrients, such as iron, zinc, and biotin, can contribute to hair loss due to the insufficient production of keratin, a protein necessary for hair growth.
  • Hairstyling: Excessive heat styling, tight hairstyles such as braids and ponytails, and the use of harsh chemical treatments can cause hair breakage and fall due to the mechanical stress and damage to the hair follicles.
  • Skin Conditions: Certain skin conditions, such as alopecia areata, psoriasis, and eczema, can cause hair loss due to inflammation and autoimmune responses that damage the hair follicles.
  • Medications: Certain medications, including those used to treat depression, high blood pressure, and cancer, can cause hair loss as a side effect due to the disruption of the normal hair growth cycle.
  • Infections: Infections such as ringworm and folliculitis can cause hair loss due to the inflammation and damage to the hair follicles.
  • Chemical Exposure: Prolonged exposure to chemicals such as pesticides and heavy metals can cause hair loss due to the disruption of the normal hair growth cycle and damage to the hair follicles.
