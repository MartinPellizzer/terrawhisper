What is hair fall and how it affects your life?

Hair fall, also known as hair loss or alopecia, is a condition where hair falls out from the scalp at a rate that is considered excessive or abnormal.

This can be a distressing and emotionally draining experience, affecting not just one's physical appearance but also overall well-being. Hair fall can lead to low self-esteem and confidence, making everyday activities feel like a struggle. For instance, a person with hair fall may feel anxious about going out in public, or avoid social events where they may feel self-conscious about their appearance.

Even simple tasks like tying their hair back or washing it can become a daunting chore. Furthermore, hair fall can also have a ripple effect on one's relationships, as individuals may become withdrawn and isolated, preferring to avoid social interactions altogether. In extreme cases, it can even impact one's professional life, with hair fall being a visible sign of stress, fatigue, or poor health, potentially leading to lost productivity and opportunities.

Overall, hair fall can have a profoundly negative impact on one's life, affecting both mental and physical health.
