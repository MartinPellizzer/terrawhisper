Gum Swelling: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Gum swelling, also known as gingival enlargement, is a condition characterized by the abnormal growth of gum tissue, often causing discomfort and pain.

It can be caused by factors such as poor oral hygiene, gum disease, hormonal changes, and certain medications. If left untreated, gum swelling can lead to tooth loss, difficulty eating and speaking, and increased risk of heart disease and diabetes. To alleviate gum swelling, herbal remedies such as aloe vera, chamomile, and calendula can be used to reduce inflammation and promote healing, while also providing soothing relief.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or allergies, and to follow proper preparation and dosage guidelines.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of gum swelling, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of gum swelling?

The main causes of gum swelling are inflammation due to bacterial accumulation, typically from poor oral hygiene and inadequate brushing/flossing leading to plaque formation, which can cause gingivitis and periodontitis, an infection of the gums that can be exacerbated by systemic conditions such as diabetes and hormonal changes during pregnancy.

Additionally, trauma to the gums, such as biting the cheek or tongue, can also cause swelling. Furthermore, some medications like beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers, and certain antidepressants can cause gum overgrowth as a side effect. In rare cases, gum swelling can be a symptom of underlying conditions such as leukemia, lymphoma, or other blood disorders.

Moreover, gum inflammation can be triggered by smoking, which is known to increase the risk of gum disease.

  • Periodontal Disease: Periodontal disease is a bacterial infection of the gums, caused by poor oral hygiene and a diet high in sugar, leading to inflammation and gum swelling.
  • Gingivitis: Gingivitis is an inflammation of the gums caused by the buildup of plaque and tartar, leading to redness, swelling, and bleeding.
  • Dental Abscess: A dental abscess is a painful infection of the gums and surrounding bone, caused by a bacterial infection that has spread from a tooth or gum.
  • Gum Injury: A gum injury, such as a cut or bruise, can cause gum swelling due to inflammation and bleeding.
  • Medication Side Effect: Certain medications, such as antidepressants and steroids, can cause gum swelling as a side effect.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes, such as those experienced during pregnancy or menstruation, can cause gum swelling due to increased blood flow and sensitivity.
  • Nutritional Deficiencies: Nutritional deficiencies, such as a lack of vitamin C or calcium, can cause gum swelling due to impaired immune function and bone health.
  • Infections: Other infections, such as strep throat or a sinus infection, can cause gum swelling due to the spread of bacteria or inflammation.
  • Cancer: Rarely, gum swelling can be a symptom of oral cancer, which requires prompt medical attention.
