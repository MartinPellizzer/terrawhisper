Reddened Corners In Mouth: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Reddened corners in the mouth, also known as angular cheilitis, is a condition characterized by inflammation and redness at the corners of the mouth, often accompanied by cracked or crusted skin.

It can be caused by factors such as vitamin deficiencies, particularly vitamin B2 (riboflavin), vitamin B12, or iron, as well as dry mouth, poor oral hygiene, and certain medications. If left untreated, it can lead to discomfort, pain, and difficulty speaking or eating. To alleviate reddened corners in the mouth, herbal remedies such as aloe vera gel, calendula cream, and tea tree oil can be applied topically, while vitamin supplements and probiotics may be beneficial in addressing underlying deficiencies.

When using medicinal herbs, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional and follow proper application guidelines to avoid allergic reactions or interactions with medications.

What are the main causes of reddened corners in mouth?

The main causes of reddened corners in mouth are angular cheilitis, a condition characterized by inflammation and redness at the corners of the mouth, often caused by dry mouth, vitamin deficiencies (particularly B2 and B12), and impaired immune function.

Oral thrush, a fungal infection caused by Candida, can also lead to reddened corners in the mouth, especially in people with weakened immune systems or those taking corticosteroids. Irritation from the use of certain medications, such as isotretinoin and phenytoin, can cause this condition. Allergic reactions to food, dental appliances, or lip balms can also lead to reddened corners in the mouth.

Additionally, poor oral hygiene and habits, such as biting or chewing on the inside of the mouth, can cause friction and irritation, resulting in reddened corners.

  • Periodontal Disease: Periodontal disease is a bacterial infection of the gums that can cause reddened corners in the mouth due to inflammation and irritation of the gum tissue.
  • Mucositis: Mucositis is an inflammation of the mucous membranes in the mouth, often caused by chemotherapy or radiation therapy, that can lead to reddened corners in the mouth due to damage to the mucous membranes.
  • Oral Lichen Planus: Oral lichen planus is a chronic autoimmune disorder that can cause reddened corners in the mouth due to inflammation and irritation of the mucous membranes.
  • Oral Thrush: Oral thrush is a fungal infection caused by Candida that can cause reddened corners in the mouth due to overgrowth of the fungus on the mucous membranes.
  • Gingivitis: Gingivitis is a bacterial infection of the gums that can cause reddened corners in the mouth due to inflammation and irritation of the gum tissue.
  • Cheilitis: Cheilitis is an inflammation of the lips and mouth that can cause reddened corners in the mouth due to irritation and dryness of the mucous membranes.
  • Dental Erosion: Dental erosion is the wearing away of tooth enamel, often caused by acid, that can cause reddened corners in the mouth due to exposure of the dentin underneath the enamel.
  • Oral Cancer: Oral cancer is a type of cancer that can cause reddened corners in the mouth due to growth and spread of cancer cells in the mucous membranes.
