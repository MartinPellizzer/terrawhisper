Burning Feet: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Burning feet, characterized by a painful sensation, often accompanied by redness, itchiness, and a feeling of warmth or heat in the feet, can be a distressing and debilitating condition.

Caused by factors such as nerve damage, vitamin deficiencies, certain medications, diabetes, and exposure to extreme temperatures, burning feet can lead to discomfort, reduced mobility, and sleep disturbances. Fortunately, herbal remedies like peppermint, chamomile, and aloe vera can provide relief by soothing and cooling the affected area.

However, when using medicinal herbs, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional and follow proper dosages to avoid interactions or allergic reactions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of burning feet, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of burning feet?

The main causes of burning feet are usually related to medical conditions, such as peripheral neuropathy, a result of diabetes, which damages nerve endings, causing pain and discomfort.

Another cause is excessive sweating, which can lead to fungal infections like athlete's foot, further exacerbating the burning sensation. Certain medications like antibiotics and antihistamines can also cause burning feet as a side effect. In some cases, vitamin deficiencies, particularly of vitamin B12, can lead to burning sensations in the feet due to nerve damage.

Additionally, conditions like Raynaud's disease and poor circulation can also cause burning feet due to reduced blood flow.

  • Diabetes: Diabetes can cause burning feet due to nerve damage or neuropathy, which is a complication of the disease that affects the nerves, leading to numbness, tingling, and pain in the feet.
  • Athlete's Foot: Athlete's foot is a fungal infection that causes burning feet due to the irritation and inflammation caused by the fungus, especially in warm and moist environments.
  • Raynaud's: Raynaud's disease is a condition that affects blood flow to the extremities, including the feet, causing episodes of burning and numbness due to the reduced blood flow.
  • Vitamin Deficiency: Vitamin deficiencies, particularly a lack of vitamin B12, can cause burning feet due to the nerve damage and inflammation caused by the deficiency.
  • Poor Footwear: Poorly fitting or worn-out shoes can cause burning feet due to the pressure and friction caused by the shoes, leading to irritation and inflammation of the skin.
  • Foot Sweating: Excessive foot sweating can cause burning feet due to the irritation and inflammation caused by the sweat, especially in people with foot conditions such as athlete's foot.
  • Nerve Compression: Nerve compression, such as in the case of a pinched nerve, can cause burning feet due to the pressure on the nerve, leading to pain and numbness.
  • Medication Side Effects: Certain medications, such as antibiotics and antihistamines, can cause burning feet as a side effect due to the changes in blood flow and nerve function.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes, such as those experienced during pregnancy, can cause burning feet due to the changes in blood flow and nerve function.
  • Thyroid Disease: Thyroid disease, particularly an overactive thyroid, can cause burning feet due to the increased metabolism and nerve stimulation.
