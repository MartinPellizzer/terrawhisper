What are the main causes of white patches in month?

The main causes of white patches in mouth are primarily related to bacterial, viral, or fungal infections, as well as certain medical conditions.

One common cause is thrush, a fungal infection caused by Candida albicans, which can occur due to antibiotic use, hormone imbalances, or poor oral hygiene. Another common cause is leukoplakia, a condition characterized by the growth of white patches on the tongue or inside the cheeks, often linked to smoking, tobacco chewing, or excessive consumption of spicy foods and drinks. Other potential causes include mouth sores caused by viral infections like herpes simplex, or allergic reactions to certain substances or medications.

In some cases, white patches may also be a sign of an underlying medical condition such as Crohn's disease, oral lichen planus, or geographic tongue.

The most common causes of white patches in month are listed below.

  • Oral Thrush (Candidiasis): A fungal infection caused by an overgrowth of yeast, often due to antibiotics or other medications that disrupt the natural balance of bacteria in the mouth.
  • Leukoplakia: A condition characterized by thick, white patches on the tongue and mucous membranes, often linked to tobacco use or excessive consumption of spicy foods.
  • Lichen Planus: An autoimmune disorder causing a distinctive pattern of white lesions with red borders on the inner surfaces of the mouth, cheeks, and gums.
  • Vitiligo: A chronic condition in which the immune system attacks and destroys pigment-producing cells, resulting in white patches or spots on the skin, including the mouth.
  • Psoriasis: An autoimmune disorder causing red, scaly patches on the skin, including the mouth and lips, sometimes accompanied by white patches.
  • Geographic Tongue (Benign Migratory Glossitis): A condition characterized by small, round or oval-shaped patches on the tongue that change color from pink to white due to changes in blood flow.
  • Oral Erythema Multiforme: An allergic reaction causing white, red, and blister-like lesions on the lips, cheeks, and gums, often triggered by food allergens or medications.
  • Traumatic Injuries: Physical trauma to the mouth, such as a fall or biting tongue, can cause white patches due to bleeding and subsequent scarring.
  • Denture Irritation: Improperly fitting dentures or dental appliances can cause friction and irritation, leading to the formation of white patches on the gums and inner cheek.
  • Irritant Contact Dermatitis: Allergic reactions to certain substances, such as mouthwashes or toothpaste ingredients, causing redness, itching, and white patches on the lips and gums.
