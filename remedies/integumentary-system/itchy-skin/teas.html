1. Yarrow tea

Yarrow tea helps with itchy skin because it contains anti-inflammatory and anti-pruritic properties that can soothe and alleviate skin irritation.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in yarrow tea that aid with itchy skin.

  • Astringent: Yarrow tea's astringent properties help tighten skin and reduce secretions, which is important for itchy skin as it can help dry out weeping or oozing rashes. The tannins in yarrow are responsible for its astringent properties.
  • Antipruritic: Yarrow tea has antipruritic properties, which means it can help alleviate itching. This is essential for itchy skin as it can provide immediate relief and reduce the urge to scratch. The lactones and essential oils in yarrow contribute to its antipruritic effects.
  • Antimicrobial: Yarrow tea's antimicrobial properties help prevent and treat skin infections, which is important for itchy skin as scratching can introduce bacteria and cause infections. The active constituents responsible for this property include volatile oils, lactones, and flavonoids.
  • Analgesic: Yarrow tea's analgesic properties help relieve pain, which is crucial for itchy skin as constant scratching can cause pain and irritation. The sesquiterpene lactones and alkaloids in yarrow contribute to its analgesic effects.

The list below shows the primary parts of yarrow tea used to make teas for itchy skin.

  • Flowers: Rich in anti-inflammatory compounds, yarrow flowers help soothe itchy skin.
  • Leaves: Containing azulene, yarrow leaves can reduce inflammation and irritation on the skin.
  • Stems: High in sesquiterpene lactones, yarrow stems help alleviate itching and skin redness.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yarrow tea for itchy skin.

  1. Gather 2-3 teaspoons of dried yarrow flowers and leaves.
  2. Crush the yarrow herb to release its oils, then place it in a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the yarrow herb and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea to discard the herb.
  5. Drink the yarrow tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day, to relieve itchy skin.
