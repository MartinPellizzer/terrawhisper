The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic field horsetail for puffy eyes.

  1. Measure 20-30 grams of dried equisetum arvense herb into a clean glass container.
  2. Combine the measured herb with 500 milliliters of boiling water in a large saucepan.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer the mixture for 10-15 minutes, allowing the herbs to steep.
  4. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into another clean glass container.
  5. Allow the cooled decoction to sit at room temperature for an additional 30-60 minutes before refrigerating.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic mallow for puffy eyes.

  1. Gather one teaspoon of dried malva sylvestris flowers and grind them into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle.
  2. Combine the ground malva sylvestris with eight ounces of boiling water in a heat-resistant glass cup or teapot.
  3. Allow the mixture to steep for 10-15 minutes, allowing the herbal properties to infuse into the water.
  4. Strain the decoction through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a clean glass container, discarding the solids.
  5. Let the cooled decoction chill in the refrigerator for at least two hours before using it as an eye compress or applying it directly to the affected area with a cotton pad.
