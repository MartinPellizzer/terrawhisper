10 Herbal Tinctures For Dental Plaque

Herbal tinctures can help with dental plaque, the sticky film that forms on your teeth and can lead to gum disease.

Some herbs, like Zingiber officinale (ginger), have natural antibacterial properties that fight off the bacteria that cause plaque. When you use a tincture made from ginger, it helps reduce the amount of plaque on your teeth. Salvia officinalis (sage) is another herb that can help. Its antibacterial properties also kill the bacteria that cause plaque, and its anti-inflammatory properties help soothe irritated gums. Using a sage tincture can help keep your gums healthy and prevent gum disease.

Eucalyptus globulus (eucalyptus) is a different story. While it does have antibacterial properties, its main benefit for dental plaque is that it helps reduce inflammation in the gums. When your gums are inflamed, they can be painful and bleed easily. Using a eucalyptus tincture can help calm the inflammation and keep your gums healthy. Herbal tinctures can be a good addition to your oral care routine if you want to avoid using chemical-based products. They're also gentle and non-irritating, making them a good choice for people with sensitive teeth or gums.

And because they're natural, you don't have to worry about any harsh chemicals getting into your body.

Consuming herbal teas daily for dental plaque might not be effective.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in dental plaque?

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for dental plaque?

Drinking herbal tea for dental plaque in the morning is a good idea.

This is because you're already getting rid of bacteria in your mouth from the night before.
