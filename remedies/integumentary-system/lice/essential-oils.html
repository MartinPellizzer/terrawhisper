1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Tea tree essential oils helps with lice because of its potent antiparasitic properties.

The oil's active compound, terpinen-4-ol, targets and destroys lice and their eggs, providing a natural solution for those struggling with infestations. When applied topically to the scalp or hair, tea tree essential oil can help break the lice life cycle, preventing re-infestation.

Its de-greasing properties also aid in removing lice residue, making it an effective complement to traditional treatment methods.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in tea tree essential oils that aid with lice.

  • Antiparasitic property: TTO's antiparasitic properties help to kill lice and their eggs (nits) by disrupting their nervous systems and dehydrating them.
  • Antimicrobial property: TTO's antimicrobial properties help to prevent the spread of infection caused by lice bites, which can lead to secondary infections like ringworm, impetigo, or cellulitis.
  • Anti-inflammatory property: TTO's anti-inflammatory properties help to reduce itching, redness, and swelling associated with lice infestations, making it more comfortable for individuals to manage the condition.

The list below shows the primary parts of tea tree essential oil used to make essential oils for lice.

  • Leaves: Rich in cineole, a compound that has been shown to repel and kill lice.
  • Buds: Containing high levels of terpinen-4-ol, an active component responsible for its insecticidal properties against lice.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic tea tree essential oil for lice.

  1. Harvest fresh melaleuca alternifolia leaves from a pesticide-free source weighing approximately 100 grams.
  2. Dry the harvested leaves by spreading them in a single layer on paper towels for 24-48 hours.
  3. Steam distill the dried leaves using a clevenger apparatus at a temperature range of 100-150°c for 1 hour.
  4. Collect the resulting essential oil and filter it through a coffee filter or cheesecloth to remove impurities weighing approximately 10 milliliters.
  5. Store the filtered essential oil in a dark glass bottle with an orifice reducer tightly sealed and kept refrigerated at a temperature below 40°c.
