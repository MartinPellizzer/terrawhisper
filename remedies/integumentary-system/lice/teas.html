The list below shows the primary parts of neem tea used to make teas for lice.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and azadirachtin, neem leaves are commonly used to make medicinal tea for lice due to their insecticidal and larvicidal properties.
  • Seeds: Containing high levels of limonoids, neem seeds are used to make medicinal tea for lice treatment, as they disrupt the life cycle of lice and inhibit their ability to lay eggs.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic neem tea for lice.

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot, then add 2 tablespoons of dried neem leaves.

  4. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  5. Pour the neem tea over the scalp and hair, massaging it gently, then leave it on for 30 minutes before rinsing.
