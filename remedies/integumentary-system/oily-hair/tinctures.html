Medicinal Constituents

Parts Used

The list below shows the primary parts of sesame used to make tinctures for oily hair.

  • Seeds: Sesame seeds are commonly used in tinctures for oily hair due to their ability to balance scalp pH and reduce excess oil production.
  • Leaves: The leaves of Sesamum indicum are used to make tinctures that help to control oiliness and promote a healthy scalp environment.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic sesame for oily hair.

  1. Harvest 30-40 grams of fresh sesamum indicum flowers when in full bloom and dry them for 2 weeks.
  2. Grind 10-15 grams of the dried flowers into a fine powder using a coffee grinder.
  3. Combine the powdered sesamum indicum with 90-100 ml of 95% ethanol in a clean glass jar.
  4. Steep the mixture for 2-3 weeks in a cool dark place shaking the jar every 2-3 days.
  5. Strain the tincture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into a clean glass bottle and discard the solids.
