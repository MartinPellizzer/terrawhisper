1. Rosemary tincture

Rosemary tincture helps with oily hair because it has natural astringent properties that can help regulate sebum production in the scalp.

Sebum is an oily substance that helps keep the hair and skin moisturized, but an overproduction of sebum can lead to oily hair and other scalp issues. The rosemary tincture helps to balance the scalp's natural oil production, leaving the hair looking and feeling cleaner for longer periods.

Additionally, rosemary tincture has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help soothe and protect the scalp, promoting healthy hair growth and reducing the likelihood of dandruff and other scalp irritations.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in rosemary tincture that aid with oily hair.

  • Astringent: Rosemary tincture helps to regulate sebum production in the scalp, reducing excess oiliness in hair. This property is due to the presence of tannins and volatile oils such as cineole and camphor.
  • Antimicrobial: It inhibits the growth of microorganisms on the scalp, preventing dandruff and other related issues. The main active constituents responsible for this property are caffeic acid, rosemarinic acid, and carnosol.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Rosemary tincture reduces inflammation and irritation on the scalp, promoting a healthy environment for hair growth. The anti-inflammatory effects are attributed to the presence of rosmarinic acid and carnosol.
  • Circulation stimulant: It improves blood flow to the scalp, promoting hair growth and reducing oiliness. The active constituents responsible for this property include ursolic acid and carnosic acid.
  • Antioxidant: Rosemary tincture protects the scalp and hair from environmental stressors and damage, maintaining overall hair health. The main antioxidant compounds found in rosemary are carnosol, carnosic acid, and rosmarinic acid.

The list below shows the primary parts of rosemary tincture used to make tinctures for oily hair.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the rosemary plant are rich in essential oils and antioxidants, making them ideal for creating medicinal tinctures that can help regulate sebum production in the scalp and promote hair growth.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the rosemary plant contain high levels of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, making them useful for creating tinctures that can help soothe an itchy scalp and reduce inflammation associated with oily hair.
  • Stems: The stems of the rosemary plant contain beneficial compounds that can help stimulate blood flow to the scalp, promoting healthy hair growth and reducing excess oil production.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the rosemary plant are rich in fatty acids and antioxidants, making them useful for creating tinctures that can help nourish and strengthen the hair follicles, reducing breakage and promoting healthy, shiny hair.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic rosemary tincture for oily hair.

  1. Chop 2 cups of fresh rosemary leaves, place them in a clean jar, then pour 1 cup of vodka or rum over the herbs until fully submerged.
  2. Seal the jar tightly and store it in a cool, dark place for 2-6 weeks, shaking it gently every few days.
  3. After the desired steeping time, strain the mixture using cheesecloth or a fine mesh strainer, squeezing out the liquid and discarding the plant material.
  4. Transfer the rosemary-infused liquid to a dark glass bottle, adding vegetable glycerin (optional) for a more nourishing tincture, then seal and label the bottle.
  5. Use 1-2 tablespoons of the rosemary tincture as a hair rinse after shampooing, massaging it into the scalp and leaving it on for 5-10 minutes before rinsing with water.
