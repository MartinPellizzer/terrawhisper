11 Best Herbal Teas For Oily Hair

Herbal teas for Oily hair are a natural remedy that involves the use of various plant-based teas to help regulate oil production and balance the scalp's natural pH.

1. Rosmarinus officinalis teas

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, derived from the leaves of the rosemary plant, have been traditionally used to treat oily hair and scalp issues.

The antiseptic and astringent properties of this herbal preparation help to control oil production and reduce the appearance of oily hair.

The bioactive constituents, including camphor, bornel, and rosmarinic acid, help to balance the scalp's natural pH and reduce inflammation, thereby treating oily hair.

Regular consumption of Rosmarinus officinalis teas has been found to provide numerous benefits, including reduced oiliness, improved scalp health, and a refreshing, clean scalp.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Rosmarinus officinalis leaves or 1 teaspoon of dried leaves.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the Rosmarinus officinalis leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and let it cool before using as a final rinse for oily hair.

Usage:

  1. Mix 2 teaspoons of Rosmarinus officinalis tea with 1 cup of water.
  2. Shampoo your hair as usual, then pour the tea mixture over it.
  3. Leave the tea on for 5-10 minutes, then rinse thoroughly.
  4. Use the tea 1-2 times a week for best results on oily hair.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea as a supplement to improve scalp health.

Rosmarinus officinalis teas can be an effective treatment for oily hair, but it may also cause side effects such as dizziness, nausea, and stomach upset in some individuals due to its high content of essential oils.

To minimize the risk of these side effects, it's essential to start with a low dose, gradually increase the amount as needed, and avoid consuming the tea on an empty stomach, especially for those with sensitive stomachs or allergies to rosemary.

2. Lavandula angustifolia teas

Recipe:

Usage:

Lavandula angustifolia teas can be used to treat oily hair by balancing the scalp's oil production, but it may cause side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, or an increase in dandruff due to its drying properties.
