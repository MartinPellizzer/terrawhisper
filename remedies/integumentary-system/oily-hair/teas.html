1. Lemon balm tea

Lemon balm tea helps with oily hair because it has antiseptic and astringent properties that can help regulate sebum production on the scalp.

The tea's natural components, such as rosmarinic acid and eugenol, can help to cleanse and purify the scalp, reducing the excess oil that can lead to greasy-looking hair. Additionally, lemon balm tea contains antioxidants that can help to protect the hair follicles from damage, promoting healthy hair growth.

Regular use of lemon balm tea as a hair rinse can help to balance the scalp's natural oil production, leaving hair looking clean, fresh, and revitalized.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in lemon balm tea that aid with oily hair.

  • Astringent: Lemon balm tea helps to remove excess oil from the scalp and hair, due to its tannin content which has astringent properties.
  • Antibacterial: It helps to maintain a healthy scalp by preventing the growth of bacteria and fungi, thanks to its rosmarinic acid content which has antibacterial and antifungal properties.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Lemon balm tea can help to reduce inflammation and irritation on the scalp, caused by excess oil production, due to its eugenol content which has anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Antioxidant: It helps to protect the hair and scalp from damage caused by free radicals, due to its high content of rosmarinic acid and other antioxidant compounds.
  • Regulates sebum production: Lemon balm tea can help to regulate sebum production on the scalp, preventing the overproduction of oil, due to its polyphenol content which has been shown to inhibit 5-alpha-reductase, an enzyme that contributes to sebum production.

The list below shows the primary parts of lemon balm tea used to make teas for oily hair.

  • Leaves: The leaves of lemon balm are commonly used in making medicinal teas for oily hair due to their high concentration of essential oils that can help regulate sebum production.
  • Flowers: The flowers of lemon balm contain antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe an itchy scalp and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Stems: Although less commonly used, the stems of lemon balm also contain therapeutic compounds that can help balance oil production in the scalp and strengthen hair follicles.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic lemon balm tea for oily hair.

  1. In a pot, boil 2 cups of water and add 2 tablespoons of dried lemon balm leaves, letting it steep for 15-20 minutes.
  2. After steeping, strain the mixture to remove the lemon balm leaves.
  3. Allow the tea to cool down to a comfortable temperature.
  4. Mix the cooled lemon balm tea with 1 cup of warm water.
  5. Rinse your oily hair with the mixture, massaging it into your scalp, and leaving it on for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out.
