1. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia, also known as tea tree, essential oils helps with oily hair because of its natural astringent and antibacterial properties.

It helps to balance the scalp's natural pH, reducing the production of sebum and preventing clogged pores. The oil's ability to penetrate deep into the hair follicles and scalp also helps to break down excess oil and debris, leaving hair looking and feeling cleaner and healthier.

Medicinal Constituents

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils that help with oily hair.

  • Cineole: It helps reduce sebum production and balances the scalp's natural oil production, leading to a decrease in oily hair.
  • Linalool: It has antiseptic and antifungal properties that help control scalp infections and inflammation, which can contribute to oily hair and dandruff.
  • Piperitone: It has antiseptic and astringent properties, helping to balance the scalp's natural oil production and reduce the appearance of oily hair.

Parts Used

The list below shows the primary parts of tea tree used to make essential oils for oily hair.

  • Leaves: The leaves of Melaleuca alternifolia are commonly used to produce Tea Tree essential oil, which is known for its antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help control oily hair and scalp conditions.
  • Stems: The stems of the plant are also used to produce essential oil, which is rich in compounds like cineole and terpinen-4-ol that help reduce oil production and prevent scalp irritation.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic tea tree for oily hair.

  1. Harvest mature melaleuca alternifolia leaves and stems from the field when the plant is in full bloom.
  2. Steam distill the plant material in a stainless steel apparatus for 2-3 hours to extract the essential oil.
  3. Collect the condensed steam and separate it from the plant material using a separator or distillation apparatus.
  4. Filter the essential oil to remove any impurities or plant residue using a filter paper or cartridge.
  5. Store the melaleuca alternifolia essential oil in a dark glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid to preserve its quality.
