Swollen Feet: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Swollen feet, also known as edema, is a condition characterized by excessive fluid accumulation in the feet, causing them to become puffy, heavy, and sometimes painful.

This condition can be caused by factors such as poor circulation, prolonged standing, pregnancy, and underlying medical conditions like heart disease or kidney problems. If left untreated, swollen feet can lead to complications like skin infections and ulcers.

To alleviate swollen feet, herbal remedies like witch hazel, calendula, and lavender can be used to reduce inflammation and promote blood circulation. A warm bath with witch hazel and calendula can help to ease swelling, while topical creams or ointments infused with lavender oil can promote relaxation and reduce pain.

When using medicinal herbs, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional and follow proper dosing instructions, as some herbs may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of swollen feet, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of swollen feet?

The main causes of swollen feet are fluid retention, increased blood pressure, and poor circulation, which can be attributed to various factors such as obesity, hormonal changes during pregnancy, and menopause.

Additionally, medical conditions such as kidney disease, heart failure, and liver disease can also contribute to swollen feet due to the buildup of toxins in the body. Standing or sitting for prolonged periods can also cause fluid to accumulate in the feet and ankles, leading to swelling. Furthermore, certain medications, such as steroids and certain antidepressants, can cause water retention and swelling in the feet.

In some cases, swollen feet can be a symptom of an underlying condition, such as deep vein thrombosis or lymphedema.

  • Edema: Edema is a medical condition where fluid accumulates in the feet due to poor circulation, heart failure, kidney disease, or liver disease, causing swelling.
  • Ankles Sprain: Ankle sprain is a common injury where the ligaments surrounding the ankle joint are stretched or torn, leading to inflammation and fluid buildup in the feet.
  • Varicose Veins: Varicose veins are abnormally swollen veins in the legs that can cause fluid to leak into the surrounding tissue, resulting in swollen feet.
  • Obesity: Obesity is a medical condition where excess body fat can put pressure on the blood vessels in the legs, leading to poor circulation and swelling in the feet.
  • Kidney Disease: Kidney disease can cause the body to retain fluid, leading to swelling in the feet, ankles, and legs due to the buildup of waste products and excess fluids.
  • Pregnancy: Pregnancy can cause fluid retention and swelling in the feet due to the increased blood volume and pressure on the blood vessels in the legs.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Hormonal imbalances, particularly those caused by thyroid or adrenal gland disorders, can lead to fluid retention and swelling in the feet.
  • Infection: Infection, such as cellulitis or abscesses, can cause swelling in the feet due to inflammation and fluid buildup in the affected area.
  • Injury: Physical injuries, such as broken bones or muscle strains, can cause swelling in the feet due to inflammation and fluid buildup in the affected area.
  • Medication Side Effect: Certain medications, such as steroids or antidepressants, can cause fluid retention and swelling in the feet as a side effect.
