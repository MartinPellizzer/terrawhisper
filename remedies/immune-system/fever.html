What to know about fever before treating it with medicinal herbs

A fever is an abnormally high body temperature, typically above 100.4°F (38°C), caused by an imbalance in the body's regulatory mechanisms or as a response to infection.

What is fever and how it affects your life?

Fever is a pesky unwanted guest that can turn your world upside down.

It's an unwelcome visitor that arrives uninvited, bringing with it a bundle of misery and discomfort. When you're struck by a fever, you'll likely find yourself feeling achy all over, with a throbbing headache and a body temperature that seems to be on fire. Simple tasks like getting out of bed or taking a shower become daunting challenges as fatigue washes over you.

You may struggle to concentrate at work or school, your usual productivity replaced by a foggy brain and a sense of lethargy. Even social gatherings can become torture, as you'd rather curl up in a ball than attempt to engage with others. Fever can also wreak havoc on your appetite, leaving you feeling nauseous and unwilling to eat a thing. And to make matters worse, it's not uncommon for fever to disrupt sleep patterns, leading to restless nights and grumpy mornings.

Overall, living with a fever is a miserable experience that can quickly turn your daily routine into a never-ending struggle.

What are the main causes of fever?

The main causes of fever are usually related to infections, inflammation, or an overactive immune response.

When our body detects the presence of pathogens such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites, it responds by increasing its internal temperature, which is a natural defense mechanism to combat the infection. This can occur through various means, including the production of chemical mediators like pyrogens, which stimulate the hypothalamus in the brain to raise body temperature. Additionally, inflammation caused by tissue damage or injury can also trigger a fever response as the body attempts to repair and heal the affected area.

In some cases, fevers may also be caused by an overactive immune response, such as in autoimmune disorders like rheumatoid arthritis or lupus, where the body mistakenly attacks its own tissues, leading to inflammation and increased body temperature.

The most common causes of fever are listed below.

  • Common Cold: The common cold is a viral infection that affects the upper respiratory tract, causing symptoms such as runny nose, sore throat, and cough. Fever can occur in some cases.
  • Influenza (Flu): Influenza is a contagious respiratory illness caused by the influenza virus, characterized by symptoms such as fever, cough, sore throat, and fatigue.
  • Urinary Tract Infections (UTIs): UTIs are bacterial infections that occur in the urinary tract, often causing symptoms such as burning sensation while urinating, frequent urination, and abdominal pain.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis is an inflammation or infection of the sinuses, which can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi, resulting in symptoms such as headache, facial pain, and nasal congestion.
  • Tonsillitis: Tonsillitis is an inflammation of the tonsils, often caused by bacterial or viral infections, resulting in symptoms such as sore throat, fever, and swollen lymph nodes.
  • Middle Ear Infections (Otitis Media): Otitis media is a type of infection that occurs in the middle ear, usually caused by bacteria, and can result in symptoms such as ear pain, fever, and difficulty hearing.
  • Pneumonia: Pneumonia is an inflammation or infection of the lungs, often caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi, resulting in symptoms such as cough, chest pain, and difficulty breathing.
  • Kidney Infections (Pyelonephritis): Pyelonephritis is a type of urinary tract infection that affects one or both kidneys, causing symptoms such as flank pain, fever, and frequent urination.
  • Gastroenteritis: Gastroenteritis, also known as food poisoning, is an inflammation of the stomach and intestines caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites, resulting in symptoms such as diarrhea, vomiting, and abdominal cramps.
  • Viral Gastroenteritis (Stomach Flu): Viral gastroenteritis is a type of viral infection that affects the stomach and intestines, causing symptoms such as diarrhea, vomiting, and abdominal cramps.
