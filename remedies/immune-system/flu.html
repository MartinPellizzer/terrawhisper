What is flu and how it affects your life?

The flu, also known as influenza, is a highly contagious respiratory illness that can wreak havoc on one's daily life.

When you're struck with the flu, you'll often wake up feeling tired and sluggish, with a scratchy throat and aching body. As the day goes on, your energy levels will plummet, making it difficult to perform even the simplest tasks, like getting out of bed or preparing a meal. Your usual routine is turned upside down as you're forced to cancel plans with friends and family, miss work or school, and struggle to keep up with household chores.

The flu's debilitating symptoms can also disrupt your mental well-being, leaving you feeling irritable, anxious, and depressed. You may find yourself constantly coughing and sneezing, which can be embarrassing in public and frustrating at home. Even simple activities like watching TV or reading a book become exhausting endeavors, as the fatigue and discomfort of the flu make it hard to focus and relax.

The flu's impact on daily life is far-reaching, leaving you feeling helpless and frustrated as you struggle to recover from this dreaded illness.

What are the main causes of flu?

The main causes of flu are a combination of viral and environmental factors.

Influenza viruses, which are highly contagious, are the primary cause of the flu. There are three main types of influenza viruses - A, B, and C - and they can spread through the air when an infected person talks, coughs, or sneezes, as well as through close contact with an infected person's respiratory droplets. The flu virus is also highly adaptable and can mutate frequently, which allows it to evade immunity and cause new strains of the disease.

Additionally, certain environmental factors such as stress, lack of sleep, and poor nutrition can weaken a person's immune system, making them more susceptible to contracting the flu.

The most common causes of flu are listed below.

  • Influenza A virus: This type of virus is responsible for causing seasonal outbreaks and epidemics, and can range from mild to severe in severity.
  • Influenza B virus: Similar to influenza A, this virus also causes seasonal outbreaks and can cause a range of symptoms from mild to severe.
  • Influenza C virus: This type of virus is less well-known and tends to cause milder symptoms than influenza A or B viruses.
  • Human coronavirus NL63: This virus is a common cause of the common cold, but can also cause more serious respiratory illness in young children and people with weakened immune systems.
  • RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus): RSV is a highly contagious virus that causes severe respiratory illness in young children and older adults.
  • Parainfluenza virus: This virus causes symptoms similar to the common cold, but can also cause more serious respiratory illnesses like bronchiolitis and pneumonia.
  • Adenovirus: This type of virus is known for causing respiratory infections like the common cold, but can also cause conjunctivitis (pink eye) and gastrointestinal illness.
  • Enteroviruses: This group of viruses includes viruses that cause symptoms ranging from mild to severe, including hand-foot-and-mouth disease, herpangina, and meningitis.
  • Human metapneumovirus: This virus causes respiratory illnesses like bronchiolitis and pneumonia, particularly in young children and older adults.
  • HCoV-HKU1: This coronavirus is a less well-known cause of respiratory illness, but can cause symptoms similar to those caused by HCoV-NL63.

What precautions to take when using herbal remedies for flu?

When considering the use of herbal remedies to alleviate symptoms of the flu, it's essential to exercise caution and consult with a healthcare professional first.

While certain herbs like echinacea, ginger, and elderberry may have antiviral properties and potential health benefits, they should not be used as a replacement for medical treatment. Before using herbal remedies, individuals with a confirmed case of the flu or suspected flu infection should ensure they are receiving appropriate medical care, such as antiviral medication if recommended by their healthcare provider. Additionally, it's crucial to research the herb and its interactions with other medications, as well as potential side effects, allergies, or contraindications.

Furthermore, herbal remedies should not be used in place of essential flu prevention measures, such as getting vaccinated, practicing good hygiene, and staying hydrated.

The most important precautions to take when using herbal remedies for flu are listed below.

  • Research thoroughly before trying any herbal remedy for flu
  • Consult with a healthcare professional before taking any new supplements
  • Read labels carefully and check expiration dates

  • Be aware of potential interactions with prescription medications
  • Check the quality and purity of the herbal product
  • Monitor for side effects and allergic reactions
  • Follow recommended dosages and instructions
  • Keep in mind that herbal remedies may not be regulated by the FDA
