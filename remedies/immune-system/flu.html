What to know about flu before treating it with medicinal herbs

Flu, also known as influenza, is a viral respiratory infection that can cause mild to severe symptoms such as fever, cough, sore throat, runny or stuffy nose, muscle or body aches, headaches, and fatigue.

The flu can have a significant impact on people's lives, leading to missed work or school, hospitalization, and in some cases, death. The virus is primarily spread through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. Several herbs have been found to be effective in managing flu symptoms. Echinacea, for example, can boost the immune system and reduce the severity of symptoms.

These herbs can be taken as teas, tinctures, or capsules. However, it's important to take precautions when using herbs medicinally for flu. Pregnant women, people with autoimmune diseases, and those taking certain medications should consult with a healthcare provider before using herbs.

What is flu and how it affects your life?

The flu, also known as influenza, is a highly contagious viral infection that primarily attacks the respiratory system.

Once contracted, it can lead to a variety of uncomfortable and sometimes severe symptoms, such as fever, chills, body aches, fatigue, sore throat, and cough. These symptoms can have a significant negative impact on an individual's daily life, causing them to miss work or school, and experience a decrease in productivity and energy levels. Furthermore, the flu can also lead to more serious complications, such as pneumonia, bronchitis, and ear infections, which may require hospitalization and can even be life-threatening.

Additionally, the flu can also have a social impact, as it is easily spread through coughing, sneezing, and touching, which can lead to the infection of others, particularly those who are more vulnerable, such as young children, the elderly, and people with weakened immune systems.
