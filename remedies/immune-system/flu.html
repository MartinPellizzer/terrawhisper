Flu: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

The flu, or influenza, is a highly contagious respiratory illness caused by the influenza virus, characterized by symptoms such as fever, cough, sore throat, runny nose, and body aches.

It typically occurs when the virus infects the nose, throat, or lungs, and can be triggered by factors such as stress, fatigue, and weakened immune systems. If left untreated, the flu can lead to serious health complications, including pneumonia, bronchitis, and sinus and ear infections. Fortunately, various herbal remedies like ginger, elderberry, and echinacea have been found to effectively alleviate flu symptoms.

To use these herbs safely, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional and follow proper dosages, as some herbs can interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of flu, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of flu?

The main causes of flu are primarily attributed to the influenza A and B viruses, which are highly contagious respiratory pathogens.

These viruses are spread through the air by respiratory droplets generated when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks, allowing the virus to be transmitted from person to person. In addition, the flu virus can also be spread through contact with contaminated surfaces and objects, as well as through close contact with an infected person, such as touching or shaking hands. Furthermore, individuals who work in high-risk settings, such as healthcare workers or those in childcare or education, are more likely to be exposed to the flu virus due to increased contact with large numbers of people.

In rare cases, the flu virus can also be transmitted through contaminated food and water, particularly in areas with inadequate sanitation and hygiene practices.

  • Rhino: Rhino viruses are one of the most common causes of the flu, as they are highly contagious and can be spread through respiratory droplets, close contact, and contaminated surfaces.
  • Influenza A: Influenza A viruses are a common cause of the flu, as they can cause seasonal outbreaks and are responsible for a significant number of flu-related hospitalizations and deaths each year.
  • Influenza B: Influenza B viruses are another common cause of the flu, as they can cause mild to severe illness and are often responsible for outbreaks in schools and healthcare settings.
  • Adenovirus: Adenoviruses are a common cause of the flu, especially in children and young adults, as they can cause respiratory illness and are highly contagious.
  • Coronavirus: Coronaviruses, such as SARS-CoV and SARS-CoV-2, are a common cause of the flu, as they can cause respiratory illness and are highly contagious.
  • Respiratory Syncytial Virus: Respiratory syncytial viruses are a common cause of the flu, especially in young children and older adults, as they can cause respiratory illness and are highly contagious.
  • Parainfluenza Virus: Parainfluenza viruses are a common cause of the flu, especially in young children, as they can cause respiratory illness and are highly contagious.
  • Human Metapneumovirus: Human metapneumoviruses are a common cause of the flu, especially in young children and older adults, as they can cause respiratory illness and are highly contagious.
