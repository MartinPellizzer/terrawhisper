What is inflammation and how it affects your life?

Inflammation is the body's natural response to injury or infection, characterized by redness, heat, swelling, and pain.

While it is a necessary process for healing, chronic inflammation can have negative effects on one's health and quality of life. For example, it can lead to the development of various diseases such as arthritis, causing joint pain and stiffness that can make daily activities difficult.

Inflammation in the digestive tract can result in conditions like inflammatory bowel disease, leading to abdominal pain, diarrhea, and weight loss. Chronic inflammation has also been linked to an increased risk of heart disease, stroke, and cancer. Furthermore, it can negatively impact mental health, contributing to depression and anxiety.

Therefore, while inflammation is a vital process, unchecked or prolonged inflammation can have significant negative consequences on one's health and well-being.
