1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus essential oils contains compounds like cineole, limonene, and alpha-pinene that are known to ease allergy symptoms.

When inhaled, these oils can help reduce inflammation in the nasal passages and sinuses, making it easier to breathe. This is because they have anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties that can help break down mucus and open up airways.

To use Eucalyptus globulus essential oils for allergies, try inhaling it through steam inhalation or a diffuser. You can also add a few drops to your bathwater or apply it to your chest and temples using a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil. When inhaled, you may start to feel relief from congestion and sinus pressure within 15-30 minutes.

The likely outcome is that you will be able to breathe more easily, and your symptoms such as sneezing and coughing may reduce.

2. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia essential oils contains compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties.

This helps to reduce the symptoms of allergies, such as itching, congestion, and redness. When inhaled, the oil can also help to relax the body and mind, which can further alleviate allergy symptoms.

To use Lavandula angustifolia essential oils for allergies, you can add a few drops to a diffuser or inhale it directly from a cloth or handkerchief. When using Lavandula angustifolia essential oils for allergies, you may notice a reduction in your symptoms, such as less itching, congestion, and redness.

You may also feel more relaxed and calm, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety related to allergies.

3. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils contains compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These properties help to combat the root cause of allergic reactions, which is often an overactive immune response. The essential oils contain constituents such as cineole, limonene, and beta-pinene, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and fight off pathogens that can trigger allergic reactions. When using Melaleuca alternifolia essential oils for allergies, it's recommended to apply a few drops to the affected area, inhale it through steam inhalation, or add it to a warm bath.
