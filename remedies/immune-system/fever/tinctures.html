FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent fever from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce a fever, but it won't prevent it from forming in the first place.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe a sore throat and calm the body.

However, if your body is fighting an infection, it's likely to develop a fever, even if you drink herbal tea.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for fever every day?

It's generally okay to drink herbal teas for fever regularly, but be careful with the amount and type.

Some herbal teas, like peppermint and ginger, are soothing and gentle. However, others, like those with ephedra or yarrow, can be too strong and cause problems.

Just stick to small amounts and see how your body reacts.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in fever?

Herbal teas like peppermint and ginger can help reduce fever in a few hours.

They work by thinning mucus and making it easier to breathe. Some people notice relief within 30 minutes to an hour, while others may take a few hours to feel better.

The timing depends on the severity of the fever and the individual's body.
