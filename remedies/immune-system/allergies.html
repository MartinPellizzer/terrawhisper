What to know about allergies before treating it with medicinal herbs

Allergies are an overreaction of the immune system to typically harmless substances, causing symptoms such as itching, sneezing, and inflammation.

These allergens can be inhaled, ingested, or come into contact with the skin. Negative impacts of allergies include discomfort, decreased quality of life, and potential for life-threatening reactions. Common causes of allergies include pollen, dust mites, and certain foods.

Medicinal herbs such as butterbur, stinging nettle, and turmeric have been found to alleviate allergy symptoms. Butterbur can be taken as a capsule or tea, stinging nettle as a tea or tincture, and turmeric as a capsule or added to food. Precautions when using herbs medicinally for allergies include potential interactions with other medications, and the possibility of allergic reactions to the herbs themselves.

It is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new herbal regimen.

What is allergies and how it affects your life?

Allergies refer to an overreaction of the immune system to harmless substances called allergens, which can be found in various things such as pollen, dust mites, pet dander, certain foods, and insect venom.

When the body encounters an allergen, it releases chemicals called histamines, which cause symptoms such as itching, sneezing, runny nose, congestion, hives, and swelling. Allergies can significantly affect a person's quality of life by causing discomfort, disrupting daily activities, and leading to missed work or school days. In severe cases, allergies can trigger life-threatening reactions called anaphylaxis, which requires immediate medical attention.

Additionally, allergies can also lead to other health problems such as sinus infections, asthma, and ear infections, further impacting a person's overall well-being.
