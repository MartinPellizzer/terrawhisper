What is allergy and how it affects your life?

Allergies are a common condition that can significantly impact daily life, causing discomfort, distress, and even danger.

When an individual has an allergy, their body overreacts to a normally harmless substance, such as food, pollen, dust mites, mold, pet dander, or certain medications. This overreaction leads to the release of chemicals like histamine, which cause a range of symptoms including hives, itching, swelling, stomach cramps, diarrhea, and difficulty breathing.

For example, someone with a peanut allergy may break out in hives and experience severe anaphylaxis if they accidentally ingest even a small amount of peanuts, requiring immediate medical attention. Similarly, individuals with seasonal allergies may suffer from constant congestion, itchy eyes, and fatigue during peak pollen seasons, making everyday activities like walking or working outdoors unbearable. Others may experience debilitating reactions to certain foods, such as life-threatening anaphylaxis following ingestion of shellfish or severe stomach cramps after consuming gluten-containing products.

In addition, allergies can also lead to missed work or school days, cancelled social events, and increased reliance on medications, ultimately affecting one's quality of life and overall well-being.

What are the main causes of allergy?

The main causes of allergies are the overreaction of the immune system to certain substances, such as proteins, pollen, dust mites, and pet dander.

This overreaction occurs when the immune system mistakenly identifies these harmless substances as harmful invaders, triggering an immune response that leads to the release of histamine and other chemical mediators. These chemicals cause the characteristic symptoms of allergies, including itching, sneezing, runny nose, and watery eyes. In some cases, this reaction can be severe and even life-threatening, as in the case of anaphylaxis.

The main causes of allergies are genetic predisposition, environmental factors such as exposure to allergens at a young age, and changes in the gut microbiome that disrupt the normal functioning of the immune system.

The most common causes of allergy are listed below.

  • Peanuts: Peanut allergy is one of the most severe and potentially life-threatening food allergies, causing anaphylaxis in some cases.
  • Dust mites: Dust mites are tiny insects that live in bedding, carpets, and upholstered furniture, producing waste that can trigger allergic reactions.
  • Pollen: Pollen from trees, grasses, and weeds is a common allergen that causes hay fever (allergic rhinitis) and asthma symptoms.
  • Milk: Milk allergy is an immune reaction to one of the proteins in milk, often causing symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.
  • Eggs: Egg allergy is another common food allergy, triggering reactions like hives, itching, and swelling when egg protein is consumed.
  • Tree nuts: Tree nut allergy is similar to peanut allergy, causing anaphylaxis in some cases, especially when the allergenic proteins are ingested.
  • Wheat: Wheat allergy is a reaction to one of the proteins in wheat, often manifesting as symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling after consuming baked goods or other wheat products.
  • Soy: Soy allergy is an immune response to soy protein, commonly causing symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling when soybeans or soy-based products are consumed.
  • Fish: Fish allergy is a reaction to the proteins in fish, often triggering symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling after consuming fish or seafood.
  • Sulfa drugs: Sulfa drug allergy is a reaction to certain antibiotics, such as sulfamethoxazole (Bactrim) and trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (Septra), causing symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

What precautions to take when using herbal remedies for allergy?

Firstly, consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new herbal supplement, as some herbs can interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions. Secondly, choose reputable brands that source high-quality ingredients and follow proper manufacturing processes to minimize contamination risks.

Additionally, be aware of potential allergic reactions to individual herbs or interactions between multiple herbs, and start with small doses to monitor for adverse effects. Finally, note that herbal remedies are not regulated by the FDA like pharmaceuticals, so it's crucial to research the scientific evidence supporting their use and effectiveness in treating allergies.

By taking these precautions, you can minimize risks and maximize benefits when using herbal remedies to alleviate allergy symptoms.
