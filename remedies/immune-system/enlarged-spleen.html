What is enlarged spleen and how it affects your life?

An enlarged spleen, also known as splenomegaly, is a medical condition where the spleen becomes larger than normal due to various reasons, leading to a range of uncomfortable and potentially life-altering symptoms.

An enlarged spleen can cause the abdomen to feel heavy or tender to the touch, and in severe cases, it can even lead to breathing difficulties due to the spleen compressing adjacent organs. Furthermore, it can cause fatigue, weakness, and shortness of breath due to the spleen's reduced ability to filter the blood. Simple everyday activities like climbing stairs, exercising, or even walking can become exhausting.

Digestion and nutrient absorption can also be affected, leading to weight loss and decreased appetite. Additionally, an enlarged spleen can lead to bruising, swelling, and pain in various parts of the body, as well as an increased risk of bleeding and infection. In extreme cases, it can even lead to jaundice, a condition characterized by a yellowish discoloration of the skin and eyes due to an accumulation of bilirubin.

Overall, an enlarged spleen can significantly disrupt daily life, making even the simplest tasks a challenge.

What are the main causes of enlarged spleen?

An enlarged spleen, also known as splenomegaly, is a medical condition where the spleen, an organ located in the upper left side of the abdomen, becomes abnormally large.

This can cause a range of symptoms and complications that can significantly impact daily life. For example, a person with an enlarged spleen may experience severe pain in the left upper abdomen, which can be exacerbated by physical activity or deep breathing. They may also feel tired and weak due to anemia, a condition where the spleen can absorb red blood cells at a rate that is faster than the body can replace them.

Furthermore, an enlarged spleen can put pressure on surrounding organs, leading to difficulty breathing, digestive issues, and even increased risk of bleeding or bruising due to decreased platelet function. Additionally, the enlarged spleen can also make it difficult to engage in physical activities, as even minor movements can cause discomfort or pain. In extreme cases, the spleen may rupture, leading to life-threatening internal bleeding.

Overall, an enlarged spleen can significantly reduce a person's quality of life, affecting not just their physical well-being but also their mental and emotional state.
