7 Best Herbal Teas For Flu

Herbal teas for flu are a type of natural remedy that involves brewing leaves, flowers, roots, or fruits from various plants to create a soothing and medicinal drink.

These teas are effective in treating the flu ailment due to their antiviral and antibacterial properties, which help to boost the immune system and alleviate symptoms such as fever, cough, and congestion.

Some examples of herbal teas used to treat the flu include ginger tea, which helps to reduce nausea and inflammation, peppermint tea, which eases respiratory issues and soothes a sore throat, and echinacea tea, which enhances the body's natural defense against infections.

Other herbal teas, such as elderberry tea, thyme tea, and yarrow tea, also possess antiviral properties that can help to combat the flu virus, making them a popular choice for those looking for a natural and holistic approach to managing the flu.

1. Echinacea purpurea teas

Echinacea purpurea teas have been used for centuries to help alleviate the symptoms of the flu, leveraging its anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties.

The bioactive constituents of Echinacea purpurea, including alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids, work together to reduce inflammation, modulate the immune response, and inhibit the replication of viral particles.

By consuming Echinacea purpurea teas, individuals may experience a reduction in the severity and duration of flu symptoms, such as fever, cough, and fatigue.

The benefits of using Echinacea purpurea teas to treat the flu include promoting a faster recovery, reducing the risk of complications, and providing a natural, non-pharmacological approach to managing this common illness.

Related Study

According to "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", Echinacea purpurea teas for flu, specifically in the form of Echinaforce and its concentrated preparation, were found to be significantly more effective than the placebo in reducing symptoms when taken for the treatment of common cold.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Measure 2 tablespoons of honey (optional) and set it aside.
  3. Pour boiling water over the dried Echinacea flowers in a cup.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the flowers.
  5. Add honey to taste, if using, and drink the tea immediately.

Usage:

  1. Drink 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea tea, 3 times a day.
  2. Start taking tea as soon as flu symptoms appear.
  3. Continue tea consumption for 5-7 days to reduce symptoms.
  4. Use 1-2 teaspoons of dried root or 1 tablespoon of fresh root per cup.
  5. Consult a healthcare professional for dosage and frequency adjustments.

Echinacea purpurea teas can be used to alleviate flu symptoms, but they may cause side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea in some individuals, particularly when consumed in excess or for extended periods.

To minimize potential side effects, individuals should start with small doses, avoid taking Echinacea purpurea teas during pregnancy and breastfeeding, and be cautious when combining them with other medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications.

2. Eucalyptus globulus teas

Eucalyptus globulus teas have been traditionally used to treat the flu ailment due to their decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties, which help to relieve symptoms such as cough, sore throat, and body aches.

The bioactive constituents of Eucalyptus globulus, including eucalyptol, cineole, and beta-pinene, have been shown to exhibit antiviral and antimicrobial activities, which can help to combat the underlying viral infection.

By reducing inflammation and congestion in the respiratory tract, Eucalyptus globulus teas can help to alleviate flu symptoms and promote a speedy recovery.

The benefits of using Eucalyptus globulus teas to treat the flu include natural relief from symptoms, reduced risk of complications, and a lower risk of antibiotic resistance.

Related Study

According to "European journal of integrative medicine", Eucalyptus globulus teas were found to be one of the most frequently used remedies by herbalists in Salé Prefecture for the prevention and treatment of COVID-19.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh eucalyptus leaves (Eucalyptus globulus).
  2. Combine the leaves with 1 quart (4 cups) of boiling water in a large pot.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the liquid.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to taste, if desired, and mix well.
  5. Drink 1 cup of the tea 3-4 times a day to help alleviate flu symptoms.

Usage:

  2. Take 1 tablespoon of Eucalyptus oil in hot water 2 times a day.
  3. Use Eucalyptus globulus tea bags or loose tea for 5-7 days.
  4. Combine Eucalyptus globulus tea with lemon and honey for taste.
  5. Consult doctor before giving Eucalyptus globulus tea to children under 12.

Eucalyptus globulus teas can be used to treat the flu, but it may cause side effects such as stomach upset, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications, particularly blood thinners and diabetes medications.

When using Eucalyptus globulus teas to treat the flu, it is essential to take precautions, such as starting with small amounts, avoiding consumption in large quantities, and being cautious if you have kidney or liver problems, as excessive consumption may exacerbate these conditions.

3. Zingiber officinale teas

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been traditionally used to treat the flu ailment due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

This herbal preparation helps to treat the flu by soothing the throat, reducing fever, and alleviating nausea and vomiting associated with the illness.

The bioactive constituents of ginger tea, including gingerols and shogaols, are responsible for its medicinal properties, which help to combat the flu by inhibiting the replication of viral particles and modulating the immune response.

The benefits of ginger tea in treating the flu include its ability to reduce the severity and duration of symptoms, promote relaxation and reduce fatigue, and provide a natural and non-addictive alternative to pharmaceutical medications.

Related Study

According to the study, Zingiber officinale teas for flu may be beneficial due to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunostimulatory properties, which can help in stimulating immune systems and lowering the risk of severe flu complications.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of water and 1 tablespoon of fresh Zingiber officinale (ginger) root
  2. Peel the ginger root and slice it thinly
  3. Boil the water in a pot and add the sliced ginger
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for 5-7 minutes, then strain the tea
  5. Drink the tea warm, 2-3 times a day to help relieve flu symptoms

Usage:

  1. Drink 1 cup of Zingiber officinale tea 2-3 times a day.
  2. Start with 1 tablespoon of dried ginger per cup of hot water.
  3. Adjust dosage to 1-2 teaspoons if symptoms persist after 3 days.
  4. Use Zingiber officinale tea for 7-10 days or until symptoms subside.
  5. Consult a doctor if flu symptoms worsen or last longer than 10 days.

Zingiber officinale teas can be an effective remedy to alleviate flu symptoms, but consuming it may lead to side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea, especially when consumed in large quantities.

To use Zingiber officinale teas safely, it's essential to start with small doses, avoid it if you have digestive issues, and be cautious when combining it with other medications, as it may interact with them and exacerbate existing health conditions.

4. Sambucus nigra teas

Sambucus nigra teas, also known as elderberry tea, have been traditionally used to treat the flu ailment due to their antiviral properties.

The bioactive constituents of Sambucus nigra, including anthocyanins and flavonoids, help to inhibit the replication of the influenza virus, reducing the severity and duration of the flu.

The sambucol, a flavonoid compound found in Sambucus nigra, has been shown to have immunomodulatory effects, helping to boost the body's immune system and fight off the infection.

Drinking Sambucus nigra teas has been reported to provide relief from flu symptoms, such as fever, fatigue, and body aches, and may also help to reduce the risk of complications associated with the flu.

Related Study

According to "Phytotherapy research : PTR", Sambucus nigra teas for flu may offer some protection and alleviation of disease symptoms due to its immunomodulatory, immunostimulatory, and anti-inflammatory effects, although more research is needed to fully establish its efficacy and safety.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Sambucus nigra flowers and leaves.
  2. Measure 250ml of water in a pot and bring it to a boil.
  3. Add 2 tablespoons of the Sambucus nigra flowers and leaves to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink 1-2 cups throughout the day to help relieve flu symptoms.

Usage:

  1. Consult doctor before using Sambucus nigra teas for flu treatment.
  2. Start with 1 cup, 3 times a day, for 5-7 days.
  3. Gradually increase to 2 cups, 3 times a day, if needed.
  4. Monitor temperature, symptoms, and overall health.
  5. Stop using teas if symptoms worsen or persist.

Sambucus nigra teas can be used to treat the flu, but it may cause side effects such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with other medications, particularly blood thinners and diabetes medications.

Precautions should be taken when using Sambucus nigra teas, including avoiding excessive consumption, which can lead to drowsiness, and being mindful of potential interactions with other herbal preparations and substances that may exacerbate the flu symptoms.

5. Ginkgo biloba teas

Ginkgo biloba teas have been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of the flu, thanks to their anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and immune-boosting properties.

The bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide, help to reduce inflammation, fight off viral infections, and stimulate the production of white blood cells to combat the flu.

By consuming Ginkgo biloba teas, individuals can experience relief from fever, cough, and fatigue associated with the flu, as well as improve their overall well-being.

The benefits of using Ginkgo biloba teas to treat the flu include reduced risk of complications, shorter recovery time, and a stronger immune system to prevent future illnesses.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of honey (optional) for sweetness.
  3. Steep the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Add the honey (if using) and stir well before drinking.

Usage:

  1. Drink 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba tea 3 times a day for 3-5 days.
  2. Start with 1 tablespoon of Ginkgo biloba per cup of boiling water.
  3. Increase dosage to 2 tablespoons per cup if symptoms persist.
  4. Combine Ginkgo biloba tea with rest, hydration, and a balanced diet.
  5. Consult a doctor if symptoms worsen or last longer than 5 days.

Ginkgo biloba teas can help alleviate flu symptoms, but may cause side effects such as dizziness, stomach upset, and allergic reactions in some individuals, especially when consumed in large quantities or combined with other medications.

To use Ginkgo biloba teas safely, take small amounts regularly and avoid consuming it with blood-thinning medications, as it may enhance their effects and increase the risk of bleeding complications.

Rosmarinus officinalis teas, derived from the leaves of the rosemary plant, have been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of the flu.

Rosmarinus officinalis teas can be used to help alleviate symptoms of the flu, but it may cause side effects such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with certain medications.
