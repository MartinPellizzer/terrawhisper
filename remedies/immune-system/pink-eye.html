Pink Eye: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Pink eye, medically known as conjunctivitis, is an inflammation or infection of the transparent outer layer that covers the white part of the eye and the inside of the eyelid.

Caused by bacteria, viruses, allergens, or irritants, it can lead to discomfort, redness, and vision impairment. If left untreated, pink eye can cause complications such as corneal ulcers or even blindness. To alleviate symptoms, herbal remedies like calendula, echinacea, and tea tree oil can be used to create eye drops or compresses.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs to avoid any adverse reactions, especially if you have pre-existing conditions or take medications.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of pink eye, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of pink eye?

The main causes of pink eye are viral or bacterial infections, with adenovirus being the most common cause of viral pink eye, accounting for approximately 70% of cases.

Bacterial pink eye, often caused by Staphylococcus aureus, can be spread through direct contact with infected individuals or contaminated surfaces. Poor hygiene, sharing makeup or makeup brushes, and touching the eyes without washing hands can also contribute to the spread of pink eye. Additionally, individuals with weakened immune systems, such as those with HIV/AIDS or undergoing chemotherapy, are more susceptible to pink eye.

Other factors, including allergies, chlorine exposure, and foreign objects in the eye, can also trigger pink eye symptoms.

  • Viral: Viral conjunctivitis is caused by an infection with a virus, such as the common cold or flu, which leads to inflammation and redness of the conjunctiva, causing pink eye.
  • Bacterial: Bacterial conjunctivitis is caused by a bacterial infection, typically with Streptococcus pneumoniae or Haemophilus influenzae, which leads to inflammation and redness of the conjunctiva, causing pink eye.
  • Allergies: Allergic conjunctivitis is caused by an allergic reaction to substances such as pollen, dust, or pet dander, which leads to inflammation and redness of the conjunctiva, causing pink eye.
  • Chemical: Chemical conjunctivitis is caused by exposure to chemicals or irritants, such as chlorine or ammonia, which leads to inflammation and redness of the conjunctiva, causing pink eye.
  • Physical: Physical conjunctivitis is caused by physical factors, such as foreign bodies or exposure to wind or sun, which leads to inflammation and redness of the conjunctiva, causing pink eye.
  • Fungal: Fungal conjunctivitis is caused by a fungal infection, which leads to inflammation and redness of the conjunctiva, causing pink eye, often seen in people with weakened immune systems.
  • Parasitic: Parasitic conjunctivitis is caused by a parasitic infection, such as toxoplasmosis, which leads to inflammation and redness of the conjunctiva, causing pink eye.
