1. Curcuma longa

Turmeric capsules help with inflammation because they contain curcumin, a potent bioactive compound that has natural anti-inflammatory properties.

Curcumin works by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and reducing oxidative stress in the body. This results in decreased swelling, pain, and stiffness associated with conditions such as arthritis, gout, and joint pain.

Additionally, curcumin's antioxidant properties help to neutralize free radicals, further reducing inflammation and promoting overall health and wellbeing.

Medicinal Constituents

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Curcuma longa capsules that help with inflammation.

  • Curcumin: A polyphenolic compound that helps reduce inflammation by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, thereby reducing oxidative stress and inflammation.
  • Demethoxycurcumin: A bioactive compound that exhibits anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, helping to reduce inflammation by suppressing the activity of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines.
  • Bisdemethoxycurcumin: A curcuminoid that helps reduce inflammation by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, suppressing the activity of inflammatory cytokines, and exhibiting antioxidant properties.

Parts Used

The list below shows the primary parts of turmeric used to make capsules for inflammation.

  • Rhyzomes: Turmeric rhyzomes are primarily used due to their high concentration of curcumin, a bioactive compound with potent anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Roots: The roots of Curcuma longa also contain curcumin, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory effects and is often used in capsule form for this purpose.
  • Seeds: Turmeric seeds, although less commonly used, may be included in some capsule blends for their potential anti-inflammatory properties, although their efficacy is less well-studied compared to rhyzomes and roots.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric for inflammation.

  1. Weigh 500 grams of curcuma longa rhizomes to ensure accurate dosage for capsule production.
  2. Grind 200 grams of the weighed rhizomes into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle.
  3. Mix 20 grams of gelatin with 50 milliliters of distilled water in a separate bowl.
  4. Combine the gelatin mixture with the curcuma longa powder and stir until well combined.
  5. Fill a capsule machine with the mixture and fill 200 capsules in about 10 minutes.

Parts Used

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger for inflammation.

  3. Mix 250 mg of the powder with 500 mg of gelatin powder in a small bowl.
  4. Add 250 ml of distilled water to the bowl and stir the mixture until the gelatin dissolves completely.
  5. Fill gelatin capsules with the herbal mixture and seal them immediately to preserve freshness.

  4. Mix 1 part of the powdered bark with 2 parts of a binding agent like starch or cellulose to form a uniform blend.
  5. Fill gelatin capsules with 200-300 milligrams of the powder blend and seal them immediately.
