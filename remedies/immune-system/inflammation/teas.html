1. Yarrow tea

Yarrow tea helps with inflammation because it contains anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce inflammation in the body.

The herb, scientifically known as Achillea millefolium, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including inflammation. Yarrow tea is rich in antioxidants, flavonoids, and sesquiterpene lactones, which are known to have anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds work by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which are responsible for triggering the inflammatory response in the body.

By reducing inflammation, yarrow tea may help alleviate symptoms of various inflammatory conditions, such as arthritis, muscle pain, and skin irritation.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in yarrow tea that aid with inflammation.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Yarrow tea contains anti-inflammatory compounds like flavonoids and sesquiterpene lactones, which help reduce inflammation by inhibiting the production of inflammatory chemicals in the body.
  • Analgesic: Yarrow tea has pain-relieving properties due to the presence of alkaloids and lactones, which help alleviate pain and discomfort associated with inflammation.
  • Antioxidant: Yarrow tea is rich in antioxidants, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, that help protect cells from oxidative stress and damage, thereby reducing inflammation.
  • Astringent: Yarrow tea has astringent properties due to its tannin content, which helps constrict blood vessels and reduce swelling, providing relief from inflammation.
  • Immune-boosting: Yarrow tea contains compounds like alkaloids, flavonoids, and polyacetylenes that support the immune system and help the body fight off infections, reducing the risk of inflammation caused by pathogens.

The list below shows the primary parts of yarrow tea used to make teas for inflammation.

  • Flowers: Yarrow flowers are rich in anti-inflammatory compounds, making them ideal for reducing inflammation when brewed as a tea.
  • Leaves: Yarrow leaves contain potent anti-inflammatory properties, making them useful for creating medicinal teas to alleviate inflammation.
  • Stems: Yarrow stems have anti-inflammatory effects, making them suitable for use in teas designed to reduce inflammation.
  • Roots: Yarrow roots are known for their anti-inflammatory properties, making them an excellent choice for medicinal tea to treat inflammation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yarrow tea for inflammation.

  1. Measure out 2-3 teaspoons of dried yarrow flowers and leaves.
  2. Place the herbs in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  3. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the herbs and let it steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the infuser or strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day for inflammation relief.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic white tea for inflammation.

  5. Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spent tea leaves and herbs. Enjoy the herbal white tea warm, up to three times a day for best results.
