What is hyperacidity and how it affects your life?

Hyperacidity is a condition characterized by an excessive production of stomach acid, which can have far-reaching consequences on daily life.

For individuals suffering from hyperacidity, even the simplest activities can become daunting tasks. The constant burning sensation in the chest and throat can make it difficult to enjoy favorite foods, as even mild spices or acidic ingredients like citrus or tomatoes can trigger intense discomfort. This can lead to a restrictive diet, limiting social gatherings and outings that involve food.

Furthermore, hyperacidity can also affect mental performance, making it challenging to focus or concentrate due to the constant discomfort. It's not uncommon for individuals with hyperacidity to experience anxiety, irritability, and mood swings as a result of the chronic pain and distress. Additionally, the acid reflux symptoms associated with hyperacidity can lead to sleep disturbances, impacting overall quality of life and energy levels.

Even simple tasks like reading, watching TV, or engaging in hobbies can become unbearable due to the constant discomfort, making it difficult to maintain a normal routine and daily rhythm.

What are the main causes of hyperacidity?

The main causes of hyperacidity are a combination of dietary and lifestyle factors that disrupt the body's natural pH balance.

A diet rich in processed foods, sugar, and acidic substances such as citrus fruits and tomatoes can contribute to an overload of acid production in the body. Additionally, stress, lack of sleep, and an imbalance of gut bacteria can also lead to hyperacidity. Furthermore, certain medications like NSAIDs and antihistamines can also cause acidity by disrupting the natural pH balance of the stomach.

Moreover, a sedentary lifestyle and lack of physical activity can also contribute to hyperacidity as it can slow down digestion and increase the time that acidic digestive juices remain in the stomach.

The most common causes of hyperacidity are listed below.

  • Poor Diet: Consuming foods that are high in sugar, salt, and unhealthy fats can lead to an imbalance in gut bacteria, causing hyperacidity.
  • Stress: Stress can disrupt digestion and cause a buildup of stomach acid, leading to hyperacidity.
  • Lack of Digestive Enzymes: Inadequate production or secretion of digestive enzymes can prevent proper breakdown of food, leading to an accumulation of undigested protein and fat that can irritate the stomach lining and cause acidity.
  • Food Allergies or Intolerances: Consuming foods to which you are allergic or intolerant can cause inflammation in the gut, leading to hyperacidity.
  • Helicobacter Pylori (H. Pylori) Infection: This type of bacteria can colonize the stomach lining and produce enzymes that break down proteins and peptides, contributing to increased acidity.
  • Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD): A condition in which stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, causing heartburn and hyperacidity.
  • Eating Too Quickly or Not Chewing Food Properly: Poor eating habits can lead to swallowing large amounts of air, which can put pressure on the stomach and cause it to produce more acid.
  • Drinking too Much Caffeine or Carbonated Beverages: Consuming excessive amounts of caffeine or carbonated drinks can relax the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus.
  • Medications such as Nonsteroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs (NSAIDs): Certain medications can irritate the stomach lining and cause inflammation, leading to hyperacidity.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal fluctuations during pregnancy, menopause, or polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) can lead to changes in digestion and increase acidity.

When using herbal remedies to alleviate symptoms of hyperacidity, it's essential to take certain precautions to ensure safe and effective treatment.

Some herbs, such as slippery elm and marshmallow root, can interact with blood thinners, while others like ginger and peppermint can cause digestive upset if consumed in excess. Furthermore, monitor your body's response to the remedies and discontinue use if you experience adverse effects or no improvement in symptoms.

By taking these precautions, you can safely harness the natural benefits of herbal remedies to manage hyperacidity and promote overall digestive well-being.
