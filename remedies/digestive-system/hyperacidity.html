What is hyperacidity and how it affects your life?

Hyperacidity is a condition characterized by an excessive production of stomach acid, which can lead to a number of unpleasant symptoms and complications.

When the stomach produces too much acid, it can cause a burning sensation in the chest and throat, commonly known as heartburn. This discomfort can make it difficult to sleep or lie down, and can even wake a person up in the middle of the night.

In addition to heartburn, hyperacidity can also cause stomach pain, nausea, and vomiting. Over time, these symptoms can lead to more serious complications, such as ulcers, esophagitis, and even cancer of the esophagus or stomach.

Hyperacidity can also affect a person's quality of life by limiting the types of food they can eat and making it difficult to engage in physical activity.

What are the main causes of hyperacidity?

The main causes of hyperacidity are mainly related to lifestyle and dietary habits.

One of the most common causes is the frequent consumption of spicy, fatty, or fried foods, which can increase the production of stomach acid. Additionally, eating large meals, especially close to bedtime, can also contribute to hyperacidity. Other factors that can cause hyperacidity include stress, smoking, alcohol consumption, and the use of certain medications, such as nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) and corticosteroids.

Furthermore, certain medical conditions, such as gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) and Helicobacter pylori infection, can also lead to hyperacidity.

The most common causes of hyperacidity are listed below.

  • Overeating: Consuming large amounts of food at once can lead to hyperacidity as the stomach produces excessive acid to digest the large quantity of food.
  • Spicy and fatty foods: Foods that are spicy or high in fat can irritate the stomach lining and cause the production of excess acid.
  • Irregular eating habits: Skipping meals or eating at irregular times can disrupt the stomach's normal acid-production cycle, leading to hyperacidity.
  • Stress and anxiety: These emotional states can increase the production of stomach acid and lead to hyperacidity.
  • Certain medications: Some medications, such as nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) and corticosteroids, can irritate the stomach lining and cause hyperacidity.
  • Alcohol consumption: Drinking alcohol can increase stomach acid production, leading to hyperacidity.
  • Smoking: Smoking can weaken the muscle that controls the opening between the esophagus and stomach, allowing stomach acid to flow back into the esophagus and causing hyperacidity.
  • Hiatal hernia: A hiatal hernia occurs when a part of the stomach protrudes through the diaphragm, the muscle that separates the chest and abdomen. This can cause hyperacidity by allowing stomach acid to flow back into the esophagus.
  • Pregnancy: Hormonal changes and the pressure of a growing fetus on the stomach can lead to hyperacidity during pregnancy.
  • Obesity: Excess weight can put pressure on the abdomen, causing stomach acid to back up into the esophagus and leading to hyperacidity.
