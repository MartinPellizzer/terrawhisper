Bloating: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Bloating is a common digestive issue characterized by the accumulation of gas in the gastrointestinal tract, leading to discomfort, pain, and swelling in the abdomen.

It is often caused by swallowing air, eating certain foods, irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), hormonal fluctuations, and underlying medical conditions such as celiac disease or gastroparesis. Chronic bloating can lead to fatigue, decreased appetite, and negatively impact overall health and well-being.

To alleviate bloating, herbal remedies such as peppermint, ginger, and fennel can be used to relax the digestive system and reduce gas. Medicinal preparations like teas and capsules can also be effective.

However, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies, especially if you have underlying medical conditions or take prescription medications, as some herbs can interact or exacerbate symptoms.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of bloating, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of bloating?

The main causes of bloating are often attributed to swallowing air, consuming certain foods and beverages, and underlying medical conditions.

Aerophagia, the swallowing of air, can be caused by eating or drinking too quickly, chewing gum, or smoking. A high-FODMAP diet, which includes foods containing fructans, galactans, and polyols, can also lead to bloating in individuals with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). Hormonal fluctuations during menstruation and menopause can cause water retention and bloating due to increased estrogen levels.

Additionally, certain medications such as antidepressants and blood pressure medications can also contribute to bloating as a side effect.

  • Food Intolerance: Food Intolerance occurs when the body is unable to digest certain foods, leading to the accumulation of undigested carbohydrates in the gut, which are then fermented by bacteria, producing gas and causing bloating.
  • Fructose Malabsorption: Fructose Malabsorption is a condition in which the small intestine is unable to properly absorb fructose, a sugar found in many fruits and vegetables, leading to its fermentation by bacteria in the gut and subsequent bloating.
  • SIBO: Small Intestine Bacterial Overgrowth (SIBO) occurs when bacteria overpopulate the small intestine, leading to the fermentation of undigested carbohydrates and the production of gas, which can cause bloating.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes during menstruation, menopause, or pregnancy can lead to changes in digestion and bowel habits, including bloating.
  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome: Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) is a chronic condition characterized by abdominal pain, changes in bowel movements, and bloating, often related to changes in gut motility and sensitivity.
  • Medications: Certain medications, such as antibiotics, antacids, and painkillers, can disrupt gut bacteria, slow down digestion, or relax the muscles in the digestive tract, leading to bloating.
  • Constipation: Constipation can cause bloating as waste material accumulates in the colon and is fermented by bacteria, producing gas.
  • Stress: Chronic stress can slow down digestion, lead to changes in gut motility, and alter the balance of gut bacteria, contributing to bloating.
  • Underlying Medical Conditions: Underlying medical conditions, such as gastroparesis, celiac disease, or inflammatory bowel disease, can cause bloating by disrupting digestion and gut function.
