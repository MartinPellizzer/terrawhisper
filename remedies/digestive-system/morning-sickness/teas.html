Ginger teas helps with morning sickness because it has natural anti-inflammatory properties that help to alleviate nausea and vomiting.

The spicy and warming nature of ginger also stimulates digestion, reducing stomach discomfort and bloating that often accompanies morning sickness.

  • Antioxidant properties: Ginger's antioxidants help to protect against oxidative stress and inflammation in the body, which can contribute to morning sickness and its associated symptoms.
  • Vomiting inhibition: The volatile oils present in ginger have been shown to inhibit vomiting reflexes, helping to alleviate the intense nausea and vomiting often experienced during morning sickness.

The list below shows the primary parts of ginger tea used to make teas for morning sickness.

  • Rhyzomes: The thick, underground stems of ginger have been used for centuries to treat nausea and vomiting due to their natural anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Buds: Ginger buds are known for their high concentration of essential oils, which can help alleviate symptoms of morning sickness by reducing inflammation and soothing the stomach.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tea for morning sickness.

  1. Gather fresh ginger roots zingiber officinale about 2-3 inches long and 0.25-0.5 inch thick for each cup of tea.
  2. Peel the ginger roots using a vegetable peeler or sharp knife removing any blemishes or skin imperfections.
  3. Slice the peeled ginger into thin rounds approximately 1/16 inch thick to ensure even infusion during brewing.
  4. Combine the sliced ginger with boiling water in a large pot or kettle allowing it to steep for 5-7 minutes releasing its active compounds.
  5. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth discarding the solids and serve warm or at room temperature as needed.
