Ginger tinctures helps with morning sickness because of its natural anti-inflammatory properties, which ease digestive discomfort and alleviate nausea.

The bioactive compounds in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, work to calm stomach muscles and reduce inflammation in the gut, helping to stabilize blood sugar levels and soothe an upset stomach.

The list below shows the primary parts of ginger tincture used to make tinctures for morning sickness.

  • Rhyzomes: The rhyzome is the thick, underground stem of the ginger plant, which contains a high concentration of bioactive compounds like gingerols and shogaols that help alleviate nausea and vomiting associated with morning sickness.
  • Roots: Ginger roots have been traditionally used to treat digestive issues, including morning sickness, due to their ability to stimulate digestion and reduce inflammation in the stomach.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tincture for morning sickness.

  1. Gather organic fresh or dried ginger root of 100-150 grams per batch and clean glass jars.
  2. Chop the ginger root into small pieces and combine with high-proof vodka or everclear in a 1:5 ratio.

  4. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass container discarding the solids.
  5. Transfer the tincture to smaller dark glass bottles and store in the refrigerator for up to one year.
