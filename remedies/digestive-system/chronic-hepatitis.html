What is chronic hepatitis and how it affects your life?

Chronic hepatitis is a persistent liver infection that can significantly impact an individual's quality of life.

The condition can cause persistent fatigue, leading to difficulties in performing daily tasks and maintaining a job or social activities. Chronic hepatitis can also lead to abdominal pain, nausea, and vomiting, making it challenging to eat a balanced diet or engage in hobbies that require physical activity.

Furthermore, the infection can cause itching and jaundice, resulting in feelings of self-consciousness and embarrassment. Additionally, chronic hepatitis can increase the risk of liver damage and cirrhosis, which can lead to complications such as easy bruising, bleeding easily, and fatigue. Moreover, the condition can also affect mental health, causing anxiety, depression, and irritability.

As a result, individuals with chronic hepatitis may experience social isolation, reduced productivity, and decreased overall well-being, ultimately affecting their relationships, career prospects, and overall quality of life.

What are the main causes of chronic hepatitis?

The main causes of chronic hepatitis are viral infections, autoimmune disorders, and metabolic diseases.

Hepatitis B and C viruses are responsible for most cases of chronic hepatitis worldwide, with an estimated 350-400 million people infected globally. These viruses can remain in the body for years or even decades, leading to progressive liver damage and increased risk of liver cancer.

Autoimmune hepatitis occurs when the immune system mistakenly attacks healthy liver cells, causing inflammation and damage. Metabolic diseases such as non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) and non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH), which are often associated with obesity and type 2 diabetes, can also lead to chronic hepatitis.

In addition, certain medications and environmental toxins can also contribute to the development of chronic hepatitis.

The most common causes of chronic hepatitis are listed below.

  • Hepatitis B Virus (HBV): A viral infection that can lead to chronic hepatitis, cirrhosis, and liver cancer if left untreated or treated inadequately.
  • Hepatitis C Virus (HCV): The most common cause of chronic hepatitis worldwide, HCV is a viral infection that can cause liver damage, cirrhosis, and liver cancer.
  • Non-Alcoholic Steatohepatitis (NASH): A type of fatty liver disease caused by excess fat accumulation in the liver, which can lead to inflammation and scarring over time.
  • Autoimmune Hepatitis: A condition where the immune system mistakenly attacks the liver, leading to chronic inflammation and damage.
  • Primary Biliary Cholangitis (PBC): An autoimmune disorder that damages the bile ducts inside the liver, causing inflammation and scarring.
  • Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis (PSC): A rare autoimmune disorder that causes inflammation and scarring of the bile ducts, leading to chronic hepatitis and liver damage.
  • Hepatitis D Virus (HDV): A viral infection that can cause acute and chronic hepatitis in individuals already infected with HBV.
  • Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency: An inherited disorder that affects the production of alpha-1 antitrypsin, a protein important for liver function, leading to chronic hepatitis and scarring.
  • Wilson's Disease: A rare genetic disorder that causes excess copper accumulation in the liver, leading to chronic hepatitis, cirrhosis, and brain damage if left untreated.
  • Schistosomiasis: A parasitic infection caused by schistosomes, a type of flatworm, which can cause chronic hepatitis and liver scarring if left untreated.

What precautions to take when using herbal remedies for chronic hepatitis?

When considering the use of herbal remedies for chronic hepatitis, it is essential to exercise caution and consult with a healthcare provider first.

While some herbs may have potential benefits in alleviating symptoms or slowing disease progression, they can also interact with prescribed medications or worsen liver function if not used correctly. Key precautions include selecting high-quality products from reputable sources, starting with low doses and gradually increasing as needed, monitoring for adverse effects such as allergic reactions or gastrointestinal upset, and avoiding self-treatment with herbs that have not been scientifically proven to be safe and effective in treating chronic hepatitis.

Additionally, individuals taking prescription medications should consult their healthcare provider before combining them with herbal remedies to ensure safe and effective treatment.

The most important precautions to take when using herbal remedies for chronic hepatitis are listed below.

  • Consult carefully with your healthcare provider before using any herbal remedy to ensure it won’t interact with medications or worsen symptoms

  • Be aware of potential allergic reactions to new herbs, especially if you have a history of allergies
  • Monitor your liver function tests regularly while using herbal remedies to ensure they are not causing damage
  • Start with small doses and gradually increase as needed and under medical supervision to avoid side effects
  • Choose reputable sources for purchasing herbal supplements, such as licensed healthcare practitioners or well-established manufacturers
  • Be cautious when combining multiple herbal remedies or mixing them with other medications without your healthcare provider’s guidance
  • Document all herbal remedies you’re using, including dosages and duration of use, to track any potential interactions or side effects
  • Avoid self-diagnosing or treating chronic hepatitis with herbal remedies alone, as a medical diagnosis and treatment plan should be established first
  • Discontinue use if you experience any adverse reactions, such as worsened symptoms or abnormal liver function tests
