What is chronic hepatitis and how it affects your life?

Chronic hepatitis is a long-term inflammation of the liver that can lead to serious health problems if left untreated.

It is a condition where the liver becomes damaged over time, often due to a viral infection, but the causes can vary. The effects of chronic hepatitis on a person's life can be significant and negatively impact their overall well-being.

For instance, individuals with chronic hepatitis may experience chronic fatigue, loss of appetite, and unexplained weight loss, making it difficult for them to maintain a normal routine or perform daily activities. Additionally, the condition can lead to complications such as liver scarring (cirrhosis), liver failure, and liver cancer, which can require hospitalization, surgery, or even a liver transplant.

Furthermore, chronic hepatitis can also affect a person's mental health, causing anxiety, depression, and decreased quality of life.
