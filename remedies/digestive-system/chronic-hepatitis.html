Chronic Hepatitis: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Chronic hepatitis is a long-term inflammation of the liver, characterized by a persistent infection with hepatitis viruses, such as hepatitis B and C, or non-viral causes like autoimmune hepatitis or non-alcoholic fatty liver disease.

This condition can lead to liver fibrosis, cirrhosis, and even liver failure. The causes of chronic hepatitis include viral infections, autoimmune disorders, and lifestyle factors like excessive alcohol consumption and obesity.

If left untreated, chronic hepatitis can result in liver damage, fatigue, and an increased risk of liver cancer. Herbal remedies like milk thistle, turmeric, and licorice root have anti-inflammatory properties that may help alleviate symptoms, while preparations like silymarin and curcumin have shown promise in reducing liver inflammation.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as they can interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of chronic hepatitis, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of chronic hepatitis?

The main causes of chronic hepatitis are primarily viral infections, with hepatitis B (HBV) and hepatitis C (HCV) being the most common.

HBV is transmitted through bodily fluids, such as blood and semen, while HCV is primarily spread through blood-to-blood contact. Other causes include autoimmune hepatitis, where the body's immune system attacks liver cells, and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), which is linked to obesity and insulin resistance. Alcoholic liver disease and certain medications, like anabolic steroids and certain antibiotics, can also lead to chronic hepatitis.

Additionally, rare genetic disorders, such as alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency, can cause chronic hepatitis, highlighting the multifactorial nature of this condition.

  • HCV: Hepatitis C virus infection causes chronic inflammation and scarring of the liver due to the persistence of the virus in the liver cells.
  • HBV: Hepatitis B virus infection leads to chronic hepatitis due to the virus's ability to integrate into the host's genome, resulting in a persistent infection.
  • NASH: Non-alcoholic steatohepatitis, a form of non-alcoholic fatty liver disease, causes chronic inflammation and scarring of the liver due to the accumulation of fat in liver cells.
  • Autoimmune: Autoimmune hepatitis is a chronic inflammatory disease of the liver caused by the immune system's attack on liver cells, leading to liver damage and scarring.
  • Alcohol: Chronic alcohol consumption leads to liver damage and scarring due to the toxic effects of alcohol on liver cells and the formation of scar tissue.
  • HA: Hemochromatosis, a genetic disorder, causes chronic hepatitis due to the accumulation of iron in the liver, leading to liver damage and scarring.
  • Alpha-1: Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency, a genetic disorder, causes chronic hepatitis due to the accumulation of abnormal alpha-1 antitrypsin protein in the liver, leading to liver damage and scarring.
  • Wilson's: Wilson's disease, a genetic disorder, causes chronic hepatitis due to the accumulation of copper in the liver, leading to liver damage and scarring.
  • Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis: Primary sclerosing cholangitis, a chronic inflammatory disease, causes chronic hepatitis due to the inflammation and scarring of bile ducts in the liver, leading to liver damage and scarring.
