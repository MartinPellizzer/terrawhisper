1. Silybum marianum teas

Silybum marianum teas, also known as milk thistle teas, have been traditionally used to treat jaundice due to its hepatoprotective properties.

The sesquiterpene lactones, particularly silymarin, in these teas help to treat jaundice by supporting liver function, reducing oxidative stress, and promoting the regeneration of liver cells.

The flavonoids and polyphenols present in Silybum marianum teas also aid in the treatment of jaundice by enhancing bile flow and reducing inflammation in the liver.

The benefits of using Silybum marianum teas to treat jaundice include reduced liver damage, improved liver function, and enhanced overall health.

Related Study

According to "Frontiers in pharmacology", Silybum marianum teas for jaundice have been found to contain silybin, a biologically active ingredient that leads to bilirubin reduction and normalization of relevant serum enzymes.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Silybum marianum flowers.
  2. Measure 8 ounces of boiling water and pour it over the flowers.
  3. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to mix.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  5. Drink 1/4 cup of the tea, 2-3 times a day, as needed.

Usage:

  1. Consult a doctor before using Silybum marianum tea for jaundice treatment.
  2. Drink 1 cup of Silybum marianum tea, 3 times a day, for 7-10 days.
  3. Start with 1 teaspoon of dried Silybum marianum per cup of boiling water.
  4. Increase the dosage to 2 teaspoons per cup after 3-5 days, if needed.
  5. Monitor liver function and bilirubin levels to adjust the treatment plan.

Silybum marianum teas can be used to treat jaundice, but its use may cause side effects such as diarrhea, stomach upset, and allergic reactions in some individuals.

Precautions should be taken when using Silybum marianum teas, including avoiding its use in pregnant or breastfeeding women, and individuals with kidney or liver problems, as it may exacerbate these conditions.
