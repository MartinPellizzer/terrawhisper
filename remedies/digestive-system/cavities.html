What is cavities and how it affects your life?

Cavities, also known as tooth decay, are small holes or pits that form in teeth due to the breakdown of enamel, the hard outer layer of the tooth.

This can lead to a range of negative effects on one's life, both physically and mentally. For instance, a cavity can cause pain and sensitivity in the tooth, making it difficult to eat or drink certain foods and beverages. This can lead to changes in diet, such as avoiding hot or cold foods, or even requiring a diet of soft foods. Additionally, cavities can lead to more complex and costly dental procedures, such as fillings, crowns, or even root canals.

Furthermore, if left untreated, cavities can lead to abscesses, which are painful and potentially life-threatening infections. This can lead to time off work or school, and even affect one's overall quality of life. Moreover, the pain and discomfort caused by cavities can also lead to anxiety and stress, which can further exacerbate the problem. In extreme cases, cavities can even lead to the loss of teeth, affecting one's appearance and self-confidence.

Overall, cavities can have a significant impact on one's daily life, causing physical pain, emotional distress, and financial burden.
