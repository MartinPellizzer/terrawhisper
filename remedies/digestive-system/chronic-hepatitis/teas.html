1. Astragalus membranaceus teas

Astragalus membranaceus teas, also known as Huang Qi, have been traditionally used to treat chronic hepatitis due to their immunomodulatory, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

The bioactive constituents of Astragalus membranaceus, such as polysaccharides, flavonoids, and saponins, help to enhance liver function, reduce oxidative stress, and modulate immune responses to alleviate hepatitis symptoms.

By promoting liver regeneration, reducing inflammation, and improving immune function, Astragalus membranaceus teas can help to alleviate symptoms and improve quality of life for individuals with chronic hepatitis.

The benefits of using Astragalus membranaceus teas to treat chronic hepatitis include improved liver function, reduced inflammation, and enhanced immune function, making it a promising complementary therapy for this condition.

Recipe:

  1. Gather Astragalus membranaceus roots (about 10 grams) and a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot and let it cool for 1 minute.
  3. Add 2-3 teaspoons of Astragalus membranaceus roots to the tea infuser or directly to the cup.
  4. Pour the cooled water over the roots and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day, ideally on an empty stomach.

Usage:

  1. Consult a doctor before using Astragalus membranaceus tea for chronic hepatitis.
  2. Drink 1 cup of Astragalus membranaceus tea 3 times a day.
  3. Start with 1 teaspoon of dried Astragalus per cup of boiling water.
  4. Gradually increase to 2-3 teaspoons per cup as your body adapts.
  5. Use Astragalus membranaceus tea for 3-6 months, and monitor your liver function.

Astragalus membranaceus teas can be used to treat chronic hepatitis, but may cause side effects such as gastrointestinal upset, including diarrhea and nausea, as well as allergic reactions like skin rash and itching.

Precautions should be taken, including starting with low doses and gradually increasing as needed, and avoiding Astragalus membranaceus teas in people with bleeding disorders or taking blood-thinning medications, as they may exacerbate these conditions.

The benefits of using Panax ginseng teas to treat chronic hepatitis include improved liver function, reduced inflammation, and enhanced overall well-being.

Related Study

According to "The Journal of international medical research", Panax ginseng teas for chronic hepatitis may improve the detoxifying activity of the liver in elderly patients with toxin-induced chronic liver disease.

Recipe:

  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried Panax ginseng roots and place it in the tea infuser or strainer.
  3. Steep the ginseng roots in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool for 2 minutes.

  5. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day for chronic hepatitis.

  1. Consult a doctor before using Panax ginseng tea for chronic hepatitis treatment.
