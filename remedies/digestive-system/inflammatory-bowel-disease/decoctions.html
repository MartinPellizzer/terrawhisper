1. Curcuma longa

Turmeric decoctions helps with inflammatory bowel disease because of its potent anti-inflammatory compound, curcumin.

This natural substance reduces inflammation in the digestive tract by inhibiting pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, thereby alleviating symptoms such as abdominal pain, diarrhea, and bloating associated with conditions like ulcerative colitis and Crohn's disease.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in turmeric decoctions that aid with inflammatory bowel disease.

  • Curcuminoids: Curcuminoids, specifically curcumin, have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the gut, alleviating symptoms of IBD such as diarrhea, abdominal pain, and fatigue.
  • Cyclooxygenase (cox) inhibition: Turmeric decoctions contain compounds that inhibit COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes, thereby reducing the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins and thromboxanes, which contribute to the inflammation and damage in IBD.

The list below shows the primary parts of turmeric decoction used to make decoctions for inflammatory bowel disease.

  • Rhyzomes: Rich in curcuminoids, which have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making them effective against inflammatory bowel disease symptoms.
  • Roots: Containing a rich concentration of volatile oils, flavonoids, and other bioactive compounds that help alleviate digestive issues and reduce inflammation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic turmeric decoction for inflammatory bowel disease.

  1. Weigh 10 grams of dried turmeric root and place it in a clean glass jar.
  2. Boil one liter of water in a saucepan for 5-7 minutes to prepare the base solution.
  3. Add the weighed turmeric root to the boiling water and let it steep for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a separate container.
  5. Pour the final decoction into glass bottles and store them in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
