The list below shows the primary parts of aloe vera tincture used to make tinctures for inflammatory bowel disease.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the aloe vera plant contain a gel-like substance that has anti-inflammatory properties, making it useful for treating inflammatory bowel disease.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic aloe vera tincture for inflammatory bowel disease.

  1. Chop 2 cups of fresh aloe vera leaves and blend with 2 cups of 80-proof vodka in a blender for 2 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth into a sterilized glass jar, pressing the solids to extract all liquid.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of dried anti-inflammatory herbs (such as chamomile, calendula, or turmeric) per 1 cup of aloe vera-alcohol mixture.
  4. Seal the jar and store in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking daily.
  5. Strain the tincture again through cheesecloth, discard the solids, and store the final tincture in a sterilized glass bottle with a tight-fitting lid.
