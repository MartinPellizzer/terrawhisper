What is jaundice and how it affects your life?

Jaundice is a condition characterized by a yellowing of the skin and eyes due to an excess of bilirubin in the blood.

This debilitating condition can have a profound impact on one's quality of life, causing numerous physical and emotional challenges. For instance, individuals with jaundice may experience fatigue, weakness, and lethargy, making it difficult to perform even the simplest daily tasks.

Additionally, their skin may take on a dull, yellowish hue, affecting their self-esteem and confidence. Furthermore, jaundice can cause digestive issues such as dark urine, pale or clay-colored stools, and abdominal pain, leading to frequent hospital visits and medical interventions. Moreover, it can also affect one's mental health, causing feelings of anxiety, depression, and frustration.

Simple activities like socializing, working, and enjoying hobbies become exhausting due to the constant fatigue, further isolating individuals with jaundice from their loved ones and communities.

What are the main causes of jaundice?

The main causes of jaundice are usually related to an imbalance in the production or processing of bile, a greenish-yellow fluid produced by the liver that helps digest fats and absorbs fat-soluble vitamins.

In most cases, jaundice is caused by underlying medical conditions such as hepatitis (inflammation of the liver), cirrhosis (scarring of the liver), or bile duct obstruction (blockage of the tubes that carry bile from the liver to the small intestine). Other potential causes of jaundice include pancreatic disorders, such as pancreatitis or cancer; medication side effects; blood disorders like hemolytic anemia; and genetic conditions like Gilbert's syndrome.

In rare cases, jaundice can also be caused by inherited metabolic disorders, infections, or certain cancers that have spread to the liver.

The most common causes of jaundice are listed below.

  • Gilbert's Syndrome: A genetic disorder that affects the metabolism of bilirubin, leading to increased levels in the blood and yellowing of the skin and eyes.
  • Hepatitis B or C Infection: Viral infections that inflame the liver and cause scarring, leading to elevated bilirubin levels and jaundice.
  • Obstruction of the Bile Ducts: Blockages or narrowing of the bile ducts can prevent bile from flowing properly, causing bilirubin levels to rise and resulting in jaundice.
  • Pancreatitis: Inflammation of the pancreas that can cause bile duct obstruction and increased bilirubin levels, leading to jaundice.
  • Liver Cirrhosis: Scar tissue formation in the liver due to chronic alcohol abuse or other causes, which can lead to impaired liver function and jaundice.
  • Erythropoietic Protoporphyria (EPP): A rare genetic disorder that affects the production of heme, leading to increased bilirubin levels and jaundice.
  • Hemolytic Anemia: Destruction of red blood cells, which can lead to an increase in bilirubin production and jaundice.
  • Cancer: Certain types of cancer, such as pancreatic or liver cancer, can cause jaundice due to the obstruction of bile ducts or invasion of surrounding tissues.
  • Intrahepatic Cholestasis of Pregnancy (ICP): A condition that occurs during pregnancy when the liver is unable to eliminate bile salts properly, leading to increased bilirubin levels and jaundice.
  • Drugs and Chemicals: Certain medications, such as statins or antibiotics, can cause liver damage and increase bilirubin levels, leading to jaundice.

What precautions to take when using herbal remedies for jaundice?

When considering the use of herbal remedies for jaundice, it is crucial to exercise caution and consult with a healthcare professional first.

Some herbs may interact with prescription medications or worsen the underlying condition. For instance, St. John's Wort, a popular herb, can reduce the efficacy of certain medications used to treat jaundice.

Additionally, some herbs like turmeric and ginger may increase liver function and potentially exacerbate existing liver damage. Furthermore, herbal remedies may not be standardized in terms of dosage or quality, making it difficult to ensure effective treatment.

As such, it is essential to work with a healthcare professional who can monitor your condition and advise on the appropriate use of herbal remedies to avoid any potential harm or complications.
