Jaundice: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Jaundice is a medical condition characterized by a yellowing of the skin and eyes due to an excessive buildup of bilirubin in the body, often caused by liver or bile duct disorders, infections, or certain medications.

It can lead to fatigue, dark urine, and loss of appetite, ultimately affecting the liver's ability to detoxify and filter blood. To alleviate jaundice, herbal remedies such as milk thistle, turmeric, and dandelion root can be used to support liver health and stimulate bile production.

However, it's crucial to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, especially if you're pregnant or breastfeeding, to avoid potential interactions or adverse effects.

What are the main causes of jaundice?

The main causes of jaundice are multifactorial, involving liver dysfunction, bile duct obstruction, and hemolytic diseases.

Liver dysfunction is often caused by viral hepatitis (e.g., hepatitis A, B, C, D, and E), autoimmune hepatitis, and alcoholic liver disease. Bile duct obstruction, also known as obstructive jaundice, is typically caused by gallstones, pancreatic cancer, and bile duct cancer. Hemolytic diseases, such as sickle cell anemia, glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase deficiency, and malaria, can also lead to jaundice due to excessive red blood cell breakdown.

Additionally, genetic disorders like Dubin-Johnson syndrome and Rotor syndrome can cause chronic jaundice due to impaired bile excretion.

  • Biliary Atresia: Biliary atresia is a rare but serious condition in newborns where the bile ducts are absent or blocked, causing a buildup of bilirubin in the blood and leading to jaundice.
  • Gilbert Syndrome: Gilbert syndrome is a genetic disorder that affects the liver's ability to process bilirubin, leading to a mild but chronic buildup of bilirubin in the blood and causing jaundice.
  • Hemolytic Disease: Hemolytic disease is a condition where the mother's immune system produces antibodies against the baby's red blood cells, causing the premature destruction of red blood cells and leading to a buildup of bilirubin in the blood and jaundice.
  • Pancreatitis: Pancreatitis is inflammation of the pancreas that can cause the bile ducts to become blocked, leading to a buildup of bilirubin in the blood and jaundice.
  • Medullary Sponge Kidney: Medullary sponge kidney is a congenital disorder where cysts form in the kidneys, leading to the buildup of bilirubin in the blood and jaundice.
  • Dubin-Johnson Syndrome: Dubin-Johnson syndrome is a rare genetic disorder where the liver is unable to remove bilirubin from the blood, leading to a buildup of bilirubin and jaundice.
  • Rotor Syndrome: Rotor syndrome is a rare genetic disorder similar to Dubin-Johnson syndrome, where the liver is unable to remove bilirubin from the blood, leading to a buildup of bilirubin and jaundice.
  • Cholestasis: Cholestasis is a condition where the flow of bile is reduced or blocked, causing a buildup of bilirubin in the blood and jaundice.
  • Hepatitis: Hepatitis is inflammation of the liver caused by viral or bacterial infections, which can cause the liver to become damaged and unable to remove bilirubin from the blood, leading to jaundice.
  • Liver Tumors: Liver tumors, such as hepatocellular carcinoma, can cause the liver to become damaged and unable to remove bilirubin from the blood, leading to jaundice.
