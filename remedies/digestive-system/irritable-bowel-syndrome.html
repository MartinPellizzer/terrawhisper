The exact cause of IBS is unknown, but it is thought to be related to abnormalities in the nervous system, inflammation, severe infection, or changes in gut bacteria.

What is irritable bowel syndrome and how it affects your life?

Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) is a common disorder that affects the large intestine, leading to a variety of uncomfortable and sometimes painful symptoms.

These symptoms can include abdominal cramping, bloating, gas, diarrhea, and constipation. The severity of these symptoms can vary greatly from person to person, with some individuals experiencing mild discomfort and others suffering from debilitating pain.

IBS can have a significant negative impact on a person's quality of life, making it difficult for them to perform daily activities, enjoy meals, or even leave the house due to fear of an unexpected flare-up. It can also lead to anxiety and depression as individuals struggle to manage their symptoms and cope with the uncertainty of when their next episode will occur.

Additionally, IBS can cause individuals to miss work or school, leading to financial strain and difficulties in personal and professional relationships.
