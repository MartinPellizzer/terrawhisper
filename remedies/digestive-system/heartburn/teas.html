9 Best Herbal Teas For Heartburn

Herbal teas for Heartburn are natural remedies that involve infusing the leaves, roots, or flowers of various herbs in hot water to create a soothing and calming drink, aimed at alleviating the symptoms of heartburn.

This ailment occurs when stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, causing a burning sensation in the chest.

Herbal teas can effectively treat heartburn due to their anti-inflammatory and calming properties, which help to reduce inflammation and relax the muscles in the esophagus, thereby alleviating the discomfort.

Examples of herbal teas that can help treat heartburn include chamomile, which soothes the digestive system, peppermint, which relaxes the muscles and reduces inflammation, licorice root, which coats the esophagus and protects it from acid, ginger, which reduces inflammation and nausea, and slippery elm, which forms a protective barrier in the esophagus to prevent acid from flowing back up.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra teas

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, derived from the roots of the licorice plant, have been traditionally used to treat heartburn ailments due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

The herbal preparation helps to treat heartburn by reducing inflammation in the esophagus and stomach, thereby alleviating symptoms such as acid reflux and burning sensations.

The bioactive constituents of Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, including glycyrrhizin and flavonoids, play a crucial role in reducing inflammation and protecting the mucous membranes from acid damage.

The benefits of using Glycyrrhiza glabra teas to treat heartburn include natural relief from symptoms, improved digestion, and reduced risk of complications associated with long-term heartburn.

Related Study

According to the study on "Microbial pathogenesis", Glycyrrhiza glabra teas for heartburn may help alleviate symptoms due to the antimicrobial properties demonstrated by extracts from Glycyrrhiza glabra against Helicobacter pylori, which can contribute to heartburn.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Glycyrrhiza glabra roots or 2 teaspoons of dried roots.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water and let it cool for 5 minutes.
  3. Add 1-2 teaspoons of the Glycyrrhiza glabra roots to the cooled water.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes. Strain the liquid and discard the solids.
  5. Drink 1/2 cup of the tea 30 minutes before meals to help prevent heartburn.

Usage:

  1. Drink 1 cup of Glycyrrhiza glabra tea 1-2 times a day
  2. Start with 1 teaspoon of dried root per cup of boiling water
  3. Steep for 5-7 minutes and strain the tea
  4. Take 1/2 to 1 teaspoon of dried root 3 times a day as needed
  5. Use for up to 2 weeks, then take a 2-week break

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can be an effective remedy for heartburn, but its prolonged use may cause side effects such as headaches, dizziness, and changes in blood pressure.

To use Glycyrrhiza glabra teas safely, it's essential to start with low doses and gradually increase as needed, and to avoid consuming it for extended periods to minimize the risk of potassium depletion and other complications.

Recipe:

Silybum marianum teas can be effective in treating heartburn, but possible side effects may include gastrointestinal upset, such as nausea, diarrhea, and abdominal pain, as well as allergic reactions like skin rashes and itching.
