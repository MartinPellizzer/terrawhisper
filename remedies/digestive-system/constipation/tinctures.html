1. Psyllium

Psyllium tincture helps with constipation because it contains soluble fiber that absorbs water and expands, forming a gel-like substance.

This substance softens and adds bulk to the stool, making it easier to pass through the intestines. As a result, psyllium tincture can promote regular bowel movements and alleviate constipation. Additionally, psyllium can help regulate the speed of digestion, preventing the stomach from emptying too quickly or too slowly, which can both contribute to constipation.

Overall, psyllium tincture's ability to absorb water and increase stool bulk makes it an effective natural remedy for constipation.

  • High fiber content: Slippery elm bark is rich in fiber, which adds bulk to stools, stimulates muscle contractions in the intestines, and promotes bowel movements.
  • Anti-inflammatory effects: Slippery elm has anti-inflammatory properties due to the presence of tannins and other compounds. These help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, which can alleviate constipation and promote regular bowel movements.
  • Prebiotic properties: Slippery elm bark contains prebiotics, which support the growth of beneficial gut bacteria. These bacteria produce short-chain fatty acids that promote gut motility and can help alleviate constipation.
  • Demulcent properties: Demulcents, like slippery elm, form a protective film along the digestive tract, reducing irritation and inflammation. This soothing effect helps regulate bowel movements and may help with constipation.
  • Nutrient absorption: Slippery elm bark has been shown to improve nutrient absorption, facilitating the uptake of necessary vitamins and minerals. By enhancing overall gut health, it can indirectly help combat constipation by promoting a balanced digestive system.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in psyllium that help with constipation.

  • Husks: The outer coating of psyllium seeds, called husks, are also rich in soluble fiber, providing bulk to stools, making them easier to pass.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic psyllium for constipation.

  1. Chop 1 cup of psyllium husks into small pieces and combine with 2 cups of boiling water in a pot.
  2. Reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool down to room temperature.
  4. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth-lined sieve into a clean jar, squeezing out excess liquid.
  5. Store the psyllium tincture in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks, taking 1 tablespoon daily as needed for constipation.
