What is cholecystitis and how it affects your life?

Cholecystitis is a medical condition characterized by inflammation of the gallbladder, a small organ located beneath the liver that stores and concentrates bile, a digestive fluid produced by the liver.

When the gallbladder becomes inflamed, it can cause a range of uncomfortable and potentially serious symptoms that can significantly impact a person's quality of life. For example, cholecystitis can cause severe abdominal pain, often located in the upper right part of the abdomen, that may worsen after eating or lying down.

It can also lead to nausea, vomiting, fever, and chills, making it difficult to perform daily activities or maintain a normal routine. In addition, cholecystitis can cause jaundice, a yellowing of the skin and eyes, as well as dark urine and pale stools, all of which can be embarrassing and uncomfortable. In severe cases, cholecystitis can lead to complications such as gallstones, infection, or even tissue death, requiring hospitalization and surgery.

Overall, cholecystitis can have a significant negative impact on a person's physical, emotional, and social well-being, affecting their ability to work, attend school, or engage in other activities they enjoy.

What are the main causes of cholecystitis?

The main causes of cholecystitis are typically related to gallstones or abnormalities in the bile ducts.

Gallstones, which are small, hard deposits that form in the gallbladder, can obstruct the cystic duct and cause inflammation in the gallbladder. This is the most common cause of cholecystitis.

Another possible cause is bile duct obstruction, which can be due to tumors, scar tissue, or gallstones. When the bile ducts are obstructed, bile builds up in the gallbladder, leading to inflammation and infection. In some cases, cholecystitis can also be caused by injuries or infections in the abdomen.

However, in a small percentage of cases, the cause of cholecystitis remains unknown.

The most common causes of cholecystitis are listed below.

  • Gallstones: The most common cause of cholecystitis is the formation of gallstones, which can block the cystic duct and cause inflammation.
  • Bacterial infection: In some cases, cholecystitis can be caused by a bacterial infection, often as a complication of gallstones.
  • Tumors: Tumors in the gallbladder or nearby organs can cause cholecystitis by blocking the bile ducts.
  • Trauma: Physical injury to the abdomen can cause cholecystitis by damaging the gallbladder.
  • Blood vessel problems: Conditions that affect blood flow to the gallbladder, such as atherosclerosis or sickle cell disease, can cause cholecystitis.
  • Immune system disorders: Certain immune system disorders, such as sarcoidosis or vasculitis, can cause cholecystitis.
  • Medications: Certain medications, such as ceftriaxone and octreotide, can increase the risk of cholecystitis.
  • Rapid weight loss: Rapid weight loss, such as that caused by bariatric surgery or crash dieting, can increase the risk of cholecystitis.
  • Pregnancy: Hormonal changes during pregnancy can increase the risk of gallstone formation and cholecystitis.
  • Age: The risk of cholecystitis increases with age, particularly after age 40.
