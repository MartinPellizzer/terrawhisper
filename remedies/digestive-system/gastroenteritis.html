What is gastroenteritis and how it affects your life?

Gastroenteritis, commonly referred to as stomach flu or food poisoning, is a highly contagious and uncomfortable gastrointestinal illness that can wreak havoc on one's daily life.

When afflicted with this condition, sufferers experience frequent and intense episodes of diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal cramps, and fever, leading to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances. This can result in a multitude of negative effects, including the inability to work or attend school due to persistent nausea and fatigue, missed social events and gatherings, and compromised overall well-being.

Moreover, gastroenteritis can also lead to financial losses due to medical expenses, lost productivity, and reduced earning potential. Furthermore, it can disrupt one's daily routine, making simple tasks like cooking, cleaning, and personal hygiene a daunting challenge. Even mundane activities like watching TV or reading a book become a struggle as the constant urge to use the bathroom takes over, leaving individuals feeling exhausted, frustrated, and utterly miserable.

As if all these physical and emotional tolls weren't enough, gastroenteritis can also damage relationships with loved ones, friends, and colleagues, causing feelings of isolation and guilt.

What are the main causes of gastroenteritis?

The main causes of gastroenteritis are a combination of bacterial, viral, and parasitic infections that affect the stomach and intestines.

Viral gastroenteritis, also known as the stomach flu, is caused by noroviruses, rotaviruses, and adenoviruses, which are highly contagious and can spread through contaminated food, water, and close contact with an infected person. Bacterial gastroenteritis, on the other hand, is typically caused by enterotoxigenic Escherichia coli (ETEC), Salmonella, Campylobacter, and Shigella, which can be transmitted through undercooked meat, unpasteurized dairy products, contaminated water, or poor hygiene.

Additionally, parasitic infections such as Giardia and Cryptosporidium can also cause gastroenteritis, often through contaminated food, water, or close contact with an infected person.

The most common causes of gastroenteritis are listed below.

  • Norovirus: Norovirus is a highly contagious virus that spreads through close contact with an infected person, contaminated food or water, and poor hygiene practices. It's often referred to as the "stomach flu" due to its symptoms of vomiting and diarrhea.
  • Rotavirus: Rotavirus is another highly contagious virus that primarily affects children under the age of 5. It spreads through close contact with an infected person, contaminated surfaces, or through contaminated food and water.
  • Salmonella: Salmonella is a type of bacteria found in contaminated food, particularly poultry, beef, eggs, and produce. It can also spread through poor hygiene practices and contact with infected animals.
  • Escherichia coli (E. coli): E. coli is a type of bacteria that can cause gastroenteritis through contaminated food or water, or through close contact with an infected person.
  • Campylobacter: Campylobacter is a type of bacteria found in contaminated poultry, pork, and produce. It's often spread through undercooked meat or unpasteurized milk.
  • Shigella: Shigella is a type of bacteria that primarily affects children and young adults. It spreads through close contact with an infected person, contaminated surfaces, or through contaminated food and water.
  • Giardia: Giardia is a type of parasite found in contaminated water, particularly in lakes, rivers, and streams. It can also spread through poor hygiene practices and contact with infected animals.
  • Cryptosporidium: Cryptosporidium is another type of parasite found in contaminated water and food. It's often spread through swimming in contaminated pools or lakes, consuming contaminated water, or through contact with infected animals.
  • Vibrio: Vibrio is a type of bacteria found in contaminated seafood, particularly oysters and mussels. It can also spread through poor hygiene practices and contact with infected animals.
  • Clostridioides difficile (C. diff): C. diff is a type of bacteria that spreads through contaminated surfaces, food, or water, and can cause diarrhea and abdominal pain.
