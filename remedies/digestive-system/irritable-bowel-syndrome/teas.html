1. Peppermint tea

Peppermint tea helps with irritable bowel syndrome because it contains menthol, a compound that relaxes the gastrointestinal muscles, reducing spasms and pain.

  • Relaxant: It helps relax gastrointestinal (GI) tract smooth muscle, which can alleviate IBS symptoms, due to the presence of menthol and flavonoids.
  • Antimicrobial: Peppermint tea has antimicrobial properties that can help maintain a healthy gut microbiome, which is crucial for managing IBS, thanks to menthol and other volatile oils.

The list below shows the primary parts of peppermint tea used to make teas for irritable bowel syndrome.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the peppermint plant are commonly used in medicinal tea for irritable bowel syndrome due to their antispasmodic properties, which can help relieve intestinal cramping and spasms.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the peppermint plant are sometimes included in medicinal tea blends for irritable bowel syndrome, as they contain volatile oils that may help relax the gastrointestinal tract and alleviate symptoms of bloating and gas.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for irritable bowel syndrome.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves with 8 ounces of boiling water.
  2. Allow the mixture to steep for 5-10 minutes to extract the herb's beneficial compounds.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, ensuring a smooth and enjoyable drink.
  4. Optionally, add a small amount of honey or lemon to taste, but avoid milk or sugar.
  5. Drink the tea while warm, 2-3 times a day, especially after meals, for optimal irritable bowel syndrome relief.
