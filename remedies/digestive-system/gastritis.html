What is gastritis and how it affects your life?

Gastritis is a condition that causes inflammation and irritation of the lining of the stomach, leading to a range of uncomfortable and debilitating symptoms.

Those affected by gastritis often experience persistent burning sensations in the chest and abdominal area, which can be exacerbated by certain foods, stress, or other triggers. As a result, daily activities become a chore, as even simple tasks like eating or sleeping are met with excruciating discomfort.

Social gatherings and leisure activities become a burden, as gastritis sufferers may need to constantly excuse themselves to alleviate their symptoms. Work performance suffers as fatigue and abdominal pain take its toll, leading to decreased productivity and increased absenteeism. Even everyday routines, such as getting dressed or taking a shower, can become daunting tasks due to the constant discomfort and pain.

In extreme cases, gastritis can lead to malnutrition, weight loss, and mood disturbances, further impacting one's overall quality of life and ability to participate in daily activities with ease.

What are the main causes of gastritis?

The main causes of gastritis are multifactorial and can be triggered by a combination of factors.

The most common causes include Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori) infection, which is responsible for about 70% of cases worldwide. Other bacterial infections, such as Campylobacter jejuni and Clostridium difficile, can also contribute to gastritis.

Non-infectious causes include consumption of irritating substances like spicy foods, citrus juice, or caffeine, which can trigger inflammation in the stomach lining. Additionally, chronic use of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), such as aspirin or ibuprofen, can lead to gastritis due to their ability to irritate the stomach lining.

Other potential causes include smoking, stress, and certain medical conditions like gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) and autoimmune disorders.

The most common causes of gastritis are listed below.

  • Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori) infection: H. pylori bacteria infect the stomach lining, causing inflammation and damage to the stomach lining, leading to gastritis.
  • Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs): Use of NSAIDs such as aspirin, ibuprofen, or naproxen can cause gastritis by irritating the stomach lining and reducing its natural protective mucus layer.
  • Stress: Physical or emotional stress can trigger gastritis by altering digestion and increasing acid production in the stomach.
  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD): GERD can lead to gastritis as stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus and irritates the stomach lining.
  • Viral infections: Certain viruses, such as the Epstein-Barr virus or herpes simplex virus, can cause gastritis by infecting the stomach lining.
  • Cancer treatment: Chemotherapy and radiation therapy used to treat cancer can damage the stomach lining and lead to gastritis.
  • Hormonal changes: Hormonal fluctuations during menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause can cause gastritis in some women.
  • Smoking: Smoking can irritate the stomach lining and increase acid production, leading to gastritis.
  • Eating spicy foods: Consuming extremely spicy or acidic foods can irritate the stomach lining and cause gastritis.
  • Vitamin deficiencies: Deficiencies in vitamins such as vitamin B12, iron, or folate can contribute to gastritis by impairing digestion and increasing inflammation.

What precautions to take when using herbal remedies for gastritis?

When considering the use of herbal remedies for gastritis, it is essential to take certain precautions.

First and foremost, always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement regimen, as some herbs may interact with prescribed medications or worsen underlying health conditions. Additionally, choose high-quality herbal products from reputable manufacturers that adhere to good manufacturing practices (GMPs) to ensure potency, purity, and safety.

Be cautious when consuming herbs that may exacerbate symptoms such as bloating, abdominal pain, or nausea, and start with small doses to assess tolerance. Furthermore, avoid self-medication with herbal remedies for gastritis if you have a history of bleeding disorders, are taking anticoagulant medications, or have an active stomach ulcer, as some herbs like ginger and turmeric may increase the risk of bleeding.

By taking these precautions, you can minimize potential risks and maximize the benefits of using herbal remedies to alleviate gastritis symptoms.
