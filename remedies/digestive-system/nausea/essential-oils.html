1. Zingiber officinale

Ginger essential oils helps with nausea because of its natural anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe an upset stomach and calm digestive discomfort.

The oil's active compound, gingerol, has been shown to bind to serotonin receptors in the gut, helping to reduce nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, ginger's warming and stimulating effects can help to improve digestion and alleviate symptoms of motion sickness, morning sickness, and chemotherapy-induced nausea.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger essential oils that aid with nausea.

  • Digestive stimulant: Ginger essential oil stimulates digestive enzymes and relaxes the muscles in the stomach, helping to alleviate nausea by improving digestion and reducing inflammation.
  • Anti-inflammatory: The anti-inflammatory compounds present in ginger essential oil help reduce inflammation in the stomach and intestines, which can contribute to feelings of nausea and vomiting.
  • Vomiting relief agent: Ginger essential oil has been shown to have a direct effect on the vagus nerve, which regulates stomach contractions and relaxes muscles to relieve vomiting and nausea.

The list below shows the primary parts of ginger essential oil used to make essential oils for nausea.

  • Rhyzomes: The thick, underground stems of ginger contain compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have natural anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate nausea.
  • Buds: Fresh ginger buds are rich in volatile oils that can be extracted to create a gentle, yet effective essential oil for relieving nausea and digestive discomfort.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger essential oil for nausea.

  1. Harvest fresh ginger roots from your garden or local market weigh 200g.
  2. Clean the ginger roots thoroughly under cold running water scrubbing off dirt and debris.
  3. Chop the clean ginger roots into small pieces weighing 150g.
  4. Combine the chopped ginger with 500ml of carrier oil such as coconut or sweet almond oil in a clean glass jar.
  5. Steep the mixture in a cool dark place for 2 weeks shaking daily until the oil has turned golden yellow.

  5. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass container and discard the solids, then store the mentha x piperita essential oil in an amber glass bottle for up to 6 months.
