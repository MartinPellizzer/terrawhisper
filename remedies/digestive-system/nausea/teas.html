1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale, also known as ginger, teas helps with nausea because it contains compounds that have anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

The active ingredients in ginger, such as gingerols and shogaols, work to calm the stomach and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. This helps to alleviate nausea and vomiting, making ginger tea a popular remedy for morning sickness, motion sickness, and other types of nausea.

The soothing effects of ginger tea can also help to ease digestive discomfort and promote a sense of calm.

Medicinal Constituents

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Zingiber officinale teas that help with nausea.

  • Gingerols: Gingerols are a group of phenolic compounds that help alleviate nausea by inhibiting the serotonin and dopamine pathways in the brain, which are responsible for inducing nausea and vomiting.
  • Shogaols: Shogaols are a type of gingerol derivative that have potent anti-inflammatory and anti-emetic properties, helping to reduce nausea and vomiting by blocking the action of cholecystokinin, a hormone that stimulates nausea.
  • Zingiberene: Zingiberene is a sesquiterpene that has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, helping to reduce nausea and vomiting by blocking the action of inflammatory mediators and reducing the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines.

Plant's Parts

The list below shows the primary parts of ginger used to make teas for nausea.

  • Rhyzomes: The most commonly used part for nausea teas due to their high concentration of gingerols and shogaols, which have anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Roots: The roots of Zingiber officinale are also used for nausea teas as they contain similar compounds to the rhyzomes, providing relief from nausea and stomach discomfort.
  • Buds: Some nausea teas may use the buds of Zingiber officinale, which are rich in bioactive compounds and have been traditionally used to alleviate nausea and digestive issues.

Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger for nausea.

  1. Measure 2-4 grams of dried ginger root and place it in a tea infuser or a heat-resistant cup.
  2. Boil 250-500 milliliters of water in a kettle or on the stovetop for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the ginger root and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using the infuser or a fine-mesh sieve and discard the solids.
  5. Serve the ginger tea hot or iced and adjust the sweetness to taste with honey or sugar.
