1. Zingiber officinale

Ginger capsules helps with nausea because of its natural ability to soothe and calm the digestive system.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, have anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce inflammation in the stomach and intestines, which can contribute to feelings of nausea.

Additionally, ginger has a natural motion-sickness reducing effect, making it an effective remedy for alleviating nausea caused by travel, seasickness, or other forms of motion-induced discomfort.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger capsules that aid with nausea.

  • Shogaol: A bioactive compound found in ginger, shogaol has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help to reduce nausea and vomiting by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that can cause stomach contractions.
  • Gingerols: Another active compound in ginger, gingerols have analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects that help to alleviate nausea and vomiting by reducing inflammation in the digestive tract and calming the stomach muscles.
  • Zingibain: A proteolytic enzyme found in ginger, zingibain helps to break down proteins and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, which can contribute to nausea and vomiting.

The list below shows the primary parts of ginger used to make capsules for nausea.

  • Rhyzomes: The rhizome is the underground stem of the ginger plant, which contains a high concentration of active compounds like gingerols and shogaols, making it effective in treating nausea.
  • Roots: Ginger roots are used to produce medicinal capsules as they contain a rich source of bioactive compounds, including phenolic acids and flavonoids, that help alleviate nausea symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger for nausea.

  1. Harvest 100 grams of fresh ginger root for grinding into fine powder.
  2. Dry the ground ginger in a low-temperature oven at 60°c for two hours.
  3. Measure 250 milliliters of water and mix with 20 grams of dried ginger powder.
  4. Simmer the mixture on medium heat for 30 minutes to create a decoction.
  5. Fill empty gelatin capsules with one teaspoon of the cooled decoction mixture.
