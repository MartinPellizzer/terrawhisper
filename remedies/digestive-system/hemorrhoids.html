What is hemorrhoids and how it affects your life?

Hemorrhoids refer to swollen veins in the lower part of the anus and rectum, which can cause discomfort, pain, and bleeding.

This condition can significantly affect a person's quality of life, leading to symptoms such as itching, burning, and pain during bowel movements. Hemorrhoids can also cause discomfort while sitting or standing for prolonged periods, making daily activities such as working, studying, or exercising difficult.

In severe cases, hemorrhoids can lead to anemia due to excessive blood loss, requiring medical intervention. Furthermore, the fear of pain or discomfort during bowel movements can cause individuals to avoid defecating, leading to constipation and worsening of hemorrhoid symptoms.

The emotional distress and embarrassment associated with hemorrhoids can also impact a person's mental health, causing anxiety and depression.
