1. Mentha x piperita

Peppermint essential oils helps with upset stomach because of its natural digestive properties.

The oil's active compound, menthol, stimulates digestion and relieves symptoms such as bloating, cramps, and nausea. It also helps to relax the muscles in the stomach, reducing spasms that can contribute to discomfort.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint essential oils that aid with upset stomach.

  • Digestive stimulant: Peppermint oil's primary constituent, menthol, stimulates digestive enzymes and increases gastric motility, helping to relieve symptoms such as bloating, cramps, and indigestion.
  • Antispasmodic: The menthol in peppermint oil relaxes the smooth muscle lining of the stomach and intestines, reducing spasms and cramping associated with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) and other digestive disorders.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Peppermint oil's flavonoids and phenolic acids have anti-inflammatory properties, which may help reduce inflammation in the gut and alleviate symptoms such as abdominal pain, diarrhea, and constipation.

The list below shows the primary parts of peppermint essential oil used to make essential oils for upset stomach.

  • Roots: Rich in menthol and other bioactive compounds, peppermint roots help alleviate digestive issues and reduce nausea and vomiting associated with upset stomach.
  • Leaves: Peppermint leaves contain high levels of rosmarinic acid, which has anti-inflammatory properties that can soothe the stomach lining and ease symptoms of indigestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint essential oil for upset stomach.

  1. Harvest fresh mentha x piperita leaves from your garden or obtain them from a trusted supplier.
  2. Wash the harvested leaves with distilled water and mild soap to remove dirt and impurities.
  3. Steam distill the washed leaves in a copper alembic still at 100°c for 2 hours.
  4. Separate the essential oil from the distillation residue using a separating funnel and collect it in a dark glass bottle.
  5. Store the extracted essential oil in a cool place away from direct sunlight and heat.
