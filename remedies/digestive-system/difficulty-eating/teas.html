1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains a compound called gingerol, which is known for its anti-inflammatory properties.

This makes it a potential remedy for people experiencing difficulty eating due to conditions like irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) or after surgery. When consumed as a tea, gingerol can help to soothe the digestive system and reduce nausea and discomfort. To use Zingiber officinale teas to help with difficulty eating, you can start by brewing one teaspoon of fresh ginger or one-half teaspoon of dried ginger in a cup of boiling water. Let it steep for 5-10 minutes before straining and drinking.
