1. Peppermint tea

Peppermint tea helps with stomach pain because it contains menthol, a compound that has antispasmodic properties, which can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles and reduce pain.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that aid with stomach pain.

  • Antispasmodic: Peppermint tea helps relax the gastrointestinal muscles, reducing stomach pain and cramping caused by spasms. This property is due to the active constituent menthol.
  • Carminative: Peppermint tea aids in the expulsion of gas from the digestive tract, alleviating bloating, discomfort, and pain. The active constituent, menthol, contributes to this carminative effect.
  • Analgesic: Peppermint tea has pain-relieving properties, which can help alleviate the pain associated with stomach ailments. The active constituent, menthol, is responsible for this analgesic effect.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Peppermint tea reduces inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract, which can help soothe and heal stomach pain. The active constituents, rosmarinic acid and flavonoids, contribute to this anti-inflammatory effect.
  • Antiemetic: Peppermint tea can help alleviate nausea and vomiting, common symptoms associated with stomach pain. The active constituent, menthol, is responsible for this antiemetic effect.

The list below shows the primary parts of peppermint tea used to make teas for stomach pain.

  • Leaves: Contain menthol and methyl salicylate, which have antispasmodic and analgesic properties that can help relieve stomach pain.
  • Flowers: Rich in volatile oils, including menthol, that can help relax the gastrointestinal muscles and alleviate stomach discomfort.
  • Seeds: Contain high levels of antioxidants and have anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe an upset stomach.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for stomach pain.

  1. Measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried peppermint leaves, adjusting for personal taste.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and let it cool for 1-2 minutes to ensure it's not too hot.
  3. Steep the peppermint leaves in the hot water for 5-10 minutes, covered.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, discarding them safely.
  5. Sip the herbal peppermint tea slowly, allowing it to soothe your stomach pain.
