1. Marshmallow tea

Marshmallow tea helps with diarrhea because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes a gel when mixed with water.

This gel-like consistency can help to soothe and protect the inflamed or irritated mucous membranes in the digestive tract, reducing inflammation and promoting healing. Additionally, marshmallow tea has been shown to have antibacterial and antiviral properties, which may help to eliminate the underlying cause of diarrhea. The tea is also hydrating, which is important for people with diarrhea as they can become dehydrated due to fluid loss.

Drinking marshmallow tea may help to restore electrolyte balance and promote proper digestive function, ultimately alleviating diarrhea symptoms.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in marshmallow tea that aid with diarrhea.

  • Demulcent property: Marshmallow tea contains mucilage, a substance that forms a soothing film over the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, protecting them from irritation and reducing inflammation, which is crucial for alleviating diarrhea caused by gut irritation.
  • Emollient property: The tea's ability to soften and soothe the skin and mucous membranes helps reduce discomfort and inflammation in the digestive tract, which is important for addressing diarrhea's symptoms.
  • Anti-inflammatory property: Marshmallow tea has been shown to reduce inflammation in the gut, which can help manage diarrhea caused by inflammatory bowel conditions. This is attributed to its high flavonoid content.
  • Antidiarrheal property: Marshmallow tea can help increase the viscosity of intestinal contents, slowing down gut motility and thus reducing the frequency of stools, which is beneficial for managing diarrhea.
  • Mild astringent property: The tannins present in marshmallow tea have a mild astringent effect, helping to tighten and contract tissues, reducing secretions, and alleviating symptoms of diarrhea.

The list below shows the primary parts of marshmallow tea used to make teas for diarrhea.

  • Leaves: Rich in tannins, marshmallow leaves can help soothe inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate diarrhea.
  • Roots: The roots of the marshmallow plant contain mucilage, which can help thicken stools and reduce the frequency of diarrhea.
  • Flowers: Marshmallow flowers have anti-inflammatory properties and can help reduce irritation in the gut, providing relief from diarrhea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for diarrhea.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 cup of water, 2 tablespoons of dried marshmallow root, and 1 tablespoon of dried chamomile flowers.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the solid parts, then let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  4. Stir in 1-2 teaspoons of honey for added flavor and to soothe the stomach.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, between meals, to help manage diarrhea.

How to best treat diarrhea with herbal teas?

The best way to treat diarrhea with herbal teas is by choosing teas with tannins, such as black or red tea, or herbs known to reduce intestinal inflammation, like chamomile or peppermint.

Tannins can help alleviate diarrhea by slowing down the digestion process, while the anti-inflammatory properties of chamomile and peppermint can help soothe the gastrointestinal tract. It's important to drink these teas unsweetened, as added sugars can worsen diarrhea.

However, if diarrhea persists for more than a few days, it's crucial to seek medical attention, as it could indicate a more serious condition.

What ailments similar to diarrhea are treated with herbal teas?
