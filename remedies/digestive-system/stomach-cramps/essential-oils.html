The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint essential oil for stomach cramps.

  1. Harvest fresh peppermint leaves from your garden or store-bought ones with stems trimmed to 2-3 inches length.
  2. Dry the peppermint leaves in small bunches tied with twine, placing them upside down in a warm dry room for 7-10 days.
  3. Transfer the dried peppermint leaves to a glass jar and cover them with 100-proof vodka or everclear, ensuring complete coverage of the leaves.
  4. Store the jar in a dark cupboard at room temperature for 2-3 weeks, shaking it twice daily to allow steam distillation, then strain and discard solids.
  5. Collect the peppermint essential oil in a separate glass bottle after filtering the liquid through cheesecloth or coffee filters into an airtight container with tight-fitting lid for storage.
