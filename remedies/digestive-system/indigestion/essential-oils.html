1. Zingiber officinale

Ginger essential oils helps with indigestion because it contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol that have natural anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe digestive issues such as bloating, nausea, and stomach discomfort.

When inhaled or applied topically, the oil can stimulate digestion and alleviate symptoms of indigestion by reducing inflammation in the stomach lining and improving gut motility, making it a natural and effective remedy for promoting overall digestive health.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger essential oils that aid with indigestion.

  • Anti-inflammatory properties: Herbal ginger essential oil's anti-inflammatory compounds, such as gingerols and shogaols, help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, alleviating symptoms of indigestion and bloating.
  • Digestive enzyme stimulant properties: The oils' volatile compounds stimulate the production of digestive enzymes, enhancing digestion and absorption of nutrients, which can help alleviate nausea, bloating, and discomfort associated with indigestion.
  • Spasmolytic and carminative properties: Herbal ginger essential oil's ability to relax smooth muscle tissues and its carminative effects (preventing gas and bloating) can help soothe spasms in the digestive tract, reducing symptoms of indigestion such as cramps, flatulence, and discomfort.

The list below shows the primary parts of ginger essential oil used to make essential oils for indigestion.

  • Roots: The roots are good for making medicinal essential oils for indigestion because they contain compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have natural anti-inflammatory properties.
  • Rhyzomes: Rhyzomes are the thick, underground stems of the ginger plant that are rich in volatile oils, making them an excellent source for extracting essential oils with digestive benefits.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger essential oil for indigestion.

  1. Harvest fresh ginger roots weighing about 100 grams cut them into small pieces.
  2. Dry the ginger pieces in a warm place or using a food dehydrator for 24 hours.
  3. Steam distill the dried ginger pieces using 500 milliliters of water at atmospheric pressure.
  4. Collect and filter the extracted essential oil through a coffee filter paper within 30 minutes.
  5. Store the obtained zingiber officinale essential oil in dark glass bottles with tight-fitting lids.
