1. Peppermint tea

Peppermint tea helps with indigestion because it contains menthol, a compound that relaxes the gastrointestinal muscles, allowing for the smooth passage of food and reducing the likelihood of indigestion.

Additionally, peppermint tea stimulates the flow of bile, which is necessary for fat digestion, and its anti-inflammatory properties can help soothe inflammation in the digestive tract. Furthermore, peppermint tea has been shown to alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome, such as bloating, gas, and abdominal pain, all of which can contribute to indigestion.

Overall, peppermint tea is a natural and effective remedy for promoting healthy digestion and alleviating indigestion symptoms.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that aid with indigestion.

  • Antispasmodic: Peppermint tea helps relieve indigestion by reducing smooth muscle spasms in the gastrointestinal tract, which can alleviate symptoms like cramping, bloating, and gas. This is mainly due to the presence of menthol and menthone.
  • Carminative: Peppermint tea promotes the expulsion of gas from the digestive tract, reducing feelings of bloating and discomfort. This is attributed to the oil's ability to increase bile flow and relax the gastrointestinal tissue.
  • Analgesic: Peppermint tea has pain-relieving properties that can soothe digestive discomfort and pain associated with indigestion, thanks to the presence of menthol.
  • Antiemetic: Peppermint tea can help alleviate nausea and vomiting, common symptoms of indigestion, due to its calming effects on the gastrointestinal lining.
  • Cholagogue: Peppermint tea stimulates the production of bile in the liver, which aids in the digestion of fats and the absorption of nutrients, thus improving overall digestion.

The list below shows the primary parts of peppermint tea used to make teas for indigestion.

  • Leaves: Peppermint leaves are the most commonly used part of the plant in medicinal teas for indigestion due to their high concentration of menthol and methyl salicylate, which help relax the gastrointestinal muscles and provide digestive relief.
  • Flowers: Peppermint flowers contain essential oils that can aid in digestion, making them a valuable addition to medicinal teas for indigestion.
  • Seeds: Peppermint seeds are rich in antioxidants and can help soothe the digestive system, making them a useful ingredient in medicinal teas for indigestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint tea for indigestion.

  1. In a teapot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried peppermint leaves with 8 ounces of boiling water.
  2. Steep the peppermint leaves for 5-10 minutes to allow the active compounds to infuse.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the spent peppermint leaves.
  4. Optionally, add a small amount of honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the tea while it is still warm, up to three times a day as needed for indigestion relief.
