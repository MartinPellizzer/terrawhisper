1. Zingiber officinale teas

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been traditionally used to treat indigestion due to its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties.

These properties help to soothe the stomach, reduce nausea and alleviate discomfort, thereby providing relief from indigestion.

The bioactive constituents of ginger tea, including gingerols and shogaols, have been shown to have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which contribute to its therapeutic benefits.

Regular consumption of ginger tea has been found to provide relief from indigestion by reducing inflammation, promoting digestion, and alleviating symptoms such as bloating and nausea.

Featured Study

Recipe:

  1. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  2. Take 1 teaspoon of dried Zingiber officinale root (or 1-inch piece of fresh root) and place it in a tea infuser.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the tea infuser and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the root.
  5. Drink the tea as needed for indigestion relief.

Usage:

  1. Drink 1 cup of warm Zingiber officinale tea after meals
  2. Take 1/2 teaspoon of Zingiber officinale powder daily
  3. Use 1-2 cups of tea per day for indigestion relief
  4. Drink tea 30 minutes before eating to prevent indigestion
  5. Continue for 1-2 weeks to experience full benefits

Precautions should be taken when using these teas, including starting with small doses, avoiding consumption before bedtime, and being cautious of interactions with other medications or health conditions, especially pregnancy and breastfeeding.
