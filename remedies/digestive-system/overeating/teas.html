1. Peppermint tea

Peppermint tea helps with overeating because it has been shown to reduce appetite and decrease hunger cravings.

The menthol present in peppermint tea is known to stimulate the satiety center in the brain, which can help you feel full and satisfied. Additionally, the soothing and calming properties of peppermint tea can help reduce stress and anxiety, which are often linked to overeating.

Drinking peppermint tea before or after meals can also help with digestion and prevent bloating, making it an effective tool in maintaining a healthy weight.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint tea that aid with overeating.

  • Appetite suppression: Peppermint tea can help reduce overeating by promoting feelings of fullness, thanks to its active constituents including menthol and methyl salicylate which activate TRPM8 receptors in the gastrointestinal tract.
  • Improved digestion: Peppermint tea aids in proper digestion, preventing indigestion and bloating, which can contribute to overeating; this is due to the presence of volatile oils like menthol and menthone.
  • Stress reduction: Peppermint tea has calming properties that can help alleviate stress-related overeating, as it contains active compounds like rosmarinic acid and various flavonoids that have anxiolytic effects.
  • Metabolism boost: Peppermint tea can slightly increase metabolism, aiding in weight management; this is attributed to the presence of catechins, a type of antioxidant that has been linked to increased energy expenditure.
  • Blood sugar regulation: Peppermint tea may help regulate blood sugar levels, potentially reducing cravings for sugary foods; this is due to the active constituents like rosmarinic acid and other polyphenols that have been shown to improve insulin sensitivity.
