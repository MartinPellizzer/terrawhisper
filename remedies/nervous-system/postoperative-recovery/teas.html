The list below shows the primary parts of tea used to make teas for postoperative recovery.

  • Leaves: They are the most commonly used part due to their high antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which aid in reducing pain and inflammation after surgery.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic tea for postoperative recovery.

  1. Harvest 25-30 camellia sinensis leaves and buds for a single serving of loose-leaf tea.
  2. Rinse the camellia sinensis leaves and buds in cold water for 30 seconds to remove impurities.
  3. Steep 1 teaspoon of camellia sinensis leaves and buds in 8 ounces of boiling water for 3-5 minutes.
  4. Strain the camellia sinensis tea through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a teapot or cup.
  5. Serve the camellia sinensis tea immediately and enjoy while it is still warm and fragrant.
