1. Ginkgo biloba teas

Ginkgo biloba teas have been traditionally used to treat numbness in hands, also known as peripheral neuropathy, due to their vasodilatory and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help to improve blood circulation and reduce inflammation in the nerves, thereby alleviating numbness and tingling sensations in the hands.

The bioactive constituents of Ginkgo biloba tea, including flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide, help to increase blood flow and reduce oxidative stress, which contribute to the relief of numbness in the hands.

The benefits of using Ginkgo biloba teas to treat numbness in hands include improved circulation, reduced inflammation, and enhanced nerve function, making it a popular natural remedy for this condition.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water, 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves, and a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Place the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the tea infuser or strainer.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the leaves and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day, ideally after meals, for numbness in hands.

Usage:

  1. Consult doctor about using Ginkgo biloba tea for numbness in hands
  2. Start with 1/2 cup of Ginkgo biloba tea 2 times a day
  3. Drink tea 30 minutes before meals for better absorption
  4. Increase to 1 cup of tea 3 times a day if numbness persists
  5. Monitor numbness and adjust dosage as advised by doctor

Ginkgo biloba teas can be used to treat numbness in hands, but possible side effects may include dizziness, headaches, and stomach upset due to its blood-thinning properties, as well as allergic reactions in some individuals.

To avoid adverse effects, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase it, be cautious when consuming ginkgo biloba with other medications, and monitor blood pressure, as excessive consumption can lead to increased risk of bleeding.
