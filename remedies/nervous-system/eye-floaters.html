Eye Floaters: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Eye floaters are tiny, dark spots that appear to float within the visual field, caused by the deterioration of the vitreous gel in the eye, often due to aging, eye injury, or diabetes.

These floaters can be a nuisance, obstructing vision and causing eye strain, potentially leading to headaches, blurred vision, and decreased visual acuity. To alleviate eye floaters, herbal remedies such as ginkgo biloba, bilberry, and chamomile can be used in the form of teas, tinctures, or eye drops.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as they may interact with other medications or exacerbate underlying conditions, and to follow proper dosages and preparation methods to avoid any adverse effects.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of eye floaters, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of eye floaters?

The main causes of eye floaters are typically related to age-related changes, such as posterior vitreous detachment (PVD), where the vitreous gel in the eye separates from the retina.

This condition is often benign and does not cause significant vision problems. However, in some cases, the pulling force of PVD can cause small retinal tears or even retinal detachments, which can be a medical emergency. Other potential causes of eye floaters include diabetes, myopia (nearsightedness), and eye injuries that can lead to inflammation or scarring in the vitreous gel.

In rare cases, eye floaters can also be caused by systemic conditions such as uveitis or retinal vein occlusion.

  • Age: As people age, the vitreous gel in the eye can liquefy and cause small particles to float in the visual field, leading to eye floaters.
  • Myopia: High myopia can cause the vitreous gel to pull away from the retina, resulting in eye floaters due to the formation of adhesions between the vitreous and the retina.
  • Diabetes: Diabetes can cause damage to the blood vessels in the eye, leading to the formation of microvascular damage and bleeding that can cause eye floaters.
  • Trauma: Eye trauma can cause bleeding in the vitreous gel, resulting in the formation of small particles that can cause eye floaters.
  • Detachment: Rhegmatogenous retinal detachment can cause eye floaters due to the formation of adhesions between the retina and the vitreous gel.
  • Surgery: Eye surgery, such as cataract surgery, can cause eye floaters due to the manipulation of the vitreous gel and the formation of adhesions.
  • Inflammation: Posterior uveitis can cause inflammation in the eye, leading to the formation of small particles and eye floaters.
  • Tumors: Vitreous and retinal tumors can cause eye floaters due to the bleeding and formation of adhesions associated with these conditions.
  • Infections: Bacterial and fungal infections in the eye can cause inflammation and bleeding, resulting in the formation of eye floaters.
