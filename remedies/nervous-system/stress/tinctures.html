1. Ashwagandha

Ashwagandha tincture helps with stress because it contains adaptogenic properties that enable the body to better cope with stress.

This herbal extract works by regulating the levels of cortisol, a stress-induced hormone, in the body. By reducing cortisol levels, ashwagandha tincture promotes relaxation, improves mood, and enhances mental clarity, all of which contribute to stress reduction.

Moreover, it supports the adrenal glands, which are responsible for releasing stress hormones, allowing them to function optimally and minimizing the negative effects of stress on the body.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ashwagandha that aid with stress.

  • Anti-anxiety: St. John's wort contains active constituents like hypericin and hyperforin, which have been shown to help reduce anxiety levels, thus alleviating stress.
  • Antidepressant: The herb has natural antidepressant properties due to its ability to increase the levels of certain neurotransmitters in the brain, thereby improving mood and minimizing stress.
  • Nervine: St. John's wort acts as a nerve tonic, helping to soothe and calm the nervous system, which is crucial in managing stress and promoting relaxation.
  • Adaptogenic: The herb has adaptogenic properties that help the body adapt to stress and maintain emotional balance, thereby promoting overall well-being and resilience.
  • Anti-inflammatory: St. John's wort contains various anti-inflammatory compounds, which can help reduce inflammation in the body, thereby alleviating stress-related symptoms and improving overall health.
  • Sedative: The herb has mild sedative properties, which can help promote better sleep and reduce stress-induced insomnia, thus improving stress management.
  • Antioxidant: St. John's wort contains potent antioxidants that help protect the body from oxidative stress and damage, thereby promoting overall health and reducing stress-related symptoms.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in ashwagandha that help with stress.

  • Roots: The roots of ashwagandha are used to make medicinal tea for stress due to their high concentration of withanolides, which have been shown to reduce stress and anxiety.
  • Leaves: The leaves of ashwagandha contain adaptogenic properties that help the body cope with stress and promote relaxation.
  • Flowers: The flowers of ashwagandha contain antioxidants that help protect the body from the damaging effects of stress.
  • Seeds: The seeds of ashwagandha contain triethylene glycol, which has been shown to have a sedative effect, making it useful for reducing stress and promoting sleep.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ashwagandha for stress.

  1. Chop 2 ounces of dried ashwagandha root into small pieces.
  2. Combine root pieces and 1 pint of vodka or rum in a glass jar.
  3. Seal the jar and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks.
  4. Shake the jar gently every few days to help the extraction process.
  5. Strain the mixture and store the resulting tincture in a dark glass bottle.

Right below you will find a list of the most important parts in golden root that help with stress.

  • Rhizomes: Rich in antioxidants that protect cells from damage caused by stress.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic golden root for stress.

  1. Chop 1 oz of dried golden root (Rhodiola rosea) root into small pieces and combine with 2 cups of 40% alcohol or higher in a clean glass jar.
  2. Seal the jar and let it steep in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking vigorously every few days.
  3. After steeping, strain the mixture through cheesecloth into a clean, dark glass bottle, squeezing out all liquid from the plant material.
  4. Discard the plant material and store the tincture in a cool, dark place.
  5. Take 30 drops of the tincture 1-3 times daily, as needed, for stress relief.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic skullcap for stress.

  1. Harvest 1 ounce of dried skullcap herb, cutting stems and flowers at peek bloom, and allow to air dry for 3 days.
  2. Fill a clean mason jar with 8 ounces of 80 proof vodka, completely submerging the skullcap herb.
  3. Secure a lid on the jar, store in a cool, dry place, and shake vigorously for 1 minute every day for 2 weeks.
  4. Strain the liquid using cheesecloth into a dark glass dropper bottle, squeezing out all excess liquid and discarding the plant material.
  5. Label the bottle with the name, ingredients, date, and dosage suggestions, storing in a cool, dark place for up to 2 years.
