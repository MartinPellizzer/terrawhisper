1. Valeriana officinalis teas

Valeriana officinalis teas have been used for centuries to treat stress and anxiety due to their sedative and relaxing properties.

The herbal preparation helps to treat stress by promoting a calm and peaceful state of mind, reducing feelings of anxiety and restlessness, and improving sleep quality.

The bioactive constituents of Valeriana officinalis teas, including valerenic acid, valeranone, and valepotriates, are responsible for its anxiolytic and sedative effects, which help to alleviate stress and promote relaxation.

Regular consumption of Valeriana officinalis teas has been shown to provide numerous benefits, including reduced stress and anxiety levels, improved sleep quality, and enhanced overall well-being.

Related Study

According to "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", Valeriana officinalis teas for stress show to significantly attenuate the subjective emotional stress response during an acute stress situation, as evidenced by reduced self-reported anxiety responses in comparison to placebo and no treatment.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Valeriana officinalis root.
  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried Valeriana officinalis root per cup of boiling water. If you prefer a stronger tea, use 2 teaspoons.
  3. Steep the Valeriana officinalis root in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using a tea strainer or a piece of cheesecloth. Discard the root.
  5. Drink the tea as needed to help with stress relief. You can drink it up to 3 times a day.

Valeriana officinalis teas can cause drowsiness and stomach upset in some individuals, particularly when consumed in large quantities or before driving or operating heavy machinery.

To minimize potential side effects, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase as needed, and to avoid consuming Valeriana officinalis teas with other sedatives or medications that can enhance its sedative effects.
