1. Zingiber officinale

Ginger tinctures helps with nausea because of its natural anti-inflammatory properties, which work to calm the digestive system and alleviate symptoms associated with motion sickness, morning sickness, and chemotherapy.

The active compounds in ginger, such as gingerol and shogaol, bind to receptors in the stomach, reducing inflammation and soothing the digestive tract.

This results in a reduction of nausea and vomiting, allowing for a smoother and more comfortable digestive process.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger tinctures that aid with nausea.

  • Anti-inflammatory compounds: Ginger contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have potent anti-inflammatory effects that help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, alleviating nausea and discomfort.
  • Digestive enzymes: Ginger stimulates the production of digestive enzymes in the stomach, such as gastric amylase and lipase, which helps to break down food more efficiently and absorb nutrients properly, reducing nausea and bloating.
  • Antioxidants and free radical scavengers: Ginger's antioxidant properties help to neutralize free radicals that can cause oxidative stress and damage to the digestive system, contributing to nausea and discomfort.

The list below shows the primary parts of ginger tincture used to make tinctures for nausea.

  • Roots: The roots are rich in bioactive compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that help alleviate nausea.
  • Rhyzomes: As the storage organ of the ginger plant, rhyzomes contain concentrated amounts of essential oils and bioactive compounds that are specifically designed to soothe digestive issues and reduce nausea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tincture for nausea.

  1. Harvest high-quality zingiber officinale rhizomes at the peak of freshness and cleanliness.
  2. Chop fresh or dried ginger root into small pieces ensuring uniform size for optimal extraction.
  3. Combine one part chopped ginger with five parts 95% ethanol in a clean glass jar.
  4. Allow the mixture to steep in a cool dark place for four weeks shaking daily, ensuring complete extraction.
  5. Strain and filter the liquid through cheesecloth or a coffee filter discarding solids and transferring tincture to a dark glass bottle.
