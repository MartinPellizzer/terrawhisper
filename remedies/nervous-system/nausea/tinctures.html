1. Ginger tincture

Ginger tincture helps with nausea because it contains active compounds called gingerols and shogaols, which have been shown to alleviate digestive issues and reduce feelings of queasiness.

These compounds work by blocking the serotonin receptors in the gut that trigger nausea and vomiting. Additionally, ginger has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe an irritated stomach lining, further reducing nausea symptoms.

Its rapid absorption into the bloodstream allows for quick relief, making it a popular natural remedy for nausea caused by various factors such as motion sickness, chemotherapy, or pregnancy.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger tincture that aid with nausea.

  • Anti-emetic: Ginger tincture can help reduce the sensation of nausea and prevent vomiting by blocking the serotonin receptors in the gut, thanks to its active constituents, gingerols and shogaols.
  • Gastroprotective: Ginger tincture helps protect the stomach lining from irritation and inflammation, reducing nausea caused by gastric disturbances, due to the presence of gingerols and zingerone.
  • Prokinetic: Ginger tincture can stimulate gastric motility, promoting the faster movement of food from the stomach to the intestines, alleviating nausea caused by delayed gastric emptying, owing to the action of gingerols and shogaols.
  • Analgesic: Ginger tincture has pain-relieving properties that can help alleviate nausea caused by abdominal pain or discomfort, as its active constituents, gingerols and shogaols, inhibit the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes.
  • Anxiolytic: Ginger tincture may help reduce anxiety and stress, which can contribute to nausea symptoms, by modulating neurotransmitter activity in the brain, particularly GABA, serotonin, and dopamine, due to the presence of gingerols and shogaols.

The list below shows the primary parts of ginger tincture used to make tinctures for nausea.

  • Rhizomes: Ginger rhizomes are commonly used to make medicinal tea for nausea due to their potent anti-emetic properties.
  • Roots: Ginger roots contain bioactive compounds that help alleviate nausea and vomiting, making them an excellent choice for herbal teas.
  • Seeds: Ginger seeds have been used in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues such as nausea, as they contain compounds that promote gastrointestinal motility.
  • Flowers: Ginger flowers can help soothe the stomach and reduce nausea symptoms, as they contain volatile oils that have carminative and anti-inflammatory properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger tincture for nausea.

  1. Chop 1 cup of fresh ginger root into small pieces and place in a clean glass jar.
  2. Pour 2 cups of 80-proof vodka or rum over the ginger, ensuring it is fully submerged.
  3. Secure a lid on the jar and store in a cool, dark place for 2-4 weeks, shaking daily.
  4. Strain the mixture using cheesecloth, discarding the ginger solids.
  5. Store the resulting tincture in a tightly sealed glass bottle and use 1-2 mL as needed for nausea.
