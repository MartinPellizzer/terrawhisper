1. Zingiber officinale

Ginger essential oils helps with nausea because of its natural anti-inflammatory properties, which soothe the stomach lining and reduce digestive discomfort.

The oil's active compounds, such as gingerol and shogaol, have been shown to bind to receptors in the brain, suppressing the symptoms of nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, ginger's aroma can also stimulate digestion and relieve bloating, further alleviating queasy feelings and providing fast relief from morning sickness and motion-related nausea.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger essential oils that aid with nausea.

  • Anti-inflammatory compounds: Ginger's essential oil contains anti-inflammatory compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate nausea.
  • Digestive stimulants: The oil has natural digestive stimulants that can help stimulate digestion, relieve bloating, and calm the stomach muscles, reducing nausea and vomiting.
  • Antiemetic properties: Herbal ginger essential oil has been shown to have antiemetic properties, which means it can help prevent or reduce the frequency and severity of nausea, vomiting, and other symptoms associated with motion sickness, morning sickness, and chemotherapy-induced nausea.

The list below shows the primary parts of ginger used to make essential oils for nausea.

  • Roots: The roots are a rich source of volatile compounds that have been shown to alleviate nausea and vomiting.
  • Rhyzomes: The rhyzome, or underground stem, contains a high concentration of gingerols and shogaols, which have potent anti-nausea properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger for nausea.

  1. Harvest fresh ginger roots of zingiber officinale at dawn when they are most potent and store them in a cool dry place for up to two weeks.
  2. Wash the harvested ginger roots thoroughly under cold running water removing any dirt or debris from their surfaces.
  3. Dry the washed ginger roots by spreading them out on paper towels or clean cloth towels allowing excess moisture to evaporate completely.
  4. Steam distill the dried ginger roots using a still apparatus with 20 kilograms of roots per liter of distilled water for three hours at atmospheric pressure.
  5. Collect and filter the resulting essential oil using cheesecloth or a coffee filter discarding any impurities and storing the pure zingiber officinale essential oil in dark glass bottles tightly sealed at room temperature.
