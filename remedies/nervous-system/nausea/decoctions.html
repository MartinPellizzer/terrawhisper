Ginger decoctions helps with nausea because of its unique composition that targets the underlying causes of queasiness.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginger decoctions that aid with nausea.

  • Ginger's anti-inflammatory compounds: Ginger contains compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have potent anti-inflammatory effects, helping to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract and alleviate nausea symptoms.
  • Ginger's 5-ht3 receptor antagonism: Ginger has been shown to interact with serotonin receptors in the gut, blocking the action of serotonin on 5-HT3 receptors, which can help to reduce nausea and vomiting by altering the body's neurotransmitter balance.

The list below shows the primary parts of ginger used to make decoctions for nausea.

  • Roots: Ginger roots are used to make decoctions that soothe the stomach and calm digestive issues, making them an effective remedy for nausea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger for nausea.

  1. Measure one teaspoon of dried ginger root per cup of water.
  2. Add the dried ginger root to a pot filled with cold water and bring it to boil.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes or until desired strength is achieved.
  4. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean cup.
  5. Let the decoction cool down slightly before consumption.
