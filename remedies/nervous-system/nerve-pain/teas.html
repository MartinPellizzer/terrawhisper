Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian, teas helps with nerve pain because of its natural ability to calm the nervous system.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian for nerve pain.

  1. Harvest valeriana officinalis roots in late summer or early fall when the plant is mature and the roots are fully developed.
  2. Clean the harvested valeriana officinalis roots thoroughly with cold running water to remove any dirt or debris.
  3. Dry the cleaned valeriana officinalis roots in a warm dry place away from direct sunlight for several weeks.
  4. Grind the dried valeriana officinalis roots into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle or a coffee grinder to desired consistency.
  5. Steep 1 teaspoon of ground valeriana officinalis in 8 ounces of boiling water for 5 to 10 minutes to make a soothing tea.
