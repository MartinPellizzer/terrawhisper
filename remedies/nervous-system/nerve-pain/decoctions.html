1. Valeriana officinalis

Valerian decoctions helps with nerve pain because it has a profound impact on the nervous system.

The decotion's active compounds, such as valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, interact with the brain's GABA receptors, promoting relaxation and reducing excitability. This can help alleviate symptoms of neuropathic pain, which are often characterized by numbness, tingling, and burning sensations.

By calming the nervous system, valerian decoctions may also help reduce inflammation and promote healing in damaged nerve tissue, providing relief from chronic nerve pain.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian decoctions that aid with nerve pain.

  • Sedative and anxiolytic compounds: Valerian contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and volatile oils that have sedative and anxiolytic effects, which can help reduce nervous tension and anxiety that may exacerbate nerve pain.
  • Antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties: The decotion's antispasmodic compounds, such as isovaleric acid and valerenic acid, help to relax muscle spasms and reduce inflammation in the nerves, providing relief from nerve pain.
  • Gaba-mimetic activity: Valerian contains GABA-like compounds that mimic the action of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter that helps regulate neuronal excitability and can contribute to nerve pain; this property helps to reduce hyperexcitability in nerves, alleviating pain.

The list below shows the primary parts of valerian used to make decoctions for nerve pain.

  • Roots: Rich in sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, valerian roots are known for their calming and sedative properties, which can help relieve nerve pain.
  • Rhyzomes: The underground stem of the valerian plant, rhyzomes contain a higher concentration of active compounds than roots, making them effective in treating nerve-related disorders.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian for nerve pain.

  1. Harvest fresh or dried valeriana officinalis roots in the morning after sunrise for maximum potency.
  2. Rinse the harvested material with clean water and chop it into small pieces to enhance solubility.
  3. Combine 10-20 grams of chopped valeriana officinalis roots with 500 milliliters of boiling water in a saucepan.
  4. Simmer the mixture at a gentle boil for 15-20 minutes, allowing the active compounds to infuse into the liquid.
  5. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean container, discarding the solids and retaining the herbal liquid.
