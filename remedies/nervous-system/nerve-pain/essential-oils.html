1. Valeriana officinalis

Valerian essential oils helps with nerve pain because of its natural analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The oil's compounds, such as valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, have been shown to reduce inflammation and calm irritated nerves, providing relief from chronic nerve pain conditions like sciatica, neuropathy, and fibromyalgia.

Additionally, the oil's sedative properties can help promote relaxation and reduce stress, which are often exacerbating factors of nerve pain.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian essential oils that aid with nerve pain.

  • Analgesic: Valerian oil has analgesic properties, which means it can reduce or eliminate pain by interacting with opioid receptors in the brain and spinal cord, providing relief from nerve-related discomfort.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Valerian oil's anti-inflammatory properties can help reduce swelling and inflammation around affected nerves, which can contribute to nerve pain, making it easier to manage symptoms.
  • Muscle relaxant: Valerian oil has a calming effect on muscles and nervous tissue, relaxing tense muscles and reducing muscle cramps, spasms, and twitching that can exacerbate nerve pain.

The list below shows the primary parts of valerian essential oil used to make essential oils for nerve pain.

  • Roots: Valerian roots are rich in sesquiterpenes and other bioactive compounds, making them a popular choice for creating calming and sedative essential oils that can help alleviate nerve pain.
  • Rhyzomes: Valerian rhyzomes contain a high concentration of valerenic acid, which is believed to have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, making them useful for reducing nerve pain and inflammation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian essential oil for nerve pain.

  1. Harvest high-quality dried valeriana officinalis roots and stems from reputable sources weighing at least 100 grams for optimal results.
  2. Crush the dried plant material into small pieces using a mortar and pestle or food processor to release its natural oils and flavors.
  3. Transfer the crushed plant material to a clean glass jar with a tight-fitting lid and add 300 milliliters of carrier oil such as jojoba or sweet almond oil at a ratio of 1:3.
  4. Store the mixture in a cool dark place for six to eight weeks allowing the valeriana officinalis roots and stems to infuse their natural properties into the carrier oil.
  5. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another clean glass container discarding the solids and storing the resulting essential oil in dark glass bottles with tight-fitting lids for up to six months.
