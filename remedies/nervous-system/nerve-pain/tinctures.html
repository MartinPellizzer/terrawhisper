Licorice tinctures helps with nerve pain because of its potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice for nerve pain.

  1. Harvest dried glycyrrhiza glabra roots from a reputable supplier or grow your own in well-draining soil and full sun.
  2. Chop the dried roots into small pieces and combine them with 50% vodka at a ratio of one part root to three parts solvent.
  3. Store the mixture in an amber glass jar with a tight-fitting lid and allow it to macerate for four weeks, shaking daily.
  4. After four weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another amber glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Allow the tincture to mature for six months before decanting it into smaller glass bottles with dropper lids and storing them in a cool dark place.
