1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Licorice tinctures helps with nerve pain because of its potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Glycyrrhizin, a compound found in licorice root, has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, reducing inflammation and oxidative stress in the body. This can help alleviate symptoms of nerve damage, such as numbness, tingling, and shooting pain.

Additionally, licorice's flavonoids and saponins may also help to calm irritated nerves, promoting a sense of relaxation and ease in individuals suffering from neuropathic pain.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in licorice tinctures that aid with nerve pain.

  • Glycyrrhizin: This compound has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which can help reduce inflammation and pain associated with nerve damage or neuropathy.
  • Flavonoids: Licorice contains a range of flavonoids, including liquiritin and isoliquiritigenin, that have been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help protect against oxidative stress and nerve damage.
  • Saponins: The saponin content in licorice tincture has been found to have a neuroprotective effect, helping to preserve the integrity of nerve tissue and reduce pain signals transmitted to the brain.

The list below shows the primary parts of licorice tincture used to make tinctures for nerve pain.

  • Roots: Rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound that has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making it effective for soothing nerve pain.
  • Rhyzomes: Contains flavonoids and phenolic acids that have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, helping to reduce inflammation and discomfort associated with nerve pain.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice tincture for nerve pain.

  1. Harvest dried glycyrrhiza glabra roots from a reputable supplier or grow your own in well-draining soil and full sun.
  2. Chop the dried roots into small pieces and combine them with 50% vodka at a ratio of one part root to three parts solvent.
  3. Store the mixture in an amber glass jar with a tight-fitting lid and allow it to macerate for four weeks, shaking daily.
  4. After four weeks, strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter into another amber glass jar, discarding the solids.
  5. Allow the tincture to mature for six months before decanting it into smaller glass bottles with dropper lids and storing them in a cool dark place.

Bell pepper tinctures helps with nerve pain because it contains a unique blend of compounds, including capsaicin and antioxidants, that work synergistically to reduce inflammation and alleviate neuralgia.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic bell pepper tincture for nerve pain.

  1. Harvest fresh or dried capsicum annuum leaves and stems in late summer or early fall when they are most potent.
  2. Cleanly chop 2 cups of the harvested plant material into small pieces to facilitate extraction.
  3. Combine the chopped plant material with 4 cups of 95% ethanol in a clean glass jar and cover it with a lid.
