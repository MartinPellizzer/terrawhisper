Sleep Deprivation: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Sleep deprivation is a state of inadequate sleep, typically defined as less than 7-8 hours of sleep per night, leading to fatigue, decreased cognitive function, and impaired daily activities.

Common causes include irregular sleep schedules, stress, and lifestyle factors such as excessive caffeine and screen time. Prolonged sleep deprivation can lead to serious health consequences, including cardiovascular disease, obesity, and compromised immune function. To alleviate sleep deprivation, herbal remedies such as valerian root, chamomile, and lavender can be used in teas, tinctures, or supplements.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding individuals, as they may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of sleep deprivation, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of sleep deprivation?

The main causes of sleep deprivation are primarily linked to lifestyle factors such as irregular sleep schedules (1-2% of adults experience shift work sleep disorder), excessive exposure to electronic devices and blue light emissions (71% of adults report using a phone or tablet before bedtime), physical inactivity (sedentary behavior is associated with 30-40% lower sleep efficiency), consuming stimulating substances like caffeine and nicotine (caffeine can delay sleep onset by 30-60 minutes), and stress and anxiety (40% of adults experience sleep disruption due to stress).

  • Work Shifts: Working non-traditional hours or irregular schedules, such as night shifts, rotating shifts, or working long hours, can disrupt the body's natural sleep-wake cycle and lead to sleep deprivation.
  • Stress: Chronic stress can interfere with sleep by increasing the production of stress hormones like cortisol and adrenaline, making it difficult to fall asleep or stay asleep.
  • Sleep Disorders: Conditions such as insomnia, sleep apnea, restless leg syndrome, and narcolepsy can cause sleep deprivation by disrupting normal sleep patterns.
  • Lifestyle Factors: Engaging in activities that interfere with sleep, such as consuming caffeine, nicotine, or heavy meals close to bedtime, can lead to sleep deprivation.
  • Aging: As people age, their sleep patterns can change, leading to lighter, less restorative sleep and increased risk of sleep deprivation.
  • Travel: Crossing time zones or traveling to a new environment can disrupt the body's natural sleep-wake cycle and lead to sleep deprivation.
  • Pregnancy: Pregnancy can lead to sleep deprivation due to discomfort, frequent urination, and anxiety.
  • Medications: Taking certain medications, such as those used to treat depression, ADHD, and high blood pressure, can interfere with sleep and lead to sleep deprivation.
  • Illness: Having a chronic illness, such as chronic pain or fibromyalgia, can lead to sleep deprivation due to discomfort, pain, and anxiety.
