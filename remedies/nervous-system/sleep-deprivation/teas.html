Valeriana officinalis teas have been traditionally used to treat sleep deprivation due to their sedative and relaxing properties.

The herbal preparation helps to treat this ailment by promoting a state of deep relaxation, reducing anxiety and stress levels, and regulating sleep patterns.

Valeriana officinalis teas contain bioactive constituents such as valerenic acid and valepotriates, which are responsible for their sedative and anxiolytic effects, helping individuals to fall asleep faster and sleep more soundly.

The benefits of using Valeriana officinalis teas to treat sleep deprivation include improved sleep quality, reduced stress and anxiety levels, and enhanced overall well-being.

Related Study

According to the study, Valeriana officinalis teas for sleep deprivation are associated with improvements in some sleep parameters, such as sleep latency and quality of sleep, when used on its own or in combination with hops.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 teaspoon of dried Valeriana officinalis root, 1 cup of boiling water, and a heat-resistant cup.
  2. Pour the boiling water over the dried root in the cup.
  3. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to release.
  4. Strain the liquid into a new cup using a tea strainer or a piece of cheesecloth.
  5. Drink the tea 30 minutes to 1 hour before bedtime to help with sleep.

Usage:

  1. Check package for recommended dosage, usually 1-2 teaspoons per cup.
  2. Steep tea for 5-7 minutes, as instructed on packaging.
  3. Drink 1 cup 30 minutes before bedtime, as needed.
  4. Start with low dose (1 teaspoon) and adjust as needed.
  5. Use Valeriana officinalis tea for 7-10 nights to establish a sleep routine.

To minimize side effects, it is recommended to start with low doses and gradually increase as needed, and to avoid consuming Valeriana officinalis teas with other substances that may interact with it, such as alcohol, certain medications, or other herbal remedies.
