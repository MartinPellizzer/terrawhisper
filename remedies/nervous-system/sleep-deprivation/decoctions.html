Valerian decoctions helps with sleep deprivation because it promotes relaxation and calms the mind, allowing individuals to fall asleep faster.

The list below shows the primary parts of valerian used to make decoctions for sleep deprivation.

  • Roots: Valerian roots are considered one of the most effective parts of the plant for promoting relaxation and inducing sleep due to their high concentration of valerenic acid.
  • Rhyzomes: Valerian rhizomes contain a unique combination of alkaloids that help regulate sleep patterns, making them an ideal part of the plant for medicinal decoctions.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian for sleep deprivation.

  1. Gather dried valeriana officinalis root and boiling water according to the recommended ratio of one tablespoon of dried root per cup of water.
  2. Combine the dried root with boiling water in a saucepan and reduce heat to simmer for 10-15 minutes to steep the herb.
  3. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a teapot or cup, discarding the solids and retaining the liquid decoction.
  4. Allow the decoction to cool to room temperature before transferring it to an airtight container for storage in the refrigerator overnight or up to 24 hours before consumption.
  5. Consume the cooled decoction as needed up to one hour prior to bedtime, ideally one-half cup twice daily, and repeat as necessary until sleep is restored.
