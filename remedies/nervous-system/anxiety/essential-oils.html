1. Lavandula angustifolia

English lavender essential oils helps with anxiety because its calming and soothing properties can promote relaxation and reduce stress levels.

The sweet, floral scent of lavender oil has been shown to slow down heart rate and lower blood pressure, calming the nervous system and quieting a racing mind.

By reducing feelings of anxiety and promoting a sense of calm, English lavender essential oils can help individuals cope with everyday worries and improve their overall mental well-being.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in english lavender essential oils that aid with anxiety.

  • Anxiolytic (calming) effects: The calming compounds in Lavender oil, such as linalool and linalyl acetate, can help reduce anxiety by promoting relaxation and decreasing stress levels.
  • Sedative properties: Lavender oil's sedative effects can help slow down brain activity, reducing feelings of overwhelm, nervousness, and agitation that often accompany anxiety.
  • Antidepressant properties: The antidepressant compounds in Lavender oil may help reduce symptoms of depression, which are closely linked to anxiety, by increasing the production of serotonin and other neurotransmitters that regulate mood.

The list below shows the primary parts of english lavender essential oil used to make essential oils for anxiety.

  • Roots: The roots are used due to their ability to promote relaxation and reduce anxiety, as they contain a high concentration of calming compounds like apigenin.
  • Leaves: Lavender leaves are used because they possess a unique composition of volatile compounds that can help soothe the nervous system, alleviating symptoms of anxiety.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic english lavender essential oil for anxiety.

  1. Harvest dried lavender flowers at dawn when fragrant oil content is highest for maximum potency.
  2. Steam distill the harvested lavender flowers using a clevenger apparatus at 100°c temperature for 2 hours.
  3. Collect and filter the obtained essential oil from steam distillation process into dark glass containers immediately.
  4. Blend 10% lavandula angustifolia essential oil with 90% carrier oil such as sweet almond oil or coconut oil in a ratio.
  5. Allow the blended mixture to mature for at least 24 hours before use or further processing.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic chamomile essential oil for anxiety.

  1. Harvest fresh matricaria chamomilla flowers on a sunny morning after the dew has evaporated using scissors.
  2. Dry the harvested flowers in a warm ventilated area away from direct sunlight for 7-10 days or until completely dry using wire racks.
  3. Combine 100g of dried matricaria chamomilla flowers with 500ml of carrier oil such as coconut or olive oil in a glass jar with lid.
  4. Leave the mixture to steep for 2 weeks at room temperature shaking the jar daily to infuse the oils.
  5. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth into another clean container discarding the solids and store the infused oil in dark glass bottles for future use.
