1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba teas contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are compounds that help improve blood flow to the eyes and reduce inflammation.

These properties may help alleviate eye floaters, which are caused by small pieces of debris floating in the vitreous gel of the eye. The antioxidants in Ginkgo biloba also protect the eyes from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to the formation of eye floaters. If you're experiencing eye floaters and want to try Ginkgo biloba teas, start by steeping a teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it twice a day.

You can also add honey or lemon to make it more palatable. Some people find it helpful to drink the tea for 2-3 months to see noticeable improvements. When taking Ginkgo biloba teas, you may notice a decrease in the frequency and severity of eye floaters within a few weeks. The improved blood flow and reduced inflammation may also help with other eye-related issues, such as blurred vision or eye strain.

However, it's essential to note that Ginkgo biloba teas may interact with certain medications or worsen underlying medical conditions, so consult with a healthcare professional before starting treatment.

2. Curcuma longa

To try Curcuma longa teas for eye floaters, start by brewing a cup using one teaspoon of turmeric powder or a single teaspoon of dried Curcuma longa root in eight ounces of boiling water. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain and drink. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day.

You can also add a pinch of black pepper to enhance curcumin's absorption. As you continue to drink Curcuma longa teas, you may notice a reduction in the number of eye floaters you see. In some cases, the floaters may even disappear altogether.
