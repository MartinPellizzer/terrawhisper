1. Valeriana officinalis

Valerian teas helps with insomnia because of its unique ability to calm the mind and body, promoting relaxation and reducing stress.

The tea's active compounds, such as valeric acid and isovaleric acid, work together to slow down brain activity, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep throughout the night.

Additionally, valerian teas have been shown to increase the levels of GABA, a neurotransmitter that regulates sleep, allowing for a deeper and more restful sleep.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian teas that aid with insomnia.

  • Sedative and anxiolytic properties: Valerian tea contains compounds like valerenic acid, which have a calming effect on the nervous system, helping to reduce stress and anxiety, making it easier to fall asleep.
  • Muscle relaxation properties: The alpha-linolenic acid present in valerian root helps to relax muscles, reducing muscle cramps, spasms, and restlessness, allowing for a more peaceful sleep.
  • Gaba-enhancing properties: Valerian tea contains GABA-like compounds that enhance the activity of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), an inhibitory neurotransmitter that promotes relaxation and reduces anxiety, making it easier to fall into a deeper sleep.

The list below shows the primary parts of valerian used to make teas for insomnia.

  • Roots: The roots are the most commonly used part for making valerian tea, as they contain the highest concentration of valepotriates, which have a sedative effect on the nervous system.
  • Rhyzomes: Valerian rhyzomes, also known as rhizomes, are the underground stems that store nutrients and compounds responsible for their medicinal properties, making them an excellent choice for insomnia tea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian for insomnia.

  1. Harvest dried roots of valeriana officinalis for tea preparation.
  2. Heat fresh filtered water in a kettle or pot to a boil.
  3. Combine dried valerian roots with hot water in a teapot.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink valeriana officinalis tea at bedtime 30 minutes before sleep.
