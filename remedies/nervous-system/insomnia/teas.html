1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis, also known as valerian, teas helps with insomnia because of its unique ability to promote relaxation and reduce stress levels.

The roots of the valerian plant contain a group of compounds called valerenic acids, which have a sedative effect on the nervous system. As a result, valerian teas can help regulate sleep patterns, calm the mind, and induce a restful night's sleep.

This natural remedy has been used for centuries to alleviate insomnia and improve overall sleep quality.

Medicinal Constituents

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Valeriana officinalis teas that help with insomnia.

  • Valerenic acid: A sesquiterpene acid that helps reduce anxiety and stress, promoting relaxation and calmer sleep patterns.
  • Valeranone: A valerenic acid derivative that has a sedative effect on the nervous system, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep throughout the night.
  • Isovaleric acid: A short-chain fatty acid that has a sedative effect and can help reduce restlessness, promoting a more peaceful and restorative sleep.

Plant's Parts

The list below shows the primary parts of valerian used to make teas for insomnia.

  • Roots: They are the most commonly used part due to their high concentration of valerenic acid, a key compound responsible for the sedative effects.
  • Stems: While less commonly used than roots and rhizomes, stems are sometimes used in tea blends to provide a milder sedative effect.

Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian for insomnia.

  1. Harvest valeriana officinalis roots in the fall when the plant is mature and the roots are fully developed for better flavor and medicinal properties.
  2. Clean and dry the harvested roots thoroughly for at least two days to prevent any contamination or bacterial growth.
  3. Chop the dried roots into small pieces and measure out 2 to 3 teaspoons per 8 ounce cup of water for a standard infusion.
  4. Steep the chopped roots in boiling water for 5 to 7 minutes to release the active compounds and essential oils.
  5. Strain and serve the tea immediately to prevent any further extraction of the active ingredients.
