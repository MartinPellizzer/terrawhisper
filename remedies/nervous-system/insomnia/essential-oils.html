1. Valeriana officinalis

Valerian essential oils helps with insomnia because it promotes relaxation, calms the mind, and soothes the body.

The oil contains sesquiterpenes, which have a sedative effect on the nervous system, helping to quiet the brain and ease tension. When inhaled or applied topically before bed, valerian essential oil can help regulate sleep patterns, reduce restlessness, and increase the quality of sleep.

As a result, individuals who struggle with insomnia can find it easier to drift off to sleep and stay asleep throughout the night.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian essential oils that aid with insomnia.

  • Sedative and hypnotic properties: Valerian oil has a calming effect on the nervous system, helping to reduce stress and anxiety, making it easier to fall asleep.
  • Muscle relaxant properties: Valerian oil can help to relieve muscle tension and cramps, which is common among insomniacs, promoting relaxation and reducing discomfort that may keep individuals awake.
  • Anxiolytic (anxiety-reducing) properties: The anxiolytic effects of valerian oil help to reduce anxiety and stress levels, allowing individuals to calm their minds and bodies before sleep.

The list below shows the primary parts of valerian essential oil used to make essential oils for insomnia.

  • Roots: The roots are rich in sesquiterpenes, which have a sedative effect and are responsible for the relaxing properties of Valerian oil.
  • Rhizomes: The rhizomes contain valerenic acid, a compound that has been shown to promote relaxation and reduce stress, making them ideal for insomnia relief.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian essential oil for insomnia.

  1. Harvest fresh or dried valerian roots from sustainable sources weighing approximately 100g.
  2. Dry the valerian roots in a cool dark place for at least two weeks to reduce moisture content.
  3. Steam distill the dried valerian roots in a copper still using 500ml of water at 100°c for four hours.
  4. Collect and separate the essential oil from the distilled steam using a separator funnel at room temperature.
  5. Filter and bottle the valeriana officinalis essential oil in dark glass containers with tight-fitting lids and store in cool dark places.
