1. Valeriana officinalis

Valerian tinctures helps with insomnia because they contain compounds that interact with the brain's GABA receptors, promoting relaxation and reducing stress.

This calming effect allows individuals to fall asleep faster and stay asleep longer. Additionally, valerian root has a sedative property that helps quiet the mind and body, making it easier to drift off to sleep.

As a natural sleep aid, herbal valerian tinctures can be used as a safe and effective solution for improving sleep quality and reducing insomnia symptoms.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian tinctures that aid with insomnia.

  • Sedative and anxiolytic effects: Valerian root has been shown to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, making it easier to fall asleep.
  • Muscle relaxant properties: Valerian's sedative effects also help to relieve muscle tension and spasms, which can contribute to insomnia, allowing for a more restful sleep.
  • Gaba-mimetic activity: Valerian root contains compounds that mimic the action of GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid), a neurotransmitter that helps regulate sleep and relaxation, promoting deeper and more peaceful sleep.

The list below shows the primary parts of valerian tincture used to make tinctures for insomnia.

  • Roots: Valerian roots have been traditionally used to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality, making them an ideal starting point for medicinal tinctures.
  • Rhyzomes: Valerian rhyzomes, which are underground stems that store energy, contain a high concentration of active compounds that help regulate sleep patterns.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian tincture for insomnia.

  1. Harvest high-quality fresh or dried valerian root in the morning when dew is still present on the plants for optimal potency.
  2. Clean and chop the valerian root into small pieces to release its active compounds before maceration.
  3. Combine one part chopped valerian root with three parts pure grain alcohol such as vodka in a clean glass jar to create the tincture mixture.
  4. Allow the mixture to steep for 14 days at room temperature and shake gently every day to facilitate solvent extraction of active compounds.
  5. Strain the tincture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass bottle after the maceration period is complete to discard solids and achieve a clear liquid.
