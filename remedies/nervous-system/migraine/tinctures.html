9 Herbal Tinctures For Migraine

1. Valeriana officinalis

Valeriana officinalis tinctures contains valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, which are known for their sedative and anti-spasmodic properties.

These compounds can help to relax the muscles in the head and neck, reducing the frequency and severity of migraines. By doing so, it can also lower stress levels and anxiety, which are common triggers for migraine attacks. When using Valeriana officinalis tinctures for migraines, it's recommended to take a 10-20 drop dose 2-3 times a day, mixed with water.

Some people also find it helpful to take it at the onset of a migraine, as the anti-inflammatory properties may help to reduce pain and discomfort. It's also essential to note that Valeriana officinalis tinctures can interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and sedatives, so consult a healthcare professional before using it. Taking Valeriana officinalis tinctures for migraines may lead to a reduction in the frequency and severity of attacks, as well as a decrease in pain and discomfort. Some people may also experience improved sleep quality and reduced stress levels, which can contribute to a decrease in migraine occurrences.

However, it's essential to note that everyone's body is different, and individual results may vary.

2. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba tinctures contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are powerful compounds that help to reduce migraine symptoms.

These compounds work by improving blood flow to the brain, reducing inflammation, and preventing platelet aggregation. When taken as a tincture, Ginkgo biloba's flavonoids, particularly quercetin, kaempferol, and isorhapontigenin, can help to relax blood vessels and reduce the frequency and severity of migraine attacks. To use Ginkgo biloba tinctures for migraine, start by taking 20-30 drops, 2-3 times a day, mixed with water or another liquid.

It's recommended to take the tincture 15-30 minutes before a migraine is expected to occur. Some people may experience a reduction in migraine frequency and intensity within a few days to a week of consistent use. Others may need to use the tincture for several weeks to notice improvements.

As with any new supplement, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before starting to use Ginkgo biloba tinctures, especially if you're already taking medications or have any underlying health conditions.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent migraine from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent migraines.

Some teas, like feverfew and ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties that can reduce pain and discomfort. These teas can also help relax muscles and calm the mind, which may reduce migraine triggers.

Regularly sipping on herbal tea might help reduce migraine frequency and intensity.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for migraine every day?

While some herbal teas may help ease migraine symptoms, drinking them every day can be a concern.

Some herbs, like feverfew, may interact with medications or worsen conditions. Others, like peppermint, might cause stomach upset if consumed excessively.

Moderation is key when using herbal teas for migraine relief.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in migraine?

For some people, herbal teas can start to help with migraines after a few days.

However, it can take up to two weeks to notice any significant changes. Everyone's body is different, so it's hard to predict exactly when you'll see results.

Just be patient and keep trying different teas if needed.
