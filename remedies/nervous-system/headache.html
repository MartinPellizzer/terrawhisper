Headaches: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

A headache is a common and complex medical condition characterized by pain or discomfort in the head, neck, or scalp, often accompanied by sensitivity to light and sound.

The causes of headaches are multifaceted, including stress, muscle tension, dehydration, hormonal changes, and certain foods or substances. Prolonged or frequent headaches can lead to sleep disturbances, digestive problems, and decreased productivity, negatively impacting overall health and well-being. To alleviate headaches, herbal remedies such as feverfew, ginger, and peppermint can be used in teas, tinctures, or topical preparations.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, especially if you have underlying medical conditions or take prescription medications, as they may interact or exacerbate the issue.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of headaches, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of headaches?

The main causes of headaches are multifaceted and can be attributed to a combination of factors, including dehydration, which affects the brain's ability to function properly, with studies indicating that up to 90% of headaches are linked to dehydration.

Tension and stress are also significant contributors, with research suggesting that 80-90% of headaches are caused by muscle tension and stress. Other common causes include overuse or misalignment of the neck muscles, known as cervical spine abnormalities, which can lead to cervicogenic headaches. Hormonal fluctuations, such as those experienced during menstruation or menopause, can also trigger headaches in some individuals.

Furthermore, underlying medical conditions like sinus infections, migraines, and temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders can also lead to headaches.

  • Tension: Tension headaches are caused by muscle contractions in the neck and scalp, often resulting from stress, anxiety, or poor posture.
  • Migraine: Migraines are a neurological disorder that causes headaches due to abnormal brain activity, leading to blood vessel dilation and pain in the head and neck.
  • Sinus: Sinus headaches are caused by inflammation and congestion in the sinuses, often due to allergies, colds, or sinus infections.
  • Dehydration: Dehydration headaches are caused by a lack of fluids in the body, leading to contraction of blood vessels and increased pain sensitivity.
  • Eye Strain: Eye strain headaches are caused by prolonged use of computers, smartphones, or other digital devices, leading to eye fatigue and muscle strain.
  • Hormonal: Hormonal headaches are caused by fluctuations in hormone levels, particularly estrogen and progesterone, which can affect blood vessels and lead to headaches.
  • Food: Food headaches are caused by sensitivities or intolerances to certain foods, such as gluten, sugar, or caffeine, which can trigger inflammation and pain in the head.
  • Sleep: Sleep headaches are caused by lack of sleep, sleep disorders, or poor sleep quality, leading to fatigue, stress, and increased pain sensitivity.
  • Medication: Medication headaches are caused by side effects of certain medications, such as pain relievers, which can lead to rebound headaches and increased pain sensitivity.
  • Injury: Injury headaches are caused by direct trauma to the head or neck, leading to inflammation, pain, and muscle strain.
  • Infection: Infection headaches are caused by bacterial or viral infections, such as meningitis or encephalitis, which can lead to inflammation and pain in the head and neck.
