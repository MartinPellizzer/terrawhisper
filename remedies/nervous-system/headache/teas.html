10 best herbal teas for headaches

Herbal teas for headaches are caffeine-free, warm beverages made from various dried herbs, flowers, and spices.

They are a natural and effective way to alleviate headaches, as they contain soothing and anti-inflammatory properties. Some popular herbal teas for headaches include chamomile, ginger, feverfew, and willow bark. Chamomile tea, for instance, helps relax muscles and reduce stress, which can trigger headaches.

Ginger tea, on the other hand, has potent anti-inflammatory properties that can help ease headache pain. By incorporating these herbal teas into their daily routine, individuals can find relief from headaches and improve their overall quality of life. For example, a stressed-out student who frequently suffers from tension headaches can drink chamomile tea before bed to relax and reduce the likelihood of headaches.

Similarly, a construction worker who experiences migraines due to inflammation can consume ginger tea during breaks to alleviate pain and continue with their workday comfortably.

The following article describes in detail the most important teas for headaches, including medicinal properties, parts of herbs to use, and recipes for preparations.

1. Black cohosh tea

Black cohosh tea helps with headaches because it contains phytoestrogens, which are plant-based compounds that can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

Fluctuations in estrogen levels are known to trigger headaches and migraines in some individuals, particularly women. By regulating estrogen levels, black cohosh tea can help alleviate headache symptoms. Additionally, black cohosh has anti-inflammatory properties, which can further reduce headache pain and inflammation in the blood vessels surrounding the brain.

Drinking black cohosh tea may provide natural relief for those who experience headaches related to hormonal changes or inflammation.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in black cohosh tea that aid with headaches.

  • Anti-inflammatory: Black cohosh tea contains salicylic acid, which helps reduce inflammation, a common cause of headaches.
  • Analgesic: The isoferulic acid and triterpene glycosides in black cohosh have pain-relieving properties that can help alleviate headache pain.
  • Antispasmodic: Black cohosh tea can help relax muscles and alleviate tension, which may contribute to the onset of headaches.
  • Nervine: The nervine properties of black cohosh tea help to calm the nervous system, reducing stress and anxiety that can trigger headaches.
  • Vasodilatory: Black cohosh tea contains compounds that can help dilate blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing the risk of headaches caused by vasoconstriction.

The list below shows the primary parts of black cohosh tea used to make teas for headaches.

  • Roots: Black cohosh tea made from the roots is commonly used to treat headaches due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
  • Rhizomes: The rhizomes of black cohosh contain active compounds that help alleviate headaches, especially those related to menstrual cycles and hormonal imbalances.
  • Leaves: The leaves of black cohosh are used less frequently than the roots and rhizomes, but they can still provide some relief for headaches due to their soothing and calming effects.
  • Flowers: Although not as potent as the roots or rhizomes, black cohosh flowers can be used in tea to help reduce headache symptoms, thanks to their mild anti-inflammatory properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic black cohosh tea for headaches.

  1. Measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried black cohosh root.
  2. Place the herb in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  3. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the black cohosh.
  4. Allow the tea to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Remove the infuser or strain the tea before drinking.

  • Sedative: The tea contains compounds like tetrahydropalmatine and cnidilide, which have calming and relaxing effects, aiding in headache relief by reducing tension and anxiety.
  • Antioxidant: Yanhusuo tea is rich in antioxidants like ferulic acid and ligustilide, which protect cells from damage, supporting overall brain health and potentially reducing the frequency and severity of headaches.

The list below shows the primary parts of yanhusuo tea used to make teas for headaches.

  • Roots: Yanhusuo tea's roots contain alkaloids that help reduce the frequency and severity of headaches.

  • Leaves: Yanhusuo tea's leaves contain compounds that help relax blood vessels, reducing pressure and pain in the head.
  • Flowers: Yanhusuo tea's flowers have calming effects that can help reduce tension and stress-related headaches.
  • Seeds: Yanhusuo tea's seeds contain essential oils that can help soothe and relax the mind, reducing headache symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yanhusuo tea for headaches.

  1. Measure out 2-3 grams of dried Yanhusuo root and rinse it under cold water.
  2. Place the rinsed Yanhusuo root in a pot and add 300 milliliters of water.
  3. Bring the water to a boil and then reduce heat to a simmer, allowing the tea to steep for 30 minutes.

  • Vasoactive properties: Feverfew tea helps regulate the dilation and constriction of blood vessels, which is crucial for headache relief, and can be attributed to parthenolide and other sesquiterpene lactones.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the feverfew plant are commonly used in medicinal tea due to their ability to help relieve headaches and migraines.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic feverfew tea for headaches.
