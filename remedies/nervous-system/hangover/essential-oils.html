Peppermint essential oils helps with hangover because its invigorating and refreshing properties can help alleviate the symptoms of a hangover, such as nausea and headaches.

The menthol in peppermint oil can also help to ease stomach cramps and discomfort caused by excessive drinking. Additionally, peppermint's calming effects can soothe an irritated nervous system, reducing feelings of anxiety and fatigue that often accompany a hangover.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in peppermint essential oils that aid with hangover.

  • Digestive aid: Peppermint essential oil can help alleviate nausea, stomach cramps, and diarrhea caused by excessive drinking due to its carminative properties, which ease digestive discomfort.
  • Antioxidant: Peppermint essential oil's antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals that can cause oxidative stress and damage to cells during hangover, reducing inflammation and promoting recovery.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Peppermint essential oil contains compounds like menthol, which have anti-inflammatory effects, helping to reduce swelling in the brain, liver, and other organs caused by excessive alcohol consumption, and alleviating headache and fatigue symptoms.

The list below shows the primary parts of peppermint used to make essential oils for hangover.

  • Roots: The roots are rich in menthol, which helps to alleviate nausea and vomiting associated with hangovers.
  • Leaves: The leaves contain high amounts of menthone, a natural analgesic that can help relieve headaches and migraines caused by excessive drinking.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic peppermint for hangover.

  1. Harvest fresh peppermint leaves in early morning when dew still present on leaves for optimal oil content.
  2. Rinse leaves gently with distilled water to remove dirt and debris then pat dry with clean cloth.
  3. Steam distill 200g of dried peppermint leaves with 400ml of distilled water for 45 minutes at 100°c.
  4. Collect and filter the resulting essential oil through activated charcoal to remove impurities and contaminants.
  5. Store the final product in dark glass bottle with tight-fitting lid at room temperature away from direct sunlight.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginger for hangover.

  2. Dry the ginger in a food dehydrator at 150°f for 6 hours or until completely dry and brittle.
  3. Grind 1 cup of dried ginger into a fine powder using a coffee grinder or spice mill.
  4. Steam distill the powdered ginger in a double boiler with 1 cup of water for 30 minutes to extract essential oils.
  5. Filter the resulting oil mixture through a cheesecloth or paper filter and store it in a dark glass bottle for up to 6 months.
