1. Hypericum perforatum

St John's Wort tinctures helps with depression because it contains hyperforin, a unique compound that has been shown to interact with neurotransmitters in the brain, such as serotonin and dopamine.

This interaction can help alleviate symptoms of depression by increasing the levels of these neurotransmitters, which are often imbalanced in people with depression.

Additionally, St John's Wort has a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing anxiety and stress, which are common companions to depression.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in st john's wort tinctures that aid with depression.

  • Hyperforin: This compound has been shown to inhibit the reabsorption of serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine in the brain, thereby increasing their availability for neurotransmission, which can help alleviate symptoms of depression.
  • Adhyperforin: Similar to hyperforin, adhyperforin also inhibits the reabsorption of neurotransmitters, but is more specific to serotonin and has been shown to have a positive effect on mood regulation.
  • Flavonoids: The flavonoids present in St John's Wort tinctures have been found to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help protect against oxidative stress and inflammation that can contribute to depression.

The list below shows the primary parts of st john's wort used to make tinctures for depression.

  • Roots: The roots of St John's Wort contain a high concentration of hypericin, a compound responsible for the plant's antidepressant properties.
  • Leaves: Leaves of St John's Wort have been traditionally used to treat mild depressive symptoms, with their flavonoids and phenolic acids contributing to their therapeutic effects.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic st john's wort for depression.

  1. Harvest hypericum perforatum flowers and leaves fresh or dried for optimal potency.
  2. Combine one part dried hypericum perforatum plant material with two parts high-proof vodka in a clean glass jar.
  3. Allow the mixture to steep in a cool dark place for six weeks shaking the jar daily for thorough extraction.
  4. Strain the liquid through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another clean glass jar discarding the solids and retaining the tincture.
  5. Transfer the tincture to amber glass bottles and store it in a cool dark place for up to two years or refrigerate for extended shelf life.
