1. Hypericum perforatum

St John's Wort tinctures helps with depression because it contains hyperforin, a unique compound that has been shown to interact with neurotransmitters in the brain, such as serotonin and dopamine.

This interaction can help alleviate symptoms of depression by increasing the levels of these neurotransmitters, which are often imbalanced in people with depression.

Additionally, St John's Wort has a calming effect on the nervous system, reducing anxiety and stress, which are common companions to depression.

Medicinal Constituents

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Hypericum perforatum tinctures that help with depression.

  • Hyperforin: This phenolic compound has shown to act as a serotonin reuptake inhibitor, which can help increase serotonin levels in the brain, thereby alleviating symptoms of depression.
  • N-feruloyltyramine: This alkaloid has demonstrated antidepressant activity by increasing the release of neurotransmitters such as serotonin and dopamine, and by inhibiting the reuptake of these neurotransmitters.
  • Hypericin: This naphthodianthrone compound has been found to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can contribute to its antidepressant effects by protecting neurons from oxidative stress and promoting neuronal health.

Plant's Parts

The list below shows the primary parts of st john's wort used to make tinctures for depression.

  • Leaves: The leaves contain hypericin and hyperforin, which are the primary active compounds responsible for the antidepressant effects of the plant.
  • Stems: The stems also contain hypericin and hyperforin, making them a suitable source for extracting these compounds for tincture production.
  • Flowers: The flowers are a rich source of flavonoids, including quercetin and rutin, which contribute to their antidepressant and mood-enhancing properties.

Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic st john's wort for depression.

  1. Harvest hypericum perforatum leaves and flowers in the morning after the dew has evaporated for fresh material.
  2. Chop the fresh plant material into small pieces weighing approximately one part plant material to two parts solvent.
  3. Steep the chopped plant material in a clean glass jar with a solvent such as 70 ethanol by volume for 2-4 weeks.
  4. Strain the liquid mixture through a cheesecloth or coffee filter to separate the plant material from the solvent.
  5. Store the resulting tincture in dark glass bottles and keep it out of direct sunlight in a cool place for up to 2 years.
