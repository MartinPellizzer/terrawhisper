1. Lavandula angustifolia

English lavender essential oils helps with depression because it has a calming and soothing effect on the mind and body.

The sweet, floral scent of lavender oil can slow down breathing and heart rate, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety levels. This, in turn, can help alleviate symptoms of depression such as irritability, mood swings, and insomnia.

Additionally, the aromatic properties of lavender oil can also uplift and calm the emotions, promoting a sense of serenity and tranquility that is essential for managing depressive episodes.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in english lavender essential oils that aid with depression.

  • Anxiolytic and sedative properties: Lavender oil's anxiolytic and sedative properties help to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, which can alleviate symptoms of depression by calming the mind and body.
  • Mood-boosting aromachemicals: The aroma of lavender oil contains mood-boosting compounds such as linalool and linalyl acetate, which have been shown to increase serotonin levels in the brain, helping to improve mood and reduce symptoms of depression.
  • Antidepressant activity: Lavender oil has been found to have antidepressant activity due to its ability to inhibit the reuptake of neurotransmitters such as serotonin and dopamine, which can help regulate mood and alleviate symptoms of depression.

The list below shows the primary parts of english lavender essential oil used to make essential oils for depression.

  • Roots: The roots of English lavender are rich in sesquiterpenes, which have been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system and can help reduce symptoms of anxiety and depression.
  • Flowers: Lavender flowers contain linalool, a natural mood stabilizer that has been used for centuries to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic english lavender essential oil for depression.

  1. Harvest 30 grams of fresh or dried lavandula angustifolia flowers in the morning when oil content is highest.
  2. Dry the harvested flowers for 10-14 days in a well-ventilated area protected from direct sunlight and moisture.
  3. Combine the dried lavender flowers with 500 ml of carrier oil such as jojoba or sweet almond oil at a ratio of 1:5.
  4. Steep the mixture in a glass container at room temperature for 14 days to allow for slow diffusion and extraction of essential oils.
  5. Strain the mixture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into another glass container discarding the solids and store the extracted essential oil.
