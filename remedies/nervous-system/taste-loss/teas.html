7 Herbal Teas For Taste Loss

If you're experiencing taste loss, also known as ageusia, it can be really frustrating.

Plus, many of these teas are naturally caffeine-free and can be enjoyed hot or cold, making them a great addition to your daily routine.

So, if you're experiencing taste loss, give these herbal teas a try and see if they can help you regain your sense of taste and improve your overall well-being.

1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contains licorice root, which is rich in a compound called glycyrrhizin.

This substance has anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe the mucous membranes in the mouth and throat, reducing inflammation and irritation that can cause taste loss. Glycyrrhizin also has a sweet, pleasant taste that can stimulate the taste buds, making it easier to experience flavors again. Additionally, licorice root has antioxidant properties that can help protect the taste buds from damage and promote their recovery. If you're experiencing taste loss, try drinking Glycyrrhiza glabra teas 2-3 times a day, warm or at room temperature. You can also add honey or sugar to taste, but be sure to follow the recommended dose to avoid excessive consumption of glycyrrhizin, which can cause side effects like high blood pressure and low potassium levels.

Start with a small amount and gradually increase the dose as needed. It's also essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using licorice root tea, especially if you have underlying medical conditions or take medications that may interact with glycyrrhizin. As you drink Glycyrrhiza glabra teas regularly, you may start to notice a gradual improvement in your taste sensation. Your sense of taste may become more sensitive, and you may be able to appreciate flavors and aromas more intensely. You may also notice a decrease in the sensation of bitterness or metallic tastes, and an increase in the sweetness and pleasantness of foods.

However, it's essential to be patient, as the recovery of taste sensation can take time, and it may take several weeks or even months to experience significant improvements.

2. Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains compounds that may help with taste loss due to their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The tea is rich in rosmarinic acid, a natural compound that has been shown to reduce inflammation in the mouth and throat, which can cause taste loss. Other constituents like camphor and bornel may also contribute to its potential benefits. When experiencing taste loss, you can try drinking Rosmarinus officinalis tea, ideally 2-3 times a day, for 5-7 days.

It is recommended to use high-quality tea leaves and to follow the instructions for brewing. As you consume the tea, you may start to notice an improvement in your sense of taste, and your appetite may also increase. Some people have reported a decrease in bad breath and a reduction in the burning sensation in their mouth and throat.

However, it's essential to note that the effectiveness of Rosmarinus officinalis tea for taste loss may vary from person to person, and individual results may differ.

3. Curcuma longa

Curcuma longa teas contains a compound called curcumin, which is responsible for its potential benefits in treating taste loss.

Curcumin has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help reduce inflammation in the taste buds, restoring their normal functioning. It also has neuroprotective properties that may help protect the taste nerves from damage. If you're experiencing taste loss, you can try taking Curcuma longa teas by steeping the roots or rhizomes in hot water. Start with a small dose of 1/2 teaspoon per cup of water and adjust to taste.

You can also add honey or lemon to make the tea more palatable. It's recommended to drink 2-3 cups per day for best results. As you take Curcuma longa teas, you may start to notice an improvement in your taste sensation within a few days to a week. Your taste buds may become more sensitive, allowing you to enjoy your favorite foods and flavors again. Some people may also experience a reduction in bad breath, a common symptom of taste loss.

However, it's essential to note that the effectiveness of Curcuma longa teas may vary depending on the individual and the underlying cause of taste loss.

4. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which are responsible for its potential benefits in addressing taste loss.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory properties that may help alleviate the pain and discomfort associated with taste loss, often caused by conditions such as chemotherapy, radiation therapy, or infections. The anti-inflammatory effects of ginger can also reduce swelling in the mouth and throat, allowing for the restoration of normal taste. To use Zingiber officinale teas for taste loss, start by preparing a warm tea by steeping 1/2 to 1 teaspoon of dried ginger in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Strain the liquid and drink it slowly. You can also add honey or lemon to taste, but be cautious not to irritate your mouth further. If you experience a reduction in taste sensitivity and an improvement in appetite after consuming the tea, it may indicate that the anti-inflammatory effects are working. Additionally, you may notice a decrease in the frequency and severity of nausea and vomiting associated with taste loss.

However, if your taste loss persists or worsens, consult a healthcare professional for further guidance and support.

5. Cinnamomum verum

Cinnamomum verum teas contain a compound called cinnamaldehyde, which is responsible for their distinctive flavor and aroma.

Cinnamomum verum teas also contain antioxidants, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can help to protect the taste buds and surrounding tissues from damage. To try Cinnamomum verum teas for taste loss, steep a teaspoon of the dried leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day.

You can also add honey or lemon to taste. If you have underlying medical conditions or take medications, consult with your doctor before trying Cinnamomum verum teas. Taking Cinnamomum verum teas may help to restore your sense of taste by reducing inflammation and protecting the taste buds. You may notice an improvement in your ability to taste sweet, sour, salty, and bitter flavors within a few days to a week of regular consumption. However, if your taste loss is caused by a underlying medical condition, such as a neurological disorder or a vitamin deficiency, Cinnamomum verum teas may not be effective in restoring your sense of taste.

In some cases, you may need to seek medical attention to determine the underlying cause of your taste loss and receive proper treatment.
