1. Valeriana officinalis

Valerian essential oils helps with fatigue because it possesses a unique ability to promote relaxation, calmness, and reduce stress levels.

The oil's sedative properties help to slow down an overactive mind, allowing the body to rest and recharge. Additionally, valerian essential oil has been shown to increase serotonin production in the brain, which can help to regulate mood and reduce feelings of exhaustion.

By using valerian essential oils, individuals can experience a natural energy boost that leaves them feeling revitalized and refreshed.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in valerian essential oils that aid with fatigue.

  • Adaptogenic properties: Valerian essential oil helps to adapt to stress and promotes relaxation, reducing feelings of exhaustion and increasing energy levels by calming the nervous system.
  • Antioxidant activity: Valerian essential oil contains antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to fatigue and improve overall energy production in the body.
  • Sedative properties: Valerian essential oil has a sedating effect on the brain, promoting deep relaxation and reducing mental fatigue by slowing down racing thoughts and promoting a restful state.

The list below shows the primary parts of valerian essential oil used to make essential oils for fatigue.

  • Roots: Valerian roots are rich in sesquiterpenes, which contribute to their sedative and relaxing properties, making them effective for relieving fatigue.
  • Rhizomes: The rhizome, or underground stem, of the valerian plant contains a high concentration of essential oils that promote relaxation and reduce feelings of fatigue.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic valerian essential oil for fatigue.

  1. Harvest 2 cups of fresh valeriana officinalis roots in the morning when dew is still present on leaves.
  2. Wash the harvested roots thoroughly with cold running water and pat dry with a clean cloth.
  3. Chop the cleaned roots into small pieces and then transfer them to a stainless steel steam distillation apparatus.
  4. Steam distill the chopped valeriana officinalis roots for 2 hours and collect the obtained essential oil in a glass vial.
  5. Filter the collected essential oil through a coffee filter paper to remove any impurities or sediment.
