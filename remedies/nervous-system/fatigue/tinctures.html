1. Panax ginseng

Ginseng tinctures helps with fatigue because it contains adaptogenic compounds that help to regulate the body's energy levels.

The active ingredients in ginseng, such as ginsenosides and saponins, work to improve mental clarity and focus, reducing feelings of exhaustion and lethargy. Additionally, ginseng has been shown to increase oxygenation of the body's cells, allowing for more efficient energy production and reduced fatigue.

By promoting overall physical and mental well-being, ginseng tinctures can help individuals overcome persistent feelings of tiredness and regain their natural vitality.

Medicinal Constituents

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Panax ginseng tinctures that help with fatigue.

  • Ginsenosides: These triterpene saponins help alleviate fatigue by enhancing mitochondrial function and improving energy production in cells, thereby increasing physical endurance and reducing mental exhaustion.
  • Glycosides: Ginseng glycosides, particularly ginsenoside Rb1, have been shown to reduce fatigue by modulating the body's response to stress and improving the balance of neurotransmitters in the brain, leading to improved mood and reduced fatigue.
  • Eleutherosides: These triterpene saponins, which are also found in Panax ginseng, have been demonstrated to help combat fatigue by stimulating the release of certain neurotransmitters, such as dopamine and serotonin, and by increasing the body's adaptive response to stress.

Plant's Parts

The list below shows the primary parts of ginseng used to make tinctures for fatigue.

  • Roots: They are the primary part used due to their high concentration of ginsenosides, which are believed to have adaptogenic and anti-fatigue properties.
  • Stems: The stems are used as they contain ginsenosides and other bioactive compounds that help in reducing fatigue and improving mental clarity.
  • Leaves: The leaves are also utilized due to their ability to enhance cognitive function and reduce fatigue, although their ginsenoside content is lower compared to roots and stems.

Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginseng for fatigue.

  1. Harvest the panax ginseng roots in the fall season when their color has changed to a lighter shade.
  2. Clean and dry the panax ginseng roots thoroughly in a warm place for at least seven days.
  3. Chop the dried panax ginseng roots into small pieces using a sharp knife and weighing about 250 grams.
  4. Steep the chopped panax ginseng roots in 1 liter of 80% ethanol for 2 weeks in a cool dark place.
  5. Strain the liquid mixture and discard the solids then filter it through a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle.
