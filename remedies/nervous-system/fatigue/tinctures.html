1. Panax ginseng

Ginseng tinctures helps with fatigue because it contains adaptogenic compounds that help to regulate the body's energy levels.

The active ingredients in ginseng, such as ginsenosides and saponins, work to improve mental clarity and focus, reducing feelings of exhaustion and lethargy. Additionally, ginseng has been shown to increase oxygenation of the body's cells, allowing for more efficient energy production and reduced fatigue.

By promoting overall physical and mental well-being, ginseng tinctures can help individuals overcome persistent feelings of tiredness and regain their natural vitality.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in ginseng tinctures that aid with fatigue.

  • Adaptogenic properties: Ginseng tincture helps to adapt the body's response to stress, which can contribute to fatigue, by promoting resilience and reducing feelings of exhaustion.
  • Antioxidant properties: Ginseng tincture contains antioxidants that help protect cells from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to chronic fatigue and weakness.
  • Cognitive-enhancing properties: Ginseng tincture has been shown to improve cognitive function, including memory, attention, and mental clarity, which can help alleviate feelings of fatigue and mental fogginess.

The list below shows the primary parts of ginseng tincture used to make tinctures for fatigue.

  • Roots: Rich in bioactive compounds, ginseng roots are considered the most valuable part of the plant for their adaptogenic properties, which help alleviate fatigue and improve energy levels.
  • Rhyzomes: The fleshy underground stems of ginseng, known as rhyzomes, contain high concentrations of ginsenosides, which have been shown to enhance physical performance and reduce mental fatigue.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic ginseng tincture for fatigue.

  1. Harvest dried panax ginseng roots of high quality in quantities sufficient for desired dosage.
  2. Chop the dried roots into smaller pieces and combine with 80 proof vodka or neutral spirits.
  3. Transfer the root mixture to a clean glass jar leaving one inch headspace and seal tightly.
  4. Allow the mixture to macerate in a cool dark place for six weeks or more shaking daily.
  5. Strain the tincture through cheesecloth or a coffee filter into a clean glass bottle discarding solids.
