What is numbness in hands and how it affects your life?

Numbness in hands refers to a loss of sensation or feeling in the hands, typically affecting one or both hands.

This can manifest as a tingling, pins-and-needles sensation, or a complete lack of sensation. When hands are numb, it can be challenging to perform everyday activities, making life more difficult and inconvenient. For instance, it may be hard to grasp objects, cook meals, or even button shirts, which can be frustrating and embarrassing.

Additionally, numbness in hands can affect work and daily routine, making it difficult to type on a keyboard, use a mouse, or even drive a car. It can also impact hobbies and leisure activities, such as playing musical instruments, painting, or gardening, which can be a significant source of enjoyment and relaxation. Furthermore, numbness in hands can lead to decreased productivity, strained relationships, and feelings of anxiety and depression.

Overall, numbness in hands can significantly disrupt daily life, making even simple tasks a struggle.
