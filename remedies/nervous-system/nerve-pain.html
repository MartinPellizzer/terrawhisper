Nerve Pain: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Nerve pain, also known as neuropathic pain, is a condition characterized by intense, burning, or stabbing sensations in the nerves, often resulting from damage or compression.

Common causes include diabetes, shingles, injuries, and infections. If left untreated, nerve pain can lead to fatigue, sleep disturbances, and decreased quality of life.

Fortunately, herbal remedies such as capsaicin, turmeric, and ashwagandha can help alleviate symptoms. To relieve nerve pain, consider topical creams, ointments, or teas made from these herbs.

However, consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as some may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of nerve pain, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of nerve pain?

The main causes of nerve pain are primarily attributed to damage or compression of the nerves, which can be a result of various underlying conditions such as diabetes mellitus, leading to diabetic neuropathy, a common cause of nerve pain affecting approximately 60-70% of people with the condition.

Additionally, other medical conditions including multiple sclerosis, peripheral artery disease, and certain types of cancer, such as pancreatic cancer and lung cancer, can also cause nerve pain. Trauma to the nerves, including injuries and surgical procedures, can also lead to nerve pain, as can infections like shingles, a viral infection caused by the reactivation of the varicella-zoster virus. Furthermore, certain medications, such as certain antidepressants and blood pressure medications, can also cause nerve pain as a side effect.

Genetic conditions, such as Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease, can also contribute to nerve pain.

  • Diabetes: Diabetes is a leading cause of nerve pain due to high blood sugar levels damaging the nerve fibers over time, causing numbness, tingling, and pain in the hands and feet.
  • Herniated Disc: A herniated disc can compress or pinch nearby nerves, causing nerve pain in the back, neck, and legs due to the pressure on the nerve roots.
  • Multiple Sclerosis: Multiple sclerosis is an autoimmune disease that damages the protective covering of nerve fibers, leading to nerve pain, numbness, and weakness in various parts of the body.
  • Shingles: Shingles is a viral infection that causes nerve pain due to the reactivation of the varicella-zoster virus, which can lead to a painful rash and nerve inflammation.
  • Carpal Tunnel Syndrome: Carpal tunnel syndrome is a condition that causes nerve pain in the wrist and hand due to the compression of the median nerve, which can lead to numbness, tingling, and weakness in the hand.
  • Sciatica: Sciatica is a condition that causes nerve pain in the lower back, buttocks, and legs due to the compression of the sciatic nerve, which can lead to numbness, tingling, and weakness in the leg.
  • Postherpetic Neuralgia: Postherpetic neuralgia is a condition that causes nerve pain due to the nerve damage caused by shingles, which can lead to chronic pain in the affected area.
  • Lupus: Lupus is an autoimmune disease that can cause nerve pain due to the inflammation of the nerve tissues, which can lead to numbness, tingling, and weakness in various parts of the body.
  • Tumors: Tumors, whether benign or malignant, can compress or invade nearby nerves, causing nerve pain due to the pressure on the nerve roots or the invasion of the tumor into the nerve tissue.
  • Injuries: Injuries, such as fractures or sprains, can cause nerve pain due to the compression or stretching of the nerve fibers, which can lead to numbness, tingling, and weakness in the affected area.

Ginkgo biloba, Curcuma longa, and Hypericum perforatum possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the nervous system, alleviating nerve pain.

Additionally, Valeriana officinalis, Passiflora incarnata, Withania somnifera, and Cannabis sativa exert their analgesic effects through mechanisms involving GABA, serotonin, and opioids, further contributing to the relief of nerve pain.
