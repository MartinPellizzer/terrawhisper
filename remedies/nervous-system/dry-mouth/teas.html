10 best herbal teas for dry mouth

Chamomile, sage, and licorice root teas are popular choices for this purpose. Licorice root tea can increase saliva flow, reducing dry mouth symptoms.

By drinking these herbal teas, individuals can improve their quality of life by reducing discomfort, preventing oral infections, and maintaining overall oral health.

1. Sage tea

Sage tea helps with dry mouth because it contains antibacterial and astringent properties that can increase saliva production and reduce oral bacteria.

The active compounds in sage, such as rosmarinic acid and carnosic acid, have anti-inflammatory effects that can soothe irritated mucous membranes in the mouth and throat. Additionally, sage tea can help to moisturize and lubricate dry tissues, providing temporary relief from symptoms of dry mouth.

Its natural properties make it a safe and effective alternative to commercial mouthwashes and artificial saliva products.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in sage tea that aid with dry mouth.

  • Antihydrotic property: Sage tea helps reduce saliva production, which can alleviate the symptoms of dry mouth. This is due to the presence of tannins and volatile oils like thujone, which have astringent properties.
  • Anti-inflammatory property: Sage tea can help reduce inflammation in the mouth and throat, providing relief from dry mouth-related discomfort. This is attributed to the presence of rosmarinic acid and various flavonoids.
  • Antimicrobial property: Sage tea inhibits the growth of harmful bacteria and fungi in the mouth, promoting a healthy oral environment. This is due to the presence of camphor, cineole, and other volatile oils.
  • Demulcent property: Sage tea forms a protective film over the mucous membranes in the mouth, providing temporary relief from dry mouth. This is due to the presence of mucilaginous compounds in sage.
  • Stimulant property: Sage tea can stimulate the production of saliva, which can help alleviate dry mouth symptoms. This is due to the presence of alpha-thujone and other volatile oils, which can stimulate the salivary glands.

The list below shows the primary parts of sage tea used to make teas for dry mouth.

  • Leaves: Rich in antioxidants and tannins, sage leaves help stimulate saliva production, alleviating dry mouth symptoms.
  • Flowers: High in essential oils, sage flowers can help soothe irritation and promote saliva flow, providing relief for dry mouth.
  • Stems: Containing beneficial compounds, sage stems can help reduce inflammation and increase saliva secretion, making them useful for dry mouth remedies.
  • Roots: Packed with nutrients and active ingredients, sage roots can stimulate saliva production and provide anti-inflammatory benefits, helping to combat dry mouth.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic sage tea for dry mouth.

  1. Boil 8 ounces of water to a rolling boil for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of dried sage leaves to a tea infuser or teapot.
  3. Pour the boiling water over the sage leaves and let them steep for 5-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the sage leaves and optionally add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the sage tea while warm, up to three times a day, to alleviate dry mouth symptoms.
