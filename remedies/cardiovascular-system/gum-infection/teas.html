7 Herbal Teas For Gum Infection

Herbal teas have been used for centuries to alleviate gum infections and other oral health issues.

These teas work by reducing inflammation, fighting bacteria, and promoting healing in the affected area. For instance, Eucalyptus globulus, a popular herbal remedy, contains compounds that have natural antibacterial properties, which help to combat the underlying infection. Echinacea purpurea, on the other hand, is known for its ability to boost the immune system, making it easier for your body to fight off the infection. Salvia officinalis, or sage, contains compounds that have anti-inflammatory properties, which help to reduce swelling and pain in the gums. Drinking these herbal teas can have a significant impact on your life.

By alleviating gum infections, you can prevent more severe issues, such as tooth loss and bad breath. This can also boost your confidence and overall oral health, making it easier to eat and speak without discomfort. Additionally, many of these herbs are rich in antioxidants, which can help to protect against other health issues, such as heart disease and cancer. By incorporating herbal teas into your daily routine, you can take a proactive approach to maintaining your oral health and overall well-being. When using these herbal teas, it's essential to note that they may not be as effective as prescription medications, but they can be a helpful complement to traditional treatment.

To get the most out of these teas, make sure to drink them regularly, ideally 2-3 times a day, and combine them with good oral hygiene practices, such as brushing and flossing your teeth regularly.

1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains compounds like eucalyptol, which has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that help reduce swelling and fight bacteria that cause gum infections.

The tea also contains flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties that help protect the gums from damage. When used as a mouthwash or gargle, Eucalyptus globulus tea can help loosen and remove plaque, a sticky film that forms on teeth and can lead to gum infections. To use Eucalyptus globulus tea for gum infection, make a tea by steeping one teaspoon of dried leaves in a cup of hot water for 5-7 minutes, then let it cool.

Swish the tea around in your mouth for 30 seconds to 1 minute, then spit it out. Repeat this process 2-3 times a day. After using Eucalyptus globulus tea, you may notice a reduction in gum inflammation and pain, and your gums may look healthier.

However, if your gum infection is severe, you should still consult a dentist for proper treatment.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and polyphenols, which have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help combat gum infection.

The flavonoids and phenolic acids in Echinacea purpurea may reduce inflammation and kill bacteria that cause gum disease. If you're experiencing a gum infection, try drinking Echinacea purpurea tea 2-3 times a day, ideally after meals, to help soothe and calm the infected area.

You can also rinse your mouth with the tea or apply it as a mouthwash to get the benefits directly on the affected area. However, it's essential to note that if you have a severe gum infection, you should consult your dentist or healthcare provider for proper treatment.

Taking Echinacea purpurea tea may lead to reduced swelling and pain, and possibly even a decrease in the severity of the infection, allowing the gum tissue to heal faster.

3. Salvia officinalis

Salvia officinalis teas contains compounds like tannins, flavonoids, and rosmarinic acid that help combat gum infections.

These compounds have antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can reduce swelling and fight off the bacteria causing the infection. The tea also contains antioxidants that can help repair damaged gum tissue and promote healing. To use Salvia officinalis teas for gum infection, you can steep a teaspoon of dried sage leaves in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes.

Then, strain the tea and let it cool before rinsing your mouth with it. You can also use a mouthwash made from the tea to help kill bacteria and reduce inflammation. When you start using Salvia officinalis teas for gum infection, you may notice a reduction in swelling and pain within a few days. Your gums may start to heal, and the infection may clear up within 1-2 weeks.

However, it's essential to continue using the tea as directed and to also practice good oral hygiene, such as brushing and flossing regularly, to prevent the infection from coming back.
