1. Garlic tea

Garlic tea helps with hypertension because it contains allicin, a compound that has been shown to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow.

This action helps to reduce high blood pressure, making garlic tea a natural and beneficial supplement for those looking to manage their hypertension. Additionally, garlic has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can further support cardiovascular health and contribute to overall well-being.

By incorporating garlic tea into their daily routine, individuals with hypertension may be able to reduce their dependence on medication and enjoy a healthier, more balanced lifestyle.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in garlic tea that aid with hypertension.

  • Vasodilation: Garlic tea contains allicin, a compound that helps dilate blood vessels, reducing the pressure on artery walls and improving blood flow, which is crucial for managing hypertension.
  • Antiplatelet aggregation: Allicin in garlic tea also inhibits platelet aggregation, preventing the formation of blood clots that can obstruct blood flow and increase blood pressure, making it beneficial for hypertension management.
  • Antioxidant properties: Garlic tea contains polyphenols and flavonoids that act as antioxidants, neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress, which contributes to endothelial dysfunction and hypertension development.
  • Hypolipidemic effects: Garlic tea has been shown to lower low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol and triglyceride levels, reducing the risk of atherosclerosis and improving overall cardiovascular health, which is essential for controlling hypertension.
  • Diuretic properties: Garlic tea may increase urine production, helping the body eliminate excess sodium and fluids, which can contribute to high blood pressure, making it an important medicinal property for hypertension management.

The list below shows the primary parts of garlic tea used to make teas for hypertension.

  • Leaves: Contain high levels of allicin, which may help relax blood vessels and reduce hypertension.
  • Bulbs: Rich in beneficial compounds like allicin and diallyl trisulfide, which have been shown to lower blood pressure.
  • Stems: May contain useful bioactive compounds, although less potent than bulbs and leaves, contributing to the overall effectiveness of the tea.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic garlic tea for hypertension.

  1. Chop 3 cloves of garlic and let it sit for 10 minutes to activate its medicinal properties.
  2. In a pot, boil 2 cups of water and add the chopped garlic.
  3. Simmer for 15 minutes, then add 1 tablespoon of dried hibiscus flowers and 1 tablespoon of dried lemongrass.
  4. Steep for an additional 5 minutes, then strain the tea to remove solids.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy up to three cups daily for hypertension management.
