Garlic tincture helps with hypertension because it contains allicin, a compound that has been shown to help lower blood pressure.

  • Antiplatelet aggregation: It inhibits the clumping of platelets, preventing the formation of blood clots that can cause blockages in blood vessels, which is important for hypertension management, as it improves blood flow and reduces the risk of heart attack and stroke.

  • Diuretic action: Garlic tincture supports the elimination of excess fluids and sodium from the body, helping to lower blood volume and reduce the strain on the cardiovascular system, which is crucial for managing hypertension.

The list below shows the primary parts of garlic tincture used to make tinctures for hypertension.

  • Bulbs: Garlic bulbs are widely used to make medicinal tinctures for hypertension due to their high concentration of allicin, a compound known for its blood pressure-lowering properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic garlic tincture for hypertension.

  1. Chop 1 cup of fresh garlic cloves and place them in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add 2 cups of a menstruum (50/50 mixture of vodka and distilled water) to the jar, ensuring the garlic is fully submerged.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks, shaking it gently every few days.
  4. After the maceration period, strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out the garlic pulp to extract all liquid.
  5. Store the final garlic tincture in a tightly sealed glass container, away from light and heat, for up to a year.

What ailments similar to hypertension are treated with herbal tinctures?

Ailments similar to hypertension that are treated with herbal tinctures include mild to moderate essential hypertension, prehypertension, and hypertensive emergencies.

Some herbs used for these conditions are hawthorn, garlic, and motherwort. Hawthorn is known to dilate coronary blood vessels and improve cardiac output, while garlic has been found to reduce blood pressure by promoting vasodilation. Motherwort, on the other hand, has a mild sedative effect that can help manage stress-induced hypertension.

However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before starting any herbal treatment.
