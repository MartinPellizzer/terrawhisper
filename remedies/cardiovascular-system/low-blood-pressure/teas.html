1. Astragalus membranaceus teas

Astragalus membranaceus teas, also known as Huangqi in traditional Chinese medicine, have been used for centuries to treat low blood pressure due to their adaptogenic and cardiovascular-protective properties.

The bioactive constituents of Astragalus membranaceus, including flavonoids, saponins, and polysaccharides, help to increase blood flow and improve cardiovascular function, thus helping to alleviate symptoms of low blood pressure.

By enhancing the body's natural response to stress and promoting the production of red blood cells, Astragalus membranaceus teas help to increase blood pressure and improve overall cardiovascular health.

Regular consumption of Astragalus membranaceus teas has been shown to provide benefits in treating low blood pressure, including improved blood circulation, enhanced immune function, and reduced risk of cardiovascular disease.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 2 teaspoons of dried Astragalus membranaceus root.
  2. Boil 1 cup of water in a pot.
  3. Add the 2 teaspoons of dried Astragalus membranaceus root to the boiling water.
  4. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it warm, 2-3 times a day.

Usage:

  1. Drink 1 cup of Astragalus membranaceus tea 2-3 times a day.
  2. Start with 1 teaspoon of dried root per cup of boiling water.
  3. Gradually increase to 2 teaspoons per cup as needed and tolerance.
  4. Monitor blood pressure and adjust dosage accordingly every week.
  5. Consult a healthcare professional for personalized treatment plan.

Astragalus membranaceus teas can cause potential side effects such as dizziness, nausea, and stomach upset when used to treat low blood pressure, due to the increased blood flow and heart rate it stimulates.

To minimize these side effects, it is essential to start with a low dose and gradually increase it, avoid consuming Astragalus membranaceus teas before bedtime, and stay hydrated to prevent dehydration, especially in people with underlying kidney problems.

5. Glycyrrhiza glabra teas

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas, also known as licorice root tea, have been traditionally used to treat low blood pressure due to their ability to increase blood pressure by stimulating the release of aldosterone, a hormone that helps regulate electrolyte balance.

The bioactive constituents, including glycyrrhizin and triterpenoids, help to constrict blood vessels and increase blood pressure by blocking the action of 11β-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase, an enzyme that converts cortisol into its inactive form.

This herbal preparation helps to treat low blood pressure by increasing blood volume and cardiac output, leading to improved circulation and reduced symptoms of dizziness and fainting.

The benefits of using Glycyrrhiza glabra teas to treat low blood pressure include a natural and non-invasive approach, with minimal side effects and no interactions with other medications, making it a popular choice for those seeking alternative treatments.

  3. Boil the 2 cups of water and pour it over the roots in the infuser or strainer. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes.

  5. Drink 1-2 cups of the tea per day, 2-3 times a week, to help manage low blood pressure. Consult a doctor before using as a treatment.

Usage:

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas can be used to treat low blood pressure, but it may cause side effects such as increased blood pressure, electrolyte imbalances, and fluid retention due to its mineralocorticoid properties.
