Palpitation: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Palpitation refers to an irregular or rapid heartbeat, often felt as a pounding or fluttering sensation in the chest.

It can be caused by stress, anxiety, caffeine consumption, certain medications, or underlying medical conditions such as hypertension or hyperthyroidism. If left untreated, palpitations can lead to fatigue, shortness of breath, and decreased cardiac function. To alleviate palpitations, herbal remedies such as passionflower, lavender, and holy basil can be used to calm the nervous system.

However, individuals should consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as they may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of palpitation, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of palpitation?

The main causes of palpitation are typically related to the heart's electrical system and include abnormal heart rhythms, such as atrial fibrillation, supraventricular tachycardia, and ventricular tachycardia.

These irregular heartbeats can be caused by underlying heart conditions like hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, mitral valve prolapse, and coronary artery disease. Other factors that can contribute to palpitations include stress, anxiety, and panic attacks, as well as stimulants like caffeine, nicotine, and certain medications. Additionally, electrolyte imbalances, particularly those involving potassium, magnesium, and calcium, can also trigger palpitations.

Furthermore, thyroid disorders, anemia, and other systemic conditions can also lead to palpitations due to their effects on the heart's electrical system.

  • Anxiety: Anxiety triggers palpitations due to the release of stress hormones, such as adrenaline, which can cause an increase in heart rate and cardiac output.
  • Caffeine: Caffeine consumption can lead to palpitations because it stimulates the heart and nervous system, increasing heart rate and cardiac output.
  • Hypoglycemia: Hypoglycemia, or low blood sugar, can cause palpitations because the brain and heart require a constant supply of glucose to function properly, and a lack of glucose can lead to an irregular heartbeat.
  • Dehydration: Dehydration can cause palpitations because it can lead to a drop in blood volume, causing the heart to pump faster to maintain circulation.
  • Hypertension: Hypertension, or high blood pressure, can cause palpitations due to the heart working harder to pump blood through the body, leading to an irregular heartbeat.
  • Electrolyte imbalance: An electrolyte imbalance, particularly a low level of potassium, can cause palpitations because electrolytes play a crucial role in maintaining a normal heart rhythm.
  • Thyroid disorders: Thyroid disorders, particularly hyperthyroidism, can cause palpitations because an overactive thyroid gland can lead to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output.
  • Medication side effects: Certain medications, such as decongestants, asthma inhalers, and some antibiotics, can cause palpitations as a side effect due to their stimulatory effects on the heart and nervous system.
  • Cardiac arrhythmias: Cardiac arrhythmias, or irregular heart rhythms, can cause palpitations due to abnormal electrical activity in the heart.
  • Heart attack: A heart attack, or myocardial infarction, can cause palpitations due to damage to the heart muscle, leading to irregular heart rhythms.
