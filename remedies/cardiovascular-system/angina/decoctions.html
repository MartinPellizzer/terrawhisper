The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hawthorn for angina.

  1. Rinse dried crataegus monogyna leaves and flowers thoroughly with distilled water.
  2. Combine 10-15 grams of the rinsed crataegus monogyna material with 500 milliliters of distilled water in a glass container.

  4. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into another glass container.
  5. Store the cooled crataegus monogyna decoction in an amber glass bottle at room temperature for up to three days.
