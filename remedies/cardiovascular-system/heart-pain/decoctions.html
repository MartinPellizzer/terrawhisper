1. Crataegus monogyna

Hawthorn decoctions helps with heart pain because they contain flavonoids, oligomeric proanthocyanidins, and other bioactive compounds that have a direct impact on cardiovascular health.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in hawthorn decoctions that aid with heart pain.

  • Cardiac glycosides: Hawthorn decoctions contain cardiac glycosides, which help to regulate heart rhythm and improve cardiovascular function by increasing the strength and efficiency of contractions.
  • Flavonoids: Flavonoids present in hawthorn decoctions have antioxidant properties that help to protect the heart from damage caused by oxidative stress and inflammation, reducing the risk of cardiac arrhythmias and fibrosis.
  • Quercetin: Quercetin, a flavonoid found in hawthorn decoctions, has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects that help to reduce symptoms of angina pectoris (chest pain) by relaxing blood vessels and improving blood flow.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic hawthorn for heart pain.

  1. Measure out 2 grams of dried crataegus monogyna leaves and flowers into a clean glass container.
  2. Combine the measured herbs with 250 milliliters of boiling water in a heat-resistant decanter or teapot.
  3. Allow the mixture to steep for 15-20 minutes, ensuring the herbal ingredients have time to release their active compounds.
  4. Strain the decoction through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into another clean glass container, discarding the solids and reserving the liquid extract.
  5. Store the resulting decoction in the refrigerator at a temperature of 2-8 degrees celsius for up to 24 hours before use.
