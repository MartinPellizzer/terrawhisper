Common hawthorn tea helps with heart pain because it contains antioxidant flavonoids that can help dilate blood vessels, improve blood flow, and protect against damage from oxidative stress.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in common hawthorn tea that aid with heart pain.

  • Vasodilation: Hawthorn tea contains active compounds like rutin and hyperoside, which act as vasodilators, relaxing blood vessels and improving blood flow, thereby alleviating heart pain and lowering blood pressure.
  • Positive inotropic effect: Hawthorn tea enhances the force of heart muscle contractions due to its active constituents, such as flavonoids and procyanidins, which can help improve cardiac function and reduce heart pain.
  • Negative chronotropic effect: Hawthorn tea has a mild slowing effect on the heart rate, attributed to active components like vitexin and rutin, which can help regulate the heartbeat and alleviate heart pain caused by irregular heart rhythms.
  • Anti-inflammatory properties: Hawthorn tea contains various anti-inflammatory compounds, such as quercetin and epicatechin, which can help reduce inflammation in the cardiovascular system, thereby alleviating heart pain and promoting overall heart health.

The list below shows the primary parts of common hawthorn tea used to make teas for heart pain.

  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids, which have been shown to help reduce symptoms of heart failure and high blood pressure.
  • Flowers: Contain high levels of antioxidants, which can help protect the heart from damage and improve overall cardiovascular health.
  • Berries: Packed with anthocyanins, which have been linked to reduced risk of heart disease and improved blood pressure control.
  • Bark: Has been used traditionally to treat heart conditions, as it contains compounds that can help dilate blood vessels and improve blood flow.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic common hawthorn tea for heart pain.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 teaspoon of dried common hawthorn leaves and flowers with 8 ounces of water.
  2. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove the pot from heat and strain the tea into a cup, discarding the solids.
  4. Allow the tea to cool slightly, then drink it while still warm.
  5. Repeat the process up to three times a day, as needed.
