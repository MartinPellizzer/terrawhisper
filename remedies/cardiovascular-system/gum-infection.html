Gum Infection: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

A gum infection, also known as periodontal disease or pyorrhea, is a bacterial infection affecting the gums and supporting tissues surrounding the teeth.

It is often caused by poor oral hygiene, smoking, diabetes, and certain medications. If left untreated, gum infections can lead to tooth loss, bone loss, and systemic health issues like heart disease and respiratory infections.

Herbal remedies like aloe vera, turmeric, and tea tree oil can be used to alleviate gum infection symptoms. Aloe vera gel can be applied topically to soothe inflamed gums, while turmeric's antibacterial properties can help reduce infection. Tea tree oil, rich in antimicrobial properties, can be mixed with water and used as a mouthwash.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as they may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of gum infection, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of gum infection?

The main causes of gum infection are poor oral hygiene, leading to the accumulation of plaque, a sticky film of bacteria, on teeth and gums.

Bacteria in plaque can cause inflammation and infection of the gums, known as gingivitis, if not removed through regular brushing and flossing. Smoking and tobacco use can also increase the risk of gum infection by reducing blood flow to the gums and making them more susceptible to bacterial invasion. Additionally, certain medical conditions, such as diabetes and heart disease, can affect the body's ability to fight off infection, making it more likely for gum infections to occur.

Furthermore, genetics can play a role, with some people being more prone to gum disease due to their individual oral anatomy and bacterial composition.

  • Poor Oral Hygiene: Poor oral hygiene is the most common cause of gum infection due to the buildup of plaque and tartar on teeth, leading to inflammation and infection of the gums.
  • Smoking: Smoking is a significant cause of gum infection because it reduces blood flow to the gums, making them more susceptible to disease and reducing the ability of the immune system to fight off infection.
  • Genetic Predisposition: Genetic predisposition is a common cause of gum infection because some people are more prone to gum disease due to their genetic makeup, which can affect the shape and size of their teeth and gums.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes are a common cause of gum infection because fluctuations in hormone levels during pregnancy, menstruation, and menopause can increase the risk of gum disease.
  • Bacterial Imbalance: Bacterial imbalance is a common cause of gum infection because an overgrowth of certain bacteria in the mouth can lead to the formation of plaque and tartar, causing inflammation and infection of the gums.
  • Medication Use: Medication use is a less common cause of gum infection because certain medications, such as antidepressants and steroids, can increase the risk of gum disease by altering the immune response and increasing the risk of bleeding.
  • Nutritional Deficiencies: Nutritional deficiencies are a less common cause of gum infection because a lack of essential nutrients, such as vitamin C and calcium, can weaken the gums and make them more susceptible to disease.
  • Gastrointestinal Disease: Gastrointestinal disease is a less common cause of gum infection because conditions such as Crohn's disease and celiac disease can increase the risk of gum disease by altering the immune response and increasing the risk of malnutrition.
