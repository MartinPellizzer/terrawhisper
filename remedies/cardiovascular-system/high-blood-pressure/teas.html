10 best herbal teas for high blood pressure

Herbal teas for high blood pressure are caffeine-free beverages made from various plants, flowers, and seeds, which have been used in traditional medicine to help manage hypertension.

These teas often contain compounds that promote vasodilation, improve circulation, and reduce inflammation, all of which contribute to lowering blood pressure. Examples of herbal teas beneficial for high blood pressure include hibiscus, green tea, chamomile, and Hawthorn. By incorporating these teas into a daily routine, individuals can potentially reduce their reliance on medication, lower their risk of heart disease, and enhance their overall quality of life.

For instance, a study published in the Journal of Hypertension found that consuming hibiscus tea for six weeks significantly reduced systolic and diastolic blood pressure in mildly hypertensive adults.

The following article describes in detail the most important teas for high blood pressure, including medicinal properties, parts of herbs to use, and recipes for preparations.

1. Common hawthorn tea

Common hawthorn tea helps with high blood pressure because it contains active compounds such as oligomeric procyanidins and flavonoids, which have been found to relax and dilate blood vessels, thereby improving blood flow and reducing the stress on the heart.

Additionally, hawthorn tea has been shown to increase the strength of the heartbeat, which can help to improve overall cardiovascular function. Furthermore, hawthorn tea has antioxidant properties that can help to protect the blood vessels from damage caused by free radicals, which can contribute to high blood pressure.

Therefore, drinking hawthorn tea regularly may help to lower blood pressure and promote cardiovascular health.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in common hawthorn tea that aid with high blood pressure.

  • Vasodilation: Hawthorn tea helps widen blood vessels, reducing resistance against blood flow and thereby lowering high blood pressure. This is mainly due to the presence of flavonoids and proanthocyanidins, which have vasodilatory effects.
  • Cardiotonic: Hawthorn tea improves the overall function of the heart, which is important for high blood pressure as it helps the heart pump blood more efficiently, reducing the pressure on the artery walls. The active constituents responsible for this are flavonoids, oligomeric procyanidins (OPCs), and saponins.
  • Antioxidant: Hawthorn tea contains powerful antioxidants that help protect blood vessels from damage, which is crucial for high blood pressure as oxidative stress can contribute to the development and progression of hypertension. The main antioxidant compounds are flavonoids, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins.
  • Diuretic: Hawthorn tea promotes diuresis, helping the body eliminate excess fluid and sodium. This is important for high blood pressure as reducing fluid volume in the bloodstream can help lower blood pressure. The active constituents responsible for this property are flavonoids and saponins.
  • Nervous system relaxation: Hawthorn tea has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help reduce stress and anxiety, both of which can contribute to high blood pressure. The active constituents responsible for this property are alkaloids, flavonoids, and triterpene acids.

The list below shows the primary parts of common hawthorn tea used to make teas for high blood pressure.

  • Leaves: Rich in flavonoids, which have been shown to help dilate blood vessels and reduce blood pressure.
  • Flowers: Contain high levels of antioxidants, which can help improve blood circulation and reduce inflammation, thus lowering blood pressure.
  • Berries: Packed with compounds that can help relax blood vessels, improve blood flow, and reduce high blood pressure.
  • Bark: Has been used in traditional medicine to treat high blood pressure due to its vasodilatory properties, which help widen blood vessels and improve circulation.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic common hawthorn tea for high blood pressure.

  1. Measure out 1-2 teaspoons of dried common hawthorn leaves and flowers.
  2. Place the herbs in a tea infuser or teapot with a strainer.
  3. Pour 8 ounces of boiling water over the herbs and let it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Remove the tea infuser or strain the tea into a cup.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of hawthorn tea daily, preferably between meals, for high blood pressure management.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic midland hawthorn tea for high blood pressure.
