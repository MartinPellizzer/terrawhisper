1. Garlic tincture

Garlic tincture helps with high blood pressure because it contains allicin, a compound that has been shown to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow.

  • Platelet aggregation inhibition: Garlic tincture inhibits platelet aggregation, preventing the formation of blood clots that can cause high blood pressure, due to the presence of active constituents such as ajoene and allicin.
  • Cholesterol-lowering: Garlic tincture helps lower high blood pressure by reducing cholesterol levels, particularly LDL ("bad") cholesterol, due to the presence of active compounds like allicin, alliin, and allyl sulfides.

The list below shows the primary parts of garlic tincture used to make tinctures for high blood pressure.

  • Bulbs: Garlic bulbs are commonly used to make medicinal tea for high blood pressure due to their high concentration of allicin, a compound known for its blood pressure-lowering properties.
  • Leaves: While not as potent as the bulbs, garlic leaves can still be used in medicinal teas for high blood pressure, as they contain various beneficial compounds, including flavonoids and steroidal saponins.
  • Flowers: Garlic flowers can be used in medicinal teas for high blood pressure, as they contain allicin and other bioactive compounds, although in lower concentrations compared to the bulbs.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic garlic tincture for high blood pressure.

  1. Chop 1 cup of fresh organic garlic cloves and place them in a clean glass jar.
  2. Add 2 cups of a menstruum, such as 50% distilled water and 50% vodka or rum, covering the garlic.
  3. Tightly seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks, shaking it daily.
  4. After the maceration period, strain the mixture using cheesecloth, squeezing out the liquid.
  5. Store the final tincture in a dark glass bottle, in a cool, dark place, and take 1-2 ml three times a day for high blood pressure.
