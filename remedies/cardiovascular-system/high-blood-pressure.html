What is high blood pressure and how it affects your life?

High blood pressure, also known as hypertension, is a medical condition where the force of blood against the walls of your arteries is consistently too high.

This force is created by the heart pumping blood through the body to supply tissues and organs with oxygen and nutrients. When blood pressure is high, the heart works harder and the risk of damage to the arteries and organs increases.

Over time, high blood pressure can lead to serious health problems such as heart disease, stroke, kidney disease, and vision loss. It can also cause cognitive impairment, sexual dysfunction, and bone loss. High blood pressure often has no warning signs or symptoms, making it difficult to detect without regular check-ups.

If left untreated, it can significantly reduce a person's quality of life and shorten their lifespan.

What are the main causes of high blood pressure?

The main causes of high blood pressure are a combination of genetic and lifestyle factors.

Genetically, some people are predisposed to develop high blood pressure due to inherited genes. Lifestyle factors that can lead to high blood pressure include a diet high in sodium, lack of physical activity, being overweight or obese, excessive alcohol consumption, and chronic stress. Additionally, certain medical conditions such as diabetes, kidney disease, and sleep apnea can also contribute to the development of high blood pressure.

It's important to note that in some cases, the cause of high blood pressure may be unknown, in which case it is referred to as essential or primary hypertension.

The most common causes of high blood pressure are listed below.

  • Obesity: Excess body weight requires the heart to work harder, causing an increase in blood pressure.
  • Physical Inactivity: Lack of regular physical activity can lead to weight gain, which can increase blood pressure.
  • Poor Diet: Consuming a diet high in sodium, saturated fats, and cholesterol can contribute to high blood pressure.
  • Genetics: A family history of high blood pressure can increase the likelihood of developing it.
  • Smoking: Nicotine in cigarettes can cause the blood vessels to narrow, leading to an increase in blood pressure.
  • Excessive Alcohol Consumption: Drinking more than moderate amounts of alcohol can raise blood pressure over time.
  • Stress: High levels of stress can cause temporary increases in blood pressure, and chronic stress can lead to long-term increases.
  • Sleep Apnea: This condition causes pauses in breathing during sleep, which can increase blood pressure.
  • Diabetes: High blood sugar levels can damage blood vessels, leading to an increase in blood pressure.
  • Age: Blood pressure tends to increase with age due to changes in the arteries.
