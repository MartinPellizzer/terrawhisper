Ovulation Pain: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Ovulation pain, also known as mittelschmerz, is a sharp, stabbing, or dull ache in the lower abdomen that occurs when an ovum is released from the ovary, typically lasting a few hours.

It's often caused by the rupturing of the follicle and the release of blood and fluid. If left untreated, ovulation pain can lead to increased anxiety, reduced fertility, and even chronic pain. To alleviate this discomfort, herbal remedies such as ginger, turmeric, and cramp bark can be effective.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as they can interact with other medications and exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of ovulation pain, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of ovulation pain?

The main causes of ovulation pain are the release of an ovum from the ovary, which triggers the contraction of the uterine muscles, and the release of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances produced by the ovary that stimulate uterine contractions.

These contractions can cause a sharp, stabbing pain in the lower abdomen, which is often felt on one side. Other contributing factors include the twisting of the ovary's ligaments, called the ovarian ligaments, which can cause irritation and pain. The increase in estrogen levels before ovulation can also cause the uterine muscles to contract, leading to pain.

Additionally, some women may experience ovulation pain due to a condition called Mittelschmerz, characterized by the presence of blood in the fallopian tubes.

  • Ovarian Cysts: Ovarian cysts can cause ovulation pain due to the release of enzymes that stimulate the ovary to rupture, leading to inflammation and pain.
  • Ectopic Pregnancy: Ectopic pregnancy can cause ovulation pain due to the implantation of the fertilized egg outside the uterus, causing the fallopian tube to rupture and leading to severe pain.
  • Ovarian Torsion: Ovarian torsion can cause ovulation pain due to the twisting of the ovary, cutting off blood supply and causing severe pain and potentially leading to ovarian necrosis.
  • Endometriosis: Endometriosis can cause ovulation pain due to the growth of endometrial tissue on the ovaries, leading to inflammation and adhesions that cause pain during ovulation.
  • Pelvic Inflammatory Disease: Pelvic inflammatory disease can cause ovulation pain due to the infection of the fallopian tubes and ovaries, leading to inflammation and scarring that cause pain during ovulation.
  • Mittelschmerz: Mittelschmerz is a normal ovulation pain caused by the release of an egg from the ovary, leading to irritation of the fallopian tube and causing a sharp, stabbing pain on one side of the pelvis.
  • Adhesions: Adhesions can cause ovulation pain due to the formation of scar tissue that adheres to the ovaries and fallopian tubes, leading to inflammation and pain during ovulation.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Hormonal imbalance can cause ovulation pain due to the disruption of the normal ovulation process, leading to irregular ovulation and pain.
