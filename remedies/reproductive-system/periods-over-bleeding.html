What is over bleeding during periods and how it affects your life?

Over bleeding during periods, also known as heavy menstrual bleeding, occurs when a woman experiences abnormally heavy or prolonged menstrual flow.

This can lead to a significant impact on one's daily life. For instance, it can cause embarrassment and distress when pads or tampons are seen through thin clothing, making it difficult to go out in public without worrying about leakage or accidents. It can also disrupt work or school schedules due to frequent and extended bathroom breaks, as well as the need to frequently change pads or tampons.

Over bleeding can also affect sleep, as women may need to wake up multiple times a night to change sanitary products or clean up spills. Furthermore, the physical strain of heavy bleeding can lead to fatigue, headaches, and cramping, making everyday activities feel like a chore. Additionally, over bleeding can impact relationships and social activities, as women may feel self-conscious or hesitant to participate in social events due to their menstrual cycle.

The emotional toll of over bleeding can also contribute to anxiety, depression, and feelings of isolation, further affecting overall well-being.

What precautions to take when using herbal remedies for over bleeding during periods?

When using herbal remedies to manage heavy or prolonged bleeding during menstruation, it's essential to exercise caution and take necessary precautions.

Always consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplements or herbs, especially if you have a history of blood clotting disorders, are taking anticoagulant medications, or experience heavy menstrual bleeding. Certain herbs like ginkgo biloba and ginger may interact with medications or worsen bleeding, while others like turmeric and chamomile may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. Additionally, it's crucial to follow proper dosing instructions and monitor your body's response to the herbal remedies.

If you experience increased bleeding, severe cramping, or other adverse effects, discontinue use and consult with a healthcare provider for further guidance.
