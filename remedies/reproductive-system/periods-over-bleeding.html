What is over bleeding during periods and how it affects your life?

Over bleeding during periods, also known as menorrhagia, can be a debilitating and frustrating experience for women.

It refers to menstrual bleeding that is heavier or longer than normal, often lasting more than 7 days with heavy flow. This excessive bleeding can have a significant impact on daily life, causing emotional distress, fatigue, and physical discomfort. For example, over bleeding may require frequent trips to the bathroom, making it difficult to attend work or school regularly, or even participate in social activities.

Additionally, the constant need for sanitary products can be expensive and inconvenient, leading to financial strain and feelings of embarrassment. Furthermore, heavy bleeding can also affect relationships, as women may feel self-conscious about their appearance or unable to engage in physical intimacy due to vaginal dryness and discomfort. Moreover, over bleeding can also disrupt normal activities like exercise, hobbies, and even sleep, leaving women feeling drained and overwhelmed by the constant demands it places on their bodies.

Overall, over bleeding during periods can be a significant source of stress and anxiety, making it essential for women to seek medical attention to manage this condition effectively.

What are the main causes of over bleeding during periods?

The main causes of over bleeding during periods are hormonal imbalances, polyps or fibroids in the uterus, and an abnormality in the cervix.

Hormonal fluctuations can cause an imbalance in estrogen and progesterone levels, leading to excessive bleeding. Uterine polyps or fibroids can also cause irregular bleeding as they grow and put pressure on surrounding tissues. Additionally, cervical abnormalities such as cervical cancer, cervical dysplasia, or cervical stenosis can lead to heavy menstrual bleeding due to abnormal growths or blockages in the cervix.

These factors can disrupt normal menstruation and result in excessive bleeding, which can be painful, inconvenient, and even affect daily life.

The most common causes of over bleeding during periods are listed below.

  • Hormonal Imbalance: An imbalance of estrogen and progesterone hormones can lead to irregular menstrual cycles and heavy bleeding.
  • Adenomyosis: A condition in which tissue similar to the lining of the uterus grows into the muscle of the uterus, leading to heavy bleeding and painful periods.
  • Fibroids: Non-cancerous growths that develop in the wall of the uterus can cause heavy bleeding, pain, and irregular menstrual cycles.
  • Polyps: Growths that attach to the lining of the uterus or cervix and can cause bleeding, pain, and infertility.
  • Endometrial Hyperplasia: A condition in which the lining of the uterus becomes too thick, leading to excessive bleeding and polyps.
  • Uterine Infection (Endometritis): Bacterial infection of the uterus that can cause heavy bleeding, cramping, and fever.
  • Cervical Stenosis: Narrowing of the opening of the cervix that can prevent menstrual blood from flowing out properly, leading to heavy bleeding and cramping.
  • Thyroid Disorders: Certain thyroid conditions, such as hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism, can affect menstruation and lead to excessive bleeding.
  • Anemia: Iron deficiency anemia, in particular, can cause heavy menstrual bleeding due to the body's inability to produce adequate hemoglobin.
  • IUD (Intrauterine Device): Some IUDs are designed to prevent pregnancy by releasing hormones that thin the uterine lining, leading to heavier or more frequent periods.

What precautions to take when using herbal remedies for over bleeding during periods?

When using herbal remedies to manage heavy or prolonged bleeding during menstruation, it's essential to exercise caution and take necessary precautions.

Always consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplements or herbs, especially if you have a history of blood clotting disorders, are taking anticoagulant medications, or experience heavy menstrual bleeding. Certain herbs like ginkgo biloba and ginger may interact with medications or worsen bleeding, while others like turmeric and chamomile may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. Additionally, it's crucial to follow proper dosing instructions and monitor your body's response to the herbal remedies.

If you experience increased bleeding, severe cramping, or other adverse effects, discontinue use and consult with a healthcare provider for further guidance.
