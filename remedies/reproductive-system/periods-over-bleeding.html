Over Bleeding During Periods: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Over bleeding during periods, also known as menorrhagia, refers to abnormally heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding that can last for more than 7 days, exceeding the typical 3-5 days.

This condition can be caused by hormonal imbalances, uterine fibroids, or other underlying health issues. Excessive bleeding can lead to anemia, fatigue, and weakness, negatively impacting overall health and daily life. To alleviate symptoms, herbal remedies such as shepherd's purse, blue cohosh, and ginger can be used to reduce bleeding and alleviate cramps.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, especially if taking medications or experiencing severe symptoms, to ensure safe and effective use.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of over bleeding during periods, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of over bleeding during periods?

The main causes of over bleeding during periods are hormonal imbalances, particularly an excess of estrogen, which can cause the uterine lining to grow too thick and lead to heavy bleeding.

Other causes include the presence of polyps or fibroids in the uterus, which can cause bleeding irregularities and increase blood loss. Additionally, certain medical conditions, such as endometriosis, adenomyosis, and thyroid disorders, can also lead to over bleeding. Furthermore, the use of certain medications, including hormonal contraceptives, can disrupt hormone levels and contribute to heavy bleeding.

Research has also shown that genetic predisposition, with a family history of heavy menstrual bleeding, can also increase the risk of over bleeding.

  • Hormonal Imbalance: Hormonal imbalance, particularly an excess of estrogen and a deficiency of progesterone, can lead to over bleeding during periods as it disrupts the normal menstrual cycle and causes the uterus to shed its lining in excess.
  • Fibroids: Uterine fibroids, noncancerous growths in the uterus, can cause over bleeding during periods by interfering with the normal shedding of the uterine lining and increasing the size and blood supply to the uterus, leading to heavier menstrual flow.
  • Polyps: Endometrial polyps, growths on the lining of the uterus, can cause over bleeding during periods by increasing the surface area of the uterus and promoting the growth of the uterine lining, leading to heavier menstrual flow.
  • Adenomyosis: Adenomyosis, a condition in which endometrial tissue grows into the uterine muscle, can cause over bleeding during periods by increasing the size and blood supply to the uterus and disrupting the normal menstrual cycle, leading to heavier menstrual flow.
  • Pregnancy: Pregnancy, particularly miscarriage or ectopic pregnancy, can cause over bleeding during periods as the body's hormone levels increase and the uterus prepares for implantation, leading to heavier menstrual flow.
  • Stress: Chronic stress can cause over bleeding during periods by increasing the production of stress hormones, such as cortisol, which can disrupt the normal menstrual cycle and lead to heavier menstrual flow.
  • Infections: Infections, such as pelvic inflammatory disease (PID), can cause over bleeding during periods by causing inflammation and irritation in the uterus and fallopian tubes, leading to heavier menstrual flow.
  • Thyroid Disorders: Thyroid disorders, particularly hyperthyroidism, can cause over bleeding during periods by disrupting the body's hormone balance and leading to an increase in estrogen production, which can cause heavier menstrual flow.
  • Anemia: Anemia, particularly iron deficiency anemia, can cause over bleeding during periods as the body's iron stores are depleted, leading to a decrease in the production of hemoglobin and an increase in menstrual flow.
  • Certain Medications: Certain medications, such as anticoagulants and anti-inflammatory medications, can cause over bleeding during periods by increasing the risk of bleeding and disrupting the normal menstrual cycle.
