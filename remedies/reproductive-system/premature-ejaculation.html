Premature Ejaculation: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Premature ejaculation (PE) is a condition characterized by the inability to delay ejaculation during intercourse, occurring before or shortly after penetration, often accompanied by distress or anxiety.

It can be caused by psychological factors such as performance anxiety, relationship issues, or past traumatic experiences, as well as physiological factors like hormonal imbalances, nerve damage, or underlying medical conditions like diabetes. PE can negatively impact a person's self-esteem and overall well-being. Herbal remedies such as ashwagandha, ginseng, and ginkgo biloba have been traditionally used to alleviate PE symptoms.

However, when using medicinal herbs, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional and follow recommended dosages, as they can interact with other medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of premature ejaculation, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of premature ejaculation?

The main causes of premature ejaculation are a complex interplay of psychological, neurological, and physiological factors.

Psychological factors, such as anxiety, stress, and depression, can contribute to premature ejaculation by increasing arousal and decreasing control over ejaculation (Hartmann & Kircher, 2010). Neurological factors, including serotonin levels and dopamine regulation, play a crucial role in ejaculation control, with low serotonin levels being a common finding in men with premature ejaculation (Kaplan et al., 2011). Physiological factors, such as age, hormonal imbalances, and certain medical conditions, such as diabetes and prostate issues, can also contribute to premature ejaculation (Hartmann & Kircher, 2010).

Additionally, genetic predisposition and past experiences, such as traumatic events or past sexual abuse, can also increase the risk of premature ejaculation (Rowland et al., 2013).

  • Stress: Stress and anxiety can cause premature ejaculation by increasing the body's fight-or-flight response, which can lead to increased muscle tension and a rapid release of semen.
  • Erectile Dysfunction: Erectile dysfunction can cause premature ejaculation because when a man is unable to maintain an erection, he may become anxious or frustrated, leading to a quick release of semen.
  • Performance Anxiety: Performance anxiety can cause premature ejaculation because the pressure to perform can cause a man to become overly focused on the outcome, leading to a loss of control over his bodily functions.
  • Genetic Factors: Genetic factors can cause premature ejaculation because some men may inherit a tendency to experience rapid ejaculation from their parents.
  • Hormonal Imbalances: Hormonal imbalances, particularly low levels of testosterone, can cause premature ejaculation because hormones play a crucial role in regulating ejaculation.
  • Medication Side Effects: Medication side effects, such as those caused by antidepressants and some blood pressure medications, can cause premature ejaculation because certain medications can increase serotonin levels in the brain, leading to delayed ejaculation, but in some cases, it can lead to premature ejaculation.
  • Nervous System Disorders: Nervous system disorders, such as multiple sclerosis and spinal cord injuries, can cause premature ejaculation because damage to the nervous system can disrupt normal ejaculation processes.
  • Medical Conditions: Medical conditions, such as diabetes, prostate enlargement, and urinary tract infections, can cause premature ejaculation because certain medical conditions can cause nerve damage or inflammation in the pelvic area.
  • Substance Abuse: Substance abuse, particularly the use of cocaine and other stimulants, can cause premature ejaculation because these substances can increase heart rate and blood pressure, leading to a rapid release of semen.
  • Age: Age can cause premature ejaculation because as men get older, their ability to control their ejaculation may decline, leading to premature ejaculation.
  • Lifestyle Factors: Lifestyle factors, such as smoking and excessive caffeine consumption, can cause premature ejaculation because these factors can increase heart rate and blood pressure, leading to a rapid release of semen.
