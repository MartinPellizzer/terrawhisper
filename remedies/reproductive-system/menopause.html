Menopause: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Menopause is a natural biological process in women, typically occurring between the ages of 45 and 55, where the ovaries gradually stop producing eggs, leading to a decline in estrogen levels.

This hormonal shift is often triggered by aging, but can also be caused by surgery, chemotherapy, or other medical conditions. Menopause can have negative impacts on health, including hot flashes, night sweats, mood swings, and osteoporosis.

However, various herbal remedies, such as black cohosh, red clover, and evening primrose oil, have been found to provide relief from these symptoms. These medicinal preparations can be consumed as teas, capsules, or tinctures.

It's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies, as some may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of menopause, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of menopause?

The main causes of menopause are the depletion of ovarian follicles, which results in a decrease in estrogen production, leading to the cessation of menstruation.

This decrease in estrogen levels triggers a cascade of physiological changes, including the reduction of follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) sensitivity and the subsequent increase in FSH levels. The average age of menopause is around 51 years, although it can occur between 40 and 60 years. Factors such as smoking, family history, and certain medical conditions, including premature ovarian failure, can also contribute to early menopause.

Additionally, chemotherapy and other treatments that damage the ovaries can induce menopause in some women.

  • Genetic Age: Genetic age is the most common cause of menopause, as it is the natural aging process of the ovaries, which eventually leads to the depletion of eggs and the cessation of menstruation.
  • Tubal Ligation: Tubal ligation, or sterilization, is a surgical procedure that blocks the fallopian tubes, preventing eggs from being fertilized and leading to menopause, as the ovaries continue to produce hormones but there is no pregnancy to stimulate their function.
  • Ovarian Surgery: Ovarian surgery, such as oophorectomy, is a surgical procedure that removes the ovaries, leading to the immediate cessation of hormone production and menstruation, and resulting in menopause.
  • Chemo- and Radiation Therapy: Chemo- and radiation therapy can damage the ovaries and disrupt hormone production, leading to menopause, as the ovaries are unable to recover from the damage and resume normal function.
  • Premature Ovarian Failure: Premature ovarian failure, also known as premature ovarian insufficiency, is a condition where the ovaries stop functioning and hormone production decreases, leading to menopause, often due to genetic or autoimmune disorders.
