1. Ginkgo biloba teas

Ginkgo biloba teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate symptoms of premenstrual syndrome (PMS), a common condition affecting many women.

The herbal preparation's properties, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, help to reduce symptoms such as mood swings, bloating, and breast tenderness.

The bioactive constituents of Ginkgo biloba, including flavonoids, terpenoids, and bilobalide, have been shown to modulate neurotransmitters and hormones, which helps to regulate menstrual cycles and alleviate symptoms of PMS.

The benefits of Ginkgo biloba teas in treating PMS include reduced mood swings, improved sleep quality, and a decrease in menstrual cramp severity, making it a popular natural remedy for women seeking alternative relief from PMS symptoms.

Related Study

According to "Disease-a-month : DM", Ginkgo biloba teas may help improve premenstrual syndrome symptoms.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water, 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves, and a tea infuser or strainer.
  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of dried Ginkgo biloba leaves and place them in the tea infuser or strainer.
  3. Pour 1 cup of boiling water over the Ginkgo biloba leaves in the infuser or strainer.
  4. Steep the mixture for 5-7 minutes, then remove the infuser or strainer.
  5. Drink the tea and consider adding honey or lemon to taste, 2-3 times a day as needed for premenstrual syndrome relief.

Usage:

  1. Consult a doctor before using Ginkgo biloba tea for premenstrual syndrome.
  2. Start with 1 cup of Ginkgo biloba tea, 2-3 times a day, for 7 days.
  3. Monitor symptoms and adjust dosage to 1-2 cups, 2-3 times a day, if needed.
  4. Continue using Ginkgo biloba tea throughout the menstrual cycle, adjusting dosage as needed.
  5. Track symptoms and adjust usage after 3 months to optimize benefits.

Ginkgo biloba teas can be used to treat premenstrual syndrome (PMS), but possible side effects may include dizziness, nausea, and stomach upset due to its blood-thinning properties, as well as allergic reactions or interactions with certain medications.

To minimize side effects, individuals should start with a low dose, monitor their body's response, and avoid consuming Ginkgo biloba teas with other medications, especially blood thinners, and during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
