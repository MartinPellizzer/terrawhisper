1. Paeonia officinalis

Pink peony essential oils helps with menstrual cramps because it possesses anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, providing natural relief from the discomforts associated with PMS.

The oil's chemical composition includes flavonoids and terpenes, which have been shown to reduce prostaglandins, the hormone-like substances responsible for uterine contractions that cause menstrual cramps.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in pink peony essential oils that aid with menstrual cramps.

  • Antispasmodic property: Herbal pink peony essential oil helps to relax and calm spasming uterine muscles, providing relief from menstrual cramps (dysmenorrhea). This is because the oil's antispasmodic compounds can bind to receptors in the uterus, reducing muscle contractions.
  • Anti-inflammatory property: Pink peony essential oil has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce inflammation and swelling in the uterine lining, which can contribute to menstrual cramps. By reducing inflammation, the oil may also help alleviate other symptoms associated with PMS.
  • Pain-relieving property: The essential oil contains analgesic compounds that can help reduce pain perception, providing relief from menstrual cramp discomfort and associated abdominal cramping.

The list below shows the primary parts of pink peony used to make essential oils for menstrual cramps.

  • Roots: Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, which help to reduce menstrual cramp severity by blocking prostaglandins.
  • Rhyzomes: Contain flavonoids and saponins that ease pain and inflammation, making them effective against menstrual cramps.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic pink peony for menstrual cramps.

  1. Harvest fresh paeonia officinalis root material early in the morning after dew has dried.
  2. Dry the harvested root material in a warm, well-ventilated area for at least 14 days.
  3. Blend 20 grams of dried paeonia officinalis root with 100 milliliters of high-proof ethanol and let sit for 21 days.
  4. Filter the mixture through cheesecloth into a clean glass bottle and discard solids after 24 hours.
  5. Allow the liquid to decant for 7 days before transferring it to dark glass bottles for storage.
