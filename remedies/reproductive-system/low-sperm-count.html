Low Sperm Count: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Low sperm count, medically known as oligospermia, is a condition where a man has fewer than 15 million sperm per milliliter of semen, which can lead to difficulty conceiving a child.

Causes of low sperm count include hormonal imbalances, infection, exposure to toxins, and certain medical conditions. It can also lead to feelings of anxiety and depression. To address this issue, herbal remedies such as ashwagandha, ginseng, and saw palmetto have been found to improve sperm count and motility.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as they may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of low sperm count, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of low sperm count?

The main causes of low sperm count are genetic factors, such as Klinefelter syndrome and Y chromosome microdeletions, which account for approximately 13% of cases, while environmental factors, including exposure to toxins like pesticides and heavy metals, are responsible for around 10%.

Additionally, hormonal imbalances, particularly low levels of testosterone, can lead to low sperm count, affecting about 20% of men. Lifestyle factors, including smoking, obesity, and excessive heat, which can cause testicular damage, contribute to 15% of cases. Medical conditions, such as varicocele, orchitis, and prostatitis, can also cause low sperm count, affecting about 10% of men.

Furthermore, certain medications, including anabolic steroids and certain antidepressants, can also lower sperm count, affecting approximately 5% of cases.

  • Varicocele: A varicocele is an enlargement of the veins within the scrotum, which can cause damage to the sperm and lead to low sperm count due to increased temperature and oxidative stress.
  • Ejaculation Frequency: Frequent ejaculation can deplete the sperm count, as it can take several days for the body to replenish sperm.
  • Testicular Infection: Infections such as epididymitis can cause inflammation and damage to the testicles, leading to low sperm count and infertility.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Hormonal imbalances, particularly low levels of testosterone, can disrupt sperm production and lead to low sperm count.
  • Age: As men age, their sperm count naturally declines, with the average sperm count decreasing by about 1% per year after the age of 20.
  • Obesity: Excess body fat can lead to inflammation and oxidative stress, which can damage sperm and reduce sperm count.
  • Smoking: Smoking has been linked to reduced sperm count, as it can damage sperm DNA and reduce sperm motility.
  • Environmental Toxins: Exposure to environmental toxins such as pesticides and heavy metals can damage sperm and reduce sperm count.
  • Certain Medications: Certain medications, such as anabolic steroids and some antidepressants, can affect sperm production and lead to low sperm count.
  • Chronic Stress: Chronic stress can disrupt the body's hormonal balance, including testosterone levels, which can affect sperm production and lead to low sperm count.
