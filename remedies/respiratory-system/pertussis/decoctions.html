Additionally, rue has antimicrobial properties that help combat the bacteria that causes pertussis, further supporting recovery.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in rue decoctions that aid with pertussis.

  • Antispasmodic: Rue decoction helps relax constricted airways, relieving symptoms such as coughing and wheezing associated with pertussis by reducing spasms in the bronchial muscles.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Rue decoction reduces inflammation in the respiratory tract, which can help alleviate congestion, fever, and cough severity in patients with pertussis.
  • Antimicrobial: Rue decoction has antibacterial properties that may help combat Bordetella pertussis bacteria, potentially reducing the duration and severity of the infection.

The list below shows the primary parts of rue used to make decoctions for pertussis.

  • Roots: Rich in volatile oils, which help to relax bronchial muscles and ease congestion associated with pertussis.
  • Rhyzomes: Containing a high concentration of flavonoids, these aid in reducing inflammation and relieving cough symptoms.
  • Barks: The bark's tannins have anti-inflammatory properties, making it effective in soothing irritated airways and reducing fever.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic rue for pertussis.

  1. Harvest fresh ruta graveolens leaves and flowers in the morning after dew has dried.
  2. Rinse the herb material with filtered water to remove any dirt or debris.
  3. Combine 10 grams of ruta graveolens with 500 milliliters of boiling water in a heat-resistant container.
  4. Simmer the mixture for 15-20 minutes to allow for optimal extraction of active compounds.
  5. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass container for consumption.
