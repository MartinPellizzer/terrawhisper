7 Best Herbal Teas For Pertussis

Additionally, other herbal teas like Ginger tea, which aids digestion and reduces inflammation, Slippery elm tea, which soothes the mucous membranes, and Wild cherry bark tea, which helps to reduce coughing fits, may also be beneficial in managing the symptoms of pertussis.

1. Echinacea purpurea teas

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to treat pertussis, also known as whooping cough, due to their anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties.

The herbal preparation helps to treat this ailment by enhancing the body's immune response and reducing inflammation in the respiratory tract, thereby alleviating symptoms such as coughing and wheezing.

The bioactive constituents of Echinacea purpurea teas, including alkylamides, glycoproteins, and polyphenols, are responsible for these benefits, which help to modulate the immune system and reduce oxidative stress.

Drinking Echinacea purpurea teas may help to provide relief from pertussis symptoms, promote recovery, and reduce the duration of the illness.

Related Study

According to "BMC veterinary research", Echinacea purpurea teas were found to be one of the most promising candidates for diseases of the respiratory tract, including pertussis.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Steep the flowers in the boiling water for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Strain the mixture using a tea strainer or a piece of cheesecloth.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of the tea per day to help alleviate symptoms of pertussis.

Usage:

  1. Drink 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea tea 3 times a day.
  2. Start with 1 teaspoon of dried Echinacea per cup of boiling water.
  3. Gradually increase dosage to 2 teaspoons per cup as needed.
  4. Continue using Echinacea tea for 2-3 weeks to help alleviate pertussis.
  5. Consult a healthcare professional before using Echinacea for pertussis treatment.

Echinacea purpurea teas can be used to treat the pertussis ailment, but possible side effects may include allergic reactions, such as hives or itching, and interactions with other medications, potentially leading to increased bleeding or reduced blood pressure.

To use Echinacea purpurea teas safely, take it in moderation, monitor your body's response, and avoid consuming it with other medications that may have similar effects, such as blood thinners or medications for low blood pressure.

2. Sambucus nigra teas

Sambucus nigra teas, derived from the elderberry plant, have been traditionally used to treat pertussis, commonly known as whooping cough.

The antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties of this herbal preparation help to reduce the severity and duration of the symptoms, including coughing fits and fever.

The bioactive constituents, such as flavonoids, anthocyanins, and phenolic acids, in Sambucus nigra teas have been found to exhibit immunomodulatory effects, which aid in boosting the immune system to fight off the Bordetella pertussis bacteria responsible for the infection.

The benefits of using Sambucus nigra teas to treat pertussis include reduced risk of complications, faster recovery time, and a decrease in the transmission of the disease to others.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Sambucus nigra flowers, or 1 tablespoon of dried flowers.
  2. Combine the Sambucus nigra flowers with 1 cup of boiling water in a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-10 minutes, then strain it into another cup.
  4. Add honey to taste, if desired, and stir well.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of the tea throughout the day, as needed, for relief from pertussis symptoms.

Usage:

  1. Consult a doctor before using Sambucus nigra tea for pertussis treatment.
  2. Prepare tea according to doctor's instructions.
  3. Drink 2-3 cups of tea 3-4 times a day.
  4. Maintain the dosage for 2-3 weeks as directed by the doctor.
  5. Monitor symptoms and adjust treatment plan as advised by the doctor.

Sambucus nigra teas can be used to treat the pertussis ailment, but possible side effects may include allergic reactions, such as hives and itching, as well as gastrointestinal issues like nausea and stomach cramps.

When using Sambucus nigra teas to treat pertussis, it is essential to note that it can interact with certain medications and may not be suitable for individuals with severe allergies or weakened immune systems, so it is recommended to use it in moderation and monitor personal health closely.

3. Zingiber officinale teas

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been used to help alleviate symptoms of pertussis, also known as whooping cough.

Eucalyptus globulus teas have been studied for their potential in treating pertussis, also known as whooping cough, due to their expectorant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Additionally, the antimicrobial properties of Eucalyptus globulus teas help to combat secondary bacterial infections that may complicate pertussis.

Overall, Eucalyptus globulus teas may provide relief from pertussis symptoms and support the body's recovery process by promoting drainage and reducing the risk of complications.

  5. Drink the tea 3-4 times a day, as needed, to help alleviate pertussis symptoms.

Usage:

  1. Consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment of pertussis.

  3. Use 1 tablespoon of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves per cup of boiling water.
  4. Continue treatment for at least 7-10 days or as directed by a healthcare professional.
  5. Monitor cough and fever symptoms, and adjust treatment plan if necessary.

Eucalyptus globulus teas can be used to treat pertussis, but possible side effects may include digestive issues, allergic reactions, and an increased heart rate due to its essential oils.
