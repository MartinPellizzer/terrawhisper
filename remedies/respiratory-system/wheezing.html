Wheezing: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Wheezing is a high-pitched whistling sound while breathing out, typically a symptom of respiratory conditions such as asthma, bronchitis, or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

Wheezing is usually caused by inflammation, airway constriction, or mucus buildup in the lungs. If left untreated, wheezing can lead to respiratory failure, increased risk of infections, and reduced lung function. To alleviate wheezing, herbal remedies such as thyme, eucalyptus, and ginger are used in teas, infusions, or capsules.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as some may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of wheezing, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of wheezing?

The main causes of wheezing are typically related to obstructive airway diseases, such as asthma, chronic bronchitis, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

In these conditions, inflammation and airway constriction cause the airways to narrow, resulting in a high-pitched whistling sound when breathing out, known as wheezing. Other causes include allergies, respiratory infections, and gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), which can trigger wheezing in susceptible individuals. Additionally, environmental irritants like tobacco smoke, pollution, and dust mites can also contribute to wheezing by irritating the airways and triggering inflammation.

In some cases, wheezing can also be a symptom of cardiac conditions, such as a heart attack or cardiogenic shock, which can compress the airways and disrupt breathing.

  • Asthma: Asthma is a chronic inflammatory disease of the airways that causes the airway muscles to contract and the airway lining to swell, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath due to airflow obstruction.
  • COPD: Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is a progressive lung disease that damages the airways and makes it difficult to breathe, causing wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath due to airflow limitation.
  • Allergies: Allergies, such as allergic rhinitis or allergic asthma, trigger the release of histamine and other chemical mediators that cause the airway muscles to contract and the airway lining to swell, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.
  • GERD: Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) causes stomach acid to flow up into the esophagus, irritating the airway lining and triggering inflammation and constriction of the airway muscles, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.
  • Bronchitis: Acute bronchitis is an inflammation of the bronchial tubes that causes the airway muscles to contract and the airway lining to swell, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath due to airflow obstruction.
  • Pneumonia: Bacterial or viral pneumonia causes inflammation of the lung tissue and airways, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath due to airflow obstruction and impaired gas exchange.
  • Anaphylaxis: Anaphylaxis is a severe, life-threatening allergic reaction that causes rapid constriction of the airway muscles and increased mucus production, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath due to airflow obstruction.
  • Pneumothorax: Pneumothorax, or collapsed lung, causes air to leak into the space between the lung and chest wall, leading to decreased lung expansion and airflow obstruction, resulting in wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.
  • Heart failure: Right heart failure causes fluid buildup in the lungs, leading to inflammation and constriction of the airway muscles, and decreased lung expansion, resulting in wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath due to impaired gas exchange.
  • Neuromuscular diseases: Neuromuscular diseases, such as muscular dystrophy or multiple sclerosis, affect the nerves that control the airway muscles, leading to impaired contraction and relaxation of the airway muscles and resulting in wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath due to airflow obstruction.
