1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus creams contains eucalyptol, a compound that helps break down mucus and relieve congestion.

It also contains menthol, which cools the nasal passages and helps reduce inflammation. When you apply the cream to your nose, the eucalyptol and menthol are absorbed into your skin and released into your nasal passages, where they can help ease a runny nose.

To use Eucalyptus globulus cream, gently rub a small amount onto your nostrils and the bridge of your nose, 2-3 times a day, or as needed. You may start to feel relief from your runny nose within 15-30 minutes, as the eucalyptol and menthol work to clear out excess mucus and reduce swelling in your nasal passages.

As you continue to use the cream, you may find that your symptoms decrease and your nose becomes less runny, making it easier to breathe and feel more comfortable.

2. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea creams contain active compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids that help reduce nasal congestion and runny nose.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties, which calm the nasal passages and prevent the release of histamine, a chemical that causes congestion and runny nose. When used topically, Echinacea purpurea creams may help to soothe and calm the nasal mucosa, reducing the production of mucus and alleviating runny nose symptoms.

To use Echinacea purpurea creams for runny nose, apply a thin layer to the nose and surrounding area 2-3 times a day, gently massaging it into the skin until fully absorbed. Be sure to follow the product instructions and start with a small amount to test for any sensitivity. As the cream works to calm and soothe the nasal passages, you may notice a reduction in nasal congestion and runny nose symptoms within a few hours.

Some people may experience additional benefits, such as a decrease in sinus pressure and a clearer sense of smell.

3. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale creams contain active compounds like gingerol and shogaol, which have natural anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties.

These properties help to reduce swelling in the nasal passages and thin out mucus, making it easier to breathe and relieving runny nose symptoms. To use Zingiber officinale creams for a runny nose, apply a thin layer to the affected area, usually the bridge of the nose or the nostrils, and gently massage it in. You can do this 2-3 times a day or as needed.

When applied correctly, you can expect to experience a decrease in nasal congestion and runny nose symptoms within 15-30 minutes. The cream's decongestant properties may also help to reduce sinus pressure and promote a sense of relief. However, keep in mind that Zingiber officinale creams may not completely eliminate runny nose symptoms, especially if they are caused by an underlying condition.

If your symptoms persist or worsen, it's best to consult with a healthcare professional for further guidance.

4. Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia creams contain active compounds like cineole, linalool, and terpinen-4-ol, which are known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

These constituents help to reduce nasal congestion and fight off infections that can cause a runny nose. When you apply a Melaleuca alternifolia cream to your nose, the active ingredients work to calm down the nasal passages and reduce the production of excess mucus. To use a Melaleuca alternifolia cream for a runny nose, start by applying a small amount to the affected area, ideally 2-3 times a day.

You can also mix a few drops of the cream with a carrier oil like coconut or olive oil and inhale it through your nose to help ease congestion. Be sure to clean and dry your nose before application to get the best results. As you use the cream, you may start to notice a decrease in nasal discharge and a reduction in congestion. Your nose may also feel less itchy and irritated, allowing you to breathe more easily.

In some cases, you may also notice an improvement in your sense of smell, as the inflammation in your nasal passages subsides.

5. Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia creams contains properties that may help alleviate a runny nose.

This is because it contains constituents such as linalool and linalyl acetate, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. When applied topically, these compounds can help reduce swelling and ease congestion in the nasal passages, making it easier to breathe.

To use Lavandula angustifolia creams for a runny nose, apply a small amount to the chest or nose area and gently massage it in. You can also add a few drops of the cream to your pillow or inhale it by mixing it with a humidifier. By doing so, the active ingredients may help calm the nasal tissues and reduce mucus production, leading to a decrease in nasal discharge.

As a result, you may experience relief from a stuffy or runny nose, making it easier to breathe and sleep through the night.

6. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids that help with a runny nose.

The flavonoids, specifically quercetin, have anti-inflammatory properties that reduce swelling in the nasal passages. This helps to open up the airways and improve breathing. Gently massage the cream into your skin until it's fully absorbed. You can also apply the cream to your fingers and gently rub it onto the inside of your nostrils. By using Ginkgo biloba cream, you may start to feel relief from your runny nose within a few hours. The swelling in your nasal passages may decrease, and your breathing may become easier. You may be able to breathe more comfortably, and your runny nose may become less noticeable.

As the inflammation decreases, you may also start to feel a reduction in sinus pressure and congestion.

Thymol works by reducing swelling and killing bacteria, viruses, or fungi that may be contributing to the congestion.
