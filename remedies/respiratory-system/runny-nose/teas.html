7 Best Herbal Teas For Runny Nose

Herbal teas for Runny nose are a type of herbal remedy made from various plants, flowers, and herbs that are used to alleviate and treat nasal congestion, sinus pressure, and excessive mucus production.

These teas work by soothing and calming the nasal passages, reducing inflammation, and promoting drainage of excess mucus, thereby providing relief from the discomfort and inconvenience of a runny nose.

Some examples of herbal teas that can be used to treat runny nose include peppermint tea, which helps to open up airways and reduce congestion, ginger tea, which has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe the nasal passages, eucalyptus tea, which has decongestant properties that help to clear mucus and sinus pressure, and thyme tea, which has antimicrobial properties that help to combat infections that can cause runny nose.

Additionally, other herbal teas such as elderflower, yarrow, and licorice root can also be used to treat runny nose, as they have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that help to reduce congestion and promote drainage.

1. Eucalyptus globulus teas

Eucalyptus globulus teas have been traditionally used to treat the runny nose ailment due to their decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The herbal preparation helps to treat this ailment by reducing nasal congestion, relieving sinus pressure, and promoting a clear respiratory tract.

The bioactive constituents of Eucalyptus globulus teas, including eucalyptol, alpha-pinene, and beta-pinene, contribute to its therapeutic effects by inhibiting the production of mucus and reducing inflammation in the nasal passages.

Regular consumption of Eucalyptus globulus teas can provide relief from runny nose symptoms and promote overall respiratory health, making it a popular natural remedy for colds and flu.

Related Study

According to the African journal of traditional, complementary, and alternative medicines : AJTCAM, Eucalyptus globulus teas may be used for runny nose, as part of traditional management of asthma in the Togolese traditional medicine.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of boiling water and 1 tablespoon of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves.
  2. Measure 1 teaspoon of honey (optional) and set aside.
  3. Add the Eucalyptus leaves to the boiling water and let it steep for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the mixture into a cup and discard the leaves.
  5. Add honey (if using) and stir well before drinking.

Usage:

  1. Drink 1 cup of Eucalyptus globulus tea 3 times a day.
  2. Use 1 tablespoon of dried Eucalyptus globulus leaves per cup.
  3. Steep for 5-7 minutes to release essential oils.
  4. Drink tea hot or warm, not cold.
  5. Continue for 3-5 days for best results.

Eucalyptus globulus teas can help alleviate a runny nose, but possible side effects may include stomach upset, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications.

When using Eucalyptus globulus teas to treat a runny nose, it's essential to take precautions, such as starting with small doses, avoiding excessive consumption, and being cautious if you have sensitive stomachs or allergies.

2. Echinacea purpurea teas

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to treat the runny nose ailment due to their anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties.

The bioactive constituents of Echinacea purpurea, including alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and flavonoids, help to modulate the immune system and reduce inflammation in the nasal passages, thereby alleviating symptoms of a runny nose.

The benefits of consuming Echinacea purpurea teas include reduced nasal congestion, decreased production of mucus, and a shorter duration of illness.

Regular consumption of Echinacea purpurea teas may also help to boost the immune system and provide long-term protection against respiratory infections.

Related Study

According to "The Cochrane database of systematic reviews", Echinacea purpurea teas may be better than a placebo for relieving runny nose, although there is not enough evidence to recommend a specific product.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh Echinacea purpurea flowers, stems, and leaves.
  2. Combine the Echinacea mixture with 1 cup of boiling water in a pot.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain it through a cheesecloth or a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of honey to the tea, if desired, to make it sweeter.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help relieve a runny nose.

Usage:

  1. Drink 1/2 cup of Echinacea purpurea tea 3 times a day.
  2. Start with low dosage of 1 teaspoon per cup and adjust as needed.
  3. Use 2-3 cups per day for 5-7 days to relieve runny nose symptoms.
  4. Take breaks for 2-3 days and restart if symptoms persist.
  5. Consult a healthcare professional for prolonged use or severe symptoms.

Echinacea purpurea teas can help alleviate symptoms of a runny nose, but they may also cause side effects such as stomach upset, diarrhea, and allergic reactions in some individuals.

Precautions to take when using Echinacea purpurea teas include starting with a low dose to assess tolerance, avoiding it with other immunomodulatory herbs, and being mindful of potential interactions with medications.

4. Zingiber officinale teas

Zingiber officinale teas, derived from the rhizome of the ginger plant, have been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of a runny nose.

The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of this herbal preparation help to reduce nasal congestion and combat underlying infections that can cause this condition.

The bioactive constituents, including gingerols and shogaols, exhibit expectorant and mucolytic activities, facilitating the expulsion of mucus and relieving sinus pressure.

Regular consumption of Zingiber officinale teas can provide relief from a runny nose, promoting a healthy respiratory system and overall well-being.

Related Study

According to the Journal of ethnopharmacology, Zingiber officinale teas have been traditionally used to cure various diseases including cold and respiratory disorders, suggesting that they may help alleviate symptoms associated with a runny nose.

  2. Boil the water in a pot and add the grated ginger root.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the liquid into a cup and discard the ginger root.
  5. Add honey to taste, if desired, and drink warm.

Usage:

  1. Consult doctor to confirm runny nose is not a symptom of an underlying condition.
  2. Drink 1 cup of Zingiber officinale tea, 3 times a day, for 3 days.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of Zingiber officinale powder to warm water or tea, 2 times a day.
  4. Increase dosage to 2 cups of tea or 2 teaspoons of powder, if symptoms persist.
  5. Monitor and adjust usage as directed by doctor or herbalist, for up to 7 days.

Zingiber officinale teas can help alleviate the symptoms of a runny nose, but it may also cause side effects such as stomach upset, heartburn, and diarrhea due to its high ginger content.

To use this herbal preparation safely, take it in moderation, especially with an empty stomach, and avoid consuming it before or after surgery as it may interfere with blood clotting.
