Tea tree essential oils helps with fungal infection because of its powerful antifungal properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic tea tree essential oil for fungal infection.

  1. Harvest fresh leaves of melaleuca alternifolia plants at dawn on a dry day, using clean scissors and gloves.
  2. Dry the harvested leaves in a warm dark place for 7-10 days, stirring daily to ensure even drying.
  3. Place 100g of dried melaleuca alternifolia leaves in a glass jar with a lid and cover them with 200ml of high-proof vodka.
  4. Allow the mixture to steep for 14-21 days at room temperature, shaking the jar daily to facilitate infusion.
  5. Strain the liquid through a coffee filter or cheesecloth into another glass jar, discarding the solids and storing the resulting essential oil in an amber bottle with a tight-fitting lid.
