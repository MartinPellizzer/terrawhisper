7 Herbal Teas For Phlegm

Herbal teas have been used for centuries to help relieve phlegm, and for good reason.

Phlegm is a thick, sticky mucus that can build up in your lungs and airways, causing congestion and difficulty breathing. Herbal teas work by loosening and thinning this mucus, making it easier to expel from your body. One example of an herbal tea that can help with this is ginger tea, made from the root of the Zingiber officinale plant. Ginger has natural anti-inflammatory properties that help to reduce swelling in the airways and loosen phlegm.

Another example is eucalyptus tea, made from the leaves of the Eucalyptus globulus plant. Eucalyptus has a decongestant effect, helping to clear out mucus and make it easier to breathe. Licorice root tea, made from the root of the Glycyrrhiza glabra plant, is also a good option. Licorice root has anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe and calm the airways, reducing the production of phlegm. Drinking these teas can help to alleviate symptoms of phlegm, such as congestion, coughing, and difficulty breathing.

By using herbal teas to relieve phlegm, you can feel more comfortable and able to breathe easily, which can improve your overall quality of life.

1. Zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale teas contains active compounds like gingerols and shogaols, which have natural expectorant properties.

These properties help to loosen and clear mucus and phlegm from the lungs, making it easier to cough up and breathe. The warming and anti-inflammatory effects of ginger also help to reduce congestion and soothe a sore throat. To use Zingiber officinale teas for phlegm, you can try the following: Start by brewing a cup of ginger tea by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of fresh ginger in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink it 2-3 times a day, ideally before meals.

You can also add honey and lemon to taste. For better results, try combining ginger tea with other natural expectorants like thyme or eucalyptus. When you take action with Zingiber officinale teas, you may notice a reduction in congestion and mucus production within a few hours. The tea may help you cough up phlegm more easily, and you may feel a sense of relief in your chest and throat. Regular consumption of ginger tea may also help to prevent future episodes of phlegm and congestion.

However, if your symptoms persist or worsen, consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

2. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus teas contains a potent blend of compounds, including eucalyptol, a natural decongestant, and menthol, which helps to break down and clear mucus.

When you drink Eucalyptus globulus tea, the eucalyptol is absorbed into your bloodstream, where it reaches your lungs and sinuses, helping to reduce inflammation and thin out phlegm. This makes it easier to cough up and expel, providing quick relief from congestion.

To use Eucalyptus globulus tea for phlegm, start by drinking 1-2 cups a day, ideally before bedtime, as it can help you sleep better and clear out your airways while you rest. You can also inhale the steam from a hot cup of tea or add a few drops of eucalyptus oil to a humidifier to help loosen up phlegm. As you continue to drink the tea, you may notice that your cough becomes less frequent and your breathing becomes easier, allowing you to sleep better and feel more energized during the day.

Your sinuses may also become less congested, and you may be able to breathe more easily through your nose.

3. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Glycyrrhiza glabra teas contain compounds like glycyrrhizic acid and flavonoids that help to reduce inflammation and congestion in the body.

These properties make it effective in clearing phlegm and mucus, which can clog the airways and make breathing difficult. The flavonoids in Glycyrrhiza glabra teas have anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties that help to soothe the throat and lungs, reducing the production of excess mucus. If you're looking to use Glycyrrhiza glabra teas for phlegm, start by steeping 1-2 teaspoons of dried roots in a cup of boiling water for 5-7 minutes. You can drink it 2-3 times a day, depending on your body's needs.

It's also recommended to combine it with other herbs like thyme or eucalyptus to enhance its decongestant properties. When you take Glycyrrhiza glabra teas for phlegm, you may notice a decrease in mucus production and a reduction in congestion. If you experience any adverse reactions or have underlying health conditions, consult with a healthcare professional before using Glycyrrhiza glabra teas.

Thymus vulgaris teas contains thymol and carvacrol, compounds that help break down and expel phlegm from the body.

Strain the tea and drink it two to three times a day.

When taking Thymus vulgaris teas for phlegm, you may experience a reduction in congestion and coughing within a few days. Your airways may feel clearer, and you may be able to breathe more easily. Echinacea purpurea teas contains compounds like alkylamides, glycosides, and phenolic acids that help reduce phlegm production.

Rosmarinus officinalis teas contains compounds like camphor, borneol, and bornyl acetate, which have natural decongestant properties that help to break down and clear excess mucus from the airways.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for phlegm?

Drinking herbal tea for phlegm is best done in the morning or early afternoon.

The herbs can then work throughout the day to keep your airways clear and your respiratory system healthy.
