1. Marshmallow tea

Marshmallow tea helps with phlegm because it contains mucilage, a type of soluble fiber that becomes slippery when mixed with water.

This slippery substance coats the throat and soothes irritation, reducing the urge to cough and clear phlegm. Additionally, marshmallow tea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract, making it easier to expel phlegm. The tea also has expectorant properties, which can help loosen and thin mucus, making it easier to cough up and expel.

Overall, marshmallow tea can provide relief for people suffering from respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, coughs, and colds.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in marshmallow tea that aid with phlegm.

  • Expectorant: The mucilage in marshmallow tea also has expectorant properties, helping to loosen and expel phlegm from the respiratory tract, making it easier to cough up and expel.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Marshmallow tea contains flavonoids and phenolic acids that exhibit anti-inflammatory effects, reducing inflammation in the respiratory tract and alleviating symptoms associated with phlegm.
  • Antitussive: The soothing effect of marshmallow tea on the respiratory tract can help suppress coughing, which is often triggered by the presence of phlegm.
  • Antimicrobial: Marshmallow tea has antimicrobial properties due to the presence of tannins and other compounds, which can help prevent infections that may exacerbate phlegm production and respiratory issues.

The list below shows the primary parts of marshmallow tea used to make teas for phlegm.

  • Roots: The roots of the marshmallow plant contain mucilage, which helps soothe irritated throats and loosen phlegm.
  • Leaves: The leaves of the marshmallow plant also contain mucilage and have anti-inflammatory properties, making them effective in treating phlegm.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the marshmallow plant contain flavonoids and polysaccharides, which have expectorant and anti-tussive properties, making them useful in treating phlegm.
  • Seeds: The seeds of the marshmallow plant are rich in mucilage and have a demulcent effect, making them helpful in soothing irritated throats and reducing phlegm.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic marshmallow tea for phlegm.

  1. In a pot, combine 1 quart of water, 2 tablespoons of dried marshmallow root, and 1 tablespoon of dried thyme; bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat, add 1 tablespoon of dried mullein leaves, and let it steep for an additional 10 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea to remove solids, then add honey to taste while the tea is still warm.
  5. Drink the tea throughout the day, up to three times daily, to help alleviate phlegm.
