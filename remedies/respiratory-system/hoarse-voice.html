Hoarse Voice: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

A hoarse voice, also known as dysphonia, is a condition characterized by a raspy, rough, or strained sound when speaking.

It can be caused by various factors, including vocal strain, laryngitis, acid reflux, allergies, and respiratory infections. Prolonged hoarseness can lead to breathing difficulties, throat pain, and fatigue. To alleviate hoarseness, herbal remedies such as slippery elm, licorice root, and marshmallow root can be used in teas, infusions, or lozenges.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional and follow proper preparation and dosage guidelines to avoid adverse reactions or interactions with medications.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of hoarse voice, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of hoarse voice?

The main causes of hoarse voice are inflammation, irritation, or damage to the vocal cords, which can be caused by factors such as overuse or misuse of the voice, vocal strain from screaming, loud talking, or singing, smoking, excessive drinking, acid reflux, infections like laryngitis or tonsillitis, or exposure to pollutants like dust, chemicals, or loud noises.

Viral infections such as the common cold or flu can also cause hoarseness due to the inflammation they trigger in the throat. In addition, certain medical conditions like thyroid problems, Parkinson's disease, or a tumor on the vocal cords can also cause hoarseness. Sometimes, hoarseness can be caused by a sudden change in voice due to a neurological condition or a side effect of certain medications.

Furthermore, allergies, sinusitis, or a postnasal drip can also irritate the vocal cords and cause hoarseness.

  • Laryngitis: Laryngitis is the most common cause of hoarse voice due to inflammation of the vocal cords, typically caused by a viral or bacterial infection, which results in redness, swelling, and a decrease in the vocal cord's ability to vibrate, producing a hoarse or raspy sound.
  • Postnasal Drip: Postnasal drip, caused by excess mucus in the throat due to allergies, colds, or sinus infections, irritates the vocal cords, causing inflammation and a hoarse voice.
  • Acid Reflux: Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), also known as acid reflux, causes stomach acid to flow up into the throat, irritating the vocal cords and leading to a hoarse voice.
  • Vocal Strain: Vocal strain, caused by overusing the voice, singing, or shouting, can lead to vocal cord fatigue and inflammation, resulting in a hoarse voice.
  • Allergies: Allergies, particularly those causing postnasal drip, can irritate the vocal cords and lead to a hoarse voice.
  • Tonsillitis: Tonsillitis, an inflammation of the tonsils, can cause a hoarse voice due to the spread of infection to the vocal cords.
  • Mononucleosis: Mononucleosis, also known as glandular fever, can cause a hoarse voice due to the inflammation of the lymph nodes and vocal cords.
  • Thyroid Disorders: Thyroid disorders, such as hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism, can cause vocal cord changes and a hoarse voice due to the increased or decreased production of thyroid hormones.
  • Smoking: Smoking can cause a hoarse voice due to the inflammation and scarring of the vocal cords, as well as the production of chemicals that irritate the vocal cords.
  • Aging: Aging can cause a hoarse voice due to the natural wear and tear on the vocal cords, as well as the loss of muscle mass and flexibility.
