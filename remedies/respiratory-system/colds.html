What is colds and how it affects your life?

A cold is a viral infection that primarily affects the nose and throat.

It is a common illness that can cause various unpleasant symptoms, such as a runny or stuffy nose, sore throat, cough, and sneezing. In addition, people with colds may also experience fatigue, headache, and body aches.

These symptoms can significantly impact a person's daily life, making it difficult to concentrate, complete tasks, or participate in usual activities. For example, a stuffy nose can make it hard to breathe, sleep, or taste food, while a persistent cough can disrupt sleep and cause discomfort. Furthermore, colds can also lead to missed work or school days, and in some cases, can even progress to more severe illnesses such as pneumonia or bronchitis.

Overall, a cold can be a significant inconvenience and can negatively affect a person's physical, emotional, and social well-being.

What are the main causes of colds?

The main causes of colds are viruses, particularly rhinoviruses.

These viruses are highly contagious and can be transmitted through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes, or through physical contact with contaminated surfaces. People often mistake the common cold for the flu, but they are different illnesses.

While the flu is caused by influenza viruses, the common cold is caused by over 200 different viruses, with rhinoviruses being the most common. Other factors that can increase the risk of getting a cold include a weakened immune system, exposure to cold temperatures, and stress.

However, it's important to note that getting chilled or wet does not directly cause a cold, although these factors may weaken the immune system and make a person more susceptible to infection.

The most common causes of colds are listed below.

  • Rhinoviruses: These are the most common cause of the common cold, accounting for around 50% of all cases.
  • Coronaviruses: After rhinoviruses, coronaviruses are the second most common cause of colds, responsible for about 10-15% of cases.
  • Human parainfluenza viruses: These viruses can cause both the common cold and more severe respiratory infections.
  • Adenoviruses: These viruses can cause a wide range of illnesses, including the common cold, bronchitis, and pneumonia.
  • Respiratory syncytial virus (RSV): While RSV is best known for causing respiratory infections in young children, it can also cause colds in adults.
  • Enteroviruses: These viruses can cause a variety of illnesses, including the common cold, hand, foot, and mouth disease, and conjunctivitis.
  • Human metapneumovirus: This virus can cause both upper and lower respiratory tract infections, including colds.
  • Bocavirus: This virus is a common cause of respiratory tract infections in young children, including colds.
  • Human parechovirus: This virus can cause a range of illnesses, including colds, meningitis, and pneumonia.
  • Influenza viruses: While influenza viruses are most commonly associated with the flu, they can also cause symptoms similar to the common cold. However, influenza viruses are typically more severe than rhinoviruses and other common cold viruses.
