1. Echinacea purpurea teas

Echinacea purpurea teas have been traditionally used to help alleviate the symptoms of colds and flu, thanks to their immunomodulatory and anti-inflammatory properties.

This herbal preparation helps to treat the colds ailment by stimulating the immune system, reducing the severity and duration of symptoms, and protecting against infections.

The bioactive constituents of Echinacea purpurea teas, including alkylamides, caffeic acid derivatives, and polysaccharides, contribute to its therapeutic effects by enhancing the production of white blood cells and activating natural killer cells.

The benefits of using Echinacea purpurea teas to treat colds include reduced congestion, fever, and sore throat, as well as improved overall well-being and a shorter recovery time.

Related Study

According to the provided study, Echinacea purpurea teas for colds have been shown to reduce the total number of cold episodes, days with colds, and cold episodes requiring additional medication.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 2 cups of fresh or dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and leaves.
  2. Measure 1 tablespoon of the Echinacea mixture and add it to 1 cup of boiling water.
  3. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Strain the tea through a fine-mesh sieve into a cup. Discard the solids.
  5. Drink the tea warm, up to 3 times a day, when needed for cold relief.

Echinacea purpurea teas can be effective in treating colds, but some users may experience side effects such as nausea, stomach upset, or allergic reactions like hives or itching, particularly when consumed in large amounts or for extended periods.

To minimize potential risks, users should start with small doses, gradually increase as needed, and be cautious of interactions with other medications or health conditions, such as high blood pressure or allergies.

2. Eucalyptus globulus teas

Eucalyptus globulus teas are a popular herbal remedy used to treat colds and flu due to their decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The menthol and eucalyptol present in this herbal preparation help to relieve congestion and open up airways, making it easier to breathe.

Bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes contribute to its antimicrobial and antiviral properties, which aid in fighting off the underlying infection.

By using Eucalyptus globulus teas, individuals can benefit from its ability to reduce coughing, fever, and body aches, ultimately promoting faster recovery from colds and flu.

Related Study

According to "Evidence-based complementary and alternative medicine : eCAM", Eucalyptus globulus teas for colds are mentioned as one of the top-cited plant species used in the treatment of respiratory tract infections (RTIs), with a frequency of citation (FOC) of 6.8%.

Recipe:

  1. Get 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Take 5-7 fresh eucalyptus leaves or 1 teaspoon of dried eucalyptus leaves.
  3. Add the eucalyptus leaves to 1 cup of boiling water.
  4. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the leaves.
  5. Drink the tea hot or cold, 2-3 times a day to help relieve cold symptoms.

Eucalyptus globulus teas can help alleviate cold symptoms, but possible side effects may include digestive issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications.

Precautions when using this herbal preparation include avoiding use in children and pregnant women, as well as taking it in moderation to prevent over-stimulation and potential respiratory issues.

3. Zingiber officinale teas

Zingiber officinale teas, also known as ginger tea, have been traditionally used to treat the common cold and its associated symptoms such as congestion and cough.

The properties of this herbal preparation help to treat the cold by reducing inflammation, relieving pain, and loosening mucus, making it easier to expel.

The bioactive constituents of ginger tea, including gingerols and shogaols, possess anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that help to alleviate cold symptoms.

The benefits of using ginger tea to treat the cold include its ability to soothe a sore throat, reduce congestion, and promote relaxation, making it a popular natural remedy for cold relief.

Related Study

According to "Pharmaceuticals (Basel, Switzerland)", Zingiber officinale teas for colds may be used as a therapy or complementary self-treatment, relieving symptoms such as fever, nasal congestion, and sneezing.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 tablespoon of dried ginger root and 1 cup of boiling water.
  2. Grate the dried ginger root to release its flavor and aroma.
  3. Add the grated ginger to the boiling water and stir well.
  4. Let the mixture steep for 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to combine.
  5. Strain the tea and drink it while it's still warm to help relieve cold symptoms.

Zingiber officinale teas can be beneficial in alleviating cold symptoms, but they may cause stomach upset and heartburn in some individuals, particularly those with pre-existing digestive issues, due to its spicy and warming properties.

To minimize potential side effects, it is recommended to consume Zingiber officinale teas in moderation, especially on an empty stomach, and to avoid taking it with other spicy or acidic foods.

4. Sambucus nigra teas

Sambucus nigra teas, also known as elderberry tea, have been traditionally used to treat colds and flu due to their antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties.

The bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, anthocyanins, and phenolic acids, help to combat the infection by inhibiting the replication of the virus and reducing inflammation in the body.

By consuming elderberry tea, individuals can experience relief from cold symptoms such as fever, cough, and sore throat, and may also benefit from reduced duration and severity of the illness.

The benefits of using elderberry tea to treat colds include its natural and non-invasive approach, making it a popular choice among those seeking a holistic remedy for this common ailment.

Related Study

According to "Phytotherapy research : PTR", Sambucus nigra teas for colds have been shown to contain antiviral and antimicrobial properties, which may be beneficial in treating viral infections, including influenza.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 1 cup of fresh or dried Sambucus nigra flowers, or 1 teaspoon of dried flowers per cup of boiling water.
  2. Combine the flowers with 1 cup of boiling water in a saucepan or teapot.
  3. Let the mixture steep for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to release.
  4. Strain the tea into a cup using a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  5. Add honey to taste, if desired, and drink while warm to help soothe a cold.

Sambucus nigra teas can be effective in alleviating cold symptoms, but possible side effects include allergic reactions, such as hives, itching, or difficulty breathing, as well as digestive issues, like nausea and stomach cramps.

When using Sambucus nigra teas to treat colds, it's essential to take precautions, such as starting with small doses to monitor individual tolerance and potential interactions with other medications, and being cautious when consuming Sambucus nigra teas with certain health conditions, like high blood pressure.

Thymus vulgaris teas, also known as thyme tea, have been used for centuries to treat colds due to their antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties.

Thymus vulgaris tea contains bioactive constituents such as thymol, carvacrol, and rosmarinic acid, which have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria, viruses, and fungi, thus helping to alleviate symptoms of colds.

The benefits of Thymus vulgaris tea in treating colds include its ability to reduce the severity and duration of symptoms, promote a speedy recovery, and provide relief from congestion and coughs.
