1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus, also known as Tasmanian blue gum, essential oils helps with dry nose because of its decongestant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The oil's active compound, eucalyptol, helps to break down mucus, reducing congestion and promoting drainage. Its expectorant properties also aid in loosening and removing excess mucus from the nasal passages, providing quick relief from dryness and discomfort.

As a result, Eucalyptus globulus essential oil can be an effective natural remedy for soothing a dry and irritated nose.

Medicinal Constituents

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Eucalyptus globulus essential oils that help with dry nose.

  • Eucalyptol: This terpene acts as a natural expectorant, helping to loosen and clear mucus from the nasal passages, relieving congestion associated with dry nose.
  • Cineole: Similar to eucalyptol, cineole has expectorant properties that help to thin mucus and facilitate its removal from the nasal passages, providing relief from dry nose symptoms.
  • Α-pinene: This terpene has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce swelling and irritation in the nasal passages, thereby alleviating the discomfort and dryness associated with dry nose.

Parts Used

The list below shows the primary parts of tasmanian blue gum used to make essential oils for dry nose.

  • Leaves: They are the most commonly used part for essential oil production due to their high eucalyptol content, which helps in relieving congestion and soothing a dry nose.
  • Barks: Eucalyptus bark is used to create essential oils that help in decongesting the nasal passages and providing relief from dryness and irritation.
  • Stems: Some essential oil manufacturers use the stems of Eucalyptus globulus to create oils that aid in relieving respiratory issues and soothing a dry nose, although this is less common compared to leaves.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic tasmanian blue gum for dry nose.

  1. Harvest 15 kg of fresh eucalyptus globulus leaves from mature plants in early morning to ensure optimal oil content.
  2. Dry the eucalyptus leaves in a hot air oven at 40°c for 6 hours to reduce moisture levels.
  3. Steam distill the dried eucalyptus leaves at 100°c for 2 hours to extract the essential oil.
  4. Collect the essential oil and separate it from the water through decantation and centrifugation within 1 hour.
  5. Filter the essential oil through a 0.2 micron filter to remove any impurities and contaminants within 30 minutes.
