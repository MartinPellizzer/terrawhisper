10 best herbal teas for chronic bronchitis

1. Great mullein tea

Great mullein tea helps with chronic bronchitis because it contains mucilage, a substance that coats and soothes mucous membranes, providing relief to irritated throats and bronchial tubes.

It also has expectorant properties that help loosen and expel mucus from the lungs, making it easier to breathe. Additionally, mullein tea contains saponins, which have antiviral and antibacterial effects, potentially reducing the severity and duration of chronic bronchitis symptoms.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in great mullein tea that aid with chronic bronchitis.

  • Antibacterial: This herbal tea can help fight bacterial infections contributing to bronchitis, as it contains compounds like rotenone and verbascoside.
  • Demulcent: Great mullein tea forms a protective film over inflamed tissues, reducing irritation and fostering healing, because of its high mucilage content.

The list below shows the primary parts of great mullein tea used to make teas for chronic bronchitis.

  • Leaves: The leaves of the great mullein plant are commonly used to make medicinal teas for chronic bronchitis due to their anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.
  • Flowers: The flowers of the great mullein plant are used in teas to soothe irritated throats and reduce inflammation, common symptoms of chronic bronchitis.
  • Seeds: Mullein seeds are often used in teas for their mucilaginous properties, which can help to relieve congestion and coughing associated with chronic bronchitis.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic great mullein tea for chronic bronchitis.

  1. Gather 2-3 teaspoons of dried mullein leaves and carefully crush them to release their beneficial compounds.
  2. In a teapot or heat-resistant container, place the crushed mullein leaves and pour 8 ounces of boiling water over them.
  3. Allow the mixture to steep for 10-15 minutes to extract the medicinal properties of the mullein leaves.
  4. Strain the tea to remove the leaves, pour it into a cup, and let it cool down to a drinkable temperature.
  5. Drink 2-3 cups of this mullein tea daily, between meals, to help alleviate chronic bronchitis symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic indian tobacco tea for chronic bronchitis.

  1. Boil one cup of water and add one teaspoon of dried Indian tobacco leaves, letting it steep for 10-15 minutes.
  2. While the tea is steeping, chop one inch of fresh ginger root and one teaspoon of licorice root, then add them to the tea.
  3. After steeping, strain the tea to remove the solid particles.
  4. Add one teaspoon of honey and a squeeze of lemon juice for taste.
  5. Drink the tea while it is still warm, up to three times a day for best results.
