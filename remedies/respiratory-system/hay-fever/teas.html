1. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea teas contains properties that may help alleviate hay fever symptoms.

The tea is rich in flavonoids, alkylamides, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antihistamine effects. To use Echinacea purpurea teas for hay fever, steep one teaspoon of dried root in boiling water for 5-7 minutes. Strain the tea and drink 2-3 cups a day, ideally one cup in the morning and one in the evening.

It's essential to start consuming the tea a few weeks before the hay fever season begins to allow the body to build up its natural defenses. You can also combine Echinacea purpurea tea with other herbs, such as peppermint or ginger, to enhance its benefits and make it more palatable. When taken regularly, Echinacea purpurea teas may help reduce the severity and frequency of hay fever symptoms. If your symptoms are severe or persistent, consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.
