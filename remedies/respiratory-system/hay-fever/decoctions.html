1. Petasites hybridus

Butterbur decoctions helps with hay fever because its active compounds, including petasin and isopetasin, have natural anti-inflammatory properties that help alleviate nasal congestion and sinus pressure.

The decoction's flavonoids also work to reduce histamine release, which in turn reduces symptoms such as itching, sneezing, and runny nose.

Additionally, butterbur's antioxidant properties may help protect the respiratory tract from oxidative stress caused by airborne allergens, making it a natural and effective remedy for managing hay fever symptoms.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in butterbur decoctions that aid with hay fever.

  • Antihistamine and anti-inflammatory effects: Butterbur decoctions have been shown to inhibit the release of histamine from mast cells, reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms such as itching, sneezing, and congestion.
  • Spassmolytic properties: The compounds in butterbur, particularly petasin and isopetasin, have been found to relax bronchial smooth muscle, helping to relieve spasms and constriction of the airways, which can be exacerbated by hay fever.
  • Antioxidant activity: Butterbur decoctions contain antioxidants that help protect the body from oxidative stress caused by allergen exposure, reducing inflammation and damage to tissues in the respiratory tract.

The list below shows the primary parts of butterbur used to make decoctions for hay fever.

  • Roots: Rich in compounds that help to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of hay fever.
  • Rhizomes: Contain bioactive compounds that exhibit anti-allergic and anti-inflammatory properties, making them effective against hay fever.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic butterbur for hay fever.

  1. Harvest high-quality petasites hybridus roots and clean them thoroughly by rinsing under cold running water.
  2. Chop the cleaned petasites hybridus roots into small pieces using a sharp knife or mortar and pestle.
  3. Measure out 15-20 grams of the chopped petasites hybridus root material and combine it with one liter of boiling water in a large saucepan.
  4. Simmer the petasites hybridus mixture for 30 minutes, reducing the liquid by half to create a concentrated decoction.
  5. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean glass container and discard the solids.
