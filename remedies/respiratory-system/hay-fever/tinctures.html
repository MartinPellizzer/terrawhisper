Butterbur tinctures helps with hay fever because it has natural anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that reduce symptoms such as congestion, itching, and sneezing.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in butterbur tinctures that aid with hay fever.

  • Isovitexin: Isovitexin is a flavonoid that exhibits antihistamine-like effects, helping to reduce the release of histamine from mast cells and alleviate symptoms like itching, sneezing, and runny nose.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic butterbur tincture for hay fever.

  1. Harvest high-quality petasites hybridus roots and clean them thoroughly removing any dirt or debris.
  2. Chop the cleaned roots into small pieces and combine them with 80% vodka in a clean glass jar.

  4. Strain the liquid using cheesecloth or a coffee filter discarding the solids and transfer the tincture to dark glass bottles.
  5. Store the finished tincture in a cool dry place away from direct sunlight for at least 6 months before use.
