The list below shows the primary parts of yarrow tincture used to make tinctures for hay fever.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yarrow tincture for hay fever.

  1. Harvest aerial parts of yarrow (Achillea millefolium) plant, ensuring they are in full bloom, and dry them in a dark, well-ventilated area for 1-2 weeks.
  2. Chop dried yarrow material into small pieces and fill a clean jar up to half, then add 80 proof or higher alcohol (vodka works well) until the plant material is completely submerged.
  3. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks, shaking it gently every few days.
  4. After the maceration period, strain the liquid through a cheesecloth or a fine mesh strainer, squeezing out the plant material to extract as much liquid as possible.
  5. Store the final yarrow tincture in a dark glass bottle, in a cool, dark place, and take 20-30 drops up to three times a day for hay fever relief.
