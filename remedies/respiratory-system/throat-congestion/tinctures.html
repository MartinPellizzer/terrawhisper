1. Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus globulus tinctures contains compounds like eucalyptol, a primary component responsible for its decongestant properties.

Eucalyptol works by loosening and thinning mucus, making it easier to expel from the body, which in turn helps to relieve congestion in the throat. This is because eucalyptol has natural expectorant properties, which helps to break down and clear out excess mucus and phlegm. When you apply eucalyptus tincture to your throat, the eucalyptol is absorbed through your skin and into your bloodstream, where it can then reach your airways and help to ease congestion. To use eucalyptus globulus tincture for throat congestion, apply a few drops to your neck or chest, then massage it gently into your skin.

You can also add a few drops to a warm bath or inhale the vapors from a bowl of hot water with a few drops of the tincture. When you inhale the vapors, the eucalyptol can reach your lungs and airways directly, further helping to relieve congestion. As you use the tincture, you should start to feel your throat congestion easing, and you may notice that you can breathe more easily and that your cough becomes less frequent. The eucalyptol in the tincture can also help to reduce inflammation and soothe an itchy or sore throat, making it easier to swallow and speak. It's essential to note that eucalyptus tincture can cause skin irritation in some people, so start with a small amount and test it on a small area of skin before using it extensively.

Also, if your symptoms persist or worsen, consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.
