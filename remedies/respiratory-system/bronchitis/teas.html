1. Great mullein tea

Great mullein tea helps with bronchitis because it contains mucilage, a substance that forms a soothing film over the mucous membranes in the respiratory tract, providing relief from irritation and inflammation.

It also has expectorant properties that help loosen and expel mucus from the lungs, making it easier to breathe. Additionally, mullein contains saponins, which have been shown to have antiviral and antibacterial effects, potentially helping to reduce the severity and duration of bronchitis symptoms.

Furthermore, the tea is rich in antioxidants that help protect the body from damage caused by free radicals, which can exacerbate respiratory infections.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in great mullein tea that aid with bronchitis.

  • Expectorant: Great mullein tea helps clear mucus from the respiratory tract by stimulating the expulsion of phlegm, providing relief from bronchitis symptoms. This property is due to the presence of mucilage and saponins.
  • Anti-inflammatory: The tea can reduce inflammation in the airways, alleviating irritation and swelling associated with bronchitis, thanks to its flavonoids and iridoid glycosides content.
  • Antitussive: Great mullein tea can soothe coughs and suppress spasms, easing discomfort caused by persistent coughing in bronchitis, due to its demulcent and mild analgesic properties.
  • Antimicrobial: The tea may inhibit the growth of bacteria and viruses, contributing to the prevention and treatment of bronchitis caused by infections, as a result of its verbascoside and other phenylethanoid glycosides.
  • Antioxidant: Great mullein tea can help protect lung tissue from oxidative damage, supporting overall respiratory health and recovery from bronchitis, owing to its high levels of various antioxidant compounds.

The list below shows the primary parts of great mullein tea used to make teas for bronchitis.

  • Leaves: The leaves of great mullein are commonly used to make medicinal tea for bronchitis due to their soothing effect on the respiratory tract.
  • Flowers: Great mullein flowers are used in teas to help alleviate bronchitis symptoms, as they have anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.
  • Buds: The buds of the great mullein plant are used in teas for bronchitis because they contain mucilage, which can help soothe irritated membranes in the respiratory tract.
  • Stems: Although not as commonly used, the stems of great mullein can be included in teas for bronchitis, as they contain compounds with anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic great mullein tea for bronchitis.

  1. Pick 2-3 teaspoons of dried mullein leaves and flowers, or use 1 tea bag, and place them in a tea infuser or teapot.
  2. Boil 8 ounces of water and pour it over the mullein herb, allowing it to steep for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Cover the teapot or cup to preserve the volatile oils during the steeping process.
  4. After the steeping time, remove the tea infuser or tea bag and optionally add honey or lemon to taste.
  5. Drink the mullein tea 2-3 times a day, as needed, to help soothe bronchitis symptoms.

Peppermint and ginger teas can alleviate symptoms of congestion, sore throat, and coughing. Chamomile tea has calming effects and can help with sleep disturbances caused by respiratory issues.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using herbal teas as a treatment for any medical condition.
