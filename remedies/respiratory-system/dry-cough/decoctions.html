1. Glycyrrhiza glabra

Licorice decoctions helps with dry cough because it provides a soothing and protective coating to the throat, reducing irritation and inflammation.

The glycyrrhizin present in licorice root has anti-inflammatory properties that help to calm the mucous membranes, allowing for easier removal of phlegm and mucus. This helps to relieve congestion and dryness, providing quick relief from uncomfortable coughing fits.

  • Expectorant and demulcent properties: The glycyrrhizin present in licorice root helps to loosen and clear mucus from the airways, while its demulcent properties create a protective barrier that soothes and shields the mucous membranes, making it easier to expel phlegm and relieve coughing.

The list below shows the primary parts of licorice decoction used to make decoctions for dry cough.

  • Roots: Rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound that helps soothe and moisturize the throat, providing relief from dry cough symptoms.
  • Rhyzomes: Contain bioactive compounds like licoricidin, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce coughing and congestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic licorice decoction for dry cough.

  1. Harvest high-quality glycyrrhiza glabra roots in the spring or fall, ensuring they are free from contaminants and mold.
  2. Clean and chop the harvested roots into small pieces, measuring approximately 10-15 grams per serving.
  3. Boil one quart of filtered water and add the chopped roots, allowing them to simmer for 20-30 minutes or until the liquid reaches a volume of about half a quart.
  4. Strain the decoction through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve into a clean container, discarding the solids and retaining the liquid.
  5. Store the decoction in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or freeze it for later use, reheating as needed before consumption.
