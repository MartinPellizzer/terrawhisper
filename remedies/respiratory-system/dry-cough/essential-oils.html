Purple coneflower essential oils helps with dry cough because of its natural anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The oil, extracted from the flowers of Echinacea purpurea, contains compounds such as cichoric acid and echinacin that soothe irritated throats and reduce inflammation. The list below shows the primary active constituents in purple coneflower essential oils that aid with dry cough.

  • Antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities: These properties can help reduce inflammation in the throat and airways, which can contribute to alleviating symptoms of dry cough.
  • Immune system support: Echinacea has been shown to stimulate the immune system, which may help boost the body's natural defense against respiratory infections that can cause dry cough.

The list below shows the primary parts of purple coneflower essential oil used to make essential oils for dry cough.

  • Roots: Rich in anti-inflammatory compounds, making them effective in soothing and calming irritated mucous membranes associated with dry cough.
  • Seeds: Containing high levels of fatty acids, seeds help to reduce inflammation and alleviate cough symptoms.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic purple coneflower essential oil for dry cough.

  1. Harvest 50 grams of fresh echinacea purpurea flowers at dawn on a sunny day when dew is still present.
  2. Steam-distill the harvested flowers using 200 ml of water and a distillation apparatus for 30 minutes.
  3. Collect 10 ml of essential oil from the distillation process, which is rich in alkamides and phenolic acids.
  4. Add 5 drops of the collected essential oil to 20 ml of carrier oil such as sweet almond or grapeseed oil with a ratio of 1:4.
  5. Store the finished herbal essential oil blend in dark glass bottles with tight-fitting lids, away from direct sunlight, for up to 6 months.
