Ear Blockage: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Ear blockage, also known as ear congestion or ear fullness, is a condition characterized by a feeling of blockage or pressure in the ear, often accompanied by discomfort or pain.

It can be caused by factors such as excessive earwax, allergies, sinus infections, colds, or even a side effect of certain medications. If left untreated, ear blockage can lead to hearing loss, vertigo, or even more severe complications. To alleviate ear blockage, herbal remedies like garlic oil, tea tree oil, and eucalyptus oil can be used in the form of ear drops, ear baths, or compresses.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using any medicinal herbs, especially if you have a perforated eardrum or are allergic to certain substances, and to follow proper precautions to avoid further irritation or infection.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of ear blockage, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of ear blockage?

The main causes of ear blockage are often attributed to a combination of anatomical, physiological, and pathological factors.

Eustachian tube dysfunction, which prevents the normal exchange of air and pressure in the middle ear, is a common cause of ear blockage. Middle ear infections, such as otitis media, can cause fluid buildup and blockage in the ear. Allergies, sinus infections, and colds can also lead to ear blockage due to the production of excess mucus.

Additionally, earwax buildup and nasal congestion can further contribute to ear blockage by obstructing the ear canal and Eustachian tube.

  • Eustachian Tube Dysfunction: Eustachian tube dysfunction occurs when the tube that regulates air pressure in the middle ear becomes blocked, swollen, or fails to open properly, leading to a buildup of fluid and mucus that can block the ear.
  • Earwax Buildup: Earwax buildup occurs when the production of earwax in the ear canal exceeds its normal clearance rate, causing a blockage that prevents sound from entering the ear.
  • Allergies: Allergies can cause the nasal passages and sinuses to become inflamed, leading to postnasal drip that can flow into the Eustachian tube and cause ear blockage.
  • Cold or Sinus Infection: A cold or sinus infection can cause the nasal passages and sinuses to become inflamed, leading to postnasal drip that can flow into the Eustachian tube and cause ear blockage.
  • Swimmer's Ear: Swimmer's ear, also known as otitis externa, occurs when bacteria or fungi infect the ear canal, causing inflammation and blockage.
  • Middle Ear Infection: Middle ear infection, also known as otitis media, occurs when bacteria or viruses infect the middle ear, causing fluid buildup and blockage.
  • Anatomical Abnormalities: Anatomical abnormalities, such as a narrow ear canal or a deviated septum, can cause ear blockage by restricting the flow of air and mucus.
  • Tumors: Tumors can cause ear blockage by growing in the ear canal or middle ear and blocking the normal flow of air and mucus.
  • Foreign Objects: Foreign objects, such as cotton swabs or earplugs, can cause ear blockage by pushing earwax or other debris deeper into the ear canal.
  • Aging: Aging can cause ear blockage by reducing the production of earwax and changing the shape of the ear canal, making it more prone to blockage.
  • Genetic Disorders: Genetic disorders, such as Down syndrome, can cause ear blockage by affecting the development of the ear and Eustachian tube.
