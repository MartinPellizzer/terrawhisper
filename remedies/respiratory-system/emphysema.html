It is primarily caused by long-term exposure to air pollutants, such as cigarette smoke, and genetic factors. This condition negatively impacts people's lives by limiting their physical activities, causing fatigue, and increasing the risk of respiratory infections.

What is emphysema and how it affects your life?

Emphysema is a chronic lung condition that is characterized by shortness of breath, coughing, and wheezing.

It is a type of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) that causes damage to the air sacs in the lungs, making it difficult to breathe. As the disease progresses, it can have a significant negative impact on a person's quality of life. For example, individuals with emphysema may experience fatigue and lack of energy due to the constant struggle to breathe.

They may also have difficulty sleeping, which can lead to insomnia and other sleep disorders. Emphysema can make it difficult to perform daily activities, such as climbing stairs, carrying groceries, or even walking short distances. It can also increase the risk of respiratory infections, such as pneumonia and bronchitis. Furthermore, emphysema can lead to social isolation, as individuals may avoid social situations due to fear of experiencing shortness of breath or coughing fits.

Overall, emphysema can significantly impair a person's physical, emotional, and social well-being, making it a challenging and debilitating condition to manage.

What are the main causes of emphysema?

The main causes of emphysema are long-term exposure to air pollution, chemical fumes, and mostly, cigarette smoke.

These substances damage the air sacs in the lungs, making it harder for the individual to breathe. When someone smokes, the tiny hair-like structures in the lungs, called cilia, become paralyzed and cannot effectively clean out mucus and dirt. Over time, the air sacs lose their elasticity, leading to the classic symptoms of emphysema such as shortness of breath and coughing.

Additionally, genetic factors can also play a role in the development of emphysema, particularly in cases of alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency.

The most common causes of emphysema are listed below.

  • Smoking: By far the most common cause of emphysema, smoking damages the air sacs in the lungs, making it difficult to breathe.
  • Secondhand smoke: Exposure to secondhand smoke can also cause emphysema, even for nonsmokers.
  • Air pollution: Long-term exposure to polluted air, such as that found in some workplaces or cities, can increase the risk of emphysema.
  • Occupational dusts and chemicals: Certain occupations, such as coal mining, construction, and farming, can expose individuals to dusts and chemicals that can lead to emphysema.
  • Age: While emphysema can develop at any age, it is more commonly diagnosed in older adults.
  • Genetics: A rare genetic disorder called alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency can cause emphysema, even in nonsmokers.
  • Frequent lower respiratory infections: Repeated respiratory infections, particularly in childhood, can increase the risk of developing emphysema.
  • Poor nutrition: A diet lacking in essential nutrients, particularly antioxidants, can increase the risk of emphysema.
  • Sedentary lifestyle: Lack of physical activity can contribute to the development of emphysema.
  • Socioeconomic factors: Lower socioeconomic status, often associated with poorer access to healthcare and unhealthier living conditions, can increase the risk of emphysema.
