Dry Throat: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Dry throat, also known as pharyngitis or xerostomia, refers to a condition characterized by an abnormally dry and irritated throat, often accompanied by a persistent cough and hoarseness.

The causes of dry throat can be attributed to environmental factors such as dry air, excessive screaming or talking, and consumption of hot beverages, as well as underlying health conditions like diabetes, sinus infections, and thyroid disorders. Prolonged dry throat can lead to complications such as vocal cord strain, throat ulcers, and increased susceptibility to respiratory infections.

To alleviate dry throat, herbal remedies like slippery elm, marshmallow root, and licorice root can be used to soothe and moisturize the throat. These herbs can be consumed as teas or added to warm water to create a soothing drink. However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, especially if you have any underlying health conditions or allergies.

Furthermore, individuals with high blood pressure or kidney disease should exercise caution when consuming licorice root, as it may interact with medications or exacerbate existing conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of dry throat, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of dry throat?

The main causes of dry throat are dehydration, which can be caused by inadequate water intake or excessive sweating, often due to physical activity or hot weather.

Infections such as a cold, flu, or sinusitis can also contribute to dryness in the throat, as they can cause inflammation and mucus production. Other factors include allergies, smoking, and exposure to pollutants like dust, smoke, and chemicals, which can dry out the mucous membranes in the throat. Additionally, certain medications like decongestants, antihistamines, and pain relievers can dry out the throat as a side effect.

Furthermore, a lack of humidity in the air, often in air-conditioned spaces, can also contribute to dry throat.

  • Dehydration: Dehydration is the most common cause of dry throat due to the insufficient amount of water in the body, which leads to a decrease in saliva production and a dryness in the throat.
  • Allergies: Allergies are a common cause of dry throat as they trigger the production of excess mucus, which can cause the throat to feel dry and irritated.
  • Air pollution: Air pollution is a common cause of dry throat due to the presence of airborne particles and chemicals that can dry out the mucous membranes in the throat.
  • Smoking: Smoking is a common cause of dry throat due to the chemicals and tar in cigarette smoke that can dry out the mucous membranes in the throat and reduce saliva production.
  • Acid reflux: Acid reflux is a common cause of dry throat as the stomach acid that flows up into the throat can cause irritation and dryness.
  • Medications: Medications such as decongestants, antihistamines, and blood pressure medications can cause dry mouth and dry throat as a side effect.
  • Hormonal changes: Hormonal changes during menopause, pregnancy, or thyroid disorders can cause dry mouth and dry throat due to changes in saliva production.
  • Anxiety and stress: Anxiety and stress can cause dry mouth and dry throat due to the increased production of stress hormones that can reduce saliva production.
  • Asthma: Asthma is a common cause of dry throat due to the inflammation and irritation of the airways that can cause the throat to feel dry and irritated.
  • Colds and flu: Colds and flu are common causes of dry throat due to the inflammation and irritation of the mucous membranes in the throat caused by viral infections.
