Acute Bronchitis: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Acute bronchitis is a common respiratory condition characterized by inflammation and irritation of the bronchial tubes, leading to a persistent cough, often accompanied by mucus production and difficulty breathing.

It is typically caused by viral infections, bacterial infections, or exposure to irritants such as pollution, tobacco smoke, and strong chemicals. If left untreated, acute bronchitis can lead to complications like pneumonia, bronchiectasis, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), compromising lung function and overall health. To alleviate symptoms, herbal remedies such as thyme, eucalyptus, and licorice root can be used in teas, tinctures, or inhalations.

However, it is crucial to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as some may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions, and to follow proper dosages and preparation guidelines to avoid adverse effects.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of acute bronchitis, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of acute bronchitis?

The main causes of acute bronchitis are respiratory tract infections, typically caused by viral pathogens such as adenovirus, coronavirus, influenza, and respiratory syncytial virus (RSV).

Bacterial infections, including Mycoplasma pneumoniae and Chlamydophila pneumoniae, can also trigger acute bronchitis. Exposure to tobacco smoke, air pollution, and strong chemicals can irritate the airways and increase the risk of developing acute bronchitis. Additionally, individuals with underlying conditions, such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), asthma, or heart disease, are more susceptible to acute bronchitis.

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), acute bronchitis is often a complication of common colds and flu, which are highly contagious and can spread through airborne droplets.

  • Viral Infection: Viral infections such as respiratory syncytial virus, adenovirus, and rhinovirus are the most common causes of acute bronchitis due to their ability to infect the cells lining the bronchial tubes and cause inflammation.
  • Bacterial Infection: Bacterial infections such as Streptococcus pneumoniae, Haemophilus influenzae, and Moraxella catarrhalis can also cause acute bronchitis, particularly in people with underlying health conditions or weakened immune systems.
  • Smoking: Smoking and exposure to secondhand smoke can cause acute bronchitis by damaging the cilia in the bronchial tubes and making it harder for the lungs to clear mucus, leading to inflammation and infection.
  • Allergies: Allergies such as allergic rhinitis and asthma can cause acute bronchitis by triggering an allergic reaction in the airways, leading to inflammation and increased mucus production.
  • Environmental Pollution: Environmental pollution such as exposure to air pollution, dust, and chemicals can cause acute bronchitis by irritating the bronchial tubes and causing inflammation.
  • Hypertension: Hypertension can cause acute bronchitis by increasing blood pressure in the lungs and causing inflammation in the bronchial tubes.
  • Other Medical Conditions: Other medical conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) can increase the risk of developing acute bronchitis by weakening the immune system and making it harder for the lungs to function properly.
