1. Thymus vulgaris

Thyme teas helps with asthma because of its natural expectorant properties, which help loosen and clear out mucus from the lungs.

The herb's antispasmodic compounds also relax the airway muscles, reducing inflammation and constriction that can trigger asthma attacks. By soothing and calming the airways, thyme teas can provide relief from wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath associated with asthma.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in thyme teas that aid with asthma.

  • Expectorant properties: Thyme tea helps to loosen and clear out mucus from the airways, making it easier to breathe for people with asthma.
  • Antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compounds: Thyme contains antioxidants like flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can help reduce inflammation in the lungs and airways, a common trigger of asthma symptoms.
  • Bronchodilatory effects: The essential oils present in thyme tea, such as thymol, have been shown to relax bronchial smooth muscle, helping to open up airways and improve airflow for people with asthma.

The list below shows the primary parts of thyme used to make teas for asthma.

  • Roots: Thyme roots are rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, making them effective in soothing bronchial inflammation and reducing asthma symptoms.
  • Leaves: Fresh thyme leaves contain a high concentration of essential oils, particularly thymol, which has natural decongestant and expectorant properties that help to ease respiratory congestion.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic thyme for asthma.

  1. Harvest 2 tablespoons of fresh thymus vulgaris leaves from your garden or store-bought supply for brewing.
  2. Rinse the harvested thyme leaves gently with filtered water to remove any dirt or debris thoroughly.
  3. Combine the rinsed thyme leaves with 8 ounces of boiling water in a heat-resistant cup or teapot to steep.
  4. Allow the mixture to infuse for 5-7 minutes, then strain and discard the solids before drinking the tea immediately.
  5. Consume the herbal thyme tea twice daily, preferably during meals, to alleviate asthma symptoms effectively within hours.
