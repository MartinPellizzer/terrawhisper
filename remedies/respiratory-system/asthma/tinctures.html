1. Yarrow tincture

Yarrow tincture helps with asthma because it contains anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties that can help alleviate asthma symptoms.

The herb's active compounds, such as lactones and flavonoids, work to reduce inflammation in the airways, thereby decreasing the likelihood of asthma attacks. The list below shows the primary active constituents in yarrow tincture that aid with asthma.

  • Bronchodilator: The bronchodilator effect of yarrow tincture, attributed to lactones like costunolide, aids in relaxing bronchial muscles, alleviating asthma symptoms.
  • Antispasmodic: Yarrow tincture's antispasmodic property, owing to alkaloids, assists in relieving spasms in the respiratory tract, minimizing asthma attacks.
  • Expectorant: The expectorant property of yarrow tincture, resulting from its volatile oils, helps clear mucus from the respiratory tract, improving asthma-related congestion.
  • Antioxidant: Yarrow tincture's antioxidant property, attributed to phenolic acids, aids in protecting lung tissue from oxidative stress, supporting overall lung health in asthma management.

The list below shows the primary parts of yarrow tincture used to make tinctures for asthma.

  • Flowers: Rich in flavonoids, flowers help reduce inflammation in the airways, making it easier to breathe.
  • Leaves: Contain anti-spasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties, helping to alleviate asthma symptoms by relaxing bronchial muscles.
  • Seeds: High in essential fatty acids, seeds can help reduce inflammation and support respiratory health.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic yarrow tincture for asthma.

  1. Harvest 2 ounces of fresh yarrow flowers and leaves, ensuring they are fully grown and free from pesticides.
  2. Rinse the herbs gently, then let them air dry or pat lightly with a clean cloth.
  3. Chop the yarrow into small pieces and place them in a clean, dry jar, filling it up to three-quarters capacity.
  4. Pour 10 ounces of 80-proof or higher alcohol (such as vodka) into the jar, ensuring all plant material is submerged.
  5. Seal the jar and store it in a cool, dark place for 4-6 weeks, shaking it gently once a day. After this period, strain the liquid and store the yarrow tincture in a tightly sealed glass container, away from light. Take 1-2 mL up to three times a day for asthma symptoms.
