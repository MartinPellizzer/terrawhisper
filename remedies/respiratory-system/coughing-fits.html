What is coughing fits and how it affects your life?

Coughing fits can be a debilitating and distressing experience that takes over one's daily life, causing significant disruptions to routines, relationships, and overall well-being.

A severe cough can strike at any moment, leaving you gasping for air, struggling to speak, and feeling helpless as you're overwhelmed by the uncontrollable urge to cough. This can lead to missed workdays, abandoned social plans, and even cancelled appointments with healthcare providers, resulting in mounting stress and anxiety. The constant interruptions from coughing fits can also disrupt sleep patterns, making it difficult to rest or recharge, and further exacerbating fatigue and irritability.

Additionally, the physical exhaustion and discomfort caused by prolonged coughing can lead to headaches, body aches, and sore throats, further complicating daily activities such as work, exercise, or even simple tasks like cooking or cleaning.

What are the main causes of coughing fits?

The main causes of coughing fits are typically related to respiratory issues, allergies, and infections.

Respiratory problems such as bronchitis, asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and pneumonia can cause severe coughing fits due to inflammation or infection in the airways. Allergies, especially to airborne particles like pollen, dust mites, or pet dander, can trigger coughing episodes when the body reacts to these substances. Additionally, viral and bacterial infections such as the common cold, sinusitis, and laryngitis can also cause persistent and severe coughing fits.

Furthermore, other factors like acid reflux, gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), and even some medications or irritants can contribute to coughing episodes.

The most common causes of coughing fits are listed below.

  • Acid reflux: Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) can cause stomach acid to flow back up into the throat, irritating the lining and triggering a cough.
  • Allergies: Exposure to allergens like dust, pollen, or pet dander can stimulate the nasal passages and sinuses, leading to postnasal drip and subsequent coughing fits.
  • Asthma: Airway constriction and inflammation in people with asthma can cause wheezing, shortness of breath, and a dry, hacking cough that may worsen at night or in response to triggers like cold air or exercise.
  • Bronchitis: Inflammation of the bronchi (airways) can lead to a persistent, productive cough (mucus-filled) accompanied by fever, wheezing, and shortness of breath.
  • Colds and flu: Respiratory viruses can cause inflammation and irritation in the throat and airways, leading to a dry, hacking cough that may persist for several days or weeks.
  • Emphysema: Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) can lead to lung damage and scarring, resulting in chronic bronchitis and coughing fits.
  • Lung infection: Pneumonia, pleurisy, and other bacterial or viral infections of the lungs can cause inflammation, irritation, and coughing fits that may persist for weeks or months.
  • Pertussis (whooping cough): A highly contagious bacterial infection characterized by a severe, persistent cough and potentially life-threatening complications in young children, adults over 65, and people with weakened immune systems.
  • Smoking: Cigarette smoking can damage the lungs and airways, leading to chronic bronchitis, emphysema, and coughing fits that may worsen over time.
  • Sinusitis: Inflammation of the paranasal sinuses can cause nasal congestion, pressure, and postnasal drip, which may lead to a persistent cough that is often worse at night or when lying down.
