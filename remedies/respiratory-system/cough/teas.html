13 Best Herbal Teas For Cough

Herbal teas for cough are a type of remedy made from the leaves, flowers, roots, or stems of plants, used to treat and soothe coughs due to their natural antiviral, antibacterial, and anti-inflammatory properties.

Some popular herbal teas for cough include peppermint, which helps to relax the throat muscles and reduce inflammation; ginger, which has anti-inflammatory properties that help to soothe the throat; licorice root, which has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that help to combat infection; thyme, which has antimicrobial properties that help to fight off germs and bacteria; and eucalyptus, which has decongestant properties that help to ease congestion and coughing.

Recipe:

  1. Gather 2 tablespoons of dried Echinacea purpurea flowers and roots.
  2. Measure 1 cup of boiling water in a teapot.
  3. Add the Echinacea purpurea mixture to the boiling water.
  4. Steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the mixture.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help soothe a cough.

Usage:

  1. Drink 1 cup of Echinacea purpurea tea 2-3 times a day.
  2. Start with 1 teaspoon of dried herb per cup of boiling water.
  3. Steep for 5-10 minutes, then strain the tea.
  4. Take Echinacea purpurea tea for 5-7 days to help reduce cough.
