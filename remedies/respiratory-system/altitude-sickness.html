Altitude Sickness: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Altitude sickness, also known as Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS), is a condition that occurs when the body cannot adapt quickly enough to high elevations, typically above 8,000 feet.

It's caused by a rapid decrease in oxygen levels, leading to symptoms such as headaches, fatigue, and nausea. If left untreated, AMS can have severe negative impacts on health, including respiratory problems and even death. To alleviate these symptoms, herbal remedies like Rhodiola rosea and Andean mint can be used to boost oxygenation and reduce inflammation.

However, it's essential to consult with a medical professional before using medicinal herbs, and to start with low doses to avoid adverse reactions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of altitude sickness, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of altitude sickness?

The main causes of altitude sickness are the rapid increase in atmospheric pressure, resulting in a decrease in available oxygen for the body to breathe.

At high elevations, the atmospheric pressure is lower, leading to a decrease in oxygen availability, which can cause the body's tissues to become hypoxic. Hypoxia triggers a series of physiological responses, including increased breathing rate, heart rate, and cardiac output, as the body attempts to compensate for the lower oxygen levels. Additionally, the decreased oxygen availability can lead to a buildup of lactic acid, which can exacerbate symptoms of altitude sickness, such as headaches and fatigue.

Research has shown that individuals who ascend to high altitudes too quickly are more likely to experience altitude sickness, with most cases occurring above 8,000 feet (2,438 meters) in elevation.

  • Rapid Ascent: Rapid ascent to high altitudes can cause altitude sickness due to the insufficient time for the body to acclimatize to the lower oxygen levels, which can lead to dehydration, headaches, and other symptoms.
  • High Altitude: High altitude itself is a primary cause of altitude sickness, as the air pressure and oxygen levels decrease with increasing elevation, making it difficult for the body to function normally.
  • Physical Conditioning: Poor physical conditioning can increase the risk of altitude sickness, as individuals with pre-existing health conditions or those who are not physically fit may be more susceptible to the effects of high altitude.
  • Sleep Deprivation: Sleep deprivation can exacerbate the symptoms of altitude sickness, as lack of sleep can impair the body's ability to adapt to the high altitude environment and increase the risk of dehydration and other complications.
  • Dehydration: Dehydration is a common cause of altitude sickness, as the dry air at high altitudes can cause excessive water loss, leading to headaches, fatigue, and other symptoms.
  • Genetic Predisposition: Some people may be genetically predisposed to altitude sickness due to differences in the way their bodies respond to high altitude, with certain genetic variants increasing the risk of developing altitude sickness.
  • Age: Older adults may be more susceptible to altitude sickness due to decreased lung function and other age-related health changes that can impair the body's ability to adapt to high altitude.
  • Pregnancy: Pregnant women may be more susceptible to altitude sickness due to the increased metabolic demand and decreased oxygen delivery to the fetus, which can increase the risk of complications.
  • Underlying Medical Conditions: Underlying medical conditions such as heart disease, lung disease, and anemia can increase the risk of altitude sickness, as these conditions can impair the body's ability to adapt to the high altitude environment.
  • Medication Use: Certain medications such as sedatives and antidepressants can increase the risk of altitude sickness by impairing the body's ability to adapt to the high altitude environment and increasing the risk of dehydration and other complications.
