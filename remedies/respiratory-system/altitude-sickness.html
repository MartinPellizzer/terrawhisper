What to know about altitude sickness before treating it with medicinal herbs

Altitude sickness, also known as Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS), occurs when the body fails to adapt to high altitudes, causing symptoms such as headache, nausea, fatigue, and shortness of breath.

If left untreated, AMS can lead to life-threatening complications. The causes of altitude sickness include rapid ascent, high elevations, and individual susceptibility.

What is altitude sickness and how it affects your life?

Altitude sickness, a debilitating condition that can strike at high elevations, leaves its victims struggling to perform even the simplest daily tasks.

At first, symptoms may seem like mere fatigue and mild headaches, but as the condition progresses, they become increasingly severe and debilitating. Sufferers may find themselves unable to sleep or concentrate, leaving them exhausted and irritable. Even everyday activities like walking up stairs can feel like climbing a mountain, as labored breathing and dizziness become the norm.

In extreme cases, altitude sickness can lead to more serious complications, such as chest tightness, nausea, and even seizures. This can render individuals unable to work or engage in social activities, severely impacting their mental and emotional well-being. Simple tasks like grocery shopping or cooking a meal can become daunting challenges, leaving sufferers feeling frustrated and demoralized. Even simple conversations with loved ones can be exhausting, as the constant fatigue and discomfort make it difficult to muster the energy to interact.

As altitude sickness takes hold, it can feel like a dark cloud is following you everywhere, casting a shadow over even the simplest aspects of life.

What are the main causes of altitude sickness?

The main causes of altitude sickness are a sudden increase in elevation, lack of acclimatization to high altitudes, and individual susceptibility.

When you ascend to higher elevations, the air pressure decreases, causing a decrease in oxygen levels. This can lead to altitude sickness, which is a condition where the body cannot adapt quickly enough to the lower oxygen levels. If you rapidly move from low to high altitudes, your body does not have time to adjust to the change, and this can cause symptoms such as headaches, nausea, fatigue, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, individual factors like age, physical fitness level, and pre-existing medical conditions can also affect the likelihood of developing altitude sickness.

The most common causes of altitude sickness are listed below.

  • Rapid Ascent: Going up to high elevations too quickly can cause altitude sickness. When you ascend rapidly, your body doesn't have enough time to acclimate to the lower air pressure and oxygen levels.
  • High Altitude Exposure: Spending extended periods at high elevations can increase the risk of altitude sickness. The longer you stay at high elevation, the more susceptible you become to its effects.
  • Physical Conditioning: People who are out of shape or have a low level of physical fitness are more prone to altitude sickness. Their bodies may not be able to adapt as quickly to the lower oxygen levels.
  • Age: Older adults are more susceptible to altitude sickness due to decreased lung function and reduced ability to acclimate to high elevations.
  • Dehydration: Not drinking enough water or becoming dehydrated at high elevation can exacerbate altitude sickness symptoms.
  • Pre-Existing Medical Conditions: Certain medical conditions, such as heart problems, anemia, or lung disease, can increase the risk of developing altitude sickness.
  • Medications: Taking certain medications, such as sedatives, antihistamines, and some antidepressants, can worsen altitude sickness symptoms.
  • Smoking: Smokers are more likely to experience altitude sickness due to their reduced lung function and increased carbon monoxide levels in their blood.
  • Acute Respiratory Infections: Having an acute respiratory infection, such as a cold or bronchitis, at the time of ascent can increase the risk of developing altitude sickness.
  • High-Elevation Activity Level: Engaging in high-intensity activities, such as skiing or climbing, at high elevation can increase the risk of altitude sickness due to increased carbon dioxide production and oxygen consumption.

What precautions to take when using herbal remedies for altitude sickness?

When using herbal remedies to alleviate symptoms of altitude sickness, it is crucial to exercise caution and follow proper guidelines.

Some herbs like ginkgo biloba and rhodiola rosea can increase blood flow and oxygenation to the brain, which may worsen high-altitude headaches or exacerbate existing conditions. Additionally, certain herbs like yohimbine and ma huang can stimulate the nervous system and increase heart rate, which may be contraindicated for individuals with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions. It is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using any herbal remedies at high altitudes, as they may interact with medications or worsen symptoms.

Furthermore, start with small doses and gradually increase as needed, monitoring body responses carefully, and be aware of potential allergic reactions or interactions with other herbs or substances.
