Post Nasal Drip: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Post nasal drip, also known as upper airway mucus secretion, is a condition characterized by the excessive flow of mucus from the nasal passages into the throat.

This occurs when the nasal mucosa produces more mucus than usual, often due to allergies, sinus infections, or environmental irritants such as pollution and tobacco smoke. If left untreated, post nasal drip can lead to complications like respiratory infections, sore throats, and difficulty swallowing.

To alleviate post nasal drip, herbal remedies like thyme, eucalyptus, and slippery elm can be used. Thyme has antimicrobial properties, while eucalyptus helps to thin mucus, and slippery elm soothes the mucous membranes.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, as some may interact with medications or exacerbate underlying conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of post nasal drip, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of post nasal drip?

The main causes of post nasal drip are various and multifaceted, involving both physiological and pathological factors.

Anatomical issues such as a deviated septum, nasal polyps, and enlarged turbinate bones can obstruct the nasal passage and lead to post nasal drip. Allergies, specifically those caused by exposure to allergens like dust, pollen, and pet dander, can also trigger the release of mucus. Infections like sinusitis, common cold, and flu can cause an overproduction of mucus, leading to post nasal drip.

Furthermore, gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) can also contribute to the condition by allowing stomach acid to flow up into the throat and irritate the nasal passages.

  • Allergies: Allergies trigger the release of histamine, which stimulates the nasal mucosa, leading to increased mucus production and post nasal drip.
  • Cold: A viral upper respiratory tract infection, such as a cold, causes inflammation and swelling in the nasal passages, leading to excess mucus production and post nasal drip.
  • Sinusitis: Sinusitis, an infection or inflammation of the paranasal sinuses, causes nasal congestion and excess mucus production, leading to post nasal drip.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes, such as those experienced during pregnancy, menstruation, or menopause, can lead to increased mucus production and post nasal drip.
  • Anatomical Abnormalities: Anatomical abnormalities, such as a deviated septum or nasal polyps, can cause nasal congestion and excess mucus production, leading to post nasal drip.
  • Acid Reflux: Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), or acid reflux, can cause stomach acid to flow up into the esophagus and throat, irritating the nasal mucosa and leading to post nasal drip.
  • Overuse of Nasal Decongestants: Overuse of nasal decongestants can lead to rebound congestion, causing the nasal passages to become dependent on the medication and leading to post nasal drip.
  • Tobacco Smoke: Exposure to tobacco smoke can cause inflammation and irritation in the nasal passages, leading to excess mucus production and post nasal drip.
  • Environmental Irritants: Exposure to environmental irritants, such as pollution, dust, or chemicals, can cause inflammation and irritation in the nasal passages, leading to post nasal drip.
  • Nasal Irrigants: Excessive use of nasal irrigants can cause irritation and inflammation in the nasal passages, leading to excess mucus production and post nasal drip.
