The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic tasmanian blue gum for chest congestion.

  1. Harvest fresh eucalyptus leaves by pruning branches in the morning after dew has dried, collecting about 1 cup of leaves.
  2. Dry the harvested eucalyptus leaves by spreading them thinly on paper towels for 7-10 days, or until crispy and brittle.
  3. Steam-distill the dried eucalyptus leaves using a still apparatus with 500ml of water at 100°c for 1 hour, collecting the essential oil as it separates from the distillation liquid.
  4. Filter the essential oil through cheesecloth or a coffee filter to remove any remaining particles and impurities, then transfer to dark glass bottles with tight-fitting lids.
  5. Store the finished eucalyptus globulus essential oil in a cool, dark place for up to 6 months, shaking well before use due to its potential separation from water and sediment.
