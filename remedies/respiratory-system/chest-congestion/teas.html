1. Great mullein tea

Great mullein tea helps with chest congestion because it contains mucilage, a substance that becomes slippery when wet, which coats and soothes mucous membranes, making it an effective aid in eliminating excess mucus from the respiratory tract.

Additionally, mullein contains saponins, compounds that produce a foam in the tea, which can help to loosen and break up congestion in the lungs, making it easier to expel.

Furthermore, mullein has anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties that can help reduce inflammation in the airways and promote the expulsion of phlegm, providing relief from chest congestion.

The list below shows the primary active constituents in great mullein tea that aid with chest congestion.

  • Expectorant: Great mullein tea helps loosen and expel mucus from the lungs, making it easier to breathe. This property is due to the mucilage content in the leaves.
  • Anti-inflammatory: The tea can reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract, alleviating irritation and discomfort caused by chest congestion. This is attributed to the saponins and flavonoids present in the plant.
  • Antitussive: Great mullein tea can help suppress coughs, providing relief from constant, painful coughing fits. This effect is attributed to the verbascoside content in the leaves.
  • Demulcent: The tea forms a soothing film over mucous membranes, protecting them from further irritation. This property is due to the high mucilage content in the leaves.
  • Antimicrobial: Great mullein tea may help inhibit the growth of bacteria and viruses, potentially reducing the severity and duration of chest infections. This is attributed to the rotenone and coumarin content in the plant.

The list below shows the primary parts of great mullein tea used to make teas for chest congestion.

  • Leaves: The leaves of great mullein are commonly used to make medicinal tea for chest congestion due to their expectorant and soothing properties, which help to relieve congestion and calm coughs.
  • Flowers: The flowers of great mullein are used in tea to alleviate chest congestion as they contain mucilage, which helps to soothe irritated mucous membranes and reduce inflammation.
  • Buds: The buds of great mullein are rich in saponins, which have expectorant properties, making them useful in tea to help break up and expel mucus from the chest.
  • Stems: The stems of great mullein contain fiber that can help soothe irritated throats and provide relief from congestion when used in tea.
  • Seeds: The seeds of great mullein are sometimes included in tea for chest congestion as they contain oil that has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract.

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic great mullein tea for chest congestion.

  1. Boil one cup of water and add one teaspoon of dried mullein leaves.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea to remove the mullein leaves.
  4. Add honey or lemon to taste, if desired.
  5. Drink the tea while it's still warm, up to three times a day.
