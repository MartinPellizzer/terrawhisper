7 Herbal Essential Oils For Altitude Sickness

Herbal essential oils can be a helpful remedy for altitude sickness.

This condition occurs when your body doesn't get enough oxygen at high elevations. Herbal teas, made from plants like Ginkgo biloba, Zingiber officinale, and Echinacea purpurea, can help alleviate its symptoms. Ginkgo biloba, for example, improves blood flow to the brain, which can help with headaches and dizziness. Zingiber officinale, or ginger, reduces nausea and vomiting, common symptoms of altitude sickness. Echinacea purpurea has anti-inflammatory properties that can help with fatigue and joint pain.

These herbal teas work by stimulating the body's natural response to high elevations. They increase blood flow, which helps deliver oxygen to your cells more efficiently. This can also improve your mood and energy levels. By using herbal essential oils, you can reduce the discomfort of altitude sickness and enjoy your trip or daily activities at high elevations. You can inhale the essential oils directly from the bottle, or use a diffuser to release their beneficial properties into the air.

This can help you breathe more easily and feel more comfortable in high-altitude environments.

1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba essential oils contains flavonoids and terpenoids that help with altitude sickness by improving blood flow to the brain and other organs.

The main constituents, bilobalide and ginkgolides, widen blood vessels and reduce inflammation, making it easier for the body to adapt to high altitudes. When taking Ginkgo biloba essential oils for altitude sickness, it's recommended to inhale the oil through steam inhalation or apply it topically to the skin in moderation.

To use it effectively, mix a few drops of the oil with a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil and apply it to your wrists, temples, or chest. As the oil works to improve circulation and reduce inflammation, you may start to feel relief from altitude sickness symptoms like headaches, fatigue, and shortness of breath.

This may happen within 30 minutes to an hour after application, and the effects can last for several hours.

2. Apply a few drops of ginger essential oil to your pulse points (like your wrists or temples) to improve circulation and reduce inflammation. 3. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of ginger may help to protect your body from the oxidative stress caused by high altitude, which can further reduce the severity of symptoms.

3. Echinacea purpurea

Echinacea purpurea essential oils contains properties that can help alleviate altitude sickness.

This condition is often caused by a lack of oxygen at high elevations, which can lead to headaches, fatigue, and nausea. The oil's constituents, including alkylamides and caffeic acid, have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can help mitigate the negative effects of altitude sickness. Specifically, the alkylamides in Echinacea purpurea have been shown to improve circulation and reduce inflammation, which can help the body adapt to the lower oxygen levels at high elevations.

To use Echinacea purpurea essential oils for altitude sickness, inhale the oil through steam inhalation or apply it topically to the skin, ideally on the chest, wrists, or temples. When taking action, it's essential to start with a low dose and gradually increase as needed. The likely outcomes from using Echinacea purpurea essential oils for altitude sickness include reduced symptoms of headaches and fatigue, improved circulation, and enhanced antioxidant defenses.

However, it's crucial to note that Echinacea purpurea essential oils should not be relied upon as the sole treatment for altitude sickness, but rather as a complementary therapy to be used in conjunction with medical advice and conventional treatments.

When inhaled, the aroma of Rosmarinus officinalis essential oils can also help to reduce stress and anxiety that often accompany altitude sickness. FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent altitude sickness from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help reduce altitude sickness symptoms.

Ginger tea, for example, is known to ease nausea and headaches, common symptoms at high elevations. Peppermint tea can also help with digestion issues that may arise from traveling to higher altitudes.

Some herbal teas may ease discomfort, but prevention is still uncertain.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for altitude sickness every day?

While herbal teas like ginger and peppermint can help alleviate altitude sickness symptoms, consuming them daily may not be effective for everyone.

Some herbal teas can interact with medications or worsen underlying health conditions.

It's best to drink them in moderation, a few times a day, and listen to your body's response.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in altitude sickness?

Herbal teas like Rhodiola and Ginkgo Biloba are believed to help with altitude sickness.

Studies suggest that drinking these teas can reduce symptoms like headaches and fatigue within 1-3 days after reaching high altitudes.

Some people may experience relief sooner, while others may take a bit longer.
