1. Ginkgo biloba

Ginkgo biloba creams contains flavonoids and terpenoids, which are thought to help alleviate altitude sickness by improving blood flow and reducing inflammation.

The flavonoids, particularly quercetin and kaempferol, have antioxidant properties that may protect against oxidative stress caused by high altitudes. The terpenoids, such as ginkgolides, may help relax blood vessels and increase oxygen delivery to the body. If you're planning to travel to high altitudes, consider applying Ginkgo biloba creams to your skin, especially on your hands and feet, as these areas are most prone to coldness and numbness due to poor circulation.

Apply a thin layer 1-2 hours before ascending and reapply every 4-6 hours or as needed. You may start to feel a warming sensation, improved circulation, and reduced discomfort in your extremities. Some people may also experience a decrease in headaches and dizziness associated with altitude sickness.

However, it's essential to note that Ginkgo biloba creams should be used in conjunction with other altitude sickness prevention methods, such as gradual ascent and staying hydrated, and not as a replacement for medical treatment.

FAQ

Can drinking herbal tea prevent altitude sickness from forming?

Drinking herbal tea may help prevent altitude sickness, but its effectiveness is not proven.

Ginger tea, for example, can ease nausea and headaches, which are common symptoms of altitude sickness. However, drinking herbal tea won't replace the need to ascend gradually and stay hydrated.

It might provide some relief, but more research is needed to confirm its benefits.

Is it safe to consume herbal teas for altitude sickness every day?

While herbal teas can help alleviate altitude sickness symptoms, consuming them daily isn't a straightforward answer.

Some teas, like ginkgo biloba and ginger, can help with oxygen flow and nausea. However, excessive consumption of certain herbs might interact with other medications or have adverse effects.

Moderation is key when it comes to herbal teas.

How long does it take for herbal teas to show results in altitude sickness?

Herbal teas like ginger and ginkgo biloba may help alleviate altitude sickness symptoms.

Their effects can be noticed within 30 minutes to 2 hours after consumption.

As you ascend to higher elevations, these teas can help reduce dizziness and nausea, allowing you to adapt more comfortably.

What time of day is best to drink herbal tea for altitude sickness?

At high altitudes, the air is thinner and your body needs extra help to cope.

Drinking herbal tea in the morning can help.

Ginger tea, in particular, is good at this time as it can help ease nausea and boost energy.
