What is mucus in throat and how it affects your life?

Mucus in the throat, also known as postnasal drip, refers to the excessive production and accumulation of mucus at the back of the throat.

This slimy, sticky substance is naturally produced by the mucous membranes lining the nose and throat to trap dust, bacteria, and viruses, keeping the airways clean and moist. However, when the body produces too much mucus, it can lead to several unpleasant symptoms that can negatively affect one's quality of life.

For instance, it can cause a persistent cough, sore throat, difficulty swallowing, and hoarseness, making it challenging to speak or communicate effectively. Additionally, mucus in the throat can lead to bad breath, interfere with sleep, and cause discomfort and embarrassment in social situations.

Furthermore, it can increase the risk of respiratory infections, such as bronchitis and pneumonia, and exacerbate symptoms of existing respiratory conditions, such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

What are the main causes of mucus in throat?

The main causes of mucus in throat are postnasal drip, allergies, irritants, infections, and acid reflux.

Postnasal drip occurs when mucus from the nose drips down the back of the throat, causing irritation and mucus buildup. Allergies such as hay fever or dust mite allergies can also cause mucus production.

Irritants like smoke, pollution, or strong odors can irritate the throat and cause mucus production. Infections like the common cold or flu can cause mucus buildup as well.

Lastly, acid reflux, a condition where stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, can cause mucus production and throat irritation.

The most common causes of mucus in throat are listed below.

  • Common cold: A viral infection that typically causes a runny or stuffy nose, sore throat, and often mucus buildup.
  • Flu: A viral infection that attacks your respiratory system, leading to symptoms such as fever, cough, sore throat, runny or stuffy nose, and muscle or body aches.
  • Allergies: An overreaction of the immune system to substances that are generally harmless, such as pollen, dust mites, or pet dander, which can cause inflammation and mucus production.
  • Sinusitis: An infection or inflammation of the sinuses, which are air-filled pockets in the skull located behind the forehead, cheeks, and eyes, often resulting in thick mucus and postnasal drip.
  • Environmental irritants: Exposure to pollutants, smoke, or chemicals can irritate the throat and nasal passages, leading to mucus production.
  • Asthma: A chronic lung disease that inflames and narrows the airways, causing symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath, which can also result in mucus buildup.
  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD): A condition where stomach acid flows back up into the esophagus, causing symptoms such as heartburn, chest pain, and difficulty swallowing, and sometimes leading to mucus in the throat.
  • Postnasal drip: The excessive production of mucus in the nasal passages that drains down the back of the throat, causing a persistent cough and throat irritation.
  • Viral bronchitis: An inflammation of the bronchial tubes (the airways that lead to the lungs), often caused by a viral infection, leading to a persistent cough, wheezing, and mucus production.
  • Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD): A group of lung conditions that cause breathing difficulties, including emphysema and chronic bronchitis, which can result in mucus buildup in the lungs and throat.
