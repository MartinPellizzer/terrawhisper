Laryngitis: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Laryngitis is an inflammation of the larynx, or voice box, causing hoarseness, soreness, and a scratchy voice.

Typically caused by overuse, viral infections, or exposure to irritants like smoke or chemicals, laryngitis can also be exacerbated by allergies and acid reflux. If left untreated, it can lead to permanent voice damage and even affect breathing. To alleviate symptoms, herbal remedies such as licorice root, slippery elm, and marshmallow root can be used in teas, infusions, or lozenges.

However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using medicinal herbs, especially for pregnant women, children, or those with underlying health conditions.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of laryngitis, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of laryngitis?

The main causes of laryngitis are viral infections, such as the common cold or flu, which account for approximately 90% of cases.

Bacterial infections, such as strep throat, can also cause laryngitis, particularly in individuals with weakened immune systems. Overuse or misuse of the voice, such as in singers or public speakers, can lead to vocal strain and subsequent laryngitis. Allergies, acid reflux, and smoking are also contributing factors, as they can irritate the larynx and lead to inflammation.

Additionally, exposure to pollution, dust, and chemicals can cause laryngitis in some individuals.

  • Viral Infection: A viral infection, often caused by the common cold or flu viruses, leads to inflammation and swelling of the larynx, resulting in laryngitis.
  • Overuse of Voice: Repeatedly using the voice for an extended period, such as in singing, public speaking, or shouting, causes strain and irritation to the vocal cords, leading to laryngitis.
  • Smoking: Inhaling tobacco smoke damages the mucous membranes in the throat and larynx, leading to chronic inflammation and increasing the risk of developing laryngitis.
  • Allergies: Respiratory allergies, such as asthma or hay fever, cause chronic inflammation in the airways, including the larynx, leading to laryngitis.
  • Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease: Gastroesophageal reflux disease, a condition where stomach acid flows back up into the throat, causes irritation and inflammation of the larynx, leading to laryngitis.
  • Bacterial Infection: A bacterial infection, often caused by Streptococcus or Haemophilus influenzae, leads to inflammation and swelling of the larynx, resulting in laryngitis.
  • Environmental Irritants: Inhaling environmental irritants, such as dust, chemicals, or pollution, causes irritation and inflammation of the larynx, leading to laryngitis.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal fluctuations during pregnancy, menstruation, or menopause can lead to changes in the vocal cords and larynx, resulting in laryngitis.
  • Medication Side Effects: Certain medications, such as antihistamines or decongestants, can cause dryness and irritation of the larynx, leading to laryngitis.
  • Anatomical Abnormalities: Anatomical abnormalities, such as a deviated septum or a narrow airway, can cause chronic inflammation and irritation of the larynx, leading to laryngitis.
