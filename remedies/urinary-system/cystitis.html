What is cystitis and how it affects your life?

Cystitis is a painful and debilitating urinary tract infection that can wreak havoc on daily life.

This condition causes a burning sensation while urinating, frequent trips to the bathroom, and an overwhelming urge to go despite a full bladder. The constant discomfort can make it difficult to focus at work or school, leading to decreased productivity and absenteeism. Simple activities like getting dressed or taking a walk become daunting tasks due to the intense pain and discomfort.

Social gatherings and intimate relationships can also be severely impacted, as individuals may feel embarrassed or ashamed about their condition, causing them to isolate themselves from loved ones. Furthermore, cystitis can disrupt sleep patterns, leading to fatigue and irritability, making it challenging to maintain a sense of normalcy in daily life. As the condition persists, it can also affect mental health, causing anxiety, depression, and decreased self-esteem.

Ultimately, living with cystitis can be a constant struggle, leaving individuals feeling helpless and frustrated as they try to manage their symptoms and regain control over their lives.

What are the main causes of cystitis?

The main causes of cystitis are typically related to issues with the urinary tract, particularly the bladder and urethra.

Bacterial infections are a common trigger, often caused by bacteria such as E. coli that enter the urinary tract through the urethra and multiply in the bladder. Women are more susceptible to bacterial cystitis due to their shorter urethra, making it easier for bacteria to reach the bladder. Other factors can also contribute to cystitis, including poor hygiene, constipation, and anatomical issues such as a narrowed or blocked urethra.

Additionally, hormonal changes during menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause can increase the risk of developing cystitis.

The most common causes of cystitis are listed below.

  • Bacterial Infection: The most common cause of cystitis, bacterial infections occur when bacteria from the gut or vagina enter the urinary tract and multiply in the bladder. Symptoms include burning sensation while urinating, frequent urination, and cloudy or bloody urine.
  • E. coli: E. coli is a type of bacteria that is commonly found in the gut and can cause cystitis by infecting the bladder and urinary tract.
  • Streptococcus: Streptococcus is another type of bacteria that can cause cystitis, especially in women who are pregnant or have diabetes.
  • Klebsiella: Klebsiella is a type of bacteria that can cause cystitis, particularly in people with weakened immune systems or those who have undergone surgery.
  • Candida Overgrowth: Candida is a fungus that is naturally present in the body but can overgrow and cause infection in the bladder and urinary tract.
  • Vaginal Yeast Infection: Vaginal yeast infections can spread to the urinary tract and cause cystitis, especially in women who are taking antibiotics or have weakened immune systems.
  • Kidney Stones: Kidney stones can block the flow of urine and cause bacteria to multiply in the bladder, leading to cystitis.
  • Urethral Trauma: Injury to the urethra during sexual activity or insertion of a catheter can cause bacteria to enter the urinary tract and lead to cystitis.
  • Weakened Immune System: A weakened immune system due to illness, chemotherapy, or other conditions can increase the risk of developing cystitis.
  • Poor Hygiene: Poor hygiene practices, such as not wiping correctly after using the bathroom or sharing personal care items with others, can increase the risk of bacterial transfer and lead to cystitis.
