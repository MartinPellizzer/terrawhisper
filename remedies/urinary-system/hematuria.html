Hematuria: Causes, Medicinal Herbs and Herbal Preparations

Hematuria refers to the presence of blood in the urine, which can be a symptom of various underlying conditions such as kidney stones, urinary tract infections, or bladder cancer.

The recurring bleeding can lead to anemia, kidney damage, and increased risk of urinary tract infections. To alleviate hematuria, herbal remedies such as dandelion root, uva ursi, and corn silk can be used. Medicinal preparations like uva ursi tea and dandelion root capsules can help soothe the urinary tract and reduce bleeding.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using herbal remedies, as some may interact with medications or worsen the condition.

This article explains in detail what are the causes of hematuria, what medicinal herbs to use to relieve this problem and how to prepare these herbs to get the best results.

What are the main causes of hematuria?

The main causes of hematuria are urinary tract infections (UTIs), kidney stones, bladder cancer, and benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH).

UTIs are often caused by bacterial infections, such as Escherichia coli (E. coli), which can damage the bladder and kidneys, leading to bleeding. Kidney stones, typically composed of calcium oxalate or uric acid, can cause hematuria due to their abrasive nature and the resulting inflammation. Bladder cancer, most commonly transitional cell carcinoma, can also cause hematuria, especially if left undiagnosed or untreated.

BPH, characterized by an enlarged prostate, can compress the urethra and cause hematuria, particularly in older men.

  • Kidney Stones: Kidney stones are hard, pebble-like masses that form in the kidneys when there is a concentration of minerals in the urine, which can cause bleeding in the kidneys, leading to hematuria.
  • Urinary Tract Infections: Urinary tract infections, typically caused by bacteria such as E. coli, can cause inflammation in the urinary tract, leading to bleeding in the kidneys or bladder and resulting in hematuria.
  • Kidney Disease: Kidney disease, including chronic kidney disease and kidney failure, can cause damage to the blood vessels in the kidneys, leading to bleeding and hematuria.
  • Prostate Enlargement: Prostate enlargement, typically associated with benign prostatic hyperplasia, can cause obstruction of urine flow, leading to increased pressure in the urinary tract and bleeding in the bladder or kidneys, resulting in hematuria.
  • Bladder Cancer: Bladder cancer, a type of malignant tumor, can cause bleeding in the bladder, leading to hematuria, often presenting as painless gross hematuria.
  • Kidney Cysts: Kidney cysts, including simple cysts and polycystic kidney disease, can cause damage to the kidneys and bleeding in the kidneys, resulting in hematuria.
  • Urethritis: Urethritis, an inflammation of the urethra, often caused by sexually transmitted infections such as gonorrhea and chlamydia, can cause bleeding in the urethra and hematuria.
  • Trauma: Trauma to the kidneys or bladder, including injuries from accidents or falls, can cause damage to the blood vessels and bleeding in the kidneys or bladder, resulting in hematuria.
  • Vasculitis: Vasculitis, an inflammation of the blood vessels, can cause bleeding in the kidneys or bladder and hematuria, often associated with autoimmune disorders such as lupus and rheumatoid arthritis.
  • Glomerulonephritis: Glomerulonephritis, an inflammation of the glomeruli, the filtering units of the kidneys, can cause damage to the kidneys and bleeding in the kidneys, resulting in hematuria.
