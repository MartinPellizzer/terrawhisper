Sambucus nigra teas have been traditionally used to treat cystitis, a common urinary tract infection characterized by painful urination and bladder inflammation.

  1. Gather Sambucus nigra flowers (about 1 cup) and dried peppermint leaves (about 1 teaspoon).
  2. Measure 1 cup of boiling water and pour it over the flowers and peppermint leaves in a heat-resistant cup.
  3. Let it steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain the mixture using a tea strainer or cheesecloth.
  4. Add honey to taste (about 1-2 teaspoons) to make the tea sweet and soothing.
  5. Drink the tea 2-3 times a day to help alleviate cystitis symptoms.

Usage:

  1. Drink 1 cup of Sambucus nigra tea 3 times a day
  2. Use 1-2 teaspoons of dried Sambucus nigra per cup of boiling water
  3. Steep for 5-7 minutes, then strain and discard the solids
  4. Continue usage for 7-10 days or as directed by a healthcare professional
  5. Monitor your symptoms and adjust usage as needed with a healthcare professional

Sambucus nigra teas can be used to treat cystitis, but possible side effects may include allergic reactions, such as hives or itching, and interactions with other medications, which can lead to increased blood pressure or other cardiovascular issues.

Precautions when using Sambucus nigra teas to treat cystitis include using them in moderation, avoiding consumption during pregnancy or breastfeeding, and being aware of potential interactions with other herbs or supplements.
