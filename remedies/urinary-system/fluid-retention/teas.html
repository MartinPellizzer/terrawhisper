1. Arctostaphylos uva ursi

Arctostaphylos uva ursi, also known as bearberry, teas helps with fluid retention because it contains arbutin, a compound that inhibits the reabsorption of sodium and water in the kidneys.

This process reduces the amount of fluid retained in the body, thereby alleviating symptoms of water retention and swelling. Additionally, bearberry's diuretic properties help increase urine production, promoting the removal of excess fluids from the body.

This natural diuretic effect makes bearberry tea a popular remedy for managing fluid retention.

The list below shows the primary medicinal constituents in Arctostaphylos uva ursi teas that help with fluid retention.

  • Gallic acid: A phenolic acid that has a diuretic effect by stimulating the kidneys to excrete excess fluids and electrolytes, thereby reducing fluid retention.
  • Arbutin: A glycosidic alkaloid that acts as a mild diuretic by increasing urine production and reducing the reabsorption of water and electrolytes in the kidneys.

  • Barks: The barks of Arctostaphylos uva ursi contain tannins, which aid in reducing fluid retention and alleviating related issues.
  • Fruits: The fruits of the plant are used in teas to help manage fluid retention due to their diuretic properties, promoting urine production and reducing water retention.

Quick Recipe

The following recipe gives a procedure to make a basic bearberry for fluid retention.

  1. Harvest the arctostaphylos uva ursi leaves in late summer when they are fully ripe and dry.
  2. Dry the harvested leaves in a low-temperature oven at 150 degrees fahrenheit for 2 hours.
  3. Measure out 1 teaspoon of dried arctostaphylos uva ursi leaves per 8 ounces of boiling water.
  4. Steep the dried leaves in the boiling water for 5 to 7 minutes to release the herbal properties.
  5. Strain the tea and discard the solids before serving the arctostaphylos uva ursi tea.
