Coleus (Coleus forskohlii) Medicinal Guide: Benefits, Constituents, and Preparations

Coleus, scientifically known as Coleus forskohlii, is a versatile herbaceous perennial plant used in various ways due to its medicinal properties. Native to southern India and Sri Lanka, it has been utilized for centuries in traditional medicine systems like Ayurveda. The plant contains the chemical compound forskolin, which is known to exhibit beneficial effects on heart health, respiration function and weight management. Additionally, Coleus leaves can be used as a flavoring agent in culinary preparations, particularly in Asian and Thai cuisines where they are often added to curries and stir-fries.

As an ornamental plant, Coleus forskohlii is prized for its attractive foliage that comes in diverse shapes and a wide range of colors including green, purple, pink, yellow, white, and variegated. This characteristic has led to the development of numerous cultivars with unique leaf patterns and textures. The plant thrives well in partial shade or full sun and requires well-drained soil with average fertilization for optimal growth. Furthermore, Coleus plants can be easily propagated through stem cuttings and tend to flower under optimum growing conditions.

In terms of botanical significance and history, the cultivation of Coleus forskohlii began when it was first introduced into Europe during the mid-19th century. Since then, this plant has been extensively studied for its potential therapeutic uses and ornamental values in horticulture.This article explains in details the medicinal properties of Coleus and how to use this plant to boost your health.

What Are The Possible Health Side Effects Of Coleus?

Headaches and nausea might occur when taking it in large amounts or for an extended period of time. You can't predict who will experience these symptoms, but they may be more pronounced if one has a sensitivity to the plant.

In some cases, Coleus could even lead to vomiting or diarrhea due to its potential interaction with other medications and substances in the body. It's important to consult a healthcare professional before using it alongside any prescribed medication you're taking.

Another negative aspect of Coleus is that it may affect your vision, causing blurriness or even disorientation if consumed in high doses. This could be especially concerning for those who rely on their eyesight for daily tasks and activities.

In some cases, this herb has been known to worsen seizures or cause irregular heartbeats which can lead to further complications. It's essential to consider any pre-existing health conditions before using it to prevent possible issues down the line.

Slower heart rates and potential abnormal heart rhythms could result from consuming Coleus, so it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional if you have any concerns related to your cardiovascular health.

In terms of digestion, stomach aches might arise as an unwanted side effect when taking the plant, which can be uncomfortable and disruptive to one's daily routine. This is why it's important to pay attention to your body's response after consuming this herb.

Furthermore, Coleus might increase your risk of bleeding, making certain procedures or surgeries more difficult. Be sure to discuss any potential interactions with medical professionals before starting its use.
