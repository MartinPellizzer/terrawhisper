10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Paeonia lactiflora (White Peony)

Paeonia lactiflora, also known as the Chinese peony, is a plant that has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries.

One common preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made by steeping the dried or fresh root in hot water. This tisane is believed to have various health benefits, including reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms of premenstrual syndrome.

Another preparation is a tincture, which is made by soaking the root in alcohol to extract its active compounds. The tincture can be taken orally and is thought to have similar benefits to the tisane, as well as potential additional benefits such as pain relief and immune system support. However, it is important to note that the effectiveness of these preparations and their potential side effects have not been thoroughly studied and more research is needed.

Additionally, it is always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new medicinal regimen.

1. Infusion

Paeonia Lactiflora infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping dried root or rhizome of the white peony plant in hot water, which releases the plant's beneficial compounds into a liquid form.

This infusion is known to have several health benefits, including reducing inflammation, alleviating menstrual cramps, and promoting relaxation. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the dried root or rhizome of the Paeonia lactiflora plant.

To make a Paeonia lactiflora infusion, pour boiling water over the dried root or rhizome and let it steep for 10-15 minutes. It is important to strain the liquid before consuming. When using this Paeonia lactiflora preparation, it is recommended to start with a low dose and increase gradually, as it can cause digestive upset in some individuals.

Additionally, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using this infusion if you are taking any medications or have any pre-existing medical conditions.

2. Tincture

Paeonia Lactiflora tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking the dried root or underground stem (rhizome) of the white peony plant in alcohol or another solvent to extract its active compounds.

The main health benefits of this tincture include anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antispasmodic effects, which can help alleviate symptoms of menstrual cramps, muscle spasms, and headaches, among other conditions. The root or rhizome of the Paeonia lactiflora plant is used to make the tincture, and it contains various bioactive compounds such as paeoniflorin, which is believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

To make the tincture, the dried and chopped root or rhizome is soaked in a solvent, such as ethanol or glycerin, for several weeks, and then the liquid is filtered and stored in a cool, dark place. When using Paeonia Lactiflora tincture, it is essential to follow the recommended dosage and consult with a healthcare provider, as it can interact with certain medications and have side effects such as digestive upset or skin irritation.

Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using this tincture, as its safety during pregnancy and lactation has not been established.
