10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Salvia officinalis (Sage)

Salvia officinalis, also known as garden sage, is a versatile herb with a long history of medicinal use.

One popular preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made by steeping fresh or dried sage leaves in hot water. This soothing drink is often used to alleviate digestive discomfort, as sage is believed to have antispasmodic and carminative properties.

Additionally, sage tisane may help alleviate symptoms of sore throats and coughs, thanks to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Another common preparation is a tincture, which involves soaking sage leaves in alcohol or vinegar to extract their medicinal compounds. Sage tincture can be used to treat a variety of ailments, including skin conditions, menstrual cramps, and memory loss, due to its estrogenic and neuroprotective properties.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new medicinal preparations, as sage can interact with certain medications and may not be suitable for everyone.

1. Infusion

Salvia Officinalis infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping dried leaves of the common sage plant in hot water, releasing its beneficial compounds into the liquid.

The main health benefits of this sage infusion include alleviating symptoms of digestive disorders, reducing inflammation, and soothing sore throats. The primary plant parts used are the fragrant, grey-green leaves, which are harvested, dried, and then steeped in boiling water. To prepare a sage infusion, pour boiling water over 1-2 teaspoons of dried sage leaves and let it steep for 5-10 minutes before straining.

When using sage infusion, it is crucial to be aware of potential precautions, such as avoiding high doses during pregnancy, and consulting a healthcare professional if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking medication.

2. Tincture

Salvia Officinalis tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking leaves of the common sage plant in alcohol, which extracts its active compounds.

Its main health benefits include improving digestion, reducing inflammation, alleviating menopause symptoms, and providing antioxidant and antimicrobial effects. The leaves are the primary plant part used for this preparation.

To make Salvia Officinalis tincture, steep fresh or dried sage leaves in alcohol, such as vodka or grain alcohol, for several weeks before straining. The recommended dosage is usually 2-4 ml up to three times a day. It's crucial to consult a healthcare professional before using Salvia Officinalis tincture, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women, people with seizure disorders, and those taking diabetes or sedative medications.

Additionally, it may interact with certain herbs and supplements, so it's essential to inform healthcare providers of any complementary medicine use.

3. Dry powder

Salvia Officinalis dry powder is a medicinal preparation made from the dried leaves of the Salvia officinalis plant, also known as common sage.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antibacterial properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of conditions such as sore throats, digestive issues, and skin irritations. The leaves are the primary plant part used for this preparation, and they can be dried and ground into a fine powder.

To make the dry powder, first, carefully wash and dry the sage leaves, then grind them into a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle. To use, mix the powder with a liquid such as water or oil before consuming. When using Salvia officinalis dry powder, it is important to start with a low dose and gradually increase as needed, as high doses can have adverse effects.

Additionally, it is not recommended for use during pregnancy or while breastfeeding, and it may interact with certain medications, so it is best to consult with a healthcare provider before use.

4. Capsules

Salvia Officinalis capsules are dietary supplements that contain ground or powdered leaves of the Salvia officinalis plant, commonly known as sage.

The main health benefits of this preparation include improved cognitive function, reduced inflammation, and enhanced oral health. The leaves and stems of the Salvia officinalis plant are used to make these capsules.

To make the capsules, the leaves and stems are harvested, dried, and then ground into a fine powder that is encapsulated. When taking Salvia officinalis capsules, it is important to follow the recommended dosage and not exceed the maximum daily intake. Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before taking Salvia officinalis capsules if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

It is also important to note that Salvia officinalis may interact with certain medications, so it is crucial to inform your healthcare provider of any medications you are currently taking.

5. Oil

Salvia Officinalis oil is a type of essential oil that is extracted from the leaves of the Salvia officinalis plant, also known as common sage.

The main health benefits of Salvia Officinalis oil include its ability to aid in digestion, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of menopause. The leaves and stems of the Salvia officinalis plant are used to produce the oil through a process of steam distillation.

To make Salvia Officinalis oil, the leaves and stems are harvested, dried, and then steam-distilled to extract the essential oil. When using Salvia Officinalis oil, it is important to dilute it with a carrier oil and to avoid using it internally or on broken skin. Additionally, it is recommended to perform a patch test before using it, as some people may experience skin irritation.

Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using Salvia Officinalis oil, as well as people with epilepsy or who are taking medication that may interact with it.

6. Salve

Salvia Officinalis salve is a medicinal preparation made from the common sage plant, Salvia officinalis, which is known for its healing properties.

The main health benefits of this salve include its ability to soothe skin irritations, reduce inflammation, and alleviate pain due to its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antiseptic properties. The leaves and flowers of the Salvia officinalis plant are typically used to make this preparation. To make the salve, the leaves and flowers are first dried and then crushed into a fine powder.

The powder is then mixed with a carrier oil, such as olive or coconut oil, and heated until the oil takes on the properties of the sage. The mixture is then strained and allowed to cool before being stored in airtight containers. When using Salvia Officinalis salve, it is important to avoid contact with the eyes and to discontinue use if a rash or other adverse reaction occurs.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using this preparation if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

7. Cream

Salvia Officinalis cream is a medicinal preparation that is made from the common sage plant, known scientifically as Salvia officinalis.

The main health benefits of this Salvia officinalis preparation include its ability to soothe skin irritations, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing. The leaves and stems of the Salvia officinalis plant are typically used to make this medicinal cream.

To make the cream, the leaves and stems are first dried and then crushed into a fine powder. This powder is then mixed with a carrier oil, such as coconut oil or olive oil, and beeswax to create a smooth, creamy consistency. It is important to note that Salvia officinalis cream should not be used by pregnant women, as it may stimulate uterine contractions.

Additionally, it is recommended to perform a patch test on a small area of skin before using the cream more broadly, as some people may experience skin irritation or an allergic reaction.

8. Liniment

Salvia Officinalis liniment is a topical preparation made from the common sage plant, Salvia officinalis, that is used for its potential medicinal properties.

The main health benefits of this Salvia officinalis preparation include its potential anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial effects, making it a popular choice for treating muscle and joint pain, soreness, and inflammation. The leaves and stems of the Salvia officinalis plant are typically used to make the liniment, and they can be prepared by infusing them in a carrier oil or alcohol. To make the liniment, fresh or dried sage leaves and stems can be steeped in a carrier oil such as olive or almond oil, or in a high-proof alcohol such as vodka, for several weeks.

After steeping, the mixture is strained to remove the plant material, and the resulting liniment can be applied directly to the skin. However, it is important to take precautions when using Salvia officinalis liniment, as it can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in some people. It is recommended to do a patch test on a small area of skin before using the liniment more broadly, and to avoid using it on broken or irritated skin.

Additionally, it is important to keep the liniment out of reach of children and pets, as it can be harmful if ingested.

9. Mouthwash

Salvia Officinalis mouthwash is a medicinal preparation made from the common sage plant, Salvia officinalis, which is known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The main health benefits of this mouthwash include reducing bacteria in the mouth, promoting gum health, and freshening breath. The aerial parts of the Salvia officinalis plant, including the leaves and flowers, are typically used to make this preparation. To make Salvia Officinalis mouthwash, steep fresh or dried sage in hot water, allow it to cool, and strain out the plant material before use.

It is important to note that Salvia officinalis mouthwash should not be used by pregnant women, as it may stimulate menstruation, and it should be used with caution by individuals taking diabetes medication, as it may lower blood sugar levels.

10. Gargle

Salvia Officinalis gargle is a medicinal preparation made by steeping leaves of the Salvia officinalis plant, also known as common sage, in hot water to extract its active compounds, which is then used as a mouthwash or gargle.

The main health benefits of this preparation include its ability to soothe sore throats, freshen breath, and its potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The leaves are the primary plant part used for this preparation.

To make a Salvia Officinalis gargle, fresh or dried leaves can be steeped in boiling water for 10-15 minutes, then allowed to cool before use. It's important to dilute the preparation with water and not to swallow it.

Precautions to take when using this Salvia Officinalis preparation include discontinuing use if any adverse reactions occur, and avoiding its use during pregnancy or while breastfeeding, as its safety has not been established in these populations.
