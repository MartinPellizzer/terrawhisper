10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Chrysanthemum morifolium (Ju Hua)

Chrysanthemum morifolium, also known as Chrysanthemum tea, is a popular herbal remedy in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its flowers are commonly used to prepare a tisane, or herbal tea, which is believed to have various health benefits such as alleviating eye strain, reducing fever, and soothing the digestive system. The flowers can also be used to make a tincture, an alcohol-based extract, which can be taken orally for its medicinal properties. Chrysanthemum morifolium is rich in antioxidants, which are thought to contribute to its therapeutic effects.

However, it is important to note that while Chrysanthemum morifolium has a long history of use in traditional medicine, its medicinal properties have not been thoroughly studied and more research is needed to confirm its effectiveness and safety.

1. Infusion

Chrysanthemum Morifolium infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping dried flowers of the Chrysanthemum morifolium plant in hot water, which releases the plant's beneficial compounds into the water.

This infusion is known for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving eye health, and supporting respiratory and cardiovascular function. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the dried flower heads, which are harvested, dried, and then steeped in hot water.

To make the infusion, place about one to two teaspoons of dried Chrysanthemum morifolium flowers in a cup of hot water, let it steep for 5-10 minutes, and then strain before drinking. When using this Chrysanthemum morifolium preparation, it is important to be aware of potential precautions. Some people may experience allergic reactions to Chrysanthemum morifolium, so it is important to discontinue use if any adverse reactions occur.

Additionally, people who are taking medication for blood pressure or blood sugar regulation should consult with a healthcare provider before using this infusion, as it may interact with these medications.
