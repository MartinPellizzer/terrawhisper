10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Vaccinium myrtillus (Bilberry)

Vaccinium myrtillus, also known as bilberry, has a long history of use in medicinal preparations.

Its berries and leaves are used to make a variety of remedies, including tisanes and tinctures. A tisane, or herbal tea, can be made by steeping dried bilberry leaves in hot water.

This soothing drink is said to have a number of health benefits, including aiding digestion and alleviating diarrhea. Tinctures, on the other hand, are made by soaking the berries or leaves in alcohol to extract their active compounds. These concentrated extracts can be taken orally to treat a range of conditions, such as mouth and throat inflammations, and have been shown to help regulate blood sugar levels.

It is important to note that while bilberry preparations have been used traditionally for medicinal purposes, their effectiveness and safety have not been fully studied and any use should be discussed with a healthcare provider.

1. Infusion

Vaccinium Myrtillus infusion is an herbal preparation made by steeping dried bilberry leaves or fruits in hot water, which releases the plant's beneficial compounds.

The main health benefits of this preparation include improving eye health, reducing inflammation, and supporting digestive health due to its high antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The primary plant parts used are the leaves and fruits of the bilberry plant. To make a bilberry infusion, pour boiling water over 1-2 teaspoons of dried bilberry leaves or fruits and let it steep for 10-15 minutes before straining.

Some precautions to take when using this preparation include avoiding it during pregnancy and breastfeeding, and monitoring blood sugar levels if taking medication for diabetes, as bilberry may lower blood sugar levels.

2. Tincture

Vaccinium Myrtillus tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking crushed bilberry fruit and leaves in a solution of alcohol and water, allowing the active compounds to extract into the liquid.

This tincture has numerous health benefits, including improving eye health and vision, reducing inflammation, and providing antioxidants that help protect against oxidative stress and damage. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the fruit and leaves of the Vaccinium Myrtillus plant.

To make the tincture, the crushed bilberry fruit and leaves are placed in a jar and covered with a mixture of 50% ethanol and 50% water, then sealed and allowed to steep for several weeks. The resulting tincture should be strained and stored in a cool, dark place. When using this Vaccinium Myrtillus preparation, it is important to follow the recommended dosage, as excessive consumption can cause side effects such as diarrhea and stomach upset.

Additionally, individuals who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a history of liver or kidney problems should consult with a healthcare provider before using bilberry tincture.
