10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Zea mays (Corn)

Zea mays, commonly known as corn, has a long history of use in medicinal preparations.

A tisane can be made by steeping the dried corn silk in hot water, which is believed to help with issues such as bladder infections and prostate problems. A tincture can also be made by soaking the corn silk in alcohol, which can be used to treat various ailments including kidney stones and urinary tract infections.

The corn oil, extracted from the germ of the corn, is rich in vitamin E and essential fatty acids, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products for its moisturizing properties. Additionally, the cornstarch, derived from the endosperm of the corn, is used as an anti-diarrheal and to soothe sunburns.

The leaves of Zea mays are also used in traditional medicine, they are applied as a poultice to treat swelling and inflammation.

1. Infusion

Zea Mays infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the leaves, stems, or ears of the Zea mays plant in hot water to extract its beneficial compounds.

This preparation is known to provide several health benefits, including relief from painful conditions such as inflammation, swelling, and sore throat, as well as promoting healthy skin and aiding in digestion. The leaves and stems are the primary plant parts used in this infusion, although the ears (or kernels) can also be used.

To prepare the infusion, simply pour boiling water over the plant material and let it steep for 10-15 minutes before straining. When using Zea Mays infusion, it is important to be aware of potential allergic reactions and discontinue use if any occur.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using this or any other herbal preparation, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women, and those taking other medications.

2. Tincture

Zea Mays tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking corn in alcohol or glycerin to extract its active compounds.

Its main health benefits include reducing inflammation, relieving pain, and improving digestion due to its rich nutrient content. The main plant parts used are the kernels and sometimes the leaves, both of which are rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds.

To make the tincture, the corn kernels or leaves are chopped and soaked in alcohol or glycerin for several weeks, then strained. The resulting liquid is the Zea Mays tincture. However, it is important to take precautions when using this tincture, as it can cause allergic reactions in some people, especially those with corn allergies.

Additionally, it should be used in moderation and under the guidance of a healthcare professional, as excessive use can lead to imbalances in the body due to its high nutrient content.

3. Decoction

Zea Mays decoction is a type of herbal preparation made by boiling and simmering the corn plant's leaves, stalks, or seeds in water for a prolonged period to extract their active compounds.

The main health benefits of this Zea mays preparation include its ability to soothe digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and provide antioxidants that can help protect the body from damage. The leaves, stalks, and seeds of the corn plant are the primary plant parts used in this preparation.

To make a Zea Mays decoction, place the desired plant material in a pot, cover with water, bring to a boil, and then reduce heat and let simmer for several hours. It is essential to strain the liquid before consuming it. When using Zea Mays decoction, it is crucial to be aware of potential allergic reactions and to discontinue use if any adverse effects occur.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using Zea Mays decoction as a treatment for any medical condition.

4. Powder

Zea Mays powder is a fine, light-colored substance made from grinding dried corn kernels into a consistent, powdery texture.

Its main health benefits include providing easily digestible carbohydrates, dietary fiber, and trace minerals, making it a valuable addition to various dietary and medicinal applications. The primary plant parts used for this Zea mays preparation are the dried corn kernels, which are cleaned, processed, and milled into a fine powder.

To make Zea Mays powder, first, clean and dry the corn kernels, then carefully grind them into a fine powder using a grain mill or food processor. Sift the resulting powder to remove any larger particles, ensuring a consistent texture.

Main precautions when using Zea Mays powder include avoiding inhalation during preparation and consumption, as well as monitoring blood sugar levels if using it as a dietary supplement, as excessive consumption may lead to spikes in blood sugar.

5. Capsule

Zea Mays capsule is a medicinal preparation derived from the Zea mays plant, also known as corn.

6. Oil

Zea Mays oil, also known as corn oil, is obtained from the germ of corn plants (Zea mays).

It is rich in antioxidants and vitamin E, making it beneficial for heart health, lowering bad cholesterol levels, and improving immune function. The main plant parts used are the germs of the corn kernels, which are extracted and processed to produce the oil.

To make Zea Mays oil, the germs are first cleaned and dried, then pressed using an oil press or extracted using a solvent. It is important to consult a healthcare professional before using Zea Mays oil, as it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals and should be avoided by those with existing allergies to corn or corn products.

Additionally, it should be stored in a cool, dark place to prevent it from becoming rancid and should not be used as a cooking oil at high temperatures, as it can produce harmful compounds.

7. Salve

Zea Mays salve is a medicinal preparation made from corn (Zea mays) that is used topically to treat various skin conditions.

Its main health benefits include providing relief from skin irritations, moisturizing dry skin, soothing minor burns and sunburns, and promoting faster healing of minor cuts and scrapes. The primary plant parts used for this preparation are the corn kernel oil, which is rich in antioxidants and fatty acids, and beeswax, which acts as a natural emulsifier and thickening agent. To make Zea Mays salve, first, the corn kernels are cooked and strained to extract the oil.

The oil is then infused with healing herbs, if desired, and combined with beeswax over low heat. Once the mixture has cooled and solidified, it can be applied directly to the skin. When using Zea Mays salve, it is important to test a small amount on a patch of skin first to ensure no allergic reaction occurs.

Additionally, it should not be used on deep wounds or severe burns, and it is always recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using any new medicinal preparation.
