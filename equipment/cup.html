Cups For Herbalism

Cups are small vessels typically made of glass or ceramic used in herbalism for various therapeutic purposes.

They are often employed in techniques like cupping therapy to create suction on the skin, which can help relieve muscle tension and improve circulation.

Cups are important in herbalism because they facilitate the application of herbal oils, balms, or infusions directly to the skin, enhancing absorption and effectiveness.

Using cups can improve an herbalist's practice by offering a hands-on method for delivering treatments and deepening the connection between the practitioner and the client.

What are cups?

Cups are shallow, bowl-shaped vessels traditionally used in herbalism for cupping therapy.

They are typically made of glass, bamboo, or silicone and are used to create suction on the skin to promote healing.

The general function of cups is to enhance blood circulation, relieve muscle tension, and stimulate the body's natural healing processes.

Common variations include fire cups, bamboo cups, and silicone cups, which may be used depending on the practitioner's technique and the client's needs.
