10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Crataeva nurvala (Varuna)

Crataeva nurvala, also known as varuna, is a plant that has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for centuries.

Two common medicinal preparations of Crataeva nurvala are tisanes and tinctures. A tisane is an herbal tea made by steeping the crushed leaves or bark of the Crataeva nurvala plant in hot water.

This soothing beverage is often used to treat digestive issues such as constipation, indigestion, and irritable bowel syndrome. A tincture, on the other hand, is a more concentrated extract of the plant, made by soaking the leaves or bark in alcohol or glycerin. Tinctures can be taken in small doses, often just a few drops at a time, and are used to treat a wider range of conditions, including kidney stones, urinary tract infections, and rheumatism.

Both tisanes and tinctures are believed to have anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and analgesic properties, making them valuable additions to any natural medicine cabinet.

1. Decoction

Crataeva Nurvala decoction is a traditional Ayurvedic preparation made by boiling and reducing the bark or roots of the Crataeva nurvala tree in water.

Its main health benefits include treating kidney stones, urinary tract infections, and digestive issues such as constipation and inflammation. The bark and roots of the Crataeva nurvala tree are the primary plant parts used for this preparation.

To make the decoction, take 2-3 teaspoons of the bark or root powder, add it to a pot with 4-5 cups of water, bring it to a boil, and then simmer it until the liquid is reduced by half. As for precautions, it is recommended to consult a healthcare professional before using Crataeva nurvala decoction if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking any medications.

Additionally, it is important to monitor your kidney function if you have any pre-existing kidney conditions, as the decoction may affect kidney function.

2. Infusion

The bark and leaves of the Crataeva nurvala tree are the main plant parts used for this preparation.

To make a Crataeva nurvala infusion, bring a cup of water to a boil, remove from heat, and add 1-2 teaspoons of crushed Crataeva nurvala bark or leaves.
