Phytochemicals: Plant Compounds in Herbal Medicine

Phytochemicals are naturally occurring chemical compounds produced by plants, particularly as secondary metabolites involved in defense, signaling, and environmental adaptation. In herbal medicine, phytochemicals such as alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds are responsible for many therapeutic effects because they interact with biological systems to produce antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and other pharmacological activities.

Definition

Phytochemicals are naturally occurring compounds found in plants that contribute to their medicinal properties and are used in herbal medicine to support health and treat various conditions.

Quercetin

Quercetin is a flavonoid phytochemical commonly found in plants such as onions, apples, and berries, known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Curcumin

Curcumin is a polyphenolic compound found primarily in the rhizome of Curcuma longa, commonly known as turmeric, and is renowned for its potent antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and potential anti-cancer properties.

Resveratrol

Resveratrol is a polyphenolic compound found in the skin of red grapes, berries, and some plants, known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Lycopene

Lycopene is a carotenoid pigment found in red fruits and vegetables, particularly tomatoes, and is known for its potent antioxidant properties that contribute to various health benefits.

Catechins

Catechins are a class of polyphenolic compounds found predominantly in plants, particularly in the leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant, and are known for their antioxidant properties and potential health benefits.

Flavonoids

Flavonoids are a diverse group of plant-derived compounds known for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and potential therapeutic properties in herbal medicine.

Alkaloids

Alkaloids are a class of naturally occurring organic compounds found in plants, characterized by their complex ring structures and often potent physiological effects on humans and animals.

Terpenoids

Terpenoids are a large and diverse class of organic compounds derived from the mevalonate pathway, commonly found in plants and responsible for a wide range of biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer properties.

Saponins

Saponins are a class of phytochemicals characterized by their soap-like properties, known for their ability to form stable foams when agitated in water and for their diverse biological activities including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects.

Phenolic Acids

Phenolic acids are a class of phytochemicals characterized by the presence of a hydroxyl group attached to an aromatic ring, and they play a significant role in the medicinal properties of many herbs due to their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.
