10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Artemisia absinthium (Wormwood)

Artemisia absinthium, also known as wormwood, has been used in medicinal preparations for centuries due to its bitter taste and therapeutic properties.

A common preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made by steeping the leaves and flowers in hot water. This warm beverage is often used to alleviate symptoms of digestive issues such as bloating, gas, and loss of appetite.

Additionally, a tincture can be made by soaking the plant material in alcohol, which extracts the active compounds. This concentrated liquid can be used to treat a variety of ailments, including menstrual pain, fever, and even certain types of intestinal worms.

However, it is important to note that wormwood contains thujone, a compound that can be toxic in large amounts, so it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

1. Infusion

When using this Artemisia absinthium preparation, it is important to be aware that it contains thujone, a compound that can be toxic in large amounts, so it should not be consumed in excess or by pregnant women.
