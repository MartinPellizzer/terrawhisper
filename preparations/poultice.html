Herbal Poultice: How to Prepare It Correctly

A herbal poultice is a topical application technique used in herbal medicine to deliver medicinal compounds from fresh or dried plant parts directly to the skin.

This method relies on the principle of maceration, where plant materials such as comfrey leaves or plantain are crushed and held against the skin to allow active ingredients to penetrate through gentle heat or wrapping. By using specific plant materials like willow bark or chamomile, practitioners target localized inflammation, wounds, or muscle strain with high concentration. This approach is one of several effective herbal preparation methods designed to treat external ailments without systemic absorption.

It remains a foundational practice for beginners learning how to utilize plants for immediate physical relief.
