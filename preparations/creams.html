What to know about creams for medicinal use?

Creams are semi-solid preparations made by combining oils, water, and emulsifying agents to create a smooth, spreadable texture.

In herbalism, creams are used to deliver medicinal herbs directly to the skin, allowing for localized treatment of conditions like inflammation, eczema, and muscle pain. They often contain extracts of herbs such as calendula, chamomile, or lavender, which have soothing and healing properties. The formulation helps to enhance the absorption of active herbal compounds through the skin.

Creams are a versatile and effective method of applying herbal remedies for topical health concerns.

What is a cream?

Creams are semi-solid preparations that contain a mixture of water, oil, and emulsifiers, often combined with herbal extracts to provide topical relief.

Unlike tinctures, which are liquid extracts made with alcohol, or salves, which are thicker and oil-based, creams are lighter and more easily absorbed into the skin. They are commonly used for moisturizing, soothing skin irritations, and delivering herbal compounds in a non-irritating form. Compared to other herbal preparations, creams offer a more user-friendly application and can be formulated for a variety of skin conditions.

Their benefits include enhanced skin hydration, localized treatment, and a pleasant sensory experience, making them a versatile option in herbal skincare.

How cream works?

Creams work by delivering active herbal compounds through the skin via emulsification, where water-soluble and oil-soluble ingredients are combined into a stable formulation.

The preparation typically involves extraction using heat, alcohol, or water to dissolve the active constituents from the plant material, followed by infusion or maceration to concentrate the extract. Common active constituents include essential oils, alkaloids, flavonoids, and tannins, which are known for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, or analgesic properties. These compounds are absorbed through the skin's stratum corneum and delivered to underlying tissues, enhancing local therapeutic effects while minimizing systemic absorption.

The controlled release and targeted delivery of the active ingredients improve the potency and efficacy of the herbal treatment.

What are the best herbal creams?

Some of the best herbal creams for medicinal purposes are Zingiber officinale, Glycyrrhiza glabra, Lavandula angustifolia, Echinacea purpurea, and Curcuma longa.

Zingiber officinale, or ginger, is known for its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties. Glycyrrhiza glabra, or licorice root, has soothing and antimicrobial effects that help with skin irritation.

Lavandula angustifolia, or lavender, is prized for its calming and healing properties that promote skin recovery. Echinacea purpurea boosts the immune system and reduces inflammation, making it ideal for topical use.

Curcuma longa, or turmeric, contains curcumin, a powerful antioxidant that reduces inflammation and promotes skin health.
