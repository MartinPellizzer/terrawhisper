What to know about suppositories for medicinal use?

Suppositories are solid dosage forms designed to melt at body temperature, allowing the active ingredients to be absorbed through the rectum or vagina.

In herbalism, they are used to deliver medicinal herbs directly into the bloodstream, bypassing the digestive system for faster and more consistent absorption. They are often made from natural waxes or oils and can contain various herbs known for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, or pain-relieving properties. Suppositories are commonly used for treating conditions such as infections, inflammation, or gastrointestinal issues.

This method of administration is particularly beneficial for individuals who cannot take oral medications due to nausea or other digestive issues.

What is a suppository?

Suppositories are medicated forms of herbal preparations designed to be inserted into the rectum or vagina for localized treatment.

Unlike oral herbal preparations, which are ingested and absorbed through the digestive system, suppositories deliver the active ingredients directly into the bloodstream through the rectal or vaginal mucosa. Suppositories are particularly useful for treating ailments such as hemorrhoids, infections, or inflammation in the lower gastrointestinal or reproductive tract.

Compared to topical applications or oral formulations, suppositories offer a targeted and efficient delivery system with potentially fewer systemic side effects.

How suppository works?

Suppositories work by delivering medicinal compounds directly into the rectum or vagina, where they dissolve and are absorbed into the bloodstream.

The preparation relies on the solubility of active ingredients in a base such as cocoa butter or glycerin, which melts at body temperature. Extraction methods often involve infusion or maceration, sometimes with the aid of heat or alcohol to enhance the release of bioactive compounds. Common active constituents include alkaloids, flavonoids, and essential oils, which are known for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, or analgesic properties.

Once absorbed, suppositories provide targeted delivery, enhancing potency and minimizing systemic side effects compared to oral administration.

These herbs are highly effective due to their anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and soothing properties. Calendula promotes healing and reduces irritation, making it ideal for sensitive tissues. Chamomile has calming effects and can help with digestive discomfort when used internally.

Lavender is known for its calming and pain-relieving qualities, enhancing the overall therapeutic benefit of the suppositories.
