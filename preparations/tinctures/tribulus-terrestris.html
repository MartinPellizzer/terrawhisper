Tribulus terrestris tincture for medicinal use

Tribulus terrestris tincture is a concentrated herbal preparation made from the seeds of the Tribulus terrestris plant, commonly known as puncture vine.

This tincture is traditionally used in herbalism to support vitality, enhance libido, and promote overall wellness. It is often taken as a supplement to help with energy levels and hormonal balance. The active compounds in the tincture are believed to support kidney function and improve physical performance.

It is typically used in small doses and is popular in traditional medicine systems like Ayurveda and Chinese herbalism.

Plant Parts

Tribulus terrestris tincture is primarily made from the seeds, which contain the highest concentration of active compounds like saponins.

The leaves are also commonly used, as they provide additional nutrients and phytochemicals that support overall health. While the roots are less frequently used in tinctures, they are sometimes included for their potential tonic properties. The flowers and stems are occasionally incorporated, but they are not as potent as the seeds or leaves.

Most traditional formulations prioritize the seeds and leaves for their medicinal benefits.

Side Effects

Tribulus terrestris tincture can cause gastrointestinal upset, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, especially when taken in high doses.

It may also lead to increased heart rate and elevated blood pressure in some individuals. Users might experience insomnia or restlessness due to its stimulating effects. In rare cases, it can cause allergic reactions such as skin rashes or itching.

Long-term use may result in hormonal imbalances, particularly in testosterone levels.
