Teucrium chamaedrys juice for medicinal use

Teucrium chamaedrys juice is a preparation made from the fresh or dried leaves and stems of the rock rose plant, which is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

This juice is traditionally used in herbalism for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It has been historically applied to treat skin conditions, such as eczema and wounds, due to its soothing and healing effects. The preparation may also support respiratory health when used in steam inhalations or as a gargle.

In some traditional systems, it is believed to aid in digestion and relieve symptoms of colds and flu.

Preparation

To make Teucrium chamaedrys juice, first gather fresh leaves of the plant, ensuring they are clean and free from pesticides.

Wash the leaves thoroughly under running water and pat them dry with a clean cloth. Next, blend the leaves with a small amount of water in a blender until a smooth pulp is formed. Strain the mixture through a fine mesh sieve or cheesecloth to separate the liquid from the pulp.

Finally, store the extracted juice in a sterilized glass container in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Side Effects

Teucrium chamaedrys juice may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its strong medicinal properties.

It may also cause allergic reactions in individuals sensitive to plants in the Lamiaceae family. Prolonged use could potentially lead to liver toxicity, as some compounds in the juice may be hepatotoxic. Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the liver or blood clotting.

It is important to consult a healthcare professional before using this preparation, especially for prolonged periods or in high doses.
