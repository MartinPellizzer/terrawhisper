Arctium lappa tea for medicinal use

Arctium lappa tea is a herbal preparation made from the dried leaves and seeds of the greater burdock plant.

It has been traditionally used in herbalism for its purported detoxifying and anti-inflammatory properties. The tea is often consumed to support digestive health and skin conditions. It may also be used to alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues and to promote lymphatic drainage.

Due to its potent nature, it is typically used in short cycles under the guidance of a qualified herbalist.

Health Benefits

Arctium lappa tea boosts immune system by stimulating the production of white blood cells, which help fight off infections.

It eases digestive issues due to its high content of compounds that promote healthy gut flora and reduce inflammation. The tea is known to cure worm infestations as it contains anthelmintic properties that expel parasites from the body. It supports liver function by detoxifying the body and aiding in the removal of toxins.

Additionally, Arctium lappa tea improves wound healing by enhancing collagen production and reducing inflammation in the affected areas.

Bioactive Constituents

Arctium lappa tea contains inulin, which acts as a prebiotic to support gut health by promoting the growth of beneficial bacteria.

It also includes saponins, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that may help in reducing cholesterol levels. Flavonoids present in the tea contribute to its antioxidant activity, protecting cells from oxidative stress and inflammation. Additionally, the tea contains fatty acids that may support skin health and reduce inflammation.

Polysaccharides in Arctium lappa tea are known for their immune-boosting effects and potential role in managing blood sugar levels.

Plant Parts

Arctium lappa tea is primarily made from the dried roots of the plant, which contain high concentrations of compounds like chicory acid and inulin.

The roots are the most commonly used part due to their potent medicinal properties and high nutritional value. While the leaves are sometimes used, they are less prevalent in traditional formulations compared to the roots. The flowers and seeds are occasionally included for additional benefits, but the roots remain the core component.

This focus on the roots highlights their significance in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.
