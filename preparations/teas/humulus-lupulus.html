Humulus lupulus tea for medicinal use

Humulus lupulus tea is a herbal preparation made from the dried flowers of the hops plant, which is commonly used in brewing beer but also has a long history in herbal medicine.

This tea is often consumed for its calming effects and is used to support sleep and reduce anxiety. It is believed to have mild sedative properties that can help ease restlessness and promote relaxation. In herbalism, it is sometimes combined with other calming herbs to enhance its soothing effects.

Humulus lupulus tea is typically prepared by steeping the dried hops in hot water for several minutes.

Health Benefits

Humulus lupulus tea relaxes nervous system by promoting a calming effect on the body, which can help alleviate tension and foster a sense of tranquility.

It relieves anxiety symptoms through its natural compounds that interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, reducing feelings of worry and unease. This tea also reduces stress hormones like cortisol, helping the body manage stress more effectively. Additionally, it has anti-inflammatory properties that can support overall bodily health and reduce chronic inflammation.

Finally, Humulus lupulus tea improves sleep quality by promoting relaxation and regulating the sleep-wake cycle.

Bioactive Constituents

Humulus lupulus tea contains xanthohumol, a prenylated flavonoid known for its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Alongside xanthohumol, the tea also includes humulone, a bitter compound that acts as a natural preservative and may have antimicrobial effects. Lupulone, another key constituent, contributes to the tea's bitterness and has been studied for its potential anti-cancer and anti-inflammatory benefits. Hesperidin, a flavonoid glycoside, supports cardiovascular health and enhances the bioavailability of other compounds.

Finally, kaempferol, a flavonol, exhibits strong antioxidant activity and may help in reducing oxidative stress in the body.

Plant Parts

Humulus lupulus tea, commonly known as hop tea, is primarily made from the cone-shaped flowers of the Humulus lupulus plant.

These flowers, called hop cones, are the most commonly used part for making the tea due to their high concentration of compounds like humulone and lupuline. While the leaves and stems are sometimes used in traditional preparations, they are less common compared to the hop cones. The roots of the plant are rarely used in medicinal teas, as they contain fewer active compounds.

Overall, the hop cones are the most essential and widely utilized part of the plant in the preparation of this type of tea.
