Angelica archangelica tea for medicinal use

Angelica archangelica tea is a herbal preparation made from the dried roots and leaves of the Angelica archangelica plant, commonly known as garden angelica.

This tea is traditionally used in herbalism to support digestive health, alleviate menstrual discomfort, and promote respiratory wellness. It is often consumed as a warming tonic to help with nausea, bloating, and cold symptoms. The plant is also valued for its potential to boost energy and support the immune system.

Due to its strong potency, it is typically used in small doses and under the guidance of a qualified herbalist.

Health Benefits

Angelica archangelica tea boosts immune system by providing essential nutrients and antioxidants that help the body fight off infections.

It also heals digestive issues by soothing the stomach lining and promoting healthy gut flora. This tea aids respiratory health by reducing inflammation and easing symptoms of coughs and bronchitis. Additionally, it treats nausea symptoms by calming the digestive system and reducing stomach discomfort.

Lastly, Angelica archangelica tea treats skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial and fungal growth.

Bioactive Constituents

Angelica archangelica tea contains ferulic acid, a powerful antioxidant that helps protect cells from damage.

It also includes ascorbic acid, commonly known as vitamin C, which supports immune function and skin health. The tea is rich in coumarin, a compound with anticoagulant properties that may aid in preventing blood clots. Additionally, it contains various phenolic acids and polyphenols, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

These bioactive constituents work together to provide the tea with its potential health benefits.

Plant Parts

Angelica archangelica tea is primarily made using the roots, stems, and leaves of the plant, with the roots being the most commonly used part due to their high concentration of medicinal compounds.

The roots are typically dried and ground into a powder before being brewed into tea, offering a strong and earthy flavor. The leaves, while less commonly used, can also be dried and infused to create a milder version of the tea. In some traditional preparations, the seeds and flowers may be included for additional herbal benefits.

Overall, the roots, stems, and leaves are the most valued parts for their therapeutic properties in Angelica archangelica tea.

Side Effects

Angelica archangelica tea can cause gastrointestinal discomfort such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea in some individuals.

It may also lead to allergic reactions, including skin rashes or itching, especially in people sensitive to related plants. The tea has been associated with increased menstrual flow and cramping, making it unsuitable for pregnant or breastfeeding women. Long-term use might result in liver toxicity, as some studies suggest potential harm to liver function.

Additionally, it can cause dizziness or headaches in certain users due to its mild sedative properties.
