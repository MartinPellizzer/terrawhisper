Glycyrrhiza glabra tea for medicinal use

Glycyrrhiza glabra tea is a herbal preparation made from the dried root of the licorice plant, known for its sweet taste and medicinal properties.

It is commonly used in herbalism to support respiratory health, soothe sore throats, and reduce inflammation. The tea may also help with digestive issues and is sometimes used as an expectorant to ease coughing. In traditional medicine, it has been valued for its adaptogenic qualities, helping the body manage stress.

However, long-term use can lead to side effects, so it should be used under professional guidance.

Health Benefits

Glycyrrhiza glabra tea eases digestive issues by soothing the stomach lining and reducing inflammation.

It treats ulcers effectively due to its high concentration of glycyrrhizin, which has potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This tea eases coughs quickly by acting as an expectorant and reducing irritation in the respiratory tract. It treats skin problems by promoting healing and reducing redness and irritation.

Additionally, Glycyrrhiza glabra tea eases joint pains by reducing inflammation and providing natural pain relief.

Bioactive Constituents

Glycyrrhiza glabra tea contains glycyrrhizin, a potent glycyrrhizic acid derivative known for its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

Saponins present in the tea contribute to its immune-modulating effects and help in reducing cholesterol levels. Triterpenoids found in the tea exhibit antioxidant and anti-cancer activities, supporting overall health. Lipids in the tea may aid in nutrient absorption and contribute to the tea's smooth flavor profile.

Additionally, saponin glycosides enhance the bioavailability of other compounds and support the tea's therapeutic benefits.

Plant Parts

Glycyrrhiza glabra tea is primarily made from the roots of the plant, which contain the highest concentration of active compounds like glycyrrhizin.

The roots are the most commonly used part because they provide the characteristic bitterness and medicinal properties associated with licorice tea. While the leaves and stems are sometimes used, they contribute less to the overall potency and flavor profile. The root is typically dried and ground into a powder or sliced for infusion.

This part of the plant is also valued for its anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects in traditional medicine.
