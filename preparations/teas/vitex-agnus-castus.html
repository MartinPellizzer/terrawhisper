Vitex agnus-castus tea for medicinal use

Vitex agnus-castus tea is made from the dried flowers of the chasteberry plant, which has been used for centuries in traditional herbal medicine.

It is commonly used to support hormonal balance, particularly in women, to alleviate symptoms related to menstruation, PMS, and menopause. The tea is believed to influence the pituitary gland and regulate the production of hormones like prolactin and estrogen. It is often prepared by steeping the dried flowers in hot water for several minutes.

In herbalism, it is also used to address issues such as irregular menstrual cycles and emotional symptoms associated with hormonal fluctuations.

Uses

Vitex agnus-castus tea has been used to support hormonal balance, particularly in women, for centuries.

Historically, it was revered in ancient Greece and Rome for its purported ability to regulate menstrual cycles and ease symptoms of premenstrual syndrome. Traditional herbal medicine systems, such as those in Europe and the Middle East, have long utilized it for its purported calming and reproductive health benefits. In modern times, it is often consumed as a natural remedy for menstrual irregularities, menopause symptoms, and emotional well-being.

Scientific research continues to explore its potential effects on the endocrine system, though more studies are needed to fully understand its mechanisms and efficacy.
