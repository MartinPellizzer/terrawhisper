Crataegus monogyna tea for medicinal use

Crataegus monogyna tea is a herbal preparation made from the leaves and flowers of the hawthorn tree, known for its calming and cardiovascular benefits.

It is commonly used in herbalism to support heart health, reduce stress, and promote relaxation. The tea is often recommended for individuals experiencing mild anxiety, insomnia, or high blood pressure. Its mild flavor and soothing properties make it a popular choice in traditional medicine.

Regular consumption may help improve circulation and ease symptoms of nervous exhaustion.

Health Benefits

Crataegus monogyna tea lower cholesterol by helping to reduce low-density lipoprotein (LDL) levels, which is often referred to as "bad" cholesterol.

It also has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce systemic inflammation, which is linked to various chronic diseases. This tea may contribute to lowering blood pressure by promoting better vascular function and improving blood flow. Its positive effects on heart health are attributed to its ability to support healthy circulation and reduce oxidative stress.

Additionally, Crataegus monogyna tea may protect liver function by supporting detoxification processes and reducing liver inflammation.

Bioactive Constituents

Crataegus monogyna tea contains linoleic acid, an omega-6 fatty acid that supports cardiovascular health and skin integrity.

It also includes crataegus monogyna flavonoids, which act as antioxidants and may reduce inflammation. Quercetin, a potent flavonoid found in the tea, exhibits anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. Additionally, the tea contains crataegus monogyna triterpenes, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects.

Ursolic acid, another key triterpene, is known for its ability to support metabolic health and reduce oxidative stress.

Plant Parts

Crataegus monogyna tea is primarily made from the leaves, flowers, and fruits of the plant, with the leaves being the most commonly used part due to their high concentration of bioactive compounds.

The flowers are also frequently included for their mild, aromatic properties and additional health benefits. While the fruits, known as haws, are sometimes used, they are less common in traditional preparations. Roots are rarely used in medicinal tea formulations because they contain fewer active compounds compared to the leaves and flowers.

Overall, the leaves and flowers are the most valued components for their therapeutic effects in Crataegus monogyna tea.
