Citrus reticulata tea for medicinal use

Citrus reticulata tea is a herbal preparation made from the dried peel of the mandarin orange, known for its aromatic and slightly sweet flavor.

This tea is commonly used in herbalism to support digestion and relieve symptoms of nausea and bloating. It is also valued for its calming properties and is often consumed to reduce stress and promote relaxation. In traditional medicine, it is believed to aid in detoxification and improve liver function.

Its high content of flavonoids and vitamin C contributes to its antioxidant and immune-boosting benefits.

Plant Parts

Citrus reticulata tea is primarily made using the leaves, peel, and fruit of the plant.

The leaves are commonly used for their aromatic properties and are often dried and steeped to create a fragrant infusion. The peel, especially the outer white part known as the pith, is also popular for its citrusy flavor and potential health benefits. While the roots are less frequently used in tea preparation, they may be included in some traditional formulations.

Overall, the leaves and peel are the most utilized parts for making medicinal tea from Citrus reticulata.

Side Effects

Citrus reticulata tea can cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort such as nausea, bloating, or diarrhea in some individuals, especially when consumed in large quantities.

It may also lead to increased heart rate or mild anxiety due to its natural citrus compounds and bioactive ingredients. Some people may experience skin irritation or allergic reactions if they are sensitive to citrus fruits. Long-term use might interfere with certain medications, particularly those affecting the liver or blood pressure.

Overall, it is generally safe when consumed in moderation and as part of a balanced diet.
