Allium sativum cream for medicinal use

Allium sativum cream is a topical preparation made from garlic, which is known for its medicinal properties.

It is created by extracting the active compounds from garlic cloves and incorporating them into a base suitable for application on the skin. In herbalism, this cream is used to treat various skin conditions, such as fungal infections and inflammation. The active components in garlic, like allicin, have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

It is often applied directly to the affected area to promote healing and reduce symptoms.

Bioactive Constituents

Allium sativum cream contains allicin, a sulfur-containing compound known for its potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Diallyl disulfide, another key constituent, contributes to the cream's ability to support skin health and reduce oxidative stress. Sulfenic acid, a reactive compound formed when allicin is released, enhances the therapeutic effects of the cream. Additionally, sulfur compounds in the cream play a crucial role in promoting cellular detoxification and improving skin texture.

Quercetin, a natural flavonoid, further supports the cream's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits, making it a valuable ingredient for topical applications.

Plant Parts

Allium sativum cream is primarily made using the bulbs of the garlic plant, which are the most commonly utilized part for their medicinal properties.

The bulbs contain essential oils, sulfur compounds, and other bioactive substances that contribute to its therapeutic effects. While the leaves and roots of Allium sativum are sometimes used, they are less common in commercial medicinal creams compared to the bulbs. The outer layers of the bulb, known as the skin, are often removed before processing.

These components work together to provide anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and pain-relieving benefits in the cream.

Side Effects

Allium sativum cream can cause skin irritation, including redness, itching, and a burning sensation, especially in individuals with sensitive skin.

It may also lead to allergic reactions, such as hives or rash, in those who are allergic to garlic or related compounds. Prolonged use might result in dryness or peeling of the skin due to its antimicrobial properties. In rare cases, it could cause contact dermatitis, which is an inflammatory skin reaction.

It is important to perform a patch test before applying it to a larger area of the skin.
