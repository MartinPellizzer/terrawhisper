Aloe vera cream for medicinal use

Aloe vera cream is a topical preparation made from the gel-like substance extracted from the leaves of the Aloe barbadensis plant.

It is widely used in herbalism for its soothing and healing properties, particularly for skin conditions such as burns, eczema, and psoriasis. The cream contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial compounds that help reduce redness, irritation, and infection. It is often applied directly to the skin to promote wound healing and moisturize dry areas.

In traditional herbal practices, it is also used to support digestive health when consumed in small amounts.

Health Benefits

Aloe vera cream heals burns quickly due to its soothing and regenerative properties that promote skin cell repair.

It treats sunburn fast by cooling the skin and reducing redness and irritation. Aloe vera cream heals wounds faster by creating a protective barrier and stimulating collagen production. It soothes skin issues such as acne, eczema, and rashes with its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Additionally, it reduces inflammation fast, making it an effective remedy for various skin conditions.

Bioactive Constituents

Aloe vera cream contains mucopolysaccharides, which help in maintaining skin hydration and promoting tissue repair.

Anthraquinones, another key constituent, exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that aid in soothing irritated skin. Beta-sitosterol contributes to the cream's ability to reduce inflammation and enhance skin barrier function. Terpenoids provide antioxidant benefits, protecting the skin from oxidative stress and environmental damage.

Additionally, the presence of vitamins, such as vitamin A and C, supports skin health by promoting cell regeneration and collagen synthesis.

Plant Parts

Aloe vera cream is primarily made from the gel found inside the leaves of the Aloe vera plant.

The leaves are the most commonly used part because they contain the thick, clear gel that is rich in nutrients and bioactive compounds. This gel is extracted by carefully slicing the leaves and removing the pulp. The outer layer of the leaf, known as the skin, is sometimes used as well, but it is less common in commercial products.

The roots of the Aloe vera plant are rarely used in medicinal creams due to their limited availability and lower concentration of beneficial compounds.

Side Effects

Aloe vera cream can cause skin irritation, especially in individuals with sensitive skin or allergies to the plant.

It may lead to a burning or stinging sensation when applied to open wounds or broken skin. Some people may experience redness, itching, or a rash as a reaction to the active ingredients. In rare cases, it can cause more severe allergic reactions such as hives or swelling.

It is important to perform a patch test before using aloe vera cream to minimize the risk of adverse effects.
