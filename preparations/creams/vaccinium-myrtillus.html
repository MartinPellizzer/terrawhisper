Vaccinium myrtillus cream for medicinal use

Vaccinium myrtillus cream is a topical herbal preparation made from the berries of the bilberry plant, which is known for its high concentration of antioxidants and anthocyanins.

This cream is used in herbalism to support skin health, reduce inflammation, and promote healing of minor wounds and irritations. It is often applied directly to the skin to alleviate conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and minor burns. The active compounds in bilberries help to strengthen blood vessels and improve circulation, making it beneficial for skin repair.

Herbalists also recommend it for its potential protective effects against UV damage and its soothing properties for sensitive skin.

Health Benefits

Vaccinium myrtillus cream boosts immune system by stimulating the production of white blood cells, which are essential for fighting off infections.

It also eases inflammation by containing anti-inflammatory compounds that reduce swelling and pain in affected areas. The cream helps reduce blood pressure through its vasodilatory effects, promoting better blood flow and cardiovascular health. Additionally, it fights oxidative stress by neutralizing free radicals that can damage cells and tissues.

Overall, it supports heart health by improving circulation and reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases.

Bioactive Constituents

Vaccinium myrtillus cream contains anthocyanins, which are powerful antioxidants that help reduce inflammation and support skin health.

It also includes quercetin, a flavonoid known for its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. The cream provides a range of amino acids that contribute to skin repair and maintenance of its structural integrity. Ellagic acid is present, offering additional antioxidant benefits and potential anti-cancer properties.

Lastly, gallic acid is included, which has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects that further enhance the cream's therapeutic value.

Plant Parts

Vaccinium myrtillus cream is primarily made using the leaves and flowers of the plant, which are rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds.

The leaves are often harvested during the summer months when their medicinal properties are most concentrated. The flowers, known for their high levels of flavonoids, are also commonly used to enhance the cream's therapeutic effects. Roots are occasionally included in some formulations, though they are less frequently used compared to leaves and flowers.

These parts are carefully processed to extract their active compounds, ensuring the cream's efficacy and safety.

Side Effects

Vaccinium myrtillus cream can cause mild skin irritation, including redness and itching, in some individuals.

It may also lead to a burning sensation or tingling upon application. Some users might experience allergic reactions, such as hives or rash. In rare cases, it can cause contact dermatitis, especially in people with sensitive skin.

It is important to perform a patch test before using the cream regularly.
