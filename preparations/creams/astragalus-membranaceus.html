Astragalus membranaceus cream for medicinal use

Astragalus membranaceus cream is a topical herbal preparation made from the root of the Astragalus membranaceus plant, commonly known as Huang Qi in traditional Chinese medicine.

This cream is often used in herbalism to support immune function and promote skin health due to the plant's adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties. The active compounds in the cream, such as polysaccharides and flavonoids, are believed to enhance the body's natural defenses and reduce inflammation. It is applied externally to treat conditions like eczema, psoriasis, and skin infections.

In traditional practices, it is also used to aid in the recovery from illnesses and to improve overall vitality.

Astragalus membranaceus cream reduces inflammation by containing compounds that inhibit inflammatory pathways in the body.

It supports liver function by promoting detoxification processes and protecting liver cells from damage. The cream also supports the respiratory system by enhancing lung function and reducing mucus production. Bioactive Constituents

Astragalus membranaceus cream contains phenylethanoid glycosides, which are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Polysaccharides in the cream contribute to immune modulation and cellular protection. Saponins provide additional anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits. Astragalosides, a group of flavonoid glycosides, support cardiovascular health and enhance the body's natural defenses.

Monosaccharides in the formulation may aid in energy metabolism and skin hydration.

Plant Parts

Astragalus membranaceus cream primarily utilizes the roots of the plant, which are the most medicinally potent part.

The roots contain high concentrations of bioactive compounds such as polysaccharides, flavonoids, and saponins. These compounds are extracted and processed to create the active ingredients for the cream. The leaves are sometimes used as a secondary source, but they contribute less to the medicinal properties compared to the roots.

The root extract is the key component that provides the plant's immune-boosting and anti-inflammatory benefits in the cream.

Side Effects

Astragalus membranaceus cream can cause mild skin irritation, including redness, itching, or a burning sensation, especially in individuals with sensitive skin.

Some users may experience an allergic reaction, such as hives or swelling, if they are allergic to any of the ingredients. It is also possible to develop contact dermatitis, which is a type of eczema triggered by the cream's components. In rare cases, the cream may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort if applied near mucous membranes.

As with any herbal product, it is advisable to perform a patch test before full application to minimize adverse effects.
