Boswellia serrata lozenge for medicinal use

Boswellia serrata lozenge is a herbal preparation made from the resin of the Boswellia serrata tree, commonly known as Indian frankincense.

It is used in herbalism to support respiratory health and reduce inflammation in the airways. The lozenge form allows for direct application to the throat, making it effective for soothing soreness and easing coughing. It is often recommended for individuals suffering from conditions like bronchitis or chronic cough.

This preparation is valued for its natural anti-inflammatory and bronchodilating properties.

Preparation

To make Boswellia serrata lozenge, first gather dried Boswellia serrata resin, also known as frankincense.

Next, grind the resin into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle or a spice grinder. Then, mix the powdered resin with a binding agent such as honey or glycerin, and add a small amount of water to create a paste. Shape the mixture into small lozenges using a mold or by hand, and allow them to dry in a cool, dark place for several days.

Finally, store the lozenges in an airtight container to preserve their potency and freshness.

Side Effects

Boswellia serrata lozenge may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort, including nausea, diarrhea, and stomach pain, especially when taken in high doses.

It can also cause allergic reactions in individuals sensitive to frankincense, which is the resin from which Boswellia serrata is derived. Some users may experience headaches or dizziness as side effects. Long-term use might interfere with blood clotting, increasing the risk of bleeding.

It is important to consult a healthcare provider before using Boswellia serrata lozenge, particularly if you are on other medications or have existing health conditions.
