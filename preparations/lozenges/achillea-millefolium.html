Achillea millefolium lozenge for medicinal use

Achillea millefolium lozenge is a herbal preparation made from the dried herb of the yarrow plant, known for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

It is commonly used in herbalism to soothe sore throats, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of colds and respiratory infections. The lozenge form allows for direct application to the throat, enhancing its local effects. It may also help in reducing excess mucus and promoting healing of minor oral irritations.

This preparation is often recommended as a natural alternative to conventional throat lozenges.

Uses

Achillea millefolium lozenge has been used to treat respiratory and oral health issues for centuries.

Historically, it was valued by ancient civilizations such as the Greeks and Romans for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. Traditional uses included soothing sore throats, reducing inflammation, and treating coughs and colds. In modern times, it is still used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health and as a natural remedy for minor oral infections.

Its continued use highlights its enduring reputation as a versatile and effective herbal preparation.

Constituents

Achillea millefolium lozenge active constituents include sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, volatile oils, and polyphenols.

These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The sesquiterpene lactones are known for their ability to reduce inflammation and support immune function. Flavonoids help protect cells from oxidative stress, while volatile oils may aid in soothing respiratory discomfort.

Preparation

To make Achillea millefolium lozenge, first gather fresh or dried yarrow (Achillea millefolium) leaves and flowers.

Next, crush the plant material and steep it in hot water to create a strong herbal infusion. After the infusion cools, strain the liquid to remove plant matter. Then, mix the liquid with a binding agent such as honey or glycerin to form a thick syrup.

Finally, pour the syrup into molds and allow it to set in a cool, dry place to create the lozenges.

Side Effects

Achillea millefolium lozenge may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort, including nausea, vomiting, and stomach cramps, due to its high content of volatile oils.

It may also cause allergic reactions in individuals sensitive to plants in the Asteraceae family, such as ragweed. Prolonged use could result in liver toxicity, as some compounds in the herb may be hepatotoxic. It is contraindicated for pregnant women and those with bleeding disorders due to its potential anticoagulant effects.

Always consult a healthcare provider before using this preparation, especially if taking other medications.
