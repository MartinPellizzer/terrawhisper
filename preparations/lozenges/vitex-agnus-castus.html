Vitex agnus-castus lozenge for medicinal use

Vitex agnus-castus lozenge is a herbal preparation made from the dried fruit of the chasteberry plant, commonly used in herbal medicine.

The lozenge form allows for easy administration and may help with symptoms such as mood swings and breast tenderness. It is typically taken as a dietary supplement, often in combination with other herbs.

Uses

Vitex agnus-castus lozenge has been used to support hormonal balance and alleviate symptoms related to menstrual cycles for centuries.

Historically, it was revered in ancient Greek and Roman medicine for its purported ability to regulate female reproductive health. Traditional uses also included treating infertility and easing mood swings, particularly during menstruation and menopause. In modern times, it is often used as a natural remedy for PMS, irregular periods, and hormonal imbalances.

Today, lozenges containing Vitex agnus-castus are popular in herbal medicine for their purported supportive role in women's health.

The lozenge form allows for easy consumption and can provide a steady release of the herb's active compounds. Vitex agnus-castus lozenge is often used as a natural remedy for women's health concerns and can be a complementary therapy under professional guidance.

Preparation

To make Vitex agnus-castus lozenge, start by selecting high-quality Vitex agnus-castus herb, typically in dried form.

Crush the dried herb into a fine powder and mix it with a binding agent such as honey or a sugar-free syrup to help hold the lozenge together. Shape the mixture into small, easy-to-chew lozenges using a mold or by hand. Allow the lozenges to dry completely in a cool, dark place to preserve their potency and extend shelf life.

These lozenges can be used as a natural remedy for hormonal balance and menstrual support.
