Preparation

To make Eleutherococcus senticosus lozenge, first gather the dried root of Eleutherococcus senticosus, also known as Siberian ginseng.

Next, grind the root into a fine powder and mix it with a small amount of honey or a sugar-free sweetener to create a paste. Then, add a binding agent such as cornstarch or gelatin to help form the lozenges. Shape the mixture into small lozenges and allow them to dry completely in a cool, dark place.

Finally, store the lozenges in an airtight container to preserve their potency and freshness.

Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners or antidepressants, potentially increasing the risk of adverse effects.
