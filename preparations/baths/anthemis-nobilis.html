Anthemis nobilis bath for medicinal use

Anthemis nobilis bath is a traditional herbal preparation made by infusing the dried flowers of chamomile into water, which is then used for bathing.

This bath is known for its calming and soothing properties, often used to relieve stress, anxiety, and skin irritations. Chamomile contains compounds like bisabolol and alpha-bisabolol that have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects. It is commonly used in herbalism to promote relaxation and aid in the healing of minor skin conditions.

The warm water helps enhance the absorption of the herbal compounds, making it a popular remedy for both physical and emotional well-being.

Uses

Anthemis nobilis bath has been used to promote relaxation, alleviate anxiety, and soothe skin irritations for centuries.

Historically, it was valued in ancient Greece and Rome for its calming properties, often incorporated into bath rituals to ease stress and improve mood. Traditional herbalists in various cultures have used it to treat insomnia and digestive issues, believing its essential oils had a harmonizing effect on the body. In modern times, it is widely used in aromatherapy and skincare products for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

Today, it remains a popular choice for creating a soothing bath experience that supports both mental and physical well-being.

Preparation

To make Anthemis nobilis bath, start by boiling 1 liter of water in a pot.

Add 1 to 2 tablespoons of dried Anthemis nobilis (chamomile) to the boiling water and let it steep for 10 to 15 minutes. Strain the liquid into a large bathtub or a container, then add warm water to fill the tub. Allow the bath to cool to a comfortable temperature before soaking for 15 to 30 minutes.
