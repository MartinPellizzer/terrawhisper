Sambucus nigra linctuse for medicinal use

Sambucus nigra linctuse is a traditional herbal preparation made from the berries of the European elder tree, commonly used in historical herbal medicine.

It is typically prepared as a syrup or tincture and is known for its expectorant and antitussive properties. This preparation is used to help alleviate symptoms of respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis by loosening mucus and reducing irritation in the throat. In herbalism, it is often recommended for its soothing effects on the respiratory tract.

However, it should be used with caution, as raw elder berries can be toxic.

Uses

Sambucus nigra linctuse has been used to treat respiratory conditions such as coughs, colds, and bronchitis for centuries.

Historically, it was valued in traditional medicine across Europe for its expectorant and antiseptic properties, often prepared as a syrup or tincture. In modern times, it is still used in herbal remedies to soothe sore throats and reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract. Its active compounds, including flavonoids and mucilage, are believed to contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Today, it is available in various forms and is often recommended as a natural alternative for mild respiratory ailments.

Benefits

Sambucus nigra linctuse has health benefits such as soothing respiratory ailments, reducing inflammation, and supporting immune function.

It is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of coughs, colds, and bronchitis due to its expectorant and antispasmodic properties. The preparation contains compounds like flavonoids and mucilage, which help to loosen mucus and ease breathing. It may also have antioxidant effects that contribute to overall wellness.

However, it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

Constituents

Sambucus nigra linctuse active constituents include flavonoids, sambucol, mucilage, and essential oils.

These components contribute to its traditional use in soothing respiratory tract irritation and reducing coughing. Flavonoids possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, while sambucol is believed to have antiviral effects. Mucilage helps to coat and protect the throat, providing relief from irritation.

Essential oils may offer antimicrobial benefits, supporting overall respiratory health.

Preparation

To make Sambucus nigra linctuse, start by harvesting the ripe berries of the elder tree, ensuring they are fully mature and free from any signs of mold or spoilage.

Clean the berries thoroughly and remove the stems and seeds. Next, combine the berries with a small amount of water in a saucepan and simmer gently over low heat for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent burning. Strain the mixture through a fine mesh sieve to remove the solids, then add honey or sugar to taste and stir until fully dissolved.

Allow the mixture to cool before transferring it to a clean, airtight container for storage.

Side Effects

Sambucus nigra linctuse may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high mucilage content.

It may also cause allergic reactions in individuals sensitive to plants in the Caprifoliaceae family. Prolonged use could potentially lead to kidney damage, as some compounds in the preparation may be nephrotoxic. There is also a risk of liver toxicity with long-term or excessive consumption.

It is important to consult a healthcare professional before using this preparation, especially for individuals with pre-existing health conditions.
