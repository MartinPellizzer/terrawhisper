Angelica archangelica essential oil for medicinal use

Angelica archangelica essential oil is a potent and aromatic oil derived from the root of the angelica plant, known for its strong, spicy scent and high concentration of compounds like angelica acid and sesquiterpenes.

It has been traditionally used in herbalism to support digestive health, alleviate respiratory issues, and promote circulation due to its warming and stimulating properties. This essential oil is often diluted and used in aromatherapy or applied topically to relieve muscle pain and congestion. It is also valued for its ability to support the nervous system and is sometimes used in blends for its uplifting and calming effects.

However, it should be used with caution due to its potential toxicity if ingested in large amounts.

Health Benefits

Angelica archangelica essential oil boosts immune system by stimulating the production of white blood cells and enhancing the body's natural defenses against pathogens.

It also heals digestive issues by reducing inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract and promoting healthy gut flora. This essential oil aids respiratory health by acting as a natural expectorant and helping to clear mucus from the airways. Additionally, it treats nausea symptoms through its calming and anti-emetic properties, making it useful for motion sickness or morning sickness.

Lastly, Angelica archangelica essential oil treats skin infections due to its antimicrobial and antifungal compounds that combat bacterial and fungal growth.

Bioactive Constituents

Angelica archangelica essential oil contains ferulic acid, which is a powerful antioxidant known for its ability to neutralize free radicals and support skin health.

Alongside ferulic acid, the oil also includes ascorbic acid, commonly known as vitamin C, which enhances immune function and promotes collagen synthesis. Coumarin is another key constituent, recognized for its aromatic properties and potential anti-inflammatory effects. The presence of various phenolic acids and polyphenols contributes to the oil's overall antioxidant and antimicrobial activity.

Together, these bioactive compounds make Angelica archangelica essential oil a valuable ingredient in natural health and cosmetic formulations.

Plant Parts

Angelica archangelica essential oil is primarily derived from the roots and seeds of the plant, which are the most commonly used parts for extraction.

The roots contain high concentrations of volatile compounds that contribute to the oil's therapeutic properties. While the leaves and stems are sometimes used, they yield a lower quantity of essential oil compared to the roots. The seeds are also valued for their aromatic profile and are often used in conjunction with the roots.

Overall, the roots and seeds are the most significant parts in the production of Angelica archangelica essential oil.

Side Effects

Angelica archangelica essential oil can cause skin irritation, including redness, itching, and burning, especially when applied undiluted.

It may also lead to gastrointestinal discomfort such as nausea or vomiting if ingested. Some individuals may experience allergic reactions, including hives or respiratory issues. Prolonged use can result in hormonal imbalances due to its phytoestrogen content.

It is important to consult a healthcare professional before using this oil, particularly for pregnant or nursing women.
