Eurycoma longifolia essential oil for medicinal use

Eurycoma longifolia essential oil is a potent aromatic extract derived from the roots of the Eurycoma longifolia plant, commonly known as Tongkat Ali.

It is widely used in traditional herbal medicine, particularly in Southeast Asia, for its purported adaptogenic and aphrodisiac properties. The oil is often employed to enhance vitality, reduce stress, and support sexual health due to its active compounds like quassinoids. In herbalism, it is typically diluted with a carrier oil and applied topically or used in aromatherapy to promote energy and well-being.

Its use extends to supporting immune function and improving physical performance in various traditional practices.

Plant Parts

Eurycoma longifolia essential oil is primarily derived from the roots and bark of the plant, which contain the highest concentration of bioactive compounds.

The roots are the most commonly used part for extraction due to their rich content of alkaloids and flavonoids. The bark is also frequently utilized, as it provides a significant amount of essential oils with therapeutic properties. Leaves are sometimes used, but they contribute less oil compared to the roots and bark.

Overall, the roots and bark are the most valuable parts for producing the medicinal essential oil of Eurycoma longifolia.

Side Effects

Eurycoma longifolia essential oil can cause skin irritation, including redness, itching, and burning sensations, particularly when applied directly to the skin without dilution.

It may also lead to allergic reactions in some individuals, manifesting as hives or more severe dermatitis. Gastrointestinal discomfort, such as nausea or upset stomach, is another common side effect when ingested in high concentrations. Prolonged use may result in hormonal imbalances due to its potent phytoestrogenic properties.

As with any essential oil, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with sensitive skin or existing health conditions.
