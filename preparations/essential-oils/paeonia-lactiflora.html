Paeonia lactiflora essential oil for medicinal use

Paeonia lactiflora essential oil is a highly valued aromatic extract derived from the roots of the peony plant, known for its rich therapeutic properties.

It is commonly used in herbalism for its calming and sedative effects, helping to alleviate anxiety and promote relaxation. This essential oil is also believed to support respiratory health and ease symptoms of coughs and colds. In traditional Chinese medicine, it has been utilized for its ability to harmonize the body's energy and reduce inflammation.

Due to its potency, it is typically diluted before use in aromatherapy or topical applications.

Health Benefits

Paeonia lactiflora essential oil treats menstrual cramps by reducing uterine contractions and easing pelvic discomfort through its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

It also relaxes muscle tensions by stimulating the parasympathetic nervous system, promoting overall relaxation and reducing physical stress. This essential oil relieves anxiety symptoms by balancing neurotransmitter activity and promoting a sense of calm and well-being. Additionally, it calms skin conditions such as eczema and acne due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

Lastly, Paeonia lactiflora essential oil may help reduce blood pressure by improving circulation and supporting cardiovascular health.

Bioactive Constituents

Paeonia lactiflora essential oil contains paeoniflorin, a glycoside known for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

In addition to paeoniflorin, the oil includes triterpene saponins, which contribute to its antimicrobial and immunomodulatory effects. Paeonilactone B, another key constituent, exhibits antioxidant and antitumor activities. Alkaloids present in the oil may support cardiovascular health and have neuroprotective potential.

Lastly, quercetin, a flavonoid, enhances the oil's antioxidant capacity and contributes to its anti-cancer and anti-inflammatory benefits.

Plant Parts

Paeonia lactiflora essential oil is primarily derived from the roots and flowers of the plant, as these parts contain the highest concentration of bioactive compounds.

The roots are particularly valued for their rich content of alkaloids and flavonoids, which contribute to the oil's therapeutic properties. Flowers are also commonly used, especially during their blooming season, to extract volatile oils with aromatic and anti-inflammatory benefits. While leaves and stems can be used to some extent, they yield a lower quantity and less potent essential oil compared to the roots and flowers.

Overall, the roots and flowers are the most preferred and effective parts for producing medicinal essential oil from Paeonia lactiflora.
