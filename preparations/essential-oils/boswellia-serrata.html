Boswellia serrata essential oil for medicinal use

Boswellia serrata essential oil is derived from the resin of the frankincense tree, known for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

It is widely used in herbalism to support joint health and alleviate symptoms of conditions like arthritis. The oil is often applied topically to reduce pain and inflammation in muscles and joints. It may also be used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and ease respiratory congestion.

Due to its potent nature, it is typically diluted before use to avoid skin irritation.

Health Benefits

Boswellia serrata essential oil lowers blood pressure by promoting vasodilation and reducing stress-induced hypertension.

The oil also fights osteoarthritis pain by inhibiting enzymes that contribute to cartilage degradation. Additionally, it reduces inflammation throughout the body, supporting overall immune health and cellular function.

Lastly, Boswellia serrata essential oil fights anxiety attacks by modulating neurotransmitter activity and calming the nervous system.

Bioactive Constituents

Boswellia serrata essential oil contains boswellic acids, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Among these, acetyl-11-keto-beta-boswellic acid is particularly effective in inhibiting 5-lipoxygenase, a key enzyme in inflammation. Ferulic acid and caffeic acid contribute to the oil's antioxidant activity, helping to neutralize free radicals. Quercetin, a flavonoid present in the oil, further enhances its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Together, these bioactive constituents make Boswellia serrata essential oil a valuable natural remedy for various inflammatory conditions.
