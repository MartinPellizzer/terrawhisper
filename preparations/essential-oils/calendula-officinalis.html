Calendula officinalis essential oil for medicinal use

Calendula officinalis essential oil is a concentrated extract derived from the flowers of the Calendula plant, known for its vibrant orange hue and potent therapeutic properties.

It is commonly used in herbalism for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing benefits. This oil is often applied topically to treat skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and minor burns. It may also be used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

Due to its strong potency, it is typically diluted before use to ensure safety and effectiveness.

Health Benefits

Calendula officinalis essential oil reduce inflammation now by containing anti-inflammatory compounds that calm irritated tissues.

It reduces redness fast due to its ability to modulate histamine response and promote skin healing. This oil fights off bacteria through its natural antimicrobial properties, making it effective for preventing infections. It heal wounds quickly by stimulating cell regeneration and enhancing tissue repair processes.

Additionally, it soothe sunburned skin with its cooling and moisturizing effects, offering relief from heat and irritation.

Bioactive Constituents

Calendula officinalis essential oil contains bisabolol, a key monoterpene that exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

It also includes flavonoids, which contribute to its antioxidant and photoprotective effects. Caffeic acid and quercetin are present, both known for their strong antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Additionally, the oil contains sesquiterpenes, which play a role in its antifungal and anti-inflammatory actions.

Together, these bioactive constituents make Calendula officinalis essential oil valuable in pharmaceutical and cosmetic applications.

Plant Parts

Calendula officinalis essential oil is primarily derived from the flowers of the plant, which contain the highest concentration of active compounds.

The petals are the most commonly used part for extraction, as they provide the majority of the essential oil's therapeutic properties. While leaves and stems can also be used, they yield a lower volume of oil compared to the flowers. Roots are rarely used in essential oil production due to their minimal contribution to the oil's composition.

The flowers are typically harvested at full bloom to ensure maximum potency and aroma.

Side Effects

Calendula officinalis essential oil can cause skin irritation, redness, and itching in some individuals, especially when applied directly to the skin without dilution.

It may also lead to allergic reactions, such as hives or contact dermatitis, in people sensitive to plants in the Asteraceae family. Some users may experience a burning or stinging sensation upon application, which can be uncomfortable. In rare cases, it may trigger respiratory symptoms like coughing or wheezing, particularly in those with asthma.

It is important to perform a patch test before using this oil to minimize the risk of adverse effects.
