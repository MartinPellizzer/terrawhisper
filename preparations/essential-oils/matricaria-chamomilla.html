Matricaria chamomilla essential oil for medicinal use

Matricaria chamomilla essential oil is a highly valued essential oil derived from the flowers of the chamomile plant.

It is commonly used in herbalism for its calming and soothing properties. This preparation is known for its ability to relieve stress, promote relaxation, and aid in sleep. It is often used in aromatherapy, topical applications, and as a natural remedy for digestive issues.

Its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities make it a versatile tool in holistic medicine.

Health Benefits

Matricaria chamomilla essential oil relieves digestive issues by soothing the gastrointestinal tract and reducing inflammation.

It treats insomnia problems through its calming properties that promote relaxation and ease mental tension. This oil improves sleep quality by regulating the nervous system and encouraging a sense of tranquility. Additionally, it reduces stress levels by interacting with the body's stress response mechanisms.

Furthermore, Matricaria chamomilla essential oil reduces nausea levels, making it beneficial for individuals experiencing motion sickness or morning sickness.

Bioactive Constituents

Matricaria chamomilla essential oil contains flavonoids, which are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Among these, apigenin is a prominent flavone that contributes to the oil's calming and analgesic effects. Bisabolol, a sesquiterpene, is another key constituent that exhibits anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. Terpinen-4-ol is a major monoterpenoid that provides antimicrobial and antifungal benefits.

Additionally, flavonol glycosides enhance the oil's overall bioavailability and contribute to its therapeutic profile.

Plant Parts

Matricaria chamomilla essential oil is primarily derived from the flower heads of the plant, which contain the highest concentration of active compounds.

The flower heads, specifically the small yellow flowers, are the main part used for extraction due to their rich content of chamazulene and other beneficial compounds. While the leaves and stems may contribute some volatile oils, they are not the primary source for medicinal essential oil. The roots of Matricaria chamomilla are rarely used in essential oil production as they contain fewer aromatic compounds.

Overall, the flower heads remain the most crucial and widely utilized part of the plant for creating therapeutic essential oils.

Side Effects

Matricaria chamomilla essential oil can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in some individuals, particularly those with sensitive skin or a history of allergies.

It may also lead to mild gastrointestinal discomfort, such as nausea or upset stomach, when ingested in large amounts. Prolonged use or high concentrations can result in headaches or dizziness in certain people. Additionally, it may interact with medications, especially those affecting the central nervous system.

As with any essential oil, it is important to perform a patch test and consult a healthcare professional before use.
