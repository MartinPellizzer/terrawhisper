Astragalus membranaceus essential oil for medicinal use

Astragalus membranaceus essential oil is a concentrated aromatic extract derived from the root of the Astragalus plant, which is native to Asia.

It is traditionally used in herbalism to support immune function and enhance vitality. The oil is often employed in aromatherapy to promote energy and resilience during periods of stress or illness. It may also be applied topically to aid in wound healing and reduce inflammation.

However, due to its potency, it is typically diluted before use and should be handled with care.

Health Benefits

Astragalus membranaceus essential oil reduces inflammation by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which can help alleviate symptoms in conditions like arthritis and autoimmune disorders.

It supports liver function by promoting detoxification processes and protecting liver cells from oxidative stress and toxins. The oil also supports the respiratory system by reducing mucus production and improving lung function, making it beneficial for individuals with chronic respiratory issues. Additionally, it boosts the immune system by enhancing the activity of white blood cells and increasing the production of interferons.

Finally, it fights infections due to its antimicrobial and antiviral properties, helping the body defend against various pathogens.

Bioactive Constituents

Astragalus membranaceus essential oil contains phenylethanoid glycosides, which are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Polysaccharides in the oil contribute to immune modulation and enhance the body's defense mechanisms. Saponins present in the oil exhibit antimicrobial and anti-cancer activities. Astragalosides, another key constituent, support cardiovascular health and have hepatoprotective effects.

Monosaccharides contribute to the oil's overall bioavailability and metabolic support.

Plant Parts

Astragalus membranaceus essential oil is primarily derived from the roots of the plant, which contain the highest concentration of active compounds.

The roots are the most commonly used part for extraction due to their rich content of saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides. While leaves and stems may also be used in some formulations, they contribute less to the overall potency of the essential oil. The root's complex chemical composition makes it the preferred source for medicinal applications.

This plant is widely valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its immune-boosting properties.
