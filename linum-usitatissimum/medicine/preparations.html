10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Linum usitatissimum (Flaxseed)

Linum usitatissimum, also known as flaxseed or common flax, has been used for centuries in medicinal preparations.

A popular preparation is a tisane, or herbal tea, made from the seeds. To make the tisane, the seeds are first soaked in water and then brought to a boil. The mixture is then allowed to steep for several minutes before being strained and consumed.

Flaxseed tincture is another medicinal preparation made from flaxseed. To make the tincture, the seeds are soaked in alcohol for several weeks before being strained. The resulting liquid is then taken orally, often in small doses. Both of these preparations are believed to have a number of health benefits, including improving digestion, reducing inflammation, and lowering cholesterol levels.

However, it is important to note that the effectiveness of these preparations has not been extensively studied and they should not be used as a substitute for professional medical advice.

1. Infusion

Linum Usitatissimum infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the seeds of the flax plant in hot water to extract its beneficial compounds.

The main health benefits of this preparation include providing a rich source of omega-3 fatty acids, promoting digestive health due to its high fiber content, reducing inflammation, and supporting cardiovascular health by potentially lowering blood pressure and cholesterol levels. The seeds of the flax plant are primarily used for this infusion, although the oil and flour derived from the seeds can also be used. To prepare a Linum Usitatissimum infusion, add 1-2 teaspoons of ground flaxseed to a cup of hot water, let it steep for 5-10 minutes, and strain before consuming.

It is important to drink the infusion while it is still warm for optimal benefits. When using this preparation, it is recommended to start with a small dose and gradually increase to avoid potential side effects such as bloating, gas, and diarrhea. Additionally, it is advised to consume an adequate amount of water throughout the day to prevent dehydration due to the high fiber content of flaxseed.

Pregnant and breastfeeding women should consult their healthcare provider before consuming flaxseed infusion.

2. Decoction

Linum Usitatissimum decoction is a medicinal preparation made by boiling the seeds of the Linum usitatissimum plant in water to extract its beneficial compounds.

The main health benefits of this preparation include providing relief from constipation, reducing inflammation, lowering cholesterol levels, and supporting heart health. The seeds and occasionally the husks of the flaxseed plant are used to make this decoction.

To prepare the decoction, add one to two teaspoons of flaxseed to a cup of water and bring it to a boil, then simmer for 10 minutes before straining. It is important to drink the decoction while it is still warm.

Some precautions to take when using this preparation include drinking it with plenty of water to prevent the mucilage from causing obstruction in the digestive tract, and avoiding it during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to its potential estrogenic effects.

3. Tincture

Linum Usitatissimum tincture is a medicinal preparation made by soaking crushed flaxseeds in alcohol to extract their active compounds.

Its main health benefits include providing a rich source of omega-3 fatty acids, aiding in digestion and promoting regularity, reducing inflammation, and supporting cardiovascular health. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the seeds of the Linum usitatissimum plant.

To make the tincture, the crushed seeds are soaked in a solution of alcohol and water for several weeks, then strained. Main precautions to take when using Linum usitatissimum tincture include avoiding its use during pregnancy, as it may stimulate uterine contractions, and monitoring its use in individuals with a history of hormone-sensitive cancers due to its phytoestrogenic properties.

Additionally, it is important to follow recommended dosages and consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

4. Flaxseed oil

Linum Usitatissimum flaxseed oil, derived from the seeds of the flax plant, is a popular medicinal preparation due to its numerous health benefits.

It is rich in essential fatty acids, particularly omega-3, which can help reduce inflammation, lower the risk of heart disease, and improve digestive health. The oil is extracted from the seeds of the plant, which are harvested and cold-pressed to maintain their nutritional value.

To make flaxseed oil at home, the seeds can be ground and then pressed using a seed mill or oil press. However, it is important to note that flaxseed oil should be stored in a cool, dark place and used within a few weeks of opening, as it can quickly become rancid.

Additionally, those with a history of hormone-sensitive cancers, such as breast or prostate cancer, should avoid consuming flaxseed oil, as it contains phytoestrogens that may stimulate the growth of these types of cancers.

5. Ground flaxseed

Linum Usitatissimum ground flaxseed is a common medicinal preparation made from the crushed or milled seeds of the flax plant.

Its main health benefits include improving digestion, promoting regular bowel movements, reducing cholesterol levels, and providing a good source of omega-3 fatty acids, fiber, and lignans. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the seeds of the flax plant, which are typically dried and then ground into a fine powder.

To make ground flaxseed, simply purchase whole flaxseeds and grind them in a coffee grinder or blender until they reach a powdery consistency. When using ground flaxseed, it is important to drink plenty of water, as it is highly absorbent and can cause digestive issues if not properly hydrated.

Additionally, it is recommended to store ground flaxseed in a cool, dark place to preserve its nutritional value, and to consume it within a few weeks of grinding for maximum freshness.

6. capsules

Linum Usitatissimum capsules are dietary supplements that contain ground or powdered flaxseeds, also known as Linum usitatissimum, encapsulated in gelatin or plant-based capsules for easy consumption.

The main health benefits of this preparation include providing a rich source of dietary fiber, promoting regular bowel movements, supporting heart health by reducing inflammation and lowering cholesterol levels, and providing a vegetarian source of omega-3 fatty acids. The main plant parts used for this preparation are the seeds of the Linum usitatissimum plant, which are harvested, dried, and ground into a fine powder.

To make this preparation, the ground flaxseeds can be encapsulated in gelatin or plant-based capsules, or mixed with water or other liquids and consumed directly. When using this preparation, it is important to drink plenty of water to prevent the fiber from causing constipation, and to avoid consuming excessive amounts, as this may cause digestive discomfort and interfere with the absorption of other nutrients.

7. tea

Linum Usitatissimum tea, also known as flaxseed tea, is a medicinal preparation made from the seeds of the Linum usitatissimum plant.

The main health benefits of this tea include improving digestion, reducing inflammation, and providing relief from constipation. The seeds and outer shell of the flaxseed are used to make this tea.

To make the tea, crush the flaxseeds and soak them in hot water for several hours before straining and drinking the liquid. When using Linum usitatissimum tea, it is important to drink it in moderation as excessive consumption can cause digestive discomfort.

Additionally, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before using this tea if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have a medical condition.

8. poultice

Linum Usitatissimum poultice is a medicinal preparation made from the crushed or ground seeds and outer coat of the Linum usitatissimum plant, also known as flaxseed.

The main health benefits of this preparation include providing relief from inflammation, reducing pain, and promoting healing of wounds and skin conditions such as eczema and acne. The seeds and outer coat of the Linum usitatissimum plant are the main plant parts used for this preparation.

To make a Linum usitatissimum poultice, the seeds and outer coat are finely ground and mixed with a small amount of water to form a paste, which is then applied directly to the skin. When using a Linum usitatissimum poultice, it is important to test a small area of skin first to ensure no adverse reactions occur. Additionally, it is recommended to only use a Linum usitatissimum poultice for a short period of time, as prolonged use may cause skin irritation.

It is also important to note that Linum usitatissimum poultice should not be used by pregnant women, as it may stimulate menstruation and potentially lead to miscarriage.

9. compress

Linum Usitatissimum compress is a medicinal preparation made from the crushed seeds and/or fibers of the Linum usitatissimum plant, also known as flaxseed or common flax.

The main health benefits of this preparation include providing relief from inflammation, constipation, and skin irritations. The seeds and fibers of the flax plant are the primary plant parts used in creating this compress.

To make a Linum usitatissimum compress, the seeds and/or fibers are finely ground and mixed with a suitable liquid, such as water or oil, to create a paste that can be applied directly to the skin or used as a poultice. When using a Linum usitatissimum compress, it is essential to take precautions such as avoiding contact with the eyes, as it can cause irritation.

Additionally, people with allergies to flaxseed or its components should exercise caution and consult a healthcare professional before using this preparation.

10. bath

Linum Usitatissimum bath, also known as flaxseed bath, is a medicinal preparation that involves steeping flaxseeds in water to create a warm, soothing bath.

The main health benefits of this preparation include providing relief for sore muscles, reducing inflammation, and improving skin health due to its anti-inflammatory and moisturizing properties. The flaxseeds, specifically the mucilage found in the seeds, are the primary plant part used for this preparation. To make a Linum Usitatissimum bath, first, grind or crush flaxseeds and steep them in boiling water for about 15-20 minutes.

Strain the mixture and add it to a warm bath. For best results, soak in the bath for at least 20 minutes. When using a Linum Usitatissimum bath, it's important to take certain precautions, such as avoiding the use of hot water, as it can further irritate the skin. Additionally, people with allergies to flaxseed or related plants should avoid using this preparation.

It's also recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using a Linum Usitatissimum bath, especially for pregnant women, children, and individuals with pre-existing medical conditions.
