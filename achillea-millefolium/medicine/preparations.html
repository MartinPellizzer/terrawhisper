10 Best Medicinal Preparations of Achillea millefolium (Yarrow)

Achillea millefolium, also known as yarrow, is a plant that has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries.

Its leaves and flowers are commonly used to make herbal teas, or tisanes, which may help alleviate symptoms such as coughs, colds, and fevers. Yarrow tinctures, which are made by soaking the plant material in alcohol, are also used in herbal medicine.

These tinctures can be taken orally and are believed to have anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and astringent properties. Yarrow may also be applied topically in the form of a salve or compress to help heal wounds and reduce inflammation. It is important to note that yarrow should not be used by pregnant women, as it may stimulate uterine contractions.

As with any herbal remedy, it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before using yarrow or any other medicinal plant.

1. infusion

Achillea Millefolium infusion is a medicinal preparation made by steeping the flowers, leaves, and stems of the Yarrow plant in hot water, which releases the plant's active compounds into the liquid.

The main health benefits of this preparation include reducing inflammation, relieving symptoms of cold and flu, promoting wound healing, and alleviating digestive issues. The flowers, leaves, and stems of the Achillea millefolium plant are used to make the infusion.

To prepare the infusion, pour boiling water over the plant material and let it steep for 10-15 minutes before straining. When using this preparation, it is important to be aware of potential side effects such as skin irritation and allergic reactions, and to consult with a healthcare provider before use if pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking medication.

Additionally, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase as needed, and to discontinue use if any adverse reactions occur.
