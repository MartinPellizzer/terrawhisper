Clausena lenis

What's the taxonomical classification of Clausena lenis?

Clausena lenis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Sapindales. It is further categorized under the family Rutaceae, specifically within the genus Clausena, which culminates in its unique species designation, Clausena lenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clausena lenis has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are characterized by a smooth, entire margin and a glossy, coriaceous texture. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green coloration on the upper surface with a slightly paler underside. These leaves are arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the slender, woody stems. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in axillary positions. Its overall growth habit is that of a compact shrub with fine branching structures.

What are the pharmacological activities of Clausena lenis?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its anti-HIV, anti-inflammatory, antiproliferative, and neuroprotective activities. Its anti-HIV properties suggest a capacity to interfere with viral replication processes within the body. The presence of anti-inflammatory compounds helps in reducing systemic swelling and immune responses. Additionally, the antiproliferative effects indicate that its extracts may inhibit the rapid growth of abnormal cells. Finally, the neuroprotective qualities offer a mechanism for preserving neuronal health against oxidative stress and degeneration.

As discussed in a study, the stems of Clausena lenis contain three new prenylated coumarins, clauselenins A-C (1-3), and nine known prenylated coumarins (4-12). All 12 isolated prenylated coumarins displayed remarkable inhibitory effects against nitric oxide production induced by lipopolysaccharide in mouse macrophage RAW 264.7 cells in vitro. These inhibitory effects, characterized by IC50 values, are comparable to hydrocortisone. Additionally, the prenylated coumarins 1-12 exhibited considerable anti-HIV-1 reverse transcriptase (RT) activities. These activities possessed EC50 values in the range of 0.17-9.08 µM.
