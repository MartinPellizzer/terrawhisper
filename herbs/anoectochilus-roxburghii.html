What's the taxonomical classification of Anoectochilus roxburghii?

Anoectochilus roxburghii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the Orchidaceae family, falling under the genus Anoectochilus, and is specifically identified by its species name, roxburghii.

Anoectochilus roxburghii has creeping rhizomes that produce fleshy, distichous leaves arranged in a fan-like pattern. These flowers feature a prominent, fleshy lip that is often trilobed and serves as a key diagnostic feature. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires a warm, humid environment with bright, filtered sunlight and a well-draining orchid bark substrate to ensure healthy root development.

Because it is an epiphytic species, it thrives when mounted on cork bark or placed in slatted wooden baskets that allow for maximum air circulation around the roots. Frequent misting or high ambient humidity is essential to mimic its natural tropical habitat and prevent dehydration. Water the plant thoroughly when the medium feels nearly dry, being careful to avoid standing water in the crown to prevent rot.

Maintaining consistent temperatures between 65 and 85 degrees Fahrenheit will promote optimal growth and blooming cycles.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Anoectochilus roxburgii (AR) is a traditional Chinese medicinal plant reported to exhibit anti-inflammatory, hypoglycemic, hepatoprotective, and analgesic properties. The primary bioactive glycoside found in AR is kinsenoside (KD). In studies using KD at doses of 2.5, 5, and 10 mg/kg, the high dose of 10 mg/kg significantly reduced serum lipase by 53.62% in the caerulein + LPS model and 41.14% in the NaT model. This same 10 mg/kg dose also reduced amylase by 28.13% and 27.99%, respectively. Furthermore, KD supplementation at concentrations of 100, 200, and 400 μM was analyzed for its effects on macrophage phenotype. At a concentration of 400 μM, KD significantly reduced the proportion of M1 macrophages and downregulated the mRNA expression of genes including Nos2, Tnf, Il1b, and Il6.
