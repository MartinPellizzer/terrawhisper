What is Dysphania anthelmintica?

Dysphania anthelmintica is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It has a taproot, a single stem, and alternate, lanceolate leaves that are 2-10 cm long and 1-4 cm wide. The leaves are dark green to yellowish-green and have a glabrous or sparsely hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish or reddish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are arranged in a raceme.

The flowers are bisexual, with five sepals and five petals, and are followed by small, dry fruits in the form of achenes.
