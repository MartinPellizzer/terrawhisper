Lindera thomsonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindera thomsonii has deciduous, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically three to seven centimeters long and characterized by entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage displays a smooth texture and is arranged alternately along slender, woody stems. During the flowering season, small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a bright red or orange color upon ripening. The overall growth habit forms a small shrub or sapling with fine branching patterns.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the southwestern Cape province of South Africa, specifically restricted to the coastal regions of the Western Cape. It grows within the fynbos biome, a diverse shrubland ecosystem characterized by nutrient-poor soils. Its population is primarily concentrated in specific areas such as the Cape Peninsula and the surrounding mountain ranges. The species thrives in sandy soils often found in coastal sand dunes or rocky slopes. Because of this narrow range, the plant is highly specialized to its local environment.
