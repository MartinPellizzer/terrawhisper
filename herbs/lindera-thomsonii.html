Lindera thomsonii

What's the taxonomical classification of Lindera thomsonii?

Lindera thomsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through the hierarchical ranks, it is placed in the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific classification within the genus Lindera, reaching its unique identification at the species level as Lindera thomsonii.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lindera thomsonii can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if the plant's oils come into direct contact with sensitive human skin. While extensive clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many species within the Lauraceae family contain aromatic compounds that act as potential allergens. Individuals with known sensitivities to related spice plants might experience redness, itching, or localized swelling upon exposure. Ingestion of the plant should be avoided as the specific toxicological profile for internal consumption is not well documented. Always exercise caution and consult a medical professional if an adverse reaction occurs after handling this species.
