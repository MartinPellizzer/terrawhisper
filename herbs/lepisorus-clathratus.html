What's the taxonomical classification of Lepisorus clathratus?

Lepisorus clathratus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. This fern is a part of the family Polypodiaceae, falling within the genus Lepisorus, and is specifically identified by the species name clathratus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lepisorus clathratus has small, thin, ovate to lanceolate fronds that are arranged in a creeping or somewhat erect habit with distinctively thin, translucent margins. Its rhizomes are slender and creeping, often found anchoring the plant to rocky or mossy substrates. The sori are located on the underside of the pinnules and are protected by kidney-shaped indusia. This morphology allows the fern to thrive in moist, shaded environments where its delicate structure can capture filtered light.
