Rudbeckia laciniata

What's the taxonomical classification of Rudbeckia laciniata?

Rudbeckia laciniata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a large group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Rudbeckia. Its unique scientific identity is completed by its specific epithet, resulting in the species name Rudbeckia laciniata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rudbeckia laciniata has lanceolate leaves that are deeply lobed or divided into narrow, linear segments, a central disk composed of numerous tiny yellow tubular florets, and prominent ray florets that typically range from yellow to brownish-orange. The plant's stem is erect and can reach heights of up to one meter, often featuring a slightly hairy texture. Its flower heads are characterized by a distinct conical center that rises above the surrounding petals. The foliage is arranged alternately along the stem and becomes more deeply incised as the plant matures. These morphological traits distinguish it from other members of the Rudbeckia genus.
