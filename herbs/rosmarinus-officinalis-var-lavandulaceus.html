Lavender Rosemary (Rosmarinus Officinalis Var. Lavandulaceus)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Rosmarinus officinalis var. lavandulaceus.

Lavender rosemary, scientifically known as Rosmarinus officinalis var. lavandulaceus, is a rare hybrid of rosemary and lavender, prized for its medicinal and aromatic properties. This adaptogen and spice is renowned for its calming effects, antimicrobial qualities, and ability to support respiratory and digestive health. Historically, it has been used in traditional herbal medicine across Mediterranean cultures to treat anxiety, headaches, and respiratory ailments. In modern wellness practices, it is incorporated into aromatherapy, herbal teas, and topical salves for stress relief and skin care. Its unique blend of lavender's soothing essence and rosemary's stimulating aroma, along with its rare essential oil compounds, makes it a distinctive and valuable plant in both traditional and contemporary holistic health.
