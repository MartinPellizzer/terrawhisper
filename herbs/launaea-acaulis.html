Launaea acaulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Launaea acaulis?

Launaea acaulis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Asterales, placing it within the widely recognized family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Launaea, and its unique species designation, acaulis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Launaea acaulis has an acaulescent growth habit characterized by a rosette of deeply lobed or pinnatifid leaves that emerge directly from a woody base without an elongated stem. The leaf margins are typically irregular and often display dentate or serrated edges. Each leaf blade is relatively thick and can vary in color from bright green to a more muted, grayish hue depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flower heads that sit atop short peduncles emerging from the center of the leaf cluster. These inflorescences are typically composed of numerous tiny florets arranged in a compact cylindrical head.
