Adenophora stenanthina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora stenanthina has slender, linear leaves with entire margins and small, tubular purple flowers that emerge from a stem bearing several bracts. The plant typically features a herbaceous habit with upright, somewhat thin stems that support its delicate foliage. The leaves are arranged oppositely or in small clusters along the length of the stem. Its inflorescence consists of small bell-shaped corollas that are characteristic of the Campanulaceae family. The root system is generally composed of a slender rhizome that allows the plant to spread within its natural habitat.

    Adenophora stenanthina is prepared primarily as a decoction where the dried roots are boiled in water to extract their medicinal compounds. This traditional method allows the active constituents to be released into a concentrated liquid form for oral consumption.
