Osmanthus matsumuranus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Osmanthus matsumuranus has small, leathery, evergreen leaves that are elliptical or lanceolate in shape with finely serrated margins and a glossy dark green upper surface. The plant typically grows as a dense shrub or small tree reaching heights of several meters. Its flowers are tiny, white, and highly fragrant, blooming in clusters from the leaf axils. These blossoms are often followed by small, fleshy drupes that turn dark when ripe. The overall structure is characterized by a compact, spreading growth habit with woody stems.
