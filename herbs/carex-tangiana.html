What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex tangiana has perennial, rhizomatous growth habits characterized by long, slender leaves and small, yellowish-green spikes. Its reproductive structures consist of compact spikes that are often arranged in a way that distinguishes it from similar sedges. The scales of the perigynia are a defining morphological feature, often possessing specific shapes or colors that assist in identification.
