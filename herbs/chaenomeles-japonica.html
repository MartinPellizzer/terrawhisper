Chaenomeles japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Chaenomeles japonica?

Chaenomeles japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. It is further categorized under the family Rosaceae, which places it within a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is specifically identified by the genus Chaenomeles and is designated by the species name japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chaenomeles japonica has a perennial woody base with erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and feature finely serrated leaf margins. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an ovate shape with distinctively pointed tips. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, displaying a pale pink to white coloration. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy, reddish drupes that serve as the plant's fruit. The overall structure is characterized by its herbaceous appearance despite the presence of a woody rhizome.
