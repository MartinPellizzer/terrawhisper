Cymbidium hookerianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cymbidium hookerianum?

Cymbidium hookerianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asparagales. As a member of the Orchidaceae family, it is situated within the genus Cymbidium, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species hookerianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium hookerianum has sympodial growth characterized by long, arching pseudobulbs that terminate in distichous, leathery leaves. These leaves are typically lanceolate in shape and provide a dense canopy for the plant. The inflorescence consists of a long, pendulous raceme bearing numerous fragrant, waxy flowers. Each flower features a prominent, trilobed lip and a dorsal sepal that contributes to its intricate structure. The coloration often includes shades of pale green or yellow with distinct reddish or purple markings on the labellum.
