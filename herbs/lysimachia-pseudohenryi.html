Lysimachia pseudohenryi

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia pseudohenryi?

Lysimachia pseudohenryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Ericales, this plant is situated in the family Primulaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Lysimachia, specifically identified by its species name, pseudohenryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia pseudohenryi has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are arranged oppositely along a slender, upright stem.

The foliage typically presents a smooth texture with entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. Small, yellowish flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often featuring five distinct petals. The plant's reproductive structures include central stamens that are characteristic of the Primulaceae family.

Its overall growth habit is herbaceous and relatively compact, suitable for woodland environments.
