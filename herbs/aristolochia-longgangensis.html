What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia longgangensis?

Aristolochia longgangensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a taxonomic lineage that places it in the order Piperales. Within this order, the plant is a representative of the family Aristolochiaceae. It is further categorized under the genus Aristolochia, with its specific identification being the species longgangensis.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe nephrotoxicity, nephropathy, uropathy, carcinogenicities, and renal failure due to the presence of aristolochic acids. These toxic compounds target the urinary system, often leading to permanent damage to the kidney tissues. The resulting nephropathy can progress rapidly toward acute or chronic renal failure. Furthermore, the plant is known for its significant carcinogenicity, which is frequently linked to the development of upper urinary tract cancers. This combination of uropathy and cellular mutation poses a grave risk to human health.
