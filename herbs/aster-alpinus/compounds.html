Alpine Aster (Aster alpinus) Compounds

The primary compounds of Alpine Aster include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins and saponins contribute to its astringent effects, while coumarin and essential oils provide aromatic and potential antispasmodic benefits. Glycosides and terpenoids support its overall medicinal profile, and anthocyanins contribute to its color and antioxidant activity.

Alpine Aster (Aster alpinus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Alpine Aster (Aster alpinus) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are commonly found in the plant's aerial parts and are used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Alpine Aster (Aster alpinus) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps protect cells from oxidative stress and supports the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Its presence enhances the overall therapeutic potential of the herb.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Alpine Aster (Aster alpinus) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe mucous membranes. It is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its ability to support digestive health and promote wound healing.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Alpine Aster (Aster alpinus). It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.
