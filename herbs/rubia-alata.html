Rubia alata

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubia alata?

Rubia alata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Gentianales and into the family Rubiaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Rubia, and its specific species designation, alata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubia alata has erect, woody stems that are characterized by quadrangular shapes and are covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are arranged in whorls of four, appearing opposite each other along the stem with an elliptical to oblong shape. Each leaf possesses an acute apex and an entire margin, typically presenting a bright green color on the upper surface. Small, axillary flowers emerge from the leaf nodes, eventually developing into fleshy drupes. These morphological features distinguish the species within the Rubiaceae family through its specific stem texture and leaf arrangement.
