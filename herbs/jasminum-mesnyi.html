Jasminum mesnyi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum mesnyi has woody, sprawling stems that produce opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves measuring approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length. The foliage is typically bright green with smooth margins and a slightly textured surface. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, tubular white blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils. These fragrant flowers often appear in clusters, adding a delicate visual element to the dense, bushy growth habit. The overall structure allows the plant to form a low-growing, spreading mound or a trailing groundcover.
