What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum mesnyi?

Jasminum mesnyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales. The plant is a member of the family Oleaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Jasminum, with its unique designation being the species mesnyi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum mesnyi has woody, sprawling stems that produce opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves measuring approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length.

The foliage is typically bright green with smooth margins and a slightly textured surface. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, tubular white blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils. These fragrant flowers often appear in clusters, adding a delicate visual element to the dense, bushy growth habit.

The overall structure allows the plant to form a low-growing, spreading mound or a trailing groundcover.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

Some people might experience redness, itching, or a rash upon touching the plant. If accidentally swallowed, it could lead to stomach discomfort or nausea. It is always best to exercise caution when handling any ornamental plant to avoid these potential reactions.
