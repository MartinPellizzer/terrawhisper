Jasminum mesnyi

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum mesnyi?

Jasminum mesnyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales. The plant is a member of the family Oleaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Jasminum, with its unique designation being the species mesnyi.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Jasminum mesnyi can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or mild digestive upset if ingested. While generally considered non-toxic to humans, direct contact with the foliage or flowers may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience redness, itching, or a rash upon touching the plant. If accidentally swallowed, it could lead to stomach discomfort or nausea. It is always best to exercise caution when handling any ornamental plant to avoid these potential reactions.
