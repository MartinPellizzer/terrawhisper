Spiraea thunbergii

What's the taxonomical classification of Spiraea thunbergii?

Spiraea thuunbergii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Spiraea, and its specific species name, thunbergii.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases such as eczema, psoriasis, and various types of dermatitis. The leaves contain natural compounds that possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties beneficial for topical applications. These medicinal qualities help to soothe irritated skin and reduce redness associated with chronic conditions. When applied correctly, the plant extract can assist in managing the itching and discomfort caused by skin eruptions. Local traditional practices often utilize these specific botanical properties to address dermatological ailments.
