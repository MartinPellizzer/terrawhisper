What's the taxonomical classification of Spiraea thunbergii?

Spiraea thuunbergii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Spiraea, and its specific species name, thunbergii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spiraea thunbergii has deciduous, shrubby growth characterized by slender, arching stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically serrated along the margins.

The foliage is often a bright green color and provides a dense texture to the plant's overall structure. During its blooming period, the shrub produces numerous small, white flowers that are tightly clustered in corymb-like inflorescences. These floral displays emerge from the terminal ends of the branches, creating a delicate, frothy appearance.

The plant generally maintains a compact and rounded form as it matures.
