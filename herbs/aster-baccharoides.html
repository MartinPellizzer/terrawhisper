Aster baccharoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster baccharoides has perennial herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and feature alternate, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The leaves are generally green, often possessing a slightly hairy texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Its inflorescences consist of numerous small flower heads arranged in corymbose clusters at the terminal ends of the branches. Each individual flower head contains central disc florets surrounded by a single row of pale violet or white ray florets. The involucral bracts are arranged in multiple rows and provide protective coverage for the developing seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, spanning across northern India, Nepal, Bhutan, and parts of southern Tibet. It primarily thrives in montane environments where it can access specific temperate climates. Within these mountainous terrains, the species is often found growing in rocky crevices or alpine meadows. Its distribution is largely constrained by the specific elevation requirements necessary for its survival.       What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Individuals sensitive to the Asteraceae family may experience redness, itching, or swelling upon contact with the leaves or flowers. If the plant is accidentally consumed, symptoms might include nausea, stomach cramping, or vomiting. Some people may also encounter respiratory irritation if they inhale pollen from the blooms. Careful handling is recommended to avoid these adverse reactions.
