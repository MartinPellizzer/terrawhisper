Ampelopsis aconitifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Ampelopsis aconitifolia?

Ampelopsis aconitifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is situated under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Vitales and belongs to the family Vitaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Ampelopsis and is specifically designated as the species aconitifolia.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Ampelopsis aconitifolia can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains toxic compounds that may trigger nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal cramping upon consumption. Direct contact with the foliage or stems can result in contact dermatitis, characterized by redness and itching. In more serious cases of accidental ingestion, individuals might experience dizziness or more profound systemic toxicity. Always use caution and avoid skin contact to prevent these adverse reactions.
