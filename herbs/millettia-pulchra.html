Millettia pulchra

What's the taxonomical classification of Millettia pulchra?

Millettia pulchra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Within this lineage, it is identified under the genus Millettia, specifically designated as the species pulchra.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Millettia pulchra can cause allergic skin reactions, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if consumed in uncontrolled doses. While specific clinical studies on human side effects are limited, the presence of bioactive compounds suggests that skin contact may lead to contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of concentrated extracts might trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping due to its chemical composition. There is also a risk of unforeseen systemic interactions if the plant is used alongside pharmaceutical medications. Users should approach the plant with caution and avoid direct exposure to undocumented extracts.
