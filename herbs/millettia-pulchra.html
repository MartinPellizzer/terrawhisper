Millettia pulchra

What's the taxonomical classification of Millettia pulchra?

Millettia pulchra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Within this lineage, it is identified under the genus Millettia, specifically designated as the species pulchra.

What are the pharmacological activities of Millettia pulchra?

This plant has analgesic, antiinflammatory, cardiovascular remodeling remedy, and hypertension remedy pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to alleviate pain and reduce systemic inflammation in various biological models. These properties extend to the heart, where it acts as a remedy for cardiovascular remodeling by preventing adverse structural changes in cardiac tissue. Additionally, the plant serves as an effective hypertension remedy by helping to regulate and lower blood pressure levels. Such diverse medicinal effects highlight its significant therapeutic potential in managing multiple chronic conditions.

According to a study published by "Biological & pharmaceutical bulletin", the ethanol extract of the stem of Millettia pulchrha (EMP) was found to reduce xylene-induced ear edema and relieve both acetic acid-induced pain and pain in the hot plate test. The study also observed a significant decrease in nitric oxide (NO) production in cells treated with isolated compounds from the plant. Among the tested compounds, Lanceolatin B showed the greatest inhibition of NO synthesis. This specific compound also reduced levels of interleukin-1 beta (IL-1β), IL-6, tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-α), cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), nuclear factor-kappa B (NF-κB), and phosphorylation inhibitory kappa B alpha (p-IκBα) in a dose-dependent manner. These findings provide evidence that EMP and individual isolated compounds possess significant antiinflammatory and analgesic activities.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several medicinal compounds including the specific bioactive molecule YLP-1, the sugars arabinose, glucose, and mannose, and various complex polysaccharides. These identified polysaccharides are essential components that contribute to the plant's therapeutic potential. The presence of YLP-1 suggests unique pharmacological properties that warrant further scientific investigation. Additionally, the various monosaccharides like glucose and mannose serve as fundamental building blocks within its chemical structure. Together, these substances form a diverse phytochemical profile used in medicinal research.

According to a study published by "Carbohydrate polymers", the root of Millettia pulchrha contains polysaccharides with potential antidepressant effects. A novel homogeneous polysaccharide named YLP-1 was purified from the crude polysaccharides of the root and is mainly composed of glucose, arabinose, and mannose with a molar ratio of 87.25%, 10.77%, and 1.98%, respectively. This YLP-1 is an alpha-glucan featuring a backbone of 1,4-Glcp and branches at C6 of 1,4,6-Glcp to combine 1,4-Manp and 1,5-Araf. The compound effectively ameliorated depression-like behaviors and restored decreased catecholamine levels in chronic variable stress-induced depression rats. Furthermore, YLP-1 mitigated the disruption of tryptophan metabolites in urine and serum and promoted short-chain fatty acids production by Lactobacillus spp.
