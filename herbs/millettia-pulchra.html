Millettia pulchra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Within this lineage, it is identified under the genus Millettia, specifically designated as the species pulchra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds arabinose, glucose, mannose, polysaccharides, and YLP-1. These specific sugars and complex carbohydrates serve as the fundamental building blocks for its unique chemical profile. The presence of diverse polysaccharides contributes to various biological activities within the organism. Additionally, the specialized compound YLP-1 plays a significant role in the plant's therapeutic potential. Together, these substances form a complex medicinal matrix used in pharmacological research.

As discussed in a study, the root of Millettia pulchrha contains polysaccharides with potential antidepressant effects. A novel homogeneous polysaccharide named YLP-1 was purified from the crude polysaccharides of the root. This YLP-1 is mainly composed of glucose, arabinose, and mannose with a molar ratio of 87.25%, 10.77%, and 1.98%, respectively. It is a novel α-glucan featuring a backbone of 1,4-Glcp that is branched at C6 of 1,4,6-Glcp to combine 1,4-Manp and 1,5-Araf. These compounds effectively ameliorate depression-like behaviors and restore decreased catecholamine levels in rats.
