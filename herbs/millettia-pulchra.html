What's the taxonomical classification of Millettia pulchra?

Millettia pulchra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Within this lineage, it is identified under the genus Millettia, specifically designated as the species pulchra.

What are the pharmacological activities of Millettia pulchra?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, cardiovascular remodeling remedy, and hypertension remedy pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce pain sensations and suppress systemic inflammatory responses in various biological models. Researchers have also identified its potential to mitigate pathological structural changes in the heart through its cardiovascular remodeling remedy properties. Furthermore, the plant functions as a hypertension remedy by helping to regulate blood pressure levels effectively. These diverse medicinal actions make it a significant subject for further pharmaceutical development.

As discussed in a study, the ethanol extract of the stem of M. pulchrha (EMP) demonstrated antiinflammatory and analgesic activities by reducing xylene-induced ear edema and relieving both acetic acid-induced pain and pain in the hot plate test. Isolated compounds from EMP caused a significant decrease in nitric oxide (NO) production in treated cells. Among the tested compounds, Lanceolatin B showed the greatest inhibition of NO synthesis. Lanceolatin B also reduced levels of interleukin-1 beta (IL-1β), IL-6, tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-α), cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), nuclear factor-kappa B (NF-κB), and phosphorylation inhibitory kappa B alpha (p-IκBα) in a dose-dependent manner. These findings provide evidence that EMP and individual isolated compounds possess significant antiinflammatory and analgesic activities.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds arabinose, glucose, mannose, polysaccharides, and YLP-1. These specific sugars and complex carbohydrates serve as the fundamental building blocks for its unique chemical profile. The presence of diverse polysaccharides contributes to various biological activities within the organism. Additionally, the specialized compound YLP-1 plays a significant role in the plant's therapeutic potential. Together, these substances form a complex medicinal matrix used in pharmacological research.

As discussed in a study, the root of Millettia pulchrha contains polysaccharides with potential antidepressant effects. A novel homogeneous polysaccharide named YLP-1 was purified from the crude polysaccharides of the root. This YLP-1 is mainly composed of glucose, arabinose, and mannose with a molar ratio of 87.25%, 10.77%, and 1.98%, respectively. It is a novel α-glucan featuring a backbone of 1,4-Glcp that is branched at C6 of 1,4,6-Glcp to combine 1,4-Manp and 1,5-Araf. These compounds effectively ameliorate depression-like behaviors and restore decreased catecholamine levels in rats.
