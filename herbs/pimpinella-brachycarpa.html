What's the taxonomical classification of Pimpinella brachycarpa?

Pimpinella brachycarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, this plant is further categorized under the genus Pimpinella, with its specific identification designated as the species brachycarpa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pimpineilla brachycarpa has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically grows to a height of 30 to 70 centimeters and features pinnately compound leaves with narrow, serrated leaflets.

The inflorescence consists of small, white flowers arranged in compound umbels supported by distinct rays. Its fruit is characterized by a short, somewhat compressed schizocarp that distinguishes it from closely related species. The foliage often exhibits a delicate, feathery texture due to the fine division of the leaf segments.

These morphological traits collectively help botanists identify the species within the Apiaceae family.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Pimpineilla brachycarpa can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress in sensitive individuals. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, members of the Apiaceae family often contain furocoumarins that increase skin sensitivity to sunlight. Exposure to the plant's oils may result in phytophotodermatitis, which manifests as redness or blistering after UV exposure. Some users might also experience nausea or stomach discomfort if the plant is ingested accidentally. Monitoring for localized rashes or digestive upset is recommended when handling this species.
