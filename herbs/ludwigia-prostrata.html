Ludwigia prostrata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ludwigia prostrata has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats, small lanceolate to elliptical leaves arranged oppositely, and four-petaled yellow flowers. The stems often root at the nodes where they touch the soil, allowing the plant to spread rapidly across the ground. Leaves are typically sessile or short-petioled with entire margins and a smooth texture. The bright yellow blossoms are relatively small and emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These reproductive structures eventually give way to small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
