Black Pepper (Piper amalago) Compounds

The primary compounds in black pepper include alkaloids, such as piperine, which enhances absorption of other substances. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant properties. Terpenoids and essential oils provide anti-inflammatory effects, while saponins and glycosides support digestive health. Coumarin and anthocyanin add additional therapeutic benefits.

Black Pepper (Piper amalago) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

The alkaloid piperine is a key active compound in Black Pepper (Piper amalago). It enhances the absorption of other nutrients and has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Piperine also contributes to the plant's traditional use in digestive and pain-relief applications.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Black Pepper (Piper amalago). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and immune support. These compounds enhance the overall therapeutic potential of Black Pepper in natural remedies.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Black Pepper (Piper amalago) that contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and supporting digestive health by strengthening mucous membranes. Tannins in Black Pepper are also believed to enhance the absorption of other nutrients.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Black Pepper (Piper amalago). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and supports digestive health by enhancing nutrient absorption. This compound plays a vital role in the therapeutic effects of Black Pepper in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Black Pepper (Piper amalago), contributing to its therapeutic properties. Piperine, a key terpenoid, enhances absorption of other compounds and has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. It supports digestive health and may aid in pain management.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Black Pepper (Piper amalago). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation, making it relevant in herbal medicine applications.

7. Essential oil

The essential oil of Black Pepper (Piper amalago) contains volatile compounds like piperine and other aromatic substances. It contributes to the plant's pungent aroma and is used in traditional medicine for its warming and digestive properties. The oil is often extracted through distillation and is a key component in various herbal formulations.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Black Pepper (Piper amalago). It contributes to the plant's digestive and anti-inflammatory properties by enhancing nutrient absorption and reducing gut inflammation. Saponin also supports the immune system and may aid in detoxification processes.

9. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Black Pepper (Piper amalago). It contributes to the plant's pungent flavor and may support digestive health by enhancing nutrient absorption. Glycosides in Black Pepper are part of its complex profile of bioactive compounds that influence various physiological processes.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Black Pepper (Piper amalago) that contributes to its color and has antioxidant properties. It supports overall health by neutralizing free radicals and may enhance the bioavailability of other compounds in the plant. While not the primary active compound, anthocyanin plays a role in the plant's medicinal benefits.
