Cymbidium faberi

What's the taxonomical classification of Cymbidium faberi?

Cymbidium faberi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is placed in the order Asparagales. It is a member of the family Orchidaceae, specifically falling under the genus Cymbidium, with its specific designation being the species faberi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium faberi has long, leathery, linear leaves that grow from a creeping rhizome and produce arching inflorescences bearing multiple pale pink to white flowers with prominent yellow-spotted lips. The plant's growth habit is characterized by sympodial development, where new shoots emerge from the base of older ones. Each individual flower features three sepals and three petals, with the specialized labellum serving as a landing platform for pollinators. The stems are relatively stout and support a dense arrangement of foliage that can reach significant heights. This species is distinguished by its delicate floral pigmentation and the specific structure of its column and pollinia.
