Devil's Ivy

Cissus quadrangularis

Devil's Ivy (Cissus quadrangularis) is a climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Asia, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating bone fractures and joint pain.

Botanical Identification

Devil's Ivy, or Cissus quadrangularis, is a climbing plant in the family Vitaceae. Native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, it is known by various common names such as devil's ivy and stonebreaker. The plant has four-sided stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small greenish flowers. It is also referred to as Indian nettle in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rhamnales
  • Family: Rhamnaceae
  • Genus: Cissus
  • Species: Cissus quadrangularis

Active Compounds

Devil's Ivy contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Safety Profile

Devil's Ivy is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with pre-existing conditions.
