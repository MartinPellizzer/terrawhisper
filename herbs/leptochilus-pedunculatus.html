Leptochilus pedunculatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leptochilus pedunculatus has a perennial herb habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal or distributed along the stem in an opposite arrangement, displaying an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Its most distinguishing feature is the long-peduncled inflorescence, where individual flowers are held on slender, elongated stalks. The flowers themselves are tubular and zygomorphic, often featuring a distinct bilabiate structure common to the Lamiaceae family. These floral structures are frequently marked by subtle color patterns that serve to attract specific pollinators.
