Monodora crispata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Monodora crispata has woody climbing vines characterized by highly undulate or wavy leaf margins and coriaceous, leathery textures. The leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern and exhibit a distinctively wrinkled appearance due to their irregular edges. Its stems function as lianas that wrap around supporting vegetation to reach sunlight in tropical forest canopies. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often serving as a primary feature for pollinators. The overall structure is adapted for life in dense, humid environments where structural support is essential for growth.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of West Africa, specifically spanning from Sierra Leone through Liberia, Ivory Coast, Ghana, and into Nigeria. It thrives primarily within the humid, lowland equatorial forests where high rainfall and consistent temperatures are present. The species is most commonly found in the understory of dense jungle environments characterized by rich, organic soil. Localized populations may also appear in fragmented forest patches across the Guineo-Congolian region.
