What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Yulania denudata has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by upright stems and small, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along the branches. The foliage typically features a smooth texture with entire margins and a dull green coloration. Its reproductive structures consist of inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These flowers transition into small, dry fruits that facilitate the dispersal of seeds within its native habitat. The overall architecture of the plant is relatively compact and shrub-like in form.
