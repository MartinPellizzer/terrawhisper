What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saraca dives has pinnate leaves consisting of several pairs of leaflets, woody stems with a climbing habit, and small, yellowish flowers arranged in axillary racemes. The leaflets are typically elliptic or oblong in shape with entire margins and a smooth texture. This species often functions as a woody climber or scandent shrub, utilizing its flexible stems to navigate through forest canopies. The inflorescence emerges from the leaf axils, supporting small blossoms that are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. What side-effects this plant can have?

Saraca dives can cause severe skin irritation, intense itching, and localized swelling upon contact with the flesh. The plant contains caustic sap that may lead to chemical burns if not washed away immediately. Some individuals might experience an allergic reaction characterized by redness or hives. Prolonged exposure to the leaves can result in blistering or long-lasting dermatological discomfort. It is essential to avoid touching the foliage to prevent these adverse physical reactions.
