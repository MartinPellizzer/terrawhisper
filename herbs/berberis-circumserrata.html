Berberis circumserrata

What's the taxonomical classification of Berberis circumserrata?

Berberis circumserrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ranunculales and the family Berberidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Berberis, and its unique species designation, circumserrata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Berberis circumserrata contains high concentrations of isoquinoline alkaloids, specifically berberine, palmatine, and jatrorrhizine, which serve as its primary bioactive agents. The presence of these alkaloids allows the plant to be utilized in traditional medicine for treating gastrointestinal infections and skin ailments.
