Scientific Classification

Red Root, or Lithospermum erythrorhizon, belongs to the plant kingdom (Plantae), division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Boraginaceae, genus Lithospermum, and species Lithospermum erythrorhizon. It is a flowering plant known for its root used in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Red Root is Lithospermum erythrorhizon, a binomial name introduced by Carl von Linné. It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is also known by several synonyms, including Lithospermum officinale and Lithospermum rubrum. This nomenclature reflects its historical use in herbal medicine across various regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Red Root, or Lithospermum erythrorhizon, is a herbaceous perennial with a taproot system that is thick, reddish, and used in herbal medicine. Its stems are upright, branched, and covered in fine hairs. Leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy surface. The inflorescence is a raceme of small, pale purple flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Red Root (Lithospermum erythrorhizon) can be distinguished by its deep red roots and purple stems. It has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are pink to purple and appear in clusters. It is often confused with other Lithospermum species, but its distinct root color and leaf shape help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Red Root (Lithospermum erythrorhizon) is native to eastern North America, particularly the eastern United States and parts of Canada. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils, often found in woodland edges and open fields. It prefers loamy or sandy soils and grows at elevations ranging from 200 to 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Red Root typically blooms from June to August. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the late summer to early autumn when the roots are most developed and the plant is mature.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Red Root (Lithospermum erythrorhizon) belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is closely related to other Lithospermum species. It shares characteristics with its genus, such as hairy stems and blue or purple flowers. Its phylogenetic placement reflects broader family traits, including specialized seed structures and chemical compositions.
