10 Possible Side Effects Of Lithospermum Erythrorhizon

Lithospermum erythrorhizon has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and cardiovascular problems.

These side effects may occur due to overdosing, interaction with other medications, or individual sensitivity to the herb's compounds.

For instance, gastrointestinal issues can lead to malnutrition and weight loss, while allergic reactions can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical attention.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Lithospermum erythrorhizon causes allergic reactions due to its inherent ability to stimulate an immune response.

The herb contains allergenic compounds that can bind to immunoglobulin E (IgE) antibodies in the body, triggering a cascade of chemical reactions that lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and respiratory distress.

Additionally, some individuals may have pre-existing sensitivities to specific molecules within the plant, further increasing their susceptibility to an allergic response.

2. Induces gastrointestinal upset

Lithospermum erythrorhizon induces gastrointestinal upset in some individuals due to its ability to stimulate digestive processes.

The saponins and flavonoids present in the herb can irritate the stomach lining and intestines, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal cramps.

This reaction is thought to occur because these compounds enhance the movement of food through the digestive tract, causing an increase in gut motility and secretions.

3. Triggers respiratory distress

Lithospermum erythrorhizon triggers respiratory distress due to its potential allergenic and irritating properties.

The plant's essential oils can stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical that causes blood vessels to dilate and bronchial smooth muscle to constrict, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, red shiso's saponins and flavonoids may exacerbate respiratory issues in individuals with pre-existing conditions like asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

4. Produces skin rash

Lithospermum erythrorhizon produces skin rash when used.

This is due to its high concentration of alkaloids and flavonoids, which can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals. The skin rash may manifest as redness, itching, or irritation, often accompanied by a burning sensation.

The exact mechanism behind this reaction is not fully understood, but it is believed to be linked to the herb's ability to stimulate the immune system and trigger an inflammatory response.

5. Increases blood pressure

Lithospermum erythrorhizon increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system and increase peripheral resistance.

The plant contains flavonoids and triterpenoids that can cause vasoconstriction, leading to increased blood pressure.

Additionally, Lithospermum erythrorhizon has been shown to have a direct effect on the cardiovascular system, causing an increase in heart rate and cardiac output.

6. Lowers blood sugar

Lithospermum erythrorhizon lowers blood sugar by inhibiting the activity of certain enzymes in the body that regulate glucose metabolism.

Specifically, it has been shown to inhibit the activity of alpha-glucosidases, which are enzymes responsible for breaking down carbohydrates into simple sugars.

This can lead to a decrease in the absorption of glucose from the gut, resulting in lower blood sugar levels.

7. Affects kidney function

Lithospermum erythrorhizon affects kidney function by inhibiting the activity of certain enzymes responsible for filtering waste products from the blood.

This may lead to a buildup of toxins in the body and potentially impair kidney function over time.

Additionally, the flavonoids present in Lithospermum erythrorhizon may alter the absorption of certain nutrients and increase the risk of kidney damage due to their antioxidant properties.

8. Interferes with coagulation

Lithospermum erythrorhizon interferes with coagulation by inhibiting platelet aggregation and the production of thromboxane A2, a potent vasoconstrictor.

This can lead to an increased risk of bleeding or bruising, particularly in individuals taking anticoagulant medications or suffering from bleeding disorders.

The herb's mechanism of action is attributed to its ability to suppress platelet function, thereby disrupting the blood clotting process.

9. Enhances photosensitivity

Lithospermum erythrorhizon enhances photosensitivity due to its ability to increase the skin's sensitivity to ultraviolet (UV) radiation.

The herb's flavonoids and phenolic compounds enhance the skin's response to UV light, leading to increased sun sensitivity and an increased risk of phototoxic reactions.

This side effect is thought to occur because the herb's active ingredients interfere with the skin's natural protection against UV damage, making it more susceptible to harm from sunlight.

10. Increases heart rate

Lithospermum erythrorhizon increases heart rate due to its stimulating effects on the cardiovascular system.

The herb contains compounds that can dilate blood vessels and increase cardiac output, leading to an elevation in heart rate.

This increased stimulation is thought to be responsible for the rapid heartbeat experienced by some individuals taking Lithospermum erythrorhizon.
