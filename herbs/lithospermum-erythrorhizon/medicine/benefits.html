10 Health Benefits Of Lithospermum Erythrorhizon (Red Shiso)

Lithospermum erythrorhizon, also known as red shiso, has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These medicinal properties help reduce pain and swelling, as well as protect against cell damage and oxidative stress. This can improve the lives of individuals with arthritis or other inflammatory conditions by alleviating discomfort and promoting overall well-being.

Furthermore, red shiso's antiviral and antibacterial properties can aid in wound healing and boost the immune system.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Lithospermum erythrorhizon.

1. Helps lower blood pressure

Lithospermum erythrorhizon helps lower blood pressure because it is rich in flavonoids and saponins, which have been shown to exhibit vasodilatory and hypotensive effects.

The flavonoids in red shiso, such as lithospermic acid, have been found to relax blood vessels, reducing peripheral resistance and subsequently lowering blood pressure. Additionally, the saponins in red shiso may help to reduce blood pressure by inhibiting the production of vasoconstricting hormones.

Overall, the combination of these bioactive compounds makes red shiso a potentially valuable natural remedy for managing hypertension.

2. Reduces inflammation levels

Lithospermum erythrorhizon reduces inflammation levels because it contains a rich source of flavonoids, particularly lithospermin, which has been shown to exhibit potent anti-inflammatory properties.

Specifically, lithospermin has been found to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, such as TNF-alpha and IL-1 beta, which are key players in the inflammation response.

Additionally, red shiso's flavonoids have been shown to possess antioxidant activity, further contributing to its anti-inflammatory effects.

3. Fights cancer cells

Lithospermum erythrorhizon fights cancer cells because of its unique ability to selectively target and destroy cancer cells while leaving healthy cells intact.

The plant's active compound, lithospermic acid, has been shown to induce apoptosis (cell death) in cancer cells through the suppression of telomerase activity and the regulation of key signaling pathways.

This selective cytotoxicity makes red shiso a promising natural therapy for cancer treatment.

4. Antioxidizes free radicals

Lithospermum erythrorhizon antioxidizes free radicals because its unique chemical composition allows it to effectively scavenge and neutralize reactive oxygen species (ROS) in the body.

The plant's rich antioxidant profile, which includes flavonoids, phenolic acids, and other polyphenols, enables it to capture and stabilize free radicals, thereby preventing oxidative stress and cellular damage.

This remarkable property makes red shiso a valuable natural remedy for protecting against chronic diseases and promoting overall health.

5. Inhibits angiogenesis growth

Lithospermum erythrorhizon inhibits angiogenesis growth because it contains a range of bioactive compounds, including lithospermic acid and its derivatives.

These compounds have been shown to exhibit anti-angiogenic properties, which means they can prevent the formation of new blood vessels.

By inhibiting angiogenesis, red shiso may help to reduce the growth and progression of certain types of cancer, as well as prevent excessive blood vessel growth in diseases such as diabetic retinopathy.

6. Supports bone health

Lithospermum erythrorhizon supports bone health because of its rich antioxidant and flavonoid content.

The plant's polyphenolic compounds, including lithospermic acid, have been shown to inhibit the activity of certain enzymes that break down bone tissue, thereby promoting bone density and strength.

Additionally, red shiso's antioxidant properties help protect against oxidative stress, which can contribute to age-related bone loss and osteoporosis.

7. Relieves menopausal symptoms

Lithospermum erythrorhizon relieves menopausal symptoms because of its rich concentration of flavonoids, particularly baicalin.

This potent compound has been shown to modulate hormonal changes associated with menopause, reducing hot flashes, night sweats, and mood swings. Additionally, red shiso's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties help alleviate vaginal dryness and joint pain, common complaints during menopause.

Its overall soothing effects on the body make it a valuable natural remedy for women navigating this life transition.

8. Reduces fever quickly

Lithospermum erythrorhizon reduces fever quickly because of its unique biochemical composition.

The plant contains a rich array of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and other bioactive compounds that work in synergy to produce a potent antipyretic effect. These compounds interact with the body's heat-regulating mechanisms, quickly reducing body temperature and alleviating symptoms of fever.

This natural remedy has been used for centuries in traditional Japanese medicine, and its efficacy has been confirmed through modern studies, making it a safe and effective alternative to conventional fever reducers.

9. Kills bacteria effectively

Lithospermum erythrorhizon kills bacteria effectively because of its unique compound, lithospermic acid.

This acid has been shown to exhibit antibacterial properties, inhibiting the growth of various microorganisms, including methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) and Escherichia coli. The acid works by disrupting the bacterial cell membrane, ultimately leading to cell death and preventing the spread of infection.

Its efficacy in combating antibiotic-resistant bacteria has made red shiso a promising natural alternative in the fight against bacterial infections.
