Red Root (Lithospermum erythrorhizon) Compounds

Red Root contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Glycosides and coumarins support its wound-healing and antimicrobial effects. Alkaloids and terpenoids enhance its analgesic and antispasmodic actions, while saponins and essential oils contribute to its detoxifying and digestive benefits. Anthocyanins provide additional antioxidant support.

Red Root (Lithospermum erythrorhizon) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Red Root (Lithospermum erythrorhizon). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. 2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Red Root (Lithospermum erythrorhizon). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support skin health and wound healing. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal potency.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Red Root (Lithospermum erythrorhizon). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound is responsible for some of the therapeutic effects associated with Red Root in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Glycoside

Red Root (Lithospermum erythrorhizon) contains the glycoside lithospermic acid, which is responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This compound is extracted from the roots and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat skin infections and wounds.

7. Terpenoid
