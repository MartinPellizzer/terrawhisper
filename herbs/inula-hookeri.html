Inula hookeri

What's the taxonomical classification of Inula hookeri?

Inula hookeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling under the genus Inula, and is specifically identified by its species name, hookeri.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Inula hookeri contains sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, organic acids, and essential oils that serve as its primary bioactive constituents. These chemical compounds are responsible for the plant's significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Research indicates that the sesquiterpene lactones play a crucial role in modulating immune responses and inhibiting tumor growth in various experimental models. Additionally, the presence of specific flavonoids assists in protecting cells against oxidative stress and cellular damage. These medicinal components work synergistically to support the plant's traditional uses in treating inflammatory conditions and promoting overall physiological health.

What are the therapeutic actions of Inula hookeri?

Inula hookeri has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties that make it effective for treating respiratory infections, digestive disorders, and skin inflammation. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids that help reduce oxidative stress in body tissues. These chemical constituents work to suppress inflammatory pathways, providing relief from swelling and pain. When applied topically or ingested in traditional preparations, it aids in clearing phlegm and soothing mucosal membranes. Research suggests its phytochemical profile also supports immune system modulation against various pathogens.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat stomach aches, intestinal worms, skin infections, and various types of sores. The leaves and roots are frequently prepared as medicinal decoctions to alleviate digestive discomfort and expel parasites from the gut. In traditional practices, topical applications of the plant are common for healing wounds and addressing dermatological issues. Some local communities also utilize it to manage inflammation and general bodily aches. These varied applications highlight its role as a versatile remedy in ethnobotanical medicine.
