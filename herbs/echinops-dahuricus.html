What's the taxonomical classification of Echinops dahuricus?

Echinops dahuricus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is further categorized into the genus Echinops, with its specific identification being the species dahuricus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Echinops dahuricus has spiny, globose flower heads that are approximately 3 to 5 centimeters in diameter and sit atop stiff, upright stems reaching up to 60 centimeters in height. The foliage consists of deeply lobed, grayish-green leaves that possess a somewhat hairy texture to reduce moisture loss. Each individual floret within the spherical inflorescence is tubular and possesses sharp, prickly bracts that protect the developing seeds. The plant's root system is typically composed of a sturdy rhizome that allows for perennial growth in its native habitats. These distinctive architectural features combine to create a highly textured and structural appearance in garden settings.
