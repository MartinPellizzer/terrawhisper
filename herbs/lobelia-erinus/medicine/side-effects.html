10 Possible Side Effects Of Lobelia Erinus (Sweet Alyssum)

Lobelia erinus has some side effects when used improperly, such as cardiac arrhythmias, tremors, and seizures, which can occur due to its toxic alkaloid content and high levels of lobeline and lobelanine.

These side effects can worsen people's lives by causing anxiety, panic attacks, and even comas in severe cases.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption can lead to chronic toxicity, making it essential to use this herb under professional guidance and in limited quantities.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Lobelia erinus if used imporperly.

The plant contains lobeline, a stimulant that can cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure when ingested. Additionally, the plant's volatile oils can be released into the air, causing respiratory irritation and inflammation.

This reaction can lead to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.
