Cascara Sagrada

Rhamnus purshiana

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Cascara Sagrada, scientifically known as Rhamnus purshiana, belongs to the family Rhamnaceae. It is also called buckthorn and is a deciduous shrub native to western North America. The plant has thick, ridged bark, alternate leaves, and small greenish flowers. Synonyms include "Oregon grape" and "bog buckthorn" in some herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Cascara Sagrada contains alkaloids, tannins, and flavonoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to stimulate bowel movements by increasing intestinal motility. Tannins may also contribute to its laxative effects through their astringent properties.

  • Alkaloid
  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Digestive Constipation, gastroesophageal reflux, diarrhea, inflammatory bowel disease, gastritis Laxative, antispasmodic, astringent, anti-inflammatory
Musculoskeletal Inflammation, arthritis, rheumatoid arthritis Anti-inflammatory
Endocrine Diabetes Hypoglycemic

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and constipation due to its laxative properties.

Poultice: Applied externally for skin conditions and inflammation.

Extract: Used for constipation and to stimulate bowel movements.

Safety Profile

Cascara Sagrada is generally safe when used as directed, but it should not be taken for extended periods. It is contraindicated for pregnant women, nursing mothers, and individuals with severe constipation or intestinal obstruction. Prolonged use may lead to dependency and electrolyte imbalances.
