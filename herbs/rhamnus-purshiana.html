Cascara Sagrada

Rhamnus purshiana

Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana) is a species of shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada, known for its use in traditional and modern herbal medicine to treat constipation and promote digestive health.

Taxonomy

Cascara Sagrada, also known as Rhamnus purshiana, is a species within the genus Rhamnus of the family Rhamnaceae, classified under the order Rhamnales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Cascara Sagrada, or Rhamnus purshiana, is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark, opposite simple leaves with serrated margins, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries.

Distribution

Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana) is native to the western regions of North America, specifically found in areas of the Pacific Northwest, including parts of Washington, Oregon, and northern California.

Traditional Uses

Cascara Sagrada has been traditionally used by Native American tribes, particularly the Native Americans of the Pacific Northwest, as a laxative to treat constipation and digestive issues.

Medicinal Systems

Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana) has been traditionally used in Native American medicine, particularly by the Nez Perce and other tribes, as well as in Ayurvedic and Chinese traditional medicine systems for its laxative and digestive properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana) is characterized by the presence of anthraquinone glycosides, particularly cascarenol and emodin, which are responsible for its laxative effects and medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana) exhibits potent laxative effects through its stimulation of intestinal motility and reduction of water absorption in the gastrointestinal tract, making it effective for relieving constipation.

Botanical Identification

Cascara Sagrada, scientifically known as Rhamnus purshiana, belongs to the family Rhamnaceae. It is also called buckthorn and is a deciduous shrub native to western North America. The plant has thick, ridged bark, alternate leaves, and small greenish flowers. Synonyms include "Oregon grape" and "bog buckthorn" in some herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Cascara Sagrada contains alkaloids, tannins, and flavonoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to stimulate bowel movements by increasing intestinal motility. Tannins may also contribute to its laxative effects through their astringent properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and constipation due to its laxative properties.

Safety Profile

Cascara Sagrada is generally safe when used as directed, but it should not be taken for extended periods. It is contraindicated for pregnant women, nursing mothers, and individuals with severe constipation or intestinal obstruction. Prolonged use may lead to dependency and electrolyte imbalances.
