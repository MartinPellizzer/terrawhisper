Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana)

Cascara Sagrada (Rhamnus purshiana) is a member of the Rhamnaceae family, native to North America, Western United States, and Pacific Northwest. Traditionally, its bark, roots, and leaves have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its astringent, tonic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial shrub.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 5 to 15 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with entire margins, upper surface glossy green, lower surface duller green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are clusters of small greenish-yellow flowers with five sepals and five petals, arranged in a dense spike, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry, lacking distinct markings or color variations.
Stems
Cylindrical, woody, and branched with opposite phyllotaxis, the stems are covered with short, stiff hairs and bear persistent, scale-like stipules at the nodes.
