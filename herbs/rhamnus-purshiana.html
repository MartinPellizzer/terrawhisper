Cascara Sagrada, scientifically known as Rhamnus purshiana, is a medicinal herb native to the western United States, traditionally used for its purgative properties. It is valued for its ability to support digestive health by stimulating bowel movements, making it a key ingredient in natural laxatives. Historically, Native American tribes such as the Cherokee and Navajo used Cascara Sagrada to treat constipation and other gastrointestinal issues, recognizing its potent effects on the digestive system. In modern wellness practices, it is still used as a gentle laxative, though its use has declined due to concerns about long-term toxicity and regulatory restrictions. Notably, Cascara Sagrada contains unique compounds like anthraquinones, which contribute to its strong laxative action, and it has a distinctive earthy, slightly bitter flavor that sets it apart from other herbal remedies.
