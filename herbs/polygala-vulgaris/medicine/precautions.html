10 Precautions To Take When Using Polygala Vulgaris (Milkwort)

Polygala vulgaris has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as monitoring blood sugar levels and adjusting insulin dosages, especially for individuals with diabetes.

This is because the herb can affect blood sugar regulation and potentially lower blood sugar levels.

If not monitored, this can lead to hypoglycemia, causing symptoms like dizziness, shakiness, and fatigue.

1. Check for contaminants before use

When using Polygala vulgaris medicinally, it's important to consult a doctor before use.

This is because the herb can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes drugs, which can lead to serious health consequences if not monitored properly. Additionally, Polygala vulgaris may not be suitable for people with kidney or liver disease, or those taking immunosuppressants, without proper medical guidance.

A doctor's consultation ensures safe and effective use of the herb, minimizing potential risks and maximizing its therapeutic benefits.

When using Polygala vulgaris medicinally, it's important to avoid during pregnancy and breastfeeding.

This precaution is necessary because the plant's active compounds may stimulate uterine contractions, potentially leading to miscarriage or premature labor. Additionally, its stimulant properties could transfer to the baby through breastmilk, causing adverse effects on the infant's nervous system development.

Therefore, it is crucial to exercise caution and consult with a healthcare professional before using Polygala vulgaris medicinally during these sensitive periods.
