1. Reduce inflammation

Inflammation is a physiological response to injury, infection, or irritation, often characterized by redness, swelling, and pain.

Chronic inflammation, a prolonged and persistent form of this response, can lead to tissue damage and various diseases.

It is commonly caused by factors such as poor diet, stress, environmental toxins, and genetic predispositions.

People with conditions like arthritis, diabetes, or autoimmune disorders often experience chronic inflammation.

The polygala vulgaris plant has been studied for its potential to reduce inflammation by modulating immune responses and inhibiting inflammatory pathways.

How this herb helps with reduce inflammation?

Polygala vulgaris reduce inflammation because it contains bioactive constituents such as alkaloids and flavonoids.

These compounds possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help suppress the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines. Alkaloids in the plant may inhibit enzymes involved in the inflammatory response, while flavonoids contribute to reducing oxidative stress. Together, these components work synergistically to alleviate symptoms associated with inflammatory conditions.

The combination of these natural compounds makes Polygala vulgaris a promising candidate for supporting inflammatory health.

2. Relieve constipation issues

Constipation, characterized by infrequent bowel movements and difficulty passing stool, is a common digestive issue.

It can be caused by a lack of dietary fiber, dehydration, lack of physical activity, or certain medications.

Individuals with a slow metabolism, older adults, and those with a poor diet are more likely to experience constipation.

Polygala vulgaris, also known as the common milkvetch, has been traditionally used to alleviate constipation by promoting digestive health and improving bowel regularity.

How this herb helps with relieve constipation issues?

These compounds help stimulate bowel movements by increasing intestinal motility. The alkaloids in Polygala vulgaris may interact with the nervous system to promote digestive function. Flavonoids contribute to the plant's overall anti-inflammatory and digestive support effects.

Together, these constituents make Polygala vulgaris a traditional remedy for easing constipation.
