Polygala (Polygala vulgaris) Uses

Polygala vulgaris is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, cough, and respiratory tract infections. It also aids in managing allergic rhinitis and supports wound healing for skin infections and wounds. Additionally, it helps alleviate mental fatigue.

Polygala (Polygala vulgaris) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Polygala vulgaris is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. It helps reduce inflammation and soothe coughing by acting as an expectorant and antimicrobial agent. Its bitter compounds stimulate mucus clearance and may enhance immune response in the respiratory tract.

2. Asthma

Polygala vulgaris has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. It may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing bronchial inflammation and improving airflow. Its expectorant properties aid in clearing mucus, making breathing easier. It is often used in herbal formulations for chronic respiratory conditions.

3. Cough

Polygala vulgaris is used to alleviate coughs by soothing the respiratory tract and reducing inflammation. It helps loosen mucus and ease bronchial congestion, making it effective for both dry and productive coughs. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture to support respiratory health.

4. Respiratory tract infection

Polygala vulgaris is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing irritated airways. It may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections such as coughing and congestion. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture to promote easier breathing and clear mucus. Its expectorant properties aid in expelling phlegm and easing respiratory discomfort.

5. Allergic rhinitis

Polygala vulgaris is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose by modulating immune responses. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tincture or decoction and taken orally to manage seasonal allergies effectively.

6. Respiratory infections

Polygala vulgaris is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and easing coughing. It may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections such as bronchitis and pneumonia. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tincture or decoction to promote clear breathing and soothe irritation in the airways.

7. Skin infections

Polygala vulgaris is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce inflammation and promotes healing. The plant's extracts can be applied directly to affected areas. It is particularly effective against bacterial and fungal infections.

8. Wound healing

Polygala vulgaris is used to promote wound healing due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps in reducing swelling, preventing infection, and accelerating tissue repair. Traditionally applied topically, it is valued for its ability to support the body's natural healing processes in minor cuts and abrasions.

9. Wounds

Polygala vulgaris has been traditionally used to support wound healing due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote tissue repair. Applications include topical use on minor cuts and abrasions, often in poultice or salve form. Its ability to enhance cellular regeneration makes it a valued herb in herbal wound care.

10. Mental fatigue

Polygala vulgaris is used to alleviate mental fatigue by enhancing cognitive function and reducing mental exhaustion. It supports focus and clarity, making it beneficial for those experiencing stress-related mental weariness. Traditionally, it has been valued for its ability to restore mental vitality and promote calmness.
