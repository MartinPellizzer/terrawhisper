Cynanchum forrestii

What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum forrestii?

Cynanchum forrestii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Gentianales. This plant is further categorized under the family Apocynaceae, placing it within a group of flowering plants often known for their milky sap. Within this family, it is situated in the genus Cynanchum, specifically identified by its unique species designation, forrestii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum forrestii has slender, twining stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that possess entire margins and acute apices.

The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a relatively thin lamina. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves in clusters. These blossoms are characterized by a complex structure typical of the Apocynaceae family, often exhibiting a corona.

The plant produces elongated follicles that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
