Cynanchum forrestii

What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum forrestii?

Cynanchum forrestii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Gentianales. This plant is further categorized under the family Apocynaceae, placing it within a group of flowering plants often known for their milky sap. Within this family, it is situated in the genus Cynanchum, specifically identified by its unique species designation, forrestii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum forrestii has slender, twining stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that possess entire margins and acute apices. The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a relatively thin lamina. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves in clusters. These blossoms are characterized by a complex structure typical of the Apocynaceae family, often exhibiting a corona. The plant produces elongated follicles that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (+)5'-methoxyisolariciresinol 3a-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, beta-sitosterol, daucosterol, hexahydroxycholest-7-en-6-one, and tylophorinidine. These specific phytochemicals represent a diverse range of lignans, sterols, and alkaloids found within the species. The presence of beta-sitosterol and daucosterol highlights the plant's rich sterol profile. Additionally, the identification of hexahydroxycholest-7-en-6-one and the lignan derivative contributes to its unique chemical signature. The alkaloid tylophorinidine further characterizes the complex secondary metabolites extracted from Cynanchum forrestii.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", eight compounds were isolated from the 95% ethanol extract of the roots of Cynanchum forrestii. These medicinal compounds include (+)-5'-methoxyisolariciresinol 3a-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (1), hexahydroxycholest-7-en-6-one (2), tylophorinidine (3), sucrose (4), palmitic acid (5), beta-sitosterol (6), daucosterol (7), and nonanedioic acid (8). Within this specific plant, compounds 4, 5, 6, 7, and 8 were obtained for the first time. The study used chromatographic techniques to isolate the constituents and identified them using physico-chemical constants and spectroscopic data.
