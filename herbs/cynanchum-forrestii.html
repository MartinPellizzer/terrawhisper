Cynanchum forrestii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum forrestii has slender, twining stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that possess entire margins and acute apices. The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a relatively thin lamina. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves in clusters. These blossoms are characterized by a complex structure typical of the Apocynaceae family, often exhibiting a corona. The plant produces elongated follicles that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
