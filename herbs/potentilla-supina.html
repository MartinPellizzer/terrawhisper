What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla supina?

Potentiella supina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Rosaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Potentiella. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, supina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella supina has a prostrate, mat-forming growth habit characterized by creeping stems that root at the nodes and small, trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The leaves typically consist of three leaflets that are ovate to elliptical in shape and covered in fine, soft hairs. Tiny, five-petaled flowers emerge from the foliage, usually displaying a bright yellow color. These blossoms are supported by short pedicels that arise from the low-growing stems. The overall structure creates a dense, ground-covering carpet of greenery.
