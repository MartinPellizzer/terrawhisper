Vitis betulifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitis betulifolia has woody, climbing vines characterized by trifoliate or pentafoliate leaves with serrated margins and ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The foliage typically displays a distinct texture with prominent venation that distinguishes it from other grapevine species. Its stems are equipped with specialized tendrils that emerge from the nodes to facilitate upward growth and support. The inflorescences consist of small, greenish flowers that eventually develop into clusters of berries. These fruits transition in color as they ripen, providing a key morphological marker for identification.
