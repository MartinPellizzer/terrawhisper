Glochidion puberum

What's the taxonomical classification of Glochidion puberum?

Glochidion puberum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Glochidion, with its specific designation being the species puberum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glochidion puberum has small, elliptical to oblong leaves that are densely covered in fine, soft hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. These leaves are arranged alternately along the stems and possess entire margins with acute or obtuse tips. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that lack petals and are typically found in axillary clusters. Its woody stems are often characterized by a puberulent texture that matches the foliage. The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that eventually splits to reveal seeds.
