Glochidion puberum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glochidion puberum has small, elliptical to oblong leaves that are densely covered in fine, soft hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. These leaves are arranged alternately along the stems and possess entire margins with acute or obtuse tips. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that lack petals and are typically found in axillary clusters. Its woody stems are often characterized by a puberulent texture that matches the foliage. The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that eventually splits to reveal seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically distributed across parts of Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. Its range is restricted to these specific equatorial zones where the climate remains stable throughout the year.       What are the herbal preparations of Glochidion puberum?

      Glochidion puberum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the specific medicinal application required. The dried leaves are often boiled in water to create a liquid tea used for internal consumption. For external ailments like skin irritations, the plant material is crushed into a thick paste to be applied directly to the affected area.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Glochidion puberum can cause severe skin irritation, intense itching, and localized inflammation due to the presence of irritating hairs or chemical compounds on its surface. Contact with the plant often leads to contact dermatitis, which manifests as red, swollen, or bumpy patches on the skin. These reactions can become particularly painful if the plant's fine structures penetrate the dermal layer. Some individuals may also experience allergic hypersensitivity, resulting in prolonged discomfort or secondary infections from scratching. Avoiding direct physical contact is the most effective way to prevent these adverse dermatological responses.
