Clematis otophora

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis otophora?

Clematis otophora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Clematis, with the specific identification belonging to the species otophorus.

What are the therapeutic actions of Clematis otophora?

Clematis otophora has therapeutic properties primarily used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of respiratory ailments, specifically targeting coughs, asthma, and bronchial congestion. The plant contains bioactive compounds that act as expectorants to help clear mucus from the airways. It is also frequently applied topically to reduce inflammation and soothe skin irritations. Some practitioners utilize its derivatives to help regulate blood circulation and manage minor swelling. These medicinal applications are rooted in its historical use within regional herbal practices to support lung health.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin infections, and respiratory ailments. It contains bioactive compounds that may help alleviate symptoms of swelling and localized irritation. In some traditional practices, preparations are applied topically to address dermatological issues or taken internally to soothe bronchial discomfort. The efficacy of these treatments often relies on the specific concentration of its medicinal constituents. Further research is typically required to determine precise dosages and long-term safety profiles for these applications.
