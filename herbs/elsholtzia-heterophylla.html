Elsholtzia heterophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia heterophyllia has pinnately compound leaves that can be imparipinnate or even bipinnate, featuring a variable number of leaflets that range from 10 to 30 per leaf. The leaflets themselves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins and acute tips. This species produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in panicle-like clusters. The fruit consists of small, winged samaras that are held upright on the branches. Its bark is generally greyish and becomes deeply fissured as the tree reaches maturity.
