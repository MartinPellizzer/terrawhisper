What's the taxonomical classification of Anemone rupicola?

Anemone rupicola belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Ranunculales, this plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. It is further categorized under the genus Anemone, specifically identified by the species epithet rupicola.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone rupicola has a basal rosette of deeply lobed, hairy leaves and solitary, actinomorphic flowers with five to seven white or pale pink petals. The leaves are typically divided into narrow, linear segments that emerge from a short, creeping rhizome. Each flower sits atop a slender, pubescent scape that can reach heights of approximately ten to twenty centimeters. The reproductive center consists of a cluster of numerous stamens surrounding a central aggregate of carpels. These delicate structures are adapted to thrive in rocky, alpine environments where soil is sparse.
