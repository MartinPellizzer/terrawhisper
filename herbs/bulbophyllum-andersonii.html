What's the taxonomical classification of Bulbophyllum andersonii?

Bulbophyllum andersonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized into the order Asparagales. As a member of the diverse family Orchidaceae, it is placed within the genus Bulbophyllum, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species andersonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum andersonii has a creeping rhizome that produces small, ovate to elliptical pseudobulbs, each bearing a single apical leaf.

The leaves are typically leathery in texture and relatively small compared to other members of its genus. Its inflorescence consists of a slender scape that bears multiple small, fragrant flowers arranged in a raceme. The individual flowers feature complex labella and specialized column structures characteristic of the orchid family.

These botanical features allow the plant to effectively colonize various forest substrates.
