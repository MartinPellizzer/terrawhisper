What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum andersonii has a creeping rhizome that produces small, ovate to elliptical pseudobulbs, each bearing a single apical leaf. Its inflorescence consists of a slender scape that bears multiple small, fragrant flowers arranged in a raceme. The individual flowers feature complex labella and specialized column structures characteristic of the orchid family.
