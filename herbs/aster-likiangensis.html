Aster likiangensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster likiangensis has perennial, herbaceous stems that reach heights of approximately 10 to 40 centimeters and bear lanceolate to linear leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically characterized by a grayish-green hue and a texture that can range from smooth to slightly pubescent. Its inflorescence consists of solitary, radiate flower heads featuring prominent yellow disc florets surrounded by numerous white or pale lavender ray florets. The involucral bracts are arranged in multiple overlapping rows, providing structural support to the developing flower heads. These distinctive morphological features allow the plant to thrive within its specific montane habitats.
