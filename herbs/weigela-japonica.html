Weigela japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Weigela japonica?

Weigelia japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Dipsacales. The plant is a member of the family Caprifoliaceae, which places it within the genus Weigela, and it is specifically identified by its species name, japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Weigela japonica has opposite, elliptical leaves that measure up to 5 centimeters in length and are characterized by finely serrated margins and a smooth, dark green surface.

The plant typically grows as a spreading deciduous shrub with arching, woody stems that can reach several feet in height. During the flowering season, it produces small, trumpet-shaped blossoms that are usually pink or white and emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers are often accompanied by small, rounded fruit capsules that develop as the season progresses.

The overall structure of the shrub is dense and bushy, making it a robust ornamental feature in garden landscapes.
