Weigela japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Weigela japonica?

Weigelia japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Dipsacales. The plant is a member of the family Caprifoliaceae, which places it within the genus Weigela, and it is specifically identified by its species name, japonica.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Weigela japonica can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or pets. Ingestion of the plant parts might lead to vomiting or stomach upset in small animals. Most dermatological reactions are mild and resolve quickly after avoiding further contact with the plant. Careful observation is recommended if children or pets interact with the shrub.
