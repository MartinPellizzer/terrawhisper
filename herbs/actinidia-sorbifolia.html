Actinidia sorbifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Actinidia sorbifolia has woody, climbing vines with opposite, ovate to broadly elliptical leaves that feature serrated margins and prominent venation. The foliage typically presents a bright green color and a somewhat leathery texture to the touch. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the growing season. These blooms eventually develop into small, fleshy berries that serve as the plant's fruit. The stems are characterized by their twining habit, allowing the plant to ascend through surrounding vegetation.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and western China, spanning provinces such as Sichuan, Hubei, Gansu, and Shaanxi. It typically thrives in temperate montane environments located at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Within these habitats, it is frequently found growing in shaded forest understories and along rocky slopes. The distribution is largely dictated by the availability of moist, well-drained soils characteristic of these highland ecosystems.
