What to know about Armoracia rusticana (horseradish) before using it medicinally

Horseradish, scientifically known as Armoracia rusticana, is a potent herb that has been prized for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to boost immunity and reduce inflammation.

In addition to its medicinal uses, horseradish is also a versatile ingredient in many culinary traditions, often used to add spice and flavor to sauces, marinades, and dips. From a horticultural perspective, horseradish is a low-maintenance perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Botanically speaking, horseradish belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which also includes mustard and cabbage.

With a history dating back over 2,000 years to ancient Rome, where it was considered a medicinal staple, horseradish has been a prized ingredient for centuries, with its uses documented by numerous historical figures from Pliny the Elder to Apicius.
