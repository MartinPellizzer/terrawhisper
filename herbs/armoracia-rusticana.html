Armoracia rusticana leaf has been historically valued for its medicinal properties, particularly due to its high concentration of compounds like sinigrin and allyl isothiocyanate, which contribute to its pungent flavor and therapeutic effects.

Overall, the Armoracia rusticana leaf offers a range of potential health benefits, making it a valuable component in both culinary and medicinal applications.

This herb can also lead to coughing by inflaming the airways, making it particularly troublesome for individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions. In addition to coughing, Armoracia rusticana causes nausea and induces vomiting, as its strong compounds can upset the gastrointestinal system.

The herb may also trigger throat irritation, leading to a sore or scratchy sensation, and in some cases, it can cause respiratory irritation, resulting in difficulty breathing or wheezing. Furthermore, Armoracia rusticana may induce skin rash in sensitive individuals, as contact with its oils can provoke allergic reactions.

These side effects highlight the importance of caution when using this herb, especially for those with known sensitivities or chronic health conditions.
