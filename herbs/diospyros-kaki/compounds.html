Kaki (Diospyros kaki) Compounds

The primary compounds in Kaki include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and tannins, which have astringent properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, while anthocyanins provide vibrant color and potential cardiovascular benefits. Glycosides, saponins, and coumarin also play roles in its medicinal and therapeutic properties.

Kaki (Diospyros kaki) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Kaki (Diospyros kaki). They contribute to the fruit's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. These compounds support various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular protective effects.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Kaki (Diospyros kaki). It exhibits astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine to reduce inflammation and aid digestion. Tannins also contribute to the fruit's antioxidant capacity, supporting overall health and immune function.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Kaki (Diospyros kaki). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular protective effects.

4. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Kaki (Diospyros kaki), contributing to its antioxidant properties. Found in the fruit's skin and flesh, it helps protect cells from oxidative stress. This compound supports cardiovascular health and may reduce inflammation. Anthocyanin is also linked to improved vision and immune function in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycosides in Kaki (Diospyros kaki) are naturally occurring compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties. These glycosides, such as diosgenin, have been studied for their potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They are often extracted and used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic value.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Kaki (Diospyros kaki). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Saponin also aids in enhancing nutrient absorption and supporting immune function.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a natural compound found in Kaki (Diospyros kaki). It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in promoting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation. Coumarin also acts as a phytoestrogen, supporting hormonal balance in herbal medicine applications.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Kaki (Diospyros kaki). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities. These compounds are often responsible for the therapeutic effects associated with Kaki in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Kaki (Diospyros kaki) contains volatile compounds like limonene and linalool. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and may support respiratory and digestive health. Essential oil is often used in aromatherapy and as a complementary treatment in herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Kaki (Diospyros kaki) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds with potential pharmacological effects. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. However, research on their specific roles and mechanisms in Kaki remains limited.
