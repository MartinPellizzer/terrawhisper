Crotalaria tetragona

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria tetragonia has a sprawling or erect herbaceous habit characterized by quadrangular, angled stems that can reach heights of up to one meter. The leaves are primarily alternate and trifoliate, featuring small, ovate to elliptic leaflets with entire margins. Small, bright yellow flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a typical pea-like structure characteristic of the Fabaceae family. After pollination, the plant produces distinctive bladder-like, inflated seed pods that rattle when shaken. These pods are typically oblong or ellipsoid and serve to protect the developing seeds inside.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, ranging from the southern United States through Central America and deep into South America. It is most frequently encountered in diverse habitats such as disturbed roadsides, open grasslands, and coastal scrublands. Populations are widely distributed across countries including Mexico, Brazil, and Argentina. The species thrives in warm climates where seasonal rainfall supports its growth in sunny, open environments. Due to its hardiness, it has expanded its presence across various disturbed landscapes within these specific latitudinal belts.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Crotalaria tetragona targets specific inflammatory enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha). These biochemical interactions are primarily driven by the plant's rich profile of pyrrolizidine alkaloids and flavonoids. By inhibiting the COX-2 enzyme, the plant's compounds effectively reduce the production of prostaglandins that trigger pain and swelling. Additionally, the modulation of iNOS helps decrease nitric oxide levels, which mitigates oxidative stress in damaged tissues. This multi-target approach allows the plant to exert significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects within biological systems.

What are the therapeutic actions of Crotalaria tetragona?

Crotalaria tetragona has medicinal properties that allow it to function as an astringent, hemostatic, anti-inflammatory, and wound healing agent. The plant contains bioactive compounds that help constrict body tissues to reduce swelling and irritation. Its hemostatic qualities are particularly useful for controlling bleeding at the site of an injury. By reducing inflammation, the plant assists in calming localized redness and pain. These combined actions accelerate the natural wound healing process by protecting damaged skin and promoting tissue repair.

Crotalaria tetragona is prepared as a decoction of the roots, a powder from the dried leaves, or a topical paste made from the crushed plant parts. What side-effects this plant can have?

Crotalaria tetragona can cause severe liver damage, neurological impairment, and death due to the presence of toxic pyrrolizidine alkaloids in its tissues. When ingested, these alkaloids undergo metabolic activation in the liver, leading to progressive hepatic fibrosis and potentially fatal cirrhosis. Beyond liver toxicity, the plant's chemical components can disrupt central nervous system functions and lead to systemic organ failure. These toxic effects are particularly dangerous for livestock and humans if the plant is consumed in significant quantities. Regular exposure or accidental ingestion poses a high risk of chronic or acute poisoning.
