What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria tetragonia has a sprawling or erect herbaceous habit characterized by quadrangular, angled stems that can reach heights of up to one meter. The leaves are primarily alternate and trifoliate, featuring small, ovate to elliptic leaflets with entire margins. Small, bright yellow flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a typical pea-like structure characteristic of the Fabaceae family. After pollination, the plant produces distinctive bladder-like, inflated seed pods that rattle when shaken. These pods are typically oblong or ellipsoid and serve to protect the developing seeds inside.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe toxicity in humans and animals due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids within its tissues. Ingesting these chemical compounds can lead to significant liver damage, including conditions like cirrhosis or hepatic degeneration. Initial symptoms of poisoning often manifest as gastrointestinal distress, specifically nausea and vomiting. If the plant is consumed regularly, the cumulative effects may lead to irreversible organ failure. Constant monitoring is necessary if accidental ingestion is suspected.
