American Ginseng (Panax americanus) Uses

American Ginseng is commonly used to support the immune system and alleviate symptoms of the common cold and influenza. It may also help manage respiratory conditions like asthma and bronchitis, as well as reduce the severity of skin infections and promote wound healing, including chronic wounds. Additionally, it is used to address allergies and inflammatory conditions such as psoriasis.

American Ginseng (Panax americanus) Monograph

1. Common cold

American Ginseng is used to support immune function and may help alleviate symptoms of the common cold. It is believed to enhance respiratory health and reduce the severity of cold-related discomfort. Traditionally, it is taken as a tonic to promote overall vitality during illness. Its mild nature makes it suitable for long-term use in supporting immune resilience.

2. Influenza

American Ginseng supports immune function and may reduce the severity of influenza symptoms. It contains adaptogenic compounds that enhance the body's resilience to viral infections. Clinical studies suggest it can shorten recovery time and alleviate fever and fatigue associated with the flu.

3. Asthma

American Ginseng may support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and improving lung function. Some studies suggest it may help alleviate asthma symptoms, though more research is needed. It is often used in herbal formulations to complement conventional asthma treatments. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.

4. Bronchitis

American Ginseng is used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. It helps reduce inflammation and soothe coughing by enhancing immune function and promoting mucus clearance. Its adaptogenic properties may also aid in reducing fatigue associated with prolonged illness.

5. Respiratory infections

American Ginseng supports respiratory health by enhancing immune function and reducing inflammation. It may help alleviate symptoms of colds, bronchitis, and other respiratory infections. Its adaptogenic properties boost the body's resilience to stress, which can indirectly support recovery from illness. Clinical studies suggest it may reduce the duration and severity of respiratory infections when used consistently.

6. Skin infections

American Ginseng has been traditionally used to support skin health and may help in managing infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is believed to enhance immune function, which can aid in the body's response to bacterial and fungal skin infections. However, it should be used as a complementary therapy under professional guidance.

7. Psoriasis

American Ginseng (Panax americanus) may help manage psoriasis by modulating immune response and reducing inflammation. It supports skin health through its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Studies suggest it may alleviate symptoms when used as part of a holistic treatment plan. Its adaptogenic qualities also help reduce stress, a known trigger for psoriasis flare-ups.

8. Wound healing

American Ginseng supports wound healing by enhancing immune function and reducing inflammation. Its active compounds, such as ginsenosides, promote tissue repair and cellular regeneration. Clinical studies suggest it may accelerate healing and reduce infection risk in chronic wounds. It is often used in herbal formulations for its synergistic effects on recovery.

9. Chronic wounds

American Ginseng supports wound healing in chronic wounds by enhancing immune function and reducing inflammation. It promotes tissue regeneration and improves circulation, which are essential for healing non-healing ulcers and sores. Its adaptogenic properties help the body manage stress, further supporting recovery processes.

10. Allergies

American Ginseng may help alleviate allergy symptoms by supporting immune function and reducing inflammatory responses. It contains compounds that may inhibit histamine release, potentially lessening nasal congestion and sneezing. Studies suggest it can be a complementary therapy for seasonal allergies when used alongside conventional treatments.
