Cotoneaster bullatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cotoneaster bullatus?

Cotoneaster bullatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down its taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Cotoneaster, specifically identified by its species name, bullatus.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cotoneaster bullatus?

Cotoneaster bullatus has no documented therapeutic actions or medicinal properties in human pharmacology or traditional herbalism. While many plants in the Rosaceae family contain various bioactive compounds, this specific species is primarily utilized in landscaping for its ornamental value. Because it is not classified as a medicinal herb, consuming any part of the plant could potentially be toxic rather than healing. Consequently, it should be treated strictly as a decorative shrub rather than a therapeutic agent.

What are the herbal preparations of Cotoneaster bullatus?

Cotoneaster bullatus is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture derived from its dried berries and leaves. The berries are often boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal use. Leaves may be steeped in hot water to form a mild herbal tea or infusion. For a more potent medicinal application, the plant material is soaked in alcohol to produce a tincture. These various forms allow for different concentrations of the plant's active compounds.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cotoneaster bullatus can cause gastrointestinal distress and potential toxicity if the berries are ingested by humans or pets. The plant contains cyanogenic glycosides which may release toxic hydrogen cyanide when the fruit is consumed. Symptoms of poisoning often include vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. In severe cases of ingestion, more serious neurological or systemic issues could arise from the toxic compounds. It is essential to keep this shrub away from curious children and domestic animals to prevent accidental poisoning.
