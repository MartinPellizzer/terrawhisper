What's the taxonomical classification of Cotoneaster bullatus?

Cotoneaster bullatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down its taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Cotoneaster, specifically identified by its species name, bullatus.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Cotoneaster bullatus can cause gastrointestinal distress and potential toxicity if the berries are ingested by humans or pets. The plant contains cyanogenic glycosides which may release toxic hydrogen cyanide when the fruit is consumed. Symptoms of poisoning often include vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. In severe cases of ingestion, more serious neurological or systemic issues could arise from the toxic compounds. It is essential to keep this shrub away from curious children and domestic animals to prevent accidental poisoning.
