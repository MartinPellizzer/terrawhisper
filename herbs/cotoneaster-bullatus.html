Cotoneaster bullatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cotoneaster bullatus has small, densely packed, oval-shaped leaves that are characterized by a distinctly convex or bullate surface texture. These leaves are typically dark green on the upper side and often possess a slightly fuzzy or pubescent underside. The plant exhibits a low-growing, spreading growth habit with woody stems that form a compact mound. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in clusters during the spring season. Following the flowering period, the plant produces small, fleshy red berries that persist into the colder months.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Cotoneaster bullatus can cause gastrointestinal distress and potential toxicity if the berries are ingested by humans or pets. The plant contains cyanogenic glycosides which may release toxic hydrogen cyanide when the fruit is consumed. Symptoms of poisoning often include vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. In severe cases of ingestion, more serious neurological or systemic issues could arise from the toxic compounds. It is essential to keep this shrub away from curious children and domestic animals to prevent accidental poisoning.
