Pongamia pinnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pongamia pinnata?

Pongamia pinnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Fabales. The plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, falling under the genus Pongamia, and is specifically identified by its species name, pinnata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pongamia pinnata has a medium to large tree structure characterized by a spreading canopy, a thick trunk with fissured greyish bark, and pinnately compound leaves that are elliptical or oblong in shape.

These leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and slightly paler underneath, often measuring between 10 to 25 centimeters in length. The tree produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that grow in axillary racemes. Its fruit is a woody, indehiscent pod that is roughly spherical or ovoid and contains a single large seed.

These seeds are oily and serve as a primary morphological feature used for identifying the species.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Pongamia pinnata contains medicinal compounds found in its seeds, bark, leaves, and roots that are utilized for diverse therapeutic purposes. The oil extracted from the seeds is widely used to treat various skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and fungal infections. Bark extracts are often applied topically or used in decoctions to manage inflammation and promote wound healing. The leaves are frequently crushed into a paste to serve as an antiseptic or used in traditional remedies to alleviate respiratory issues. Additionally, the roots provide specific alkaloids that contribute to the plant's overall pharmacological profile in traditional medicine.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex profile of bioactive molecules including flavonoids, terpenoids, alkaloids, phenolics, fatty acids, steroids, and coumarins.

These chemical constituents contribute to the diverse pharmacological activities observed in Pongamia pinnata, such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. The presence of specific phenolics and flavonoids provides significant antioxidant properties that protect cells from oxidative stress. Additionally, the terpenoids and alkaloids are often linked to its traditional use in treating various skin conditions and infections. The fatty acids found in the seed oil serve as a fundamental component for therapeutic applications.

Together, these diverse compounds form the basis of its extensive medicinal utility.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Pongamia pinnata targets various enzymes and signaling proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and tumor necrosis factor-alpha to mediate its anti-inflammatory and anticancer activities. The bioactive compounds within the plant, such as karanjin and nimbin, function by inhibiting the NF-kappaB signaling pathway to reduce systemic inflammation. These phytochemicals also interact with apoptotic regulators like caspases and Bcl-2 family proteins to induce programmed cell death in malignant cells. Furthermore, the plant's constituents can modulate various kinases and antioxidant enzymes to mitigate oxidative stress at the cellular level. Through these diverse molecular interactions, the plant exerts its broad range of pharmacological effects.

Pongamia pinnata has therapeutic properties characterized by its bitter and astringent tastes which facilitate various physiological healing processes.

Additionally, the botanical extracts serve as a purgative to aid digestion and act as a vulnerary to promote the healing of wounds.

This plant is used to treat skin diseases such as eczema, psoriasis, and scabies, as well as inflammatory conditions like arthritis and various bacterial or fungal infections.

Its oil possesses potent antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help clear dermatological issues. Additionally, the plant is applied topically to soothe wounds and promote the healing of minor burns or ulcers. What are the herbal preparations of Pongamia pinnata?

Pongamia pinnata is prepared as an oil extracted from its seeds, a decoction made from its bark, and various topical pastes derived from its leaves. The seed oil, often called Karanja oil, serves as a primary component in many traditional dermatological treatments and insect repellents. Bark extracts are frequently boiled to create medicinal infusions used for their antimicrobial properties. Leaf preparations can be crushed into a pulp to form poultices for skin infections or applied as a liquid wash. These diverse forms allow the plant to be utilized for both internal wellness and external topical applications.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Pongamia pinnata can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested due to the presence of toxic alkaloids and rotenoids. These bioactive compounds are known to be cytotoxic, meaning they can damage cells when they come into direct contact with sensitive tissues. Topical application of certain extracts may lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling in individuals with hypersensitivity. If consumed in significant quantities, the plant can induce nausea, vomiting, and more severe systemic toxicity. Researchers advise extreme caution because the biological potency of these chemicals can lead to unpredictable physiological reactions.
