What to know about Ilex paraguariensis (yerba mate) before using it medicinally

Yerba mate, scientifically known as Ilex paraguariensis, is a nutrient-rich herb that has been touted for its numerous health benefits.

Its high antioxidant content and ability to boost metabolism have made it a popular ingredient in weight loss supplements and energy drinks. In culinary applications, yerba mate is often used as a tea substitute or added to soups, stews, and sauces for its unique flavor. Horticulturally, yerba mate is a woody perennial shrub native to South America that thrives in subtropical climates.

With its rich history dating back to the indigenous Guarani people of Paraguay, who revered it as a sacred plant, yerba mate has been a staple in traditional medicine and daily life for centuries.
