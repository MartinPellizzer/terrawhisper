Cyclopia (Cyclopia intermedia): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Cyclopia intermedia, commonly known as cyclopia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa and is widely used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported cognitive and respiratory benefits.

What is Cyclopia (Cyclopia Intermedia)?

Cyclopia intermedia, commonly known as cyclopia, is a South African herb belonging to the Geraniaceae family, characterized by its distinctive single eye-like structure on its leaves and is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported cognitive and calming properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Cyclopia is Cyclopia intermedia. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Cyclopia. It is commonly known as cyclopia in South Africa and as the African dandelion in parts of East Africa. Synonyms for this plant include Cyclopia intermedia, Cyclopia africana, and Cyclopia tectorum.

Plant Description and Morphology

Cyclopia is a small, perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with a compact, bushy structure and multiple branching stems.

Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged alternately along the stems, often measuring up to 5 cm in length and 1 cm in width. The flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, typically ranging in color from pale yellow to orange, with a subtle, sweet fragrance. The root system is fibrous and moderately deep, anchoring the plant firmly in the soil and facilitating nutrient absorption.

The plant has a rough, slightly hairy texture on its stems and leaves, with a mild, earthy scent that is more pronounced when the leaves are crushed.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Cyclopia is native to southern Africa, specifically South Africa, Namibia, and Botswana, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its geographic distribution is primarily concentrated in the Karoo and Succulent Karoo biome, with smaller populations extending into parts of eastern and western Africa. Cyclopia prefers warm, dry climates with minimal rainfall, typically growing in sandy or rocky soils. It is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture levels, making it resilient in harsh environments.

The plant's ecological conditions favor well-drained soil and full sun exposure, which are essential for its growth and medicinal properties.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The primary plant parts used medicinally in Cyclopia intermedia are the leaves and flowers.

These parts are harvested during the flowering period to ensure maximum potency of active compounds. The leaves are typically dried and ground into a powder for use in herbal formulations. Flowers are often used in their fresh state or dried for infusion.

Both parts are valued for their traditional medicinal properties in African herbal medicine.
