Cyclopia

Cyclopia intermedia

Cyclopia (Cyclopia intermedia) is a flowering plant native to South Africa, commonly known as the "chamomile of the veld." It is primarily used in traditional medicine to support mental clarity and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression.

Botanical Identification

Cyclopia intermedia, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. The plant has narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers, and it is also referred to as Cyclopia africana in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Asterales
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Genus: Cyclopia
  • Species: Cyclopia intermedia

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Cyclopia intermedia is generally safe when used as directed. However, it should not be used by pregnant or breastfeeding women due to potential risks. Always consult a healthcare professional before use, especially if you have existing medical conditions or are on other medications.
