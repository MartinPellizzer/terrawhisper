10 Medicinal Constituents Of Rosa Centifolia (Damask Rose)

Rosa centifolia has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils.

These constituents contribute to its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiseptic effects. They can improve people's lives by reducing stress and anxiety, promoting wound healing, and alleviating digestive issues.

For example, the essential oil has been traditionally used to soothe skin irritations and promote sleep quality.

1. Rosmarinic acid

Rosa centifolia rosmarinic acid is a bioactive compound isolated from its leaves and flowers.

It has been traditionally used in medicine for centuries due to its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Rosmarinic acid has been shown to exhibit potent activity against various diseases, including cardiovascular, neurological, and cancer disorders.

Its ability to scavenge free radicals also makes it a potential natural preservative and antioxidant agent.

2. Quercetin

Rosa centifolia quercetin is a flavonoid compound extracted from the flowers of this species.

This antioxidant has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Quercetin has been shown to exhibit neuroprotective effects, improve cardiovascular health, and possess anti-cancer properties.

Its unique structure allows it to interact with various enzymes, making it a valuable compound in the development of new treatments for various diseases.

3. Kaempferol

Rosa centifolia kaempferol is a flavonoid glycoside isolated from the petals of this species.

It has been found to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, kaempferol has been reported to have antimicrobial effects against certain bacteria and fungi.

Its chemical structure consists of a kaempferol aglycone with rhamnose and glucuronic acid residues.

4. Caffeic acid

Rosa centifolia caffeic acid is a phenolic compound found in its petals.

This compound has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Caffeic acid has been shown to exhibit antimicrobial activity against certain bacteria and fungi, making it a promising natural preservative.

Additionally, it may also have therapeutic applications in the treatment of various diseases, such as cancer and cardiovascular disorders.

5. Gallic acid

Rosa centifolia gallic acid is a polyphenolic compound extracted from its flowers.

It exhibits significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products. Gallic acid has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antitumor activities, which may contribute to its potential therapeutic benefits for various skin conditions.

Its unique combination of bioactive compounds offers promising opportunities for natural product development.

6. Salicylic acid

Rosa centifolia salicylic acid is a key component extracted from its petals.

This alpha-hydroxy acid (AHA) has been used for centuries in skincare and cosmetics due to its remarkable benefits. Salicylic acid exfoliates the skin, unclogs pores, and reduces inflammation, making it an effective treatment for acne-prone skin and hyperpigmentation.

Its antibacterial properties also help combat bacteria that can cause breakouts.

7. Rosin

Rosa centifolia rosin is a type of resin obtained from the buds and leaves of this fragrant flower.

The resin is extracted through steam distillation and has been used for centuries in perfumery, cosmetics, and traditional medicine. Rosa centifolia rosin is prized for its sweet, floral aroma and is often used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

It is also valued for its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

8. Geraniol

Rosa centifolia geraniol is a natural fragrance oil extracted from the flowers of this species.

It has been used for centuries in perfumery and aromatherapy due to its sweet, floral scent and numerous therapeutic benefits.

Geraniol is said to promote relaxation, reduce stress and anxiety, and possess antibacterial properties, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products and fragrances.

9. Linalool

Rosa centifolia linalool is a naturally occurring compound found in the essential oil of this fragrant flower.

Linalool is a sweet and floral-scented terpene that contributes to the distinctive aroma of Rosa centifolia oil.

It has been used for centuries in perfumery and aromatherapy due to its calming and relaxing properties, as well as its ability to promote emotional balance and reduce anxiety.

10. Citronellol

Rosa centifolia citronellol is a type of sesquiterpene aldehyde that is naturally occurring in the essential oil of the plant.

It is responsible for its distinctive sweet and floral fragrance, which is often used in perfumes, cosmetics, and aromatherapy products.

Citronellol has antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products as well.
