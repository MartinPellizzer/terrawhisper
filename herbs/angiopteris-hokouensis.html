Angiopteris hokouensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angiopteris hokouensis has slender, dichotomously branching stems that are typically upright and reach heights of several centimeters. The plant lacks true leaves, instead utilizing small, scale-like structures along its thin axes for photosynthetic purposes. Its reproductive structures are found in specialized sporangia that are borne on the upper portions of the branches. These branches often appear finely divided, creating a delicate and airy texture in its natural habitat. The overall structure is characteristic of small, primitive vascular plants found in specialized environments.
