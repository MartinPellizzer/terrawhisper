Clerodendrum lindleyi

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum lindleyi?

Clerodendrum lindleyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Clerodendrum, and its species designation, lindleyi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum lindleyi has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically dark green with serrated margins and a slightly textured surface. The plant features striking terminal panicles of tubular, bright red flowers that are contrasted by long, protruding white stamens. These inflorescences emerge from woody stems that exhibit a sprawling or upright growth habit depending on the environment. Once pollination occurs, the plant produces small, fleshy berries that transition in color as they mature. The overall structure of the shrub is characterized by dense foliage that provides a lush backdrop for its vivid floral displays.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, commonly known as Glory Bower, thrives in tropical and subtropical climates when grown in well-draining, organic-rich soil with consistent moisture and bright, indirect sunlight.

It requires a warm environment and should be protected from frost to prevent damage to its delicate foliage. Regular watering is essential to keep the soil moist, though it should never sit in stagnant water to avoid root rot. Providing nitrogen-rich fertilizer during the growing season will encourage the production of its vibrant, red and white tubular flowers.

Periodic pruning helps maintain a tidy shape and stimulates new growth for more prolific blooming.
