Clerodendrum lindleyi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum lindleyi has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically dark green with serrated margins and a slightly textured surface. The plant features striking terminal panicles of tubular, bright red flowers that are contrasted by long, protruding white stamens. These inflorescences emerge from woody stems that exhibit a sprawling or upright growth habit depending on the environment. Once pollination occurs, the plant produces small, fleshy berries that transition in color as they mature. The overall structure of the shrub is characterized by dense foliage that provides a lush backdrop for its vivid floral displays.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically found throughout Thailand, Malaysia, and parts of Indonesia. It thrives in the warm, humid environments characteristic of these equatorial climates. Within these territories, the species is often located in secondary forests or along the edges of wooded areas where sunlight is filtered. Its distribution is primarily restricted to these low-to-mid altitude tropical zones. Because of its specific environmental needs, it does not occur naturally in temperate or arid landscapes.

      Clerodendrum lindleyi is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the specific medicinal purpose.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clerodendrum lindleyi can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized rashes upon direct contact with its foliage or stems. It is advisable to avoid touching the plant if you have sensitive skin or known allergies to similar botanical species.
