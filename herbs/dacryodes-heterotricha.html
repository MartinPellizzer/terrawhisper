Dacryodes heterotricha

What's the taxonomical classification of Dacryodes heterotricha?

Dacryodes heterotricha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Sapindales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Burseraceae, falling under the genus Dacryodes, with its specific identity defined by the species name heterotricha.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dacryodes heterotricha contains bioactive phytochemicals including tannins, saponins, alkaloids, and various terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's bark and leaves. Researchers have identified these compounds as having significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. In traditional medicinal practices, extracts from the plant are often utilized to treat skin infections and inflammatory conditions. The specific synergistic effects of these secondary metabolites provide a foundation for studying the plant's potential in pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dacryodes heterotricha?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, intestinal parasites, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are often prepared into medicinal decoctions to address digestive ailments and stomach discomfort. Local healers frequently apply crushed plant parts directly to skin lesions to promote healing and combat fungal or bacterial growth. Some traditional practices also utilize its properties to mitigate pain and reduce swelling in affected tissues. This botanical species serves as a vital component in many regional pharmacopeias for managing diverse physical maladies.
