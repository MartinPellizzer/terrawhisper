Uraria lagopodioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Uraria lagopodioides has a woody, climbing habit characterized by slender, twining stems that often bear small, inconspicuous hairs. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound with several pairs of small, elliptical to oblong leaflets that feature entire margins. The inflorescences consist of small, axillary clusters of flowers that exhibit a yellowish or greenish hue. Each flower possesses a small, tubular corolla that is characteristic of the Fabaceae family. The resulting fruit is a small, indehiscent legume containing one or two seeds.

What are the therapeutic actions of Uraria lagopodioides?

Uraria lagopodioides has hepatoprotective, cholagogue, diuretic, and aperient properties that support liver and digestive function. Its bitter components stimulate the appetite and trigger the secretion of bile to improve digestion. These combined actions make it a useful botanical agent for managing various metabolic and gastrointestinal concerns.
