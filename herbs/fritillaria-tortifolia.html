What's the taxonomical classification of Fritillaria tortifolia?

Fritillaria tortifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Liliaceae, specifically belonging to the genus Fritillaria, with the unique species designation tortifolia.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-drained, sandy loam soil and a location with full sun to partial shade to ensure healthy bulb development.

Growers should plant the bulbs in early spring once the danger of frost has passed to allow for steady growth. Regular watering is necessary during the active growing season, but the soil must dry out completely during the summer dormancy period to prevent bulb rot. Providing organic matter through compost can significantly improve the soil structure and nutrient availability for these flowering perennials.

Proper drainage is the most critical factor in successful cultivation to avoid moisture-related diseases.
