Chrysophyllum lacourtianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysophyllum lacourtianum has lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are opposite, coriaceous, and feature a distinctively shiny, dark green upper surface with a paler underside. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub with smooth, brownish bark and a compact branching structure. Its inflorescences are small and axillary, bearing numerous tiny, cream-colored flowers. The fruit is a fleshy, globose drupe that matures to a yellowish or brownish hue. Small, fine hairs may be present on the young stems and leaf margins during early development.
