Chrysophyllum lacourtianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysophyllum lacourtianum?

Chrysophyllum lacourtianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. This plant is a representative of the family Sapotaceae, specifically situated within the genus Chrysophyllum. Its complete scientific designation is finalized at the species level as Chrysophyllum lacourtianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysophyllum lacourtianum has lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are opposite, coriaceous, and feature a distinctively shiny, dark green upper surface with a paler underside.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub with smooth, brownish bark and a compact branching structure. Its inflorescences are small and axillary, bearing numerous tiny, cream-colored flowers. The fruit is a fleshy, globose drupe that matures to a yellowish or brownish hue.

Small, fine hairs may be present on the young stems and leaf margins during early development.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Chrysophyllum lacourtianum can cause unpredictable allergic reactions, gastrointestinal distress, or skin irritation in sensitive individuals due to its complex chemical composition. While much of the plant is used in traditional medicine, the specific concentrations of bioactive compounds remain insufficiently studied by modern science. Consuming large quantities or improperly prepared parts could potentially lead to nausea, stomach cramping, or vomiting. Some users might also experience dermatological issues like rashes or itching if the plant sap makes direct contact with the skin. Because clinical data is limited, caution is necessary when using this species for medicinal purposes.
