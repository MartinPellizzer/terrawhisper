Bredia sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Bredia sinensis?

Bredia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Myrtales. Within this order, it is a member of the family Melastomataceae. Specifically, the plant is categorized under the genus Bredia, with its unique identity defined by the species name sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bredia sinensis has a woody, perennial shrubby habit characterized by slender, upright stems and small, lanceolate leaves that are typically opposite or sub-opposite. The foliage features entire margins and acute apices, often displaying a bright green color throughout the growing season. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blooms are typically white or pale in color and are followed by the development of small, fleshy fruits. The overall structure of the plant remains relatively compact, making it suitable for various ornamental landscape applications.
