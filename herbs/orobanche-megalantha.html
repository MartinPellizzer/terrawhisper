Orobanche megalantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Orobanche megalantha has a holoparasitic structure characterized by a fleshy, brownish-to-purplish stem that lacks chlorophyll and root systems. The plant typically reaches heights between 10 and 30 centimeters with scale-like leaves instead of true green foliage. Its most distinctive feature is the large, tubular flowers that possess a wide corolla and a prominent, bilabiate shape. The floral structure is often pale yellow or cream-colored, providing a stark contrast to the darker stem. These specialized morphological adaptations allow the plant to derive all its nutrients directly from its host plant.
