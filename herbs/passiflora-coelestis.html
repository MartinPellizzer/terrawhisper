Blue Passionflower (Passiflora Coelestis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Passiflora coelestis.

Blue passionflower, scientifically known as Passiflora coelestis, is a medicinal herb revered for its calming and adaptogenic properties. It is traditionally used to support mental well-being, alleviate anxiety, and promote restful sleep due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids. In traditional medicine, particularly in South America, it has been employed for centuries to treat nervous disorders, insomnia, and digestive issues, often prepared as a tea or tincture. Today, it is embraced in modern wellness practices as a natural alternative for stress relief and mood stabilization, often incorporated into herbal supplements and calming formulations. Notably, its unique blue-violet flowers and distinctive sweet, aromatic flavor make it both an ornamental and culinary curiosity, while its rare active compounds continue to inspire research into its therapeutic potential.
