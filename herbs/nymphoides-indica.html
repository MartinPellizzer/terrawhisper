Nymphoides indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Nymphoides indica?

Nymphoides indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Asterales and belongs to the family Menyanthaceae. Finally, the plant is identified within the genus Nymphoides, with its specific designation being the species indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nymphoides indica has floating, kidney-shaped leaves that are typically two to ten centimeters in diameter, featuring a smooth margin and a long petiole connected to a submerged rhizome. The foliage is bright green and often displays a glossy texture on its upper surface. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the water, characterized by five white petals that frequently contain yellow spots at the base. These blooms are accompanied by slender, upright stems that extend slightly above the water level. The plant's underwater structure consists of creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread across the surface of stagnant or slow-moving water bodies.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, Nymphoides indica, requires cultivation in shallow, stagnant, or slow-moving freshwater environments with abundant sunlight and nutrient-rich muddy substrates.

It thrives best when submerged or floating in ponds, marshes, or rice paddies where its creeping rhizomes can anchor into the sediment. To ensure healthy growth, the water temperature should remain warm and the pH levels should stay relatively neutral to slightly acidic. Regular monitoring of water clarity is essential as this aquatic species prefers stable conditions to spread its delicate, floating leaves.

What are the pharmacological activities of Nymphoides indica?

This plant has anti-proliferative, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities. The anti-proliferative properties of Nymphoides indica suggest a significant potential for its use in cancer research and therapy. It also contains various bioactive compounds that help neutralize free radicals to prevent oxidative stress in cells. Additionally, the plant exhibits the ability to inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria and reduce inflammatory responses within biological systems. These diverse medicinal qualities make it a subject of intense interest in pharmacological studies.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the medicinal plant recipe N039 containing Nymphoides indica L. showed anti-proliferative activity on the HeLa cell line that was 1.29 times higher than the standard anti-cancer drug cisplatin. The recipe N040, which also includes Nymphoides indica L. Kuntze, exhibited the highest anti-proliferative activity at 30 times that of cisplatin (p<0.05). This specific recipe N040 also demonstrated MMP-2 inhibition on the HeLa cancer cell line at threefold of cisplatin (p<0.05). A positive relationship was observed between the anti-proliferative activity of these extracts and both MMP-2 inhibition activity and metal chelating inhibition activity. Phytochemicals identified in these extracts included alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and xanthones, though anthraqquinones and carotenoids were not found.
