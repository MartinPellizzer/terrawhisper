Microglossa afzelii

What's the taxonomical classification of Microglossa afzelii?

Microglossa afzelii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which places it among the diverse group of composite flowers. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Microglossa and is defined by its unique species name, afzelii.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat intestinal worms, stomach aches, fever, and various skin infections. Traditional healers often prepare decoctions from its roots or leaves to expel parasites from the digestive tract. It is also applied topically to soothe inflammatory skin conditions and promote healing. Some practitioners utilize the plant's properties to reduce high body temperatures during bouts of malaria or flu. Its medicinal application remains a significant component of local herbal practices in its native regions.

What are the herbal preparations of Microglossa afzelii?

Microglossa afzelii is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a crushed paste for topical application. The leaves and roots are often boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for internal ailments. In certain traditional practices, the fresh plant material is ground into a pulp to be applied directly to the skin. This paste serves as a remedy for localized inflammation or minor wounds. These diverse preparation methods allow practitioners to utilize the plant's bioactive compounds for both systemic and localized treatments.

What side-effects this plant can have?
