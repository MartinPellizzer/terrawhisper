What's the taxonomical classification of Lindernia ciliata?

Lindernia ciliata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Linderniaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Lindernia, and its species designation, ciliaata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia ciliata has slender, erect stems that reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and feature oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The leaves are typically small, measuring between 5 and 15 millimeters in length, and possess acute tips. Fine hairs or cilia often decorate the edges of the foliage, providing a characteristic texture. Small, white to pale lavender flowers emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. These blossoms feature a distinct bilabiate structure typical of the Linderniaceae family. The plant ultimately produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
