What's the taxonomical classification of Calanthe brevicornu?

Calanthe brevicornu belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls within the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the Orchidaceae family, situated specifically within the genus Calanthe, and is identified by its unique species name, brevicornu.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Calanthe brevicornu can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and digestive upset if ingested by humans or animals. Contact with the plant's sap may result in redness, itching, or a localized rash on sensitive skin areas. Ingestion of the plant parts could lead to nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting due to unidentified chemical compounds. While specific toxicity levels are not extensively documented, caution is advised when handling the foliage. Always use gloves to prevent direct contact and keep the plant away from curious pets.
