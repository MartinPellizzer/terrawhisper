Ilex centrochinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex centrochinensis?

Ilex centrochinenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Aquifoliales and falls under the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, its taxonomic position is completed within the genus Ilex, specifically identified as the species centrochinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex centrochinensis has small, evergreen leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The leaf texture is leathery and glossy, often featuring a dark green upper surface and a paler underside. Its stems are woody and branch in a way that creates a dense, shrubby or small tree-like habit. The plant produces inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into small, dark drupes. These morphological characteristics allow the species to adapt to its specific montane forest environment.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the temperate rainforests of central Chile, specifically within the coastal and Andean mountain ranges. It grows primarily in the Valdivian temperate rain forest biome under dense canopy cover. Its range is restricted to specific altitudinal zones where moisture levels remain consistently high throughout the year. Populations are often found in fragmented patches of old-growth forest across the central southern latitudes. What are the pharmacological activities of Ilex centrochinensis?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, cytotoxic, and free radical scavenging activities due to its unique phytochemical profile. The presence of specific polyphenolic compounds allows it to effectively inhibit inflammatory mediators in biological systems. Furthermore, its cytotoxic properties suggest a potential role in evaluating its efficacy against various cancer cell lines. The plant also demonstrates significant antioxidant capacity through its ability to neutralize harmful free radicals. These combined pharmacological effects make it a subject of interest for future medicinal research.

According to a study published by "Environmental toxicology and pharmacology", the ethanol extract of Ilex centrochinenensis (EICC) showed a significant anti-inflamatory effect during both acute and chronic inflammation without any acute oral toxicity. The EICC significantly inhibited the formation of MDA and markedly raised the activities of SOD, CAT and GSH-Px. Furthermore, the free radical scavenging activities of EICC were found to be higher than those of EIFH. These results suggest that EICC possesses significant anti-inflamatory and free radical scavenging activities. The anti-inflamatory mechanism is attributed, in part, to the enhancement of the activities of antioxidant enzymes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds kaempferol, naringenin, oleanolic acid, quercetin, and ursolic acid. These specific phytochemicals are known for their diverse biological activities and potential therapeutic benefits. The presence of flavonoids like kaempferol, naringenin, and quercetin suggests significant antioxidant properties. Additionally, the triterpenoids oleanolic acid and ursolic acid contribute to the plant's complex chemical profile. Together, these substances characterize the pharmacological potential of Ilex centrochinenensis.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", ten compounds were isolated from the leaves of Ilex centrochinenensis, identified as 1,4-benzenediol (1), (2S)-5,4'-dihydroxy-7,3'-dimethoxyflavan (2), (2S)-5,4'-dihydroxy-7-methoxyflavan (3), kaempferol (4), quercetin (5), naringenin (6), ursolic acid (7), uvaol (8), oleanolic acid (9) and beta-sitosterols (10). Compounds 1-5, 7, and 8 were isolated from this species for the first time, including compounds 1-3 which were isolated from the Ilex genus for the first time. Regarding cytotoxic activity, compounds 2 and 3 showed strong effects against Huh7 cell lines with IC50 values of 8.98 and 13.04 mg x L(-1). Additionally, compounds 7-9 exhibited weak cytotoxic activity against Caco-2 cell lines with IC50 values of 28.52, 38.28, and 33.04 mg x L(-1).
