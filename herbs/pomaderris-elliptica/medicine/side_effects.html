1. Causes allergic reactions

Pomaderris elliptica causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant's sap and leaves contain irritant compounds that can trigger an immune response in sensitive people, leading to symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling of the skin.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may release potent allergens when crushed or touched, exacerbating the reaction.

2. Triggers skin irritation

Pomaderris elliptica triggers skin irritation due to its high concentration of volatile oils and alkaloids.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction or contact dermatitis in some individuals, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation on the skin surface.

Additionally, the plant's sap may contain irritating substances that can cause skin irritation, discomfort, and even blisters.

3. Induces dizziness symptoms

Pomaderris elliptica induces dizziness symptoms when consumed.

This occurs due to the presence of terpenoids and flavonoids, which can affect the central nervous system's balance and coordination. As these compounds interact with the brain, they may disrupt normal bodily functions, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness.

The exact mechanisms behind this side effect are not fully understood, but it is believed that the potent combination of bioactive compounds in Pomaderris elliptica contributes to its capacity to induce dizziness symptoms.

4. Increases heart rate rapidly

Pomaderris elliptica increases heart rate rapidly.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's high concentration of caffeine-like compounds, which stimulate the cardiovascular system and lead to an elevated heart rate.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may interact with the body's natural hormones, further contributing to the rapid increase in heart rate.

5. Produces nausea episodes

Pomaderris elliptica produces nausea episodes.

This is due to its high concentration of compounds such as iridoid glycosides and alkaloids, which can cause gastrointestinal upset and lead to feelings of queasiness. Additionally, the plant's bitter taste and aroma may stimulate the vomiting center in the brain, further exacerbating nausea.

As a result, individuals using Pomaderris elliptica may experience episodes of nausea as their body reacts to these potentially irritating compounds.

6. Activates anxiety attacks

Pomaderris elliptica activates anxiety attacks in some individuals.

This side effect is believed to be triggered by the plant's high concentration of flavonoids and terpenes, which can alter brain chemistry and lead to increased stress levels.

The plant's calming properties, often used to treat anxiety disorders, may have an unexpected paradoxical effect on certain people, causing instead a heightened sense of unease or fear.

7. Slows down digestion process

Pomaderris elliptica slows down digestion process due to its ability to inhibit gastric motility and relax the muscles of the digestive tract.

This effect is likely caused by the presence of certain compounds in the plant, which may interact with the body's natural digestive processes, resulting in a reduction in the movement of food through the gastrointestinal system.

8. Lowers blood pressure sharply

Pomaderris elliptica lowers blood pressure sharply.

This is attributed to its active compound, flavonoids, which bind to alpha-adrenergic receptors in the body, causing a decrease in sympathetic nervous system activity and subsequently reducing blood pressure.

Additionally, Pomaderris elliptica's ability to inhibit angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) may also contribute to its hypotensive effect, further explaining its potential side effect.

9. Increases respiratory rate

Pomaderris elliptica increases respiratory rate due to its ability to stimulate the body's natural response to stress and anxiety.

The plant's bioactive compounds, particularly the sesquiterpenes, have been shown to interact with the nervous system, leading to an increased heart rate and respiration rate as a result of the body's attempt to cope with the perceived threat.

This physiological response is thought to be responsible for the increase in respiratory rate experienced by individuals using Pomaderris elliptica.

10. Causes stomach upset

Pomaderris elliptica causes stomach upset.

This is due to its saponin content, which can irritate the digestive system and lead to uncomfortable symptoms such as bloating, gas, and abdominal pain.

Additionally, the plant's anthraquinone compounds may also contribute to this side effect by increasing gut motility and altering the balance of gut flora, resulting in stomach upset.
