What's the taxonomical classification of Spermacoce articularis?

Spermacoce articularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized under Equisetopsida and falls into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Gentianaales and the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Spermacoce, and its species designation, articularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spermacoce articularis has a prostrate, creeping growth habit characterized by thin, wiry stems that form dense, mat-like mats across the ground. The plant features small, narrow, linear leaves that are often closely pressed against the stem. Its flowers are minute and typically emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in small clusters. The stems are notably articulated or jointed, which contributes to its unique creeping structure. This morphology allows the plant to effectively colonize open, sandy, or rocky substrates.
