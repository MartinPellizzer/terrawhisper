Spermacoce articularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Spermacoce articularis?

Spermacoce articularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized under Equisetopsida and falls into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Gentianaales and the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Spermacoce, and its species designation, articularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spermacoce articularis has a prostrate, creeping growth habit characterized by thin, wiry stems that form dense, mat-like mats across the ground. The plant features small, narrow, linear leaves that are often closely pressed against the stem. Its flowers are minute and typically emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in small clusters. The stems are notably articulated or jointed, which contributes to its unique creeping structure. This morphology allows the plant to effectively colonize open, sandy, or rocky substrates.

What are the pharmacological activities of Spermacoce articularis?

This plant has antioxidant and nitric oxide inhibitory activities that contribute to its medicinal potential. The antioxidant properties help neutralize harmful free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates the ability to inhibit nitric oxide production, which can help manage inflammatory responses. These combined pharmacological effects suggest that the species may be useful in treating conditions related to inflammation and cellular damage. Research into these specific mechanisms continues to explore its therapeutic applications.

As discussed in a study, the methanol extracts of Spermacoce articularis exhibited strong antioxidant activity in the FTC and TBA methods that was comparable to or higher than alpha-tocopherol, BHT, and quercetin. This plant also showed strong DPPH free radical scavenging activity comparable with quercetin, BHT, and Vit C. Furthermore, Spermacoce articularis demonstrated strong inhibitory activity on nitric oxide production in LPS and IFN-gamma-induced RAW 264.7 cells during the Griess assay.
