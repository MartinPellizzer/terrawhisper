Dioclea hexandra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioclea hexandra has climbing woody vines characterized by trifoliate leaves, small yellowish flowers with six stamens, and large, woody, oblong-ellipsoid pods. The leaves consist of three leaflets that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with smooth margins. Its inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils and bear petals that are often marked with distinct patterns to attract pollinators. The most prominent morphological feature is the fruit, which develops into a thick-walled legume containing several seeds. These seeds are frequently encased in a hard, durable structure suited for dispersal within its tropical forest habitat.
