Ohwia caudata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ohwia caudata?

Ohwia caudata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, falling under the genus Ohwia, with the specific species designation of caudata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ohwia caudata contains bioactive compounds including polyphenols, flavonoids, lignans, and various polysaccharides that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that the lignans found in the root can assist in regulating metabolic functions and potentially supporting liver health. Additionally, the high concentration of polysaccharides may help modulate immune responses within the body. These diverse medicinal components make the plant a subject of interest for managing oxidative stress and chronic inflammation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ohwia caudata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, metabolic disorders, and digestive issues such as gastritis, diabetes, and hyperlipidemia. Researchers have investigated its potential to manage chronic inflammatory diseases by modulating immune responses. It is also studied for its ability to regulate blood glucose levels and improve lipid profiles in patients with metabolic syndrome. Furthermore, its antioxidant properties may help mitigate oxidative stress associated with many degenerative ailments. Traditional medicine often utilizes its extracts to support gastrointestinal health and reduce systemic inflammation.
