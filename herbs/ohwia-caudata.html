Ohwia caudata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ohwia caudata has an upright, deciduous shrub habit characterized by slender, grayish-brown stems and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 3 to 10 centimeters in length. The foliage typically features serrated margins and a distinctively textured surface with prominent venation. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, inconspicuous, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually give way to small, dry, one-seeded fruits known as achenes. The overall structure of the plant allows it to form dense thickets in its natural woodland habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, Korea, and the Russian Far East. It thrives primarily in mountainous areas and forested hillsides where moisture is consistent. The species is frequently found in well-drained soils within deciduous or mixed forest ecosystems. Its range extends from the southern reaches of the Korean Peninsula up into the colder northern territories of Siberia. These specific environmental conditions allow the plant to establish stable populations within its natural habitat.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Ohwia caudata targets several key proteins including peroxisome proliferator-activated receptors (PPARs), adenosine receptors, and various inflammatory cytokines such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). The plant's bioactive compounds, particularly its polyphenols and lignans, act as ligands that modulate the activity of these specific signaling pathways. By interacting with PPAR-gamma, the extracts help regulate glucose metabolism and lipid profiles in the body. Furthermore, the inhibition of cyclooxygenase (COX) and lipoxygenase (LOX) enzymes contributes to its potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. These multi-target molecular interactions collectively explain its diverse pharmacological effects on metabolic and immune health.

Ohwia caudata has diverse therapeutic actions including its roles as an adaptogen, tonic, antioxidant, diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective agent.
