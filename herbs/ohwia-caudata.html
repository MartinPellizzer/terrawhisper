Ohwia caudata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ohwia caudata?

Ohwia caudata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, falling under the genus Ohwia, with the specific species designation of caudata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ohwia caudata has an upright, deciduous shrub habit characterized by slender, grayish-brown stems and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 3 to 10 centimeters in length. The foliage typically features serrated margins and a distinctively textured surface with prominent venation. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, inconspicuous, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually give way to small, dry, one-seeded fruits known as achenes. The overall structure of the plant allows it to form dense thickets in its natural woodland habitat.

Its range extends from the southern reaches of the Korean Peninsula up into the colder northern territories of Siberia. What are the pharmacological activities of Ohwia caudata?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities, most notably potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties derived from its high concentration of bioactive polyphenols. The antioxidant capacity of Ohwia caudata helps neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within cellular structures. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory effects function by inhibiting specific inflammatory mediators that contribute to chronic swelling and pain. These combined biological actions make the plant a subject of interest for managing metabolic and inflammatory conditions. Ongoing research continues to explore how these chemical constituents interact with various physiological pathways.

According to a study published by "Life (Basel, Switzerland)", Ohwia caudata possesses anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The hydroethanolic extract of Ohwia caudata leaves contains key phytochemical constituents including harmine, swertisin, isoliquiritigenin, eupatilin, 3',4'-dimethoxyflavone, and neroolidol. This extract significantly reduces transepidermal water loss and enhances hydration to improve skin barrier integrity. It works by downregulating IL-6 and TNF-α while restoring TGF-β expression. Furthermore, the extract suppresses ERK and downstream eIF2α/ATF-4/CHOP activation while enhancing SIRT1/Nrf2/HO-1 antioxidant signaling.
