Arabidopsis gemmifera

Arabidopsis gemmifera, commonly known as Japanese cress, is a type of flowering plant that belongs to the Brassicaceae family.

It is used for its medicinal properties and is known to have several health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, which can help to reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as cancer and cardiovascular disease.

The therapeutic actions of Arabidopsis gemmifera include its ability to stimulate digestion and relieve gastrointestinal issues, as well as its potential to lower blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include isothiocyanates, flavonoids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and have been shown to have anti-cancer and anti-inflammatory effects.
