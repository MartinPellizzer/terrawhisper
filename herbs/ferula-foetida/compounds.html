Ferula Weed (Ferula foetida) Compounds

The primary compounds of Ferula foetida include essential oil, coumarin, terpenoids, phenolic acids, flavonoids, tannins, glycosides, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins. These bioactive components contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Ferula Weed (Ferula foetida) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Ferula foetida contains volatile compounds like limonene and pinene, which contribute to its aromatic properties. These components are extracted through distillation and are used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. The oil is a key component in the therapeutic applications of Ferula foetida.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Ferula foetida, known for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and respiratory conditions. Coumarin also exhibits mild sedative effects, supporting its role in herbal medicine for calming and healing purposes.

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Ferula foetida. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's strong aroma and are used in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory support.
