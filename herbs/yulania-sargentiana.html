What's the taxonomical classification of Yulania sargentiana?

Yuylania sargentiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Magnoliales and belongs to the family Magnoliaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Yulania, and its species designation, sargentiana.

This traditional method allows the medicinal properties of the plant to be concentrated into a potent liquid form for consumption. In some regional practices, the plant material may also be processed into a powder that is mixed into warm water or tea. Some practitioners utilize the plant in the form of an external wash or soak for topical applications. These various methods ensure that the botanical constituents are effectively delivered to the body.

While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in similar families can trigger contact dermatitis upon contact with the skin. It is also possible that the chemical constituents within the plant could induce an immune system response in people with existing botanical allergies. Due to these unknown risks, careful handling and professional medical consultation are necessary before any use.
