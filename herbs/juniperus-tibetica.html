Juniperus tibetica

What's the taxonomical classification of Juniperus tibetica?

Juniperus tibetica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae, which falls under the order Pinales. Within this order, it is a member of the family Cupressaceae. Finally, it is placed in the genus Juniperus, with its specific designation being the species tibetica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juniperus tibetica has scale-like, spreading leaves that are densely packed on its branches and reddish-brown, berry-like cones that measure approximately 6 to 10 millimeters in diameter. The tree typically exhibits a coniferous growth habit with a trunk that can reach several meters in height. Its foliage is characterized by small, overlapping scales that provide a textured appearance to the canopy. The woody stems often show a somewhat irregular branching pattern adapted to high-altitude environments. These small, fleshy cones turn bluish or purplish upon ripening to facilitate seed dispersal.
