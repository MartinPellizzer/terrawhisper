Zanthoxylum simulans

What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum simulans?

Zanthoxylum simulans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, the plant is placed in the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae. Finally, the specific taxonomic identity of the organism is established through its genus, Zanthoxylum, and its unique species designation, simulans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zanthoxylum simulans has a woody, deciduous habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves, small greenish flowers, and small drupaceous fruits. The leaves typically feature several pairs of leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape with serrated margins. Its bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured depending on the maturity of the specimen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous blossoms that eventually develop into fleshy fruits containing a single seed. These morphological traits help distinguish it from other species within the Rutaceae family.

What are the pharmacological activities of Zanthoxylum simulans?

This plant has demonstrated potent pharmacological activities including anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, anti-proliferative, antimicrobial, and cytotoxic properties. Its chemical constituents exhibit significant cytotoxic activity that allows it to target and destroy various malignant cell lines. These anti-proliferative effects work by inhibiting the rapid division and growth of cancerous cells within a biological system. Additionally, the plant provides antimicrobial defense by suppressing the growth of harmful pathogens. The presence of anti-inflammatory compounds further assists in reducing physiological swelling and immune responses.

According to a study published by "BMC complementary and alternative medicine", Zanthoxylum simulans contains 17 unique peaks and 13 common peaks found across all three species. The alkaloids of benzophenanthridine type within Z. simulans have a relative content of 30.52%. The crude alkaloid extracts demonstrated inhibitory activities against four carcinoma cell lines, including stomach tumor cells (SGC-7901), cervical tumor cells (Hela), colon tumor cells (HT-29), and Hepatic tumor cells (Hep G2). These inhibitory rates ranged from 60.71 to 93.63% at a concentration of 200 μg/mL. The potential anti-cancer activity in the species is attributed to quaternary alkaloids.
