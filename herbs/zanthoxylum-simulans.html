Zanthoxylum simulans

What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum simulans?

Zanthoxylum simulans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, the plant is placed in the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae. Finally, the specific taxonomic identity of the organism is established through its genus, Zanthoxylum, and its unique species designation, simulans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zanthoxylum simulans has a woody, deciduous habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves, small greenish flowers, and small drupaceous fruits. The leaves typically feature several pairs of leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape with serrated margins. Its bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured depending on the maturity of the specimen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous blossoms that eventually develop into fleshy fruits containing a single seed. These morphological traits help distinguish it from other species within the Rutaceae family.

As discussed in a study, Zanthoxylum simulans contains 17 unique peaks within its alkaloid profile. The alkaloids of benzophenanthridine type are commonly present in this species with a relative content of 30.52%. In inhibitory activity tests against four carcinoma cell lines, which include SGC-7901, Hela, HT-29, and Hep G2, the crude alkaloid extracts showed remarkable results. These potential anti-cancer activities are attributed to the quaternary alkaloids found in the species.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

The presence of these specific alkaloids suggests significant biochemical activity within the plant's chemical makeup. Researchers often study these particular benzophenanthridine derivatives to understand their potential therapeutic applications. Such chemical complexity defines the medicinal value attributed to this species.

As discussed in a study, two new benzo[c]phenanthridine alkaloids, 6-methyldihydrochelerythrine [1] and 6-methylnorchelerythrine [2], were isolated from the root bark of Zanthoxylum simulans. Along with these 2 new alkaloids, 23 known compounds were also isolated from the plant. Among the isolated substances, the pyranoquinoline alkaloids zanthosimuline [3] and huajiaosimuline [4] exhibited cytotoxic activity. Additionally, compound 4 demonstrated significant antiplatelet aggregation activity. Compound 4 also induced terminal differentiation when used with cultured HL-60 cells.
