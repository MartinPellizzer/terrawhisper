Symplocos glauca

What's the taxonomical classification of Symplocos glauca?

Symplocos glauca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is a member of the order Ericales and the family Symplocaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Symplocos and is identified by the specific epithet glauca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Symplocos glauca has simple, entire, elliptic to oblong leaves that are characterized by a distinctively glaucous, bluish-grey underside and a dark green upper surface.

The leaf margins are smooth and lack serration, while the texture often feels leathery to the touch. These leaves are arranged alternately along the woody stems of this evergreen shrub or small tree. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically appear in axillary clusters, eventually developing into small, fleshy drupes.

The overall growth habit reflects a dense, bushy structure supported by grayish bark.
