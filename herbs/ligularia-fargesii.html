What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia fargesii?

Ligularia fargesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asterales, it falls under the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Ligularia, and its unique species designation, fargesii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia fargesii has large, broadly ovate to cordate leaves that can reach up to 40 centimeters in length and feature deeply serrated or dentate margins. These massive, leathery leaves are often characterized by prominent venation and a textured surface. The plant produces tall, upright flowering stalks that emerge from a basal rosette of foliage. These inflorescences consist of dense, terminal clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like capitula. Each individual flower head contains numerous disc florets surrounded by ray florets, creating a striking visual display.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude mountain regions of central and western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments found along stream banks and in alpine meadows. The species is often located at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 3,500 meters above sea level. These specific montane habitats provide the cool, humid conditions necessary for its survival.
