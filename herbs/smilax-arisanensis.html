Smilax arisanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Smilax arisanensis?

Smilax arisanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Smilacaceae, which contains the genus Smilax, and is specifically identified by the species epithet arisanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax arisanensis has climbing stems equipped with sharp, recurved prickles and leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate with entire or slightly wavy margins. The foliage is characterized by a glossy texture and prominent pinnate venation that extends toward the leaf edges. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils, often displaying a yellowish or greenish hue. Following flowering, the plant produces small fleshy berries that serve as its primary reproductive structures. These morphological traits allow the vine to scramble effectively through dense forest undergrowth.
