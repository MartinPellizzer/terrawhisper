Lemon Grass

Cymbopogon martini

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Lemon Grass, scientifically known as Cymbopogon martini, belongs to the Poaceae family. It is also called Cymbopogon citratus in some regions. A perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia, it has aromatic leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. Its common names include lemon grass and citron grass, with synonyms often used in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Lemon grass contains essential oil, flavonoids, and terpenoids as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide its therapeutic effects, such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The essential oil, particularly citral, is the main contributor to its medicinal benefits.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Terpenoid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Asthma, bad breath, cough, inflamed throat, bronchitis Bronchodilator, deodorant, expectorant, anti-inflammatory
Integumentary Skin infections, fungal infections, tinea Antimicrobial, antifungal, antiseptic
Immune Inflammation, malaria, allergic reactions, allergies, fever Anti-inflammatory, antimalarial, antihistaminic

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and digestive problems.

Essential oil: Used for aromatherapy and to treat headaches and stress.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to relieve nausea.

Safety Profile

Lemon grass is generally safe when used in typical medicinal doses. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks to the fetus or infant. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
