What to know about Cymbopogon martini (lemongrass) before using it medicinally

Lemongrass, scientifically known as Cymbopogon martini, is a versatile and aromatic herb that has been prized for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to alleviate digestive issues, reduce fever and inflammation, and even exhibit antimicrobial properties.

In addition to its medicinal uses, lemongrass is also a popular ingredient in many cuisines around the world, particularly in Thai and Indian cooking, where it adds a bright, citrusy flavor to dishes like curries, soups, and marinades. From a horticultural perspective, lemongrass is an easy-to-grow perennial that thrives in warm climates and can be harvested year-round. Botanically speaking, lemongrass belongs to the grass family and gets its distinctive lemon scent from the oils present in its leaves.

With its rich history dating back thousands of years to ancient India and China, where it was used in traditional medicine and as a culinary staple, lemongrass remains a beloved and highly valued herb around the world today.
