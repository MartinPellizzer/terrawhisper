Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini)

Lemon Grass (Cymbopogon martini) is a member of the Poaceae family, native to India, Sri Lanka, and Southeast Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and oleoresin have been used for infusions, decoctions, and oil infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, carminative, and stimulant actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Culmous, herbaceous, cylindrical, solid, hollow, fibrous, aromatic, glabrous, terete, erect, unbranched, solid, with longitudinal ridges, leaf sheaths fused to nodes, nodes prominent, internodes distinct, vascular bundles arranged in a ring.
