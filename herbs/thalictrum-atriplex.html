What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum atriplex?

Thalictrum atriplex belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its hierarchical descent, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is a member of the order Ranunculales and falls under the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Thalictrum and the species atriplex.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum atriplex has erect stems that reach heights of up to 1 meter, bearing compound leaves composed of several leaflets with ovate or oblong shapes and serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a delicate, somewhat glaucous texture and a distinctively divided structure. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in loose clusters, featuring five to eight petals and numerous prominent stamens. These floral structures are typically yellowish or greenish in color, blending into the surrounding greenery. The plant eventually produces small, dry achenes that facilitate seed dispersal.

What are the pharmacological activities of Thalictrum atriplex?

This plant has cytotoxic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities that stem from its diverse secondary metabolites. Research indicates that its extracts can induce cell death in various cancer cell lines, demonstrating significant cytotoxic potential. Beyond its ability to target malignant cells, the plant exhibits properties that help reduce inflammation in biological systems. It also shows effectiveness in inhibiting the growth of certain pathogenic microorganisms. These combined biological effects highlight the therapeutic versatility of Thalictrum atriplex in medicinal studies.

As discussed in a study, Thalictrum atriplex contains two new cycloartane glycosides, cycloatriosides A and B (1-2), and a new oleanolic acid glycoside, thaliatrioside A (3). The plant also yields 7 known triterpenoids (4-10) that were isolated during the research. Cytotoxic activities of these compounds were evaluated against human lung cancer cells A549 and human breast cancer cells MDA-MB-231 using the MTT method. Specifically, compound 9 demonstrated cytotoxicity against the MDA-MB-231 cell line. This activity was measured with an IC50 value of 72.53 ± 1.08 μM.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds beta-sitosterol, kaempferol, caffeic acid, p-coumaric acid, and protocatechuic acid. These specific phytochemicals are known for contributing to various therapeutic properties within the organism. For instance, beta-sitosterol and kaempferol often provide significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits. The phenolic acids, including caffeic acid, p-coumaric acid, and protocatechuic acid, further support cellular health through their scavenging of free radicals. Together, these bioactive molecules form a complex chemical profile that characterizes the plant's medicinal potential.

As discussed in a study, chromatography and spectral analysis were used to isolate and elucidate the chemical constituents in the aerial part of Thalictrum atriplex. Six compounds were isolated from the aerial part of Thalictrum atriplex. These constituents were elucidated as protocatechuic acid, caffeic acid, p-coumaric acid, kaempferol, beta-sitosterol and N-methylcorydaldine. All 6 of these compounds were isolated from the plant for the first time.
