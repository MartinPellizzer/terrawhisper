Symplocos wikstroemiifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Symplocos wikstroemiifolia has elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins, an acute apex, and a distinctively coriaceous texture. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the woody stems. Its surface often exhibits a lustrous appearance on the upper side, while the underside may appear slightly paler. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves to facilitate reproduction. The plant eventually develops small drupes that serve as its characteristic fruit.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Symplocos wikstroemiifolia can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested in large quantities due to its secondary metabolites. While some traditional practices use the plant, the lack of rigorous clinical human trials means that its exact safety profile remains uncertain. Contact with the plant material may trigger allergic reactions or contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If consumed, users might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Careful attention must be paid to dosage and preparation to avoid unintended adverse reactions.
