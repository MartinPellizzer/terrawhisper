Briggsia kurzii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Briggsia kurzii has an herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems, ovate-lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and tubular, bilabiate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The foliage is arranged oppositely along the stem and features a soft, pubescent texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of several flowers emerging from the leaf axils, often displaying a distinct pale throat or spotting within the corolla. The floral structure is adapted for specific pollinators, featuring a prominent lower lip that provides a landing platform. This morphology is consistent with other members of the Gesneriaceae family, reflecting specialized adaptations to shaded forest understories.
