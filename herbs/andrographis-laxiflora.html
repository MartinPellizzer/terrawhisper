Andrographis laxiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Andrographis laxiflora?

Andrographis laxiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Acanthaceae, falling within the genus Andrographis, and is specifically identified by the species name laxiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Andrographis laxiflora has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, lanceolate leaves with acute apices. The foliage is characterized by a smooth, glabrous texture and a bright green coloration. Small, white to pale lilac flowers emerge from the axils in loose, lax panicles, which distinguishes this species from its more densely flowered relatives. The corolla is bilabiate in structure, often displaying subtle markings on the lower lip to assist in pollination. Each plant produces small, oblong capsules that contain numerous tiny, brown seeds upon maturation.
