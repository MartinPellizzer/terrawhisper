Pulmonaria officinalis subsp. affinis

Pulmonaria officinalis subsp.

affinis, also known as lungwort, is a perennial plant species within the Pulmonaria genus, characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and spotted leaves.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its expectorant properties, helping to relieve respiratory issues such as bronchitis, coughs, and colds, by promoting the secretion of mucus and facilitating its expulsion from the lungs.

The health benefits of Pulmonaria officinalis subsp.

affinis include its ability to soothe sore throats, reduce inflammation, and support the immune system.

Herbal preparations of the plant can be made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, which are commonly used in herbal remedies to address respiratory issues and promote overall well-being.
