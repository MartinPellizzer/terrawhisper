Valerian (Valeriana Officinalis Var. Major)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Valeriana officinalis var. major.

Valerian, scientifically known as Valeriana officinalis var. major, is a medicinal herb renowned for its calming properties and use as an adaptogen. It is primarily valued for its ability to promote relaxation, improve sleep quality, and reduce anxiety, making it a popular remedy for insomnia and stress-related disorders. Traditionally used in European herbal medicine for centuries, Valerian has been employed to treat nervous disorders, headaches, and digestive issues, often prepared as a tincture or tea. In modern wellness practices, it is increasingly incorporated into natural sleep aids, stress relief supplements, and aromatherapy blends. One of its unique features is its distinctive, earthy, and slightly sweet aroma, which comes from the compound valerenic acid, and it has a long history dating back to ancient Roman times.
