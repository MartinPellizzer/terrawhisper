What's the taxonomical classification of Orostachys fimbriata?

Orostachys fimbriata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the taxonomic order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Crassulaceae. At the most specific levels of its biological classification, it is identified by the genus Orostachys and the species name fimbriata.

      What are the herbal preparations of Orostachys fimbriata?

      Orostachys fimbriata is prepared primarily as a decoction or a medicinal infusion involving the processed rhizomes and roots of the plant. Practitioners often boil the dried plant material in water to extract its active chemical compounds for internal consumption. Some traditional applications also include using the plant in topical poultices where the crushed parts are applied directly to the skin.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Orostachys fimbriata can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and mild gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or fine hairs often triggers redness and itching on sensitive areas of the skin. Some individuals may experience more severe swelling or hives as an immune response to its chemical compounds. If consumed accidentally, the plant might lead to nausea or stomach cramps in susceptible people. Monitoring for these specific reactions is essential when handling or being near the species.
