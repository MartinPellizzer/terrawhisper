Thesium longifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Thesium longifolium?

Thesium longifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is organized under the order Santalales and is a member of the family Santalaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Thesium and is specifically designated as the species longifolium.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin diseases, gastrointestinal issues, and inflammatory conditions. It is frequently applied in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of eczema and other topical irritations. Some practitioners utilize its properties to soothe stomach aches and manage digestive discomfort. The plant also possesses qualities that help reduce swelling and general bodily inflammation. These medicinal applications are primarily rooted in regional folk healing practices.
