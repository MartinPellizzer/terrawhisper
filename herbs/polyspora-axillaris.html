What's the taxonomical classification of Polyspora axillaris?

Polyspora axillaris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Theaceae, which places it within the genus Polyspora, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species axillaris.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polyspora axillaris has a slender, creeping rhizome that produces upright, unbranched stems reaching heights of approximately 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape and arranged in a single whorl near the apex of each stem. These foliage elements are typically bright green and possess a smooth, entire margin. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves rather than at the stem tips.
