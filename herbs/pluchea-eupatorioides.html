Pluchea eupatorioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Pluchea eupatorioides?

Pluchea eupatorioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its lineage through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is part of the genus Pluchea, and is specifically identified by its species name, eupatorioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pluchea eupatorioides has opposite, simple, lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically entire or slightly serrated with a somewhat pubescent surface. The plant reaches a height of roughly 0.5 to 1.5 meters and features woody stems that are often branched. Small, pink to purplish flower heads are arranged in terminal, corymb-like clusters. Each individual head is composed of numerous tubular disc florets that lack ray florets. The plant also produces small, dry achenes that are often topped by a pappus for wind dispersal.
