Pluchea eupatorioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pluchea eupatorioides has opposite, simple, lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically entire or slightly serrated with a somewhat pubescent surface. The plant reaches a height of roughly 0.5 to 1.5 meters and features woody stems that are often branched. Small, pink to purplish flower heads are arranged in terminal, corymb-like clusters. Each individual head is composed of numerous tubular disc florets that lack ray florets. The plant also produces small, dry achenes that are often topped by a pappus for wind dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the southeastern and central United States, extending from the Gulf Coast states up through the Appalachian Mountains and into the Midwest. It is commonly found in moist habitats such as marshes, ditches, riverbanks, and wet prairies. Its range often follows low-lying areas where soil remains consistently damp or saturated. While primarily centered in the American South and East, some populations may also be found in parts of Mexico. The distribution is largely dictated by the availability of wetlands and disturbed moist soils.
