Viola mandshurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Viola mandshurica?

Viola mandshurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its systematic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Violaceae, eventually reaching its specific classification within the genus Viola. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species designation, mandshurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola mandshurica has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a creeping rhizome and basal leaves that are typically cordate or reniform with crenate margins. The leaves are petiolate and often exhibit a dark green color with slightly wavy edges. Its flowering stems are relatively short and bear zygomorphic flowers that are usually violet or pale purple in hue. Each flower consists of five petals, with the lower petal often featuring distinct nectar guides or pale markings. The fruit is a small, three-valved capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds for dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically distributed across eastern China, Korea, Japan, and the Russian Far East. It thrives primarily in temperate regions where seasonal shifts provide necessary moisture. Within these territories, it is often found in forest edges, grasslands, and along riverbanks. Its range extends from the subtropical zones of southern China up into the more northern latitudes of the Sakhalin islands. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds hesperetin, luteolin, resveratrol, tectoriidin, and cyclotides. These diverse chemical constituents contribute to a wide range of potential therapeutic applications in human health. Hesperetin and luteolin are known flavonoids that often provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. The presence of resveratrol and tectoriidin further enhances the plant's ability to combat oxidative stress at a cellular level. Additionally, the unique cyclic peptides known as cyclotides offer specialized biological activities that distinguish this species from others.

As discussed in a study, the chloroform extract of fermented Viola mandshurica contains main phenolic compounds identified as tectoriidin, luteolin, resveratrol, and hesperetin. This extract, referred to as CEFV, significantly inhibited NO production and reduced the expression of iNOS at both protein and mRNA levels. The CEFV also decreased PGE2 production and suppressed COX-2 expression. Furthermore, it inhibited the activation of the ERK and JNK pathways but not the p38 pathway. These actions allowed the CEFV to suppress NF-κB activation in RAW264.7 cells.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating airway inflammation, allergic asthma, bronchial asthma, and various forms of general inflammation. Its medicinal properties are particularly effective at reducing swelling within the respiratory tract. By addressing inflammation, it helps ease the breathing difficulties associated with bronchial asthma. It also provides relief for patients suffering from hypersensitivity reactions known as allergic asthma. These therapeutic actions make it a valuable component in traditional remedies for respiratory distress.

As discussed in a study, Viola mandshurica ethanolic extract was investigated for its efficacy in a BALB/c mouse model where mice were sensitized on days 0 and 14 and given intranasal OVA on days 28-30. The VM EtOH extract was administered orally from days 28 to 30 to treat the asthma model. This extract significantly inhibited increases in total immunoglobulin E (IgE) and cytokines IL-4 and IL-13 levels in both serum and bronchoalveolar lavage fluid (BALF). Furthermore, VM EtOH extract effectively suppressed airway hyperresponsiveness (AHR), eosinophilia, and mucus hypersecretion. These results suggest that Viola mandshurica may be a valuable lead material for anti-asthma drug development.
