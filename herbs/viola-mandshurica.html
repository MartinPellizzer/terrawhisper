Viola mandshurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Viola mandshurica?

Viola mandshurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its systematic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Violaceae, eventually reaching its specific classification within the genus Viola. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species designation, mandshurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola mandshurica has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a creeping rhizome and basal leaves that are typically cordate or reniform with crenate margins. The leaves are petiolate and often exhibit a dark green color with slightly wavy edges. Its flowering stems are relatively short and bear zygomorphic flowers that are usually violet or pale purple in hue. Each flower consists of five petals, with the lower petal often featuring distinct nectar guides or pale markings. The fruit is a small, three-valved capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds for dispersal.

What are the pharmacological activities of Viola mandshurica?

This plant has potent pharmacological properties including significant anti-inflammatory and anti-asthmatic activities. Researchers have identified various bioactive compounds within the species that help suppress excessive immune responses in the body. These constituents work to reduce swelling and pain by inhibiting specific inflammatory pathways. Furthermore, the plant's ability to relax bronchial muscles makes it a valuable subject for studying asthma treatments. Ongoing studies continue to explore how these medicinal components can be utilized in therapeutic applications.

As discussed in a study, Viola mandshurica ethanolic extract demonstrates efficacy in treating bronchial asthma within a model using female BALB/c mice. The treatment process involved sensitization on days 0 and 14, followed by intranasal administration on days 28-30. Once receiving the extract orally from days 28 to 30, the mice showed significant inhibition of increased total immunglobulin E (IgE) and cytokines IL-4 and IL-13 levels in both serum and bronchoalveolar lavage fluid (BALF). Furthermore, the extract effectively suppressed airway hyperresponsiveness (AHR), eosinophilia, and mucus hypersecretion. These findings suggest that Viola mandshurica could serve as a useful herbal medicine or a valuable lead material for anti-asthma drug development.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds hesperetin, luteolin, resveratrol, tectoriidin, and cyclotides. These diverse phytochemicals contribute to various therapeutic activities including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. Hesperetin and luteolin function as potent flavonoids that support vascular health and reduce oxidative stress. Resveratrol and tectoriidin are known for their ability to modulate metabolic processes and protect cells from damage. Additionally, the presence of cyclotides provides unique structural properties that are highly valued in pharmacological research for their bioactive potential.

As discussed in a study, the chloroform extract of fermented Viola mandshurica contains the main phenolic compounds tectoriidin, luteolin, resveratro, and hesperetin. This extract, known as CEFV, significantly inhibited NO production and reduced the expression of iNOS at both protein and mRNA levels in a dose-dependent manner. Furthermore, CEFV decreased PGE2 production and suppressed COX-2 expression in RAW 264.7 macrophages. The substance also inhibited the activation of the ERK and JNK pathways, though it did not affect the p38 pathway. Ultimately, CEFV suppressed NF-κB activation to exhibit potent anti-inflammatory effects.
