Pyracantha koidzumii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyracantha koidzumii has woody, evergreen stems equipped with sharp, single thorns and small, leathery, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length. The foliage is typically dark green with a smooth texture and slightly serrated margins. During the flowering season, small white blossoms emerge in dense clusters from the axils of the leaves. These flowers eventually give way to numerous small, globose berries that transition from green to a vibrant red or orange color. The plant maintains a dense, spreading growth habit, forming thickets through its branched architecture.
