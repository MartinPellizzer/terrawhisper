Pyracantha koidzumii

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyracantha koidzumii?

Pyracantha koidzumii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of biological classification, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Pyracantha, and its unique species designation, koidzumii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyracantha koidzumii has woody, evergreen stems equipped with sharp, single thorns and small, leathery, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length.

The foliage is typically dark green with a smooth texture and slightly serrated margins. During the flowering season, small white blossoms emerge in dense clusters from the axils of the leaves. These flowers eventually give way to numerous small, globose berries that transition from green to a vibrant red or orange color.

The plant maintains a dense, spreading growth habit, forming thickets through its branched architecture.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Pyracantha koidzumii targets inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS) to exert its potent anti-inflammatory effects. The phytochemicals found in this plant, particularly various flavonoids and triterpenoids, modulate the signaling pathways associated with these proteins. By inhibiting the expression of COX-2, the plant reduces the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins that contribute to pain and swelling. Furthermore, its interaction with the NF-kappaB signaling pathway prevents the transcriptional activation of cytokines that drive chronic inflammation. These molecular interactions collectively support the plant's traditional use in managing systemic inflammation and oxidative stress.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pyracantha koidzumii can cause physical injury through its sharp, woody thorns and may trigger skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. The spines are often long and rigid, posing a significant risk for puncture wounds or scratches to the skin during pruning or contact. Once a puncture occurs, there is a potential for localized swelling, redness, or even infection if the area is not cleaned properly. Some people may also experience contact dermatitis after touching the foliage or berries. While the berries are generally not considered highly toxic to humans, accidental ingestion could lead to mild gastrointestinal upset.
