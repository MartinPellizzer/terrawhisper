Bloodtwig Dogwood (Cornus sanguinea) Uses

Bloodtwig Dogwood is used to treat arthritis, gout, and joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It also helps with respiratory infections, cold symptoms, tuberculosis, cough, bronchitis, and asthma by supporting immune function and easing breathing.

Bloodtwig Dogwood (Cornus sanguinea) Monograph

1. Arthritis

Bloodtwig Dogwood is used externally for arthritis due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. A poultice made from the bark and leaves can be applied to reduce joint swelling and pain. It is particularly effective for rheumatic conditions and is often combined with other herbs in traditional herbal medicine.

2. Gout

Bloodtwig Dogwood is used in herbal medicine to support the treatment of gout due to its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties. It helps reduce uric acid levels and alleviate joint pain associated with gout. The plant's resins and roots are typically prepared as tinctures or poultices for topical and internal use.

3. Joint pain

Bloodtwig Dogwood is used to alleviate joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The bark contains compounds that reduce swelling and ease discomfort in conditions like arthritis. It is typically prepared as a decoction or tincture for internal use. External applications may also help with topical inflammation and stiffness.

4. Rheumatism

Bloodtwig Dogwood is traditionally used to alleviate rheumatic pain and joint stiffness. Its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce swelling and discomfort associated with rheumatism. It is often prepared as a tincture or decoction to support mobility and ease arthritis symptoms.

5. Respiratory infections

Bloodtwig Dogwood is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and easing congestion. It is particularly effective for conditions like bronchitis and coughs. Its antimicrobial properties help fight infections, while its expectorant action aids in clearing mucus from the airways.

6. Cold symptoms

Bloodtwig Dogwood is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. It helps reduce fever, soothe sore throats, and ease congestion. Traditionally prepared as a tea, it supports the body's natural defenses during respiratory infections. Its mild action makes it suitable for long-term use in managing seasonal colds.

7. Tuberculosis

Bloodtwig Dogwood has been historically used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health, including in the treatment of tuberculosis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties may help clear mucus and soothe lung tissue, though it is not a substitute for modern medical treatments. Always consult a healthcare professional for proper care.

8. Cough

Bloodtwig Dogwood is used to soothe coughs, particularly those accompanied by inflammation or excessive mucus. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce irritation in the respiratory tract and promote easier breathing. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for its calming and cleansing effects on the lungs.

9. Bronchitis

Bloodtwig Dogwood is used to support respiratory health in bronchitis by reducing inflammation and soothing irritated airways. Its anti-inflammatory properties help ease coughing and mucus production, while its expectorant effects aid in clearing the lungs. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate symptoms during acute or chronic bronchial inflammation.

10. Asthma

Bloodtwig Dogwood (Cornus sanguinea) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing bronchial swelling and aiding mucus clearance. However, it should be used under professional guidance due to potential interactions and varying efficacy.
