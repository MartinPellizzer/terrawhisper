Botanical Identification of Bloodtwig Dogwood (Cornus sanguinea)

To identify Bloodtwig Dogwood (Cornus sanguinea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Bloodtwig Dogwood, or Cornus sanguinea, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Cornaceae, genus Cornus, and species Cornus sanguinea. These classifications place it within the broader group of flowering plants and the Cornaceae family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Bloodtwig Dogwood is Cornus sanguinea, first described by the Swedish botanist Carl Peter Thunberg in 1794. It is also known by several synonyms, including Cornus sanguinea var. sanguinea and Cornus sanguinea subsp. sanguinea. Common names include Bloodtwig Dogwood, Red Dogwood, and White Dogwood, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Bloodtwig Dogwood is a deciduous shrub with a compact growth habit, typically reaching 1-3 meters in height. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, providing stability in moist, well-drained soils. The stems are smooth, reddish-brown, and often have distinctive blood-red twigs that give the plant its common name.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Bloodtwig Dogwood can be distinguished by its distinctive red stems with prominent white pith, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small white flowers. It is often confused with Cornus s实, but lacks the green stems and has a more upright growth habit. The bark peels in thin, papery layers, and the fruit is a small, dark red drupe.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Bloodtwig Dogwood is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and can grow at altitudes up to 1,500 meters. This plant is commonly found in woodlands and stream banks.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Bloodtwig Dogwood blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to September. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during late spring to early summer when the plant is in full bloom and the distinctive red twigs are visible.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Bloodtwig Dogwood (Cornus sanguinea) belongs to the genus Cornus, closely related to species like Cornus florida and Cornus sanguiflora. It shares family traits of the Cornaceae, including opposite leaves and drupes. Its phylogenetic position reflects its close ties within the genus and broader family characteristics.
