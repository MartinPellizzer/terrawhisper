What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium pylzowii has upright, herbaceous stems that support deeply lobed, palmate leaves with serrated margins and a characteristic bluish-green hue. Each flower typically exhibits a prominent nectar spur extending from the rear of the corolla. Small, glandular hairs often cover the stems and calyx, providing a fine texture to the plant's exterior.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Delphinium pylzowii can cause severe poisoning characterized by gastrointestinal distress, neurological dysfunction, and potential cardiac arrest due to its high concentration of toxic alkaloids. Ingesting even small amounts of the plant may lead to intense nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. As toxins enter the bloodstream, symptoms often progress to muscle tremors, dizziness, and profound weakness. In more critical cases, individuals may experience respiratory failure or irregular heart rhythms that can be fatal. Rapid medical intervention is necessary to manage these life-threatening systemic reactions.
