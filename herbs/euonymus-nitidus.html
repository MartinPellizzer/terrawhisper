Euonymus nitidus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euonymus nitidus has glossy, ovate to elliptic leaves that are oppositely arranged on woody, spreading branches. The foliage typically measures between 2 and 6 centimeters in length and possesses entire margins with a smooth, waxy texture. Its flowers are small, inconspicuous, and usually appear in clusters toward the ends of the stems. Following flowering, the plant produces distinctive capsule-like fruits that eventually split to reveal bright orange or red seeds. This evergreen shrub maintains a dense, bushy habit that can reach several meters in height depending on its environment.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Euonymus nitidus can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if the berries or leaves are ingested by humans or animals. Contact with the plant's sap often results in contact dermatitis, manifesting as redness, itching, or a burning sensation on the skin. If consumed, the plant may lead to nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea due to its chemical composition. Pets that chew on the foliage are particularly susceptible to these adverse effects and should be monitored closely for signs of illness. Avoiding direct contact and preventing ingestion are the primary methods for mitigating these health risks.
