What to know about Ipomoea purga (jalap) before using it medicinally

Jalap, scientifically known as Ipomoea purga, is a herb that has gained recognition for its impressive health properties, which include reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and even exhibiting potential anti-cancerous effects.

In the culinary world, jalap is often used to add a burst of flavor and heat to dishes, particularly in Mexican cuisine where it's commonly paired with chilies and spices. Horticulturally speaking, jalap is a tender perennial that thrives in warm climates and can be grown from seed or stem cuttings. From a botanical perspective, jalap belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

Historically, jalap has been referenced as far back as Aztec times, where it was used not only for its flavor but also for its medicinal properties.
