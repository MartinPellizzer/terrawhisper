Salvia roborowskii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvía roborowskii has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, square-shaped stems that reach heights of approximately 40 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, featuring serrated margins and a textured, rugose surface. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes that bear small, tubular flowers typically colored in shades of violet or light blue. These flowers emerge from prominent, colorful calyxes that provide additional visual interest to the plant. The overall structure is robust, allowing it to form tidy clumps within its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Tibet. It typically thrives in montane environments where rocky slopes and alpine meadows provide suitable habitats. The species is often found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level. These specific mountain ranges offer the cool, moist climates necessary for its survival. Consequently, its range is restricted to these distinct topographical niches within the eastern Himalayas and surrounding highlands.

      What are the herbal preparations of Salvia roborowskii?

      Salvia roborowskii is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture derived from the dried leaves and aerial parts of the plant.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Salvia roborowskii can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, digestive upset, or allergic responses if ingested or applied topically. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many members of the Salvia genus contain volatile oils that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingesting concentrated extracts could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress or unintended neurological effects due to active chemical compounds. It is also important to consider that individual sensitivities vary significantly when interacting with unknown plant alkaloids. Therefore, caution should be exercised when handling or consuming any part of this species.
