Scutellaria omeiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Scutellaria omeiensis?

Scutelariia omeiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Lamiaceae, where it is placed within the genus Scutellaria, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species Scutellaria omeiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutellaria omeiensis has opposite, decussate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The stems are quadrangular in cross-section, which is a characteristic trait of many plants within the Lamiaceae family. Small, tubular flowers are arranged in verticillasters along the upper portions of the flowering stalks. The calyx is bilabiate and persistent, while the corolla displays a distinct two-lipped structure. These morphological features collectively define the plant's structural identity within its high-altitude habitat.

Additionally, its compounds can interact with various mitogen-activated protein kinases (MAPKs) to regulate cellular responses to injury and infection. This multi-target mechanism explains the plant's broad pharmacological profile, including its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and neuroprotective properties.
