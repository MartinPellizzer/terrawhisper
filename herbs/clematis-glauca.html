Clematis glauca

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis glauca?

Clematis glauca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, which falls under the genus Clematis, and is specifically identified by its species name, glauca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis glauca has woody, climbing stems that produce opposite, trifoliate leaves characterized by a distinct glaucous, bluish-gray waxy coating on both surfaces.

The leaflets are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, bell-shaped, creamy-white to pale yellowish flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The inflorescences are often held on slender pedicels, leading to a delicate appearance against the muted tones of the foliage.

Following pollination, the plant develops feathery, persistent achenes that facilitate wind dispersal.

        What are the herbal preparations of Clematis glauca?

        Clematis glauca is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture derived from its roots and leaves. The roots are often simmered in water to create a potent decoction for internal use. Leaves may be steeped in hot water to produce a milder infusion for topical applications.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Clematis glauca can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurological issues if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that triggers contact dermatitis upon skin exposure. Symptoms of this reaction typically include redness, burning, and blistering. If the plant is swallowed, it can lead to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. In more extreme cases of ingestion, central nervous system effects such as convulsions may occur.
