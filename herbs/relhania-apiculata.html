What is Relhania apiculata?

Relhania apiculata is a shrubby perennial plant belonging to the family Asteraceae, native to the southwestern region of Western Australia.

Its leaves are elliptical to lanceolate, 5-15 cm long, and 1-3 cm wide, with a pointed apex and a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a dense corymb of yellow daisy-like flowers, 2-3 cm in diameter, with 10-12 yellow ray florets and a yellow disc. The plant grows up to 1-2 meters in height and produces brown, woody fruits, 5-7 mm in diameter, which contain 10-15 seeds.

It is typically found in well-drained sandy soils in heathlands and woodlands.
