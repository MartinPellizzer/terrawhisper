Burmannia coelestis

What's the taxonomical classification of Burmannia coelestis?

Burmannia coelesteis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Dioscoreales. This plant is a member of the family Burmanniaceae, situated within the genus Burmannia, and is specifically identified by the species epithet coelestis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Burmannia coelestis has small, succulent, translucent green leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape and grow in a dense rosette pattern from a slender, underground rhizome.

The plant typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, often appearing as a tiny tuft amidst moss. Its flowers are diminutive and white, characterized by a tubular corolla with distinct lobes that emerge from the leaf axils. The reproductive structures are delicate, often requiring specific environmental moisture to support their development.
