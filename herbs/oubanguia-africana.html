Oubanguia africana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oubanguia africana has a woody shrub or small tree structure characterized by simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to lanceolate in shape with entire margins. The foliage often displays a coriaceous texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. Its flowers are generally small and arranged in axillary clusters or panicles. The fruit develops into a fleshy drupe that serves as a key identification feature for the species. The bark is relatively smooth and can change in texture as the plant matures.

The distribution is closely tied to high humidity levels and consistent annual rainfall characteristic of these biomes.       Oubanguia africana can cause skin irritation, digestive distress, and potential neurological disturbances if ingested or applied improperly.
