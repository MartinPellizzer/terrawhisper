Oubanguia africana has a woody shrub or small tree structure characterized by simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to lanceolate in shape with entire margins. The fruit develops into a fleshy drupe that serves as a key identification feature for the species. The bark is relatively smooth and can change in texture as the plant matures.
