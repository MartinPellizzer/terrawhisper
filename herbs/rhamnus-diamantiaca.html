Rhamnus diamantiaca

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhamnus diamantiaca has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape and possess finely serrated margins. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it feels somewhat leathery to the touch. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the branches during the blooming season. The plant structure often features woody stems that support a dense, shrubby growth habit. Its fruit develops into small drupes that change in color as they reach maturity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Diamantina Plateau in the state of Minas Gerais, Brazil. It is specifically restricted to the rocky outcrops and high-altitude environments characteristic of this unique landscape. The species thrives within the specialized vegetation zones found across this distinct geological formation. Its range is highly localized, meaning it cannot be found in any other geographical region globally. Because of this limited distribution, the plant is considered a narrow endemic species.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Rhamnus diamantiaca can cause gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, abdominal cramping, and diarrhea, due to the presence of anthraquinone glycosides in its tissues. These compounds act as potent stimulant laxatives that may lead to dehydration if consumed in large quantities. Frequent use or accidental ingestion can also result in electrolyte imbalances within the body. Furthermore, some individuals might experience localized skin irritation or allergic reactions upon direct contact with the plant material. Careful handling is necessary to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
