Common Hawthorn (Crataegus Extraria)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus extraria.

Common hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus extraria, is a small shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, widely recognized as a medicinal herb with adaptogenic properties. It has been traditionally used for its cardiovascular benefits, including supporting heart health, improving circulation, and reducing symptoms of mild heart failure. In traditional European and Chinese medicine, hawthorn has been employed to treat digestive issues, anxiety, and insomnia, often prepared as a tea or tincture. Modern wellness applications include its use in herbal formulations for stress relief, blood pressure regulation, and as a natural complement to cardiovascular support. One of its unique features is the presence of flavonoids and oligomeric procyanidins, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, making it a valuable plant in both historical and contemporary herbal practices.
