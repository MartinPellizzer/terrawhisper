What to know about Myrrhis odorata (sweet cicely) before using it medicinally

Sweet cicely, scientifically known as Myrrhis odorata, is an herb that boasts numerous health benefits, its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties having been shown to aid in reducing inflammation and improving digestion.

In the culinary world, sweet cicely's subtle flavor and aroma make it a versatile ingredient, often used in salads, soups, and sauces, while also pairing well with cheeses and fruits. As a hardy perennial herb, sweet cicely is relatively low-maintenance, requiring minimal care and making it an attractive addition to any garden or container arrangement. From a botanical perspective, sweet cicely's leaves are characterized by their distinctive fern-like shape and sweet, anise-like fragrance, while its flowers produce tiny white umbels that resemble those of the carrot family.

With a long history dating back to ancient Greece, where it was prized for its medicinal properties and used in traditional remedies, sweet cicely has been a beloved herb for centuries, with many cultures continuing to cherish its unique flavor and aroma to this day.
