10 Medicinal Constituents Of Tilia × Europaea (Lime)

Tilia × europaea has active constituents such as flavonoids, terpenes, and saponins.

These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities. The flavonoids, in particular, have been shown to improve blood vessel health, reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease. Additionally, the saponins may help alleviate respiratory issues by relaxing bronchial muscles.

By incorporating Tilia × europaea into traditional remedies or supplements, individuals may experience improved overall well-being and reduced symptoms associated with various health conditions.

1. Quercetin

Tilia × europaea quercetin is a bioactive flavonoid compound found in the flowers and leaves of the plant.

Quercetin has been studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. It has been shown to have a positive effect on cardiovascular health, immune function, and may even have antiviral activity.

Research suggests that quercetin may be beneficial in preventing chronic diseases such as cancer and diabetes.

2. Kaempferol

Tilia × europaea kaempferol is a flavonoid compound found in the flowers and leaves of the Tilia cordata tree.

It has been reported to exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it potentially beneficial for human health.

Kaempferol has been studied for its ability to protect against cell damage and reduce inflammation, which may have implications for the prevention and treatment of various diseases.

3. Isoquercetin

Tilia × europaea isoquercetin is a bioactive flavonoid compound isolated from the leaves of this plant species.

Research has shown that it exhibits a range of biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

It has been found to possess potential therapeutic effects, such as improving cardiovascular health, reducing cancer risk, and alleviating respiratory disorders.

4. Flavonoids

Tilia × europaea flavonoids is a class of bioactive compounds extracted from its flowers, leaves, and stems.

These flavonoids have been extensively studied for their potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. They have been shown to exhibit immunomodulatory effects, improve cardiovascular health, and possess neuroprotective activity.

The unique combination and concentration of flavonoids in Tilia × europaea make it an attractive subject for research into their therapeutic applications.

5. Ursolic acid

Tilia × europaea ursolic acid is a naturally occurring compound found in its leaves and flowers.

This triterpenoid compound has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral activities.

Ursolic acid has also been shown to have antioxidant and cytotoxic effects, making it a promising candidate for the treatment of various diseases, including cancer and cardiovascular disorders.

6. Oleanolic acid

Tilia × europaea oleanolic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from the leaves and flowers of the Tilia europaea tree.

It has been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it a promising natural agent for treating various diseases.

Studies have highlighted its potential in reducing cardiovascular risk factors, improving immune function, and even exhibiting anticancer activity, solidifying its reputation as a valuable compound in traditional medicine and modern research.

7. Betulinic acid

Tilia × europaea betulinic acid is a natural compound found in the leaves of the plant.

It has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a potential therapeutic agent for various diseases.

Research has demonstrated its ability to inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells and reduce inflammation, highlighting its promising applications in medicine.

8. Gallic acid

Tilia × europaea gallic acid is a key compound present in its leaves.

It is a type of phenolic acid that has been widely studied for its potential health benefits and antioxidant properties. Gallic acid has been found to have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antitumor activities, making it a promising area of research in the field of medicine.

Its presence in lime trees also contributes to their traditional use in herbal remedies.

9. Chlorogenic acid

Tilia × europaea chlorogenic acid is a phenolic compound found in the leaves and flowers of this species.

It has been shown to have various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Chlorogenic acid has been studied for its potential therapeutic effects on cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Its unique structure and bioactivity make it an interesting compound for further research and potential applications.

10. Caffeoylquinic acid

Tilia × europaea caffeoylquinic acid is a type of polyphenolic compound found in its flowers and leaves.

It has been shown to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

This compound may contribute to the traditional uses of Tilia flowers in folk medicine, particularly for treating respiratory issues and skin conditions.
