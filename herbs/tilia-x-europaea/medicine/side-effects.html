1. Causes allergic reactions

Tilia × europaea causes allergic reactions due to the presence of proteins in its pollen and sap.

These allergens can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling of the skin, respiratory issues like congestion and coughing, and potentially life-threatening anaphylaxis.

The high concentration of these allergens in lime flowers and leaves makes them a common culprit behind seasonal allergies.

2. Increases sedation levels

Tilia × europaea increases sedation levels due to its ability to interact with the body's nervous system.

The herb contains compounds that can slow down brain activity and promote relaxation, leading to feelings of drowsiness or sleepiness.

This is particularly true when taking high doses or combining it with other sedative herbs or medications.

3. Produces dizziness attacks

Tilia × europaea produces dizziness attacks due to its ability to interact with certain medications and affect blood pressure.

The herb can enhance the effects of sedatives, tranquilizers, and antidepressants, leading to increased feelings of drowsiness and lightheadedness.

Additionally, Tilia × europaea may cause a drop in blood pressure, particularly when standing up or changing positions quickly, which can contribute to dizziness and vertigo symptoms.

4. Triggers headaches frequently

Tilia × europaea triggers headaches frequently due to its ability to cause an increase in blood flow and pressure.

The essential oils present in the plant can stimulate the nerves and brain, leading to a buildup of tension and discomfort, which may manifest as frequent headaches.

Additionally, the caffeine-like compounds found in Tilia × europaea can further exacerbate this issue by constricting blood vessels and reducing oxygen flow to the brain, contributing to the onset of headaches.

5. Amplifies anxiety feelings

Tilia × europaea amplifies anxiety feelings due to its ability to affect the neurotransmitter serotonin, which plays a crucial role in regulating mood and emotional responses.

The increased levels of serotonin can lead to feelings of nervousness, restlessness, and apprehension, exacerbating existing anxiety disorders.

Additionally, Tilia × europaea's effect on the GABA receptor system can also contribute to its anxiogenic properties, further enhancing its potential to amplify anxiety feelings in some individuals.

6. Enhances heart palpitations

Tilia × europaea enhances heart palpitations.

This is due to its ability to stimulate the autonomic nervous system, which can cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure. Additionally, the flavonoids present in Tilia × europaea can interact with cardiac rhythm-regulating medications, further exacerbating heart palpitation symptoms.

As a result, individuals may experience rapid or irregular heartbeat, often accompanied by anxiety or panic attacks.

7. Induces stomach upset often

Tilia × europaea induces stomach upset often.

This is attributed to its high content of saponins and flavonoids, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the gastrointestinal tract, leading to discomfort, nausea, and diarrhea.

Additionally, the plant's ability to slow down gastric emptying can further exacerbate digestive issues, potentially resulting in stomach pain, bloating, and flatulence.

8. Worsens insomnia symptoms

Tilia × europaea worsens insomnia symptoms by disrupting the body's natural sleep-wake cycle.

The essential oils present in its flowers and leaves can increase alertness and arousal levels, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

Additionally, the herbal extract can stimulate the central nervous system, leading to an exacerbation of insomnia symptoms.

9. Lowers blood pressure significantly

Tilia × europaea lowers blood pressure significantly.

This is due to its ability to dilate blood vessels and reduce peripheral resistance, thereby decreasing blood pressure. The flavonoids present in the plant are thought to be responsible for this vasodilatory effect, which can lead to a decrease in systolic blood pressure.

Additionally, Tilia × europaea's beta-blocker-like activity may also contribute to its hypotensive effects by inhibiting the action of norepinephrine on vascular smooth muscle cells.

10. Creates mental fogginess

Tilia × europaea creates mental fogginess due to its ability to interact with certain neurotransmitters in the brain.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and terpenes, can bind to receptors in the brain, disrupting normal cognitive function and leading to feelings of confusion and mental haze.

This interaction can also affect the body's natural serotonin levels, further exacerbating mental fogginess.
