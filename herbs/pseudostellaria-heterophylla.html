Pseudostellaria heterophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pseudostellaria heterophylla has heterophyllous leaves characterized by a distinct variation in shape and size between juvenile and adult stages. The immature foliage typically displays narrow, linear, or lanceolate forms that emerge from the stem. As the plant matures, the leaf morphology shifts toward broader, more complex shapes that may appear more lobed or ovate. This structural transition serves as a primary diagnostic feature for identifying the species in different developmental phases. These morphological changes are closely linked to the plant's growth cycle and environmental adaptation.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the southwestern Andes, primarily spanning parts of Peru and Bolivia. It thrives specifically within the alpine tundra and puna ecosystems located at elevations typically exceeding 3,500 meters. Its range is restricted to these specialized montane habitats where it can withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and intense solar radiation.         What side effects this plant can have?
