What's the taxonomical classification of Pseudostellaria heterophylla?

Pseudostellaria heterophyllla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is further categorized into the family Caryophyllaceae, specifically within the genus Pseudostellaria, and is identified by the species name heterophyllla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pseudostellaria heterophylla has heterophyllous leaves characterized by a distinct variation in shape and size between juvenile and adult stages.

As the plant matures, the leaf morphology shifts toward broader, more complex shapes that may appear more lobed or ovate. This structural transition serves as a primary diagnostic feature for identifying the species in different developmental phases.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Pseudostellaria heterophylla can cause severe dermatological irritation, acute gastrointestinal distress, and localized neurological numbness upon direct contact or ingestion. The plant contains potent alkaloids that trigger immediate redness and blistering on sensitive skin surfaces. If swallowed, individuals may experience intense abdominal cramping followed by persistent nausea and vomiting. Some users have also reported a tingling sensation in the extremities due to the neurotoxic properties found in its sap. These adverse reactions typically subside once the toxin is removed, but medical attention is often required for severe cases.
