Mussaenda breviloba

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mussaenda breviloba has a woody shrub structure characterized by oppositely arranged, elliptic to oblong leaves that possess entire margins and acute tips. The plant is distinguished by its large, showy calyx lobes which are often bright red or orange in color and serve as the primary visual attractant. These enlarged sepals surround much smaller, inconspicuous true flowers that are typically white or cream. The foliage remains a deep green, providing a stark contrast to the vibrant, petal-like bracts. Its branching pattern allows it to form dense thickets in its native tropical habitats.
