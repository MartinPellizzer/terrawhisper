Hydrangea chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrangea chinensis?

Hydrangea chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Cornales. This plant is a member of the family Hydrangeaceae and falls within the genus Hydrangea, ultimately reaching its specific taxonomic designation as the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrangea chinensis has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length and possess serrated margins and acute tips. These leaves are typically dark green with a somewhat textured surface and prominent venation. The plant features woody stems that form a multi-stemmed shrub reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its inflorescences consist of small, delicate flowers arranged in terminal corymbs. The individual florets often display a mix of fertile and sterile varieties depending on the specific cultivar.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe poisoning through symptoms including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. Ingestion of the plant may also lead to neurological issues such as dizziness, confusion, and seizures. More critical complications can arise if the toxins affect the heart, potentially causing cardiac arrhythmias. These dangerous side effects occur because the plant contains cyanogenic glycosides that release cyanide into the body. Immediate medical attention is necessary if any part of the hydrangea is consumed.
