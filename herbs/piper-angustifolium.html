What is Piper'S Betony (Piper Angustifolium)?

Piper's Betony, also known as Piper angustifolium, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its upright growth, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Piper's betony is Piper angustifolium. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Piperales, family Piperaceae, genus Piper, and species angustifolium. It is commonly known as betony in North America and as kōkō in parts of the Pacific Northwest. The synonyms for this plant include Piper angustifolium and Piper betonicum.
