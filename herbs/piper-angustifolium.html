What is Piper'S Betony (Piper Angustifolium)?

Piper's Betony, also known as Piper angustifolium, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its upright growth, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Piper's betony is Piper angustifolium. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Piperales, family Piperaceae, genus Piper, and species angustifolium. It is commonly known as betony in North America and as kōkō in parts of the Pacific Northwest. The synonyms for this plant include Piper angustifolium and Piper betonicum.

Plant Description and Morphology

Piper's betony is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, with a sprawling, mat-forming structure.

Its leaves are lance-shaped, narrow, and pointed, with a slightly toothed margin and a bluish-green hue on the upper surface. The flowers are small, tubular, and borne in dense clusters at the tips of the stems, displaying a vibrant purple to violet color. The root system consists of fibrous roots that spread horizontally, aiding in the plant's ability to colonize disturbed soils.

The plant has a mild, aromatic scent, with leaves that are rough to the touch and have a slightly woody texture.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Piper's betony is native to the western United States, particularly in regions of Washington, Oregon, and California, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
