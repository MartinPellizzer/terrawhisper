Narcissus major var. bicolor

Narcissus major var. bicolor, also known as the bi-colored narcissus or double daffodil, is a type of flowering plant that belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in the production of galantamine, a compound used to treat Alzheimer's disease and other neurological disorders.

The health benefits of Narcissus major var. bicolor include its potential to improve cognitive function, memory, and mood, as well as its ability to reduce stress and anxiety.

Herbal preparations of this plant typically involve drying the flowers and leaves, which are then used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions for medicinal purposes.
