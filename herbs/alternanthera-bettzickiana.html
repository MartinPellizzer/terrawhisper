Alternanthera bettzickiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Alternanthera and the species bettzickiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alternanthera bettzickiana has trailing or creeping stems that produce ovate to elliptical leaves ranging from one to two inches in length with entire margins and prominent venation.

The foliage is highly ornamental, featuring dark purple or burgundy centers surrounded by bright lime-green or chartreuse margins. These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along the slender, flexible branches. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, though they are rarely seen in garden settings.

The plant's overall structure is compact and spreading, making it an ideal groundcover for containers or landscape borders.
