What's the taxonomical classification of Alternanthera bettzickiana?

Alternanthera bettzickiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Alternanthera and the species bettzickiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alternanthera bettzickiana has trailing or creeping stems that produce ovate to elliptical leaves ranging from one to two inches in length with entire margins and prominent venation. The foliage is highly ornamental, featuring dark purple or burgundy centers surrounded by bright lime-green or chartreuse margins. These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along the slender, flexible branches. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, though they are rarely seen in garden settings. The plant's overall structure is compact and spreading, making it an ideal groundcover for containers or landscape borders.

What are the pharmacological activities of Alternanthera bettzickiana?

This plant has demonstrated anti-arthritic, cytotoxic, and mutagenic pharmacological activities through various biochemical assessments. Research indicates that specific extracts can inhibit inflammatory processes associated with arthritis. Studies on its cytotoxic properties reveal how certain compounds interact with cellular viability, which is significant for potential cancer research. However, evaluations of its mutagenic activity are essential to determine if its chemical constituents cause genetic damage. Understanding these diverse biological responses helps clarify the medicinal potential and safety profile of the species.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in pharmacology", the ethanolic extract of Alternanthera bettzickiana (ABEE) possesses anti-arthritic potential and low toxicity. In acute oral toxicity testing, a dose of 2000 mg/kg of ABEE resulted in no mortality or morbidity, indicating an LD50 greater than 2000 mg/kg. When administered at dose levels of 125 mg/kg, 250 mg/kg, and 500 mg/kg for 10 days, ABEE decreased paw diameter and restored hematological and biochemical changes. The extract increased SOD and CAT levels while simultaneously decreasing MDA, NO, TNF-α, and IL-6 levels in arthritic rats. These findings suggest that Alternanthera bettzickiana can be used for the treatment of arthritis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These specific phytochemicals contribute to the plant's potential therapeutic properties and biological activities. The presence of flavonoids and tannins often provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Additionally, the glycosides and saponins play crucial roles in various physiological interactions within medicinal applications. Terpenoids further enhance the chemical complexity and medicinal value of the species.

According to a study published by "Cellular and molecular biology (Noisy-le-Grand, France)", the aqueous-methanolic extract of Alternanthera bettzickiana contains saponins, tannins, terpenoids, flavonoids and glycosides. The n-hexane extract of the plant revealed the presence of tannins and terpenoids only. Both extracts were found to be mutagenic to the TA 102 tester strain at a concentration as low as 15mg/mL. The aqueous-methanolic and n-hexane whole plant extracts of Alternanthera bettzickiana showed an IC-50 of 493 and 456 µg/mL in BHK-21 cells respectively.
