What's the taxonomical classification of Alternanthera bettzickiana?

Alternanthera bettzickiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Caryophyllales, it falls under the family Amaranthaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Alternanthera and the species bettzickiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alternanthera bettzickiana has trailing or creeping stems that produce ovate to elliptical leaves ranging from one to two inches in length with entire margins and prominent venation. The foliage is highly ornamental, featuring dark purple or burgundy centers surrounded by bright lime-green or chartreuse margins. These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along the slender, flexible branches. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, though they are rarely seen in garden settings. The plant's overall structure is compact and spreading, making it an ideal groundcover for containers or landscape borders.

What are the pharmacological activities of Alternanthera bettzickiana?

This plant has anti-arthritic, cytotoxic, and non-mutagenic pharmacological activities that suggest potential therapeutic applications. Research indicates that certain extracts demonstrate the ability to reduce inflammation associated with arthritic conditions. Studies on cell viability have also identified specific cytotoxic properties that may be useful in cancer research. Importantly, toxicological assessments have shown that the plant lacks mutagenic activity, meaning it does not induce genetic mutations. These diverse biological properties make Alternanthera bettzickiana a subject of significant interest in pharmacology.

As discussed in a study, Alternanthera bettzickiana ethanolic extract (ABEE) demonstrates anti-arthritic potential and low toxicity. In acute oral toxicity testing, a dose of 2000 mg/kg of ABEE resulted in no mortality or morbidity, indicating the LD50 is greater than 2000 mg/kg. When tested against an arthritic model using dose levels of 125 mg/kg, 250 mg/kg, and 500 mg/kg, the extract decreased paw diameter. The treatment restored hematological and biochemical changes by increasing SOD and CAT levels. Additionally, ABEE decreased levels of MDA, NO, TNF-α, and IL-6 in arthritic rats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains flavonoids, glycosides, saponins, tannins, and terpenoids which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These diverse phytochemicals contribute to the plant's broad range of therapeutic properties and biological activities. Flavonoids and glycosides often act as potent antioxidants that protect cells from oxidative stress. The presence of saponins and terpenoids can provide antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects within the body. Additionally, tannins function as astringents that help in tissue contraction and wound healing. Together, these bioactive substances form a complex chemical profile responsible for the plant's healing potential.

As discussed in a study, the aqueous-methanolic extract of Alternanthera bettzickiana contains saponins, tannins, terpenoids, flavonoids and glycosides. In contrast, the n-hexane extract of the plant revealed the presence of tannins and terpenoids only. Both extracts were found to be mutagenic to the TA 102 tester strain at a concentration as low as 15mg/mL. The aqueous-methanolic and n-hexane whole plant extracts of Alternanthera bettzickiana showed an IC-50 of 493 and 456 µg/mL in BHK-21 cells respectively. These results indicate that the plant exhibited mutagenic activity in a bacterial reverse mutation assay.
