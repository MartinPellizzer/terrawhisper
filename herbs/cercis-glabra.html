Cercis glabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Cercis glabra?

Cercis glabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, the plant is placed in the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Cercis, resulting in its scientific name, species glabra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cercis glabra has a spreading, multi-stemmed growth habit characterized by smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that measure two to five inches across. The foliage is typically deciduous and displays a soft, pubescence on the undersides of the blades. During the spring, the plant produces clusters of small, pea-like pink or purple flowers that emerge directly from the woody stems. These blossoms eventually give way to flat, elongated seed pods that persist on the branches.
