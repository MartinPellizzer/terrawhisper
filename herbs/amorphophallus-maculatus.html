What's the taxonomical classification of Amorphophallus maculatus?

Amorphophallus maculatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Alismatales. This plant is further classified under the family Araceae, which places it within the genus Amorphophallus, and is specifically identified by its species name, maculatus.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe skin irritation, chemical burns, and intense swelling upon contact with its sap. The primary irritant is a calcium oxalate crystal structure that acts like microscopic needles when pressed into the skin. These crystals can penetrate the dermal layers, leading to redness, itching, and painful blisters. If the sap accidentally touches the eyes, it may cause temporary blindness or significant corneal damage. Ingestion of any part of the plant can lead to debilitating throat swelling and gastrointestinal distress.
