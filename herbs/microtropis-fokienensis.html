What are the pharmacological activities of Microtropis fokienensis?

This plant has antitubercular, antimicrobial, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. Researchers have identified specific bioactive compounds within Microtropis fokienenensis that contribute to its diverse therapeutic potential. Its ability to combat Mycobacterium tuberculosis makes it a subject of significant interest in drug discovery efforts. Additional studies suggest that its phytochemical profile also offers protective effects against oxidative stress and various bacterial pathogens. These combined properties highlight the plant's importance in traditional medicine and modern pharmacology.

As discussed in a study, four new dihydroagarofuranoid sesquiterpene compounds 1-4 and one new hydroxybenzylsalicylaldehyde forkieniin 5 were isolated from the roots of Microtropis fokienensis. Along with these, nine known compounds were also identified from the plant. Among the isolated substances, compounds 3, 4, 7, and 8 exhibited potent antitubercular activities. These specific compounds showed minimum inhibitory concentrations (MICs) less than or equal to 26.0 microM against Mycobacterium tuberculosis 90-221387 in vitro.
