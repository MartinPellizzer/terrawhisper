What is Arabis laevigata?

Arabis laevigata is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is a small to medium-sized plant, typically growing to 10-30 cm in height, with a slender stem and narrow, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are 2-6 cm long and 1-3 cm wide. The leaves are alternate, simple, and glabrous, with a smooth margin and a prominent midvein. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in a raceme, with four petals and six stamens, in late spring and early summer.

The flowers are followed by small, globular siliques that contain numerous seeds.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Arabis laevigata are infusion, tea, tincture, salve, and decoction.

Infusion and tea are made by steeping the leaves and flowers in hot water to create a medicinal drink, often used to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs due to its expectorant properties. Tincture is made by soaking the plant in a solvent like ethanol to extract its active compounds, commonly used to treat digestive issues and as an anti-inflammatory agent. Salve is a topical preparation made by infusing the plant in a carrier oil, used to treat skin conditions such as eczema and acne due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Decoction is a preparation made by boiling the plant in water to create a liquid extract, often used to treat urinary tract issues and as a diuretic.
