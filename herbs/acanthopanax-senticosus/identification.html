Botanical Identification of Acanthopanax Senticosus (Acanthopanax senticosus)

To identify Acanthopanax Senticosus (Acanthopanax senticosus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Acanthopanax senticosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Araliaceae, genus Acanthopanax, and species Acanthopanax senticosus. Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Acanthopanax senticosus, first described by the botanist Friedrich Anton Wilhelm von Forster in 1832. It is also known by several synonyms, including Acanthopanax sessilifolius and Eleutherococcus senticosus, reflecting historical taxonomic changes. Common names include Siberian ginseng and prickly ash, while regional names vary across different cultures and regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Acanthopanax senticosus is a deciduous shrub or small tree. It has a taproot system with lateral roots. The stem is straight, woody, and often branched. Leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have serrated margins. The inflorescence is a panicle. Flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered. The fruit is a drupe, and seeds are small, black, and covered in a fleshy aril.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Acanthopanax senticosus can be distinguished by its compound leaves with 5-7 pairs of opposite leaflets, serrated margins, and orange-red berries. It differs from similar species like A. kirilowii by its more branched stems and smaller, more numerous leaflets. The presence of a distinctive milky sap and specific leaflet shape also aid in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Acanthopanax senticosus is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Russia. It thrives in temperate climates with cool summers and cold winters. The plant prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at elevations between 1,000 and 3,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Acanthopanax senticosus blooms in late summer to early autumn, typically between July and September. Seed formation occurs from late autumn to early winter, around October to December. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the summer when leaves are fully developed and the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Acanthopanax senticosus is closely related to species within the same genus, such as Acanthopanax sessilifolius. It shares key traits with other members of the Araliaceae family, including compound leaves and milky sap. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other Asian species in the genus.
