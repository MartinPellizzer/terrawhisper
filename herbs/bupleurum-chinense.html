Chinese Bupleurum

Bupleurum chinense

Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense) is a member of the Apiaceae family, traditionally used in Chinese medicine for liver and gallbladder disorders, fever and malaria, and digestive issues. The roots and rhizomes of the plant are primarily utilized in these applications, reflecting its historical role in regional herbal practices.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Bupleurum, scientifically known as Bupleurum chinense, belongs to the Apiaceae family. It is also called Chinese Peony root or Szechuan Bupleurum in regional contexts. A perennial herb native to China, it has slender roots, pinnate leaves, and yellow flowers. Synonyms include Bupleurum scorzoneraefolium and Bupleurum tenuifolium in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Chinese Bupleurum contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, saponins, and glycosides. These compounds work synergistically to modulate immune responses and reduce inflammation. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic effects in traditional Chinese medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Tincture: Used for treating fever and inflammation.

Extract: Used for enhancing immune function and reducing inflammation.

Safety Profile

Chinese Bupleurum is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated in pregnant women and those with a history of allergic reactions to similar plants. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
