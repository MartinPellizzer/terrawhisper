Bioactive Constituents

Bupleurum chinense phytosterols, such as stigmasterol and sitosterol, are key constituents known for their anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties.

These compounds help regulate the immune system by modulating cytokine production and reducing excessive inflammatory responses. In addition to phytosterols, Bupleurum chinense contains saponins, which exhibit antioxidant and antiviral activities, contributing to the herb's therapeutic effects.

Flavonoids present in the herb, such as baicalin and wogonin, are responsible for its anti-inflammatory, antithrombotic, and neuroprotective benefits. Polysaccharides from Bupleurum chinense also play a vital role in enhancing immune function by stimulating the production of white blood cells and promoting overall health.

Together, these medicinal constituents make Bupleurum chinense a valuable herb in traditional medicine for treating a variety of ailments, including liver diseases, inflammation, and immune-related disorders.

Medicinal Parts

Bupleurum chinense root is one of the most commonly utilized medicinal parts of this plant in traditional Chinese medicine, valued for its ability to regulate the liver and improve qi circulation.

The root contains compounds such as saikosaponins, which are believed to have anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and immunomodulatory properties. It is often used to treat conditions like hepatitis, liver dysfunction, and fever, as well as to alleviate symptoms of depression and anxiety.

In addition to the root, the leaves and seeds of Bupleurum chinense are also used in herbal formulations, though they are less common than the root. The leaves are typically used for their mild antiseptic properties, while the seeds are occasionally employed for their potential digestive benefits. However, the root remains the most significant and widely studied part of the plant, with extensive research supporting its role in various therapeutic applications.

Overall, the different parts of Bupleurum chinense contribute to a diverse range of medicinal uses, highlighting the plant's importance in traditional and modern herbal medicine.
