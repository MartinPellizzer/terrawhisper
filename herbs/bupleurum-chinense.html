What to know about Bupleurum chinense (thorow-wax) before using it medicinally

Thorow-wax, scientifically known as Bupleurum chinense, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to improve respiratory function, reduce inflammation, and even alleviate symptoms of depression.

While its culinary uses are often overshadowed by more popular herbs, thorow-wax is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine to add flavor and depth to soups, stews, and braises. In horticultural contexts, thorow-wax is prized for its striking yellow flowers and ability to attract beneficial insects to the garden. From a botanical standpoint, thorow-wax belongs to the carrot family and boasts a unique blend of chemical compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Despite its ancient use in traditional Chinese medicine, thorow-wax remains somewhat shrouded in mystery, with few historical references available to shed light on its origins or widespread cultivation.
