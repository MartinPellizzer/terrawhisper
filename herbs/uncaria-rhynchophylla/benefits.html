Uncaria rhynchophylla, also known as the hook vine, has been studied for its potential to combat malaria due to its bioactive compounds that may inhibit the growth of the parasite.

Flavonoids contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting immune response.

Together, these constituents make Uncaria rhynchophylla a valuable natural remedy in the fight against malaria.
