Cissus arborea

Cissus arborea, commonly known as Adam's Tree or the Vine of Youth, is a perennial climber native to the tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, which include reducing inflammation and improving bone density due to its anti-inflammatory and osteogenic properties.

The therapeutic actions of Cissus arborea are attributed to its bioactive constituents, which include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, that exhibit antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-cancer activities.

The extracts of Cissus arborea have shown promise in treating various health conditions, including arthritis, osteoporosis, and certain types of cancer, although further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
