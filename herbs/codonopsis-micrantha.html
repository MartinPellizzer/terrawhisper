Codonopsis micrantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Codonopsis micrantha?

Codonopsis micrantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further categorized within the order Asterales and the family Campanulaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Codonopsis, and its species designation, micrantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Codonoopsis micrantha has long, lanceolate leaves that grow in a basal rosette and small, tubular, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense terminal racemes.

The foliage typically features prominent longitudinal veins and a smooth or slightly textured surface texture. Each individual flower is quite minute, which gives the species its specific epithet. The inflorescence consists of multiple small blossoms clustered tightly along a central stalk that rises above the leaves.

These reproductive structures are supported by slender stems that emerge directly from the base of the plant.
