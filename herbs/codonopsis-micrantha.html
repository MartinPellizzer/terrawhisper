Codonopsis micrantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Codonopsis micrantha?

Codonopsis micrantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further categorized within the order Asterales and the family Campanulaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Codonopsis, and its species designation, micrantha.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves are often prepared as a poultice to soothe skin irritations or applied to open cuts to promote healing. When consumed, parts of the plant can help alleviate gastrointestinal distress and abdominal discomfort. Local healers also utilize its properties to manage fever and general body aches. These medicinal applications highlight its importance in traditional herbal medicine for treating both external and internal ailments.
