Michelia foveolata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Michelia fooveolata has elliptic to oblong leaves that measure approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length and possess a distinctive pitted or foveolate surface texture on the underside. The foliage is typically coriaceous, meaning it has a leathery consistency, and displays a dark green upper surface with a paler underside. Its woody stems support these leaves in an alternate arrangement along the branches. The plant produces fragrant, cream-colored flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during its blooming period. Small, fleshy fruits eventually develop from the fertilized ovaries, often appearing in clusters.
