Synsepalum dulcificum

What's the taxonomical classification of Synsepalum dulcificum?

Synsepalum dulcificum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Sapotaeceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Synsepalum, with its specific designation being the species dulcificum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Synsepalum dulcificum has woody stems, simple leaves with entire margins, and distinctive fruit structures that define its physical appearance. The leaves are typically arranged in an opposite pattern and possess a coriaceous texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils to eventually develop into large, fleshy fruits. These fruits are characterized by a thick pericarp and contain numerous seeds embedded within a sweet pulp. The overall growth habit is that of a climbing shrub or woody vine within the Sapindaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of West Africa, specifically ranging across countries such as Nigeria, Cameroon, and Gabon. It thrives primarily in lowland equatorial forests where moisture and shade are abundant. Within these habitats, it is often found growing in the understory layer of dense vegetation. The species follows a distribution pattern typical of many members of the Sapindaceae family in the Guineo-Congolian forest block. Localized populations are concentrated in areas with high annual rainfall and stable humid conditions.
