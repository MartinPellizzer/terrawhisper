Botanical Identification of Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus)

To identify Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Yellow Milkvetch, Astragalus membranaceus, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Astragalus, and species Astragalus membranaceus. It is a member of the legume family and is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Yellow Milkvetch is Astragalus membranaceus, first described by the botanist Peter Forsskål in 1775. It is also known by several synonyms, including Astragalus adsurgens and Astragalus transiliens, reflecting historical taxonomic changes. Common names such as Yellow Milkvetch and Milkvetch are widely used in herbal medicine contexts. Regional and historical names further highlight its traditional significance in various cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Yellow Milkvetch is a perennial herb with a taproot system that can grow up to 3 meters deep. It has upright, branching stems that are typically hairy. Its leaves are compound, with 5-7 pairs of oval leaflets. The plant produces a dense, globular inflorescence with yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The fruit is a pod that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Yellow Milkvetch can be distinguished by its yellow flowers, hairy stems, and leaves with three leaflets. It differs from similar species like Astragalus adsurgens by having more prominent hairs and a distinct leaf shape. The presence of a membranous leaf base and specific flower structure also aid in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Yellow Milkvetch is native to regions of China, Mongolia, and parts of Russia. It thrives in dry, temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Yellow Milkvetch blooms from June to August, producing yellow flowers in clusters. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early fall. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in late summer to early autumn when the seeds are mature and the plant is at its peak botanical characteristics.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is closely related to other Astragalus species within the legume family. It shares genus traits such as compound leaves and pod fruits, and exhibits family characteristics like nitrogen fixation and typical Fabaceae morphology. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other legumes and closely related Astragalus species.
