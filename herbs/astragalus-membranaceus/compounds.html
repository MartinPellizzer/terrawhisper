Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) Compounds

The primary compounds in Yellow Milkvetch include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and saponins, which may support immune function. Glycosides and terpenoids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, while phenolic acids and tannins provide additional antioxidant benefits. Coumarin, alkaloids, essential oils, and anthocyanins also play roles in its therapeutic properties.

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus). They contribute to the plant's immunomodulatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for boosting vitality and immune function.

2. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus). It contributes to the plant's immune-modulating and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is a compound found in Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. 9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus). The essential oil is extracted through steam distillation, preserving its active constituents for medicinal applications.

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is often associated with the plant's traditional use in enhancing vitality and resilience.
