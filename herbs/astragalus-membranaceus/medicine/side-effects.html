10 Possible Side Effects Of Astragalus Membranaceus (Huang Qi)

Astragalus membranaceus has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, nausea, and stomach upset due to excessive dosing or allergic reactions.

Prolonged use can also cause digestive issues, interact with blood thinners, or worsen kidney problems.

For individuals with compromised immune systems, Astragalus may exacerbate autoimmune conditions, making it challenging to manage symptoms and maintain a quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Astragalus membranaceus if used imporperly.

1. Increases risk of toxicity

Astragalus membranaceus causes allergic reactions due to its potential cross-reactivity with other plants in the Fabaceae family.

This may trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

The exact mechanisms behind this reaction are not fully understood, but it is thought that the plant's proteins or polysaccharides may be responsible for triggering the allergic response.

2. Increases risk of toxicity

Astragalus membranaceus triggers blood pressure fluctuations due to its ability to stimulate the body's natural production of cortisol, a hormone that can raise blood pressure.

Additionally, Astragalus membranaceus has been shown to increase heart rate and cardiac output, further contributing to potential blood pressure fluctuations.

The root cause is the herb's inherent properties, which affect the body's cardiovascular system in this way.

3. Increases risk of toxicity

Astragalus membranaceus induces respiratory distress in some individuals.

This is due to its potential allergenic and immunogenic properties, which can stimulate an overactive immune response in the lungs.

Additionally, Astragalus membranaceus may interact with medications that affect respiration, such as bronchodilators or corticosteroids, leading to adverse effects on breathing patterns.

4. Increases risk of toxicity

Astragalus membranaceus stimulates kidney stone formation.

This is due to its ability to increase the concentration of oxalate and calcium in the urine, which can lead to the formation of kidney stones. Additionally, astragalus may alter the composition of gut bacteria, leading to changes in the metabolism of nutrients that contribute to the development of kidney stones.

The combination of these effects can increase an individual's risk of forming kidney stones while using astragalus membranaceus.

5. Increases risk of toxicity

Astragalus membranaceus increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to thin the blood and inhibit platelet aggregation.

This is primarily caused by its potential to interact with blood thinner medications and reduce blood clotting factors, making it essential for individuals with coagulation disorders or those taking anticoagulant therapy to exercise caution when using this herb.

6. Increases risk of toxicity

Astragalus membranaceus aggravates thyroid disorders due to its ability to stimulate the immune system and increase the production of antibodies.

This can exacerbate autoimmune thyroid diseases such as Hashimoto's thyroiditis, where the body produces antibodies that attack the thyroid gland.

Additionally, Astragalus membranaceus may also interfere with the absorption of thyroxine (T4) and triiodothyronine (T3), leading to an imbalance in thyroid hormone levels and potentially causing hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism.

7. Increases risk of toxicity

Astragalus membranaceus suppresses immune function by inhibiting the production of cytokines, which are essential for a proper immune response.

This is due to its ability to modulate the activity of immune cells, such as macrophages and T-lymphocytes, leading to reduced levels of immunoglobulins and decreased antibody production.

As a result, individuals may experience impaired immune function, making them more susceptible to infections and diseases.

8. Increases risk of toxicity

Astragalus membranaceus worsens diabetes complications by stimulating insulin production and increasing glucose uptake in cells.

This may exacerbate hyperglycemia in individuals with uncontrolled or poorly managed diabetes.

Additionally, Astragalus membranaceus has been shown to enhance the activity of aldose reductase, an enzyme that contributes to diabetic complications such as cataracts, neuropathy, and nephropathy.

9. Increases risk of toxicity

Astragalus membranaceus enhances hypoglycemia symptoms by its ability to stimulate insulin secretion and increase glucose uptake in the body.

This may cause people with diabetes or those taking blood sugar-lowering medications to experience an increase in low blood sugar episodes.

As a result, individuals using Astragalus membranaceus may exhibit signs of hypoglycemia such as shakiness, dizziness, and confusion.

10. Increases risk of toxicity

Astragalus membranaceus causes digestive upset in some individuals due to its potential to alter gut microbiota and increase production of gastric acid.

The saponins present in Astragalus can irritate the mucous membranes of the digestive tract, leading to symptoms such as bloating, nausea, and stomach discomfort.

Additionally, the plant's ability to stimulate the immune system can also contribute to gastrointestinal side effects, including changes in appetite and bowel habits.
