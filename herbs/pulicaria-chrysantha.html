Pulicaria chrysantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pulicaria chrysantha has an erect, perennial habit characterized by stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and are often covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem, featuring an oblong or lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. At the apex of the stems, the plant produces dense, terminal heads of bright yellow discoid flowers. Each flower head consists of numerous tubular florets packed closely together within a common receptacle. The involucral bracts surrounding the base of the flower heads are often hairy, providing additional protection to the developing seeds.
