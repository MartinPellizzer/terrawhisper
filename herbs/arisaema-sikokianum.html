Arisaema sikokianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arisaema sikokianum has a single, large, cordate leaf that can reach up to 50 centimeters in width, supported by a long, fleshy petiole. This leaf is characterized by a smooth, entire margin and a deep, heart-shaped base. The plant produces a solitary inflorescence consisting of a spathe that encloses a spadix. The spathe typically displays a pale green to creamy white coloration, which transitions into a purplish hue near the base or opening. Below the surface, the plant develops a thick, tuberous rhizome that serves as its primary storage organ.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe oral irritation, swelling of the mouth and tongue, a burning sensation, throat irritation, difficulty swallowing, nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain upon ingestion. The presence of calcium oxalate crystals in the tissues triggers immediate physical distress when the plant comes into contact with mucous membranes. This intense irritation often leads to significant swelling that can obstruct the airway or make swallowing extremely painful. Once the toxins reach the digestive system, they commonly provoke intense nausea and vomiting. Most victims also experience sharp abdominal pain following the initial contact with the mouth and throat.
