Peruvian Moonflower (Turnera diffusa var aphrodisiaca)

Peruvian Moonflower (Turnera diffusa var aphrodisiaca) is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to Central America, South America, and Caribbean. Traditionally, its leaves, resin, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, tonic, and stimulant actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and african traditional medicine.
