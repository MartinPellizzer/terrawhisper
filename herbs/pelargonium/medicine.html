What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Pelargonium (Scented Geranium)?

Pelargonium, also known as scented geranium, has health benefits such as reducing stress and anxiety, as well as treating digestive issues and skin irritations.

Its medicinal constituents include flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties. Pelargonium can be prepared as teas, infusions, or essential oils to treat a range of ailments.

However, individuals with allergies or sensitivities should exercise caution, as high doses may cause skin irritation or interact with certain medications.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Pelargonium.

What are the health benefits of Pelargonium?

Pelargonium has health benefits such as reducing stress and anxiety through its calming properties.

It is also used to alleviate symptoms of insomnia, digestive issues, and skin irritations. The plant's essential oil has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular natural remedy for wound care and sore throat relief.

Its antiseptic and antifungal properties also help in preventing infections.

What are the active constituents of Pelargonium?

The health benefits of Pelargonium comes from its active constituents, such as geraniol, linalool, and citronellol, which are known for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

These volatile oils also contain flavonoids, like rosmarinic acid, which have been shown to have anti-cancer and cardio-protective effects.

The herb's medicinal properties are also attributed to its phenolic acids and terpenoids, which contribute to its analgesic and anti-inflammatory activities.

What are the medicinal preparations of Pelargonium?

Pelargonium has different medicinal preparations, such as essential oil, extract, and tincture.

The essential oil is used topically to treat skin conditions like eczema and acne. The extract is used in teas and infusions to treat digestive issues like bloating and irritable bowel syndrome.

The tincture is used internally to treat anxiety, stress, and insomnia, and also as an anti-inflammatory.

What are the possible side effect of using Pelargonium improperly?

Improper use of Pelargonium increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and respiratory problems.

Ingestion can lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, while topical application may cause rashes, itching, and redness. Long-term use has been linked to increased risk of kidney and liver damage, as well as hormonal imbalances and thyroid problems.

It may also interact with certain medications, exacerbating underlying conditions.

What precautions to take when using Pelargonium medicinally?

Before using Pelargonium for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional, especially if you have allergies or are pregnant/breastfeeding.

Be cautious of interactions with medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications, and monitor your blood sugar levels if using for this purpose.

Avoid using undiluted essential oils, and opt for a diluted tincture or herbal tea.
