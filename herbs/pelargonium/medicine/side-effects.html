10 Possible Side Effects Of Pelargonium

Pelargonium has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects are often caused by ingesting large amounts or using the herb without proper dilution. For instance, someone who is already experiencing acid reflux may find that Pelargonium worsens their symptoms, leading to discomfort during meals and difficulty sleeping.

In severe cases, improper use can also cause anaphylactic reactions, which can be life-threatening.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Pelargonium causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, which are a type of compound found in the plant. These compounds can trigger an allergic response in susceptible individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, and respiratory issues.

In rare cases, an allergic reaction can progress to more severe symptoms.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Pelargonium triggers asthma attacks in some individuals due to the presence of sesquiterpene and phenolic compounds.

These natural chemicals can cause inflammation and irritation in the airways, leading to bronchospasm and wheezing. Furthermore, the essential oil's high concentrations of linalool and geraniol can also exacerbate respiratory issues.

As a result, individuals with asthma may experience worsening symptoms when exposed to Pelargonium.

3. Induces diarrhea

Pelargonium induces diarrhea in some individuals due to its high iridoid and tannin content.

These compounds can irritate the digestive system and increase the frequency of bowel movements. The bioactive compounds in Pelargonium can also stimulate the gut's motility, leading to accelerated digestion and bowel movements.

This reaction is more likely to occur in individuals who are sensitive to these compounds or have underlying gastrointestinal issues.

4. Exacerbates eczema

Pelargonium exacerbates eczema due to its potential allergenic properties.

The plant contains compounds that can trigger an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to an exacerbation of eczema symptoms. This is thought to be caused by the plant's essential oils, particularly geraniol, which can irritate the skin and cause inflammation.

As a result, individuals with eczema may experience worsened symptoms when using Pelargonium or products that contain its extracts.

5. Aggravates bronchitis

Pelargonium aggravates bronchitis due to its volatile oils, such as linalool and geraniol, which can irritate the airways and exacerbate respiratory conditions.

As a result, individuals with bronchitis may experience worsening symptoms, including increased mucus production, coughing, and difficulty breathing.

The essential oils in Pelargonium may also stimulate the production of histamine, leading to further inflammation and congestion in the airways.

6. Stimulates rapid heartbeat

Pelargonium stimulates rapid heartbeat due to its high concentration of flavonoids, such as luteolin and quercetin, which have a direct impact on the cardiovascular system.

These compounds cause an increase in heart rate by stimulating the beta-adrenergic receptors in the heart, leading to a faster heartbeat.

This effect is particularly pronounced when consumed in large quantities or with certain medications.

7. Increases blood pressure

Pelargonium increases blood pressure in some individuals due to its bioactive compounds, particularly flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds can affect the body's ability to regulate blood pressure by influencing the activity of certain enzymes and receptors involved in the renin-angiotensin system. As a result, Pelargonium may cause blood vessels to constrict, leading to elevated blood pressure levels.

This effect is often seen in people who are already prone to hypertension.

8. Decreases appetite significantly

Pelargonium decreases appetite significantly.

This side effect is attributed to the herb's essential oils, specifically geraniol and linalool, which have been shown to have a suppressive effect on the appetite centers in the brain. The oils may also alter the balance of neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, which play a role in regulating hunger and satiety.

As a result, individuals using Pelargonium may experience a marked decrease in their desire to eat.

9. Produces drowsiness rapidly

Pelargonium produces drowsiness rapidly due to its sedative properties.

This is primarily caused by the presence of iridoids, such as pelargonin and isovaltrate, which are known to have a calming effect on the nervous system. Additionally, the herb's volatile oils, including linalool and geraniol, contribute to its sedative action.

As a result, individuals may experience drowsiness or sleepiness after consuming Pelargonium.

10. Enhances sedative effects

Pelargonium enhances sedative effects by increasing the activity of GABA, a neurotransmitter that helps regulate sleep and relaxation.

As a result, individuals may experience increased drowsiness, fatigue, and a general feeling of calmness, which can be beneficial for those struggling with anxiety or insomnia.

The sedative properties of Pelargonium can also lead to a reduced alertness and concentration.
