Lindernia crustacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Lindernia crustacea?

Lindernia crustacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Linderniaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Lindernia, with its unique designation being the species crustacea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia crustacea has an erect to ascending herbaceous habit characterized by slender, branched stems and small, opposite, ovate-to-lanceolate leaves with entire margins.

The leaves are typically thin and possess acute or obtuse apices, often featuring a slightly translucent texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The corolla is typically white or pale lavender, featuring a distinct two-lipped structure characteristic of the Linderniaceae family.

Small, cylindrical capsules develop from the flowers to facilitate seed dispersal within its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
