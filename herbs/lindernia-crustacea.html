Lindernia crustacea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia crustacea has an erect to ascending herbaceous habit characterized by slender, branched stems and small, opposite, ovate-to-lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The leaves are typically thin and possess acute or obtuse apices, often featuring a slightly translucent texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The corolla is typically white or pale lavender, featuring a distinct two-lipped structure characteristic of the Linderniaceae family. Small, cylindrical capsules develop from the flowers to facilitate seed dispersal within its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
