Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium) Compounds

Blueberry contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which are antioxidants that support cardiovascular health. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties, while anthocyanins give it its deep color and offer protective benefits. These compounds work together to provide the plant's medicinal and health-promoting effects.

Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. These compounds are also associated with improved cardiovascular health and potential anti-inflammatory effects.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports its traditional use in promoting cardiovascular health and cognitive function.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and promoting gut health. Tannins also act as antioxidants, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body.

4. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium). It is a type of flavonoid responsible for the fruit's deep blue color and contributes to its antioxidant properties. Anthocyanin supports eye health, reduces inflammation, and may improve cognitive function. It is commonly used in herbal medicine for its protective effects on the cardiovascular and nervous systems.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting antioxidant activity and promoting cardiovascular health. Glycosides in blueberries are known for their role in improving vision and reducing oxidative stress.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin helps support digestive health and may enhance the absorption of nutrients from other compounds in the blueberry.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium). It contributes to the plant's mild anticoagulant properties and may support cardiovascular health. Coumarin's presence also influences the plant's aromatic profile and potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium) contribute to its antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health. They are present in various parts of the plant, including leaves and fruit. Terpenoids may also influence the plant's aroma and potential therapeutic effects.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium) contains volatile compounds like methyl salicylate and limonene. These contribute to its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The oil is often used in aromatherapy and may support digestive and respiratory health. It is extracted through steam distillation from the leaves and flowers.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium). While blueberries are more renowned for their flavonoids, alkaloids are present in smaller amounts. These compounds may contribute to the plant's potential therapeutic effects, though research on their specific roles in blueberry is limited.
