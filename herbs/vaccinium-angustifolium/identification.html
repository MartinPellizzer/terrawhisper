Botanical Identification of Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium)

To identify Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium) belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Ericales, family Ericaceae, genus Vaccinium, and species angustifolium. These classifications reflect its role in the plant kingdom and its botanical relationships within the heath family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Blueberry is Vaccinium angustifolium, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is part of the Ericaceae family and is also known by various common names such as wild blueberry and lowbush blueberry. Synonyms include Vaccinium myrtillus and Vaccinium corymbosum, though these refer to different species. Regional names vary, with some areas calling it "bogberry" or "blue cowberry."

Morphological Characteristics

Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium) is a low-growing shrub with a dense root system that anchors it in acidic, well-drained soil. Its stems are erect and woody, producing multiple shoots. Leaves are oval, leathery, and dark green, with a serrated edge. The inflorescence is a cluster of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in white or pale pink. The fruit is a small, round berry that turns blue when mature, containing one to three seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium) can be distinguished by its small, bell-shaped flowers with pinkish-white petals, oval blue berries that turn dark when ripe, and alternate, oval leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in open woodlands or heathlands. It is often confused with other Vaccinium species, but its leaf shape and berry color are key differentiators.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium) is native to North America, particularly eastern Canada and the northeastern United States. It thrives in cool, temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found at elevations between 200 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Blueberry (Vaccinium angustifolium) blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August. Harvest timing for the fruit is in early to mid-summer, usually between June and July, depending on regional climate.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Vaccinium angustifolium belongs to the Ericaceae family, sharing traits with other Vaccinium species. It is closely related to V. corymbosum and V. myrtilloides. These species are part of the same genus, exhibiting similar morphological and biochemical characteristics.
