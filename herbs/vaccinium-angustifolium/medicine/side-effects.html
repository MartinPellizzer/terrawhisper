10 Possible Side Effects Of Vaccinium Angustifolium (Blueberry)

Vaccinium angustifolium has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and kidney strain.

Overconsumption of the herb can cause these side effects, particularly if taken in excess. In severe cases, kidney strain can lead to kidney disease, impacting daily life and potentially requiring dialysis or a kidney transplant.

This can cause significant stress, financial burden, and affect one's overall quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Vaccinium angustifolium if used imporperly.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling.

The high antioxidant content of Vaccinium angustifolium may also contribute to its allergenic potential, making it a potential allergen for those sensitive to its compounds.

The allergenic compounds are particularly concentrated in the fruit's skin and seeds.
