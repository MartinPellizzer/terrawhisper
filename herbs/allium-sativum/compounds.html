Garlic (Allium sativum) Compounds

Garlic contains several key compounds, including essential oil, which gives it its characteristic odor. Terpenoids and flavonoids contribute to its antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids and saponins support anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides and tannins may aid in digestion, while coumarin and alkaloids have additional medicinal properties. Anthocyanins provide pigmentation and potential health benefits.

Garlic (Allium sativum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Garlic (Allium sativum). It contains volatile compounds like allicin, which contribute to garlic's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. This oil is responsible for many of the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Garlic (Allium sativum). They contribute to the plant's distinctive odor and have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Specific terpenoids like allicin play a key role in Garlic's therapeutic effects. These compounds are central to its use in herbal medicine for digestive and immune support.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Garlic (Allium sativum). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. These compounds may support cardiovascular health and have anti-inflammatory effects.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Garlic (Allium sativum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting cardiovascular and immune health. This compound is abundant in garlic's bulbs and is released when the plant is crushed or chopped.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Garlic (Allium sativum). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins help in reducing cholesterol levels and may support immune function. They are part of the complex bioactive components in garlic that contribute to its medicinal value.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Garlic (Allium sativum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in garlic are metabolized into active compounds like allicin, enhancing the plant's therapeutic effects.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Garlic (Allium sativum) that contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and may support digestive health by binding to proteins and reducing intestinal permeability. Tannins also play a role in preserving the plant's active components.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Garlic (Allium sativum). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and antithrombotic properties. Coumarin may help reduce blood clotting and support cardiovascular health. Its presence in garlic supports its traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Garlic (Allium sativum). While garlic is primarily known for its sulfur-containing compounds, alkaloids are present in smaller amounts. These compounds may contribute to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They are part of the complex biochemical profile of garlic.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Garlic (Allium sativum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cardiovascular health. While not the primary bioactive component, anthocyanin adds to the overall therapeutic profile of garlic.
