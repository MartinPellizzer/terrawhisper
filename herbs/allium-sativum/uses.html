Garlic (Allium sativum) Uses

Garlic is used to treat respiratory infections, including the common cold, bronchitis, cough, influenza, pneumonia, and asthma. It may help reduce high blood pressure and lower cholesterol levels due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Garlic (Allium sativum) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Garlic has been traditionally used to support respiratory health due to its antimicrobial and immune-boosting properties. It may help alleviate symptoms of colds, coughs, and bronchitis by reducing inflammation and inhibiting viral replication. Allicin, a key compound in garlic, is believed to enhance the body's defenses against respiratory pathogens. Regular consumption may support overall respiratory function and reduce the risk of infections.

2. Common cold

Garlic has been traditionally used to support immune function and may help alleviate symptoms of the common cold. Its active compound, allicin, possesses antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies suggest it may reduce the duration and severity of cold symptoms when taken regularly. However, it is not a cure and should complement, not replace, medical advice.

3. Bronchitis

Garlic has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, including in cases of bronchitis. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce mucus production and soothe airway irritation. It is often used in combination with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in treating respiratory infections.

4. Cough

Garlic has been traditionally used to alleviate cough symptoms due to its expectorant and antimicrobial properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce respiratory tract infections. Studies suggest it may inhibit bacterial growth and soothe throat irritation. However, it should be used cautiously in high doses to avoid gastrointestinal side effects.

5. Influenza

Garlic has been traditionally used to support immune function and may help reduce the severity and duration of influenza symptoms. Its active compound allicin possesses antimicrobial and antiviral properties that can inhibit the spread of viral infections. Garlic is often recommended as a natural adjunct in managing flu symptoms due to its ability to enhance respiratory health and reduce inflammation.

6. Pneumonia

Garlic has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, including in cases of pneumonia. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties may help combat bacterial infections and reduce inflammation in the lungs. While not a substitute for conventional treatment, garlic can be a complementary remedy when used alongside medical care.

7. Colds

Garlic has been traditionally used to support immune function and may help alleviate cold symptoms due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Allicin, a key compound in garlic, is believed to reduce the severity and duration of colds when consumed regularly. It is often recommended as a natural remedy during the early stages of a cold.

8. Asthma

Garlic has been traditionally used to support respiratory health in herbal medicine. It may help reduce inflammation and mucus production in the airways, potentially easing asthma symptoms. However, its effectiveness varies, and it should not replace prescribed treatments. Always consult a healthcare provider before using garlic for asthma.

9. High blood pressure

Garlic has been shown to help lower high blood pressure due to its ability to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow. Allicin, a key compound in garlic, contributes to this effect by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation. Regular consumption may support healthy blood pressure levels when combined with a balanced diet and lifestyle.

10. High cholesterol

Garlic has been shown to lower total cholesterol and LDL cholesterol levels due to its sulfur compounds, which inhibit cholesterol synthesis. Clinical studies suggest regular consumption may reduce harmful fats in the blood, supporting cardiovascular health. It is often used as a complementary therapy in managing hypercholesterolemia.
