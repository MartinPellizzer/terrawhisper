Jasminum nudiflorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum nudiflorum has arching, deciduous woody stems that produce small, oval, dark green leaves arranged in opposite pairs. The plant typically grows as a sprawling shrub with slender, flexible branches that can reach several feet in length. Unlike many other jasmine species, this plant produces bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom on bare stems before the foliage emerges in spring. The leaves are evergreen in milder climates but often fall during colder periods. Each leaf possesses a smooth margin and a pointed tip, contributing to its dense, bushy appearance once the new growth matures.
