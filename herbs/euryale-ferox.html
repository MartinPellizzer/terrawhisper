What's the taxonomical classification of Euryale ferox?

Euryale ferox belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Nymphaeaales. This plant is further categorized under the family Nymphaeaceae, placing it within the genus Euryale, and is specifically identified by its species name, ferox.

      What are the herbal preparations of Euryale ferox?

      Euryale ferox is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried roots in water to extract its bioactive compounds. This method is preferred because the tough, woody structure of the root requires extended heat to release its medicinal properties. Some practitioners also utilize a tincture by soaking the crushed plant material in high-proof alcohol for several weeks. This liquid extract allows for more concentrated dosing and a longer shelf life compared to aqueous preparations. Infusions are less common due to the plant's density, but they may be used for gentler applications.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Euryale ferox can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of unidentified secondary metabolites. While specific clinical studies on humans are limited, the plant is generally categorized as non-culinary and potentially hazardous. Contact with the foliage may trigger localized dermatitis or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Ingesting the plant parts could lead to nausea, vomiting, or more severe internal discomfort. Therefore, caution is advised when handling or interacting with this species in its natural habitat.
