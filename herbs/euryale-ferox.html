Euryale ferox

What's the taxonomical classification of Euryale ferox?

Euryale ferox belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Nymphaeaales. This plant is further categorized under the family Nymphaeaceae, placing it within the genus Euryale, and is specifically identified by its species name, ferox.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euryale ferox has thick, leathery, evergreen leaves that are elliptical or lanceolate in shape and arranged in a basal rosette. These leaves typically feature prominent veins and a glossy surface that helps the plant retain moisture in its woodland habitat. From the center of the rosette, a single slender stem emerges to support a solitary, white, star-shaped flower. The bloom is characterized by several delicate petals and a central cluster of numerous stamens. Once the flowering period concludes, the plant produces small, fleshy, dark red or black berries that often persist into the winter months.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to eastern North America, ranging from southern Canada through the eastern United States and extending as far south as the Gulf Coast. It commonly populates various wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of slow-moving streams. Within these regions, the species is most frequently encountered in temperate zones characterized by moist soils. What are the pharmacological activities of Euryale ferox?

This plant has anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, apoptotic, and hypoglycemic pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals to prevent cellular damage. The plant also demonstrates anti-inflammatory effects by reducing harmful swelling and irritation in biological systems. Furthermore, it exhibits anti-cancer capabilities by triggering apoptotic processes that lead to programmed cell death in malignant cells. Finally, its hypoglycemic activity assists in lowering blood glucose levels, making it relevant for metabolic studies.

As discussed in a study, Euryale ferox Salisb is a medicinal plant that is effective against different types of cancers. The Euryale ferox Salisb extract (ESE) induces apoptosis in A549 human Caucasian lung carcinoma cancer cells. This pharmacological activity is exerted through the inhibition of the Akt protein and the activation of the p53 protein. The ESE induces apoptosis via the regulation of mitochondrial outer membrane potential and the generation of reactive oxygen species (ROS). These apoptotic effects were also observed in vivo in Balb/c-nu mice bearing A549 xenografts. Ultimately, the results suggest that Euryale ferox Salisb extracts exert anti-cancer effects in a p53-dependent manner.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains one terpenoid, twenty-nine polyphenols, an alkaloid, anthocyanins, and flavonoids. These diverse medicinal compounds work together to provide significant therapeutic benefits to the human body. Additionally, the presence of anthocyanins and flavonoids offers strong antioxidant properties that protect cells from damage. These twenty-nine polyphenols further enhance the complex chemical profile used in various medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, Euryale ferox seeds possess known antioxidant, hypoglycemic, and renal protection effects. Seed germination leads to a 1.7 times increase in total phenol content and a 1.9 times increase in flavonoid content within the extract. This process greatly increases the contents of 29 polyphenols and 1 terpenoid. These medicinal compounds help improve hyperglycemia, abnormal lipid metabolism, and renal tissue lesions in diabetic mice. The extracts improve kidney physiological indicators by regulating the Keap1/Nrf2/HO-1 and AMPK/mTOR pathways.
