Lonchocarpus sericeus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonchocarpus sericeuus has a woody structure characterized by a trunk with relatively smooth bark and a spreading crown of pinnately compound leaves. The leaflets are typically small, elliptical, and arranged in opposite pairs along the rachis to maximize light absorption. Its reproductive morphology includes small, inconspicuous flowers that are often borne in axillary clusters or panicles. The fruit is a legume that develops from a woody pod, which eventually dries and splits to release the seeds. These morphological traits are evolutionary adaptations designed to facilitate survival in seasonally dry tropical environments.
