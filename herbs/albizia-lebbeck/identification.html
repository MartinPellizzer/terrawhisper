Botanical Identification of Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck)

To identify Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Silk Tree, or Albizia lebbeck, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Albizia, and species Albizia lebbeck. This classification places it within the legume family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and medicinal uses.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the Silk Tree is Albizia lebbeck, first described by the botanist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck in 1783. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is also known by several synonyms, including Albizia lebbeci and Albizia lebeckii. Common names such as Silk Tree, Mimosa, and Indian Silk Tree are widely used, while regional and historical names reflect its cultural significance across different regions.

Morphological Characteristics

The Silk Tree, Albizia lebbeck, is a deciduous tree with a tall, straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. It has a deep taproot system and lateral roots that help stabilize the tree. The stem is smooth, greyish-brown, and becomes fissured with age. Leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval leaflets arranged in feathery clusters. The inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a flat, pod-like structure containing several oval seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck) can be distinguished by its bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets, yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters, and flat, fibrous pods. It is often confused with other legumes, but its unique leaf structure and flowering pattern help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

The Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck) is native to southern and eastern Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with warm temperatures and moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 200 and 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck) blooms in spring, typically between March and May, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to October. For identification purposes, seeds are best harvested when the pods turn brown and begin to dry.

Phylogenetic Relationships

The Silk Tree, Albizia lebbeck, is closely related to species within the genus Albizia. It shares characteristics with other legumes in the Fabaceae family, such as pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its close ties to other tropical legumes, highlighting its evolutionary and ecological significance.
