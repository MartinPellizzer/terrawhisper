10 Possible Side Effects Of Albizia Lebbeck (Lebbeck)

Albizia lebbeck has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive issues.

These side effects can be caused by ingesting large quantities of the herb, or using it without proper preparation. For instance, if taken orally in excess, Albizia lebbeck can lead to stomach pain and nausea, making daily activities and work challenging.

In severe cases, it can also cause liver and kidney damage.

1. Increases risk of toxicity

Albizia lebbeck causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of a protein called legumin, which can trigger an immune response and lead to symptoms such as skin rashes, itching, and respiratory issues like asthma or bronchospasms.

Additionally, the plant's sap may contain allergenic compounds that can cause irritation and inflammation upon contact with human skin.

2. Increases risk of toxicity

Albizia lebbeck triggers respiratory distress due to its inherent allergenic and irritating properties.

The plant's extracts contain compounds that can cause bronchospasm, inflammation, and constriction of airways, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath. Additionally, the presence of terpenoids and flavonoids in lebbeck can further exacerbate respiratory issues by increasing mucus production and airway resistance.

As a result, individuals using Albizia lebbeck may experience severe respiratory distress.

Albizia lebbeck induces kidney damage due to its toxic compounds such as flavonoids and alkaloids, which can accumulate in the kidneys and cause nephrotoxicity.

The plant's ability to inhibit certain enzymes involved in protein metabolism may also contribute to its potential to harm kidney function.

Additionally, the plant's high levels of oxalates may increase the risk of kidney stone formation, further compromising kidney health.
