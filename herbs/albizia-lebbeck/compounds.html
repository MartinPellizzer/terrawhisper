Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck) Compounds

The primary compounds of Silk Tree include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Saponins contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while coumarin has antispasmodic qualities. These compounds work synergistically to support the plant's medicinal uses.

Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck) contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help in reducing oxidative stress and supporting overall health. They are commonly found in the bark and leaves of the plant. Flavonoids may also aid in managing stress and promoting relaxation.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antimicrobial effects, enhancing the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and calming effects, commonly used in herbal medicine for stress relief and skin conditions. Saponin's ability to reduce inflammation supports its traditional use in treating various ailments.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating anxiety and insomnia. Coumarin also exhibits antispasmodic and mild sedative properties. Its presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine for calming effects.

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting mental well-being and calming effects.
