Ajuga multiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ajuga multiflora has a creeping growth habit characterized by stoloniferous stems that produce dense, low-lying mats of basal, ovate-to-oblong leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a textured surface with distinct venation and may show varying degrees of pubescence depending on the specific environment. During the flowering period, the plant produces upright, terminal racemes that bear multiple small, zygomorphic flowers. These blossoms are typically tubular in shape and range in color from pale blue to violet. The overall structure allows the plant to spread efficiently across ground surfaces through its vegetative runners.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ajuga multiflora can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched. Some individuals may experience contact dermatitis characterized by redness, itching, or a rash upon touching the leaves. If consumed, the plant might lead to stomach upset or other digestive disturbances due to its chemical composition. There is also a risk of hypersensitivity in people who are allergic to members of the Lamiaceae family. Because specific toxicological data on this particular species is limited, caution is advised when handling it.
