Ammannia auriculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ammannia auriculata?

Ammannia auriculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Myrtales. It is a member of the family Lythraceae, specifically falling under the genus Ammannia, with its unique designation as the species auriculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ammannia auriculata has small, opposite, sessile leaves that feature distinct ear-like lobes at their base and a submerged or floating growth habit.

The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly serrated, helping the plant adapt to aquatic environments. Its stems are slender and often branched, providing structural support as it grows in shallow water. The flowers are relatively tiny and are usually positioned in the axils of the leaves.

These reproductive structures eventually develop into small capsules that facilitate seed dispersal within its wetland habitat.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ammannia auriculata?

Ammannia auriculata has therapeutic actions as a bitter, diuretic, stomachic, febrifuge, and astringent agent.

Its bitter properties help to stimulate the appetite and improve digestive secretions. As a stomachic, the plant aids in normalizing gastric function and supporting overall gastrointestinal health. The diuretic components promote the excretion of excess fluids through the kidneys.

Additionally, its febrifuge qualities assist in reducing fever, while its astringent properties help to tone and tighten body tissues.
