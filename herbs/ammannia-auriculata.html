Ammannia auriculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ammannia auriculata?

Ammannia auriculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Myrtales. It is a member of the family Lythraceae, specifically falling under the genus Ammannia, with its unique designation as the species auriculata.

While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in this family may contain bioactive compounds that trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Contact with the leaves or stems could lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. If consumed, the plant might provoke nausea, stomach cramps, or other digestive disturbances. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid direct contact until comprehensive toxicological profiles are fully established.
