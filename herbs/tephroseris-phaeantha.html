What's the taxonomical classification of Tephroseris phaeantha?

Tephroseris phaeantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, specifically categorized within the genus Tephroseris, and is identified by its unique species name, phaeantha.

      What are the herbal preparations of Tephroseris phaeantha?

      Tephroseris phaeantha is prepared as a decoction, a topical poultice, or an infusion depending on the specific ailment being treated. The plant parts are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal medicinal purposes.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Tephroseris phaeantha can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and localized swelling upon direct physical contact with its foliage or sap. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness or itching if the plant touches their body. Some people might also develop a more severe hypersensitivity reaction if the plant material is inhaled or ingested accidentally. While specific toxicological studies are limited, common characteristics of similar Asteraceae species suggest a potential for contact-based reactions. It is advisable to avoid skin contact to prevent these dermatological issues.
