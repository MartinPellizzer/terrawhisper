Anemone raddeana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone raddeana has basal leaves that are deeply lobed or divided into narrow segments and solitary, actinomorphic flowers characterized by white to pale pinkish tepals. The foliage typically grows in a basal rosette pattern with long petioles that elevate the leaves from the ground. Each flower features multiple prominent stamens surrounding a central cluster of carpels. The plant also possesses a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread through its woodland habitat. These morphological traits help distinguish it from other species within the Ranunculaceae family.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This substance acts as a powerful irritant to the skin and mucous membranes of potential herbivores.
