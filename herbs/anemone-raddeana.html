Anemone raddeana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone raddeana has basal leaves that are deeply lobed or divided into narrow segments and solitary, actinomorphic flowers characterized by white to pale pinkish tepals. The foliage typically grows in a basal rosette pattern with long petioles that elevate the leaves from the ground. Each flower features multiple prominent stamens surrounding a central cluster of carpels. The plant also possesses a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread through its woodland habitat. These morphological traits help distinguish it from other species within the Ranunculaceae family.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Anemone raddeana can cause skin irritation, blistering, and localized inflammation upon direct contact with its sap. This plant contains protoanemonin, a toxic chemical that reacts with skin oils to create irritating substances. If the sap touches the eyes, it may result in intense stinging or temporary vision impairment. Ingesting any part of the plant can lead to severe gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting and abdominal pain. Because of these potential reactions, careful handling is required to prevent accidental injury.
