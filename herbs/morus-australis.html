Morus australis

What's the taxonomical classification of Morus australis?

Morus australis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Moraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Morus, and its specific species designation, australis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Morus australis contains a diverse array of bioactive compounds including flavonoids, alkaloids, tannins, and various phenolic acids. These phytochemicals contribute to the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Specific alkaloids within the species are often studied for their potential effects on metabolic regulation and glucose management. The presence of tannins provides antimicrobial benefits that can assist in combating certain bacterial infections. Research into these chemical constituents aims to better understand their therapeutic applications in modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Morus australis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments like coughs and asthma, digestive issues such as diarrhea, and inflammatory skin conditions. Its bioactive compounds possess antimicrobial and antioxidant properties that help soothe irritated mucous membranes. In traditional herbal practices, extracts are often applied topically to reduce swelling or used orally to manage fever. The bark and leaves are particularly valued for their ability to mitigate symptoms of various bacterial infections. This diverse medicinal profile makes it a significant resource in ethnobotanical studies.
