What is Centella capensis?

Centella capensis is an annual or perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It has palmately lobed, crenate leaves that are alternate and pinnate, with leaflets typically 3-5 in number. The plant produces small white or pinkish flowers in umbels, with 5-7 rays and 5-7 flowers per ray, and is dioecious, meaning the male and female flowers are separate. The fruit is a schizocarp, consisting of two separate mericarps, each containing a single seed.

Centella capensis is native to South Africa and is also known as lion's tail or wild potato.

The possible side effects of using Centella capensis improperly include experiencing nausea due to its saponin and triterpenoid content, which can irritate the stomach and cause gastrointestinal discomfort.

This plant has also been linked to hepatotoxicity, specifically liver damage, resulting from its high concentration of triterpenoids, which can induce oxidative stress and disrupt liver function. Furthermore, excessive consumption of Centella capensis can lead to skin irritation, such as rashes and dermatitis, due to its high saponin content, which can cause skin inflammation and allergic reactions. Additionally, high doses of Centella capensis have been reported to cause seizures in some individuals, likely due to its neurotoxic effects, which can disrupt normal neurotransmitter function and lead to convulsions.

These adverse reactions highlight the importance of proper dosing and consultation with a healthcare professional when using Centella capensis for medicinal purposes.
