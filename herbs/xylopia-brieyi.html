Xylopia brieyi

What's the taxonomical classification of Xylopia brieyi?

Xylopia brieyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Magnoliales, the plant is further categorized into the family Annonaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed within the genus Xylopia, under the specific species name brieyi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Xylopia brieyi contains a diverse range of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, terpenoids, saponins, tannins, and flavonoids. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the bark, leaves, and roots of the tree. Research indicates that the presence of these compounds contributes to the plant's significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Many traditional medicinal practices utilize these extracts to treat ailments such as fever, stomach aches, and skin infections. The synergistic effect of these diverse phytochemicals makes the plant a valuable subject for pharmacological studies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Xylopia brieyi?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat malaria, skin infections, stomach aches, coughs, and various types of inflammatory conditions. The leaves and bark are frequently prepared as decoctions to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and fever. Local communities also apply crushed parts of the plant topically to combat fungal infections and wounds. Its medicinal properties are believed to extend to managing respiratory issues like bronchitis or persistent coughs. These applications highlight its significant role in traditional herbal medicine across West Africa.
