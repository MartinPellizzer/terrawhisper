What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paspalum conjugatum has stoloniferous growth habits with slender, creeping stems that produce dense, low-growing mats of spreading foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, featuring a characteristic midrib and a texture that can range from smooth to slightly scabrous. Each stem produces small, branched panicles that carry numerous delicate spikelets arranged along the branches. These spikelets are small and oblong, often appearing in clusters that give the inflorescence a loose, airy appearance. The plant's ability to spread through these horizontal runners allows it to colonize open ground effectively.
