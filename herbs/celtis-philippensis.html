Celtis philippensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celtris philippensis has an upright tree structure characterized by an alternate phyllotaxy of simple, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and prominent pinnate venation. The bark typically presents a smooth texture that becomes slightly fissured as the specimen matures. Its reproductive morphology involves small, inconspicuous flowers that are borne in axillary clusters. The resulting fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that transitions in color as it reaches ripeness. These structural features collectively define the plant's vegetative and reproductive identity within its ecosystem.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across India, Myanmar, Thailand, and parts of China. Within these habitats, it is often found in well-drained soils ranging from lowlands to moderate elevations. The distribution is largely influenced by monsoon patterns and the availability of seasonal rainfall. What are the herbal preparations of Celtis philippensis?

Celtis philippensis is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a juice extracted from the leaves, or a topical paste made from the crushed fruit. These various preparations are utilized in traditional medicine to address diverse ailments ranging from skin conditions to internal digestive issues. When the bark is boiled in water, the resulting liquid serves as a potent medicinal tea for systemic treatment. The leaf juice is often applied directly to affected areas to provide soothing relief for localized inflammation. Additionally, the pulp from the fruit can be processed into a soft mass for external applications.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Celtis philippensis can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. While specific clinical trials are limited, traditional use suggests that improper dosage may lead to stomach upset or nausea. Some users might experience localized dermatological issues if the plant material comes into direct contact with the skin. It is essential to monitor for any unexpected bodily responses when using this species for medicinal purposes.
