Celtis philippensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis philippensis?

Celtis philippensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Rosales and belongs to the family Cannabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Celtis, and its specific species designation, philippensis.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Celitis philippensis contains medicinal properties found in its bark, leaves, and roots. The bark is frequently utilized in traditional remedies to treat various ailments including skin diseases and inflammation. Extracts from the leaves are often applied or ingested to manage digestive issues and fever. The roots are also valued in folk medicine for their potential to serve as a remedy for specific infections. These various components work together to provide a wide range of therapeutic applications in different cultures.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its diverse therapeutic properties.

The presence of specific alkaloids is often linked to its ability to interact with various biological systems in the body. Flavonoids and phenolic compounds within the plant provide significant antioxidant activity by neutralizing free radicals. Additionally, the terpenoids found in its tissues assist in producing anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Together, these bioactive constituents form the chemical basis for its traditional medicinal uses.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Alkaloids
  • Flavonoids
  • Terpenoids
  • Phenolic compounds

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Celtis philippensis targets various enzymes and receptors including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various inflammatory cytokines to mediate its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive phytochemicals like flavonoids and alkaloids that inhibit the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway to reduce inflammation. These compounds also demonstrate an ability to modulate nitric oxide synthase and modulate various signaling kinases within cellular pathways. Such interactions contribute to its documented pharmacological activities, particularly its antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. By suppressing oxidative stress through the regulation of antioxidant enzymes, the plant helps protect cells from damage.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celtis philippensis?

Celtis philippensis has potent medicinal properties that include antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing activities primarily derived from its bark and leaves. The plant contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and alleviate pain in various inflammatory conditions. Its extracts demonstrate effectiveness in combating bacterial infections, making it a useful traditional remedy for skin ailments. Furthermore, the botanical constituents assist in accelerating tissue regeneration during the healing of cuts and sores. These therapeutic actions are widely utilized in traditional medicine to manage systemic ailments and localized injuries.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, inflammation, digestive issues, and fever.

The bark or leaves are often prepared into decoctions or poultices to soothe various dermatological conditions. It acts as an anti-inflammatory agent to reduce swelling and pain in different parts of the body. When consumed, the plant helps stabilize the digestive system and alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort.

Additionally, it serves as a traditional remedy to help lower high body temperatures during a fever.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Skin diseases
  • Inflammation
  • Digestive issues
  • Fever

What are the herbal preparations of Celtis philippensis?

Celtis philippensis is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a juice extracted from the leaves, or a topical paste made from the crushed fruit. These various preparations are utilized in traditional medicine to address diverse ailments ranging from skin conditions to internal digestive issues. When the bark is boiled in water, the resulting liquid serves as a potent medicinal tea for systemic treatment. The leaf juice is often applied directly to affected areas to provide soothing relief for localized inflammation. Additionally, the pulp from the fruit can be processed into a soft mass for external applications.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Celtis philippensis can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. While specific clinical trials are limited, traditional use suggests that improper dosage may lead to stomach upset or nausea. Some users might experience localized dermatological issues if the plant material comes into direct contact with the skin. It is essential to monitor for any unexpected bodily responses when using this species for medicinal purposes. Always consult a healthcare professional before incorporating unknown botanical substances into a wellness regimen.
