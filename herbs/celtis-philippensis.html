Celtis philippensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis philippensis?

Celtis philippensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Rosales and belongs to the family Cannabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Celtis, and its specific species designation, philippensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Celtis philippensis contains bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and steroids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the plant's documented antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers have identified various phenolic compounds within the bark and leaves that help mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. The presence of these diverse secondary metabolites supports the traditional use of the plant in treating skin ailments and gastrointestinal issues. Further studies suggest that these compounds interact with cellular pathways to provide significant pharmacological benefits.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celtis philippensis?

Celtis philippensis has potent medicinal properties that include antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing activities primarily derived from its bark and leaves. The plant contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and alleviate pain in various inflammatory conditions. Its extracts demonstrate effectiveness in combating bacterial infections, making it a useful traditional remedy for skin ailments. Furthermore, the botanical constituents assist in accelerating tissue regeneration during the healing of cuts and sores. These therapeutic actions are widely utilized in traditional medicine to manage systemic ailments and localized injuries.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, digestive disorders, respiratory ailments, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and heal wounds. When prepared as a decoction, it can help alleviate issues within the gastrointestinal tract. It is also utilized in traditional practices to manage symptoms related to coughs and breathing difficulties. These medicinal properties make it a versatile resource in regional herbal medicine.

What are the herbal preparations of Celtis philippensis?

Celtis philippensis is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a juice extracted from the leaves, or a topical paste made from the crushed fruit. These various preparations are utilized in traditional medicine to address diverse ailments ranging from skin conditions to internal digestive issues. When the bark is boiled in water, the resulting liquid serves as a potent medicinal tea for systemic treatment. The leaf juice is often applied directly to affected areas to provide soothing relief for localized inflammation. Additionally, the pulp from the fruit can be processed into a soft mass for external applications.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Celtis philippensis can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. While specific clinical trials are limited, traditional use suggests that improper dosage may lead to stomach upset or nausea. Some users might experience localized dermatological issues if the plant material comes into direct contact with the skin. It is essential to monitor for any unexpected bodily responses when using this species for medicinal purposes. Always consult a healthcare professional before incorporating unknown botanical substances into a wellness regimen.
