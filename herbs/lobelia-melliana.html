Lobelia melliana

What's the taxonomical classification of Lobelia melliana?

Lobelia melliana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Campanulaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its assignment to the genus Lobelia, with the species name being melliana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lobelia melliana contains bioactive alkaloids, primarily lobeline, alongside various glycosides and phenolic compounds that facilitate its medicinal properties. These chemical constituents function as potent emetics and expectorants when processed for therapeutic use. The presence of specific alkaloids allows the plant to interact with nicotinic acetylcholine receptors in the human body. Additionally, the secondary metabolites found in its tissues contribute to its traditional roles in treating respiratory ailments and digestive issues. This complex chemical profile makes the species a significant subject in ethnobotanical research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lobelia melliana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as asthma, bronchitis, and persistent coughing. Its primary therapeutic function involves acting as an expectorant to help clear mucus from the lungs. The plant contains alkaloids that can relax the bronchial muscles to ease breathing difficulties. Historically, practitioners have also utilized its properties to soothe irritation in the throat. Careful dosage is essential because the active components can be toxic if consumed in excess.
