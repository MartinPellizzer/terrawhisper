Deparia lancea

What's the taxonomical classification of Deparia lancea?

Deparia lancea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, it falls under the order Polypodiales. This plant is specifically situated within the family Aspleniaceae, belonging to the genus Deparia, with its specific designation being the species lancea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Deparia lancea has lanceolate to narrowly ovate fronds that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length with pinnate division into several pairs of lobes. The stipes are long and slender, often featuring a dark brown or blackish coloration near the base. Each pinna is characterized by serrated or slightly lobed margins and a distinctively tapered shape. The venation pattern is prominent, with veins branching toward the edges of the leaf segments. Small, clustered sori are located on the undersides of the pinnules to facilitate spore production.
