Sweet Flag (Acorus Triqueter)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Acorus triqueter.

Sweet flag, scientifically known as Acorus triqueter, is a medicinal herb native to wetlands and marshy areas, valued for its aromatic rhizomes and unique properties. It is traditionally used as an adaptogen and digestive stimulant, offering primary benefits such as enhancing cognitive function, supporting digestion, and reducing inflammation. In ancient Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine, sweet flag was employed to treat respiratory issues, nervous disorders, and as a tonic for vitality. Modern wellness applications include its use in aromatherapy, herbal supplements, and as a flavoring agent in traditional foods, owing to its distinctive spicy, camphor-like aroma. This rare plant contains the active compound acorone, which has been studied for its potential neuroprotective effects, and it has a long history of use dating back thousands of years in various cultures.
