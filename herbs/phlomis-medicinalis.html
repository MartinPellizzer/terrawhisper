Phlomis medicinalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Phlomis medicinalis?

Phlomis medicinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Ultimately, its specific identity is defined by its placement within the genus Phlomis, resulting in its scientific name, Phlomis medicinalis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis medicinalis has a perennial, rhizomatous habit featuring upright, square stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. The leaves are large, ovate, and covered in dense, greyish-white hairs that give them a soft, velvety texture. These leaves are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem, often appearing somewhat rugose or wrinkled on the surface. At the top of the plant, the inflorescence consists of dense, whorled clusters of tubular, yellowish-green flowers. Each flower possesses a prominent upper lip and a smaller lower lip, characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. The plant's overall structure is robust and distinctly herbaceous, adapted to well-drained, rocky environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds phloyoside II, sesamoside, shanzhicide methyl ester, 6-O-acetyl-shanzhicide methyl ester, and 8-O-acetyl-shanzhicide methyl ester. These specific chemical constituents belong to various classes of bioactive phytochemicals found within the Phlomis medicinalis species. Researchers often study these molecules to understand their potential therapeutic properties and biological activities. The presence of these diverse derivatives highlights the complex chemical profile of the plant. Such detailed composition is essential for evaluating its traditional and modern medicinal uses.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", eight compounds were isolated and elucidated from the root of Phlomis medicinalis. These compounds include 5-Hydroxy-7-methoxy-4, 6-dimethylphthalide (1), 4-hydroxymethyl-2-furaldehyde (2), and six iridoid glucosides. The six iridoid glucosides consist of 6-O-acetyl-shanzhiside methyl ester (3), 8-O-acetyl-shanzhiside methyl ester (4), shanzhiside methyl ester (5), sesamoside (6), phloyoside II (7), and dehydropentstemoside (8). All 8 compounds were isolated from the plant for the first time. Additionally, compounds 1 and 3 were obtained from the plants of Phlomis for the first time.
