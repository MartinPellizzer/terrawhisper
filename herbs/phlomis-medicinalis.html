Phlomis medicinalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Phlomis medicinalis?

Phlomis medicinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Ultimately, its specific identity is defined by its placement within the genus Phlomis, resulting in its scientific name, Phlomis medicinalis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis medicinalis has a perennial, rhizomatous habit featuring upright, square stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. The leaves are large, ovate, and covered in dense, greyish-white hairs that give them a soft, velvety texture. These leaves are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem, often appearing somewhat rugose or wrinkled on the surface. At the top of the plant, the inflorescence consists of dense, whorled clusters of tubular, yellowish-green flowers. Each flower possesses a prominent upper lip and a smaller lower lip, characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. The plant's overall structure is robust and distinctly herbaceous, adapted to well-drained, rocky environments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Phlomis medicinalis?

Phlomis medicinalis has therapeutic actions as a bitter, diuretic, emmenagogue, diaphoretic, and expectorant agent used to treat various systemic ailments. Finally, its expectorant qualities aid in the clearing of mucus from the respiratory tract to alleviate coughs.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 6-O-acetyl-shanzhiside methyl ester, 8-O-acetyl-shanzhiside methyl ester, dehydropentstemoside, phloyoside II, and sesamoside. These specific phytochemicals are often classified as iridoid glycosides or phenylethanoid glycosides. Such compounds are frequently studied for their potential pharmacological activities in various biological systems. The presence of these diverse molecules contributes to the complex chemical profile of the plant. Researchers analyze these constituents to understand how they interact with human physiological processes.

As discussed in a study, the root of Phlomis medicinalis contains 8 compounds that were isolated and purified using macroporous resin, silica gel column chromatography, TLC and Prep-HPLC. The identified medicinal compounds include 5-Hydroxy-7-methoxy-4, 6-dimethylphthalide (1) and 4-hydroxymethyl-2-furaldehyde (2). Additionally, there are 6 iridoid glucosides consisting of 6-O-acetyl-shanzhiside methyl ester (3), 8-O-acetyl-shanzhiside methyl ester (4), shanzhiside methyl ester (5), sesamoside (6), phloyoside II (7) and dehydropentstemoside (8). All 8 of these compounds were isolated from the plant for the first time. Furthermore, compounds 1 and 3 were obtained from the plants of Phlomis for the first time.
