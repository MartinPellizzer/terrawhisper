What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains steroidal alkaloids, such as hookerianine and hookerianine B, which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These specific chemical constituents are known for demonstrating significant biological activities in pharmacological studies. Researchers have identified that these alkaloids contribute to the plant's diverse therapeutic potential. Beyond these major substances, other secondary metabolites are also present within the species. Understanding these components is essential for developing new treatments derived from the plant.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", the medicinal plant Sarcococca hookeriana of Nepalese origin has resulted in the isolation of 17 active steroidal alkaloids labeled 1-17. Compounds 1, 2, and 10 underwent derivatization to produce five chemically derived derivatives known as 1a, 2a, 10a, 10b, and 10c. These natural compounds and their derivatives demonstrated antileishmanial properties with IC50 values ranging from 0.20 to 61.44 microg mL(-1). For comparison, the IC50 value of the standard drug amphotericin B was 0.12 microg mL(-1). The study indicated that functionalities in ring A of these steroidal alkaloids play a characteristic role in increasing antileishmanial activity.
