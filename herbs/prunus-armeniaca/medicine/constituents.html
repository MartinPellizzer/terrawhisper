10 Medicinal Constituents Of Prunus Armeniaca (Apricot)

Prunus armeniaca has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils.

These constituents possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The flavonoids and phenolic acids help protect against cardiovascular diseases and certain cancers, while the essential oils soothe digestive issues and promote skin health.

For example, apricot kernel oil has been used to alleviate symptoms of eczema and dermatitis, improving skin quality and reducing inflammation.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Prunus armeniaca.

1. Amygdalin

Prunus armeniaca amygdalin is a naturally occurring compound found in the fruit's kernel, pit, and leaves.

It is a cyanogenic glycoside, consisting of glucose, mandelonitrile, and hydrogen cyanide molecules. Amygdalin has been studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer properties.

However, its effects on humans are not yet fully understood and further research is needed to determine its safety and efficacy as a therapeutic agent.

2. Quercetin

Prunus armeniaca quercetin is a type of flavonoid compound that is abundant in the fruit.

It has been widely studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Quercetin has been shown to have protective effects against cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurological disorders, making it a valuable component of a healthy diet.

3. Chlorogenic acid

Prunus armeniaca chlorogenic acid is a naturally occurring phenolic compound found in its fruit and leaves.

This bioactive agent has been extensively studied for its numerous health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Chlorogenic acid has also been shown to possess antidiabetic and cardiovascular protective effects, making it a potential natural remedy for various diseases.

Its presence in apricot products may contribute to the fruit's reputation as a healthy and nutritious snack.

4. Caffeic acid

Prunus armeniaca caffeic acid is a phenolic compound that has been isolated from its fruit.

This antioxidant exhibits various biological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a potential agent in the prevention of chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Additionally, caffeic acid has been found to inhibit the growth of certain microorganisms, demonstrating its efficacy against bacterial and fungal infections.

5. Kaempferol

Prunus armeniaca kaempferol is a flavonoid compound extracted from the fruit and leaves of the tree.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-cancer properties. Kaempferol has been shown to inhibit the growth of cancer cells and induce apoptosis (cell death) in various types of cancer.

Additionally, it has been found to possess antiviral activity and may help protect against cardiovascular disease.

6. Gallic acid

Prunus armeniaca gallic acid is a phenolic compound that plays a crucial role in its nutritional profile.

It is a powerful antioxidant responsible for the fruit's ability to protect against oxidative stress and inflammation.

Gallic acid has been shown to exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties, making it a valuable component of apricot extract used in various applications including food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics.

7. Ferulic acid

Prunus armeniaca ferulic acid is a potent antioxidant compound found in its fruit, leaves, and pit.

It exhibits numerous biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiproliferative properties.

Ferulic acid has been shown to protect against oxidative stress, cardiovascular disease, and certain types of cancer by scavenging free radicals and inhibiting enzymes involved in lipid peroxidation and tumor growth.

8. Benzaldehyde

Prunus armeniaca benzaldehyde is a major volatile compound responsible for its characteristic sweet and almond-like aroma.

It is produced in small amounts during the ripening process of fresh apricots and is a key contributor to their distinctive flavor and fragrance.

Benzaldehyde is often used as a flavoring agent in food products, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals, and has also been shown to have antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

9. Hydrogen cyanide

Prunus armeniaca hydrogen cyanide is a naturally occurring toxic compound found in the fruit's pit and leaves.

It can be released when the pit is crushed or chewed, causing respiratory problems, nausea, and even death if ingested.

Hydrogen cyanide is particularly high in apricot kernels, which are often consumed as a natural remedy in some cultures despite the risks.

10. Glucose

Prunus armeniaca glucose is a type of disaccharide sugar found in high concentrations within the fruit's pulp and juice.

It plays a crucial role in the ripening process, acting as a natural sweetener that enhances the flavor and aroma of the fruit. Apricot glucose has been shown to possess antioxidant properties, making it a valuable component in traditional medicine and food products.

Its unique characteristics also make it an attractive ingredient for various industries, including pharmaceuticals and cosmetics.
