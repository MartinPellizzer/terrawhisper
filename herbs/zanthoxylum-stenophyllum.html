Zanthoxylum stenophyllum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zanthoxylum stenophyllum has pinnately compound leaves with small, narrow leaflets that are typically lanceolate or linear in shape. The plant exhibits a woody stem structure characteristic of its genus, often featuring small prickles or spines on the branches. Its flowers are small and arranged in complex inflorescences that emerge from the axillary regions. The fruit consists of small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they reach maturity. These morphological features help distinguish the species from other members of the Rutaceae family.
