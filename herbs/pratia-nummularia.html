What's the taxonomical classification of Pratia nummularia?

Pratia nummularia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Asterales and falls within the family Campanulaceae. Finally, its specific biological identification is defined by the genus Pratia and the species nummularia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pratia nummularia has small, rounded, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green and grow in a dense, creeping mat. These leaves are often coin-shaped and possess smooth margins with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces tiny, tubular white or pale blue flowers that emerge from the foliage on slender stalks. Its stems are trailing and prostrate, allowing the plant to spread rapidly across the ground via stolons. This low-growing habit makes the plant an effective groundcover in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
