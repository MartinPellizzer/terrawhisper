What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pratia nummularia has small, rounded, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green and grow in a dense, creeping mat. The plant produces tiny, tubular white or pale blue flowers that emerge from the foliage on slender stalks. Its stems are trailing and prostrate, allowing the plant to spread rapidly across the ground via stolons. This low-growing habit makes the plant an effective groundcover in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
