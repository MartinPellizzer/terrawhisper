Pratia nummularia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pratia nummularia has small, rounded, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green and grow in a dense, creeping mat. These leaves are often coin-shaped and possess smooth margins with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces tiny, tubular white or pale blue flowers that emerge from the foliage on slender stalks. Its stems are trailing and prostrate, allowing the plant to spread rapidly across the ground via stolons. This low-growing habit makes the plant an effective groundcover in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of southern China. It thrives primarily in humid lowland forests and disturbed habitats within these specific latitudes. The species is commonly found growing in moist, shaded environments where it can climb using its creeping stems. Its distribution is closely tied to the warm, wet climates characteristic of the Indomalayan realm. Because it prefers high humidity, it is rarely encountered in arid or temperate zones.
