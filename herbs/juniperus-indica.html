Juniperus indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Juniperus indica?

Juniperus indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae, following the biological order Pinales. As a member of the family Cupressaceae, it falls under the genus Juniperus, with the specific designation of the species indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juniperus indica has an upright, evergreen growth habit characterized by scale-like leaves and woody, spreading branches. The foliage consists of small, overlapping, decussate leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed along the stems. Its branches often exhibit a somewhat irregular or spreading architecture depending on the specific cultivar or environment. Small, berry-like cones develop from the plant, turning a bluish-purple color when they reach maturity. The bark is generally thin and brownish, eventually becoming somewhat fissured on older specimens.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, spanning across northern India, Nepal, Bhutan, and parts of southern Tibet. It typically thrives in mountainous terrain at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters above sea level. These populations are often found on rocky slopes and dry, temperate ridges where drainage is excellent. The species is widely distributed across the subalpine zones of the Himalayan mountain range. Its presence is closely linked to the specific climatic conditions found in these high-elevation ecosystems.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be processed into an essential oil, an infusion, a decoction, or a tincture to harness its medicinal properties.
