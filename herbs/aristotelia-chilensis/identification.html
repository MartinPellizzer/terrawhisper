Morphological Characteristics

Chilean Wineberry is a shrub with a compact growth habit. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, adapting to rocky soils. The stems are woody and branched, with a rough texture. Leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a bluish-green color. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small white flowers. The fruit is a red, fleshy berry with a single seed inside.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chilean Wineberry can be distinguished by its distinctive red, fleshy berries that resemble small grapes, its simple, alternate leaves with a leathery texture, and its small, yellowish flowers. It is also notable for its unique habitat preference, growing primarily in coastal regions of Chile. These features help differentiate it from other similar species in the same family.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chilean Wineberry is native to southern Chile and adjacent regions of Argentina. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained soils, often found in coastal areas. It typically grows at altitudes between 0 and 1,000 meters.

Chilean Wineberry blooms from late autumn to early winter, typically between September and November. Seed formation occurs in the following spring, from late winter to early spring. Harvest timing for identification purposes is during late spring to early summer, when fruits are fully mature and ready for collection.
