Chilean Wineberry (Aristotelia chilensis) Compounds

The primary compounds in Chilean Wineberry include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Tannins and anthocyanins provide astringent and anti-inflammatory effects, while coumarin and glycosides may support blood circulation. Saponins, terpenoids, and essential oils add additional therapeutic benefits, and alkaloids may influence nervous system function.

Chilean Wineberry (Aristotelia chilensis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Chilean Wineberry (Aristotelia chilensis) contribute to its antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health. They are naturally present in the berries and may aid in reducing inflammation and oxidative stress. Flavonoids are a key component of the plant's medicinal value.

Phenolic acid in Chilean Wineberry (Aristotelia chilensis) contributes to its antioxidant properties. This compound helps neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health. 3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chilean Wineberry (Aristotelia chilensis). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antioxidant effects, potentially aiding in inflammation reduction and tissue repair.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Chilean Wineberry (Aristotelia chilensis).
