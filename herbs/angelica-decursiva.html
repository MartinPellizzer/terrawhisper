Angelica decursiva

What's the taxonomical classification of Angelica decursiva?

Angelica decursiva belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, this plant is placed within the genus Angelica, ultimately identified by its specific species epithet, decursiva.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Angelica decursiva contains high concentrations of coumarins, specifically decursin and decursinol angelate, which serve as its primary bioactive constituents. These specific compounds are credited with providing significant neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory effects in various biological models. Research suggests that these molecules may help mitigate oxidative stress and protect nerve cells from damage. Additionally, the plant possesses antioxidant properties that support overall cellular health. These chemical profiles make the species a subject of interest in pharmacological studies focused on cognitive enhancement.

What are the therapeutic actions of Angelica decursiva?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various gynecological disorders including menstrual irregularities, dysmenorrhea, and infertility. It is also applied in traditional medicine to address circulatory issues and improve blood flow. Practitioners often utilize its properties to alleviate symptoms of anemia and promote overall blood health. Additionally, it may be used to combat certain skin conditions and inflammatory responses. These therapeutic applications rely on the plant's ability to regulate and nourish the blood.
