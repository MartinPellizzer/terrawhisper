Angelica decursiva

What's the taxonomical classification of Angelica decursiva?

Angelica decursiva belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, this plant is placed within the genus Angelica, ultimately identified by its specific species epithet, decursiva.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angelica decursiva has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and features leaves with long, decurrent petioles that extend down the stem. The foliage is characterized by large, compound leaves that are divided into multiple leaflets with serrated margins. Small, white flowers are arranged in dense, terminal umbels consisting of numerous tiny individual florets. The plant produces small, oblong fruits that turn from green to brown upon maturity. Its root system is robust and fleshy, providing the structural foundation for its upright growth habit.

What are the pharmacological activities of Angelica decursiva?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antitussive, and expectorant pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents work to alleviate pain and reduce swelling in various bodily tissues. The antioxidant properties help protect cells from oxidative stress caused by free radicals. Additionally, the plant acts as an effective remedy for respiratory issues by suppressing coughs and thinning mucus. These combined effects make it a significant subject for therapeutic applications in traditional medicine.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the roots of Angelica decursiva have been used in traditional medicine as anti-inflammatory, antitussive, analgesic agents and expectorant for conditions like cough, asthma, bronchitis and upper respiratory tract infections. Water and 70% ethanol extracts of the roots showed considerable inhibitory activity against LPS-induced lung inflammation in mice following oral administration at a dose of 400 mg/kg. Five coumarin derivatives were isolated from the plant, including columbianadin, umbelliferone, umbelliferone 6-carboxylic acid, nodakenin and nodakenetin. The constituent columbianadin possesses strong inhibitory activity against the inflammatory response of IL-1β-treated A549 cells and LPS-treated MH-S cells. Additionally, columbianadin was proved to possess significant inhibitory activity against LPS-induced lung inflammation following oral administration at a dose of 20-60 mg/kg.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 6-formyl umbelliferone, bergapten, imperatorin, nodakenetin, and scopoleton. These specific chemical constituents are classified as coumarins and are primarily responsible for the plant's therapeutic properties. Research indicates that these substances contribute to various pharmacological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The presence of these bioactive molecules makes Angelica decursiva a significant subject in traditional and modern medicine. Studying the synergy of these compounds helps scientists understand how the plant interacts with biological systems.

According to a study published by "Plant physiology and biochemistry : PPB", Angelica decursiva is an important traditional medicinal plant that possesses unique pharmacological activity. Its principal active ingredients are coumarins, including scopoletin, bergapten, and imperatorin. The biosynthesis of these furanocoumarins involves 2 O-methyltransferases, identified as AdOMT1 and AdOMT2, which were found after screening 14 candidate O-methyltransferases. AdOMT1 showed a higher catalytic efficiency for bergaptol with a Kcat/Km value of 3123.70. Key catalytic residues for the O-methylation of bergaptol and xanthotoxol in these enzymes include His126/Asn132, Phe171/Phe177, Trp261/Trp267, and Asn312/Ile317.
