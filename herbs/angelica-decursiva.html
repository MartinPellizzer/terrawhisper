Angelica decursiva

What's the taxonomical classification of Angelica decursiva?

Angelica decursiva belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, this plant is placed within the genus Angelica, ultimately identified by its specific species epithet, decursiva.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angelica decursiva has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and features leaves with long, decurrent petioles that extend down the stem.

The foliage is characterized by large, compound leaves that are divided into multiple leaflets with serrated margins. Small, white flowers are arranged in dense, terminal umbels consisting of numerous tiny individual florets. The plant produces small, oblong fruits that turn from green to brown upon maturity.

Its root system is robust and fleshy, providing the structural foundation for its upright growth habit.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Angelica decursiva contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its roots and rhizomes. These underground structures are harvested and processed to extract essential oils and coumarins used in traditional therapies. Practitioners often utilize these specific parts to treat various ailments related to blood circulation and inflammation. The chemical constituents found within the root help regulate physiological functions and support overall wellness. Consequently, the root serves as the most significant therapeutic component of the entire plant.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains coumarins, flavonoids, phenolic acids, triterpenoids, and alkaloids that contribute to its diverse pharmacological profile.

The presence of various coumarins and flavonoids provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Phenolic acids within the plant structure support cellular protection against oxidative stress. Additionally, the triterpenoids and alkaloids contribute to its complex biological effects, including potential antimicrobial properties.

These combined chemical constituents make the species a subject of interest in traditional and modern medicinal studies.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Angelica decursiva targets several key signaling pathways and enzymes, most notably inhibiting nitric oxide synthase, cyclooxygenase-2, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to reduce systemic inflammation. Its primary bioactive compound, decursin, exerts significant influence by modulating the NF-kappaB signaling pathway and suppressing the expression of inducible nitric oxide synthase. Furthermore, the plant interacts with the PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway, which plays a critical role in regulating cell proliferation and survival in various disease models. These molecular interactions also extend to the modulation of mitochondrial function and oxidative stress responses through the regulation of antioxidant enzymes. By targeting these specific protein networks, the plant achieves its diverse pharmacological effects, including anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, and neuroprotective properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Angelica decursiva?

Angelica decursiva has therapeutic properties that include diuretic, bitter, carminative, stomachic, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic actions.

Its bitter components stimulate the digestive system, functioning effectively as a stomachic to improve appetite and digestion. The plant acts as a carminative to reduce intestinal gas and provides antispasmodic effects to soothe muscle contractions. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory properties help mitigate swelling and irritation within the body.

Finally, its diuretic action assists in increasing urine production to support renal function and fluid balance.

What are the herbal preparations of Angelica decursiva?

Angelica decursiva is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the dried roots in water, though it can also be processed into powders, tinctures, or topical extracts. The root is the most significant part of the plant used in traditional herbal medicine to extract its active constituents. When making a decoction, the roots are simmered for an extended period to ensure the medicinal properties are released into the liquid. Tinctures provide a more concentrated form of the herb by using alcohol as a solvent to capture specific compounds. These various preparations allow for different methods of administration depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Angelica decursiva can cause photosensitivity, gastrointestinal upset, and potential allergic skin reactions in sensitive individuals. The plant contains furanocoumarins, which are compounds known to increase the skin's vulnerability to ultraviolet light. This reaction can lead to redness, itching, or blistering if the skin is exposed to sunlight after contact or consumption. Some users may also experience nausea, diarrhea, or abdominal discomfort following ingestion. It is important to consult a medical professional before using this herb to avoid these adverse responses.
