Artocarpus integer

What's the taxonomical classification of Artocarpus integer?

Artocarpus integer belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Moraceae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Artocarpus and the specific species designation integer.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artocarpus integer has large, coriaceous leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape, possessing an entire margin and an acute to acuminate apex. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and paler beneath, with a distinctively smooth or slightly leathery texture. The plant features a woody trunk that can reach significant heights, often supporting a dense, spreading canopy. Its reproductive structures consist of fleshy, syncarpous fruits that are covered in small, soft protuberances. The flowers are small and arranged in axillary inflorescences, characteristic of the Moraceae family.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Artocarpus integer targets various inflammatory enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha). The bioactive flavonoids and polyphenols within the fruit act as potent inhibitors of these specific proteins to reduce systemic inflammation. By suppressing the COX-2 enzyme, the plant's compounds mitigate the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. Furthermore, the modulation of TNF-alpha and iNOS pathways suggests significant potential for managing autoimmune responses and oxidative stress. These molecular interactions collectively contribute to the plant's observed antioxidant and anti-inflammatory medicinal properties.

Artocarpus integer has therapeutic properties including astringent, antidiarrheal, antipyretic, and anti-inflammatory actions that are often utilized in traditional medicine.
