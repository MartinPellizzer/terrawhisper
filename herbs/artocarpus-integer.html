Artocarpus integer

What's the taxonomical classification of Artocarpus integer?

Artocarpus integer belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Moraceae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Artocarpus and the specific species designation integer.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Artocarpus integer contains medicinal properties found primarily in its bark, leaves, and fruit pulp. The bark is frequently decocted to treat various ailments such as skin infections and stomach disorders. Extracts from the leaves are sometimes applied topically or ingested to manage inflammation and fever. The fruit itself is utilized for its nutritional benefits and as a remedy for certain digestive issues. These diverse plant parts contribute to a wide range of traditional therapeutic applications.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains flavonoids, terpenoids, steroids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its diverse therapeutic properties.

These bioactive constituents are primarily concentrated in the fruit, leaves, and bark of the species. The presence of phenolic compounds and flavonoids provides significant antioxidant activities that help neutralize free radicals in the body. Additionally, the terpenoids and steroids within the plant structure are associated with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Alkaloids further enhance the medicinal profile by interacting with various biological pathways to offer potential healing benefits.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Flavonoids
  • Terpenoids
  • Phenolic compounds
  • Steroids
  • Alkaloids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Artocarpus integer targets various inflammatory enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha). The bioactive flavonoids and polyphenols within the fruit act as potent inhibitors of these specific proteins to reduce systemic inflammation. By suppressing the COX-2 enzyme, the plant's compounds mitigate the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. Furthermore, the modulation of TNF-alpha and iNOS pathways suggests significant potential for managing autoimmune responses and oxidative stress. These molecular interactions collectively contribute to the plant's observed antioxidant and anti-inflammatory medicinal properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Artocarpus integer?

Artocarpus integer has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties that contribute to its use in treating various ailments. The plant contains bioactive compounds like flavonoids and phenols that help neutralize free radicals in the body. These chemical constituents allow the fruit and bark to reduce oxidative stress and mitigate inflammation in tissues. Additionally, its natural extracts demonstrate the ability to inhibit the growth of certain pathogenic bacteria. Such therapeutic actions suggest its potential role in supporting digestive health and overall immune function.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat diarrhea, dysentery, stomach ache, and skin inflammation.

Various parts of the plant, including the roots and bark, are prepared into decoctions to soothe digestive distress. These traditional remedies help calm the bowels and relieve sharp abdominal pains. Additionally, topical applications of the plant extracts are applied to the body to reduce skin inflammation.

This multifaceted approach makes the plant a valuable resource in many traditional medicinal practices.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Diarrhea
  • Dysentery
  • Stomach ache
  • Skin inflammation

What are the herbal preparations of Artocarpus integer?

Artocarpus integer is prepared as decoctions, infusions, and topical pastes derived from its various plant parts including the bark, leaves, and fruit. The bark is frequently boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for addressing systemic issues or skin ailments. Leaves can be crushed into a poultice or steeped to produce a liquid extract for external application. The fruit itself is often consumed directly or processed into juices for its nutritional and therapeutic properties. These diverse methods allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be utilized for both internal and external healing purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Artocarpus integer can cause allergic reactions such as skin irritation, itching, or swelling in sensitive individuals. While the fruit is generally considered safe for consumption, some people may experience digestive discomfort if eaten in excessive amounts. There is limited clinical data regarding long-term toxicity or specific systemic side effects in humans. Most documented issues are limited to localized contact dermatitis from the sticky latex found in the tree. Caution is advised for those with known sensitivities to the Moraceae family of plants.
