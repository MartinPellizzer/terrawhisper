Alpine Fireweed (Epilobium Alpinum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Epilobium alpinum.

Alpine fireweed, scientifically known as Epilobium alpinum, is a medicinal herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia, prized for its adaptogenic properties and potential health benefits. This plant is traditionally used to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and promote skin healing, with its leaves and flowers containing compounds like flavonoids and tannins that contribute to these effects. Historically, it has been employed in traditional European herbal medicine to treat coughs, bronchitis, and skin conditions, often prepared as a tea or poultice. In modern wellness practices, Alpine fireweed is gaining attention as a natural remedy for stress relief and immune support, with some studies suggesting its potential anti-cancer properties. Its unique feature is its striking red-purple flowers, which not only add aesthetic value but also symbolize resilience, as the plant thrives in harsh mountain environments.
