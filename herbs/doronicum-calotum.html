What is Doronicum calotum?

Doronicum calotum is a perennial plant species in the family Asteraceae.

It has yellow flowers with 4-5 mm long tubular corolla, 2-3 cm long peduncle and 2-3 cm long leaves with 5-7 lobes. The plant grows up to 30-50 cm in height and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are ovate-elliptic to lanceolate, with a short petiole and a few coarse hairs.

The plant blooms from June to September, producing yellow flowers with 30-40 florets in a capitulum.

What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

The main therapeutic properties of Doronicum calotum are anti-inflammatory, which have been attributed to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, particularly doronic acid, that inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators.

This plant also possesses antioxidant properties, which are primarily due to the flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, that scavenge free radicals and protect against oxidative stress. The antimicrobial activity of Doronicum calotum is attributed to the volatile oils, including thujone and borneol, that exhibit antibacterial and antifungal effects. Additionally, the analgesic and antispasmodic properties of this plant are attributed to the saponins, which modulate the activity of neurotransmitters and relax smooth muscle, providing relief from pain and spasms.

The bioactive compounds present in Doronicum calotum contribute to its therapeutic potential and warrant further investigation into its medicinal properties.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Doronicum calotum are tincture, infusion, tea, salve, and decoction.

Decoction is a liquid preparation made by boiling the plant's roots and stems, often used for gastrointestinal issues.
