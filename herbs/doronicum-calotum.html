What's the taxonomical classification of Doronicum calotum?

Doronicum calotum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is specifically categorized under the genus Doronicum, and its unique biological identity is defined by its species name, calotum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Doronicum calotum has a perennial growth habit characterized by a basal rosette of leaves and bright yellow, daisy-like flower heads. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins and a textured surface. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flower heads arranged in a corymb-like cluster at the top of a sturdy stem. Each individual flower head features prominent ray florets surrounding a central disc of smaller tubular florets. The plant's stems are relatively unbranched and provide support for the dense flowering canopy.
