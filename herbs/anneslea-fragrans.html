Anneslea fragrans

What's the taxonomical classification of Anneslea fragrans?

Anneslea fragrans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Ericales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Pentaphylacaceae, which contains the genus Anneslea, ultimately leading to its specific designation as the species fragrans.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Anneslea fragrans contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, terpenoids, saponins, and flavonoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated in the leaves and roots of the plant. The presence of specific alkaloids provides the species with significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory capabilities. Additionally, the terpenoid compounds are often studied for their potential antioxidant activities in medicinal preparations. These diverse chemical profiles support the plant's traditional use in treating various ailments in indigenous medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Anneslea fragrans?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat malaria, stomach aches, skin infections, respiratory issues, and various types of fever. Its medicinal properties are largely attributed to the presence of essential oils and bioactive compounds within its leaves and bark. In many traditional medicine practices, the crushed leaves are applied topically to soothe skin irritations or wounds. When prepared as a decoction, the plant serves as a remedy for gastrointestinal distress and persistent coughs. These diverse applications make it a significant botanical resource in several regional healthcare systems.
