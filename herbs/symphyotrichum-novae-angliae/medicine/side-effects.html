10 Possible Side Effects Of Symphyotrichum Novae-Angliae (New England Aster)

Symphyotrichum novae-angliae has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and increased heart rate.

These side effects are caused by the herb's active compounds, including sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, which can interact with medications and exacerbate underlying conditions. For example, people with pre-existing hypertension may experience worsened symptoms, leading to increased hospitalizations and decreased quality of life.

Additionally, allergic reactions can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition.
