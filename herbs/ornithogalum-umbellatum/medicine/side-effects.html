10 Possible Side Effects Of Ornithogalum Umbellatum (Star-Of-Bethlehem)

Ornithogalum umbellatum has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

These side effects can be caused by ingesting excessive amounts of the herb.

For instance, consuming large quantities can worsen digestive issues and lead to dehydration, hindering daily activities and causing discomfort in people who rely heavily on their physical well-being.

1. Interacts with medications

Ornithogalum umbellatum causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is likely due to its sap, which contains allergenic compounds such as alkaloids and glycosides that can trigger an immune response.

The skin, respiratory system, and mucous membranes are common sites of allergic reaction, resulting in symptoms like itching, swelling, redness, and difficulty breathing.

2. Interacts with medications

Ornithogalum umbellatum triggers asthma attacks due to its potential allergenic properties.

The plant's sap and pollen contain allergens that can cause an immune response in people with existing respiratory issues, leading to constricted airways and acute breathing difficulties. This reaction is thought to be exacerbated by the presence of certain compounds, such as alkaloids, which can further stimulate the immune system.

As a result, individuals with asthma may experience severe symptoms when exposed to Ornithogalum umbellatum.

4. Interacts with medications

Ornithogalum umbellatum induces seizures due to its toxic compounds, particularly calcium oxalate and saponins.

These substances can cause excitability in the nervous system, leading to convulsions and seizure activity.

The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it's thought that the toxins may alter neurotransmitter levels or disrupt normal brain function, resulting in seizures.

5. Interacts with medications

Ornithogalum umbellatum increases blood pressure due to its stimulatory effects on the cardiovascular system.

The plant contains a compound called galanthamine, which affects the nervous system and can cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, Ornithogalum umbellatum has been shown to interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and high blood pressure medications, which can further contribute to elevated blood pressure.

6. Interacts with medications

Ornithogalum umbellatum reduces liver function due to its toxic compounds, specifically the alkaloids ornithalin and umbellatine.

These toxins can accumulate in the body, causing damage to liver cells and potentially disrupting normal liver function.

This may lead to impaired detoxification processes, increased levels of liver enzymes, and even liver failure in severe cases.

7. Interacts with medications

Ornithogalum umbellatum affects kidney function.

The plant's toxic compounds, such as alkaloids and glycosides, can cause damage to the kidneys' delicate filtering systems, leading to decreased filtration and waste removal.

This may result in elevated levels of waste products in the blood, potentially causing a range of symptoms including fatigue, nausea, and swelling.

8. Interacts with medications

Ornithogalum umbellatum causes diarrhea due to its chemical composition.

The plant contains saponins and alkaloids, which can irritate the gastrointestinal tract and disrupt normal bowel function, leading to an increased frequency of bowel movements or loose stools.

This side effect is thought to be a result of the plant's ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase gut motility.

9. Interacts with medications

Ornithogalum umbellatum produces stomach pain due to its toxic properties, which can cause gastrointestinal irritation.

The plant's bulbs contain calcium oxalate crystals and saponins, which can lead to inflammation in the digestive tract when ingested.

As a result, people who use Ornithogalum umbellatum may experience abdominal discomfort, nausea, and stomach pain.

10. Interacts with medications

Ornithogalum umbellatum hinders digestion due to its ability to interact with certain enzymes in the digestive system.

The compound responsible for this effect is believed to be a glycoside called ornithogalin, which can slow down the breakdown of carbohydrates and lead to indigestion, bloating, and other gastrointestinal issues.
