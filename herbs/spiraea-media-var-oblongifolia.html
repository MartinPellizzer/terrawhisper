Spiraea media var. oblongifolia

Spiraea media var. oblongifolia, commonly known as Japanese Spirea, is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate various health issues, including its potential to reduce inflammation, fever, and as an antiseptic to speed up wound healing.

The plant's therapeutic actions are attributed to its ability to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may contribute to its health benefits in treating conditions such as sore throats, fever, and skin irritations.

The bioactive constituents of Japanese Spirea include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and potential health benefits.
