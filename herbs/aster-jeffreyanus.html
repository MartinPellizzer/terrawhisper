Aster jeffreyanus

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster jeffreyanus?

Aster jeffreyanus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, it is identified within the genus Aster, specifically as the species jeffreyanus.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Aster jeffreyanus can cause allergic skin reactions, such as contact dermatitis, when the plant's sap or foliage comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. Individuals with existing sensitivities to the Asteraceae family may experience redness, itching, or localized swelling at the site of exposure. Ingestion of the plant could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress or other systemic complications, though specific toxicity data is limited. It is important to handle the specimen with care to avoid accidental irritation. Always consult a medical professional if any adverse reactions occur after contact.
