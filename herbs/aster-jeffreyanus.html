What's the taxonomical classification of Aster jeffreyanus?

Aster jeffreyanus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, it is identified within the genus Aster, specifically as the species jeffreyanus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster jeffreyanus has lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves that are typically entire or slightly serrated along the margins.

The plant exhibits a perennial growth habit with stems that can vary in height depending on the specific environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of small, discoid or radiate flower heads that are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. The floral structures often feature yellowish or white ray florets surrounding a central disc.

These morphological traits distinguish the species within its specific ecological niche.
