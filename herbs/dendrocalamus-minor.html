What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrocalamus minor?

Dendrocalamus minor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, positioned within the order Poales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Poaceae, specifically within the genus Dendrocalamus, with its unique identity defined by the species epithet minor.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrocalamus minor has a slender, branching stem structure characterized by thin, woody culms that emerge from a creeping rhizome system.

These stems typically reach a modest height and feature distinct nodes and internodes along their length. The foliage consists of narrow, lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the upper portions of the branches. Small, inconspicuous inflorescences often develop at the terminal ends or within the leaf axils.

The overall architecture of the plant is compact and provides a fine-textured appearance to its growth habit.
