What's the taxonomical classification of Arenaria quadridentata?

Arenaria quadridentata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic position is defined by its placement within the genus Arenaria, with the unique designation of the species quadridentata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arenaria quadridentata has an erect, branched stem with small, opposite leaves and distinctively four-toothed fruit capsules.

The leaves are typically linear or lanceolate in shape and possess a smooth texture. Small, white to pale pink flowers emerge from the axils of the foliage. The most defining morphological feature is the capsule, which splits into four distinct segments upon maturity.

This plant generally maintains a compact growth habit suitable for rocky or sandy habitats.
