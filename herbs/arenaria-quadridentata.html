What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arenaria quadridentata has an erect, branched stem with small, opposite leaves and distinctively four-toothed fruit capsules. The most defining morphological feature is the capsule, which splits into four distinct segments upon maturity. This plant generally maintains a compact growth habit suitable for rocky or sandy habitats.
