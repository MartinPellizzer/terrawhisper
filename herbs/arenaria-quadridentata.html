Arenaria quadridentata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arenaria quadridentata has an erect, branched stem with small, opposite leaves and distinctively four-toothed fruit capsules. The leaves are typically linear or lanceolate in shape and possess a smooth texture. Small, white to pale pink flowers emerge from the axils of the foliage. The most defining morphological feature is the capsule, which splits into four distinct segments upon maturity. This plant generally maintains a compact growth habit suitable for rocky or sandy habitats.
