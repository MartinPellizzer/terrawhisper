Mentha × rubra var. crepiniana

Mentha × rubra var. crepiniana, also known as Red Mint, is a hybrid species of mint, specifically a variety of the wild mint (Mentha × rubra).

This plant has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, boasting medicinal properties that include anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and digestive-soothing effects.

The health benefits of Mentha × rubra var. crepiniana include relief from nausea, headaches, and digestive issues, as well as its ability to aid in relaxation and improve respiratory health.

In herbal preparations, the leaves of this plant are often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures that can be consumed orally to harness its medicinal properties.
