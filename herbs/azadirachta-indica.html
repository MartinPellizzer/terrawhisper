Indian Lilac

Azadirachta indica

Indian Lilac (Azadirachta indica) is a tropical tree native to the Indian subcontinent, widely recognized for its traditional medicinal properties and use in natural pest control. It is primarily used to improve skin conditions, reduce inflammation, and treat parasitic infections due to its potent antifungal, antibacterial, and antiviral compounds.

Botanical Identification

Azadirachta indica, commonly known as Indian lilac, is a tropical tree native to the Indian subcontinent. It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is often referred to by regional names like neem in Hindi and margosa in English. The plant is a deciduous evergreen, characterized by its dark green leaves, fragrant flowers, and medicinal properties widely documented in herbal literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Meliaceae
  • Genus: Azadirachta
  • Species: Azadirachta indica

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of Indian Lilac include saponins, terpenoids, and flavonoids. These compounds work synergistically to disrupt insect physiology and reduce microbial growth. Their combined action enhances the plant's efficacy in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Saponin
  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory infections and skin conditions.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as an antimicrobial agent.

Powder: Used for skin conditions and as a dietary supplement.

Safety Profile

Azadirachta indica, or Indian Lilac, has a safety profile that includes potential contraindications for pregnant and lactating women due to its phytoestrogenic properties. It may also cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for prolonged or high-dose applications.
