What to know about Azadirachta indica (neem) before using it medicinally

Neem, scientifically known as Azadirachta indica, is a plant that has been revered for its numerous health benefits, from reducing inflammation and improving skin conditions to boosting immunity and fighting off infections.

In culinary traditions, neem leaves are commonly used in Indian and Middle Eastern cooking, added to curries, salads, and teas for their bitter flavor and perceived medicinal properties. Horticulturally, neem is known as a natural pest control, its seeds and oil used to repel mosquitoes and other pests that can damage crops. From a botanical standpoint, neem belongs to the mahogany family, Meliaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers.

Historically, neem has been mentioned in Ayurvedic texts dating back over 4,000 years, with references to its use for treating various ailments including fever, skin conditions, and respiratory problems.
