Azadirachta Indica Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Azadirachta indica, commonly known as neem, is a tropical evergreen tree native to India, widely recognized for its numerous medicinal properties.

The therapeutic actions of neem are attributed to its bioactive constituents such as nimbin, azadirachtin, and quercetin, which possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Neem can be prepared in various herbal forms, including infusions, decoctions, and topical applications, offering versatile uses in traditional and modern medicine.

Side Effects of azadirachta indica

Azadirachta indica leads to skin irritation, which can manifest as redness, itching, or a burning sensation in individuals who come into contact with the plant's extracts or leaves.

This reaction is often due to the presence of bioactive compounds such as azadirachtin, which can be irritating to sensitive skin. In addition to skin irritation, Azadirachta indica can cause diarrhea, as its compounds may disrupt the normal functioning of the digestive system.

Stomach upset and nausea are also common side effects, particularly when consumed in higher doses or on an empty stomach. These gastrointestinal issues can sometimes progress to vomiting, further indicating the herb's potential to cause discomfort in the digestive tract. While Azadirachta indica is widely used in traditional medicine for its insecticidal and antimicrobial properties, its use should be approached with caution, especially for individuals with sensitive skin or a history of digestive disorders.

It is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating this herb into one's regimen to minimize the risk of adverse effects.
