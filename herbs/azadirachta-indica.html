Indian Lilac (Azadirachta indica)

Indian Lilac (Azadirachta indica) is a member of the Meliaceae family, native to Southern Asia, India, and Sub-Himalayan Region. Traditionally, its leaves, seeds, and bark have been used for powders, decoctions, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and native american herbal medicine.

Flowers
Small white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, actinomorphic, with five petals and five sepals, lacking distinctive markings.
Stems
Cylindrical, woody, branching in a sympodial pattern, with smooth, glabrous surfaces and opposite phyllotaxis.
