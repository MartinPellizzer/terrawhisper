What to know about neem before using it medicinally

Neem, scientifically known as Azadirachta indica, is a versatile herb that has been used for centuries in various aspects of human life.

Its health properties are numerous, including its ability to improve skin health, boost the immune system, and even help in the treatment of certain types of cancer. In the culinary world, neem leaves are used in a variety of dishes, particularly in Indian cuisine, for their bitter taste and health benefits.

In horticulture, neem is valued for its insecticidal and medicinal properties, with neem oil and neem cake being widely used as organic pesticides and fertilizers. Botanically, neem is a fast-growing tree that can reach up to 20 meters in height, with a wide canopy and a straight trunk.

Historically, neem has been referenced in ancient texts such as the Vedas and the Ayurveda, where it was used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments.

What are the side effects of neem when used improperly?

When used improperly, neem increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as digestive issues, skin irritation, and potential harm to the liver and kidneys.

Overuse or incorrect administration of neem products can lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. Additionally, topical application of neem can cause skin irritation, redness, and itching.

Prolonged usage or high doses of neem may also negatively impact the liver and kidneys, potentially leading to damage or failure. It's important to note that neem can interact with certain medications and lower blood sugar levels, which can be dangerous for individuals with diabetes. Pregnant women should avoid neem altogether, as it may cause miscarriage or harm to the fetus.

Therefore, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using neem for medicinal purposes.
