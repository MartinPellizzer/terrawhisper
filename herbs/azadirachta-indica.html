What to know about Azadirachta indica before using it medicinally

Azadirachta indica, commonly known as neem, is a medicinal herb with a wide range of applications in treating various health conditions, including skin infections, wounds, and inflammatory diseases such as arthritis and gout.

The main health benefits associated with neem include its antimicrobial, antifungal, and antiparasitic properties, which help to combat infections and promote overall well-being. The herb's therapeutic properties are attributed to its ability to modulate the immune system, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antioxidant activity, making it a valuable resource in traditional medicine. The bioactive compounds present in neem include nimbin, nimnodal, and azadirachtin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In Ayurvedic medicine, neem is commonly prepared as a paste or oil by grinding the leaves and mixing them with water or oil, which is then used topically to treat a variety of skin conditions and promote wound healing.
