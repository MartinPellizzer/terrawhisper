Buxus harlandii

What's the taxonomical classification of Buxus harlandii?

Buxus harlandii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Buxales and the family Buxaceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Buxus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species harlandii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buxus harlandii has small, elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and slightly paler underneath.

These evergreen leaves are arranged oppositely along woody, upright stems that form a dense, shrubby habit. The plant features tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in axillary clusters. Its fruit consists of small, fleshy drupes that mature into a characteristic color.

The overall texture of the foliage is stiff and smooth, contributing to its compact appearance.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Buxus harlandii can cause gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain if consumed. The plant contains various alkaloids that may lead to systemic toxicity in sensitive individuals. Some users might experience dizziness or neurological symptoms following ingestion. Skin irritation or allergic reactions may also occur upon direct contact with the plant's foliage. Detailed toxicological studies on humans are limited, so caution is necessary when handling this species.
