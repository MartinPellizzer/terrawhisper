What to know about Lobaria pulmonaria (lungwort) before using it medicinally

Lungwort, scientifically known as Lobaria pulmonaria, is a versatile and medicinal herb that has been touted for its impressive health benefits.

Its leaves have been traditionally used to soothe respiratory issues such as bronchitis, asthma, and chronic coughs, while its roots are believed to possess anti-inflammatory properties that can aid in wound healing. In the culinary world, lungwort is often used to add flavor to soups, stews, and salads, particularly in Eastern European and Asian cuisine. Horticulturally, lungwort thrives in well-draining soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden enthusiasts.

Botanically, lungwort belongs to the family Lobariaceae, and its name is derived from its leaves, which resemble miniature lungs due to their distinctive markings.
