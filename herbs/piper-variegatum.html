What is Piper variegatum?

Piper variegatum is a species of plant that belongs to the family Piperaceae, a family of flowering plants commonly known as pepper plants.

It is a climbing shrub with woody stems and dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape and have entire margins. The leaves are 5-13 cm long and 2.5-6 cm wide, with prominent midrib and lateral veins. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers in axillary clusters, which are followed by red to purple berries.

The berries are 6-8 mm in diameter and have 4-6 seeds.
