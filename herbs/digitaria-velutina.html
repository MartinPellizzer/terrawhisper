What's the taxonomical classification of Digitaria velutina?

Digitaria velutina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Poales. Further taxonomic refinement places it in the family Poaceae, which contains the genus Digitaria, and it is specifically identified as the species velutinina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Digitaria velutinina has a prostrate or decumbent growth habit characterized by slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes to form dense mats. The leaves are narrow and linear with a soft, velvety texture caused by fine hairs on the surface. Its inflorescence consists of several digitately arranged spikes that branch from a single point at the top of the stem. These spikes bear small, cylindrical spikelets that are typically arranged in two rows along the rachis. The overall structure allows the plant to spread effectively across disturbed or open soil surfaces.
