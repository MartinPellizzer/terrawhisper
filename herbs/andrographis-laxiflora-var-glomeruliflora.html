Cluster-Flowered Andrographis (Andrographis Laxiflora Var. Glomeruliflora)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Andrographis laxiflora var. glomeruliflora.

Andrographis laxiflora var. glomeruliflora is a lesser-known variant of the Andrographis genus, which is traditionally used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine for its potent medicinal properties. This variant is particularly valued for its high concentration of andrographolide, a bitter lactone known for its strong antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. It has been used in folk medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including respiratory infections, fever, and digestive disorders. The plant's leaves and stems are typically harvested and processed into extracts or decoctions for internal use. Due to its therapeutic potential, ongoing research is exploring its applications in modern pharmacology for treating inflammatory and infectious diseases.
