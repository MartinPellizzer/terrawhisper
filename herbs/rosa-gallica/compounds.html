French Rose (Rosa gallica) Compounds

French Rose (Rosa gallica) contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its antioxidant properties. Coumarin and glycosides are also present, offering anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits. Essential oils and terpenoids provide aromatic and therapeutic effects, while anthocyanins, saponins, and alkaloids add to its medicinal profile.

French Rose (Rosa gallica) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in French Rose (Rosa gallica). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for skin health and immune support. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative stress and may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in French Rose (Rosa gallica). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its potential to reduce inflammation and support digestive health. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal qualities during preparation.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in French Rose (Rosa gallica). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting skin health and addressing inflammatory conditions.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in French Rose (Rosa gallica). It contributes to the plant's anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural antiseptic and may support skin health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of French Rose in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in French Rose (Rosa gallica). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Glycosides in Rosa gallica are often responsible for its calming and digestive benefits in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Essential oil

The essential oil of French Rose (Rosa gallica) is a concentrated aromatic compound extracted through distillation. It contains volatile compounds like citral and geraniol, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. This oil is valued for its calming effects and is used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in French Rose (Rosa gallica). They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related ailments.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in French Rose (Rosa gallica). It contributes to the flower's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative stress and support overall health. Anthocyanin is also valued for its potential anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in French Rose (Rosa gallica). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and skin-soothing properties. Saponin helps in detoxifying the body and may support respiratory health. It is commonly used in herbal remedies for its mild cleansing and therapeutic effects.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in French Rose (Rosa gallica). It contributes to the plant's potential therapeutic effects, though research on its specific role in herbal medicine is limited. Alkaloids are typically associated with complex physiological actions, but their presence in French Rose remains understudied compared to other compounds.
