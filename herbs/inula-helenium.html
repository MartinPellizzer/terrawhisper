Inula helenium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Inula helenium has upright, herbaceous stems that reach heights of up to one meter and bear large, bright yellow composite flower heads measuring 5 to 10 centimeters in diameter. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, featuring a rough, hairy texture and serrated margins. Higher up the stem, the leaves become smaller and more elliptical as they transition toward the flowering portion of the plant. The inflorescence consists of numerous ray florets surrounding a central cluster of disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. These golden blooms are supported by sturdy, branched stalks that emerge from the central stem during the peak growing season.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Inula helenium targets several key proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, various lipoxygenases, and tumor necrosis factor-alpha to mediate its anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects. The sesquiterpene lactones found within the plant act as inhibitors of these enzymes to reduce the production of pro-inflammatory mediators. Additionally, certain compounds in the extract interact with nuclear factor kappa B signaling pathways to suppress excessive immune responses. These molecular interactions contribute to the plant's efficacy in treating respiratory inflammation and gastrointestinal distress. By modulating these specific protein targets, the plant exerts a systemic influence on inflammatory cascades.

What are the therapeutic actions of Inula helenium?

Inula helenium has therapeutic properties as a bitter stomachic and digestive stimulant that promotes healthy gastrointestinal function. Additionally, its febrifuge qualities allow it to assist in the reduction of fever during various illnesses.
