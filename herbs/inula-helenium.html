Helenium (Inula helenium)

Helenium (Inula helenium) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North America. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 2.5 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface ranging from gray-green to dark green and lower surface pale white to whitish, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Radial symmetry, yellow to orange flowers with 12 to 18 ray florets and 12 to 18 disk florets arranged in a single head, with ligulate corollas extending outward and tubular corollas inward.
Stems
Cylindrical, hollow, and ridged, the stems are terete, with a smooth surface and a tendency to branch in a whorled pattern.
