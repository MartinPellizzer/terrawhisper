What to know about Anethum graveolens (dill) before using it medicinally

Dill, scientifically known as Anethum graveolens, is an herb that has been revered for its impressive health benefits.

Rich in antioxidants and essential oils, dill has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory properties, improve digestion, and even exhibit antimicrobial effects. In the kitchen, dill's sweet, pungent flavor makes it a staple in many cuisines, particularly in Scandinavian, Mediterranean, and Indian cooking, where it's often used to season pickles, sauces, and soups. As an annual herb, dill is relatively easy to cultivate and requires minimal maintenance, making it a popular choice among gardeners.

With its origins tracing back thousands of years to ancient Egypt and Greece, where it was revered for its medicinal and culinary properties, dill has been a beloved ingredient in many cultures throughout history.
