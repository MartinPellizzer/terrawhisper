Clematis finetiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis finetiana?

Clematis finetiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, situated within the genus Clematis, and is specifically identified by its species name, finetiaana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Clematis finetiana contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including terpenoids, flavonoids, and alkaloids that contribute to its pharmacological potential. These specific chemical constituents are often studied for their ability to interact with biological pathways related to inflammation and oxidative stress. The presence of polyphenolic compounds suggests that the plant may possess significant antioxidant properties within its cellular structure. Researchers examine these specific molecular profiles to determine how the species might be utilized in developing new therapeutic agents. Further investigation into the synergistic effects of these compounds is necessary to fully understand their medicinal efficacy.

What are the therapeutic actions of Clematis finetiana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments, skin inflammations, and various types of digestive distress. In traditional herbal medicine, its extracts are often applied topically to soothe irritation or swelling on the skin. Some practitioners also utilize the plant to alleviate symptoms of coughs and bronchitis through decoctions. While its specific applications vary by region, the plant is generally valued for its potential anti-inflammatory properties. Careful dosage is required because the chemical compounds within the species can be potent.
