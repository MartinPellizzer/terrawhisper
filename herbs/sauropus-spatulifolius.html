Sauropus spatulifolius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sauropus spatulifolius has elliptical to spatulate leaves that are typically dark green, glossy, and arranged in an opposite or whorled pattern along the stem. The leaf margins are generally entire, and the texture often feels somewhat fleshy or leathery to the touch. Its stems are woody and branched, providing structural support for the dense foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, globose fruits. These fruits are often used in traditional medicinal practices and are characteristic of the Phyllanthaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of Thailand. It thrives primarily in humid, lowland forest environments where shade and high moisture levels are abundant. Its distribution is largely confined to the equatorial belt where consistent rainfall supports its growth requirements. Within these habitats, it can be found growing in the understory layer of dense vegetation. The specific microclimates of these maritime and continental tropical zones dictate its limited geographic range.
