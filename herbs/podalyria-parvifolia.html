    What is Podalyria parvifolia?

    Podalyria parvifolia is a shrub or small tree species within the Fabaceae family, native to the southwestern region of Western Australia.

    The plant is characterized by its glabrous or sparsely hairy branches, and its compound leaves, consisting of 2-5 pairs of leaflets, are dark green in color and have a length of approximately 6-15 mm. The leaflets are narrowly elliptical to lanceolate in shape, with an acute apex and a rounded base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in axillary clusters, which are about 5-6 mm in diameter and have a 5-merous corolla.

    The flowers are followed by flat, linear seed pods, approximately 10-12 mm in length, which contain 4-6 seeds.
