Hymenophyllum badium

What's the taxonomical classification of Hymenophyllum badium?

Hymenophyllum badium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, it occupies a specific evolutionary niche within the order Hymenophyllales. The plant is a representative of the family Hymenophyllaceae and falls under the genus Hymenophyllum, ultimately being identified by its specific epithet, badium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hymenophyllum badium has thin, translucent, dark brown to reddish-brown fronds that lack a distinct midrib and exhibit a highly dissected, almost filamentous structure.

These delicate leaves are characterized by a fine, lace-like appearance that allows light to pass through easily. The plant grows in small, compact tufts, often forming dense mats in shaded, moist environments. Its reproductive structures consist of small sporangia located along the margins of the tiny, branched segments.

This unique morphology is an adaptation to its specific ecological niche within the understory of tropical forests.
