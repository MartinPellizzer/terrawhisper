What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia parishii?

Vernonia parishii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. As a member of the widely recognized family Asteraceae, this plant is placed within the genus Vernonia, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species parishii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia parishii has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, branching stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters. The leaves are primarily lanceolate to narrowly elliptic in shape, featuring entire margins and an alternate arrangement along the stem. Small, inconspicuous bracts surround the flowering heads, which are clustered in terminal or axillary cymes. The inflorescence consists of discoid flower heads that produce tubular, purple-to-lavender corollas. These structures are supported by a persistent involucral series of phyllaries that protect the developing florets.
