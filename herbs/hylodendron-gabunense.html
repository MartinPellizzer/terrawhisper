Hylodendron gabunense

What's the taxonomical classification of Hylodendron gabunense?

Hylodendron gabunense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Fabales. This species is a member of the family Fabaceae and is defined by its specific placement within the genus Hylodendron, ultimately being identified by its unique species name, gabunense.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. Local communities utilize its bark and leaves to facilitate the healing of cuts and abrasions. The botanical extracts are also applied topically to soothe skin irritations and reduce swelling. Some traditional practices incorporate the plant to manage pain associated with minor ailments. These medicinal applications rely on the chemical properties found within the plant's tissues.
