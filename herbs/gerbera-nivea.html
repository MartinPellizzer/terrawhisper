Gerbera nivea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gerbera nivea has a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in dense, white, felt-like hairs and bright white ray florets surrounding a yellow central disc. The foliage is characterized by a deeply lobed or pinnatifid structure with a soft, velvety texture caused by these trichomes. These hairy surfaces serve to protect the plant from moisture loss and intense sunlight. The flower heads are composite structures where the outer rays provide a stark contrast to the inner disc florets. Each stem is relatively short and sturdy to support the weight of the large, snowy blooms.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of South Africa, specifically within the Drakensberg mountain range. It thrives in the rocky crevices and alpine grasslands found at significant elevations. Because it is native to these specific montane environments, its range is quite restricted compared to other members of the genus. The species is typically found in cool, moist habitats that characterize these mountain ecosystems. Its distribution is closely tied to the unique climatic conditions of the southern African highlands.
