What's the taxonomical classification of Gerbera nivea?

Gerbera nivea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its descent through the order Asterales, the plant is situated within the family Asteraceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Gerbera, with its unique classification completed at the species level as nivea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gerbera nivea has a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in dense, white, felt-like hairs and bright white ray florets surrounding a yellow central disc. The foliage is characterized by a deeply lobed or pinnatifid structure with a soft, velvety texture caused by these trichomes. These hairy surfaces serve to protect the plant from moisture loss and intense sunlight. The flower heads are composite structures where the outer rays provide a stark contrast to the inner disc florets. Each stem is relatively short and sturdy to support the weight of the large, snowy blooms.
