Botanical Identification of Banisteria Caapi Vine (Banisteria caapi)

To identify Banisteria Caapi Vine (Banisteria caapi) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Banisteria caapi belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Loganiaceae, family Loganiaceae, genus Banisteria, and species Banisteria caapi. It is a vine native to the Amazon, known for its traditional use in Ayahuasca preparations.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Banisteria caapi is the accepted scientific name for the Banisteria caapi Vine. It was first described by the botanist Carl von Linné in 1753. The species has several synonyms, including Banisteria caapi var. reticulata and Banisteria caapi var. tucanensis. It is also known by various common names, such as Ayahuasca vine, and regional names like Yagé in the Amazon.

Morphological Characteristics

Banisteria caapi is a climbing vine with a fibrous root system that anchors it to host trees. Its stems are woody and twining, often reaching several meters in length. Leaves are alternate, elliptic to ovate, with smooth margins and a slightly glossy surface. The inflorescence is axillary, bearing small, clustered flowers. Flowers are greenish-white with five petals, and the fruit is a small, fleshy capsule containing numerous seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Banisteria caapi can be distinguished by its dark purple to black stems with longitudinal ridges, opposite heart-shaped leaves, and small greenish-white flowers. It is often found in humid lowland forests. The bark is thick and has a distinct, aromatic scent when cut. It is commonly confused with other similar species but can be reliably identified through these key morphological features.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Banisteria caapi is native to the Amazon rainforest, primarily in Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. It thrives in warm, humid climates with high rainfall. It prefers nutrient-rich, well-drained soils and is typically found at low to mid-altitude regions, usually below 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Banisteria caapi blooms during the rainy season, typically between March and June. Seed formation occurs 6-8 months after flowering, usually in late summer to early fall. Harvesting is most effective during the dry season when the vines are mature and the bark is rich in alkaloids.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Banisteria caapi belongs to the Rubiaceae family, sharing traits with other members like Psychotria and Uncaria. It is closely related to species within the genus Banisteria, which includes Banisteriaopsis. These relationships highlight its evolutionary ties to other climbing plants with similar morphological and chemical characteristics.
