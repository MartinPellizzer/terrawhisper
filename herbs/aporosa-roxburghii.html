What is Aporosa roxburghii?

Aporosa roxburghii is a species of plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is an evergreen shrub or small tree that grows up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and a smooth, grey bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, measuring 4-10 cm long and 2-5 cm wide, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale green lower surface. The inflorescence is a catkin-like structure bearing small, unisexual flowers, with male flowers in a terminal cluster and female flowers in axillary clusters.

The plant fruits are small, green, and globose, measuring 6-8 mm in diameter, containing a single seed.
