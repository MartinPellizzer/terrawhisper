Jasminum seguinii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum seguinii has opposite, pinnate leaves that typically consist of five to seven small, ovate leaflets with entire margins. The plant features woody stems that can grow into a climbing or spreading shrubby habit. Its flowers are small, white, and tubular, often arranged in terminal cymes. The fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that matures into a dark color. This species is characterized by its slender branches and relatively delicate foliage structure.
