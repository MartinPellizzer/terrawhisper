Erythroxylum coca

What's the taxonomical classification of Erythroxylum coca?

Erythroxylum coca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and into the family Erythroxylaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Erythroxylum, specifically identified as the species coca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erythroxylum coca has elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically two to five centimeters long, possessing an entire margin and a prominent midrib.

These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along the woody stems of the shrub or small tree. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. Following pollination, the plant develops small, drupe-like fruits that transition from green to a bright red color when ripe.

The root system is robust, helping the plant anchor itself within the moist, tropical soils of the Andean region.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Erythroxylum coca contains medicinal alkaloids primarily located in its dried leaves. These leaves are traditionally chewed or brewed into teas to provide stimulant effects and alleviate fatigue. The plant parts are also utilized to suppress hunger and reduce feelings of exhaustion during strenuous physical labor. Additionally, certain preparations of the leaves serve as a topical anesthetic to numb pain in various parts of the body. The concentration of active compounds is most potent when the foliage is processed in specific ways.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the alkaloid cocaine alongside several other significant medicinal and psychoactive compounds including ecgonine, benzoylecgognine, cinnamoylcocaine, truxilline, and hygrine.

Cocaine serves as the primary active constituent known for its potent anesthetic and stimulant properties. The presence of ecgonine and benzoylecgognine highlights the complex chemical structure of the plant's ester alkaloids. Additionally, the alkaloids truxilline and hygrine contribute to the diverse chemical profile found within the leaves.

These various compounds work together to define the unique pharmacological identity of the species.

  • Cocaine

  • Benzoylecgonine
  • Cinnamoylcocaine
  • Truxilline
  • Hygrine

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Erythroxylum coca targets the dopamine transporter (DAT), the norepinephrine transporter (NET), and the serotonin transporter (SERT) to produce its primary pharmacological effects. By binding to these specific transmembrane proteins, the plant's alkaloids inhibit the reuptake of neurotransmitters within the synaptic cleft. This inhibition leads to an increased concentration of monoamines, which stimulates the central nervous system and induces euphoria. The interaction with dopamine transporters is particularly significant for its reinforcing and stimulant properties. These molecular pathways also contribute to the local anesthetic effects seen through the blockade of voltage-gated sodium channels in nerve membranes.

What are the therapeutic actions of Erythroxylum coca?

Erythroxylum coca has therapeutic properties acting as a stimulant, an analeptic, a local anesthetic, an anti-fatigue agent, a euphoriant, and an appetite suppressant.

The alkaloids within the leaves function as an analeptic to stimulate the central nervous system and increase alertness. Its local anesthetic qualities allow for the numbing of tissues when applied topically to specific areas. As a powerful stimulant and anti-fatigue agent, the plant helps individuals maintain energy levels during prolonged periods of physical exertion. The presence of euphoriant effects can temporarily alter mood and perception.

Additionally, the plant serves as an effective appetite suppressant by reducing the sensation of hunger.

The primary therapeutic actions of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Stimulant
  • Analeptic
  • Anesthetic (local)
  • Anti-fatigue
  • Euphoriant
  • Appetite suppressant

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat fatigue, depression, hunger, and altitude sickness.

Its leaves are frequently chewed by indigenous populations to provide a sustained energy boost during long periods of physical labor. The alkaloids within the plant help to suppress hunger and appetite in environments where food is scarce. Additionally, it is widely utilized to mitigate the symptoms of altitude sickness, such as headaches and nausea, in high-mountain regions.

Many users also find that the substance helps to lift low spirits and alleviate mild symptoms of depression.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Fatigue
  • Depression
  • Hunger
  • Altitude sickness

What are the herbal preparations of Erythroxylum coca?

Erythroxylum coca is prepared as dried leaves for chewing, as a fine powder for insufflation, or as an aqueous infusion for drinking. Traditional users often place the dried leaves in the mouth and mix them with an alkaline substance like lime to facilitate alkaloid extraction. These leaves can also be steeped in hot water to create a tea that provides mild stimulation. In some regions, the plant material is processed into concentrated extracts or topical applications for medicinal use. Modern pharmaceutical preparations may also involve refined alkaloids derived from the botanical source.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Erythroxylum coca can cause physiological and psychological side effects including increased heart rate, elevated blood pressure, anxiety, insomnia, and significant appetite suppression. Frequent consumption may lead to more severe neurological issues such as irritability, tremors, or paranoia. Long-term use is often associated with cardiovascular strain and potential damage to the digestive system. In cases of extreme toxicity, users may experience hallucinations or seizures. These symptoms vary depending on the method of administration and the dosage consumed.
