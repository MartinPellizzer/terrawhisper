Erythroxylum coca

What's the taxonomical classification of Erythroxylum coca?

Erythroxylum coca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and into the family Erythroxylaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Erythroxylum, specifically identified as the species coca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erythroxylum coca has elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically two to five centimeters long, possessing an entire margin and a prominent midrib. These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along the woody stems of the shrub or small tree. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. Following pollination, the plant develops small, drupe-like fruits that transition from green to a bright red color when ripe. The root system is robust, helping the plant anchor itself within the moist, tropical soils of the Andean region.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of western South America, specifically concentrated in the Andes mountains of Colombia, Peru, Bolivia, and Ecuador. It thrives primarily in high-altitude environments where the climate remains humid and warm. The species is most commonly found growing on steep mountain slopes and within densely forested valleys. While its natural range is restricted to these specific Andean corridors, it is also cultivated extensively throughout various parts of the continent. Its distribution is strictly limited by its need for particular soil types and consistent tropical moisture levels.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the alkaloid cocaine along with alpha-truxilline, beta-truxilline, cuscohygrine, and hygrine. These specific medicinal compounds are primarily found within the leaves of the Erythroxylum coca species. The presence of these various alkaloids contributes to the complex chemical profile and pharmacological effects of the plant. Scientists study these substances to understand their interactions with the human nervous system. Each compound plays a distinct role in the overall chemical composition of the foliage.

As discussed in a study, the cultivated Erythroxylum coca varieties contain 18 alkaloids identified so far. These compounds belong to the tropanes, pyrrolidines and pyridines, with cocaine serving as the main alkaloid. The reported alkaloids include cocaine, cinnamoylcocaine, benzoylecgonine, methylecgonine, pseudotropine, benzoyltropine, tropacocaine, alpha- and beta-truxilline, hygrine, cuscohygrine and nicotine. While the biological activity of cocaine and nicotine is discussed elsewhere, hardly anything is known about the biological activity of the other alkaloids present in the four varieties mentioned.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as masticated leaves, an infusion, a tincture, or an extract. Traditional use involves chewing the raw leaves to release alkaloids through saliva. An infusion is created by steeping the dried leaves in hot water to produce a stimulating tea. For more concentrated medicinal applications, a tincture is prepared by soaking the plant material in alcohol. Finally, a liquid extract can be produced through various solvent processes to isolate specific bioactive compounds.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause a wide range of physiological and psychological side effects including nausea, dizziness, insomnia, anxiety, tachycardia, hypertension, addiction, anorexia, paranoia, euphoria, agitation, tremors, mydriasis, confusion, hallucinations, seizures, cardiac arrhythmia, and depression. Users often experience intense euphoria and agitation which can quickly transition into paranoia, confusion, or frightening hallucinations. Physical symptoms such as tachycardia, hypertension, and cardiac arrhythmia pose significant risks to cardiovascular health, while tremors and seizures can indicate severe toxicity. Prolonged use frequently leads to chronic issues like addiction, depression, and anorexia. The presence of mydriasis and persistent insomnia further illustrates the profound impact this substance has on the nervous system.
