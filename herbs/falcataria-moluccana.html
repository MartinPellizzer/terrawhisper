Falcataria moluccana

What's the taxonomical classification of Falcataria moluccana?

Falcataria moluccana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Fabales. The plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it within the genus Falcataria, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species moluccana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Falcataria moluccana has a large, spreading crown with pinnately compound leaves that consist of many small, oblong, bright green leaflets. The tree develops a straight, cylindrical trunk characterized by smooth, light grey bark that may become slightly fissured with age. Its woody structure is known for rapid vertical growth and a relatively light, soft wood density. Small, fragrant, pale yellow flowers emerge in clusters from the axils of the branches. The plant produces flat, papery, brown seed pods that eventually dehisce to release winged seeds for wind dispersal.
