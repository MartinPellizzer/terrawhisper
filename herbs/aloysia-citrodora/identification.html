Botanical Identification of Citronella (Aloysia citrodora)

To identify Citronella (Aloysia citrodora) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Citronella, or Aloysia citrodora, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Verbenaceae, genus Aloysia, and species Aloysia citrodora. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the verbena family, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Citronella is Aloysia citrodora, first described by the French botanist Antoine Laurent de Jussieu in the 18th century. It is also known by several common names, including citronella grass and lemon verbena, and has been referred to by regional and historical names in various cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Citronella, or Aloysia citrodora, is a shrub with a dense, upright growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly. The stems are woody and covered in fine hairs. Leaves are opposite, elliptical, and aromatic. The inflorescence is a terminal panicle, with small white flowers that emit a strong citrus scent. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule containing tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Citronella, or Aloysia citrodora, can be distinguished by its strong lemon scent, narrow leaves with a rough texture, and yellowish-white flowers. It differs from similar species like A. odorata by its more intense fragrance and narrower leaf blades. The plant also has a taller, more upright growth habit compared to its relatives.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Aloysia citrodora, or citronella, is native to the Mediterranean region, including southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. It thrives in warm, temperate climates with well-drained soils. It typically grows at elevations between 0 and 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Citronella (Aloysia citrodora) blooms from late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer, following successful pollination. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in late summer to early fall when the leaves are mature and aromatic.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Aloysia citrodora is closely related to species within the same genus, such as A. triphylla and A. mollis. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family, sharing characteristics like opposite leaves and showy flowers. Its phylogenetic position reflects evolutionary ties to other aromatic plants in the family.
