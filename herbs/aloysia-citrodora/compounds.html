Citronella (Aloysia citrodora) Compounds

The primary compounds in Citronella (Aloysia citrodora) include essential oil, which gives it its characteristic scent and medicinal properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin and terpenoids are also present, offering additional therapeutic benefits. These compounds work together to support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

Citronella (Aloysia citrodora) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Aloysia citrodora contains active compounds like citral and geraniol. These compounds contribute to its antimicrobial and insect-repelling properties. The oil is commonly used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic benefits. It is extracted through distillation of the plant's flowers and leaves.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Aloysia citrodora contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine. They help in reducing oxidative stress and may aid in treating ailments like respiratory and skin conditions. Flavonoids are part of the plant's complex bioactive profile.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Aloysia citrodora, commonly known as citronella. It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has mild anticoagulant effects. Coumarin is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and calming properties.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of compounds found in Aloysia citrodora, commonly known as citronella. These compounds contribute to the plant's characteristic aroma and possess antimicrobial and insect-repelling properties, making them valuable in herbal medicine for their natural therapeutic applications.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Aloysia citrodora, commonly known as citronella. It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating infections and reducing inflammation.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Aloysia citrodora, also known as citronella. It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Glycosides in citronella may support digestive health and aid in respiratory conditions.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Aloysia citrodora, commonly known as citronella. 8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Aloysia citrodora. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin aids in supporting digestive health and may help in reducing skin irritations when applied topically.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Aloysia citrodora, commonly known as citronella. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in its use for treating digestive issues and respiratory conditions. 10. Anthocyanin
