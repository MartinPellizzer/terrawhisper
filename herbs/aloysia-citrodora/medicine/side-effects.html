10 Possible Side Effects Of Aloysia Citrodora

Aloysia citrodora has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive issues.

These side effects can occur due to overconsumption, allergic sensitization, or interactions with other medications. For instance, severe skin irritation can lead to blisters and long-term scarring, affecting a person's self-confidence and daily activities.

Additionally, digestive issues can cause stomach pain, nausea, and vomiting, impacting daily productivity and overall quality of life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Aloysia citrodora causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is likely due to the presence of certain compounds such as citral and geraniol, which are known to trigger allergic responses in susceptible people.

These compounds can cause an overactive immune response, leading to symptoms like skin rash, itching, and swelling.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Aloysia citrodora triggers asthma attacks due to its potent allergenic properties and high concentration of volatile oils, particularly citral and geranial.

These compounds can cause the airways to constrict, leading to increased bronchial constriction, wheezing, and shortness of breath characteristic of an asthma attack.

The release of histamine in response to these allergens can also exacerbate symptoms, further triggering an asthmatic response.

3. Induces bronchospasm

Aloysia citrodora induces bronchospasm.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's naturally occurring compounds, including limonene and citral, which are known to cause constriction of the airways and trigger spasms in individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

4. Increases heart rate

Aloysia citrodora increases heart rate due to its stimulating properties.

The essential oils present in this plant, such as citral and geraniol, can cause an increase in heart rate by directly affecting the body's autonomic nervous system, which regulates various bodily functions, including heart rate.

This effect is thought to be a result of the plant's ability to stimulate the release of certain neurotransmitters, leading to increased cardiac activity.

5. Lowers blood pressure

Aloysia citrodora lowers blood pressure by influencing the body's response to stress and anxiety.

Its compounds, such as citral and limonene, have a calming effect on the nervous system, leading to a decrease in heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, Aloysia citrodora has been shown to increase parasympathetic activity, which promotes relaxation and reduces sympathetic tone, both of which contribute to lower blood pressure.

6. Disrupts digestive rhythms

Aloysia citrodora disrupts digestive rhythms due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and alter the normal flow of digestion.

The essential oils present in Aloysia citrodora can irritate the stomach lining, leading to changes in appetite, nausea, and altered bowel habits.

Additionally, the herb's natural diuretic properties may also contribute to disruptions in digestive rhythms, causing an imbalance in water and electrolyte levels that can affect digestion and bowel movements.

7. Produces dizziness

Aloysia citrodora produces dizziness due to its potential interaction with certain medications, such as antidepressants and antihistamines.

The herb contains compounds like limonene and citral, which can affect the central nervous system, leading to lightheadedness and dizziness in some individuals.

Additionally, Aloysia citrodora's ability to slow down heart rate and lower blood pressure may also contribute to feelings of dizziness or lightheadedness.

8. Causes headaches

Aloysia citrodora causes headaches due to its stimulating properties that can affect the nervous system.

The essential oil extracted from its leaves contains compounds like citral and geraniol, which can cause increased mental alertness and energy, but may also lead to a racing mind and potential headaches in some individuals.

This side effect is thought to be related to the oil's ability to increase blood flow to the brain, causing discomfort and tension.

9. Induces insomnia

Aloysia citrodora induces insomnia due to its stimulating properties, which can affect an individual's ability to fall asleep.

The citrus-like scent and aroma of the herb can stimulate the nervous system, leading to increased alertness and restlessness, making it difficult for people to wind down and sleep peacefully at night.

10. Aggravates anxiety

Aloysia citrodora aggravates anxiety due to its strong citrusy scent and potential interactions with the body's serotonin levels.

The aroma of lemon verbena can be overwhelming for some individuals, leading to increased feelings of nervousness and unease.

Furthermore, Aloysia citrodora contains compounds that may affect serotonin receptors in the brain, potentially exacerbating existing anxiety disorders or inducing new episodes of anxiety in sensitive individuals.
