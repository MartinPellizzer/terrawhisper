Citronella (Aloysia citrodora) Uses

Citronella is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, respiratory tract infections, and sinusitis. It helps alleviate symptoms like cough and mucous membrane irritation. Its anti-inflammatory properties may reduce inflammation in the respiratory system. It is also used for allergic rhinitis and tonsillitis.

Citronella (Aloysia citrodora) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Aloysia citrodora, or citronella, is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its expectorant properties help alleviate symptoms of bronchitis by loosening mucus and reducing inflammation in the airways. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease coughing and improve breathing.

2. Respiratory tract infections

Aloysia citrodora, or citronella, is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its essential oils have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections. It is often used in steam inhalations or as a supportive remedy during colds and bronchitis.

3. Respiratory infection

Aloysia citrodora, or citronella, is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections, such as coughs and congestion. It may also help clear airways and reduce mucus buildup. Regular use can support the body's natural defenses against respiratory pathogens.

4. Allergic rhinitis

Aloysia citrodora, or citronella, is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its antihistaminic and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and sneezing by soothing mucous membranes and inhibiting allergic reactions. Its essential oils are often applied topically or used in steam inhalation for respiratory relief.

5. Cough

Citronella (Aloysia citrodora) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate cough symptoms due to its expectorant and antispasmodic properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce bronchial irritation, making it useful for treating persistent or dry coughs. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to support respiratory health.

6. Sinusitis

Aloysia citrodora, or citronella, is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of sinusitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and inflammation, supporting respiratory health. Essential oils from the plant may be used in steam inhalation or topical applications to relieve sinus pressure and promote drainage.

7. Mucous membrane irritation

Citronella (Aloysia citrodora) may irritate mucous membranes due to its strong essential oils. This irritation is commonly experienced in the throat, nose, and digestive tract when consumed in large amounts. It is important to use citronella in moderation and consult a qualified herbalist to avoid adverse effects.

8. Mucous membrane inflammation

Aloysia citrodora, or citronella, is used in herbal medicine to soothe mucous membrane inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps reduce irritation and swelling in the respiratory and digestive tracts, making it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis and sore throat. Its essential oils are often diluted and applied topically or used in steam inhalation for targeted relief.

9. Tonsillitis

Citronella (Aloysia citrodora) has been traditionally used to support the treatment of tonsillitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Its essential oils, particularly citral, help reduce throat inflammation and combat bacterial infections. It is often used in herbal formulations or as a steam inhalation aid to relieve symptoms and support healing.

10. Respiratory infections

Aloysia citrodora, or citronella, is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections such as bronchitis and colds. It may aid in clearing mucus and reducing congestion when used as a steam inhalation or in herbal teas.
