Delphinium omeiense

What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium omeiense?

Delphinium omeiense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further classification reveals that it is a member of the order Ranunculales and falls under the family Ranunculaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Delphinium, and its unique species designation, omeiense.

What are the herbal preparations of Delphinium omeiense?

Delphinium omeiense is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste for medicinal use. The plant material is often dried and ground into a fine powder to facilitate ingestion or application. Practitioners may boil the roots or leaves in water to create a concentrated liquid extract known as a decoction. In some traditional practices, the crushed plant parts are mixed with a carrier to form a paste for external skin treatments. These various forms allow for different absorption rates and targeted therapeutic effects.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Delphinium omeiense can cause severe poisoning characterized by cardiac arrhythmia, respiratory failure, and intense gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains potent neurotoxins and alkaloids that disrupt the nervous system and heart rhythm upon contact or consumption. Symptoms often include vomiting, abdominal pain, and muscle tremors shortly after exposure. In extreme cases, the toxins can lead to convulsions or death due to the paralysis of vital organs. Careful handling is necessary to avoid skin irritation or accidental ingestion of any part of the specimen.
