Celtis koraiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis koraiensis?

Celtis koraiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls into the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, the plant is part of the order Rosales and the family Cannabaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Celtis, with its specific designation being the species koraiensis.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Celtis koraiensis contains medicinal properties primarily located within its bark and leaves. The bark is often harvested to prepare decoctions used for treating various inflammatory conditions and digestive ailments. Its leaves are frequently crushed or boiled to create topical applications or infusions intended to soothe skin irritations. Local practitioners sometimes utilize the roots in traditional formulations to address more systemic health concerns. These specific plant components provide the bioactive compounds necessary for these therapeutic uses.

    Celtis koraiensis has medicinal properties that are primarily utilized in traditional folk medicine to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and digestive issues.     What are the herbal preparations of Celtis koraiensis?

    Celtis koraiennis is prepared as a decoction from its bark, a leaf infusion, or a topical paste using the crushed foliage. The bark is typically boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal medicinal purposes. Leaf infusions are often brewed to soothe digestive issues or to alleviate minor inflammatory conditions. For external ailments, the fresh leaves are ground into a thick paste and applied directly to the skin as a poultice. These varied preparations allow different parts of the plant to target specific physiological needs.

While comprehensive clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, potential irritants within the plant's chemical composition may trigger redness or swelling in some individuals. Some people might also experience gastrointestinal discomfort if any part of the plant is ingested accidentally. It is important to exercise caution and avoid prolonged exposure to the plant's tissues to prevent these adverse reactions. Always consult a medical professional if you suspect a reaction has occurred after contact.
