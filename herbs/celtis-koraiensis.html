Celtis koraiensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celtis koraienensis has simple, alternate leaves with ovate to elliptical shapes, serrated margins, and prominent pinnate venation. The leaf surfaces are often slightly scabrous or textured, providing a distinct tactile quality. Its branches are characterized by woody stems that exhibit slightly swollen nodes where the petioles attach. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes. These fruits serve as a primary morphological feature for identification during the ripening season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Within this small territory, populations are primarily concentrated in rocky coastal crevices and scrubland habitats. Because it occupies such a restricted range, the species is highly susceptible to local environmental changes.
