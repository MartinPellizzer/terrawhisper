Corydalis pseudoalpestris

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis pseudoalpestris?

Corydalis pseudoalpestris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. The plant is further categorized under the family Papaveraceae, which defines its botanical characteristics. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Corydalis, and its specific identity is defined by the species epithet pseudoalpestris.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis pseudoalpestris has glaucous, pinnately dissected leaves and zygomorphic flowers characterized by long, nectar-bearing spurs. The foliage typically exhibits a bluish-green hue and is divided into numerous narrow, lanceolate segments. Its flowering structures feature a distinct upper petal that forms a prominent, elongated spur extending backward. The petals are often colored in shades of pale yellow or cream, sometimes marked with subtle purple veins.
