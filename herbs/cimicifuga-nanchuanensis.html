Cimicifuga nanchuanensis has rhizomes that are woody and perennial, stems that are erect and reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, and leaves that are petiolate with large, broadly ovate to cordate blades. The leaf margins are typically serrated or dentate, while the venation pattern is prominent across the expanded lamina. Its inflorescence consists of terminal racemes bearing numerous small, white to cream-colored flowers. Each flower possesses five distinct petals and several stamens characteristic of the Cimicifugaceae family.
