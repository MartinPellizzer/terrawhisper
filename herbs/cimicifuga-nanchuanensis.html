Cimicifuga nanchuanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cimicifuga nanchuanensis?

Cimicifuga nanchuanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically placed within the genus Cimicifuga, and is identified at the species level as nanchuanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cimicifuga nanchuanensis has rhizomes that are woody and perennial, stems that are erect and reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, and leaves that are petiolate with large, broadly ovate to cordate blades.

The leaf margins are typically serrated or dentate, while the venation pattern is prominent across the expanded lamina. Its inflorescence consists of terminal racemes bearing numerous small, white to cream-colored flowers. Each flower possesses five distinct petals and several stamens characteristic of the Cimicifugaceae family.

The overall vegetative structure is robust, allowing the plant to thrive in shaded forest understories.
