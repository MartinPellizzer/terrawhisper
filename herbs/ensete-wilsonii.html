Ensete wilsonii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ensete wilsonii?

Ensete wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. It follows the taxonomic order Zingiberales and is a member of the family Musaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Ensete and is specifically designated as the species wilsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ensete wilsonii has large, oblong-lanceolate leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length and are characterized by prominent parallel venation.

The plant features a thick, fleshy pseudostem formed by tightly overlapping leaf bases that function as a central structural axis. Its foliage is typically bright green and possesses a smooth, somewhat waxy texture on the upper surface. During the flowering stage, a large, branched inflorescence emerges from the center of the leaf rosette.

This structure eventually gives rise to small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered along the central panicle.
