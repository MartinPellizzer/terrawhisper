Ensete wilsonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ensete wilsonii has large, oblong-lanceolate leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length and are characterized by prominent parallel venation. The plant features a thick, fleshy pseudostem formed by tightly overlapping leaf bases that function as a central structural axis. Its foliage is typically bright green and possesses a smooth, somewhat waxy texture on the upper surface. During the flowering stage, a large, branched inflorescence emerges from the center of the leaf rosette. This structure eventually gives rise to small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered along the central panicle.
