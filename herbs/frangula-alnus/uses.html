Frangula (Frangula alnus) Uses

Frangula alnus is used to treat constipation and regulate bowel movements. It helps reduce gastrointestinal irritation, inflammation, and spasms. It may also alleviate bloating and discomfort associated with conditions like colitis and gastric issues. Its effects support proper gastrointestinal motility and overall digestive health.

Frangula (Frangula alnus) Monograph

1. Constipation

Frangula alnus is used to treat constipation due to its high mucilage content, which acts as a bulk-forming laxative. It softens stool and promotes regular bowel movements. The plant is typically prepared as a powder or decoction and is valued for its gentle yet effective action on the digestive system.

2. Gastrointestinal irritation

Frangula alnus is used to treat gastrointestinal irritation due to its mild astringent properties. It helps soothe inflamed tissues in the digestive tract and can reduce symptoms like diarrhea. Its resinous compounds provide a protective effect on the mucous membranes. It is often used in herbal formulations for digestive support.

3. Gastric discomfort

Frangula alnus is used to alleviate gastric discomfort due to its mild astringent properties. It helps soothe inflammation and reduce excess mucus in the digestive tract. Often prepared as a decoction, it supports digestion and can ease symptoms of indigestion and mild stomach upset. Its soothing effects make it a gentle remedy for digestive distress.

4. Bowel movement regulation

Frangula alnus is used to regulate bowel movements due to its high content of emodin, a compound with mild laxative properties. It helps soften stool and promote gentle intestinal motility, making it useful for mild constipation. However, it should be used cautiously to avoid overstimulation of the digestive system.

5. Gastrointestinal motility

Frangula alnus supports gastrointestinal motility by stimulating digestive tract movement. Its compounds enhance peristalsis, aiding in the efficient passage of food through the intestines. This makes it useful for conditions involving sluggish digestion or constipation. It is often used in herbal formulations to improve bowel function.

6. Gastrointestinal inflammation

Frangula alnus has been traditionally used to support gastrointestinal health, particularly in reducing inflammation of the digestive tract. Its high mucilage content helps soothe irritated tissues, making it beneficial for conditions like colitis and irritable bowel syndrome. It is often prepared as a demulcent tea or poultice to alleviate discomfort and promote healing.

7. Bloating

Frangula alnus is used to alleviate bloating due to its mild laxative properties. It helps reduce gas and discomfort by promoting gentle bowel movements. Typically prepared as a decoction, it supports digestive health without causing strong side effects. Its action is gentle, making it suitable for occasional use in mild digestive issues.

8. Gastrointestinal spasms

Frangula alnus has been traditionally used to relieve gastrointestinal spasms due to its mild cathartic properties. It helps soothe cramping and discomfort by gently stimulating bowel movements and reducing muscle tension in the digestive tract. This makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for managing conditions like irritable bowel syndrome and constipation.

9. Gastrointestinal discomfort

Frangula alnus is used to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort due to its mild laxative properties. It helps relieve constipation by stimulating bowel movements without causing harsh side effects. The compound responsible is emodin, which is found in its bark and leaves. It is often used in herbal formulations for digestive support.

10. Colitis

Frangula alnus has been traditionally used to treat colitis due to its high mucilage content, which soothes irritated intestinal lining. It helps reduce inflammation and frequent bowel movements. The bark and leaves are typically prepared as a decoction or powder. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects in digestive disorders.
