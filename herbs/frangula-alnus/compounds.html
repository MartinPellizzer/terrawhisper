Frangula (Frangula alnus) Compounds

Frangula alnus contains several primary active compounds, including tannins, which have astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while coumarin may support digestive health. Other compounds like glycosides, saponins, and terpenoids also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Frangula (Frangula alnus) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Frangula alnus. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its ability to reduce inflammation and support digestive health. Tannins in Frangula alnus are primarily found in the bark and seeds.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Frangula alnus. They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are commonly used in herbal medicine to support digestive health and reduce inflammation.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Frangula alnus. It contributes to the plant's laxative effects by stimulating bowel movements. This compound is particularly abundant in the bark and fruits of the plant. Its presence supports the traditional use of Frangula in treating constipation.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Frangula alnus. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and as a mild laxative. Coumarin also has anticoagulant properties, though its use requires caution due to potential toxicity at high doses.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Frangula alnus. It contributes to the plant's laxative properties by stimulating bowel movements. This compound is typically present in the bark and fruits of the plant. It is commonly used in herbal medicine for digestive support.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Frangula alnus. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and as a mild laxative. Saponins help stimulate bile production and improve gut motility. Their presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine for gastrointestinal health.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Frangula alnus are a group of bioactive compounds that contribute to the plant's medicinal properties. These compounds are known for their laxative effects and are primarily responsible for the plant's use in treating constipation and digestive issues. They are found in the bark and roots of Frangula alnus.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Frangula alnus. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These properties support its traditional use in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. Anthocyanin also enhances the plant's overall therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Frangula alnus contains volatile compounds like limonene and cineole. These oils are traditionally used for their digestive and antimicrobial properties. They may support gut health and help with mild digestive discomfort. However, their use should be guided by professional expertise.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Frangula alnus. It contributes to the plant's laxative properties by stimulating bowel movements. This compound is particularly effective in treating constipation and is commonly used in herbal medicine for digestive health.
