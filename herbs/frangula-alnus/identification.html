Botanical Identification of Frangula (Frangula alnus)

To identify Frangula (Frangula alnus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Frangula alnus belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rhamnales, family Rhamnaceae, genus Frangula, and species Frangula alnus. These classifications place it within the broader group of flowering plants and highlight its evolutionary relationships within the Rhamnaceae family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Frangula alnus, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Rumex alnus and Aesculus alnus. Common names include buckthorn and alder buckthorn, while regional names vary across Europe. Historically, it was referred to as alder tree or alder buckthorn in older botanical texts.

Morphological Characteristics

Frangula alnus is a shrub or small tree with a sprawling growth habit. It has a deep and extensive root system, which helps stabilize soil. Its stems are woody and often branch from the base. Leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The inflorescence is a catkin, with male and female flowers on separate plants. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule containing three seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Frangula alnus can be distinguished by its alternate, compound leaves with 3-5 pairs of oval leaflets, dark green on top and lighter underneath. The bark is smooth and dark grey, while the fruit is a small, yellowish, three-lobed drupe. It is often found near water, which helps differentiate it from similar species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Frangula alnus is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in lowland areas up to 1,000 meters in elevation.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Frangula alnus blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the early stages of seed development, when the fruits are still green and distinguishable from other species.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Frangula alnus belongs to the Rutaceae family, sharing traits with other members like citrus and buckthorn. Its closest relatives include other Frangula species, which are distinguished by similar leaf and fruit structures. The genus relations highlight its placement within the tribe Aegicerae, reflecting shared morphological and genetic characteristics.
