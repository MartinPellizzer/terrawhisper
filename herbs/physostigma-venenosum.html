What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Physostigma venenosum targets nicotinic acetylcholine receptors, acetylcholinesterase, and various voltage-gated ion channels through its bioactive alkaloids. The primary mechanism involves the inhibition of acetylcholinesterase, which prevents the breakdown of acetylcholine and increases its availability at the synaptic cleft. Many of the plant's tryptamine-type alkaloids also act as agonists or antagonists at serotonin receptors, modulating neurotransmission within the central nervous system. These interactions with transmembrane proteins allow the plant to exert significant effects on neuromuscular transmission and cognitive functions. Additionally, certain constituents may influence calcium channels, contributing to the complex physiological responses observed during exposure.
