Physostigma venenosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Physostigma venenosum has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, green stems and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically features prominent venation and a smooth texture across the leaf surface. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying a pale color that contrasts with the dark green vegetation. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy berries that contain the plant's seeds. The overall structure is compact yet allows the plant to spread through its branching stem system.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Physostiigma venenosum contains high concentrations of physostigmine, a potent parasympathomimetic alkaloid that acts as a reversible acetylcholinesterase inhibitor. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

The primary mechanism involves the inhibition of acetylcholinesterase, which prevents the breakdown of acetylcholine and increases its availability at the synaptic cleft. Many of the plant's tryptamine-type alkaloids also act as agonists or antagonists at serotonin receptors, modulating neurotransmission within the central nervous system. These interactions with transmembrane proteins allow the plant to exert significant effects on neuromuscular transmission and cognitive functions. Additionally, certain constituents may influence calcium channels, contributing to the complex physiological responses observed during exposure.
