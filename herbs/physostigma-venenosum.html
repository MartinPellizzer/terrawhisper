Physostigmma venenosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the pharmacological activities of Physostigma venenosum?

This plant has pharmacological activities including analgesic, cholinesterase-inhibitor, and ocular hypotensive properties through the action of its primary alkaloid, physostigmine. This specific alkaloid acts by inhibiting the enzyme acetylcholinesterase, which leads to increased levels of acetylcholine at neuromuscular junctions and cholinergic receptors. Due to these effects, the plant is highly significant in the medical treatment of glaucoma. By increasing the outflow of aqueous humor, it helps reduce intraocular pressure effectively. These diverse biological actions make the chemical constituents of Physostigma venenosum vital to neuropharmacology.

What side effects this plant can have?

The ingestion of its toxic compounds primarily affects the gastrointestinal system before progressing to the central nervous system. Victims may experience intense mental disturbances such as hallucinations and profound confusion. Physical complications can escalate into dangerous neurological events like seizures or life-threatening cardiovascular issues like tachycardia. In extreme cases, the toxins lead to critical respiratory depression that can be fatal.
