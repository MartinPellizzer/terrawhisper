Lepisorus ussuriensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lepisorus ussuriensis has a creeping rhizome that is scaly and produces pinnate fronds measuring between 10 and 30 centimeters in length. The leaves are typically once or twice pinnate with narrowly lanceolate to linear segments that exhibit slightly wavy margins. Each segment features small sori located on the underside of the pinnules, often protected by a thin indusium. The stipe is relatively slender and covered in fine hairs or scales near the base. These morphological traits distinguish the species from other members of the Polypodiaceae family.
