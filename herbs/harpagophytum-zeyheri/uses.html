Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) Uses

Feverfew is primarily used to treat conditions involving joint and muscle pain, such as osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and gout. It helps reduce inflammation and pain associated with rheumatic fever and rheumatism. It is also used for chronic back pain and muscle inflammation, offering relief through its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) Monograph

1. Osteoarthritis

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of osteoarthritis. It contains compounds that may reduce inflammation and pain. Studies suggest it can help ease joint stiffness and improve mobility. It is often used as a complementary therapy alongside conventional treatments.

2. Joint pain

Feverfew is traditionally used to alleviate joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling and stiffness, making it a common remedy for conditions like arthritis. The active compounds in Feverfew may inhibit pain signals and support joint mobility. Regular use under guidance can provide relief for chronic joint discomfort.

3. Rheumatoid arthritis

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) is used traditionally to alleviate symptoms of rheumatoid arthritis due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce joint pain, swelling, and stiffness, making it a valuable complementary therapy in herbal medicine for managing inflammatory arthritis.

4. Gout

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) is used in herbal medicine to support joint health and reduce inflammation associated with gout. Its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties help alleviate pain and swelling in gouty arthritis. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in managing gout symptoms.

5. Chronic back pain

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) is used traditionally to alleviate chronic back pain. Its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties help reduce muscle tension and inflammation. It is often taken as a supplement in herbal medicine practices for long-term pain management. Clinical studies suggest it may offer relief comparable to some conventional treatments.

6. Muscle pain

Feverfew is used to alleviate muscle pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce inflammation and ease discomfort in conditions like arthritis and muscle strains. The active compounds, such as parthenin, contribute to its effectiveness in managing pain and promoting recovery. Regular use may support relief from persistent muscle soreness and joint-related discomfort.

7. Joint inflammation

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) is used to reduce joint inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps alleviate pain and swelling associated with conditions like arthritis. The active compounds in feverfew inhibit inflammatory pathways in the body. It is often used as a complementary therapy in herbal medicine for managing inflammatory joint conditions.

8. Rheumatic fever

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of rheumatic fever due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce joint pain and inflammation associated with the condition. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture. Its efficacy is supported by historical use and some modern studies.

9. Rheumatism

Feverfew is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of rheumatism, such as joint pain and inflammation. Its active compounds, including parthenin and sesquiterpene lactones, are believed to reduce inflammatory responses in the body. It is often used in herbal medicine to support joint health and ease discomfort associated with rheumatic conditions.

10. Muscle inflammation

Feverfew is used to alleviate muscle inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce swelling and pain in muscles, often associated with conditions like arthritis or muscle strains. Its active compounds, such as parthenolide, inhibit inflammatory responses in the body. This makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for managing chronic and acute muscle inflammation.
