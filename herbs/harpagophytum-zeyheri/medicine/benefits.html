10 Health Benefits Of Harpagophytum Zeyheri (Devil'S Claw)

Harpagophytum zeyheri, also known as devil's claw, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation, pain relief, and improving sleep quality.

1. Reduces pain

Harpagophytum zeyheri reduces pain because it contains a unique combination of bioactive compounds that target multiple pathways involved in pain perception.

The plant's extracts have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory molecules, reduce the activity of nociceptors (pain-sensing neurons), and increase the release of natural pain-relievers such as serotonin and dopamine.

This multi-faceted approach helps to alleviate a range of painful conditions, from arthritis and fibromyalgia to back pain and headaches.

2. Alleviates osteoarthritis symptoms

Harpagophytum zeyheri alleviates osteoarthritis symptoms because of its potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

3. Improves rheumatoid arthritis symptoms

Harpagophytum zeyheri improves rheumatoid arthritis symptoms because of its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's active compounds, including harpagoside and procumbent saponins, have been shown to reduce inflammation and pain in the joints by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and enzymes.

This leads to a decrease in joint stiffness, swelling, and tenderness, allowing patients with rheumatoid arthritis to experience improved mobility and quality of life.

4. Decreases inflammation

Harpagophytum zeyheri decreases inflammation because of its potent anti-inflammatory compounds, including harpagoside and beta-sitosterol.

These bioactive molecules have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), and reduce the activity of inflammatory signaling pathways, thereby mitigating inflammatory responses.

Additionally, devil's claw has been found to exhibit antioxidant properties, further contributing to its anti-inflammatory effects by neutralizing free radicals that can exacerbate inflammation.
