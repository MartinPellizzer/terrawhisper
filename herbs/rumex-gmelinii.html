Rumex gmelinii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rumex gmelinii has erect, perennial stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, bearing lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire or slightly wavy margins. The leaf bases are typically cordate or truncate, while the upper leaves become progressively smaller and more narrow. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal panicles featuring small, greenish flowers enclosed by specialized structures called valves. These valves are characterized by distinct, enlarged, and winged margins that aid in seed dispersal. The resulting fruits are small achenes located at the center of these distinctive winged structures.

      Rumex gmelinii is prepared primarily as an aqueous infusion or decoction using the dried leaves or whole aerial parts of the plant.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Rumex gmelinii can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential toxicity if consumed in large quantities due to the presence of oxalates. High levels of oxalic acid in the plant may lead to kidney stone formation or renal irritation if ingested regularly. Some individuals might experience contact dermatitis or localized rashes after handling the foliage directly. There is also a risk of stomach upset or nausea following accidental ingestion. Careful identification is necessary to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
