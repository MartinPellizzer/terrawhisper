Lilium dauricum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium dauricum?

Lilium dauricum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the biological hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Liliales, it is further organized into the family Liliaceae. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Lilium, and its unique species designation, dauricum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium dauricum has lanceolate leaves that are arranged in dense whorls or spirals along a tall, erect stem and produce bright orange, trumpet-shaped flowers with dark spots. The foliage is typically dark green and pointed, providing a sharp contrast to the vibrant blooms. Each flower features six tepals that curve outward, often displaying prominent nectar guides in the form of dark spots near the center. The plant grows from a bulbous root system that stores energy for seasonal development. These morphological traits are characteristic of the species' adaptation to its specific highland habitats.
