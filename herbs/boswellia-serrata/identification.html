Botanical Identification of Indian Frankincense (Boswellia serrata)

To identify Indian Frankincense (Boswellia serrata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Boswellia serrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Burserales, family Burseraceae, genus Boswellia, and species Boswellia serrata. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader context of plant taxonomy.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Boswellia serrata is the scientific name for Indian Frankincense. It was first described by the botanist Carl von Linné in 1753. Common synonyms include Boswellia carteri and Boswellia thurifera. It is known by various regional and historical names, reflecting its widespread use and cultural significance across different regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Boswellia serrata is a small to medium-sized tree with a straight, often branching trunk. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly in the soil. The stem is smooth and grayish-brown, while the leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a leathery texture. The inflorescence consists of clustered, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a capsule containing small, dark brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Boswellia serrata can be distinguished by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and yellowish-white resinous sap. It differs from similar species like Boswellia frereana through its leaf shape and the texture of its bark. The presence of specific aromatic compounds also aids in its identification. These features are key for accurate botanical recognition in herbal medicine.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Boswellia serrata, or Indian Frankincense, is native to the dry, arid regions of India, northern Africa, and the Middle East. It thrives in warm, dry climates with minimal rainfall. It prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Boswellia serrata blooms from December to March, producing small yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from April to June. Harvesting of the resin, used for identification, typically happens during the dry season, from October to February.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Boswellia serrata is closely related to other Boswellia species within the Burseraceae family. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with species like Boswellia sacra and Boswellia carterii. These relationships are evident in their resin composition and ecological adaptations. The genus is characterized by its aromatic bark and ability to produce frankincense.
