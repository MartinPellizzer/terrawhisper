10 Medicinal Constituents Of Boswellia Serrata (Frankincense)

Boswellia serrata has active constituents such as boswellic acids, beta-boswellic acid, and alpha-pinene.

These constituents are responsible for its anti-inflammatory, anti-arthritic, and anti-oxidant properties. The boswellic acids have been shown to inhibit 5-lipoxygenase, an enzyme involved in inflammation. This can lead to a reduction in symptoms of arthritis and asthma, improving the quality of life for individuals suffering from these conditions.

Regular use of frankincense has also been linked to reduced pain and inflammation.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Boswellia serrata.

1. Akba

Boswellia serrata akba is a unique and highly prized resin extracted from the bark of the Boswellia tree.

It has been used for centuries in traditional Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation, pain, and stress. Akba is believed to have potent anti-inflammatory properties, which may help reduce swelling and alleviate symptoms associated with arthritis and other chronic conditions.

Its use has also been linked to improved mental clarity and emotional balance.

2. Beta-boswellic acid

Boswellia serrata beta-boswellic acid is a pentacyclic triterpenoid compound extracted from the resin of this plant.

It has been extensively studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which may be beneficial in treating conditions such as arthritis and inflammatory bowel disease.

Beta-boswellic acid has also shown antioxidant activity, which can help protect cells from damage caused by free radicals.

3. Kurkuol

Boswellia serrata kurkuol is a unique extract derived from the resin of the Boswellia serrata tree.

Kurkuol has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for centuries to treat various health conditions, including inflammation and arthritis.

Its bioactive compound, 3-kurkumenoic acid, exhibits potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a popular natural remedy for promoting overall well-being and alleviating chronic pain.

4. Acetyl-11-keto-beta-boswellic acid (akba)

Boswellia serrata acetyl-11-keto-beta-boswellic acid (AKBA) is a bioactive compound extracted from the resin of this tree.
