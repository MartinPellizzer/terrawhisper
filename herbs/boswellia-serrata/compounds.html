Indian Frankincense (Boswellia serrata) Compounds

The primary compounds in Indian Frankincense include terpenoids, which are responsible for its aromatic properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Essential oils, coumarin, and glycosides also play roles in its therapeutic effects.

Indian Frankincense (Boswellia serrata) Monograph

1. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Boswellia serrata, commonly known as Indian Frankincense. These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, making them important for traditional and modern herbal medicine applications.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Boswellia serrata, commonly known as Indian Frankincense. These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for conditions like arthritis and inflammatory disorders.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Indian Frankincense (Boswellia serrata). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties by inhibiting enzymes involved in inflammatory responses. This compound supports the traditional use of Boswellia serrata in treating conditions like arthritis and digestive disorders.

4. Essential oil

The essential oil of Boswellia serrata is a key active compound derived from the resin of the Indian Frankincense tree. It contains boswellic acids, which have potent anti-inflammatory properties. This oil is used in herbal medicine to support joint health and reduce inflammation. Its traditional use aligns with modern research on its therapeutic potential.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Boswellia serrata, commonly known as Indian Frankincense. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties by inhibiting enzymes involved in inflammatory responses. Tannins also help in reducing swelling and pain, making Boswellia serrata a valuable herb in herbal medicine for conditions like arthritis.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Indian Frankincense (Boswellia serrata). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. Coumarin helps modulate enzymatic activity and reduces oxidative stress, supporting the therapeutic uses of Boswellia serrata in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Indian Frankincense (Boswellia serrata). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties. Glycosides in Boswellia serrata are believed to inhibit enzymes involved in inflammatory responses. These compounds are central to the plant's traditional and modern medicinal uses.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Boswellia serrata, known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating joint pain and inflammatory conditions. Saponin helps modulate immune responses and may support overall wellness. It is one of the key bioactive components in Indian Frankincense.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Boswellia serrata, commonly known as Indian Frankincense. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by influencing inflammatory responses and supporting joint health. Alkaloids in Boswellia serrata are part of the resinous sap that has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its therapeutic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a compound found in Boswellia serrata, known as Indian Frankincense. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects. This compound plays a role in the traditional use of Boswellia serrata for treating conditions like arthritis and respiratory issues.
