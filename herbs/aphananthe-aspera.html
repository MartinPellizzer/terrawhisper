Aphananthe aspera has perennial, creeping rhizomes that produce small, tufted, evergreen leaves which are linear, lanceolate, and characterized by a distinctly scabrid or rough texture. These flowers are usually solitary or few in number and lack prominent petals, often appearing somewhat inconspicuous to the eye.
