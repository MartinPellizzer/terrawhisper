Sedirea subparishii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedirea subparishii has an erect, slender stem that typically reaches a height of 10 to 40 centimeters and is characterized by a smooth, glabrous surface. The leaves are arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem and possess a linear to lanceolate shape with entire margins. Each leaf features a prominent central vein and tapers toward a pointed apex. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, terminal spikes. These flowering structures are often surrounded by tiny, scale-like bracts that protect the developing reproductive organs.
