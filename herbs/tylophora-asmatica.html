Tylophora Asmatica Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Tylophora asmatica, commonly known as Indian Bellflower, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia, valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.

This herb is known for its potential to support cognitive function, reduce anxiety, and improve mental clarity.

The bioactive constituents in Tylophora asmatica include alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Tylophora asmatica cardiac glycosides, including compounds like tylophorine and its derivatives, exhibit strong cardiotoxic effects by inhibiting the Na+/K+-ATPase pump, making them historically significant in traditional medicine for heart-related ailments.

Tylophora asmatica tylophorine is a key alkaloidal component that has been studied for its potential anticancer properties, particularly in inhibiting tumor cell proliferation and inducing apoptosis through various molecular pathways.

Tylophora asmatica alkaloids, such as tylophorine and other related compounds, are valued for their diverse pharmacological activities, including antitumor, antimicrobial, and neuroprotective effects, which have spurred modern research into their therapeutic applications.
