What's the taxonomical classification of Sedum lineare?

Sedum lineare belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Saxifragales. This plant is a member of the family Crasulaceae, specifically placed within the genus Sedum, and is identified by the species name lineare.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedum lineare has succulent, linear leaves that are cylindrical or semi-cylindrical in shape and typically measure between 5 and 25 millimeters in length. These narrow, fleshy leaves grow in an alternate or spiral arrangement along creeping, trailing stems. The foliage often exhibits a bright green color that can transition to reddish hues under stressful environmental conditions. Small, star-shaped flowers emerge from the stem tips, characterized by five spreading petals. The overall growth habit is low-growing and mat-forming, allowing the plant to spread across rocky surfaces or sandy soil.
