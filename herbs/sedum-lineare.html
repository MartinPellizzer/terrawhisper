Sedum lineare

What's the taxonomical classification of Sedum lineare?

Sedum lineare belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Saxifragales. This plant is a member of the family Crasulaceae, specifically placed within the genus Sedum, and is identified by the species name lineare.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedum lineare has succulent, linear leaves that are cylindrical or semi-cylindrical in shape and typically measure between 5 and 25 millimeters in length. These narrow, fleshy leaves grow in an alternate or spiral arrangement along creeping, trailing stems. The foliage often exhibits a bright green color that can transition to reddish hues under stressful environmental conditions. Small, star-shaped flowers emerge from the stem tips, characterized by five spreading petals. The overall growth habit is low-growing and mat-forming, allowing the plant to spread across rocky surfaces or sandy soil.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the western portion of North America, specifically ranging from British Columbia in Canada down through California and into parts of Mexico. It is primarily found in coastal and montane environments where rocky slopes and sandy soils are prevalent. Within these regions, it often colonizes arid habitats such as cliffs, dunes, and open scrublands. The distribution is heavily influenced by the availability of well-draining substrates and specific moisture patterns along the Pacific coast. This localized range makes it a characteristic species of the Mediterranean-type climates found in these western territories.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds hyperin, astragaloside, and isoquercitrin. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile found within the species. Research into these molecules often focuses on their potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Scientists study how these compounds interact with biological systems to develop new therapeutic applications. Understanding the concentration of these substances helps in evaluating the medicinal efficacy of Sedum lineare.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", the HPLC fingerprint of Sedum lineare was used to identify the medicinal compounds hyperin, isoquercitrin, and astragaloside. The study found that Sedum lineare growing in the same area but different environments were basically identical. However, there were remarkable differences in Sedum lineare from the same place when harvested during different periods, specifically with changes in the area of most common peaks and a slightly different peak number. The HPLC fingerprints of the two species showed 4 common peaks. The optimal harvest period for these species should be the full-bloom stage.
