Picea meyeri

What's the taxonomical classification of Picea meyeri?

Picea meyeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida, the subclass Pinidae, and the order Pinales. Within this framework, it is a member of the family Pinaceae and falls under the genus Picea, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species meyeri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picea meyeri has a conical to pyramidal crown with branches that are densely packed and slightly drooping towards the tips. The needles are short, stiff, and pointed, typically measuring between 1 and 2 centimeters in length. These needles are arranged spirally around the twigs and exhibit a deep green color on both surfaces. The bark of the tree transitions from a reddish-brown to a grayish hue as the specimen matures. Small, egg-shaped cones develop on the branches, eventually turning brown as they reach full maturity.
