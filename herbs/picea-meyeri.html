Picea meyeri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picea meyeri has a conical to pyramidal crown with branches that are densely packed and slightly drooping towards the tips. The needles are short, stiff, and pointed, typically measuring between 1 and 2 centimeters in length. These needles are arranged spirally around the twigs and exhibit a deep green color on both surfaces. The bark of the tree transitions from a reddish-brown to a grayish hue as the specimen matures. Small, egg-shaped cones develop on the branches, eventually turning brown as they reach full maturity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to a narrow region of the high-altitude mountains in western China, specifically within the Sichuan and Gansu provinces. It typically occupies subalpine zones at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 3,500 meters above sea level. These trees are found in cool, moist habitats such as slopes and mountain ridges where they thrive in temperate climates. The species is highly localized, meaning its natural range is restricted to specific mountain ranges rather than being widely spread across the continent.
