Gomphrena celosioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Gomphrena celosioides?

Gomphrena celosioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Amaranthaceae, specifically within the genus Gomphrena, and is identified by the unique species name celosioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gomphrena celosioides has erect, branched stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and support opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a light green coloration that provides a backdrop for the inflorescence. Its flowers are small and clustered into dense, globular heads that transition from pink to deep magenta as they mature. These colorful flower heads are subtended by papery, bract-like structures that give the blooms a distinctive, everlasting appearance. The entire plant structure is adapted to thrive in warm, sunny environments through its sturdy, herbaceous growth habit.

What are the pharmacological activities of Gomphrena celosioides?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anticancer, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective properties. Its antioxidant capacity helps neutralize harmful free radicals to prevent cellular damage within the body. The plant also demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects by reducing pain and swelling associated with various conditions. Furthermore, research indicates that its extracts possess anticancer potential by inhibiting the growth of certain malignant cells. Finally, its hepatoprotective qualities assist in safeguarding the liver from chemical or oxidative injury.

According to a study published by "Regulatory toxicology and pharmacology : RTP", Gomphrena celosioides is a medicinal plant used as a natural analgesic. For the acute toxicity test, 2000 mg/kg of the ethanolic extract from the aerial parts of Gomphrena celosioides (EEGC) was administered orally to male and female Wistar rats. In the subacute toxicity test, Swiss mice received 75, 150, or 300 mg/kg of EEGC. Animals treated with an only dose of 2000 mg/kg EEGC showed no clinical signs of toxicity, indicating that the LD50 is higher than this dose. According to the Globally Harmonized System of classification, the EEGC dosages can be in Category 5, which is the least toxic or non-toxic one.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds aurantiamide and aurantiamide acetate. These specific alkaloids are primarily located within the plant's tissues and contribute to its pharmacological profile. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential therapeutic applications in various medical treatments. The presence of such chemical constituents distinguishes Gomphrena celosioides from other species in its genus. Understanding these molecular structures is essential for advancing botanical drug discovery.

According to a study published by "Iranian journal of pharmaceutical research : IJPR", the plant Gomphrena celosioides C. Mart. was subjected to soxhlet extraction with n-hexane. From the flakes recovered from the n-hexane extract on cooling, two specific compounds were isolated. These medicinal compounds were separated using column chromatography with 5% chloroform in n-hexane. The structures of the isolated aurantiamide and aurantiamide acetate were elucidated through spectroscopic analysis using IR, NMR ((1)H and (13)C), and EI-MS. This research represents the first report of the isolation of these compounds in Gomphrena celosioides.

The study observed that Gomphrena celosioides did not enhance the phagocytic function of macrophages.
