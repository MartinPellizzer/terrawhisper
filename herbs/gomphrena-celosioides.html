Gomphrena celosioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gomphrena celosioides has erect, branched stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and support opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a light green coloration that provides a backdrop for the inflorescence. Its flowers are small and clustered into dense, globular heads that transition from pink to deep magenta as they mature. These colorful flower heads are subtended by papery, bract-like structures that give the blooms a distinctive, everlasting appearance. The entire plant structure is adapted to thrive in warm, sunny environments through its sturdy, herbaceous growth habit.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Gomphrena celosioides can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, redness, or itching when the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience localized swelling or irritation if they handle the foliage or flowers without protection. While there is limited clinical data on systemic toxicity, ingesting the plant could potentially lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or nausea. It is also possible for people with known allergies to pollen to react to the airborne particles released by the blooms. Always exercise caution and consult a medical professional if any adverse symptoms occur after exposure.
