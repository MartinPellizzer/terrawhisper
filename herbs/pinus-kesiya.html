Pinus kesiya

What's the taxonomical classification of Pinus kesiya?

Pinus kesiya belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Pinales and the family Pinaceae. Ultimately, it is placed within the genus Pinus, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, kesiya.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pinus kesiya contains bioactive compounds such as alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, limonene, and various diterpenoids that provide its medicinal properties. These terpenoids are primarily extracted from the plant's resin and essential oils. They exhibit significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant activities when applied or processed correctly. Some studies suggest these chemical constituents can assist in wound healing and provide relief from respiratory congestion. The specific chemical profile of the species allows it to be used in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pinus kesiya?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and respiratory ailments through its bioactive compounds. The essential oils extracted from its needles and bark exhibit significant antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help combat various pathogens. When applied topically, the resin can assist in the healing process of cuts and minor burns by reducing swelling. Some traditional practices also involve using infusions of the plant to alleviate symptoms of coughs and congestion. These medicinal applications rely heavily on the presence of terpenes and other phytochemicals found within the species.
