What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tolypanthus esquirolii has a prostrate or decumbent growth habit characterized by woody, trailing stems and small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and somewhat leathery. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blooms are generally pale pink to white and hang delicately from the stems.
