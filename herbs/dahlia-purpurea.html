Dahlia purpurea

Dahlia purpurea, commonly known as the Purple Dahlia, is a flowering plant species in the dahlia genus, native to Mexico and Central America.

The Purple Dahlia is valued for its medicinal and ornamental properties.

The plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues.

The bioactive constituents of Dahlia purpurea include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic actions such as sedative, antispasmodic, and antiseptic effects.

Medicinal Parts of Dahlia purpurea

Dahlia purpurea has many medicinal parts, such as its roots, leaves, flowers, and tubers.

The roots of the dahlia plant contain inulin, a type of soluble fiber, which can help regulate blood sugar levels and support digestive health.

The leaves and tubers of the plant contain antioxidants and flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, which have anti-inflammatory properties and may help protect against cell damage.

The flowers of the dahlia plant contain saponins and glycosides, which have been traditionally used to treat skin conditions and reduce inflammation.
