Saxifraga stolonifera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga stolonifera has a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are dark green with distinct reddish margins and a slightly succulent texture. These leaves are arranged in a compact circle at the base of the plant to maximize surface area for light absorption. From the center of this rosette, a slender flowering stalk rises to bear small, five-petaled white flowers. The most defining morphological feature is the production of long, thin runners called stolons that extend from the parent plant to form new clonal offspring. These runners eventually develop small plantlets at their tips, allowing the species to spread effectively across its habitat.
