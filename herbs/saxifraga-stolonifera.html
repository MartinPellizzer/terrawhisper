Saxifraga stolonifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Saxifraga stolonifera?

Saxifraga stolonifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's taxonomic progression continues through the order Saxifragaales and the family Saxifragaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by its genus, Saxifraga, and its specific species name, stolonifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga stolonifera has a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are dark green with distinct reddish margins and a slightly succulent texture. These leaves are arranged in a compact circle at the base of the plant to maximize surface area for light absorption. From the center of this rosette, a slender flowering stalk rises to bear small, five-petaled white flowers. The most defining morphological feature is the production of long, thin runners called stolons that extend from the parent plant to form new clonal offspring. These runners eventually develop small plantlets at their tips, allowing the species to spread effectively across its habitat.

According to a study published by "Journal, genetic engineering & biotechnology", silver nanoparticles synthesized by endophytic fungi from the medicinal plant Saxifraga stolonifera were assessed for their bioactivities. These nanoparticles demonstrated antimicrobial, anticancer, and antioxidant effects. The anticancer potential was observed in the A549, A375, HeLa, and HepG2 cell lines. At a concentration of 200 µg/mL, the nanoparticles showed scavenging rates of 90.49 % against DPPH, 60.38 % against ABTS, and 38.76 % against OH radicals. Furthermore, treatment at 25.00 µg/mL reduced intracellular ROS levels in HaCaT cells by 44.8 % while enhancing SOD, CAT, and GSH activities by 106.7 %, 2036.1 %, and 43.5 % respectively.
