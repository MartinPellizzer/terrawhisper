Lysimachia hemsleyana

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia hemsleyana?

Lysimachia hemsleyana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. The plant is further categorized into the family Primulaceae, placing it within the genus Lysimachia, and is specifically identified by its species name, hemsleyana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia hemsleyana has creeping rhizomes that produce small, lanceolate leaves arranged in opposite pairs along slender, ascending stems.

The foliage typically features a distinct variegated pattern, often displaying creamy white or pale yellow margins against a green center. Small, yellow, star-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These blossoms consist of five distinct petals that surround a central cluster of stamens.

The plant's overall structure allows it to spread effectively across moist soil surfaces through its underground runners.
