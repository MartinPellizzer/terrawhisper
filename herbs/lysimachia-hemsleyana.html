Lysimachia hemsleyana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia hemsleyana has creeping rhizomes that produce small, lanceolate leaves arranged in opposite pairs along slender, ascending stems. The foliage typically features a distinct variegated pattern, often displaying creamy white or pale yellow margins against a green center. Small, yellow, star-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These blossoms consist of five distinct petals that surround a central cluster of stamens. The plant's overall structure allows it to spread effectively across moist soil surfaces through its underground runners.
