Yellow milkvetch

Astragalus membranaceus

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is a perennial legume native to Asia, known for its use in traditional Chinese medicine to support immune function and improve conditions such as fatigue and respiratory infections.

Taxonomy

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae, belonging to the genus Astragalus within the order Fabales.

Morphology

The Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems that grow up to 60 cm tall, with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters at the顶端 of the stems.

Distribution

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of China, particularly in the northern and western provinces such as Gansu, Qinghai, Xinjiang, and Ningxia.

Traditional Uses

Yellow Milkvetch has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to support kidney function, promote bone health, and enhance vitality, often prepared as a decoction or tonic.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional Chinese medicine, Tibetan medicine, and Native American healing practices, Yellow Milkvetch has been historically used for its purported health benefits.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is characterized by the presence of flavonoids, saponins, polysaccharides, alkaloids, and volatile oils, with the most notable compounds including astragaloside IV, formononetin, and calycosin, which contribute to its traditional use in enhancing immune function and promoting overall vitality.

Pharmacology

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) exhibits immunomodulatory, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that support immune function, reduce inflammation, and protect cellular integrity.

Medicinal Uses

Yellow milkvetch is used to treat conditions such as fatigue, diabetes, hypertension, inflammation, and respiratory disorders, as well as to support immune function and enhance vitality.

Preparations

Yellow milkvetch is used to prepare various herbal formulations, including decoctions, tinctures, and powders, which are commonly utilized in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Safety

While Astragalus membranaceus is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, it may cause side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications, particularly immunosuppressants and anticoagulants.

Evidence

Clinical evidence suggests that Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) may support immune function and has been studied for its potential role in enhancing recovery from illness and stress, though more research is needed to confirm its efficacy and safety in various conditions.

Cultivation

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is typically cultivated in arid or semi-arid regions with well-drained soil and full sunlight, often in regions of China where it is traditionally harvested for its medicinal roots.

Processing

The roots of Yellow Milkvetch are typically harvested in the early autumn when the plant has accumulated the highest concentration of active compounds, then cleaned, sliced, and dried in a well-ventilated area to preserve potency and prevent mold.

Identification

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of China, characterized by its upright stems, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August.

Sustainability

The sustainability of Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) as a herbal medicine is a critical concern due to its status as an endangered species, requiring careful harvesting practices and conservation efforts to ensure its continued availability and ecological balance.

Related

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is closely related to other species within the Astragalus genus, including Astragalus adsurgens, Astragalus adsurgens var. adsurgens, and Astragalus adsurgens var. mongolicus, which are also used in traditional Chinese medicine for similar therapeutic purposes.

Research Gap

While there is growing interest in the pharmacological potential of Yellow Milkvetch, significant research gaps remain in understanding its long-term safety, optimal dosage, and mechanisms of action in human clinical trials.
