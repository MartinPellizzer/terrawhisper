Yellow milkvetch

Astragalus membranaceus

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is a perennial legume native to Asia, known for its use in traditional Chinese medicine to support immune function and improve conditions such as fatigue and respiratory infections.

Taxonomy

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae, belonging to the genus Astragalus within the order Fabales.

Morphology

The Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems that grow up to 60 cm tall, with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters at the顶端 of the stems.

Distribution

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of China, particularly in the northern and western provinces such as Gansu, Qinghai, Xinjiang, and Ningxia.

Traditional Uses

Yellow Milkvetch has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to support kidney function, promote bone health, and enhance vitality, often prepared as a decoction or tonic.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional Chinese medicine, Tibetan medicine, and Native American healing practices, Yellow Milkvetch has been historically used for its purported health benefits.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is characterized by the presence of flavonoids, saponins, polysaccharides, alkaloids, and volatile oils, with the most notable compounds including astragaloside IV, formononetin, and calycosin, which contribute to its traditional use in enhancing immune function and promoting overall vitality.

Pharmacology

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) exhibits immunomodulatory, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that support immune function, reduce inflammation, and protect cellular integrity.

Botanical Identification

Yellow Milkvetch, scientifically known as Astragalus membranaceus, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of northern China. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is also known as Huang Qi in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant features a deep taproot, trifoliate leaves, and yellow pea-like flowers. It has been referred to by various synonyms in herbal texts, including Milkvetch and Chinese Astragalus.
