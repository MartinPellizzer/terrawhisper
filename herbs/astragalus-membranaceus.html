Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is a perennial legume native to Asia, known for its use in traditional Chinese medicine to support immune function and improve conditions such as fatigue and respiratory infections.

What is Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus Membranaceus)?

Yellow Milkvetch, also known as Astragalus membranaceus, is a perennial legume native to the northwestern regions of China, widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported immune-boosting and adaptogenic properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Yellow Milkvetch, or Astragalus membranaceus, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of northern China, Mongolia, and Siberia, belonging to the Fabaceae family and the genus Astragalus.

Common Names and Synonyms

Yellow Milkvetch, also known as Astragalus membranaceus, is commonly referred to by various names including Huang Qi, Chinese milkvetch, and membranous milkvetch, and is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Plant Description and Morphology

The Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems that grow up to 60 cm tall, with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters at the顶端 of the stems.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of northern China, including the provinces of Gansu, Shaanxi, Qinghai, and Xinjiang, as well as parts of Mongolia and the western United States.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The roots, leaves, and flowers of Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) are used medicinally, with the roots being the most commonly utilized part due to their high concentration of bioactive compounds.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) includes bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, saponins, polysaccharides, alkaloids, and glycosides, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for immune support and anti-inflammatory effects.

Pharmacological Properties

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) exhibits immunomodulatory, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, primarily due to its high content of polysaccharides, flavonoids, and saponins, which contribute to its traditional use in enhancing immune function and supporting overall health.

Mechanisms of Action

The mechanisms of action of Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) involve immunostimulatory effects through the enhancement of T-cell and macrophage activity, modulation of cytokine production, and the activation of various signaling pathways such as NF-κB and MAPK, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Yellow milkvetch is used to treat conditions such as fatigue, diabetes, hypertension, inflammation, and respiratory disorders, as well as to support immune function and enhance vitality.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is typically prepared as a dried root decoction, powder, or tincture, with the root being the primary part used in formulations.

Dosage and Administration

The standard dosage of Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) for adults is typically 3 to 10 grams per day, usually divided into two or three doses, though specific dosing may vary depending on the form of the herb (e.g., powder, tincture, or extract) and the individual's health condition.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Astragalus membranaceus is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, it may cause side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications, particularly immunosuppressants and anticoagulants.

Drug Interactions

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) may interact with certain medications by affecting the immune system, potentially altering the efficacy or increasing the risk of side effects when used concurrently with immunosuppressants, anticoagulants, or chemotherapy drugs.

Toxicity and Precautions

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts as a herbal supplement, but it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals and should be avoided by those with hypersensitivity to legumes or allergic reactions to related plants.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) is typically cultivated in arid or semi-arid regions with well-drained soil and full sunlight, often in regions of China where it is traditionally harvested for its medicinal roots.

Processing and Extraction

The roots of Yellow Milkvetch are typically harvested in the early autumn when the plant has accumulated the highest concentration of active compounds, then cleaned, sliced, and dried in a well-ventilated area to preserve potency and prevent mold.

Storage and Shelf Life

Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, and its shelf life is typically 2 to 3 years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Yellow Milkvetch (Astragalus membranaceus) has primarily focused on its immunomodulatory properties, with studies suggesting it may enhance immune function through the stimulation of T-cell activity and the production of cytokines, although clinical evidence supporting its efficacy in human therapeutic applications remains limited.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Yellow Milkvetch has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for centuries to support kidney function, strengthen the immune system, and promote longevity, with historical records dating back to the Shennong Ben Cao Jing, one of the earliest herbal texts.

Regulatory Status
