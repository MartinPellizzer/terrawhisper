Betula albosinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Betula albosinensis?

Betula albosinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the evolutionary hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Fagales and the family Betulaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Betula, with the specific epithet albosinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Betula albosinensis has an upright, deciduous growth habit characterized by slender, silver-white bark and ovate-shaped leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically features acute tips and a doubly serrated edge that creates a textured appearance. Its branches are often pendulous, contributing to a graceful, weeping silhouette when the plant reaches maturity. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in catkins, which transition into winged nutlets for wind dispersal. The woody stems possess lenticels that appear as small, horizontal pores against the pale bark.
