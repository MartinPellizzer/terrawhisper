Monachosorum davallioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Monachosorum davallioides?

Monachosorum davallioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is further categorized within the family Dennstaedtiaceae, specifically under the genus Monachosorum, with its specific designation being the species davallioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Monachosorum davallioides has large, simple, cordate leaves that are dark green and possess a prominent venation pattern characteristic of its genus.

The leaf blades are typically thin and membranous, extending from a long petiole that attaches to the base of the plant. These leaves often display a glossy texture and can reach significant sizes depending on the environmental moisture levels. The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead utilizing its robust petioles to elevate the foliage toward light sources.

Its morphology is closely adapted to shaded, humid tropical forest floors where it grows as an understory herb.
