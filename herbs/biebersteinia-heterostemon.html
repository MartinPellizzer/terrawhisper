Biebersteinia heterostemon

What's the taxonomical classification of Biebersteinia heterostemon?

Biebersteinia heterostemon belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Sapindales and is a member of the family Biebersteiniaceae. Specifically, it is placed within the genus Biebersteinia, with the full biological designation being the species Biebersteinia heterostemon.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal alkaloids galegine, cis-4-hydroxygalegine, and trans-4-hydroxygalegine. These specific chemical constituents are identified as primary bioactive compounds within the Biebersteinia heterostemon species. Researchers have focused on these molecules due to their potential pharmacological properties and unique structural configurations. The presence of both cis and trans isomers of 4-hydroxygalegine allows for detailed comparative studies of their biological activities. Understanding these specific compounds is essential for exploring the plant's therapeutic applications in modern medicine.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", three isoprenyl guanidine derivatives were isolated from the whole plant of Biebersteinia heterostemon. These specific compounds were identified as galegine (1), cis-4-hydroxygalegine (2), and trans-4-hydroxygalegine (3). The study utilized spectroscopic evidence including MS, IR, NMR, and X-ray crystallographic analysis to identify the components from the ethanol extract. These 3 compounds were isolated from this plant for the first time.
