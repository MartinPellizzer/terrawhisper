Biebersteinia heterostemon

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Biebersteinia heterostemon has heterophyllous leaves that vary significantly in shape between the basal and upper portions of the stem. The basal leaves are typically petiolate and possess larger, more rounded or ovate lobes compared to the upper foliage. As the plant climbs, the cauline leaves become smaller, more reduced, and often appear almost sessile or lanceolate. The stems are slender and characterized by a twining habit that allows the plant to scramble through surrounding vegetation. Its floral structure features small, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils.
