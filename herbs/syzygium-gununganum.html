What is Syzygium gununganum?

Syzygium gununganum is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to Indonesia.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, measuring 10-20 cm in length, and 3-6 cm in width, with a cuneate base and a rounded apex, and are glabrous on both surfaces. The inflorescence is a axillary corymb, with small, greenish-white flowers that are 4-5 mm in diameter, and the fruit is a small, red drupe, 1-2 cm in diameter, with a single seed.

The species has been found to grow in tropical forests at elevations of up to 1,500 meters above sea level.
