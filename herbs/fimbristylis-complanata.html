Fimbristylis complanata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fimbristylis complanata has a creeping rhizome that produces small, dark green, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and distinct venation. The plant typically exhibits a tufted growth habit with leaves that are often slightly fleshy in texture. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The fertile fronds or stems are generally more slender and darker than the sterile foliage. These morphological features collectively allow the species to thrive in damp, shaded forest environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Fimbristylis complanata can cause severe dermal irritation, localized swelling, and acute gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap often results in contact dermatitis characterized by redness and itching. Some individuals may experience more intense allergic reactions including hives or shortness of breath. Internal consumption of any part of the plant can lead to nausea and stomach cramping. It is essential to avoid direct contact with the foliage to prevent these adverse reactions.
