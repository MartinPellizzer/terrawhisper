What is Chrysanthemum kennedyi?

Chrysanthemum kennedyi is a perennial plant endemic to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect stems, which can grow up to 30-60 cm tall, and its alternate, simple, and entire leaves that are 2-7 cm long and 0.5-2 cm wide. The plant produces a solitary flower head with 10-20 yellow or white ray florets and 20-40 yellow or purple disc florets, each 1-2 mm in diameter. The flower head is 2-5 cm in diameter and is surrounded by a ring of scale-like phyllaries.

Chrysanthemum kennedyi typically blooms from May to August.
