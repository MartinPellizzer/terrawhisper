Clematis leschenaultiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis leschenaultiana has climbing stems that branch out to support large, pinnate leaves typically composed of five to seven ovate leaflets. The foliage is characterized by a soft texture and a vibrant green color that provides a lush backdrop for its flowering period. Its flowers are notable for having four to six large, pale lavender or whitish tepals that create a striking visual display. These blooms are often accompanied by prominent stamens that add complexity to the floral structure. As the plant matures, it develops feathery, persistent styles on the seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis leschenaultiana can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of protoanemonin. This chemical compound is released when the plant tissue is damaged or crushed. Contact with the sap can lead to redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If swallowed, it may trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain in humans and animals. It is important to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse reactions.
