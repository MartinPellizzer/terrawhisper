Silene napuligera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Silene napuligera has erect stems that reach up to 40 centimeters in height and are characterized by opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically green and serves as a structural base for the numerous small flowers produced by the plant. Each flower features a tubular calyx and five distinct petals that are often white or pale pink in color. These blooms are clustered in terminal cymes, which makes the flowering heads appear quite dense. The reproductive structures include central stamens that emerge from within the floral tube to facilitate pollination.

    What are the herbal preparations of Silene napuligera?

    Silene napuligera is prepared as an infusion, a decoction, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic use. The dried aerial parts are often steeped in hot water to create a tea used for internal consumption. When a stronger concentration is required, the plant material is boiled in liquid to form a decoction.     Silene napuligera can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many members of the Caryophyllaceae family contain saponins that may act as irritants. Contact with the plant's sap might lead to localized redness or itching for individuals with sensitive skin. If consumed, the chemical compounds could potentially trigger nausea or stomach discomfort. It is important to handle the specimen with caution to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
