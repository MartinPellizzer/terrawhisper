Mukdenia rossii

What's the taxonomical classification of Mukdenia rossii?

Mukdenia rossii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Saxifragales and the family Saxifragaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Mukdenia, and its specific species name, rossii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mukdenia rossii has woody, rhizomatous stems that produce pinnately compound leaves with 5 to 9 ovate, serrated leaflets. These leaves are typically dark green and can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. During the flowering stage, small, yellowish-white flowers emerge in dense, terminal clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy, red berries that serve as its fruit. Its overall structure is low-growing and often forms spreading mats across the forest floor.
