What is Erigeron fraternus?

Erigeron fraternus is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It is an annual or perennial herb with a taproot and a branching stem, typically growing to a height of 1-3 cm. The leaves are lance-shaped, 1-4 cm long, and are arranged oppositely on the stem. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in a dense cluster at the tip of the stem, which are 3-5 mm in diameter and have 5 petals.

The flowers are arranged in a capitulum, a type of inflorescence characteristic of the Asteraceae family, and are surrounded by a ring of small, scale-like leaves called phyllaries.
