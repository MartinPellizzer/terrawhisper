What's the taxonomical classification of Quercus franchetii?

Quercus franchetii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Fagales and the family Fagaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Quercus, specifically identified by the species name franchetii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Quercus franchetii has an upright, spreading crown with rough, deeply fissured grey bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to obovate in shape. The undersides of the foliage often feature fine pubescence, providing a slightly lighter appearance compared to the top. Small, acorn-shaped fruits develop from woody cups that are characterized by their scaly texture. This deciduous species reaches significant heights, forming a sturdy and dense canopy in its natural habitat.
