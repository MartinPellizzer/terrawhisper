Tamarix leptostachya

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tamarix leptostachya has slender, scale-like leaves arranged in dense, overlapping patterns on thin, woody branches that often exhibit a weeping habit. The plant is characterized by its delicate, thread-like branchlets which give the foliage a soft, feathery appearance. Small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in elongated, spike-like racemes that emerge from the axils of the scales. These inflorescences produce numerous tiny petals, often in shades of white or pale pink, to facilitate pollination. The overall structure is highly adapted to arid environments, utilizing its reduced leaf surface area to minimize moisture loss.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Central Asia. It primarily inhabits sandy or saline environments such as desert margins, dry riverbeds, and coastal dunes. Its range extends from the Atlantic coast of Morocco through the Nile Valley and across the Arabian Peninsula. You can also find it growing in various parts of the Indian subcontinent and extending into parts of Southeast Asia. This distribution reflects its high tolerance for drought and salt-heavy soil conditions.
