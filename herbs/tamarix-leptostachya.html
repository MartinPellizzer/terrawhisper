What's the taxonomical classification of Tamarix leptostachya?

Tamarix leptostachya belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is specifically situated within the family Tamaricaceae, and its taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement in the genus Tamarix, resulting in its unique scientific designation as the species Tamarix leptostachyas.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tamarix leptostachya has slender, scale-like leaves arranged in dense, overlapping patterns on thin, woody branches that often exhibit a weeping habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in elongated, spike-like racemes that emerge from the axils of the scales. These inflorescences produce numerous tiny petals, often in shades of white or pale pink, to facilitate pollination. The overall structure is highly adapted to arid environments, utilizing its reduced leaf surface area to minimize moisture loss.
