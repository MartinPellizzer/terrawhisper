What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Epipactis papillosa has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and support several lanceolate to elliptical leaves arranged in an alternate or spiral pattern. These leaves are generally bright green and possess smooth or slightly wavy margins. The inflorescence consists of a terminal raceme featuring small, greenish-pink flowers that are characterized by a distinctively fleshy, papillose lip. Each flower contains a prominent spur located at the base of the labellum, which serves to store nectar. The overall structure is delicate, allowing the plant to thrive in shaded, moist forest environments.
