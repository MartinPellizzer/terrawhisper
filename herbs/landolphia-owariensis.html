Landolphia owariensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Landolphia owariensis?

Landolphia owariensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Landolphia, with the unique identifier being the species owariensis.

Landolphia owarienensis has large, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically coriaceous with entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. Its woody stem structure supports a canopy characterized by dense, spreading branching patterns. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves to facilitate pollination. What are the pharmacological activities of Landolphia owariensis?

This plant has demonstrated significant antioxidant and neuroprotective pharmacological activities through its bioactive phytochemical constituents. Research indicates that its extracts can effectively neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress within biological systems. These antioxidant properties contribute to its potential in protecting neural tissues from damage caused by cellular oxidation. Furthermore, the plant exhibits neuroprotective effects that may help preserve cognitive functions and mitigate neurodegenerative processes. Such medicinal properties suggest that Landolphia owarienensis holds great promise for future therapeutic developments in brain health.

According to a study published by "Folia medica", Landolphia owariensis was assessed for its effect on alcohol-induced neurodegeneration of the cerebellar cortex in rats. The study involved groups of rats fed 5 g/kg ethanol (25% w/v) and others receiving 5 g/kg ethanol (25% w/v) plus Landolphia owariensis at 100 mg/kg body weight in a diluted nutritionally complete diet (50% v/v). After 4 days of binge alcohol treatment, Landolphia owariensis supplementation significantly lowered the levels of histologic and biochemical indices of neurodegeneration. This plant reduced the level of neurodegeneration and cytoa rchitecture distortion of the cerebellar cortex in rats exposed to ethanol. Landolphia owariensis attenuates alcohol-induced cerebellar degeneration by alleviating oxidative stress and the alteration of neurofilament protein expression in the Purkinje cells.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive medicinal compounds including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins. These secondary metabolites contribute to the plant's significant pharmacological potential in traditional medicine. The presence of alkaloids and flavonoids often provides antioxidant and antimicrobial properties to the extracts. Additionally, the saponins and tannins found within the species assist in various physiological functions and therapeutic applications. Such chemical diversity makes Landolphia owarienensis a subject of interest for pharmacological research.

According to a study published by "Pharmaceuctical biology", Landolphia owariensis leaf extract and its fractions showed significant suppression of parasitemia at 29-86, 18-95, and 75-96% for early, established, and residual infections. The ED50 values for the suppressive activity of LOE, LOHF, LOEF, and LomF were 266.56, 514.93, 392.95, and 165.70 mg/kg, respectively. Post-day 30-survival indices were recorded as 16.7-50, 16.7, 16.7-66.7, and 50-83.3% for the corresponding LOE, LOHF, LOEF, and LomF treatments. An oral LD50 value of >5000 mg/kg was established for LOE in mice. The antimalarial activity of the plant may be attributed to alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins present in the LomF fraction.

According to a study published by "Phytotherapy research : PTR", Landolphia owariensis P. Beauv is used in folklore medicine. The stringy seed pulp contains 4 bioactive compounds, including (E)-3-(3,4-Dihydroxylcinnamoyl)quinic acid, (E)-3-(3,4-Dihydroxylcinnamoyl)quinic acid methyl ester, 3,4-Dihydroxylbenzoic acid, and 22,23-Dihydrostigmaster-3β-ol. The 3 phenylpropanoids and phenolic acids identified as compounds I, II, and III are notable standard antioxidants with confirmed hepatic-protective activity. Additionally, compound IV has been reported to possess anti-inflammatory activity, anti-colon cancer action, and a cholesterol-lowering effect.
