Yellow wood

Maclura pomifera

Yellow Wood (Maclura pomifera) is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America, known for its yellowish wood and distinctive fruit, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

Botanical Identification

Maclura pomifera, commonly known as Yellow Wood, belongs to the family Moraceae. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America. The plant is characterized by its yellowish wood, heart-shaped leaves, and unique fig-like fruits. Synonyms include Yellow Mulberry and Curculigo, though these are sometimes confused with other species in herbal literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Urticales
  • Family: Moraceae
  • Genus: Maclura
  • Species: Maclura pomifera

Active Compounds

Yellow Wood contains key active compounds such as coumarin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal properties, influencing anti-inflammatory and antioxidant pathways. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in traditional herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Tincture: Used for respiratory conditions and as a general tonic due to its active compounds.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as an antioxidant due to its high concentration of nutrients.

Safety Profile

Yellow Wood, or Maclura pomifera, is generally considered safe when used in traditional herbal medicine. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised for those with known sensitivities to its compounds.
