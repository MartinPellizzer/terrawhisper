Potentilla glabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla glabra?

Potentiilla glabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is completed by its placement in the genus Potentiella, with the specific epithet glabra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella glabra has a low-growing, perennial habit characterized by creeping rhizomes and basal leaves that are typically pinnately divided into five to seven leaflets with serrated margins. The leaves are mostly hairless, which distinguishes the species from many of its more pubescent relatives. Slender, leafless stems rise from the base to bear small, solitary flowers that feature five bright yellow petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens. The fruit consists of small, dry achenes that develop within the receptacle following pollination. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in open, rocky, or grassy habitats.
