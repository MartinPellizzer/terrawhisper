Potentilla glabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla glabra?

Potentiilla glabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is completed by its placement in the genus Potentiella, with the specific epithet glabra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Potentiella glabra contains various secondary metabolites including tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its biological activity. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant astringent and antioxidant properties. The presence of tannins specifically allows the plant to be used in traditional applications for treating skin irritations or digestive issues. Flavonoids within the plant tissue further support its potential for reducing oxidative stress in cellular environments. Researchers study these specific compounds to understand how they interact with biological systems for future medicinal development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Potentilla glabra?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, digestive disorders, and skin irritations. Its astringent properties are often utilized to manage symptoms of diarrhea and gastrointestinal distress. Historically, herbalists have also applied its extracts to soothe wounds and minor skin abrasions. Some traditional practices incorporate the plant to help mitigate discomfort from minor swellings or localized inflammation. These medicinal uses stem primarily from the presence of tannins found within its tissues.
