Manilkara obovata

What's the taxonomical classification of Manilkara obovata?

Manilkara obovata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Ericales. This plant is a representative of the family Sapotaeceae, falling under the genus Manilkara, and is specifically identified by its species name, obovata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Manilkara obovata has obovate leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long, woody stems with a corky texture, and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The leaves feature an obtuse apex and a cuneate base, often displaying a somewhat leathery feel. Its stems are characterized by a distinctively rough or fissured bark that can become quite thick with age. The flowers are relatively inconspicuous but produce fleshy fruits that develop into small drupes. These morphological traits are characteristic of its adaptation to tropical dry forest environments.
