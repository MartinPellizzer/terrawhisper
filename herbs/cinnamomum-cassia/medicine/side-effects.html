10 Possible Side Effects Of Cinnamomum Cassia (Cassia)

Cinnamomum cassia has some side effects when used improperly, such as stomach upset, diarrhea, and allergic reactions.

These side effects are often caused by excessive consumption or poor quality of the herb. In severe cases, these reactions can lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that can worsen one's life by causing respiratory distress and even organ failure.

Furthermore, excessive cassia consumption can also lead to liver damage, worsening one's quality of life and potentially requiring costly medical treatments.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Cinnamomum cassia if used imporperly.
