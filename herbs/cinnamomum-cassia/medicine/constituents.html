10 Medicinal Constituents Of Cinnamomum Cassia (Cassia)

Cinnamomum cassia has active constituents such as cinnamaldehyde, cinnamyl acetate, and linalool.

These constituents confer medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects. By reducing inflammation and fighting infections, cassia may alleviate symptoms of arthritis, improve wound healing, and protect against diseases caused by free radicals.

Regular consumption of cassia tea or supplements may also help lower blood sugar levels and cholesterol, thereby improving the quality of life for individuals with diabetes or cardiovascular conditions.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Cinnamomum cassia.

1. Cinnamic acid

Cinnamomum cassia cinnamic acid is a type of phenolic compound extracted from the bark of the cinnamon tree.

It is responsible for the distinctive flavor and aroma of cinnamon, as well as its medicinal properties.

Cinnamic acid has been traditionally used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory problems.

2. Cinnamate

Cinnamomum cassia cinnamate is a naturally occurring compound derived from the bark of Cinnamomum verum trees.

It is a sweet and warm-tasting oil extracted through steam distillation or solvent extraction. Cassia cinnamate has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory problems.

Its distinctive flavor and aroma are commonly used as a food additive and in perfumes.

3. Coumarin

Cinnamomum cassia coumarin is a natural compound extracted from the bark of the cinnamon tree.

It is a potent allergen and has been linked to respiratory issues, skin irritation, and liver damage in high doses.

Coumarin is often used in food products, perfumes, and medicines, but its use has been restricted in some countries due to concerns over its potential toxicity.

4. Linalool

Cinnamomum cassia linalool is a chemical compound that is found in the bark of the Cinnamomum cassia plant.

It is a natural oil extracted from the cinnamon sticks, which are commonly used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Linalool has been shown to have various health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, as well as sedative and antidepressant effects.

5. Benzaldehyde

Cinnamomum cassia benzaldehyde is a key component responsible for the distinctive fragrance and flavor of cinnamon.

It's a naturally occurring compound found in the bark of Cinnamomum cassia trees, which are native to southern China.

Benzaldehyde is a sweet, almond-like aroma that gives cinnamon its characteristic warm, spicy scent, making it a popular ingredient in baked goods, beverages, and perfumes.

6. Terpinen-4-ol

Cinnamomum cassia terpinen-4-ol is a naturally occurring compound found in the bark of the Cassia tree.

This essential oil has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Terpinen-4-ol has also been shown to have antimicrobial and antioxidant effects, making it a valuable ingredient in various personal care products, such as soaps, creams, and lotions.

7. Cinnamyl acetate

Cinnamomum cassia cinnamyl acetate is a natural ester compound derived from the bark of the Cinnamomum cassia tree.

It is a sweet and warm-smelling aroma that is often used in aromatherapy and perfumery due to its ability to calm and relax the mind and body.

Additionally, it has been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products.

8. Eugenol

Cinnamomum cassia eugenol is a type of natural oil extracted from the bark of the Cassia tree.

It has been used for centuries in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various ailments such as digestive issues and respiratory problems. Eugenol has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it effective against fungal infections and skin irritations.

Additionally, its sweet, spicy aroma is often used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

9. Methyl cinnamate

Cinnamomum cassia methyl cinnamate is a naturally occurring compound found in cinnamon bark.

It's a colorless oil with a sweet and spicy aroma, commonly used in the food, pharmaceutical, and cosmetic industries.

Methyl cinnamate has been traditionally used in traditional medicine to treat various health issues, such as digestive problems, fever, and inflammation.

10. Cinnamaldehyde

Cinnamomum cassia cinnamaldehyde is a natural compound extracted from the bark of the Cassia tree.

It is a major constituent of cinnamon essential oil and is responsible for its distinctive aroma and flavor.

Cinnamaldehyde has been shown to have various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it a popular ingredient in traditional medicine and food industries.
