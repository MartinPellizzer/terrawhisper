10 Health Benefits Of Cinnamomum Cassia (Cassia)

Cinnamomum cassia, also known as cassia, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving digestion due to its high content of cinnamaldehyde, a polyphenolic compound with potent antioxidant properties.

This can alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome and diabetes, promoting regular bowel movements and blood sugar control.

Additionally, its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory effects can aid in wound healing and pain management, ultimately enhancing overall well-being and quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Cinnamomum cassia.

1. Reduces blood sugar levels

Cinnamomum cassia reduces blood sugar levels because it contains a compound called cinnamic acid, which has been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes that break down carbohydrates into glucose.

Additionally, cassia has been found to increase insulin sensitivity and improve glucose uptake in the body's cells, allowing for more efficient use of insulin and reducing blood sugar levels.

2. Improves insulin sensitivity

Cinnamomum cassia improves insulin sensitivity because of its ability to enhance insulin receptor activity and glucose uptake in muscle cells.

The active compound cinnamic acid has been shown to increase the expression of genes involved in glucose metabolism, leading to improved insulin signaling and reduced insulin resistance.

Additionally, cassia extracts have been found to inhibit the activity of enzymes that break down glycogen, allowing for better glucose storage and utilization.

3. Lowers ldl cholesterol levels

Cinnamomum cassia lowers LDL cholesterol levels because it possesses a unique compound called cinnamic acid, which has been shown to inhibit the absorption of dietary cholesterol in the gut.

Additionally, cassia has been found to increase the production of bile acids, which helps remove excess cholesterol from the bloodstream, thereby reducing its levels.

This dual action makes cassia a valuable natural remedy for cardiovascular health by helping to reduce the risk of heart disease and stroke.

4. Increases hdl cholesterol levels

Cinnamomum cassia increases HDL cholesterol levels because it contains a compound called cinnamic acid, which has been shown to inhibit the activity of an enzyme responsible for breaking down HDL cholesterol.

As a result, more HDL cholesterol is available in the bloodstream to remove excess cholesterol from peripheral tissues and transport it back to the liver for excretion, thus increasing overall HDL levels and potentially reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease.

5. Reduces inflammation

Cinnamomum cassia reduces inflammation because of its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compounds.

The essential oil extracted from cassia bark contains cinnamic acid, which inhibits the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines. Additionally, cassia's flavonoids and phenolic acids have been shown to scavenge free radicals and reduce oxidative stress, further contributing to its anti-inflammatory effects.

This natural compound has been traditionally used in traditional medicine to treat various inflammatory conditions, including arthritis and gout.

6. Has antioxidant properties

Cinnamomum cassia has antioxidant properties because it contains a high amount of polyphenolic compounds, particularly cinnamic acid and coumarin.

These compounds can neutralize free radicals in the body, protecting cells from damage caused by oxidative stress.

Additionally, cassia's essential oil has been shown to inhibit lipid peroxidation, further demonstrating its potential antioxidant effects.

7. Protects against heart disease

Cinnamomum cassia protects against heart disease because of its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The compound cinnamic acid in cassia has been shown to improve blood flow, reduce inflammation in the blood vessels, and prevent platelet aggregation, all of which can contribute to the development of cardiovascular disease.

Additionally, cassia has been found to lower cholesterol levels and triglycerides, further reducing the risk of heart disease.

8. Fights bacterial and fungal infections

Cinnamomum cassia fights bacterial and fungal infections because of its unique chemical composition.

The essential oil extracted from cassia bark contains high amounts of cinnamic aldehyde, a powerful antimicrobial compound that inhibits the growth of bacteria and fungi.

Additionally, cassia's coumarin and lignan compounds exhibit antifungal properties, making it an effective natural remedy against infections caused by Candida, Aspergillus, and other pathogens.

9. Reduces risk of cancer

Cinnamomum cassia reduces risk of cancer because it is rich in a polyphenol compound called cinnamic acid.

This antioxidant has been shown to inhibit the growth and proliferation of cancer cells, particularly in the breast, lung, and colon. Additionally, cassia has been found to induce apoptosis (cell death) in cancer cells, thereby reducing the risk of tumor formation and progression.

Its anti-inflammatory properties also contribute to its potential anti-cancer effects by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation.

10. Alleviates pms symptoms

Cinnamomum cassia alleviates PMS symptoms because of its natural anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The compound cinnamic acid in cassia has been shown to reduce inflammation and alleviate cramps, bloating, and mood swings associated with PMS. Additionally, cassia's ability to regulate blood sugar levels can help mitigate symptoms such as fatigue, anxiety, and depression.

This herb's natural relief from PMS symptoms makes it a popular alternative to synthetic medications.
