Botanical Identification of Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia)

To identify Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Chinese Cinnamon, or Cinnamomum cassia, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Laurales, family Lauraceae, genus Cinnamomum, and species Cinnamomum cassia. It is a member of the laurel family, known for its aromatic bark used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chinese Cinnamon is Cinnamomum cassia, first described by the botanist Blume in 1825. It is also known by several synonyms, including Cinnamomum burmannii and Cinnamomum aromaticum. Common names such as Chinese cinnamon, cassia, and sweet cinnamon are widely used, while regional and historical names reflect its long-standing use in traditional medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) is a deciduous tree with a straight trunk and smooth, grayish-brown bark. It has a taproot system with lateral roots, and its stem is cylindrical, often fissured. The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy green upper surface. The inflorescence is a terminal umbel, with small white flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous aromatic seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) can be distinguished by its thick, aromatic bark with longitudinal fissures, dark brown outer layer, and light yellow inner layer. It has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin and small yellow flowers in panicles. The leaves are usually darker green on the upper surface compared to similar species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, Indonesia, and India. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and moderate temperatures. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 200 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) blooms in spring, typically between March and May. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. Harvest timing for bark is usually in late winter to early spring, when the bark is mature and easiest to peel.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cinnamomum cassia is closely related to species like Cinnamomum loureirii and Cinnamomum burmannii. It belongs to the genus Cinnamomum, which is part of the Lauraceae family. The family traits include aromatic oils, simple leaves, and small flowers, which are also characteristic of C. cassia.
