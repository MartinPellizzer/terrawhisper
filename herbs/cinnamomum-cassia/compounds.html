Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) Compounds

The primary compounds in Chinese cinnamon include essential oil, which contributes to its aromatic profile, and coumarin, known for its anti-inflammatory properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids offer antioxidant benefits, while tannins and terpenoids support antimicrobial activity. Glycosides, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further contribute to its therapeutic potential.

Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia). It contains aromatic compounds like cinnamaldehyde and eugenol, which contribute to its warming properties and therapeutic effects. This oil is used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and circulatory benefits.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant effects. Coumarin is also known to interact with various metabolic pathways in the body.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds support traditional uses of cinnamon in treating digestive issues and respiratory conditions. Flavonoids are also believed to enhance the plant's ability to improve circulation and reduce oxidative stress.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Found in the bark, it plays a role in the traditional use of cinnamon for digestive and respiratory ailments. Its chemical structure supports its pharmacological activities in herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a significant compound found in Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to aid in reducing inflammation and supporting digestive health. Tannins in cinnamon may also enhance the absorption of other active components, thereby increasing the overall efficacy of the herb.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. These compounds are commonly extracted and used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in promoting circulation and reducing inflammation. Glycosides are known for their ability to support cardiovascular health and may aid in managing conditions like diabetes.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting circulation and reducing inflammation. Saponins also exhibit antimicrobial properties, supporting the plant's role in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Alkaloids in cinnamon are often associated with the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory conditions.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Chinese Cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia). It contributes to the plant's color and has antioxidant properties. This compound may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal medicine.
