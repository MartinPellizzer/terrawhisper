Black pepper

Piper amalago

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Botanical Identification

Black Pepper, scientifically known as Piper nigrum, belongs to the family Piperaceae. It is a flowering vine native to South Asia, particularly India. The plant is a perennial with heart-shaped leaves and small green flowers. Common names include Long Pepper and Kali Mirch, while synonyms in herbal literature include Piper longum and P. reticulatum.

Active Compounds

Black Pepper contains alkaloids, flavonoids, and tannins as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to enhance absorption and exert anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Flavonoids and tannins may synergize with alkaloids to amplify the herb's therapeutic potential.

  • Alkaloid
  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive problems and as a general tonic.

Extract: Used for pain relief and inflammation reduction.

Safety Profile

Black Pepper (Piper amalago) is generally safe when used in typical medicinal doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal irritation in some individuals. Long-term or excessive use can lead to stomach ulcers or bleeding, so caution is advised for those with digestive issues or on blood-thinning medications.
