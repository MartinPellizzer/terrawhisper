Black Pepper (Piper amalago)

Black Pepper (Piper amalago) is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, and Indo-Malayan Region. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Erect, woody, branching in opposite or alternate pattern, with smooth, ridged surface and adventitious root scars.
