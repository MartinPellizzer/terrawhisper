Distemonanthus benthamianus

What's the taxonomical classification of Distemonanthus benthamianus?

Distemonanthus benthamianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Distemonanthus, with its specific designation being the species benthamianus.

What are the pharmacological activities of Distemonanthus benthamianus?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its ability to inhibit acetylcholinesterase (AChE), butyrylcholinesterase (BChE), tyrosinase, and alpha-amylase. These inhibitory activities suggest that extracts from Distemonanthus benthamianus could be utilized in managing neurodegenerative diseases and metabolic disorders like diabetes. Furthermore, the plant exhibits potent antioxidant properties that help neutralize harmful free radicals in the body. Such multifaceted biological effects highlight its value as a source of bioactive compounds for pharmaceutical development. The combination of enzyme inhibition and oxidative stress reduction provides a strong basis for its traditional medicinal uses.

As discussed in a study, Distemonanthus benthamianus displayed the highest DPPH and ABTS scavenging, ferric and cupric reducing, and total antioxidant capacity in the phosphomolybdenum assay. This plant also exhibited high inhibition for AChE, BChE, and tyrosinase, along with a comparable α-amylase inhibitory effect. The total phenolic content (TPC) was highest in D. benthamianus, measuring between 135.33-192.29 mg GAE/g. Bioactive compounds identified in the extracts included genistein (m/z 271.06), procyanidin B (m/z 577.13), and procyanidin C (m/z 865.19) isomers. Consequently, D. benthamianus can be considered a potential pharmaceutical agent or functional food component that could reduce the risks of oxidative stress-related disorders.
