Gerbera tanantii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gerbera tanantii has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lanceolate to oblong in shape with serrated margins and a distinctively hairy texture. The foliage often features long petioles that connect the blades to a central growing point. From this center, the plant produces tall, slender scapes that support solitary flower heads. These inflorescences consist of numerous ray florets arranged around a central disc of smaller tubular florets. The overall structure is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and specialized floral arrangement.
