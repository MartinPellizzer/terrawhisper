What's the taxonomical classification of Gerbera tanantii?

Gerbera tanantii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, a group known for its diverse flowering members, and is further categorized into the genus Gerbera. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species designation, tanantii.

      What are the herbal preparations of Gerbera tanantii?

      Gerbera tanantii is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried roots or as a concentrated aqueous infusion of the leaves. These liquid preparations are often administered orally to utilize the plant's bioactive compounds. Some traditional practitioners also grind the dried plant material into a fine powder to be encapsulated or mixed with honey.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Gerbera tanantii can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, redness, or itching upon direct physical contact with its tissues. These dermatological responses are typically triggered by specific proteins or sap components found within the plant structure. Some individuals may also experience respiratory irritation or sneezing if they inhale pollen or airborne particles from the flower. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, sensitive individuals should exercise caution when handling the specimen. Always wash hands thoroughly after contact to minimize the risk of irritation.
