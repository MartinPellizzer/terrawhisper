Ceiba tunariensis

Ceiba tunariensis, commonly known as the Tunari tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

This plant is native to the tropical regions of South America and has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.

The Tunari tree has been reported to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which are attributed to its therapeutic actions such as wound healing, pain relief, and fever reduction.

The bioactive constituents of Ceiba tunariensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and have been used to treat various health conditions, including respiratory issues, gastrointestinal problems, and skin infections.

Possible Side Effects of Ceiba tunariensis

Ceiba tunariensis can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high concentration of alkaloids and saponins.

In some cases, excessive consumption may lead to headaches, dizziness, and fatigue, as the body struggles to process the plant's compounds.

Overuse of Ceiba tunariensis can also cause cardiovascular problems, such as rapid heart rate and blood pressure fluctuations, in individuals with pre-existing heart conditions.

Furthermore, improper use of this plant may interact with medications, including blood thinners and diabetes treatments, exacerbating existing health conditions.
