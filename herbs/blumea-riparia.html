Blumea riparia

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea riparia?

Blumea riparia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Blumea, and its unique species designation, riparia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea riparia has a perennial herb habit characterized by erect stems that often reach heights of up to one meter and are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The leaves are simple, broadly ovate to lanceolate, and possess serrated margins along with a distinctively fuzzy texture on both surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of many small, yellowish flower heads arranged in dense, terminal panicles. These flower heads are surrounded by specialized bracts that contribute to the plant's overall soft, velvety appearance. The root system typically consists of a sturdy rhizome that helps the plant establish itself in moist, riparian environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically occurring in countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of southern China. It is commonly found growing in moist, riparian habitats near riverbanks and along the edges of streams. These environments provide the high humidity and consistent soil moisture necessary for its growth. The species thrives in low-lying areas where water is frequently present in the landscape. Consequently, its distribution is closely tied to the availability of freshwater ecosystems within these warm climatic zones.
