Salvia chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia chinensis?

Salvia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a prominent representative of the family Lamiaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Salvia, with its unique identity defined by the specific epithet chinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Salviva chinensis contains diverse bioactive compounds including diterpenoids, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and various essential oils. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented pharmacological properties such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Specific diterpene lactones are believed to play a significant role in its traditional medicinal applications. Additionally, the presence of polyphenols supports its potential use in mitigating oxidative stress in biological systems. Research continues to explore how these specific molecular structures interact with human physiological pathways.

What are the therapeutic actions of Salvia chinensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various conditions including inflammation, fever, cough, and respiratory ailments. It is frequently applied in traditional medicine to address symptoms of the common cold and bronchitis. Practitioners also utilize its properties to help manage skin irritations and minor wounds. Some therapeutic applications focus on its potential to reduce swelling and ease localized pain. Its medicinal value is often linked to its ability to clear heat and resolve toxins within the body.
