Salvia chinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia chinensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, square-shaped stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and feature serrated margins with a textured, rugose surface. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal verticillasters that produce small, two-lipped flowers ranging in color from pale blue to violet. These blossoms are supported by bracts that often exhibit a distinct purplish hue. The plant develops via a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread through garden beds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, Korea, and Taiwan. It typically thrives in temperate climates where it can be found in various habitats ranging from forest edges to grassy meadows. Within these regions, the species is often localized in mountainous areas or along riverbanks where moisture is consistent. Its distribution follows a pattern common to many perennial herbs of the Lamiaceae family throughout the subtropical and temperate zones of the continent.

What are the therapeutic actions of Salvia chinensis?

Salvias chinensis has therapeutic actions that include bitter, diuretic, anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, hemostatic, and astringent properties. It also serves as an anti-inflammatory and antipyretic agent to reduce swelling and lower fevers. Furthermore, its hemostatic and astringent effects assist in controlling bleeding and tightening tissues.
