Ligustrum pedunculare

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligustrum pedunculare?

Ligustrum pedunculare belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Oleaceae and is further categorized under the genus Ligustrum, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species pedunculare.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligustrum pedunculare has opposite, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically 5 to 12 centimeters long and feature entire margins with prominent pinnate venation. The plant is a woody shrub characterized by its slender, grayish-brown stems and spreading growth habit. Its reproductive structures consist of small, white, tubular flowers that are arranged in elongated, drooping clusters known as panicles. These peduncled inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils and are supported by long, distinct stalks. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark purple or black color when mature.
