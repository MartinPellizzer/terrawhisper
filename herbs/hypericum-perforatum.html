What to know about Hypericum perforatum (st john's wort) before using it medicinally

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St John's Wort, is a medicinal herb that has been valued for its potential to improve mood and alleviate symptoms of mild to moderate depression, anxiety, and insomnia through its adaptogenic properties.

As a versatile garden plant, Hypericum perforatum is relatively easy to cultivate, thrives in well-drained soil, and can grow up to 90 cm in height, making it a popular choice among gardeners. Botanically, the herb belongs to the Hypericaceae family, is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive perforated leaves, and is native to Europe and Asia.

Historically, the herb has been used since ancient times for its medicinal properties, with references dating back to the Greek physician Hippocrates, who used it to treat melancholy and insomnia.

What are the medicinal properties of Hypericum perforatum?

Hypericum perforatum helps with mild to moderate depression, anxiety disorders, and sleep disturbances. It has been used to treat symptoms of premenstrual syndrome, seasonal affective disorder, and postpartum depression. Studies also suggest its effectiveness in treating obsessive-compulsive disorder and attention deficit hyperactivity disorder.

The active constituents of st john's wort include hyperforin, hypericin, and flavonoids, which contribute to its antidepressant and anxiolytic properties. These compounds interact with neurotransmitters in the brain, influencing mood and emotions. Research indicates that hyperforin, in particular, has a rapid onset of action and may contribute to the plant's antidepressant efficacy.

The leaves and flowers of st john's wort are commonly used for medicinal purposes. The leaves contain the highest concentration of hyperforin and hypericin, while the flowers are richer in flavonoids. The plant's aerial parts, including stems, leaves, and flowers, are typically dried and prepared as infusions, capsules, or tablets.

Improper use of st john's wort can lead to adverse effects, particularly when combined with other medications. The plant's constituents can interact with antidepressants, such as selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors, and decrease their effectiveness. Additionally, st john's wort can enhance the effects of blood thinners and increase the risk of bleeding.

Precautions when using st john's wort medicinally include avoiding its use with other antidepressants, antihistamines, and anticoagulants. Patients should also consult their healthcare provider before using st john's wort, especially if they are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have kidney or liver disease. Dosing and preparation guidelines should be carefully followed to minimize potential side effects and ensure efficacy.

Hypericum perforatum grow in a wide range of environments, tolerating full sun to partial shade and well-drained to moist soils with a pH range of 6.0-7.0. It can thrive in a variety of conditions, from dry meadows to wetlands. Soil fertility is also not a limiting factor for its growth.

Proper planting of Hypericum perforatum involves selecting a location with suitable conditions. Plant seeds or seedlings in the fall or early spring, spacing them 12-18 inches apart. Water consistently during the first growing season, reducing moisture as the plant matures. Mulching around the base can help retain moisture and suppress weeds.

Harvesting Hypericum perforatum typically occurs in mid to late summer, when the flowers are fully open. Cut the stems at the base, using clean, sharp tools to minimize damage to the plant.
