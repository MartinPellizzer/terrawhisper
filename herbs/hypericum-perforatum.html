St. John's Wort

Hypericum perforatum

St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America, known for its bright yellow flowers and medicinal properties. It has been traditionally used to treat mild to moderate depression and other emotional disorders.

Botanical Identification

St. John's Wort is Hypericum perforatum, a member of the Clusiaceae family. Common names include St. John's Wort and Klamath tea. It is a perennial shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant has yellow flowers with red veins, and its leaves are dotted with oil glands. It is also known as Hypericum in scientific literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Garryales
  • Family: Clusiaceae
  • Genus: Hypericum
  • Species: Hypericum perforatum

Active Compounds

St. John's Wort contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids. These compounds work synergistically to influence mood and brain function. They are believed to modulate neurotransmitters like serotonin and dopamine, contributing to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Terpenoid

St. John's Wort Uses.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Extract: Used for depression and anxiety due to its antidepressant and mood-stabilizing effects.

Tincture: Used for mild depression and skin conditions due to its bioavailability and active compound content.

Safety Profile

St. John's Wort is generally well-tolerated but may cause mild side effects like gastrointestinal discomfort or skin reactions. It interacts with numerous medications, reducing their effectiveness or increasing side effects. Due to these interactions, it should be used with caution and under medical supervision.
