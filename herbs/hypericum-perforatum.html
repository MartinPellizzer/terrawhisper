What to know about Hypericum perforatum before using it medicinally

Hypericum perforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including mild to moderate depression, anxiety disorders, and sleep disorders.

The main health benefits associated with St. John's Wort include its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. The herb's therapeutic properties include its ability to inhibit the reuptake of neurotransmitters such as serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine, which are involved in mood regulation. The bioactive compounds present in St. John's Wort include hyperforin, hypericin, and flavonoids, which are responsible for its pharmacological activities.

People often prepare a tea or infusion by steeping the dried flowers and leaves of the plant in hot water, which is used to alleviate symptoms of depression and anxiety.
