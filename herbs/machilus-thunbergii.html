What is Machilus thunbergii?

Machilus thunbergii is a species of plant in the family Lauraceae, native to East Asia.

It is an evergreen shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 10 meters in height, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and 7-13 cm long, with a leathery texture and entire margins, featuring 10-14 pairs of secondary veins. The inflorescences are axillary, comprising 2-4 white flowers with four petals and a prominent, four-lobed calyx.

The fruit is a small, blue-black drupe, approximately 1-1.5 cm long, with a single seed.
