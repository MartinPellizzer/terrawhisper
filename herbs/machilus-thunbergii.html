Machilus thunbergii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Machilus thunbergii has evergreen leaves that are lanceolate to oblong, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with smooth, entire margins and a glossy upper surface. The tree typically reaches moderate heights and possesses a sturdy trunk with grayish-brown bark. Its floral structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters along the branches. The fruit is characterized by a small, fleshy drupe that often rests atop a persistent cupule. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species within its genus.

    What are the herbal preparations of Machilus thunbergii?

    Machilus thunbergii is prepared as a decoction by boiling the leaves, bark, or twigs in water to extract its medicinal properties. This aqueous extraction is the most common method used in traditional medicine to treat ailments like fever or inflammation. Some practitioners also utilize the plant to create topical poultices from crushed fresh leaves applied directly to the skin.     Machilus thunbergii can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many species within the Lauraceae family contain volatile oils that may act as contact allergens. If consumed, the plant could potentially trigger nausea or abdominal discomfort due to its chemical composition. Users should exercise caution and consult professional toxicological data before any medicinal application.
