Pineapple (Ananas comosus) Uses

Pineapple is used to treat acne and skin irritation due to its bromelain content, which reduces inflammation. It aids in wound healing and fights skin and fungal infections. Its anti-inflammatory properties help with respiratory infections, colds, and coughs by supporting immune function and reducing mucus buildup.

Pineapple (Ananas comosus) Monograph

1. Acne

Pineapple contains bromelain, an enzyme with anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce redness and swelling associated with acne. It also exfoliates the skin by breaking down dead skin cells, preventing clogged pores. Regular use of pineapple in skincare routines may support clearer, healthier skin by promoting cell renewal and balancing oil production.

2. Skin irritation

Pineapple contains bromelain, an enzyme with anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce skin irritation. It may soothe redness and swelling associated with minor skin conditions. Applying fresh pineapple juice or using it in topical formulations can provide relief. However, it should be used cautiously to avoid allergic reactions.

3. Wound healing

Pineapple contains bromelain, an enzyme that reduces inflammation and promotes tissue repair. It also aids in reducing swelling and accelerating the healing process of wounds. Bromelain helps break down dead tissue, making it useful for treating cuts, burns, and other skin injuries. Its natural properties make pineapple a valuable ingredient in herbal wound care remedies.

4. Skin infections

Pineapple contains bromelain, an enzyme with antimicrobial properties that can help treat skin infections. It reduces inflammation and promotes wound healing. Topical application of pineapple extract may inhibit bacterial growth and speed recovery from minor skin infections. Its natural enzymes also help break down dead tissue, aiding in the healing process.

5. Fungal infections

Pineapple contains bromelain, an enzyme with antifungal properties that can help combat certain fungal infections. It may support immune function and reduce inflammation, aiding in the body's natural defense against pathogens. While not a substitute for conventional treatments, pineapple can be a complementary element in holistic fungal care.

6. Inflammation

Pineapple contains bromelain, an enzyme with anti-inflammatory properties that helps reduce swelling and pain. It is commonly used to alleviate symptoms of inflammation in conditions like arthritis and muscle injuries. Bromelain also supports digestion and may enhance the effectiveness of certain medications. Regular consumption of pineapple can contribute to managing chronic inflammatory conditions.

7. Respiratory infection

Pineapple contains bromelain, an enzyme with anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce symptoms of respiratory infections. It supports immune function and may ease congestion and coughing. Its natural antiseptic qualities also aid in clearing mucus and promoting healing in the respiratory tract.

8. Respiratory infections

Pineapple contains bromelain, an enzyme with anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce symptoms of respiratory infections. It supports immune function and may alleviate congestion and mucus buildup. Bromelain also aids in tissue repair, promoting faster recovery from respiratory ailments.

9. Colds

Pineapple contains bromelain, an enzyme with anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce symptoms of colds by alleviating nasal congestion and throat irritation. It also supports immune function, potentially shortening the duration of colds when consumed regularly. Its vitamin C content further aids in boosting the body's defenses against viral infections.

10. Cough

Pineapple contains bromelain, an enzyme that helps reduce inflammation and loosen mucus, making it beneficial for relieving cough symptoms. It also has antioxidant properties that support the immune system. Consuming pineapple juice or fresh pineapple may help ease persistent coughs and improve respiratory comfort.
