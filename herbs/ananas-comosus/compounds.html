Pineapple (Ananas comosus) Compounds

The primary compounds in pineapple include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins contribute to its astringent taste and have antimicrobial effects. Terpenoids and glycosides support immune function, while saponins may help in reducing cholesterol. Essential oils and coumarin add to its aromatic profile, and anthocyanins provide antioxidant benefits, though alkaloids are present in smaller amounts.

Pineapple (Ananas comosus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Pineapple (Ananas comosus). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing inflammation. These compounds are also linked to digestive health and may aid in managing conditions like diabetes and cardiovascular diseases.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Pineapple (Ananas comosus). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports overall health and is part of the traditional herbal use of pineapple in medicinal practices.

3. Tannin

Tannin, found in Pineapple (Ananas comosus), contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and supporting wound healing. Tannins also aid in digestive health by binding to proteins and reducing intestinal bleeding. This compound is widely used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic benefits.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Pineapple (Ananas comosus) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting digestive health and immune function. They are naturally occurring in the fruit and are part of its traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Pineapple (Ananas comosus). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in pineapple are also linked to antioxidant effects, enhancing the plant's role in herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Pineapple (Ananas comosus). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin also aids in improving digestion and may support immune function. Its presence in Pineapple makes it valuable in herbal medicine for various therapeutic applications.

7. Essential oil

The essential oil of Pineapple (Ananas comosus) contains volatile compounds like methyl cyclopropyl ketone and geraniol. These compounds contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and may have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They are often extracted through distillation and used in traditional herbal remedies.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Pineapple (Ananas comosus). It contributes to the fruit's aroma and has mild anticoagulant properties. In herbal medicine, coumarin may support cardiovascular health and is often used in formulations for its soothing effects. Its presence in Pineapple highlights the plant's diverse therapeutic potential.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Pineapple (Ananas comosus) that contributes to its vibrant color. It acts as an antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. While not the primary medicinal component of pineapple, anthocyanin supports overall health by reducing oxidative stress.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of naturally occurring compounds found in many plants, including Pineapple (Ananas comosus). While Pineapple is not known for containing significant amounts of alkaloids, some studies suggest trace alkaloid-like compounds may contribute to its medicinal properties, though their role remains under investigation.
