Wood Betony (Stachys officinalis) Uses

Wood Betony is used to treat respiratory issues such as cough, bronchitis, and respiratory infections. It also helps with cold symptoms and inflammation. The herb supports wound healing and is applied for skin infections and wounds. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties make it valuable in herbal medicine.

Wood Betony (Stachys officinalis) Monograph

1. Cough

Wood Betony is used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. It may also soothe irritated airways and reduce inflammation. Traditionally, it has been used in herbal formulations for persistent or productive coughs.

2. Inflammation

Wood Betony is used to reduce inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory compounds, such as flavonoids and tannins. It supports healing in conditions like skin irritations and digestive inflammation. Its soothing properties make it a valued herb in herbal medicine for managing inflammatory symptoms.

3. Cold symptoms

Wood Betony is used to alleviate cold symptoms such as congestion and sore throat. It acts as an expectorant, helping to loosen mucus and ease breathing. Its antimicrobial properties may support immune function during colds. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for respiratory relief.

4. Respiratory infections

Wood Betony is traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating mild respiratory infections. It is believed to help reduce inflammation and ease congestion. Its expectorant properties may assist in clearing mucus from the lungs. It is often used in herbal blends for coughs and colds.

5. Bronchitis

Wood Betony is used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. It helps reduce inflammation and soothe irritated airways, promoting easier breathing. Its expectorant properties aid in clearing mucus, while its calming effects may ease coughing and discomfort associated with bronchial infections.

6. Tonsillitis

Wood Betony is traditionally used for tonsillitis due to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce throat inflammation and soothe irritation. The herb can be prepared as a gargle or infusion to support healing. It is often recommended as a supportive treatment in herbal medicine for sore throat symptoms.

7. Respiratory tract infections

Wood Betony is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing irritation in the respiratory tract. It may help alleviate symptoms of coughs, sore throats, and mild respiratory infections. Its antimicrobial properties may aid in clearing infections, though it is typically used as a supportive remedy rather than a primary treatment.

8. Skin infections

Wood Betony is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and promotes healing in conditions like eczema and fungal infections. Its essential oils have been traditionally applied to cleanse and protect the skin from bacterial and fungal invasion.

9. Wound healing

Wood Betony promotes wound healing by stimulating circulation and reducing inflammation. It contains compounds that support tissue regeneration and prevent infection. Traditionally used for cuts and sores, it aids in faster recovery and skin repair. Its astringent properties help close wounds and promote healing.

10. Wounds

Wood Betony is used topically to treat wounds due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bleeding, promote healing, and prevent infection. A poultice or infusion can be applied directly to the affected area for effective wound care. Its traditional use supports its role in herbal medicine for skin injuries.
