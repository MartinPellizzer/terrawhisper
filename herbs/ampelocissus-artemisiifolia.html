Ampelocissus artemisiifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ampelocissus artemisiifolia has a climbing habit characterized by slender, twining stems and deeply pinnatisect leaves that resemble those of the Artemisia genus. The foliage consists of multiple narrow, linear leaflets arranged along a central axis to create a feathery appearance. These leaves are typically a bright green color and possess a fine, delicate texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during its growing season. Its root system is generally shallow, allowing it to spread effectively through surrounding vegetation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ampelocissus artemisiifolia?

Ampelocisus artemisiifolia has therapeutic properties that function primarily as a bitter stomachic, an aperient, an anthelmintic, and a febrifuge. Its intense bitter components stimulate the secretion of digestive enzymes to improve overall appetite and digestion. When used as an aperient, the plant helps to promote regular bowel movements and relieve mild constipation. The anthelmintic qualities allow it to assist in the expulsion of intestinal worms from the digestive tract. Additionally, its febrifuge actions provide relief by helping to reduce elevated body temperatures during a fever.
