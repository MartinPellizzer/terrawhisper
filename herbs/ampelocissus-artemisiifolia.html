What's the taxonomical classification of Ampelocissus artemisiifolia?

Ampelocissus artemisiifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Vitales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Vitaceae, falling under the genus Ampelocissus, with its specific designation being the species artemisiifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ampelocissus artemisiifolia has a climbing habit characterized by slender, twining stems and deeply pinnatisect leaves that resemble those of the Artemisia genus. The foliage consists of multiple narrow, linear leaflets arranged along a central axis to create a feathery appearance. These leaves are typically a bright green color and possess a fine, delicate texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during its growing season. Its root system is generally shallow, allowing it to spread effectively through surrounding vegetation.
