Tricyrtis maculata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tricyrtis maculata has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by creeping rhizomes, ovate to cordate leaves with serrated margins, and bell-shaped flowers that are creamy white with distinct purple or reddish-brown spots. The foliage typically grows in basal clusters, providing a lush green foundation for the seasonal blooms. Each individual flower hangs from a slender pedicel, often appearing in small groups or solitary arrangements. The spotted pattern on the corolla serves as a visual guide for pollinators during the flowering period. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its genus and help identify it in its natural forest habitat.
