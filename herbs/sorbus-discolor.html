Sorbus discolor

What's the taxonomical classification of Sorbus discolor?

Sorbus discolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized into the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Further taxonomic refinement places it within the genus Sorbus, specifically identified by its species name, discolor.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal ailments including diarrhea, dysentery, and stomach cramps. The berries contain organic acids and tannins that act as astringents to help soothe the digestive tract. These properties make the fruit a traditional remedy for managing various inflammatory conditions within the gut. Some historical herbal practices also suggest its use in calming minor digestive upsets through its chemical composition.
