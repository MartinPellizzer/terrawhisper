What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carpesium cernuum has a woody, perennial stem structure characterized by dense, lignified tissue and a highly branched architectural pattern.

The leaves are arranged in an alternate phyllotaxy along the primary axes, displaying an elliptical shape with serrated margins. Each leaf possesses a thick cuticle that serves to minimize moisture loss during periods of high transpiration. The reproductive organs consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from axillary buds located near the nodes.

Fine trichomes cover the underside of the foliage, providing a protective layer against herbivory and environmental stressors.

        Carpesium cernuum is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the woody fruit kernels in water to release active constituents.         What side-effects this plant can have?

Some users may also experience mild neurological symptoms such as dizziness or lightheadedness. In rare cases, prolonged exposure might lead to more significant allergic reactions or skin irritation upon contact. It is essential to monitor how your body reacts to any small dose to avoid these adverse effects.
