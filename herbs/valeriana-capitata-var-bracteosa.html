Valeriana capitata var. bracteosa

Valeriana capitata var. bracteosa, also known as Cape valley sweet-scented valerian, is a species of flowering plant in the family Valerianaceae, native to the southwestern Cape Province in South Africa.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, which include sedative and anxiolytic effects, due to the presence of valerenic acid and isovaleric acid.

The plant is believed to provide various health benefits, such as reducing stress and anxiety, promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality, as well as exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

In herbal preparations, Valeriana capitata var. bracteosa is often used in teas, tinctures, and capsules, and is also used as a flavoring agent in food products.
