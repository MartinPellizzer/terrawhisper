Clematis henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis henryi has opposite, trifoliate leaves featuring three ovate to elliptical leaflets with entire margins and smooth, slightly leathery textures. The foliage is typically a deep, lustrous green that provides a dense backdrop for its blooms. This woody climber utilizes twisting petioles to scale surrounding structures and vegetation. Its flowers are large, star-shaped, and characterized by four to six creamy white to pale pink sepals. These showy blossoms often feature a prominent center of numerous yellowish stamens.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis henryi can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if the plant is ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that can lead to blistering or dermatitis upon contact with the skin. If swallowed, it may cause nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its irritant properties. Pets that chew on the foliage may experience similar inflammatory reactions in their mouths or digestive tracts. It is important to handle this climber with gloves to avoid accidental contact with its sap.
