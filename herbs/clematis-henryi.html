Clematis henryi

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis henryi?

Clematis henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Clematis, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species henryi.

    Clematis henryi is prepared primarily as a liquid decoction or a concentrated tincture derived from its dried stems and leaves.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Clematis henryi can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if the plant is ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that can lead to blistering or dermatitis upon contact with the skin. If swallowed, it may cause nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its irritant properties. Pets that chew on the foliage may experience similar inflammatory reactions in their mouths or digestive tracts. It is important to handle this climber with gloves to avoid accidental contact with its sap.
