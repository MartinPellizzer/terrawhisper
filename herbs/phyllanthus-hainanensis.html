Phyllanthus hainanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllanthus hainanensis has woody stems that branch out to support small, elliptical leaves arranged in a pseudo-distichous pattern along the lateral twigs. The leaves are typically thin and possess entire margins with acute to acuminate tips. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in a somewhat clustered arrangement. The plant develops small, globose fruits that typically split open to release seeds upon maturity. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the Phyllanthaceae family found in similar tropical habitats.
