Dodder

Cuscuta epithymum

Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum) is a parasitic flowering plant that has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential to improve urinary function and support kidney health.

Botanical Identification

Dodder, scientifically known as Cuscuta epithymum, is an annual parasitic plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family. Commonly called "dodder" or "love vine" in various regions, it lacks roots, leaves, and chlorophyll, relying entirely on host plants for nutrients. Native to temperate regions, it is often found parasitizing crops and wild plants.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Solanales
  • Family: Convolvulaceae
  • Genus: Cuscuta
  • Species: Cuscuta epithymum

Safety Profile

Dodder, or Cuscuta epithymum, is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be used with caution in individuals with bleeding disorders due to its potential anticoagulant properties.
