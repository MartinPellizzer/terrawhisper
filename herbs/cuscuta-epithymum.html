What to know about Cuscuta epithymum (dodder) before using it medicinally

Dodder, scientifically known as Cuscuta epithymum, is a versatile herb that has been harnessed for its numerous health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and boosting the immune system.

In the culinary world, dodder is often used to add flavor and texture to soups, stews, and salads. From a horticultural perspective, dodder is an important component of many ecosystems, serving as a vital food source for various pollinators and insects. Botanically speaking, dodder is classified as a parasitic plant that obtains its nutrients by attaching itself to other plants through its tendrils.

With a rich history dating back thousands of years, dodder has been referenced in ancient texts such as the Talmud and Chinese medical treatises, solidifying its reputation as a valuable herbal remedy.
