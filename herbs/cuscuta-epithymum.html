Side Effects of cuscuta epithymum

Cuscuta epithymum may cause dizziness, which can impair coordination and lead to a risk of falls, particularly in older adults or those with pre-existing balance issues.

The herb can also cause skin irritation, manifesting as redness, burning, or a prickling sensation upon contact with the skin. Eye irritation is another potential side effect, with symptoms such as redness, itching, and excessive tearing if the herb comes into contact with the eyes.

Similarly, it may cause mouth irritation, leading to a burning or tingling sensation, which can affect taste and swallowing. Additionally, the herb may induce skin rash, itching, and throat swelling, which can be particularly dangerous if it leads to respiratory distress.

These side effects highlight the importance of using Cuscuta epithymum under professional supervision and avoiding direct contact with the skin or mucous membranes to minimize adverse reactions.
