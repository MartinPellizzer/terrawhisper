Medicinal Constituents of catharanthus roseus

Catharanthus roseus vinblastine is a potent alkaloid that functions as a microtubule inhibitor, disrupting the formation of the mitotic spindle and thereby arresting cell division, making it a key component in the treatment of various cancers.

Catharanthus roseus vincristine is another alkaloid with a similar mechanism of action, inhibiting microtubule polymerization and leading to cell cycle arrest, particularly effective against leukemia and lymphoma.

Catharanthus roseus leucrosine is a less commonly studied alkaloid that exhibits antitumor properties, though its exact mechanism and clinical applications are still under investigation.

Catharanthus roseus vindesine is a vinca alkaloid that also interferes with microtubule dynamics, contributing to its efficacy in chemotherapy regimens for solid tumors and hematologic malignancies.
