Madagascar periwinkle

Catharanthus roseus

Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus) is a flowering plant native to Madagascar, known for its ornamental value and significant medicinal properties, particularly in the treatment of certain types of cancer and diabetes.

Botanical Identification

Catharanthus roseus, commonly known as Madagascar periwinkle, is a flowering plant in the Apocynaceae family. Native to Madagascar, it is an evergreen shrub with lance-shaped leaves and tubular flowers. It is also known as Vinca or periwinkle in various regions.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Apocynales
  • Family: Apocynaceae
  • Genus: Catharanthus
  • Species: Catharanthus roseus

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for treating cancer and other diseases due to the presence of alkaloids like vinblastine and vincristine.

Essential oil: Used for treating skin conditions and as a natural insect repellent.

Tincture: Used for managing pain and inflammation due to its alkaloid content.

Safety Profile

The Madagascar Periwinkle is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it contains alkaloids that may cause side effects such as nausea and vomiting. It should be avoided during pregnancy and by individuals with certain heart conditions due to its potential to affect cardiac function.
