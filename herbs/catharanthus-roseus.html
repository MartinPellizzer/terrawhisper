Scientific and Botanical Profile

Madagascar Periwinkle, with botanical name Catharanthus roseus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is also known by several common names, including Rose Periwinkle, Catharanthus, Rose Carnation, and Carnation, reflecting its vibrant and delicate flowers. Native to Madagascar and parts of southern Africa, this plant has been widely cultivated around the world for its ornamental value and medicinal properties. Morphologically, it is an evergreen shrub with opposite, simple, and ovate leaves, and it produces clusters of tubular, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant is notable for its alkaloid content, which has significant applications in pharmaceuticals, particularly in the treatment of certain cancers and cardiovascular diseases.

History and Cultural Relevance

Madagascar Periwinkle was used by indigenous peoples of Madagascar for centuries in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including diabetes, inflammation, and skin conditions.

The plant's alkaloids, such as vincristine and vinblastine, were later isolated and became vital in modern chemotherapy treatments for cancers like leukemia and lymphoma. In local cultures, the plant is also associated with spiritual and ritualistic practices, often used in ceremonies to promote healing and protection. Folk traditions include using its leaves and flowers in poultices to alleviate pain and as a natural dye for textiles.

Today, its traditional remedies continue to influence contemporary herbal medicine, highlighting its enduring cultural and therapeutic significance.

Forms, Preparation and Usage

Catharanthus roseus has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with various forms available including fresh plant, dried tincture, powder, essential oil, and capsule.

It can be prepared as a tea, decoction, or infusion by steeping the dried plant material in hot water, while topical applications involve using the essential oil or powder directly on the skin. The recommended dosage for adults is typically 1-2 capsules per day, while children should only use it if deemed safe by a healthcare professional.

It is advised to use catharanthus roseus sparingly, with a frequency and duration that remain conservative to avoid potential toxicity.

Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Catharanthus roseus can be used in medicinal preparations, particularly for its potential anticancer properties due to compounds like vincristine and vinblastine, but its use requires caution.

While it is generally considered safe when used in controlled medicinal formulations, it can cause side effects such as gastrointestinal upset, dizziness, and skin irritation when used topically or in high doses. It may interact with certain drugs, including chemotherapy agents, anticoagulants, and immunosuppressants, potentially increasing toxicity or reducing efficacy. Special populations, such as pregnant or breastfeeding women, should avoid it due to potential risks to the fetus or infant, and individuals with chronic illnesses should consult a healthcare provider before use.

For safe use, it is important to follow medical guidance, use only standardized products, and monitor for adverse reactions, especially when combining with other medications.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Catharanthus roseus grows best in well-drained, fertile soil with a pH between 5.

5 and 6. 5, requiring full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. It thrives in warm climates with consistent moisture, needing regular watering but with good drainage to prevent root rot. To ensure healthy growth, regular pruning and fertilization are essential, along with monitoring for pests such as aphids and spider mites. The best time to harvest the leaves and flowers is in the early morning, when the plant's alkaloid content is highest, using sharp scissors to cut the stems just above a node.

For storage, the harvested material should be dried in a cool, shaded area, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dark place to preserve potency and extend shelf life.
