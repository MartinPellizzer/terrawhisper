Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus)

Madagascar Periwinkle (Catharanthus roseus) is a member of the Apocynaceae family, native to Madagascar, Southeast Africa, and Comoros Islands. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for decoctions, infusions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
Typically reaching a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.
Leaves
Opposite, ovate leaves with dark green upper surface and lighter green lower surface, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Bilaterally symmetrical flowers with five petals, two of which are fused at the base, pink to white coloration, arranged in clusters of two to five, with a spurred corolla tube and a distinctive blue-violet glandular marking on the inner surface of the lower petal.
Stems
Climbing stems with opposite branching, covered in short, glandular hairs, exhibiting a succulent texture with nodes bearing leaf scars.
