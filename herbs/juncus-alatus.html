Juncus alatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Juncus alatus?

Juncus alatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. It further belongs to the order Poales and is a member of the family Juncaceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Juncus, and its specific designation is the species alatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juncus alatus has cylindrical, unbranched stems that are characterized by prominent, wing-like longitudinal ridges running along their length. These flattened wings distinguish the species from other rush plants and give the stems a distinctively angular appearance. The leaves are typically reduced to small, inconspicuous sheaths at the base of the stems to minimize water loss. Small, brownish flowers are arranged in compact clusters located near the tips of the stalks. The fruit consists of a small capsule that contains numerous tiny, dark seeds designed for dispersal.
