Astragalus floridulus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus floridulus has a perennial growth habit characterized by prostrate to ascending stems that originate from a woody caudex and bear pinnately compound leaves. Each leaf typically consists of several pairs of small, oval leaflets arranged along a central axis. The flowers are small, pea-like, and usually appear in compact heads or clusters at the ends of the stems. These blossoms exhibit a color palette ranging from white to pale pink or lavender. The plant also develops small, compressed seed pods that follow the flowering stage.
