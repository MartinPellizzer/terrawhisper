Delphinium grandiflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium grandiflorum?

Delphinium grandiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Delphinium, and is specifically identified by the species name grandiflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium grandiflorum has tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to two meters, topped by large, showy terminal racemes of blue or violet flowers. The leaves are deeply palmately lobed and possess a dark green color with a somewhat leathery texture. Each individual flower features a prominent, nectar-producing spur that extends backward from the sepals. The plant also develops a robust root system that supports its significant vertical growth in alpine environments. Small, fine hairs may occasionally be found along the stems and leaf undersides.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically spanning across parts of India, Nepal, Bhutan, and China. It thrives primarily in montane forests and subalpine meadows located at elevations between 2,500 and 4,500 meters. The species prefers the cool, moist environments found on steep slopes and rocky terrain within these mountain ranges. Because of its specific preference for temperate alpine climates, its range is restricted to these high-elevation pockets. Its distribution is closely tied to the seasonal monsoon patterns that provide necessary moisture to its habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Delphinium grandiflorum?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its anti-cancer, antioxidant, and protective effects on H2O2-induced cardiomyocytes injury. Research indicates that its chemical constituents can effectively inhibit the proliferation of various malignant cell lines. Additionally, the plant possesses robust antioxidant properties that help neutralize harmful free radicals within biological systems. These bioactive compounds also play a crucial role in safeguarding heart cells against oxidative stress caused by hydrogen peroxide. Such multi-faceted therapeutic actions suggest that the species could serve as a valuable source for future drug development.

As discussed in a study, Delphinium grandiflorum L. serves as the source for an endophytic fungus named Penicillium griseofulvum. The liquid fermentation product of this fungus, identified as EX-9, exhibits significant anti-cancer activity against triple-negative breast cancer. This product demonstrated remarkable efficacy both in vivo and in vitro while maintaining negligible toxicity. The potential mechanism for the anti-TNBC effect of EX-9 involves the inhibition of the PI3K/Akt signaling pathway. Researchers utilized liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry technology and network pharmacology methods to uncover the pharmacological substance basis of EX-9.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Grandifline A, Grandifline B, diterpenoid alkaloids, and grandiflodines. These specific chemical constituents are often studied for their potential pharmacological activities. The presence of diterpenoid alkaloids is particularly significant in the context of plant secondary metabolism. Furthermore, the unique structural properties of the grandiflodines contribute to the plant's overall chemical profile. Researchers continue to investigate how these various substances might interact with biological systems.

As discussed in a study, the aerial parts of Delphinium grandiflorum contain three new diterpenoid alkaloids labeled 1-3 and eight known alkaloids labeled 4-11. A newly discovered class of rearranged C19-diterpenoid alkaloid scaffolds named grandiflodines (B-12) was identified. Within these compounds, Grandifline A (1) features a C-7NC17 hemiaminal moiety and a lactone fragment via a C-17OC19 unit linkage. Grandifline B (2) is identified as the first naturally-occurring 7,17-secolycoctoniine diterpenoid alkaloid with a C-7OC17 unit forming a hemiacetal. Regarding protective effects on H2O2-induced cardiomyocytes injury, compounds 6 and 10 demonstrated significant results with IC50 values of 1.881 ± 0.680 μM and 1.904 ± 0.750 μM respectively. Additionally, compound 6 can reduce oxidative damage by inhibiting cell death through the AMPK/AKT/mTOR signaling pathway in H2O2-induced H9C2 cells.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe poisoning characterized by nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, and muscle weakness. Ingestion may lead to critical neurological issues such as convulsions and paralysis. Serious systemic complications include cardiac arrhythmia and dangerous respiratory depression. If these symptoms are not treated immediately, the toxic effects can culminate in death.
