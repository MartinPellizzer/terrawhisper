Delphinium grandiflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium grandiflorum?

Delphinium grandiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Delphinium, and is specifically identified by the species name grandiflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium grandiflorum has tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to two meters, topped by large, showy terminal racemes of blue or violet flowers. The leaves are deeply palmately lobed and possess a dark green color with a somewhat leathery texture. Each individual flower features a prominent, nectar-producing spur that extends backward from the sepals. The plant also develops a robust root system that supports its significant vertical growth in alpine environments. Small, fine hairs may occasionally be found along the stems and leaf undersides.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically spanning across parts of India, Nepal, Bhutan, and China. It thrives primarily in montane forests and subalpine meadows located at elevations between 2,500 and 4,500 meters. The species prefers the cool, moist environments found on steep slopes and rocky terrain within these mountain ranges. Because of its specific preference for temperate alpine climates, its range is restricted to these high-elevation pockets. Its distribution is closely tied to the seasonal monsoon patterns that provide necessary moisture to its habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Delphinium grandiflorum?

This plant has anti-cancer, antioxidant, and protective effects on H2O2-induced cardiomyocytes injury. Its chemical constituents work to neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress within biological systems. In oncology studies, specific extracts demonstrate the ability to inhibit the proliferation of various malignant cell lines. Furthermore, the plant provides significant defense against oxidative damage in heart muscle cells caused by hydrogen peroxide exposure. These diverse pharmacological properties suggest that the species holds great potential for future therapeutic applications.

According to a study published by "Journal of Asian natural products research", the medicinal plant Delphinium grandiflorum L. serves as the source for the endophytic fungus Penicillium griseofulvum. The liquid fermentation product of this fungus, known as EX-9, has exhibited significant anti-cancer activity against triple-negative breast cancer. This substance demonstrated remarkable efficacy against TNBC in both in vivo and in vitro investigations. The study also noted that EX-9 showed negligible toxicity during these tests. Researchers used liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry (LC-MS) technology and network pharmacology methods to uncover the potential pharmacological substance basis of EX-9.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several potent medicinal compounds including grandifline A, grandifline B, diterpenoid alkaloids, and grandiflodinines. These specific diterpenoid alkaloids are primarily responsible for the biological activity observed in the species. Research into these chemical constituents focuses on their unique structural properties and potential pharmacological applications. The presence of grandiflodinines further distinguishes the chemical profile of this particular Delphinium species. Scientists continue to study how these individual molecules interact with various cellular targets.

According to a study published by "Bioorganic & medicinal chemistry", three new diterpenoid alkaloids (1-3) and eight known alkaloids (4-11) were isolated from the aerial parts of Delphinium grandiflorum. Grandifline A (1) features a C-7NC17 hemiaminal moiety and a lactone fragment via a C-17OC19 unit, belonging to a new class of rearranged C19-diterpenoid alkaloid scaffolds named grandiflodines (B-12). Grandifline B (2) is identified as the first naturally-occurring 7,17-secolycoctonine diterpenoid alkaloid with a C-7OC17 unit forming a hemiacetal. Among the compounds 1-11 assayed for protective effects on H2O2-induced cardiomyocytes injury, compounds 6 and 10 showed significant results. Specifically, compound 6 demonstrated an IC50 value of 1.881 ± 0.680 μM, while compound 10 showed an IC50 value of 1.904 ± 0.750 μM. Compound 6 also reduced oxidative damage by inhibiting cell death through the AMPK/AKT/mTOR signaling pathway in H2O2-induced H9C2 cells.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe poisoning characterized by nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, and muscle weakness. Ingestion may lead to critical neurological issues such as convulsions and paralysis. Serious systemic complications include cardiac arrhythmia and dangerous respiratory depression. If these symptoms are not treated immediately, the toxic effects can culminate in death.
