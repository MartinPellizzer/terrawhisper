Canella Winterana Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Canella winterana, commonly known as the "false cinnamon" or "white cinnamon," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America, valued for its aromatic bark and leaves.

This herb is known for its warming properties and is used in traditional medicine to support digestive health and reduce inflammation.

The bioactive constituents in Canella winterana include cinnamaldehyde, eugenol, and various essential oils, which contribute to its antimicrobial, antispasmodic, and analgesic effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and poultices can be made from its bark and leaves to harness its therapeutic benefits.
