Winter's Canella

Canella winterana

Winter's Canella (Canella winterana) is a medicinal plant native to tropical regions, known for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive disorders.

Botanical Identification

Winter's Canella, scientifically known as Canella winterana, belongs to the Lauraceae family. Commonly called Winter's canella or tropical canella, it is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. The plant has aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers, with a woody root system. It is also referred to as "canella" in various traditional herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Gentianales
  • Family: Gentianaceae
  • Genus: Canella
  • Species: Canella winterana

Winter's Canella Identification.

Active Compounds

Winter's Canella contains essential oil, flavonoids, and coumarin as its main active compounds. These compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Flavonoids and coumarin may synergistically enhance the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive issues due to its antimicrobial properties.

Infusion: Used for colds and flu due to its expectorant and antiseptic properties.

Poultice: Used for skin infections and wounds due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Safety Profile

Winter's Canella is generally considered safe when used in recommended dosages. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult a healthcare provider before use.
