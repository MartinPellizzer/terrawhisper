What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia klattiana?

Lysimachia klattiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Ericales and the family Primulaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Lysimachia, with its specific designation being the species klattiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia klattiana has erect, glabrous stems that support opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute apices.

The foliage is typically bright green and arranged in a way that creates a dense, layered appearance along the stem. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, solitary, yellow flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms feature five distinct petals that form a star-like shape when fully open.
