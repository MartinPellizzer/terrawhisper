What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia klattiana has erect, glabrous stems that support opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute apices. The foliage is typically bright green and arranged in a way that creates a dense, layered appearance along the stem. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, solitary, yellow flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms feature five distinct petals that form a star-like shape when fully open. The overall structure of the plant is herbaceous, often forming low-growing clumps in its natural habitat.
