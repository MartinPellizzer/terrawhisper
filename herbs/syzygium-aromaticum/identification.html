Botanical Identification of Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum)

To identify Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Cloves, or Syzygium aromaticum, belong to the plant kingdom, specifically the flowering plants. They are classified under the magnoliophyta division, eudicots class, sapindales order, myrtaceae family, and the genus syzygium. The species is Syzygium aromaticum, known for its aromatic buds used in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name for cloves is Syzygium aromaticum, a species within the Myrtaceae family. This name was first formally described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Eugenia aromatica and Syzygium claviflorum. Common names such as clove, clove bud, and flower of the clove tree are widely used globally.

Morphological Characteristics

Cloves are small, woody trees with a dense, evergreen canopy. They have a deep taproot system that anchors the plant and accesses moisture. The stem is straight, cylindrical, and covered in rough, reddish bark. Leaves are opposite, elliptical, and dark green with a slightly serrated edge. The inflorescence is a spike of small, clustered flowers. Flowers are fragrant, pinkish-white, and bloom in clusters. The fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to red, containing a single seed.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cloves are distinguished by their compact, reddish-brown flower buds, which are harvested and dried. They have a distinct aromatic scent and a rough, woody texture. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface. The plant is part of the myrtaceae family, which helps differentiate it from similar-looking spices.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cloves are native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia. They thrive in tropical climates with high humidity and warm temperatures. They prefer well-drained, loamy soils. They are typically found at altitudes between 200 and 800 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cloves bloom from September to December, with flowers transitioning from white to pink as they mature. Seed formation occurs in the following months, from January to March. Harvesting takes place in late April to early May, when the buds are fully developed but before they open, making it crucial for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cloves belong to the genus Syzygium within the Myrtaceae family. Their closest relatives include species like Syzygium jambolanum (Java plum) and Syzygium malaccense (West Indian cherry). These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, reflecting their close phylogenetic relationship.
