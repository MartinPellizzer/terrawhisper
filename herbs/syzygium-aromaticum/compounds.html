Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum) Compounds

The primary compounds in clove include essential oil, which contains eugenol, a key component responsible for its aromatic and antimicrobial properties. Terpenoids and flavonoids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects. Phenolic acids and tannins provide antioxidant benefits, while coumarin and glycosides support various therapeutic actions. Saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further enhance its medicinal profile.

Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum) is the primary active compound, containing eugenol as its main constituent. It possesses potent antimicrobial, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating dental pain, infections, and skin conditions.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic effects. These compounds are key to the therapeutic uses of cloves in herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of compounds found in Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. These compounds may support immune function and have anti-inflammatory effects. Flavonoids are also believed to enhance the therapeutic potential of clove in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps in reducing oxidative stress and supporting immune function. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of clove in herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in cloves (Syzygium aromaticum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in cloves also have antioxidant and antimicrobial effects, enhancing the plant's traditional uses in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a naturally occurring compound found in cloves (Syzygium aromaticum). It contributes to the spice's aromatic profile and has potential pharmacological properties, including anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is also known for its role in the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has mild antiseptic effects. Glycosides in cloves may support digestive health and help reduce inflammation. They are part of the complex mixture of active compounds in this traditional medicinal plant.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial properties by disrupting cell membranes of pathogens. Saponin also aids in the plant's ability to repel insects and may support digestive health when consumed in herbal remedies.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by interacting with biological systems. While not the primary active component, alkaloids may support other therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a polyphenolic compound found in Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum). It contributes to the deep red color of the flower buds and possesses antioxidant properties. While not the primary active constituent, anthocyanin may support overall plant health and contribute to the therapeutic effects of clove in herbal medicine.
