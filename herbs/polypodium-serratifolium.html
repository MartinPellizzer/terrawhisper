Polypodium Serratifolium

Polypodium serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated holly fern, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and polysaccharides, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties. It has been used in folk medicine to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing. Some studies suggest that extracts from Polypodium serratifolium may exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant activities.

However, further scientific research is needed to fully validate its therapeutic potential and ensure its safe use in modern medicine.
