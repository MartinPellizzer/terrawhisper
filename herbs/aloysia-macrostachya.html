What is Aloysia macrostachya?

Aloysia macrostachya is a perennial shrub native to South America, belonging to the family Verbenaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems, which can grow up to 3 meters tall, and its opposite, lanceolate leaves, measuring 2-5 centimeters long and 0.5-1 centimeter wide. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers in axillary racemes, with four-merous corolla and a calyx with four lobes. The flowers are followed by small, brownish-purple fruits, which contain several seeds.

Aloysia macrostachya is a member of the Aloysia genus, which comprises approximately 40 species of shrubs and small trees.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Aloysia macrostachya are tea, infusion, tincture, salve, and essential oil.

Aloysia macrostachya tea is traditionally made by infusing the leaves in hot water, often used to treat digestive issues and as a mild sedative. Infusions can be prepared by steeping the leaves in cold water for a longer period, allowing for a more subtle extraction of bioactive compounds. A tincture is made by extracting the bioactive compounds from the leaves using a solvent like ethanol or glycerin, often used in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oil of Aloysia macrostachya can be obtained through steam distillation and has been reported to have antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, making it suitable for use in skincare and wound healing preparations, such as salves.
