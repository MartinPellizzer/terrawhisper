Canscora lucidissima

What's the taxonomical classification of Canscora lucidissima?

Canscora lucidissima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down its taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Gentianaales and falls within the family Gentianaceae. Finally, it is defined by the genus Canscora and is identified by the specific species epithet lucidissima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Canscora lucidissima has glossy, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along a woody, upright stem. The foliage is characterized by a deep green color and a highly reflective surface texture. Its flowers are small and typically emerge in clusters from the leaf axils. The plant develops a branched structure that supports its dense, shrub-like growth habit. These morphological traits help the species thrive in its specific ecological niche.

What are the pharmacological activities of Canscora lucidissima?

This plant has anti-arrhythmic, anti-inflammatory, antitumor, ferroptosis inhibitory, and protective effects on anoxia/reoxygenation damages on myocardium pharmacological activities. These diverse therapeutic properties suggest that the chemical constituents of Canscora lucidissima can influence multiple cellular pathways. Specifically, its ability to inhibit ferroptosis may help prevent cell death caused by iron-dependent oxidative stress. The plant also demonstrates a capacity to mitigate myocardial injury during periods of oxygen deprivation and subsequent restoration. Furthermore, its potential to combat inflammation and tumor growth highlights its broader medicinal importance.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", the xanthones 1,8-dihydroxy-3,5-dimethoxyxanthone (Xan-I), 1-hydroxy-5-dimethoxyxanthone (Xan-II), and 1-hydroxy-3,7,8-trimethoxyanthone (Xan-III) from Canscora lucidissima show protective effects against arrhythmia induced by myocardial ischemia-reperfusion. An intravenous injection of 1 mg/kg of these xanthones 3 min before left coronary artery ligation significantly reduces the incidence and duration of ventricular arrhythmia. These substances diminish the release of glutamic oxaloacetic transaminase and lactate dehydrogenase while elevating superoxide dismutase activity and reducing malondialdehyde amounts. The potency of these effects follows the order of Xan-I > Xan-III > Xan-II. The protective mechanism is associated with the reduction of myocardial lipid peroxidation and the enhancement of SOD activity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains various medicinal compounds including the xanthone glycosides canlucones A-I, the specific molecule canscorin A (1), and 1-hydroxy-5-dimethoxyxanthone (Xam-II). These bioactive xanthones represent a significant portion of the chemical profile found within the species. The presence of these diverse xanthone glycosides suggests potential pharmacological applications for the plant. Researchers continue to study these specific constituents to understand their biological activities. Such chemical diversity makes the plant a subject of interest in natural product chemistry.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", nine undescribed xanthones named canlucones A-I (1-9) and eleven known analogues (10-20) were isolated from the whole plants of Canscora lucidissima. These isolates include six sets of isomers: 1/11/17, 2/3/5, 4/6/12, 7/9, 8/16/19, and 13/14/15/18. Canlucones A-I from the whole plant exhibit anti-inflammatory and anti-tumor activities. Compounds 7 and 11 showed significant inhibitory effects on nitric oxide production with IC50 values of 23.39 and 13.90 μM, respectively. Furthermore, compound 7 exhibited cytotoxic activity against rat glioma C6 cells with an IC50 value of 28.85 μM.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating inflammation, inflammatory diseases, tumors, glioma, and myocardial ischemia-reperfusion injury. Its bioactive compounds demonstrate significant potential in reducing systemic inflammation and managing various inflammatory diseases. Research also suggests the plant may inhibit the growth and progression of tumors, specifically targeting glioma cells in the brain. Furthermore, it shows promise in protecting cardiac tissues during myocardial ischemia-reperfusion injury. These diverse therapeutic properties make it a subject of intense pharmacological interest.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", canlucones A-I from the whole plant of Canscora lucidissima exhibit anti-inflamatory and antitumor activities. Nine undescribed xanthones named canlucones A-I and eleven known analogues were isolated from the whole plants. Compounds 7 and 11 demonstrated significant inhibitory effects on nitric oxide production with IC50 values of 23.39 and 13.90 μM, respectively. These compounds also markedly reduced the production of pro-inflamatory cytokines TNF-α and IL-6. Furthermore, compound 7 exhibited cytotoxic activity against rat glioma C6 cells with an IC50 value of 28.85 μM.
