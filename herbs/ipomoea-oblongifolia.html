Ipomoea oblongifolia

Ipomoea oblongifolia, commonly known as the "Indian morning glory" or "Bindweed", is a perennial plant species native to tropical Asia and Africa.

This plant has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its medicinal properties, which include providing health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, as well as treating various health conditions like fever, rheumatism, and skin ailments.

The therapeutic actions of Ipomoea oblongifolia include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, making it useful for wound healing and treating infections.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties and health benefits.
