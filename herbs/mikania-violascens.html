What is Mikania violascens?

Mikania violascens is a perennial climbing plant in the family Asteraceae, native to Central and South America.

It has woody stems with a diameter of up to 10 mm, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, measuring 5-15 cm in length and 2-7 cm in width, with a petiole of 1-6 cm. The plant produces small white or purple flowers in a panicled inflorescence, which are arranged in a corymbiform pattern, and the achenes are 2.5-3 mm long with 10-12 ribs. The plant's growth habit is characterized by its ability to climb using its long petioles and adventitious roots, allowing it to grow up to 10 m in length.

Mikania violascens has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including fever and rheumatism.
