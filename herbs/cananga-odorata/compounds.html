Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) Compounds

The primary compounds of the Lantern Tree include essential oil, which contributes to its aromatic properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Terpenoids and coumarin also play roles in its medicinal value, while phenolic acids and glycosides support anti-inflammatory actions. Tannins, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further contribute to its diverse therapeutic potential.

Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) is a key active compound, known for its aromatic properties and therapeutic uses. It contains compounds like sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, which contribute to its calming and anti-inflammatory effects in traditional herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata). These compounds contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. They are often used in traditional herbal medicine to support skin health and treat inflammatory conditions.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds found in the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata). These compounds contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has been studied for its potential medicinal effects, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic actions. Coumarin is often extracted from the flower buds and used in traditional herbal remedies.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound is often extracted from the flower buds and used in traditional herbal remedies. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Cananga odorata in treating various ailments.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine. Glycosides in Cananga odorata are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. These compounds are often extracted and used in formulations for their medicinal benefits.

7. Tannin

Tannin in the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) contributes to its astringent properties. It is found in the bark and leaves, and may support wound healing and reduce inflammation. Tannins also act as natural preservatives, enhancing the plant's traditional medicinal uses.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support immune function and are often extracted for use in natural remedies.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential effects on the nervous system. Alkaloids from Cananga odorata may help in treating conditions like anxiety and insomnia, though research is ongoing.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in the Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower colors and has antioxidant properties. These compounds may support skin health and reduce oxidative stress, making them relevant in herbal medicine for their potential therapeutic benefits.
