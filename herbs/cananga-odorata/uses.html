Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) Uses

The Lantern Tree is used to treat respiratory issues such as asthma, bronchitis, and cough by reducing inflammation and clearing mucous congestion. It also aids in healing skin infections, wounds, and inflammatory skin conditions, including fungal infections and inflamed skin.

Lantern Tree (Cananga odorata) Monograph

1. Asthma

The Lantern Tree's flowers are used in traditional medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms. The essential oil, rich in compounds like linalool and geraniol, has bronchodilating properties that help ease breathing. It is often prepared as a steam inhalation or topical application to reduce respiratory congestion.

2. Respiratory infections

The Lantern Tree's essential oil is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing and congestion by supporting bronchial health. Often applied in steam inhalations or as a topical remedy, it is valued for its soothing effects on the respiratory system.

3. Bronchitis

The Lantern Tree's essential oil is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat bronchitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce airway inflammation, easing breathing in those with chronic bronchial irritation. The oil is often inhaled or applied topically to support respiratory health.

4. Cough

The lantern tree's essential oil is used to alleviate cough symptoms due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Typically, it is diluted and inhaled or applied topically for localized relief. Its effectiveness is supported by traditional use in herbal medicine practices.

5. Mucous congestion

The Lantern Tree's flowers are used to alleviate mucous congestion due to their expectorant properties. They help loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract, easing symptoms of coughs and colds. This traditional use is supported by its long history in herbal medicine for respiratory support.

6. Skin infections

The Lantern Tree's essential oil is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce bacterial and fungal growth, making it effective for conditions like eczema and fungal infections. The oil is often diluted before application to prevent skin irritation.

7. Wound healing

The Lantern Tree's essential oils are applied topically to promote wound healing by reducing inflammation and preventing infection. Its antimicrobial properties accelerate tissue repair and enhance skin regeneration, making it a valuable ingredient in traditional herbal remedies for burns, cuts, and skin injuries.

8. Inflammatory skin conditions

The Lantern Tree's essential oil is used topically to treat inflammatory skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and irritation, promoting skin healing. The oil is often diluted before application to avoid skin sensitivity.

9. Fungal infections

The Lantern Tree's essential oil, rich in compounds like safrole and linalool, exhibits antifungal properties. It is traditionally used to treat fungal infections such as athlete's foot and ringworm. The oil's ability to inhibit fungal growth makes it a valuable component in herbal remedies for skin infections. Its antimicrobial effects support its role in natural treatments for various fungal conditions.

10. Inflamed skin

The Lantern Tree's leaves and flowers are used topically to treat inflamed skin due to their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. A poultice made from crushed leaves can soothe rashes, eczema, and minor burns. The essential oil extracted from the flowers also helps reduce redness and irritation when applied carefully.
