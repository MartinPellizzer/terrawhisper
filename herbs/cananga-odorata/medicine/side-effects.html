10 Possible Side Effects Of Cananga Odorata

Cananga odorata has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and respiratory issues.

These reactions can occur due to its high concentration of linalool, a potential allergen.

In severe cases, side effects can lead to life-altering consequences, such as chronic skin conditions, breathing difficulties, or even anaphylaxis, severely impacting daily life and quality of time with loved ones.

1. Causes dizziness

Cananga odorata causes dizziness due to its ability to affect the brain's limbic system.

The essential oil's volatile compounds, particularly linalool and beta-caryophyllene, can influence serotonin levels and alter neurotransmitter activity. This may lead to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness in some individuals.

As a result, the senses of balance and equilibrium are disrupted, causing disorientation and possible fainting spells.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Cananga odorata triggers allergic reactions due to its high concentration of allergenic compounds such as sesquiterpenes and sesquiphellandrene.

These chemicals can cause an immune response in sensitive individuals, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the oil's high ester content may also contribute to an allergic reaction, making it essential for users to be aware of this potential side effect when using Cananga odorata products.

3. Induces nausea

Cananga odorata induces nausea due to its strong and pungent aroma.

The essential oil's high concentration of aldehydes can cause an overwhelming sensation in some individuals, leading to feelings of queasiness and discomfort.

Additionally, the oil's sweet and floral notes can also trigger a release of serotonin, a neurotransmitter that regulates digestive processes, potentially contributing to nausea.

4. Produces headaches

Cananga odorata produces headaches due to its potent and intense aroma, which can cause sensitivity in some individuals.

The strong scent of the essential oil can irritate the nervous system, leading to a feeling of pressure or tension in the head, ultimately resulting in headaches.

Additionally, the stimulating properties of Cananga odorata may also contribute to this side effect by increasing heart rate and blood flow, exacerbating any existing sensitivity.

5. Increases heart rate

Cananga odorata increases heart rate due to its stimulating effect on the nervous system.

The essential oil's beta-amyrin and sesquiterpenes have been found to increase blood pressure and heart rate by directly interacting with the autonomic nervous system, leading to an increase in sympathetic tone and a decrease in parasympathetic tone.

This can cause palpitations, tachycardia, and other cardiovascular symptoms.

6. Lowers blood pressure

Cananga odorata lowers blood pressure due to its potent vasodilatory properties.

The essential oil's active compounds, such as benzaldehyde and linalool, cause the blood vessels to relax and dilate, leading to a decrease in blood pressure. This can result in dizziness, lightheadedness, and even fainting in some individuals.

As the body adjusts to the reduced blood flow, other side effects may include headaches, nausea, and fatigue.

7. Interferes with sleep

Cananga odorata interferes with sleep due to its stimulating effect on the mind and body.

The oil's sweet and floral scent can increase heart rate and blood pressure, making it difficult for individuals to relax and fall asleep.

Additionally, the essential oil's sedative properties may be overpowered by its invigorating aroma, leading to feelings of restlessness and agitation rather than drowsiness, ultimately disrupting normal sleep patterns.

8. Affects digestion

Cananga odorata affects digestion by stimulating the release of stomach acid and enzymes, which can lead to an upset stomach, nausea, and diarrhea in some individuals.

This is due to its natural properties that interact with the digestive system, potentially causing an imbalance in gut bacteria.

As a result, users may experience discomfort, bloating, or stomach pain when using Cananga odorata essential oil.

9. Irritates skin

Cananga odorata irritates skin due to its high concentration of volatile compounds, particularly linalool and β-pinene.

These essential oils can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and even contact dermatitis in some individuals.

The fragrance molecules penetrate the skin deeply, triggering an inflammatory response and leading to redness, itching, and discomfort.

10. Causes respiratory problems

Cananga odorata causes respiratory problems due to its potent allergenic properties.

The plant's fragrance molecules can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to inflammation and constriction of the airways.

This reaction can cause symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath, especially in people with pre-existing respiratory conditions or allergies.
