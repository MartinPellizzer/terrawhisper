10 Medicinal Constituents Of Cananga Odorata (Ylang-Ylang)

Cananga odorata has active constituents such as volatile oils, flavonoids, and sesquiterpenes.

These compounds confer medicinal properties that include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiseptic effects. The constituents of ylang-ylang oil can help to soothe skin irritations, reduce stress and anxiety, and improve cardiovascular health by lowering blood pressure and cholesterol levels. Additionally, its antimicrobial properties make it effective against infections and wounds, allowing for faster healing and reduced risk of complications.

These properties can significantly improve the quality of life for individuals suffering from various ailments, promoting overall well-being and enhancing their mental and physical health.

1. Linalool

Cananga odorata linalool is a naturally occurring compound found in the essential oil of the plant.

It is responsible for its sweet and floral fragrance. Linalool is a sesquiterpene that has been traditionally used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation, reduce stress and anxiety, and improve mood.

Its calming properties make it an effective ingredient in skincare products, helping to soothe and calm irritated skin.

In addition, it has been shown to have antimicrobial and antifungal properties, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products.

3. Geraniol
