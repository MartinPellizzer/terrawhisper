What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Cananga Odorata (Ylang-Ylang)?

Cananga odorata, also known as ylang-ylang, has health benefits such as reducing stress and anxiety, promoting relaxation, and soothing skin irritations.

The medicinal constituents of this herb include volatile oils like linalool and linalyl acetate, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. Medicinal preparations of ylang-ylang include essential oils, infusions, and topical creams. Side effects of excessive use may include skin irritation or allergic reactions.

Precautions include patch testing and consulting with a healthcare professional, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding individuals.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Cananga odorata.

What are the health benefits of Cananga odorata?

Cananga odorata has health benefits such as reducing stress and anxiety due to its calming effects on the nervous system.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers has been shown to lower blood pressure, improve sleep quality, and alleviate symptoms of depression.

Additionally, ylang-ylang's antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties can help soothe skin conditions like acne and eczema, making it a popular ingredient in natural skincare products.

What are the active constituents of Cananga odorata?

The health benefits of Cananga odorata comes from its active constituents, such as beta-caryophyllene, a sesquiterpene with anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

It also contains flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, which have antioxidant and antimicrobial activities.

Additionally, ylang-ylang essential oil is rich in geraniol, linalool, and benzyl acetate, which contribute to its calming, sedative, and antidepressant effects, making it a popular ingredient in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

What are the medicinal preparations of Cananga odorata?

Cananga odorata has different medicinal preparations, such as essential oil, extracts, and infusions.

The essential oil is used to treat anxiety, depression, and stress due to its calming properties. Ylang-ylang extracts are used in skincare products for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, while infusions made from the flowers are consumed as a tea to reduce fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

These preparations can also be used topically to soothe wounds and skin irritations, making ylang-ylang a versatile herb with various medicinal applications.

What are the possible side effect of using Cananga odorata improperly?

Improper use of Cananga odorata increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, respiratory issues, and skin irritation.

Ingesting large amounts can cause dizziness, nausea, vomiting, and even kidney damage. It may interact with medications, leading to adverse interactions, while applying it undiluted can cause skin burns and redness.

Additionally, ylang-ylang oil may exacerbate pre-existing health conditions like epilepsy, diabetes, or high blood pressure, requiring immediate medical attention if symptoms worsen.

What precautions to take when using Cananga odorata medicinally?

Before using Cananga odorata for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise caution when using ylang-ylang essential oil due to its potential effects on hormone regulation.

Furthermore, individuals with allergies or sensitivities to related plants should avoid using ylang-ylang altogether.
