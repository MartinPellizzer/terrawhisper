Clematis potaninii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis potaninii has climbing stems with opposite, trifoliate leaves consisting of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets that feature serrated margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically a bright green color with a slightly textured surface. Its flowers are characterized by four to six large, petal-like sepals that range in color from pale violet to deep purple. These blossoms are often accompanied by numerous prominent stamens that extend outward from the center. The plant produces small, achene-type fruits that are topped by persistent, feathery styles.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of central and western China, specifically within the provinces of Gansu, Qinghai, Sichuan, and Tibet. It primarily inhabits montane environments where rocky slopes and alpine meadows provide suitable growing conditions. The species is often found at elevations ranging from medium to high mountain levels. Within these specific habitats, it thrives in the cool, temperate climates characteristic of the eastern Tibetan Plateau. Its distribution is relatively localized to these mountainous corridors of East Asia.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis potaninii can cause skin irritation, swelling, or dermatitis upon direct contact with its sap or foliage. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that acts as a significant irritant to the mucous membranes and skin. If the juices from the plant come into contact with sensitive areas, they may trigger redness or itchy rashes. Accidental ingestion of the plant material can lead to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea and vomiting. It is important to wear gloves when pruning or handling this species to prevent these adverse reactions.
