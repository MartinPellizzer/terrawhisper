Cyrtomium urophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyrtomium urophyllum?

Cyrtomium urophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Polypodiales and is a member of the family Polypodiaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Cyrtomium and the species urophyllum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyrtomium urophyllum has large, deeply divided, bipinnate to tripinnate fronds that typically reach lengths of 20 to 60 centimeters.

The individual pinnules are characterized by an oblong or lanceolate shape with distinctively lobed or sinuate margins. These delicate, feathery leaves emerge from a short, creeping rhizome that stays close to the substrate. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori located on the underside of the fertile leaflets.

This fern is often recognized by its graceful, arching habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist forest environments.
