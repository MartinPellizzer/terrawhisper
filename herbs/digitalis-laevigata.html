Common Foxglove (Digitalis Laevigata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Digitalis laevigata.

Common foxglove, scientifically known as Digitalis laevigata, is a medicinal herb native to the British Isles and parts of Europe, valued for its historical and contemporary therapeutic applications. This plant contains cardiac glycosides, such as digoxin, which have been historically used to treat heart conditions by strengthening heart contractions and regulating heart rhythm. Traditionally, it was used in herbal medicine to address ailments like dropsy (edema) and respiratory issues, and it held cultural significance in folk remedies and apothecary practices. In modern wellness, it is still used under strict medical supervision for heart failure and arrhythmias, though its use is limited due to its potent and toxic nature. Its unique feature lies in its striking bell-shaped flowers and the presence of rare cardiac-active compounds, making it both a botanical curiosity and a potent medicinal plant with a rich historical legacy.
