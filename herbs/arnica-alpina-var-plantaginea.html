Possible Side Effects of Arnica alpina var. plantaginea

Arnica alpina var. plantaginea can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased risk of bleeding due to its anticoagulant properties.

Ingesting large amounts of the plant's extracts can lead to nausea, vomiting, and stomach ulcers.

Prolonged use or excessive application of arnica gel or oil on the skin may cause photosensitivity, making the skin more susceptible to sunburn.

It is also essential to note that arnica should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding, as it may stimulate uterine contractions and affect fetal development.
