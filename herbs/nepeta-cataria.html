What to know about Nepeta cataria (catnip) before using it medicinally

Catnip, scientifically known as Nepeta cataria, is a herb renowned for its impressive health properties, which have been proven to alleviate stress and anxiety in humans while also improving digestion and reducing inflammation.

While it's often associated with its stimulating effects on cats, catnip is also used in various culinary dishes, particularly in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisine, where it adds a unique flavor and aroma to teas, soups, and sauces. As an ornamental plant, catnip boasts attractive purple flowers and leaves that are easy to grow and maintain, making it a popular choice among gardeners. From a botanical perspective, catnip belongs to the mint family and contains compounds like nepetalactone, which is responsible for its distinctive scent and potency.

With historical references dating back to ancient Greece and Rome, where it was used as a medicinal herb and ingredient in cooking, catnip has been prized for centuries for its numerous benefits and uses.
