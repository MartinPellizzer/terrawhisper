Botanical Identification

Catnip, scientifically known as Nepeta cataria, belongs to the Lamiaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. Commonly called catnip, it has opposite, serrated leaves and blue-violet flowers, with a distinctive scent due to its volatile oils. Synonyms include "catmint" and "nepeta," often used in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Catnip contains essential oil, coumarin, and flavonoids as its primary active compounds. The essential oil, rich in nepetalactone, is responsible for its psychoactive effects on cats. These compounds work synergistically to influence neurotransmitter activity and may contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Essential oil: Used for treating anxiety, insomnia, and headaches due to its calming properties.

Tincture: Used for digestive issues and nervous system support due to its active compounds.

Oil infusion: Used for muscle pain and inflammation due to its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Safety Profile

Catnip, or Nepeta cataria, is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by people with epilepsy or seizure disorders due to its potential to lower the seizure threshold.
