Nepeta cataria

What's the taxonomical classification of Nepeta cataria?

Nepeta cataria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized into the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, falling under the genus Nepeta, and is specifically identified by its species name, catarica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Nepeta catarica contains nepetalactone, a volatile monoterpene alcohol that serves as its primary bioactive compound. This specific chemical is responsible for the plant's distinct aroma and its ability to interact with insect nervous systems. In addition to nepetalactone, the plant contains various flavonoids and tannins that contribute to its overall chemical profile. These secondary metabolites are often studied for their potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties in traditional medicine. Researchers also investigate the presence of other essential oils within the leaves that may offer mild sedative effects.

What are the therapeutic actions of Nepeta cataria?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including digestive issues, respiratory infections, nervous system disorders, and skin irritations. Its volatile oils provide mild sedative properties that help alleviate anxiety and insomnia. When applied topically, the plant can soothe minor skin inflammations and wounds. It also functions as a carminative to reduce bloating and gas within the digestive tract. Additionally, the herb acts as an expectorant to help clear mucus during common colds.
