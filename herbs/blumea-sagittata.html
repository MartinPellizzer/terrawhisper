Blumea sagittata

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea sagittata?

Blumea sagittata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae and is specifically identified within the genus Blumea, ultimately reaching its scientific designation at the species level as Blumea sagittata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea sagittata has lanceolate to sagittate leaves with acute tips, serrated margins, and a distinctively hairy or tomentose texture on both surfaces. The stems are typically erect and covered in fine, soft pubescence that provides a velvety feel. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flower heads arranged in dense, terminal clusters or panicles. The individual florets are tubular and characteristic of the Asteraceae family, often displaying a subtle scent. These morphological features collectively help distinguish the species from other members of its genus in tropical environments.
