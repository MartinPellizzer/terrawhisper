What's the taxonomical classification of Berberis johannis?

Berberis johannis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is organized under the order Ranunculales and falls within the family Berberidaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Berberis, with the unique designation of the species johannis.

These blossoms eventually develop into fleshy, oblong berries that serve as a primary reproductive feature.
