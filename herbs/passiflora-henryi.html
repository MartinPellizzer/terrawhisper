Passiflora henryi

What's the taxonomical classification of Passiflora henryi?

Passiflora henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its progression through the order Malpighiales, the plant is situated within the family Passifloraceae. Finally, it is identified under the genus Passiflora, specifically as the species henryi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Passiflora henryi contains various bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These secondary metabolites are primarily concentrated in the leaves and stems of the plant. The presence of specific flavonoids suggests that the species may possess significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research into its alkaloid profile indicates a potential for calming or sedative effects on the central nervous system. Understanding the precise concentration of these compounds is essential for determining their pharmacological efficacy in medicinal applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Passiflora henryi?

Passiflora henryi has sedative, anxiolytic, and antispasmodic therapeutic actions used primarily to alleviate insomnia and nervous tension. The plant contains bioactive alkaloids and flavonoids that interact with the central nervous system to promote relaxation. These compounds help modulate neurotransmitter activity, which reduces feelings of anxiety and mental agitation. Additionally, its antispasmodic properties assist in soothing muscle contractions and gastrointestinal distress. Regular use is often focused on stabilizing mood and improving the overall quality of sleep through these calming effects.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat insomnia, generalized anxiety disorder, and various forms of nervous tension or agitation. The botanical properties of Passiflora henryi are believed to help stabilize the central nervous system through its sedative effects. Many practitioners utilize its extracts to calm restlessness and improve sleep quality in patients suffering from stress. It is often incorporated into herbal remedies aimed at reducing symptoms of emotional instability. While research continues, its traditional application focuses on managing psychological discomfort and sleep disturbances.
