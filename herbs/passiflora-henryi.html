Passiflora henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Passiflora henryi has a climbing habit characterized by slender, twining stems that produce large, deeply three-lobed leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a vibrant green color and a somewhat coriaceous texture. Its inflorescences feature complex flowers with distinctive corona filaments that surround a prominent central structure of anthers and a trifid style. The fruit develops into an ovoid or globose berry that transitions in color as it reaches maturity. These morphological traits are essential for identifying this specific species within the Passiflora genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of Colombia, specifically within the Andean mountain ranges. It typically thrives in cloud forests and montane ecosystems located at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 3,000 meters above sea level. These specific tropical mountain habitats provide the consistent moisture and cool temperatures necessary for its survival. Its distribution is relatively localized compared to more widespread passionflower species. Consequently, finding this particular species requires searching within these niche ecological zones of the Colombian Andes.
