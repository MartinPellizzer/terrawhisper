Trichilia prieureana

What's the taxonomical classification of Trichilia prieureana?

Trichilia prieureana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This species is a member of the order Sapindales and falls under the family Meliaceae. Finally, its taxonomic placement is completed within the genus Trichilia, specifically identified as the species prieureana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichilia prieureana has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of oblong to elliptic leaflets with entire margins and acute apices. The bark of the tree is typically smooth and greyish, while the branches exhibit a woody structure characteristic of the Meliaceae family. Small, fragrant flowers are arranged in panicle inflorescences, often appearing in axillary clusters. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits to reveal seeds frequently enveloped in a fleshy aril. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its tropical forest habitat.
