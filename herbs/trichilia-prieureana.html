Trichilia prieureana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichilia prieureana has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of oblong to elliptic leaflets with entire margins and acute apices. The bark of the tree is typically smooth and greyish, while the branches exhibit a woody structure characteristic of the Meliaceae family. Small, fragrant flowers are arranged in panicle inflorescences, often appearing in axillary clusters. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits to reveal seeds frequently enveloped in a fleshy aril. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its tropical forest habitat.

    What are the herbal preparations of Trichilia prieureana?

    Trichilia prieureana is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using the plant's bark, leaves, and roots. The bark is often boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for internal ailments. Leaves may be crushed into a fine paste to be applied directly to the skin for treating sores or infections.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Trichilia prieureana can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, the plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. Topical application of its extracts might lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling upon contact with the dermis. If consumed, the chemical constituents could potentially disrupt digestive processes or cause nausea. Users should exercise caution and avoid direct contact with raw plant material to prevent these adverse reactions.
