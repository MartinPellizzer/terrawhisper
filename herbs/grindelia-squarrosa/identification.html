Botanical Identification of Desert Gumweed (Grindelia squarrosa)

To identify Desert Gumweed (Grindelia squarrosa) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Desert Gumweed, Grindelia squarrosa, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Compositae, also known as the Asteraceae family. The genus is Grindelia, and the species is Grindelia squarrosa. The division, class, and order are not specified in the provided data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Desert Gumweed is Grindelia squarrosa, first described by the botanist Thomas Nuttall in 1818. It has no widely recognized synonyms, though it is commonly known as Desert Gumweed. Regional and historical names are not well documented, but it has been referred to in some older texts as "Desert Gumweed" or "Squarrosa Gumweed."

Morphological Characteristics

Desert Gumweed is a shrub with a woody root system that anchors it in arid environments. Its stems are erect and often branched, covered in a sticky resin. Leaves are gray-green, narrow, and covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence is a dense cluster of yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. The fruit is a small capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Desert Gumweed can be distinguished by its distinctive yellow-green, woolly leaves and spherical, resinous seed heads. It has a strong, aromatic scent when crushed, and its leaves are typically more hairy and broader than those of similar species like Grindelia lyrata. The stem is also more branched and covered in fine hairs.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Desert Gumweed is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. It prefers well-drained soils, often found in sandy or gravelly areas. It typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 4,000 feet.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Desert Gumweed blooms from April to June, producing yellowish-green flower heads. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the most accurate harvest time is during the flowering period, when the plant is most distinguishable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Grindelia squarrosa is most closely related to species within the genus Grindelia, particularly Grindelia leucophaea. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, sharing traits like composite flowers and glandular hairs. Its phylogenetic placement reflects adaptations to arid environments common in the tribe Amblygastrae.
