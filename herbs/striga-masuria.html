Striga masuria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Striga, with its unique designation being the species masuria.

        What are the herbal preparations of Striga masuria?

        Striga masuria is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried roots, a concentrated tincture made with high-proof alcohol, or a topical poultice using crushed fresh leaves. The decoction method involves simmering the woody root parts in water to extract deep-seated alkaloids for internal use.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Striga masuria can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. If swallowed, the chemical compounds within the plant might lead to nausea or abdominal pain. It is important to exercise caution when working near this species to avoid these adverse reactions.
