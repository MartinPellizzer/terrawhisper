Pholidota cantonensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pholidota cantonensis?

Pholidota cantonensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asparagales. As a member of the family Orchidaceae, it is placed in the genus Pholidota, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species cantonensis.

According to a study published by "Plant foods for human nutrition (Dordrecht, Netherlands)", Pholidota cantonensis Rolfe is an edible medicinal plant reported to possess anti-α-glucosidase activity. The 50% ethanol fraction obtained from D101 macroporous adsorption resin column chromatography, known as the D50 fraction, demonstrated the most prominent α-glucosidase inhibitory activity with an IC50 of 30.01 ± 7.30 µg/mL. This D50 fraction contained a total phenol content of 353.83 ± 6.06 mg GAE/g and included 45 identified compounds. Molecular docking of six main constituents showed binding energies between -29.95 and -11.41 kJ/mol. These findings suggest the D50 fraction has potential value for developing innovative drugs for the prevention and treatment of diabetes mellitus.
