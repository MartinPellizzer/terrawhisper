Possible Side Effects of Avonia herreana

Avonia herreana can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals, particularly when applied to broken skin or taken orally without proper guidance.

Ingestion of large amounts can lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Additionally, prolonged exposure to the plant's sap can cause skin discoloration and blistering.

Prolonged internal use can also cause liver and kidney damage due to its toxic compounds.
