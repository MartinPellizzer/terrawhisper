What's the taxonomical classification of Ephedra minuta?

Ephedra minuta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this botanical framework, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Gnetidea. The plant is further categorized under the order Ephedrales and is the sole representative of the family Ephedraceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement within the genus Ephedra, specifically as the species minuta.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Ephedra minuta contains medicinal alkaloids primarily located within its aerial stems and green shoots. The stems are typically harvested and processed to create various traditional decoctions or standardized extracts. Practitioners often utilize these stem-derived components to manage respiratory ailments like asthma or to stimulate the central nervous system. What are the therapeutic actions of Ephedra minuta?

Ephedra minuta has therapeutic properties derived from its alkaloids that act as a potent stimulant, bronchodilator, sympathomimetic, vasoconstrictor, and diaphoretic. As a sympathomimetic agent, it mimics the effects of the sympathetic nervous system to increase physiological activity. This stimulation results in bronchodilation, which helps to open the airways and ease breathing. The plant also functions as a vasoconstrictor by narrowing blood vessels, which can influence blood pressure. Additionally, its diaphoretic action promotes sweating to assist in regulating body temperature.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause serious cardiovascular and neurological side effects including nausea, dizziness, tachycardia, hypertension, palpitations, insomnia, anxiety, tremors, headache, and restlessness. The presence of ephedrine alkaloids often leads to an elevated heart rate and significantly increased blood pressure. Users frequently report feeling physically agitated or experiencing a racing heart that feels like palpitations. Mental disturbances such as anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness are also common as the stimulant affects the central nervous system. Physical discomfort may further manifest through persistent headaches, tremors, or a general sense of dizziness and nausea.
