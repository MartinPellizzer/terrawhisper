What's the taxonomical classification of Anemone tomentosa?

Anemone tomentosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Anemone, and is specifically identified by its species name, tomentoza.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone tomentosa has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a woody rhizome and stems that are densely covered in soft, woolly hairs. These hairs, which give the species its name, extend across the stems and the undersides of the leaf segments. The inflorescence consists of solitary flowers that feature delicate petals and a central cluster of prominent stamens.
