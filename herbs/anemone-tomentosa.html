Anemone tomentosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone tomentosa has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a woody rhizome and stems that are densely covered in soft, woolly hairs. The leaves are typically basal and deeply divided into narrow, linear lobes that provide a fine texture to the foliage. These hairs, which give the species its name, extend across the stems and the undersides of the leaf segments. The inflorescence consists of solitary flowers that feature delicate petals and a central cluster of prominent stamens. This dense pubescence serves as a key diagnostic feature for distinguishing the plant from related species within its genus.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Anemone tomentosa can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense redness upon direct contact with its toxic sap. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical irritant that triggers inflammatory reactions in humans and animals. If the sap touches the skin, it may result in blistering or painful dermatitis. Accidental ingestion of the plant can lead to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea and vomiting. Because of these potent irritants, extreme caution must be exercised when handling any part of the specimen.
