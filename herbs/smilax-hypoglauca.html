What's the taxonomical classification of Smilax hypoglauca?

Smilax hypoglauca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Liliales, it is further nested within the family Smilacaceae. The plant is represented by the genus Smilax, specifically identified by its unique species designation, hypoglauca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax hypoglauca has woody, climbing stems equipped with axillary spines and leaves that are typically lanceolate to ovate in shape. The foliage is characterized by a lack of a glaucous underside, which distinguishes it from many related species in the genus. These leaves are arranged alternately along the climbing vines and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils to eventually produce clusters of berries. The plant relies on its specialized stem structure to scramble through dense forest undergrowth.
