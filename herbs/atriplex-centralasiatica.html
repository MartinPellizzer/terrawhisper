Atriplex centralasiatica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Atriplex centralasiatica has prostrate or ascending perennial herbs characterized by woody bases and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically fleshy with entire or slightly wavy margins. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a mealy or scurfy texture due to the presence of specialized bladder cells. Its inflorescences are arranged in compact panicles or spikes, containing small, inconspicuous greenish flowers. The fruit consists of a small utricle enclosed by fleshy, bladder-like perianth segments. These morphological traits help the plant adapt to the arid environments of Central Asian steppes.
