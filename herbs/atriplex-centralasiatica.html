Atriplex centralasiatica

What's the taxonomical classification of Atriplex centralasiatica?

Atriplex centralasiatica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further classified under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Amaranthaceae, which places it within the genus Atriplex, eventually reaching its specific identification at the species level as Atriplex centralasiatica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Atriplex centralasiatica has prostrate or ascending perennial herbs characterized by woody bases and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically fleshy with entire or slightly wavy margins. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a mealy or scurfy texture due to the presence of specialized bladder cells. Its inflorescences are arranged in compact panicles or spikes, containing small, inconspicuous greenish flowers. The fruit consists of a small utricle enclosed by fleshy, bladder-like perianth segments. These morphological traits help the plant adapt to the arid environments of Central Asian steppes.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the medicinal compounds beta-sitosterol, isoorientin, isorhamnetin, quercetin-7-O-alpha-L-rhamnoside, and tricin. These specific bioactive molecules contribute to the plant's diverse therapeutic profile. Many of these substances belong to the flavonoid group, which is known for providing antioxidant support to cells. The presence of sterols like beta-sitosterol further enhances the chemical complexity of the plant's extract. Together, these compounds work through various biological pathways to offer potential health benefits.

    As discussed in a study, the chemical constituents in the seeds of Atriplex centralasiatica were isolated using solvent extractions and column chromatography. Six specific compounds were identified through physicochemical constants and spectral data. These isolated compounds include isorhampetin(I), tricin(II), querceetin-7-O-alpha-L-rhamnoside(III), isoorientin(IV), beta-sitosterol(V), and beta-daucosterlo(VI).
