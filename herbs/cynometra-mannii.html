What's the taxonomical classification of Cynometra mannii?

Cynometra mannii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae, a group well-known for its diverse range of legumes. Finally, it is identified by the genus Cynometra and is specifically designated as the species mannii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynometra mannii has a woody structure characterized by a medium to large tree habit with an erect trunk and spreading crown. The leaves are alternate and compound, consisting of several pairs of leaflets that are typically elliptic or oblong in shape. The margins of the leaflets are entire, and the venation is distinct on the underside of the foliage. Small, fragrant flowers are produced in clusters, eventually developing into woody pods that contain the seeds.
