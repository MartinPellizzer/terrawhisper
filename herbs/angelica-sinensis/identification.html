Botanical Identification of Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis)

To identify Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Chinese Angelica, or Angelica sinensis, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Apiaceae, genus Angelica, and species Angelica sinensis. It is a member of the carrot family and is native to China.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chinese Angelica is Angelica sinensis, first described by the Swedish botanist Carl Peter Thunberg in 1794. It is also known by several synonyms, including Angelica sinensis var. tangutica and Dagasteris tatarica. Common names include Chinese angelica, dong quai, and female herb, while regional and historical names reflect its traditional use in Chinese medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Angelica is a biennial herb with a thick, fleshy taproot. It has a straight, hollow stem that is typically reddish-brown. The leaves are large, pinnately compound, and have a strong aroma. The inflorescence is a terminal umbel, with small, greenish-yellow flowers. The fruit is a spherical, two-seed burr, and the seeds are small, black, and covered in fine hairs.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chinese Angelica can be distinguished by its thick, dark brown roots with a strong aroma, large bipinnate leaves with serrated margins, and clustered purple flowers. It differs from similar species like Angelica pubescens by its more robust root system and the presence of a distinct, aromatic resinous substance in the root tissue.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chinese Angelica is native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in Sichuan, Yunnan, and Qinghai. It thrives in temperate climates with cool summers and cold winters. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at elevations between 1,500 and 3,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chinese Angelica typically blooms from June to August. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around September. Harvesting is usually done in autumn, when the roots are fully mature, making it crucial for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) belongs to the Apiaceae family and is closely related to other Angelica species. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with its genus relatives, such as large leaves and aromatic roots. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other Apiaceae plants, aiding in accurate botanical identification.
