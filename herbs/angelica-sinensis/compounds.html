Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) Compounds

The primary compounds of Chinese Angelica include essential oil, coumarin, and flavonoids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Glycosides and terpenoids also play roles in its therapeutic actions, while saponins and tannins support digestive health. Alkaloids and anthocyanins add to its diverse pharmacological profile.

Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis). It contributes to the herb's aromatic properties and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The oil is extracted from the roots and is used in traditional medicine to support blood circulation and alleviate pain.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting blood circulation and reducing pain. Coumarin also exhibits anticoagulant properties, supporting its role in herbal formulations for cardiovascular health.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Angelica sinensis, a traditional Chinese medicinal plant. They contribute to the herb's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its use in treating conditions like menstrual disorders and cardiovascular issues. These compounds are part of the complex pharmacological profile of Angelica sinensis.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological effects, including anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. Glycosides in Angelica sinensis are often extracted and used in traditional medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating menstrual disorders and enhancing blood circulation. This compound is also believed to aid in immune modulation and tissue repair.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Angelica sinensis, a key medicinal plant in traditional Chinese medicine. These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, playing a significant role in its therapeutic applications. They are also responsible for some of the plant's aromatic and pharmacological effects.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis). It contributes to the herb's anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. Saponins help enhance the therapeutic effects of Chinese Angelica in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis). It contributes to the herb's astringent properties and may aid in reducing inflammation and supporting digestive health. Tannins are also known for their antioxidant effects, which can help protect cells from damage.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a class of compounds found in Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis). These alkaloids contribute to the plant's pharmacological effects, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. They are among the key active constituents responsible for the traditional therapeutic uses of this herb.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) that contributes to its medicinal properties. It exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting cardiovascular and neurological health. This compound is valued for its role in enhancing the therapeutic benefits of the herb.
