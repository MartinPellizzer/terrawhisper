10 Medicinal Constituents Of Angelica Sinensis (Dong Quai)

Angelica sinensis has active constituents such as ferulic acid, lignans, and volatile oils.

These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and vasodilatory effects. The constituents in dong quai help to improve blood circulation, relieve menstrual cramps and symptoms of menopause, and alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma. Additionally, ferulic acid has antioxidant properties that may protect against cancer and cardiovascular disease.

By harnessing these beneficial compounds, dong quai has been used for centuries in traditional Chinese medicine to promote overall health and wellbeing.

1. Ferulic acid

Angelica sinensis ferulic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from its roots.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Ferulic acid has been shown to exhibit potent free radical-scavenging activity, making it effective against various diseases, such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Additionally, it may also help regulate blood sugar levels and improve immune function.

2. Ligustilide

Angelica sinensis ligustilide is a bioactive compound extracted from its essential oil.

It has been extensively studied for its numerous pharmacological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antispasmodic properties.

Ligustilide has been shown to exhibit potent inhibitory effects on various enzymes and proteins involved in inflammation and oxidative stress, making it a potential therapeutic agent for the treatment of various diseases, such as cardiovascular disorders and inflammatory conditions.

3. Butylidenephthalide

Angelica sinensis butylidenephthalide is a bioactive compound isolated from the roots of Angelica sinensis, a plant commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine.

This sesquiterpene lactone has been shown to possess various pharmacological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and cardiovascular protective effects.

It has also been reported to exhibit analgesic and antipyretic properties, making it a valuable compound for the development of novel therapeutic agents.

4. Sesquiterpenes

Angelica sinensis sesquiterpenes is a type of bioactive compound found in the plant.

These compounds are responsible for its medicinal properties and have been traditionally used to treat various women's health issues such as menstrual cramps, PMS symptoms, and menopause-related hot flashes.

Sesquiterpenes in Angelica sinensis have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and estrogenic activities, making them a valuable component of traditional Chinese medicine.

5. Angelicin

Angelica sinensis angelicin is a bioactive compound extracted from the roots of the plant.

It has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat various women's health issues, such as menstrual cramps, irregular periods, and menopause symptoms.

Angelicin has been shown to have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and estrogen-like properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal supplements and teas aimed at promoting female reproductive health and well-being.

6. Cumarins

Angelica sinensis cumarins is a group of natural compounds found in the plant.

These bioactive molecules have been extensively studied for their potential health benefits, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.

Cumarins are responsible for the plant's anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and vasodilatory properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal remedies for women's health issues, such as menstrual cramps, menopause symptoms, and infertility.

7. Phenolic acids

Angelica sinensis phenolic acids is a type of compound that is found in high concentration in the root of this plant.

These compounds have been shown to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties.

Phenolic acids in Angelica sinensis are believed to contribute to its traditional uses in Chinese medicine for treating menstrual disorders, cardiovascular disease, and other health conditions.

8. Flavonoids

Angelica sinensis flavonoids is a class of compounds that are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

These flavonoids include vitexin, orientin, and isorhapontigenin, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and estrogenic activities.

They may help alleviate menopausal symptoms, improve cardiovascular health, and protect against cancer and neurodegenerative diseases.

9. Terpenes

Angelica sinensis terpenes is a complex mixture of compounds found in the roots and rhizomes of the plant.

These terpenes are responsible for its distinctive aroma and flavor, and have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries to treat various health conditions, including menstrual cramps, anxiety, and insomnia.

The terpenes in dong quai have also been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a popular ingredient in modern skincare products.

10. Sesquiterpene lactones

Angelica sinensis sesquiterpene lactones is a type of bioactive compound extracted from the root of this traditional Chinese herb.

These compounds have been found to exhibit numerous biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects.

They have also shown potential in the prevention and treatment of various diseases, such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.
