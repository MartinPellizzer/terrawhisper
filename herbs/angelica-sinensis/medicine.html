What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Angelica Sinensis (Dong Quai)?

Angelica sinensis has health benefits such as regulating menstrual cycles and alleviating symptoms of menopause.

The herb contains medicinal constituents like ferulic acid, ligustilide, and senkyunolide, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Dong quai is often prepared in teas, capsules, or tablets to promote blood circulation and alleviate pain associated with gynecological disorders.

However, it may cause allergic reactions, interact with blood thinners, and worsen bleeding conditions, so it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using dong quai and to follow the recommended dosage.

What are the health benefits of Angelica sinensis?

Angelica sinensis has health benefits such as alleviating menstrual cramps and heavy bleeding in women, due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

It is also effective in reducing fever and treating various respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma.

Additionally, dong quai has been used traditionally to lower blood pressure and cholesterol levels, making it a potential natural remedy for cardiovascular health.

What are the active constituents of Angelica sinensis?

The health benefits of Angelica sinensis comes from its active constituents, such as volatile oils like angelic acid and ligustilide, which possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The plant's flavonoids, including isoorientin and vicenin, exhibit anti-cancer and anti-aging effects.

Additionally, the saponins present in dong quai have been shown to improve circulation and alleviate menopausal symptoms, making it a popular herbal remedy for women's health issues.

What are the medicinal preparations of Angelica sinensis?

Angelica sinensis has different medicinal preparations, such as dried roots, decoctions, tablets, capsules, and topical creams.

Dried roots are often used to make teas or infusions for menstrual relief and menopausal symptoms. Decoctions are made by boiling the roots in water and are used to treat digestive issues and fever. Tablets and capsules contain powdered dong quai root and are taken orally to alleviate symptoms of menstruation, menopause, and cardiovascular disease.

Topical creams containing dong quai extracts are applied directly to the skin to treat skin conditions such as eczema and dermatitis.

What are the possible side effect of using Angelica sinensis improperly?

Improper use of Angelica sinensis increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, stomach upset, and diarrhea.

Prolonged use can lead to increased risk of bleeding due to its anticoagulant properties, as well as interactions with blood thinners and other medications. Additionally, excessive consumption may cause headaches, dizziness, and fatigue.

In some cases, dong quai has been linked to liver damage, kidney problems, and even seizures in rare instances.

What precautions to take when using Angelica sinensis medicinally?

Before using Angelica sinensis for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and duration of treatment.

Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using it due to its potential effects on fetal development or infant growth.

Elderly individuals may also need to use it under close medical supervision.
