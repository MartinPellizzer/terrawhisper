What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Angelica Sinensis (Dong Quai)?

Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, has health benefits such as alleviating menstrual cramps and relieving symptoms of menopause.

Its medicinal constituents include ferulic acid, isoflavones, and ligustilide, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Preparations of dong quai include capsules, tablets, and teas, which are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Side effects may include gastrointestinal upset, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using dong quai, particularly for individuals taking blood thinners or with bleeding disorders.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Angelica sinensis.

What are the health benefits of Angelica sinensis?

Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, has health benefits such as reducing menstrual cramps and PMS symptoms, as well as alleviating menopausal symptoms like hot flashes and night sweats.

Its anti-inflammatory properties may also help to alleviate joint pain and swelling.

Additionally, dong quai is believed to have antioxidant and anti-aging properties, which may help to protect against oxidative stress and promote overall well-being.

What are the active constituents of Angelica sinensis?

Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, has active constituents such as ferulic acid, ligustilide, and butylphthalide, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Ferulic acid exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, while ligustilide has been shown to possess antiplatelet and anti-inflammatory activities. Butylphthalide, a sesquiterpene lactone, has been found to possess vasodilatory and anti-inflammatory properties.

These constituents contribute to dong quai's traditional use in treating menstrual disorders, menopausal symptoms, and cardiovascular diseases.

What are the medicinal preparations of Angelica sinensis?

Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, has medicinal preparations such as capsules, tablets, and teas.

The root of the plant is traditionally used to make these preparations. They are often standardized to contain specific amounts of ferulic acid and ligustilide, which are believed to be responsible for the plant's medicinal properties.

Dong quai is used to treat various conditions, including menopausal symptoms, menstrual cramps, and digestive problems.

What are the possible side effect of using Angelica sinensis improperly?

Improper use of Angelica sinensis, also known as dong quai, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as dizziness, stomach upset, and diarrhea.

Long-term or excessive use can cause interactions with blood thinners and other medications, leading to increased bleeding risks.

In rare cases, dong quai may cause more severe side effects, including liver damage and kidney problems, particularly when combined with other herbal supplements.
