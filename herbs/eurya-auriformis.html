Eurya auriformis

What's the taxonomical classification of Eurya auriformis?

Eurya auriformis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Ericales, the plant is categorized under the family Pentaphylacaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Eurya, with the unique species designation auriformis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eurya auriformis has small, elliptical to obovate leaves that are typically 5 to 20 millimeters long, featuring entire margins and a coriaceous texture. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems and exhibit a glossy, dark green upper surface. The undersides of the foliage often display a paler coloration compared to the tops. Its flowers are minute and appear in axillary clusters, characterized by small white petals. The plant also develops small, fleshy berries that turn red or black upon ripening.
