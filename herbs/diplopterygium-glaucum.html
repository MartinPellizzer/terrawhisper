Diplopterygium glaucum

What's the taxonomical classification of Diplopterygium glaucum?

Diplopterygium glaucum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Gleicheniales and the family Gleicheniaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Diplopterygium, and its specific species name, glaucum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diplopterygium glaucum has woody, climbing stems that produce opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves characterized by a distinctively glaucous, blue-gray waxy coating on the surface. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly wavy, and the venation is somewhat obscure on the upper side. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that transition in color as they mature. These morphological features help distinguish this climbing shrub within its specific forest habitat.
