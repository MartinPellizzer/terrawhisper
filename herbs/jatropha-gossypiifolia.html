Jatropha gossypiifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Jatropha gossypiifolia?

Jatrophia gossypiifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Jatropha and the species gossypiifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Jatrophia gossypiifolia contains bioactive alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and diterpenes that provide various therapeutic properties. The plant's chemical profile includes specific compounds like jatrophine which contribute to its pharmacological activities. These constituents are often utilized in traditional medicine to treat inflammation, skin infections, and digestive issues. Research indicates that these phytochemicals may also possess antimicrobial and antioxidant capabilities. The specific concentration of these compounds can vary significantly depending on the plant part used and its growing environment.

What are the therapeutic actions of Jatropha gossypiifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, digestive ailments, inflammation, and various types of pain. Its leaves are frequently applied topically to soothe wounds, eczema, and insect bites due to their antimicrobial properties. In traditional medicine, decoctions made from the plant are consumed to manage intestinal parasites and dysentery. The various parts of the species also serve as remedies for respiratory issues and fever. Practitioners often utilize its bioactive compounds to mitigate swelling and localized discomfort.
