Common Goldenrod (Solidago occidentalis)

Common Goldenrod (Solidago occidentalis) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to North America, Western United States, and Rocky Mountains. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and roots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface yellow-green and lower surface gray-green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along midrib.
Flowers
Racemed inflorescence with actinomorphic flowers having yellow petals and yellow disk florets arranged in two series, with 5 ray florets per flower and 10 to 15 disk florets per flower.
Stems
Erect, branched, herbaceous stems with opposite leaf arrangement, glabrous surface, and nodes bearing adventitious root hairs.
