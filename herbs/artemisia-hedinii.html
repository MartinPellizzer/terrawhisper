What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia hedinii?

Artemisia hedinii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. As a member of the widely recognized family Asteraceae, it is placed within the genus Artemisia, ultimately reaching its specific taxonomic designation as the species hedinii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia hedinii has a perennial growth habit characterized by woody bases and deeply pinnatisect leaves that are silvery-gray due to dense, fine hairs. These inflorescences are typically composed of numerous tiny, tubular florets that lack prominent petals. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of central and western China, specifically within the provinces of Qinghai, Gansu, Sichuan, and Tibet. It thrives primarily in alpine meadows and rocky slopes found at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters. The distribution is closely tied to the cool, semi-arid climates characteristic of the Tibetan Plateau.
