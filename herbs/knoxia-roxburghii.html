Knoxia roxburghii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Knoxia roxburghii has a woody, perennial shrubby habit characterized by slender, erect stems and simple, opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The leaf blades typically possess acute or acuminate tips and a slightly pubescent texture on both surfaces. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in terminal or axillary cymes, displaying a tubular structure typical of the Rubiaceae family. The fruit develops into a small, dry capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species within its natural forest habitats.
