Medicinal Parts

Ipomoea tricolor leaf is one of the most commonly used medicinal parts of this plant, known for its vibrant blue and purple flowers and its traditional applications in herbal medicine.

The leaves are often harvested during the early stages of growth when they contain the highest concentration of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and alkaloids, which are believed to contribute to its therapeutic properties. In traditional medicine, the leaves are used to treat ailments such as fever, inflammation, and digestive disorders due to their anti-inflammatory and antipyretic effects. Additionally, the leaves are sometimes prepared as a tea or applied topically to soothe skin irritations and reduce swelling.

While the flowers and seeds of Ipomoea tricolor are also used in some medicinal preparations, the leaves remain a primary component in many formulations due to their versatile healing properties and widespread availability.
