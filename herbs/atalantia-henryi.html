Atalantia henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Atalantia henryi has small, pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of elliptic to oblong leaflets that feature entire margins and acute tips. The plant typically exhibits a shrubby or small tree growth habit with woody stems. Its flowers are generally small and white, often appearing in axillary clusters. The fruit is a small, globose berry that matures to a yellowish or green hue. These botanical characteristics are typical of many species within the Rutaceae family.
