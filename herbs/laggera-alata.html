Laggera alata

What's the taxonomical classification of Laggera alata?

Laggera alata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. Its specific taxonomic identification is completed through the genus Laggera and the species name alata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Laggera alatata contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins that provide its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents work together to exhibit significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities when processed for medicinal use. Traditional practitioners often utilize the plant's extracts to treat various skin ailments and inflammatory conditions. The presence of specific phenolic compounds further supports its potential role in managing oxidative stress within biological systems. Ongoing phytochemical research seeks to isolate these individual compounds to better understand their precise pharmacological mechanisms.

What are the therapeutic actions of Laggera alata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, boils, abscesses, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves are often crushed or decocted to serve as a topical poultice for healing sores. It is also frequently applied to insect bites and stings to reduce itching and swelling. Some traditional practices involve using the plant to alleviate symptoms of rheumatism and joint pain. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's natural antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
