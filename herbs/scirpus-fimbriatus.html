What is Scirpus fimbriatus?

Scirpus fimbriatus is a perennial plant that belongs to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a tussock-forming sedge with a stout stem, 0.5-1.5 meters tall, and leaves that are flat, linear, and 2-6 millimeters wide, having a prominent midrib and a fringe of hairs along the margin. The plant produces brownish-purple spikes, 3-10 centimeters long, with 4-8 spikelets per node, and the spikelets are 3-6 millimeters long, with 3-5 flowers. The fruit is a trigonous achene, 2-3 millimeters long, and is dispersed by wind.

Scirpus fimbriatus is native to the southeastern United States and is typically found in wet environments, such as marshes and swamps.
