Common Jasmine (Jasminum Fragrans)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Jasminum fragrans.

Jasminum fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant jasmine, has been traditionally used in various medicinal practices across different cultures for its therapeutic properties. The plant contains essential oils and bioactive compounds that exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects, making it valuable in treating skin infections and respiratory ailments. In Ayurvedic medicine, jasmine is often used to alleviate stress and anxiety due to its calming aromatic properties. It is also incorporated into herbal remedies to promote wound healing and improve circulation. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using jasmine for medicinal purposes to ensure safe and effective application.
