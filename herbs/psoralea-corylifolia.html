Corylifolia Psoralea

Psoralea corylifolia

Corylifolia Psoralea (Psoralea corylifolia) is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions, known for its use in traditional medicine to improve bone health and treat conditions such as osteoporosis and arthritis.

Botanical Identification

Psoralea corylifolia, also known as Babool, belongs to the legume family Fabaceae. It is a deciduous shrub native to southern Asia. The plant has compound leaves, yellow flowers, and a thick, fleshy root used in traditional medicine. Common synonyms include Indian Bdellium and Kachnar.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Psoralea
  • Species: Psoralea corylifolia

Active Compounds

The primary active compounds in Corylifolia Psoralea include flavonoids, coumarins, and tannins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. These compounds work synergistically to enhance antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Infusion: Used for skin conditions and urinary tract issues.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

Psoralea corylifolia is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in some individuals. Prolonged use or high doses could lead to toxicity, so it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.
