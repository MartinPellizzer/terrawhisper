Bioactive Constituents

Psoralea corylifolia psoralen is a key bioactive compound found in the herb, known for its phototoxic properties and ability to enhance the effects of ultraviolet light.

This compound is part of a broader group of substances called furocoumarins, which include bergapten, isoimperatorin, and various coumarins. These compounds are widely studied for their potential therapeutic applications, particularly in the treatment of skin conditions and immune-related disorders.

Psoralen, along with bergapten, has been used in phototherapy to treat psoriasis and vitiligo by promoting melanin synthesis and modulating immune responses. Additionally, isoimperatorin and other coumarins exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, contributing to the overall medicinal value of Psoralea corylifolia.

These constituents work synergistically to provide a range of health benefits, making the herb a valuable component in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

Medicinal Parts

Psoralea corylifolia seed, also known as *Bai Jia Shen*, is one of the most significant medicinal parts of this herb, widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its warming and tonifying properties.

The seeds are rich in alkaloids, flavonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to their therapeutic effects, including enhancing kidney function and promoting bone health. In traditional practices, they are often used to treat conditions such as osteoporosis, rheumatism, and male infertility due to their ability to improve circulation and strengthen the body's yang energy. The seeds are typically processed through methods like honey-frying or stir-frying to enhance their medicinal potency and reduce potential toxicity.

Additionally, the seed is believed to support reproductive health and is often incorporated into herbal formulations aimed at improving vitality and longevity.
