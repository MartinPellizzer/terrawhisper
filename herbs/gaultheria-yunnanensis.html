Gaultheria yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Gaultheria yunnanensis?

Gaultheria yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further belongs to the order Ericales and is a member of the family Ericaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified under the genus Gaultheria, with its unique designation being the species yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gaultheria yunnanensis has evergreen, opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically 3 to 7 centimeters long with slightly serrated margins and a leathery texture. The plant features woody stems that form a low-growing shrubby habit suitable for montane environments. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying white or pale pink hues. Following pollination, the plant produces fleshy, globose berries that turn a deep red or purple color when mature. These berries are a defining characteristic of the genus and serve as a vital food source for local fauna.

Its distribution is closely tied to specific elevations where organic-rich soil and consistent humidity are present. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds gaultheroside A, methyl salicylate, (+)-lyoniresinol-2a-O-beta-D-glucoxylopyranoside, (-)-8'-methoxyisolariciresinol-2a-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside, and (-)-isolariciresinol-2a-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse therapeutic potential found within the species. Methyl salicylate is particularly noted for its role as a natural analgesic and anti-inflammatory agent. The various glycosylated lignans also play significant roles in the plant's chemical profile. Research into these constituents helps scientists understand the complex biological activities of Gaultheria yunnanensis.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", the roots of Gaultheria yunnanensis contain 4 lignan glycosides. These medicinal compounds are identified as D1: gaultheroide A, D2: (-)-8'-methoxyisolariciresinol-2a-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside, D3: (-)-isolariciresinol-2a-O-beta-D-xylopyranside, and D4: (+)-lyoniresinol-2a-O-beta-D-glucoxylopyranoside. The study utilized Rp-HPLC to separate and analyze these specific components within the plant. This method was used to determine the retention times for all 4 lignan glycosides.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, or potential salicylate toxicity if consumed in large quantities. Because it contains methyl salicylate, similar to aspirin, it may trigger stomach irritation or bleeding in sensitive individuals. People with aspirin allergies or those taking blood-thinning medications should exercise extreme caution when handling or ingesting it. Contact with the plant may also lead to localized redness or itching for those with hypersensitivity. Monitoring for signs of nausea or dizziness is essential when exploring its medicinal properties.
