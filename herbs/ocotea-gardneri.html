What is Ocotea gardneri?

Ocotea gardneri is an evergreen tree endemic to the subtropical forests of Argentina and Chile, belonging to the family Lauraceae.

Its botanical characteristics include a height of up to 40 meters, with a straight trunk diameter of 1-2 meters, and a smooth, grayish-brown bark that exfoliates in thin scales. The leaves are elliptical, measuring 6-18 centimeters long and 2-6 centimeters wide, with a leathery texture and a glossy, dark green color. The flowers are greenish-yellow, arranged in axillary corymbs, and produce a small, globose berry containing 1-2 seeds.

Ocotea gardneri is a member of the Ocotea genus and shares similarities with other species within the Lauraceae family.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Ocotea gardneri are tincture, tea, Ocotea tea, essential oil, and Ocotea tincture.

These preparations are made from the bark of the Ocotea gardneri tree, which is native to South America. A tincture is a concentrated extract made by soaking the bark in a solvent such as ethanol, while tea is made by infusing the bark in hot water. Essential oil is obtained through steam distillation, which captures the volatile compounds of the bark.

Ocotea tea and Ocotea tincture are specifically prepared to contain high concentrations of alpha-asarone, a compound that has been studied for its potential health benefits.
