Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) Compounds

The primary compounds in Common Agrimony include tannins, which have astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while glycosides and saponins support digestive health. Coumarin and terpenoids add to its aromatic profile, and essential oils enhance its therapeutic potential.

Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used to treat digestive issues and inflammation. Tannins help reduce bleeding and soothe irritated tissues, making them valuable in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are responsible for some of the plant's traditional uses in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. Flavonoids are widely studied for their potential therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in digestive and skin-related ailments. This compound helps modulate gut health and may aid in wound healing.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Glycosides help in reducing swelling and tightening tissues, making Common Agrimony useful in treating digestive and skin conditions.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa). It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and mild sedative properties. Coumarin also has anticoagulant effects, though it should be used cautiously in individuals on blood-thinning medications.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and digestive properties. Saponins help reduce irritation and support gut health, making them valuable in herbal treatments for digestive disorders.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and skin conditions. They are responsible for some of the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa) contains volatile compounds like cineole and camphor. These components contribute to its aromatic properties and may support digestive and antimicrobial functions. The oil is often used in herbal preparations for its soothing and cleansing effects.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting skin health and reducing inflammation. Found in the flowers and leaves, anthocyanin also aids in protecting cells from oxidative stress.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Common Agrimony (Agrimonia pilosa). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though specific alkaloids and their roles in traditional herbal use require further research. Its presence may support the plant's use in digestive and anti-inflammatory applications.
