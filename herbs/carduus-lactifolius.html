Milk Thistle (Carduus Lactifolius)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Carduus lactifolius.

Carduus lactifolius, commonly known as the milk thistle, has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in supporting liver health. The plant contains a group of compounds called silymarin, which is believed to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. It is often used to treat liver conditions such as cirrhosis, hepatitis, and toxic liver damage due to its potential to protect liver cells from damage. Additionally, some studies suggest that silymarin may help in the detoxification process and promote the regeneration of liver tissue. While more research is needed to confirm its full efficacy, milk thistle remains a popular herbal remedy in both traditional and complementary medicine practices.
