Aconitum polyschistum

What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum polyschistum?

Aconitum polyschistum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. It is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, which is further subdivided into the genus Aconitum, and is specifically identified by its species name, polyschistum.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Aconitum polyschisum targets voltage-gated sodium channels, various inflammatory cytokines, and specific enzymes involved in the arachidonic acid cascade. The primary bioactive alkaloids in this plant, such as aconitine derivatives, modulate sodium channel conductance to exert potent analgesic and antiarrhythmic effects. Furthermore, its phytochemical constituents inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. These multi-target interactions facilitate its traditional uses in treating inflammation, pain, and certain infectious diseases.
