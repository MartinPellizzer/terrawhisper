What's the taxonomical classification of Amorphophallus dunnii?

Amorphophalus dunnii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic path continues through the order Alismatales into the family Araceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Amorphophalus, and its unique species designation, dunnii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amorphophallus dunnii has a large underground corm that produces a single, massive unifoliate leaf consisting of a long petiole and a complexly divided leaf blade. The petiole is thick and fleshy, functioning as a primary storage organ for nutrients. The leaf blade features multiple leaflets that are deeply lobed or pinnately divided to increase surface area. During its flowering stage, the plant produces a large, fleshy spadix surrounded by a spathe that often emits a strong odor.
