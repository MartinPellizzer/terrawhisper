Amorphophallus dunnii

What's the taxonomical classification of Amorphophallus dunnii?

Amorphophalus dunnii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic path continues through the order Alismatales into the family Araceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Amorphophalus, and its unique species designation, dunnii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amorphophallus dunnii has a large underground corm that produces a single, massive unifoliate leaf consisting of a long petiole and a complexly divided leaf blade.

The petiole is thick and fleshy, functioning as a primary storage organ for nutrients. The leaf blade features multiple leaflets that are deeply lobed or pinnately divided to increase surface area. During its flowering stage, the plant produces a large, fleshy spadix surrounded by a spathe that often emits a strong odor.

This morphology is characteristic of the Araceae family and is adapted for efficient nutrient storage and specialized pollination strategies.

        What are the herbal preparations of Amorphophallus dunnii?

        Amorphophallus dunnii is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a crushed paste for medicinal use. The tubers are typically harvested and dried to facilitate the creation of fine powders. These powders are often mixed with water or other liquids to create a drinkable medicinal solution. In some traditional practices, the fresh tissue is crushed into a paste to be applied directly to the skin. This variety of preparation methods allows the plant's properties to be administered both internally and externally.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Amorphophallus dunnii can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if the plant parts are ingested. Like many members of its genus, this plant contains calcium oxalate crystals that act as microscopic needles upon contact with sensitive tissues. These crystals can trigger intense burning sensations, swelling, and redness in the mouth or on the skin. If swallowed, the substance may lead to vomiting, abdominal pain, or inflammation of the throat. Careful handling is necessary to prevent accidental contact with the eyes or mucous membranes.
