Amorphophallus dunnii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amorphophallus dunnii has a large underground corm that produces a single, massive unifoliate leaf consisting of a long petiole and a complexly divided leaf blade. The petiole is thick and fleshy, functioning as a primary storage organ for nutrients. The leaf blade features multiple leaflets that are deeply lobed or pinnately divided to increase surface area. During its flowering stage, the plant produces a large, fleshy spadix surrounded by a spathe that often emits a strong odor. This morphology is characteristic of the Araceae family and is adapted for efficient nutrient storage and specialized pollination strategies.

        What side effects this plant can have?
