Achillea acuminata

What's the taxonomical classification of Achillea acuminata?

Achillea acuminata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. It is further categorized under the genus Achillea, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species acuminata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Achillea acuminata has perennial, herbaceous stems that grow upright and are accompanied by finely dissected, pinnatifid leaves arranged in a basal rosette and alternately along the stem. The foliage is characterized by narrow, linear segments that give the plant a feathery or fern-like appearance. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, white, discoid flower heads clustered into dense, flat-topped corymbs. Each individual flower head features tubular florets, often lacking prominent ray petals typical of other species in the genus. The root system is typically composed of creeping rhizomes that allow the plant to spread through its habitat.
