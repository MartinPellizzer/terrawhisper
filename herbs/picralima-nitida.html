What's the taxonomical classification of Picralima nitida?

Picralima nitida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Picralima, and its unique species designation, nitida.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal alkaloids akuammicine, akuammidine, akuammigine, akuammine, and pseudoakuammigine. These specific indole alkaloids are characteristic components of the plant's chemical profile. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential biological activities and pharmacological properties. The presence of such diverse compounds suggests a complex chemical defense mechanism within the vegetation. Understanding the precise concentrations of these alkaloids is essential for further scientific investigation.

As discussed in a study, extracts of the seeds of Picralima nitida have been reported to have opioid analgesic activity involving five alkaloids. Akuammidine showed a preference for mu-opioid binding sites with Ki values of 0.6, 2.4 and 8.6 microM at mu-, delta- and kappa-opioid binding sites, respectively. Akuammine showed the highest affinity for mu-opioid binding sites with a Ki of 0.5 microM and acted as an antagonist with a pK(B) of 5.7. Akuammicine possesses the highest affinity for kappa-opioid binding sites with a Ki of 0.2 microM, acting as a full agonist in the guinea pig ileum and a partial agonist in the mouse and rabbit vasa deferentia. Akuammigine and pseudoakuammigine showed little or no efficacy, and none of the alkaloids had significant activity for ORL1-binding sites with Ki values >> 10 microM.
