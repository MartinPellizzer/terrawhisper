Begonia leprosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia leprosa?

Begonia leprosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Cucurbitales, it is situated in the family Begoniaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Begonia and is specifically designated as the species leprosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia leprosa has asymmetrical, ovate leaves that are characterized by a distinctively coarse, scaly, and lepidote texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. The leaf margins are irregularly serrated or crenate, contributing to its rugged appearance. These leaves are supported by fleshy, succulent stems that often exhibit a reddish or brownish pigmentation. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in a typical begonia fashion. The plant's overall morphology is defined by this rough, sandpaper-like tactile quality across its vegetative structures.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight, high humidity levels above sixty percent, and consistently moist but well-draining soil to thrive.
