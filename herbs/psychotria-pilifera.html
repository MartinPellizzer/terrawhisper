Psychotria pilifera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Psychotria pilifera has woody stems characterized by a dense covering of fine, spreading hairs that give the plant a distinctly pilose texture. The leaves are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape with entire margins and an acute apex. These foliage structures are arranged oppositely along the branches and possess a prominent midrib. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often featuring tubular corollas. The fruit develops into a small fleshy berry that facilitates seed dispersal within its native tropical habitat.
