Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum) Compounds

The primary compounds of Chinese Prickly Ash include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Alkaloids and phenolic acids contribute to its medicinal and antimicrobial activities. Essential oils and coumarin provide aromatic and anti-inflammatory benefits, while terpenoids, glycosides, saponins, and anthocyanins support various therapeutic effects.

Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, playing a key role in its traditional use for treating ailments like pain and digestive issues. These compounds are often isolated and studied for their therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins are found in the bark and fruits, supporting traditional uses in treating digestive and skin conditions.

3. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum) are bioactive compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating pain and digestive disorders. These alkaloids are extracted from the bark and fruits, supporting their role in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating conditions like pain and digestive disorders.

5. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like limonene and α-pinene, which contribute to its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties. This oil is commonly used in traditional medicine for treating pain and digestive issues.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Found in the bark and fruit, coumarin is used in traditional medicine to treat pain and inflammatory conditions. Its chemical structure also supports its role as a natural anticoagulant.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for treating pain and digestive issues. These compounds are often extracted and utilized in herbal formulations.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and pain. Glycosides in this plant are often responsible for its bitter taste and therapeutic effects. They are typically extracted and used in herbal formulations.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum). It exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments. Saponin also aids in enhancing the absorption of other bioactive components in the herb.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Chinese Prickly Ash (Zanthoxylum piperitum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting health and treating inflammatory conditions.
