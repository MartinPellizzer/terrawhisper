Herrania purpurea

Herrania purpurea, commonly known as the "Bastard Cupana" or "Bastard Cocoa", is a plant species native to the Amazon rainforest.

This plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, cough, and respiratory issues, due to its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

The therapeutic actions of Herrania purpurea are attributed to its ability to stimulate digestion, reduce fever, and act as an expectorant, making it useful in treating respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma.

The bioactive constituents of Herrania purpurea include alkaloids, phenolic compounds, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
