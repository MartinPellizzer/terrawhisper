Periwinkle

Vinca minor

Botanical Identification

Vinca minor, commonly known as periwinkle, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and blue-violet flowers. The plant has a trailing habit with woody stems and is sometimes referred to as myrtle or wood-sorrel in historical herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Periwinkle contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides. These compounds work together to provide its medicinal effects, with alkaloids playing a central role in its therapeutic properties. Flavonoids and glycosides may enhance its overall efficacy through synergistic interactions.

  • Alkaloid
  • Flavonoid
  • Glycoside

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for treating ailments like inflammation and pain due to its alkaloid content.

Tincture: Used for addressing symptoms like anxiety and insomnia due to its calming properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments due to its mild stimulant properties.

Safety Profile

Periwinkle, or Vinca minor, contains alkaloids that can be toxic in high doses. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential harm to the fetus or infant. Caution is advised for individuals with kidney disease or those taking medications that affect the heart.
