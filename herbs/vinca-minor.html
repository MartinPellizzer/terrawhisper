Periwinkle (Vinca minor): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) is a low-growing evergreen perennial plant native to Europe, known for its bright blue or purple flowers and its long history of use in traditional medicine. It is primarily used to support cardiovascular health and has been shown to improve conditions such as high blood pressure and irregular heartbeats.

What is Periwinkle (Vinca Minor)?

Periwinkle, or Vinca minor, is a low-growing evergreen perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and clusters of blue-violet flowers, commonly used in herbal medicine for its medicinal properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Periwinkle, or Vinca minor, is a low-growing evergreen perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, belonging to the family Apocynaceae and characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and clusters of blue-violet flowers.

Common Names and Synonyms

Periwinkle, also known as Vinca minor, is a low-growing evergreen perennial plant commonly referred to by other names such as small periwinkle, ground ivy, and myrtle, though these latter names are sometimes used for different species.

Plant Description and Morphology

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) is a low-growing evergreen perennial with spreading, trailing stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage and producing clusters of blue-violet flowers in late spring.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) is native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, typically growing in shaded, moist woodlands and rocky soils.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves, stems, and roots of Periwinkle (Vinca minor) are the primary plant parts used medicinally.

Phytochemical Composition

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) contains alkaloids such as vincamine, vincamine, and vinca alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and are primarily concentrated in the leaves and stems.

Pharmacological Properties

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) contains alkaloids such as vincamine and vincamine alkaloids that exhibit cholinesterase inhibitory activity, vasodilatory effects, and potential neuroprotective properties, making it relevant in traditional and modern herbal medicine for cognitive and circulatory support.

Mechanisms of Action

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) exerts its pharmacological effects primarily through the alkaloids vincaisoine and vincamine, which enhance cerebral circulation by dilating blood vessels and improving neurovascular function.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Periwinkle is used to treat conditions such as hypertension, diabetes, leukemia, and various skin disorders.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) is commonly prepared as a tincture, infusion, or dried herb for use in herbal formulations, with the specific method depending on the intended application and traditional practices.

Dosage and Administration

Dosage and administration of Periwinkle (Vinca minor) should be determined by a qualified healthcare practitioner, as it varies based on the specific preparation, intended use, and individual health conditions.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Periwinkle (Vinca minor) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, skin irritation, and in some cases, allergic reactions, particularly in individuals with a history of sensitivity to related plants.

Drug Interactions

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) may interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants, antidepressants, and drugs that affect the heart, due to its potential to influence blood pressure, heart rate, and neurotransmitter activity.

Toxicity and Precautions

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) contains alkaloids such as vincamine and vinca alkaloids, which can be toxic in high doses and may cause side effects including gastrointestinal distress, dizziness, and in severe cases, cardiac issues, so it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a qualified healthcare professional.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) is typically cultivated in shaded, moist soils with well-drained conditions, preferring partial to full shade and thriving in USDA hardiness zones 4 to 8.

Processing and Extraction

Processing Periwinkle (Vinca minor) involves harvesting the above-ground parts of the plant during early summer when the foliage is most abundant, followed by drying the material in a shaded, well-ventilated area to preserve its active compounds.

Storage and Shelf Life

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, and its shelf life is typically up to two years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Periwinkle (Vinca minor) has primarily focused on its alkaloid content, particularly vincamine, which has shown potential in improving cognitive function and vascular health, though more studies are needed to confirm its efficacy and safety in clinical settings.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Periwinkle (Vinca minor) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to treat wounds, skin conditions, and as a mild sedative, with historical records dating back to ancient Greek and Roman times.

Regulatory Status
