    What is Asplenium mohillense?

    Asplenium mohillense is a species of fern in the family Aspleniaceae.

    It has a terrestrial habit, growing up to 10-40 cm tall, with a rhizome producing short, brown, hairy, and grooved scales. The fronds are lanceolate to elliptical, 2-10 cm long, and 1-5 cm wide, with a long, narrow base and a short, rounded tip, having 20-40 pairs of pinnae and a leathery texture. The plant is diploid, with 22-24 pairs of chromosomes, and is known to reproduce via spores in the summer months.

    The spores are released from the sporangia on the underside of the pinnae.
