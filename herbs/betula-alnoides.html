Betula alnoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Betula alnoides?

Betula alnoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Fagales and the family Betulaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Betula. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique specific epithet, alnoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Betula alnoiides has ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins, slender branches, and small, pendulous catkins. The foliage typically features a bright green color and fine, pointed teeth along the leaf edges. Its bark is characterized by a smooth, greyish texture that may become slightly fissured on older specimens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into winged seeds known as samaras. These structures are essential for wind dispersal across its natural woodland habitat.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the triterpenoids betulon, betulinic acid, lupeol, and ursolic acid, along with various flavonoids that contribute to its medicinal properties. These specific chemical constituents are primarily extracted from the bark and leaves to exploit their diverse biological activities. Betulin and betulinic acid are often studied for their significant anti-inflammatory and potential antitumor effects. The presence of lupeol and ursolic acid adds further value due to their roles in modulating immune responses and protecting cells from oxidative stress. Additionally, the flavonoids within the plant provide antioxidant benefits that support overall cellular health.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Betula alnoides targets cyclooxygenase enzymes, lipoxygenase proteins, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. The plant contains diverse phenolic compounds and flavonoids that inhibit the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. These bioactive molecules act as competitive inhibitors for COX-1 and COX-2, effectively reducing the synthesis of prostaglandins. Additionally, its phytochemical constituents modulate the activity of tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6 to mitigate systemic inflammation. Such multi-target interactions contribute to the plant's observed antioxidant and anti-inflammatory therapeutic potential.

What are the therapeutic actions of Betula alnoides?

Betula alnoides has diuretic, astringent, anti-inflammatory, and tonic properties that make it useful in traditional herbal medicine. It also functions as an anti-inflammatory agent to reduce swelling and irritation in various bodily systems. Finally, the inclusion of tonic properties supports the overall strengthening of bodily functions and systemic vitality.

As discussed in a study, Betula alnoides is a medicinal plant used in Thai traditional longevity preparations. Through bioassay-guided fractionation of the ethanol extract of the stems, five pentacyclic triterpenoid compounds were obtained, which include betulinic acid (1), betulin (2), lupeol (3), oleanolic acid (4), and ursolic acid (5). Regarding anti-inflammatory activity, all of these compounds exerted significant inhibitory effects on LPS-induced nitric oxide production. The study reported an IC50 value of less than 68.7 μM for this inhibitory effect. This research provides scientific support for the use of Betula alnoides in traditional medicine.
