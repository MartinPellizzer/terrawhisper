Betula alnoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Betula alnoides?

Betula alnoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Fagales and the family Betulaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Betula. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique specific epithet, alnoides.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Betula alnoiides contains bioactive compounds including betulin, betulinic acid, lupeol, and various phenolic compounds like flavonoids and tannins. These specific triterpenoids are highly valued for their potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. Researchers have studied these constituents for their ability to inhibit certain cancer cell lines and modulate immune responses. Additionally, the presence of tannins and phenolic acids contributes to the plant's ability to combat oxidative stress in biological systems. These chemical profiles suggest that extracts from the species may have practical applications in pharmaceutical development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Betula alnoides?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, acne, and various inflammatory dermatological ailments. The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that provide antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties to the skin. These substances help to soothe irritation and reduce redness in affected areas. Some traditional applications also include using extracts to assist with wound healing and minor infections. The plant serves as a natural remedy for managing topical discomfort through its medicinal chemistry.
