Citrus nana

Citrus nana, commonly known as the Fingered Citron, is a species of citrus that is characterized by its unique finger-like or cordate-shaped segments.

It is believed to have various health benefits, including its potential to act as an antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial agent, which may help to protect against chronic diseases such as cancer and cardiovascular disease.

The therapeutic actions of Citrus nana include its use in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, including digestive issues, fever, and respiratory problems, due to its bioactive constituents, which include limonoids, flavonoids, and terpenoids.

The bioactive constituents of Citrus nana are also responsible for its potential to have anti-cancer, anti-diabetic, and anti-hypertensive effects.

Citrus nana has many active constituents, such as limonoids, flavonoids, coumarins, and phenolic acids, which possess numerous medicinal properties.

Additionally, the flavonoids present in Citrus nana, such as hesperidin and eriodictyol, have been found to have vasodilatory and neuroprotective effects, potentially lowering blood pressure and improving cognitive function.

Possible Side Effects of Citrus nana

Citrus nana can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

In severe cases, improper use of citrus nana can cause respiratory problems, such as bronchitis or asthma attacks, in individuals with pre-existing conditions.

Additionally, skin contact with the plant's sap can cause burns, blisters, or rashes in people with sensitive skin.
