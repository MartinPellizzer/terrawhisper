What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases such as eczema, psoriasis, and various types of dermatitis. The leaves contain bioactive compounds that help reduce localized inflammation and itching on the skin surface. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to manage minor wounds or skin irritations caused by external allergens. Practitioners often apply extracts topically to soothe redness and promote faster healing of epidermal layers. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's natural antimicrobial and soothing properties.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Ixeridium laevigatum can cause severe skin irritation, localized dermatitis, and potential allergic reactions upon direct contact with the human body. Ingestion of the plant is generally discouraged as it may lead to gastrointestinal distress or systemic toxicity. Monitoring for any signs of an adverse reaction is essential when handling this species in a natural environment.
