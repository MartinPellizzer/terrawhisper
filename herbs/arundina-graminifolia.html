Arundina graminifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arundina graminifolia has large, fleshy, tuberous rhizomes and thick, succulent stems that can reach heights of up to two meters. The leaves are lanceolate, grass-like, and arranged in a distichous manner along the stem. Its inflorescence consists of large, showy flowers that typically feature shades of pink, purple, or white with intricate spotting on the labellum. The floral structure includes a prominent three-lobed lip and a central column characteristic of the orchid family. These plants often develop a dense clump of foliage that supports their substantial vertical growth.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, ranging from India and Sri Lanka through Indochina and the Malay Peninsula to parts of Indonesia and northern Australia. It typically thrives in moist, lowland habitats such as forest edges, riverbanks, and seasonally flooded areas. Within these diverse environments, the species prefers humid climates where consistent moisture is available for its growth. Its presence is often linked to specific soil compositions found in the understory of tropical woodlands.
