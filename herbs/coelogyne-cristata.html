Coelogyne cristata

What's the taxonomical classification of Coelogyne cristata?

Coelogyne cristata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family, specifically falling within the genus Coelogyne, and is identified by the species name cristata.

      What are the herbal preparations of Coelogyne cristata?

      Coelogyne cristata is prepared as a liquid decoction or a concentrated infusion derived from its fleshy pseudobulbs and aerial roots. These botanical parts are typically simmered in water to extract soluble alkaloids and polysaccharides for medicinal use. Once the extraction is complete, the liquid can be administered orally or used as a topical wash for skin ailments. Some practitioners also dry the plant material to create a fine powder for encapsulation. This powder is often mixed with honey to improve palatability and shelf stability.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Coelogyne cristata can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or mild respiratory discomfort if its sap or pollen comes into direct contact with sensitive areas. While these orchids are primarily grown for ornamental purposes, individual sensitivities to specific plant proteins can trigger localized redness or itching. Some people may experience swelling or hives if the plant material is handled without gloves. Inhalation of airborne particles during maintenance or pruning could potentially irritate the mucous membranes of the nose and throat. Monitoring for these reactions is recommended when introducing the plant into a new indoor environment.
