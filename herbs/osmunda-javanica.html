What's the taxonomical classification of Osmunda javanica?

Osmunda javanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Osmundales. It is situated within the family Osmundaceae and is further categorized under the genus Osmunda, with its specific designation being the species javanica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Osmunda javanica has large, bipinnate fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length, consisting of light green, sterile pinnae and dark brown, fertile pinnae located at the tips. The rhizomes are thick, woody, and creeping, providing a sturdy base for the upright stipes. Each stipe is smooth and lacks scales, transitioning into a spreading crown of foliage. The fertile segments are distinctively colored and contain the spore-producing structures. This morphological distinction between sterile and fertile tissue is a key characteristic of the species.
