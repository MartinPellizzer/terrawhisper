Spring corydalis

Corydalis ternata

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Spring Corydalis (Corydalis ternata) is a perennial herb in the family Fumariaceae. Commonly known as "Three-lobed Corydalis" in some regions, it is native to eastern Asia. The plant has trifoliate leaves, slender stems, and delicate pinkish flowers. It is also referred to by various synonyms in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Spring Corydalis contains key active compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides. These compounds work together to exert their medicinal effects, with alkaloids contributing to its analgesic properties. Flavonoids and glycosides support anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities, enhancing the herb's therapeutic potential.

  • Alkaloid
  • Flavonoid
  • Glycoside

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for nervous system disorders and pain relief.

Extract: Used for treating inflammation and pain.

Safety Profile

Spring Corydalis (Corydalis ternata) should be used with caution during pregnancy and lactation due to its potential uterine stimulant effects. It is contraindicated in individuals with bleeding disorders or those taking anticoagulant medications. Always consult a qualified herbalist or healthcare provider before use.
