Corydalis ternata

Spring Corydalis (Corydalis ternata) is a flowering plant native to Asia, known for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal medicine to treat conditions such as pain and inflammation.

Morphology

Spring Corydalis (Corydalis ternata) is a low-growing herb with slender, branched stems, opposite lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

Distribution

Spring Corydalis (Corydalis ternata) is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates and moist, shaded woodland environments.

Traditional Uses

Spring Corydalis (Corydalis ternata) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat pain, inflammation, and neurological disorders due to its alkaloid content, which exhibits analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional Chinese medicine and Tibetan medicine, Spring Corydalis (Corydalis ternata) has been utilized for its analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and circulatory benefits.

Phytochemistry

Spring Corydalis (Corydalis ternata) contains bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and traditional use in herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

Spring Corydalis (Corydalis ternata) exhibits analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and sedative properties, making it effective in alleviating pain, reducing inflammation, and promoting relaxation.

Botanical Identification

Spring Corydalis (Corydalis ternata) is a perennial herb in the family Fumariaceae. Commonly known as "Three-lobed Corydalis" in some regions, it is native to eastern Asia. The plant has trifoliate leaves, slender stems, and delicate pinkish flowers. It is also referred to by various synonyms in traditional herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Spring Corydalis (Corydalis ternata) should be used with caution during pregnancy and lactation due to its potential uterine stimulant effects. It is contraindicated in individuals with bleeding disorders or those taking anticoagulant medications. Always consult a qualified herbalist or healthcare provider before use.
