Celastrus orbiculatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus orbiculatus has opposite, simple, elliptical to ovate leaves with entire margins and a woody, upright shrubby stem. The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a dark green color on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters during the growing season. These blooms eventually give way to distinctive, rounded capsules that split open to reveal bright red or orange arils. The plant's overall structure is characterized by its compact, spreading habit and branching woody architecture.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Caribbean, specifically occurring throughout the Greater Antilles and the Lesser Antilles. It typically inhabits coastal environments such as rocky shores and limestone cliffs where it can thrive in saline conditions. The species is also found in certain parts of Central and South America depending on the specific subspecies.     What are the herbal preparations of Celastrus orbiculatus?

    Celastrus orbiculatus is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to extract its medicinal alkaloids. These liquid preparations are often used to harness the plant's purgative and emetic properties. In some traditional practices, the dried bark or leaves may be boiled in water to create a concentrated tea.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Celastrus orbiculatus can cause severe skin irritation, stomach upset, vomiting, and potential neurological distress if ingested. The plant contains bioactive compounds that act as irritants when they come into direct contact with human tissue. Contact with the leaves or berries may result in redness, itching, or painful rashes on the skin. If a person swallows any part of the plant, they may experience significant gastrointestinal distress including nausea and abdominal pain. In some cases, the toxins present in the specimen can lead to more serious symptoms like dizziness or tremors. Always use caution and avoid handling this species without protective gear.
