Celastrus orbiculatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus orbiculatus?

Celastrus orbiculatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Celastrus. Finally, it is identified at the species level as orbiculatus.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celastrus orbiculatus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, intestinal worms, and various types of inflammation. The bark and leaves are frequently employed in traditional medicine to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing. It also contains compounds that help expel parasitic organisms from the digestive tract. Furthermore, its properties are utilized to alleviate internal swelling and systemic discomfort. These medicinal applications are primarily rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical practices.
