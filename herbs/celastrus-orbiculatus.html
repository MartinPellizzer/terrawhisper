Celastrus orbiculatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus orbiculatus?

Celastrus orbiculatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Celastrus. Finally, it is identified at the species level as orbiculatus.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Celastrus orbiculatus contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its bark, leaves, and fruits. The bark is frequently harvested to prepare decoctions intended to treat various ailments. Extracts from the leaves are also utilized for their potential therapeutic properties in traditional applications. Some preparations involving the fruit are used to address specific internal conditions. These different botanical parts provide a diverse range of chemical constituents used in folk medicine.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Celastrus orbiculatus targets various inflammatory enzymes and oxidative stress markers, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes and reducing levels of pro-inflammatory cytokines like TNF-alpha and interleukin-6. These phytochemical components exert their medicinal effects by modulating the arachidonic acid pathway to mitigate pain and swelling. Additionally, the bioactive compounds interact with antioxidant enzymes such as superoxide dismutase to neutralize reactive oxygen species within cells. By regulating these specific protein pathways, the plant demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective potential. This complex interaction with cellular signaling proteins underpins its traditional use in treating various systemic ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celastrus orbiculatus?

Celastrus orbiculatus has medicinal properties that primarily include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic activities used to treat various ailments. The plant contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and alleviate physical pain in different parts of the body. Its antimicrobial characteristics allow it to combat certain pathogens, making it useful in traditional treatments for infections. Some practitioners also utilize its extracts to address digestive issues or skin irritations. These therapeutic actions are often achieved through the preparation of decoctions or topical applications of the plant material.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, intestinal worms, and various types of inflammation. The bark and leaves are frequently employed in traditional medicine to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing. It also contains compounds that help expel parasitic organisms from the digestive tract. Furthermore, its properties are utilized to alleviate internal swelling and systemic discomfort. These medicinal applications are primarily rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical practices.

What are the herbal preparations of Celastrus orbiculatus?

Celastrus orbiculatus is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to extract its medicinal alkaloids. These liquid preparations are often used to harness the plant's purgative and emetic properties. In some traditional practices, the dried bark or leaves may be boiled in water to create a concentrated tea. Tinctures made with alcohol allow for a more stable and long-term storage of the active chemical compounds. Practitioners must carefully control the dosage because the plant's potency can be quite high.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Celastrus orbiculatus can cause severe skin irritation, stomach upset, vomiting, and potential neurological distress if ingested. The plant contains bioactive compounds that act as irritants when they come into direct contact with human tissue. Contact with the leaves or berries may result in redness, itching, or painful rashes on the skin. If a person swallows any part of the plant, they may experience significant gastrointestinal distress including nausea and abdominal pain. In some cases, the toxins present in the specimen can lead to more serious symptoms like dizziness or tremors. Always use caution and avoid handling this species without protective gear.
