Silk Tree

Albizia lebbeck

Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck) is a leguminous tree native to South and Southeast Asia, known for its therapeutic properties and traditional use in treating anxiety, stress, and insomnia.

Botanical Identification

Albizia lebbeck, commonly known as Silk Tree, belongs to the Fabaceae family. It is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia. The plant has bipinnate leaves, yellow flowers, and a deep root system. It is also referred to as Indian silk tree or silk cotton tree in various regions.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Albizia
  • Species: Albizia lebbeck

Active Compounds

The Silk Tree contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Silk Tree Uses.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Silk Tree (Albizia lebbeck) is generally considered safe when used appropriately. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated in patients with known allergies to legumes or those with a history of adverse reactions to similar plants. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
