10 Possible Side Effects Of Cnicus Benedictus (Blessed Thistle)

Cnicus benedictus has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and increased risk of bleeding.

These issues can arise from consuming excessive amounts, interacting with medications, or having underlying health conditions.

In severe cases, these side effects can worsen people's lives, causing discomfort, disability, or even life-threatening complications, as seen in individuals who experience severe allergic reactions, digestive problems, or uncontrollable bleeding.

1. Worsens autoimmune disorders

Cnicus benedictus causes allergic reactions due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids, which can trigger an immune response in sensitive individuals.

These compounds can stimulate the release of histamine, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

In rare cases, more severe reactions including anaphylaxis can occur, requiring immediate medical attention.

Cnicus benedictus induces kidney damage due to its ability to reduce blood flow and cause constriction of blood vessels in the kidneys.

This can lead to decreased glomerular filtration rate, resulting in impaired waste removal and potentially even chronic kidney disease.

The saponins present in Cnicus benedictus may also contribute to this effect by increasing inflammation and oxidative stress in the kidneys.

This may lead to symptoms such as bloating, abdominal discomfort, and changes in bowel habits. The saponins present in Cnicus benedictus are thought to be responsible for this effect, as they can irritate the mucous membranes of the digestive tract and cause inflammation.

As a result, individuals using blessed thistle supplements may experience gastrointestinal disturbances.
