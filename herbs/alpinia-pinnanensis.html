Alpinia pinnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Alpinia pinnanensis?

Alpinia pinnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Zingiberales. This species is a member of the family Zingiberaceae and is categorized under the genus Alpinia, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species pinnanenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia pinnanensis has large, oblong-shaped leaves that are arranged in a pinnate pattern along its upright, herbaceous stems. These leaves feature prominent parallel venation and a smooth, waxy surface texture. The plant develops long, fleshy rhizomes that spread underground to form dense clumps. Its inflorescences consist of terminal spikes that produce showy, tubular flowers. The structure of the plant is characterized by its robust, leafy canopy and its ability to reach significant heights in tropical environments.
