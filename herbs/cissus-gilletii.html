Cissus gilletii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cissus gilletii has woody, climbing stems that utilize tendrils to ascend through its habitat. The leaves are typically arranged in an opposite pattern and feature prominent venation across their surfaces. These leaves often exhibit a cordate or ovate shape with varying degrees of lobing depending on the specific specimen. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the foliage to facilitate reproduction. The overall structure of the plant is adapted for a scandent growth habit, allowing it to compete for light in dense vegetation.
