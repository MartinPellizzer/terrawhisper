What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its inflorescence consists of multiple compound umbels that hold numerous small, yellowish-green flowers. Each individual flower possesses five petals and is arranged in dense clusters to attract pollinators. What are the pharmacological activities of Angelica anomala?

This plant has potent anti-inflammatory and antibacterial pharmacological activities that make it a valuable subject for medicinal research. Its phytochemical constituents work to suppress inflammatory responses by inhibiting specific biochemical pathways in the body. Additionally, the plant exhibits significant efficacy in combating various bacterial strains, preventing their growth and proliferation. Further investigation into its bioactive compounds may reveal even broader medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "The Journal of general and applied microbiology", Angelica anomala demonstrated strong inhibitory effects against both P. acnes and S. epidermidis. The MIC values for Angelica anomala were 15.6 microg/ml against P. acnes and 125 microg/ml against S. epidermidis. The extract exhibited low cytotoxicity at 200 microg/ml in both human dermal fibroblasts and HaCaT cells. Furthermore, the extract reduced the P. acnes-induced secretion of interleukin-8 and tumor necrosis factor-alpha in THP-1 cells, indicating anti-inflammatory effects. These results suggest that Angelica anomala is an attractive acne-mitigating candidate for topical application.
