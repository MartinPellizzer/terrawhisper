Angelica anomala

What's the taxonomical classification of Angelica anomala?

Angelica anomala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Apiales and falls within the family Apiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Angelica, and its unique species designation, anomala.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Angelica anomala contains various bioactive compounds including coumarins, essential oils, and organic acids that contribute to its pharmacological properties. These phytochemicals are primarily responsible for the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Specifically, the presence of furanocoumarins plays a central role in its medicinal potential. Researchers have also identified various terpenes within the essential oil fraction that exhibit antimicrobial effects. These diverse chemical constituents make the plant a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Angelica anomala?

Angelica anomala has medicinal properties that primarily include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities used to treat digestive and respiratory ailments. The plant contains various essential oils and coumarins that work together to soothe gastrointestinal distress and stimulate digestion. Its high concentration of bioactive compounds helps neutralize free radicals, which supports the body's immune response against oxidative stress. Additionally, the extract is often utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of coughs and bronchial inflammation. These combined actions make it a valuable component in various herbal formulations for systemic wellness.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various gastrointestinal issues such as indigestion, stomach aches, and intestinal spasms. Its medicinal properties are also frequently applied to alleviate symptoms of respiratory ailments like coughs and bronchitis. Some traditional practices utilize its extracts to support digestive functions and reduce bloating. Additionally, it may be used in herbal remedies to help manage menstrual discomfort and general inflammation.
