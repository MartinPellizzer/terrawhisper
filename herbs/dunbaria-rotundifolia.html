Dunbaria rotundifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Dunbaria rotundifolia?

Dunbaria rotundifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it among the legumes, and is further categorized into the genus Dunbaria. Finally, its specific taxonomic designation is completed by the species name rotundifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dunbaria rotundifolia has a prostrate or decumbent growth habit characterized by woody stems and trifoliate leaves with obovate to orbicular leaflets. The leaves typically feature a terminal leaflet that is significantly larger than the lateral ones, often displaying a rounded or slightly notched apex. Small, papilionaceous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, appearing in small clusters or solitary units. The legume fruit is a small, compressed pod that contains a limited number of seeds.
