Distylium racemosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Distylium racemosum has a woody, spreading shrub habit characterized by small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and leathery. The foliage is arranged in a dense manner along the stems, often presenting a somewhat imbricate appearance. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that are borne on elongated, upright racemes. These inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils and can extend well beyond the leafy canopy. The overall structure of the plant is compact, allowing it to thrive in rocky or coastal environments.
