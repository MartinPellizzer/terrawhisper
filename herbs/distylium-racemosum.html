Distylium racemosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Distylium racemosum?

Distylium racemosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Hamamelidaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, it is identified by the genus Distylium and the species name racemosum.

These inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils and can extend well beyond the leafy canopy. The overall structure of the plant is compact, allowing it to thrive in rocky or coastal environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Distylium racemosum?

This plant has various pharmacological activities including α-glucosidase inhibitory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. Its chemical constituents contribute to these biological effects by modulating enzyme activities within the body. Researchers are investigating how these bioactive compounds interact with physiological pathways to improve metabolic health. These diverse medicinal properties make Distylium racemosum a significant subject for further pharmacological study.
