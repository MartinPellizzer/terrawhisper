What's the taxonomical classification of Distylium racemosum?

Distylium racemosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Hamamelidaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, it is identified by the genus Distylium and the species name racemosum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Distylium racemosum?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including α-glucosidase inhibitory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The inhibition of the α-glucosidase enzyme suggests its potential utility in managing postprandial blood glucose levels in diabetic patients. Research indicates that various bioactive compounds within its extracts contribute to these therapeutic effects. These phytochemicals work by neutralizing free radicals and disrupting the growth of harmful microorganisms. Such multifaceted biological actions highlight the medicinal importance of Distylium racemosum in traditional and modern pharmacology.

According to a study published by "Pakistan journal of biological sciences : PJBS", the methanolic leaf extracts of Distylium racemosum were evaluated for their pharmacological activities. Specifically, the research investigated the α-glucosidase inhibitory activity of the plant. The α-glucosidase inhibition assay revealed that Distylium racemosum possessed the highest activity among the plants tested. This activity was characterized by an IC50 value of 22.6 ± 1.9 μg mL(-1). Consequently, the results highlight the potential of Distylium racemosum as an alternative source for α-glucosidase inhibitors.
