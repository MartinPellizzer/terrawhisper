Distylium racemosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Distylium racemosum?

Distylium racemosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Hamamelidaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, it is identified by the genus Distylium and the species name racemosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Distylium racemosum has a woody, spreading shrub habit characterized by small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and leathery.

The foliage is arranged in a dense manner along the stems, often presenting a somewhat imbricate appearance. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that are borne on elongated, upright racemes. These inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils and can extend well beyond the leafy canopy.

The overall structure of the plant is compact, allowing it to thrive in rocky or coastal environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Distylium racemosum can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if the plant material comes into direct contact with sensitive human tissue. While comprehensive toxicological studies on humans are currently limited, contact with the foliage may lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling. Individuals with known sensitivities to certain plant alkaloids should exercise caution when handling this species. There is also a potential risk of gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is accidentally ingested. Monitoring for these physical reactions is recommended for anyone working closely with the plant.
