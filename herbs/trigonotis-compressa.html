Trigonotis compressa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trigonotis compressa has a prostrate or ascending growth habit characterized by slender, often compressed stems and small, opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically green and lacks significant pubescence, providing a smooth texture to the plant's surface. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers feature a tubular corolla and are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms common to the Rubiaceae family. The overall structure remains compact, allowing the plant to thrive in low-growing niches.
