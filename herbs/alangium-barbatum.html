Alangium barbatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alangium barbatum has an upright, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, branching stems that can reach heights of up to fifty centimeters. The leaves are typically lanceolate or narrowly elliptical with smooth or slightly serrated margins and an alternate arrangement along the stem. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in terminal cymes. The plant often features fine, hair-like structures on the stems and leaf undersides, which contribute to its specific epithet. These morphological traits help distinguish the species within its botanical family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Mediterranean Basin, spanning across southern Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. It is commonly found growing in rocky areas, dry grasslands, and along the edges of scrublands within these regions. The species thrives in warm climates that experience seasonal rainfall and significant sun exposure. Its presence is particularly notable in coastal areas where the soil is well-drained and stony. This distribution patterns reflect the plant's adaptation to the specific ecological niches of the Mediterranean climate.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alangium barbatum?

Alangium barbatum has carminative, antispasmodic, bitter, stomachic, nervine, diaporetic, and cephalic therapeutic actions that support various bodily systems. What are the herbal preparations of Alangium barbatum?

Alangium barbatum is prepared as a liquid infusion, a decoction, a tincture, or a topical poultice. Most commonly, the dried leaves are steeped in hot water to create a fragrant tea used for digestive or respiratory support. Tinctures made from an alcohol base allow for a more concentrated extraction of the plant's essential oils and volatile compounds. For external applications, the plant material can be crushed into a paste or applied as a wash to soothe skin irritations. These various methods ensure that the medicinal properties of the herb are accessible for both internal and external therapeutic use.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Alangium barbatum can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal upset, skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and potential liver toxicity if consumed in excessive amounts. Some individuals may experience nausea, abdominal pain, or diarrhea after ingestion. Topical application of the plant's oils or extracts can trigger redness, itching, or rashes in sensitive users. Long-term or high-dose consumption may also interfere with certain metabolic processes or drug interactions. It is essential to monitor for these symptoms when using the herb for medicinal purposes.
