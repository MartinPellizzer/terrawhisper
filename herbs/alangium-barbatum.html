Alangium barbatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Alangium barbatum?

Aliangium barbatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Cornales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Cornaceae, falling under the genus Aliangium, and is specifically identified by the species epithet barbatum.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Alangium barbatum contains essential oils primarily extracted from its aromatic leaves and flowering tops for medicinal use. These specific parts are harvested and processed to treat various ailments ranging from digestive issues to respiratory congestion. The leaves are often used in infusions or teas to alleviate symptoms of the common cold and soothe stomach discomfort. Additionally, the volatile compounds found in the plant's upper foliage provide antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These concentrated extracts serve as the main active components in traditional herbal remedies.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex profile of medicinal compounds including flavonoids, terpenoids, phenolic compounds, and alkaloids.

These bioactive constituents work synergistically to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. The presence of phenolic compounds and flavonoids helps neutralize free radicals within biological systems. Additionally, the terpenoids contribute to the plant's antimicrobial properties, while the alkaloids play a role in modulating various physiological responses.

Together, these chemical groups form the basis for the plant's therapeutic potential in traditional medicine.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Flavonoids
  • Terpenoids
  • Phenolic compounds
  • Alkaloids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Alangium barbatum targets various enzyme systems and neurotransmitter receptors, most notably inhibiting acetylcholinesterase and modulating GABAergic signaling pathways. The plant contains essential oils and polyphenols that interact with the cholinergic system to enhance cognitive function and memory retention. It also shows an ability to influence nitric oxide synthase, which helps regulate vascular relaxation and inflammatory responses. Furthermore, specific phytochemical constituents can bind to muscarinic acetylcholine receptors to exert sedative or calming effects. These multi-target interactions explain its traditional use in treating nervous disorders and digestive issues.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alangium barbatum?

Alanium barbatum has carminative, antispasmodic, and sedative properties that primarily function to soothe the digestive system and calm the central nervous system. It works by relaxing the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract to relieve bloating, gas, and abdominal cramps. The plant also contains volatile oils that assist in reducing intestinal spasms and promoting regular digestion. Beyond digestive benefits, its mild sedative effects can help alleviate tension and mild anxiety. These combined actions make it a versatile botanical remedy for managing both physical discomfort and nervous stress.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat digestive disorders, respiratory ailments, menstrual irregularities, and fever.

Its herbal properties help soothe stomach discomfort and assist with various gastrointestinal issues. It is also frequently employed to alleviate symptoms associated with respiratory ailments like coughs or congestion. For women, the plant serves as a remedy to help regulate menstrual irregularities.

Additionally, it possesses antipyretic qualities that make it effective for reducing a fever.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Digestive disorders
  • Respiratory ailments
  • Menstrual irregularities
  • Fever

What are the herbal preparations of Alangium barbatum?

Alangium barbatum is prepared as a liquid infusion, a decoction, a tincture, or a topical poultice. Most commonly, the dried leaves are steeped in hot water to create a fragrant tea used for digestive or respiratory support. Tinctures made from an alcohol base allow for a more concentrated extraction of the plant's essential oils and volatile compounds. For external applications, the plant material can be crushed into a paste or applied as a wash to soothe skin irritations. These various methods ensure that the medicinal properties of the herb are accessible for both internal and external therapeutic use.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Alangium barbatum can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal upset, skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and potential liver toxicity if consumed in excessive amounts. Some individuals may experience nausea, abdominal pain, or diarrhea after ingestion. Topical application of the plant's oils or extracts can trigger redness, itching, or rashes in sensitive users. Long-term or high-dose consumption may also interfere with certain metabolic processes or drug interactions. It is essential to monitor for these symptoms when using the herb for medicinal purposes.
