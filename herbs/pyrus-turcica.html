Possible Side Effects of Pyrus turcica

Pyrus turcica can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high concentration of amygdalin which can release cyanide, a toxic substance, when ingested.

Prolonged use of the plant can also lead to headaches, fatigue, and dizziness in some individuals.

In severe cases, excessive consumption of Pyrus turcica can result in respiratory problems, including shortness of breath and wheezing, as a result of cyanide poisoning.

Furthermore, pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid using the plant altogether due to the potential risks to the fetus or infant.
