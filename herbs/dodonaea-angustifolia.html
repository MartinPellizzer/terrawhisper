Dodonaea angustifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Dodonaea angustifolia?

Dodonaea angustifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Dodonaea, with its specific designation being the species angustifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dodonaea angustifolia has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along its woody, sprawling stems. The foliage typically exhibits a dull green color and can vary in size depending on the specific environmental conditions. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often appearing in late summer or autumn. These plants frequently develop winged seeds that facilitate dispersal through the wind. The overall growth habit is characterized by a dense, shrubby structure that helps it adapt to various habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of northern Australia, specifically spanning across the Northern Territory, Queensland, and Western Australia. It is commonly found inhabiting diverse environments such as coastal dunes, mangrove margins, and rocky outcrops. The species also extends its range into parts of New Guinea and several neighboring Pacific islands. Within these habitats, it tends to thrive in sandy or rocky soils where drainage is sufficient. This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and antiviral properties. What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating inflammation, healing wounds, and managing human immunodeficiency virus type 1 (HIV-1) and human immunodeficiency virus type 2 (HIV-2). Its medicinal properties are frequently utilized to reduce swelling and promote the rapid recovery of skin tissues. Research into its bioactive compounds suggests a potential role in addressing complications associated with HIV. Specifically, studies have examined how extracts might impact the progression of both HIV-1 and HIV-2 infections. These various applications highlight the plant's importance in traditional and experimental medicine.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as a decoction by boiling the tough woody stems and roots in water to extract medicinal compounds for internal use.
