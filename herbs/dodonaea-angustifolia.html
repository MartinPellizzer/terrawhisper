Dodonaea angustifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Dodonaea angustifolia?

Dodonaea angustifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Dodonaea, with its specific designation being the species angustifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dodonaea angustifolia has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along its woody, sprawling stems. The foliage typically exhibits a dull green color and can vary in size depending on the specific environmental conditions. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often appearing in late summer or autumn. These plants frequently develop winged seeds that facilitate dispersal through the wind. The overall growth habit is characterized by a dense, shrubby structure that helps it adapt to various habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of northern Australia, specifically spanning across the Northern Territory, Queensland, and Western Australia. It is commonly found inhabiting diverse environments such as coastal dunes, mangrove margins, and rocky outcrops. The species also extends its range into parts of New Guinea and several neighboring Pacific islands. Within these habitats, it tends to thrive in sandy or rocky soils where drainage is sufficient. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Dodonaea angustifolia targets cyclooxygenase enzymes, alpha-glucosidase, and various proinflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. The plant contains bioactive flavonoids and terpenoids that inhibit the cyclooxygenase-2 protein, which reduces the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. Its phytochemical constituents also interact with alpha-glucosidase, an enzyme responsible for carbohydrate breakdown, thereby helping to regulate blood glucose levels. Furthermore, certain compounds in the extract suppress the expression of tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6 to mitigate systemic inflammation. These diverse protein interactions contribute to the plant's documented antioxidant, anti-diabetic, and anti-inflammatory properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dodonaea angustifolia?

Dodonaea angustifolia has medicinal properties that allow it to function as an astringent, antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, hemostatic, and cicatrizant agent in traditional healing practices. Its chemical constituents work to reduce swelling and soothe irritated tissues through its anti-inflammatory effects. The plant also acts as an antiseptic to prevent infection in open wounds while serving as a hemostatic to stop bleeding. Furthermore, its astringent qualities help tighten skin tissues, which supports its role as a cicatrizant for promoting faster wound healing and scar reduction.

Studies suggest the plant may play a role in addressing the underlying biological challenges associated with both HIV-1 and HIV-2 infections. What side effects this plant can have?

Therefore, cautious handling and avoiding ingestion are recommended to prevent adverse physiological responses.
