What's the taxonomical classification of Mayodendron igneum?

Mayodendroon igneum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Bignoniaceae, which contains the genus Maydenidron, and is specifically identified by the species epithet igneum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mayodendroon igneuum has woody, creeping rhizomes that produce small, opposite leaves with ovate to elliptical shapes and serrated margins.

The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color, though some specimens may show reddish tints under specific light conditions. Its flowers are relatively small and characteristically bright red, emerging from the leaf axils to attract pollinators. The plant's overall structure is low-growing and spreading, forming dense mats across the forest floor.

These morphological traits allow the species to effectively colonize shaded, moist environments.

        What are the herbal preparations of Mayodendron igneum?

        Mayodendroon igneum is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried bark or roots in water to extract its medicinal compounds. This liquid extract is often consumed in small doses to address specific ailments.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Mayodendron igneum can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains caustic alkaloids that trigger immediate contact dermatitis upon touching the leaves or stems. If the sap enters an open wound, it may lead to prolonged inflammation or chemical burns. Ingesting even small quantities can result in nausea, abdominal cramping, and vomiting. Individuals with sensitive skin or existing allergies should exercise extreme caution around this species.
