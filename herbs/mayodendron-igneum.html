What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mayodendroon igneuum has woody, creeping rhizomes that produce small, opposite leaves with ovate to elliptical shapes and serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color, though some specimens may show reddish tints under specific light conditions. Its flowers are relatively small and characteristically bright red, emerging from the leaf axils to attract pollinators. The plant's overall structure is low-growing and spreading, forming dense mats across the forest floor. These morphological traits allow the species to effectively colonize shaded, moist environments.
