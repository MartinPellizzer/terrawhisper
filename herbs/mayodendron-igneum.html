Mayodendroon igneum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is further categorized into the family Bignoniaceae, which contains the genus Maydenidron, and is specifically identified by the species epithet igneum.

What are the herbal preparations of Mayodendron igneum?

Mayodendroon igneum is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried bark or roots in water to extract its medicinal compounds. This liquid extract is often consumed in small doses to address specific ailments. Some practitioners also transform the plant material into a tincture by soaking it in high-proof alcohol for an extended period. Tinctures provide a more concentrated form of the plant's essence and offer a longer shelf life than aqueous preparations. Additionally, the dried plant matter can be finely ground into a powder for use in capsules or topical applications.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Mayodendron igneum can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains caustic alkaloids that trigger immediate contact dermatitis upon touching the leaves or stems. If the sap enters an open wound, it may lead to prolonged inflammation or chemical burns. Ingesting even small quantities can result in nausea, abdominal cramping, and vomiting. Individuals with sensitive skin or existing allergies should exercise extreme caution around this species.
