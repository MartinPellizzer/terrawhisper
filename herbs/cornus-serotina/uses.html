Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) Uses

Red Osier Dogwood is used to treat arthritis, gout, joint pain, rheumatism, and osteoarthritis due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It also helps with muscle spasms and is applied topically for skin infections, inflammation, rashes, and wounds.

Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) Monograph

1. Arthritis

Red Osier Dogwood is used in herbal medicine to support joint health and reduce inflammation associated with arthritis. Its anti-inflammatory properties help alleviate pain and swelling, making it a valued remedy for those suffering from chronic joint conditions. The bark and leaves are typically prepared as tinctures or poultices for topical and internal use.

2. Gout

Red Osier Dogwood is used in herbal medicine to support gout management due to its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties. It helps reduce uric acid levels and alleviate joint pain associated with gout. Traditionally, it has been prepared as a tea or tincture to aid in detoxification and ease symptoms.

3. Joint pain

Red Osier Dogwood is used to alleviate joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The bark contains compounds that reduce swelling and stiffness, making it a traditional remedy for rheumatism and arthritis. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to support joint health and mobility.

4. Rheumatism

Red Osier Dogwood is used in herbal medicine to support joint health and alleviate symptoms of rheumatism. Its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce pain and swelling, making it a valued remedy for arthritis and other inflammatory conditions. It is often prepared as a tincture or decoction to harness its therapeutic benefits.

5. Osteoarthritis

Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) has been traditionally used to support joint health, particularly in managing symptoms of osteoarthritis. Its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties help reduce pain and swelling, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for arthritis support.

6. Muscle spasms

Red Osier Dogwood is used to alleviate muscle spasms due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. It helps relax overactive muscles and reduce cramping, making it useful for conditions like restless leg syndrome or menstrual cramps. The bark and leaves are traditionally prepared as teas or tinctures for internal use.

7. Skin infections

Red Osier Dogwood has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to reduce inflammation and promote healing. Its high tannin content helps to disinfect wounds and soothe irritated skin.

8. Skin inflammation

Red Osier Dogwood is used topically to reduce skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps soothe irritated skin, reduce redness, and promote healing. The bark and leaves are often prepared as poultices or infusions for direct application on affected areas.

9. Skin rash

Red Osier Dogwood is used externally to treat skin rashes due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. A decoction of the bark can be applied as a poultice to reduce redness and irritation. It helps soothe inflamed skin and promote healing in cases of eczema or contact dermatitis.

10. Wounds

Red Osier Dogwood is used topically to treat wounds due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling, promote healing, and prevent infection. A decoction of the bark can be applied directly to the affected area. It is particularly effective for minor cuts, abrasions, and skin irritations.
