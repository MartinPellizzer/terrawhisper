Botanical Identification of Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina)

To identify Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Cornaceae, genus Cornus, and species Cornus serotina. These classifications reflect its position within the plant kingdom and its evolutionary relationships.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name for Red Osier Dogwood is Cornus serotina, first described by Carl Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Cornus stolonifera and Cornus alba. Common names vary, but it is widely recognized as Red Osier Dogwood, with regional names such as Dogwood and Red Osier. Historically, it has been referred to by names like White Dogwood and Redstem.

Morphological Characteristics

Red Osier Dogwood is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a multi-stemmed growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. Its stems are slender, often with a red hue in winter, and bear opposite, oval leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces a cluster of white, flat-topped flowers in late spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits containing small, hard seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Red Osier Dogwood can be distinguished by its white, showy bracts that resemble petals, opposite leaves with smooth edges, and distinctive red stems that are prominent in winter. It also has small, yellowish-green flowers and berries that mature to a dark red color. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Cornus florida and Cornus sanguinea.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Red Osier Dogwood is native to eastern North America, ranging from Canada to the northeastern United States. It prefers moist, temperate climates and thrives in clay or loamy soils. It commonly grows in wetlands, stream banks, and woodlands, typically at low to mid-elevation areas.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Red Osier Dogwood blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period when the plant is most visually distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) is closely related to other species within the genus Cornus, such as Cornus sanguinea and Cornus florida. It belongs to the family Cornaceae, which includes other dogwoods and related shrubs. The genus Cornus is part of the tribe Corni, characterized by its opposite leaves and showy flowers.
