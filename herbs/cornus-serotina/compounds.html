Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) Compounds

Red Osier Dogwood contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin and anthocyanins are also present, offering potential benefits for skin and circulatory health. Glycosides and saponins support immune function, while essential oils and terpenoids may have antimicrobial effects. Alkaloids contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support immune function and may aid in reducing oxidative stress. They are found in the bark and roots, often used in traditional herbal preparations. Flavonoids play a key role in the plant's therapeutic applications.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its ability to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. Tannins also help in the preservation of the plant's medicinal qualities.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps support skin health and may aid in reducing oxidative stress. It is part of the plant's broader profile of bioactive compounds used in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and as a mild sedative. Coumarin also exhibits anticoagulant properties, making it relevant in herbal formulations for circulatory health. Its presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine for its therapeutic potential.

5. Glycoside

Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) contains the glycoside arbutin, which is known for its mild antibacterial properties. Arbutin is primarily found in the bark and roots of the plant and is used in herbal medicine to support urinary tract health. It is converted in the body to hydroquinone, which has antimicrobial effects.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. They also play a role in the plant's ecological interactions and defense mechanisms.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support immune function and help in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) contains volatile compounds like limonene and pinene, which contribute to its aromatic profile. These compounds may support respiratory and digestive health, though research on their specific therapeutic effects is limited.

9. Alkaloid

Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina) contains the alkaloid coriamyrtine. This compound has been studied for its potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting respiratory and digestive health. Coriamyrtine is one of several bioactive compounds found in Red Osier Dogwood.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus serotina). It contributes to the plant's vibrant red and purple pigments and has antioxidant properties that support cellular health. Found in the bark and berries, anthocyanin may aid in reducing inflammation and supporting immune function.
