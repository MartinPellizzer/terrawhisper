Bulbophyllum kwangtungense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum kwangtungense has a creeping rhizome with small, ovate to elliptical pseudobulbs that bear a single, apical, lanceolate leaf. The inflorescence is a short, congested raceme that emerges from the base of the pseudobulbs. Each individual flower features a small, fleshy lip and distinct sepals that often exhibit subtle color variations. The plant structure is compact and adapted to growing in shaded, moist forest environments. Its overall morphology is characterized by these diminutive, specialized parts typical of its genus.
