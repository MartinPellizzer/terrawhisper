Dialium guineense

What's the taxonomical classification of Dialium guineense?

Dialium guineense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Dialium, and its specific species epithet, guineense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dialium guineense has a large woody structure characterized by a trunk that can reach up to thirty meters in height with bark that is typically greyish-brown and slightly fissured. The leaves are pinnate, arranged alternately along the branches, and consist of several pairs of elliptical leaflets with smooth margins. Its flowers are small and yellowish, often appearing in clusters from the axils of the leaves or on older wood. The plant produces distinctive woody pods that are hard, black, and contain a sweet, brownish pulp surrounding the seeds. These pods are highly durable and serve as a primary morphological feature for identifying the species in tropical forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforests and moist woodlands of West and Central Africa, extending from Senegal and Guinea through Nigeria to the Congo Basin. It is primarily found in the Guineo-Congolian regional center of endemism where humidity levels remain consistently high. Within these habitats, it thrives in secondary forests and gallery forests along riverbanks. Its distribution follows the belt of lowland vegetation across the equatorial African zone. Local populations are often concentrated in areas with well-drained, fertile soils characteristic of the tropical forest biome.

What are the pharmacological activities of Dialium guineense?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including analgesic, anti-ulcer, anticancer, antioxidant, and bilharzia-preventing molluscicidal properties. Its antioxidant capabilities help neutralize harmful free radicals within the body to prevent cellular damage. The plant exhibits significant analgesic effects by reducing pain and serves as an effective anti-ulcer agent to protect the gastric mucosa. Furthermore, its anticancer properties show potential in inhibiting the growth of malignant cells. Finally, it functions as a molluscicide that helps prevent bilharzia by targeting the snails that act as intermediate hosts for the parasite.

As discussed in a study, Dialium guineense is used for the treatment of fever, headache, and other diverse ailments. The methanolic stem bark extract was administered at three test doses of 250, 500, and 1000 mg/kg body weight. In the acetic acid-induced writhing reflex model, the extract significantly decreased abdominal constriction with P values between 0.014 and 0.002. The percentage inhibition of the abdominal constriction reflex increased from 0% in the negative control to 71% at the highest dose of 1000 mg/kg. At a dose of 1000 mg/kg, the extract significantly increased the pain reaction time in the tail immersion model with a P value of 0.048. The extract also significantly increased the mean pain reaction time in the hot plate model at the doses of 500 and 1000 mg/kg with P values ranging from 0.048 to 0.05.
