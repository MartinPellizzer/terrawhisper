Dialium guineense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dialium guineense has a large woody structure characterized by a trunk that can reach up to thirty meters in height with bark that is typically greyish-brown and slightly fissured. The leaves are pinnate, arranged alternately along the branches, and consist of several pairs of elliptical leaflets with smooth margins. Its flowers are small and yellowish, often appearing in clusters from the axils of the leaves or on older wood. The plant produces distinctive woody pods that are hard, black, and contain a sweet, brownish pulp surrounding the seeds. These pods are highly durable and serve as a primary morphological feature for identifying the species in tropical forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforests and moist woodlands of West and Central Africa, extending from Senegal and Guinea through Nigeria to the Congo Basin. It is primarily found in the Guineo-Congolian regional center of endemism where humidity levels remain consistently high. Within these habitats, it thrives in secondary forests and gallery forests along riverbanks. Its distribution follows the belt of lowland vegetation across the equatorial African zone. Local populations are often concentrated in areas with well-drained, fertile soils characteristic of the tropical forest biome.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dialium guineense contains high concentrations of bioactive phytochemicals including tannins, saponins, flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids. These secondary metabolites are responsible for the plant's significant pharmacological activities in traditional medicine. The presence of tannins provides strong astringent and antimicrobial properties used to treat wounds and skin infections. Flavonoids and alkaloids contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects when applied to various ailments. What side effects this plant can have?
