What's the taxonomical classification of Adiantum pedatum?

Adiantum pedatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and is further organized into the order Polypodiales. Within the family Pteridaceae, this plant is identified by the genus Adiantum, and it is specifically designated as the species pedatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adiantum pedatum has a distinctive growth habit characterized by large, fan-shaped fronds that spread out from a central rhizome in a single plane. Each frond consists of multiple pinnate leaflets that are arranged in a delicate, lace-like pattern. The individual segments are typically wedge-shaped and taper toward the base, creating a graceful, spreading appearance. A defining morphological feature is the dark, wiry, and highly polished stipes that contrast sharply with the bright green foliage.
