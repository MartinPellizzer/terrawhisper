Adiantum pedatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Adiantum pedatum?

Adiantum pedatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and is further organized into the order Polypodiales. Within the family Pteridaceae, this plant is identified by the genus Adiantum, and it is specifically designated as the species pedatum.

Adiantum pedatum has medicinal properties primarily utilized in traditional practices to treat respiratory ailments, skin irritations, and inflammatory conditions. Some herbal practitioners also apply preparations of the fern topically to alleviate minor skin issues or itching. This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as coughs, asthma, bronchitis, and various forms of congestion. Traditional practitioners often employ its properties to soothe inflamed airways and ease breathing difficulties. Some herbal preparations focus on its potential to reduce inflammation within the lungs and throat. It is also sometimes applied in folk medicine to address skin irritations or minor wounds. These applications vary significantly depending on the specific cultural medicinal practices being followed.
