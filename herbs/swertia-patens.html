Swertia patens

What's the taxonomical classification of Swertia patens?

Swertia patens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Gentianales. The plant is a member of the family Gentianaceae and is specifically identified under the genus Swertiia and the species patens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Swertia patens has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by slender branches and opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves that typically measure 5 to 12 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are usually entire or slightly serrated, while the surfaces exhibit a smooth or finely pubescent texture depending on the specific specimen. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in small clusters or solitary arrangements. These blossoms possess a tubular corolla that transitions into a spreading structure during anthesis. The plant's overall structure is defined by its compact growth form and the distinct arrangement of its foliage along the stem.
