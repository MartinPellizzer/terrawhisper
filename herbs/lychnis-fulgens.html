Lychnis fulgens

What's the taxonomical classification of Lychnis fulgens?

Lychnis fulgens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae, specifically placed within the genus Lychnis, and is identified by the species name fulgens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lychnis fulgens has erect, branching stems that reach heights of approximately 30 to 60 centimeters and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The leaves are lanceolate or narrowly ovate in shape, arranged oppositely along the stem, and possess a slightly textured surface. At the terminal ends of the branches, the plant produces clusters of vibrant, scarlet-red flowers characterized by five distinct petals. Each petal is deeply notched at the tip, giving the bloom a delicate, fringed appearance.

The calyx is tubular and prominent, supporting the bright corolla during the flowering period.

        Lychnis fulgens is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical salve derived from its dried aerial parts. It is important to keep this plant away from curious pets and small children to prevent accidental poisoning.
