What's the taxonomical classification of Inula rhizocephala?

Inula rhizocephala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling into the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, this plant is further defined by its placement in the genus Inula, with its specific identification being the species rhizocephala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Inula rhizocephala has a perennial growth habit characterized by a thick, woody rhizome and a rosette of long-petioled, broadly ovate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a somewhat rugose texture on the upper surface. Its flowering stems are erect and terminate in solitary, discoid flower heads that lack prominent ray florets. These capitula feature numerous yellow tubular florets surrounded by a distinct involucre of graduated bracts. The plant's overall structure is adapted to stabilize itself within its specific rocky or montane habitat.
