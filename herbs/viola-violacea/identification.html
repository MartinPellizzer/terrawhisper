Botanical Identification of Purple Violet (Viola violacea)

To identify Purple Violet (Viola violacea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Purple Violet (Viola violacea) belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Violales, family Violaceae, genus Viola, and species Viola violacea. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the violet family, which includes many herbs used in traditional medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Purple Violet is Viola violacea, a species within the Violaceae family. It was first described by Carl von Linné, and while it has few synonyms, it is commonly known by various regional and historical names reflecting its widespread use and appearance in traditional herbal practices.

Morphological Characteristics

Purple Violet (Viola violacea) is a low-growing perennial with a rhizomatous root system. Its stems are square and hairy, often branching near the base. Leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, arranged alternately along the stem. The inflorescence consists of a single terminal flower. Flowers are violet with yellow markings, and the fruit is a capsule containing small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Purple Violet (Viola violacea) can be distinguished by its deep purple flowers with a white throat, heart-shaped leaves with scalloped edges, and a trailing growth habit. It differs from similar species like Viola odorata by its more intense purple coloration and smaller, more compact foliage. The presence of a violet scent when crushed is another key differentiator.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Purple Violet (Viola violacea) is native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It thrives in cool, moist climates with partial shade. Prefers well-drained, loamy soil rich in organic matter. Typically found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Purple Violet (Viola violacea) blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the most accurate harvest timing is during the flowering period, when the plant's identifying features are most visible.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Purple Violet (Viola violacea) is closely related to other violas within the Viola genus. It shares key characteristics with species like Viola tricolor and Viola odorata, such as heart-shaped leaves and spurred petals. These relationships are defined by shared morphological traits and genetic similarities within the Violaceae family.
