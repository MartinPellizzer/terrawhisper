Syzygium bullockii

What's the taxonomical classification of Syzygium bullockii?

Syzygium bullockii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Myrtales and falls within the family Myrtaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Syzygium, with its specific designation being the species bullockii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syzygium bullockii has an erect, woody habit characterized by a spreading canopy, elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are leathery in texture, and small white flowers clustered in terminal panicles.

The foliage typically displays a glossy upper surface with prominent pinnate venation. Its inflorescences consist of numerous individual blossoms that produce fleshy, globose drupes upon maturation. These fruits transition from green to a deep reddish or purple hue when fully ripe.

The bark is generally smooth and greyish, providing a stark contrast to the dense green canopy.
