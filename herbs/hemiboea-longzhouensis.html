Hemiboea longzhouensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hemiboea longzhouensis?

Hemiboea longzhouensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Gesneriaceae, specifically within the genus Hemiboea, and is identified by its unique species name, longzhouensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemiboea longzhouensis has leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length, with a distinctively coriaceous texture and an entire margin.

The leaf surfaces exhibit a glossy appearance, often possessing a deep green coloration on the adaxial side and a slightly paler hue on the abaxial side. Its inflorescence is characterized by a terminal spike that bears numerous small, tubular flowers. These flowers typically feature a bright pigmentation that serves to attract specific pollinators within its native habitat.

The stems are relatively slender and woody, providing structural support for the dense foliage.
