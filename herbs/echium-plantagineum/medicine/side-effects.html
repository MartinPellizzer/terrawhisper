10 Possible Side Effects Of Echium Plantagineum (Viper'S Bugloss)

Echium plantagineum has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal issues.

These side effects can be caused by the herb's high levels of alkylphenol glycosides, which can be toxic in large amounts.

For example, skin irritation can lead to redness, itching, and blistering, making everyday activities like showering and dressing a painful and frustrating experience.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Echium plantagineum if used imporperly.

1. Enhances risk of toxicity

Echium plantagineum causes allergic reactions due to its ability to stimulate an immune response in some individuals.

This can lead to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, and hives. The allergenic properties of the plant are thought to be linked to the presence of certain compounds, including sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, which can cause an overactive immune response in susceptible people.

As a result, some users may experience adverse reactions when coming into contact with Echium plantagineum or its products.

2. Enhances risk of toxicity

Echium plantagineum induces skin irritation due to its essential oils and saponins.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction, leading to redness, itching, and blistering of the skin. The sap of the plant contains irritating chemicals that can trigger contact dermatitis, a common issue experienced by people handling Echium plantagineum.

This skin sensitivity may be more pronounced in individuals with pre-existing skin conditions or allergies.

3. Enhances risk of toxicity

Echium plantagineum triggers gastrointestinal issues due to its saponin content, which can cause irritation and inflammation in the digestive tract.

The consumption of this herb may lead to symptoms such as bloating, diarrhea, stomach cramps, and nausea as a result of the saponins' ability to disrupt the normal functioning of the gut flora.

4. Enhances risk of toxicity

Echium plantagineum interferes with blood clotting due to its ability to thin the blood by inhibiting platelet aggregation and reducing platelet adhesiveness.

This is thought to be caused by the presence of flavonoids, such as kaempferol, in the plant, which have been shown to have anti-platelet effects.

Additionally, Echium plantagineum contains saponins, which may also contribute to its anticoagulant properties.

5. Enhances risk of toxicity

Echium plantagineum increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to thin blood and lower platelet counts.

This is primarily attributed to the presence of glycosides and alkaloids in the herb, which can inhibit blood clotting and lead to prolonged bleeding times.

Additionally, Echium plantagineum may interact with anticoagulant medications, further increasing the risk of bleeding complications.

6. Enhances risk of toxicity

Echium plantagineum affects kidney function.

The compound echinocystic acid present in this herb may interfere with the normal functioning of kidneys by altering the balance of electrolytes and fluids within the body. This could potentially lead to issues such as kidney damage or impaired excretion of waste products.

Regular consumption of Echium plantagineum may also cause the kidneys to work harder, further straining their function over time.

7. Enhances risk of toxicity

Echium plantagineum impairs liver function due to its high concentration of pyrrolizidine alkaloids.

These compounds can cause damage to the liver cells and lead to scarring and inflammation, which may result in liver dysfunction.

The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it is thought that these alkaloids are metabolized by the liver and accumulate in this organ, causing toxicity over time.

Echium plantagineum increases heart rate due to its stimulating effects on the cardiovascular system.

The herb contains compounds such as sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids that can cause an increase in sympathetic nervous activity, leading to a rise in heart rate and blood pressure.

This stimulatory effect is thought to be responsible for the increased heart rate experienced by individuals using Echium plantagineum.

Echium plantagineum lowers blood pressure.

This side effect is attributed to the presence of alkaloids and glycosides in the plant, which have been shown to dilate blood vessels and reduce peripheral resistance. As a result, blood pressure levels may decrease, potentially causing dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting in some individuals.

The exact mechanism of action involves the interaction between these compounds and the body's cardiovascular system, leading to this hypotensive effect.

10. Enhances risk of toxicity

Echium plantagineum induces seizures due to its potential interaction with certain medications, particularly those used to treat epilepsy.

The seed extract of this plant contains alkaloids that can increase the levels of these medications in the body, leading to an exacerbation of seizure activity.

Additionally, Echium plantagineum may cause seizures in individuals who are sensitive to its chemical constituents or have a history of seizure disorders.
