10 Possible Side Effects Of Citrus Bergamia

Citrus bergamia has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and phototoxicity.

These effects can occur when the essential oil is applied to the skin without proper dilution or if it is used in excessive amounts.

In severe cases, it can cause blistering, redness, and discomfort, potentially leading to social isolation and emotional distress.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Citrus bergamia causes allergic reactions due to its potent oil content and high concentration of allergenic compounds such as limonene, alpha-pinene, and beta-pinene.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, swelling, and redness.

Additionally, the peel and juice of Citrus bergamia may also contain other allergenic proteins and lectins that can cause adverse reactions.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Citrus bergamia triggers asthma attacks due to its potent terpenes and flavonoids.

The high concentration of limonene and linalyl acetate in Citrus bergamia can cause bronchial constriction, leading to an asthma attack.

Additionally, the citrus scent may also trigger allergic reactions or sensitivities in some individuals, further exacerbating respiratory issues.

3. Lowers blood pressure

Citrus bergamia lowers blood pressure due to its ability to relax blood vessels and slow down heart rate.

The citrus flavonoids present in bergamot extract, such as naringenin and bergapten, have been shown to stimulate the production of nitric oxide, a potent vasodilator that widens blood vessels and improves circulation.

As a result, blood pressure is reduced, and overall cardiovascular health may be improved.

4. Increases heart rate

Citrus bergamia increases heart rate due to its high content of bergapten and other furanocoumarin compounds.

These chemicals stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure. Additionally, bergamot's ability to enhance adrenaline production and release further contributes to this side effect.

As a result, individuals may experience palpitations, rapid heartbeat, or even arrhythmias when using Citrus bergamia products.

5. Disrupts digestive system

Citrus bergamia disrupts digestive system.

The essential oil extracted from its peel may cause gastrointestinal issues due to its high concentration of citral and limonene compounds, which can irritate the stomach lining and gut walls.

Additionally, bergamot's citrus flavonoids may alter the balance of gut bacteria, leading to changes in bowel habits and potentially causing diarrhea or constipation.

6. Affects thyroid function

Citrus bergamia affects thyroid function by altering the body's production of hormones.

The essential oil extracted from its peel contains compounds that may interfere with the synthesis and metabolism of thyroid hormones, potentially leading to changes in thyroid function and related symptoms.

This effect is attributed to the oil's ability to bind to enzymes involved in thyroid hormone regulation, ultimately influencing the delicate balance of thyroid hormones in the body.

7. Interacts with medications

Citrus bergamia interacts with medications such as statins, blood thinners, and diabetes medications.

The bioflavonoids present in bergamot extract can increase the risk of bleeding when taken with anticoagulant medications, while its ability to lower cholesterol levels may lead to adverse effects when combined with statins.

Additionally, bergamot's natural sugar-lowering properties may interact with diabetes medications, potentially causing blood sugar fluctuations.

9. Increases risk of bleeding

Citrus bergamia increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and activity.

This means that it can interfere with the blood's natural clotting process, potentially leading to excessive bleeding or bruising.

Additionally, Citrus bergamia may interact with medications such as anticoagulants, further increasing the risk of bleeding complications.

10. Produces skin rashes

Citrus bergamia produces skin rashes due to its high concentration of limonene and linalool, which can cause allergic reactions in some individuals.

Additionally, bergamot's natural oil, bergapten, has been shown to increase sensitivity to sunlight, making users more prone to phototoxicity, a condition that causes redness, itching, and blistering upon exposure to UV rays.

As a result, people may experience skin rashes as an adverse reaction to using Citrus bergamia.
