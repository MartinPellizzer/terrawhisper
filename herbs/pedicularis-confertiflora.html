Pedicularis confertiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis confertiflora has a basal rosette of leaves that are typically lanceolate or oblong and an upright stem bearing a dense, spike-like inflorescence of tubular, bilabiate flowers. The leaves are often deeply lobed or pinnatifid, providing a textured appearance to the lower portion of the plant. Its flowers exhibit a characteristic hooded upper lip and a broader, three-lobed lower lip, often displaying hues of pink, red, or purple. The inflorescence is notably compact, with many individual flowers clustered tightly along the upper portion of the central stalk. This morphological structure is specifically adapted to facilitate pollination by specialized long-tongued insects.
