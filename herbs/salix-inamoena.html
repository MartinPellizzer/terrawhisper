What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Historically, decoctions made from the woody stems or leaves have been used to alleviate fevers and various aches.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential salicylate toxicity if ingested in large quantities. Because it belongs to the willow family, it contains salicin which acts similarly to aspirin. Individuals with a known sensitivity to salicylates may experience hives, swelling, or respiratory issues upon contact or consumption.
