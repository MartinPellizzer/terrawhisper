What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia ciliata?

Elsholtzia ciliata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Elsholtzia, and its specific species epithet, ciliata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia ciliata has pinnately compound leaves consisting of 7 to 15 leaflets that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape and possess serrated margins. The leaflets exhibit a smooth texture with prominent pinnate venation and a light green color on their undersides. The tree features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in drooping panicles. Its bark is characterized by a gray or brownish hue that becomes deeply fissured with age. The fruit is a small, winged samara that facilitates wind dispersal.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacolgy", Elsholtzia ciliata used in traditional medicine possesses medicinal characteristics including antiviral, antibacterial, antipyretic, diaphoretic, carminaative, astringent, and diuretic effects. The study evaluated the anti-inflammatorty properties of Elsholtzia ciliata extract (ECE) in RAW264.7 cells treated with polyinosinic polycytidylic acid (Poly I:C). Detections revealed that ECE inhibited the levels of nitric oxide and central inflammatory mediators such as iNOS and COX-2. Furthermore, ECE downregulated the expression of pro-inflammatorty cytokines including PGE2, IL-1β, TNF-α, IFN-β, and IL-6. Finally, ECE inhibited the phosphorylation of several cell signaling pathways including AKT, TBK1/IRF3, MAPK, and NF-κB in the treated RAW264.7 cells.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", the Elsholtzia ciliata herb ethanolic extract contains phenolics, volatile compounds, and essential oil. The extract specifically contains elsholtzia and dehydroeilsholtzia ketones that can be encapsulated. To evaluate the encapsulation of these compounds, a D-optimal mixture composition design used sodium caseinate (0.5-1 g), skim milk (6-10 g), resistant-maltodextrin (8-12 g), and beta-cyclodextrin (0.5-1 g). The optimal mixture composition for these components was determined to be 0.54 g of sodium caseinate, 10 g of skim milk, 8.96 g of resistant-maltodextrin, and 0.5 g of beta-cyclodextrin. These microcapsules loaded with the bioactive compounds of Elsholtzia ciliata have potential for use in various pharmaceutical forms or food supplements.
