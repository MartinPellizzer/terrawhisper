Elsholtzia ciliata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia ciliata has pinnately compound leaves consisting of 7 to 15 leaflets that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape and possess serrated margins. The leaflets exhibit a smooth texture with prominent pinnate venation and a light green color on their undersides. The tree features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in drooping panicles. Its bark is characterized by a gray or brownish hue that becomes deeply fissured with age. The fruit is a small, winged samara that facilitates wind dispersal.
