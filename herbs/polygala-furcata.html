What's the taxonomical classification of Polygala furcata?

Polygala furcata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Fabales and the family Polygalaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Polygala, specifically identifying as the species furcata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygala furcata has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, branched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are small, linear to lanceolate in shape, and arranged oppositely along the slender stems. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the inflorescence, which consists of small, zygomorphic flowers that are often pink or white. The floral structure includes two large, petal-like sepals that mimic true petals to attract pollinators. These flowers are clustered in axillary racemes that emerge from the leaf nodes. The plant's overall structure is delicate yet robust enough to thrive in sandy or rocky environments.
