Schizaea digitata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schizaea digitata has a creeping rhizome that produces pinnate fronds characterized by deeply divided, finger-like segments. The fertile fronds are typically larger and more complex than the sterile ones, often exhibiting a more intense color. Each pinna is further subdivided into narrow, linear lobes that give the fern its distinct digitata appearance. The stipe is generally dark and can be quite robust to support the spreading foliage. These morphological features allow the plant to efficiently colonize damp, shaded environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, and the Philippines. It thrives predominantly in low-lying coastal areas and moist lowland forests where humidity is high. Within these habitats, the species is often found growing in disturbed sites or along the edges of riverbanks. Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of consistent rainfall and warm temperatures characteristic of the equatorial belt. Consequently, the plant remains concentrated in these specific maritime and mainland Southeast Asian territories.

    Schizaea digitata can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested in significant quantities. Therefore, cautious handling and avoiding consumption is advised for those with unknown sensitivities.
