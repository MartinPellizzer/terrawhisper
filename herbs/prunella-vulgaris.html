Selfheal

Prunella vulgaris

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Botanical Identification

Prunella vulgaris, commonly known as selfheal, is a perennial herb in the Lamiaceae family. Native to Europe, it has regional names like "self-heal" and "pennywort." It features square stems, lance-shaped leaves, and purple flowers. Synonyms include "Prunella minor" and "P. coerulea" in historical herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Prunella vulgaris contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to support healing and reduce inflammation, making the herb valuable in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Sore throat, cough, tonsillitis Antimicrobial, expectorant, anti-inflammatory
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, skin irritation Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antifungal
Urinary Urinary tract infections, bladder inflammation, kidney stones Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, diuretic

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic due to its ability to extract alkaloids and minerals.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as an antiseptic due to its high concentration of active compounds.

Safety Profile

Selfheal is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated in pregnancy and should be avoided by those with known allergies to plants in the Boraginaceae family.
