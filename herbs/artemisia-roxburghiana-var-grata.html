Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia Roxburghiana Var. Grata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Artemisia roxburghiana var. grata.

Sweet wormwood, scientifically known as Artemisia roxburghiana var. grata, is a medicinal herb widely revered for its adaptogenic and therapeutic properties. It is traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine to support digestion, detoxification, and immune function, owing to its rich content of compounds like thujone and artemisinin, which have demonstrated antiparasitic and anti-inflammatory effects. In traditional practices, it has been employed to treat ailments such as malaria, digestive disorders, and respiratory infections, with historical records tracing its use back centuries in India and Southeast Asia. Today, it is increasingly utilized in modern wellness for its calming and mood-enhancing effects, often incorporated into herbal teas and supplements to promote mental clarity and stress relief. Its distinctive camphor-like aroma and slightly bitter taste make it a unique spice and herbal remedy, valued for both its cultural heritage and contemporary health benefits.
