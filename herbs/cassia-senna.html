Senna (Cassia senna)

Senna (Cassia senna) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Africa, Asia, and Middle East. Traditionally, its leaves, seeds, and fruits have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, laxative, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Opposite, ovate, green upper surface with a pale midrib and yellow lower surface, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are yellow with five petals arranged in a raceme, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry and possessing a distinctive bilateral marking on the standard petal.
Stems
Erect growth habit with alternate branching, smooth surface, and presence of stipular sheaths.
