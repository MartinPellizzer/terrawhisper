Cassia Senna Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Cassia senna, commonly known as Alexandrian senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, native to regions of Africa and the Middle East.

This herb is renowned for its laxative properties and is widely used in traditional medicine for digestive health.

The health benefits of Cassia senna include promoting bowel movements, aiding in detoxification, and supporting overall gastrointestinal function.

Its therapeutic actions are attributed to bioactive constituents such as sennosides, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which have mild stimulant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Herbal preparations of Cassia senna can be made as infusions, decoctions, or tinctures, and are often used in herbal formulations for constipation relief.

Cassia senna sennosides are the primary active glycosides responsible for the herb's laxative effects, acting as stimulant laxatives by increasing intestinal motility and promoting bowel movements.

Cassia senna anthraquinones are potent compounds that exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antidiarrheal properties, and they also contribute to the herb's ability to stimulate the digestive system.

Cassia senna rutin is a flavonoid glycoside that enhances the bioavailability of other compounds, supports vascular health, and may contribute to the herb's overall therapeutic effects.
