10 Possible Side Effects Of Citrus Aurantifolia

Citrus aurantifolia has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can be caused by consuming excessive amounts, having a pre-existing allergy, or using it undiluted on the skin.

For example, digestive issues can lead to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, affecting a person's daily life and work productivity.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Citrus aurantifolia causes allergic reactions when its essential oils come into contact with human skin.

The allergenic compounds present in the oil, such as limonene and citral, can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like redness, itching, swelling, and rashes.

This reaction may occur due to individual sensitivities or intolerances to these chemicals, which are naturally found in the peel, pulp, and leaves of the lime tree.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Citrus aurantifolia triggers asthma attacks due to the high levels of citral and limonene present in its essential oil.

These compounds can cause bronchial constriction and inflammation, leading to increased airway resistance and breathing difficulties.

Additionally, the peel and pulp of the fruit contain allergenic proteins that may exacerbate respiratory issues, particularly in individuals with pre-existing asthma conditions.

3. Induces nausea and vomiting

Citrus aurantifolia induces nausea and vomiting due to its high concentration of citric acid and limonoids.

These compounds can stimulate the vagus nerve, which regulates digestion and gut motility, leading to increased stomach contractions and a sense of discomfort. The acidity of the lime juice can also irritate the mucous membranes in the mouth, throat, and stomach, triggering an adverse reaction in some individuals.

This side effect is more likely to occur when consuming large amounts or taking citrus-based products without proper digestion.

4. Irritates skin and mucous

Citrus aurantifolia irritates skin and mucous due to its high concentration of limonene and other citric compounds.

These natural oils can cause allergic reactions, inflammation, and discomfort upon contact with the skin or mucous membranes.

Additionally, the acidity in lime juice can further exacerbate irritation, leading to redness, itching, and burning sensations.

5. Increases heart rate rapidly

Citrus aurantifolia increases heart rate rapidly.

This is due to its high concentration of caffeine and limonoids, which can stimulate the cardiovascular system and cause a rapid heartbeat. The essential oils present in lime, particularly citral and limonene, may also contribute to this effect by increasing sympathetic nervous activity and cardiac output.

As a result, individuals using Citrus aurantifolia may experience an increase in their heart rate without any apparent warning signs or symptoms.

6. Lowers blood pressure sharply

Citrus aurantifolia lowers blood pressure sharply due to its high content of flavonoids and limonoids, which have been shown to inhibit the enzyme angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), a key factor in blood pressure regulation.

Additionally, the fruit's citric acid and limonene may contribute to this effect by causing vasodilation and relaxing blood vessels, leading to a decrease in blood pressure.

7. Interacts with medications poorly

Citrus aurantifolia interacts with medications poorly due to its potential to alter the way certain drugs are metabolized by the liver.

The bioactive compounds present in lime, such as flavonoids and limonoids, may increase or decrease the levels of medication in the body, potentially leading to adverse reactions or reduced efficacy.

8. Affects digestive system badly

Citrus aurantifolia affects digestive system badly.

The essential oils present in the peel and juice of this citrus fruit can cause stomach upset, diarrhea, and nausea due to its high concentration of limonene and flavonoids, which can irritate the mucous membranes lining the digestive tract.

Additionally, the high acidity level in lime juice can disrupt the balance of gut bacteria, leading to digestive discomfort and unpleasant symptoms.

9. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Citrus aurantifolia disrupts menstrual cycles due to its estrogenic properties, which can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

This may cause an imbalance in hormone levels, leading to irregular periods, prolonged bleeding, or even amenorrhea.

The essential oils present in the peel and leaves of the plant, particularly limonene, have been found to stimulate the production of estrogen receptors, further exacerbating this side effect.

10. Causes drowsiness suddenly

Citrus aurantifolia causes drowsiness suddenly due to its ability to increase the production of serotonin in the brain.

This neurotransmitter helps regulate sleep and relaxation, leading to a sudden feeling of fatigue and drowsiness.

Additionally, the high levels of citral and limonene in lime essential oil can also contribute to this sedative effect, making it easier to fall asleep or feel lethargic.
