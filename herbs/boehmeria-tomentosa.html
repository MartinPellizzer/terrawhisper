Boehmeria tomentosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Boehmeria tomentosa has an erect perennial habit characterized by opposite leaves with serrated margins and densely tomentose, or hairy, undersides. The stems are typically woody at the base and covered in fine pubescence that provides a soft texture. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary clusters. These floral structures are often accompanied by bracts that share the plant's characteristic hairy coating. The overall morphology reflects its adaptation to specific humid environments through its specialized leaf surfaces.

Some people might also experience discomfort if the plant is accidentally ingested, although detailed toxicological data on human consumption remains limited. Because individual sensitivities vary significantly, anyone handling the specimen should use protective gloves to avoid potential dermatological issues.
