What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pterygocalyx volubilis has elongated, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive winged stem structure that facilitates its climbing habit. Small, pale flowers emerge from axillary clusters, featuring delicate petals and a prominent calyx. The reproductive organs are centered within the bloom, providing a clear focal point for pollinators.
