Chinese fringe tree

Camptotheca acuminata

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Camptotheca acuminata, commonly known as the Chinese Fringe Tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. Belonging to the Nyssaceae family, it is also referred to as Fringe Tree or Kuan Tzu in various regions. The plant is a perennial with distinctive fringed flowers and compound leaves. Synonyms in traditional herbal texts include "Xian Lian" and "Hua Zhi."

Active Compounds

The Chinese Fringe Tree contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to modulate cellular processes, potentially supporting therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

  • Alkaloid
  • Flavonoid
  • Terpenoid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive and respiratory issues.

Extract: Used for cancer and inflammation-related conditions.

Safety Profile

Camptotheca acuminata contains toxic compounds, including paclitaxel, which can be harmful if ingested. It is contraindicated for use during pregnancy and by individuals with hypersensitivity to its components. Always consult a qualified herbalist or healthcare provider before use.
