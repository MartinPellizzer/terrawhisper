Abelia biflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Abelia biflora?

Abelia biflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the biological order Dipsacales. Within this hierarchy, the plant is a member of the family Caprifoliaceae and is categorized under the genus Abelia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species biflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Abelia biflora has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure between one and two inches in length and possess finely serrated margins. The plant grows as a spreading, deciduous shrub reaching heights of three to six feet with arching, woody stems. Small, tubular, white to pale pink flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils during the summer months. These blossoms are followed by distinctive, papery, reddish-brown calyxes that persist through the winter. The overall structure provides a dense, textured appearance due to the compact arrangement of its foliage and branching patterns.
