What's the taxonomical classification of Urophyllum chinense?

Urophyllum chinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Taxonomically, it is situated within the genus Urophyllum, with its specific designation being the species chinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Urophyllum chinense has large, simple, elliptic to oblong leaves that measure up to 40 centimeters in length and feature a glossy, dark green upper surface with a pale green underside. The leaf margins are entire, and the venation is prominent, with secondary veins branching out from a central midrib. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with a fleshy rhizomatous stem that supports the long-petioled foliage. Its reproductive structure consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy drupes. These morphological traits distinguish it from other members of the Araliaceae family.
