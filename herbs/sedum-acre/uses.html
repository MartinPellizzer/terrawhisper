Common Stonecrop (Sedum acre) Uses

Common Stonecrop is used to treat various skin conditions such as infections, inflammation, eczema, and fungal infections. It supports wound healing and can alleviate skin irritation and minor burns. Its anti-inflammatory properties make it useful for soothing sunburn and other inflammatory skin issues.

Common Stonecrop (Sedum acre) Monograph

1. Skin infection

Common Stonecrop has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce inflammation and promote healing in conditions like eczema and fungal infections. Topical application of its leaves or extracts can soothe irritated skin and prevent bacterial growth.

2. Wound healing

Common Stonecrop (Sedum acre) is used traditionally to promote wound healing due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps in reducing swelling, preventing infection, and accelerating tissue repair. The plant's high mucilage content supports skin regeneration and is often applied topically to minor cuts and abrasions.

3. Skin irritation

Common Stonecrop is used to treat skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It can soothe rashes, eczema, and minor burns when applied topically. The leaves are often crushed and used as a poultice or infused into oils for direct application.

4. Minor burns

Common Stonecrop is used topically for minor burns due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. A crushed leaf or stem can be applied directly to the affected area to soothe pain and promote healing. It helps reduce redness and prevents infection in superficial burns.

5. Skin eczema

Common Stonecrop has been traditionally used to treat skin eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and irritation associated with eczema. Topical application of a prepared salve or infusion can provide relief and promote skin healing. Its astringent qualities also help to tighten and protect the skin barrier.

6. Skin inflammation

Common Stonecrop is used topically to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation, making it effective for conditions like eczema and minor burns. The plant's high mucilage content soothes the skin and promotes healing. It is typically applied as a poultice or infused oil.

7. Skin infections

Common Stonecrop has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce inflammation and promotes healing in conditions like fungal infections and minor wounds. The plant's active compounds inhibit bacterial growth, making it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for skin ailments.

8. Inflammatory skin conditions

Common Stonecrop (Sedum acre) has been traditionally used to treat inflammatory skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help soothe irritation and reduce redness. The plant's sap can be applied topically to affected areas for localized relief. It is often used in herbal salves and poultices for its healing effects on the skin.

9. Fungal infections

Common Stonecrop has been traditionally used to treat fungal infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It contains compounds that inhibit the growth of fungi, making it effective for conditions like athlete's foot and ringworm. Its astringent qualities also help reduce inflammation and promote healing in affected areas.

10. Sunburn

Common Stonecrop (Sedum acre) has been traditionally used to treat sunburn due to its cooling and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps soothe skin irritation and reduce redness caused by excessive sun exposure. The plant's high water content aids in rehydration, while its compounds may provide mild pain relief. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil.
