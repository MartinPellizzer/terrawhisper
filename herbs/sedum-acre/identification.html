Botanical Identification of Common Stonecrop (Sedum acre)

To identify Common Stonecrop (Sedum acre) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Common Stonecrop, or Sedum acre, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Saxifragaceae, genus Sedum, and species Sedum acre. This classification places it within the broader group of flowering plants commonly found in various habitats.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Common Stonecrop is Sedum acre, first described by the Swedish botanist Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Sedum sedoides and Sedum pulchellum, reflecting its historical classification and regional variations. Common names such as Stonecrop, Common Stonecrop, and Chickweed are widely used, though they can sometimes be confused with other plants. Regional and historical names vary, often tied to local uses in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Common Stonecrop is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and survive in rocky or dry environments. Its stems are fleshy and upright, bearing alternate, spoon-shaped leaves that are thick and waxy. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Common Stonecrop can be distinguished by its fleshy, blue-green leaves arranged in a tight rosette and its upright, branched stems with yellow flowers. It has no leaves along the stem, unlike Sedum rupestre, which has leaf-like structures. The leaves are shorter and more rounded compared to the elongated leaves of Sedum kamtschaticum.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Common Stonecrop is native to Europe and western Asia. It prefers temperate climates with full sun. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils. It is typically found at low to moderate altitudes, up to around 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Common Stonecrop blooms from June to August, producing star-shaped yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, it is best harvested in early spring before flowering to ensure accurate identification and potency.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Common Stonecrop (Sedum acre) belongs to the genus Sedum, which is part of the family Crassulaceae. It shares close relations with other sedums, characterized by fleshy leaves and adaptive growth forms. Its phylogenetic placement reflects evolutionary ties to other succulent species within the same family.
