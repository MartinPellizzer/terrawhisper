Common Stonecrop (Sedum acre) Compounds

The primary compounds in Common Stonecrop include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins and glycosides contribute to its astringent and medicinal effects, while terpenoids and essential oils provide aromatic and antimicrobial benefits. Coumarin and anthocyanin add to its anti-inflammatory and protective qualities, and saponins and alkaloids support its overall therapeutic potential.
