Agarwood (Aquilaria Agallocha)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Aquilaria agallocha.

Agarwood, scientifically known as Aquilaria agallocha, is a rare and highly valued resinous wood derived from the Aquilaria tree, often referred to as the "wood of the gods" in traditional medicine. This unique substance forms when the tree is infected by a specific type of fungi, leading to the production of a rich, aromatic resin that is prized for its medicinal and therapeutic properties. Its primary benefits include its use in treating digestive disorders, respiratory ailments, and emotional stress due to its calming and anti-inflammatory effects. In traditional Chinese medicine and Ayurveda, agarwood has been used for centuries as an adaptogen to enhance resilience to stress and promote mental clarity, while in cultural practices, it is burned as incense to purify the air and foster spiritual connection. Modern wellness applications include its incorporation into aromatherapy, supplements, and skincare products, with its distinctive, woody-sweet aroma and the presence of unique compounds like alpha-santalol contributing to its enduring popularity.
