Cirsium griseum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cirsium griseum?

Cirsium griseum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Asterales and into the family Asteraceae, a group well-known for its diverse floral structures. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Cirsium and is specifically designated as the species griseum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium griseum has erect stems that reach heights of up to 100 centimeters and are covered in dense, grayish-white tomentose hairs. The leaves are deeply lobed and lanceolate, featuring a distinct silvery appearance due to the thick layer of fine pubescence on both surfaces. These leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem and possess serrated margins. The plant produces terminal flower heads that are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. These inflorescences are characterized by their pale lavender to pinkish color and are surrounded by involucral bracts.
