Chirita tenuituba

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chirita tenuituba has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are thin and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage is typically a soft green color with a relatively smooth texture. Its reproductive structures consist of tubular, zygomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These corollas are characterized by a narrow tube and spreading lobes that facilitate pollination. The plant maintains a low-growing, herbaceous habit suitable for its forest floor environment.
