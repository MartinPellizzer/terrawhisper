Chirita tenuituba

What's the taxonomical classification of Chirita tenuituba?

Chirita tenuituba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This specific plant is a part of the family Gesneriaceae, situated within the genus Chirita, and is identified by its unique species designation, tenuituba.

What are the therapeutic actions of Chirita tenuituba?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, burns, and various inflammatory conditions. The medicinal properties of the plant are primarily utilized for their ability to promote healing in damaged tissue. Local applications of its extracts can help reduce swelling and alleviate pain associated with cutaneous injuries. It is often employed in traditional practices to manage topical ailments and minor skin irritations. The effectiveness of the plant lies in its natural bioactive compounds that target localized infections.
