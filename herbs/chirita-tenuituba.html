What's the taxonomical classification of Chirita tenuituba?

Chirita tenuituba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This specific plant is a part of the family Gesneriaceae, situated within the genus Chirita, and is identified by its unique species designation, tenuituba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chirita tenuituba has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are thin and possess finely serrated margins.

The foliage is typically a soft green color with a relatively smooth texture. Its reproductive structures consist of tubular, zygomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These corollas are characterized by a narrow tube and spreading lobes that facilitate pollination.

The plant maintains a low-growing, herbaceous habit suitable for its forest floor environment.
