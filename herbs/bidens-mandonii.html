What is Bidens mandonii?

Bidens mandonii is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, native to Central and South America.

It is an annual or biennial herb with a height of 30-80 cm, bearing ovate to lanceolate leaves that are 3-10 cm long and 1-3 cm wide. The plant produces white to yellowish or reddish flowers in a terminal corymb, arranged in a capitulum with 8-12 florets per head, and achenes that are 2-3 mm long and have a pappus of 5-7 bristles. The inflorescence is a panicle or a corymb of corymbose cymes, and the plant has a taproot and a slender stem.

The species was first described by Pierre Jean Marie Delile in 1830.
