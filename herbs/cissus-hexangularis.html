Cissus hexangularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cissus hexangularis?

Cissus hexaengularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Vitales. The plant is further categorized into the family Vitaceae, which is the specific group to which its genus, Cissus, belongs. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species hexaengularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cissus hexaangularis has a woody, climbing vine morphology characterized by pentagonal or hexagonal stems that possess prominent longitudinal ridges. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that typically display five to seven distinct lobes with serrated margins. Its climbing habit is facilitated by specialized tendrils that emerge from the nodes to anchor the vine to supporting structures. The foliage is often glossy and deep green, providing a dense canopy as the plant ascends. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters, eventually giving way to fleshy berries.
