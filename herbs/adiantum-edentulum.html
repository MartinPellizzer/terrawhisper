Adiantum edentulum

What's the taxonomical classification of Adiantum edentulum?

Adiantum edentulum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Pteridaceae, within the genus Adiantum, specifically identifying it as the species edentulum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adiantum edentulum has delicate, fan-shaped pinnules with deeply lobed, dark green margins and a distinctive thin, black, wire-like stipe. The fronds are composed of multiple small leaflets that grow from a central rachis, creating a feathery and intricate appearance. These leaflets possess a smooth, almost translucent texture that allows light to pass through their fine structure. The reproductive structures are located on the underside of the pinnules, where small, dark sori are arranged in a characteristic marginal pattern. This combination of slender stems and lacy foliage defines its elegant morphological profile.
