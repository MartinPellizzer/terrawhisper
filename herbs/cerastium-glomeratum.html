Cerastium glomeratum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cerastium glomeratum has a basal rosette of mostly linear to lanceolate leaves and stems that produce small, five-petaled white flowers held in dense, clustered terminal cymes. The leaves are typically entire or slightly toothed along the margins and grow closely together at the base of the plant. Its flowering structures consist of several closely packed cymes that create a compact appearance at the tips of the branches. The stems are often reddish or green and can be slightly hairy or glabrous depending on the specific environment. Each flower features five distinct petals that are often slightly notched at the apex.
