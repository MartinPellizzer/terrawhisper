Begonia acetosella

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia acetosella?

Begonia acetosella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Cucurbitales. This plant is a member of the family Begoniaceae, specifically belonging to the genus Begonia, with its unique identification at the species level as acetosella.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia acetosella has asymmetrical, kidney-shaped leaves that are characterized by a distinctively puckered or rugose surface texture and a sharp, acid-like taste when touched. The foliage displays a striking color pattern of dark green with prominent silvery-white or light green marbled patches across the blades. These leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, often appearing somewhat fleshy and slightly hairy along the margins and undersides. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that typically emerge in clusters from the center of the leaf grouping. Its stems are succulent and often reddish in hue, providing structural support for the spreading leaf arrangement.
