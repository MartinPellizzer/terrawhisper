Sorbus glomerulata

What's the taxonomical classification of Sorbus glomerulata?

Sorbus glomerulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. The plant is further categorized under the family Rosaceae, which places it among a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is assigned to the genus Sorbus, and its specific identity is defined by the species name glomerulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sorbus glomerulata has pinnate leaves with 7 to 13 serrated leaflets and small, clustered white flowers that transition into dense clusters of orange-red pomes. Its stems are characterized by a somewhat woody texture and can vary in color depending on the age of the branch. The fruit develops into small, spherical berries that stay attached to the branches well into the autumn season. These morphological features collectively distinguish this species within its genus.
