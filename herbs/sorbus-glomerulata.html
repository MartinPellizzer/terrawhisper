Sorbus glomerulata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sorbus glomerulata has pinnate leaves with 7 to 13 serrated leaflets and small, clustered white flowers that transition into dense clusters of orange-red pomes. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green color on the upper surface while the underside remains noticeably paler. Its stems are characterized by a somewhat woody texture and can vary in color depending on the age of the branch. The fruit develops into small, spherical berries that stay attached to the branches well into the autumn season. These morphological features collectively distinguish this species within its genus.
