What's the taxonomical classification of Vaccinium mandarinorum?

Vaccinium mandarinorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Ericaceae, falling under the genus Vaccinium, and is specifically identified by its species name, mandarinorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vaccinium mandarinorum has woody, perennial stems with small, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that feature entire margins and acute apices. Its inflorescence consists of small, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into fleshy, globose berries that serve as the primary fruit of the species. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by a compact, shrubby growth habit.
