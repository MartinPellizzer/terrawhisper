Vaccinium mandarinorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vaccinium mandarinorum has woody, perennial stems with small, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that feature entire margins and acute apices. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and may exhibit a lighter shade underneath. Its inflorescence consists of small, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into fleshy, globose berries that serve as the primary fruit of the species. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by a compact, shrubby growth habit.
