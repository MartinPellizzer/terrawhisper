Astragalus ceratoides

Astragalus ceratoides, also known as Mongolian milkvetch or Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial plant species belonging to the pea family (Fabaceae).

It has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries, valued for its adaptogenic and immunomodulatory properties, which help to boost the body's natural defenses and balance its response to stress.

The medicinal properties of Astragalus ceratoides have been found to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral activities, leading to a range of health benefits, including improved immune function, reduced fatigue, and enhanced cardiovascular health.

Astragalus ceratoides is often prepared as a decoction, tincture, or capsule, and is also used in combination with other herbs in traditional Chinese medicine formulas.
