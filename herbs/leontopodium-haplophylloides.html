What's the taxonomical classification of Leontopodium haplophylloides?

Leontopodium haplophylloides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asterales, it falls under the widely recognized family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Leontopodium, and its species designation, haplophylloides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the stem junctions. The root system is relatively shallow, anchoring the plant into its specific substrate.
