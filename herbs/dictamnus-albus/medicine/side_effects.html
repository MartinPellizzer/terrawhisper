1. Causes allergic reactions

Dictamnus albus causes allergic reactions due to its unique composition of volatile oils and alkaloids.

These chemicals can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin rashes, hives, and respiratory issues.

The allergic reaction is often a result of the body's attempt to defend against the perceived foreign substances present in the plant.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Dictamnus albus triggers asthma attacks due to its volatile oils, particularly dictamine, which can release irritating particles into the air.

As these particles are inhaled, they can cause constriction of the airways, leading to wheezing and shortness of breath characteristic of asthma attacks.

This is especially concerning for individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions or sensitivities to environmental allergens.

3. Induces vomiting

Dictamnus albus induces vomiting due to its toxic properties and active compounds, such as dictamine and dictamoic acid.

These substances can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, stimulating a strong response and leading to nausea and vomiting.

This side effect is often a result of accidental ingestion or misuse of the plant, highlighting the importance of careful handling and use of this potentially hazardous herb.

4. Provokes diarrhea

Dictamnus albus provokes diarrhea due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase bowel movements.

The plant's volatile oils, particularly dictamine, have been found to irritate the mucous membranes of the gut, leading to increased water absorption in the colon and subsequent loosening of stools.

This may result in frequent and watery bowel movements, which can be uncomfortable and inconvenient for some individuals.

5. Causes skin rashes

Dictamnus albus causes skin rashes due to its ability to release an irritating resinous sap.

This sap can come into contact with the skin, causing an allergic reaction and resulting in redness, itching, and blisters.

The chemical composition of the sap, including coumarin and other volatile oils, is thought to be responsible for this adverse effect, making it important for individuals handling or using Dictamnus albus to exercise caution to avoid direct contact with the plant.

6. Increases heart rate

Dictamnus albus increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

The plant's active compounds, such as scopoletin and dictammine, can cause an increase in blood pressure and cardiac output, leading to a faster heartbeat. This is because these compounds can directly affect the autonomic nervous system, which regulates various bodily functions, including heart rate and blood pressure.

As a result, individuals using Dictamnus albus may experience an elevated heart rate as a side effect.

7. Lowers blood pressure

Dictamnus albus lowers blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the vagus nerve, which is responsible for regulating heart rate and blood pressure.

The beta-blocker-like effects of Dictamnus albus can cause a decrease in heart rate and cardiac output, leading to a reduction in blood pressure.

This may be especially noticeable in individuals with pre-existing hypotension or those who are taking medications that also lower blood pressure.

8. Affects liver function

Dictamnus albus affects liver function due to its high content of alkaloids, particularly dictamine and citral.

These compounds can cause hepatotoxicity, leading to inflammation and damage to the liver cells.

The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it's believed that the alkaloids disrupt normal liver metabolism and may trigger an immune response, resulting in liver dysfunction.

9. Interferes with digestion

Dictamnus albus interferes with digestion due to its ability to stimulate the production of stomach acid and digestive enzymes.

This can lead to symptoms such as bloating, discomfort, and changes in bowel movements.

Additionally, the plant's high levels of glycosides and alkaloids may also contribute to digestive issues by irritating the mucous membranes lining the gastrointestinal tract, further disrupting normal digestion processes.

10. Increases sensitivity to light

Dictamnus albus increases sensitivity to light due to its chemical composition, which includes alkaloids and lignans.

These compounds can affect the body's natural response to light exposure, leading to increased sensitivity and potential discomfort.

As a result, individuals using Dictamnus albus may find themselves more susceptible to sunburn, eye strain, or other phototoxic reactions.
