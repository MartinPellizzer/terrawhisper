Scientific Classification

The botanical identification of Japanese Inula (Inula japonica) places it in the Plantae kingdom. Its family is Compositae, and it belongs to the genus Inula. The species is Inula japonica, with the broader classification details for division, class, and order not fully provided in the data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Japanese Inula is Inula japonica, first described by the botanist Carl Peter Thunberg in 1794. It is also known by several synonyms, including Inula hirta and Inula ciliata, which reflect historical taxonomic revisions. Common names include Japanese Inula, and it is referred to by regional names such as Yamabuki in Japan. Historically, it has been known by various local names in traditional herbal medicine practices.

Morphological Characteristics

Japanese Inula (Inula japonica) is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system. It has erect, branching stems that are covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have serrated edges. The plant produces a terminal inflorescence of yellow composite flowers. The fruits are small, dry achenes surrounded by bracts.

Diagnostic Identification Features

To distinguish Inula japonica from similar species, examine its grayish-brown, rough stems with longitudinal ridges, and its yellow-orange flower heads with radiating ligules. It has serrated, heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its aromatic scent and distinctive leaf venation also aid in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Inula japonica is native to Japan and parts of East Asia. It prefers temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. It is typically found at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Inula japonica blooms from July to August, producing yellow composite flowers. Seed formation occurs from September to October. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in late summer to early autumn when the flowers are fully open and the seeds are mature.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Inula japonica is closely related to species like Inula helenium and Inula britanica. It belongs to the genus Inula within the Asteraceae family. Its phylogenetic position is marked by shared morphological and genetic traits with other Inula species. Family traits such as composite flowers and ray florets support its classification.
