Gnaphalium pensylvanicum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gnaphalium pensylvanicum has an erect, branching stem that is covered in dense, white, woolly hairs and features small, alternate, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a silvery-white appearance due to the thick layer of trichomes coating the surface. These leaves are typically sessile and become more crowded toward the top of the plant. At the terminal ends of the branches, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flower heads arranged in terminal clusters. The overall structure is compact and herbaceous, giving the plant a soft, felt-like texture.
