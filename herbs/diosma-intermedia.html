What is Diosma intermedia?

Diosma intermedia is a species of plant in the family Rutaceae, native to the Western Cape province of South Africa.

It is a shrublet, growing up to 60 cm in height, with a woody base and leafy branches, the leaves being oppositely arranged and linear in shape, 2-10 mm long and 0.5-2 mm wide, with a distinct midrib and reticulate venation. The leaves are aromatic, bearing a distinctive fragrance when bruised, and are dark green in color. The plant produces small, white, hermaphroditic flowers in clusters, 5-8 mm in diameter, with 5 petals and a 5-lobed calyx.

The flowers are pollinated by insects and produce small, round, brown fruit 5-8 mm in diameter.
