What are the horticulural aspects of Paspalum urvillei?

Paspalum urvillei grow in tropical and subtropical regions, requiring full sun to partial shade, and well-drained soil with a pH between 5.5 and 7.0. It tolerates drought and can thrive in areas with high salt concentrations.

Planting tips for Paspalum urvillei include using fresh seed, sowing 1-2 inches deep, and 1-2 inches apart, in a nursery or direct sowing in fields. Fertilization is not required at the time of planting, but it can be applied 4-6 weeks later. Watering is essential during the first few weeks.

For harvesting Paspalum urvillei, wait until the grass reaches a height of 12-18 inches. Then, cut the plants at 3-4 inches from the ground, leaving a small portion for regrowth. Harvesting should be done when the plants are fully mature, to prevent regrowth from weakening the plant.

Common pests and diseases affecting Paspalum urvillei include nematodes, which cause root damage, and fungal infections such as powdery mildew and rust, which can be controlled with fungicides and good agricultural practices. Regular monitoring and prompt treatment can prevent damage and promote healthy growth.
