From a botanical standpoint, Paspalum urvillei is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 2 meters in height.

Historically, this herb has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, with early references dating back to the 17th century in European colonies in the Caribbean and South America.
