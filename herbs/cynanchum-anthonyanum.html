Cynanchum anthonyanum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum anthonyanum has climbing, twining stems that produce opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute apices. The foliage typically displays a smooth texture and a vibrant green coloration throughout the growing season. Small, actinomorphic flowers are arranged in complex umbel-like cymes, often featuring a distinct corona structure characteristic of the Apocynaceae family. These floral structures are frequently white or pale in hue and possess delicate, fleshy petals. The plant also develops elongated, follicular fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
