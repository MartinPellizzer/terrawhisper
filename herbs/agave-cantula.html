Agave cantula

What's the taxonomical classification of Agave cantula?

Agave cantula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Asparagales and the family Asparagaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by its genus, Agave, and its specific species designation, cantula.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Agave cantula has thick, succulent leaves that grow in a dense rosette pattern, characterized by a blue-green color and sharp, terminal spines at the tips. The leaf margins are lined with small, reddish teeth that provide a distinct textural contrast to the smooth surface of the foliage. This plant typically reaches a compact size, making it ideal for rock gardens or container cultivation. Its structural form is designed to minimize water loss through its fleshy, water-storing tissues. When mature, the plant may produce a tall flowering stalk that emerges from the center of the rosette.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid regions of central and northern Mexico, specifically occurring in states like San Luis Potosi and Querétaro. It thrives primarily in limestone-rich soils within desert scrublands and rocky slopes. The species is adapted to these specific microclimates where drainage is rapid and sunlight is intense. Its distribution is relatively localized compared to more widespread agave species. This restricted range makes its specific environmental requirements essential for its survival in the wild.
