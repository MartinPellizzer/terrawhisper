1. Prevents scurvy

Scurvy is a disease caused by a severe deficiency of vitamin C, leading to symptoms like fatigue, bleeding gums, and weakened immune function.

Also known as ascorbic acid deficiency, it occurs when the body lacks sufficient vitamin C to produce collagen, a vital component of skin, blood vessels, and connective tissue.

This condition historically affected sailors on long sea voyages who had limited access to fresh fruits and vegetables.

Acacia catechu plant has been traditionally used to prevent scurvy due to its high content of bioactive compounds that may support vitamin C levels and overall immune health.

How this herb helps with prevents scurvy?

Acacia catechu prevents scurvy because it contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids and alkaloids.

These compounds contribute to its high vitamin C content, which is essential for collagen synthesis and immune function. The presence of these nutrients helps prevent the deficiency that leads to scurvy. Additionally, the plant's phytochemical profile supports overall skin and tissue health.

Regular consumption of Acacia catechu may thus serve as a natural remedy for preventing scurvy.

Scientific Research

According to the study, Acacia catechu has been found to exhibit strong α-glucosidase inhibition, specifically the water fraction of Acacia catechu exhibited an IC50 of 9.0 ± 0.6 μg/mL, which may be related to its potential to prevent conditions associated with scurvy.

It is commonly experienced by individuals exposed to environmental pollutants, smokers, and those with poor dietary habits or chronic illnesses.
