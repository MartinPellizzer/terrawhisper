Catechu (Acacia catechu) Compounds

The primary compounds in Catechu include tannins, which contribute to its astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids and coumarin also play roles in its medicinal benefits, while glycosides and saponins may support anti-inflammatory actions. Terpenoids, alkaloids, essential oils, and anthocyanins further enhance its therapeutic potential.

Catechu (Acacia catechu) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Acacia catechu, a medicinal plant used in herbal medicine. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is valued for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins help in treating wounds, diarrhea, and skin conditions, making Acacia catechu a significant herb in traditional healing practices.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Catechu (Acacia catechu). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for skin conditions and digestive health. These compounds are key to the plant's therapeutic effects.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Catechu (Acacia catechu). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. This compound is widely used in traditional herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and digestive issues.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Catechu (Acacia catechu). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and as an astringent. Coumarin's pharmacological properties include antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects, making it valuable in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Catechu (Acacia catechu). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Glycosides in Catechu are often extracted and used in formulations for skin healing and digestive support.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Catechu (Acacia catechu). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. Saponins have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making them valuable in herbal medicine. They also help in the astringent and healing effects of the plant.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Acacia catechu. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds are responsible for many of the therapeutic effects attributed to catechu in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Acacia catechu, a medicinal plant used in traditional herbal medicine. It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Alkaloids in Catechu are valued for their potential therapeutic applications in treating various health conditions.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Catechu (Acacia catechu). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic benefits.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Catechu (Acacia catechu). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by acting as an antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent. Anthocyanin supports the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and digestive issues. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Catechu in herbal medicine.
