Catechu (Acacia catechu) Uses

Catechu is used to treat diarrhea, gastritis, and gastrointestinal ulcers due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bleeding and inflammation in the digestive tract, making it effective for conditions like gastric ulcers and inflammatory bowel disease. It also combats dental caries and gastrointestinal parasites through its antimicrobial effects.

Catechu (Acacia catechu) Monograph

1. Diarrhea

Catechu is used to treat diarrhea due to its astringent properties, which help reduce intestinal inflammation and firm stools. It is often prepared as a decoction or powder and is effective in cases of dysentery and infectious diarrhea. Its tannin content aids in healing gut lining and preventing further digestive issues.

2. Gastritis

Catechu is used in herbal medicine to treat gastritis due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce stomach lining irritation and excess mucus, promoting healing. Often prepared as a decoction or powder, it is valued for its ability to soothe digestive discomfort associated with gastritis.

3. Ulcer

Catechu is used to treat ulcers due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps in reducing gastric acid secretion and protecting the stomach lining. It is often prepared as a powder or decoction for internal use. Its ability to promote tissue repair makes it effective in healing ulcers.

4. Dental caries

Catechu is used in traditional medicine to treat dental caries due to its astringent and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce plaque and bacterial growth in the mouth. It is often applied as a mouthwash or used in formulations to strengthen teeth and prevent decay. Its ability to bind to dental surfaces also aids in remineralization.

5. Gastrointestinal inflammation

Catechu is used to treat gastrointestinal inflammation due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce mucosal irritation and promotes healing in conditions like gastritis and enteritis. Its tannins form a protective layer over the digestive tract lining. This makes it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for soothing and repairing gut inflammation.

6. Gastric ulcers

Catechu has been traditionally used to treat gastric ulcers due to its astringent properties, which help reduce inflammation and protect the stomach lining. It contains tannins that promote healing and prevent acid secretion, making it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for digestive tract conditions.

7. Gastrointestinal parasites

Catechu is used to treat gastrointestinal parasites due to its astringent and antiparasitic properties. It helps expel worms and other intestinal parasites by irritating the gut lining and promoting expulsion. It is often prepared as a decoction or powder for internal use. Its efficacy is supported by traditional use and some scientific studies.

8. Gastrointestinal bleeding

Catechu is used to treat gastrointestinal bleeding due to its astringent properties, which help to constrict blood vessels and promote clotting. It is often applied topically or taken internally in traditional formulations to reduce bleeding from ulcers, gastritis, or other digestive tract injuries. Its ability to strengthen mucous membranes also supports healing in the digestive system.

9. Gastrointestinal ulcers

Catechu has been traditionally used to treat gastrointestinal ulcers due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps in reducing gastric acid secretion and promoting tissue repair in the stomach lining. Its tannin content contributes to its healing effects on ulcers. It is often prepared as a decoction or powder for internal use.

10. Inflammatory bowel disease

Catechu has shown potential in managing inflammatory bowel disease due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps reduce intestinal inflammation and mucosal damage, supporting gut healing. Its astringent qualities may also aid in maintaining intestinal integrity. Clinical studies suggest it could be a complementary therapy for IBD management.
