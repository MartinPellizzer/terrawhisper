Botanical Identification of Catechu (Acacia catechu)

To identify Catechu (Acacia catechu) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Catechu (Acacia catechu) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Catechu, or Acacia catechu, belongs to the plant kingdom Plantae. It is part of the division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Acacia, and species Acacia catechu. This classification highlights its role within the legume family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Acacia catechu is the scientific name for Catechu, with the author citation L., referring to the botanist who first described it. It has several synonyms, including Acacia farnesiana and Acacia nilotica, though these are now considered different species. Common names include Indian sumac and catechu tree, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Catechu, or Acacia catechu, is a deciduous shrub or small tree. It has a taproot system with lateral roots, a slender stem with thorns, bipinnate leaves, yellowish inflorescences, small yellow flowers, and flat, pod-like fruits containing elongated seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Catechu, or Acacia catechu, can be distinguished by its pale green to yellowish bark, which peels in longitudinal strips. It has bipinnate leaves with 20-30 pairs of leaflets, and produces yellow flowers in globular clusters. The wood is heavy and dark, with a distinctive sweet, astringent smell when cut.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Catechu (Acacia catechu) is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly India, Sri Lanka, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in warm climates with high humidity and well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. This species is typically found at altitudes ranging from 200 to 1,200 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Catechu (Acacia catechu) blooms from February to April, producing yellow to orange flowers. Seed formation occurs from May to July, with pods developing and ripening by late summer. Harvesting is typically done in late summer to early autumn when the bark is most mature and contains the highest concentration of tannins.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Catechu (Acacia catechu) is closely related to species within the Acacia genus, sharing common traits such as thorny stems and pod fruits. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by nitrogen-fixing capabilities and compound leaves. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other legumes, influencing its chemical composition and medicinal uses.
