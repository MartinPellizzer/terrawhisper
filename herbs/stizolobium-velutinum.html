Stizolobium velutinum

Stizolobium velutinum, also known as the velvet bean or Stizolobium, is a perennial vine native to Central and South America.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties, primarily due to its high content of L-DOPA, a precursor to dopamine, which has been used to treat Parkinson's disease and other conditions related to dopamine deficiency.

The health benefits of Stizolobium velutinum include improved mood, reduced anxiety, and enhanced cognitive function, although it can also cause adverse effects such as nausea and vomiting.
