What's the taxonomical classification of Equisetum diffusum?

Equisetum diffusum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Equisetidae. Following its hierarchical classification further, it falls under the order Equisetales and the family Equisetaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Equisetum, and its specific species designation, diffusum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Equisetum diffusum has a perennial, rhizomatous growth habit characterized by hollow, jointed, and dichotomously branched green stems that lack true leaves. The vegetative stems are cylindrical and feature distinct nodes where small, scale-like leaves are fused into a sheath. These stems are highly silicified, giving the plant a rough, abrasive texture to the touch. Reproductive structures consist of specialized fertile stems that bear terminal sporangiophores containing spores. The plant lacks a traditional root system, instead utilizing underground rhizomes for nutrient storage and clonal expansion.
