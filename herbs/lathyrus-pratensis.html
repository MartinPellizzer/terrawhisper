Lathyrus pratensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lathyrus pratensis has slender, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and are characterized by being smooth and slightly angular. The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several pairs of narrow, lanceolate leaflets that terminate in a tendril used for climbing. Its flowers are pea-like and display a vibrant yellow color, often featuring a larger upper petal called a standard and smaller wing and keel petals. The inflorescence usually presents as a raceme containing several individual blooms emerging from the leaf axils. Following flowering, the plant produces small, flattened green pods that eventually turn brown as they mature and release seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to much of Europe, extending from the Mediterranean region in the south to Scandinavia in the north, and reaches into western Asia. It is commonly found inhabiting moist habitats such as meadows, marshes, and riverbanks across these diverse territories. In the United Kingdom and Ireland, it is widely distributed throughout the warmer and more temperate parts of the islands. The species also appears in various parts of North Africa and the Middle East where suitable damp conditions exist. Its range is largely defined by the presence of consistent moisture in the soil.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lathyrus pratensis can cause gastrointestinal distress, nausea, vomiting, and potential neurological impairment if consumed in significant quantities due to the presence of non-protein amino acids. These toxic compounds, specifically beta-N-oxalyl-L-alpha,beta-diaminopropionic acid, interfere with normal protein synthesis in the body. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic in small amounts for most animals, concentrated ingestion can lead to more serious complications. Regular consumption by livestock may result in permanent nerve damage or motor dysfunction. It is important to identify this species correctly to avoid accidental poisoning during foraging.
