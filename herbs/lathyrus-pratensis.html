Lathyrus pratensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Lathyrus pratensis?

Lathyris pratensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Lathyris, specifically identified by the species name pratensis.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Lathyrus pratensis can cause gastrointestinal distress, nausea, vomiting, and potential neurological impairment if consumed in significant quantities due to the presence of non-protein amino acids. These toxic compounds, specifically beta-N-oxalyl-L-alpha,beta-diaminopropionic acid, interfere with normal protein synthesis in the body. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic in small amounts for most animals, concentrated ingestion can lead to more serious complications. Regular consumption by livestock may result in permanent nerve damage or motor dysfunction. It is important to identify this species correctly to avoid accidental poisoning during foraging.
