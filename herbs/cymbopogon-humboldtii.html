Cymbopogon humboldtii

Cymbopogon humboldtii, also known as lemongrass, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America, characterized by its long, slender leaves and citrusy fragrance.

The medicinal properties of Cymbopogon humboldtii lie in its essential oils, which contain compounds like citral and geraniol, offering antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antifungal properties.

The health benefits of lemongrass include reducing stress and anxiety, improving digestion, and relieving pain and fever, making it a popular herbal remedy in traditional medicine.

Cymbopogon humboldtii is commonly used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and essential oils, which can be applied topically or ingested to harness its medicinal properties.
