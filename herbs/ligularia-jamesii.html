Ligularia jamesii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia jamesii?

Ligularia jamesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further classified under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is part of the genus Ligularia, and is specifically identified by its species name, jamesii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia jamesii has large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically serrated along the margins and possess a textured, slightly hairy surface.

These basal leaves grow on long petioles that emerge from a sturdy, rhizomatous rootstock. During the flowering season, the plant produces tall, erect stems that can reach significant heights. At the apex of these stems, clusters of bright yellow ray florets form dense, composite flower heads.

The overall structure provides a lush, spreading appearance suitable for moist woodland environments.
