Saussurea hieracioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea hieracioides?

Saussurea hieracioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned in the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling under the genus Saussurea, with the specific species designation hieracioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea hieracioides has a basal rosette of lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically hairy and possess entire or slightly dentate margins.

The flowering stems are erect and can reach significant heights, often bearing several small, yellow flower heads in a panicle-like arrangement. Each flower head consists of numerous tiny yellow florets enclosed by several rows of overlapping phyllaries. The leaves transition from larger, more robust forms at the base to smaller, narrower bracts along the stem.

This plant's overall structure is characteristic of many high-altitude aster species found in alpine environments.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Saussurea hieracioides can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. While specific clinical data on human toxicity is limited, members of the Asteraceae family often contain sesquiterpene lactones that trigger sensitivities. Individuals with known allergies to ragweed or daisies may experience localized swelling or itching upon contact with the plant. Ingesting concentrated extracts might lead to nausea or stomach cramps in sensitive populations. It is essential to exercise caution when handling this species due to these potential biological reactions.
