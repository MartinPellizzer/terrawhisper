Saussurea hieracioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea hieracioides has a basal rosette of lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically hairy and possess entire or slightly dentate margins. The flowering stems are erect and can reach significant heights, often bearing several small, yellow flower heads in a panicle-like arrangement. Each flower head consists of numerous tiny yellow florets enclosed by several rows of overlapping phyllaries. The leaves transition from larger, more robust forms at the base to smaller, narrower bracts along the stem. This plant's overall structure is characteristic of many high-altitude aster species found in alpine environments.

Its presence is most frequent in calcareous soils found at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.
