Betula humilis var. vulgaris

Betula humilis var. vulgaris, also known as dwarf birch, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub that belongs to the Betulaceae family.

The bark and leaves of the dwarf birch have been used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and astringent properties, which make them useful for treating a range of health issues, including wounds, skin conditions, and gastrointestinal problems.

The medicinal properties of dwarf birch are attributed to the presence of betulin, a triterpenoid compound that has been shown to have antimicrobial and antioxidant activities, contributing to its health benefits, such as reducing inflammation and improving immune function.

Dwarf birch can be prepared into various herbal remedies, including infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, which can be used topically or internally to harness its medicinal properties.
