What's the taxonomical classification of Leontopodium leontopodioides?

Leontopodium leontopodioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, which places it among the composite flowers, and it is specifically categorized under the genus Leontopodium with the species designation leontopodioides.

This specific tremetone derivative is often studied for its unique chemical structure and potential biological activities. Researchers examine these compounds to understand how they interact with various cellular pathways in the body. Detailed chemical analysis helps scientists map the concentration of these substances within different parts of the plant.

    As discussed in a study, the aerial parts of Leontopodium leontopodioides contain two new compounds, a neolignan (1) and a benzofuran derivative (2), along with 6 known compounds (3-8). The neolignan was identified as (7R,8S)-3,5'-dimethoxy-4',7-epoxy-8,3'-neolignane-5,9,9'-triol (1) and the benzofuran derivative was identified as (2R)-12-hydoxy-4-methoxy-tremeton (2). These isolates were evaluated for their ability to inhibit nitric oxide production in lipopolysaccharide-activated RAW264.7 macrophages. Among the isolates, compounds 1 and 8 exhibited inhibitory activity. Compound 1 had an IC50 value of 35.80 μM, while compound 8 had an IC50 value of 24.41 μM.
