Sonchus oleraceus

What's the taxonomical classification of Sonchus oleraceus?

Sonchus oleraceus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it falls under the genus Sonchus, and its specific scientific designation is the species oleraceus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Sonchus oleraceus contains bioactive secondary metabolites including sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and various polysaccharides. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's leaves and stems. The sesquiterpene lactones are particularly noted for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Additionally, the presence of flavonoids contributes antioxidant activity that may assist in neutralizing free radicals. Researchers often study these specific compounds to understand their therapeutic implications in traditional medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Sonchus oleraceus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various digestive ailments, inflammatory skin conditions, and respiratory issues. Its bitter compounds are traditionally employed to stimulate digestion and alleviate constipation. The plant also serves as a topical remedy for soothing minor skin irritations and swelling. Additionally, some herbal traditions utilize its properties to help clear congestion in the lungs. These therapeutic applications are rooted in its diverse phytochemical profile.
