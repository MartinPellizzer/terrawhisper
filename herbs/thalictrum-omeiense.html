Thalictrum omeiense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. According to a study published by "Journal of Asian natural products research", the roots of Thalictrum omeiense contain two new apoarphine alkaloids, 6aR-2'-(3-oxobutenyl)-thaliadin (1) and N-methylthalisopynine (2), as well as ten known analogs (3-12). Compounds 1-7 and 9-12 were tested for their antiproliferative effects in vitro against two human cancer cell lines, A549 and MCF-7.
