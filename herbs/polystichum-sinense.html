Polystichum sinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Polystichum sinense?

Polystichum sinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Polypodiinae and falls under the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Polypodiaceae, which contains the genus Polystichum, and is finally identified at the species level as sinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polystichum sinense has pinnate fronds that measure up to 1 meter in length, featuring dark green, leathery pinnae with serrated margins and a distinctively glossy texture. The individual leaflets are lanceolate in shape and are arranged symmetrically along a central, sturdy stipe. Each pinna often displays a slightly wavy edge that adds to the complex structural appearance of the foliage. On the underside of the fronds, small, kidney-shaped sori are arranged in neat rows to facilitate spore production. This morphological structure allows the plant to thrive in shaded, moist forest environments.

What side effects this plant can have?
