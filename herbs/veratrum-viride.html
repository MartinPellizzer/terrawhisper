Veratrum Viride

Veratrum viride, commonly known as green hellebore, is a toxic plant native to northern regions of North America and parts of Asia, historically used for its potent medicinal properties.

It contains alkaloids such as veratridine and jervine, which have been employed in traditional medicine for their effects on the cardiovascular and nervous systems. In the past, it was used to treat conditions like hypertension and heart failure due to its ability to strengthen heart contractions and lower blood pressure. However, due to its high toxicity, it is no longer used in modern medicine and is considered a dangerous plant to handle without proper expertise.

Despite its risks, research into its alkaloids continues to explore potential therapeutic applications with controlled dosages.
