What's the taxonomical classification of Citrus × limon?

Citrus × limon belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae, placing it within the genus Citrus. Finally, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species name, limon.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Citrus × limon has evergreen, woody stems characterized by small thorns, elliptical leaves with serrated margins, and yellow, globose fruits covered in a thick, oil-rich rind. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, often displaying a winged petiole that is characteristic of the citrus genus. Its flowers are white, fragrant, and pentamerous, typically appearing in small clusters. The fruit, known as a lemon, develops from an inferior ovary and contains numerous juice-filled vesicles within its pulp. Mature specimens can grow into large shrubs or small trees depending on the environmental conditions and cultivation methods used.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Farmers frequently grow these trees in areas that avoid prolonged freezing temperatures to ensure consistent fruit production. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause dental erosion, heartburn, acid reflux, nausea, stomach upset, and mouth irritation due to its high citric acid content. Consuming large amounts of the fruit may lead to the thinning of tooth enamel, which results in dental erosion.
