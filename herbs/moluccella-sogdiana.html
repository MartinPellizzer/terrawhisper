What is Moluccella sogdiana?

Moluccella sogdiana is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae, native to Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It has a height of 1-2 meters, with a woody base and a branched stem bearing oppositely arranged, lance-shaped leaves that are 4-8 cm long and 1-2 cm wide. The leaves are glabrous, with a crenulate margin and a short petiole. Moluccella sogdiana produces terminal spikes of small, fragrant, blue flowers in summer, which are 5-6 mm long and 4-5 mm wide, and are arranged in a condensed inflorescence.

The flowers are hermaphroditic, with a 2-lipped corolla and four stamens.
