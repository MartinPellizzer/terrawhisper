Lilium fargesii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium fargesii has erect stems reaching up to 1.5 meters in height that bear several large, nodding, trumpet-shaped flowers ranging from pale yellow to deep orange. The leaves are arranged in whorls along the upper portion of the stem and feature an lanceolate shape with acute tips. Each flower typically possesses six tepals that are often marked with dark spots or speckles near the center. The base of the tepals usually transitions into a greenish or lighter shade compared to the outer edges. This plant is characterized by its robust structural form and striking floral presentation in mountainous habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and western China, specifically occurring in provinces such as Sichuan, Hubei, and Gansu. It typically inhabits high-altitude environments found within temperate forest zones. These lilies thrive in the moist, shaded soils of montane slopes and woodland margins. Their distribution is largely restricted to these specific topographical features of the East Asian landscape. Consequently, the species is localized to certain ecological niches within its primary range.

Lilium fargesii is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried bulbs in water or as a powder that is mixed into medicinal formulas. What side-effects this plant can have?

Lilium fargesii can cause gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain if consumed improperly. While some traditional practices utilize the plant, there is a lack of extensive clinical data regarding its long-term toxicity in humans. Some users may experience allergic reactions such as skin rashes or respiratory irritation upon direct contact. It is important to note that many species within the Lilium genus are highly toxic to cats and other domestic animals. Always consult a medical professional before using any wild plant for medicinal purposes to avoid potential poisoning.
