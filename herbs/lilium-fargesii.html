What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium fargesii?

Lilium fargesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Liliales. It is a member of the family Liliaceae, which is further narrowed down to the genus Lilium, with the specific designation of the species fargesii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium fargesii has erect stems reaching up to 1.5 meters in height that bear several large, nodding, trumpet-shaped flowers ranging from pale yellow to deep orange. The leaves are arranged in whorls along the upper portion of the stem and feature an lanceolate shape with acute tips. Each flower typically possesses six tepals that are often marked with dark spots or speckles near the center. The base of the tepals usually transitions into a greenish or lighter shade compared to the outer edges. This plant is characterized by its robust structural form and striking floral presentation in mountainous habitats.
