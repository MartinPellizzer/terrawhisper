Sarcopyramis bodinieri

What's the taxonomical classification of Sarcopyramis bodinieri?

Sarcopyramis bodinieri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Myrtales. More specifically, this plant is a part of the family Melastomataceae. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Sarcopyramis, with its specific identification being the species bodinieri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sarcopyramis bodinieri has a single, fleshy, unbranched stem that terminates in a distinctive pyramidal inflorescence composed of numerous small, brownish-red flowers.

The plant lacks true leaves, instead featuring several large, basal, deeply lobed leaves that emerge directly from a thick rhizome. These leaves are typically leathery in texture and display a complex, pinnatifid structure designed to capture light in shaded environments. The inflorescence is characterized by a dense cluster of flowers that create a cone-like shape atop the central stalk.

This unique morphology allows the plant to thrive in the specific ecological niches of its native habitat.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Sarcopyramis bodinieri can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, the plant contains chemical compounds that may trigger inflammatory responses upon contact with sensitive skin. Exposure to the sap or foliage might result in redness, itching, or a burning sensation in affected areas. If accidental consumption occurs, individuals may experience nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting. It is advisable to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse physiological reactions.
