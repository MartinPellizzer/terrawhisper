What's the taxonomical classification of Amphimas ferrugineus?

Amphimas ferrugineus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized into the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, falling under the genus Amphimas, and is specifically identified by the species name ferrugineus.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Amphimas ferrugineus can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and potential allergic reactions upon direct physical contact. The plant contains chemical compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If the sap touches the skin, it often results in redness and an intense itching sensation. Some people might experience blistering or persistent rashes if the exposure is prolonged. It is important to avoid handling this species without protective gloves to prevent these adverse physiological responses.
