Chrysobalanus icaco belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Chrysobalanaeceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Chrysobalanus, with its specific designation being the species icaco.

    As discussed in a study, Chrysobalanus icaco is a medicinal plant used to treat fungal infections in the Brazilian Amazonian region. The hydroalcoholic extract of Chrysobalanus icaco, known as HECi, contains a high amount of flavonoid content measured at 132,959.33 ± 12,598.23 μg quercetin equivalent/g of extract. The antifungal activity of HECi against 11 oral clinical isolates was evaluated, showing minimum inhibitory concentrations of 3.12 mg/mL for Candida albicans and 6.25 mg/mL for Candida parapsilosis. Additionally, HECi demonstrated activity against the Candida albicans American Type Culture Collection with a minimum inhibitory concentration of less than 1.56 mg/mL.
