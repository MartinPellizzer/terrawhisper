What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone dichotoma has a perennial rootstock that produces leaves which are divided into several narrow, linear segments. The plant structure is characterized by its dichotomous branching pattern, where stems split into two equal parts at each node. Its flowers typically feature multiple petals or petaloid sepals that surround a central cluster of numerous stamens. The foliage is often arranged in basal clusters, providing a dense ground cover during the growing season.
