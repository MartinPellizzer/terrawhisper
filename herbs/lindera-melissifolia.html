What is Lindera melissifolia?

Lindera melissifolia is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Lauraceae.

It is an evergreen shrub or small tree that grows up to 3 meters in height, with a rounded crown and a straight or slightly curved trunk. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, measuring 5-12 centimeters long and 2-4 centimeters wide, with a leathery texture and a cuneate base. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow in color, and are arranged in axillary cymes, with the male flowers appearing before the female flowers.

The plant typically flowers in late spring, and the fruit is a drupe, 1-1.5 centimeters long, with a single seed.
