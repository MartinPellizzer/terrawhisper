Tylophora rotundifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tylophora rotundifolia has climbing, herbaceous stems with opposite, orbicular to ovate leaves that possess entire margins and acute apices. The foliage is characterized by a smooth, glabrous surface and a relatively thin texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, typically displaying a pale or greenish hue. These blooms are arranged in small clusters or solitary configurations along the vine. The plant structure supports a sprawling growth habit suitable for climbing over surrounding vegetation.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across India, China, Thailand, and parts of Indochina. It thrives primarily in moist, shaded environments such as forest edges and secondary jungles. The species is frequently found growing as a climber in low-elevation habitats where humidity is consistently high. Its distribution follows a pattern typical of many members of the Apocynaceae family within the Oriental kingdom. Localized populations are often concentrated in areas with rich, well-drained soil.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Tylophora rotundifolia targets various signaling pathways and enzymes, primarily through the inhibition of cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes and the modulation of pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha). The plant contains diverse bioactive alkaloids and glycosides that interact with protein kinases to regulate cellular inflammation and immune responses. Furthermore, specific constituents may interact with adenosine receptors or other G protein-coupled receptors to mediate its sedative or analgesic properties. Such multifaceted protein interactions underpin the plant's traditional uses in treating inflammatory conditions and pain.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tylophora rotundifolia?

Tylophora rotundifolia has therapeutic actions characterized by its bitter, astringent, anti-inflammatory, anti-pyretic, emetic, and alterative properties.
