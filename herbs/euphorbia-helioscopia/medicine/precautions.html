When using Euphorbia helioscopia medicinally, it's important to take by prescription only.

Without proper medical supervision, individuals may not be able to accurately assess their body's response to the treatment, which can lead to serious consequences or interactions with other medications.

Additionally, Euphorbia helioscopia may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes drugs, requiring close monitoring by a healthcare professional.

2. Store out of children's reach

When using Euphorbia helioscopia medicinally, it's important to avoid during pregnancy and breastfeeding.

This is crucial because the plant contains latex sap that can cause contractions in the uterus and potentially lead to miscarriage or premature labor. Additionally, the saponins present in the plant may pass through breast milk, affecting infant development and potentially causing harm.

By taking this precaution, pregnant and breastfeeding women can ensure their safety and the well-being of their child.
