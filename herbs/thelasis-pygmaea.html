Thelasis pygmaea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thelasis pygmaeae has small, slender stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 25 centimeters and bear several small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are narrow, lanceolate, or linear in shape and are arranged alternately along the stem. Each flower features a characteristic three-lobed lip that is often slightly larger than the other petals. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its overall structure is delicate and diminutive, reflecting its specialized niche in forest undergrowth.
