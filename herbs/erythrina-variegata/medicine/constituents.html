10 Medicinal Constituents Of Erythrina Variegata (Coral Tree)

Erythrina variegata has active constituents such as alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids.

These constituents possess medicinal properties that have been traditionally used to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. For instance, the plant's flavonoids exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities, which can help alleviate symptoms of arthritis and improve cardiovascular health.

The alkaloids, meanwhile, show potential as antiparasitic agents, providing relief from intestinal worms and other parasitic infections.

Erythrina variegata strychnine is a toxic alkaloid extracted from the seeds of this plant.

It is a powerful natural poison that was historically used in traditional medicine and as a rat poison. Strychnine is a highly potent and dangerous substance, with a LD50 value of 1-2 mg/kg, making it one of the most toxic substances known.

Its use is highly restricted due to its extreme toxicity and risk of accidental ingestion.
