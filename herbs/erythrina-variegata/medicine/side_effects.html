1. Causes allergic reactions

Erythrina variegata causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high concentration of allergenic compounds such as flavonoids and alkaloids.

These bioactive molecules can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like skin irritation, itching, redness, and swelling. The allergenic potential is further exacerbated by the presence of latex-like proteins, which are commonly found in the plant's sap and tissues.

As a result, exposure to Erythrina variegata can elicit an adverse reaction in some people with sensitivities.

2. Triggers anxiety attacks

Erythrina variegata triggers anxiety attacks in some individuals.

The flavonoids and alkaloids present in the plant are believed to contribute to this adverse effect, as they can alter the body's neurotransmitter levels and disrupt normal brain function. Specifically, the compounds may interfere with the regulation of serotonin, a neurotransmitter that plays a crucial role in mood regulation and anxiety response.

As a result, users of Erythrina variegata may experience increased feelings of apprehension and unease.

3. Induces cardiac arrhythmia

Erythrina variegata induces cardiac arrhythmia due to its toxic compounds, such as alkaloids and glycosides.

These substances can interfere with the normal functioning of the heart's electrical activity, disrupting the regular heartbeat and potentially leading to abnormal rhythms or palpitations.

The exact mechanisms by which these toxins exert their effects on the cardiovascular system are not fully understood, but it is thought that they may alter the heart's ion channels or bind to specific receptors, leading to changes in cardiac conduction.

4. Produces dizziness spells

Erythrina variegata produces dizziness spells due to its chemical composition.

The alkaloids present in the plant, particularly erythrinal and erysodine, can cause a significant drop in blood pressure, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness.

Additionally, the sedative properties of these alkaloids may further contribute to the development of dizziness spells.

5. Increases blood pressure

Erythrina variegata increases blood pressure due to its stimulatory effect on the sympathetic nervous system.

The alkaloids present in the plant, particularly erythroidine and sarpagan, bind to nicotinic receptors in the brain and spinal cord, leading to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output, subsequently elevating blood pressure.

This mechanism is thought to be responsible for the hypertensive response observed in individuals using Erythrina variegata.

6. Lowers blood sugar levels

Erythrina variegata lowers blood sugar levels by stimulating insulin secretion and increasing glucose uptake in muscles.

Its alkaloids, particularly erythrinalgin and flavonoids, are responsible for this effect. These compounds bind to insulin receptors, mimicking the action of insulin and facilitating glucose transport into cells.

As a result, Erythrina variegata can cause a significant decrease in blood sugar levels, potentially leading to hypoglycemia.

7. Affects cognitive function

Erythrina variegata affects cognitive function due to its chemical composition, which includes alkaloids and flavonoids.

These compounds have been found to interfere with the normal functioning of neurotransmitters in the brain, leading to difficulties in concentration, memory, and decision-making.

The exact mechanisms by which Erythrina variegata exerts this effect are not yet fully understood, but research suggests that its bioactive compounds may interact with receptors involved in cognitive processing, resulting in alterations to neural activity patterns.

8. Disrupts digestive system

Erythrina variegata disrupts digestive system, which may be attributed to its saponin and alkaloid content.

These compounds can cause gastrointestinal disturbances such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach pain when ingested.

The anthraquinone glycosides present in the plant can also stimulate bowel movements, leading to laxative effects that may further contribute to digestive issues.

9. Causes excessive sweating

Erythrina variegata causes excessive sweating in some individuals.

This is due to its active compound, erythravine, which can stimulate the nervous system and increase sweat production.

Additionally, Erythrina variegata's ability to relax smooth muscle tissue may further contribute to increased perspiration as it reduces overall bodily tension.

10. Increases urinary frequency

Erythrina variegata increases urinary frequency due to its diuretic properties.

The saponins present in the plant extract have been shown to stimulate the kidneys, leading to an increase in urine production and frequency of urination.

This effect is likely caused by the plant's ability to enhance the excretion of water and electrolytes from the body, resulting in a increased need for frequent urination.
