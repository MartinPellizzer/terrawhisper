Botanical Identification of Variegated Erythrina (Erythrina variegata)

To identify Variegated Erythrina (Erythrina variegata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Variegated Erythrina, or Erythrina variegata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Erythrina, and species Erythrina variegata. This classification places it within the legume family, known for its nitrogen-fixing properties and medicinal uses.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Variegated Erythrina is Erythrina variegata, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Erythrina variegata L. and Erythrina variegata Linnaeus. Common names such as "Variegated Coral Tree" and "Rain Tree" are widely used, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Variegated Erythrina is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a shallow, fibrous root system. Its stem is straight, often branching from the base, and covered with smooth, greyish bark. Leaves are alternate, pinnate, and have three to five leaflets with serrated edges. Inflorescences are terminal panicles, bearing clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The fruit is a flat, woody pod that splits open to release elongated, kidney-shaped seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Variegated Erythrina can be distinguished by its distinctive variegated leaves with alternating green and white patterns, along with its bright orange-red flowers. It differs from Erythrina indica by having more pronounced leaf variegation and smaller, more clustered flowers. The seeds are also larger and have a more elongated shape compared to other species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Erythrina variegata is native to Southeast Asia, India, and parts of Africa. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is typically found at low altitudes, usually below 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Variegated Erythrina blooms from March to June, producing vibrant red and orange flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For herbal identification, seeds are harvested in late autumn when they are fully mature and dry.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Erythrina variegata belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is closely related to other Erythrina species. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with species like Erythrina indica and Erythrina crista-galli. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its close ties within the genus and broader legume family.
