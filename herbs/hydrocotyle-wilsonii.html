What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrocotyle wilsonii?

Hydrocotyle wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its order, Apiales, the plant is further assigned to the family Araliaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed through its classification within the genus Hydrocotyle, specifically as the species wilsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrocotyle wilsonii has small, circular to kidney-shaped leaves that measure between 5 and 15 millimeters in diameter and possess crenate or scalloped margins.

These leaves are typically bright green and are borne on slender, creeping petioles that emerge from a rhizomatous stem. The plant exhibits a prostrate growth habit, forming dense mats of foliage close to the ground. Its reproductive structures consist of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in small umbels.

The overall structure allows the plant to spread effectively across moist soil or shallow aquatic environments.
