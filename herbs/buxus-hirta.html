Buxus hirta

Buxus hirta, also known as the hairy boxwood or boxwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Buxaceae.

It is a small shrub native to China and India, known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant has been used to treat various health issues, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

In herbal preparations, the leaves and roots of Buxus hirta are often used to make teas, tinctures, and infusions, which can be consumed to reap its health benefits, including reducing inflammation and promoting skin health.
