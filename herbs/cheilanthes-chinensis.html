Cheilanthes chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cheilanthes chinensis?

Cheilanthes chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Polypodiinae and falls under the order Polypodiales. This fern is a member of the family Pteridaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Cheilanthes, and is identified by its specific species name, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cheilanthes chinensis has pinnate fronds characterized by deeply lobed or dissected segments that are often fleshy and leathery in texture.

The leaves frequently exhibit a somewhat coriaceous habit with margins that may be entire or slightly dentate depending on the specific specimen. The stipe is typically dark or blackish and possesses a distinctively smooth, somewhat thickened appearance. On the underside of the blades, the sori are arranged in discrete clusters located near the margins or along the veins.

These morphological features allow the fern to thrive in humid, shaded environments where moisture is consistent.
