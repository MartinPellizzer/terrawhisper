Cheilanthes chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cheilanthes chinensis?

Cheilanthes chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Polypodiinae and falls under the order Polypodiales. This fern is a member of the family Pteridaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Cheilanthes, and is identified by its specific species name, chinensis.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cheilanthes chinensis targets several key inflammatory and oxidative enzymes, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and inducible nitric oxide synthase to reduce systemic inflammation. Its bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and triterpenoids, interact with these proteins to suppress the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like prostaglandins and nitric oxide. Furthermore, the plant exhibits antioxidant activity by modulating the expression of antioxidant enzymes including superoxide dismutase and catalase. These molecular interactions help mitigate cellular damage caused by reactive oxygen species during oxidative stress. Through these specific protein interactions, the plant exerts its observed anti-inflammatory and cytoprotective medicinal effects.
