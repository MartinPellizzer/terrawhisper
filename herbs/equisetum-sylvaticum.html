Equisetum sylvaticum

What's the taxonomical classification of Equisetum sylvaticum?

Equisetum sylvaticum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Equisetidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Equisetales and the family Equisetaceae. Finally, it is situated within the genus Equisetum, specifically identified by the species name sylvaticum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Equisetum sylvaticum has a perennial, underground rhizome system that produces upright, photosynthetic, unbranched stems characterized by distinct, ring-like nodes and whorls of small, scale-like leaves. These stems are hollow and ribbed, reaching heights typically between ten and thirty centimeters. The nodes feature small, dark, triangular bracts that protect the emerging tissue at each joint. During the reproductive phase, the plant produces specialized fertile stems that bear terminal sporangiophores containing spores. These fertile structures appear separately from the sterile, green vegetative stems to facilitate wind dispersal.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant grows best in consistently moist to wet, acidic soils within partial shade or dappled sunlight environments.

Because it spreads via underground rhizomes, it requires contained planting areas like large pots or dedicated garden beds to prevent it from becoming invasive. You should maintain high humidity and avoid letting the substrate dry out completely during the growing season. Regular watering ensures the vascular structure remains robust and vibrant.

Once established, the species is quite hardy and can tolerate various temperate climates.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Equisetum sylvaticum contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its aerial stems, which are harvested for their high silica content and diuretic properties.
