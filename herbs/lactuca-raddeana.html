What's the taxonomical classification of Lactuca raddeana?

Lactuca raddeana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic placement continues through the order Asterales and into the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Lactuca, and its unique species designation, raddeana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lactuca raddeana has a taprooted herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are often oblong or lanceolate with dentate margins and upright, branching flowering stems. The leaves are typically sparsely distributed along the stem and possess a somewhat glaucous or greenish appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellow flower heads arranged in corymbose or paniculate clusters at the terminal ends of the branches. Each individual head contains numerous tubular florets typical of the Asteraceae family. The overall structure is slender and adapted to the rocky or montane environments where it is commonly found.
