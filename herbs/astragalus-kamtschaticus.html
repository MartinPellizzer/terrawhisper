Astragalus Kamtschaticus

Astragalus kamtschaticus, commonly known as the Caucasian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, including parts of Russia and Georgia.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported immune-boosting properties, attributed to its high content of flavonoids, saponins, and polysaccharides. In modern herbal medicine, it is often employed to support respiratory health, particularly in the treatment of chronic bronchitis and other inflammatory conditions. The plant's roots are typically harvested and dried for use in tinctures, decoctions, or capsules.

Due to its adaptogenic qualities, Astragalus kamtschaticus is also believed to help the body resist stress and enhance overall vitality.
