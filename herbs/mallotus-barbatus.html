Mallotus barbatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Mallotus barbatus?

Mallotus barbatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Malpighiales. This plant is further categorized under the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically within the genus Mallotus, and is identified by its unique species designation, barbaptus.

What are the pharmacological activities of Mallotus barbatus?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. Its bioactive compounds work to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators in the body. These chemical constituents also help neutralize free radicals to prevent oxidative stress. Furthermore, the plant exhibits the ability to suppress the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Such diverse therapeutic effects make it a subject of interest for developing new medicinal treatments.

As discussed in a study, the bark of Mallotus barbatus contains a new megastigmane glycoside, barbatcoside A (1), and two new phenol glycosides, barbatcoside B (2) and C (3), alongside eight known compounds (4-11). The anti-inflammatory activities of these compounds (1-11) from Mallotus barbatus were evaluated using LPS-stimulated RAW 264.7 cell models. Among these, compounds 2 and 3 showed the ability to substantially inhibit the release of NO. Specifically, compound 2 demonstrated an IC50 value of 34.78 μM. Compound 3 exhibited an IC50 value of 20.73 μM.
