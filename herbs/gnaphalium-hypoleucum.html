Gnaphalium hypoleucum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gnaphaliuum hypoleucum has a perennial, woody-based habit characterized by silver-grey, tomentose foliage and small, discoid flower heads arranged in dense terminal clusters. The leaves are typically linear or lanceolate, covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs that give the plant its distinctive dusty appearance. Its stems are often branched and may become somewhat woody at the base as the plant matures. The inflorescences consist of numerous small, yellowish-white flower heads that lack ray florets. This hairy texture serves as an adaptation to reduce moisture loss in its native environments.

What are the herbal preparations of Gnaphalium hypoleucum?

Gnaphalium hypoleucum is prepared as infusions, decoctions, washes, and poultices for various therapeutic uses. What side-effects this plant can have?

Gnaphalium hypoleucum can cause contact dermatitis, allergic skin reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may act as irritants when touched by sensitive skin. Some individuals might experience redness, itching, or swelling in the area of contact. If consumed accidentally, it could lead to nausea or stomach upset depending on the amount swallowed. Caution is advised when handling this species to avoid these adverse physical responses.
