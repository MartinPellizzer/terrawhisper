Elaeagnus umbellata

What's the taxonomical classification of Elaeagnus umbellata?

Elaeagnus umbellata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Elaeagnaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Elaeagnus, and its specific species designation, umbellata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elaeagnus umbellata has an evergreen growth habit characterized by spreading, rounded crowns and small, leathery, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green upper surface with a paler, slightly pubescent underside. Its branches are woody and can develop a somewhat irregular or weeping structure as the tree matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually giving way to fleshy, drupe-like fruits that turn reddish or brownish when ripe. The bark is generally smooth and greyish, providing a subtle texture to the trunk and larger limbs.

What are the pharmacological activities of Elaeagnus umbellata?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antibacterial, antimicrobial, and hepatoprotective pharmacological activities. The presence of various bioactive compounds allows it to effectively neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. It also demonstrates significant potential in inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, its properties help protect liver cells from chemical or toxin-induced damage. These diverse therapeutic effects make it a subject of great interest in medicinal research.

According to a study published by "Cell biochemistry and biophysics", Elaeagnuus umbellata exhibits anti-ulcer, antimutagenic, antimicrobial, and neuroprotective properties. The leaves of this plant also demonstrate anti-inflammatory and anticancer effects. Regarding antibacterial potential, the extract produced a 25 mm zone of inhibition against Pseudomonas Aeruginosa 25619 and a 26 mm zone of inhibition against Enterococcus faecalis 29212. While no inhibition was noted for Klebsiella pneumonia 43816, the standard imipenem showed an average of 34 mm zone of inhibition. Fourteen compounds were identified in the crude methanolic extract of the leaves. This extract also possesses substantial hepatoprotective properties against CCl4-induced liver toxicity in a dose-dependent manner.

These results indicate that the crude Met-Ext of Elaeagnus umbellata exhibits considerable hepatoprotective effects in a dose-dependent manner.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing diabetes mellitus, providing hepatoprotective effects, offering hypolipidemic effects, exerting nephroprotective effects, and reducing oxidative stress. Its bioactive compounds work to stabilize blood glucose levels in individuals suffering from metabolic disorders. The plant also helps protect the liver from chemical damage and lowers unhealthy lipid concentrations in the bloodstream. Furthermore, its antioxidant properties mitigate damage caused by oxidative stress in vital organs. These combined actions contribute to the overall protection of kidney function against various stressors.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Elaeagnus umbellata Thunb. is a medicinal plant used for the management of stress related disorders like diabetes and other health complications. The plant's isolated compound morin shows potential to treat diabetes mellitus and bacterial infections. In a streptozotocin-induced diabetic rat model, morin was tested at doses of 2, 10, 15, 30 and 50 mg/kg body weight. These doses significantly restored fasting blood glucose levels and body weight loss while decreasing cholesterol, triglycerides, low density lipoprotein, and HbA1c levels. Furthermore, the plant extracts help improve renal functions like serum creatinine and increase antioxidant enzyme activities such as GPx and GSH content.
