Elaeagnus umbellata has an evergreen growth habit characterized by spreading, rounded crowns and small, leathery, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length. Its branches are woody and can develop a somewhat irregular or weeping structure as the tree matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually giving way to fleshy, drupe-like fruits that turn reddish or brownish when ripe. The bark is generally smooth and greyish, providing a subtle texture to the trunk and larger limbs.
