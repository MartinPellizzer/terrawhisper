Silver Bush (Elaeagnus umbellata)

Silver Bush (Elaeagnus umbellata) is a member of the Elaeagnaceae family, native to Asia, North America, and Europe. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Leaves
Opposite, broad, leathery leaves with upper surface silver-gray and lower surface pale green, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, white with yellow stamens, arranged in racemes, having four petals and four sepals, with prominent staminodes.
Stems
Stems are erect, branched, with opposite arrangement, covered in short, glandular hairs, and bearing stipulate nodes.
