Elaeagnus umbellata

What's the taxonomical classification of Elaeagnus umbellata?

Elaeagnus umbellata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Elaeagnaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Elaeagnus, and its specific species designation, umbellata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Elaeagnus umbellata contains high concentrations of phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and various antioxidant polyphenols. These bioactive substances contribute to significant anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities within the plant's tissues. Researchers have identified specific tannins and organic acids that help mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. The presence of these chemical constituents suggests potential therapeutic applications for treating inflammatory conditions. Additionally, the extracts are often studied for their ability to protect cells from free radical damage.

What are the therapeutic actions of Elaeagnus umbellata?

Elaeagnus umbellata has medicinal properties primarily centered on its ability to act as an antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent through its high concentration of polyphenols and flavonoids. These bioactive compounds help neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress within various biological systems. Some traditional applications suggest that extracts from the plant can assist in regulating metabolic processes and supporting digestive health. Furthermore, the presence of specific tannins may contribute to its astringent effects, which are often used to soothe mucosal membranes. While much of its therapeutic potential is being studied in modern pharmacology, these natural constituents form the basis of its traditional use.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and various types of dermatitis. The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These substances help reduce redness and irritation on the surface of the skin. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize extracts from the plant to soothe itchy or inflamed areas. Researchers continue to study its chemical composition to better understand its potential in dermatological care.

What are the herbal preparations of Elaeagnus umbellata?

Elaeagnus umbellata is prepared as a decoction from the dried bark, a tea from the leaves, or an infused oil derived from the seeds. The bark is often boiled in water to extract its bitter compounds for various traditional medicinal uses. Leaf infusions are commonly consumed to address digestive issues or to act as a mild tonic. Additionally, the nutrient-rich seeds can be pressed to create oils used in both topical applications and culinary preparations. These diverse forms allow the plant's bioactive constituents to be utilized effectively for different health purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Elaeagnuas umbellata can cause allergic skin reactions like contact dermatitis and respiratory irritation in sensitive individuals due to its pollen and oils. Some people may experience itching, redness, or a rash after touching the leaves or bark of the plant. The pollen produced by these trees is also a known allergen that can trigger hay fever or asthma symptoms in those with pollen sensitivities. Additionally, the small fruits can occasionally cause gastrointestinal upset if ingested in large quantities by humans. While generally considered non-toxic, caution is advised when handling the plant to avoid these common physiological responses.
