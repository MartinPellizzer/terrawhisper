Elaeagnus umbellata

What's the taxonomical classification of Elaeagnus umbellata?

Elaeagnus umbellata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Elaeagnaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Elaeagnus, and its specific species designation, umbellata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elaeagnus umbellata has an evergreen growth habit characterized by spreading, rounded crowns and small, leathery, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green upper surface with a paler, slightly pubescent underside. Its branches are woody and can develop a somewhat irregular or weeping structure as the tree matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually giving way to fleshy, drupe-like fruits that turn reddish or brownish when ripe. The bark is generally smooth and greyish, providing a subtle texture to the trunk and larger limbs.

As discussed in a study, Elaeagnuus umbellata exhibits anti-ulcer, antimutagenic, antimicrobial, and neuroprotective properties. The leaves also possess antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer effects. In terms of antibacterial potential, the extract showed a 25 mm zone of inhibition against Pseudomonas Aeruginosa 25619 and a 26 mm zone of inhibition against Enterococcus faecalis 29212. While no inhibition was noted for Klebsiella pneumonia 43816, the standard imipepem had an average of 34 mm zone of inhibition. Furthermore, fourteen compounds were identified in the crude methanolic extract of the leaves. This crude Met-Ext exhibits considerable hepatoprotective effects against CCl4-induced liver toxicity in a dose-dependent manner.

Furthermore, the concentrations of total phenolic and total flavonoid groups serve as primary indicators of the plant's overall therapeutic potential. As discussed in a study, Elaeagnuus umbellata exhibits high medicinal value and contains a phytochemical fingerprint of berries with a high content of polyphenols, mainly anthocyanins, followed by monoterpenes and vitamin C.
