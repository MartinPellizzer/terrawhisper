Allium polyrhizum

What's the taxonomical classification of Allium polyrhizum?

Allium polyrhizum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Asparagales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Allium, with its specific identification being the species polyrhizum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Allium polyrhizum contains high concentrations of organosulfur compounds, specifically allicin, quercetin, and various sulfur-containing amino acids. These bioactive molecules function primarily through their potent antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties. The organosulfur components act by inhibiting the growth of diverse pathogens while simultaneously reducing oxidative stress in biological tissues. Furthermore, the presence of flavonoids like quercetin supports cardiovascular health by modulating blood pressure and improving lipid profiles. Together, these chemical constituents provide a multifaceted pharmacological profile suitable for various therapeutic applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Allium polyrhizum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat hypertension, high cholesterol, cardiovascular diseases, and various respiratory infections. Its medicinal properties are primarily attributed to the presence of organosulfur compounds that help regulate blood pressure. These compounds also aid in reducing lipid levels within the bloodstream to prevent arterial buildup. Additionally, the plant's antimicrobial effects make it a useful remedy for fighting common bacterial and viral ailments. Regular consumption is often linked to improved immune response and overall heart health.
