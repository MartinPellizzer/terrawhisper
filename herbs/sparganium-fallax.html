What's the taxonomical classification of Sparganium fallax?

Sparganium fallax belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Poales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Typhaceae, falling under the genus Sparganium, and is specifically identified by the species name fallax.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sparganium fallax has a cap that measures between 1 and 4 centimeters in diameter, featuring a convex to plano-convex shape with a distinctly hygrophanous, brownish, and slightly viscid surface. Its stipe is slender, cylindrical, and often shares the same brownish coloration as the cap. The flesh is thin and lacks a distinct odor or taste.
