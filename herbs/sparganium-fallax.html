Sparganium fallax

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sparganium fallax has a cap that measures between 1 and 4 centimeters in diameter, featuring a convex to plano-convex shape with a distinctly hygrophanous, brownish, and slightly viscid surface. The gills are relatively close together, yellowish to brownish in color, and typically adnate to the stipe. Its stipe is slender, cylindrical, and often shares the same brownish coloration as the cap. The flesh is thin and lacks a distinct odor or taste. The spore print is typically white to pale cream in color.
