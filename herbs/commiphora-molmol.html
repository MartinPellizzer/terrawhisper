Commiphora Molmol: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Commiphora molmol, commonly known as olibanum, is a resinous substance derived from the bark of the Commiphora molmol tree, native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

This ancient herb has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its diverse health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and analgesic properties.

Therapeutically, it supports respiratory health, aids in digestion, and may help alleviate symptoms of arthritis and skin conditions.

The bioactive constituents of olibanum include terpenoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal effects.
