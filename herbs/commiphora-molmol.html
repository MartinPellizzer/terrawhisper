Myrrh

Commiphora molmol

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Myrrh (Commiphora molmol) is a resinous tree native to East Africa. It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is also known as African myrrh or molmol. The plant is a small evergreen tree with aromatic bark and yellowish resinous exudates. It is often confused with Commiphora myrrha, but both are distinct species within the same genus.

Active Compounds

Myrrh contains key active compounds such as tannins, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. These compounds work synergistically to reduce inflammation and support wound healing. Their combined action enhances the herb's effectiveness in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Tannin
  • Terpenoid
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and as a general tonic.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a remedy for skin conditions.

Safety Profile

Myrrh is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks. Long-term use may cause gastrointestinal irritation, and allergic reactions are possible in sensitive individuals.
