Horticulturally, myrrh is a shrub native to the Middle East and North Africa, which requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive. Botanically, myrrh is characterized by its resinous sap, which is obtained through a process of incision and collection.

With a history dating back thousands of years, myrrh has been mentioned in ancient texts such as the Bible and the Egyptian Ebers Papyrus, solidifying its place as one of the most revered and sought-after herbs in human history.
