Myrrh (Commiphora molmol)

Myrrh (Commiphora molmol) is a member of the Burseraceae family, native to East Africa, Sudan, and Madagascar. Traditionally, its resin, bark, and wood have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Leaves
Lateral, narrow, linear leaves with white stomatal bands on the lower surface and dark green upper surface.
Flowers
Flowers are small, yellowish, arranged in clusters, actinomorphic, with four petals and three sepals, and have prominent staminodes.
Stems
Cylindrical, woody, branched, with smooth, pale gray bark, bearing opposite, lanceolate leaves and exuding a resinous sap when incised.
