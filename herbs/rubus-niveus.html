What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus niveus?

Rubus niveus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Rubus and is specifically designated as the species niveus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus niveus has arching or erect woody stems that are densely covered in fine, white, velvety pubescence and armed with sharp, curved prickles. The leaves are typically trifoliate or palmately lobed with serrated margins and a distinctive soft, white felt-like coating on the undersides. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the branches during the blooming season. These flowers eventually give way to aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep, shiny black when fully ripe. The overall structure of the plant allows it to form thick, sprawling thickets through its spreading growth habit.

While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, physical injury from the prickly structure remains the primary concern.
