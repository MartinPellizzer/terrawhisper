Marrubium Vulgare Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Marrubium vulgare, commonly known as horehound, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is widely used in traditional medicine for its soothing and expectorant properties.

The herb offers various health benefits, including relieving coughs, reducing inflammation, and supporting respiratory health.

Its therapeutic actions are attributed to bioactive constituents such as marrubium, flavonoids, and mucilage.

Health Benefits of marrubium vulgare

Marrubium vulgare soothes digestive issues because it contains compounds that have antispasmodic properties, which can ease cramping and irritation in the gastrointestinal tract.

Marrubium vulgare relieves coughs as it possesses expectorant properties that help loosen mucus in the respiratory tract, making it easier to expel and reducing coughing fits.

Marrubium vulgare reduces inflammation due to its rich content of flavonoids and tannins, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects that help calm irritated tissues throughout the body.

Medicinal Constituents of marrubium vulgare

Marrubium vulgare marrubiin, a unique iridoid glycoside, is known for its antispasmodic, sedative, and anticonvulsant properties, making it valuable in treating nervous system disorders and gastrointestinal spasms.

Marrubium vulgare flavonoids, such as quercetin and rutin, act as potent antioxidants and anti-inflammatory agents, supporting cardiovascular health and reducing oxidative stress in the body.

Medicinal Parts of marrubium vulgare

Marrubium vulgare root is known for its bitter principles and mucilage, which contribute to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The leaves of Marrubium vulgare contain the same bitter compounds and are often used for their digestive and respiratory benefits.

The flowers, while less commonly used, can also be harvested and employed for their mild expectorant and calming effects.

The main herbal preparations from these parts include tinctures, decoctions, and infusions, with the root and leaves being most frequently used in formulations for respiratory and digestive support.
