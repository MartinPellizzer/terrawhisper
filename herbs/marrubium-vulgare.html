Marrubium Vulgare Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Marrubium vulgare, commonly known as horehound, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its hairy leaves and small blue or purple flowers.

This herb is traditionally used for its expectorant and antispasmodic properties, making it beneficial for respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis.

The bioactive constituents in horehound include mucilage, flavonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its therapeutic actions by soothing mucous membranes and reducing inflammation.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and syrups can be made from horehound to harness its medicinal benefits.
