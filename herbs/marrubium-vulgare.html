Horehound, scientifically known as Marrubium vulgare, is an herb that has been revered for its health-promoting properties, which include soothing digestive issues and relieving respiratory troubles.

In the culinary world, horehound is often used to add a unique flavor to teas, candies, and baked goods. As a hardy perennial plant, horehound is also prized by gardeners for its ability to thrive in dry soil and withstand drought conditions, making it an excellent choice for xeriscaping. Botanically speaking, horehound belongs to the mint family and is characterized by its hairy leaves and small white flowers.

With a history dating back thousands of years to ancient Egyptian and Greek civilizations, horehound has long been a valued herb in traditional medicine and cooking practices around the world.
