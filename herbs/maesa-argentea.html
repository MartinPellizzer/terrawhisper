What's the taxonomical classification of Maesa argentea?

Maesa argentea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Ericales and is a member of the family Primulaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, it is identified by the genus Maesa, with the unique species designation argentea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Maesa argentea has silvery, lanceolate leaves characterized by a dense covering of fine trichomes and a distinctively metallic sheen. These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along slender, woody stems that exhibit a light gray coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the foliage. Its root system is deep and fibrous, providing stability in well-drained, rocky soils. The overall structure of the plant remains compact and shrub-like throughout its growth cycle.
