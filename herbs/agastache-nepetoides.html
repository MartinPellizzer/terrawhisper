Botanical Identification

Blue Hyssop, Agastache nepetoides, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is also known as blue mistflower or blue hyssop. The plant features square stems, lance-shaped leaves, and spikes of blue-violet flowers. It is sometimes referred to as Hyssopus in older herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Blue Hyssop contains essential oils, flavonoids, and terpenoids as its main active compounds. These compounds work together to provide its therapeutic effects, with essential oils contributing to its aromatic and antimicrobial properties. Flavonoids and terpenoids support anti-inflammatory and antioxidant actions, enhancing the herb's overall efficacy in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Terpenoid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions like coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to relieve nausea.

Essential oil: Used for respiratory support and to relieve headaches.

Safety Profile

Blue Hyssop (Agastache nepetoides) is generally safe when used as directed, but it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Caution is advised for people with autoimmune disorders or those taking immunosuppressants.
