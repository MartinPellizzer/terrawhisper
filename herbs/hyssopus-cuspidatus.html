Hyssopus cuspidatus has an erect, perennial herbaceous structure characterized by square stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically serrated along the margins. During its flowering period, it produces terminal clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color. These blossoms emerge from the upper portions of the stems to attract various pollinators. The root system is fibrous, helping to anchor the plant firmly within its natural habitat.
