What's the taxonomical classification of Hyssopus cuspidatus?

Hyssopus cuspidatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which is further refined into the genus Hyssopus, with the specific identifier being the species cuspidatus.

What are the pharmacological activities of Hyssopus cuspidatus?

This plant has documented pharmacological activities including antiasthmatic, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. Its antiasthmatic potential is often linked to its ability to reduce airway inflammation and ease respiratory distress. The antimicrobial activity suggests that extracts from the plant can effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Additionally, the antioxidant effects help neutralize free radicals, protecting cells from oxidative damage. These diverse biological functions make Hyssopus cuspstatus a subject of significant interest in medicinal research.

According to a study published by "BioMed research international", Hyssopus cuspidatus is a traditional Chinese medicine commonly used in the treatment of asthma. The study identified 23 active compounds within Hyssopus cuspidatus that share 122 common asthma-related targets. Furthermore, 43 miRNAs were identified as regulating 19 key targets involved in the antiasthmatic effects of the plant. These antiasthmatic effects mainly occur through bioactive compounds such as caffeic acid, methyl rosmarinate, luteolin, esculetin, and 8-hydroxycirsimaritin. These specific compounds interact with multiple miRNAs, including miR-99a, miR-498, miR-33b, and miR-18a, to regulate genes like JAK, STAT3, EGFR, LYN, and IL-6.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds rosmarinic acid, hyssopusizine, butyl rosmarinate, methyl rosmarinate, and caffeic acid. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the various biological activities observed in the species. Rosmarinic acid and caffeic acid are well-known polyphenols that provide significant antioxidant properties. The presence of hyssopusizine represents a unique alkaloid characteristic of this botanical specimen. Additionally, the esters butyl rosmarinate and methyl rosmarinate further define its complex chemical profile.

According to a study published by "Food chemistry", LC-MS/MS analysis of the ethyl acetate fraction of Hyssopus cuspidatus identified sixty-four compounds, with phenolic compounds serving as the dominant components. Systematic separation and purification of this fraction led to the isolation of thirty-four compounds. Within this group, six compounds were identified to be new and eighteen compounds were discovered from Hyssopus cuspidatus for the first time. The major components of the ethyl acetate fraction were determined to be rosmarinic acid, methyl rosmarinate, butyl rosmarinate, and salvigenin. Most of these isolated compounds exhibited significant or moderate antioxidant and antimicrobial activities.
