What's the taxonomical classification of Hyssopus cuspidatus?

Hyssopus cuspidatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which is further refined into the genus Hyssopus, with the specific identifier being the species cuspidatus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds rosmarinic acid, butyl rosmarinate, caffeic acid, methyl rosmarinate, and ferulic acid. These phenolic acids and esters function primarily as potent antioxidants within the plant's chemical profile. Rosmarinic acid and caffeic acid are well-known for their ability to reduce oxidative stress in biological systems. Additionally, the esters butyl rosmarinate and methyl rosmarinate contribute to the unique therapeutic properties of the species. The presence of ferulic acid further enhances the plant's potential for anti-inflammatory applications.

As discussed in a study, LC-MS/MS analysis of the ethyl acetate fraction of Hyssopus cuspidatus identified sixty-four compounds. Systematic separation and purification of this fraction led to the isolation of thirty-four compounds. Within these findings, six compounds were identified to be new and eighteen compounds were discovered from Hyssopus cuspidatus for the first time. The major components of the ethyl acetate fraction were identified as rosmarinic acid, methyl rosmarinate, butyl rosmarinate, and salvigenin. Most of these isolated compounds exhibited significant or moderate antioxidant and antimicrobial activities.
