Onychium japonicum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Onychium japonicum has a basal rosette of fleshy, oblong to lanceolate leaves that are typically bright green and possess a slightly succulent texture. The leaf margins are entire or minutely serrated, often appearing somewhat wavy in shape. From the center of this rosette, long, slender peduncles emerge to bear small, tubular flowers. These blossoms are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in compact heads at the ends of the stems. The plant also features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread through rocky or shallow soil environments.

      Onychium japonicum is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried aerial parts or as a topical paste made from the crushed fresh plant material.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Onychium japonicum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or localized redness if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive human tissue. While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, many plants in similar families contain compounds that trigger inflammatory responses. Individuals with known sensitivities to botanical extracts may experience itching or swelling upon handling the leaves or stems. It is generally advised to avoid skin contact to prevent unpredictable dermatological reactions. Always wash the affected area thoroughly with soap and water if accidental exposure occurs.
