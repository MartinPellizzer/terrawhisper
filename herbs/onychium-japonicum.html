Onychium japonicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Onychium japonicum?

Onychium japonicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is a representative of the family Pteridaceae, specifically situated within the genus Onychium, and is identified by the species name japonicum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Onychium japonicum contains bioactive compounds including flavonoids, triterpenoids, and various phenolic acids that provide its therapeutic properties. These secondary metabolites contribute to its significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities within biological systems. Research suggests that its chemical constituents may help mitigate oxidative stress by neutralizing free radicals. Furthermore, certain extracts from the plant have demonstrated potential antimicrobial effects against specific pathogens. These pharmacological actions make the species a subject of interest for developing natural medicinal agents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin conditions including eczema, dermatitis, and fungal infections. Its bioactive compounds possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that help soothe irritated skin tissue. Researchers study its extracts to determine how these substances combat pathogenic microbes. When applied topically, the plant may help reduce redness and swelling associated with dermatological ailments. Ongoing investigations aim to isolate specific chemical constituents for pharmaceutical development.
