Onychium japonicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Onychium japonicum?

Onychium japonicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is a representative of the family Pteridaceae, specifically situated within the genus Onychium, and is identified by the species name japonicum.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Onychium japonicum contains medicinal compounds primarily extracted from its entire aerial portion, which includes the leaves, stems, and flowers. The whole plant is traditionally utilized in various herbal preparations to treat inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. Additionally, decoctions made from the stems and foliage are sometimes consumed in traditional practices to address internal imbalances. These diverse components work together to provide the plant's characteristic therapeutic effects.

      What are the herbal preparations of Onychium japonicum?

      Onychium japonicum is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried aerial parts or as a topical paste made from the crushed fresh plant material. These liquid extracts are frequently ingested in traditional medicine to address internal ailments related to heat or inflammation. The topical application of the pulverized leaves or stems is commonly utilized to soothe skin irritations and promote wound healing.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Onychium japonicum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or localized redness if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive human tissue. While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, many plants in similar families contain compounds that trigger inflammatory responses. Individuals with known sensitivities to botanical extracts may experience itching or swelling upon handling the leaves or stems. It is generally advised to avoid skin contact to prevent unpredictable dermatological reactions. Always wash the affected area thoroughly with soap and water if accidental exposure occurs.
