What's the taxonomical classification of Onychium japonicum?

Onychium japonicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is a representative of the family Pteridaceae, specifically situated within the genus Onychium, and is identified by the species name japonicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Onychium japonicum has a basal rosette of fleshy, oblong to lanceolate leaves that are typically bright green and possess a slightly succulent texture. The leaf margins are entire or minutely serrated, often appearing somewhat wavy in shape. From the center of this rosette, long, slender peduncles emerge to bear small, tubular flowers. These blossoms are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in compact heads at the ends of the stems. The plant also features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread through rocky or shallow soil environments.
