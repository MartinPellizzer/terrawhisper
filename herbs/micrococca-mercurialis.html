Micrococca mercurialis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Micrococca mercurialis has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and features small, ovate leaves arranged oppositely along the length of the plant. The foliage consists of simple, entire-margined leaves that are often bright green and slightly fleshy in texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils, displaying a subtle structure characteristic of the species. The plant's overall morphology is defined by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. These physical traits collectively distinguish it from similar herbaceous species within its taxonomic group.

Some individuals may experience more systemic reactions such as nausea or vomiting following accidental consumption. Because the specific chemical composition can vary, the intensity of these side effects may differ between users. It is important to avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse physical reactions.
