Micrococca mercurialis

What's the taxonomical classification of Micrococca mercurialis?

Micrococca mercurialis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Micrococca and the species name mercurialis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Micrococca mercurialis has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and features small, ovate leaves arranged oppositely along the length of the plant.

The foliage consists of simple, entire-margined leaves that are often bright green and slightly fleshy in texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils, displaying a subtle structure characteristic of the species. The plant's overall morphology is defined by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

These physical traits collectively distinguish it from similar herbaceous species within its taxonomic group.

      What are the herbal preparations of Micrococca mercurialis?

      Micrococca mercurialis is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using the dried aerial parts of the plant to extract its medicinal properties. These liquid preparations are often administered to treat various ailments associated with its traditional uses.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Micrococca mercurialis can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap often leads to redness, itching, or blistering on the affected area of the skin. Some individuals may experience more systemic reactions such as nausea or vomiting following accidental consumption. Because the specific chemical composition can vary, the intensity of these side effects may differ between users. It is important to avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse physical reactions.
