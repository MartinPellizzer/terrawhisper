What to know about Justicia adhatoda (adhatoda) before using it medicinally

Adhatoda, scientifically known as Justicia adhatoda, is a herb that has been touted for its impressive health properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects that can help alleviate respiratory issues and boost the immune system.

In culinary uses, adhatoda is often added to soups and stews to enhance flavor and aroma, while also serving as a natural remedy for digestive issues. As a horticultural plant, adhatoda is relatively easy to cultivate and can thrive in a variety of environments, making it a popular choice among gardeners and herbalists. From a botanical perspective, adhatoda is classified as a member of the Acanthaceae family and features small, white flowers that bloom on tall stalks.

With references dating back to ancient Ayurvedic and Unani medicine, adhatoda has been prized for its medicinal properties for centuries, earning it a place in traditional healing practices around the world.

What parts of Justicia adhatoda are used for medicinal purposes?

The most commonly used parts of Justicia adhatoda for medicinal purposes are the leaves and roots, which have been utilized in traditional medicine for centuries.

The leaves are rich in alkaloids, particularly vasicine, vasicinone, and vasicinol, which possess anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and bronchodilatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's ability to alleviate respiratory issues such as asthma, bronchitis, and chronic coughs.

The roots of J. adhatoda, on the other hand, contain a higher concentration of alkaloids, including isovasicinone, which has been shown to exhibit antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Traditionally, the leaves are often used in decoctions or infusions to treat respiratory issues, while the roots are employed in combination with other herbs to address digestive problems such as dysentery and diarrhea.

The pharmacological properties of J. adhatoda have also led to its use in the treatment of various systemic conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin diseases.

What are the side effects of Justicia adhatoda when used improperly?

When used improperly, Justicia adhatoda increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as gastrointestinal disturbances, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

This is because the plant contains a high concentration of saponins, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract when ingested in large or excessive quantities. Furthermore, improper use may lead to allergic reactions, such as skin rashes, itching, and swelling, particularly if the individual has a pre-existing allergy to plants in the Acanthaceae family.

Additionally, using Justicia adhatoda for medicinal purposes without proper guidance or supervision can result in interactions with other medications, potentially leading to adverse effects on blood pressure, heart rate, and respiratory function. In rare cases, improper use may even trigger more severe reactions, such as anaphylaxis, which requires immediate medical attention.

Therefore, it is essential to consult with a qualified healthcare professional before using Justicia adhatoda or any other herbal remedy for medicinal purposes, to minimize the risk of unwanted side effects and ensure safe and effective treatment.

What precautions to take before using Justicia adhatoda medicinally?

The precautions to take before using Justicia adhatoda medicinally are essential to ensure safe and effective use of this herb, particularly when consumed in large quantities or for extended periods.

First and foremost, individuals with cardiovascular disease, high blood pressure, or arrhythmia should exercise caution due to the herb's potential to lower blood pressure and affect heart rate. Additionally, those taking medications for hypertension, anticoagulants, or vasodilators may need to adjust their dosages under medical supervision.

Finally, standardized dosages and quality-controlled preparations are crucial to ensure efficacy and minimize the risk of adverse reactions.
