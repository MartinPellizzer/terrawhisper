Justicia adhatoda

What's the taxonomical classification of Justicia adhatoda?

Justicia adhatoda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and belongs to the family Acanthaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Justicia and the species name adhatoda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Justicia adhatoda has opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins and a smooth surface, upright square stems, and tubular, bilabiate flowers that are typically white with purple markings. The foliage is characterized by prominent venation and a slightly glossy texture that distinguishes its vegetative structure. Its inflorescence consists of dense terminal spikes where the flowers emerge from specialized bracts. The plant exhibits a characteristic herbaceous habit with a branching pattern that supports its floral display. These morphological features collectively define its appearance within the Acanthaceae family.

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South America, specifically spanning across Brazil, Colombia, Venezuela, and parts of the Caribbean. What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Justicia adhatoda contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its leaves and roots. What traditional systems uses this plant?

This plant Justicia adhatoda, commonly known as vasaka, has been utilized for centuries in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine to treat respiratory ailments like asthma, bronchitis, and coughs.

Its leaves contain bioactive alkaloids, specifically vasicine, which act as potent expectorants to help clear mucus from the airways. Traditional practitioners often prepare decoctions or infusions from the leaves to soothe inflammation in the lungs and bronchial tubes. Historically, the plant was also employed to manage bleeding disorders due to its believed hemostatic properties.

This long-standing herbal practice highlights its central role in ancient South Asian medicinal traditions.

What are the pharmacological activities of Justicia adhatoda?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antifungal, and antiviral properties. The analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects allow it to effectively manage pain and reduce swelling in various biological models. Its antimicrobial profile includes the ability to inhibit the growth of various bacteria and fungi that cause infection. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates antiviral capabilities that can help combat certain viral pathogens. These multifaceted therapeutic actions make it a significant subject of medicinal research.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in pharmacology", a gas chromatography-mass spectrometry analysis of the ethyl acetate fraction of Justicia adhatoda identified 21 compounds, 14 of which are recognized for their pharmacological potential. Among the four evaluated fractions, the chloroform fraction exhibited 46.51% anti-inflammatory activity in carrageenan-induced inflammation. The ethyl acetate fraction showed analgesic potential at 300 mg/kg in the acetic acid-induced test and achieved maximum inhibition in the formalin test during both early (74.35%) and late (88.38%) phases. This same ethyl acetate fraction at 300 mg/kg recorded a maximum inhibition of pyrexia at 77.98%. In the DPPH assay, the ethyl acetate fraction demonstrated scavenging potential where 50 μg/ml resulted in 50.40% and 100 μg/ml resulted in 66.74% scavenging.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal quinazoline alkaloids vasicine, vasicinone, vasicoline, vasicolinone, and adhatodine. These specific chemical compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's potent bronchodilator and expectorant properties. Vasicine and its derivatives work effectively to relax smooth muscles in the respiratory tract. Scientific studies often focus on these alkaloids due to their potential applications in treating asthma and other pulmonary conditions. The presence of these bioactive substances makes the species a significant subject in pharmacological research.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", Justicia adhatoda L. leaf is used in a fixed combination as an aqueous ethanolic extract. The dosage investigated for this combination was 30 ml/day, which contains 762 mg of genuine extracts. These extracts have standardized contents of 0.2 mg/ml vasicine and 0.8 mg/ml chicoric acid. The study observed these components in patients aged 18-65 years old during a 5 day period.

Its medicinal properties are frequently utilized to soothe respiratory distress and alleviate persistent coughing. The leaves and extracts also serve as natural remedies to reduce high body temperatures during a fever. Additionally, the plant contains bioactive compounds that help manage systemic inflammation. These qualities make it a valuable resource for addressing a wide range of inflammatory disorders.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Justicia adhatoda L. is used as traditional medicine in Nepal to treat cough, asthma, and inflammatory disorders. The plant is indicated as Asuro and its leaves are used worldwide for their cardiotonic, expectorant, anti-asthmatic, and bronchodilatory properties. The study assessed its anti-inflammatory potential through NF-κB and AP-1 inhibition assays in THP-1 cells. The J. adhatoda methanol extract (JAME) inhibited NF-κB and AP-1 expression to a greater extent than the positive control prednisolone. Additionally, the plant showed moderate radical-quenching properties in DPPH and ABTS assays, while its anti-carotene bleaching activity was significantly higher than the reference BHV.
