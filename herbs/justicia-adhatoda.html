Justicia adhatoda

What's the taxonomical classification of Justicia adhatoda?

Justicia adhatoda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and belongs to the family Acanthaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Justicia and the species name adhatoda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Justicia adhatoda has opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins and a smooth surface, upright square stems, and tubular, bilabiate flowers that are typically white with purple markings. The foliage is characterized by prominent venation and a slightly glossy texture that distinguishes its vegetative structure. Its inflorescence consists of dense terminal spikes where the flowers emerge from specialized bracts. The plant exhibits a characteristic herbaceous habit with a branching pattern that supports its floral display. These morphological features collectively define its appearance within the Acanthaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South America, specifically spanning across Brazil, Colombia, Venezuela, and parts of the Caribbean. It thrives primarily in moist, lowland environments and forest margins where humidity is consistently high. The species is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks that provide the necessary soil moisture. Its range is limited by its preference for warmer climates that lack harsh freezing temperatures. This localized distribution makes it a characteristic element of the Neotropical flora.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Justicia adhatoda contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its leaves and roots. The leaves are the most frequently utilized part due to their high concentration of vasicine, which serves as a potent bronchodilator. These leaves are often processed into juices or decoctions to treat respiratory ailments such as asthma, bronchitis, and coughs. Additionally, the roots are sometimes employed in traditional formulations to address various inflammatory conditions. The combination of these botanical parts provides a diverse range of therapeutic properties used in herbal medicine.

What are the pharmacological activities of Justicia adhatoda?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antifungal, and antioxidant pharmacological activities that contribute to its significant medicinal value. Its ability to reduce pain and swelling makes it a useful traditional remedy for managing various inflammatory conditions. The presence of bioactive compounds allows it to inhibit the growth of several harmful bacteria and fungi. Additionally, its potent antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals and protect cells from oxidative stress. These diverse therapeutic effects highlight the plant's potential for developing new pharmaceutical treatments.

As discussed in a study, a total of 21 compounds were identified in the gas chromatography-mass spectrometry analysis of the ethyl acetate fraction of Justicia adhatoda, with 14 recognized for their pharmacological potential. The chloroform fraction exhibited high anti-inflammatoty activity of 46.51% in carrageenan-induced inflammation. In the acetic acid-induced test, the ethyl acetate fraction was most effective at 300 mg/kg for analgesic potential. For the formalin test, the ethyl acetate fraction showed maximum inhibition of 74.35% in the early phase and 88.38% in the late phase. Additionally, the ethyl acetate fraction at 300 mg/kg recorded a maximum inhibition of pyrexia at 77.98%. In the DPPH assay, the ethyl acetate fraction resulted in 50.40% scavenging at 50 μg/ml and 66.74% scavenging at 100 μg/ml.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several potent medicinal alkaloids, most notably the quinazoline derivatives vasicine, vasicinone, vasicoline, and vasicilone. These specific alkaloids are the primary bioactive constituents responsible for the plant's various pharmacological properties. Research indicates that vasicine and its related compounds often exhibit significant bronchodilatory and expectorant effects in biological systems. The presence of these specialized alkaloids makes the plant a valuable subject in the study of natural medicinal chemistry. Understanding the concentrations of vasicinone and vasicolinone helps scientists determine the overall therapeutic potential of the species.

As discussed in a study, Justicia adhatoda L. leaf is part of an aqueous ethanolic extract used in a dose of 30 ml/day. This dosage contains 762 mg of genuine extracts. The extracts are standardized to contain specific medicinal compounds, including 0.2 mg/ml of vasicine and 0.8 mg/ml of chicoric acid. These components contribute to the antitussive effects observed during the 5 day period of the clinical trial.
