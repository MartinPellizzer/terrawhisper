Adhatoda

Justicia adhatoda

Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda) is a flowering plant native to India and parts of Southeast Asia, commonly known for its use in traditional medicine to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma.

Morphology

Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda) is a flowering plant characterized by its square, hairy stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems.

Distribution

Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda) is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, Thailand, and parts of Southeast Asia, with its natural habitat primarily in humid, moist environments such as forests and riverbanks.

Traditional Uses

Adhatoda has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine to treat respiratory conditions such as asthma, bronchitis, and cough due to its bronchodilatory and expectorant properties.

Medicinal Systems

Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda) has been traditionally used in Ayurveda, Siddha, and Unani medicinal systems for its therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including alkaloids such as vasicine, vasicinone, and justicidin, which are responsible for its pharmacological activities.

Pharmacology

Adhatoda (Justicia adhatoda) exhibits pharmacological properties including bronchodilatory, antispasmodic, expectorant, and antimicrobial activities, which support its traditional use in respiratory and digestive disorders.

Medicinal Uses

Adhatoda is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and cough, as well as digestive issues like indigestion and gastrointestinal discomfort.

Preparations

Adhatoda is used to prepare various formulations such as decoctions, tinctures, powders, and extracts, which are commonly employed in traditional herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Evidence
