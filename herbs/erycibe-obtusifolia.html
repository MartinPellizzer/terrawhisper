Erycibe obtusifolia has woody, scrambling stems that form dense, tangled thickets characterized by small, simple, opposite leaves with obtuse or rounded apices. The fruit consists of fleshy berries that vary in color depending on the stage of maturity. This morphological structure allows the species to thrive in shaded, forest understory environments.
