Calendula (Calendula Sventenii)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Calendula sventenii.

Calendula, scientifically known as Calendula sventenii, is a medicinal herb widely recognized for its therapeutic properties and historical use in traditional medicine. This flowering plant, native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, is known for its bright yellow-orange flowers and has been used for centuries for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing benefits. Traditionally, Calendula has been employed in herbal remedies to treat skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and minor burns, as well as to soothe digestive issues and promote circulation. In modern wellness practices, it is commonly found in topical creams, ointments, and even as a dietary supplement, supporting skin health and immune function. One of its unique features is the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its potent medicinal effects, and its historical association with the Roman Empire, where it was used for both medicinal and cosmetic purposes.
