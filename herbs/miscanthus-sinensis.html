Miscanthus sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Miscanthus sinensis?

Miscanthus sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Miscanthus, and its unique species designation, sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Miscanthus sinensis has upright, tufted perennial growth habit characterized by long, linear leaf blades that reach up to 1.5 meters in length and terminal panicles of small, brownish inflorescences. The leaves are typically lanceolate with prominent parallel venation and slightly serrated margins toward the tips. Stems are slender, cylindrical, and develop a dense rhizomatous root system that facilitates spreading through clonal growth. During the flowering season, the plant produces airy, branched flower clusters that emerge from the upper nodes of the culms. The overall structure provides a tall, grass-like appearance suitable for ornamental landscaping.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of medicinal compounds including flavonoids, flavones, flavone-glycosides, hydroxycinnamates, and soluble phenols. The presence of various flavones and their corresponding flavone-glycosides contributes to the plant's significant antioxidant potential. Additionally, the hydroxycinnamates found within the tissue provide essential bioactive properties that support cellular health. Soluble phenols further enhance the therapeutic value of the plant by offering protective qualities against oxidative stress. Together, these phytochemicals create a complex chemical synergy that defines the plant's medicinal efficacy.

As discussed in a study, Miscanthus sinensis contains particularly high levels of soluble phenols, specifically hydroxycinnamates and flavonoids. The research identified ten hydroxycinnamates, which are O-cinnamoylquinic acids, within the leaf tissue. The study also quantified several flavones, including one mono-O-glycosyl flavone, eight mono-C-glycosyl flavones, two di-C-glycosyl flavones, five O-glycosyl-C-glycosyl flavones, and nine 2″-O-glycosyl-C-glycosyl flavones. These compounds were measured in two hundred progeny along with maternal and paternal plants during the seedling stage. The analysis involved screening plants at seven months of growth to determine the abundance of these phenols at their highest biomass.
