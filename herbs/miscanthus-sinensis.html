Miscanthus sinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Miscanthus sinensis has upright, tufted perennial growth habit characterized by long, linear leaf blades that reach up to 1.5 meters in length and terminal panicles of small, brownish inflorescences. The leaves are typically lanceolate with prominent parallel venation and slightly serrated margins toward the tips. Stems are slender, cylindrical, and develop a dense rhizomatous root system that facilitates spreading through clonal growth. During the flowering season, the plant produces airy, branched flower clusters that emerge from the upper nodes of the culms. The overall structure provides a tall, grass-like appearance suitable for ornamental landscaping.
