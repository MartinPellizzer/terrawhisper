Miscanthus sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Miscanthus sinensis?

Miscanthus sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Miscanthus, and its unique species designation, sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Miscanthus sinensis has upright, tufted perennial growth habit characterized by long, linear leaf blades that reach up to 1.5 meters in length and terminal panicles of small, brownish inflorescences. The leaves are typically lanceolate with prominent parallel venation and slightly serrated margins toward the tips. Stems are slender, cylindrical, and develop a dense rhizomatous root system that facilitates spreading through clonal growth. During the flowering season, the plant produces airy, branched flower clusters that emerge from the upper nodes of the culms. The overall structure provides a tall, grass-like appearance suitable for ornamental landscaping.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive substances including flavonoids, flavones, flavone-glycosides, hydroxycinnamates, and soluble phenols. These chemical constituents contribute to the complex pharmacological profile of Miscanthus sinensis. The presence of various flavones and their glycoside derivatives suggests significant potential for antioxidant activity. Furthermore, the hydroxycinnamates and soluble phenols provide additional therapeutic properties that may assist in biological processes. Together, these medicinal compounds form the basis for studying the plant's medicinal utility.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", Miscanthus sinensis contains particularly high levels of soluble phenols, including hydroxycinnamates and flavonoids, which may have applications in the nutraceutical, cosmetic, and pharmaceutical industries. Research involving the leaf tissue of two hundred progeny along with maternal and paternal plants identified ten hydroxycinnamates, specifically O-cinnamoylquinic acids. The study also identified several flavones, consisting of one mono-O-glycosyl flavone, eight mono-C-glycosyl flavones, two di-C-glycosyl flavones, five O-glycosyl-C-glycosyl flavones, and nine 2″-O-glycosyl-C-glycosyl flavones. These compounds were quantified during the seedling stage and later screened at seven months of growth to determine abundance at highest biomass. Generally, the concentrations of these phenols decreased as the leaves matured.
