What's the taxonomical classification of Viscum multinerve?

Viscum multinerve belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Santalales and the family Santalaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Viscum, with its specific identification being the species multinerve.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe physiological distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain due to its toxic properties. Consuming parts of the plant may also lead to neurological symptoms such as dizziness. More dangerous cardiovascular effects can occur, specifically involving a significant drop in blood pressure known as hypotension. Additionally, the plant may cause bradycardia, which is an abnormally slow heart rate. These side effects represent serious medical risks for anyone who ingests the specimen.
