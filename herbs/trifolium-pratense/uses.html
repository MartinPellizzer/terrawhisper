Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) Uses

Red Clover is used to alleviate menstrual cramps due to its phytoestrogen content. It also supports skin health by treating infections, inflammation, and irritation, and aids in wound healing and burn recovery. Additionally, it may help with allergic rhinitis, respiratory infections, and joint-related conditions like osteoarthritis and joint pain.

Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) Monograph

1. Menstrual cramps

Red Clover is used to alleviate menstrual cramps due to its phytoestrogen content, which helps regulate hormonal imbalances. It may reduce uterine contractions and inflammation, easing pain and discomfort during menstruation. Traditionally, it has been prepared as a tea or tincture for this purpose.

2. Skin infections

Red Clover is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It contains compounds like isoflavones and saponins that help combat bacterial and fungal infections. Its soothing effects also reduce redness and irritation associated with skin conditions. Apply as a poultice or infused oil for localized relief.

3. Wound healing

Red Clover contains compounds that promote tissue repair and reduce inflammation, making it beneficial for wound healing. It supports collagen synthesis and may enhance the body's natural regenerative processes. Traditionally used to accelerate healing and prevent infection in minor wounds.

4. Skin inflammation

Red Clover is used topically to reduce skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It contains compounds like isoflavones and flavonoids that help calm irritated skin. Applications include poultices, creams, or infusions to alleviate conditions like eczema or psoriasis. Its gentle nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin.

5. Skin irritation

Red Clover is used to alleviate skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It can help reduce redness, itching, and inflammation associated with conditions like eczema or psoriasis. Topical application of Red Clover extracts or infusions may promote skin healing and provide relief from minor irritations. Its mild nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin when used appropriately.

6. Burns

Red Clover is used topically for burns due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce pain and promote healing by cooling the skin and preventing infection. A poultice made from fresh leaves or a infused oil can be applied to minor burns for relief. Its mild nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin.

7. Allergic rhinitis

Red Clover is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It contains compounds like isoflavones and quercetin that may reduce nasal inflammation and histamine release. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture and is considered a supportive remedy for managing seasonal allergies.

8. Respiratory infections

Red Clover is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and mucus production. It may help alleviate symptoms of colds, bronchitis, and other respiratory infections. Its expectorant properties aid in clearing the airways, while its antimicrobial effects may combat infection-causing pathogens.

9. Osteoarthritis

Red Clover contains isoflavones that may help reduce inflammation and pain associated with osteoarthritis. These compounds support joint health by inhibiting enzymes that break down cartilage. It is often used in herbal formulas to alleviate symptoms and improve mobility in affected joints.

10. Joint pain

Red Clover contains compounds like isoflavones and saponins that may help reduce inflammation and alleviate joint pain. It has been traditionally used to support joint health and ease symptoms of conditions like arthritis. Its anti-inflammatory properties may contribute to its effectiveness in managing discomfort associated with joint issues.
