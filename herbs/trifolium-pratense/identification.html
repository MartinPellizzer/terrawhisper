Botanical Identification of Red Clover (Trifolium pratense)

To identify Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Red Clover, Trifolium pratense, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Trifolium, and species Trifolium pratense. It is a member of the legume family and is widely used in herbal medicine for its nutritional and medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Red Clover is Trifolium pratense, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Trifolium redactum and Trifolium minus. Common names such as Red Clover, Meadow Clover, and Silky Clover are widely used. Regional and historical names vary, reflecting its long use in herbal medicine across different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) is a herbaceous perennial with a taproot system and extensive lateral roots. It has upright, branching stems that are hairy and often reddish near the base. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three oval, serrated leaflets with a small terminal spur. The inflorescence is a dense, globular head of small pink to red flowers. Each flower has five petals and produces a legume pod containing several elliptical, dark brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) can be distinguished by its trifoliate leaves with reddish stems, pink to purple flowers, and hairy stems. It differs from white clover by its red stems and larger, pink flowers. It is also distinct from other Trifolium species by its specific leaf shape and flowering pattern.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the most accurate harvest timing is during the flowering stage, when the plant is most recognizable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) is closely related to other Trifolium species within the Legume family, Fabaceae. It shares genus traits like trifoliate leaves and pod fruits with other members. Its closest relatives include species such as Trifolium hybridum and Trifolium dubium, which also exhibit similar morphological and ecological characteristics.
