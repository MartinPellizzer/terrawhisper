Croton tchibangensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Croton tchibangensis?

Croton tchibangensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Malpighiales. This plant is further categorized under the family Euphorbiaceae, falling into the genus Croton, and is specifically identified by its species name, tchibangensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Croton tchibangensis has large, elliptical to oblong leaves that typically measure between 15 and 30 centimeters in length and feature prominent pinnate venation. The leaf margins are generally entire, though they can occasionally exhibit slight undulations. These leaves are arranged in an alternate pattern along woody stems that characterize its shrubby or small tree-like growth habit. The foliage often displays a glossy texture and varying shades of green depending on the light exposure. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, following the typical reproductive structure of the Euphorbiaceae family.
