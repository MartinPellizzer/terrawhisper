10 Possible Side Effects Of Catha Edulis

Catha edulis has some side effects when used improperly, such as anxiety, insomnia, and increased heart rate.

These effects occur due to the presence of cathinone, a stimulant that can lead to physical and emotional dependence.

If left unmanaged, these side effects can worsen a person's life by affecting their relationships, work performance, and overall well-being, potentially even leading to mental health conditions like depression and anxiety disorders.

1. Causes agitation increases

Catha edulis causes agitation increases due to its stimulant properties, which can lead to feelings of euphoria and excitement.

The plant's active ingredients, including cathinone and cathine, interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, such as dopamine and norepinephrine, causing an overactivation of these systems.

This can result in increased heart rate, blood pressure, and anxiety levels, leading to a sense of agitation and restlessness.

2. Induces anxiety attacks frequently

Catha edulis induces anxiety attacks frequently.

This is due to the presence of cathinone and norephedrine, alkaloids that stimulate the central nervous system, leading to increased heart rate, blood pressure, and respiration.

As a result, individuals may experience feelings of restlessness, agitation, and panic, which can escalate into full-blown anxiety attacks.

3. Increases heart rate rapidly

Catha edulis increases heart rate rapidly due to its high concentration of cathinone and cathine, alkaloids that stimulate the central nervous system.

As a result, users may experience an intense sense of excitement, euphoria, and energy, accompanied by an accelerated heart rate.

This increased heart rate is attributed to the stimulant properties of these alkaloids, which can lead to a rapid heartbeat and palpitations.

4. Produces palpitations continuously

Catha edulis produces palpitations continuously due to its stimulatory effects on the central nervous system.

The plant contains alkaloids such as cathinone and cathine, which increase heart rate and blood pressure by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system.

As a result, users may experience persistent palpitations, characterized by an irregular or racing heartbeat, often accompanied by feelings of anxiety and jitteriness.

5. Elicits insomnia consistently

Catha edulis elicits insomnia consistently due to its stimulating effects on the nervous system.

The plant's alkaloids, such as cathinone and norephedrine, increase alertness and energy by blocking the reabsorption of certain neurotransmitters like dopamine and serotonin.

As a result, users may experience difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep, leading to chronic insomnia.

6. Triggers headaches strongly

Catha edulis triggers headaches strongly due to its stimulant properties.

The plant's active compounds, including cathinone and norephedrine, can cause vasoconstriction, leading to a reduction in blood flow to the brain and subsequent headache development. Additionally, khat use has been linked to increased stress levels, anxiety, and insomnia, which can also contribute to the onset of headaches.

As a result, users may experience frequent or severe headaches as a direct consequence of consuming Catha edulis.

7. Prompts hallucinations suddenly

Catha edulis prompts hallucinations suddenly due to its high levels of cathinone and cathine.

These psychoactive alkaloids interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, altering normal neural activity and leading to vivid visual distortions and auditory hallucinations.

The potent stimulant effects of Catha edulis can cause a sense of euphoria, but this can rapidly escalate into intense psychological disturbances, including disorganized thinking, agitation, and even paranoia.

8. Evokes dizziness frequently

Catha edulis evokes dizziness frequently due to its high concentration of alkaloids, particularly cathinone and cathine.

These chemicals can stimulate the central nervous system, leading to feelings of excitement, anxiety, and unease, which may manifest as dizziness in some individuals.

The rapid release of these substances into the bloodstream also contributes to the potential for adverse reactions, making khat consumption a potentially hazardous activity.

9. Leads to tremors severely

Catha edulis leads to tremors severely due to its high concentration of cathinone and cathine.

These stimulants can cause an overproduction of neurotransmitters such as dopamine and adrenaline, leading to an increased heart rate, blood pressure, and muscle activity.

This excessive stimulation can result in uncontrollable shaking or trembling, a common side effect experienced by users of Catha edulis.

10. Enhances aggression violently

Catha edulis enhances aggression violently by stimulating the brain's neurotransmitters, such as dopamine and norepinephrine.

This stimulation can lead to increased feelings of excitement, agitation, and irritability, which may manifest in aggressive behavior towards others or oneself.

The plant's cathinone content is responsible for this effect, which can be particularly pronounced when consumed in large quantities or over extended periods.
