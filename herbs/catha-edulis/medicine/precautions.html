10 Precautions To Take When Using Catha Edulis (Khat)

Catha edulis has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as maintaining adequate hydration levels and avoiding excessive consumption.

Dehydration can lead to headaches, fatigue, and decreased cognitive function. For instance, neglecting to drink enough water while using khat may result in dehydration headaches, which can worsen over time and lead to more severe health issues.

Adequate hydration helps to mitigate these side effects.

1. Discontinue use if depression worsens

When using Catha edulis medicinally, it's important to take Catha edilis orally.

This is crucial because injecting or smoking the plant can lead to serious health complications, such as cardiac problems, convulsions, and even death. Oral consumption, on the other hand, allows for a more controlled release of its active compounds, reducing the risk of adverse effects while still reaping its potential therapeutic benefits.

Additionally, oral use eliminates the risk of contaminating equipment or spreading blood-borne diseases associated with injection or smoking practices.

3. Discontinue use if depression worsens

When using Catha edulis medicinally, it's important to start with low doses first.

This precaution is crucial because excessive consumption of the plant can lead to adverse effects such as anxiety, agitation, insomnia, and rapid heart rate. By starting with low doses, users can monitor their response to the medication and adjust accordingly, minimizing the risk of negative reactions.

4. Discontinue use if depression worsens

When using Catha edulis medicinally, it's important to monitor vital signs closely, especially heart rate and blood pressure, as its stimulant effects can cause significant increases in these parameters.

This is particularly crucial for individuals with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions or those taking medications that may interact with the plant's active compounds.

Failure to monitor vital signs could lead to adverse events, such as cardiac arrhythmias, hypertension, or even stroke.

5. Discontinue use if depression worsens

When using Catha edulis medicinally, it's important to avoid mixing with MAOIs.

This precaution is crucial because Catha edulis can cause significant increases in blood pressure and heart rate, which can be exacerbated by the potent vasoconstrictive effects of MAOIs.

Combining these two substances can lead to a life-threatening hypertensive crisis, putting individuals at risk for cardiovascular complications, including stroke and cardiac arrhythmias.
