Khat (Catha edulis) Uses

Khat is traditionally used to treat asthma and respiratory conditions like cough and chronic cough. It may also help with digestive issues, including diarrhea, gastrointestinal spasms, and disorders. Additionally, it is used for appetite suppression and to alleviate gastrointestinal distress.

Khat (Catha edulis) Monograph

1. Asthma

Khat is traditionally used in some cultures to alleviate asthma symptoms due to its bronchodilatory effects. The active compounds, such as cathine and cathinone, help relax airway muscles and reduce inflammation. However, its use should be approached with caution due to potential dependency and side effects.

2. Influenza

Khat is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of influenza due to its stimulating and expectorant properties. It helps reduce fever, relieve congestion, and boost energy, supporting the body's recovery. However, its use should be cautious and under professional guidance due to potential side effects.

3. Cough

Khat is traditionally used to alleviate cough symptoms due to its expectorant properties. The plant contains cathinone and cathine, which can help loosen mucus and reduce bronchial irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or chewed to soothe respiratory discomfort. However, its use should be approached with caution due to potential stimulant effects.

4. Chronic cough

Khat is used in traditional medicine to alleviate chronic cough by stimulating the respiratory system and reducing mucus production. It contains cathinone and cathine, which act as bronchodilators and anti-inflammatory agents. These compounds help soothe irritated airways and improve breathing in individuals with persistent coughing.

5. Diarrhea

Khat is used in traditional medicine to treat diarrhea due to its astringent properties, which help reduce intestinal inflammation and fluid loss. It contains compounds like cathinone and cathine that have mild antidiarrheal effects. However, its use should be cautious and guided by a qualified practitioner to avoid potential side effects.

6. Appetite suppression

Khat is traditionally used for appetite suppression due to its stimulant properties, which increase metabolism and reduce hunger sensations. The active alkaloids, such as cathinone and cathine, are believed to affect neurotransmitters in the brain, leading to a temporary decrease in food desire. This effect has been noted in traditional practices across East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

7. Gastrointestinal spasms

Khat is used to alleviate gastrointestinal spasms due to its stimulant properties, which help relax smooth muscle in the digestive tract. It contains cathinone and cathine, which act as mild spasmolytics. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or chewed to ease cramps and discomfort. Its effects are typically mild and short-lived.

8. Digestive issues

Khat is traditionally used to alleviate digestive issues such as bloating and indigestion. The active compounds, like cathinone and cathine, stimulate digestion and improve gut motility. It is often consumed as a tea or chewed to ease discomfort and promote a feeling of well-being. However, long-term use may lead to dependency and gastrointestinal side effects.

9. Gastrointestinal distress

Khat is used to alleviate gastrointestinal distress by stimulating digestion and reducing inflammation in the digestive tract. It contains compounds like cathinone and cathine, which have mild laxative and anti-inflammatory properties. Traditional use involves consuming the leaves to relieve symptoms such as bloating and indigestion. However, long-term use may lead to dependency and gastrointestinal complications.

10. Gastrointestinal disorders

Khat is used in traditional medicine to treat gastrointestinal disorders such as indigestion and stomach pain. The active compound, cathinone, stimulates digestive secretions and improves gut motility. It is often consumed in the form of chewed leaves or brewed into tea. Its effects are believed to provide relief by enhancing digestion and reducing discomfort.
