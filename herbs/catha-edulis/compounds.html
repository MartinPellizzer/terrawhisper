Khat (Catha edulis) Compounds

The primary compounds in Khat include alkaloids, which are the most active and responsible for its stimulating effects. Terpenoids and flavonoids contribute to its flavor and potential health benefits. Phenolic acids and tannins provide antioxidant properties, while glycosides and saponins may support digestive health. Coumarin and essential oils add to its aromatic profile, and anthocyanins contribute to its color and possible anti-inflammatory effects.

Khat (Catha edulis) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound in Khat (Catha edulis), a medicinal plant native to East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It contributes to the plant's stimulating effects and has been traditionally used for its perceived medicinal properties.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Khat (Catha edulis), a plant used in traditional herbal medicine. These compounds contribute to the plant's stimulant effects and are responsible for some of its pharmacological properties. They are often extracted and used in various medicinal preparations.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Khat (Catha edulis). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective effects. These compounds are often studied for their potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Khat (Catha edulis). It contributes to the plant's stimulating effects by interacting with neurotransmitters in the brain. This compound is also associated with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Khat (Catha edulis). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may influence its traditional use in stimulating alertness and appetite. Tannins are found in various parts of the plant and are known for their complex interactions with proteins and other compounds.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Khat (Catha edulis). It contributes to the plant's stimulant effects by interacting with neurotransmitter systems. Glycosides in Khat are often broken down into active alkaloids, which are responsible for its psychoactive and medicinal properties.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Khat (Catha edulis). It contributes to the plant's stimulant effects and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant activities. However, its use in herbal medicine remains limited due to varying potency and potential side effects.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Khat (Catha edulis). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though its specific therapeutic effects are less well-documented compared to other alkaloids. Saponins may have mild detoxifying properties and are often present alongside other bioactive compounds in the plant.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Khat (Catha edulis) contains volatile compounds like pinocarvyl acetate and catharanthine. These compounds contribute to the plant's stimulating effects and are used in traditional medicine for their purported appetite suppression and mood elevation properties.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Khat (Catha edulis). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. In traditional herbal medicine, anthocyanin may support overall health by neutralizing free radicals and promoting cellular integrity.
