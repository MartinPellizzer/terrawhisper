Chinese Soapberry (Sapindus mukorossi)

Chinese Soapberry (Sapindus mukorossi) is a member of the Sapindaceae family, native to India, Nepal, and Bhutan. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its astringent, diuretic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and korean traditional medicine.
