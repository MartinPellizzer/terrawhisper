Caragana brevifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Caragana brevifolia?

Caragana brevifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. This plant is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its distinct floral and fruit structures. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Caragana, with its specific classification being the species brevifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caragana brevifolia has small, elliptical to ovate leaflets arranged in a pinnate configuration along a central rachis, typically measuring only a few centimeters in length. Its stems are woody and robust, providing structural support for the spreading canopy typical of this species. The plant also features inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves during its blooming period.
