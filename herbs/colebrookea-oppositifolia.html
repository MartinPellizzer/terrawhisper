Colebrookea oppositifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Colebrookea oppositifolia?

Colebrookea oppositifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Lamiaceae and is a member of the genus Colebrookea, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species oppositifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Colebrookea oppositifolia contains various bioactive phytochemicals including tannins, saponins, alkaloids, and flavonoids that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the leaves and roots of the plant. Traditional medicinal practices frequently utilize these compounds to treat ailments such as diarrhea, stomach aches, and skin infections. Research suggests that the presence of these specific secondary metabolites provides the plant with significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The complex interaction of these molecules allows the plant to serve as a vital resource in ethnobotanical medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Colebrookea oppositifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, stomach ailments, coughs, and inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to soothe various dermatological issues through topical preparations. Practitioners also utilize the plant to address gastrointestinal distress and digestive irregularities. In some traditional practices, it serves as a remedy for respiratory problems like persistent coughing. These diverse applications highlight its importance in local herbal medicine systems.

What are the herbal preparations of Colebrookea oppositifolia?

Colebrookea oppositifolia is prepared primarily as a decoction of the roots, a leaf infusion, or a bark extract to treat various ailments. These liquid preparations are often administered orally to address issues such as skin infections, intestinal parasites, or fever. In some traditional practices, the plant material is also processed into a topical paste for direct application to wounds or sores. The leaves may be crushed and used in warm water to create a wash for localized inflammation. These diverse methods allow the active botanical compounds to be utilized for both internal and external medicinal purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Colebrookea oppositifolia can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea if consumed in inappropriate quantities. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may lead to unpredictable toxic reactions in sensitive individuals. There is also a risk of systemic toxicity if the dosage is not carefully controlled during traditional medicinal use. Potential side effects may also involve neurological symptoms or metabolic disturbances depending on the part of the plant used. Proper caution is necessary because the exact threshold for human toxicity remains insufficiently documented in clinical studies.
