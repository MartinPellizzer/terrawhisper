Colebrookea oppositifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Colebrookea oppositifolia has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The leaf margins are entire and the venation is distinct, often showing a prominent midrib. This woody shrub or small tree features small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in axillary clusters. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that develops from the flowering structures. Potential side effects may also involve neurological symptoms or metabolic disturbances depending on the part of the plant used. Proper caution is necessary because the exact threshold for human toxicity remains insufficiently documented in clinical studies.
