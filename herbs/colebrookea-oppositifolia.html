Colebrookea oppositifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Colebrookea oppositifolia?

Colebrookea oppositifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Lamiaceae and is a member of the genus Colebrookea, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species oppositifolia.

What are the pharmacological activities of Colebrookea oppositifolia?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-microbial, antibacterial, and antifungal pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents allow it to effectively reduce pain and diminish swelling in biological tissues. The plant also demonstrates significant antimicrobial properties that inhibit the growth of various pathogens. Specifically, it exhibits potent antibacterial effects against certain bacterial strains and antifungal action against diverse fungal species. These multifaceted bioactive properties make the species a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Current topics in medicinal chemistry", Colebrookea oppositifolia is used for the mitigation of nervous system disorders like epilepsy and for treating traumatic injuries, fever, rheumatoid arthritis, headache, and gastric problems. The presence of polyphenols and flavonoids, such as quercetin, landenein, chrysin, and 5, 6, 7-trimethoxy flavones, causes protein denaturation of microbial cell walls. The phenylethanoid glycoside acteoside, isolated from aerial parts, has been evaluated for immunomodulatory, neuroprotective, hepatoprotective, analgesic, anti-tumour, antispasmodic, antioxidant, antibacterial, and free radical scavenger activities. Acteoside specifically shows neuroprotective activities against Aβ-peptide. Additionally, the petroleum ether extract of leaves contains compounds like sitosterol, n-triaconatane, hydroxydotriacontyl ferulate, acetyl alcohol, and 3,7,4,2-tetramethoxyflavones which demonstrate hepatoprotective potential.
