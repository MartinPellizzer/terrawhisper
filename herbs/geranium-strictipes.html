What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium strictipes?

Geranium strictipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Geraniales and the family Geraniaceae. Finally, it is identified at the genus level as Geraniuim and is specifically designated as the species strictipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium strictipes has erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The leaves are deeply palmately lobed with serrated margins and are arranged in a basal rosette or along the stem. Its flowers consist of five bright pink to purple petals that surround a central cluster of numerous stamens. The pedicels are relatively long and thin, supporting the blooms in a loose, upright structure. This plant's overall morphology is characterized by a delicate, upright growth habit suited for rocky or open habitats.
