Ophioglossum vulgatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophioglossum vulgatum?

Ophioglossum vulgatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Ophioglossidae and the order Ophioglossales. This plant is a representative of the family Ophioglossaceae, falling under the genus Ophioglossum, with its specific designation being the species vulgatum.

What are the herbal preparations of Ophioglossum vulgatum?

Ophioglossum vulgatum is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried rhizome or as a tincture extracted in alcohol. Practitioners often use these liquid preparations to concentrate the plant's active constituents for medicinal use. The powdered rhizome can also be incorporated into capsules or combined with other botanical ingredients in traditional formulations. Some historical applications involve applying a poultice made from the crushed plant material directly to the skin. These various methods allow for different absorption rates and therapeutic concentrations depending on the intended use.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Ophioglossum vulgatum can cause skin irritation, redness, and localized allergic reactions if handled without protection. While the plant is not typically classified as highly toxic to humans, direct contact with its spores or tissues may trigger dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience itching or a burning sensation upon touching the foliage. There is also a risk of gastrointestinal distress if any part of the fern is accidentally ingested. It is always advisable to use gloves when working with this species to prevent potential dermatological issues.
