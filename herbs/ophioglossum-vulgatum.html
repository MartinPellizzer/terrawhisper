What's the taxonomical classification of Ophioglossum vulgatum?

Ophioglossum vulgatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Ophioglossidae and the order Ophioglossales. This plant is a representative of the family Ophioglossaceae, falling under the genus Ophioglossum, with its specific designation being the species vulgatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophioglossum vulgatum has a single, fleshy, unbranched subterranean stem that produces a simple, linear, grass-like sterile leaf and a separate, upright, fertile spike. The sterile portion consists of a long, narrow, dark green blade that lacks a distinct midrib. This blade emerges directly from the ground to provide photosynthetic energy for the plant. The fertile spike grows centrally from the base and carries numerous small, brownish spores in dense clusters. Once the spores are released, the entire structure typically withers away.
