Gynura bicolor

What's the taxonomical classification of Gynura bicolor?

Gyunura bicolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asterales, the plant is placed in the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Gyunura and the species bicolor.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Gynura bicolor contains bioactive phytochemicals including flavonoids, terpenoids, and various phenolic compounds that provide therapeutic potential. These secondary metabolites are often studied for their antioxidant properties which help neutralize free radicals in biological systems. Some research suggests that the plant's chemical profile may contribute to anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. The specific concentration of these compounds can vary significantly based on the plant's environmental conditions and soil composition. Understanding these chemical constituents is essential for evaluating the plant's potential in traditional and modern pharmacological applications.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various gastrointestinal ailments, including stomach aches, diarrhea, and general indigestion. It contains bioactive compounds that help soothe the digestive tract and reduce inflammation. Local traditional medicine often utilizes its properties to manage intestinal parasites and dysentery. Some practitioners also apply the plant to address skin infections and minor wounds. These medicinal applications are rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical practices within specific regional cultures.
