What's the taxonomical classification of Decumaria sinensis?

Decumaria sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the order Cornales and is a member of the family Hydrangeaceae. Taxonomically, it is placed within the genus Decumaria, with its specific identification being the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Decumaria sinensis has woody stems with pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of leaflets that are elliptic to oblong in shape. The foliage is characterized by an entire margin and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, typically appearing in clusters. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. The overall growth habit is that of a climbing or scrambling shrub found in tropical forest understories.
