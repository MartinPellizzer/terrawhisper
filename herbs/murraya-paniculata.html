Murraya paniculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Murraya paniculata?

Murraya paniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Rutaceae and is situated in the genus Murraya, specifically identified by its species name, paniculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murraya paniculata has pinnately compound leaves consisting of five to nine elliptical, glossy green leaflets that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are entire and the texture is somewhat coriaceous, providing a dense, bushy appearance to the shrub. Small, white, fragrant flowers emerge from terminal panicles and possess five delicate petals. These blossoms transition into small, fleshy, oval berries that change from green to a bright red color when fully ripe. The plant typically develops a woody stem structure that supports its compact and spreading growth habit.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning from India and Sri Lanka through Indochina to parts of southern China. It thrives in various habitats such as secondary forests, scrublands, and coastal areas where moisture is abundant. Within these regions, it is often found growing in shaded environments under the canopy of larger trees. The species is also distributed across several island territories in the Indo-Malayan realm. Its range is largely defined by warm climates that support its perennial growth patterns.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds murranganone, paniculatin, yuehchukene, ellagic acid, and spathulenol. These specific bioactive molecules contribute to the diverse therapeutic potential observed in the plant's chemical profile. Murranganone and paniculatin are notable for their distinct structural roles in biological interactions. The presence of ellagic acid and spathulenol often suggests significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Together, these constituents form a complex chemical foundation that researchers study for pharmacological development.

As discussed in a study, Murraya paniculata contains three natural coumarins which include 2'-O-ethylmurrangatin (1), murranganone (2), and paniculatin (3) isolated from its leaves. While compound 1 was found to be inactive against both enzymes, compounds 2 and 3 were identified as potent inhibitors of AChE and BChE. The most active compound, paniculatin (3), showed a ligand-AChE complex stability comparable to the drug Tacrine according to MD simulation studies. These findings suggest that the coumarin nucleus with diverse side chains at the C-8 position can serve as a potential inhibitor of cholinesterases.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive upset if ingested in significant quantities. While the flowers are often used for fragrance, direct contact with the sap may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some reports suggest that consuming parts of the plant could lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. There is limited clinical research available to fully document the long-term toxicological effects on humans. Consequently, caution should be exercised when handling or consuming any part of the Murraya paniculata.
