Melodinus suaveolens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Melodinus suaveolens has a prostrate, creeping growth habit characterized by slender, trailing stems and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The foliage is typically thin and delicate, often exhibiting a slightly succulent texture depending on environmental moisture. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters or solitary arrangements. These reproductive structures are generally pale in color and possess a subtle fragrance that contributes to the species' specific epithet. The root system is relatively shallow, allowing the plant to spread efficiently across sandy or loose substrates.
