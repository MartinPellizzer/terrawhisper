Side Effects of corylus avellana

Corylus avellana leads to allergic reactions in individuals who are sensitive to hazelnuts or related allergens, manifesting as hives, itching, or even anaphylaxis in severe cases.

It can cause skin irritation, resulting in redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon contact with the plant's oils or extracts. The herb may provoke nasal congestion and sneezing fits, similar to allergic rhinitis symptoms, due to its pollen or chemical components. It also induces skin rashes and redness, often appearing as a delayed hypersensitivity response.

Additionally, Corylus avellana can trigger eye inflammation and irritation, leading to redness, itching, or a gritty sensation in the eyes. Furthermore, it may cause respiratory issues such as coughing or wheezing, especially in individuals with asthma or existing respiratory conditions. The herb can also result in digestive discomfort, including nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramps, when ingested in large quantities.

Overall, while Corylus avellana has potential medicinal uses, its side effects highlight the importance of caution and proper identification of allergic sensitivities before use.
