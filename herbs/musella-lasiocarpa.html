Musella lasiocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Musella lasiocarpa has woody stems and large, ovate leaves characterized by a dense covering of fine, soft hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. The foliage displays a deep green color and prominent venation that is easily visible under close inspection. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the production of fruit covered in thick, woolly pubescence. These fuzzy structures emerge from the axils of the leaves during the reproductive phase. The overall architecture of the plant reflects its adaptation to specific environmental niches through its textured surface area.
