What's the taxonomical classification of Musella lasiocarpa?

Musella lasiocarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Zingiberales. This species is a member of the family Musaceae, falling under the genus Musella, and is specifically identified by its species name, lasiocarpa.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in plant science", Musella lasiocarpa is a phenylphenalenone-rich plant species. The study identified 158 WRKY genes, known as MlWRKYs, within the plant. These MlWRKY genes are clustered on 9 respective chromosomes and are classified into 3 distinct subfamilies: type I, type II, and type III. Specific candidate genes identified for regulating phenylphenalenone biosynthesis include MlWRKY15, MlWRKY111, and MlWRKY122.
