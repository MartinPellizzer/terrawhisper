What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Davalliia trichomanoides has a creeping rhizome covered in dense, white, fuzzy scales that protects the plant's growing points. The plant's reproductive structures are located on the underside of the leaves in the form of small, round sori. This unique morphology allows the fern to cling effectively to surfaces while maximizing surface area for light absorption.
