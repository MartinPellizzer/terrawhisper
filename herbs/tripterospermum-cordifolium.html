Tripterospermum cordifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tripterospermum cordifoliuum has heart-shaped leaves with entire margins, a woody stem structure, and distinctive three-winged seeds. The foliage is characterized by its cordate shape and prominent venation patterns that extend from the base toward the edges. Its flowering stems produce small blossoms that eventually develop into the specialized fruit. These fruits are most notable for their winged appendages which facilitate wind dispersal. The overall morphology reflects an adaptation to specific environmental niches within its native habitat.
