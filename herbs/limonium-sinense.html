Limonium sinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Limonium sinense?

Limonium sinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is categorized under the order Caryophyllales. It is a member of the family Plumbaginaceae and is further defined by its placement within the genus Limonium, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species sinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Limonium sinense has perennial, herbaceous, or subshrubby growth habits characterized by erect, branching stems and narrow, linear, or lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in basal rosettes. The foliage often exhibits a grayish-green hue due to a fine covering of hairs on the leaf surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal panicles composed of numerous small, delicate flowers that can vary in color from white to pale lavender. The individual flowers are structurally complex, featuring small petals and prominent stamens that contribute to the textured appearance of the cluster. The root system is generally sturdy and well-developed to support its upright structure in coastal or saline environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the coastal regions of southeastern China, specifically within provinces such as Zhejiang and Fujian. It primarily inhabits saline environments such as salt marshes and coastal wetlands where it can tolerate high salt concentrations. Its distribution is closely tied to tidal zones and sandy shorelines along the East China Sea. The species thrives in these specific maritime habitats due to its specialized physiological adaptations to brackish water. Consequently, its range remains restricted to these particular coastal ecosystems.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant Limonium sinense requires well-draining, sandy or loamy soil and full sunlight to thrive in a temperate or subtropical climate.

It is best cultivated in areas that experience moderate moisture but avoid prolonged waterlogging around the root zone. Regular watering is necessary during the active growing season, though the species exhibits significant drought tolerance once established. Providing adequate spacing between individual plants helps ensure sufficient air circulation to prevent fungal issues.

What are the pharmacological activities of Limonium sinense?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidative, anti-tumor, antiproliferative, and hepatoprotective pharmacological activities. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds effectively reduce oxidative stress and combat systemic inflammation. These properties also allow the plant to exhibit potent anti-tumor and antiproliferative effects against various cancer cell lines. Furthermore, its hepatoprotective qualities suggest a significant ability to protect the liver from chemical or toxic damage. These diverse biological functions highlight its potential value in medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", UPLC-MS/MS methods identified 109 compounds in Limonium sinense, which mainly include flavonoids, alkaloids, and polyphenols. The potential bioactivities of Limonium sinense considered in the study are anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidative, anti-tumor, hepatoprotective, and hyperglycemic activities. Four derivatives of 12-oxo-phytoienoic acid and butenolide, including 2 new ones identified as 1 and 2, were isolated from the whole plants of Limonium sinense. All of these isolates were evaluated for their anti-inflammatory activity. Compound 1 exhibited moderate anti-inflammatory activity with an IC50 value of 37.5±1.2 μM on the NO production level.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds sinenseines A-F, flavonoids, flavonols, sesquiterpenoids, and phenolics. These diverse chemical constituents contribute to the biological activities and therapeutic potential of the species. The presence of various flavonoids and flavonols provides significant antioxidant properties to the plant matter. Additionally, the sesquiterpenoids and phenolic compounds play critical roles in its overall chemical profile. Together, these substances form a complex pharmacological foundation for the plant.

According to a study published by "RSC advances", ten drimane-type sesquiterpenoids and four triterpenoids, including six new ones named sinenseines A-F, were isolated from the whole plant of Limonium sinense. Twelve terpenoids, including nine sesquiterpenoids, were identified using UPLC-MS/MS and GNPS methods. Compounds 2-4, 6, 13, and 14 demonstrated moderate anti-tumor effects on A549, H1299, HepG2 and A2780 cells with IC50 values ranging from 35.2 ± 2.0 to 90.5 ± 3.1 μM. Additionally, compound 1 exhibited significant anti-inflammatory activity against NO production in LPS-induced RAW 264.7 macrophages with an IC50 value of 8.3 ± 1.2 μM.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating hepatitis, liver damage, hyperglycemia, inflammation, and tumors. It contains bioactive compounds that help protect hepatic cells from injury and promote healing in the liver. The plant also helps regulate blood sugar levels to combat hyperglycemia. Its natural anti-inflammatory properties assist in reducing swelling and systemic irritation throughout the body. Additionally, certain extracts are being studied for their potential role in inhibiting tumor growth.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in plant science", Limonium sinense (Girard) Kuntze is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb that demonstrates activities such as blood replenishment, anti-tumour, anti-hepatitis, and immunomodulation. Water extracts from Limonium sinense (LSW) exhibited a strong growth inhibitory effect on multiple cells within both 2D and 3D cultures. Global transcriptomic profiling and connectivity map (CMap) analysis identified the plant's bioactive ingredients as potential tubulin inhibitors and hypoxia-inducible factor (HIF) modulators. Furthermore, flow cytometry verified the effect of LSW on the cell cycle by showing a G2/M phase arrest.
