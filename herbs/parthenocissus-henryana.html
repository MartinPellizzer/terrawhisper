What is Parthenocissus henryana?

Parthenocissus henryana is a deciduous climber native to China, belonging to the family Vitaceae.

It has pinnate leaves with 7-11 leaflets, 6-12 cm long, that are opposite and stipulate, with a rounded base and a pointed tip, and a wavy margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, 10-15 cm long, in summer, and red or purple berries, 5-6 mm in diameter, in autumn. The stems are twining, 3-5 mm in diameter, with 1-2 mm long, brown, and hairy nodes.

The plant grows to 4-6 m in length, and is often used as an ornamental, for its rapid growth and attractive foliage.
