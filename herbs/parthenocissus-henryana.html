Parthenocissus henryana

What's the taxonomical classification of Parthenocissus henryana?

Parthenociscus henryana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Vitales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Vitaceae, within the genus Parthenocissus, and specifically identifies it as the species henryana.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Parthenocissus henryana can cause skin irritation, redness, and blistering upon contact due to the presence of urushiol, the same allergenic oil found in poison ivy. This organic compound can trigger a delayed hypersensitivity reaction known as contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Symptoms typically manifest as an itchy, bumpy rash that may persist for several days or weeks. While the risk is lower than that of poison ivy, direct handling of the leaves or stems should still be avoided to prevent an outbreak. People with known allergies to toxicodendron species should exercise extreme caution around this climbing vine.
