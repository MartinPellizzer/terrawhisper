What's the taxonomical classification of Primula forrestii?

Primula forrestii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Primulaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Primula, with its specific identity defined by the species name forrestii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula forrestii has a basal rosette of long, elliptical, bright green leaves that feature finely serrated margins and slightly wrinkled surfaces. These leaves are supported by fleshy petioles that emerge from a central crown. The plant produces upright flowering stalks that can reach several inches in height. Each stalk bears a cluster of salverform flowers characterized by deep violet or purple corollas and distinct yellow eyes. The delicate texture of the foliage complements the vibrant, clustered blooms typical of this primrose species.
