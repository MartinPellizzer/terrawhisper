    What is Rhipsalis cruciformis?

    Rhipsalis cruciformis is a species of epiphytic cactus that belongs to the family Cactaceae.

    It is characterized by its cruciform (cross-shaped) stems, which are green, slender, and up to 20 cm in length. The stems are covered with short, needle-like spines, and are supported by a network of fine, white roots. The plant produces small, white flowers in the summer months, which are followed by red or yellow fruits.

    Rhipsalis cruciformis is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, and is often found growing on trees and rocks in humid, shaded areas.
