Myricaria rosea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Myrica rosea has an erect, woody shrub or small tree growth habit characterized by lanceolate, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along its branches. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color on the upper surface and often features a paler, sometimes silvery underside. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, leading to the development of fleshy, drupe-like fruits. These fruits are often reddish or pinkish in hue, giving the plant its specific name. The bark on mature stems tends to become textured and develops a grayish-brown appearance over time.
