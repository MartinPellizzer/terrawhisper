What are the medicinal properties of Ervatamia coronaria?

Alkaloids like vincamine and ervatamine exhibit vasodilatory and spasmolytic activities. Flavonoids and terpenoids also contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

The parts of the Ervatamia coronaria plant most used for medicinal purposes are its leaves, roots, and stem. The leaves are rich in flavonoids and terpenoids, while the roots and stem contain alkaloids. The leaves and roots are used to treat fever, while the stem is used for rheumatism and skin problems.

Improper use of Ervatamia coronaria can lead to gastrointestinal side effects, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. High doses can also cause cardiovascular problems, such as hypertension and arrhythmia. Long-term use may result in dependence and toxicity.

Precautions when using Ervatamia coronaria medicinally include consulting a healthcare professional before use, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women, and those with kidney or liver problems. Dose and duration of treatment should be strictly followed, and patients should be aware of potential side effects and interactions with other medications.
