Crown Vetch (Ervatamia coronaria)

Crown Vetch (Ervatamia coronaria) is a member of the Acanthaceae family, native to Mediterranean Region, Southern Europe, and Middle East. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, tonic, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and african traditional medicine.

Inflorescences are racemose clusters of small white zygomorphic flowers with five fused petals and a prominent yellow keel.
Stems
Erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth surface, and presence of stipitate nodes.
