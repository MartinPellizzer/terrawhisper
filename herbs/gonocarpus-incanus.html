What is Gonocarpus incanus?

Gonocarpus incanus is a shrub-like plant that belongs to the family Haloragaceae.

It has a distinctive leaf arrangement, with opposite, simple, and entire leaves that are 1-7 cm long and 5-20 mm wide, and have a glabrous or slightly hairy surface. The leaves are sessile or have a very short petiole, and the leaf margin is ciliate. The plant produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers in axillary cymes, which are arranged in a racemose inflorescence.

The flowers are 4-merous, with 4 sepals and 4 petals, and contain 8 stamens and a pistil with 4 carpels.
