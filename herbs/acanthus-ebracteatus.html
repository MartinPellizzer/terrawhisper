Acanthus ebracteatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Acanthus ebracteatus?

Acanthus ebracteatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Lamiales and belongs to the family Acanthaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by its genus, Acanthus, and its unique species designation, ebracteatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acanthus ebracteatous has deeply lobed, sessile leaves with spiny margins and small, inconspicuous white to pale pink flowers that lack bracts. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green color and a rugged, architectural texture common to the Acanthaceae family. Its stems are erect and somewhat woody at the base, supporting the heavy, serrated leaf structure. The inflorescence is relatively subtle compared to its close relatives, appearing as small clusters rather than large, showy spikes. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in shaded understory environments where its broad leaves can capture minimal light.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains ebracteatoside A, ebracteatosides B-D, benzoxazinoid glycosides, a significant total phenolic content, and megastigmane. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the complex pharmacological profile of the species. The presence of ebracteatosides suggests specialized secondary metabolites that may play roles in biological defense. Furthermore, the benzoxazinoid glycosides and high total phenolic content are often linked to various antioxidant properties. The inclusion of megastigmane adds another layer to the diverse array of bioactive molecules found within its tissues.

As discussed in a study, the aerial part of Acanthus ebracteatus contains several medicinal compounds. Researchers isolated 1 megastigmane glycoside known as ebracteatoside A. The plant also contains 3 aliphaic alcohol glycosides called ebracteatosides B-D. Additionally, the compound 7-chloro-(2R)-2-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-4-hydroxy-2H-1,4-benzoxazin-3(4H)-one, or 7-Cl-DIBOA-Glc, was identified. These substances were isolated along with 22 known compounds.
