What's the taxonomical classification of Hibiscus surattensis?

Hibiscus surattensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the biological order Malvales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Malvaceae and the genus Hibiscus, ultimately identified by its specific species name, surattensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hibiscus surattensis has woody, perennial stems with ovate, serrated leaves and large, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically feature five petals in shades of red, pink, or yellow. Its reproductive structures include a prominent central column where the numerous stamens are fused around the style. The flowers are supported by long pedicels and emerge from the leaf axils or terminal positions. The root system is robust and woody, providing stability for its shrubby growth habit.
