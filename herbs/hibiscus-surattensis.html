Hibiscus surattensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hibiscus surattensis?

Hibiscus surattensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the biological order Malvales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Malvaceae and the genus Hibiscus, ultimately identified by its specific species name, surattensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hibiscus surattensis has woody, perennial stems with ovate, serrated leaves and large, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically feature five petals in shades of red, pink, or yellow. The leaves are characterized by an acute apex and a distinctively serrated margin, often displaying a glossy texture on the upper surface. Its reproductive structures include a prominent central column where the numerous stamens are fused around the style. The flowers are supported by long pedicels and emerge from the leaf axils or terminal positions. The root system is robust and woody, providing stability for its shrubby growth habit.

What are the pharmacological activities of Hibiscus surattensis?

This plant has potent antiplasmodial and dermatological activities that make it a valuable subject for medicinal research. The extracts derived from its various parts demonstrate the ability to inhibit the growth of Plasmodium parasites, which are responsible for malaria. Additionally, the phytochemical constituents contribute to its effectiveness in treating various dermatological infections through antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory mechanisms. These bioactive compounds work by disrupting the cellular processes of pathogens on the skin surface. Consequently, Hibiscus suratensis serves as a promising natural source for developing new therapeutic agents against both parasitic and skin-related ailments.

According to a study published by "Journal of parasitology research", the ethanolic plant extract of Hibiscus surattensis L. has shown inhibitory activity against asexual P. falciparum. This extract selected new allelic variants of the Msp1 and Msp2 genes compared to unexposed parasites. The newly selected allelic variants included K1_100bp and RO33_300bp of the Msp1 gene. Additionally, the Msp2 gene variants selected were FC27_150bp, FC27_300bp, FC27_400bp, and FC27_600bp. There was no significant difference in the multiplicity of infection (MOI) between drug-exposed and unexposed parasites.
