Tansy (Tanacetum annuum) Compounds

Tansy contains essential oil as its primary compound, rich in terpenoids like thujone and camphor. It also includes flavonoids, coumarin, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Tannins, saponins, and glycosides provide additional therapeutic effects, while alkaloids and anthocyanins add to its complex chemical profile.

Tansy (Tanacetum annuum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Tansy (Tanacetum annuum) is a key active compound, containing compounds like thujone and camphor. It has antimicrobial and antiparasitic properties, making it historically used for treating digestive issues and expelling worms. The oil's potency requires careful handling due to its toxicity in high doses.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Tansy (Tanacetum annuum). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. These compounds are responsible for the plant's distinctive aroma and are often used in herbal formulations for their therapeutic benefits.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Tansy (Tanacetum annuum) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They support the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory issues. Flavonoids enhance the plant's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Tansy (Tanacetum annuum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly its antispasmodic and anticoagulant effects. Coumarin also plays a role in the plant's ability to repel pests and has been studied for its potential therapeutic uses in herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Tansy (Tanacetum annuum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This compound helps in treating digestive issues and skin infections. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Tansy in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Tansy (Tanacetum annuum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antimicrobial effects, which enhance Tansy's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Tansy (Tanacetum annuum). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has mild detergent properties. Saponin may support digestive health and has been traditionally used for its antimicrobial effects.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Tansy (Tanacetum annuum). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has potential antimicrobial properties. Glycosides in Tansy may support digestive health and have traditional uses in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Tansy (Tanacetum annuum) are bioactive compounds with potential medicinal properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in digestive and anti-inflammatory treatments. These compounds may support the plant's role in herbal medicine, though research on their specific effects is ongoing.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Tansy (Tanacetum annuum). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower colors and has antioxidant properties. While not the primary medicinal component, anthocyanin may support overall health by neutralizing free radicals. Its role in Tansy's therapeutic use is secondary to other compounds like thujone.
