Botanical Identification of Tansy (Tanacetum annuum)

To identify Tansy (Tanacetum annuum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Tansy (Tanacetum annuum) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Tansy, or Tanacetum annuum, is classified within the plant kingdom, specifically in the division of flowering plants. It belongs to the class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, and family Asteraceae, which includes many familiar flowering plants. The genus Tanacetum encompasses various species, with T. annuum being one of the most recognized.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Tanacetum annuum is the scientific name for Tansy. It was first described by Carl von Linné, hence the author citation is Linnaeus. Synonyms include Anthemis annua and T. vulgare. Common names include Tansy, Yellow buttons, and Golden Button. Regional names vary, with some areas calling it Queen Anne's Lace. Historically, it was also known as Common Tansy and Yellow Tansy.

Morphological Characteristics

Tansy is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. It has upright, branching stems that are hairy and typically grow up to 60 cm tall. The leaves are dark green, alternate, and have a strong, pungent scent. The plant produces a flat-topped inflorescence with yellow composite flowers, and the fruit is a small, dry achene with a pappus for wind dispersal.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Tansy can be distinguished by its bright yellow buttonhead flower, finely divided grey-green leaves, and distinctive white, tubular flower stems. It grows in moist areas and has a strong, aromatic scent. It is often confused with similar yellow-flowered plants but lacks the fuzzy texture and different leaf shape of others.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Tansy is native to Europe and parts of Asia, thriving in temperate climates. It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. It grows best at low to moderate altitudes, typically between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Tansy blooms from late summer to early autumn, typically between July and September. Seed formation occurs in late autumn, around October to November. For identification purposes, harvest timing is most relevant during the flowering period when the plant is most easily recognized.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Tansy (Tanacetum annuum) is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family, particularly species within the Tanacetum genus. It shares key family traits such as composite flowers and aromatic compounds. Its closest relatives include other yellow-flowered plants like Pyrethrum and certain Artemisia species. These relationships help in accurate identification and understanding its medicinal properties.
