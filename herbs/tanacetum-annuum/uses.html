Tansy (Tanacetum annuum) Uses

Tansy is used to treat respiratory infections, including bronchitis, the common cold, sinusitis, and asthma. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing, runny nose, and inflammation. Its antimicrobial properties support healing in skin infections and may ease allergic rhinitis by reducing nasal congestion. It is also used for flu symptoms and general cold relief.

Tansy (Tanacetum annuum) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Tansy is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and clearing mucus. It may help alleviate symptoms of bronchitis, coughs, and other respiratory infections. Its antimicrobial properties can assist in fighting bacterial and viral infections. Tansy is often prepared as a tea or tincture for internal use.

2. Bronchitis

Tansy has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce mucus production and soothe bronchial irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease coughing and improve breathing. However, it should be used with caution due to its potential toxicity if not properly prepared.

3. Common cold

Tansy is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its antiseptic and expectorant properties. It helps reduce congestion and soothe sore throats, often prepared as a tea or steam inhalation. Its effectiveness is supported by historical use in herbal medicine for respiratory support during colds.

4. Sinusitis

Tansy is used to treat sinusitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and combat bacterial infections in the sinuses. Typically prepared as a steam inhalation or gargle, it provides relief from symptoms associated with sinus inflammation. Its effectiveness is supported by traditional herbal practices and some modern research.

5. Asthma

Tansy has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. Its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce bronchial constriction and mucus production. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance due to its potential toxicity.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Tansy has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and sneezing by supporting the body's natural response to allergens. Its essential oils contain compounds that may inhibit histamine release, offering a natural alternative for managing seasonal allergies.

7. Cold symptoms

Tansy is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its antispasmodic and expectorant properties. It helps reduce congestion and soothe respiratory discomfort. Often prepared as a tea, it supports the body's natural healing process during mild colds. Its bitter compounds stimulate mucus flow and ease breathing.

8. Cough

Tansy is used to treat coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Typically prepared as a tea, it supports clearing congestion and easing breathing in mild respiratory conditions.

9. Influenza

Tansy has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, including symptoms of influenza. Its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce fever, congestion, and cough. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease flu symptoms and support the immune system. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.

10. Skin infections

Tansy is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce bacterial growth and inflammation, making it effective for conditions like eczema and fungal infections. Its essential oils are often diluted and applied directly to the affected area for localized treatment.
