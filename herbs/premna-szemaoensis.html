Premna szemaoensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Premna szemaoensis?

Premna szemaoensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized into the order Lamiales. As a member of the family Lamiaceae, this plant is situated within the genus Premna, where it is specifically identified by the species name szemaoensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Premna szemaoensis has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins and a characteristic puberulent surface. The foliage typically exhibits prominent pinnate venation and a slightly coriaceous texture. Its stems are woody and can develop a somewhat rough bark as the plant matures. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary cymes. These flowers eventually produce small, fleshy drupes that serve as the plant's primary fruit structure.

What are the pharmacological activities of Premna szemaoensis?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer properties through various bioactive compounds. Its antioxidant capabilities help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. The anti-inflammatory effects are characterized by the plant's ability to modulate immune responses and decrease swelling. Furthermore, research suggests that certain extracts from the species exhibit anticancer potential by inhibiting the growth of malignant cells. These multifaceted medicinal qualities make it a subject of interest for drug development and natural therapy research.

As discussed in a study, the natural compound Teuvincenone F was isolated and purified from the stems and leaves of Premna szemaoensis. This compound significantly inhibits lipopolysaccharide (LPS)-induced pro-inflammatory cytokines production and NLRP3 inflammasome activation. Teuvincenone F attenuates K63-linked ubiquitination of NF-κB-essential modulator (NEMO) to suppress LPS-induced phosphorylation of NF-κB. It also inhibits the mRNA expression of IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α, and NLRP3. Furthermore, the decreased NLRP3 expression by Teuviincenone F suppresses NLRP3 inflammasome activation and IL-1β/IL-18 maturation. In vivo results revealed that Teuviincenone F treatment relieved LPS-induced inflammation.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Cirsitakaoside, Teuviincenone F, abiatene diterpenoids, and sesquiterpenoids A-L. These bioactive constituents contribute to the complex chemical profile of the species. The abiatene diterpenoids and various sesquiterpenoids A-L are often studied for their specific biological activities. Additionally, the presence of Cirsitakaoside and Teuviincenone F highlights the diverse secondary metabolites found within its tissues. Researchers examine these specific substances to understand their potential therapeutic applications.

As discussed in a study, cirsitakaoside is a natural compound isolated from Premna szemaoensis. This compound could suppress the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines such as interleukin-1β (IL-1β), interleukin-6 (IL-6) and tumor necrosis factor alpha (TNF-α) in a dose-dependent manner in LPS-stimulated mouse peritoneal macrophages and RAW264.7 cells. It also inhibits inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS) mRNA and cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) mRNA expression. These effects were partially carried out by inactivated nuclear factor-κB (NF-κB) and Mitogen-activated protein kinases (MAPKs) pathways via inhibiting the phosphorylation of the IKKα/β, IκBα and c-Jun N-terminal kinase/stress-activated protein kinase (JNK) in LPS-stimulated murine macrophages. In vivo, cirsitakaoside could relieve LPS-induced inflammation response.
