Premna szemaoensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Premna szemaoensis?

Premna szemaoensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized into the order Lamiales. As a member of the family Lamiaceae, this plant is situated within the genus Premna, where it is specifically identified by the species name szemaoensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Premna szemaoensis has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins and a characteristic puberulent surface. The foliage typically exhibits prominent pinnate venation and a slightly coriaceous texture. Its stems are woody and can develop a somewhat rough bark as the plant matures. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary cymes. These flowers eventually produce small, fleshy drupes that serve as the plant's primary fruit structure.

What are the pharmacological activities of Premna szemaoensis?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer properties. Its bioactive compounds work to neutralize free radicals, which helps protect cells from oxidative damage. These chemical constituents also effectively reduce inflammation by modulating specific biological pathways within the body. Furthermore, research indicates that certain extracts from the plant can inhibit the growth and spread of various cancer cell lines. These diverse medicinal qualities make it a subject of great interest in pharmaceutical development.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in pharmacology", the natural compound Teuvincenone F was isolated and purified from the stems and leaves of Premna szemaoensis. This compound could significantly inhibit lipopolysaccharide (LPS)-induced pro-inflammatory cytokines production and NLRP3 inflammasome activation. Teuviincenone F attenuated K63-linked ubiquitination of NF-κB-essential modulator (NEMO) to suppress LPS-induced phosphorylation of NF-κB and inhibited mRNA expression of IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α, and NLRP3. Furthermore, the decreased NLRP3 expression caused by Teuviincenone F suppressed NLRP3 inflammasome activation and IL-1β/IL-18 maturation. In vivo results revealed that Teuviincenone F treatment relieved LPS-induced inflammation.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds cirsitakaoside, teuviincenone F, abiatene diterpenoids, and szemaoenoids A-L. These specific phytochemicals are responsible for the diverse biological activities associated with the species. Research into these chemical constituents often focuses on their potential therapeutic properties. The structural variety of these molecules provides a foundation for further pharmacological studies. Understanding these compounds helps clarify how the plant interacts with biological systems.

According to a study published by "International immunopharmacology", cirsitakaoside is a natural compound isolated from Premna szemaoensis. This medicinal compound can suppress the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines including interleukin-1β (IL-1β), interleukin-6 (IL-6), and tumor necrosis factor alpha (TNF-α) in a dose-dependent manner. In LPS-stimulated mouse peritoneal macrophages and RAW264.7 cells, cirsitakaoside inhibits inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS) mRNA and cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) mRNA expression. The effects are partially carried out by inactivating the NF-κB and MAPKs pathways through the inhibition of phosphorylation of IKKα/β, IκBα, and c-Jun N-terminal kinase/stress-activated protein kinase (JNK). In vivo results further demonstrate that cirsitakaoside can relieve LPS-induced inflammation responses.
