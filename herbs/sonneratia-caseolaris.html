Sonneratia caseolaris

What's the taxonomical classification of Sonneratia caseolaris?

Sonneratia caseolaris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and placed in the order Myrtales. The plant is a member of the family Lythraceae, falling under the genus Sonneratia, with its specific designation being the species caseolaris.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Sonneratia caseolaris contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and terpenoids that exhibit significant pharmacological properties. These chemical constituents provide the plant with potent antioxidant and antimicrobial capabilities. Research indicates that the extracts possess anti-inflammatory effects that can help mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Additionally, the presence of specific phenolic compounds contributes to its potential as a natural source for therapeutic development. The diverse profile of these medicinal compounds makes the species a subject of interest in ethnopharmacology and drug discovery.

What are the therapeutic actions of Sonneratia caseolaris?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, diarrhea, and various types of inflammation. The leaves and roots contain bioactive compounds that possess significant antimicrobial and analgesic properties. In traditional medicine, a paste made from the leaves is often applied topically to promote healing in cuts and sores. Additionally, decoctions derived from the plant are ingested to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and reduce internal swelling. These therapeutic applications make it a versatile component in many herbal remedy practices.
