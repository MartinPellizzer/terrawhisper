Actaea erythrocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Actaea erythrocarpa has herbaceous stems that produce pinnately compound leaves and terminal clusters of small white flowers followed by bright red fleshy berries. The foliage consists of multiple leaflets that are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape with serrated margins. During the flowering stage, the inflorescence presents as a dense raceme that draws pollinators with its contrasting colors. As the plant matures, the development of the scarlet drupes becomes the most distinctive morphological feature of the species. These vibrant fruits are held upright on the stalks to facilitate seed dispersal.

      Actaea erythrocarpa can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential neurological symptoms if ingested or handled improperly. Caution is necessary because these physiological responses can vary depending on the individual's sensitivity and the amount of exposure.
