Aster incisus

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster incisus?

Aster incisus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Aster, specifically identified as the species inci sus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aster incisus contains high concentrations of sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and various phenolic acids that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These sesquiterpene lactones are primarily responsible for the plant's significant anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. The presence of specific flavonoids helps provide antioxidant protection by neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. Additionally, the phenolic compounds assist in supporting immune responses and protecting cellular structures from oxidative stress. Together, these chemical constituents form a complex profile used in traditional herbal medicine to treat skin irritations and internal inflammation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aster incisus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as coughs, bronchitis, and asthma, as well as digestive issues like stomach aches and indigestion. The leaves and flowers often contain compounds that act as expectorants to help clear mucus from the lungs. Some traditional medicinal practices also utilize the plant to soothe skin inflammations and minor wounds. Its antimicrobial properties may assist in fighting mild infections throughout the body. Practitioners typically prepare it as a tea or a topical poultice depending on the specific ailment.
