What's the taxonomical classification of Teucrium simplex?

Teucrium simplex belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Lamiaceae, where it is placed within the genus Teucrium, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species simplex.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Teucrium simplex has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by slender, greyish-brown stems and small, lanceolate leaves that are typically opposite and serrated.

Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in compact clusters. These blossoms are usually pale pink or white and possess a distinct bilabiate structure typical of the Lamiaceae family.

The overall architecture of the plant remains compact and bushy, allowing it to thrive in rocky or arid environments.
