Teucrium simplex

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Teucrium simplex has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by slender, greyish-brown stems and small, lanceolate leaves that are typically opposite and serrated. The foliage is often densely packed along the branches, displaying a grayish-green hue due to fine pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in compact clusters. These blossoms are usually pale pink or white and possess a distinct bilabiate structure typical of the Lamiaceae family. The overall architecture of the plant remains compact and bushy, allowing it to thrive in rocky or arid environments.
