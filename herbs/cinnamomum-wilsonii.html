What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum wilsonii?

Cinnamomum wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae, eventually reaching its specific placement within the genus Cinnamomum. Finally, the plant is identified at the species level as wilsonii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive medicinal compounds including Wilsonols A-L, megastigmane, and various sesquiterpenoids. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for many of the plant's unique pharmacological properties. The Wilsonols A-L group consists of specialized triterpene derivatives that contribute to its chemical profile. Additionally, the presence of megastigmane and sesquiterpenoids suggests significant potential for anti-inflammatory or antioxidant activities. Together, these molecules form a complex phytochemical landscape used in medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, twelve new megastigmane sesquiterpenoids named wilsonols A-L (1-12) were isolated from the leaves of Cinnamomum wilsonii. These were found alongside seven known analogues (13-19). The absolute configurations of compounds 1-5 were determined using single-crystal X-ray diffraction with Cu Kα irradiation, while 6-12 were determined via the modified Mosher's method. Compounds 1-9 and 13-19 were evaluated for in vitro cytotoxicity against five human cancer cell lines, including HL-60, SMMC-7721, A-549, MCF-7, and SW-480. Specifically, compound 13 exhibited IC50 values ranging from 2.5 to 12 μM and selectivity indices of >10 against the SMMC-7721, A-549, and MCF-7 cell lines.
