Cinnamomum wilsonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cinnamomum wilsonii has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are leathery in texture and arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along its woody stems. The leaf margins are typically entire, while the surfaces often exhibit a glossy appearance on the upper side. This plant develops small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually give way to woody capsules containing seeds. The overall structure of the shrub or small tree is characterized by dense, spreading branches. These morphological features help identify the species within its specific ecological niche.
