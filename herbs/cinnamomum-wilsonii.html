What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the unique medicinal compounds Wilsonol A, Wilsonol B, Wilsonol C, Wilsonol D, and Wilsonol E. These specific bioactive molecules are secondary metabolites found within the chemical profile of Cinnamonum wilsonii. Researchers study these particular substances to understand their potential pharmacological properties and biological activities. The presence of these five distinct compounds defines the chemical signature of this specific species. Detailed analysis of these components helps scientists identify how the plant may interact with various physiological systems.

As discussed in a study, twelve new megastigmane sesquiterpenoids named wilsonols A-L (1-12) were isolated from the leaves of Cinnamomum wilsonii. These were found alongside seven known analogues (13-19). The absolute configurations of compounds 1-5 were determined using single-crystal X-ray diffraction with Cu Kα irradiation, while 6-12 were determined via the modified Mosher's method. Compounds 1-9 and 13-19 were evaluated for in vitro cytotoxicity against five human cancer cell lines, including HL-60, SMMC-7721, A-549, MCF-7, and SW-480. Specifically, compound 13 exhibited IC50 values ranging from 2.5 to 12 μM and selectivity indices of >10 against the SMMC-7721, A-549, and MCF-7 cell lines.
