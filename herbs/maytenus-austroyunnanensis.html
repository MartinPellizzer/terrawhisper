Maytenus austroyunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. The plant is further categorized into the family Celastraeceae, specifically within the genus Maytenus, eventually reaching its unique designation as the species austroyunnanenensis.

Continued investigation into these chemical constituents could reveal new ways to treat various ailments.

As discussed in a study, the traditional Chinese medicinal plant Maytenus austroyunnanensis is associated with the fermentation broth of Jiangella alba. From this broth, three pyrrol-2-aldehyde derivatives were isolated, including one new compound named jiangrine G (JG) and two known compounds, jiangrine A (JA) and pyrrolezanthine (PZ). The anti-inflammatory activities assay showed that JG and JA suppressed the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines including NO, IL-1β, and IL-6. These compounds also inhibited the expression of iNOS in LPS-induced RAW 264.7 cells in a dose-dependent manner. While PZ dramatically suppressed the protein expression of TNF-α at high concentrations, it stimulated the release of IL-1β and IL-6. The signaling pathways involved for JG, JA, and PZ in modulating cytokine expression include MAPK p38 and NF-κB.
