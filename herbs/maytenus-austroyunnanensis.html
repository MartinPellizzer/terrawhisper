Maytenus austroyunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. The plant is further categorized into the family Celastraeceae, specifically within the genus Maytenus, eventually reaching its unique designation as the species austroyunnanenensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds jiangrine A, jiangrine G, and pyrrolezanthine. These specific chemical constituents are categorized as alkaloids that are unique to the Maytenus austroyannensis species. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential pharmacological properties and biological activities. The presence of these diverse compounds highlights the complex chemical profile found within the plant's tissues. Such detailed phytochemical analysis provides a foundation for future drug discovery efforts.

According to a study published by "Bioorganic chemistry", three pyrrol-2-aldehyde derivatives were isolated from the fermentation broth of Jiangella alba associated with the medicinal plant Maytenus austroyunnanensis. These compounds include one new compound, jiangrine G (JG), and two known compounds, jiangrine A (JA) and pyrrolezanthine (PZ). The anti-inflammatory activities assay demonstrated that JG and JA suppressed the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines including NO, IL-1β, and IL-6. Additionally, JG and JA inhibited the expression of iNOS in LPS-induced RAW 264.7 cells. While PZ dramatically suppressed the protein expression of TNF-α at high concentrations, it stimulated the release of IL-1β and IL-6. These compounds modulated the expression of pro-inflammatory cytokines via the MAPK p38 and NF-κB signaling pathways.
