Viburnum betulifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum betulifolium?

Viburnum betulifoliuum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Dipsacales. This plant is a member of the family Viburnaceae, falling within the genus Viburnum, and is specifically identified by its species name, betulifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum betulifoliuum has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins with prominent pinnate venation. The foliage typically exhibits a bright green color on the upper surface and may appear slightly paler underneath. Its stems are woody and can range from greenish to reddish-brown depending on the maturity of the branch. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in dense, flat-topped cymes during the blooming season. These floral clusters eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that change color as they ripen.
