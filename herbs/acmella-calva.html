Acmella calva

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acmella calva has a prostrate or ascending growth habit characterized by glandular-pubescent stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features a branched structure. Its inflorescences consist of small, yellowish flower heads arranged in terminal clusters. These heads are surrounded by involucral bracts that protect the developing florets. The overall morphology is defined by its hairy texture and compact, herbaceous form.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and temperate regions of East Asia, specifically found across parts of China, Japan, and Korea. It typically inhabits moist environments such as forest edges, shaded ravines, and riverbanks where soil moisture is consistent. Within these regions, the species often thrives in mountainous terrains or hilly landscapes that provide necessary dappled sunlight. Localized populations are frequently concentrated in areas with well-draining but humid microclimates. This specific distribution pattern limits its natural occurrence to these distinct ecological zones in the Eastern Hemisphere.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Acmella calva contains bioactive compounds primarily located in its entire aerial portion, specifically within the leaves and the flowering heads. The leaves are frequently harvested for their ability to provide topical relief due to their high concentration of alkylamides. These chemical constituents are responsible for the characteristic numbing or tingling sensation experienced when the plant is applied to the skin or mucous membranes. Medicinal preparations often utilize the whole plant to treat various dermatological conditions and oral ailments. The effectiveness of these extracts depends heavily on the concentration of spilanthol found throughout the vegetative parts.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Acmella calva contains high concentrations of spilanthol, a bioactive alkylamide that provides its characteristic numbing and anesthetic effects. This primary compound acts on sensory neurons to induce localized desensitization of the mucous membranes. In addition to spilanthol, the plant possesses various essential oils and terpenoids that contribute to its antimicrobial properties. These diverse secondary metabolites work together to support its traditional use in treating oral pain and inflammatory conditions. The synergy of these chemical constituents makes the plant a significant subject for dermatological and dental pharmacological research.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Acmella calva targets specific ion channels, primarily the voltage-gated sodium channels and nicotinic acetylcholine receptors, to induce its characteristic numbing effects. The bioactive spilanthol molecule acts as a potent local anesthetic by modulating these membrane proteins to inhibit nerve impulse conduction. Additionally, the plant interacts with various inflammatory mediators, including cyclooxygenase enzymes, which helps reduce swelling and pain. Its ability to influence neurotransmitter release further contributes to its role in muscle relaxation and topical analgesia. These diverse protein interactions explain why the plant is widely used in traditional medicine for treating dental pain and skin irritation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Acmella calva?

Acmella calva has diverse therapeutic properties including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, analgesic, anesthetic, astringent, and bitter actions. The plant contains spilanthol, which provides a potent numbing sensation through its anesthetic and analgesic effects. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory qualities help reduce infections and swelling in various tissues. The bitter components stimulate digestion and bile production when consumed. Additionally, its astringent properties assist in tightening tissues and managing moisture in oral applications.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic reaction, nausea, vomiting, and stomach upset in some individuals. Contact with the topical areas may lead to redness or localized itching. When ingested, the plant is known to trigger various gastrointestinal issues. These digestive disturbances often manifest as significant nausea or forceful vomiting. Some users may also experience general stomach upset following consumption.
