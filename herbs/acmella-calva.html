Acmella calva

What's the taxonomical classification of Acmella calva?

Acmella calva belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Acmella, and its unique species designation, calva.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Acmella calva contains high concentrations of spilanthol, a bioactive alkylamide that provides its characteristic numbing and anesthetic effects. This primary compound acts on sensory neurons to induce localized desensitization of the mucous membranes. In addition to spilanthol, the plant possesses various essential oils and terpenoids that contribute to its antimicrobial properties. These diverse secondary metabolites work together to support its traditional use in treating oral pain and inflammatory conditions. The synergy of these chemical constituents makes the plant a significant subject for dermatological and dental pharmacological research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Acmella calva?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, intestinal parasites, and digestive issues. It contains bioactive compounds that provide significant antibacterial and antifungal properties for topical applications. When consumed, the plant helps expel worms and other intestinal parasites from the body. It is also frequently utilized in traditional medicine to soothe gastrointestinal distress and inflammation. Local healers often prepare extracts from the plant to accelerate the healing process of various skin ailments.
