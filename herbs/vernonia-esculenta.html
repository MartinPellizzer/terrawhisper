Vernonia esculenta

What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia esculenta?

Vernia esculenta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific biological identification is defined by the genus Vernonia and the species esculenta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia esculenta has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by multiple stems that typically reach heights of 0.5 to 1.5 meters. The leaves are lanceolate to narrowly elliptical in shape, featuring serrated margins and an alternate arrangement along the stem. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal heads composed of numerous small, tubular disc florets. These flower heads are typically pale purple or lavender in color and are supported by involucral bracts. The root system is robust and tuberous, which allows the plant to store nutrients for regrowth.
