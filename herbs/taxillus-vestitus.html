Taxillus vestitus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Taxillus vestitus has a creeping, prostrate growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems and small, lanceolate leaves that are often densely covered in fine, protective hairs. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate or sub-opposite pattern along the stem, providing a textured appearance to the plant. Its leaves feature entire margins and a relatively thin cuticle, which helps the organism manage moisture in its specific habitat. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to colonize ground surfaces effectively while minimizing environmental stress.
