Afrolicania elaeosperma

What's the taxonomical classification of Afrolicania elaeosperma?

Afrolicania elaeosperma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, the plant is situated in the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Chrysobalanaeae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Afrolicania and is specifically recognized by its species name, elaeosperma.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Afrolicania elaeosperma has a woody structure characterized by small, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the stems.

        What are the herbal preparations of Afrolicania elaeosperma?

        Afrolicania elaeosperma is prepared primarily as a decoction of the bark or as a crushed paste of the leaves for topical application. The boiled bark extract is often consumed as a liquid tea to treat internal ailments. When applied to the skin, the mashed leaf paste serves as a medicinal poultice for localized healing. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant in infusions to soothe various symptoms. These diverse preparation methods allow for both systemic and localized medicinal use.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Afrolicania elaeosperma can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and allergic contact dermatitis upon direct exposure to its sap or foliage. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger redness or itching in sensitive individuals. If the substance comes into contact with the eyes, it can lead to significant inflammation and temporary vision blurring. Ingestion of the plant parts is generally discouraged due to the risk of gastrointestinal distress or more systemic toxic reactions. Careful handling is essential to prevent accidental contact with the skin or mucous membranes.
