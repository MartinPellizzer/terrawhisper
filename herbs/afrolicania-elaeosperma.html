Afrolicania elaeosperma

What's the taxonomical classification of Afrolicania elaeosperma?

Afrolicania elaeosperma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, the plant is situated in the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Chrysobalanaeae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Afrolicania and is specifically recognized by its species name, elaeosperma.

What are the therapeutic actions of Afrolicania elaeosperma?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various internal inflammatory conditions. Local practitioners often prepare decoctions or poultices from the plant parts to address these ailments. The medicinal properties are frequently applied topically to accelerate the healing of cuts and sores. In some traditional practices, the extracts are also ingested to alleviate digestive discomfort. These applications demonstrate the diverse therapeutic role the species plays in folk medicine.
