Orthosiphon marmoritis

What's the taxonomical classification of Orthosiphon marmoritis?

Orthosiphon marmoritis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Lamiaceae, which contains the genus Orthosiphon. Finally, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species designation, marmoritis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Orthosiphon marmoritis contains a diverse array of secondary metabolites including phenolic acids, flavonoids, tannins, and various terpenoids. These bioactive compounds are primarily concentrated within the leaves and stems of the plant. The presence of these phytochemicals suggests significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties that are characteristic of the Menispermaceae family. Researchers often investigate these chemical profiles to determine potential therapeutic applications for inflammatory conditions. Further extraction of these specific alkaloids may reveal more precise pharmacological activities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Orthosiphon marmoritis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat kidney stones, urinary tract infections, renal inflammation, and various bladder disorders. It acts as a potent diuretic that helps flush toxins and mineral deposits from the urinary system. The plant's chemical properties assist in breaking down small stones to facilitate easier passage through the urethra. Traditional practitioners also utilize it to soothe discomfort associated with kidney ailments and systemic inflammation. By promoting increased urine production, it effectively aids in the cleansing of the renal tract.
