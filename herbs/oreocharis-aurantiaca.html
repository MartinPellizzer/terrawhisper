What's the taxonomical classification of Oreocharis aurantiaca?

Oreocharis aurantiaca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Lamiales and falls within the family Gesneriaceae. Its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Oreocharis, with its unique designation being the species aurantiaca.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Oreocharis aurantiaca targets various inflammatory mediators and enzymes, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and several pro-inflammatory cytokines like TNF-alpha and IL-6. These biochemical interactions are primarily driven by its rich content of bioactive flavonoids and triterpenoid saponins. By suppressing the COX-2 pathway, the plant effectively reduces the synthesis of prostaglandins, which helps alleviate pain and swelling. Additionally, the modulation of iNOS prevents the excessive production of nitric oxide, contributing to its overall anti-inflammatory profile. These molecular mechanisms collectively support the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and oxidative stress.
