Geranium wilfordii

What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium wilfordii?

Geranium wilfordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Geraniales and the family Geraniaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Geraniuim, and its unique species designation, wilfordii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium wilfordii has creeping rhizomes that produce small, rounded to kidney-shaped leaves with shallowly lobed margins and hairy surfaces. The foliage grows in low-lying mats, often reaching a height of only a few inches above the ground. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that typically appear in shades of pale pink or white. These blossoms emerge on slender stems that rise slightly above the dense leaf canopy. The overall structure allows the plant to spread effectively through ground cover.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the bioactive ellagitannins corilagin and geraniin, which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These specific polyphenols are responsible for the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research suggests that corilagin contributes to various protective effects within the digestive and hepatic systems. Geraniin also plays a crucial role in neutralizing free radicals and inhibiting certain bacterial strains. Together, these compounds form the chemical basis for the traditional medicinal uses of the species.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Geranium wilfordii Maxim. is a traditional medicinal plant rich in ellagitannins such as corilagin (CG) and geraniin (GR). These specific compounds possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The study utilized a method to quantify these substances within a range of 25-300 µg/mL with an R2 > 0.995. This analytical approach demonstrated a precision with RSD < 2.7% and an accuracy with recovery between 99.5-101.2%. Additionally, the detection limits for these medicinal compounds were found to be <3 µg/mL.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating Helicobacter pylori infection, gastrointestinal disorders, dysentery, diarrhea, and various infectious diseases. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to soothe inflammation within the digestive tract. Researchers study its components to understand how it combats pathogens like the bacteria responsible for stomach ulcers. It serves as a traditional remedy for managing acute bouts of intestinal distress and liquid stools. By addressing these specific ailments, the plant plays a significant role in holistic digestive care.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Geranium wilfordii Maxim has been extensively used in Chinese Herbal Medicine for treating gastrointestinal disorders, diarrhea and dysentery. The study investigated the anti-Helicobacter pylori activity of ethanol extracts and main active compounds such as corilagin and 1,2,3,6-tetra-O-galloyl-β-D-glucose. Testing against a standard strain and 5 clinical isolates showed that the ethanol fraction, ethyl acetate fraction, corilagin, and 1,2,3,6-tetra-O-galloyl-β-D-glucose were strongly inhibitory to Helicobacter pylori. These components exhibited MICs of 40, 30, 4, and 8μg/ml respectively. The ethanol and ethyl acetate extracts displayed the most significant inhibition to the growth of Helicobacter pylori.
