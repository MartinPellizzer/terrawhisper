What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium wilfordii?

Geranium wilfordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Geraniales and the family Geraniaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Geraniuim, and its unique species designation, wilfordii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium wilfordii has creeping rhizomes that produce small, rounded to kidney-shaped leaves with shallowly lobed margins and hairy surfaces. The foliage grows in low-lying mats, often reaching a height of only a few inches above the ground. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that typically appear in shades of pale pink or white. These blossoms emerge on slender stems that rise slightly above the dense leaf canopy. The overall structure allows the plant to spread effectively through ground cover.
