Geranium wilfordii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium wilfordii has creeping rhizomes that produce small, rounded to kidney-shaped leaves with shallowly lobed margins and hairy surfaces. The foliage grows in low-lying mats, often reaching a height of only a few inches above the ground. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that typically appear in shades of pale pink or white. These blossoms emerge on slender stems that rise slightly above the dense leaf canopy. The overall structure allows the plant to spread effectively through ground cover.

Geranium wilfordii is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste using the dried roots or aerial parts of the plant. What side-effects this plant can have?

Geranium wilfordii can cause gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, if consumed in improper quantities. Some individuals may also experience allergic skin reactions or localized irritation when the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive tissue. Because the plant contains bioactive compounds that affect physiological processes, it may interact unpredictably with prescription medications. Excessive intake could potentially lead to more systemic complications due to its concentrated chemical properties. Always consult a medical professional before using this plant for any medicinal purposes.
