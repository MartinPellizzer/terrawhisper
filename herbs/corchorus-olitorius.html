Sudanese spinach

Corchorus olitorius

Sudanese Spinach (Corchorus olitorius) is a leafy green vegetable native to Africa and belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family. Traditionally, it has been used to address digestive issues, act as a diuretic, and treat skin conditions. The leaves are the primary part used, often prepared as a cooked vegetable or brewed into a tea.

Botanical Identification

Corchorus olitorius, commonly known as Sudanese spinach, belongs to the family Malvaceae. It is an annual plant native to Africa, characterized by heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. Known by various regional names, it is widely used in herbal medicine for its nutritional and therapeutic properties.

Active Compounds

Sudanese Spinach contains flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their synergistic interaction enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and inflammation due to its mild laxative properties.

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments and as a mild diuretic.

Powder: Used for constipation and as a dietary supplement due to its high fiber content.

Safety Profile

Sudanese Spinach is generally safe when consumed in moderate amounts. However, it should be avoided by individuals with kidney disorders due to its high oxalate content. Large quantities may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some people.
