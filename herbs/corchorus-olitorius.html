Sudanese Spinach (Corchorus olitorius)

Sudanese Spinach (Corchorus olitorius) is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, native to Africa, Middle East, and South Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and shoots have been used for culinary uses, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.
