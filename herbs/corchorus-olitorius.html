Corchorus olitorius

What's the taxonomical classification of Corchorus olitorius?

Corchorus olitorius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the biological lineage of the order Malvales. It is further categorized under the family Malvaceae, which places it within the genus Corchorus, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species olitorius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corchorus olitorius has an erect, annual herb morphology characterized by slender, branched stems that typically reach heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate with entire margins and distinctively long, tapering bases. Small, yellow, five-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters. The fruit consists of elongated, cylindrical capsules that are significantly longer than they are wide and contain numerous tiny, brownish seeds. This plant structure allows it to thrive in disturbed soils and various tropical environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Corchorus olitorius?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The presence of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and polyphenols allows it to effectively reduce pain and swelling in various biological models. Its strong antimicrobial action helps inhibit the growth of several pathogenic bacteria and fungi. These diverse medicinal qualities make the species a valuable subject for natural drug development.

As discussed in a study, the leaves of Corchorus olitorius L. are used for the treatment of pain, fever, and inflammation. A water-soluble extract from these leaves, known as WM, significantly inhibited lipid accumulation in 3T3-L1 adipocytes. Mice treated with 100 mg/kg WM showed 13.1% lower body weights, 52.4% lower gut permeability, and 17.4% lower hepatic lipid accumulation compared to the HFD group. Furthermore, WM consumption influenced gut health by inhibiting metabolic endotoxemia and colonic inflammation. This treatment also altered gut microbiota by increasing the abundance of Lactobacillus and decreasing Desulfovibrio.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains ascorbic acid, chlorogenic acid, flavonoids, kaempferol, and rutin, which collectively provide significant therapeutic benefits. The presence of ascorbic acid serves as a potent antioxidant that supports immune function and collagen synthesis. Chlorogenic acid contributes to metabolic health by helping to regulate blood sugar levels and reduce inflammation. Various flavonoids, including the specific compounds kaempferol and rutin, offer neuroprotective and cardioprotective properties. These diverse medicinal compounds work synergistically to combat oxidative stress within the body.

As discussed in a study, Corchorus olitorius is used in folk medicine for the treatment of fever, chronic cystitis, cold and tumours. The hydrophilic extract contains 630.8 mg/100 g of total phenol, 227.8 mg/100 g of total flavonoid, 403.0 mg/100 g of non-flavonoid polyphenols, and 32.6 mg/100 g of ascorbic acid. This extract demonstrated a 1,1-diphenyl-2-picrylhydrazyl radical-scavenging ability of 9.6-84.4% in aqueous and 2.0-20.4% in hexane, a reducing power of 0.67 mmol ascorbic acid equivalent/g in aqueous and 0.49 mmol ascorbic acid equivalent/g in hexane, and a trolox equivalent antioxidant capacity of 2.3 mmol/g in aqueous and 1.1 mmol/g in hexane. The lipophilic extract possesses a high total carotenoid content of 42.5 mg/100 g and a significantly higher OH. scavenging activity of 44.5-46.2%. The Fe(II) chelating ability was 57.7-66.7% for the hydrophilic extract and 56.4-61.1% for the lipophilic extract.
