Swertia racemosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Gentianales. The plant is a member of the family Gentianaceae, specifically falling under the genus Swertia, and is identified by its unique species name, racemosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Swertia racemosa has erect, woody stems that branch extensively and bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The leaves are typically arranged in pairs along the stem and possess prominent pinnate venation. Small, fragrant, white or pale lilac flowers emerge from axillary racemes that extend along the upper portions of the branches. Each flower consists of five petals and is supported by a short pedicel. The plant eventually produces small, dehiscent capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds for dispersal.
