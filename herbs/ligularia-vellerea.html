Ligularia vellerea

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia vellerea?

Ligularia vellerea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. It is further defined by its genus, Ligularia, and is specifically identified by its species name, vellerea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia vellerea has large, cordate to ovate leaves that are prominently serrated along the margins and possess a distinctively rugose texture. These leaves grow on long, sturdy petioles that emerge from a basal rosette. The plant produces terminal clusters of bright yellow ray florets that surround central disc florets in a composite flower head. The stems are erect and can reach significant heights to support the heavy floral displays. Throughout the growing season, the foliage remains dense and provides a lush, green base for the striking blooms.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to a very narrow geographic range located exclusively in the high-altitude regions of the southeastern Alps within northern Italy. It is specifically restricted to certain rocky habitats and alpine meadows in the province of Belluno. Due to this extremely limited distribution, the species is considered rare and highly localized. Its survival depends on the specific microclimates found within these specific mountainous territories. Consequently, the plant cannot be found naturally in any other part of the world.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 4-hydroxyacetophenone, beta-sitosterol, daucosterin, stigmasterol, and umbelliferone. These identified medicinal compounds include 4-hydroxyacetophenone, 8 alpha-hydroxy-7(11)-eremophilen-12, 8 beta-olide, umbelliferone, tiglic acid, 6 beta-hydroxy-7(11)-eremophilen-12, 8 alpha-olide, daucosterin, beta-sitosterol and stigmasterol. All 7 of these compounds were isolated for the first time from this specific plant. The chemical constituents were isolated using column chromatography and structures were identified via NMR spectral data.
