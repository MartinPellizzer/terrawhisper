Xylocarpus granatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Xylocarpus granatum?

Xylocarpus granatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Sapindales. The plant is further categorized into the family Meliaceae, within which it is placed in the genus Xylocarpus, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species granatum.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Xylocarpus granatum contains medicinal compounds found primarily in its bark, leaves, and fruit. The bark is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as skin diseases and inflammation. Extracts from the leaves are also applied for their potential antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Additionally, the fruit and seeds contain bioactive substances that are studied for their therapeutic effects. These different plant parts serve as a natural source for developing diverse pharmacological treatments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, tannins, steroids, and lignans.

These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the bark, leaves, and seeds of the species. The presence of alkaloids and flavonoids contributes to significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Additionally, the terpenoids and steroids play crucial roles in its potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Lignans and tannins further enhance the medicinal value of the plant through their protective biological effects.

  • Tannins
  • Steroids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Xylocarpus granatum targets various enzymes and signaling proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various inflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. The plant contains bioactive xanthones and terpenoids that inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators through the modulation of the NF-kappaB signaling pathway. These secondary metabolites also interact with topoisomerase enzymes and various kinases to demonstrate potent anticancer activities. Additionally, specific compounds within the plant act on antioxidant enzymes to mitigate oxidative stress in cellular environments. Through these diverse protein interactions, the species provides therapeutic potential for treating inflammation, cancer, and oxidative damage.

What are the therapeutic actions of Xylocarpus granatum?

Xylocarpus granatum has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties derived primarily from its bioactive xanthones and terpenoids. These chemical compounds work by inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi, making the plant a candidate for treating infectious diseases. The presence of specific polyphenols also helps to reduce oxidative stress and cellular damage within biological systems. Additionally, its extracts demonstrate significant anti-inflammatory activity which can help alleviate swelling and pain in different physiological models. These diverse therapeutic actions highlight the plant's potential utility in developing new pharmacological treatments.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat various skin diseases, open wounds, and internal or external inflammation.

The bark and fruit of the species possess medicinal properties that help soothe irritated tissue. Practitioners often apply extracts directly to skin conditions to reduce redness and discomfort. Furthermore, the plant's compounds assist in the healing process of cuts and abrasions by preventing infection.

Its natural anti-inflammatory effects make it a valuable resource for managing swelling in different parts of the body.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Skin diseases
  • Wounds
  • Inflammation

What are the herbal preparations of Xylocarpus granatum?

Xylocarpus granatum is prepared as aqueous decoctions of the bark, infusions of the leaves, or topical pastes made from the crushed fruit and root. These liquid preparations are frequently consumed to address digestive issues and inflammatory conditions. In traditional medicine, the powdered bark is sometimes mixed into ointments for external application on skin ailments. Some practitioners also utilize the juice extracted directly from the fruit for specific therapeutic purposes. These various forms allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be administered according to the intended ailment.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Xylocarpus granatum can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if consumed in improper doses due to the presence of bioactive alkaloids and terpenoids. While many parts of the plant are used in traditional medicine, the concentration of these chemical compounds can trigger nausea or vomiting in sensitive individuals. Direct contact with the plant material may also lead to localized dermatological issues like redness or itching on the skin. Because the exact toxicological thresholds are not fully established in modern clinical studies, caution is necessary when using its extracts. Researchers continue to investigate the safety profiles of its specific chemical constituents to prevent accidental poisoning.
