Xylocarpus granatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Xylocarpus granatum?

Xylocarpus granatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Sapindales. The plant is further categorized into the family Meliaceae, within which it is placed in the genus Xylocarpus, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species granatum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Xylocarpus granatum contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including limonoids, xanthones, alkaloids, and terpenoids. These complex chemical structures, particularly the highly oxygenated limonoids, serve as the primary source of its pharmacological potential. Research indicates that these compounds exhibit significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Some extracts have also demonstrated cytotoxic activities that are being investigated for potential anticancer applications. The specific chemical profiles often vary depending on which part of the plant, such as the bark or fruit, is being utilized for extraction.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including malaria, skin infections, inflammation, and gastrointestinal disorders. The bark and fruit contain bioactive compounds that demonstrate significant antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. These medicinal qualities allow it to serve as a traditional remedy for managing wounds and parasitic infections. Researchers are currently investigating its chemical constituents to better understand their efficacy against specific pathogens. This botanical resource remains a vital component in many indigenous medical practices across coastal regions.
