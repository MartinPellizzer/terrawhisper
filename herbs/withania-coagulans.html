Withania coagulans

Withania coagulans

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Withania coagulans, also known as Indian withania, belongs to the Solanaceae family. It is a perennial shrub native to India and Southeast Asia. The plant has yellow flowers, oval leaves, and a taproot system. Common synonyms include Ashwagandha and Indian ginseng, though these refer to Withania somnifera.

Active Compounds

Withania coagulans contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, saponins, and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to support various therapeutic effects, enhancing the herb's efficacy in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Alkaloid
  • Saponin
  • Flavonoid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Musculoskeletal Arthritis, osteoarthritis, muscle pain Anti-inflammatory
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, skin aging Antimicrobial, antioxidant
Nervous Anxiety, stress-related disorders, anxiety disorders Anxiolytic, adaptogen, sedative, analgesic

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Powder: Used for general health support and as a dietary supplement.

Extract: Used for enhancing vitality and reducing stress.

Safety Profile

Withania coagulans is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you have existing health conditions or are taking other medications.
