Scientific and Botanical Profile

Withania Coagulans, with botanical name Withania coagulans, is a member of the Solanaceae family, commonly known as the nightshade family.

Native to regions across India, Pakistan, Afghanistan, Nepal, Bhutan, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Tibet, Myanmar, China, Maldives, Arabian Peninsula, and the Middle East, this plant is widely distributed in tropical and subtropical climates. Morphologically, it is an erect, herbaceous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1-2 meters, featuring hairy stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The flowers are yellowish-green to white, clustered in umbels, and give way to fleshy, red or orange berries that are often referred to as Withania fruit.

Common names for this species include Indian Nightshade, Black Nightshade, Chewstick, Indian Trumpet, Indian Touch-Me-Not, Sarpaganda, Coral Plant, Coral Trumpet, Coagulant Nightshade, Cape Gooseberry, Red Trumpet, Common Nightshade, and Withania Fruit, reflecting its widespread use and diverse cultural significance.

History and Cultural Relevance

Withania Coagulans was used in traditional medicine systems across various cultures, particularly in South Asia, where it has been historically valued for its purported healing properties.

It has also played a role in indigenous rituals and folk traditions, often being used in ceremonies to promote healing and spiritual balance. Today, it remains a subject of interest in both traditional and alternative medicine, reflecting its enduring cultural significance and the ongoing quest for natural remedies.

Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Withania coagulans can be used with caution due to its potential effects on the body, though it is not widely studied in modern pharmacological contexts.

While some traditional uses suggest it may have anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties, there is limited scientific evidence supporting these claims. Potential side effects may include gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, and interactions with other medications, particularly those affecting blood clotting or the immune system. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to insufficient safety data, and its use should be avoided in individuals with chronic illnesses or those taking anticoagulants, anti-inflammatory drugs, or immunosuppressants.

To ensure safety, consult a healthcare provider before use, start with low doses, and monitor for adverse reactions.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Withania coagulans grows best in well-drained, loamy soil that is rich in organic matter, with a pH ranging from 6.0 to 7.5.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade, requiring at least 6 hours of direct sunlight daily, and needs consistent moisture without waterlogging. Regular watering is essential during the growing season, but the soil should be allowed to dry slightly between waterings to prevent root rot. For optimal growth, mulching around the base of the plant helps retain moisture and suppress weeds, while periodic fertilization with a balanced organic fertilizer supports healthy foliage and fruit development. Harvesting should occur when the fruits turn from green to a deep red or orange hue, typically 60 to 90 days after planting, using pruning shears to cut the stems just above the fruiting nodes.

To preserve its potency, the harvested fruits should be dried in a shaded, well-ventilated area or using a dehydrator at low temperatures, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dry place or refrigerated to maintain their medicinal properties.
