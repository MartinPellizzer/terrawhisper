Areca catechu

What's the taxonomical classification of Areca catechu?

Areca catechu belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Arecales and falls under the family Arecaceae. Finally, it is identified within the genus Areca, with its specific designation being the species catechu.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Areca catechu contains alkaloids such as arecoline, arecaidine, guvacine, and guvacoline that function as primary bioactive constituents. These specific compounds act as mild stimulants by interacting with muscarinic acetylcholine receptors in the central nervous system. Beyond these alkaloids, the plant also possesses polyphenolic compounds, including catechins and tannins, which provide antioxidant properties. Some studies suggest these phytochemicals may exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities in certain biological contexts. However, the high concentration of alkaloids also necessitates caution regarding potential toxicity and carcinogenic risks.

What are the therapeutic actions of Areca catechu?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat intestinal worms, skin diseases, oral hygiene issues, and various digestive ailments. The seeds contain alkaloids that act as anthelmintics to expel parasitic worms from the human body. In traditional medicine, the nut is often chewed to stimulate saliva production and maintain oral health. It is also applied topically to manage certain skin infections and inflammatory conditions. These medicinal properties are frequently utilized in various herbal formulations across Southeast Asia.
