Mussaenda hirsutula

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mussaenda hirsutula has a woody shrub growth habit characterized by densely hairy or hirsute stems and foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate in shape with serrated margins and a textured, pubescent surface. Its most striking feature is the presence of large, brightly colored petaloid sepals that surround much smaller, inconspicuous true flowers. These enlarged sepals serve to attract pollinators through their vibrant pigmentation and expansive surface area. The overall structure of the plant is defined by this combination of rough, hairy textures and soft, colorful bract-like structures.
