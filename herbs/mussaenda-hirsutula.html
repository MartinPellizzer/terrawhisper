Mussaenda hirsutula

What's the taxonomical classification of Mussaenda hirsutula?

Mussaenda hirsutula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Mussaenda, specifically identified by its species name, hirsutula.

      What are the herbal preparations of Mussaenda hirsutula?

      Mussaenda hirsutula is prepared as a decoction, a paste, or a topical poultice depending on the specific medicinal requirement. The leaves are often boiled in water to create a liquid infusion used for internal consumption or washing affected areas.       What side-effects this plant can have?

While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, many plants in this genus contain compounds that may trigger dermatitis in sensitive individuals. It is important to handle the plant with care to prevent accidental contact with the eyes or mucous membranes.
