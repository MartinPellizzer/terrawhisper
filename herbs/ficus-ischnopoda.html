Ficus ischnopoda

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus ischnopoda has small, elliptical leaves with prominent venation and a woody stem structure characterized by distinctive, foot-like root patterns. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a somewhat leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Its branching pattern creates a dense, shrub-like canopy that provides significant cover in its native habitat. The plant's unique name refers specifically to the specialized morphology of its roots which spread outward in a specific shape. These specialized aerial or surface roots allow the species to anchor effectively in its specific ecological niche.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if the milky sap is ingested or comes into contact with sensitive areas. The sap contains proteolytic enzymes that trigger redness, itching, and blistering upon skin contact. If accidentally swallowed, individuals may experience intense burning in the mouth and throat along with nausea or vomiting. Ocular contact with the sap can lead to significant inflammation and temporary vision impairment. Therefore, protective gear should be worn when handling this species to prevent accidental exposure.
