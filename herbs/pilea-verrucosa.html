Pilea verrucosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea verrucosa?

Pilea verrucosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Rosalales. This plant is further classified under the family Urticaceae, which places it within the genus Pilea, and it is specifically identified by its species name, verrucosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea verrucosa has fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with slightly wavy margins and a distinctive textured surface. The stems are succulent and often exhibit a reddish or brownish tint depending on the light exposure. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters along the leaf axils. The plant's namesake characteristic is the presence of small, wart-like bumps or verrucae on the stems and petioles. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its native tropical habitats.
