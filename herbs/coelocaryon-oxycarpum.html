Coelocaryon oxycarpum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coelocaryon oxycarpum has pinnate leaves composed of multiple pairs of leaflets, small yellowish flowers, and distinctive hard-shelled, woody fruits. The foliage consists of bright green leaflets that are typically arranged along a central rachis. Its inflorescences emerge in small clusters, often appearing in the leaf axils or at the ends of branches. The most defining morphological feature is the fruit, which possesses a thick, protective pericarp. This specialized structure helps protect the seeds during their development within the plant's woody framework.
