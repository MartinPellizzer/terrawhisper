Coelocaryon oxycarpum

What's the taxonomical classification of Coelocaryon oxycarpum?

Coelocaryon oxycarpum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its lineage, it falls under the order Magnoliales and is a member of the family Myristicaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Coelocaryon, and its specific species name, oxycarpum.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Coelocaryon oxycarpum can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically without proper processing. The plant contains specific secondary metabolites that may act as irritants to sensitive mucous membranes. Individuals with known hypersensitivity to similar botanical species might experience localized swelling or rashes upon contact. Ingesting raw parts of the plant without caution can lead to nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. Because clinical data on human toxicity is limited, extreme caution should be exercised when handling this species.
