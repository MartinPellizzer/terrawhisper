Coelocaryon oxycarpum

What's the taxonomical classification of Coelocaryon oxycarpum?

Coelocaryon oxycarpum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its lineage, it falls under the order Magnoliales and is a member of the family Myristicaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Coelocaryon, and its specific species name, oxycarpum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Coelocaryon oxycarpum contains a diverse profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's leaves and bark. Researchers have identified specific saponins and flavonoids that contribute to the species' potential pharmacological activities. Studies suggest these compounds may possess significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Such chemical complexity provides a basis for investigating the plant's use in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Coelocaryon oxycarpum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat intestinal parasites, stomach ailments, skin infections, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are often prepared into decoctions or infusions to expel worms from the digestive tract. Local healers also apply crushed plant materials topically to soothe skin irritations and heal wounds. Some traditional practices involve using extracts to alleviate symptoms of indigestion and abdominal pain. These medicinal applications highlight the plant's importance in folk medicine for managing both internal and external health issues.
