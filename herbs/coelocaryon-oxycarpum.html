Coelocaryon oxycarpum

What's the taxonomical classification of Coelocaryon oxycarpum?

Coelocaryon oxycarpum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its lineage, it falls under the order Magnoliales and is a member of the family Myristicaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Coelocaryon, and its specific species name, oxycarpum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coelocaryon oxycarpum has pinnate leaves composed of multiple pairs of leaflets, small yellowish flowers, and distinctive hard-shelled, woody fruits.

The foliage consists of bright green leaflets that are typically arranged along a central rachis. Its inflorescences emerge in small clusters, often appearing in the leaf axils or at the ends of branches. The most defining morphological feature is the fruit, which possesses a thick, protective pericarp.

This specialized structure helps protect the seeds during their development within the plant's woody framework.
