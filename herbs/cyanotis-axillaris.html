Cyanotis axillaris

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyanotis axillaris?

Cyanotis axillaris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Commelinares and the family Commelineaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Cyanotis, and its specific species name, axillaris.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyanotis axiellaris has fleshy, creeping stems that produce linear to lanceolate leaves arranged in a distichous pattern along the axis. The leaves are typically bright green, possessing a smooth texture and distinct parallel venation characteristic of monocots. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often featuring three delicate petals that vary in color from white to pale blue. These blooms are supported by slender pedicels and are frequently surrounded by protective bracts. The plant often develops small, tuberous structures at the nodes to aid in vegetative propagation.
