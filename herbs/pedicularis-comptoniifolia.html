Pedicularis comptoniifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis comptoriifolia has a low-growing, caespitose habit characterized by small, pinnately divided leaves and dense, upright spikes of tubular flowers. The foliage consists of narrow, linear segments that create a fine-textured appearance near the ground. Its inflorescences are typically composed of numerous small, zygomorphic flowers that exhibit a range of colors from pink to reddish-purple. The corollas feature a distinct upper lip and a lower lip, which is a hallmark of the genus. Stems are often short and stout, supporting the compact floral displays throughout the growing season.
