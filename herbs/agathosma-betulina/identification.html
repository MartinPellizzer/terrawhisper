Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Agathosma betulina, first described by the botanist Johann Reinhold Forster. It is also known by several synonyms, including Buchu betulina and Ptychotropis betulina. Common names include buchu and African buchu, while regional and historical names vary across different cultures and time periods.

Agathosma betulina is a low-growing shrub with a dense root system that anchors it firmly in sandy soils. Its stems are woody and branched, often forming a compact shrub. Leaves are narrow, leathery, and aromatic, with a distinctive silver-white underside. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences, and its flowers are tiny, yellowish-green, and inconspicuous. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Agathosma betulina can be distinguished by its small, yellowish-green leaves with a slightly serrated margin and its distinctive aromatic, woody stems. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Agathosma crenata.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Agathosma betulina is native to South Africa, particularly the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 200 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Agathosma betulina blooms between September and October. Seed formation occurs from November to February. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested during its flowering period, from September to October, when the characteristics for accurate identification are most apparent.
