Agathosma (Agathosma betulina) Compounds

The primary compounds of Agathosma betulina include essential oil, terpenoids, flavonoids, phenolic acids, coumarin, tannins, glycosides, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Agathosma (Agathosma betulina) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Agathosma betulina contains bioactive compounds like cineole and beta-caryophyllene. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds in Agathosma betulina, commonly known as buchu. These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating respiratory and urinary tract infections.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Agathosma betulina. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects. This compound plays a key role in the therapeutic potential of Agathosma betulina in herbal medicine.
