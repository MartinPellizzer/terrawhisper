Pholidota yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pholidota yunnanensis has small, fleshy, yellowish-brown to brownish-orange fruit bodies that grow in dense clusters from decaying wood. Each individual fungus typically measures between 0.5 and 2 centimeters in diameter and possesses a cap that is rounded or slightly convex. The texture of the pileus is notably viscid or sticky when moist, often appearing somewhat waxy. Beneath the cap, the hymenium consists of small, crowded pores rather than gills. The stipe is short, sturdy, and often partially embedded within the mass of fruiting bodies.

    What are the herbal preparations of Pholidota yunnanensis?

    Pholidota yunnanensis is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the dried fungus in water to extract its active medicinal compounds. This method of simmering the material allows the solutes to penetrate the liquid for consumption in traditional formulas. In some instances, the dried fungus may be ground into a fine powder to be encapsulated or mixed into other substances.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pholidota yunnanensis can cause gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or abdominal pain if consumed in improper amounts or by sensitive individuals. While it is frequently used in traditional medicine, its pharmacological profile lacks extensive human clinical trials to confirm long-term safety. Some users may experience allergic reactions ranging from mild skin rashes to more severe respiratory issues. Because its chemical composition is complex, there is also a potential risk of drug interactions with existing medications. It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using this fungus to avoid unpredictable adverse reactions.
