What's the taxonomical classification of Pholidota yunnanensis?

Pholidota yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Pholidota, with its unique designation being the species yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pholidota yunnanensis has small, fleshy, yellowish-brown to brownish-orange fruit bodies that grow in dense clusters from decaying wood. The texture of the pileus is notably viscid or sticky when moist, often appearing somewhat waxy. Beneath the cap, the hymenium consists of small, crowded pores rather than gills. The stipe is short, sturdy, and often partially embedded within the mass of fruiting bodies.
