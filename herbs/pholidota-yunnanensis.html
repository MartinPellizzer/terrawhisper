Pholidota yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pholidota yunnanensis?

Pholidota yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asparagales, this plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Pholidota, with its unique designation being the species yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pholidota yunnanensis has small, fleshy, yellowish-brown to brownish-orange fruit bodies that grow in dense clusters from decaying wood. Each individual fungus typically measures between 0.5 and 2 centimeters in diameter and possesses a cap that is rounded or slightly convex. The texture of the pileus is notably viscid or sticky when moist, often appearing somewhat waxy. Beneath the cap, the hymenium consists of small, crowded pores rather than gills. The stipe is short, sturdy, and often partially embedded within the mass of fruiting bodies.

What are the pharmacological activities of Pholidota yunnanensis?

This plant has potent anti-neuroinflammatory, antioxidant, and neuroprotective pharmacological activities that contribute to its medicinal value. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively suppress the activation of microglia and reduce the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines in the brain. These mechanisms help mitigate oxidative stress and prevent neuronal damage caused by various neurodegenerative processes. Additionally, its ability to modulate inflammatory pathways suggests potential therapeutic applications for treating conditions like Alzheimer's disease. Through these diverse actions, Pholidota yunnanensis serves as a significant subject for neurological drug development.

As discussed in a study, eleven undescribed axially chiral dihydrophenanthrene dimers and twenty-five known dihydrophenanthrenes were isolated from the entire plant of Pholidota yunnanensis. The activities of all isolated compounds were assessed in LPS-induced BV-2 microglial cells. Most dihydrophenthrenes exhibited significant NO inhibitory activities. Compound 7 showed the most potent inhibitory effect with an IC50 value of 1.5 μM compared to the positive control minocycline. Furthermore, compound 7 suppressed the expression of Iba-1, iNOS, and COX-2 in LPS-stimulated BV-2 microglial cells.

These identified compounds include n-nonacosane, cyclopholidone, n-dotriacontanoic acid, n-octacostyl ferulate, cyclopholidonol, cycloneolitsol, and beta-sitosterol.
