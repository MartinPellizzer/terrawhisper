Polygonum ocreatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. This plant is further categorized under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Polygonaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Polygonum and the species ocreatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum ocreatum has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 50 centimeters and feature prominent, membranous stipules known as ochreae at each node. These ochreae are characteristic of the Polygonaceae family and serve to wrap around the stem where the leaves attach. The leaves are generally lanceolate or narrowly elliptical in shape with smooth margins and an entire base. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in terminal or axillary clusters, often appearing white or pinkish in color.
