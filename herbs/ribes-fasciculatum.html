Ribes fasciculatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ribes fasciculatum has woody, deciduous stems characterized by a densely clustered arrangement of leaves and small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically palmately lobed with serrated margins and a slightly pubescent texture on their surfaces. Small, globose berries develop from the blossoms, serving as the primary fruit structure of the plant. The branching pattern often forms thickets due to the compact growth habit of the individual stems. These morphological traits collectively define the shrubby architecture of this specific species.
