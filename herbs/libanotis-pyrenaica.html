What is Libanotis pyrenaica?

Libanotis pyrenaica is a perennial plant species belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It has a height of 20-50 cm, with a single stem and a bipinnate, umbellate inflorescence composed of 5-10 umbels. The leaflets are 2-3 times pinnate, with 5-9 pairs of segments, 3-5 mm long, and have a dentate margin. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and have 5 petals, while the fruit is an oblong, ribbed schizocarp, 3-4 mm long.

The plant prefers calcareous soils in the mountainous regions of the Pyrenees.

Additionally, the antimicrobial properties of Libanotis pyrenaica are attributed to the presence of volatile oils, which exhibit broad-spectrum activity against bacteria, fungi, and viruses, making it a potential natural remedy for wound infections.

The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties of this plant also contribute to its antiseptic and healing properties, making it a valuable resource for traditional medicine.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Libanotis pyrenaica are sesquiterpene lactones, including pyrenanolide, pyrenolide and cnicin, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities.

Flavonoids such as pyrenolin and pyrenochromene have also been identified, displaying antioxidant properties. The presence of phenolic acids including caffeic acid and ferulic acid contribute to its free radical scavenging capacity. Additionally, the plant contains coumarin derivatives like umbelliferone which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

These bioactive compounds contribute to the medicinal properties of Libanotis pyrenaica.

The main herbal preparations of Libanotis pyrenaica are infusion, tincture, tea, salve, and decoction.

Tea is prepared by steeping the leaves in hot water to alleviate respiratory issues and as a diuretic.

Decoctions are made by boiling the roots and leaves in water to treat fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.
