Common Hawthorn (Crataegus x bornmuelleri)

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus x bornmuelleri) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to China, and Eastern Asia. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its sedative, tonic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Flowers
Flowers are white to pale pink, arranged in clusters of 2 to 5, actinomorphic, with five ovate petals and five stamens.
Stems
Woody, branched, with opposite or alternate phyllotaxis, and bearing thorn-like stipules.
