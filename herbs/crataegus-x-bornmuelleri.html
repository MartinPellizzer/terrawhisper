What to know about Crataegus × bornmuelleri (hawthorn) before using it medicinally

Hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus × bornmuelleri, is a herb that has been revered for its remarkable health properties, which have been shown to improve heart health by lowering blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

Beyond its medicinal uses, hawthorn is also a popular ingredient in many culinary dishes, particularly in traditional European cuisine, where it's often used to make jams, preserves, and teas. In the garden, hawthorn is prized for its showy white flowers and bright red berries, making it a stunning ornamental plant that attracts pollinators and birds alike. Botanically speaking, hawthorn belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and lobed leaves.

With roots dating back thousands of years, hawthorn has been mentioned in ancient Greek and Roman texts as well as in traditional Chinese medicine, solidifying its place in the annals of herbalism history.
