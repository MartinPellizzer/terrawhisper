What is Drynaria browniana?

Drynaria browniana is a species of fern in the family Polypodiaceae, native to Australia and New Caledonia.

It has rhizomes that produce creeping stems with linear-lanceolate to lanceolate leaves that are pinnate and have a glandular apex. The leaves are 3-5 times pinnate, with 10-20 pairs of pinnae, and the fronds reach 30-60 cm in height. The sporangia are tetrahedral and have 64 spores per sporangium, and the plant reproduces via spores.

The specific epithet 'browniana' refers to the collector of the species, Robert Brown.

This is because the aristolochic acid in Drynaria browniana is metabolized into aristolactam, a potent nephrotoxin that can cause renal failure. Prolonged or excessive consumption of Drynaria browniana can lead to severe nephrotoxicity, resulting in chronic kidney disease and end-stage renal disease. Additionally, the high levels of aristolochic acid in Drynaria browniana can cause liver damage and failure, especially when combined with other hepatotoxic substances.

The risk of these side effects is significantly increased when Drynaria browniana is consumed in high doses, for extended periods, or in combination with other medications.
