Aster tsarungensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster tsarungensis has perennial, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and are adorned with lanceolate to linear leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is often arranged in a basal rosette during the early stages of growth before transitioning to stem leaves. Its inflorescences consist of solitary, radiate flower heads that feature numerous central disc florets surrounded by a ring of ligulate ray florets. The ray florets are generally white to pale pink, providing a distinct contrast to the darker centers. The involucral bracts are arranged in several rows, protecting the developing flower heads.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Populations are primarily found growing in rocky crevices and alpine meadows at significant elevations. This narrow range suggests that the species is highly specialized to its local environment.       What are the herbal preparations of Aster tsarungensis?

      Aster tsarungensis is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical ointment derived from its dried flowering tops and leaves.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Aster tsarungensis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if ingested or handled without protection. While specific toxicological data for this particular species is limited, many plants in the Asteraceae family contain sesquiterpene lactones that trigger contact dermatitis. Individuals with known sensitivities to daisies or ragweed may experience heightened inflammatory responses such as redness or swelling. Internal consumption could potentially lead to nausea or stomach cramps due to the presence of bioactive compounds. Therefore, cautious handling and avoiding ingestion are essential precautions when working with this plant.
