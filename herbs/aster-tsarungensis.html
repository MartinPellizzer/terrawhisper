Aster tsarungensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster tsarungensis?

Aster tsarungensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae and is situated within the genus Aster, specifically identified by the species name tsarungensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster tsarungensis has perennial, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and are adorned with lanceolate to linear leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is often arranged in a basal rosette during the early stages of growth before transitioning to stem leaves. Its inflorescences consist of solitary, radiate flower heads that feature numerous central disc florets surrounded by a ring of ligulate ray florets. The ray florets are generally white to pale pink, providing a distinct contrast to the darker centers. The involucral bracts are arranged in several rows, protecting the developing flower heads.
