What is Oxycedrus Juniper (Juniperus Oxycedrus)?

Oxycedrus Juniper, or Juniperus oxycedrus, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense foliage, aromatic berries, and historical use in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and digestive properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Oxycedrus Juniper is Juniperus oxycedrus. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Coniferophyta, class Pinopsida, order Pinales, family Cupressaceae, genus Juniperus, and species Juniperus oxycedrus. It is commonly known as the Cyprus juniper in the Mediterranean region and the Anatolian juniper in Turkey. The plant is also referred to as Oxycedrus in some botanical classifications and has regional names such as "Tavşan ağacı" in Turkey.

Plant Description and Morphology

Oxycedrus Juniper is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows between 2 to 6 meters in height, with a compact, rounded crown and a dense, gnarled trunk.

Its leaves are scale-like, tightly packed on the branches, and measure approximately 1 to 2 millimeters in length, forming a dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous, blue-green flowers that appear in clusters during the spring, which are followed by berry-like cones containing aromatic, dark blue to black seeds. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, adapting well to rocky and arid soils, and providing stability in harsh environments.

The bark is rough and fissured, with a reddish-brown color, while the leaves have a resinous smell and a leathery texture.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Oxycedrus Juniper (Juniperus oxycedrus) is native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean, specifically found in parts of Turkey, Syria, Lebanon, and Israel, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters.
