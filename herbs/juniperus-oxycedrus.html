What is Oxycedrus Juniper (Juniperus Oxycedrus)?

Oxycedrus Juniper, also known as Juniperus oxycedrus, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small blue-black berries that are used in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Oxycedrus Juniper, scientifically known as Juniperus oxycedrus, is a species of juniper native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean, including parts of Turkey, Lebanon, and Syria, and is classified under the family Cupressaceae.

Oxycedrus Juniper, or Juniperus oxycedrus, is a small evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves arranged in flattened sprays, blue-green foliage, and small, dark blue to blackish berries that ripen in autumn.
