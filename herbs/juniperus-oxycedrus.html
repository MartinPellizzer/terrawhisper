Oxycedrus Juniper

Juniperus oxycedrus

Oxycedrus Juniper (Juniperus oxycedrus) is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region, known for its aromatic foliage and small blue-black berries, and traditionally used for its medicinal properties to improve urinary tract health and reduce inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Oxycedrus Juniper, scientifically known as Juniperus oxycedrus, belongs to the family Cupressaceae. Commonly called "Oxycedrus" in some regions, it is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean. It has needle-like leaves and small, blue-black berries. The plant is also referred to as "Turkish Juniper" in herbal literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Coniferophyta
  • Class: Pinopsida
  • Order: Pinales
  • Family: Cupressaceae
  • Genus: Juniperus
  • Species: Juniperus oxycedrus

Active Compounds

Oxycedrus Juniper contains essential oil, terpenoids, and tannins as its main active compounds. These bioactive components work synergistically to provide therapeutic effects. The essential oil and terpenoids contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, while tannins support astringent and antioxidant actions.

  • Essential oil
  • Terpenoid
  • Tannin

Oxycedrus Juniper Uses.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and as a general tonic.

Tincture: Used for muscle pain and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Oxycedrus Juniper is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential toxicity. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
