What to know about Artemisia capillaris (wormwood) before using it medicinally

Wormwood, scientifically known as Artemisia capillaris, is an herb that has been revered for its health-promoting properties, which have been shown to improve digestion and alleviate symptoms of liver and skin conditions.

In culinary circles, wormwood is prized for its pungent flavor and aroma, often used in combination with other herbs like mint and sage to add depth to teas, salads, and sauces. From a horticultural standpoint, wormwood is a low-maintenance plant that can thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice among gardeners. Botanically speaking, wormwood belongs to the Asteraceae family and features small white or yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

With ancient references in traditional medicine dating back to Chinese and Greek civilizations, wormwood has been used for centuries to treat a range of ailments, solidifying its place as a revered herb in the world of natural remedies.
