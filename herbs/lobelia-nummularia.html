Lobelia nummularia

What's the taxonomical classification of Lobelia nummularia?

Lobelia nummularia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asterales, this plant is situated in the family Campanulaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Lobelia, with its specific designation being the species nummularia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lobelia nummularia contains various alkaloids, specifically lobeline, which acts as a stimulant for the central nervous system and can induce emesis. These bioactive compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's traditional use as an expectorant to help clear respiratory passages. The chemical structure of these alkaloids allows them to interact with nicotinic acetylcholine receptors in the body. While these substances possess therapeutic potential for treating congestion, they can also cause nausea or tremors if consumed in large quantities. Careful dosage is essential because the potency of the alkaloids varies significantly between individual plants.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lobelia nummularia?

Lobelia nummularia has medicinal properties that primarily function as an emmenagogue to stimulate menstrual flow and an abortifacient to induce uterine contractions. Historical herbal practices suggest the plant can be used to regulate menstrual cycles and assist in clearing the uterine lining. Some traditional applications also include its use as a mild sedative to calm the nervous system. It is often utilized in folk medicine to treat various digestive ailments and systemic inflammation. However, its potent physiological effects require extreme caution due to the risk of toxicity.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as asthma, bronchitis, and chronic coughs. It functions primarily as an expectorant to help clear mucus from the lungs. Historically, practitioners also utilized its properties to soothe irritation within the throat and bronchial tubes. Some traditional applications include managing various inflammatory conditions of the respiratory tract. Careful dosage is necessary because the plant contains potent alkaloids that can be toxic.
