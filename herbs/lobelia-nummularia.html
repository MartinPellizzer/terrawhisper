Lobelia nummularia

What's the taxonomical classification of Lobelia nummularia?

Lobelia nummularia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asterales, this plant is situated in the family Campanulaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Lobelia, with its specific designation being the species nummularia.

During its flowering period, the plant produces tiny, delicate blue or pale flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lobelia nummularia contains various alkaloids, specifically lobeline, which acts as a stimulant for the central nervous system and can induce emesis. These bioactive compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's traditional use as an expectorant to help clear respiratory passages. The chemical structure of these alkaloids allows them to interact with nicotinic acetylcholine receptors in the body. While these substances possess therapeutic potential for treating congestion, they can also cause nausea or tremors if consumed in large quantities. Careful dosage is essential because the potency of the alkaloids varies significantly between individual plants.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Lobelia nummularia targets nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (nAChRs) in the central and peripheral nervous systems through its primary alkaloid content, most notably lobeline. This interaction occurs because the chemical structure of lobeline mimics nicotine, allowing it to act as a partial agonist or antagonist depending on the specific receptor subtype and concentration. By modulating these cholinergic pathways, the plant can influence neurotransmitter release and affect muscle contractions or respiratory stimulation. Additionally, secondary metabolites within the plant may interact with various enzyme systems to exert mild sedative or antispasmodic effects. These biochemical pathways collectively contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory ailments and managing muscular tension.

Lobelia nummularia has therapeutic actions that include being a bitter, emetic, expectorant, diaphoric, and alterative.
