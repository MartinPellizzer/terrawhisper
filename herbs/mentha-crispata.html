Mentha crispata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Mentha, and its specific identification is completed by its species name, crispata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mentha crispata has decumbent to erect stems that are quadrangular in cross-section and bear ovate to lanceolate leaves with distinctly crenate or serrated margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in dense terminal verticillasters, typically presenting in shades of pale purple or white. The plant often exhibits a creeping growth habit due to its stoloniferous nature, allowing it to spread across moist substrates.
