Jasminum coarctatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum coarctatum?

Jasminum coarctatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Oleaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Jasminum, and its specific species name, coarctatum.

Traditional practitioners also utilize parts of the plant to manage stomach pains and other gastrointestinal distress. Its medicinal properties are primarily leveraged through these specific systemic and external applications.

What are the herbal preparations of Jasminum coarctatum?

Jasminum coarctatum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using the plant's dried leaves and flowers. These herbal preparations are commonly used to extract bioactive compounds for medicinal purposes. In many traditional practices, the leaves are boiled in water to create a liquid tonic. The flowers are often steeped to produce a fragrant tea or used in aromatic oils. Additionally, crushed plant material can be applied directly to the skin as a soothing poultice.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Jasminum coarctatum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if ingested or handled improperly. While many jasmine species are used for fragrance, direct contact with the plant's sap may lead to contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of unknown plant parts can result in nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps. There is also a risk of hypersensitivity reactions such as itching or redness upon touching the foliage. Always exercise caution and consult a professional before any direct exposure to the plant.
