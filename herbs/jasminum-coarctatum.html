Jasminum coarctatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum coarctatum has a woody, scrambling shrub habit characterized by slender, twining stems and opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically entire or slightly serrated. The foliage displays a glossy texture and can vary in size depending on the specific environmental conditions of its habitat. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant, white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. These blossoms feature a tubular corolla that opens into five distinct lobes, attracting various pollinators. The plant often develops dense, tangled growth patterns as the stems intertwine with surrounding vegetation.
