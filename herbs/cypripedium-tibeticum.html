Cypripedium tibeticum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cypripedium tibeticum?

Cypripedium tibeticum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Asparagales. As a member of the Orchidaceae family, it is further categorized into the genus Cypripedium, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species tibeticum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cypripedium tibeticum has a terrestrial habit characterized by several large, ovate-lanceolate leaves that emerge from a fleshy rhizome and produce a single, upright inflorescence bearing one to several showy flowers. The labellum is a prominent, pouch-shaped structure that is typically yellowish or greenish with reddish markings. Surrounding the pouch, the lateral petals are often elongated and display intricate patterns of purple or brownish veins. The sepals and dorsal petal are similarly decorated, contributing to the complex visual symmetry of the bloom. These morphological features serve as specialized adaptations for attracting specific insect pollinators within its high-altitude habitat.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the unique medicinal quinones cypritibetquinone A and cypritibetquinone B. These specific bioactive compounds are characteristic markers used to identify the chemical profile of Cypripedium tibeticum. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential pharmacological properties in treating various ailments. The presence of these specialized metabolites distinguishes this species from other members of the orchid family. Ongoing investigations aim to further isolate these molecules for potential therapeutic applications.

According to a study published by "Yao xue xue bao = Acta pharmaceutica Sinica", the chemical constituents of Cypripedium tibeticum were studied through repeated silica gel chromatography and purification on Sephadex LH-20. Two new phenanthraquinones, named cypritibetquinone A and B, were isolated from the ethyl acetate residue. The structure of cypritibetquinone A was determined as 7-hydroxy-2-methoxy-1 4-phenanthraquinone (1). Additionally, the structure of cypritibetquinone B was determined to be 7-hydroxy-2, 10-dimethoxy-l1 4-phenanthraquinone (2). These compounds were identified through extensive spectral analyses.
