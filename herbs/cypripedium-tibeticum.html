Cypripedium tibeticum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cypripedium tibeticum has a terrestrial habit characterized by several large, ovate-lanceolate leaves that emerge from a fleshy rhizome and produce a single, upright inflorescence bearing one to several showy flowers. The labellum is a prominent, pouch-shaped structure that is typically yellowish or greenish with reddish markings. Surrounding the pouch, the lateral petals are often elongated and display intricate patterns of purple or brownish veins. The sepals and dorsal petal are similarly decorated, contributing to the complex visual symmetry of the bloom. These morphological features serve as specialized adaptations for attracting specific insect pollinators within its high-altitude habitat.

      What are the herbal preparations of Cypripedium tibeticum?

      Cypripedium tibeticum is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical salve depending on the intended medicinal application.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cypripedium tibeticum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested due to the presence of unstudied alkaloids and organic compounds. Because this species is a rare orchid with limited toxicological data, any direct contact with the sap may lead to redness or itching for sensitive individuals. Ingestion of the plant is strongly discouraged as it could result in nausea, vomiting, or more severe internal reactions. People with known allergies to various orchidaceae species should exercise extreme caution when handling this specimen.
