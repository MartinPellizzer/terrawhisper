Cypripedium tibeticum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cypripedium tibeticum?

Cypripedium tibeticum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Asparagales. As a member of the Orchidaceae family, it is further categorized into the genus Cypripedium, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species tibeticum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cypripedium tibeticum has a terrestrial habit characterized by several large, ovate-lanceolate leaves that emerge from a fleshy rhizome and produce a single, upright inflorescence bearing one to several showy flowers. The labellum is a prominent, pouch-shaped structure that is typically yellowish or greenish with reddish markings. Surrounding the pouch, the lateral petals are often elongated and display intricate patterns of purple or brownish veins. The sepals and dorsal petal are similarly decorated, contributing to the complex visual symmetry of the bloom.
