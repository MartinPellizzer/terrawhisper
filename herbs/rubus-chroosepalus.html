Rubus chroosepalus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus chroosepaulus has arching, woody stems characterized by a dense covering of sharp, recurved prickles and a rough, glandular texture. The leaves are typically odd-pinnate with serrated margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the branches, featuring five distinct petals surrounding a central cluster of stamens. These blossoms eventually develop into aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep, fleshy red upon maturity. The root system consists of a perennial woody base that allows the plant to spread through various vegetative means.
