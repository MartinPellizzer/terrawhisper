Rubus chroosepalus

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus chroosepalus?

Rubus chroosepalus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Rubus, and its specific species designation, cheroosepaulus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus chroosepaulus has arching, woody stems characterized by a dense covering of sharp, recurved prickles and a rough, glandular texture. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the branches, featuring five distinct petals surrounding a central cluster of stamens. These blossoms eventually develop into aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep, fleshy red upon maturity. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds known as the acetonide of IIIa and IIIb. These specific derivatives are synthesized within the plant's tissues to provide various therapeutic properties. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential applications in modern pharmacology. The presence of these acetonides suggests a complex biochemical pathway used for defense or signaling. Such chemical diversity makes this species a significant subject for botanical investigation.

As discussed in a study, seven compounds were isolated and identified from the root of Rubus chroosepalus. These identified medicinal compounds include 2 alpha, 3 beta-dihydroxy-urs-12, 19-dien-23, 28-oic acid (I), 2 alpha, 3 beta, 23-trihydroxy-urs-12,18-dien-28-oic acid (IIa), and 2 alpha, 3 beta, 23-trihydroxy-urs-12,19-dien-28-oic acid (IIb). Additional substances found were 2 alpha, 3 alpha-dihydroxy-urs-12,18-dien-28-oic acid (IIIa), 2 alpha, 3 alpha-dihydroxy-urs-12,19-dien-28-oic acid (IIIb), and the acetonide of IIIa and IIIb (IVa and IVb). Compound I was specifically found to be a new pentacyclic triterpenoid acid.
