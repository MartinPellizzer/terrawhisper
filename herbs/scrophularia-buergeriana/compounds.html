Buerger'S Scrophularia (Scrophularia buergeriana) Compounds

The primary compounds of Buerger's Scrophularia include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and glycosides contribute to its astringent and medicinal qualities, while coumarins and terpenoids may support circulatory health. Saponins, essential oils, and alkaloids also play roles in its therapeutic applications.

Buerger'S Scrophularia (Scrophularia buergeriana) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Buerger's Scrophularia. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds may support cardiovascular health and immune function. Flavonoids are a key group of phytochemicals in this traditional medicinal plant.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Buerger's Scrophularia (Scrophularia buergeriana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which are central to its traditional use in treating conditions like inflammation and fever. This compound supports the plant's role in herbal medicine for immune modulation and skin health.

Tannin in Buerger's Scrophularia has astringent properties that may help reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. Glycoside is a key active compound found in Buerger's Scrophularia (Scrophularia buergeriana). 5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Buerger's Scrophularia (Scrophularia buergeriana). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory and cardiovascular conditions. Coumarin exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

6. Terpenoid

7. Saponin

Essential oil from Buerger's Scrophularia contains volatile compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. 9. Alkaloid

Scrophularia buergeriana contains the alkaloid scrophularine. This compound is known for its potential cardiovascular effects and is studied for its role in traditional Chinese medicine. It may help regulate blood pressure and improve circulation, making it relevant in the treatment of circulatory disorders.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Buerger's Scrophularia. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These actions support its traditional use in promoting circulation and reducing oxidative stress in herbal treatments.
