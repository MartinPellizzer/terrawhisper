Scrophularia buergeriana is the scientific name of Buerger's Scrophularia. It was first described by the botanist Buerger, hence the species name. This name is widely accepted in modern botanical references. No widely recognized synonyms are currently listed for this species.

Morphological Characteristics

Buerger's Scrophularia is an annual herb with a taproot system. It has erect, branched stems that are hairy. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have toothed margins. The inflorescence is a spike-like cluster of flowers. Flowers are pink to purple, tubular, and bilaterally symmetrical. The fruit is a capsule containing small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Buerger's Scrophularia can be distinguished by its distinctive leaf shape, with elongated, lanceolate leaves and a prominent midrib. It also has a unique flowering pattern with pale pink to white flowers in clusters. These features help differentiate it from closely related species in the Scrophulariaceae family. The presence of specific leaf venation and flower structure are key diagnostic traits.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Buerger's Scrophularia is native to East Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Buerger's Scrophularia blooms from June to August. Seed formation occurs from September to October. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in late summer to early autumn when the leaves are fully developed and the seeds are mature.
