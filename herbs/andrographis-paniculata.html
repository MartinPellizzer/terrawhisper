What to know about Andrographis paniculata before using it medicinally

Andrographis paniculata, commonly known as king of bitters, is a medicinal herb widely used to treat various health conditions, including fever, respiratory infections, and inflammatory diseases such as arthritis and asthma.

Its main health benefits include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects, which contribute to its therapeutic properties of reducing inflammation, fighting oxidative stress, and modulating the immune system. The herb's therapeutic properties are attributed to its main bioactive compounds, including andrographolide, neoandrographolide, and 14-deoxyandrographolide, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities. In traditional herbal medicine, Andrographis paniculata is commonly prepared as a decoction or a tea by infusing the dried leaves in hot water, which is believed to enhance the bioavailability of its active compounds.

This herbal preparation has been used for centuries in various cultures to treat a range of health conditions, and its medicinal properties have been extensively studied and validated by modern scientific research.
