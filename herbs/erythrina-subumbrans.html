Erythrina subumbrans

What's the taxonomical classification of Erythrina subumbrans?

Erythrina subumbrans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Fabales. It is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which is known for its diverse range of legumes. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Erythrina, and its specific identity is defined by its species name, subumbrans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erythrina subumbrans has trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct leaflets, bright red papilionaceous flowers, and woody stems that often develop sharp spines. The leaflets are typically elliptic or ovate in shape and possess a smooth, entire margin. Its most striking feature is the large, vibrant floral structure which attracts specific pollinators through its intense coloration. What are the pharmacological activities of Erythrina subumbrans?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its anti-inflammatory, anticancer, antidiabetic, antimicrobial, and cytotoxic activities. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, the plant exhibits potent cytotoxic properties that contribute to its ability to combat cancer cell proliferation. These metabolic effects extend to managing blood glucose levels, showcasing its therapeutic promise for antidiabetic applications. Additionally, its ability to reduce systemic inflammation makes it a candidate for treating chronic inflammatory conditions.

As discussed in a study, the phytochemical investigation of Erythrina subumbrans resulted in the isolation of a new pterocarpan, erythrinocarpan (1), and 27 known compounds (2-28). Compounds 3, 8, 9, and 22 demonstrated alpha-glucosidase inhibitory activity with IC50 values of 13.4 ± 0.05, 24.5 ± 0.13, 29.0 ± 0.05, and 12.8 ± 0.14 μM, respectively. Additionally, compound 2 inhibited alpha-amylase activity with an IC50 value of 67.6 ± 1.12 μM, while compounds 22 and 24 inhibited glycation activity with IC50 values of 36.9 ± 0.62 and 40.5 ± 0.37 μM. From cell-based assays, compound 27 showed a 2.8-fold glucose uptake, an IC50 of 29.1 ± 0.86 μM for inducing glucose consumption, and an IC50 of 52.5 ± 0.56 μM for inhibiting nitric oxide production. Finally, compound 9 exhibited antimicrobial activities against Gram-positive bacteria and fungi with MIC values ranging from 2-4 μg/mL.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 1-methoxyerythrabyssin II, 5-hydroxysophoranone, isolupalbigenin, abyssinone V, and erybraedin A. These specific bioactive molecules are often studied for their potential pharmacological properties and chemical complexity. The presence of such diverse secondary metabolites suggests that the plant possesses significant therapeutic potential. Researchers analyze these compounds to understand their individual effects on human biological systems. Continued investigation into these substances may lead to the development of new pharmaceutical treatments.

As discussed in a study, the bark of Erythrina subumbrans contains three new erythrina alkaloids, (+) -10,11-dioxoerythratine (1), (+) -10,11-dioxoepierythratidine (2), and (+) -10,11-dioxoerythratidinone (3). This plant also yields a new pterocarpan identified as 1-methoxyerythrabyssin II (4) alongside 7 known pterocarpans. The chemical profile includes 3 flavanones, specifically 5-hydroxysophoranone, abyssinone V, and lespedezaflavanone B. Additionally, the species contains 3 triterpene compounds and 1 isoflavanone named vogelin C. Some of these isolated compounds were tested for antiplasmodial, antimycobacterial, and cytotoxic activities.
