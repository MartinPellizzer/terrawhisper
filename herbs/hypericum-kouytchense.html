Hypericum kouytchense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypericum kouytchense has erect, woody stems that support opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins and distinct glandular dots. The foliage is typically dark green and arranged densely along the branches. Small, five-petaled yellow flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the stems during the blooming season. These blossoms feature prominent central stamens that provide a feathery appearance to the inflorescence. The plant's overall structure is characterized by its shrubby habit and compact growth form.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Because it can influence neurotransmitter levels, it may trigger mood swings or restlessness in sensitive users. Always consult a medical professional before using this herb to avoid unexpected complications.
