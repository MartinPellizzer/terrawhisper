Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Dahurian Angelica (Angelica Dahurica)?

Dahurian Angelica, or Angelica dahurica, is a biennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, known for its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves, and used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Dahurian Angelica is Angelica dahurica. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Apiaceae, genus Angelica, and species Angelica dahurica. It is commonly known as Dahurian Angelica in Russia and China, and as Chinese Angelica in parts of East Asia. The synonyms for this plant include Angelica sinensis and Angelica archangelica.

Plant Description and Morphology

Dahurian Angelica is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with a straight, hollow stem that is slightly hairy.

Its leaves are large, pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. The root system consists of a thick, fleshy taproot that is often branched and has a distinctive aromatic odor.

The plant has a strong, camphor-like scent, with a rough, fibrous texture on the stem and a white, fleshy interior in the roots.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Dahurian Angelica is native to the colder regions of East Asia, including parts of Russia, China, and Mongolia.

It is commonly found in mountainous areas and temperate forests, particularly in the Russian Far East and the Altai Mountains. The plant thrives in cool to temperate climates with well-drained soils and moderate moisture. It prefers partial to full shade and is often found in moist, loamy soil conditions.

Its natural range extends across the northern hemisphere, particularly in regions with long winters and short summers.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The roots, leaves, and seeds of Dahurian Angelica are used medicinally, with the roots being the most commonly utilized part for its therapeutic properties.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of Dahurian Angelica includes volatile oils, alkaloids, flavonoids, coumarins, and lignans, with key constituents such as ligustilide, butylidenephthalide, and angelica acid contributing to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacological Properties

Dahurian Angelica exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antispasmodic effects, primarily due to its active compounds such as phytosterols, essential oils, and alkaloids.

Mechanisms of Action

The primary mechanisms of action of Dahurian Angelica involve its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects, primarily mediated through the bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and essential oils that influence cellular signaling pathways and reduce oxidative stress.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica) is traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health, alleviate pain, reduce inflammation, and promote digestion due to its rich content of bioactive compounds such as volatile oils, alkaloids, and flavonoids.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Dahurian Angelica is typically prepared as a tincture, decoction, or powder, with the most common method involving soaking the dried root in alcohol for several weeks to extract its active compounds.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Angelica dahurica for adults is 2-4 grams of dried root per day, usually divided into two or three doses, and it is commonly prepared as a decoction by boiling the root in water for 15-20 minutes.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Dahurian Angelica is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, or interact with certain medications, and it is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks to the fetus or infant.

Drug Interactions

Individuals using Dahurian Angelica should be aware that it may interact with medications that affect blood clotting, such as anticoagulants and antiplatelet drugs, potentially increasing the risk of bleeding.

While Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals and should be avoided during pregnancy due to its potential uterine stimulant effects.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Dahurian Angelica is typically cultivated in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring a cold winter dormancy period and being harvested primarily in late summer to early autumn when the roots are most potent.

Processing and Extraction

The processing and extraction of Dahurian Angelica involve harvesting the dried roots and rhizomes, which are then cleaned, sliced, and subjected to methods such as maceration, steam distillation, or solvent extraction to obtain active compounds like ligustilide and butylphthalide.

Storage and Shelf Life

Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, and its shelf life is typically 2 to 3 years when properly preserved.
