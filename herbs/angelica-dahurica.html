Dahurian Angelica

Angelica dahurica

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Angelica dahurica, commonly known as Dahurian Angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia, including regions of Russia and China. The plant features a thick, aromatic root, large compound leaves, and yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. It is also referred to as Dahurian angelica or Chinese angelica in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The primary active compounds in Dahurian Angelica include essential oil, coumarin, and flavonoids. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Essential oil contributes to its aromatic properties, while coumarin and flavonoids support its medicinal actions in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Coumarin
  • Flavonoid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action

Digestive Gastritis, gastrointestinal discomfort, nausea, stomach ulcers, gastric ulcers Antispasmodic, spasmolytic, antiemetic, antibacterial

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments due to its warming and carminative properties.

Infusion: Used for colds, flu, and respiratory infections due to its expectorant and antispasmodic effects.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and digestive issues due to its concentrated active compounds.

Safety Profile

Dahurian Angelica is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulation effects. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions.
