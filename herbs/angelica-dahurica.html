Angelica Dahurica Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Angelica dahurica, commonly known as Chinese angelica or Dahurian angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to East Asia, prized for its medicinal properties.

It is a member of the carrot family and is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and circulatory benefits.

The herb contains bioactive constituents such as volatile oils, lignans, and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Angelica dahurica can be prepared as an infusion, tincture, or decoction, and is often used to support respiratory, digestive, and immune health.

Medicinal Parts

Angelica dahurica root is one of the most prized medicinal parts of this traditional Chinese herb, widely used in both Chinese and Western herbal medicine.

It is harvested during the late autumn or early winter when the plant's active compounds are most concentrated, and it is typically dried and processed for use. The root contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including essential oils, alkaloids, and coumarins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

In traditional medicine, it is valued for its ability to promote circulation, reduce inflammation, and support respiratory and digestive health. Modern research has also shown that the root may have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, making it a valuable component in the treatment of various ailments.
