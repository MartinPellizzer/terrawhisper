Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica)

Dahurian Angelica (Angelica dahurica) is a member of the Apiaceae family, native to China, Korea, and Eastern Russia. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Flowers

Cylindrical, hollow, ribbed, and pithy, with a distinct ring of longitudinal ridges and a smooth, glabrous surface.
