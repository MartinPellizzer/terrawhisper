Kokum (Garcinia indica) Compounds

The primary compounds of Kokum include flavonoids, which are known for their antioxidant properties. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects. Terpenoids and glycosides are also present, offering additional therapeutic benefits. Saponins, alkaloids, and coumarin further enhance its medicinal profile, while essential oils and anthocyanins provide aromatic and color properties.

Kokum (Garcinia indica) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Kokum (Garcinia indica). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds support the therapeutic use of Kokum in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and skin health. Flavonoids enhance the plant's pharmacological profile.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Kokum (Garcinia indica). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Kokum help in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. They also support wound healing and reduce excess body fluids.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Kokum (Garcinia indica). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound helps protect cells from oxidative stress and supports overall health. Its presence in Kokum makes it valuable in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Kokum (Garcinia indica). They contribute to the fruit's medicinal properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic uses in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Kokum (Garcinia indica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting digestive health and managing lipid levels. Glycosides in Kokum are known for their potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are often extracted and used in traditional herbal formulations.

6. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Kokum (Garcinia indica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by interacting with biological systems to potentially support digestive and metabolic functions. Alkaloids in Kokum are studied for their role in traditional herbal medicine practices.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Kokum (Garcinia indica). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine due to its cleansing and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponin helps in detoxification and may support digestive health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Kokum in various medicinal applications.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Kokum (Garcinia indica). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and inflammation. Coumarin's antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties support its role in herbal medicine. It is also noted for its potential in managing gastrointestinal disorders.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Kokum (Garcinia indica). It is extracted from the fruit's rind and contains volatile aromatic compounds. This oil is valued for its antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Kokum (Garcinia indica). It contributes to the fruit's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties. These antioxidants help neutralize free radicals, supporting overall health and potentially reducing oxidative stress in the body.
