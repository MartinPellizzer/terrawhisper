Kokum (Garcinia indica) Uses

Kokum is used to manage obesity and type 2 diabetes by improving insulin sensitivity and reducing lipid levels. It helps with metabolic syndrome and high cholesterol, supporting cardiovascular health. It also aids in treating gastric ulcers and gastroesophageal reflux disease due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

Kokum (Garcinia indica) Monograph

1. Obesity

Kokum, or Garcinia indica, is used in traditional medicine to support weight management. Its active compound, hydroxycitric acid, may inhibit fat production and increase metabolism. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effects on reducing body fat and improving metabolic health.

2. Diabetes

Kokum fruit contains compounds that may help regulate blood sugar levels. It is traditionally used in Ayurveda to support metabolic health. Studies suggest its polyphenols may enhance insulin sensitivity. This makes it a potential natural aid for managing diabetes.

3. Lipid disorders

Kokum (Garcinia indica) is used in traditional herbal medicine to address lipid disorders due to its high content of hydroxycitric acid, which may inhibit fat synthesis and support weight management. It is often incorporated into formulations aimed at reducing cholesterol and triglyceride levels, promoting cardiovascular health.

4. Weight gain

Kokum, or Garcinia indica, is traditionally used to promote weight gain by enhancing appetite and improving digestion. Its high concentration of hydroxycitric acid supports nutrient absorption and metabolic efficiency, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for those seeking to increase body mass in a healthy manner.

5. Insulin resistance

Kokum has been traditionally used to support metabolic health. Its bioactive compounds may help improve insulin sensitivity by modulating glucose metabolism. Studies suggest it may assist in managing insulin resistance, though more research is needed for definitive conclusions.

6. Metabolic syndrome

Kokum (Garcinia indica) is used in traditional herbal medicine to support metabolic health. Its active compounds, such as hydroxycitric acid, may help regulate blood sugar and lipid levels, which are key factors in metabolic syndrome. Studies suggest it can aid in weight management and improve insulin sensitivity, making it a valuable natural supplement for managing metabolic disorders.

7. High cholesterol

Kokum (Garcinia indica) is used in traditional medicine to help manage high cholesterol due to its high content of hydroxycitric acid, which may inhibit fat synthesis. It also supports liver function, aiding in the metabolism of lipids and the regulation of cholesterol levels in the blood.

8. Type 2 diabetes

Kokum has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support blood sugar regulation. Its bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and polyphenols, may enhance insulin sensitivity and reduce glucose absorption. Studies suggest it may help manage blood sugar levels in individuals with type 2 diabetes. However, it should complement, not replace, conventional medical treatment.

9. Gastric ulcer

Kokum fruit extract contains tannins and flavonoids that protect the gastric lining by reducing acid secretion and promoting mucosal repair. It is traditionally used in Ayurveda to treat gastric ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Clinical studies suggest it may help in the healing of ulcers by enhancing the protective barrier of the stomach.

10. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Kokum (Garcinia indica) is used in traditional medicine to manage gastroesophageal reflux disease due to its anti-inflammatory and gastroprotective properties. It helps reduce stomach acid and soothe the esophageal lining, offering relief from symptoms like heartburn and regurgitation. Its natural compounds may also enhance digestive function and prevent acid reflux.
