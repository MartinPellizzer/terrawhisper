Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Kokum is Garcinia indica, a species belonging to the family Clusiaceae. It was first described by the botanist Robertus, and it has several synonyms such as Garcinia cowa and Garcinia gurjao. Common names include Kokum, Indian gooseberry, and kokum berry, while regional names vary across South Asian countries. Historically, it was also known as 'Gurjao' in some parts of India.

Morphological Characteristics

Kokum (Garcinia indica) is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense, rounded crown. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly in the soil. The stem is straight and cylindrical, with a smooth, slightly glossy bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The inflorescence consists of clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a rough, yellowish drupe with a thick, acidic pulp and a single seed inside.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Kokum, or Garcinia indica, can be distinguished by its smooth, yellowish-green leaves with prominent veins, orange to red berries containing multiple seeds, and its distinctive, sour-sweet aroma. It differs from similar species like Garcinia morella by its smaller, more acidic berries and the presence of a fleshy, segmented outer layer. The plant's unique scent and berry structure are key identifiers in botanical recognition.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Kokum (Garcinia indica) is native to the Western Ghats of India. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and moderate temperatures. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 300 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Kokum trees bloom between March and May, producing yellowish-green flowers. Seed formation occurs from June to August. Harvesting of the fruit typically happens from September to December, when the fruit is fully mature and the outer husk turns dark red.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Kokum (Garcinia indica) is closely related to species within the Garcinia genus. It shares morphological and genetic traits with other members of the Clusiaceae family. These relationships help in understanding its evolutionary history and medicinal properties.
