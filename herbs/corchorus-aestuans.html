Corchorus aestuans

What's the taxonomical classification of Corchorus aestuans?

Corchorus aestuans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Malvales. It is further classified under the family Malvaceae, which places it within the genus Corchorus, ultimately identifying it by its specific species name, aestuans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corchorus aestuans has an erect, slender herbaceous habit characterized by narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The stems are typically thin and can grow quite tall in suitable environments. Each leaf is simple and possesses a long, tapering base that often appears somewhat amplexicaul around the stem. The flowers are actinomorphic with five distinct petals and are usually borne in the leaf axils. Small, elongated capsules serve as the fruit, containing numerous tiny seeds that facilitate its reproduction.

Corchorus aestuans targets cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various inflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. These bioactive compounds primarily act by inhibiting the enzyme cyclooxygenase-2, which significantly reduces the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins.
