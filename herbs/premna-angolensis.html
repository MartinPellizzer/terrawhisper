Premna angolensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Premna angolensis has opposite, decussate leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape, featuring entire margins and an acute or acuminate apex. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it feels somewhat leathery to the touch. Its stems are woody and can develop a rough bark as the shrub or small tree matures. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often arranged in axillary cymes. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that serve as a primary dispersal mechanism.
