Cypripedium flavum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cypripedium flavum?

Cypripedium flavum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its biological classification, it falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cypripedium, and its unique species designation, flavum.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cypripedium flavum can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While specific toxicological studies on this particular orchid species are limited, many members of the Cypripedium genus contain compounds that may trigger sensitive reactions in humans. Direct contact with the plant's sap or tissues could lead to localized redness, itching, or swelling on the skin. If someone accidentally consumes any part of the plant, they might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. It is always best to avoid ingestion and handle the plant with care to prevent unexpected physical discomfort.
