Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) Compounds

Common Hawthorn contains several primary active compounds, including glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds contribute to its cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and coumarin also play roles in its therapeutic properties, while saponins and terpenoids support digestive health. Essential oils, anthocyanins, and alkaloids add to its diverse pharmacological profile.

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) Monograph

1. Glycoside

Active compounds of Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) include glycosides, which are known for their cardiovascular benefits. These compounds help regulate heart rate and improve blood flow, making them valuable in treating conditions like hypertension and arrhythmia. Glycosides contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for heart health.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha). They contribute to the plant's cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting traditional uses in herbal medicine for heart health and stress-related conditions.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports cardiovascular health and may aid in managing conditions like hypertension and arrhythmia. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Common Hawthorn in herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antioxidant effects, which may help reduce inflammation and protect cells from damage.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and aid in reducing anxiety-related symptoms. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of Common Hawthorn in herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating cardiovascular conditions by potentially improving heart function and reducing blood pressure. Saponins may also have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's role in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic effects, particularly in supporting cardiovascular health and reducing anxiety. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) contains volatile compounds like limonene and alpha-pinene. These components contribute to its aromatic properties and may support cardiovascular health by promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support cardiovascular health. Found in the berries, anthocyanin may help reduce oxidative stress and improve blood circulation.

10. Alkaloid

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) contains alkaloids, which are nitrogen-containing compounds found in various plants. These alkaloids contribute to the plant's pharmacological properties, though they are present in smaller quantities compared to other active constituents like flavonoids and saponins.
