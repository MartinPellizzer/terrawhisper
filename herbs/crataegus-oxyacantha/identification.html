Botanical Identification of Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha)

To identify Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Common Hawthorn, Crataegus oxyacantha, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically within the flowering plants, eudicots, and the rose order. It is part of the rose family, genus Crataegus, and the species Crataegus oxyacantha. This classification reflects its botanical relationship to other plants in the same family and order.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Common Hawthorn is Crataegus oxyacantha, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It has several synonyms, including Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata, which reflect historical taxonomic revisions. Common names include Hawthorn, Common Hawthorn, and Maytree, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Common Hawthorn is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a rounded growth habit. It has a deep taproot system and woody stems that often branch near the base. Leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a rough texture. The inflorescence is a rounded cyme with white to pale pink flowers. The fruit is a red, fleshy pome containing one or two seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Common Hawthorn can be distinguished by its thorny stems, opposite leaf arrangement, and clusters of white flowers followed by red berries. It differs from similar species like Crataegus monogyna by its more prominent thorns and distinct fruit shape. The leaves are oval with serrated edges, and the bark is grey with shallow fissures.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Common Hawthorn is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. It prefers well-drained soils, such as loamy or sandy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Common Hawthorn blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, when the plant is most easily recognized.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) belongs to the genus Crataegus in the Rosaceae family. It is closely related to other hawthorn species within the same genus. Its phylogenetic position is defined by shared morphological and genetic traits with other Rosaceae members, particularly those in the subfamily Maloideae.
