Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) Uses

Common Hawthorn is used to treat hypertension, arrhythmia, atherosclerosis, heart failure, and chest pain. It helps regulate heart rhythm and improve blood flow, making it effective for conditions like angina, tachycardia, and cardiac arrhythmia. Its compounds support cardiovascular health and are commonly used in herbal medicine for heart-related ailments.

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) Monograph

1. Hypertension

Common Hawthorn has been traditionally used to support cardiovascular health, including managing hypertension. It contains flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins that may help dilate blood vessels and improve circulation. Its mild sedative effect can also reduce stress-related spikes in blood pressure. It is often used in herbal formulations for long-term cardiovascular support.

2. Arrhythmia

Common Hawthorn is used to support heart health and manage arrhythmia due to its flavonoids and antioxidant properties. It helps regulate heart rhythm by improving blood flow and reducing oxidative stress. Clinical studies suggest it may aid in managing irregular heartbeats when used under professional guidance.

3. Atherosclerosis

Common Hawthorn supports cardiovascular health by improving blood flow and reducing arterial stiffness. It contains flavonoids and proanthocyanidins that may help prevent atherosclerosis by inhibiting oxidative stress and inflammation. Studies suggest it may lower LDL cholesterol and enhance endothelial function, making it a valuable herb for managing arterial plaque formation.

4. Heart failure

Common Hawthorn supports heart function by improving circulation and reducing strain on the heart muscle. It is used to manage symptoms of heart failure, such as fatigue and shortness of breath. Its active compounds, including flavonoids and oligomeric procyanidins, help strengthen cardiac muscle and regulate blood pressure.

5. Chest pain

Common Hawthorn is used to alleviate chest pain, particularly in cases of angina or heart-related discomfort. It supports cardiovascular health by improving blood flow and reducing arterial stiffness. Its mild sedative effects also help ease anxiety, which can contribute to chest pain. This makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for heart support.

6. Angina

Common Hawthorn is used to support heart health and may help alleviate symptoms of angina by improving blood flow and reducing cardiac strain. Its compounds, such as flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins, have mild vasodilatory and antioxidant effects. It is typically used in combination with other herbs in traditional herbal formulations for cardiovascular support.

7. High blood pressure

Common Hawthorn has been traditionally used to support cardiovascular health, including managing high blood pressure. It contains bioflavonoids and antioxidants that may help relax blood vessels and improve circulation. Its mild sedative properties can also reduce stress-related hypertension. Regular use under guidance may complement conventional treatments.

8. Tachycardia

Common Hawthorn is used to support heart health and may help manage tachycardia by strengthening cardiac function and regulating rhythm. Its active compounds, such as flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins, improve blood vessel elasticity and reduce oxidative stress, which can contribute to irregular heartbeats. It is often used in herbal formulations for mild cardiac support under professional guidance.

9. Angina pectoris

Common Hawthorn is used to support heart health and may help manage angina pectoris by improving circulation and reducing cardiac strain. It contains flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins that enhance coronary blood flow and strengthen heart muscle function. Traditionally, it is often combined with other herbs in cardiovascular formulas. Its mild action makes it suitable for long-term use under professional guidance.

10. Cardiac arrhythmia

Common Hawthorn has been traditionally used to support heart health, particularly in managing cardiac arrhythmia. It contains bioactive compounds that may help regulate heart rhythm by influencing neurotransmitters and improving blood flow. Its mild action makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for supporting steady heart function.
