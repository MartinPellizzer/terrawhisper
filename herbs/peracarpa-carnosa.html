Peracarpa carnosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Peracarpa carnosa has a woody, shrub-like structure characterized by elongated stems, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and fleshy, succulent fruits. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern and possesses a leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into the namesake fleshy drupes. The root system is extensive and fibrous, providing stability within its native soil environments. These morphological traits collectively enable the plant to thrive in specific ecological niches.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?
