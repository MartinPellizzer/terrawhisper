What is Peracarpa carnosa?

Peracarpa carnosa is a species of flowering plant in the family Campanulaceae.

It is an annual or perennial herb, 10-30 cm tall, with simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves, 2-10 cm long, and 0.5-2.5 cm wide. The leaves are sessile, glabrous, and have a cuneate base. The plant produces solitary, axillary flowers, 1-2 cm in diameter, with a campanulate corolla, five-lobed, and white or purple in color, and has five stamens, with the two upper ones longer than the lower three.

The fruit is a capsule, 3-5 mm long, containing numerous seeds.
