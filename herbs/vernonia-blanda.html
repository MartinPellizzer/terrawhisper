Vernonia blanda

What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia blanda?

Vernonia blanda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, and its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Vernonia and the species blanda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia blanda has erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 0.5 to 1.5 meters and are characterized by alternate, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and prominent veins. The foliage is often slightly hairy or glabrous depending on the specific variety and environmental conditions. At the apex of the stems, the plant produces clusters of discoid flower heads that are predominantly purple or violet in color. Each flower head consists of numerous tubular florets that lack ray flowers, a distinguishing feature of the genus. These inflorescences are arranged in terminal corymbose cymes that provide a dense display of color during the late summer blooming season.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?
