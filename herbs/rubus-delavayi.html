What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus delavayi?

Rubus delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Rosales and falls within the family Rosaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by its genus, Rubus, and its specific species designation, delaivayi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus delavayi has arching or erect stems that are densely covered in reddish-brown prickles and feature compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. Each leaflet is typically ovate or lanceolate in shape, possessing serrated margins and a slightly pubescent texture on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that emerge from terminal or axillary clusters. These blossoms eventually develop into aggregate drupelets that transition from green to a dark, fleshy color when ripe. The overall structure is characterized by a woody, bramble-like habit suitable for mountain habitats.
