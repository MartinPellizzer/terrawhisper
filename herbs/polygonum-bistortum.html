Bistort (Polygonum bistortum): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Bistort (Polygonum Bistortum)?

Bistort, or Polygonum bistortum, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Bistort, or Polygonum bistortum, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae, commonly known as the buckwheat family, and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Common Names and Synonyms

Common names for Bistort (Polygonum bistortum) include Bistort root, Narrow-leaved bistort, and Red bistort, with synonyms such as Bistort herb and Polygonum corymbosum.

Plant Description and Morphology

Bistort (Polygonum bistortum) is a perennial herb with a stout, branching stem that rises from a rhizomatous root system, typically growing to a height of 30-60 cm, bearing alternate, heart-shaped to ovate leaves with prominent veins and small, clustered, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Bistort (Polygonum bistortum) is native to Europe, particularly thriving in moist, calcareous soils across regions such as the British Isles, France, and parts of northern Italy.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The roots and rhizomes of Bistort (Polygonum bistortum) are the primary plant parts used medicinally.

Pharmacological Properties
