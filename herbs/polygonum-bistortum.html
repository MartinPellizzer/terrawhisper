Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Polygonum bistortum can be used as a medicinal plant for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, but it should be used with caution due to its possible side effects.

Some individuals may experience gastrointestinal upset, including nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea, and in rare cases, allergic reactions such as rash or itching may occur. It is important to note that Polygonum bistortum may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the liver or blood clotting, and may reduce the effectiveness of anticoagulants or antiplatelet drugs. Special populations, such as pregnant or breastfeeding women, should avoid using this herb due to insufficient safety data, and individuals with chronic illnesses should consult a healthcare provider before use.

To ensure safe use, always consult a qualified healthcare professional, start with a low dose, and discontinue use if adverse effects occur.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Polygonum bistortum grows in moist, well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates with consistent moisture.

It prefers loamy or sandy soils rich in organic matter and should be planted in areas with good air circulation to prevent fungal diseases. Regular watering is essential during the growing season, but the soil should not be waterlogged. To maintain healthy growth, periodic weeding and light fertilization in early spring can promote vigorous foliage and root development. Harvesting is best done in late summer to early autumn when the leaves and roots are most potent, using clean tools to cut the stems just above the ground level.

After harvesting, the plant material should be dried in a cool, shaded area with good airflow to preserve its medicinal properties, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dry place to maintain potency over time.
