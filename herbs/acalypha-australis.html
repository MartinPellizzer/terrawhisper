Acalypha australis

What's the taxonomical classification of Acalypha australis?

Acalypha australis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae. Finally, its specific biological classification is defined by the genus Acalypha and the species australis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acalypha australis has an erect or ascending perennial habit characterized by stems that are often reddish, hairy, and branched from the base. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters or catkins. These inflorescences typically appear in the leaf axils and are often accompanied by specialized bracts. The plant's overall morphology allows it to thrive in disturbed soils and various tropical environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Acalypha australis?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, detoxifying, diuretic, heat-clearing, and hemostatic properties. Its chemical constituents allow it to reduce swelling and soothe bodily inflammation through various biological pathways. Finally, its hemostatic qualities enable it to effectively control and stop bleeding in different tissues.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Acalypha australis L. (AAL) is a traditional medicinal herb used for heat-clearing, detoxifying, and diuretic properties, as well as treating diarrhea and dysentery. UPLC-QTOF-MS analysis identified 47 compounds in AAL, including flavonoids, terpenoids, and polyphenols, with bergaptol and corilagin identified as major constituents. AAL mitigates chronic colitis by modulating the FABP4/PPARγ/NF-κB signaling pathway, which involves suppressing FABP4 expression, enhancing PPARγ levels, and reducing p-P65 expression. The treatment preserves intestinal mucosal integrity by upregulating the expression of MUC2, ZO-1, and Occludin. These pharmacological activities result in reduced DAI scores, restoration of body weight, and improved colon length.
