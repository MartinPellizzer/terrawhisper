Corydalis wuzhengyiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis wuzhengyiana has herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, supporting deeply divided, pinnately compound leaves with ovate or lanceolate leaflets. The inflorescences consist of several zygomorphic flowers characterized by a long, slender, upward-curving nectar spur at the base of the corolla. These flowers display a color palette ranging from pale yellow to light violet, often featuring delicate veining patterns on the petals. The plant also produces small, capsule-like fruits that develop from the ovaries after successful pollination. Its root system is comprised of thickened, tuberous structures that serve as energy storage organs for the perennial life cycle.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Researchers have specifically focused on these alkaloids for their potential to treat inflammatory conditions and gastrointestinal disorders. What side-effects this plant can have?

Corydalis wuzhengyiana can cause gastrointestinal distress, dizziness, nausea, and potential neurological impairment due to its high concentration of bioactive alkaloids. These chemical compounds interact with the central nervous system, which may lead to unexpected sedation or altered mental states if consumed in improper dosages. Prolonged or excessive use might also result in adverse reactions involving the digestive tract or respiratory rhythm. Because the specific toxicity levels vary based on individual sensitivity, users should approach the plant with significant caution.
