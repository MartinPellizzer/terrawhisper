Chlorophora reticulata

Chlorophora reticulata, also known as the Ipe rocha or West African teak, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, which are attributed to its high concentration of flavonoids and phenolic compounds.

Chlorophora reticulata has been found to have various health benefits, such as reducing fever, treating skin conditions, and aiding in the treatment of respiratory infections.

Herbal preparations of the plant include teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to harness its medicinal properties and promote overall well-being.
