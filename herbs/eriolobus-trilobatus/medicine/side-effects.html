10 Possible Side Effects Of Eriolobus Trilobatus

Eriolobus trilobatus has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, dizziness, and headaches.

These symptoms occur when the herb is consumed in excess or combined with other medications.

For individuals with pre-existing conditions, such as high blood pressure or anxiety, these side effects can worsen and lead to serious health complications.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Eriolobus trilobatus causes allergic reactions due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant's sap contains potent allergens that can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, hives, and even anaphylaxis.

2. Increases blood pressure

Eriolobus trilobatus increases blood pressure due to its high levels of glycosides and saponins.

These compounds can cause vasoconstriction, leading to increased blood vessel resistance and elevated blood pressure.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids may enhance the activity of certain enzymes that regulate blood pressure, further contributing to the increase in blood pressure.

3. Triggers asthma attacks

Eriolobus trilobatus triggers asthma attacks due to its high pollen content, which can cause respiratory irritation and exacerbate symptoms in individuals with pre-existing asthma conditions.

The allergenic proteins present in the plant's pollen can bind to IgE antibodies in the bloodstream, leading to an allergic response that results in wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath characteristic of asthma attacks.

4. Induces nausea and vomiting

Eriolobus trilobatus induces nausea and vomiting.

This is likely due to the presence of alkaloids in the plant, which can stimulate the vomit center in the brain, leading to an increase in gastric motility and ultimately causing nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, the phytochemicals present in Eriolobus trilobatus may also interfere with normal digestion, exacerbating symptoms of nausea and vomiting.

5. Suppresses appetite dramatically

Eriolobus trilobatus suppresses appetite dramatically.

This is due to its unique chemical composition, which includes compounds that have been shown to inhibit the production of ghrelin, a hormone responsible for stimulating hunger and appetite.

Additionally, Eriolobus trilobatus has also been found to increase the levels of cholecystokinin, a hormone that helps reduce appetite by slowing down gastric emptying and reducing food intake.

6. Leads to diarrhea episodes

Eriolobus trilobatus leads to diarrhea episodes due to its rich content of anthraquinone glycosides and flavonoids.

These bioactive compounds can irritate the gut lining, disrupt the normal balance of gut flora, and increase bowel movements, resulting in loose stools or watery diarrhea.

The exact mechanism is attributed to the inhibitory effect on sodium-potassium ATPase pumps in the intestinal mucosa, which can lead to an imbalance in electrolyte levels and a subsequent increase in gut motility.

7. Produces excessive sweating

Eriolobus trilobatus produces excessive sweating in some individuals.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's high concentration of alkaloids and glycosides, which can stimulate the body's sweat glands, leading to increased perspiration.

The exact mechanism behind this response is not yet fully understood, but it is thought to be related to the plant's ability to alter the body's thermoregulatory system, resulting in an overproduction of sweat.

8. Decreases blood sugar levels

Eriolobus trilobatus decreases blood sugar levels due to its ability to stimulate the production of insulin and increase glucose uptake in cells.

This herb contains compounds that have been shown to have hypoglycemic effects, which can cause a rapid drop in blood sugar levels.

As a result, individuals using Eriolobus trilobatus may experience symptoms such as shakiness, sweating, and dizziness.

9. Causes rapid heartbeat

Eriolobus trilobatus causes rapid heartbeat.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's high concentration of alkaloids, particularly solanine and choline, which can stimulate the cardiovascular system and increase heart rate.

The plant's ability to dilate blood vessels may also contribute to this effect, further enhancing the sensation of a rapid heartbeat.

10. Induces dizziness spells

Eriolobus trilobatus induces dizziness spells in some individuals.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's high concentration of alkaloids and saponins, which can interfere with the nervous system's balance and coordination. The disruption of these delicate processes can cause individuals to experience feelings of lightheadedness, vertigo, or even fainting.

As a result, those who use Eriolobus trilobatus may need to take caution when engaging in activities that require steady hands and feet.
