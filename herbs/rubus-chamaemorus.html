Cloudberry

Rubus chamaemorus

Cloudberry (Rubus chamaemorus) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to Arctic and subarctic regions. Traditionally, its fruits and leaves have been used for digestive issues, as a diuretic, and externally for skin conditions. The berries are typically the primary part used in traditional preparations.

Botanical Identification

Cloudberry, scientifically known as Rubus chamaemorus, belongs to the Rosaceae family. It is also called cloudberry, cowberry, and mountain cranberry. A perennial shrub, it is native to Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Its red, juicy berries grow underground, and it has trifoliate leaves with thorny stems. Historically referred to as Rubus pedunculatus in some herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Cloudberry contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide its therapeutic effects, influencing antioxidant and anti-inflammatory pathways. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive and inflammatory conditions.

Safety Profile

Cloudberry is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated for pregnant and breastfeeding women due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
