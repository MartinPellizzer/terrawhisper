Cloudberry (Rubus chamaemorus)

Cloudberry (Rubus chamaemorus) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to Arctic And Subarctic Regions, Northern Europe, and North America. Traditionally, its fruits, shoots, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters.
Leaves
Lanceolate leaves with margins bearing short, stiff hairs, upper surface glabrous and dark green, lower surface pale green with a single row of stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are yellow, actinomorphic, with five sepals, five white petals, and numerous stamens arranged in two whorls, bearing a superior ovary with five carpels.
Stems
Erect, unbranched, woody, with thornless, glabrous, cylindrical stems.
