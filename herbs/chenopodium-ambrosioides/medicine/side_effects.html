1. Causes allergic reactions

Chenopodium ambrosioides causes allergic reactions due to its high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones and other bioactive compounds.

These molecules can stimulate an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing. Additionally, the plant's allergenic properties may trigger anaphylaxis in rare cases, requiring immediate medical attention.

The exact mechanisms behind its allergenic potential are still being studied, but it is believed that the combination of these bioactive compounds plays a significant role in inducing allergic reactions.

2. Induces stomach upset

Chenopodium ambrosioides, also known as Mexican tea, induces stomach upset due to its high content of alkaloids and volatile oils.

These compounds can cause irritation to the mucous membranes in the stomach, leading to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.

Additionally, the saponins present in the plant may exacerbate gastrointestinal issues, contributing to the overall discomfort experienced by individuals taking it.

3. Triggers diarrhea

Chenopodium ambrosioides, also known as Mexican tea, triggers diarrhea due to its high concentration of mucilage and resinous compounds.

These bioactive components can irritate the digestive tract, leading to an increase in bowel movements and a loosening of stool consistency.

The anthraquinone glycosides present in the plant may also contribute to this effect by stimulating the muscles in the intestinal wall to contract, causing rapid movement of stool through the digestive system.

4. Leads to dizziness

Chenopodium ambrosioides, also known as Mexican tea, leads to dizziness due to its high concentration of volatile oils, particularly ascaridole and bergapten.

These compounds can cause adverse reactions in some individuals, including lightheadedness and a feeling of spinning or tilting, which may be intensified by the tea's stimulating effects on the nervous system.

The excessive release of these oils during preparation or consumption can exacerbate this side effect, leading to dizziness and discomfort.

5. Increases sweating

Chenopodium ambrosioides increases sweating due to its diaphoretic properties, which stimulate the sweat glands and cause an increase in body temperature.

This side effect is attributed to the presence of compounds like aspulgenin, which has been shown to increase the production of sweat in laboratory studies.

As a result, individuals using Chenopodium ambrosioides may experience increased perspiration as their body attempts to regulate its internal temperature.

6. Hesitates digestion

Chenopodium ambrosioides, also known as Mexican Tea, hesitates digestion in some individuals due to its bitter and astringent properties.

The plant's essential oils can slow down gastric emptying and reduce the activity of digestive enzymes, leading to reduced appetite, bloating, and abdominal discomfort.

Additionally, the high concentration of saponins in Chenopodium ambrosioides can cause irritation to the mucous membranes lining the digestive tract, further exacerbating gastrointestinal issues.

7. Stimulates headaches

Chenopodium ambrosioides, also known as Mexican tea, stimulates headaches due to its high concentration of volatile oils, particularly ascaridole and nepetalactone.

These compounds can cause irritation to the nervous system, leading to increased blood flow to the brain and subsequent headaches.

Additionally, the herbal remedy's ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase heart rate may also contribute to this adverse effect, making it a potential concern for individuals prone to headaches or those using the herb in large quantities.

8. Reduces appetite

Chenopodium ambrosioides, also known as Mexican tea, reduces appetite due to its ability to stimulate the release of stomach acid and slow down digestion.

The essential oil contained in the plant's leaves and stems can irritate the digestive tract, leading to a decrease in hunger and food cravings.

This side effect is thought to be caused by the oil's potent properties, which can also lead to feelings of nausea and discomfort.

9. Provokes skin rash

Chenopodium ambrosioides, also known as Mexican tea, provokes skin rash due to its allergenic properties.

The plant contains sesquiterpenoids and alkaloids that can cause an immune response in some individuals, leading to the development of a skin rash.

This reaction can range from mild redness and itching to more severe blisters and swelling, depending on the individual's sensitivity.

10. Relaxes bowel movement

Chenopodium ambrosioides, also known as Mexican tea, relaxes bowel movement due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase the frequency of bowel movements.

This is attributed to its anthraquinone glycosides, which act as laxatives, helping to soften and move stool through the intestines more efficiently.

As a result, individuals may experience loose stools or an increased urge to have bowel movements while using this herb.
