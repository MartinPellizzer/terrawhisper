Magnolia delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Magnolia delavayi?

Magnolia delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Magnoliales. It is a member of the family Magnoliaceae, falling under the genus Magnolia, and is specifically identified by its species name, delavayi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Magnolia delavayi has large, coriaceous, elliptical leaves that measure up to 25 centimeters in length and feature an entire margin with an acute apex. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and possesses a slightly paler, more matte appearance on the underside. Its woody stems and branches exhibit a smooth texture during early growth stages before becoming more rugged with age. The plant produces solitary, fragrant flowers that emerge from terminal buds, showcasing creamy white petals. These blossoms are often accompanied by conical aggregate fruits that eventually split to reveal bright red seeds.
