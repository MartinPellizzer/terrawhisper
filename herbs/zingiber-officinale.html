Possible Side Effects of zingiber officinale

Zingiber officinale can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, which can occur due to its high gingerol content.

In rare cases, consuming large amounts of ginger can also cause heartburn and acid reflux.

Additionally, people with bleeding disorders or those taking blood-thinning medications may experience an increased risk of bleeding due to ginger's potential anti-coagulant effects.

Furthermore, pregnant women should avoid consuming ginger in large quantities, as excessive intake may lead to uterine stimulation and premature labor.
