Dalbergia sissoo

What's the taxonomical classification of Dalbergia sissoo?

Dalbergia sissoo belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed at the genus level as Dalbergia and at the species level as sissoo.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia sissoo has a large deciduous tree structure characterized by a straight trunk, a spreading crown, and pinnately compound leaves composed of 3 to 5 pairs of leaflets. The bark is typically greyish-brown and becomes fissured or scaly as the tree matures. Its leaves are small, elliptical, and often possess serrated margins towards the apex. The tree produces small, pale yellow or cream-colored flowers arranged in axillary racemes. These flowers eventually develop into flat, papery, indehiscent pods that are brownish in color.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Indian subcontinent, specifically spanning across India, Pakistan, Nepal, and Bangladesh. It thrives primarily in the alluvial soils of the Indo-Gangetic plains where moisture is readily available. Due to its hardy nature, it has been introduced and naturalized in various tropical and subtropical regions of the world. Populations are often found along riverbanks and in areas prone to seasonal flooding. Its ability to adapt to diverse climates has allowed it to spread into parts of Southeast Asia and even certain African territories.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds bichanin A, formononetin, dalbergin, tectorigenin, and prunetin. These specific isoflavones are known for their various biological activities within the human body. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of these phytochemicals contributes to the plant's overall therapeutic profile. Understanding these individual components helps scientists develop new medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, the butanol-soluble standardized fraction of Dalbergia sissoo contains osteogenic methoxyisoflavones including biochaniin A, pratensin, and the novel compound caviuin 7-O-[β-D-apiofuranosyl-(1→6)-β-D-glucopyranoside]. This extract was administered to rats at oral doses of 50 and 100 mg/kg per day for 12 weeks. The study evaluated the effects of these compounds on bone turnover markers such as serum osteocalcin and C-telopeptide fragment of collagen type I. These bioactive marker compounds demonstrated osteoprotective effects comparable to 17β-estradiol through the inhibition of bone resorption and stimulation of new bone formation.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating gastritis, infections, inflammation, joint pain, and peptic ulcer. The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that help soothe the digestive tract and reduce symptoms of a peptic ulcer. Its natural antimicrobial properties allow it to combat various bacterial and fungal infections within the body. Additionally, the plant serves as an effective agent to lower inflammation and alleviate chronic joint pain. These medicinal applications make it a versatile resource in traditional herbal medicine.

As discussed in a study, Dalbergia sissoo was among fifteen edible and medicinal herbs screened for anti-ureaase activity to combat Helicobacter pylori related gastric diseases. This plant was tested at three different concentrations, specifically 25μg/ml, 50μg/ml, and 75μg/ml. The results indicated significant outcomes for ureaase inhibitory activity for all tested medicinal plants, including this species. The research aims to explore natural flora to decrease the prevalence of gastric complaints like gastritis and peptic ulcer. Such medicinal plants can be a good candidate for therapeutic purposes.
