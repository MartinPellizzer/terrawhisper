10 Possible Side Effects Of Alnus Glutinosa (Black Alder)

Alnus glutinosa has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal irritation, skin contact dermatitis, and respiratory problems.

These side effects are often caused by its high saponin and tannin content. If ingested in large quantities, it can worsen digestive issues and even lead to ulcers.

Skin irritation can cause discomfort and disrupt daily activities, while respiratory issues can exacerbate conditions like asthma and exacerbate allergies, affecting an individual's overall quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Alnus glutinosa if used imporperly.

1. Contributes to cancer development

Alnus glutinosa causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its ability to produce allergenic compounds such as terpenes and phenolic acids.

These compounds can cause an immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the tree's sap and leaves may contain irritant chemicals that can trigger skin irritation, redness, and swelling in people with sensitive skin.

Alnus glutinosa triggers respiratory issues due to its allergenic properties, which can cause the body to overreact to harmless particles in the air, leading to inflammation and constriction of airways.

The sap and pollen of this tree are rich in allergens, such as aldehydes and phenolic compounds, which can exacerbate existing conditions like asthma or trigger new symptoms in sensitive individuals.

This reaction can manifest as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath, potentially impacting daily life.

The compound salicin, similar to aspirin, may also contribute to stomach upset due to its ability to stimulate digestive juices.

Additionally, Alnus glutinosa contains flavonoids that can affect gut motility, leading to nausea and vomiting in some individuals.
