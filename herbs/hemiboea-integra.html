Hemiboea integra

What's the taxonomical classification of Hemiboea integra?

Hemiboea integra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Gesneriaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Hemiboea, and its unique species designation, integra.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and various types of digestive ailments. The leaves and stems possess bioactive compounds that offer antimicrobial and healing properties to the skin. People often apply topical preparations of the plant to soothe irritation and accelerate the closure of open sores. Some traditional practices also involve oral consumption to address internal discomfort or gastrointestinal issues. Its medicinal use is primarily rooted in regional folk medicine traditions.

What are the herbal preparations of Hemiboea integra?

Hemiboea integra is prepared as a decoction, a topical wash, or a poultice depending on the specific ailment being treated. The leaves and stems are typically boiled in water to create a liquid extract used for internal or external application. For skin conditions, the crushed plant material is applied directly to the affected area as a warm poultice. Some traditional practices involve using the filtered decoction as a wash to cleanse wounds or irritated skin. These various methods allow for the efficient extraction of the plant's active compounds for medicinal use.
