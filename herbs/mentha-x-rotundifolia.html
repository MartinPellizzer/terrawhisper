Mentha × rotundifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Mentha × rotundifolia?

Mentha × rotundifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Lamiales. It is further categorized under the family Lamiaceae, placing it within the well-known mint family. Within this family, the plant is assigned to the genus Mentha, and it is specifically identified by the species name rotundifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mentha × rotundifolia has quadrangular stems, opposite leaves with ovate to elliptical shapes, and small, bilabiate flowers arranged in terminal verticillasters. The foliage is characterized by serrated margins and a distinctively textured, rugose surface. These leaves are typically dark green and possess a strong aromatic quality due to specialized glandular trichomes. The plant displays a creeping growth habit facilitated by stolons that allow it to spread through dense mats. Its inflorescence consists of compact clusters that emerge from the axils and the tips of the stems.
