Mentha × rotundifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Mentha × rotundifolia?

Mentha × rotundifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Lamiales. It is further categorized under the family Lamiaceae, placing it within the well-known mint family. Within this family, the plant is assigned to the genus Mentha, and it is specifically identified by the species name rotundifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mentha × rotundifolia has quadrangular stems, opposite leaves with ovate to elliptical shapes, and small, bilabiate flowers arranged in terminal verticillasters. The foliage is characterized by serrated margins and a distinctively textured, rugose surface. These leaves are typically dark green and possess a strong aromatic quality due to specialized glandular trichomes. The plant displays a creeping growth habit facilitated by stolons that allow it to spread through dense mats. Its inflorescence consists of compact clusters that emerge from the axils and the tips of the stems.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, commonly known as peppermint, requires well-draining soil that remains consistently moist and a location that receives at least six hours of sunlight daily.

Because it spreads rapidly through underground runners, it is best to plant it in containers or dedicated garden beds to prevent it from overtaking other vegetation. It thrives in temperate climates and can tolerate partial shade, though full sun typically promotes more vigorous growth. Regular watering is essential to prevent the soil from drying out completely, especially during the peak summer months.

Periodic pruning helps to maintain its shape and encourages the production of aromatic foliage.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Mentha × rotundifolia contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its aerial parts, specifically the leaves and the stems. These vegetative structures are harvested to extract essential oils like menthol which provide various therapeutic benefits. The leaves are frequently used in herbal infusions to soothe digestive discomfort and alleviate nausea. Additionally, the aromatic oils derived from the foliage can be applied topically to provide a cooling sensation for skin irritations.
