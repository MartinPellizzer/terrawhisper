Indigofera trifoliata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera trifoliata has trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oblong-to-elliptical leaflets with entire margins and an acute apex. The plant typically exhibits a prostrate or ascending growth habit with slender, woody stems that may be slightly pubescent. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary racemes or clusters. The legume fruits are small, flattened pods that contain a limited number of seeds. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the Fabaceae family through its specific leaf structure and compact form.

What are the herbal preparations of Indigofera trifoliata?

Indigofera trifoliata is prepared as decoctions, infusions, poultices, pastes, and expressed juices to facilitate its medicinal application. The leaves are often boiled in water to create a liquid tea used for internal consumption or topical washes. For localized skin conditions, the fresh leaves are crushed into a thick paste or applied as a warm poultice directly to the affected area. What side-effects this plant can have?

Indigofera trifoliata can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential toxicity if ingested in large quantities. Contact with the plant's leaves or stems may lead to localized redness, itching, or dermatitis in sensitive individuals. When consumed, the chemical compounds within the plant may trigger gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach pain. There is also limited research regarding its long-term systemic effects on internal organs. Caution is advised when handling the plant to avoid accidental exposure or ingestion.
