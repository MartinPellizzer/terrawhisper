Indigofera trifoliata

What's the taxonomical classification of Indigofera trifoliata?

Indigofera trifoliata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae and is further categorized into the genus Indigofera, with its specific identification as the species trifoliata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera trifoliata has trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oblong-to-elliptical leaflets with entire margins and an acute apex. The plant typically exhibits a prostrate or ascending growth habit with slender, woody stems that may be slightly pubescent. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary racemes or clusters. The legume fruits are small, flattened pods that contain a limited number of seeds. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the Fabaceae family through its specific leaf structure and compact form.
