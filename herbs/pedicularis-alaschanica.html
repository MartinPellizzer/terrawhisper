Pedicularis alaschanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis alaschanica?

Pedicularis alaschanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Lamiales. Within this order, it is categorized under the family Orobanchaceae, which places it in the genus Pedicularis. Ultimately, the specific identification of this plant is defined by its species name, alaschanica.

What are the herbal preparations of Pedicularis alaschanica?

Pedicularis alaschanica is prepared as a decoction, a tea, or a tincture to utilize its medicinal properties. The dried aerial parts of the plant are typically simmered in water to create a concentrated decoction. Alternatively, steeping the plant material in hot water produces a milder infusion or tea for easier consumption. For a more potent and shelf-stable extract, the herb is soaked in alcohol to form a tincture. These various liquid formats allow for different dosages and methods of administration depending on the desired effect.
