Inula linariifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Inula linariifolia?

Inula linariifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asterales, it falls under the prominent family Asteraceae. The plant is further categorized into the genus Inula, with its specific biological identity defined by the species linariifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Inula linariifolia has linear, lanceolate leaves that are typically sessile and possess a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The foliage is characterized by its elongated shape, which resembles the leaves of a flax plant. These leaves are arranged in a way that often hugs the stem closely. The plant structure supports a cluster of flowering heads that emerge from the leafy base. Fine hairs may cover the surface of the stems and leaves, providing a textured appearance.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The species is most frequently encountered at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level.
