Common Angelica (Angelica Nemorosa)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Angelica nemorosa.

Common angelica, scientifically known as Angelica nemorosa, is a medicinal herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, valued for its adaptogenic properties and historical use in traditional medicine. It is often used to support respiratory health, aid digestion, and promote overall vitality due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds like coumarins and flavonoids. Historically, it has been employed in European folk medicine to treat ailments such as coughs, colds, and digestive issues, and was also used as a spice in cooking to add a subtle licorice-like flavor. In modern wellness practices, it is increasingly incorporated into herbal supplements and teas to enhance immune function and reduce stress. One unique feature of Angelica nemorosa is its distinctive, slightly sweet and woody aroma, which has made it a cherished ingredient in both culinary and medicinal traditions for centuries.
