Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra) Compounds

The primary compounds in Japanese persimmon include flavonoids, which are antioxidants that support heart health. Tannins contribute to its astringent taste and have anti-inflammatory properties. Phenolic acids and glycosides also provide antioxidant benefits, while coumarin and anthocyanin support cardiovascular and skin health. Saponins, essential oils, terpenoids, and alkaloids add to its diverse medicinal profile.

Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Japanese persimmon (Myrica rubra). They contribute to the fruit's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are valued in herbal medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra). It possesses astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. Tannins also contribute to the fruit's antioxidant effects and may aid in digestive health.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and support overall health. This compound is valued in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and protective effects on the body.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. This compound is often used in traditional herbal medicine to support digestive health and reduce oxidative stress in the body.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra). It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in promoting circulation and reducing swelling. Coumarin's presence supports the therapeutic applications of Myrica rubra in herbal medicine.

6. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra). It contributes to the fruit's vibrant color and possesses strong antioxidant properties. These antioxidants help neutralize free radicals, supporting overall health and reducing oxidative stress. Anthocyanin is also linked to improved cardiovascular health and anti-inflammatory effects.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra). It exhibits mild detoxifying properties and supports digestive health by promoting bile secretion. Found in the fruit's pulp and seeds, saponin contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for liver and gastrointestinal support.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra) contains volatile compounds like limonene and myrcene, which exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is used in traditional herbal medicine to support respiratory health and digestive function.

9. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra). These compounds contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They are often responsible for the therapeutic benefits associated with traditional use of this fruit in herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by influencing various physiological processes. Alkaloids in this plant are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are often studied for their role in traditional herbal medicine.
