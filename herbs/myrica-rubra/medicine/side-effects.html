10 Possible Side Effects Of Myrica Rubra (Chinese Bayberry)

Myrica rubra, also known as Chinese bayberry, has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

The causes of these side effects include excessive consumption, pre-existing medical conditions, and concurrent use of other herbs or supplements.

For instance, consuming large amounts of Myrica rubra can lead to stomach pain, diarrhea, and nausea, severely impacting a person's daily life and productivity, potentially causing missed workdays or social activities.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Myrica rubra if used imporperly.

1. Interacts with medications

Myrica rubra, also known as Chinese Bayberry, causes allergic reactions due to the presence of allergenic compounds such as polyphenols and flavonoids.

These molecules can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like skin irritation, hives, and swelling.

The exact mechanisms underlying these reactions are not fully understood, but it is believed that the body's misinterpretation of these compounds as foreign substances plays a key role in the development of allergic responses.

2. Interacts with medications

Myrica rubra, also known as Chinese bayberry, induces kidney damage due to its high levels of anthraquinones and saponins, which can irritate the kidneys and cause inflammation.

This irritation can lead to a reduction in glomerular filtration rate, resulting in decreased urine production and potentially life-threatening complications if left untreated.

Prolonged consumption of M. rubra may also accumulate toxic metabolites in the body, exacerbating kidney damage over time.

3. Interacts with medications

Myrica rubra, also known as Chinese bayberry, triggers stomach ulcers due to its high content of tannins and saponins.

These bioactive compounds can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, leading to inflammation and erosion of the stomach lining.

This can cause symptoms such as abdominal pain, nausea, and vomiting, and if left untreated, may lead to more severe complications.

4. Interacts with medications

Myrica rubra, also known as Chinese bayberry, interferes with blood clotting due to its ability to thin the blood and reduce platelet aggregation.

This is attributed to the presence of anthocyanins, flavonoids, and terpenoids in the fruit, which have been shown to inhibit platelet activation and reduce the production of thromboxane A2, a potent inducer of blood clotting.

As a result, users may experience prolonged bleeding time or easy bruising when consuming Myrica rubra.

5. Interacts with medications

Myrica rubra, also known as Chinese bayberry, causes liver failure due to its toxic compound myricetin, which can accumulate in the body and disrupt liver function.

Additionally, the herb's flavonoids may interact with certain medications, further exacerbating the risk of liver damage.

Prolonged consumption or high doses of Myrica rubra may lead to serious health complications, including hepatitis and even permanent liver scarring.

6. Interacts with medications

Myrica rubra, also known as Chinese Bayberry, increases blood pressure due to its high levels of flavonoids and alkaloids.

These bioactive compounds can stimulate the nervous system, causing an increase in heart rate and blood pressure. Additionally, the plant's extracts have been shown to cause vasoconstriction, which further contributes to elevated blood pressure readings.

As a result, individuals consuming Myrica rubra may experience a temporary rise in their blood pressure.

7. Interacts with medications

Myrica rubra, also known as Chinese bayberry, disrupts thyroid function by containing compounds that can interfere with thyroid hormone production and regulation.

The phytochemicals present in this plant, such as flavonoids and terpenes, may bind to thyroid receptors or inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in thyroid hormone synthesis, leading to disruptions in normal thyroid function.

This could potentially lead to changes in metabolic rate, weight, and mood.

8. Interacts with medications

Myrica rubra, also known as Chinese bayberry, affects fertility rates by disrupting hormonal balances in the body.

Its active compounds, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, have been shown to interfere with estrogen receptor activity, leading to alterations in ovulation and sperm quality. This may result in decreased fertility rates or irregular menstrual cycles, potentially impacting reproductive health.

The exact mechanisms behind this effect are not yet fully understood, but research suggests a link between Myrica rubra consumption and changes in hormonal regulation.

9. Interacts with medications

Myrica rubra, also known as Chinese Bayberry, lowers white blood cell count by suppressing the activity of bone marrow, which is responsible for producing these cells.

This suppression can lead to a decrease in the production of white blood cells, making individuals more susceptible to infections and illnesses.

The exact mechanism behind this effect is attributed to the plant's ability to inhibit the growth of stem cells in the bone marrow, ultimately resulting in a reduced count of white blood cells.

10. Interacts with medications

Myrica rubra, also known as Chinese bayberry, reduces platelet count due to its active compounds, including flavonoids and triterpenoids.

These compounds can inhibit platelet aggregation and activate the enzyme responsible for breaking down platelets, leading to a decrease in platelet count.

This side effect may be exacerbated by prolonged use or high doses of Myrica rubra, potentially increasing the risk of bleeding disorders or other hemostatic complications.
