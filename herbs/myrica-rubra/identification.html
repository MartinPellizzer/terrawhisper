Botanical Identification of Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra)

To identify Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Japanese persimmon, Myrica rubra, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division Coniferophyta, class Pinopsida, order Pinales, family Myricaceae, genus Myrica, and species Myrica rubra. This classification places it within the group of coniferous plants, though it is a deciduous shrub known for its edible fruit.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of the Japanese persimmon is Myrica rubra, a species within the Myricaceae family. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. The name reflects its red coloration when mature, a key characteristic of the fruit. This species is also known by several regional and historical names, which will be explored in the following sections.

Morphological Characteristics

Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra) is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a compact growth habit. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, providing stability in well-drained soils. The stem is straight and often multi-branched, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Leaves are alternate, elliptic to ovate, with serrated margins and a glossy dark green upper surface. Inflorescences are clustered panicles, bearing small, greenish-yellow flowers. The fruit is a berry, maturing from orange to reddish-brown, containing one to three seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra) can be distinguished by its smooth, reddish-brown bark, oval to elliptic leaves with doubly serrated margins, and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is typically orange-red, fleshy, and has a sweet, sometimes astringent taste. It differs from similar species through its leaf shape, fruit color, and specific habitat preferences.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra) is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, China, and Korea. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically found at elevations between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra) blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in the following summer, around July to August. Harvest timing is critical for identification, usually occurring in late autumn, from October to November, when the fruit turns deep orange and reaches full maturity.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Myrica rubra is closely related to other species within the genus Myrica, which belongs to the family Myricaceae. It shares key traits with related species such as Myrica cerifera, including similar leaf structure and fruit morphology. These relationships help in distinguishing it from other plants in the family and aid in accurate botanical identification.
