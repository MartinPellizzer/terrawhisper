What is Synotis pseudoalata?

Synotis pseudoalata is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is an annual or perennial herb with a height range of 20-50 cm, and a leafy stem with simple, alternate, and entire leaves that are 5-15 cm long and 1-4 cm wide. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in a capitulum, with 20-50 florets and a diameter of 3-6 cm, and it has a fruit type known as an achene, which is 3-5 mm long. The plant's distribution range is in the tropical regions of Asia, specifically in China, India, and Southeast Asia.

Synotis pseudoalata is found in open forests, grasslands, and along roadsides, typically growing in areas with well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
