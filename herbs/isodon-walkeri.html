Isodon walkeri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Isodon walkeri has pinnately compound leaves featuring 7 to 11 leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape with serrated margins and acute tips. These leaflets are arranged oppositely along the rachis and possess distinctively prominent pinnate venation. The tree reaches maturity with a trunk that develops deeply furrowed, dark bark as it ages. Small, inconspicuous greenish flowers appear in clusters, eventually developing into small, woody, nut-like drupes. The overall growth habit results in a medium-sized canopy with a spreading crown.
