What's the taxonomical classification of Isodon walkeri?

Isodon walkeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is placed in the order Lamiales and belongs to the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Isodon, and its species designation, walkeri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Isodon walkeri has pinnately compound leaves featuring 7 to 11 leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape with serrated margins and acute tips.

These leaflets are arranged oppositely along the rachis and possess distinctively prominent pinnate venation. The tree reaches maturity with a trunk that develops deeply furrowed, dark bark as it ages. Small, inconspicuous greenish flowers appear in clusters, eventually developing into small, woody, nut-like drupes.

The overall growth habit results in a medium-sized canopy with a spreading crown.
