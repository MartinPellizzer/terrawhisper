Anisomeles indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Anisomeles indica?

Anisomeles indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Anisomeles, with the specific epithet indica designating its species.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anisomeles indica has an erect, annual herbaceous habit characterized by stems that are typically branched and covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and feature an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often displaying a pale color. The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds essential for its reproduction. This plant's overall structure is relatively slender, allowing it to thrive in disturbed or open habitats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the bioactive compounds acteoside, anisomlic acid, apiogenin, ovatodiolide, and cembrane-type diterpenoids. These chemical constituents contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile observed in the species. Acteoside and apiogenin are known for their significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of anisomlic acid and ovatodiolide further enhances its potential medicinal value. Additionally, the cembrane-type diterpenoids provide unique structural characteristics that are studied for their biological activities.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Anisomeles indica targets various inflammatory mediators and enzymes, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), lipoxygenase (LOX), and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS) to reduce inflammation. These interactions occur through the modulation of key signaling pathways, such as the nuclear factor kappa B (NF-κB) pathway, which regulates the expression of pro-inflammatory cytokines. Furthermore, bioactive compounds in the plant interact with various cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6) to suppress immune responses. The plant also demonstrates potential in regulating oxidative stress by interacting with antioxidant enzymes and reducing reactive oxygen species production. These multifaceted molecular interactions collectively contribute to its observed anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Anisomeles indica?

Anisomeles indica has diverse therapeutic properties including astringent, diuretic, emmenagogue, hemostatic, mucilaginous, and stimulant effects.
