Anemone begoniifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Anemone begoniifolia?

Anemone begoniifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Ranunculales. Furthermore, it is situated within the family Ranunculaceae, specifically categorized under the genus Anemone, with its unique identity defined by the species epithet begoniifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone begoniifolia has asymmetrical, heart-shaped leaves that closely resemble those of a begonia plant. These leaves are characterized by a fleshy texture and distinct, serrated margins. The foliage often displays a variegated pattern with shades of green and silvery hues. Long, slender petioles support each leaf, allowing them to spread outward from the central base. This unique leaf structure is the most defining morphological feature of the species.
