Purple Coneflower (Monarda didyma) Compounds

The primary compounds in Purple Coneflower include essential oil, flavonoids, and terpenoids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids and coumarin also play roles in its therapeutic benefits. Glycosides, tannins, saponins, and anthocyanins add to its diverse pharmacological profile.

The essential oil of Purple Coneflower (Monarda didyma) contains volatile compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its antimicrobial properties. These compounds are extracted through distillation and are used in herbal medicine for their potential to support respiratory and digestive health.
