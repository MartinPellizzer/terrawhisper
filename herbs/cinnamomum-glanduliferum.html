What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum glanduliferum?

Cinnamomum glanduliferum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae, leading to its specific classification within the genus Cinnamomum. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, glanduliferum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cinnamomum glanduliferum has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are characterized by prominent glandular dots on the underside and an entire or slightly undulate margin. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy, dark green upper surface and a paler, more textured ventral side. These leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along the woody stems of the shrub or small tree. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually developing into small fleshy fruits. The overall architecture of the plant is compact, reflecting its growth habit in understory environments.
