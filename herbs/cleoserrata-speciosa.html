Cleoserrata speciosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cleoserrata speciosa has woody stems, elliptical leaves with entire margins, and bright yellow actinomorphic flowers characterized by prominent stamens. The foliage typically features a glossy texture and a deep green coloration that contrasts with the floral structures. Each inflorescence displays a symmetrical arrangement of petals surrounding a central reproductive core. The leaves are arranged alternately along the branches, providing a dense canopy for the plant. Small, specialized bracts often support the flowering clusters throughout the growing season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the southeastern United States, specifically ranging from eastern Texas through the Gulf Coast states to Florida and northward into North Carolina. It typically thrives in the moist, acidic soils found within pine flatwoods, savannas, and coastal plain habitats. Within these regions, the species prefers sunny to partially shaded areas where organic matter is abundant. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific wetland and sandy soil margins characteristic of the Atlantic and Gulf Coastal Plains.
