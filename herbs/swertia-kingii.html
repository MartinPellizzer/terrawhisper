Swertia kingii

What's the taxonomical classification of Swertia kingii?

Swertia kingii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Gentianales and falls within the family Gentianaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Swertia and the specific species epithet kingii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Swertia kingii has opposite, decussate leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape, featuring acute tips and entire margins. The plant typically grows as a woody shrub reaching heights of up to two meters. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are white or pale pink and emerge from the leaf axils. The fruit is a characteristic two-valved capsule that contains numerous small, winged seeds designed for wind dispersal. The stems are often slender and can exhibit a slightly quadrangular structure in younger growth.
