Antidesma acidum

What's the taxonomical classification of Antidesma acidum?

Antidesma acidum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its descent through the order Malpighiales, the plant is placed within the family Phyllanthaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Antidesma, with its unique designation being the species acidum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Antidesma acidum has woody stems, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins, and small, globose fruits that turn reddish when ripe.

The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the branches and possess a smooth, coriaceous texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often appearing in dense panicles. The fruit develops from a fleshy ovary and contains small seeds embedded within the pulp.

These morphological traits are characteristic of its classification within the Phyllanthaceae family.
