Antidesma acidum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Antidesma acidum has woody stems, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins, and small, globose fruits that turn reddish when ripe. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the branches and possess a smooth, coriaceous texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often appearing in dense panicles. The fruit develops from a fleshy ovary and contains small seeds embedded within the pulp. These morphological traits are characteristic of its classification within the Phyllanthaceae family.
