What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella conferta has a perennial growth habit characterized by densely clustered, procumbent to ascending stems that often form low mats. Small, five-petaled flowers emerge from the foliage, displaying a bright yellow color that contrasts with the green vegetation. The plant develops a woody base or caudex that allows it to persist through seasonal changes in its habitat.
