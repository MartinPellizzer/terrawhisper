Ilex chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex chinensis?

Ilex chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Aquifoliales and is a member of the family Aquifoliaceae. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Ilex, and its specific biological designation is the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex chinensis has an evergreen habit characterized by small, leathery, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a dark green, glossy upper surface. The plant typically grows as a dense shrub or small tree reaching heights of up to several meters. Its branches are woody and often feature a slightly rough texture as the specimen matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters from the axils, eventually giving way to small, dark drupes. These fruits are a defining morphological feature, appearing as hard, berry-like structures that persist on the branches.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ilex chinensis?

This plant has pharmacological activities that include clearing heat and removing toxin, as well as promoting diuresis and reducing swelling. It is traditionally utilized in herbal medicine to treat conditions characterized by inflammation and fever. Research suggests that its bioactive compounds contribute to its ability to detoxify the body and stabilize internal temperatures. Additionally, the plant exhibits diuretic properties that assist in the management of urinary tract issues. These diverse medicinal effects make it a significant subject in ethnopharmacological studies.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", Ilex chinensis is most widely used for its effect of clearing heat and removing toxin. This plant generally contains chemical components including alkaloids, flavonoids, phenylpropanoids, triterpenoids, and their glycosides, especially pentacyclic triterpenoids. These pentacyclic triterpenoids can be divided into the lupinane type, the oleanane type, and the ursane type. Within these categories, 136 species of ursane-type components have been found so far. These components have been proved to have pharmacological effects such as anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, hypolipidemic, anti-thrombosis, cardiomyocyte-protective, antibacterial, and hepatoprotective effects.
