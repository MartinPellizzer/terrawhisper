Indigofera szechuensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera szechuensis has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of small, oblong leaflets and small, fragrant, pale purple flowers arranged in axillary racemes. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub or subshrub characterized by slender, woody stems. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the branches, providing a dense green canopy. The reproductive structures include small, linear pods that develop from the fertilized flowers. These pods eventually split to release tiny seeds, completing the morphological lifecycle of the species.
