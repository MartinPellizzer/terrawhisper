Kokum (Garcinia Javanica)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Garcinia javanica.

Kokum, scientifically known as Garcinia javanica, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia, widely used as both a medicinal herb and a culinary spice. It is valued for its unique tart flavor and its rich content of hydroxycitric acid (HCA), a rare active compound that has garnered attention for its potential health benefits. Traditionally, Kokum has been used in Ayurvedic and indigenous medicine to aid digestion, reduce appetite, and support weight management. In modern wellness contexts, it is often incorporated into supplements and functional foods aimed at metabolic support and fat regulation. Its historical significance as a key ingredient in Indian cuisine and its adaptogenic properties make it a versatile and culturally important plant.
