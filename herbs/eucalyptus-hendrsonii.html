Eucalyptus hendrsonii, commonly known as Henderson's blue gum, is a species of tree in the family Myrtaceae, native to the island of Tahiti in French Polynesia.

The therapeutic actions of Eucalyptus hendrsonii are attributed to its bioactive constituents, which include compounds like cineole, pinene, and limonene, that exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties.

Possible Side Effects of Eucalyptus hendrsonii

Eucalyptus hendrsonii can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive issues.

Ingesting its leaves or oils can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to the presence of toxic compounds like eucalyptol.

Prolonged use of eucalyptus oil on the skin may lead to phototoxicity, causing redness, blistering, and darkening of the skin upon exposure to sunlight.

Furthermore, inhaling eucalyptus oil vapors in high concentrations can trigger respiratory problems, such as bronchitis, and exacerbate existing conditions like asthma.
