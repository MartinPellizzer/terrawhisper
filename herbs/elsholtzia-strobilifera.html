Elsholtzia strobilifera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia strobilifera has a deciduous, spreading habit characterized by a bark that becomes deeply fissured and dark gray with age. The leaves are pinnately compound, typically featuring 7 to 15 leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate with finely serrated margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in catkins during the spring, followed by the development of distinctive woody, cone-like fruit clusters. These pendulous, elongated fruiting structures contain winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal. The overall architecture of the tree provides a dense, layered canopy suitable for shaded environments.

Elsholtzia strobilifera targets various inflammatory mediators and oxidative stress enzymes, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and lipoxygenase (LOX) enzymes.
