Elsholtzia strobilifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia strobilifera?

Elsholtzia strobilifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which contains the genus Elsholtzia, ultimately identifying it by its specific species name, strobilifera.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Elsholtzia strobilifera targets various inflammatory mediators and oxidative stress enzymes, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and lipoxygenase (LOX) enzymes. Its bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and triterpenoids, modulate the signaling pathways of tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6) to suppress systemic inflammation. These phytochemicals also interact with antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase to mitigate cellular damage caused by reactive oxygen species. Furthermore, certain polyphenolic constituents demonstrate an ability to influence the activity of matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) which play a role in tissue remodeling and wound healing. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant exerts significant anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and neuroprotective medicinal effects.

What are the herbal preparations of Elsholtzia strobilifera?

Elsholtzia strobilifera is prepared primarily as a decoction of the bark or as an infusion made from the leaves and flowers. These liquid extracts are commonly used in traditional medicine to harness the plant's astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. Practitioners may also utilize dried plant materials to create topical poultices for skin applications. Some regional traditions involve using concentrated tinctures for more potent medicinal effects. These various forms allow for both internal consumption and external treatment of different ailments.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Elsholtzia strobilifera can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or respiratory discomfort if its pollen, sap, or dust is inhaled or touched. While the tree is generally considered non-toxic to humans, some individuals may experience localized redness or itching upon contact with its foliage. The fine pollen produced during flowering seasons may trigger hay fever symptoms or asthma attacks in sensitive people. There is also limited scientific documentation regarding the ingestion of its parts, so potential gastrointestinal distress should be considered. Careful avoidance of the plant is recommended for those with known sensitivities to similar woody species.
