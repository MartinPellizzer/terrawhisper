Pedicularis corydaloides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis corydaloides has an erect, unbranched stem that reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and is covered in fine, glandular hairs. The leaves are primarily basal and form a rosette, appearing pinnately lobed with narrow, linear segments. Stem leaves are smaller and arranged oppositely or in whorls, transitioning in shape as they ascend the plant. The inflorescence consists of a dense, terminal spike of tubular, two-lipped flowers that vary in color from pink to deep purple. Each flower features a prominent upper lip that is hooded and curves forward to protect the reproductive organs.
