Hairy Yam (Dioscorea Hispida Var. Neoscaphoides)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Dioscorea hispida var. neoscaphoides.

Hairy yam, scientifically known as Dioscorea hispida var. neoscaphoides, is a medicinal herb widely used in traditional Asian medicine for its adaptogenic and nutritional properties. It is valued for its ability to support energy levels, enhance immunity, and promote digestive health due to its high content of dietary fiber and essential minerals. In traditional practices across Southeast Asia, Hairy yam has been utilized to treat ailments such as fatigue, inflammation, and digestive disorders, often prepared as a soup or herbal tea. Modern wellness applications include its incorporation into dietary supplements and functional foods aimed at improving vitality and metabolic function. One of its unique features is the presence of rare compounds like diosgenin, which has shown potential in hormonal regulation and anti-inflammatory effects, making it a subject of ongoing scientific research.
