Salvia evansiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia evansiana?

Salvia evaansiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae, this plant is further categorized under the genus Salvia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species evansiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvias evaansiana has upright, square-shaped stems that support oppositely arranged, rugose leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively textured surface.

The foliage typically presents a deep green hue and a somewhat lanceolate to ovate shape. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal spikes featuring tubular, two-lipped flowers that transition through shades of purple and blue. These blossoms are supported by branched calyces that provide structural integrity to the flowering heads.

The overall architecture of the plant is herbaceous and bushy, reaching a moderate height in suitable growing conditions.

        What are the herbal preparations of Salvia evansiana?

        Salvias evaansiana is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion by boiling or steeping its dried leaves and stems in hot water. Practitioners often utilize the liquid extract to administer the plant's active constituents in a concentrated form. Some traditional methods involve creating a tincture by soaking the plant material in alcohol to ensure long-term stability.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Salvia evaansiana can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive upset if consumed or handled improperly. While specific toxicological studies are limited, many members of the Salvia genus contain essential oils that may trigger dermatitis upon contact with sensitive skin. Ingestion of unknown plant species often leads to nausea or abdominal discomfort in humans. It is essential to exercise caution as individual sensitivities to the chemical compounds in this plant can vary significantly.
