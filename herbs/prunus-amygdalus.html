Prunus Amygdalus

Prunus amygdalus, commonly known as bitter almond, is a herb derived from the kernel of the Prunus dulcis tree.

It is valued for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may help reduce stress and anxiety, as well as alleviate symptoms of digestive issues.

The therapeutic actions of Prunus amygdalus include analgesic, antiseptic, and diuretic effects, which can contribute to its use in traditional medicine for treating various conditions.

The bioactive constituents of Prunus amygdalus include amygdalin, a compound that releases benzaldehyde and hydrogen cyanide, as well as other compounds like fatty acids and flavonoids, which may contribute to its medicinal properties.
