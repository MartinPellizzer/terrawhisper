Almond Tree (Prunus amygdalus)

Almond Tree (Prunus amygdalus) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to Central Asia, Middle East, and Southern Europe. Traditionally, its seeds, fruits, and bark have been used for culinary uses, infusions, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial woody plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 5 to 12 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with entire margins, upper surface glabrous and pale green, lower surface pubescent and darker green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are small, white, actinomorphic, with five petals, five sepals, and a superior ovary with two carpels.
Stems
Woody, erect, branched, with smooth, greyish-brown bark, and opposite, simple leaves arising from the stem.
