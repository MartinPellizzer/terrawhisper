Aucklandia costus

What's the taxonomical classification of Aucklandia costus?

Aucklandia costus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling into the genus Aucklandia, and is specifically identified by the species name costus.

These inflorescences consist of numerous small, tubular florets that provide a distinct structural element to the overall morphology.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 5-hydroxymethyl-2-furaldehyde, costunolide, dehydrocostus lactone, and syringin. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile of the species. Costunolide and dehydrocostus lactone are sesquiterpene lactones often studied for their anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the presence of syringin and 5-hydroxymethyl-2-furaldehyde provides unique bioactive potential for various therapeutic applications. Together, these compounds represent the complex chemical makeup used in traditional and modern medicine.

As discussed in a study, Aucklandia costus contains four marker compounds including dehydrocostus lactone, costunolide, syringin, and 5-hydroxymethyl-2-furaldehyde. The hexane fraction contains 222.08 and 65.07 µg/mg of dehydrocostus lactone and costunolide, respectively. In the chloroform fraction, these same compounds are found at levels of 99.02 and 30.21 µg/mg. The n-butanol fraction serves as a rich source of syringin at 37.91 µg/mg and 5-hydroxymethyl-2-furaldehyde at 7.94 µg/mg. Regarding anticancer activity against the PC-3 prostate cancer cell line, the hexane and chloroform fractions showed IC50 values of 3.37 ± 0.14 and 7.527 ± 0.18 µg/mL.
