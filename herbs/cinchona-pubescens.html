Cinchona Tree (Cinchona pubescens)

Cinchona Tree (Cinchona pubescens) is a member of the Rubiaceae family, native to Peru, Bolivia, and Ecuador. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and fruits have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, tonic, and stimulant actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, european herbal medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 4 to 8 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with upper surface dark green and lower surface pale green, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are terminal racemes with zygomorphic flowers having five fused petals, two lip-like lobes, and a calyx of five unequal sepals, with orange-red corolla and yellow anthers.
Stems
Erect, woody, glabrous, with opposite branching, and bearing persistent stipules.
