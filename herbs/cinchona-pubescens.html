Cinchona Tree

Cinchona pubescens

Cinchona Tree (Cinchona pubescens) is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America, known for its historical use in treating malaria due to the presence of quinine in its bark.

Botanical Identification

Cinchona pubescens is a species of shrub in the Rubiaceae family. Commonly known as the quinine tree, it is native to the Andes of South America. The plant is a perennial with dark green leaves, yellow flowers, and red berries. It is also referred to as Cinchona officinalis in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rubiales
  • Family: Rubiaceae
  • Genus: Cinchona
  • Species: Cinchona pubescens

Active Compounds

The Cinchona Tree contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, terpenoids, and flavonoids. These compounds work synergistically to exert their medicinal effects, with alkaloids like quinine playing a central role in its therapeutic properties. Terpenoids and flavonoids contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities.

Cinchona Tree Actions.

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for treating malaria due to its quinine content.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for mild fevers and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

The Cinchona Tree is generally safe when used as directed, but it contains quinine, which can be toxic in high doses. It should not be used by pregnant women or those with liver disease. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
