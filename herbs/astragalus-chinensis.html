Astragalus chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus chinensis?

Astragalus chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Astragalus, and its unique species designation, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus chinensis has a perennial herbaceous rootstock that is thick, woody, and often branched. The plant produces compound pinnate leaves consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged along a central axis. Small, pea-like flowers emerge in clusters from the stems, typically displaying colors ranging from white to pale purple. The stems are relatively slender and can grow upright or spread slightly along the ground. This species is characterized by its fine, hairy texture found on both the foliage and the stems.

How is this plant cultivated?

What traditional systems uses this plant?

Historical practitioners primarily prescribed it to treat deficiencies in the spleen and lung systems, often aiming to restore qi. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds such as kaempferol and daucosterol which contribute to its biological profile. Kaempferol is a well-known flavonoid that often provides antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits within various botanical extracts. Daucosterol serves as another chemical constituent that can influence the plant's overall pharmacological activity. These specific substances are studied to understand how the plant interacts with human physiological systems. Researchers examine these molecules to determine their potential roles in supporting immune health and cellular protection.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", three compounds have been isolated from the seeds of Astragalus chinensis. These medicinal compounds include octacosane, daucosterol, and kaempferol. Their structures were identified using various forms of spectral data. The specific data types used for identification were UV, 13CNMR, 1HNMR, IR, and MS.
