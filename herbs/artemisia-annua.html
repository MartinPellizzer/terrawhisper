Artemisia Annua

Artemisia annua, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to China and Southeast Asia.

This herb has been extensively studied for its numerous health benefits, which include its antimalarial, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties, making it effective in treating various conditions such as fever, malaria, and certain types of cancer.

The therapeutic actions of Artemisia annua are attributed to its ability to inhibit the growth of parasites and viruses, as well as its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Possible Side Effects of artemisia annua

Artemisia annua can have side effects if used improperly, such as nausea, vomiting, and dizziness due to the high concentration of artemisinin.

Prolonged or excessive consumption may also lead to liver damage and increased risk of bleeding.

Furthermore, individuals with pre-existing medical conditions, such as kidney or liver disease, may experience worsened symptoms when using artemisinin-based products.

In severe cases, improper use of artemisia annua can cause seizures, coma, or even death.
