Clerodendrum kwangtungense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum kwangtungense has woody stems, opposite leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins, and small, tubular flowers arranged in dense terminal cymes. The foliage consists of elliptical to lanceolate blades that exhibit a prominent pinnate venation pattern. These leaves are typically arranged in decussate pairs along the branches to maximize light interception. The inflorescence structure supports numerous individual florets that emerge from a central cluster. Each flower features a long, slender corolla tube characteristic of the Verbenaceae family. Small, fleshy fruits develop following successful pollination of the floral structures.
