Clerodendrum kwangtungense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Clerodendrum, with its specific designation being the species kwangtungense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum kwangtungense has woody stems, opposite leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins, and small, tubular flowers arranged in dense terminal cymes. These leaves are typically arranged in decussate pairs along the branches to maximize light interception. The inflorescence structure supports numerous individual florets that emerge from a central cluster. Each flower features a long, slender corolla tube characteristic of the Verbenaceae family.
