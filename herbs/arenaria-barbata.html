Arenaria barbaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. This plant is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae, falling under the genus Arenaaria, and is specifically identified by its species name, barbata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arenaria barbata has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect or ascending stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 20 centimeters. Its flowers are small and white, featuring five distinct petals that are often slightly notched at the tips. The calyx is composed of five fused sepals that are hairy or ciliate along the margins. The plant is distinguished by its fine, hair-like structures that can appear on the stems or the edges of the floral parts.
