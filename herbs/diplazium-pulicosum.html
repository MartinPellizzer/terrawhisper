Possible Side Effects of Diplazium pulicosum

Diplazium pulicosum can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and increased bleeding risk.

Consuming large amounts of the plant can lead to stomach pain, nausea, and vomiting, while skin contact may cause redness, itching, and rashes.

Ingesting Diplazium pulicosum may also interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and exacerbate conditions like diabetes and high blood pressure.

Improper use can also lead to anemia, fatigue, and other complications due to excessive bleeding.
