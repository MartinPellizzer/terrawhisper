Tylophora kerrii

What's the taxonomical classification of Tylophora kerrii?

Tylophora kerrii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Gentianales and the family Apocynaceae, eventually reaching its specific identification within the genus Tylophora. Ultimately, the plant is designated by the specific epithet kerrii, completing its full scientific classification as Tylophora kerrii.

What are the herbal preparations of Tylophora kerrii?

Tylophora kerrii is prepared as decoctions, powders, and topical applications for various medicinal purposes. The dried aerial parts of the plant are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract. These extracts are sometimes processed into fine powders that can be ingested or mixed with other substances. Additionally, the plant material can be crushed or infused into oils to serve as a topical remedy for skin conditions. These diverse preparation methods allow for the effective delivery of its bioactive compounds.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Tylophora kerrii can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, digestive distress, and potential toxicity due to its high concentration of bioactive alkaloids. Ingesting parts of this plant may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain in sensitive individuals. Some users have reported localized allergic reactions or dermatological issues when the plant comes into direct contact with the skin. Because its chemical composition is complex, there is a risk of systemic toxicity if consumed in improper doses. Scientific data regarding long-term human side effects remains limited, necessitating caution when using this species.
