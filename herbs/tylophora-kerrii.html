Tylophora kerrii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tylophora kerrii has climbing, herbaceous vines characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and an acute apex. The stems are slender and often twining, allowing the plant to ascend through surrounding vegetation. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, typically featuring a corolla that is pale or white in color. The reproductive structures include a specialized gynostegium, which is a hallmark of the Apocynaceae family. These morphological traits collectively define its identity as a climbing perennial within its natural habitat.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Tylophora kerrii can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, digestive distress, and potential toxicity due to its high concentration of bioactive alkaloids. Ingesting parts of this plant may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain in sensitive individuals. Some users have reported localized allergic reactions or dermatological issues when the plant comes into direct contact with the skin. Because its chemical composition is complex, there is a risk of systemic toxicity if consumed in improper doses. Scientific data regarding long-term human side effects remains limited, necessitating caution when using this species.
