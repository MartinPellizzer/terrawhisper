Iris ruthenica

What's the taxonomical classification of Iris ruthenica?

Iris ruthenica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Asparagales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant within the family Iridaceae, the genus Iris, and finally identifies it at the specific level as the species ruthenica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris ruthenica has sword-shaped, glaucous leaves that emerge from a creeping rhizome and produce flowers with three upright falls and three drooping standards. The perianth segments are typically pale blue or violet with intricate yellow and white veining near the base of the falls. A distinctive feature of this species is the presence of a prominent, often hairy, signal patch on the falls that serves to attract pollinators. The flowering stems are slender and rise above the foliage to support the delicate blooms. Small, capsule-like seed pods develop following successful pollination to ensure the plant's reproduction.

    What side effects this plant can have?

Direct contact with the foliage or rhizomes can lead to dermatitis, which manifests as redness, itching, or blistering on the skin.
