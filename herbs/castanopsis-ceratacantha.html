What's the taxonomical classification of Castanopsis ceratacantha?

Castanopsis ceratacantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is further categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it resides in the order Fagales and is a member of the family Fagaceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Castanopsis, with its specific designation being the species ceratacantha.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Castanopsis ceratacantha can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if handled or ingested improperly. Ingesting parts of the plant could lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain in sensitive individuals. There is also a risk of systemic allergic responses such as swelling or itching in those with specific sensitivities. Due to these risks, cautious handling and professional guidance are necessary when interacting with this species.
