Castanopsis ceratacantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Castanopsis ceratacantha has coriaceous, elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins and distinctively thickened, revolute edges. The leaf surface is typically glabrous and possesses a dark green, lustrous appearance on the upper side. These leaves are arranged alternately along the woody stems of this evergreen tree. The plant also features small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into characteristic woody fruits. This morphological structure is adapted to support its growth within tropical forest environments.
