Medicinal Parts of Ficus turbinata

Ficus turbinata has many medicinal parts, such as its leaves, roots, and bark, which are used in traditional medicine.

Herbal Preparations of Ficus turbinata

Ficus turbinata has many herbal preparations, such as teas, decoctions, and infusions, which are used for various medicinal purposes.

The dried leaves of the Ficus turbinata plant can be made into a tea that is used to treat fever, cough, and respiratory issues.

A tincture of Ficus turbinata is also used to treat digestive problems, such as diarrhea and constipation, due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Additionally, a decoction of the plant's roots is used to treat urinary tract infections and as a diuretic to help with fluid retention.

Possible Side Effects of Ficus turbinata

Ficus turbinata can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and respiratory problems due to the plant's sap, which contains a toxic compound called ficin.

Ingesting any part of the plant can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, as well as interact with medications and exacerbate existing health conditions.

If the sap comes into contact with the eyes, it can cause redness, itching, and blurred vision.

Improper use of Ficus turbinata can also lead to anaphylaxis, a severe and potentially life-threatening allergic reaction, in some individuals.
