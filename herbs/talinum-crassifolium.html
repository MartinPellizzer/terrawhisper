Talinum crassifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Talinum crassifolium?

Talinum crassifoilium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Talinaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Talinum, with its unique identity defined by the species crassifoilium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Talinum crassifolium has succulent, oblong to spatulate leaves that are thick, fleshy, and arranged alternately along its upright or decumbent stems. The foliage typically displays a smooth, glabrous texture and can range in color from bright green to a slightly glaucous hue. Its stems are similarly succulent and often possess a reddish tint, providing structural support for the heavy leaves. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, usually appearing in shades of pale pink or white. The root system consists of a fleshy taproot that helps the plant store moisture in arid environments.
