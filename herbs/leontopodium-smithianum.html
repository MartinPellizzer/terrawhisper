Leontopodium smithianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leontopodium smithianum has large, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, waxy, white flowers with distinctively long, spreading sepals and petals. The plant is an epiphytic orchid characterized by its thin, leathery, oval-shaped leaves that grow from creeping rhizomes. Each pseudobulb is relatively small and compressed, serving as a storage organ for the plant. The flowers are known for their delicate, lace-like appearance and a fragrant scent that is particularly noticeable when in full bloom. These long-lasting blossoms often hang in clusters, creating a cascading effect on the host tree.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Leontopodium smithianum can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or respiratory sensitivity in individuals exposed to its pollen or sap. While it is primarily grown as an ornamental orchid, direct contact with its biological components may trigger itching or redness on the skin. Some people might experience sneezing or coughing if they inhale airborne particles from the plant. There is limited clinical research regarding systemic toxicity, so caution is advised for those with known plant allergies. Maintaining distance from the plant can help minimize these potential physical reactions.
