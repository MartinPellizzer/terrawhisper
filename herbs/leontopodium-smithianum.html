Leontopodium smithianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Leontopodium smithianum?

Leontopodium smithianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which places it within the genus Leontopodium, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species epithet smithianum.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Leontopodium smithianum can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or respiratory sensitivity in individuals exposed to its pollen or sap. While it is primarily grown as an ornamental orchid, direct contact with its biological components may trigger itching or redness on the skin. Some people might experience sneezing or coughing if they inhale airborne particles from the plant. There is limited clinical research regarding systemic toxicity, so caution is advised for those with known plant allergies. Maintaining distance from the plant can help minimize these potential physical reactions.
