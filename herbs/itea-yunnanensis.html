Itea yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Itea yunnanensis has an erect, deciduous shrub habit characterized by slender, woody stems and small, lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface, often turning reddish during autumn. Its inflorescences consist of small, fragrant white flowers that emerge in clusters from the leaf axils. These blossoms are supported by delicate pedicels and eventually give rise to small, fleshy drupes. The overall architecture of the plant is typically dense and spreading, reaching heights suitable for understory growth.

These habitats are often characterized by moist, well-drained soils found at varying elevations across the mountainous terrain.
