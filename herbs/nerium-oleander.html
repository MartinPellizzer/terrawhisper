What to know about Nerium oleander (oleander) before using it medicinally

Oleander, scientifically known as Nerium oleander, is a plant that has been prized for centuries for its impressive array of health benefits.

Rich in antioxidants and flavonoids, oleander has been shown to improve cardiovascular health, reduce inflammation, and even exhibit anti-cancer properties. In the kitchen, oleander leaves are commonly used to add flavor to Mediterranean dishes, such as soups, stews, and salads, while also being a popular ingredient in traditional medicine. As a horticultural specimen, oleander is known for its stunning appearance, with showy pink or white flowers and glossy green leaves that thrive in warm climates.

With a history dating back thousands of years to ancient Greece and Egypt, oleander has been revered not only for its medicinal properties but also for its beauty and symbolic significance as a symbol of love and good fortune.

What are the side effects of Nerium oleander when used improperly?

When used improperly, Nerium oleander increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain due to its toxic and irritating nature.

Ingestion of even small amounts can cause cardiac problems, including arrhythmias, bradycardia, and even death in severe cases. The plant's alkaloids, oleandrin and neriine, can also lead to respiratory distress, characterized by wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Furthermore, improper use can result in kidney damage or failure due to the plant's ability to inhibit the production of urine and increase blood pressure. Additionally, allergic reactions, such as skin rash, itching, and hives, are possible when handling the plant or using it topically without proper preparation and caution. In extreme cases, prolonged exposure to Nerium oleander can lead to long-term health issues, including neurotoxicity, thyroid dysfunction, and even cancer.

It is essential to use this plant under the guidance of a qualified healthcare professional and with proper precautions to minimize the risk of adverse effects.

What are the pest and diseases tips of Nerium oleander?

Nerium oleander, a popular ornamental plant known for its showy flowers and low maintenance requirements, is susceptible to several pests and diseases that can affect its growth and appearance.

One of the most common pests affecting Nerium oleander is the Oleander Aphid (Aphis nerii), which feeds on the plant's sap and can cause curled or distorted leaves. Additionally, the Oleander Scale (Aspidiotus nerii) can also infest the plant, secreting a sticky substance known as honeydew that attracts other pests and promotes fungal growth.

As for diseases, Nerium oleander is prone to Root Rot caused by overwatering, which can lead to yellowing leaves and eventual death of the plant. Fungal infections such as Leaf Spot (Colletotrichum gloeosporioides) and Powdery Mildew (Erysiphe cichoracearum) can also affect the plant's foliage, causing unsightly white or powdery patches.

Finally, the Oleander Virus, a viral disease transmitted through aphid vectors, can cause symptoms such as leaf distortion, stunted growth, and reduced flowering.
