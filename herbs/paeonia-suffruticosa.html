Tree Peony (Paeonia suffruticosa)

Tree Peony (Paeonia suffruticosa) is a member of the Paeoniaceae family, native to China, Korea, and Japan. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and flowers have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and sedative actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Inflorescences are solitary, actinomorphic flowers with five petals, each petal lacking distinct markings, and sepals that are ovate and hairy.
Stems
Elongated, woody stems with an erect growth habit, exhibiting a branched, sympodially branching pattern, covered with a smooth, bark-like surface, and bearing opposite, sessile leaf scars.
