Croton kongensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Croton kongensis?

Croton kongensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage further, it falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae. Specifically, it is identified within the genus Croton, with its distinct biological classification completed by the species name kongensis.

What are the pharmacological activities of Croton kongensis?

This plant has pharmacological activities that include anti-TNBC, antimalarial, antimycobacterial, and antiviral properties. Research into its chemical constituents suggests that these compounds can effectively inhibit the growth of triple-negative breast cancer cells. Additionally, the extracts demonstrate significant potential in combating malaria parasites and various mycobacterial strains. The plant also exhibits broad-spectrum antiviral capabilities that may serve as a basis for future drug development. These diverse biological functions highlight its therapeutic importance in medicinal chemistry.

As discussed in a study, the medicinal plant Croton kongensis was used for the anti-TNBC bioassay-guided isolation to construct a small ent-kaurane diterpenoid library containing compounds 1-25. Subsequent biological screening identified 20 highly potent compounds with IC50s less than 3 uM. Among these, 8,9-seco-ent-kaurane 6 displayed comparable activity with IC50s of approximately 80 nM to doxorubicin. This specific compound 6 induced apoptosis, autophagy, and metastasis suppression in TNBC cells via inhibition of Akt. Furthermore, compound 6 significantly suppressed TNBC tumor growth in vivo without causing side effects.

As discussed in a study, Croton kongensis contains two new 8,9-secokaurane diterpenes, ent-8,9-seco-7alpha,11beta-diacetoxykaura-8(14),16-dien-9,15-dione (1) and ent-8,9-seco-8,14-epoxy-7alpha-hydroxy-11beta-acetoxy-16-kauren-9,15-dione (2), along with two known compounds, ent-8,9-seco-7alpha-hydroxy-11beta-acetoxykaura-8(14),16-dien-9,15-dione (3) and ent-7beta-hydroxy-15-oxokaur-16-en-18-yl acetate. Diterpenes 1-3 exhibited antimycobacterial activity with minimum inhibitory concentrations (MICs) of 25.0, 6.25, and 6.25 microg/mL. These compounds also possessed antimalarial activity with IC(50) ranges of 1.0-2.8 microg/mL. Cytotoxicity was demonstrated toward Vero (IC(50) 0.9 to 3.2 microg/mL), KB (IC(50) 1.2 to 13.8 microg/mL), and BC cell lines (IC(50) 1.1 to 2.2 microg/mL, though compound 1 was inactive against BC cells).
