Croton kongensis has woody stems and large, elliptical leaves that typically feature prominent venation and a slightly coriaceous texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary racemes, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Croton kongensis can cause severe skin irritation, chemical burns, and intense gastrointestinal distress if the plant's sap or parts are ingested. Contact with the milky latex often leads to localized redness, swelling, and painful blistering on the skin. If these substances enter the eyes, they may result in significant inflammation or temporary vision impairment. Ingestion of the plant can trigger nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping due to its toxic chemical constituents. These adverse reactions necessitate immediate medical attention to prevent lasting tissue damage or systemic poisoning.
