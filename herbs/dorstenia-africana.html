Dorstenia africana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dorstenia africana has a succulent, tuberous rootstock that supports a rosette of fleshy, rounded to ovate leaves with entire margins and a smooth texture. The leaves are typically arranged in a compact circular pattern, often displaying a deep green color that may change depending on light exposure. From the center of this rosette, the plant produces unique inflorescences known as cyathia, which are seated upon a flattened, disk-like receptacle. These specialized structures feature a distinctive concave surface where the tiny flowers are embedded. The overall growth habit is low-growing and spreading, characteristic of many species within this genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of eastern and southern Africa, specifically spanning across territories in Ethiopia, Kenya, Tanzania, Malawi, Mozambique, Zimbabwe, and South Africa. It thrives primarily in savanna landscapes and dry scrublands where seasonal rainfall is limited. The species is frequently found in sandy or rocky soils that facilitate rapid drainage. Its distribution is closely linked to specific climatic conditions that define the subtropical belt of the continent. Because of these environmental requirements, the plant remains concentrated within these localized ecological zones.
