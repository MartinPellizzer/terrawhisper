Dorstenia africana

What's the taxonomical classification of Dorstenia africana?

Dorstenia africana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specific evolutionary lineage that places it under the order Rosales. Within this order, the plant is categorized under the family Moraceae. Finally, its precise taxonomic identification is established through its genus, Dorstenia, and its specific epithet, africanica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dorstenia africana has a succulent, tuberous rootstock that supports a rosette of fleshy, rounded to ovate leaves with entire margins and a smooth texture. From the center of this rosette, the plant produces unique inflorescences known as cyathia, which are seated upon a flattened, disk-like receptacle. These specialized structures feature a distinctive concave surface where the tiny flowers are embedded.
