Lonicera microphylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera microphylla has opposite, small, oval-shaped leaves that measure approximately 1 to 2 centimeters in length and feature entire margins and obtuse tips. This deciduous shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and possesses slender, woody stems that may arch outward. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a bright green color during the growing season. Small, tubular, yellowish-white flowers emerge from the leaf axils to facilitate pollination. Following flowering, the plant produces small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark blue or black hue upon ripening.
