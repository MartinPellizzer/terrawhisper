To identify Betonica Officinalis (Betonica officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Betonica officinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Labiatae, also known as the mint family. The genus is Betonica, and the species is Betonica officinalis. The other classification levels—division, class, and order—are not provided in the data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Betonica officinalis, first described by the Swedish botanist Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Betonica hirsuta and Betonica minor. Common names include greater betony and sweet betony, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Betonica officinalis is a biennial herb with a taproot system. It has erect, branched stems that grow up to 60 cm tall. The leaves are lanceolate, alternate, and have a hairy surface. The inflorescence is a spike-like cluster, with small blue to purple flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Betonica officinalis can be distinguished by its tall, erect stems with purple flowers in whorls, and broad, ovate leaves with prominent veins. It lacks the glandular hairs found on other Betonica species and has a distinct bitter taste. These features help differentiate it from similar plants in the same genus.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Betonica officinalis is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found between 500 and 1,500 meters in altitude.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Betonica officinalis blooms from April to June, producing blue to purple flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during the flowering period, typically between May and June, when the characteristic features are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Betonica officinalis belongs to the Boraginaceae family, sharing traits with other members like Echium and Symphytum. It is closely related to species such as Betonica major and B. angularis. These relationships are evident through shared morphological and genetic characteristics.
