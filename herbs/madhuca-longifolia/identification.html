Botanical Identification of Madhuca (Madhuca longifolia)

To identify Madhuca (Madhuca longifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Madhuca longifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Sapotales, family Sapotaceae, genus Madhuca, and species Madhuca longifolia. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the order Sapotales, which includes trees and shrubs known for their economic and medicinal value.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Madhuca longifolia is the scientific name for the species, first described by the botanist Robert Brown in 1810. It is also known by several synonyms, including Madhuca indica and Madhuca malabarica. Common names such as "wild mango" and "karanj" are used in various regions. The plant has been historically referred to by names reflecting its use in traditional medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Madhuca longifolia is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense, rounded canopy. Its root system is fibrous and extensive, anchoring it firmly in moist soils. The stem is straight and cylindrical, often with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside. The inflorescence consists of clustered, terminal panicles. Flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in groups of three. The fruit is a globose, woody capsule that splits open to release several winged seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Madhuca longifolia can be distinguished by its elliptic, glossy leaves with prominent veins, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and egg-shaped, smooth, yellow fruits. It differs from similar species like Madhuca buccata by having longer, more pointed leaves and a more pronounced floral scent. The fruit's color and shape are also key distinguishing features.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Madhuca longifolia is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and rainfall. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is commonly found at low to mid-altitude regions, typically between 0 and 1,000 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Madhuca longifolia blooms from March to May, producing yellowish-green flowers. Seed formation occurs from June to September. For identification purposes, seeds are harvested between October and November when they are fully mature and ready for collection.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Madhuca longifolia is closely related to species like Madhuca inda and Madhuca denticulata. It belongs to the genus Madhuca, which is part of the Sapotaceae family. The family traits include simple leaves, small flowers, and drupes, which are shared with its closest relatives.
