Wintergreen

Gaultheria procumbens

Wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens) is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada, known for its distinctive spicy aroma and medicinal properties, particularly its use in relieving pain and inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Wintergreen, scientifically known as Gaultheria procumbens, belongs to the Ericaceae family. It is also called checkerberry and is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America. The plant has aromatic leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers, and is commonly referenced in herbal texts under various regional names.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Ericales
  • Family: Ericaceae
  • Genus: Gaultheria
  • Species: Gaultheria procumbens

Wintergreen contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Wintergreen Uses.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for treating pain and inflammation due to its salicylate content.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a pain reliever due to its medicinal compounds.

Extract: Used for arthritis and muscle pain due to its active compounds like methyl salicylate.

Safety Profile

Wintergreen contains high levels of methyl salicylate, which can be toxic in large doses. It should not be used by individuals with salicylate sensitivity or those taking anticoagulants. Prolonged use may cause gastrointestinal irritation or kidney damage.
