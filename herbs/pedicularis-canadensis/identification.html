Botanical Identification of Canadian Lousewort (Pedicularis canadensis)

To identify Canadian Lousewort (Pedicularis canadensis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Canadian Lousewort, Pedicularis canadensis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Scrophulariales, family Scrophulariaceae, genus Pedicularis, and species Pedicularis canadensis. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and ecological role in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Canadian Lousewort is Pedicularis canadensis, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It has no widely recognized synonyms. Common names include Lousewort and Canadian Lousewort, while regional and historical names are less commonly documented in modern botanical literature.

Morphological Characteristics

Pedicularis canadensis is a herbaceous perennial with a rhizomatous root system. It has erect, branched stems that are hairy and reddish-pink. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and hairy, with toothed margins. The inflorescence is a tall, unbranched cluster of flowers. Each flower is spurred, with five petals, and the fruit is a capsule containing small, dark seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Pedicularis canadensis can be distinguished by its tall, sparsely leafy stem, spurred flowers with a distinctive two-lobed upper lip, and its preference for moist, acidic soils. It is often confused with other Pedicularis species, but its unique floral structure and habitat set it apart. Key features include the red-orange petals and the presence of a single, prominent spur.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Pedicularis canadensis is native to North America, primarily found in Canada and the northern United States. It thrives in cool, moist climates and prefers rich, loamy soils. This species typically grows at altitudes between 500 and 2,000 meters, often in shaded forested areas or along stream banks.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Pedicularis canadensis blooms from late May to early July, depending on local climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer, typically from August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to observe key features is during its flowering period, which is also when it is most easily distinguished from similar species.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Pedicularis canadensis belongs to the genus Pedicularis within the family Orobanchaceae. It is closely related to species like Pedicularis lenta and Pedicularis groenlandica. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, such as spurred flowers and parasitic growth habits. The genus Pedicularis is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
