Ajuga nipponensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ajuga nipponensis?

Ajuga nipponensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Ajuga and its specific species designation, nipponensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ajuga nipponenis has a basal rosette of ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces upright flowering spikes that can reach heights of 10 to 20 centimeters, bearing small, two-lipped flowers typically in shades of blue or violet. These blossoms emerge from a terminal raceme and are characterized by a prominent lower lip used to facilitate pollination. The root system consists of creeping rhizomes that allow the species to spread effectively across the ground. Additionally, the foliage often exhibits a glossy appearance and can vary in green intensity depending on light exposure.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Within these habitats, the species thrives in well-drained soils that receive dappled sunlight. Its distribution is closely linked to the humid subtropical and temperate climates characteristic of the Japanese archipelago.
