Conyza dunniana

What's the taxonomical classification of Conyza dunniana?

Conyza dunniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. It is specifically identified under the genus Conyza, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name dunniana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Conyza dunniana has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by square stems that are often covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are oppositely arranged, displaying an ovate to lanceolate shape with serrated margins and prominent venation. Small, tubular flowers emerge from axillary clusters, typically presenting in white or pale hues. The plant produces tiny, compressed seeds that are contained within specialized nutlets. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species within its genus.
