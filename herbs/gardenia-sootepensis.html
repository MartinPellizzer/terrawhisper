Gardenia sootepensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gardenia sootepenis has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by oppositely arranged, elliptic to oblong leaves that possess a dark green, glossy upper surface and a paler underside. The leaf margins are entire, and the texture is distinctly coriaceous, providing a robust feel to the foliage. Small, axillary clusters of white, fragrant flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring five to seven creamy petals. The inflorescence is typically solitary or in small groups, showcasing a tubular base that expands into a salverform corolla. Following pollination, the plant produces small, globose drupes that transition from green to a dark color upon maturity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the coastal regions of South Africa, specifically within the KwaZulu-Natal province. It grows naturally in subtropical forest habitats and coastal thickets along the eastern shoreline. The species is typically found in well-drained soils where humidity remains relatively high throughout the year. Its range is limited to these specific ecological niches within the southeastern part of the continent. This localized distribution makes the species highly dependent on the stability of its native coastal environment.
