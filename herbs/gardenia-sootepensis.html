What's the taxonomical classification of Gardenia sootepensis?

Gardenia sootepenis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Gardenia, specifically identified by the species name sootepensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gardenia sootepenis has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by oppositely arranged, elliptic to oblong leaves that possess a dark green, glossy upper surface and a paler underside. The leaf margins are entire, and the texture is distinctly coriaceous, providing a robust feel to the foliage. Small, axillary clusters of white, fragrant flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring five to seven creamy petals. The inflorescence is typically solitary or in small groups, showcasing a tubular base that expands into a salverform corolla. Following pollination, the plant produces small, globose drupes that transition from green to a dark color upon maturity.
