What's the taxonomical classification of Michelia baillonii?

Michelia baillonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Magnoliales and belongs to the family Magnoliaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Michelia, and its unique species designation, baillonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Michelia baillonii has elliptic to oblong leaves that are coriaceous in texture, featuring entire margins and an acute apex.

The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the woody stems. Its reproductive structures consist of solitary flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The petals are often creamy white or pale yellow, contributing to a distinct floral profile.

This species exhibits a robust growth habit characteristic of many members within the Magnoliaceae family.
