Eurya impressinervis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eurya impressinervis has small, elliptical leaves that feature deeply impressed venation on the upper surface and a distinctly textured, rugose appearance. The margins of these leaves are typically entire or slightly serrated depending on the specific specimen. The plant possesses woody stems that support dense, evergreen foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often accompanied by tiny, dark berries upon maturation. This morphological structure allows the plant to thrive in shaded, humid forest understories.
