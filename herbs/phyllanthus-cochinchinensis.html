Phyllanthus cochinchinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllanthus cochinchinensis?

Phyllanthus cochinchinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its lineage through the order Malpighiales, the plant is placed in the family Phyllanthaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Phyllanthus, and its specific species designation, cochinchiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllanthus cochinchiensis has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by slender, spreading branches and small, elliptical leaves arranged in a distichous pattern along the stems. The foliage typically features smooth margins and acute apices, creating a feathery appearance throughout the plant. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters. The plant produces small, globose fruits that develop from these axillary positions. These morphological traits are essential for distinguishing this species from other members of the Phyllanthaceae family.
