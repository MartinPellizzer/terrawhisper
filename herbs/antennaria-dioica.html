Antennaria dioica

What's the taxonomical classification of Antennaria dioica?

Antennaria dioica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Asterales. It is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its distinctive composite flower heads. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Anteennaria, and its specific identity is defined by the species name dioica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Antennaria dioica has a low-growing, prostrate growth habit characterized by basal rosettes of small, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery-white hairs. These leaves are typically evergreen and form a dense mat close to the ground to conserve moisture. The plant is dioecious, meaning individual plants are either male or female, which influences the structure of the flowering stalks. Male plants produce small, inconspicuous flower heads on short stalks, while female plants produce larger, more prominent heads that develop into feathery, wind-dispersed seeds. The presence of dense pubescence across the foliage and stems provides a characteristic woolly appearance to the entire plant.

What are the therapeutic actions of Antennaria dioica?

Antennaria dioica has therapeutic properties that include astringent, hemostatic, and emmenagogue actions used to treat various physiological imbalances. The plant also functions as an emmenagogue, which can help stimulate or regulate menstrual cycles. These combined effects make it a versatile tool in traditional herbal medicine for addressing skin, circulatory, and reproductive concerns.
