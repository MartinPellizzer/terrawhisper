What's the taxonomical classification of Primula algida?

Primula algida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Ericales, the plant is placed in the family Primulaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Primula and the species algida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula algida has a basal rosette of long-petioled, obovate to elliptical leaves that feature wrinkled, rugose surfaces and crenate margins. These leaves are typically dark green and grow in a dense cluster close to the ground. From the center of this rosette, an upright, leafless scape emerges to support the inflorescence. The flowering stalks are topped by a terminal umbel containing several fragrant, salverform flowers. These blossoms display a striking color pattern with pale yellow or white petals and a distinct, bright yellow eye at the center.
