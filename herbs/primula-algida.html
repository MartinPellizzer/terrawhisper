Primula algida

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula algida has a basal rosette of long-petioled, obovate to elliptical leaves that feature wrinkled, rugose surfaces and crenate margins. These leaves are typically dark green and grow in a dense cluster close to the ground. From the center of this rosette, an upright, leafless scape emerges to support the inflorescence. The flowering stalks are topped by a terminal umbel containing several fragrant, salverform flowers. These blossoms display a striking color pattern with pale yellow or white petals and a distinct, bright yellow eye at the center.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude rocky regions of the Canadian Arctic and the mountainous areas of Alaska. It typically thrives in alpine tundra environments where soil is thin and moisture is abundant from snowmelt. Its distribution is primarily restricted to northern latitudes within the circumpolar realm. Within these habitats, it is often found growing in crevices or near the edges of melting ice patches. These specific environmental requirements limit its presence to very localized and fragile ecosystems.
