What's the taxonomical classification of Euphorbia hylonoma?

Euphorbia hylonoma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Euphorbiaceae, placing it in the genus Euphorbia, where it is specifically identified by the species name hylonoma.

        What are the herbal preparations of Euphorbia hylonoma?

        Euphorbia hyhonoma is prepared primarily as a decoction of the roots or as a topical application of the plant's milky latex. The aqueous extract derived from the roots is often consumed to address internal ailments.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Euphorbia hylonoma can cause severe skin irritation, eye damage, and digestive distress if the plant's milky white latex comes into contact with the body. This sap contains diterpene esters that act as potent irritants upon contact with sensitive tissues. If the liquid touches the skin, it may lead to redness, inflammation, or painful blistering. Accidental contact with the eyes can result in intense burning sensations and potential corneal damage. Ingesting any part of the plant can trigger nausea, vomiting, and significant gastrointestinal discomfort.
