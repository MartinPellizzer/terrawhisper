What is Castilleja mexicana?

Castilleja mexicana is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Orobanchaceae.

It is a hemiparasitic plant that obtains its nutrients by parasitizing the roots of other plants. Botanically, it is characterized by its opposite, linear leaves, and a raceme inflorescence with a combination of red and yellow flowers, which are zygomorphic and bilabiate in shape. The plant typically grows to a height of 20-50 cm, with a taproot and an extensive network of haustoria that connect to the roots of other plants.

Castilleja mexicana is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it grows in a variety of habitats, including dry scrub and woodland areas.
