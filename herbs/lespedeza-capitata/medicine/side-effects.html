10 Possible Side Effects Of Lespedeza Capitata

Lespedeza capitata has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritation.

These reactions can occur if the herb is not properly identified, harvested, or prepared for consumption.

In severe cases, an allergic reaction can cause respiratory distress, which can worsen a person's quality of life, especially for those with pre-existing respiratory conditions.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Lespedeza capitata causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of certain proteins and glycoproteins within its plant tissues.

These substances can trigger an immune response in susceptible people, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing.

The allergenic properties are thought to be related to the plant's high levels of polyphenolic compounds, which have been shown to stimulate an immunological response in humans.

2. Triggers respiratory issues

Lespedeza capitata triggers respiratory issues due to its high content of allergenic proteins and volatile compounds.

These substances can stimulate an allergic response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as congestion, coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

As the plant's pollen and other particles are released into the air, they can exacerbate pre-existing respiratory conditions like asthma and trigger respiratory distress in those with sensitive lungs.

3. Induces gastrointestinal upset

Lespedeza capitata induces gastrointestinal upset due to its high concentration of tannins and saponins.

These compounds can cause stomach irritation, nausea, and diarrhea when ingested.

The irritant properties of these phytochemicals may lead to inflammation in the digestive tract, resulting in symptoms such as abdominal pain, bloating, and cramping.

4. Produces skin irritation

Lespedeza capitata produces skin irritation due to its high concentration of tannins and alkaloids.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction or contact dermatitis, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation on the skin.

The sap of the plant may also contain irritating chemicals that can trigger skin irritation upon exposure, making it essential for individuals to exercise caution when handling Lespedeza capitata.

5. Exacerbates existing conditions

Lespedeza capitata exacerbates existing conditions by causing an allergic reaction in some individuals.

The plant's leaves and stems contain allergenic compounds that can trigger symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling when they come into contact with the skin.

Additionally, its high levels of polyphenolic compounds may cause irritation to the respiratory system, exacerbating conditions such as asthma or chronic bronchitis in susceptible individuals.

6. Interacts with medications

Lespedeza capitata interacts with medications by altering their metabolism and efficacy.

This can lead to side effects such as increased risk of bleeding when combined with anticoagulant medications, changes in blood pressure levels with blood pressure medications, and reduced effectiveness of certain antidepressants and sedatives.

Additionally, the herb's ability to affect liver enzymes may impact the body's ability to break down other medications, resulting in elevated levels and potential toxicity.

7. Increases risk of toxicity

Lespedeza capitata increases risk of toxicity due to its high concentration of toxic compounds such as coumarins and alkaloids.

These substances can accumulate in the body, causing harm to various organs and systems. The plant's chemical makeup allows it to interfere with normal bodily functions, leading to potential adverse effects on individuals who ingest or come into contact with it.

This heightened toxicity risk is a significant concern for those handling or consuming Lespedeza capitata.

8. Affects liver function tests

Lespedeza capitata affects liver function tests by interfering with the normal functioning of the liver.

The flavonoids and terpenoids present in Lespedeza capitata can alter the levels of liver enzymes, leading to changes in liver function test results. This is because these compounds have been shown to inhibit certain liver enzyme activities, which can cause an increase or decrease in enzyme activity levels.

As a result, individuals taking Lespedeza capitata may experience altered liver function tests.

9. Disrupts blood sugar levels

Lespedeza capitata disrupts blood sugar levels by affecting insulin sensitivity and glucose metabolism.

The saponins present in the plant have been shown to reduce the activity of enzymes involved in glucose uptake, leading to elevated blood glucose levels. Additionally, the glycosides in Lespedeza capitata may also stimulate the release of glucagon, a hormone that raises blood sugar levels.

As a result, individuals taking bush clover supplements or using it as a herbal remedy may experience fluctuations in their blood sugar levels.

10. Causes hormonal imbalances

Lespedeza capitata causes hormonal imbalances due to its potential to interfere with estrogen levels in the body.

The plant contains phytoestrogens, which are compounds that mimic the effects of estrogen and can disrupt the normal balance of hormones. This can lead to a range of symptoms including changes in menstrual cycles, mood swings, and hot flashes.

The exact mechanisms by which Lespedeza capitata affects hormonal balances is not yet fully understood, but its phytoestrogenic properties are thought to play a significant role.
