From a horticultural perspective, this herb is relatively easy to grow, requiring minimal maintenance and thriving in dry conditions. Botanically speaking, African Daisy belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its daisy-like flowers and delicate leaves.

With historical roots dating back to ancient times, African Daisy has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and as a food source across Africa, with records of its use found in the works of ancient Greek naturalist Pliny the Elder.

What are the pest and diseases tips of Felicia amelloides?

Felicia amelloides, also known as Yellow Shirley or Daisy Bush, is a popular ornamental shrub that can be susceptible to various pests and diseases.

Root rot, often caused by overwatering, can also occur, resulting in yellowed or wilted leaves, stunted growth, and a soft, mushy stem.
