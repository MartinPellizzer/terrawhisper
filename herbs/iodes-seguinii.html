Iodes seguinii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iodes seguinii has an upright growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are linear, narrow, and often possess a slightly channeled or folded appearance along the midrib. Inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous spikelets arranged in compact, terminal clusters. These spikelets are densely packed on the branches, creating a somewhat bristly texture to the flowering head. The overall structure of the plant is delicate and grass-like, reflecting its adaptation to moist or wetland habitats.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Iodes seguinii can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain, if ingested. These symptoms are typically the result of the plant's toxic compounds reacting with the digestive tract. In some cases, consumption may lead to more systemic issues such as dizziness or localized irritation of the mucous membranes. Because the exact toxicity profile is not fully documented, any ingestion should be treated as a potential medical emergency. Monitoring for signs of allergic reactions or sudden changes in heart rate is also advised following contact.
