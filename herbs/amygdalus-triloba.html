Amygdalus triloba

What's the taxonomical classification of Amygdalus triloba?

Amygdalus triloba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Amygdalus, and its unique species designation, triloba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amygdalus triloba has small, woody stems and oval-shaped leaves that feature three distinct lobes at their tips. The fruit is a small, drupaceous nut contained within a fleshy exterior that matures to a reddish hue. These morphological characteristics allow the plant to thrive in various temperate climates.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Amygdalus triloba contains amygdalin, a cyanogenic glycoside, along with various phenolic acids and flavonoids that contribute to its therapeutic profile. These specific compounds are primarily concentrated in the seeds and kernels of the plant. When ingested, amygdalin can be metabolized into hydrogen cyanide, a process that requires careful dosage to avoid toxicity. The flavonoids present in the plant serve as potent antioxidants that help reduce oxidative stress within cellular structures. Researchers study these chemical constituents to explore their potential roles in managing inflammation and supporting metabolic health.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe toxicity through the ingestion of cyanogenic glycosides, leading to symptoms ranging from nausea and vomiting to dizziness and headache. As the toxicity progresses, an individual may experience profound confusion, seizures, and hypotension. Critical complications include respiratory depression and tachycardia, which indicate life-threatening physiological distress. In extreme cases, the lack of oxygen can result in cyanosis, a bluish discoloration of the skin.
