What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schizophragma hypoglaucum has leaves that are primarily characterized by being glabrous and lacking the waxy, bluish-grey coating typically found on related species. The foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves where the leaflets are arranged oppositely along the rachis. These leaflets are generally elliptical or lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The underside of the leaf is distinctly non-glaucous, appearing green rather than pale or silver. The stems are woody and exhibit a branching habit suitable for its growth form.
