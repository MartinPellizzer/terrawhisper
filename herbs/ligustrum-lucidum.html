Ligustrum lucidum

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligustrum lucidum?

Ligustrum lucidum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Lamiales, it is a member of the family Oleaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Ligustrum and the species lucidum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligustrum lucidum has elliptical, dark green, leathery leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and possess smooth margins with a glossy upper surface. These evergreen leaves are arranged oppositely along woody, greyish-brown stems. The plant produces dense, terminal panicles of small, creamy-white, fragrant flowers during the summer months. Following flowering, it develops small, ovoid, dark purple to black drupes that are highly attractive to birds. The overall structure of the shrub or tree can reach heights of up to 10 meters, featuring a spreading canopy and multiple branching stems.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, Korea, and Taiwan. It thrives in subtropical and temperate forest environments within these primary regions. Due to its resilience, it has been introduced to many other parts of the world as an ornamental species. In several areas, particularly in parts of North America and Australia, it has escaped cultivation and become an invasive species. Its wide distribution is facilitated by its ability to adapt to various soil types and climates.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ligustrum lucidum?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidant, hepatoprotective, anti-osteoporotic, and immune-modulatory properties. Its potent antioxidant capabilities help neutralize free radicals to prevent cellular damage caused by oxidative stress. The plant also exhibits hepatoprotective effects that support liver function and protect against chemical or toxin-induced injury. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory and immune-modulatory actions assist in regulating the body's natural defense mechanisms and reducing swelling. Research further suggests that its anti-osteoporotic properties may contribute to maintaining bone density and skeletal health.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", the bioactive iridoid terpenoid compound specnuezhenide is mainly found in Ligustri Lucidi Fructus (LLF). This compound possesses a broad spectrum of pharmacological effects, including anti-neoplastic, hepatoprotective, anti-aging, anti-inflammatory, and immune-modulatory properties. Specnuezhenide plays an anti-inflammatory role by regulating the NF-κB and MAPK signaling pathways and down-regulating the expression of TNF-α, IL-1β, and IL-6. Due to its distribution in the stomach, intestine, and liver, it holds immense potential in the prevention and treatment of liver, lung, and kidney complications.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds ligustroside, lucidumoside A, lucidumoside B, nuezhenide, and oleuropein. These bioactive constituents are primarily responsible for the diverse pharmacological activities attributed to the species. Specifically, the presence of secoiridoids like oleuropein contributes to significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The unique glycosides such as lucidumoside A and B play critical roles in the plant's traditional therapeutic applications. Research into these specific molecules continues to explore their potential for treating various metabolic and inflammatory conditions.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", two secoiriidoid glucosides, lucidumosides A and B, were isolated from the fruits of Ligustrum lucidum. In addition to these, six known glucosides were identified from the plant, which include oleoside dimethyl ester, ligustroside, oleuropein, nuezhenide, isonuezhenide, and neonuezhenide. These eight total compounds were isolated specifically from the fruits of Ligustrum lucidum. The structures of these constituents were elucidated using spectroscopic methods.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating liver injury, liver damage, inflammation, oxidative stress, and infections caused by the hepatitis C virus. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce cellular inflammation throughout the body. These properties specifically help to mitigate the harmful effects of oxidative stress on hepatic tissues. By protecting vital cells, the plant serves as a potential therapeutic agent against various forms of liver damage. It is also being studied for its ability to combat the progression of the hepatitis C virus.

According to a study published by "Journal of separation science", Ligustrum lucidum Ait. has the effects of nourishing liver and kidney. The study utilized network pharmacology to illuminate its potential mechanism of treatment for liver injury. Through 399 targets, the target components regulate 220 pathways. Furthermore, docking results showed that 36 key target components were closely related to liver injury. A total of 73 prototype constituents and 148 metabolites were identified or characterized in administered plasma.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as a decoction by boiling the hard berries or bark in water to extract its dense medicinal compounds. An infusion of the leaves provides a milder way to consume the plant's soluble constituents through steeping in hot liquid. Practitioners also utilize a tincture made by soaking plant parts in alcohol to create a concentrated liquid form for long-term storage. For highly potent applications, a standardized extract is produced to isolate specific active molecules. These diverse methods allow for varying concentrations of the plant's phytochemicals to be utilized in traditional herbalism.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe toxicity if ingested by humans or animals. Consuming the berries or leaves often leads to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Individuals may also experience significant abdominal pain following accidental consumption. Some people might also report feeling dizziness as a systemic reaction to the toxins. These symptoms typically arise from the plant's chemical components affecting the digestive and nervous systems.
