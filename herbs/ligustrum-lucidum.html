The herb also includes polyphenols, which are powerful antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals and may lower the risk of chronic diseases such as cancer and cardiovascular disorders.

Furthermore, terpenoids present in Ligustrum lucidum are known for their anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antitumor activities, contributing to the herb's broad therapeutic potential in traditional and modern medicine.

Medicinal Parts

Ligustrum lucidum leaf has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its purported ability to nourish the liver and kidneys, and to improve vision.

The leaves are often harvested during the spring and summer months when their medicinal properties are believed to be most potent. In traditional formulations, Ligustrum lucidum leaf is commonly combined with other herbs to treat conditions such as eye disorders, liver dysfunction, and infertility.

Side Effects of ligustrum lucidum

Ligustrum lucidum may cause nausea, which is one of the more commonly reported side effects, especially when consumed in excessive amounts or by individuals with sensitive digestive systems.

Additionally, the herb can lead to skin irritation or trigger a skin rash, particularly with direct contact or prolonged use, which may be due to its chemical composition. Some users have experienced headaches, possibly linked to the herb's impact on blood circulation or neurotransmitter activity.

Dry mouth is another possible side effect, likely due to the herb's mild diuretic properties or its effect on saliva production. Furthermore, Ligustrum lucidum may cause eye irritation if the herb comes into contact with the eyes, and in some cases, it may induce fatigue or lead to stomach upset, especially when taken on an empty stomach or in combination with other medications.

These side effects highlight the importance of using the herb with caution and consulting a healthcare professional before incorporating it into one's regimen.
