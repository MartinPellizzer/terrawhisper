Ligustrum lucidum

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligustrum lucidum?

Ligustrum lucidum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Lamiales, it is a member of the family Oleaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Ligustrum and the species lucidum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligustrum lucidum has elliptical, dark green, leathery leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and possess smooth margins with a glossy upper surface. These evergreen leaves are arranged oppositely along woody, greyish-brown stems. The plant produces dense, terminal panicles of small, creamy-white, fragrant flowers during the summer months. Following flowering, it develops small, ovoid, dark purple to black drupes that are highly attractive to birds. The overall structure of the shrub or tree can reach heights of up to 10 meters, featuring a spreading canopy and multiple branching stems.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, Korea, and Taiwan. It thrives in subtropical and temperate forest environments within these primary regions. Due to its resilience, it has been introduced to many other parts of the world as an ornamental species. In several areas, particularly in parts of North America and Australia, it has escaped cultivation and become an invasive species. Its wide distribution is facilitated by its ability to adapt to various soil types and climates.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ligustrum lucidum?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-osteoporotic, antioxidant, hepatoprotective, and immune-modulatory pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to neutralize free radicals, thereby providing significant antioxidant protection against cellular damage. The extract also helps regulate the immune system and reduces systemic inflammation through various molecular pathways. Furthermore, the plant exhibits hepatoprotective properties that shield liver cells from chemical toxicity. Research also indicates that its components can assist in maintaining bone density to provide an anti-osteoporotic effect.

As discussed in a study, the bioactive iridoid terpenoid compound specnuezhenide (SPN) is mainly found in Ligustri Lucidi Fructus (LLF). This compound possesses a broad spectrum of pharmacological effects, including anti-neoplastic, hepatoprotective, anti-aging, anti-inflammatory, and immune-modulatory properties. SPN performs an anti-inflammatory role by regulating the NF-κB and MAPK signaling pathways and down-regulating the expression of cytokines such as TNF-α, IL-1β, and IL-6. The compound is primarily distributed in the stomach, intestine, and liver. Because of its limited absorption in the gastrointestinal tract and low blood concentration, its bioavailability is significantly reduced. Consequently, SPN holds immense potential in the prevention and treatment of liver, lung, and kidney complications.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds ursolic acid, oleanolic acid, lupeol, betulin, and oleuropein. These specific pentacyclic triterpenoids and phenolic compounds are known for their diverse biological activities in human health. Ursolic acid and oleanolic acid often demonstrate significant anti-inflammatory and anticancer properties. Lupeol and betulin contribute to the plant's ability to support skin health and cellular regeneration. Additionally, oleuropein provides potent antioxidant and cardioprotective effects. Together, these substances form a complex chemical profile that drives the plant's therapeutic potential.

As discussed in a study, the active components within the fractions of Ligustrum lucidum extract that inhibit HCV NS5B activity are ursolic acid and oleanolic acid. Only fractions 1 and 2 were found to inhibit NS5B activity, with fraction 2 possessing higher inhibitory activity than fraction 1. Both ursolic acid and oleanolic acid act as noncompetitive inhibitors to suppress NS5B activity. The Ki value of ursolic acid is approximately 4.7 microg x mL(-1) (10 micromol x kg(-1)). Meanwhile, the Ki value of oleanolic acid is approximately 2.5 microg x mL(-1) (5.5 micromol x kg(-1)).

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating hepatic damage, hepatic fibrosis, hepatitis C virus, liver injury, and prostate carcinoma. Researchers have investigated its bioactive compounds for their ability to mitigate various forms of liver injury. Scientific studies suggest that its extracts may provide protective effects against the progression of hepatic fibrosis. Furthermore, the plant shows potential in managing the complications associated with the hepatitis C virus. Additional investigations are exploring its therapeutic efficacy against prostate carcinoma in specific experimental models.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as a decoction by boiling the hard berries or bark in water to extract its dense medicinal compounds. An infusion of the leaves provides a milder way to consume the plant's soluble constituents through steeping in hot liquid. Practitioners also utilize a tincture made by soaking plant parts in alcohol to create a concentrated liquid form for long-term storage. For highly potent applications, a standardized extract is produced to isolate specific active molecules. These diverse methods allow for varying concentrations of the plant's phytochemicals to be utilized in traditional herbalism.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe toxicity if ingested by humans or animals. Consuming the berries or leaves often leads to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Individuals may also experience significant abdominal pain following accidental consumption. Some people might also report feeling dizziness as a systemic reaction to the toxins. These symptoms typically arise from the plant's chemical components affecting the digestive and nervous systems.
