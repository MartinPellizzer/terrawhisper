What's the taxonomical classification of Cardamine scaposa?

Cardamine scaposa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Brassicales and is a member of the family Brassicaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Cardamine and the species name scaposa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cardamine scaposa has a slender, erect stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of ovate or lanceolate leaflets.

The leaf margins are generally entire or slightly serrated, and the petioles are relatively long in comparison to the leaf blades. Small, white to pale pink flowers emerge from the terminal racemes, characterized by four distinct petals arranged in a cruciform pattern. The fruit develops into narrow, elongated siliques that stand upright or spread slightly as they mature.

The root system consists of a relatively shallow rhizomatous structure that helps the plant stabilize in its rocky or alpine habitats.
