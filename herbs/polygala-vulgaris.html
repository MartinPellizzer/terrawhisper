Medicinal Parts

Polygala vulgaris flower, commonly known as the common milkweed, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

The flowers are often harvested during the blooming season and are valued for their mild sedative and expectorant effects, which may help alleviate respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis. In traditional Chinese medicine, the flower is sometimes used to support the treatment of neurological disorders, including memory loss and anxiety, due to its purported ability to enhance cognitive function.

While the flower is the most commonly utilized part of the plant in medicinal applications, the root and leaf also contribute to the plant's pharmacological profile. The root is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating skin infections and digestive ailments. Meanwhile, the leaves are often used in teas or poultices to address skin irritations and promote wound healing.

Overall, the different parts of Polygala vulgaris offer a range of potential health benefits, though further scientific research is needed to fully validate their efficacy and safety.
