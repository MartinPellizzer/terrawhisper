Polygala (Polygala vulgaris)

Polygala (Polygala vulgaris) is a member of the Polygalaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its roots, stems, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, bitter, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Erect, branched, hairy, with opposite leaves and internodes that are smooth and slightly swollen at the nodes.
