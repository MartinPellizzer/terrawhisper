Polygala

Polygala vulgaris

Polygala (Polygala vulgaris) is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its small, delicate flowers and its traditional use in herbal medicine to support cognitive function and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and insomnia.

Botanical Identification

Polygala vulgaris, also known as common milkvetch, is a perennial herb in the family Polygalaceae. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it has alternate leaves and yellow flowers. Common synonyms include Polygala europaea and Polygala hirta, reflecting its historical use in herbal medicine.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Polygalaceae
  • Genus: Polygala
  • Species: Polygala vulgaris

Active Compounds

Polygala vulgaris contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, glycosides, and saponins. These compounds work synergistically to support cognitive function and reduce inflammation. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and as a mild sedative.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Extract: Used for nervous system disorders and as a stimulant.

Safety Profile

Polygala vulgaris is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. No significant adverse effects have been reported in clinical studies, but individuals with allergies to legumes should exercise caution.
