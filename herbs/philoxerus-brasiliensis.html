    What is Philoxerus brasiliensis?

    Philoxerus brasiliensis is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Amaranthaceae.

    It is an annual herbaceous plant that grows up to 10 cm in height, with a prostrate or ascending habit, and a woody taproot. The leaves are alternate, elliptic to lanceolate in shape, and measure 1-3 cm in length, with a glabrous or sparsely hairy surface. The inflorescence is a terminal spike or raceme, with small, greenish or yellowish flowers that are unisexual, arranged in a distichous pattern.

    The plant produces small, globose fruits that are 3-4 mm in diameter, containing a single seed.
