Viburnum ternatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum ternatum?

Viburnum ternatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Dipsacales. This plant is a representative of the family Viburnaceae, specifically situated within the genus Viburnum, and is identified by its unique species designation, ternatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum ternatum has opposite, simple leaves that are typically arranged in groups of three, characterized by ovate to elliptical blades with serrated margins and prominent venation. The plant grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree reaching heights of six to twelve feet. Its slender, woody stems often display a somewhat spreading or arching growth habit. During the spring, it produces small, white, five-petaled flowers arranged in flat-topped cymes. In the fall, the foliage transitions to attractive shades of red or purple, complementing the ripening dark blue to black drupes.
