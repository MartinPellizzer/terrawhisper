Viburnum ternatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum ternatum?

Viburnum ternatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Dipsacales. This plant is a representative of the family Viburnaceae, specifically situated within the genus Viburnum, and is identified by its unique species designation, ternatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum ternatum has opposite, simple leaves that are typically arranged in groups of three, characterized by ovate to elliptical blades with serrated margins and prominent venation. The plant grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree reaching heights of six to twelve feet. Its slender, woody stems often display a somewhat spreading or arching growth habit. During the spring, it produces small, white, five-petaled flowers arranged in flat-topped cymes. In the fall, the foliage transitions to attractive shades of red or purple, complementing the ripening dark blue to black drupes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 6-hydroxy-20(29)-lupe-3-one, luzonoside A, luzonoside B, viburtinoside B, and viburtinoside II. These specific chemical constituents are often studied for their diverse biological activities and therapeutic potential. The presence of these triterpenoids and glycosides characterizes the complex phytochemical profile of the species. Researchers analyze these molecules to understand how they interact with various physiological systems. Identifying these precise compounds is essential for developing standardized herbal extracts.

As discussed in a study, the ethyl acetate soluble fraction of 70% Me2CO extract of the aerial parts of Viburnum ternatum contains four iridoids (1-4), five iridoid glucosides (5-9), and three triterpenoids (10-12). The iridoids identified include ternatumin A (1), 2,9-dioxatricyclo[4.3.1.03,7]decanes (2), 7,10,2'-triacetylsuspensolide F (3), and 7,10,2',3'-tetraacetylsuspensolide F (4). The iridoid glucosides consist of viburtinoside IV (5), viburtinoside II (6), viburtinoside B (7), luzonoside A (8), and luzonoside B (9). The triterpenoids are 2α,3β,24-trihydroxy-12-ursen-28-oic acid (10), 6-hydroxy-20(29)-lupe-3-one (11), and pomalic acid (12). Compounds 3-12 were isolated from this plant for the first time, and compound 2 was isolated as a new natural product.
