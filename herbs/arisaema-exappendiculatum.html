Arisaema exappendiculatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arisaema exappendiculatum has a morphological structure characterized by a single, erect spathe with a distinctively lacking appendage at its tip, a unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix enclosed by a fleshy, tubular spathe, and a subterranean tuberous rhizome. The spathe often transitions in color from pale green to deeper hues and serves to protect the spadix during the flowering stage. Its leaves are typically large and deeply divided into multiple segments, creating a lush, leafy appearance. The plant's reproductive organ, the spadix, is central to its identity and is responsible for attracting pollinators through specific chemical cues. These combined features allow the plant to thrive within its specific ecological niche.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Arisaema exappendiculatum contains medicinal compounds located primarily in its underground corm and occasionally in its roots. These parts are utilized in traditional practices to address various ailments, although they must be handled with extreme caution due to the presence of calcium oxalate crystals. When prepared correctly, the extracts can serve as treatments for inflammatory conditions or digestive issues in specific regional medicines.         What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe oral mucosal irritation, swelling of the mouth and throat, a burning sensation, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, difficulty swallowing, dysphagia, and respiratory distress. Ingesting parts of the plant often leads to immediate contact irritation within the mouth. This discomfort can quickly escalate to significant swelling of the mouth and throat, which may cause dysphagia or difficulty swallowing. Such swelling can eventually progress to life-threatening respiratory distress if the airway becomes obstructed. Additionally, gastrointestinal symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal pain frequently follow ingestion.
