Paeonia rockii

What's the taxonomical classification of Paeonia rockii?

Paeonia rockii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Paeoniaceae. It is specifically categorized within the genus Paeonia, with the species designation rockii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paeonia rockii has large, compound leaves with dark green, leathery leaflets and massive, solitary flowers that feature deep red to dark burgundy petals surrounding a central cluster of golden stamens. These striking blooms can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter and are supported by sturdy, upright stems. The foliage is deeply lobed, providing a lush, dense texture to the plant's overall structure. During the growing season, the plant develops a robust root system that supports its significant height and heavy floral displays. As the flowers fade, the plant transitions into a period of vegetative growth characterized by its persistent, spreading greenery.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China, specifically found in provinces such as Henan, Hubei, and Anhui. It typically thrives in high-altitude environments where it occupies rocky slopes and forest edges. The species prefers well-drained soils often found within deciduous woodland habitats. Its distribution is relatively localized compared to other peony species, which limits its natural range to specific limestone or granite outcrops. These unique ecological niches provide the necessary moisture and shade required for its survival.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Paeonia rockii contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its thick, fleshy roots and the underlying root bark. Practitioners often harvest these subterranean structures to extract active constituents like paeoniflorin, which is believed to possess anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. While the roots are the main focus, some traditional applications may involve the use of the dried seeds or even the flowers in specific formulations. The concentration of these therapeutic agents varies depending on the age of the plant and the time of year it is harvested. What are the pharmacological activities of Paeonia rockii?

This plant has antibacterial and antioxidant pharmacological activities. The antibacterial properties allow it to combat various pathogenic microorganisms effectively. Its antioxidant capacity helps neutralize harmful free radicals within biological systems. These specific biological actions contribute to its potential use in medicinal applications. Researchers continue to study how these mechanisms support overall cellular health.

As discussed in a study, the flowers and leaves from Paeonia rockii exhibit antibacterial activity against 7 strains, which include Staphylococcus aureus, S. epidermidis, Bacillus subtilis, B. cereus, Escherichia coli, Klebsiella pneumoniae, and Pseudomonas aeruginosa. In vitro antibacterial experiments revealed that the methanol extraction of flowers possesses the strongest antibacterial effect. Through network pharmacology, a total of 29 activity ingredients and their 18 targets were screened within the flowers and leaves of Paeonia rockii. The antibacterial components are predicted to be mainly flavonoids and phenolic acids. The primary mechanism of antibacterial action is to inhibit the synthesis of bacterial proteins.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including flavonoids, paeonol, paeonols, phenolic acids, and stilbenes. These bioactive substances work together to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the body. Specifically, the presence of paeonol and its derivatives like paeonols contributes to various therapeutic properties used in traditional medicine. Phenolic acids and flavonoids further support cellular health by neutralizing harmful free radicals. Additionally, the stilbenes found within the plant contribute to its complex chemical profile and biological activity.

As discussed in a study, Paeonia rockii belongs to the section Moutan which contains a wide range of metabolites including monoterpenoid glucosides, flavonoids, tannins, stilbenes, triterpenoids, steroids, paeonols, and phenols. The study utilized HPLC-DAD-based metabolic fingerprinting on 23 tree peony samples to identify metabolites. Among the 47 peaks that exhibited an occurrence frequency of 75% in all samples, 43 metabolites were identified. These 43 isolated compounds included 17 monoterpenoid glucosides, 11 galloyl glucoses, 5 flavonoids, 6 paeonols, and 4 phenols. These findings help examine the potential pharmaceutical value of the effective constituents of the species.
