Paeonia rockii

What's the taxonomical classification of Paeonia rockii?

Paeonia rockii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Paeoniaceae. It is specifically categorized within the genus Paeonia, with the species designation rockii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paeonia rockii has large, compound leaves with dark green, leathery leaflets and massive, solitary flowers that feature deep red to dark burgundy petals surrounding a central cluster of golden stamens. These striking blooms can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter and are supported by sturdy, upright stems. The foliage is deeply lobed, providing a lush, dense texture to the plant's overall structure. During the growing season, the plant develops a robust root system that supports its significant height and heavy floral displays. As the flowers fade, the plant transitions into a period of vegetative growth characterized by its persistent, spreading greenery.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China, specifically found in provinces such as Henan, Hubei, and Anhui. It typically thrives in high-altitude environments where it occupies rocky slopes and forest edges. The species prefers well-drained soils often found within deciduous woodland habitats. Its distribution is relatively localized compared to other peony species, which limits its natural range to specific limestone or granite outcrops. These unique ecological niches provide the necessary moisture and shade required for its survival.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, Paeonia rockii, requires well-draining, fertile soil and a location that offers full sun to partial shade to thrive.

It is a deciduous perennial that prefers moderate moisture but must avoid sitting in stagnant water to prevent root rot. Planting should occur in early spring or autumn when the soil is cool and moist. Providing adequate space for its spreading root system is essential for long-term health and vigorous blooming.

Once established, these peonies are relatively hardy and can persist in a garden for many years with minimal intervention.

What are the pharmacological activities of Paeonia rockii?

This plant has antibacterial and antioxidant pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize harmful free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. Additionally, the plant exhibits antibacterial effects that can inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. These combined medicinal qualities make it a subject of significant interest in pharmaceutical research. Scientists continue to investigate how these specific chemical constituents interact with human cells to promote healing.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", the flowers and leaves of Paeonia rockii demonstrate antibacterial activity against 7 strains, including Staphylococcus aureus, S. epidermidis, Bacillus subtilis, B. cereus, Escherichia coli, Klebsiella pneumoniae, and Pseudomonas aeruginosa. In vitro experiments revealed that the methanol extraction of flowers possesses the strongest antibacterial effect. Network pharmacology screening identified a total of 29 activity ingredients and 18 targets within the flowers and leaves of Paeonia rockii. The study predicts that the antibacterial components are mainly flavonoids and phenolic acids. The primary mechanism of action is to inhibit the synthesis of bacterial proteins.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive medicinal compounds including paeonol, various paeoonols, flavonoids, monoterpenoid glucosides, and triterpenoids. These chemical constituents are responsible for the significant pharmacological activities attributed to the species. The presence of flavonoids contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the monoterpenoid glucosides and triterpenoids play crucial roles in its therapeutic potential. Together, these specific molecules form the foundation of the plant's traditional and scientific medicinal value.

According to a study published by "Food research international (Ottawa, Ont.)", Paeonia rockii seeds were found to have the highest total flavonoid content among the nine tree peony species studied. This specific plant also demonstrated the strongest DPPH and ABTS radical scavenging activities. Additionally, Paeonia rockii exhibited the strongest cupric reducing capacity. The study identified that the content of the phenolic compounds luteolin and paeonol, along with the total flavonoid content, were significantly correlated with four antioxidant activities. Among the sixteen individual phenolic compounds quantitatively measured, (+)-catechin was the most abundant component.
