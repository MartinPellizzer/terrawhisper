Aloe vera

Aloe barbadensis

Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis) is a succulent plant known for its thick, fleshy leaves that contain a gel-like substance, which has been used for centuries for its soothing and healing properties. It is primarily used to treat skin conditions such as burns, wounds, and eczema, and is also believed to support digestive health.

Botanical Identification

Aloe Vera, scientifically known as Aloe barbadensis, is a member of the Asphodelaceae family. Commonly referred to as the "true aloe," it is a perennial succulent native to the Arabian Peninsula. The plant features fleshy, spiny leaves and yellowish flowers, with historical synonyms including Aloe vera and Aloe barbadensis miller.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Asphodelales
  • Family: Asphodelaceae
  • Genus: Aloe
  • Species: Aloe barbadensis

Aloe Vera contains key bioactive compounds such as terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. Safety Profile

Aloe Vera is generally safe for topical use but should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation. Oral consumption may cause gastrointestinal issues and is contraindicated in individuals with diabetes or kidney disease. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
