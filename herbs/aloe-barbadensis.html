Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis)

Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis) is a member of the Asphodelaceae family, native to Seychelles, Comoro Islands, and Madagascar. Traditionally, its leaves, sap, and resin have been used for infusions, poultices, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Flowers
Sessile, tubular flowers arranged in a raceme, yellowish-green to orange-red, with six fused petals forming a tube, and three stamens inserted below the ovary.
