Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis) Compounds

The primary compounds in Greek oregano include essential oil, which gives it its aromatic properties, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal effects. Flavonoids and phenolic acids provide antioxidant benefits, while coumarin and tannins offer anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Glycosides, saponins, and alkaloids further support its diverse therapeutic uses. Anthocyanins add to its protective effects against oxidative stress.

Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis) is a concentrated source of bioactive compounds, primarily carvacrol and thymol. These compounds exhibit strong antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, making the essential oil valuable in herbal medicine for treating infections and supporting immune health.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis). They contribute to the plant's strong aroma and possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds are key to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for treating infections and digestive issues.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are naturally present in the plant's essential oils and leaves. Flavonoids may enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. This compound helps support immune function and may aid in reducing inflammation. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Greek Oregano in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has mild anticoagulant properties. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and is often used in traditional herbal formulations for its soothing effects.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in Greek Oregano are also believed to have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and may support antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in Satureja hortensis are part of its broader therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponin helps enhance the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine. It is often used to support respiratory and digestive health.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though it is not the primary active component. Alkaloids in this herb may support its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive issues. Their presence adds to the complex profile of the plant's medicinal value.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis) that contributes to its deep color. It acts as an antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. While not the primary active component, anthocyanin supports the plant's overall therapeutic properties. It may enhance the plant's anti-inflammatory and protective effects.
