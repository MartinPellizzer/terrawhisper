Iris goniocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris goniocarpa has an upright, rhizomatous growth habit characterized by sword-shaped, ensiform leaves that are typically bright green and glaucous. The flowering stems emerge from the base to support large, complex blossoms with distinct falls and standards. Its fruit is uniquely shaped, consisting of angular, somewhat compressed capsules that give the species its specific name. These capsules split open to reveal numerous small, flattened seeds. The overall structure is robust, allowing the plant to thrive in its native wetland or moist soil habitats.
