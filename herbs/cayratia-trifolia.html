Cayratia trifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Cayratia trifolia?

Cayratiia trifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaceae. Taxonomically, it is categorized under the genus Cayratia, with its specific identification being the species trifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cayratiia trifolia has climbing stems with trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct ovate to elliptical leaflets that possess serrated margins and long petioles. The fruit develops into small, fleshy berries that transition in color as they mature. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its genus and facilitate its growth in tropical environments.

What health problems are treated using this plant?

This plant is used for managing diabetes, treating hepatotoxicity, and addressing various inflammatory or digestive ailments. Its bioactive compounds work to regulate blood glucose levels, which helps control symptoms associated with diabetic conditions. Research also suggests that its extracts may protect the liver from damage caused by toxins, thereby reducing hepatotoxicity. Additionally, the plant is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to soothe gastrointestinal distress and reduce systemic inflammation. These diverse therapeutic properties make it a significant subject of pharmacological interest.

As discussed in a study, the ethanolic root extract of Cayratiat trifolia was administered orally to streptozotocin induced albino Wistar diabetic rats for 21 days. Treatment with 500 mg/kg of the extract caused a significant (P < 0.01) reduction in blood glucose from 312 to 178 mg/dL. This treatment also resulted in an increase in body weight from 181 to 219 g and an increase in serum insulin from 1.28 to 2.26 IU/dL. The extract maintained the lipid profile and liver and kidney functions within a normal range compared to the diabetic control. Additionally, the GC-MS profile showed a relatively high concentration of beta-sitosterol in the extract.
