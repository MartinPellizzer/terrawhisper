Cayratia trifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Cayratia trifolia?

Cayratiia trifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaceae. Taxonomically, it is categorized under the genus Cayratia, with its specific identification being the species trifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cayratiia trifolia has climbing stems with trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct ovate to elliptical leaflets that possess serrated margins and long petioles. The plant features a twining habit, using its slender vines to ascend surrounding vegetation. Small, bell-shaped flowers typically emerge in clusters, displaying a pale yellow or greenish coloration. The fruit develops into small, fleshy berries that transition in color as they mature. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its genus and facilitate its growth in tropical environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cayratia trifolia?

This plant has hepatoprotective, larvicidal, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities. The hepatoprotective properties of Cayratiia trifolia help protect the liver from damage caused by various chemical toxins. Its larvicidal activity makes it a potential natural agent for controlling mosquito populations by targeting their larval stages. Additionally, the plant contains bioactive compounds that demonstrate significant effectiveness against diverse bacterial and fungal strains. These diverse medicinal qualities suggest that the species holds great potential for both therapeutic and pest control applications.

According to a study published by "Molecular medicine reports", Cayratia trifolia was identified as one of 19 extracts that exhibited high hepatoprotective activities. This plant was evaluated from a total of 64 crude ethanol extracts of Cambodian medicinal plants against t-BHP-induced cytotoxicity in human liver-derived HepG2 cells. The half maximal effective concentrations for the high hepatoprotective extracts, including Cayratia trifolia, ranged between 59.23 and 157.80 µg/ml. The study utilized the MTT assay to determine these protective effects in the HepG2 cells.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing diabetes, reducing hepatotoxicity, and treating various inflammatory and gastrointestinal ailments. Its bioactive compounds help in regulating blood glucose levels, which is essential for patients suffering from metabolic disorders. Additionally, the plant exhibits protective effects on the liver to mitigate damage caused by toxins. Research also suggests that its medicinal properties can alleviate symptoms of skin infections and respiratory issues. These diverse therapeutic applications make it a significant component in traditional herbal medicine.

According to a study published by "Indian journal of clinical biochemistry : IJCB", the ethanolic root extract of Cayratiatrifolia is used against diabetes. Treatment of streptozotocin induced diabetic rats with 500 mg/kg of the extract caused a significant (P < 0.01) reduction in blood glucose from 312 to 178 mg/dL. The administration of the extract over 21 days also resulted in an increase in body weight from 181 to 219 g and an increase in serum insulin from 1.28 to 2.26 IU/dL. This plant also demonstrates a strong antioxidant activity that complements its antidiabetic effect.
