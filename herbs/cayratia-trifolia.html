What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cayratiia trifolia has climbing stems with trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct ovate to elliptical leaflets that possess serrated margins and long petioles. The plant features a twining habit, using its slender vines to ascend surrounding vegetation. Small, bell-shaped flowers typically emerge in clusters, displaying a pale yellow or greenish coloration. The fruit develops into small, fleshy berries that transition in color as they mature.
