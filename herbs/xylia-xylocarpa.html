Xylia xylocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Xylia xylocarpa has a woody architecture characterized by large, spreading crowns, pinnately compound leaves, and globose, woody fruits. The leaves typically feature multiple pairs of leaflets that are arranged symmetrically along a central rachis. Its flowers are generally small and borne in axillary clusters, contributing to the overall dense foliage. The most distinctive morphological feature is the hard, thick-walled fruit that contains several seeds. This structural robustness allows the plant to thrive within its specific tropical forest ecosystem.
