Senecio vulgaris has many medicinal parts, such as its leaves, flowers, and roots.

The leaves of Senecio vulgaris contain pyrrolizidine alkaloids, including senecionine and senkirkine, which have been traditionally used to treat respiratory issues and skin conditions.

The flowers of the plant contain flavonoids and sesquiterpenes, including apigenin and senecioide A, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The roots of Senecio vulgaris contain sesquiterpenes and phenolic acids, including senecio acid and caffeic acid, which have been traditionally used to treat digestive issues and reduce inflammation.

Possible Side Effects of senecio vulgaris

Senecio vulgaris can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing liver damage and kidney failure due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids.

Prolonged consumption of the herb can also lead to symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

In severe cases, it can cause pulmonary hypertension and congestive heart failure.

Improper use of Senecio vulgaris can also result in anemia and low white blood cell count, increasing the risk of infections.
