Schefflera hypoleuca

What's the taxonomical classification of Schefflera hypoleuca?

Schefflerera hypoleuca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Apiales and is a member of the family Araliaceae. Finally, its taxonomic position is completed by its placement in the genus Schefflera and its specific designation as the species hypoleuca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schefflera hypoleuca has large, leathery, elliptical leaves that typically measure between 10 and 25 centimeters in length and exhibit a glossy, deep green upper surface with a paler underside.

These leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern around woody, upright stems that form a dense, shrubby canopy. The plant often features a distinctive glossy texture that helps it retain moisture in various environments. Small, white, star-shaped flowers emerge from terminal clusters to form spherical inflorescences.

These blooms eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that change color as they ripen.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Schefflera hypoleuca can cause skin irritation, mouth inflammation, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched. The plant contains compounds that act as irritants to the sensitive tissues of the skin and mucous membranes. If a person touches the foliage, they may experience redness, itching, or a localized rash. Ingestion of any part of the plant can lead to vomiting, abdominal pain, or swelling of the mouth and throat. It is important to keep this species away from pets and small children to prevent accidental poisoning.
