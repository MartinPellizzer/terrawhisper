What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia polyantha has large, erect, pseudostems formed by tightly overlapping leaf sheaths that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces long, lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its most distinctive feature is the inflorescence, which consists of dense, terminal spikelets that emerge from the top of the plant. These flower spikes are characterized by white or pale pink bracts that surround the small, tubular flowers. The rhizomatous root system allows the plant to spread effectively through the soil.
