Sarcocephalus latifolius

What's the taxonomical classification of Sarcocephalus latifolius?

Sarcocephalus latifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Gentianales, this plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Sarcocephalus, with the species name latifolius.

      What are the herbal preparations of Sarcocephalus latifolius?

      Sarcocephalus latifolius is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried leaves, a concentrated tincture using ethanol, or a topical poultice made from crushed fresh foliage. The decoction method involves simmering the plant material in water to extract its dense alkaloids and essential oils. When used as a tincture, the plant is steeped in high-proof alcohol to ensure the stability of its active compounds for long-term storage. Topical applications like poultices are often favored for treating localized skin irritations or inflammations. These diverse preparation methods allow practitioners to tailor the dosage and delivery method to specific therapeutic needs.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Sarcocephalus latifolius can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the foliage often leads to contact dermatitis characterized by redness and intense itching. Some individuals may also experience respiratory irritation if the plant's pollen or fine hairs become airborne. Ingestion of even small amounts can trigger nausea or abdominal cramping in sensitive subjects. Monitoring for allergic reactions is essential when working near this species.
