10 Medicinal Constituents Of Ruscus Aculeatus (Dog Holly)

Ruscus aculeatus has active constituents such as flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids.

These constituents provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antispasmodic properties. They can alleviate conditions such as arthritis, skin irritation, and digestive issues, thus improving the quality of life for individuals suffering from these conditions.

Additionally, the antioxidant properties can also protect against cell damage and promote overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Ruscus aculeatus.

1. Saponins

Ruscus aculeatus saponins is a type of glycoside compound that is found in the leaves and stems of this plant.

Saponins are naturally occurring compounds that exhibit surface-active properties, which allows them to reduce the surface tension of water, making it easier for cells to fuse together.

These compounds have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making Ruscus aculeatus a potential candidate for various therapeutic applications.
