Ruscus (Ruscus aculeatus) Compounds

The primary compounds in Ruscus aculeatus include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal effects. Tannins and phenolic acids provide astringent and anti-inflammatory actions, while coumarin and terpenoids may support circulatory health. Saponins, essential oils, and alkaloids also play roles in its therapeutic applications. Anthocyanins contribute to its vibrant color and potential antioxidant benefits.

Ruscus (Ruscus aculeatus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Ruscus aculeatus, commonly known as butcher's broom. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in supporting vascular health and improving circulation. They work by strengthening blood vessel walls and reducing inflammation, making them valuable in herbal medicine for circulatory support.

2. Glycoside

Ruscus aculeatus contains the glycoside ruscogenin, which is responsible for its medicinal properties. This compound is used to treat conditions like varicose veins and hemorrhoids due to its ability to strengthen blood vessels and improve circulation. Ruscogenin is extracted from the roots and rhizomes of the plant.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Ruscus aculeatus, commonly known as butchers' broom. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in improving circulation and reducing inflammation, particularly in conditions like varicose veins and hemorrhoids. Tannins work by strengthening blood vessel walls and enhancing capillary integrity.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Ruscus aculeatus, commonly known as butchers' broom. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in improving circulation and may support vascular health by influencing blood vessel function.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Ruscus aculeatus. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating cardiovascular conditions. Coumarin exhibits anticoagulant properties, which may support its role in improving circulation. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of Ruscus in herbal medicine.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Ruscus aculeatus. They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including its use in treating cardiovascular conditions. These compounds help improve circulation and reduce inflammation, supporting the plant's traditional applications in herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Ruscus aculeatus. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating cardiovascular conditions. Saponin helps improve circulation and may support heart health. Its presence makes Ruscus aculeatus valuable in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Ruscus aculeatus contains volatile compounds like sesquiterpenes and monoterpene alcohols. It is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and circulatory benefits. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation from the dried plant material. It complements the plant's other active compounds in supporting vascular health.

9. Alkaloid

Ruscus aculeatus contains the alkaloid ruscine, which contributes to its traditional use in treating venous insufficiency. This compound works by strengthening blood vessel walls and improving circulation, making it a key component in herbal formulations for circulatory disorders.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are flavonoid compounds found in Ruscus aculeatus. They contribute to the plant's vibrant color and possess antioxidant properties. These compounds may support vascular health and are often studied for their potential therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.
