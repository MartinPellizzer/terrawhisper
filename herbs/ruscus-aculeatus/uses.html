Ruscus (Ruscus aculeatus) Uses

Ruscus aculeatus is used to treat varicose veins, circulatory weakness, and venous insufficiency. It supports circulation in conditions like hypertension and venous leg ulcers. It may aid in reducing symptoms of poor circulation and trophic ulcers, and is sometimes used for venous thrombosis.

Ruscus (Ruscus aculeatus) Monograph

1. Varicose veins

Ruscus aculeatus has been traditionally used to support the treatment of varicose veins. It is believed to strengthen blood vessel walls and improve circulation. Its active compounds may help reduce inflammation and swelling associated with varicose veins. It is often used in herbal formulations for venous insufficiency.

2. Circulatory weakness

Ruscus aculeatus is used to support circulatory weakness by strengthening blood vessel walls and improving blood flow. It is particularly beneficial for conditions like varicose veins and hemorrhoids. Its active compounds help reduce inflammation and promote tissue repair in the circulatory system.

3. Hypertension

Ruscus aculeatus has been traditionally used to support cardiovascular health, particularly in managing hypertension. It is believed to help regulate blood pressure by promoting circulation and reducing vascular tension. Its active compounds may contribute to calming the nervous system and improving arterial function. This makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for those seeking natural support for hypertension.

4. Venous insufficiency

Ruscus aculeatus is used to support venous insufficiency by improving circulation and reducing swelling. It contains compounds that strengthen blood vessel walls and enhance blood flow. Traditionally, it has been applied topically or taken internally for varicose veins and leg cramps. Its effects are attributed to its ability to stimulate vascular function.

5. Venous leg ulcers

Ruscus aculeatus is used topically for venous leg ulcers due to its ability to improve blood circulation and reduce inflammation. It contains saponins and flavonoids that strengthen blood vessel walls and promote healing. Often applied as a gel or ointment, it supports the regeneration of skin tissue in chronic wounds.

6. Trophic ulcers

Ruscus aculeatus is used topically to treat trophic ulcers, particularly those resulting from poor circulation or diabetes. Its active compounds promote vascular health and enhance tissue repair. It is often applied as a gel or ointment to reduce inflammation and support healing. This makes it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for chronic wound management.

7. Poor circulation

Ruscus aculeatus is used to support poor circulation, particularly in conditions like varicose veins and chronic venous insufficiency. It helps strengthen blood vessel walls and improve blood flow. Often prepared as a tincture or capsule, it is taken internally under professional guidance. Its active compounds promote vascular health and reduce swelling.

8. Circulatory insufficiency

Ruscus aculeatus is used to support circulatory insufficiency by improving blood flow and reducing venous congestion. It is particularly effective in treating conditions like varicose veins and chronic venous insufficiency. The active compounds help strengthen blood vessel walls and enhance circulation. It is often recommended for individuals with poor circulation or swollen legs.

9. High blood pressure

Ruscus aculeatus has been traditionally used to support cardiovascular health, including managing high blood pressure. Its active compounds may help relax blood vessels and improve circulation. It is often used in herbal formulations for its potential to reduce tension and promote steady blood flow. However, it should be used under professional guidance.

10. Venous thrombosis

Ruscus aculeatus has been traditionally used to support venous health and may help in the management of venous thrombosis. Its active compounds, such as cardioactive glycosides, are believed to improve blood circulation and reduce venous congestion. It is often used in herbal formulations to alleviate symptoms associated with poor venous return.
