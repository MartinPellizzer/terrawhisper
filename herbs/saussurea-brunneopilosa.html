Saussurea brunneopilosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea brunneopilosa has a perennial growth habit characterized by a dense rosette of leaves covered in thick, brownish hairs and a central flowering stalk topped with a compact head of yellow florets. The leaves are typically lanceolate or oblong in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. This dense pubescence serves as an adaptation to protect the plant from intense ultraviolet radiation and extreme temperature fluctuations in its high-altitude habitat. The inflorescence consists of multiple capitula clustered together at the apex of the stem. Each flower head is surrounded by involucral bracts that are also coated in fine, protective hairs.
