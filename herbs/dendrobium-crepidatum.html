What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium crepidatum?

Dendrobium crepidatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, which places it among the diverse orchids. Specifically, its taxonomic hierarchy reaches the genus Dendrobium, ultimately identifying it by its unique species name, crepidatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium crepidatum has elongated, pendulous pseudobulbs that are typically cylindrical or fusiform in shape and reach lengths of up to 40 centimeters. These thickened stems serve as water storage organs and are characterized by a smooth, slightly ridged surface. From the nodes of these pseudobulbs, several small, elliptical, or lanceolate leaves emerge. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that feature distinctively shaped petals and sepals. According to a study published by "Scientific reports", the plant Dendrobium crepidatum was used to isolate two new indolizidine alkaloids, crepidatumines A (1) and B (2). These compounds were identified alongside the stereoisomer of dendrocrepidine B (3) and the known analog dendrocrepine (4). The structural analysis showed that compound (1) possesses a (5/6/6/5) tetra-hetero-cyclic ring system. Additionally, compound (2) contains a tricyclic system featuring an unusual bridged ring. Biological evaluation of the isolated substances revealed that dendrocrepine (4) displayed a potent hypoglycemic effect in vitro.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", the medicinal plant Dendrobium crepidatum contains the endophytic Streptomyces sp. MG-F-1. Through a co-culture strategy with Bacillus cereus MG-1, five previously undescribed S-bridged pyranonaphthoquinones known as crepidamycins A-E (1-5) and five known analogues (6-10) were isolated. These compounds 1-10 were assayed for anti-inflammatory potential using LPS-stimulated RAW264.7 cells. Specifically, compound 4 showed a strong nitric oxide inhibitory effect with an IC50 of 0.22 ± 0.16 μM. Results suggest that this compound may play a role by regulating PKM2-mediated glycolysis.
