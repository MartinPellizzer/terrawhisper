Dendrobium crepidatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium crepidatum?

Dendrobium crepidatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, which places it among the diverse orchids. Specifically, its taxonomic hierarchy reaches the genus Dendrobium, ultimately identifying it by its unique species name, crepidatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium crepidatum has elongated, pendulous pseudobulbs that are typically cylindrical or fusiform in shape and reach lengths of up to 40 centimeters. These thickened stems serve as water storage organs and are characterized by a smooth, slightly ridged surface. From the nodes of these pseudobulbs, several small, elliptical, or lanceolate leaves emerge. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that feature distinctively shaped petals and sepals.
