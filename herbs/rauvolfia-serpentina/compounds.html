Indian Snakeroot (Rauvolfia serpentina) Compounds

Rauvolfia serpentina contains several key compounds, including alkaloids like reserpine, which is used for its hypotensive effects. Flavonoids and glycosides contribute to its antioxidant properties. Terpenoids and saponins support anti-inflammatory actions, while tannins and phenolic acids provide additional health benefits.

Indian Snakeroot (Rauvolfia serpentina) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Indian Snakeroot (Rauvolfia serpentina). It is responsible for the plant's medicinal properties, particularly its hypotensive effects. Alkaloids like reserpine are used in traditional and modern medicine for treating hypertension and other cardiovascular conditions.

Flavonoids in Indian Snakeroot (Rauvolfia serpentina) are a group of bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating cardiovascular and neurological conditions, supporting its role in herbal medicine.
