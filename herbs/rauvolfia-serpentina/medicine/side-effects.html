10 Possible Side Effects Of Rauvolfia Serpentina

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian snakeroot, has some side effects when used improperly, such as dizziness, nausea, and vomiting.

These symptoms can be caused by taking high doses or mixing with other medications. For example, if a person with pre-existing heart conditions takes Rauvolfia serpentina, they may experience worsening heart palpitations, leading to anxiety and disrupted daily life.

This can negatively impact work or school performance and overall well-being.

1. Increases heart rate slows

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian Snakeroot, increases heart rate slows, and this side effect is attributed to its ability to affect the autonomic nervous system.

The increased heart rate may be a result of Rauvolfia's sympathomimetic properties, which stimulate the body's fight or flight response, leading to an elevated heart rate.

Simultaneously, it also has parasympatholytic effects, slowing down the heart rate by suppressing the parasympathetic nervous system, which is responsible for regulating the body's natural relaxation response.

2. Causes dizziness fainting

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian Snakeroot, causes dizziness and fainting.

This is due to its ability to lower blood pressure significantly, which can lead to a sudden drop in blood flow to the brain, resulting in lightheadedness and even loss of consciousness.

Additionally, the herb's sedative properties can also contribute to feelings of dizziness and disorientation.

3. Lowers blood pressure drops

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian snakeroot, lowers blood pressure drops due to its active compound reserpine's ability to inhibit the release of neurotransmitters such as norepinephrine and serotonin.

This inhibition leads to a decrease in sympathetic tone, resulting in vasodilation and reduced peripheral resistance, ultimately causing a drop in blood pressure.

The alkaloids present in Rauvolfia serpentina also interact with the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system, further contributing to hypotension.

4. Induces hallucinations vivid

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian snakeroot, induces hallucinations vivid due to its ability to interact with serotonin levels in the brain.

The plant's alkaloids, particularly reserpine and deserpidine, have been shown to affect serotonin receptors, leading to altered perceptions of reality.

This can result in a range of visual, auditory, or tactile hallucinations, which may be disorienting and disturbing for some individuals.

5. Triggers respiratory depression

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian Snakeroot, triggers respiratory depression due to its ability to slow down the central nervous system and affect the brain's respiratory centers.

The alkaloids present in the plant, such as reserpine and deserpidine, are responsible for this effect, causing a decrease in breathing rate and depth.

This can lead to symptoms like shortness of breath, slowed heart rate, and even coma in severe cases.

6. Exacerbates anxiety panic

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian snakeroot, exacerbates anxiety panic in some individuals due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase serotonin levels.

This can lead to feelings of restlessness, agitation, and heightened alertness, which may worsen underlying anxiety disorders.

Additionally, Rauvolfia serpentina's effects on the central nervous system can also cause increased sensitivity to stress and stimuli, further contributing to anxiety-related side effects.

7. Relaxes muscles too much

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian snakeroot, relaxes muscles too much due to its high concentration of alkaloids such as reserpine and ajmaline.

These compounds bind to serotonin receptors in the brain, leading to a decrease in muscle tone and an increase in relaxation. As a result, users may experience excessive muscle relaxation, which can cause feelings of sedation or drowsiness.

This side effect is often attributed to the plant's traditional use as an anxiolytic and antihypnotic agent.

8. Stimulates urinary incontinence

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian snakeroot, stimulates urinary incontinence by affecting the parasympathetic nervous system.

The alkaloids present in the plant, particularly rescinnamine and deserpidine, can cause an increase in the release of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that regulates bladder function. This excessive release can lead to muscle relaxation and decreased sphincter tone, resulting in urinary incontinence.

Additionally, Rauvolfia serpentina's ability to decrease blood pressure may also contribute to this side effect by reducing the pressure on the bladder and urethra.

9. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian snakeroot, produces gastrointestinal upset due to its high concentration of alkaloids and glycosides.

These compounds can cause stomach pain, nausea, and vomiting by irritating the mucous membranes in the digestive tract.

The root's bitter taste and astringent properties may also contribute to this side effect, leading to discomfort and distress for some individuals who use Rauvolfia serpentina.

10. Increases salivation excessive

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian snakeroot, increases salivation excessive in some individuals due to its ability to stimulate the parasympathetic nervous system.

This can lead to an overproduction of saliva, resulting in an excessive flow of saliva.

The increased activity of the neurotransmitter acetylcholine is believed to play a role in this side effect, as it is responsible for stimulating the salivary glands.
