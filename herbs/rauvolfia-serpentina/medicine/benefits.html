3. Reduces blood pressure

Rauvolfia serpentina reduces blood pressure because it contains a number of bioactive compounds, including reserpine, which has been shown to have a potent effect on blood pressure regulation.

Specifically, reserpine has been found to inhibit the release of norepinephrine, a neurotransmitter that can cause blood vessels to constrict and blood pressure to rise.

By reducing the release of norepinephrine, Rauvolfia serpentina is able to help relax blood vessels and lower blood pressure.

4. Stops seizures immediately

Rauvolfia serpentina, also known as Indian snakeroot, stops seizures immediately because of its unique compound, reserpine, which acts as a potent anticonvulsant.

Reserpine binds to GABA receptors in the brain, increasing the inhibitory effects of GABA, a neurotransmitter that calms neuronal activity. This rapid binding and action halt the seizure activity, providing immediate relief from convulsive episodes.

Additionally, reserpine's vasodilatory properties help to reduce blood pressure, further contributing to its antiseizure effects.
