Corydalis kingii

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis kingii?

Corydalis kingii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Ranunculales and the family Papaveraceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by the genus Corydalis and its specific species designation, kingii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis kingii has pinnately divided leaves with several pairs of leaflets and elongated, spurred flowers that are typically pale yellow or cream-colored. The foliage is delicate and deeply lobed, creating a fine texture along the ground. Each flower features a prominent nectar spur extending from the back of the corolla to facilitate pollination. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that develop after the flowering period concludes. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Specifically, many of these alkaloids exhibit analgesic and sedative properties by interacting with neurotransmitter receptors.
