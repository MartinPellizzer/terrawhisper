Anoectochilus formosanus

What's the taxonomical classification of Anoectochilus formosanus?

Anoectochilus formosanus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Asparagales. As a member of the family Orchidaceae, it is specifically categorized under the genus Anoectochilus, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, formosanus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anoectochilus formosanus has small, fleshy, elliptical leaves that are arranged in a distichous pattern along a creeping rhizome. The inflorescence consists of a raceme bearing several small, fragrant flowers characterized by a prominent, trilobed lip. This lip features a distinctively thickened, fleshy callus that extends toward the column. The petals and sepals are typically greenish or yellowish with subtle reddish markings. These morphological traits distinguish this miniature epiphytic orchid from similar species in its genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of Taiwan, where it is primarily found growing in montane forests. It specifically thrives in humid environments such as mossy ravines and shaded slopes at various elevations. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, filtered light and high humidity levels between 60 and 80 percent to thrive in a controlled environment.

Growers should use a well-draining substrate, such as a mixture of fine bark and sphagnum moss, to prevent root rot. Frequent watering is necessary to keep the media moist, but the orchid must be allowed to dry slightly between applications to mimic its natural habitat. Maintaining consistent air circulation around the foliage is vital for preventing fungal diseases.

These specific conditions ensure the plant develops healthy pseudobulbs and produces its characteristic fragrant blooms.

According to a study published by "Journal of biomedical science", the herbal extract of Anoectochilus formosanus (AF) is a popular folk medicine with anticancer activity. The bioactivities of this plant extract were characterized in MCF-7 cancer cells using metabolite profiling and DNA microarray analysis of global gene expression profiles. When compared to the single-compound drug plumbagin, the AF extract showed a similar level of complexity in transcriptional regulation at the genomic level. This similarity was revealed by the number of up- or downregulated genes and by specific but distinct patterns found in the gene-clustering analysis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive medicinal compounds including kinsenoside, specifically 3-(R)-3-beta-D-glucopyranosyloxybutanolide, corchoionoside C, flavonoids, and gastrodin. These various chemical constituents contribute to the diverse therapeutic properties attributed to the species. The presence of kinsenosides is particularly significant for its biological activities. Additionally, the flavonoids and gastrodin within the plant support its traditional medicinal uses. These specific compounds collectively define the pharmacological profile of Anoectochilus formosanus.

According to a study published by "Chemical & pharmaceuatl bulletin", the glycosidic constituents of whole plants of Anoectochilus formosanus propagated by tissue culture were investigated. One new compound identified was 2-(beta-D-glucopyranosyloxymethyl)-5-hydroxymethylfuran. The study also isolated the known compound 3-(R)-3-beta-D-glucopyranosyloxybutanolide, also known as kinsenoside. Other isolated substances include 3-(R)-3-beta-D-glucopyranosyloxy-4-hydroxybutanoic acid and 1-O-isopropyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside. Additionally, the research identified (R)-(+)-3,4-dihydroxy-butanoic acid y-lactone, 4-(beta-D-glucopyranosyloxy)benzyl alcohol, (6R,9S)-9-hydroxy-megastigma-4,7-dien-3-one-9-O-beta-glucopyranoside, and corchoionoside C.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing colon cancer, various human cancers, tumors, diabetes, and hypertension. Researchers study its bioactive compounds to determine how they might inhibit the growth of malignant cells. Specifically, its properties are investigated for their potential to reduce the size of a tumor in different medical models. The plant also shows promise in regulating blood sugar levels for those suffering from diabetes. Furthermore, its medicinal components may help in lowering blood pressure to treat hypertension.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", the water extract of Anoectochilus formosanus Hayata showed potent tumor inhibitory activity in BALB/c mice after the transplantation of CT-26 murine colon cancer cells. Mice pre-administered with the plant for 2 days before transplantation and treated for 12 consecutive days showed tumor-inhibition ratios of 55.4% and 58.9% at oral doses of 50 and 10 mg/mouse per day. For tumor-bearing mice treated for 12 consecutive days, tumor inhibition ratios remained at 23.8% and 40.5% at doses of 50 and 10 mg/mouse. The plant may also activate immune responses by stimulating lymphoid tissue proliferation and activating the phagocytosis of peritoneal macrophages against Staphylococcus aureus. This suggests the antitumor activity of Anoectochilus formosanus may be linked to its potent immunostimulating effect.
