Phlomis atropurpurea

What's the taxonomical classification of Phlomis atropurpurea?

Phlomis atropurpurea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales. It is a constituent of the family Lamiaceae, falling under the genus Phlomis, and is specifically identified by its species name, atropurpurea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis atropurpurea has woody, upright stems that reach heights of approximately 60 to 100 centimeters and are topped by dense, whorled clusters of tubular, dark purple flowers. The leaves are large, ovate to oblong, and possess a distinctively rugose, hairy texture that gives them a grayish-green appearance. These leaves are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem, often becoming more crowded toward the flowering tips. The inflorescence is characterized by its compact, verticillate structure, which serves to showcase the vibrant, velvety corollas. Small, fine hairs cover much of the vegetative surfaces, providing a tactile, felt-like quality to the entire plant.
