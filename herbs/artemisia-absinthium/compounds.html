Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) Compounds

The primary compounds in Wormwood include essential oil, which contains thujone and other volatile compounds. Terpenoids like artemisinin contribute to its medicinal properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids provide antioxidant benefits. Coumarin and tannins support its anti-inflammatory and digestive effects.

Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) contains compounds like thujone and limonene. It is used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic and digestive properties. The oil is often employed in aromatherapy and as a flavoring agent. Its potency requires careful handling due to potential toxicity.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium). They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and have demonstrated anti-inflammatory and antiparasitic properties. Terpenoids like thujone and camphor are particularly notable for their roles in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory conditions. They also enhance the plant's overall therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium). It contributes to the plant's characteristic bitter taste and has anticoagulant properties. Coumarin may also play a role in the plant's traditional use for digestive and anti-inflammatory purposes. Its presence influences both the pharmacological effects and the potential risks associated with Wormwood use.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has antioxidant properties. This compound may support digestive health and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory effects. It plays a role in the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antimicrobial effects, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating infections and inflammation.

7. Alkaloid

Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) contains the alkaloid thujone. Thujone is known for its potent effects on the central nervous system and is responsible for some of Wormwood's traditional uses in herbal medicine, though its safety and dosage remain subjects of ongoing research.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support digestive health and immune function, making them a valuable component in herbal formulations.

9. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Glycosides in Wormwood may support digestive health and have traditional uses in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. While not the primary active component, anthocyanin enhances the overall therapeutic profile of Wormwood in herbal medicine.
