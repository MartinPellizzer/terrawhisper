Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) Uses

Wormwood is used to treat gastrointestinal discomfort, including indigestion, stomach cramps, and gas. It helps with digestive issues like bloating and gastric discomfort. It also aids in expelling intestinal worms and can alleviate toothache caused by gastrointestinal disturbances.

Wormwood (Artemisia absinthium) Monograph

1. Gastrointestinal discomfort

Wormwood is used to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort by stimulating digestion and reducing bloating. Its compounds help ease symptoms like indigestion and gas. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture. Regular use may support digestive health when taken in appropriate doses.

2. Gastrointestinal disorders

Wormwood is used to treat gastrointestinal disorders such as indigestion, bloating, and parasitic infections. Its compounds stimulate digestive enzymes and have antiparasitic properties. It is often prepared as a tincture or tea to support digestion and expel intestinal worms.

3. Indigestion

Wormwood is traditionally used to support digestion by stimulating the production of digestive enzymes and bile. It helps alleviate symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and gas. Its bitter compounds enhance gastric secretions, promoting better nutrient absorption and easing digestive discomfort.

4. Stomach cramps

Wormwood is used to relieve stomach cramps due to its antispasmodic properties. It helps relax the muscles of the digestive tract, reducing pain and discomfort. Often prepared as a tea, it supports digestion and alleviates bloating. Its essential oils and compounds like thujone contribute to its effectiveness in treating gastrointestinal spasms.

5. Gastrointestinal gas

Wormwood is used to alleviate gastrointestinal gas due to its carminative properties. It helps expel trapped air and reduce bloating by stimulating digestive enzymes. Traditionally, it has been employed to relieve flatulence and promote healthy digestion. Its essential oils also have mild antispasmodic effects on the gut.

6. Gastric discomfort

Wormwood is used to alleviate gastric discomfort by stimulating digestive secretions and reducing bloating. It may help with indigestion and gas, though it should be used cautiously due to its bitterness and potential for side effects. Always consult a qualified herbalist before use.

7. Toothache

Wormwood has been traditionally used to alleviate toothache due to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. It can be made into a gargle or poultice to reduce gum inflammation and kill bacteria. Its essential oils help soothe pain and promote healing in dental infections.

8. Gastrointestinal disturbances

Wormwood is used to address gastrointestinal disturbances such as indigestion and bloating. Its bitter compounds stimulate digestive enzymes and improve gut motility. It may also help alleviate symptoms of parasitic infections in the digestive tract. Regular use can support overall digestive health.

9. Digestive gas

Wormwood is used to alleviate digestive gas by stimulating bile production and improving digestion. It helps reduce bloating and discomfort caused by gas buildup in the gastrointestinal tract. Its bitter compounds promote healthy digestion and can ease symptoms of indigestion and flatulence.

10. Worm infestation

Wormwood is traditionally used to treat parasitic worm infestations due to its anthelmintic properties. The active compounds, such as thujone and artemisinic acid, disrupt the nervous systems of parasites. It is often prepared as a tincture or infusion to support digestive health and expel intestinal worms.
