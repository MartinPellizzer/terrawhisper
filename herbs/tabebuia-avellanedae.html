Trumpet Tree (Tabebuia avellanedae)

Trumpet Tree (Tabebuia avellanedae) is a member of the Bignoniaceae family, native to South America, Central America, and Caribbean. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and flowers have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, native american herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Simple leaves with acuminate tips, opposite arrangement, upper surface dull green, lower surface pale green, prominent midrib with stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are zygomorphic, with five petals arranged in a tubular corolla, three of which are fused at the base, and two lobes forming a narrow, elongated tube.
Stems
Woody, erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth bark, and persistent stipules.
