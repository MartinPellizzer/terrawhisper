Trumpet Tree

Tabebuia avellanedae

Trumpet Tree (Tabebuia avellanedae) is a flowering tree native to Central and South America, known for its vibrant orange and yellow flowers and its use in traditional medicine to treat respiratory conditions such as asthma and bronchitis.

Morphology

The Trumpet Tree (Tabebuia avellanedae) is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are opposite and alternate, and clusters of vibrant yellow or orange trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the dry season.

Distribution

The Trumpet Tree (Tabebuia avellanedae) is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, including countries such as Mexico, Guatemala, Belize, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, Panama, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Venezuela, and Brazil.

Traditional Uses

The Trumpet Tree (Tabebuia avellanedae) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its bark and leaves, which contain compounds with anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties, often applied to treat infections, skin conditions, and pain-related ailments.
