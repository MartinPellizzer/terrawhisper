What is Suaeda fruticosa?

Suaeda fruticosa is a perennial halophytic shrub belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its greyish-green, linear to lanceolate leaves that are 2-10 cm long and 0.5-2 cm wide, and its small, greenish-yellow flowers that are arranged in axillary spikes, producing purple, oval-shaped fruits. The stems are erect, branching, and 10-50 cm tall, with a woody base. Suaeda fruticosa is found in salt marshes, salt steppes, and coastal areas of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, where it tolerates high salinity and waterlogging.

Its growth habit is often stunted and dense, forming a mat-like cover.
