Sopubia trifida

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sopubia trifida has trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct, elliptical leaflets that are arranged oppositely along a slender, herbaceous stem. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly serrated, providing a textured appearance to the foliage. These leaves are supported by thin petioles that emerge from a central axis. The plant often exhibits a sprawling or climbing growth habit depending on the available structural support. Small, inconspicuous flowers may appear among the foliage during its reproductive phase.
