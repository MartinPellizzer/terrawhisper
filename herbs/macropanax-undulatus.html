Macropanax undulatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Macropanax undulatus?

Macropanax undulatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Apiales. The plant is a representative of the family Araliaceae, specifically situated within the genus Macropanax, with its specific identification being the species undulatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Macropanax undulatus has large, palmately compound leaves that typically feature seven to nine distinct, deeply serrated leaflets arranged around a central point. The leaf blades are characterized by a prominent, undulating margin and a distinctively textured surface with visible venation. These compound leaves grow on long, sturdy petioles that emerge from a woody, perennial shrub or small tree. The plant also produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, dark-colored drupes. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate and subtropical mountain forests of China, spanning various provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, and Hubei. It typically inhabits montane regions at elevations ranging from several hundred to over two thousand meters above sea level. The species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest edges and rocky slopes where humidity is consistent. Its range is closely tied to the specific climatic conditions found within these East Asian highland ecosystems.
