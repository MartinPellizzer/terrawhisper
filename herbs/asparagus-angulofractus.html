What's the taxonomical classification of Asparagus angulofractus?

Asparagus angulofractus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asparagales, this plant is a member of the family Asparagaceae. Finally, it is identified under the genus Asparagus and is specifically designated by the species name anguifolictus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asparagus anguifractus has sprawling, arching stems that reach up to 10 feet in length and are covered in small, needle-like cladodes rather than true leaves. These photosynthetic stems emerge from a thick, fleshy rhizome system that allows the plant to spread through underground networks. The plant features tiny, inconspicuous white flowers that transition into small, fleshy berries which vary in color from red to black as they mature. Cladodes are arranged in a somewhat irregular, spreading pattern along the woody branches to maximize light absorption. This morphological structure enables the species to form dense, tangled mats in woodland understories.
