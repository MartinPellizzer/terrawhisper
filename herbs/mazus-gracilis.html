Mazus gracilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Mazus gracilis?

Mazus gracilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Mazaceae and falls under the genus Mazus, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species gracilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mazus gracilis has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats through the production of slender, elliptical to lanceolate leaves measuring approximately 10 to 30 millimeters in length. These small, bright green leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems and feature finely serrated margins. The plant produces small, zygomorphic flowers that typically emerge in axillary clusters. These blossoms are characterized by a two-lipped corolla that displays colors ranging from pale lavender to light purple with white markings. Tiny, inconspicuous roots develop at the nodes where the stems make contact with the soil to facilitate spreading.
