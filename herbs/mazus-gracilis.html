What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mazus gracilis has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats through the production of slender, elliptical to lanceolate leaves measuring approximately 10 to 30 millimeters in length. These blossoms are characterized by a two-lipped corolla that displays colors ranging from pale lavender to light purple with white markings. Tiny, inconspicuous roots develop at the nodes where the stems make contact with the soil to facilitate spreading.
