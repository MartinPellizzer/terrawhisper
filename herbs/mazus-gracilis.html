Mazus gracilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mazus gracilis has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats through the production of slender, elliptical to lanceolate leaves measuring approximately 10 to 30 millimeters in length. These small, bright green leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems and feature finely serrated margins. The plant produces small, zygomorphic flowers that typically emerge in axillary clusters. These blossoms are characterized by a two-lipped corolla that displays colors ranging from pale lavender to light purple with white markings. Tiny, inconspicuous roots develop at the nodes where the stems make contact with the soil to facilitate spreading.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically occurring in parts of Japan, China, Korea, and Taiwan. It typically inhabits moist, shaded environments such as forest edges and mountain slopes within these regions. While its primary range is confined to these Asian territories, it has been introduced to other temperate zones as an ornamental species. In its natural habitat, the plant thrives in areas with consistent moisture and dappled sunlight. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific microclimates found in these subtropical and temperate woodland ecosystems.
