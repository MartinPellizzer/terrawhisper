Botanical Identification of French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas)

To identify French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

French Lavender, Lavandula stoechas, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Lavandula, and species Lavandula stoechas. It is a member of the mint family and is closely related to other aromatic herbs. This classification reflects its botanical relationships and evolutionary lineage.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of French Lavender is Lavandula stoechas, a classification established by the botanist Linnaeus in 1753. This species is also known by several synonyms, including Lavandula latifolia and Lavandula multiflora, though these are now considered outdated. Common names such as French Lavender, Spanish Lavender, and Lavandin reflect its widespread use and regional significance in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas) is a shrub with a compact, upright growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it well in soil. Its stems are square and woody, with a grayish-green hue. Leaves are linear, aromatic, and covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence is a spike of showy purple flowers with distinctive yellow glandular tips. Flowers are tubular with bilabiate lips, and the fruit is a capsule containing small, dark seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas) can be distinguished by its distinctive purple flower spikes with red-brown bracts, narrow gray-green leaves, and square, hairy stems. It differs from Lavandula angustifolia by its more branched inflorescences and shorter, narrower leaves. The presence of glandular dots on the leaves and a strong aromatic scent also aid in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas) is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly southern France, Spain, and North Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with mild, wet winters and dry summers. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and typically grows at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas) blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, when the spikes are in full bloom and the leaves are still fresh.

Phylogenetic Relationships

French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas) belongs to the genus Lavandula within the family Lamiaceae. It is closely related to other Lavandula species, sharing characteristics such as aromatic leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Its phylogenetic position reflects evolutionary ties to other members of the Lamiaceae family, which include plants like mint and oregano.
