French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas) Compounds

French Lavender contains essential oil as its primary compound, rich in terpenoids like linalool and lavandin. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant properties, while coumarin and tannins support anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides, saponins, and alkaloids add to its diverse therapeutic potential. Anthocyanins provide additional color and health benefits.

French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas) contains volatile compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate. These compounds contribute to its calming and antiseptic properties, commonly used in herbal medicine for stress relief and skin care.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are key to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for calming and skin-soothing applications.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds support skin health and may aid in reducing stress and promoting relaxation. Flavonoids are key to the therapeutic effects of French Lavender in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and promoting relaxation. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of French Lavender in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas). It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has mild anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is often used in herbal medicine for its calming effects and potential support in reducing stress and anxiety.

6. Tannin

Tannin in French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and may support wound healing. Tannins also act as antioxidants, protecting cells from oxidative stress. This compound plays a role in the plant's traditional use for skin conditions and digestive health.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas). It contributes to the plant's calming and anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides are often responsible for the therapeutic effects of many herbal remedies, including those derived from lavender.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin helps support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for skin conditions and respiratory health.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its specific effects and mechanisms are less studied compared to other compounds like linalool. Alkaloids in Lavender may play a role in its calming and sedative effects, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in French Lavender (Lavandula stoechas). It contributes to the plant's vibrant purple color and possesses antioxidant properties. These compounds help protect the plant from environmental stress and may support human health by reducing oxidative damage.
