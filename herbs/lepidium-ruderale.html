Lepidium ruderale

What's the taxonomical classification of Lepidium ruderale?

Lepidium ruderale belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Brassicales. This plant is a part of the family Brassicaceae, which places it within the genus Lepidium, specifically identified by the species name rudeale.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lepidium ruderale has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 50 centimeters and features pinnatifid to lyrate-pinnatifid leaves. The basal leaves are generally larger and more deeply lobed than the smaller, more simplified leaves found along the upper stem. Its inflorescence consists of small, white, four-petaled flowers arranged in dense terminal racemes. The fruit is a characteristic silicle, which is a small, flattened, orbicular pod containing a single seed. These structures distinguish the species from other members of the Brassicaceae family.
