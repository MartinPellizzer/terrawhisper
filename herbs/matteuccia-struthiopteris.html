Matteuccia struthiopteris

What's the taxonomical classification of Matteuccia struthiopteris?

Matteucciopteris struthiopteris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Within this hierarchical structure, the plant is situated in the family Aspleniaceae, which contains the genus Matteuccia. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, struthiopteris.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Matteuccia struthiopteris has large, bipinnate-pinnatifid fronds that grow in a distinctive, upright circular pattern known as a crown. Each frond can reach heights of up to one meter and consists of numerous sterile blades that emerge from a central rhizome. The individual pinnae are broadly lanceolate with deeply lobed margins that give the plant a feathery appearance. In the spring, specialized fertile fronds emerge from the center of the crown to produce spores on their upper surfaces. These large, structural leaves are arranged symmetrically to maximize light interception in shaded woodland habitats.

What are the pharmacological activities of Matteuccia struthiopteris?

This plant has a protective effect on lupus-like syndrome through its potent anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. Research indicates that extracts from Matteuccia struthiopteris can mitigate the autoimmune responses characteristic of systemic lupus erythematosus. These biological activities are largely attributed to the presence of specific secondary metabolites that regulate cytokine production. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates significant antioxidant capacity which helps reduce tissue damage caused by oxidative stress in autoimmune models. Such pharmacological potential suggests that its bioactive compounds may serve as templates for developing new therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, Matteuccia struthiopteris contains potential active components such as polysaccharides that show protective effects against lupus-like syndrome. When administered at doses of 30 and 15 mg x kg(-1), these polysaccharides reduced weight loss in BALB/c mice compared to the SLE model group. Specifically, the 15 mg x kg(-1) dose reduced spleen swelling with a significance of P < 0.05. The treatment also significantly inhibited the increased production of autoantibodies and total IgG. Furthermore, Matteuccia struthiopteris polysaccharides protected the kidney against glomerular injury, resulting in reduced immuglobulin deposition and lowered proteinuria with a significance of P < 0.01.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds such as 4-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-p-coumaric acid, 4-O-beta-D-glucopyranosylcaffeic acid, apigenin, polysaccharides, and woodwardic acid. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the biological activity and therapeutic potential of the species. The presence of apigenin often provides antioxidant properties, while the various polysaccharides may assist in immune modulation. Additionally, the phenolic acids like the glucopyranosyl derivatives contribute to the overall chemical complexity of the plant. Together, these diverse molecules form a sophisticated chemical profile used in various medicinal studies.

As discussed in a study, the rhizome of Matteuccia struthiopteris contains 6 isolated compounds. These identified medicinal compounds include woodwardic acid (1), ergost-6,22-diene-3beta,5alpha,8alpha-triol (2), apiigenin (3), riboflavin (4), 4-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-p-coumaric acid (5), and 4-O-beta-D-glucopyranosylcaffeic acid (6). All of these 6 compounds were obtained from this plant for the first time. The isolation process utilized normal phase silica gel chromatography to separate the constituents. Structures for each of the 6 compounds were identified through physical and spectral data.
