Melaleuca

Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca (Melaleuca alternifolia) is a flowering plant native to Australia, commonly known as tea tree, and is widely recognized for its antimicrobial properties, particularly in the treatment of skin infections and respiratory conditions.

Botanical Identification

Melaleuca alternifolia, commonly known as tea tree, belongs to the Myrtaceae family. It is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, with white flowers in clusters. The species is also referred to as melaleuca in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Myrtaceae
  • Genus: Melaleuca
  • Species: Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca Identification.

Active Compounds

Melaleuca alternifolia contains essential oils, terpenoids, and flavonoids as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. The essential oils, particularly tea tree oil, are the main contributors to its therapeutic properties.

  • Essential oil
  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid

Melaleuca Compounds.

Melaleuca Actions.

Preparation Methods

Essential oil: Used for respiratory infections, skin conditions, and as an antiseptic.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Tincture: Used for topical applications and internal ailments like colds.

Safety Profile

Melaleuca, also known as tea tree oil, is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it should not be ingested and can cause skin irritation in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always perform a patch test before use and consult a healthcare professional for specific guidance.
