Melaleuca (Melaleuca alternifolia)

Melaleuca (Melaleuca alternifolia) is a member of the Myrtaceae family, native to Australia. Traditionally, its leaves, resin, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and oil infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and expectorant actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Simple, narrow, needle-like leaves with whitish stomatal bands on the lower surface and glossy dark green upper surface.
Flowers
Inflorescences are dense, globular clusters of small, actinomorphic flowers with white to pale yellow petals and yellow stamens.
Stems
Cylindrical, woody, aromatic, with opposite, ascending, slightly angled, and prominently ribbed nodes.

Medicinal Actions

Anti-inflammatory
Traditionally described as a moderate anti-inflammatory, in topical or internal use contexts.
Astringent
In herbal texts, considered a mild astringent, in tissue-toning contexts.
Expectorant
Historically regarded as a cooling expectorant, in respiratory system contexts.
Diuretic
Commonly referenced as a calming diuretic, for elimination-focused applications.
