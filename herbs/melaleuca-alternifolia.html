What to know about Melaleuca alternifolia before using it medicinally

Melaleuca alternifolia, commonly known as tea tree, is a native Australian herb that has been widely used for its medicinal properties.

It is particularly renowned for its effectiveness in treating various health conditions, including acne, wounds, and infections, as well as skin conditions such as eczema and rosacea. The main health benefits of this herb include its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties, which help to promote healing and prevent infection. The main therapeutic properties of Melaleuca alternifolia include its ability to inhibit the growth of bacteria, fungi, and viruses, as well as its capacity to reduce inflammation and soothe irritated skin.

The herb contains a range of bioactive compounds, including terpinen-4-ol, cineole, and limonene, which are responsible for its medicinal properties, and is often used to make a tea or oil that is used topically to treat various skin and wound conditions.
