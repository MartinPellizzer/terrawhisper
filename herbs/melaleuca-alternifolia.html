Melaleuca alternifolia

Melaleuca alternifolia, commonly known as the tea tree, is a native Australian plant belonging to the myrtle family, characterized by its small, white, and highly fragrant flowers.

The melaleuca alternifolia plant has been widely used for its medicinal properties, primarily due to the presence of compounds such as cineole and terpinen-4-ol, which exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The health benefits of tea tree oil, derived from the plant, include its ability to soothe skin irritations, reduce acne and wounds, and combat fungal and bacterial infections.

Herbal preparations of melaleuca alternifolia include tea tree oil, which can be applied topically or added to skincare products, as well as dried leaves and branches used in herbal teas and infusions.
