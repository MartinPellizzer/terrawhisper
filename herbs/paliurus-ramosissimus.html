Paliurus ramosissimus

What's the taxonomical classification of Paliurus ramosissimus?

Paliurus ramosissimus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Rosales and the family Rhamnaceae. Finally, the plant is identified within the genus Paliurus, with its specific designation being the species ramosissimus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paliurus ramosissimus has an erect or arching shrubby habit characterized by densely branched, thorny stems and small, alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, often appearing in clusters along the woody branches. Its stems are armed with sharp, stipular spines that emerge at the nodes to protect the plant from herbivores. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-white flowers that give rise to fleshy, red drupes. These berries are a distinctive feature that persists on the plant, aiding in seed dispersal.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds bergapten, byakangelicin, scaroone, umbelliferone, and xanthotoxol. These specific furanocoumarins are known for their significant biological activities and pharmacological potential. Many of these chemical constituents are studied for their ability to interact with ultraviolet light and influence skin sensitivity. Research into these compounds often focuses on their antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties. Understanding the concentration of these molecules is essential for determining the plant's therapeutic efficacy.

As discussed in a study, the fruits of Paliurus ramosissimus contain nine specific medicinal compounds. These nine constituents were separated using various chromatographic techniques and identified through spectral analysis and physico-chemical properties. The isolated compounds are umbelliferone (1), scoparone (2), aurapten (3), bergapten (4), isopimpinellin (5), byakangelicin (6), xanthotoxol (7), isosakuranin (8), and poncirin (9). These compounds, labeled 1 through 9, were isolated from the fruits of P. ramosissimus for the first time.
