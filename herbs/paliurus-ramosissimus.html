Paliurus ramosissimus

What's the taxonomical classification of Paliurus ramosissimus?

Paliurus ramosissimus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Rosales and the family Rhamnaceae. Finally, the plant is identified within the genus Paliurus, with its specific designation being the species ramosissimus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paliurus ramosissimus has an erect or arching shrubby habit characterized by densely branched, thorny stems and small, alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, often appearing in clusters along the woody branches. Its stems are armed with sharp, stipular spines that emerge at the nodes to protect the plant from herbivores. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-white flowers that give rise to fleshy, red drupes. These berries are a distinctive feature that persists on the plant, aiding in seed dispersal.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds bergapten, byakangelicin, isopiminelellin, scoparone, and umbelliferone. These specific chemical constituents are classified as coumarins and are known for their various biological activities. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential pharmacological effects on human health. The presence of these compounds contributes to the overall therapeutic profile of the species. Each molecule plays a distinct role in the chemical complexity of the plant's extracts.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", nine compounds were isolated and identified from the fruits of Paliurus ramosissimus. These chemical constituents, which were isolated for the first time, include umbelliferone (1), scoparone (2), aurapten (3), bergapten (4), isopimpinellin (5), byakangelicin (6), xanthotoxol (7), isosakuranin (8), and poncirin (9). The study utilized various chromatographic techniques to separate these substances. Their structures were subsequently elucidated through spectral analysis and physico-chemical properties.
