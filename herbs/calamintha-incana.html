Calamintha incana

Calamintha incana, commonly known as Mountain Mint or Grey Calamint, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

The therapeutic actions of Calamintha incana are attributed to its bioactive constituents, which include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils such as camphor, linalool, and borneol, that exhibit sedative, expectorant, and antispasmodic effects.

Calamintha incana has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to promote relaxation, ease coughs, and calm digestive issues.
