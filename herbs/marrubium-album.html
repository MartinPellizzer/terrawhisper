White Horehound (Marrubium Album)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Marrubium album.

White horehound, scientifically known as Marrubium album, is a perennial medicinal herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, valued for its adaptogenic properties and historical use in traditional medicine. It is traditionally used to soothe respiratory conditions such as coughs and sore throats, and is also believed to support digestive health due to its bitter compounds and mucilage content. Historically, it has been employed in folk medicine for its calming effects and was once used as a natural remedy for anxiety and stress, earning it a place in ancient herbal texts. In modern wellness practices, it is often used in herbal teas and supplements to promote respiratory health and reduce inflammation. One of its unique features is its distinctive licorice-like flavor, which has made it a valued spice in traditional culinary applications, while its rare active compound, marrubiin, contributes to its therapeutic potential.
