Pisonia umbellifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Pisonia umbellifera?

Pisonia umbellifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Caryophyllales. It is further categorized within the family Nyctaginaceae, specifically belonging to the genus Pisonia, with the specific epithet umbellifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pisonia umbellifera has woody, spreading shrubs or small trees reaching up to 5 meters in height, characterized by serrated, ovate leaves and dense clusters of pink or white flowers. The foliage is typically leathery with distinctively toothed margins that vary in size depending on the age of the plant. Its inflorescences are arranged in terminal umbels, creating rounded bunches of small blossoms. The fruit produced is a small, fleshy drupe that often turns dark when mature. This species is frequently found in coastal dunes where its resilient structure helps it withstand salty winds.

The species is often found in well-drained habitats where it can tolerate salt spray and maritime conditions. Its distribution is closely tied to these specific seaside environments across the Indian Ocean coastline.
