Aster tongolensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster tongolensis has perennial herbaceous stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters and bear lanceolate to linear leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem and displays a distinct green pigmentation. Its inflorescence consists of small, discoid flower heads that are clustered in terminal corymbs. Each flower head features numerous tiny tubular florets surrounded by several rows of overlapping phyllaries. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate floral structures adapted to its specific highland habitat.
