Aster tongolensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster tongolensis has perennial herbaceous stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters and bear lanceolate to linear leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem and displays a distinct green pigmentation. Its inflorescence consists of small, discoid flower heads that are clustered in terminal corymbs. Each flower head features numerous tiny tubular florets surrounded by several rows of overlapping phyllaries. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate floral structures adapted to its specific highland habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Aster tongolensis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled without protection. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may lead to localized dermatitis or itching for sensitive individuals. If consumed, users might experience nausea, stomach cramps, or other digestive disturbances. There is also a potential for respiratory irritation if the pollen is inhaled in large quantities. Due to these risks, careful handling and avoidance of ingestion are recommended.
