What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sparganium simplex has a small, globose sporangium that measures between 100 and 250 micrometers in diameter and is supported by a short, slender stalk. The outer surface of the sporangium is characterized by a thin, translucent peridium that eventually breaks away to reveal the spore mass. Within this structure, the spores are arranged in a single layer and possess a distinctive, finely ornamented surface. The capillitium is typically inconspicuous or absent, which distinguishes this species from many other members of its genus.
