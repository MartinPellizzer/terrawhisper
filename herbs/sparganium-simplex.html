What's the taxonomical classification of Sparganium simplex?

Sparganium simplex belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Poales and the family Typhaceae. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Sparganium, and its species designation, simplex.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sparganium simplex has a small, globose sporangium that measures between 100 and 250 micrometers in diameter and is supported by a short, slender stalk.

The outer surface of the sporangium is characterized by a thin, translucent peridium that eventually breaks away to reveal the spore mass. Within this structure, the spores are arranged in a single layer and possess a distinctive, finely ornamented surface. The capillitium is typically inconspicuous or absent, which distinguishes this species from many other members of its genus.

These compact morphological features are essential for the efficient dispersal of its spores in its natural habitat.
