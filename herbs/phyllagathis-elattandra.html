What are the therapeutic actions of Phyllagathis elattandra?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, wounds, stomach ailments, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves are often applied topically to manage infections or soothe irritated skin areas. When ingested in traditional preparations, it acts to alleviate digestive issues and gastrointestinal discomfort. Some medicinal practices also utilize the plant to reduce swelling and manage pain associated with internal inflammation. This botanical species remains a significant component in regional folk medicine for these specific ailments.

What are the herbal preparations of Phyllagathis elattandra?

Phyllagathis elattandra is prepared as a decoction, a paste, or a poultice for various therapeutic uses. The plant material is often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption or external washing. For topical applications, the leaves are frequently crushed into a thick paste to be applied directly to the skin. This paste can also be formulated into a poultice to treat localized inflammation or wounds. These different preparation methods allow the medicinal properties of the plant to be administered effectively based on the specific ailment.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Phyllagathis elattandra can cause localized skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. While specific clinical data on human toxicity is limited, traditional uses suggest that concentrated extracts may act as irritants to sensitive mucosal membranes. Some individuals might experience redness or itching upon direct contact with the plant's foliage or sap. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid self-administration without professional guidance to prevent unexpected physiological responses. Always consult a medical professional if symptoms occur after exposure to this species.
