Rosa rubus

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa rubus?

Rosa rubus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Rosales. It is a member of the family Rosaceae, specifically situated within the genus Rosa, and is identified by the species name rubus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa rubus has pinnately compound leaves consisting of five to seven oval leaflets with serrated margins and a stem covered in sharp, woody prickles. The foliage typically presents a deep green color on the upper surface while the underside may appear slightly paler. Its flowering structures consist of solitary or clustered blossoms featuring five distinct petals that range from pale pink to deep crimson. Following pollination, the plant develops fleshy aggregate fruits known as hips that transition from green to a bright red or orange hue. The root system is composed of a perennial woody structure that allows the plant to spread through rhizomatic growth.
