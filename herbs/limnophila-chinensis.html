Limnophila chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Limnophila chinensis?

Limnophila chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Plantaginaceae, falling under the specific genus Limnophila, with its unique taxonomic identity completed by the species epithet chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Limnophila chinensis has slender, cylindrical stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and feature small, lanceolate leaves arranged in whorls of three to four around the axis. The foliage is characterized by a bright green color and a delicate, feathery texture that allows it to sway easily in water currents. Each leaf is relatively thin with entire margins, contributing to the plant's fine-textured appearance in aquatic environments. The root system consists of fine, fibrous structures that anchor the stem into soft substrates. These morphological traits collectively enable the species to efficiently absorb nutrients directly from the water column.
