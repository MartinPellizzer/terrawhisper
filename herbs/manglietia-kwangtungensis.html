Manglietia kwangtungensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Manglietia kwangtungensis has deciduous leaves that are broadly elliptic to ovate in shape, measuring approximately 10 to 20 centimeters in length with entire margins and a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that emerge from terminal buds, often appearing before or alongside the new foliage. These blossoms feature thick, waxy petals that typically transition from a pale cream color to a deep yellowish hue as they mature. The reproductive structures consist of a central receptacle bearing numerous stamens and carpels, which eventually develop into aggregate fruits. This species maintains a woody, upright growth habit with bark that becomes textured as the tree ages.
