Piper pingbienense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper pingbienense has woody stems with opposite, ovate leaves that possess an acute apex and an entire margin. The foliage typically features prominent venation and a smooth or slightly coriaceous texture on the surface. Its inflorescences consist of slender, spike-like structures that emerge from the leaf axils. These spikes bear small, densely packed flowers that eventually develop into fleshy berries. The overall growth habit is that of a perennial shrub or climbing vine.
