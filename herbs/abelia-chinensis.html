Abelia chinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Abelia chinensis has opposite, elliptical to ovate leaves that measure between one and three inches in length and feature finely serrated margins. The foliage is typically a deep green color, though some cultivars exhibit variegated patterns of white or cream. Its stems are woody and can become somewhat arching as the plant matures into a spreading shrub. Small, tubular, white or pinkish flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters throughout the summer. These blossoms are followed by small, brownish, papery sepals that persist into the winter months.
