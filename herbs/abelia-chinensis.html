What's the taxonomical classification of Abelia chinensis?

Abelia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Dipsacales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Caprifoliaceae, which further narrows down to the genus Abelia. Ultimately, the specific biological identity of this plant is defined by its species name, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Abelia chinensis has opposite, elliptical to ovate leaves that measure between one and three inches in length and feature finely serrated margins. Its stems are woody and can become somewhat arching as the plant matures into a spreading shrub. Small, tubular, white or pinkish flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters throughout the summer. These blossoms are followed by small, brownish, papery sepals that persist into the winter months.
