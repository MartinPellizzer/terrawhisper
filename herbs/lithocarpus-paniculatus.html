Lithocarpus paniculatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lithocarpus paniculatus has simple, alternate, coriaceous leaves that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and acute tips. The leaf surfaces are often glabrous or possess a fine pubescence on the underside. Its inflorescence is characterized by a branched panicle structure that carries the flowering parts. The plant produces woody acorns enclosed in cupules that are often covered in scales or fine hairs. These morphological features distinguish the species within its taxonomic group.
