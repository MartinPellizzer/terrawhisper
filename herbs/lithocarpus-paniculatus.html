Lithocarpus paniculatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lithocarpus paniculatus?

Lithocarpus paniculatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Fagales and the family Fagaceae, ultimately placing it within the genus Lithocarpus. As a distinct member of this group, its specific designation is the species paniculatus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lithocarpus paniculatus contains high concentrations of polyphenolic compounds, specifically tannins, flavonoids, and saponins, which provide its primary medicinal potential. These bioactive molecules are known for possessing significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties that can protect cells from oxidative stress. Researchers have identified specific triterpenoids within the plant that may contribute to anti-inflammatory effects in biological systems. The presence of these secondary metabolites suggests that extracts from the plant could be utilized in developing natural therapeutic agents. Further pharmacological studies are often required to isolate specific alkaloids that might influence metabolic pathways.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lithocarpus paniculatus?

Lithocarpus paniculatus has bioactive compounds that exhibit significant antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties within traditional medicinal practices. The plant contains various polyphenols and tannins that assist in neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress in biological systems. These chemical constituents are often utilized to treat skin infections and promote wound healing through their ability to inhibit bacterial growth. Additionally, extracts from the bark and leaves are sometimes employed to alleviate internal inflammation and soothe digestive distress. Research continues to explore how these specific phytochemicals can be isolated for targeted therapeutic applications.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin ailments including eczema, wounds, and inflammatory dermatological conditions. The extracts derived from its bark and leaves possess significant antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial infections. Local traditional practices often apply these botanical components to soothe itching and promote the healing of minor abrasions. Some studies suggest that the chemical constituents in the plant may also assist in reducing internal inflammation. This medicinal application relies on the specific bioactive compounds found within its foliage and woody tissues.
