Botanical Identification of Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata)

To identify Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Common Hawthorn, Crataegus laevigata, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class of eudicots, order of Rosales, family of Rosaceae, genus Crataegus, and species Crataegus laevigata. This classification places it within the rose family, known for its diverse and economically important plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Common Hawthorn is Crataegus laevigata, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus oxyacantha. Common names such as Hawthorn, Mayflower, and Common Thorn are widely used, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Common Hawthorn is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a rounded growth habit. It has a deep, fibrous root system that anchors it firmly. Its stems are thorny and branched, often with a reddish-brown bark. Leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a smooth upper surface. The inflorescence consists of clusters of white flowers in spring. The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that turns orange-red in autumn, containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Common Hawthorn can be distinguished by its smooth, grey bark, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers followed by red berries. It differs from similar species like Crataegus monogyna by having more rounded leaves and fewer thorns. The berries are typically more elongated and contain more seeds.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Common Hawthorn is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cold winters. It prefers well-drained soils, such as loam or sandy soil. It typically grows at altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Common Hawthorn blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to observe key features is during the flowering period, which aids in accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata) belongs to the genus Crataegus within the family Rosaceae. It is closely related to other hawthorn species, sharing similar morphological and genetic traits. Its phylogenetic position reflects its close ties to other members of the Rosaceae family, particularly within the subfamily Rosoideae.
