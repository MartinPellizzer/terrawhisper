Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata) Compounds

The primary compounds in Common Hawthorn include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to its astringent effects. Phenolic acids and glycosides also play roles in its medicinal benefits, while coumarin and saponins may support cardiovascular health. Terpenoids, anthocyanins, essential oils, and alkaloids add to its diverse therapeutic potential.

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata). They contribute to the plant's cardiovascular benefits by improving blood flow and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are found in the flowers, leaves, and berries of the plant. Flavonoids support heart health and are a primary reason for the plant's use in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support cardiovascular health by improving blood vessel function. Tannins also have antimicrobial effects, enhancing the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports cardiovascular health and may aid in managing conditions like hypertension and anxiety.

4. Glycoside

Glycosides are important active compounds in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata). They contribute to the plant's cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are often responsible for the herb's traditional use in treating heart conditions and digestive issues.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic effects, often used in herbal medicine for cardiovascular support and stress relief. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in promoting calmness and improving heart function.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata). It contributes to the plant's cardiovascular benefits by supporting healthy blood pressure and circulation. Saponins also exhibit mild anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, enhancing the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including potential cardiovascular and sedative effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's aroma and may support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cardiovascular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is also responsible for the plant's vibrant red and purple hues in its flowers and fruits.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like geraniol and linalool, which contribute to its calming effects. This oil is used in herbal medicine to support cardiovascular health and reduce anxiety. Its therapeutic properties are derived from the plant's flowering parts.

10. Alkaloid

Active compounds of Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata) include alkaloids, which are nitrogen-containing compounds found in various parts of the plant. These alkaloids contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for their potential cardiovascular and sedative effects.
