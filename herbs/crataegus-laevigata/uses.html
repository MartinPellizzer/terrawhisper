Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata) Uses

Common Hawthorn is used to treat cardiac arrhythmia, hypertension, and heart failure due to its cardiovascular benefits. It helps manage high blood pressure and cholesterol levels, supporting heart health. It may also aid in reducing edema and improving sleep, making it useful for insomnia and overall cardiovascular support.

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata) Monograph

1. Cardiac arrhythmia

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus laevigata) is used in herbal medicine to support heart health, particularly in managing cardiac arrhythmia. It contains flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins that may help regulate heart rhythm and improve blood flow. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tincture or infusion to support cardiovascular function.

2. Hypertension

Common Hawthorn is used in herbal medicine to support cardiovascular health, particularly in managing hypertension. It contains flavonoids and antioxidants that may help relax blood vessels and improve blood flow. Traditionally, it has been employed to reduce high blood pressure and support overall heart function. Its mild action makes it a preferred choice in long-term herbal management of hypertension.

3. High blood pressure

Common Hawthorn is used in herbal medicine to support cardiovascular health, including managing high blood pressure. It contains flavonoids and antioxidants that may help relax blood vessels and improve circulation. Traditionally, it has been used to reduce hypertension and promote overall heart function. Its mild action makes it a gentle option for long-term use under professional guidance.

4. Heart failure

Common Hawthorn has been traditionally used to support heart function in cases of heart failure. It contains flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins that may improve circulation and strengthen cardiac muscle. It is often used as a mild tonic to enhance heart health and manage symptoms associated with chronic heart conditions.

5. Edema

Common Hawthorn is used to treat edema due to its ability to improve circulation and reduce fluid retention. It supports cardiovascular health, which can alleviate swelling in the legs and ankles. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tincture or tea to promote lymphatic drainage and reduce inflammation. Its mild action makes it suitable for long-term use in managing chronic edema.

6. Cholesterol

Common Hawthorn supports cardiovascular health by helping regulate cholesterol levels. It contains compounds that may inhibit cholesterol synthesis and enhance its excretion. This makes it a valuable herb in managing hypercholesterolemia. Regular use may contribute to maintaining healthy lipid profiles.

7. Tachycardia

Common Hawthorn is used to support heart health and may help manage tachycardia by regulating heart rhythm. Its compounds, such as flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins, have mild sedative and vasodilating effects. It is often combined with other herbs in formulations to calm the nervous system and reduce cardiac overactivity.

8. Heart disease

Common Hawthorn is used in herbal medicine to support heart health. It contains flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins that may improve blood flow and reduce oxidative stress. Traditionally, it has been used to manage symptoms of heart disease, including high blood pressure and arrhythmia. Its mild action makes it a gentle support for cardiovascular function.

9. High cholesterol

Common Hawthorn is used to support cardiovascular health by potentially lowering cholesterol levels. It contains compounds that may help reduce low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol. Regular use may improve lipid profiles and support overall heart function. It is often combined with other herbs in traditional herbal medicine for this purpose.

10. Insomnia

Common Hawthorn is used to treat insomnia due to its calming effects on the nervous system. It helps reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, making it easier to fall asleep. The herb is often prepared as a tincture or tea and is typically taken in the evening to support restful sleep. Its mild sedative properties make it a gentle option for those seeking natural relief from sleeplessness.
