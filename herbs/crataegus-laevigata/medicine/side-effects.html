10 Possible Side Effects Of Crataegus Laevigata

Crataegus laevigata has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive problems, and interactions with medications.

These issues can be caused by high doses, prolonged use, or combining with other substances.

For instance, allergic reactions can cause skin rashes and respiratory issues, which can worsen someone's quality of life by limiting their physical activities and social interactions.

1. Causes nausea occurs

Crataegus laevigata causes nausea occurs when its saponins and flavonoids interact with the gastrointestinal system.

These bioactive compounds may stimulate the vagus nerve, leading to increased salivation and stomach contractions, which can cause feelings of queasiness and discomfort.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids may also contribute to an increase in gut motility, further exacerbating nausea symptoms.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Crataegus laevigata triggers allergic reactions due to the presence of proteins in its leaves and flowers that can cause an immune response.

This reaction can lead to symptoms such as itching, swelling, and rashes, particularly in individuals with a history of allergies or sensitivities.

3. Induces stomach upset

Crataegus laevigata induces stomach upset due to its bitter compounds, which can irritate the digestive tract and slow down gastric emptying.

The flavonoids present in the plant may also contribute to this side effect by altering gut motility and increasing the secretion of stomach acid.

Additionally, the anthraquinone glycosides found in hawthorn have been shown to cause gastrointestinal disturbances in some individuals.

4. Produces dizziness attacks

Crataegus laevigata produces dizziness attacks due to its ability to dilate blood vessels and lower blood pressure.

This vasodilatory effect can cause a sudden drop in blood pressure, leading to feelings of lightheadedness, dizziness, and even fainting.

Additionally, the herb's potential interaction with certain medications, such as blood thinners, may also contribute to these adverse effects.

5. Increases blood pressure

Crataegus laevigata increases blood pressure due to its ability to constrict blood vessels and increase cardiac contractility.

The flavonoids present in the plant, such as quercetin and epicatechin, are believed to contribute to this effect by activating alpha-adrenergic receptors and enhancing sympathetic nervous system activity.

This can lead to a rise in blood pressure, potentially affecting individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions.

6. Lowers heart rate

Crataegus laevigata lowers heart rate due to its ability to slow down cardiac activity.

This occurs because the extract of C. laevigata contains flavonoids and procyanidins, which have been shown to relax blood vessels and reduce sympathetic tone, leading to a decrease in heart rate and blood pressure.

7. Provokes anxiety episodes

Crataegus laevigata provokes anxiety episodes due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase heart rate.

The herb's flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins (OPCs) may amplify the body's natural stress response, leading to feelings of unease, apprehension, and heightened alertness.

Additionally, hawthorn's cardiotonic effects may exacerbate existing anxiety disorders by increasing blood pressure and heart rate, further contributing to the onset of anxious episodes.

8. Stimulates insomnia patterns

Crataegus laevigata stimulates insomnia patterns by its ability to alter the body's natural sleep-wake cycle.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and triterpenoids, can interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to increased alertness and difficulty falling asleep. Additionally, hawthorn's vasodilatory effects may cause a sense of restlessness or anxiety, further disrupting sleep quality.

As a result, some individuals may experience insomnia or other sleep-related disturbances when using Crataegus laevigata.
