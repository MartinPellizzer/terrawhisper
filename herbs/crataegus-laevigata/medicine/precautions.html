10 Precautions To Take When Using Crataegus Laevigata (Hawthorn)

Crataegus laevigata has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as monitoring blood pressure and renal function, especially in patients with hypertension or kidney disease.

Failure to do so may lead to increased blood pressure, edema, or even kidney damage.

It's also crucial to avoid using hawthorn with other medications that interact with blood pressure or blood thinners, such as warfarin.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Crataegus laevigata medicinally.

1. Store properly to maintain quality

When using Crataegus laevigata medicinally, it's important to take under medical supervision.

This is crucial because hawthorn can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, and may worsen existing heart conditions.

Additionally, hawthorn can cause dizziness, nausea, and irregular heartbeat in some individuals, highlighting the need for close monitoring by a healthcare professional to ensure safe and effective treatment.

When using Crataegus laevigata medicinally, it's important to avoid concomitant medication use due to its potential interactions with various drugs.

Hawthorn can alter blood pressure and cardiac rhythms, which may amplify or counteract the effects of medications for hypertension, heart failure, and other cardiovascular conditions.
