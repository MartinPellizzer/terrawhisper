9 Best Crataegus Laevigata Benefits

The plant also possesses anti-inflammatory properties that may help alleviate symptoms of conditions like arthritis, making it easier to perform routine tasks without pain.

1. Lowers cholesterol levels

Hypercholesterolemia, a condition characterized by elevated levels of cholesterol in the bloodstream, can lead to serious cardiovascular complications.

This metabolic disorder is often caused by an imbalance in dietary intake, genetic predispositions, or a sedentary lifestyle.

Individuals with a family history of heart disease or those following a high-fat diet are particularly susceptible to this condition.

The Crataegus laevigata plant has been studied for its potential to reduce cholesterol levels through its bioactive compounds.

By promoting liver function and enhancing lipid metabolism, this plant may help manage hypercholesterolemia in at-risk populations.

How this herb helps with lowers cholesterol levels?

Crataegus laevigata lowers cholesterol levels because it contains bioactive constituents such as alkaloids and flavonoids, which exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help reduce low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol, often referred to as "bad" cholesterol, by inhibiting its synthesis in the liver. Additionally, flavonoids in the plant may enhance the function of high-density lipoprotein (HDL) cholesterol, promoting healthier lipid metabolism. The presence of these natural compounds also supports the overall cardiovascular health by preventing oxidative stress.

Research suggests that regular consumption of Crataegus laevigata may contribute to maintaining optimal cholesterol levels.

Scientific Research

According to "Indian journal of biochemistry & biophysics", Crataegus laevigata (also known as Crataegus oxyacantha) lowers cholesterol levels in hyperlipidemic rats by causing a significant decrease in lipid deposits in the liver and aorta, as well as a remarkable reduction in cholesterol levels in low-density lipoprotein (LDL) and very low-density lipoprotein (VLDL) fractions.

Cardiovascular disease refers to a range of conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels, often leading to reduced blood flow and increased risk of heart attacks or strokes.

This condition is typically caused by the buildup of plaque in the arteries, a process known as atherosclerosis, which can be exacerbated by factors such as high cholesterol, high blood pressure, and poor dietary habits.

It commonly affects older adults, individuals with a family history of heart problems, and those who lead sedentary lifestyles.

The Crataegus laevigata plant, also known as the common hawthorn, has been studied for its potential to improve heart health by promoting better blood flow and reducing oxidative stress.

Its bioactive compounds may support cardiovascular function and contribute to the prevention and management of cardiovascular disease.

How this herb helps with improves heart health?

Crataegus laevigata improves heart health because it contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids and alkaloids.

These compounds help to strengthen cardiac muscle function and improve blood circulation. Flavonoids act as antioxidants, reducing oxidative stress and inflammation in the cardiovascular system. Alkaloids may support healthy heart rhythms by modulating neurotransmitter activity.

Overall, these natural compounds contribute to the plant's reputation as a cardiovascular support supplement.

Scientific Research

According to "Arzneimittel-Forschung", Crataegus laevigata improves heart health in a positive way by having a cardioprotective effect on the ischemic-reperfused heart, especially with a concentration of 0.05% as demonstrated by the study on the isolated, perfused working rat heart during ischemia and reperfusion.

3. Improves bone density

Osteoporosis, a condition characterized by reduced bone strength and increased fracture risk, is a major concern for many individuals.

This health issue, often referred to as brittle bone disease or low bone mass, results from an imbalance between bone resorption and formation.

It commonly affects postmenopausal women, older adults, and individuals with a sedentary lifestyle or poor nutrition.

The crataegus laevigata plant has been studied for its potential to enhance bone density by promoting mineralization and supporting osteoblastic activity.

Incorporating this plant into a balanced diet may offer a natural approach to mitigating the progression of osteoporosis.

How this herb helps with improves bone density?

Crataegus laevigata improves bone density because it contains bioactive constituents such as alkaloids and flavonoids, which have been shown to stimulate osteoblast activity and inhibit osteoclast-mediated bone resorption.

These compounds contribute to the regulation of bone remodeling processes, enhancing the formation of new bone tissue. Alkaloids in the plant may modulate inflammatory responses that negatively affect bone health. Flavonoids, known for their antioxidant properties, help reduce oxidative stress, which is linked to bone degradation.

Together, these constituents support the maintenance and strengthening of skeletal structure.

According to "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", Crataegus laevigata (also known as Seabuckthorn in the study) can meliorate bone metabolism in post-menopause and heighten the bone density.

4. Reduces blood clots

Blood Clot Formation, also known as thrombosis, is a condition where clots develop in blood vessels, potentially blocking blood flow.

This issue arises when the body's natural clotting process becomes overactive, leading to the formation of abnormal clots that can be dangerous if they travel to vital organs.

Thrombosis is commonly experienced by individuals with a sedentary lifestyle, smokers, or those with underlying conditions such as diabetes or hypertension.

The Crataegus laevigata plant has been studied for its potential to reduce blood clot formation by influencing platelet activity and improving circulation.

Its bioactive compounds may support cardiovascular health by preventing excessive clotting without compromising the body's essential clotting mechanisms.

How this herb helps with reduces blood clots?

Crataegus laevigata reduces blood clots because it contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids and alkaloids.

These compounds possess antiplatelet and anticoagulant properties that inhibit the aggregation of platelets in the bloodstream. Flavonoids help in reducing oxidative stress, which is a key factor in the formation of blood clots. Alkaloids contribute to the regulation of vascular tone and blood flow.

Together, these compounds support cardiovascular health by preventing the development of harmful clots.

Scientific Research

According to "Thrombosis research", Crataegus laevigata reduces blood clots, specifically showing significant antithrombotic activity at 24th hour with doses of 200 and 300 mg/kg.
