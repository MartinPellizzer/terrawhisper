What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Crataegus Laevigata (Hawthorn)?

Crataegus laevigata, also known as hawthorn, has health benefits such as improving cardiovascular health by reducing blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

The herb contains bioflavonoids, such as oligomeric proanthocyanidins (OPCs), which are responsible for its medicinal properties. The bioflavonoids in hawthorn can be found in various medicinal preparations, including extracts, tinctures, and capsules. However, high doses of hawthorn may cause side effects such as dizziness and digestive issues.

It is recommended to consult a healthcare professional before using hawthorn, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Crataegus laevigata.

What are the health benefits of Crataegus laevigata?

Crataegus laevigata has health benefits such as reducing symptoms of cardiovascular disease by improving blood vessel function and lowering blood pressure.

Its antioxidant properties may help protect against oxidative stress and inflammation, while its flavonoids may have anti-cancer properties.

Additionally, hawthorn is believed to have antidiabetic effects, potentially aiding in glucose metabolism and insulin sensitivity.

What are the active constituents of Crataegus laevigata?

The health benefits of Crataegus laevigata comes from its active constituents, such as flavonoids, triterpenic acids, and oligomeric procyanidins.

Flavonoids like quercetin and epicatechin have antioxidant properties, while triterpenes like ursolic acid exhibit anti-inflammatory effects.

Oligomeric procyanidins, found in the fruit and leaves, have been shown to improve blood flow to the heart and reduce blood pressure, making hawthorn a popular herbal remedy for cardiovascular health.

What are the medicinal preparations of Crataegus laevigata?

Crataegus laevigata has different medicinal preparations, such as dried flowers and leaves, which are used to treat cardiovascular issues like high blood pressure, coronary heart disease, and congestive heart failure.

Crataegus extract is often consumed as capsules or tablets to improve cardiac function and reduce symptoms of angina.

Topical applications, like ointments and tinctures, may be used for skin conditions like eczema and wounds, while Hawthorn tea is a popular remedy for digestive issues and stress relief.

What are the possible side effect of using Crataegus laevigata improperly?

Improper use of Crataegus laevigata increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as nausea, diarrhea, stomach cramps, and headache.

Long-term consumption may lead to kidney or liver damage, interactions with blood thinners, and decreased effectiveness of diabetes medications. In rare cases, hawthorn can cause allergic reactions, respiratory issues, and cardiovascular complications like arrhythmias and hypotension.

It may also interact with certain antidepressants, causing serotonin syndrome or worsen symptoms in individuals with anxiety disorders.

What precautions to take when using Crataegus laevigata medicinally?

Before using Crataegus laevigata for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Additionally, pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise caution, as hawthorn may stimulate uterine contractions or affect lactation.
