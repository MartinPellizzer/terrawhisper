Oxycedrus Juniper (Juniperus oxycedrus) Compounds

The primary compounds in Oxycedrus Juniper include essential oil, terpenoids, tannins, flavonoids, phenolic acids, coumarin, glycosides, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects.

Oxycedrus Juniper (Juniperus oxycedrus) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Oxycedrus Juniper contains compounds like pinene and limonene, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health and aid digestion. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and berries.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key group of active compounds in Oxycedrus Juniper. These compounds contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are responsible for the plant's distinctive aroma and are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their health benefits.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Oxycedrus Juniper (Juniperus oxycedrus) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in reducing inflammation and treating digestive issues. It also helps in preserving the plant's medicinal qualities by stabilizing active components.

4. Flavonoid
