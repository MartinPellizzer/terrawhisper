Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans) Compounds

The primary compounds in nutmeg include essential oil, which gives it its aromatic flavor, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids act as antioxidants, while coumarin has anticoagulant effects. Glycosides, saponins, and alkaloids also play roles in its therapeutic actions.

Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans) is a key active compound, containing compounds like myristicin and elemicin. It is used in herbal medicine for its warming properties and potential digestive and antimicrobial effects. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the nutmeg seed.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and have shown potential anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties. These compounds are often responsible for the therapeutic effects attributed to nutmeg in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory health.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to aid in reducing inflammation and digestive issues. Tannins in nutmeg may also support skin health and have antimicrobial effects.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound is also associated with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has mild anticoagulant properties. Coumarin is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by interacting with bodily systems to support digestive and circulatory health. Glycosides in nutmeg are also known for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may also support digestive health by aiding in the emulsification of fats.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in the medicinal plant Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). It contributes to the plant's psychoactive and stimulant properties, though its role in traditional herbal medicine is less emphasized compared to other constituents like essential oils and sesquiterpenoids.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. While not the primary active compound in nutmeg, anthocyanin supports overall health by enhancing cellular protection.
