Botanical Identification of Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans)

To identify Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Nutmeg, or Myristica fragrans, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Myristicaceae, genus Myristica, and species Myristica fragrans. This classification places it among flowering plants with complex structures and a wide distribution in tropical regions.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Nutmeg is Myristica fragrans, a classification established by the botanist Linnaeus in 1753. This name is widely recognized in botanical literature. It has several synonyms, including Myristica fragrans Hume, reflecting historical taxonomic revisions. Common names such as nutmeg and mace are used globally, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Nutmeg is an evergreen tree with a straight trunk and dense foliage. Its root system is fibrous and extensive, anchoring it firmly in the soil. The inflorescence is a panicle, with small white flowers that are fragrant and bisexual. The fruit is a drupe, turning from green to yellow, enclosing a single seed called a nutmeg.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans) can be distinguished by its heart-shaped, thick, and aromatic seeds with a rough surface. The outer covering, or aril, is bright red and fleshy. The plant also has broad, leathery leaves and produces small white flowers. These features help differentiate it from similar species like mace or other Myristica varieties.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Nutmeg is native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and warm temperatures. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Nutmeg trees bloom between February and April, producing small green flowers. Seed formation occurs six months after flowering, resulting in round, red fruits. Harvesting occurs when the fruit turns from red to yellow, typically 6 to 8 months after flowering, which is key for identifying mature nutmegs.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Nutmeg (Myristica fragrans) is closely related to species like Myristica malabarica and Myristica dioica. It belongs to the genus Myristica within the family Myristicaceae. The family is characterized by aromatic, evergreen trees with thick, waxy leaves and fragrant, oily seeds.
