Apium humile

Apium humile, also known as wild celery or small celery, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and native to Europe and North Africa.

The plant's leaves, seeds, and stems have been used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The health benefits of Apium humile include its ability to aid digestion, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma.

Apium humile is often prepared as a herbal tea or used in infusions, with some recipes also incorporating the plant's seeds or roots into salves and ointments for topical applications.
