What to know about Erythronium americanum (trout lily) before using it medicinally

Trout lily, scientifically known as Erythronium americanum, is a revered herb that has been prized for its numerous health benefits.

Rich in antioxidants and flavonoids, the leaves and flowers of trout lily have been shown to improve respiratory function, reduce inflammation, and boost the immune system. In the kitchen, trout lily is often used as a flavoring agent in soups, stews, and salads, adding a subtle onion-like flavor to dishes. Horticulturally, trout lily is a low-maintenance perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Botanically, the plant's distinctive yellow flowers and mottled leaves have been used for centuries in traditional medicine, while historical records dating back to the 17th century attest to its use by Native American tribes for various ailments.
