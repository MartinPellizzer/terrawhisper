Ligularia cymbulifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia cymbulifera?

Ligularia cymbulifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is specifically categorized under the genus Ligularia, and its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, cymbulifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia cymbulifera has large, broadly ovate to cordate leaves with coarsely serrated margins and a distinctively textured, somewhat rugose surface. The foliage is characterized by prominent venation that creates a quilted appearance across the leaf blade. These leaves are arranged oppositely along sturdy, upright stems that support the plant's overall structure. The inflorescence consists of bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk, typically arranged in terminal clusters. This morphological structure allows the plant to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
