Pedicularis vialii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis vialii has erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 40 centimeters and are topped by dense, terminal spikes of tubular, bright crimson or deep pink flowers. The inflorescence consists of numerous small, irregular blossoms that create a striking, brush-like appearance. Below the flower spike, the plant features basal leaves that are long, lanceolate, and deeply lobed or pinnatifid. The stem leaves are smaller and more sparsely arranged, often becoming narrow or bract-like as they ascend. This morphological structure allows the plant to stand prominently amidst rocky alpine vegetation.

Its distribution is closely tied to specific mountain ecosystems characterized by cool temperatures and seasonal snowmelt.
