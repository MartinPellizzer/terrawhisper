What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis vialii?

Pedicularis vialii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary path follows the order Lamiales, placing it within the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, the plant is situated in the genus Pedicularis, with its specific designation being the species vialii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis vialii has erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 40 centimeters and are topped by dense, terminal spikes of tubular, bright crimson or deep pink flowers.

The inflorescence consists of numerous small, irregular blossoms that create a striking, brush-like appearance. Below the flower spike, the plant features basal leaves that are long, lanceolate, and deeply lobed or pinnatifid. The stem leaves are smaller and more sparsely arranged, often becoming narrow or bract-like as they ascend.

This morphological structure allows the plant to stand prominently amidst rocky alpine vegetation.

      Pedicularis vialii is prepared as a decoction by boiling the dried aerial parts in water, as a simple infusion of the leaves and stems, or as a concentrated tincture using ethanol. Therefore, it is important to handle the plant with caution and keep it away from children or pets.
