Laxa Anthemis

Anthemis laxa

Laxa Anthemis (Anthemis laxa) is a member of the Compositae family, traditionally used in various regions for its digestive aid, antispasmodic, and mild sedative properties. The plant parts, including leaves and flowers, have been historically utilized in herbal preparations.

Botanical Identification

Laxa Anthemis, scientifically known as Anthemis laxa, belongs to the Asteraceae family. Commonly called "Laxa" in some regions, it is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. The plant has alternate leaves and yellow flowers, with a taproot system. It is also referred to as "Lesser Chamomile" in herbal texts.

Laxa Anthemis Identification.

Active Compounds

Laxa Anthemis contains essential oil, flavonoids, and terpenoids as its main active compounds. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The essential oil provides aromatic properties, while flavonoids and terpenoids contribute to its therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive problems and as a calming tea.

Essential oil: Used for skin conditions and as a natural insect repellent.

Safety Profile

Laxa Anthemis is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Individuals with allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family should exercise caution.
