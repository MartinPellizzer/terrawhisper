Not only does it possess potent anti-inflammatory and antiseptic compounds that can alleviate symptoms of colds, flu, and other minor ailments, but it also contains flavonoids and terpenes that have been shown to lower blood pressure and reduce the risk of heart disease. In the culinary world, yarrow is often used as a garnish or added to soups, salads, and teas for its mild, slightly bitter flavor. As an ornamental plant, yarrow is prized for its delicate white blooms and feathery foliage, making it a popular choice for cottage gardens and wildflower arrangements.

With its rich botanical history dating back thousands of years, yarrow has been referenced in ancient Greek, Roman, and Chinese medicinal texts as a treatment for everything from wounds to menstrual cramps.
