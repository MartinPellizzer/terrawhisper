What to know about Rheum officinale before using it medicinally

Rheum officinale, commonly known as turkey rhubarb, is a medicinal herb used to treat various health conditions, including dysentery, constipation, and diarrhea.

The herb has been found to possess several health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. Its main therapeutic properties include anthelmintic, antiseptic, and purgative actions, making it a valuable remedy for gastrointestinal issues. Rheum officinale contains bioactive compounds such as emodin, rhein, and anthraquinones, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Rheum officinale is often used to make a tea or decoction, which is a popular herbal preparation for treating digestive problems.
