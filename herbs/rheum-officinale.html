Rhubarb (Rheum officinale)

Rhubarb (Rheum officinale) is a member of the Rheaceae family, native to China, Korea, and Japan. Traditionally, its rhizomes, roots, and stems have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, bitter, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, european herbal medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.
