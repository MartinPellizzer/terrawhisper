Tylophora arenicola

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tylophora arenicola has a perennial climbing habit characterized by slender, twining stems and opposite, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically exhibits a smooth texture and a somewhat leathery consistency depending on the maturity of the specimen. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The fruit develops into a characteristic follicle that contains numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds equipped with a coma of fine hairs. These morphological features are consistent with other members of the Apocynaceae family.
