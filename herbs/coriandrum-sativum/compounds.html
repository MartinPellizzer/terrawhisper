Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum) Compounds

Cilantro contains several primary compounds, including essential oil, which gives it its aromatic properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant effects. Coumarin and terpenoids are also present, offering potential anti-inflammatory benefits. Saponins, tannins, and glycosides support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum) contains bioactive compounds like linalool and terpinolene. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and may have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Essential oil is a key component in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory support.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, which may support anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects. These compounds are also associated with potential neuroprotective benefits. Flavonoids in cilantro may aid in reducing oxidative stress and supporting overall health.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has mild anticoagulant properties. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and is often used in herbal formulations for its soothing effects.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and immune support. This compound helps neutralize free radicals and may aid in reducing oxidative stress in the body.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum). They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and have demonstrated anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds may support digestive health and help reduce oxidative stress in the body.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has mild surfactant properties. Saponin may support digestive health and has potential anti-inflammatory effects. It is one of several bioactive compounds in cilantro that contribute to its traditional medicinal uses.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and promoting gut integrity. Tannins in cilantro also have antioxidant effects, potentially aiding in detoxification and immune support.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and may have mild antimicrobial effects. Glycosides in cilantro are often associated with its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive support and as a mild tonic.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though specific alkaloids in cilantro are less well-studied compared to other compounds. Research suggests it may support digestive health and has potential anti-inflammatory effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports overall health by enhancing cellular protection and may aid in inflammatory conditions.
