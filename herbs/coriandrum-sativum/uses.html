Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum) Uses

Cilantro is commonly used to treat digestive issues such as nausea, indigestion, and stomach cramps. It helps alleviate gastrointestinal gas and constipation by promoting healthy digestion. Its anti-inflammatory properties may also support respiratory health, making it useful for coughs and respiratory infections.

Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum) Monograph

1. Cough

Cilantro is used in herbal medicine to alleviate cough symptoms due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture. Its antimicrobial effects may also support respiratory health.

2. Respiratory infections

Cilantro has been traditionally used to support respiratory health by helping to reduce congestion and soothe inflammation. Its antimicrobial and expectorant properties may aid in clearing mucus and fighting infections. It is often used in teas or steam inhalations to relieve symptoms of colds, coughs, and bronchitis.

3. Digestive upset

Cilantro is used to alleviate digestive upset by stimulating bile production and improving digestion. It helps relieve bloating, nausea, and indigestion. Its mild antispasmodic properties can ease stomach cramps. Regular consumption may support overall digestive health.

4. Gastrointestinal gas

Cilantro is used to alleviate gastrointestinal gas by promoting digestion and reducing bloating. Its compounds help relax digestive tract muscles, easing discomfort. It is often recommended for digestive issues like indigestion and flatulence. Regular use may support overall gut health.

5. Nausea

Cilantro is used to alleviate nausea due to its carminative and anti-emetic properties. It helps settle the stomach and reduce vomiting, often used in small doses. It is particularly effective for motion sickness and morning sickness, though results may vary among individuals.

6. Constipation

Cilantro contains compounds that promote digestive health and can help alleviate constipation. Its mild laxative properties aid in stimulating bowel movements. Regular consumption of cilantro in moderate amounts may support regularity and ease digestive discomfort. It is often used in traditional herbal remedies for mild digestive issues.

7. Indigestion

Cilantro is used to alleviate indigestion due to its carminative and digestive-stimulating properties. It helps reduce bloating and gas by promoting the movement of food through the digestive tract. Its mild peppery flavor also aids in soothing the stomach and improving overall digestion.

8. Stomach cramps

Cilantro is used to alleviate stomach cramps due to its carminative properties, which help expel gas and reduce bloating. It also has mild antispasmodic effects that can ease muscle contractions in the digestive tract. Often prepared as a tea or added to meals, cilantro supports digestion and relieves discomfort associated with indigestion or gas.

9. Digestive issues

Cilantro supports digestive health by stimulating bile production and improving nutrient absorption. It can alleviate bloating, indigestion, and gas when consumed in teas or infused oils. Its antimicrobial properties also help balance gut flora, promoting overall digestive wellness.

10. Stomach pain

Cilantro is used to alleviate stomach pain due to its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties. It helps reduce gas, bloating, and cramping by soothing the digestive tract. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or infused oil to ease gastrointestinal discomfort. Its mild nature makes it suitable for regular use in managing mild to moderate stomach issues.
