Leptopus chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Leptopus chinensis?

Leptopus chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually falling under the order Malpighiales. At the more specific levels of its classification, this plant is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae and the genus Leptopus, ultimately identifying as the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leptopus chinensis has an erect, woody shrub structure reaching heights of up to two meters with slender, branching stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length. The foliage typically features entire margins and an acute apex, presenting a smooth or slightly textured surface. Small, inconspicuous white or cream-colored flowers emerge in terminal or axillary clusters during the blooming season. These floral structures are supported by thin pedicels and eventually produce small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature. The overall morphology is characterized by a delicate, spreading habit that allows it to thrive in diverse forest understories.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 3beta-O-trans-coumaroylbetulinic acid and betulinic acid. These specific triterpenoids are known for their diverse biological activities and therapeutic potential. Betulinic acid is frequently studied for its significant anti-tumor and anti-inflammatory properties. The derivative 3beta-O-trans-coumaroylbetulinic acid also contributes to the plant's chemical complexity and medicinal value. Together, these substances represent key components used in pharmacological research.

As discussed in a study, the chemical constituents of the ethyl ether extract of Leptopus chinensis were investigated through chromatography and identified using spectra data and physicochemical characters. Three specific compounds were successfully isolated from the plant. These identified compounds are friedelane-2alpha, 3beta-diol (1), betulinic acid (2), and 3beta-O-trans-coumaroylbetulinic acid (3). This research represents the first time these 3 compounds have been isolated from Leptopus chinensis.
