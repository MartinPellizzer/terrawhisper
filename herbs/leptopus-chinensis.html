What's the taxonomical classification of Leptopus chinensis?

Leptopus chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually falling under the order Malpighiales. At the more specific levels of its classification, this plant is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae and the genus Leptopus, ultimately identifying as the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leptopus chinensis has an erect, woody shrub structure reaching heights of up to two meters with slender, branching stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length. Small, inconspicuous white or cream-colored flowers emerge in terminal or axillary clusters during the blooming season. These floral structures are supported by thin pedicels and eventually produce small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature.
