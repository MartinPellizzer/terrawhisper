Mentha longifolia var. flaccida

Mentha longifolia var. flaccida, also known as Egyptian pennyroyal, is a variety of the mint plant native to Egypt and the Middle East.

This herb has been used for its medicinal properties, including its ability to act as an antispasmodic and a digestive aid, due to its high content of carvone and limonene.

The health benefits of Mentha longifolia var. flaccida include relief from menstrual cramps, digestive issues, and anxiety, as well as its potential to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria and fungi.

Herbal preparations of Mentha longifolia var. flaccida often include essential oils, teas, and infusions, which can be used to promote relaxation and soothe digestive issues.

Related Study

According to "PloS one", mentha longifolia var. flaccida extracts obtained through certain methods such as HDC, HDM, and SDK showed antimicrobial activity, specifically against Gram-negative bacteria Shigella dysenteriae, with the lowest minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) value of 1000 μg/mL.
