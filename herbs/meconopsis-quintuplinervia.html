What's the taxonomical classification of Meconopsis quintuplinervia?

Meconopsis quintuplinervia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Ranunculales. It is a member of the family Papaveraceae, specifically falling under the genus Meconopsis, with its precise biological identity defined by the species quintuplinervia.

What are the pharmacological activities of Meconopsis quintuplinervia?

As discussed in a study, the active ingredients of analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties from Meconopsis quintuplinervia were investigated. Five compounds were obtained through chromatography, including O-methylflavinanthin (I), flavinanthin (II), tricin (III), quercitriin (IV), and methyl linoleate (V). The chemical structure of these compounds was elucidated using MS and NMR spectroscopy methods. Notably, compound I is obtained from the plant for the first time.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds kaempferol, luteolin, naringenin, quercetin, and catechin. These specific flavonoids are recognized for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Each individual compound plays a unique role in neutralizing free radicals within the body. Research suggests that these substances may also assist in supporting cardiovascular health and immune function. By working synergistically, these phytochemicals provide a comprehensive profile of therapeutic potential.

As discussed in a study, Meconopsis quintuplinervia contains a total phenolic content of 90.54 ± 0.91 mg/g and a total flavonoid content of 44.48 ± 0.43 mg/g. LC-MS/MS analysis identified taxifolin as the predominant constituent at approximately 2.39%. The plant contains several flavonoids including catechin, isorhamnetin, kaempferol, luteolin, naringenin, nobiletin, quercetin, and taxifolin. These medicinal compounds interact with therapeutic targets by downregulating pro-inflammatory cytokines such as TNF-α, IL-6, and IL-1β. The extract also demonstrates the ability to reduce ROS levels in H2O2-induced B16-F10 and RAW264.7 cells.
