What to know about Myrica cerifera (bayberry) before using it medicinally

Bayberry, scientifically known as Myrica cerifera, is a herb that has been revered for its numerous health benefits.

Rich in antioxidants and antimicrobial properties, it is believed to improve cardiovascular health, reduce inflammation, and even exhibit antiviral and antibacterial effects. In the culinary world, bayberry is often used to flavor sauces, marinades, and baked goods, particularly in traditional Mediterranean and European cuisine. As a horticultural plant, bayberry is relatively low-maintenance and can thrive in a variety of climates, making it a popular choice for home gardeners.

With its rich history dating back to ancient civilizations such as the Aztecs and Native Americans, who used it for medicinal and spiritual purposes, bayberry has been a staple herb for centuries, with references found in numerous historical texts and folklore.
