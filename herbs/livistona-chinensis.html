Livistona chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Arecales, it is placed within the family Arecaceae. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified under the genus Livistona and is distinguished as the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Livistona chinensis has a solitary, stout trunk that can reach heights of up to 10 meters and is topped by a dense crown of large, arching, pinnate leaves measuring up to 3 meters in length. The individual leaflets are long, narrow, and lanceolate, characterized by a distinctively waxy, dark green surface. A defining feature of the foliage is the presence of fine, hair-like fibers that emerge from the margins of the leaflets, creating a fringed appearance. The plant produces large, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. These flowers eventually develop into fleshy, ovoid fruits that turn a deep blue or black when fully ripe.
