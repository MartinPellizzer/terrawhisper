What are the pharmacological activities of Chrysosplenium axillare?

This plant has anti-aging, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to neutralize free radicals, which helps prevent oxidative stress in cellular structures. These properties contribute to a significant anti-aging effect by protecting tissues from premature degradation. Additionally, the plant exhibits potent capabilities in inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Such diverse medicinal actions make it a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, the XiZang medicinal plant Chrysosplenium axillare Maxim. contains flavonol glycosides known as Chrysosplenosides I and A, also referred to as CA1 and CA2. These two compounds exhibit potent anti-aging effects on intestinal stem cells by restraining excessive proliferation to preserve intestinal homeostasis. In aged mouse intestinal organoids, CA1 and CA2 enhance growth and budding, which indicates improved regenerative capacity. The pharmacological activity of CA1 and CA2 is achieved through the activation of the peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor-gamma (PPARγ) and the concurrent inhibition of the epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) signaling pathways. These findings position CA1 and CA2 as promising candidates for ameliorating intestinal stem cell aging.
