Linum amurense

What's the taxonomical classification of Linum amurense?

Linum amurense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Malpighiales and the family Linaceae. Finally, its specific identification is defined by the genus Linum and the species name amurense.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Linum amurense targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its therapeutic effects. These bioactive compounds primarily interact with the NF-kappaB signaling pathway to inhibit the expression of genes responsible for systemic inflammation. Additionally, the plant's phytochemicals can modulate various matrix metalloproteinases, which helps in regulating tissue remodeling and reducing oxidative stress. By interacting with these specific enzymatic and signaling proteins, the plant demonstrates significant potential for treating inflammatory diseases and related metabolic disturbances.
