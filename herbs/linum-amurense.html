Linum amurense

What's the taxonomical classification of Linum amurense?

Linum amurense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Malpighiales and the family Linaceae. Finally, its specific identification is defined by the genus Linum and the species name amurense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Linum amurense has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and feature small, narrow, lanceolate leaves arranged alternately along the stalk. The foliage is characterized by a fine, delicate texture with a light green coloration. Small, five-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying a pale blue or lavender hue. These blossoms are supported by thin pedicels that contribute to the plant's airy appearance. The plant's overall structure is quite lightweight, allowing it to sway easily in gentle breezes.
