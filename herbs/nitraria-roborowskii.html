Nitraria roborowskii

What's the taxonomical classification of Nitraria roborowskii?

Nitraria roborowskii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Sapindales and the family Nitrariaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Nitraria and the species roborowskii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nitraria roborowskii has small, simple, lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and paler underneath.

These leaves are arranged alternately along woody stems that can develop a shrubby or small tree-like growth habit. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes. These fruits are often reddish or yellowish when ripe and serve as a key identifying feature of the species.

The overall structure of the plant is compact and adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
