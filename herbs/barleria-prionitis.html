Barleria prionitis

What's the taxonomical classification of Barleria prionitis?

Barleria prionitis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Lamiales. This plant is a representative of the family Acanthaceae, specifically falling under the genus Barleria, with the specific epithet prionitis denoting its unique species identity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Barleria prionitis contains bioactive compounds such as barlerin, barleric acid, barleric acid methyl ester, and various flavonoids like quercetin and kaempferol. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The presence of triterpenoids specifically contributes to its effectiveness in treating skin ailments and reducing swelling. Additionally, the alkaloids found within its tissues support its traditional use in managing digestive issues and fever. These diverse medicinal elements make the plant a valuable subject in ethnobotanical research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Barleria prionitis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, wounds, inflammation, respiratory ailments, and various digestive disorders. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to heal ulcers and soothe skin irritations through topical applications. In traditional medicine, extracts from the plant are often used to alleviate symptoms of asthma and coughs. It also serves as a remedy for abdominal pains and helps manage symptoms related to intestinal issues. The various parts of the shrub contribute to its wide range of therapeutic uses in folk medicine.
