Bioactive Constituents

Citrus limon limonene is a key constituent of the lemon herb, known for its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Limonene, a volatile terpene, is responsible for the characteristic aroma of lemons and has been studied for its potential to support digestive health and reduce stress. In addition to limonene, citrus limon is rich in vitamin C, a powerful antioxidant that boosts the immune system and promotes skin health by neutralizing free radicals.

Citric acid, another important component, aids in digestion and helps in the absorption of minerals like iron and calcium. Naringenin, a flavonoid found in citrus fruits, has shown promise in reducing inflammation and improving insulin sensitivity, making it beneficial for metabolic health.

Together, these medicinal constituents contribute to the therapeutic value of citrus limon, supporting overall well-being and offering potential health benefits when consumed as part of a balanced diet.

Side Effects of citrus limon

Citrus limon may cause allergic reactions in individuals who are sensitive to citrus fruits, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, or even anaphylaxis in severe cases.

It can also cause skin irritation, particularly when applied topically, resulting in redness, burning, or rashes due to its high concentration of citric acid and essential oils. Prolonged consumption of citrus limon may lead to tooth erosion because the acidic nature of the herb can wear down tooth enamel over time.

Additionally, it might trigger digestive upset, including symptoms like bloating, gas, or discomfort, as it can be harsh on the stomach lining. Furthermore, citrus limon can induce nausea, stomach cramps, or acid reflux in some people, especially when consumed in large amounts or on an empty stomach, due to its stimulating effect on gastric secretions.

These side effects highlight the importance of moderation and caution when using citrus limon, particularly for individuals with pre-existing conditions or sensitivities.
