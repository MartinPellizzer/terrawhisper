Lemon (Citrus limon)

Lemon (Citrus limon) is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to Southern Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and resin have been used for infusions, decoctions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with dorsiventral coloration, upper surface pale green, lower surface darker green, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
White flowers arranged in clusters, actinomorphic, with five petals and five sepals, bearing yellow stamens and a superior ovary.
Stems
Cylindrical, herbaceous, quadrangular, hairy, opposite branching, adventitious root nodes present, persistent stipules.
