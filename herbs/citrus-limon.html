Citrus Limon

Citrus limon, commonly known as lemons, is a small, evergreen tree native to Asia and widely cultivated for its fruit and essential oil.

This herb is valued for its numerous health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and boosting the immune system.

Medicinal Parts of citrus limon

Citrus limon has many medicinal parts, such as the peel, leaves, flowers, and fruit.

The peel, or rind, contains compounds like limonene and limonin, which have been shown to have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The leaves of the citrus limon plant contain flavonoids like naringenin and hesperidin, which have been used to treat conditions such as high blood pressure and fever.

The flowers of the citrus limon plant are also used in traditional medicine, containing essential oils like limonene and beta-pinene, which have been shown to have antiseptic and expectorant properties.

Possible Side Effects of citrus limon

Citrus limon can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea and diarrhea, due to its high acidity and potential interaction with certain medications.

Excessive consumption may also lead to heartburn, acid reflux, and stomach pain in some individuals.

Additionally, people with kidney stones or certain kidney disorders should use caution when consuming citrus limon, as it may exacerbate their condition.

In rare cases, it may also cause an allergic reaction, characterized by symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.
