1. Causes allergic reactions

Rhus glabra causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of urushiol oil, a potent allergen found in its sap and leaves. When ingested, handled, or even simply being nearby, this oil can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, inhaling the pollen or dust from Rhus glabra plants may also cause allergic reactions in susceptible persons, exacerbating respiratory issues like asthma.

2. Induces severe skin irritation

Rhus glabra induces severe skin irritation due to its high concentration of urushiol, a potent allergen.

This oil is responsible for triggering an immune response in some individuals, leading to inflammation, redness, and blistering of the skin upon contact.

As a result, people who come into contact with Rhus glabra may experience intense discomfort, pain, and potentially even long-term scarring.

3. Triggers respiratory distress

Rhus glabra triggers respiratory distress due to its allergenic properties.

The plant's sap and resin contain irritating compounds that can cause inflammation in the lungs and airways when inhaled, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the pollen from Rhus glabra plants can also exacerbate respiratory issues in individuals with existing conditions like asthma.

4. Increases blood pressure

Rhus glabra increases blood pressure due to its stimulatory effects on the body.

The alkaloids present in this herb constrict blood vessels and stimulate the heart to beat faster and stronger, leading to an increase in blood pressure.

Additionally, Rhus glabra may also cause the release of stress hormones such as epinephrine and norepinephrine, which can further exacerbate hypertension.

5. Produces excessive sweating

Rhus glabra produces excessive sweating due to its warming and drying properties.

The herb contains volatile oils that can stimulate the sweat glands, causing an increase in perspiration. Additionally, Rhus glabra's bitter principles can irritate the skin and mucous membranes, leading to excessive sweating as a way for the body to rid itself of this irritation.

This side effect is often reported by individuals who use Rhus glabra in traditional medicine or as a herbal remedy.

6. Increases heart rate

Rhus glabra increases heart rate due to its stimulant properties.

The plant's extracts contain alkaloids and volatile oils that can cause an increase in cardiac output by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system. This leads to an elevated heart rate, often accompanied by other symptoms such as palpitations, tremors, and anxiety.

As Rhus glabra is known for its medicinal and herbal uses, it is essential to understand these potential effects on cardiovascular activity.

7. Causes stomach upset

Rhus glabra causes stomach upset due to its ability to stimulate digestive enzymes and alter gut flora.

The compound rhusglaberine, a unique flavonoid found in the plant, has been shown to increase gut motility and secretion of stomach acids, leading to discomfort, nausea, and possibly diarrhea or abdominal pain.

Additionally, smooth sumac's tannins may bind to proteins in the digestive system, further contributing to gastrointestinal irritation.

8. Leads to liver damage

Rhus glabra leads to liver damage due to its high concentration of anthraquinones and other bioactive compounds.

These substances can cause toxic reactions in the liver, leading to inflammation and scarring.

Repeated exposure to Rhus glabra may exacerbate this effect, causing chronic liver damage and potential long-term health consequences.

9. Elicits anaphylaxis symptoms

Rhus glabra elicits anaphylaxis symptoms due to its high content of urushiol oil, a potent allergen.

When in contact with the skin or ingested, urushiol can trigger a severe allergic reaction, causing symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling. In rare cases, anaphylaxis can occur, characterized by difficulty breathing, rapid heartbeat, and a sudden drop in blood pressure.

The severity of these reactions is often dependent on individual sensitivity to the allergen.

10. Activates muscle spasms

Rhus glabra activates muscle spasms due to its high concentration of tannins and anthraquinones.

These compounds can stimulate the nervous system, leading to increased muscle activity and contractions.

Additionally, the herb's ability to affect blood circulation may contribute to the development of muscle spasms by altering the normal flow of oxygenated blood to muscles.
