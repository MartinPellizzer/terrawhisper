Primula obconica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula obconica has a basal rosette of oblanceolate to spatulate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long and feature dentate or serrated margins. The leaf surfaces can range from glabrous to slightly pubescent, often possessing a distinctively wrinkled or rugose texture. Its flowering stalks are relatively short and terminate in umbels composed of several small, tubular flowers. These blossoms display a range of colors, most commonly pale pink to lavender, with a distinct yellow eye at the center of the corolla. The plant's overall structure remains low to the ground, adapted to the damp, shaded environments it inhabits.
