Lysimachia phyllocephala

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia phyllocephala has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are arranged in dense, overlapping whorls along the stem, giving the plant its characteristic phyllocephalous appearance. These leaves are generally lanceolate to linear in shape with entire margins and acute tips. The inflorescence consists of small, bright yellow flowers with five petals that emerge from the leaf axils. Each flower features a central cluster of stamens that contrast against the vibrant petal color. The root system is composed of slender rhizomes that allow the plant to spread slowly through its habitat.
