Alnus japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Alnus japonica?

Alnus japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Fagales and the family Betulaceae, which characterizes its structural and reproductive features. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Alnus, with its specific designation being the species japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alnus japonica has deciduous, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, featuring serrated margins and an acute apex. The leaf surfaces are typically dark green and glossy on the upper side, while the undersides present a paler, matte appearance. Its bark is greyish-brown and becomes deeply fissured as the tree matures into a woody shrub or small tree. The plant produces small, cone-like woody structures known as strobiles that contain numerous tiny seeds. Branching patterns are often dense, contributing to a multi-stemmed growth habit in many environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Within its native range, the species is commonly found in mountainous areas and coastal zones. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Alnus japonica targets cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. The bioactive compounds found in its leaves and bark, such as tannins and flavonoids, act by inhibiting the enzymatic pathways responsible for inflammation. These secondary metabolites specifically suppress the production of prostaglandins and nitric oxide, which are key mediators in pain and swelling. Furthermore, the plant's polyphenolic constituents interact with signaling proteins to modulate the immune response and reduce oxidative stress. Through these multiple protein interactions, the species demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alnus japonica?

Alnus japonica has therapeutic properties that include astringent, diuretic, hemostatic, and anti-inflammatory actions. The plant contains bioactive compounds that help shrink tissues and reduce swelling when applied topically or ingested. Its diuretic effects assist the body in flushing out excess fluids and toxins through the renal system. Additionally, the hemostatic qualities allow it to effectively stem bleeding by promoting blood coagulation. These combined medicinal functions make the species a significant subject in traditional herbal treatments.
