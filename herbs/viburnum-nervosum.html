Viburnum nervosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum nervosum?

Viburnum nervosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Dipsacales. Its taxonomic journey continues through the family Viburnaceae, where it is categorized under the genus Viburnum, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species nervosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum nervosum has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, measuring between 3 and 8 centimeters in length, and feature prominent, deeply impressed veins on their upper surfaces. The leaf margins are characterized by fine, serrated teeth that become more pronounced toward the apex. These leaves are often a dark, lustrous green during the growing season and transition to vibrant shades of red or purple in the autumn. The plant's woody stems are somewhat spreading and can develop a grayish bark as the shrub matures. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in flat-topped cymes during the late spring or early summer.
