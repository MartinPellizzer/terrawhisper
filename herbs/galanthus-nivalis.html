Snowdrop

Galanthus nivalis

Snowdrop (Galanthus nivalis) is a small, bulbous plant known for its delicate white flowers that bloom in early spring, often among the last plants to emerge after winter. It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat ailments such as respiratory infections and joint pain, though its primary medicinal use is in the treatment of respiratory conditions like coughs and bronchitis.

Botanical Identification

Snowdrop, scientifically known as Galanthus nivalis, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family. Commonly called snowdrop in English, it is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia. It features white, bell-shaped flowers and is often referenced in herbal literature under various regional names.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Asparagales
  • Family: Amaryllidaceae
  • Genus: Galanthus
  • Species: Galanthus nivalis

Safety Profile

Snowdrop (Galanthus nivalis) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it contains toxic alkaloids that can be harmful in large amounts. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by individuals with known allergies to related plants. Always consult a qualified herbalist or healthcare provider before use.
