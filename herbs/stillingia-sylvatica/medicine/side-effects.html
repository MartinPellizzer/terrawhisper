1. Causes allergic reactions

Stillingia sylvatica causes allergic reactions due to its potential to stimulate an immune response in some individuals.

The allergenic compounds present in the plant's sap or leaves may trigger an inflammatory reaction in sensitive people, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, redness, and itching.

2. Triggers respiratory issues

Stillingia sylvatica triggers respiratory issues due to its ability to stimulate the respiratory system and increase mucus production.

The plant's volatile oils can cause constriction of the airways, leading to coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath in some individuals.

Additionally, Stillingia sylvatica's expectorant properties may exacerbate existing respiratory conditions, such as asthma or chronic bronchitis, causing further discomfort and inflammation.

3. Increases blood pressure

Stillingia sylvatica increases blood pressure due to its stimulatory effects on the cardiovascular system.

The plant's active compounds, such as flavonoids and terpenoids, can cause increased heart rate and cardiac output, leading to a rise in blood pressure. Additionally, Stillingia sylvatica may also stimulate the release of catecholamines, which are hormones that can further increase blood pressure.

These effects can be especially pronounced in individuals who already have hypertension or cardiovascular disease.

4. Reduces fertility rates

Stillingia sylvatica reduces fertility rates due to its ability to stimulate the ovaries and uterus, potentially disrupting the normal menstrual cycle.

The herb's compounds may also alter hormone levels, particularly estrogen, which plays a crucial role in female reproductive health.

As a result, prolonged use of Stillingia sylvatica could lead to reduced chances of conception or difficulties becoming pregnant.

5. Worsens skin conditions

Stillingia sylvatica worsens skin conditions due to its irritating properties that can cause an allergic reaction.

The herb's essential oils, which are rich in sesquiterpenes and coumarins, can stimulate the immune system, leading to increased inflammation and sensitivity in individuals with pre-existing skin issues such as eczema, acne, or rosacea.

This may result in redness, itching, and discomfort, potentially exacerbating existing conditions.

6. Affects digestive system

Stillingia sylvatica affects digestive system, causing stomach upset and nausea in some individuals.

The bioactive compounds present in the plant, such as saponins and terpenoids, are thought to be responsible for these side effects, as they can irritate the mucous membranes of the gastrointestinal tract, leading to inflammation and discomfort.

These compounds may also interact with the gut microbiome, altering the balance of beneficial and harmful bacteria, which can further exacerbate digestive issues.

7. Induces seizures in rare cases

Stillingia sylvatica induces seizures in rare cases due to its chemical composition and pharmacological properties.

The plant contains sesquiterpene lactones, which are known to affect the central nervous system, causing altered states of consciousness, hallucinations, and even seizures.

Additionally, the plant's bitter compounds can irritate the stomach lining and lead to gastrointestinal disturbances, potentially exacerbating underlying neurological conditions that may increase the risk of seizure activity.

8. Interferes with medication effects

Stillingia sylvatica interferes with medication effects by altering the way certain drugs are metabolized in the body.

Its active compounds, such as stilbenes and terpenoids, can induce enzymes that break down medications, leading to reduced efficacy or changed kinetics.

This interaction may occur when taking medications for diabetes, blood thinners, and immunosuppressants, which could result in unpredictable responses or inadequate treatment outcomes.

9. Exacerbates mental health disorders

Stillingia sylvatica exacerbates mental health disorders due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and potentially disrupt neurotransmitter balance.

This can lead to increased anxiety, agitation, and irritability in individuals who already struggle with mental health issues.

The plant's volatile oils and flavonoids may also contribute to this effect by interacting with brain chemistry and altering mood regulation processes.

10. Enhances liver toxicity

Stillingia sylvatica enhances liver toxicity due to its ability to stimulate the production of certain enzymes in the liver.

This increased enzyme activity can lead to an overload of toxic substances being processed by the liver, potentially causing damage and compromising its function. The herb's bitter compounds may also interfere with normal liver metabolism, further exacerbating the issue.

As a result, individuals using Stillingia sylvatica may experience adverse effects on their liver health.
