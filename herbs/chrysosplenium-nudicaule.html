Chrysosplenium nudicaule

What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysosplenium nudicaule?

Chrysospeltiun nudicaule belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Saxifragales and the family Saxifragaceae, ultimately placing it within the genus Chrysospeltiun, with the specific designation of the species nudicaule.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Chrysosplenium nudicaule can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or handled improperly. Contact with the plant's sap often leads to localized dermatitis characterized by redness and intense itching. If the botanical material is consumed, individuals may experience nausea, abdominal cramping, and persistent vomiting. Some documented cases also suggest that systemic absorption can trigger dizziness or lightheadedness. It is essential to use protective gear when working near this species to prevent accidental exposure.
