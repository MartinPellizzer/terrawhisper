What is Austrobuxus swainii?

Austrobuxus swainii is a species of evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Picrodendraceae, a family of flowering plants characterized by the presence of milky sap. Botanically, Austrobuxus swainii features simple, alternate leaves with entire margins, typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape, 2-6 cm long, and 0.5-2 cm wide, with a glabrous or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in axillary clusters, followed by globose, red or purple berries, 6-8 mm in diameter.

The specific epithet 'swainii' honors William Swain, a botanist who first described the species.
