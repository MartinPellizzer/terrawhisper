Clematis apiifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis apiifolia?

Clematis apiifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is categorized within the genus Clematis, and its specific identification is defined by the species epithet apiifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis apiifolia has opposite, pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of five to seven leaflets with ovate to lanceolate shapes and finely serrated margins. The plant utilizes slender, woody climbing stems that use petioles to wrap around surrounding vegetation for support. Its flowers are small, creamy-white, and four-petaled, appearing in clusters during the summer months. After flowering, the species develops distinctive feathery, achene-bearing seed heads that facilitate wind dispersal. This herbaceous perennial reaches significant heights through its vigorous climbing habit.
