Heracleum vicinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Heracleum vicinum?

Heracleum vicinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Heracleum, with its specific designation being the species vicinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Heracleum vicinum has large, deeply lobed, and serrated leaves that emerge from a thick, fleshy stem and feature prominent, hollow petioles. These massive leaves often reach several feet in length and possess a distinctively coarse texture. The plant produces expansive compound umbels composed of numerous tiny, white flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its stems are stout, ribbed, and typically covered in fine hairs or small bumps. The root system is substantial, allowing the plant to support its heavy, herbaceous structure throughout the growing season.
