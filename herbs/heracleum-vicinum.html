What's the taxonomical classification of Heracleum vicinum?

Heracleum vicinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Heracleum, with its specific designation being the species vicinum.

The plant produces expansive compound umbels composed of numerous tiny, white flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its stems are stout, ribbed, and typically covered in fine hairs or small bumps.
