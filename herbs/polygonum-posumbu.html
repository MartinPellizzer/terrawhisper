What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum posumbu?

Polygonum posumbu belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further into its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Polygonaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Polygonum and the species posumbu.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum posumbu has prostrate to ascending stems that are branched, cylindrical, and characterized by distinct swollen nodes. The leaves are small, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and possess entire margins with an alternate arrangement along the stem. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne in axillary clusters or solitary within the leaf axils. The plant features a fine pubescence on its surfaces and develops small, dry achenes as its primary fruit type. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in disturbed or sandy habitats.
