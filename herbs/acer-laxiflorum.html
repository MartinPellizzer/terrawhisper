Acer laxiflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Acer laxiflorum?

Acer laxiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Sapindales. The plant is a member of the family Sapindaceae and falls under the genus Acer, specifically identified by the species name laxiflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer laxiflorum has opposite, simple leaves that are typically three to five lobed with serrated margins and a base that ranges from cordate to truncate. The leaf blades are often broadly ovate or rhombic in shape and can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters. Its woody stems feature a smooth to slightly fissured bark that transitions from grey to brown as the tree matures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that emerge in drooping panicles during the spring season. These inflorescences eventually develop into winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal of the seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of China, specifically distributed across provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, Hubei, and Shaanxi. It typically thrives at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters above sea level. These species are often found in moist environments like forest edges or near streams within temperate broadleaf forests. The specific topography of these central and southwestern provinces provides the necessary cool, humid climate for its growth.
