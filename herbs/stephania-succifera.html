Stephania succifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Stephania succifera?

Stephania succifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Ranunculales and falls under the family Menispermaceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Stephania, with its specific designation being the species succifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephania succifera has a tuberous stem, simple or deeply lobed leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is characterized by a large, fleshy caudex that serves as a water storage organ. Its leaves typically exhibit a cordate or lobed shape with smooth margins and a glossy texture. The inflorescence consists of small, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These botanical features allow the species to thrive in seasonal tropical environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including aporphine-type alkaloids, benzylisoquinoline-type alkaloids, lactam-type alkaloids, proaporphine-type alkaloids, and protoberberine-type alkaloids. These specialized secondary metabolites are responsible for the plant's significant pharmacological potential. The benzylisoquinoline-type alkaloids serve as the foundational chemical structure from which many other derivatives are formed. Within this complex chemical profile, the aporphine-type and proaporphine-type alkaloids often contribute to various biological activities. Additionally, the presence of lactam-type and protoberberine-type alkaloids adds to the plant's unique therapeutic complexity.

As discussed in a study, fifty-six alkaloidal compounds, including protoberberiine-, apo-rhine-, pro-aporphine-, benzylisoquinoline-, and lactam-type alkaloids, were identified or tentatively identified in the roots and leaves of Stephania succifera. Among these, forty-one compounds have not been previously reported in Stephania succifera, and eight of them have not been previously reported in the literature. The research found that twenty-four alkaloidal compounds were present in both the roots and leaves of the plant. Using the PharmMapper server, twelve potential targets with different indications were predicted for some of these alkaloids. This identification of diverse bioactive alkaloids in the leaves as well as the roots provides a better understanding of the medicinal components of Stephania succifera.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe neurological distress, including convulsions, muscular spasms, and respiratory paralysis due to its high concentration of toxic alkaloids. These alkaloids act as potent neurotoxins that interfere with the central nervous system if ingested. Symptoms of poisoning may also include dilated pupils, rapid heartbeat, and significant gastrointestinal upset. Because of these dangerous properties, the plant is considered highly poisonous to humans and animals. Medical intervention is necessary immediately following any accidental consumption.
