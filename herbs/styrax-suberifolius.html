Styrax suberifolius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Styrax suberifolius has simple, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that measure approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with pinnate venation. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it feels somewhat leathery to the touch. Small, white, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. These flowers are often accompanied by small, fleshy drupes that turn dark upon ripening. The overall growth habit of the plant forms a small tree or shrub with smooth bark.
