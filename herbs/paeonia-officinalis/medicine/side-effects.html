10 Possible Side Effects Of Paeonia Officinalis (Pink Peony)

Paeonia officinalis has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can occur due to excessive consumption, interactions with other medications, or individual sensitivities. For instance, a person with a history of kidney stones may experience worsened symptoms if they take Paeonia officinalis and experience kidney irritation.

Furthermore, the dizziness and allergic reactions can interfere with daily life, affecting work and social activities.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Paeonia officinalis if used imporperly.

Paeonia officinalis causes allergic reactions due to its potential to trigger an immune response.

The plant contains various compounds that can bind to allergenic proteins, leading to the release of histamine and other chemicals in the body.

This reaction can cause symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing, making it essential for individuals to be aware of these possible effects when using this herb.

Paeonia officinalis triggers respiratory distress when used, which is attributed to its ability to stimulate the parasympathetic nervous system.

This stimulation can cause a slowing of breathing rate and an increase in lung capacity, leading to feelings of shortness of breath, wheezing, and coughing.

Additionally, the plant's active compounds, including paeoniflorin and benzoic acid, may also contribute to respiratory constriction and spasms, further exacerbating breathing difficulties.

Paeonia officinalis induces stomach upset due to its ability to slow down gastric emptying and reduce stomach contractions.

This can lead to feelings of bloating, discomfort, and nausea in some individuals. The plant's constituents, particularly the alkaloids and glycosides, may contribute to this effect by altering gut motility and increasing the release of gastrin, a hormone that stimulates stomach acid production.

As a result, users may experience mild gastrointestinal disturbances when consuming Paeonia officinalis.
