Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) Uses

Common Peony is used to treat menstrual cramps and disorders, as well as menopausal symptoms. It also alleviates joint pain, rheumatism, and inflammatory conditions like arthritis and gout. Its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce skin inflammation and support joint health.

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) Monograph

1. Menstrual cramps

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is used to alleviate menstrual cramps due to its antispasmodic and analgesic properties. It helps reduce uterine contractions and inflammation, easing pain during menstruation. Traditionally, it has been a key herb in herbal medicine for managing dysmenorrhea. Its active compounds, such as paeoniflorin, contribute to its effectiveness in this regard.

2. Menopausal symptoms

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is used to alleviate menopausal symptoms such as hot flashes and mood swings. Its active compounds help regulate hormonal balance and reduce inflammation. It is often prepared as a tincture or decoction and is valued for its gentle, supportive effects during menopause.

3. Menstrual disorders

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is used to treat menstrual disorders by regulating hormonal balance and reducing inflammation. It helps alleviate symptoms such as pain, irregular cycles, and heavy bleeding. Its active compounds support uterine health and improve blood flow during menstruation. It is often prepared as a decoction or tincture for internal use.

4. Joint pain

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is used traditionally to alleviate joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce swelling and stiffness, making it beneficial for conditions like arthritis. The root is often prepared as a decoction or tincture to support joint health and mobility.

5. Rheumatism

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate rheumatic conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce joint pain and swelling associated with rheumatism by improving circulation and reducing inflammation in the affected areas.

6. Arthritis

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) has been traditionally used to alleviate arthritis symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The root contains compounds that help reduce joint inflammation and pain, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for managing arthritis. It is often prepared as a tincture or decoction to support joint health and mobility.

7. Rheumatoid arthritis

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of rheumatoid arthritis. Its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties help reduce joint pain and swelling. It is often prepared as a tincture or decoction to support joint health and mobility in chronic inflammatory conditions.

8. Inflammatory joint pain

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate inflammatory joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce swelling and pain associated with conditions like arthritis by modulating inflammatory responses in the body. The active compounds, such as paeoniflorin, contribute to its therapeutic effects on joint inflammation.

9. Gout

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is used in traditional herbal medicine to help manage gout symptoms. It contains compounds that may reduce inflammation and uric acid levels, which are key factors in gout. The root is often prepared as a decoction or tincture to support joint health and alleviate pain associated with gout attacks.

10. Skin inflammation

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is used in herbal medicine to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation, making it effective for conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The root is typically prepared as a decoction or tincture for topical application.
