Botanical Identification of Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis)

To identify Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) Monograph

Scientific Classification

The Common Peony, Paeonia officinalis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Paeoniales, family Paeoniaceae, genus Paeonia, and species Paeonia officinalis. These classifications help identify its place in the plant kingdom and its relationships with other species.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the Common Peony is Paeonia officinalis, first described by Carl von Linné. It is also known by several synonyms, including Paeonia tenuifolia and Paeonia suffruticosa. Common names include peony, common peony, and Chinese peony. Regional and historical names vary, reflecting its widespread use in traditional medicine across different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is a herbaceous perennial with a thick, fleshy root system. Its stems are sturdy and upright, supporting large, compound leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces a single, showy flower with five petals and numerous stamens, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The fruit is a follicle containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) can be distinguished by its large, showy flowers with five petals, thick stems, and deeply lobed leaves. It has a strong, distinctive aroma and produces pink to red flowers. It differs from similar species like Paeonia lactiflora by its more compact growth and less showy blooms.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is native to Europe and parts of Western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to observe key characteristics is during the flowering period, when the plant is most visually distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Paeonia officinalis is closely related to other peony species within the Paeonia genus. It shares common traits with related species in the family Ranunculaceae, such as compound leaves and showy flowers. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its close evolutionary ties to other peonies and related flowering plants.
