Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) Compounds

The primary compounds of Common Peony include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and glycosides, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant activity, while coumarin and tannins support its antimicrobial and astringent qualities. Saponins and terpenoids also play roles in its medicinal effects.

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds are widely used in herbal medicine for their therapeutic effects on various health conditions. Flavonoids enhance the medicinal value of Paeonia officinalis in traditional remedies.

2. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Glycosides are often responsible for the therapeutic effects of peony in traditional herbal medicine. They are extracted from the roots and used in various formulations.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for treating conditions like inflammation and skin disorders.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis). It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming effects and potential to support digestive health.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Tannins help in reducing excess mucus and are often employed in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and infections. Saponin also aids in improving digestion and reducing cholesterol levels.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making them important for its traditional use in herbal medicine. Specific terpenoids like paeoniflorin have been studied for their therapeutic potential.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) contains volatile compounds like limonene and alpha-pinene. It is extracted through steam distillation and used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil contributes to the plant's therapeutic profile in herbal treatments.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in reducing inflammation and pain. Alkaloids in peony are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower color and possesses antioxidant properties. This compound supports anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine.
