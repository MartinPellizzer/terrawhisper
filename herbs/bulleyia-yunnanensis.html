Bulleyia yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulleyia yunnanensis has a woody habit characterized by spreading branches and leaves that are typically lanceolate to elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage often displays a coriaceous texture and can vary in color from bright green to darker shades depending on the maturity of the specimen. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The fruit develops into a capsule that eventually splits to release small, winged seeds designed for wind dispersal. These morphological traits are essential adaptations for survival in its specific high-altitude forest habitats.
