Naravelia pilulifera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Naravelia pilulifera has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems and deeply lobed, pinnatifid leaves with hairy surfaces. The foliage typically features serrated margins and a texture that feels somewhat rough to the touch due to fine pubescence. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils or terminal positions, often presenting in clusters. The plant is distinguished by its unique seed structure which aids in dispersal. Its root system is well-developed to support its upright growth throughout the growing season.
