Elsholtzia bodinieri

What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia bodinieri?

Elsholtzia bodinieri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is further categorized within the family Lamiaceae, specifically residing in the genus Elsholtzia, with its unique designation being the species bodinieri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia bodinieri has compound leaves consisting of 7 to 15 leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins and acute tips. The leaflets typically measure between 3 and 7 centimeters in length and exhibit a smooth, glabrous texture on both surfaces. Its bark is characterized by a relatively smooth texture when young, eventually developing shallow longitudinal fissures as the tree matures. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are borne in drooping panicles. The resulting fruit consists of winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal through the forest canopy.

What are the pharmacological activities of Elsholtzia bodinieri?

This plant has lipid-reducing, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities. These biological properties are primarily attributed to the presence of diverse secondary metabolites found within its various extracts. Research indicates that the bioactive compounds can effectively lower serum cholesterol levels and mitigate oxidative stress in cellular models. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates potential in modulating immune responses to reduce systemic inflammation. These multifaceted medicinal effects suggest significant therapeutic potential for managing metabolic disorders.

According to a study published by "Lipids", the essential oil of Elsholtzia bodinieri Vaniot (EBVEO) demonstrated a significant reduction in lipid droplets, intracellular triglyceride (TG), and total cholesterol (TC) levels in HepG2 lipid-accumulating cells. EBVEO upregulated the expression of key proteins, including SIRT1, AMPK, PPARα, and CD36, to promote enhanced β-oxidation and fat breakdown. This regulatory effect on lipid metabolism was also observed in a high-fat Caenorhabditis elegans model. Through qPCR and parallel reaction monitoring (PRM), the activation of the AAK-2/NHR-49 pathway was revealed, which are homologs of PPARα and AMPK. This activation led to a substantial increase in fat oxidation and a notable reduction in fat accumulation in C. elegans. EBVEO positions itself as a promising lipid metabolism enhancer through the SIRT1/AMPK and AAK-2/NHR-49 pathways.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating cough, diaphoresis, dyspepsia, fever, and pharyngitis. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed to soothe an inflamed throat through the treatment of pharyngitis. It also serves as a remedy for respiratory distress by easing a persistent cough. Additionally, the plant can be applied to manage digestive issues like dyspepsia. To address systemic symptoms, it is utilized to induce diaphoresis and reduce a fever.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the aerial parts of Elsholtiza bodinieri Vaniot have been used traditionally as an ethnomedicine. These parts are used to treat diaphoresis, headache, fever, cough, pharyngitis, dyspepsia, and hepatitis. The study evaluated the safety of a hot water extract of E. bodinieri through a 14-day acute, 28-day subacute, and 90-day subchronic toxicity assessment. During these tests, a maximum dose of 5000 mg/kg was administered to evaluate toxicity. For the 28-day subacute assessment, repeated doses of 2500, 1250, 625, and 312.5 mg/kg were used. The 90-day subchronic evaluation utilized doses of 1500, 1000, and 500 mg/kg.
