What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia bodinieri has compound leaves consisting of 7 to 15 leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins and acute tips. Its bark is characterized by a relatively smooth texture when young, eventually developing shallow longitudinal fissures as the tree matures. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are borne in drooping panicles. The resulting fruit consists of winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal through the forest canopy.
