Bupleurum scorzonerifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Bupleurum scorzonerifolium?

Bupleurum scorzonerifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned in the order Apiales. The plant is a member of the family Apiaceae, which places it within the genus Bupleurum, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species scorzonerifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bupleurum scorzonerifolium has pinnatisect leaves that are deeply divided into narrow, linear segments and a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems. The foliage typically displays a bright green color and a delicate, feathery texture due to the fine dissection of the leaf blades. Its inflorescence consists of compound umbels containing numerous small, yellowish-green flowers. These flowers are clustered in terminal structures that emerge from the top of the branching stems. What are the pharmacological activities of Bupleurum scorzonerifolium?

As discussed in a study, the aerial part of Bupleurum scorzonerifolium, which accounts for 80% of the plant, has shown benefits in the treatment of inflammation-related diseases. Ten extracts of this aerial part effectively alleviated the increase in LPS-induced proinflammatory cytokines in RAW 264.7 cells. From these extracts, 40 components were identified, including 27 common components. Eight components were specifically correlated with anti-inflammatory effects and were confirmed to reverse the expression of proinflammatory and anti-inflammatory factors in a zebrafish model. Five quality markers, including chlorogenic acid, hypericin, rutin, quercetin, and isorharnetin, were also identified through HPLC.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds saikosaponin a, saikosaponin d, saikosaponin v, isorhamnetin, and quercetin. These specific triterpenoid saponins are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and immunosuppressive activities. The flavonoids present in the plant contribute additional antioxidant properties that help protect cells from oxidative stress. Researchers often study these bioactive molecules to understand their therapeutic effects on various biological systems. Together, these substances form a complex chemical profile that defines the plant's pharmacological utility.

As discussed in a study, Bupleurum scorzonerifolium contains various medicinal components and metabolites that respond to potassium levels. Low potassium levels stimulate the content of saikosaponin a in all plant parts. Conversely, high potassium levels inhibit the accumulation of most saikosaponin a, c, and d. Through gas chromatography-mass spectrometry, a total of 77 metabolites were identified within the plant. Key metabolites identified include glycerol, d-glucose, silane, and copper phthalocyanine. These metabolites are mapped into insulin signaling pathways, streptomycin biosynthesis, and galactose metabolism.
