What to know about Crataegus laevigata (hawthorn) before using it medicinally

Hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus laevigata, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to improve cardiovascular well-being by reducing blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

In addition to its medicinal uses, hawthorn is also prized for its culinary applications, where it is often used in jams, preserves, and teas due to its sweet-tart flavor and fragrance. As a horticultural staple, hawthorn is valued for its ornamental qualities, with its white or pink flowers and crimson berries adding beauty to gardens and landscapes. From a botanical standpoint, hawthorn belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

With historical roots dating back thousands of years, hawthorn has been mentioned in ancient texts and folklore as a symbol of protection, loyalty, and fertility.

What are the side effects of Crataegus laevigata when used improperly?

Additionally, improper use of Crataegus laevigata may lead to allergic reactions, such as skin rashes, itching, and swelling, particularly among individuals with a history of allergies or sensitivities. Furthermore, high doses or prolonged administration of the plant can cause blood pressure fluctuations, which may be problematic for individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions.

In rare cases, improper use of Crataegus laevigata has been linked to more severe side effects, including kidney damage and liver toxicity, highlighting the importance of consulting with a qualified healthcare professional before using the plant for medicinal purposes.

The precautions to take before using Crataegus laevigata medicinally are crucial to ensure its safe and effective use.

When using C. laevigata medicinally, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and duration of treatment, as excessive consumption can lead to adverse effects such as gastrointestinal disturbances, allergic reactions, and even kidney damage in rare cases.
