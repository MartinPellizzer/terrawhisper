Common Hawthorn

Crataegus laevigata

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Common Hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus laevigata, belongs to the Rosaceae family. It is also called Smooth Hawthorn and is a deciduous shrub native to Europe. The plant has thorny branches, ovate leaves, and clusters of white flowers, with red berries in autumn. It is sometimes referred to as Common Hawthorn in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Common Hawthorn contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to support cardiovascular health and may influence enzyme activity and antioxidant defense mechanisms.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Cardiovascular Cardiac arrhythmia, hypertension, high blood pressure Antioxidant, antihypertensive, vasodilator, cardiotonic
Nervous Insomnia, anxiety, depression Sedative, anxiolytic, antidepressant
Digestive Gastrointestinal spasms, constipation, stomach ulcers, gastritis Spasmolytic, laxative, anti-ulcer, anti-inflammatory

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for cardiovascular conditions and mild digestive issues.

Decoction: Used for digestive disorders and respiratory ailments.

Tincture: Used for heart conditions and circulatory disorders.

Safety Profile

Common Hawthorn is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should not be used by individuals with severe hypotension or those taking medications for heart conditions. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
