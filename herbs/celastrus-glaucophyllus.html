Celastrus glaucophyllus

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus glaucophyllus?

Celastrus glaucophyllus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, representing a specific lineage of vascular plants. Within the order Celastrales, it is placed in the family Celastraceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Celastrus, with its specific identity defined by the species name glaucophyllus.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celastrus glaucophyllus?

Celastrus glaucophyllus has medicinal properties primarily utilized for treating skin ailments, digestive issues, and inflammatory conditions through its bioactive phytochemical constituents. The plant contains various alkaloids and terpenoids that contribute to its overall pharmacological profile. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, stomach ailments, and various types of fever. The bark and leaves are often prepared into medicinal decoctions to address internal inflammations. In certain traditional practices, the plant material is applied topically to soothe skin irritations or infections. It is also utilized in folk medicine to alleviate digestive discomfort and promote gut health. These applications demonstrate the plant's role in managing diverse physiological symptoms.
