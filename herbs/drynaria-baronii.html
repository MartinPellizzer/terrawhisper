Drynaria baronii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Drynaria baronii has woody, climbing stems and pinnately compound leaves composed of several pairs of leaflets. The leaflets are typically elliptic or oblong in shape with entire margins and acute tips. The inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense axillary racemes. The plant also produces distinctive woody pods that are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. These structural features allow the species to thrive in tropical forest environments.

    Drynaria baronii can cause adverse physiological reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin irritation, and potential toxicity if ingested in unregulated quantities. Further clinical research is required to fully document the long-term systemic effects of its bioactive constituents.
