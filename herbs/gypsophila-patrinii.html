Gypsophila patrinii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gypsophila patrinii has a perennial growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems and small, lanceolate to linear leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stalks. The inflorescence consists of many small, five-petaled flowers that emerge from terminal cymes. These flowers are generally white or pale pink in color and possess delicate, spreading petals. The plant's foliage is often quite fine, giving the overall structure a light and airy appearance. Its root system is well-developed to support its upright, branching architecture in its natural habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Gypsophila patrinii can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if ingested. If consumed, the compounds can lead to nausea, vomiting, or more severe gastrointestinal discomfort. It is important to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse biological responses.
