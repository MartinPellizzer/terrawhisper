Malaisia scandens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Malaisia scandens has woody, climbing stems that utilize adventitious roots to cling to supporting structures. Its leaves are typically arranged in an opposite pattern and feature an elliptical or lanceolate shape with entire margins. The leaf texture often appears leathery, which helps the plant retain moisture during its climbing process. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. The plant's overall structure is characterized by its sprawling, vine-like growth habit.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically occurring in countries such as Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, and the Philippines. It thrives primarily within lowland rainforest ecosystems where humidity remains consistently high. Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of dense canopy cover and moist soil conditions found in these equatorial zones. While its range is concentrated in the Indo-Malayan realm, specific populations are often localized to specific forest types.
