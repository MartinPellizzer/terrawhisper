What is Cordia parviflora?

Cordia parviflora is a species of plant in the family Boraginaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree that grows up to 10 meters tall, with a woody stem and a greyish-brown bark. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, measuring 2-7 cm in length, with a leathery texture and a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, bearing small, white flowers that are 2-3 mm in diameter, with five petals and a bilabiate shape.

The plant produces small, globose fruits that are 6-7 mm in diameter, containing two to four seeds.
