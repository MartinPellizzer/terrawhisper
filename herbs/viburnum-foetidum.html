Viburnum foetidum

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum foetidum?

Viburnum foetidum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Dipsacales. The plant is further categorized into the family Viburnaceae, which contains the genus Viburnum, and is specifically identified by the species name foetidum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum foetidum has large, leathery, dark green leaves that are broadly ovate or elliptical in shape with serrated margins and prominent veins. These evergreen shrubs typically reach heights of up to four meters and develop a dense, spreading growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, creamy white flowers that emit a strong, unpleasant odor during the blooming season. Following flowering, the plant develops small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a deep blue or black color when ripe. The bark on older stems becomes somewhat rough and develops a woody texture over time.
