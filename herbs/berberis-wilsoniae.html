What's the taxonomical classification of Berberis wilsoniae?

Berberis wilsoniae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the evolutionary hierarchy, it falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Berberidaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed within the genus Berberis, specifically identified as the species wilsoniae.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berberis wilsoniae has small, leathery, obovate leaves that are typically dark green with slightly serrated margins. The plant produces small, fragrant, pale yellow flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the spring season. Following the bloom, it develops small, dark blue or blackish berries that are characteristic of the species. The overall growth habit is that of a spreading, low-growing shrub with a dense texture.

As discussed in a study, the cell cultures of Berberis wilsoniae contain an enzyme called (S)-tetrahydroprotoberberine oxidase with a molecular mass of 105 kDa. This metabolic pathway eventually leads to the production of medicinal compounds including berberine, jatrorrhizine, and palmatine. The process involves the removal of four hydrogen atoms and the simultaneous production of 1 mol H2O2 and H2O.
