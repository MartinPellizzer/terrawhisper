What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum przewalskii?

Thalictrum przewalskii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. This plant is a specific representative of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Thalictrum, and is identified by its unique species epithet, przewalskii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum przewalskii has herbaceous stems that reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and bear compound, ternately divided leaves characterized by ovate or elliptical leaflets with serrated margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose, terminal panicles that often exhibit a pale violet or white coloration. Each flower typically consists of four to five petal-like sepals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens. The reproductive structures are supported by slender pedicels that allow the inflorescence to sway easily in the wind. These morphological traits are well-adapted to the moist, alpine environments where the species is commonly found.
