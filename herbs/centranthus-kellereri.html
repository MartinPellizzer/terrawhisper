Centranthus Kellereri

Centranthus kellereri, also known as the Kashmir carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Asia, and it has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and other traditional medicine systems for its potential health benefits.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and alkaloids, which may contribute to its medicinal properties. It has been historically employed to treat respiratory conditions, such as coughs and bronchitis, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory effects. Some studies suggest that Centranthus kellereri may possess antimicrobial and antioxidant properties, supporting its use in treating infections and supporting immune function.

However, more scientific research is needed to fully validate its therapeutic applications and ensure its safe use in modern medicine.
