What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum oxyphyllum?

Zanthoxylum oxyphyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Sapindales and is a member of the family Rutaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Zanthoxylum, with its precise biological designation being the species oxyphyllum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Zanthoxylum oxyphyllum?

This plant has antiproliferative activities that allow it to inhibit the growth and spread of various cancer cells. The secondary metabolites found within the species contribute to this ability to arrest the cell cycle. Research indicates that specific compounds extracted from the plant can trigger apoptosis in malignant cell lines. These biological mechanisms suggest that the plant holds significant potential for developing new anti-cancer therapeutic agents. Further investigation into its chemical constituents may reveal more specific pathways for controlling tumor progression.

According to a study published by "Phytotherapy research : PTR", Zanthoxylum oxyphyllum bark and root extracts demonstrated potent antiproliferative activity against the growth of human keratinocytes (HaCaT cells). The extract from Zanthoxylum oxyphyllum barks showed an IC50 value of 53 micrograms/mL, while the extract from the roots had an IC50 value of 57 micrograms/mL. This antiproliferative activity was not caused by cytotoxic effects on cell membranes, as the lactate dehydrogenase released from the cytoplasm of keratinocytes did not exceed the control value. Additionally, these extracts provided protection against radical-induced damage to model membranes stimulated with 2,2'-azo-bis(2-amidinopropane) dihydrochloride.
