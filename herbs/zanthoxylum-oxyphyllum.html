Zanthoxylum oxyphyllum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zanthoxylum oxyphyllum has a woody structure characterized by pinnately compound leaves with numerous narrow, lanceolate leaflets and small, inconspicuous greenish flowers. The leaves typically consist of several pairs of leaflets that possess entire margins and acute tips. Its bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured depending on the age of the specimen. The plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature. This species also features a robust stem system capable of supporting significant height in its natural habitat.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Zanthoxylum oxyphyllum can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential allergic hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. Topical application of the plant's extracts has been known to trigger localized redness or itching on the skin. Because specific toxicological data on humans is limited, there is a risk of unpredictable systemic reactions. Users should exercise caution and consult medical professionals to avoid unintended physiological complications.
