What's the taxonomical classification of Isodon lophanthoides?

Isodon lophanthoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Isodon, with its specific designation being the species lophanthoides.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive medicinal compounds including 11-hydroxyferruginol, abiatanne-type diterpenoids, ababiatriene, ferruginol, and lophanic acid. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the diverse pharmacological properties observed in the species. Researchers have identified various abiatanne-type diterpenoids as significant components within its chemical profile. The presence of ferruginol and its derivative, 11-hydroxyferruginol, contributes to its biological activity. Additionally, unique substances such as ababiatriene and lophanic acid further characterize its medicinal potential.

According to a study published by "Planta", Isodon lophanthoides is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb that is rich in highly oxidized abiatanne-type diterpenoids. These medicinal compounds exhibit a wide range of pharmaceutical activities. Within the plant, two ferruginol synthases, specifically CYP76AH42 and CYP76AH43, are involved in hydroxylating the C12 position of abietatriene to produce ferruginol. Additionally, a 11-hydroxyferruginol synthase, identified as CYP76AH46, catalyzes two successive oxidations at the C12 and C11 positions of abietatriene. The identified CYP76AHs possess low protein sequence identities ranging from 51-63% and may be involved in at least 2 independent abiatanne biosynthetic pathways.

By targeting these specific ailments, the plant supports the recovery of the digestive and hepatic systems.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", Isodon lophanthoides aqueous extract is used for its protective effect on acute hepatic injury induced by D-galactosamine in rats. In the study involving 60 rats, the extract was administered at doses of 15, 7.5, or 3.75 g/kg daily for 7 consecutive days. The treatment helped decrease serum levels of ALT, AST, ALP, TBA, and T-Bil while increasing TP, ALB, and hepatic glycogen content. Furthermore, the plant helped restrain the enlargement of the liver and the shrinkage of the thymus. Through pathological observation, it was also found that the extract could reduce necrosis.
