Isodon lophanthoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Isodon lophanthoides has deciduous, opposite, elliptical leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins with prominent pinnate venation. The plant grows as a small to medium-sized tree, often reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its bark is typically grayish and develops shallow longitudinal fissures as the specimen matures. During the flowering season, it produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in terminal panicles. The resulting fruit consists of small, dry, ovoid capsules that split open to release winged seeds.
