Isodon lophanthoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Isodon lophanthoides?

Isodon lophanthoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Isodon, with its specific designation being the species lophanthoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Isodon lophanthoides has deciduous, opposite, elliptical leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins with prominent pinnate venation. The plant grows as a small to medium-sized tree, often reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its bark is typically grayish and develops shallow longitudinal fissures as the specimen matures. During the flowering season, it produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in terminal panicles. The resulting fruit consists of small, dry, ovoid capsules that split open to release winged seeds.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive medicinal compounds including 11-hydroxyferruginol, abiatanne-type diterpenoids, ababietaetriene, ferruginol, and lophanic acid. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for much of the plant's therapeutic potential in traditional medicine. The presence of ferruginol and its derivative, 11-hydroxyferruginol, contributes significantly to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Furthermore, the abiatanne-type diterpenoids and ababietaetriene provide unique structural frameworks that influence its biological activities.
