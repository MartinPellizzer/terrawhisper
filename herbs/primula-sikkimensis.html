Primula sikkimensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Primula sikkimensis?

Primula sikkimensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Primulaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Primula, with its unique designation being the species sikkimensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula sikkimensis has a basal rosette of long-petioled, obovate to elliptical leaves that feature crenate or dentate margins and a slightly wrinkled surface texture. The leaves are typically dark green and grow densely from a central rhizome to form a compact cluster. From this rosette, an elongated, leafless scape emerges, often reaching heights of several decimeters. This flowering stalk is topped by an umbel of several salverform flowers, which display colors ranging from pale yellow to deep orange. Each individual blossom possesses a narrow tube that opens into five spreading lobes, often marked with subtle nectar guides.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Himalayan mountain range, specifically found in regions of Sikkim, Nepal, Bhutan, and parts of China. It typically inhabits high-altitude environments ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters above sea level. These plants are most commonly located in moist, alpine meadows and along the edges of rocky slopes. They thrive in cool, damp climates where they can grow amidst subalpine vegetation.
