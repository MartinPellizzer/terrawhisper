Peristrophe japonica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Peristrophe japonica has prostrate or climbing woody stems that produce elliptical, opposite leaves with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy texture and a deep green color throughout the growing season. Small, tubular flowers emerge in clusters, featuring a pale pink or white corolla that transitions into a funnel shape. These blossoms are supported by slender pedicels and are often accompanied by small, rounded capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. The overall growth habit allows the plant to form dense mats or scramble over other vegetation.

    What are the herbal preparations of Peristrophe japonica?

    Peristrophe japonica is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried leaves or as a topical poultice made from crushed fresh plant material. The leaves are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption to address various ailments. Additionally, the plant matter can be macerated in alcohol to produce a tincture for more potent medicinal applications. These different preparations allow practitioners to tailor the delivery method to the specific therapeutic need.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Peristrophe japonica can cause severe skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if the plant parts are ingested. The sap contains various chemical compounds that act as irritants when they come into direct contact with sensitive human skin. Individuals handling the plant may experience redness, itching, or even blistering at the site of contact. If the plant is consumed accidentally, it may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Due to these toxic properties, careful handling and avoidance of ingestion are necessary when encountering this species.
