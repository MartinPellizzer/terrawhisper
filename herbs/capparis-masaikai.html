Capparis masaikai

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Capparis masaikai has a woody, perennial shrubby habit characterized by long, spreading branches and trifoliate leaves with elliptical or oblong leaflets. The foliage typically features smooth margins and a somewhat leathery texture suited to its environment. Its flowers are striking, presenting large white petals and numerous prominent, long stamens that form a brush-like appearance. The inflorescence emerges from the leaf axils, often appearing solitary or in small clusters. Following pollination, the plant develops globular, fleshy fruits that serve as a key identification feature.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the dry, rocky slopes and deciduous forests of the Masakaai region in East Africa. Its range is primarily restricted to specific elevations within the savanna ecosystems of Kenya and Tanzania. The species thrives in well-drained soils that are characteristic of these semi-arid landscapes. Local environmental conditions dictate its patchy distribution across the landscape. These specific habitat requirements limit its presence to isolated pockets rather than a continuous widespread coverage.
