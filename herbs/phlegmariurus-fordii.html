What's the taxonomical classification of Phlegmariurus fordii?

Phlegmariurus fordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Lycopodiales. Further taxonomic refinement places it within the family Lycopodiaceae, specifically under the genus Phlegmariurus. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species foridii.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including respiratory conditions like asthma and bronchitis, as well as digestive issues and menstrual irregularities. It is often utilized in traditional medicine to help alleviate symptoms of coughs and congestion. Some practitioners also employ it to address inflammation and promote better blood circulation within the body. Additionally, its properties are sometimes applied to support general wellness and manage minor feverish symptoms.
