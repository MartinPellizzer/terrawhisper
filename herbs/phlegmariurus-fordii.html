Phlegmariurus fordii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlegmariurus fordii has pendulous, unbranched or sparsely branched stems that are covered in small, imbricate, scale-like microphylls. These leaves are narrowly triangular to lanceolate in shape and are arranged in a dense, overlapping pattern along the length of the axis. The plant typically exhibits a creeping or hanging growth habit, often found growing as an epiphyte in humid montane forests. Small, sporangia-bearing structures are located on the underside of the microphylls to facilitate reproduction. The overall morphology is characterized by a slender, wiry appearance that allows it to drape elegantly from mossy branches.
