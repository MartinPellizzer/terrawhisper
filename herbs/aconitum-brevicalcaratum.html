What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum brevicalcaratum?

Aconitum brevicalcaratum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Ranunculales and belongs to the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Aconitum and is specifically designated as the species brevicalcaratum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds brevicanines A, brevicanines A-D, brevicanines B, and scaconine. These specific alkaloids are characteristic of the chemical profile found within Aconitum brevicalcaratum. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential pharmacological properties and biological activities. The presence of these diverse brevicanine derivatives contributes to the overall complexity of the plant's secondary metabolites. Such detailed chemical analysis is essential for identifying the therapeutic potential of this species.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", four new C19-diterpenoid alkaloids named brevicanines A-D (1-4) were isolated from Aconium brevicalcaratum. These medicinal compounds all possess an unusual axial chiral phenyl-quina-zolineside chain. The structures of brevicanines A and B were further confirmed through semi-synthesis from their parent compound scaconine. Variable-temperature NMR spectroscopy was used to investigate the atropisomers of brevicanine A, where two sets of signals in 1H NMR spectra were observed at room temperature. These signals in the 1H NMR spectra eventually coalesced over 140 °C.
