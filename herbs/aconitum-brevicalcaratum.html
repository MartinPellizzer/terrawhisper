Aconitum brevicalcaratum

What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum brevicalcaratum?

Aconitum brevicalcaratum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Ranunculales and belongs to the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Aconitum and is specifically designated as the species brevicalcaratum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains complex diterpene alkaloids such as aconitine, mesaconitine, and hypaconitine that serve as its primary medicinal compounds.

These specific alkaloids function by interacting with voltage-gated sodium channels in the nervous system. While these substances exhibit potent analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties, they are also characterized by extreme systemic toxicity. Precise pharmacological applications require careful dosage control to prevent fatal cardiac or respiratory failure.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Aconitum brevicalcaratum can cause severe poisoning characterized by intense numbness, tingling, and paralysis of the respiratory and cardiovascular systems upon contact or ingestion. The plant contains potent alkaloids that disrupt sodium channels in the human body. Once absorbed, these toxins can trigger life-threatening cardiac arrhythmias and irregular heartbeats. Victims may also experience profound gastrointestinal distress, including nausea and vomiting. In extreme cases, the neurological impact leads to muscle weakness and eventual respiratory failure.
