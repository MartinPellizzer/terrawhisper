Thalictrum foliolosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum foliolosum?

Thalictrum foliolosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, positioning it within the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Thalictrum, with its specific designation being the species foliolosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum foliolosum has compound leaves characterized by multiple small, ovate to elliptical leaflets with serrated margins and a distinctively delicate texture. These leaves are typically arranged alternately along slender, herbaceous stems that rise from a rhizomatous base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that often feature petals and sepals of similar color and shape. These floral structures are clustered in loose panicles at the terminal ends of the branches. The overall morphology reflects a fine-textured appearance common to many members of the Ranunculaceae family.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating hepatitis, jaundice, inflammation, rheumatism, and snakebite. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to reduce swelling and soothe systemic inflammation throughout the body. Practitioners often utilize its extracts to address liver-related issues such as hepatitis and the resulting jaundice. Additionally, the plant serves as a traditional remedy for managing the pain associated with rheumatism. It also holds a specialized role in providing treatment for victims of a snakebite.

As discussed in a study, Thalictrum foliolosum DC. was examined for its potential to treat hepatitis illness through the evaluation of phytochemicals in three separate extracts including methanol-polar, chloroform-non-polar, and aqueous-polar. The methanolic extract contained the highest content of phytochemicals, while the aqueous extract showed the least amount. This methanolic extract also demonstrated potential antioxidant activity, evidenced by the lowest IC50 values in DPPH and ABTS assays and a high %inhibition in µM Fe equivalent of FRAP assays. In-silico investigations using default docking algorithms identified rutin as the dominant phytoconstituent with the highest binding affinity of 8.2 kcal/mol. This rutin interacts with the enzyme sphingomyelin phosphodiesterase Leptospira interrogans (PDB: 5EBB) to prevent interaction with Leptospira interrogans spp. which causes jaundice.
