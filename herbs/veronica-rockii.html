Veronica rockii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Veronica rockii has perennial, creeping stems that form dense mats through the production of stolons and rhizomes. The leaves are primarily basal or low on the stem, appearing small, ovate to lanceolate, and possessing finely serrated margins. These leaves are typically bright green with a slightly textured surface. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that emerge from terminal racemes. Each flower features a four-lobed corolla with the lower lobe being notably larger than the others.
