Aster smithianus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster smithianus has climbing stems that produce opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively textured surface. The foliage is typically dark green and can reach significant lengths as the plant utilizes its twining habit to ascend supports. Small, actinomorphic flowers emerge from the axils, displaying a composite structure characteristic of the Asteraceae family. These inflorescences often feature ray florets surrounding a central disc, providing a visual contrast to the dense greenery. The overall morphology is adapted for a scandent lifestyle in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
