What's the taxonomical classification of Primula maximowiczii?

Primula maximowiczii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, which contains various primrose species. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Primula, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, maximowiczii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula maximowiczii has a basal rosette of long-petioled, ovate to oblong leaves characterized by crenate margins and a wrinkled or rugose surface texture. The foliage is typically dark green and grows in a dense cluster near the soil surface. From the center of this rosette, an erect, leafless scape emerges to support the inflorescence. The flowers are arranged in a terminal umbel and feature five-lobed corollas that vary in color from pale yellow to deep orange. Each individual floret possesses a distinct tube that expands into a spreading limb.
