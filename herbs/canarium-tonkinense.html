Canarium tonkinense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Canarium tonkinense has a tree form characterized by an erect trunk, simple leaves with elliptical or oblong blades, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems and possess distinct pinnate venation. Its bark is often textured, providing protection for the vascular tissues as the tree matures in its tropical habitat. The fruit is a drupe that develops from the fertilized ovaries of the flowers, housing a single seed. These morphological traits are essential for the species to thrive within its specific ecological niche.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Atlantic Forest biome within the state of Paraná in southern Brazil. It is specifically found in the high-altitude regions of the Serra do Mar mountain range. These populations thrive in moist, subtropical environments characterized by dense forest cover. The species occupies narrow ecological niches that are highly sensitive to changes in local climate and habitat fragmentation. Due to this restricted range, its natural distribution is quite localized.

While many parts are used traditionally, concentrated extracts might interact unexpectedly with certain medications. It is essential to exercise caution and consult a professional before using any part of this species for medicinal purposes.
