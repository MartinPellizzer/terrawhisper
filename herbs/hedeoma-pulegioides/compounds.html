Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) Compounds

Pennyroyal contains essential oil as its primary compound, rich in monoterpene hydrocarbons like pulegone. Other key compounds include flavonoids, coumarin, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties and aromatic profile.

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) is its primary active compound, containing high concentrations of pulegone. This volatile oil is responsible for the plant's strong aroma and many of its traditional medicinal properties, including antiseptic and vermifuge effects.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support its traditional use in herbal medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides). It contributes to the plant's antifungal and antiparasitic properties. Coumarin is also responsible for the characteristic aroma of Pennyroyal. Its presence influences the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support skin health and wound healing. Tannins also have antimicrobial effects, which align with Pennyroyal's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and antifungal properties. This compound is involved in the plant's ability to repel pests and may support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of organic compounds found in Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides). They contribute to the plant's characteristic aroma and are believed to play a role in its traditional use for digestive and antimicrobial purposes. However, some terpenoids may have toxic effects, necessitating caution in its use.

7. Glycoside

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) contains the glycoside pulegone. This compound is known for its potent antiseptic and vermifuge properties, traditionally used to expel parasites. However, it is toxic in higher doses and should be used with caution under professional guidance.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides). It contributes to the plant's expectorant and antiseptic properties, aiding in respiratory health. Saponins also have mild antimicrobial effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides) contains the alkaloid pulegone. This compound is known for its potent effects, including antiseptic and vermifuge properties. However, it is also toxic in higher doses and can cause severe side effects, making its use in herbal medicine highly regulated.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Pennyroyal (Hedeoma pulegioides). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine. Anthocyanin may help protect cells from oxidative stress, though its role in the plant's therapeutic effects is still under study.
