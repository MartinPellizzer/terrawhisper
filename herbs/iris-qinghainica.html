What's the taxonomical classification of Iris qinghainica?

Iris qinghainica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asparagales, it is a member of the family Iridaeceae. Finally, the plant is placed in the genus Iris, with its specific classification being the species qinghainica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris qinghainica has rhizomatous stems that produce sword-shaped, grayish-green leaves and flowers featuring violet-blue petals with yellow and white signal patches on the falls. The plant typically grows in clumps with leaves that are narrow and upright, often reaching lengths of several decimeters. Its floral structure consists of three upright standards and three drooping falls, which are characteristic of the Iridaceae family. The flowers emerge during the late spring or early summer season, displaying a delicate texture. Small, capsule-like seed pods develop following successful pollination to facilitate reproduction.
