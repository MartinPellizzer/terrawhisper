What to know about Anthriscus cerefolium (chervil) before using it medicinally

Chervil, scientifically known as Anthriscus cerefolium, is a culinary and medicinal herb that boasts impressive health benefits, including antibacterial and antifungal properties that can aid in digestion and boost immunity.

In the kitchen, chervil's delicate flavor and aroma make it a popular addition to salads, soups, and sauces, particularly when paired with fish, poultry, or vegetables. As an ornamental plant, chervil is prized for its dainty foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a charming addition to borders and containers. Botanically speaking, chervil belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its feathery leaves and umbrella-like flower heads.

With ancient references dating back to Roman and Greek cuisine, chervil has a rich historical significance in traditional cooking and herbalism practices.
