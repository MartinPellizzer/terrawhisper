What's the taxonomical classification of Euonymus euscaphis?

Euonymus euscaphis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its specific genus, Euonymus, and its unique species designation, euscaphis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euonymus euscaphis has opposite, simple, elliptic to ovate leaves that measure 3 to 7 centimeters in length and feature finely serrated margins.

Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that transition into distinctive, four-lobed, bright pink or red capsules. These capsules eventually split open to reveal bright orange seeds that are held in place by fleshy, lobed arils.
