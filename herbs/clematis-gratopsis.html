Clematis gratopsis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis gratopsis has opposite, trifoliate leaves characterized by ovate to lanceolate leaflets with entire or slightly wavy margins and a dull green surface. The stems are typically slender and woody, often featuring fine hairs that aid in climbing through surrounding vegetation. Its flowers are actinomorphic and solitary, presenting four to five petaloid sepals that range in color from pale cream to yellowish-white. The reproductive structures consist of numerous central stamens and pistils that later develop into feathery, achene-bearing styles. These specialized seed heads facilitate wind dispersal once the plant reaches maturity.
