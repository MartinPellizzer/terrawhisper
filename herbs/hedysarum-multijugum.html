Hedysarum multijugum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedysarum multijugum has pinnately compound leaves with multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets and woody, scrambling stems that form dense thickets. The foliage typically consists of several pairs of leaflets arranged along a central rachis, which helps the plant navigate through surrounding vegetation. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying colors that range from yellow to pale cream. The plant produces legume-style seed pods that are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. These woody climbers utilize their structural morphology to climb over other plants in their native habitats.
