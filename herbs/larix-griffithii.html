Larix griffithii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Larix griffithii has deciduous needles that are arranged spirally on short shoots and long shoots, accompanied by small, woody cones that contain numerous seeds. The foliage typically exhibits a bright green color during the growing season before turning golden yellow in the autumn. Its bark is thick and reddish-brown, often becoming deeply fissured with age to protect the inner tissue. The tree develops a conical to pyramidal crown that becomes more irregular as the specimen matures. Small, woody cones are upright and typically measure between one and two centimeters in length.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude coniferous forests of Taiwan, where it is primarily found growing on the central mountain ranges. Its distribution is restricted to montane and subalpine zones, typically occurring at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters. Within these rugged terrains, the species occupies specific ecological niches characterized by cool temperatures and moist environments. Because it is endemic to this single island, its entire natural range is geographically isolated from other larch species. The population density often varies depending on local slope aspects and the availability of suitable rocky substrates.

    What are the herbal preparations of Larix griffithii?

    Larix griffithii is prepared as decoctions from the bark, infusions from the needles, and topical applications using resin or essential oils. The bark is typically boiled in water for extended periods to extract its medicinal compounds for internal use. Needle infusions provide a gentler method for consuming the plant's antioxidants through steeped tea. Resin extracted from the trunk can be applied directly to the skin to treat minor abrasions or inflammatory conditions. These diverse preparation methods allow the plant's chemical constituents to be utilized for both systemic and localized therapeutic purposes.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Larix griffithii can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and respiratory distress if its pollen or oils are inhaled or touched. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or a localized rash upon direct contact with the tree's needles or bark. Inhalation of airborne particles during pollination seasons can trigger sneezing, coughing, or more severe asthma-like symptoms in sensitive people. While much research is limited, caution is advised when handling the plant to prevent these adverse physical reactions. Monitoring for any swelling or discomfort is important if exposure occurs.
