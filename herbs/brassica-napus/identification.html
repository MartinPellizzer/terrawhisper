Botanical Identification of Rapeseed (Brassica napus)

To identify Rapeseed (Brassica napus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Rapeseed (Brassica napus) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Rapeseed, or Brassica napus, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Brassicales, family Brassicaceae, genus Brassica, and species Brassica napus. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the mustard family, which includes many economically important crops.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Rapeseed is Brassica napus, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is also known by several synonyms, including Brassica rapa subsp. napus. Common names such as rape, oilseed rape, and colza are widely used, while regional and historical names reflect its long-standing agricultural significance.

Morphological Characteristics

Rapeseed (Brassica napus) is an annual plant with a robust taproot system. It has a straight, cylindrical stem that is often hairy. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a smooth margin. The plant produces a terminal raceme of bright yellow flowers, which are typically 4-5 cm in diameter. The fruit is a thick, pod-like silique containing multiple seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Rapeseed can be distinguished by its large, yellow, cruciform flowers and triangular, lobed leaves. The stem is hairy, and the pods are cylindrical with straight or slightly curved seams. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be confused with other mustard family plants, but its distinctive color and leaf shape help in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Rapeseed (Brassica napus) is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with cool winters and warm summers. It prefers well-drained loamy soils and is commonly found at altitudes between 200 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Rapeseed blooms in early to mid-summer, typically between May and June, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. Harvest timing is crucial for identification, as mature seeds are ready for collection in late September to early October.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Rapeseed, or Brassica napus, is closely related to species like Brassica rapa and Brassica campestris. It belongs to the genus Brassica within the mustard family, Brassicaceae. The family is characterized by features such as four-petaled flowers and silique fruits, which help in its identification.
