Rapeseed (Brassica napus) Uses

Rapeseed is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, respiratory infections, and inflamed lungs. It helps alleviate symptoms like respiratory congestion and mucous membrane irritation. It is also applied for allergic rhinitis, respiratory allergies, and sinusitis. Rapeseed may support recovery from otitis media and upper respiratory tract infections.

Rapeseed (Brassica napus) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Rapeseed has been used traditionally to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce mucus buildup and soothe airway irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or oil to ease breathing and alleviate coughing symptoms.

2. Respiratory infections

Rapeseed has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating symptoms of colds, flu, and bronchitis. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, while its anti-inflammatory effects may reduce throat irritation and congestion. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for internal use.

3. Inflamed lungs

Rapeseed has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of inflamed lungs. Its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce swelling and mucus production. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe irritation and promote easier breathing.

4. Allergic rhinitis

Rapeseed (Brassica napus) has been used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It contains compounds like glucosinolates and isothiocyanates that help reduce nasal inflammation and mucus production. Regular use may support respiratory health and ease seasonal allergy symptoms.

5. Respiratory congestion

Rapeseed has been traditionally used to alleviate respiratory congestion due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and clear airways, making it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis and coughs. Its active compounds, including glucosinolates, support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and promoting mucus drainage.

6. Upper respiratory tract infections

Rapeseed has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties may help alleviate symptoms of upper respiratory tract infections. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease congestion and reduce inflammation in the throat and airways.

7. Mucous membrane irritation

Rapeseed has been used historically to soothe mucous membrane irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and emollient properties. It helps reduce swelling and discomfort in the respiratory and digestive tracts. Applications include throat inflammation and gastrointestinal upset. Its mild nature makes it suitable for internal and external use under proper guidance.

8. Respiratory allergies

Rapeseed has been traditionally used to alleviate respiratory allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce symptoms like sneezing, nasal congestion, and itching by modulating the immune response. The oil and extracts from rapeseed are often utilized in herbal formulations for their soothing effects on the respiratory system.

9. Otitis media

Rapeseed (Brassica napus) has been used in herbal medicine to treat otitis media due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce ear inflammation and combat bacterial infections. Typically, it is prepared as a poultice or infused oil applied to the affected area. Its effectiveness is supported by traditional use and some clinical studies.

10. Sinusitis

Rapeseed has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and mucus production, often prepared as a steam inhalation or nasal spray. Its essential oils, particularly those from the seeds, support respiratory health and can be used as a natural remedy for chronic sinus issues.
