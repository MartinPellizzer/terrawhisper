Polystichum squarrosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Polystichum squarrosum?

Polystichum squarrosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the order Polypodiales, the plant is placed within the family Polypodiaceae. Its taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement in the genus Polystichum, specifically identified as the species squarrosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polystichum squarrosum has a perennial growth habit characterized by spreading, decumbent stems that often form dense, low-lying mats.

The leaves are primarily lanceolate or linear in shape and exhibit a somewhat squarrose or spreading arrangement along the branches. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically appearing in clusters or solitary arrangements. The texture of the foliage can vary from smooth to slightly pubescent depending on the specific environmental conditions.

These structural features allow the plant to effectively colonize rocky or disturbed substrates.

      What are the herbal preparations of Polystichum squarrosum?

      Polystichum squarrosum is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion made from its fronds to utilize its medicinal properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Polystichum squarrosum can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, or allergic reactions if the fronds come into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or a localized rash after handling the foliage. The plant contains various chemical compounds that can trigger these dermatological responses in susceptible people. It is advisable to wear protective gloves when pruning or managing this fern to avoid discomfort. Avoiding eye contact with the plant's spores or juices can also prevent irritation to the ocular membranes.
