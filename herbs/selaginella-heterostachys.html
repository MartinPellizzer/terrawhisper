Selaginella heterostachys

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella heterostachys has a creeping rhizome that produces small, scale-like leaves arranged in four distinct rows along its branched stems. The plant displays a heterosporous reproductive strategy where it produces two different types of spores within specialized sporangia. These sporangia are typically found in dense, localized clusters located at the tips or axils of the branches. The foliage consists of tiny, overlapping microphylls that create a textured, moss-like appearance. This specific morphology allows the plant to thrive in moist, shaded environments where it can spread horizontally across the substrate.
