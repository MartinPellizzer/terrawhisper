Dendrobium williamsonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium williamsonii has sympodial pseudobulbs that are cylindrical, fleshy, and reach lengths of approximately 15 to 30 centimeters. These pseudobulbs are typically green and support several thin, leathery leaves at their apex. The inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are predominantly white or pale cream with subtle yellow or pinkish markings in the throat. Each flower features a distinctive labellum that provides a landing platform for pollinators. The overall growth habit is epiphytic, allowing the plant to anchor itself firmly to tree branches in its natural habitat.
