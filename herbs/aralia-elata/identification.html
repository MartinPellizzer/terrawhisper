Botanical Identification of Manchurian Aralia (Aralia elata)

To identify Manchurian Aralia (Aralia elata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Manchurian Aralia belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Araliaceae, genus Aralia, and species Aralia elata. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Manchurian Aralia is Aralia elata, first described by the botanist Carl Peter Thunberg in 1794. It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is also known by several synonyms, including Aralia mandshurica and Aralia davidiana. This name reflects its native range in eastern Asia, particularly in China and Russia.

Morphological Characteristics

Manchurian Aralia is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a compact growth habit. Its root system is fibrous and moderately deep, supporting its upright structure. The stem is smooth, slender, and often branched, growing up to 3 meters in height. Leaves are pinnately compound, with 5-9 leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The inflorescence is a branched compound umbel, bearing small greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a spherical, yellowish berry that matures in late summer, containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Manchurian Aralia can be distinguished by its compound leaves with 5-7 pairs of leaflets, opposite arrangement, and smooth, dark green stems. It has large, umbel-like inflorescences with white flowers and produces berries that turn from green to red. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Aralia spinosa.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Manchurian Aralia is native to eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Russia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Manchurian Aralia blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early fall, around July to August. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, when the plant is most distinctive.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Manchurian Aralia (Aralia elata) is closely related to species like Aralia mandshurica and Aralia chinensis. It belongs to the genus Aralia, which is part of the Araliaceae family. The family is characterized by compound leaves, berries, and often aromatic foliage.
