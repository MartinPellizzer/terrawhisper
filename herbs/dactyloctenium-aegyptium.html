Dactyloctenium aegyptium

What's the taxonomical classification of Dactyloctenium aegyptium?

Dactyloctenium aegyptium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Poales. This plant is further categorized into the family Poaceae, which places it within the genus Dactyloctenium, ultimately identifying it as the specific species aegyptium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dactyloctenium aegyptium has a tufted, perennial growth habit characterized by dense, upright culms that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are linear and narrow, featuring a prominent midrib and a scabrous texture along the margins. Its inflorescence is a compact, cylindrical spike composed of numerous small spikelets arranged tightly around a central axis. Each spikelet contains multiple florets that eventually mature into small, rounded caryopses. This grass is highly adapted to arid environments due to its specialized fibrous root system.
