Gardenia hainanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Gardenia hainanensis?

Gardenia hainanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Gentianales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae and falls under the genus Gardenia, specifically identified by its species name, hainanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gardenia hainanenensis has dark green, elliptical leaves with an entire margin and a glossy texture that typically measures between five and twelve centimeters in length. Its flowers are solitary and white, featuring large, fragrant petals that emerge from terminal clusters. The reproductive organs consist of a prominent style and several stamens located at the center of the corolla. Small, fleshy drupes develop from the fertilized flowers, eventually turning dark as they mature.
