Allium thunbergii

What's the taxonomical classification of Allium thunbergii?

Allium thunbergii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, which falls under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Asparagales and into the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Allium, specifically identified by the species epithet thunbergii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium thunbergii has a bulbous root system, linear to lanceolate leaves, and an umbel-shaped inflorescence composed of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows from a compact bulb that provides nutrients for its seasonal development. Its leaves are slender and emerge directly from the base to support the upright flowering stem. The dense clusters of flowers are held aloft on a sturdy, hollow scape that can reach varying heights depending on environmental conditions. Each individual blossom features delicate tepals and prominent reproductive organs characteristic of the Amaryllidaceae family.
