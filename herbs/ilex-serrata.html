Ilex serrata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex serrata?

Ilex serrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further belongs to the order Aquifoliales and is a member of the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Ilex, with its specific identification being the species serrata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex serrata has small, dark green, elliptical leaves that feature distinctively serrated margins and a leathery texture. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody, grayish-brown stems that support a compact, spreading growth habit. The plant produces inconspicuous, small white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. Following pollination, the plant develops small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a deep black color upon ripening. This shrubby species typically maintains a dense canopy of foliage that provides significant structural complexity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils within mountainous regions and shaded woodland areas. The species occupies a specific ecological niche characterized by cool, humid climates and moderate rainfall. While its natural range is restricted to the Japanese archipelago, it is occasionally introduced to other temperate regions for ornamental purposes.
