Brazilian Feverfew (Baccharis dracunculifolia) Compounds

The primary compounds in Brazilian feverfew include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin and terpenoids contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. Essential oils and glycosides also play roles in its therapeutic effects.
