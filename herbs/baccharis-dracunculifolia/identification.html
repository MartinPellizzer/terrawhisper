Baccharis dracunculifolia is a shrub with a compact growth habit. It has a deep taproot system, supporting its upright stem. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and hairy. The plant produces dense clusters of small yellow flowers, followed by small, dry fruits containing tiny seeds.

Baccharis dracunculifolia can be distinguished by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and its clusters of small yellow flowers. Native to South America, Brazilian Feverfew thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found in lowland areas. Its geographic distribution spans Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina, typically growing at elevations between 300 and 1,000 meters.

Brazilian Feverfew blooms from September to November, with seed formation occurring from October to December. Harvesting is typically done during the flowering period, from September to November, to ensure optimal identification and potency of the plant.
