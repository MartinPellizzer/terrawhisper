Rubus sachalinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus sachalinensis?

Rubus sachalinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Rubus and is specifically designated as the species sachalinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus sachalinensis has arching, woody stems characterized by a dense covering of sharp, recurved prickles and a distinctively glandular-pubescent surface. The leaves are typically trifoliate or quinquefoliate, featuring serrated margins and a texture that can vary from glabrous to densely hairy depending on the specific variety. Small, white to pale pink flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the branches, showcasing five distinct petals and numerous stamens. Following pollination, the plant produces aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep, succulent red or black upon ripening. This morphology is well-adapted for climbing and spreading through its surrounding forest habitat.
