Ophiopogon mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiopogon mairei?

Ophiopogon mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Asparagales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Asparagaceae, which contains the genus Ophiopogon, and it is specifically identified by its species name, mairei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophiopogon mairei has narrow, linear, dark green leaves that form dense tufts and bear small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. The foliage typically reaches a height of about twenty centimeters and features a smooth, grass-like texture. Following the flowering period, the plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that hang from thin pedicels. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that allow it to spread slowly through the soil. These structural characteristics make it a popular choice for low-maintenance groundcover in shaded garden settings.
