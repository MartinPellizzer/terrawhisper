Ophiopogon mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiopogon mairei?

Ophiopogon mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Asparagales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Asparagaceae, which contains the genus Ophiopogon, and it is specifically identified by its species name, mairei.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ophiopogon mairei contains saponins, alkaloids, and various flavonoids that serve as its primary bioactive components. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory ailments and inflammation. The saponins within the plant are particularly noted for their potential to promote blood circulation and act as mild expectorants. Researchers study these compounds to understand their specific roles in modulating immune responses and protecting cellular structures. Detailed pharmacological analysis suggests that these medicinal properties are central to the species' value in herbal medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ophiopogon mairei?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as cough, asthma, and bronchitis, as well as fever and inflammatory conditions. It functions primarily by moistening the lungs and clearing heat from the body to alleviate dryness. In traditional Chinese medicine, it is often prescribed to soothe a sore throat or reduce palpitations. The plant's medicinal properties are frequently utilized to address symptoms caused by yin deficiency. This makes it a valuable component in various herbal formulas designed to restore internal balance.
