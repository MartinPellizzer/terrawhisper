Asplenium nidus

What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium nidus?

Asplenium nidus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the hierarchical order of Polypodiales, the plant is placed in the family Aspleniaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Asplenium and the specific species nidus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium nidus has large, undivided, lanceolate fronds that grow in a circular, rosette-like arrangement from a central rhizome. These bright green leaves feature long, tapering tips and prominent midribs that provide structural support for the wide blades. The foliage is smooth and leathery in texture, often reaching significant lengths depending on the plant's maturity. Because the fronds emerge from a central point, the plant creates a nest-like shape that naturally collects organic debris. This unique growth pattern allows the fern to thrive as an epiphyte in tropical forest canopies.
