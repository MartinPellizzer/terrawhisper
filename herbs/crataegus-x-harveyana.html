Harvey'S Hawthorn (Crataegus × Harveyana)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus × harveyana.

Harvey's hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus × harveyana, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States, prized for its medicinal properties and adaptogenic qualities. It is traditionally used to support cardiovascular health due to its high concentration of flavonoids and oligomeric proanthocyanidins, which help improve circulation and strengthen heart function. Historically, Native American tribes used Harvey's hawthorn to treat ailments such as heart conditions, anxiety, and insomnia, while in modern wellness practices, it is incorporated into herbal supplements for stress relief and cognitive enhancement. The plant is also valued for its unique, tart flavor, making it a rare spice in traditional cuisines, and its historical significance as a symbol of resilience in Appalachian folklore adds to its cultural appeal. Its combination of traditional wisdom and scientific validation makes it a noteworthy herb in both herbal medicine and contemporary holistic health practices.
