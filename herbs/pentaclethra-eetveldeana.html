Pentaclethra eetveldeana

What's the taxonomical classification of Pentaclethra eetveldeana?

Pentaclethra eetveldanea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, which falls under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Pentaclethra, with its unique species designation being eetveldanea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pentaclethrha eetveldana contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, tannins, saponins, and flavonoids that serve as its primary medicinal components. These chemical constituents are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as stomach aches and skin infections. The presence of tannins specifically provides significant antimicrobial and astringent properties to the plant extracts. Research suggests that the various phytochemicals work synergistically to offer anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Consequently, these specific compounds form the pharmacological basis for the plant's therapeutic applications in local communities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pentaclethra eetveldeana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat malaria, dysentery, diarrhea, stomach aches, and various skin infections. Its leaves and roots are often prepared as decoctions or infusions to alleviate these specific ailments. Local communities frequently rely on these medicinal properties for gastrointestinal distress and febrile illnesses. The plant is also applied topically to address dermatological issues and wound healing. These traditional uses highlight the significant ethnobotanical value of the species in regional healthcare.
