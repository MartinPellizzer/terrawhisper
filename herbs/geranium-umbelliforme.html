What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium umbelliforme?

Geranium umbelliforme belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further belongs to the order Geraniales and the family Geraniaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Geranium and is specifically identified by its species name, umbelliforme.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium umbelliforme has a creeping, rhizomatous growth habit characterized by long, trailing stems that produce rounded, palmately lobed leaves and small, five-petaled flowers arranged in terminal umbels. The foliage typically consists of several deeply divided lobes that radiate from a central point, creating a fan-like appearance. Its flowers are generally pink or white and emerge from the ends of the stems on slender peduncles. The plant often develops a dense mat-like structure as its stems root at various nodes along the ground.
