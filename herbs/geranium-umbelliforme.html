Geranium umbelliforme

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium umbelliforme has a creeping, rhizomatous growth habit characterized by long, trailing stems that produce rounded, palmately lobed leaves and small, five-petaled flowers arranged in terminal umbels. The foliage typically consists of several deeply divided lobes that radiate from a central point, creating a fan-like appearance. Its flowers are generally pink or white and emerge from the ends of the stems on slender peduncles. The plant often develops a dense mat-like structure as its stems root at various nodes along the ground. This morphological adaptation allows it to spread effectively across rocky or shaded substrates.

      Geranium umbelliforme is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using its fresh or dried leaves and stems.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Geranium umbelliforme can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if the plant is ingested or touched directly. While there is limited clinical data on human toxicity, many species within the Geranium genus contain compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingesting any part of the plant could lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its chemical constituents. It is best to use gloves when handling the foliage to prevent potential dermatological issues. Always keep this plant out of reach of pets and children to avoid accidental poisoning.
