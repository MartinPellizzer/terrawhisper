Squarrose Trigonella (Trigonella Squarrosa)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Trigonella squarrosa.

Squarrose trigonella, scientifically known as Trigonella squarrosa, is a medicinal herb native to the Mediterranean region, valued for its adaptogenic and nourishing properties. This small legume is rich in essential nutrients, including vitamins, minerals, and amino acids, and has been traditionally used to support digestive health and overall vitality. In ancient Greek and Arabian medicine, it was prized for its ability to aid in weight management and improve skin condition, often incorporated into herbal teas and tonics. Today, it is gaining recognition in modern wellness for its potential to enhance metabolic function and support immune health, especially when consumed as a powder or in capsule form. Notably, Trigonella squarrosa contains unique compounds like saponins and flavonoids, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and may offer additional therapeutic benefits.
