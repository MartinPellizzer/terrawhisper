    What is Myrcia sagotii?

    Myrcia sagotii is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

    It is a small tree or shrub, typically growing up to 10 meters in height, with a dense, grayish-brown bark and a straight, branchless trunk. The plant has elliptical leaves, 6-12 centimeters long and 2-4 centimeters wide, with an acute apex and an entire margin, arranged oppositely on the branches. The inflorescences are axillary corymbs, bearing 4-6 white, 4-merous flowers, 1-2 centimeters in diameter, with 4 stamens and a 4-carpellate ovary.

    The fruits are small, yellowish-brown, 6-8 millimeter drupes, containing a single seed.
