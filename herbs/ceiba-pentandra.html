Ceiba pentandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Ceiba pentandra?

Ceiba pentandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Ceiba, reaching its specific identification at the species level as pentandra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ceiba pentandra has a large deciduous tree structure characterized by a thick, grayish-brown trunk that often features prominent, conical spines on its younger branches. The foliage consists of large, paripinnate leaves with five leaflets that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. During its flowering stage, the tree produces large, cream-colored flowers that are solitary or arranged in small clusters. Once pollinated, these flowers develop into woody, capsule-like fruits that eventually split to release numerous small, winged seeds. The overall architecture of the tree is spreading, often forming a wide and majestic canopy.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning from India and Sri Lanka through Myanmar, Thailand, and into parts of Indochina. It thrives predominantly in moist deciduous forests and along riverbanks where soil moisture is consistent. Its range extends across various elevations within these tropical climates, allowing it to colonize diverse ecological niches. The distribution is largely dictated by seasonal monsoon patterns that provide the necessary rainfall for its growth. What are the pharmacological activities of Ceiba pentandra?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, cytotoxic, and antiandrepanocytary activities. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds work to neutralize free radicals, which provides a strong antioxidant effect. The plant also exhibits the ability to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, its cytotoxic properties suggest potential applications in cancer research by targeting specific cell types. Studies have also identified its unique antiandrepanocytary activity, which may assist in managing sickle cell disease.

As discussed in a study, the ethyl acetate extract of Ceiba pentandra reduces methotrexate-induced renal damage through antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antiapoptotic actions. This extract demonstrated powerful in vitro radical-scavenging potential with an IC50 of 0.0716. When administered at 400 mg/kg/day orally for 5 days before and 5 days after a single 20 mg/kg methotrexate injection, Ceiba pentandra showed slight superiorities over silymarin at 100 mg/kg/day in restoring renal functions. The extract produced approximately twofold decreases in overall kidney function tests and improved renal antioxidant capacity. Additionally, Ceiba pentandra inhibited apoptotic and inflammatory cascades while attenuating histopathological changes in renal tissue architecture.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (+) -catechin, beta-sitosterol, mangiferin, quercitrin, and vavain. These bioactive molecules contribute to the diverse therapeutic profile often associated with the species. Mangiferin and (+) -catechin are particularly noted for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the presence of beta-sitosterol and quercitrin may offer various health benefits related to cellular protection. The inclusion of vavain further enhances the complex chemical makeup of the plant's extract.

As discussed in a study, the bark of Ceiba pentandra contains the known flavan-3-ol (+)‐catechin and two new isoflavones, the glucoside vavain 3'-O-beta-d-glucoside (1) and its aglycon, vavain (2). These compounds were identified as 5-hydroxy-7,4',5'-trimethoxyisoflavone 3'-O-beta-D-glucoside (1) and 5,3'-dihydroxy-7,4',5'-trimethoxyisoflavone (2). In a cyclooxygenase-1-catalyzed prostaglandin biosynthesis assay, compound 1, compound 2, and (+)‐catechin exhibited IC50 values of 381, 97, and 80 microM, respectively. When testing for inhibitory effects on cyclooxygenase-2-catalyzed prostaglandin biosynthesis, compound 1 and compound 2 were found to be inactive with IC50 values of > 1200 and > 900 microM.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating various medical conditions including Trypanoosoma brucei brucei, leishmaniasis, diabetes mellitus, angiogenesis, and sickle cell anemia. Researchers investigate its chemical properties to combat parasitic infections caused by protozoa like Trypanoosoma brucei brucei and the agents responsible for leishmaniasis. Its bioactive compounds also show potential in managing blood glucose levels for those suffering from diabetes mellitus. Furthermore, the plant is studied for its ability to influence angiogenesis, which is crucial in understanding tumor growth and wound healing. Finally, its medicinal applications extend to providing therapeutic benefits for patients living with sickle cell anemia.
