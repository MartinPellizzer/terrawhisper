Ceiba pentandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Ceiba pentandra?

Ceiba pentandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Ceiba, reaching its specific identification at the species level as pentandra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ceiba pentandra contains alkaloids, saponins, tannins, and flavonoids that serve as the primary bioactive constituents in its medicinal applications. The bark of the tree is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin infections and inflammatory conditions due to these chemical properties. Extracts from the leaves are often employed to manage digestive issues and fever through their antimicrobial effects. Various parts of the plant also exhibit antioxidant qualities that help mitigate oxidative stress in the body. These diverse compounds work together to provide a broad range of therapeutic benefits in herbal remedies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ceiba pentandra?

Ceiba pentandra has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties derived primarily from its bark, leaves, and roots. The plant is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea and dysentery. Its extracts can also help reduce swelling and alleviate localized pain through natural anti-inflammatory mechanisms. Additionally, certain preparations are used to manage skin infections and promote wound healing. These therapeutic effects make it a valuable resource in various ethnobotanical practices for addressing systemic and topical ailments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, digestive issues, rheumatism, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are often prepared into decoctions to alleviate stomach aches and intestinal discomfort. Topical applications of the plant material can help soothe skin irritations and promote healing of wounds. It is also frequently employed in traditional medicine to reduce joint pain associated with rheumatic ailments. Additionally, certain parts of the plant are utilized to manage fever and other systemic inflammatory responses.
