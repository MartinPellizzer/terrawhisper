What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Avicennia marina has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are opposite or sub-opposite, ranging from 2 to 7 centimeters in length, and possess a distinct grayish-green color with a salty texture.

The plant is a woody shrub or tree characterized by a complex branching system and a rugged bark texture. To manage high salinity, it utilizes specialized salt glands on its leaf surfaces to excrete excess salt crystals. It also develops pneumatophores, which are pencil-like aerial roots that grow upward from the anaerobic mud to facilitate gas exchange.

These morphological adaptations allow the species to thrive in harsh intertidal environments.
