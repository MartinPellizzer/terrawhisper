What are the therapeutic actions of Avicennia marina?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, gastrointestinal ailments, and various inflammatory conditions. Extracts from its leaves and bark often possess antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial and fungal pathogens. It is frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and accelerate the healing of minor cuts. Some traditional medicinal practices also utilize the plant to alleviate symptoms of stomach aches and digestive discomfort. Research continues to investigate its bioactive compounds for potential therapeutic applications in modern medicine.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Avicennia marina can cause skin irritation, digestive distress, and potential toxicity if ingested in significant quantities due to its high concentration of alkaloids and tannins. While the plant is often studied for medicinal properties, these secondary metabolites can trigger allergic reactions upon direct contact with the dermis. Consuming unprocessed parts of the plant may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain in sensitive individuals. Furthermore, the specific chemical profile of the mangrove species means that its safety depends heavily on the method of preparation and dosage. Careful scientific scrutiny is required to avoid the adverse physiological effects associated with its bioactive compounds.
