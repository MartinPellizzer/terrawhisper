Launaea rarifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Launaea rarifolia?

Launaea rarifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Launaea and is designated by the species name rarifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Launaea rarifolia has an erect, woody-based herbaceous habit characterized by sparsely distributed, linear-oblong leaves and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant typically presents a branched structure with stems that are often covered in fine, spreading hairs. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and possess entire to slightly serrated margins. The inflorescence consists of discoid heads that are clustered in terminal or axillary positions. Each head is supported by an involucre of narrow, linear phyllaries.
