What is Pouteria rigida?

Pouteria rigida is a species of flowering plant in the family Sapotaceae.

It is a deciduous tree, typically growing to 15-20 meters in height, with a straight bole and a rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, 10-20 cm long, and elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture and entire margins. The flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in axillary corymbs, while the fruits are a syncarp of 2-5 berries, 6-10 cm in diameter, and red or yellow when ripe.

Pouteria rigida is native to the tropical forests of South America, specifically in the countries of French Guiana, Guyana, Suriname, and Venezuela.
