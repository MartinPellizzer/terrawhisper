Salvia przewalskii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvias przewalskii has a perennial growth habit characterized by upright, square-shaped stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and bear opposite, rugose leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a textured, wrinkled surface that distinguishes its vegetative structure. Its inflorescence consists of elongated terminal spikes featuring hooded bilabiate flowers that transition from blue to violet hues. Each flower is supported by a prominent calyx that often exhibits a reddish or purplish tint. The plant's root system is robust, allowing it to establish a stable base for its vertical flowering stalks.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude mountainous regions of central and western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Gansu, and Qinghai. It typically thrives in alpine meadows and rocky slopes located at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 3,500 meters above sea level. The distribution follows the rugged terrain of the eastern edge of the Tibetan Plateau. Within these specific microclimates, the species relies on the cool, moist conditions provided by mountain ecosystems.
