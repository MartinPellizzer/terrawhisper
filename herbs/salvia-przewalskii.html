Salvia przewalskii

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia przewalskii?

Salvia przewalskii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Lamiales and belongs to the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Salvia, specifically identified by its unique species designation, przewalskii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvias przewalskii has a perennial growth habit characterized by upright, square-shaped stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and bear opposite, rugose leaves with serrated margins.

The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a textured, wrinkled surface that distinguishes its vegetative structure. Its inflorescence consists of elongated terminal spikes featuring hooded bilabiate flowers that transition from blue to violet hues. Each flower is supported by a prominent calyx that often exhibits a reddish or purplish tint.

The plant's root system is robust, allowing it to establish a stable base for its vertical flowering stalks.

        What side-effects this plant can have?
