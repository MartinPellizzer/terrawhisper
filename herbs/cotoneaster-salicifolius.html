Cotoneaster salicifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. It is specifically identified as a member of the genus Cotoneaster, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name salicifolius.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cotoneaster salicifoлия can cause gastrointestinal distress including vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain if the berries are ingested by humans or animals. The primary danger stems from the presence of cyanogenic glycosides within the fruit which can release toxic cyanide upon consumption. While the leaves and stems are generally less concentrated, they still pose a risk of irritation or mild toxicity if eaten in large quantities. Children and pets are particularly vulnerable to these effects because they are more likely to mistake the small red berries for food. Proper identification and avoidance of ingestion are necessary to prevent these adverse physiological reactions.
