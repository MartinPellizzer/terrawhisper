What's the taxonomical classification of Cotoneaster salicifolius?

Cotoneaster salicifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the evolutionary lineage of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. It is specifically identified as a member of the genus Cotoneaster, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name salicifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cotoneaster salicifolius has woody, spreading stems that produce lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves measuring between 3 and 7 centimeters in length with finely serrated margins.

The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and often displays a paler underside. During the flowering season, small white flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy red berries that persist on the branches.

The overall growth habit is that of a deciduous shrub with a dense and somewhat irregular structure.
