Scaevola taccada

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scaevola taccada has large, fleshy, lanceolate leaves that reach up to 60 centimeters in length and grow in a basal rosette pattern. These evergreen leaves feature a smooth, somewhat leathery texture and a deep green color. The plant produces unique, fan-shaped white or blue flowers that are arranged in terminal clusters. Each individual floret possesses a distinct semi-circular shape, which gives the inflorescence its characteristic asymmetric appearance. This tropical shrub typically grows to a height of about one meter, supported by sturdy, succulent stems.
