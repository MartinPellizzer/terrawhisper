Eriophyton wallichii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eriophyton wallichii has a microscopic, worm-like body shape characterized by a long, tapered anterior end and a blunt posterior that lacks legs or wings. The mouthparts are specialized into a piercing-sucking stylet used to feed on host plant cells. Small sensory structures called epistomes are located near the head to help navigate its environment.

The structures of these 7 compounds were elucidated through spectral analysis and physicochemical properties.
