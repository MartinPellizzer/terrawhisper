What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oreodoxa regia has a woody structure characterized by upright stems, lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are typically arranged oppositely along the branches and possess a leathery texture. Its stem development follows a perennial growth pattern, providing a sturdy framework for the foliage. The reproductive organs are relatively subtle compared to the expansive vegetative parts of the plant. These morphological traits collectively define its specific niche within its native habitat.

      What are the herbal preparations of Oreodoxa regia?

      Oreodoxa regia is prepared as decoctions, infusions, poultices, and topical oils to utilize its various medicinal properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Oreodoxa regia can cause skin irritation, digestive distress, and potential allergic reactions when handled or consumed improperly. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may lead to localized redness, itching, or contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If ingested, users might experience symptoms such as nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting. Some people may also develop more severe hypersensitivity symptoms like swelling or respiratory discomfort. It is important to exercise caution and consult a medical professional if any adverse reactions occur.
