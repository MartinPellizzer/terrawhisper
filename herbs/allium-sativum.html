Allium Sativum: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Allium sativum, commonly known as garlic, is a flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family, widely cultivated for its pungent bulb used in cooking and traditional medicine.

It is renowned for its potent health benefits, including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and cardiovascular support properties.

Therapeutically, garlic helps lower blood pressure, improve cholesterol levels, and boost immune function.

Its bioactive constituents, such as allicin, sulfur compounds, and flavonoids, contribute to its wide range of medicinal effects.
