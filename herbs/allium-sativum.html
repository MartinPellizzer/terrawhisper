The therapeutic actions of Allium sativum include its ability to stimulate digestion, improve cardiovascular health, and act as an expectorant to relieve respiratory issues.

Possible Side Effects of allium sativum

Allium sativum can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with medications.

Consuming large amounts of garlic, the herb derived from Allium sativum, can cause stomach upset, diarrhea, and nausea.

Improper use of Allium sativum may also lead to interactions with blood thinners, resulting in increased bleeding risk, and exacerbate conditions like high blood pressure and acid reflux.

Furthermore, Allium sativum may not be suitable for individuals with certain medical conditions, such as kidney or liver disease, and should be used under medical guidance.
