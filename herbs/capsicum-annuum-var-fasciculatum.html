Poblano Pepper (Capsicum Annuum Var. Fasciculatum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Capsicum annuum var. fasciculatum.

Poblano pepper, scientifically known as Capsicum annuum var. fasciculatum, is a versatile medicinal herb and spice widely used in traditional and modern wellness practices. It is valued for its capsaicin content, which provides anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antioxidant benefits, making it useful for pain relief and digestive health. In traditional Mexican and Central American cultures, Poblano peppers have been used to treat ailments such as coughs, respiratory issues, and skin conditions. Today, they are incorporated into wellness routines for their ability to boost metabolism, reduce inflammation, and support cardiovascular health. Notably, the pepper's distinctive mild to medium heat and rich, earthy flavor make it a staple in culinary and therapeutic applications, with a historical presence dating back to the Aztec civilization.
