Impatiens davidi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens davidi has succulent, erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and feature lanceolate, serrated leaves with pointed tips. The foliage is characterized by a bright green color and a smooth, waxy texture that aids in moisture retention. Its flowers are zygomorphic and appear in shades of pink or pale purple, often emerging from the leaf axils. Each blossom features a distinct nectar spur at the rear of the petal structure. The plant concludes its growth cycle by producing small, elongated seed capsules that burst upon contact.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan, Sichuan, and Guizhou. It typically thrives in the moist, shaded environments found along mountain slopes and near forest streams. Within these mountainous territories, it occupies specific ecological niches defined by temperate climates and consistent moisture. Some populations may also be found in localized areas of neighboring Southeast Asian highlands. Its distribution is closely tied to the unique topography and rainfall patterns of the Hengduan Mountains.
