What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens davidi?

Impatiens davidi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Ericales. The plant is a member of the family Balsaminaceae, which falls under the genus Impatiens, and is specifically identified by its species name, davidi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens davidi has succulent, erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and feature lanceolate, serrated leaves with pointed tips. The foliage is characterized by a bright green color and a smooth, waxy texture that aids in moisture retention. Its flowers are zygomorphic and appear in shades of pink or pale purple, often emerging from the leaf axils. Each blossom features a distinct nectar spur at the rear of the petal structure. The plant concludes its growth cycle by producing small, elongated seed capsules that burst upon contact.

Within these mountainous territories, it occupies specific ecological niches defined by temperate climates and consistent moisture.
