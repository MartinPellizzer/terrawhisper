Garcinia paucinervis

What's the taxonomical classification of Garcinia paucinervis?

Garcinia paucinervis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Malpighiales and falls under the family Clusiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Garcinia and is specifically designated as the species paucinervis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Garcinia paucinervis contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including xanthones, prenylated flavonoids, and various terpenoids. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's pericarp and stem bark. Researchers have identified specific xanthone derivatives that exhibit significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies also suggest that these compounds possess potential antimicrobial and cytotoxic activities against certain cancer cell lines. The unique chemical profile of this species makes it a subject of interest for developing new pharmacological agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Garcinia paucinervis?

Garcinia paucinervis has potent antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties derived from its high concentration of bioactive xanthones and polyphenolic compounds. These secondary metabolites work by inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi through the disruption of cellular membranes. Additionally, the plant extracts neutralize free radicals, which helps to reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. Its ability to modulate inflammatory pathways makes it a subject of interest for managing swelling and tissue irritation. Researchers continue to investigate these phytochemicals for their potential applications in developing new pharmaceutical agents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and inflammatory conditions. Local communities often apply its extracts topically to promote healing and combat bacterial growth on the skin. When ingested, the plant is believed to help alleviate digestive discomfort and gastrointestinal distress. Its chemical properties are also studied for their potential to reduce systemic inflammation within the body. These traditional medicinal uses highlight the plant's diverse therapeutic applications in various regional cultures.
