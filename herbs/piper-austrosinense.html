What's the taxonomical classification of Piper austrosinense?

Piper austrosinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Piperales, it is a member of the family Piperaceae. Finally, its taxonomic placement is completed at the genus level as Piper and at the specific level as austrosinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper austrosinense has woody stems with opposite or subopposite leaves that feature an ovate to elliptic shape, an acute apex, and an entire or slightly undulate margin. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, often displaying a lustrous surface on the upper side. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils. These spikes are characterized by a fleshy texture and can vary in length depending on the maturity of the plant. The fruit consists of tiny, succulent drupes that are tightly packed along the central axis of the spike.
