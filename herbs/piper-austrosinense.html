Piper austrosinense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper austrosinense has woody stems with opposite or subopposite leaves that feature an ovate to elliptic shape, an acute apex, and an entire or slightly undulate margin. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, often displaying a lustrous surface on the upper side. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils. These spikes are characterized by a fleshy texture and can vary in length depending on the maturity of the plant. The fruit consists of tiny, succulent drupes that are tightly packed along the central axis of the spike.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically concentrated within the archipelago of Indonesia. It thrives in humid, low-altitude rainforest environments where moisture levels remain consistently high. Most recorded populations are found on islands such as Sumatra, Java, and Borneo. The species occupies the understory layer of these forests, benefiting from the shaded and warm microclimates provided by the canopy. Its distribution is largely restricted to these specific equatorial zones due to its reliance on stable tropical weather patterns.
