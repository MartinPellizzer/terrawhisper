What's the taxonomical classification of Viola collina?

Viola collina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Malpighiales. Within this order, the plant is placed in the family Violaceae, which contains the genus Viola. Finally, it is identified at the specific level as the species collina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola collina has an herbaceous perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are reniform to cordate in shape with crenate margins and a distinctively long petiole. The plant features a creeping rhizome that facilitates spreading through its preferred rocky or montane habitats. Its inflorescences produce small, zygomorphic flowers that typically exhibit a violet or pale purple coloration with a yellow throat. The leaves possess thin, membranous textures and are arranged in a basal rosette pattern. Small, ovoid capsules develop from the flowers to distribute seeds upon maturity.
