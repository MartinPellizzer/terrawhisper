Polystichum craspedosorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polystichum craspedosorum has pinnate fronds that typically measure between 30 and 60 centimeters in length with individual pinnae that are oblong and slightly serrated along the margins. The texture of the foliage is leathery and thick, providing a robust structure to the overall appearance of the fern. Each leaflet possesses a distinct midrib and often features small, dark scales along the stipe and rachis. The underside of the fronds contains conspicuous sori arranged in neat rows under protective indusia. These morphological traits collectively distinguish this species within its genus.

      Polystichum craspedosorum is prepared as a decoction, a concentrated tincture, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic application.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Polystichum craspedosorum can cause acute contact dermatitis, localized swelling, and intense skin itching upon direct exposure to its fronds. The plant contains specific irritants that trigger inflammatory responses in sensitive individuals. Users may experience redness or small hives if the sap comes into contact with bare skin. Prolonged contact can lead to more persistent rashes that require topical treatment. It is advisable to wear protective gloves when handling this species to prevent these adverse reactions.
