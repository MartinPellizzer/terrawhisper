As a horticultural specimen, larch trees are prized for their striking yellow fall foliage and ability to thrive in challenging climates. From a botanical standpoint, larch belongs to the pine family (Pinaceae) and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and distinctive resinous sap.

With ancient historical references dating back to traditional Chinese medicine and medieval European folk remedies, larch has been a valuable ingredient for centuries, earning it a revered place in the world of herbs and botany.
