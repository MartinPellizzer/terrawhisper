Melilotus albus

What's the taxonomical classification of Melilotus albus?

Melilotus albus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it among the legumes, and is further categorized into the genus Melilotus, with the specific designation of the species albus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Melilotus albus contains coumarin, flavonoids, and various phenolic acids that provide its primary medicinal properties. The presence of coumarin allows the plant to function as a precursor for anticoagulant treatments in pharmacological applications. Flavonoids within the plant contribute antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects that help mitigate oxidative stress in cells. Additionally, its various phenolic compounds offer antimicrobial properties that may assist in fighting certain pathogens. These chemical constituents collectively make the plant a subject of interest for studying natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Melilotus albus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, digestive disorders, respiratory ailments, and certain skin irritations. Its medicinal properties are largely attributed to the presence of coumarins, which possess significant anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant effects. Historically, it has been applied to soothe sore throats and alleviate symptoms of coughs or bronchitis. In topical applications, extracts are sometimes used to reduce swelling and manage minor skin inflammations. Careful dosing is essential because the plant's chemical composition can impact blood clotting mechanisms.
