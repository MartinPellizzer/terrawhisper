Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis) Uses

Field Marigold is used to treat eczema, wound healing, skin infections, and dermatitis. It also helps with skin allergies, burns, sunburn, and skin inflammation. The plant is effective for itching and skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis) Monograph

1. Eczema

Field Marigold is effective for eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties. It helps reduce skin irritation and redness while promoting healing. Topical applications of its infused oil or salves can soothe affected areas. It is often used in herbal remedies for chronic or recurring eczema.

2. Wound healing

Field Marigold is valued for its wound-healing properties, promoting tissue repair and reducing inflammation. Its active compounds, including flavonoids and triterpenes, support skin regeneration and prevent infection. It is commonly used topically to treat cuts, abrasions, and minor burns. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects make it a key herb in traditional herbal medicine for healing wounds.

3. Skin infections

Field Marigold is effective for treating skin infections due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. It can be applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to reduce redness, swelling, and infection. Its active compounds help soothe irritated skin and promote healing in conditions like eczema and minor wounds.

4. Skin allergies

Field Marigold is used to alleviate skin allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and irritation associated with allergic reactions. Topical applications of its infused oil or salves can soothe sensitive skin and promote healing. It is particularly effective for eczema, hives, and contact dermatitis.

5. Burns

Field Marigold is used topically to treat burns due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps reduce pain, swelling, and infection risk. A poultice or infused oil made from its flowers can be applied to minor burns to promote healing and soothe the skin.

6. Dermatitis

Field Marigold is used to treat dermatitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps reduce skin irritation, redness, and infection. Infusions or ointments made from its flowers are commonly applied topically for conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Its active compounds promote healing and soothe inflamed skin.

7. Sunburn

Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis) is used topically to soothe sunburn due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation caused by UV exposure. The plant's compounds promote skin healing and provide a cooling effect. It is often applied as a salve or infused oil for relief.

8. Skin inflammation

Field Marigold is used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation. Topical applications are common, often in the form of creams or ointments. It is particularly effective for conditions like eczema and minor burns.

9. Itching

Field Marigold is used to alleviate itching caused by skin conditions such as eczema or insect bites. Its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties help reduce irritation and promote healing. Topical applications of its infused oil or poultice are commonly recommended for localized relief.

10. Skin irritation

Field Marigold is effective for treating skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It can soothe rashes, eczema, and minor burns when applied topically. Its active compounds reduce redness and promote healing. It is commonly used in salves and creams for external skin conditions.
