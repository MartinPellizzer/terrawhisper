10 Possible Side Effects Of Calendula Arvensis

Calendula arvensis has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased sensitivity to sunlight.

These side effects can be caused by topical application or ingestion of excessive amounts.

For example, skin irritation can lead to discomfort, itching, and redness, while increased sensitivity to sunlight can cause painful blistering or burning, potentially requiring medical attention and impacting daily activities and quality of life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Calendula arvensis causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The allergenic properties of this plant are attributed to its high concentration of sesquiterpenes, specifically sesquiterpene lactones, which can trigger an immune response and lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the flavonoids present in Calendula arvensis may also contribute to the allergenic effect, making it a potential irritant for those with sensitive skin or allergies.

2. Triggers skin irritation

Calendula arvensis triggers skin irritation.

This can be attributed to the plant's high concentration of sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, which are known to cause allergic reactions in some individuals. The oil extracted from Calendula arvensis may also contain compounds that stimulate the skin's immune response, leading to inflammation and discomfort.

As a result, users of Calendula arvensis products may experience redness, itching, or rashes on the skin.

3. Induces gastrointestinal upset

Calendula arvensis induces gastrointestinal upset due to its potential interaction with the gut microbiome.

The bioactive compounds present in Calendula arvensis, such as flavonoids and sesquiterpenes, may disrupt the balance of beneficial bacteria, leading to symptoms like nausea, diarrhea, or abdominal pain.

This is because these compounds can alter the integrity of the epithelial lining, increasing permeability and allowing toxins to pass through, ultimately causing gastrointestinal discomfort.

4. Provokes respiratory distress

Calendula arvensis provokes respiratory distress due to its ability to stimulate the mucous membranes in the lungs and airways.

The plant's bitter compounds can cause inflammation and constriction of the bronchial tubes, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the irritating effects of Calendula arvensis can exacerbate existing respiratory conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

5. Stimulates excessive sweating

Calendula arvensis stimulates excessive sweating due to its ability to increase sweat gland activity.

This is attributed to its high concentration of flavonoids and triterpenes, which have been shown to enhance the body's natural thermoregulatory mechanisms.

As a result, individuals using Calendula arvensis may experience increased perspiration as their body attempts to cool itself more efficiently.

6. Increases heart rate variability

Calendula arvensis increases heart rate variability due to its ability to stimulate the vagus nerve, which regulates heart function.

This stimulation can lead to increased parasympathetic activity, causing a rise in heart rate and blood pressure variability.

As a result, some individuals may experience an elevated heart rate or palpitations when using Calendula arvensis, although this effect is generally considered mild and temporary.

7. Weakens immune system response

Calendula arvensis weakens immune system response by suppressing the activity of natural killer cells and macrophages, which are essential for fighting off infections and diseases.

This suppression can lead to reduced resistance to pathogens and increased susceptibility to illness.

As a result, users may experience decreased ability to fight off infections, making them more prone to illnesses and reducing their overall well-being.

8. Disrupts hormonal balance

Calendula arvensis disrupts hormonal balance due to its ability to mimic estrogen in the body.

As a phytoestrogen, it can bind to estrogen receptors, potentially altering the natural balance of hormones and leading to changes in menstrual cycles, mood swings, and other hormone-related issues.

9. Interferes with medication absorption

Calendula arvensis interferes with medication absorption by binding to proteins in the gut and altering the way nutrients are digested.

This interaction can reduce the efficacy of certain medications, including oral antibiotics and antihistamines. The flavonoids and other compounds present in Calendula arvensis may also slow down gastric emptying, leading to a decrease in medication absorption rates.

As a result, patients may experience reduced therapeutic effects or increased risk of side effects due to altered medication levels in the body.

10. Produces kidney stone risk

Calendula arvensis produces kidney stone risk.

This is due to its high concentration of oxalates, which can increase the formation of kidney stones by binding to calcium and other minerals in the urine.

Additionally, the herb's ability to stimulate digestive enzymes may also contribute to an increased risk of kidney stone formation by altering the body's natural pH levels.
