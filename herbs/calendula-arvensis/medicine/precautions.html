10 Precautions To Take When Using Calendula Arvensis (Pot Marigold)

Calendula arvensis has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as being cautious with its use in large doses or prolonged periods, as it can cause gastrointestinal upset and interact with certain medications.

Ingesting high amounts can lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Additionally, using Calendula arvensis internally without proper guidance can lead to allergic reactions and liver toxicity, so it's essential to follow recommended dosages and consult with a healthcare professional to avoid potential side effects.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Calendula arvensis medicinally.

1. Consult a doctor if unsure

When using Calendula arvensis medicinally, it's important to consume responsibly in pregnancy.

This precaution is necessary because there is limited research available on the safety of Calendula arvensis during pregnancy and breastfeeding. The herb may stimulate uterine contractions or cause other adverse effects that could harm the fetus or newborn.

Therefore, pregnant women should consult with a healthcare provider before using Calendula arvensis medicinally to ensure safe and effective treatment.
