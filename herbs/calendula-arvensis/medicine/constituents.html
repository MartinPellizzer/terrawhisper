10 Medicinal Constituents Of Calendula Arvensis (Pot Marigold)

Calendula arvensis has active constituents such as flavonoids, triterpenes, and saponins.

These compounds are responsible for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiseptic properties, making it a valuable herb in traditional medicine. For example, the flavonoids in calendula can help soothe skin irritations and wounds, reducing redness and inflammation, while the triterpenes may help to reduce fever and alleviate respiratory issues.

These medicinal properties make calendula an effective remedy for improving skin health, wound healing, and overall well-being.

1. Carotenoids

Calendula arvensis carotenoids is a rich source of bioactive compounds that have been extensively studied for their potential health benefits.

Calendula arvensis quercetin is a bioflavonoid compound extracted from its flowers.
