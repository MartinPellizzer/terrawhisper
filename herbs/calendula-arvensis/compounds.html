Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis) Compounds

Field Marigold contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, terpenoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Tannins and phenolic acids provide astringent and protective effects, while coumarin and glycosides support its therapeutic uses. Saponins, anthocyanins, and alkaloids further enhance its medicinal profile.

Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce skin irritation and promote wound healing. They are found in the flowers and are often used in topical treatments. Flavonoids support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making them valuable in herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and infections. These compounds are often extracted for use in topical formulations.

3. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like cineole and germacrene, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This oil is often used in topical applications for skin healing and wound care.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support wound healing by promoting tissue repair and reducing inflammation. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal properties during storage.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for skin healing and reducing oxidative stress.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin may also support skin healing and has mild anticoagulant effects. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Field Marigold in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. Glycosides in Calendula arvensis are known to support skin regeneration and reduce irritation. These compounds are commonly used in herbal remedies for their soothing effects.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins also aid in the plant's ability to deter herbivores and enhance soil health. These effects make saponin an important component in herbal medicine applications.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are pigments found in Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis) that contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative stress and may support skin healing and immune function. They are particularly concentrated in the flowers and are used in herbal preparations for their therapeutic benefits.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Field Marigold (Calendula arvensis). While alkaloids are more commonly associated with other medicinal plants, Field Marigold contains trace amounts of alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic properties, though its primary active compounds are flavonoids and triterpenes.
