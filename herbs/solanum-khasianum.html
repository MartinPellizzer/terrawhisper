Solanum khasianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Solanum khasianum?

Solanum khasianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is part of the order Solanales. This plant is a member of the family Solanaceae, specifically situated within the genus Solanum, and is identified by its unique species name, khasianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Solanum khasianum has a perennial herb morphology characterized by erect, branched stems that are typically covered in dense, stellate hairs. The leaves are large, ovate to elliptic in shape, and possess deeply lobed margins with a velvety texture. Small, white, star-shaped flowers emerge from the axils in cymes, featuring prominent yellow anthers that form a central cone. The plant produces globose berries that transition from green to a dark purple or black color upon maturity. These morphological features are adapted to the humid, subtropical environments of the Himalayan foothills where the species is natively found.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of Northeast India, specifically within the state of Meghalaya. Its distribution is closely tied to the moist, hilly terrains and forest ecosystems characteristic of the Khasi Hills. Beyond this primary location, it is found in similar ecological niches across parts of Southeast Asia where high humidity is prevalent. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds solamarine, solasodine, and solasodine glycoside. These specific steroidal alkaloids are primary bioactive constituents found within the plant's chemical structure. Solasodine serves as a crucial precursor in the synthesis of various steroid drugs used in modern medicine. The presence of solasodine glycosides further contributes to the complex pharmacological profile of the species. Researchers often study these compounds for their potential therapeutic applications in treating various ailments.

As discussed in a study, the medicinal compound solasodine is found within the fruits of Solanum khasianum. Under mild water stress conditions in a field experiment, the solasodine content was not affected. However, under conditions of severe water stress in a pot experiment, an increase in solasodine content per dry weight was observed. Despite this increase in concentration, the solasodine yield per plant was lower due to a loss in fruit yield. Under conditions of extreme water stress, the plant developed more negative water and osmotic potentials while maintaining turgor in the leaves.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, due to the presence of toxic alkaloids. These alkaloids act as irritants when ingested in significant quantities or in an unprepared state. Some individuals may also experience dizziness or neurological symptoms if they consume parts of the plant containing high concentrations of these compounds. Careful handling is necessary to avoid accidental poisoning through ingestion or improper preparation. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when interacting with any member of the Solanum genus.
