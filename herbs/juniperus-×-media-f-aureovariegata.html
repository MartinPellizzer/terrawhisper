Juniperus × media f. aureovariegata

Juniperus × media f. aureovariegata, commonly known as Golden Variegated Juniper, is a cultivar of the Juniperus × media, a hybrid of Eastern Red Cedar (Juniperus virginiana) and Rocky Mountain Juniper (Juniperus scopulorum).

This plant is valued for its ornamental value, with its golden-yellow variegated foliage adding a unique touch to landscapes and gardens.

Juniperus × media f. aureovariegata has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, with its essential oil exhibiting expectorant properties, helping to relieve congestion and coughs.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include terpenes, such as alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and terpinen-4-ol, which contribute to its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial actions.
