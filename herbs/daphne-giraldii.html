What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Daphne giraldii has an evergreen shrub habit characterized by arching, woody stems and lanceolate, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and paler underneath. The foliage typically grows in dense whorls or clusters toward the tips of the branches. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, tubular, fragrant blossoms that emerge in clusters from the leaf axils. These flowers are usually a creamy white or pale pink color and possess a waxy texture. The overall structure of the plant remains compact and rounded as it matures.
