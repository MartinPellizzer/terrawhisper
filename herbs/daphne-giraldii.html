Daphne giraldii

What's the taxonomical classification of Daphne giraldii?

Daphne giraldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Malvales. This plant is a member of the family Thymelaeaceae and is situated within the genus Daphne, where it is specifically identified by its species name, giraldii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Daphne giraldii has an evergreen shrub habit characterized by arching, woody stems and lanceolate, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and paler underneath. The foliage typically grows in dense whorls or clusters toward the tips of the branches. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, tubular, fragrant blossoms that emerge in clusters from the leaf axils. These flowers are usually a creamy white or pale pink color and possess a waxy texture. The overall structure of the plant remains compact and rounded as it matures.

What are the pharmacological activities of Daphne giraldii?

These biological effects are attributed to the unique secondary metabolites found in its tissues. Continued investigation into these pathways could reveal new applications for the plant in medical science.

As discussed in a study, the stem rind of Daphne giraldii contains constituents with pain-relieving activity. The ethanol extract underwent partition and chromatographic separation to monitor analgesic pharmacological activity. From the pain-relieving fraction, 4 compounds were isolated. Among these, 3 were identified as the diterpenes gniditrin (1), gnidicin (2), and daphnetoxin (3). The remaining compound, 4, was determined to be Z-octadecyl caffeate. Compounds 1, 2, and 4 were isolated from the plant for the first time.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex array of medicinal compounds specifically categorized as daphne diterpenes, including daphnetoxin, genkwanin, gnidicin, and gniditrin. These secondary metabolites are primarily responsible for the plant's potent biological activities and its characteristic toxicity. Daphnetoxin acts as a powerful irritant that can cause severe skin inflammation upon contact. The other components, such as genkwanin and gnidicin, contribute to the unique chemical profile used in various pharmacological studies. Together, these specific diterpenoids define the chemical identity and medicinal potential of the species.

As discussed in a study, 16 new daphnane diterpenes were isolated from Daphne giraldii. These compounds belong to a group of diterpenes that includes 10 known types. The isolated diterpenes from this plant showed antiferitility activities.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, dizziness, seizures, and death if ingested or touched. Contact with the plant's sap often leads to painful skin irritation or rashes.
