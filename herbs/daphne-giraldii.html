Daphne giraldii

What's the taxonomical classification of Daphne giraldii?

Daphne giraldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Malvales. This plant is a member of the family Thymelaeaceae and is situated within the genus Daphne, where it is specifically identified by its species name, giraldii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Daphne giraldii has an evergreen shrub habit characterized by arching, woody stems and lanceolate, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and paler underneath.

The foliage typically grows in dense whorls or clusters toward the tips of the branches. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, tubular, fragrant blossoms that emerge in clusters from the leaf axils. These flowers are usually a creamy white or pale pink color and possess a waxy texture.

The overall structure of the plant remains compact and rounded as it matures.

      What are the herbal preparations of Daphne giraldii?

      Daphne giraldii is prepared as a topical medicinal application, such as an ointment or a decoction, due to the high concentration of toxic alkaloids found within its tissues.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Daphne giraldii can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and gastrointestinal distress if the plant's toxic compounds come into contact with the body. The shrub contains diterpene esters that act as potent irritants to human tissue upon contact. Touching the leaves or stems often results in painful dermatitis or chemical burns on the skin. If any part of the plant is accidentally ingested, it can lead to intense abdominal pain, vomiting, and diarrhea. Because of these significant risks, careful handling and keeping the plant away from children and pets is essential.
