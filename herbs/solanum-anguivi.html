Solanum anguivi

What's the taxonomical classification of Solanum anguivi?

Solanum anguivi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Solanales and falls under the family Solanaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Solanum, and its specific species epithet, anguivi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Solanum anguivi contains a diverse profile of phytochemicals including alkaloids, saponins, flavonoids, tannins, and steroidal glycosides. These bioactive compounds are primarily concentrated in the leaves, fruits, and roots of the plant. The presence of specific alkaloids contributes to its significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the antioxidant capacity of the plant is driven by its rich flavonoid content which helps neutralize free radicals. Researchers often study these chemical constituents to develop natural treatments for various infectious diseases and metabolic disorders.

What are the therapeutic actions of Solanum anguivi?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including stomach aches, diarrhea, malaria, and skin infections. The leaves and roots are frequently prepared as medicinal decoctions to alleviate digestive distress and intestinal issues. It is also commonly utilized in traditional medicine to reduce fever associated with malaria symptoms. Some cultures apply the crushed plant matter topically to soothe skin irritations and wounds. These diverse applications highlight its importance in local herbal practices for managing both internal and external health concerns.
