Oxytropis oxyphylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxytropis oxyphylla has a perennial herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, ascending to erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are pinnately compound and consist of several pairs of narrow, linear to lanceolate leaflets that give the foliage a fine texture. Small, papilionaceous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying colors that range from pale yellow to deep orange. The calyx is notably hairy, while the petals often feature a distinct keel structure typical of the Fabaceae family. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in the specific rocky or alpine environments it inhabits.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Oxytropis oxyphylla can cause severe poisoning characterized by gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential death if ingested by livestock or humans. The plant contains toxic alkaloids that disrupt the central nervous system upon consumption. Animals that eat the plant often exhibit symptoms such as muscle tremors, incoordination, and extreme weakness. In many cases, high doses lead to sudden collapse or respiratory failure. Because the toxicity can be fatal, careful management of grazing areas is necessary to prevent accidental ingestion.
