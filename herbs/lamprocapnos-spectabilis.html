Lamprocapnos spectabilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lamprocapnos spectabilis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender, ascending stems that emerge from a creeping rhizome and produce compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The foliage consists of pinnately divided leaves that are typically glabrous and display a delicate, fern-like texture. Its inflorescence features small, nodding flowers that exhibit a distinct blue or violet hue with white markings on the petals. The reproductive structures are arranged in loose racemes that extend above the main foliage during the blooming period. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its taxonomic group and are adapted to its specific ecological niche.
