What's the taxonomical classification of Lamprocapnos spectabilis?

Lamprocapnos spectabilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the Papaveraceae family, specifically situated within the genus Lamprocapnos, and is identified by its unique species name, spectabilis.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lamprocapnos spectabilis can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential death if ingested due to the presence of toxic alkaloids. Symptoms often include intense nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea following consumption. The toxins may also impact the central nervous system, leading to symptoms such as dizziness, confusion, or seizures. In some cases, the plant's chemical properties can interfere with cardiac function or respiratory stability. Because of these significant risks, all parts of the plant should be handled with extreme caution and kept away from children and pets.
