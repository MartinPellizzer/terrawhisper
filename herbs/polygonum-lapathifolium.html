Polygonum lapathifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum lapathifolium?

Polygonum lapathifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a representative of the family Polygonaceae, falling under the genus Polygonum, and is specifically identified by its species name, lapathifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum lapathifolium has lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins, swollen nodes, and small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The stems are typically erect and often possess a reddish tint near the joints. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and feature a characteristic sheath at the base. The plant produces small, ovoid fruits that transition from green to a dark reddish-brown color when mature. These morphological traits help distinguish the species from other members of the Polygonaceae family.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds lapathoside A, lapathoside B, lapathoside C, lapathoside D, and vanicoside B. These specific phenylethanoid glycosides serve as primary chemical markers for the species. Researchers study these constituents to understand the plant's potential therapeutic properties. The presence of these glycosides is significant for identifying the quality of the botanical extract. Such chemical profiling helps in validating the traditional uses of Polygonum lapathifolium.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", four new phenylpropanoid esters of sucrose named lapathosides A (1), B (2), C (3), and D (4) were isolated from the aerial parts of Polygonum lapathifolium. These compounds were found alongside known esters vanicoside B (5) and hydropiperoside (6). The structures of 1-4 were determined using 1D and 2D NMR and MS analysis. Specifically, lapathoside A (1) and vanicoside B (2) demonstrated significant inhibitory effects on the Epstein-Barr virus early antigen activation caused by tumor-promoters.
