Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Pennyroyal, scientifically known as Levisticum officinale, is a perennial herb native to Europe and Asia, traditionally valued in herbal medicine for its purported ability to induce menstruation and as a vermifuge.

Here you can find a more about medicinal plants.

What is Pennyroyal (Levisticum Officinale)?

Pennyroyal, or Levisticum officinale, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its aromatic leaves and historical use in herbal medicine for digestive and menstrual support.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Pennyroyal is Levisticum officinale. This plant belongs to the family Apiaceae, order Apiales, class Magnoliopsida, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae. It is commonly known as pennyroyal in the United Kingdom and as bishop's weed in parts of Europe. Synonyms include Levisticum officinale var. major, Levisticum piperitum, and regional names such as wild chervil and garden burnet.

Plant Description and Morphology

Pennyroyal is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10-30 centimeters, with a branching, upright stem covered in fine, hairy texture.

Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, with a pointed tip and serrated edges, and are arranged in pairs along the stem. The small, fragrant flowers are pale blue to purple in color, clustered in whorls along the stem, and bloom in early summer. The root system consists of fibrous roots that spread horizontally near the soil surface, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The plant emits a strong, minty aroma, with leaves that are slightly hairy, soft to the touch, and have a distinctive, pungent smell.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Pennyroyal is native to western and central Europe, particularly the British Isles, France, and parts of Scandinavia.

It thrives in temperate climates with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soils. The plant is commonly found in shaded woodlands, along stream banks, and in damp meadows. Its geographic distribution extends to parts of North Africa and the Middle East, though it is less common there.

Pennyroyal prefers partial shade to full sun and is often associated with moist, nutrient-rich soils in its natural habitat.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves and flowering tops of Pennyroyal are primarily used medicinally.

The leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties. The flowering tops, which include the flower buds and open flowers, are also harvested for their active compounds. The entire plant is typically dried and used in preparations such as tinctures, teas, and oils.

These parts are valued for their aromatic and antiseptic qualities.

Phytochemical Composition

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) contains a range of active compounds, including monoterpene derivatives such as p-cymene and limonene, which contribute to its aromatic and medicinal properties.

Secondary metabolites, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, play a key role in its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. The plant's chemical markers include volatile oils, which are responsible for its characteristic scent and therapeutic actions. These compounds are primarily concentrated in the leaves and stems, with variations depending on growing conditions.

The phytochemical profile of pennyroyal supports its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory support.

Pharmacological Properties

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) exhibits pharmacological properties including antiseptic, antispasmodic, and carminative effects, primarily due to its high concentration of essential oils such as menthol, p-cymene, and limonene, which contribute to its medicinal applications in traditional herbal medicine.

Mechanisms of Action

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) exerts its effects primarily through the presence of volatile oils, particularly p-menth-2-en-1-ol and p-cymene, which exhibit antiseptic, antimicrobial, and antifungal properties, and may also influence hormonal activity through phytoestrogenic compounds.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to induce menstruation, alleviate digestive discomfort, and support respiratory health, though its efficacy and safety remain subjects of scientific debate.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Pennyroyal is traditionally prepared as a tincture, infusion, or decoction, with the most common method involving steeping the dried leaves in alcohol or oil to extract its essential oils and active compounds.

Dosage and Administration

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) is typically administered in small, measured doses, usually ranging from 10 to 30 drops of a tincture or 1 to 2 grams of dried herb per day, depending on the specific condition being treated and the form of the preparation.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) should be used with caution due to potential toxicity, especially in higher doses, and is contraindicated during pregnancy, as it may cause uterine contractions and fetal harm.

Drug Interactions

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) may interact with medications that affect the liver or blood clotting, and its use should be avoided during pregnancy or while breastfeeding due to potential risks.

Toxicity and Precautions

Pennyroyal is toxic when ingested in large quantities and can cause severe adverse effects, including liver damage, gastrointestinal distress, and central nervous system depression, with potential fatalities, especially in pregnant women and animals.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Pennyroyal is typically cultivated in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure, requiring minimal care and harvested primarily during the summer months when the leaves are most potent, with the entire plant being cut just before flowering to maximize essential oil content.

Processing and Extraction

Processing and extraction of Pennyroyal involve harvesting the leaves and flowering tops during the early flowering stage, followed by drying to reduce moisture content, then using steam distillation or cold pressing to obtain the essential oil, which contains the primary active compounds such as pulegone and methyl pulegone.

Storage and Shelf Life

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, in an airtight container to maintain potency and prevent moisture damage, with a typical shelf life of up to two years when properly stored.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) has primarily focused on its potential medicinal properties, including antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory effects, with some studies suggesting its use in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory conditions.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Pennyroyal has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to expel parasites, induce menstruation, and treat respiratory and digestive ailments, though its safety and efficacy remain controversial.

Regulatory Status

In many countries, Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) is classified as a herbal medicine and is subject to regulations that vary by region, with some areas requiring specific licensing or oversight for its sale and use.
