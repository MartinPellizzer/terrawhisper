Side Effects of levisticum officinale

Levisticum officinale triggers heartburn due to its potential to relax the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing stomach acid to flow back into the esophagus.

This herb can also cause stomach upset, as it may irritate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to discomfort, bloating, or cramping. In more severe cases, it may result in vomiting, particularly if consumed in large quantities or by individuals with preexisting digestive sensitivities.

Additionally, Levisticum officinale may lead to nausea, which can be exacerbated by its effects on the digestive system and possibly its interaction with other substances. Some users have reported experiencing palpitations, dizziness, or even sweating, which may be linked to its impact on the cardiovascular system or its ability to affect blood pressure and heart rate.

These side effects highlight the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before using this herb, especially for individuals with conditions such as acid reflux, heart issues, or gastrointestinal disorders.
