Levisticum officinale

What's the taxonomical classification of Levisticum officinale?

Levisticum officinale belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Apiales. It is a prominent member of the family Apiaceae, falling specifically under the genus Levisticum, with its precise scientific designation being the species officinale.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Levisticum officinale has a thick, fleshy rhizome and compound, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 60 centimeters in length. The leaves are characterized by deep, serrated lobes and a distinctively strong, aromatic scent. Its stems are hollow, erect, and can grow to heights between one and two meters. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in large, branched umbels. The fruit consists of small, ribbed schizocarps that contain essential oils.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to much of temperate Europe, extending across the British Isles and into parts of Western and Central Asia. It thrives primarily in moist, shaded environments such as damp woodlands and rocky ravines. Within these habitats, the species is frequently found in mountainous regions where humidity levels remain consistently high. Its distribution is often limited by the requirement for stable, cool microclimates that prevent desiccation. Consequently, its presence is most reliable in areas with significant rainfall or proximity to water sources.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Levisticum officinale contains therapeutic compounds primarily located within its roots and rhizomes. These underground structures are harvested and dried for use in traditional herbal remedies. The roots provide a distinctive aromatic profile that is often utilized to support digestive health. Additionally, the volatile oils extracted from these parts are frequently employed to alleviate various gastrointestinal discomforts. Some practitioners also utilize the leaves for their specific chemical properties, though the root remains the most significant medicinal component.

What are the therapeutic actions of Levisticum officinale?

Levisticum officinale has therapeutic actions as a carminative, stomachic, bitter, antispasmodic, digestive stimulant, expectorant, and diaphoretic. Its bitter properties serve to stimulate the digestive process and increase appetite. As a stomachic and digestive stimulant, the plant helps improve overall gastric function and nutrient absorption. It also provides carminative and antispasmodic effects to relieve gas and soothe intestinal cramping. Additionally, its role as an expectorant helps clear respiratory secretions, while its diaphoretic action promotes sweating to assist in temperature regulation.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains 7-methoxy-3-propylidenephthalide, coumarins, flavonoids, phthalides, and xanthotoxin. These medicinal compounds work together to provide various therapeutic benefits to the human body. Phthalides and 7-methoxy-3-propylidenephthalide are often studied for their specific biological activities. Additionally, the presence of flavonoids contributes to the antioxidant properties of the plant. Xanthotoxin and other coumarins further define the unique chemical profile of these botanical extracts.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat digestive disorders like bloating and flatulence, respiratory conditions such as coughs and asthma, and various forms of rheumatism or joint pain. Its carminative properties help alleviate intestinal gas and indigestion by stimulating the digestive tract. When used for respiratory issues, it often serves as an expectorant to help clear mucus from the lungs. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects make it a traditional remedy for easing muscle aches and arthritis symptoms. These diverse medicinal applications stem from the plant's potent essential oils and bioactive compounds.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating various conditions affecting the urinary tract and reproductive systems, specifically urinary tract infections, renal stones, cystitis, pyuria, and prostatitis. Its medicinal properties help manage inflammation and infections within the bladder and kidneys. By addressing pyuria, it assists in reducing the presence of pus in the urine caused by underlying infections. The herb also plays a role in alleviating the discomfort associated with cystitis and renal stone passage. Furthermore, its therapeutic effects extend to managing symptoms related to prostatitis in men.

As discussed in a study, Levisticum officinale is one of the medicinal plants contained in the phytotherapeutic drug Canephron® N. This drug is used for the prophylaxis and treatment of urinary tract infections in both adults and children. The review evaluated 17 clinical studies from 18 selected publications regarding its effectiveness and safety. Among the 3115 patients included in this review, only 1 adverse effect, a skin rash, was reported. The study also noted that the drug was used for the treatment and prevention of urinary tract infections and other gestational diseases in pregnancy.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, dizziness, and various allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Digestive distress often manifests as stomach cramps or an upset feeling shortly after ingestion. Some people may experience lightheadedness or a spinning sensation known as dizziness. Additionally, skin rashes or respiratory issues may occur as part of an allergic reaction. Monitoring these physiological responses is essential when interacting with this species.
