Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) is a member of the carrot family, known for its aromatic leaves and roots, and has been used historically for its digestive and respiratory benefits, particularly in improving conditions like indigestion and congestion.

What is Pennyroyal (Levisticum Officinale)?

Pennyroyal, or Levisticum officinale, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its aromatic leaves and historical use in herbal medicine for digestive and menstrual support.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Pennyroyal, or Levisticum officinale, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family, commonly known as the carrot or parsley family.

Common Names and Synonyms

Pennyroyal, also known as wild pennyroyal, garden pennyroyal, and British pennyroyal, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family and is commonly used in herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Plant Description and Morphology

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, aromatic leaves that form a dense mat, and small white or pale blue flowers that appear in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) is native to the moist woodlands and meadows of western and central Europe, particularly in regions such as France, Italy, and the British Isles, and has been historically cultivated in temperate climates for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves and flowers of Pennyroyal are primarily used medicinally, with the leaves being the most commonly harvested part for herbal preparations.

Phytochemical Composition

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including monoterpenes such as pulegone, menthone, and limonene, as well as sesquiterpenes like cadinene and humulene, along with flavonoids and essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacological Properties

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) exhibits pharmacological properties including antispasmodic, carminative, and mild emmenagogue effects, primarily due to its volatile oil content, which contains compounds such as methyl salicylate and ascaridole.

Mechanisms of Action

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) exerts its effects primarily through its volatile oils, which contain compounds such as p-menth-2-en-1-ol and 1,8-cineole, known to influence reproductive systems and exhibit antifungal and antimicrobial properties.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Pennyroyal is used to treat ailments such as digestive issues, menstrual cramps, respiratory infections, skin irritations, and as a natural insect repellent.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Pennyroyal is traditionally prepared as a tincture, infusion, or essential oil, with the leaves and flowering tops used most commonly, though the roots may also be employed in certain formulations.

Dosage and Administration

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) is typically administered in doses ranging from 1 to 2 grams per day, either in the form of a tincture, infusion, or topical application, depending on the intended use and individual health condition.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While pennyroyal is traditionally used in herbal medicine, it can cause side effects such as gastrointestinal distress, headaches, and in some cases, liver damage, particularly with excessive or prolonged use.

Drug Interactions

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the liver or central nervous system, due to its potential hepatotoxic and sedative properties.

Toxicity and Precautions

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) is toxic to humans and animals, particularly in large doses, and can cause severe liver damage, gastrointestinal distress, and even death, necessitating strict avoidance during pregnancy, by individuals with liver disease, and in cases of accidental ingestion.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) is typically cultivated in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring consistent moisture during its growing season.

Processing and Extraction

Pennyroyal is typically processed by harvesting the leaves and flowering tops during the early flowering stage, then drying them in a cool, dark place to preserve their potency and aroma.

Storage and Shelf Life

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) should be stored in an airtight container in a cool, dry, and dark place, away from direct sunlight and moisture, with a shelf life of up to two years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) has primarily focused on its traditional uses in herbal medicine, with studies examining its potential effects on reproductive health, antimicrobial properties, and phytochemical composition.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Pennyroyal has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to stimulate menstruation, expel parasites, and treat respiratory and digestive ailments, though its safety and efficacy remain controversial.

Regulatory Status

Pennyroyal (Levisticum officinale) is not approved as a safe or effective drug by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) and is not recommended for use as a medicinal herb due to its potential toxicity, particularly in pregnancy and during lactation.
