Chassalia curviflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Chassalia curviflora?

Chassalia curviflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Gentianales and falls within the family Rubiaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Chassalia and the species curviflora.

      What are the herbal preparations of Chassalia curviflora?

      Chassalia curviflora is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice to address various medicinal needs. The leaves and stems are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. This liquid can also be used as a wash to soothe skin irritations or inflammation. In some traditional practices, the plant material is crushed into a paste to be applied directly to wounds. These different methods allow for the extraction of specific bioactive compounds depending on the intended application.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Chassalia curviflora can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant's sap or leaves come into direct contact with human skin. Many members of the Rubiaceae family contain bioactive compounds that act as chemical irritants to sensitive tissues. Individuals may experience redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon touching the foliage. In some cases, repeated exposure can lead to more persistent allergic reactions or dermatological inflammation. It is important to wear protective gloves when handling this species to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
