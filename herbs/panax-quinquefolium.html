American Ginseng (Panax quinquefolium)

American Ginseng (Panax quinquefolium) is a member of the Araliaceae family, native to Eastern United States, Canada, and Northern United States. Traditionally, its roots, leaves, and fruits have been used for decoctions, infusions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and stimulant actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Sessile stipules, opposite phyllotaxy, leaflets with serrate margins, upper surface glabrous, lower surface pubescent.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of solitary, yellowish-green flowers with five sepals and five petals, arranged in a terminal spike, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry, with two longitudinal purple veins on the petals.
Stems
Erect, unbranched, woody, with opposite, lanceolate leaves and stipules.
