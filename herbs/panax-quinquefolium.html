Medicinal Parts

Panax quinquefolium root, commonly known as American ginseng, is one of the most widely used medicinal parts of this plant, valued for its adaptogenic and tonic properties.

The root contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including ginsenosides, which are believed to enhance cognitive function, reduce stress, and improve overall vitality. In traditional Chinese medicine, the root has been used for centuries to support energy levels, boost immunity, and promote longevity.

Modern research suggests that the root may also have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial for various health conditions. Additionally, the root is often harvested after several years of growth, as it is believed that the longer the plant grows, the more potent its medicinal properties become. The leaf of Panax quinquefolium, while less commonly used than the root, also contains some bioactive compounds and has been studied for its potential health benefits, including its antioxidant properties and ability to support metabolic functions.

However, the root remains the primary medicinal part due to its higher concentration of active compounds and its long history of use in herbal medicine.
