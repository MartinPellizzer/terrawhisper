Oryza rufipogon

What's the taxonomical classification of Oryza rufipogon?

Oryza rufipogon belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed through its designation in the genus Oryza and the specific species epithet rufipogon.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oryza rufipogon has an erect, annual growth habit characterized by slender, branched culms that can reach heights of up to 100 centimeters. The leaves are typically narrow and linear with a distinctively scabrous texture along the margins and midrib. Its inflorescence consists of an open panicle containing numerous small, lanceolate spikelets. Each spikelet features a lemma that is often pointed and may be covered in fine hairs or bristles. The glumes are relatively short and serve to protect the developing grain during its maturation process.
