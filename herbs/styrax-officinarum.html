Frankincense (Styrax Officinarum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Styrax officinarum.

Frankincense, scientifically known as Styrax officinarum, is a resinous sap derived from the bark of the frankincense tree, traditionally used as a medicinal herb and adaptogen. It is renowned for its primary benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties, which support immune function and promote emotional well-being. In traditional and cultural practices across the Middle East, Africa, and Asia, frankincense has been used for centuries in religious ceremonies, as a natural perfume, and for treating ailments such as respiratory infections and skin conditions. Today, it is incorporated into modern wellness routines through aromatherapy, meditation, and skincare products, valued for its calming and grounding effects. One of its unique features is the presence of boswellic acids, a rare active compound with potent anti-inflammatory properties, and its historical use dates back over 5,000 years, making it one of the oldest known medicinal substances.
