What's the taxonomical classification of Oyama wilsonii?

Oyama wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Magnoliales and the family Magnoliaceae. Ultimately, its specific identity is defined by its placement within the genus Oyama, with the specific epithet wilsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oyama wilsonii has a rhizomatous growth habit characterized by large, leathery, pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters.

Each compound leaf consists of numerous narrow, lanceolate leaflets arranged symmetrically along a central rachis. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous white or cream-colored flowers that emerge from the base of the leaf stalks. These flowers are followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that contribute to the plant's reproductive cycle.

The overall structure provides a lush, fern-like appearance common to many species in the Melanthiaceae family.
