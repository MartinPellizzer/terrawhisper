What's the taxonomical classification of Allium mongolicum?

Allium mongolicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its classification continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Asparagales. As a member of the family Amaryllidaceae, it is further refined into the genus Allium, with its specific identity defined by the species name mongolicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium mongolicum has linear, hollow leaves that emerge from a bulbous base and terminate in a spherical umbel of small, star-shaped flowers. These flowers typically display a pale pink to white coloration and are supported by slender, upright scapes. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clumps around the base of the plant. The bulb structure serves as a storage organ, allowing the plant to persist through varying environmental conditions. Small, globose capsules develop from the flower heads following successful pollination.
