Cotoneaster moupinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cotoneaster moupinensis?

Cotoneaster moupinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Rosales. It is a member of the family Rosaceae, which places it within the genus Cotoneaster, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species moupinensis.

      What are the herbal preparations of Cotoneaster moupinensis?

      Cotoneaster moupinensis is prepared as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant material in water to release its medicinal compounds. This traditional method allows the active constituents to infuse thoroughly into the liquid for easier consumption. In some applications, the plant parts may also be dried and ground into a fine powder to be used as a supplement. These powders can be incorporated into other foods or mixed with warm water to create a consistent medicinal dose.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cotoneaster moupinensis can cause gastrointestinal distress and potential poisoning if any part of the plant, particularly its red berries, is ingested by humans or pets. The small fruits contain cyanogenic glycosides which may lead to symptoms such as vomiting, abdominal pain, or dizziness upon consumption. While the plant is primarily used for ornamental purposes, accidental ingestion poses a significant risk to small children and animals. It is important to keep these berries away from areas where they could be easily mistaken for edible fruit. Proper identification and supervision are necessary to prevent any adverse health reactions.
