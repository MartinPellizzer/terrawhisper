Crataegus dahurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Crataegus dahurica?

Crataegus dahurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is a member of the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Crataegus and the species name dahurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crataegus dahurica has small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated margins, thorny branches, and small red pomaceous fruits. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stems and often possess a slightly hairy texture on their surfaces. Its woody stems are characterized by sharp, single or paired spines that serve as a defense mechanism. The fruit develops from a single ovary and ripens to a bright crimson color during the autumn season. Small, five-petaled white flowers emerge in clusters during the spring flowering period.
