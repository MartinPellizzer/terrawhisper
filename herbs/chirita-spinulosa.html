Chirita spinulosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Chirita spinulosa?

Chirita spinulosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Gesneriaceae, falling under the genus Chirita, and is specifically identified by its species name, spinulosa.

      What are the herbal preparations of Chirita spinulosa?

      Chirita spinulosa is prepared as a decoction of its dried leaves and stems to create a medicinal liquid for topical or oral use. The plant material is typically simmered in water to extract its active phytochemical constituents. Once the infusion is ready, it can be applied directly to the skin to soothe irritation or consumed in small, measured doses. Some practitioners also process the plant into a fine powder for easier storage and gradual integration into other formulas. These various methods allow for the diverse application of the plant's traditional therapeutic properties.
