What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia stenocephala?

Ligularia stenocephala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the evolutionary hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asterales, it falls into the widely recognized family Asteraceae. Its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Ligularia, with its unique biological classification completed by the species epithet stenocephala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its inflorescences consist of bright yellow ray and disc florets arranged in compact, elongated clusters atop sturdy stems. What are the pharmacological activities of Ligularia stenocephala?

This plant has significant anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities. Researchers have identified various bioactive compounds within its tissues that help suppress inflammatory responses in biological systems. These chemical constituents also play a vital role in neutralizing free radicals to prevent cellular oxidative damage. Additionally, certain extracts from the plant demonstrate the ability to inhibit the growth of specific pathogenic microorganisms. Such diverse properties suggest that the species holds considerable potential for further development in medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "Antioxidants (Basel, Switzerland)", complex extracts of Ligularia stenocephala (LSE) were used to prepare TEES-10, which was administered in 150 mg powder containing capsules twice daily to a test group of 100 participants. In vitro investigations showed that TEES-10 significantly increased periodontal ligament (PDL) cell viability compared to administering LSE alone. The treatment also suppressed the expression of NF-κB, p-ERK, ERK, COX-2, c-Fos, and p-STAT, while promoting the expression of PPARγ in LPS induced PDL cells. In a clinical trial lasting 4 weeks, significant improvements in the gingival index (GI) and bleeding on probing (BOP) were observed. Additionally, salivary MMP-8 and MMP-9 were significantly decreased compared to the placebo group. These results suggest that the extract has anti-inflammatory potential in chronic gingivitis patients.
