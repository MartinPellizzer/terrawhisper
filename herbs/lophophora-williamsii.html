Peyote

Lophophora williamsii

Peyote (Lophophora williamsii) is a small, spineless cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico and the southwestern United States, known for its psychoactive properties and traditional use in religious and medicinal practices.

Botanical Identification

Peyote, scientifically known as Lophophora williamsii, is a cactus belonging to the family Cactaceae. It is also called "button cactus" and is native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico and southwestern United States. The plant is a perennial shrub with small, spineless stems and yellow flowers. Synonyms include "peyotl" and "Lophophora diffusa" in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Caryophyllales
  • Family: Cactaceae
  • Genus: Lophophora
  • Species: Lophophora williamsii

Safety Profile

Peyote contains mescaline, a potent hallucinogen that can cause psychological and physical effects. It is contraindicated for individuals with a history of mental illness, heart conditions, or substance use disorders. Use under medical supervision is strongly recommended due to its potential risks.
