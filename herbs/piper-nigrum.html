Medicinal Parts of piper nigrum

Piper nigrum has many medicinal parts, such as the seed, fruit, and leaves.

The leaves, on the other hand, contain flavonoids and phenolic acids, which have been reported to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Possible Side Effects of piper nigrum

Piper nigrum can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing digestive issues like bloating, diarrhea, and stomach cramps due to its high piperine content.

Prolonged consumption of piper nigrum can also lead to interactions with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications, potentially resulting in adverse reactions.

In some cases, excessive intake of piper nigrum can cause allergic reactions, such as skin rashes, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, individuals with certain health conditions, like high blood pressure and kidney problems, may need to limit or avoid piper nigrum altogether to prevent exacerbating their conditions.
