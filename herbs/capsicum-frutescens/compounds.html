Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens) Compounds

The primary compounds in chili peppers include capsaicin, a potent alkaloid responsible for heat, and capsiate, a related fatty acid. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to antioxidant properties, while terpenoids and essential oils provide aromatic and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds work synergistically to offer medicinal benefits.

Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects. These compounds are part of the complex phytochemical profile that gives chili peppers their medicinal value in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for pain relief and digestive health. This compound is also linked to potential cardiovascular benefits.

3. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens). It contributes to the plant's pungency and has various medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Alkaloids in chili peppers are often used in herbal medicine to support pain relief and digestive health.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens). They contribute to the plant's pungency and have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds support the therapeutic use of chili peppers in herbal medicine for pain relief and digestive health.

5. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens). It contains compounds like capsaicin and other terpenoids that contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. The essential oil is used in herbal medicine for pain relief and as a natural stimulant.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation, though its use should be monitored due to potential interactions with certain medications.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins may help reduce inflammation and support digestive health when used in herbal remedies.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin also aids in the digestion of fats and may support cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the overall therapeutic potential of the chili pepper in herbal medicine.

9. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens). It contributes to the plant's pungency and has potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Glycosides in chili peppers may support digestive health and have antimicrobial effects. They are part of the plant's natural defense mechanisms and are used in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens). It contributes to the red and purple pigments in the fruit and has antioxidant properties that may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation.
