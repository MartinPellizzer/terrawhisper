Medicinal Parts

Ammi visnaga root is one of the most commonly used medicinal parts of this plant, known for its high concentration of compounds like apiin and visnagin, which have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

It is traditionally used to treat respiratory conditions such as asthma and bronchitis due to its ability to relax bronchial muscles and reduce inflammation. The root is also employed in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders, including colic and spasms, owing to its carminative effects.

In addition to its roots, the leaves of Ammi visnaga are also utilized, often in the form of decoctions or infusions, to address skin conditions and as a digestive aid. The seeds, though less commonly used, contain essential oils and have been historically used to treat ailments like coughs and respiratory infections, though their medicinal use is more limited compared to the root and leaves.

Overall, the root, leaf, and seed of Ammi visnaga contribute to its wide range of therapeutic applications in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.
