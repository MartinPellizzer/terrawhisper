Ammi visnaga

What's the taxonomical classification of Ammi visnaga?

Ammi visnaga belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its lineage further, it falls under the order Apiales and is a member of the family Apiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Ammi, and its species name, visnaga.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ammi visnaga has an erect, herbaceous annual stem that typically reaches a height of 30 to 100 centimeters and branches extensively from the base. The leaves are bipinnate or tripinnate, featuring finely dissected, feathery segments that give the foliage a delicate appearance. It produces small, white, five-petaled flowers arranged in compound umbels that can reach several centimeters in diameter. The fruit is a small, dry schizocarp characterized by prominent longitudinal ribs and a flattened shape. This plant's morphology is defined by its airy structure and the characteristic umbrella-like arrangement of its flowering heads.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Ammi visnaga contains medicinal compounds primarily concentrated in its seeds, fruits, and roots. The seeds are the most frequently utilized part for extracting khell, an essential oil used to treat various ailments. These seeds are often employed in traditional medicine to induce labor or manage spasmodic conditions. Additionally, the roots and occasionally the leaves are used for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers focus on these specific parts to isolate alkaloids that influence smooth muscle relaxation.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ammi visnaga?

This plant has anti-hypertensive, anti-diabetic, antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. Its diverse chemical constituents allow it to effectively combat oxidative stress and reduce cellular damage. The plant also demonstrates significant potential in regulating blood glucose levels and lowering high blood pressure. Additionally, its antimicrobial properties help inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. These combined therapeutic effects make it a subject of intense interest for modern medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, Ammi visnaga essential oils are mainly represented by linalool at 38.29 %. This essential oil demonstrated noticeable antimicrobial activity against both bacteria and yeast, with MIC, MBC, and MFC values ranging from 0.015 % to 0.5 %. The MBC/MIC and MFC/MIC ratios were less than or equal to 4.0 % (v/v), which indicates noticeable bactericidal and candidacidal efficacy. Furthermore, the plant showed significant inhibitory effects on α-amylase and α-glucosidase enzymes with a p < 0.05. It also exerted remarkable activity on FRAP, β-carotene, and DPPH radicals.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds visnadin, visnagin, psoralens, pyranocoumarins, and furanochromones. These specific chemical constituents are primarily categorized as furanocoumarins, which are known for their biological activity. The presence of visnadin and visnagin provides unique pharmacological properties often studied in various medical applications. Furthermore, the psoralens within the plant are frequently utilized in light-sensitizing therapies. These diverse pyranocoumarins and furanochromones collectively define the chemical profile of the species.

As discussed in a study, the most active lipophilic extract of Ammi visnaga fruits, known as DAB 10, was investigated using K+ (60 mM)-depolarized guinea-pig aortic strips. The effective principles identified in this testing model were visnadin, khellin, and visnagin. The dihydropyranocoumarin visnadin was determined to be the most active compound among them. Selectivity tests were also performed against norepinephrine (100 microM)-induced contractions to evaluate the compounds. While the furanochromones khellin and visnagin inhibited both spasms to a similar extent, visnadin showed significantly higher activity against K(+) spasms.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating hypertension, urinary tract infections, kidney stones, nephrolithiasis, and urinary lithiasis. Its diuretic properties help flush the renal system to alleviate symptoms associated with urinary lithiasis. By increasing urine production, it assists in the passage of small stones to manage nephrolithiasis. The plant also contains compounds that help regulate blood pressure to combat hypertension. Additionally, its antimicrobial effects can assist in managing various urinary tract infections.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause a range of adverse reactions including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain, dizziness, photosensitivity, hypotension, and increased gastric acid secretion. Gastrointestinal distress such as stomach pain and diarrhea are common responses to ingestion. Some individuals may also experience dizziness or a significant drop in blood pressure, known as hypotension. The plant is also associated with increased gastric acid secretion which can further irritate the digestive system. Additionally, exposure may lead to photosensitivity, making the skin more vulnerable to sunlight.
