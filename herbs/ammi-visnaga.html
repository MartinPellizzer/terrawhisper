What to know about Ammi visnaga (khella) before using it medicinally

Khella, scientifically known as Ammi visnaga, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive array of health benefits, which include reducing inflammation and alleviating respiratory issues such as asthma.

In the kitchen, khella is often used to add a unique flavor profile to Middle Eastern and North African dishes, particularly in soups, stews, and salads. From a horticultural standpoint, khella is an annual plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for home gardens and commercial crops. Botanically speaking, khella belongs to the carrot family and features long, thin leaves with a delicate, almost lacy appearance.

With its rich history dating back thousands of years, khella has been mentioned in ancient texts such as the Talmud and the writings of Arabic physicians like Avicenna.
