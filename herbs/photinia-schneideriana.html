What's the taxonomical classification of Photinia schneideriana?

Photinia schneideriana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Photinia and is specifically designated as the species schneideriana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Photinia schneideriana has an upright, bushy growth habit characterized by densely packed, elliptical leaves that transition from a bright red color in new spring growth to a deep, lustrous green as they mature.

The leaf margins are finely serrated, providing a textured appearance to the dense foliage. During the blooming season, the plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters called corymbs. These inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils and attract various pollinators.

Following the flowering period, the plant may develop small, fleshy drupes that serve as a key identifying feature.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Photinia schneideriana can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if the leaves or berries are ingested. The plant contains compounds that may trigger localized redness, itching, or swelling upon direct contact with the skin. Some sensitive individuals might experience an intensified allergic reaction known as contact dermatitis. If any part of the plant is eaten, it can lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. These adverse effects are most common when people handle the foliage without protection or accidentally consume the decorative red berries.
