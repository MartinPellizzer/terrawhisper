Symplocos lancifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Symplocos lancifolia has lanceolate, simple leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the woody stems. These leaves possess entire margins and acute to acuminate tips, often displaying a smooth or slightly leathery texture. The foliage is characterized by a distinct dark green upper surface and a paler underside. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils to eventually produce small drupes. The overall growth habit forms a dense shrub or small tree with fine branching patterns.

      Symplocos lancifolia is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using the leaves and bark of the plant.
