False Leaf (Phyllanthus amarus) Compounds

The primary compounds in False Leaf include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and saponins contribute to its astringent and digestive properties. Terpenoids and glycosides support its traditional use in treating infections. Alkaloids, coumarin, essential oils, and anthocyanins also play roles in its medicinal profile.
