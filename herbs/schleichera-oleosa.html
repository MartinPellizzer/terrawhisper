Schleichera oleosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Schleichera oleosa?

Schleichera oleosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Sapindales. The plant is a member of the family Sapindaceae, which contains the genus Schleichera, and it is specifically identified by its species name, oleosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schleichera oleosa has pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of 7 to 15 leaflets arranged along a central axis. These leaflets are oblong to lanceolate in shape and possess an entire margin with an acute or acuminate apex. The tree exhibits a spreading crown with branches that often feature a rough, fissured bark as the specimen matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in axillary panicles, eventually giving way to fleshy, drupaceous fruits. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and a robust woody trunk.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of bioactive medicinal compounds including betulinic acid, lupeol, lupeol acetate, various triterpenoids, and β-sitosterol. These specific substances contribute significantly to the plant's biological activities and therapeutic potential. The presence of triterpenoids provides a foundation for many of the plant's known pharmacological effects. Additionally, compounds such as lupeol and its derivative, lupeol acetate, play crucial roles in its chemical makeup. The inclusion of β-sitosterol and betulinic acid further enhances the medicinal complexity of the species.

As discussed in a study, the n-hexane fraction (SOH) of Schleichera oleosa bark was used to isolate lupeol, lupeol acetate, betulinic acid, and β-sitosterol. LC-HRMS profiling confirmed that this fraction contains triterpenoids and phytosterols. The study evaluated the methanol extract (SOM) along with its fractions including n-hexane (SOH), ethyl acetate (SOE), n-butanol (SOB), and aqueous residue (SOA). Testing of the SOH fraction was conducted in rats at doses of 200 and 400 mg/kg. These compounds helped lower levels of pro-inflammatory cytokines such as TNF-α, IL-1β, and IL-6.
