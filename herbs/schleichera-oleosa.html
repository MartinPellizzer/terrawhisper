Schleichera oleosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Schleichera oleosa?

Schleichera oleosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Sapindales. The plant is a member of the family Sapindaceae, which contains the genus Schleichera, and it is specifically identified by its species name, oleosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schleichera oleosa has pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of 7 to 15 leaflets arranged along a central axis.

These leaflets are oblong to lanceolate in shape and possess an entire margin with an acute or acuminate apex. The tree exhibits a spreading crown with branches that often feature a rough, fissured bark as the specimen matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in axillary panicles, eventually giving way to fleshy, drupaceous fruits.

The overall structure of the plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and a robust woody trunk.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Schleichera oleosa contains medicinal properties found in its seeds, bark, leaves, and roots. The oil extracted from the seeds is frequently used to treat skin conditions and serve as a topical emollient. Bark extracts are often utilized in traditional medicine to manage inflammation and digestive ailments. The leaves and roots are also processed into various decoctions to address different systemic health issues. These diverse plant components collectively contribute to its wide range of therapeutic applications.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains flavonoids, terpenoids, phenolic compounds, fatty acids, alkaloids, and steroids that contribute to its diverse pharmacological activities.

The presence of phenolic compounds and flavonoids provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties to the plant extracts. Terpenoids and steroids are also found within the tissues, often playing roles in antimicrobial and cytotoxic functions. Additionally, the concentration of fatty acids in the seeds supports various skin-related medicinal applications.

Alkaloids further enhance the therapeutic potential by interacting with specific biological pathways in the body.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Schleichera oleosa targets various inflammatory enzymes and oxidative stress pathways, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), lipoxygenase (LOX), and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS) to produce anti-inflammatory effects. Its bioactive triterpenoids and polyphenolic compounds act by modulating the expression of pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). These phytochemicals also interact with antioxidant enzymes, such as superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase, to neutralize reactive oxygen species within cells. Furthermore, certain glycosides present in the plant exert neuroprotective actions by influencing acetylcholinesterase activity. This multifaceted interaction with cellular signaling proteins allows the plant to address inflammation, oxidative damage, and neurodegeneration.

What are the therapeutic actions of Schleichera oleosa?

Schleichera oleosa has medicinal properties that function as an astringent, emollient, anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and emetic agent.

The plant extracts can be applied topically to soothe irritated skin due to their emollient and anti-inflammatory characteristics. Its antiseptic qualities help prevent infections in wounds, while the astringent properties assist in tightening tissues and reducing secretions. In specific traditional practices, the plant is also utilized as an emetic to induce vomiting for detoxification purposes.

These diverse therapeutic actions make the species valuable in various herbal remedies.

  • Anti-inflammatory

  • Emetic

What are the herbal preparations of Schleichera oleosa?

Schleichera oleosa is prepared as a fixed oil, a topical salve, or an infused lipid for various medicinal and cosmetic applications. The seeds contain a high concentration of fatty acids, making them ideal for cold-pressing into a nutrient-rich base oil. This oil can be blended with beeswax to create protective ointments used to soothe skin irritations. Additionally, the plant material is often infused into carrier oils to extract its antioxidant properties for therapeutic massage. These diverse preparations leverage the plant's natural emollient qualities to support skin health.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Schleichera oleosa can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive upset if ingested improperly. The plant contains various phytochemicals that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon direct skin exposure. While the oil is often used topically, concentrated extracts can lead to redness or itching. Ingestion of certain parts of the tree without proper processing might result in nausea or stomach discomfort. Therefore, caution is necessary when applying its derivatives to the body or consuming them.
