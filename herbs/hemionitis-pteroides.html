What is Hemionitis pteroides?

Hemionitis pteroides is a species of fern in the family Pteridaceae.

It is a terrestrial fern, with a rhizome that is covered with brown, hairy scales, and bears alternate, simple, leathery fronds. The fronds are lanceolate to ovate in shape, 20-50 cm in length, and 10-30 cm in width, with a distinct petiole and a pinnate to bipinnate venation pattern. The frond margins are entire or slightly lobed, and the lamina is dark green on the upper surface and lighter green on the lower surface.

The fertile fronds have smaller, narrower, and more deeply lobed laminae with spores borne on the underside.
