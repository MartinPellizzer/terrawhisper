Cicer Arietinum

Cicer arietinum, commonly known as chickpea or garbanzo bean, has been widely used for its medicinal properties throughout history.

It is rich in nutrients such as fiber, protein, vitamins, and minerals, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like saponins, flavonoids, and phytoestrogens that may help reduce cholesterol levels and improve heart health. Additionally, chickpea has been studied for its potential role in managing diabetes by regulating blood sugar levels and improving insulin sensitivity.

Its traditional use in various cultures for digestive health and as an anti-inflammatory agent further highlights its value in natural medicine.
