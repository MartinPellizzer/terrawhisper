Chickpea (Cicer arietinum)

Chickpea (Cicer arietinum) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Middle East, Southwest Asia, and Eastern Mediterranean. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and fruits have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Leaves
Pinnately compound leaves with stipules, upper surface glabrous and green, lower surface pale green with a single row of stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, solitary, with five white petals and five yellow sepals, lacking distinct markings or specialized structures.
Stems
Woody, unbranched, cylindrical stems with a smooth surface, exhibiting a climbing growth habit and opposite leaf arrangement.
