Researchers study these benzamide derivatives to understand their potential therapeutic applications in pharmacology. The presence of such compounds often contributes to the overall biological activity of the species. Understanding the concentration of these substances helps scientists determine the plant's medicinal value.

As discussed in a study, extract of Naravelia zeylanica yielded three simple benzamides. These C6C1 metabolites include 3,4-methylenedioxybenzamide, 4-methoxybenzamide and 4-hydroxy-3-methoxybenzamide. The compounds were identified through direct comparison of 1H- and 13C-NMR spectral data and GCMS chromatographic data. Authentic 4-hydroxy-3-methoxybenzamide was synthesized in 1 step by treating 4-hydroxy-3-methoxybenzonitrile with sodium perboraate. Additionally, authentic 3,4-methylenedioxybenzamide was synthesized from the corresponding acid.
