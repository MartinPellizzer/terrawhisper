What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 4-hydroxy-3-methoxybenzamide and 4-methoxybenzamide. These specific chemical constituents are identified as key components of the plant's unique biochemical profile. Researchers study these benzamide derivatives to understand their potential therapeutic applications in pharmacology. The concentration of these molecules can vary depending on the environmental conditions in which the plant grows. Understanding these compounds helps scientists unlock the natural healing properties inherent in the species.

As discussed in a study, the extract of Naravelia zeylanica yielded three simple benzamides. These three simple C6C1 metabolites include 3,4-methylenedioxybenzamide, 4-methoxybenzamide, and 4-hydroxy-3-methoxybenzamide. The compounds were identified by direct comparison of their 1H- and 13C-NMR spectral data and GCMS chromatographic data with authentic samples. One specific compound, authentic 4-hydroxy-3-methoxybenzamide, was synthesized in 1 step by the treatment of 4-hydroxy-3-methoxybenzonitrile with sodium perboraate. Additionally, authentic 3,4-methylenedioxybenzamide was synthesized from the corresponding acid.
