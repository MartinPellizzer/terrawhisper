Anaphalis bicolor

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anaphalis bicolor has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by upright, branched stems and distinctive flower heads that feature white ray florets surrounding a yellowish central disc. The leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern and possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with serrated margins. Fine, white hairs often cover the vegetative parts of the plant, providing a soft texture to the foliage and stems. The inflorescence consists of numerous small capitula that cluster at the ends of the branches. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in its specific ecological niche.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude montane regions of East Africa, specifically occurring in parts of Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda. It typically thrives in subalpine zones and afro-alpine moorlands where rocky terrain and cool temperatures are common. These populations are often found growing in open grasslands or on the slopes of volcanic mountains. The distribution is restricted by the specific ecological requirements of its habitat, which prevent it from spreading to lower elevations. Consequently, its range is fragmented across isolated mountain peaks within the Eastern Arc Mountains and surrounding highlands.

What are the herbal preparations of Anaphalis bicolor?

Anaphalis bicolor is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a poultice using different parts of the plant such as the leaves and roots. What side-effects this plant can have?

Anaphalis bicolor can cause skin irritation, localized redness, and allergic contact dermatitis upon direct physical contact with its tissues. The plant contains chemical compounds that may trigger sensitivity in individuals with pre-existing dermatological conditions. While systemic toxicity is not extensively documented in human literature, cautious handling is necessary to prevent unexpected inflammatory responses. Some users may experience itching or swelling if the sap comes into contact with sensitive mucosal membranes. It is generally advised to avoid prolonged exposure to the plant to minimize these dermatological risks.
