Rauvolfia tetraphyllla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. It is a member of the order Gentianales and is situated within the family Apocynaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Rauvolfia and is specifically designated as the species tetraphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its stems are woody and can develop a somewhat spreading or shrubby growth habit depending on the environment. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying a white to pale pink coloration. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several potent indole alkaloids, most notably the antiarrhythmic agent ajmaline and the antihypertensive compound reserpine. These specific chemical constituents are derived from the plant's roots and have been studied extensively for their physiological effects on the human nervous and cardiovascular systems. Reserpine functions by depleting monoamine neurotransmitters, which helps in reducing blood pressure. Meanwhile, ajmaline acts on the sodium channels of the heart to regulate cardiac rhythm. The presence of these alkaloids makes the species a significant subject in pharmacological research.
