What is Drimopogon rosea?

Drimopogon rosea is a species of flowering plant that belongs to the family Rosaceae.

It is an annual or perennial herb with a slender stem and a rosette of leaves, typically 1-5 cm in length. The leaves are glabrous or pubescent, with a crenate or dentate margin, and are arranged oppositely or in a whorl. The inflorescence is a corymb or a panicle, bearing 5-10 pink or rose-colored flowers, each 1-2 cm in diameter, with 5 petals and 15-20 stamens.

The fruit is an achene, 2-3 mm in length, with a fleshy pericarp and a single seed.
