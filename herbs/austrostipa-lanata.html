What is Austrostipa lanata?

Austrostipa lanata is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is an annual or perennial plant, typically growing to 0.3-1.2 meters in height, with a spread of 0.2-0.8 meters. The culms are slender, hairless or scantly hairy, with a smooth or slightly ridged surface, while the leaves are linear or lanceolate, flat or folded, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The inflorescence is a panicle, typically 5-20 cm long, composed of several spikelets, each containing 2-5 florets, with the lower florets reduced to bristles.

The spikelets are usually densely hairy, with a silky or woolly texture, and are an important characteristic of this species.
