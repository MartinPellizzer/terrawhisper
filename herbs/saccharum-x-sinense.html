Saccharum × sinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Saccharum × sinense?

Saccharum × sinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Poales, it falls into the family Poaceae. The taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its placement in the genus Saccharum, specifically identified by the species name sinese.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saccharum × sinense has tall, perennial, grass-like stems that can reach several meters in height and are characterized by thick, fibrous internodes. The leaves are long, linear, and lanceolate with prominent midribs and sharp, serrated margins along the edges. Dense clusters of panicles emerge from the top of the culms to produce numerous small, feathery spikelets. The root system consists of a robust rhizomatous structure that allows the plant to spread effectively through the soil. These morphological features collectively enable the hybrid to thrive in warm, humid tropical environments.
