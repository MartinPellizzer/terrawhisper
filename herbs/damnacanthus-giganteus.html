Damnacanthus giganteus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Damnacanthus giganteus has thick, lanceolate leaves measuring up to thirty centimeters in length that emerge from a central woody rhizome. These leaves possess a waxy cuticle and serrated margins to minimize water loss in its native habitat. The plant produces large, bell-shaped flowers that transition from a pale cream to a deep ochre as they mature. Each inflorescence is supported by a sturdy, fibrous stem capable of reaching heights of two meters. The root system consists of a dense network of tuberous structures designed for nutrient storage.
