Geranium erianthum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium erianthum has deeply palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs and produces five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features a perennial growth habit with stems that are often reddish and can become slightly woody at the base over time. Its foliage is characterized by prominent venation and a textured surface that helps distinguish it from similar species. The inflorescence consists of small clusters of flowers held atop slender pedicels. Long, thin seed capsules develop after pollination, eventually splitting to disperse the small seeds.

These populations are often located in open meadows or along forest edges at high elevations.
