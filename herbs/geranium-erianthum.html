Geranium erianthum

What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium erianthum?

Geranium erianthum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Geraniales and the family Geraniaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Geranium, and its unique species designation, erianthum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium erianthum has deeply palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs and produces five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its foliage is characterized by prominent venation and a textured surface that helps distinguish it from similar species. The inflorescence consists of small clusters of flowers held atop slender pedicels. Long, thin seed capsules develop after pollination, eventually splitting to disperse the small seeds.
