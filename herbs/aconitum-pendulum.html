Aconitum pendulum

What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum pendulum?

Aconiatum pendulum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. The plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, which places it within the genus Aconitum, and is specifically identified by its species name, pendulum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aconitum pendulum contains high concentrations of diterpene alkaloids, most notably aconitine, mesaconitine, and hypaconitine, which act as potent neurotoxins. These specific compounds function by binding to voltage-gated sodium channels, causing them to remain open and leading to intense physiological effects. In traditional medicinal practices, these alkaloids are processed through complex heating or boiling methods to convert highly toxic aconitine into much safer derivatives like benzoylaconine. Once chemically modified, these substances are used in very small, controlled doses to treat conditions such as chronic pain, rheumatism, and certain inflammatory ailments. However, the narrow therapeutic window between a medicinal dose and a fatal dose requires extreme caution during preparation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aconitum pendulum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat symptoms of rheumatism, sciatica, neuralgia, and various types of localized pain. In traditional medicine, preparations derived from the plant are applied topically to alleviate stiffness and inflammation in the joints. It has also been utilized in specific historical contexts to address certain types of fever and convulsions. However, the plant contains potent alkaloids that make it extremely toxic if handled or ingested incorrectly. Precise dosage and careful preparation are essential to avoid severe poisoning or death.
