Chives (Allium schoenoprasum)

Chives (Allium schoenoprasum) is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its leaves, bulbs, and stems have been used for infusions, culinary uses, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its carminative, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and japanese kampo medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Linear leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface whitish, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, bell-shaped, actinomorphic flowers with six tepals of equal length, white to pale purple in color, arranged in a spiral pattern along the flowering stem.
Stems
Cylindrical, hollow, herbaceous, glabrous, erect, cylindrical, and terete, with a solid, white, fibrous interior.
