European Lime (Tilia x europaea)

European Lime (Tilia x europaea) is a member of the Tiliaceae family, native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and expectorant actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Simple leaves with entire margin, upper surface dark green, lower surface lighter green, having prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are small, yellowish-green, actinomorphic, arranged in upright racemes, with five ovate sepals and five elliptic petals.
Stems
Erect, woody, glabrous, with opposite branching and persistent leaf scars.
