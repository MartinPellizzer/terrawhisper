Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) Uses

Chamomile is commonly used to treat indigestion, gastroenteritis, and digestive discomfort. It helps relieve gastrointestinal gas, spasms, and nausea. It also addresses gastroesophageal reflux and can ease symptoms of allergic rhinitis and respiratory infections.

Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) Monograph

1. Indigestion

Chamomile soothes indigestion by reducing stomach acid and calming the digestive tract. It is often used as a tea to relieve bloating, gas, and nausea. Its mild sedative effect can also ease stress-related digestive issues. Regular consumption may improve overall digestive comfort.

2. Gastroenteritis

Chamomile is used to alleviate symptoms of gastroenteritis due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce intestinal inflammation and soothe cramping, promoting digestive comfort. Its mild sedative effects can also ease nausea and support overall gut health during gastrointestinal distress.

3. Digestive upset

Chamomile aids digestion by reducing inflammation and soothing the gastrointestinal tract. It can relieve symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and mild stomach cramps. Its mild sedative effect also helps ease digestive discomfort associated with stress. Regular use may support healthy digestion and reduce the frequency of digestive upset.

4. Gastrointestinal gas

Chamomile soothes gastrointestinal gas by relaxing smooth muscle spasms in the digestive tract. Its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties reduce bloating and discomfort. It is commonly used as a tea to ease symptoms of indigestion and flatulence. Clinical studies support its efficacy in alleviating gas-related discomfort.

5. Gastrointestinal spasms

Chamomile is used to relieve gastrointestinal spasms due to its antispasmodic properties. It soothes smooth muscle contractions in the digestive tract, reducing cramping and discomfort. Often consumed as a tea, it provides a calming effect on the stomach and intestines. Its mild action makes it a preferred remedy for mild digestive issues.

6. Digestive discomfort

Chamomile is commonly used to alleviate digestive discomfort such as bloating, gas, and mild stomach cramps. Its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties help soothe the gastrointestinal tract. It is often prepared as a tea and is considered safe for most adults when consumed in moderate amounts.

7. Gastroesophageal reflux

Chamomile has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce stomach acid production and soothe esophageal irritation. Clinical studies suggest it may improve reflux symptoms when used as a herbal remedy. Its mild action makes it a popular choice in herbal medicine for digestive discomfort.

8. Nausea

Chamomile is used to alleviate nausea due to its calming and anti-spasmodic properties. It helps soothe the digestive system and reduce stomach discomfort. Often used in teas, it can ease symptoms of motion sickness, morning sickness, and digestive upset. Its mild sedative effect also supports overall digestive wellness.

9. Allergic rhinitis

Chamomile has been used traditionally to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and sneezing by soothing the respiratory tract. Chamomile tea or essential oil inhalation can provide relief, though it should be used cautiously in those with allergies to related plants.

10. Respiratory infection

Chamomile is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing irritation in the airways. It may help alleviate symptoms of mild respiratory infections such as coughs and sore throats. Its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties can aid in clearing congestion and promoting healing. Chamomile can be consumed as a tea or used in steam inhalations for respiratory relief.
