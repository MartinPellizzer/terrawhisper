Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) Compounds

Chamomile contains essential oil as its main aromatic component, rich in compounds like coumarin, which has antispasmodic properties. Flavonoids and terpenoids contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids, glycosides, and tannins support its soothing and digestive benefits. Saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins add to its diverse therapeutic profile.

Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) is a key active compound, containing compounds like bisabolol and chamazulene. These contribute to its anti-inflammatory and sedative properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating anxiety and digestive issues.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla). It contributes to the plant's calming and anti-inflammatory effects. Found in the essential oils of chamomile, coumarin helps soothe digestive issues and reduce anxiety. Its presence is crucial for the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in calming digestive issues and promoting skin health. Specific flavonoids like apigenin and quercetin are known for their therapeutic effects.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and calming effects. Key terpenoids like bisabolol and chamazulene are responsible for its therapeutic properties. These compounds are essential for the medicinal use of Chamomile in herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce oxidative stress and supports the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and calming effects. Glycosides in chamomile support its use in treating digestive issues and mild anxiety. These compounds are extracted from the flower's essential oils and aerial parts.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Tannins help soothe irritated tissues and may aid in digestive health. They are found in the flower's essential oils and water extracts.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and mild laxative properties. Saponins help in reducing skin irritation and may support digestive health. They are part of the complex mixture of bioactive substances in chamomile that contribute to its therapeutic uses.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla). While chamomile is primarily known for its flavonoids and terpenoids, alkaloids are present in smaller quantities. These compounds may contribute to the plant's mild sedative and anti-inflammatory effects, though their role is less prominent compared to other constituents.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and calming actions.
