10 Possible Side Effects Of Matricaria Chamomilla (Chamomile)

Matricaria chamomilla has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, drowsiness, and digestive issues.

These side effects can occur if the herb is ingested in large quantities or if an individual is sensitive to its constituents, such as apigenin.

For instance, if someone takes excessive amounts of chamomile tea, they may experience extreme drowsiness, leading to missed work or school, and impacting their relationships and daily responsibilities.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Matricaria chamomilla if used imporperly.

Matricaria chamomilla causes allergic reactions due to its similarity in structure and composition to ragweed pollen, a common allergen.

This can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing in some individuals. Additionally, the flavonoids and terpenoids present in Matricaria chamomilla may also contribute to the allergic potential of this herb.

As a result, some people may exhibit adverse reactions to its use.

The plant contains a significant amount of apigenin, an allergenic compound that can stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical responsible for triggering asthma symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, chamomile's high concentration of terpenoids and flavonoids can exacerbate existing respiratory issues, leading to severe asthmatic attacks.
