Pilea glaberrima

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea glaberrima has dark green to near-black, ovate leaves with finely serrated margins and a smooth, glabrous texture. The foliage grows in an opposite arrangement along slender, upright stems. These leaves are characterized by a glossy sheen that reflects light across their slightly textured surfaces. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge from the leaf axils during the growing season. The plant generally maintains a compact, bushy habit as it develops its distinctive dark canopy.
