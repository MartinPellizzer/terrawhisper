Pleurospermum wrightianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pleurospermum wrightianum has a woody, climbing habit characterized by slender, twining stems and oppositely arranged, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically glabrous and exhibits prominent pinnate venation on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often appearing in a somewhat racemose arrangement. The reproductive structures develop into small, fleshy berries that serve as the primary means of seed dispersal.
