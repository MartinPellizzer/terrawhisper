Dichondra micrantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichondra micrantha has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats with small, kidney-shaped to reniform leaves measuring roughly 5 to 15 millimeters in diameter. These leaves are typically bright green, possess entire margins, and are arranged alternately along the slender, rooting stems. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow and emerge from the leaf axils. Its foliage is characterized by a smooth or slightly textured surface with subtle venation patterns visible on the lamina. This creeping growth habit allows the plant to spread rapidly across the soil surface through stoloniferous expansion.

      What are the herbal preparations of Dichondra micrantha?

      Dichondra micrantha is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice to treat various ailments. The leaves are typically boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for internal consumption. This liquid preparation can also be applied externally to soothe skin irritations or wounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Dichondra micrantha can cause skin irritation or allergic dermatitis in sensitive individuals through direct contact with its leaves or stems. While generally considered non-toxic to humans, some people may experience redness, itching, or a localized rash after touching the foliage. There is limited scientific data regarding its long-term physiological effects on humans, so caution is advised for those with known plant sensitivities. It is also important to monitor pets, as ingestion might lead to mild digestive upset in certain animals. Always wash your hands thoroughly after handling the plant to prevent any accidental skin reactions.
