What's the taxonomical classification of Dichondra micrantha?

Dichondra micrantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is part of the order Solanales and falls under the family Convolvulaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Dichondra, and its specific species name, micrantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichondra micrantha has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats with small, kidney-shaped to reniform leaves measuring roughly 5 to 15 millimeters in diameter. These leaves are typically bright green, possess entire margins, and are arranged alternately along the slender, rooting stems. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow and emerge from the leaf axils. Its foliage is characterized by a smooth or slightly textured surface with subtle venation patterns visible on the lamina. This creeping growth habit allows the plant to spread rapidly across the soil surface through stoloniferous expansion.
