Leonurus Sibiricus (Leonurus sibiricus) Compounds

Leonurus sibiricus contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent effects. Coumarin and glycosides may support anti-inflammatory actions. Terpenoids, alkaloids, and essential oils provide additional therapeutic benefits. Saponins and anthocyanins further enhance its medicinal profile.

Leonurus Sibiricus (Leonurus sibiricus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Leonurus sibiricus contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. They may support cardiovascular health and reduce oxidative stress. Flavonoids are key components in the plant's therapeutic applications.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Leonurus sibiricus contributes to its astringent properties and may support digestive health. It interacts with proteins, potentially aiding in reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing. Tannins are also believed to enhance the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Leonurus sibiricus. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and respiratory conditions.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Leonurus sibiricus. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and circulatory conditions. Coumarin exhibits antispasmodic and anticoagulant properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine. It is also known for its potential anti-inflammatory effects.

5. Glycoside

Leonurus sibiricus contains the glycoside leonurine, which exhibits cardiovascular and neuroprotective effects. This compound helps regulate blood pressure and improve circulation, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating conditions like hypertension and neurological disorders.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Leonurus sibiricus contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds are known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They support the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for treating respiratory and circulatory conditions. Terpenoids enhance the overall therapeutic value of Leonurus sibiricus.

7. Alkaloid

Leonurus sibiricus contains the alkaloid leonurine, which is known for its potential cardiovascular benefits. This compound may help improve blood circulation and has been studied for its role in supporting heart health. It is one of the key active components contributing to the traditional use of this plant in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Leonurus sibiricus. It contains volatile aromatic compounds like limonene and cineole, which contribute to its medicinal properties. This oil is used in herbal medicine for its calming and antispasmodic effects.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Leonurus sibiricus. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, exhibiting mild diuretic and expectorant properties. Saponin helps support respiratory and urinary system health, aligning with the plant's historical applications.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Leonurus sibiricus. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation.
