Blumea axillaris

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea axillaris has an erect, perennial herb structure characterized by ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively hairy or pubescent surface. The stems are typically woody at the base and can reach significant heights depending on the growing environment. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flower heads arranged in dense, terminal clusters known as capitula. These flowers are housed within involucral bracts that protect the reproductive structures. The plant also features a specialized root system that aids in its stabilization within tropical soil types.
