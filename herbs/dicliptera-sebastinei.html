What is Dicliptera sebastinei?

Dicliptera sebastinei is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is an annual herb with erect to ascending stems, measuring up to 40 cm in height, and has linear-lanceolate leaves that are 5-13 cm long and 0.8-1.8 cm wide. The leaves are glabrous to pubescent on the lower surface, and have a cuneate base and a obtuse to acute apex. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, and the flowers are tubular, with a purple corolla that is 2.5-3.5 cm long and a white and purple throat.

The plant is endemic to the Western Ghats of India.
