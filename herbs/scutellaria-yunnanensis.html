Scutellaria yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Scutellaria yunnanensis?

Scutelariia yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Scutellaria, and its unique species designation, yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutelariia yunnanensis has opposite, decussate leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The stems are often quadrangular and exhibit a woody or semi-woody texture as the plant matures. Its flowers are zygomorphic and emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by a distinct two-lipped corolla structure. The calyx is tubular and often features prominent longitudinal ribs. Small, nut-like fruits develop following successful pollination to facilitate seed dispersal.
