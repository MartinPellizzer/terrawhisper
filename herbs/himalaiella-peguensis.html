Himalaiella peguensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Himalaiella peguensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and small, actinomorphic flowers arranged in terminal clusters. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a slightly leathery texture to withstand its native environment. Each flower features five distinct petals that range in color from pale white to a subtle lavender hue. The reproductive structures include a central ovary surrounded by numerous stamens that facilitate pollination. Small, capsule-like fruits develop following successful fertilization to ensure seed dispersal.
