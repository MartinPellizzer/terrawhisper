Phlegmariurus squarrosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Lycopodiales and the family Lycopodiaceae, eventually reaching its specific classification within the genus Phlegmariurus. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific epithet, resulting in its full scientific name, Phlegmariurus squarrosus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds huperzine A (HupA) and huperzine B (HupB), which function primarily as potent acetylcholinesterase inhibitors. These alkaloids are highly valued in neuropharmacology for their ability to increase acetylcholine levels in the brain. Such biochemical activity is frequently studied for its potential to enhance cognitive function and memory retention. Researchers often investigate these specific substances to develop treatments for neurodegenerative conditions like Alzheimer's disease. The presence of these compounds makes the plant a significant subject in both traditional medicine and modern clinical research.

As discussed in a study, all methanolic extracts of nine plants, including Phlegmariurus squarrosus (G. Forst.), showed AChE inhibition ranging from 8.55 to 71.81%. High-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC) demonstrated that Phlegmariurus squarrosus biosynthesizes both HupA and HupB. The concentrations of HupA and HupB found in six plants were greater than those previously reported for Lycopodiaceae species.
