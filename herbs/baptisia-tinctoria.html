The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including the treatment of digestive issues, skin conditions, and respiratory problems.

The bioactive constituents of Baptisia tinctoria include alkaloids, glycosides, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic actions, such as antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant properties.

Possible Side Effects of Baptisia tinctoria

Baptisia tinctoria can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

When ingested, it may cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to its high content of alkaloids like baptifoline and baptifine.

External application of the plant or its extracts may lead to skin rashes, itching, and redness, especially in individuals with sensitive skin.

Improper use of Baptisia tinctoria can also interact with certain medications, exacerbating conditions like high blood pressure and kidney disease.
