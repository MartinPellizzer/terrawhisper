Stephania mashanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephania mashanica has a large, tuberous, globose caudex that serves as the primary storage organ for the plant. From this thickened base, slender and twining stems emerge to create a climbing or trailing growth habit. The leaves are typically simple, ovate to elliptical in shape, and possess smooth margins with prominent venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the foliage, often appearing in clusters or singly depending on the developmental stage. The entire structure is adapted for survival in seasonally dry environments by utilizing the moisture stored within the central tuber.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Stephania mashanica can cause severe physiological distress including nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and potential heart palpitations due to the presence of toxic alkaloids. These bioactive compounds act on the central nervous system and can lead to unpredictable neurological reactions if ingested. Because the plant contains complex chemical structures, accidental consumption may result in intense abdominal cramping or gastrointestinal upset. There is also a risk of cardiovascular complications such as irregular heartbeats in sensitive individuals. Due to these significant risks, the plant should be handled with extreme caution and never consumed.
