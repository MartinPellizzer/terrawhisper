Stephania mashanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephania mashanica has a large, tuberous, globose caudex that serves as the primary storage organ for the plant. From this thickened base, slender and twining stems emerge to create a climbing or trailing growth habit. The leaves are typically simple, ovate to elliptical in shape, and possess smooth margins with prominent venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the foliage, often appearing in clusters or singly depending on the developmental stage. The entire structure is adapted for survival in seasonally dry environments by utilizing the moisture stored within the central tuber.
