Rhaphidophora hookeri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhaphidophora hookeri has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense, tufted clumps from a creeping rhizome. These slender foliage blades typically reach lengths of several decimeters and exhibit a deep green color. The plant lacks true stems, instead relying on its underground stems to spread through the soil. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge on slender stalks to produce tiny seeds. This morphology allows the species to form thick mats that effectively colonize moist, shaded forest floors.
