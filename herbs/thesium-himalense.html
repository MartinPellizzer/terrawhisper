Thesium himalense

What's the taxonomical classification of Thesium himalense?

Thesium himalense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Santalales and falls within the family Santalaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Thesium, specifically identified by the species epithet himalense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thesium himalenense has a perennial, subshrubby growth habit characterized by slender, erect stems and small, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often reduced or scale-like.

The plant typically features a woody base with branching stems that can reach heights of several dozen centimeters. Its inflorescences are composed of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. The floral structures are often greenish or pale in color, reflecting its hemiparasitic nature.

These specialized morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in high-altitude Himalayan environments.
