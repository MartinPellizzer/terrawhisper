Thesium himalense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thesium himalenense has a perennial, subshrubby growth habit characterized by slender, erect stems and small, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often reduced or scale-like. The plant typically features a woody base with branching stems that can reach heights of several dozen centimeters. Its inflorescences are composed of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. The floral structures are often greenish or pale in color, reflecting its hemiparasitic nature. These specialized morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in high-altitude Himalayan environments.

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically occurring within the mountain ranges of India, Nepal, Bhutan, and parts of China. The species is frequently found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level.
