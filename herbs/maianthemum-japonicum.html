What's the taxonomical classification of Maianthemum japonicum?

Maianthemum japonicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asparagales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Asparagaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Maianthemum, with its unique species designation being japonicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Maianthemum japonicum has rhizomatous stems that produce ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged in an alternate pattern along the stalk. These leaves typically feature prominent parallel venation and smooth margins. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that grow in a terminal raceme. Following the flowering period, it develops fleshy red berries that are characteristic of the species. The overall structure remains relatively low-growing and spreads through its underground rhizome network.
