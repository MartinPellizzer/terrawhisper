Marasca Plum (Prunus Marasca)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Prunus marasca.

Marasca plum, scientifically known as Prunus marasca, is a small, dark red fruit native to the Balkan region, prized for its medicinal properties and rich nutritional profile. This fruit is often classified as a medicinal herb due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds, including antioxidants, polyphenols, and flavonoids, which contribute to its therapeutic potential. Traditionally, it has been used in folk medicine to aid digestion, reduce inflammation, and support cardiovascular health, with its juice and extracts playing a central role in Slavic herbal remedies. In modern wellness practices, Marasca plum is valued for its potential to improve gut health, lower cholesterol, and enhance immune function, making it a popular ingredient in natural supplements and functional foods. Its unique, tart flavor and rare compound called anthocyanin make it a distinctive and historically significant fruit, with its cultivation dating back centuries in the Balkans.
