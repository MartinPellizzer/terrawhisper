Linalool and p-cymene are frequently recognized for their calming and antimicrobial properties. Meanwhile, spathulenol and β-elemene provide unique bioactive actions that support various biological functions. The presence of cis-sabinene hydrate further enhances the complex chemical synergy within the plant's essential oils.

As discussed in a study, the leaf essential oil of Acanthopanax leucorrhizus contains 59 identified components representing 93.1% of the total oil. The main chemical components identified include beta-pinene at 7.3%, linalool at 6.5%, p-cymene at 6.3%, beta-elemene at 3.8%, gamma-terpinene at 3.7%, spathulenol at 3.2%, and cis-sabinene hydrate at 3.1%. This oil demonstrated moderate cytotoxicity on human tumor cells with specific IC50 values of 25.65μg/ml for Hep G2, 28.71μg/ml for Hela, 30.15μg/ml for Bel-7402, and 37.55μg/ml for A-549.
