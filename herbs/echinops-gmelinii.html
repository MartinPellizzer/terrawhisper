Echinops gmelinii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Echinops gmelinii has a perennial, herbaceous growth habit characterized by silver-grey, deeply lobed leaves and spherical, spiny flower heads that can reach several centimeters in diameter. The foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs that give the plant a distinct metallic sheen and help regulate moisture. Long, sturdy stems support the heavy globose inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. Each flower head is protected by a dense arrangement of sharp, prickly bracts that serve as a defense mechanism. These architectural structures allow the plant to thrive in arid environments while attracting specific pollinators.
