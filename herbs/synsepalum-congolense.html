Synsepalum congolense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Synsepalum congolenesse has woody stems, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fleshy, globose fruits. The leaves are typically arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the branches. The venation of the foliage is often prominent, helping to identify the species within its tropical habitat. Its reproductive structures are characterized by a distinct calyx that persists during the fruit development stage. These morphological features collectively define its structural identity within the Icacinaceae family.
