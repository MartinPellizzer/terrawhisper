Abrus pulchellus

What's the taxonomical classification of Abrus pulchellus?

Abrus pulchellus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed within the genus Abrus, specifically as the species pulchellus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its foliage is characterized by a delicate texture and a deep green color that provides a backdrop for its vibrant floral displays. The flowers are notable for their striking contrast between deep crimson petals and lighter colored centers.       What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe systemic toxicity if it contains the lethal protein abrin, which inhibits protein synthesis in human cells. Ingestion of toxic parts often leads to acute gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. These physical symptoms can escalate into more serious neurological issues such as dizziness. Depending on the concentration of the toxin, these side effects can progress to organ failure or death. Careful avoidance of all parts of the plant is necessary to prevent these dangerous reactions.
