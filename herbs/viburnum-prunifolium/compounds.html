Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium) Compounds

Sweet Viburnum contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and tannins, which have astringent properties. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, while coumarin and glycosides may support cardiovascular health. Terpenoids and saponins provide additional anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits.

Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant properties. They help neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and immune-supporting benefits.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe mucous membranes. It is found in the bark and leaves, and is valued for its potential role in digestive and respiratory health.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium) contributes to its antioxidant properties. It helps neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health. This compound is part of the plant's broader therapeutic profile in herbal medicine. Its presence enhances the plant's potential for use in treating oxidative stress-related conditions.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin is also known for its potential blood thinning effects and is used in herbal medicine to support cardiovascular health.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including its use in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory support. Glycosides are often responsible for the therapeutic effects of many herbal remedies.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, supporting traditional uses for digestive and respiratory support. They are found in the plant's berries and leaves, enhancing its role in herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponins are also known to support digestive health and may enhance the absorption of certain nutrients.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium). It contributes to the plant's deep purple color and has antioxidant properties. Found in the berries, anthocyanin supports cardiovascular health and may reduce inflammation. Its presence highlights the plant's potential in herbal medicine for supportive therapeutic uses.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium) contains volatile compounds like limonene and linalool, which contribute to its aromatic profile. These compounds are often used in herbal medicine for their potential calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Sweet Viburnum (Viburnum prunifolium). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific effects and mechanisms are less well-studied compared to other constituents. Alkaloids in Viburnum prunifolium may support traditional uses in herbal medicine, particularly for digestive and respiratory support.
