Justicia patentiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Justicia patentiflora?

Justicia patentiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. As a member of the family Acanthaceae, it is specifically identified by the genus Justicia and the species patentiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Justicia patentiflora has opposite, decussate leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape with entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color and grows along square-shaped stems characteristic of the Acanthaceae family. Its inflorescence consists of terminal spikes containing small, tubular flowers that often feature white or pale pink corollas. The plant develops a spreading or decumbent growth habit, allowing it to form dense groundcover in its natural habitat. These morphological traits collectively assist the species in competing for light and space within tropical environments.
