Saxifraga laciniata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga laciniata has lanceolate, deeply lobed leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette and feature serrated margins. These leaves are typically succulent in texture and possess a distinctively pointed shape that tapers toward the tip. The plant's stems are relatively short, supporting small, delicate flowers that emerge from the center of the foliage. Each flower usually consists of five petals and is characteristic of the Saxifraga genus in its structure. The overall morphology is adapted to rocky or alpine environments where moisture retention is crucial.
