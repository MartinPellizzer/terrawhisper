Saxifraga laciniata

What's the taxonomical classification of Saxifraga laciniata?

Saxifraga laciniata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Saxifragales. This plant is further categorized into the family Saxifragaceae, which contains the genus Saxifraga, and is specifically identified by its unique species name, laciniaata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga laciniata has lanceolate, deeply lobed leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette and feature serrated margins. These leaves are typically succulent in texture and possess a distinctively pointed shape that tapers toward the tip. The plant's stems are relatively short, supporting small, delicate flowers that emerge from the center of the foliage. Each flower usually consists of five petals and is characteristic of the Saxifraga genus in its structure.
