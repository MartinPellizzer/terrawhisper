What's the taxonomical classification of Rubia wallichiana?

Rubia wallichiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which places it within the genus Rubia, and it is specifically identified by its species name, wallichiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubia wallichiana has a prostrate or climbing perennial herb morphology characterized by slender, quadrangular stems that are often rooting at the nodes. The leaves are arranged in whorls of four to six, possessing an oblong or lanceolate shape with entire margins and acute tips. Small, tubular, white or pale pink flowers emerge from the leaf axils in dense clusters. The fruit is a small, fleshy, red berry that develops from a superior ovary.
