Taraxacum bicorne

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Taraxacum bicorne has a basal rosette of deeply lobed, serrated leaves and a single hollow stem topped by a flower head featuring two distinct horn-like protrusions. The foliage is characterized by irregular teeth along the margins and a slightly hairy texture on the underside. Its central stem rises vertically to support a composite inflorescence that transitions from yellow petals to a seed head. The unique namesake horns emerge from the base of the bracts surrounding the flower. This specialized structure distinguishes the plant from other members of its genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Andes mountains in South America, specifically concentrated within the montane ecosystems of Peru and Bolivia. It thrives in specialized microclimates located at elevations ranging from three thousand to four thousand meters above sea level. The distribution is restricted to rocky outcrops and alpine meadows where moisture levels remain consistent throughout the growing season. Because it occupies such niche ecological zones, its range is fragmented rather than continuous across the continent. Localized environmental factors like soil composition and sunlight exposure dictate the exact patches where populations can persist.

      What are the herbal preparations of Taraxacum bicorne?

      Taraxacum bicorne is prepared as a decoction of the dried roots, a tincture made from the fresh aerial parts, or a topical salve using the crushed leaves. These liquid extracts are commonly used to harness the plant's bitter principles for digestive support. When the roots are boiled into a concentrated tea, they provide a potent way to ingest the botanical compounds. For external applications, the leaves are often infused into a fatty base to create a soothing ointment for the skin. These various methods allow practitioners to tailor the dosage and delivery method to specific therapeutic needs.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Taraxacum bicorne can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, acute allergic reactions, and potential neurotoxicity if ingested in large quantities. Some individuals may experience nausea, abdominal cramping, or persistent vomiting shortly after consumption. Skin contact with the plant's sap can lead to localized dermatitis or painful, itchy rashes. In rare cases, systemic exposure might trigger dizziness and a rapid increase in heart rate. It is essential to avoid all contact with this species due to these unpredictable physiological responses.
