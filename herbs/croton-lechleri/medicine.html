What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Croton Lechleri (Dragon'S Blood)?

Croton lechleri, also known as dragon's blood, has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antimicrobial properties.

The medicinal constituents of this herb include beta-alanine, succinic acid, and tannins. Traditionally, Croton lechleri is prepared as a tincture, ointment, or tea to support digestive and wound healing. However, using this herb in excess can cause gastrointestinal irritation, diarrhea, and allergic reactions.

It is recommended to consult a healthcare professional before using Croton lechleri, especially if pregnant or breastfeeding.

What are the health benefits of Croton lechleri?

Croton lechleri has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that can help alleviate wounds, burns, and skin irritations.

Its resin has been traditionally used to treat various conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues.

Additionally, studies have shown that dragon's blood extract may exhibit antioxidant and anticancer activities, making it a potential natural remedy for various diseases.

What are the medicinal preparations of Croton lechleri?

Croton lechleri has different medicinal preparations, such as fresh sap, dried sap, tincture, oil, and ointment.

The fresh sap is used topically to treat wounds, skin conditions, and hemorrhoids due to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. Dried sap is used in teas and infusions for internal use, treating digestive issues and infections. Tincture is a concentrated liquid extract used orally or topically for its antimicrobial and antioxidant effects.

Oil and ointment are used for skin conditions, such as eczema and psoriasis, due to their moisturizing and soothing properties.

What are the possible side effect of using Croton lechleri improperly?

Improper use of Croton lechleri increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, skin rashes, and gastrointestinal disturbances like nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Long-term use may lead to liver damage, kidney problems, and changes in blood pressure. It may also interact with medications, causing adverse reactions or decreased effectiveness.

Furthermore, improper usage can result in respiratory issues, such as bronchitis or asthma attacks, and even increased risk of bleeding or bruising due to platelet inhibition.
