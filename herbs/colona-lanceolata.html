What is Colona lanceolata?

Colona lanceolata is an evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa, belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It grows up to 3 meters tall, with a spreading habit and elliptical to lanceolate leaves, 2-7 cm long and 0.5-1.5 cm wide, with a short petiole and serrated margin. The flowers are small, pale yellow to greenish-yellow, in axillary clusters, with 5-7 petals, and staminate flowers are present in larger numbers than pistillate flowers. The fruit is a dehiscent capsule, 2-4 cm long and 1-2 cm wide, with numerous seeds.

The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and a dense, pubescent young growth.
