Acer henryi

What's the taxonomical classification of Acer henryi?

Acer henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its descent through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Sapindales. This species is a member of the family Sapindaceae and is situated within the genus Acer, ultimately being identified by its specific species name, henryi.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Acer henryi can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals who come into direct contact with its sap or foliage. While it is primarily cultivated as an ornamental tree, the chemical compounds within the plant may trigger redness, itching, or swelling upon contact. There is also a potential for digestive upset if any part of the plant is accidentally ingested by humans or pets. Careful handling is recommended to avoid unexpected dermatological reactions during pruning or maintenance. Monitoring for these symptoms is important for anyone working closely with the species in a garden setting.
