Iris bulleyana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris bulleyaana has sword-shaped, ensiform leaves that emerge from a rhizomatous base and produce upright, branched stems bearing large, violet-to-blue flowers with prominent yellow signals on the falls. The plant exhibits a robust growth habit with foliage that is typically glaucous and leathery in texture. Each flower features three delicate standards that stand erect while the three falls droop downward to reveal intricate nectar guides. The reproductive structures are centered within the perianth tube, supporting the development of seed capsules after successful pollination. This species is characterized by its specific floral proportions and the distinct color patterns found on its tepals.

      What are the herbal preparations of Iris bulleyana?

      Iris bulleyana is prepared as a decoction of the rhizome, a powder derived from the dried root, or a topical poultice. The rhizome is typically boiled in water to extract its medicinal compounds for internal consumption. When processed into a fine powder, it can be mixed with honey or water to facilitate easier swallowing.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Iris bulleyana can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if the plant parts are ingested. The plant contains iridin, a glycoside that may trigger allergic contact dermatitis or rashes upon skin contact. If consumed, the chemical compounds can lead to nausea, vomiting, and stomach cramps in sensitive individuals. While specific human clinical data is limited, many members of the Iris genus are known to be toxic to both humans and animals. Caution should be exercised when handling this species to avoid accidental exposure or poisoning.
