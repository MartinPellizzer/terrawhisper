What's the taxonomical classification of Iris bulleyana?

Iris bulleyaana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Asparagales and falls within the family Iridaeceae. Ultimately, it is identified by its specific genus, Iris, and its unique species designation, bulleyaana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris bulleyaana has sword-shaped, ensiform leaves that emerge from a rhizomatous base and produce upright, branched stems bearing large, violet-to-blue flowers with prominent yellow signals on the falls. Each flower features three delicate standards that stand erect while the three falls droop downward to reveal intricate nectar guides. The reproductive structures are centered within the perianth tube, supporting the development of seed capsules after successful pollination.
