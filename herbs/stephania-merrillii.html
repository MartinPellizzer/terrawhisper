Stephania merrillii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephania merrillii has a tuberous root system, pinnate leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is a climbing woody vine characterized by its distinctively swollen, woody caudex that serves as a storage organ. Its foliage consists of compound leaves where individual leaflets are arranged in a regular, feather-like pattern. The inflorescences typically bear small, yellowish or greenish flowers that lack showy petals. This species belongs to the Menispermaceae family and exhibits specialized growth habits suited for forest understories.

The plant contains isoquinoline alkaloids that primarily act as ligands for cholinergic receptors in the central and peripheral nervous systems. What are the therapeutic actions of Stephania merrillii?

Stephania merrillii has therapeutic actions that include being an emmenagogue, an abortifacient, and a uterine stimulant. These properties allow the plant to influence the menstrual cycle by promoting blood flow through the pelvic region. By acting as a uterine stimulant, the plant can induce contractions in the muscles of the uterus. Because of these powerful physiological effects, it is frequently used in traditional medicine to stimulate menstruation or terminate a pregnancy.
