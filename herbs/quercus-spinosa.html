Quercus spinosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Quercus spinosa?

Quercus spinosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Fagales and the family Fagaceae. Ultimately, it is defined by its specific biological classification as part of the genus Quercus, with the unique species designation spinosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Quercus spinosa has small, coriaceous leaves that are characterized by deeply lobed margins ending in sharp, spine-like teeth and an elliptical to obovate shape. The leaf surface is typically dark green and leathery, providing a structural defense against environmental stressors. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody stems that exhibit a rugged, spreading growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into characteristic acorns held in shallow cups.
