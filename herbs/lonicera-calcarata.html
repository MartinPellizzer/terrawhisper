Lonicera calcarata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera calcarata has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape, often measuring between 2 and 5 centimeters in length with entire or slightly serrated margins. The stems are woody and climbing, allowing the plant to weave through surrounding vegetation. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically displaying a pale yellow or creamy white coloration. The fruit develops into small, fleshy berries that transition in color as they reach maturity. These morphological traits are characteristic of many species within the Caprifoliaceae family.
