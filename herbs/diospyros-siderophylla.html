What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diospyros siderophylla has opposite, coriaceous leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess a distinctively thick, leathery texture. The leaf margins are typically entire, while the upper surfaces exhibit a dark green hue and the undersides often show a more matte appearance. Its woody stem structure supports a robust canopy characteristic of many members of the Ebenaceae family. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy fruits. These fruits serve as a primary morphological feature for identification within its native habitat.
