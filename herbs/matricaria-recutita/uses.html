Chamomile (Matricaria recutita) Uses

Chamomile is commonly used for allergic rhinitis, hay fever, and allergic conjunctivitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It also helps alleviate nasal congestion, cold symptoms, and mild respiratory infections. Chamomile can soothe skin irritation and minor wounds, often applied topically as a compress or ointment.

Chamomile (Matricaria recutita) Monograph

1. Allergic rhinitis

Chamomile has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose by soothing irritated mucous membranes. Chamomile tea or nasal sprays are common methods of application, offering a natural alternative for managing seasonal allergies.

2. Mild respiratory infections

Chamomile is used to support mild respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps soothe throat irritation and reduce coughing. Often prepared as a tea, it can ease symptoms and promote healing. Its mild nature makes it suitable for frequent use during minor respiratory issues.

3. Hay fever

Chamomile is used to alleviate hay fever symptoms due to its antihistamine properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and eye irritation. Studies suggest it may inhibit histamine release, offering a natural alternative for allergy relief. Regular use can support seasonal allergy management when combined with other herbal remedies.

4. Mild infections

Chamomile exhibits antimicrobial properties that make it effective for mild infections, particularly in the respiratory and urinary tracts. It can be used as a tea or topical application to reduce inflammation and combat bacterial or viral infections. Its mild nature makes it suitable for children and those with sensitive systems. Always consult a healthcare provider for persistent or severe infections.

5. Nasal congestion

Chamomile is used to alleviate nasal congestion due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce swelling in the nasal passages and ease breathing. Inhalation of steam infused with chamomile can provide immediate relief. It is often recommended as a natural alternative to over-the-counter decongestants.

6. Common cold

Chamomile may help alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It can soothe sore throats and reduce congestion when used as a tea. Some studies suggest it may support immune function, though more research is needed. It is typically consumed as a warm infusion to provide comfort during cold symptoms.

7. Allergic conjunctivitis

Chamomile (Matricaria recutita) has been used to alleviate symptoms of allergic conjunctivitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and swelling in the eyes, often through the application of diluted chamomile tea as eye compresses. Its soothing effect makes it a gentle alternative for managing mild allergic eye reactions.

8. Cold symptoms

Chamomile is used to alleviate cold symptoms such as coughing, sore throat, and nasal congestion. Its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce inflammation and soothe irritated tissues. It can be consumed as a tea or used in steam inhalations for respiratory relief. Chamomile's mild action makes it a gentle option for supporting recovery from mild colds.

9. Skin irritation

Chamomile is used topically to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It can help reduce redness, itching, and inflammation associated with conditions like eczema or minor burns. Chamomile-infused creams or compresses are commonly applied to affected areas for relief. It is generally safe for external use but should be tested for allergic reactions.

10. Minor wounds

Chamomile is used topically to treat minor wounds due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote healing. A chamomile-infused compress or ointment can be applied directly to the wound. It is particularly effective for small cuts, scrapes, and insect bites.
