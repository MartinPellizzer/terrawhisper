Botanical Identification of Chamomile (Matricaria recutita)

To identify Chamomile (Matricaria recutita) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Chamomile, or Matricaria recutita, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Compositae, commonly known as the aster family. The genus is Matricaria, and the species is Matricaria recutita. The division, class, and order are not specified in the provided data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chamomile is Matricaria recutita, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is also known by various synonyms, common names, and regional names that reflect its widespread use and historical significance in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Chamomile (Matricaria recutita) is an annual herb with a branched root system. It has erect, hairy stems that are aromatic. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence is a flat, yellow disk with white ray florets. The flowers develop into a spherical, dry fruit containing small, pale seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chamomile (Matricaria recutita) can be distinguished by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, opposite linear leaves, and a strong, sweet aroma. It is often confused with other chamomile species, but its distinctive leaf shape and floral structure help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chamomile (Matricaria recutita) is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chamomile blooms from late spring to early autumn, typically between May and September, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to October. For identification, the flower heads are harvested when they are fully open and the yellow center is visible, usually in mid to late summer.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Chamomile (Matricaria recutita) is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family, particularly within the genus Matricaria. It shares key family traits such as composite flower heads and ligulate florets. Its closest species include other chamomile varieties like Anthemis nobilis, which are often confused due to similar appearance and medicinal uses.
