Chamomile (Matricaria recutita) Compounds

Chamomile contains several key compounds, including essential oil, which gives it its characteristic aroma. Coumarin contributes to its calming effects, while flavonoids and phenolic acids provide antioxidant properties. Terpenoids and glycosides support anti-inflammatory actions, and tannins and saponins aid in digestive health. Alkaloids and anthocyanins also contribute to its therapeutic profile.

Chamomile (Matricaria recutita) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Chamomile (Matricaria recutita) is a key active compound, containing compounds like bisabolol and chamazulene. These components contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and calming properties, making it widely used in herbal medicine for digestive and skin conditions.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Chamomile (Matricaria recutita). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin also has antispasmodic properties, making it useful in treating digestive issues and promoting relaxation. Its presence enhances the overall therapeutic profile of Chamomile.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Chamomile (Matricaria recutita). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in soothing digestive issues and promoting relaxation. Specific flavonoids like apigenin and quercetin are known for their therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chamomile (Matricaria recutita). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps soothe digestive issues and reduce skin irritation, supporting Chamomile's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Chamomile (Matricaria recutita). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and sedative effects. Common terpenoids include bisabolol and chamazulene, which are responsible for many of Chamomile's therapeutic properties. These compounds are extracted and used in herbal remedies for their calming and healing benefits.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Chamomile (Matricaria recutita). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and calming properties. Glycosides help soothe digestive issues and reduce anxiety. They are responsible for many of Chamomile's therapeutic effects.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chamomile (Matricaria recutita). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and helps in reducing inflammation and irritation. Tannins also support the healing of wounds and soothe digestive discomfort.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Chamomile (Matricaria recutita). It contributes to the plant's mild anti-inflammatory and digestive properties. Saponins may help reduce irritation and support gut health, making them valuable in herbal formulations for digestive and skin care.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Chamomile (Matricaria recutita). While alkaloids are more commonly associated with other plants, Chamomile contains trace amounts of alkaloids, which contribute to its overall pharmacological profile. These compounds may play a minor role in the plant's therapeutic effects, though the primary active components are sesquiterpene lactones.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Chamomile (Matricaria recutita). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation. While not the primary active component, anthocyanin supports the overall therapeutic effects of chamomile in traditional herbal medicine.
