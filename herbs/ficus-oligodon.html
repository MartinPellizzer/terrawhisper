Ficus oligodon

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus oligodon?

Ficus oligodon belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Rosales. As a member of the family Moraceae, it is situated in the genus Ficus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species oligodon.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Ficus oligodon contains medicinal properties primarily derived from its bark, leaves, and roots. The bark is often decocted to treat various ailments including skin infections and inflammatory conditions. Leaves from the plant are frequently applied topically or used in traditional preparations to address digestive issues. The roots are also utilized in several regional folk medicine practices to manage specific bodily discomforts. These diverse parts provide a variety of phytochemical compounds that contribute to the plant's therapeutic potential.

    What are the herbal preparations of Ficus oligodon?

    Ficus oligodon is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the plant's leaves, bark, or roots in water to extract its medicinal properties. This liquid extract is often consumed orally to address various traditional health concerns. In some regional practices, the plant material is also processed into a topical paste for external application. Some preparations involve drying the components first to ensure a longer shelf life before brewing. These diverse methods allow for the targeted delivery of the plant's bioactive compounds.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Ficus oligodon can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if the milky sap is ingested or comes into contact with sensitive tissue. The plant contains a white, milky latex that serves as a primary irritant to the skin and eyes upon contact. If this sap is swallowed, it may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Some individuals might also experience allergic reactions or dermatitis after handling the stems or leaves. Therefore, careful handling and avoidance of ingestion are necessary to prevent these adverse reactions.
