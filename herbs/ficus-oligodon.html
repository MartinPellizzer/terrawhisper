Ficus oligodon

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus oligodon?

Ficus oligodon belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Rosales. As a member of the family Moraceae, it is situated in the genus Ficus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species oligodon.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus oligodon has a woody trunk with smooth, greyish bark and leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with an entire margin and an acute apex. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it feels leathery to the touch, and the surface often displays a lustrous green appearance. Its inflorescences are enclosed within specialized structures called syconia, which are small, globose fruits that contain numerous tiny flowers. These fruits transition from green to a yellowish or reddish hue as they reach maturity. The plant's overall architecture is characterized by spreading branches that support a dense canopy of foliage.
