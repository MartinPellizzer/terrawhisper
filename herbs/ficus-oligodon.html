Ficus oligodon

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus oligodon has a woody trunk with smooth, greyish bark and leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with an entire margin and an acute apex. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, meaning it feels leathery to the touch, and the surface often displays a lustrous green appearance. Its inflorescences are enclosed within specialized structures called syconia, which are small, globose fruits that contain numerous tiny flowers. These fruits transition from green to a yellowish or reddish hue as they reach maturity. The plant's overall architecture is characterized by spreading branches that support a dense canopy of foliage.

Individual populations are often localized to specific microclimates that support its growth requirements.       What are the herbal preparations of Ficus oligodon?

      Ficus oligodon is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the plant's leaves, bark, or roots in water to extract its medicinal properties. This liquid extract is often consumed orally to address various traditional health concerns. In some regional practices, the plant material is also processed into a topical paste for external application.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ficus oligodon can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if the milky sap is ingested or comes into contact with sensitive tissue. The plant contains a white, milky latex that serves as a primary irritant to the skin and eyes upon contact. If this sap is swallowed, it may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Some individuals might also experience allergic reactions or dermatitis after handling the stems or leaves. Therefore, careful handling and avoidance of ingestion are necessary to prevent these adverse reactions.
