Botanical Identification of Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis)

To identify Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Rosemary, scientifically known as Rosmarinus officinalis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Rosmarinus, and species Rosmarinus officinalis. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the mint family, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Rosmarinus officinalis is the scientific name for rosemary, first formally described by the Swedish botanist Carl von Linné in 1753. It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is also known by several synonyms, including Rosmarinus marinus and Rosmarinus ericoides. Common names include rosemary, maritime pine, and Greek tea, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Rosemary is a shrub with a compact, upright growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly. The stems are woody and aromatic, bearing opposite, linear leaves. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, with small blue to purple flowers. The fruit is a small capsule containing tiny, aromatic seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Rosemary can be distinguished by its fragrant, needle-like leaves and blue or purple flowers. It has a woody stem and linear, opposite leaves. It is often confused with sage, but sage has larger, broader leaves and a different scent. Its aromatic properties and growth habit help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Rosemary is native to the Mediterranean region, including southern Europe, northern Africa, and western Asia. It thrives in warm, dry climates with full sun exposure. It prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Rosemary blooms from late spring to early summer, producing blue-violet flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, when the plant is most aromatic and the leaves are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Rosemary belongs to the genus Rosmarinus within the family Lamiaceae. Its closest relatives include Origanum and Salvia, which share similar morphological and chemical characteristics. These species are closely related due to shared evolutionary traits, such as aromatic foliage and square stems, typical of the Lamiaceae family.
