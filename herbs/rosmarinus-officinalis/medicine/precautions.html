10 Precautions To Take When Using Rosmarinus Officinalis (Rosemary)

Rosmarinus officinalis has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as avoiding high doses, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women.

Consuming excessive amounts can lead to uterine contractions and reduced milk production. Not taking this precaution may result in side effects like decreased fetal movement or even preterm labor.

Additionally, individuals with low blood pressure or those taking blood thinners should use rosemary with caution to avoid exacerbating these conditions.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Rosmarinus officinalis medicinally.

When using Rosmarinus officinalis medicinally, it's important to take after consulting a doctor.

10. Follow recommended dosing

When using Rosmarinus officinalis medicinally, it's important to start low and gradually increase the dosage to avoid potential adverse effects.

This precaution is crucial because rosemary can stimulate the cardiovascular system and may lead to increased heart rate, blood pressure, and even seizures in high doses.

Starting with a low dose allows for tolerance assessment and minimizes the risk of unintended consequences, ensuring a safe and effective therapeutic outcome.
