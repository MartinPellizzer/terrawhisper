Lysimachia microcarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia microcarpa?

Lysimachia microcarpra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. This plant is specifically situated within the family Primulaceae, placed in the genus Lysimachia, and is identified by the species name microcarpra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia microcarpa has slender, upright stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and bear small, bright yellow, five-petaled flowers.

The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an elliptical or lanceolate shape with entire margins. These leaves are generally much larger than the tiny, star-shaped blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant often features a creeping rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread through moist habitats.

Small, capsule-like fruits develop following pollination to facilitate seed dispersal.
