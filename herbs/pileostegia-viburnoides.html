Pileostegia viburnoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Pileostegia viburnoides?

Pileostegia viburnoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Cornales. This plant is further categorized under the family Hydrangeaceae, which places it in the genus Pileostegia, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species viburnoides.

Pileostegia viburnoides contains various secondary metabolites including flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its biological activities. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammation, skin infections, respiratory issues, and various digestive disorders. Its medicinal properties are often attributed to its bioactive compounds that possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory qualities. In traditional practices, decoctions or infusions made from the plant are applied topically to soothe skin irritations or wounds. It is also sometimes consumed to help alleviate symptoms of coughs or minor stomach ailments. Researchers continue to study its chemical constituents to better understand how it interacts with different pathogens.
