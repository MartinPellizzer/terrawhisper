What's the taxonomical classification of Psychotria henryi?

Psychotria henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical descent through the order Gentianaales, the plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Psychotria, specifically identified by the species name henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Psychotria henryi has opposite, elliptic to oblong leaves with acute apices and entire margins that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant exhibits a woody shrub habit with stems that often show a slightly quadrangular structure in younger growth. Its inflorescences are arranged in small clusters, bearing tubular flowers that eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes. The reproductive organs are characterized by a long corolla tube and small, inconspicuous petals.

These morphological features are consistent with its classification within the Rubiaceae family.
