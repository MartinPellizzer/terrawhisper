Wendlandia tinctoria

What's the taxonomical classification of Wendlandia tinctoria?

Wendlandiia tinctoria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Wendlandia, and its unique species designation, tinctoria.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wendlandiia tinctoria has woody stems with opposite, simple, elliptical leaves that possess entire margins and acute apices. The foliage is typically bright green and can reach several centimeters in length depending on the specimen's maturity. Small, actinomorphic flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying a pale coloration. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that serve as the primary reproductive structures. Its overall growth habit is that of a spreading shrub or small tree adapted to tropical environments.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Wendlandia tinctoria targets various inflammatory mediators including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and tumor necrosis factor-alpha to exert its medicinal effects.
