Celastrus kusanoi

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus kusanoi?

Celastrus kusanoi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae, eventually reaching the genus Celastrus. Finally, the specific identification of this plant is completed by its species name, kusanoi.

By modulating these enzymatic pathways, the extracts effectively suppress the inflammatory cascade in various cellular models. These molecular interactions underpin the plant's potential applications in treating inflammatory diseases and oxidative stress.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Celastrus kusanoi can cause skin irritation, localized inflammation, and digestive distress if ingested. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger allergic contact dermatitis upon contact with sensitive skin. If accidental consumption occurs, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. These adverse reactions are primarily linked to the chemical constituents present in the foliage and berries. Careful handling is recommended to avoid these potential physiological complications.
