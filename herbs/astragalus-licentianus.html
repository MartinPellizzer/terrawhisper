What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus licentianus?

Astragalus licentianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further classified under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae and falls within the genus Astragalus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species licentianus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus licentianus has a perennial rootstock that produces upright to ascending stems bearing pinnately compound leaves with numerous small, closely spaced leaflets. The leaflets are typically elliptical or oblong in shape and possess entire margins. Inflorescences consist of dense, capitate or somewhat elongated clusters of pea-like flowers that vary in color from white to pale purple. The calyx is characterized by its pubescence, while the larger corolla petals are prominent against the smaller keel and wings. These morphological traits help distinguish this specific legume species from other members of its genus.
