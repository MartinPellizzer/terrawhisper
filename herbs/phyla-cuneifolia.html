What is Phyla cuneifolia?

Phyla cuneifolia is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Verbenaceae.

It is a shrubby perennial herb with woody base, growing up to 1 meter in height, and has linear, entire leaves that are 2-10 cm long and 1-3 mm wide. The plant produces small, axillary, solitary flowers that are 5-6 mm in diameter, with 5-6 petals that are blue-purple in color. The inflorescence is a spike-like raceme, and the fruit is a small, dry schizocarp.

The plant is native to South America, specifically in Argentina and Uruguay, and is also found in other countries in the region.
