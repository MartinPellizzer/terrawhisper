Entada mannii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Entada mannii has woody climbing vines characterized by large pinnate leaves, robust stems, and massive woody pods containing large seeds. The leaves typically consist of numerous leaflets arranged along a central rachis, providing a dense canopy for the climber. Its stems are capable of twisting around host trees to reach sunlight in tropical forest environments. The most distinctive morphological feature is the elongated, woody fruit pod that can grow to significant lengths. Inside these pods, the seeds are often hard and dark, serving as a primary identifier for the species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of West and Central Africa, specifically spanning countries such as Nigeria, Cameroon, Gabon, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It thrives primarily within the moist, lowland forest ecosystems where high humidity and consistent rainfall are present. Its distribution follows the dense vegetation belts characteristic of the Guineo-Congolian region. Because it relies on specific ecological conditions, the species is typically found in undisturbed or semi-disturbed primary forests. This localized presence makes its range highly dependent on the preservation of African tropical forest habitats.
