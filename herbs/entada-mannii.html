Entada mannii

What's the taxonomical classification of Entada mannii?

Entada mannii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further organized into the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Within this botanical hierarchy, it is specifically assigned to the genus Entada, with its unique designation being the species mannii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Entada mannii has woody climbing vines characterized by large pinnate leaves, robust stems, and massive woody pods containing large seeds.

The leaves typically consist of numerous leaflets arranged along a central rachis, providing a dense canopy for the climber. Its stems are capable of twisting around host trees to reach sunlight in tropical forest environments. The most distinctive morphological feature is the elongated, woody fruit pod that can grow to significant lengths.

Inside these pods, the seeds are often hard and dark, serving as a primary identifier for the species.
