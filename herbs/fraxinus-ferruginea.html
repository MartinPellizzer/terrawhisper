What's the taxonomical classification of Fraxinus ferruginea?

Fraxinus ferruginea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Lamiales. It is situated within the family Oleaceae, a group known for its distinct floral structures, and is specifically identified under the genus Fraxinus. Ultimately, the unique biological identity of this organism is defined by its specific designation as the species ferruginea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fraxinus ferruginea has pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 13 lanceolate leaflets that feature serrated margins and a distinct rusty-colored pubescence on their undersides. The tree develops a spreading crown with bark that becomes deeply fissured and scaly as the specimen matures. Its flowers are small, yellowish-white, and arranged in terminal panicles that emerge during the spring. The resulting fruit consists of winged samaras that are held in dense clusters to facilitate wind dispersal. The woody stems often exhibit a reddish-brown hue when young, transitioning to a darker greyish tone in older growth.
