Dalbergia polyadelpha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia polyadelpha has a medium to large deciduous tree habit characterized by a spreading crown, pinnately compound leaves, and small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers arranged in axillary racemes. The leaves typically consist of several pairs of leaflets that are ovate or elliptical in shape with entire margins. Its bark is generally greyish or light brown and may exhibit fine textures depending on the age of the specimen. The fruit is a small, flat, indehiscent legume that contains one or two seeds. This species often features a dense branching pattern that supports its canopy structure.

Dalbergia polyadelpha can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and hypersensitivity reactions in individuals sensitive to its chemical constituents. Some users might experience localized inflammation if the sap or processed parts touch unprotected skin. While traditional medicinal uses exist, the lack of extensive clinical data means systemic toxicity remains a potential but understudied concern. Always exercise caution and consult a medical professional before any exposure to the plant material.
