Paederia pertomentosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Paederia pertomentosa?

Paederia pertomentoosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary lineage of the order Gentianales. As a member of the family Rubiaceae, it is categorized under the genus Paederia, specifically identified by the species epithet pertomentoosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paederia pertomentosa has woody, climbing stems that feature rough, scabrous textures and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with prominent veins. Small, tubular flowers emerge in clusters, typically displaying a pale color that attracts various pollinators. The plant also produces small, fleshy fruits that transition in color as they reach maturity. These morphological traits allow the species to effectively climb and spread through diverse tropical habitats.
