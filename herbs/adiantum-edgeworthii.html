Adiantum edgeworthii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adiantum edgeworthii has delicate, fan-shaped pinnules with dark, wiry, blackish-brown stipes and highly dissected, laciniate margins. The fronds are typically bipinnate or tripinnate, creating a feathery and airy appearance characteristic of the maidenhair fern family. Each small segment is thin and translucent, often displaying a deep green color when healthy. The dark, slender rachis provides a stark structural contrast to the light, intricate texture of the foliage. These fine, needle-like divisions allow the plant to efficiently capture moisture from its humid environment.
