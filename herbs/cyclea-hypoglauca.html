Cyclea hypoglauca

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyclea hypoglauca has climbing stems with trifoliate leaves that feature a distinctively glossy, dark green upper surface and a paler, non-glaucous underside. The leaflets are typically ovate to elliptical in shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often appearing in shades of pale yellow or cream. The plant develops small, fleshy fruits that eventually transition to a darker color upon maturity. These morphological traits help distinguish this species from other members of the Menispermaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of the Philippines. It thrives primarily in lowland forest habitats where humidity and shade are abundant. Its distribution is closely tied to the moist, equatorial climates characteristic of these maritime and continental Southeast Asian territories. Within these regions, the species is often found climbing through dense vegetation in undisturbed jungle ecosystems.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause allergic skin reactions, digestive distress, and potential toxicity if ingested in uncontrolled amounts. Contact with the sap or leaves may trigger contact dermatitis, resulting in redness, itching, or swelling on the skin. When consumed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its bioactive compounds. There is also a risk of systemic toxicity if the plant is used medicinally without proper scientific standardization. Because clinical data on human consumption is limited, these adverse effects remain a significant concern for safety.
