What's the taxonomical classification of Deparia giraldii?

Deparia giraldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is a member of the family Aspleniaceae, falling under the genus Deparia, and is specifically identified by the species name giraldii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Deparia giraldii has pinnate fronds typically measuring 20 to 40 centimeters in length with 10 to 20 pairs of lanceolate to oblong pinnae that feature serrated margins. The stipe is slender and often displays a brownish or reddish hue near the base. Each individual leaflet is characterized by a distinctively tapering shape and prominent venation that reaches the edges. The texture of the foliage is relatively thin and herbaceous, allowing the plant to thrive in shaded, moist environments. Small sori are arranged along the underside of the pinnules, which is a key feature for its identification among similar fern species.
