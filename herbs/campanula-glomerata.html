What's the taxonomical classification of Campanula glomerata?

Campanula glomerata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Campanulaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Campanula, and its unique species designation, glomerata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Campanula glomerata has upright, hairy stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and bear dense, terminal clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate with serrated margins, while the upper stem leaves are smaller and more narrow. Each individual flower features a five-lobed corolla that creates a distinct campanulate shape. The inflorescence is characterized by its crowded arrangement, where many blossoms are packed tightly at the top of the plant. This morphological structure allows the plant to present a concentrated display of color to pollinators.
