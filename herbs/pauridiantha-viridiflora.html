Pauridiantha viridiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pauridiantha viridiflora has small, green, actinomorphic flowers characterized by five distinct petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant displays a compact growth habit with slender stems that support these inconspicuous blooms. The foliage consists of simple, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. These leaves possess smooth margins and a subtle texture that complements the delicate appearance of the floral structures. The overall morphology is defined by its subtle coloration and diminutive scale within its natural habitat.
