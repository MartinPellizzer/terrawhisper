Arisaema sinii

What's the taxonomical classification of Arisaema sinii?

Arisaema sinii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated under the order Alismatales. This plant is further categorized into the family Araceae, falling under the genus Arisaema, and is specifically identified by the species epithet sinii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arisaema sinii has a single, large, cordate leaf that emerges from a tuberous rhizome and is supported by a long, fleshy petiole. The leaf blade is characterized by a deep, heart-shaped base and a prominent central vein that extends toward the apex. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix partially enclosed by a spathe, which typically features a greenish to purplish coloration. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a bright red color as they reach maturity. These morphological features are characteristic of the Araceae family and help identify the species within its native habitat.

Arisaema sinii contains high concentrations of calcium oxalate crystals, saponins, and various alkaloids that serve as its primary bioactive components. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe irritation of the mouth, a burning sensation in the throat, swelling of the throat, difficulty swallowing, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and respiratory distress. Upon contact or ingestion, the calcium oxalate crystals within the plant trigger immediate discomfort in the mucous membranes. This irritation often progresses to significant swelling that can obstruct the airway and lead to life-threatening respiratory distress. Gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal pain typically follow the initial oral contact. Immediate medical attention is necessary if any difficulty swallowing or breathing occurs.
