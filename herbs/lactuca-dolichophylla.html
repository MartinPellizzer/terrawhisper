Lactuca dolichophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Lactuca dolichophylla?

Lactuca dolichophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and placed in the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Specifically, it is classified under the genus Lactuca, and its unique identity is defined by its specific epithet, dolichophylla.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lactuca dolichophylla contains sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and various phenolic compounds that provide its primary therapeutic potential. These secondary metabolites are responsible for the plant's observed antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The sesquiterpene lactones specifically contribute to its potential antimicrobial activity against certain pathogens. Additionally, the presence of organic acids and essential oils supports its traditional use in soothing digestive distress. Researchers continue to investigate these specific chemical profiles to better understand their pharmacological applications in modern medicine.
