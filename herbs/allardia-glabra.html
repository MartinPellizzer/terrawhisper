Allardia glabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Asterales. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allardia glabra has upright to ascending stems that are typically reddish or brownish and densely covered in fine, short hairs. The leaves are small, lanceolate to ovate, and arranged in a somewhat crowded fashion along the stem. These leaves possess a single costa that often reaches the apex and lack prominent marginal teeth. The plant is characterized by its lack of a well-developed rhizoid system compared to other mosses, often growing on rocky or sandy substrates.
