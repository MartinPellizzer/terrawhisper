10 Medicinal Constituents Of Cissus Quadrangularis (Veldt Grape)

Cissus quadrangularis has active constituents such as flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

These constituents have anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antidiabetic effects, which can alleviate conditions such as joint pain and osteoarthritis, promote wound healing, and regulate blood sugar levels.

Consequently, individuals can benefit from improved mobility and reduced chronic pain, enhanced recovery from injuries, and better management of diabetes.

1. Oleic acid

Cissus quadrangularis oleic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from its stem.

This fatty acid has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health issues, including inflammation and pain. Studies have shown that oleic acid exhibits potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential therapeutic agent for the management of chronic diseases such as arthritis and cardiovascular disorders.

Its analgesic effects also make it a promising treatment option for pain relief.

2. Ursolic acid

Cissus quadrangularis ursolic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from the plant's leaves and stems.

It has been studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

Ursolic acid has been shown to inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells and may have applications in the treatment of various diseases, such as Alzheimer's and cardiovascular disease.

3. Β-sitosterol

Cissus quadrangularis β-sitosterol is a type of plant sterol that has been isolated from this herb.

It is a potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compound that has been found to exhibit numerous health benefits, including reducing cholesterol levels, improving cardiovascular health, and alleviating symptoms of osteoarthritis.

Further research is needed to fully understand its effects on human health.

4. Quercetin

Cissus quadrangularis quercetin is a bioflavonoid compound found in its leaves and stem.

Quercetin has been traditionally used to treat various health issues, including inflammation, allergies, and cardiovascular diseases. It exhibits anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help protect cells from damage and reduce the risk of chronic diseases.

Quercetin also possesses anti-cancer and antimicrobial activities, making it a potential natural remedy for various medical conditions.

5. Kaempferol

Cissus quadrangularis kaempferol is a flavonoid glycoside compound found in various plant extracts of Cissus quadrangularis.

It has been identified as one of the major bioactive compounds responsible for the herb's medicinal properties.

Kaempferol possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, which may contribute to its traditional use in folk medicine for treating various health conditions such as arthritis and inflammation.

6. Gallic acid

Cissus quadrangularis gallic acid is a naturally occurring phenolic compound found in its leaves and stems.

This bioactive molecule has been extensively studied for its various health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Gallic acid is believed to play a crucial role in the traditional use of Cissus quadrangularis as a treatment for joint pain, arthritis, and other inflammatory conditions.

7. Caffeic acid

Cissus quadrangularis caffeic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from its stem and leaves.

This polyphenolic acid has been traditionally used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Research suggests that caffeic acid may exhibit activity against certain diseases, such as cancer, diabetes, and cardiovascular disorders, making it a promising natural product for further investigation and development into therapeutic applications.

8. Vanillic acid

Cissus quadrangularis vanillic acid is a bioactive compound isolated from the stem of this plant.

It has been reported to possess various pharmacological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. Vanillic acid has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, making it a potential natural remedy for the treatment of inflammatory diseases.

Additionally, its antioxidant activity may help protect against oxidative stress and cell damage.

9. Triterpenoids

Cissus quadrangularis triterpenoids is a class of bioactive compounds isolated from the plant's leaves and stems.

These molecules have been found to exhibit various pharmacological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties.

The triterpenoids in Cissus quadrangularis have been shown to inhibit the growth of cancer cells, reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, and even display neuroprotective effects.

10. Flavonoids

Cissus quadrangularis flavonoids is a group of bioactive compounds found in its leaves and stem.

These flavonoids have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making them potentially useful for treating various health conditions such as arthritis, diabetes, and wound healing.

The flavonoids in Cissus quadrangularis have also been found to exhibit antiviral and antibacterial activities, contributing to its traditional use as a medicine in many cultures.
