10 Possible Side Effects Of Cissus Quadrangularis

Cissus quadrangularis has some side effects when used improperly, such as stomach upset, diarrhea, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can be caused by consuming large doses or combining it with other medications. In severe cases, they can worsen one's life by causing dehydration, nutrient deficiencies, and even triggering other health conditions, such as kidney or liver problems.

This may lead to missed work or school, social isolation, and decreased productivity.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Cissus quadrangularis causes allergic reactions due to its high concentration of phytochemicals and saponins.

These compounds can stimulate an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

The exact mechanisms behind these reactions are not fully understood, but it is thought that the body's attempt to break down the active components may trigger an allergic response, resulting in uncomfortable and potentially life-threatening symptoms.

2. Triggers stomach ulcers

Cissus quadrangularis triggers stomach ulcers due to its high concentration of polyphenolic compounds and anthraquinones, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract.

These compounds can disrupt the natural balance of gut bacteria, leading to an imbalance that allows harmful bacteria to thrive and cause inflammation in the stomach lining, resulting in ulceration.

The herb's ability to stimulate digestion and increase stomach acid production may also contribute to this side effect.

3. Induces kidney damage

Cissus quadrangularis induces kidney damage due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides and flavonoids.

These compounds can irritate the kidneys and cause inflammation, leading to damage and potential long-term harm.

Additionally, Cissus quadrangularis may also interact with certain medications and increase the risk of kidney problems in individuals with pre-existing conditions or those taking nephrotoxic substances.

4. Increases blood sugar

Cissus quadrangularis increases blood sugar levels in some individuals.

This is attributed to its ability to stimulate insulin secretion and improve glucose tolerance, which can lead to hyperglycemia in people with pre-existing diabetes or those who are at risk of developing the condition.

Additionally, Cissus quadrangularis has been shown to increase cortisol levels, which can also contribute to elevated blood sugar levels.

5. Reduces fertility

Cissus quadrangularis reduces fertility by suppressing estrogen levels and disrupting hormonal balance.

This can lead to a decrease in sperm count and quality in men, while women may experience irregular menstrual cycles or even stop menstruating altogether due to its phytoestrogenic properties.

The plant's ability to mimic the effects of estrogen can also cause issues with ovulation and fertilization.

6. Interacts with warfarin

Cissus quadrangularis interacts with warfarin by potentially increasing its levels in the blood.

This may lead to an over-anticoagulant effect, causing bleeding or bruising more easily. The exact mechanism is unclear, but it's thought that Cissus quadrangularis may inhibit the breakdown of warfarin in the body, allowing it to accumulate and enhance its effects.

As a result, individuals taking warfarin should be cautious when using Cissus quadrangularis concurrently.

7. Disrupts liver function

Cissus quadrangularis disrupts liver function due to its high concentration of flavonoids and tannins.

These compounds can cause liver enzyme inhibition, leading to impaired metabolism and detoxification processes.

Additionally, Cissus quadrangularis's ability to stimulate the production of bilirubin may contribute to this disruption, potentially resulting in abnormal liver function test results.

8. Affects heart rhythm

Cissus quadrangularis affects heart rhythm by altering the body's natural electrical activity.

This can cause irregular heartbeats, palpitations, and even tachycardia in some individuals. The herb contains compounds that may interact with cardiac medications or exacerbate underlying heart conditions, leading to these cardiovascular issues.

Additionally, Cissus quadrangularis may also increase heart rate and blood pressure due to its stimulatory effects on the nervous system.

9. Lowers blood pressure

Cissus quadrangularis lowers blood pressure by interacting with the body's natural ability to regulate blood flow.

The compound ursolic acid in Cissus quadrangularis helps to dilate blood vessels, which reduces blood pressure and can cause a decrease in systemic vascular resistance. This mechanism of action is thought to be responsible for the hypotensive effect observed in some individuals taking Cissus quadrangularis supplements.

As a result, people may experience decreased blood pressure, potentially leading to symptoms such as dizziness or lightheadedness.

10. Triggers thyroid issues

Cissus quadrangularis triggers thyroid issues due to its potential interaction with certain medications and its ability to affect hormone levels.

The herb contains compounds that may bind to thyroid receptors, altering the normal functioning of the thyroid gland. This can lead to changes in thyroid hormone production, potentially causing hypothyroidism or hyperthyroidism.

Additionally, Cissus quadrangularis may enhance the effects of thyroid medications, exacerbating existing thyroid conditions.
