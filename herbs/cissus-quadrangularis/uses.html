Devil'S Ivy (Cissus quadrangularis) Uses

Devil's Ivy is used to treat osteoarthritis and joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It supports fracture healing and improves bone density, aiding in the recovery from bone fractures. The plant also helps with muscle injuries and post-surgical recovery by reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair. It is effective for muscle pain and rheumatoid arthritis as well.

Devil'S Ivy (Cissus quadrangularis) Monograph

1. Osteoarthritis

Cissus quadrangularis, or Devil's Ivy, is used traditionally to support joint health in osteoarthritis. It contains compounds that may reduce inflammation and promote cartilage repair. Studies suggest it can alleviate pain and improve mobility in affected individuals. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties contribute to its therapeutic effects.

2. Fracture healing

Cissus quadrangularis, or Devil's Ivy, is renowned for its ability to accelerate fracture healing. Its active compounds, including flavonoids and calcium oxalate, stimulate bone cell growth and enhance collagen synthesis. Traditionally used in Ayurvedic and African medicine, it supports tissue regeneration and reduces inflammation, making it a valuable herbal remedy for bone repair.

3. Joint pain

Cissus quadrangularis is used to alleviate joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce swelling and stiffness, making it beneficial for conditions like arthritis. Traditionally, it is prepared as a decoction or powder and consumed regularly for sustained relief. Its effectiveness is attributed to compounds like flavonoids and saponins.

4. Bone density loss

Cissus quadrangularis has been traditionally used to support bone health and may help prevent bone density loss. Its rich content of calcium, magnesium, and other minerals may contribute to maintaining strong bones. Studies suggest it may enhance bone formation and reduce bone resorption, making it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for skeletal health.

5. Bone fractures

Cissus quadrangularis is used traditionally to support bone healing due to its high concentration of calcium and other minerals. It is believed to stimulate bone cell growth and enhance fracture repair. Studies suggest it may reduce inflammation and promote tissue regeneration, making it a valuable herb in traditional medicine for treating bone fractures.

6. Muscle injury

Cissus quadrangularis is used to support muscle recovery and reduce inflammation in injuries. It contains compounds that promote tissue repair and may enhance bone and muscle regeneration. Traditionally applied topically or consumed internally, it is valued for its ability to accelerate healing and alleviate pain in strained or broken muscles.

Cissus quadrangularis supports post-surgical recovery by promoting tissue repair and reducing inflammation.
