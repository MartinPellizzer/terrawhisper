Bupleurum kweichowense

What's the taxonomical classification of Bupleurum kweichowense?

Bupleurum kweichowense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Apiales. It is further categorized into the family Apiaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive flowering structures. Within this family, the plant is placed in the genus Bupleurum, ultimately reaching its specific taxonomic designation as the species kweichowense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bupleurum kweichowense has perennial herbaceous stems that support alternate, ovate-oblong leaves with serrated margins and a characteristic yellowish-green coloration. The foliage is typically arranged in a way that creates a somewhat whorled appearance near the top of the plant. Small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered into dense, terminal umbels that sit atop long, slender peduncles. These inflorescences consist of numerous tiny florets that provide a delicate texture to the overall structure. The root system is robust and woody, allowing the plant to anchor firmly in its native habitat.
