10 Possible Side Effects Of Asparagus Cochinchinensis (Wild Asparagus)

Asparagus cochinchinensis has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

These side effects are caused by excessive consumption, individual sensitivities, and combinations with other substances.

For instance, overconsumption of Asparagus cochinchinensis can exacerbate gastrointestinal problems in individuals with pre-existing conditions, affecting their daily routine and quality of life.

1. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Asparagus cochinchinensis causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of specific proteins and allergenic compounds in its leaves, stems, and roots.

The exact mechanisms behind these allergic responses are not fully understood, but it is believed that the immune system mistakes these substances for foreign invaders, leading to an overactive response and symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Oxalates are naturally occurring compounds found in many plants and can increase the risk of developing kidney stones by increasing calcium levels in urine and reducing the ability of kidneys to concentrate urine.
