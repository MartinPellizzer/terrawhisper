Ixora nienkui

What's the taxonomical classification of Ixora nienkui?

Ixora nienkui belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Gentianaales. This plant is further categorized under the family Rubiaceae, a prominent group of flowering plants, and is specifically identified within the genus Ixora. Its complete scientific designation is defined by its species name, nienkui.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ixora nienkui has woody, evergreen shrub characteristics defined by elliptic to oblong leaves that are dark green, leathery, and arranged in opposite pairs along its stems.

The foliage typically features a glossy upper surface and a slightly paler underside with prominent venation. Clusters of tubular, salverform flowers emerge from terminal cymes, often presenting in vibrant shades of red or orange. These dense inflorescences are supported by sturdy peduncles that lift the blooms above the leafy canopy.

The plant's overall structure is compact and bushy, making it a common choice for ornamental hedging.

        What are the herbal preparations of Ixora nienkui?

        Ixora nienkui is prepared as a decoction, a topical paste, or an infused tea using its leaves and stems. The leaves are often crushed into a fine paste to be applied directly to the skin for its localized effects.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Ixora nienkui can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may trigger localized dermatitis characterized by redness and itching in sensitive individuals. If a person consumes any part of the shrub, they might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. While specific toxicological data for this particular species is limited, general caution regarding Ixora varieties is recommended. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when handling or being near the plant.
