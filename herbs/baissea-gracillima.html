Baissea gracillima

What's the taxonomical classification of Baissea gracillima?

Baissea gracillima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Gentianaales. This specific plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae, falling under the genus Baissea, with its unique identification completed at the species level as gracillima.

Baissea gracillima is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion where the dried plant parts are steeped in hot water to extract active compounds. What side-effects this plant can have?

Baissea gracillima can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and potential allergic reactions if the plant comes into direct contact with human tissue. Individuals may experience redness, itching, or localized swelling upon touching the leaves or stems. Some people might also develop more severe inflammatory responses depending on their specific sensitivity to the plant's chemical compounds. It is important to avoid handling this species without protective gloves to prevent these dermatological issues. Monitoring for any signs of an adverse reaction is recommended if accidental contact occurs.
