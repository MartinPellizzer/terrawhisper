What's the taxonomical classification of Trichosanthes pedata?

Trichosanthes pedata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Cucurbitales. It is further categorized into the family Cucurbitaceae, which places it among a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Trichosanthes and is specifically recognized by its species name, pedata.

What are the therapeutic actions of Trichosanthes pedata?

Trichosanthes pedata has various therapeutic actions including diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and cooling properties used primarily to treat urinary tract infections and fever. The plant contains bioactive compounds that help reduce inflammation in the body and promote the excretion of excess fluids through the kidneys. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of heat-related illnesses and systemic toxicity. By stimulating urination, it assists in flushing out metabolic waste and pathogens from the renal system. These medicinal qualities make it a valuable component in herbal formulations targeting kidney and bladder health.
