Trichosanthes pedata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichosanthes pedata has a prostrate, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to several meters. The leaves are typically cordate or ovate with serrated margins and are arranged alternately along the climbing vines. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often presenting in clusters that lack highly decorative petals. The reproductive structures include small, fleshy berries that develop after pollination. These berries are frequently used to distinguish the species from other members of the Cucurbitaceae family.
