Scabiosa comosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Scabiosa comosa?

Scabiosa comosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Dipsacales and falls under the family Caprifoliaceae. Finally, it is classified within the genus Scabiosa, specifically identified by its species name, comosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scabiosa comosa has woody, branched stems that support terminal flower heads composed of numerous small, tubular florets surrounded by a ring of larger, petal-like outer florets. The leaves are typically deeply lobed or pinnatifid with serrated margins, appearing primarily along the lower portions of the plant. The inflorescence is characterized by a dense, rounded head that sits atop a long, slender peduncle. These flower heads often transition in color from pale lilac to deeper shades of blue as they mature. The overall structure is bushy and compact, reflecting its adaptation to open, rocky environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including flavonoids, alkaloids, essential oils, iridoids, and terpenoids. The flavonoids provide significant antioxidant properties that help protect cells from damage. Within its chemical structure, alkaloids act as potent bioactive agents that can influence various physiological processes. Essential oils contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and offer antimicrobial benefits. Additionally, the presence of iridoids and terpenoids supports its complex therapeutic potential through diverse biological activities.

As discussed in a study, Scabiosa comosa inflorescence is a traditional Mongolian medicine used in the treatment of liver diseases. The study utilized a flavonoid-rich Scabiosa comosa inflorescence extract, referred to as TF-SC, to investigate anti-fibrotic efficacy. In the research, rats weighing 200-250 g were injected with CCl4 at a dose of 2 mL/kg body weight. TF-SC was shown to decrease hydroxyproline content and collagen deposition in liver tissues. Furthermore, the extract decreased the expression of alpha-SMA, collagen I, and fibronectin in rats with CCl4-induced hepatic fibrosis. Mechanistically, TF-SC attenuated fibrosis by selectively inhibiting Smad3 phosphorylation.
