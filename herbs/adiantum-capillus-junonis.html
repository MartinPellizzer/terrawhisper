Adiantum capillus-junonis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adiantum capillus-junonis has delicate, fan-shaped pinnules that are dark green, finely dissected, and attached to thin, black, wire-like stipes. The leaflets exhibit a characteristic laciniate or deeply lobed margin that gives the fronds a lacy appearance. These small, triangular segments are arranged in a complex, multi-pinnate structure along the rachis. The plant is distinguished by its dark, almost ebony-colored veins and stems which contrast sharply with the green foliage. This combination of wiry stems and intricate leaf geometry defines its unique morphological profile.
