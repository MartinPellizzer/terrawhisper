Hibiscus × rosa-sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hibiscus × rosa-sinensis?

Hibiscus × rosa-sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Hibiscus, and its specific species designation, rosa-sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hibiscus × rosa-sinensis has woody, perennial stems that support large, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a glossy, dark green texture. The flowers are showy and trumpet-shaped, featuring five distinct petals that come in various colors like red, pink, or yellow. A prominent characteristic is the long, protruding staminal column that emerges from the center of the flower. This column houses both the numerous pollen-bearing anthers and the single style with its five-lobed stigma. The plant typically produces these blossoms on terminal or axillary branches throughout the growing season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to subtropical and tropical regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Taiwan, and parts of Southeast Asia. Because it thrives in warm, frost-free environments, it has been widely naturalized in many other tropical and subtropical zones around the world. Many gardeners in the Americas and other warm climates cultivate it for its ornamental value. It struggles to survive in temperate regions where freezing temperatures are common. Consequently, its global presence is largely determined by human introduction and the local climate's ability to support tropical growth.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining soil rich in organic matter and a location that provides full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

To ensure healthy development, it should be watered regularly to keep the soil consistently moist but never waterlogged. Mulching around the base helps retain necessary moisture and regulates soil temperature during extreme weather. Pruning the shrub in early spring encourages vigorous new growth and maintains a tidy shape.

Regular fertilization during the growing season provides the nutrients required for abundant flowering.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Hibiscus × rosa-sinensis contains medicinal properties primarily found in its flowers, leaves, and roots. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause side effects including allergic reaction, nausea, dizziness, stomach upset, hypoglycemia, and hypotension. Some individuals may experience skin irritation or respiratory issues as part of an allergic reaction. Digestive disturbances such as nausea and stomach upset are also reported by some users. The plant may lead to dizziness due to its physiological effects on the body. Furthermore, it can cause dangerous drops in blood sugar levels known as hypoglycemia. It is also known to lower blood pressure, which results in hypotension.
