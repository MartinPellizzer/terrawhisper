Scutia guayaquilensis

Scutia guayaquilensis, also known as the Andean rosewood or scutea, is a plant species native to South America, characterized by its fragrant flowers and aromatic wood.

The medicinal properties of Scutia guayaquilensis include its ability to reduce inflammation and exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant activities.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, as well as to reduce pain and improve digestion.

Herbal preparations of Scutia guayaquilensis can be found in the form of infusions, decoctions, and essential oils, which are used to promote overall well-being and alleviate symptoms of various health issues.
