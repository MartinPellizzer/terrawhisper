Alpine Buttercup

Doronicum pardalianches

Alpine Buttercup (Doronicum pardalianches) is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe, known for its bright yellow flowers and historical use in traditional medicine to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of respiratory conditions.

Morphology

The Alpine Buttercup (Doronicum pardalianches) is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems that are typically hairy, bearing alternate, doubly pinnate leaves with finely divided segments and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes at the tips of the stems.

Distribution

The Alpine Buttercup (Doronicum pardalianches) is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, including the Pyrenees, Alps, and Carpathians, and is commonly found in rocky meadows, alpine grasslands, and forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters.

Traditional Uses

In traditional herbal medicine, Alpine Buttercup (Doronicum pardalianches) has been used to treat skin conditions, digestive issues, and as a vulnerary to promote wound healing.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and European herbalism, the Alpine Buttercup (Doronicum pardalianches) has been historically used for its purported anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and wound-healing properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Alpine Buttercup (Doronicum pardalianches) is characterized by the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds, which contribute to its potential medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

The Alpine Buttercup (Doronicum pardalianches) exhibits anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and analgesic properties, making it traditionally used in herbal medicine to address conditions such as digestive disorders, respiratory ailments, and musculoskeletal pain.

Botanical Identification

Alpine Buttercup, scientifically known as Doronicum pardalianches, belongs to the family Ranunculaceae. It is also called the Alpine Crowfoot and is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. The plant has a rhizomatous root system, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers with five petals. It is sometimes referred to as "Pardaline" in older herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Alpine Buttercup (Doronicum pardalianches) is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential toxicity. It should be avoided by individuals with known allergies to plants in the Ranunculaceae family. Always consult a qualified herbalist or healthcare provider before use.
