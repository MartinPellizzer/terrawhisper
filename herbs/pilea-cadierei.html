Pilea cadierei

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea cadierei?

Pilea cadierei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Rosales. This species is a part of the family Urticaceae and is specifically identified by its genus, Pilea, and its unique species designation, cadierei.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pilea cadierei can cause mild skin irritation or allergic reactions such as redness, itching, or a rash if its sap comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. While there is limited clinical data regarding its toxicity, caution is advised because many members of the Urticaceae family contain compounds that trigger dermatological sensitivities. If the plant is accidentally ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress, including stomach aches or nausea. Pet owners should also be aware that contact with the leaves might cause discomfort for cats or dogs if they attempt to chew on them. It is always best to handle this plant with care and wash your hands thoroughly after pruning.
