What's the taxonomical classification of Daphniphyllum paxianum?

Daphniphyllum paxianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Saxifragales. This specific plant is a constituent of the family Daphniphyllaceae, falling under the genus Daphniphyllum, and is identified by the specific epithet paxianum.

According to a study published by "Organic letters", the stem of Daphniphyllum paxianum Rosenth contains the first diamino Daphniphyllum alkaloid, daphnipaxinin (1). This specific compound features an unprecedented heterohexacyclic fused skeleton. In addition to daphnipaxinin (1), the plant also contains a known alkaloid identified as daphnicyclidin A (2). Researchers established the structure and absolute stereochemistry of compound 1 using spectral methods, particularly two-dimensional NMR techniques and CD analysis.
