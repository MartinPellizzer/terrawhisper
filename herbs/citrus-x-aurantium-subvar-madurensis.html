Kumquat (Citrus × Aurantium Subvar. Madurensis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Citrus × aurantium subvar. madurensis.

Kumquat, scientifically known as Citrus × aurantium subvar. madurensis, is a small citrus fruit that is also considered a medicinal herb due to its rich nutritional profile and bioactive compounds. It is valued for its primary benefits, including its high content of vitamin C, antioxidants, and anti-inflammatory properties, which support immune function and overall health. Traditionally used in Chinese and Ayurvedic medicine, kumquat has been employed to aid digestion, reduce stress, and promote respiratory health. In modern wellness practices, it is often incorporated into superfood blends, skincare products, and herbal teas for its detoxifying and rejuvenating effects. Its unique feature is its sweet, tangy flavor and the presence of hesperidin, a rare flavonoid that contributes to its medicinal value and distinguishes it from other citrus fruits.
