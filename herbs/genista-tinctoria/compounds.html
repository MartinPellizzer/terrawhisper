Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria) Compounds

Yellow Broom contains several key active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to astringent effects. Phenolic acids and coumarin are also present, offering anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits. Terpenoids and glycosides may support immune function, while saponins and essential oils provide additional therapeutic potential. Alkaloids and anthocyanins further contribute to the plant's medicinal profile.

Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria) that contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral effects, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating respiratory and skin conditions. They are commonly extracted from the plant's flowers and leaves.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in reducing inflammation and bleeding. It is found in the plant's leaves and flowers, supporting its traditional use in treating wounds and digestive issues. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal potency during preparation.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria) contributes to its antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing inflammation. This compound is found in the plant's leaves and flowers, and is believed to aid in digestive health and detoxification processes. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating urinary tract infections and as a diuretic. Coumarin also has anticoagulant properties, though its effects can vary based on dosage and individual physiology.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria) are bioactive compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. These compounds are extracted from the plant's flowers and leaves, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

6. Glycoside

The glycoside known as genistin is found in Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and as a mild laxative. Genistin is also valued for its potential antioxidant properties.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting mild cleansing and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponins may support digestive health and are often used in traditional herbal preparations. Their presence enhances the plant's overall therapeutic potential.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria) contains volatile compounds like limonene and pinene, which contribute to its aromatic profile. These compounds may support respiratory and digestive health, though research on their therapeutic applications is limited. The oil is often used in aromatherapy and traditional herbal practices.

9. Alkaloid

The alkaloid sparteine is found in Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria). It has been traditionally used for its antispasmodic and sedative properties. Sparteine may help relieve muscle spasms and promote relaxation, though its use requires caution due to potential toxicity.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Yellow Broom (Genista tinctoria). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support overall health. This compound is valued in herbal medicine for its potential to reduce inflammation and enhance immune function.
