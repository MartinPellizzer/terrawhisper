Atalantia kwangtungensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Atalantia kwangtungensis?

Atalantia kwangtungensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Sapindales. This plant is a member of the family Rutaaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Atalantia, reaching its final taxonomic designation at the species level as Atalantia kwangtungensis.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, digestive issues, respiratory ailments, and certain inflammatory conditions. Its leaves and fruits are often prepared in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of stomach aches and intestinal discomfort. In some regional practices, the plant is applied topically to address dermatological irritations or infections. It is also believed to possess properties that help mitigate coughing and other lung-related distress. These therapeutic applications rely on the bioactive compounds found within its various parts.
