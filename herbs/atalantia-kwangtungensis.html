Atalantia kwangtungensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Atalantia kwangtungensis?

Atalantia kwangtungensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Sapindales. This plant is a member of the family Rutaaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Atalantia, reaching its final taxonomic designation at the species level as Atalantia kwangtungensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Atalantia kwangtungensis has small, evergreen leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape with entire margins and an acute apex.

The plant typically exhibits a shrubby growth habit with slender, woody branches. Its foliage often possesses a slightly leathery texture and a dark green coloration on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually developing into small, globose fruits.

These fruits are characterized by a relatively thin rind and a fleshy interior containing multiple seeds.
