Anaphalis hancockii

What's the taxonomical classification of Anaphalis hancockii?

Anaphalis hancockii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Anaphalis, and its species name, hancockii.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Anaphalis hancockii can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant's sap or tissues come into direct contact with sensitive skin. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in the Asteraceae family contain sesquiterpene lactones that act as potent allergens. Individuals with known sensitivities to daisies or ragweed may experience heightened reactions such as redness, itching, or blistering. These dermatological responses are typically localized to the area of contact but can vary in intensity based on individual immune sensitivity. Careful handling is advised to prevent unexpected hypersensitivity reactions during physical interaction with the species.
