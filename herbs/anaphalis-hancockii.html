Anaphalis hancockii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anaphalis hancockii has perennial, woody subshrubs characterized by linear to lanceolate leaves and small, white, disciform flower heads arranged in terminal corymbs. The foliage consists of narrow, entire leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its inflorescences feature numerous tiny tubular florets clustered closely together, lacking the large ray petals found in many other Asteraceae. The stems are often branched and can develop a somewhat woody texture at the base as the plant matures. These morphological traits are adapted to its specific high-altitude habitat.

Its distribution is restricted to specific montane ecosystems characterized by rocky terrain and cold climates.
