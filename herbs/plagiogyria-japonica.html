Plagiogyria japonica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Plagiogyria japonica has a thalloid morphology consisting of a thin, branched, and creeping prothallus that typically grows in a flattened, spreading pattern. The thallus is dark green to brownish in color and lacks a distinct midrib or complex internal differentiation. Small, cup-shaped gametophores emerge from the surface of the thallus to facilitate reproduction. These reproductive structures are often clustered and can be seen spreading across moist substrates. The overall structure is delicate and adapted for life in damp, shaded environments.
