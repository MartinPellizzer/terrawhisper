Polemonium caeruleum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polemonium caeruleum has basal, compound leaves that are deeply lobed into narrow, blue-green segments and upright stems bearing terminal clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers with white centers. The foliage exhibits a soft texture due to fine hairs covering the leaf surfaces and stems. Each flower possesses five petals that flare outward, often featuring distinct veining that guides pollinators toward the center. The plant typically grows in clumps, reaching heights of twelve to twenty-four inches depending on environmental conditions. Small, rounded seed capsules develop following pollination to ensure the next generation of growth.
