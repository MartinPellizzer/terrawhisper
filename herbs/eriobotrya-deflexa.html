Eriobotrya deflexa

What's the taxonomical classification of Eriobotrya deflexa?

Eriobotrya deflexa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Eriobotrya, with its specific designation being the species deflexa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eriobotrya deflexa has large, leathery, evergreen leaves that are oblong-lanceolate in shape, featuring serrated margins and a distinctive dense covering of brownish-white tomentum on the undersides. The upper surfaces of the foliage are dark green and glabrous, providing a sharp contrast to the fuzzy texture below. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody stems that become increasingly textured with age. During the flowering season, the plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into large, fleshy pome fruits. The overall structure is that of a dense, spreading shrub or small tree with a robust canopy.

What are the pharmacological activities of Eriobotrya deflexa?

This plant has antioxidant and inhibitor of matrix metalloproteinase-9 activity. These specific pharmacological properties suggest that Eriobotrya deflexa extracts can help mitigate oxidative stress and prevent tissue degradation. By neutralizing free radicals, the plant protects cells from damage caused by reactive oxygen species. Furthermore, its ability to inhibit matrix metalloproteinase-9 makes it a potential candidate for treating inflammatory conditions and skin aging. Such mechanisms highlight the medicinal importance of this species in modern therapeutic research.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (3S)-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->3)-[4-O-(E)-coumaroyl]-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->6)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-linalool and (3S)-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->3)-[4-O-(Z)-coumaroyl]-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->6)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-linalool. These complex glycosides are characterized by their specific rhamnosyl and glucosyl structures attached to a linaool core. The presence of coumaroyl groups in different isomeric forms, specifically the E and Z configurations, distinguishes these two distinct molecules. Such specialized chemical structures are often studied for their unique biological activities and therapeutic potential. These compounds represent the intricate phytochemical profile found within the plant's tissues.

As discussed in a study, a phytochemical study on a methanolic extract of leaves of Eriobotrya deflexa led to the isolation and characterization of 9 terpenoid compounds. 4 of these are new chemical entities, which include 2 monoterpene glycosides identified as (3S)-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->3)-[4-O-(E)-coumaroyl]-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->6)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-linalool (1) and (3S)-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->3)-[4-O-(Z)-coumaroyl]-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->6)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-linalool (2). Additionally, 2 triterpene acids were isolated, specifically 1beta,2alpha,19alpha-trihydroxy-3-oxo-12-ursen-28-oic acid (3) and 2alpha,3alpha,19alpha-trihydroxy-12-oleanen-28-oic acid (4). The activities of these isolates were determined in an in vitro antiproliferation test.
