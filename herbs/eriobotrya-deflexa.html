Eriobotrya deflexa

What's the taxonomical classification of Eriobotrya deflexa?

Eriobotrya deflexa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Eriobotrya, with its specific designation being the species deflexa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eriobotrya deflexa has large, leathery, evergreen leaves that are oblong-lanceolate in shape, featuring serrated margins and a distinctive dense covering of brownish-white tomentum on the undersides. The upper surfaces of the foliage are dark green and glabrous, providing a sharp contrast to the fuzzy texture below. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody stems that become increasingly textured with age. During the flowering season, the plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into large, fleshy pome fruits. The overall structure is that of a dense, spreading shrub or small tree with a robust canopy.

The species is typically distributed across temperate zones that offer consistent moisture and moderate temperatures. What are the pharmacological activities of Eriobotrya deflexa?

This plant has antioxidant properties and acts as an inhibitor of matrix metalloproteinase-9 activity. These specific pharmacological actions suggest that extracts from Eriobotrya deflexa may play a role in protecting cells from oxidative stress. By suppressing the activity of matrix metalloproteinase-9, the plant might also help prevent the degradation of the extracellular matrix. Such biological mechanisms are often studied for their potential in managing inflammatory processes and tissue remodeling. These combined effects highlight the medicinal potential of the species in various therapeutic contexts.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", the 70% aqueous acetone extracts of Eriobotrya deflexa were evaluated using various antioxidant assays. These extracts effectively inhibited the formation of .OH generated in the Fenton reaction system. Among the extracts whose reducing power activities were determined, Eriobotrya deflexa showed high activity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (3S)-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->3)-[4-O-(E)-coumaroyl]-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->6)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-linalool and (3S)-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->3)-[4-O-(Z)-coumaroyl]-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->6)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-linalool. These complex glycosides are specialized secondary metabolites found within the Eriobotrya deflexa species. The chemical structures of these molecules are characterized by their specific coumaroyl substitutions and rhamnosyl linkages. Researchers study these unique glycosylated derivatives to understand their potential biological activities and therapeutic properties. Such detailed phytochemical profiles contribute to the overall medicinal value of the plant.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", a phytochemical study on a methanolic extract of leaves of Eriobotrya deflexa led to the isolation and characterization of nine terpenoid compounds. Four of these are new chemical entities, which include two monoterpene glycosides identified as (3S)-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->3)-[4-O-(E)-coumaroyl]-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->6)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-linalool (1) and (3S)-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->3)-[4-O-(Z)-coumaroyl]-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->6)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-linalool (2). Additionally, the study isolated two triterpene acids, specifically 1beta,2alpha,19alpha-trihydroxy-3-oxo-12-ursen-28-oic acid (3) and 2alpha,3alpha,19alpha-trihydroxy-12-oleanen-28-oic acid (4). The structures of these isolates were elucidated through spectroscopic analysis. The activities of these isolates were also determined in an in vitro antiproliferation test.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating skin aging, reducing inflammation, and managing oxidative stress in various biological tissues. Its bioactive compounds help mitigate the visible effects of the aging process in the skin by promoting cellular regeneration. The plant also possesses antioxidant properties that protect cells from damage caused by free radicals. Additionally, its medicinal qualities are applied to soothe irritated skin and support overall dermatological health. These multifaceted benefits make it a valuable component in many topical treatments.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Eriobotrya deflex is used for its potential to serve as an inhibitor of the ageing process in the skin. The plant was among eight native Taiwanese materials screened for phenolic contents and their ability to measure matrix metalloproteinase-9 (MMP-9) activity. The extracted yields of plants in the study ranged from 3.7 % to 16.9 %. Total phenolic contents ranged from 25.4 to 36.8 mg GAE/g dry material. The extract from Eriobotrya deflex was shown to inhibit MMP-9 activity of WS-1 cell after ultraviolet B irradiation.
