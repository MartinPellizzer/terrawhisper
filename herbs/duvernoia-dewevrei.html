Duvernoia dewevrei

What's the taxonomical classification of Duvernoia dewevrei?

Duvernoia dewevrei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Lamiales and into the family Acanthaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Duvernoia, and its species name, dewevrei.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Duvernoia dewevrei targets various enzymes and ion channels through its bioactive alkaloids, specifically targeting acetylcholinesterase and nicotinic acetylcholine receptors to exert neurological effects. The plant contains specialized alkaloids that act as potent agonists or antagonists at cholinergic sites within the nervous system. These chemical constituents interact with specific protein binding sites to modulate neurotransmission and signal transduction pathways. By influencing the activity of these protein targets, the plant exhibits significant pharmacological properties related to neuromuscular stimulation. Research continues to investigate how these specific molecular interactions contribute to the plant's broader medicinal and toxicological profile.

    What are the therapeutic actions of Duvernoia dewevrei?

    Duvernoia dewevrei has potent neurotoxic properties due to the presence of jatrorrhizine and other isoquinoline alkaloids that act on the central nervous system. These chemical compounds induce paralysis by interfering with neuromuscular transmission and altering ion channel functions. When consumed, the plant can cause significant respiratory distress and muscle weakness in animals. Such toxicological effects are often utilized in traditional practices involving the preparation of poison for hunting purposes. The physiological impact is characterized by a rapid onset of motor dysfunction.

When applied topically as a poultice, the plant helps reduce the swelling associated with insect bites and small cuts. Some practitioners also use a decoction of the bark to alleviate persistent coughs.

      What are the herbal preparations of Duvernoia dewevrei?

      Duvernoia dewevrei is prepared primarily as an infusion or decoction made from the dried leaves and stems to extract its bioactive compounds. These liquid preparations are often consumed orally to address various traditional medicinal purposes. Some practitioners may also utilize the plant in the form of a topical poultice by crushing the fresh foliage. This method allows for the direct application of the plant's properties to the skin. Additionally, concentrated extracts can be produced through maceration in alcohol or oil for long-term storage.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Duvernoia dewevrei can cause severe neurotoxicity, paralysis, and potentially fatal respiratory failure if ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains potent diterpene quinones that act as powerful neurotoxins. These toxins interfere with the nervous system's ability to signal muscles, leading to progressive weakness. Symptoms often include loss of coordination and a total inability to move limbs. Because of this rapid onset of paralysis, consumption often results in death due to the inability to breathe.
