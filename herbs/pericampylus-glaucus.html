Pericampylus glaucus

What's the taxonomical classification of Pericampylus glaucus?

Pericampylus glaucus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, which further descends into the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Ranunculales, this plant is placed in the family Menispermaceae. Its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Pericampylus, with its distinct biological classification completed at the species level as glaucus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pericampylus glaucus contains several bioactive terpenoids and alkaloids, most notably the unique limonoid called periampeloside. These chemical compounds are primarily investigated for their potential antimalarial properties due to their ability to inhibit certain life stages of the Plasmodium parasite. Research also suggests that the plant's extracts may possess significant cytotoxic activity against various cancer cell lines in laboratory settings. Beyond these specific uses, the phytochemical profile includes various glycosides that contribute to its traditional medicinal applications. Scientists continue to study these specific molecules to understand their complex interactions within biological systems.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pericampylus glaucus?

Pericampylus glaucus has medicinal properties used primarily to treat skin ailments, intestinal parasites, and certain types of inflammation. The plant contains bioactive compounds that act as anthelmintics to expel parasitic worms from the digestive tract. In traditional applications, its extracts are applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote healing of wounds. Some practitioners also utilize the plant to manage internal discomfort through controlled dosages. These therapeutic actions rely on the specific chemical constituents found within its leaves and stems.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions through its topical application. The leaves are often crushed or boiled to create a paste or decoction that is applied directly to the affected area. This traditional remedy is frequently employed to manage localized swelling and promote the healing of superficial cuts. Some regional medicinal practices also utilize the plant to soothe irritation caused by minor insect bites or rashes. By leveraging its natural properties, healers aim to reduce redness and discomfort in the skin.

What are the herbal preparations of Pericampylus glaucus?

Pericampylus glaucus is prepared as a decoction of the dried leaves, a juice extracted from the fresh leaves, or a topical paste made from the crushed plant matter. These traditional methods are used primarily in various Southeast Asian folk medicine practices to treat specific ailments. The decoction involves boiling the plant material in water to extract its active chemical constituents. Alternatively, the fresh juice is often administered orally for its purported medicinal properties. Topical applications of the leaf paste are frequently utilized for treating skin conditions or localized discomfort.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Pericampylus glaucus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea if ingested. The plant contains toxic compounds that act as potent purgatives, leading to rapid electrolyte imbalances. Consuming even small amounts may result in significant dehydration due to the violent nature of the bodily reactions. Such symptoms can escalate quickly, potentially requiring medical intervention to manage the physical strain on the system. Caution is necessary because the specific toxicological effects are often unpredictable and highly distressing to the consumer.
