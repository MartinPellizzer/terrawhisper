Dendrobium moniliforme

What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium moniliforme?

Dendrobium moniliforme belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asparagales. As a member of the diverse family Orchidaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Dendrobium, with its specific designation being the species moniliforme.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium moniliiforme has sympodial pseudobulbs that are cylindrical, segmented, and resemble a string of beads. These fleshy stems are relatively short and grow in clusters from a creeping rhizome. Each segment is typically green and contains a small, fleshy leaf at its nodes. The plant produces small, white to pale lilac flowers that emerge from the upper nodes of the stems. The roots are thick and adventitious, allowing the orchid to cling to its natural substrates in the wild.
