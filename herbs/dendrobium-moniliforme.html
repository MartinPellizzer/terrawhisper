Dendrobium moniliforme

What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium moniliforme?

Dendrobium moniliforme belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asparagales. As a member of the diverse family Orchidaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Dendrobium, with its specific designation being the species moniliforme.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dendrobium moniliforme contains a complex array of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, phenanthrenes, steroids, and various polysaccharides. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's significant pharmacological activities in traditional medicine. The specific alkaloids and phenanthrene derivatives are studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Additionally, the high polysaccharide content is believed to contribute to immune-modulating effects within biological systems. Researchers continue to investigate these specific compounds to better understand their therapeutic applications in modern medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dendrobium moniliforme?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat coughs, phlegm, fever, and inflammatory conditions such as sore throats. It is frequently applied in traditional medicine to alleviate respiratory distress and clear congestion from the lungs. The plant also possesses properties believed to reduce systemic inflammation and lower body temperature during febrile episodes. Some practitioners utilize its extracts to address digestive irregularities and promote overall detoxification. These therapeutic applications are rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical practices within specific regional cultures.
