Saussurea alatipes

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea alatipes has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are characterized by prominent, wing-like margins extending along the midrib. These leaves are typically arranged in a basal rosette and possess a distinctively serrated or dentate edge. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem that supports a dense inflorescence of yellowish or greenish flower heads. Each individual floret is small, but they are clustered together to form a prominent capitulum. The overall architecture of the plant is adapted for survival in high-altitude alpine environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to high-altitude regions of the eastern Himalayas, specifically distributed across parts of Tibet, Bhutan, and northern India. It typically thrives in alpine or subalpine zones where rocky terrain and scree slopes are common. These populations are often found at elevations ranging from 3,500 to 4,800 meters above sea level. The species occupies specific microhabitats that provide the necessary moisture and temperature regulation for its survival. Because it is restricted to these specialized mountain environments, its range is naturally fragmented and localized.
