Matricaria recutita

What's the taxonomical classification of Matricaria recutita?

Matricaria recutiita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further classified under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling into the genus Matricaria, and is identified by its specific species name, recutiita.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Matricaria recutiita has pinnately divided, feathery leaves that are deep green and composed of small, narrow segments. The plant typically grows to a height of 15 to 60 centimeters with slender, branching stems. Its most distinctive feature is the composite flower head, which consists of a central disc of yellow tubular florets surrounded by a ring of white ray florets. These flower heads are supported by long, thin peduncles that emerge from the leaf axils. The overall structure is delicate and herbaceous, characteristic of many members of the Asteraceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia, though it has become widely naturalized across much of the temperate Northern Hemisphere. It is frequently found growing in disturbed soils, roadsides, and agricultural fields throughout North America. Its ability to thrive in diverse climates has allowed it to spread into various regions of Australia and other parts of the world. The species favors open, sunny habitats where it can easily colonize disturbed ground. Because of its resilient nature, it is often distributed globally via human activity and international trade.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Matricaria recutiita contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its dried flower heads. These composite blossoms house essential oils and flavonoids that provide most of the plant's therapeutic properties. While the leaves and stems are present, they are rarely harvested for medicinal purposes due to their lower concentration of active constituents. Practitioners typically use the inflorescences to create infusions, tinctures, or topical applications. The chemical profile of these flower heads is essential for treating digestive issues and inflammatory conditions.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex profile of bioactive constituents including flavonoids, essential oils, apigenin, chamazulene, and alpha-bisabolol. The essential oils serve as a primary source of therapeutic properties, particularly through the presence of chamazulene which provides significant anti-inflammatory effects. Within these oils, alpha-bisabolol acts as a soothing agent that helps reduce skin irritation and gastrointestinal distress. The plant is also rich in flavonoids, a diverse class of compounds known for their antioxidant capabilities. Among these, apigenin stands out as a specific flavonoid that contributes to the sedative and calming effects often associated with chamomile.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Matricaria recutiita targets cyclooxygenase-1 and cyclooxygenase-2 enzymes, as well as various proinflammatory cytokines and neurotransmitter receptors to exert its medicinal effects. Its primary bioactive compounds, such as apigenin and chamazulene, act by inhibiting the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway to reduce inflammation. These phytochemicals also interact with GABA-A receptors in the central nervous system, which contributes to the plant's well-known sedative and anxiolytic properties. Furthermore, certain terpenoids within the plant may modulate tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6 to suppress systemic immune responses. Through these diverse molecular interactions, the plant achieves its characteristic anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and calming therapeutic profile.

What are the therapeutic actions of Matricaria recutita?

Matricaria recutita has diverse therapeutic actions including carminative, nervine, anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, bitter, emmenagogue, diaporetic, and cholagogue properties. As a bitter tonic, it stimulates digestion and functions as a cholagogue to promote bile flow from the liver. Its antispasmodic and carminative effects help soothe gastrointestinal cramping and reduce intestinal gas. The plant also serves as a gentle nervine to calm the nervous system and an anti-inflammatory to reduce swelling. Furthermore, it can act as an emmenagogue to regulate menstrual cycles and a diaphoretic to assist in lowering fevers through perspiration.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic reactions ranging from mild skin irritation to life-threatening anaphylaxis in sensitive individuals. Some people may experience gastrointestinal distress such as nausea and vomiting after consumption. The herb is also known to induce drowsiness and dizziness in certain users. Careful monitoring is necessary for those with known sensitivities to the Asteraceae family.

The main side effects this plant can have are shown in the list below.

  • Allergic reactions (including anaphylaxis)
  • Nausea
  • Drowsiness
  • Skin irritation

  • Vomiting
