Matricaria recutita

What's the taxonomical classification of Matricaria recutita?

Matricaria recutiita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further classified under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling into the genus Matricaria, and is identified by its specific species name, recutiita.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Matricaria recutita contains bioactive compounds such as apigenin, bisabolol, chamazulene, and various flavonoids that provide its therapeutic properties. The essential oil is particularly rich in alpha-bisabolol and chamazulene, which contribute to its potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Flavonoids like apigenin act as mild sedatives by binding to specific receptors in the central nervous system. Additionally, coumarins and phenolic acids within the plant support digestive health and antioxidant functions. These diverse chemical constituents work synergistically to soothe gastrointestinal distress and reduce skin irritation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Matricaria recutita?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive disorders like indigestion and gas, inflammatory skin conditions such as eczema, and various anxiety-related sleep disturbances. Its active compounds help soothe the gastrointestinal tract by reducing spasms and promoting smoother digestion. When applied topically, the chamomile extract serves as an anti-inflammatory agent to calm irritated or sensitive skin. It is also frequently utilized in herbal teas to alleviate mild symptoms of the common cold and seasonal allergies. Additionally, the plant's mild sedative properties assist individuals struggling with restlessness or insomnia.

What are the herbal preparations of Matricaria recutita?

Matricaria recutita is prepared as teas, tinctures, extracts, ointments, and essential oils to utilize its various therapeutic properties. Dried flower heads are commonly steeped in hot water to create infusions used for digestive or sedative purposes. Alcohol-based tinctures provide a concentrated method for long-term storage and precise dosing of the plant's active compounds. Topical applications like creams or salves utilize the plant to soothe skin irritations and inflammation. Additionally, the steam distilled essential oil is frequently used in aromatherapy or diluted for topical use.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Matricaria recutiita can cause allergic reactions including skin rashes, itching, or swelling in individuals sensitive to the Asteraceae family. People allergic to ragweed, daisies, marigolds, or chrysanthemums are at a higher risk for these adverse effects. Some users may experience gastrointestinal upset or nausea when consuming the plant in concentrated forms. While generally considered safe, there is a potential for drug interactions with blood-thinning medications or sedatives. It is important to consult a medical professional before integrating this herb into a regular wellness routine.
