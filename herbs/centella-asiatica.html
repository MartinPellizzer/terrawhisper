Centella Asiatica

Centella asiatica, commonly known as gotu kola, is a traditional herb native to tropical and subtropical regions around the world.

This herb is valued for its potential health benefits, which include improving cognitive function, reducing inflammation, and enhancing wound healing.

The therapeutic actions of Centella asiatica include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and neuroprotective effects, which can contribute to its use in treating conditions such as anxiety, depression, and skin disorders.

Possible Side Effects of centella asiatica

Centella asiatica can have side effects if used improperly, such as inducing allergic reactions, skin irritation, and increased sensitivity to sunlight.

In some cases, excessive consumption of centella asiatica may lead to gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, diarrhea, and stomach pain.

Additionally, individuals with bleeding disorders or taking anticoagulant medications may experience increased bleeding risks due to the herb's potential blood-thinning effects.

Furthermore, pregnant or breastfeeding women may need to avoid centella asiatica due to its potential impact on fetal development and infant health.
