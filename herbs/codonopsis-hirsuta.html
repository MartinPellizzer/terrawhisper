Codonopsis hirsuta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Codonopsis hirsuta has lanceolate leaves that are densely covered in fine, spreading hairs and a single, upright flowering stalk bearing numerous small, tubular flowers. The foliage is characterized by a distinct pubescence that gives the surface a velvety texture. These leaves emerge from a basal rosette and typically exhibit a deep green color. The inflorescence consists of a terminal raceme where the individual blossoms are often white or pale pink. Small, inconspicuous bracts may also be present along the length of the stem.
