Codonopsis hirsuta

What's the taxonomical classification of Codonopsis hirsuta?

Codonopsis hirsuta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls into the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Asterales and the family Campanulaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Codonopsis, with its specific designation being the species hirsuta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Codonopsis hirsuta has lanceolate leaves that are densely covered in fine, spreading hairs and a single, upright flowering stalk bearing numerous small, tubular flowers.

The foliage is characterized by a distinct pubescence that gives the surface a velvety texture. These leaves emerge from a basal rosette and typically exhibit a deep green color. The inflorescence consists of a terminal raceme where the individual blossoms are often white or pale pink.

Small, inconspicuous bracts may also be present along the length of the stem.
