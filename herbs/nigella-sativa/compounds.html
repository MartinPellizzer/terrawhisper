Black Cumin (Nigella sativa) Compounds

The primary compounds of Black Cumin include essential oil, which contains thymoquinone, a key bioactive component. Phenolic acids and flavonoids contribute to its antioxidant properties. Coumarin and terpenoids offer anti-inflammatory effects, while glycosides and saponins support digestive health. Alkaloids and anthocyanins also play roles in its medicinal benefits.

Black Cumin (Nigella sativa) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Black Cumin (Nigella sativa) is a key active compound containing over 100 bioactive components, including thymoquinone. It exhibits potent antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating various health conditions.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Black Cumin (Nigella sativa). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory conditions. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Black Cumin in herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Black Cumin (Nigella sativa). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds may support immune function and reduce oxidative stress. Flavonoids are part of the complex profile of active constituents in Black Cumin.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Black Cumin (Nigella sativa). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory and digestive conditions. Coumarin exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Black Cumin (Nigella sativa). These compounds contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They play a key role in the medicinal applications of Black Cumin, supporting its use in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Black Cumin (Nigella sativa). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may aid in reducing inflammation and supporting gut health. Tannins in Black Cumin are also believed to enhance the absorption of other active constituents, potentially increasing the plant's therapeutic effects.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Black Cumin (Nigella sativa). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting immune function and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in Black Cumin are known for their potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Black Cumin (Nigella sativa). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin may enhance the absorption of other bioactive compounds in the plant. Its presence supports the overall therapeutic potential of Black Cumin in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

The alkaloid thymoquinone is a key active compound in Black Cumin (Nigella sativa). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, and is responsible for many of its traditional medicinal uses.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Black Cumin (Nigella sativa). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting health and treating inflammatory conditions.
