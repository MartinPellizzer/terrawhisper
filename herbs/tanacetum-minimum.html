Tansy (Tanacetum Minimum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Tanacetum minimum.

Tansy, scientifically known as Tanacetum minimum, is a medicinal herb prized for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine. It is commonly recognized as an adaptogen, helping the body manage stress and promote balance, while also serving as a spice with a distinctive, slightly bitter, and camphor-like flavor. Traditionally, tansy has been used in herbal remedies to treat digestive issues, fevers, and respiratory ailments, and it played a role in folklore as a protective charm against evil spirits. In modern wellness practices, tansy is utilized in aromatherapy and as a natural remedy for skin conditions and insect repellent due to its potent essential oils. One of its unique features is the presence of thujone, a rare active compound with potential therapeutic effects, though its use must be cautious due to its toxicity in high concentrations.
