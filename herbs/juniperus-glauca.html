Juniperus glauca

Juniperus glauca, commonly known as the Japanese red fir or Sakhalin fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree species native to the Russian Far East and northeastern Japan.

The therapeutic actions of Juniperus glauca include its ability to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, as well as its use in treating digestive problems and skin conditions.
