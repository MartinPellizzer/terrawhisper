Odontiites vulgaris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Odontites vulgaris has a taproot system with basal leaves that are lyrate-pinnatifid and cauline leaves that are smaller and lanceolate. The stem is typically erect and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are bright yellow, characterized by a central disc and outer ray florets, arranged in terminal heads. The involucral bracts are linear and arranged in several rows surrounding the flower head. The fruit consists of small, cylindrical achenes topped with a pappus of fine bristles.
