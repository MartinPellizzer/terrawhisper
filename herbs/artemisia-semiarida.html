Artemisia semiarida

Artemisia semiarida, also known as Russian tarragon or semi-arid wormwood, is a plant species in the sunflower family native to Russia and other parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a sweet, anise-like flavor and aroma, and has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The health benefits of Artemisia semiarida include its ability to aid digestion, relieve menstrual cramps, and soothe respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma.

Artemisia semiarida can be prepared as a tea, tincture, or essential oil, and is also used as a flavoring agent in various culinary applications.
