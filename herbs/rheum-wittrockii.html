Rheum wittrockii

What's the taxonomical classification of Rheum wittrockii?

Rheum wittrockii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a representative of the family Polygonaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Rheum, with its unique designation being the species wittrockii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rheum wittrockii has thick, fleshy rhizomes and large, basal leaves characterized by broad, ovate shapes and prominent, deeply impressed venation. The leaf margins are often slightly wavy and can possess small, blunt teeth. From the center of these leaves, sturdy, upright flowering stalks emerge that reach significant heights. The inflorescence consists of dense, branched panicles covered in small, inconspicuous flowers. These floral structures are typically greenish or reddish in hue depending on the specific stage of development.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau, specifically localized within the mountains of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau. It primarily inhabits alpine zones where rocky crevices and scree slopes provide suitable substrates for growth. The species is typically found at elevations ranging from 3,500 to 4,500 meters above sea level. Its distribution is restricted by the extreme cold and harsh environmental conditions characteristic of these montane ecosystems. Because of this narrow range, the plant exists in fragmented populations across specific mountain ranges.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Rheum wittrockii contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its rhizomes and roots. These underground structures are harvested to extract anthraquinone glycosides which serve as the active pharmaceutical agents. The concentrated extracts from these parts are traditionally utilized for their potent laxative properties to treat constipation. Beyond digestive aid, the chemical constituents found in the roots may also exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds epipinoresinol, isovaniillic acid, and protocatechuic acid. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's complex pharmacological profile. Epipinoresinol belongs to the lignan class and is often studied for its potential antioxidant properties. Both isovaniillic acid and protocatechuic acid are phenolic acids known for their ability to mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Together, these substances represent the primary bioactive elements responsible for the plant's therapeutic characteristics.

As discussed in a study, the roots of Rheum wittrockii serve as a source of phenolic compounds, specifically through its chloroform fraction (FG2). This FG2 fraction contains a Total Phenolic Content (TPC) of 12.5 mg/g GAE and demonstrated strong antioxidant activities of 85±0.22% at a dose of 100 mg/mL with an IC50 of 6.25 mg/mL. It also exhibited alpha-amylase inhibition activities with an IC50 value of 432 µg/mL, whereas the standard acarbosose had an IC50 of 125 µg/mL. The FG2 fraction was used to isolate three known phenolic compounds for the first time from this plant: protocatecutic acid (1), isovaniillic acid (2), and epipinoresinol (3). These isolated compounds contribute to the potent antioxidant, anti-diabetic, and antibacterial effects against MRSA observed in the plant.
