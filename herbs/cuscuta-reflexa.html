What to know about Cuscuta reflexa (dodder) before using it medicinally

Dodder, scientifically known as Cuscuta reflexa, is a plant that has been revered for its numerous health benefits, from treating respiratory issues and digestive problems to reducing inflammation and improving circulation.

In the culinary world, dodder is often used in traditional Chinese medicine to prepare soups and teas, valued for its sweet and slightly bitter flavor. Horticulturists admire dodder for its unique ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a valuable addition to any garden or landscape. From a botanical perspective, dodder is classified as an annual parasite, using its tendrils to wrap around other plants and absorb their nutrients.

Throughout history, dodder has been mentioned in ancient Chinese texts and has been used by indigenous cultures for centuries, earning its reputation as a highly valued and versatile plant.

What are the side effects of Cuscuta reflexa when used improperly?

When used improperly, Cuscuta reflexa increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as gastrointestinal disturbances, allergic reactions, and interactions with other medications.

In rare cases, improper use of Cuscuta reflexa has been linked to more severe side effects, such as anaphylaxis, a life-threatening allergic reaction that requires immediate medical attention.

It is essential to consult with a qualified healthcare professional and follow recommended dosages and preparation methods to minimize the risk of adverse reactions when using Cuscuta reflexa for medicinal purposes.
