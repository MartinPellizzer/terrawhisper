Crocus Sativus

Crocus sativus, commonly known as the saffron crocus, has been valued for its medicinal properties for centuries.

The dried stigmas of the flower are used in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including depression, inflammation, and digestive disorders. Saffron contains bioactive compounds such as crocin, croetin, and safranal, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. Modern research suggests that saffron may have neuroprotective properties and could aid in the treatment of conditions like Alzheimer's disease and chemotherapy-induced nausea.

Due to its potent bioactive components, Crocus sativus continues to be a subject of scientific study for its potential in pharmaceutical and nutraceutical applications.
