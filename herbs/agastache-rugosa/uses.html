Agastache (Agastache rugosa) Uses

Agastache rugosa is commonly used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, sinusitis, and bronchitis. It helps alleviate cold symptoms, sore throat, and allergic rhinitis. Its antimicrobial properties support recovery from tonsillitis and upper respiratory infections. It may also assist in managing asthma symptoms due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Agastache (Agastache rugosa) Monograph

1. Cough

Agastache rugosa is used to treat coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections and bronchitis. Its warming effects also support overall respiratory health.

2. Sinusitis

Agastache rugosa is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and clear sinus infections by promoting mucus drainage and supporting immune function. Often prepared as a decoction or essential oil, it is valued for its effectiveness in alleviating symptoms associated with chronic and acute sinus inflammation.

3. Respiratory tract infections

Agastache rugosa is used to treat respiratory tract infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing, sore throat, and congestion. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture to support immune function and reduce infection severity. Its essential oils contain compounds that target pathogens in the respiratory system.

4. Cold symptoms

Agastache rugosa is used to relieve cold symptoms such as coughing, sore throat, and nasal congestion. Its antiviral and expectorant properties help reduce mucus and soothe respiratory discomfort. It is often brewed as a tea to support the immune system during colds.

5. Sore throat

Agastache rugosa is used to soothe sore throats due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce throat irritation and inflammation, making it a common remedy in traditional herbal medicine. Often prepared as a tea, it supports respiratory health and can alleviate symptoms of coughs and colds.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Agastache rugosa is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties help reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to support respiratory health during seasonal allergies.

7. Tonsillitis

Agastache rugosa is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat tonsillitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce throat inflammation and soothe pain associated with bacterial or viral infections. The herb is often prepared as a decoction or tea to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms.

8. Bronchitis

Agastache rugosa is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat bronchitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate coughing and clear mucus from the respiratory tract. The herb is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effects on respiratory health. It is typically prepared as a tea or decoction.

9. Asthma

Agastache rugosa is used in traditional Chinese medicine to help alleviate asthma symptoms by promoting respiratory function and reducing phlegm. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its expectorant and bronchodilating effects, supporting clearer breathing and easier airway passage.

10. Upper respiratory infections

Agastache rugosa is used to support the treatment of upper respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like sore throat and cough by reducing mucus production and soothing irritated tissues. Often used in herbal formulations, it is valued for its ability to enhance immune response and shorten recovery time.
