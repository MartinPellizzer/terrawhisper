10 Possible Side Effects Of Agastache Rugosa

Agastache rugosa has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can occur due to overconsumption, sensitivity to the plant's compounds, or cross-reactivity with other allergies.

For instance, severe allergic reactions can cause anaphylaxis, respiratory distress, and life-threatening conditions, while gastrointestinal issues can lead to nausea, diarrhea, and electrolyte imbalances, hindering daily activities and overall well-being.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Agastache rugosa causes allergic reactions due to its potent essential oils and compounds, such as rosmarinic acid and eugenol.

These chemical constituents can stimulate the immune system, triggering an inflammatory response in some individuals.

As a result, users may experience symptoms like skin irritation, itching, redness, or respiratory issues, including congestion and difficulty breathing.

2. Triggers digestive issues

Agastache rugosa triggers digestive issues when consumed, likely due to its high content of volatile oils such as thujone and camphor.

These compounds can irritate the stomach lining and cause symptoms like bloating, gas, and stomach discomfort.

Additionally, Agastache rugosa's bitter taste may stimulate an overproduction of gastric acid, further contributing to digestive problems.

3. Induces respiratory problems

Agastache rugosa induces respiratory problems due to its potent essential oils and volatile compounds.

Specifically, the high concentration of thymol and camphor in the plant's leaves and stems can cause bronchial constriction and irritation, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

As a result, individuals may experience respiratory difficulties when inhaling or ingesting Agastache rugosa products, including teas, infusions, and essential oils.

4. Increases heart rate

Agastache rugosa increases heart rate due to its stimulating effects on the cardiovascular system.

The herb contains compounds that can cause a rise in blood pressure and cardiac output, leading to an increase in heart rate. This is thought to be related to the presence of essential oils and flavonoids, which can stimulate the nervous system and alter physiological responses.

As a result, individuals may experience palpitations or tachycardia when using Agastache rugosa.

5. Lowers blood pressure

Agastache rugosa lowers blood pressure by stimulating the production of nitric oxide in the body.

This relaxation response helps to dilate blood vessels, reducing blood pressure and promoting a sense of calmness.

The herb's active compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, work synergistically to enhance this effect, contributing to its potential benefits for cardiovascular health.

6. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Agastache rugosa disrupts menstrual cycles due to its estrogenic and antifertility properties.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, may interact with the body's hormonal balance, leading to irregular or absent menstruation.

Additionally, Agastache rugosa's ability to stimulate uterine contractions may contribute to changes in menstrual patterns.

7. Stimulates anxiety attacks

Agastache rugosa stimulates anxiety attacks by containing a high concentration of essential oils that can affect the central nervous system.

The menthol and camphor present in its leaves and flowers may cause an individual's heart rate to increase and their senses to become heightened, leading to feelings of panic and unease.

Additionally, the plant's ability to alter brain chemistry through its volatile compounds may disrupt emotional balance, exacerbating anxiety symptoms.

8. Aggravates insomnia

Agastache rugosa aggravates insomnia due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase alertness.

The essential oils present in this herb, such as camphor and thujone, can interact with the brain's chemistry, leading to an increased sense of wakefulness and difficulty falling asleep.

Additionally, the plant's bitter compounds can affect digestion, causing restlessness and anxiety that may also contribute to insomnia.

9. Reduces appetite significantly

Agastache rugosa reduces appetite significantly, likely due to its ability to stimulate digestive processes and increase saliva production.

This may lead to a temporary decrease in hunger sensation, as the herb's compounds work to enhance gastric secretions and promote gut motility.

As the body adapts to the herbal influence, appetite levels may gradually return to normal, but this initial reduction in hunger can be an expected side effect of using Agastache rugosa.

10. Causes stomach ulcers

Agastache rugosa causes stomach ulcers due to its irritating and drying properties.

The essential oils present in the plant can irritate the mucous membranes of the digestive tract, leading to inflammation and ulceration.

Additionally, Agastache rugosa contains a compound called thymol, which has been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties that can exacerbate stomach ulcers.
