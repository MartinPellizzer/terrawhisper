Botanical Identification of Agastache (Agastache rugosa)

To identify Agastache (Agastache rugosa) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Agastache (Agastache rugosa) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Agastache rugosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Agastache, and species rugosa. These classifications place it within the mint family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Agastache rugosa is the scientific name for the species, with the author citation given as (L.) R.Br. It is also known by several synonyms, including Anisodus rugosus and Hyptis rugosa. Common names include American hyssop, wild hyssop, and bee balm, while regional and historical names vary across different cultures and periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Agastache rugosa is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system. It has upright, branching stems that are square in cross-section. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and aromatic. The plant produces dense spikes of lavender to pinkish-purple flowers. The fruit is a dry capsule containing small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Agastache rugosa can be distinguished by its square, hairy stems, gray-green leaves with serrated edges, and spikes of purple-lavender flowers. It differs from similar species like Agastache foeniculum by its more compact growth habit and the presence of a strong, minty aroma. The leaves are also narrower and more pointed compared to other Agastache species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Agastache rugosa is native to East Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Agastache rugosa blooms from late summer to early fall, typically between July and September. Seed formation occurs in late fall, around October to November. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during the flowering period to ensure accurate recognition of the plant's key characteristics.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Agastache rugosa is closely related to species like Hyssopus and Monarda. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics such as square stems and aromatic leaves. Its phylogenetic position reflects evolutionary ties to other mint family members, aiding in its identification and understanding within herbal medicine.
