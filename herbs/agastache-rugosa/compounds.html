Agastache (Agastache rugosa) Compounds

Agastache rugosa contains essential oil, which gives it its aromatic properties. Flavonoids and terpenoids contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids and glycosides support digestive health. Coumarin, tannins, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further enhance its medicinal profile.

Agastache (Agastache rugosa) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Agastache rugosa contains aromatic compounds like monoterpenes and sesquiterpenes. These oils are used in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. They support respiratory health and are commonly used in aromatherapy and traditional remedies.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Agastache rugosa, a traditional medicinal plant used in herbal medicine. These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its use in respiratory and digestive health. They also enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal formulations.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are key active compounds in Agastache rugosa, contributing to its aromatic properties and medicinal effects. They exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities, supporting the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive health.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Agastache rugosa, contributing to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps support respiratory health and may aid in digestive wellness. Found in high concentrations in the plant's essential oils and leaves, phenolic acid plays a crucial role in the therapeutic effects of Agastache rugosa.

5. Glycoside

Glycosides in Agastache rugosa are bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds are known for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in respiratory and digestive health. They play a key role in the therapeutic applications of Agastache rugosa in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Agastache rugosa. It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin may support digestive health and help alleviate mild nervous system tension. It is commonly found in the essential oils of this herb.

7. Tannin

Tannin in Agastache rugosa contributes to its astringent properties and supports digestive health. It helps reduce inflammation and may aid in managing symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders. Tannins also play a role in preserving the plant's medicinal qualities.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Agastache rugosa. It contributes to the plant's expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, aiding in respiratory health. Saponins also support immune function and may help in managing digestive issues. Their presence enhances the therapeutic value of Agastache rugosa in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Agastache rugosa contains the alkaloid scopolamine, which has anticholinergic properties. This compound may help reduce symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders by affecting nerve signal transmission. Scopolamine is also used in treating motion sickness and as a sedative. Its presence in Agastache rugosa supports its traditional use in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Agastache rugosa, contributing to its vibrant color and antioxidant properties. Found in the flowers and leaves, it supports respiratory health and may help reduce inflammation. This compound is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
