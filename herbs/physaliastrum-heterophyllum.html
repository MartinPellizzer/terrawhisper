Physaliastrum heterophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Physaliastrum heterophyllum?

Physaliastrum heterophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Solanales and falls within the family Solanaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific genus, Physaliastrum, and its unique species designation, heterophyllum.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Physaliastrum heterophyllum can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and allergic dermatitis upon direct contact with its sap or foliage. The chemical compounds within the plant often trigger redness and an intense itching sensation in sensitive individuals. If the juices are accidentally ingested, they may lead to gastrointestinal distress including nausea or stomach cramping. Prolonged exposure to the plant's toxins can result in more persistent inflammatory responses on the dermal layer. It is essential to handle this species with gloves to avoid these physiological reactions.
