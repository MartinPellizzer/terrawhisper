Thladiantha dentata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Cucurbitales and the family Cucurbitaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Thladiantha, and its species name, dentata.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Thladiantha dentata can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled without protection. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may result in localized dermatitis or itchy rashes on sensitive skin areas. If swallowed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping due to the plant's chemical composition. Some people may also exhibit hypersensitivity symptoms like swelling or redness upon exposure. Monitoring for these adverse reactions is essential when working near this species.
