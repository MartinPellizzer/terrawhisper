Dipteris conjugata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dipteris conjugata has pinnately compound leaves consisting of 5 to 15 pairs of oblong to lanceolate leaflets that are arranged oppositely along the rachis. The leaflets typically measure between 2 and 10 centimeters in length and feature entire or slightly serrated margins. This perennial herb grows from a woody rhizome and can reach a height of approximately 30 to 60 centimeters. During the flowering season, small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers emerge from the leaf axils in drooping racemes. The plant's overall structure is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and slender, upright stems.
