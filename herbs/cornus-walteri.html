Cornus walteri

What's the taxonomical classification of Cornus walteri?

Cornus walteri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Cornales. As a member of the family Cornaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Cornus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species walteri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cornus walteri has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, featuring prominent pinnate venation and entire to slightly serrated margins.

The plant typically exhibits a multi-stemmed shrubby growth habit with smooth, grayish bark. Its inflorescences consist of small, white, four-petaled flowers clustered in dense cymes. Following flowering, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark color upon maturity.

These botanical features distinguish it from other species within the dogwood family.
