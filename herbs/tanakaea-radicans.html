What's the taxonomical classification of Tanakaea radicans?

Tanakaea radicans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Saxifragales and into the family Saxifragaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Tanakaea, and its unique species designation, radicans.

      Tanakaea radicans can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Some people may also experience localized rashes or itching if the plant comes into direct contact with the skin. Because the specific toxicity profile is not extensively documented in medical literature, caution is advised when handling any part of the species. Monitoring for unexpected systemic responses is essential for anyone exposed to this plant.
