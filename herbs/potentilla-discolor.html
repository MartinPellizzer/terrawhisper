Potentilla discolor

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella discolor has a perennial growth habit characterized by a creeping rhizome and leaves that are distinctly bicolor, featuring dark green upper surfaces and densely felted, brownish-white undersides. The foliage consists of trifoliate or palmately lobed leaves with serrated margins and a hairy texture. Its small, yellow flowers emerge on slender stalks and typically possess five petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens and pistils. The plant often forms low-growing mats or clumps in its natural alpine or subalpine habitats. These morphological traits, particularly the contrasting leaf colors, serve as key identifiers for the species.
