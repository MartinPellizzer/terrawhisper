Potentilla discolor

What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla discolor?

Potentiella discolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Potentiella, and its unique species designation, discolor.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella discolor has a perennial growth habit characterized by a creeping rhizome and leaves that are distinctly bicolor, featuring dark green upper surfaces and densely felted, brownish-white undersides.

The foliage consists of trifoliate or palmately lobed leaves with serrated margins and a hairy texture. Its small, yellow flowers emerge on slender stalks and typically possess five petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens and pistils. The plant often forms low-growing mats or clumps in its natural alpine or subalpine habitats.

These morphological traits, particularly the contrasting leaf colors, serve as key identifiers for the species.
