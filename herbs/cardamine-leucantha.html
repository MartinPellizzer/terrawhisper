Cardamine leucantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cardamine leucantha has pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of elliptical or lanceolate leaflets and white, four-petaled flowers arranged in terminal racemes. The plant typically features slender, upright stems that support these delicate floral clusters. Each individual flower possesses a distinct cruciform shape characteristic of the Brassicaceae family. The leaves are often arranged alternately along the stem to maximize light absorption. Small, rounded seed pods develop following pollination to ensure the next generation of growth.
