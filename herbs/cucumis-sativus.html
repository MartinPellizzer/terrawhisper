Cucumber

Cucumis sativus

Cucumber (Cucumis sativus) is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, commonly used in traditional practices for its cooling and detoxifying properties. The plant parts, primarily the fruit, are utilized in various cultural and regional contexts for digestive aid and skin care.

Botanical Identification

Cucumber is scientifically known as Cucumis sativus, belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family. It is an annual vine with elongated, edible fruits. Common names include "kheer" in India and "agri" in parts of Africa. Native to South Asia, it has palmate leaves and yellow flowers. Synonyms in herbal texts include "kharaj" and "sura."

Cucumber Identification.

Active Compounds

Cucumber contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarins. These compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their synergistic interaction enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Phenolic acid
  • Coumarin

Preparation Methods

Culinary use: Used for digestive issues and as a diuretic.

Infusion: Used for reducing inflammation and treating skin conditions.

Poultice: Used for treating skin infections and reducing swelling.

Safety Profile

Cucumber is generally safe when consumed in normal food amounts. However, excessive consumption may cause digestive issues. It is contraindicated in individuals with kidney disorders due to its high water content and potential diuretic effects. Always consult a healthcare provider for personalized advice.
