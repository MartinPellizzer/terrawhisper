Cucumber (Cucumis sativus)

Cucumber (Cucumis sativus) is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, native to Southern Asia, Middle East, and North Africa. Traditionally, its fruits, seeds, and leaves have been used for culinary uses, infusions, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Annual herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Sessile, broad, ovate leaves with upper surface a deep green and lower surface a lighter green, possessing prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are yellow with five fused sepals and five petals arranged in a spiral, actinomorphic, with a superior ovary and five stamens.
Stems
Elongated, cylindrical, herbaceous stems with ascending growth habit, opposite branching, and pubescent surface.
