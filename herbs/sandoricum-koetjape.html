Sandoricum koetjape

What's the taxonomical classification of Sandoricum koetjape?

Sandoricum koetjape belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Sapindales and the family Meliaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Sandoricum, and its unique species designation, koetjape.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Sandoricum koetjape contains bioactive compounds such as santolides, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins primarily located in its fruit pulp and bark. These specific chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Research indicates that the presence of these phytochemicals can help mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Some studies also suggest that the extracts may possess anti-inflammatory and hypoglycemic effects. Consequently, these medicinal compounds make the plant a subject of interest for developing natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Sandoricum koetjape?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat fever, malaria, skin infections, and various types of stomach ailments like diarrhea or dysentery. The fruit and bark contain bioactive compounds that demonstrate significant antimicrobial and antipyretic properties. In traditional medicine, extracts from the leaves are often applied topically to soothe inflammation or manage wound healing. Some cultures also utilize the seeds or fruit pulp to alleviate digestive discomfort and intestinal parasites. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's diverse phytochemical profile to combat different pathogens.

What are the herbal preparations of Sandoricum koetjape?

Sandoricum koetjape is prepared as decoctions, infusions, and topical pastes using various parts of the plant such as the leaves, bark, and fruit pulp. The leaves are frequently boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for treating fever or skin ailments. Bark extracts are often decocted to manage inflammatory conditions or digestive issues. For localized skin treatments, the fleshy fruit or crushed leaves can be applied directly as a poultice. These diverse preparations allow different bioactive compounds to be extracted for specific therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Sandoricum koetjape can cause allergic skin reactions, gastrointestinal discomfort, or potential toxicity if the seeds or bark are consumed in excessive quantities. While the fruit pulp is generally considered safe for consumption, some individuals may experience localized itching or rashes upon contact with the sap. The seeds contain various bioactive compounds that have not been extensively studied for long-term human safety in large doses. There is also a lack of comprehensive clinical data regarding how the plant interacts with specific medications or its effects on pregnant and breastfeeding women. Therefore, caution is advised when using any part of the plant for medicinal purposes without professional guidance.
