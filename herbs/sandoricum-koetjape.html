Sandoricum koetjape belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Sapindales and the family Meliaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Sandoricum, and its unique species designation, koetjape.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sandoricum koetjape has evergreen leaves that are oblong or elliptical in shape, measuring between 10 to 25 centimeters in length, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a paler underside. The tree typically grows to a height of 15 to 25 meters and features a straight trunk with smooth, greyish bark. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters from the axils of the leaves or branches. The fruit is a globose drupe that transitions from green to a bright yellow or orange color when mature. Inside the fleshy pulp, the plant contains several large, woody seeds that are characteristic of the Meliaceae family.

What are the pharmacological activities of Sandoricum koetjape?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, anticancer, antioxidant, and cytotoxic pharmacological activities derived from its diverse phytochemical constituents. The presence of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and tannins contributes significantly to its ability to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. These combined therapeutic properties make Sandoricum koetjape a valuable subject for pharmacological investigation and drug development.

As discussed in a study, Sandoricum koetjape Merr. contains about 30 natural products of interest, including flavonoids, limonoids, and terpenoids. The plant possesses limonoids like sandoripins A-B, sanjecumins A-B, and sandrapins A-E, while flavonoids such as sandoricin and sandoripin derivatives provide antioxidant, antibacterial, and insecticidal properties. The seeds contain insecticidal limonoids, and the plant is used traditionally for inflammatory disorders and digestive troubles. A key triterpene, koetjapic acid (KA), serves as the main product and offers anti-inflammatory and anticancer properties, including antimetasatatic and antiangiogenic effects. The water-soluble derivative potassium koetjapate (KK) is an orally bioavailable option with superior antiangiogenic properties compared to KA.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 3-oxo-olean-12-en-29-oic acid, Koetjapic acid, katonic acid, quercetin, and tannin. The presence of these triterpenoids and organic acids often provides significant biological activities. Additionally, the flavonoids and polyphenols work alongside these compounds to offer antioxidant benefits. Together, these elements form a complex chemical makeup used in various medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, Sandoricum koetjape stems contain a cytotoxic Et2O-soluble extract consisting of a new ring-A secotriterpene called koetjapic acid [1], five known compounds including 3-oxo-olean-12-en-29-oic acid [2], katonic acid [3], (-)-alloaromadenrener, (-)-caryophyllene oxide, and (+)-spathulenol. Among these, compounds 2 and 3 demonstrated significant cytotoxic activity against cultured P-388 cells with ED50 values of 0.61 and 0.11 microgram/ml, respectively. While significant cytotoxicity was observed with a variety of cultured human cancer cells, these effects were less intense. Aside from compounds 2 and 3, these substances were not toxic with cultured cells. The 13C-nmr chemical shifts of these triterpene substances were assigned unambiguously using selective INEPT nmr experiments.
