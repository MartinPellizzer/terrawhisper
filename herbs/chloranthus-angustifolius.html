Chloranthus angustifolius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chloranthus angustifolius has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are oppositely arranged along a woody stem and possess entire margins. The foliage typically exhibits a smooth texture with prominent pinnate venation visible on the leaf surface. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that lack petals and are instead characterized by prominent tepals. The plant develops into a perennial shrub or small tree with a branching habit that supports its dense canopy. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its specific botanical family and habitat.
