Spearmint (Mentha punctata)

Spearmint (Mentha punctata) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Asia, Australia, and Pacific Islands. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its carminative, antispasmodic, and expectorant actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Carminative

Expectorant
