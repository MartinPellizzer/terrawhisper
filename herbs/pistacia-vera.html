Pistacia vera

What's the taxonomical classification of Pistacia vera?

Pistacia vera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Sapindales and the family Anacardiaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by the genus Pistacia and is specifically designated by the species name vera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pistacia vera has a deciduous, spreading tree structure characterized by pinnately compound leaves that typically contain 14 to 30 small, lanceolate leaflets. The bark of the mature tree is thick and develops deep longitudinal fissures as it ages. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in panicles, which eventually develop into drupe-like fruits known as pistachios. These fruits transition from green to a reddish or yellowish hue when they reach maturity. The woody branches are relatively sturdy and can support a large, rounded canopy.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, the Middle East, and the Mediterranean basin. It thrives in areas with long, hot summers and cold winters, particularly in countries like Iran, Turkey, and the United States. In the United States, cultivation is most prominent in the Central Valley of California due to the suitable climate. Beyond its natural range, it has been introduced to many other Mediterranean-style climates around the world. Its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil quality allows it to occupy specific ecological niches within these diverse geographic zones.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Pistacia vera contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its resin, seeds, and bark. The resin, often referred to as mastic when sourced from related species, is utilized for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The seeds or kernels are frequently used in traditional medicine to support digestive health and manage lipid profiles. Additionally, extracts from the plant's bark may be applied to treat skin conditions due to their antioxidant activity. These various components work together to provide a range of therapeutic benefits in herbal applications.

What are the pharmacological activities of Pistacia vera?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-cancer, anti-tumor, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective properties. Its rich profile of bioactive compounds allows it to effectively neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within the body. These antioxidant qualities contribute significantly to its ability to protect liver cells from chemical damage and toxicity. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates potential in inhibiting the growth and spread of malignant cells through its anti-tumor and anti-cancer mechanisms. By modulating immune responses, it also serves as a potent anti-inflammatory agent to combat various systemic ailments.

As discussed in a study, pistachio pericarp extract (PPE) and pistachio kernel extract (PKE) exhibit anti-tumor activity against PC-3 prostate cancer cells. These extracts show a greater anti-proliferative effect when combined with cisplatin compared to using PPE or PKE alone. The IC50 values for PKE+CP were 4.141, 2.140, and 0.884 ug/mL after 24 h, 48 h, and 72 h of treatment, respectively. For PPE+CP, the IC50 values were 2.754, 2.061, and 0.753 ug/mL at the same 24 h, 48 h, and 72 h intervals. The extracts work by modulating gene expression, specifically increasing mRNA expression of BAX and P53 while decreasing BCL-2, KLK2, TNF, TGF, and NANOG.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds resveratrol, catechin hydrate, flavonoid, gallic acid, and phenol. These bioactive constituents contribute to the complex chemical profile of the species. Resveratrol and gallic acid are particularly noted for their potential antioxidant activities within the body. The presence of flavonoid and phenol groups helps protect cells from oxidative stress. Furthermore, catechin hydrate plays a significant role in the plant's overall therapeutic properties.

As discussed in a study, Pistacia vera contains the naturally occurring nutraceutical compound known as Resveratrol. This specific compound is found in pistachio along with grape and peanut. Resveratrol is noted for having several favorable biological and therapeutic activities. These include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antitumor, hepatoprotective, cardioprotective, and antidiabetic effects. The study demonstrates how this compound modulates the Wnt signaling pathway to exert its pharmacological effects.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the management of cancer, inflammation, oxidative stress, skin pigmentation disorders, and peptic ulcer through its bioactive compounds. The high concentration of antioxidants within the seeds helps neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing cellular damage caused by oxidative stress. These properties also play a significant role in suppressing systemic inflammation and protecting against the development of various types of cancer. Furthermore, the plant's therapeutic potential extends to treating skin pigmentation disorders by regulating melanin production. Research also suggests that its protective qualities can aid in the healing and prevention of peptic ulcer complications.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic reactions in sensitive individuals and gastrointestinal issues such as nausea, diarrhea, abdominal pain, bloating, and gas. Some people may experience skin rashes or respiratory distress if they are allergic to the proteins found in the seeds. Digestive discomfort often manifests as cramping or a feeling of excessive fullness after consumption. These symptoms typically arise when the plant is ingested in large quantities or by those with specific food intolerances. Monitoring your physical response is essential when incorporating this plant into your diet.
