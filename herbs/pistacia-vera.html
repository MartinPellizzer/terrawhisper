Pistachio (Pistacia vera)

Pistachio (Pistacia vera) is a member of the Anacardiaceae family, native to Middle East, Southern Europe, and North Africa. Traditionally, its fruits, resin, and bark have been used for infusions, powders, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Leaves
Simple leaves with upper surface dark green and lower surface lighter green, exhibiting prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescences are spike-like clusters with actinomorphic flowers having five white to yellow petals and five sepals, each bearing a prominent glandular appendage at the base.
Stems
Woody, erect, simple branching, glabrous surface, angular cross-section, persistent buds.
