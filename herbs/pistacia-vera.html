Pistacia vera

What's the taxonomical classification of Pistacia vera?

Pistacia vera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Sapindales and the family Anacardiaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by the genus Pistacia and is specifically designated by the species name vera.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pistacia vera contains bioactive compounds including phenolic acids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and various carotenoids that provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These phytochemicals work together to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. Specific compounds such as quercetin and kaempferin contribute to its ability to protect cells from damage. Additionally, the presence of fatty acids and sterols supports cardiovascular health and metabolic stability. Research suggests these medicinal components may also play a role in regulating blood glucose levels.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pistacia vera?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea, dysentery, and stomach cramps through its astringent properties. The seeds and resin contain bioactive compounds that help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. Some traditional practices also utilize extracts to manage skin conditions and minor wounds due to antimicrobial effects. Research suggests that the antioxidants in the plant may also assist in combating oxidative stress related to various systemic ailments. These therapeutic applications rely heavily on the specific chemical constituents found within the kernels and bark.
