Olgaea leucophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Olgaea leucophylla?

Olgaea leucophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Asterales. It is further categorized under the family Asteraceae, falling into the genus Olgaea, and is specifically identified by the species name leucophylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Olgaea leucophylla has small, lanceolate leaves characterized by a distinct white or silvery pubescence on their surfaces that gives the plant its specific name. These leaves are arranged oppositely along thin, wiry stems that allow the plant to grow in compact, low-lying clusters. The foliage often exhibits a pale, glaucous appearance due to the dense covering of fine hairs. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during its blooming period.
