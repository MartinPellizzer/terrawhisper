Olgaea leucophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Olgaea leucophylla has small, lanceolate leaves characterized by a distinct white or silvery pubescence on their surfaces that gives the plant its specific name. These leaves are arranged oppositely along thin, wiry stems that allow the plant to grow in compact, low-lying clusters. The foliage often exhibits a pale, glaucous appearance due to the dense covering of fine hairs. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during its blooming period. This specialized leaf morphology serves as an adaptation to reflect intense light and conserve moisture in its native habitat.
