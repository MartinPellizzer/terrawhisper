European ash

Fraxinus excelsior

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Fraxinus excelsior, commonly known as European ash, belongs to the Oleaceae family. It is a perennial tree native to Europe and western Asia. The plant has opposite, compound leaves and produces small, greenish flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include white ash and common ash.

Active Compounds

The European Ash contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Coumarin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Bronchitis, respiratory infections, cough Antimicrobial, expectorant, anti-inflammatory, antihistaminic
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, skin irritation Antimicrobial, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antipruritic
Immune Inflammatory disorders, allergic reactions, allergies, inflammatory conditions Anti-inflammatory, antihistaminic

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to extract bitter compounds from the bark.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and as an antiseptic due to its active compounds.

Safety Profile

European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior) is generally considered safe when used in traditional herbal remedies. However, it should not be used by individuals with known allergies to the ash tree or its components. Prolonged use may lead to toxicity, so it is important to consult a healthcare professional before use.
