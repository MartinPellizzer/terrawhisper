Rehmannia henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rehmannia henryi has erect, rhizomatous stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter and feature large, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a soft, hairy texture and a deep green color that provides a lush appearance. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, tubular, purplish-red flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are typically arranged in dense cymes, attracting various pollinators to the plant. The overall structure is herbaceous, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
