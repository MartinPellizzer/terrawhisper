Rubus hirsutus

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus hirsutus?

Rubus hirsutus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is part of the order Rosales and falls under the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Rubus, specifically identified by its species name, hirsutus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus hirsutus has woody, arching stems that are densely covered in fine, soft hairs and equipped with small, sharp prickles. The leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern and are trifoliate or pinnate with serrated margins. Each leaflet displays a distinct texture, often appearing somewhat rugose or wrinkled on the upper surface. The underside of the foliage is frequently more pubescent than the top, reflecting the species' namesake hirsute quality. Small, white to pale pink flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the branches to facilitate pollination. These floral structures eventually develop into aggregate fruits known as drupelets.
