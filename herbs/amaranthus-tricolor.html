Amaranthus tricolor

What's the taxonomical classification of Amaranthus tricolor?

Amaranthus tricolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Caryophyllales. The plant is a member of the family Amaranthaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Amaranthus, with its unique designation being the species tricolor.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amaranthus tricolor has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by smooth, branching stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, featuring entire margins and prominent venation that can vary in color from green to vibrant shades of red, pink, or yellow. Dense, terminal panicles composed of small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the upper leaf axils to form colorful clusters. These inflorescences often display intense pigmentation that complements the variegated foliage of the plant. The root system is comprised of a sturdy taproot that provides stability for its upright structure.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, ranging from the southern United States through Central America and into South America. Many populations have become naturalized in various temperate zones where the climate allows for seasonal growth. What are the pharmacological activities of Amaranthus tricolor?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antihyperglycaemic, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and antiulcer properties. Its rich profile of bioactive compounds allows it to combat oxidative stress by neutralizing harmful free radicals in the body. These phytochemicals also assist in regulating blood glucose levels, making it a potential candidate for managing diabetes. Additionally, the plant exhibits the ability to inhibit the growth of various pathogens and reduce inflammation in damaged tissues. Finally, its medicinal components contribute to the protection and healing of gastric mucosa against ulcer formation.

As discussed in a study, Amaranthus tricolor possesses medicinal properties that provide a neuroprotective effect against toxicity caused by advanced glycation end-products. Preincubation of SH-SY5Y cells with petroleum ether, dichloromethane, or methanol extracts of A. tricolor dose-dependently attenuated neuron toxicity. Furthermore, extracts of A. tricolor significantly downregulated the gene expression of the pro-inflammatory cytokines TNF-α, IL-1, and IL-6 in AGEs-induced cells. These findings demonstrate that A. tricolor has anti-inflammatory activity by reducing the expression of these specific genes. This suggests the plant may be useful for treating chronic inflammation associated with neurodegenerative disorders.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds including Atr-AMP1, Atr-DEF1, Atr-LTP1, Atr-SN1, and defensin. These specific proteins function as potent antimicrobial agents that help protect the organism from various pathogens. The Atr-AMP1 and Atr-DEF1 components are particularly notable for their ability to disrupt microbial membranes. Additionally, the presence of Atr-LTP1 and Atr-SN1 contributes to the complex chemical defense system of the plant. These diverse bioactive molecules work together to provide robust biological resistance.

As discussed in a study, the medicinal plant Amaranthus tricolor contains a novel 1.7 kDa antimicrobial peptide identified as Atr-AMP1. This peptide is a positively charged proline-rich molecule found within a heterogeneous population of multiple peptidofoms. The peptide possesses several post-translational modifications, including a disulfide bond, methionine oxidation, and proline hydroxylation. Atr-AMP1 demonstrated strong activity against Escherichia coli ATCC 25922. Additionally, bioactivity screening of a simplified fraction containing the 1.7 kDa peptide revealed activity against Staphylococcus aureus LAC.
