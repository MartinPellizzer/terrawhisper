What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gnetum gracilipes has slender, woody stems that branch extensively to form an open, shrubby architecture characterized by small, lanceolate leaves and distinct, scale-like structures. Its stems are often reddish or brownish in color and serve as the primary structural support for the plant's sparse foliage. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous cones that develop along the branches. This species is distinguished by its delicate, wiry appearance compared to other members of the Gnetaceae family.
