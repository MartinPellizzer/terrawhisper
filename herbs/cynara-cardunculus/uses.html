Cardoon (Cynara cardunculus) Uses

Cardoon is used to treat gastritis, gastric ulcers, and gastroesophageal reflux disease due to its anti-inflammatory and protective effects on the stomach lining. It also aids with constipation, indigestion, and gastrointestinal gas by promoting healthy digestion. Additionally, it supports liver function and may help with hepatitis and inflammatory bowel disease.

Cardoon (Cynara cardunculus) Monograph

1. Gastritis

Cardoon has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in cases of gastritis. Its anti-inflammatory and mucosal protective properties help soothe stomach lining irritation. It may reduce excess stomach acid and promote healing, making it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for managing gastritis symptoms.

2. Constipation

Cardoon is used to treat constipation due to its high fiber content, which promotes bowel movement. It also contains compounds that stimulate digestive secretions. The leaves and stems are commonly prepared as teas or infusions. Regular consumption can help maintain regularity and alleviate symptoms of digestive sluggishness.

3. Gastric ulcer

Cardoon has been traditionally used to support gastric health, particularly in the treatment of gastric ulcers. Its high mucilage content forms a protective layer over the stomach lining, reducing irritation and promoting healing. It also contains compounds with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that aid in ulcer recovery.

4. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Cardoon has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in managing gastroesophageal reflux disease. Its high mucilage content helps soothe the lining of the esophagus and stomach, reducing irritation and inflammation. It may also promote bile flow and alleviate symptoms such as heartburn and indigestion.

5. Inflammatory bowel disease

Cardoon has shown potential in managing inflammatory bowel disease due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Compounds like cynarins and flavonoids help reduce intestinal inflammation and oxidative stress. Preliminary studies suggest it may support gut health, though more research is needed for clinical application.

6. Hepatitis

Cardoon has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support liver health. It contains compounds that may help detoxify the liver and reduce inflammation. Its use is particularly associated with supporting liver function in cases of hepatitis. Studies suggest it may aid in the regeneration of liver cells.

7. Gastrointestinal gas

Cardoon is used to alleviate gastrointestinal gas due to its high mucilage content, which coats the digestive tract and reduces inflammation. It also aids in soothing bloating and indigestion, making it a useful remedy for digestive discomfort. Its mild laxative properties help regulate bowel movements and ease gas buildup in the intestines.

8. Gastrointestinal ulcers

Cardoon has been traditionally used to support gastrointestinal health, particularly in treating ulcers. Its high mucilage content forms a protective layer over the stomach lining, reducing irritation. It also contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that may aid in ulcer healing and prevent further damage.

9. Indigestion

Cardoon is used to alleviate indigestion due to its mild cholagogue properties, which stimulate bile production and aid in digestion. It helps reduce bloating and discomfort by promoting the movement of food through the digestive tract. Often prepared as a tea, it supports digestive health and can be a gentle remedy for mild digestive issues.

10. Gastrointestinal infections

Cardoon has been used traditionally to support digestive health and may help alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It can aid in reducing inflammation in the gut and promoting the healing of intestinal tissues. Its high fiber content also supports healthy digestion and regular bowel function.
