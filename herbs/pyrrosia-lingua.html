Pyrrosia lingua

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrrosia lingua?

Pyrrosia lingua belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Polypodiales and belongs to the family Polypodiaceae. Finally, its specific identity is defined by the genus Pyrrosia and the species lingua.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia lingua has lanceolate, bright green leaves that are sessile and possess distinctively serrated margins along their entire length. These leaves typically grow in a basal rosette pattern, spreading outward from a central point near the soil surface. The leaf texture is relatively thin and smooth, lacking significant pubescence on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the center of the foliage on slender, upright stalks. This structural arrangement allows the plant to efficiently capture sunlight while maintaining a low profile against the ground.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Pyrrosia lingua targets cyclooxygenase enzymes, lipoxygenase, and various proinflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds that inhibit the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway, which reduces the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes. By suppressing these inflammatory mediators, the extracts can mitigate pain and swelling in biological tissues. Additionally, certain phytochemicals within the plant interact with nuclear factor kappa B to downregulate the expression of genes involved in the immune response. These multi-target interactions contribute to the plant's overall anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Pyrrosia lingua has therapeutic properties that function as a bitter digestive stimulant, a carminative, and an aperient.
