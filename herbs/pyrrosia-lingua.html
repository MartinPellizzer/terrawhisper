Chinese Brake Fern

Pyrrosia lingua

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Botanical Identification

Pyrrosia lingua, also known as Chinese Brake Fern, belongs to the family Polypodiaceae. It is a perennial evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia. The plant has rhizomatous roots, pinnately compound leaves, and no flowers. Common names include Lingzhi and Fu ling in traditional Chinese texts.

Active Compounds

The Chinese Brake Fern contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Powder: Used for digestive and skin conditions.

Infusion: Used for respiratory and skin ailments.

Safety Profile

Chinese Brake Fern (Pyrrosia lingua) is generally considered safe when used appropriately. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised during pregnancy and lactation due to limited research on its effects.
