Chinese Brake Fern

Pyrrosia lingua

Chinese Brake Fern (Pyrrosia lingua) is a traditional Chinese medicinal plant known for its use in treating respiratory conditions such as asthma and cough, as well as its anti-inflammatory properties.

Taxonomy

The Chinese Brake Fern, Pyrrosia lingua, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Pteridophyta, class Leptosporangiata, order Polypodiales, family Pteridaceae, genus Pyrrosia, and species Pyrrosia lingua.

Morphology

The Chinese Brake Fern (Pyrrosia lingua) is a small, evergreen fern with a compact, rosette-like growth form, characterized by its leathery, dark green, lanceolate to ovate-shaped leaves that are typically 2-5 cm in length, with a serrated or toothed margin and a short, petiole-like base.

Distribution

The Chinese Brake Fern (Pyrrosia lingua) is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is commonly found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

Traditional Uses

Chinese Brake Fern (Pyrrosia lingua) has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat respiratory conditions such as coughs, asthma, and bronchitis, as well as to alleviate symptoms of inflammation and promote wound healing.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional Chinese medicine, the Chinese Brake Fern (Pyrrosia lingua) has been utilized for its purported ability to clear heat, detoxify the body, and treat conditions such as fever, inflammation, and skin disorders.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Chinese Brake Fern (Pyrrosia lingua) is characterized by the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, polysaccharides, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory properties.

Pharmacology

Chinese Brake Fern (Pyrrosia lingua) exhibits pharmacological properties including anticoagulant, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing effects, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating conditions such as hemostasis disorders, inflammatory diseases, and skin injuries.

Botanical Identification

Pyrrosia lingua, also known as Chinese Brake Fern, belongs to the family Polypodiaceae. It is a perennial evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia. The plant has rhizomatous roots, pinnately compound leaves, and no flowers. Common names include Lingzhi and Fu ling in traditional Chinese texts.

Safety Profile

Chinese Brake Fern (Pyrrosia lingua) is generally considered safe when used appropriately. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised during pregnancy and lactation due to limited research on its effects.
