Cremanthodium oblongatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cremanthodium oblongatum?

Cremanthodium oblongatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further classified under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, which places it among the diverse group of composite flowers. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Cremanthodium, specifically as the species oblongatum.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cremanthodium oblongatum can cause severe nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea due to the presence of toxic alkaloids and glycosides. These chemical compounds may trigger significant gastrointestinal distress if the plant is ingested accidentally. Prolonged exposure or high doses could potentially lead to more systemic toxicity affecting the nervous system or cardiac function. It is crucial to avoid all contact with the plant to prevent accidental poisoning. Always consult a medical professional immediately if symptoms arise after contact with this species.
