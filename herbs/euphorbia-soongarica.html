Euphorbia soongarica

What's the taxonomical classification of Euphorbia soongarica?

Euphorbia soongarica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, falling under the genus Euphorbia, and is specifically identified by its species name, soongarica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euphorbia soongarica has succulent, cylindrical stems that are bright green and characterized by distinct, longitudinal ridges. The plant produces specialized inflorescences called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbia genus. These reproductive structures are often accompanied by colorful bracts that serve to attract pollinators.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Local populations are often restricted to certain microclimates that provide the necessary moisture and shade.     What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains medicinal compounds including 3-O-methylellagic acid-4'-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside, aesculetin-6-O-(6'-O-galloyl)-beta-D-galactopyranoside, coumarin glycosides, ellagic acid, and fraxeitin-8-O-(6'-O-galloyl)-beta-D-galactopyranoside. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the complex chemical profile used in various therapeutic applications. The presence of ellagic acid and diverse coumarin glycosides is particularly notable for their biological activity. Furthermore, the galloylated derivatives like fraxeitin-8-O-(6'-O-galloyl)-beta-D-galactopyranoside and aesculetin-6-O-(6'-O-galloyl)-beta-D-galactopyranoside suggest potent antioxidant potential. Together, these secondary metabolites define the plant's medicinal characteristics.

    As discussed in a study, ten chemical constituents were isolated from the n-BuOH fraction of Euphorbia soongarica roots. These compounds include ellagic acid (1), 3, 3'-di-O-methylellagic acid (2), 3, 3'-di-O-methylellagic acid-4'-O-alpha-D-arabinfuranoside (3), 3, 3'-di-O-methylellagic acid-4'-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside (4), and 3, 3'-di-O-methylellagic acid-4-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (5). Further identified substances include 3, 3', 4'-tri-O-methylellagic acid (6), 3-O-methylellagic acid-4'-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside (7), 3, 3', 4-tri-O-methylellagic acid-4'-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (8), brevifolin (9), and ethyl brevifolin carboxylate (10). All 10 of these compounds were obtained from this plant for the first time.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause skin irritation, eye irritation, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain if its toxic sap makes contact with the body or is ingested. Contact with the milky latex often leads to redness, burning, or rashes on the skin. If the sap enters the eyes, it can cause significant irritation and potential damage to the ocular tissue. Ingestion of any part of the plant typically results in severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea and vomiting. This digestive upset is frequently accompanied by diarrhea and intense abdominal pain.
