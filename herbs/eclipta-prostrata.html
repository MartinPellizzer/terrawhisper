Eclipta prostrata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eclipta prostrata has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire margins. Small, white, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring five spreading lobes and a central yellow eye. The plant produces small, cylindrical achenes that are often ribbed and contain numerous tiny seeds. These morphological features collectively distinguish it from other species within the Asteraceae family.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Eclipta prostrata targets cyclooxygenase-2, various pro-inflammatory cytokines, and specific enzymes involved in oxidative stress pathways to exert its medicinal effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like wedelolactone that inhibit the NF-kappaB signaling pathway, thereby reducing the expression of inflammatory mediators. Its phytochemical constituents also interact with antioxidant enzymes such as superoxide dismutase and catalase to neutralize reactive oxygen species. Furthermore, certain alkaloids in the plant may influence acetylcholinesterase activity, which contributes to its neuroprotective properties. These multifaceted interactions with proteins and enzymes allow the plant to function as an anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and potentially neuroprotective agent.

Eclipta prostrata has diverse therapeutic actions including bitter, diuretic, astringent, hepatoprotective, anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and emmenagogue properties. Additionally, the plant acts as an anti-inflammatory and antipyretic agent to reduce swelling and lower fevers. Finally, its use as an emmenagogue can assist in regulating menstrual cycles.

What are the herbal preparations of Eclipta prostrata?

Eclipta prostrata is prepared as a decoction, infusion, juice, paste, oil, or powder for various therapeutic applications. The fresh juice is frequently extracted by crushing the leaves to treat skin conditions or hair loss. Practitioners often create a paste from the ground plant material to apply topically to wounds or inflammations. Decoctions and infusions are commonly consumed as teas to address internal ailments such as digestive issues or fever. Additionally, the plant can be infused into oils to create hair treatments that promote scalp health.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Eclipta prostrata can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if consumed improperly. While often used in traditional medicine, direct contact with the plant may trigger contact dermatitis or localized redness in sensitive individuals. Oral ingestion of concentrated extracts has been linked to stomach upset or nausea in some documented cases. Because scientific research on its long-term toxicity is limited, there is a risk of unforeseen adverse reactions during prolonged use. It is important to approach this plant with caution due to these potential physiological responses.
