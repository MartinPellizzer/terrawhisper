Eclipta prostrata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eclipta prostrata has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire margins. Small, white, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring five spreading lobes and a central yellow eye. The plant produces small, cylindrical achenes that are often ribbed and contain numerous tiny seeds. These morphological features collectively distinguish it from other species within the Asteraceae family.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Eclipta prostrata targets cyclooxygenase-2, various pro-inflammatory cytokines, and specific enzymes involved in oxidative stress pathways to exert its medicinal effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like wedelolactone that inhibit the NF-kappaB signaling pathway, thereby reducing the expression of inflammatory mediators. Furthermore, certain alkaloids in the plant may influence acetylcholinesterase activity, which contributes to its neuroprotective properties. These multifaceted interactions with proteins and enzymes allow the plant to function as an anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and potentially neuroprotective agent.
