Iris lactea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris lactea has lanceolate, glaucous leaves that grow in a basal rosette and slender, pale lavender to white flowers with distinct yellow markings on the falls. The foliage is characterized by a waxy, bluish-green coating that helps the plant retain moisture in arid environments. Each flower features three upright petals known as standards and three drooping petals called falls, which serve to guide pollinators toward the nectar. The plant's rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread slowly across the soil surface. These sturdy underground structures also act as storage organs for nutrients during dormant periods.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Iris lactea contains iridin, isorididin, and various glycosides that function as potent bioactive agents. Research indicates that the iridoid compounds within the rhizomes can help modulate immune responses and reduce swelling. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Iris lactea targets specific proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, tumor necrosis factor-alpha, and various matrix metalloproteinases to mediate its anti-inflammatory and anticancer activities. Its bioactive iridoids and flavonoids work by inhibiting the NF-kappaB signaling pathway to suppress the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines. These compounds also interact with apoptotic proteins like Bax and Bcl-2 to regulate programmed cell death in malignant cells. Additionally, certain extracts demonstrate the ability to inhibit protein kinases that drive tumor cell proliferation and migration. By modulating these diverse enzymatic and regulatory pathways, the plant exerts broad therapeutic effects on cellular inflammation and oncology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Iris lactea?

Iris lactea has medicinal properties that include diuretic, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, expectorant, and astringent actions.
