What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium ensifolium has long, sword-shaped leaves that are leathery in texture and grow in an upright, fan-like arrangement from a creeping rhizome. The plant produces tall, arching flower spikes that emerge from the base of the leaves to display numerous fragrant blossoms. Each individual flower features a complex labellum and petals that often exhibit subtle color variations depending on the specific cultivar.
