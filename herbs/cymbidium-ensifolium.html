Cymbidium ensifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium ensifolium has long, sword-shaped leaves that are leathery in texture and grow in an upright, fan-like arrangement from a creeping rhizome. These foliage structures are typically deep green and taper to a sharp point at the apex. The plant produces tall, arching flower spikes that emerge from the base of the leaves to display numerous fragrant blossoms. Each individual flower features a complex labellum and petals that often exhibit subtle color variations depending on the specific cultivar. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by its robust, clump-forming growth habit and sturdy vegetative base.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the montane forests of central and southern China, specifically occurring in provinces such as Hubei, Hunan, Jiangxi, and Guizhou. It typically thrives in high-altitude regions where it finds suitable microclimates among limestone cliffs and shaded rocky slopes. The species is often found in moist, well-drained environments within temperate broadleaf or mixed forests. Its specific ecological niche is limited to these particular mountainous terrains, which prevents a widespread global distribution.
