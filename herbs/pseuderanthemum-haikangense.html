What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pseuderanthemum haikangense has perennial herbaceous stems that arise from a woody rhizome and produce leaves that are primarily opposite or whorled with lanceolate to ovate shapes. The leaf margins are typically serrated or entire, depending on the specific specimen, and the surfaces may exhibit varying degrees of pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of small, actinomorphic flowers arranged in compact cymes or clusters at the terminal ends of the branches. The corolla is characterized by a tubular structure that expands into distinct lobes, often displaying pale colors. These morphological features are adapted to its specific high-altitude habitat within the Scrophulariaceae family.
