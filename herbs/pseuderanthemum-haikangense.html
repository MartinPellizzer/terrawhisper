Pseuderanthemum haikangense

What's the taxonomical classification of Pseuderanthemum haikangense?

Pseuderanthemum haikangense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Acanthaceae, falling specifically within the genus Pseuderanthemum, and is identified by the species epithet haikangense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pseuderanthemum haikangense has perennial herbaceous stems that arise from a woody rhizome and produce leaves that are primarily opposite or whorled with lanceolate to ovate shapes.

The leaf margins are typically serrated or entire, depending on the specific specimen, and the surfaces may exhibit varying degrees of pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of small, actinomorphic flowers arranged in compact cymes or clusters at the terminal ends of the branches. The corolla is characterized by a tubular structure that expands into distinct lobes, often displaying pale colors.

These morphological features are adapted to its specific high-altitude habitat within the Scrophulariaceae family.
