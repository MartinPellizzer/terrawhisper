Artemisia keiskeana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia keiskeana has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, woody stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 80 centimeters. The leaves are deeply pinnatifid or bipinnatifid, featuring narrow, linear lobes that give the foliage a delicate, feathery appearance. These leaves are covered in fine, silvery-white hairs that provide a distinctive grayish-green coloration to the plant. During the flowering season, the species produces small, inconspicuous flower heads arranged in branched panicles. The overall texture of the plant is soft and fine-textured due to the intricate division of its leaf margins.

    What are the herbal preparations of Artemisia keiskeana?

    Artemisia keiskeana is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the specific medicinal requirement. The dried leaves are often simmered in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. When a milder effect is desired, the plant material is steeped in hot water to produce a gentle tea. For external ailments like skin irritations, the crushed plant is applied directly to the body as a moist compress.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Artemisia keiskeana can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, redness, and itching upon direct exposure to its leaves or oils. This plant contains sesquiterpene lactones which act as potent sensitizers for many individuals. Once a person becomes sensitized, even minimal contact with the plant can trigger an immediate inflammatory response. Symptoms may include localized swelling, blistering, or a persistent rash that requires topical treatment. It is important to wear protective clothing when handling this species to avoid these dermatological complications.
