Carex lanceolata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex lanceolata has lanceolate leaves that are typically 2 to 6 millimeters wide, slender culms reaching heights of 10 to 40 centimeters, and perigynia that are narrowly elliptical with a distinctly long, tapering beak. The foliage is characterized by a somewhat rigid texture and a bright green color that distinguishes it from many other sedges. Its inflorescence consists of several distinct spikes, often featuring a mix of male and female flowers arranged along the stem. The perigynia are notably smooth and contain a single seed, which is a key diagnostic feature for identifying the species. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to thrive in the specific acidic, moist habitats it prefers.
