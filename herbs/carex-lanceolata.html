What's the taxonomical classification of Carex lanceolata?

Carex lanceolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Poales and falls within the family Cyperaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement in the genus Carex, specifically identified by the species name lanceolata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex lanceolata has lanceolate leaves that are typically 2 to 6 millimeters wide, slender culms reaching heights of 10 to 40 centimeters, and perigynia that are narrowly elliptical with a distinctly long, tapering beak. The foliage is characterized by a somewhat rigid texture and a bright green color that distinguishes it from many other sedges. Its inflorescence consists of several distinct spikes, often featuring a mix of male and female flowers arranged along the stem. The perigynia are notably smooth and contain a single seed, which is a key diagnostic feature for identifying the species.
