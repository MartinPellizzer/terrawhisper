Botanical Identification of Nux Vomica (Strychnos nux-vomica)

To identify Nux Vomica (Strychnos nux-vomica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Nux Vomica, scientifically known as Strychnos nux-vomica, belongs to the plant kingdom Plantae. It is classified under the division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Loganiaceae, family Loganiaceae, genus Strychnos, and species Strychnos nux-vomica. This classification places it within the broader group of flowering plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Strychnos nux-vomica is the scientific name for Nux Vomica. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. Common synonyms include Strychnos toxifera and Strychnos venenifera. Regional names include Indian Bittersweet and Yellow Bitternut, while historical names include Nux Vomica and Vomica.

Morphological Characteristics

Nux Vomica is a deciduous tree with a straight trunk and spreading branches. Its root system is taproot-like, and the stem is smooth, often slightly woody. Leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a capsule that splits into three parts, releasing seeds with a tuft of hair.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Nux Vomica can be distinguished by its smooth, dark green leaves with prominent veins, yellow flowers in panicles, and red, fleshy fruits containing yellow seeds. It differs from similar species through its leaf shape, fruit structure, and the absence of thorns. Key features include the presence of yellow flowers and the characteristic red, fleshy fruits.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Nux Vomica is native to parts of India, Southeast Asia, and tropical regions of Africa. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained, loamy soils. It is typically found at altitudes ranging from 300 to 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Nux Vomica blooms from April to June, producing yellowish-green flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. Harvesting is typically done when the seeds are fully mature, usually in late summer to early autumn, to ensure proper identification and potency.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Nux Vomica belongs to the genus Strychnos in the family Loganiaceae. Its closest relatives include species like Strychnos ignatii and Strychnos nux-vomica var. variegata. The genus Strychnos is characterized by its woody structure, simple leaves, and berries. Family traits include toxic alkaloids and specific floral and fruit morphology.
