What's the taxonomical classification of Panicum repens?

Panicum repens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Poales and falls within the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Panicum and the species repens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Panicum repens has a perennial growth habit characterized by creeping, stoloniferous rhizomes that allow the plant to spread horizontally through the soil. The stems are slender and erect, typically reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. Leaves consist of narrow, linear blades with parallel venation and a prominent midrib. The inflorescence is a compact, terminal panicle composed of numerous small, spikelet-like branches. Each spikelet is relatively small and contains several florets that are essential for its reproductive process.
