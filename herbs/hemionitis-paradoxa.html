What is Hemionitis paradoxa?

Hemionitis paradoxa is a species of fern in the family Pteridaceae.

It has rhizomes that are horizontal and creeping, with fronds that are bipinnate to tripinnate, pinnate or simple. The fronds are 30-120 cm long and have rachises and pinnae that are covered with dark brown to black scales. The plant has fertile fronds that are smaller than the sterile fronds, with sori that are linear to sublinear and contain 16-24 sporangia per sorus.

The sporangia are monolete and dehiscence is lateral.
