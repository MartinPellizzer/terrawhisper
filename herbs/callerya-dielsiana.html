What's the taxonomical classification of Callerya dielsiana?

Callerya dielsiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. This plant is a representative of the family Fabaceae, further categorized into the genus Callerya, and is specifically identified by its species name, dielsiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callerya dielsiana has large, broadly ovate, leathery leaves that grow from thick, creeping rhizomes and produce showy, fragrant flowers with wide, spreading petals and sepals.

The inflorescence consists of several large blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils on sturdy peduncles. Each individual flower typically features a prominent, patterned labellum that serves to attract specific pollinators. The plant's overall structure is characterized by an epiphytic growth habit with robust pseudobulbs that store water.
