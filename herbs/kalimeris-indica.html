Kalimeris indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Kalimeris indica?

Kalimeris indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further organized under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is categorized within the genus Kalimeris, specifically identified by the species name indica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Kalimeris indica contains high concentrations of bioactive secondary metabolites including terpenoids, flavonoids, polyphenols, and essential oils. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the plant's significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Research indicates that the presence of various phenolic compounds helps neutralize free radicals within biological systems. Furthermore, the terpenoid profiles contribute to its potential efficacy in treating inflammatory conditions. These medicinal compounds collectively support the plant's traditional use in various folk remedies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Kalimeris indica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and various gastrointestinal ailments. Its bioactive compounds provide significant antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial and fungal pathogens. Researchers have also noted its potential in managing oxidative stress and reducing swelling in different bodily tissues. Furthermore, its medicinal applications often extend to soothing digestive discomfort through its natural healing agents. These diverse therapeutic uses make it a valuable subject in ethnobotanical studies.
