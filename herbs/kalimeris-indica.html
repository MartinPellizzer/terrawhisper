Kalimeris indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Kalimeris indica?

Kalimeris indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further organized under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is categorized within the genus Kalimeris, specifically identified by the species name indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kalimeris indica has a perennial, tufted growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical, and smooth stems that can reach several decimeters in length. The leaves are arranged in dense, terminal clusters and exhibit a linear or lanceolate shape with entire margins. These leaves are typically bright green and possess a somewhat fleshy texture adapted to its aquatic or semi-aquatic environment. The plant lacks true roots, instead utilizing specialized submerged structures to anchor itself within the substrate. Small, inconspicuous flowers may appear in the axils of the leaves during its reproductive phase.

What are the pharmacological activities of Kalimeris indica?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory properties, the ability to reduce swelling, wound healing capabilities, enhancement of human immunity, and the capacity to reduce blood pressure. Its bioactive compounds work to mitigate painful inflammation and minimize localized edema in affected tissues. The medicinal extracts also promote faster tissue regeneration to support efficient wound healing processes. Furthermore, the plant serves to strengthen the human immune system against various pathogens. It also offers cardiovascular benefits by helping to effectively reduce elevated blood pressure levels.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Kalimeris indica is a medicinal plant used to treat various inflammation-related diseases. After extracting the dried herb with 50% aqueous ethanol, five fractions labeled A through E were separated. Fractions C and E showed significant inhibition of NO and TNF-α production in murine macrophage RAW 264.7 cells. Fraction C contained 6 identified constituents, including 3,4-dicaffeoylquinic acid (1), 3,5-dicaffeoylquinic acid (2), 1,5-dicaffeoylquinic acid (3), rutin (4), 1-malonyl-3,5-dicaffeoylquinic acid (5), and 4,5-dicaffeoylquinic acid (6). Fraction E contained 4 compounds, specifically 13-tetradecenal (7), (Z,Z)-9,12-octadecadienoic acid (8), (3α)-12-oleanen-3-yl acetate (9), and (+)-3-oxo-urs-12-en-24-oic acid methyl ester (10). These components serve as the major constituents and possible anti-inflammatory principles of the plant.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", eleven compounds were isolated and identified from Kalimeris indica. These medicinal constituents include dammaradienyl acetate(1), friedelin(2), friedelinol(3), beta-amyrin(4), alpha-amyrin(5), lupenyl acetate(6), beta-sitosterol(7), alpha-spinasterol(8), alpha-spinasterone(9), stigmasterol(10), and daucosterol(11). Compounds 4-6 and 8-11 were isolated from the genus Kalimeris for the first time. The structures of these compounds were elucidated through their physicochemical properties and spectral data analysis.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating diarrhea, gastric ulcers, inflammation-related diseases, internal heat, and swellings. Its medicinal properties allow it to soothe the digestive tract and alleviate painful stomach lesions. By reducing internal heat, it helps stabilize the body's physiological balance. The plant also serves as an effective agent to diminish swelling and calm various inflammatory responses. These diverse applications make it a significant resource in traditional herbal medicine.

According to a study published by "Biomedical chromatography : BMC", Kalimeris indica (L) Sch-Bip is a medicinal plant used by the Miao ethnic group in the Guizhou province of China. This plant is widely used as a fresh vegetable to treat colds, diarrhea and gastric ulcers. Researchers evaluated the anticomplement and antitussive activities of different polar extracts of K. indica. Through ultra-high-performance liquid chromatography quadrupole time-of-flight tandem mass spectrometry, 59 compounds were identified in the K. indica extract. A method was also established to simultaneously determine the anticomplement and antitussive activity of 5 compounds in K. indica. Among these, 3,5-dicaffeoylquinic acid and 4,5-dicaffeoylquinic acid exhibited significantly higher anticomplementary activity than the other 3 compounds.
