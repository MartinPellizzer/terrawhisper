Kalimeris indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Kalimeris indica?

Kalimeris indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further organized under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is categorized within the genus Kalimeris, specifically identified by the species name indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kalimeris indica has a perennial, tufted growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical, and smooth stems that can reach several decimeters in length.

The leaves are arranged in dense, terminal clusters and exhibit a linear or lanceolate shape with entire margins. These leaves are typically bright green and possess a somewhat fleshy texture adapted to its aquatic or semi-aquatic environment. The plant lacks true roots, instead utilizing specialized submerged structures to anchor itself within the substrate.

Small, inconspicuous flowers may appear in the axils of the leaves during its reproductive phase.
