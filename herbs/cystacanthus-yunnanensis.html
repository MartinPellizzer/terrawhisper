Cystacanthus yunnanensis has woody, climbing stems with opposite, elliptical, or oblong leaves that possess entire margins and acute tips. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often presenting in clusters. These blossoms are characterized by their delicate structure and specific colorations suited for local pollinators.
