Cystacanthus yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cystacanthus yunnanensis has woody, climbing stems with opposite, elliptical, or oblong leaves that possess entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically dark green and grows in a manner that allows the plant to scramble through surrounding vegetation. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often presenting in clusters. These blossoms are characterized by their delicate structure and specific colorations suited for local pollinators. The overall growth habit is that of a climbing shrub or liana, typical of many species within its genus.
