Helichrysum arenarium

What's the taxonomical classification of Helichrysum arenarium?

Helichrysum arenarium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical order of Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its classification within the genus Helichrysum, specifically as the species arenarium.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Helichrysum arenarium contains high concentrations of sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and essential oils that provide its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities. The presence of specific terpenoids suggests potential applications in soothing skin irritations and reducing oxidative stress in tissues. Additionally, the complex profile of phenolic compounds supports its traditional use in treating various inflammatory conditions. Researchers continue to study these specific metabolites to understand their full pharmacological potential.

What are the therapeutic actions of Helichrysum arenarium?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various dermatological conditions, respiratory ailments, and digestive issues through its medicinal properties. It is frequently applied topically to soothe skin inflammations, wounds, and minor irritations. Some traditional practices utilize its extracts to alleviate coughs and bronchial congestion. Additionally, it may be consumed in specific preparations to assist with gastrointestinal discomfort. These therapeutic applications rely on the bioactive compounds found within its leaves and flowers.
