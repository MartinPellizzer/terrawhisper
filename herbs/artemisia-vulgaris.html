Artemisia vulgaris

What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia vulgaris?

Artemisia vulgaris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. Its taxonomic journey continues through the family Asteraceae, which is the characteristic family for this plant. Ultimately, it is categorized under the genus Artemisia and is identified by its specific species name, vulgaris.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Artemisia vulgaris contains a complex profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including thujone, cineole, camphor, and various sesquiterpene lactones. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antimicrobial and antispasmodic properties. The presence of volatile oils allows the plant to interact with biological systems to potentially reduce inflammation and soothe digestive distress. Additionally, flavonoids found within the leaves contribute antioxidant activities that support cellular health. Researchers continue to study these compounds to better understand their potential applications in modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Artemisia vulgaris?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive disorders, menstrual irregularities, fever, and parasitic infections. It contains bioactive compounds that help alleviate symptoms of intestinal worms and various stomach ailments. Historically, herbalists have also utilized its properties to regulate menstrual cycles and reduce heavy bleeding. Some traditional practices incorporate it to help lower body temperature during febrile illnesses. While widely used in folk medicine, its potency requires careful attention to dosage to avoid toxicity.
