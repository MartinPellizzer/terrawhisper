Nanocnide lobata

What's the taxonomical classification of Nanocnide lobata?

Nanocnide lobata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Rosales and the family Urticaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Nanocnide, and its species designation, lobata.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Nanocnide lobata can cause severe poisoning characterized by nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and potentially fatal cardiac arrhythmias due to its high concentration of cyanogenic glycosides. When these compounds are ingested, they release hydrogen cyanide into the bloodstream, which prevents cells from using oxygen effectively. This chemical reaction can lead to rapid breathing, dizziness, and a sudden drop in blood pressure. In extreme cases of toxicity, a person may experience seizures, loss of consciousness, or complete respiratory failure. Immediate medical intervention is necessary if exposure to this plant is suspected.
