Cajanus mollis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cajanus mollis?

Cajanus mollis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Cajanus, with the individual species designated as mollis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cajanus mollis has trifoliate leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets that possess a soft, pubescent texture and a dull green surface.

The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub or small tree characterized by a woody stem structure. Its flowers are zygomorphic and emerge from axillary positions, often displaying a range of yellow or cream hues. The fruit develops into small, compressed legumes that contain several seeds protected by a thin pod.

These pods eventually dehisce to release the seeds, which are essential for the plant's reproductive cycle.

        Cajanus mollis is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated tea made by boiling the plant parts in water to extract its medicinal properties.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Cajanus mollis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. The plant contains various phytochemicals that may trigger inflammatory responses upon direct contact with sensitive skin. If the seeds or foliage are consumed in large quantities, individuals might experience nausea, stomach cramps, or diarrhea. Some people may also exhibit localized swelling or itching at the site of exposure. It is essential to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse physiological reactions.
