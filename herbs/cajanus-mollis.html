Cajanus mollis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cajanus mollis has trifoliate leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets that possess a soft, pubescent texture and a dull green surface. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub or small tree characterized by a woody stem structure. Its flowers are zygomorphic and emerge from axillary positions, often displaying a range of yellow or cream hues. The fruit develops into small, compressed legumes that contain several seeds protected by a thin pod. These pods eventually dehisce to release the seeds, which are essential for the plant's reproductive cycle.
