Pyrrosia piloselloides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia piloselloides has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by upright, glandular-pubescent stems and basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptical with dentate margins. The foliage typically exhibits a somewhat fleshy texture and is covered in fine, sticky hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal racemes or panicles. The floral structures feature delicate petals that are often pale in color, contrasting with the hairy surface of the calyx. This morphological suite of traits distinguishes it from related species within the Saxifragaceae family.
