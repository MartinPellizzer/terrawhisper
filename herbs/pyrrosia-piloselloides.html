Pyrrosia piloselloides

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrrosia piloselloides?

Pyrrosia piloselloides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and is further categorized within the order Polypodiales. This plant is a representative of the family Polypodiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Pyrrosia, and is identified by its unique species designation, piloselloides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia piloselloides has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by upright, glandular-pubescent stems and basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptical with dentate margins.

The foliage typically exhibits a somewhat fleshy texture and is covered in fine, sticky hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal racemes or panicles. The floral structures feature delicate petals that are often pale in color, contrasting with the hairy surface of the calyx.

This morphological suite of traits distinguishes it from related species within the Saxifragaceae family.

        What are the herbal preparations of Pyrrosia piloselloides?

        Pyrrosia piloselloides is prepared as an infusion, a decoction, or a topical tincture for medicinal application.
