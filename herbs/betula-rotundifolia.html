Betula rotundifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Betula rotundifolia?

Betula rotundifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Fagales. It is situated within the family Betulaceae, falling under the genus Betula, and is specifically identified by its species name, rotundifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Betula rotundifolia has small, ovate to nearly circular leaves with serrated margins and a characteristically blunt or rounded apex. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface while the undersides remain a paler shade. Its branches are slender and often display a slightly drooping habit, especially toward the tips. The bark is thin and typically develops a yellowish or greyish hue as the tree matures. Small, pendulous catkins emerge during the flowering season to facilitate wind pollination.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?
