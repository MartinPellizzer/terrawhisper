Betula rotundifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Betula rotundifolia?

Betula rotundifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Fagales. It is situated within the family Betulaceae, falling under the genus Betula, and is specifically identified by its species name, rotundifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Betula rotundifolia contains bioactive secondary metabolites such as betulin, betulinic acid, and various polyphenolic compounds that possess significant pharmacological properties. These triterpenoids are recognized for their potent anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antitumor activities in various biological models. The plant also provides flavonoids and tannins which serve as powerful antioxidants to combat oxidative stress in cells. Researchers study these specific chemical constituents to develop new treatments for skin disorders and metabolic diseases. Through these diverse medicinal compounds, the species demonstrates considerable potential for pharmaceutical applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Betula rotundifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat urinary tract infections, bladder inflammation, and kidney stones by acting as a natural diuretic. The leaves contain specific compounds that help flush toxins and bacteria from the renal system through increased urine production. It is also frequently applied in traditional medicine to manage symptoms of gout and reduce uric acid levels in the body. These properties make it a valuable component in herbal remedies aimed at supporting overall kidney health and soothing irritated mucosal membranes.
