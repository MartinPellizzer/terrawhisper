Aster turbinatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster turbinatus?

Aster turbinatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Specifically, this plant is categorized within the genus Aster, and its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, turbinatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster turbinatus has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 50 centimeters. The leaves are generally lanceolate or linear in shape, possessing serrated margins and a texture that varies from smooth to slightly pubescent. Its inflorescence consists of small, discoid or radiate flower heads that are often arranged in terminal clusters. The individual florets display a range of colors including pale yellow, white, or light purple depending on the specific variety. The plant's root system is fibrous, allowing it to stabilize within its natural meadow or grassland habitats.
