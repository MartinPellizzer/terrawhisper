Aster turbinatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster turbinatus?

Aster turbinatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Specifically, this plant is categorized within the genus Aster, and its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, turbinatus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aster turbinatus contains bioactive compounds such as sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and various polysaccharides that provide its therapeutic effects. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's ability to reduce inflammation and clear heat within the body. Research suggests that the sesquiterpene lactones play a significant role in its antimicrobial and anti-tumor activities. Additionally, the flavonoids contribute to its antioxidant properties by neutralizing free radicals. These diverse medicinal components make the plant a valuable element in traditional herbal formulations.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aster turbinatus?

Aster turbinatus has medicinal properties that primarily target the lungs and spleen to clear heat, resolve phlegm, and regulate the kidney function. It is frequently utilized in traditional herbal formulas to treat coughs characterized by yellow, thick phlegm and chest congestion. By addressing internal heat and dampness, the plant helps to alleviate respiratory distress and improve lung clarity. Furthermore, its ability to harmonize the digestive system supports the spleen in managing fluid metabolism. These combined actions make it a significant component in treating complex conditions involving both respiratory and gastrointestinal imbalances.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammation, fever, skin infections, and various digestive disorders. It contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and lower high body temperatures in traditional medicine. The plant is often applied topically to soothe skin irritation or wounds to prevent infection. Some practitioners also use it to alleviate stomach discomfort and improve gastrointestinal health. Its medicinal properties are primarily attributed to its antioxidant and antimicrobial effects.
