What's the taxonomical classification of Angelica oncosepala?

Angelica oncosepala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated under the order Apiales. This plant is a representative of the family Apiaceae, which is characterized by its complex floral structures. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Angelica, specifically identified by its unique species name, oncosepala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

The sepals are notably oblong or lance-shaped, which provides the specific namesake characteristic for the species.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, digestive upset, and potential allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Because many members of the Apiaceae family contain furanocoumarins, exposure to the plant's oils may lead to phytophotodermatitis when the skin is subsequently exposed to sunlight. Ingesting the plant in uncontrolled amounts might trigger nausea, abdominal pain, or diarrhea. Some users may also experience localized rashes or swelling if the sap comes into direct contact with the epidermis. It is essential to exercise caution and consult professional guidance before any application or consumption.
