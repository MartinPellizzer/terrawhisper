Angelica oncosepala

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angelica oncosepala has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by hollow, ridged stems and large, compound leaves with lanceolate leaflets. The foliage features deeply serrated margins and a distinctively textured surface. Its inflorescence consists of broad, flat-topped umbels that support numerous small, yellowish-green flowers. The sepals are notably oblong or lance-shaped, which provides the specific namesake characteristic for the species. These floral structures are supported by long, sturdy peduncles that elevate the blooms above the primary leaf mass.

      What are the herbal preparations of Angelica oncosepala?

      Angelica oncosepala is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using its dried roots and rhizomes to extract active medicinal compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Angelica oncosepala can cause skin irritation, photosensitivity, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or applied incorrectly. Because it belongs to the Apiaceae family, certain compounds in the plant may react with sunlight to trigger severe dermatitis upon skin contact. Ingestion of concentrated amounts might lead to nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting in sensitive individuals. Detailed toxicological studies on this specific species are limited, so caution is necessary when handling the plant. Users should avoid contact with eyes and mucous membranes to prevent inflammatory reactions.
