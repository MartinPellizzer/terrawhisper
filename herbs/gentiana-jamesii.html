Gentiana jamesii

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana jamesii?

Gentiana jamesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a representative of the family Gentianaceae, specifically situated within the genus Gentiana, and is identified by its unique species name, jamesii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana jamesii has opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are sessile and possess entire or slightly wavy margins. The stems are typically erect and can reach heights of up to 15 centimeters. Its most distinguishing feature is the funnel-shaped, blue to violet flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms often feature pale longitudinal stripes or markings on the interior of the corolla lobes. The plant typically grows in low-lying clumps within alpine or subalpine meadow environments.
