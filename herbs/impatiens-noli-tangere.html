Impatiens noli-tangere

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens noli-tangere?

Impatiens noli-tangere belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Balsaminaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Impatiens, and its specific species designation, noli-tangere.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Impatiens noli-tangere contains various bioactive compounds including flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These secondary metabolites are primarily utilized in traditional medicine to address skin inflammations and minor wounds. Some studies suggest that the presence of these phytochemicals may provide antimicrobial and antioxidant properties to the plant tissue. Researchers investigate these chemical constituents to understand how they interact with biological systems to reduce oxidative stress. The specific chemical profile of the species varies depending on the environmental conditions in which the plant grows.

What are the therapeutic actions of Impatiens noli-tangere?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, dermatitis, psoriasis, and various types of inflammation or minor wounds. The plant contains compounds that act as natural anti-inflammatory agents to soothe irritated tissue. When applied topically, the crushed leaves or juices can help reduce swelling and redness on the skin surface. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to alleviate the itching associated with insect bites. Its medicinal properties are primarily focused on topical applications for dermatological relief.
