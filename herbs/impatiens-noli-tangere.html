What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens noli-tangere?

Impatiens noli-tangere belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Balsaminaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Impatiens, and its specific species designation, noli-tangere.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens noli-tangere has succulent, bright green stems that are opposite in arrangement and bear serrated, ovate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a smooth, waxy texture and a somewhat fleshy consistency. Small, five-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a pale pink to white color with delicate veining. A defining morphological feature is the explosive seed capsules that burst upon contact to disperse seeds. The plant typically maintains a low-growing, spreading habit in shaded environments.
