Glochidion daltonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glochidion daltonii has simple, alternate leaves with an elliptical to oblong shape, entire margins, and acute to acuminate apices. The leaf surfaces are typically glabrous and possess a distinctively coriaceous texture. Its woody stems exhibit a brownish bark that provides structural support for the spreading canopy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are characteristic of the Phyllanthaceae family. Following pollination, the species develops small, fleshy fruits that often contain several seeds.
