Sterculia hainanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sterculia hainanenensis has a woody tree structure characterized by alternate, imparipinnate leaves that typically consist of five to nine leaflets with entire margins and obtuse apices. The leaflets exhibit a leathery texture and are often covered in fine hairs on their undersides. The flowers are relatively small and are produced in clusters from the leaf axils. These blossoms feature a distinctively shaped calyx that surrounds the reproductive organs. The fruit develops into a woody capsule that eventually splits open to reveal seeds.
