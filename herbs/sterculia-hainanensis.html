Sterculia hainanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Sterculia hainanensis?

Sterculia hainanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned within the order Malvales. It is further categorized under the family Malvaceae, which contains the genus Sterculia, and is specifically identified by its species name, hainanenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sterculia hainanenensis has a woody tree structure characterized by alternate, imparipinnate leaves that typically consist of five to nine leaflets with entire margins and obtuse apices.

The leaflets exhibit a leathery texture and are often covered in fine hairs on their undersides. The flowers are relatively small and are produced in clusters from the leaf axils. These blossoms feature a distinctively shaped calyx that surrounds the reproductive organs.

The fruit develops into a woody capsule that eventually splits open to reveal seeds.
