Morinda umbellata

What's the taxonomical classification of Morinda umbellata?

Morinda umbellata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Gentianales, it is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is established within the genus Morinda, with its unique designation as the species umbellata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Morinda umbellata has a woody, scrambling shrub or small tree habit characterized by opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy green and often feature prominent veins. The foliage typically measures between 10 and 25 centimeters in length and possesses a somewhat leathery texture. Its inflorescences are arranged in dense, terminal clusters that produce small, fragrant white flowers. These flowers eventually develop into fleshy, globose syncarps that turn yellowish or reddish as they mature. The plant also exhibits a complex branching structure that allows it to climb or spread through dense vegetation.

What are the pharmacological activities of Morinda umbellata?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, curative against diarrhea, and curative against dysentery pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce systemic swelling and pain by modulating inflammatory pathways in the body. When used to treat gastrointestinal distress, the plant helps stabilize intestinal motility to stop frequent bowel movements. It also acts effectively against dysentery by targeting the pathogens and irritation in the digestive tract. These diverse medicinal properties make it a valuable resource in traditional herbal medicine.

According to a study published by "Toxicology research", Morinda umbellaata L. is a traditional medicinal plant used for curative effects against diarrhea, dysentery, and other stomach disorders. The active fraction of the plant, known as MUAF, was shown to significantly inhibit proinflamatory cytokines such as TNF-alpha, IL-1beta, and IL-6 in a dose-dependent manner. GC-MS analysis of the MUAF leaves revealed the presence of 8 compounds. The study further demonstrated that MUAF down-regulated the expression of TNF-alpha, IL-1beta, IL-6, iNOS, COX-2, and NF-kappaB genes. These findings suggest that MUAF effectively inhibits LPS-induced proinflamatory cytokines and suppresses the over expression of TNF-alpha, IL-1beta, IL-6, iNOS, and COX-2 by downregulating NF-kappaB activation. Oral acute toxicity results suggested the non-toxic nature of MUAF.
