What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea sericans has an erect, perennial herb morphology characterized by a woody base, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and small, yellowish-white flower heads arranged in terminal clusters. The inflorescence consists of numerous small capitula that emerge from the upper leaf axils. These flowers are held in dense, corymbose arrangements to attract pollinators.
