Chelidonium Majus Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Chelidonium majus, commonly known as greater celandine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its yellow flowers and toxic yellow latex.

This herb has been traditionally used for its detoxifying properties and is believed to support liver function and skin health.

The bioactive constituents include alkaloids such as chelidonine and sanguinarine, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and topical salves can be made from its leaves and roots, though caution is advised due to its toxicity.

Side Effects of chelidonium majus

Chelidonium majus causes skin irritation, which can manifest as redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon contact with the plant's sap or when ingested.

This herb is known to induce vomiting and lead to nausea, often as a result of its toxic alkaloids, which can irritate the gastrointestinal tract. Prolonged or excessive use of Chelidonium majus may cause liver damage, as it has been associated with hepatotoxic effects, particularly when consumed in high doses or over extended periods.

Additionally, the herb can cause eye irritation, including redness, tearing, and discomfort, if the sap comes into contact with the eyes. It may also result in a rash, drowsiness, and other systemic effects, making it a potentially dangerous plant for both topical and internal use.

Due to these significant health risks, Chelidonium majus should be handled with caution and is generally not recommended for medicinal use without professional supervision.
