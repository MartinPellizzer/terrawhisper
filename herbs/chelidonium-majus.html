Chelidonium Majus

Chelidonium majus, commonly known as Greater Celandine, is a flowering plant that has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties.

It contains alkaloids such as chelidonine, which have shown potential in treating skin conditions and gastrointestinal issues. Historically, it has been employed in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of liver disease and inflammation. However, due to its toxicity, especially when consumed internally, its use is cautioned and often limited to topical applications.

Despite its historical significance, modern scientific research continues to explore its potential benefits and safe usage in alternative medicine.
