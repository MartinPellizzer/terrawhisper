What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium trigonopus has pendulous, cylindrical pseudobulbs that are typically fleshy and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The inflorescence is characterized by small, delicate flowers that feature three distinct sepals and petals arranged in a triangular fashion. These blossoms are often pale in color and possess a subtle fragrance to attract specific pollinators.
