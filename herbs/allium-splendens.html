What's the taxonomical classification of Allium splendens?

Allium splendens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the hierarchy of biological classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic ranks, it falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Allium and the species splendens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium splendens has globose umbels containing numerous small, star-shaped, pink to rose-colored flowers supported by long, slender scapes. The individual florets possess six tepals that spread widely to reveal central reproductive structures. Below the flowering head, the plant produces linear, dark green leaves that emerge from the bulb and often arch gracefully outward. The underground bulb serves as the primary storage organ, providing the energy required for its seasonal growth cycle. This morphological structure allows the plant to effectively display its blooms above the foliage to attract pollinators.
