10 Possible Side Effects Of Hydrangea Macrophylla

Hydrangea macrophylla has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritation.

These side effects can be caused by overconsumption or sensitivities to the herb's glycosides or tannins. For example, individuals with gastrointestinal problems may experience worsening symptoms, while skin sensitivity can lead to discomfort and social isolation.

In extreme cases, severe allergic reactions can be life-threatening.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Hydrangea macrophylla causes allergic reactions when its sap comes into contact with skin or mucous membranes.

The allergenic compounds present in the plant's sap trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, swelling, and blistering.

Additionally, inhaling the plant's pollen can exacerbate allergies, causing respiratory issues like congestion, coughing, and sneezing.

2. Triggers skin irritation

Hydrangea macrophylla triggers skin irritation due to its allergenic properties and the presence of toxic compounds such as cyanogenic glycosides.

These chemicals can cause contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, and blistering on the skin.

Additionally, the plant's sap may contain irritating alkaloids that can lead to skin reactions upon contact.

3. Increases bleeding risk

Hydrangea macrophylla increases bleeding risk due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

This is because it contains compounds such as caffeic acid and quinic acid, which have antiplatelet properties that can interfere with blood clotting.

Additionally, Hydrangea macrophylla may also increase the levels of liver enzymes involved in metabolism, further contributing to its anticoagulant effects.

4. Interferes with clotting

Hydrangea macrophylla interferes with clotting by inhibiting platelet aggregation and prolonging bleeding time.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, such as eupatoriopicrin and centaureidin, which have antiplatelet properties that disrupt the normal blood coagulation process.

As a result, individuals taking Hydrangea macrophylla may experience prolonged bleeding after injury or surgery.

5. Worsens kidney damage

Hydrangea macrophylla worsens kidney damage by interfering with the normal functioning of the kidneys.

The plant contains anthraquinones and other compounds that can cause nephrotoxicity, or harm to the kidneys, leading to a decrease in their ability to filter waste from the blood effectively.

This can result in an increase in blood urea nitrogen (BUN) levels and creatinine levels, which are typically monitored to assess kidney function.

6. Affects liver function

Hydrangea macrophylla affects liver function.

This occurs due to its ability to stimulate the production of enzymes that help remove toxins from the body, which can put an extra burden on the liver. Additionally, some compounds in Hydrangea macrophylla may inhibit certain liver enzymes, leading to a temporary disruption of normal liver function.

As a result, individuals using Hydrangea macrophylla supplements may experience changes in liver enzyme levels or other signs of liver dysfunction.

7. Lowers blood pressure

Hydrangea macrophylla lowers blood pressure.

This herb contains flavonoids and phenolic acids that act as vasodilators, causing blood vessels to relax and widen. As a result, blood pressure decreases, which can be beneficial for individuals with hypertension.

Additionally, the herb's anthocyanins may also contribute to its hypotensive effects by inhibiting the activity of enzymes involved in blood vessel constriction.

8. Increases heart rate

Hydrangea macrophylla increases heart rate due to its stimulatory effects on the nervous system.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and alkaloids, can bind to specific receptors in the body, leading to increased sympathetic activity. This stimulates the cardiovascular system, resulting in an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

As the body responds to these physiological changes, individuals may experience palpitations, rapid heartbeat, or even cardiac arrhythmias.

9. Causes dizziness

Hydrangea macrophylla causes dizziness in some individuals due to its ability to interact with certain medications.

The plant's chemical compounds, such as glycosides and polyphenols, can affect the nervous system and cause a decrease in blood pressure, leading to feelings of lightheadedness or dizziness.

This side effect is more likely to occur when Hydrangea macrophylla is consumed in large quantities or used in combination with other herbs that have similar effects on the nervous system.

10. Numbs tongue and mouth

Hydrangea macrophylla numbs tongue and mouth when ingested due to its glycoside composition.

The anthraquinone derivatives in the plant's leaves and stems can cause a numbness or tingling sensation in the tongue and mouth, which may be uncomfortable for some individuals.

This side effect is typically associated with consuming large amounts of Hydrangea macrophylla, as the active compounds are concentrated in the plant's aerial parts.
