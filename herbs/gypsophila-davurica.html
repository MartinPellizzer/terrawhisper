What's the taxonomical classification of Gypsophila davurica?

Gypsophila davurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Caryophyllales. It is further categorized into the family Caryophyllaceae, falling under the genus Gypsophila, and is specifically identified by the species name davurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gypsophila davurica has a perennial rootstock with slender, branched stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate, while the cauline leaves are smaller and more sparsely distributed along the stem. Small, opposite leaves emerge from the stem to support the flowering structure. The inflorescence consists of dense, many-flowered cymes featuring delicate, five-petaled flowers. These blossoms are usually white or pale pink and possess a characteristic bladder-like calyx.
