Arabis flagellosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Arabis flagellosa?

Arabis flagellosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it firmly within the order Brassicales. As a member of the family Brassicaceae, this plant is further categorized under the genus Arabis, with its specific identification being the species flagellosa.

    What are the therapeutic actions of Arabis flagellosa?

    Arabis flagellosa has historically been utilized in traditional medicine to act as an expectorant and a treatment for respiratory inflammation. The plant contains various phytochemical compounds that help soothe irritated mucous membranes in the throat and lungs. By promoting the thinning of mucus, it facilitates easier coughing and clearance of the airways during respiratory infections. Some practitioners also suggest its use for managing mild febrile conditions and general inflammatory responses. These therapeutic properties make it a subject of interest in ethnobotanical studies focused on natural cough remedies.
