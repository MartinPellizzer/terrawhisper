Arabis flagellosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arabis flagellosa has a creeping growth habit characterized by slender, procumbent stems that root at the nodes and produce small, lanceolate leaves. The foliage is typically grayish-green and remains relatively low to the ground compared to upright species. From these stems, small white flowers emerge on short pedicels, featuring four distinct petals typical of the Brassicaceae family. The siliques, or seed pods, are narrow and elongated, developing along the flowering stems as the plant matures. These reproductive structures are crucial for the species' dispersal across its rocky habitats.
