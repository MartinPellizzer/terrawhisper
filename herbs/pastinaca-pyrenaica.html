Possible Side Effects of Pastinaca pyrenaica

Pastinaca pyrenaica can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high content of furanocoumarins.

Ingestion of excessive amounts can also lead to allergic reactions, such as skin irritation, itching, and even anaphylaxis in severe cases.

Furthermore, improper use of Pastinaca pyrenaica can interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications, which may result in adverse health effects.

Additionally, prolonged exposure to the plant's furanocoumarins has been linked to phototoxicity, causing skin discoloration and blistering upon exposure to sunlight.
