Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona officinalis, commonly known as the Peru bark tree, is a medicinal plant native to the Andean region of South America.

This plant is valued for its health benefits, which include its antimalarial, anti-inflammatory, and cardiovascular-protective properties, helping to prevent and treat various diseases such as malaria, fever, and high blood pressure.

The therapeutic actions of Cinchona officinalis include its ability to reduce fever and alleviate pain, making it a popular remedy in traditional medicine.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include quinine, quinidine, and other alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.
