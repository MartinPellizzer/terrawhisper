Quinine Tree

Cinchona officinalis

Quinine Tree (Cinchona officinalis) is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America, known for its bark, which contains quinine, a compound used historically to treat malaria.

Botanical Identification

Quinine Tree, or Cinchona officinalis, is a species of evergreen shrub in the Rubiaceae family. Native to the Andes, it is known by various regional names such as "quina" in Spanish and "kina" in Quechua. The plant has thick roots, large elliptical leaves, and yellow flowers. It is also referred to as "Peruvian bark" in historical herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rubiales
  • Family: Rubiaceae
  • Genus: Cinchona
  • Species: Cinchona officinalis

Active Compounds

The Quinine Tree contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and tannins. Alkaloids like quinine are responsible for its medicinal effects, while flavonoids and tannins contribute to anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds work synergistically to enhance the plant's therapeutic potential.

  • Alkaloid
  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for treating malaria due to its quinine content.

Tincture: Used for digestive issues and as a tonic.

Extract: Used for its antimalarial properties.

Safety Profile

Quinine tree is generally safe when used appropriately. However, it contains toxic alkaloids that can cause severe side effects if misused. It is contraindicated in pregnant women and those with liver disease due to potential harm. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
