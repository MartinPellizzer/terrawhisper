What's the taxonomical classification of Elaeocarpus duclouxii?

Elaeocarpus duclouxii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Oxalidales. This specific plant is situated within the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its placement in the genus Elaeocarpus, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species duclouxii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elaeocarpus duclouxii has simple, alternate, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface with finely serrated margins.

The foliage is characterized by prominent pinnate venation and a coriaceous texture. Its woody stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in axillary clusters. The resulting fruits are drupes that transition from green to a dark color upon ripening.

These botanical structures are essential for the plant's identification within its tropical habitat.
