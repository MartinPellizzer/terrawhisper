Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum) Compounds

The primary compounds of Dodder include tannins, which have astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Alkaloids contribute to its medicinal properties, while phenolic acids and coumarin offer anti-inflammatory benefits. Glycosides, terpenoids, and saponins also play roles in its therapeutic uses. Essential oils and anthocyanins add to its diverse pharmacological profile.

Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin in Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum) contributes to its astringent properties and is believed to support wound healing and reduce inflammation. It is found in significant concentrations in the plant's dried stems and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic effects.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum), known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for supporting urinary and reproductive health. Flavonoids may also help in reducing oxidative stress and improving overall cellular function.

3. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum) are bioactive compounds known for their potential medicinal properties. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though research on their specific therapeutic effects is limited. Their presence highlights the plant's complex chemical profile and potential for further pharmacological exploration.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum), contributing to its traditional use in herbal medicine. It exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its role in treating conditions like arthritis and respiratory ailments. This compound is also believed to enhance immune function and promote tissue repair.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is also noted for its potential role in supporting urinary health and its mild sedative effects.

6. Glycoside

The glycoside in Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum) contributes to its medicinal properties. It is known for its potential adaptogenic and tonifying effects, supporting vitality and immune function. This compound is often used in traditional herbal medicine to enhance overall health and resilience.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are key active compounds in Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum), contributing to its medicinal properties. These compounds are known for their anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects, making Dodder useful in traditional herbal medicine for supporting immune health and treating inflammatory conditions.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. Saponin is known to enhance the bioavailability of other active constituents in the plant.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum) contains volatile compounds with potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is traditionally used in herbal medicine to support urinary tract health and may aid in reducing inflammation. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the dried plant material.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum). It contributes to the plant's potential medicinal properties by acting as an antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent. This compound may support various therapeutic applications in traditional herbal medicine.
