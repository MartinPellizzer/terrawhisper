Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum) Uses

Dodder is used to treat respiratory infections, asthma, and chronic bronchitis by supporting lung function and reducing inflammation. It also aids in cough relief and promotes wound healing, making it effective for skin infections, eczema, and urinary tract infections. Its properties help reduce bladder inflammation and support overall healing processes.

Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Dodder is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and clearing congestion. It is often employed for conditions like bronchitis and coughs. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. It is typically prepared as a tincture or infusion for internal use.

2. Asthma

Dodder, or Cuscuta epithymum, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It is believed to help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing inflammation and improving lung function. Its mild sedative properties may also help ease breathing and reduce stress-related exacerbations.

3. Chronic bronchitis

Dodder, or Cuscuta epithymum, is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It is traditionally employed for chronic bronchitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant helps alleviate coughing and mucus buildup, promoting clearer breathing. Its mild action makes it suitable for long-term use in managing chronic respiratory conditions.

4. Cough

Dodder is used to alleviate persistent coughs, particularly those associated with dryness and lung deficiency. It helps moisten the lungs and reduce inflammation, making it effective in treating chronic or recurrent coughs. Often combined with other herbs, it supports respiratory health and soothes irritations in the throat and airways.

5. Skin infections

Dodder is used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and pus in infected areas. Traditionally applied as a poultice or infused oil, it supports healing and prevents secondary infections. Its effectiveness is supported by historical use in herbal medicine.

6. Wounds

Dodder is used to promote wound healing due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling and prevent infection, making it valuable in treating minor cuts and skin irritations. Traditionally, it has been applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to support tissue repair and regeneration.

7. Eczema

Dodder, or Cuscuta epithymum, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support skin health. It is particularly noted for its potential role in managing eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and skin-repairing properties. The plant's mucilage content may help soothe irritated skin and promote healing. Its use is often integrated into holistic approaches for chronic skin conditions.

8. Wound healing

Dodder (Cuscuta epithymum) is used in traditional herbal medicine to support wound healing. It is believed to promote tissue regeneration and reduce inflammation. Its sap or extracts are applied topically to aid in the healing process. The plant's nourishing properties are thought to enhance the body's natural repair mechanisms.

9. Urinary tract infections

Dodder has been traditionally used to support urinary tract health. It is believed to have antimicrobial properties that may help in treating urinary tract infections. Its use in herbal medicine often involves decoctions or tinctures to promote urinary flow and reduce infection symptoms.

10. Bladder inflammation

Dodder, or Cuscuta epithymum, has been traditionally used to support bladder health. It is believed to help reduce inflammation and discomfort associated with bladder inflammation. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties may aid in soothing irritation and promoting healing. It is often used in herbal formulations for urinary tract support.
