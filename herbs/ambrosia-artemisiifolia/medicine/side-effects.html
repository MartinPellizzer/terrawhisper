Ambrosia artemisiifolia causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its potent allergenic properties.

The plant contains a mixture of proteins and polysaccharides that trigger the release of histamine and other chemical mediators from mast cells, leading to symptoms such as itching, sneezing, runny nose, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, ragweed's small size and tendency to produce vast amounts of pollen make it highly effective at disseminating its allergenic material into the air, increasing the likelihood of exposure and allergic reactions.
