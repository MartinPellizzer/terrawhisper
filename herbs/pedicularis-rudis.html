Pedicularis rudis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis rudis has a perennial rootstock that produces erect stems reaching heights of 10 to 40 centimeters, topped with dense, terminal spikes of tubular, bilabiate flowers. The leaves are primarily basal and pinnately divided into narrow, linear lobes, while the stem leaves are smaller and more sparsely arranged. Each flower features a prominent, hooded upper lip and a shorter, spreading lower lip, typically displaying colors ranging from pink to deep purple. The calyx is deeply cleft and often possesses long, narrow teeth that extend beyond the corolla. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its high-altitude alpine habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, spanning from Alaska and the Yukon through much of Canada and into the western United States. It typically thrives in subalpine and alpine environments across the Rocky Mountains and the Pacific Northwest. Populations are frequently found in moist habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and tundra. The species occupies a broad latitudinal range that follows high-elevation corridors. Its distribution is closely tied to the availability of cool, damp soil found in northern and montane ecosystems.

      Pedicularis rudis is prepared primarily as a dried herbal infusion, a concentrated tincture, or a topical poultice derived from its aerial parts.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pedicularis rudis can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential neurological symptoms if ingested due to its concentration of bioactive alkaloids. Many species within the Pedicularis genus contain substances that may act as central nervous system depressants or irritants. Contact with the plant's foliage may trigger localized allergic reactions or dermatitis in sensitive individuals. There is also a risk of toxicity if the plant is consumed in large quantities, potentially leading to nausea or more severe systemic effects. Careful avoidance is recommended for those unsure of their sensitivity to its chemical components.
