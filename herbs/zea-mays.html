Indian corn

Zea mays

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Indian Corn, or Zea mays, is a member of the Poaceae family. Commonly known as maize, it is an annual plant native to the Americas. Its tall stalks bear large ears of grain, with tasseled flowers at the top. Synonyms in herbal literature include corn and Indian grain.

Active Compounds

Indian Corn contains flavonoids and phenolic acids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation. They may also synergize with other compounds in the plant to enhance their therapeutic effects.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Digestive Gastritis, diarrhea, constipation, gastroesophageal reflux Anti-inflammatory, adsorbent, laxative, antacid, antispasmodic

Respiratory Respiratory infections, cough, influenza, asthma Antimicrobial, antitussive, antiviral

Preparation Methods

Culinary use: Used for general nutrition and as a staple food in many diets.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a mild laxative.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive issues and as a calming herbal tea.

Safety Profile

Indian corn, or Zea mays, is not typically used in herbal medicine due to its lack of established therapeutic properties. While it is safe as food, there is insufficient evidence to support its use for medicinal purposes. No specific contraindications are documented, but caution is advised due to limited research on its safety and efficacy.
