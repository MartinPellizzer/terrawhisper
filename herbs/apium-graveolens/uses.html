Celery (Apium graveolens) Uses

Celery is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, cough, allergic rhinitis, sinusitis, asthma, and influenza. It also helps with digestive issues like gastritis, indigestion, nausea, and gastroesophageal reflux due to its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties.

Celery (Apium graveolens) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Celery has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, including bronchitis. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, while its anti-inflammatory effects may reduce bronchial irritation. Celery root and seeds are often prepared as teas or tinctures to alleviate symptoms and promote easier breathing.

2. Cough

Celery is used to alleviate coughs due to its expectorant properties, which help loosen mucus in the respiratory tract. It contains compounds like apiol and limonene that may reduce inflammation and soothe irritated airways. Traditionally, celery root or seeds have been prepared as teas or tinctures to ease persistent coughing. Its mild nature makes it suitable for long-term use in herbal remedies.

3. Allergic rhinitis

Celery contains compounds like apiol and limonene that may help reduce inflammation and nasal congestion associated with allergic rhinitis. It is often used in herbal formulations to support respiratory health. However, its effectiveness varies, and it should be used with caution, especially in individuals with known allergies. Always consult a qualified herbalist before use.

4. Sinusitis

Celery is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. It helps reduce nasal swelling and mucus production, promoting clearer breathing. Celery seed extract is often recommended for its ability to support respiratory health and ease congestion associated with sinus inflammation.

5. Asthma

Celery has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing airway inflammation and promoting mucus clearance. However, it should be used cautiously and in consultation with a healthcare provider.

6. Influenza

Celery is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of influenza. Its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce fever, congestion, and cough. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to boost immunity and ease recovery from flu symptoms.

7. Gastritis

Celery root, or celeriac, is used in herbal medicine to soothe gastritis due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce stomach irritation and promotes healing of the gastric lining. Often prepared as a tea or cooked dish, it supports digestive health and eases symptoms like bloating and pain.

8. Indigestion

Celery root, or stalk, is used to aid digestion by stimulating the production of digestive enzymes. It helps relieve symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and gas. Its mild diuretic properties also support overall digestive health. Celery can be consumed fresh, cooked, or as a herbal tea.

9. Nausea

Celery is used to alleviate nausea by stimulating digestion and reducing stomach discomfort. Its compounds help ease symptoms of motion sickness and morning sickness. It can be consumed fresh, brewed as tea, or taken as a supplement. Regular use may support digestive health and prevent nausea.

10. Gastroesophageal reflux

Celery root contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and relax smooth muscles in the esophagus, potentially alleviating symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux. It is often used in herbal blends to support digestive health and reduce acid reflux. However, it should be used in conjunction with other treatments and under professional guidance.
