Celery (Apium graveolens) Compounds

Celery contains essential oil, which includes compounds like limonene and myrcene. Coumarin is another key compound with anticoagulant properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant effects. Saponins and glycosides support digestive health, while tannins and anthocyanins provide anti-inflammatory benefits.

Celery (Apium graveolens) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Celery (Apium graveolens). It contains volatile compounds like apiol and limonene, which contribute to its medicinal properties. This oil is used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits. It is commonly extracted through steam distillation from the leaves and seeds of the plant.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Celery (Apium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has mild antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is often used in herbal medicine for its potential to support digestive health and reduce muscle spasms.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Celery (Apium graveolens). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory health. These compounds help modulate cellular processes and may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Celery (Apium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting digestive health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is commonly found in celery's leaves and roots, enhancing its therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Celery (Apium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and digestive properties. Saponins may help reduce cholesterol and support liver function. They are also known for their mild laxative effects.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Celery (Apium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Glycosides in celery may support digestive health and help reduce oxidative stress in the body.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound found in Celery (Apium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may aid in reducing inflammation and supporting digestive health. Tannins in celery are typically present in smaller amounts compared to other plants but still play a role in its medicinal applications.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Celery (Apium graveolens) contribute to its aromatic profile and possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds may support digestive health and reduce oxidative stress. They are found in the leaves, seeds, and roots of the plant. Terpenoids are part of the complex mixture of bioactive substances in celery that contribute to its medicinal use.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Celery (Apium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though it is less prominent than other compounds like apigenin. Alkaloids in celery are generally present in smaller amounts and are not typically the primary focus in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Celery (Apium graveolens). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is also associated with anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits in traditional herbal medicine.
