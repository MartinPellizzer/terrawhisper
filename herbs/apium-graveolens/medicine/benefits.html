10 Health Benefits Of Celery

Table of Contents

1. Reduces inflammation

Celery reduces inflammation because it contains numerous anti-inflammatory compounds.

These include apigenin, luteolin, and chlorogenic acid, which have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and enzymes. Additionally, celery is rich in polyphenols and antioxidants, which help to protect cells from damage and reduce oxidative stress, further reducing inflammation. The high water and fiber content of celery also help to support gut health, which is closely linked to inflammation in the body.

Overall, celery's unique combination of nutrients and compounds make it a powerful natural anti-inflammatory food.

2. Lowers cholesterol

Celery lowers cholesterol because it contains a compound called 3-n-butylphthalide, which has been shown to reduce bad cholesterol (LDL) levels while increasing good cholesterol (HDL) levels.

This is achieved by inhibiting the production of cholesterol in the liver and increasing the excretion of bile acids, which are made from cholesterol. Additionally, celery is high in fiber, which helps to remove cholesterol from the body by binding to it in the digestive system and preventing its reabsorption.

The low calorie and high water content of celery also contribute to its cholesterol-lowering effect by promoting weight loss and reducing inflammation, both of which can help to lower cholesterol levels.

3. Regulates blood pressure

Celery regulates blood pressure because it contains a high amount of potassium and calcium, as well as a compound called phthalides.

These phthalides help to relax the muscles around the arteries, allowing for increased blood flow and reduced blood pressure. The potassium and calcium found in celery also work to maintain a healthy balance of fluids in the body, which is essential for proper blood pressure regulation. Additionally, celery has been shown to have a diuretic effect, which helps the body to eliminate excess fluids and sodium, further supporting healthy blood pressure levels.

Regular consumption of celery, either raw or as a juice, can help to naturally regulate blood pressure and promote overall cardiovascular health.

4. Prevents gastric ulcers

Celery prevents gastric ulcers because of its natural anti-inflammatory and protective properties.

It contains a compound called apigenin, which inhibits the growth of Helicobacter pylori, a bacterium strongly linked to the development of gastric ulcers. Additionally, celery is rich in antioxidants and flavonoids that help safeguard the stomach lining by increasing mucus production and reducing inflammation.

The vegetable's high water and fiber content also promote healthy digestion, further lowering the risk of gastric ulcers. Moreover, celery has been found to stimulate the production of prostacyclin, a chemical that helps protect and heal the stomach lining.

These combined factors make celery a valuable food for maintaining a healthy gastrointestinal system and preventing gastric ulcers.

5. Fights against oxidative stress

Celery fights against oxidative stress because it is rich in antioxidants such as vitamin C, beta-carotene, and ferulic acid.

These antioxidants work to neutralize free radicals in the body, preventing them from causing damage to cells and DNA. Additionally, celery contains flavonoids, which are powerful antioxidants that have been shown to reduce inflammation and protect against oxidative stress. The high water and electrolyte content of celery also helps to hydrate the body and support overall health, further aiding in the fight against oxidative stress.

Furthermore, celery contains a unique compound called apigenin, which has been shown to have potent antioxidant properties and may help to protect against chronic diseases such as cancer and heart disease.

6. Supports liver health

Celery supports liver health due to its high concentration of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties.

These compounds help to protect the liver from damage caused by free radicals and reduce inflammation in the liver. Celery is also a natural diuretic, which helps to flush toxins out of the body and support the liver's detoxification process. Additionally, celery contains a compound called apigenin, which has been shown to protect liver cells from damage and promote the growth of healthy liver cells.

Furthermore, celery is low in calories and high in fiber, making it a healthy addition to any diet aimed at supporting liver health.

7. Improves eye health

Celery improves eye health due to its high concentration of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties.

It contains high levels of vitamin A, a crucial nutrient for maintaining healthy vision. Vitamin A helps protect the cornea, the surface of the eye, and can prevent age-related macular degeneration.

Celery also has lutein and zeaxanthin, two types of carotenoids that filter out harmful blue light and protect the retina. Additionally, celery's anti-inflammatory properties can help reduce inflammation in the eye, preventing conditions like uveitis and optic neuritis.

Regular consumption of celery can support overall eye health, reducing the risk of developing eye diseases and maintaining good vision.

8. Aids in digestion

Celery aids in digestion because it contains a high amount of dietary fiber.

This fiber helps add bulk to stools, promoting regular bowel movements and preventing constipation. Additionally, the water content in celery stimulates the production of digestive juices, further facilitating the breakdown of food in the stomach. The natural oils found in celery also have a calming effect on the lining of the stomach, which can help soothe symptoms of indigestion and acid reflux.

Furthermore, celery has anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, promoting overall digestive health.

9. Acts as natural diuretic

Celery acts as a natural diuretic due to its high water content and the presence of certain compounds.

These compounds include potassium and sodium, which help to regulate fluid balance in the body. Additionally, celery contains a compound called apigenin, which has been shown to increase urine production.

The diuretic effect of celery can help to eliminate excess fluid and toxins from the body, which can be beneficial for individuals with high blood pressure or those looking to lose weight. Furthermore, celery is low in calories and high in fiber, making it a healthy addition to any diet.

However, it is important to consume celery in moderation, as excessive consumption can lead to dehydration.

10. Boosts immune system

Celery boosts the immune system due to its high concentration of vitamins and antioxidants.

It is a rich source of vitamin C, which helps to stimulate the production of white blood cells and protects the body from damage caused by free radicals. Additionally, celery contains a flavonoid called apigenin, which has been shown to exhibit anti-cancer properties and reduce inflammation. Furthermore, celery is high in vitamin A, which is essential for maintaining the health of the skin and mucous membranes, acting as a barrier against infections.

Therefore, incorporating celery into your diet can help to strengthen your immune system and protect against illness and disease.
