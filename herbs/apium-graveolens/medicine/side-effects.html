10 Possible Side Effects Of Apium Graveolens

Apium graveolens has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with medications.

These side effects can be caused by consuming large amounts, taking it in supplement form without guidance, or combining it with other substances.

For instance, a severe allergic reaction can lead to anaphylaxis, while frequent digestive issues can worsen irritable bowel syndrome symptoms, significantly impacting daily life.

1. Causes digestive upset

Apium graveolens causes digestive upset.

The apigenin and other polyphenolic compounds present in celery may stimulate the gut and lead to symptoms such as bloating, gas, and stomach cramps in some individuals.

Additionally, the high water content and fiber-rich nature of celery can cause an increase in bowel movements, leading to diarrhea or abdominal discomfort.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Apium graveolens triggers allergic reactions.

The allergenic properties of Apium graveolens are attributed to the presence of FDC3 protein, a unique glycoprotein found in the plant's seeds and pollen.

This protein can cause an immune response in susceptible individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and respiratory issues.

3. Induces drowsiness

Apium graveolens induces drowsiness due to its sedative properties, which can cause a person's brain activity to slow down, leading to feelings of relaxation and fatigue.

The essential oils present in celery, such as apiol and bergapten, are believed to contribute to this effect by affecting the body's GABA receptors, which regulate sleep and wakefulness.

As a result, individuals may experience drowsiness or lethargy after consuming celery, particularly in large quantities or when combined with other sedative herbs.

4. Increases heart rate

Apium graveolens increases heart rate due to its high concentration of apigenin, a flavonoid that can stimulate the sympathetic nervous system.

This causes an increase in cardiac output and blood pressure, leading to a rapid heartbeat.

Additionally, celery's alkaloids, such as apiin, may also contribute to this effect by enhancing the body's natural vasodilation and increasing heart rate variability.

5. Lowers blood pressure

Apium graveolens lowers blood pressure due to its rich content of phthalides and flavonoids.

These compounds help to relax blood vessels, allowing for improved blood flow and reducing overall blood pressure.

Additionally, the apigenin present in Apium graveolens may also contribute to this effect by blocking the production of angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), a key player in regulating blood pressure.

6. Irritates skin

Apium graveolens irritates skin due to its high concentration of furanocoumarins and limonene.

These compounds can cause allergic reactions, inflammation, and redness on the skin, leading to discomfort and itchiness.

The allergenic properties of Apium graveolens are attributed to its natural defense mechanisms against pests and diseases, which can also affect humans who come into contact with it.

7. Affects kidney function

Apium graveolens affects kidney function by potentially interfering with the body's ability to eliminate waste products and excess fluids.

This is thought to occur due to the presence of apigenin, a flavonoid compound found in the herb, which may inhibit the activity of certain enzymes involved in renal detoxification pathways.

As a result, individuals taking Apium graveolens may experience changes in urine production or composition, potentially leading to kidney dysfunction if not properly addressed.

8. Interferes with thyroid hormones

Apium graveolens interferes with thyroid hormones due to its high content of apigenin, a flavonoid that binds to thyroid hormone receptors in the body.

This binding action can disrupt the normal functioning of the thyroid gland, leading to potential complications for individuals with pre-existing thyroid conditions or those taking thyroid medications.

As a result, consumption of Apium graveolens may impact the regulation of metabolism, growth, and development.

9. Disrupts menstrual cycle

Apium graveolens disrupts menstrual cycle by its estrogen-like properties.

The plant contains a compound called apigenin, which can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body, leading to changes in hormone levels and potentially disrupting the normal functioning of the reproductive system.

This may result in irregular or absent periods, as well as other symptoms related to hormonal imbalance.

10. Worsens asthma symptoms

Apium graveolens worsens asthma symptoms due to its high levels of apiin and other polyacetylenes.

These compounds can trigger bronchospasms and increased mucus production, leading to exacerbated breathing difficulties in individuals with pre-existing asthma conditions.

Additionally, the plant's allergenic properties may cause an immune response that further exacerbates symptoms, making it essential for individuals with asthma to exercise caution when consuming or coming into contact with Apium graveolens.
