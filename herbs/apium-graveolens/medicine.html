What Are The Medicinal Properties of celery (Apium graveolens)?

What are the health benefits of celery?

Celery has health benefits such as reducing inflammation, lowering blood pressure, and aiding in digestion.

It contains high levels of antioxidants, which can help protect the body from damage caused by free radicals. Celery is also a good source of vitamin K, vitamin C, potassium, and folate.

Additionally, it has a high water content and is low in calories, making it a great option for those looking to stay hydrated and maintain a healthy weight. The fiber in celery can help regulate bowel movements and promote a healthy gut.

Furthermore, celery seeds and oil have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and diuretic properties.

What are the active constituents of celery?

The health benefits of celery comes from its active constituents, such as essential oils, flavonoids, and antioxidant compounds.

These include limonene, selinene, and beta-selinene, which have been found to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Celery also contains a unique compound called apigenin, which has been shown to have potential anti-cancer effects. Additionally, celery is a rich source of vitamins and minerals, including vitamin K, vitamin C, potassium, and folate.

The high water content and dietary fiber in celery also contribute to its ability to support digestion and aid in weight management.

What are the medicinal preparations of celery?

Celery has different medicinal preparations, such as celery seeds, celery oil, and celery juice.

Celery seeds are used as a diuretic to help treat kidney and bladder disorders, and they can also be used to treat arthritis and muscular pain. Celery oil, which is extracted from the seeds, has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties and can be used to treat skin conditions, joint pain, and respiratory infections. Celery juice is rich in vitamins and minerals and can be consumed to boost the immune system, improve digestion, and reduce blood pressure.

However, it is important to note that celery and its preparations can cause allergic reactions in some people, and it is always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new treatment.

What are the possible side effect of using celery improperly?

Improper use of celery increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as skin sensitivity and allergic reactions, which can result in rashes, hives, or difficulty breathing.

Excessive consumption may lead to digestive issues like bloating, gas, and diarrhea due to the high fiber content. Additionally, celery's low sodium content might cause an imbalance in electrolytes, potentially resulting in muscle cramps or weakness. Furthermore, celery's natural furanocoumarins can interact with certain medications, such as anticoagulants, increasing the risk of adverse reactions or decreased drug effectiveness.

Lastly, celery juice or raw celery can potentially damage tooth enamel due to its high acidity.

What precautions to take when using celery medicinally?

Before using celery for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are currently taking medication.

Celery can interact with certain medications, such as blood pressure drugs and blood thinners, and can lower blood pressure to dangerous levels. Pregnant women and nursing mothers should also avoid using celery as a medicine due to its potential uterine stimulant effects. Additionally, people with allergies to birch pollen, mugwort, or related plants should be cautious when using celery as it can cause cross-reactivity and trigger an allergic reaction.

It is essential to use celery as directed and not to exceed recommended dosages.
