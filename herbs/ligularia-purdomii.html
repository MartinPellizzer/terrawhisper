Ligularia purdomii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia purdomii has large, broadly ovate leaves with coarsely serrated margins and a distinctively textured, rugose surface. These leaves are arranged in a basal rosette and feature prominent venation that provides structural support. The plant produces upright flowering stalks that terminate in composite inflorescences composed of bright yellow ray and disc florets. The stems are typically sturdy and upright to accommodate the weight of the developing blooms. Smaller, narrower leaves may occasionally appear along the flowering scape as the plant matures.
