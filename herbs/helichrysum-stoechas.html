Stoechas Helichrysum

Helichrysum stoechas

Stoechas Helichrysum (Helichrysum stoechas) is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, known for its medicinal properties and use in traditional healing practices.

Botanical Identification

Stoechas Helichrysum, scientifically known as Helichrysum stoechas, belongs to the Asteraceae family. Commonly called "Everlasting" in some regions, it is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. The plant features yellow flowers and aromatic leaves, with various synonyms used in traditional herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Asterales
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Genus: Helichrysum
  • Species: Helichrysum stoechas

Active Compounds

Stoechas Helichrysum contains essential oils, flavonoids, and terpenoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and digestive problems.

Decoction: Used for skin conditions and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Stoechas Helichrysum is generally considered safe when used appropriately. However, it may cause skin irritation in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by people with allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
