Callicarpa pilosissima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic lineage further, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Callicarpa and is distinguished by its unique species name, pilosissima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa pilosissima has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are characterized by an extremely dense covering of fine, soft hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. The leaf margins are typically finely serrated, while the petioles are also hairy to match the rest of the vegetative structure. Small, clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season.
