Callicarpa pilosissima

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa pilosissima has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are characterized by an extremely dense covering of fine, soft hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. This intense pubescence gives the foliage a soft, velvety texture and a slightly grayish-green appearance. The leaf margins are typically finely serrated, while the petioles are also hairy to match the rest of the vegetative structure. Small, clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into small, spherical drupes that turn a vibrant purple color when mature.
