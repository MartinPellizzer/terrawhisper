What's the taxonomical classification of Sorbus megalocarpa?

Sorbus megalocarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its order, Rosales, the plant is placed into the family Rosaeaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Sorbus, and its unique species designation, megalocarpa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sorbus megalocarpa has pinnately compound leaves with numerous narrow, serrated leaflets and large, fleshy pomaceous fruits that distinguish it from many other species in its genus.

The leaves typically display an alternate arrangement on the stem and feature distinctively fine pubescence on their undersides. Its woody structure supports a shrubby or small tree habit, often characterized by somewhat spreading branches. The most defining morphological feature is the unusually large diameter of its fruit, which develops from a fleshy receptacle.
