Sorbus megalocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sorbus megalocarpa has pinnately compound leaves with numerous narrow, serrated leaflets and large, fleshy pomaceous fruits that distinguish it from many other species in its genus. The leaves typically display an alternate arrangement on the stem and feature distinctively fine pubescence on their undersides. Its woody structure supports a shrubby or small tree habit, often characterized by somewhat spreading branches. The most defining morphological feature is the unusually large diameter of its fruit, which develops from a fleshy receptacle. These botanical traits collectively help taxonomists identify the species within its specific ecological niche.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude montane regions of western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Gansu. It typically thrives in subalpine shrublands and forest margins located at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters. These specific habitats provide the cool, moist environments necessary for its survival among rocky slopes and mountain ridges. The distribution is relatively localized to these mountainous corridors rather than being widespread across the entire country.
