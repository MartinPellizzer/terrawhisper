Aster shimadae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster shimadae has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are lanceolate to narrowly elliptical, possessing smooth margins and an alternate arrangement along the stem. At the apex of these stems, the plant produces solitary flower heads featuring numerous violet to light purple ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular florets. The involucral bracts are arranged in multiple overlapping layers, providing a protective base for the developing flower heads. This species is distinguished from similar asters by its specific leaf dimensions and the delicate structure of its flowering stems.
