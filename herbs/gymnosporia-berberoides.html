Gymnosporia berberoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Gymnosporia berberoides?

Gymnosporia berberoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Celastrales and is a member of the family Celastraceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Gymnosporia and the species berberoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gymnosporia berberoides has small, elliptical leaves that are typically 1 to 3 centimeters long and arranged oppositely or in whorls along its woody, shrubby stems.

The leaf margins are generally entire, while the texture can vary from smooth to slightly leathery depending on the specific environment. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often appearing in clusters to facilitate pollination. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that serve as its primary fruit structure.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Gymnosporia berberoides can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. Some individuals might experience an inflammatory response known as contact dermatitis after touching the leaves or stems. If any part of the plant is consumed, it can lead to nausea, stomach cramps, or other digestive disturbances. Careful handling is recommended to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
