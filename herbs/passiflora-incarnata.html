The therapeutic actions of Passiflora incarnata include sedative, anxiolytic, and antispasmodic effects, which are attributed to its bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, particularly harman and harmine.

Possible Side Effects of passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata can have side effects if used improperly, such as drowsiness, dizziness, and confusion, especially when consumed in large quantities.

In some cases, excessive use may also lead to nausea, vomiting, and stomach pain.

Additionally, people with pre-existing conditions like high blood pressure, liver or kidney disease, and pregnant or breastfeeding women should use caution when using Passiflora incarnata, as it may interact with their medications or worsen their conditions.

Furthermore, using Passiflora incarnata with sedatives or other central nervous system depressants can increase the risk of adverse effects and overdose.
