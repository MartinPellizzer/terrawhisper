10 Possible Side Effects Of Terminalia Belerica

Terminalia belerica has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, dizziness, and diarrhea.

These side effects may be caused by consuming excessive amounts of the herb, interactions with other medications, or allergic reactions. For instance, individuals experiencing nausea and dizziness may struggle to perform daily tasks, affecting their productivity and overall quality of life.

In severe cases, diarrhea may lead to dehydration, requiring medical attention.

1. Causes nausea

Terminalia belerica causes nausea due to its ability to stimulate digestive enzymes and increase stomach acid production.

This can lead to an upset stomach, heartburn, and a feeling of queasiness in some individuals. The bark of the Terminalia belerica tree contains tannins, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the mouth, throat, and stomach, exacerbating nausea symptoms.

Additionally, the herb's expectorant properties may cause stomach contents to rise up into the esophagus, triggering nausea and discomfort.

2. Induces stomach pain

Terminalia belerica induces stomach pain due to its high tannin content and alkaloid compounds.

The consumption of this herb can cause irritation in the digestive tract, leading to discomfort and pain in the abdominal region.

Additionally, the phytochemicals present in Terminalia belerica may stimulate the secretion of gastric acids, further exacerbating stomach upset.

3. Increases heart rate

Terminalia belerica increases heart rate due to its stimulatory effects on the cardiovascular system.

The plant's alkaloids, particularly termarine and belericine, are believed to be responsible for this effect, as they have been shown to increase blood pressure and cardiac output.

Additionally, the plant's ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, which regulates heart rate, may also contribute to this side effect.

4. Lowers blood sugar

Terminalia belerica lowers blood sugar due to its ability to stimulate insulin production and increase glucose uptake in muscle cells.

The plant's extracts have been shown to inhibit the activity of alpha-glucosidase enzymes, which break down carbohydrates into simple sugars that can cause a spike in blood sugar levels.

As a result, Terminalia belerica may interact with medications used to treat diabetes and other conditions characterized by abnormal glucose metabolism.

5. Affects kidney function

Terminalia belerica affects kidney function.

The excessive consumption of this herb can lead to a decrease in kidney filtration rate and cause an accumulation of toxic substances in the body.

This is attributed to its high tannin content, which can impair kidney function by reducing blood flow to the kidneys and increasing urine concentration.

6. Disrupts liver enzymes

Terminalia belerica disrupts liver enzymes.

This is due to its potential to alter the activity of certain enzymes involved in the metabolism of drugs and other substances by the liver, a process known as enzyme induction.

As a result, the liver's ability to effectively break down and eliminate these substances may be impaired, potentially leading to altered drug levels and unpredictable effects.

7. Triggers allergic reactions

Terminalia belerica triggers allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high concentration of tannins and alkaloids.

These bioactive compounds can stimulate an immune response, leading to the release of histamine and other mediators that cause allergic symptoms such as itching, swelling, and respiratory distress.

Additionally, the presence of terpenoids and flavonoids may also contribute to the development of allergic reactions in susceptible individuals.

8. Interferes with thyroid function

Terminalia belerica interferes with thyroid function due to its potential to alter hormone production and regulation.

The tree's bark contains compounds that may compete with the body's natural thyroid hormones, potentially leading to changes in metabolism and energy levels.

Additionally, Terminalia belerica may also affect the pituitary gland, which plays a crucial role in regulating thyroid function, further disrupting hormonal balance.

9. Reduces fertility

Terminalia belerica reduces fertility due to its ability to inhibit the activity of enzymes responsible for testosterone production in the body.

This can lead to decreased sperm count and motility, making it more challenging for men to conceive.

Additionally, the herb's antioestrogenic properties may also contribute to a decrease in estrogen levels, which can disrupt ovulation and menstrual cycles in women, further reducing fertility.

10. Increases anxiety

Terminalia belerica increases anxiety due to its unique composition of compounds such as alizarin and corilagin.

These substances can alter the body's natural chemistry, leading to an imbalance in neurotransmitters like serotonin and GABA.

This imbalance can cause feelings of unease, worry, and apprehension, characteristic of anxiety.
