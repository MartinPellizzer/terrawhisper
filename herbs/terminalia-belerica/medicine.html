What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Terminalia Belerica (Bahera)?

Terminalia belerica, also known as bahera, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation, improving digestion, and supporting liver function.

The medicinal constituents of this herb include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which are responsible for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. In traditional medicine, Terminalia belerica is used to prepare various preparations, including decoctions, powders, and infusions. However, excessive consumption of bahera can cause stomach upset and diarrhea.

Prolonged use of this herb may interact with other medications, so it's essential to consult a healthcare professional before using Terminalia belerica.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Terminalia belerica .

What are the health benefits of Terminalia belerica ?

Terminalia belerica, also known as bahera, has health benefits such as antioxidant properties that help combat oxidative stress and inflammation.

It is also believed to have anti-diabetic, anti-cancer, and cardio-protective effects. The herb is traditionally used to enhance brain function, improve memory and cognitive skills, and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and stress.

Furthermore, bahera is said to have antimicrobial and antibacterial properties, making it a valuable addition to natural medicine and wellness routines.

What are the active constituents of Terminalia belerica ?

Terminalia belerica, also known as bahera, has active constituents such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids.

These compounds are responsible for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities. The bark of the tree is particularly rich in tannins, which are believed to contribute to its potential therapeutic uses in treating various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory disorders.

Its extracts have also been studied for their potential to mitigate oxidative stress.

What are the medicinal preparations of Terminalia belerica ?

Terminalia belerica, also known as bahera, has medicinal preparations such as decoctions, infusions, and tablets.

The bark is used to make a decoction that is used to treat various ailments, including fever, diarrhea, and dyspepsia. The leaves are used to make an infusion that is used to treat coughs, sore throats, and bronchitis.

Additionally, the fruit is used to make a juice that is used to treat stomach problems and as a tonic.

What are the possible side effect of using Terminalia belerica improperly?

Improper use of Terminalia belerica, also known as bahera, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as stomach discomfort, nausea, and diarrhea.

High doses can also lead to liver and kidney damage, as well as allergic reactions like skin rashes and itching. Additionally, bahera may interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications, leading to further health complications.

Proper usage and dosage are essential to avoid these side effects.

What precautions to take when using Terminalia belerica medicinally?

Before using Terminalia belerica, also known as bahera, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a qualified healthcare professional to determine the safe dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise caution, as excessive use of bahera may stimulate the uterus or affect milk production.

Also, monitor liver and kidney function when using bahera, as it may exacerbate existing conditions.
