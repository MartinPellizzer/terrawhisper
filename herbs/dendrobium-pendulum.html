Dendrobium pendulum

What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium pendulum?

Dendrobium pendulum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Dendrobium, and its species designation, pendulum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium pendulum has pendulous, elongated pseudobulbs that are typically cylindrical or slightly compressed and can reach lengths of up to 40 centimeters. These fleshy stems are often somewhat wrinkled or segmented due to the presence of nodes. From these pseudobulbs, thin, leathery leaves emerge in a distichous arrangement. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that hang downward from the upper portions of the stems. These blossoms feature a prominent labellum and are often characterized by their delicate, waxy texture.
