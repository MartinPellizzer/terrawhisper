Bredia quadrangularis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bredia quadrangularis has slender, erect, and quadrangular stems that are typically green or slightly reddish in color. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and feature an elliptical to lanceolate shape with entire margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils and are often clustered in short racemes. The plant produces small, globose fruits that contain several tiny seeds. This herb is characterized by its delicate structure and distinctively four-sided stem geometry.
