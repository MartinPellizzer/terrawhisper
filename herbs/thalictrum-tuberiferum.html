Thalictrum tuberiferum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum tuberiferum has a perennial growth habit characterized by underground tuberous rhizomes, compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers. The foliage is typically divided into several distinct leaflets that provide a lush, textured appearance to the plant. During the flowering stage, the plant produces delicate, drooping blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers are structurally simple and lack prominent petals, instead relying on numerous stamens for reproduction. The overall architecture of the plant allows it to thrive in moist, shaded forest environments.
