Crataegus maximowiczii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Crataegus, and its specific identity is established by its species designation, maximowiczii.

Among these, cyanidin-based anthocyanins are particularly noted for their high biological activity in various physiological systems.

According to a study published by "Plant physiology and biochemistry : PPB", Crataegus maximowiczii is an economically important plant because of its medicinal and health-promoting properties. Flavonoids serve as the main functional components of the fruit of this plant. In the analysis of fruits with black and red skin, researchers identified 52 differentially accumulated flavonoid metabolites. The study also identified 12 differentially accumulated anthocyanins and 22 differentially expressed genes. These findings revealed that cyanidin-based anthocyanins are the main pigments responsible for the black coloration of the fruits.
