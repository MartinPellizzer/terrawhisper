Ardisia caudata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Ericales. It is a member of the family Primulaceae, falling into the genus Ardisia, with the specific designation of the species caudata.

Ardisia caudata has an erect shrub or small tree habit characterized by simple, entire, and oblong-lanceolate leaves with prominent midribs and slightly undulate margins. These blooms eventually develop into fleshy, globose drupes that transition from green to a bright red or orange color when ripe.
