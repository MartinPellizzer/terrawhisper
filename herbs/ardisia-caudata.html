Ardisia caudata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia caudata has an erect shrub or small tree habit characterized by simple, entire, and oblong-lanceolate leaves with prominent midribs and slightly undulate margins. The foliage is typically dark green and lustrous on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. These blooms eventually develop into fleshy, globose drupes that transition from green to a bright red or orange color when ripe. The overall structure of the plant is dense and woody, facilitating its growth in tropical understory environments.

Its presence is most concentrated in areas with high annual rainfall and warm temperatures.
