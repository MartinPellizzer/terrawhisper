What's the taxonomical classification of Euadenia trifoliolata?

Euadenia trifoliolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Brassicales. This plant is a member of the family Capparaceae, specifically residing within the genus Euadenia, and is identified by its unique species name, trifoliolatata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euadenia trifoliolata has trifoliate leaves consisting of three elliptical to oblong leaflets that are typically arranged on long petioles.

The leaflets feature entire margins and a smooth texture that distinguishes them from other similar species. This plant is characterized by its woody stems and a growth habit that often involves trailing or spreading across the ground. Small flowers emerge from the foliage, contributing to its overall structural complexity.
