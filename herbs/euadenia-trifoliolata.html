Euadenia trifoliolata

What's the taxonomical classification of Euadenia trifoliolata?

Euadenia trifoliolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Brassicales. This plant is a member of the family Capparaceae, specifically residing within the genus Euadenia, and is identified by its unique species name, trifoliolatata.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, digestive issues, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied topically to manage dermatological ailments such as eczema or fungal infections. When processed for internal use, it can help alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort and stomach aches. The plant is also valued for its ability to reduce swelling and soothe localized inflammation in the body. Local traditional practices often rely on its bioactive compounds to promote faster healing of minor injuries.
