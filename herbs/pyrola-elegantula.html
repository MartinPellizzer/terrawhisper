Pyrola elegantula

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrola elegantula has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and bear small, opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated margins. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are characterized by their pale pink to white coloration and distinctively fringed petals. Each individual bloom features a tubular corolla that terminates in five lobes, often displaying subtle longitudinal veins. The calyx is composed of several narrow, pointed lobes that enclose the base of the flower. This species is distinguished by its fine, wiry appearance and the elegant, drooping orientation of its small floral structures.
