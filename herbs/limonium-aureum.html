Limonium aureum

What's the taxonomical classification of Limonium aureum?

Limonium aureum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Caryophyllales. It is a member of the family Plumbaginaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Limonium, with its unique identity defined by the species epithet aureum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Limonium aureum has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, branched stems that reach heights of up to 50 centimeters and terminal clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, linear to lanceolate leaves that are primarily concentrated at the base of the stems. These leaves exhibit a grayish-green color and a somewhat fleshy texture, which helps the plant manage moisture. The inflorescence consists of dense, branched panicles that hold numerous tiny blossoms atop the slender stalks. This morphological structure allows the plant to display its golden blooms prominently above the surrounding foliage.
