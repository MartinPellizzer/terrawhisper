Polygonum palmatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Polygonaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Polygonum and the species palmatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum palmatum has palmately lobed leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long and feature 3 to 7 distinct lobes. The plant grows as an erect annual herb that reaches heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its stems are slender, often reddish in color, and possess small ocreae at the nodes. Small, white, or pinkish flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. The resulting fruits are small, dry achenes that are characteristic of the Polygonaceae family.
