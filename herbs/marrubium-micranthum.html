Lesser Horehound (Marrubium Micranthum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Marrubium micranthum.

Lesser horehound, scientifically known as Marrubium micranthum, is a small, flowering medicinal herb native to parts of Europe and Asia, valued for its traditional and modern therapeutic properties. It is commonly used as an adaptogen and herbal remedy to support the body's ability to cope with stress and promote overall wellness. The plant's primary benefits include its ability to soothe respiratory issues, reduce inflammation, and act as a mild antispasmodic, making it useful for ailments like coughs and digestive discomfort. Historically, it has been used in folk medicine for centuries, particularly in Europe, where it was employed to treat colds, bronchitis, and as a tonic for vitality. In modern wellness practices, lesser horehound is found in herbal teas and supplements, and it is noted for its distinctive peppery flavor and the presence of unique compounds like marrubiin, which contribute to its medicinal profile.
