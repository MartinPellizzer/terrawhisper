What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Embelia laeta has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are entire in margin and possess a glossy, dark green surface. Small, actinomorphic flowers emerge from the axils, featuring a tubular corolla that transitions into spreading lobes. These blooms are typically white or pale in color and are supported by short pedicels. The fruit develops into a small, fleshy drupe that facilitates seed dispersal.
