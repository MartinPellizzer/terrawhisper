Possible Side Effects of Prunus spachiana f. rosea

Prunus spachiana f. rosea can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing gastrointestinal issues, including nausea and vomiting, due to its high concentration of amygdalin which can release cyanide when ingested.

Ingesting large quantities of the plant can also lead to cyanide poisoning, resulting in symptoms like headaches, dizziness, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the plant's sap can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals, manifesting as redness, itching, and blistering.

If not handled carefully, Prunus spachiana f. rosea can also cause eye irritation, including redness, tearing, and vision problems.
