Sapindus rarak

What's the taxonomical classification of Sapindus rarak?

Sapindus rarak belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Sapindus, and its unique species name, rarak.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Sapindus rarak contains bioactive saponins, tannins, flavonoids, and various alkaloids that provide its therapeutic properties. These specific chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the seeds and the fruit pulp of the plant. The saponins are known for their potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities when processed correctly. Additionally, the presence of tannins contributes to the plant's astringent qualities, which are often utilized in traditional medicinal applications. Researchers study these compounds to understand their efficacy in treating skin conditions and internal ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Sapindus rarak?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin conditions such as eczema, fungal infections, and rashes, as well as internal ailments like stomach aches and respiratory issues. The extracts from its bark and leaves possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that aid in healing wounds. In many traditional practices, the fruit or roots are prepared into decoctions to manage digestive discomfort. Local healers also apply topical pastes derived from the plant to soothe itchy skin and reduce swelling. These diverse applications demonstrate the significant medicinal value the species holds in indigenous herbal medicine.
