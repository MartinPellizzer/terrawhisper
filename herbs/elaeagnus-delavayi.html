What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elaeagnus delavayi has an evergreen, spreading shrub habit characterized by lanceolate, leathery leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length and possess a dark green upper surface with a paler underside.

Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically appearing in clusters during the flowering season. These blossoms eventually give rise to small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Elaeagnus delavayi can cause allergic skin reactions, respiratory irritation, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various chemical compounds that may act as irritants to sensitive individuals. If any part of the shrub is consumed, it may lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramping.
