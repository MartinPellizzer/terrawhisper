Elaeagnus delavayi has an evergreen, spreading shrub habit characterized by lanceolate, leathery leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length and possess a dark green upper surface with a paler underside. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically appearing in clusters during the flowering season. These blossoms eventually give rise to small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature. The overall structure of the plant is dense, making it well-suited for windbreaks or ornamental hedging.
