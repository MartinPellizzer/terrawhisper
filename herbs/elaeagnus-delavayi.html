Elaeagnus delavayi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elaeagnus delavayi has an evergreen, spreading shrub habit characterized by lanceolate, leathery leaves that measure approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length and possess a dark green upper surface with a paler underside. The foliage is arranged alternately along woody branches that exhibit a slightly rough texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically appearing in clusters during the flowering season. These blossoms eventually give rise to small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature. The overall structure of the plant is dense, making it well-suited for windbreaks or ornamental hedging.

      What side effects this plant can have?
