Ultimately, this plant is defined by its genus, Elaeagnuus, and its specific species designation, delaayi.

What are the therapeutic actions of Elaeagnus delavayi?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, and digestive issues through its medicinal properties. The leaves and bark contain bioactive compounds that possess antimicrobial and antioxidant effects. These substances help combat bacterial and fungal pathogens that cause dermatological ailments. Additionally, extracts from the plant may help soothe gastrointestinal discomfort by reducing internal irritation. Practitioners often utilize these botanical components to manage localized swelling and minor wounds.

What are the herbal preparations of Elaeagnus delavayi?

Elaeagnus delavayi is prepared as a decoction or infusion by boiling the leaves and stems in water to extract medicinal compounds. The resulting liquid is often consumed orally to address various internal ailments. Some practitioners also use the plant to create topical poultices by crushing the fresh foliage into a paste. This paste is applied directly to the skin to soothe localized inflammation or irritation. These different methods allow for both systemic and localized therapeutic applications of the plant.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Elaeagnus delavayi can cause allergic skin reactions, respiratory irritation, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various chemical compounds that may act as irritants to sensitive individuals. Contact with the foliage or bark can trigger contact dermatitis, resulting in redness, itching, or a localized rash. Inhaling pollen or dust from the plant might provoke sneezing, coughing, or more severe allergic asthma symptoms. If any part of the shrub is consumed, it may lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramping.
