Gymnocladus chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Gymnocladus chinensis?

Gymnocladus chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Gymnocladus, with its unique species designation being chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gymnocladus chinensis has evergreen leaves that are pinnately compound with 7 to 15 oblong, leathery leaflets arranged in pairs along a central axis. The individual leaflets feature entire margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface that contrasts with a paler underside. These leaves are borne on woody branches that exhibit a spreading canopy structure typical of subtropical forest trees. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, globose fruits. The bark of the mature tree is thick and textured, providing a protective layer for the woody stem.

What are the pharmacological activities of Gymnocladus chinensis?

This plant has anti-HIV, antioxidant, antiproliferative, and hypoglycemic pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds demonstrate a capacity to inhibit viral replication, making it a subject of interest for HIV research. The plant also contains phytochemicals that neutralize free radicals to mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Additionally, its antiproliferative properties suggest potential utility in restricting the growth of certain cancer cells. Finally, the plant exhibits hypoglycemic effects that can assist in managing elevated blood glucose levels.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", gymnocladus saponin G was isolated from the fruits of Gymnocladus chinensis as an anti-HIV principle. This compound demonstrated inhibitory effects against HIV replication in H-9 cells with an EC50 value of 2.7 microM. The study suggested that the unusual monoterpenyl moieties in the isolated saponin are essential for its anti-HIV activity. These findings highlight the pharmacological potential of the plant in relation to anti-AIDS agents.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", Gymnocladus chinensis Baill. polysaccharides show potential for addressing specific health conditions through their biological activities. The plant's polysaccharides, including fractions GCBP-40, GCBP-60, and GCBP-80 with sugar contents of 64.33%, 59.16%, and 59.46%, demonstrate excellent antioxidant activity. This antioxidant capacity is measured through 2,2-diphenyl-1-picrylhydrazyl free-radical scavenging activity, 2,2-azinobis (3-ethylbenzothiazoline-6-sulfonic acid) free-radical scavenging activity, and OH- free-radical scavenging activity. Furthermore, the components exhibit hypoglycemic activity via α-glucosidase inhibitory activity. These results were derived using optimal extraction parameters including a liquid-solid ratio of 50.4 mL/g, a temperature of 60°C, a time of 43 min, and a power of 240 W to reach a 50.1% extraction rate.
