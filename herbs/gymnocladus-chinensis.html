Gymnocladus chinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gymnocladus chinensis has evergreen leaves that are pinnately compound with 7 to 15 oblong, leathery leaflets arranged in pairs along a central axis. The individual leaflets feature entire margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface that contrasts with a paler underside. These leaves are borne on woody branches that exhibit a spreading canopy structure typical of subtropical forest trees. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, globose fruits. The bark of the mature tree is thick and textured, providing a protective layer for the woody stem.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical montane forests of southern China, specifically distributed across provinces such as Guangdong, Guangxi, Hainan, Hunan, Jiangxi, and Yunnan. It typically inhabits moist, shaded environments at various elevations within these mountainous regions. The species is often found growing in well-drained soils along forest margins or within dense canopy layers. Its range is restricted to these specific climatic zones which provide the necessary humidity for its survival. Consequently, the distribution follows the unique topographical patterns of the southern Chinese landscape.
