Paphinia cristata

Paphinia cristata, commonly known as the Paphiopedilum cristatum or Venus slipper orchid, is a species of orchid.

It is a type of epiphytic orchid that has striking flowers with distinctive markings and is native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

This plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

The bioactive constituents of Paphinia cristata include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which may contribute to its therapeutic actions such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and vasodilatory effects.

Paphinia cristata can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal problems.

Improper use of paphinia cristata can also trigger respiratory issues, such as bronchospasm and asthma-like symptoms, in individuals with pre-existing conditions.
