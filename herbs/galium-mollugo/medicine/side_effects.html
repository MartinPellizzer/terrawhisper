1. Causes allergic reactions

Galium mollugo causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of chemical compounds, such as iridoid glycosides and alkaloids, which can stimulate the immune system, leading to an allergic response.

The symptoms of these reactions can range from mild skin irritation to more severe manifestations like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing, indicating an immune system overreaction to the plant's bioactive compounds.

2. Increases blood pressure

Galium mollugo increases blood pressure due to its bioactive compounds, particularly flavonoids and iridoid glycosides.

These compounds can stimulate the heart to beat faster, leading to an elevation in blood pressure. Additionally, Galium mollugo's vasopressor activity may contribute to the increase in blood pressure, as it can cause the blood vessels to constrict, reducing blood flow and pressure.

This effect can be a concern for individuals with pre-existing hypertension.

3. Stimulates kidney failure

Galium mollugo stimulates kidney failure due to its high saponin and oxalic acid content.

These compounds can accumulate in the kidneys, causing damage and impairing their function. The saponins can also lead to inflammation and scarring in the renal tissues, further exacerbating kidney failure.

This can ultimately lead to a range of complications, including electrolyte imbalances and acid-base disturbances.

4. Triggers seizures

Galium mollugo triggers seizures due to its neurotoxic properties.

The plant contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can cross the blood-brain barrier and disrupt normal neuronal activity.

The mechanism of seizure induction may involve the interaction of these compounds with voltage-gated sodium channels, leading to an excessive release of neurotransmitters and the onset of seizure activity.

5. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Galium mollugo produces gastrointestinal upset.

This side effect is likely due to the plant's iridoid glycosides, specifically aucubin and catalpol, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and laxative properties.

These compounds can cause stomach discomfort, nausea, and diarrhea by increasing gut motility and fluid secretion, leading to gastrointestinal upset in some individuals.

6. Lowers blood sugar levels

Galium mollugo lowers blood sugar levels due to its ability to stimulate the release of insulin from pancreatic cells.

This is thought to be attributed to the presence of iridoid glycosides, a class of compounds that have been shown to have insulin-releasing properties.

The mechanism by which this occurs is not fully understood, but it is believed to be related to the glycosides' ability to modulate glucose metabolism in the body.

7. Inhibits liver function

Galium mollugo inhibits liver function due to its bioactive compounds, including iridoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds interfere with the liver's ability to detoxify and metabolize substances, potentially leading to a range of liver problems. The liver's compromised function can result in reduced bile production, decreased enzyme activity, and impaired nutrient processing, ultimately affecting overall health.

The severity of this side effect may vary depending on individual factors and usage.

8. Worsens arrhythmia

Galium mollugo worsens arrhythmia due to its glycoside content, particularly galioside.

These compounds increase cardiac excitability, leading to an irregular heartbeat and arrhythmia. The increased heart rate and contraction force may also contribute to the exacerbation of arrhythmia.

As a result, individuals with pre-existing arrhythmia conditions may experience worsening symptoms when using Galium mollugo.

9. Induces coma

Galium mollugo induces coma due to its potent neurotoxic compounds, including galitoxin and methyl gallate.

These toxins interfere with neurotransmitter function, disrupting normal brain activity and leading to a range of neurological symptoms, including sedation, confusion, and ultimately, a coma.

The severity of the coma can vary depending on the individual's sensitivity to the toxins and the amount consumed.

10. Activates uterine contractions

Galium mollugo activates uterine contractions due to the presence of iridoid glycosides, particularly asperuloside.

This compound has been found to stimulate smooth muscle contraction, which can cause the uterus to contract.

As a result, individuals using Galium mollugo may experience increased uterine activity, potentially leading to discomfort or other related symptoms.
