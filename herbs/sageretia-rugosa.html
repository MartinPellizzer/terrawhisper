Sageretia rugosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Sageretia rugosa?

Sageretia rugosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Rosales, this plant is a member of the family Rhamnaceae. Its specific classification is defined by the genus Sageretia, with the individual species being identified as rugosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sageretia rugosa has a woody, perennial shrub structure characterized by densely rugose, wrinkled leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the stems, featuring a rough texture that gives the species its specific epithet. Its leaves are often elliptical or lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The inflorescence consists of small, yellowish or greenish flowers that are often tucked within the leaf axils. During its growth phase, the plant develops a sturdy branching habit that supports its textured canopy.
