Artocarpus lakoocha

What's the taxonomical classification of Artocarpus lakoocha?

Artocarpus lakoocha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Moraceae. Finally, it is defined by the genus Artocarpus and is specifically identified by the species name lakoocha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artocarpus lakoocha has large, alternate, elliptical leaves that are dark green, leathery in texture, and possess prominent pinnate venation. The tree typically develops a spreading crown with branches that bear these dense, glossy foliage clusters. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, aggregate fruits. These fruits are often globose or ovoid in shape and feature a specialized outer rind. The woody stem and bark provide a sturdy structural foundation for the expansive canopy.

What are the pharmacological activities of Artocarpus lakoocha?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, anticancer, cytotoxic, and wound-healing properties. Its high concentration of bioactive compounds allows it to neutralize free radicals and mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. The plant also demonstrates significant cytotoxic effects that can inhibit the proliferation of various malignant cell lines. These anticancer mechanisms work in tandem with its ability to reduce swelling and pain through anti-inflammatory pathways. Furthermore, the medicinal extracts promote tissue regeneration and accelerate the wound-healing process in damaged skin.

As discussed in a study, the latex of Artocarpus lakoocha contains a novel heme-peroxidase that has shown potential anti-inflammatory and wound-healing capabilities. This protein demonstrates structural integrity across a wide pH range of 3-10 and can withstand temperatures up to 70°C. It also maintains stability in high concentrations of urea at 8 M and GuHCl at 5 M. The study identified several folding states, including a molten globule at pH 2, an acid-unfolded state at pH 1.5 or lower, and an acid-refolded state at pH 0.5. Additionally, alkaline denatured states occur at pH 8-12, while a third denatured state appears at GuHCl concentrations exceeding 5 M.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds lakoochiins A, lakoochiins B, and oxyresveratrol, which are often studied within a crude aqueous extract. These specific phytochemicals are known for their potent biological activities, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that these substances can interact with biological processes such as heme-peroxidase activity. Scientists frequently utilize the crude aqueous extract to isolate these bioactive molecules for pharmaceutical testing. Understanding how these compounds function provides insight into the plant's traditional therapeutic uses.

As discussed in a study, two new stilbene derivatives, lakoochiins A (1) and B (2), were isolated from the roots of Artocarpus lakoocha. These compounds exhibited antimycobacterial activity with MIC values of 12.5 and 50 microg/mL for lakoochin A (1) and B (2) respectively. Compound 1 was cytotoxic against the BC cell line with an IC(50) of 6.1 microg/mL, but it remained inactive at 20 microg/mL toward KB cells. Additionally, compound 2 possessed cytotoxicity against the BC and KB cell lines with IC(50) values of 3.1 and 6.1 microg/mL.
