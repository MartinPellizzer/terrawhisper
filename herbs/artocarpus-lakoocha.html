Artocarpus lakoocha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artocarpus lakoocha has large, alternate, elliptical leaves that are dark green, leathery in texture, and possess prominent pinnate venation. The tree typically develops a spreading crown with branches that bear these dense, glossy foliage clusters. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, aggregate fruits. These fruits are often globose or ovoid in shape and feature a specialized outer rind. The woody stem and bark provide a sturdy structural foundation for the expansive canopy.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Artocarpus lakoocha targets various inflammatory mediators and enzymatic proteins, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), lipoxygenase (LOX), and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS). These interactions occur primarily through the plant's rich profile of bioactive flavonoids and polyphenols that disrupt the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. By suppressing COX-2 and LOX, the plant effectively reduces the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins and leukotrienes. Additionally, its ability to downregulate iNOS leads to a significant decrease in nitric oxide levels, which mitigates oxidative stress and cellular damage. These molecular mechanisms collectively contribute to the plant's potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant pharmacological properties.
