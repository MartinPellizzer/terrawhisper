Artocarpus lakoocha

What's the taxonomical classification of Artocarpus lakoocha?

Artocarpus lakoocha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Moraceae. Finally, it is defined by the genus Artocarpus and is specifically identified by the species name lakoocha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artocarpus lakoocha has large, alternate, elliptical leaves that are dark green, leathery in texture, and possess prominent pinnate venation. The tree typically develops a spreading crown with branches that bear these dense, glossy foliage clusters. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, aggregate fruits. These fruits are often globose or ovoid in shape and feature a specialized outer rind. The woody stem and bark provide a sturdy structural foundation for the expansive canopy.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds lakoochins A, lakoochins B, and oxyresveratrol, which are primarily isolated from the crude aqueous extract of its roots. These bioactive molecules are studied for their significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties in various biological systems. Researchers utilize the crude aqueous extract to investigate how these specific polyphenols interact with cellular processes. One area of interest involves the inhibition of heme-peroxidase to understand how these compounds mitigate oxidative stress. Together, these constituents provide a chemical basis for the plant's traditional therapeutic applications.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Artocarpus lakoocha targets various inflammatory mediators and enzymatic proteins, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), lipoxygenase (LOX), and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS). These interactions occur primarily through the plant's rich profile of bioactive flavonoids and polyphenols that disrupt the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. By suppressing COX-2 and LOX, the plant effectively reduces the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins and leukotrienes. Additionally, its ability to downregulate iNOS leads to a significant decrease in nitric oxide levels, which mitigates oxidative stress and cellular damage. These molecular mechanisms collectively contribute to the plant's potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant pharmacological properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Artocarpus lakoocha?

Artocarpus lakoocha has therapeutic properties that include acting as an astringent, bitter, diuretic, anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, and hemostatic agent. The plant's bitter components are often utilized to stimulate digestion and improve metabolic functions. Its astringent and hemostatic qualities allow it to effectively control bleeding and tighten body tissues. Additionally, the botanical extracts serve as an anti-inflammatory to reduce swelling and an antipyretic to lower fevers. The diuretic action further assists in promoting fluid excretion to support renal health.
