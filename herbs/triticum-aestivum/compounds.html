Wheat (Triticum aestivum) Compounds

Wheat contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins provide astringent effects, while glycosides and saponins have detoxifying benefits. Coumarin and terpenoids support metabolic functions, and anthocyanins offer protective effects against oxidative stress.

Wheat (Triticum aestivum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Wheat (Triticum aestivum). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cardiovascular and immune health. These compounds are naturally present in various parts of the wheat plant, including the grain and bran.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Wheat (Triticum aestivum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support digestive health. This compound is found in various parts of the wheat plant, including the bran and germ. Its presence plays a role in the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Wheat (Triticum aestivum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in wheat may support digestive health and help in managing conditions like diarrhea. They are also used in traditional herbal medicine for their healing properties.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Wheat (Triticum aestivum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in wheat are often used in herbal remedies to promote gut function and overall wellness.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Wheat (Triticum aestivum). It exhibits mild detergent properties and may support digestive health by promoting bile secretion. Found in the bran and germ, saponin contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential cleansing and anti-inflammatory effects.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a natural compound found in Wheat (Triticum aestivum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has mild anticoagulant effects. Coumarin is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential benefits in improving circulation and reducing inflammation.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Wheat (Triticum aestivum). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's ability to support digestive and immune health.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Wheat (Triticum aestivum). It acts as an antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent, supporting cardiovascular health and immune function. Its presence in wheat contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory ailments.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds found in Wheat (Triticum aestivum). While wheat is not a traditional source of alkaloids, certain alkaloid-like compounds may be present in trace amounts during processing. These compounds are not the primary active components of wheat in herbal medicine.

10. Essential oil

Essential oil from Wheat (Triticum aestivum) contains volatile compounds like terpenes and phenolic acids. These oils are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They are extracted through steam distillation and used in formulations for respiratory and digestive health.
