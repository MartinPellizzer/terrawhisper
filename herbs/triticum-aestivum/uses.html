Wheat (Triticum aestivum) Uses

Wheat is used to treat asthma by reducing airway inflammation. It helps alleviate coughs and allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory properties. Wheat can also address skin infections, irritation, and inflammation, promoting wound healing. It is beneficial for gastritis and constipation, supporting digestive health.

Wheat (Triticum aestivum) Monograph

1. Asthma

Wheat (Triticum aestivum) has been used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and mucus production, potentially alleviating asthma symptoms. However, it should be used cautiously due to its potential to trigger allergic reactions in some individuals.

2. Cough

Wheat, particularly its grain and derived products, has been traditionally used to alleviate cough symptoms. It is valued for its mucilage content, which soothes irritated airways and reduces inflammation. In herbal medicine, wheat-based remedies are often used as a gentle demulcent to ease persistent or dry coughs.

3. Allergic rhinitis

Wheat (Triticum aestivum) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and sneezing by modulating histamine response. However, it should be used cautiously in individuals with gluten sensitivity or celiac disease.

4. Skin infections

Wheat is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The bran and germ contain compounds that inhibit bacterial growth. It can be applied as a poultice or incorporated into salves. This application is supported by traditional herbal practices.

5. Skin irritation

Wheat is used in herbal medicine to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and emollient properties. It can be applied topically as a poultice or incorporated into salves to reduce redness and promote healing. Its high nutrient content supports skin regeneration and provides a gentle, natural remedy for minor irritations.

6. Skin inflammation

Wheat is used in herbal medicine to address skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce redness, irritation, and itching associated with conditions like eczema and psoriasis. External applications of wheat-based preparations can promote skin healing and relieve discomfort.

7. Wound healing

Wheat (Triticum aestivum) supports wound healing through its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It promotes tissue regeneration and reduces infection risk. Wheat-based products like pastes or poultices can be applied topically to accelerate recovery. Its high nutrient content aids in skin repair and overall healing processes.

8. Psoriasis

Wheat (Triticum aestivum) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. It contains bioactive compounds like lignans and peptides that may help reduce skin inflammation and regulate immune responses. However, it should be used cautiously in individuals with gluten sensitivity or celiac disease.

9. Gastritis

Wheat (Triticum aestivum) is used in herbal medicine to soothe gastritis due to its mucilage content, which forms a protective layer over the stomach lining. It helps reduce inflammation and irritation, making it useful in managing symptoms like heartburn and indigestion. Wheat-based remedies are often prepared as decoctions or pastes for internal use.

10. Constipation

Wheat, particularly whole grains, can help relieve constipation due to their high fiber content. The bran in wheat adds bulk to stool and promotes regular bowel movements. However, individuals with gluten intolerance should avoid wheat-based remedies.
