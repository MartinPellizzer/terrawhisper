1. Causes allergic reactions

Triticum aestivum causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of specific proteins such as gliadin and secalin, which are found in the gluten component of wheat. These proteins can stimulate an immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the lectins present in wheat can cause a reaction, releasing histamine and other chemicals that contribute to allergic symptoms.

2. Triggers celiac disease

Triticum aestivum triggers celiac disease due to the presence of a protein called gliadin in its endosperm.

When individuals with celiac disease consume wheat products containing gliadin, their immune system reacts by damaging the lining of the small intestine, leading to malabsorption and potentially life-threatening complications.

The reaction occurs because the body mistakenly identifies gliadin as a foreign invader, triggering an inflammatory response that disrupts normal digestive processes.

3. Induces digestive issues

Triticum aestivum induces digestive issues in some individuals due to its high content of gluten, a protein that can cause inflammation and irritation in the gut.

The consumption of wheat products can lead to symptoms such as bloating, abdominal pain, and changes in bowel habits, making it challenging for those with non-celiac gluten sensitivity or irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) to tolerate.

4. Interferes with nutrient absorption

Triticum aestivum interferes with nutrient absorption by containing a compound called phytate.

Phytate can bind to minerals such as zinc, iron, and calcium in the gut, preventing their proper absorption into the bloodstream.

This can lead to deficiencies of these essential nutrients over time, potentially causing a range of symptoms including fatigue, weakness, and poor wound healing.

5. Stimulates blood sugar increase

Triticum aestivum stimulates blood sugar increase due to its high glycemic index.

The body rapidly breaks down the complex carbohydrates in wheat into simple sugars, causing a swift spike in blood glucose levels.

This is particularly concerning for individuals with diabetes or those at risk of developing insulin resistance, as it can exacerbate these conditions and lead to further complications.

6. Increases risk of osteoporosis

Triticum aestivum increases risk of osteoporosis due to its high phytic acid content.

Phytic acid can bind to minerals such as zinc, iron, and calcium, reducing their absorption in the gut, leading to deficiencies that may contribute to bone weakening and increased fracture risk.

Additionally, some compounds found in Triticum aestivum have been shown to alter the gut microbiome, potentially disrupting calcium metabolism and further exacerbating osteoporosis development.

7. Contributes to thyroid problems

Triticum aestivum contributes to thyroid problems due to the presence of a protein called gliadin, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

The body mistakenly identifies gliadin as a foreign substance and produces antibodies to fight it, leading to inflammation and potentially disrupting thyroid function.

This reaction can cause symptoms such as fatigue, weight gain, and mood changes, which are often indicative of thyroid issues.

8. Aggravates asthma symptoms

Triticum aestivum aggravates asthma symptoms due to its high concentration of alpha-amylase and amylase enzymes.

These enzymes can break down gluten into smaller peptides that trigger an immune response in individuals with asthma, leading to inflammation and airway constriction.

Additionally, the proteins present in Triticum aestivum can cause mast cells to release histamine, further exacerbating symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

9. Worsens eczema conditions

Triticum aestivum worsens eczema conditions by triggering an immune response in individuals who have a gluten intolerance or sensitivity.

The consumption of gluten-containing foods can cause the immune system to mistakenly attack healthy tissues, leading to inflammation and skin irritation that exacerbates eczema symptoms.

This reaction can lead to increased redness, itching, and dryness, further complicating eczema management.

10. Activates migraine triggers

Triticum aestivum activates migraine triggers by releasing a group of proteins called gliadins.

These proteins can stimulate an immune response in individuals who are sensitive to gluten, leading to the activation of trigeminal nerves and subsequent migraine headaches.

The breakdown of gliadin proteins during digestion releases opioid peptides that bind to opioid receptors in the brain, further exacerbating the migraine symptoms.
