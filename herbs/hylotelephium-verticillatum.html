Hylotelephium verticillatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hylotelephium verticillatum has succulent, obovate leaves that measure between 10 and 25 millimeters in length and are arranged in distinct whorls of three to five around a creeping stem. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly crenate, and the foliage exhibits a range of colors from bright green to reddish hues depending on environmental stress. These leaves are thick and fleshy, a morphological adaptation designed for efficient water storage in its rocky, coastal habitats. The plant's stems are somewhat prostrate or ascending, allowing it to form dense, mat-like cushions over stone surfaces. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, contributing to its compact and spreading growth habit.
