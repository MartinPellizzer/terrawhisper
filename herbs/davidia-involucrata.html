Davidia involucrata

What's the taxonomical classification of Davidia involucrata?

Davidia involucrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Cornales and the family Nyyssaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Davidia. Finally, the plant is identified by its unique species name, involucrata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Davidia involucrata has a deciduous growth habit characterized by large, woody stems and pinnately compound leaves that can reach lengths of up to 60 centimeters.

The most striking morphological feature is the pair of large, white, petal-like bracts that emerge from the terminal buds to surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. These bracts serve as functional mimics of petals to attract pollinators to the plant's actual inflorescence. The foliage is typically dark green with serrated margins and a texture that becomes leathery as the leaves mature.

During the autumn season, the canopy undergoes a significant transformation as the leaves transition to vibrant shades of yellow and red.
