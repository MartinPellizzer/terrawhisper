Siphonostegia chinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Siphonostegia chinensis has a perennial climbing habit characterized by slender, twining stems that produce small, tubular, yellowish-white flowers and lanceolate to ovate leaves. The foliage typically features entire margins and a smooth texture, often arranged oppositely along the climbing vines. Its reproductive structures consist of inconspicuous corollas that emerge from axillary positions. The root system is adapted to support its sprawling growth pattern across various substrates. These morphological traits allow the plant to effectively compete for light in its natural habitat.

What are the therapeutic actions of Siphonostegia chinensis?

Siphonostegia chinensis has therapeutic actions that include being a bitter, diuretic, cholagogue, stomachic, and febrifuge. Additionally, the herb acts as a diuretic to increase urine output and functions as a febrifuge to reduce fever.
