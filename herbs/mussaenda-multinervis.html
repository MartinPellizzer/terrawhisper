Mussaenda multinervis

What's the taxonomical classification of Mussaenda multinervis?

Mussaenda multinervis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Gentianaales. As a member of the family Rubiaceae, this plant is further defined by its membership in the genus Mussaenda, with its specific identification being the species multinervis.

These different preparations are utilized to target various ailments depending on whether the treatment is internal or external.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Mussaenda multinervis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized swelling if its sap or foliage comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, many plants in this family contain compounds that may trigger inflammatory responses. Contact with the eyes can lead to redness, stinging, or temporary irritation. There is also a potential risk of gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is accidentally ingested. Individuals with known hypersensitivity to tropical flora should exercise caution when handling this species.
