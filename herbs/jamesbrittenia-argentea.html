What is Jamesbrittenia argentea?

Jamesbrittenia argentea is a shrubby perennial herb, native to the Western Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its greyish-green, succulent leaves, which are oval to elliptical in shape and have entire margins. The leaves are 1-2 cm long and are arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces white, trumpet-shaped flowers, which are 3-4 mm long and are clustered in axillary cymes.

The flowers have a 4-lobed corolla and are pollinated by bees and butterflies.
