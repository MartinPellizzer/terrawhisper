Gonocarpus chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Gonocarpus chinensis?

Gonocarpus chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Haloragaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Gonocarpus, with its unique species designation being chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gonocarpus chinensis has prostrate, spreading stems that form dense mats, with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that feature serrated margins and a slightly hairy texture. The foliage is typically bright green and can grow up to several centimeters in length depending on environmental conditions. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, showcasing a delicate structure that often appears white or pale pink. These blossoms eventually produce tiny, dry fruits that facilitate the plant's rapid colonization of ground cover. The overall growth habit is low-lying, making it an effective groundcover in various soil types.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, known as Gonocarpus chinensis or common amaranth, thrives in well-draining, fertile soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH under full sun exposure.

Regular watering is necessary to maintain consistent moisture, although the plant is relatively drought-tolerant once its root system is established.
