Allamanda schottii

What's the taxonomical classification of Allamanda schottii?

Allamanda schootti belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Gentianaales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Allamanda, and its specific species designation, schootti.

What are the therapeutic actions of Allamanda schottii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions through its topical application. The leaves are often prepared as a decoction or poultice to address localized dermatological issues. Some traditional practices utilize the plant's extracts to soothe minor irritations and promote the healing of open cuts. Beyond skin ailments, its medicinal properties are sometimes explored for their potential to mitigate general bodily inflammation. Researchers continue to examine the specific bioactive compounds that contribute to these therapeutic effects.
