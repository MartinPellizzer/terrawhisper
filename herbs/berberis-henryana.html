Berberis henryana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berberis henryana has small, leathery, obovate leaves that are dark green with distinctive creamy-white margins and a highly serrated edge. These leaves grow in dense clusters along stiff, woody stems that are typically armed with sharp spines. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the spring season. Following the flowering period, it develops small, dark blue berries that add color to the foliage. The overall growth habit is that of a compact, spreading shrub with a textured appearance.
