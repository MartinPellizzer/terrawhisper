African Geranium (Pelargonium sidoides) Compounds

The primary compounds in African Geranium include flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids and saponins contribute to antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Terpenoids and essential oils provide aromatic and therapeutic benefits, while coumarin and glycosides support various medicinal effects. Alkaloids and anthocyanins further enhance the plant's pharmacological profile.

African Geranium (Pelargonium sidoides) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in African Geranium (Pelargonium sidoides) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce symptoms of respiratory infections by supporting immune function and decreasing airway inflammation. They are among the key bioactive constituents responsible for the plant's therapeutic effects.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in African Geranium (Pelargonium sidoides). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Phenolic acid is a key active compound in African Geranium (Pelargonium sidoides). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, supporting its traditional use in treating respiratory and urinary tract infections. 4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in African Geranium (Pelargonium sidoides). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in treating respiratory infections and other inflammatory conditions.
