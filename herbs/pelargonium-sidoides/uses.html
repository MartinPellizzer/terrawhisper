African Geranium (Pelargonium sidoides) Uses

African Geranium is primarily used to treat respiratory infections such as the common cold, bronchitis, and pneumonia. It helps alleviate symptoms of sinusitis, otitis media, and influenza by reducing inflammation and supporting immune function. It is also used for conditions like laryngitis, tonsillitis, and pharyngitis due to its antimicrobial and antiviral properties.

African Geranium (Pelargonium sidoides) Monograph

1. Common cold

African Geranium, or Pelargonium sidoides, is used traditionally to treat the common cold due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce symptoms like sore throat and nasal congestion. Studies suggest it may shorten the duration of cold symptoms when taken early. It is commonly prepared as a tincture or tea in herbal medicine practices.

2. Bronchitis

Pelargonium sidoides has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating bronchitis. It helps reduce inflammation and mucus production in the airways, easing symptoms like coughing and congestion. Its antimicrobial properties may also help combat infections associated with bronchial inflammation.

3. Lung infection

Pelargonium sidoides, known as African Geranium, is used traditionally to support respiratory health. It is particularly valued for its potential to alleviate symptoms of lung infections, including coughing and mucus production. Its active compounds may help reduce inflammation and enhance immune response in the respiratory tract.

4. Sinusitis

Pelargonium sidoides, or African Geranium, is used to treat sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and mucus production, supporting respiratory health. Clinical studies suggest it can shorten the duration of acute sinusitis symptoms when taken regularly. It is often used as a natural alternative to conventional treatments.

5. Otitis media

African Geranium, or Pelargonium sidoides, is used traditionally to treat otitis media due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce ear inflammation and combat infections, offering relief from symptoms like pain and discharge. Clinical studies support its efficacy in managing acute middle ear infections, making it a valuable herbal remedy in herbal medicine practice.

6. Pneumonia

African Geranium, or Pelargonium sidoides, is used traditionally to treat pneumonia due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce coughing, clear mucus, and alleviate respiratory symptoms. Clinical studies support its effectiveness in shortening the duration of pneumonia symptoms. It is often taken as a standardized extract in herbal formulations.

7. Influenza

Pelargonium sidoides, or African Geranium, is used in herbal medicine to support the treatment of influenza. It helps reduce symptoms like fever and cough by boosting the immune system and has antiviral properties that may inhibit viral replication. Its effectiveness is supported by clinical studies, making it a valued remedy in traditional and complementary medicine for respiratory infections.

8. Laryngitis

African Geranium, or Pelargonium sidoides, is used in herbal medicine to treat laryngitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce throat inflammation and soothe irritation, making it a common remedy for coughs and sore throats associated with laryngitis. Its efficacy is supported by traditional use and some clinical studies.

9. Tonsillitis

Pelargonium sidoides, also known as African Geranium, is used traditionally to treat tonsillitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling and soothe throat pain, often leading to quicker recovery. Clinical studies support its effectiveness in alleviating symptoms associated with bacterial and viral tonsillitis.

10. Pharyngitis

African Geranium (Pelargonium sidoides) is used to treat pharyngitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce throat pain, swelling, and mucus production. Standardized extracts are commonly taken as a syrup or tincture. Clinical studies support its effectiveness in alleviating symptoms of bacterial and viral pharyngitis.
