Devil's Club

Oplopanax horridus

Devil's Club (Oplopanax horridus) is a spiky evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, known for its traditional use by Indigenous peoples in treating various ailments, including diabetes and digestive issues.

Botanical Identification

Devil's Club, scientifically known as Oplopanax horridus, is a member of the Araliaceae family. Common names include devil's club and wild ginger, with regional variations. It is a perennial shrub native to western North America. The plant features spiny stems, large palmate leaves, and yellowish flowers. Historically referred to as "devil's club" in herbal texts, it has also been called "mountain cedar" in some regions.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Apiales
  • Family: Araliaceae
  • Genus: Oplopanax
  • Species: Oplopanax horridus

Safety Profile

Devil's Club contains toxic compounds and should not be used during pregnancy or by individuals with hypersensitivity. It may cause gastrointestinal upset and skin irritation. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for long-term or high-dose applications.
