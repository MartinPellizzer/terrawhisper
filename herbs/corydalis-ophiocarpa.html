Corydalis ophiocarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis ophiocarpa?

Corydalis ophiocarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the Papaveraceae family, specifically within the genus Corydalis, and is identified by its unique species name, ophiocarpa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis ophiocarpa has delicate, deeply lobed, bluish-green leaves and small, zygomorphic flowers that typically feature pale lavender or white petals with yellow markings. The plant grows from a fleshy tuberous rhizome that provides energy for its seasonal development. Its stems are relatively slender and support the terminal clusters of nodding blossoms. The fruit develops into an elongated, capsule-like structure that contains numerous small seeds. This morphology allows the species to thrive in the moist, shaded environments of temperate forest floors.
