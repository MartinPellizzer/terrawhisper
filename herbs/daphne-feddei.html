Daphne feddei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Daphne feddei has woody, perennial stems that support opposite, lanceolate to oblong leaves with entire margins and a leathery texture. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy green surface and can vary in size depending on the age of the plant. During its flowering period, the species produces small, fragrant blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers are often tubular or bell-shaped and can be white or pale pink in color. The plant eventually develops small, fleshy drupes that serve as its fruit.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Sichuan province in southwestern China. It specifically thrives within the montane ecosystems of the eastern edge of the Tibetan Plateau. These populations are typically found growing in rocky crevices and subalpine shrublands at significant elevations. The local climate of this specific geographic range provides the necessary cool and moist conditions required for its survival.       Daphne feddei is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried roots or stems in water to extract its medicinal compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Daphne feddei can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and intense gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is ingested or touched. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage often leads to contact dermatitis, which manifests as painful redness and swelling on the skin. If the toxic components are swallowed, an individual may experience nausea, vomiting, and debilitating abdominal pain. These reactions occur because the plant contains potent chemical compounds that act as irritants to biological tissues. Proper precautions must be taken to avoid direct contact to prevent these hazardous medical symptoms.
