Daphne feddei

What's the taxonomical classification of Daphne feddei?

Daphne feddei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Malvales. Within this botanical hierarchy, the plant is a member of the family Thymelaeaceae and is situated in the genus Daphne, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species feddei.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Daphne feddei can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and intense gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is ingested or touched. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage often leads to contact dermatitis, which manifests as painful redness and swelling on the skin. If the toxic components are swallowed, an individual may experience nausea, vomiting, and debilitating abdominal pain. These reactions occur because the plant contains potent chemical compounds that act as irritants to biological tissues. Proper precautions must be taken to avoid direct contact to prevent these hazardous medical symptoms.
