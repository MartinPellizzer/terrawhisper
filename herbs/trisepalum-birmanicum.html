Trisepalum birmanicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage leading to the order Lamiales. This species is a specific representative of the family Gesneriaceae, falling under the genus Trisepa Lum and is ultimately identified by its unique species name, birmanicum.

Trisepalum birmanicum has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin infections and localized swelling.
