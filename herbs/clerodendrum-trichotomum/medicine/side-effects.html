10 Possible Side Effects Of Clerodendrum Trichotomum (Harlequin Glory Bower)

Clerodendrum trichotomum has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, allergic reactions to certain compounds, or skin exposure to the plant's sap. For example, excessive use can lead to stomach pain, diarrhea, and nausea, hindering daily activities and work performance.

In severe cases, allergic reactions can trigger life-threatening anaphylaxis, and skin irritation can lead to painful rashes and blisters.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Clerodendrum trichotomum if used imporperly.
