1. Causes allergic reactions

Clerodendrum trichotomum causes allergic reactions.

This is likely due to the presence of certain compounds in its leaves and stems, such as terpenoids and flavonoids, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

The exact mechanisms behind these reactions are not fully understood, but it is thought that exposure to these compounds can cause symptoms such as skin irritation, rashes, and respiratory issues.

2. Induces severe headaches

Clerodendrum trichotomum induces severe headaches due to its potential to interact with certain medications, particularly blood thinners and anti-inflammatory drugs.

The herb's ability to stimulate the nervous system and alter neurotransmitter levels can also contribute to the occurrence of headaches.

Additionally, some individuals may be more sensitive to the plant's chemical compounds, leading to adverse effects such as headaches.

3. Triggers respiratory distress

Clerodendrum trichotomum triggers respiratory distress due to its volatile oils and saponins.

These compounds can cause inflammation in the lungs and airways, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

The irritation caused by these compounds can further exacerbate existing respiratory conditions like asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

4. Produces nausea symptoms

Clerodendrum trichotomum produces nausea symptoms due to its high concentration of essential oils, particularly clerodin and caryophyllene.

These oils can stimulate the digestive system, leading to feelings of queasiness, dizziness, and vomiting in some individuals.

Additionally, the plant's bitter compounds may irritate the stomach lining, exacerbating existing gastrointestinal issues and causing nausea as a side effect.

5. Causes dizziness attacks

Clerodendrum trichotomum causes dizziness attacks.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's essential oil, which contains a high concentration of alkaloids and volatile compounds that can affect the nervous system.

When ingested or inhaled, these substances may disrupt the body's balance and lead to feelings of lightheadedness, vertigo, and disorientation, ultimately triggering dizziness attacks.

6. Leads to stomach upset

Clerodendrum trichotomum leads to stomach upset due to its rich content of saponins and flavonoids.

These bioactive compounds can cause gastrointestinal irritation, resulting in symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal discomfort when ingested.

The plant's high polyphenol content may also contribute to the development of gastric issues, making it essential to be aware of these potential adverse reactions when using Clerodendrum trichotomum.

7. Increases blood pressure

Clerodendrum trichotomum increases blood pressure due to its high levels of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds can cause the body's blood vessels to constrict, leading to an elevation in blood pressure. Additionally, Clerodendrum trichotomum has been found to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, which can also contribute to increased blood pressure.

As a result, individuals taking this supplement may experience increased blood pressure readings.

8. Enhances anxiety levels

Clerodendrum trichotomum enhances anxiety levels due to its ability to interact with the body's neurotransmitters, particularly serotonin and dopamine.

The plant contains compounds that can alter the normal functioning of these chemicals, leading to an increase in feelings of unease, worry, and apprehension.

Additionally, the aromatic oils present in Clerodendrum trichotomum may stimulate the nervous system, further contributing to elevated anxiety levels.

9. Affects kidney function

Clerodendrum trichotomum affects kidney function due to its ability to interfere with the normal functioning of the kidneys.

The plant's active compounds, including flavonoids and terpenes, can cause a reduction in glomerular filtration rate, leading to a decrease in urine production and an accumulation of waste products in the body.

This may result in symptoms such as increased blood pressure, edema, and electrolyte imbalances.

10. Interferes with digestion

Clerodendrum trichotomum interferes with digestion due to its high content of alkaloids and flavonoids.

These bioactive compounds can alter the normal functioning of the digestive system, leading to reduced appetite, nausea, and changes in bowel movements.

The plant's ability to stimulate the release of gastric acid and digestive enzymes may also contribute to this effect, potentially causing discomfort or pain in the abdomen.
