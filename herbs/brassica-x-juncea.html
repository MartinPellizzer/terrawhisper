Brassica × juncea

What's the taxonomical classification of Brassica × juncea?

Brassica × juncea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Brassicales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Brassicaceae, specifically within the genus Brassica, and is identified as the species juncea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Brassica × juncea has an erect or ascending growth habit characterized by an annual or biennial life cycle, featuring a central stem that can reach heights of up to one meter. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem and exhibit a diverse range of shapes from lanceolate to ovate with serrated or lobed margins. Its floral structure consists of four petals arranged in a cruciform pattern, which is a defining trait of the Brassicaceae family. The plant produces small, globular siliques that contain numerous tiny seeds used for culinary and oil purposes. The root system is generally a taproot structure that provides stability and nutrient uptake throughout the growing season.
