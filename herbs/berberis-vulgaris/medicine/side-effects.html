10 Possible Side Effects Of Berberis Vulgaris (Barberry)

Berberis vulgaris has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal problems, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

These issues arise due to excessive consumption, individual sensitivities, or combined use with other substances.

In severe cases, these side effects can lead to complications like digestive bleeding, anaphylaxis, or unintended medication effects, significantly impacting daily life and overall health.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Berberis vulgaris if used imporperly.

1. Increases risk of cancer

Berberis vulgaris causes allergic reactions due to its content of berberine, a yellow alkaloid that can trigger an immune response.

The presence of this compound in the plant's leaves and stems may cause hypersensitivity reactions in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the plant's high concentration of beta-carboline alkaloids may also contribute to allergic responses, making it essential for individuals with known allergies to exercise caution when handling or consuming Berberis vulgaris.

These compounds can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.
