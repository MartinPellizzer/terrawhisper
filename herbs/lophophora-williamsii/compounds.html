Peyote (Lophophora williamsii) Compounds

Peyote contains several primary compounds, including alkaloids like mescaline, which is responsible for its psychoactive effects. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant properties. Terpenoids and saponins provide additional pharmacological benefits, while coumarin and anthocyanin contribute to its chemical complexity and potential therapeutic uses.

Peyote (Lophophora williamsii) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of naturally occurring compounds found in Peyote, Lophophora williamsii. These compounds, including mescaline, are responsible for the psychoactive and medicinal effects associated with Peyote. They play a key role in traditional and modern uses of the plant in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Peyote (Lophophora williamsii). These compounds contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cellular health. While not psychoactive, they play a role in the overall therapeutic potential of Peyote in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Peyote, Lophophora williamsii. They contribute to the plant's psychoactive and medicinal properties. Specific terpenoids in Peyote may influence its effects on the nervous system and have been studied for potential therapeutic applications.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Peyote (Lophophora williamsii). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may influence its traditional uses in herbal medicine. Tannins are found in various parts of the plant and are known for their potential role in digestive and antimicrobial effects.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Peyote (Lophophora williamsii). It contributes to the plant's psychoactive effects and has potential therapeutic properties, including anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory actions. However, its role in traditional use of Peyote is less prominent compared to other alkaloids like mescaline.

6. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Peyote (Lophophora williamsii). It contributes to the plant's overall pharmacological profile, though its specific effects and mechanisms require further research. This compound is part of the complex mixture responsible for Peyote's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

The glycoside in Peyote (Lophophora williamsii) refers to the sugar-bound alkaloids found in its cactus body. These compounds are key to its psychoactive and medicinal effects, contributing to its traditional use in healing and spiritual practices. Glycosides in Peyote are primarily responsible for its pharmacological activity.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Peyote (Lophophora williamsii). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has mild detergent properties. While not psychoactive, saponins may support digestive health and have antimicrobial effects. Their presence in Peyote highlights the plant's complex chemical profile.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Peyote (Lophophora williamsii) contains volatile compounds that contribute to its aromatic profile. While not the primary psychoactive component, it may play a role in the plant's traditional use and overall therapeutic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are pigments found in Peyote (Lophophora williamsii) that contribute to its deep red and blue hues. While not psychoactive, they may support antioxidant activity and cellular health. These compounds are part of the plant's complex chemistry, though their medicinal role in traditional use is less well-defined compared to other constituents.
