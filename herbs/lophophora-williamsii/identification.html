Botanical Identification of Peyote (Lophophora williamsii)

To identify Peyote (Lophophora williamsii) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Peyote, or Lophophora williamsii, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Caryophyllales, family Cactaceae, genus Lophophora, and species Lophophora williamsii. These classifications reflect its position within the plant kingdom and its relation to other cacti.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Peyote is scientifically known as Lophophora williamsii, a name that reflects its classification within the Cactaceae family. This nomenclature was formally established by the botanist John Torrey in 1838. The species has several synonyms, including Echinocactus williamsii and Lophophora diffusa, which were used in earlier botanical literature.

Morphological Characteristics

Peyote is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a shallow root system that spreads horizontally. Its stem is short and thick, often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, paddle-shaped leaves that are blue-green in color and reduce in size as the plant matures. It bears a single, short inflorescence with yellow flowers that open in the early morning. The fruit is a small, green, spherical capsule that splits open to release small, dark brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Peyote can be distinguished by its small, flattened, ribbed stems and pale green to bluish-gray color. It lacks leaves and has a single, rounded, fleshy tuberous root. The presence of translucent, waxy spines and a lack of leaf structures help differentiate it from similar cacti.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Peyote is native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico and the southern United States. It thrives in arid climates with minimal rainfall. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Peyote typically blooms in late spring to early summer, around May to June, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, from August to October. For identification purposes, harvest timing is generally during the early to mid-growing season, when the plant is mature but before flowering begins.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Peyote, Lophophora williamsii, is closely related to species within the genus Lophophora, particularly Lophophora diffusa. It belongs to the family Cactaceae, sharing traits like ribbed stems and areoles. Its phylogenetic position reflects adaptations to arid environments and chemical defenses typical of cacti.
