Red-Seed Dandelion (Taraxacum erythrospermum)

Red-Seed Dandelion (Taraxacum erythrospermum) is a member of the Compositae family, native to South America, and Central America. Traditionally, its roots, leaves, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, bitter, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface pale white, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Radially symmetrical flowers with yellow disc florets surrounded by white ray florets arranged in a single head, with 16 to 32 white ray florets and a central yellow disk composed of numerous small tubular florets.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit, opposite branching pattern, glabrous surface, presence of persistent stipules.
