Botanical Identification

Red-Seed Dandelion, scientifically known as Taraxacum erythrospermum, is a perennial plant in the Asteraceae family. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is commonly called "red-seed dandelion" or "false dandelion." It has a taproot, alternate leaves, and yellow flowers with a distinctive crown. It is sometimes referred to as "herb of the sun" in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The Red-Seed Dandelion contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. These compounds work synergistically to support anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, enhancing the herb's efficacy in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Coumarin

Therapeutic Indications

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Decoction: Used for gastrointestinal disorders and inflammation.

Oil infusion: Used for skin conditions and topical pain relief.

Safety Profile

Red-Seed Dandelion is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated for pregnant and breastfeeding women due to potential risks to the fetus or infant. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
