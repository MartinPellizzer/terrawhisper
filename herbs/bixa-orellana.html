Bixin Tree

Bixa orellana

Bixin Tree (Bixa orellana) is a tropical plant native to the Americas, known for its bright red seeds that contain the natural red pigment called bixin, commonly used in food coloring and traditional medicine. It is primarily used to treat digestive issues such as stomach ulcers, diarrhea, and intestinal parasites due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Botanical Identification

Bixa orellana, commonly known as the bixin tree, belongs to the family Rubiaceae. It is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant has reddish roots, broad leaves, and produces small yellow flowers. It is also referred to as annatto tree or kokum in various regional contexts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Bixales
  • Family: Bixaceae
  • Genus: Bixa
  • Species: Bixa orellana

Active Compounds

The Bixin Tree contains key bioactive compounds like flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work together to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, enhancing the herb's therapeutic potential. Their synergistic interactions support its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and colds.

Safety Profile

The Bixin Tree is generally safe when used in moderation. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or with pre-existing medical conditions.
