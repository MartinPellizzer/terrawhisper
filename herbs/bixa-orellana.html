Bixa orellana

What's the taxonomical classification of Bixa orellana?

Bixa orellana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Malvales and within the family Bixaceae. Finally, it is situated in the genus Bixa, specifically identified as the species orellana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bixa orellana has woody, perennial shrubs or small trees characterized by simple, elliptical leaves with entire margins and a distinctively coriaceous texture. The foliage often features prominent venation that provides structural support to the broad leaf blades. During flowering, the plant produces small, fragrant blossoms that typically emerge in clusters from the axils of the leaves. These flowers eventually give rise to dense, ovoid capsules that are covered in fine, soft bristles. Inside these specialized fruits, numerous small, reddish seeds are housed, which contain the concentrated pigments characteristic of the genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical lowland rainforests of the Amazon Basin, primarily spanning across Brazil, Peru, and parts of Colombia. Its distribution is closely tied to high humidity and consistent warm temperatures found near river systems. Within these ecosystems, it thrives in the understory where it can access filtered sunlight. The species is not found in arid or high-altitude regions due to its specific moisture requirements. Consequently, its range is geographically restricted to the humid equatorial belt of South America.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Bixa orellana contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its seeds, leaves, and bark. The bright red seeds are harvested for their high concentration of carotenoids and antioxidants, which are used to combat oxidative stress. Extracts from the leaves are often applied topically or consumed to address inflammatory conditions and digestive issues. Additionally, the bark is utilized in traditional practices to treat skin ailments and promote wound healing. These various plant parts collectively offer a wide range of therapeutic properties in ethnobotanical applications.

What are the pharmacological activities of Bixa orellana?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antioxidant, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities. The presence of bioactive compounds like carotenoids allows it to combat oxidative stress through its potent antioxidant properties. These chemical constituents also serve to inhibit the growth of various pathogens, demonstrating significant antimicrobial and antibacterial effects. Furthermore, the plant exhibits the ability to reduce pain and swelling due to its analgesic and anti-inflammatory actions. Such diverse medicinal properties make Bixa orellana a valuable subject for pharmaceutical research.

As discussed in a study, Bixa orellana leaf extracts exhibit antioxidant, broad antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, analgesic, hypoglycemic, and antidiarrheal activities. While no studies have specifically assessed inhibiting urogenital infections, products have been used in folkloric medicine to treat gonorrhea. A 6 month study in humans was conducted using 750 mg of leaf powder per day. This specific study demonstrated no significant or serious adverse effects. Consequently, Bixa leaf extracts appear to be safe when given under current conditions of use.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds bixin, ellagic acid, tocotrienols, geranylgeraniol, and a significant total phenolic content. These specific substances contribute to the overall therapeutic potential and antioxidant properties of the species. Ellagic acid and the total phenolic content provide defense against oxidative stress in biological systems. The presence of tocotrienols suggests beneficial effects on cardiovascular and neurological health. Additionally, bixin and geranylgeraniol offer unique chemical profiles that are often studied for their bioactive roles.

As discussed in a study, Bixa orellana seeds are a source of the pigment bixin. This specific pigment is one of 4 pigments that can be used to intensify the natural color of products derived from red pepper. The 4 pigments identified in the research are bixin, lycopene, canthaxanthin, and beta-apo-8'-carotenal. Analytical methods such as thin layer or liquid chromatography are proposed to detect these components. The method can be used to control these pigments in their natural sources.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating bacterial infections, diabetes mellitus, diarrhea, inflammation, and skin diseases. Its bioactive compounds are often applied topically to soothe various skin diseases and reduce localized inflammation. When consumed, extracts may help manage symptoms related to diabetes mellitus by influencing glucose levels. What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be processed into oil from its seed extract, a fine powder, a concentrated tincture, or a liquid infusion for various applications. The red seed oil is most commonly utilized as a natural coloring agent in both culinary and cosmetic products. Dried seeds are often ground into a powder to be used as a pigment or spice in traditional recipes. Herbalists may create a tincture by soaking the plant parts in alcohol to extract specific bioactive compounds. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain when ingested in improper amounts. Some individuals may also experience adverse physical responses such as dizziness during consumption. Beyond digestive issues, the plant is known to trigger various allergic reactions in sensitive people. Monitoring these symptoms is essential for anyone interacting with the species.
