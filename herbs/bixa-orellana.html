Bixin Tree, scientifically known as Bixa orellana, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America, renowned for its medicinal and culinary uses.

It is a medicinal herb and spice, best known for its bright red-orange seeds that contain the carotenoid pigment bixin, which is responsible for its unique flavor and color. The primary benefits of the Bixin Tree include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating skin conditions, digestive issues, and infections. Traditionally, it has been used in indigenous cultures for its healing properties, such as in the treatment of wounds, toothaches, and as a dye for body art and textiles.

In modern wellness practices, the Bixin Tree is gaining attention for its potential in natural skincare products and as a source of natural food coloring, with its rare active compound bixin also showing promise in pharmaceutical applications.

Scientific and Botanical Profile

Bixin Tree, with botanical name Bixa orellana, is a tropical shrub or small tree belonging to the family Bixaceae.

Native to Central and South America, it is also known by various common names such as Bixa, Redwood Tree, Annatto, Achiote, Red Paint Tree, Achiote Tree, Orellana Tree, and Crimsonwood. Morphologically, it is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and small yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright orange, spiny capsules containing elliptical seeds.

The tree is well-known for its seeds, which contain the red-orange pigment bixin, used extensively in food coloring, traditional medicine, and natural dyes.

History and Cultural Relevance

Bixin Tree was used for centuries by indigenous and local communities across Central and South America for its vibrant red pigments and medicinal properties.

The tree, scientifically known as Bixa orellana, has a long history in traditional medicine systems, where its seeds were employed to treat ailments such as skin infections, inflammation, and digestive issues. In various cultural rituals, the Bixin Tree holds symbolic significance, often featured in ceremonies for fertility, protection, and spiritual cleansing. Its natural red pigment, extracted from the seeds, has been used in body art, food coloring, and even as a natural dye for textiles.

Today, traditional remedies derived from the Bixin Tree, such as topical applications for wound healing and as a natural anti-inflammatory, continue to be valued in folk medicine and complementary health practices.

Forms, Preparation and Usage

Bixa orellana has a variety of forms available, including fresh seeds, dried tincture, powder, essential oil, and capsule, each offering different methods of preparation and usage.

The plant can be prepared as a tea by steeping the dried seeds in hot water, or as a decoction by boiling the seeds for a longer period to extract more active compounds. It can also be made into an infusion, similar to tea, or applied topically as a paste or oil for skin conditions. For adults, the recommended dosage is typically 1-2 grams of the powdered seeds per day, while children may require lower doses if deemed safe by a healthcare provider.

Use should be limited to short durations, typically no more than two weeks, to avoid potential side effects and ensure safe long-term use.

Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Bixa orellana can be used as a medicinal plant for its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, but it should be used with caution due to its possible side effects.

Some reported side effects include gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, and skin irritation, particularly with excessive or prolonged use. It may interact with certain medications, such as anticoagulants or diabetes medications, potentially increasing the risk of adverse effects. Special populations, including pregnant and breastfeeding women, should avoid bixa orellana due to insufficient safety data, while individuals with chronic illnesses should consult a healthcare provider before use.

To ensure safe use, it is recommended to use bixa orellana in moderation, follow recommended dosages, and consult with a qualified healthcare professional before incorporating it into a treatment regimen.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Bixa orellana grows best in well-drained, loamy soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH, in a warm and sunny location with at least six hours of direct sunlight daily.

It requires consistent moisture during the growing season but should not be overwatered to avoid root rot, with regular watering during dry periods. To ensure healthy growth, it benefits from occasional fertilization with a balanced organic fertilizer and pruning to promote bushier growth and better fruit production. The plant is typically harvested when the orange-red seeds inside the pods have fully matured, which occurs around 6 to 8 months after planting, using a gentle technique to avoid damaging the pods.

After harvesting, the seeds should be dried in a cool, shaded area to preserve their potency, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dry place or refrigerated to maintain their medicinal properties for extended periods.
