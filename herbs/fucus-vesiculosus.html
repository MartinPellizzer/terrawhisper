Bladderwrack (Fucus vesiculosus)

Bladderwrack (Fucus vesiculosus) is a member of the Phaeophyceae family, native to Atlantic Ocean, Baltic Sea, and North Sea. Traditionally, its stems, resin, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.
