Cephalotaxus sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cephalotaxus sinensis?

Cephalotaxus sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified into the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales. This plant is a member of the family Cephalotaxaceae, falling under the genus Cephalotaxus, and is specifically identified by the species name sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cephalotaxus sinensis has evergreen, leathery leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape and arranged spirally along the branches. The foliage typically measures between 2 and 5 centimeters in length and possesses a dark green color with a somewhat glossy surface. Its woody stems exhibit a slow growth habit, often forming dense, spreading shrubs or small trees. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous cones that eventually produce fleshy, red-colored arils surrounding the seeds. These morphological traits are characteristic of the Cephalotaxaceae family, aiding in the plant's survival in subtropical forest environments.
