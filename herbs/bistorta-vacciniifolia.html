What is Bistorta vacciniifolia?

Bistorta vacciniifolia is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its glabrous stems, 20-60 cm tall, with alternate, simple, and ovate leaves, 3-10 cm long, and 2-6 cm wide, having a cordate base and an acuminate apex. The leaves are glabrous on both surfaces, and the margins are entire. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers, 4-6 mm in diameter, in a terminal, erect panicle, from June to September.

The flowers are dioecious, with staminate and pistillate flowers on separate plants.
