Eriobotrya cavaleriei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eriobotrya cavaleriei has large, leathery, evergreen leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and feature a densely pubescent, velvety underside. The leaf margins are typically finely serrated, while the upper surface remains relatively smooth and dark green. Its woody stems develop a rugged bark texture as the plant matures into a small tree or large shrub. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from axillary clusters, eventually giving way to fleshy, pome-like fruits. These fruits are characterized by their distinct shape and are often covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Eriobotrya cavaleriei can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential toxicity if large quantities of its seeds or unripe fruit are ingested. The seeds contain cyanogenic glycosides which may release cyanide into the bloodstream upon digestion. Some individuals might experience itching, redness, or hives if they come into direct contact with the plant's sap or foliage. Excessive consumption of the fruit can lead to nausea or stomach cramps in sensitive populations. Monitoring intake is essential to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
