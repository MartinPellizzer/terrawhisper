What side-effects this plant can have?

Pellegriniodendron diphyllum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping, if ingested. These adverse reactions often stem from the presence of toxic alkaloids or glycosides found within the plant tissue. If consumed in significant quantities, the toxins may lead to more systemic issues such as dizziness or heart palpitations. It is crucial to avoid any contact with the plant's sap, as it might also trigger localized skin irritation or allergic dermatitis. Because the specific toxicity levels can vary, any accidental ingestion requires immediate medical evaluation.
