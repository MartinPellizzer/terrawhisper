Viburnum opulus

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum opulus?

Viburnum opulus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Dipsacales and falls under the family Viburnaceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Viburnum, specifically within the species opulus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Viburnum opulus contains high concentrations of anthocyanins, flavonoids, iridoids, and organic acids like citric and malic acid. These specific chemical constituents provide the plant with potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of glycosides and various tannins contributes to its traditional use in supporting cardiovascular health and easing menstrual discomfort. Additionally, the high vitamin C content found in the berries helps to bolster the immune system. Researchers continue to study these bioactive compounds to better understand their potential therapeutic applications in modern medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Viburnum opulus?

Viburnum opulus has antispasmodic, analgesic, and emmenagogue properties that primarily target the relief of uterine cramping and dysmenorrhea. The plant contains various glycosides and flavonoids that help to relax smooth muscle tissues throughout the body. These bioactive compounds work to reduce the intensity of painful menstrual contractions and stabilize pelvic congestion. Additionally, the botanical extract can assist in regulating menstrual cycles by promoting blood flow to the pelvic region. Its mild sedative effects may also help alleviate the secondary physical tension caused by chronic pain.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat menstrual disorders, specifically dysmenorrhea, pelvic congestion, and uterine inflammation. Its active components act as a uterine tonic to regulate menstrual cycles and ease painful cramping. It is frequently employed in herbal medicine to address symptoms of heavy bleeding or irregular periods. Practitioners also utilize it to support the relief of pelvic pain associated with various gynecological issues. These medicinal applications primarily focus on the plant's ability to influence smooth muscle contraction within the reproductive system.

What are the herbal preparations of Viburnum opulus?

Viburnum opulus is prepared as a decoction of the dried roots, a tincture of the bark or rhizomes, or a syrup made from the ripened berries. The roots and bark are typically simmered in water to extract their astringent compounds for medicinal use. Tinctures provide a concentrated alcohol-based method to preserve the active constituents for long-term storage. Berries are often processed into syrups or jams to mitigate their natural tartness and make them more palatable. These various forms allow the plant's properties to be applied to different therapeutic needs.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Viburnum opulus can cause gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps if the raw berries are consumed without proper processing. The plant contains glycosides that may lead to more severe complications if ingested in large quantities. Some individuals might experience allergic skin reactions or rashes after direct contact with the plant material. It is generally advised to avoid using the plant during pregnancy because of its potential effects on uterine contractions. Always consult a medical professional before using any part of this shrub for medicinal purposes.
