Viburnum Opulus Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Viburnum opulus, commonly known as common guelder rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

This herb has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and diuretic effects, supporting urinary tract health and digestive function.

The bioactive constituents in Viburnum opulus include tannins, flavonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and decoctions can be made from its leaves and flowers to harness its medicinal benefits.

Health Benefits of viburnum opulus

Viburnum opulus has many health benefits, such as boosting the immune system due to its high content of antioxidants and polyphenols, which help neutralize free radicals and stimulate the production of immune cells.

It reduces inflammation by containing anti-inflammatory compounds like flavonoids and tannins, which inhibit the activity of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines in the body.

Viburnum opulus lowers cholesterol by promoting the excretion of low-density lipoprotein (LDL) and increasing the metabolism of high-density lipoprotein (HDL), thus improving overall lipid profiles.

It enhances circulation by improving blood flow and reducing the viscosity of blood, thanks to its vasodilatory properties and ability to support cardiovascular health.
