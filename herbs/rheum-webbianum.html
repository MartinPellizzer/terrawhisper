Rheum webbianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Rheum webbianum?

Rheum webbianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is further categorized into the family Polygonaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Rheum, with its unique designation being the species webbianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rheum webbianum has thick, fleshy rhizomes and large, basal leaves that are broadly ovate to cordate with wavy, irregularly dentate margins. The leaf surfaces often feature a wrinkled or rugose texture and are typically covered in fine hairs or scales. Long, sturdy petioles support these massive leaves, emerging directly from the ground level. As the plant matures, it produces a central, erect flowering stalk that can reach significant heights. The inflorescence consists of numerous small, densely packed flowers arranged in branched panicles.

What are the pharmacological activities of Rheum webbianum?

This plant has potent anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidant, anti-cancer, gastrointestinal control, and suppression of fibrosis pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to neutralize harmful free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within cellular environments. These chemical constituents also inhibit the development of malignant cells and mitigate the inflammatory processes that trigger disease. Furthermore, the plant plays a significant role in regulating gastrointestinal functions and preventing the excessive accumulation of connective tissue. Such diverse medicinal properties suggest that its extracts could serve as valuable therapeutic agents for various chronic conditions.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Rheum webbianum exhibits key pharmacological effects including anticancer activity, gastrointestinal control, anti-inflammatory properties, and suppression of fibrosis. In-vitro assessments of the methanolic and ethanolic extracts showed a dose and time-dependent reduction in HCT-116 cell viability by up to 85% and 90% at 200 μg/ml. The extracts effectively restored hematological parameters that had decreased by 49.13% in erythrocytes, 32.18% in hemoglobin, and 26.79% in hematocrit, while also addressing a 79.62% increase in white blood cells and a 68.96% rise in platelets. RW treatment significantly reduced serum ALT and AST levels which had increased by 36.78% and 33.12% due to DMH exposure. Furthermore, the extracts demonstrated antioxidant efficacy by reducing MDA levels, which had increased by 195.59%, and enhancing SOD, CAT, GSH, and GR activities that had decreased by 33%, 48%, 58%, and 49% respectively.
