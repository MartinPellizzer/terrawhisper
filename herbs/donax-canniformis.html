Donax canniformis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Donax canniformis has erect, rhizomatous stems that produce long, lanceolate leaves with parallel venation and prominent midribs. The foliage typically grows in dense clumps, reaching significant heights depending on the moisture availability of its habitat. Its flowers are arranged in terminal panicles, displaying delicate structures that are characteristic of the Donacaceae family. The plant often features a robust root system designed to stabilize it in wetland or riparian environments. These morphological traits allow it to thrive in saturated soils where competition for space is high.

      Donax canniformis is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using the plant's rhizomes and leaves. If ingested, the plant might lead to nausea, stomach cramps, or more severe digestive disturbances. Because the exact toxicological profile is not fully documented, caution is necessary when interacting with this species.
