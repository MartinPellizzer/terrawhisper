Aconitum changianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum changianum?

Aconitum changianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically situated within the genus Aconitum, and is identified by the specific epithet changianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aconitum changianum has herbaceous stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters, bearing pinnately divided leaves with deeply lobed, ovate segments. The inflorescence consists of dense racemes where the flowers exhibit a characteristic helmet-like upper sepal. These zygomorphic flowers are typically colored in shades of blue or violet and possess a prominent nectar-producing spur. The root system is composed of thick, tuberous rhizomes that store nutrients for the plant. Small, ovate bracts are positioned along the floral axis to support the developing buds.
