Cynanchum amplexicaule

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum amplexicaule has twining, herbaceous stems that wrap tightly around host plants, opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate, and small, tubular, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbel-like cymes. The foliage is typically smooth and lacks significant pubescence, allowing the plant to climb efficiently through dense vegetation. Its leaf bases are notably expanded or amplexicaul, meaning they partially clasp the stem, which is a defining characteristic of the species. The inflorescence consists of multiple small blossoms that emerge from axillary positions. These delicate flowers eventually produce follicle-type fruits containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
