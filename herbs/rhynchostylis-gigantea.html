Rhynchostylis gigantea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhynchostylis gigantea has a monopodial growth habit characterized by thick, fleshy, pendulous roots and large, distichous, oblong leaves that grow in pairs. The leaves are typically dark green and leathery, providing a sturdy base for the plant's elongated stems. Long, drooping inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils, bearing numerous fragrant, waxy flowers. Each flower features a prominent, elongated lip that is a defining characteristic of the species. The overall structure of the plant is designed to cling to trees in tropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning from the eastern Himalayas through Myanmar, Thailand, Laos, Vietnam, and into southern China. It thrives primarily in humid, lowland forest environments where it can grow as an epiphyte on trees. Its range extends southward through the Malay Peninsula and across the islands of Indonesia. Within these diverse territories, the species is often found in areas with consistent moisture and dappled sunlight. This widespread distribution allows the orchid to inhabit various microclimates across the Indo-Malayan realm.
