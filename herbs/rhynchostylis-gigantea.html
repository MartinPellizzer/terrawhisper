What's the taxonomical classification of Rhynchostylis gigantea?

Rhynchostylis gigantea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Asparagales. This beautiful plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, specifically falling within the genus Rhynchostylis, with the species name gigantea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhynchostylis gigantea has a monopodial growth habit characterized by thick, fleshy, pendulous roots and large, distichous, oblong leaves that grow in pairs. The leaves are typically dark green and leathery, providing a sturdy base for the plant's elongated stems. Long, drooping inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils, bearing numerous fragrant, waxy flowers. Each flower features a prominent, elongated lip that is a defining characteristic of the species. The overall structure of the plant is designed to cling to trees in tropical environments.
