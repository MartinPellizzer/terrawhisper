What is Geum humile?

Geum humile is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It has a low-growing, spreading habit, reaching 10-30 cm in height, with a woody base and hairy stems and leaves. The leaves are pinnately compound, with 3-5 leaflets, 1-3 cm long, and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pinkish-red flowers, 1-2 cm in diameter, in a dense, flat-topped inflorescence, from June to September.

The flowers are hermaphroditic and have 5 petals and a 5-merous calyx.

What ailments can you heal with this herb?

The most common ailments you can heal with Geum humile are digestive issues, such as irritable bowel syndrome, by reducing inflammation and improving gut health.

Geum humile has also been used to treat skin conditions, including eczema, due to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. In traditional medicine, it has been employed to alleviate respiratory problems like bronchitis by acting as an expectorant and loosening mucus. Additionally, Geum humile has been used to treat urinary tract infections by inhibiting the growth of pathogens and reducing inflammation.

Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties also make it effective in treating wounds and preventing infection.

What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

The main therapeutic properties of Geum humile are anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant.

The anti-inflammatory property of Geum humile is attributed to the presence of flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines. The antimicrobial property of this plant is due to the presence of saponins, which can disrupt the cell membrane of microorganisms, thereby inhibiting their growth and reproduction. The antioxidant property of Geum humile is attributed to the presence of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and ascorbic acid, which can scavenge free radicals and prevent oxidative stress.

Additionally, Geum humile has been found to exhibit hepatoprotective and gastroprotective properties, which are likely due to the presence of saponins and other bioactive compounds that can protect the liver and stomach from damage.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Geum humile are flavonoids, specifically quercetin, kaempferol, and isorhapontigenin, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The plant also contains phenolic acids, such as caffeic acid and ferulic acid, which have been shown to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria and fungi. Geum humile also contains triterpenoid saponins, which may have anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects. Additionally, the plant contains alkaloids, including geumonin and geumaline, which have been reported to have antimicrobial and antiviral activities.

These constituents contribute to the plant's potential therapeutic applications in treating various conditions, including inflammation, infections, and oxidative stress.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Geum humile are geum tea, geum tincture, infusion, geum salve, and infused oil.

Geum tea is prepared by steeping the flowers in hot water to treat gastrointestinal issues and fever, while geum tincture is a concentrated liquid extract used to treat respiratory problems and skin conditions. Geum infusion is similar to tea but made with cold water and used to treat digestive issues and as a calming agent. Geum salve is a topical application made by mixing geum flowers with a carrier oil, such as beeswax, to treat skin irritations and wounds, while infused oil is used to treat skin conditions and as a carrier oil for aromatherapy.

These preparations are effective due to the presence of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and other bioactive compounds in Geum humile that exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.
