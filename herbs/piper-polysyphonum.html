Piper polysyphonum

What's the taxonomical classification of Piper polysyphonum?

Piper polysyphonum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the biological hierarchy through the order Piperales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Piperaceae and is categorized under the genus Piper, ultimately being identified by its specific species name, polysyphonum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper polysyphonum has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by opposite, ovate leaves with acuminate tips and a distinctively serrated margin. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a slightly coriaceous texture and prominent venation patterns. Its inflorescences consist of dense, cylindrical spikes that are typically reddish or brownish in color. These spikes are composed of numerous small, closely packed flowers arranged along a central axis. The plant's stems are often quadrangular in cross-section during early growth stages before becoming more cylindrical and woody.
