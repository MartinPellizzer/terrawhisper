Saxifraga tangutica

What's the taxonomical classification of Saxifraga tangutica?

Saxifraga tangutica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the biological hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Saxifrages (Saxifragales) and belongs to the family Saxifragaceae. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Saxifraga, and its unique species designation, tangutica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga tangutica has a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are bright green, slightly hairy, and feature serrated margins.

These leaves grow densely together to form a compact mound close to the rocky substrate. From the center of this rosette, a slender, upright flowering stalk emerges to support multiple small blossoms. The flowers typically possess five white petals and prominent yellowish centers, often appearing in small clusters.

This low-growing structure allows the plant to thrive in harsh, alpine environments.
