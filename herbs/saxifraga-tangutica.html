Saxifraga tangutica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga tangutica has a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are bright green, slightly hairy, and feature serrated margins. These leaves grow densely together to form a compact mound close to the rocky substrate. From the center of this rosette, a slender, upright flowering stalk emerges to support multiple small blossoms. The flowers typically possess five white petals and prominent yellowish centers, often appearing in small clusters. This low-growing structure allows the plant to thrive in harsh, alpine environments.
