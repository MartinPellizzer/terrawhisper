Mimosa tenuiflora

Mimosa tenuiflora, commonly known as jateorhiza or mucuna, is a plant species native to the tropical forests of Central and South America.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and gastrointestinal disorders, and its health benefits include antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

The therapeutic actions of Mimosa tenuiflora include analgesic, antispasmodic, and expectorant properties, which can help relieve pain, muscle spasms, and respiratory issues.

The bioactive constituents of the plant include alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, such as jateorrhizine and mucuna alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
