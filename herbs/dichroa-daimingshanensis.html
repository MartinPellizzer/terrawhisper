Dichroa daimingshanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichroa daimingshanensis has perennial herbaceous stems that grow from a rhizomatous base, bearing lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins and acute apices. The foliage typically exhibits a bright green coloration and is arranged alternately along the slender stems. Its reproductive structures consist of small, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers feature distinct petals and stamens that are characteristic of the Melastomataceae family. The fruit develops into a small capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.
