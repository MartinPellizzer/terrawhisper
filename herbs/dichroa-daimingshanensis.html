Dichroa daimingshanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dichroa daimingshanensis?

Dichroa daimingshanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cornales. This plant is a representative of the family Hydrangeaceae, situated within the genus Dichroa, and is specifically identified by its species name, daimingshanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichroa daimingshanensis has perennial herbaceous stems that grow from a rhizomatous base, bearing lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins and acute apices.

The foliage typically exhibits a bright green coloration and is arranged alternately along the slender stems. Its reproductive structures consist of small, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers feature distinct petals and stamens that are characteristic of the Melastomataceae family.

The fruit develops into a small capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.
