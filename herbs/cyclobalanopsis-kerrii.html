What's the taxonomical classification of Cyclobalanopsis kerrii?

Cyclobalanopsis kerrii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fagales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Fagaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Cyclobalanopsis, and is identified by its species name, kerrii.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cyclobalanopsis kerrii can cause severe skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger allergic reactions in sensitive individuals upon direct physical contact. These reactions often manifest as redness, itching, or swelling at the site of exposure. If the sap or foliage is accidentally swallowed, it can lead to nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting. Because scientific documentation on this specific species is limited, caution should be exercised when handling it.
