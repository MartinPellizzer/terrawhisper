What are the horticulural aspects of Centaurium erythraea?

Centaurium erythraea grow in well-drained soil and partial shade. The ideal conditions include a slightly acidic pH between 5.5 and 6.5. Full sun can lead to leaf scorch and root rot. It thrives in moist but not waterlogged areas.

Plant Centaurium erythraea in the spring or autumn, when temperatures range from 10 to 15°C (50-59°F). Water sparingly until established. Plant in a 15-20 cm (6-8 in) deep hole, with the crown at soil level. Space 15-30 cm (6-12 in) apart for optimal growth.

Harvest Centaurium erythraea leaves and flowers from mid-summer to early autumn, when in full bloom. Pinch or cut off individual stems to prevent seed production and promote regeneration. Harvest in the morning, after dew has dried, to minimize leaf bruising.

Pests and diseases affecting Centaurium erythraea include aphids, slugs, and snails, as well as root rot caused by Phytophthora and Pythium. Leaf spot caused by Cercospora and Septoria can also occur. Regular monitoring and treatment as needed are necessary to prevent infestation and disease spread.
