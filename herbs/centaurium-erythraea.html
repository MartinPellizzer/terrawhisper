Red Top

Centaurium erythraea

Red Top (Centaurium erythraea) is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments.

Botanical Identification

Red Top, or Centaurium erythraea, is a perennial herb in the family Rubiaceae. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is known by regional names like "Red Top" and "Bloodroot." The plant has red stems, lance-shaped leaves, and small, red flowers. It is occasionally referenced in herbal texts under synonyms like "Centaury."

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Caryophyllales
  • Family: Caryophyllaceae
  • Genus: Centaurium
  • Species: Centaurium erythraea

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a mild sedative.

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and to stimulate appetite.

Safety Profile

Red Top (Centaurium erythraea) should be used with caution during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. It is contraindicated in individuals with known allergies to plants in the Rubiaceae family. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
