Anise (Pimpinella anisum) Compounds

The primary compounds of Anise include essential oil, which gives it its distinctive aroma, and coumarin, known for its antispasmodic properties. Phenolic acids and flavonoids contribute to its antioxidant effects, while terpenoids and glycosides support digestive health. Saponins, tannins, and alkaloids also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Anise (Pimpinella anisum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Anise (Pimpinella anisum) contains compounds like anethol and estragol, which contribute to its aromatic profile and medicinal properties. These oils are commonly used in herbal medicine for their carminative, antispasmodic, and expectorant effects.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Anise (Pimpinella anisum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has mild antispasmodic and carminative effects. Coumarin is also used in traditional medicine to support digestive health and relieve mild gastrointestinal discomfort.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Anise (Pimpinella anisum). It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has antioxidant properties that support digestive health and may help reduce inflammation. This compound is found in significant amounts in the seeds of Anise, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are naturally occurring compounds found in Anise (Pimpinella anisum). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Anise (Pimpinella anisum). They contribute to the plant's characteristic aroma and possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds are key to Anise's use in traditional medicine for digestive and respiratory support.

6. Glycoside

Active compounds of Anise (Pimpinella anisum) include the glycoside anethol. Anethol contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has mild carminative and antispasmodic properties. It is often used in traditional medicine to alleviate digestive discomfort and support respiratory health.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Anise (Pimpinella anisum). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and may have mild detoxifying properties. Saponins can help support digestive health and may assist in reducing inflammation. They are part of the complex mix of bioactive compounds in Anise that contribute to its medicinal uses.

8. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Anise (Pimpinella anisum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may aid in reducing inflammation and digestive issues. Tannins in Anise are typically present in lower concentrations compared to other herbs but still play a role in its traditional medicinal uses.

9. Alkaloid

Anise (Pimpinella anisum) contains the alkaloid anisodamine. This compound is known for its antispasmodic and bronchodilatory effects, making it useful in treating gastrointestinal and respiratory conditions. It is derived from the seeds of the plant and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its therapeutic properties.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Anise (Pimpinella anisum). It contributes to the plant's color and may have antioxidant properties. While not the primary active compound in Anise, anthocyanin supports the plant's overall medicinal profile by potentially enhancing its therapeutic effects.
