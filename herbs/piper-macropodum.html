Piper macropodum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper macropodum has woody stems with ovate to elliptical leaves that feature an acute apex and an entire or slightly wavy margin. The leaf arrangement is typically opposite or sub-opposite along the branches. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. These spikes are characterized by a distinctive elongated shape that gives the species its name. The fruits are small drupes that develop directly from the floral axis.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Piper macropodum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if consumed improperly. While the plant is often used in traditional medicine, its chemical constituents may trigger localized inflammation upon contact with sensitive tissue. Some individuals might experience redness or itching if the plant matter touches the skin directly. If ingested in large amounts, it could potentially lead to stomach upset or nausea. It is essential to exercise caution as individual sensitivities to its specific alkaloids can vary significantly.
