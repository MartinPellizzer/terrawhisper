Cardamine macrophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cardamine macrophylla has large, deeply lobed, compound leaves that typically consist of several pairs of leaflets arranged along a central petiole. The individual leaflets are often ovate or elliptical in shape with serrated or dentate margins. This herbaceous perennial features a basal rosette from which erect flowering stems emerge during the growing season. The inflorescence is characterized by small, four-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. These floral structures are arranged in racemes at the terminal ends of the stems.
