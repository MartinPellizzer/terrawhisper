Mischocarpus pentapetalus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mischocarpus pentapetalus has woody climbing vines characterized by pentamerous flowers, elliptical leaves with entire margins, and small, fleshy drupes. The leaves are typically arranged alternately and possess distinct pinnate venation patterns. Its inflorescences consist of small, pale flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The fruit develops into a single-seeded drupe that often turns dark or reddish upon maturity. These morphological features help distinguish the species from other members of the Sapindaceae family.
