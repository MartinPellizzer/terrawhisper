Billbergia pyramidalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Billbergia pyramidalis?

Billbergia pyramidalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its specific biological hierarchy, it falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Bromeliaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement within the genus Billbergia, specifically identified as the species pyramidalis.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Billbergia pyramidalis can cause localized skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if the plant's sap or foliage comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. While it is generally considered non-toxic to humans, some individuals may experience redness or itching upon touching the plant. The physical structure of the leaves also possesses a slightly waxy or textured surface that might trigger sensitivities in certain people. There is limited scientific research available regarding the systemic effects of ingesting this specific bromeliad. Therefore, cautious handling is recommended to avoid potential dermatological reactions.
