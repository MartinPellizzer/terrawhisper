Billbergia pyramidalis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Billbergia pyramidalis has a rosette-forming growth habit characterized by stiff, channeled leaves that form a central water-holding tank. These leaves are typically lanceolate in shape and exhibit a smooth, somewhat waxy texture on their surfaces. The foliage often displays subtle longitudinal striations and can range in color from light green to silvery hues depending on light exposure. At the center of the plant, a prominent inflorescence emerges, featuring tightly packed, brightly colored bracts that create a conical or pyramidal structure. This striking floral arrangement serves as the defining morphological feature of the species.
