What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eragrostis minor has an annual growth habit characterized by slender, tufted stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 25 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of several small, spike-like panicles that emerge from the leaf axils. The spikelets are compact and contain a single floret enclosed by a membranous lemma.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Eragrostis minor can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential digestive upset if ingested by humans or livestock. While primarily considered a common weed, its coarse texture and sharp edges may physically scratch the skin upon contact. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or a mild rash after handling the plant without protection. If consumed in large quantities, it could lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or minor inflammation in the digestive tract.
