What's the taxonomical classification of Eragrostis minor?

Eragrostis minor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Poales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Poaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Eragrostis, with the specific epithet minor identifying the individual species.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eragrostis minor has an annual growth habit characterized by slender, tufted stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 25 centimeters.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often featuring a distinct midrib and a somewhat involute or rolled appearance. Its inflorescence consists of several small, spike-like panicles that emerge from the leaf axils. The spikelets are compact and contain a single floret enclosed by a membranous lemma.

These morphological traits distinguish it from similar grass species found in moist, disturbed habitats.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Eragrostis minor can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential digestive upset if ingested by humans or livestock. While primarily considered a common weed, its coarse texture and sharp edges may physically scratch the skin upon contact. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or a mild rash after handling the plant without protection. If consumed in large quantities, it could lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or minor inflammation in the digestive tract. There is limited clinical research available regarding long-term toxicological effects in humans.
