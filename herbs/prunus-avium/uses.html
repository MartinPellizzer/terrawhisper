European Plum (Prunus avium) Uses

European plum is used to treat cough, asthma, and respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It also supports wound healing and reduces skin infections, irritation, and dermatitis. Its anti-inflammatory effects may help with gout and other inflammatory conditions.

European Plum (Prunus avium) Monograph

1. Cough

European plum is used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant properties, which help clear mucus from the respiratory tract. It also has mild antispasmodic effects that can ease bronchial irritation. The fruit's high vitamin C content supports immune function during respiratory infections. It is often prepared as a syrup or tea for its calming and healing effects on the throat and lungs.

2. Asthma

European plum has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It is believed to help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing inflammation and promoting easier breathing. Its antispasmodic properties may ease bronchial constriction, though it should be used under professional guidance.

3. Respiratory infections

European plums have been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Their high vitamin C content may help reduce the duration and severity of colds and other respiratory infections. The anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties of plum compounds can soothe irritated airways and boost immune function.

4. Skin infection

European Plum (Prunus avium) has been used traditionally to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The fruit's essential oils and compounds help combat bacterial and fungal infections. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to reduce inflammation and promote healing. Its astringent qualities also aid in drying out wounds and preventing further infection.

5. Wound healing

European Plum is used in herbal medicine to support wound healing due to its high vitamin C content, which promotes collagen synthesis. The fruit's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties help reduce infection risk and speed up tissue repair. It is often prepared as a poultice or infused oil to apply directly to wounds.

6. Skin infections

European Plum has antimicrobial properties that help treat skin infections. Its compounds inhibit bacterial growth, making it effective for conditions like eczema and fungal infections. It can be applied topically as a poultice or used internally to support immune response. Regular use may reduce inflammation and promote healing.

7. Skin irritation

European plum is used to treat skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. The fruit's high content of antioxidants and vitamin C helps reduce redness and promote skin healing. It can be applied topically as a poultice or used in herbal infusions for localized relief. Its mild nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin conditions.

8. Dermatitis

European Plum is used externally for dermatitis due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps soothe irritated skin and reduce redness. A poultice or infused oil made from the fruit can be applied to affected areas. It is particularly effective for eczema and psoriasis when used as part of a broader herbal regimen.

9. Inflammation

European plums contain compounds like anthocyanins and flavonoids that exhibit anti-inflammatory properties. These help reduce swelling and pain associated with inflammatory conditions. They are often used in herbal formulations to support joint health and manage mild inflammatory symptoms. Regular consumption may contribute to overall inflammatory balance in the body.

10. Gout

European plum has been used in herbal medicine to support joint health and reduce inflammation, making it beneficial for gout management. Its high levels of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds may help alleviate pain and swelling associated with gout. It is often recommended as part of a holistic approach to managing gout symptoms.
