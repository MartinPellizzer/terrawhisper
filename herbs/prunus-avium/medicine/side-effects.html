10 Possible Side Effects Of Prunus Avium

Prunus avium has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

The causes of these side effects are often due to excessive consumption, pre-existing medical conditions, or allergic sensitivities.

For example, severe allergic reactions can cause anaphylaxis, leading to respiratory failure, while prolonged consumption can lead to digestive problems, such as diarrhea or stomach pain, significantly impacting one's daily activities and quality of life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Prunus avium causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of a protein called Pru p 1.

This protein is responsible for triggering an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the fruit's pit or stone contains amygdalin, which can release cyanide when ingested, causing adverse reactions in those with certain genetic mutations.

2. Triggers digestive upset

Prunus avium triggers digestive upset due to its high content of sorbitol, a natural sugar that is not fully absorbed by the body.

When consumed, sorbitol can cause an imbalance in the gut microbiome, leading to symptoms such as bloating, gas, and diarrhea.

Additionally, the pit or stone of the cherry, which contains amygdalin, a compound that breaks down into cyanide, may also contribute to digestive issues when ingested.

3. Induces vomiting

Prunus avium induces vomiting in some individuals due to its high concentration of anthocyanins and flavonoids.

These bioactive compounds can stimulate the gut's natural defenses, triggering a response that leads to nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, the fruit's tart taste and acidity may also contribute to this side effect by irritating the stomach lining and causing discomfort.

4. Lowers blood pressure

Prunus avium lowers blood pressure due to its rich content of anthocyanins and flavonoids, which have a direct impact on blood vessels.

The bioactive compounds in cherry extract cause vasodilation, leading to reduced peripheral resistance and decreased systolic pressure. This effect is attributed to the inhibition of angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) activity, thereby preventing the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II, a potent vasoconstrictor.

As a result, blood vessels relax, and blood pressure declines.

5. Increases heart rate

Prunus avium increases heart rate due to its high concentration of epinephrine and norepinephrine, natural stimulants that can cause a rapid heartbeat.

These compounds, found in the fruit's skin and pit, can stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to increased blood pressure and heart rate.

This side effect is more likely to occur when consuming large amounts or using Prunus avium for extended periods.

6. Causes headaches

Prunus avium causes headaches due to its high concentration of a compound called amygdalin.

When consumed, this compound can release cyanide into the body, leading to increased blood pressure and constricted blood vessels, which may cause mild to moderate headaches in some individuals.

The severity and frequency of these headaches can vary depending on the individual's sensitivity to the compound and the amount of cherry consumed.

7. Produces dizziness

Prunus avium produces dizziness due to its ability to interact with certain medications and alter blood pressure.

The high levels of potassium and anthocyanins present in the fruit can affect heart function and lead to a decrease in blood pressure, causing lightheadedness or dizziness.

Additionally, the tartaric acid found in cherries can irritate the stomach, leading to digestive issues that may also contribute to feelings of dizziness.

8. Creates nausea

Prunus avium creates nausea due to its high concentration of a compound called amygdalin.

When consumed, amygdalin breaks down into cyanide, which can cause stomach upset and discomfort leading to feelings of queasiness and vomiting in some individuals.

Additionally, the tartaric acid present in cherries may also contribute to the development of gastrointestinal issues, further exacerbating nausea symptoms.

9. Elicits anxiety

Prunus avium elicits anxiety in some individuals due to its unique chemical composition.

The fruit contains a compound called amygdalin, which can release cyanide and other toxins into the body when ingested. This release of toxic compounds can cause feelings of unease, apprehension, and even full-blown anxiety attacks in susceptible individuals.

Additionally, the high levels of phenolic acids and flavonoids in cherries may also contribute to anxiogenic effects by influencing neurotransmitter activity and brain chemistry.

10. Stimulates insomnia

Prunus avium stimulates insomnia due to its high concentration of caffeine-like compounds called heterosides.

These alkaloids can interfere with normal sleep patterns by increasing alertness and energy levels, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

Additionally, Prunus avium's stimulating effects on the nervous system may also contribute to insomnia by causing restlessness and anxiety.
