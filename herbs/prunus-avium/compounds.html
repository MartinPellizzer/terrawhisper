European Plum (Prunus avium) Compounds

The primary compounds in European Plum include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins and glycosides provide astringent effects, while anthocyanins give the fruit its vibrant color and also have antioxidant benefits. Coumarin and essential oils contribute to its aromatic profile and potential therapeutic uses.

European Plum (Prunus avium) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in European Plum (Prunus avium). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. These compounds may support cardiovascular health and have anti-inflammatory effects.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in European Plum (Prunus avium). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports overall health by enhancing immune function and protecting cells from damage.

3. Tannin

Tannin in European Plum (Prunus avium) contributes to its astringent properties and may support digestive health. It interacts with proteins, potentially aiding in reducing inflammation and promoting gut healing. Tannins are also believed to have antimicrobial effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in European Plum (Prunus avium). It contributes to the plant's anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is also used in herbal medicine for its potential to support cardiovascular health. Its presence in European Plum highlights the plant's therapeutic value in traditional herbal practices.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in European Plum (Prunus avium). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic effects, particularly in supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides are naturally occurring substances that often have mild pharmacological properties when derived from plants like the European Plum.

6. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in European Plum (Prunus avium), is a flavonoid responsible for the fruit's vibrant color. It exhibits strong antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. These effects support overall health and may contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Essential oil

The essential oil of European Plum (Prunus avium) contains volatile compounds like limonene and alpha-pinene. These components contribute to its aromatic properties and may support digestive and respiratory health. The oil is often used in herbal medicine for its calming and antispasmodic effects.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in European Plum (Prunus avium). They contribute to the plant's aroma and may have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds are often used in herbal medicine for their potential therapeutic benefits.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in European Plum (Prunus avium). It exhibits mild detergent properties and may support digestive health by aiding bile secretion. Saponins also contribute to the plant's potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in European Plum (Prunus avium). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by influencing various physiological processes. Alkaloids in European Plum are studied for their potential effects on digestion and mood regulation. Their presence highlights the plant's role in traditional herbal medicine.
