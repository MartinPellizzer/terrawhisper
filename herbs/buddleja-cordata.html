Buddleja cordata

What's the taxonomical classification of Buddleja cordata?

Buddleja cordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Lamiales and the family Scrophulariaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific genus, Buddlejia, and its species designation, cordata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Buddlejia cordata contains various bioactive phytochemicals such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids that contribute to its medicinal potential. These chemical constituents are often studied for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties within traditional botanical applications. Specific studies suggest that the plant's extracts may exhibit antimicrobial activity against certain pathogens. Researchers focus on isolating these individual compounds to better understand their pharmacological effects on human health. Further investigation into its chemical profile continues to explore its role in developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Buddleja cordata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive disorders, inflammatory conditions, skin infections, and certain types of fever. Traditional healers often prepare decoctions from the leaves to alleviate stomach aches and gastrointestinal distress. Its antimicrobial properties make it a useful remedy for treating topical wounds and skin irritations. Some cultures also utilize the plant to help reduce systemic inflammation within the body. These medicinal applications are rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical practices.
