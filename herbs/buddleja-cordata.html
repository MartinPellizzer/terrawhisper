Buddleja cordata

What's the taxonomical classification of Buddleja cordata?

Buddleja cordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Lamiales and the family Scrophulariaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific genus, Buddlejia, and its species designation, cordata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buddlejia cordata has heart-shaped, opposite leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and are covered in fine, soft hairs on both surfaces. These leaves feature prominent veins and slightly serrated margins that contribute to their distinctive shape. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters with multiple woody stems. During the flowering season, it produces dense, terminal panicles of small, tubular flowers that are usually light purple or lilac in color. This morphological structure attracts various pollinators, making the plant a significant feature in many garden ecosystems.
