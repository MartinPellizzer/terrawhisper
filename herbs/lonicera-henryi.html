Lonicera henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera henryi has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape and measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly wavy, and the texture can range from smooth to somewhat pubescent. This deciduous shrub features arching woody stems that produce small, tubular, yellowish-white flowers during the blooming season. These flowers are arranged in pairs or small clusters along the branches. The plant eventually develops small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark reddish or black color upon ripening.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the southeastern United States, specifically distributed across parts of Alabama, Georgia, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Tennessee. Its range is concentrated in the Appalachian region and the Piedmont area. Localized populations often thrive in shaded areas where moisture is consistent throughout the growing season.

    What are the herbal preparations of Lonicera henryi?

    Lonicera henryi is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid made by boiling the stems and leaves in water to extract its medicinal compounds. This boiled liquid can also be used as a topical wash for skin irritations or diluted into a tea for internal consumption. Some traditional practitioners may transform the plant material into a tincture by soaking it in alcohol to preserve its active constituents. These liquid preparations are the most common methods for utilizing the plant's properties in herbal medicine. Occasionally, the dried bark or foliage might be ground into a fine powder to be used in poultices.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lonicera henryi can cause skin irritation, nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains various alkaloids and compounds that may trigger allergic reactions upon contact with the skin. If a person consumes the berries or stems, it often leads to significant gastrointestinal distress. Some individuals might also experience dizziness or headaches following accidental exposure. It is important to avoid contact with the plant to prevent these adverse physiological responses.
