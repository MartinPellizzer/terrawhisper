What to know about bog myrtle before using it medicinally

Bog myrtle, scientifically known as Myrica gale, is a versatile and fascinating herb with a variety of uses and properties.

Rich in health benefits, bog myrtle contains several compounds that can improve overall well-being, including antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and insect-repelling properties. In the culinary world, bog myrtle leaves are often used as a spice or flavoring agent, particularly in dishes that feature game or fish.

As a horticultural plant, bog myrtle is a hardy and adaptable shrub that thrives in wet and boggy environments, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking to add some variety to their landscapes. From a botanical perspective, bog myrtle is a member of the Myricaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of small, yellow-green flowers.

Historically, bog myrtle has been used for a variety of purposes, including as a medicinal herb, a flavoring agent, and even as a component in traditional Scottish bagpipes.
