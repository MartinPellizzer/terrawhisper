What are the medicinal preparations of Silphium laciniatum?

Silphium laciniatum has different medicinal preparations, such as dried leaves, essential oil, and leaf infusion.

The dried leaves are used to treat respiratory issues like bronchitis and coughs due to their expectorant properties. Essential oil is used topically to relieve pain, reduce inflammation, and improve skin conditions. Leaf infusion is consumed as a tea to alleviate digestive issues like indigestion, nausea, and diarrhea.

Additionally, alexandrian laurel preparations are also used to treat fever, rheumatism, and menstrual cramps due to their antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.

What are the possible side effect of using Silphium laciniatum improperly?

Improper use of Silphium laciniatum increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, and vomiting due to its bitter taste.

It can cause allergic reactions like hives, itching, and swelling, while excessive consumption may lead to dizziness, headaches, and blurred vision.

Additionally, it may interact with blood thinners, increase blood pressure, and exacerbate kidney or liver problems, particularly when taken in large quantities or without proper medical supervision.
