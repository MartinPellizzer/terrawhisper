Mango (Mangifera indica) Actions

Mango has several primary therapeutic actions, including tonic, anti-inflammatory, and diuretic properties. It also acts as an antispasmodic, carminative, and expectorant. Additionally, it has astringent, bitter, stimulant, and sedative effects, making it a versatile herb in herbal medicine.

Mango (Mangifera indica) Monograph

1. Tonic

Mango has a tonic action, supporting overall vitality and energy levels. It nourishes the body and helps restore strength, particularly after illness or during periods of stress. Rich in nutrients, it promotes digestive health and enhances immune function. Its mild, sweet flavor makes it easily digestible and beneficial for the digestive system.

2. Anti-inflammatory

Mango has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its high content of polyphenols and carotenoids. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and inhibit inflammatory pathways in the body. Studies suggest that mango extracts can suppress the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines. This makes it beneficial for conditions involving chronic inflammation.

3. Diuretic

Mango has a diuretic action, promoting the excretion of excess fluids and toxins through urine. This effect helps reduce swelling and supports kidney function. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to manage fluid retention and urinary issues. The diuretic properties are attributed to its high potassium content and other bioactive compounds.

4. Antispasmodic

Mango has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing smooth muscle tissue. This effect is attributed to its high content of nutrients like potassium and magnesium, which support muscle function and reduce involuntary contractions. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to address digestive and muscular discomfort.

5. Carminative

Mango has a carminative action, aiding in the expulsion of gas from the digestive tract. This property helps relieve bloating and discomfort. It supports healthy digestion and reduces flatulence. The fruit's enzymes and nutrients contribute to this effect.

6. Expectorant

Mango has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It stimulates the production of bronchial secretions, making it beneficial for conditions like coughs and bronchitis. This effect is attributed to its high content of enzymes and certain phytochemicals.

7. Astringent

Mango has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretions and tighten tissues. This property is beneficial for soothing sore throats, reducing excess mucus, and promoting wound healing. It is commonly used in traditional medicine for its ability to calm inflammation and support digestive health.

8. Bitter

Mango has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and helps clear heat from the body. This property supports detoxification and can aid in reducing inflammation. It is particularly beneficial for conditions involving dampness and stagnation. The bitter taste also enhances the absorption of nutrients.

9. Stimulant

Mango has a stimulant action, enhancing mental alertness and energy levels. It contains compounds that support nervous system function, aiding in focus and productivity. This effect is particularly beneficial during periods of fatigue or mental exhaustion. Its natural properties make it a useful herbal remedy for mild stimulation.

10. Sedative

Mango has a sedative action, helping to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety. It contains compounds like mangiferin and certain flavonoids that may promote relaxation. This effect supports its traditional use in herbal medicine for stress relief and sleep improvement.
