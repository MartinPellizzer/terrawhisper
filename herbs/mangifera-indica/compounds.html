Mango (Mangifera indica) Compounds

The primary compounds in mango include flavonoids, which are antioxidants that support heart health. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins and terpenoids have detoxifying and immune-boosting effects. Glycosides, coumarin, anthocyanins, essential oils, and alkaloids also play roles in therapeutic and protective functions.

Mango (Mangifera indica) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Mango (Mangifera indica). They contribute to the fruit's antioxidant properties and may support immune function and reduce inflammation. These compounds are often studied in herbal medicine for their potential therapeutic benefits.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Mango (Mangifera indica) that contributes to its medicinal properties. It exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's role in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and immune support.

3. Tannin

Tannin, found in Mangifera indica, contributes to the fruit's astringent properties. It has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine. Tannins may support digestive health and help in wound healing. They are commonly used in formulations for their therapeutic potential.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Mango (Mangifera indica). It possesses antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine. Saponin may support digestive health and immune function, contributing to the therapeutic potential of Mango in traditional remedies.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Mango (Mangifera indica). They contribute to the fruit's aroma and have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds are studied for their potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Mango (Mangifera indica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides are naturally occurring sugars bound to other molecules, enhancing the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Mango (Mangifera indica). It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. Coumarin also acts as a natural sunscreen, protecting the fruit from UV damage. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Mango in herbal medicine.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Mango (Mangifera indica). It acts as a powerful antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound contributes to the fruit's vibrant color and may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Mango (Mangifera indica) contains aromatic compounds like limonene and methyl anthranilate. It is used in traditional herbal medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation and applied topically or inhaled for therapeutic benefits.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of naturally occurring compounds found in Mango (Mangifera indica). While mango is not a primary source of alkaloids, some alkaloid-like compounds may contribute to its medicinal properties, though research on their specific roles in traditional herbal medicine remains limited.
