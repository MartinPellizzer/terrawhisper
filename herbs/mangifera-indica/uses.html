Mango (Mangifera indica) Uses

Mango is used to treat cough and asthma due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It also helps with tuberculosis and bronchitis by supporting respiratory health. The fruit aids in wound healing and managing skin and fungal infections. Additionally, it can alleviate gastrointestinal inflammation, diarrhea, and stomach ulcers due to its soothing and antimicrobial effects.

Mango (Mangifera indica) Monograph

1. Cough

Mango leaves are used in traditional medicine to alleviate cough symptoms. A decoction of dried leaves is prepared and consumed to soothe the throat and reduce inflammation. The expectorant properties help loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. This remedy is commonly used in Ayurveda and other herbal systems.

2. Asthma

Mango leaves contain compounds that may help reduce inflammation and bronchial constriction, potentially alleviating asthma symptoms. Traditional use involves steam inhalation of boiled leaves or consuming leaf extracts. However, scientific evidence remains limited, and it should not replace prescribed medical treatments. Always consult a healthcare professional before using mango for asthma.

3. Tuberculosis

Mango leaves and bark have been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. They contain compounds that may help reduce inflammation and ease symptoms of tuberculosis. In some regions, decoctions of these parts are used as complementary therapy alongside conventional treatments.

4. Bronchitis

Mango leaves and pulp are used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis. They help reduce inflammation and mucus production in the airways. The antimicrobial and antioxidant properties of mango support respiratory health. Regular consumption of mango-based remedies may ease coughing and improve breathing in bronchitis patients.

5. Skin infections

Mango (Mangifera indica) is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The fruit's pulp and leaves contain compounds that inhibit bacterial and fungal growth, helping to reduce symptoms of conditions like eczema and fungal infections. It is often applied topically as a poultice or used in herbal formulations for its soothing and healing effects.

6. Fungal infections

Mango (Mangifera indica) has shown potential in combating fungal infections due to its antimicrobial compounds, particularly in the bark and leaves. Extracts from these parts contain polyphenols and flavonoids that inhibit fungal growth. Research suggests its use in traditional herbal medicine for treating skin and respiratory fungal infections. Further studies are needed to confirm its efficacy and standardize its application.

7. Wound healing

Mango (Mangifera indica) is used in traditional herbal medicine for wound healing due to its high content of vitamins, antioxidants, and enzymes. The fruit's pulp and leaves are applied topically to promote tissue repair, reduce inflammation, and prevent infection. Its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties accelerate the healing process and enhance skin regeneration.

8. Gastrointestinal inflammation

Mango (Mangifera indica) has been traditionally used to address gastrointestinal inflammation due to its high content of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds. These properties help reduce irritation and inflammation in the digestive tract, supporting overall gut health. Its mild astringent properties also aid in soothing conditions like gastritis and inflammatory bowel disorders.

9. Diarrhea

Mango leaves contain tannins that help reduce intestinal inflammation and firm stools. They are used in traditional remedies to alleviate diarrhea by soothing the digestive tract. Fresh leaves can be boiled and consumed as a tea to provide relief. This method is commonly practiced in herbal medicine for its natural and gentle effects.

10. Stomach ulcers

Mango leaf extract contains tannins and flavonoids that promote gastric mucus secretion and protect the stomach lining. It helps reduce acid production and inflammation, making it effective for treating and preventing stomach ulcers. Regular consumption of mango leaf tea or extracts may support ulcer healing and prevent recurrence.
