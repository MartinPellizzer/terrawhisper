10 Possible Side Effects Of Mangifera Indica

Mangifera indica has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with medications.

These side effects can be caused by consuming excessive amounts, allergic sensitivities, or mixing it with other herbs or medications.

For example, gastrointestinal problems can lead to dehydration, which may worsen kidney function in individuals with pre-existing conditions, ultimately affecting their quality of life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Mangifera indica causes allergic reactions due to the presence of a protein called profilin in its fruit and leaves.

This protein can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing in some individuals. The allergenic potential is higher for those who consume mango juice or pulp, as it contains a higher concentration of profilin than the fruit itself.

Additionally, cross-reactivity with birch pollen may also occur, exacerbating the allergic response in susceptible individuals.

2. Triggers digestive issues

Mangifera indica triggers digestive issues due to its high content of sugars, fibers, and tannins.

The rapid absorption of sugars can lead to a sudden spike in blood sugar levels, causing discomfort, bloating, and diarrhea. Additionally, the fiber and tannins present in mango can be difficult for some individuals to digest, potentially resulting in constipation or stomach upset.

This is especially true for those with pre-existing digestive conditions or food sensitivities.

3. Produces respiratory problems

Mangifera indica produces respiratory problems due to its high allergenic potential.

The fruit's proteins and pollen can trigger allergic reactions in some individuals, causing symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

This is particularly common among people with pre-existing respiratory conditions or a history of allergies.

4. Lowers blood pressure

Mangifera indica lowers blood pressure due to its rich content of potassium, a mineral that helps counteract the effects of sodium in the body.

Regular consumption of mango can lead to increased potassium levels, which in turn dilate blood vessels and improve circulation, resulting in a decrease in blood pressure.

5. Increases heart rate

Mangifera indica increases heart rate due to its high concentration of flavonoids and polyphenols.

These bioactive compounds stimulate the nervous system, leading to a surge in cardiac activity. The increased heart rate is believed to be caused by the enhanced sympathetic tone and vasodilation induced by these compounds.

This effect can be attributed to the specific combination and concentration of flavonoids present in Mangifera indica, which has been linked to cardiovascular benefits.

6. Induces kidney damage

Mangifera indica induces kidney damage due to its high content of oxalate and anthraquinone glycosides.

These compounds can inhibit the function of the kidneys' nephrons, leading to a buildup of toxic substances in the body and potentially causing damage to the kidneys over time.

This may result in symptoms such as increased urination, blood in the urine, and kidney stones.

7. Causes skin irritation

Mangifera indica causes skin irritation due to its high concentration of urushiol, a potent allergen that can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

The oil present in the fruit's peel and pulp contains urushiol, which can cause redness, itching, and rashes on the skin when it comes into contact with human skin.

This sensitivity is often accompanied by inflammation and swelling, making Mangifera indica a potential allergen to be cautious of.

8. Enhances anxiety levels

Mangifera indica enhances anxiety levels due to its high concentration of polyphenolic compounds and flavonoids.

These bioactive compounds can cause an increase in cortisol production, leading to feelings of unease and heightened alertness. Additionally, the seed extract may interact with GABA receptors, further exacerbating anxiety symptoms.

As a result, individuals may experience increased tension, restlessness, and difficulty relaxing.

9. Produces sleep disturbances

Mangifera indica produces sleep disturbances due to its high content of flavonoids and polyphenols.

These compounds can interact with the body's natural sleep-wake cycle, causing an increase in cortisol levels and a decrease in melatonin production, leading to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep.

Additionally, the fruit's sweet and sour taste can stimulate the brain's reward system, making it difficult for individuals to wind down before bedtime.

10. Affects menstrual cycles

Mangifera indica affects menstrual cycles due to its estrogen-like properties.

The fruit's high content of phytoestrogens, particularly isoflavones and lignans, can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body. This may disrupt hormonal balances, leading to changes in menstrual patterns, such as irregular periods or delayed menstruation.

Additionally, mango's flavonoids and polyphenols can also affect the liver's metabolism of hormones, potentially contributing to menstrual cycle disturbances.
