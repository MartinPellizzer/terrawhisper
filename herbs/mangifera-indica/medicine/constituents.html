10 Medicinal Constituents Of Mangifera Indica (Mango)

Mangifera indica has active constituents such as vitamins A and C, potassium, and antioxidants like flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These constituents confer medicinal properties such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities, which can help alleviate conditions like diabetes and cardiovascular disease.

By improving insulin sensitivity and reducing oxidative stress, the constituents of mango can improve the quality of life for individuals with these conditions, enabling them to maintain a healthier lifestyle and reduce their risk of related complications.

1. Flavonoids

Mangifera indica flavonoids is a group of plant secondary metabolites found in its peel and pulp.

These compounds have been extensively studied for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making them potential candidates for the prevention and treatment of various diseases.

The flavonoids present in mango include quercetin, kaempferol, and galangin, which have been shown to exhibit significant bioactive effects, contributing to the fruit's nutritional value and medicinal properties.

2. Gallic acid

Mangifera indica gallic acid is a phenolic compound derived from the fruit pulp and seeds of the mango tree.

It has been extensively studied for its antioxidant and antimicrobial properties, making it a potential natural preservative in food and pharmaceutical industries. Gallic acid has been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antitumor activities, as well as inhibit the growth of certain pathogens.

Its potential uses are vast, ranging from cosmetics to medicine.

3. Ellagic acid

Mangifera indica ellagic acid is a powerful polyphenolic compound extracted from its fruit.

This bioactive compound has been extensively studied for its numerous health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimutagenic properties. Ellagic acid has been shown to exhibit cytotoxic effects against various types of cancer cells, making it a promising agent in the fight against cancer.

Its potential applications also extend to the prevention of cardiovascular diseases and neurodegenerative disorders.

4. Kaempferol

Mangifera indica kaempferol is a flavonoid compound that is found in various parts of the plant, including the leaves and bark.

It has been shown to possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential natural remedy for various diseases such as diabetes, cancer, and cardiovascular disease.

Kaempferol has also been found to have antibacterial and antifungal activities, which can help protect against infections.

5. Quercetin

Mangifera indica quercetin is a flavonoid compound that is abundant in its fruit.

It has been found to possess numerous bioactive properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, which can contribute to the prevention of various diseases such as cancer, cardiovascular disease, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Quercetin has also been shown to have antimicrobial and antiviral effects, making it a potential agent against infectious agents.

6. Chlorogenic acid

Mangifera indica chlorogenic acid is a naturally occurring phenolic compound found in the fruit and leaves of this tropical plant.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Chlorogenic acid has also been shown to possess inhibitory effects on enzymes involved in glucose metabolism, making it a promising agent for managing type 2 diabetes and other metabolic disorders.

7. Protocatechuic acid

Mangifera indica protocatechuic acid is a type of polyphenolic compound found in various parts of the plant, including its fruit, leaves, and seeds.

It has been reported to possess several biological activities, such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Protocatechuic acid has been shown to exhibit potential therapeutic effects, including reducing oxidative stress and protecting against diseases.

8. Catechin

Mangifera indica catechin is a type of polyphenolic compound found in the fruit's bark, leaves, and seeds.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Catechin has been shown to possess antimicrobial and anticancer activities, making it a promising natural product for the development of novel pharmaceuticals and food products.

9. Epicatechin

Mangifera indica epicatechin is a potent flavonoid compound extracted from the fruit of the mango tree.

It has been extensively studied for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been linked to various health benefits including reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Epicatechin also exhibits antimicrobial and antiviral activities, making it a promising candidate for the development of novel therapeutic agents.

10. Rutin

Mangifera indica rutin is a bioflavonoid compound extracted from the bark of the mango tree.

It is widely recognized for its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been shown to benefit cardiovascular health, improve eye function, and even exhibit antitumor activity.

Additionally, rutin has been used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including hemorrhoids, varicose veins, and allergies, making it a valuable natural compound with potential therapeutic applications.
