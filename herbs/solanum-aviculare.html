Solanum aviculare

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Solanum aviculare has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems that often exhibit a light pubescence. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with slightly wavy margins and a dull green coloration. Small, actinomorphic flowers emerge in clusters, featuring five distinct petals that are usually pale lilac or white in hue. The fruit develops into small, globose berries that transition from green to a deep purple or black color when fully ripe. These botanical features distinguish it from other closely related members of the Solanaceae family.
