Syringa pubescens

What's the taxonomical classification of Syringa pubescens?

Syringa pubevescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Lamiales and the family Oleaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Syringa, and its unique species designation, pubevescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syringa pubescens has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with pubescent undersides and terminal panicles of fragrant, tubular, pale lilac to white flowers. The foliage is typically soft to the touch due to a fine layer of hairs covering the leaf surfaces. Its stems transition from woody bases to flexible, green young shoots that can exhibit varying degrees of pubescence. The plant often develops a spreading or shrubby habit with multiple stems emerging from the ground. Small, woody capsules containing winged seeds are produced following a successful flowering period.

What are the therapeutic actions of Syringa pubescens?

Syringa pubescens has therapeutic properties that include acting as an astringent, diaphoretic, febrifuge, tonic, and sedative. Finally, its sedative effects can assist in calming the nervous system and promoting relaxation.
