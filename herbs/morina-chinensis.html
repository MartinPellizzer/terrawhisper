Morina chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Morina chinensis?

Morina chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Dipsacales and the family Caprifoliaceae. The plant is specifically identified by the genus Morina and the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Morina chinensis has a woody, perennial shrub structure characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of up to two meters. The leaves are large, ovate to elliptical in shape, and feature serrated margins with distinct venation patterns. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal panicles composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. These flowers are usually pale pink or white and emerge from bracts that provide a protective covering during development. The plant's root system is robust, allowing it to stabilize itself in its natural highland habitats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a specific profile of medicinal compounds including morinins A-G, morinins H-K, 4-O-methyl-(E)-coniferyl alcohol, 4-O-methylcinnamyl acetate, and 4-O-methylcinnamyl alcohol. These specialized morinin derivatives serve as key bioactive constituents within the plant's chemical structure. The presence of 4-O-methyl-(E)-coniferyl alcohol highlights the complexity of its phenylpropanoid content. Additionally, the acetate and alcohol forms of 4-O-methylcinnamyl provide further chemical diversity to its medicinal properties. Together, these diverse molecules define the therapeutic potential of the plant's secondary metabolites.

As discussed in a study, the roots of the medicinal Chinese plant Morina chinensis contain seven new phenylpropanol derivatives named morinins A-G (1-7). These seven new compounds are found alongside five known compounds within the plant. The identified known compounds include 4-O-methylcinnamyl alcohol, 4-O-methylcinnamyl methyl ether, 4-O-methylcinnamyl acetate, p-methoxybenzaldehyde, and 4-O-methyl-(E)-coniferyl alcohol. The structures of these specific medicinal compounds were determined using spectral data, particularly 2D NMR and HRMSS.
