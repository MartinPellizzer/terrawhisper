Coumarin is a key compound with anticoagulant properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to antioxidant effects. Essential oils provide aromatic and antimicrobial benefits. Queen Anne'S Lace (Daucus carota) Monograph

1. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Queen Anne's Lace (Daucus carota). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also has anticoagulant effects and is used in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and nervous system support.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Queen Anne's Lace (Daucus carota) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in the roots and seeds of the plant, often used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Queen Anne's Lace (Daucus carota). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound supports various health benefits associated with Queen Anne's Lace, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

4. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Queen Anne's Lace (Daucus carota). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like limonene and phellandrene, which contribute to its medicinal properties. This oil is used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Queen Anne's Lace (Daucus carota). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in Queen Anne's Lace are known for their mild laxative effects and potential anti-cancer properties.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Queen Anne's Lace (Daucus carota). It contributes to the plant's cleansing properties and may support digestive health. Saponins can help break down fats and may have mild detoxifying effects. They are often used in herbal formulations for their gentle cleansing action.

7. Tannin

Tannin in Queen Anne's Lace (Daucus carota) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing. It also supports digestive health by binding to proteins and reducing gut irritation. Tannins are abundant in the roots and leaves, making them a key component in traditional herbal preparations.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Queen Anne's Lace, Daucus carota. They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and have potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds may support various therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Queen Anne's Lace, known for its antioxidant properties. It supports cardiovascular health and may reduce inflammation. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory support.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Queen Anne's Lace (Daucus carota). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its specific role in traditional herbal medicine is less well-defined compared to other constituents like sesquiterpene lactones. Alkaloids in this plant are generally present in smaller quantities and may support its overall therapeutic effects.
