What's the taxonomical classification of Malus rockii?

Malus rockii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Malus, and its unique species designation, rockii.

Its foliage is often described as being somewhat lustrous and can exhibit a slight pubescence on the undersides. What are the pharmacological activities of Malus rockii?

This plant has analgesic, antimalarial, and hypolipidemic pharmacological activities derived from its diverse bioactive compounds. Researchers have identified that its extracts can effectively reduce pain perception through specific biochemical pathways. The plant also demonstrates significant potential in combating malaria parasites, offering a natural approach to treating the infection. Furthermore, its ability to lower blood lipid levels suggests it may assist in managing cholesterol and preventing cardiovascular issues. These medicinal properties make Malus rockii a subject of intense pharmacological interest.

According to a study published by "Planta medica", Malus rockii Rehder contains a high accumulation of phloridzin, which reaches a percentage of 15.54% in the dry leaves. Phloridzin from this plant exhibited significant hypolipidemic activity in golden Syrian hamsters at doses of 50 mg/kg and 200 mg/kg. The compound also showed moderate antimalarial activity with an inhibition ratio of 31.3 ± 10.9% at a dose of 25 mg/kg/day. Furthermore, it demonstrated moderate analgesic activity with 28.0% inhibition at a dose of 50 mg/kg.
