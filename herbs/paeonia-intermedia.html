Paeonia intermedia

What's the taxonomical classification of Paeonia intermedia?

Paeonia intermedia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is situated in Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is organized into the order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Paeoniaceae. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Paeonia, specifically identifying as the species intermedia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paeonia intermedia has thick, fleshy tuberous roots, erect stems reaching up to 40 centimeters, and large, deeply lobed leaves that are divided into several narrow segments. The foliage typically displays a bright green color and provides a dense base for the flowering structures. Large, solitary flowers emerge from the stems, featuring numerous petals that can vary in color from deep red to soft pink. At the center of these blooms, a cluster of prominent stamens surrounds the developing carpels. Once pollination occurs, the plant develops large, woody follicles that hold the seeds until they are ready for dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and western Turkey, specifically within the Anatolian plateau. Its distribution is primarily concentrated in high-altitude habitats ranging from subalpine meadows to rocky slopes. These populations are often found in specific microclimates that provide the necessary drainage and seasonal moisture levels. The species tends to inhabit limestone-rich soils found across various provinces in the Turkish interior.
