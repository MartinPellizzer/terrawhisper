What's the taxonomical classification of Athyrium delavayi?

Athyrium delaavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Aspleniaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Athyrium, specifically identified as the species delaavayi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Athyrium delavayi has large, pinnate fronds that reach up to 1.5 meters in length with lanceolate leaflets that feature serrated margins and acute tips. The underside of the leaflets contains small sori protected by indusia, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.
