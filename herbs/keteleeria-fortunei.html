Keteleeria fortunei

What's the taxonomical classification of Keteleeria fortunei?

Keteleeria fortunei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and falls into the subclass Pinidae. Following the hierarchy further, it is part of the order Pinales and the family Pinaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus Keteleeria and its specific species name, fortunei.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Keteleeria fortunei contains a variety of bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Specific alkaloids within the species are often studied for their potential effects on the central nervous system and metabolic regulation. The presence of diverse phenolic acids further supports its use in traditional medicine to combat oxidative stress. Researchers continue to isolate these specific molecules to understand their precise pharmacological mechanisms.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat ailments including cough, asthma, bronchitis, and various respiratory infections. Its leaves are frequently prepared as a decoction or tea to help loosen phlegm and soothe irritated airways. In traditional medicinal practices, it is also applied to address skin issues and certain types of inflammation. The plant's chemical properties are believed to provide relief from congestion and shortness of breath. Local healers often rely on its bioactive compounds to manage chronic lung-related discomfort.
