Pteris plumbea

What's the taxonomical classification of Pteris plumbea?

Pteris plumbea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Polypodiales and is a member of the family Pteridaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Pteris and the species plumbea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris plumbea has pinnate fronds characterized by narrow, grayish-green leaflets that are arranged along a central rachis. The texture of the foliage often appears somewhat leathery or matte due to a fine layer of pubescence. These leaves grow from a creeping rhizome that provides a structural base for the plant's spreading habit. The margins of the individual pinnae are typically entire or slightly wavy, lacking deep serration. Small sori are located on the undersides of the mature fronds to facilitate spore production.
