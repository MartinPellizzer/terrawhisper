Hemiboea cavaleriei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemiboea cavaleriei has opposite, elliptic to oblong leaves that are typically glabrous and possess entire margins. The foliage is characterized by prominent longitudinal venation that extends toward the leaf apex. Its stems are woody and serve as the structural base for the plant's growth habit. The inflorescences consist of dense racemes that emerge from the leaf axils. Small, tubular flowers are arranged along these axes to facilitate pollination.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Atlantic Forest biome of southeastern Brazil, specifically within the states of Rio de Janeiro and Espírito Santo. It primarily inhabits moist, shaded environments such as lowland rainforests and montane forests. The species is often found growing as an epiphyte on trees or in the understory of dense tropical vegetation. Its distribution is restricted to these specific coastal and mountain habitats where high humidity is maintained.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Hemiboea cavaleriei can cause severe skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and potential allergic reactions if the plant material touches human tissue. The sap or foliage often contains irritants that trigger redness, itching, or swelling upon direct contact. Some individuals may experience more intense inflammatory responses depending on their personal sensitivity to the plant's chemical compounds. If ingested, the plant could lead to gastrointestinal distress or systemic toxicity. Careful handling is necessary to avoid accidental exposure to these physiological complications.
