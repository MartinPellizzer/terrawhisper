Alnus lanata

What's the taxonomical classification of Alnus lanata?

Alnus lanaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fagales. It is further categorized within the family Betulaceae, specifically situated in the genus Alnus, and is identified by its unique species name, lanata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Alnus lanata contains a diverse profile of bioactive compounds including tannins, flavonoids, terpenoids, and various phenolic acids. These secondary metabolites are primarily concentrated in the bark and leaves of the plant. The high concentration of tannins provides significant astringent properties that are often utilized for wound healing and skin treatments. Flavonoids within the plant contribute antioxidant activities that help mitigate oxidative stress in cellular environments. Researchers study these chemical constituents to understand their potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alnus lanata?

Alnus lanaata has medicinal properties that primarily involve its use as an astringent to treat skin irritations, wounds, and gastrointestinal issues. The bark and leaves contain tannins which help to shrink tissues and reduce inflammation when applied topically. When used in decoctions, the plant can assist in soothing digestive upset by tightening loose tissues in the gut. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to help control bleeding due to its ability to constrict blood vessels. These therapeutic actions make it a versatile component in various indigenous herbal remedies.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions including skin irritations, wounds, and digestive ailments. The bark and leaves contain tannins that act as astringents to help heal surface injuries and soothe skin rashes. Some traditional practices also utilize its extracts to alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal discomfort. Additionally, its medicinal properties are believed to assist with respiratory issues through its soothing effects. These applications rely on the plant's natural chemical compounds to reduce swelling and promote tissue repair.
