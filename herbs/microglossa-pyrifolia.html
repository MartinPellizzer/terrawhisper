Microglossa pyrifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Microglossa pyrifolia?

Microglossa pyrifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a large group of flowering plants, and is specifically identified by its genus, Microglossa, and its species, pyrifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microglossa pyrifolia has small, fleshy, pyriform leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite pattern along its slender stems. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a somewhat succulent appearance due to its thickened cellular structure. Its stems are relatively thin and provide support for the compact grouping of leaves. The plant often displays a low-growing or creeping habit depending on its immediate environmental conditions. These morphological features collectively allow the species to adapt to its specific ecological niche.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains acetylenic glucosides and the sesquiterpene germacrene-d as its primary medicinal compounds. These chemical constituents are often studied for their potential biological activities and pharmacological properties. The presence of acetylenic glucosides is particularly significant due to their unique structural characteristics. Additionally, germacrene-d contributes to the complex chemical profile that defines the species. Such compounds play a vital role in understanding the plant's medicinal value.

According to a study published by "Planta medica", four new acetylenic glucosides, numbered 1-4, have been isolated from the leaves of Microglossa pyrifolia Lam. Within these isolated substances, the aglucones 2a and 4a represent new polyynes. The aglucone 3a was specifically isolated as a mixture of E/Z-isomers in a ratio of 4:1. Furthermore, compound 1 demonstrates antibacterial activity.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating malaria caused by Plasmodium falciparum, reducing fever, managing skin diseases, and healing wounds. The medicinal properties of the leaves are particularly effective at combating the parasitic infections that trigger high body temperatures. Local healers often apply extracts directly to the skin to soothe various dermatological conditions and infections. Furthermore, the plant acts as a topical agent to promote the rapid closure of open wounds. These diverse applications demonstrate the significant role the species plays in traditional medicine.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Microglossa pyrifolia is used in the treatment of malaria. The leaf extract of this plant demonstrated antiplasmodial activity. This activity was observed against both the chloroquine-sensitive Plasmodium falciparum strain (3D7) and the chloroquine-resistant strain (W2). The extract was active with an IC50 value of less than 15 ug/ml.
