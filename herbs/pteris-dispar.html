Pteris dispar

What's the taxonomical classification of Pteris dispar?

Pteris dispar belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is further categorized into the family Pteridaceae, specifically within the genus Pteris, and is identified by its unique species name, dispar.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris dispar has pinnate fronds that reach up to 60 centimeters in length, characterized by a light green color and a distinctively uneven or dissimilar division of the pinnae. The rachis is typically slender and slightly woody, supporting leaflets that vary in size across the length of the blade. Each segment is finely lobed with serrated margins, creating a delicate and textured appearance. The underside of the foliage often reveals small, dark sori arranged in rows near the edges of the leaflets. This morphological variation in leaf shape provides a unique visual identity compared to other species in its genus.

What are the pharmacological activities of Pteris dispar?

This plant has significant anti-melanogenic pharmacological activities that allow it to inhibit the production of melanin in skin cells. Researchers have identified that its bioactive compounds work by suppressing the enzyme tyrosinase, which is essential for pigment formation. These chemical constituents also help to prevent hyperpigmentation and promote a more even skin tone. Beyond its effects on pigmentation, the plant exhibits various antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that protect skin health. Such diverse pharmacological actions make it a promising candidate for developing natural dermatological treatments.
