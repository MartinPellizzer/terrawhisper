Pteris dispar

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris dispar has pinnate fronds that reach up to 60 centimeters in length, characterized by a light green color and a distinctively uneven or dissimilar division of the pinnae. The rachis is typically slender and slightly woody, supporting leaflets that vary in size across the length of the blade. Each segment is finely lobed with serrated margins, creating a delicate and textured appearance. The underside of the foliage often reveals small, dark sori arranged in rows near the edges of the leaflets. This morphological variation in leaf shape provides a unique visual identity compared to other species in its genus.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pteris dispar can cause skin irritation and allergic contact dermatitis upon direct contact with its fronds. The plant contains certain compounds that may trigger redness, itching, or a rash in sensitive individuals. While systemic toxicity is less documented, caution should be exercised regarding accidental ingestion. Some people may experience localized swelling or discomfort if the plant's tissues are crushed against the skin. Maintaining distance from the foliage can help prevent these adverse reactions.
