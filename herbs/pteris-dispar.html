Pteris dispar

What's the taxonomical classification of Pteris dispar?

Pteris dispar belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is further categorized into the family Pteridaceae, specifically within the genus Pteris, and is identified by its unique species name, dispar.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris dispar has pinnate fronds that reach up to 60 centimeters in length, characterized by a light green color and a distinctively uneven or dissimilar division of the pinnae. The rachis is typically slender and slightly woody, supporting leaflets that vary in size across the length of the blade. Each segment is finely lobed with serrated margins, creating a delicate and textured appearance. The underside of the foliage often reveals small, dark sori arranged in rows near the edges of the leaflets.
