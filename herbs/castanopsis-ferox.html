Castanopsis ferox

What's the taxonomical classification of Castanopsis ferox?

Castanopsis ferox belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fagales. This species is further categorized within the family Fagaceae, specifically situated in the genus Castanopsis, with its unique designation being the species ferox.

What are the herbal preparations of Castanopsis ferox?

Castanopsis ferox is prepared as a decoction, a tea, or a topical paste derived from its bark, leaves, or roots. Traditional practitioners often boil the plant materials in water to extract potent medicinal compounds for internal consumption. The resulting liquid is frequently ingested to address various systemic ailments or digestive issues. Additionally, the plant can be crushed into a fine pulp to create a paste applied directly to the skin for external healing. These diverse methods allow the medicinal properties of the plant to be utilized for both systemic and localized treatments.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Castanopsis ferox can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and allergic contact dermatitis upon direct contact with its sap or foliage. The plant contains irritants that may trigger redness and itching if the substance touches sensitive areas of the body. Some individuals may experience more significant reactions, such as blistering or painful rashes, depending on their level of sensitivity. It is important to avoid touching the plant to prevent these adverse physical responses. Protective clothing should be worn when handling the species to mitigate the risk of accidental exposure.
