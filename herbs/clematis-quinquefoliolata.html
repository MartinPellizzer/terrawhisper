Clematis quinquefoliolata

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis quinquefoliolata?

Clematis quinquefoliolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Clematis and the species quinquefoliolatata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis quinquefoliolata has compound leaves typically composed of five distinct, lanceolate to ovate leaflets that emerge from a central petiole. These leaflets are arranged in a palmate pattern and often feature serrated or entire margins depending on the specific specimen. The plant utilizes slender, twining stems to climb surrounding vegetation through its climbing habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, usually appearing in shades of pale yellow or cream. The overall structure is characterized by its delicate, spreading foliage and climbing growth form.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive compounds including protoanemonin, ranunculin, and various glycosides that serve as its primary medicinal components. The protoanemonin is a highly reactive lactone formed when the plant tissues are damaged or crushed. Additionally, the presence of ranunculin allows the plant to produce these chemical agents through enzymatic processes within its cells.
