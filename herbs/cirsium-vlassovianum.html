Cirsium vlassovianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium vlassovianum has upright, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and are characterized by a distinctively winged appearance caused by longitudinal leaf tissue extending down the stalk. The leaves are deeply lobed or pinnatifid with sharp, spreading spines along the margins and a coarse, sandpaper-like texture. Its inflorescences consist of solitary, discoid flower heads that are densely packed with tubular florets and surrounded by overlapping involucral bracts. These bracts often feature spreading, spiny tips that provide a protective layer for the developing seeds. The plant produces small, dry achenes equipped with a pappus of fine hairs to facilitate wind dispersal.

Cirsium vlassovianum targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines like TNF-alpha and IL-6. These interactions are often mediated through the modulation of the NF-kappaB signaling pathway, which regulates the expression of genes involved in the immune response.
