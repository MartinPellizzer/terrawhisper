What is Ferulago galbanifera?

Ferulago galbanifera is an annual or biennial plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is a herbaceous plant with a height of 30-120 cm and a basal rosette of pinnate leaves, which are 3-5 times divided into linear or lanceolate leaflets. The inflorescence is a compound umbel, bearing 5-20 rays, each with 3-5 small white or yellowish flowers. The fruits are schizocarps, consisting of 2-3 mericarps, 3-5 mm long, with a narrow wing along the middle suture.

Ferulago galbanifera is native to the Mediterranean region and the Middle East, where it grows on dry places.

The main herbal preparations of Ferulago galbanifera are galbanum oil, galbanum tincture, galbanum infusion, galbanum salve, and galbanum tea.

Galbanum infusion is a herbal tea made by steeping the plant's leaves and flowers in hot water, which is traditionally used to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs.

Galbanum salve is a topical preparation made by infusing the plant's roots and rhizomes in a carrier oil, such as olive or coconut oil, and is used to soothe skin irritations and wounds, while galbanum tea is prepared in a similar manner to the infusion but is typically consumed in smaller amounts.
