Clinopodium confine

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clinopodium confine has square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and long petioles. The foliage is typically arranged in pairs along the stem and possesses a slightly rugose texture. Small, tubular, two-lipped flowers emerge from the leaf axils in dense, terminal clusters. These flowers are generally pale lilac or white and belong to the Lamiaceae family. The plant develops a bushy habit with a spreading root system that supports its herbaceous structure.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Clinopodium confine can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if consumed in large amounts. Some individuals may experience contact dermatitis or localized redness when the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. If ingested, the essential oils within the plant might trigger nausea, stomach cramps, or indigestion. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, caution is advised due to the potential for hypersensitivity in certain populations.
