What's the taxonomical classification of Euonymus acanthocarpus?

Euonymus acanthocarpus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Euonymus, specifically identified as the species acanthocarpus.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Euonymus acanthocarpus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains alkaloids and glycosides that may lead to vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea upon consumption. Contact with the foliage or stems can result in contact dermatitis, manifesting as redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. These physiological reactions are often triggered by the chemical compounds present within the plant's tissues. Careful avoidance of all parts of the shrub is necessary to prevent these adverse health effects.
