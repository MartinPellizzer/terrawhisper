Euonymus acanthocarpus

What's the taxonomical classification of Euonymus acanthocarpus?

Euonymus acanthocarpus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Euonymus, specifically identified as the species acanthocarpus.

Euonymus acanthocarpus has medicinal properties that primarily involve its use as a purgative, emetic, and tonic in traditional herbal practices. These chemical constituents are often utilized in controlled doses to address digestive stagnation or to cleanse the system of toxins. Some traditional applications also suggest its use in managing inflammatory conditions through its subtle systemic effects.
