Carpinus pubescens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carpinus pubescens has ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive pubescence on the undersides. The foliage often displays a light green color when young, transitioning to a deeper hue as the season progresses. Its branches are characterized by a fine covering of soft hairs that protect the developing stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into nutlets enclosed in leafy bracts. These bracts facilitate wind dispersal of the seeds across the forest floor.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across northern and central China, the Korean Peninsula, and parts of the Russian Far East. It typically inhabits mountainous areas and forest edges where the soil is well-drained. Within these habitats, the species thrives in deciduous or mixed forests at various elevations. Its distribution is largely determined by the availability of seasonal moisture and specific temperature ranges found in these subtropical and temperate zones.
