Saussurea leucoma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea leucoma has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal rosettes of lanceolate to oblong leaves that are densely covered in white, woolly hairs. These leaves feature prominent veins and entire or slightly dentate margins, providing a soft, silvery appearance to the foliage. The inflorescence consists of a compact, head-like capitulum supported by a long, sturdy scape. Each individual floret within the head displays specialized morphology typical of the Asteraceae family, often accompanied by pale or whitish involucral bracts. The dense pubescence serves as a structural adaptation to protect the plant from intense ultraviolet radiation and moisture loss in its high-altitude habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau and surrounding mountainous areas in China. It grows specifically in alpine meadows and rocky scree slopes at elevations typically ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters. Its distribution is restricted to these cold, high-elevation habitats where specialized environmental conditions exist. The species is most frequently documented within the provinces of Qinghai, Tibet, and Sichuan. Because of its preference for these specific montane ecosystems, its range remains fragmented across the Himalayan and Hengduan mountain systems.

These interactions primarily occur through bioactive sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids present within the plant's chemical profile. By inhibiting the expression of iNOS and COX-2, the plant effectively reduces the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like nitric oxide and prostaglandins. Furthermore, its ability to modulate the NF-kappaB signaling pathway helps suppress the cellular cascades that drive chronic inflammation. These specific protein interactions account for the plant's traditional use in treating swelling, pain, and various inflammatory conditions.
