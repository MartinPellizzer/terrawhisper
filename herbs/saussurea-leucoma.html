Saussurea leucoma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea leucoma has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal rosettes of lanceolate to oblong leaves that are densely covered in white, woolly hairs. The inflorescence consists of a compact, head-like capitulum supported by a long, sturdy scape. Each individual floret within the head displays specialized morphology typical of the Asteraceae family, often accompanied by pale or whitish involucral bracts.
