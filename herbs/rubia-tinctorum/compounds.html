Madder (Rubia tinctorum) Compounds

The primary active compounds of Madder include tannins, flavonoids, and anthocyanins. Tannins contribute to astringency and anti-inflammatory effects, while flavonoids offer antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. Anthocyanins provide color and may have antioxidant properties. Other compounds like coumarin and saponins also play roles in its medicinal actions.

Madder (Rubia tinctorum) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Madder (Rubia tinctorum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and wound-healing effects. Tannins in Madder may support digestive health and skin conditions when applied topically or ingested in moderate amounts.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Madder (Rubia tinctorum). They contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, offering antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds support the plant's role in treating skin conditions and promoting wound healing. Flavonoids enhance the therapeutic potential of Madder in herbal formulations.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Madder (Rubia tinctorum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and inflammation. This compound exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Madder (Rubia tinctorum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and inflammation. Coumarin also has anticoagulant properties and is used in herbal formulations for its therapeutic effects.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Madder (Rubia tinctorum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and as a dye. Glycosides in Madder are responsible for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects. They are often extracted and used in herbal formulations.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Madder (Rubia tinctorum) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds, including flavonoids and anthraquinones, support anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They are derived from the roots and are used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits. Terpenoids enhance the plant's ability to treat skin conditions and promote wound healing.

7. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Madder (Rubia tinctorum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and may have antimicrobial properties. While not the primary active component, it plays a role in the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Madder (Rubia tinctorum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting mild detoxifying effects and supporting liver function. Saponins also aid in enhancing the absorption of certain nutrients, making them valuable in herbal formulations.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Madder (Rubia tinctorum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in dyeing and has antioxidant properties. Anthocyanin is also believed to support skin health and may have anti-inflammatory effects. Its presence in Madder underscores the plant's historical and potential medicinal significance.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Madder (Rubia tinctorum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its role is less prominent than other constituents like anthraquinones. Alkaloids in Madder are typically present in smaller amounts and are often studied for their potential pharmacological effects.
