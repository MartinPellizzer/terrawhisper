Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition characterized by red, scaly patches on the skin.

Also known as psoriatic dermatitis or psoriatic plaque, this inflammatory disorder occurs when the immune system mistakenly attacks healthy skin cells, leading to rapid skin cell growth.

It commonly affects individuals between the ages of 15 and 35, though it can occur at any age, and is more prevalent in people with a family history of the condition.

The rubia tinctorum plant has been traditionally used to treat skin diseases like psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Its bioactive compounds may help reduce inflammation and promote skin healing in those suffering from such conditions.

How this herb helps with treats skin diseases?

Rubia tinctorum treats skin diseases because it contains bioactive constituents such as alkaloids and flavonoids, which possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

These compounds help reduce skin irritation and prevent infections. Alkaloids like rubiadin and rubiadinone contribute to the plant's therapeutic effects on the skin. Flavonoids act as antioxidants, protecting skin cells from damage.

Overall, the combination of these bioactive compounds makes Rubia tinctorum a valuable natural remedy for various skin conditions.
