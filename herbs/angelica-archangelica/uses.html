Archangelica (Angelica archangelica) Uses

Archangelica is used to treat respiratory issues such as coughs, colds, and asthma. It may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections and headaches. It is also used for mental health conditions like depression, insomnia, and anxiety, though evidence is limited. Migraines are another area where it is traditionally applied.

Archangelica (Angelica archangelica) Monograph

1. Cough

Archangelica is used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. It contains compounds that may help soothe irritated airways and reduce mucus production. Often prepared as a tea, it is considered a mild expectorant. Its warming properties are believed to ease congestion and promote easier breathing.

2. Cold symptoms

Archangelica is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its expectorant and antispasmodic properties. It helps clear mucus from the respiratory tract and soothe coughing. Often prepared as a tea, it supports the body's natural recovery process during colds and respiratory infections. Its warming effects also help relieve congestion and ease discomfort.

3. Asthma

Archangelica has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. It acts as a mild bronchodilator, helping to ease breathing by reducing airway constriction. Its expectorant properties also assist in clearing mucus, making it a valuable herb in respiratory support formulations.

4. Respiratory infections

Archangelica is used to support respiratory health by helping to clear mucus and soothe inflamed airways. It may be helpful for conditions like coughs, bronchitis, and sinusitis. Its expectorant properties aid in reducing congestion and promoting easier breathing. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for respiratory support.

5. Headache

Angelica archangelica has been traditionally used to alleviate headaches, particularly those related to colds or sinus pressure. Its expectorant properties help clear nasal passages, while its mild sedative effects may reduce tension headaches. It is often prepared as a tincture or tea and is considered safe when used in moderate amounts.

6. Depression

Archangelica has been historically used to support mood regulation and may help alleviate symptoms of mild depression. Its compounds, including volatile oils and flavonoids, are believed to influence neurotransmitter activity. It is often used in herbal formulations for its calming and uplifting effects. However, it should be used under professional guidance.

7. Insomnia

Archangelica has been traditionally used to support sleep by calming the nervous system. Its compounds may help reduce anxiety and promote relaxation, aiding those with insomnia. It is often prepared as a tea and is considered safe when used in moderate amounts.

8. Anxiety

Archangelica is used to support emotional balance and reduce anxiety due to its calming properties. It contains compounds that may influence neurotransmitters, promoting relaxation. Often prepared as a tea, it is valued in herbal medicine for its gentle, soothing effects on the nervous system.

9. Migraine

Archangelica is used to alleviate migraine symptoms due to its ability to reduce inflammation and ease muscle tension. It may help by supporting circulatory health and calming the nervous system. Typically, it is prepared as a tea or tincture and taken in small doses during migraine episodes. Its effects are often noted within hours of consumption.

10. Migraines

Archangelica has been traditionally used to alleviate migraines due to its antispasmodic and circulatory properties. It helps reduce tension and improve blood flow, which may ease migraine symptoms. Often prepared as a tea or tincture, it is valued for its calming and supportive effects on the nervous system.
