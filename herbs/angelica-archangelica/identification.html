Botanical Identification of Archangelica (Angelica archangelica)

To identify Archangelica (Angelica archangelica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Archangelica, or Angelica archangelica, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Apiaceae, genus Angelica, and species Angelica archangelica. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Archangelica is Angelica archangelica, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Angelica sylvestris and Archangelica sylvestris. Common names such as "garden angelica" and "bishop's root" reflect its historical and cultural significance. Regional and historical names vary, often tied to its use in traditional medicine and folklore.

Morphological Characteristics

Archangelica is a biennial plant with a tall, hollow stem that grows up to 2 meters. It has a thick, fleshy root system and large, compound leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces a compound umbel of small white flowers in summer, followed by dark, ridged fruits containing small, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Archangelica can be distinguished by its large, compound leaves with prominent veins and its thick, aromatic roots. It has large, umbrella-shaped flower clusters and a strong, licorice-like odor. It is often confused with other Angelica species, but its size and distinct leaf structure help in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Archangelica is native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with cool summers and cold winters. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found at elevations between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Archangelica blooms in late summer to early autumn, typically between July and September. Seed formation occurs in late summer, with seeds maturing by early autumn. For identification purposes, the plant is most easily recognized during its flowering stage, which is key for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Archangelica belongs to the genus Angelica in the family Apiaceae. Its closest relatives include other Angelica species such as Angelica sinensis and Angelica archangelica var. gigas. It shares key family traits like umbellate flowers and aromatic compounds, which are common among Apiaceae plants.
