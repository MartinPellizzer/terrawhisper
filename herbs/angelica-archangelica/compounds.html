Archangelica (Angelica archangelica) Compounds

The primary compounds in Archangelica include essential oil, coumarin, and flavonoids. These compounds contribute to its aromatic profile and therapeutic properties. Other notable components are glycosides, phenolic acids, and tannins, which support its use in herbal medicine for digestive and anti-inflammatory effects.

Archangelica (Angelica archangelica) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Angelica archangelica contains compounds like limonene and methyl chavicol. These contribute to its aromatic properties and potential therapeutic effects. It is commonly used in herbal medicine for its digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Angelica archangelica. It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin also acts as a mild anticoagulant, supporting circulation and reducing blood clot risk. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in digestive and respiratory ailments.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Angelica archangelica. They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by acting as antioxidants and anti-inflammatory agents. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. Flavonoids also enhance the overall efficacy of Angelica archangelica in herbal formulations.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Archangelica (Angelica archangelica). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and may support digestive and circulatory health. Glycosides in Archangelica are often associated with its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and nervous system support.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Angelica archangelica. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is often found in high concentrations in the roots and seeds of the plant. Its presence enhances the overall therapeutic potential of Angelica archangelica in herbal medicine.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Archangelica (Angelica archangelica) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting digestive and respiratory health. They are responsible for the plant's aromatic profile and are often used in traditional remedies.

7. Tannin

Tannin in Angelica archangelica contributes to its astringent properties, helping to soothe inflammation and protect mucous membranes. It works alongside other compounds to support digestive health and may aid in reducing excess mucus. Tannins also enhance the plant's overall therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Archangelica (Angelica archangelica). It contributes to the plant's expectorant properties, aiding in the removal of mucus from the respiratory tract. Saponins also exhibit mild anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in respiratory and digestive health.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Archangelica (Angelica archangelica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its role is less prominent than other constituents like essential oils. Alkaloids in Archangelica are generally present in smaller quantities and are not the primary active components responsible for its therapeutic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Angelica archangelica. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and support cellular health. This compound is also associated with anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects in herbal medicine.
