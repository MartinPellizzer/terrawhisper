Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Pinales and the family Pinaceae, eventually leading to its placement within the genus Pinus. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pinus taiwanensis has needles arranged in pairs that are 5 to 10 centimeters long, grayish-green in color, and possess a distinctively thickened base. The tree typically reaches heights of 20 to 30 meters and develops a straight, cylindrical trunk with reddish-brown bark that becomes scaly with age. Its cones are relatively small and ovoid, measuring approximately 4 to 7 centimeters in length. The reproductive structures are characterized by woody scales that protect the developing seeds within the cone.
