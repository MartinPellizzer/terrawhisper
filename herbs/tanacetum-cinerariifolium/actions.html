Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) Actions

Cineraria exhibits several therapeutic actions, including being bitter, anti-inflammatory, and carminative. It acts as a tonic and stimulant, aiding digestion and energy. Additionally, it is diuretic, astringent, expectorant, antispasmodic, and sedative, making it useful for a range of health conditions.

Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) Monograph

1. Bitter

Cineraria has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and increases appetite. This property helps to clear mucus and reduce respiratory congestion. It also supports liver function and can aid in the elimination of toxins from the body.

2. Anti-inflammatory

Cineraria has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its high content of sesquiterpene lactones, which inhibit inflammatory pathways. It is used to reduce inflammation in conditions like respiratory and skin disorders. Its effects are supported by traditional use and some preliminary scientific studies.

3. Carminative

Cineraria has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating in the digestive system. It works by stimulating the movement of the intestines and reducing the formation of gas. This makes it useful for treating mild digestive discomfort and indigestion.

4. Tonic

Cineraria has a tonic action, supporting overall vitality and energy levels. It is traditionally used to invigorate the body and enhance physical strength. This property makes it valuable in herbal formulations aimed at boosting health and resilience.

5. Stimulant

Cineraria has a stimulant action, enhancing mental alertness and physical energy. It is used to invigorate the nervous system and improve overall vitality. This effect supports its traditional use in treating fatigue and low energy states.

6. Diuretic

Cineraria has a diuretic action, promoting the excretion of urine and aiding in the removal of excess fluids from the body. This effect helps reduce swelling and supports kidney function. It is commonly used to manage conditions involving fluid retention.

7. Astringent

Cineraria has an astringent action, which helps to reduce secretions and tighten tissues. This property is useful in treating conditions like diarrhea and respiratory infections. It works by stimulating the contraction of mucous membranes. Its astringent effect is mild compared to other herbs but still effective for mild ailments.

8. Expectorant

Cineraria has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It is commonly used for conditions like bronchitis and coughs where mucus buildup is a concern. Its active compounds stimulate the secretion of fluids, aiding in clearing the airways. This makes it a valuable herb in respiratory support.

9. Antispasmodic

Cineraria has an antispasmodic action, making it effective for relieving muscle spasms and cramps. It works by relaxing smooth muscle tissue, which can help with conditions like menstrual cramps, gastrointestinal spasms, and respiratory issues. This property is supported by its traditional use in herbal medicine.

10. Sedative

Cineraria has a sedative action, helping to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety. It is often used in herbal formulations to promote relaxation and ease restlessness. Its mild calming properties make it suitable for mild stress and sleep support.
