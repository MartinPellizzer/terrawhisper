Botanical Identification of Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium)

To identify Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Cineraria, or Tanacetum cinerariifolium, belongs to the Plantae kingdom and the Compositae family. Its genus is Tanacetum, and it is classified under the family Compositae, which includes many aromatic plants used in herbal medicine. The specific species is Tanacetum cinerariifolium, known for its medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Cineraria is Tanacetum cinerariifolium, first described by the botanist Carl von Linné. This name reflects its leaf structure, with "cinerariifolium" meaning "like the leaves of Cineraria." It has several synonyms, including Chrysanthemum cinerariifolium, which highlights its resemblance to chrysanthemums.

Morphological Characteristics

Cineraria, or Tanacetum cinerariifolium, is a biennial herb with a taproot system. It has a slender, upright stem that is covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in a grayish-white powdery coating. The plant produces a flat, branched inflorescence with small yellow flowers. The fruits are tiny achenes, each with a single seed.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cineraria, or Tanacetum cinerariifolium, can be distinguished by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture and its small, yellowish composite flower heads. It lacks the strong aromatic scent of similar species like tansy, and its leaves are more delicate and less robust compared to other members of the Asteraceae family.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cineraria, or Tanacetum cinerariifolium, is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during its flowering stage, which is crucial for accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cineraria, or Tanacetum cinerariifolium, is closely related to other members of the Tanacetum genus within the Asteraceae family. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with related species like T. vulgare and T. corymbosum. These relationships help in understanding its ecological and medicinal significance.
