Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) Compounds

The primary compounds in Cineraria include essential oils, terpenoids, and coumarin, which contribute to its aromatic properties and potential therapeutic effects. Flavonoids and phenolic acids provide antioxidant benefits, while tannins and glycosides may support anti-inflammatory actions. Saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further add to its complex pharmacological profile.

Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) is a key active compound, rich in monoterpene hydrocarbons and oxygenated compounds. It is traditionally used for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and calming properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for respiratory and skin conditions.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds found in Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium). These compounds contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are often responsible for the plant's characteristic aroma and are used in traditional herbal medicine for various therapeutic purposes.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly its antispasmodic and sedative effects. Coumarin is also known for its potential anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant activities. Its presence supports the use of Cineraria in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are commonly found in the plant's aerial parts and are used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic effects.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports the traditional use of Cineraria in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Its presence enhances the plant's overall therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin in Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and may support digestive health. Tannins are found in the leaves and are part of the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. They also contribute to the plant's bitter taste and preservation qualities.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Glycosides in Cineraria are often used in herbal formulations to support digestive and respiratory health.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin helps in treating respiratory conditions and skin infections. Its presence supports the traditional use of Cineraria in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid in Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) is a bioactive compound with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and skin conditions. Its presence supports the plant's role in treating ailments like coughs and infections.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by acting as an antioxidant. Anthocyanin may help reduce inflammation and support overall health, though research on its specific effects in Cineraria is limited.
