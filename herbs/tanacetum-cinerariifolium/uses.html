Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) Uses

Cineraria is used to treat respiratory infections, bronchial congestion, and asthmatic symptoms. It helps alleviate mucous membrane irritation and upper respiratory tract infections. It is also applied for cough, nasal congestion, bronchitis, allergic rhinitis, and sinusitis. Its properties support clearing mucus and reducing inflammation in the respiratory system.

Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Cineraria is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and clearing mucus. It may help alleviate symptoms of bronchitis, coughs, and other respiratory infections. Its antiseptic and expectorant properties aid in respiratory tract healing. It is often prepared as a tincture or tea for internal use.

2. Bronchial congestion

Cineraria is traditionally used to alleviate bronchial congestion due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce swelling in the airways, making it beneficial for respiratory conditions like bronchitis. Its use is supported by historical herbal practices and is often prepared as a tea or tincture.

3. Asthmatic symptoms

Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) has been traditionally used to alleviate asthmatic symptoms due to its expectorant and bronchodilator properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce airway constriction, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for respiratory support. Its use is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in managing chronic asthma.

4. Mucous membrane irritation

Cineraria, or Tanacetum cinerariifolium, is known to cause mucous membrane irritation due to its volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones. These compounds can lead to burning, redness, and discomfort when in contact with sensitive tissues. It is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion to prevent adverse reactions.

5. Upper respiratory tract infections

Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) is used traditionally to support the treatment of upper respiratory tract infections. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce symptoms like coughing and congestion. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe the throat and clear nasal passages.

6. Cough

Cineraria is used to alleviate coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Traditionally, it has been prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe coughing and improve breathing. Its effectiveness is supported by historical use in herbal medicine for respiratory conditions.

7. Nasal congestion

Cineraria, or Tanacetum cinerariifolium, is used to alleviate nasal congestion due to its anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. It helps reduce mucus production and soothe irritated nasal passages, providing relief for conditions like the common cold and allergies. Its essential oil is often applied topically or used in steam inhalation for targeted respiratory support.

8. Bronchitis

Cineraria, or Tanacetum cinerariifolium, has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce bronchial irritation and ease coughing. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe symptoms and promote mucus clearance.

9. Allergic rhinitis

Cineraria, or Tanacetum cinerariifolium, is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis. Its antihistaminic properties help reduce nasal congestion and sneezing. It is typically prepared as a tincture or tea. Regular use may support immune regulation and lessen seasonal allergy reactions.

10. Sinusitis

Cineraria (Tanacetum cinerariifolium) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and mucus production, supporting respiratory clarity. Often prepared as a steam inhalation or tincture, it is valued for its gentle yet effective action on the respiratory system.
