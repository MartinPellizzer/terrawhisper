Clematis clarkeana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis clarkeana has woody, climbing stems with opposite, trifoliate leaves composed of three lanceolate to ovate leaflets. The foliage is typically a deep green color and serves to support the vine as it scales through surrounding vegetation. Its flowers are star-shaped and feature four to six creamy white to pale yellowish petals known as sepals. These blooms are often accompanied by prominent, yellowish stamens that create a central focal point. Following pollination, the plant develops feathery, achene-bearing seed heads that facilitate wind dispersal.
