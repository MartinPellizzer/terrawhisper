Cicuta Virosa: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Cicuta virosa, commonly known as water hemlock, is a highly toxic herb native to North America, belonging to the parsley family.

It contains potent bioactive constituents such as cicutoxin, which can cause severe neurological effects and even death if ingested.

Despite its toxicity, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic actions, including inducing vomiting and treating parasitic infections.

However, due to its extreme danger, it is not recommended for use in modern herbal practices.
