What to know about Cicuta virosa (water hemlock) before using it medicinally

Water hemlock, scientifically known as Cicuta virosa, is an herb that has been revered for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to alleviate digestive issues and reduce inflammation.

In culinary practices, water hemlock is often used in soups, stews, and teas due to its delicate flavor and aroma, which is similar to that of sweet cicely. From a horticultural standpoint, water hemlock is relatively easy to grow and can thrive in a variety of environments, making it a popular choice among gardeners. Botanically speaking, water hemlock belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and features distinctive umbels of small white flowers.

With ancient roots dating back to medieval Europe, water hemlock has been a staple ingredient in traditional folk remedies and medicinal practices for centuries, earning its reputation as a versatile and potent herb.
