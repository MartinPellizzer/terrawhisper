Cicuta virosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Cicuta virosa?

Cicuta virosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Apiales and the family Apiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Cicuta and the specific species name virosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cicuta virosa has a thick, fleshy, tuberous rhizome that serves as a central storage organ for the plant. This perennial herb typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters with a hollow, grooved stem. The leaves are compound and bipinnate, featuring deeply lobed segments that give them a delicate, feathery appearance. Small, white, five-petaled flowers are arranged in dense, umbrella-like umbels at the top of the stems. The plant also produces small, rounded fruits that are characteristic of the Apiaceae family.

Within these mountain ranges, it is often found growing near stream banks, damp meadows, or in subalpine zones. What are the pharmacological activities of Cicuta virosa?

This plant has potent neurotoxic pharmacological activities primarily driven by the presence of cicutoxin, a highly toxic unsaturated alcohol. This specific compound acts as a noncompetitive antagonist of gamma-aminobutyric acid receptors in the central nervous system. By inhibiting these inhibitory neurotransmitters, the toxin triggers uncontrolled electrical activity in the brain. Such mechanisms lead to severe physiological effects, including violent convulsions and respiratory failure. Consequently, the pharmacological profile of Cicuta virosa is defined by its extreme toxicity rather than therapeutic utility.
