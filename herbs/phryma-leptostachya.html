Phryma leptostachya

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phryma leptostachya has an erect, slender stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 50 centimeters and features opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The leaves are sessile or short-petioled, often tapering toward the base and displaying a smooth or slightly hairy surface texture. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, elongated spike containing small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white. Each individual flower possesses a five-lobed corolla and is supported by a distinct calyx that remains persistent after blooming. The plant's overall structure is characterized by its delicate, upright habit and specialized floral arrangement.
