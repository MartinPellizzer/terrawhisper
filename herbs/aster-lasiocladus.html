Aster lasiocladus

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster lasiocladus?

Aster lasiocladus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is further classified under the family Asteraceae, which places it among a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Aster, and its specific biological designation is the species lasiocladus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aster lasiocladus contains bioactive secondary metabolites including sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that contribute to its biological activities. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's leaves and flowering heads. The sesquiterpene lactones are particularly noted for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Researchers study these compounds to understand how they interact with cellular pathways to inhibit oxidative stress. Further analysis of the flavonoid profile provides insight into the plant's antioxidant capacity.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aster lasiocladus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, gastrointestinal distress, and inflammatory conditions. The leaves and stems are often prepared as infusions or topical applications to soothe irritation. Some traditional practices utilize the plant to combat stomach aches and digestive irregularities. It is also believed to possess properties that help reduce swelling in localized areas of the body. Researchers continue to study its chemical components to better understand its medicinal efficacy.
