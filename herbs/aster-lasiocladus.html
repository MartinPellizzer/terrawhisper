Aster lasiocladus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster lasiocladus has perennial stems that are upright, woody at the base, and typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal or cauline, characterized by an oblong to lanceolate shape with serrated margins and a soft, pubescent texture. These plants produce terminal heads of small, white or pale lavender discoid flowers arranged in compact clusters. The involucral bracts surrounding the flower heads are often covered in fine, silky hairs that contribute to the plant's overall soft appearance. Throughout the growing season, the foliage remains densely hairy, a trait that helps the species manage moisture and light exposure.
