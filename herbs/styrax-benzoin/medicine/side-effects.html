10 Possible Side Effects Of Styrax Benzoin

Styrax benzoin has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and respiratory issues.

Causes of these side effects include excessive use, improper handling, and sensitivity to certain compounds found in the herb.

These side effects can worsen people's lives by causing painful rashes, respiratory distress, and potentially life-threatening allergic reactions, which can lead to hospitalization or long-term health consequences.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Styrax benzoin causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of terpenes and resins in the oil, which can trigger an immune response and lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

The allergenic properties of Styrax benzoin are thought to be caused by its ability to bind to IgE antibodies, leading to the release of histamine and other chemical mediators that contribute to allergic reactions.

2. Triggers respiratory distress

Styrax benzoin triggers respiratory distress due to its allergenic and irritating properties.

The resin's volatile oils can cause airway constriction, inflammation, and increased mucus production, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

In severe cases, Styrax benzoin may exacerbate existing conditions like asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), potentially life-threatening in extreme situations.

3. Induces vomiting episodes

Styrax benzoin induces vomiting episodes due to its irritating properties on the mucous membranes.

The resin's volatile compounds can cause inflammation and irritation of the gastrointestinal tract, leading to nausea and vomiting in some individuals. This reaction is typically associated with high concentrations or prolonged exposure to Styrax benzoin.

As a result, users may experience symptoms of vomiting, often accompanied by stomach discomfort and distress.

4. Produces skin irritation

Styrax benzoin produces skin irritation due to its high concentration of benzoic acid and cinnamic acid.

These compounds can cause allergic reactions, inflammation, and redness on the skin, especially in individuals with sensitive skin or pre-existing skin conditions.

The irritant properties of benzoin can also trigger an immune response, leading to itching, burning, and blistering.

5. Elicits anaphylactic shock

Styrax benzoin elicits anaphylactic shock in some individuals due to its allergenic properties.

The resin, which is derived from the sap of the Styrax tree, contains a compound called benzoic acid that can cause severe allergic reactions in susceptible persons.

When exposed to this compound, the immune system overreacts, leading to a life-threatening condition characterized by symptoms such as rapid heartbeat, difficulty breathing, and a sudden drop in blood pressure.

6. Leads to coma states

Styrax benzoin leads to coma states due to its toxic compounds, specifically benzoic acid and vanillin.

When ingested or absorbed through the skin, these chemicals can cause a range of adverse reactions, including altered mental status, loss of consciousness, and eventually, comatose states.

The exact mechanism by which Styrax benzoin induces coma is unclear, but it is thought to involve its ability to disrupt normal brain function and metabolism.

7. Inflames digestive tracts

Styrax benzoin inflames digestive tracts due to its high resin content, which can irritate the mucous membranes lining the stomach and intestines.

This irritation may lead to inflammation, redness, and swelling in the digestive tract, causing discomfort, pain, and potentially even gastrointestinal bleeding.

The severity of this side effect varies from person to person, with some individuals experiencing mild symptoms while others may experience more severe reactions.

8. Exacerbates existing allergies

Styrax benzoin exacerbates existing allergies by releasing allergenic compounds into the air and causing an immune response.

The resin of the styrax tree contains a mixture of volatile oils and gums that can stimulate allergic reactions in susceptible individuals.

This may lead to symptoms such as skin irritation, respiratory issues, or other adverse effects.

9. Provokes seizure activity

Styrax benzoin provokes seizure activity due to its ability to interfere with the normal functioning of the brain's electrical activity.

This is thought to be caused by the resin's constituent compounds, particularly cinnamic acid and cinnamaldehyde, which can alter the concentration of neurotransmitters such as GABA and glutamate.

As a result, it may trigger seizure activity in some individuals who are prone to epilepsy or have a history of seizures.

10. Increases blood pressure

Styrax benzoin increases blood pressure.

This is due to the presence of coniferaldehyde, a chemical compound that stimulates the sympathetic nervous system, leading to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, benzoin's antispasmodic properties can cause blood vessels to constrict, further contributing to elevated blood pressure levels.
