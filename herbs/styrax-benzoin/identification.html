Benzoin, or Styrax benzoin, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Styracaceae, genus Styrax, and species Styrax benzoin. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the order that includes other aromatic wood resins.

The scientific name of Benzoin is Styrax benzoin, first described by the French botanist Jean-Baptiste de Jussieu in 1789. This name is widely accepted in botanical classification, though it has been synonymized with several other names over time, reflecting historical taxonomic revisions.

Morphological Characteristics

Benzoin, or Styrax benzoin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree. It has a shallow root system and a slender, branched stem. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic with a pointed tip. The plant produces racemose inflorescences with fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Benzoin (Styrax benzoin) can be distinguished by its woody, aromatic resinous exudate, which forms a yellowish or reddish gum. It has opposite, ovate leaves with smooth margins and small, white, clustered flowers. The bark is smooth and light brown, and the plant is typically shrub-like in habit. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Styrax species with different leaf shapes or resin types.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Benzoin (Styrax benzoin) is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and warm temperatures. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically found at low to mid-altitude ranges, usually between 300 and 1,000 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Benzoin (Styrax benzoin) blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in the following autumn, around September to October. Harvesting of the resin is done during the dry season, usually between November and March, when the resin is most abundant and easily collected.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Benzoin (Styrax benzoin) belongs to the genus Styrax in the family Styracaceae. Its closest relatives include species like Styrax japonicus and Styrax obassia. The genus Styrax is closely related to other genera in the family, sharing traits such as resin production and similar floral structures.
