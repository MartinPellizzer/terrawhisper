Cibotium barometz

What's the taxonomical classification of Cibotium barometz?

Cibotium barometz belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, it falls under the order Cyatheales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Cyatheaceae, within the genus Cibotium, and it is specifically identified by the species name barometz.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Cibotium barometz contains a specialized, blood-like exudate found within the rhizome that is used primarily in traditional medicine. This reddish fluid is harvested from the root structure and is highly valued in various Asian medicinal practices. Practitioners often utilize this substance to treat conditions related to blood deficiency and to promote circulation. It is also frequently incorporated into formulas intended to restore energy and address menstrual irregularities. Because the substance is quite rare, it is considered a precious ingredient in many classical herbal remedies.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Cibotium barometz targets several key proteins including cyclooxygenase enzymes, various inflammatory cytokines, and specific neurotransmitter receptors to produce its therapeutic effects. The bioactive compounds within the plant act primarily by inhibiting the cyclooxygenase-2 pathway, which reduces the production of pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. Additionally, these molecules interact with the cholinergic system to modulate neurological functions and alleviate certain types of pain. Some research suggests that the plant's extracts can influence the expression of tumor necrosis factor-alpha to suppress systemic inflammation. These diverse protein interactions collectively contribute to its traditional uses in treating gynecological issues and inflammatory conditions.

    What are the therapeutic actions of Cibotium barometz?

    Cibotium barometz has therapeutic properties primarily used in traditional medicine to treat hemorrhoids, promote blood circulation, and stop bleeding. The rhizome is frequently prepared as a decoction to alleviate symptoms of varicose veins and intestinal issues. It is also believed to possess diuretic effects that help reduce swelling and promote fluid balance in the body. In certain folk practices, the plant is utilized to treat skin conditions and inflammatory ailments through topical applications. These traditional uses focus heavily on its perceived ability to regulate blood flow and stabilize internal secretions.

    What diseases this plant treats?

    This plant is used to treat ailments related to the reproductive system, specifically menstrual irregularities, infertility, and various gynecological disorders. In traditional Chinese medicine, it is frequently prescribed to regulate menstrual cycles and alleviate painful periods. Practitioners also utilize it to enhance fertility and address issues concerning the uterus. Beyond reproductive health, it is sometimes applied to treat certain types of skin conditions or to promote blood circulation. The substance is highly valued for its perceived ability to balance internal energies related to the blood.

    What are the herbal preparations of Cibotium barometz?

    Cibotium barometz is prepared primarily as a dried rhizome that is either sliced into thin pieces for decoctions or ground into a fine powder for use in medicinal granules and pills. This plant, often referred to as dragon's blood herb, is frequently infused into alcohol to create potent tinctures or medicinal wines. In some traditional practices, the rhizome is also processed into a concentrated paste or combined with honey to form a soothing syrup. These various preparations are utilized to address specific ailments such as blood circulation issues and menstrual irregularities. The choice of form depends on the required potency and the specific method of administration desired by the practitioner.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Cibotium barometz can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential toxicity if ingested due to its complex chemical composition. While historically used in traditional medicine, the lack of rigorous clinical testing means users may experience unpredictable nausea or vomiting. Some individuals might develop localized dermatitis or itching if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. There is also a risk of systemic toxicity because the exact concentration of bioactive compounds can vary significantly between specimens. Because its safety profile is not fully established by modern medical standards, consumption poses a significant health risk.
