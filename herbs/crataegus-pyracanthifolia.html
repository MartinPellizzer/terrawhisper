Pyracanthifolia Hawthorn (Crataegus Pyracanthifolia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus pyracanthifolia.

Crataegus pyracanthifolia, commonly known as the thorned hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its cardiovascular benefits. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, proanthocyanidins, and oligomeric procyanidins, which are believed to support heart health by improving blood flow and reducing oxidative stress. In traditional Chinese medicine, it has been utilized to treat hypertension, arrhythmia, and other cardiovascular conditions due to its potential to regulate blood pressure and strengthen heart muscle function. Modern studies suggest that extracts from C. pyracanthifolia may have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a promising candidate for complementary therapeutic applications. However, further clinical research is needed to fully elucidate its efficacy and safety for medicinal use.
