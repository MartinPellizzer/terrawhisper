Cotoneaster ambiguus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cotoneaster ambiguus?

Cotoneaster ambiguus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Cotoneaster and the species ambiguus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cotoneaster ambiguus has a prostrate or decumbent growth habit characterized by woody, spreading stems and small, elliptical, leathery leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 millimeters in length.

The foliage is typically dark green with a smooth texture and may feature slightly serrated margins. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in clusters from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy, globose berries that transition from green to a bright red or dark color when ripe.

The overall structure of the plant allows it to form dense, ground-covering mats in its natural habitat.

      What are the herbal preparations of Cotoneaster ambiguus?

      Cotoneaster ambiguus is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a concentrated tincture derived from its dried berries or bark.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cotoneaster ambiguus can cause gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting and abdominal pain, if the berries are ingested by humans or animals. The plant contains cyanogenic glycosides which can lead to more severe symptoms such as dizziness or nausea upon consumption. While the toxicity level is generally low for healthy adults, accidental ingestion poses a risk to young children and pets. It is important to identify the plant correctly to avoid any potential poisoning from its fruit. Protective measures should be taken to ensure that the berries are not consumed by vulnerable individuals.
