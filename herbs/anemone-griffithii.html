Anemone griffithii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone griffithii has basal, deeply lobed leaves that are trifoliate in structure and hairy on their undersides. The plant produces solitary, actinomorphic flowers that typically feature white or pale pink petals surrounding a central cluster of yellow stamens. These blossoms are supported by long, slender pedicels that emerge from a rhizomatous root system. The foliage often forms dense clumps that spread through the slow expansion of these underground stems. Each leaf consists of distinct leaflets with serrated edges that contribute to its textured appearance.
