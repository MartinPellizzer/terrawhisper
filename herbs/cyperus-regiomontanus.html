What is Cyperus regiomontanus?

Cyperus regiomontanus is a species of sedge that belongs to the family Cyperaceae.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 2 meters tall, with a rhizome that produces long stems and narrow, linear leaves that are 1-2 mm in width and 30-60 cm long. The inflorescence is a large, compound spike that is 30-60 cm long, composed of numerous spikelets, each bearing 2-4 male flowers and 1-2 female flowers. The male flowers have 3 stamens, while the female flowers have 2 stigmas.

The plant is native to Mexico and Central America, and is found in wet areas such as marshes and swamps.
