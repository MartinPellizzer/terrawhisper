Pterolobium punctatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pterolobium punctatum has pinnately compound leaves composed of several pairs of small, oblong leaflets that are typically covered in fine, glandular punctations. The plant exhibits a woody, scrambling habit characterized by slender, somewhat twining stems that can reach significant lengths. Small, bright yellow flowers emerge from axillary positions, often appearing in clusters along the stem. The fruit is a small, somewhat compressed legume that contains a limited number of seeds. These morphological features collectively distinguish it from other species within the Fabaceae family.

    What are the herbal preparations of Pterolobium punctatum?

    Pterolobium punctatum is prepared primarily as an aqueous decoction or infusion using the leaves and bark to extract its medicinal properties. These liquid preparations are the most traditional methods for utilizing the plant's bioactive compounds in folk medicine.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pterolobium punctatum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may lead to contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, or swelling on the skin. Some individuals might experience more severe systemic allergic responses if the plant is handled without protection. Ingestion of the plant parts could potentially lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its chemical composition. It is important to exercise caution when interacting with this species to avoid these adverse health effects.
