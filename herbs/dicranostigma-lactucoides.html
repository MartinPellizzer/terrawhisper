What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the benzophenanthridine alkaloids chelerythrine and sanguinarine, along with several other bioactive secondary metabolites. These specific chemical compounds are recognized for their potent biological activities, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that these alkaloids can interact with various cellular targets to influence physiological processes. The presence of such complex molecules makes the species a subject of interest in pharmacological studies. Understanding the concentration of these substances helps scientists evaluate the potential medicinal value of the plant.

According to a study published by "Journal of pharmaceuctical and biomedical analysis", the underground part of Dicranostigma lactucoides HOOK.f.et THOMS contains seven quaternary benzo[c]phenanthridine alkaloids (QBA). These alkaloids include sanguinarine (SA), chelerythrine (CHE), chelirubine (CHR), chelilutine (CHL), sanguilutine (SL), sanguirubine (SR), and macarpine (MA). The highest content of SA was found in the roots of D. lactucoides at 1.99% of the dried roots. Additionally, the concentration of CHE in the roots of D. lactucoides was measured at 3.43% of the dried roots. Detection of these compounds was performed at 280 nm using an HPLC method.
