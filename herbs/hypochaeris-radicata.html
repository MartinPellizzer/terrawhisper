Botanical Identification

Yellow Hawkweed, scientifically known as Hypochaeris radicata, belongs to the Asteraceae family. Commonly called hawkweed or lesser hawkweed, it is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It has a creeping root system, alternate leaves, and yellow flowers with ray florets. Synonyms include Leontodontum radicatum and Hypochaeris hypochaeris in older herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Yellow Hawkweed contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarins. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Coumarin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Respiratory infections, respiratory congestion, allergic rhinitis, bronchitis, cold symptoms Antimicrobial, expectorant, antihistaminic
Integumentary Skin infections, inflammatory skin conditions, wound healing, skin irritation, eczema Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antihistaminic, antifungal
Immune Allergic reactions Antihistaminic

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and as a diuretic.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as an antispasmodic.

Tincture: Used for topical applications and to treat skin infections.

Safety Profile

Yellow Hawkweed is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Individuals with allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family should also exercise caution.
