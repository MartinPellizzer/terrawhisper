Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as digestive issues and respiratory problems, thanks to its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. In the kitchen, cat's ear is prized for its mild, slightly sweet flavor and is often added to salads, soups, and stews for a burst of fresh flavor. As an ornamental plant, it is also highly valued for its delicate white flowers and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

With roots dating back to ancient Greek and Roman civilizations, cat's ear has been documented in herbal texts as far back as the 4th century BC, solidifying its position as a revered and timeless botanical treasure.
