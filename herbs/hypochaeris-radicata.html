Yellow hawkweed

Hypochaeris radicata

Yellow Hawkweed (Hypochaeris radicata) is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America, commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It is known for its bright yellow flowers and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Taxonomy

Yellow Hawkweed (Hypochaeris radicata) is a flowering plant belonging to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Brassicales, family Brassicaceae, genus Hypochaeris, and species Hypochaeris radicata.

Morphology

Yellow Hawkweed (Hypochaeris radicata) is a low-growing perennial herb with a creeping rhizomatous root system, characteristically bearing alternate, spoon-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a yellowish-green hue, and bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals arranged in a composite head.

Distribution

Yellow Hawkweed (Hypochaeris radicata) is native to Europe and western Asia but has been introduced to North America, where it is commonly found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and open fields across the continental United States and southern Canada.

Traditional Uses

Yellow Hawkweed (Hypochaeris radicata) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to treat skin conditions, reduce inflammation, and support digestive health, often prepared as a tea or poultice.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and Native American herbal practices, Yellow Hawkweed (Hypochaeris radicata) has been historically used for its purported anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and wound-healing properties.

Phytochemistry

Yellow Hawkweed (Hypochaeris radicata) contains a variety of bioactive compounds including flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, coumarins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and potential therapeutic applications.

Pharmacology

Yellow Hawkweed (Hypochaeris radicata) exhibits anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and vulnerary properties, making it useful in the treatment of respiratory conditions, digestive disorders, and skin ailments.

Botanical Identification

Yellow Hawkweed, scientifically known as Hypochaeris radicata, belongs to the Asteraceae family. Commonly called hawkweed or lesser hawkweed, it is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It has a creeping root system, alternate leaves, and yellow flowers with ray florets. Synonyms include Leontodontum radicatum and Hypochaeris hypochaeris in older herbal texts.
