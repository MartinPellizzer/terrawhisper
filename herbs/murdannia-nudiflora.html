Murdannia nudiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Murdannia nudiflora?

Murdannia nudiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Commelinares. This plant is a member of the family Commelinaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Murdannia, with its unique designation being the species nudiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murdannia nudiflora has opposite, lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are typically sessile or short-petioled with entire margins and prominent longitudinal veins. The plant features a creeping or ascending stem that often forms dense mats in moist environments. Its small, white to pale lavender flowers are solitary and emerge from the leaf axils, showcasing three distinct petals and a central yellow throat. The fruit is a small, globose capsule that splits to release tiny seeds upon maturity. These morphological traits distinguish it from other species within the Linderniaceae family.
