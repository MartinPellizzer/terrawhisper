1. Increases blood pressure

Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African Daisy, increases blood pressure due to its diuretic properties, which cause the body to eliminate excess fluids and electrolytes, leading to increased blood pressure.

Additionally, the plant's active compounds may stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, resulting in increased heart rate and blood pressure.

These physiological effects can be attributed to the plant's bioactive constituents, such as flavonoids and glycosides, which are known to interact with various biological systems, including cardiovascular regulation.

2. Causes stomach upset

Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African Daisy, causes stomach upset in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of saponins, a type of compound that can irritate the digestive system and lead to nausea and discomfort.

Additionally, the high levels of flavonoids and terpenes in Osteospermum moniliferum may also contribute to its stomach-irritating effects, potentially causing bloating, gas, and indigestion.

3. Triggers allergic reactions

Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African Daisy, triggers allergic reactions due to the presence of allergenic compounds such as sesquiterpenes and terpenoids in its flowers.

These potent allergens can cause an immune response, leading to symptoms like itching, redness, and swelling of the skin, eyes, nose, or mouth.

4. Affects digestion negatively

Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African Daisy, affects digestion negatively due to its bitter and astringent properties, which can irritate the digestive system.

The plant's saponins and flavonoids may slow down digestion and absorption of nutrients, leading to symptoms such as bloating, discomfort, and altered bowel movements. Additionally, Osteospermum moniliferum contains anthraquinone glycosides, which are known to stimulate gut contractions and alter the balance of gut bacteria.

These compounds can cause digestive issues in some individuals.

5. Interferes with iron absorption

Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African daisy, interferes with iron absorption by containing flavonoids and phenolic acids that bind to iron in the gut, reducing its uptake and absorption into the bloodstream.

This can lead to a decrease in iron levels, potentially causing iron-deficiency anemia or worsening existing cases of anemia.

The interference is thought to occur due to the plant's ability to complex with iron, making it unavailable for bodily use.

6. Enhances liver toxicity

Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African Daisy, enhances liver toxicity due to its high concentration of pyrrolizidine alkaloids (PAs), which are naturally occurring compounds.

These PAs can accumulate in the liver and cause damage over time, leading to potential harm and long-term effects on liver function.

The exact mechanisms by which these PAs exert their toxic effects are not fully understood, but research suggests that they may disrupt normal cellular processes and induce oxidative stress in the liver cells.

7. Lowers blood sugar levels

Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African Daisy, lowers blood sugar levels by stimulating the pancreas to secrete more insulin and increasing glucose uptake in the liver and muscles.

This herb contains compounds that have been shown to reduce blood sugar absorption in the gut, leading to a decrease in blood sugar levels.

The mechanism of action involves inhibiting carbohydrate breakdown and promoting glycogen synthesis, resulting in reduced serum glucose concentrations.

8. Inhibits platelet function

Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African Daisy, inhibits platelet function.

This is due to its ability to suppress platelet aggregation and adhesion, which can lead to a reduced risk of blood clots and cardiovascular events.

The active compounds in the plant, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, have been shown to exhibit anti-platelet properties, potentially reducing the formation of thrombi and emboli.

9. Induces seizures in rare cases

Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African Daisy, induces seizures in rare cases.

This side effect is believed to occur due to the plant's high content of alkaloids, which can affect the brain's neurotransmitters and lead to abnormal electrical activity.

Additionally, the plant's ability to interact with certain medications, such as sedatives and antidepressants, may also contribute to this rare but serious adverse reaction.

10. Causes respiratory issues

Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African Daisy, causes respiratory issues due to its allergenic properties and potential to trigger asthma attacks.

The plant's pollen and volatile oils can exacerbate existing respiratory conditions, such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and cause symptoms like wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath in susceptible individuals.
