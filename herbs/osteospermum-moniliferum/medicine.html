What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Osteospermum Moniliferum (African Daisy)?

Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African daisy, has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

The herb contains flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. Traditionally, it is used to make infusions, decoctions, and tinctures to treat fever, digestive issues, and wounds. However, excessive consumption can cause gastrointestinal upset, while interactions with medications such as blood thinners may also occur.

Prolonged use without medical supervision is not recommended.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Osteospermum moniliferum.

What are the medicinal preparations of Osteospermum moniliferum?

Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African Daisy, has different medicinal preparations, such as infusions, decoctions, and tinctures.

The infusion is used to treat fever, diarrhea, and coughs, while the decoction is used to relieve respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma. The tincture is applied topically to soothe skin irritations and wounds.

Additionally, Osteospermum moniliferum is also used in traditional medicine to treat digestive problems, such as constipation and dysentery, and is believed to have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

What are the possible side effect of using Osteospermum moniliferum improperly?

Improper use of Osteospermum moniliferum, also known as African Daisy, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and digestive issues.

It may cause stomach cramps, diarrhea, nausea, and vomiting in some individuals. In rare cases, it could lead to liver damage, kidney failure, or even seizures if consumed in excessive quantities.

Additionally, improper use may result in interactions with medications, exacerbate existing medical conditions, and trigger respiratory problems such as bronchitis or asthma attacks.
