Typha laxmannii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Typha laxmannii has long, linear, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant features a distinctive cylindrical flowering spike that consists of a dense, velvety brown female portion topped by a thinner, more loosely arranged male spike. These inflorescences emerge on sturdy, upright stalks that can grow significantly taller than the surrounding foliage. The leaf blades are narrow and taper to a fine point, often exhibiting a slight fold along the midrib. The entire structure is supported by a creeping rhizome system that allows the plant to form dense colonies in wetland environments.

Its distribution follows specific hydrological patterns, often appearing in areas with muddy or silty substrates.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Typha laxmannii can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested without proper preparation.
