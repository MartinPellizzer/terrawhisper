10 Possible Side Effects Of Passiflora Incarnata

Passiflora incarnata has some side effects when used improperly, such as drowsiness, fatigue, and increased sedation.

These effects can be caused by its high content of flavonoids and alkaloids, which can interact with other medications and exacerbate conditions like respiratory issues or cardiovascular problems.

In severe cases, prolonged use can lead to increased dependency on the herb, worsen sleep quality, and even affect daily life, making it challenging to work, maintain relationships, or engage in physical activities.

1. Causes sedation

Passiflora incarnata causes sedation due to its ability to bind to GABA receptors in the brain, increasing the activity of the neurotransmitter and enhancing its calming effects.

As a natural relaxant, Passiflora incarnata can help reduce anxiety and promote a sense of calmness, which may lead to feelings of drowsiness or sleepiness.

The sedative properties of this herb are also attributed to its flavonoids and alkaloids, which contribute to its anxiolytic and hypnotic effects.

2. Induces dizziness

Passiflora incarnata induces dizziness in some individuals due to its sedative properties.

The herb contains flavonoids and alkaloids that can affect the nervous system, causing a decrease in blood pressure and slowing down heart rate, leading to feelings of dizziness or lightheadedness.

Additionally, Passiflora incarnata's ability to relax skeletal muscles may also contribute to this side effect, as it can lead to a sense of disorientation.

3. Provokes anxiety attacks

Passiflora incarnata provokes anxiety attacks due to its potential interaction with certain medications and neurotransmitters.

The herb's ability to affect GABA receptors in the brain can lead to feelings of restlessness and apprehension, potentially exacerbating existing anxiety disorders.

Additionally, the sudden onset of sedation or drowsiness caused by Passiflora incarnata may also contribute to increased anxiety levels as users may experience a sense of disorientation or loss of control.

4. Elicits allergic reactions

Passiflora incarnata elicits allergic reactions in some individuals due to its ability to stimulate an immune response.

The plant's flavonoids and alkaloids can bind to IgE antibodies in the bloodstream, triggering the release of histamine and other chemicals that cause symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

5. Triggers insomnia

Passiflora incarnata triggers insomnia due to its stimulating effect on the central nervous system.

The herb contains a compound called harmine, which can increase alertness and make it difficult for individuals to fall asleep.

Additionally, Passiflora's bitter taste can also lead to an increased heart rate and blood pressure, further contributing to restlessness and insomnia.

6. Enhances depression

Passiflora incarnata enhances depression in some individuals.

This side effect is attributed to its ability to alter neurotransmitter levels and activity, particularly serotonin and GABA, which can exacerbate existing depressive symptoms.

Additionally, the herb's sedative properties may worsen feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and lethargy, further contributing to a heightened sense of depression.

7. Increases blood pressure

Passiflora incarnata increases blood pressure due to its stimulating effect on the nervous system.

The plant contains compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids, which can increase heart rate and cardiac output, leading to a rise in blood pressure.

Additionally, maypop has been shown to exhibit vasoconstrictive properties, causing blood vessels to constrict and further elevating blood pressure.

8. Stimulates hallucinations

Passiflora incarnata stimulates hallucinations due to its ability to interact with the brain's neurotransmitters.

The herb contains flavonoids and alkaloids that can alter the levels of serotonin, dopamine, and other neurotransmitters, leading to altered perception, distorted thinking, and vivid visuals, which can manifest as hallucinations.

This effect is more likely to occur when Passiflora incarnata is used in high doses or in combination with other substances that also affect brain chemistry.

9. Produces numbness

Passiflora incarnata produces numbness due to its sedative properties and ability to interact with neurotransmitters in the brain.

The plant's alkaloids, including harmine, flavonoids, and glycosides, can affect the nervous system, causing a numbing sensation in some individuals.

As Passiflora incarnata works to calm the mind and body, it may also alter the way pain signals are transmitted, leading to feelings of numbness or tingling.

10. Hints at withdrawal

Passiflora incarnata hints at withdrawal symptoms in some individuals who abruptly stop using it after prolonged use.

This can occur because of the brain's adaptation to the herb's sedative and anxiolytic effects, leading to dependence on its presence.

As a result, users may experience unpleasant symptoms such as agitation, anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness when they suddenly discontinue use.
