Xanthoceras sorbifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Xanthoceras sorbifolium?

Xanthoceras sorbifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Sapindales. The plant is further categorized into the family Sapindaceae, which contains the genus Xanthoceras, and is specifically identified as the species sorbifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Xanthoceras sorbifolium has large, pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of oblong leaflets with serrated margins and an alternate arrangement on woody stems. The plant develops a sturdy, deciduous shrub or small tree habit with grayish bark. Its flowers are prominent and showy, featuring large yellow petals that often fade to a reddish hue as they age. These blossoms eventually give way to woody, dehiscent capsules that contain numerous seeds. The overall structure is characterized by its robust woody architecture and distinctively textured foliage.
