Botanical Identification of Purple Foxglove (Digitalis purpurea)

To identify Purple Foxglove (Digitalis purpurea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Purple Foxglove (Digitalis purpurea) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Purple Foxglove, Digitalis purpurea, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Plantaginales, family Plantaginaceae, genus Digitalis, and species Digitalis purpurea. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the order and family associated with plantains and related species.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Purple Foxglove is Digitalis purpurea, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Digitalis purpurea L., and has common names such as Purple Foxglove, Fairy Gloves, and Dog's Heart. Regional and historical names vary, reflecting its widespread use in traditional herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Purple Foxglove is a biennial plant with a taproot system. It has upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces a tall spike of tubular flowers, typically purple or pink, arranged in a helix. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, coin-shaped seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Purple Foxglove can be distinguished by its tall, erect stems with purple-tinted leaves and spurred orange-red flowers. It has a distinctive bell-shaped corolla with two upper lobes that are longer than the lower ones. The leaves are opposite, egg-shaped, and have a velvety texture. It lacks the hairy stems and different flower structure of similar species like Digitalis ferruginea.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Purple Foxglove is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Purple Foxglove blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the most accurate time to observe key features is during its blooming period in late spring to early summer.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Purple Foxglove belongs to the genus Digitalis, closely related to species like Digitalis ferruginea and Digitalis lanata. It shares family traits with the Scrophulariaceae family, including opposite leaves and spurred flowers. Its phylogenetic position reflects evolutionary links within the genus and broader plant families.
