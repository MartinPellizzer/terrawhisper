Aloe Vera (Aloe vera) Compounds

Aloe Vera contains several primary active compounds, including terpenoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its antimicrobial activity, while saponins and tannins support digestive health. Glycosides, coumarin, essential oils, alkaloids, and anthocyanins also play roles in its therapeutic effects.

Aloe Vera (Aloe vera) Monograph

1. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Aloe Vera, contributing to its medicinal properties. These compounds, including acemannan and various sterols, support the plant's anti-inflammatory and wound-healing effects. They play a key role in the therapeutic uses of Aloe Vera in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Aloe Vera. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting skin health and wound healing. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and may enhance the therapeutic effects of Aloe Vera in various medical applications.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Aloe Vera (Aloe vera), contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps protect the plant from pathogens and supports its therapeutic effects in treating skin conditions and digestive issues.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Aloe Vera. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin helps in soothing skin irritations and supporting digestive health. It is one of the main components responsible for the therapeutic effects of Aloe Vera.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Aloe Vera. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may help in reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing. Tannins in Aloe Vera are also believed to support digestive health by binding to proteins and reducing excess mucus.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Aloe Vera (Aloe vera). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting cellular repair and hydration. Glycosides in Aloe Vera are known for their anti-inflammatory and soothing effects on the skin and digestive system. These compounds are essential for the plant's therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Aloe Vera. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. Coumarin may help reduce swelling and prevent blood clot formation. Its presence supports Aloe Vera's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Aloe Vera. It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. The essential oil is extracted from the leaf's gel and is used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic benefits.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Aloe Vera (Aloe vera). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Alkaloids in Aloe Vera may support skin healing and immune function, though research on their specific roles is ongoing.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Aloe Vera, contributing to its antioxidant properties. It helps neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound plays a role in the plant's therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and skin-repairing benefits.
