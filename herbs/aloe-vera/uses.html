Aloe Vera (Aloe vera) Uses

Aloe Vera is used to treat psoriasis, burns, and skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce acne and sunburn by soothing the skin and promoting healing. Aloe Vera also aids in wound healing, mouth ulcers, and eczema by moisturizing and repairing damaged skin. It can relieve skin irritation and constipation through its digestive and soothing effects.

Aloe Vera (Aloe vera) Monograph

1. Psoriasis

Aloe Vera has been used traditionally to manage psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and moisturizing properties. It helps reduce redness, scaling, and itching associated with the condition. Some studies suggest it may support skin healing and reduce flare-ups when applied topically. However, results may vary, and it should be used as part of a comprehensive treatment plan.

2. Burns

Aloe Vera is effective for treating burns due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce pain, swelling, and infection risk. The gel soothes the skin and promotes healing. It is commonly applied directly to minor burns and blisters.

3. Skin infections

Aloe Vera has antimicrobial properties that help treat skin infections such as fungal and bacterial infections. Its gel contains compounds that reduce inflammation and promote healing. It can be applied topically to infected areas to soothe irritation and support recovery. It is a natural alternative for managing minor skin infections in herbal medicine.

4. Acne

Aloe Vera contains anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial compounds that help reduce acne by calming skin irritation and fighting bacteria. It also helps regulate oil production and exfoliate dead skin cells, preventing clogged pores. Regular use can improve skin texture and reduce the frequency of breakouts.

5. Sunburn

Aloe Vera gel can soothe sunburned skin by reducing inflammation and promoting healing. It contains compounds that help rehydrate the skin and alleviate pain. Applying it directly to the affected area can provide rapid relief. It is a natural and effective remedy for mild to moderate sunburn.

6. Wound healing

Aloe Vera promotes wound healing by reducing inflammation and preventing infection. Its gel contains compounds that stimulate cell regeneration and enhance skin repair. Studies show it can accelerate healing and reduce scarring in minor cuts and burns. It is commonly used topically for its soothing and antimicrobial properties.

7. Mouth ulcers

Aloe Vera gel can be applied directly to mouth ulcers to soothe pain and promote healing. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce infection risk and accelerate tissue repair. Regular application may shorten the duration of ulcers and improve oral comfort. It is a natural, accessible remedy for mild to moderate mouth sores.

8. Eczema

Aloe Vera has been traditionally used to treat eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and skin irritation associated with eczema. Its gel contains compounds that promote skin healing and hydration, making it a natural remedy for managing eczema symptoms.

9. Skin irritation

Aloe Vera is effective for skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and pain associated with burns, rashes, and eczema. The gel contains compounds that promote skin healing and hydration, making it a versatile natural remedy for various skin conditions.

10. Constipation

Aloe Vera is known to support digestive health by promoting regular bowel movements. Its mucilage content helps soften stools, while its anti-inflammatory properties may reduce intestinal irritation. It is often used as a natural remedy for mild constipation, though it should complement a balanced diet and hydration.
