10 Possible Side Effects Of Aloe Vera

Aloe vera has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal issues.

These side effects can be caused by the plant's sap, which contains anthraquinones, and by using Aloe vera in large quantities.

For example, excessive skin irritation can cause discomfort and disrupt daily activities, while gastrointestinal issues can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and digestive problems.

1. Irritates skin causes redness

Aloe vera irritates skin causing redness because it contains malic acid and other compounds that can stimulate the skin's natural reaction, leading to inflammation.

Additionally, some individuals may be allergic to certain components of aloe, such as anthraquinones, which can cause an adverse reaction resulting in redness and irritation.

2. Burns mucous membranes badly

Aloe vera burns mucous membranes badly when used topically.

This is because the high concentration of aloin and aloe-emodin in aloe gel can cause irritation, inflammation, and damage to the delicate tissues of the mouth, throat, and digestive tract.

These compounds can stimulate an allergic response, leading to redness, swelling, and blistering, especially when applied undiluted or in excess.

3. Increases risk of allergy

Aloe vera increases risk of allergy due to its unique composition.

The gel inside the leaf contains aloin and aloe-emodin, two compounds that can trigger allergic reactions in some individuals.

Additionally, the plant's saponins, which are responsible for its soothing properties, can also cause an immune response, leading to an allergic reaction.

4. Causes stomach upset frequently

Aloe vera causes stomach upset frequently due to its high concentration of anthraquinones, a group of compounds that can stimulate bowel movements and increase gut contractions.

This may lead to cramping, bloating, and diarrhea in some individuals, especially when taken orally or applied topically in large quantities.

The irritant properties of aloe's sap can also cause discomfort and nausea in those with sensitive stomachs.

5. Interferes with blood pressure

Aloe vera interferes with blood pressure by affecting the body's natural balance of potassium and sodium levels.

The compounds present in aloe vera, such as aloin and aloe-emodin, can increase urine production, leading to a loss of electrolytes like potassium, which can lower blood pressure.

Additionally, aloe vera may also stimulate the heart to beat faster, further contributing to changes in blood pressure.

6. Inhibits healing process slowly

Aloe vera inhibits healing process slowly because it contains compounds like aloin and aloe-emodin that can suppress collagen synthesis and inhibit cell proliferation.

This may delay the natural healing process of wounds, skin irritations, and other conditions treated with Aloe vera.

The plant's anti-inflammatory properties may also slow down wound closure by reducing the initial inflammation response, which is essential for the healing process to begin.

7. Triggers allergic reactions strongly

Aloe vera triggers allergic reactions strongly due to the presence of anthraquinone compounds such as aloin and aloe-emodin.

These compounds can cause skin irritation, redness, and itching in some individuals, leading to an allergic response. The risk of an allergic reaction is higher for those who have sensitive skin or are prone to allergies.

Additionally, Aloe vera's high concentration of sulfur can also contribute to its allergenic properties.

8. Disrupts electrolyte balance quickly

Aloe vera disrupts electrolyte balance quickly because it contains compounds that can alter the body's natural sodium-potassium balance.

The anthraquinone glycosides present in aloe vera, particularly aloin and aloe-emodin, are responsible for this effect by inhibiting the absorption of electrolytes in the intestines.

As a result, excessive use of aloe vera products can lead to an imbalance of essential minerals such as potassium, calcium, and sodium.

9. Affects kidney function negatively

Aloe vera affects kidney function negatively.

This is due to its high concentration of anthraquinone compounds, such as aloin and aloe-emodin, which can increase urine production and potentially lead to dehydration.

Additionally, the saponins present in Aloe vera may interfere with the body's ability to absorb essential nutrients, further compromising kidney function.

10. Worsens inflammatory conditions

Aloe vera worsens inflammatory conditions by stimulating the immune system and releasing chemical compounds that can exacerbate existing inflammation.

The aloin present in aloe vera gel can cause gastrointestinal irritation and discomfort, potentially triggering or worsening conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome, Crohn's disease, and ulcerative colitis.

Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of aloe vera may paradoxically trigger an inflammatory response in some individuals, leading to further complications.
