10 Possible Side Effects Of Aloe Vera (Aloe)

Aloe vera has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal issues.

These side effects can be caused by the plant's sap, which contains anthraquinones, and by using Aloe vera in large quantities.

For example, excessive skin irritation can cause discomfort and disrupt daily activities, while gastrointestinal issues can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and digestive problems.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Aloe vera if used imporperly.

Aloe vera increases risk of allergy due to its unique composition.

The gel inside the leaf contains aloin and aloe-emodin, two compounds that can trigger allergic reactions in some individuals.

Aloe vera causes stomach upset frequently due to its high concentration of anthraquinones, a group of compounds that can stimulate bowel movements and increase gut contractions.

This may lead to cramping, bloating, and diarrhea in some individuals, especially when taken orally or applied topically in large quantities.

The irritant properties of aloe's sap can also cause discomfort and nausea in those with sensitive stomachs.
