Ficus rubicunda

Ficus rubicunda, commonly known as the Indian laurel or Indian banyan, is a species of fig tree native to the tropical forests of the Western Ghats in India.

This plant is valued for its potential health benefits, including its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to reduce stress and promote overall well-being.

The bioactive constituents of Ficus rubicunda include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which are believed to contribute to its therapeutic actions, such as pain relief, wound healing, and antiviral activity.

Additionally, the plant's therapeutic actions are thought to include anxiolytic, anti-depressant, and anti-anxiety effects, making it a potential natural remedy for various health conditions.
