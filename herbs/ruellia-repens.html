Ruellia repens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ruellia repens has a climbing or scrambling habit characterized by hollow, herbaceous stems and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically bright green and can reach several centimeters in length. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the tubular, pendulous flower which often displays a striking orange or red coloration. These blossoms are zygomorphic and emerge from the leaf axils to attract specific pollinators. The plant also produces small, dehiscent capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds for reproduction.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, skin irritation, dizziness, and photosensitivity in individuals who come into contact with it or ingest it. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to significant skin irritation or allergic reactions upon touching the leaves or stems. Exposure to sunlight after handling the plant can trigger photosensitivity, resulting in increased sensitivity to UV rays. Ingestion of the plant is known to disrupt the digestive system, leading to stomach upset and nausea. Furthermore, systemic toxicity may cause neurological symptoms such as dizziness.
