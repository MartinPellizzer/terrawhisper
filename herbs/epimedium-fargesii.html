Epimedium fargesii

What's the taxonomical classification of Epimedium fargesii?

Epimedium fargesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Berberidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Epimedium, with its unique biological identity defined by the species fargesii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Epimedium fargesii has basal, cordate to ovate leaves that are typically leathery in texture and measure several centimeters in length. These leaves often exhibit a deep green color and may develop reddish hues during seasonal transitions. The plant produces delicate, spreading inflorescences characterized by small, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its stems are slender and emerge from a creeping rhizomatous root system that allows the plant to colonize woodland areas. The overall structure is low-growing and herbaceous, making it well-suited for shaded environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds epimediin B, epimediin C, epimedoside A, hyperin, and icarin. These bioactive constituents are primarily classified as flavonol glycosides that contribute to the plant's pharmacological profile. Researchers have studied these specific molecules for their potential roles in regulating various physiological processes. The presence of these compounds is often used as a chemical marker to ensure the quality and authenticity of the species. Consequently, the synergy between these substances defines the therapeutic value of the plant extracts.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", six chemical constituents were isolated from the whole plant of Epimedium fargesii. These constituents were identified as epimedoside A, ikarisosids C, epimedin B, epimedin C, icariin and hyperin. The identification was performed by means of UV, IR, 1H-NMR, 13C-NMR and MS spectral analysis. These specific compounds were isolated from this plant for the first time.
