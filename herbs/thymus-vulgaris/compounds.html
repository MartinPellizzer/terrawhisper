Thyme (Thymus vulgaris) Compounds

Thyme contains essential oils, primarily thymol and carvacrol, which are terpenoids known for their antimicrobial properties. Flavonoids like luteolin and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant effects. Coumarin and tannins add to its anti-inflammatory and astringent qualities. Other compounds such as glycosides, saponins, and alkaloids support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Thyme (Thymus vulgaris) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Thyme (Thymus vulgaris) is a complex mixture of aromatic compounds, primarily containing thymol and carvacrol. These compounds contribute to its antimicrobial, antifungal, and antioxidant properties, making it a key component in traditional herbal medicine for treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds found in Thyme (Thymus vulgaris). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and possess antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects. Common terpenoids in Thyme include thymol and carvacrol, which are responsible for its medicinal uses in respiratory and digestive health.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Thyme (Thymus vulgaris). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support respiratory and immune health. These compounds are often present alongside essential oils like thymol and carvacrol, enhancing the plant's therapeutic potential.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Thyme (Thymus vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Found in high concentrations in Thyme essential oil and leaves, it supports its traditional use in respiratory and digestive health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Thyme in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Thyme (Thymus vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has antispasmodic and mild anticoagulant effects. Coumarin also supports digestive health and may help reduce inflammation in the respiratory system.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Thyme (Thymus vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and supports its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive issues. Tannins also act as antioxidants and may help reduce inflammation.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Thyme (Thymus vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides in Thyme support respiratory health and are used in herbal remedies for coughs and bronchitis. They are often extracted and used in essential oils and tinctures.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Thyme (Thymus vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins help in enhancing the absorption of nutrients and may support digestive health. They are also known for their potential immune-boosting effects.

9. Alkaloid

Thyme (Thymus vulgaris) contains the alkaloid thymol. Thymol is a potent antimicrobial and antifungal compound that contributes to thyme's traditional use in treating respiratory infections and skin conditions. It also aids in expectoration and has mild analgesic properties.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Thyme (Thymus vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. While not the primary active component, anthocyanin supports the overall therapeutic effects of thyme in traditional herbal medicine.
