Canarium pimela

What's the taxonomical classification of Canarium pimela?

Canarium pimela belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Sapindales, this plant is a member of the family Burseraceae. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Canarium, and its specific designation is the species pimela.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Canarium pimela contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds that provide its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents contribute to significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties observed in the plant's extracts. Research suggests that specific triterpenoids within the species may assist in protecting cells from oxidative stress. Additionally, the presence of certain alkaloids and tannins provides antimicrobial activity against various pathogens. These diverse medicinal compounds make the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological studies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Canarium pimela?

Canarium pimela has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties derived from its bioactive secondary metabolites. These therapeutic actions are primarily driven by the presence of essential oils and phenolic compounds found within the plant material. When applied topically, the extracts help to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms and reduce localized swelling. The plant also facilitates tissue regeneration, making it useful in traditional medicine for treating skin infections and minor injuries. Such biological activities suggest significant potential for developing new pharmaceutical agents for dermatological use.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin ailments, digestive issues, and respiratory infections. The medicinal properties of its extracts are often applied to soothe inflammatory skin conditions and promote wound healing. In some traditional practices, preparations made from the plant are consumed to alleviate stomach discomfort and gastrointestinal distress. It is also utilized in herbal remedies to help mitigate symptoms associated with common coughs and congestion. Researchers continue to study its bioactive compounds to better understand its potential therapeutic applications.
