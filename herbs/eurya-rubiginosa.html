Eurya rubiginosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eurya rubiginosa has small, elliptical, evergreen leaves that are typically 1 to 3 centimeters long, featuring a glossy dark green upper surface and a paler underside. The leaf margins are generally entire or slightly serrated, while the texture is somewhat coriaceous or leathery to the touch. Its stems are woody and slender, often branching out to form a dense, spreading shrubby habit. Small, inconspicuous white flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually giving way to tiny, round fruits. These fruits transition from green to a dark color upon maturity, aiding in the plant's reproductive cycle.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Eurya rubiginosa can cause skin irritation, redness, and contact dermatitis upon direct contact with its leaves or stems. While comprehensive toxicological data on humans is limited, the plant contains phytochemicals that may trigger allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Accidental ingestion could lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach pain. It is important to handle the plant with care to avoid localized inflammation or rashes. Always consult a medical professional if you suspect a reaction has occurred after exposure.
