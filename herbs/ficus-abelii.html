Ficus abelii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus abelii?

Ficus abelii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Rosales and belongs to the family Moraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Ficus, and its specific species name, abelii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus abelii has elliptical to oblong leaves that measure approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length and possess an entire margin with an acute apex. The foliage is characterized by a glossy, dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside. These leaves are arranged in an alternate pattern along the woody stems of the tree. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are borne in axillary clusters. Following pollination, the tree develops small, round syconia that transition from green to a reddish or yellowish hue when mature.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically ranging from India and Sri Lanka through Myanmar and Thailand to parts of Indochina. It thrives in diverse habitats ranging from low-altitude coastal areas to more elevated montane forests. Within these ecosystems, it is commonly found in evergreen forests and along riverbanks where moisture is abundant. Due to its adaptability to warm climates, it has also been introduced to other tropical regions around the world for ornamental use. This widespread distribution is supported by its ability to tolerate various soil types found across its native range.
