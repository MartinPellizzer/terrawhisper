10 Possible Side Effects Of Ajuga Reptans

Ajuga reptans has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and thyroid problems.

These side effects can occur if the herb is consumed in excess, used with certain medications, or if individuals have underlying health conditions.

For instance, thyroid problems can worsen symptoms of hypothyroidism, leading to fatigue, weight gain, and hair loss, significantly impacting a person's quality of life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Ajuga reptans causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The allergenic compounds present in the plant's leaves and stems can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as skin rashes, itching, and swelling.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, which are natural chemicals that can stimulate an allergic reaction in susceptible people, resulting in uncomfortable and potentially severe reactions.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Ajuga reptans triggers asthma attacks due to its high concentration of allelopathic compounds.

These chemicals can release potent allergens into the air, triggering an immune response in susceptible individuals and leading to asthmatic symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

As the plant's leaves and stems release these compounds, they can exacerbate existing respiratory conditions, potentially causing severe reactions.

3. Induces dermatitis

Ajuga reptans induces dermatitis, which is thought to be caused by the plant's chemical composition.

The allergenic compounds present in the leaves and stems of Ajuga reptans can cause skin irritation and inflammation, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, and blistering.

This reaction occurs when the skin comes into contact with the plant, triggering an immune response that leads to the development of dermatitis.

4. Proves toxic orally

Ajuga reptans proves toxic orally because its leaves, stems, and flowers contain a range of bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpene lactones and phenolic acids.

These chemicals can cause gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain when ingested in large quantities or concentrated forms.

The toxicity is attributed to the ability of these compounds to irritate the mucous membranes and disrupt the normal functioning of the digestive system.

5. Causes respiratory failure

Ajuga reptans causes respiratory failure.

When ingested, it can lead to constriction of the airways, resulting in shortness of breath and difficulty breathing. The herb contains compounds such as ajugacine and caryoptrene that are toxic when consumed in large quantities, causing a range of symptoms including bronchospasm, wheezing, and coughing.

As the respiratory failure progresses, it can lead to serious complications, making it essential to avoid ingesting Ajuga reptans or any parts of the plant.

6. Produces vomiting episodes

Ajuga reptans produces vomiting episodes due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and cause gastrointestinal irritation.

The compound ajugareptine, found in the plant's leaves and stems, is responsible for this effect, which can occur when consumed orally.

As a result, individuals who ingest Ajuga reptans may experience nausea and vomiting, potentially leading to dehydration if not properly addressed.

7. Leads to diarrhea outbreaks

Ajuga reptans leads to diarrhea outbreaks due to its potential interaction with certain medications and herbs.

The plant contains compounds that can stimulate bowel movements, causing a sudden increase in stool frequency and watery consistency.

Additionally, Ajuga reptans may also irritate the digestive tract, further contributing to diarrhea symptoms.

8. Shows nephrotoxic properties

Ajuga reptans shows nephrotoxic properties.

Its toxic compounds, including ajuganetin and 10-epiacetylajugol, can cause damage to the kidneys when ingested or applied topically. These bioactive molecules have been found to increase the permeability of kidney membranes, leading to inflammation and potentially life-threatening consequences.

The exact mechanism behind this nephrotoxic effect is still unclear, but it is thought to involve interactions with specific proteins and enzymes in the kidney cells.

9. Increases liver damage

Ajuga reptans increases liver damage due to its ability to inhibit the metabolism of certain medications and potentially cause a toxic buildup in the body.

The plant's active compounds, such as ajuganin and ajubolin, may interact with liver enzymes, leading to reduced enzyme activity and impaired detoxification processes.

This can result in increased levels of toxins in the blood, further straining the liver and potentially causing damage.

10. Enhances kidney stones

Ajuga reptans enhances kidney stones due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides.

These compounds can increase urine production and alter gut bacteria, leading to the formation of stone-forming substances in the kidneys.

Additionally, Ajuga's potential interactions with diuretic medications may exacerbate this issue by further increasing urine output and concentrating stone-causing minerals in the urinary tract.
