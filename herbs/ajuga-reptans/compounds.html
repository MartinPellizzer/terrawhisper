Ajuga (Ajuga reptans) Compounds

Ajuga reptans contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to its astringent effects. Coumarin is present and may have anti-inflammatory benefits, while phenolic acids and terpenoids support its overall medicinal profile. Glycosides, saponins, and essential oils also play roles in its therapeutic effects. Alkaloids and anthocyanins further contribute to its pharmacological activity.

Ajuga (Ajuga reptans) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Ajuga reptans. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory ailments. Flavonoids may also enhance the overall efficacy of Ajuga-based remedies.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Ajuga reptans, contributing to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and bleeding by tightening tissues. Tannins also support digestive health and may aid in detoxification. This compound plays a significant role in the traditional use of Ajuga reptans in herbal medicine.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Ajuga reptans, known for its anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Coumarin also exhibits mild sedative effects, supporting its role in herbal medicine for calming and soothing applications.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Ajuga reptans. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, which may support immune function and reduce inflammation. This compound is part of the broader group of bioactive substances in Ajuga that are studied for their potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Ajuga reptans, known for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Specific terpenoids in Ajuga may support immune function and reduce oxidative stress.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is an active compound found in Ajuga reptans. It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, particularly in reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing. Glycosides in Ajuga are often extracted for their potent biological effects. These compounds are key to the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Ajuga reptans, known for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive ailments. Saponins may also support immune function and have mild detoxifying effects.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Ajuga reptans contains volatile compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its antimicrobial properties. These compounds are extracted through steam distillation and are used in herbal medicine for their potential therapeutic effects.

9. Alkaloid

Ajuga reptans contains the alkaloid ajugine. This compound is known for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Ajugine is one of several bioactive compounds found in Ajuga reptans that support its role in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Ajuga reptans, known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Anthocyanin also supports cardiovascular health by reducing oxidative stress. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Ajuga reptans in herbal medicine.
