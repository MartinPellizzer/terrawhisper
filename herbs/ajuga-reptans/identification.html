To identify Ajuga (Ajuga reptans) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Ajuga reptans belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Dipsacales, family Caprifoliaceae, genus Ajuga, and species Ajuga reptans. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader system of plant life.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Ajuga reptans is the scientific name for the common bugleweed. It was first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. The species has several synonyms, including Galium reptans and Ajuga reptans L. It is also known by various common names such as carpet grass and false forget-me-not.

Morphological Characteristics

Ajuga reptans is a low-growing perennial with a rhizomatous root system that spreads horizontally. Its stems are upright and often branched, reaching up to 20 cm in height. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a dark green color. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences with small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule containing tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Ajuga reptans can be distinguished by its low-growing habit, dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its spikes of blue to purple flowers. It differs from similar species like Ajuga chamaecaulis by having more elongated leaves and a more upright flowering stem. Its leaves are typically broader and less hairy, and it often forms dense mats.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Ajuga reptans is native to Europe and parts of Asia, particularly in temperate regions. It prefers moist, shaded environments with well-drained soil. It thrives in altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters, commonly found in woodlands and rocky slopes.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Ajuga reptans typically blooms from late spring to early summer, producing small blue or purple flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer, with seeds maturing by early fall. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest the plant is during the flowering period, when leaves and flowers are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Ajuga reptans is most closely related to species within the same genus, Ajuga, which belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family. It shares key family traits such as opposite leaves and showy flowers, but differs in leaf shape and growth habits.
