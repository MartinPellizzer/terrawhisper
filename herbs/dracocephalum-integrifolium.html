Dracocephalum integrifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Dracocephalum integrifolium?

Dracocephalum integrifoliuum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae, which characterizes its structural and botanical relationships. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Dracocephalum, and its unique species designation, integrifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum integrifoliuum has square stems, opposite leaves, and small blue to violet bilabiate flowers arranged in dense terminal whorls. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate with entire margins and a slightly textured surface. Its flowering spikes are compact, often appearing as crowded clusters at the ends of the branches. The plant produces small, nut-like fruits that are characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. These morphological traits help distinguish the species from other members of the dragonhead genus.
