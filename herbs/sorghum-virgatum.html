What is Sorghum virgatum?

Sorghum virgatum is a perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It has a slender stem, 1-2 meters tall, and a leafy growth habit, with lance-shaped leaves 5-20 centimeters long and 1-5 centimeters wide. The inflorescence is a panicle, 10-40 centimeters long, bearing 2-4 florets per spikelet, with a glume and a lemma. Sorghum virgatum is native to tropical Africa and is a wild relative of domesticated sorghum species.

The plant flowers from June to October and produces brown, hairy seeds 2-3 millimeters long.
