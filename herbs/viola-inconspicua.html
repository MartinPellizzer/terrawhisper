What's the taxonomical classification of Viola inconspicua?

Viola inconspicua belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Violaceae. At a more specific level, it is situated within the genus Viola, eventually reaching its unique designation as the species inconspicua.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola inconspicua has small, creeping rhizomes with basal, ovate to elliptical leaves that possess long petioles and crenate margins. The flowers are typically solitary and emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a delicate violet or pale lavender coloration. Each blossom features five petals, including two larger upper petals and three lower petals that often contain darker nectar guides. The plant maintains a low-growing, herbaceous habit that allows it to spread through shaded forest floors. Its reproductive structures consist of a small capsule that dehisces to release numerous tiny seeds.
