Scirpus lupulinus

Scirpus lupulinus, commonly known as wolf's beard or wolf's tail, is a type of wetland grass that belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including reducing inflammation, relieving respiratory issues such as bronchitis, and acting as an expectorant to aid in the removal of mucus from the lungs.

The therapeutic actions of Scirpus lupulinus include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antispasmodic properties, which contribute to its ability to soothe and calm the body.

The bioactive constituents of Scirpus lupulinus include flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.
