Dichondra repens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichondra repens has a creeping growth habit characterized by prostrate, trailing stems that form a dense mat via stolons. The leaves are small, kidney-shaped, and possess smooth margins with a glossy, dark green surface. These foliage structures are arranged alternately along the slender, rooting stems that spread horizontally across the soil. Tiny, inconspicuous yellowish flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the warmer growing seasons. The plant's root system is shallow, allowing it to colonize groundcover areas effectively through its spreading stems.

      Dichondra repens is prepared primarily as a liquid infusion, decoction, or topical poultice for external application.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Dichondra repens can cause mild skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals who come into direct contact with its foliage. While generally considered non-toxic to humans, some people may experience itching or redness upon touching the leaves. There is limited research regarding its safety for pets, so caution is advised if animals ingest the plant. Most side effects are localized to the skin and typically resolve quickly once contact is avoided. Always monitor for unexpected reactions if you are introducing this groundcover to a new landscape.
