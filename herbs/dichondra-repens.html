What's the taxonomical classification of Dichondra repens?

Dichondra repens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Solanales and into the family Convolvulaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Dichondra, and its unique species designation, repens.

These compounds also act on superoxide dismutase and catalase to mitigate oxidative stress at a cellular level. Through these specific protein interactions, the plant demonstrates potential as an anti-inflammatory and antioxidant agent.

What are the herbal preparations of Dichondra repens?

Dichondra repens is prepared primarily as a liquid infusion, decoction, or topical poultice for external application. The leaves and stems are often steeped in hot water to create a mild tea used for soothing skin irritations. When prepared as a decoction, the plant material is boiled to extract more potent compounds for topical washes. Crushed fresh leaves can also be applied directly to the skin in the form of a poultice to address minor abrasions. These various preparations allow the plant's properties to be utilized for both gentle cleansing and localized skin relief.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dichondra repens can cause mild skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals who come into direct contact with its foliage. While generally considered non-toxic to humans, some people may experience itching or redness upon touching the leaves. There is limited research regarding its safety for pets, so caution is advised if animals ingest the plant. Most side effects are localized to the skin and typically resolve quickly once contact is avoided. Always monitor for unexpected reactions if you are introducing this groundcover to a new landscape.
