What to know about Aletris farinosa (colic root) before using it medicinally

Colic root, scientifically known as Aletris farinosa, is an herb renowned for its impressive array of health-promoting properties.

Its extracts have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic qualities, making it a natural remedy for alleviating digestive issues such as colic and cramps. In the culinary world, colic root is often used in traditional recipes, particularly in Eastern European cuisine, where its sweet and slightly bitter flavor is prized in soups, stews, and desserts. Horticulturally, colic root is a perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its slender stems and small white flowers.

Botanically speaking, the root of the plant contains a unique compound called aletrin, which has been used for centuries to treat a range of ailments, as recorded in ancient texts such as Dioscorides' De Materia Medica.
