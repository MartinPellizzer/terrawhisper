Maranthes chrysophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Maranthes chrysophylla?

Maranthes chrysophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Chrysobalanaeaceae. Ultimately, its specific identity is defined by its placement within the genus Maranthes, with its unique biological classification completed by its species name, chrysophylla.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Maranthes chrysophylla contains high concentrations of bioactive alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids that provide significant therapeutic properties. These specific secondary metabolites are responsible for the plant's observed antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects in traditional medicine. Researchers have identified various phenolic compounds within the leaves that help mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Furthermore, the presence of specific glycosides contributes to the plant's potential use in managing metabolic disorders. These chemical constituents make the species a subject of interest for developing new pharmaceutical agents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, digestive issues, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves are often crushed and applied topically to soothe skin irritations or promote the healing of minor cuts. In traditional medicinal practices, decoctions made from the plant are consumed to address stomach ailments and internal discomfort. Its bioactive compounds are believed to possess antimicrobial and analgesic properties that aid in recovery. This versatile herb serves as a vital component in folk medicine for managing diverse physical ailments.
