What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus rosthornianus?

Celastrus rosthornianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Celastrales. Within this order, it is situated in the family Celastraceae, falling under the genus Celastrus, and is specifically identified by its species name, rosthornianus.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Celastrus rosthornianus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of bioactive alkaloids and glycosides. Consumption of the plant parts often leads to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Topical contact with the foliage or stems may result in contact dermatitis or localized redness on the skin. There is also a risk of systemic toxicity if large quantities are consumed by humans or animals. Because the specific toxicological profile is not extensively documented in clinical literature, caution is advised when handling any part of this species.
