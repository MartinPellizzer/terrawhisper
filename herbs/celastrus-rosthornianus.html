Celastrus rosthornianus

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus rosthornianus?

Celastrus rosthornianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Celastrales. Within this order, it is situated in the family Celastraceae, falling under the genus Celastrus, and is specifically identified by its species name, rosthornianus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Celastrus rosthornianus contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including triterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds that exhibit significant pharmacological potential. These chemical constituents are primarily studied for their ability to demonstrate antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities. Research suggests that the specific glycosides and terpenoid profiles found in the plant may contribute to its traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions. Further biochemical analysis focuses on isolating these specific molecules to understand their direct interaction with cellular pathways. Such investigations aim to validate the medicinal efficacy of the species for future therapeutic applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celastrus rosthornianus?

Celastrus rosthornianus has pharmacological properties that primarily manifest through its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial activities. These therapeutic effects are largely attributed to the presence of specific bioactive secondary metabolites such as alkaloids and flavonoids found within its tissues. Research indicates that extracts from the plant can effectively inhibit pain signaling and reduce swelling in biological models. Additionally, the plant exhibits potential in combating various bacterial strains, making it a subject of interest for developing new antimicrobial agents. These diverse biological actions suggest its utility in traditional medicinal practices for managing infections and pain.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, various types of fever, and intestinal inflammation. Its medicinal properties are primarily utilized to manage dermatological conditions through topical applications. Additionally, decoctions of the plant are sometimes employed to alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal distress. Practitioners also rely on its extracts to help regulate body temperature during febrile episodes. These traditional uses highlight the plant's role in addressing both external and internal ailments.

What are the herbal preparations of Celastrus rosthornianus?

Celastrus rosthornianus is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using its dried bark, leaves, or stems to extract active phytochemicals. These liquid extracts are often administered orally to treat various inflammatory or digestive ailments. In some traditional medicinal practices, the plant material may also be processed into a topical paste for external application on the skin. Some practitioners utilize concentrated tinctures made with alcohol to preserve the plant's potent medicinal properties for longer periods. This variety of preparation methods allows for different absorption rates and therapeutic applications depending on the intended treatment.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Celastrus rosthornianus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of bioactive alkaloids and glycosides. Consumption of the plant parts often leads to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Topical contact with the foliage or stems may result in contact dermatitis or localized redness on the skin. There is also a risk of systemic toxicity if large quantities are consumed by humans or animals. Because the specific toxicological profile is not extensively documented in clinical literature, caution is advised when handling any part of this species.
