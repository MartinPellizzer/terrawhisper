Chrysosplenium nepalense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysosplenium nepalense has perennial rhizomatous herbs characterized by erect or ascending stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and alternate, featuring long petioles and ovate to lanceolate blades with serrated margins. The inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flower heads arranged in terminal corymbose cymes. Each individual floret is actinomorphic and possesses fine, capillary pappus bristles that aid in wind dispersal. These structural features collectively distinguish the species within its specific montane habitat.
