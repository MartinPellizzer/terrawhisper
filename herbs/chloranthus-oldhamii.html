Chloranthus oldhamii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chloranthus oldhamii has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that measure approximately 10 to 20 centimeters in length with entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. The stems are woody and upright, typically reaching heights suitable for a subshrub or small shrub habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often appearing in the leaf axils during the blooming season. The plant's reproductive structures include specialized bracts that may surround the tiny, yellowish floral components. Its overall growth form is characterized by a dense, bushy arrangement of foliage that provides a lush green appearance.
