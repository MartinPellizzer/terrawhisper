What's the taxonomical classification of Elaeagnus pungens?

Elaeagnus pungens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Rosales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Elaeagnaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Elaeagnus, with its unique identity defined by the species name pungens.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 3-methylkaempferol, 4-hydroxybenzoic acid, 4-methoxybenzoic acid, caffeic acid methyl ester, and kaempferol-3-O-beta-D-glucoside. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse therapeutic potential often associated with its biological activities. Additionally, the flavonoid constituents such as kaempferol derivatives play crucial roles in its chemical profile. Together, these bioactive molecules form the basis for its pharmacological relevance.

As discussed in a study, five compounds were isolated from the ethyl acetate ether extract of leaves of Elaeagnus pungens. The structures of these constituents were elucidated as 4-hydroxybenzoic acid (1), 3, 3'-dimethoxyquercitin (2), caffeic acid methyl ester (3), methyl 3, 4-dihydroxybenzoate (4), spingic acid (5), 4-methoxybenzoic acid (6), 3-methylkaempferol (7), kaempferol-3-O-beta-D-glucoside (8), and daucosterol (9). Compounds 1 through 8 were isolated from this plant for the first time. These chemical constituents possess cytotoxicity activity.
