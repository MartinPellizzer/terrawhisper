What's the taxonomical classification of Elaeagnus pungens?

Elaeagnus pungens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Rosales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Elaeagnaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Elaeagnus, with its unique identity defined by the species name pungens.

What are the herbal preparations of Elaeagnus pungens?

Elaeagnuus pungens is prepared as a decoction from the bark, an infusion from the leaves, or a topical salve using the crushed plant material. These liquid preparations are commonly used to extract the bioactive compounds necessary for medicinal applications. The decoction method involves boiling the tougher bark parts to ensure a potent concentration of constituents. Alternatively, steeping the leaves in hot water creates a milder infusion suitable for different therapeutic needs. External applications often involve mixing the plant into an oil or wax base to create a soothing salve for the skin.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Elaeagnus pungens can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or mild gastrointestinal distress if its parts are ingested. The prickly leaves and stems may cause physical abrasions or small punctures upon contact with the skin. Some individuals might experience a localized rash or redness due to sensitive reactions to the plant's oils or compounds. While not typically classified as highly toxic, accidental consumption of the berries or foliage can lead to stomach upset in humans or pets. Monitoring for these symptoms is advised if contact or ingestion occurs.
