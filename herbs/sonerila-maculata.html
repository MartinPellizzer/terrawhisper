Sonerila maculata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sonerila maculata has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and distinct purplish spots on the undersides. The stems are typically slender and herbaceous, often exhibiting a reddish tint in certain growth stages. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring five petals that are usually white or pale pink. The inflorescence is characterized by a terminal raceme that carries multiple individual blossoms. These plants often grow in moist, shaded environments where their low-lying structure thrives.
