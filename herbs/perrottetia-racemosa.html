Perrottetia racemosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Huerteales and the family Dipentodontaceae, which ultimately leads to its placement within the genus Perrottetia and its specific designation as the species racemosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Perrottetia racemosa has small, lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along its slender, woody stems.

These leaves feature entire margins and a texture that is often slightly leathery or coriaceous. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are organized into distinct raceme inflorescences. These flower clusters emerge from the axils of the leaves and extend outward on delicate peduncles.

The overall structure of the shrub is characterized by its fine, branching habit and compact growth form.
