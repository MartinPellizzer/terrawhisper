Mitreola pedicellata

What's the taxonomical classification of Mitreola pedicellata?

Mitreola pedicellaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Gentianaales and the family Loganiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Mitreola, and its unique species designation, pedicellaata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mitreola pedicellaata has a small, fusiform shell characterized by a high spire, numerous axial ribs, and a distinctively flared, pedal-shaped aperture.

The shell surface typically exhibits fine spiral ornamentation that intersects with the prominent vertical ribs to create a beaded appearance. Its color palette often consists of pale cream or light brown tones, sometimes featuring subtle darker markings. The aperture is notably wide and often features a thickened outer lip that contributes to its unique shape.

These morphological traits are essential for distinguishing it from other similar gastropod species within its genus.
