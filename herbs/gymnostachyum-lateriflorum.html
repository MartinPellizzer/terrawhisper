What is Gymnostachyum lateriflorum?

Gymnostachyum lateriflorum is an evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats in India, belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is an annual or perennial herb with erect or prostrate stems, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Leaves are oppositely arranged, simple, and elliptic in shape, measuring 4-14 cm in length and 1.5-5 cm in width, with an acute apex and entire margin. The plant produces axillary, corymbiform cymes, with numerous small white or pale yellow flowers, each measuring 2-3 mm in diameter, and bearing four-lobed, tubular corolla.

The fruit is a small, four-valved capsule, containing numerous small seeds.
