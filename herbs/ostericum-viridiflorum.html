Ostericum viridiflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Ostericum viridiflorum?

Ostericum viridiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned within the order Apiales. This plant is a representative of the family Apiaceae, which is characterized by its complex floral structures. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Ostericum, with its specific designation being the species viridiflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ostericum viridiflorum has an erect, rhizomatous habit featuring compound leaves that are bipinnate or tripinnate with serrated leaflets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, displaying a lush, herbaceous structure. Its flowers are small and green, arranged in terminal umbels that lack showy petals. The stems are relatively slender and often exhibit a slightly reddish tint near the base. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive within the shaded, moist environments of deciduous forests.
