Trollius macropetalus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trollius macropetalus has large, globe-shaped yellow flowers composed of numerous petaloid sepals and a cluster of small, central true petals. The plant features deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves that are dark green and possess serrated margins. Its stems are sturdy and erect, typically reaching heights that support the heavy floral heads. The root system consists of a thick, perennial rhizome that allows the species to persist through multiple growing seasons. These morphological traits are adaptations suited for its specific montane habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Trollius macropetalus can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of ranunculin. This chemical compound converts into protoanemonin when the plant tissues are damaged or crushed. Contact with the sap can lead to blistering or redness on the skin through contact dermatitis. If eaten, individuals may experience nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. It is essential to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse physiological reactions.
