Siberian Ginseng (Eleutherococcus senticosus) Compounds

Siberian Ginseng contains several key compounds, including saponins, which have adaptogenic properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Glycosides and alkaloids contribute to its stimulating and immune-supporting actions. Phenolic acids and tannins provide additional antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits.

Siberian Ginseng (Eleutherococcus senticosus) Monograph

1. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Siberian Ginseng, Eleutherococcus senticosus. It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic properties by supporting the body's stress response and enhancing immune function. Saponins also have mild antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, making them important for the plant's therapeutic applications.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Siberian Ginseng, Eleutherococcus senticosus. They contribute to the plant's adaptogenic properties by supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are often present in high concentrations in the roots and leaves of the plant.

3. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Siberian Ginseng (Eleutherococcus senticosus). It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic properties, supporting stress resistance and energy metabolism. Glycosides in Siberian Ginseng are often associated with enhanced physical endurance and cognitive function.

4. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Siberian Ginseng (Eleutherococcus senticosus). It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic properties, supporting the body's ability to resist stress and maintain homeostasis. Alkaloids in Siberian Ginseng are known to enhance physical performance and cognitive function.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Siberian Ginseng (Eleutherococcus senticosus). It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic properties by supporting cellular protection and metabolic function. This compound helps enhance physical endurance and reduce stress response in the body.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Siberian Ginseng, Eleutherococcus senticosus. They contribute to the plant's adaptogenic properties, enhancing physical and mental performance under stress. These compounds support immune function and help regulate physiological processes.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Siberian Ginseng (Eleutherococcus senticosus). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support immune function and antioxidant activity. Tannins are found in the roots and berries of this medicinal plant, enhancing its traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Siberian Ginseng (Eleutherococcus senticosus). It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic properties, helping the body manage stress. Coumarin also has mild anticoagulant effects and may support cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Siberian Ginseng (Eleutherococcus senticosus). It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic properties, supporting stress resistance and immune function. Found in the roots and stems, it contains volatile compounds that enhance metabolic processes and mental clarity.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key compound in Siberian Ginseng, Eleutherococcus senticosus, known for its antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic effects by neutralizing free radicals and supporting cellular health. This compound is found in the roots and stems of the plant. Its presence enhances the overall therapeutic potential of Siberian Ginseng.
