10 Possible Side Effects Of Eleutherococcus Senticosus (Siberian Ginseng)

Eleutherococcus senticosus has some side effects when used improperly, such as dizziness, insomnia, and digestive problems.

These side effects may be caused by excessive consumption or interactions with medications. For instance, a person suffering from high blood pressure may experience worsened hypertension due to Eleutherococcus senticosus, potentially leading to cardiovascular issues or even a heart attack.

Additionally, its overuse may exacerbate existing anxiety or depression.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Eleutherococcus senticosus if used imporperly.

1. Affects mental clarity

Eleutherococcus senticosus, also known as Siberian ginseng, increases blood pressure by stimulating the body's stress response and releasing adrenaline, a hormone that constricts blood vessels.

This can lead to a temporary increase in blood pressure, which may be more pronounced in individuals who are already experiencing stress or anxiety.

Additionally, Eleutherococcus senticosus can also enhance the effects of other herbs that stimulate the nervous system, further contributing to increased blood pressure.

The herb's stimulating effects can lead to an overload of sensory information, causing dizziness or lightheadedness, particularly when taken in large doses or combined with other stimulants.

Additionally, Eleutherococcus senticosus may also exacerbate existing anxiety or stress disorders, further contributing to the occurrence of dizziness attacks.

The allergenic compounds present in Eleutherococcus senticosus, including polyacetylenes and sesquiterpenes, may also bind to proteins on skin cells, leading to the development of an allergic response.

As a result, individuals with pre-existing allergies or sensitivities may experience adverse reactions when using this herb.
