What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron orbiculare?

Rhododendron orbiculare belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Ericales and the family Ericaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Rhododendron, and its unique species designation, orbiculare.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron orbiculare has evergreen, leathery leaves that are distinctly orbicular or broadly oval in shape, typically measuring 3 to 8 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are entire and slightly revolute, while the upper surface is a deep, lustrous green and the underside is paler. These leaves are arranged in terminal clusters at the ends of the woody, spreading branches. During the flowering season, the plant produces bell-shaped corollas that range in color from pale pink to deep rose. The inflorescence consists of several individual flowers held in a compact raceme.
