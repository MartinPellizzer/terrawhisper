Chrysanthemum (Chrysanthemum morifolium)

Chrysanthemum (Chrysanthemum morifolium) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to China, Korea, and Japan. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, sedative, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 0.3 to 1.5 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface ranging from dark green to bluish-green and lower surface lighter green, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Radially symmetrical flowers with involucral bracts, tubular central cone surrounded by two whorls of ray florets with yellow to white ligules and numerous disc florets with yellow petals.
Stems
Climbing, cylindrical, woody, with opposite branching, covered in fine pubescence.
