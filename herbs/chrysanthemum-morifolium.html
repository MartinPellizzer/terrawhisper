Side Effects of chrysanthemum morifolium

Chrysanthemum morifolium eyes irritate due to its high concentration of volatile oils and pollen, which can cause redness, itching, and excessive tearing, particularly in individuals with sensitive eyes or existing ocular conditions.

This herb is also a common allergy trigger, as it contains compounds that may provoke allergic reactions in people with respiratory or skin sensitivities, leading to symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, and even asthma exacerbation. Ingesting large quantities of chrysanthemum morifolium can result in stomach upset, nausea, and vomiting, as its bitter compounds may irritate the gastrointestinal tract. Additionally, it can cause mouth irritation and throat discomfort, especially when consumed in teas or supplements, due to its strong flavor and potential for mucosal irritation.

While it is known for its immune-boosting properties, excessive use may lead to sleep disturbance, headaches, and vision blur, highlighting the importance of moderation and proper usage guidelines when incorporating this herb into one's health regimen.
