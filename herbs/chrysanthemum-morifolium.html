Chrysanthemum Morifolium: What To Know Before Using It For Medicinal Purposes

Chrysanthemum morifolium, commonly known as the garden chrysanthemum, has been widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries due to its various medicinal properties.

It contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, polysaccharides, and essential oils, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. In traditional medicine, it is often used to treat conditions such as hypertension, fever, and skin infections. Modern research has also shown potential benefits in improving immune function and reducing oxidative stress.

However, it is important to use Chrysanthemum morifolium under the guidance of a healthcare professional to ensure safe and effective usage.

Health Benefits

Chrysanthemum morifolium has several health benefits, such as its ability to support cardiovascular health by helping to lower blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

It is also known for its antioxidant properties, which can help protect cells from damage caused by free radicals. The plant contains compounds like flavonoids and polyphenols that may reduce inflammation and improve immune function. In traditional medicine, it has been used to alleviate symptoms of headaches, fever, and eye strain.

Additionally, chrysanthemum tea is often consumed for its calming effects and to promote digestive health.

Bioactive Constituents

Chrysanthemum morifolium has several bioactive constituents, such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its wide range of medicinal properties.

Flavonoids, including chrysanthemin and luteolin, exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and neuroprotective effects. Terpenoids like sesquiterpene lactones possess antimicrobial and antitumor activities, making them valuable in traditional and modern medicine. Polysaccharides from the plant have immunomodulatory properties, enhancing the body's immune response.

These bioactive compounds collectively support the use of Chrysanthemum morifolium in treating ailments such as inflammation, allergies, and respiratory disorders.

Medicinal Preparations

Chrysanthemum morifolium has several medicinal preparations, such as teas, tinctures, and extracts, which have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

One of the most common forms is chrysanthemum tea, made by steeping dried flowers in hot water, and it is often consumed for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects. Tinctures of chrysanthemum are also popular, typically prepared by soaking the dried flowers in alcohol to extract their active compounds. These preparations are valued for their ability to support respiratory health, reduce fever, and alleviate symptoms of allergies and headaches.

In modern herbal medicine, chrysanthemum is also used in combination with other herbs to create formulations that address a wide range of health concerns.

Side Effects

Chrysanthemum morifolium can have some side effects, such as gastrointestinal discomfort, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, especially when consumed in large quantities.

It may also cause allergic reactions in individuals sensitive to plants in the Asteraceae family, leading to symptoms like rash, itching, or respiratory issues. Prolonged use of chrysanthemum may interfere with liver function, as it contains compounds that can be toxic in high doses. Additionally, it may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the central nervous system or blood pressure.

Therefore, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using chrysanthemum for medicinal purposes.
