Chrysanthemum Morifolium Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Chrysanthemum morifolium, commonly known as the chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia, widely used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties.

This herb is valued for its ability to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and improve vision, making it a popular remedy in Chinese medicine.

The bioactive constituents responsible for its therapeutic actions include flavonoids, volatile oils, and polysaccharides, which exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and decoctions can be made from its dried flowers to harness its medicinal benefits.

Medicinal Constituents of chrysanthemum morifolium

Chrysanthemum morifolium flavonoids, such as apigenin, luteolin, and chrysanthemin, are known for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the herb's traditional use in treating conditions like headaches and fever.

Chrysanthemum morifolium phenolic acids, including caffeic acid and chlorogenic acid, exhibit strong antioxidant activity and have been associated with anti-cancer and neuroprotective effects due to their ability to scavenge free radicals.

Chrysanthemum morifolium fatty acids, primarily unsaturated fats like linoleic and oleic acid, contribute to the herb's anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits by modulating lipid metabolism and reducing oxidative stress.

Chrysanthemum morifolium terpenes, such as α-pinene and β-caryophyllene, possess antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and sedative properties, making them valuable for their therapeutic effects in respiratory and digestive health.

Medicinal Parts of chrysanthemum morifolium

Chrysanthemum morifolium flower and leaf are both valued for their medicinal properties, with the flower being commonly used for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, while the leaf is known for its cooling and detoxifying properties.

The main herbal preparations from the flower include infusions, decoctions, and tinctures, often used to treat headaches, fever, and respiratory conditions.

From the leaf, similar preparations such as teas and poultices are made to address skin irritations and digestive issues.

Both parts are essential in traditional Chinese medicine and are used to support overall health and balance.
