Xylocarpus moluccensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Xylocarpus moluccensis?

Xylocarpus moluccensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Sapindales. It is further categorized into the family Meliaceae, within the genus Xylocarpus, and is specifically identified by its species name, moluccensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Xylocarpus moluccensis contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including limonoids, xanthones, coumarins, and various terpenoids. These chemical constituents, particularly the highly oxygenated limonoids, exhibit significant pharmacological potential in laboratory settings. Research indicates that these compounds possess notable antibacterial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies also suggest that extracts from the plant bark and fruit may demonstrate cytotoxic activities against specific cancer cell lines. The complex chemical profile of this mangrove species makes it a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Xylocarpus moluccensis?

Xylocarpus moluccensis has pharmacological properties that include antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant activities derived primarily from its bioactive secondary metabolites. The plant contains significant concentrations of xanthones and terpenoids which contribute to its ability to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. These chemical constituents work by neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress within biological systems. Furthermore, extracts from the bark and fruit are often studied for their potential to modulate inflammatory responses in cellular models. This diverse chemical profile makes the species a subject of interest for developing new natural therapeutic agents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach ailments, inflammation, and various types of tumors. The bark and roots are frequently employed in traditional medicine to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and digestive issues. Its extracts also demonstrate antibacterial properties that help manage topical skin conditions and wounds. Some studies suggest that the chemical compounds within the plant may possess anti-inflammatory and potential anti-cancer activities. These medicinal applications vary significantly depending on the specific part of the plant used and the preparation method.

What are the herbal preparations of Xylocarpus moluccensis?

Xylocarpus moluccensis is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a powder from the dried fruit, or an infusion of the leaves to treat various ailments. The bark is often boiled in water to create a medicinal liquid used for its astringent properties. Practitioners may also grind the dried seeds or fruit into a fine powder to be ingested or applied topically. In some traditional practices, the leaves are steeped in hot water to make a tea for systemic relief. These different forms allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be utilized for specific therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Xylocarpus moluccensis can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if its bioactive compounds are ingested or applied improperly to the body. The plant contains various secondary metabolites, including xanthones and terpenoids, which may trigger allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Contact with the bark or fruit extracts can lead to localized dermatitis or redness on the skin. While some traditional practices use the plant medicinally, concentrated doses may disrupt internal biological processes. Therefore, caution is necessary when handling any part of this mangrove species to avoid unintended physiological harm.
