Cycas revoluta

What's the taxonomical classification of Cycas revoluta?

Cycas revoluta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Cycadidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Cycadales and the family Cycadaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Cycas, and its specific species name, revoluta.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Cycas revoluta contains medicinal compounds primarily extracted from its seeds and roots, although these parts are highly toxic if not processed correctly. The starch extracted from the seeds is traditionally used in various cultures to create medicinal preparations after undergoing extensive detoxification. Some traditional practices involve using the root extract to treat skin conditions or as a remedy for certain inflammatory ailments. However, the plant contains potent neurotoxins and carcinogens like cycasin that require extreme caution during any medicinal application. Most modern use focuses on studying these chemical constituents rather than direct consumption due to the risk of severe poisoning.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains cycasin, a toxic glucoside with alkaloid-like properties, alongside methylenecyclopropyl amino acid (MCPAA), flavonoids, terpenoids, phenolic compounds, and sterols.

While cycasin is known for its significant toxicity, certain components like MCPAA have been researched for potential neuroprotective effects. The presence of flavonoids and phenolic compounds provides the plant with various antioxidant activities. Additionally, the terpenoids and sterols contribute to its complex chemical profile and biological interactions.

These diverse medicinal compounds collectively define the phytochemical landscape of Cycas revoluta.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cycas revoluta targets various enzymes and signaling pathways including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various proinflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. These bioactive compounds primarily act by inhibiting the production of inflammatory mediators that drive pain and swelling in the body. Some phytochemicals within the plant also demonstrate an ability to modulate antioxidant enzymes such as superoxide dismutase and catalase to mitigate oxidative stress. Additionally, certain extracts interact with specific receptor sites to influence cellular metabolism and potentially provide neuroprotective benefits. Through these multifaceted molecular interactions, the plant demonstrates significant pharmacological potential in treating inflammation and oxidative damage.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cycas revoluta?

Cycas revoluta has no recognized therapeutic actions in human medicine because its chemical composition is highly toxic and potentially lethal if ingested. The plant contains potent neurotoxins and carcinogens, such as cycasin, which can cause severe liver damage and neurological impairment. Ingesting any part of the plant may lead to acute poisoning characterized by vomiting, abdominal pain, and even death. Because the risks of toxicity far outweigh any potential benefits, it is not used for medicinal purposes. Research is primarily focused on understanding its toxicity rather than finding healing applications.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and various inflammatory rashes through the application of its topical extracts.

The plant contains specific chemical compounds that can help soothe irritated skin when processed correctly. Some traditional practices involve using the plant to manage localized swelling or minor dermatological ailments. It is important to note that the plant is highly toxic if ingested, so its medicinal use is strictly limited to external applications.

Careful preparation is necessary to avoid accidental poisoning during the treatment process.

    What are the herbal preparations of Cycas revoluta?

    Cycas revoluta is prepared as a decoction, powder, or topical paste derived from its starch-rich seeds, leaves, or roots. Historically, the starch extracted from the seeds has been processed into flour for food or used in medicinal infusions. The leaves are often crushed into a poultice to treat skin ailments or topical inflammations. Some traditional practices involve boiling the plant parts to create concentrated liquid extracts. It is crucial to note that all preparations must undergo extensive detoxification to remove highly toxic cycasin.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Cycas revoluta can cause severe organ failure, vomiting, diarrhea, and death if any part of the plant is ingested due to its high concentration of cycasin. This potent neurotoxin and carcinogen primarily targets the liver, potentially leading to irreversible hepatic necrosis. Symptoms of poisoning often manifest within hours of consumption and can escalate quickly in children or pets. Beyond internal organ damage, contact with the plant may also cause skin irritation or dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Because the toxins are present in all parts of the plant, including the seeds and roots, extreme caution is necessary when handling it.
