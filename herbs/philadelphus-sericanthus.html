Philadelphus sericanthus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Philadelphus sericanthus has opposite, elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a woody, shrubby growth habit reaching up to two meters in height. The stems are characterized by a somewhat rough texture and a branching structure that supports dense foliage. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge in clusters, featuring four distinct petals and prominent central stamens. The plant typically produces small, rounded woody capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. This morphology allows the species to thrive in various woodland and scrubland environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Philadelphus sericanthus can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals when direct contact is made with its foliage or stems. Some people may experience redness, itching, or a localized rash upon touching the plant. While widespread toxicity is not extensively documented, ingestion of any part of the shrub could potentially lead to gastrointestinal upset. It is advisable to wear gloves when pruning the plant to avoid these dermatological reactions. Always consult a medical professional if symptoms persist after exposure.
