What's the taxonomical classification of Heisteria zimmereri?

Heisteria zimmereri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Santalales. It is specifically categorized under the family Olacaceae, falling within the genus Heisteria, with the specific epithet zimmereri completing its scientific designation.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Heisteria zimmereri has an erect, woody stem characterized by a rough, fissured bark and a branching pattern that produces dense, spreading foliage.

Each leaf features a prominent midrib and a deep green coloration that provides a stark contrast against the lighter undersides. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axillary buds during the peak growing season.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Heisteria zimmereri can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, acute neurological impairment, and potentially fatal respiratory failure if ingested. Victims may also experience profound disorientation or loss of consciousness shortly after exposure. Medical intervention is typically required to manage the systemic toxicity and prevent long-term organ damage.
