Croton wellensii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Croton wellensii has elliptic to oblong leaves that are typically dark green with prominent venation and a woody, shrubby growth habit. The plant features a branching structure that supports dense foliage throughout its stems. Its leaves are arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches. The texture of the foliage can vary depending on the specific environmental conditions of its habitat. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, characteristic of many species within the Euphorbiaceae family.

      What are the herbal preparations of Croton wellensii?

      Croton wellensii is prepared primarily as a decoction of the roots, a leaf infusion, or a topical paste made from crushed plant matter.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Croton wellensii can cause skin irritation, blistering, and eye inflammation if its sap comes into direct contact with sensitive tissues. The plant contains caustic compounds that trigger contact dermatitis in many individuals upon touching the foliage or stems. If accidental ingestion occurs, it may lead to severe gastrointestinal distress such as vomiting or abdominal pain. People with existing skin sensitivities should exercise extreme caution when handling the plant to avoid allergic reactions. Always wear gloves and protective eyewear when pruning or repotting this species to minimize exposure risks.
