Croton wellensii

What's the taxonomical classification of Croton wellensii?

Croton wellensii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is organized under the order Malpighiales and the family Euphorbiaceae. It is specifically identified within the genus Croton, ultimately reaching its unique designation at the species level as Croton wellensii.

What are the therapeutic actions of Croton wellensii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach ailments, and various types of fever. The leaves and extracts are often applied topically to promote healing in damaged tissue or to combat fungal and bacterial growths. When ingested in specific preparations, it is believed to alleviate digestive discomfort and internal inflammation. Local medicinal practices also rely on its properties to manage systemic symptoms associated with febrile illnesses. These traditional applications highlight the plant's diverse role in addressing both external and internal health concerns.
