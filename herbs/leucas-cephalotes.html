Leucas cephalotes

What's the taxonomical classification of Leucas cephalotes?

Leucas cephalotes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Leucas, with its specific designation being the species cephalotes.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and stomach-related ailments. The leaves and bark are often prepared as decoctions or poultices to soothe localized irritation. In traditional medicinal practices, extracts from the plant are applied topically to promote the healing of minor cuts and scrapes. Some communities also consume preparations of the plant to alleviate digestive discomfort or internal inflammation. These applications rely on the plant's natural bioactive compounds to reduce swelling and combat pathogens.
