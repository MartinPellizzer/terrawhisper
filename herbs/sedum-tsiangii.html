Sedum tsiangii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedum tsiangii has succulent, cylindrical leaves that are light green to yellowish-green and arranged in dense, overlapping clusters along its stems. The leaves are typically smooth and fleshy, serving as a primary storage organ for water within the plant. Its stems are somewhat upright or trailing, often becoming woody at the base as the plant matures. The inflorescence consists of small, star-shaped flowers that emerge from the terminal ends of the stems. These blossoms are usually pale or yellowish, providing a delicate contrast to the thick, robust foliage.
