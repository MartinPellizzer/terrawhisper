Indian gooseberry

Emblica officinalis

Indian Gooseberry (Emblica officinalis) is a small, yellow-orange fruit native to the Indian subcontinent, known for its rich content of vitamin C and various bioactive compounds, and is widely used in traditional medicine to support digestive health, boost immunity, and promote skin health.

Botanical Identification

Indian Gooseberry, or Emblica officinalis, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is also known as Amla in Sanskrit. The plant is perennial, with elliptical leaves, yellow flowers, and edible, astringent berries. It has been referenced by various synonyms in traditional herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Malpighiales
  • Family: Euphorbiaceae
  • Genus: Emblica
  • Species: Emblica officinalis

Active Compounds

The Indian Gooseberry contains key bioactive compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immune-boosting effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Safety Profile

Indian Gooseberry is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated in people with ulcers or gastritis due to its high acidity. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or with existing medical conditions.
