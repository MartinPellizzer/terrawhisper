Indian Gooseberry (Emblica officinalis)

Indian Gooseberry (Emblica officinalis) is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, native to India, Nepal, and Bhutan. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and unani medicine.
