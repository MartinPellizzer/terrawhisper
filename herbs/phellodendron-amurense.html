Phelloidenron amurense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Sapindales and is a member of the family Rutaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Phelloidenron, with its unique species designation being amurense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phellodendroon amurense has woody, climbing stems that produce large, simple, opposite leaves with ovate to elliptical shapes and serrated margins. The foliage typically measures several centimeters in length and features prominent venation on the underside of the blades. These plants often develop a scrambling habit, using their stems to ascend through surrounding vegetation in forest understories. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy berries. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in temperate and subtropical woodland environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Phellodendron amurense?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antibacterial, antifungal, and antimicrobial properties. Research indicates that its chemical constituents effectively combat oxidative stress by neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. These bioactive compounds also exhibit potent inhibitory effects against various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. Such antimicrobial actions suggest that the plant could serve as a natural source for developing new therapeutic agents. These diverse biological capabilities highlight its potential utility in both traditional medicine and modern pharmacology.

As discussed in a study, Phellodendri Amurensis cortex (PAC) is a traditional Chinese herb used to treat various liver and kidney sickness and possesses good anti-inflammatory efficacy. In studies involving rat models established with 6.5 mg/kg doxorubicin injections for five weeks, PAC treatment notably decreased urinary protein, serum Cr content, and renal tissue lesions while increasing serum TP and ALB content. When administered once a day at doses of 216, 432, and 864 mg/kg, PAC demonstrated the ability to recall metabolite levels. Specifically, the high-dose group recalled 18 metabolites, the medium-dose group recalled 13 metabolites, and the low-dose group recalled 8 metabolites. These metabolites were involved in nine primary metabolic pathways, including steroid hormone biosynthesis and alanine, aspartate and glutamate metabolism. Ultimately, PAC possesses a renoprotective effect against doxorubicin-induced nephritis by improving renal function and regulating key enzymes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds berberine, jatrorrhizin, palmatine, magnoflorine, and quercetin. These specific alkaloids and flavonoids work together to provide various pharmacological benefits to the human body. Berberine and palmatine are well-known for their potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of jatrorrhizin and magnoflorine further enhances the plant's biological activity against different pathogens. Additionally, the antioxidant capabilities of quercetin contribute to the overall therapeutic profile of these chemical constituents.

As discussed in a study, Phellodendroon amurense bark contains both alkaloid and non-alkaloid fractions. The non-alkaloid fraction of this plant extract contains limonoids such as limonin and obakunone, which suppress nitric oxide production with IC50 values of 16 and 2.6 μM, respectively. Additionally, the plant contains alkaloids like berbermin which displayed an IC50 value of 12 μM. These constituents contribute to the anti-inflammatory effects of the Phellodendri Cortex extract.
