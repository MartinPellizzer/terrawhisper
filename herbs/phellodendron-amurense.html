Amur Corktree

Phellodendron amurense

Amur Corktree (Phellodendron amurense) is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating skin conditions and inflammatory disorders.

Botanical Identification

Phellodendron amurense, commonly known as Amur Corktree, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, corky bark, compound leaves, and yellowish flowers. It is also known as Huang Bai in traditional Chinese medicine.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Rutaceae
  • Genus: Phellodendron
  • Species: Phellodendron amurense

Amur Corktree Identification.

Active Compounds

The Amur Corktree contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to support various therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial actions. Their combined presence enhances the herb's efficacy in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Alkaloid
  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions like coughs and colds due to its expectorant properties.

Extract: Used for treating infections and inflammation due to its high concentration of active compounds.

Safety Profile

Amur Corktree is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential harm to the fetus or infant. It may also interact with medications metabolized by the liver, so caution is advised. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
