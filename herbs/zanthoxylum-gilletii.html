Zanthoxylum gilletii

What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum gilletii?

Zanthoxylum gilletii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Sapindales. The plant is a member of the family Rutaceae, falling under the genus Zanthoxylum, and is specifically identified by its species name, gilletii.

What are the pharmacological activities of Zanthoxylum gilletii?

This plant has potent antioxidant, cytotoxicity, and enzyme inhibitory pharmacological activities driven by its diverse secondary metabolites. The antioxidant properties allow the extracts to scavenge free radicals and mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Researchers have also observed significant cytotoxicity, which suggests potential applications in developing anti-tumor or anti-proliferative agents. Furthermore, the plant exhibits enzyme inhibitory effects that can regulate specific metabolic pathways in the body. These multifaceted biological actions make Zanthoxylum gilletii a valuable subject for further drug discovery and therapeutic development.

According to a study published by "Journal of pharmaceutical and biomedical analysis", Zanthoxylum gilletii (De Wild.) P. G. Waterman possesses antioxidant and enzyme inhibitory effects. The plant extracts were tested through free radical scavenging, metal chelator, reducing power, and phosphomolybdenum assays. All extracts of the plant exhibited good clinical enzyme inhibitory activities against acetylcholinesterase (AChE), butrylcholinesterase (BChE), tyrosinase, α-amylase, and α-glucosidase. HPLC-ESI-MS characterization revealed that the stem bark contained similar concentrations of phenolic acids and flavonoids at levels of 2.8-3.1 mg/g DE. Based on these biological activities, Zanthoxylum gilletii can be considered a potential bioresource for the development of nutraceuticals and pharmaceutical applications.
