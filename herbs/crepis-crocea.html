Crepis crocea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crepis crocea has a basal rosette of oblong to lanceolate leaves that are typically entire or slightly dentate along the margins and possess long petioles. The flowering stems are erect, often branched, and characterized by a sparsely hairy surface. Its inflorescence consists of several yellow flower heads arranged in a corymbose manner. Each individual flower head contains numerous ligulate florets that give the plant its characteristic golden appearance. The pappus is composed of fine, white bristles that facilitate seed dispersal by wind.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe, specifically spanning from the Alps through the Carpathians and into the Balkan Peninsula. It is predominantly found in alpine and subalpine habitats at high elevations. Populations are often localized to rocky slopes and calcareous grasslands where the soil is well-drained. Its distribution is closely tied to specific climatic conditions found in these elevated European mountain ranges. While it is widespread across certain ranges, it remains restricted to these high-altitude ecological niches.
