Hepatica henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hepatica henryi has leathery, kidney-shaped to suborbicular leaves that are dark green, glabrous, and characterized by prominent, deeply impressed veins. These leaves are typically arranged in a basal rosette and feature slightly lobed or crenate margins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually white or pale blue with five petals. These blossoms emerge from short pedicels and are often accompanied by small, inconspicuous bracts. The reproductive structures consist of numerous stamens and a central cluster of carpels that develop into small achenes.
