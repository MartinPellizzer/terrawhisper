Winter Savory (Satureja montana)

Winter Savory (Satureja montana) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Southern Europe, Mediterranean Region, and Central Europe. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its carminative, antispasmodic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and native american herbal medicine.

Cylindrical, woody, branched, with opposite decussate leaves and pustules on the surface.
