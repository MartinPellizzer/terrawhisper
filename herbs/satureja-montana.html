What to know about Satureja montana (summer savory) before using it medicinally

Summer savory, scientifically known as Satureja montana, is a fragrant and flavorful herb that boasts an array of health benefits, from reducing inflammation to calming digestive issues.

In the culinary world, summer savory is a versatile ingredient, often used to add depth and warmth to soups, stews, and braises, as well as to season meats, vegetables, and grains. From a horticultural perspective, summer savory is an easy-to-grow perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners and chefs alike. Botanically speaking, summer savory belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), with its delicate purple flowers and needle-like leaves.

With historical roots dating back to ancient Greece and Rome, where it was prized for its medicinal and culinary uses, summer savory has been a staple ingredient in many cuisines for centuries, with its unique flavor profile and numerous health benefits making it a beloved addition to many recipes today.
