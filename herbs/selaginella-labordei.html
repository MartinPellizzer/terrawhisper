Selaginella labordei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella labordei has a creeping rhizomatous growth habit characterized by small, scale-like microphylls arranged in four distinct ranks along the stem. The leaves are diminutive and triangular in shape, often displaying a delicate texture that facilitates moisture absorption. These plants typically form dense, low-growing mats that spread across damp substrates. The branching pattern is irregular, contributing to a complex, bushy appearance in its natural habitat. Tiny sporangia are produced in specialized clusters known as strobili located at the tips of the branches.
