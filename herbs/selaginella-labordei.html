What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella labordei?

Selaginella labordei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Ultimately, this plant is situated within the genus Selaginella, specifically identified as the species labordei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella labordei has a creeping rhizomatous growth habit characterized by small, scale-like microphylls arranged in four distinct ranks along the stem. The leaves are diminutive and triangular in shape, often displaying a delicate texture that facilitates moisture absorption. These plants typically form dense, low-growing mats that spread across damp substrates. The branching pattern is irregular, contributing to a complex, bushy appearance in its natural habitat. What are the pharmacological activities of Selaginella labordei?

This plant has anti-viral, antioxidant, and xanthine oxidase inhibiting pharmacological activities. Its ability to combat viruses makes it a potential candidate for treating various infectious diseases. The antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. Additionally, the plant acts as an inhibitor of xanthine oxidase, which is a key mechanism for managing hyperuricemia and gout. These diverse therapeutic actions suggest that Selaginella labordei holds significant potential for medicinal development.

As discussed in a study, extracts from Selaginella labordei inhibited xanthine oxidase and lipoxygenase activities. This plant also functioned as a scavenger of the ABTS+ radical cation when using the Trolox equivalent antioxidant capacity assay (TEAC). Additionally, Selaginella labordei was found to down-regulate cyclooxygenase-2 gene expression in human colon adenocarcinoma CaCo-2 cells. This specific gene expression was measured by real-time RT-PCR.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 4'-methylether robustaflavone, amentoflavone, eriodictyol, and robustaflavone. These specific flavonoids are often studied for their potential bioactive properties in human health. Researchers investigate these chemical constituents to understand how they interact with various biological pathways. The presence of these compounds contributes to the overall phytochemical profile of the species. Understanding these substances helps scientists identify the therapeutic potential inherent in the plant.

As discussed in a study, four flavone compounds were isolated from the medicinal plant Selaginella labordei, which include 4'-methylether robustaflavone (1), robustaflavone (2), eriodictyol (3) and amentoflavone (4). The 50% inhibitory concentration (IC50) of these three compounds inhibiting XOD were 61.0, 0.199, 16.0 and 32.0 mg L(-1), respectively. These compounds were isolated from the species for the first time, and eriodictyol was found from Selaginellaceae for the first time. Among the isolated compounds, robustaflavone has been reported as an effective compound against the hepatitis B virus.
