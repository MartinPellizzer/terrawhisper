Melaleuca rupestris

Melaleuca rupestris, commonly known as the Rock Melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, native to Western Australia.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering several health benefits, including antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, which can help to soothe and heal wounds, reduce fever, and fight off infections.

The therapeutic actions of Melaleuca rupestris include its ability to stimulate the immune system, reduce pain and inflammation, and exhibit antioxidant properties, which can contribute to its overall therapeutic potential.

The bioactive constituents of this plant are primarily terpenoids, including cineole, linalool, and globulol, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and are often used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy applications.
