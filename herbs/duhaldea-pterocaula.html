Duhaldea pterocaula

What's the taxonomical classification of Duhaldea pterocaula?

Duhaldea pterocaula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Duhaldea, with the specific epithet pterocaula defining its unique species status.

Duhaldea pterocaula contains bioactive alkaloids, flavonoids, and various terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. What are the therapeutic actions of Duhaldea pterocaula?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, respiratory ailments, and gastrointestinal disturbances. The leaves and bark contain bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and soothe irritated tissue. When applied topically, the plant acts as an antiseptic to combat bacterial or fungal growth on the skin. It is also utilized in traditional practices to alleviate symptoms of coughs and congestion. Additionally, certain preparations are ingested to calm stomach upset and promote digestive regularity.
