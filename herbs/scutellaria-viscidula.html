Scutellaria viscidula

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutellaria viscidula has a glandular-viscid texture across its stems and leaves, characterized by small, sticky hairs that coat the plant surfaces. The leaves are typically opposite, lanceolate to ovate in shape, and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. Its stems are square in cross-section, which is a common trait among members of the Lamiaceae family. The inflorescence consists of dense verticillasters that emerge from the leaf axils. Flowers are tubular and bilabiate, often displaying colors that range from pale blue to purple.
