What is Tylecodon cacalioides?

Tylecodon cacalioides is a succulent plant species in the family Crassulaceae, native to the Western Cape province of South Africa.

It has a compact, rounded growth habit, typically reaching a height of 10-30 cm, with a stout stem and fleshy leaves that are narrowly lanceolate to ovate in shape, 2-6 cm long and 1-3 cm wide. The leaves are oppositely arranged, glabrous, and have a cuneate base and a serrate margin. The inflorescence is a terminal raceme, bearing white to pale yellow flowers that are 1-2 cm in diameter, with five petals that are free and notched at the apex, and numerous stamens.

The fruits are three-angled capsules, containing numerous seeds.
