What's the taxonomical classification of Trichosanthes pilosa?

Trichosanthes pilosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and belongs to the order Cucurbitaleales. As a member of the family Cucurbitaceae, it is placed within the genus Trichosanthes, specifically identifying as the species pilosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichosanthes pilosa has a prostrate or climbing herbaceous habit characterized by slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The leaves typically feature three distinct leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered in fine pubescence. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often presenting in clusters or solitary arrangements. The plant produces elongated, fleshy fruits that are typically cylindrical and covered with soft spines or tubercles. These morphological features, particularly the pilose hairs on the stems and leaves, are diagnostic for identifying the species within its genus.
