Rauvolfia verticillata

What's the taxonomical classification of Rauvolfia verticillata?

Rauvolfia verticillata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its order, Gentianaales, the plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Its taxonomic journey concludes with its placement in the genus Rauvolfia, specifically identified by the species name verticillata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rauvolfia verticillata has an erect, woody shrub morphology characterized by opposite, whorled leaves and small, tubular white flowers. Small, fleshy berries develop from the flowers, transitioning in color as they reach maturity. The overall growth habit is compact, making it a distinctive component of understory vegetation.

What are the pharmacological activities of Rauvolfia verticillata?

This plant has antihypertensive, sedative, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities driven by its unique bioactive alkaloids. The presence of reserpine-like compounds allows the plant to effectively lower blood pressure by reducing sympathetic nervous system activity. Additionally, its sedative properties can assist in managing anxiety and promoting sleep regulation. Various studies also indicate that the plant exhibits significant inhibitory effects against certain bacterial and fungal pathogens. These diverse medicinal qualities make it a subject of interest for further pharmacological development.

According to a study published by "Yao xue xue bao = Acta pharmaceutica Sinica", the research on Rauvolfia verticillata focused on identifying its chemical constituents and pharmacological activity. Through chromatography on silica gel, Sephadex LH-20, and ODS open columns, the study isolated 3 indole alkaloids and 1 acridone alkaloid from the chloroform layer extract. These identified compounds include ajmalicine B (1), sandwicine (2), raunescine (3), and 7-hydroxynoracronycine (4). The acridone alkaloid (4) represents a new type of compound isolated from the Rauvolfia genus for the 1st time. Biological activity research was conducted on compound (4) to explore pharmacological activities in addition to its antihypertensive activity.
