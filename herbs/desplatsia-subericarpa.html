Desplatsia subericarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Desplatsia subericarpa?

Desplatsia subericarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Malvales. This plant is a member of the family Malvaceae, falling under the genus Desplatsia, and is specifically identified by its species name, subericarpa.

What are the therapeutic actions of Desplatsia subericarpa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach aches, fever, and various types of inflammation. The bark and leaves are often prepared as decoctions or infusions to soothe digestive distress. Medicinal applications also include topical uses for managing skin irritations and wounds. Local traditional knowledge suggests its efficacy in reducing body temperature during febrile illnesses. These therapeutic properties make it a significant resource in regional herbal medicine.

What are the herbal preparations of Desplatsia subericarpa?

Desplatsia subericarpa is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using its bark and leaves to treat various ailments. The bark is often boiled in water to extract active compounds for medicinal use. Leaves can also be steeped to create a tea for internal consumption. In some traditional practices, these preparations are applied topically to soothe skin irritations. These herbal methods allow for the efficient extraction of the plant's bioactive constituents.

Consequently, the absence of established safety data makes any exposure potentially hazardous to human health.
