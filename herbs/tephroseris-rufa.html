Tephroseris rufa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tephroseris rufa has a perennial, woody-based herb characterized by linear to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and often have dentate margins. The foliage is arranged alternately along the stems, which are frequently covered in fine, soft pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of numerous yellow discoid flower heads organized into terminal clusters or corymbose panicles. Each flower head is supported by an involucre of overlapping bracts that exhibit a distinct texture. The plant's overall structure is compact, often forming low-growing clumps suited for its specific habitat.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tephroseris rufa?

Tephroseris rufa has diuretic, hemostatic, astringent, and emmenagogue properties that contribute to its diverse medicinal applications. The plant acts as a diuretic to help increase urine production and assist in the elimination of excess fluids from the body. Its astringent qualities allow it to contract tissues, which works alongside its hemostatic effects to help control or stop bleeding. Additionally, the plant serves as an emmenagogue to stimulate or increase menstrual flow. These combined therapeutic actions make it a significant subject in traditional herbal medicine.

    What are the herbal preparations of Tephroseris rufa?

    Tephroseris rufa is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste for various medicinal applications. The leaves and stems are commonly boiled in water to create a liquid extract used for internal consumption. In some traditional practices, the plant material is crushed into a fine paste to be applied directly to the skin.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Tephroseris rufa can cause contact dermatitis, allergic skin reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various phytochemicals that may irritate the skin upon direct contact, leading to redness, itching, or rashes. Individuals with heightened sensitivities might experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions when exposed to its foliage or sap. Ingestion of the plant is generally discouraged because it can lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. While specific toxicity levels are not extensively documented for humans, caution is advised to avoid any accidental exposure.
