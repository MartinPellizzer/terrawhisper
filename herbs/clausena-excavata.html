Clausena excavata

What's the taxonomical classification of Clausena excavata?

Clausena excavata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Sapindaales. It is a member of the family Rutaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Clausena, ultimately reaching its taxonomic designation at the species level as Clausena excavata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Clausena excavata contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including diterpenoids, triterpenoids, flavonoids, and various polyphenolic compounds. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated in the plant's leaves and roots, where they serve as defense mechanisms. Research indicates that the specific diterpene derivatives found in the species exhibit significant anti-inflammatory and cytotoxic properties. Furthermore, the presence of certain alkaloids contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating various ailments through their biological activity. These complex molecular structures provide a scientific basis for its ethnobotanical applications in treating skin infections and metabolic issues.

What are the therapeutic actions of Clausena excavata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach pains, diarrhea, and various types of inflammatory conditions. The leaves and roots are often prepared into decoctions or poultices to address these ailments. Specifically, it is frequently employed in traditional medicine to soothe gastrointestinal distress and promote healing of the digestive tract. Some practitioners also utilize its components to manage wounds and topical skin irritations. These medicinal applications highlight the plant's diverse role in managing both internal and external health issues.
