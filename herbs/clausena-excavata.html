Clausena excavata

What's the taxonomical classification of Clausena excavata?

Clausena excavata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Sapindaales. It is a member of the family Rutaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Clausena, ultimately reaching its taxonomic designation at the species level as Clausena excavata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clausena excavata has small, opposite, elliptic to obovate leaves that feature distinctly concave or excavated surfaces on their upper side. The leaf margins are typically entire and the texture can vary from slightly fleshy to thin. The plant exhibits a woody structure characteristic of small shrubs or subshrubs within its genus. Its flowers are minute and arranged in inconspicuous clusters, while the fruit develops into small, fleshy drupes. This specific morphological trait of concave leaf blades serves as a primary diagnostic feature for distinguishing the species.

What are the pharmacological activities of Clausena excavata?

This plant has anti-HBV, anti-infective, anti-malarial, anti-proliferative, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities. These diverse medicinal properties make Clauseuna excavata a significant subject in ethnopharmacological research. Its anti-infective and antimicrobial actions allow it to combat various pathogenic organisms effectively. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates specific efficacy against the hepatitis B virus and malaria parasites. Recent studies also highlight its anti-proliferative potential, which suggests its utility in inhibiting the growth of certain cancer cells.

As discussed in a study, four natural pyranocoumarin derivatives, clausenidin (1), nordentatin (2), clausarin (3), and xanthoxyletin (4), were isolated from Clausena excavata. Compounds 1 and 2 suppressed hepatitis B virus surface antigen in HepA2 cells, while 1, 2, and 3 showed cytotoxic activity against 4 human cancer cell lines including A549, MCF7, KB, and KB-VIN. Among 17 designed pyranocoumarin analogues (5-21), compounds 5 and 10 were most potent against hepatitis B virus with EC50 values of 1.14 and 1.34 microM, respectively. Furthermore, compounds 1, 5, and 6 demonstrated significant activity against the 1 multi-drug resistant cell line KB-VIN without activity against the KB cell line.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds carbazole alkaloids, clausine E, clausine H, clausine L, and dentatin. These specific secondary metabolites are primarily responsible for the biological activities observed in the species. Research suggests that the carbazole alkaloids play a significant role in the plant's chemical defense mechanisms. The clausine derivatives, specifically E, H, and L, are structurally unique components found within the plant's chemical profile. Furthermore, the presence of dentatin adds to the complex diversity of the plant's therapeutic potential.

As discussed in a study, 5 new carbazole alkaloids named clausines B, E, H, I and K were isolated from the stem bark of Clausena excavata. Along with these new substances, 22 known compounds were also identified from the plant. These specific compounds demonstrated significant inhibition of rabbit platelet aggregation and caused vasoconstriction. The crude methanol extract, partitioned layers and chromatographic fractions contained both promotive and inhibitive constituents simultaneously. Such variations in dose and content may explain the different therapeutic effects observed in Chinese medicine.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating malaria caused by Plasmodium falciparum, various bacterial infections, the hepatitis B virus, and certain types of cancer. Researchers have identified bioactive compounds within the plant that exhibit potent antimicrobial and antiviral properties. These substances are particularly noted for their ability to inhibit the growth of specific pathogens in laboratory settings. Studies also suggest that the plant contains phytochemicals that may interfere with the progression of malignant cells. Such medicinal applications highlight its significant potential in pharmacological research for managing diverse diseases.

As discussed in a study, a new carbazole alkaloid named sansoakamine (8) and 11 known compounds were isolated from the stems of Clausena excavata. All structures from the plant were elucidated using spectroscopic methods. Among the isolated substances, compound 7 exhibited moderate anti-malarial activity against Plasmodium falciparum. This specific activity was measured with a MIC value of 6.74 microg/ml.
