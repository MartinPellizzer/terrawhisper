The extract functioned by reducing the number of writhes in acetic acid-induced models and reducing total fecal output in castor oil-induced models compared to the negative control. In combination treatments, the higher dose of MCT significantly (p < 0.05) synergized the activity of positive controls in both models. These findings suggest that MCT may be beneficial for the management of pain and diarrhea.
