Thuja koraiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Thuja koraiensis?

Thuja koraienensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales. As a member of the family Cupressaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Thuja, specifically identifying as the species koraiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thuja koraienensis has scale-like, overlapping leaves that are arranged in flattened, fan-like sprays and possess a distinct yellowish-green color. The foliage is composed of small, decussate scales that tightly hug the slender, woody twigs. These plants typically exhibit a columnar or conical growth habit with a dense, evergreen canopy. The bark is thin and reddish-brown, eventually peeling away in long, narrow strips as the tree matures. Small, woody cones emerge from the branches to complete the structural complexity of the species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It typically thrives in subalpine zones where rocky terrain and steep slopes are prevalent. The species is often found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level. Within these rugged habitats, it occupies specific ecological niches characterized by cool temperatures and significant seasonal moisture.
