Stereospermum chelonoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Stereospermum chelonoides?

Stereospernum chelonoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a representative of the family Bignoniaceae, falling under the genus Stereospernum, and is specifically identified by its species name, chelonoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stereospermum chelonoides has a woody habit characterized by spreading branches, pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of leaflets, and large, campanulate, violet-to-purple flowers that emerge from axillary clusters.

The leaflets are typically elliptic or lanceolate in shape and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. Its floral structure features a long, curved corolla tube that serves to accommodate specific pollinators. The fruit develops into a long, woody, cylindrical capsule that eventually dehisces to release numerous winged seeds.
