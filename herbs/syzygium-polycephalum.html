What is Syzygium polycephalum?

Syzygium polycephalum is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is an evergreen shrub or small tree that grows up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and a smooth, grey bark. The leaves are oppositely arranged, elliptical to lanceolate in shape, and measure 2-6 centimeters long by 0.8-2 centimeters wide, with a prominent midrib and a hairy underside. The inflorescences are terminal, 2-5 centimeters long, and produce small, white, tubular flowers with 4-5 stamens and a 4-lobed ovary.

The fruits are small, globose drupes, 0.5-1 centimeter in diameter, with a single seed and a leathery, reddish-brown exocarp.
