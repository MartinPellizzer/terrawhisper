What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitis chunganensis has woody, climbing vines characterized by trifoliate or pentafoliate leaves with serrated margins and specialized tendrils for support. The leaf blades are typically ovate or cordate in shape, featuring distinct venation patterns that help distinguish it from related species. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into clusters of berries. These fruits transition in color as they mature, providing a key morphological marker for identification. The stems exhibit a bark texture that becomes increasingly rugged as the plant ages.
