What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Synotis nagensium has perennial herbaceous roots, an upright growth habit, and lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is typically characterized by a distinct venation pattern and a texture that varies depending on environmental moisture. Its inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers arranged in clusters that emerge from the upper leaf axils. The reproductive structures include slender filaments and anthers that facilitate pollination within its specific montane habitat.
