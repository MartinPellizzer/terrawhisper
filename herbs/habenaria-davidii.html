Habenaria davidii

What's the taxonomical classification of Habenaria davidii?

Habenaria davidii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the biological lineage of the order Asparagales. It is further categorized under the family Orchidaceae, placing it among the diverse group of orchids. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Habeanra and is specifically distinguished as the species davidii.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various types of coughs, bronchitis, and respiratory inflammation. It is traditionally utilized in herbal medicine to soothe irritated throat passages and ease breathing difficulties. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may help reduce swelling in the lungs and airways. Practitioners often incorporate it into remedies aimed at managing congestion and phlegm buildup. These therapeutic applications focus primarily on the management of symptoms associated with the lower respiratory tract.

What are the herbal preparations of Habenaria davidii?

Habenaria davidii is prepared primarily as a decoction or a concentrated tincture derived from its dried tubers. The medicinal parts are typically harvested during the dormant season to ensure maximum concentration of active compounds. Once the tubers are cleaned and dried, they can be boiled in water to create a traditional herbal tea. Alternatively, soaking the plant material in high-proof alcohol produces a tincture for more long-term storage and precise dosing. These preparations are often utilized in traditional practices to address specific physiological needs.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Habenaria davidii can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions if its fluids come into direct contact with sensitive tissues. While specific toxicity levels in humans are not extensively documented, many orchid species contain alkaloids that may trigger dermatological discomfort. Contact dermatitis often manifests as redness, itching, or a mild rash on the affected area. Individuals with known sensitivities to botanical extracts should exercise caution when handling this plant. Proper skin protection is recommended to avoid any potential adverse physiological responses.
