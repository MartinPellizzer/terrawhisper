Ligusticum pteridophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligusticum pteridophyllum?

Ligusticum pteridophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Apiales and is a member of the family Apiaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Ligusticum, with the specific epithet pteridophyllum identifying the individual species.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligusticum pteridophyllum has compound leaves that are bipinnate to tripinnate in structure, featuring finely divided segments that resemble the foliage of ferns. The leaf blades consist of multiple small, lanceolate leaflets arranged along branching axes to create a delicate, lace-like appearance. These leaves are typically dark green in color and possess a somewhat herbaceous texture. The plant also produces umbel-shaped inflorescences that hold clusters of small flowers atop sturdy, hollow stems.
