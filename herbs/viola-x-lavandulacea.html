Lavender Violet (Viola × Lavandulacea)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Viola × lavandulacea.

Lavender violet, scientifically known as Viola × lavandulacea, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and widely cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal value. This medicinal herb is valued for its adaptogenic properties, helping the body resist stress and maintain balance. Traditionally used in European folk medicine, it was employed to treat respiratory ailments, digestive issues, and skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities. In modern wellness practices, lavender violet is incorporated into herbal teas and supplements to support immune function and promote relaxation. Its unique lavender-like fragrance and subtle floral flavor make it a sought-after ingredient in both culinary and therapeutic applications, with historical records tracing its use back to the 16th century.
