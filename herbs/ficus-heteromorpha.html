Ficus heteromorpha

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus heteromorpha?

Ficus heteromorpha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Rosales, it is placed in the family Moraceae and is further identified by its genus, Ficus. Ultimately, the specific biological identity of this plant is defined by its species name, heteromorpha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus heteromorpha has heterophyllous leaves that transition from entire, elliptical shapes in juvenile stages to deeply lobed or pinnatifid structures in mature specimens. The leaf blades vary significantly in size and margin configuration depending on the developmental age of the plant. These leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the woody stems. The venation is prominent, often featuring distinct secondary veins that extend toward the leaf edges. This morphological variation in foliage is a key identifying characteristic of the species.
