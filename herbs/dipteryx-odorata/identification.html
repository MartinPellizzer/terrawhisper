Botanical Identification of Tonka Bean Tree (Dipteryx odorata)

To identify Tonka Bean Tree (Dipteryx odorata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Tonka Bean Tree, Dipteryx odorata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Dipteryx, and species Dipteryx odorata. These classifications reflect its position within the plant kingdom and its relationship to other leguminous plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of the Tonka Bean Tree is Dipteryx odorata, a species within the Fabaceae family. It was first described by Carl von Linne in 1782. The tree is also known by several synonyms, including Dipteryx alba and Dipteryx parviflora, which reflect historical taxonomic variations. Common names such as tonka bean, coumarou, and tona are widely used in different regions.

Morphological Characteristics

The Tonka Bean Tree is a medium to large evergreen tree with a straight trunk and dense crown. It has a deep taproot system, providing stability. The stem is smooth and grayish-brown, with a fibrous outer layer. Leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic with a glossy dark green upper surface. The inflorescence consists of axillary clusters of fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule containing several bean-shaped seeds, which are aromatic and used in herbal medicine.

Diagnostic Identification Features

The Tonka Bean Tree can be distinguished by its fragrant, swollen pods containing dark brown seeds, its aromatic bark with a vanilla-like scent, and its alternate, compound leaves. It is often confused with other Dipteryx species, but its unique seed pods and strong odor set it apart.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

The Tonka Bean Tree is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Bolivia, and Peru. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained, loamy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 300 and 1,200 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

The Tonka Bean Tree blooms during the dry season, typically from December to March. Seed formation occurs 6 to 8 months after flowering, usually between June and August. Harvesting of the seeds, known as tonka beans, is done when the pods are fully mature and dry, which is around September to November.

Phylogenetic Relationships

The Tonka Bean Tree, Dipteryx odorata, is closely related to species within the same genus, Dipteryx, and shares key family traits with the Fabaceae family. It is most closely related to Dipteryx micrantha and Dipteryx granulosperma, which also exhibit similar morphological and chemical characteristics. These relationships help in understanding its ecological and medicinal significance.
