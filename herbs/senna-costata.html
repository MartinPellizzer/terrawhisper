Senna costata

Senna costata, also known as the Cape senna or fan senna, is a plant species belonging to the pea family, characterized by its distinctive fan-shaped leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant has been used for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anthraquinone glycosides, which have laxative and purgative effects.

Senna costata is believed to provide several health benefits, including the treatment of constipation, weight loss, and reduction of blood sugar levels, although its effectiveness for these purposes requires further research.

The plant can be prepared as a decoction, infusion, or tincture, and is sometimes used in herbal remedies, but caution is advised due to potential side effects and interactions with other medications.
