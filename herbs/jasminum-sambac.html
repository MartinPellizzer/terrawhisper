Common Jasmine

Jasminum sambac

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is a fragrant flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia, known for its sweetly scented white flowers and its use in traditional medicine for centuries.

Taxonomy

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is a species belonging to the family Oleaceae, within the class Eudicots, order Oleales, and is classified under the genus Jasminum in the kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is a shrub characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers, opposite simple leaves with serrated margins, and a branched, woody stem that typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters.

Distribution

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly India, and is also found in parts of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Bangladesh, Nepal, and Sri Lanka.

Traditional Uses

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming effects, to treat respiratory conditions, and as a natural remedy for skin ailments and digestive issues.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Unani, and Traditional Chinese Medicine, Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) has been utilized for its therapeutic properties to treat various ailments including respiratory disorders, skin conditions, and emotional imbalances.

Phytochemistry

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) contains bioactive compounds such as essential oils, flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by its ability to exert sedative, antispasmodic, and mild antidepressant effects, primarily through its influence on the central nervous system and its capacity to promote relaxation and emotional balance.

Medicinal Uses

Common Jasmine is used to treat insomnia, anxiety, digestive issues, skin infections, respiratory ailments, and menstrual disorders.

Preparations

Common Jasmine is used to prepare essential oils, tinctures, and herbal infusions that are traditionally employed in herbal medicine for their calming, antispasmodic, and mild sedative properties.

Safety

When used in excess or improperly, Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, and skin irritation, particularly in individuals with sensitivities to related plants.

Evidence

Clinical evidence for Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) in herbal medicine primarily focuses on its potential antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, with some studies suggesting it may support skin health and respiratory function, though more rigorous research is needed to confirm these effects.

Cultivation

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is typically cultivated in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure, requiring regular watering and pruning to maintain its fragrant blooms.

Processing

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is typically processed by distilling its fragrant flowers to produce essential oil, which is then used in herbal medicine for its calming, antispasmodic, and antimicrobial properties.

Identification

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is a flowering plant native to southern Asia, characterized by its fragrant white flowers, opposite simple leaves, and a woody stem with smooth, pale green bark.

Sustainability

The sustainability of Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is a critical concern due to its high demand in the fragrance and herbal medicine industries, which has led to overharvesting and habitat degradation, necessitating conservation efforts and sustainable cultivation practices to preserve its ecological role and medicinal value.

Related

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is closely related to other jasmine species within the genus Jasminum, including Jasminum officinale, Jasminum grandiflorum, and Jasminum polyanthum, which share similar botanical characteristics and medicinal uses.
