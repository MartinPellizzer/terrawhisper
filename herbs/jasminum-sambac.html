Jasminum sambac

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum sambac?

Jasminum sambac belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Lamiales. This aromatic plant is a member of the family Oleaceae, specifically falling under the genus Jasminum, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name sambac.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Jasminum sambac contains diverse bioactive compounds including indole alkaloids, terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids such as gallic acid and caffeic acid. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that its essential oils are rich in benzyl acetate and linalool, which contribute to its sedative and calming effects. Some studies also suggest that specific alkaloids within the plant may possess antimicrobial and analgesic activities. These multifaceted medicinal qualities make the species a subject of interest in both traditional medicine and modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Jasminum sambac?

Jasminum sambac has potent sedative, anxiolytic, and antidepressant properties due to its high concentration of linalool and benzyl acetate. These chemical constituents interact with the central nervous system to reduce cortisol levels and promote relaxation. When used in aromatherapy, the scent helps alleviate symptoms of anxiety and emotional distress by soothing the limbic system. Additionally, the plant exhibits mild antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects that can support skin health when applied topically. Its calming influence also makes it a popular natural remedy for improving sleep quality and managing stress.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, respiratory congestion, digestive issues, and inflammatory conditions. Its essential oils and floral extracts possess antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial and fungal skin diseases. In traditional medicine, infusions are often used to soothe coughs and alleviate symptoms of asthma or bronchitis. The plant also contains compounds believed to reduce swelling and ease muscle aches through topical application. Additionally, its calming scent is sometimes employed to manage stress-related headaches and anxiety.
