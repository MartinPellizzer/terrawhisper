Common Jasmine

Jasminum sambac

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is a fragrant flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia, known for its sweetly scented white flowers and its use in traditional medicine for centuries.

Taxonomy

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is a species belonging to the family Oleaceae, within the class Eudicots, order Oleales, and is classified under the genus Jasminum in the kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is a shrub characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers, opposite simple leaves with serrated margins, and a branched, woody stem that typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters.

Distribution

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly India, and is also found in parts of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Bangladesh, Nepal, and Sri Lanka.

Traditional Uses

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming effects, to treat respiratory conditions, and as a natural remedy for skin ailments and digestive issues.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Unani, and Traditional Chinese Medicine, Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) has been utilized for its therapeutic properties to treat various ailments including respiratory disorders, skin conditions, and emotional imbalances.

Phytochemistry

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) contains bioactive compounds such as essential oils, flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by its ability to exert sedative, antispasmodic, and mild antidepressant effects, primarily through its influence on the central nervous system and its capacity to promote relaxation and emotional balance.

Botanical Identification

Jasminum sambac, commonly known as Common Jasmine, belongs to the Oleaceae family. It is a perennial shrub native to South and Southeast Asia. The plant features fragrant white flowers, opposite leaves, and a woody stem. Synonyms include Arabian Jasmine and Night-Blooming Jasmine, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Active Compounds

Common Jasmine contains essential oil, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to provide its therapeutic effects, with essential oil contributing to its aromatic and antimicrobial properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids offer antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits, enhancing the herb's overall medicinal value.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive issues due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Infusion: Used for calming effects and to treat mild anxiety or insomnia due to its soothing properties.

Essential oil: Used for skin conditions and stress relief due to its aromatic and antimicrobial properties.

Safety Profile

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause skin irritation in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for prolonged or high-dose applications.
