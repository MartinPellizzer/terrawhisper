Side Effects of jasminum sambac

Jasminum sambac leads to allergic reactions in some individuals, manifesting as symptoms such as sneezing, runny nose, and itchy eyes.

Prolonged exposure to the herb can cause skin irritation, resulting in redness, rashes, and a burning sensation on the skin. The volatile compounds in jasmine may also trigger respiratory issues, including nasal congestion, throat irritation, and even coughing. In more severe cases, it can lead to eye irritation, causing redness, watering, and a gritty feeling.

Additionally, some people may experience headaches or swelling as a result of an overactive immune response to the herb. While many people tolerate jasmine safely, those with sensitive skin or allergies should exercise caution when using or handling the plant. It is advisable to perform a patch test before applying jasmine-based products and to avoid direct contact with the plant if symptoms arise.

Overall, while Jasminum sambac is widely appreciated for its fragrance, it is important to be aware of its potential health side effects to ensure safe usage.
