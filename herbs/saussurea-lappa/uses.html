Gao Ben (Saussurea lappa) Uses

Gao Ben is used to treat respiratory infections, bronchitis, allergic rhinitis, asthma, and chronic cough. It also helps with tuberculosis, sinusitis, and tonsillitis. Its anti-inflammatory properties make it effective for gastrointestinal inflammation.

Gao Ben (Saussurea lappa) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Gao Ben is used to treat respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce symptoms like coughing and congestion by supporting lung function and clearing phlegm. Often combined with other herbs, it is effective in addressing conditions such as bronchitis and pneumonia. Its warming nature also aids in dispelling cold pathogens in the respiratory system.

2. Bronchitis

Gao Ben is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat bronchitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps clear mucus and reduce airway inflammation, easing breathing difficulties. Often combined with other herbs, it supports respiratory health in chronic bronchial conditions. Its efficacy is supported by historical use and modern pharmacological studies.

3. Allergic rhinitis

Gao Ben is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose by regulating immune responses and improving sinus drainage. Often combined with other herbs, it supports long-term relief and reduces the frequency of allergic symptoms.

4. Asthma

Gao Ben is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat asthma by promoting lung function and reducing phlegm. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effects on respiratory health. Its properties help to relieve bronchial spasms and improve airflow in asthma patients.

5. Chronic cough

Gao Ben is used to treat chronic cough by clearing dampness and transforming phlegm. It helps reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract and soothe persistent coughing. This herb is often combined with others to enhance its effectiveness in long-term respiratory conditions. It is particularly beneficial for coughs caused by internal dampness and cold.

6. Tuberculosis

Gao Ben is used in traditional Chinese medicine to support lung health and treat tuberculosis. It helps reduce inflammation and clear phlegm, aiding in respiratory function. Often combined with other herbs, it is prescribed to alleviate symptoms and enhance recovery in tuberculosis patients. Its warming properties help to dispel cold and dampness associated with the condition.

7. Sinusitis

Gao Ben is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and inflammation, alleviating symptoms such as headaches and nasal discharge. Often combined with other herbs, it supports respiratory health and enhances immune response in sinus infections.

8. Lung cancer

Gao Ben is used in traditional Chinese medicine to support lung health and may help manage symptoms associated with lung cancer by enhancing immune function and reducing inflammation. It is often combined with other herbs to improve respiratory function and overall vitality in patients undergoing treatment.

9. Tonsillitis

Gao Ben is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat tonsillitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling and alleviate throat pain associated with infections. Often combined with other herbs, it supports the body's immune response and promotes healing in the throat.

10. Gastrointestinal inflammation

Gao Ben is used to treat gastrointestinal inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps reduce swelling and pain in the stomach and intestines. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in treating conditions like gastritis and colitis. This application is supported by traditional Chinese medicine practices.
