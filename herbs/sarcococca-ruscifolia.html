Sarcococca ruscifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Sarcococca ruscifolia?

Sarcococca ruscifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specific evolutionary lineage that places it under the order Buxales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Buxaceae. Finally, its precise biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Sarcococca, with the specific epithet rusciifolia completing its scientific name.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sarcococca ruscifolia has evergreen, opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long and possess a leathery texture. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy, globose drupes that turn a dark color upon maturity. The foliage often displays a glossy surface which helps the plant retain moisture in its native habitat.

As discussed in a study, Sarcococca ruscifolia is part of a TCM powder used at a 2% supplementation level. This powder was tested on 100 10-day-old male Xianghuang chickens divided into groups with 5 replicate cages of 10 birds each. The supplementation increased T-SOD and CAT activities in the jejunal mucosa with a p-value < 0.05. It also resulted in a higher villus height to crypt depth ratio in the jejunum and ileum where p < 0.05. Furthermore, the powder led to the downregulation of TNF-α gene expression in the jejunal mucosa and spleen with p < 0.05. These effects demonstrate enhanced antioxidant and anti-inflammatory functions without affecting growth performance metrics like ADFI, ADG, or F:G where p > 0.05.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds Sarcorusones A, B, C, and D. These unique bioactive molecules are secondary metabolites responsible for many of the species' biological activities. Researchers study these compounds to understand their potential role in pharmacological applications. The chemical structure of these specific sarcorusones allows them to interact with various cellular targets. Further investigation into these substances may reveal new therapeutic possibilities for modern medicine.

As discussed in a study, Sarcococca ruscifolia contains four new androstane C19-steroid derivatives named Sarcorusones A-D (1-4) along with 5 known compounds. These new structures share common 14-hydroxyl and 17-ketone functional groups, while compounds 2-4 feature a seneciamide group at the C-3 position. The inhibitory activities of all isolates were evaluated against melanoma cell B16F10 and lung cancer cell H1299. Compounds 2, 3, 5, and 6 exhibited moderate cytotoxic activities against these B16F10 and H1299 cell lines. These specific activities were measured with IC50 values ranging from 2.7-8.0 μM.
