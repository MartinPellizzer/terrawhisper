Sarcococca ruscifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Sarcococca ruscifolia?

Sarcococca ruscifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specific evolutionary lineage that places it under the order Buxales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Buxaceae. Finally, its precise biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Sarcococca, with the specific epithet rusciifolia completing its scientific name.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sarcococca ruscifolia has evergreen, opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long and possess a leathery texture. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy, globose drupes that turn a dark color upon maturity. What are the pharmacological activities of Sarcococca ruscifolia?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties derived from its bioactive secondary metabolites. The presence of specific phenolic compounds allows the plant to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. These antioxidant mechanisms work in tandem with its ability to inhibit inflammatory mediators, making it a subject of interest for therapeutic research. Studies suggest that these combined effects can help mitigate tissue damage caused by chronic inflammation and oxidative injury. Consequently, the phytochemical profile of Sarcococca rusciifolia offers promising potential for developing new natural medicinal agents.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in veterinary science", a TCM powder containing Sarcococca ruscifolia Stapf was tested on 100 10-day-old male Xianghuang chickens. Supplementing the diet with 2% of the TCM powder increased total superoxide dismutase (T-SOD) and catalase (CAT) activities in the jejunal mucosa (p < 0.05). This powder also caused the gene expression of tumor necrosis factor alpha (TNF-α) to be downregulated in the jejunal mucosa and spleen (p < 0.05). Additionally, the ratio of the villus height to the crypt depth was higher in the jejunum and ileum of the TCM group compared to the control group (p < 0.05). These results suggest the powder enhances antioxidant and anti-inflammatory functions.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a unique group of bioactive triterpene glycosides known as Sarcorusones A-D. These specific medicinal compounds are structurally characterized by their complex steroid-like frameworks and sugar moieties. Research indicates that these molecules possess significant pharmacological potential, particularly regarding their biological activity in cellular studies. Beyond these primary compounds, the plant's chemical profile contributes to its traditional use in various medicinal applications. The isolation of these substances provides a foundation for developing new therapeutic agents derived from natural sources.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", Sarcococca ruscifolia contains four new androstane C19-steroid derivatives identified as Sarcorusones A-D (1-4). These four new structures are characterized by common 14-hydroxyl and 17-ketone functional groups, while compounds 2-4 also feature a seneciamide group at the C-3 position. Along with these new derivatives, five known compounds were also identified from the plant. The study evaluated the inhibitory activities of all isolates against melanoma cell B16F10 and lung cancer cell H1299. Specifically, compounds 2, 3, 5, and 6 exhibited moderate cytotoxic activities against the B16F10 and H1299 cell lines with IC50 values ranging from 2.7-8.0 μM.

These effects are observed in birds analyzed on D28 without affecting the average daily feed intake (ADFI), average daily gain (ADG), or feed-to-gain (F:G) ratio (p > 0.05).
