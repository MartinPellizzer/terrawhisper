What is Syzygium bleeseri?

Syzygium bleeseri is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the Western Ghats of India.

The plant is a small tree or shrub with a height range of 3-10 meters, characterized by a straight bole, a rounded crown, and smooth, grey bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate, measuring 4-12 centimeters in length and 1-3 centimeters in width, with a cuneate base and an acute apex. Syzygium bleeseri produces white, fragrant flowers in axillary cymes, with four-merous sepals and four-merous petals, and a calyx of four sepals.

The fruit is a drupe, ellipsoidal in shape, measuring 1.5-2 centimeters in length, and containing a single seed.
