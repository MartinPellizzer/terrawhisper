What's the taxonomical classification of Fritillaria crassicaulis?

Fritillaria crassicaulis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Liliaceae and is categorized within the genus Fritillaria, specifically identified by its species name, crassicaulis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fritillaria crassicaulis has a thick, fleshy stem that supports several whorls of lanceolate leaves and nodding, bell-shaped flowers with brownish-purple markings. Its bulb is large and tuberous, serving as a primary nutrient storage organ for the species. The flowers feature recurved tepals that create a distinct campanulate shape during the blooming period. These morphological traits are characteristic of its adaptation to specific montane environments.
