Terminalia citrina

What's the taxonomical classification of Terminalia citrina?

Terminalia citrina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Myrtales and the family Combretaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific placement within the genus Terminalia, specifically identified as the species citrina.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Terminalia citrina contains high concentrations of tannins, flavonoids, saponins, and various terpenoids that provide its primary therapeutic properties. These phytochemicals work together to exhibit significant antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory activities within biological systems. The presence of specific polyphenolic compounds allows the plant to combat oxidative stress by neutralizing free radicals. Additionally, the tannins found in the bark and fruit are often utilized for their astringent qualities to treat digestive issues. These diverse medicinal constituents make the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological research into natural healing agents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including digestive disorders, skin infections, respiratory issues, and inflammatory conditions. The fruit and bark are frequently employed to alleviate symptoms of diarrhea and dysentery due to their astringent properties. In traditional medicine, the leaves are often applied topically to manage wounds and fungal infections on the skin. Decoctions made from the plant parts are also utilized to soothe coughs and assist in managing fever. Its diverse chemical composition allows it to address a wide range of minor bacterial and parasitic infections.
