Terminalia citrina

What's the taxonomical classification of Terminalia citrina?

Terminalia citrina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Myrtales and the family Combretaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific placement within the genus Terminalia, specifically identified as the species citrina.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Terminalia citrina contains medicinal compounds found primarily in its bark, leaves, and fruit. The bark is often processed to create decoctions used for treating skin ailments and digestive issues. Its leaves are frequently utilized in traditional remedies to manage inflammation and fever. The fruit serves as a source of various bioactive substances that may assist in metabolic regulation. Together, these different parts provide a versatile range of therapeutic applications in ethnobotanical practices.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains flavonoids, tannins, phenolic compounds, saponins, terpenoids, and alkaloids that contribute to its diverse pharmacological activities.

The presence of phenolic compounds and flavonoids provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties to the species. Tannins within the plant are known for their astringent qualities and ability to aid in wound healing. Saponins and terpenoids further enhance its biological profile by offering potential antimicrobial and cytotoxic effects.

Additionally, the alkaloids present in Terminalia citrina are associated with various physiological responses and therapeutic potential.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Terminalia citrina targets enzymes such as alpha-glucosidase, alpha-amylase, and cyclooxygenase-2 to exert its hypoglycemic and anti-inflammatory effects. The bioactive polyphenols and tannins found in the plant act as competitive inhibitors of these specific proteins to regulate blood glucose levels. By suppressing cyclooxygenase-2, the plant's compounds reduce the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like prostaglandins. Additionally, the phytochemicals may interact with various antioxidant enzymes to mitigate oxidative stress within cellular environments. These protein interactions collectively contribute to the plant's documented pharmacological potential in treating metabolic and inflammatory disorders.

What are the therapeutic actions of Terminalia citrina?

Terminalia citrina has potent antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties that are primarily utilized to treat skin infections, digestive issues, and respiratory ailments. The plant contains bioactive compounds like tannins and flavonoids that help neutralize free radicals and reduce cellular damage. Its extracts are frequently applied topically to soothe wounds and clear bacterial or fungal dermatological conditions. Additionally, certain preparations are ingested to alleviate gastrointestinal distress by regulating gut flora. These diverse therapeutic actions make it a significant component in various traditional medicinal practices.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including digestive disorders, skin infections, respiratory issues, and inflammatory conditions. The fruit and bark are frequently employed to alleviate symptoms of diarrhea and dysentery due to their astringent properties. In traditional medicine, the leaves are often applied topically to manage wounds and fungal infections on the skin. Decoctions made from the plant parts are also utilized to soothe coughs and assist in managing fever. Its diverse chemical composition allows it to address a wide range of minor bacterial and parasitic infections.

What are the herbal preparations of Terminalia citrina?

Terminalia citrina is prepared as a decoction, infusion, or powder derived from its fruits, bark, and leaves for medicinal use. The fruit is often processed into a juice or syrup to utilize its high vitamin C content. Practitioners frequently boil the bark to create a concentrated liquid tea used for its astringent properties. Additionally, the dried leaves can be ground into a fine powder for topical applications or included in herbal capsules. These diverse preparation methods allow for the extraction of specific phytochemicals tailored to different therapeutic needs.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Terminalia citrina can cause gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain if consumed in improper quantities. Some individuals may also experience allergic skin reactions like redness or itching when coming into direct contact with the plant's sap. There is limited scientific data regarding its long-term toxicity in humans, so caution is necessary when using it for medicinal purposes. It is also advisable to avoid use during pregnancy or breastfeeding due to a lack of safety studies. Consulting a healthcare professional is recommended before incorporating any part of this plant into a health regimen.
