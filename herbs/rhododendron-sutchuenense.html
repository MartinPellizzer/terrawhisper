Rhododendron sutchuenense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron sutchuenense has leathery, elliptic to oblong leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and are characterized by a dark green upper surface and a paler underside. The plant grows as a woody evergreen shrub reaching heights of up to 3 meters in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of funnel-shaped flowers that typically display shades of pink, rose, or white. These blooms are supported by sturdy, woody stems that emerge from densely branched structures. The foliage remains durable throughout the year, providing a consistent textural element to the shrub's appearance.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan. It typically grows in the montane forests and scrublands found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters. The species prefers the cool, moist environments provided by the rugged terrain of the Hengduan Mountains. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific microclimates found along the eastern edge of the Tibetan Plateau. Localized populations are often concentrated in areas with acidic soils and dappled sunlight.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe toxicity through the ingestion of grayanotoxins, leading to nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. Victims may also experience neurological symptoms such as dizziness, confusion, and seizures. Cardiovascular complications are common and include arrhythmia and hypotension. In extreme cases of poisoning, the individual may suffer from life-threatening respiratory depression.
