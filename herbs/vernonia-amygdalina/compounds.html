Bitterleaf (Vernonia amygdalina) Compounds

The primary compounds in Bitterleaf include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its antimicrobial activity, while alkaloids may have medicinal effects. Saponins and glycosides support its traditional use in treating ailments. Coumarin, terpenoids, essential oils, and anthocyanins also contribute to its therapeutic potential.

Bitterleaf (Vernonia amygdalina) Monograph

Flavonoids in Bitterleaf (Vernonia amygdalina) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and may support immune function.
