Schefflera pauciflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Schefflera pauciflora?

Schefflerera pauciflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Apiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Araliaceae, specifically within the genus Schefflera, and is identified by its unique species designation, pauciflora.

    Schefflera pauciflora is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the specific therapeutic requirement. Alternatively, finely crushed plant material can be applied directly to the skin in the form of a paste to treat localized ailments. These diverse methods allow practitioners to utilize different parts of the plant for varied medicinal purposes. Such preparations are fundamental to the traditional application of this botanical species.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Schefflera pauciflora can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested by humans or pets. The plant contains compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis or allergic reactions upon skin contact. If consumed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain. Pets such as cats and dogs are particularly sensitive to its chemical makeup and may suffer more severe symptoms. It is important to handle the foliage with care to avoid these adverse effects.
