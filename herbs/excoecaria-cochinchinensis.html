What's the taxonomical classification of Excoecaria cochinchinensis?

Excoecaria cochinchiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Malpighiales. It is further categorized into the family Euphorbiaceae, which defines its botanical characteristics. Within this family, the plant is placed in the genus Excoecaria, and its specific biological identity is established by its species name, cochinchiensis.

What traditional systems uses this plant?

This plant, known commonly as cochineal or lac plant, has been traditionally used throughout Southeast Asia for centuries to produce a deep red dye from the secretions of scale insects found on its branches.

Historically, indigenous communities harvested these insects to color textiles, ritual garments, and various ceremonial objects. The bark and twigs of the plant were also utilized in regional folk medicine to treat skin ailments and inflammatory conditions. Beyond dyeing, the plant played a significant role in early trade networks as a valuable commodity for pigment production.

This long-standing relationship between the plant and human industry highlights its central importance to historical material culture.

What are the pharmacological activities of Excoecaria cochinchinensis?

This plant has antibabesial activity, which means it possesses the medicinal capability to combat Babesia parasites. These protozoan organisms are responsible for causing babesiosis, a serious disease that infects red blood cells. By utilizing the bioactive compounds found within Excoecaria cochinchiensis, researchers aim to target these specific pathogens. Understanding this pharmacological mechanism is essential for developing potential treatments for blood-borne parasitic infections. Ongoing studies continue to explore how the plant's chemical profile can effectively inhibit the lifecycle of such parasites.

According to a study published by "Bioscience, biotechnology, and biochemistry", the plant Excoecaria cochinchiensis yields the compound (+)-epi lolide (4). This compound was investigated for its antibabesial activities through in vitro examination. The study determined that compound 4 has an IC50 value of 10.0 microg/ml. This value is compared against diminazen aceturate, which demonstrated an IC50 value of 0.6 microg/ml.
