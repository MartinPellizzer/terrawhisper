The therapeutic actions of Morus alba subvar. romana include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antidiabetic effects, making it a potential treatment for various health conditions.

Possible Side Effects of Morus alba subvar. romana

Morus alba subvar. romana can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal problems.

Consuming large amounts of its extracts can lead to liver damage and kidney impairment due to its high concentration of anthraquinones.

Additionally, using its leaves or bark for medicinal purposes without proper preparation and dosage can cause stomach ulcers, diarrhea, and vomiting in some individuals.

In extreme cases, improper use can also lead to anaphylaxis, a severe and potentially life-threatening allergic reaction.
