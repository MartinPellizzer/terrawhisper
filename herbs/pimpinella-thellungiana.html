Pimpinella thellungiana

Pimpineilla thellungiana has a perennial growth habit characterized by compound leaves with multiple leaflets and small, white, umbel-shaped flower clusters. The leaves are typically dissected and possess thin, delicate textures that emerge from a basal rosette. Its stems are slender and erect, supporting the delicate inflorescences throughout the flowering season. The plant produces tiny seeds that are often contained within small schizocarps typical of the Apiaceae family. These morphological features help distinguish it from other closely related species within its genus.

Because the specific toxicological profile of this plant is not extensively documented in human clinical trials, users may experience unpredictable hypersensitivity. Caution is advised for pregnant or breastfeeding individuals since the safety standards for this species are not well established.
