What's the taxonomical classification of Diplazium chinense?

Diplazium chinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Taxonomically, this plant is a member of the family Aspleniaceae and is placed within the genus Diplazium, with its specific identification being the species chinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diplazium chinense has large, pinnate fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length, characterized by oblong or lanceolate leaflets with entire or slightly serrated margins. The stipes are long, slender, and often possess a brownish or dark green coloration with a smooth texture. Each pinna is arranged oppositely or sub-oppositely along the central rachis, creating a lush, feathery appearance. Small, round sori are located on the underside of the pinnules, typically arranged in rows near the margins. These reproductive structures are protected by a thin, kidney-shaped indusium that is characteristic of the genus.
