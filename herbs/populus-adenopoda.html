Populus adenopoda

What's the taxonomical classification of Populus adenopoda?

Populus adenopoda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Malpighiales and the family Salicaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is completed through its placement in the genus Populus, with the unique species designation of adenopoda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Populus adenopoda has simple, alternate leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins and acute apices. The leaf blades feature prominent pinnate venation and a distinctively textured surface. Its stems are woody and characterized by a grayish bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that typically appear in clusters. These reproductive structures eventually develop into small, wind-dispersed seeds contained within capsules.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The species prefers moist, temperate environments where it can grow alongside other high-elevation flora.
