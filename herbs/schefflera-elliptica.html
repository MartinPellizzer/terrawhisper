    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Schefflera elliptica can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested by humans or pets. The plant contains calcium oxalate crystals that trigger immediate burning or stinging sensations upon contact with the mouth or skin. If a person or animal consumes the leaves, they may experience swelling of the throat, vomiting, or abdominal pain. These crystals act like tiny needles that irritate soft tissues upon contact. Owners of cats and dogs should keep this plant out of reach to prevent accidental poisoning.
