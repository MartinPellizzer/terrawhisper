Schefflera elliptica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schefflerera elliptica has elliptical to oblong leaves that are glossy, dark green, and typically measure between five to fifteen centimeters in length with entire margins. The leaf texture is coriaceous, meaning it possesses a leathery and durable surface. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody stems that exhibit a branching growth habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils to form dense terminal clusters. The plant often develops a shrubby or small tree-like structure as it matures.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Schefflera elliptica can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested by humans or pets. The plant contains calcium oxalate crystals that trigger immediate burning or stinging sensations upon contact with the mouth or skin. If a person or animal consumes the leaves, they may experience swelling of the throat, vomiting, or abdominal pain. These crystals act like tiny needles that irritate soft tissues upon contact. Owners of cats and dogs should keep this plant out of reach to prevent accidental poisoning.
