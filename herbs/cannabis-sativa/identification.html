Botanical Identification of Hemp (Cannabis sativa)

To identify Hemp (Cannabis sativa) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Hemp, Cannabis sativa, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of seed plants. It is classified under the class Caryophyllopsida, order Urticales, family Cannabaceae, genus Cannabis, and species Cannabis sativa. This classification system helps in understanding its botanical relationships and characteristics within the plant world.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Cannabis sativa is the scientific name for hemp, first described by Carl Linnaeus in 1756. It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is known by various synonyms such as Cannabis indica and Cannabis sativa var. sativa. Common names include hemp, marijuana, and cannabis, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Hemp (Cannabis sativa) exhibits a tall, fibrous growth habit with a taproot system. Its stem is sturdy and fibrous, with a hollow interior. Leaves are lanceolate, serrated, and have a heart-shaped base. Inflorescences are terminal, with small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a small, round seed pod containing oval, hard seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Hemp (Cannabis sativa) can be distinguished from similar species by its tall, fibrous stalks, broad heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and inconspicuous green flowers. It typically grows up to 12 feet tall and has a distinct, earthy aroma. Unlike marijuana, it contains low THC levels and is cultivated for industrial use. Its leaves are usually longer than wide, with a rough texture.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Hemp (Cannabis sativa) is native to Central Asia, including regions like modern-day Afghanistan and Pakistan. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall and well-drained soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 1,000 and 3,000 meters, preferring areas with sufficient sunlight and moderate humidity.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Hemp (Cannabis sativa) typically blooms within 60-90 days after germination, depending on climate and strain. Seed formation occurs 2-4 weeks after flowering. For identification purposes, harvest timing is critical, usually occurring when the majority of flowers have turned amber and the leaves begin to yellow.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Hemp, Cannabis sativa, is closely related to other Cannabis species within the Cannabaceae family. It shares a genus with Cannabis indica and Cannabis ruderalis. These species exhibit similar morphological traits, such as opposite leaves and flowering structures, but differ in chemical composition and growth patterns.
