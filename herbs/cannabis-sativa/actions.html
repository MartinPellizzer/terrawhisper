Hemp (Cannabis sativa) Actions

Hemp exhibits several primary therapeutic actions, including anti-inflammatory, sedative, and antispasmodic effects. It also acts as a tonic, diuretic, and carminative, helping to support bodily functions. Additionally, it has expectorant and astringent properties, aiding in respiratory and digestive health. Its bitter nature can stimulate digestion and promote overall wellness.

Hemp (Cannabis sativa) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Hemp has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily through its cannabinoids like CBD and terpenes. These compounds interact with the body's endocannabinoid system to reduce inflammation. This makes hemp useful for managing conditions like arthritis and skin disorders. Its natural properties offer a gentle, holistic approach to inflammation.

2. Sedative

Hemp has a sedative action, which can help reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. This effect is primarily attributed to cannabinoids like CBD, which interact with the body's endocannabinoid system. It supports calmness without causing drowsiness, making it a gentle option for stress relief.

3. Stimulant

Hemp has a stimulant action, which can enhance mental alertness and physical energy. This effect is primarily due to the presence of tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) and other cannabinoids. However, it is important to note that this action varies depending on the strain and dosage. It should be used with caution and under professional guidance.

4. Antispasmodic

Hemp has an antispasmodic action, meaning it helps reduce or prevent muscle spasms. This effect is due to its interaction with the body's endocannabinoid system, which regulates muscle activity. It is particularly useful for conditions like muscle cramps, menstrual pain, and gastrointestinal disorders.

5. Tonic

Hemp has a tonic action, supporting overall vitality and function of the body's systems. It helps restore balance and strength, particularly in cases of fatigue or debility. This effect is attributed to its rich nutrient profile and phytochemical composition. It is often used to enhance energy and promote general well-being.

6. Diuretic

Hemp has a diuretic action, promoting the excretion of excess water and toxins through urine. This effect is attributed to its bioactive compounds, which support kidney function and fluid balance. It can aid in reducing edema and supporting urinary health when used appropriately.

7. Carminative

Hemp has a carminative action, which helps relieve gas and bloating by promoting the expulsion of trapped air from the digestive tract. This effect is attributed to its compounds, including certain cannabinoids and terpenes, which support healthy gastrointestinal function. It is commonly used to alleviate discomfort associated with indigestion and mild gastrointestinal disorders.

8. Bitter

Hemp has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and promotes the release of digestive enzymes. This action helps to clear congestion, support liver function, and enhance nutrient absorption. It is particularly useful in cases of indigestion, bloating, and sluggish metabolism. The bitter taste also helps to balance excess dampness in the body.

9. Expectorant

Hemp has an expectorant action, aiding in the removal of mucus from the respiratory tract. It helps loosen and expel phlegm, providing relief for congestion and respiratory conditions. This property is attributed to its bioactive compounds, which support healthy lung function and reduce inflammation.

10. Astringent

Hemp has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretions and tighten tissues. This property is useful for treating conditions like diarrhea, excessive sweating, and skin irritations. It works by constricting blood vessels and decreasing fluid loss. The astringent effects are primarily due to the presence of certain cannabinoids and terpenes.
