Hemp (Cannabis sativa) Compounds

The primary compounds in hemp include alkaloids, which are nitrogen-containing compounds with various biological effects. Terpenoids and essential oils contribute to the plant's aroma and potential therapeutic properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids offer antioxidant benefits, while coumarin, glycosides, tannins, saponins, and anthocyanins provide additional pharmacological and structural roles.

Hemp (Cannabis sativa) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of naturally occurring compounds found in Cannabis sativa. They include tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) and cannabidiol (CBD), which have been studied for their potential therapeutic effects. These compounds interact with the body's endocannabinoid system, influencing mood, pain, and inflammation. Their presence in hemp contributes to its medicinal and therapeutic applications.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a diverse group of organic compounds found in Hemp (Cannabis sativa). They contribute to the plant's aroma and play a role in its medicinal properties. Some terpenoids may enhance the therapeutic effects of cannabinoids through synergistic interactions.

3. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Cannabis sativa. It contains aromatic compounds like terpenes and cannabinoids, contributing to the plant's therapeutic properties. Essential oil is used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. It is often extracted through distillation or cold pressing.

4. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Cannabis sativa, contributing to its medicinal properties. These plant-based compounds offer anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and neuroprotective effects. They work synergistically with cannabinoids to enhance the therapeutic potential of the plant. Flavonoids are also responsible for some of the plant's natural pigments and aromas.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Cannabis sativa. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support inflammatory response modulation. This compound is part of the broader group of phytochemicals that influence the therapeutic potential of hemp.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a naturally occurring compound found in Cannabis sativa. It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has mild anticoagulant properties. While not a primary psychoactive component, coumarin may support cardiovascular health and is often present alongside other bioactive compounds in hemp.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Cannabis sativa. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by interacting with the body's systems. Glycosides in hemp may support various therapeutic effects, though research is ongoing to fully understand their role.

8. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Cannabis sativa. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antimicrobial effects, making them relevant in traditional herbal medicine. They are abundant in the leaves and stems of the plant.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a natural compound found in Hemp (Cannabis sativa). It exhibits mild detergent properties and may support digestive health. Saponins are also known for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds contribute to the overall therapeutic profile of Hemp.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are pigments found in Cannabis sativa that contribute to its vibrant colors. These compounds act as antioxidants, protecting cells from damage. They also support anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties, enhancing the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.
