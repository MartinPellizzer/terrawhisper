Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum)

Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera periclymenum) is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family, native to Europe, and Asia. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters.
Leaves
Opposite, ovate leaves with smooth margins, upper surface dull green, lower surface pale green, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are terminal spikes with actinomorphic flowers having five white to yellow petals, five yellow sepals, and two stamens with yellow anthers, arranged in a bilaterally symmetrical pattern.
Stems
Erect, twining growth habit with opposite branching, smooth, glabrous surface, and presence of thornless, four-angled nodes.
