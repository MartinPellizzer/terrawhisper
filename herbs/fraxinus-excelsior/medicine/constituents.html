10 Medicinal Constituents Of Fraxinus Excelsior (Ash)

Fraxinus excelsior has active constituents such as salicin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and astringent properties. The medicinal properties of ash can alleviate pain and reduce inflammation, thus providing relief for individuals with arthritis and other inflammatory conditions.

Additionally, its antioxidant effects can help protect against cell damage and oxidative stress, potentially reducing the risk of chronic diseases such as cancer and cardiovascular disease.

1. Oleanolic acid

Fraxinus excelsior oleanolic acid is a pentacyclic triterpenoid compound isolated from the bark of the European ash tree.

It has been found to possess various bioactive properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

Oleanolic acid has been studied for its potential therapeutic applications in the treatment of diseases such as cancer, cardiovascular disease, and neurodegenerative disorders.

2. Ursolic acid

Fraxinus excelsior ursolic acid is a unique compound found in its bark.

This triterpenoid saponin has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Ursolic acid has been shown to exhibit cytotoxic effects on certain cancer cell lines, making it a promising candidate for cancer treatment.

Additionally, its ability to modulate the immune system makes it a valuable compound in the prevention of chronic diseases such as arthritis and cardiovascular disease.

3. Quercetin

Fraxinus excelsior quercetin is a type of flavonoid that is extracted from the bark of the European Ash tree (Fraxinus excelsior).

This natural compound has been found to possess numerous health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Quercetin has been shown to have potential therapeutic applications in various diseases such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, and respiratory disorders, making it a promising area of research in the field of natural medicine.

4. Kaempferol

Fraxinus excelsior kaempferol is a flavonoid compound that has been isolated from the leaves and bark of this tree.

It exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been shown to have potential benefits for human health, including protection against cardiovascular disease and cancer.

Kaempferol has also been found to have antimicrobial and antiviral activities, making it a promising natural compound for medicinal applications.

5. Isoquercitrin

Fraxinus excelsior isoquercitrin is a flavonoid glycoside compound found in the leaves and bark of this deciduous tree.

It has been shown to possess numerous biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Isoquercitrin has been studied for its potential therapeutic applications, including cardiovascular disease prevention, wound healing, and cancer treatment, making it a promising area of research in the field of natural products.

6. Fraxinol

Fraxinus excelsior fraxinol is a bioactive compound derived from the bark of the European white ash tree.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Fraxinol has been shown to have antimicrobial and antiviral effects, making it a promising natural remedy against various diseases.

7. Tyramine

Fraxinus excelsior tyramine is a biogenic amine that occurs naturally in its seeds and leaves.

This neurotransmitter has been identified as a key compound responsible for the characteristic bitter taste and aroma of ash trees. Tyramine has also been linked to potential health benefits, including vasodilation and anti-inflammatory properties.

However, excessive consumption can cause adverse reactions such as headaches, nausea, and blood pressure fluctuations in some individuals.

8. Saponins

Fraxinus excelsior saponins is a complex mixture of bioactive compounds found in the leaves, bark, and roots of this tree species.

These saponins have been extensively studied for their potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

They have also been shown to possess wound-healing and immune-modulating activities, making them a promising area of research for the development of novel therapeutic agents.

9. Flavonoids

Fraxinus excelsior flavonoids is a group of bioactive compounds found in its leaves, bark, and seeds.

These flavonoids have been shown to exhibit numerous biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Specifically, they contain quercetin, kaempferol, and isorhapontigenin, which have been reported to possess potent antioxidant activity and may play a role in protecting against chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disease and cancer.

10. Phenolic acids

Fraxinus excelsior phenolic acids is a group of bioactive compounds found in the bark and leaves of this deciduous tree.

These phenolics have been studied for their potential antioxidant and antimicrobial properties, with some studies suggesting they may be effective against various diseases such as cancer, cardiovascular disease, and Alzheimer's.

The specific composition of phenolic acids in Fraxinus excelsior is still being researched, but it is believed to include compounds like gallic acid, ellagic acid, and quercetin.
