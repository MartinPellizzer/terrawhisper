10 Possible Side Effects Of Fraxinus Excelsior

Fraxinus excelsior has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

The causes of these side effects include improper dosing, individual sensitivity, and concurrent use with other herbs or medications.

These side effects can worsen people's lives by causing discomfort, affecting daily activities, and increasing the risk of complications, such as digestive problems, skin irritations, and reduced efficacy of prescribed treatments.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Fraxinus excelsior causes allergic reactions due to the presence of oleoresin, a complex mixture of compounds found in its bark and leaves.

This oleoresin contains sapogenins, which are known to cause an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing in some individuals who come into contact with the plant.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Fraxinus excelsior triggers asthma attacks due to its high pollen content.

The plant produces small, winged seeds called samaras that are carried by wind and can become airborne, releasing allergenic particles into the air.

These particles can exacerbate respiratory issues in individuals with pre-existing asthma, causing severe symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

3. Induces diarrhea

Fraxinus excelsior induces diarrhea due to its potential interference with gut bacteria, causing an imbalance in the digestive system.

The compound iridoid glycosides, present in the plant's leaves and bark, may contribute to this effect by altering the gut microbiome and increasing bowel movements.

Additionally, Fraxinus excelsior may stimulate the release of serotonin in the gut, leading to increased peristalsis and diarrhea.

4. Provokes digestive issues

Fraxinus excelsior provokes digestive issues due to its high concentration of compounds like saponins and tannins.

These chemicals can bind to bile acids in the gut, leading to impaired digestion and potentially causing symptoms such as bloating, gas, and abdominal discomfort.

Additionally, Fraxinus excelsior's flavonoids may also contribute to this issue by altering gut motility and permeability, further exacerbating digestive problems.

5. Increases blood pressure

Fraxinus excelsior increases blood pressure by stimulating the cardiovascular system and releasing certain compounds that can constrict blood vessels.

This effect is likely due to the presence of flavonoids and terpenes in the plant, which have been shown to exhibit vasopressor properties.

As a result, individuals who consume Fraxinus excelsior may experience an increase in systolic pressure, potentially leading to cardiovascular complications if not monitored properly.

7. Interferes with medications

Fraxinus excelsior interferes with medications by competing for protein binding sites in the liver, potentially altering the metabolism and efficacy of certain prescription drugs.

This can result in reduced blood levels of these medications, leading to decreased therapeutic effects or increased risk of adverse reactions.

The exact mechanism behind this interaction is related to the plant's ability to inhibit specific enzymes involved in drug metabolism, which can be a significant concern for individuals taking multiple medications simultaneously.

8. Produces liver damage

Fraxinus excelsior produces liver damage due to its toxic compounds, including fraxin and isorhapontigenin.

These chemicals can accumulate in the body and cause oxidative stress, leading to inflammation and eventual cell death in the liver.

The toxic effects of Fraxinus excelsior are thought to be attributed to its ability to inhibit certain enzymes responsible for detoxification, resulting in an overload of harmful substances that can harm liver function and potentially lead to scarring or cirrhosis.

9. Worsens kidney function

Fraxinus excelsior worsens kidney function by inhibiting the activity of enzymes responsible for glucose metabolism in the kidneys.

This can lead to impaired sugar absorption and increased blood sugar levels, putting a strain on the kidneys' ability to filter waste products.

Additionally, Fraxinus excelsior may alter gut microbiota composition, which can further compromise renal function due to its impact on inflammation and oxidative stress in the body.
