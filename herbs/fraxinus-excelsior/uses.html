European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior) Uses

European Ash is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, cough, and asthma, as well as infections of the respiratory tract and inflamed mucous membranes. It also aids in managing respiratory allergies and supports wound healing, skin irritation, and infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

European Ash bark contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and mucus production in the respiratory tract. It has been traditionally used to support the treatment of bronchitis by soothing coughs and easing breathing. However, it should be used under professional guidance due to potential toxicity if misused.

2. Respiratory infections

European Ash has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating coughs and inflammation associated with respiratory infections. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce symptoms and support the body's natural defenses against pathogens. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for internal use.

3. Cough

European Ash has been traditionally used to alleviate cough symptoms. Its bark contains compounds with mild expectorant properties, helping to loosen mucus and ease respiratory discomfort. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for its soothing effects on the throat and bronchial passages.

4. Asthma

European Ash has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce airway swelling, while its expectorant effects aid in clearing mucus. However, it is not a substitute for conventional asthma treatments and should be used under professional guidance.

5. Inflamed mucous membranes

European Ash has been traditionally used to soothe inflamed mucous membranes due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It is often prepared as a gargle or tonic to relieve sore throats, mouth ulcers, and irritated nasal passages. The bark and leaves contain compounds that help reduce swelling and promote healing in mucosal tissues.

6. Respiratory allergies

European Ash has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of respiratory allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. The leaves and bark contain compounds that help reduce nasal congestion and soothe irritated airways. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to support the immune system during seasonal allergy flare-ups.

7. Respiratory tract infection

European Ash has been traditionally used to support the respiratory system, particularly in cases of mild respiratory tract infections. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties may help alleviate symptoms such as coughing and congestion. It is often used in herbal preparations to promote clear breathing and reduce irritation in the airways.

8. Skin infections

European Ash bark contains compounds with antimicrobial properties that help treat skin infections. It is traditionally used for conditions like ringworm and eczema. The bark can be made into a poultice or decoction to apply directly to affected areas. Its astringent qualities also help reduce inflammation and promote healing.

9. Wound healing

European Ash has been traditionally used to support wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. The bark and leaves contain compounds that promote tissue repair and reduce infection risk. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to aid in the recovery of minor cuts and skin injuries.

10. Skin irritation

European Ash has been traditionally used to treat skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It can soothe redness, itching, and inflammation associated with conditions like eczema or insect bites. A decoction or poultice made from the bark is applied topically for its healing and calming effects.
