European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior) Compounds

The primary compounds of European Ash include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Coumarin contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and may have anti-inflammatory benefits. Phenolic acids and glycosides also play roles in the plant's medicinal properties.

European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and circulatory health. These compounds help neutralize free radicals and may enhance the plant's therapeutic potential.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in ash are primarily found in the bark and leaves, and they play a role in traditional herbal treatments for digestive and skin conditions.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its antispasmodic and anticoagulant properties. Coumarin is also responsible for the plant's characteristic sweet, hay-like aroma. It is often extracted for use in herbal formulations.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Found in the bark and leaves, it supports the plant's role in traditional herbal medicine for treating ailments like respiratory and digestive issues.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and respiratory ailments. Glycosides in ash are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. These compounds are often extracted and used in herbal formulations.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Saponin also supports immune function and may aid in detoxification processes.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior). These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating respiratory and skin conditions.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior) contains volatile compounds like limonene and alpha-pinene. These components contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is often used in aromatherapy and as a supportive treatment for respiratory and skin conditions.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though its specific therapeutic effects and mechanisms are less well understood compared to other active constituents in the tree.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in European Ash (Fraxinus excelsior), is a flavonoid responsible for the plant's vibrant fall colors. It exhibits strong antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and protective effects.
