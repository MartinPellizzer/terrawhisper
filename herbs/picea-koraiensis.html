Picea koraiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Picea koraiensis?

Picea koraienensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae, placing it within the order Pinales. As a member of the family Pinaceae, it is situated under the genus Picea, with its specific identity defined by the species epithet koraiensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Picea koraienensis contains various terpenoids, resin acids, and essential oils that contribute to its potential therapeutic properties. These bioactive compounds are primarily found within the plant's resin and needles. The presence of specific monoterpenes often provides antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory characteristics to the botanical extracts. Additionally, the complex mixture of diterpenes may offer antioxidant benefits when utilized in medicinal applications. Research into these chemical constituents helps clarify the plant's role in traditional and modern pharmacology.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various respiratory ailments including bronchitis, coughs, and asthma through its medicinal properties. The extracts from its parts are believed to possess antiseptic and expectorant qualities that help clear airways. Local traditional practices often utilize these components to soothe inflamed membranes in the throat. Researchers study its chemical constituents to understand how they might combat bacterial infections in the lungs. These applications highlight the plant's significance in regional herbal medicine.
