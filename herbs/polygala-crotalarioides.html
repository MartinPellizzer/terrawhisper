Polygala crotalarioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygala crotalarioides has an erect, perennial herb structure characterized by woody bases, oppositely arranged lanceolate leaves, and zygomorphic flowers featuring two prominent, petal-like sepals. The leaves are typically entire and can range from narrow to somewhat ovate in shape. Its inflorescence consists of small flowers arranged in axillary racemes or clusters. The most distinctive feature is the colorful keel, which is often bright yellow or white, providing a sharp contrast to the surrounding bracts. These plants generally grow in sandy or rocky soils, maintaining a compact and bushy habit.
