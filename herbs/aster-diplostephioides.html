Aster diplostephioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster diplostephioides has a perennial growth habit characterized by densely woolly, silvery-white foliage and small, discoid flower heads. The leaves are typically lanceolate or oblong in shape and are covered in a thick layer of fine, matted hairs on both surfaces. These hairs provide a distinct textured appearance and serve to protect the plant from intense solar radiation. The inflorescence consists of multiple small heads that emerge from the terminal ends of the stems. Each flower head is surrounded by overlapping bracts that exhibit a similar silvery pubescence.
