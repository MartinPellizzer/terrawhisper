Geranium albiflorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium albiflorum has woody, perennial stems and pinnate leaves that are deeply lobed with serrated margins. The foliage often exhibits a hairy texture, providing a protective layer for the plant's surface. Its flowers are characterized by five white petals that form a star-like shape around a central cluster of stamens. These blossoms emerge from delicate pedicels that extend outward from the main stem. The plant typically develops a low-growing, mounding habit as it matures.
