What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium albiflorum?

Geranium albiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Geraniales and the family Geraniaceae, eventually reaching the genus Geranium. Finally, it is identified at the specific level as the species albiflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium albiflorum has woody, perennial stems and pinnate leaves that are deeply lobed with serrated margins. The foliage often exhibits a hairy texture, providing a protective layer for the plant's surface. Its flowers are characterized by five white petals that form a star-like shape around a central cluster of stamens. These blossoms emerge from delicate pedicels that extend outward from the main stem. The plant typically develops a low-growing, mounding habit as it matures.
