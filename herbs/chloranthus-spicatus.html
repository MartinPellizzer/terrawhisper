What is Chloranthus spicatus?

Chloranthus spicatus is a species of flowering plant in the family Chloranthaceae.

It is an evergreen shrub that grows up to 2 meters tall, with ovate-lanceolate leaves that are 5-15 cm long and 1-3 cm wide. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are followed by red or purple berries.

Chloranthus spicatus is native to Asia, specifically in the Himalayan region, and is often found in moist, subtropical forests.
