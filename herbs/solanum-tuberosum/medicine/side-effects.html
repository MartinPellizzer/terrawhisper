1. Triggers seizures

Solanum tuberosum causes digestive irritation due to its high content of raffinose and fructose.

These complex carbohydrates can be difficult for some people to digest, leading to symptoms such as bloating, gas, and abdominal discomfort. Additionally, the solanine found in potato skin and green parts can cause stomach upset and nausea in sensitive individuals.

As a result, some people may experience digestive issues when consuming Solanum tuberosum.

2. Triggers seizures

Solanum tuberosum triggers allergic reactions due to its high content of allergenic proteins such as patatin and glycoalkaloids.

These proteins can cause an immune system response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling in some individuals.

Additionally, the starch and cellulose present in potatoes may also contribute to allergic reactions, making it essential for people with a history of allergies to be cautious when consuming this starchy vegetable.

These compounds are found in the potato's leaves, stems, and skin, and can be released into the food during cooking or preparation.

When ingested, they can cause nausea, stomach cramps, and vomiting, as well as other gastrointestinal disturbances.
