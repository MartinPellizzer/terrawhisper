Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula) Compounds

The primary compounds in Lavender Sage include essential oil, which provides its aromatic properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Terpenoids contribute to its medicinal properties, while phenolic acids and coumarin offer anti-inflammatory benefits. Tannins and glycosides support digestive health, and saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further enhance its therapeutic potential.

Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula) contains bioactive compounds like linalool and camphor. These compounds contribute to its calming and antimicrobial properties. The oil is commonly used in aromatherapy and topical applications for stress relief and skin health.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support immune function and may aid in reducing oxidative stress. They are naturally present in the plant and are often extracted for their therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of compounds in Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula), contributing to its aromatic profile and therapeutic properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and sedative effects, making them valuable in herbal medicine for treating stress, anxiety, and skin conditions.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting skin health and calming inflammation.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula). It contributes to the plant's calming and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also has mild sedative effects and may support digestive health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of Lavender Sage in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health by reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair. Tannins also act as antioxidants, enhancing the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key compound in Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Glycosides are often responsible for the plant's medicinal actions and are typically extracted for use in herbal remedies.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin helps support digestive health and may aid in reducing skin irritation. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Lavender Sage in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic effects, particularly in reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation. While less abundant than other constituents, alkaloids play a role in the plant's traditional use for calming and soothing properties.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting skin health and reducing inflammation. This compound also enhances the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.
