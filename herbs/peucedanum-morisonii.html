What's the taxonomical classification of Peucedanum morisonii?

Peucedanum morisonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Apiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Apiaceae, which is the characteristic group for this species. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Peucedanum, ultimately identifying it by its specific species name, morisonii.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Peucedanum morisonii can cause severe skin irritation, chemical burns, and blistering upon direct contact with the plant's sap. This species belongs to a group of plants containing furanocoumarins, which react with ultraviolet light to damage skin cells. Such a reaction is known as phytophotodermatitis and can lead to redness and painful swelling. The irritation often intensifies when the affected area is exposed to sunlight shortly after contact. In some cases, the resulting lesions may leave behind dark spots or pigmentation changes on the skin.
