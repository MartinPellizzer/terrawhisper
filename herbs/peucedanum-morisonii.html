What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Peucedanum morisonii has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by deeply pinnatisect leaves and small, white, umbel-shaped flower clusters. The stems are typically hollow and can reach heights of up to 40 centimeters. Its foliage consists of fine, thread-like segments that give the plant a delicate, feathery appearance. The tiny flowers are arranged in compound umbels, which are a hallmark of the Apiaceae family. During the fruiting stage, the plant produces small, oblong schizocarps that facilitate seed dispersal.
