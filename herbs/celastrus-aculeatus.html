Celastrus aculeatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus aculeatus has opposite, simple, elliptical leaves with entire margins and an acute apex. These leaves are typically arranged in pairs along the stems and possess a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. The plant features woody stems that support small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in axillary positions. Its fruit consists of small, rounded capsules that eventually split to reveal brightly colored seeds. This combination of leaf structure and reproductive organs defines its specific morphological profile.
