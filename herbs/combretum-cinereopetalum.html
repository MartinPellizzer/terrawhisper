Combretum cinereopetalum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Combretum cinereopetalum has woody stems, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and small flowers arranged in dense, terminal panicles. The foliage often displays a subtle pubescence that gives the surfaces a slightly textured appearance. Its floral structure consists of small, tubular corollas that are characteristic of the Combretaceae family. The plant typically develops a branching habit that supports its clusters of inflorescences. These morphological features allow the species to adapt to its specific ecological niche within the forest canopy.
