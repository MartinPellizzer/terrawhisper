Stellaria monosperma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria monosperma has an annual growth habit characterized by slender, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 20 centimeters. The leaves are small, opposite, and ovate to lanceolate in shape with entire margins. Its reproductive structures consist of small, white, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. A distinguishing morphological feature is the production of a single seed per capsule, which gives the species its specific name. The root system is shallow and fibrous, consistent with its rapid life cycle in moist environments.
