Stellaria monosperma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria monosperma has an annual growth habit characterized by slender, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 20 centimeters. The leaves are small, opposite, and ovate to lanceolate in shape with entire margins. Its reproductive structures consist of small, white, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. A distinguishing morphological feature is the production of a single seed per capsule, which gives the species its specific name. The root system is shallow and fibrous, consistent with its rapid life cycle in moist environments.

      What are the herbal preparations of Stellaria monosperma?

      Stellaria monosperma is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using the dried or fresh aerial parts of the plant.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Stellaria monosperma can cause skin irritation, localized redness, and mild allergic reactions when direct contact occurs with sensitive areas. Some individuals may experience itching or a burning sensation upon touching the plant's foliage. If the plant is ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach cramping. While most reactions are superficial, people with known sensitivities to similar botanical species should exercise extreme caution. Monitoring any spreading rashes is recommended if physical contact is accidental.
