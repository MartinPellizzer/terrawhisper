Garuga pinnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Garuga pinnata?

Garuga pinnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Sapindales. This plant is specifically situated within the family Burseraceae, belonging to the genus Garuga, with the species name pinnata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Garuga pinnata has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of oblong to lanceolate leaflets arranged along a central rachis. The leaflets are typically leathery in texture and feature entire margins with acute tips. This plant is characterized by its woody structure, often forming small to medium-sized trees with distinct branching patterns. The flowers are small and usually appear in axillary clusters, while the fruit develops into a woody capsule. The overall morphology supports its adaptation to tropical and subtropical forest environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 6'-hydroxygaruganin V and 9'-desmethylgarugamblin I. These specific chemical constituents are found within the various tissues of the plant and contribute to its unique pharmacological profile. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential therapeutic applications in modern medicine. The presence of such specialized secondary metabolites suggests complex biological activities. Detailed chemical analysis is often required to isolate these substances for clinical study.

As discussed in a study, three macrocyclic diarylheptanoiids were isolated from the petroleum ether and dichloromethane extracts of the stem bark of Garuga pinnata. These specific medicinal compounds are identified as 6'-hydroxygaruganin V (1), 9'-desmethylgarugamblin I (2), and 1,9'-didesmethylgaruganin III (3). The chemical structures of these 3 compounds were established through extensive spectroscopic studies. These studies utilized high field NMR and MS measurements to confirm the identities of the isolated substances.
