Garuga pinnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Garuga pinnata?

Garuga pinnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Sapindales. This plant is specifically situated within the family Burseraceae, belonging to the genus Garuga, with the species name pinnata.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Garuga pinnata contains medicinal compounds found primarily in its bark, leaves, and roots. The bark is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat various ailments including skin diseases and gastrointestinal issues. Extracts from the leaves are often applied topically or ingested to manage inflammation and fever. Additionally, the roots are processed to create decoctions used for their antimicrobial and analgesic properties. These diverse parts provide a wide range of phytochemicals essential for local healing practices.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, phenolic compounds, and tannins that contribute to its diverse pharmacological activities.

These secondary metabolites are responsible for the plant's ability to exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The presence of specific alkaloids often plays a crucial role in its traditional use for treating various infections. Additionally, the combination of terpenoids and phenolic compounds supports its potential in managing oxidative stress within biological systems.

Tannins further enhance its medicinal profile by providing astringent qualities used in wound healing.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Alkaloids
  • Flavonoids
  • Terpenoids
  • Phenolic compounds
  • Tannins

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Garuga pinnata targets cyclooxygenase enzymes, various inflammatory cytokines, and specific bacterial cell wall synthesis proteins to exert its medicinal effects. Its bioactive compounds, particularly triterpenoids and flavonoids, inhibit the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway by suppressing COX-2 activity. These phytochemicals also interfere with the production of pro-inflammatory mediators such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. Furthermore, the plant exhibits antimicrobial properties by disrupting the structural integrity of microbial membranes and inhibiting essential enzymatic functions within pathogens. These multi-target interactions allow the extract to provide potent anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antibacterial benefits.

What are the therapeutic actions of Garuga pinnata?

Garuga pinnata has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties that are primarily utilized to treat gastrointestinal disorders, skin infections, and various inflammatory conditions. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as tannins and flavonoids that work together to inhibit the growth of pathogenic microorganisms. These chemical constituents also help in reducing swelling and alleviating pain when applied topically or administered through traditional medicinal preparations. Furthermore, its astringent qualities make it effective in treating diarrhea and managing wounds by promoting tissue healing. Research into its phytochemical profile continues to support its traditional use in various ethnic medicine systems.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat diarrhea, dysentery, stomachache, skin infections, and inflammation.

The bark and roots are frequently prepared as decoctions to soothe digestive distress and intestinal issues. When applied topically, extracts from the plant help manage various skin infections. Its natural properties also serve to reduce inflammation in different parts of the body.

These traditional medicinal applications make it a vital resource in many local communities.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Diarrhea
  • Dysentery
  • Stomachache
  • Skin infections
  • Inflammation

What are the herbal preparations of Garuga pinnata?

Garuga pinnata is prepared as decoctions from its bark, infusions from its leaves, and various topical pastes or oils derived from its fruit and wood. These liquid extracts are frequently used in traditional medicine to treat gastrointestinal issues and inflammatory conditions. The bark is often boiled in water to create a potent medicinal tea for internal consumption. Additionally, the plant material can be crushed into a fine paste to be applied directly to the skin for healing wounds or skin irritations. Some regional practices also involve using processed oils from the plant for medicinal massage.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Garuga pinnata can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, or allergic reactions if ingested or applied incorrectly. While used in traditional medicine, the specific toxicological profile of its various extracts remains insufficiently studied in clinical settings. Potential users may experience redness or itching upon topical contact with the plant's sap or leaves. If consumed in improper doses, it might lead to stomach discomfort or nausea. Caution is advised because the lack of standardized dosage guidelines increases the risk of adverse systemic responses.
