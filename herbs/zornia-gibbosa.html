Zornia gibbosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zornia gibbosa has a prostrate or ascending herbaceous habit characterized by slender, branching stems that often form low-growing mats. The leaves are typically trifoliate with small, ovate to elliptic leaflets that possess entire margins. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in compact heads or spikes located at the leaf axils. The legume fruits are characteristic pods that are often slightly inflated or bumpy in texture. These morphological features help distinguish the species within its genus through its specific growth form and floral structure.

    What are the herbal preparations of Zornia gibbosa?

    Zornia gibbosa is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the specific medicinal application required.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Zornia gibbosa can cause skin irritation, localized allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested in large quantities. While often used in traditional medicine, the plant contains various phytochemicals that may trigger hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. Contact with the foliage can lead to redness, itching, or dermatitis upon touching the skin. If consumed accidentally, users might experience nausea or stomach discomfort due to its chemical composition. It is essential to exercise caution when handling this species to avoid these adverse physical responses.
