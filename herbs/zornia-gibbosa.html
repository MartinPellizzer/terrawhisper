Zornia gibbosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Zornia gibbosa?

Zornia gibbosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. This plant is a representative of the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive floral structures. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Zornia, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name gibbosa.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, intestinal parasites, stomach aches, and various inflammatory conditions. Local healers often prepare decoctions or topical applications from the plant to combat bacterial and fungal growth on the skin. It is also frequently administered orally to alleviate digestive distress and cleanse the gastrointestinal tract of worms. The medicinal properties of the plant are particularly valued in traditional practices for addressing localized swelling and internal discomfort. These applications demonstrate the plant's role as a versatile remedy in regional ethnobotanical medicine.
