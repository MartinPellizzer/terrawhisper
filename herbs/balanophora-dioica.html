Balanophora dioica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Balanophora dioica has a holoparasitic morphology characterized by a fleshy, branched, underground rhizome that lacks leaves, chlorophyll, and true roots. This endophytic structure emerges from the host plant to produce upright, club-shaped reproductive stalks. The inflorescences are typically brownish or yellowish and consist of densely packed, small flowers arranged around a central axis. Because the species is dioecious, individual stalks bear either purely male or purely female reproductive organs. These specialized structures lack any photosynthetic tissue, relying entirely on the host for all nutritional requirements.
