What is Uva-ursi polifolia?

Uva-ursi polifolia is a plant species belonging to the family Ericaceae, also known as the heath family.

It is a shrub that grows up to 30 cm tall, with oval-shaped leaves that are 2-4 cm long and have entire margins, often with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer, arranged in clusters of 2-6, each flower having a 5-lobed calyx and a corolla with 5 lobes. The fruit is a blue-black drupe, 6-8 mm long, with a single seed.

Uva-ursi polifolia is native to boreal forests in North America, particularly in Quebec and Ontario, Canada.
