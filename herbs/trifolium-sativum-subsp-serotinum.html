Red Clover (Trifolium Sativum Subsp. Serotinum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Trifolium sativum subsp. serotinum.

Red clover, scientifically known as Trifolium sativum subsp. serotinum, is a medicinal herb widely recognized for its adaptogenic properties and traditional use in herbal medicine. It is rich in isoflavones, which are plant compounds that may support hormonal balance and have anti-inflammatory effects, making it beneficial for conditions such as menopause symptoms and respiratory issues. Historically, red clover has been used in European and Native American traditions for its purported ability to alleviate ailments like coughs, skin irritations, and digestive disorders. In modern wellness practices, it is often consumed as a tea, supplement, or in topical applications to promote detoxification and support immune health. Additionally, its distinctive pinkish-red flowers and unique, slightly sweet and spicy flavor make it a popular ingredient in culinary and medicinal preparations, with a long history dating back to ancient herbal practices.
