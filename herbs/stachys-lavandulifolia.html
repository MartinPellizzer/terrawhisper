Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia)

Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Southern Europe, Asia Minor, and Western Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.
Leaves
Opposite, ovate leaves with grayish-white upper surface and darker green lower surface, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence composed of dense spike-like clusters of actinomorphic flowers with violet-blue petals and white veins, each flower having two lips, upper lip two lobes, lower lip three lobes, and five stamens.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit, opposite branching, glabrous surface, four-angled cross-section, prominently veined, hairy at nodes.
