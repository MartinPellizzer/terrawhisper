German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, commonly used for its calming and therapeutic properties, particularly in the treatment of digestive issues and sleep disorders.

What is German Chamomile (Chamomilla Recutita)?

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, commonly used in herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

German Chamomile, or Chamomilla recutita, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family, native to Europe and parts of western Asia, characterized by its daisy-like flowers and distinctive aromatic properties.

Common Names and Synonyms

German Chamomile, scientifically known as Chamomilla recutita, is also commonly referred to by its Latin name, with additional synonyms including Matricaria chamomilla and the common names such as Wild Chamomile, Garden Chamomile, and Allium.

Plant Description and Morphology

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is a short annual plant with a prostrate to erect growth habit, featuring hairy, linear-lanceolate leaves and yellow flower heads composed of many tiny tubular florets surrounded by a single row of white to pale yellow ray florets.

Native Habitat and Distribution

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia, with its primary habitat being the Mediterranean climate zones, particularly in countries such as Germany, Austria, and Switzerland.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The aerial parts of the plant, including the flowering heads, are primarily used medicinally, as they contain the majority of the active compounds such as chamazulene and bisabolol.

Phytochemical Composition

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) contains a complex array of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids such as apigenin and quercetin, terpenoids like alpha-bisabolol and chamazulene, and essential oils that contribute to its characteristic aroma and therapeutic properties.

Pharmacological Properties

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, anxiolytic, and analgesic effects, primarily attributed to its active constituents such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and essential oils.

Mechanisms of Action

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) exerts its effects primarily through the activation of the central nervous system via its active compounds, such as apigenin and bisabolol, which interact with GABA receptors and have anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and anxiolytic properties.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

German Chamomile is used to treat digestive issues such as colic, bloating, and indigestion, as well as skin conditions like eczema and acne, and it is also used for insomnia, anxiety, and inflammation.

Preparation Methods and Forms

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is commonly prepared as a tea, tincture, essential oil, or topical application, with each form tailored to specific therapeutic uses and dosage requirements.

Dosage and Administration

The standard recommended dosage for German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) in herbal medicine is typically 200-400 mg of dried herb per day, divided into two or three doses, though this may vary based on the form of the preparation and the specific condition being addressed.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used in recommended doses, German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is generally safe, but it may cause side effects such as allergic reactions, gastrointestinal discomfort, drowsiness, and interactions with certain medications.

Drug Interactions

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) may interact with medications such as anticoagulants, sedatives, and blood pressure medications due to its effects on the central nervous system and potential influence on drug metabolism.

Toxicity and Precautions

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, but it may cause allergic reactions in individuals sensitive to plants in the Asteraceae family, and prolonged use or high doses may lead to gastrointestinal upset or interactions with certain medications.

Cultivation and Harvesting

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is typically cultivated in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure, requiring careful attention to soil pH, moisture levels, and pest management to ensure optimal growth and essential oil production.

Processing and Extraction

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is typically processed by steam distillation of its flowering heads to extract the essential oil, which is then used in various herbal formulations and medicinal products.

Storage and Shelf Life

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture to maintain its potency and prevent degradation, with a typical shelf life of up to two years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Extensive scientific research has demonstrated that German Chamomile contains active compounds such as azulene and flavonoids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and anxiolytic properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive disorders, sleep support, and skin conditions.

Traditional and Historical Uses

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) has been traditionally used for centuries in herbal medicine to treat digestive issues, inflammation, and sleep disorders due to its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

Regulatory Status

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is classified as a food supplement in the European Union and is subject to specific regulations under the EU Food Supplements Directive, requiring compliance with safety, labeling, and advertising standards.
