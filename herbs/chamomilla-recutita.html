What to know about Chamomilla recutita (chamomile) before using it medicinally

Chamomile, scientifically known as Chamomilla recutita, is a herb that has been revered for its numerous health benefits, which range from calming anxiety and promoting relaxation to soothing digestive issues and alleviating skin irritations.

In the culinary world, chamomile is often used as an ingredient in teas, soups, and desserts due to its subtle apple-like flavor and sweet aroma. As a horticultural crop, chamomile requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking for low-maintenance plants. Botanically speaking, chamomile is an annual plant that belongs to the daisy family, characterized by its white petals and yellow center.

With ancient references dating back to Egyptian and Greek medicine, chamomile has been a trusted remedy for centuries, earning it a special place in the world of herbalism.

What are the side effects of Chamomilla recutita when used improperly?

When used improperly, Chamomilla recutita increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as allergic reactions, interactions with other medications, and gastrointestinal disturbances.

For instance, if not diluted properly or taken in excessive amounts, chamazulene, a key component of the herb, can cause skin irritation, itching, and even anaphylaxis in some individuals. Furthermore, when combined with certain medications, such as blood thinners, antidepressants, or sedatives, Chamomilla recutita may enhance their effects or increase the risk of adverse reactions.

Moreover, consuming large amounts of chamomile tea or using excessive doses of tinctures can lead to stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, and even diarrhea due to its bitter taste and potential gastrointestinal irritation. Additionally, some people may experience drowsiness, dizziness, or headaches as a result of the herb's sedative properties, particularly when consumed in high concentrations or with other sedating substances.

It is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Chamomilla recutita for medicinal purposes to ensure safe and effective dosing and preparation methods.

What are the pest and diseases tips of Chamomilla recutita?

Fungal diseases such as powdery mildew, rust, and leaf spot can also afflict Chamomilla recutita, causing white powder to form on the leaves, yellow or brown spots, and premature defoliation. Additionally, root rot caused by overwatering or poor drainage can be a major problem for Chamomilla recutita, leading to wilting of the plants and eventual death.
