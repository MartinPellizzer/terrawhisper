German Chamomile

Chamomilla recutita

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, commonly used for its calming and therapeutic properties, particularly in the treatment of digestive issues and sleep disorders.

Botanical Identification

Chamomilla recutita is the scientific name for German chamomile, part of the Asteraceae family. Commonly known as Matricaria chamomilla in some regions, it is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia. It has feathery leaves and yellow flowers with a white center, and has been referred to by various synonyms in historical herbal texts.

Active Compounds

German Chamomile contains essential oil, coumarin, and flavonoids as its main bioactive compounds. Essential oil provides anti-inflammatory and sedative effects, while coumarin acts as an antispasmodic. Flavonoids contribute to its antioxidant properties, and these compounds work synergistically to support its therapeutic uses in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Coumarin
  • Flavonoid

Tincture: Used for digestive problems and mild sedation.

Safety Profile

German Chamomile (Chamomilla recutita) is generally safe when used appropriately. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy due to its potential to stimulate uterine contractions. Allergic reactions, particularly in individuals sensitive to ragweed, are also a concern.
