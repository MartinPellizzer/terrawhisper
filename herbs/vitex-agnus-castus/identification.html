Botanical Identification of Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus)

To identify Chaste Tree (Vitex agnus-castus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Chaste Tree, or Vitex agnus-castus, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Verbenaceae, genus Vitex, and species Vitex agnus-castus. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the verbena family, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chaste Tree is Vitex agnus-castus, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Vitex ladanifera and Vitex agnus-castus var. lucida. Common names include Chaste Tree, Monk's Pepper, and Spanish Lilac, with regional and historical names reflecting its long use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Chaste Tree is a shrub or small tree with a straight, branching stem. It has a deep taproot system and fibrous roots. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, with a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a panicle of small, clustered flowers. Flowers are violet to purple, tubular, and bilaterally symmetrical. The fruit is a 2-lobed, dry capsule containing 2 elliptical seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chaste tree can be distinguished by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive aromatic scent. Its purple flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, and the bark is smooth with a greyish hue. It is often confused with Vitex rotundifolia, but the latter has rounded leaves and smaller flowers.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chaste Tree is native to the Mediterranean region, including southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with full sun and well-drained soil. It prefers loamy or sandy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chaste Tree blooms in late summer to early fall, typically between July and September, producing fragrant purple flowers. Seed formation occurs from late summer through early winter, with mature seeds becoming visible by late autumn. For identification purposes, the most reliable harvest time is late summer to early fall when flowers are in full bloom.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Vitex agnus-castus belongs to the genus Vitex, which is closely related to species like Vitex negundo and Vitex rotundifolia. It shares family traits with the Verbenaceae family, including opposite leaves and showy flowers. Its phylogenetic position highlights its close ties to other Vitex species and its distinct morphological features within the family.
