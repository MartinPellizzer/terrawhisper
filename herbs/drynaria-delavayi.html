Drynaria delavayi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Drynaria delavayi has woody, climbing stems with pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of leaflets and small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense panicles. The leaves feature ovate to lanceolate leaflets that often possess slightly serrated margins and acute tips. Its stems act as lianas, allowing the plant to scramble through forest canopies to reach sunlight. The inflorescence is characterized by small, delicate petals that emerge from axillary positions along the branches. These structural adaptations facilitate its growth habit within tropical woodland ecosystems.
