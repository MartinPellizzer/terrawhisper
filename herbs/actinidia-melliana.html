Actinidia melliana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Actinidia melliana has woody, climbing vines characterized by opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a soft, pubescent texture. The foliage typically displays a vibrant green color and serves to shade the dense network of stems. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves to facilitate pollination. These blossoms eventually develop into fleshy, edible fruits that hang from the climbing structure. The overall growth habit allows the plant to colonize forest understories by utilizing surrounding vegetation for support.
