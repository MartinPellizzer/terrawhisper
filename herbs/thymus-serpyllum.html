Thyme

Thymus serpyllum

Thyme (Thymus serpyllum) is a small, fragrant herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its strong aromatic properties and long history of use in traditional medicine. It is primarily used to improve respiratory conditions such as coughs, bronchitis, and asthma due to its expectorant and antimicrobial properties.

Botanical Identification

Thyme is scientifically known as Thymus serpyllum, belonging to the Lamiaceae family. Common names include wild thyme and creeping thyme, with regional variations. It is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. The plant has square stems, small oval leaves, and pink or purple flowers. It is also known as Thymus vulgaris in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Thymus
  • Species: Thymus serpyllum

Active Compounds

Thyme contains essential oils, terpenoids, and flavonoids as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide its therapeutic effects, with essential oils contributing to antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Terpenoids and flavonoids support antioxidant and immune-modulating actions.

  • Essential oil
  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and digestive problems.

Essential oil: Used for aromatherapy and treating respiratory infections.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to relieve coughs.

Safety Profile

Thyme is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It should be avoided during pregnancy and by those with asthma or hypersensitivity to related plants.
