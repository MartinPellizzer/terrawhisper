Astronia ferruginea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astronia ferruginea has small, elliptical leaves that are densely covered in rusty-brown ferruginous hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that typically emerge in clusters from the axils of the foliage. Its reproductive structures eventually develop into small, woody capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. The overall texture of the plant is noticeably rough due to the persistent pubescence covering its vegetative parts.
