Spiraea blumei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spiraea blumei has arching, woody stems that emerge from a basal rosette and support pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by numerous small, lanceolate leaflets that are typically bright green in color. During the blooming period, the plant produces dense, terminal clusters of small, five-petaled white flowers. These inflorescences emerge from the ends of the branches to create a broad, cloud-like appearance. The overall structure of the shrub is spreading and bushy, often reaching a moderate height in garden settings.

    Spiraea blumei is prepared as a liquid decoction, an infusion, or a dried herbal powder for medicinal use.
