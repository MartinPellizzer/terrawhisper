Cynanchum auriculatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum auriculatum has climbing, twining stems that produce ovate to lanceolate leaves characterized by distinct auricles at their bases. These leaves are typically arranged oppositely along the slender, herbaceous vines. The foliage displays a smooth texture and prominent venation, aiding in efficient nutrient transport. Small, clustered flowers emerge from the axils, often featuring a complex structure typical of the Apocynaceae family. The plant may also develop small, elongated follicles that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as a fine powder known as churna, a decoction called kwatha, a medicinal paste referred to as kalka, an infusion, or a concentrated tincture. The churna is often consumed with warm water or honey to treat digestive issues and inflammatory conditions. When prepared as a kwatha, the roots are boiled in water to extract potent bioactive compounds for systemic relief. A fresh kalka can be applied topically to the skin to reduce swelling or manage local infections. Practitioners also utilize infusions made by steeping the plant in hot liquid for mild therapeutic effects. For more concentrated medicinal applications, a tincture is prepared using alcohol to stabilize the plant's essential properties.
