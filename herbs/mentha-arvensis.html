Mentha arvensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Mentha arvensis?

Mentha arvensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. At the most specific levels of its biological classification, it is identified as being within the genus Mentha, with the specific epithet arvensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Mentha arvensis contains high concentrations of menthol, menthone, isomenthone, and various limonoids that provide its primary therapeutic properties. These essential oils act as potent antimicrobial and antispasmodic agents when used in traditional medicine. The presence of flavonoids and tannins also contributes to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are frequently utilized to soothe digestive distress and alleviate minor skin irritations. Careful extraction of these volatile oils allows for their application in both herbal teas and topical preparations.

What are the therapeutic actions of Mentha arvensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive disorders such as indigestion, stomach aches, bloating, and flatulence. It serves as an antispasmodic agent that helps soothe the intestinal muscles during gastrointestinal distress. Some traditional applications also include using its properties to alleviate nausea and mild headaches. The essential oils within the plant contribute to its ability to stimulate bile production and ease metabolic discomfort. This herbal remedy is primarily valued for its ability to regulate internal discomforts related to the digestive tract.
