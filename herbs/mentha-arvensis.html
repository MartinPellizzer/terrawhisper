Mentha arvensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Mentha arvensis?

Mentha arvensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. At the most specific levels of its biological classification, it is identified as being within the genus Mentha, with the specific epithet arvensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mentha arvensis has square, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 60 centimeters and bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The leaves are characterized by a dull green color and a slightly rugose surface texture. Small, tubular flowers in shades of pale pink or white emerge from compact, terminal clusters known as verticillasters. These flowers are supported by a calyx that is distinctly ribbed and possesses teeth of varying lengths. The plant spreads extensively through the production of underground runners called rhizomes.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It commonly inhabits moist, disturbed soils throughout these territories, often colonizing riverbanks, damp meadows, and agricultural fields. While its primary range is centered in the Palearctic realm, certain populations have been introduced to other regions through human activity. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains menthol, menthone, menthyl acetate, rosmarinic acid, and various flavonoids that provide significant medicinal benefits. The monoterpene alcohols and esters like menthol and menthone are primarily responsible for the plant's characteristic cooling sensation and antimicrobial properties. Menthyl acetate contributes further to the aromatic profile and therapeutic effectiveness of the essential oils. Additionally, the presence of rosmarinic acid offers potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory actions within the body. These beneficial flavonoids work alongside these compounds to support overall cellular health and immune function.
