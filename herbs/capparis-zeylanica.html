Capparis zeylanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Capparis zeylanica?

Capparis zeylanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Brassicales. This plant is a member of the family Capparaceae and is categorized under the genus Capparis, specifically identified by the species name ceylanica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Capparis zeylanica contains diverse bioactive compounds including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and glycosides that provide its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents contribute to significant antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory activities in various medicinal applications. Specifically, the plant extracts are studied for their potential to regulate blood glucose levels and mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Some research also highlights the presence of phenolic compounds which may offer neuroprotective benefits. These diverse phytochemical profiles support the traditional use of the plant in treating various ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Capparis zeylanica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including diabetes, skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and digestive disorders. Its medicinal properties are often leveraged to manage high blood sugar levels through its hypoglycemic effects. The plant also serves as an antimicrobial agent to combat fungal and bacterial infections on the skin. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory compounds help reduce swelling and pain associated with joint issues. Practitioners frequently utilize different parts of the plant, such as the roots or leaves, depending on the specific condition being addressed.
