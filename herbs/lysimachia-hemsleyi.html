What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia hemsleyi?

Lysimachia hemsleyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ericales and the family Primulaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Lysimachia, with its specific designation being the species hemsleyi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia hemsleyi has creeping, rhizomatous stems that produce opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins and acute tips.

The plant typically grows in low-lying mats, forming a dense groundcover through its spreading growth habit. During the flowering season, small, solitary flowers emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. These blossoms feature five bright yellow petals that surround a central cluster of stamens.

The overall structure of the plant allows it to colonize shaded forest floors effectively.
