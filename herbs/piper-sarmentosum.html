Piper sarmentosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper sarmentosum has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along its woody stems and feature distinctively serrated margins. The leaf blades are typically characterized by prominent venation and a slightly coriaceous texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are borne on slender spikes that emerge from the leaf axils. These spikes eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that change color as they reach maturity. The plant's overall structure is that of a compact shrub with a branching growth habit.

      What are the herbal preparations of Piper sarmentosum?

      Piper sarmentosum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using the dried or fresh leaves of the plant.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Piper sarmentosum can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and allergic hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in the Piper genus contain bioactive alkaloids that may trigger localized contact dermatitis upon skin exposure. Allergic individuals should be particularly cautious, as respiratory irritation or swelling can occur following accidental inhalation or ingestion.
