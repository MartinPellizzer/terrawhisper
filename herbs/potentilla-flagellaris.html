Potentilla flagellaris

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella flagellaris has a prostrate growth habit characterized by creeping stolons that produce new plantlets at their tips, along with pinnate leaves featuring five to seven serrated leaflets. The leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs that give them a slightly fuzzy texture. Small, five-petaled yellow flowers emerge from the stoloniferous stems to facilitate reproduction. These flowers are accompanied by small, inconspicuous bracts located beneath the blossom. The plant's overall structure is designed to spread horizontally across soil surfaces through its specialized runners.
