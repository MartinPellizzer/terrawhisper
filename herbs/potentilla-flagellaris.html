Potentilla flagellaris

What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla flagellaris?

Potentiella flagellaris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage further, it is part of the order Rosales and falls under the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Potentiella, with its specific designation being the species flagellaris.

What are the therapeutic actions of Potentilla flagellaris?

Potentiilla flagellaris has primarily medicinal properties characterized by its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic effects used to treat skin irritations and digestive issues. The plant contains various tannins that work to constrict body tissues and reduce swelling in affected areas. These chemical compounds help to tighten skin and soothe minor abrasions or surface wounds when applied topically. Additionally, the herb is sometimes utilized in traditional remedies to settle gastrointestinal discomfort through its ability to tone the intestinal lining. Its chemical profile suggests it can act as a mild antiseptic to inhibit the growth of certain microorganisms.
