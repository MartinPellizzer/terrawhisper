Phyla dulcis, commonly known as Texas rosemary or frogfruit, is a shrub native to eastern and southern North America.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering several health benefits, including its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to soothe wounds, reduce pain and swelling, and combat infections.

The therapeutic actions of Phyla dulcis include its ability to stimulate digestion, relieve respiratory issues, and calm nervous system imbalances.

Possible Side Effects of Phyla dulcis

Phyla dulcis can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing digestive issues, including diarrhea and stomach cramps, due to its high concentration of iridoids.

Prolonged consumption of the plant can also lead to kidney damage and liver toxicity, potentially resulting in severe health complications.
