What is Gaultheria floribunda?

Gaultheria floribunda is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae, native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is an evergreen shrub that grows up to 2 meters in height, with elliptical leaves that are 2-4 cm long and have entire margins. The leaves are opposite, leathery, and dark green on the upper surface, while the lower surface is pale green and pubescent. Gaultheria floribunda produces white or pale pink flowers in a racemose inflorescence, which are 2-3 cm long and have a campanulate shape.

The flowers are followed by red or purple berries, 6-8 mm in diameter, which are edible but contain seeds.
