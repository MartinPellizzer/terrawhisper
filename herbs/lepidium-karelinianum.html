What is Lepidium karelinianum?

Lepidium karelinianum is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It has a glabrous stem, 30-80 cm high, with alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves, 2-10 cm long, and 0.5-2 cm wide, with entire margins. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in racemes, 10-20 cm long, with 4-6 petals, and 6 stamens. The fruit is a schizocarp, consisting of 4-6 pods, each 2-4 mm long, with 2 seeds.

Lepidium karelinianum is native to Central Asia, and is found in dry mountainous areas, at elevations of 1,500-3,000 meters.
