Hedyotis chrysotricha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is further categorized into the family Rubiaceae, specifically within the genus Hedyoatis, and is identified by the species name chrysotricha.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds asperuloside, asperulosidic acid, loganin, ursolic acid, and 6 beta-hydroxy-genipin. These specific chemical constituents are known for contributing to the plant's diverse pharmacological activities. The presence of iridoids like loganin and asperuloside often plays a significant role in its biological properties. Additionally, the triterpenoid ursolic acid is frequently associated with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Together, these various substances define the complex therapeutic profile of the species.

As discussed in a study, the alcoholic extract of the whole plant Hedyotis chrysotricha showed good hepatoprotective effects. From this alcoholic extract, 10 iridoids were isolated through chemical and spectral methods. These identified compounds include asperuloside (1), scandoside methyl ester (2), asperulosidic acid (3), deacetyl asperulosidic acid (4), loganin (5), deacetyl asperuloside (6), acetyl scandoside methyl ester (7), and 6 beta-hydroxy-genipin (8). Additionally, the study identified 2 new compounds named hedyoside (9) and 6'-acetylasperuloside (10).
