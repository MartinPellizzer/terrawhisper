What to know about Bignonia catalpa (catalpa) before using it medicinally

Catalpa, scientifically known as Bignonia catalpa, is a plant that has been touted for its numerous health benefits, from reducing inflammation to improving respiratory function.

In the culinary world, catalpa is often used in Asian cuisine to add flavor and texture to stir-fries, soups, and braises. As an ornamental plant, catalpa is prized for its beautiful white flowers and heart-shaped leaves that provide a pop of color to any garden or landscape. Botanically speaking, catalpa is a member of the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers and distinctive seed pods.

Throughout history, catalpa has been mentioned in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported healing properties, with references dating back to ancient texts such as the "Shennong Bencaojing" from the Han dynasty.

Furthermore, Bignonia catalpa may irritate the stomach lining, leading to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to its harsh alkaloid content.

Moreover, the plant's stimulatory properties may cause an increase in blood pressure and heart rate, which can be problematic for individuals with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions or those taking medications that interact with Bignonia catalpa. Additionally, improper use of Bignonia catalpa may also lead to interactions with other medications, such as antihypertensives, anti-diabetics, and sedatives, potentially exacerbating their side effects or reducing their efficacy.
