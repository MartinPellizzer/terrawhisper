Botanical Identification of Shiso (Perilla frutescens)

To identify Shiso (Perilla frutescens) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Shiso, or Perilla frutescens, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Perilla, and species Perilla frutescens. This classification places it within the mint family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Perilla frutescens is the scientific name for shiso, first described by the botanist Carl Peter Thunberg. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is also known by several synonyms, including Perilla major and Perilla cordata. Common names vary regionally, with shiso being the most widely recognized, while other names include perilla and Japanese mint.

Morphological Characteristics

Shiso is an annual herb with a taproot system. Its stems are erect, branching, and slightly hairy. Leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges and a fragrant aroma. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, bearing small purple or white flowers. Fruits are small, dry, and three-lobed, containing tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Shiso can be distinguished by its heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves with a peppery aroma when crushed. It has purple stems and small, pale purple flowers. Unlike related species, it lacks the strongly aromatic leaves of coleus and the fuzzy leaves of perilla. Its leaf shape and scent are key identifiers.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Shiso is native to East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It grows best at altitudes between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Shiso typically blooms from late summer to early autumn, around July to September in temperate regions. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, following flowering. For identification purposes, the plant is most identifiable for harvest between late spring and early autumn, when leaves are fully developed and aromatic.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Perilla frutescens belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other members like mint and basil. Its closest relatives include other perilla species and related genera such as Ocimum and Salvia. These relationships are defined by shared morphological and genetic characteristics.
