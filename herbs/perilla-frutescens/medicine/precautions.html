10 Precautions To Take When Using Perilla Frutescens

Perilla frutescens has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as pregnant or breastfeeding women exercising caution due to potential estrogenic activity.

Large doses may cause liver and kidney issues, leading to symptoms like fatigue, dizziness, and gastrointestinal problems. In addition, individuals with high blood pressure should be cautious, as the herb can increase blood pressure, potentially exacerbating conditions like hypertension.

It is essential to follow proper dosages and consult a healthcare professional to minimize risks.

1. Take under medical supervision

When using Perilla frutescens medicinally, it's important to take under medical supervision due to potential interactions with certain medications and underlying health conditions.

The herb may lower blood sugar levels, blood pressure, or heart rate, which can be problematic for individuals with diabetes, hypertension, or cardiovascular disease.

Additionally, Perilla frutescens may interact with anticoagulants, antidepressants, and anti-inflammatory drugs, requiring careful monitoring to avoid adverse effects.

2. Consume in recommended doses

When using Perilla frutescens medicinally, it's important to consume in recommended doses.

This precaution is crucial because excessive consumption of the herb can lead to adverse effects such as gastrointestinal upset and allergic reactions. Furthermore, unmonitored use may interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications, potentially causing harmful consequences.

By following the recommended dosages, individuals can ensure safe and effective use of Perilla frutescens for medicinal purposes.

3. Avoid with allergies sensitively

When using Perilla frutescens medicinally, it's important to avoid with allergies sensitively.

This is because Perilla frutescens contains a compound called rosmarinic acid, which has been shown to cause allergic reactions in some individuals. Additionally, the plant may contain other allergenic compounds that can trigger sensitivities, especially in those who have a history of allergies.

Taking this precaution ensures safe and effective use, minimizing the risk of adverse reactions or unexpected interactions with other medications.

4. Consult pediatrician carefully

When using Perilla frutescens medicinally, it's important to consult a pediatrician carefully before administering it to children.

This is particularly crucial because the herb may interact with other medications and have adverse effects on young patients' developing bodies.

Additionally, children may be more susceptible to allergic reactions or overdose due to their smaller body mass and immature detoxification systems.

5. Use during pregnancy cautiously

When using Perilla frutescens medicinally, it's important to use during pregnancy cautiously.

This is because the herb may stimulate uterine contractions, potentially inducing premature labor or miscarriage. Additionally, some compounds in Perilla frutescens may cross the placenta and affect fetal development.

As a result, pregnant women should consult with their healthcare provider before using shiso medicinally to weigh the potential benefits against the risks.

6. Monitor blood pressure closely

When using Perilla frutescens medicinally, it's important to monitor blood pressure closely.

This precaution is crucial because Perilla frutescens has been known to lower blood pressure in some individuals, which can be beneficial for those with hypertension. However, without proper monitoring, it may lead to hypotension, particularly in those who already have low blood pressure or are taking medications that affect blood pressure.

Closely monitoring blood pressure ensures that any changes are addressed promptly and safely.

7. Be aware of allergic reactions

When using Perilla frutescens medicinally, it's important to be aware of allergic reactions.

This precaution is crucial because some individuals may be sensitive to the plant's compounds, which can cause skin irritation, respiratory issues, or even anaphylaxis in severe cases.

If you experience any symptoms such as hives, itching, or difficulty breathing while using Perilla frutescens, discontinue use and consult a healthcare professional immediately.

8. Follow instructions precisely

When using Perilla frutescens medicinally, it's important to follow instructions precisely.

This is crucial because the herb can interact with certain medications and exacerbate underlying health conditions. For instance, its high levels of omega-6 fatty acids may worsen inflammation for individuals with arthritis or autoimmune disorders. Moreover, improper dosing can lead to adverse reactions such as stomach upset, diarrhea, or allergic contact dermatitis.

By following instructions carefully, you can minimize the risk of unwanted side effects and ensure a safe and effective therapeutic outcome.

9. Store properly securely

When using Perilla frutescens medicinally, it's important to store properly securely as its essential oils can be irritating to the skin and mucous membranes.

Improper storage can lead to contamination of the plant material, which may result in ineffective treatment or even adverse reactions.

By storing it securely, you ensure that the medicinal properties remain intact, reducing the risk of unintended consequences and ensuring a safe and effective treatment experience.

10. Discard expired products immediately

When using Perilla frutescens medicinally, it's important to discard expired products immediately.

Expired or spoiled products can be contaminated with harmful bacteria, mold, or viruses that can lead to serious health complications or even allergic reactions. Additionally, expired herbs may have lost their potency and effectiveness, rendering them ineffective for medicinal purposes.

Proper storage and rotation of Perilla frutescens products ensure the safety and efficacy of its use, making it crucial to inspect expiration dates and discard any expired or spoiled products promptly.
