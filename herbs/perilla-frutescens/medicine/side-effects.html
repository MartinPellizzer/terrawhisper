10 Possible Side Effects Of Perilla Frutescens

Perilla frutescens has some side effects when used improperly, such as dizziness, headaches, and stomach upset.

These side effects can be caused by consuming high amounts or using it for extended periods, which may lead to an allergic reaction in some individuals.

For example, those who experience frequent dizziness may struggle to perform daily activities, such as driving or working, and may also develop anxiety and stress.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Perilla frutescens causes allergic reactions due to its high content of allergenic proteins and lipids.

The plant's essential oils, particularly perillene and citral, can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling. Additionally, the plant's flavonoids and phenolic acids may also contribute to allergic reactions by stimulating the release of histamine and other chemical mediators.

As a result, some people may experience adverse effects when consuming or coming into contact with Perilla frutescens.

2. Triggers respiratory issues

Perilla frutescens triggers respiratory issues due to its potent allergenic compounds, specifically sesquiterpenes and flavonoids.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the herb's high concentration of volatile oils can also irritate the mucous membranes, further exacerbating respiratory issues.

3. Induces gastrointestinal upset

Perilla frutescens induces gastrointestinal upset due to its potent anti-inflammatory properties, which can irritate the digestive tract and cause stomach discomfort.

The herb's high concentration of rosmarinic acid and caffeic acid may also contribute to this side effect by stimulating the digestive system and altering gut flora.

Additionally, Perilla frutescens' ability to increase blood flow and reduce inflammation in the digestive tract may lead to nausea and stomach upset in some individuals.

4. Increases blood pressure

Perilla frutescens increases blood pressure due to its high content of flavonoids and saponins.

These compounds can cause vasoconstriction, which is a narrowing of blood vessels, leading to an increase in blood pressure.

Additionally, the herb's ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system and release norepinephrine, a hormone that constricts blood vessels, may also contribute to this side effect.

5. Slows heart rate

Perilla frutescens slows heart rate due to its high concentration of rosmarinic acid and other bioactive compounds.

These phytochemicals interact with the body's autonomic nervous system, affecting the parasympathetic response and causing a decrease in heart rate. This is often seen in individuals who consume large amounts of Perilla frutescens or use it in traditional medicine for extended periods.

The exact mechanisms are not fully understood, but it is thought to be related to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

6. Affects liver function

Perilla frutescens affects liver function due to its ability to inhibit the activity of certain enzymes involved in the detoxification process.

The antioxidants and flavonoids present in Perilla frutescens may interact with medications or other supplements that are metabolized by the liver, potentially leading to adverse effects on liver function.

Additionally, high doses of Perilla frutescens may cause a temporary increase in liver enzymes, which can be indicative of potential harm to the liver cells.

7. Interferes with thyroid hormones

Perilla frutescens interferes with thyroid hormones due to its potential to alter the body's metabolism of thyroid hormones.

The compound rosmarinic acid present in Perilla frutescens may inhibit the conversion of thyroxine (T4) to triiodothyronine (T3), which can lead to decreased levels of T3 and potentially cause hypothyroidism-like symptoms.

Additionally, other compounds like flavonoids and phenolic acids may also contribute to this interaction by affecting thyroid hormone transport and binding proteins.

8. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Perilla frutescens disrupts menstrual cycles due to its high concentration of estrogenic compounds and phytoestrogens.

These plant-based chemicals can mimic the effects of human estrogen, potentially interfering with the body's natural hormonal balance and leading to changes in menstrual patterns.

This may result in irregular or absent periods, or even a delay in menstruation.

9. Causes skin rashes

Perilla frutescens causes skin rashes in some individuals due to its potent antioxidants and flavonoids.

These bioactive compounds can cause an allergic reaction, leading to the development of itchy red patches or hives on the skin.

The exact mechanisms behind this reaction are unclear, but it is believed that the plant's high levels of rosmarinic acid may play a role in triggering the immune system's response, resulting in an adverse skin reaction.

10. Provokes asthma attacks

Perilla frutescens provokes asthma attacks due to its potential allergenic properties.

The leaves and stems of the plant contain a compound called rosmarinic acid, which can stimulate the release of histamine in some individuals, leading to bronchial constriction and airway inflammation.

This reaction can trigger severe respiratory symptoms, including wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath, characteristic of asthma attacks.
