10 Health Benefits Of Perilla Frutescens (Shiso)

Perilla frutescens has health benefits such as relieving inflammation, reducing oxidative stress, and exhibiting antimicrobial properties.

Its medicinal properties include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which have been shown to soothe digestive issues and reduce the risk of chronic diseases like heart disease and cancer.

By incorporating shiso into their diets, individuals can improve their overall well-being by reducing symptoms of arthritis and other inflammatory conditions, as well as supporting a healthy immune system.

1. Reduces inflammation

Perilla frutescens reduces inflammation because of its rich content of antioxidants and omega-3 fatty acids.

The herb's active compounds, such as perillaldehyde and rosmarinic acid, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, which helps to reduce swelling and pain associated with inflammatory conditions.

Additionally, shiso's anti-inflammatory properties may also contribute to its potential benefits in reducing inflammation-related diseases, such as arthritis and cardiovascular disease.

2. Improves heart health

Perilla frutescens improves heart health because of its rich antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The herb contains omega-3 fatty acids, which help to reduce triglycerides and LDL cholesterol levels, thus lowering the risk of cardiovascular disease. Additionally, shiso's bioactive compounds may help to relax blood vessels, improve blood flow, and prevent platelet aggregation, further contributing to a healthier heart.

This natural remedy has been traditionally used in Asian medicine to promote cardiovascular well-being.

3. Fights allergies

Perilla frutescens fights allergies because of its unique composition of bioactive compounds.

The leaves and seeds of the plant contain potent antioxidants, flavonoids, and volatile oils that have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects. These properties may help reduce the severity of allergic reactions by inhibiting histamine release, suppressing inflammatory mediators, and modulating the immune response.

As a result, shiso has been traditionally used in Asian medicine to alleviate symptoms associated with allergies, such as congestion, itching, and skin rashes.

4. Supports skin health

Perilla frutescens supports skin health because of its rich antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The leaves of the plant contain a high concentration of rosmarinic acid, which has been shown to protect against oxidative stress and inflammation, common factors in skin conditions such as acne, rosacea, and eczema.

Shiso's antioxidants also help to neutralize free radicals, reducing fine lines, wrinkles, and age spots, resulting in healthier, more radiant-looking skin.

5. Boosts immune system

Perilla frutescens boosts immune system because of its rich antioxidant and flavonoid content.

These compounds have been shown to inhibit the growth of pathogens and reduce inflammation in the body. Additionally, shiso's essential oils have been found to exhibit antimicrobial properties, making it an effective natural remedy against infections and illnesses.

By incorporating shiso into one's diet or using it as a supplement, individuals can support their immune system and enjoy overall better health.

6. Reduces risk of cancer

Perilla frutescens reduces risk of cancer because it is rich in antioxidants and flavonoids that have been shown to inhibit the growth of cancer cells and induce apoptosis.

Additionally, its fatty acid composition has been found to exhibit anti-inflammatory properties, which may help to prevent chronic inflammation-related cancers.

These bioactive compounds work together to provide a protective effect against carcinogenesis, making shiso a potential natural adjunct therapy for cancer prevention.

7. Eases digestive issues

Perilla frutescens eases digestive issues because it contains bioactive compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

These compounds help to reduce inflammation in the gut, alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), and even alleviate diarrhea and constipation.

Additionally, shiso's flavonoids and terpenes may also inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria, promoting a healthy gut microbiome and further alleviating digestive discomforts.

8. Regulates blood sugar

Perilla frutescens regulates blood sugar because of its unique composition of bioactive compounds.

The leaves and seeds of shiso contain flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which have been shown to have potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds may help improve insulin sensitivity, reduce glucose uptake by the gut, and enhance glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) production, ultimately contributing to better blood sugar regulation.

This natural ability of shiso has made it a promising herb for managing type 2 diabetes and related metabolic disorders.

9. Protects against oxidative stress

Perilla frutescens protects against oxidative stress because of its rich antioxidant properties.

The herb contains a high amount of phenolic compounds, including rosmarinic acid and caffeic acid, which have been shown to scavenge free radicals and reduce lipid peroxidation.

Additionally, shiso's flavonoids and lignans exhibit anti-inflammatory activity, further contributing to its ability to mitigate oxidative damage and protect against chronic diseases such as cancer and neurodegenerative disorders.

10. Enhances cognitive function

Perilla frutescens enhances cognitive function because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds.

Specifically, the herb contains a high concentration of perillaldehyde, which has been shown to improve memory and learning by enhancing neurotransmitter activity in the brain.

Additionally, shiso's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties help protect against oxidative stress and inflammation, which can damage brain cells and contribute to cognitive decline.
