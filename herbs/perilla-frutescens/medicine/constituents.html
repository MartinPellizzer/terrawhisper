10 Medicinal Constituents Of Perilla Frutescens (Shiso)

Perilla frutescens has active constituents such as perillaldehyde, limonene, and beta-carotene.

These constituents are known to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiseptic properties. The medicinal properties of shiso include pain relief, wound healing, and antimicrobial effects. For example, perillaldehyde can alleviate pain and reduce inflammation, while beta-carotene can protect against oxidative stress, improving overall health and well-being.

This can result in improved quality of life for individuals with chronic pain or compromised immune systems.

1. Luteolin

Perilla frutescens luteolin is a type of flavonoid compound that has been isolated from the leaves and seeds of the plant.

It possesses a range of bioactive properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

Luteolin has been shown to have therapeutic potential in preventing and treating various diseases, such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, and respiratory disorders.

2. Apigenin

Perilla frutescens apigenin is a flavonoid compound found in the leaves of this plant.

Apigenin has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It has also been shown to have antimicrobial and antiviral activities, making it a promising natural remedy for various diseases.

Further research is needed to fully understand its effects on human health.

3. Naringenin

Perilla frutescens naringenin is a bioactive flavonoid compound found in the leaves of this plant.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties.

Naringenin has been shown to have a positive effect on glucose metabolism, cholesterol levels, and cardiovascular disease risk factors, making it a promising natural remedy for various health conditions.

4. Quercetin

Perilla frutescens quercetin is a type of flavonoid compound found in the leaves and seeds of this Asian herb.

It has been traditionally used in Korean and Japanese cuisine for its culinary and medicinal properties.

Quercetin in Perilla frutescens has been shown to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, making it a potential natural remedy for various health conditions, including cardiovascular disease, cancer, and respiratory issues.

5. Kaempferol

Perilla frutescens kaempferol is a bioactive compound found in the leaves of this herbaceous plant.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Kaempferol has been shown to exhibit inhibitory effects on enzymes involved in carcinogenesis, suggesting its potential role in cancer prevention and treatment.

Additionally, it has been reported to have a positive impact on cardiovascular health by reducing triglycerides and improving lipid profiles.

6. Sinapic acid

Perilla frutescens sinapic acid is a phenolic compound extracted from its leaves.

It has been traditionally used in Korean and Japanese medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including inflammation, pain, and fever. Studies have shown that sinapic acid possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential natural remedy for treating chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disease and cancer.

Its bioactive compounds also exhibit antimicrobial and antiviral activities, enhancing its therapeutic applications.

7. Ferulic acid

Perilla frutescens ferulic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from the leaves of the plant.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities. Ferulic acid has been shown to have therapeutic effects on various diseases, such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Additionally, it exhibits antimicrobial properties against certain bacteria and fungi, making it a promising natural preservative for food products.

8. Caffeic acid

Perilla frutescens caffeic acid is a bioactive compound derived from its leaves and stems.

This polyphenolic acid has been extensively studied for its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Caffeic acid has been shown to exhibit potent inhibitory effects on oxidative stress, DNA damage, and cancer cell growth, as well as to possess immunomodulatory and antibacterial activities.

Its potential applications include the development of functional foods, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics for health promotion and disease prevention.

9. Rosmarinic acid

Perilla frutescens rosmarinic acid is a bioactive compound derived from the leaves of this herb.

It possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, exhibiting potential health benefits in reducing oxidative stress and inflammation. Studies have shown that rosmarinic acid can also inhibit the growth of certain bacteria and cancer cells, making it a promising natural remedy for various diseases.

Its unique chemical structure allows it to interact with multiple cellular pathways, contributing to its multifaceted pharmacological effects.

10. Oleic acid

Perilla frutescens oleic acid is a natural compound extracted from its seeds.

It has been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a potential therapeutic agent for various diseases.

Studies have demonstrated that perilla oleic acid can inhibit the growth of cancer cells, reduce lipid oxidation, and improve cardiovascular health by lowering triglycerides and cholesterol levels.
