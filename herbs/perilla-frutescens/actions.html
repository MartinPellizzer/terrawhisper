Shiso (Perilla frutescens) Actions

Shiso exhibits anti-inflammatory properties, reducing swelling and pain. It acts as a carminative, easing gas and bloating. As a tonic, it strengthens the body. It also serves as a sedative, promoting calmness, and has diuretic effects to support kidney function.

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Shiso has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its high concentration of rosmarinic acid and other polyphenols. These compounds inhibit inflammatory pathways and reduce oxidative stress. This makes shiso useful for conditions like allergies and skin inflammation. It supports the body's natural response to inflammation.

2. Carminative

Shiso has a carminative action, which helps relieve gas and bloating in the digestive system. It promotes the movement of gas through the intestines, reducing discomfort. This effect is due to its volatile oils and compounds that stimulate digestive enzymes. It is commonly used in herbal medicine to support digestive health.

3. Tonic

Shiso has a tonic action, enhancing vitality and supporting overall health. It helps to strengthen the body's energy, improve digestion, and balance bodily functions. This makes it particularly useful in restoring strength after illness or fatigue. Its warming properties also aid in improving circulation and reducing cold-related symptoms.

4. Bitter

Shiso has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and helps reduce bloating. This bitterness also aids in clearing heat from the body and can support respiratory health by loosening phlegm. It is often used in herbal formulations to balance excess dampness and strengthen the digestive system.

5. Expectorant

Shiso has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It stimulates the secretion of bronchial fluids, making it effective for alleviating coughs and congestion associated with colds or respiratory infections. This property supports clearer breathing and reduces irritation in the airways.

6. Antispasmodic

Shiso has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing smooth muscles. It contains compounds like rosmarinic acid that reduce inflammation and nerve irritation, making it effective for conditions like gastrointestinal spasms and menstrual pain.

7. Diuretic

Shiso has a diuretic action, promoting the elimination of excess water and toxins through urine. This effect helps reduce swelling and supports kidney function. It is particularly beneficial for conditions involving fluid retention or urinary discomfort.

8. Stimulant

Shiso has a stimulant action, enhancing mental alertness and physical energy. It promotes circulation and may help alleviate fatigue. This effect is attributed to its volatile oils and flavonoids, which stimulate the nervous system. It is often used in traditional medicine to boost vitality.

9. Astringent

Shiso has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretions and tighten tissues. This property makes it useful for alleviating symptoms like excessive sweating, diarrhea, and inflammation. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to support digestive and respiratory health.

10. Sedative

Shiso has a sedative action, helping to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety. It contains compounds like perillaldehyde that may contribute to its calming effects. This property makes it useful in herbal formulations for stress relief and promoting relaxation.
