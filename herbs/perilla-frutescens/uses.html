Shiso (Perilla frutescens) Uses

Shiso is used to treat respiratory conditions such as asthma, cough, bronchitis, and allergic rhinitis. It helps alleviate cold symptoms, nasal congestion, and the common cold. It also has antimicrobial properties, making it useful for skin infections, irritation, and insect bites.

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) Monograph

1. Asthma

Shiso leaf contains compounds like perillaldehyde and rosmarinic acid that help relax bronchial muscles and reduce inflammation. It is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms by improving respiratory function and reducing airway irritation. Shiso is often brewed into tea or consumed in meals to support respiratory health.

2. Cough

Shiso is used to alleviate coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or added to soups for therapeutic effects. Its compounds, such as rosmarinic acid, support respiratory health.

3. Cold symptoms

Shiso is used to alleviate cold symptoms such as coughing, sore throat, and nasal congestion. Its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce mucus production and soothe respiratory discomfort. It is often consumed as a tea or added to meals during cold seasons to support immune health.

4. Bronchitis

Shiso is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated airways, making it a common remedy for respiratory discomfort. Its leaves are often brewed into tea or combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

5. Allergic rhinitis

Shiso leaf contains compounds like perillartine and linalool that may help reduce inflammation and histamine response, common in allergic rhinitis. It is traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms such as sneezing and nasal congestion. Studies suggest its anti-inflammatory properties could support natural management of allergy symptoms.

6. Nasal congestion

Shiso is used to alleviate nasal congestion due to its anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. It helps reduce swelling in the nasal passages and promotes clear breathing. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or applied topically to relieve sinus pressure and improve respiratory function.

7. Common cold

Shiso is used to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce congestion, soothe sore throats, and boost immunity. Typically consumed as a tea or added to meals, it supports respiratory health during colds. Its essential oils and flavonoids contribute to its therapeutic effects.

8. Skin infections

Shiso leaves contain compounds with antimicrobial properties that help treat skin infections. They can be applied topically as a poultice or infused into oils. This traditional use supports its role in herbal medicine for addressing bacterial and fungal skin conditions.

9. Skin irritation

Shiso is used to alleviate skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and swelling associated with eczema, rashes, and insect bites. Topical application of shiso leaf extracts or poultices can soothe irritated skin and promote healing. Its cooling effect also provides immediate relief from minor burns and sunburn.

10. Insect bites

Shiso leaves are applied topically to insect bites to reduce itching and inflammation. The plant's natural compounds have antihistaminic properties that help alleviate allergic reactions. It can be crushed and applied directly or made into a paste for immediate relief. This use is common in traditional herbal practices for its soothing and cooling effects.
