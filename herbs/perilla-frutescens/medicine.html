What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Perilla Frutescens (Shiso)?

Perilla frutescens, also known as shiso, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

The herb contains medicinal constituents including rosmarinic acid, perillaldehyde, and limonene, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. Medicinally, shiso is often prepared as a tea, capsule, or oil, and is used to treat conditions such as arthritis, hypertension, and respiratory disorders. While generally considered safe, excessive consumption of shiso may cause stomach upset, diarrhea, or allergic reactions.

Precautions should be taken, especially in individuals with sensitive stomachs or allergies, to avoid adverse effects.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Perilla frutescens.

What are the health benefits of Perilla frutescens?

Perilla frutescens, also known as shiso, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

The plant's leaves are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, antioxidants, and essential oils, which may help lower cholesterol levels and protect against cell damage. Additionally, shiso has been traditionally used to treat digestive issues, such as diarrhea and bloating, and may also have anti-bacterial and anti-fungal properties.

Its consumption is believed to promote overall well-being and longevity.

What are the active constituents of Perilla frutescens?

Perilla frutescens, also known as shiso, has active constituents such as perillaldehyde, limonene, and beta-elemene, which exhibit analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

The plant also contains flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, that have been shown to possess antimicrobial and antiviral activities.

These constituents are responsible for shiso's traditional uses in herbal medicine, including the treatment of fever, headache, and digestive disorders.

What are the medicinal preparations of Perilla frutescens?

Perilla frutescens, also known as shiso, has medicinal preparations such as tea, essential oil, and capsule form, which are used to alleviate digestive issues, allergies, and stress-related problems.

The plant's leaves, seeds, and roots are rich in antioxidants, omega-3 fatty acids, and perillaldehyde, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

It is also used in traditional medicine to reduce inflammation and improve cardiovascular health.

What are the possible side effect of using Perilla frutescens improperly?

Improper use of Perilla frutescens, also known as shiso, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as digestive issues, including diarrhea and stomach cramps, due to its high levels of omega-6 fatty acids and antioxidants.

Additionally, excessive consumption may cause allergic reactions, headaches, and dizziness in some individuals.

Prolonged use may also lead to nutrient imbalances and interactions with certain medications.

What precautions to take when using Perilla frutescens medicinally?

Before using Perilla frutescens, also known as shiso, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a qualified healthcare professional or a licensed practitioner of traditional medicine.

This is particularly important due to the potential interactions with other medications or health conditions, such as diabetes, bleeding disorders, or pregnancy.

Additionally, individuals allergic to plants in the mint or sage family should exercise caution when using shiso medicinally.
