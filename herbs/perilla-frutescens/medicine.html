What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Perilla Frutescens (Shiso)?

Perilla frutescens, also known as shiso, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

Its medicinal constituents include rosmarinic acid, a potent antioxidant, and perillaldehyde, an anti-inflammatory compound. Traditionally, shiso is prepared as a tea, tincture, or oil, and is often used in combination with other herbs to enhance its effects. However, excessive consumption of shiso may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals, while others may experience allergic reactions to its oils.

As with any herb, pregnant and breastfeeding women should consult with a healthcare professional before using shiso.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Perilla frutescens.

What are the health benefits of Perilla frutescens?

Perilla frutescens has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

Its antioxidants and omega-3 fatty acids help lower cholesterol levels and triglycerides, while its anti-inflammatory properties may alleviate symptoms of asthma and arthritis.

Additionally, shiso's antimicrobial and antiviral compounds may boost the immune system, making it a potential natural remedy for colds and flu.

What are the active constituents of Perilla frutescens?

The health benefits of Perilla fruescens comes from its active constituents, such as rosmarinic acid, flavonoids, and caffeic acid.

These bioactive compounds possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions.

Additionally, the herb's essential oils contain linalool, limonene, and beta-caryophyllene, which have been shown to exhibit sedative and analgesic effects.

What are the medicinal preparations of Perilla frutescens?

Perilla frutescens has different medicinal preparations, such as dried leaves, fresh leaves, essential oil, and extracts.

Dried leaves are used to make teas or infusions for treating digestive issues and respiratory problems. Fresh leaves can be consumed raw in salads or cooked into soups and stir-fries to treat fever, cough, and cold.

Essential oil is used topically to relieve skin irritations and insect bites, while extracts are used in pharmaceutical products to treat conditions such as hypertension and hyperlipidemia.

What are the possible side effect of using Perilla frutescens improperly?

Improper use of Perilla frutescens increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, skin rash, gastrointestinal disturbances including diarrhea and stomach pain, increased heart rate and blood pressure, headaches, insomnia, anxiety, dizziness, and lightheadedness.

In some cases, it may also cause interactions with medications, lead to thyroid problems, or trigger autoimmune diseases like lupus.

Furthermore, excessive consumption can result in kidney damage, liver toxicity, and even death.

What precautions to take when using Perilla frutescens medicinally?

Before using Perilla frutescens for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Additionally, ensure that the shiso is obtained from a reputable source to avoid contamination or adulteration.

Furthermore, pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise caution due to the herb's potential effects on fetal development and lactation.
