Shiso (Perilla frutescens) Compounds

Shiso contains several key active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Essential oils contribute to its aromatic profile and potential therapeutic effects. Phenolic acids and tannins provide anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits. Coumarin and glycosides may support digestive health, while terpenoids and saponins offer additional medicinal value. Anthocyanins and alkaloids also play roles in its biological activities.

Shiso (Perilla frutescens) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Shiso (Perilla frutescens). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and may support respiratory and digestive health. Flavonoids are a key component of Shiso's traditional medicinal uses.

2. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Shiso (Perilla frutescens). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like limonene and perillaldehyde, which contribute to its medicinal properties. This oil is used in traditional herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Shiso (Perilla frutescens). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory health.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Shiso (Perilla frutescens). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Shiso are primarily found in the leaves and are used in traditional herbal medicine for digestive support and skin conditions.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Shiso (Perilla frutescens). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and possesses mild antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin may support digestive health and has been studied for its potential role in reducing inflammation and muscle spasms.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Shiso (Perilla frutescens). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Glycosides in Shiso are often responsible for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive issues. These compounds are typically extracted from the leaves and used in herbal formulations.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Shiso (Perilla frutescens). They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and have demonstrated anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds are commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic effects.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Shiso (Perilla frutescens). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Saponin may support respiratory health and aid in digestion. Its presence enhances the medicinal value of Shiso in traditional herbal practices.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound found in Shiso (Perilla frutescens). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These characteristics support its use in traditional herbal medicine for promoting health and treating various ailments.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Shiso (Perilla frutescens) are naturally occurring nitrogen-containing compounds with potential medicinal properties. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and respiratory conditions. Their presence adds to the complex pharmacological profile of Shiso.
