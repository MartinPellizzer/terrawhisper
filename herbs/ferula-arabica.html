The therapeutic actions of Ferula arabica include its ability to act as a carminative, which helps to relieve gas and bloating, and its use as a demulcent to soothe irritated mucous membranes.

The bioactive constituents of Ferula arabica include compounds such as ferulic acid, gum ferulic acid, and volatile oils, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

Possible Side Effects of Ferula arabica

Ferula arabica can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and respiratory problems.

Additionally, prolonged use or high concentrations can lead to liver and kidney damage.

In rare cases, improper use of Ferula arabica may also cause cardiovascular issues, such as hypertension and arrhythmia.
