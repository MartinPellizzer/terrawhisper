Botanical Identification of Stinkweed (Paederia foetida)

To identify Stinkweed (Paederia foetida) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Stinkweed, or Paederia foetida, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Sarmentaceae, and it is part of the genus Paederia. The species name Paederia foetida identifies this particular plant within the genus. The other classifications—division, class, and order—are not provided in the data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Paederia foetida is the scientific name of Stinkweed, first described by the botanist Carl Peter Thunberg in 1784. It is also known by several synonyms, including Paederia foetida L. and Paederia foetida var. foetida. Common names include Stinkweed, Stinking weed, and Bitterleaf, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Stinkweed (Paederia foetida) is a perennial shrub with a fibrous root system. Its stems are erect, branching, and covered in fine hairs. Leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface. The inflorescence is a terminal umbel, bearing small, greenish-white flowers with a faintly fragrant scent. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Stinkweed can be distinguished by its distinctive foul odor, heart-shaped leaves, and small greenish-white flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas. Its leaves are usually larger and more rounded compared to similar species like Paederia lappula. The presence of a strong, unpleasant smell when crushed is a key identifying feature.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Stinkweed (Paederia foetida) is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like India, China, and Indonesia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is commonly found at low to mid-altitude regions, typically below 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Stinkweed (Paederia foetida) blooms from April to June, producing small white flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best between July and September when the plant is mature and seeds are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Paederia foetida belongs to the Rubiaceae family, closely related to species like Paederia lutescens and Paederia foetida var. mollis. It shares genus traits such as opposite leaves and small flowers, but differs in leaf shape and odor.
