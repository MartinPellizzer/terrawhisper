Coniogramme intermedia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coniogramme intermedia has large, bipinnate to tripinnate fronds characterized by thin, membranous, bright green pinnae with deeply incised, lanceolate lobes. The stipe is typically dark and slender, supporting a wide, spreading lamina that can reach significant dimensions. Sori are arranged in rows along the underside of the segments, appearing as small, circular clusters of sporangia. These reproductive structures are often protected by a distinct, thin indusium that may become inconspicuous as the plant matures. The overall texture of the foliage is delicate and somewhat translucent compared to other fern species.
