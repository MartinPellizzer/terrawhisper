Ophiopogon reversus

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiopogon reversus?

Ophiopogon reversus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Asparagaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Ophiopogon, with its specific designation being the species reversus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophiopogon reversus has linear, grass-like leaves that are characterized by a distinctively dark green upper surface and a lighter, silvery-green underside. The plant features small, fleshy roots that form a rhizomatous structure to anchor itself in the soil. During its flowering period, it produces inconspicuous white or pale pinkish flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are followed by the development of small, dark blue to blackish berries that hang close to the foliage.
