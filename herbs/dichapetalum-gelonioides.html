Dichapetalum gelonioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Dichapetalum gelonioides?

Dichapetalum gelonioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Dichapetalaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Dichapetalum and the species gelonioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichapetalum gelonioides has woody, erect shrubs reaching up to four meters in height characterized by simple, alternate, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically dark green and slightly leathery in texture, often possessing a somewhat shiny surface. Its flowers are actinomorphic and funnel-shaped, featuring five fused petals that transition from a pale greenish-white to a light pink hue. These blossoms are borne in terminal or axillary clusters and contain prominent stamens that extend from the floral tube. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that eventually turn a reddish color upon maturation.

What are the pharmacological activities of Dichapetalum gelonioides?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including potent anti-malarial and cytotoxic properties. Research indicates that its chemical constituents can effectively inhibit the growth of Plasmodium falciparum parasites. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates cytotoxic effects by inducing cell death in various cancer cell lines during laboratory studies. These biological actions are largely attributed to the presence of specific bioactive alkaloids and flavonoids found within its leaves. Such therapeutic potential makes it a valuable subject for further drug development and medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, the methanolic extracts of Dichapetalum gelonioides were evaluated for in vitro antimalarial activity against Plasmodium falciparum strains. The plant showed moderate activity against the chloroquine sensitive 3D7 strain with an IC50 of 19.15 µg/mL. Additionally, it displayed promising activity against the resistant Dd2 strain with an IC50 of 10.43 µg/mL. This assessment used Histidine-rich protein 2 based ELISA to determine the inhibitory effects.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds betulinic acid, dichapetalins A (1), dichapetalins I (2), dichapetalins J (3), and dichapetalins K (4). These specific chemical constituents are often studied for their potential biological activities and therapeutic properties. Betulinic acid is well known in pharmacological research for its anti-inflammatory and antitumor effects. The various dichapetalins belong to a group of specialized metabolites that contribute to the plant's unique chemical profile. Researchers analyze these specific molecules to better understand how the plant interacts with human biological systems.

As discussed in a study, the stem bark of Dichapetalum gelonioides collected in the Philippines contains several medicinal compounds including three dichapetalin-type triterpenoids known as dichapetalins A (1), I (2), and J (3). The plant also yields two dolabrane norditerpenoids (6, 7) as well as triterpenoids zeylanol (8), 28-hydroxyzeylanol (9), and betulinic acid. Additional isolation from re-collected material produced two further phenylpyranotriterpenoids identified as dichapetalins K (4) and L (5). While compounds 4-6 and the methyl ester 6a showed broad cytotoxic activity, dichapetalin A (1) was not active in an in vivo hollow fiber assay within the dose range of 1-6 mg/kg. Compounds 1-3 specifically exhibited promising selectivity against the SW626 human ovarian cancer cell line.
