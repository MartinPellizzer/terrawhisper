10 Possible Side Effects Of Geranium Nepalense (Nepalese Cranesbill)

Geranium nepalense, also known as Nepalese cranesbill, has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

The causes of these side effects are mainly due to the herb's saponins, essential oils, and rosmarinic acid, which can cause digestive problems, skin irritation, and exacerbate existing health conditions.

For instance, severe allergic reactions can worsen asthma symptoms, while interactions with blood thinners can increase the risk of bleeding, potentially leading to hospitalization.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Geranium nepalense if used imporperly.
