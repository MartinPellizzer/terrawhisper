10 Possible Side Effects Of Geranium Nepalense

Geranium nepalense, also known as Nepalese cranesbill, has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

The causes of these side effects are mainly due to the herb's saponins, essential oils, and rosmarinic acid, which can cause digestive problems, skin irritation, and exacerbate existing health conditions.

For instance, severe allergic reactions can worsen asthma symptoms, while interactions with blood thinners can increase the risk of bleeding, potentially leading to hospitalization.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Geranium nepalense causes allergic reactions when used due to its high concentration of allergenic compounds such as flavonoids and terpenes.

These substances can trigger an immune response in sensitive individuals, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and redness of the skin.

Additionally, some people may experience respiratory issues or gastrointestinal disturbances after exposure to Geranium nepalense.

2. Induces vomiting

Geranium nepalense induces vomiting in some individuals due to its irritating properties.

The essential oils present in the plant can stimulate the digestive system, causing an increase in stomach contractions and leading to nausea and vomiting.

This side effect is often temporary and may be more pronounced when using high doses or long-term exposure to the herb.

3. Stimulates uterine contractions

Geranium nepalense stimulates uterine contractions due to its high concentration of rosmarinic acid and other flavonoids.

These compounds are thought to mimic the effects of estrogen in the body, leading to increased contractions of the uterus.

This property has been traditionally used to treat menstrual disorders and induce labor, but it can also pose risks for individuals with a history of uterine issues or those taking medications that may interact with its effects.

4. Triggers cardiac arrhythmia

Geranium nepalense triggers cardiac arrhythmia.

This is due to the herb's ability to increase heart rate and blood pressure, which can put a strain on the cardiovascular system. The combination of geraniin, a compound found in the plant, with other bioactive molecules may cause irregular heartbeat or palpitations in some individuals.

As a result, people who are prone to arrhythmia or have pre-existing heart conditions should exercise caution when using Geranium nepalense.

5. Produces respiratory failure

Geranium nepalense produces respiratory failure due to its potent saponin content, which can cause bronchospasms and airway constriction.

The plant's volatile oil compounds may also irritate the lungs and airways, leading to inflammation and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the alkaloids present in Geranium nepalense may stimulate the respiratory system, causing an overactive response that can lead to respiratory failure.

6. Increases blood pressure

Geranium nepalense increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds have been shown to stimulate the body's natural production of vasopressin, a hormone that constricts blood vessels and raises blood pressure.

Additionally, the plant's alkaloid content may also contribute to this effect by increasing the heart rate and cardiac output, further exacerbating hypertension.

7. Stimulates bowel movement

Geranium nepalense stimulates bowel movement due to its natural laxative properties.

The plant contains saponins and flavonoids that stimulate the muscles in the intestines, increasing contractions and facilitating the passage of stool through the digestive system.

This can lead to an increase in frequency and ease of bowel movements, potentially alleviating symptoms of constipation or other gastrointestinal issues.

8. Causes dizziness and lightheadedness

Geranium nepalense causes dizziness and lightheadedness when used.

This is due to the presence of geraniol, a volatile oil that can affect blood flow and pressure, leading to temporary reductions in oxygen supply to the brain.

As a result, individuals may experience feelings of dizziness or lightheadedness, which can be uncomfortable but usually resolve on their own once the effects of the herb wear off.

9. Enhances menstrual bleeding

Geranium nepalense enhances menstrual bleeding by stimulating the uterine lining, which can lead to an increase in blood flow and a heavier-than-usual period.

This is due to the herb's ability to dilate blood vessels and relax uterine muscle tissue, allowing for more efficient shedding of the endometrium and resulting in longer or heavier periods.

10. Increases risk of overdose

Geranium nepalense increases risk of overdose.

This is because it is a potent herb that contains geraniin and other bioactive compounds, which can interact with medications and worsen certain health conditions when used in excess or improperly.

The risks are heightened for individuals taking sedatives, antidepressants, or blood thinners, as well as those with pre-existing liver or kidney damage.
