Cassipourea guianensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cassipourea guianensis?

Cassipourea guianensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Malpighiales and belongs to the family Rhizophoraceae. Within this family, it is identified under the genus Cassipourea, specifically as the species guianenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cassipourea guianenensis has woody stems, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense axillary clusters.

The foliage is typically characterized by a coriaceous texture and a deep green coloration on the upper surface. Its reproductive structures consist of small, actinomorphic flowers that often appear in small groups along the branches. The plant exhibits a branching habit that supports its dense canopy structure.

These morphological features are essential for its identification within its native tropical forest habitat.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

Some individuals might experience redness or itching where the plant has touched the body. Internal consumption without proper processing or scientific guidance poses risks of toxicity to the digestive system. Because the specific toxicological profile is not fully documented in all clinical studies, caution is essential when handling this species.
