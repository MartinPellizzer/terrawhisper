Polygonum amphibium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum amphibium has erect to ascending stems that reach up to 40 centimeters in height, characterized by cylindrical shapes and reddish-green pigmentation. The leaves are primarily lanceolate or narrowly elliptical, featuring smooth margins and an alternate arrangement along the stem. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often appearing in small groups near the leaf nodes. These flowers possess white to pinkish tepals and are typically much smaller than the surrounding foliage. The plant also develops small, ovoid fruits that turn from green to a reddish-brown color upon maturity.

While its primary range is centered in Europe, some populations have been introduced to other regions due to human activity. What are the therapeutic actions of Polygonum amphibium?

Polygonum amphibium has diuretic, astringent, and hemostatic properties that make it useful in traditional medicine for managing various physiological conditions. Its diuretic action helps increase urine production, which can assist in flushing toxins from the urinary tract. The astringent qualities allow the plant to contract body tissues, often helping to soothe inflamed mucous membranes. Furthermore, its hemostatic effects enable it to aid in the slowing or stopping of bleeding. These combined therapeutic actions support its historical use in treating inflammatory and circulatory issues.
