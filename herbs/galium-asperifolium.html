Galium asperifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Galium asperifoliuum has whorled, lanceolate leaves with coarsely serrated margins and a square-shaped stem. The foliage typically consists of six to eight leaves arranged in circles around the ascending stalks. These leaves possess a rough texture due to the prominent teeth along their edges. Small, white flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. The plant structure is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branched stems.
