Merremia hungaiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Merremia hungaiensis?

Merremia hungaiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Solanales and is a member of the family Convolvulaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Merremia and the specific species name hungaienis.

    What are the herbal preparations of Merremia hungaiensis?

    Merremia hungaiensis is prepared as a decoction, a tea, or a topical poultice using the plant's leaves and stems. Traditional practitioners often boil the plant material in water to extract its medicinal properties for oral consumption. This liquid preparation is frequently used to treat various internal ailments. For external applications, the crushed plant parts are applied directly to the skin as a paste. These diverse methods allow the plant to be used for both systemic and localized therapeutic purposes.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Merremia hungaiensis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains chemical compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon direct skin contact. When consumed, its alkaloids might lead to nausea, vomiting, or more severe digestive complications. There is also a potential for systemic toxicity if significant amounts are introduced into the body. Because specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, caution should be exercised when interacting with this species.
