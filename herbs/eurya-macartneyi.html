Eurya macartneyi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. This specific plant is a part of the family Pentaphylacaceae, falling under the genus Eurya, and is identified by the species epithet macartneyi.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, respiratory ailments, and digestive issues. Local traditional practitioners often apply its leaves to wounds or skin irritations to promote healing. The plant is also brewed into decoctions to alleviate symptoms of coughs and other lung-related discomforts. Furthermore, its components are believed to help soothe gastrointestinal distress when consumed in specific preparations. These medicinal uses are deeply rooted in the traditional knowledge of regional indigenous communities.

What are the herbal preparations of Eurya macartneyi?

Eurya macartneyi is prepared as a decoction, a topical poultice, or a concentrated liquid extract. The leaves are typically boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for internal consumption. For skin ailments, the crushed plant matter is applied directly to the affected area as a warm compress. Some practitioners also use the plant to produce tinctures by soaking the organic material in alcohol. These various methods allow the active compounds to be utilized for different therapeutic purposes.
