Clerodendrum indicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum indicum?

Clerodendrum indicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Clerodendrum, and its specific designation is identified by the species name indicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum indicum has opposite, ovate leaves with acuminate tips and a scabrous texture, woody stems, and small, white, tubular flowers arranged in terminal panicles. The foliage typically measures five to fifteen centimeters in length and features prominent veins on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of flowers that eventually produce small, fleshy, black drupes. The plant often exhibits a shrubby growth habit, reaching heights of up to two meters in suitable tropical environments. These morphological characteristics distinguish it from other species within the Verbenaceae family.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds betulinic acid, lupeol, oleanolic acid 3-acetate, taraxerol, and 3β-hydroxy-D:B-friedo-olean-5-ene. These specific triterpenoids are known for their diverse biological activities and potential therapeutic properties. Researchers study these molecules to understand their roles in anti-inflammatory and anticancer processes. The chemical structure of each substance contributes to the unique medicinal profile of the plant species. Such compounds are essential for developing new pharmacological treatments.

As discussed in a study, the roots of Clerodendrum indicum contain triterpenoids at a level of 0.28%. Among these, oleanolic acid 3-acetate and betulinic acid showed moderate to strong cytotoxicity against 5 human cancer cell lines with IC50 values of 1.66-20.49 µmol/L. The triterpenoids 3β-hydroxy-D:B-friedo-olean-5-ene and taraxerol were cytotoxic to the SW620 cell line with IC50 values of 23.39 and 2.09 µmol/L, respectively. Additionally, lupeol exhibited potent cytotoxicity on the SW620 cell line at 1.99 µmol/L and the KATO-III cell line at 1.95 µmol/L. The flavonoid pectolinarigenin showed moderate cytotoxicity with IC50 values of 13.05 and 24.31 µmol/L, while the steroid stigmasterol was effective against the SW620 cell line at 2.79 µmol/L. Finally, β-sitosterol was active against SW620 at 11.26 µmol/L, BT-474 at 14.11 µmol/L, and HepG2 cancer cells at 20.47 µmol/L.
