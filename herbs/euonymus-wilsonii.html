What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euonymus wilsonii has an erect, deciduous shrub habit characterized by opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves with finely serrated margins and a glossy upper surface. The plant typically reaches a height of one to two meters and features slender, woody stems that provide structural support. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters during the growing season, often appearing in the leaf axils. Following pollination, the plant produces distinctive four-lobed capsules that split open to reveal brightly colored arils surrounding the seeds. These morphological traits collectively define its identification within the Euonymus genus.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Euonymus wilsonii can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested. It is important to keep this plant away from children and pets to avoid accidental poisoning.
