What's the taxonomical classification of Euonymus wilsonii?

Euonymus wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Celastrales. This plant is a member of the family Celastraceae, falling under the genus Euonymus, and is specifically identified by its species name, wilsonii.

      What are the herbal preparations of Euonymus wilsonii?

      Euonymus wilsonii is prepared primarily as a decoction of the leaves, a concentrated liquid extraction made by boiling the plant material in water. This traditional method allows the active compounds to leach into the liquid for medicinal use. Some practitioners may also utilize dried leaf powder mixed into capsules for easier oral administration. Occasionally, topical preparations such as poultices or infused oils are created to address external issues. These various forms ensure that the plant's properties can be applied according to specific therapeutic needs.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Euonymus wilsonii can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested. Contact with the leaves or stems may trigger contact dermatitis, resulting in redness, itching, or a rash on sensitive skin. If swallowed, the alkaloids present in the plant can lead to nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. In more severe cases of ingestion, individuals might experience dizziness or more serious neurological symptoms. It is important to keep this plant away from children and pets to avoid accidental poisoning.
