Gentiana purdomii

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana purdomii?

Gentiana purdomii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it resides in the order Gentianales and the family Gentianaecae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Gentiana, and its unique species designation, purdomii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana purdomii has upright, perennial stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 15 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. Small, subtle veins often trace the surface of the corolla, adding fine detail to the floral structure. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the southern Andes within the central Peruvian highlands. It grows specifically in the alpine tundra zones of the Cordillera Vilcanota and surrounding mountain ranges. The species is typically restricted to moist, rocky crevices and grassy slopes found at elevations exceeding 4,000 meters. Its range is quite narrow, making it a localized specialist of these specific montane ecosystems.
