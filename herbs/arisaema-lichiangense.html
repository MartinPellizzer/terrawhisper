Finally, it is identified within the genus Arisaema as the specific species lichiangense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arisaema lichiangense has a morphology characterized by a solitary, spathe-like bract called a spathe that encloses a fleshy, central spadix, all arising from an underground tuberous rootstock. The plant typically produces large, cordate or sagittate leaves with prominent venation that emerge from a basal cluster. The spathe often exhibits a greenish or purplish hue and serves to protect the reproductive structures located within the spadix. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged tightly along the spadix, facilitating pollination through specialized chemical signals. As the plant matures, the spadix can become quite elongated and fleshy to support the developing berries.
