Eragrostis tenella

What's the taxonomical classification of Eragrostis tenella?

Eragrostis tenella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Poales. The plant is a member of the family Poaceae, which is further narrowed down to the genus Eragrostis, ultimately identifying the specific species as tenella.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Eragrostis tenella contains negligible amounts of documented medicinal compounds and is primarily classified as a common forage grass rather than a therapeutic plant. While specific phytochemical profiles are sparse in academic literature, grasses in this family typically possess basic structural carbohydrates and minor phenolic compounds. These constituents are more significant for supporting livestock digestion than for direct human pharmacology. Research into its specific bioactive properties remains extremely limited compared to more established medicinal herbs. Consequently, its value lies mostly in its nutritional contribution to grazing animals rather than its medicinal potential.
