Eragrostis tenella

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eragrostis tenella has slender, creeping stems that arise from a rhizomatous base and produce upright, branched flowering spikes. The leaves are narrow, linear, and typically possess a single midvein with a blunt tip. Each spikelet consists of several small, compressed florets arranged along a central axis. These spikelets are often characterized by their delicate, translucent appearance and small, pointed glumes. The plant typically grows in dense tufts or mats in moist, sandy environments.

Within these habitats, the species prefers moist to wet soils where it can establish itself among other aquatic vegetation.     What are the herbal preparations of Eragrostis tenella?

    Eragrostis tenella is prepared as a decoction, infusion, or topical poultice to harness its bioactive compounds. For internal use, the dried aerial parts are typically simmered in water to create a concentrated liquid extract. Infusions made from the leaves provide a milder tea option for gentle systemic support. When used externally, the crushed plant material is applied directly to the skin as a poultice to soothe local irritation. These diverse methods allow practitioners to tailor the dosage and delivery based on the intended therapeutic outcome.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Eragrostis tenella can cause severe allergic reactions such as contact dermatitis, skin rashes, and respiratory distress through contact with its pollen or oils. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience itching, redness, or blistering after touching the plant's leaves or stems. Inhalation of the airborne pollen can trigger hay fever symptoms, including sneezing, runny noses, and watery eyes. Some people might even experience more significant systemic responses like swelling or wheezing if exposure is prolonged. It is important to avoid direct contact to prevent these dermatological and respiratory complications.
