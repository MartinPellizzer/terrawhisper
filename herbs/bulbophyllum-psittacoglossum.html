Bulbophyllum psittacoglossum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum psittacoglossum has pseudobulbs that are ovoid or egg-shaped, single-leaved, and connected by a creeping rhizome. The leaves are typically elliptic or oblong in shape and emerge from the apex of each pseudobulb. Its flowers feature a highly distinctive, parrot-like labellum that is fleshy and deeply cleft. The sepals and petals are often elongated, contributing to the complex, specialized structure of the inflorescence. This morphology is specifically adapted to attract certain insect pollinators through its unique shape and color patterns.
