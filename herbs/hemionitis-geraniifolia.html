What is Hemionitis geraniifolia?

Hemionitis geraniifolia is a species of fern in the family Pteridaceae, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial, terrestrial plant with a rhizome and fronds that are divided into pinnae, which are further divided into pinnules. The fronds are typically 30-60 cm long, with pinnae that are 10-20 cm long and pinnules that are 1-2 cm long, and the plant has leathery, ovate-shaped frond bases. The plant's leaves are simple and have a leathery texture, with the upper surface being dark green and the lower surface being pale green.

Hemionitis geraniifolia produces sporangia in the sori on the underside of the fronds, which are used for reproduction.
