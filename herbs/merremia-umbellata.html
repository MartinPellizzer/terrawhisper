Merremia umbellata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Merremia umbellata has small, succulent, elliptical leaves that are arranged in tight, overlapping clusters at the ends of its stems. These leaves are typically fleshy and smooth, exhibiting a bright green color that aids in water storage. The plant features thin, creeping stems that allow it to spread across various surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These floral structures are often white or pale in hue, providing a soft contrast to the dense foliage.
