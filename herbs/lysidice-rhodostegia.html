Lysidice rhodostegia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysidice rhodostegia has lanceolate, serrated leaves arranged in an opposite phyllotaxy along a central woody stem. Its most striking feature is the cluster of bell-shaped, rose-colored flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Each blossom possesses five delicate petals and a slightly protruding stamen structure.
