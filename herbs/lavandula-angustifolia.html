English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Mediterranean Region, Southern Europe, and Northern Africa. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, oil infusions, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its antispasmodic, sedative, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Medicinal Actions

Antispasmodic
In herbal texts, considered a calming antispasmodic, in spasm-related situations.
Sedative
Traditionally described as a cooling sedative, for relaxation-oriented uses.
Astringent
Commonly referenced as a mild astringent, in drying-focused uses.
Diuretic
As described in traditional systems, a warming diuretic, in fluid-regulation contexts.
