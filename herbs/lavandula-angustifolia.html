Botanical Identification

English Lavender, scientifically known as Lavandula angustifolia, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is commonly referred to as true lavender. The plant features upright stems, narrow leaves, and spikes of purple flowers, with historical synonyms including "French lavender" and "English lavender" in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

English Lavender contains essential oil, terpenoids, and flavonoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to provide its therapeutic effects, with essential oils contributing to its calming properties. Terpenoids and flavonoids support anti-inflammatory and antioxidant actions, enhancing the herb's overall efficacy in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid

Preparation Methods

Essential oil: Used for skin irritation, anxiety, and insomnia due to its calming properties.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues, stress relief, and mild sedation.

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and as a mild sedative.

Safety Profile

English Lavender is generally safe when used in typical herbal doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks. Individuals with allergies to the Lamiaceae family should also exercise caution.
