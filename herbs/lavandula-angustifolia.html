What to know about Lavandula angustifolia before using it medicinally

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is a herb that has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and restlessness, as well as to treat mild to moderate depression.

The main health benefits of this herb include its ability to promote relaxation, reduce stress levels, and improve sleep quality. The main therapeutic properties of Lavandula angustifolia include its sedative, antiseptic, and anti-inflammatory properties, which contribute to its wide range of medicinal uses. The herb contains several bioactive compounds, including linalool, linalyl acetate, and camphor, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects.

People often make tea, essential oil, and topical preparations from the herb, such as lavender oil, which is commonly used in aromatherapy and skincare products.
