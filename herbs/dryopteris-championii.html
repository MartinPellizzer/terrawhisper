Dryopteris championii

What's the taxonomical classification of Dryopteris championii?

Dryopteris championii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Furthermore, its taxonomic hierarchy places it within the family Polypodiaceae, falling under the genus Dryopteris, and is specifically identified by its species name, championii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris championii has large, bipinnate to tripinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter and feature a dark brown, scaly stipe. The pinnules are typically ovate or oblong in shape with slightly serrated or lobed margins. These leaves are arranged in a dense rosette pattern arising from a stout, creeping rhizome covered in pale scales. The underside of the fronds contains small, circular sori protected by thin, round indusia. The overall texture of the foliage is somewhat leathery, contributing to its structural integrity in shaded habitats.
