Huperzia stricta

Huperzia stricta, commonly known as Chinese club moss, is a type of lycopodiophyta plant that belongs to the Huperziaceae family.

This plant has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for its health benefits, which include its potential to improve memory and cognitive function, as well as its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The therapeutic actions of Huperzia stricta include its ability to increase acetylcholine levels in the brain, which may help to alleviate symptoms of Alzheimer's disease and other neurodegenerative disorders.

The bioactive constituents of Huperzia stricta include huperzine A, a potent inhibitor of acetylcholinesterase, an enzyme that breaks down acetylcholine in the brain, as well as other alkaloids and flavonoids with potential medicinal properties.
