Salvia (Salvia officinalis) Compounds

Salvia officinalis contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Essential oils contribute to its aromatic profile and therapeutic effects. Terpenoids and phenolic acids are also present, offering anti-inflammatory benefits. Coumarin and glycosides support its use in herbal medicine for various health purposes.

Salvia (Salvia officinalis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Salvia officinalis. They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds support the therapeutic use of Salvia in traditional medicine. Flavonoids may enhance the plant's ability to address conditions like digestive issues and stress.

2. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Salvia officinalis, commonly known as common sage. It contains volatile aromatic compounds like cineole and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. The essential oil is often extracted through steam distillation from the leaves and flowers of the plant.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds found in Salvia officinalis. They contribute to the plant's aromatic profile and have various therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are responsible for many of the plant's traditional and modern medicinal uses.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Salvia officinalis. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Tannins in Salvia help in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. They also support the plant's overall therapeutic profile.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Salvia officinalis, commonly known as common sage. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in digestive and respiratory health. This compound is also involved in the plant's ability to protect against oxidative stress.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Salvia officinalis. It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and sedative properties. Found in the leaves and stems, coumarin also has mild anticoagulant effects. Its presence supports the traditional use of Salvia in calming the nervous system.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Salvia officinalis. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in Salvia are often responsible for its calming and antispasmodic effects. They are commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Salvia officinalis. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting mild detoxifying effects and supporting digestive health. Saponins in Salvia help reduce inflammation and may enhance the absorption of nutrients. These compounds are commonly found in the leaves and are valued in herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Salvia officinalis. It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound supports overall health by neutralizing free radicals and may aid in reducing inflammation. Its presence in Salvia highlights the plant's potential therapeutic benefits.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Salvia officinalis, commonly known as sage. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though it is not the primary active component. Alkaloids in sage are associated with cognitive and antimicrobial effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.
