Salvia (Salvia officinalis) Uses

Salvia officinalis is commonly used for gastrointestinal issues such as stomach ulcers, gastritis, and digestive discomfort. It helps soothe gastric distress and may reduce symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease. It also has antimicrobial properties that can address dental caries and gingivitis.

Salvia (Salvia officinalis) Monograph

1. Gastrointestinal discomfort

Salvia officinalis is used to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort such as indigestion, bloating, and nausea. Its antispasmodic properties help relax the digestive tract, while its mild sedative effects can ease stress-related digestive issues. It is often prepared as a tea to support digestive health and ease mild stomach upset.

2. Stomach ulcers

Salvia officinalis has been used traditionally to support digestive health. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties may help protect the stomach lining. Studies suggest it can reduce ulcer symptoms and promote healing. It is often used in herbal formulations for digestive support.

3. Gastric distress

Salvia officinalis is used to alleviate gastric distress by soothing the digestive tract and reducing inflammation. It helps relieve symptoms like bloating, indigestion, and nausea. Its antispasmodic properties ease stomach cramps, making it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for digestive discomfort.

4. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Salvia officinalis has been used traditionally to support digestive health. Its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties may help reduce symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease by soothing the esophageal lining and improving stomach emptying. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for this purpose.

5. Gastritis

Salvia officinalis has been traditionally used to support digestive health, particularly in managing gastritis. Its anti-inflammatory and carminative properties help reduce stomach irritation and soothe digestive discomfort. It is often prepared as a tea to alleviate symptoms and promote healing of the stomach lining.

6. Digestive discomfort

Salvia officinalis is used to alleviate digestive discomfort such as bloating, gas, and indigestion. Its antispasmodic properties help relax digestive muscles, while its mild bitterness stimulates digestion. It is often prepared as a tea to soothe the stomach and promote healthy digestion.

7. Diarrhea

Salvia officinalis is used to treat diarrhea due to its antimicrobial and astringent properties. It helps reduce intestinal inflammation and soothe the digestive tract. Infusions of the leaves are commonly prepared to alleviate symptoms and restore balance in the gastrointestinal system.

8. Gingivitis

Salvia officinalis has been used traditionally to treat gingivitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce plaque and inflammation in the gums. The essential oils in sage, such as thujone and camphor, contribute to its effectiveness in oral care. It can be used as a mouth rinse or topical application for gum health.

9. Gastric ulcer

Salvia officinalis has been traditionally used to treat gastric ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps reduce stomach acid and promote mucosal healing. Studies suggest its active compounds may inhibit ulcer-causing bacteria and protect the stomach lining. It is often used in herbal formulations for digestive support.

10. Dental caries

Salvia officinalis has been traditionally used to help prevent and treat dental caries due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds like thujone and camphor that inhibit the growth of bacteria responsible for tooth decay. It is often used in mouthwashes or as a gargle to reduce plaque and freshen breath. Regular use may support overall oral health.
