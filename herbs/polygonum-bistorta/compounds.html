Bistort (Polygonum bistorta) Compounds

Tannins and flavonoids are key compounds in Bistort, contributing to its astringent properties and antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids and glycosides support anti-inflammatory actions, while coumarin and anthocyanin provide additional antioxidant and anti-spasmodic benefits. Saponins, terpenoids, and essential oils enhance its digestive and antimicrobial properties. Alkaloids contribute to its overall medicinal potency.

Bistort (Polygonum bistorta) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Bistort (Polygonum bistorta). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its ability to reduce inflammation and support digestive health. Tannins in Bistort are also valued for their potential role in wound healing and as a natural preservative.

Flavonoids in Bistort (Polygonum bistorta) contribute to its traditional use in treating digestive and inflammatory conditions.
