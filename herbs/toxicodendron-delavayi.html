What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Toxicodendron delavayi has compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets, woody stems with grayish bark, and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense panicles. The leaflets are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape and feature serrated margins along their edges. These leaves are arranged alternately along the branches, contributing to the plant's dense canopy structure. The stems can become quite thick and gnarled as the plant matures into a large shrub or small tree. Small, drupaceous fruits develop from the flowers, eventually turning a dark color when ripe.
