        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Paragymnopteris vestita can cause severe contact dermatitis, localized skin inflammation, and acute respiratory irritation if its spores are inhaled. Some individuals may also experience swelling of the eyes and mucous membranes if the plant is handled without adequate protection. These reactions are typically most intense when the plant is crushed or disturbed during growth. Prolonged contact with the biological compounds in the leaves can lead to secondary infections or long-term sensitivity.
