Poikilospermum suaveolens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Poikilospermum suaveolens has woody, perennial stems, small elliptical to ovate leaves, and tiny, fragrant white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense terminal clusters. The foliage is typically characterized by a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface that helps retain moisture. Its leaves are arranged oppositely along the branches, providing a symmetrical appearance to the plant's structure. The inflorescences consist of numerous minute blossoms that emit a distinctively sweet scent to attract pollinators.
