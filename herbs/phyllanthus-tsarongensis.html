Phyllanthus tsarongensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllanthus tsarongensis?

Phyllanthus tsarongensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its order, Malpighiales, it falls into the family Phyllanthaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Phyllanthus, with its specific identification being the species tsarongensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllanthus tsarongensis has a woody shrub-like growth habit characterized by small, lanceolate leaves arranged in a distichous pattern along slender branches. The leaves are typically thin and possess entire margins with fine venation visible on the surface. Its flowers are minute and often appear in the axils of the leaves, appearing either solitary or in small clusters. The fruit is a small capsule that develops from the floral structure following successful pollination. These morphological features are essential for distinguishing the species from other members of its genus.
