Viola arcuata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola arcuata has a prostrate or ascending growth habit characterized by slender, creeping stems and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves with crenate margins. The foliage is typically sparsely distributed along the stems, often appearing in small clusters. Its flowers are relatively minute and exhibit a zygomorphic structure typical of the Violaceae family. The petals are often pale in color, frequently featuring subtle violet or white hues with delicate veining. Reproductive structures include a small capsule that develops from the ovary after successful pollination.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically spanning across parts of India, Nepal, Bhutan, and China. It primarily grows in alpine and subalpine zones where the terrain is rocky or grassy. These violet species are often found at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters above sea level. Their distribution is limited by the specific requirement for cold, montane climates and well-drained soil. Consequently, they are most frequently located on steep mountain slopes and meadowlands throughout the Tibetan Plateau and surrounding ranges.
