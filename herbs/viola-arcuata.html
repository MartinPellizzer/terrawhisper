What's the taxonomical classification of Viola arcuata?

Viola arcuata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized under Equisetopsida, which further descends into the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Violaceae. Finally, this plant is situated within the genus Viola, specifically identified by its unique species name, arcuata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola arcuata has a prostrate or ascending growth habit characterized by slender, creeping stems and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves with crenate margins. The foliage is typically sparsely distributed along the stems, often appearing in small clusters. Its flowers are relatively minute and exhibit a zygomorphic structure typical of the Violaceae family. The petals are often pale in color, frequently featuring subtle violet or white hues with delicate veining.
