According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", the stem bark of Calpocalyx dinklagei contains two previously undescribed aryl benzofuran derivatives named dinklagein A and B, alongside 8 known compounds. Among the isolates, dinklagein A displayed remarkably potent inhibitory activity against the production of nitric oxide in LPS induced RAW264.7 macrophages. This compound dose dependently suppressed LPS-stimulated iNOS expression at both mRNA and protein levels. Additionally, dinklagein A attenuated IL-1β release as well as the mRNA expressions of IL-1β and COX-2 at low doses. These findings suggest that dinklagein A can be developed as a natural, multi-target agent against several inflammatory diseases.
