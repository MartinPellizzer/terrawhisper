10 Possible Side Effects Of Uncaria Tomentosa

Uncaria tomentosa has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, diarrhea, and vomiting, which may be caused by excessive dosage or sensitivity to the herb's alkaloids.

These side effects can worsen people's lives by disrupting daily routines, affecting mental health, and straining relationships due to mood swings and irritability.

In severe cases, it may lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalance.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Uncaria tomentosa causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its unique composition of alkaloids and other bioactive compounds.

The most common allergen is a group of compounds called quinovic acid glycosides, which can trigger an immune response in susceptible people, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, Uncaria tomentosa's high concentration of antioxidants may also contribute to allergic reactions by stimulating the production of histamine, a chemical mediator that plays a key role in the body's response to allergens.

2. Triggers digestive issues

Uncaria tomentosa triggers digestive issues due to its high concentration of alkaloids and glycosides.

These compounds can irritate the stomach lining and intestines, leading to symptoms such as nausea, bloating, and diarrhea.

Additionally, Uncaria tomentosa may stimulate the gut, causing an imbalance in the natural bacteria flora, further exacerbating gastrointestinal disturbances.

3. Induces dizziness

Uncaria tomentosa induces dizziness due to its potential interaction with certain medications and its ability to affect blood pressure.

The herb contains alkaloids that can stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to increased heart rate and blood pressure, which may cause dizziness in some individuals.

Additionally, Uncaria tomentosa may enhance the effects of other substances that also lower blood pressure, exacerbating the likelihood of dizziness.

4. Raises blood pressure

Uncaria tomentosa raises blood pressure by stimulating the body's sympathetic nervous system, which can lead to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

This is due to the plant's high levels of alkaloids, particularly uncarine and quaternary alkaloids, which have been shown to have a direct effect on the cardiovascular system.

5. Increases heart rate

Uncaria tomentosa increases heart rate due to its high levels of alkaloids, particularly quinovic acid glycosides.

These compounds stimulate the nervous system, causing a rise in blood pressure and heart rate.

This effect is attributed to Uncaria's ability to increase the activity of neurotransmitters such as norepinephrine and epinephrine, leading to enhanced sympathetic tone and increased cardiovascular stimulation.

6. Produces respiratory distress

Uncaria tomentosa produces respiratory distress due to its ability to stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical mediator that can cause bronchoconstriction and airway inflammation.

This reaction may lead to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath, especially in individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions or allergies.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and flavonoids, are thought to be responsible for these effects.

7. Worsens kidney function

Uncaria tomentosa worsens kidney function by potentially increasing the risk of nephrotoxicity due to its high content of alkaloids such as quaternary bases and glycosides.

These compounds can accumulate in the kidneys and cause damage to the nephrons, leading to decreased glomerular filtration rate and increased levels of waste products in the blood.

8. Aggravates liver damage

Uncaria tomentosa aggravates liver damage due to its high concentration of alkaloids and glycosides.

These compounds can increase oxidative stress and inflammation in the liver, exacerbating existing damage or potentially causing harm in individuals with compromised liver function.

Additionally, Uncaria tomentosa may interfere with the body's natural detoxification processes, further contributing to liver dysfunction.

9. Initiates skin rashes

Uncaria tomentosa initiates skin rashes due to its high content of alkaloids and glycosides, which can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals.

These compounds may also irritate the skin, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation.

Additionally, the herb's potential interaction with other substances, such as blood thinners, may contribute to the development of skin rashes.

10. Causes nervousness

Uncaria tomentosa causes nervousness due to its stimulatory effect on the central nervous system.

The alkaloids and saponins present in the herb can increase heart rate and blood pressure, leading to feelings of anxiety and jitteriness.

Additionally, the plant's ability to affect serotonin levels in the brain may contribute to its nervy effects, as an imbalance of this neurotransmitter is often linked to states of heightened anxiety.
