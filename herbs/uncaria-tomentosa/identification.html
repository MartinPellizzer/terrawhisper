Botanical Identification of Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa)

To identify Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Chinese Tea Vine, or Uncaria tomentosa, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the flowering plants, eudicots, and the Rubiales order. It is part of the Rubiaceae family, commonly known as the coffee family, and is classified under the genus Uncaria with the species Uncaria tomentosa.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chinese Tea Vine is Uncaria tomentosa, first described by Linnaeus in 1782. It is also known by several synonyms, including Uncaria tomentosa and Uncaria tomentosa. Common names include Chinese Tea Vine, Spanish shrub, and wild tea. It is referred to by regional and historical names in various cultures, reflecting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa) is a climbing shrub with a woody stem and a fibrous root system. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and hairy, while its inflorescence is a raceme of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing multiple seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa) can be distinguished by its climbing habit, hirsute stems, and opposite, trifoliate leaves. It has small white flowers and red berries, setting it apart from similar species like Uncaria guianensis, which has smoother stems and different leaf venation patterns.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa) is native to the Amazon rainforests of South America. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and rainfall. It prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 200 and 800 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chinese Tea Vine blooms from April to June, producing small white or greenish flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, harvest timing is typically in late summer to early autumn when the plant is mature and the leaves are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Uncaria tomentosa belongs to the Rubiaceae family, sharing close phylogenetic relationships with species like Uncaria guianensis and Uncaria rhynchophylla. It is closely related to other members of the genus Uncaria, which are characterized by similar morphological and chemical traits. These relationships are supported by molecular and morphological analyses, highlighting its position within the subfamily Rubioideae.
