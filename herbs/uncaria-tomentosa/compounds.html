Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa) Compounds

The primary compounds in Chinese Tea Vine include alkaloids, which have stimulant properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Tannins contribute to astringency, while glycosides may support cardiovascular health. Phenolic acids and anthocyanins offer anti-inflammatory benefits, and saponins can aid in digestion.

Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of naturally occurring compounds found in Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa). These compounds, including pentacyclic and iridoid types, contribute to the plant's pharmacological properties, such as antihypertensive and anti-inflammatory effects. They are key to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for immune modulation and metabolic support.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to support digestive health and reduce inflammation. Tannins also play a role in the plant's traditional use for managing stress and enhancing mental clarity.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential effects on the nervous system and circulatory health. Glycosides in Uncaria tomentosa are believed to support cardiovascular function and may have calming properties.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support immune function. This compound is often studied for its potential role in reducing inflammation and enhancing overall health. Its presence in Uncaria tomentosa highlights the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin may also support cardiovascular health and aid in the treatment of conditions like hypertension and anxiety.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin also plays a role in the plant's ability to support cardiovascular and immune system functions.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's traditional use in supporting immune health and reducing oxidative stress.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The oil is extracted from the plant's aerial parts and is valued for its aromatic and therapeutic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and support cellular health. This compound is particularly valued for its role in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting effects.
