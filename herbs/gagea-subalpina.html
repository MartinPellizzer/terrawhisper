    What is Gagea subalpina?

    Gagea subalpina is a species of flowering plant in the family Liliaceae.

    It is a perennial herb that grows 10-15 cm tall, with a few linear to lance-shaped leaves that are 2-5 mm wide and 5-10 cm long, often with a purple or reddish tint. The plant produces a single, nodding, yellow flower, 1.5-2 cm in diameter, with 6 petals and 6 stamens, usually in late spring. The flowers are followed by a capsule, 2-3 cm long, containing 3-5 seeds, each 1-2 mm long.

    Gagea subalpina is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, specifically in the mountain ranges of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan.
