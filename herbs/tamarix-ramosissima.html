Tamarix ramosissima

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tamarix ramosissima has slender, scale-like leaves arranged in dense, overlapping patterns along thin, wiry branches that form a bushy or spreading habit. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clustered spikes, typically displaying shades of pink or white. Its woody stems are highly branched and often exhibit a reddish or grayish tint depending on the age of the growth. The overall architecture is characterized by a fine, feathery texture created by the minute foliage. This delicate structure allows the shrub to thrive in arid environments where wind resistance is beneficial.
