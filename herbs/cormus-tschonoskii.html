Cormus tschonoskii

Cormus tschonoskii, also known as the Japanese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its bark, which contains flavonoids and phenolic acids that exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities.

The health benefits of Cormus tschonoskii include its potential to lower blood pressure, improve cardiovascular health, and have antimicrobial effects, making it a popular ingredient in traditional herbal remedies.

Traditionally, the bark of the plant is used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.
