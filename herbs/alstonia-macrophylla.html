Alstonia macrophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Alstonia macrophylla?

Alstonia macrophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Gentianaales and falls under the family Apocynaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Alstonia, with its specific designation being the species macrophylla.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Alstonia macrophylla contains a diverse profile of secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its pharmacological activities. These bioactive compounds are primarily concentrated within the leaves and bark of the plant. Research indicates that these chemical constituents provide significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize extracts of the plant to treat various ailments such as skin infections and digestive issues. The specific synergy between these organic compounds allows the plant to serve as a potent natural therapeutic agent.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alstonia macrophylla?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, digestive issues, and inflammatory conditions. The leaves and bark are often prepared as decoctions or topical applications to address localized ailments. It is frequently employed in traditional medicine to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of minor cuts. Some practitioners also utilize its properties to alleviate stomach discomfort and gastrointestinal distress. Its bioactive compounds are believed to possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory qualities essential for these treatments.
