Dregea volubilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dregea volubilis?

Dregea volubiilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Apocynaceae, specifically within the genus Dregea, where it is identified as the species volubilis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dregea volubiilis contains various bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, saponins, flavonoids, and terpenoids. These secondary metabolites are primarily concentrated within the plant's leaves and roots. Research indicates that these chemical constituents contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. Some studies suggest that the presence of specific alkaloids may offer antimicrobial properties. The complex synergy of these compounds is central to the plant's perceived therapeutic potential in ethnobotanical practices.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dregea volubilis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, intestinal worms, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves and stems are often prepared into decoctions or pastes to apply directly to sores and dermatological issues. It is also utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate digestive discomfort caused by parasitic infections. Some practitioners employ the plant to reduce swelling and manage pain associated with systemic inflammation. These medicinal applications rely on the specific phytochemical properties found within its climbing vines.
