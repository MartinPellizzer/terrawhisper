Spiranthes sinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spiranthes sineensis has an herbaceous perennial structure characterized by a basal rosette of linear or lanceolate leaves and a terminal flowering spike composed of small, white, spiral-arranged flowers. The plant features a thickened underground rhizome that provides nutrient storage for its seasonal growth. Each individual flower possesses a delicate, tubular perianth with three outer tepals and three inner tepals that form a small, rounded lip. The inflorescence is typically cylindrical and exhibits a distinct clockwise or counter-clockwise twist as the blossoms ascend the stem. These flowering stems can reach varying heights depending on the environmental conditions and the age of the root system.
