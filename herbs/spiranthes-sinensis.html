Spiranthes sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Spiranthes sinensis?

Spiranthes sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, which places it within the genus Spiranthes, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spiranthes sineensis has an herbaceous perennial structure characterized by a basal rosette of linear or lanceolate leaves and a terminal flowering spike composed of small, white, spiral-arranged flowers. The plant features a thickened underground rhizome that provides nutrient storage for its seasonal growth. Each individual flower possesses a delicate, tubular perianth with three outer tepals and three inner tepals that form a small, rounded lip. The inflorescence is typically cylindrical and exhibits a distinct clockwise or counter-clockwise twist as the blossoms ascend the stem. These flowering stems can reach varying heights depending on the environmental conditions and the age of the root system.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a variety of bioactive medicinal compounds including spiranthesphenanthrenes A-F, phenanthrenes, dihydrophenanthrenes, flavonoids, and a bibenzyl compound. These specialized chemical constituents are responsible for the plant's diverse pharmacological properties. The phenanthrenes and their dihydro derivatives serve as significant structural components within its chemical profile. Additionally, the presence of flavonoids contributes to the antioxidant potential of the species. The specific bibenzyl compound further enhances the complex medicinal makeup of the organism.

According to a study published by "Journal of agricultural and food chemistry", Spiranthes sinensis contains six novel phenanthrenes called spiranthesphenanthrenes A-F (1-6) and 19 known compounds. These known compounds include seven phenanthrenes (7-13), one bibenzyl compound (14), five flavonoids (15-16 and 20-22), and six simple phenolic compounds (17-19 and 23-25). Compounds 1 and 7 exhibited moderate cytotoxic activities toward selected cancer cell lines with IC50 values ranging from 19.0 ± 7.3 to 30.2 ± 5.6 μM. Specifically, spiranthesphenantherene A (1) showed a higher cytotoxic activity than cisplatin toward the B16-F10 cell line with an IC50 of 19.0 ± 7.3 μM. Additionally, compound 1 inhibited the migration of B16-F10 cancer cells when treated with 2.5, 5, and 10 μM solutions for 24 and 48 h.
