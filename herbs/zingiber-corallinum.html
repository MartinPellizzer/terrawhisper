Zingiber corallinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Zingiber corallinum?

Zingiber corallinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Zingiberales and resides within the family Zingiberaceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Zingiber, with its specific designation being the species corallinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its foliage consists of bright green, elongated leaves that typically grow in an alternate pattern along the stem. The inflorescence is characterized by its delicate coloration and compact structure, which distinguishes it from other members of the ginger family.
