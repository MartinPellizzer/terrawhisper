Acanthocalyx alba

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acanthocalyx alba has a globular to short-cylindrical stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters and is characterized by prominent, closely spaced ribs. These ribs are adorned with sharp, dark spines that emerge from distinct areoles distributed across the plant's surface. The epidermis often exhibits a pale, whitish, or glaucous coloration which provides a stark contrast to the dark spines. Small, nocturnal white flowers emerge from the sides of the stem near the apex during its blooming period. This cactus species maintains a compact, slow-growing structure typical of many desert succulents.
