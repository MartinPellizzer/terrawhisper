What's the taxonomical classification of Cleyera japonica?

Cleyera japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Ericales and into the family Pentaphylacaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cleyera, and its unique species designation, japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cleyera japonica has a creeping, rhizomatous growth habit characterized by slender, reddish stems that produce opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. These leaves typically measure between 2 and 5 centimeters in length and possess thin, delicate textures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the growing season. Following flowering, it develops tiny, globose fruits that contribute to its spreading nature across forest floors. This low-growing morphology allows the species to form dense mats in shaded, moist environments.
