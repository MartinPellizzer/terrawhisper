What is Prestonia cyaniphylla?

Prestonia cyaniphylla is a species of plant in the family Apocynaceae, native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial climber with a woody stem, reaching up to 5 meters in height, and has simple, ovate, or lanceolate leaves that are 5-15 centimeters long and 2-5 centimeters wide. The flowers are small, white, and funnel-shaped, occurring in axillary clusters, while the fruit is a greenish-yellow, berry-like capsule containing numerous seeds. The plant has a milky latex, a characteristic common to many Apocynaceae species.

Prestonia cyaniphylla is a woody climber that grows in tropical rainforests and secondary forests, typically at elevations between 100 and 1,500 meters.
