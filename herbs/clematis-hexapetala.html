Clematis hexapetala

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis hexapetala?

Clematis hexapetala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Ranunculales. It is further categorized into the family Ranunculaceae and falls under the genus Clematis, ultimately reaching its specific classification as the species hexapetala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis hexapetala has slender, climbing stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaflets that typically consist of three to five leaflets per leaf. The plant produces distinctive flowers characterized by six petal-like sepals that are pale yellow or cream in color. These sepals surround a central cluster of numerous stamens which contribute to the floral structure. The leaves are arranged alternately along the vines and often feature finely serrated margins. Small, hairy structures can sometimes be found on the stems and petioles to assist in climbing.

As discussed in a study, Clematis hexapetala Pall. is a type of traditional Chinese medicine used to expel wind and dispel dampness. In modern clinical application, this herb demonstrates anti-inflammatory and analgesic activity. Research shows that crude and wine processed RC from this plant provides a good anti-inflammatory effect by reducing NO in RAW264.7 macrophages. Furthermore, it ameliorates inflammatory infiltration and cartilage surface erosion in CIA rats. The plant works by inhibiting glycolysis through the down-regulation of PKM2, which causes less glucose uptake and lactic acid to result in less ROS and higher MMP to normal.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds anemonin, beta-sitosterol, daucosterol, friedelin, and isolariciresinol. These specific phytochemicals are known to contribute to the diverse therapeutic properties observed in the species. The presence of sterols like beta-sitosterol and daucosterol often links the plant to anti-inflammatory activities. Additionally, compounds such as friedelin and isolariciresinol may play roles in various biological processes. Anemonin further adds to the complex chemical profile used in pharmacological studies.

As discussed in a study, the roots and rhizomes of Clematis hexapetala were analyzed to identify chemical constituents through solvent extraction, silica gel chromatography, and HPLC. A total of 9 compounds were isolated from the plant for the first time. These isolated compounds include friedelin (1), anemonin (2), beta-sitosterol (3), palmitic acid (4), vanillic acid (5), isolariciresinol (6), 5-hydroxumethyl-5H-furan-2-one (7), n-nonane (8), and daucosterol (9). The structures of these 9 substances were determined using spectral analysis.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating conditions characterized by intense, burning pain and inflammatory processes such as rheumatoid arthritis. It is frequently applied in homeopathic practices to alleviate the throbbing sensations associated with joint swelling and stiffness. Practitioners often recommend it for patients experiencing sudden, sharp pains that feel as though they are deep within the bone or muscle. The remedy is also considered useful for managing localized inflammations that present with significant heat and redness. Its application focuses on relieving the physical distress caused by these acute inflammatory symptoms.

As discussed in a study, Clematis hexapetala Pall. is one of the plants used to derive Clematidis Radix et Rhizoma. This herb has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and analgesic activity in modern clinical applications. In experiments involving RAW264.7 macrophages and CIA rats, crude and wine processed RC demonstrated a good anti-inflammatory effect by reducing NO. The treatment works by inhibiting glycolysis through the down-regulation of PKM2, which results in less glucose uptake and lactic acid. This process leads to less ROS and higher MMP levels to normal. Ultimately, Clematis hexapetala Pall. derived components ameliorate rheumatoid arthritis by inhibiting glycolysis and modulating gut microbiota together.
