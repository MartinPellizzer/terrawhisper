Dracaena cambodiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Dracaena cambodiana?

Dracaena cambodiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Asparagaceae, specifically within the genus Dracaena, and it is identified by the specific epithet cambodiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracaena cambodiana has slender, upright woody stems that support clusters of narrow, lanceolate leaves arranged in dense terminal rosettes. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color with subtle longitudinal stripes or variegation depending on the specific cultivar. Each leaf is characterized by a smooth, entire margin and a tapering tip that extends into a fine point. The plant's growth habit is characterized by its slow development and the formation of a single or branched trunk over time. According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Dracaena cambodiana Pierre ex Gagnep. is one of 6 Dracaena plants evaluated regarding its phenolic constituents and pharmacological activities. Modern pharmacological evaluations of these Dracaena species have disclosed effects including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, hypolipidemic, hypoglycemic, and cytotoxic effects. These plants also demonstrate bi-directional regulation effects on hemorheology, as well as cardiovascular and cerebrovascular effects. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the unique medicinal compound cambodianol along with the specific Loureirin derivatives Loureirin A, Loureirin B, and Loureirin D. These bioactive constituents are primarily found within the resinous substance known as dragon's blood. Researchers study these chemical profiles to understand the plant's potential pharmacological properties. The presence of these particular Loureiirins distinguishes this species from other members of its genus. Such chemical complexity makes the plant a subject of interest in natural product chemistry.

According to a study published by "Journal of Asian natural products research", the stems of Dracaena cambodiana contain a new homoisoflavonoid named cambodianol (1). This plant also contains two known flavanes, (2S)-7,3'-dihydroxy-4'-methoxy-8-methylflavane (2) and (2R)-7,4'-dihydroxy-8-methylflavane (3). The compound 1 exhibited significant cytotoxic activities against K562 and SGC-7901 cells. These activities were measured with IC50 values of 1.4 microg/ml and 2.9 microg/ml, respectively.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating chronic inflammation, general inflammation, microbial infections, swellings, and tumors. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed to reduce systemic irritation within the body. The plant also acts as a natural defense against various microbial infections. Practitioners may apply it to reduce localized swellings caused by injury or disease. Additionally, it is studied for its potential role in managing the growth of a tumor.

According to a study published by "Acta pharmaceutica Sinica. B", Dracaena cambodiana contains isomers of the biflavonoid 8-methylsocotrin-4'-ol which are used to limit inflammation. This process works by targeting 5-lipoxygenase and switching the lipid mediator profile from leukotrienes to specialized pro-resolving mediators. Elucidation of the absolute configurations of these compounds revealed that the 2S,gammaS-isomer is the most active form. The study shows that leukotriene production is suppressed in activated human neutrophils, macrophages, and blood. This mechanism was also evident in mouse peritonitis in vivo and resulted in a substantial decrease in immune cell infiltration.
