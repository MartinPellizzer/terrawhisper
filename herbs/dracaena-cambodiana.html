The plant's growth habit is characterized by its slow development and the formation of a single or branched trunk over time.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Dracaena cambodiana can cause skin irritation, mouth swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or pets. The plant contains saponins which are toxic compounds that trigger adverse reactions upon contact or consumption. If a person touches the leaves, they may experience redness or a stinging sensation on the skin. Animals that eat the foliage often suffer from vomiting, drooling, or abdominal pain. Contact with the eyes can also lead to significant discomfort and inflammation.
