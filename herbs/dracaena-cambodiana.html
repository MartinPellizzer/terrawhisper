Dracaena cambodiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Dracaena cambodiana?

Dracaena cambodiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Asparagaceae, specifically within the genus Dracaena, and it is identified by the specific epithet cambodiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

The plant's growth habit is characterized by its slow development and the formation of a single or branched trunk over time. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Loureirin A, Loureirin B, Loureirin D, resveratrol, and pterostilbene. These specific polyphenolic substances are known for their significant biological activities in various therapeutic studies. The Loureirin series of compounds often demonstrates unique structural properties that contribute to their medicinal potential. Additionally, the presence of resveratrol and pterostilbene provides potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. Together, these phytochemicals make the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological research.

As discussed in a study, Dracaena cambodiana contains five main compositions of dragon's blood. Field tests conducted for ten months showed that the contents of Loureirin D, 4,4'-dihydroxy-2'-dimethoxychalcone, Loureirin A, and Loureirin B in fungal-inoculated materials were 1.67 to 2.85 times greater than those in natural samples. Additionally, the content of 4,4'-dihydroxy-2'6'-dimethoxydihydrochalcone in each fungal-inoculated sample was more than 2 times that of each control group. The study investigated the effects of two fungi, namely BJDC01 and BJDC05, on these compositions. These results indicate that both BJDC01 and BJDC05 can stimulate the accumulation of the medicinal components in the plant.
