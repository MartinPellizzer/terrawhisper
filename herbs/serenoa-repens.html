Saw palmetto, scientifically known as Serenoa repens, is an herb that has been prized for centuries for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to improve urinary function and alleviate symptoms of benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH).

In addition to its medicinal uses, saw palmetto is also used in various culinary dishes, particularly in traditional Caribbean and North American cuisines, where it's often added to soups, stews, and sauces for its rich, slightly sweet flavor. As a horticultural plant, saw palmetto is a low-maintenance, slow-growing palm that thrives in warm, humid environments. From a botanical perspective, saw palmetto belongs to the family Arecaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fan-shaped leaves and small, purple berries.

With roots dating back to ancient Native American and European herbal traditions, saw palmetto has been a highly valued herb for centuries, with recorded references as far back as the 16th century.
