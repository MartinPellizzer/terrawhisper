What to know about Serenoa repens before using it medicinally

Serenoa repens, commonly known as saw palmetto, is a medicinal herb primarily used to address conditions such as benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) and androgenetic alopecia.

The main health benefits associated with this herb include improving urinary flow and reducing symptoms of BPH, as well as promoting hair growth and reducing male pattern baldness. The therapeutic properties of saw palmetto include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiproliferative effects, which contribute to its potential in treating various health conditions. The herb is rich in bioactive compounds such as free fatty acids, in particular, beta-sitosterol, which is believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties.

Saw palmetto is often prepared as a dietary supplement, with the most common herbal preparation being an extract of the fruit, which is often standardized to contain a specific amount of beta-sitosterol.

This effect of Serenoa repens may explain the beneficial effect of its extract in human benign prostatic hypertrophy.
