Lythrum (Lythrum salicaria) Compounds

The primary active compounds in Lythrum salicaria include flavonoids, tannins, phenolic acids, and anthocyanins, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarins and glycosides may support digestive and circulatory health, while saponins and terpenoids have potential detoxifying effects. Essential oils and alkaloids add to its diverse therapeutic profile.

Lythrum (Lythrum salicaria) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Lythrum salicaria, a medicinal plant used in herbal medicine. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use for respiratory and digestive health. These compounds are found in the flowers and leaves, enhancing the plant's therapeutic potential.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Lythrum salicaria, commonly known as purple loosestrife. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its ability to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. Tannins also help in the binding of proteins, supporting digestive health and reducing intestinal bleeding.

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Lythrum salicaria. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports the traditional use of Lythrum in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Its presence enhances the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Lythrum salicaria, commonly known as red deadnettle. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its mild anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is also responsible for the plant's characteristic sweet, hay-like aroma.

5. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Lythrum salicaria, is a powerful antioxidant responsible for the plant's vibrant purple color. It supports cardiovascular health and may reduce inflammation, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Terpenoid
