What is Zornia diphylla?

Zornia diphylla is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Fabaceae, native to tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America.

It grows up to 1 meter tall, with a woody taproot and branching stems that are pubescent and slightly hairy. The leaves are alternate, pinnate, with 2-4 pairs of leaflets and a terminal leaflet, measuring 6-10 cm long and 4-6 cm wide, with a leathery texture and serrated margins. The inflorescence is a terminal spike of small, pink to purple flowers, 3-5 mm long, with 5 petals and 10 stamens.

The fruit is a pod, 2-3 cm long and 8-10 mm wide, containing 2-4 seeds.
