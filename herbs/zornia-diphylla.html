Zornia diphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Zornia diphylla?

Zornia diphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, and its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Zornia and the species diphylla.

What are the pharmacological activities of Zornia diphylla?

This plant has significant antimicrobial and spasmolytic pharmacological activities that contribute to its medicinal potential. Research indicates that its extracts can effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates potent spasmolytic properties by relaxing smooth muscle tissues. These biological actions suggest that the species may be useful in treating infections and gastrointestinal disorders. Such diverse activities highlight the complex chemical composition found within its botanical structure.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", chloroform-methanol (1:1) extracts derived from Zornia diphylla Pers. (Fabaceae) were investigated for their effects on the spontaneous contractions of isolated rat ileum. The data showed that the extract produces a concentration-dependent inhibition of these spontaneous ileum contractions. Concentration-response curves and IC50 values were obtained to evaluate the relaxant potency of the plant. Additionally, the antimicrobial activity of the extracts was tested against pathogenic enterobacteria. These pharmacological actions support the ethnomedical use of the plant for the treatment of gastrointestinal disorders.
