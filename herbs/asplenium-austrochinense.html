Asplenium austrochinense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium austrochinenense has a perennial growth habit characterized by a creeping rhizome and large, pinnate fronds that can reach significant lengths. The leaves are composed of multiple pairs of oblong to lanceolate pinnae arranged along a central rachis. Each individual leaflet features finely serrated margins and a distinct midvein that extends toward the apex. The texture of the foliage is typically leathery, providing structural support for the expansive surface area. Sori are located on the undersides of the pinnae, protected by thin, kidney-shaped indusia.
