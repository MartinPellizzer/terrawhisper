What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Draba eriopoda has a low-growing, perennial habit characterized by dense, woolly white hairs covering its leaves, stems, and reproductive structures. The basal rosette consists of small, spatulate leaves that are heavily felted with trichomes to provide protection against harsh environments. During its flowering phase, the plant produces small, yellowish-white petals on short pedicels arising from the stem. The siliques, or seed pods, are relatively short and are typically tucked within the protective, hairy calyx.
