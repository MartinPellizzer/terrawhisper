Heracleum scabridum

What's the taxonomical classification of Heracleum scabridum?

Heracleum scabridum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, it is placed within the genus Heracleum, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species scabridum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Heracleum scabridum has a perennial growth habit characterized by large, compound leaves with deeply lobed, serrated segments and thick, hollow stems covered in fine, bristly hairs. Its inflorescence consists of large, flat-topped umbels composed of numerous small, white flowers clustered together on branched peduncles. The individual flowers possess five petals and are supported by a structure of bracts at the base of the umbellet. These chemicals react with ultraviolet light to trigger a condition known as phytophotodermatitis. Once the sap touches the epidermis, exposure to sunlight can lead to painful lesions or even long-lasting dark patches. If the sap enters the eyes, it may cause intense stinging and potential corneal damage. Individuals should avoid handling the vegetation to prevent these dermatological reactions.
