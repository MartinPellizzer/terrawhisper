Caryopteris mongholica

What's the taxonomical classification of Caryopteris mongholica?

Caryopteris mongholica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Lamiaceae, specifically falling under the genus Caryopteris, with the unique species designation mongholica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its stems are woody at the base and become more herbaceous toward the tips, providing a sturdy structure for the plant. Small, tubular flowers emerge in terminal clusters, displaying a pale blue to violet color that attracts various pollinators. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 6'-O-(p-coumaroyl) antirrinoside, 8-acetyl-6'-O-(p-coumaroyl) harpagide, 8-acetylharpagide, and harpagide. These specific chemical constituents are classified as iridoid glycosides. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential therapeutic properties in traditional medicine. The presence of these compounds contributes to the complex biochemical profile of Caryopteris mongholica. Understanding this chemical makeup is essential for exploring its pharmacological applications.

According to a study published by "Die Pharmazie", two new iridoid glucosides, 8-acetyl-6'-O-(p-coumaroyl) harpagide and 6'-O-(p-coumaroyl) antirrinoside, were isolated from Caryopteris mongholica. These new compounds were found alongside 2 known iridoid glucosides, which are 8-acetylharpagide and harpagide. The structures of these 4 compounds were elucidated through the interpretation of various spectroscopic data. These specific methods included UV, IR, MASS, 1H, 13C, 1H/1H-COSY, HMQC, HMBC, and NOESY NMR, as well as chemical methods.
