Caryopteris mongholica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caryopteris mongholica has opposite, simple leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape and possess a finely serrated margin. The foliage typically exhibits a soft, grayish-green hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. Its stems are woody at the base and become more herbaceous toward the tips, providing a sturdy structure for the plant. Small, tubular flowers emerge in terminal clusters, displaying a pale blue to violet color that attracts various pollinators. This shrubby perennial maintains a compact, mounded growth habit that defines its overall silhouette.
