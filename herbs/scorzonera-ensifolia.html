Scorzonera ensifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scorzonera ensifolia has lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically entire or slightly serrated and arranged alternately along its upright, herbaceous stems. The foliage often exhibits a somewhat fleshy texture and can range in color from bright green to a slightly darker hue depending on environmental exposure. Its flowering structures consist of yellow ligulate florets that form compact heads at the terminal ends of the branches. These composite flower heads are supported by involucres composed of multiple rows of overlapping bracts. The root system is characterized by a sturdy taproot that allows the plant to anchor firmly in its natural habitat.
