Common Germander (Teucrium Divaricatum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Teucrium divaricatum.

Common germander, scientifically known as Teucrium divaricatum, is a medicinal herb native to the Mediterranean region, valued for its adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties. It has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, aid digestion, and reduce fever, with its primary benefits stemming from its rich content of flavonoids and essential oils. In ancient Greek and Roman cultures, it was used to treat wounds, fevers, and as a tonic for overall vitality, reflecting its long-standing role in herbal medicine. Today, it is gaining attention in modern wellness practices for its potential to reduce stress and enhance immune function, often incorporated into herbal teas and supplements. Notably, its distinctive peppery, slightly bitter flavor and the presence of the rare compound teucrin make it a unique and historically significant plant in traditional and contemporary herbalism.
