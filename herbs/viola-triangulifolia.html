What's the taxonomical classification of Viola triangulifolia?

Viola triangulifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Malpighiales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Violaceae, specifically situated in the genus Viola, and is identified by its unique species name, triangulifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola triangulifolia has ovate to broadly triangular leaves with crenate margins, a basal rosette growth habit, and small zygomorphic flowers that are typically violet or pale blue in color. The leaves are characterized by long petioles and prominent venation that radiates from the base toward the edges. Its stems are relatively short and often emerge from a creeping rhizome or a small caudex. The floral structure features five petals, with the lower petal often displaying darker nectar guides to attract pollinators. This plant's overall morphology is adapted for low-growing stability in moist, shaded habitats.
