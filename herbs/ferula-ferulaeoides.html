Ferula ferulaeoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ferula ferulaeoides has a perennial, herbaceous structure characterized by thick, fleshy, and deeply grooved stems that can reach significant heights. The plant features large, compound leaves that are pinnately divided into numerous narrow, linear leaflets. Its inflorescence consists of a massive, branched umbel containing numerous small, yellowish-green flowers. The root system is dominated by a robust, tuberous rhizome that serves as a primary storage organ. Throughout its growth cycle, the plant exhibits a sturdy, upright habit typical of many members of the Apiaceae family.
