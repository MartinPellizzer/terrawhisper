Ferula ferulaeoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Ferula ferulaeoides?

Ferula ferulaeoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Apiales and is a member of the family Apiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Ferula, and its unique species designation, ferulaeoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ferula ferulaeoides has a perennial, herbaceous structure characterized by thick, fleshy, and deeply grooved stems that can reach significant heights. The root system is dominated by a robust, tuberous rhizome that serves as a primary storage organ. As discussed in a study, the active fractions of Ferula ferulaeoides demonstrate inhibitory effects on human gastric cancer MGC-803 xenografts in nude mice in a dose-dependent manner. The apoptotic ratio in these models increases as the drug concentration rises. Ferula ferulaeoides down-regulates the expression of survivin mRNA and increases the protein expression levels of Bax, Bcl-2, caspase-3, and caspase-9 in tumor tissues. Additionally, the contents of IL-10 and TGF-β1 in the plasma of nude mice decreased in the high dose group of the Ferula ferulaeoides active fractions. These results indicate that Ferula ferulaeoides can significantly inhibit the growth of the MGC-803 subcutaneously transplanted tumor.
