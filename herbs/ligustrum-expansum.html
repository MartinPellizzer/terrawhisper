Ligustrum expansum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligustrum expansum has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and feature entire margins with acute tips. The leaf surfaces are typically a lustrous dark green on the upper side and slightly paler underneath. These plants possess woody stems that develop a grayish bark as they mature into shrubs or small trees. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge in terminal panicles during the blooming season. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to black upon ripening.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ligustrum expansum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea, if any part of the plant is ingested. The berries are particularly toxic and contain glycosides that can lead to significant internal irritation. Contact with the plant's leaves or stems may also trigger allergic skin reactions such as redness, itching, or localized swelling in sensitive individuals. While systemic toxicity is primarily linked to consumption, careful avoidance of all plant parts is necessary to prevent accidental poisoning. Monitoring for these symptoms is critical if exposure is suspected.
