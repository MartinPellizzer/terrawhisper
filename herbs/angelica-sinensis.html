What to know about Angelica sinensis before using it medicinally

Angelica sinensis, commonly known as Chinese angelica, is a medicinal herb that has been used for centuries to treat various health conditions, including anemia, menopausal symptoms, and respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs.

The main health benefits of Angelica sinensis include its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its ability to reduce pain and swelling, and protect against cell damage. The therapeutic properties of this herb include its sedative, expectorant, and antiviral effects, making it a popular remedy for promoting relaxation, relieving respiratory congestion, and fighting off viral infections. The bioactive compounds present in Angelica sinensis include ferulic acid, hesperidin, and polysaccharides, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory activities.

In traditional Chinese medicine, Angelica sinensis is often prepared as a tea or decoction, and is also used in the production of medicinal wines and capsules, to treat a range of health conditions and promote overall well-being.
