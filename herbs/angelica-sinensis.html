Angelica Sinensis Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Angelica sinensis, commonly known as dong quai, is a perennial herb native to China and widely used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It is renowned for its ability to support women's health, particularly in regulating menstrual cycles and alleviating menopausal symptoms.

The herb contains bioactive constituents such as coumarins, flavonoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and circulatory benefits.

Angelica sinensis can be prepared as an infusion, tincture, or capsule, making it versatile for various herbal formulations.

Health Benefits of angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis reduces inflammation by containing compounds such as ferulic acid and ligustilide, which inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and reduce oxidative stress in the body.

Angelica sinensis boosts immune function due to its high concentration of antioxidants and essential oils that stimulate the production of white blood cells and enhance the body's defense mechanisms against pathogens.

Angelica sinensis relieves headache by improving blood circulation and reducing tension in the blood vessels, which can alleviate migraines and tension headaches.

Angelica sinensis eases pain through its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help reduce pain signals in the nervous system and promote tissue repair.

Medicinal Constituents of angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis ligustilide is a major bioactive compound found in the root of Angelica sinensis, known for its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which contribute to the herb's traditional use in promoting blood circulation and alleviating pain.

Angelica sinensis butylphthalide is another key constituent that exhibits neuroprotective effects and has been studied for its potential in treating conditions such as stroke and neurodegenerative diseases due to its ability to improve cerebral blood flow.

Angelica sinensis quercetin, a flavonoid compound, possesses strong antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-cancer activities, making it valuable for its protective effects on various organs and systems in the body.

Angelica sinensis ursolic acid is a triterpenoid compound with demonstrated anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and hepatoprotective properties, contributing to the overall therapeutic potential of the herb in traditional and modern medicine.

Medicinal Parts of angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis root, also known as Dang Gui, is the primary medicinal part of the herb and is highly valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its nourishing and blood-activating properties.

The root is rich in volatile oils, polysaccharides, and ferulic acid, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Main herbal preparations made from the root include decoctions, powders, and tinctures, commonly used to address menstrual disorders, anemia, and cardiovascular health.

It is also incorporated into formulas like Tiaogan Wan and Shenghua Tang to enhance its efficacy in treating blood deficiency and stagnation.

Herbal Preparations of angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis decoctions are one of the most commonly used preparations, typically involving simmering the dried root in water for several hours to extract its active compounds, such as ferulic acid and ligustilide, which are believed to enhance circulation and support blood health.

Angelica sinensis teas are prepared by steeping the dried herb in hot water, often combined with other herbs like ginger or chrysanthemum, and are popular for their calming and digestive benefits, as well as their ability to alleviate menstrual discomfort.

Angelica sinensis tinctures are concentrated liquid extracts made by soaking the herb in alcohol or glycerin, allowing for easier absorption of its medicinal properties and commonly used for treating anemia, fatigue, and respiratory issues.
