Medicinal Parts of angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis has many medicinal parts, such as the root, rhizome, leaf, and seed.

The leaves of Angelica sinensis contain flavonoids, such as kaempferol and quercetin, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and anti-cancer properties.

Possible Side Effects of angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing allergic reactions, skin irritation, and digestive issues in some individuals.

High doses of the herb may lead to dizziness, headaches, and nausea due to its potential to interact with blood thinners and other medications.

Additionally, improper use of Angelica sinensis may exacerbate bleeding disorders, such as hemophilia, and worsen conditions like high blood pressure.

In rare cases, it may also cause liver damage if consumed in excessive amounts.
