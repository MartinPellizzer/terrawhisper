Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China, known for its use in traditional Chinese medicine to improve blood circulation, alleviate menstrual disorders, and support overall vitality.

What is Chinese Angelica (Angelica Sinensis)?

Chinese Angelica, or Angelica sinensis, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of China, characterized by its thick, aromatic root and used extensively in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Chinese Angelica, or Angelica sinensis, is a biennial or perennial herb native to the Chinese plateau, belonging to the Apiaceae family and classified under the genus Angelica.

Common Names and Synonyms

Chinese Angelica, scientifically known as Angelica sinensis, is also commonly referred to as Dong Quai, Chinese angelica, and the root of the angelica plant, with additional regional names such as 当归 in Mandarin and 当歸 in Classical Chinese.

Plant Description and Morphology

Chinese Angelica, or Angelica sinensis, is a perennial herb characterized by its thick, fleshy roots, deeply lobed basal leaves, and tall, branched stems with small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) is native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in Sichuan, Yunnan, Qinghai, and Tibet, where it thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soils.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The roots and rhizomes of Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) are the primary parts used medicinally.

Phytochemical Composition

Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) contains a complex array of bioactive compounds, including lignans, coumarins, flavonoids, alkaloids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic effects.

Pharmacological Properties

Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, immunomodulatory, and antithrombotic effects, primarily attributed to its active compounds such as ligustilide, senkyusanin, and ferulic acid.

Mechanisms of Action

Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) exerts its therapeutic effects primarily through the modulation of inflammatory responses, enhancement of immune function, and promotion of blood circulation by influencing multiple signaling pathways including NF-κB, MAPK, and PI3K/Akt, as well as through the bioactive compounds such as ligustilide, ferulic acid, and angelica acid that interact with cellular receptors and enzymes.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Chinese Angelica is used to treat anemia, menstrual disorders, rheumatism, hypertension, asthma, diabetes, and gastrointestinal ulcers.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Chinese Angelica is typically prepared through drying, grinding into powder, or processing into tinctures, decoctions, or capsules, depending on its intended medicinal use.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) varies depending on the form of the herb and the condition being treated, with standard internal dosages ranging from 3 to 12 grams per day in decoction or powder form, and external applications typically involving smaller quantities applied directly to the affected area.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used in excessive amounts or for prolonged periods, Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, and increased bleeding risk, particularly in individuals with coagulation disorders or those taking anticoagulant medications.

Drug Interactions

Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) may interact with anticoagulant medications, such as warfarin, by increasing the risk of bleeding, and may also affect the metabolism of certain drugs due to its influence on liver enzymes.

Toxicity and Precautions

Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, but it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, or interact with certain medications, so it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a qualified healthcare professional.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Chinese Angelica is primarily cultivated in the high altitude regions of Sichuan, Qinghai, and Yunnan provinces in China, where the cool climate and well-drained soils provide optimal growing conditions for its development.

Processing and Extraction

The processing of Chinese Angelica involves harvesting the roots during specific seasons, drying them under controlled conditions, and sometimes slicing them to enhance their medicinal properties and preserve their active compounds.

Storage and Shelf Life

Proper storage of Chinese Angelica in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight is essential to maintain its potency and prevent moisture absorption, with a typical shelf life of 2 to 3 years when stored correctly.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research and evidence supporting the use of Chinese Angelica (Angelica sinensis) in herbal medicine have demonstrated its pharmacological effects, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory properties, with studies indicating its potential in treating conditions such as anemia, menstrual disorders, and cardiovascular diseases.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Chinese Angelica, or Angelica sinensis, has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for centuries to invigorate blood, regulate menstrual cycles, and alleviate symptoms of anemia and blood deficiency.

Regulatory Status

In many countries, Chinese Angelica is classified as a traditional herbal medicine, with regulatory status varying by region, often requiring specific labeling and safety testing for commercial use.
