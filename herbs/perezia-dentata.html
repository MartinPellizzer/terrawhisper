What is Perezia dentata?

Perezia dentata is a perennial herb that belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It has a rhizomatous habit and produces solitary, terminal capitulescences with 5-10 yellow ligulate florets and 10-30 yellow disc florets. The leaves are simple, opposite, and dentate, with 5-7 pairs of lobes and a cuneate base. The plant is 10-30 cm tall and has a leafy, green stem with a glabrous or pubescent surface.

The root system consists of a thick, fleshy rhizome with adventitious roots.

The main herbal preparations of Perezia dentata are decoction, infusion, tincture, and tea.

Perezia infusion is a liquid made by steeping the plant's parts in hot water, often used to treat coughs and sore throats. Perezia tincture is a concentrated liquid extracted using a solvent, commonly used to treat digestive problems and skin irritations.

Perezia tea, a less concentrated version of infusion, is used to treat various health issues, including fever, coughs, and skin conditions.
