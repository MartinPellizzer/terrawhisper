Cerastium cerastoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cerastium cerastoides has a perennial growth habit characterized by a basal rosette of leaves and upright flowering stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The basal leaves are linear to narrowly lanceolate with entire or slightly dentate margins, while the stem leaves are smaller and more sparsely distributed. Its flowers feature five petals that are often notched at the apex and are white to pale pink in color. The most distinguishing morphological feature is the long, spreading styles that extend well beyond the petals, giving the fruit a bristly appearance. These styles eventually become stiff and persistent as the seed capsules mature.
