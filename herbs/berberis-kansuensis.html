Berberis kansuensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Berberis kansuensis?

Berberis kansuensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Ranunculales and the family Berberidaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Berberis, and its specific species designation, kansuenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berberis kansuensis has woody, erect stems characterized by sharp, spine-tipped branches and small, leathery, obovate leaves with entire margins. The leaves are typically dark green and arranged in clusters along the branches. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy berries that serve as a primary reproductive feature. The plant's overall structure is dense and shrub-like, reflecting its adaptation to specific environmental niches.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Gansu. These botanical specimens are often found growing along forest edges or in rocky thickets at elevations ranging from middle to high mountain zones. The species distribution is closely tied to the temperate climate and seasonal moisture patterns characteristic of these central and western provinces. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?
