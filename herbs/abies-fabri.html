Abies fabri

What's the taxonomical classification of Abies fabri?

Abies fabri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Pinales and the family Pinaceae. Ultimately, its specific classification is defined by the genus Abies and the species fabri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Abies fabri has a conical crown with spreading branches and needles that are flattened, dark green, and typically feature two distinct white stomatal bands on their underside. The tree reaches heights of up to 30 meters, characterized by a straight trunk and a bark that becomes scaly and grayish-brown with age. Its leaves are arranged spirally but appear two-ranked due to the flattened nature of the foliage. The cones are upright and cylindrical, maturing to a dark purple or brown color before disintegrating. Small, woody cones are produced on short or long shoots depending on the specific environmental conditions.
