Pleione scopulorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pleione scopulorum?

Pleione scopulorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family, specifically situated within the genus Pleione, and is identified by its unique species name, scopulorum.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including respiratory infections, skin inflammation, and digestive issues. The rhizomes of the species are often prepared into medicinal decoctions to soothe coughing and congestion. Local practitioners also apply topical pastes derived from the plant to reduce swelling and minor skin irritations. Some traditional applications involve using the plant to manage stomach discomfort and general malaise. These therapeutic uses are deeply rooted in regional folk medicine practices.

The method of preparation depends heavily on whether a quick-acting aqueous solution or a concentrated alcoholic tincture is required.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Pleione scopulorum can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if the plant's sap or tissues come into direct contact with sensitive human skin. While specific toxicological studies on this particular orchid are limited, many members of the Orchidaceae family contain alkaloids that may trigger localized reactions. Individuals with known allergies to tropical flora should exercise caution when handling the foliage or flowers. Symptoms of such a reaction typically include redness, itching, or a mild stinging sensation at the site of contact. It is advisable to wear gloves to prevent any unforeseen dermatological discomfort during cultivation.
