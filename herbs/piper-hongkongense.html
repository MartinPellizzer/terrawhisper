What's the taxonomical classification of Piper hongkongense?

Piper hongkongense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned in the order Piperales. The plant is a member of the family Piperaceae and falls under the genus Piper, ultimately being identified by the specific epithet hongkongense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper hongkongense has woody stems with simple, alternate leaves that feature an ovate shape, an acute apex, and a cordate base.

The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly wavy, housing prominent pinnate venation that extends toward the edges. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in elongated, cylindrical spikes that emerge from the leaf axils. These inflorescences eventually produce small, fleshy drupes that change color as they mature.

The overall structure reflects a typical climbing or shrubby growth habit characteristic of the Piper genus.
