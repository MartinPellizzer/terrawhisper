Hoya mengtzeensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hoya mengtzeensis?

Hoya mengtzeensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Gentianales. It is a specialized member of the family Apocynaceae, falling under the genus Hoya, and is specifically identified by the species epithet mengtzeensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hoya mengtzeensis has thick, succulent, elliptical leaves that are dark green and reach up to 10 centimeters in length.

These leaves are arranged oppositely along trailing or climbing woody stems that allow the plant to scramble over surfaces. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers that feature a fleshy corona and a central ring of nectar. Each blossom is typically creamy white with subtle markings and emits a sweet fragrance.

Small, rounded peduncles support these floral groups to facilitate pollination in its natural habitat.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Hoya mengtzeensis can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or mild gastrointestinal distress if its sap or parts are ingested. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many members of the Apocynaceae family contain milky latex that may act as a sensitizer.
