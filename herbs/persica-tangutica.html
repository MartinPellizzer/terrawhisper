    What is Persica tangutica?

    Persica tangutica is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

    It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 5 meters tall, with a spread of about 3 meters. The plant has pinnate leaves with 5-7 leaflets, and produces white flowers with 5 petals in late spring. The flowers are followed by small red or yellow fruits, each containing 1-2 seeds, which are eaten by birds and other small animals.

    Persica tangutica is native to the mountainous regions of western China and the Himalayas.
