What is Cleisostoma sumbavense?

Cleisostoma sumbavense is a species of orchid in the family Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous habit, characterized by a short stem that bears two or three leafless branches. The plant produces a single, apical inflorescence that bears a few flowers, each with a yellowish-green sepals and petals, and a white or yellow labellum with a distinctive purple or brown callus. The flowers are relatively small, measuring approximately 1.5 cm in diameter, and are often found in clusters of two to five.

The plant is endemic to the island of Sumba in Indonesia, and is typically found growing in humid, shaded areas with high levels of rainfall.
