Paris thibetica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paris thibetica has a single, whorl-like arrangement of several large, elliptical to ovate leaves that emerge from a central stem and surround a solitary, terminal flower. The leaves are characterized by prominent parallel venation and a glossy, dark green surface. This plant typically features a fleshy rhizome that serves as its primary underground structure. The solitary flower consists of a central greenish-white ovary surrounded by distinct, petal-like sepals. Each bloom is often accompanied by small, inconspicuous bracts located at the base of the leaf whorl.
