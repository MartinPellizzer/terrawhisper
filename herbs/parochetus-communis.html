Parochetus communis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae, eventually reaching its specific identification within the genus Parochetus. Finally, the plant is identified at the species level as Parochetus communis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Parochetus communis has a low-growing, prostrate habit characterized by woody, creeping stems and evergreen, ovate leaves that measure approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a vibrant pink to purple coloration. These blossoms are arranged in dense, head-like clusters that sit close to the ground.
