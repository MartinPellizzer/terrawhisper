Parochetus communis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Parochetus communis has a low-growing, prostrate habit characterized by woody, creeping stems and evergreen, ovate leaves that measure approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly serrated, while the texture remains somewhat leathery to withstand alpine conditions. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a vibrant pink to purple coloration. These blossoms are arranged in dense, head-like clusters that sit close to the ground. The plant's overall structure allows it to form dense, mat-like carpets across rocky terrain.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, spanning across countries such as India, Nepal, Bhutan, and parts of China. It typically thrives in alpine or subalpine zones where rocky crevices and stony slopes are abundant. These populations are often found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,500 meters above sea level. The species favors cool, moist environments found within these mountainous terrains. Due to its specific habitat requirements, its distribution remains restricted to these high-elevation Asian mountain ranges.
