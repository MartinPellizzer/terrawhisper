Bromus inerbis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following the lineage of the order Poaales, the plant is a member of the family Poaceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Bromus, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species inermis.

    This plant is used to treat no known human or animal diseases. Bromus inermis, commonly known as smooth brome, is a perennial grass primarily valued for its ability to provide forage for livestock. While it is a significant component of grazing lands, it lacks medicinal properties recognized in traditional or modern pharmacology. In many regions, it is actually considered an invasive species that can outcompete native vegetation. Consequently, its utility lies in ecosystem management and animal nutrition rather than therapeutic application.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Bromus inerbis can cause physical irritation to the skin and mucous membranes due to the presence of sharp, abrasive awns on its seed heads. When these seeds come into contact with humans or animals, they can embed themselves into the flesh or sensitive tissues. This penetration often leads to localized inflammation, redness, and significant discomfort. In livestock, the sharp structures can cause more serious injuries such as skin punctures, eye irritation, or even internal damage if ingested. These mechanical injuries can ultimately result in secondary infections if the plant material remains lodged in the body.
