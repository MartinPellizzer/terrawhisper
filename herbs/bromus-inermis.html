Bromus inerbis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Bromus inermis contains no documented medicinal compounds or established pharmacological applications for human or veterinary use. This perennial grass primarily functions as a forage source for livestock and a stabilizer for soil in semi-arid ecosystems. While many grasses possess secondary metabolites, specific bioactive constituents for this species have not been isolated or characterized in medical literature. Researchers typically focus on its ecological role and nutritional value rather than its potential therapeutic properties. Consequently, there is currently no scientific basis for utilizing this plant as a medicine.
