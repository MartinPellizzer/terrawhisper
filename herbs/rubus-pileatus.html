Rubus pileatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus pileatus?

Rubus pileatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is part of the order Rosales and falls under the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Rubus, and its specific species name, pileatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus pileatus has a low-growing, procumbent growth habit characterized by trailing stems that form dense, mat-like mounds. The leaves are typically trifoliate or occasionally five-foliate, featuring deeply lobed, serrated margins and a distinctively rugose texture. Each leaflet possesses a dark green upper surface and a paler, sometimes slightly pubescent underside. The stems are relatively short and often lack the prominent, large prickles found in other species of the genus. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge from the foliage to eventually produce small, aggregate drupelets.
