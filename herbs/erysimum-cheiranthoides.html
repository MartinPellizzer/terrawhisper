Erysimum cheiranthoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Erysimum cheiranthoides?

Erysimum cheiranthoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Brassicales. As a member of the family Brassicaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Erysimum, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as Erysimum cheiranthoides.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Erysimum cheiranthoides can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive upset if ingested by humans or animals. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, the plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family which often contains glucosinolates. These compounds can trigger dermatitis or redness when the plant makes direct contact with sensitive skin. If consumed, the chemical constituents may lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain. It is generally recommended to avoid handling or eating the plant to prevent these unpredictable physiological responses.
