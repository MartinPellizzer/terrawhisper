Murdannia japonica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murdannia japonica has prostrate, creeping stems that produce small, lanceolate leaves measuring approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the slender stems and feature entire margins with acute tips. The plant produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers that emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. These blossoms typically display five petals and a central structure characteristic of the Melastomataceae family. Small, fleshy fruits develop following pollination to facilitate seed dispersal.
