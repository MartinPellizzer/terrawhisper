Murdannia japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Murdannia japonica?

Murdannia japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Commelinales and is a member of the family Commelinaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Murdannia, with the specific epithet japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murdannia japonica has prostrate, creeping stems that produce small, lanceolate leaves measuring approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the slender stems and feature entire margins with acute tips. The plant produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers that emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. These blossoms typically display five petals and a central structure characteristic of the Melastomataceae family. Small, fleshy fruits develop following pollination to facilitate seed dispersal.
