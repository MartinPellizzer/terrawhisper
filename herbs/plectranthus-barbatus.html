Mexican Mint

Plectranthus barbatus

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Botanical Identification

Mexican Mint, or Plectranthus barbatus, is a member of the Lamiaceae family. Commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, it is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America. The plant features aromatic leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, tubular flowers. Synonyms include Hierbas de San Juan and other regional names in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Mexican Mint contains essential oil, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to provide its therapeutic effects, with the essential oil contributing to its aromatic and anti-inflammatory properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids enhance antioxidant and antimicrobial activity, supporting its use in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory ailments and digestive issues.

Infusion: Used for calming effects and mild digestive support.

Essential oil: Used for respiratory conditions and as a stimulant.

Safety Profile

Mexican Mint is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause skin irritation in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by people with kidney disease due to its potential diuretic effects.
