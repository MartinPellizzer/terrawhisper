Garden Pea (Pisum sativum) Uses

Garden Pea is used to treat gastrointestinal inflammation, constipation, and gastritis. It helps manage gastroesophageal reflux disease and soothes gastrointestinal discomfort. The peas may also support healing in inflammatory bowel disease and skin infections due to their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

Garden Pea (Pisum sativum) Monograph

1. Gastrointestinal inflammation

Garden pea contains compounds that reduce inflammation in the gastrointestinal tract. It helps soothe irritation and may alleviate symptoms of conditions like gastritis and colitis. Its high fiber content also supports healthy digestion and bowel function. Regular consumption can promote gut health and reduce inflammatory responses.

2. Constipation

Garden pea is used to alleviate constipation due to its high fiber content, which promotes bowel movement. It also contains enzymes that aid digestion. Regular consumption of peas can help maintain regularity and prevent digestive discomfort. Its mild nature makes it suitable for long-term use in managing constipation.

3. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Garden pea (Pisum sativum) may help alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease due to its high fiber content, which promotes healthy digestion and reduces stomach acid production. It also contains compounds that may strengthen the lower esophageal sphincter, preventing acid reflux. Pea-based diets can support overall gastrointestinal health and reduce bloating and indigestion.

4. Gastritis

Garden pea is used in herbal medicine to soothe gastritis due to its mild anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties. It helps reduce stomach irritation and promotes healing of the gastric lining. Pea-based remedies are often recommended for their gentle action on the digestive system.

5. Diarrhea

Garden pea is used to treat diarrhea due to its mild astringent properties, which help firm stools. It contains compounds that reduce intestinal inflammation and regulate bowel movements. Pea-based preparations are often recommended for mild cases, though they should not replace medical advice for severe or persistent symptoms.

6. Gastrointestinal discomfort

Garden Pea is used to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort due to its mild laxative properties and high fiber content. It helps relieve constipation and supports healthy digestion. The seeds also contain enzymes that aid in breaking down food, promoting smoother digestion and reducing bloating.

7. Gastrointestinal upset

Garden Pea is used to alleviate gastrointestinal upset due to its mild demulcent properties, which soothe irritated mucous membranes. It contains fiber and minerals that support digestive health. Traditionally, it has been employed to ease symptoms like bloating and indigestion. Its gentle nature makes it suitable for regular use in herbal remedies for digestive discomfort.

8. Gastrointestinal disorders

Garden Pea is used to support gastrointestinal health by soothing inflammation and promoting healthy digestion. It contains fiber and nutrients that aid in regular bowel movements and may help alleviate symptoms of indigestion and irritable bowel syndrome. Its mild nature makes it suitable for various digestive conditions when prepared properly.

9. Inflammatory bowel disease

Garden pea has been studied for its potential to alleviate symptoms of inflammatory bowel disease due to its high fiber content and anti-inflammatory compounds. It may help regulate gut microbiota and reduce intestinal inflammation, though more research is needed to confirm its efficacy in clinical settings.

10. Skin infections

Garden Pea (Pisum sativum) has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The seeds contain compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and soothe inflamed skin. Poultices made from crushed peas or decoctions of pea leaves may be applied externally to address conditions like eczema and minor wounds.
