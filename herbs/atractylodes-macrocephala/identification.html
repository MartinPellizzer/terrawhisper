Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chinese Atractylodes is Atractylodes macrocephala, a member of the family Asteraceae. It was first described by the botanist Rottbühl in 1843. This species has several synonyms, including Atractylis macrocephala and Atractylodes chinensis, reflecting its historical classification and regional variations.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Atractylodes is a rhizomatous plant with a thick, fleshy root system. It has erect, cylindrical stems that are smooth and slightly hairy. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture. The inflorescence is a loose panicle, with small, greenish-yellow flowers. The fruit is a three-lobed achene, and the seeds are small, dark, and wind-dispersed.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Atractylodes macrocephala can be distinguished by its large, bulbous rhizome with a yellowish-brown exterior, dense fibrous roots, and a distinctive aromatic, slightly bitter taste. It differs from Atractylodes chinensis by its larger size and more robust rhizome structure. The leaves are broadly lanceolate with a hairy underside, and the flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chinese Atractylodes is native to regions in China, particularly the Yangtze River basin. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chinese Atractylodes typically blooms from June to August. Seed formation occurs from September to October. For identification purposes, the plant is harvested in late summer to early autumn, usually between August and September, when the roots are most developed and contain the highest concentration of active compounds.
