What's the taxonomical classification of Aralia tibetana?

Aralia tibetana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Apiales and falls under the family Araliaceae. Within this botanical framework, it is categorized under the genus Aralia, ultimately identifying it as the specific species tibetana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia tibetana has a woody, perennial habit characterized by upright stems and large, palmately compound leaves that typically consist of seven to nine distinct leaflets. The leaflets are ovate to lanceolate in shape, featuring serrated margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. These leaves are arranged alternately along the stems and can reach significant sizes depending on the environmental conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in terminal umbels that eventually develop into fleshy berries.
