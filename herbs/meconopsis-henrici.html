What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Meconopsis henrici has large, deeply lobed, basal leaves that are typically ovate or heart-shaped with serrated margins and a distinct bluish-green or glaucous coloration. These leaves often feature a fine covering of hairs that contribute to their textured appearance. The plant produces striking, solitary flowers that are characterized by numerous long, bright yellow stamens surrounding delicate blue or violet petals. Long, slender pedicels support the blooms, elevating them above the dense foliage. As the plant matures, it develops large, woody seed capsules that eventually split to release many small seeds.
