Merremia Poranoides

Merremia poranoides, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, and has been traditionally used in various traditional medicine systems for its therapeutic properties.

The plant contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal value. It has been used to treat ailments such as inflammation, respiratory conditions, and skin disorders due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Recent scientific studies have begun to validate some of these traditional uses, showing potential in managing oxidative stress and supporting immune function.

However, further research is needed to fully explore its pharmacological potential and ensure safe usage in modern medicinal applications.
