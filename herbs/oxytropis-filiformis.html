Oxytropis filiformis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxytropis filiformis has slender, thread-like leaves consisting of small, numerous leaflets and a sprawling growth habit characterized by thin, wiry stems. These leaves are typically pinnately compound with a fine texture that gives the plant its specific epithet. The stems are often prostrate or ascending, allowing the plant to navigate through rocky or alpine terrain. Small, pea-like flowers emerge from the foliage, displaying the characteristic structure of the Fabaceae family. The overall architecture is delicate and lightweight, adapted for survival in specialized ecological niches.
