Lasianthus henryi

What's the taxonomical classification of Lasianthus henryi?

Lasianthus henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and placed under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the Rubiaceae family, falling within the genus Lasianthus, and is specifically identified by its species name, henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lasianthus henryi has opposite, elliptic to oblong leaves that measure approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with acute tips.

The foliage is characterized by a glossy, dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside with distinct pinnate venation. Its stems are typically slender and woody, often displaying a slightly pubescent texture depending on the specific growth stage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters.

These floral structures eventually give rise to small, fleshy berries that serve as the primary dispersal mechanism for the species.
