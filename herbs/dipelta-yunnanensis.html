Dipelta yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dipelta yunnanensis has opposite, decussate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, featuring serrated margins and a texture that is typically glabrous or slightly pubescent. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can reach heights of several meters in its natural habitat. Its inflorescences are arranged in terminal panicles containing small, creamy white flowers. These flowers are characterized by prominent stamens and a nectar-producing structure that attracts various pollinators. The fruit develops into small, globose drupes that turn dark or reddish upon maturation.
