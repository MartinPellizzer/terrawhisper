Rauvolfia perakensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Rauvolfia perakensis?

Rauvolfia perakensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Gentianales. This plant is further categorized under the family Apocynaceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Rauvolfia, ultimately being identified by its specific species name, perakensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rauvolfia perakensis has simple, opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that typically measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length with entire margins and acute apices. Small, tubular flowers emerge in terminal or axillary cymes, displaying a pale color often ranging from white to light pink. Following pollination, the plant produces fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark red or black hue upon maturity.
