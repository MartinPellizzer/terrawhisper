Carum alpinum

Carum alpinum, commonly known as Wild Caraway, is a plant species belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, offering several health benefits such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, which can help to soothe digestive issues and alleviate symptoms of anxiety.

The therapeutic actions of Carum alpinum include its ability to relax the nervous system and reduce stress, while also exhibiting spasmolytic and antihistaminic properties.

The bioactive constituents of the plant include volatile oils, flavonoids, and other compounds that contribute to its therapeutic effects, although further research is needed to fully understand its properties.

Herbal Preparations of Carum alpinum

Possible Side Effects of Carum alpinum

Carum alpinum can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal problems.

Ingesting large amounts of the plant's essential oils can cause dizziness, headaches, and nausea.

Applying the plant's oil to the skin without proper dilution can lead to irritation and inflammation, potentially causing redness, itching, and blisters.

Excessive use of Carum alpinum can also interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, exacerbating underlying health conditions.
