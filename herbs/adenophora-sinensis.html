Adenophora sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Adenophora sinensis?

Adenophora sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Campanulaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Adenophora, with its specific designation being the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora sinensis has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils in terminal clusters, displaying shades of pale purple, lilac, or white. The corolla is tubular and flares into five distinct lobes at the apex. Below the ground, the plant develops a thick, woody rhizome that aids in its perennial survival.
