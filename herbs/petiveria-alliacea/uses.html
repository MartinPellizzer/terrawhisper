Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea) Uses

Pennyroyal is used for skin infections, wound healing, and skin irritation, including eczema and fungal infections. It helps with insect bites and inflammatory skin conditions. It also addresses respiratory infections, sinusitis, and cough. Its antifungal and antimicrobial properties support these applications.

Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea) Monograph

1. Skin infections

Pennyroyal is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce bacterial and fungal growth on the skin. However, it should be diluted before application to avoid irritation. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.

2. Wound healing

Pennyroyal is used topically to promote wound healing due to its antimicrobial and astringent properties. It helps reduce infection risk and speed tissue repair. However, internal use is toxic and should be avoided. Always consult a qualified herbalist before use.

3. Skin irritation

Pennyroyal is used topically to alleviate skin irritation by reducing inflammation and soothing redness. Its antimicrobial properties help prevent infection in minor wounds or rashes. However, it should be diluted before applying to avoid skin sensitivity. Always consult a qualified herbalist for proper preparation and use.

4. Eczema

Pennyroyal is traditionally used for eczema due to its antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce skin irritation and inflammation associated with eczema. External applications of pennyroyal oil may alleviate symptoms, though it should be diluted to avoid skin irritation. Always consult a qualified herbalist before use.

5. Fungal infections

Pennyroyal is traditionally used to treat fungal infections due to its antimicrobial and antifungal properties. It may help in managing conditions like athlete's foot and ringworm when applied topically. However, internal use should be avoided due to potential toxicity. Always consult a qualified herbalist before use.

6. Insect bites

Pennyroyal is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of insect bites by reducing inflammation and itching. Its essential oils have antiseptic properties that may help prevent infection. It is often applied topically as a diluted oil or infused poultice. However, internal use should be avoided due to potential toxicity.

7. Inflammatory skin conditions

Pennyroyal is used topically for inflammatory skin conditions due to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. It can help reduce redness, swelling, and infection in conditions like eczema and psoriasis. However, it should be diluted and used cautiously to avoid skin irritation. Always consult a qualified herbalist before use.

8. Respiratory infections

Pennyroyal is traditionally used for respiratory infections due to its expectorant and antiseptic properties. It helps clear mucus and reduce inflammation in the airways. Often prepared as a tea, it may support cough relief and ease breathing in conditions like bronchitis or colds. Its use should be cautious and under guidance due to potential side effects.

9. Sinusitis

Pennyroyal is traditionally used for sinusitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps clear nasal passages and reduce congestion. However, it should be used with caution, as it can be toxic in high doses. Always consult a qualified herbalist before use.

10. Cough

Pennyroyal is traditionally used to alleviate coughs due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. However, it should be used cautiously and under professional guidance, as it may have toxic effects if misused.
