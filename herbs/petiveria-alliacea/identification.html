Botanical Identification of Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea)

To identify Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Pennyroyal, Petiveria alliacea, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Asphodelales, family Acanthaceae, genus Petiveria, and species Petiveria alliacea. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Pennyroyal is Petiveria alliacea, first described by the Swedish botanist Carl Peter Thunberg in 1794. It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and has several synonyms, including Petroselinum alliaceum and Hydnocarpus alliaceum. Common names such as Pennyroyal, Mexican Pennyroyal, and Herba Petiveriae are widely used, reflecting its traditional and regional significance.

Morphological Characteristics

Pennyroyal is an annual herb with a sprawling growth habit. Its root system is fibrous and shallow. The stems are square and hairy, often branching at the base. Leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a strong peppery odor. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, with small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a slender, two-valved capsule containing tiny, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Pennyroyal can be distinguished by its opposite, narrow leaves with a strong minty scent, small white flowers in clusters, and the presence of glandular dots on the leaves. It is often confused with other mint family plants, but its distinct aroma and leaf structure help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Pennyroyal is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, including Mexico and parts of South America. It thrives in warm climates with mild winters and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It is typically found at low to mid-altitude ranges, often in disturbed areas or near water sources.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Pennyroyal blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to October. For identification, harvesting is best done during the flowering stage, usually in June to July, when the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea) is closely related to species within the genus Petiveria. It shares characteristics with other members of the Apiaceae family, such as umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties to other aromatic herbs in the same family.
