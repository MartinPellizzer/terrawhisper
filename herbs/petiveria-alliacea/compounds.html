Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea) Compounds

The primary compounds in Pennyroyal include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and essential oils, responsible for its aromatic and medicinal effects. Saponins and tannins contribute to its astringent qualities, while coumarin and phenolic acids provide additional therapeutic benefits. Terpenoids, glycosides, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further support its diverse pharmacological activities.

Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. They also aid in protecting cells from oxidative stress.

2. Essential oil

The essential oil of Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea) is a key active compound, known for its strong aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine. It contains volatile compounds like p-menth-2-en-1-ol and p-cymene, which contribute to its medicinal effects, though it must be used with caution due to potential toxicity.

3. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and antifungal effects. Saponin also aids in the plant's ability to repel pests and may support its use in traditional herbal remedies.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea). It contributes to the plant's characteristic scent and has antispasmodic and antifungal properties. Coumarin is also known for its ability to act as a natural anticoagulant, making it a significant component in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and antifungal properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine. This compound is particularly noted for its role in the plant's ability to repel pests and treat skin infections.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also help preserve the plant's potency and are involved in its traditional use for cleansing and reducing inflammation.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea). They contribute to the plant's characteristic aroma and are believed to play a role in its traditional use for digestive and respiratory support. Specific terpenoids like p-cymene and limonene may have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific effects and applications in herbal medicine require further study. Glycosides are often associated with therapeutic benefits in traditional uses of Pennyroyal.

9. Alkaloid

Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea) contains the alkaloid pulegone. This compound is known for its strong medicinal properties, though it can be toxic in high doses. Pulegone contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Pennyroyal (Petiveria alliacea). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine. This compound helps protect cells from oxidative stress, enhancing the plant's potential therapeutic effects.
