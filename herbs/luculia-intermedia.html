Luculia intermedia

What's the taxonomical classification of Luculia intermedia?

Luculia intermedia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Luculia, and its specific species name, intermedia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Luculia intermedia contains various phytochemicals including terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its biological activities. What are the therapeutic actions of Luculia intermedia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, inflammation, digestive disorders, and certain types of fever. In traditional medicinal practices, the bark and leaves are often prepared as decoctions to soothe internal discomfort. Some practitioners also apply topical preparations derived from the plant to manage skin irritations or wounds. While it is utilized in specific cultural remedies, research is still ongoing to fully understand its pharmacological properties.
