What's the taxonomical classification of Luculia intermedia?

Luculia intermedia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Luculia, and its specific species name, intermedia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Luculia intermedia has an evergreen shrub or small tree habit characterized by dark green, elliptic, leathery leaves that measure up to 10 centimeters in length and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage grows in an opposite arrangement along woody, grayish-brown stems. During the flowering season, the plant produces solitary, fragrant, bell-shaped blossoms that transition from a creamy white to a soft pink hue. These flowers emerge from the leaf axils and often possess a delicate, waxy texture. The overall structure is dense and bushy, providing a lush appearance to the canopy.
