Dracocephalum argunense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum argunense has decumbent to erect stems that reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and bear opposite, ovate-lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a wrinkled texture. The foliage is characterized by a grayish-green hue and a somewhat rugose surface that feels slightly rough to the touch. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal whorls of small, tubular flowers that typically display a violet or blue-purple color. These blossoms emerge from bracts that are often more pronounced than the leaves themselves. The plant's overall structure is compact yet bushy, reflecting its adaptation to meadow environments.
