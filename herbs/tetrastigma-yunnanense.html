Tetrastigma yunnanense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tetrastigma yunnanense has woody climbing vines characterized by large, simple, cordate leaves with prominent venation and a thick, succulent stem structure. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the climbing stems and possess long petioles to support their expansive surface area. Its flowering structures consist of small, inconspicuous blossoms that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The plant often develops aerial roots that assist in its climbing habit through forest canopies. These morphological traits allow the species to compete effectively for light in tropical and subtropical environments.
