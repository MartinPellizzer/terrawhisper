Theophrastus Fritillary (Fritillaria Theophrasti)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Fritillaria theophrasti.

Theophrastus fritillary, scientifically known as Fritillaria theophrasti, is a medicinal herb with a long history of use in traditional medicine, particularly in Europe and the Middle East. This bulbous plant is valued for its adaptogenic properties and has been used for centuries to support respiratory health and reduce inflammation. Its primary benefits include soothing coughs, alleviating respiratory congestion, and enhancing immune function due to its high content of bioactive compounds like saponins and alkaloids. Traditionally, it was used in herbal remedies for ailments such as bronchitis and asthma, and it was also incorporated into culinary practices as a spice with a distinctive, slightly bitter flavor. In modern wellness, it is increasingly recognized for its potential in natural remedies and is used in supplements and herbal formulations to support lung health and overall vitality.
