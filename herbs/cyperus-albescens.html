What's the taxonomical classification of Cyperus albescens?

Cyperus albescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Poales and the family Cyperaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cyperus, and its unique species designation, albescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus albescens has a perennial growth habit characterized by triangular stems, lanceolate leaves, and dense, terminal inflorescences consisting of multiple spikelets. The stems are typically slender and upright, emerging from a creeping rhizome system that allows the plant to form small colonies. The foliage consists of bright green, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal or near-basal manner. Its flowering structures are composed of numerous small, brownish or yellowish spikelets clustered together at the apex of the culm. These spikelets contain tiny flowers protected by scales, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.
