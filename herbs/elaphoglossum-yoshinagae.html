Elaphoglossum yoshinagae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elaphoglossum yoshinagae has a creeping rhizome with small, ovate leaves and a single, non-resupinate labellum characterized by a distinct trilobed structure and a fleshy callus. The inflorescence emerges from the rhizome, bearing small, greenish-white flowers that lack a prominent spur. Each flower features a narrow, elongated lip with intricate textures on the midlobe. The sepals and petals are relatively small and inconspicuous compared to the complex structure of the labellum. This orchid's morphology is specifically adapted for specialized pollination mechanisms within its forest habitat.
