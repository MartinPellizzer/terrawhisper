Lathyrus palustris

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lathyrus palustris has slender, climbing stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and are characterized by small, ovate leaflets arranged in pairs. Each leaf features a terminal tendril used for climbing through dense wetland vegetation. The inflorescence consists of pea-like flowers that are predominantly pale blue or violet in color. These flowers are borne on long peduncles and possess the characteristic keel, wings, and standard petals typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant also develops elongated, cylindrical seed pods that dehisce to release small, dark seeds.
