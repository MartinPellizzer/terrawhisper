Cyananthus longiflorus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyananthus longiflorus has long, tubular, violet-blue flowers with spreading lobes, basal rosette-forming leaves, and a slender, creeping rhizome. The corolla is distinctly elongated and features a narrow tube that widens significantly at the mouth. Its leaves are typically lanceolate or elliptical in shape, arranged in a dense cluster near the ground. The plant develops from a woody rhizome that allows it to spread slowly across rocky substrates. Small, inconspicuous bracts often surround the base of the floral structures.
