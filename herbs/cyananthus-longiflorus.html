Cyananthus longiflorus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyananthus longiflorus?

Cyananthus longiflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is further categorized into the family Campanulaceae, which houses the genus Cyananthus, and is specifically identified by its species designation, longiflorus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyananthus longiflorus has long, tubular, violet-blue flowers with spreading lobes, basal rosette-forming leaves, and a slender, creeping rhizome. Its leaves are typically lanceolate or elliptical in shape, arranged in a dense cluster near the ground. The plant develops from a woody rhizome that allows it to spread slowly across rocky substrates. Small, inconspicuous bracts often surround the base of the floral structures.
