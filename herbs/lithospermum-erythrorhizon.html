Red root

Lithospermum erythrorhizon

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Red Root, scientifically known as Lithospermum erythrorhizon, belongs to the Boraginaceae family. Commonly called red root or Chinese red root, it is a perennial herb native to East Asia. The plant features a thick, red-colored root, ovate leaves, and violet flowers. It has been referred to by various synonyms in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Red Root contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They are believed to support the body's natural healing processes.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Integumentary Acne, skin infections, wound healing Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antifungal, antiviral
Respiratory Respiratory infections, mucosal inflammation, asthma, bronchitis Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, expectorant, antiviral
Immune Inflammation, allergic reactions, immune system support, malaria, cancer Anti-inflammatory, antihistaminic, immunostimulant, antimicrobial, immunomodulatory

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for treating skin conditions and as an antiseptic due to its active compound shikonin.

Extract: Used for treating wounds and inflammation due to its high concentration of active compounds.

Tincture: Used for treating wounds and as an antiseptic due to its alcohol-based extraction method.

Safety Profile

Red Root is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential harm to the fetus or infant. It may also interact with anticoagulant medications, increasing the risk of bleeding. Use under medical supervision is recommended for individuals with bleeding disorders or those undergoing surgery.
