Lithospermum erythrorhizon

What's the taxonomical classification of Lithospermum erythrorhizon?

Lithospermum erythrorhizon belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Boraginales and the family Boraginaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Lithospermum, specifically identified by the species name erythrorhizon.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lithospermum erythrorhizon contains various naphthoquinone derivatives, most notably shikonin, alkannin, and their acetylated forms. These primary bioactive compounds are responsible for the plant's deep red pigment and its significant pharmacological properties. Beyond these naphthoquinones, the roots also possess various fatty acids, sterols, and triterpenoids that contribute to its medicinal efficacy. Research indicates that these substances provide anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing effects when applied topically. The synergistic interaction of these diverse chemical constituents makes the plant a valuable component in traditional dermatological treatments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lithospermum erythrorhizon?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, dermatitis, psoriasis, and various types of inflammatory rashes. It contains shikonin and other naphthoquinones that provide potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties to the topical preparations. These medicinal qualities help to reduce swelling and soothe irritation on the surface of the skin. In traditional practices, it is often applied to wounds or ulcers to promote faster healing and prevent infection. The extract is frequently incorporated into ointments and creams specifically formulated for dermatological care.
