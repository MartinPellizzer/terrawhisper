As discussed in a study, Illicium difengpi is well-known for its stem barks that have been widely used in Traditional Chinese Medicine for the therapy of rheumatoid arthritis and traumatic injury. A phytochemical investigation on the branches and leaves of this plant led to the isolation of one undescribed neolignan and three known lignans. The new compound, named difengpienol C, showed the strongest anti-inflammatory activity in lipopolysaccharide (LPS)-stimulated RAW264.7 cells. This compound powerfully inhibited the production of nitric oxide (NO), interleukin 6 (IL-6), and tumor necrosis factor α (TNF-α) while suppressing the mRNA transcription of iNOS, IL-6, and TNF-α. Additionally, difengpienol C blocked the activation of the TLR4/MyD88/NF-κB signaling pathway. Therefore, the non-traditional medicinal parts of Illicium difengpi can be identified as a source of natural anti-inflammatory molecules.
