Illicium difengpi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illicium difengpi has evergreen leaves that are lanceolate or elliptic in shape, featuring entire margins and a glossy, leathery texture. The leaf arrangement is typically alternate along the woody stems of this small to medium-sized shrub. These plants produce small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into distinctive star-shaped, woody follicles. The fruit consists of these aggregate follicles which often contain aromatic seeds used in traditional practices. Mature specimens exhibit a dense branching pattern that contributes to a compact growth habit.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the mountainous subtropical regions of southern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan, Guangxi, Guizhou, and Guangdong. It thrives primarily in evergreen broad-leaved forests where moisture levels remain consistently high. The species is typically found growing in the shaded understory of these forest ecosystems. Its specific habitat requirements limit its natural range to these localized subtropical zones. Because it is a narrow endemic, its distribution is restricted to these specific high-altitude environments.
