Ixeris chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ixeris chinensis?

Ixeris chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the biological hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the lineage further, it is placed in the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Ixeris, and its species name, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ixeris chinensis has slender, creeping rhizomes that produce narrow, linear leaves and small, pale pink to white tubular flowers. The leaves are typically thin and grass-like, measuring only a few millimeters in width. Its floral structure consists of small, inconspicuous petals that emerge from a compact inflorescence. The plant often forms dense mats through its spreading underground stems. These morphological traits allow it to colonize moist or muddy habitats effectively.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ixeris chinensis?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-leukemic, hepatoprotective, immunomodulatory, and antidepressant pharmacological activities. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively reduce swelling and pain by inhibiting inflammatory mediators. The extracts also demonstrate significant potential in suppressing leukemia cell growth and protecting liver cells from chemical damage. Furthermore, the plant exhibits the ability to regulate immune system responses to maintain balance. Studies suggest that its chemical constituents may also assist in managing depressive symptoms through neurological modulation.

According to a study published by "International journal of biological macromolecules", an arabinogalactan (ICPA) extracted from Ixeris chinensis (Thunb.) Nakai. exhibited excellent immunomodulatory activity. ICPA ameliorated symptoms of colitis in mice, including loss of body weight, decrease of disease activity index, shortness of colon length, and reduction of spleen index caused by DSS. The treatment inhibited the overproduction of TNF-α, IL-1β, and NLRP3 while promoting the secretion of IL-10 in colon tissues. It also restored intestinal barrier integrity by increasing the expression of ZO-1 and occluidin and regulated gut microbiota by elevating Turicibacter and Bifidobacterium and decreasing the Bacteroidetes/Firmicutes ratio. Furthermore, ICPA improved depression-like behavior and reduced the expression of NLRP3, GFAP, and Iba-1 proteins in brain tissues.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive medicinal compounds including Chinensiolide B, Chinensiolide C, Chinensiolide E, Ixerisoside A, and Ixerochinoside. These specific chemical constituents are secondary metabolites that contribute to the therapeutic properties of the species. Researchers focus on these molecules to understand how the plant interacts with biological systems. Detailed phytochemical analysis reveals that these substances belong to distinct classes of natural products. Studying these compounds helps scientists identify potential applications for future pharmacological development.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", fifteen compounds were isolated and identified from Ixeris chinensis through silica gel column chromatography, HPLC, and recrystallization. These identified constituents include Methyl-4-hydroxyphenylacetate (1), Daucosterol (2), Sitosterol (3), Luteolin-7-O-beta-D-glucoside (4), 3,11 (13)-triene-12,6-lactone (5), Chinensiolide B (6), Chinensiolide E (7), Ixerochinoside (8), Chinensiolide C (9), 10alpha-hydroxy-guaia-4(15)-ene-12,6-lactone (10), 10alpha-hydroxy-guaia-4 (15), 11 (13)-diene-12,6-lactone-3beta-O-beta-D-(6'-p-hydroxyphenylacetyl) glucopyranoside (11), Epiloliolide (12), Apigenin-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (13), Luteolin (14), and Lutein (15). Among these, compounds 1, 10, 11, 12, and 15 were isolated from this plant for the first time. The structures of these compounds were elucidated using spectral analysis.

Furthermore, ICPA improves depression-like behavior by reducing the expression of proteins NLRP3, GFAP, and Iba-1 in brain tissues.
