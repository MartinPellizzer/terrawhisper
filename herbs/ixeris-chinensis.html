Ixeris chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ixeris chinensis?

Ixeris chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the biological hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the lineage further, it is placed in the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Ixeris, and its species name, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ixeris chinensis has slender, creeping rhizomes that produce narrow, linear leaves and small, pale pink to white tubular flowers. The leaves are typically thin and grass-like, measuring only a few millimeters in width. Its floral structure consists of small, inconspicuous petals that emerge from a compact inflorescence. The plant often forms dense mats through its spreading underground stems. These morphological traits allow it to colonize moist or muddy habitats effectively.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically including countries such as China, Vietnam, Thailand, and Malaysia. It thrives in wetland habitats like marshes, riverbanks, and shallow ponds throughout these humid climates. The species is frequently found in moist soils where it can access consistent water sources. Its distribution is primarily constrained by its preference for warm temperatures and high moisture levels. Consequently, it is rarely located in arid or temperate zones far from coastal or freshwater ecosystems.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ixeris chinensis?

Ixeris chinensis has therapeutic properties that function as a diuretic, bitter, anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, hemostatic, and cholagogue agent. Its bitter components stimulate the digestive system and promote bile flow through its cholagogue action. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Apigenin-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, Luteolin, Luteolin-7-O-beta-D-glucosides, Sitosterol, and sesquiterpene lactones. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile of the species. The presence of flavonoids like Luteolin and its glucosides often provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the sterol Sitosterol supports various cellular functions within the organism. Finally, the sesquiterpene lactones play a crucial role in the plant's complex biological activities.

As discussed in a study, fifteen compounds were isolated from Ixeris chinensis using silica gel column chromatography, HPLC, and recrystallization. These constituents include Methyl-4-hydroxyphenylacetate (1), Daucosterol (2), Sitosterol (3), Luteolin-7-O-beta-D-glucoside (4), 3,11 (13)-triene-12,6-lactone (5), Chinensiolide B (6), Chinensiolide E (7), Ixerochinoside (8), Chinensiolide C (9), 10alpha-hydroxy-guaia-4(15)-ene-12,6-lactone (10), 10alpha-hydroxy-guaia-4 (15), 11 (13)-diene-12,6-lactone-3beta-O-beta-D-(6'-p-hydroxyphenylacetyl) glucopyranoside (11), Epiloliolide (12), Apigenin-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (13), Luteolin (14), and Lutein (15). The structural analysis identified that compounds 1, 10, 11, 12, and 15 are being isolated from this plant for the first time. These specific chemical constituents were elucidated through spectral analysis.
