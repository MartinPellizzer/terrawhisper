What's the taxonomical classification of Murdannia triquetra?

Murdannia triquetra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Commelinales. This plant is a representative of the family Commelineaceae, specifically falling under the genus Murdannia, with its specific designation being the species triquetra.

      What are the herbal preparations of Murdannia triquetra?

      Murdannia triquetra is prepared primarily as a decoction or an aqueous infusion made from its dried aerial parts to extract its medicinal properties. The plant material is typically boiled in water for a specific duration to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. Some traditional practices may also involve using the crushed fresh leaves to create topical poultices for localized applications.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Murdannia triquetra can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or localized swelling when direct contact is made with the plant's tissues. While extensive toxicological studies on humans are limited, the presence of certain phytochemicals may trigger sensitivity in individuals with predispositions to plant allergies. If the plant material is ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach cramping. Precise systemic toxicity levels remain poorly documented in current scientific literature. Therefore, caution is advised when handling the specimen to avoid unexpected dermatological reactions.
