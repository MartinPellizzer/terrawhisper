Dysosmoma delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dysosma delavayi has erect, glabrous stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and bear alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins.

The leaf blades are generally larger toward the base of the plant and decrease in size as they ascend the stem. Small, yellowish-green flowers are produced in terminal, many-flowered cymes that emerge from the upper leaf axils. Each flower features a five-lobed corolla and is supported by a structure characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant also develops small, three-lobed capsules that contain tiny, smooth seeds upon maturity.
