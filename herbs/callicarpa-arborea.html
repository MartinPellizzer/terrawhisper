Callicarpa arborea

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa arborea?

Callicarpa arborea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Specifically, it is identified under the genus Callicarpa, with its unique biological designation being the species arborea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Callicarpa arborea contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including terpenoids, flavonoids, tannins, and polyphenolic compounds that provide its medicinal properties. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers have identified specific triterpenoids within the leaves and bark that may offer protective effects against cellular damage. Additionally, the presence of various phenolic acids suggests potential antimicrobial capabilities. These diverse compounds work together to form the basis for its traditional use in treating various ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Callicarpa arborea?

Callicarpa arborea has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and insect-repellent properties derived from its high concentration of terpenoids and phenolic compounds. These bioactive constituents allow the plant to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. When applied topically, the extracts help reduce skin irritation and soothe localized swelling. The essential oils present in the foliage and fruit also serve as a natural deterrent against mosquitoes and other biting insects. This multifaceted chemical profile makes the species a valuable subject for developing natural medicinal and protective topical treatments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and various parasitic ailments. Extracts from the leaves and bark are often applied topically to soothe irritation and reduce swelling. Some traditional medicinal practices utilize the plant to combat fungal growths on the body. The chemical compounds within the foliage are believed to possess antimicrobial properties that aid in healing. These applications are common in specific regional herbal traditions.
