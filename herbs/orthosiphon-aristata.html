Scientific and Botanical Profile

Orthosiphon Aristata, with botanical name orthosiphon aristata, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Labiatae family, commonly known as the mint family.

Native to Indonesia, Malaysia, Southeast Asia, the Philippines, and other regions such as Sumatra, Java, Borneo, and the Sunda Islands, it is also found in parts of Timor, Sulawesi, Moluccas, New Guinea, and even Tibet. Commonly referred to as Java Tea, Water Hyssop, Java Catnip, Java Cat's Whiskers, Java Mint, Bacopa, Cat's Whiskers, Water Plantain, Orthosiphon Mint, Water Basil, Orthosiphon Herb, Karela, Kaphal, and Bitter Leaf, this plant is widely recognized for its medicinal properties. Morphologically, it features square, hairy stems, opposite leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Its aromatic leaves and adaptability to various climates have made it a popular herb in traditional medicine across its native regions.

Forms, Preparation and Usage

Orthosiphon aristata has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, with various forms available including fresh leaves, dried tincture, powder, essential oil, and capsule.

It can be prepared as a tea by steeping dried leaves in hot water, or as a decoction by boiling the plant material for a longer period. An infusion can also be made by soaking the dried leaves in water for several hours, while topical applications involve using the plant in the form of a poultice or essential oil diluted in a carrier. The recommended dosage for adults is typically 1 to 2 grams of dried leaves per cup of water, taken up to three times daily, while for children, it should only be used under medical supervision.

Due to the lack of extensive clinical studies, usage should be conservative, with short-term application preferred unless otherwise advised by a healthcare professional.

Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Orthosiphon aristata can be used as a medicinal plant for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties, but it is important to consider its safety profile.

While generally considered safe when used in moderation, it may cause side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, dizziness, and allergic reactions in some individuals. It may interact with medications like diuretics, antihypertensives, and diabetes drugs, potentially increasing their effects or causing adverse outcomes. Special populations, including pregnant and breastfeeding women, should avoid its use due to insufficient safety data, and individuals with chronic conditions like kidney disease should consult a healthcare provider before use.

To ensure safe use, it is recommended to follow recommended dosages, consult with a healthcare professional, and discontinue use if any adverse effects occur.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Orthosiphon aristata grows best in well-drained, sandy or loamy soil with a pH between 6.

0 and 7. 5, in a warm and sunny location with at least 6 hours of direct sunlight per day. It requires consistent moisture during the growing season but should not be overwatered, as it is susceptible to root rot. Regular pruning and weeding help maintain plant health and encourage new growth, while mulching can retain soil moisture and suppress weeds. Harvesting is best done in the early morning or late afternoon, when the plant's essential oils are most concentrated, by cutting the leaves and stems just above the base using clean, sharp shears.

To preserve its potency, the harvested material should be thoroughly dried in a shaded, well-ventilated area before being stored in airtight containers in a cool, dry place, or refrigerated to extend shelf life.
