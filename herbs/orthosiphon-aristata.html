Java Tea (Orthosiphon aristata)

Java Tea (Orthosiphon aristata) is a member of the Labiatae family, native to Southeast Asia, Indonesia, and Malay Peninsula. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Laminae with serrulate margins, upper surface glaucous green, lower surface pale green, stipulate with two pairs of subulate stipules.
Flowers
Small, tubular flowers arranged in dense spikes, white to pale violet in color, actinomorphic, with two lobes at the base and two lobes at the apex, each lobe bearing a distinct glandular appendage.
Stems
Erect, unbranched, glabrous, woody, with opposite, ovate, acuminate leaves.
