Papaya (Carica papaya)

Papaya (Carica papaya) is a member of the Caricaceae family, native to Central America, Northern South America, and Southern North America. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and seeds have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, carminative, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and native american herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters.
Leaves
Simple, broad, acuminate leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface pale green, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Radial symmetry, yellow-orange petals in multiples of five, clustered in a syncarpous aggregate fruit with a single pistil and stamens arranged in two whorls.
Stems
Erect, simple, herbaceous, glabrous, terete, with persistent basal leaves and a terminal inflorescence.
