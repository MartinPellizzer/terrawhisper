Solanecio biafrae

What's the taxonomical classification of Solanecio biafrae?

Solanecio biafrae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through the hierarchical ranks, the plant falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Solanecio, with the species name being biafrae.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Solanecio biafrae has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, green stems and oppositely arranged, ovate leaves with entire margins.

The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a relatively thin lamina compared to other members of its genus. Its reproductive structures consist of small, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers possess a five-lobed corolla, which is often pale or white in color.

Once pollination occurs, the plant produces small, globose berries that contain numerous tiny seeds.

        Solanecio biafrae is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the specific medicinal application required. If consumed, the chemical compounds can disrupt the central nervous system and lead to gastrointestinal distress. Symptoms may escalate rapidly depending on the concentration of the toxin and the individual's sensitivity.
