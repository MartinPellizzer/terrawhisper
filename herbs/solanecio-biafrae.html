Solanecio biafrae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Solanecio biafrae has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, green stems and oppositely arranged, ovate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a relatively thin lamina compared to other members of its genus. Its reproductive structures consist of small, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers possess a five-lobed corolla, which is often pale or white in color. Once pollination occurs, the plant produces small, globose berries that contain numerous tiny seeds.
