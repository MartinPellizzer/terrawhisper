Buddleja davidii

What's the taxonomical classification of Buddleja davidii?

Buddleja davidii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Lamiales and the family Scrophulariaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Buddleja, where it is identified as the species davidii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buddlejia davidii has opposite, lanceolate leaves that measure between two and six inches in length and are characterized by a serrated margin and a soft, velvety texture on their underside. These leaves are typically a deep green color and possess prominent pinnate venation. The plant produces large, arching panicles of small, tubular flowers that can range in color from deep purple to pale lavender or white. Each individual blossom features four petals and contains numerous stamens that emerge from the center. The stems are woody at the base and can reach several feet in height, providing a sturdy structure for the dense floral clusters.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to central and western China, specifically inhabiting mountain regions where it grows in sunny, well-drained spots. Its natural range spans various provinces including Sichuan, Hubei, and Yunnan. Because of its hardiness, it has been widely introduced to temperate regions across Europe, North America, and parts of Australia. In many of these non-native locations, the species has escaped cultivation and can be found growing wild along roadsides and riverbanks. Consequently, it is often categorized as an invasive species in certain ecosystems due to its ability to spread rapidly.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, commonly known as butterfly bush, requires full sun exposure and well-draining soil to thrive in most temperate garden settings.

It is a vigorous grower that benefits from regular pruning in early spring to encourage abundant flowering and maintain a manageable size. While it is relatively drought tolerant once established, consistent moisture during the first growing season helps ensure strong root development. Because it can spread aggressively through seed dispersal, many gardeners choose sterile cultivars to prevent it from becoming invasive in local ecosystems.

Providing ample space between plantings will help prevent overcrowding and promote healthy air circulation.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", the chemical constitutes of Buddlejia davidii were isolated and purified using silica gel column chromatography, polyamide column chromatography, and macroporous absorption resin.
