Crepis napifera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crepis napifera has a perennial growth habit characterized by a basal rosette of leaves and upright, branched flowering stems that reach heights of up to 50 centimeters. The leaves are typically oblong or lanceolate with dentate margins and are arranged mostly at the base of the plant. Its inflorescence consists of several yellow flower heads organized in a corymb-like arrangement at the ends of the stems. Each head contains numerous individual florets that eventually produce seeds equipped with a pappus for wind dispersal. The overall structure is slender and herbaceous, allowing it to thrive in various meadow environments.

      What are the herbal preparations of Crepis napifera?

      Crepis napifera is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using the dried or fresh aerial parts of the plant. The leaves and flowering stems are steeped in hot water to create medicinal teas used for internal consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Crepis napifera can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals due to its chemical composition. Contact with the plant's juices may lead to redness, itching, or a localized rash on the skin. While direct toxicity is not widely documented in humans, ingestion should be avoided to prevent unknown gastrointestinal distress. Some people may experience heightened sensitivity if the plant material is crushed and applied to broken skin. Scientific data regarding systemic toxicity remains limited, so caution is advised when handling this species.
