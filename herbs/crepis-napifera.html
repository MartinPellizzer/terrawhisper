Crepis napifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Crepis napifera?

Crepis napifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae and falls under the genus Crepis, specifically identified by its species name, napifera.

Consequently, it is not currently utilized in herbal remedies or pharmaceutical preparations.

    What are the herbal preparations of Crepis napifera?

    Crepis napifera is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using the dried or fresh aerial parts of the plant. The leaves and flowering stems are steeped in hot water to create medicinal teas used for internal consumption. For external applications, the crushed plant matter is applied directly to the skin as a poultice to address minor irritations. Some practitioners also utilize alcohol-based tinctures to extract the plant's active compounds for long-term storage. These various preparations allow for the targeted delivery of the plant's properties depending on the specific therapeutic need.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Crepis napifera can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals due to its chemical composition. Contact with the plant's juices may lead to redness, itching, or a localized rash on the skin. While direct toxicity is not widely documented in humans, ingestion should be avoided to prevent unknown gastrointestinal distress. Some people may experience heightened sensitivity if the plant material is crushed and applied to broken skin. Scientific data regarding systemic toxicity remains limited, so caution is advised when handling this species.
