Cashew (Anacardium occidentale)

Cashew (Anacardium occidentale) is a member of the Anacardiaceae family, native to Southeastern United States, Caribbean, and Central America. Traditionally, its fruits, bark, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and native american herbal medicine.
