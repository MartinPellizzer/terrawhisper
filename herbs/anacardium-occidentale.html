Cashew

Anacardium occidentale

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Cashew (Anacardium occidentale) is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America. It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is known by regional names like "anacardium" and "kaju." The plant has a deep root system, compound leaves, and produces yellowish-green flowers. It is also referred to by various synonyms in herbal medicine texts.

Active Compounds

Cashew contains key bioactive compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Bronchitis, asthma, respiratory infections, tuberculosis Expectorant, bronchodilator, antimicrobial, immunostimulant, antihistaminic
Integumentary Psoriasis, eczema, skin infections, skin rash, wound healing Anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, antifungal
Musculoskeletal Rheumatoid arthritis, gout, joint inflammation, rheumatism Anti-inflammatory

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and digestive disorders.

Decoction: Used for digestive ailments and skin conditions.

Extract: Used for inflammatory conditions and pain relief.

Safety Profile

Cashew is generally safe when consumed in moderation. However, it contains urushiol, which can cause skin irritation in some individuals. People with allergies to cashew or other anacardiaceae plants should avoid it.
