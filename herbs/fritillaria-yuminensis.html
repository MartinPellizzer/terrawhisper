Fritillaria yuminensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Fritillaria yuminensis?

Fritillaria yuminenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Liliaceae and is further categorized into the genus Fritillaria, specifically identifying as the species yuminenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Friillaria yuminenis has bulbous rhizomes, slender upright stems, and nodding bell-shaped flowers that are typically reddish-brown with dark spots. The leaves are lanceolate in shape and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. Each flower features six tepals that curve outward slightly at the tips. Small, scale-like leaves may also be present near the base of the plant. The overall structure is characteristic of high-altitude bulbous perennials found in alpine regions.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of Sichuan Province in China. It grows specifically within the montane environments of the eastern Tibetan Plateau. Its distribution is limited to certain mountain ranges where the climate supports its unique physiological needs. Populations are typically found in rocky slopes or alpine meadows at significant elevations. This localized range makes the species a specialized inhabitant of its specific mountain habitat.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining, gritty soil and a period of cold dormancy to thrive in temperate climates.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive medicinal alkaloids including imperialine, imperialine-β-D-glucosides, pengbeisine A, walujewine A, and yubeinine. These specific chemical constituents are categorized as lycorine-type alkaloids which often exhibit significant pharmacological properties. Researchers study these compounds to understand their potential impact on various biological pathways and therapeutic applications. The presence of these diverse molecules contributes to the complex chemical profile of the species. Understanding the distribution of these alkaloids is essential for advancing botanical pharmacology.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", twelve alkaloids were isolated from the bulbs of Fritillaria yuminensis using column chromatography over silica gel, ODS, and Sephadex LH-20, along with RP-HPLC. The identified structures include yubeinine(1), imperialine(2), delavinone(3), tortifoline(4), hupehenizioiside(5), imperialine-β-D-glucoside(6), kuroyurinidine(7), pengbeisine A(8), walujewine A(9), peimisine-3-O-β-D-glucopyranoside(10), solanidine-3-O-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1→2)-β-D-glucopyranoside(11), and solanidine-3-O-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1→2)-[β-D-glucopyranosyl-(1→4)]-β-D-glucopyranoside(12). These structures were mainly identified through NMR and MS analyses. Compounds 4-12 were obtained from F. yuminensis for the first time.
