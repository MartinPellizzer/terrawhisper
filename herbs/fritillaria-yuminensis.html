Fritillaria yuminensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Fritillaria yuminensis?

Fritillaria yuminenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Liliaceae and is further categorized into the genus Fritillaria, specifically identifying as the species yuminenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Friillaria yuminenis has bulbous rhizomes, slender upright stems, and nodding bell-shaped flowers that are typically reddish-brown with dark spots. The leaves are lanceolate in shape and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. Each flower features six tepals that curve outward slightly at the tips. Small, scale-like leaves may also be present near the base of the plant. The overall structure is characteristic of high-altitude bulbous perennials found in alpine regions.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the medicinal compounds imperialine, imperialine-β-D-glucoside, solanidine-3-O-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1→2)-[β-D-glucopyranosyl-(1→4)]-β-D-glucopyranoside, solanidine-3-O-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1→2)-β-D-glucopyranoside, and yubeinine. These specific alkaloids and steroidal glycosides play significant roles in the plant's chemical profile. Imperialine and its glucoside derivatives are often studied for their diverse biological activities. The complex solanidine glycosides contribute to the specialized chemical structure of the species. Furthermore, the presence of yubeinine highlights the unique alkaloid composition found within the plant tissues.

    As discussed in a study, twelve alkaloids were isolated from the bulbs of Fritillaria yuminenensis using column chromatography over silica gel, ODS, and Sephadex LH-20, as well as RP-HPLC. The identified structures included yubeinine(1), imperialine(2), delavinone(3), tortifoline(4), hupehenizioiside(5), imperialine-beta-D-glucoside(6), kuroyurinidine(7), pengbeisine A(8), walujewine A(9), peimisine-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside(10), solanidine-3-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1 to 2)-beta-D-glucopyranoside(11), and solanidine-3-O-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1 to 2)-[beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1 to 4)]-beta-D-glucopyranoside(12). Compounds 4-12 were obtained from Friitillaria yuminenensis for the first time. These specific compounds were identified mainly through NMR and MS analyses.
