Fritillaria yuminensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Fritillaria yuminensis?

Fritillaria yuminenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Liliaceae and is further categorized into the genus Fritillaria, specifically identifying as the species yuminenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Friillaria yuminenis has bulbous rhizomes, slender upright stems, and nodding bell-shaped flowers that are typically reddish-brown with dark spots. The leaves are lanceolate in shape and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. Each flower features six tepals that curve outward slightly at the tips. Small, scale-like leaves may also be present near the base of the plant. The overall structure is characteristic of high-altitude bulbous perennials found in alpine regions.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal alkaloids imperialine, imperialine-β-D-glucoside, pengbeisine A, walujewine A, and yubeinine. These specific chemical compounds are primarily found within the alkaloid profile of the species. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential pharmacological activities and therapeutic properties. Each molecule contributes to the complex chemical makeup that defines the plant's biological profile. Understanding the distribution of these alkaloids is essential for botanical and medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, twelve alkaloids were isolated from the bulbs of Fritillaria yuminensis using column chromatography over silica gel, ODS, and Sephadex LH-20, as well as RP-HPLC. These identified structures include yubeinine(1), imperialine(2), delavinone(3), tortifoline(4), hupehenizioiside(5), imperialine-β-D-glucoside(6), kuroyurinidine(7), pengbeisine A(8), walujewine A(9), peimisine-3-O-β-D-glucopyranoside(10), solanidine-3-O-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1→2)-β-D-glucopyranoside(11), and solanidine-3-O-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1→2)-[β-D-glucopyranosyl-(1→4)]-β-D-glucopyranoside(12). The study utilized NMR and MS analyses to identify these specific components. Notably, compounds 4-12 were obtained from F. yuminensis for the first time.
