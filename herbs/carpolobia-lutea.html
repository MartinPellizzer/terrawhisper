Carpolobia lutea

What's the taxonomical classification of Carpolobia lutea?

Carpolobia lutea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Fabales. More specifically, this plant is a member of the family Polygalaceae, falling under the genus Carpolobia, with its specific designation being the species lutea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carpolobia lutea has erect, woody stems characterized by a rough texture and small, alternate, ovate leaves that possess serrated margins and prominent venation.

The foliage is typically a deep green color, providing a stark contrast to the bright yellow flowers that emerge from the axillary buds. These blossoms are actinomorphic in shape and feature five distinct petals arranged symmetrically around a central cluster of stamens. The reproductive structures are well-developed, supporting the eventual formation of small, fleshy fruits.

Each morphological feature serves to optimize the plant's ability to thrive in its specific ecological niche.

        What are the herbal preparations of Carpolobia lutea?

        Carpolobia lutea is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste derived from its leaves and bark. Traditional practitioners often simmer the plant parts in water to extract active alkaloids for oral consumption. These liquid preparations are frequently used to treat internal ailments through controlled dosages.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Carpolobia lutea can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or touched. Contact with the plant's sap often results in localized redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If the plant is consumed, individuals may experience intense nausea, abdominal cramping, and vomiting. Some reports suggest that higher doses could lead to dizziness or lightheadedness. It is crucial to use protective gear when handling this species to avoid these adverse reactions.
