Carpolobia lutea

What's the taxonomical classification of Carpolobia lutea?

Carpolobia lutea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Fabales. More specifically, this plant is a member of the family Polygalaceae, falling under the genus Carpolobia, with its specific designation being the species lutea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Carpolobia lutea contains various secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids that exhibit significant biological activities. These chemical constituents are primarily studied for their potential antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Researchers have identified specific glycosides within the plant that may assist in regulating cellular responses to oxidative stress. Additionally, certain alkaloids present in the leaves and stems are being investigated for their sedative and analgesic effects. Understanding these specific molecular structures is essential for developing future pharmacological applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Carpolobia lutea?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including respiratory infections, digestive issues, skin conditions, and inflammatory disorders. The medicinal properties of its leaves and bark are often harnessed to alleviate symptoms of coughs and bronchitis. In traditional practices, decoctions are prepared to settle stomach upsets and combat gastrointestinal parasites. Some practitioners also apply topical preparations to soothe wounds or manage dermatological irritations. These diverse applications highlight the plant's significance in regional ethnobotanical knowledge.
