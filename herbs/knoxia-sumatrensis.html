Knoxia sumatrensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Knoxia sumatrensis has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender, greenish stems and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically exhibits a smooth texture and prominent venation that extends toward the leaf edges. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in dense, terminal or axillary clusters. These floral structures are supported by delicate pedicels and lead to the development of small, capsule-like fruits. The overall morphological structure reflects its adaptation to the humid, shaded environments of tropical forest understories.
