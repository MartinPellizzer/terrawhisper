Garcinia mangostana

What's the taxonomical classification of Garcinia mangostana?

Garcina mangostana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Clusiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Garcinia, and its unique species designation, mangostanana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Garcinia mangostanana has small, elliptical, dark green leaves with an entire margin and a coriaceous texture, woody stems, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically exhibits a shrubby or small tree-like growth habit characterized by dense branching. Its leaves are arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the branches. The flowers are often solitary or in small clusters, possessing a subtle structure typical of the Clusiaceae family. The resulting fruit is generally small and fleshy, developing from the fertilized ovaries of the blossoms.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of Southeast Asia, specifically occurring within the terrestrial ecosystems of Malaysia, Thailand, and parts of Indonesia. It thrives in the humid, shaded understory of lowland evergreen forests where high rainfall is consistent throughout the year. The species is typically found in areas characterized by nutrient-rich soils and dense canopy cover. Localized populations are often restricted to specific microclimates that support its specialized growth requirements. Due to its niche habitat preferences, its range is closely tied to the preservation of undisturbed tropical forest tracts.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Garcinia mangostana contains medicinal properties primarily found in its pericarp, which is the thick outer rind of the fruit. This rind is exceptionally rich in xanthones, a group of bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Practitioners often use extracts from this skin to treat various ailments, including skin infections and digestive issues. Additionally, the fruit pulp and juice are sometimes utilized for their nutritional benefits and potential antimicrobial qualities. Some traditional practices also involve using the seeds or bark, though the rind remains the most significant part for therapeutic purposes.

What are the pharmacological activities of Garcinia mangostana?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, anti-cancer, antibacterial, and antimicrobial properties. The presence of bioactive xanthones allows it to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in biological systems. Its chemical compounds also inhibit the growth of various pathogens, demonstrating significant antibacterial and antimicrobial efficacy. Furthermore, studies indicate that its extracts can suppress tumor cell proliferation, contributing to its anti-cancer potential. These diverse medicinal qualities make it a significant subject for drug development and therapeutic research.

In a 28-week randomised, multisite, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial, 70 participants were randomly allocated to mangosteen and 82 were allocated to a placebo control. Of these, 50 participants in the mangosteen condition completed the treatment period and were analysed. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several potent medicinal compounds including alpha-mangostin, beta-mangostin, gamma-mangostin, garcinone B, and gartanin. These specific xanthones and related derivatives are primarily concentrated within the plant's rind or pericarp. Researchers study these substances for their significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The presence of alpha-mangostin is often highlighted due to its ability to inhibit certain cellular processes related to disease. Together, these bioactive molecules represent the primary chemical defense and therapeutic potential of the species.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be processed into various herbal preparations including concentrated extract, dried powder, fresh juice, essential oil, and alcohol-based tincture. Fresh juice from the fruit is often utilized for its immediate nutritional and digestive properties. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, diarrhea, digestive upset, headache, dizziness, and potential liver toxicity in some individuals. Users frequently report gastrointestinal distress such as stomach pain or irregular bowel movements after consumption. Some people also experience neurological symptoms including frequent headaches and a sense of dizziness. More serious concerns involve the risk of liver toxicity if the plant is taken in excessive amounts or over long periods. It is important to monitor how your body reacts to these specific side effects.
