Garcinia mangostana

What's the taxonomical classification of Garcinia mangostana?

Garcina mangostana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Clusiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Garcinia, and its unique species designation, mangostanana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Garcinia mangostana contains high concentrations of xanthones, specifically alpha-mangostin, which possess potent antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial properties. These specialized polyphenolic compounds are primarily found within the pericarp or rind of the fruit. Research indicates that these bioactive molecules can inhibit the growth of various pathogens and reduce oxidative stress in human cells. Additionally, the plant contains various vitamins, minerals, and organic acids that contribute to its overall nutritional profile. These combined chemical constituents make the fruit a significant subject in pharmacological studies regarding metabolic and immune health.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, fungal diseases, stomach ailments, and various inflammatory conditions. Its bioactive compounds, particularly xanthones, provide potent antimicrobial and antioxidant properties that combat harmful pathogens. Extracts are frequently applied topically to manage wounds and dermatological irritations. In traditional medicine, it is also utilized to alleviate digestive issues and internal discomfort. These medicinal applications stem from the plant's ability to reduce oxidative stress within the body.
