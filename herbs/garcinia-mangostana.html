Mangosteen

Garcinia mangostana

Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana) is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, known for its striking purple rind and sweet, segmented fruit, and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to support digestive health and reduce inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Mangosteen, scientifically known as Garcinia mangostana, belongs to the Clusiaceae family. It is also called "Java apple" in some regions. A tropical perennial, it is native to Southeast Asia. The plant has dark green leaves, fragrant white flowers, and a distinctive red rind enclosing its segmented white flesh. Synonyms in herbal literature include "mangostan" and "garcinia."

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of this herb include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins. These bioactive components work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for digestive issues and inflammation due to its xanthones content.

Capsule: Used for weight management and appetite suppression.

Infusion: Used for digestive problems and as a mild diuretic.

Safety Profile

Mangosteen is generally considered safe when consumed in typical food amounts. However, it may interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting blood sugar or blood pressure. Consult a healthcare provider before using it for therapeutic purposes.
