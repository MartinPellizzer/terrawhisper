Medicinal Parts

Garcinia mangostana fruit, commonly known as the mangostan or purple mangosteen, is renowned for its vibrant color and unique flavor, but it is the pericarp, or rind, that is primarily used in traditional medicine.

The pericarp contains high concentrations of xanthones, which are potent antioxidants believed to offer anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antidiabetic properties. These compounds have been studied for their potential in supporting cardiovascular health and reducing oxidative stress in the body.

In addition to the fruit, the seeds of Garcinia mangostana are also utilized in herbal remedies, particularly for their diuretic and digestive benefits. The seeds contain compounds that may aid in reducing bloating and improving gut health. Meanwhile, the leaves of the plant are often used in traditional practices for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects, with some research suggesting they may help in managing skin conditions and supporting immune function.

Overall, various parts of Garcinia mangostana contribute to its wide range of medicinal applications, making it a valuable herb in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.

Side Effects of garcinia mangostana

Garcinia mangostana triggers diarrhea due to its laxative properties and the presence of compounds that stimulate bowel movements.

It can also cause stomach upset, as the herb may irritate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to discomfort and bloating. Some individuals may experience skin rash or allergic reactions, particularly if they are sensitive to the fruit's components.

The herb may lead to nausea and even induce vomiting in more severe cases, especially when consumed in high doses. Additionally, it may cause heartburn, cramps, and fatigue, while some users report headaches or anxiety as side effects. These symptoms are often related to the herb's impact on the digestive system and its potential to disrupt normal bodily functions.

While Garcinia mangostana is popular for its purported health benefits, it is important to use it cautiously and consult a healthcare professional before incorporating it into one’s regimen, especially for those with pre-existing health conditions or digestive sensitivities.
