Chloris virgata

What's the taxonomical classification of Chloris virgata?

Chloris virgata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Poales, it is placed under the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Chloris and the species virgata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chloris virgata has slender, creeping to erect culms that reach heights of 10 to 60 centimeters and feature branched, spreading rhizomes. The leaves are linear with a length of 2 to 15 centimeters and possess a distinctively scabrous or rough texture along the margins. Its inflorescence consists of several finger-like spikes that radiate from a single point at the top of the stem. Each spike contains numerous small, elliptical spikelets arranged in two rows along a central axis. The glumes are typically shorter than the florets and exhibit a pale green to purplish coloration.
