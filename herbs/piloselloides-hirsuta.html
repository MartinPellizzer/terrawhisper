Piloselloides hirsuta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piloselloides hirsuta has a woody, perennial habit characterized by a dense covering of long, stiff, spreading hairs across its stems and foliage. These trichomes give the plant a distinctly hirsute appearance, providing a textured surface that may assist in moisture retention. The leaves are typically small and lanceolate, often appearing crowded along the rigid branches. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The overall structural morphology is adapted to survive in specific environmental niches through its protective pubescence.
