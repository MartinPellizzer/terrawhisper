Verbascum arbusculum

Verbascum arbusculum, commonly known as Armenian mullein or woolly mullein, is a species of flowering plant in the figwort family (Scrophulariaceae).

It is known for its numerous health benefits, including its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antimicrobial properties, which have been used to treat various health conditions such as colds, sore throats, and skin irritations.

The bioactive constituents of Verbascum arbusculum include flavonoids, saponins, and glycosides, which have been found to exhibit therapeutic actions such as antioxidant, anti-cancer, and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant's therapeutic actions include soothing and calming the skin, reducing inflammation, and promoting wound healing.

Possible Side Effects of Verbascum arbusculum

Verbascum arbusculum can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritation.

Ingesting the plant can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, while topical application may lead to redness, itching, and burning sensations on the skin.

Prolonged exposure to the plant's extracts can also cause liver damage and interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications.

Furthermore, Verbascum arbusculum may exacerbate existing conditions like asthma and eczema, requiring individuals to exercise caution when handling the plant.
