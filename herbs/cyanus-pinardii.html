Cyanus pinardii

Cyanus pinardii, commonly known as Pinard's cornflower, is a plant species belonging to the family Asteraceae.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions, with its health benefits including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The therapeutic actions of Cyanus pinardii include wound healing, antiseptic, and antifungal activities, which are attributed to its bioactive constituents, such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids.

Further research is needed to fully understand the medicinal properties and potential uses of Cyanus pinardii, but it has shown promise in traditional medicine for treating skin and mucous membrane infections.
