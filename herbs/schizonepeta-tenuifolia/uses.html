Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia) Uses

Chinese Catnip is used to treat respiratory infections, including cold symptoms, bronchitis, and asthma. It helps alleviate coughing and congestion associated with the common cold and tonsillitis. Its properties may also support recovery from tuberculosis and other respiratory tract infections.

Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Chinese Catnip is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat respiratory infections due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing and congestion by supporting immune function and clearing phlegm. Often prepared as a decoction or tea, it is valued for its soothing and detoxifying effects on the respiratory system.

2. Allergic rhinitis

Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia) is used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose by regulating immune responses and improving circulation. Its antihistaminic properties make it a valuable herb for managing seasonal allergies.

3. Cold symptoms

Chinese Catnip is used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate cold symptoms such as headaches, fever, and chills. It is often prepared as a decoction or tea to promote sweating and relieve congestion. Its properties are considered warming and help to dispel wind-cold pathogens in the body.

4. Asthma

Chinese Catnip is used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms by promoting respiratory function and reducing bronchial spasms. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting clearer breathing and easing coughing in asthmatic conditions.

5. Common cold

Chinese Catnip is used to treat the common cold due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like fever, headache, and nasal congestion. It is often prepared as a decoction or tea. Its effectiveness is supported by traditional use and some modern studies.

6. Cough

Chinese Catnip is used to alleviate coughs by reducing inflammation and soothing the respiratory tract. It acts as an expectorant, helping to loosen mucus and ease breathing. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture for this purpose. Its efficacy makes it a valued herb in herbal medicine for respiratory conditions.

7. Bronchitis

Chinese Catnip is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce bronchial inflammation and ease coughing by promoting mucus expulsion. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance respiratory function and relieve symptoms associated with chronic bronchial irritation.

8. Tonsillitis

Schizonepeta tenuifolia is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat tonsillitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling and soothe throat irritation. Typically prepared as a decoction, it is taken orally to alleviate symptoms and support recovery.

9. Tuberculosis

Chinese Catnip has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to support respiratory health, including in the treatment of tuberculosis. It is believed to help clear phlegm, reduce inflammation, and strengthen the immune system, making it a valuable herb in managing symptoms associated with tuberculosis.

10. Respiratory tract infections

Schizonepeta tenuifolia is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat respiratory tract infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. It helps alleviate symptoms such as coughing, sore throat, and nasal congestion. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in clearing phlegm and reducing inflammation in the respiratory system.
