Botanical Identification of Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia)

To identify Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Chinese Catnip, or Schizonepeta tenuifolia, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Schizonepeta, and species Schizonepeta tenuifolia. This classification places it within the mint family, known for its aromatic herbs used in traditional medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chinese Catnip is Schizonepeta tenuifolia, first described by the botanist R. Br. in 1845. It has several synonyms, including Nepeta tenuifolia and Schizonepeta chinensis, reflecting its classification history. Common names include Chinese catnip, windbreak, and fragrant skullcap, while regional names vary across different Chinese provinces. Historically, it was also known as "Xiang Zi Cao" in ancient herbal texts.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Catnip is an annual herb with a branched root system. Its stems are square and hairy, often growing upright. Leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges and hairy surfaces. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, pale blue or purple flowers. The fruit is a dry, four-angled capsule containing small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chinese Catnip can be distinguished by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, square stems, and clusters of small blue to purple flowers. It differs from similar species like Nepeta cataria by its more slender leaves and the presence of a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. The flowers are typically arranged in whorled clusters, not spikes, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Schizonepeta tenuifolia is native to East Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. The plant prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 2000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chinese Catnip typically blooms from June to August, with seed formation occurring from July to September. Harvesting for identification purposes is best done during the flowering stage, usually in late summer, when the plant's characteristics are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Schizonepeta tenuifolia is closely related to species within the same genus, Schizonepeta. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with other members of the Lamiaceae family, including square stems and aromatic leaves. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties to other mint family plants.
