Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia) Compounds

The primary compounds of Chinese Catnip include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and essential oils, known for their aromatic and therapeutic effects. Coumarin contributes to its fragrance and may have antispasmodic qualities. Phenolic acids and terpenoids provide additional antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits.

Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Schizonepeta tenuifolia, commonly known as Chinese catnip. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and circulatory disorders due to their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

2. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia). It contains volatile components like nepetalactone and other terpenoids that contribute to its aromatic properties and therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia). It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and analgesic properties. Coumarin is also known for its ability to enhance the effects of other compounds in the herb. This compound plays a significant role in the therapeutic applications of Chinese Catnip.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating respiratory and circulatory conditions. This compound is often found in high concentrations in the plant's aerial parts.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds in Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic uses in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to support immune function and reduce inflammation. Tannins in this herb may also aid in wound healing and digestive health.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating colds and headaches by exerting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides are often responsible for the therapeutic properties of many medicinal plants, including Schizonepeta tenuifolia.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting circulation and treating respiratory conditions. Saponins also have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting the plant's therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological effects, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Alkaloids in this herb are often responsible for its traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing inflammation. Anthocyanin is also valued for its potential role in improving circulation and promoting skin health.
