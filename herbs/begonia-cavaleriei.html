Begonia cavaleriei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia cavaleriei has asymmetrical, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively textured, somewhat succulent surface. The foliage often displays intricate patterns of variegation or subtle color shifts across the blade. Its stems are typically fleshy and may exhibit reddish or brownish pigmentation depending on light exposure. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by their characteristic begonia structure. The overall growth habit is compact, making it a distinctive specimen in its natural habitat.

      What are the herbal preparations of Begonia cavaleriei?

      Begonia cavaleriei is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using its fresh or dried leaves and stems.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Begonia cavaleriei can cause skin irritation, mouth swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched. The plant contains oxalic acid, which acts as a chemical irritant to mucous membranes and sensitive tissues. Contact with the foliage may result in redness, itching, or a burning sensation on the skin of certain individuals. If a person consumes any part of the plant, they might experience intense stinging in the mouth and throat. In severe cases, swallowing the plant can lead to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.
