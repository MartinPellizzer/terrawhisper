What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia cavaleriei?

Begonia cavaleriei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Cucurbitaleales, it is categorized under the family Begoniaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Begonia and the unique species designation cavaleriei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its stems are typically fleshy and may exhibit reddish or brownish pigmentation depending on light exposure. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by their characteristic begonia structure. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires a substrate of well-draining organic matter, consistent humidity between sixty and eighty percent, and bright indirect light to thrive.

You should provide a potting mix composed of peat moss, perlite, and orchid bark to prevent root rot. Watering should occur only when the top layer of soil feels slightly dry to the touch.
