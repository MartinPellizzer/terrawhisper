Cycas circinalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cycas circinalis?

Cycas circinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Cycadidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Cycadales and the family Cycadaceae, eventually reaching the genus Cycas, of which it is the specific member known as the species circinalis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cycas circinalis contains various bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and terpenoids. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's diverse pharmacological profile in traditional medicine. Specifically, the extracts are often utilized for their antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies also suggest that certain compounds within the plant may exhibit potential anti-tumor activities. Practitioners frequently use different parts of the plant, such as the seeds and leaves, to treat skin diseases and digestive ailments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin disorders, and gastrointestinal ailments. The extracts from the plant are frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and manage wounds. It also holds medicinal importance in traditional practices for addressing digestive issues and internal inflammation. Some studies suggest its chemical constituents may play a role in managing systemic discomfort through its bioactive properties. The various parts of the plant, including the roots and leaves, are utilized depending on the specific ailment being targeted.
