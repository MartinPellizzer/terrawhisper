Gurmar

Gymnema sylvestre

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Gymnema sylvestre, commonly known as Gurmar, is a shrub native to India and parts of Africa. Belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family, it is also called "sweetleaf" in some regions. The plant is perennial, with heart-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers, and has been referenced by various synonyms in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Gymnema sylvestre contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, glycosides, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work together to modulate metabolic processes, particularly in relation to glucose regulation. Their synergistic interactions enhance the herb's efficacy in supporting metabolic health.

  • Flavonoid
  • Glycoside
  • Saponin

System Condidtion Action

Preparation Methods

Powder: Used for managing diabetes by helping to reduce blood sugar levels.

Tincture: Used for supporting blood sugar regulation and enhancing insulin sensitivity.

Capsule: Used for managing diabetes and supporting metabolic health.

Safety Profile

Gurmar is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may interact with medications that lower blood sugar or affect the liver. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you have diabetes or are on medication.
