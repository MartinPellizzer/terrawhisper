Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvia (Salvia officinalis) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Mediterranean Region, Southern Europe, and Middle East. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and resin have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 30 to 100 centimeters.
Leaves
Sessile, opposite, lanceolate leaves with glabrous upper surface and lighter-colored lower surface, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Bilaterally symmetrical flowers with violet-blue corollas, two-lipped structure, upper lip divided into two lobes, lower lip three-lobed, tubular throat with two anthers, and two staminodes arranged in a whorl around the ovary.
Stems
Woody, quadrangular, hairy, opposite branching, aromatic, glandular-pubescent surface.
