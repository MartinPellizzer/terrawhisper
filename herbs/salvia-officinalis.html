Salvia officinalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia officinalis?

Salvia officinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which falls under the genus Salvia, and is specifically identified by its species name, officinalis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia officinalis has a woody, perennial base with quadrangular stems that reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and bear grayish-green, rugose leaves with a velvety texture. The foliage is characterized by oblong or lanceolate shapes and crenate margins, often displaying a dense covering of fine hairs on both surfaces. During the flowering season, the plant produces verticillasters arranged in terminal racemes. These inflorescences feature bilabiate flowers that typically range in color from pale blue to violet or lavender. The floral structure is specifically adapted for pollination, featuring a prominent upper lip and a lower lip that serves as a landing platform.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region, specifically spanning across southern Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. It thrives in various microclimates ranging from rocky hillsides to coastal scrublands within these territories. Due to its long history of medicinal use, it has been naturalized in many other temperate areas around the world. Cultivation is widespread in gardens throughout Europe and North America where the soil provides sufficient drainage. Its distribution is largely influenced by its preference for sunny, warm environments and well-drained terrain.

What are the therapeutic actions of Salvia officinalis?

Salvia officinalis has a diverse range of therapeutic actions including antiseptic, astringent, bitter, stomachic, diaphoric, expectorant, and emmenagogue properties. Its bitter constituents act as a potent stomachic to stimulate digestion and improve appetite. The plant serves as an effective antiseptic and astringent for treating oral inflammations and mucosal tissues. When used to induce perspiration, it functions as a diaphoric to help manage fevers. Additionally, it acts as an expectorant to clear respiratory congestion and as an emmenagogue to stimulate menstrual flow.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds carnosic acid, carnosol, rosmarinic acid, salvianolic acid isomers, and thujone. Carnosic acid and carnosol serve as potent antioxidant components that help protect cells from oxidative damage. Rosmarinic acid provides additional anti-inflammatory properties that support overall systemic health. The salvianolic acid isomers contribute to the plant's complex chemical profile and biological activity. Finally, the presence of thujone adds a unique chemical dimension to its essential oil composition.

As discussed in a study, the plant Salvia officinalis contains the phenolic diterpene known as carnosiic acid (CA). This compound possesses documented medicinal properties including antioxidant, antimicrobial, and neuroprotective effects. Research involving seven days of CA treatment in human amniotic epithelial stem cells (hAESCs) demonstrated its ability to induce mesoderm-lineage-specific differentiation. Furthermore, gene ontology enrichment analyses revealed that CA is associated with metabolic, antioxidant, and antifibrotic activities.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, dizziness, vomiting, dry mouth, hallucinations, seizures, and confusion if consumed in excessive amounts. These adverse reactions typically occur when the active compounds in the leaves affect the central nervous system. Some individuals may experience mild discomfort like a parched throat or lightheadedness. More severe cases can lead to disorientation or intense visual disturbances. In extreme scenarios, the toxicity may trigger physical convulsions or significant mental impairment.
