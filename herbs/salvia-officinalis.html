What to know about Salvia officinalis before using it medicinally

Salvia officinalis, commonly known as common sage, is a versatile herb with a long history of medicinal use.

The main medicinal uses of Salvia officinalis include alleviating symptoms of menopausal hot flashes, improving cognitive function in individuals with Alzheimer's disease, and supporting oral health by reducing plaque formation and gingivitis. The herb is also associated with various health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. Salvia officinalis is rich in bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

People commonly prepare the herb as a tea, often in combination with other herbs, or use it as a supplement to support brain health and alleviate symptoms of neurological disorders.

Featured Study:

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", the essential oils of Salvia officinalis have been found to possess antioxidant, antidiabetic, and anti-inflammatory properties. The full flowering stage of the plant was shown to have the best inhibitory effects against α-amylase, α-glucosidase, and lipase activities, as well as a strong 5-lipoxygenase inhibitory effect. Additionally, the essential oils of Salvia officinalis demonstrated strong antibacterial activity against six bacterial strains, including bactericidal effects on certain pathogens.

What are the possible side effects of Salvia officinalis if used improperly?

The possible side effects of improperly using Salvia officinalis are experiencing gastrointestinal distress, which can range from mild diarrhea to severe abdominal pain, as well as showing signs of allergic reaction, such as hives and swelling, particularly if the plant is ingested in large quantities or over an extended period.

In some cases, experiencing cardiovascular issues, including rapid heartbeat and blood pressure fluctuations, may occur, especially in individuals with pre-existing heart conditions. Additionally, showing liver toxicity, where liver enzymes become elevated, may be a concern, especially in individuals who consume excessive amounts of the plant or have pre-existing liver conditions. Experiencing hormonal imbalance, such as thyroid dysfunction, may also occur, particularly in individuals who consume Salvia officinalis for prolonged periods.

Furthermore, experiencing neurological symptoms, including seizures and respiratory failure, is a severe and potentially life-threatening side effect, as well as kidney damage, and psychotic episodes.

The most common side effects Salvia officinalis gives people when used improperly are listed below, along with a brief explanation.

  • Experiencing Gastrointestinal Distress: Ingestion of Salvia officinalis can cause stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea due to its saponin and thymol content.
  • Showing Signs Of Allergic Reaction: Contact dermatitis, urticaria, and angioedema have been reported in individuals allergic to Salvia officinalis.
  • Experiencing Cardiovascular Issues: Salvia officinalis may cause a significant increase in heart rate and blood pressure, potentially leading to hypertension and cardiac arrhythmias.
  • Showing Liver Toxicity: Ingestion of excessive Salvia officinalis extracts can cause liver injury due to the presence of thujone, a hepatotoxic compound.
  • Experiencing Hormonal Imbalance: Salvia officinalis contains compounds that affect thyroid function and influence thyroid hormone levels.
  • Experiencing Neurological Symptoms: Salvia officinalis consumption has been associated with altered perception, confusion, disorientation, dizziness, euphoria, hallucinations, nausea, and tremors.

  • Experiencing Psychotic Episodes: High doses of Salvia officinalis, particularly among individuals with a history of psychiatric disorders, may trigger or exacerbate psychotic episodes due to its potent impact on the central nervous system and serotonin system disruption.
  • Experiencing Respiratory Failure: Respiratory failure due to severe respiratory depression caused by salvinorin A, a potent opioid compound in Salvia officinalis.
