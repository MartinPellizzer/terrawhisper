Selaginella kraussiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella kraussiana?

Selaginella kraussiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Lycopodiidae, reflecting its evolutionary lineage of vascular plants. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae, placing it firmly within the genus Selaginella. Ultimately, it is identified at the species level as Selaginella kraussiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selagineella kraussiana has creeping, branched rhizomes that support small, scale-like leaves arranged in four distinct rows along the stems.

These leaves are diminutive and triangular in shape, often displaying a dark green to brownish pigmentation. The plant's architecture is characterized by a dense, moss-like growth habit resulting from its intricate branching patterns. Microscopic examination reveals specialized sporangia located in the axils of the sporophylls for reproduction.

This morphological structure allows the species to efficiently colonize moist, shaded substrates in its natural habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Selaginella kraussiana can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or respiratory discomfort if its spores or fine hairs are inhaled or touched. While the plant is primarily used in ornamental settings, sensitive individuals may experience redness or itching upon direct contact with the foliage. There is also a potential for localized swelling if the plant's biological compounds react with sensitive skin types. Because human clinical studies regarding its toxicity are limited, precautions should be taken to avoid ingestion or prolonged exposure. Monitoring for any adverse reactions is recommended when handling this species in a domestic environment.
