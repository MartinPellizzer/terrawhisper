What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella kraussiana?

Selaginella kraussiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Lycopodiidae, reflecting its evolutionary lineage of vascular plants. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae, placing it firmly within the genus Selaginella. Ultimately, it is identified at the species level as Selaginella kraussiana.

The plant's architecture is characterized by a dense, moss-like growth habit resulting from its intricate branching patterns. Microscopic examination reveals specialized sporangia located in the axils of the sporophylls for reproduction.     What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the medicinal compounds actinobolin, bactobolin, eicosanoic acid, fulvic acid, and phenol. These substances work together to provide various therapeutic benefits to the human body. For instance, the presence of eicosanoic acid and fulvic acid can support metabolic processes and nutrient absorption. Phenol contributes antioxidant properties that help protect cells from oxidative stress. Additionally, the specific combination of actinobolin and bactobolin may offer unique antimicrobial characteristics.

    As discussed in a study, Selaginella kraussiana serves as a source of endophytic actinomycetes like Streptomyces parvulus GloL3 and Streptomyces lienomycini SK5. The Ethyl Acetate extract of SK5 demonstrated antimicrobial activity against 9 human pathogens with a minimum microbiacidal concentration of 50-300 µg mL-1. This SK5 extract contains constituents such as bactobolin, actinobolin, 5-(2-aminoethyl)-1 H imidazole-2-carbaldehyde, isovaleric acid, fulvic acid, phenol, 4-[2-(methylamino) ethyl)-, eicosanoic acid, and heptadecanoic acid. Additionally, GloL3 from the plant shows antioxidative potentialities against DPPH, ABTS, FRAP, and H2O2 generators with IC50 values of 21.18 ± 0.33, 43.58 ± 0.91, 88.24 ± 1.24, and 111.03 ± 6.42 µg mL-1. These findings suggest that metabolites from the endophytes of this medicinal plant hold potent pharmaceutical utilities.
