What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella kraussiana?

Selaginella kraussiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Lycopodiidae, reflecting its evolutionary lineage of vascular plants. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae, placing it firmly within the genus Selaginella. Ultimately, it is identified at the species level as Selaginella kraussiana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 5-(2-aminoethyl)-1 H imidazole-2-carbaldehyde, actinobolin, bactobolin, eicosanoic acid, and fulvic acid. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the diverse therapeutic profile associated with the species. Actinobolin and bactobolin represent specialized metabolites that may offer unique biological activities. The presence of eicosanoic acid provides essential fatty acid properties, while fulvic acid serves as a potent organic component for nutrient absorption. Additionally, the imidazole derivative 5-(2-aminoethyl)-1 H imidazole-2-carbaldehyde plays a significant role in the plant's complex chemical makeup.

As discussed in a study, Selaginella kraussiana contains endophytic actinomycetes that serve as potential sources of novel pharmaceutically active metabolites. The Ethyl Acetate extract of the endophyte SK5 from this plant showed antimicrobial activity against 9 human pathogens, including Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus and Candida tropicalis. This extract demonstrated a minimum microbicidal concentration of 50-300 µg mL-1. The constituents of the Ethyl Acetate extracts of SK5 include bactobolin, actinobolin, 5-(2-aminoethyl)-1 H imidazole-2-carbaldehyde, isovaleric acid, fulvic acid, phenol, 4-[2-(methylamino) ethyl]-, eicosanoic acid, and heptadecanoic acid. These findings suggest that the metabolites from the endophytes of this medicinal plant hold potent pharmaceutical utilities.
