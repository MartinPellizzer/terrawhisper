What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella kraussiana?

Selaginella kraussiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Lycopodiidae, reflecting its evolutionary lineage of vascular plants. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae, placing it firmly within the genus Selaginella. Ultimately, it is identified at the species level as Selaginella kraussiana.

According to a study published by "Brazilian journal of microbiology : [publication of the Brazilian Society for Microbiology]", Selaginella kraussiana is an Indian medicinal herb that hosts endophytic actinomycetes such as Streptomyces parvulus GloL3 and Streptomyces lienomycini SK5. The Ethyl Acetate extract of SK5 isolated from this plant showed antimicrobial activity against 9 human pathogens with a minimum microbiacidal concentration of 50-300 µg mL-1. GloL3 from the plant has antioxidative potentialities with IC50 values of 21.18 ± 0.33, 43.58 ± 0.91, 88.24 ± 1.24, and 111.03 ± 6.42 µg mL-1 against DPPH, ABTS, FRAP, and H2O2 free radical generators. The EA extracts of GloL3 and SK5 contain constituents including bactobolin, actinobolin, 5-(2-aminoethyl)-1 H imidazole-2-carbaldehyde, isovaleric acid, fulvic acid, phenol, 4-[2-(methylamino) ethyl]-, eicosanoic acid, and heptadecanoic acid.
