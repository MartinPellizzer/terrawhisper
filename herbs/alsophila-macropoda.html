Alsophila Macropoda

Alsophila macropoda, commonly known as the giant tree fern, has been traditionally used in various medicinal practices for its potential health benefits.

The fronds and roots of this plant are often prepared as decoctions or poultices to treat ailments such as skin infections, inflammation, and digestive disorders. Preliminary studies suggest that the plant contains bioactive compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. In traditional medicine systems, it is also used to support respiratory health and as a remedy for fever.

However, further scientific research is needed to fully validate its medicinal efficacy and to explore its potential applications in modern pharmacology.
