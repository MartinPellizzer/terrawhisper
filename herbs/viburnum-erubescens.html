Viburnum erubescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum erubescens has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure 2 to 5 inches long and possess serrated margins with acute tips. The foliage displays a prominent midrib and distinct venation that becomes more pronounced as the season progresses. During the spring, the plant produces terminal cymes of small, five-petaled white flowers that create a dense, creamy display. These blooms eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a striking bright red color upon maturity. The shrubby architecture of the plant is characterized by multi-stemmed woody branches that can reach heights of six to ten feet.
