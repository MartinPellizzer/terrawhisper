Stipa grandis

What's the taxonomical classification of Stipa grandis?

Stipa grandis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Poales and the family Poaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Stipa, and its specific species designation, grandis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stipa grandis has narrow, linear leaves and a dense panicle of long, awned spikelets that typically measure between 2 and 5 centimeters in length. The plant grows in tufted clumps characterized by a fibrous root system and upright, slender stems. Each spikelet is attached to a long, twisting awn that aids in seed dispersal by catching the wind or clinging to passing animals. The leaf blades are often involute, meaning they roll inward to conserve moisture in arid environments. These structural features allow the grass to thrive in open, sandy, or rocky habitats.
