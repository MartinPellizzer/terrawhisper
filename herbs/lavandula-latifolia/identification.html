Botanical Identification of Lavender Bush (Lavandula latifolia)

To identify Lavender Bush (Lavandula latifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Lavender Bush, Lavandula latifolia, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Lavandula, and species Lavandula latifolia. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the mint family, which is characteristic of many aromatic herbs used in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the Lavender Bush is Lavandula latifolia, a classification established by Linnaeus. This name reflects its botanical characteristics and is widely recognized in herbal medicine. It is also known by several synonyms, common names, and regional variations, which are detailed in subsequent sections.

Morphological Characteristics

Lavandula latifolia is a shrub with a compact, upright growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it well in well-drained soils. The stems are woody and aromatic, often branching from the base. Leaves are linear, grey-green, and aromatic, with a narrow shape and a fuzzy surface. The inflorescence consists of spike-like clusters of flowers. Each flower has violet-lavender petals, and the fruit is a capsule containing small, dark seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Lavandula latifolia can be distinguished by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and pale blue to lavender flowers. It has a more robust growth habit compared to other lavenders, and its leaves are often more leathery and wider. The plant also has a stronger scent and is typically found in wetter soils than other lavender species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Lavandula latifolia is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild, dry summers and cool, wet winters. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and is typically found at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Lavandula latifolia blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, when the spikes are in full bloom and the leaves are mature.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Lavandula latifolia belongs to the genus Lavandula in the family Lamiaceae. It is closely related to species like Lavandula angustifolia and Lavandula stoechas. Its phylogenetic position reflects shared traits with other members of the Lamiaceae family, including square stems and aromatic leaves.
