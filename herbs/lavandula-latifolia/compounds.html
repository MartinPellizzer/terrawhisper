Lavender Bush (Lavandula latifolia) Compounds

The primary compounds of Lavender Bush include essential oils, terpenoids, and flavonoids. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and medicinal effects. Phenolic acids and tannins provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. Coumarin and glycosides may support skin health and wound healing.

Lavender Bush (Lavandula latifolia) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Lavandula latifolia contains key compounds like linalool and linalyl acetate. These compounds are responsible for its calming and antiseptic properties. They are commonly used in aromatherapy and topical applications for stress relief and skin care. The oil is extracted through distillation of the plant's flowering tops.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key group of active compounds in Lavender Bush (Lavandula latifolia). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and calming effects. These compounds are widely used in herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Lavender Bush (Lavandula latifolia) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support skin health. They are commonly found in the plant's essential oils and aerial parts. Flavonoids may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Lavandula latifolia, contributing to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It supports the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and promoting relaxation. This compound enhances the overall therapeutic profile of lavender bush in herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Lavender Bush (Lavandula latifolia). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and helps in reducing inflammation and wound healing. Tannins also support digestive health by binding to proteins and reducing intestinal permeability.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Lavandula latifolia, known for its antispasmodic and sedative properties. It contributes to the plant's calming effects and is used in herbal medicine to treat anxiety and digestive issues. Coumarin also has mild anticoagulant properties, supporting its traditional use in promoting relaxation and easing muscle tension.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Lavender Bush (Lavandula latifolia). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, particularly in calming and soothing effects. Glycosides are often responsible for the plant's aromatic and medicinal qualities. They are typically extracted and used in herbal remedies for their calming and anti-inflammatory benefits.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Lavender Bush (Lavandula latifolia). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin irritations and digestive issues due to its mild detergent and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponin also supports the plant's role in herbal medicine by enhancing its therapeutic potential.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Lavender Bush (Lavandula latifolia). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its role in traditional herbal medicine is less prominent compared to other constituents like essential oils. Alkaloids in Lavender Bush are typically present in smaller quantities and may support the plant's overall therapeutic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Lavender Bush (Lavandula latifolia). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties that support skin health and reduce inflammation. Anthocyanin may also enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.
