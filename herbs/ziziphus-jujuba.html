Ziziphus jujuba

What's the taxonomical classification of Ziziphus jujuba?

Ziziphus jujuba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. Within this order, the plant is classified under the family Rhamnaceae and the genus Ziziphus, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species jujuba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ziziphus jujuba has small, elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a glossy texture, woody stems with sharp thorns, and small, yellowish-green flowers that grow in clusters. The tree typically reaches a height of five to ten meters and develops a spreading, deciduous canopy. Its bark is greyish and develops fine fissures as the plant matures. The fruit is a drupe that transitions from green to a reddish-brown color when ripe. These physical characteristics allow the plant to thrive in various temperate and subtropical environments.

As discussed in a study, Ziziphus jujuba contains enzymes used for the biosynthesis and diversification of the analgesic adoetine X and the anxiolytic sanjoinine A. These processes involve the production of a 14-membered cyclopeptide alkaloid known as lotusine A. The pathway features peptide cyclization on a ribosomal precursor peptide and includes SAM-dependent N-terminal alpha-N,N-dimethylation. Additionally, the plant utilizes non-heme-iron and 2-oxoglutarate-dependent oxidative decarboxylation to afford the C-terminal hydroxystyrylamine.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds betulinic acid, jujuboside A, ursolic acid, spinosin, and scopoletin. These bioactive molecules contribute to a wide range of pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. For instance, the presence of ursolic acid and betulinic acid is often linked to potential anti-tumor properties. Furthermore, the specific combination of spinosin and scopoletin may aid in regulating various biological pathways within the body. The inclusion of jujuboside A further enhances the complex chemical profile utilized in traditional and modern medicine.

As discussed in a study, Ziziphus jujuba contains the medicinal compound betulinic acid. This specific substance is identified as a pentacyclic tritrepeneid naturally present in jujube seeds. The study highlights that betulinic acid shows notable efficacy in combating breast cancer. It functions by targeting the GSK-3β/β-catenin/CXCL1 axis to reduce the expression of TAMs/CXCL1. By modulating this signaling, the compound suppresses the AMPK/mTOR/Beclin 1 autophagy pathway. This mechanism allows betulinic acid to act as a novel chemosensitizer to overcome chemoresistance.
