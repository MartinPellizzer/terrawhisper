Chinese Date (Ziziphus jujuba)

Chinese Date (Ziziphus jujuba) is a member of the Rhamnaceae family, native to China, Central Asia, and Middle East. Traditionally, its fruits, seeds, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its sedative, tonic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, european herbal medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 3 to 8 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with upper surface reddish-brown and lower surface pale green, bearing prominent stomatal bands along midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are racemose clusters with actinomorphic flowers having five white petals and five yellow stamens arranged in two whorls.
Stems
Elongated, woody stems with opposite branching, smooth to slightly hairy surfaces, and prominent nodes with leaf scars.
