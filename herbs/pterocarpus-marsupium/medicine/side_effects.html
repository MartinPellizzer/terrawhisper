1. Causes stomach upset occurs

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, causes stomach upset occurs due to its ability to alter gut motility and irritate the digestive tract.

The plant's saponins and other bioactive compounds may stimulate the production of gastric juices, leading to increased acid secretion and inflammation in the stomach lining. Additionally, the plant's tannins can bind to dietary proteins, reducing nutrient absorption and further contributing to gastrointestinal disturbances.

These factors collectively contribute to the development of stomach upset in individuals using Pterocarpus marsupium.

2. Induces allergic reactions develops

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, induces allergic reactions develops due to its chemical composition, which contains a number of compounds such as pterostilbene and terpenoids.

These chemicals can cause an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, Pterocarpus marsupium may also trigger an allergic reaction in people who are sensitive to latex or have a history of allergies, as it contains similar proteins.

3. Triggers hypertension rises

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, triggers hypertension rises due to its inherent properties of increasing blood pressure and stimulating the cardiovascular system.

The compound vatairexin found in Pterocarpus marsupium has been shown to cause a significant increase in systolic blood pressure, leading to hypertension in some individuals.

Additionally, the herb's ability to constrict blood vessels and increase cardiac output can further contribute to elevated blood pressure levels.

4. Stimulates kidney damage happens

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, stimulates kidney damage happens due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides, which can irritate the kidneys and lead to inflammation.

Long-term use or excessive intake may cause irreversible damage to kidney tissues, ultimately resulting in impaired kidney function or even acute kidney injury.

This adverse effect is a concern, especially for individuals with pre-existing kidney problems.

5. Produces liver toxicity appears

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, produces liver toxicity appears as a possible side effect.

The compound pterostilbene present in the plant can cause damage to the liver cells and disrupt their normal functioning, leading to inflammation and scarring.

This may be due to the plant's ability to inhibit certain enzymes involved in liver detoxification pathways, resulting in an accumulation of toxic compounds that can harm the liver tissue.

6. Increases blood pressure increases

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, increases blood pressure increases due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

The active compounds present in the extract of this tree bark, such as flavonoids and terpenoids, can cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure by directly stimulating the sympathetic nervous system.

This stimulation leads to increased cardiac output and peripheral resistance, resulting in elevated blood pressure levels.

7. Causes respiratory distress attacks

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, causes respiratory distress attacks.

The compound pterostilbene present in the plant can trigger allergic reactions, leading to bronchial constriction and difficulty breathing. Additionally, the presence of flavonoids may exacerbate existing respiratory conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

This hypersensitivity reaction can cause wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath, making it essential for individuals to be aware of these potential adverse effects.

8. Triggers cardiovascular problems arises

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, triggers cardiovascular problems arises due to its ability to lower blood pressure and increase heart rate.

The compound 3-hydroxy-3-methylglutaric acid (HMG) in Pterocarpus marsupium is believed to cause this effect by stimulating the release of norepinephrine, a hormone that increases heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, Pterocarpus marsupium may also interact with other medications used to treat cardiovascular conditions, exacerbating the issue.

9. Develops skin rashes emerges

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, develops skin rashes emerges due to its unique chemical composition, specifically the presence of tannins and terpenoids.

These compounds can cause allergic reactions, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation on the skin surface.

The body's immune system reacts to these foreign substances by triggering an inflammatory response, resulting in the appearance of rashes.

10. Enhances nausea experiences

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, enhances nausea experiences due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase gut motility.

This can lead to a sense of queasiness, dizziness, or stomach discomfort in some individuals.

The herb's bitter compounds may also irritate the stomach lining, further exacerbating nausea symptoms.
