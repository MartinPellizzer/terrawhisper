Pterocarpus (Pterocarpus marsupium) Uses

Pterocarpus marsupium is used to treat diabetes and related conditions like metabolic syndrome, inflammation, and arthritis. It helps manage high cholesterol, hypertension, and kidney disease. Its compounds may improve insulin sensitivity and reduce oxidative stress, supporting overall metabolic health.

Pterocarpus (Pterocarpus marsupium) Monograph

1. Diabetes

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian kino tree, is traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to manage diabetes. It contains compounds like pterocarpin and dihydropterocarpin that help regulate blood sugar levels. Its bark is often prepared as a decoction or powder to support pancreatic function and improve insulin sensitivity.

2. Diabetes mellitus

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, is traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to manage diabetes mellitus. It contains compounds like pterostilbene that may enhance insulin sensitivity and regulate blood sugar levels. Studies suggest it supports glucose metabolism and may help reduce complications associated with diabetes.

3. Metabolic syndrome

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, is used in traditional medicine to support metabolic health. It helps regulate blood sugar and lipid levels, making it beneficial for managing metabolic syndrome. Its active compounds may improve insulin sensitivity and reduce inflammation linked to metabolic disorders.

4. Inflammation

Pterocarpus marsupium is used to reduce inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory compounds like pterostilbene and isoflavonoids. These components inhibit inflammatory pathways, making it effective for conditions like arthritis and skin inflammation. It supports natural healing by modulating the body's inflammatory response.

5. Arthritis

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, is used in traditional medicine to alleviate arthritis symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties help reduce joint pain and swelling. The bark contains compounds that inhibit inflammatory responses, supporting joint health. It is often prepared as a decoction or powder for internal use.

6. Osteoporosis

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian satinwood, is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine to support bone health. It contains compounds that may help increase bone density and reduce the risk of osteoporosis by promoting collagen synthesis and inhibiting bone resorption. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties further contribute to its role in maintaining skeletal integrity.

7. Osteoarthritis

Pterocarpus marsupium has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to support joint health. Its active compounds, such as pterostilbene, exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that may help reduce pain and inflammation associated with osteoarthritis. It is often used in formulations to promote cartilage repair and improve mobility in affected individuals.

8. Kidney disease

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support kidney function. It contains compounds that may help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation, potentially aiding in the management of kidney diseases. Its use is often associated with traditional Ayurvedic practices for renal health.

9. High cholesterol

Pterocarpus marsupium is used in traditional medicine to help manage high cholesterol levels. It contains compounds that may inhibit cholesterol absorption and promote liver function, supporting lipid metabolism. This makes it a valued herb in herbal formulations for cardiovascular health.

10. Hypertension

Pterocarpus marsupium, also known as Indian Kino, is used in traditional medicine to manage hypertension. It contains compounds that may help relax blood vessels and improve circulation, supporting healthy blood pressure levels. Its role in herbal treatments for hypertension is supported by historical use and some modern research.
