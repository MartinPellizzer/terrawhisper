Pterocarpus (Pterocarpus marsupium) Compounds

The primary compounds in Pterocarpus marsupium include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while coumarin exhibits anticoagulant properties. Saponins and glycosides support immune function, and terpenoids may offer antimicrobial benefits. Essential oils, alkaloids, and anthocyanins also play roles in its therapeutic effects.

Pterocarpus (Pterocarpus marsupium) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Pterocarpus marsupium, known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating diabetes and inflammatory conditions. They help improve insulin sensitivity and reduce oxidative stress in the body. Flavonoids are among the primary constituents responsible for the medicinal benefits of this herbal plant.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Pterocarpus marsupium, known for its astringent properties and role in reducing inflammation. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating diabetes and digestive disorders by enhancing insulin sensitivity and protecting the gastrointestinal tract.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Pterocarpus marsupium, commonly known as Indian Kino. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in managing diabetes by enhancing glucose uptake and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is also associated with anti-inflammatory and hepatoprotective effects.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Pterocarpus marsupium, commonly known as Indian Kino. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating diabetes and inflammation by modulating glucose metabolism and reducing oxidative stress. 5. Saponin

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Pterocarpus marsupium. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting urinary health. This compound is known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in managing diabetes and metabolic disorders. 8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Pterocarpus marsupium. It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. 9. Alkaloid

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Pterocarpus marsupium, is a flavonoid responsible for the plant's deep red color. It exhibits strong antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in managing diabetes and inflammation. Its bioavailability and therapeutic potential make it a key component in herbal formulations.
