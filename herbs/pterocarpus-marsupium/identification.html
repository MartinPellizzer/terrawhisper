Botanical Identification of Pterocarpus (Pterocarpus marsupium)

To identify Pterocarpus (Pterocarpus marsupium) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Pterocarpus (Pterocarpus marsupium) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Pterocarpus marsupium belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Pterocarpus, and species Pterocarpus marsupium. This classification places it within the legume family, known for its medicinal and ecological significance.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Pterocarpus marsupium, first described by the botanist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck in 1785. It is also known by several synonyms, including Pterocarpus indicus and Pterocarpus erinaceus, which reflect its historical classification and geographic distribution. Common names such as Indian satinwood and Pterocarpus are widely used in traditional herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Pterocarpus marsupium is a deciduous tree with a straight, cylindrical trunk and a dense crown. Its root system is taproot-based, providing strong anchorage. The stem is smooth and fissured, with a reddish-brown bark. Leaves are odd-pinnate, with 11-21 elliptical leaflets. Inflorescences are racemose, bearing small yellow flowers. The fruit is a legume, containing flat, winged seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Pterocarpus marsupium can be distinguished by its unique leaflets with a prominent midrib, thick and woody stem, and distinctive yellowish-brown heart-shaped seed pods. It also has a strong, sweet scent when the bark is cut, which helps differentiate it from other similar species. The presence of a swollen, cup-like base on the seeds is another key identifying feature.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Pterocarpus marsupium is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly India, Nepal, and Bhutan. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 300 and 1,200 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Pterocarpus marsupium blooms from February to April, producing yellowish flowers. Seed formation occurs from May to July. Harvesting is typically done between August and November, when the bark is mature and contains the highest concentration of active compounds.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Pterocarpus marsupium is closely related to species within the same genus, such as Pterocarpus indicus. It shares key characteristics with other legumes in the Fabaceae family, including compound leaves and pod fruits. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties to other tropical hardwoods.
