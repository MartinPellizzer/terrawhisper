What's the taxonomical classification of Calathodes oxycarpa?

Calathodes oxycarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the order Ranunculales, placing it in the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific classification within the genus Calathodes, with its unique identity established as the species oxycarpa.

The petioles are long and slender, elevating the foliage to capture filtered light in its native habitat.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Calathea oxycarpas can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions if the sap comes into direct contact with the skin or eyes. Some individuals may experience contact dermatitis characterized by redness, itching, or a localized rash. If the plant is ingested, it can lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or vomiting. It is generally considered toxic to pets, potentially causing similar digestive issues or oral irritation in cats and dogs. Always use gloves when pruning and keep the plant out of reach of children and animals to prevent accidental exposure.
