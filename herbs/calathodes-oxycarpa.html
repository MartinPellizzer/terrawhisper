Calathodes oxycarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calathodes oxycarpa has lanceolate leaves that reach up to 40 centimeters in length and are characterized by prominent, dark green venation set against a lighter green background. The leaf margins are typically entire and slightly wavy, contributing to a lush, tropical appearance. These leaves emerge from a creeping rhizome, which allows the plant to spread across the forest floor. The petioles are long and slender, elevating the foliage to capture filtered light in its native habitat. This combination of elongated leaf shapes and rhythmic vein patterns defines its distinctive vegetative structure.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause skin irritation, nausea, vomiting, burning of the mouth or throat, and swelling if ingested or touched. These adverse reactions are typically triggered by the presence of calcium oxalate crystals within the plant's tissues. When these microscopic crystals come into contact with sensitive membranes, they can cause immediate physical discomfort. Swelling and a burning sensation in the mouth or throat are common symptoms following accidental ingestion. Additionally, skin contact may lead to localized redness or irritation for some individuals.
