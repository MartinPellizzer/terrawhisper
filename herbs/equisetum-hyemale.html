Scouring Rush

Equisetum hyemale

Scouring Rush (Equisetum hyemale) is a primitive plant that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, particularly for its potential to improve urinary tract health and support kidney function.

Botanical Identification

Scouring Rush is scientifically known as Equisetum hyemale, belonging to the family Equisetaceae. Common names include scouring rush and miller's rush. It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant has whorled, needle-like leaves and no true flowers. It is also known as Equisetum palustre in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Monilophyta
  • Class: Equisetopsida
  • Order: Equisetales
  • Family: Equisetaceae
  • Genus: Equisetum
  • Species: Equisetum hyemale

Active Compounds

Scouring Rush contains key active compounds like tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and astringent effects, supporting its use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related conditions.

  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as an anti-inflammatory agent.

Safety Profile

Scouring Rush (Equisetum hyemale) is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential toxicity. It may cause gastrointestinal irritation and should be avoided by individuals with ulcers or sensitive digestive systems. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
