Nymphaea candida

What's the taxonomical classification of Nymphaea candida?

Nymphaea candida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Nymphaeales. This aquatic plant is a member of the family Nymphaeaceae, falling under the genus Nymphaea, and is specifically identified by its species name, candida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nymphaea candida has white, star-shaped flowers with numerous petals and stamens, floating circular leaves with deep notches, and thick, submerged rhizomes. The large, leathery leaves emerge from the water's surface to provide buoyancy and light absorption for the plant. These leaves typically feature a radial notch that extends toward the center, allowing them to sit flat on the water. The blossoms are characterized by their brilliant white color and are often fragrant to attract specific pollinators.
