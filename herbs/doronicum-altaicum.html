What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Doronicum altaicum has a basal rosette of oblong to lanceolate leaves that are typically entire or slightly dentate with a hairy surface. At the top of these stems, it displays solitary or clustered flower heads consisting of bright yellow ray florets and yellow disc florets. The involucral bracts beneath the flower heads are green and covered in fine hairs. The root system consists of a sturdy, creeping rhizome that allows the species to spread through its habitat.
