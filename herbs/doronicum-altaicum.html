Doronicum altaicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Doronicum altaicum?

Doronicum altaicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, characterized by its distinctive floral structure, and is specifically categorized under the genus Doronicum. Within this genus, it is identified by the specific epithet altaicum, completing its unique taxonomic designation.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Doronicum altaicum has a basal rosette of oblong to lanceolate leaves that are typically entire or slightly dentate with a hairy surface. The plant produces upright, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to thirty centimeters. At the top of these stems, it displays solitary or clustered flower heads consisting of bright yellow ray florets and yellow disc florets. The involucral bracts beneath the flower heads are green and covered in fine hairs. The root system consists of a sturdy, creeping rhizome that allows the species to spread through its habitat.
