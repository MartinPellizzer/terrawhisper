Pteris insignis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pteris insignis?

Pteris insignis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is a member of the family Pteridaceae and falls under the genus Pteris, ultimately being identified by its specific species name, insignis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris insignis has large, spreading fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length, characterized by pinnate leaves with narrow, lanceolate pinnae that exhibit slightly wavy margins. The stipe is typically dark brown or blackish and possesses a distinctively smooth, polished texture. Each individual leaflet features a prominent midrib and fine, serrated edges that contribute to its delicate appearance. Small, dark sori are arranged in rows along the underside of the pinnules, which is a key identifying feature of the species. These complex structures are supported by a sturdy, creeping rhizome that allows the plant to spread through its substrate.
