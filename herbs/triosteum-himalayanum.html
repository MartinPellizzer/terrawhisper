Triosteum himalayanum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Triosteum himalayanum has erect, woody stems that reach heights of up to 1.5 meters and bear large, simple, alternate leaves with ovate to elliptical shapes and serrated margins. The leaf blades typically measure between 10 and 20 centimeters in length and possess prominent venation. Small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers emerge from the leaf axils throughout the growing season. These blooms eventually develop into fleshy, globose drupes that transition from green to a bright red or orange color upon ripening. The overall structure of the plant is characteristic of a deciduous shrub adapted to montane forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically found in countries such as India, Nepal, Bhutan, and parts of China. It thrives primarily in temperate montane forests and subalpine shrublands. Within these mountainous ecosystems, the species is often situated at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters above sea level. The distribution follows the specific climatic patterns of the Himalayan range, where cool temperatures and high moisture levels are prevalent.
