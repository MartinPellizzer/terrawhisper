Aporosa yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aporosa yunnanensis has woody stems with simple, opposite leaves that feature elliptical to oblong blades and entire margins. The leaves are typically coriaceous in texture and possess short petioles connected to the branches. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary clusters and bloom during the warmer seasons. The fruit is a fleshy drupe that develops into a distinct shape to facilitate seed dispersal. These morphological characteristics help distinguish the species from other members of the Euphorbiaceae family.
