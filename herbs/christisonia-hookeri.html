What's the taxonomical classification of Christisonia hookeri?

Christisonia hookeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Orobanchaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Christisonia, with its unique species designation being hookeri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Christisonia hookeri has a small, herbaceous body characterized by a lack of chlorophyll, which results in a pale, yellowish-white appearance throughout its structure.

The plant lacks true leaves, instead possessing simple, scale-like structures that serve minimal physiological functions. Its stem is slender and upright, supporting small, tubular flowers that typically exhibit a white or pale coloration. The roots are specialized for a parasitic lifestyle, allowing the plant to derive nutrients directly from host plants.

These morphological traits are essential adaptations for its existence as a mycoheterotroph.
