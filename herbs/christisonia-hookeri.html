Christisonia hookeri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Christisonia hookeri has a small, herbaceous body characterized by a lack of chlorophyll, which results in a pale, yellowish-white appearance throughout its structure. The plant lacks true leaves, instead possessing simple, scale-like structures that serve minimal physiological functions. Its stem is slender and upright, supporting small, tubular flowers that typically exhibit a white or pale coloration. The roots are specialized for a parasitic lifestyle, allowing the plant to derive nutrients directly from host plants. These morphological traits are essential adaptations for its existence as a mycoheterotroph.
