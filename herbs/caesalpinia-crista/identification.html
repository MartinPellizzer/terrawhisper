Botanical Identification of Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista)

To identify Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Coral Bean, or Caesalpinia crista, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Caesalpinia, and species Caesalpinia crista. This classification places it within the legume family, highlighting its botanical relationships and potential medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Coral Bean is Caesalpinia crista, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is also known by several synonyms, including Pithecellobium crista and Craibia crista. Common names such as Coral Bean, Crown Bean, and Red Coral are widely used, reflecting its distinctive appearance and regional significance.

Morphological Characteristics

Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) is a shrub or small tree with a branching growth habit. It has a fibrous root system and a woody stem that is often thorned. Its bipinnately compound leaves are alternate and trifoliate. The inflorescence is a raceme of bright yellow flowers with long, narrow petals. The fruit is a flat, woody pod containing several elliptical, glossy seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Coral Bean can be distinguished by its bright red, bean-like pods that split open to reveal orange seeds. It has bipinnate leaves with small, clustered leaflets and yellow-orange flowers. It is often confused with other Caesalpinia species, but its unique pod shape and color are key identifying features.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, including Central and South America. It thrives in warm climates with high humidity and well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It typically grows at low to moderate altitudes, often in coastal or riverine areas.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Coral Bean blooms from May to July, producing bright red to orange cylindrical flowers. Seed formation occurs from September to November. For identification purposes, seeds are best harvested in late fall when they are fully mature and have developed their characteristic spiky texture.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) is closely related to species within the genus Caesalpinia. It shares characteristics with other legumes in the family Fabaceae, particularly in its pod structure and leaf morphology. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties to other Caesalpinia species and related leguminous plants.
