Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) Compounds

The primary compounds in Coral Bean include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while coumarins may support blood circulation. Saponins and glycosides are noted for their potential detoxifying and immune-supporting roles. Terpenoids, essential oils, and alkaloids also play roles in its medicinal properties. Anthocyanins provide additional antioxidant benefits.

Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds may support cardiovascular health and immune function, though further research is needed to confirm their therapeutic potential.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins may support digestive health and skin conditions when applied topically or consumed in moderation.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound helps support the plant's therapeutic applications in various health conditions.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.

5. Saponin

Saponin is an active compound found in Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may also support cardiovascular health by influencing cholesterol levels.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is an active compound found in Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory effects. However, it is important to note that glycoside can be toxic in high doses, requiring careful use under professional guidance.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds are known for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They play a key role in the plant's traditional use for treating digestive and skin conditions. Terpenoids are also being studied for potential therapeutic applications in modern medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) contains bioactive compounds like alkaloids and flavonoids. It is used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is extracted from the seeds and has shown potential in treating skin infections and reducing inflammation.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though its specific therapeutic effects and safety profile require further scientific investigation.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista), is a flavonoid pigment responsible for the plant's vibrant red and purple colors. It exhibits antioxidant properties and may support cardiovascular health, though research on its medicinal applications is limited.
