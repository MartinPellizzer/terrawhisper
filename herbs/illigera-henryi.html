Illigera henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Laurales and the family Hernandiaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Illigera, specifically identifying as the species henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illigera henryi has woody climbing vines characterized by opposite, ovate leaves with entire margins and distinctively long, tapering acuminate tips.

The foliage typically exhibits a glossy texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually develop into fleshy, indehiscent drupes that serve as the plant's fruit.

The stems are flexible and allow the plant to scramble through forest undergrowth using twining mechanisms.
