Poinsettia punicea

Poinsettia punicea, commonly known as the firecracker poinsettia, is a species of flowering plant in the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae).

It is native to Mexico and Central America, and is known for its vibrant red bracts that resemble small fireworks.

The firecracker poinsettia has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, providing health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, which may help to soothe wounds and combat infections.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include triterpenoids and alkaloids, which are thought to contribute to its therapeutic actions, including antiviral and antioxidant properties, although further research is needed to fully understand its effects.

Possible Side Effects of Poinsettia punicea

Poinsettia punicea can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal problems.

It's essential to handle the plant with care and avoid eating any parts of it to prevent potential side effects.
