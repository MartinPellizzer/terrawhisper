Bulbophyllum umbellatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Bulbophyllum umbellatum?

Bulbophyllum umbellatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further classified under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling within the specific genus Bulbophyllum, and is identified by its unique species designation, umbellatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum umbellatum has a creeping rhizome from which grows distinctively clustered, ovoid pseudobulbs that typically bear a single apical leaf. The leaves are relatively small, leathery, and elliptic in shape, providing a structural base for the plant's reproductive organs. From the apex of each pseudobulb, a pendulous inflorescence emerges, carrying multiple small, yellowish-brown flowers that hang in a dense, umbrella-like arrangement. Each individual flower features a specialized labellum that is often mobile to attract specific insect pollinators.
