Bulbophyllum umbellatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum umbellatum has a creeping rhizome from which grows distinctively clustered, ovoid pseudobulbs that typically bear a single apical leaf. The leaves are relatively small, leathery, and elliptic in shape, providing a structural base for the plant's reproductive organs. From the apex of each pseudobulb, a pendulous inflorescence emerges, carrying multiple small, yellowish-brown flowers that hang in a dense, umbrella-like arrangement. Each individual flower features a specialized labellum that is often mobile to attract specific insect pollinators. The overall growth habit is characterized by this compact, clustered morphology which allows the orchid to colonize surfaces effectively.
