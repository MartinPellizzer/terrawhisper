Abutilon sinense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Abutilon sinense has ovate to heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins, woody stems, and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The foliage is typically soft and hairy, providing a textured appearance to the shrub. Its flowers are characterized by prominent, protruding stamens that emerge from the center of the corolla. As the plant matures, it develops a bushy growth habit with multiple branching stems. The fruit consists of small, capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.
