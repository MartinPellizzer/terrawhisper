Acronychia pedunculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Acronychia pedunculata?

Acronychia pedunculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Sapindales. This plant is a representative of the family Rutaceae, specifically situated within the genus Acronychia, and is identified by its unique species designation, pedunculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acronychia pedunculata has simple, alternate leaves that are broadly elliptic to ovate in shape, featuring entire margins and acute to acuminate apices. The leaf blades are typically coriaceous in texture and possess prominent pinnate venation. Its reproductive structures consist of small, greenish flowers that are borne on distinctively long, slender pedicels. The resulting fruit is a fleshy, somewhat indehiscent drupe that changes color as it matures. These morphological characteristics are key identifiers for distinguishing the species within its genus.

What are the pharmacological activities of Acronychia pedunculata?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and anti-nociceptive pharmacological activities driven by its ability to exert cyclooxygenase-2 inhibitory effects and nitric oxide inhibitory activity. These properties suggest that the extracts can effectively mitigate pain signaling and reduce swelling in biological systems. By targeting the cyclooxygenase-2 enzyme, the plant helps decrease the production of pro-inflammatory mediators. Furthermore, its capacity to inhibit nitric oxide production plays a crucial role in regulating the inflammatory response. These combined mechanisms highlight its potential as a natural source for developing therapeutic agents against pain and inflammation.

As discussed in a study, Acronychia pedunculata leaves demonstrate dose-dependent acute anti-inflammatory activity through aqueous (AELA) and 70% ethanol (EELA) crude extracts. The EELA was more active than AELA, with a 200 mg/kg body weight dose serving as the minimum effective dose that achieved a maximum inhibition of 78% of oedema at the 5th hour. Evolitrine was isolated as the major alkaloid and showed anti-inflammatory and analgesic activities at 50 mg/kg body weight that were comparable to indomethacin at 5 mg/kg body weight and acetylsalicylic acid at 100 mg/kg body weight. The EELA also exhibited significant anti-histamine, DPPH scavenging in-vitro antioxidant, in-vivo lipid peroxidation inhibitory, and nitric oxide inhibitory activities. Finally, EELA demonstrated a high safety margin during the limited dose acute toxicity study.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds acronyrone C, acronyrones A, acronyrones B, and acrovesone. These specific chemical constituents are essential to the plant's overall pharmacological profile. Researchers study these molecules to understand how they interact with biological systems. The presence of acrones and acrovesone contributes to the unique therapeutic potential of the species. Detailed analysis of these compounds helps in developing new medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, the leaves of Acronychia pedunculata contain two novel prenylated acetophenones with new carbon skeletons known as acronyrones A and B, represented as 1 and 2, alongside a new analogue called acronyrone C, or 3. These isolated compounds also include two known compounds, 4 and 5. Compounds 1 and 2 are unique because they represent the first examples of prenylated acetophenones possessing a C7 (1) and a C6 (2) side chain. Specifically, these form a 4-isobutylchroman-2-one unit and a 3-(2-methylpropylidene)benzofuran-2(3H)-one moiety with the acetophenone core. Furthermore, compound 4 demonstrated a significant dose-dependent transcriptional activation effect against retinoid X receptor-α (RXRα).
