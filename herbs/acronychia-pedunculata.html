What's the taxonomical classification of Acronychia pedunculata?

Acronychia pedunculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Sapindales. This plant is a representative of the family Rutaceae, specifically situated within the genus Acronychia, and is identified by its unique species designation, pedunculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acronychia pedunculata has simple, alternate leaves that are broadly elliptic to ovate in shape, featuring entire margins and acute to acuminate apices. The leaf blades are typically coriaceous in texture and possess prominent pinnate venation. Its reproductive structures consist of small, greenish flowers that are borne on distinctively long, slender pedicels. The resulting fruit is a fleshy, somewhat indehiscent drupe that changes color as it matures. These morphological characteristics are key identifiers for distinguishing the species within its genus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains five specific medicinal compounds known as acronycula tins A, acronycula tins B, acronycula tins C, acronycula tins D, and acronycula tins E. Researchers study these particular substances to understand their potential therapeutic applications in pharmacology. The presence of such a diverse group of tins suggests complex chemical properties within the plant's tissues. Understanding these compounds is essential for developing future botanical medicines.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", chemical investigation on the stem and root bark of Acronychia pedunculata resulted in the isolation of five new acetophenones. These metabolites are identified as acronycula tins A (1), B (2), C (3), D (4), and E (5). The structures of these 5 metabolites were established using 1D and 2D NMR spectroscopic data, mass spectrometric data, and CD spectroscopy. Additionally, the antioxidant and antityrosinase activities of these 5 metabolites and acrovestone (6) were evaluated. Among these compounds, 6 showed marginal antioxidant and antityrosinase activities.

The 70% ethanol crude extracts of the leaves, known as EELA, showed a dose-dependent acute anti-inflammarotory effect.
