Acronychia pedunculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Acronychia pedunculata?

Acronychia pedunculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Sapindales. This plant is a representative of the family Rutaceae, specifically situated within the genus Acronychia, and is identified by its unique species designation, pedunculata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Acronychia pedunculata contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, terpenoids, and glycosides that exhibit pharmacological potential. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's leaves, bark, and roots. Researchers have identified specific compounds that demonstrate significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties during laboratory testing. Some studies also suggest that extracts from this species may possess anti-inflammatory effects due to its complex chemical profile. These medicinal compounds represent a focal point for investigating the plant's traditional uses in ethnobotany.

What are the therapeutic actions of Acronychia pedunculata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, digestive issues, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are often applied topically to soothe irritations or accelerate the healing of cuts. Some traditional practices involve using extracts from the plant to alleviate stomach ailments and internal discomfort. Additionally, its chemical properties are studied for potential applications in managing systemic inflammation. This medicinal use is common among certain indigenous groups who rely on its natural healing properties.
