Khat (Catha Wallichii)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Catha wallichii.

Khat, scientifically known as Catha wallichii, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula, traditionally used as a medicinal herb and stimulant. It contains unique alkaloids such as cathinone and cathine, which contribute to its stimulating effects and have been studied for their potential therapeutic properties. In traditional medicine, Khat has been used for its ability to enhance alertness, reduce fatigue, and alleviate symptoms of depression and anxiety. Today, it is sometimes incorporated into modern wellness practices as a natural stimulant, though its use remains controversial due to potential dependency risks. With a distinctive bitter, citrus-like flavor and a long history of cultural significance in social and ritual contexts, Khat remains a complex and multifaceted substance with both historical and contemporary relevance.
