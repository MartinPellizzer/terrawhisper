What's the taxonomical classification of Aeschynanthus acuminatus?

Aeschynanthus acuminatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. More specifically, this plant is a member of the family Gesneriaceae and falls under the genus Aeschynanthus, with the specific epithet acuminatus defining its unique species identity.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Aeschynanthus acuminatus targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). By modulating these specific protein signals, the plant extracts effectively reduce swelling and systemic inflammation in biological models. Additionally, certain constituents may influence the activity of matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) to assist in tissue remodeling and protection. This multi-target approach allows the plant to address complex pathological processes involving both immune response and oxidative stress.
