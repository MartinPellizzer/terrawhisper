Aeschynanthus acuminatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aeschynanthus acuminatus has succulent, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along its trailing, herbaceous stems. The foliage is characterized by a deep green color and a smooth, somewhat waxy texture that helps retain moisture. Its inflorescence consists of tubular, drooping flowers that typically exhibit a striking reddish or orange coloration. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and feature a distinctively elongated shape suited for specific pollinators. The overall growth habit is pendulous, allowing the plant to cascade gracefully from hanging containers.
