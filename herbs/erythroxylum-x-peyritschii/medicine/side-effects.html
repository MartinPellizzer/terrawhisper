1. Causes nausea

Erythroxylum × peyritschii causes nausea due to its unique alkaloid composition, which includes bufotenine and other psychoactive compounds.

These chemicals can disrupt the normal functioning of the digestive system, leading to feelings of queasiness and vomiting.

Additionally, yopo's ability to stimulate the release of serotonin and dopamine in the brain may also contribute to nausea symptoms as these neurotransmitters play a crucial role in regulating gut function.

2. Triggers headaches

Erythroxylum × peyritschii triggers headaches due to its psychoactive properties and ability to stimulate the nervous system.

The plant's alkaloids, such as N,N-Dimethyltryptamine (DMT), can cause a surge in serotonin levels, leading to blood vessel dilation and increased blood pressure.

This may result in tension-type headaches or migraines as the brain tries to adjust to the altered chemical environment.

3. Produces dizziness

Erythroxylum × peyritschii produces dizziness due to its ability to release a large amount of adrenaline into the system.

This sudden increase in adrenaline levels can cause a rapid heart rate, increased blood pressure, and feelings of lightheadedness or dizziness.

Additionally, yopo's potent alkaloid composition may disrupt normal brain function, leading to an imbalance in neurotransmitters that regulate balance and coordination.

4. Induces vomiting

Erythroxylum × peyritschii induces vomiting.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's high concentration of alkaloids, specifically beta-carboline and harmine, which can stimulate the vagus nerve, triggering a reflexive response in the stomach, leading to nausea and subsequent vomiting.

The intense stimulation of these compounds can also cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure, further exacerbating the vomiting episode.

5. Relaxes too much

Erythroxylum × peyritschii relaxes too much due to its unique composition of alkaloids and phenolic compounds.

The psychoactive properties of these substances can induce a sense of euphoria and heightened relaxation, leading to feelings of drowsiness and lethargy in some individuals.

This side effect may be attributed to the binding of these compounds to brain receptors, altering neurotransmitter activity and influencing emotional states.

6. Increases anxiety

Erythroxylum × peyritschii increases anxiety due to its ability to release high levels of dopamine and serotonin in the brain.

The sudden surge of these neurotransmitters can overwhelm the nervous system, leading to feelings of apprehension, unease, and excessive worry.

This is a result of the plant's psychoactive properties, which are thought to mimic the effects of certain medications used to treat depression and anxiety disorders.

7. Provokes insomnia

Erythroxylum × peyritschii provokes insomnia due to its active alkaloids, which can significantly alter an individual's sleep-wake cycle.

The psychoactive effects of the plant, such as increased alertness and anxiety, can make it difficult for users to relax and fall asleep at night.

Additionally, yopo's ability to stimulate the nervous system can lead to a state of hyperarousal, making it challenging to wind down and enter a restful sleep.

8. Decreases appetite

Erythroxylum × peyritschii decreases appetite due to its ability to suppress the production of ghrelin, a hormone that regulates hunger.

Additionally, yopo's alkaloids have been shown to slow down gastric emptying, leading to feelings of fullness and reduced interest in food.

This decrease in appetite may be attributed to the stimulatory effect of yopo's chemical compounds on the brain's reward system, which can alter food intake behaviors.

9. Affects digestion

Erythroxylum × peyritschii affects digestion due to its high concentration of alkaloids, particularly D-lysine and D-alanine.

These compounds can disrupt the normal functioning of gut flora, leading to gastrointestinal issues such as bloating, nausea, and stomach cramps.

Additionally, yopo's stimulatory effects on the nervous system can slow down digestion, causing food to remain in the stomach for extended periods, further exacerbating digestive discomfort.

10. Causes stomach pain

Erythroxylum × peyritschii causes stomach pain due to its high concentration of alkaloids, which can irritate the gastrointestinal tract and disrupt digestion.

The plant's active compounds, particularly the indole alkaloids, can stimulate the production of stomach acid and lead to inflammation, resulting in discomfort, cramping, and nausea.

Additionally, the rapid absorption of these compounds into the bloodstream can cause a surge in heart rate and blood pressure, further exacerbating gastrointestinal distress.
