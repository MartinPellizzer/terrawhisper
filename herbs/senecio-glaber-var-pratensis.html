Senecio glaber var. pratensis

Senecio glaber var. pratensis, also known as rough milk thistle or green milk thistle, is a plant species that belongs to the dandelion family and is native to parts of North America.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to stimulate the liver and gallbladder, and to treat conditions such as jaundice and liver disease.

The plant's leaves and flowers contain silymarin, a flavonoid compound that has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its health benefits, including reducing oxidative stress and improving liver function.

Senecio glaber var. pratensis can be prepared as a tea, tincture, or capsule, and is sometimes used in combination with other herbs to enhance its medicinal effects.
