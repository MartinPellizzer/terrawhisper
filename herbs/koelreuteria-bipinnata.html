Koelreuteria bipinnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Koelreuteria bipinnata?

Koelreuteria bipinnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Koelreuteria, and its unique species designation, bipinnaata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Koelreuteria bipinnaata has bipinnately compound leaves featuring numerous small, oblong leaflets arranged along several pairs of lateral axes.

These leaflets are typically bright green, relatively thin, and possess entire margins. The plant also displays woody stems and branches that support a dense canopy of foliage. During its flowering stage, the morphology includes drooping clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white blossoms.

The overall structure of the foliage is designed to maximize surface area for light absorption within its natural habitat.

    What are the herbal preparations of Koelreuteria bipinnata?

    Koelreuteria bipinnata is prepared as a decoction, a topical wash, or a medicinal powder derived from its bark, leaves, and flowers. Practitioners often boil the dried bark in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. The leaves can be crushed into a fine powder to be applied directly to skin irritations or mixed into larger medicinal formulas. Some traditional methods involve steeping the flowers to create a mild tea for soothing purposes. These various forms allow the plant's active compounds to be administered according to specific therapeutic needs.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Koelreuteria bipinnata can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched. While the plant is primarily used for ornamental purposes, some individuals may experience contact dermatitis after handling its foliage or flowers. There is limited clinical data regarding the systemic toxicity of this species in humans. However, consuming parts of the plant could potentially lead to nausea or stomach upset. Caution is advised when working with this shrub to prevent unexpected sensitivity.
