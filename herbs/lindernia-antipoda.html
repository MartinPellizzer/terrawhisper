Lindernia antipoda

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia antipoda has an erect to ascending herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins and small, white, zygomorphic flowers. The leaves possess short petioles and are arranged in pairs along the length of the stem. Small, tubular corollas emerge from the leaf axils, often featuring a distinct two-lipped structure common to the Linderniaceae family.
