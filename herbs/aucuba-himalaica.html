What's the taxonomical classification of Aucuba himalaica?

Aucuba himalaica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Garryales and is a member of the family Garryaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Aucuba, with its unique identity defined by the species name himalica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aucuba himalica has large, leathery, elliptical leaves that typically measure between 15 and 25 centimeters in length and are characterized by an entire margin and an acute apex. The foliage is deep green on the upper surface, often displaying prominent pinnate venation that creates a textured appearance. These leaves are arranged oppositely or sub-oppositely along stiff, woody stems that form a dense, evergreen shrub. Small, inconspicuous yellowish-white flowers emerge from the axils during the spring season. Following flowering, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that change color as they mature.
