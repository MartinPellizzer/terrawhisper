Aucuba himalaica

What's the taxonomical classification of Aucuba himalaica?

Aucuba himalaica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Garryales and is a member of the family Garryaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Aucuba, with its unique identity defined by the species name himalica.

What are the herbal preparations of Aucuba himalaica?

Aucuba himalaica is prepared as a decoction, a topical paste, or a concentrated tincture for various traditional medicinal applications. The leaves and bark are often boiled in water to create a decoction used for internal cleansing or fever reduction. For skin ailments, the plant material is crushed into a fine paste and applied directly to affected areas to soothe inflammation. Tinctures are also crafted by soaking the botanical components in alcohol to extract potent bioactive compounds for long-term storage. These different methods allow practitioners to tailor the dosage and delivery method to the specific ailment being treated.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Aucuba himalaica can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested by humans or animals. The leaves and berries contain compounds that may trigger dermatitis or allergic reactions upon direct contact with the skin. If consumed, the plant can lead to vomiting, abdominal pain, or diarrhea due to its chemical composition. Pets are particularly susceptible to these effects, often experiencing stomach upset after chewing on the foliage. Proper precautions should be taken to keep this plant away from children and domestic animals to prevent accidental poisoning.
