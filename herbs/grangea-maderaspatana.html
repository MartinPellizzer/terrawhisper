Grangea maderaspatanana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. The presence of these compounds often defines the overall pharmacological profile of the species. Understanding the concentrations of 7-α-hydroxyfrullanolide and frullanolide is essential for developing effective herbal extracts.

As discussed in a study, Grangea maderaspatanana contains the medicinal compound 7-alpha-hydroxyfrullanolide (7HF). This compound was assessed for anti-cancer effects in TNBC cells treated with 6, 12, and 24 uM of 7HF. The low-dose 7HF primarily involves G2/M-phase arrest through the upregulation of Bub3, cyclin B1, phosphorylated Cdk1 (Tyr 15), and p53-independent p21. Additionally, the upregulation of PP2A-A subunit expression may modulate the suppression of cell survival proteins such as p-Akt (Ser 473), FoxO3a, and beta-catenin. The concurrent apoptotic effect of 7HF is mediated through the upregulation of Bax and active cleaved caspase-7-9 expression along with the downregulation of Bcl-2 and full-length caspase-7-9 expression.
