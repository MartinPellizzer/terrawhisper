10 Possible Side Effects Of Acanthus Mollis

Acanthus mollis has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and digestive issues.

These side effects can be caused by excessive use, sensitivity, or interacting with other medications. In severe cases, they can lead to difficulty breathing, swelling, and stomach problems, significantly impairing a person's daily activities and overall well-being.

For example, severe skin irritation may cause discomfort and itching, making it challenging to perform even simple tasks.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Acanthus mollis causes allergic reactions due to its high concentration of latex and saponins.

These compounds can stimulate an immune response, triggering symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling in individuals who are sensitive to them.

Additionally, the plant's sap may contain irritating chemicals that can cause skin irritation or respiratory issues in some people, further exacerbating allergic reactions.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Acanthus mollis triggers asthma attacks due to its pollen content, which can cause respiratory issues in individuals prone to allergies.

The plant's dense clusters of small flowers and leaves produce allergenic particles that become airborne when disturbed, making it difficult for people with asthma to avoid exposure.

3. Increases blood pressure

Acanthus mollis increases blood pressure due to its natural ability to stimulate the body's cardiovascular system.

The plant contains compounds that have been shown to increase heart rate and cardiac output, which can lead to elevated blood pressure in some individuals.

This effect may be more pronounced in people with pre-existing hypertension or other cardiovascular conditions.

4. Aggravates skin conditions

Acanthus mollis aggravates skin conditions due to its irritating saponins and allergenic compounds.

These bioactive molecules can cause skin irritation, inflammation, and allergic reactions in some individuals, exacerbating existing conditions such as eczema, acne, or contact dermatitis.

Additionally, the plant's sap may contain toxic alkaloids that can further irritate the skin, leading to redness, itching, and blistering.

5. Interferes with medications

Acanthus mollis interferes with medications by containing compounds that can alter the way certain prescription drugs are metabolized in the body.

The plant's bioactive substances, including flavonoids and saponins, may bind to proteins in the liver and kidneys, leading to decreased efficacy or increased risk of adverse reactions when combined with medications.

This potential interaction highlights the importance of careful consideration when using Acanthus mollis, particularly for individuals taking multiple medications.

6. Worsens respiratory issues

Acanthus mollis worsens respiratory issues due to its potential allergenic properties, causing an overactive immune response that can exacerbate conditions such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

The plant contains compounds like acanthosides, which may trigger allergic reactions in susceptible individuals, leading to increased bronchial constriction, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

This reaction is often linked to the release of histamine and other chemical mediators that can further worsen respiratory symptoms.

7. Disrupts digestive rhythms

Acanthus mollis disrupts digestive rhythms when consumed due to its bitter and astringent properties.

The plant contains compounds such as alkaloids and glycosides that can slow down the movement of food through the digestive system, leading to constipation or diarrhea in some individuals.

Additionally, Acanthus mollis has been known to alter the balance of gut bacteria, further disrupting normal digestion patterns.

8. Induces headaches

Acanthus mollis induces headaches due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds such as alkaloids and glycosides.

These compounds can stimulate the nervous system, leading to increased blood pressure, heart rate, and even migraines in some individuals. The plant's ability to alter neurotransmitter levels, particularly serotonin and dopamine, may also contribute to its potential to cause headaches.

As a result, people who use Acanthus mollis may experience recurring or severe headache episodes.

9. Lowers blood sugar levels

Acanthus mollis lowers blood sugar levels when used.

This is because it contains compounds that stimulate the pancreas to produce more insulin, which can cause a rapid drop in blood glucose levels.

Additionally, Acanthus mollis has been shown to reduce glucose absorption from the gut, further contributing to its hypoglycemic effect.

10. Affects cognitive functions

Acanthus mollis affects cognitive functions in some individuals.

The presence of furanocoumarins and other phytochemicals in this plant may interfere with the normal functioning of neurotransmitters, leading to impaired memory, attention, and decision-making skills.

Additionally, the flavonoids and alkaloids present in Acanthus mollis can also alter brain chemistry, potentially causing confusion, disorientation, and difficulty concentrating.
