What to know about Scrophularia nodosa (figwort) before using it medicinally

Figwort, scientifically known as Scrophularia nodosa, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive array of health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that have been shown to improve cardiovascular health and reduce the risk of chronic diseases.

In the culinary world, figwort is often used to add depth and warmth to soups, stews, and braises, while its flowers are also used to make flavorful teas and infusions. From a horticultural perspective, figwort is a relatively easy plant to grow, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Botanically speaking, figwort belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, nodding flowers and slender leaves.

With ancient references dating back to the Middle Ages, where it was used to treat a range of ailments from fever to skin conditions, figwort has been a staple in herbal medicine for centuries.
