Hedyotis scandens has a climbing or scrambling habit characterized by slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with entire margins. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters along the stem. The plant often utilizes surrounding vegetation for structural support as it ascends toward light sources.       What are the herbal preparations of Hedyotis scandens?

      Hedyotis scandens is prepared as a decoction, a tincture, or a topical poultice depending on the specific ailment being treated. The dried roots and stems are typically boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal consumption. For more stable medicinal use, the plant material is often macerated in high-proof alcohol to produce a potent tincture.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Hedyotis scandens can cause contact dermatitis characterized by red, itchy, and inflamed skin rashes upon physical contact with the plant. This climbing vine contains irritants that trigger an allergic reaction in many individuals. The resulting skin irritation often manifests as small bumps or blisters that may weep fluid. Symptoms typically appear shortly after the skin touches the leaves or stems. Once the irritation begins, the discomfort can persist for several days until the skin heals.
