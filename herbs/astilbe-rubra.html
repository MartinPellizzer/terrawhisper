What's the taxonomical classification of Astilbe rubra?

Astilbe rubra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its lineage through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Saxifragales. This plant is a member of the family Saxifragaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Astilbe, with the species designation rubra.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Astilbe rubra can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or localized rashes in individuals sensitive to its chemical compounds. While it is primarily grown for ornamental purposes, direct contact with the foliage or stems may trigger itching or redness on the skin. Some people may experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions if the plant's sap touches mucous membranes. There is currently limited toxicological research regarding the systemic effects of ingestion in humans. Consequently, cautious handling is advised for those with known plant allergies.
