Wendlandia merrilliana

What's the taxonomical classification of Wendlandia merrilliana?

Wendlandia merrilliana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Gentianales and falls under the family Rubiaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Wendlandiia and the species merrilliana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wendlandiia merrilliana has woody vines characterized by opposite, simple leaves with entire margins and an elliptical to lanceolate shape.

The foliage typically displays a smooth texture and a prominent midrib on the underside. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually produce woody, dehiscent capsules. These capsules contain numerous small seeds that are dispersed through various environmental mechanisms.

The overall growth habit allows the plant to climb through forest canopies using its flexible stems.
