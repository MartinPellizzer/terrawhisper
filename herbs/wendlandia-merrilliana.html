Wendlandia merrilliana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wendlandiia merrilliana has woody vines characterized by opposite, simple leaves with entire margins and an elliptical to lanceolate shape. The foliage typically displays a smooth texture and a prominent midrib on the underside. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually produce woody, dehiscent capsules. These capsules contain numerous small seeds that are dispersed through various environmental mechanisms. The overall growth habit allows the plant to climb through forest canopies using its flexible stems.
