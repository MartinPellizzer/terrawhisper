Persea domingensis

Persea domingensis, also known as the West Indian Mahogany or Dominican Avocado, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola, which includes Haiti and the Dominican Republic.

The plant's bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which can help to treat various health issues such as fever, cough, and skin conditions.

The health benefits of Persea domingensis include its potential to lower blood pressure, improve digestion, and boost the immune system, making it a valuable herbal remedy.

Herbal preparations of Persea domingensis are typically made from the bark and leaves, and can be consumed as teas, tinctures, or infusions, or used topically as a poultice or salve.
