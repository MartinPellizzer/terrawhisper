Henna (Lawsonia inermis) Uses

Henna has been traditionally used to treat scabies, skin infections, and lice infestations due to its antifungal and antibacterial properties. It is also applied for tinea, athlete's foot, psoriasis, and eczema to soothe inflammation and promote healing. Henna helps with wound healing and is used to reduce dandruff and treat alopecia by strengthening hair follicles.

Henna (Lawsonia inermis) Monograph

1. Scabies

Henna has been traditionally used to treat scabies due to its antipruritic and antimicrobial properties. The paste made from henna leaves and other natural ingredients can help alleviate skin irritation and reduce mite infestation. It is often applied topically to affected areas as part of traditional herbal remedies for scabies.

2. Skin infections

Henna has antimicrobial properties that help treat skin infections. It can be applied topically as a paste to reduce inflammation and prevent bacterial growth. Its natural astringent qualities aid in healing wounds and soothing irritated skin. It is commonly used for fungal infections like ringworm and bacterial infections such as impetigo.

3. Lice infestation

Henna is traditionally used to treat lice infestation due to its antifungal and insecticidal properties. A paste made from henna powder and coconut oil is applied to the scalp, suffocating the lice and preventing their reproduction. This remedy is effective for both head lice and pubic lice when applied consistently. It is a natural and safe alternative to chemical treatments.

4. Tinea

Henna is traditionally used to treat tinea, a fungal infection affecting the skin. Its antifungal properties help reduce symptoms like itching and scaling. Applying henna paste topically can inhibit fungal growth and promote healing. It is often used as a natural alternative to commercial antifungal treatments.

5. Athlete's foot

Henna has been traditionally used to treat athlete's foot due to its antifungal properties. The active compound lawsone inhibits fungal growth, reducing symptoms like itching and scaling. It can be applied as a paste or oil to the affected area for effective relief. Regular use helps prevent recurrence and promotes skin healing.

6. Psoriasis

Henna has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties. Topical application of henna paste may help reduce scaling and itching associated with psoriasis. However, it is important to consult a healthcare professional before using henna as a treatment for psoriasis.

7. Eczema

Henna has been traditionally used to treat eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The paste, made from dried henna leaves, helps soothe irritated skin and reduce redness. It also acts as a natural barrier, preventing further irritation and promoting skin healing. Regular application may help manage symptoms and improve skin texture in eczema cases.

8. Wound healing

Henna has been traditionally used to promote wound healing due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps prevent infection, reduce inflammation, and accelerate tissue repair. Applied as a paste or poultice, henna can be used on minor cuts, burns, and skin irritations to support natural healing processes.

9. Dandruff

Henna is used to treat dandruff due to its antimicrobial and antifungal properties. It helps reduce scalp inflammation and soothe irritation. A paste made from henna and coconut oil can be applied to the scalp to control dandruff and promote a healthy scalp environment. Regular use may improve scalp health and reduce flaking.

10. Alopecia

Henna has been traditionally used to treat alopecia, or hair loss, by stimulating hair follicles and improving scalp health. Its antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce scalp infections and irritation that contribute to hair thinning. Some studies suggest that henna can promote hair growth when applied regularly as a scalp treatment.
