What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea phaeantha?

Saussurea phaeantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asterales, this plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Saussurea and the species phaeantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea phaeantha has perennial herbaceous stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters and bear leaves with broadly lanceolate or ovate blades and deeply dentate margins. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, many-flowered capitulum located at the terminus of the stem. The individual florets exhibit a distinct brownish or dusky coloration, which is the source of its specific epithet.
