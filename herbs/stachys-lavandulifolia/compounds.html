Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia) Compounds

The primary compounds of Lavender Mint include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Terpenoids and essential oils contribute to its aromatic profile and potential therapeutic benefits. Tannins and glycosides provide astringent and digestive support, while coumarin adds antimicrobial properties.

Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in the leaves and are often used in herbal remedies for their calming and healing effects.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin health. This compound helps in reducing oxidative stress and promoting overall well-being.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to support digestive health by reducing inflammation and promoting tissue healing. Tannins are also valued for their antioxidant effects in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has mild anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is often used in herbal medicine for its calming effects and potential support in respiratory and digestive health.

5. Essential oil

The essential oil of Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia) contains bioactive compounds like linalool and limonene. These contribute to its calming and anti-inflammatory properties. It is commonly used in aromatherapy and topical applications for stress relief and skin care.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key group of active compounds in Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia). They contribute to its aromatic profile and have demonstrated antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key compound in Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Glycosides are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic actions in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin helps in supporting digestive health and may aid in reducing skin irritations. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Lavender Mint in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Alkaloids in this herb may support its use in traditional herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive health.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key compound in Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia), contributing to its vibrant color and potential health benefits. This flavonoid is known for its antioxidant properties, which may support skin health and reduce inflammation. It plays a role in the plant's therapeutic uses in traditional herbal medicine.
