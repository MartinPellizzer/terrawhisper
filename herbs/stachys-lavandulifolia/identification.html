Botanical Identification of Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia)

To identify Lavender Mint (Stachys lavandulifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Lavender Mint, or Stachys lavandulifolia, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Stachys, and species Stachys lavandulifolia. It is a member of the mint family known for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Lavender Mint is Stachys lavandulifolia, first described by Carl Peter Thunberg in 1810. It is also known by several synonyms, including Stachys lavandulifolia subsp. lavandulifolia and Stachys tenuifolia. Common names such as Lavender Mint, Mint Horsetail, and Narrowleaf Hedgenut are widely recognized. Regional and historical names vary, reflecting its use in traditional herbal medicine across different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Lavender Mint is an upright herb with a fibrous root system. Its stems are square and branched, often hairy. Leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence is a dense spike of purple flowers, each with two lips. The fruit is a small, dry capsule containing tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Stachys lavandulifolia can be distinguished by its square, hairy stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of purple-lilac flowers. It differs from similar species like Stachys palustris by its more fragrant leaves and the absence of a basal leaf rosette. The leaves also emit a minty scent when crushed, aiding in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Lavender Mint is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is typically found at elevations between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Lavender Mint typically blooms from late spring to early summer, around May to July. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification, the best time to harvest is during the flowering stage, usually in late spring to early summer, when the leaves are most aromatic and the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Stachys lavandulifolia is closely related to other members of the mint family, Lamiaceae. It shares characteristics with species like Stachys palustris and Stachys sylvatica. Its genus, Stachys, is part of the tribe Lamioideae, which includes many aromatic herbs. These relationships help in understanding its botanical and medicinal properties.
