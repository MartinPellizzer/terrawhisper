What is Syzygium porphyrocarpum?

Syzygium porphyrocarpum is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is an evergreen tree with a broad, rounded crown and a smooth, grey bark that becomes longitudinally fissured with age. The leaves are elliptic to lanceolate, 3-7 cm long, 1-2.5 cm wide, with a pointed tip and a rounded base, and are arranged oppositely on the branchlets. The flowers are white, 5-6 mm long, and are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of branchlets.

The fruit is a berry, 1.5-2 cm in diameter, with a red or purple color when ripe, and is edible, containing 3-4 seeds.
