What is Wedelia leucoglossa?

Wedelia leucoglossa is an annual herbaceous plant endemic to Mexico, belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It has a single stem, 10-60 cm in height, with few branches, covered by a layer of fine hairs. Leaves are opposite, lanceolate, 2-7 cm in length, with ciliate margins, and are arranged in a spiral along the stem. Flowers are borne in small corymbose inflorescences, with white ray florets and yellow disc florets, 1-2 cm in diameter.

Wedelia leucoglossa reproduces via achenes, small, dry fruits, which are dispersed by wind.
