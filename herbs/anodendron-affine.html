Anodendron affine

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anodendroon affine has climbing stems with large, heart-shaped leaves that possess long, tapering petioles and prominent venation. The leaf blades are typically dark green and can reach significant sizes as they climb toward the canopy. The plant produces inflorescences consisting of a spadix partially enclosed by a leaf-like spathe. These floral structures are characteristic of the Araceae family and are often found tucked within the leaf axils. The overall growth habit is that of a vigorous liana that utilizes its climbing stems to navigate forest environments.
