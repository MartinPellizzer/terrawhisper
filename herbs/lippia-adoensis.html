Lippia adoensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Lippia adoensis?

Lippia adoensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and falls under the family Verbenaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Lippia, resulting in its scientific name, species adoensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lippia adoensis contains high concentrations of bioactive secondary metabolites including terpenoids, flavonoids, tannins, and various phenolic compounds. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Research suggests that the essential oils extracted from its leaves possess significant inhibitory effects against several pathogenic microorganisms. The presence of specific alkaloids also plays a role in its traditional use for treating inflammatory conditions. Such diverse chemical profiles make the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lippia adoensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal disorders such as diarrhea, stomach aches, and intestinal worms. It also serves as a remedy for respiratory issues including coughs and bronchitis. Local communities utilize its medicinal properties to manage skin infections and minor wounds. Furthermore, it is often applied to alleviate symptoms of fever and general body aches. The leaves are typically prepared as infusions or decoctions to maximize these therapeutic effects.
