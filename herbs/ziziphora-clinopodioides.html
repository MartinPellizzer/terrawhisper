What is Summer Savory (Ziziphora Clinopodioides)?

Summer Savory (Ziziphora clinopodioides) is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small blue or purple flowers, and is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Summer Savory, or Ziziphora clinopodioides, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family, native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

Common Names and Synonyms

Plant Description and Morphology

Summer Savory (Ziziphora clinopodioides) is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branched stems that are usually hairless, and it features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins and a fragrant, aromatic scent.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Summer Savory (Ziziphora clinopodioides) is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe, northern Africa, and western Asia, and thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves and flowers of Summer Savory are primarily used medicinally, with the leaves being most commonly harvested for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.

Phytochemical Composition

Summer Savory (Ziziphora clinopodioides) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including essential oils rich in thymol, carvacrol, and terpenoids, along with flavonoids, phenolic acids, and volatile compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacological Properties
