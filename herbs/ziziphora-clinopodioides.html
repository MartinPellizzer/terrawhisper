Summer savory

Ziziphora clinopodioides

Summer Savory (Ziziphora clinopodioides) is a small, aromatic herb native to the Middle East and Central Asia, known for its use in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Botanical Identification

Ziziphora clinopodioides is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is also known as summer savory or caraway weed. The plant has aromatic leaves and small pink or white flowers, and its scientific name is sometimes cited as Origanum clinopodiodes in older herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Ziziphora
  • Species: Ziziphora clinopodioides

Active Compounds

Summer Savory contains essential oil, flavonoids, and terpenoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to exert their therapeutic effects, with the essential oil contributing to its aromatic and antimicrobial properties. Flavonoids and terpenoids support anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities, enhancing the herb's overall medicinal profile.

Summer savory Uses.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to relieve stomach discomfort.

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and to reduce inflammation.

Safety Profile

Summer Savory is generally safe when used in culinary amounts. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Long-term use or high doses may cause gastrointestinal upset, so it should be used with caution.
