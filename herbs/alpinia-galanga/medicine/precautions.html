10 Precautions To Take When Using Alpinia Galanga (Galangal)

Alpinia galanga has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as pregnant and breastfeeding women taking care to avoid consuming large quantities due to its emmenagogic properties.

Taking excessive amounts can stimulate menstruation and cause uterine contractions, potentially leading to miscarriage or preterm labor.

It's essential to follow these guidelines to avoid adverse effects, especially in vulnerable populations, where the risks of using this herb are higher.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Alpinia galanga medicinally.

When using Alpinia galanga medicinally, it's important to avoid high doses suddenly because sudden and drastic changes in dosage can lead to adverse effects on the digestive system.

Galangal is a potent herb that can stimulate the gut and cause gastrointestinal upset if not introduced gradually into the body.

Abrupt changes in dosage can also disrupt the balance of beneficial gut bacteria, leading to a range of unpleasant symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.
