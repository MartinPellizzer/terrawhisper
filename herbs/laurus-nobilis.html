Botanical Identification

Laurus nobilis, commonly known as laurel, is a species in the family Lauraceae. It is a perennial evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. Its dark green, glossy leaves are aromatic, and it produces small yellow flowers followed by oval berries. Synonyms include bay laurel and sweet bay, often used interchangeably in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Laurus nobilis contains essential oils, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. The essential oils contribute to its aromatic properties and therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Decoction: Used for gastrointestinal ailments and cold symptoms.

Essential oil: Used for respiratory infections and muscle pain.

Safety Profile

Laurus nobilis is generally safe when used in culinary amounts. However, excessive use may cause gastrointestinal discomfort. It is contraindicated in individuals with known allergies to the plant or its derivatives. Prolonged use may lead to liver toxicity, so it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.
