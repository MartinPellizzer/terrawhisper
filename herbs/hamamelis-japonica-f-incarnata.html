Hamamelis japonica f. incarnata

Hamamelis japonica f. incarnata, commonly known as Japanese Witch Hazel, is a variety of the Hamamelis japonica plant, a flowering plant native to East Asia.

This plant is valued for its medicinal and therapeutic properties, which include reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms of skin irritations, such as eczema and acne.

The health benefits of Japanese Witch Hazel are attributed to its therapeutic actions, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and astringent properties.

The bioactive constituents responsible for these effects include tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have been shown to exert antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic activities.
