Strophanthus gratus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the pharmacological activities of Strophanthus gratus?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including cardiotonic, emetic, purgative, and anthelmintic properties derived primarily from its cardiac glycoside content. The presence of compounds likeOuabain and Strophanthin allows it to exert significant effects on the strength and rate of heart contractions. These glycosides act by inhibiting the sodium-potassium pump in cardiac muscle cells, which increases intracellular calcium levels. Beyond its cardiovascular utility, the plant is used in traditional medicine to induce vomiting or treat intestinal parasites. However, its narrow therapeutic index necessitates extreme caution due to the high risk of digitalis-like toxicity.
