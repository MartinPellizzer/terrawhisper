Strophanthus gratus

What's the taxonomical classification of Strophanthus gratus?

Strophanthus gratus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical path through the order Gentianales, it is placed within the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Strophanthus, with its specific identification being the species gratus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strophantus gratus has lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green, leathery in texture, and possess entire margins.

The plant features a woody stem structure characteristic of small shrubs or subshrubs within the Euphorbiaceae family. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often arranged in axillary clusters. The fruit is a specialized capsule that typically dehisces to release seeds.

These morphological traits collectively define its growth habit and reproductive strategy within its natural habitat.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Strophanthus gratus contains cardiac glycosides primarily located in its seeds, which are the most significant medicinal component of the plant. These seeds are processed to extract compounds likeOuabain that act as potent vasodilators and cardiotonics. While the seeds are the main source of pharmaceutical interest, other parts of the plant like the roots and leaves also contain various alkaloids and glycosides. Due to the extreme toxicity of these components, they must be carefully measured to treat conditions like congestive heart failure. Practitioners must exercise great caution because even minor errors in dosage can lead to fatal cardiac effects.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains potent cardiac glycosides such as ouabain and strophanthiidin, along with various alkaloids that influence physiological processes.

These specific compounds primarily target the sodium-potassium pump within cardiac muscle cells to alter heart contractions. The presence of ouabain makes the plant highly toxic if ingested in unregulated amounts. Additionally, the alkaloids contribute to the complex chemical profile used in historical pharmacological studies.

Careful extraction of these substances is necessary due to their narrow therapeutic index and high toxicity.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Strophanthus gratus targets cardiac glycosides such as strophanthin andOuabain which primarily bind to and inhibit the Na+/K+-ATPase enzyme in cardiac myocytes. This inhibition leads to an increase in intracellular sodium concentrations, which subsequently reduces the activity of the sodium-calcium exchanger. By slowing this exchanger, the plant increases the concentration of intracellular calcium available for contraction. This specific interaction enhances myocardial contractility through a positive inotropic effect. These molecular pathways are the primary drivers of the plant's potent physiological effects on the cardiovascular system.

What are the therapeutic actions of Strophanthus gratus?

Strophanthus gratus has potent therapeutic actions characterized by its role as a powerful cardiac stimulant and cardiotonic agent used to increase the force of myocardial contractions.

The glycosides found within the plant act directly on the heart muscle to improve circulation in cases of congestive heart failure. If ingested in large amounts, the plant also functions as a dangerous emetic that induces vomiting. Its complex chemical composition can exert anticholinergic effects by interfering with neurotransmitter signaling in the body.

These combined properties necessitate extreme caution during medicinal application due to their significant physiological impact.

The primary therapeutic actions of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Cardiac stimulant
  • Cardiotonic
  • Emetic
  • Anticholinergic

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat congestive heart failure, cardiac arrhythmias, and various forms of edema caused by heart dysfunction.

It contains cardiac glycosides that work by increasing the force of myocardial contractions while regulating heart rate. These chemical compounds help manage symptoms like shortness of breath and swelling in the limbs. Because the therapeutic window is extremely narrow, medical administration requires precise dosing to avoid toxicity.

Historically, it has also been applied in limited ways to manage certain parasitic infections.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Heart palpitations
  • Irregular heartbeat (arrhythmia)
  • Congestive heart failure symptoms
  • Slow pulse (bradycardia)
  • Glaucoma

What are the herbal preparations of Strophanthus gratus?

Strophanthus gratus is prepared primarily as a concentrated liquid extract, an aqueous infusion, or a powdered form derived from the dried seeds. The seeds contain potent cardiac glycosides that must be carefully processed to ensure consistent potency and safety. In many traditional applications, the seeds are crushed and decocted in water to create a potent medicinal solution. Some preparations involve alcohol-based tinctures which help stabilize the active chemical compounds for longer storage. Due to the high toxicity of these glycosides, these preparations are strictly regulated and used in precise, minute dosages.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Strophantus gratus can cause severe vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, and potentially fatal cardiac arrhythmias due to its high concentration of toxic cardiac glycosides. These potent compounds act directly on the heart muscle, which can lead to dangerous irregularities in heart rhythm. Ingestion of the plant or its seeds may also trigger intense gastrointestinal distress and systemic toxicity. If the dose is high enough, the physiological impact can result in sudden cardiac arrest or death. Therefore, extreme caution is necessary as even small amounts can be highly dangerous to human health.
