Strophanthus gratus

What's the taxonomical classification of Strophanthus gratus?

Strophanthus gratus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical path through the order Gentianales, it is placed within the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Strophanthus, with its specific identification being the species gratus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Strophanthus gratus contains high concentrations of cardiac glycosides, most notably strophanthin and ouabain, which act directly on the heart muscle. These specific compounds work by inhibiting the sodium-potassium pump in cardiac cells to increase the force of myocardial contractions. Because of this potent mechanism, the plant has historically been used in controlled doses to treat congestive heart failure and certain arrhythmias. However, the therapeutic window is extremely narrow, meaning even slight variations in dosage can lead to fatal toxicity. Due to its extreme potency, the plant is primarily utilized in specialized pharmacological settings rather than general medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Strophanthus gratus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat congestive heart failure, cardiac arrhythmias, and various forms of edema caused by heart dysfunction. It contains cardiac glycosides that help increase the force of heart muscle contractions while regulating the heart rate. These medicinal properties allow the plant to manage conditions where the heart cannot pump blood efficiently through the circulatory system. However, the dosage must be extremely precise because the active compounds can easily become toxic to the human body. Medical practitioners historically used its derivatives to address symptoms like palpitations and fluid retention.
