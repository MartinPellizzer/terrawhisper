Mahonia (Mahonia nervosa)

Mahonia (Mahonia nervosa) is a member of the Berberidaceae family, native to North America, United States, and Canada. Traditionally, its leaves, roots, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with dark green upper surface and lighter green lower surface, bearing prominent midrib and parallel venation.
Flowers
Flowers are yellow with five sepals and five petals, arranged in a raceme, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry, and bearing distinct glandular markings on the petals.
Stems
Erect growth habit with opposite branching, woody surface, and presence of persistent stipules.
