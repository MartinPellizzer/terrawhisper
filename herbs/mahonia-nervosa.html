Mahonia

Mahonia nervosa

Mahonia (mahonia nervosa) is a member of the Berberidaceae family, native to regions of North America. Traditionally, its leaves and roots have been used for digestive issues, as a topical antiseptic, and for fever reduction. The plant is valued in various cultural practices for its medicinal properties.

Botanical Identification

Mahonia nervosa, commonly known as the nervy mahonia, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Berberidaceae family. Native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, it features evergreen leaves with prominent veins and yellow flowers. It is also known as Oregon grape or California barberry in regional herbal contexts.

Mahonia Identification.

Active Compounds

Mahonia nervosa contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, supporting its use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and as an antiseptic.

Powder: Used for topical application to treat skin conditions.

Safety Profile

Mahonia nervosa is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses should be avoided, and it is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data.
