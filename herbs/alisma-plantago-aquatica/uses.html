Water Plantain (Alisma plantago-aquatica) Uses

Water Plantain is used to treat edema, urinary tract infections, kidney stones, and respiratory infections. It helps reduce mucus buildup and is effective for coughs and tonsillitis by supporting urinary and respiratory system health.

Water Plantain (Alisma plantago-aquatica) Monograph

1. Edema

Water Plantain is used to treat edema due to its diuretic properties. It helps reduce fluid retention by increasing urine production. Traditionally, it is applied externally as a poultice or taken internally as a tea. It is particularly effective for edema caused by dampness or kidney imbalances.

2. Urinary tract infection

Water Plantain has been traditionally used to support urinary tract health. It contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and promote urine flow. Its diuretic properties can aid in flushing out infections. It is often used in herbal formulations for urinary tract infections.

3. Kidney stones

Water Plantain is used in traditional medicine to support kidney health and aid in the elimination of kidney stones. Its diuretic properties help increase urine flow, which can prevent the formation of stones and promote their passage. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in urinary tract support.

4. Urinary tract infections

Water Plantain has been traditionally used to support urinary tract health. It contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and promote urine flow. Its diuretic properties can aid in flushing out infections. It is often used in herbal formulas for urinary tract infections.

5. Respiratory infection

Water Plantain is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health, particularly in treating symptoms of respiratory infections. It helps reduce inflammation and mucus production in the airways, making it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis and coughs. Its diuretic properties also aid in clearing toxins from the body, supporting overall recovery.

6. Respiratory infections

Water Plantain is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and mucus production. It is often employed for conditions like bronchitis and coughs due to its expectorant properties. Its diuretic effects also help clear congestion and support lung function.

7. Cough

Water Plantain is used to soothe coughs by clearing phlegm and reducing inflammation in the respiratory tract. It is particularly effective for wet coughs with thick mucus. The herb supports lung function and helps ease breathing in cases of bronchitis or colds. Its diuretic properties also aid in reducing fluid retention that may worsen respiratory symptoms.

8. Tonsillitis

Water Plantain has been traditionally used to treat tonsillitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling and soothe throat irritation. Infusions or decoctions of the plant are commonly prepared to alleviate symptoms associated with this condition. Its ability to support immune function also aids in recovery.

9. Respiratory tract infections

Water Plantain has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of coughs and inflammation of the respiratory tract. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, making it easier to expel, while its anti-inflammatory effects may reduce irritation and swelling in the airways.

10. Mucus buildup

Water Plantain is used to address mucus buildup due to its demulcent properties, which soothe irritated mucous membranes. It helps reduce excess phlegm and congestion, making it beneficial for respiratory and digestive tract issues. Its mild nature supports clearing mucus without causing irritation.
