10 Possible Side Effects Of Alisma Plantago-Aquatica

Alisma plantago-aquatica has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, nausea, and stomach cramps.

These side effects can occur due to excessive consumption or poor preparation of the herb. In severe cases, they can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, and gastrointestinal bleeding.

For individuals with weakened immune systems, such as those with HIV/AIDS or undergoing chemotherapy, these side effects can be particularly debilitating, affecting their quality of life and daily activities.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Alisma plantago-aquatica causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The allergenic compounds present in its extract, such as flavonoids and terpenoids, are thought to trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling. Additionally, the plant's high concentration of saponins may also contribute to the development of allergies.

These reactions can occur due to the body's overactive response to these compounds, resulting in adverse effects on skin and mucous membranes.

2. Induces kidney damage

Alisma plantago-aquatica induces kidney damage due to its potential to alter urine composition and decrease nephron function.

The plant's bioactive compounds may interfere with the normal functioning of the kidneys, leading to reduced filtration and increased risk of nephrotoxicity.

Additionally, Alisma plantago-aquatica contains alkaloids that can cause vasoconstriction, further compromising kidney blood flow and increasing the likelihood of damage.

3. Triggers blood pressure rise

Alisma plantago-aquatica triggers blood pressure rise due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system.

The herb contains alkaloids and glycosides that can increase heart rate and cardiac output, leading to a rise in blood pressure.

Additionally, Alisma's diuretic properties can cause dehydration, which further contributes to increased blood pressure as the body attempts to maintain its fluid balance.

4. Stimulates respiratory failure

Alisma plantago-aquatica stimulates respiratory failure by potentially exacerbating pre-existing respiratory conditions such as chronic bronchitis or asthma.

Its active compounds, including flavonoids and alkaloids, may cause constriction of airways, leading to increased resistance in breathing and reduced oxygen intake.

This side effect is thought to occur due to the herb's ability to interact with the body's natural inflammatory response, potentially worsening symptoms of respiratory distress.

5. Inhibits liver function

Alisma plantago-aquatica inhibits liver function due to its potential interaction with enzymes responsible for metabolizing substances in the body.

The compound alisol B 23-acetate, found in the plant, may bind to liver enzymes and alter their activity, leading to reduced clearance of toxic compounds and increased risk of adverse effects.

This interference can result in impaired liver function, potentially causing symptoms such as fatigue, nausea, and abdominal discomfort.

6. Interferes with digestion

Alisma plantago-aquatica interferes with digestion by affecting the body's ability to absorb nutrients properly.

Its saponin content can bind to bile acids in the gut, reducing their availability for fat absorption and potentially leading to altered bowel habits, bloating, or changes in appetite.

Additionally, Alisma plantago-aquatica may also alter the balance of gut flora, further compromising digestive function.

7. Provokes skin rashes

Alisma plantago-aquatica provokes skin rashes due to its potential allergenic properties.

The plant contains a variety of compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can cause an allergic response in some individuals.

This reaction may manifest as skin irritation, redness, or even blistering.

8. Enhances nausea symptoms

Alisma plantago-aquatica enhances nausea symptoms due to its ability to stimulate digestive processes and increase gastric motility.

This may lead to an exacerbation of existing nausea issues, particularly in individuals with sensitive stomachs or irritable bowel syndrome.

The herb's bioactive compounds, such as alkaloids and saponins, are thought to contribute to this effect, potentially causing discomfort, bloating, and digestive upset.

9. Worsens asthma attacks

Alisma plantago-aquatica worsens asthma attacks due to its ability to stimulate the airways and increase bronchial constriction.

This is because Alisma contains a type of flavonoid called quercetin, which has been shown to exacerbate bronchospasm in individuals with asthma.

Additionally, the herb's saponin content may also contribute to this adverse effect by irritating the respiratory tract and triggering an inflammatory response that can worsen asthma symptoms.

10. Increases heart rate

Alisma plantago-aquatica increases heart rate due to its stimulating properties on the cardiovascular system.

This herb is believed to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, which can lead to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, Alisma plantago-aquatica contains bioactive compounds that may enhance cardiac output and vasodilation, further contributing to increased heart rate.
