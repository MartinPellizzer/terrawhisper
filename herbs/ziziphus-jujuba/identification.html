Botanical Identification of Chinese Date (Ziziphus jujuba)

To identify Chinese Date (Ziziphus jujuba) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The botanical identification of Chinese Date, Ziziphus jujuba, places it in the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rhamnales, family Rhamnaceae, genus Ziziphus, and species Ziziphus jujuba. This classification system helps in understanding its biological relationships and medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chinese Date is Ziziphus jujuba, a species within the Rhamnaceae family. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. Common synonyms include Ziziphus sativa and Ziziphus vulgaris. It is widely known by regional names such as "Chinese date" and "date plum" in various parts of Asia.

Morphological Characteristics

Ziziphus jujuba is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a deep, extensive root system. Its stems are typically thorny and branching, with alternate, ovate leaves that are glossy and have serrated edges. The plant produces panicle inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red to orange when ripe, containing one or two seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Ziziphus jujuba can be distinguished by its compound leaves with 5-9 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, red or orange fruit with a leathery skin, and the presence of a single seed. It differs from similar species like Ziziphus spinosa by having longer, more elongated leaves and smaller, less prickly thorns.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Ziziphus jujuba is native to China, particularly in regions like Shanxi, Shandong, and Hebei. It thrives in temperate climates with distinct seasons. It prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 500 and 1500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Ziziphus jujuba blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. Harvesting takes place in autumn, usually from September to November, when the fruit turns red and becomes firm, indicating maturity for identification purposes.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Ziziphus jujuba belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is closely related to other Ziziphus species. It shares characteristics with its genus, such as fruit structure and leaf morphology. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other plants in the Rhamnaceae family.
