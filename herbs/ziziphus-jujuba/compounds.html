Chinese Date (Ziziphus jujuba) Compounds

The primary compounds in Chinese Date include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and tannins, which contribute to astringency. Phenolic acids and glycosides provide additional antioxidant properties. Saponins and terpenoids may support immune function, while coumarin and anthocyanin offer anti-inflammatory effects. Essential oils and alkaloids contribute to the plant's overall pharmacological profile.

Chinese Date (Ziziphus jujuba) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Chinese Date (Ziziphus jujuba). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic effects by exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties. These compounds are believed to support cognitive function and reduce oxidative stress in the body.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Ziziphus jujuba). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in Chinese Date are also believed to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, enhancing the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Ziziphus jujuba). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the traditional use of Chinese Date in promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Ziziphus jujuba, commonly known as Chinese date. It contributes to the plant's calming and sedative effects, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating insomnia and anxiety. This compound is often extracted and used in formulations to support nervous system health.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Ziziphus jujuba). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in calming the mind and improving sleep. Saponin exhibits mild sedative effects and may support overall nervous system health. Its presence enhances the plant's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds found in Ziziphus jujuba. They contribute to the plant's sedative and anxiolytic effects, making them important for traditional uses in herbal medicine. These compounds are responsible for many of the therapeutic properties attributed to Chinese Date.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Ziziphus jujuba, commonly known as Chinese date. It contributes to the plant's sedative and anxiolytic properties, supporting its traditional use in calming the nervous system and improving sleep quality.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Ziziphus jujuba). It contributes to the fruit's deep color and has antioxidant properties that support cardiovascular and neurological health. Found in the skin and pulp, anthocyanin is valued for its potential therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Ziziphus jujuba). It contains volatile components like limonene and borneol, which contribute to its calming and antispasmodic properties. This oil is often used in traditional formulations to support nervous system health and promote relaxation.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Ziziphus jujuba, commonly known as Chinese date. It contributes to the plant's sedative and calming effects, supporting its traditional use in treating anxiety and insomnia. This alkaloid plays a key role in the herb's therapeutic properties within traditional herbal medicine.
