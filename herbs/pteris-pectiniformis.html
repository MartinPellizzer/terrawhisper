What is Pteris pectiniformis?

Pteris pectiniformis is a species of fern in the family Pteridaceae.

It is a rhizomatous perennial with fronds typically 30-100 cm long, and 10-20 cm wide, consisting of a rachis and pinnae that are linear to lanceolate in shape. The leaves are evergreen, with the stipe being 10-30 cm long and bearing a leafy crown at the apex. Pteris pectiniformis is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in China, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands, where it grows in humid forests and woodlands.

The plant's fronds are dark green with spiny or toothed margins.
