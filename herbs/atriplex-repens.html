Atriplex repens

What's the taxonomical classification of Atriplex repens?

Atriplex repens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Caryophyllales and falls under the family Amaranthaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Atriplex, with the specific designation of the species repens.

What are the therapeutic actions of Atriplex repens?

Atriplex repens has diuretic, laxative, and anti-inflammatory properties that are primarily utilized to support kidney function and digestive health. The plant contains various mineral salts and organic acids that promote the excretion of excess fluids from the body. These chemical components help reduce edema and support the metabolic processes of the renal system. Additionally, the high fiber content and specific phytochemicals assist in regulating bowel movements to prevent constipation. When used in traditional herbal medicine, it acts as a gentle cleanser for the urinary tract.
