Atriplex repens

What's the taxonomical classification of Atriplex repens?

Atriplex repens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Caryophyllales and falls under the family Amaranthaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Atriplex, with the specific designation of the species repens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Atriplex repens has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats and leaves that are typically fleshy, ovate to lanceolate, and measuring between 1 and 5 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are often dentate or wavy, while the surfaces are covered in small, bladder-like scales characteristic of the Amaranthaceae family. These scales are particularly prominent on the underside of the foliage and serve to manage salt and water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in clusters within the leaf axils. These reproductive structures eventually develop into tiny, dry fruits encased by the specialized scales.
