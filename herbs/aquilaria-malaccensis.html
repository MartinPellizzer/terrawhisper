Aquilaria malaccensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Aquilaria malaccensis?

Aquilaria malaccensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Malvales, it is a member of the family Thymelaeaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Aquilaria, with its specific designation being the species malaccensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aquilaria malaccensis contains a complex profile of sesquiterpenoids, triterpenoids, and various phenolic compounds primarily concentrated within its resinous heartwood. The most significant medicinal components are the agarosides and specific diterpenes that contribute to the plant's aromatic and bioactive properties. These chemical constituents are studied for their potential antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities in pharmacological applications. Researchers often focus on the specific molecular structures of these volatile oils to understand how they interact with biological systems. The presence of these specialized metabolites makes the species a subject of interest in traditional medicine and modern drug discovery.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aquilaria malaccensis?

Aquilaria malaccensis has bioactive properties that function primarily as an antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant agent in traditional medicinal practices. The plant contains various secondary metabolites such as terpenoids and flavonoids that help combat bacterial infections and oxidative stress. These chemical constituents work together to reduce inflammation within bodily tissues and promote cellular healing. Some studies suggest that extracts from the plant may also possess sedative or calming effects on the central nervous system. Consequently, it is often utilized in herbal formulations to treat skin ailments and digestive disturbances.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, digestive issues, and inflammation. Practitioners often apply its components topically to address wounds and fungal skin conditions. Internally, it is utilized to soothe stomach discomfort and regulate digestive functions. The plant also possesses properties that help reduce swelling and general bodily inflammation. These traditional uses highlight its importance in managing localized and systemic health concerns.

What are the herbal preparations of Aquilaria malaccensis?

Aquilaria malaccensis is prepared primarily as a resinous oil through the complex process of hydrodistillation or dry distillation of the resin-infused heartwood. This precious essential oil serves as the base for various aromatic compounds used in traditional medicine and perfumery. In some traditional practices, the dried wood or crushed resin may also be infused into liquids to create medicinal decoctions. These preparations are often applied topically or used in incense to harness the plant's therapeutic properties. The resulting extracts are highly valued for their concentrated potency and unique scent profiles.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Aquilaria malaccensis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive upset if ingested or handled without protection. The essential oils and resins extracted from the plant contain various bioactive compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. When applied topically, these substances can lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling of the skin. Ingestion of the plant material is not recommended due to the potential for gastrointestinal distress or more severe systemic toxicity. Proper precautions should be taken to avoid direct contact with the sap or concentrated extracts.
