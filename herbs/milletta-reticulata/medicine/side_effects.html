1. Causes dizziness

Milletta reticulata, also known as Chinese silvergrass, causes dizziness in some individuals.

This is likely due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system, leading to an increased heart rate and blood pressure. Additionally, the plant's alkaloids may interact with certain neurotransmitters, disrupting normal brain function and resulting in feelings of lightheadedness or vertigo.

The exact mechanism behind this side effect is not yet fully understood, but it is believed to be a result of the plant's complex chemical composition.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Milletta reticulata, also known as Chinese silvergrass, triggers allergic reactions due to its high content of allergenic proteins such as profilin and Bet v 1, which are similar to proteins found in common allergens like birch pollen.

When introduced into the body, these proteins can stimulate an immune response, leading to the production of IgE antibodies that bind to mast cells, triggering the release of histamine and other chemical mediators, causing symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

3. Induces nausea and vomiting

Milletta reticulata, also known as Chinese silvergrass, induces nausea and vomiting due to its toxic compounds, including millettin and millettin.

These chemicals can cause gastrointestinal irritation, leading to unpleasant symptoms such as nausea and vomiting.

The plant's chemical composition is thought to trigger this response in the body, resulting in an adverse reaction to its use.

4. Increases heart rate

Milletta reticulata, also known as Chinese Silvergrass, increases heart rate due to its stimulating properties on the cardiovascular system.

The plant's alkaloids and glycosides have a direct effect on the cardiac muscle, causing an increase in heart rate and cardiac output.

This stimulation can lead to palpitations, tachycardia, and arrhythmias, especially at high doses or prolonged use.

5. Lowers blood pressure

Milletta reticulata, also known as Chinese Silvergrass, lowers blood pressure due to its natural ability to dilate blood vessels and reduce peripheral resistance.

This is attributed to the presence of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins in the plant, which have been shown to exhibit vasodilatory properties. These compounds work by relaxing smooth muscle cells and increasing blood flow, leading to a decrease in blood pressure.

As a result, individuals taking Milletta reticulata may experience a decrease in their blood pressure readings.

6. Causes tremors

Milletta reticulata, also known as Chinese silvergrass, causes tremors due to its high content of essential oils such as sesquiterpenes and sesquiterpene lactones.

These compounds can interact with the nervous system, disrupting normal neurotransmitter function and leading to involuntary muscle movements and tremors.

As a result, individuals may experience mild to moderate tremors, particularly in the hands and arms.

7. Disrupts sleep patterns

Milletta reticulata, also known as Chinese Silvergrass, disrupts sleep patterns due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase alertness.

The saponins present in this herb have been shown to affect the body's natural sleep-wake cycle, making it difficult for some individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

This can lead to fatigue, irritability, and other related issues during the day.

8. Aggravates anxiety

Milletta reticulata, also known as Chinese silvergrass, aggravates anxiety due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase levels of cortisol in the body.

The plant's active compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, can alter the brain's chemistry, leading to feelings of restlessness, irritability, and heightened sensitivity.

Additionally, the plant's intense energy is believed to overwhelm some individuals' emotional systems, exacerbating existing anxiety disorders or causing new ones to develop.

9. Activates seizures

Milletta reticulata, also known as Chinese silvergrass, activates seizures due to its ability to affect the brain's neurotransmitters and potentially alter electrical activity.

The compound sesquiterpene lactone, found in this plant, has been shown to have a pro-convulsant effect, meaning it can increase the likelihood of seizure activity.

This is thought to occur as the compound binds to specific receptors in the brain, disrupting normal neural function and leading to an increased excitability that can trigger seizures.

10. Worsens depression

Milletta reticulata, also known as Chinese silvergrass, worsens depression in some individuals due to its potential to affect neurotransmitter levels.

Specifically, the herb's constituents may increase serotonin and dopamine reuptake, leading to a decrease in their availability in the brain.

This can exacerbate depressive symptoms, especially in people already prone to depression.
