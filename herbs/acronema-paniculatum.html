What is Acronema paniculatum?

Acronema paniculatum is a flowering plant that belongs to the family Apiaceae.

It is an annual or perennial herb with a stout base and a branched stem that grows up to 50-80 cm in height. The leaves are bipinnately compound, with 5-7 pairs of pinnae and 10-20 pairs of leaflets, arranged oppositely on the stem. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers in terminal panicles, which are arranged in umbels and are surrounded by a few bracts.

The flowers are followed by ovate or elliptical fruits, 4-6 mm in length, that are attached to a persistent calyx.
