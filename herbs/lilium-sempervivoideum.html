Lilium sempervivoideum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium sempervivoideum?

Lilium sempervivoideum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its order, Liliales, the plant is placed within the family Liliaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Lilium, leading to its unique designation as the species sempervivoideum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium sempervivoideum has an elongated rhizomatous stem base, lanceolate leaves arranged in whorls or spirals, and large, bell-shaped flowers that typically exhibit a pale yellowish or greenish-white coloration with distinct spotting. The leaves are relatively narrow and taper to a fine point, providing a dense green foliage throughout the growing season. Its floral structure consists of six prominent tepals that curve outward to reveal dark, concentrated spots near the center of the throat. The reproductive organs include long filaments supporting anthers and a central style that extends deep into the perianth. This perennial herb relies on its robust underground rhizomes to store nutrients and facilitate seasonal regrowth.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The species is typically found at elevations ranging from middle to high mountain zones. These specific microclimates allow the plant to thrive within its limited temperate range.
