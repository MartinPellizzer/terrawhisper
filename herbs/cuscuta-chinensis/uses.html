Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis) Uses

Chinese Dodder is used to treat respiratory infections, asthma, cough, and skin infections. It supports wound healing and is applied for kidney stones and urinary tract infections. It is also used to address anemia and improve overall kidney function.

Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis) Monograph

1. Respiratory infection

Chinese Dodder is used in traditional Chinese medicine to support respiratory health. It is often prescribed for respiratory infections due to its properties that help reduce inflammation and clear phlegm. The herb is typically combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in treating conditions like bronchitis and coughs. Its usage aims to restore balance and improve lung function.

2. Asthma

Chinese Dodder is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat asthma by promoting lung function and reducing phlegm. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effects. The plant's ability to clear heat and relieve coughing makes it a valuable component in asthma formulations. It is typically prepared as a decoction or powder for internal use.

3. Respiratory infections

Chinese Dodder is used in traditional Chinese medicine to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and clearing phlegm. It is commonly prescribed for conditions like bronchitis and coughs, helping to ease symptoms and promote recovery. Its properties are believed to strengthen the lungs and improve respiratory function.

4. Cough

Chinese Dodder is used to treat coughs characterized by a dry, persistent cough and lung heat. It helps to clear phlegm and soothe the lungs, making it effective for chronic coughs. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects in traditional formulas.

5. Skin infection

Chinese Dodder is used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and pus formation in conditions like boils and fungal infections. Often prepared as a decoction or ointment, it promotes wound healing and prevents secondary infections.

6. Skin infections

Chinese Dodder is used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on the skin. Often prepared as a decoction or ointment, it is applied topically to promote healing and prevent secondary infections. Its effectiveness is supported by traditional use and modern pharmacological studies.

7. Wound healing

Chinese Dodder is used in traditional Chinese medicine to promote wound healing due to its ability to enhance tissue regeneration and reduce inflammation. It is often applied externally as a poultice or incorporated into formulations to support the body's natural healing processes. Its properties help accelerate the closure of wounds and prevent infection.

8. Kidney stones

Chinese Dodder is used in traditional Chinese medicine to help dissolve and expel kidney stones due to its properties that promote urinary flow and reduce dampness. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in treating stone-related conditions of the urinary system.

9. Urinary tract infections

Chinese Dodder is used in traditional Chinese medicine to support urinary tract health. It is believed to help reduce inflammation and promote healing in urinary tract infections. Its antimicrobial properties may aid in clearing infections, though it is typically used as part of a broader treatment regimen.

10. Anemia

Chinese Dodder is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat anemia by nourishing the blood and replenishing qi. It is particularly effective for cases of blood deficiency with symptoms like pale complexion, dizziness, and fatigue. The herb is often combined with other blood-tonifying herbs in formulas to enhance its therapeutic effect.
