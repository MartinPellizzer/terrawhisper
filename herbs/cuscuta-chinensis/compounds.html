Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis) Compounds

The primary compounds in Chinese Dodder include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while alkaloids may influence nerve function. These compounds work together to support the plant's medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.

Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are often isolated and used in traditional herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to support wound healing and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Cuscuta chinensis are often used in traditional herbal formulations for their potential therapeutic benefits.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting kidney health and reducing inflammation. This compound is known for its antioxidant properties, supporting the plant's medicinal applications in herbal medicine.
