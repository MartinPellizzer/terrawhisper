What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia adamsii?

Artemisia adamsii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a large and diverse group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Artemisia. Its full scientific designation is completed at the species level as Artemisia adamsii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia adamsii has a perennial growth habit characterized by woody bases and deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves that are typically linear or lanceolate in shape.

The foliage often exhibits a fine, pubescent texture which provides a soft, felt-like appearance to the plant. Its flowering structures consist of small, inconspicuous heads arranged in panicles or racemes. These inflorescences emerge from the axils of the leaves and contribute to the plant's overall structural complexity.

The stems are often branched and can vary in rigidity depending on the maturity of the specimen.
