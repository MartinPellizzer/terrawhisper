What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia adamsii?

Artemisia adamsii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a large and diverse group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Artemisia. Its full scientific designation is completed at the species level as Artemisia adamsii.

    What health problems are treated using this plant?

    This plant is used for treating infections caused by the trypanosome parasites Trypanosoma gambiense, Trypanosoma brucei brucei, Trypanosoma congolense, Trypanosoma equiperdum, and Trypanosoma evansi. These specific protozoan organisms are responsible for debilitating diseases such as human African trypanosomiasis and various forms of animal nagana. Researchers investigate the medicinal properties of Artemisia adamsii to find effective ways to combat these bloodstream infections. The plant contains bioactive compounds that demonstrate potential antiparasitic activity against these diverse species. Utilizing such botanical resources may offer new therapeutic avenues for managing parasitic loads in both humans and livestock.
