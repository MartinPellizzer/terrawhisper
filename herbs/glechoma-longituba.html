Glechoma longituba

What's the taxonomical classification of Glechoma longituba?

Glechoma longituba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. The plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by its distinct morphological traits, and is specifically identified within the genus Glechoma. Ultimately, its complete scientific designation is defined by the species epithet longituba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glechoma longituba has opposite, ovate to oblong leaves with crenate margins and a distinctively long petiole that distinguishes it from similar species. The plant features a decumbent or creeping growth habit with square-shaped stems characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. Small, zygomorphic flowers emerge from the leaf axils in a loose arrangement. These blossoms are typically pale blue or lavender and possess a tubular corolla. The foliage remains relatively thin and soft throughout the growing season.

What are the pharmacological activities of Glechoma longituba?

This plant has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antitrombotic, cholitiasis treatment, and urolithiasis treatment pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents help neutralize free radicals and reduce systemic inflammation within the body. The plant also demonstrates an ability to prevent blood clot formation through its antitrombotic properties. Furthermore, it serves as a therapeutic agent for managing gallstones in cholitiasis and kidney stones in urolithiasis. These diverse biological actions make the species a significant subject in ethnopharmacological research.

What side effects this plant can have?

Because clinical studies on human safety are limited, caution is necessary when using this species.
