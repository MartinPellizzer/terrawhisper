Glechoma longituba

What's the taxonomical classification of Glechoma longituba?

Glechoma longituba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. The plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by its distinct morphological traits, and is specifically identified within the genus Glechoma. Ultimately, its complete scientific designation is defined by the species epithet longituba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glechoma longituba has opposite, ovate to oblong leaves with crenate margins and a distinctively long petiole that distinguishes it from similar species. The plant features a decumbent or creeping growth habit with square-shaped stems characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. Small, zygomorphic flowers emerge from the leaf axils in a loose arrangement. These blossoms are typically pale blue or lavender and possess a tubular corolla. The foliage remains relatively thin and soft throughout the growing season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of East Asia, specifically occurring across parts of China, Japan, and Korea. It is commonly found inhabiting moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and roadside banks within these territories. The species tends to thrive in subtropical and temperate zones where humidity levels remain relatively high. Its distribution is closely linked to the availability of damp soil and partial sunlight typical of woodland ecosystems. Localized populations may also be identified in specific mountain ranges throughout its primary range.

What are the pharmacological activities of Glechoma longituba?

This plant has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antitrombotic, cholithiasis treatment, and urolithiasis treatment pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize harmful free radicals within the body to prevent cellular damage. The anti-inflammatory effects assist in reducing swelling and pain associated with various inflammatory conditions. Furthermore, its antitrombotic activity works to prevent the formation of blood clots in the circulatory system. Finally, the plant serves as an effective medicinal agent in the treatment of both gallstones and kidney stones.

According to a study published by "Plants (Basel, Switzerland)", Glechoma longituba is one of the traditional medicinal plants that have shown antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These pharmacological activities are documented within a scoping review that screened a total of 184 studies. The plant's molecular mechanism of action involves the activation of antioxidant genes induced by Nrf2 or the suppression of inflammatory gene expression through the inhibition of NFκ-B. These effects may be modulated by bioactive compounds such as polyphenols, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and terpenes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds glecholone, glechoimanosides A through E, vomifoiliol, oleanolic acid, and ursolic acid. These bioactive constituents are responsible for the diverse pharmacological activities attributed to the species. The presence of triterpenoids like oleanolic and ursolic acid often contributes to anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Additionally, the unique glycosides known as glechoimanosides play a significant role in its chemical profile. Together, these diverse molecules form the basis for investigating the plant's potential therapeutic applications.

According to a study published by "Yao xue xue bao = Acta pharmaceutica Sinica", manifold chromatography methods were used to separate the chemical constituents of Glechoma longituba (Nakai) Kupr. Nine compounds were isolated and identified from the plant. These constituents include glecholone (1), 6R,9R-3-oxo-alpha-ionol (2), S(+)-dehydrovomifoliol (3), vomifoliol (4), corosolic acid (5), quercetin (6), stigmastenol (7), myristic acid (8) and triacontanol (9). While compound 1 is a new compound, compounds 2 through 9 were isolated from this plant for the first time.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating urolithiasis, cholelithiasis, various diseases, vision-threatening retinal degenerative disorders, and neurodegenerative disorders. Its bioactive compounds offer therapeutic potential in managing kidney stones and gallbladder stones through medicinal properties. Researchers have also identified its effectiveness in addressing diverse ailments that affect systemic health. Furthermore, it shows promise in protecting ocular health against degenerative retinal conditions. The plant also demonstrates significant utility in mitigating the progression of various neurodegenerative disorders.

According to a study published by "Plants (Basel, Switzerland)", Glechoma longituba is used for the management of urolithiasis (UL). This condition involves the presence of stones in the kidneys or urinary tract. Out of a total of 184 studies screened, the research identified that Glechoma longituba possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These effects are relevant because stone formation is related to oxidative stress and inflammation. The review included 6 studies from China (2), India (3), and Corea (1) regarding these medicinal plants.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic skin reactions, digestive upset, and potential interactions with blood-thinning medications due to its bioactive compounds. Some individuals may experience localized dermatitis or rashes upon direct contact with the leaves. Ingesting large quantities could lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or abdominal discomfort. There is also a theoretical risk that its chemical properties might interfere with how the body processes certain pharmaceutical drugs. Because clinical studies on human safety are limited, caution is necessary when using this species.
