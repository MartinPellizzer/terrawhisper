Selaginella Philipsonii

Selaginella philipsonii, a member of the Selaginellaceae family, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

This plant is known for its rich content of bioactive compounds, including polyphenols, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which may contribute to its medicinal value. Preliminary studies suggest that it possesses antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making it a candidate for treating various health conditions. In traditional systems of medicine, such as Ayurveda and Chinese medicine, it has been employed to address respiratory issues, skin disorders, and digestive ailments.

Further scientific research is needed to fully explore its pharmacological potential and validate its use in modern medicine.
