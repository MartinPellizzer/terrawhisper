Apluda mutica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Apluda mutica has a prostrate growth habit characterized by slender, creeping stems that often form dense, mat-like tufts. The leaves are typically narrow, linear, and possess a somewhat involute or rolled structure to minimize water loss. Its inflorescence consists of contracted, spike-like panicles that emerge from the leaf axils. The spikelets are relatively small and arranged in a compact fashion along the rachis. These botanical features collectively allow the plant to thrive in disturbed or open habitats.
