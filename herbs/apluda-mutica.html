Apluda mutica

What's the taxonomical classification of Apluda mutica?

Apluda mutica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated under the order Poales. This plant is a representative of the family Poaceae, falling under the genus Apluda, and is specifically identified by the species name mutica.

What are the therapeutic actions of Apluda mutica?

What side-effects this plant can have?

Apluda mutica can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or physical discomfort if handled improperly due to its structural characteristics. While not classified as highly toxic to humans, the plant's fine hairs or textures may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience redness, itching, or a mild rash upon touching the foliage. Additionally, if the plant is ingested, it could potentially cause gastrointestinal distress depending on the quantity consumed. Careful handling is recommended to avoid these minor but nuisance-level physiological responses.
