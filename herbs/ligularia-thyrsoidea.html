Ligularia thyrsoidea

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia thyrsoidea?

Ligularia thyrsoidea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. The plant is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Ligularia, and its specific identification is defined by the species name thyrsoidea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia thyrsoidea has large, cordate leaves that are characterized by coarsely serrated margins and a distinctively rugose, veined surface texture. The foliage is typically dark green and grows in a basal rosette pattern. Its flowering structure consists of dense, terminal panicles of bright yellow ray florets. These inflorescences are supported by sturdy, upright stems that rise above the dense leaf canopy. The individual flowers feature a central disk of tubular florets surrounded by prominent, spreading petals.
