Rhamnus heterophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhamnus heterophylla?

Rhamnus heterophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, ultimately placing it in the order Rosales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Rhamnaceae. More specifically, it is categorized under the genus Rhamnus, and its unique identification is completed by its specific epithet, species heterophylla.

      What are the herbal preparations of Rhamnus heterophylla?

      Rhamnus heterophyllia is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or tinctures to utilize its various bioactive compounds. The bark and leaves are most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction for medicinal use. Alternatively, steeping the plant parts in hot water produces a milder infusion suitable for daily consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

Direct contact with the foliage or bark can trigger contact dermatitis, manifesting as redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. While specific toxicological studies are limited, caution is advised to avoid accidental ingestion during foraging activities.
