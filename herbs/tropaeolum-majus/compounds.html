Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) Compounds

The primary compounds in Garden Nasturtium include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and coumarin, known for its antispasmodic effects. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to its astringent qualities, while terpenoids and essential oils provide aromatic and anti-inflammatory benefits. Glycosides, saponins, and anthocyanins add to its medicinal profile, including potential antimicrobial and cardiovascular support. Alkaloids are also present, though their specific roles require further research.
