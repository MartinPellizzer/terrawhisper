Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) Uses

Garden Nasturtium is used to treat respiratory infections, bronchial congestion, and coughs. It helps reduce mucous membrane inflammation and may support recovery from influenza. The plant also aids in wound healing and can address skin infections, irritation, fungal infections, and skin inflammation.

Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) has been used traditionally to support respiratory health. Its leaves and flowers contain compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it useful for alleviating symptoms of respiratory infections such as coughs and bronchitis. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to help clear mucus and reduce congestion.

2. Bronchial congestion

Garden Nasturtium supports bronchial congestion by acting as a respiratory stimulant and expectorant. It helps loosen mucus and ease breathing, making it useful for conditions like bronchitis and coughs. Its anti-inflammatory properties may also reduce airway swelling, promoting clearer airways.

3. Cough

Garden Nasturtium is used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. It may also have mild antimicrobial effects, aiding in clearing respiratory infections. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for internal use.

4. Influenza

Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) may support immune function and help alleviate symptoms of influenza due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is traditionally used to reduce fever, sore throat, and congestion, though it should be used cautiously and under guidance, especially for prolonged or severe cases.

5. Mucous membrane inflammation

Garden Nasturtium is used to support mucous membrane inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce irritation and swelling in the respiratory and urinary tracts. Often prepared as a tincture or tea, it is valued for its ability to soothe and protect mucous membranes.

6. Skin infections

Garden Nasturtium has been used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds that inhibit the growth of bacteria and fungi. Topical applications can help reduce symptoms of minor infections like boils and rashes. It is often used as a natural alternative in herbal medicine for skin health.

7. Wound healing

Garden Nasturtium has been traditionally used to support wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The leaves and flowers contain compounds that may help reduce infection risk and promote tissue repair. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to aid in the healing process of minor cuts and abrasions.

8. Skin irritation

Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) can be used topically to soothe skin irritation. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce redness, swelling, and infection in minor skin issues. A diluted tincture or infused oil can be applied directly to affected areas for relief. It is particularly useful for eczema, rashes, and insect bites.

9. Fungal infection

Garden Nasturtium has been traditionally used to support the treatment of fungal infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds like essential oils and tannins that exhibit antifungal activity. It is often applied topically to treat skin infections and may help prevent the spread of fungal growth. Its use aligns with herbal medicine practices aimed at natural antifungal support.

10. Skin inflammation

Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) has been traditionally used to address skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The leaves and flowers contain compounds that help reduce redness and irritation, making it useful for conditions like eczema and minor skin infections. It can be applied topically as a poultice or infused oil for targeted relief.
