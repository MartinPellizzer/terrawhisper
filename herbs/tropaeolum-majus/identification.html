Botanical Identification of Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus)

To identify Garden Nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Garden Nasturtium, Tropaeolum majus, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Tropaeolales, family Tropaeolaceae, genus Tropaeolum, and species Tropaeolum majus. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Tropaeolum majus is the scientific name for Garden Nasturtium. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. Common synonyms include Tropaeolum fendleri and Tropaeolum speciosum. It is also known by regional names such as Indian Cress and Garden Nasturtium.

Morphological Characteristics

Garden Nasturtium is an annual plant with a fibrous root system. Its stems are hairy and sprawling, often ascending or trailing. Leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy surface and prominent veins. The inflorescence is terminal, bearing clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The fruit is a capsule containing tiny, round seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Garden Nasturtium can be distinguished by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers with red centers, heart-shaped leaves, and distinctive seed pods. It differs from similar species like Tropaeolum tuberosum by its leaf shape and flower color. Its peppery taste when crushed also helps in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Garden Nasturtium is native to South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. It thrives in temperate climates with full sun and well-drained soil. It prefers loamy or sandy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Garden Nasturtium blooms from late spring to early autumn, typically between June and September. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to October. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during early bloom when leaves and flowers are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Tropaeolum majus is closely related to species within the Tropaeolum genus. It shares key characteristics with other members of the Tropaeolaceae family, such as alternate leaves and showy flowers. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other flowering plants with similar morphological traits.
