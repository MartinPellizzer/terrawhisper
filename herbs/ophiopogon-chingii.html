Ophiopogon chingii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiopogon chingii?

Ophiopogon chingii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asparagales, it is placed in the family Asparagaceae, specifically falling under the genus Ophiopogon, with the species identified as chingii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophiopogon chingii has narrow, linear, evergreen leaves that typically measure 5 to 15 centimeters in length and form dense, tufted clumps. The foliage exhibits a dark green color and a slightly leathery texture throughout the growing season. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, delicate white to pale lavender flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms eventually give way to small, globose, dark blue or black berries that persist on the stems. The overall growth habit is low-lying and spreading, making it suitable for groundcover applications.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?
