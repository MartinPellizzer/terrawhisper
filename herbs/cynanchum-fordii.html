Cynanchum fordii

What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum fordii?

Cynanchum fordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified by the genus Cynanchum and the species name fordii.

These molecules interfere with the phosphorylation of key kinases, thereby preventing the transcription of inflammatory mediators. Additionally, its chemical constituents interact with enzymes involved in oxidative stress regulation to provide neuroprotective benefits. By regulating these specific protein interactions, the plant demonstrates significant potential in treating inflammatory diseases and neurodegeneration.

What side-effects this plant can have?

While it is often studied for its medicinal properties, the exact toxicological profile in humans remains insufficiently documented in large-scale clinical trials. Some users may experience nausea or stomach cramps after ingestion. There is also a risk of unforeseen interactions with other pharmaceutical medications. Due to these uncertainties, cautious administration is necessary to avoid adverse physiological responses.
