Cynanchum fordii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum fordii has climbing stems with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that feature entire margins and acute or acuminate apices. The foliage is typically characterized by a smooth texture and distinct venation patterns. Its inflorescences consist of small, clustered flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers possess a complex structure typical of the Apocynaceae family, often featuring a corona. The plant produces slender, elongated fruit structures known as follicles that contain numerous tiny seeds.
