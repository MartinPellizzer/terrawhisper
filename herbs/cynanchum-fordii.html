Cynanchum fordii

What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum fordii?

Cynanchum fordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified by the genus Cynanchum and the species name fordii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum fordii has climbing stems with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that feature entire margins and acute or acuminate apices.

The foliage is typically characterized by a smooth texture and distinct venation patterns. Its inflorescences consist of small, clustered flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers possess a complex structure typical of the Apocynaceae family, often featuring a corona.

The plant produces slender, elongated fruit structures known as follicles that contain numerous tiny seeds.
