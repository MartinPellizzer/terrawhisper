Possible Side Effects of sanguinaria canadensis

Sanguinaria canadensis can have side effects if used improperly, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to the high levels of alkaloids present in the herb.

If taken in large quantities or for extended periods, it can also cause dizziness, headaches, and fatigue.

Additionally, sanguinaria canadensis can interact with other medications, such as blood thinners, and increase the risk of bleeding.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption of sanguinaria canadensis can also lead to kidney damage and liver toxicity.
