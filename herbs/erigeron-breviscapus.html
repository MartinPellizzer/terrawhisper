Erigeron breviscapus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Asterales. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erigeron breviscapus has a low-growing, perennial habit characterized by short, erect stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 20 centimeters and bear small, white to pale lavender flower heads. The foliage consists of basal leaves that are generally spatulate or oblanceolate in shape and often possess fine, spreading hairs along their margins. Stem leaves are fewer in number, smaller, and more widely spaced than the basal rosette. Each inflorescence features numerous narrow ray florets surrounding a central disc of yellow tubular florets. The overall structure of the plant is compact, reflecting its adaptation to open, rocky, or montane environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds breviscapine, erigeronone C, erigeronone D, eriogsteter B, and scutellarein. These specific phytochemicals are secondary metabolites that contribute to the biological profile of Erigeron brevicapus. Research into these substances often focuses on their potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of these diverse chemical constituents highlights the complex chemical makeup of the species. Scientists study these compounds to better understand how they interact with human physiological systems.

According to a study published by "Yao xue xue bao = Acta pharmaceutica Sinica", the dried whole plant of Erigeron breviscapus (Vant.) Hand-Mazz contains a flavonoid mixture known as breviscapine. The main effective constituent of this mixture is scutellarin. In a study involving 18 male Sprague-Dawley rats, breviscapine injection was administered at a dose of 20 mg x kg(-1), i.v. This investigation examined the interaction between scutellarin and valsartan at a dose of 15 mg x kg-, i.g. The data showed that the plasma clearance (CL(p)) and the bile clearance (CL(b)) of scutellarin were reduced significantly when co-administered with valsartan.
