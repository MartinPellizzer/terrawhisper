Eupatorium lindleyanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Eupatorium lindleyanum?

Eupatorium lindleyanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is one of the most prominent plant families, and is specifically categorized within the genus Eupatorium. Its full scientific designation is completed by its specific epithet, making the species Eupatorium lindleyanum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Eupatorium lindleyanum?

This plant has anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, anti-periodontitis, cytotoxicity, and phlegm-reducing pharmacological activities. Its cytotoxic properties allow it to target and destroy specific malignant cells during cancer research. The medicinal components also work to reduce inflammation and combat the progression of periodontitis in oral health. Additionally, the plant exhibits phlegm-reducing effects that can assist in managing respiratory congestion. These diverse biological actions demonstrate the significant therapeutic potential of Eupatorium lindleyanum.

As discussed in a study, Eupalinolide B is a sesquiterpenoid natural product extracted from Eupatorium lindleyanum that has been reported as a potential drug for cancers and immune disorders. This compound ameliorates periodontal inflammation and alveolar bone resorption in a ligated periodontitis mouse model. It was identified as a direct covalent binding protein target of the ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme UBEC2D3 through methods including activity-based protein profiling, biolayer interferometry, and cellular thermal shift assay. By targeting UBEC2D3, Eupalinolide B suppresses the ubiquitination degradation of IκBα to inactivate the nuclear transcription factor-κB signaling pathway. These results suggest that the compound may be a new kind of UBEC2D3 inhibitor and a promising therapeutic agent for anti-periodontitis.

As discussed in a study, the air part of Eupatorium lindleyanum contains several medicinal compounds. Through alcohol percolation, silica gel column chromatography, and re-crystallization, six specific substances were identified. These isolated compounds include Jaceosidin, kaempferol, quceritin, astragalin, trifolin, and hypersoide. Notably, Jaceosidin, kaempferol, quceritin, astragalin, and trifolin were isolated from Eupatorium lindleyanum for the first time. The structures of these compounds were elucidated using spectral evidence.
