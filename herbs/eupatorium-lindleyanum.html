What's the taxonomical classification of Eupatorium lindleyanum?

Eupatorium lindleyanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is one of the most prominent plant families, and is specifically categorized within the genus Eupatorium. Its full scientific designation is completed by its specific epithet, making the species Eupatorium lindleyanum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Eupatorium lindleyanum?

This plant has anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, cytotoxic, cough-relieving, and phlegm-reducing pharmacological activities. Its cytotoxic properties allow it to target and destroy specific abnormal cell lines, which supports its potential use in anti-cancer research. The plant also functions as an effective anti-inflammatory agent to reduce swelling and pain in various biological systems. In respiratory care, its medicinal compounds act as a cough-relieving mechanism to soothe irritation. Furthermore, it possesses phlegm-reducing qualities that help clear mucus from the airways.

According to a study published by "MedComm", Eupalinolide B is a sesquiterpenoid natural product extracted from Eupatorium lindleyanum. This compound has been reported as a potential drug for cancers and immune disorders. In a ligated periodontitis mouse model, Eupalinolide B was demonstrated to ameliorate periodontal inflammation and alveolar bone resorption. It functions by targeting the ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme UBEC2D3 to suppress the ubiquitination degradation of IκBα. This mechanism leads to the inactivation of the nuclear transcription factor-κB signaling pathway. Consequently, Eupalinolide B may serve as a new kind of UBEC2D3 inhibitor and a promising therapeutic agent for anti-periodontitis.
