What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis intricata?

Clematis intricata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the biological hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Clematis, and its specific species designation, intricata.

      What are the herbal preparations of Clematis intricata?

      Clematis intricata is prepared primarily as a decoction, a tea made by simmering the woody stems or roots in water to extract its medicinal components. Practitioners may also utilize the plant in the form of a tincture, which involves macerating the dried material in alcohol to create a concentrated liquid extract. Choosing the correct form is essential for maximizing the bioavailability of the plant's active constituents.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis intricata can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of protoanemonin. This specific chemical compound is known to act as a severe irritant to the mucous membranes and sensitive skin tissues. Contact with the plant may result in redness, itching, or blistering on the hands or legs. If any part of the vine is swallowed, it can lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and stomach pain. Because of these risks, caution should be exercised when handling the plant to avoid accidental exposure.
