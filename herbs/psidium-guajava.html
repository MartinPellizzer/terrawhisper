What are the pest and diseases tips of Psidium guajava?

Psidium guajava, commonly known as guava, is a popular fruit tree that is susceptible to several pests and diseases.

One of the most common pests affecting guava trees is the thrips, which can cause damage to leaves and fruits, leading to reduced yields and poor fruit quality. Other pests that can harm guava trees include mealybugs, aphids, and scale insects, which can weaken the tree's defenses and make it more vulnerable to diseases.

Fungal pathogens such as Botryosphaeria dothidea and Colletotrichum gloeosporioides can cause root rot, leaf spot, and fruit rot, leading to significant losses in yields and fruit quality. Bacterial diseases like Xanthomonas arboricola and Erwinia carotovora can also affect guava trees, causing leaf blight and soft rot, respectively. Additionally, viruses such as Guava ringspot virus and Guava mosaic virus can cause mottling, distortion, and stunting of leaves and fruits, further reducing the tree's productivity.

Effective management of these pests and diseases requires a combination of integrated pest and disease management (IPM) strategies, including crop rotation, pruning, sanitation, biological control, and chemical controls.
