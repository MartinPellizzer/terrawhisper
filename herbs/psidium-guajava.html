Guava (Psidium guajava)

Guava (Psidium guajava) is a member of the Solanaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, Australia, and Pacific Islands. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, native american herbal medicine, and european herbal medicine.
