Herbal Preparations

Psidium guajava teas are one of the most popular herbal preparations made from the guava leaf, known for their digestive and antimicrobial properties.

To prepare the tea, fresh or dried guava leaves are steeped in hot water for several minutes, allowing the active compounds such as flavonoids and tannins to infuse into the liquid. This tea is commonly consumed to aid digestion, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal discomfort.

In addition to teas, guava leaves can be used to make decoctions by boiling them for a longer period, which enhances the extraction of more potent compounds. These decoctions are often used in traditional medicine to treat ailments like diarrhea and respiratory infections. Other preparations such as tinctures, capsules, and oils are also derived from guava leaves, offering various therapeutic benefits depending on the method of preparation and application.

These diverse herbal preparations highlight the versatility of Psidium guajava in both traditional and modern health practices.
