Saussurea pachyneura

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea pachyneura has a perennial growth habit characterized by thick, fleshy roots and a rosette of lanceolate to oblong leaves that feature prominently thickened, bristly veins. The leaf margins are typically lined with dense, stiff hairs that give the foliage a textured appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, yellowish-to-brownish flower heads held atop sturdy, upright stems. These flowering structures are surrounded by specialized bracts that offer protection to the developing florets. The overall architecture of the plant is adapted to withstand harsh, high-altitude environments through its compact and robust form.

The distribution is largely restricted to specific subalpine zones where the climate remains cold and harsh.
