What are the pest and diseases tips of Carya ovata?

Carya ovata, also known as the Shagbark Hickory, is a deciduous tree native to North America that is susceptible to several pests and diseases.

One common pest affecting Carya ovata is the hickory horned devil (Citheronia regalis), a large moth whose caterpillars feed on the tree's leaves and stems. The emerald ash borer (Agrilus planipennis) is another significant pest that can infest Carya ovata, causing dieback of branches and eventually killing the tree.

Fungal diseases such as oak wilt (Ceratocystis fagacearum), which infects through wounds or natural openings on the bark, and black knot (Apioplagiostoma apiculatum), a fungal disease that causes cankers to form on the branches, are also prevalent in Carya ovata. Additionally, anthracnose, caused by the fungus Colletotrichum spp., can infect the tree's leaves and stems, causing lesions and defoliation.

Effective management of these pests and diseases is crucial to maintain the health and productivity of Carya ovata orchards.
