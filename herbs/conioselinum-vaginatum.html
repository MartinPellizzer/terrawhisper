Conioselinum vaginatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Conioselinum vaginatum?

Conioselinum vaginatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Apiales and falls under the family Apiaceae. At the most specific taxonomic levels, it is identified by the genus Conioselinum and the species vaginatum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds N-butylphthalide, ligustilide, bergapten, xanthotoxin, and ferulic acid. The phthalide derivatives N-butylphthalide and ligustilide are known for their potential neuroprotective and vasodilatory effects. Furanocoumarins such as bergapten and xanthotoxin are present and can influence skin sensitivity and metabolic processes. Additionally, the phenolic acid ferulic acid contributes significant antioxidant properties to the plant's chemical profile. Together, these diverse bioactive molecules drive much of the plant's therapeutic potential.

As discussed in a study, methanol extracts of Conioselinum vaginatum significantly reduced the toe swelling rate and the content of IL-1β, IL-6 and PGE2 in swollen tissues. The anti-inflamatory effect of C. vaginatum was weaker than that of L. sinense and L. jeholense. Although the study analyzed 18 batches of raw materials for Ligustici Rhizoma et Radix, it noted the specific efficacy of C. vaginatum regarding these three inflammatory markers. The research explored the spectrum-effect relationship between chemical components and pharmacodynamic indexes.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe neurological distress, including tremors, convulsions, and potential death due to its high concentration of toxic alkaloids. These substances act as potent neurotoxins that disrupt the central nervous system when ingested. Symptoms often manifest as muscle spasms or loss of motor control shortly after consumption.
