Primula cicutariifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Primula cicutariifolia?

Primula cicutariifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Primulaceae, specifically within the genus Primula, where it is identified by the unique species designation cicutariifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula cicutariifolia has lanceolate to narrowly elliptical leaves that are characteristically smooth and arranged in a basal rosette. The foliage typically displays a bright green color and features entire or slightly serrated margins depending on the maturity of the plant. Its stems are relatively short, supporting terminal clusters of flowers that emerge from the center of the leaf grouping. The blossoms consist of five petals that often transition in color from a pale base to a more vibrant hue at the tips. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to thrive in damp, meadow-like environments.
