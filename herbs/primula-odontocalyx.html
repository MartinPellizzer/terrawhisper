Primula odontocalyx

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula odontocalyx has a basal rosette of leaves characterized by ovate to oblong shapes, serrated margins, and a distinctively dentate calyx. The leaf blades are typically green and possess a slightly textured surface with prominent venation. Its flowers emerge from a central stem and feature a tubular calyx with tooth-like lobes that give the species its name. The corolla is composed of five petals that are often brightly colored to attract specific pollinators. These morphological traits are essential for distinguishing this particular species within the Primula genus.

      What are the herbal preparations of Primula odontocalyx?

      Primula odontocalyx is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to utilize its medicinal properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Primula odontocalyx can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and digestive upset if ingested. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may trigger localized redness or itching upon direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals might experience more severe dermatological reactions such as swelling or blistering depending on their personal sensitivity to the species. Internal consumption of the plant is not recommended because it may lead to nausea or stomach discomfort.
