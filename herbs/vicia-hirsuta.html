Vicia hirsuta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vicia hirsuta has pinnate leaves consisting of several pairs of leaflets that are typically elliptic to oblong in shape and covered in fine, spreading hairs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and the presence of branched tendrils that assist in climbing surrounding vegetation. Its small, papilionaceous flowers are usually pale violet or bluish and emerge from leaf axils. The fruit develops into small, oblong pods that contain several tiny seeds. This morphology distinguishes it from other vetch species through its specific pubescence and leaf structure.
