Botanical Identification

Mexican Angelica, scientifically known as Ligusticum porteri, is a member of the Apiaceae family. Commonly called Mexican Angelica or sometimes "Angelica," it is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It features a thick, aromatic root, finely divided leaves, and small yellowish-green flowers. The plant has been referenced under various names in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Mexican Angelica contains essential oil, coumarin, and flavonoids as its primary active compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal effects, with coumarin contributing to anticoagulant properties and flavonoids offering antioxidant benefits. The essential oil enhances the overall bioavailability and therapeutic potential of the herb.

  • Essential oil
  • Coumarin
  • Flavonoid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Asthma, common cold, respiratory infections, colds, allergic rhinitis Bronchodilator, antimicrobial, immunostimulant, expectorant
Musculoskeletal Rheumatoid arthritis, muscle pain, gout, tendonitis, joint pain Anti-inflammatory, analgesic
Nervous Headache, migraine, tension headaches, migraines, neurological disorders Analgesic, neuroprotective, vasodilator, tonic

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and as an anti-inflammatory agent.

Safety Profile

Mexican Angelica is generally considered safe when used in typical medicinal doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy due to its potential uterine stimulant effects. Always consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with existing health conditions.
