Myriophyllum spicatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Myriophyllum spicatum?

Myriophyllum spicatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Saxifragales and the family Haloragaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Myriophyllum and is specifically designated as the species spicatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Myriophyllum spicatum possesses long, submerged stems bearing whorls of finely dissected, thread-like leaves that typically consist of multiple pairs of narrow segments.

These leaves are arranged in circular clusters around the stem, giving the plant a feathery or hair-like appearance in the water. The plant can develop floating leaves that are much broader and less divided than the submerged foliage. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the tips of the stems.

The root system is composed of fine, fibrous structures that anchor the plant into soft aquatic sediments.
