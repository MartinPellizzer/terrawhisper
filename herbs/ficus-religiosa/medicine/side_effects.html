1. Causes allergic reactions

Ficus religiosa causes allergic reactions due to its high content of sap and latex.

The sap can cause skin irritation, itching, and rashes in some individuals, while the latex may trigger an allergic response in others, leading to respiratory issues such as wheezing and congestion.

Additionally, the pollen of Ficus religiosa has been known to exacerbate allergies in people who are already sensitive to certain plant proteins.

2. Triggers digestive issues

Ficus religiosa triggers digestive issues when used due to its high content of tannins and saponins.

These bioactive compounds can irritate the stomach lining and intestinal walls, leading to symptoms such as bloating, gas, abdominal pain, and changes in bowel movements.

Additionally, Ficus religiosa may also contain allergenic proteins that can cause an immune response, further exacerbating digestive issues.

3. Leads to insomnia

Ficus religiosa leads to insomnia due to its potential interaction with certain medications and supplements.

The tree's bark and leaves contain compounds that may stimulate the central nervous system, causing difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep. Additionally, Ficus religiosa may increase heart rate and blood pressure, leading to restlessness and anxiety, further exacerbating sleep disturbances.

As a result, people using Ficus religiosa may experience insomnia as an adverse effect.

4. Causes dizziness

Ficus religiosa causes dizziness due to its potential interaction with certain medications, such as blood thinners and antidepressants.

The extracts of the plant may alter the way these drugs are metabolized in the body, leading to a change in their efficacy or causing an increase in side effects.

Additionally, Ficus religiosa may stimulate the central nervous system, which can lead to feelings of dizziness, lightheadedness, and even vertigo.

5. Increases blood pressure

Ficus religiosa increases blood pressure due to its potential interaction with certain medications, such as beta-blockers and diuretics.

The bioactive compounds present in the plant, including flavonoids and polyphenols, may enhance the effects of these medications on blood vessels, leading to increased blood pressure.

Additionally, the stimulating properties of Ficus religiosa may also contribute to its blood-pressure-elevating effect.

6. Stimulates excessive sweating

Ficus religiosa stimulates excessive sweating due to its ability to increase heart rate and blood circulation.

The active compounds in Ficus religiosa, such as ficusin and religiosin, can cause a thermogenic effect, which is characterized by increased metabolism and heat production.

This can lead to an increased sweat production, making individuals experience excessive sweating, especially during physical activity or under hot environmental conditions.

7. Causes nausea and vomiting

Ficus religiosa causes nausea and vomiting.

This side effect is attributed to its high concentration of saponins, which are toxic compounds that can irritate the stomach lining and disrupt digestion. Additionally, the plant's latex sap contains alkaloids, such as ficin, that may stimulate the nervous system and lead to gastrointestinal upset.

As a result, some individuals may experience nausea and vomiting when consuming Ficus religiosa or its extracts.

8. Provokes anxiety attacks

Ficus religiosa provokes anxiety attacks due to its high concentration of caffeine-like compounds, particularly theobromine and theophylline.

These alkaloids can stimulate the central nervous system, leading to feelings of jitteriness, restlessness, and increased heart rate, which may manifest as anxiety attacks in some individuals.

The intense stimulating effects of these compounds can also disrupt normal sleep patterns, further exacerbating anxiety symptoms.

9. Induces headaches

Ficus religiosa induces headaches due to its high concentration of alkaloids, such as ficin and morphenol.

These compounds can stimulate the central nervous system, leading to increased blood flow and potentially causing tension and pain in the head.

Additionally, the tree's active ingredients may interact with individual sensitivities or pre-existing conditions, further exacerbating headache severity.

10. Produces respiratory problems

Ficus religiosa produces respiratory problems due to its high levels of allergenic compounds and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) released into the air.

As these particles are inhaled, they can irritate the lungs and nasal passages, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the tree's pollen can exacerbate existing respiratory conditions like asthma.
