10 Possible Side Effects Of Ficus Religiosa (Bodhi Tree)

Ficus religiosa has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

These side effects can occur due to its high oxalate content, which can cause kidney stone formation and worsen existing kidney conditions.

For instance, people with pre-existing kidney disease may experience increased symptoms, requiring them to adjust their diet and lifestyle, ultimately affecting their quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Ficus religiosa if used imporperly.

Ficus religiosa causes allergic reactions due to its high content of sap and latex.

The sap can cause skin irritation, itching, and rashes in some individuals, while the latex may trigger an allergic response in others, leading to respiratory issues such as wheezing and congestion.

Additionally, the pollen of Ficus religiosa has been known to exacerbate allergies in people who are already sensitive to certain plant proteins.
