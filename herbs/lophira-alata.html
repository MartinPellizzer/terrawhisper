Lophira alata

What's the taxonomical classification of Lophira alata?

Lophira alata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Malpighiales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Ochnaceae, falling under the genus Lophira, and is specifically identified by its species name, alata.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lophira alata?

Lophira alata has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties derived from its bioactive phytochemical constituents. The plant's extracts contain essential compounds that inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. These chemical profiles also help to reduce swelling and alleviate pain in inflamed tissues. When applied topically, the plant aids in the acceleration of skin regeneration and the protection of damaged areas. Researchers continue to investigate these medicinal qualities to better understand their potential in modern pharmacology.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, wounds, and various types of digestive ailments. It contains bioactive compounds that possess significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These medicinal qualities allow the plant to help heal topical lesions and reduce swelling in affected tissues. When ingested or applied correctly, it can also assist in managing gastrointestinal discomfort. Local traditional practices often rely on its extracts to mitigate the symptoms of these diverse health issues.

What are the herbal preparations of Lophira alata?

Lophiira alata is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic application. The roots and leaves are most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid medicine for internal consumption. For external skin ailments, the plant material is often crushed into a paste to be applied directly to the affected area. Some traditional practitioners also utilize the dried powder of the plant to create medicinal washes. These diverse methods allow the plant's active constituents to be administered effectively for various health concerns.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Lophira alata can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with human tissue. The caustic nature of its chemical compounds often results in redness and itching at the site of exposure. If the sap is accidentally ingested, it may lead to severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea and vomiting. Individuals with sensitive skin or known allergies should exercise extreme caution when handling this species. Always wear protective gloves to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
