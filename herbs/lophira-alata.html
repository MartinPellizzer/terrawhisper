What's the taxonomical classification of Lophira alata?

Lophira alata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Malpighiales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Ochnaceae, falling under the genus Lophira, and is specifically identified by its species name, alata.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lophira alata?

This plant has demonstrated diverse pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor promoter, antimalarial, antimicrobial, and antiproliferative properties. Research indicates that its chemical constituents can effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms and malaria parasites. These bioactive compounds also play a crucial role in modulating inflammatory responses within biological systems. Additionally, the plant's ability to influence tumor progression makes it a subject of interest for its anti-tumor promoter characteristics.

As discussed in a study, the medicinal plant Lophira alata provides two chalcone tetramers, lophirachalcone and the new compound alatachalcone, which act as inhibitors of Epstein-Barr virus activation induced by the tumor promoter teleocidin B-4. Both of these isolated compounds demonstrate potent inhibitory activities against inflammation on mouse ear induced by teleocidin B-4. In an initiation-promotion experiment conducted on mouse skin, alatachalcone showed significant results. Specifically, 16 nmol of alatachalcone significantly inhibited tumor promotion caused by 1.6 nmol of 12-O-tetradecanoylphorbol-13-acetate.
