What's the taxonomical classification of Osyris quadripartita?

Osyris quadripartita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Santalales. This plant is further categorized into the family Santalaceae, which places it within the genus Osyris, ultimately identifying it by its specific species name, quadripartita.

What are the pharmacological activities of Osyris quadripartita?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-malarial, anti-diarrheal, and anti-ulcer properties. Researchers have identified specific bioactive compounds within its extracts that effectively inhibit the growth of malaria-causing parasites. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates a capacity to reduce intestinal motility and fluid secretion, which helps manage diarrheal symptoms. Its ability to protect the gastric mucosa also contributes to its documented anti-ulcer efficacy. These diverse medicinal qualities make the species a valuable subject for further drug development studies.

As discussed in a study, Osyris quadripartita is used for traditional malaria treatment and was evaluated using aqueous, chloroform and methanol crude leaf extracts. When administered at oral doses of 200, 400 and 600 mg/kg, the plant material was found to be safe and chemosuppressive. These doses prevented body weight loss and hematological abnormalities while increasing the mean survival time of Swiss albino mice compared to the negative control. The chloroform extract proved to be the most efficacious, prolonging mean mouse survival past day 11 of infection. This specific extract also yielded the highest parasitemia suppression rate of 41.3% at a dose of 600 mg/kg, although parasite clearance was not achieved compared to chloroquine.
