    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Anisodus acutangulus targets acetylcholinesterase, muscarinic acetylcholine receptors, and nicotinic acetylcholine receptors to exert its primary pharmacological effects. The plant contains various tropane alkaloids that act as competitive antagonists at these specific cholinergic sites. By inhibiting the enzyme acetylcholinesterase, the bioactive compounds increase the availability of acetylcholine within the synaptic cleft. This interaction modulates the parasympathetic nervous system, leading to anticholinergic effects such as decreased secretions and smooth muscle relaxation. These molecular interactions underpin the plant's traditional use in treating gastrointestinal spasms and respiratory ailments.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Anisodus acutangulus can cause gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain if ingested in significant quantities. The plant contains various alkaloids that may lead to more severe neurological symptoms in sensitive individuals. Some reports suggest that excessive consumption might trigger dizziness or mild sedation. It is also important to note that skin contact with certain parts of the plant could potentially cause localized irritation or allergic reactions. Due to these unpredictable physiological responses, direct ingestion should be strictly avoided.
