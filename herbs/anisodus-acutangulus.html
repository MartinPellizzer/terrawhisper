What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a variety of medicinal compounds, specifically the tropane alkaloids hyoscyamine and scopolamine. These bioactive substances belong to a broader class of chemical structures known as tropane alkaloid groups. In medical applications, these alkaloids are often utilized to treat gastrointestinal spasms and motion sickness. The physiological effects of these compounds can significantly influence levels of diamine oxidases in the body. Such complex chemical interactions make the plant a subject of intense pharmacological study.

As discussed in a study, Anisodus acutangulus contains tropane alkaloids which are used medicinally as anticholinergic agents. Two full-length cDNA sequences encoding tropinone reductase I, GenBank accession number EU424321, and tropinone reductase II, GenBank accession number EU424322, were identified in this plant. These enzymes, AaTRI and AaTRII, showed an identity of only 60.8% during sequence comparison. In AaTRI-transformed hairy-root lines, there was a mean 1.87-fold higher level of hyoscyamine produced. Additionally, these lines showed a mean 8-fold higher level of scopolamine compared with control roots.
