The variegated grey willow has been used in traditional medicine to promote relaxation, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and stress, due to its therapeutic actions such as sedative, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects.

Possible Side Effects of Salix cinerea var. variegata

Salix cinerea var. variegata can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal irritation, nausea, and diarrhea, which may be caused by the plant's high concentration of salicin, a compound similar to aspirin.

Ingestion of large amounts can lead to salicylate poisoning, resulting in symptoms like dizziness, headaches, and rapid heartbeat.

Improper use of the plant may also cause allergic reactions, such as skin rashes, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Furthermore, using Salix cinerea var. variegata for an extended period can lead to stomach ulcers and interact with certain medications.
