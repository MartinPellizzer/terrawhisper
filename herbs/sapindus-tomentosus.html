Sapindus tomentosus

What's the taxonomical classification of Sapindus tomentosus?

Sapindus tomentosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. The plant is further categorized under the family Sapindaceae, falling into the genus Sapindus, and is specifically identified by the species name tomeventosus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sapindus tomentosus has pinnately compound leaves with 5 to 15 leaflets that feature a velvety, tomentose underside and an entire margin. The leaflets are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape and possess a glossy, dark green upper surface. This tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in dense panicles. The fruit is a fleshy, globose drupe that transitions from green to a bright red or orange color when ripe. Each drupe contains a single, hard seed surrounded by a pulp that is often edible.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, extending from the Indian subcontinent through much of the Malay Archipelago and into northern Australia. Within these regions, the species is frequently found in coastal forests, riverine habitats, and monsoon forests. Its range includes islands such as those in Indonesia, the Philippines, and parts of the Pacific. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea due to the presence of toxic saponins. These chemical compounds act as natural detergents that can lead to significant gastric irritation upon ingestion. If consumed in large quantities, the saponins can induce systemic toxicity within the body. Such reactions often manifest as intense cramping and digestive upset. Careful avoidance of the seeds and unripe fruit is necessary to prevent these adverse health effects.
