Cardiospermum halicacabum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cardiospermum halicacabum?

Cardiospeermum haliacabum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it in the evolutionary lineage of flowering plants. Following its taxonomic hierarchy through the order Sapindales, it is a member of the family Sapindaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cardiospeermum, and its unique species designation, haliacabum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cardiospermum halicacabum has slender, twining stems that produce trifoliate leaves characterized by ovate to lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins. The foliage is typically bright green and provides a dense canopy as the plant climbs using its delicate tendrils. Small, white or pale pink flowers emerge in drooping clusters, often appearing in the leaf axils. These blossoms eventually develop into distinctive, heart-shaped inflated capsules that turn brown as they mature. The seeds are contained within these papery pods, which assist in wind dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Mediterranean Basin, spanning across Southern Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia. It is commonly found in coastal regions where it thrives in sandy or rocky environments near the sea. Its range extends from the Iberian Peninsula eastward through the Levant and into parts of the Middle East. The species typically inhabits maritime habitats such as dunes and seaside cliffs. Localized populations often occur wherever saline soil conditions and moderate temperatures are present.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cardiospermum halicacabum?

This plant has antibacterial, antimicrobial, antioxidant, antiprotozoal, and antiulcer pharmacological activities. These therapeutic properties stem from the diverse bioactive secondary metabolites found within its chemical profile. The antioxidant capacity helps neutralize free radicals, thereby protecting cells from oxidative stress. Its antimicrobial and antibacterial effects allow it to inhibit the growth of various harmful pathogens. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates effectiveness against protozoa and provides protective mechanisms against gastric ulcers.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Cardiospermum halicacabum exhibits a broad spectrum of pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, anti-arthritic, anti-diabetic, antiulcer, apoptotic, antibacterial, antiviral, anti-diarrheal, antioxidant, hepatoprotective, and nephroprotective properties. The plant is used in traditional Indian medicine systems for the treatment of rheumatism, abdominal pain, orchitis, dropsy, lumbago, skin diseases, cough, nervous disorders, and hyperthermia. Specifically, the bioactive compound Cardio spermin, which was isolated from the root extract, has been recognized for its anxiolytic activity. Although the plant shows these diverse therapeutic properties, the bioactive compounds responsible for most of them have not been elucidated till now. Additional human clinical trials are required to determine the potent and safe dosages for treating various health abnormalities.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds luteolin, rutin, and tannins, which contribute to its diverse pharmacological activities. Luteolin acts as a potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent within the plant's chemical profile. The presence of rutin provides additional flavonoid benefits, specifically supporting vascular health and reducing oxidative stress. Tannins are also found in significant amounts, offering astringent properties that can aid in wound healing and digestive regulation. Together, these bioactive substances form the basis for the plant's traditional therapeutic uses.

According to a study published by "Drug design, development and therapy", Cardiospermum halicacabuum contains various chemical bioactive compounds. The study investigated in vitro the effect of different concentrations of the plant extract ranging from 500 to 31.25 µg. Results showed clear-cut antifungal activity of the total plant extract at the highest concentrations of 500 and 250 µg. Among the tested compounds, the molecules luteolin and rutin were identified in silico as the most important potential inhibitors of Hsp90. Both luteolin and rutin were individually assessed and demonstrated an antifungal effect, though it was lower than that of the total plant extract. These compounds, particularly luteolin and rutin, act as Hsp90 interactors to provide fungistatic activity.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, dizziness, headache, liver toxicity, hypoglycemia, and cardiac arrhythmias. Consuming the plant may lead to severe gastrointestinal distress including stomach cramps and frequent bowel movements. Some users might experience neurological symptoms such as lightheadedness or intense head pain. There is also a significant risk of metabolic issues like dangerously low blood sugar levels. Furthermore, the plant is known to potentially trigger irregular heart rhythms and cause serious damage to liver function.
