Cardiospeermum haliacabum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it in the evolutionary lineage of flowering plants. Following its taxonomic hierarchy through the order Sapindales, it is a member of the family Sapindaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cardiospeermum, and its unique species designation, haliacabum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cardiospermum halicacabum has slender, twining stems that produce trifoliate leaves characterized by ovate to lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins. The foliage is typically bright green and provides a dense canopy as the plant climbs using its delicate tendrils. Small, white or pale pink flowers emerge in drooping clusters, often appearing in the leaf axils. These blossoms eventually develop into distinctive, heart-shaped inflated capsules that turn brown as they mature. The seeds are contained within these papery pods, which assist in wind dispersal.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, dizziness, headache, liver toxicity, hypoglycemia, and cardiac arrhythmias. Consuming the plant may lead to severe gastrointestinal distress including stomach cramps and frequent bowel movements. Some users might experience neurological symptoms such as lightheadedness or intense head pain. There is also a significant risk of metabolic issues like dangerously low blood sugar levels. Furthermore, the plant is known to potentially trigger irregular heart rhythms and cause serious damage to liver function.
