Cardiospermum halicacabum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cardiospermum halicacabum?

Cardiospeermum haliacabum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it in the evolutionary lineage of flowering plants. Following its taxonomic hierarchy through the order Sapindales, it is a member of the family Sapindaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cardiospeermum, and its unique species designation, haliacabum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cardiospermum halicacabum has slender, twining stems that produce trifoliate leaves characterized by ovate to lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins. The foliage is typically bright green and provides a dense canopy as the plant climbs using its delicate tendrils. Small, white or pale pink flowers emerge in drooping clusters, often appearing in the leaf axils. These blossoms eventually develop into distinctive, heart-shaped inflated capsules that turn brown as they mature. The seeds are contained within these papery pods, which assist in wind dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Mediterranean Basin, spanning across Southern Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia. It is commonly found in coastal regions where it thrives in sandy or rocky environments near the sea. Its range extends from the Iberian Peninsula eastward through the Levant and into parts of the Middle East. The species typically inhabits maritime habitats such as dunes and seaside cliffs. Localized populations often occur wherever saline soil conditions and moderate temperatures are present.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cardiospermum halicacabum?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anthelmintic, antibacterial, antioxidant, antiprototoal, and antiprototozoic properties. The presence of bioactive compounds allows it to effectively combat various intestinal parasites and worms. Its antioxidant capacity helps in neutralizing harmful free radicals within biological systems. Additionally, the plant demonstrates strong antibacterial effects against diverse pathogenic microorganisms. These combined medicinal actions make it a valuable subject for studying natural therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, Cardiospermum halicacabum is an important folk medicinal plant used by tribal communities to treat helminth infections. The plant was evaluated for its antioxidant, antiproliferative, and anthelmintic activities. For the antiproliferative studies, cells were treated for 24 h with different doses of plant extracts ranging from 25-200 mg/mL. Both the antiproliferative and anthelmintic bioassays showed a dose-dependent efficacy for the plant. Regarding its anthelmintic activity, Cardiospermum halicacabum showed an LC50 of 13.40 mg/mL.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds luteolin, rutin, and tannins. Luteolin is a flavonoid known for its significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Rutin acts as a powerful antioxidant that helps protect blood vessels and improve circulation. The tannins present in the plant provide astringent qualities and can assist in reducing inflammation. Together, these bioactive substances contribute to the plant's diverse therapeutic potential.

As discussed in a study, Cardiospermum halicacabum contains various chemical bioactive compounds that exhibit antifungal activity. The total plant extract showed clear-cut activity at the highest concentrations of 500 and 250 µg. Among all tested compounds, the molecules luteolin and rutin were identified in silico as the most important potential inhibitors of Hsp90. Both luteolin and rutin were individually assessed and demonstrated an antifungal effect, although it was lower than that of the total extract. The study investigated different concentrations of the plant extract ranging from 500 to 31.25 µg. These compounds, particularly luteolin and rutin, act as possible Hsp90 interactors to provide a strong fungistatic effect.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating diabetes, diarrhea, gastric ulcers, inflammation, and various infectious diseases. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to help regulate blood sugar levels in patients managing diabetic conditions. The plant also acts as an effective remedy to soothe gastrointestinal issues like diarrhea and the painful erosions caused by gastric ulcers. Additionally, its natural compounds work to reduce systemic inflammation throughout the body. It is further utilized as a traditional treatment to combat a wide range of infectious diseases.

As discussed in a study, Cardiospermum haliacabuim shows a broad spectrum of pharmacological activities including anti-diabetic properties. The chemical characterization of C. haliacabuim using LC-MS/MS, HPLC, and NMR exposed the presence of chlorogenic acid, caffeic acid, coumaric acid, luteolin-7-o-glucuronide, apigenin-7-o-glucuronide, and chrysoserol. The main bioactive compounds found in the plant include phenols, phenolic acids, flavonoids, flavonoid glycosides, and flavonoid glucuronides. Although C. haliacabuim possesses these therapeutic properties, the bioactive compounds responsible for them have not been elucidated till now. Additional human clinical trials are needed to determine the potent and safe dosages for the treatment of various health abnormalities.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, dizziness, headache, liver toxicity, hypoglycemia, and cardiac arrhythmias. Consuming the plant may lead to severe gastrointestinal distress including stomach cramps and frequent bowel movements. Some users might experience neurological symptoms such as lightheadedness or intense head pain. There is also a significant risk of metabolic issues like dangerously low blood sugar levels. Furthermore, the plant is known to potentially trigger irregular heart rhythms and cause serious damage to liver function.
