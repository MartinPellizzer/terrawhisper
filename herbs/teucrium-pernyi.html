Teucrium pernyi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Teucrium pernyi has woody, perennial stems that branch out to support small, opposite leaves with serrated margins and a grayish-green color. The leaves are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape and possess a slightly textured surface. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in dense clusters. These flowers are usually white or pale pink and belong to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by their two-lipped structure. The plant often develops a compact, bushy habit as it matures in its native rocky habitats.

    What are the herbal preparations of Teucrium pernyi?

    Teucrium pernyi is prepared primarily as an aqueous infusion, a decoction, or a concentrated tincture for medicinal use.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Teucrium pernyi can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal upset if ingested or applied improperly. While specific clinical data on this exact species is limited, members of the Teucrium genus are often associated with potential hepatotoxicity in high doses. It is important to monitor for any signs of swelling or digestive distress when handling the foliage.
