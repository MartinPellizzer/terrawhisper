Murdannia divergens

What's the taxonomical classification of Murdannia divergens?

Murdannia divergens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the species in the order Commelinales and the family Commelinaeae. Ultimately, its biological identity is defined by its membership in the genus Murdannia, with its specific designation being the species divergens.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many species within the Commelinaceae family contain bioactive compounds that may trigger hypersensitivity reactions. Individuals with known sensitivities to similar herbaceous plants should exercise caution to avoid redness or itching. Contact with mucous membranes or accidental ingestion could potentially lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or more systemic distress. Monitoring for any adverse physical responses is recommended when handling this specimen.
