Murdannia divergens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murdannia divergens has lanceolate to linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along a creeping or ascending stem. The foliage typically features smooth margins and a somewhat fleshy texture characteristic of many members of the Commelinaceae family. Its inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms often display pale petals and are supported by slender pedicels. The plant's overall structure is adapted to spreading across moist ground or growing amidst dense vegetation.
