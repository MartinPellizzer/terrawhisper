What is Syzygium ilocanum?

Syzygium ilocanum is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that grows up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and a straight, smooth trunk. The leaves are elliptical in shape, 5-12 centimeters long, and 2-4 centimeters wide, with a pointed tip and a leathery texture. The flowers are white, fragrant, and arranged in clusters at the end of branches, while the fruit is a berry that is 1-2 centimeters in diameter and contains several seeds.

The bark is smooth and gray, while the wood is reddish-brown and has a dense, fine-grained texture.
