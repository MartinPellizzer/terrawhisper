Morina kokonorica

What's the taxonomical classification of Morina kokonorica?

Morina kokonorica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic order Dipsacales. This plant is further categorized under the family Caprifoliaceae, which places it within the genus Morina, specifically as the species kokonorica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Morina kokonorica has a basal rosette of thick, leathery leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape and feature prominently serrated margins.

These leaves are typically dark green and possess a somewhat rugose surface texture. From the center of this rosette, a single, upright flowering stalk emerges to support a dense inflorescence. The flowers are small and arranged in compact clusters at the top of the stem.

This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, alpine environments.

        What are the herbal preparations of Morina kokonorica?

        Morina kokonorica is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture using the dried roots of the plant. Practitioners typically simmer the root material in water to extract its active bitter principles for digestive support. When used as an infusion, the herb is steeped in hot water to create a milder tea.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Morina kokonorica can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or applied improperly. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may trigger localized redness and itching in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience swelling or a rash if the substance touches mucous membranes. If accidentally eaten, it could lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping. It is important to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse reactions.
