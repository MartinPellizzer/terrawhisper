Thalictrum minus

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum minus?

Thalictrum minus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Thalictrum, with its specific designation being the species minus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the alkaloid berberine along with several specific glycosides known as thalicoside F, thalicoside G, thalicoside H, and thalicoside I. These bioactive secondary metabolites are responsible for many of the plant's observed biological activities. Berberine is a well-studied isoquinoline alkaloid frequently recognized for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The thalicoside series consists of unique glycosidic compounds that contribute to the complex chemical profile of the species.
