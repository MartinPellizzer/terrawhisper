Thalictrum minus

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum minus?

Thalictrum minus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Thalictrum, with its specific designation being the species minus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds berberine and thalicoside F. Berberine is a potent alkaloid frequently studied for its ability to regulate glucose metabolism and provide antimicrobial properties. Thalicoside F serves as another significant bioactive component within the plant's chemical profile. These specific substances contribute to the overall therapeutic potential used in various traditional and modern medical applications. Together, these compounds illustrate the complex pharmacological diversity found in the plant.

As discussed in a study, a new triterpenoid saponin known as thalicoside F was isolated from the above-ground part of Thalictrum minus L. collected in Eastern Siberia. The structure of this medicinal compound was elucidated using a combination of 1D- and 2D-NMR spectroscopy and mass spectroscopy. The chemical formula for thalicoside F was determined to be C47H74O17. This specific compound is characterized as a 3-beta-O-[alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1-->2)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1-->4)-alpha-L-arabinopyranosyl]-11 alpha, 12 alpha-epoxyoleanane-28,13 beta-olide.
