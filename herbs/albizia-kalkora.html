Albizia kalkora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Albizia kalkora has a large deciduous tree structure characterized by a spreading crown and bipinnate leaves that typically measure 30 to 60 centimeters in length. The foliage consists of numerous small, oblong leaflets arranged symmetrically along secondary axes to maximize light absorption. Its bark is generally greyish and becomes fissured or rough as the specimen matures. During the flowering season, the tree produces small, fragrant, cream-colored flowers that appear in dense clusters. These blossoms are followed by flattened, brown seed pods that contain several small seeds essential for reproduction.
