Albizia kalkora

What's the taxonomical classification of Albizia kalkora?

Albizia kalkora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression through the order Fabales, it falls under the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its diverse range of legumes. Finally, it is identified by the genus Albizia and is specifically designated as the species kalkora.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Albizia kalkora contains diverse phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These bioactive compounds are primarily concentrated within the bark, leaves, and roots of the plant. The presence of specific flavonoids provides significant antioxidant activity, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. Additionally, the tannins found in the plant contribute to its astringent and antimicrobial qualities. These chemical constituents collectively support the plant's traditional uses in treating inflammation and skin ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Albizia kalkora?

Albizia kalkora has potent anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties that are utilized to treat various skin ailments and systemic infections. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as tannins and flavonoids which help reduce swelling and soothe irritated tissues. Its antimicrobial action allows it to combat harmful pathogens, making it effective in managing wounds and topical infections. Additionally, the antioxidant content assists in neutralizing free radicals, which promotes cellular repair and overall tissue health. These combined therapeutic mechanisms make it a significant resource in traditional medicinal practices for healing.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and accelerate the healing of minor cuts. When prepared as a decoction, it is often consumed to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and digestive discomfort. Its medicinal properties are also leveraged to reduce swelling and manage pain associated with systemic inflammation. Local practitioners rely on its phytochemical components to address these diverse ailments within traditional healing systems.
