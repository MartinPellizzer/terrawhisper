Albizia kalkora

What's the taxonomical classification of Albizia kalkora?

Albizia kalkora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression through the order Fabales, it falls under the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its diverse range of legumes. Finally, it is identified by the genus Albizia and is specifically designated as the species kalkora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Albizia kalkora has a large deciduous tree structure characterized by a spreading crown and bipinnate leaves that typically measure 30 to 60 centimeters in length.

The foliage consists of numerous small, oblong leaflets arranged symmetrically along secondary axes to maximize light absorption. Its bark is generally greyish and becomes fissured or rough as the specimen matures. During the flowering season, the tree produces small, fragrant, cream-colored flowers that appear in dense clusters.

These blossoms are followed by flattened, brown seed pods that contain several small seeds essential for reproduction.
