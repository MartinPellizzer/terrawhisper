Buxus bodinieri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buxus bodinieri has small, opposite, elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture and typically measure between 1 and 2 centimeters in length. The foliage remains evergreen, providing a dense and compact appearance to the shrubby growth habit. Its stems are woody and branch extensively to form a rounded or spreading form. The plant produces inconspicuous, yellowish-green flowers that are borne in small clusters within the leaf axils. Small, globose fruits develop from these flowers and eventually transition to a dark color upon maturity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Hengduan Mountains in southwestern China, specifically within provinces such as Yunnan, Sichuan, and Tibet. It typically thrives in montane forests and shrublands situated at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 3,800 meters. Its distribution is restricted to these specific mountain corridors where cool, moist environments are prevalent. These localized populations exist within narrow ecological niches defined by rugged terrain and temperate climates.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Buxus bodinieri can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurotoxicity if ingested due to the presence of alkaloids. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may lead to contact dermatitis or localized rashes in sensitive individuals. If consumed, the alkaloids can trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping. In more severe cases of ingestion, the compounds may affect the central nervous system. Always exercise caution when handling this species to avoid accidental exposure.
