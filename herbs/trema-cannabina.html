What's the taxonomical classification of Trema cannabina?

Trema cannabina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Rosales. The plant is a member of the family Cannabaceae and falls under the genus Trema, ultimately identified by its specific species name, cannabina.

Trema cannabinna has simple, alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively rugose texture that resembles the foliage of the cannabis plant.

Its flowers are small and typically appear in clusters, often displaying a pale coloration. The fruit is characterized by a small, somewhat fleshy capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Because the pharmacological profile is not fully documented, caution is necessary to avoid unpredictable systemic reactions.
