Dichapetalum longipetalum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichapetalum longipetalum has woody, evergreen shrubs reaching up to five meters in height, characterized by opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically dark green and shiny on the upper surface, while the underside remains a lighter shade. Its flowers are actinomorphic and borne in terminal clusters, featuring five distinct, spreading petals that are often pale pink or white in color. Numerous long stamens extend beyond the corolla, giving the blooms a delicate, brush-like appearance. The plant also produces small, globose woody capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds for dispersal.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and gastrointestinal distress if it comes into contact with or is ingested by a person. Direct contact with the foliage or sap may lead to localized redness or an itchy rash on the skin. If the plant is consumed, it often triggers significant gastrointestinal distress such as stomach pain or cramping. Such ingestion is frequently accompanied by bouts of nausea and vomiting. Some individuals may also experience bouts of dizziness following exposure to its chemical compounds.
