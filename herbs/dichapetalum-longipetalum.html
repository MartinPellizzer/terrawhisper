Dichapetalum longipetalum

What's the taxonomical classification of Dichapetalum longipetalum?

Dichapetalum longipetalum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Malpighiales. This plant is uniquely positioned within the family Dichapetalaceae, falling under the genus Dichapetalum, and is specifically identified by its species name, longipetalum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichapetalum longipetalum has woody, evergreen shrubs reaching up to five meters in height, characterized by opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins and acute tips. Its flowers are actinomorphic and borne in terminal clusters, featuring five distinct, spreading petals that are often pale pink or white in color. Numerous long stamens extend beyond the corolla, giving the blooms a delicate, brush-like appearance.
