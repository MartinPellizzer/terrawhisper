Acer ceriferum

What's the taxonomical classification of Acer ceriferum?

Acer ceriferum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, it falls under the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Acer, and its specific species designation, ceriferum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer ceriferum has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate or broadly elliptical in shape with serrated margins and distinct pinnate venation. The leaf blades often exhibit a glossy texture and can range from deep green to lighter shades depending on the age of the foliage. Its stems are woody and characterized by a smooth to slightly fissured bark texture that matures as the plant grows. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into winged samaras used for wind dispersal. These seeds are arranged in pairs and feature long, slender wings extending from the central nutlet.
