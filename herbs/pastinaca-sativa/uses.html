Parsnip (Pastinaca sativa) Uses

Parsnip is used to treat respiratory infections, including cough, tonsillitis, bronchitis, and tuberculosis. It also addresses gastrointestinal issues like inflammation, gas, and constipation, helping with gastric discomfort and overall digestive health.

Parsnip (Pastinaca sativa) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Parsnip has been traditionally used to support respiratory health due to its high vitamin C content, which boosts immune function. It may help alleviate symptoms of colds and mild respiratory infections by reducing inflammation and promoting mucus clearance. Its antiseptic properties also support healing in the respiratory tract.

2. Cough

Parsnip has been traditionally used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus in the respiratory tract, making it easier to expel. Rich in vitamins and minerals, it supports immune function and may reduce inflammation in the airways. Its mild, sweet flavor also makes it a gentle remedy for persistent or dry coughs.

3. Tonsillitis

Parsnip has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of tonsillitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It can help reduce throat irritation and swelling, often consumed as a tea or added to throat lozenges. Its high vitamin C content also supports immune function during infection.

4. Bronchitis

Parsnip has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating bronchitis. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, while its anti-inflammatory effects may reduce bronchial irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease coughing and clear congestion. Parsnip's mild nature makes it a gentle option for supporting bronchial function.

5. Tuberculosis

Parsnip has been historically used in traditional herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It is believed to help alleviate symptoms of tuberculosis by reducing inflammation and promoting mucus clearance. Its expectorant properties may assist in clearing the lungs, though modern scientific evidence for these uses is limited.

6. Gastrointestinal inflammation

Parsnip has been traditionally used to support gastrointestinal health due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, easing conditions like gastritis and irritable bowel syndrome. Its high fiber content also promotes healthy digestion and regular bowel movements.

7. Gastric discomfort

Parsnip has been traditionally used to alleviate gastric discomfort due to its mild carminative properties. It helps reduce bloating and gas by promoting digestive ease. Its gentle nature makes it suitable for mild digestive issues without causing irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or cooked vegetable to support stomach health.

8. Gastrointestinal gas

Parsnip has been traditionally used to alleviate gastrointestinal gas due to its mild carminative properties. It helps reduce bloating and discomfort by promoting the release of trapped air in the digestive tract. Its gentle action makes it suitable for regular use in herbal remedies for digestive wellness.

9. Gastrointestinal issues

Parsnip has been traditionally used to support digestive health. It contains compounds that may help soothe gastrointestinal discomfort and promote healthy digestion. Its mild laxative properties can aid in relieving constipation, while its high fiber content supports regular bowel movements.

10. Constipation

Parsnip contains mucilage, which can help soften stool and ease bowel movement. It is traditionally used to relieve constipation due to its mild laxative properties. Consuming cooked or raw parsnips may support digestive health and promote regularity.
