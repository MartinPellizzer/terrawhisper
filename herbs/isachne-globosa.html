What's the taxonomical classification of Isachne globosa?

Isachne globosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Poales, it is placed into the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Isachne, and its unique species designation, globosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Isachne globosa has a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are globose in shape and characterized by a dense covering of fine, silvery hairs.

These leaves are arranged in a compact circular pattern around a central growth point. The plant develops small, tubular flowers that emerge from the center of the leafy cluster. These blossoms are typically pale or greenish in color and possess a delicate structure.

The overall morphology is adapted for moisture retention within its specific ecological niche.
