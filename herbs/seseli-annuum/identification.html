Botanical Identification of Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum)

To identify Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Seseli annuum belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Apiaceae, genus Seseli, and species Seseli annuum. This classification places it within the carrot family, known for its aromatic herbs and medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Annual Seseli is Seseli annuum. It was first described by the botanist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck in 1786. This species has several synonyms, including Seseli annua and Seseli annuum L.. It is also known by common names such as annual seseli and field seseli, with regional variations in naming across different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is an annual herb with a taproot system. It has erect, branching stems that are hairy and typically grow up to 30 cm tall. The leaves are alternate, pinnate, and have a fern-like appearance. The inflorescence is a compound umbel, with small white to pale purple flowers. The fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Annual Seseli can be distinguished by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. It has a smooth, branched stem and produces numerous tiny seeds in a fluffy cluster. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Seseli integrifolium, which has broader leaves and different flower clusters.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It thrives in mild climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at low to mid-altitude regions, typically between 200 and 800 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Annual Seseli blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested in early to mid-summer when the flowers are in full bloom and the leaves are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Annual Seseli (Seseli annuum) is closely related to other members of the genus Seseli. It shares key characteristics with species like Seseli integrifolium and Seseli heterophyllum. Within the family Apiaceae, it exhibits typical traits such as compound umbels and aromatic foliage, which help in its identification. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other umbelliferous plants.
