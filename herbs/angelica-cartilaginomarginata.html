Angelica cartilaginomarginata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angelica cartilaginomarginata has large, compound leaves characterized by deeply lobed leaflets with distinctively thickened, cartilage-like margins. These leaves are arranged oppositely along a sturdy, hollow stem that often reaches significant heights. The plant produces flat-topped clusters of small, white flowers known as umbels that emerge from the upper leaf axils. The root system is typically robust and fleshy, providing stability for its upright growth habit. Small, glandular hairs may be present on various parts of the foliage and stems, adding to its complex surface texture.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Angelica cartilaginomarginata can cause phototoxicity, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic skin reactions when consumed or applied topically. The plant contains furanocoumarins, which are compounds known to increase skin sensitivity to ultraviolet light and lead to painful blistering. Individuals using this species may experience nausea, abdominal cramping, or diarrhea if ingested in improper quantities. Some users might also develop contact dermatitis or localized redness upon touching the foliage. Due to these risks, careful monitoring is necessary for anyone exploring its medicinal properties.
