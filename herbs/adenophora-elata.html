Adenophora elaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Asterales and falls within the family Campanulaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Adenophora and the unique species epithet elata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora elaata has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by stem heights typically ranging from 20 to 60 centimeters and leaves that are opposite or alternate with ovate to lanceolate shapes and serrated margins. The stems are generally smooth and upright, supporting clusters of bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the upper leaf axils. These inflorescences feature tubular corollas that vary in color from pale purple to deep violet. The plant produces a fibrous root system that facilitates its growth in montane or subalpine environments. Small, ovoid capsules develop following pollination to facilitate seed dispersal.
