Raspberry

Rubus idaeus

Raspberry (Rubus idaeus) is a flowering plant known for its red, edible berries and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Raspberry (Rubus idaeus) is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is also known as red raspberry, common raspberry, or garden raspberry. A perennial shrub native to Europe, it has woody stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits. Synonyms include Rubus strigosus and Rubus idaeus var. strigosus in historical herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rosales
  • Family: Rosaceae
  • Genus: Rubus
  • Species: Rubus idaeus

Safety Profile

Raspberry (Rubus idaeus) is generally safe when consumed in normal food amounts. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential hormonal effects. Large doses may cause gastrointestinal upset, and individuals with allergies to plants in the Rosaceae family should avoid it.
