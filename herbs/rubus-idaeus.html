Medicinal Parts

Rubus idaeus fruit, commonly known as the red raspberry, is widely recognized for its nutritional and medicinal properties.

The fruit is rich in vitamins, particularly vitamin C, and contains high levels of dietary fiber, antioxidants, and essential minerals such as manganese and potassium. These components contribute to its reputation as a natural remedy for boosting immunity, improving digestion, and supporting heart health. In traditional medicine, raspberry fruit has been used to alleviate symptoms of inflammation, reduce oxidative stress, and promote urinary tract health.

Additionally, the fruit's high content of ellagitannins and other polyphenols has been linked to potential anti-cancer and anti-inflammatory effects, making it a valuable component in modern herbal supplements and functional foods.
