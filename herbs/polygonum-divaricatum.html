Polygonum divaricatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum divaricatum?

Polygonum divaricatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Caryophyllales. The plant is further categorized into the family Polygonaceae, falling under the genus Polygonum, and is specifically identified as the species divaricatum.

What are the herbal preparations of Polygonum divaricatum?

Polygonum divaricatum is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical wash derived from its dried aerial parts. Some traditional practices also involve grinding the plant material into a fine powder to be mixed with oils or fats for ointment production. These various preparations allow the medicinal properties of the plant to be utilized for both internal and external health needs.
