Common onion

Allium cepa

Common Onion (Allium cepa) is a widely cultivated herb known for its pungent odor and therapeutic properties, commonly used to support respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of congestion and colds.

Botanical Identification

Common Onion (Allium cepa) is a biennial plant in the Amaryllidaceae family. Native to Central Asia, it is widely cultivated globally. It has a bulbous stem with cylindrical leaves and purple flowers. Common synonyms include garden onion and bulb onion, often used interchangeably in herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Asparagales
  • Family: Amaryllidaceae
  • Genus: Allium
  • Species: Allium cepa

Active Compounds

The Common Onion contains essential oil, flavonoids, and saponins as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to exert their effects, with essential oil providing antimicrobial properties and flavonoids offering antioxidant benefits. Saponins contribute to its anti-inflammatory and digestive support properties.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Saponin

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for respiratory infections and inflammation due to its antimicrobial properties.

Capsule: Used for general health support and immune system boosting.

Tincture: Used for digestive issues and as an antiseptic due to its concentrated active compounds.

Safety Profile

Common onion is generally safe when consumed in normal food amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal irritation in some individuals. Large quantities may lead to allergic reactions or respiratory issues in sensitive people.
