Common Onion (Allium cepa)

Common Onion (Allium cepa) is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its bulbs, leaves, and flowers have been used for culinary uses, decoctions, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, expectorant, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface gray-green and lower surface white, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of a globular cluster of small, white, six-petaled flowers with a spherical, membranous envelope, arranged in a umbel-like structure, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry.
Stems
Cylindrical, solid, and typically terete in cross-section, the stems are usually glabrous, herbaceous, and exhibit a solid, non-lignified structure with a tendency to bend and curve, often forming a bulbous base.
