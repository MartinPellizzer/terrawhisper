Allium Cepa Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Allium cepa, commonly known as the red onion, is a flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family, widely used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties.

It is known for its pungent aroma and is rich in bioactive compounds such as sulfur-containing compounds, flavonoids, and quercetin, which contribute to its health benefits.

The herb is believed to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and boost the immune system.

It can be prepared as an infusion, tincture, or poultice to harness its medicinal properties.

Bioactive Constituents

Allium cepa sulfenic acid is a volatile sulfur compound that is produced when the enzyme alliinase catalyzes the breakdown of alliin, resulting in the characteristic pungent odor of garlic and onions.

Allium cepa alliin is a sulfur-containing amino acid that serves as a precursor to various bioactive compounds, including allicin, and is stored in the cells of the plant until it is released through mechanical damage.

Allium cepa allicin is a potent organic sulfur compound known for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and cardiovascular benefits, and it is formed when alliin reacts with enzymes in the presence of water.

Allium cepa quercetin is a flavonoid antioxidant that exhibits anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and anticancer properties, contributing to the overall health benefits associated with consuming Allium cepa.

Medicinal Parts

Allium cepa seed, although less commonly used than the bulb, contains compounds that may offer some medicinal properties.

The seed is typically harvested from the mature fruit of the plant, which develops after the bulb has been harvested.

Herbal preparations from the seed often involve drying and grinding into a powder or using it in tinctures.

However, the primary medicinal use of Allium cepa is associated with its bulb, and the seed, leaf, and fruit are not as extensively studied or utilized in traditional herbal medicine.

Below you find a list of the 3 best medicinal parts of Allium cepa.

  3. Fruit: The fruit, or bulb, of Allium cepa is widely used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, and is commonly used to treat ailments like coughs, congestion, and digestive issues.
