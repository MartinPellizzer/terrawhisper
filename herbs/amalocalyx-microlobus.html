Amalocalyx microlobus

What's the taxonomical classification of Amalocalyx microlobus?

Amalocalyx microlobus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Gentianales and the family Apocynaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its membership in the genus Amalocalyx, with the distinct species designation being microlobus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Amalocalyx microlobus contains various secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These bioactive compounds are primarily concentrated in the leaves and bark of the shrub. Research suggests that the presence of these phytochemicals provides significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Such chemical constituents are often utilized in traditional medicine to treat inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. The specific synergy between these organic molecules drives the plant's biological activity in medicinal applications.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including intestinal infections, wounds, skin inflammation, and stomach pains. Its medicinal properties are primarily utilized to manage digestive issues such as diarrhea and abdominal discomfort. Local communities also apply its extracts to help heal external sores and skin irritations. The plant's bioactive components are thought to provide relief from common inflammatory conditions. This traditional use highlights its importance in folk medicine for managing localized infections and pain.
