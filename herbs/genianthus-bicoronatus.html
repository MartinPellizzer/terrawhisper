Genianthus bicoronatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Genianthus bicoronatus has opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins and prominent venation arranged on upright, herbaceous stems. The inflorescence is characterized by terminal clusters of tubular, bilabiate flowers that often exhibit a distinct two-crowned or bicolored appearance. These flowers typically feature a pale base transitioning into deeper purple or violet hues on the upper lip. The calyx is persistent and deeply lobed, providing structural support to the delicate corolla during anthesis. Small, inconspicuous bracts often accompany the flowering spikes, adding complexity to the overall floral architecture.
