Ilex macrocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex macrocarpa has small, leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically elliptical to obovate in shape with finely serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a dark green upper surface and a paler underside, often appearing somewhat glossy. Its woody stems develop a rough bark that provides protection for the slow-growing shrub or small tree. During the fruiting season, the plant produces distinctively large, dark berries that are much bigger than those of many other holly species. These fleshy fruits are held on sturdy pedicels and serve as a primary morphological feature of the species.
