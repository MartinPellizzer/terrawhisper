Jasminum elongatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum elongatum has slender, woody stems that form a climbing or trailing habit with lanceolate to elliptical leaves arranged in opposite pairs. The foliage typically features smooth margins and a glossy texture that provides a deep green appearance. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the axils, often presenting in small clusters or singly. These blossoms consist of tubular corollas that open into five spreading lobes. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by its elongated internodes and delicate, sprawling branches.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically found in countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, and parts of Malaysia. It thrives primarily in the moist, shaded understories of evergreen forests and seasonal monsoon forests. Within these habitats, the species is often distributed along riverbanks and hilly terrain where humidity remains consistently high. Localized populations may also appear in disturbed forest edges where sunlight can penetrate the canopy. This specific distribution pattern ensures the climbing vine receives the necessary moisture and indirect light for its growth.

      What are the herbal preparations of Jasminum elongatum?

      Jasminum elongatum is prepared as an infusion, decoction, or tincture to extract its bioactive aromatic compounds for medicinal or cosmetic use. The dried flowers are most commonly steeped in hot water to create a fragrant tea that serves as a soothing beverage. For topical applications, the plant material is often infused into carrier oils to produce scented body oils or salves.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Jasminum elongatum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While the plant is primarily known for its fragrance, some individuals may experience redness or itching upon direct skin contact with the leaves or stems. If parts of the plant are swallowed, it may lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps. Sensitivity to the essential oils found within the foliage can also trigger respiratory irritation in highly sensitive people. Always exercise caution and consult a professional before handling or consuming any part of this species.
