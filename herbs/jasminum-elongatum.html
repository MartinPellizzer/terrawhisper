What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum elongatum?

Jasminum elongatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Lamiales and the family Oleaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Jasminum, and its specific species epithet, elongatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum elongatum has slender, woody stems that form a climbing or trailing habit with lanceolate to elliptical leaves arranged in opposite pairs. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the axils, often presenting in small clusters or singly. These blossoms consist of tubular corollas that open into five spreading lobes.
