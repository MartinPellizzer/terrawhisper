Millingtonia hortensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Millingtonia hortensis has large, bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oblong leaflets that grow in an opposite arrangement along the stems. The tree typically features a smooth, greyish bark and produces striking, fragrant white flowers that emerge in large, terminal clusters. These blossoms consist of several spreading petals and prominent stamens that attract various pollinators. The plant also develops woody, elongated seed pods that eventually split to release small, winged seeds for wind dispersal. These structural characteristics reflect its adaptation to tropical forest environments.

    What are the herbal preparations of Millingtonia hortensis?

    Millingtonia hortensis is prepared as decoctions, infusions, and poultices derived from its bark, leaves, and flowers. Infusions made from the flowers are often consumed to address specific internal ailments.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Millingtonia hortensis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if its parts are ingested or touched without protection. The plant contains various phytochemicals that may trigger hypersensitivity reactions in individuals with sensitive skin. Contact with the sap or leaves can lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling upon exposure. If consumed accidentally, the chemical constituents might provoke nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. It is essential to handle the specimen with care to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
