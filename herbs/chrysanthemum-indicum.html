Chrysanthemum (Chrysanthemum indicum)

Chrysanthemum (Chrysanthemum indicum) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to East Asia, China, and Southeast Asia. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, sedative, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Annual herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glabrous and dull green, lower surface pale green with prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescence composed of a flat-topped cluster of ray florets with white to yellow disc florets, actinomorphic, with 8 to 14 ray florets arranged in a single plane, disc florets tubular, with 5 lobes, and 5 sepals.
Stems
Erect, branched, with opposite leaves, hairy surface, and a woody base.
