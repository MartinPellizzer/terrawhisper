Chrysanthemum

Chrysanthemum indicum

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Chrysanthemum indicum, commonly known as the Chinese chrysanthemum, belongs to the family Asteraceae. It is a perennial herb native to Asia, characterized by its daisy-like flowers and serrated leaves. The plant is also referred to by various regional names and has been widely used in traditional herbal medicine.

Active Compounds

The Chrysanthemum indicum contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Tannin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Common cold, bronchitis, allergic rhinitis Antimicrobial, expectorant, anti-inflammatory, antiviral
Integumentary Skin infections, eczema, fungal infections, wound healing, skin inflammation Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antifungal, antioxidant
Digestive Gastrointestinal inflammation, gastrointestinal infections, gastroenteritis Anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory issues and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a calming tea to reduce stress.

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and wounds due to its antiseptic and healing properties.

Safety Profile

Chrysanthemum indicum is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by those with allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family.
