Chrysanthemum indicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysanthemum indicum?

Chrysanthemum indicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the prominent family Asteraceae, falling specifically under the genus Chrysanthemum, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, indicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysanthemum indicum has an erect or ascending perennial herbaceous habit characterized by branched stems that reach heights of 30 to 80 centimeters and leaves that are oppositely arranged, deeply lobed, and serrated. The foliage typically features long petioles and distinctively shaped blades that vary from ovate to lanceolate. Its inflorescences consist of small, yellowish-white flower heads that grow in solitary or clustered arrangements at the ends of the stems. These composite flowers are composed of numerous tiny disc florets surrounded by a ring of ray florets. The plant also develops a fibrous root system that supports its growth in various temperate environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically distributed across China, Japan, Korea, and Taiwan. It thrives in a variety of habitats ranging from temperate grasslands to mountain slopes and roadside ditches. While its primary range is concentrated in these Asian countries, it has been introduced to other regions due to its medicinal and ornamental value. Some populations have established themselves in parts of Southeast Asia where the climate remains suitable for its growth. The species is often found in disturbed soils where sunlight is abundant.

What are the pharmacological activities of Chrysanthemum indicum?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, hepatoprotective, anti-cancer, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities that stem from its rich profile of bioactive compounds. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress within the body. The plant also demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory effects by modulating immune responses to mitigate swelling and pain. Furthermore, its hepatoprotective qualities assist in protecting liver cells from chemical damage and toxins. Research also indicates that its anti-cancer potential may inhibit the growth of various tumor cells, while its antimicrobial actions provide defense against diverse bacterial and fungal pathogens.

As discussed in a study, Chrysanthemum indicum L. exhibits antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial activities. The antioxidant activities, measured by total reducing capacity, DPPH, ABTS, and ·OH IC50, and anti-inflammatory activities, measured by XOD and LOX IC50, strengthened with drying, with 80%WC showing higher values than DS. Regarding antibacterial activity, extracts inhibited Staphylococcus aureus with a MIC of 1.560-3.125 mg/mL and Escherichia coli with a MIC of 3.125 mg/mL. Additionally, the DS group showed a larger inhibition zone for Listeria monocytogenes. The study examined eight chemical components, including chloroogenic acid, 3,5-O-dicaffeoylquinic acid, galuteolin, linarin, luteolin, naringenin, kaempferol, and isorharnetin, across samples with water contents of 80%WC, 60%WC, 40%WC, 20%WC, and DS.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds flavonoids, kaempferol, luteolin, quercetin, and salicylic acid. These specific flavonoids act as potent antioxidants that help neutralize harmful free radicals within the body. Within this group, kaempferol, luteolin, and quercetin provide additional anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective benefits. The presence of salicylic acid further enhances the plant's therapeutic profile by offering natural analgesic and antipyretic properties. Together, these diverse chemical constituents contribute to a wide range of pharmacological activities.

As discussed in a study, flavonoids are the most active and abundant components found in Chrysanthemum indicum tea. Researchers identified two novel hypoglycemic compounds within the total flavonoids of snow chrysanthemum, specifically sulfuretin and leptosiidin. Treatment with 12.5 μmol/L of sulfuretin stimulated cellular glucose consumption and effectively upregulated PPARγ expression with a potency comparable to a renowned agonist at 20 μmol/L. In insulin-resistant HepG2 cells, sulfuretin concentrations of 3.125, 6.25, and 12.5 μmol/L significantly mitigated glucose uptake damage. Additionally, sulfuretin interacted with the ligand-binding pocket of PPARγ through three hydrogen bond interactions with residues LYS-367, GLN-286, and TYR-477. These findings were validated through experiments involving ob/ob or high-fat diet induced mice treated for 14 weeks.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating non-alcoholic fatty liver disease, age-related macular degeneration, inflammation, liver damage, and various skin diseases. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce systemic inflammation throughout the body. Researchers have studied its ability to mitigate liver damage and improve metabolic markers associated with non-alcoholic fatty liver disease. Additionally, certain extracts may provide protective effects for the eyes to combat age-related macular degeneration. Topical applications are also frequently utilized to soothe and heal various skin diseases.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic reactions, skin irritation, nausea, digestive upset, and dizziness in some individuals. People sensitive to the Asteraceae family may experience itchy skin or rashes upon direct contact with the plant. Ingesting the herb can lead to gastrointestinal issues such as nausea and general digestive upset. Some users have also reported feeling lightheaded or experiencing dizziness after consumption. It is important to monitor how your body reacts to this species to avoid these adverse effects.
