Syringa dilatata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syringa dilatata has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that typically measure 5 to 12 centimeters in length and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a deep green color that often turns reddish in autumn. Its inflorescences consist of large, terminal panicles containing numerous small, tubular flowers that can range in color from white to pale lilac. The stems are woody and develop a greyish bark as the shrub matures into a larger spreading form. This plant typically exhibits a multi-stemmed growth habit that creates a dense, rounded shrubbery.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and temperate regions of eastern China, specifically spanning across provinces such as Zhejiang, Anhui, Jiangsu, and Fujian. It thrives primarily in hilly areas and low-altitude mountain slopes where the climate provides sufficient moisture. Within these habitats, it is frequently found growing in forest margins and along the edges of thickets. The species favors environments that offer a balance of sunlight and protection from extreme seasonal shifts. Its distribution remains concentrated within these specific East Asian ecological zones.

      Syringa dilatata is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a tea from the leaves, or a topical poultice for skin ailments.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Syringa dilatata can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested in large quantities. While most people tolerate the plant well, contact with its sap may trigger allergic reactions or localized dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If the plant is consumed, it may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort due to its chemical constituents. There is limited clinical data regarding long-term systemic effects, so caution is advised when handling or using the plant. Always consult a medical professional if you suspect accidental poisoning or a severe allergic response.
