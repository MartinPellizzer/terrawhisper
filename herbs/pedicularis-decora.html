Pedicularis decora

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis decora?

Pedicularis decora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Lamiales and into the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Pedicularis, with its specific designation being the species decora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis decora has an erect stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and features leaves that are primarily basal and deeply pinnatifid. The foliage consists of narrow, lanceolate lobes that create a complex, dissected appearance along the plant's base. Its inflorescence is a dense terminal spike containing many tubular, two-lipped flowers that exhibit varying shades of pink, purple, or reddish hues. The upper lip of the corolla is often hooded, while the lower lip serves as a landing platform for pollinators. Small, bract-like structures subtend each individual flower within the colorful floral cluster.

What are the pharmacological activities of Pedicularis decora?

This plant has significant anti-oxidation, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities driven by its unique phytochemical profile. The presence of various phenolic compounds allows it to effectively neutralize free radicals within biological systems. These antioxidant properties help protect cells from oxidative stress and subsequent damage to DNA. Furthermore, the plant exhibits potential in reducing inflammation and inhibiting the growth of certain pathogenic microorganisms. Such diverse biological effects suggest that Pedicularis decora could serve as a valuable source for developing new therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", Pedicularis decora extract can inhibit oxidation lesion in mice. When mice were administered 15 g.kg-1.d-1 of total extract, BuOH soluble components, or water soluble components after being induced with 180 mg.kg-1 i.p. alloxan, the extracts showed protective effects. All three extracts of Pedicularis decora obviously lowered the abnormal contents of SOD, GSH-Px, LPO, ALT, AST, AKP, MAO.B, and r-globulin. Furthermore, the extracts obviously improved the abnormal decrease of the liver index and spleen index. The effects of the total extract and BuOH soluble components were better than the water soluble components.
