Circaea alpina

What's the taxonomical classification of Circaea alpina?

Circaea alpina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Myrtales. The plant is a member of the family Onagraceae, which contains the genus Circaea, and is specifically identified by the species name alpinas.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Circaea alpina has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and slender, upright stems that terminate in small, four-petaled white flowers.

The leaves possess distinct venation and are typically arranged in pairs along the stem. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, elongated raceme containing numerous tiny blossoms. Each flower features four delicate white petals and several stamens that protrude slightly.

The plant eventually produces small, hooked fruits designed to cling to passing animals for seed dispersal.
