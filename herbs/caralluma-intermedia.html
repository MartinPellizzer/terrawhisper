Coral Spine (Caralluma Intermedia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Caralluma intermedia.

Coral spine, scientifically known as Caralluma intermedia, is a small, spiny shrub native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan, traditionally used as a medicinal herb and adaptogen. It is valued for its ability to enhance energy, reduce appetite, and support weight management, making it a popular choice for those seeking natural alternatives to stimulants. In traditional Ayurvedic and tribal medicine, Coral spine has been used for centuries to treat fatigue, digestive issues, and even as a remedy for diabetes. Modern wellness applications include its use in weight loss supplements and as a natural energy booster, supported by research on its active compounds like pregnenolone and alkaloids. Its unique appearance, with its distinctive spiny leaves and rare presence in traditional medicine, makes it a fascinating plant with both cultural significance and potential health benefits.
