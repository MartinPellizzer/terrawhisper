Lavender bush

Lavandula latifolia

Lavender Bush (Lavandula latifolia) is a fragrant shrub native to the Mediterranean region, known for its narrow leaves and spikes of purple flowers, and it has been traditionally used for its calming and therapeutic properties, particularly for easing symptoms of anxiety and insomnia.

Botanical Identification

Lavandula latifolia is a species of the Lamiaceae family, commonly known as the lavender bush. It is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. The plant has narrow, grey-green leaves and purple flowers, with a woody root system. It is also referred to as broad-leaved lavender in herbal literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Lavandula
  • Species: Lavandula latifolia

Active Compounds

The Lavender Bush contains essential oils, terpenoids, and flavonoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory and calming effects. Flavonoids may enhance the antioxidant properties of terpenoids, contributing to its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid

Lavender bush Uses.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and anxiety due to its calming and antispasmodic properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and skin conditions due to its antiseptic and astringent properties.

Essential oil: Used for stress relief, aromatherapy, and skin conditions due to its calming and antiseptic properties.

Safety Profile

Lavender bush is generally safe when used as directed. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks. Individuals with allergies to the Lamiaceae family should exercise caution and consult a healthcare provider before use.
