Alnus nepalensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Alnus nepalensis?

Alnus nepalensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Fagales. Within this order, it is placed in the family Betulaceae, which is further refined into the genus Alnus. Finally, its specific biological identity is established at the species level as nepalensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Alnus nepellenis contains bioactive secondary metabolites including tannins, flavonoids, saponins, and various phenolic compounds that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's demonstrated antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers have identified specific alkaloids and terpenoids within the bark and leaves that may assist in regulating physiological processes. The presence of these compounds suggests that the plant could be utilized in traditional medicine for treating skin irritations or digestive issues. Ongoing pharmacological studies aim to isolate these specific molecules to better understand their precise medicinal mechanisms.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alnus nepalensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin ailments, and digestive issues through its medicinal properties. The bark and leaves contain tannins and other bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and soothe irritated tissues. It is frequently applied topically to address skin infections, wounds, and minor abrasions. Some traditional practices also involve using preparations of the plant to alleviate gastrointestinal distress. These therapeutic applications rely on the plant's natural astringent and antimicrobial characteristics.
