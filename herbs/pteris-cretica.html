Pteris cretica

What's the taxonomical classification of Pteris cretica?

Pteris cretica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. The plant is further categorized into the family Pteridaceae, falling under the genus Pteris, and is specifically identified by its species name, cretica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris cretica has a creeping rhizome that is dark brown and scaly, supporting pinnate fronds that can reach up to 60 centimeters in length. The pinnae are typically oblong or lanceolate with slightly wavy or dentate margins. Each leaf structure consists of a central rachis from which smaller leaflets emerge in an organized, alternating pattern. The underside of the foliage often displays characteristic soral clusters protected by specialized indusia. These morphological features allow the fern to thrive in rocky or shaded environments.
