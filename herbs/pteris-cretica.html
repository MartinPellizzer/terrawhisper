Pteris cretica

What's the taxonomical classification of Pteris cretica?

Pteris cretica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. The plant is further categorized into the family Pteridaceae, falling under the genus Pteris, and is specifically identified by its species name, cretica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pteris cretica contains various secondary metabolites including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and various terpenoids that contribute to its biological activities. These bioactive compounds often exhibit antioxidant properties by scavenging free radicals within cellular environments. Some studies suggest that the plant extracts possess anti-inflammatory potential due to the presence of specific glycosides. Additionally, the chemical constituents may provide antimicrobial effects against certain bacterial strains. Researchers continue to investigate these specific phytochemical profiles to understand their therapeutic applications in pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pteris cretica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as coughs and asthma, digestive issues like stomach aches, and skin conditions including wounds or inflammation. Its medicinal properties are often utilized to manage fever and reduce pain in various parts of the body. Traditional practitioners frequently prepare decoctions or poultices from the leaves to address these diverse symptoms. The plant also shows potential in managing minor infections through its bioactive compounds. These applications highlight its significance in folk medicine across several Mediterranean regions.
