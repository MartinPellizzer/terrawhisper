What is Hemionitis affinis?

Hemionitis affinis is a species of fern in the family Pteridaceae.

It is a rhizomatous perennial with a stoloniferous habit, featuring a creeping rhizome that produces new plants at the nodes. The fronds are dimorphic, with sterile fronds being smaller and less divided than fertile fronds, which have lanceolate pinnate pinnae and a terminal sorus on the underside of the rachis. The frond scales are brown, lanceolate, and have a pointed apex.

Hemionitis affinis is native to tropical Asia, where it grows in shaded, humid environments.
