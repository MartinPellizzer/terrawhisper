Dioscorea colocasiifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea colocasiifolia?

Dioscorea colocasiifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Dioscoreales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Dioscoreaceae, placing it in the genus Dioscorea, where it is specifically identified by the species name colocasiafolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dioscorea colocasiifolia has climbing, herbaceous stems that emerge from thick, tuberous rhizomes and produce cordate or sagittate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a distinct venation pattern and a somewhat leathery texture typical of many climbing yam species. As a vine, it utilizes twining stems to ascend through surrounding vegetation in its natural habitat. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that are borne on specialized inflorescences. These morphological traits allow the plant to effectively colonize shaded forest environments.
