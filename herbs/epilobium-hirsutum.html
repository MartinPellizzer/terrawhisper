Epilobium hirsutum

What's the taxonomical classification of Epilobium hirsutum?

Epilobium hirsutum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Myrtales and is a member of the family Onagraceae. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Epilobium, and its specific identification is defined by the species hirsutum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Epiloium hirsutum has a basal rosette of hairy, oval leaves and an upright, branched stem covered in dense, glandular hairs. The leaves are typically oblong or lanceolate with slightly serrated margins and a textured, pubescent surface. Its inflorescence consists of small, four-petaled pink or purple flowers that emerge from the upper leaf axils. The plant produces elongated, capsule-like seed pods that split open to release numerous tiny seeds. This hairy texture is a defining morphological characteristic that distinguishes it from similar species in the evening primrose family.

Within these territories, it is frequently found in temperate climates where consistent moisture is available in the soil. What are the pharmacological activities of Epilobium hirsutum?

This plant has demonstrated diverse pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, anti-tumour, antioxidant, acceleration of apoptosis, and the treatment of benign prostate hyperplasia. Its antioxidant properties help mitigate oxidative stress by neutralizing free radicals within the body. The plant's anti-inflammatory effects work to reduce swelling and immune responses in various tissues. Furthermore, it exhibits anti-tumour capabilities by facilitating the acceleration of apoptosis in malignant cell lines. Research also indicates its potential effectiveness in the clinical treatment of benign prostate hyperplasia.

As discussed in a study, extracts of Epilobium hirsutum exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities. Phytochemical analysis shows that oenothein B quantities in the extracts vary between 20% and 35%, while the flavonoids content does not exceed 2%. These extracts inhibited the activity of hyaluronidase and lipoxygenase with IC50 around 5 ug/ml and 25 ug/ml, respectively. The extracts also inhibited myeloperoxidase release from stimulated neutrophils and significantly reduced the production of reactive oxygen species from f-MLP and PMA induced neutrophils with IC50 of 5 ug/ml and 25 ug/ml. The high concentration of oenothein B, which always exceeded 2% in raw material, explains the activity of the extract obtained from Epilobium.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains chlorogenic acid, ellagic acid, flavonoids, gallic acid, and tannins, which collectively provide its therapeutic properties. The presence of chlorogenic acid and gallic acid contributes to significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Ellagic acid works alongside these compounds to help protect cells from oxidative damage. Additionally, the plant's rich supply of flavonoids offers neuroprotective and cardioprotective benefits. Finally, the tannins present in the plant contribute to its astringent qualities and potential antimicrobial effects.

As discussed in a study, Epilobium hirsutum contains phenolic acids, flavonoids, and ellagitannins, including oenothein A and B. Researchers quantified 11 pharmacologically active marker compounds in 78 samples collected every 2 weeks during 2023. Chloroenic acid reached a maximum accumulation in leaves during the vegetative phase of up to 2.25 mg/g DW. The major ellagitannin oenothein B showed its highest concentrations in wet grassland leaves during flowering at 73.97 mg/g DW. These medicinal compounds were studied across various habitats and plant parts from April to October.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as an infusion, a tincture, or a decoction to extract its various therapeutic properties. A decoction is used when simmering the tougher roots or stems in boiling water to release deeper compounds. These different methods allow for varied applications in traditional herbal medicine.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or localized redness when the leaves or stems come into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience itching or a mild rash following topical application. While systemic toxicity is not extensively documented in humans, ingestion of unprocessed parts could potentially lead to gastrointestinal discomfort. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid touching the plant if you have a known history of hypersensitivity to similar botanical species. Always consult a medical professional if an adverse reaction occurs after exposure.
