Epilobium hirsutum

What's the taxonomical classification of Epilobium hirsutum?

Epilobium hirsutum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Myrtales and is a member of the family Onagraceae. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Epilobium, and its specific identification is defined by the species hirsutum.

What are the therapeutic actions of Epilobium hirsutum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat urinary tract infections, bladder inflammation, kidney stones, and various conditions involving the renal system. Its diuretic properties help flush toxins and reduce swelling within the bladder and kidneys. Practitioners often utilize it to alleviate symptoms of cystitis and promote healthy urine flow. The plant's anti-inflammatory effects are particularly useful for managing discomfort caused by kidney irritation. These medicinal applications are rooted in traditional herbal practices aimed at supporting urinary health.
