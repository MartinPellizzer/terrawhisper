Crateva unilocularis

What's the taxonomical classification of Crateva unilocularis?

Crateva unilocularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Brassicales. This plant is further categorized under the family Capparaceae, which places it within the genus Crateva, and it is specifically identified by its species name, unilocularis.

What are the herbal preparations of Crateva unilocularis?

Crateva unilocularis is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a concentrated tincture to extract its bioactive components. Liquid extracts are often derived through alcohol-based tinctures to ensure stability and ease of dosing. These preparations are sometimes combined with other botanical ingredients to enhance their therapeutic profile. Practitioners select the specific form based on the desired concentration of active alkaloids.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Crateva unilocularis can cause localized skin irritation, digestive distress, and potential allergic reactions upon direct contact or ingestion. Individuals who touch the plant may experience redness, itching, or a burning sensation on the affected areas of the skin. If accidentally swallowed, the plant may lead to nausea, stomach cramping, or vomiting. Some people might also develop a systemic hypersensitivity response characterized by hives or swelling. It is important to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
