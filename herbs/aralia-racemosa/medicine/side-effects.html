10 Possible Side Effects Of Aralia Racemosa (American Spikenard)

Aralia racemosa, also known as American spikenard, has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal irritation, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can occur due to excessive consumption, interaction with other medications, or individual sensitivities. For example, gastrointestinal irritation can lead to stomach pain, nausea, and diarrhea, making daily activities like eating and working difficult.

Additionally, allergic reactions can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Aralia racemosa if used imporperly.

Aralia racemosa, also known as American Spikenard, causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is likely due to the plant's unique chemical composition, which includes saponins and lignans that can trigger an immune response in sensitive individuals. Additionally, the presence of other compounds like alkaloids and flavonoids may further contribute to the risk of an allergic reaction.

As a result, people who have never used Aralia racemosa before may experience symptoms such as hives, itching, or swelling upon exposure to the plant.
