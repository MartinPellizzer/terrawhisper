10 Possible Side Effects Of Aralia Racemosa

Aralia racemosa, also known as American spikenard, has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal irritation, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can occur due to excessive consumption, interaction with other medications, or individual sensitivities. For example, gastrointestinal irritation can lead to stomach pain, nausea, and diarrhea, making daily activities like eating and working difficult.

Additionally, allergic reactions can cause anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Aralia racemosa, also known as American Spikenard, causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is likely due to the plant's unique chemical composition, which includes saponins and lignans that can trigger an immune response in sensitive individuals. Additionally, the presence of other compounds like alkaloids and flavonoids may further contribute to the risk of an allergic reaction.

As a result, people who have never used Aralia racemosa before may experience symptoms such as hives, itching, or swelling upon exposure to the plant.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Aralia racemosa, also known as American Spikenard, triggers asthma attacks due to its potential allergenic properties and ability to cause bronchospasms.

The plant's sap and extracts have been shown to release histamine, a chemical that can stimulate an allergic response in the lungs, leading to constriction of airways and increased mucus production.

Additionally, Aralia racemosa may also contain compounds that can irritate the respiratory tract, further exacerbating asthma symptoms.

3. Induces vomiting frequently

Aralia racemosa, also known as American Spikenard, induces vomiting frequently due to its bitter and acrid properties.

The plant's high concentration of tannins and other compounds can irritate the stomach lining, causing nausea and vomiting in some individuals.

Additionally, the plant's aromatic oils may stimulate the digestive system, leading to frequent and intense stomach contractions that result in vomiting.

4. Lowers blood pressure significantly

Aralia racemosa, also known as American Spikenard, lowers blood pressure significantly.

This is due to its ability to dilate blood vessels and improve blood flow, allowing the heart to pump more efficiently.

The herb's flavonoids and saponins are believed to be responsible for this action, which can lead to a decrease in systolic and diastolic blood pressure.

5. Increases heart rate excessively

Aralia racemosa, also known as American Spikenard, increases heart rate excessively due to its stimulatory properties, which can stimulate the sympathetic nervous system.

This effect is caused by the presence of alkaloids and other bioactive compounds in the plant's extracts that can increase cardiac output and peripheral resistance, leading to an elevated heart rate.

6. Produces kidney stones

Aralia racemosa, also known as American Spikenard, produces kidney stones due to its high concentration of oxalates, which are naturally occurring compounds found in the plant's roots and stems.

When ingested, these oxalates can increase urine oxalate levels, leading to an increased risk of kidney stone formation.

Additionally, Aralia racemosa may also exacerbate existing kidney issues, making it essential for individuals with pre-existing renal conditions to exercise caution when considering its use.

7. Stimulates seizures suddenly

Aralia racemosa, also known as American Spikenard, stimulates seizures suddenly.

The compound magnolol present in the plant has been found to interact with certain neurotransmitters in the brain, such as GABA and glutamate, leading to an imbalance that can trigger seizures.

Additionally, the plant's active ingredients may affect the levels of potassium and sodium ions in the body, which can disrupt normal brain activity and increase the risk of seizure episodes.

8. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Aralia racemosa, also known as American Spikenard, disrupts menstrual cycles due to its ability to stimulate uterine contractions and alter hormone levels.

The plant's active compounds, including alkaloids and saponins, can affect the body's natural hormonal balance, leading to changes in menstruation patterns.

This may result in irregular or absent periods, as well as other menstrual abnormalities.

9. Worsens skin rashes

Aralia racemosa, also known as American Spikenard, worsens skin rashes due to its potential allergenic properties.

The plant's chemical composition, including sesquiterpenes and lignans, may cause an adverse reaction in some individuals, leading to increased redness, itching, and inflammation of the skin.

Additionally, the plant's high concentration of saponins can disrupt the skin's natural barrier function, making it more susceptible to irritation and sensitivity.

10. Causes digestive problems

Aralia racemosa, also known as American Spikenard, causes digestive problems.

The saponins and other bioactive compounds present in the plant may irritate the mucous membranes of the gastrointestinal tract, leading to symptoms such as bloating, diarrhea, or stomach cramps.

Additionally, the plant's fiber content can slow down digestion and potentially cause constipation or abdominal discomfort.
