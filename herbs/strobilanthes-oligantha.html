Strobilanthes oligantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Strobilanthes oligantha?

Strobilanthes oligantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Acanthaceae, falling under the genus Strobilanthes, and is specifically identified by its species name, oligantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strobilanthes oligantha has woody, upright stems with opposite, decussate leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape and possess entire margins.

The leaf surfaces are typically glabrous or slightly pubescent, often displaying prominent venation on the underside. Its inflorescences are characterized by dense, terminal spikes that bear relatively few flowers compared to other species in the genus. The corollas are tubular and zygomorphic, typically presenting in shades of blue or purple.

These floral structures are supported by small, bracteate pedicels that emerge from the axillary positions of the upper leaves.

        What are the herbal preparations of Strobilanthes oligantha?

        Strobilanthes oligantha is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant material in water to release its active constituents.
