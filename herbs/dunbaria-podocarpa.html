Dunbaria podocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dunbaria podocarpa has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of small, elliptical leaflets and a woody, climbing stem structure. The plant typically exhibits a scrambling or climbing habit, often utilizing its slender branches to navigate through surrounding vegetation. Its leaflets are characterized by smooth margins and a subtle texture that distinguishes them from similar species in the Fabaceae family. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves to facilitate reproduction. The overall growth form is adapted to forest understories where it can seek light through climbing.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The distribution is largely restricted to the island's rugged interior where dense canopy cover provides necessary shade.       Dunbaria podocarpa is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the dried roots, leaves, or whole plant in water to extract its medicinal compounds.       Dunbaria podocarpa can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential allergic responses in sensitive individuals.
