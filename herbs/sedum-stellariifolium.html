Sedum stellariifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Sedum stellariifolium?

Sedum stellariifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it is placed in the order Saxifragales and the family Crassulaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Sedum, and its unique species designation, stellariifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedum stellariifolium has succulent, star-shaped leaves that are fleshy, bright green, and arranged in dense, overlapping rosettes. These small, cylindrical leaves often exhibit a slightly pointed tip and a smooth, waxy texture to prevent water loss. The plant typically grows in a low-profile, spreading mound with stems that hug the ground. During blooming periods, tiny star-like flowers emerge from the center of the foliage, adding a delicate contrast to the thick leaves. This compact structure allows the plant to thrive in rocky crevices and shallow soil.
