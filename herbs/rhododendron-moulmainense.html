What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron moulmainense?

Rhododendron moulmainense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ericales. It is further categorized under the family Ericaceae, which contains a diverse array of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Rhododendron, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species moulmainense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron moulmainense has elliptic to oblong leaves that are typically leathery, dark green on the upper surface, and possess a distinctively revolute margin. The foliage is characterized by a somewhat coriaceous texture and a glabrous finish that aids in moisture retention. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose. The stems are woody and develop a rugged bark texture as the plant matures into a shrub or small tree. These structural features collectively define the species' distinctive silhouette within its natural montane habitat.
