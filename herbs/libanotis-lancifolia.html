Libanotis lancifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Libanotis lancifolia has lanceolate leaves that are typically narrow and elongated with entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant exhibits a slender growth habit characterized by fine, delicate stems that support its foliage. Its floral structures are arranged in small clusters, often emerging from the leaf axils or terminal ends. The root system is adapted to specific soil conditions, providing stability for its upright form. These morphological traits collectively define its distinct appearance within its natural habitat.
