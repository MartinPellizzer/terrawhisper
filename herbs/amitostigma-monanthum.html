Amitostigma monanthum has a perennial rootstock that produces erect, unbranched stems reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, topped by a single, terminal, bell-shaped flower. These leaves are typically thin and membranous, often displaying a bright green color that distinguishes them from more leathery species. The solitary flower is typically white or pale pink and lacks the colorful petals found in many other members of the Gentianaceae family.
