Fragaria vesca

What's the taxonomical classification of Fragaria vesca?

Fragaria vesca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further follows the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Fragaria and the species vesca.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Fragaria vesca contains high concentrations of ascorbic acid, ellagitannins, anthocyanins, and various phenolic acids that provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. These bioactive compounds work together to neutralize free radicals and protect cells from oxidative stress. The presence of ellagitannins specifically supports digestive health and may offer protective effects against certain types of cancer. Additionally, the anthocyanins responsible for the fruit's red color contribute to cardiovascular health by improving blood vessel function. These chemical properties make the plant a valuable source of dietary nutrients for systemic wellness.

What are the therapeutic actions of Fragaria vesca?

Fragaria vesca has potent antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties primarily driven by high concentrations of ellagitannins, anthocyanins, and vitamin C. These bioactive compounds work together to neutralize free radicals and protect cells from oxidative stress. The plant's extracts are frequently used to soothe skin irritations and reduce localized swelling due to their ability to inhibit inflammatory enzymes. Furthermore, its natural astringent qualities help tighten tissues and promote wound healing in topical applications. Regular consumption or topical use can support cardiovascular health and bolster the immune system through these diverse mechanisms.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal inflammation, urinary tract infections, and minor skin irritations. The fruit and leaves contain high concentrations of antioxidants and tannins that help reduce swelling in the digestive tract. In traditional medicine, decoctions made from the leaves are often consumed to soothe symptoms of diarrhea and dysentery. Additionally, topical applications of the plant may assist in healing small abrasions or soothing inflamed mucous membranes. These medicinal properties make it a versatile botanical for managing various mild inflammatory conditions.
