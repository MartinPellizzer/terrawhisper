Fragaria vesca

What's the taxonomical classification of Fragaria vesca?

Fragaria vesca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further follows the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Fragaria and the species vesca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fragaria vesca has a perennial growth habit characterized by a creeping rhizomatous structure that produces trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and red aggregate fruits known as strawberries. These leaves are typically arranged in basal rosettes and feature three distinct leaflets with long petioles. The plant produces small, five-petaled white flowers that emerge from the foliage during the blooming season. After pollination, the receptacle swells to form the fleshy, seed-studded fruit that defines the species. Runners, or stolons, extend from the base to facilitate vegetative reproduction and spreading across the soil surface.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, spanning across Europe, much of northern Asia, and North America. It thrives in a wide variety of habitats ranging from forest edges to mountain meadows and open woodlands. Due to its ability to adapt to different climates, it can be found from subarctic regions down to more temperate latitudes. Human activity and natural dispersal have also allowed it to become established in various non-native locations globally. Its distribution is largely dictated by the availability of moist, well-drained soils and seasonal temperature fluctuations.

What are the pharmacological activities of Fragaria vesca?

This plant has antioxidant, antibacterial, antimicrobial, and cytotoxic properties, as well as the ability to cause growth inhibition of tumor intestine cells. Its high concentration of polyphenols and anthocyanins allows it to scavenge free radicals and mitigate oxidative stress. The bioactive compounds within its tissues also demonstrate significant effectiveness in suppressing the proliferation of various bacteria and microbes. Research indicates that these specific phytochemicals can induce cytotoxicity in certain cancer cell lines. Furthermore, studies have highlighted its potential to achieve growth inhibition of tumor intestine cells during laboratory assays.

As discussed in a study, Fragaria vesca possessed one of the highest levels of anti-oxidant activity in vivo and in vitro among 20 medicinal herbs. The study evaluated these properties through various methods, including the 1,1-diphenyl-2-picrylhydrazyl radical (DPPH())-scavenging assay, chelating activity, and the capacity to protect plasmid DNA against oxidative damage. The protective properties of the Fragaria vesca extracts were more closely related to the DPPH() radical-scavenging activity, tannin content, and action on anti-oxidant gene expression than to other parameters. These extracts may exert anti-oxidant effects on bacteria through pathways such as direct inhibition of reactive oxygen species, iron chelation, and anti-oxidant genes induction.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex profile of medicinal compounds including catechins, ellagitannins, flavonoids, and various organic acids. The presence of catechins provides significant antioxidant properties that help combat oxidative stress in the body. Ellagitannins contribute to its unique bioactive potential through their ability to influence cellular health. Furthermore, the diverse array of flavonoids offers anti-inflammatory benefits that support overall systemic wellness. Finally, the organic acids play a crucial role in metabolic processes and the stabilization of these phytochemicals.

As discussed in a study, the vegetative parts of Fragaria vesca are used in decoctions for hypertension treatment because of their detoxifying, diuretic, stimulant, and dermatological protective properties. The leaves serve as a source of bioactive compounds for the cosmetic and pharmaceutical industries. Through LC-ESI/LTQOrbitrap/MS analysis, 27 metabolites were putativeley identified in the leaves. These identified metabolites mainly belong to organic acids, flavonoids, catechin and its oligomers, and ellagitannins. Cultivated samples and wild samples with autochthonous germplasm from the Campania region demonstrated higher antioxidant activity compared to non-autochthonous ones.

What health problems is this plant used for?

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic reactions in sensitive individuals, ranging from localized itching and hives to severe swelling. In extreme cases, a person might experience anaphylaxis, which is a life-threatening medical emergency. Ingestion of the plant may also lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. Monitoring for these diverse symptoms is essential for anyone coming into contact with the species.
