Cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon) Uses

Cranberry is used to treat coughs, cold symptoms, and respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It may also help with tonsillitis and reduce inflammation in skin conditions. Cranberry supports wound healing and is effective against fungal infections like athlete's foot and skin irritation. Its compounds combat bacteria and fungi, aiding in skin and respiratory health.

Cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon) Monograph

1. Cough

Cranberry is used to soothe coughs due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce throat irritation and loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. Often consumed as a tea or syrup, it supports respiratory health during colds and infections.

2. Cold symptoms

Cranberry is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its high vitamin C content, which supports immune function. It also contains antioxidants that reduce inflammation and soothe sore throats. Some traditional remedies use cranberry juice or extracts to ease congestion and boost resistance to infections. Its mild antiseptic properties may help prevent secondary infections during colds.

3. Respiratory infections

Cranberry is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing sore throats. It contains compounds that may help alleviate symptoms of colds and mild respiratory infections. Its antimicrobial properties can aid in preventing bacterial growth in the throat and upper respiratory tract.

4. Tonsillitis

Cranberry is used to support the treatment of tonsillitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce throat irritation and combat bacterial infections, particularly Streptococcus. Its compounds may soothe inflamed tissues and enhance immune response, making it a valuable herbal remedy in managing symptoms of tonsillitis.

5. Skin infections

Cranberry is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce inflammation and prevent bacterial growth. Applied as a poultice or infused oil, it can alleviate symptoms of conditions like eczema and minor wounds. Its natural compounds support skin healing and resistance to infection.

6. Wound healing

Cranberry has been traditionally used to support wound healing due to its antibacterial properties and high antioxidant content. It helps prevent infection and promotes tissue repair. Its compounds may reduce inflammation and enhance cellular regeneration, making it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for treating minor wounds and infections.

7. Inflammatory skin conditions

Cranberry is used topically to treat inflammatory skin conditions due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial infections. Its high concentration of proanthocyanidins supports skin healing and prevents further irritation. It is often applied as a poultice or infused oil.

8. Athlete's foot

Cranberry is used topically to treat athlete's foot due to its antifungal properties. It helps reduce fungal growth and soothe inflammation. A diluted cranberry juice solution can be applied to affected areas. Regular application may promote healing and prevent recurrence.

9. Fungal infections

Cranberry contains proanthocyanidins that inhibit fungal adherence to urinary tract epithelial cells, making it effective for preventing and treating urinary tract infections caused by fungi. It also exhibits antifungal properties against Candida species, supporting its use in herbal medicine for fungal infections.

10. Skin irritation

Cranberry is used topically to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and itching associated with minor skin conditions. Its high antioxidant content promotes healing and protects against further irritation. Apply diluted cranberry juice or a cranberry-based topical preparation as a natural remedy for mild skin issues.
