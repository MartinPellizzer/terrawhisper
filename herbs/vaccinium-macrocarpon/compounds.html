Cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon) Compounds

Cranberry contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Anthocyanins give it its red color and support urinary health. Other compounds like tannins and glycosides also play roles in its medicinal effects.

Cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. These compounds are believed to support urinary tract health and may have anti-inflammatory effects.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support urinary tract health. This compound is found in high concentrations in cranberry juice and supplements. Its presence is linked to the traditional use of cranberries in herbal medicine.

3. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon). It contributes to the fruit's deep red color and has antioxidant properties that support urinary tract health. Anthocyanin helps inhibit bacterial adhesion to urinary tract tissues, making cranberry a traditional remedy for urinary infections.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support urinary tract health by reducing bacterial adhesion. Tannins also have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, making them valuable in herbal medicine for their broad therapeutic potential.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial properties, particularly against urinary tract infections. Glycosides in cranberries are responsible for their ability to prevent bacterial adhesion to urinary tract cells. These compounds are commonly found in the fruit's juice and pulp.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial properties and may support urinary tract health. Coumarin also has potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, though its presence in cranberry is relatively low compared to other compounds.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial properties by disrupting bacterial cell membranes. Saponin also aids in reducing urinary tract infections by preventing bacterial adhesion. Its presence supports cranberry's traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil from cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon) contains volatile compounds like methyl salicylate and limonene. These contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is used in herbal medicine to support urinary tract health and reduce bacterial adhesion.

9. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon) contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds help inhibit bacterial adhesion to urinary tract epithelial cells, supporting cranberry's traditional use in urinary health. Specific terpenoids like quercetin and isoquercetin enhance its therapeutic potential.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in cranberry (Vaccinium macrocarpon). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though research on its specific effects in herbal medicine is limited compared to other compounds like proanthocyanidins. Alkaloids in cranberry may support urinary tract health and have antimicrobial properties.
