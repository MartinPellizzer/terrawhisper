Albizia gummifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Albizia gummifera?

Albizia gummifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized into the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, a group well-known for its diverse botanical characteristics, and specifically falls under the genus Albizia. The complete scientific name for this particular species is Albizia gummifera.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Albizia gummifera contains bioactive secondary metabolites including tannins, saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Research suggests that the extracts can assist in managing inflammation and protecting cells from oxidative stress. Some studies also indicate that the presence of specific glycosides may offer mild sedative or calming effects. The synergy of these compounds makes the plant a subject of interest in ethnobotanical studies for traditional medicine.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammatory conditions, and various digestive ailments. The gum harvested from its bark possesses significant antimicrobial and astringent properties that aid in healing skin lesions. When applied topically, it helps reduce swelling and soothe irritation caused by external injuries. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to alleviate internal discomfort through specific preparations. Its medicinal value is primarily attributed to the chemical constituents found within its resinous exudate.
