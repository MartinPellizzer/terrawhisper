Albizia gummifera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Albizia gummifera has a woody structure characterized by a small to medium-sized tree habit with bipinnate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The compound leaves consist of numerous pairs of small leaflets arranged along secondary axes, providing a feathery appearance. Its bark is typically textured and can range in color from grey to brownish hues depending on the age of the specimen. The flowers are actinomorphic and possess long, prominent stamens that create a brush-like effect during blooming. Once fertilized, the plant produces distinctive legume pods that contain the seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Horn of Africa, specifically within Ethiopia, Somalia, and parts of Kenya. It typically inhabits dry lowland areas and acacia savannas where seasonal rainfall is limited. The species thrives in sandy or rocky soils that are characteristic of these subtropical environments. Its presence is often tied to specific ecological niches that support drought-resistant vegetation. Because it prefers these warm, dry landscapes, its distribution remains concentrated within these East African territories.

Albizia gummifera has therapeutic properties that function as an astringent, hemostatic, cicatrizant, and anti-inflammatory agent to treat skin and mucosal ailments.     What are the herbal preparations of Albizia gummifera?

    Albizia gummifera is prepared as a gum resin that is typically processed into decoctions, infusions, or medicinal syrups for therapeutic use. This natural exudate is harvested from the tree bark and can be dissolved in water to create liquid extracts. Practitioners often use these aqueous preparations to access the plant's bioactive compounds for systemic absorption. Some traditional methods involve mixing the resin with honey to improve taste and preserve the mixture. The resulting liquid forms allow for controlled dosing in various herbal remedies.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Albizia gummifera can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic responses in sensitive individuals. While specific clinical studies on humans are limited, the chemical constituents of many species in this genus may trigger localized dermatitis upon contact. Ingestion of unprocessed parts could potentially lead to nausea, stomach cramping, or diarrhea due to the presence of bioactive compounds. Some users might also experience headaches or dizziness if the plant is consumed in large quantities. It is essential to consult a medical professional before using any part of this plant for medicinal purposes.
