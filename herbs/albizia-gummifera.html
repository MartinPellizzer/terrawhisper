Albizia gummifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Albizia gummifera?

Albizia gummifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized into the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, a group well-known for its diverse botanical characteristics, and specifically falls under the genus Albizia. The complete scientific name for this particular species is Albizia gummifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Albizia gummifera has a woody structure characterized by a small to medium-sized tree habit with bipinnate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The compound leaves consist of numerous pairs of small leaflets arranged along secondary axes, providing a feathery appearance. Its bark is typically textured and can range in color from grey to brownish hues depending on the age of the specimen. The flowers are actinomorphic and possess long, prominent stamens that create a brush-like effect during blooming. Once fertilized, the plant produces distinctive legume pods that contain the seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Horn of Africa, specifically within Ethiopia, Somalia, and parts of Kenya. It typically inhabits dry lowland areas and acacia savannas where seasonal rainfall is limited. The species thrives in sandy or rocky soils that are characteristic of these subtropical environments. Its presence is often tied to specific ecological niches that support drought-resistant vegetation. What are the pharmacological activities of Albizia gummifera?

This plant has antibacterial, antimicrobial, and antimycobacterial activities, alongside significant cytotoxic and apoptotic effects. Research indicates that its extracts can effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms and mycobacteria. The biological compounds within the plant also demonstrate the ability to induce cell death in targeted cells through an apoptotic effect. Furthermore, the observed cytotoxicity suggests its potential role in evaluating anti-tumor properties. These diverse pharmacological actions highlight the medicinal versatility of Albizia gummifera.

As discussed in a study, the 80% methanol crude extracts prepared from the seeds of Albizia gummifera were tested for inhibitory activity against clinical isolates of six S. pneumonae and twenty two S. pyogenes. The 80% ethanol solubilized fractions of the plant were found to have antibacterial effects to all assayed bacteria. The minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) of these fractions ranged from 500 mg/ ml to 1000 mg/ml. All extracts showed antibacterial activities against the clinical isolates of S. pyogenes and S. pneumonae. These results suggest that the extracts may contain bioactive compounds of therapeutic interest.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds lupeol, lupenone, oleanolic acid, stigmasterol, and budmunchiamine K. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the biological activities and therapeutic potential of the species. The presence of triterpenoids like oleanolic acid and lupeol often suggests anti-inflammatory or antioxidant properties. Additionally, the sterol stigmasterol plays a significant role in the plant's chemical profile. The inclusion of budmunchiamine K further expands the complexity of its pharmacological constituents.

As discussed in a study, the stem bark of Albizia gummifera has yielded four triterpene medicinal compounds. These identified substances include lupeol, lupenone, vitalboside-A (1), and vitalboside-A 2'-methylglucuronate (2). The compound labeled as (2) appears to be novel.
