What's the taxonomical classification of Turczaninovia fastigiata?

Turczaninovia fastigiata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, and its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Turczaninovia, specifically as the species fastigiata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Turczaninovia fastigiata has a creeping, stoloniferous growth habit characterized by slender, prostrate stems that produce small, tubular, pinkish-purple flowers.

These delicate stems spread horizontally across the soil surface to form dense mats. The leaves are typically small, lanceolate or linear, and are arranged oppositely along the stems. Each flower emerges from the leaf axils and features a distinctively narrow corolla tube.

This low-growing morphology allows the plant to effectively colonize open, rocky, or sandy substrates.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Turczaninovia fastigiata can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and respiratory distress if its pollen or sap is inhaled or touched. Contact with the plant's alkaloids often results in painful contact dermatitis and redness on the affected area. Some individuals may also experience nausea or dizziness if the plant is ingested accidentally. These symptoms can escalate quickly depending on the individual's sensitivity to its chemical compounds. Medical attention is recommended if any allergic reactions or persistent swelling occur after exposure.
