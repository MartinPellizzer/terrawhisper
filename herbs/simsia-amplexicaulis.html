What is Simsia amplexicaulis?

Simsia amplexicaulis is a perennial shrub native to Mexico, belonging to the family Asteraceae.

Its stems are woody, 10-60 cm in height, and branching, with a simple or alternate leaf arrangement. The leaves are lanceolate, 2-7.5 cm in length, with a serrated margin and a glabrous or hairy surface. The inflorescence is a capitulum, with 10-20 florets, bearing a discoid shape and a yellow or orange color.

Simsia amplexicaulis produces achenes as its fruit, which are glabrous and 2-3 mm in diameter.
