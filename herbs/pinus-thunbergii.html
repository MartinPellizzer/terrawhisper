Pinus thunbergii

What's the taxonomical classification of Pinus thunbergii?

Pinus thunbergii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Pinales and the family Pinaceae, eventually reaching the genus Pinus. Finally, the specific classification of this plant is completed at the species level as thunbergii.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pinus thunbergii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin infections, and respiratory ailments. The resin and needles are often prepared as medicinal baths or infusions to soothe skin irritations and promote healing. Some traditional practices utilize extracts from the plant to alleviate symptoms of coughs and congestion. These applications rely on the natural antimicrobial and soothing properties found within the species. Local knowledge suggests that different parts of the plant can be tailored to address specific types of physical discomfort.
