What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia sagitta?

Ligularia sagitta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, a large group of flowering plants, and specifically falls within the genus Ligularia. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species sagitta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia sagitta has large, arrow-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed with serrated edges and a prominent central vein. These basal leaves grow on long, sturdy petioles that emerge directly from a thick rhizome. The plant produces upright, unbranched flowering stalks that can reach significant heights above the foliage. At the apex of these stalks, bright yellow composite flower heads emerge in a corymb-like arrangement. Each flower head consists of numerous small disc florets surrounded by a ring of ray florets.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the moist, high-altitude regions of central and southern Japan, specifically occurring in mountainous areas. Its distribution is primarily restricted to the islands of Honshu, Shikoku, and Kyushu. It thrives in specialized habitats such as alpine meadows, forest edges, and damp slopes where moisture is consistent. Due to its preference for these specific cool and humid climates, it does not spread into warmer or more arid lowland environments. This localized range makes the species a characteristic element of the Japanese montane flora.
