Equisetum arvense

What's the taxonomical classification of Equisetum arvense?

Equisetum arvense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Equisetidae. The plant follows the order Equisetales and is a member of the family Equisetaceae. Finally, its taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement within the genus Equisetum, specifically identifying it as the species arvense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Equisetum arvense contains high concentrations of silica, flavonoids, tannins, and various alkaloids that provide its therapeutic properties. The significant presence of silicic acid supports bone health and connective tissue regeneration by strengthening structural proteins. Its flavonoid content offers antioxidant effects that help mitigate oxidative stress within the body. Additionally, the tannins provide astringent qualities that are often utilized to treat skin irritations and mucous membrane inflammation. These diverse chemical components make the plant a valuable subject in traditional herbal medicine for diuretic and remineralizing purposes.

What are the therapeutic actions of Equisetum arvense?

Equisetum arvense has diuretic, astringent, and remineralizing therapeutic actions primarily due to its high concentration of silica and various mineral salts. The plant acts as a natural diuretic that helps flush excess fluids from the body while supporting kidney function. Its high silica content promotes the strengthening of connective tissues, such as skin, hair, nails, and bones. When used as an astringent, it helps to tone mucous membranes and reduce inflammation in the urinary tract. These combined properties make it a valuable botanical agent for supporting bone density and overall tissue integrity.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat urinary tract infections, kidney stones, edema, and various inflammatory skin conditions. Its high silica content and diuretic properties help flush toxins from the renal system. It is often applied topically to soothe wounds or skin irritations due to its astringent qualities. Some practitioners also utilize it to support bone health and strengthen connective tissues. Regular use may assist in reducing fluid retention throughout the body.
