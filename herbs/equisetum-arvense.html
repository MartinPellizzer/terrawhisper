Equisetum Arvense Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Equisetum arvense, commonly known as field horsetail, is a perennial herb belonging to the Equisetaceae family, characterized by its jointed stems and spore-producing structures.

This herb is renowned for its high concentration of silica, which contributes to its reputation for supporting bone health, skin integrity, and joint function.

The bioactive constituents in Equisetum arvense include silica, flavonoids, tannins, and various minerals such as calcium and magnesium, which provide its therapeutic actions.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and decoctions can be made from the dried stems to harness its beneficial properties.

The root, although less frequently utilized, can also be dried and incorporated into herbal formulations.

The main herbal preparations made from these parts include teas, tinctures, and topical poultices, primarily aimed at supporting urinary health and detoxification.
