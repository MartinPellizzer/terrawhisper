Hypolytrum nemorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypolytrum nemorum has large, deeply lobed, and palmately divided leaves that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in width. These leaves feature prominent veins and serrated margins that contribute to their intricate, lace-like appearance. The plant grows from a creeping rhizome, which allows it to spread across the forest floor in shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The overall structure is characterized by a low-growing, herbaceous habit suited for understory life.
