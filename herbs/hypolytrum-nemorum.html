Hypolytrum nemorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Hypolytrum nemorum?

Hypolytrum nemorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Poales, it falls into the family Cyperaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Hypolytrum, and its unique species designation, nemorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypolytrum nemorum has large, deeply lobed, and palmately divided leaves that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in width.

The plant grows from a creeping rhizome, which allows it to spread across the forest floor in shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils.

The overall structure is characterized by a low-growing, herbaceous habit suited for understory life.
