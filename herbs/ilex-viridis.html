What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex viridis?

Ilex viridis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae, eventually reaching its specific classification within the genus Ilex, with the species epithet viridis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex viridis has small, elliptical, evergreen leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and slightly paler underneath. The leaf margins are typically entire or possess very fine, inconspicuous serrations. These leaves are arranged oppositely along woody, grayish-brown stems that form a compact, shrubby growth habit. The plant often develops small, dark berries during the fruiting season. Its overall structure remains dense and bushy throughout the year due to its persistent foliage.
