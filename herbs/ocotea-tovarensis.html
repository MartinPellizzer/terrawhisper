What is Ocotea tovarensis?

Ocotea tovarensis is a species of plant that belongs to the family Lauraceae, a family of flowering plants commonly known as laurels.

Botanically, it is characterized by a tree or shrub with a height of up to 15 meters, having a trunk diameter of up to 50 centimeters, and a dark brown, smooth, and slightly fragrant bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and coriaceous, measuring 8-20 centimeters in length and 2-5 centimeters in width, with a wavy or serrated margin and an acute or obtuse apex. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in a cylindrical spike, which are followed by a red or purple berry containing several seeds.

The wood of Ocotea tovarensis is valued for its timber, used in furniture-making and construction.
