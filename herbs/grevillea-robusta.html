Side Effects of grevillea robusta

Grevillea robusta triggers allergic reaction in individuals who are sensitive to its pollen or other components, leading to symptoms such as sneezing, runny nose, and even asthma-like symptoms.

Exposure to the herb can cause skin irritation, resulting in redness, itching, and a burning sensation, particularly when the skin comes into direct contact with the plant. The herb is also known to induce eye irritation, causing redness, watering, and a gritty feeling, which can be uncomfortable and disruptive.

In more severe cases, it may lead to breathing difficulty, especially in people with pre-existing respiratory conditions, as the airborne particles can trigger inflammation in the airways. Additionally, Grevillea robusta can cause throat irritation, skin rashes, stomach pain, and headaches, making it a potentially harmful plant for those with sensitivities or allergies.

It is important to exercise caution when handling or being exposed to this herb, and individuals experiencing any of these symptoms should seek medical attention if necessary.
