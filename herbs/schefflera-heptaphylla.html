Schefflerera heptaphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. The plant exhibits a woody structure with branching stems that support the dense canopy of compound leaves. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including 3-O-caffeoylquinic acid, flavonoids, and various phenolic acids. The presence of 3-O-caffeoylquinic acid is particularly significant due to its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These phytochemicals work together to protect cells from oxidative stress and metabolic damage. Research suggests that these bioactive molecules may also contribute to neuroprotective effects in the human body. Ongoing studies continue to explore how these specific concentrations influence overall therapeutic potential.

As discussed in a study, Schefflera heptaphylla contains three caffeoylquinic acid derivatives, specifically 3,4-di-O-caffeoylquinic acid, 3,5-di-O-caffeoylquinic acid, and 3-O-caffeoylquinic acid. The 3,4-di-O-caffeoylquinic acid and 3,5-di-O-caffeoylquinic acid compounds showed potent anti-RSV activity with median inhibitory concentrations of 2.33 microM (1.2 microg/ml) and 1.16 microM (0.6 microg/ml), respectively. These dicaffeoylquinic acids exhibited minimal cytotoxicity against HEp-2 cells with a median cytotoxic concentration higher than 1000 microM. The maximal non-cytotoxic concentration for these two compounds was approximately 96.7 microM. These medicinal compounds work by inhibiting virus-cell fusion in the early stage and cell-cell fusion at the end of the RSV replication cycle.

As discussed in a study, GC-MS analysis identified 27 volatile compounds from the essential oil obtained from the leaves of Schefflera heptaphylla. Among these, 17 compounds belonged to monoterpenes or sesquiterpenes, with the main constituent being beta-pinene comprising about 22% of the total volatile components. The essential oil demonstrated antiproliferative activity against MCF-7, A375, and HepG2 cancer cell lines with IC50 values of 7.3 microg/mL, 7.5 microg/mL, and 6.9 microg/mL, respectively. Additionally, (-) -beta-pinene and (+) -beta-pinene possessed antiproliferative activity against the same MCF-7, A375, and HepG2 cells with IC50 values ranging from 147.1 to 264.7 microm.
