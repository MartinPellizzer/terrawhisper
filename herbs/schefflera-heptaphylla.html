What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schefflera heptaphylla has large, leathery, elliptical leaves that typically grow in whorls of seven or more leaflets around a central stem.

The foliage is characterized by a glossy, dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Each leaf margin is entire and smooth, lacking any serration or teeth. The plant exhibits a woody structure with branching stems that support the dense canopy of compound leaves.

These morphological traits help the species thrive in its native tropical forest environment.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Schefflera heptaphylla can cause skin irritation, mouth swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if the plant is ingested or touched. The sap contains compounds that may trigger allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon skin exposure. If a person consumes any part of the plant, they might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Children and pets are at a higher risk of adverse reactions due to accidental ingestion. It is important to wash hands thoroughly after handling the foliage to prevent irritation.
