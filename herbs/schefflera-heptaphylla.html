Schefflerera heptaphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Schefflera, and its unique species designation, heptaphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schefflera heptaphylla has large, leathery, elliptical leaves that typically grow in whorls of seven or more leaflets around a central stem. The foliage is characterized by a glossy, dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Each leaf margin is entire and smooth, lacking any serration or teeth. The plant exhibits a woody structure with branching stems that support the dense canopy of compound leaves. These morphological traits help the species thrive in its native tropical forest environment.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compound 3-O-caffeoylquinic acid along with various other bioactive polyphenols and alkaloids. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the plant's recognized antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that these compounds play a significant role in protecting cells against oxidative stress. The presence of such secondary metabolites makes the species a subject of interest for pharmacological studies. These chemical constituents are essential to understanding the traditional medicinal uses of the foliage.

As discussed in a study, Schefflera heptaphylla contains three caffeoylquinic acid derivatives, specifically 3,4-di-O-caffeoylquinic acid, 3,5-di-O-caffeoylquinic acid, and 3-O-caffeoylquinic acid. The compounds 3,4-di-O-caffeoylquinic acid and 3,5-di-O-caffeoylquinic acid demonstrated potent anti-RSV activity with median inhibitory concentrations (IC50) of 2.33 microM (1.2 microg/ml) and 1.16 microM (0.6 microg/ml), respectively. These dicaffeoylquinic acids exhibited minimal cytotoxicity against HEp-2 cells, showing a median cytotoxic concentration (CC50) higher than 1000 microM. Furthermore, the maximal non-cytotoxic concentration (MNCC) for these two compounds was approximately 96.7 microM. The study suggests these medicinal compounds exert their effects by inhibiting virus-cell fusion and cell-cell fusion during the RSV replication cycle.
