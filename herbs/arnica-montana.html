Arnica montana

Arnica montana, commonly known as mountain arnica or leopard's bane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

This plant has been used for its health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, relieving pain, and promoting wound healing.

The therapeutic actions of arnica montana are attributed to its ability to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

The bioactive constituents of arnica montana include flavonoids, terpenes, and sesquiterpenes, specifically helenin, lactones, and thymol.

Possible Side Effects of Arnica montana

Arnica montana can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its potential interaction with certain medications.

Topical application of arnica can cause skin irritation, including redness, itching, and blistering, especially in individuals with sensitive skin.

Overuse or internal consumption of arnica can lead to increased heart rate and blood pressure, as well as allergic reactions such as hives and difficulty breathing.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise caution when using arnica, as it may cause uterine contractions or affect milk production.
