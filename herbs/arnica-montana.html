Possible Side Effects of arnica montana

Arnica montana can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and increased risk of bleeding due to its anti-coagulant properties.

Applying arnica oil or cream to open wounds or sensitive areas can lead to severe skin irritation and allergic reactions, including redness, itching, and swelling.

Ingesting arnica can cause stomach upset, nausea, and vomiting, especially if taken in large quantities or combined with other medications.

In rare cases, arnica may interact with blood-thinning medications, increasing the risk of bleeding and bruising.
