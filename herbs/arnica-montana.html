What to know about Arnica montana before using it medicinally

Arnica montana, commonly known as mountain arnica, is a medicinal herb used to treat various health conditions, including bruises, sprains, and wounds, due to its anti-inflammatory and anti-arthritic properties.

The main health benefits of Arnica montana include its ability to reduce pain and swelling, promote wound healing, and improve skin conditions such as eczema and acne. The herb's therapeutic properties include its anti-inflammatory, anti-arthritic, and antiseptic properties, which contribute to its analgesic and antiphlogistic effects. The main bioactive compounds in Arnica montana are sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In herbal medicine, Arnica montana is often prepared as a topical cream or ointment, known as arnica gel or oil, which is applied directly to the affected area to promote healing and reduce inflammation.
