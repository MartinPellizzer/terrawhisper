Mountain Arnica

Arnica montana

Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia, known for its bright yellow flowers and traditional use in herbal medicine to treat inflammatory conditions and skin wounds.

Botanical Identification

Arnica montana is a perennial herb in the Asteraceae family. Commonly known as mountain Arnica, it is native to alpine regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has yellow-orange flowers, hairy leaves, and a rhizomatous root system. It is also referred to as leopard's bread or wolf's bane in some regional texts.

Mountain Arnica contains key active compounds such as terpenoids, flavonoids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Preparation Methods

Tincture: Used for treating bruises, sprains, and inflammatory conditions.

Poultice: Applied externally for muscle pain and skin irritations.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana) should not be used internally as it can cause gastrointestinal irritation and toxicity. It is contraindicated for internal use and should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation. External use should be cautious to prevent skin irritation and allergic reactions.
