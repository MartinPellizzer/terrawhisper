Alpinia hainanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Alpinia hainanensis?

Alpinia hainanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Zingiberales. This species is a representative of the family Zingiberaceae, specifically situated within the genus Alpinia, with its distinct biological identity defined by its specific epithet, hainanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia hainanenensis has erect, rhizomatous stems that reach heights of up to 2 meters and bear large, lanceolate leaves with prominent parallel venation. Its inflorescence consists of dense terminal spikes that produce tubular, cream-colored flowers with distinctively colored labella. These floral structures are often accompanied by bright red or pink bracts that provide a striking visual contrast.
