Alpinia hainanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia hainanenensis has erect, rhizomatous stems that reach heights of up to 2 meters and bear large, lanceolate leaves with prominent parallel venation. The plant features long, tapering leaf blades that typically measure between 30 and 60 centimeters in length. Its inflorescence consists of dense terminal spikes that produce tubular, cream-colored flowers with distinctively colored labella. These floral structures are often accompanied by bright red or pink bracts that provide a striking visual contrast. The overall architecture of the plant is characterized by its lush, tropical foliage and robust herbaceous structure.
