What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichophorum subcapitatum has perennial, tufted stems that are typically slender and topped by a small, somewhat capitate spike consisting of several closely packed spikelets. Each spikelet contains a single floret with long, narrow lemmas that are frequently tinged with brownish or reddish hues. The leaves are linear, grass-like, and emerge from the base of the plant to form dense clumps.
