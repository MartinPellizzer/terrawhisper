Trichophorum subcapitatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Trichophorum subcapitatum?

Trichophorum subcapitatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the hierarchical lineage of the order Poales. It is further categorized under the family Cyperaceae, which contains the genus Trichophorum, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as subcapitatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichophorum subcapitatum has perennial, tufted stems that are typically slender and topped by a small, somewhat capitate spike consisting of several closely packed spikelets.

The spikelets are characterized by having scales that are relatively small and often possess a distinctively pointed or acute apex. Each spikelet contains a single floret with long, narrow lemmas that are frequently tinged with brownish or reddish hues. The leaves are linear, grass-like, and emerge from the base of the plant to form dense clumps.

These morphological features distinguish the species from other members of the genus through its specific spike arrangement and scale structure.
