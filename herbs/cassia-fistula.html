Golden shower tree

Cassia fistula

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Cassia fistula, commonly known as the Golden Shower Tree, belongs to the Fabaceae family. It is a deciduous tree native to tropical Asia. The plant is characterized by its yellow, pendulous flowers and bipinnate leaves. It is also referred to as Kachnar in India and is widely used in traditional herbal medicine.

Active Compounds

The Golden Shower Tree contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, coumarins, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin
  • Tannin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Integumentary Skin inflammation, wound healing, fungal infections, skin infections Anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, antifungal, antimicrobial
Respiratory Respiratory infections, cough, respiratory tract infections Antimicrobial, expectorant
Digestive Gastrointestinal upset, diarrhea, hemorrhoids, dental infections, gastrointestinal inflammation Antispasmodic, antidiarrheal, astringent, antimicrobial

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and skin conditions.

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and as a calming agent.

Extract: Used for inflammation and pain relief.

Safety Profile

The Golden Shower Tree (Cassia fistula) is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses may lead to toxicity, so it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.
