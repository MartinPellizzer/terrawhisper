Paphiopediluum appletonianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paphiopedilum appletonianum has strap-shaped, leathery leaves that grow in a basal rosette and flowers featuring a waxy, yellowish-green dorsal sepal with brownish spotting.

Its most distinctive feature is the pouch-shaped labellum, which serves as a specialized trap for pollinators. The base of the flower often displays subtle reddish or brownish hues that contrast with the lighter sepals.
