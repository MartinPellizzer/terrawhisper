Angelica likiangensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angelica likiangensis has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by hollow, ribbed stems and large, compound leaves with deeply serrated leaflets. The foliage typically features ovate to lanceolate segments that emerge from long petioles. During the flowering season, the plant produces dense, branched umbels of small, white flowers. These inflorescences are supported by sturdy peduncles that rise above the vegetative mass. The root system consists of a thick, fleshy rhizome which provides structural stability and nutrient storage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude alpine regions of the Likian Mountains in southwestern China. It grows specifically within subalpine meadows and rocky slopes where the climate remains cool and moist. Its distribution is highly localized, meaning it is not found in other parts of the world outside this specific mountain range. The species relies on the unique soil compositions and environmental conditions provided by these mountain ecosystems.
