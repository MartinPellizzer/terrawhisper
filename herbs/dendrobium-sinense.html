Dendrobium sinense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium sinense has elongated, sympodial pseudobulbs that are fleshy, cylindrical, and can reach lengths of up to 50 centimeters with a slightly compressed shape. These thick stems are covered in numerous nodes and produce small, ovate, leathery leaves near the apex. The inflorescence consists of several small, fragrant flowers that typically feature white or pale pink petals with a distinctively patterned lip. Each bloom exhibits a delicate structure with a fleshy callus at the center of the labellum. The root system is composed of thick, aerial roots designed to cling to tree bark and absorb moisture from the air.

What are the herbal preparations of Dendrobium sinense?

Dendrobium sinense is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried stems in water to extract its bioactive polysaccharides and alkaloids. This herb is also frequently processed into concentrated powders or granules that can be easily dissolved in warm liquids. In some traditional applications, the dried plant material is macerated in alcohol to create medicinal tinctures. Some practitioners also incorporate the plant into honey-based syrups to improve its palatability and ease of consumption. These various forms allow for different concentrations of the plant's nourishing properties to be administered to patients.
