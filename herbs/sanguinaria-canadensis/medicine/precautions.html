1. Discontinue if no improvement

When using Sanguinaria canadensis medicinally, it's important to take under medical supervision due to its potent and potentially toxic compounds.

Bloodroot contains sanguinarine, a chemical that can cause severe allergic reactions, liver damage, and gastrointestinal problems if not used properly.

Additionally, the herb may interact with other medications, such as anticoagulants and immunosuppressants, increasing the risk of adverse effects.
