10 Possible Side Effects Of Sanguinaria Canadensis (Bloodroot)

Sanguinaria canadensis has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and potential liver damage.

These side effects can occur due to excessive dosages, prolonged use, or combination with other medications. For example, gastrointestinal upset can lead to malnutrition, energy loss, and decreased productivity, making daily tasks and activities more challenging.

Additionally, liver damage can have long-term consequences, such as liver failure or cirrhosis.

1. Affects mental clarity severely

Sanguinara canadensis causes allergic reactions.

This is often attributed to the presence of alkaloids such as sanguinarine and chelerythrine in its extracts. These compounds can stimulate an immune response, triggering symptoms like hives, itching, and flushing.

Additionally, the plant's histamine-releasing properties may contribute to allergic responses, making it essential for individuals to be aware of potential sensitivities when using Sanguinaria canadensis.

Sanguinaria canadensis induces vomiting severely due to its active ingredient, sanguinarine, which is a highly toxic and irritating alkaloid.

When ingested, this compound stimulates the stomach lining, causing it to release large amounts of stomach acid and enzymes that irritate the mucous membranes, leading to nausea and vomiting.

The intense irritation also triggers the gag reflex, further exacerbating the vomiting.
