Ottelia acuminata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ottelia acuminata?

Ottelia acuminata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Alismatales and falls within the family Hydrocharitaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Ottelia and the species acuminata.

      What are the herbal preparations of Ottelia acuminata?

      Ottelia acuminata is prepared as a decoction or a simple aqueous infusion to extract its bioactive constituents for medicinal use. The plant material is typically dried and then simmered in water to create a concentrated liquid extract.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ottelia acuminata can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals who handle the plant without protection. While it is primarily an aquatic plant, direct contact with its tissues may trigger redness, itching, or a localized rash. There is currently limited toxicological data regarding the internal ingestion of this species, so its safety for consumption remains unverified. Caution should be exercised when working in habitats where this plant grows, as many aquatic species harbor microorganisms. Avoiding skin contact is the most effective way to prevent potential adverse reactions.
