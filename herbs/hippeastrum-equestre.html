What's the taxonomical classification of Hippeastrum equestre?

Hippeastrum equestre belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression through the order Asparagales, it is placed in the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Hippeastrum, and its specific species designation, equestre.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hippeastrum equestre has thick, fleshy, strap-shaped leaves that emerge from an underground bulb and produce trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of orange or red with yellow throats. The leaves are typically dark green, lanceolate, and grow in a basal rosette pattern. Each flower sits atop a long, leafless stalk called a scape that can reach significant heights. The floral structure features six tepals that flare outward to create a distinct funnel form. These plants are characterized by their succulent-like stems and robust root systems designed for water storage.
