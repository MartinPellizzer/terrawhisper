Geophila repens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geophila repens has a slender, creeping rhizome system that produces upright, erect stems with small, opposite, elliptical leaves and minute, inconspicuous flowers. The foliage is characterized by thin, somewhat fleshy textures and a deep green coloration that helps the plant maximize light absorption in shaded environments. Its stems are relatively weak and often rely on surrounding vegetation for structural support as they spread across the forest floor. The reproductive structures are tiny and often tucked within the leaf axils, making them difficult to observe without magnification. This specialized morphology allows the species to effectively colonize moist, low-light niches within its native habitat.

    What are the herbal preparations of Geophila repens?

    Geophila repens is prepared as an aqueous decoction, a concentrated infusion, or a topical poultice for medicinal application. These various forms allow the plant's active components to be administered either internally or externally depending on the intended therapeutic effect. When used as a decoction, the plant material is boiled in water to extract soluble compounds for oral consumption.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Geophila repens can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains specific chemical compounds that may trigger redness or itching upon direct contact with the epidermis. Individuals with sensitive skin or known plant allergies are at a higher risk for developing inflammatory responses. If parts of the plant are consumed, users might experience nausea, cramping, or vomiting. It is important to handle this species with gloves to prevent unforeseen adverse reactions.
