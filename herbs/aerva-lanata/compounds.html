Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) Compounds

The primary compounds of Indian Hemp include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Saponins and saponin-related compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, while alkaloids like those found in the plant may have various therapeutic effects. These compounds work together to support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. These compounds may support immune function and help reduce oxidative stress in the body.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in reducing inflammation and treating skin conditions. It also supports wound healing and may help in managing digestive disorders due to its antimicrobial and antioxidant effects.

3. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and inflammation. Saponins exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine. They also enhance the plant's detoxifying effects.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. This compound helps modulate cellular responses and may aid in reducing oxidative stress.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues. Coumarin's antispasmodic and analgesic properties support its role in herbal medicine. It is also noted for its potential antifungal and antibacterial effects.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and inflammatory conditions. Glycosides in Aerva lanata are known for their potential antimicrobial and expectorant properties. They play a significant role in the plant's medicinal applications.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Aerva lanata contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. They are responsible for the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Terpenoids enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) contains bioactive compounds like alkaloids and terpenoids. It is used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and applied topically or inhaled for therapeutic effects.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) are bioactive compounds with diverse pharmacological properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and inflammatory conditions. These alkaloids may also possess antispasmodic and analgesic effects, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects. This compound plays a key role in the therapeutic potential of Indian Hemp in traditional herbal medicine.
