Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) Uses

Indian Hemp is used to treat various respiratory conditions such as cough, asthma, bronchitis, and lung congestion. It helps alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections, influenza, and the common cold. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties make it beneficial for allergic rhinitis and other respiratory tract infections.

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) Monograph

1. Respiratory infection

Aerva lanata is used in traditional Indian medicine to treat respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing and congestion by reducing inflammation in the airways. The plant's leaves and roots are often prepared as decoctions or pastes for internal and external application. Its efficacy is supported by historical use and preliminary scientific studies.

2. Cough

Aerva lanata is used to treat coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce throat irritation. The plant's leaves and flowers are commonly prepared as decoctions or poultices. It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its soothing effect on respiratory conditions.

3. Influenza

Aerva lanata is used in traditional Indian medicine to alleviate symptoms of influenza due to its antipyretic and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce fever and body aches associated with the flu. The plant's extracts are believed to support the immune system and ease respiratory discomfort during illness.

4. Allergic rhinitis

Aerva lanata is used in traditional Indian medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose by modulating immune responses. The plant's extracts are often prepared as decoctions or powders for internal use.

5. Common cold

Aerva lanata is used in traditional Indian medicine to alleviate symptoms of the common cold. It helps reduce fever, congestion, and sore throat due to its anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. The plant's leaves and flowers are often prepared as teas or decoctions to support respiratory health during colds.

6. Respiratory infections

Aerva lanata is used in traditional Indian medicine to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing, bronchitis, and throat infections. The plant's leaves and roots are often prepared as decoctions or pastes for internal and external application.

7. Asthma

Aerva lanata is used in traditional Indian medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms. Its leaves and roots contain compounds that act as bronchodilators, helping to open airways and reduce inflammation. It is often prepared as a decoction or paste and taken orally to ease breathing and manage respiratory distress.

8. Respiratory tract infections

Aerva lanata is used in traditional Indian medicine to treat respiratory tract infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing, congestion, and bronchitis by reducing mucus production and soothing irritated airways. The plant's bioactive compounds support immune function and may help prevent secondary infections.

9. Lung congestion

Aerva lanata is traditionally used to alleviate lung congestion due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps clear mucus and reduce swelling in the respiratory tract, making it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis and asthma. Its active compounds support respiratory health by improving airflow and easing breathing difficulties.

10. Bronchitis

Aerva lanata is used in traditional Indian medicine to treat bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce bronchial inflammation and clears mucus, easing breathing. The plant's leaves and roots are often prepared as decoctions or poultices for respiratory relief.
