Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) Actions

Indian Hemp exhibits several therapeutic actions including diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic properties. It acts as a bitter tonic and carminative, aiding digestion and reducing gas. The plant also functions as an expectorant and stimulant, helping to clear mucus and invigorate the body. Its astringent and sedative effects support tissue repair and relaxation.

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) Monograph

1. Diuretic

Indian Hemp has a diuretic action, promoting the increased excretion of urine. This effect helps in reducing fluid retention and supporting kidney function. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to treat conditions involving water retention and urinary disorders.

2. Anti-inflammatory

Indian Hemp has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to the presence of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids. These components help reduce swelling and pain by inhibiting inflammatory pathways in the body. It is commonly used in traditional medicine to treat conditions like arthritis and skin inflammation. Its effects are supported by both historical use and modern scientific research.

3. Bitter

Indian Hemp has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and increases appetite. This property helps in clearing mucus and reducing dampness in the body. It also supports the liver and gallbladder by promoting bile flow. The bitter taste is key to its effectiveness in herbal formulations.

4. Tonic

Indian Hemp has a tonic action, helping to strengthen and invigorate the body. It supports overall vitality and energy levels, making it useful for individuals experiencing fatigue or weakness. This property is particularly beneficial in restoring balance and enhancing physical and mental performance.

5. Carminative

Indian Hemp has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating by promoting the expulsion of trapped air from the digestive tract. This effect supports overall gastrointestinal comfort and can alleviate symptoms of indigestion and flatulence.

6. Expectorant

Indian Hemp has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It is used to relieve congestion and support coughing in conditions like bronchitis and colds. Its active compounds stimulate secretion and reduce mucus viscosity, aiding in clearer breathing.

7. Stimulant

Indian Hemp has a stimulant action, which enhances energy and mental alertness. It works by increasing neural activity and improving circulation. This effect is particularly useful in cases of fatigue or low vitality. It supports overall vitality and physical performance.

8. Antispasmodic

Indian Hemp has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps. It works by relaxing smooth muscles, making it useful for conditions like menstrual cramps, gastrointestinal spasms, and respiratory issues. This property is supported by its traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine.

9. Astringent

Indian Hemp has an astringent action, which helps reduce inflammation and tighten tissues. It is used to treat wounds, skin irritations, and digestive issues by promoting healing and reducing excess secretion. This property makes it valuable in herbal medicine for its ability to constrict blood vessels and soothe internal and external ailments.

10. Sedative

Indian Hemp has a sedative action, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. It works by calming the nervous system, making it useful for managing stress and insomnia. Its mild effects make it a safe option for those seeking natural relief from restlessness.
