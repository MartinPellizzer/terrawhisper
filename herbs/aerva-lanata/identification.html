Botanical Identification of Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata)

To identify Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Indian Hemp, Aerva lanata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Spermatophyta, class Caryophyllopsida, order Caryophyllales, family Amaranthaceae, genus Aerva, and species Aerva lanata. These classifications reflect its botanical relationships and place within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Indian Hemp is Aerva lanata, a species within the Amaranthaceae family. It was first described by the botanist Robert Brown in 1810. The plant is also known by various regional and historical names, reflecting its widespread use and cultural significance across different regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) is an erect, herbaceous plant with a fibrous root system. It has a branching stem that is hairy and can grow up to 2 meters tall. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have serrated edges. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) can be distinguished by its cylindrical, branched stems with a rough texture, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic with serrated edges, and yellow flowers in terminal spikes. It is also characterized by its fibrous roots and the presence of a milky sap when the stems are broken. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Aerva javanica.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Nepal, and Sri Lanka. It thrives in warm climates with high humidity and prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soils. This plant typically grows at altitudes ranging from 300 to 1,500 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) blooms from July to September, producing small white or yellowish flowers. Seed formation occurs from October to November. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested during the early flowering stage, around August to September, when morphological features are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Indian Hemp (Aerva lanata) belongs to the family Amaranthaceae, closely related to species like Aerva javanica and Aerva henryi. It shares morphological and genetic traits with other Amaranthaceae members, including similar leaf structures and reproductive features. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its close ties within the genus Aerva and broader family relationships.
