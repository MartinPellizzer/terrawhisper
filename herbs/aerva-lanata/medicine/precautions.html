10 Precautions To Take When Using Aerva Lanata (Lamb'S Tail)

Aerva lanata has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as pregnant and breastfeeding women avoiding its use.

This is because the herb may stimulate uterine contractions, which can cause preterm labor or abortion. Additionally, individuals with bleeding disorders or those taking anticoagulant medications should also exercise caution, as Aerva lanata may increase the risk of bleeding or interact with these medications.

Failure to do so may result in complications, such as miscarriage or excessive bleeding.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Aerva lanata medicinally.

1. Follow proper storage guidelines

When using Aerva lanata medicinally, it's important to avoid oral use without guidance.

This is because the plant contains anthraquinones and other bioactive compounds that can cause gastrointestinal irritation, allergic reactions, and interact with medications when taken internally without proper supervision.

Furthermore, the plant's effects on blood sugar levels and cardiovascular health are not yet fully understood, making it essential to consult a qualified healthcare professional before using Aerva lanata for therapeutic purposes.
