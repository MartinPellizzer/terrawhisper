Rubus pirifolius

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus pirifolius?

Rubus pirifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is a member of the order Rosales and falls under the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Rubus, and its specific species designation, pirifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus pirifolius has trifoliate leaves characterized by three distinct leaflets that exhibit a pear-shaped or pyriform morphology. The individual leaflets are typically serrated along the margins and possess a leathery texture with prominent venation. Its stems are woody and often feature small prickles or fine hairs that assist in climbing or sprawling through vegetation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into aggregate fruits. These botanical structures collectively define its unique classification within the Rubus genus.

What are the therapeutic actions of Rubus pirifolius?

Rubus pirifolius has therapeutic properties characterized by its ability to act as an astringent, tonic, demulcent, anti-inflammatory, and hemostatic agent.
