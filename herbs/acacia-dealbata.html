Acacia dealbata

What's the taxonomical classification of Acacia dealbata?

Acacia dealbata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Acacia and the specific species epithet dealbatia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acacia dealbata has silver-grey phyllodes that are linear or lanceolate in shape, measuring up to 15 centimeters long and approximately 1 centimeter wide. These leaf-like structures are arranged alternately along the stems and possess a distinctive glaucous coating that gives the plant its characteristic pale appearance. The tree produces bright yellow, globular flower heads that emerge from the leaf axils during the spring months. Its bark is typically smooth and grey, though it may become fissured as the specimen matures into a large tree. The fruit consists of elongated, dark brown pods that contain numerous small, hard seeds.
