Onobrychis viciifolia

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Onobrychis viciifolia, commonly known as sainfoin, belongs to the legume family Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It is also known by synonyms such as Melilotus officinalis in some historical herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Onobrychis viciifolia contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Integumentary Inflammatory skin conditions, skin irritation, skin infection Anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antimicrobial
Respiratory Respiratory infections, bronchitis, respiratory allergies Antimicrobial, antihistaminic, antioxidant, antispasmodic
Digestive Gastrointestinal inflammation, gastric ulcers, diarrhea Anti-inflammatory, antacid, antidiarrheal, antispasmodic

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and digestive issues.

Decoction: Used for digestive ailments and as a general tonic.

Powder: Used for gastrointestinal issues and as a dietary supplement.

Safety Profile

Onobrychis viciifolia is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses should be avoided, and it is contraindicated during pregnancy due to potential risks to fetal development.
