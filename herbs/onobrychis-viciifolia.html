What to know about Onobrychis viciifolia (sainfoin) before using it medicinally

Sainfoin, scientifically known as Onobrychis viciifolia, is a herb that boasts impressive health properties, renowned for its ability to improve cardiovascular health, boost digestion, and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression.

While it's often used in traditional medicine, sainfoin is also a popular ingredient in various culinary delights, such as soups, salads, and teas, where its mild flavor and versatility make it an excellent addition. As a perennial crop, sainfoin is highly prized for its hortocultural significance, being used to improve soil fertility, suppress weeds, and provide a natural source of nitrogen. Botanically speaking, sainfoin is classified as a legume, characterized by its distinctive pea-like pods and delicate white flowers.

With its long history of use dating back to ancient Greece, where it was valued for its medicinal and culinary properties, sainfoin has been a staple in traditional folk medicine for centuries, with references found in the writings of esteemed herbalists such as Dioscorides.
