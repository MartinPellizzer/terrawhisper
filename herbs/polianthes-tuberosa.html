Polianthes tuberosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Polianthes tuberosa?

Polianthes tuberosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Asparagales and the family Asparagaceae. Finally, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Polianthes, and its unique species designation, tuberosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Polianthes tuberosa contains various bioactive compounds including saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, gastrointestinal disturbances, and inflammatory conditions. The tuberous roots are often processed into medicinal pastes to soothe topical irritations and accelerate the healing of cuts. When prepared as an infusion, it can help alleviate stomach aches and digestive irregularities. Some traditional practices also utilize its properties to reduce swelling and manage systemic inflammation. These applications rely on the plant's specific phytochemical profile to combat various ailments.
