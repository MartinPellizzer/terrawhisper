Catigua (Trichilia catigua): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Catigua (Trichilia catigua) is a medicinal plant native to South America, traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antispasmodic properties.

What is Catigua (Trichilia Catigua)?

Catigua (Trichilia catigua) is a tree native to South America, primarily found in Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay, and is known for its use in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Catigua is Trichilia catigua. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Meliaceae, genus Trichilia, and species Trichilia catigua. Common names for this plant include "Catigua" in Brazil and "Catiguá" in parts of Central America. It is also known as "Cocowood" in the Caribbean and "Cocowood" in some regions of South America. Synonyms for Catigua include Trichilia stipulata and Trichilia glabra, though these are less commonly used.

Plant Description and Morphology

Catigua is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, rounded crown and branching structure.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth, waxy surface and a pointed tip. The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and clustered in axillary or terminal inflorescences, emitting a subtle, sweet fragrance. The root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing the plant to anchor firmly in moist, well-drained soils.

The bark is rough and grayish-brown, while the leaves are slightly pubescent on the underside, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Catigua is native to the tropical regions of South America, specifically Brazil, Paraguay, and northern Argentina.

It thrives in the humid lowland forests and along riverbanks of the Amazon and Pantanal basins. The plant prefers a warm, moist climate with high humidity and consistent rainfall throughout the year. It grows best in well-drained soils that are rich in organic matter and nutrients.

Catigua is typically found in secondary forests and disturbed areas where it can establish itself quickly.

Each part of the plant contributes specific therapeutic properties valued in herbal medicine.
