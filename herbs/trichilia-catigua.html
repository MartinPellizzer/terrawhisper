Catigua

Trichilia catigua

Catigua (Trichilia catigua) is a tropical tree native to Central and South America, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating various health conditions.

Taxonomy

Catigua (Trichilia catigua) is a species belonging to the genus Trichilia in the family Meliaceae, classified within the order Sapindales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Distribution

Catigua (Trichilia catigua) is native to the tropical regions of South America, primarily found in Brazil, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, and Venezuela, growing in humid lowland forests and riverine areas.

Traditional Uses

Catigua (Trichilia catigua) has been traditionally used in Brazilian herbal medicine to treat inflammation, pain, and digestive issues, particularly as a natural remedy for conditions like arthritis and gastrointestinal discomfort.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Brazilian folk medicine, Afro-Brazilian traditions like Candomblé, and indigenous South American healing practices, Catigua (Trichilia catigua) has been historically used for its purported therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Catigua (Trichilia catigua) is characterized by the presence of triterpenoids, particularly the compounds known as trichiliosides, along with flavonoids, tannins, and other bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Catigua (Trichilia catigua) exhibits pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antioxidant effects, which contribute to its traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and pain.

Botanical Identification

Catigua, scientifically known as Trichilia catigua, belongs to the family Meliaceae. It is also called "catigua" in Portuguese and "curupira" in some regions. A small evergreen tree native to South America, it has smooth bark, elliptical leaves, and yellowish flowers. Its scientific name is widely recognized in herbal literature, though some older references use synonyms like Trichilia americana.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Safety Profile

Catigua (Trichilia catigua) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential adverse effects on fetal or infant development. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
