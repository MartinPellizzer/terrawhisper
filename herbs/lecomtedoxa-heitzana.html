Lecomtedoxa heitzana

What's the taxonomical classification of Lecomtedoxa heitzana?

Lecomtedoxa heitzana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Ericales. The plant is further categorized into the family Sapotaceae and is the sole representative of its genus, Lecomtedoxa, with its specific identity defined by the species name heitzana.

    Lecomtedoxa heitzana is prepared as a decoction, infusion, or topical poultice using its various plant parts. For external applications, crushed plant material is applied directly to the skin as a poultice to address localized issues. These traditional methods ensure that the medicinal properties of the plant are effectively extracted for therapeutic purposes.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lecomtedoxa heitzana can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested. The sap contains chemical compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis or allergic reactions upon touching the skin. If swallowed, the plant can lead to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. There is also a risk of systemic effects if the toxins are absorbed into the bloodstream through open wounds. Due to these unpredictable reactions, caution should be exercised when handling this species.
