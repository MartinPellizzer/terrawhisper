Lactuca sororia

What's the taxonomical classification of Lactuca sororia?

Lactuca sororia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae, which identifies it as a member of the sunflower or daisy family. Finally, it is placed within the genus Lactuca, with its specific identification being the species sororia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lactuca sororia has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and is characterized by a sparsely branched structure. The leaves are primarily basal, forming a rosette, while the cauline leaves are smaller, alternate, and often deeply lobed or pinnatifid. This plant features a milky white latex that exudes from the stem and leaves when they are damaged. The inflorescence consists of numerous small, yellow flower heads arranged in a loose, terminal panicle. Each flower head contains several individual florets that contribute to the bright, spreading appearance of the blooming plant.
