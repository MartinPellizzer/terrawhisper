Coral Bean

Caesalpinia crista

Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America, known for its striking coral-pink flowers and its traditional use in herbal medicine to support urinary and digestive health.

Taxonomy

Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) is a species belonging to the genus Caesalpinia in the family Fabaceae, classified within the order Fabales, class Fabales, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) is a shrub or small tree characterized by its distinctive coral-red or orange-red, bean-like pods, compound leaves with 5-11 pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and yellow or orange-red flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure.

Distribution

Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries such as Nigeria, Ghana, and Sudan, and is also found in parts of southern Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

Traditional Uses

Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to stimulate the heart and increase blood pressure, often prepared as a tonic or in tincture form.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Native American practices, Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) has been used for its purported therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) is characterized by the presence of alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, with alkaloids such as caesalpinine and tetracalcium being of particular interest for their pharmacological potential.

Pharmacology

Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) exhibits pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, analgesic, antipyretic, and antispasmodic effects, which are attributed to its traditional use in managing pain, fever, and gastrointestinal discomfort.

Botanical Identification

Coral Bean is scientifically known as Caesalpinia crista, belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is also called coral tree or flame bean in various regions. A perennial shrub native to tropical Africa, it has compound leaves, bright red flowers, and a fibrous root system. It is sometimes referred to as "Coral Bean" in herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) contains toxic alkaloids and should not be used by pregnant or breastfeeding women. It may cause severe toxicity if misused, including neurological effects and gastrointestinal distress. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider before use.
