Coral Bean

Caesalpinia crista

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Coral Bean is scientifically known as Caesalpinia crista, belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is also called coral tree or flame bean in various regions. A perennial shrub native to tropical Africa, it has compound leaves, bright red flowers, and a fibrous root system. It is sometimes referred to as "Coral Bean" in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Coral Bean contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work together to exert anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their synergistic interactions enhance the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Respiratory Respiratory infection, cough, respiratory infections, chronic cough, respiratory tract infection Antimicrobial, expectorant

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for skin conditions and inflammation.

Powder: Used for digestive problems and as a laxative.

Safety Profile

Coral Bean (Caesalpinia crista) contains toxic alkaloids and should not be used by pregnant or breastfeeding women. It may cause severe toxicity if misused, including neurological effects and gastrointestinal distress. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider before use.
