Tupistra aurantiaca

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tupistra aurantiaca has opposite, elliptic to oblong leaves that are bright green with prominent venation and a slightly coriaceous texture. The plant typically exhibits a perennial growth habit with fleshy stems that support its lush foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often characterized by their delicate structure and subtle coloration. These plants often thrive in shaded, humid environments where their leaf arrangement maximizes light absorption. The overall architecture of the specimen is compact and herbaceous.
