Boerhavia diffusa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. At the most specific levels of its biological classification, it is identified by the genus Boerhavia and the species diffusa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Boehravia diffusa has slender, procumbent, or ascending stems that are often reddish in color and branch extensively from the base. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, characterized by their minute size and lack of prominent petals. The plant often features thin, thread-like pedicels that support small, globose fruits. These morphological traits allow the species to spread effectively across sandy or disturbed soils.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Boerhavia diffusa targets various proteins including cyclooxygenase enzymes, inflammatory cytokines like TNF-alpha, and various peroxisome proliferator-activated receptors to exert its medicinal effects. Its bioactive compounds, such as boeravinones, inhibit the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway by suppressing cyclooxygenase-2 activity. These phytochemicals also modulate signaling pathways involved in oxidative stress by regulating antioxidant enzymes and suppressing pro-inflammatory transcription factors. Furthermore, the plant interacts with hepatic enzymes and glucose metabolism regulators, which contributes to its known hepatoprotective and hypoglycemic properties. This multi-target interaction allows the plant to address complex physiological processes ranging from inflammation to metabolic regulation.
