Sarcandra glabra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sarcandra glabra has a creeping, procumbent perennial habit characterized by slender, smooth stems and small, opposite, lanceolate leaves that lack hairs. The foliage is typically green and relatively thin, providing a delicate texture to the ground cover. Its reproductive structures consist of inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant's morphological identity is largely defined by its glabrous, or hairless, surfaces across its vegetative parts. These specialized features allow it to adapt to its specific ecological niche within its native habitat.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Sarcandra glabra targets various inflammatory enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase (COX), lipoxygenase (LOX), and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS). What are the therapeutic actions of Sarcandra glabra?

Sarcandra glabra has therapeutic properties as a bitter tonic that functions as a digestive stimulant, an appetite stimulant, and a cholagogue. The bitter compounds within the plant stimulate the gustatory receptors to trigger the secretion of gastric juices. This process enhances the overall efficiency of the digestive system and helps resolve sluggishness. Furthermore, its action as a cholagogue promotes the flow and discharge of bile from the gallbladder into the small intestine. These combined effects assist in the breakdown of fats and support healthy metabolic function.
