10 Possible Side Effects Of Sanicula Europaea (Sanicle)

Sanicula europaea has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal irritation, allergic reactions, and increased bleeding risk.

These side effects may be caused by excessive doses or interactions with other medications.

For example, a person with bleeding disorders or taking anticoagulant medications may experience worsening symptoms, such as uncontrolled bleeding, which can lead to organ damage and affect daily life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Sanicula europaea if used imporperly.

1. Increases risk of overdose

Sanicula europaea causes allergic reactions due to the presence of certain compounds such as flavonoids and terpenes.

These bioactive molecules can trigger an immune response in sensitive individuals, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and redness of the skin. Additionally, some people may experience respiratory issues, such as sneezing or congestion, after exposure to Sanicula europaea.

The severity of these reactions can vary depending on individual tolerance and sensitivity.

2. Increases risk of overdose

Sanicula europaea triggers respiratory issues due to its ability to stimulate the respiratory system and cause an increase in bronchial secretions.

This can lead to coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may irritate the lungs and airways, exacerbating conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

3. Increases risk of overdose

Sanicula europaea induces nausea and vomiting.

This side effect is attributed to its high concentration of alkaloids, particularly apiin and sesquiterpenes, which can irritate the stomach lining and cause gastrointestinal disturbances.

Additionally, the plant's saponins may also contribute to these symptoms by altering the gut flora and disrupting digestion.

4. Increases risk of overdose

Sanicula europaea affects digestive system by irritating the mucous membranes in the stomach and intestines.

This can lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal cramps due to its bitter and astringent properties.

The saponins present in sanicle may also cause increased salivation, which can further exacerbate digestive issues.

5. Increases risk of overdose

Sanicula europaea interferes with blood clotting due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

This is primarily attributed to the presence of polyacetylenes, a type of bioactive compound found in the plant's root and leaves. These compounds disrupt the normal functioning of platelets, leading to impaired blood clotting and increased risk of bleeding complications.

As a result, individuals taking Sanicula europaea may experience prolonged or excessive bleeding from cuts or injuries.

6. Increases risk of overdose

Sanicula europaea increases heart rate due to its stimulating properties.

The herb contains alkaloids such as senkirkine and umbelliferone, which can stimulate the cardiovascular system and cause an increase in heart rate. This effect is believed to be related to the herb's ability to act as a beta-adrenergic agonist, mimicking the action of adrenaline on the heart.

As a result, individuals may experience palpitations, rapid heartbeat, or other cardiac-related symptoms when using Sanicula europaea.

7. Increases risk of overdose

Sanicula europaea lowers blood pressure by its ability to dilate blood vessels and reduce peripheral resistance.

This is due to the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids in the plant, which can bind to specific receptors in the cardiovascular system, causing a decrease in blood pressure.

As a result, people may experience dizziness or lightheadedness when using Sanicula europaea, particularly if they are already taking medications that also lower blood pressure.

8. Increases risk of overdose

Sanicula europaea disrupts sleep patterns due to its stimulating properties, which can cause increased alertness and restlessness.

The herb's ability to increase heart rate and blood pressure can further contribute to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep throughout the night.

Additionally, sanicle's bitter taste and aroma may also interfere with a person's normal sleep-wake cycle, leading to poor quality sleep or insomnia.

Sanicula europaea causes skin irritation when used.

This is due to its essential oil, which contains furanocoumarins that can cause phototoxicity and photodermatitis in some individuals. When exposed to sunlight after using the plant, it can lead to redness, itching, and blistering on the skin.

Additionally, the resinous sap of Sanicula europaea may also cause skin irritation due to its allergenic properties, leading to contact dermatitis and other skin reactions.

10. Increases risk of overdose

Sanicula europaea produces dizziness and lightheadedness due to its ability to stimulate blood circulation and lower blood pressure.

The herb's flavonoids and alkaloids work together to increase blood flow to the brain, leading to an initial feeling of giddiness or faintness.

This side effect is usually temporary and may be accompanied by a sense of euphoria or heightened awareness, as the body adjusts to the increased circulation and oxygenation.
