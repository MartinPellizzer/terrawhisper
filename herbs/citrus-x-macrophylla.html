Lemon Tree (Citrus × Macrophylla)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Citrus × macrophylla.

Lemon tree, scientifically known as Citrus × macrophylla, is a citrus fruit-bearing plant widely recognized for its medicinal properties and versatile applications. It is often regarded as a medicinal herb and adaptogen, valued for its ability to support overall well-being and resilience against stress. The primary benefits of lemon tree include its high vitamin C content, which boosts the immune system, and its antimicrobial properties that aid in fighting infections and promoting digestive health. Traditionally, it has been used in various cultures for culinary and therapeutic purposes, such as making herbal teas, remedies for colds, and skincare treatments. In modern wellness practices, lemon tree is incorporated into supplements, essential oils, and natural beauty products, while its unique features like the distinct tangy flavor and the presence of bioflavonoids highlight its historical significance and ongoing relevance in health and wellness.
