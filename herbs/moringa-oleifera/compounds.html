Moringa Tree (Moringa oleifera) Compounds

The Moringa tree contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Phenolic acids and tannins also contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins and terpenoids support detoxification and immune function, while glycosides and coumarin offer additional therapeutic benefits. Essential oils, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further enhance its medicinal value.

Moringa Tree (Moringa oleifera) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Moringa oleifera contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress, supporting immune function and cellular health. They are found in various parts of the plant, including leaves, seeds, and bark, and are known for their potential therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Moringa oleifera, known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's ability to combat oxidative stress and support immune function. This compound is widely studied for its potential health benefits in herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Moringa oleifera, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Tannins in Moringa also help in reducing oxidative stress and supporting digestive health. These properties make tannin an important component in traditional herbal medicine using Moringa.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Moringa oleifera. It exhibits antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Saponins contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating infections and digestive issues. They also enhance nutrient absorption and support immune function.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Moringa oleifera contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. These compounds are found in various parts of the plant, including leaves, seeds, and flowers, and are responsible for many of its therapeutic benefits in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Glycoside

Glycosides in Moringa oleifera contribute to its medicinal properties by supporting immune function and reducing inflammation. These compounds are naturally occurring and are part of the plant's biochemical profile, enhancing its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Moringa oleifera. It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and promoting cardiovascular health. Coumarin's presence supports Moringa's role in herbal medicine for its therapeutic benefits.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil from Moringa oleifera contains bioactive compounds like monoterpenes and sesquiterpenes. It exhibits antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. This oil is used in herbal medicine for skin care, respiratory support, and as a natural preservative. Its volatile nature makes it valuable in aromatherapy and topical applications.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Moringa oleifera include compounds like moringine and sparteine, which have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These alkaloids contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating infections and digestive issues, though more research is needed to confirm their efficacy and safety.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Moringa oleifera. It acts as a powerful antioxidant, helping to neutralize harmful free radicals in the body. This compound contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for supporting overall health.
