10 Possible Side Effects Of Moringa Oleifera (Drumstick)

Moringa oleifera has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, diarrhea, and stomach cramps, due to excessive consumption of its high polyphenol content.

These side effects can worsen people's lives by disrupting daily activities, causing dehydration, and potentially leading to malnutrition.

In severe cases, it can cause an allergic reaction, especially in individuals with pre-existing digestive conditions, leading to chronic health issues and affecting their overall quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Moringa oleifera if used imporperly.

1. Leads to liver toxicity

Moringa oleifera causes allergic reactions due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and saponins.

These substances can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

The allergenic potential is thought to be exacerbated by the plant's ability to produce large amounts of pollen, which can exacerbate respiratory issues in people with pre-existing conditions.

2. Leads to liver toxicity

Moringa oleifera triggers blood sugar spikes due to its high content of sugars and carbohydrates.

The plant's leaves, pods, and seeds are rich in simple sugars like fructose, glucose, and sucrose, which can cause a rapid increase in blood sugar levels. Additionally, moringa contains some amount of starch, which is quickly digested and absorbed by the body, leading to a swift spike in blood sugar.

This natural sweetener has been used for centuries as a traditional remedy, but its high glycemic index may not be suitable for individuals with diabetes or those who are trying to manage their blood sugar levels.

3. Leads to liver toxicity

Moringa oleifera increases anxiety levels due to its high content of alkaloids and glycosides.

These compounds can interact with certain neurotransmitters in the brain, leading to an increase in stress hormones such as cortisol and adrenaline.

This may cause feelings of restlessness, unease, and tension, making it difficult for individuals to relax and calm down.

4. Leads to liver toxicity

Moringa oleifera disrupts digestive system due to its high content of fiber and antioxidants.

The fiber in moringa can cause stomach cramps, bloating, and diarrhea as it moves through the digestive tract, potentially leading to an imbalance of gut bacteria.

Meanwhile, the antioxidants may interact with medications or worsen existing gastrointestinal conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), exacerbating symptoms like abdominal pain, gas, and irregular bowel movements.

5. Leads to liver toxicity

Moringa oleifera interacts with medications.

This is because its bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, may alter the body's ability to metabolize certain drugs. The potent antioxidant properties of moringa can enhance the effects of blood thinners, leading to an increased risk of bleeding complications.

Additionally, its potential to lower blood sugar levels may interact with medications used to treat diabetes, while its ability to stimulate liver function could affect drug metabolism and clearance.

Additionally, moringa's high concentration of omega-3 fatty acids may also contribute to hormonal imbalances.

The plant's leaves, seeds, and flowers contain isothiocyanates, which have been shown to increase the production of thyroid hormones in the body. This side effect highlights the importance of careful consideration when incorporating moringa into one's diet or supplement routine.

These chemicals can interfere with the normal functioning of the kidneys, leading to inflammation and scarring, which can cause permanent damage over time.

Additionally, moringa's high potassium content can put extra strain on already compromised kidneys, further exacerbating the problem.

10. Leads to liver toxicity

Moringa oleifera inhibits iron absorption due to its high levels of polyphenols and flavonoids.

These compounds bind to dietary iron in the gut, preventing it from being absorbed into the bloodstream. This can lead to iron deficiency anemia in individuals who already have low iron intake or those with pre-existing iron deficiency conditions.

The inhibitory effect on iron absorption may be exacerbated by the high concentration of phenolic acids and flavonoids found in moringa leaves, further complicating iron metabolism.
