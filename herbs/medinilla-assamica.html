What's the taxonomical classification of Medinilla assamica?

Medinilla assamica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the species is placed in the order Myrtales and falls under the family Melastomataceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Medinilla, with its specific designation being the species assamica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Medinilla assamica has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect, branched stems that typically reach heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are arranged in an alternate pattern and consist of trifoliate leaflets with ovate to elliptic shapes and serrated margins. Small, papilionaceous flowers emerge from the axils, displaying colors that range from white to pale pink. The reproductive structures culminate in small, elongated legume pods that contain the plant's seeds.
