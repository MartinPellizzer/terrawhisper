Hoarea Incrassata

Hoarea incrassata, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a traditional medicinal plant widely used in various African countries for its diverse therapeutic properties.

It has been traditionally employed to treat ailments such as diabetes, inflammation, and digestive disorders due to its rich content of bioactive compounds like tannins, flavonoids, and saponins. Studies have shown that extracts from H. incrassata exhibit antidiabetic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, making it a promising candidate for natural medicine. The plant is often prepared as a decoction or poultice, and its leaves and bark are the most commonly used parts.

Due to its pharmacological potential, further research is needed to fully explore its applications in modern medicine.
