Adenophora liliifolioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Adenophora liliifolioides?

Adenophora liliifolioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further classified under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Campanulaceae, specifically placed within the genus Adenophora, and is identified by its unique species name, liliifolioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora liliifolioides has lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins, a woody base, and small, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that typically range from purple to light violet in color. The foliage is characterized by a somewhat fleshy texture and is arranged oppositely or sub-oppositely along the slender, upright stems. Its inflorescence consists of small clusters or solitary flowers emerging from the leaf axils. The plant develops a perennial rootstock that supports its herbaceous growth habit throughout the season. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the Campanulaceae family.
