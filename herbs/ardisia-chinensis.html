Ardisia chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ardisia chinensis?

Ardisia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is part of the order Ericales and the family Primulaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Ardisia, with its specific designation being the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia chinensis has an evergreen shrub or small tree habit characterized by simple, leathery, opposite leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape with entire margins. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Small, white, fragrant flowers emerge in axillary clusters, featuring a tubular corolla. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy, globose drupes that transition from green to a bright red or orange color when ripe. The plant's woody stems are sturdy and contribute to its dense, bushy growth pattern.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ardisia chinensis?

Ardisia chinensis has therapeutic actions that include bitter and astringent properties used to treat gastrointestinal and febrile conditions. Its bitter components stimulate digestion and improve appetite through the stimulation of gastric secretions. The astringent qualities of the plant make it an effective antidiarrheal agent for managing loose stools and intestinal inflammation. Additionally, the plant functions as an antipyretic to help reduce elevated body temperatures during illness. These combined effects also provide an anti-inflammatory response to soothe internal swelling and irritation.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains 4-hydroxy-3-methoxy-benzoic acid, catechin, gallic acid, gallic acid ethyl ester, and salicylic acid. These specific medicinal compounds provide the plant with a diverse range of biological activities. For instance, the presence of gallic acid and its ethyl ester derivative contributes significant antioxidant properties. Catechin is well known for its ability to assist in various cellular protection processes. Additionally, salicylic acid offers natural anti-inflammatory benefits that are highly valued in traditional medicine. Together, these phytochemicals form a complex chemical profile beneficial to human health.

As discussed in a study, seven compounds were obtained from the ethyl acetate fraction of ethanol extract of Ardisia chinensis. These 7 compounds were characterized by spectroscopic analysis and comparison with published data. The identified constituents include salicylic acid (1), 4-hydroxy-3-methoxy-benzoic acid (2), gallic acid ethyl ester (3), 4-hydroxy-3,5-dimethoxy benzoic acid methyl ester (4), protocatechuic acid (5), gallic acid (6) and catechin (7). All of these 7 compounds were isolated from this plant for the first time. Furthermore, compound 7 was obtained from this genus for the first time.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating viral infections including the Coxsackie B3 virus and the duck hepatitis B virus. Researchers have investigated its potential to inhibit the replication of these specific pathogens in laboratory settings. The plant contains bioactive compounds that demonstrate significant antiviral activity against various strains. These properties suggest that its extracts could serve as a foundation for developing new therapeutic interventions. Such studies focus on how the phytochemicals interact with viral proteins to prevent cellular damage.

As discussed in a study, Ardisia chinensis Benth is a medicinal plant used in the Yao minority area of Southern China. Both the aqueous extract and the 95% ethanol extract of Ardisia chinensis showed in vitro antiviral activity against Coxsackie B3 virus, with the aqueous extract being more potent. The antiviral activity was attributed to a polysaccharide, specifically the Ardisia chinensis polysaccharide (ACP) fractionated from the aqueous extract. This ACP exhibited a 50% inhibitory concentration (IC50) of 3.9 microg/mL and a selective index (SI) over 256. The average molecular weights of ACP were determined to be 40037 Da (Mw), 28297 Da (Mn), and 33758 Da (Mp).
