Tectaria zeilanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Tectaria zeilanica?

Tectaria zeilanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the order Polypodiales, the plant is further categorized into the family Polypodiaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Tectaria, with its unique designation being the species zeilanica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Tectaria zeilanica contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its pharmacological potential. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Research suggests that the presence of specific terpenoids may offer anti-inflammatory benefits in traditional medicinal applications. Additionally, the complex profile of phenolic compounds assists in neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. These diverse medicinal compounds make the plant a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tectaria zeilanica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wound healing, digestive ailments, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves and roots are often prepared as topical applications to soothe dermatological issues like rashes or small cuts. When consumed as a decoction, it can help alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort and stomach upsets. Its medicinal properties are also frequently utilized to reduce swelling and manage pain associated with systemic inflammation. Local traditional practices rely on its diverse chemical profile to address a wide range of minor physical ailments.
