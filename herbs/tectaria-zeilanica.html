Tectaria zeilanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Tectaria zeilanica?

Tectaria zeilanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the order Polypodiales, the plant is further categorized into the family Polypodiaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Tectaria, with its unique designation being the species zeilanica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tectaria zeiplanica has a creeping rhizomatous habit with pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of 2 to 4 pairs of pinnae. Each leaflet is elliptic or oblong in shape and features entire margins with acute or obtuse apices. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are borne on short stalks near the leaf axils. These flowers eventually develop into small, globose fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. The overall structure allows the plant to spread effectively across damp, shaded substrates in its natural habitat.
