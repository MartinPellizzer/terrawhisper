Sibbaldia procumbens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is further categorized under the family Rosaceae, which places it among a diverse group of flowering plants. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Sibbaldia and its unique species designation, procumbens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sibbaldia procumbens has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats with small, oval, bright green leaves that are entire and opposite. These leaves typically measure between 1 and 3 centimeters in length and possess a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellow, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. Each flower features five distinct petals and a central cluster of numerous stamens. As the plant spreads, it develops a shallow root system that allows it to colonize open ground effectively.
