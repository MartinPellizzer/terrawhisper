Sibbaldia procumbens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sibbaldia procumbens has prostrate, creeping stems that form dense mats with small, oval, bright green leaves that are entire and opposite. These leaves typically measure between 1 and 3 centimeters in length and possess a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellow, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. Each flower features five distinct petals and a central cluster of numerous stamens. As the plant spreads, it develops a shallow root system that allows it to colonize open ground effectively.

      What are the herbal preparations of Sibbaldia procumbens?

      Sibbaldia procumbens is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using the dried or fresh aerial parts of the plant. The dried leaves and stems are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid medicine used for internal consumption. In some traditional practices, a tea is brewed from the plant to harness its specific botanical properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Sibbaldia procumbens can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized redness if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive areas of the body. While there is limited clinical data regarding systemic toxicity, some individuals may experience unexpected reactions upon ingestion. Dermatological sensitivity is the primary concern for those handling the foliage or stems. It is advisable to use gloves when gardening with this species to prevent potential rashes. Always consult a medical professional if a reaction occurs after contact.
