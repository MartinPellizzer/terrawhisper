What's the taxonomical classification of Russelia equisetiformis?

Russelia equisetiformis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is positioned in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Lamiales and the family Plantaginaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Russelia, with its specific designation being the species equisetiformis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Russelia equisetiformis has narrow, linear, needle-like leaves that are typically between 1 and 3 inches long and arranged oppositely or in whorls along slender, arching stems. These foliage structures are bright green and possess a texture similar to horsetail, which gives the plant its specific epithet. The stems are woody at the base and can grow into sprawling or upright shrubs reaching several feet in height. Small, tubular, bright red flowers emerge from the leaf axils and are densely clustered on long, thin spikes. This unique combination of fine greenery and vibrant blossoms creates a delicate, airy appearance in the landscape.
