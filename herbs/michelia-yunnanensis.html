Michelia yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Michelia yunnanensis?

Michelia yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Magnoliales. It is a member of the family Magnoliaceae and falls under the genus Michelia, ultimately being identified by its specific species name, yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Michelia yunnanensis has elliptical to oblong leaves that measure approximately 10 to 20 centimeters in length and are arranged in an alternate pattern along its woody branches. The foliage is characterized by a glossy, dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside with entire margins. During its flowering period, the plant produces large, solitary, fragrant blossoms that transition from a creamy white to a pale yellow hue. These flowers emerge from terminal or axillary positions and feature numerous tepals arranged in a spiral formation. The mature fruit consists of woody follicles that typically develop in pairs or small clusters.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical regions of southern China, specifically found in provinces such as Yunnan, Guangxi, Guangdong, Hainan, and Guizhou. Within these territories, it typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils located along hillsides or in forest margins. Its distribution is closely linked to the humid climates and moderate elevations characteristic of these southeastern Asian landscapes. Some specimens may also extend into neighboring parts of northern Vietnam depending on the local habitat availability. These specific environmental conditions define the narrow ecological niche where the species can successfully propagate in the wild.

What are the pharmacological activities of Michelia yunnanensis?

This plant has significant antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antipyretic pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents, particularly essential oils and flavonoids, contribute to its ability to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. These bioactive compounds also help neutralize free radicals, which reduces oxidative stress within biological systems. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates a capacity to suppress inflammatory responses and lower body temperature during febrile conditions. Such diverse therapeutic properties make it a subject of interest for developing new natural medicinal agents.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", the plant Michelia yunnanensis was identified as one of the most active extracts among 30 Chinese medicinal plants evaluated. The extracts were prepared using 80% ethanol and tested against clinical pathogens such as Staphylococcus aureus, Escherichia coli, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, and Candida albicans. Michelia yunnanensis exhibited minimum inhibitory concentrations (MICs) ranging from 8 to 64 μg/mL. These results align with the traditional applications of the plant for treating skin and other infections.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds bornyl acetate, camphor, caryophyllene oxide, cineole, and linalool. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's diverse therapeutic properties and aromatic profile. Bornyl acetate and linalool are often recognized for their soothing effects on the nervous system. The presence of camphor and cineole provides significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory benefits. Furthermore, caryophyllene oxide plays a crucial role in the plant's overall chemical complexity and biological activity.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", forty-five compounds were identified in the essential oil extracted from Michelia yunnanensis Franch. The chemical constituents were analyzed using GC/MS to determine their specific contents. The main compounds with high contents identified in the plant include Bornyl acetate at 12.44%. Other significant components consist of Camphor at 8.775, Caryophylle oxide at 6.93%, and Bornyl at 5.97%. Additionally, the analysis identified Cineole at 4.57% and Linalool at 1.89%.
