Rhaphiolepis umbellata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhaphiolepis umbellata has small, leathery, evergreen leaves that are elliptical to obovate in shape, typically measuring 1 to 3 centimeters in length with finely serrated margins and a dark green upper surface. The foliage possesses a thick, waxy cuticle that provides a glossy appearance and helps the plant retain moisture. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody, spreading branches that form a dense, mounded shrubby habit. During its blooming period, the plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers that emerge from terminal buds. The overall structure is characterized by compact, stiff growth that maintains a rounded silhouette throughout the year.

    What are the herbal preparations of Rhaphiolepis umbellata?

    Rhaphiolepis umbellata is prepared as a decoction, a topical paste, or a concentrated liquid extract for medicinal use.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Rhaphiolepis umbellata can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if a person touches its leaves or stems. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or a mild rash upon physical contact with the plant material. While it is not widely documented as highly toxic, ingestion of the foliage or berries could potentially lead to gastrointestinal discomfort. It is best to avoid consuming any part of the plant to prevent unexpected digestive upset. Wearing gloves while pruning or handling the shrub can help mitigate these minor dermatological risks.
