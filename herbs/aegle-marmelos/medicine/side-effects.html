10 Possible Side Effects Of Aegle Marmelos

Aegle marmelos has some side effects when used improperly, such as stomach pain, nausea, and diarrhea due to excessive consumption of its seeds, leaves, and bark.

These side effects can lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalance, which may worsen the condition of people with underlying health issues, like heart disease or diabetes.

It can also cause allergic reactions, skin irritation, and respiratory issues, negatively impacting daily life and work productivity.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Aegle marmelos causes allergic reactions.

This is attributed to the presence of certain compounds such as flavonoids and terpenes in its extracts, which can stimulate an immune response and trigger an allergic reaction in some individuals.

Additionally, the fruit's high concentration of antioxidants and polyphenols may also contribute to the development of allergic symptoms.

2. Triggers anaphylaxis attacks

Aegle marmelos triggers anaphylaxis attacks due to the presence of specific proteins and allergens in its leaves, bark, and seeds.

When ingested or comes into contact with the skin, these substances can cause a severe and potentially life-threatening allergic reaction in susceptible individuals.

The severity of the reaction depends on the individual's sensitivity to these allergens, which can vary greatly from person to person.

3. Induces vomiting episodes

Aegle marmelos induces vomiting episodes due to its inherent bitter and astringent properties.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as limonoids and flavonoids, can stimulate the digestive system, leading to increased stomach contractions and ultimately resulting in nausea and vomiting.

Additionally, Aegle marmelos' ability to alter gut motility and secretions may also contribute to its emetic effects, causing individuals to experience unpleasant symptoms.

4. Produces diarrhea outbreaks

Aegle marmelos produces diarrhea outbreaks due to its ability to stimulate bowel movements and increase gut motility.

The fruit's active compounds, such as alkaloids and glycosides, can irritate the stomach lining and intestines, leading to an increase in water secretion and a rapid transit of food through the digestive system. This can result in loose and frequent stools, or diarrhea.

The intensity and duration of these effects vary depending on individual tolerance and dosage.

5. Increases blood pressure levels

Aegle marmelos increases blood pressure levels due to its inherent ability to constrict blood vessels and stimulate the heart rate.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and glycosides, are believed to play a key role in this effect. As a result, individuals who consume Aegle marmelos may experience an increase in their systolic and diastolic blood pressure readings.

This side effect is thought to be more pronounced in people with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions.

6. Lowers sugar levels drastically

Aegle marmelos lowers sugar levels drastically.

This side effect is attributed to the presence of flavonoids and alkaloids in its extract, which have been shown to stimulate insulin secretion and increase glucose uptake in the body. Additionally, Aegle marmelos has been found to inhibit the activity of carbohydrate-metabolizing enzymes, further contributing to the decrease in blood sugar levels.

As a result, individuals with diabetes or hypoglycemia may experience significant drops in their blood sugar levels when using Aegle marmelos.

8. Activates bleeding disorders

Aegle marmelos activates bleeding disorders due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and alter blood coagulation.

The plant contains coumarin and related compounds that can interact with the body's natural blood clotting mechanisms, potentially leading to prolonged bleeding times and increased risk of hemorrhage.

This side effect may be more pronounced in individuals with pre-existing bleeding disorders or those taking anticoagulant medications.

9. Triggers thyroid problems

Aegle marmelos triggers thyroid problems due to its potential interaction with thyroid hormones.

The plant's bioactive compounds may bind to thyroid receptors, altering the body's natural thyroid function and leading to issues such as hyperthyroidism or hypothyroidism.

Additionally, Aegle marmelos has been found to contain phytoestrogens, which can mimic estrogen in the body, potentially disrupting thyroid hormone regulation and exacerbating thyroid problems.

10. Reduces fertility rates

Additionally, Aegle marmelos' antifertility properties can cause hormonal imbalances, interfering with ovulation and fertilization processes.

As a result, individuals using this herb may experience difficulty conceiving or maintaining a healthy pregnancy.
