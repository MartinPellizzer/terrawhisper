Botanical Identification of Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos)

To identify Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Bael Tree, or Aegle marmelos, is classified under the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Rutaceae, genus Aegle, and species Aegle marmelos. This classification reflects its position within the plant kingdom and its botanical relationships.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The Bael Tree is scientifically known as Aegle marmelos, a name derived from the Greek words "aegle" meaning apple and "marmelos" referring to its edible fruit. This name reflects its historical significance as a fruit-bearing tree known since ancient times.

Morphological Characteristics

The Bael tree is a deciduous tree that grows up to 15 meters tall. It has a deep taproot system with lateral roots, providing stability. The stem is smooth, greyish-brown, and becomes fissured with age. The leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a large, spiny capsule, and the seeds are enclosed in a fibrous aril.

Diagnostic Identification Features

The Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) can be distinguished by its smooth, grayish-brown bark with longitudinal fissures, opposite leaf arrangement, and distinctive yellow-orange fruit with a thick, leathery rind. It differs from similar species like Terminalia chebula by its larger, more fleshy fruit and different leaf shape. The presence of a short, stout stem and the absence of thorns also help in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

The Bael Tree is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly India, Bangladesh, and Nepal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. It prefers well-drained clay or loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes ranging from 200 to 1,200 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

The Bael tree blooms from March to May, producing yellowish-green flowers. Seed formation occurs from June to August, with fruits developing over several months. Harvesting of mature fruits typically happens from October to December, when the fruit is fully ripe and ready for identification and use in herbal medicine.

Phylogenetic Relationships

The Bael tree (Aegle marmelos) is closely related to species within the genus Aegle, particularly Aegle marmelos var. ovalis. It belongs to the family Rutaceae, sharing characteristics such as opposite leaves and fragrant flowers with other members of the family. Its phylogenetic position reflects its close evolutionary ties to other citrus-like plants.
