Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) Compounds

The Bael Tree contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Tannins contribute to its astringent effects, while coumarin is known for its anticoagulant properties. Phenolic acids, saponins, glycosides, and terpenoids also play roles in its medicinal benefits. Essential oils, alkaloids, and anthocyanins add to its therapeutic potential.

Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in the Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the tree's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. They also help in reducing oxidative stress and enhancing immune function.

2. Tannin

Tannin in the Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and aiding digestion. Tannins also provide antioxidant benefits, supporting the tree's traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in the Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos). It contributes to the tree's medicinal properties, particularly in anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant effects. Found in the fruit and bark, coumarin is used in traditional herbal medicine for its therapeutic benefits.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in the Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos). It contributes to the tree's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Bael Tree extracts.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in the Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin is commonly used in traditional herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments. Its presence supports the therapeutic value of Bael Tree in Ayurvedic medicine.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in the Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in digestive and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in Bael help in treating ailments like indigestion and inflammation by supporting gut health and reducing oxidative stress.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in the Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) are a class of bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the tree's traditional use in treating digestive disorders and skin conditions. They are found in various parts of the plant, including the fruit and bark, and are responsible for many of its therapeutic effects.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of the Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) contains bioactive compounds like limonene and myrcene. These oils are used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They are extracted through distillation and applied in formulations for digestive and skin ailments.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in the Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos) are bioactive compounds known for their pharmacological properties. They contribute to the tree's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. These alkaloids exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's role in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in the Bael Tree (Aegle marmelos), is a flavonoid responsible for the fruit's vibrant color. It exhibits strong antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports overall health by protecting cells from damage and may contribute to the tree's traditional use in herbal medicine.
