Dichocarpum basilare

What's the taxonomical classification of Dichocarpum basilare?

Dichocarpum basilare belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically placed within the genus Dichocarpum, with its distinct identity defined by the species name basilare.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichocarpum basilare has a prostrate growth habit characterized by creeping, branched rhizomes and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a distichous pattern.

The leaves are relatively thin and possess entire margins with acute tips. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous sporophytes that emerge from the stem. The capsule is characteristically short and features a distinctively basal attachment point.

These morphological features collectively distinguish it from other similar species within the genus.

        What are the herbal preparations of Dichocarpum basilare?

        Dichocarpum basilare is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a paste for various medicinal applications. The plant material is often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid infusion used for internal consumption.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Dichocarpum basilare can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically. The specific compounds within the plant may trigger localized redness or itching upon contact with sensitive areas. If consumed, individuals might experience nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting due to the plant's chemical composition. Some people may also exhibit signs of hypersensitivity, such as swelling or respiratory discomfort. Careful monitoring is necessary when interacting with any part of this species to avoid adverse health outcomes.
