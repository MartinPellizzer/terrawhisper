Dichocarpum basilare

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichocarpum basilare has a prostrate growth habit characterized by creeping, branched rhizomes and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a distichous pattern. The leaves are relatively thin and possess entire margins with acute tips. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous sporophytes that emerge from the stem. The capsule is characteristically short and features a distinctively basal attachment point. These morphological features collectively distinguish it from other similar species within the genus.
