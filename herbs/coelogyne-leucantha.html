Coelogyne leucantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coelogyne leucantha has a sympodial growth habit characterized by fleshy, elongated pseudobulbs that terminate in a single, apical leaf. These thick pseudobulbs serve as primary water storage organs to help the plant survive periods of drought. From the base of each pseudobulb, thin and wiry roots emerge to cling to host surfaces in its natural epiphytic habitat. The inflorescence produces multiple small, pure white flowers that feature a distinctively shaped lip and a waxy texture. Each bloom is held on a slender raceme that emerges from the apex of the mature pseudobulb.
