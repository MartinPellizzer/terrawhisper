Philadelphus henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Philadelphus henryi has deciduous, opposite leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptical in shape, measuring approximately 3 to 7 centimeters in length with finely serrated margins and acute tips.

The plant typically grows as a multi-stemmed shrub reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its bark is smooth and greyish, providing a subtle contrast to the dense green foliage. During the flowering season, it produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that emit a distinct citrus-like fragrance.

The stems are somewhat arching, contributing to a rounded and bushy growth habit.
