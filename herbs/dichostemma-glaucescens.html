Dichostemma glaucescens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichostemma glaucescens has succulent, cylindrical to slightly compressed leaves that are densely covered in a glaucous, waxy coating giving them a blue-gray appearance. These leaves are typically arranged in compact, overlapping clusters along woody stems to minimize water loss. The plant exhibits a low-growing, shrubby habit with small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Its root system is adapted to arid environments, allowing it to anchor firmly in rocky or sandy substrates. The overall structure is designed for high drought tolerance through its thickened, water-storing tissues.
