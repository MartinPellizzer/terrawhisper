Arthromeris mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Arthromeris mairei?

Arthromeris mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Within this taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the family Polypodiaceae, specifically within the genus Arthromeris, and is identified by the species name mairei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arthromeris mairei has a woody, perennial subshrub habit characterized by densely branched, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves.

The foliage consists of minute, imbricate leaves that tightly hug the stems, giving the plant a compact and textured appearance. These stems often exhibit a somewhat decumbent or spreading growth pattern depending on the specific environmental conditions. The fine, needle-like structure of the leaves is an adaptation to arid environments, helping to minimize moisture loss.

Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced within the axillary regions of the stem.

        What are the herbal preparations of Arthromeris mairei?

        Arthromeris mairei is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using the dried aerial parts of the plant.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Arthromeris mairei can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may trigger redness, itching, or painful rashes on sensitive skin. If accidental consumption occurs, individuals might experience nausea, stomach cramping, or vomiting. These adverse reactions are often linked to the specific chemical compounds present within the plant's tissues. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when handling the species in a natural environment.
