Arthromeris mairei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arthromeris mairei has a woody, perennial subshrub habit characterized by densely branched, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves. The foliage consists of minute, imbricate leaves that tightly hug the stems, giving the plant a compact and textured appearance. These stems often exhibit a somewhat decumbent or spreading growth pattern depending on the specific environmental conditions. The fine, needle-like structure of the leaves is an adaptation to arid environments, helping to minimize moisture loss. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced within the axillary regions of the stem.
