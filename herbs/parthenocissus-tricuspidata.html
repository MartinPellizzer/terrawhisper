Parthenocissus tricuspidata

What's the taxonomical classification of Parthenocissus tricuspidata?

Parthenociscus tricuspidata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Vitales and the family Vitaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Parthenocissus, and its unique species designation, tricuspidata.

What are the therapeutic actions of Parthenocissus tricuspidata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including chronic bronchitis, asthma, coughs, and other respiratory conditions characterized by excessive mucus. It contains compounds that act as bronchodilators to help open the airways during breathing difficulties. The plant also possesses antitussive properties that help suppress persistent coughing fits. Some traditional medicinal practices utilize its extracts to alleviate symptoms of common colds and congestion. It is primarily valued for its ability to soothe irritated mucous membranes in the throat and lungs.
