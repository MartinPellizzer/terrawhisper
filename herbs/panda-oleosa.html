Panda oleosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Panda oleosa?

Panda oleosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated under the order Malpighiales. The plant is further categorized into the family Pandaceae and the genus Panda, ultimately reaching its specific taxonomic designation as the species oleosa.

What are the pharmacological activities of Panda oleosa?

This plant has antihyperglycemic and hypoglycemic pharmacological activities that allow it to effectively regulate and lower blood glucose levels in biological systems. These properties are primarily attributed to the presence of bioactive secondary metabolites within its chemical profile. Research indicates that its extracts can significantly reduce elevated blood sugar, making it a potential candidate for managing diabetes. By inhibiting specific enzymes or improving insulin sensitivity, the plant assists in maintaining glucose homeostasis. Consequently, its medicinal application offers a natural approach to treating metabolic imbalances related to high sugar concentrations.

According to a study published by "International journal of pharmaceuics", the aqueous extract of the stem bark of the medicinal plant Panda oleosa was used to synthesize green zinc oxide nanoparticles. These green synthesized nanoparticles possessed a hydrodynamic diameter of 228.8 ± 3.0 nm and a UV absorption peak at 354 nm. Phenolic compounds accounted for 2.58 ± 0.04% of these green nanoparticles, which contributed to their stability. In an in vivo study using an oral glucose tolerance test, these green synthesized nanoparticles displayed a significant hypoglycemic effect compared to chemically synthesized ones. This increased efficiency in reducing hyperglycemia is attributed to phytocompounds like catechins and polyphenols attached to the nanoparticle surface.
