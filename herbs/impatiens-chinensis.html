Impatiens chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens chinensis?

Impatiens chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its lineage through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Balsaminaceae, specifically falling under the genus Impatiens, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens chinensis has an erect, succulent annual herb stem that typically reaches a height of 20 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are simple, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and feature serrated margins with a bright green color. Small, zygomorphic flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a delicate pink or pale purple hue. These blossoms possess a characteristic spur at the base of the petals which contains nectar. The plant also produces distinctive capsule-shaped fruits that explode upon maturity to disperse seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East Asia, specifically distributed throughout China, Japan, Korea, and Taiwan. It typically flourishes in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks within these subtropical and temperate regions. Some populations have also been observed spreading into parts of Southeast Asia where the climate remains humid. The species favors low-lying areas with consistent water availability to support its succulent stems. Its range is largely defined by these specific moisture-rich ecological niches across the Asian continent.
