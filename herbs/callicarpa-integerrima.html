Callicarpa integerrima

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa integerrima?

Callicarpa integerrima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. Further down the classification, it is a member of the family Lamiaceae, falling under the genus Callicarpa, and is specifically identified by its species name, integerrima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa integerrima has opposite, simple, entire-margined leaves that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape and possess a smooth, non-serrated edge. The foliage typically displays a lustrous green surface with distinct pinnate venation. Its stems are woody and can exhibit a somewhat quadrangular structure in younger growth. Small, axillary clusters of flowers eventually develop into spherical, fleshy drupes. These berries often transition from green to a vibrant purple or red hue when mature.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of Southern China, specifically occurring in provinces such as Guangdong, Guangxi, Hainan, and Yunnan. Its distribution is largely centered around warm, humid environments where it can thrive in forest understories. Within these habitats, it is often found in hilly or mountainous terrain characterized by moderate rainfall. The species remains localized to these specific East Asian ecological zones rather than spreading globally. This restricted range highlights its dependence on the unique climatic conditions found in southern Chinese territories.

What are the pharmacological activities of Callicarpa integerrima?

This plant has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, specifically demonstrating anti-MRSAs activity and inhibitory effects on NLRP3 inflammasome activation. The bioactive compounds found within Callicarpa integerrima serve to suppress the inflammatory response by targeting specific molecular pathways. Through its NLRP3 inflammasome inhibitory activity, the plant helps regulate the body's immune reactions to prevent excessive tissue damage. Additionally, its antimicrobial capabilities allow it to combat various pathogenic microorganisms effectively. These diverse pharmacological actions highlight the significant therapeutic potential of this species in medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, twelve new clerodane diterpenoids named callicarpanes A-L (1-12) and eight known compounds (13-20) were isolated from Callicarpa integerrima. Compounds 1, 3, 5, 9, 10, 12, 15, 16, and 19 demonstrated significant inhibitory activity for NLRP3 inflammasome activation. The IC50 against lactate dehydrogenase (LDH) release for these compounds ranged from 0.08 to 4.78 μM. Additionally, compound 10 repressed IL-1β secretion and caspase-1 maturation in J774A.1 cells. This specific compound also blocked macrophage pyroptosis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds betulinic acid, oleanolic acid, ursolic acid, 2alpha-hydroxy-oleanolic acid, and 2alpha-hydroxy-ursolic acid. These specific pentacyclic triterpenoids are recognized for their diverse biological activities. Many of these substances exhibit significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties in various studies. Research suggests that these compounds may also play roles in regulating cellular processes and supporting immune health. Scientists continue to investigate how these distinct molecular structures interact with human physiological systems.

As discussed in a study, Callicarpa integerrima Champ. contains a new triterpenoid saponin identified as 2alpha, 3beta, 19alpha, 23-tetrahydroxy-olean-12-en-28-oic acid-28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1 --> 4)-beta-D-glucopyranoside (1). The methanol extract of the stems and leaves also yielded 14 known triterpenoids. These include oleanolic acid (2), 3-acetyl oleanolic acid (3), 3beta-O-acetyl ursolic acid (4), 2alpha-hydroxy-ursolic acid (5), 2alpha, 3beta, 19alpha, 23-tetrahydroxy-urs-12-en-28-oic acid (6), alpha-amyrin-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (7), pomolic acid (8), betulinic acid (9), ursolic acid (10), 2alpha, 3beta, 19alpha, 23-tetrahydroxy-olean-12-en-28-oic acid (arjungenin) (11), 2alpha-hydroxy-oleanolic acid (12), hederagenin (13), 2alpha, 19alpha-dihydroxy-ursolic acid (14) and pruvuloside A (15). Within this group, compounds 3, 4 and 15 were isolated from the genus Callicarpa L. for the first time.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and localized swelling if its juices come into contact with human skin. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness or itching upon touching the foliage or stems. While there is limited data on systemic toxicity, ingestion of any part of the plant could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress.
