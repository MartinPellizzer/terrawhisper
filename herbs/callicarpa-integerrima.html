Callicarpa integerrima

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa integerrima?

Callicarpa integerrima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. Further down the classification, it is a member of the family Lamiaceae, falling under the genus Callicarpa, and is specifically identified by its species name, integerrima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa integerrima has opposite, simple, entire-margined leaves that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape and possess a smooth, non-serrated edge.

The foliage typically displays a lustrous green surface with distinct pinnate venation. Its stems are woody and can exhibit a somewhat quadrangular structure in younger growth. Small, axillary clusters of flowers eventually develop into spherical, fleshy drupes.

These berries often transition from green to a vibrant purple or red hue when mature.
