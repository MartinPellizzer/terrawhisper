Jasminum subhumile

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum subhumile?

Jasminum subhumile belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually placing it in the order Lamiales. As a member of the family Oleaceae, it is categorized under the genus Jasminum, with its specific taxonomic designation being the species subhumile.

      What are the herbal preparations of Jasminum subhumile?

      Jasminum subhumile is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using the dried leaves and stems of the plant. The dried plant material is often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal medicinal purposes. When used as a tea, the leaves are steeped in hot water to extract delicate volatile oils and active compounds. For skin conditions, the fresh leaves can be crushed into a fine paste and applied directly to the affected area. These various preparations allow the plant's therapeutic properties to be utilized for both systemic and localized treatments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Jasminum subhumile can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or digestive upset if ingested or applied improperly. While many species in the jasmine family are used for fragrance, certain compounds may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves can result in redness, itching, or a localized rash on the skin. If consumed accidentally, the plant may lead to nausea, stomach cramps, or other gastrointestinal disturbances. Always consult a medical professional if an adverse reaction occurs after exposure.
