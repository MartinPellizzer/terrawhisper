Spanish Daffodil (Narcissus × Andorranus)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Narcissus × andorranus.

Spanish daffodil, scientifically known as Narcissus × andorranus, is a flowering plant prized not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its medicinal properties. This plant, often considered a medicinal herb and adaptogen, has been used for centuries to support physical and mental well-being. Its primary benefits include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-boosting effects, making it valuable in traditional remedies for ailments such as respiratory issues and stress. In traditional Spanish and Mediterranean cultures, the Spanish daffodil was used to treat coughs, digestive problems, and as a tonic for vitality. Today, it is gaining attention in modern wellness practices for its potential role in natural health supplements, with its unique bioactive compounds, such as certain alkaloids, contributing to its therapeutic reputation.
