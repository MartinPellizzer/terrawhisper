Scutellaria discolor

What's the taxonomical classification of Scutellaria discolor?

Scutelariia discolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Lamiaceae, falling under the genus Scutellaria, and is specifically identified by its species name, discolor.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutellaria discolor has opposite, decussate leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape with a distinctively paler, almost white underside compared to the darker green upper surface. The stems are typically square in cross-section and exhibit a woody texture as the plant matures. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that are often arranged in verticillasters along the upper nodes of the stem. The calyx is characterized by five unequal teeth, while the corolla displays a subtle coloration typical of the Lamiaceae family. These morphological features collectively assist in the plant's identification within its natural habitat.
