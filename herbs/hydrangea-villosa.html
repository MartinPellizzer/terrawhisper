What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrangea villosa?

Hydrangea villosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy through the order Cornales, it falls into the family Hydrangeaceae. Finally, the plant is identified under the genus Hydrangea, with its specific designation being the species villosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrangea villosa has woody, deciduous stems that produce large, oppositely arranged ovate leaves characterized by a dense covering of fine, soft hairs on both surfaces. These leaves typically measure several inches in length and feature serrated margins and prominent venation. The plant produces large, terminal corymbs of flowers that transition from creamy white to soft pink as they age. Each individual flower consists of fertile blossoms surrounded by showy, sterile sepals that provide much of the plant's visual impact. The overall structure is a multi-stemmed shrub that can reach significant heights in its natural habitat.
