What's the taxonomical classification of Anemone liangshanica?

Anemone liaangshanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This species is a part of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically situated within the genus Aneomone, and is identified by its unique species designation, liaangshanica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone liangshanica has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are broadly ovate to cordate with deeply lobed or coarsely serrated margins.

Each flowering stem culminates in a single, solitary flower featuring multiple delicate petals or petaloid sepals that surround a central cluster of numerous stamens. The reproductive structure includes a central head of many pistils, which eventually develops into a cluster of small achenes.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Anemone liangshanica can cause skin irritation, redness, and blistering upon contact with the skin or mucous membranes. Like many members of the Ranunculaceae family, this plant likely contains protoanemonin, a toxic chemical that acts as a severe irritant. If ingested, the plant may lead to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Accidental contact with the eyes could result in significant inflammation and temporary vision impairment. Therefore, extreme caution should be exercised when handling this species to avoid physical injury.
