Saurauia punduana

What's the taxonomical classification of Saurauia punduana?

Saurauia punduana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Actinidiaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Saurauia, ultimately reaching its scientific designation at the species level as Saurauia punduana.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat stomach aches, diarrhea, and various digestive ailments. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to soothe gastrointestinal distress and promote bowel regularity. Local healers often prepare extracts from the leaves or stems to address these internal issues. Beyond digestive problems, its medicinal applications may extend to other minor inflammatory conditions.
