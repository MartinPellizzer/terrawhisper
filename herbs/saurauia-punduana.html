Saurauia punduana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saurauia punduana has large, broadly ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and acute apices that are arranged oppositely along its woody stems. The leaf surfaces are typically glabrous or sparsely pubescent, displaying prominent pinnate venation that extends toward the edges. Its inflorescences consist of small, actinomorphic flowers clustered in cymes that emerge from the leaf axils. The reproductive structures include five distinct petals and numerous stamens characteristic of the Saurauiaceae family. Small, fleshy drupes develop from these flowers following successful pollination.
