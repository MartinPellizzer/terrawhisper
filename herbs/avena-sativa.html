Oat (Avena sativa)

Oat (Avena sativa) is a member of the Poaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North America. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and shoots have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, tonic, and sedative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and native american herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
Typical height ranges from 100 to 150 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface green and lower surface gray-green, exhibiting stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence composed of spikelets arranged in a panicle, each spikelet bearing two to three bract-like florets with lemma and palea, florets with three stamens and a single pistil, flowers with actinomorphic symmetry, glumes present, lemma with awn, palea present.
Stems
Cylindrical, hollow, nodes present, leaf sheaths tubular, internodes elongated, surface glabrous, growth habit erect, branching pattern monopodial.
