Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and powders can be made from the dried leaves and stems of this versatile plant.

Side Effects of avena sativa

Avena sativa causes bloating due to its high fiber content, which can slow digestion and lead to a feeling of fullness in the abdomen.

It also leads to gas as the fiber ferments in the gut, producing excess air and discomfort. Some individuals may experience stomach upset or nausea as their digestive system adjusts to the herb's properties. The herb can trigger heartburn in sensitive individuals by relaxing the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing stomach acid to flow back up.

Additionally, avena sativa may cause skin rashes or allergic reactions in people with sensitivities to the plant. It can also lead to cramps, constipation, or diarrhea depending on individual tolerance and dosage. In more severe cases, it may cause dizziness or fatigue, particularly if consumed in large amounts or by those with pre-existing health conditions. These side effects highlight the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before using avena sativa, especially for those with gastrointestinal issues or allergies.

Overall, while avena sativa is often used for its health benefits, it is essential to be aware of its potential adverse effects to use it safely and effectively.
