Ehrenberg'S Stevia (Stevia Ehrenbergiana)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Stevia ehrenbergiana.

Ehrenberg's stevia, scientifically known as Stevia ehrenbergiana, is a medicinal herb native to South America, prized for its natural sweetness and therapeutic properties. This adaptogen, often used as a spice and herbal remedy, contains high levels of steviol glycosides, which are potent natural sweeteners with minimal caloric impact. Traditionally, indigenous communities have used it to treat ailments such as hypertension, diabetes, and digestive disorders, leveraging its cooling and calming effects. In modern wellness practices, it is widely consumed as a sugar substitute and for its potential to support metabolic health and stress reduction. Unique to this species is its rare active compound, stevioside, and its historical significance in traditional medicine, making it a valuable plant in both ancient and contemporary health practices.
