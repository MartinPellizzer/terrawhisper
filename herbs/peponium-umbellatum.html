What is Peponium umbellatum?

Peponium umbellatum is a species of flowering plant in the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is an herbaceous vine with twining stems and leaflets that are deeply 3-5 lobed, arranged in an alternate pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by globose fruits that are approximately 2-3 cm in diameter, containing 2-4 seeds. The flowers and fruits are dioecious, with male and female reproductive structures separate.

The plant is native to the tropical regions of eastern Africa.
