Ilex godajam

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex godajam has evergreen leaves that are elliptical or lanceolate in shape, possessing a leathery texture and serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a dark green color on the upper surface with a slightly paler underside. Its stems are woody and branch out to form a dense, shrubby or small tree-like habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers often emerge from the axils of the leaves during the growing season. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy drupes that serve as a primary feature of its reproductive morphology.
