Dontostemon glandulosus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dontostemon glandulosus has glandular-pubescent, linear to lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along a slender, upright stem. The foliage typically displays a grayish-green hue due to a fine covering of sticky hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are primarily pink to lavender and emerge from the leaf axils. These blooms are supported by delicate pedicels that contribute to the plant's airy appearance. The overall structure is characterized by its compact, herbaceous habit and subtle textures.
