What's the taxonomical classification of Dontostemon glandulosus?

Dontostemon glandulosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Brassicales and the family Brassicaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Dontostemon, and its unique species designation, glandulosus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dontostemon glandulosus has glandular-pubescent, linear to lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along a slender, upright stem. The foliage typically displays a grayish-green hue due to a fine covering of sticky hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are primarily pink to lavender and emerge from the leaf axils. These blooms are supported by delicate pedicels that contribute to the plant's airy appearance. The overall structure is characterized by its compact, herbaceous habit and subtle textures.
