Crepis rigescens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crepis rigescens has an erect, unbranched stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and features basal leaves that are long, narrow, and often lack petioles. The leaves are mostly lanceolate or linear in shape and possess entire or slightly dentate margins. At the top of the stem, the plant produces small, yellow, ligulate flower heads arranged in a terminal corymbose cyme. Each flower head contains numerous individual florets characterized by their distinctively yellow, strap-shaped petals. The plant also develops a taproot system that anchors it firmly into the soil.

      Crepis rigescens is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical tincture to utilize its bioactive components.
