10 Medicinal Constituents Of Sanguisorba Officinalis (Great Burnet)

Sanguisorba officinalis has active constituents such as saponins, phenolic acids, and flavonoids.

These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The medicinal properties of great burnet include wound healing, reducing inflammation, and protecting against infections.

For example, the saponins can help soothe and clean minor burns, wounds, and cuts, improving healing and preventing scarring, while the antioxidant properties can boost the immune system and protect against oxidative stress.

Sanguisorba officinalis flavonoids is a class of bioactive compounds found in its leaves and stems.

8. Quercetin

Sanguisorba officinalis quercetin is a bioactive compound derived from its roots and leaves.

Sanguisorba officinalis kaempferol is a flavonoid glycoside that is primarily found in the roots and stems of the plant.

It has also been reported to exhibit antimicrobial and antiviral activities, making it a potentially useful compound in the treatment of various diseases.

Sanguisorba officinalis apigenin is a flavonoid compound found in its flowers and leaves.
