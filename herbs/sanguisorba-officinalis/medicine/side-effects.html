10 Possible Side Effects Of Sanguisorba Officinalis (Great Burnet)

Sanguisorba officinalis has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and increased risk of bleeding.

These side effects can be caused by taking high doses, using the herb for prolonged periods, or combining it with other medications.

For example, severe gastrointestinal issues can lead to dehydration, malnutrition, and weight loss, worsening one's quality of life and overall health.

These bioactive molecules can stimulate an immune response, triggering an allergic reaction in some individuals. As a result, people may experience symptoms such as hives, itching, and inflammation upon exposure to Sanguisorba officinalis or its extracts.

The exact mechanisms behind these reactions are not fully understood, but the allergenic potential of great burnet has been linked to its ability to bind with antibodies in the body, triggering a cascade of immune responses.
