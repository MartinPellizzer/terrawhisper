Sanguisorba (Sanguisorba officinalis) Compounds

The primary compounds in Sanguisorba officinalis include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory actions, while glycosides and saponins support digestive health. Coumarin and terpenoids offer antispasmodic and antimicrobial benefits, and anthocyanins provide additional antioxidant support.

Sanguisorba (Sanguisorba officinalis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Sanguisorba officinalis. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory health. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic effects.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Sanguisorba officinalis. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Tannins in Sanguisorba help in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. They also play a role in the plant's ability to retain moisture and protect against pathogens.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Sanguisorba officinalis. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine. This compound helps support the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and skin conditions.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Sanguisorba officinalis. It contributes to the plant's hemostatic properties by promoting blood clotting and reducing bleeding. This compound is commonly found in the roots and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Sanguisorba officinalis. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and inflammation. Coumarin acts as an anticoagulant and may support cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the plant's therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Sanguisorba officinalis. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues. Saponins have mild detergent properties and may support liver function. They are also believed to have antimicrobial effects.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Sanguisorba officinalis. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds are often responsible for the therapeutic effects observed in traditional herbal medicine using this plant.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound found in Sanguisorba officinalis. It contributes to the plant's vibrant colors and possesses antioxidant properties. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Sanguisorba officinalis contains volatile compounds like methyl salicylate and aromatic acids. It is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The oil is extracted through distillation and is valued for its therapeutic effects in herbal formulations.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Sanguisorba officinalis. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific therapeutic effects are less well-documented compared to other compounds. Alkaloids in Sanguisorba are often studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic activities.
