The scientific name of the plant is Sanguisorba officinalis, first described by Carl von Linné. Sanguisorba officinalis is a herbaceous perennial with a rhizomatous root system. It has upright, branched stems that grow up to 1 meter tall. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have toothed margins. The inflorescence is a dense panicle, bearing small, reddish-purple flowers. The fruit is a three-winged achene, and the seeds are dispersed by wind.

Sanguisorba officinalis can be distinguished by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with white veins, pinkish-red flower clusters, and a fibrous root system. Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Sanguisorba officinalis is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It thrives in cool climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Sanguisorba officinalis blooms from June to August, producing pink to red flower clusters. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested in early summer when the leaves are fully developed but before flowering.
