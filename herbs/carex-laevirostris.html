What is Carex laevirostris?

Carex laevirostris is a species of sedge in the family Cyperaceae.

It is an perennial herb with a tufted habit, growing up to 50-100 cm in height, and has 2-4 mm wide leaves with a glabrous surface and a flat or slightly channeled base. The leaf sheaths and bracts are dark brown to reddish-brown in color, and the inflorescence is a dense, cylindrical spike, 3-6 cm in length, with 6-15 spikelets, each 5-10 mm in length. Each spikelet has 2-4 fertile and 1-2 sterile glumes, and 2-3 fertile and 1-2 sterile lemmas.

The plant produces seeds with a reddish-brown color and a size of approximately 2-3 mm.
