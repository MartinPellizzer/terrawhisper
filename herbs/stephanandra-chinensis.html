Stephanandra chinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephanandra chinensis has woody, climbing vines characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and acute apices. The stems are typically slender and can reach considerable lengths through twining growth habits. Its flowers are small, tubular, and frequently arranged in dense, terminal cymes that bloom in shades of white or pale lavender. The inflorescence is often followed by small, globose fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. These morphological traits collectively support its classification within the Verbenaceae family.
