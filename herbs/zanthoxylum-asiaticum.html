Zanthoxylum asiaticum

What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum asiaticum?

Zanthoxylum asiaticum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Sapindales and is a member of the family Rutaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Zanthoxylum and the species asiaticum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Zanthoxylum asiacticum contains a diverse profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, terpenoids, flavonoids, and coumarins. The plant is particularly noted for its high concentration of various quinoline alkaloids which exhibit significant pharmacological potential. These chemical constituents provide the plant with documented antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Additionally, specific limonoids and essential oils found within the bark and leaves contribute to its traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. Research continues to investigate how these complex compounds can be utilized for modern therapeutic applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Zanthoxylum asiaticum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive disorders such as diarrhea, dysentery, and abdominal pain, as well as various skin conditions including wounds and inflammation. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to soothe gastrointestinal distress through its potent antimicrobial and antispasmodic qualities. In traditional medicine, the bark and fruit are often prepared as decoctions to combat intestinal infections. Additionally, the plant serves as a topical remedy for managing skin irritations and promoting the healing of external lesions. Practitioners also utilize its components to address respiratory ailments and fever.
