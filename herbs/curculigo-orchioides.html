Scientific and Botanical Profile

Curculigo Orchid, with botanical name Curculigo orchioides, is a member of the family Orchidaceae, commonly known as the orchid family.

Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries such as India, China, and Southeast Asia, this plant is also found in parts of Africa and the Pacific Islands. Morphologically, it is a small herbaceous perennial with a short, fleshy rhizome and a cluster of fleshy, tuberous roots that resemble coral, hence its common name Coralroot. The plant produces showy, orchid-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

This versatile plant is known by numerous common names, including Coralroot Orchid, Orchid-Like Curculigo, Sticky Wicket Orchid, and Orchid Of The Forest, reflecting its widespread presence and unique appearance across different regions.
