Botanical Identification

Curculigo orchioides, commonly known as the orchid root, belongs to the family Orchidaceae. It is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia. The plant has tuberous roots, narrow leaves, and small white flowers. It is also referred to as "Kew tree" in some traditional texts.

Active Compounds

Curculigo orchioides contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides. These compounds work together to support various therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. Their synergistic interactions enhance the herb's efficacy in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Glycoside

Safety Profile

Curculigo orchioides is generally considered safe when used in traditional herbal medicine. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised during pregnancy and lactation due to limited research on its effects.
