Harmal (Peganum harmala)

Harmal (Peganum harmala) is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, native to Arabian Peninsula, Middle East, and Central Asia. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and stems have been used for powders, decoctions, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, tonic, and antispasmodic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.
