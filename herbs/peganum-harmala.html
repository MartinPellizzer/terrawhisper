Harmal

Peganum harmala

Harmal (peganum harmala) is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and has been traditionally used in various regions for treatment of skin infections, as a culinary spice, and in traditional medicine for respiratory issues. The plant parts commonly used include the seeds, leaves, and roots, and it has a long history of use in both culinary and medicinal practices across different cultures.

Botanical Identification

Peganum harmala, commonly known as Harmal, belongs to the family Chenopodiaceae. It is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The plant has small, greenish flowers and thick, fleshy leaves, with a distinctive root system adapted to dry environments. Synonyms include "Bhang" and "Kaner" in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Harmal contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, terpenoids, and flavonoids. These compounds work synergistically to exert their medicinal effects. Alkaloids, in particular, are responsible for many of the herb's therapeutic properties.

Safety Profile

Harmal is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential toxicity. It should be avoided by individuals with a history of seizures or mental health disorders. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
