Peganum harmala seed has been traditionally used in various medicinal systems for its potential therapeutic properties.

The seeds contain alkaloids such as harmine and harmaline, which are known for their monoamine oxidase inhibitor (MAOI) activity and have been studied for their possible effects on mental health and neurological conditions. In traditional medicine, the seeds have been used to treat ailments such as depression, anxiety, and digestive issues, although scientific evidence supporting these uses is limited. The seeds are also sometimes employed in spiritual or ritualistic practices due to their psychoactive properties.

Peganum harmala tinctures are commonly used to extract the active alkaloids, such as harmine and harmaline, which are believed to have psychoactive and medicinal properties.

These tinctures are typically made by soaking the dried plant material in alcohol or another suitable solvent for several weeks, allowing the compounds to dissolve. They are often used in traditional medicine for their potential effects on the nervous system and are sometimes incorporated into spiritual or ceremonial practices. Due to their potency, tinctures require careful preparation and controlled dosing to avoid adverse effects.

In addition to tinctures, Peganum harmala can be prepared as teas, decoctions, or capsules, each offering different methods of consumption and absorption. The versatility of Peganum harmala in various forms highlights its significance in both traditional and alternative medicine practices.
