10 Medicinal Constituents Of Petroselinum Crispum (Parsley)

Petroselinum crispum has active constituents such as apiol, myristicin, and apiole that contribute to its medicinal properties.

These constituents possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities. The plant's ability to reduce inflammation can alleviate symptoms of arthritis, improve digestion, and even help regulate blood pressure.

For example, the antioxidants in parsley may help protect against cell damage, potentially reducing the risk of certain cancers and neurological diseases.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Petroselinum crispum.

1. Apigenin

Petroselinum crispum apigenin is a flavonoid compound that has been isolated from its leaves.

Apigenin is a bioactive molecule with numerous biological and pharmacological properties. It has been shown to have antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities, as well as potential therapeutic effects on cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurological disorders.

Additionally, apigenin has been found to possess anti-anxiety and anti-depressant properties.

2. Kaempferol

Petroselinum crispum kaempferol is a flavonoid compound that has been isolated from its leaves.

3. Quercetin

Petroselinum crispum quercetin is a bioflavonoid compound found in its leaves.
