1. Causes allergic reactions

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Zostera marina triggers asthma attacks by releasing pollen into the air.

As a type of marine angiosperm, it produces flowers and fruits that are rich in allergenic compounds, which can exacerbate respiratory issues like asthma when inhaled.

When broken down by wind or water currents, these particles become airborne, leading to an increased risk of asthmatic symptoms in susceptible individuals.

3. Induces diarrhea

Zostera marina induces diarrhea due to its high concentration of saponins and polyphenols.

These compounds can irritate the digestive tract, leading to changes in bowel movements and an increased frequency of bowel movements.

Additionally, the fiber content in Zostera marina can also contribute to this side effect by slowing down digestion and altering the balance of gut bacteria, ultimately causing diarrhea.

The saponins and other bioactive compounds present in Zostera marina may bind to serotonin receptors, leading to altered serotonin levels and resulting in dizziness spells.

This interaction can cause a disruption in the body's normal balance and coordination, leading to feelings of lightheadedness or spinning sensations.

Additionally, some individuals may be allergic to certain components of the seagrass, exacerbating stomach pain and discomfort.
