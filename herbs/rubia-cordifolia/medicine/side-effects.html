10 Possible Side Effects Of Rubia Cordifolia

Rubia cordifolia has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, skin irritation, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, skin contact, or sensitivities to the herb's alkaloids. Gastrointestinal issues can lead to stomach pain, nausea, and diarrhea, interfering with daily activities and impacting work or school performance.

Skin irritation and allergic reactions can cause discomfort, itching, and redness, affecting personal and social life.

1. Causes stomach upset

Rubia cordifolia causes stomach upset in some individuals.

This is due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides, which are known to irritate the gastrointestinal tract and stimulate bowel movements.

Additionally, Rubia cordifolia may interfere with digestion and nutrient absorption, leading to symptoms such as bloating, cramping, and diarrhea.

2. Increases blood pressure

Rubia cordifolia increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the production of adrenaline and noradrenaline in the body.

These hormones are responsible for constricting blood vessels and increasing heart rate, leading to a rise in blood pressure.

Additionally, Rubia cordifolia may also interfere with the body's natural blood pressure regulation mechanisms, further contributing to the increase in blood pressure.

3. Triggers allergic reactions

Rubia cordifolia triggers allergic reactions in some individuals.

The saponins and tannins present in the plant are responsible for these adverse effects. These compounds can cause an immune response, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, redness, and rashes.

Additionally, the flavonoids and phenolic acids may also contribute to the allergic potential of Rubia cordifolia, making it essential to be aware of this potential side effect when using the plant for medicinal or other purposes.

4. Induces drowsiness

Rubia cordifolia induces drowsiness due to its sedative and anxiolytic properties.

The plant's active compounds, such as anthraquinones and alkaloids, interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, promoting relaxation and reducing stress levels.

As a result, users may experience feelings of drowsiness or sleepiness, particularly when consumed in large doses or in combination with other sedative substances.

5. Lowers blood sugar

Rubia cordifolia lowers blood sugar by containing anthraquinones, a class of compounds that have been shown to increase insulin secretion and improve glucose uptake in the body.

This can be beneficial for people with type 2 diabetes, but it may also cause hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) in some individuals, especially those who already have low blood sugar levels or are taking medications that affect blood sugar control.

6. Causes diarrhea

Rubia cordifolia causes diarrhea due to its ability to alter gut bacteria and reduce the absorption of water in the small intestine.

The active compounds present in Rubia cordifolia, such as anthraquinones, can irritate the digestive system, leading to loose stools and frequent bowel movements.

Additionally, the herb's stimulation of the liver and kidneys may also contribute to its diarrheogenic effect, resulting in an increased frequency of bowel movements.

7. Produces nausea

Rubia cordifolia produces nausea due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides, such as rubian and mollugin.

These compounds can irritate the stomach lining and cause gastrointestinal upset, leading to feelings of queasiness and discomfort.

Additionally, the herb's bitter taste and astringent properties may also contribute to this side effect, making it uncomfortable for some individuals to ingest.

8. Disrupts liver function

Rubia cordifolia disrupts liver function due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides, which can inhibit the activity of certain enzymes in the liver.

This may lead to changes in liver function tests and potentially cause damage or inflammation to the liver tissue.

The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it is thought that these compounds may interfere with the body's natural detoxification processes, causing an imbalance in liver function.

9. Affects fertility

Rubia cordifolia affects fertility due to its ability to alter hormone levels and disrupt the balance of estrogen and progesterone.

The plant's active compounds, including anthraquinones and alkaloids, can interfere with ovulation and sperm production, potentially leading to irregular menstrual cycles, decreased libido, and difficulty conceiving.

These effects are thought to be caused by the plant's interaction with the endocrine system, which regulates reproductive hormones.

10. Irritates skin

Rubia cordifolia irritates skin due to its alkaloid content, particularly rubiadin and anthraquinone glycosides.

These compounds can cause allergic reactions, resulting in redness, itching, and inflammation on the skin surface.

Additionally, the plant's high concentration of anthracene derivatives may contribute to its irritating properties, leading to discomfort and skin issues when used topically or ingested.
