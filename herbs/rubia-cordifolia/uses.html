Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) Uses

Indian Madder is used to treat skin infections, wound healing, and skin inflammation. It also addresses respiratory infections, cough, bronchitis, and asthma. It may help with tuberculosis and general inflammation due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) Monograph

1. Skin infections

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The root contains compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin. It is often prepared as a poultice or infusion for localized application. This makes it a valuable herb in traditional herbal medicine for addressing minor skin infections.

2. Wound healing

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) promotes wound healing through its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce infection risk and accelerates tissue repair. Traditionally applied as a poultice, it supports skin regeneration and reduces scarring. Its active compounds stimulate cellular renewal and enhance healing processes.

3. Skin inflammation

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) is used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation associated with conditions like eczema and dermatitis. The active compounds in the root and leaves promote healing and soothe inflamed skin tissues.

4. Respiratory infections

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. It is often prepared as a tincture or decoction to soothe coughs and reduce mucus production. This makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for addressing common respiratory ailments.

5. Cough

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate cough symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce irritation in the respiratory tract and loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for respiratory support.

6. Respiratory tract infections

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections. It is often prepared as a tincture or decoction to ease coughs and reduce mucus production.

7. Bronchitis

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce mucus congestion and soothe bronchial irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to aid in clearing airways and alleviating coughing.

8. Asthma

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing airway inflammation and improving lung function. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture and used as a complementary therapy under professional guidance.

9. Tuberculosis

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential respiratory benefits. It is believed to support lung health and may help alleviate symptoms of tuberculosis by reducing inflammation and promoting expectoration. Its active compounds are thought to enhance immune function and aid in clearing mucus from the respiratory tract.

10. Inflammation

Indian Madder (Rubia cordifolia) is used to reduce inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory compounds, including anthraquinones and tannins. It is traditionally applied topically for skin conditions and joint inflammation, and internally for digestive tract inflammation. Its astringent properties help soothe irritated tissues and reduce swelling.
