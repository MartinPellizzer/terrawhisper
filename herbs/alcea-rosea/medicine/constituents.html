10 Medicinal Constituents Of Alcea Rosea (Hollyhock)

Alcea rosea has active constituents such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and glycosides.

These constituents contribute to its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antioxidant effects. For example, flavonoids can reduce inflammation and alleviate pain, making it useful in treating conditions such as arthritis. Additionally, its antiseptic properties can aid in wound healing and prevent infections.

Overall, the constituents of Alcea rosea can improve people's lives by providing relief from pain and inflammation, as well as promoting overall health and well-being.

1. Beta-sitosterol

Alcea rosea beta-sitosterol is a naturally occurring plant sterol found in its flowers, leaves, and roots.

This phytosterol has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including cholesterol-lowering properties, anti-inflammatory effects, and antioxidant activity.

Beta-sitosterol may also help to reduce the risk of certain diseases, such as prostate cancer and cardiovascular disease, by inhibiting the growth of cancer cells and improving blood lipid profiles.

2. Salicylic acid

Alcea rosea salicylic acid is a naturally occurring compound found in its flowers and leaves.

This plant-derived ingredient has been traditionally used in traditional medicine to treat various skin conditions such as acne, eczema, and psoriasis due to its antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antifungal properties.

It helps to exfoliate the skin, unclog pores, and reduce inflammation, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products.

3. Ursolic acid

Alcea rosea ursolic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from its leaves and flowers.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties.

Ursolic acid has been shown to inhibit the growth of cancer cells and induce apoptosis, making it a promising natural compound in the development of cancer therapies.

4. Oleic acid

Alcea rosea oleic acid is a fatty acid that is extracted from the seeds of this flowering plant.

It is a powerful antioxidant with anti-inflammatory properties, which has been traditionally used to treat various skin issues such as eczema, acne, and psoriasis.

Oleic acid has also been shown to have wound-healing effects, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products for its ability to soothe and calm irritated skin.

5. Linoleic acid

Alcea rosea linoleic acid is a type of polyunsaturated fatty acid found in its seeds.

It has been studied for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving skin conditions such as acne and eczema.

Linoleic acid from Alcea rosea seed oil has also been shown to have antioxidant properties, which can help protect against cell damage and promote overall health.

6. Palmitoleic acid

Alcea rosea palmitoleic acid is a rare and valuable fatty acid found in its seeds.

This omega-7 fatty acid has been shown to have potent anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential treatment for various diseases such as skin conditions, cancer, and cardiovascular disorders. It also exhibits antioxidant and antimicrobial activities, providing protection against oxidative stress and infections.

Its unique composition sets palmitoleic acid apart from other fatty acids, offering promising therapeutic applications.

7. Campesterol

Alcea rosea campesterol is a type of triterpenoid saponin found in various parts of this flowering plant.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

Campesterol has also been reported to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it a potential candidate for the prevention and treatment of certain diseases.

8. Stigmasterol

Alcea rosea stigmasterol is a type of saponin that is naturally found in the roots and leaves of this plant.

Saponins are a class of compounds known for their soap-like properties, which are responsible for the foaming action when they come into contact with water.

Stigmasterol, specifically, has been found to have potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, making it a promising compound for further study in the fields of medicine and pharmacology.

9. Beta-amyrin

Alcea rosea beta-amyrin is a type of triterpenoid saponin found in the flowers and leaves of this plant.

It has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health issues such as skin diseases, respiratory problems, and even cancer. Beta-amyrin has also been shown to possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential natural remedy for various disorders.

Further research is needed to fully understand its therapeutic effects and potential applications.

10. Oleocanthal

Alcea rosea oleocanthal is a compound extracted from its flower extract.

This bioactive molecule has been found to possess potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Oleocanthal has been shown to inhibit the production of prostaglandins, which are involved in inflammation and pain.

It has also been demonstrated to exhibit neuroprotective effects, suggesting potential therapeutic applications for neurological disorders such as Alzheimer's disease and Parkinson's disease.
