Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea) Compounds

The primary compounds in Chinese holly include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin contributes to the plant's fragrance and may have anticoagulant effects. Anthocyanins provide color and also exhibit antioxidant activity.

Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and circulatory health. These compounds help modulate immune responses and protect cellular structures from oxidative damage.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea), contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps reduce oxidative stress and supports immune function, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating inflammatory conditions and promoting overall health.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in promoting blood circulation and reducing inflammation. Its presence supports the traditional use of Alcea rosea in herbal medicine for circulatory health.

4. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound found in Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea). It contributes to the plant's vibrant colors and has antioxidant properties that support cardiovascular and immune health. This compound is also valued for its potential anti-inflammatory effects in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides are naturally occurring substances that often enhance the therapeutic effects of herbal remedies.

6. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like linalool and geraniol, which contribute to its calming and anti-inflammatory properties. This oil is commonly used in herbal medicine for its soothing effects on the respiratory and nervous systems.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its ability to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. Tannins also help in the preservation of the plant's medicinal qualities.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic uses in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Saponins also aid in enhancing the absorption of nutrients and supporting immune function.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by interacting with biological systems. Alkaloids in Alcea rosea are studied for their potential therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.
