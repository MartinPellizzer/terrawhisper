Botanical Identification of Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea)

To identify Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Chinese Holly, or Alcea rosea, belongs to the plant kingdom, classified under Magnoliophyta, Magnoliopsida, Malvales, and Malvaceae. It is part of the genus Alcea and the species Alcea rosea, which is known for its showy flowers and medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Chinese Holly is Alcea rosea, a species within the Malvaceae family. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. Common names include rose mallow and Chinese holly, though it is not native to China. The plant is also known by various regional and historical names, reflecting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Holly, or Alcea rosea, is a herbaceous annual plant with a taproot system. It has straight, branching stems that are hairy and upright. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges. The plant produces a spike-like inflorescence with large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red. The fruit is a dry capsule containing numerous seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chinese Holly (Alcea rosea) can be distinguished by its large, showy pink or white flowers, hairy stems, and heart-shaped leaves. It differs from similar species like holly (Ilex) by its lack of spiny leaves and its flowering habit. The presence of a distinct scent and specific leaf venation also aid in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chinese Holly, or Alcea rosea, is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Alcea rosea blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to September. For identification purposes, harvesting is best done when the seeds are mature and the plant is in its peak flowering stage.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Alcea rosea belongs to the genus Alcea, which is part of the family Malvaceae. It is closely related to species like Alcea trinervis and Alcea rugosa. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, reflecting their close phylogenetic relationship within the Malvaceae family.
