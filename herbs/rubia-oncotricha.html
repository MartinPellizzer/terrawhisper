What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubia oncotricha has slender, creeping or ascending stems that typically reach lengths of 20 to 50 centimeters and are covered in small, spreading prickles. Small, tubular, white or pale pink flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters during the blooming season. These flowers are followed by small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a bright red color upon ripening. The root system consists of a relatively thin, creeping rhizome that allows the plant to spread across forest floors.
