What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium candelabrum?

Delphinium candelabrum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Ranunculales. This species is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, which is characterized by its diverse botanical features. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Delphinium, with its precise biological identity defined by the species name candelabrum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium candelabrum has a robust, erect growth habit characterized by tall, unbranched stems that can reach several feet in height and bear dense, pyramidal clusters of zygomorphic flowers. The foliage consists of deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves that are typically dark green and arranged alternately along the lower portions of the stem. Each individual flower features a prominent, nectar-producing spur extending backward from the uppermost sepal. The petals are often complemented by a central nectar guide that attracts specific pollinators. This architectural structure allows the plant to present its blossoms in a tiered, candelabra-like arrangement.
