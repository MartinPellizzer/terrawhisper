Parthenium hysterophorus

What's the taxonomical classification of Parthenium hysterophorus?

Parthenium hysterophorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its lineage, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Parthenium, with its unique designation being the species hysterophorus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Parthenium hysterophorus contains sesquiterpene lactones such as parthenin, helenalin, and parthenolide which serve as its primary bioactive medicinal compounds. These specific phytochemicals possess significant anti-inflammatory, immunosuppressive, and antimalarial properties. Research also indicates that the plant contains flavonoids and alkaloids that contribute to its diverse biological activities. Some studies suggest these compounds may offer potential in treating skin diseases and certain types of cancer cells. However, the high concentration of parthenin also makes the plant a potent allergen responsible for contact dermatitis in many species.

What are the therapeutic actions of Parthenium hysterophorus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin ailments including wounds, eczema, dermatitis, and fungal infections. Its leaves possess significant medicinal properties that aid in the healing of topical injuries and inflammation. Some traditional practices utilize extracts from the plant to manage minor skin irritations and parasitic infections. However, it is important to note that the plant can also cause allergic contact dermatitis in many sensitive individuals. Research into its bioactive compounds continues to explore its potential for dermatological applications.
