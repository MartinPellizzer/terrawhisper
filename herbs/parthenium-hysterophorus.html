Parthenium hysterophorus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Parthenium hysterophorus has an erect, herbaceous annual or perennial plant structure characterized by branched stems that reach heights of up to one meter and are covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are deeply pinnatisect with narrow, serrated lobes that give the foliage a feathery appearance. Small, white, discoid flower heads are arranged in dense terminal corymbose clusters. Each individual flower head lacks ray florets and consists solely of tubular disc florets. The plant produces small, cylindrical achenes that are easily dispersed by wind or water.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Americas and has become a widespread invasive species across tropical, subtropical, and temperate regions of the world. It is extensively distributed throughout much of Asia, Africa, and Australia, where it colonizes disturbed soils and roadsides. The species thrives in various climates, ranging from warm humid zones to more temperate environments. Its ability to spread rapidly across diverse landscapes allows it to establish dominance in many different ecosystems. Consequently, it can be found in many countries globally where it often disrupts local biodiversity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Parthenium hysterophorus contains sesquiterpene lactones such as parthenin, helenalin, and parthenolide which serve as its primary bioactive medicinal compounds. These specific phytochemicals possess significant anti-inflammatory, immunosuppressive, and antimalarial properties. Research also indicates that the plant contains flavonoids and alkaloids that contribute to its diverse biological activities. Some studies suggest these compounds may offer potential in treating skin diseases and certain types of cancer cells. However, the high concentration of parthenin also makes the plant a potent allergen responsible for contact dermatitis in many species.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause contact dermatitis, skin irritation, allergic rhinitis, asthma exacerbation, respiratory irritation, nausea, and dizziness in humans. Direct physical contact with the foliage often leads to painful skin irritation or contact dermatitis. Inhaling its airborne pollen can trigger allergic rhinitis and severe respiratory irritation. For individuals with pre-existing lung conditions, exposure may lead to a dangerous asthma exacerbation. Some people may also experience systemic symptoms such as nausea and dizziness after coming into contact with the plant.
