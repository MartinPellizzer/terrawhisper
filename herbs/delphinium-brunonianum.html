Delphinium brunonianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium brunonianum?

Delphinium brunonianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Delphinium, and is specifically identified by the species name brunonianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium brunonianum has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and bear deeply lobed, palmate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is primarily concentrated in the lower and middle portions of the plant, providing a lush green base for the flowering stalks. Its inflorescence consists of dense racemes of zygomorphic flowers that display a distinct spur at the back of the corolla. These blossoms often feature a range of colors including blue, violet, or white, which serve to attract specific pollinators. The reproductive structures are tucked within the floral tubes, ensuring specialized pollination mechanisms are engaged.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds Brunonianine D, Brunonianines D-F, C19-diterpenoid alkaloids, an amide alkaloid, and diterpenoid alkaloids. These chemical constituents are categorized as various types of nitrogenous and terpenoid substances. The presence of the amide alkaloid suggests specialized metabolic pathways within the plant's tissues. Furthermore, the diverse array of diterpenoid alkaloids, including the C19-diterpenoid variety, contributes to its unique chemical profile. These specific molecular structures are often studied for their potential pharmacological activities.

    As discussed in a study, Delphinium brunonianum contains nine diterpenoid alkaloids (1-9) and one amide alkaloid (10). Among these, three novel C19-type diterpenoid alkaloids named Brunonianines D-F (1-3) were identified. These compounds were tested against four tumor cell lines, with many showing potent inhibitory effects on Skov-3 cell lines. Specifically, the IC50 values for these compounds ranged from 2.57 to 8.05 μM. These medicinal compounds demonstrate therapeutic effects on ovarian cancer both in vitro and in vivo.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe toxicity including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, cardiac arrhythmia, convulsions, and respiratory depression. Ingestion of its toxic components can lead to immediate gastrointestinal distress such as nausea and vomiting. As the toxins enter the bloodstream, a person may experience neurological symptoms like dizziness and violent convulsions. The most dangerous effects involve the cardiovascular and pulmonary systems, specifically triggering cardiac arrhythmia. Ultimately, the poison can cause life-threatening respiratory depression in highly sensitive individuals.
