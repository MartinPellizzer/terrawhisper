Delphinium brunonianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium brunonianum?

Delphinium brunonianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Delphinium, and is specifically identified by the species name brunonianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium brunonianum has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and bear deeply lobed, palmate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is primarily concentrated in the lower and middle portions of the plant, providing a lush green base for the flowering stalks. Its inflorescence consists of dense racemes of zygomorphic flowers that display a distinct spur at the back of the corolla. These blossoms often feature a range of colors including blue, violet, or white, which serve to attract specific pollinators.
