What's the taxonomical classification of Ribes henryi?

Ribes henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Saxifragales. As a member of the family Grossulariaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Ribes, with its specific identification designated as the species henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ribes henryi has a deciduous shrub habit characterized by slender, woody stems and palmately lobed leaves that typically feature three to five distinct lobes.

The leaf margins are coarsely serrated, providing a textured appearance to the foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often appearing in clusters during the blooming season. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that transition in color as they reach maturity.

These morphological traits help distinguish this specific species from other members of the Ribes genus.
