Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis)

Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Mediterranean Region, Southern Europe, and North Africa. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, carminative, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 0.5 to 1.5 meters.
Leaves
Opposite, needle-like leaves with upper surface gray-green and lower surface white-gray, bearing stomatal bands along midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are compact clusters of small blue to purple actinomorphic flowers with four to five ovate petals and four to five linear sepals.
Stems
Erect growth habit with opposite branching, woody surface, and aromatic glandular structures.
