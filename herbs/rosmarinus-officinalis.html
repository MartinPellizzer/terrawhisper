Rosemary

Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis) is a fragrant evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region, known for its aromatic leaves and historical use in traditional medicine to improve memory, reduce inflammation, and support respiratory health.

Botanical Identification

Rosemary is Rosmarinus officinalis, a member of the Lamiaceae family. Commonly known as rosemary, it is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It has aromatic evergreen leaves and small blue or white flowers. Synonyms include "rosmary" and "romero" in various regional contexts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Rosmarinus
  • Species: Rosmarinus officinalis

Rosemary Identification.

Active Compounds

Rosemary contains essential oils, terpenoids, and flavonoids as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide its therapeutic effects, with terpenoids contributing to anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, while flavonoids support cellular protection.

  • Essential oil
  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid

Rosemary Actions.

Preparation Methods

Essential oil: Used for respiratory issues and muscle pain due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and to relieve stress due to its calming and carminative effects.

Tincture: Used for headaches and anxiety due to its ability to enhance the bioavailability of active compounds.

Safety Profile

Rosemary is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and by individuals with epilepsy due to its potential to cause adverse effects. High doses may lead to toxicity, so caution is advised.
