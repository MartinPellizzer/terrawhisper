What's the taxonomical classification of Aralia thomsonii?

Aralia thomsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Apiales, the plant is a member of the family Ara里aceae. It is specifically categorized under the genus Aralia, with its unique scientific designation being the species thomsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia thomsonii has a woody, scrambling habit characterized by slender, arching stems that often possess small, scattered prickles.

The leaves are large, compound, and palmately divided into several distinct leaflets with serrated margins. These leaflets typically exhibit a glossy texture and a deep green color on their upper surfaces. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in dense, terminal umbels.

Following flowering, the plant develops small, fleshy drupes that serve as its primary fruit structure.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Aralia thomsonii can cause localized skin irritation, redness, and itching if the sap comes into direct contact with sensitive areas of the body. Some individuals may experience an allergic reaction known as contact dermatitis after handling the foliage. These dermatological symptoms typically manifest as a rash or small bumps on the skin. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, caution is advised to prevent accidental ingestion. It is best to wear protective gloves when pruning or managing this specific plant species.
