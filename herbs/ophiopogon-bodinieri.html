Ophiopogon bodinieri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophiopogon bodinieeri has slender, linear, dark green leaves that grow in dense tufts from a creeping rhizome. These leaves typically reach a length of several centimeters and possess a slightly curved or arching habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the foliage on thin stalks. Following the bloom, it develops small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a deep blue or black color. The overall growth form is that of a low-growing, clumping perennial groundcover.
