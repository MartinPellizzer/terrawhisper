Blumea mollis

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea mollis?

Blumea mollis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Blumea and the specific species designation mollis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Blumea mollis contains bioactive sesquiterpene lactones, diterpenoids, and various triterpenoids that provide its significant pharmacological properties. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's observed anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. Specific compounds such as blumeanol and related sesquiterpenes contribute to its ability to inhibit bacterial growth. Researchers also study its flavonoid content for potential antioxidant and hepatoprotective effects. These diverse medicinal compounds make the plant a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Blumea mollis?

Blumea mollis has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties that allow it to treat various skin infections, respiratory ailments, and digestive disorders. The plant contains essential oils and bioactive compounds like camphor and terpenes that effectively inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria and fungi. These chemical constituents also work to reduce swelling and soothe painful inflammation in both topical and internal applications. When used in traditional medicine, it serves as an effective remedy for calming stomach aches and relieving coughs. Its ability to modulate immune responses further enhances its therapeutic utility in managing systemic discomfort.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, respiratory ailments, digestive issues, and inflammatory conditions. Its leaves are frequently applied topically to soothe wounds, eczema, and various dermatological irritations. When prepared as a decoction, the plant helps alleviate symptoms of cough, asthma, and common colds. It is also traditionally ingested to combat stomach aches and intestinal discomfort. The medicinal properties are largely attributed to the essential oils and bioactive compounds found within its foliage.
