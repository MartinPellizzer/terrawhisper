Blumea mollis

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea mollis?

Blumea mollis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Blumea and the specific species designation mollis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea mollis has perennial herbaceous stems that are covered in a dense layer of soft, white, tomentose hairs. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape, featuring serrated margins and a velvety texture due to their pubescence. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess a distinctively soft feel to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in terminal corymbs. These inflorescences are characterized by their fuzzy appearance, which matches the hairy quality of the foliage.
