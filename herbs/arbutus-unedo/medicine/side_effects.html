1. Causes allergic reactions

Arbutus unedo causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of certain allergenic compounds, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can trigger an immune response in susceptible people.

Additionally, the plant's sap and leaves contain latex, a common allergen found in many plant species, further increasing the risk of allergic reactions.

2. Produces skin irritation

Arbutus unedo produces skin irritation.

The saponins and phenolic compounds present in its leaves, bark, and fruit may cause an allergic reaction or contact dermatitis, resulting in symptoms such as redness, itching, swelling, and rashes.

This is often due to the irritating properties of these compounds, which can penetrate the skin and trigger an immune response.

3. Triggers respiratory issues

Arbutus unedo triggers respiratory issues due to its high levels of quercetin, a natural compound that can cause allergic reactions and irritation in some individuals.

The tree's pollen is particularly problematic for those with pre-existing respiratory conditions such as asthma or bronchitis, exacerbating symptoms like wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

4. Affects digestive system

Arbutus unedo affects digestive system by causing gastrointestinal disturbances.

The consumption of its fruit and leaves can lead to stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea due to the presence of tannins and saponins, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the gut.

Additionally, Arbutus unedo contains anthraquinone glycosides, which are known to stimulate bowel movements, potentially resulting in loose stools or abdominal cramps.

5. Induces vomiting

Arbutus unedo induces vomiting due to its high concentration of tannins and saponins.

These compounds can irritate the stomach lining, causing nausea, abdominal discomfort, and regurgitation.

The severity of these symptoms may vary depending on the individual's sensitivity to these bioactive compounds, as well as the method of preparation and dosage of Arbutus unedo consumed.

6. Stimulates excessive sweating

Arbutus unedo stimulates excessive sweating due to its ability to stimulate the sweat glands and increase blood flow to the skin.

The plant's bioactive compounds, particularly triterpenes and flavonoids, are thought to play a role in this effect by binding to receptors in the nervous system and triggering a response that increases perspiration.

As a result, individuals using Arbutus unedo may experience excessive sweating as an adverse reaction.

7. Raises blood pressure

Arbutus unedo raises blood pressure due to its stimulatory effects on the body.

The plant contains compounds such as terpenoids and flavonoids that can cause an increase in heart rate and cardiac output, leading to elevated blood pressure levels.

Additionally, Arbutus unedo may also stimulate the nervous system, releasing stress hormones that further contribute to blood pressure increases.

8. Decreases blood sugar

Arbutus unedo decreases blood sugar levels due to its ability to stimulate insulin secretion and increase glucose uptake in muscle cells.

This is attributed to the presence of anthocyanins, flavonoids, and other bioactive compounds found in its berries, leaves, and bark.

These compounds have been shown to enhance glucose metabolism and improve insulin sensitivity, leading to a decrease in blood sugar levels.

9. Worsens kidney function

Arbutus unedo worsens kidney function due to its high levels of saponins and flavonoids.

These compounds can increase urine production, leading to dehydration and putting additional strain on the kidneys.

Additionally, Arbutus unedo's antioxidant properties may interfere with the body's natural ability to regulate kidney function, exacerbating any existing kidney issues or impairing normal function in healthy individuals.

10. Increases heart rate

Arbutus unedo increases heart rate due to its stimulating properties.

The plant's chemical compounds, such as scoparin and arbutin, can interact with the cardiovascular system, leading to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

This is because these compounds have a direct effect on the heart muscle, causing it to beat faster and stronger, which can be especially pronounced when consumed in large quantities or over extended periods of time.
