Hosta ventricosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Hosta ventricosa?

Hosta ventricosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Asparagales. Further taxonomic refinement places it in the family Asparagaceae, specifically within the genus Hosta, with its unique identity defined by the species ventricosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hosta ventricosa has thick, cordate leaves that measure up to 15 centimeters in length, characterized by a distinctively puckered or rugose texture and a deep blue-green coloration. The leaf margins are smooth and slightly wavy, while the undersides often exhibit a lighter, matte finish. These broad leaves emerge from a stout, fleshy rhizome that serves as a storage organ for the plant. As the foliage matures, the prominent venation creates a deeply textured surface that distinguishes it from other hosta varieties. The plant typically reaches a height of 30 to 45 centimeters, forming a dense, mounded clump of foliage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Korea, specifically occurring in provinces like Gangwon and Gyeongsang. It primarily thrives in the temperate forest ecosystems found at moderate elevations. Within these habitats, the species prefers the shaded, moist environments provided by deciduous woodland canopies. Its specific range is limited to these East Asian mountain ranges, though it has been widely introduced to gardens globally. This localized natural distribution makes it a distinct representative of Korean flora.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires partial to full shade, well-draining soil enriched with organic matter, and consistent moisture to thrive.

Because it is a shade-loving perennial, it should be planted in locations where it is protected from harsh afternoon sun. Maintaining a steady supply of water is essential, especially during hot summer months, to prevent the foliage from wilting. You should apply a layer of mulch around the base to help retain soil moisture and suppress weed growth.
