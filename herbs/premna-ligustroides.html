What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Premna ligustroides has opposite, elliptic to oblong leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage often features a lustrous, dark green upper surface and a paler underside, sometimes displaying a slightly rugose texture. Its woody stems are characterized by a somewhat quadrangular shape in younger branches, which is a common trait among many members of the Lamiaceae family. Small, white or cream-colored flowers are produced in terminal cymes, presenting a delicate appearance against the dense greenery. These inflorescences eventually give rise to small, fleshy drupes that serve as a primary dispersal mechanism for the species.
