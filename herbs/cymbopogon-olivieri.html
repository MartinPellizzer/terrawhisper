What's the taxonomical classification of Cymbopogon olivieri?

Cymbopogon olivieri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific biological classification within the genus Cymbogon as the species olivieri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbopogon olivieri has perennial, tufted, clumping grass stems that arise from a fibrous root system and feature linear, bright green leaves. The leaf blades are relatively narrow and possess a characteristic involute shape, meaning they are rolled inward along the midrib. These blades terminate in a sharp, sometimes pungent apex and often exhibit a fine, scabrous texture on their surfaces. The inflorescence typically consists of several contracted panicles that are arranged in a somewhat spike-like fashion. These flowering structures are supported by slender peduncles that emerge from the center of the leafy clumps.
