What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbopogon olivieri has perennial, tufted, clumping grass stems that arise from a fibrous root system and feature linear, bright green leaves. The leaf blades are relatively narrow and possess a characteristic involute shape, meaning they are rolled inward along the midrib. These blades terminate in a sharp, sometimes pungent apex and often exhibit a fine, scabrous texture on their surfaces. The inflorescence typically consists of several contracted panicles that are arranged in a somewhat spike-like fashion.
