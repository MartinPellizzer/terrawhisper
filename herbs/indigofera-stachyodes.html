What's the taxonomical classification of Indigofera stachyodes?

Indigofera stachyodes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Fabales and into the family Fabaceae, which is known for its significant role in nitrogen fixation. Finally, the plant is identified within the genus Indigofera, with its specific designation being the species stachyodes.

As discussed in a study, the roots of Indigofera stachyodes contain three new flavonoids, numbered 1, 2, and 3, along with one new natural occurring product, 4, and eleven known compounds, 5-15. All isolated flavonoids were screened for antioxidant abilities to scavenge DPPH and ABTS+. Compounds 2-4, 10, and 15 exhibited remarkable scavenging activity against both ABTS+ and DPPH, with IC50 values less than 20 μM. Additionally, compounds 1, 6-9, and 13 showed potential antioxidant scavenging activities. These specific compounds exhibited IC50 values in the range of 17.96∼85.91 μM.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Additionally, the cyanidin pigment provides significant anti-inflammatory benefits through its unique chemical structure. Finally, p-coumaric acid contributes to the overall therapeutic profile by offering protective effects against oxidative stress.

As discussed in a study, proanthocyanidins (PAs) are considered important medicinal value components in Indigofera stachyodes. To understand the biosynthesis in roots, a transcriptome analysis was conducted across Leaf, Stem, RootI (one-year-old root), and RootII (two-year-old root). This process yielded 110,779 non-redundant unigenes, with 63,863 receiving functional annotation. Researchers identified 75 structural genes regulating PA biosynthesis, including 6 specific genes: IsF3'H1, IsANR2, IsLAR2, IsUGT72L1-3, IsMATE2, and IsMATE3. Additionally, co-expression network analysis identified several potential regulators: 34 IsMYBs, 18 IsbHLHs, 15 IsWRKYs, 9 IsMADSs, and 3 IsWIPs hub TFs. Among these, IsMYB24 and IsMYB79 may be closely involved in the biosynthesis of PAs in the roots.
