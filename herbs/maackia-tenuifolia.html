Maackia tenuifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Maackia tenuifolia?

Maackia tenuifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized into the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, it is specifically identified by the genus Maackia, with the species name tenuifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Maackia tenuifolia has narrow, linear, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and measure only a few millimeters in width. These leaves emerge from a rhizomatous root system and grow in dense, tufted clumps. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink and hang downward from delicate stems. Its stems are slender and can become somewhat woody at the base as the plant matures. This perennial herb maintains a compact growth habit suitable for rocky or alpine environments.
