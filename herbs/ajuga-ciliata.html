Ajuga ciliata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ajuga ciliata?

Ajuga ciliata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Lamiales. It falls under the family Lamiaceae, which is characteristic of many aromatic herbs, and is specifically categorized under the genus Ajuga. Finally, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species ciliata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ajuga ciliata has a creeping perennial habit characterized by stoloniferous stems that produce small, opposite, ovate leaves with finely serrated margins and a hairy texture. The leaves are typically dark green and arranged in dense basal rosettes along the ground. During the flowering stage, the plant produces upright, terminal racemes that feature bilabiate, tubular flowers ranging from light blue to violet. These flowers are supported by a central stem that rises above the foliage, often accompanied by small, leafy bracts. The overall structure allows the plant to spread effectively across moist, shaded soil through its rooting runners.
