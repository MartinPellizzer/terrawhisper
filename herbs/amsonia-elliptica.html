What's the taxonomical classification of Amsonia elliptica?

Amsonias elliptica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Amsonia, with its specific identity defined by the species name elliptica.

What are the pharmacological activities of Amsonia elliptica?

This plant has anti-spasmodic, central depressive, hypotensive, and preventive effects on the development of gastric erosions. Its anti-spasmodic action helps to relax smooth muscle tissues throughout the body. The central depressive effect suggests the plant can influence the nervous system to induce calming or sedative responses. Additionally, the hypotensive effect indicates a potential utility in reducing elevated blood pressure levels. Finally, the plant demonstrates a protective role by providing a preventive effect on the development of gastric erosions in the stomach lining.

According to a study published by "Nihon yakurigaku zasshi. Folia pharmacologica Japonica", the alkaloid beta-Y was isolated from the plant Amsonia elliptica Roem. et Schult. This specific alkaloid showed a mild central depressive effect in mice. It also demonstrated a week non-competitive anti-spasmodic action in the mouse intestine. Furthermore, beta-Y exhibited a hypotensive effect in rats. This hypotensive effect may be partly due to the vasodilative effect induced by its alpha-adrrenoceptor blocking action.
