Ailanthus Altissima

Ailanthus altissima, commonly known as tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to China but now widely distributed across the globe.

This herb is valued for its various health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antimicrobial properties that can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma.

The therapeutic actions of Ailanthus altissima include antioxidant, anti-allergic, and immunomodulatory effects, making it a potential treatment for allergies, eczema, and other skin conditions.

The bioactive constituents of this herb include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and make it a popular ingredient in traditional Chinese medicine.

Possible Side Effects of ailanthus altissima

Ailanthus altissima can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high concentration of tannins and saponins.

Prolonged consumption may also lead to liver and kidney damage, as the herb contains compounds that can be toxic to these organs.

Additionally, Ailanthus altissima may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, potentially causing adverse effects like bleeding or hypoglycemia.

In rare cases, excessive consumption may even cause allergic reactions, including skin rashes and respiratory problems.
