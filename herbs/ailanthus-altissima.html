What to know about Ailanthus altissima (tree of heaven) before using it medicinally

Ailanthus altissima, commonly known as tree of heaven, is a herb that has been prized for its numerous health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and boosting the immune system.

Horticulturally, this fast-growing tree is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique and elegant foliage, as well as its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions. Botanically, Ailanthus altissima is classified as a member of the Simaroubaceae family, with distinctive leaves that are often used in traditional medicine to treat a range of ailments.

Historically, the tree of heaven has been referenced in traditional Chinese medicine for over 1,000 years, and has also been mentioned in ancient texts from India and the Middle East.

This article explains the medicinal, horticultural, botanical, and historical aspects of Ailanthus altissima.

What are the medicinal properties of Ailanthus altissima?

Ailanthus altissima helps with digestive issues, skin conditions, and respiratory problems due to its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties. It has been traditionally used to treat ailments such as diarrhea, fever, and skin irritations. Its bark and leaves have been used in various remedies.

The active constituents of Ailanthus altissima include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Flavonoids exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities, while alkaloids show antimicrobial effects. Terpenes are responsible for the plant's antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The most commonly used parts of Ailanthus altissima for medicinal purposes are its bark and leaves. The bark has been used to treat skin conditions and digestive issues, while the leaves have been used to treat respiratory problems and fever. The seeds and roots have also been used in some traditional remedies.

Improper use of Ailanthus altissima can lead to side effects such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritations. The plant's alkaloids can cause toxicity if ingested in large quantities. The plant's anti-inflammatory properties can interact with certain medications, causing adverse effects.

Precautions when using Ailanthus altissima medicinally include consulting a healthcare professional, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women. The plant should be used in moderation and in combination with other treatments as directed. It is essential to identify the plant correctly and use only the recommended parts and dosages to avoid adverse effects.

What are the horticulural aspects of Ailanthus altissima?

Ailanthus altissima grow in full sun to partial shade, tolerating a wide range of soils, from dry to moist, but preferring slightly acidic to neutral pH (5.5-7.5). It is a fast-growing tree, reaching maturity in 20-30 years, with a lifespan of up to 100 years.

For optimal growth, plant Ailanthus altissima in well-draining soil, with a spacing of 10-15 feet between trees. Mulch around the base to retain moisture and suppress weeds. Avoid planting in areas with standing water or where the soil is prone to waterlogging. Young trees require staking to prevent damage from wind.

Harvesting Ailanthus altissima's seed pods typically occurs in late summer, when the pods turn brown and split open. Seeds can be collected and sown immediately or stored for up to 6 months. The wood is harvested in the spring, when the sapwood is clear of green sap, for use in furniture-making, pulpwood, and other wood products.

The tree of heaven is susceptible to pests like the longhorned beetle and the scale insect. It is also vulnerable to diseases like powdery mildew, root rot, and canker. Regular pruning and removal of infected tissue can help manage these issues, while introducing beneficial insects like ladybugs can also aid in pest control.

What are the botanical characteristics of Ailanthus altissima?

Ailanthus altissima, also known as tree of heaven or Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Simaroubaceae family.

It has a number of distinct botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows up to 100 feet (30 meters) in height, with a broad, spreading crown and a straight, grayish-brown trunk. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with 11-23 leaflets that are lance-shaped and have a pointed tip.

Each leaflet is approximately 1-3 inches (2.5-7.6 cm) long and has a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter green lower surface with minute hairs. The leaves turn yellow or brown before falling off in the fall. Ailanthus altissima produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that grow in clusters at the end of branches in late summer and early fall.

The flowers are followed by samaras, which are paired, winged seeds that resemble small helicopters and spin to the ground when mature.

What is the taxonomy of Ailanthus altissima?

Ailanthus altissima belongs to the kingdom Plantae, phylum Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Sapindales, family Simaroubaceae, genus Ailanthus, and species A. altissima.

Specifically, it is a member of the Ailanthus genus within the Simaroubaceae family, which includes about 15-20 species of trees and shrubs native to Asia, Africa, and Australia.

What is the geographic distribution of Ailanthus altissima?

Ailanthus altissima, also known as the tree of heaven, is native to China and Southeast Asia.

Its natural distribution ranges from northeastern China, including the provinces of Heilongjiang, Jilin, and Liaoning, southward through the Yangtze River Valley, and eastward to Taiwan and Japan.

It has also been widely introduced and naturalized in many other parts of the world, including Europe, North America, and Australia, often becoming an invasive species in new environments due to its ability to tolerate a wide range of soil conditions and climates.

What is the life cycle of Ailanthus altissima?

Ailanthus altissima, also known as Tree-of-Heaven, has a fast-growing and invasive life cycle.

The seed germinates in spring or early summer, and the sapling emerges from the soil in late summer to fall. As it matures, the tree can grow up to 3 inches per day, reaching heights of over 100 feet within its first decade.

Ailanthus altissima typically blooms in August and September, producing small, yellowish-green flowers that are often wind-pollinated. The tree's seed pods ripen in fall and disperse in late winter, allowing the invasive species to spread rapidly through its seeds.

In areas with mild winters, Ailanthus altissima can grow year-round, but it typically dies back to the ground during harsher winters, regrowing from the roots in the spring.

What are the historical uses of Ailanthus altissima?

Ailanthus altissima, also known as tree of heaven, has a rich history of uses.

For centuries, its wood was used for furniture-making, tool handles, and even gunstocks in Europe and Asia. Its leaves and seeds were consumed by humans and animals alike, providing sustenance in times of scarcity. In traditional Chinese medicine, the plant's bark, roots, and leaves were used to treat various ailments, including fever and rheumatism.

Its strong, pungent scent also made it a popular ingredient in perfumes and incense.

These mythological references reflect the significance and reverence for the Tree of Heaven in ancient cultures.

What are the historical texts of Ailanthus altissima?

Ailanthus altissima, also known as tree of heaven, has a rich history that dates back to ancient China.

The oldest known written record of its cultivation is found in the Chinese medicinal text "Shennong Bencaojing" from 200 AD. Later, it was mentioned in the famous pharmacopeia "Ben Cao Gang Mu" by Li Shizhen in 1593 AD.

Additionally, European explorers and botanists, such as Johann Christoph Volkamer and Carl Linnaeus, documented its introduction to the West in the 18th century.
