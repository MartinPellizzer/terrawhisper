What to know about Ailanthus altissima (tree of heaven) before using it medicinally

Tree of heaven, scientifically known as Ailanthus altissima, is a revered herb that has been touted for its impressive health-promoting properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues, reduce inflammation, and even exhibit antiviral and antibacterial activities.

In the culinary realm, tree of heaven is often used to add depth and complexity to stir-fries, braises, and soups, particularly in Asian cuisine. As an ornamental plant, it boasts stunning white flowers and heart-shaped leaves that attract butterflies and other pollinators. Botanically speaking, tree of heaven is a member of the Simaroubaceae family, characterized by its unique compound leaves and small, winged seeds.

Throughout history, tree of heaven has been referenced in traditional medicine, with records dating back to ancient Chinese and Japanese practices, solidifying its status as a highly valued and versatile herb.
