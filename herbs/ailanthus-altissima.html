Ailanthus altissima

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ailanthus altiissima has large, pinnately compound leaves that typically reach 1 to 3 feet in length and consist of 10 to 40 pairs of lanceolate leaflets arranged oppositely along a central rachis. The leaflets are characterized by an entire margin and a smooth, somewhat shiny surface texture. The tree exhibits a fast-growing, upright growth habit with bark that becomes deeply furrowed and grayish as the specimen matures. Small, yellowish-green flowers appear in dense panicles during the summer months. These flowers are eventually replaced by numerous small, winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Taiwan, and parts of Japan. Due to its ability to thrive in various climates, it has been widely introduced to other continents including North America and parts of Europe. In these non-native regions, it often colonizes disturbed areas, roadsides, and urban environments. Its rapid growth and high tolerance for different soil types allow it to spread aggressively through these new territories. Consequently, it is frequently categorized as an invasive species in many areas outside of its original range.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ailantus altissima contains several bioactive secondary metabolites including quassinoids, alkaloids, triterpenes, and flavonoids that possess diverse pharmacological activities. The most significant medicinal components are the quassinoids, such as ailanthone, which exhibit potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antimalarial properties. These compounds are often studied for their ability to inhibit protein synthesis in certain pathogens. Additionally, the presence of various alkaloids and terpenoids suggests potential applications in treating skin infections and inflammatory conditions. Research into these chemical constituents continues to explore their efficacy in modern pharmaceutical development.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Ailantus altirossima targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like quassinoids that interfere with the signaling pathways of these specific proteins. By inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2, the extracts can effectively reduce the production of prostaglandins responsible for pain and swelling. Furthermore, the modulation of nitric oxide synthase levels helps in controlling oxidative stress and cellular inflammation. These interactions with enzymatic and cytokine targets provide the biochemical basis for the plant's traditional use in treating skin diseases and inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ailanthus altissima?

Ailantus altissima has therapeutic properties characterized by its use as a bitter, purgative, and vermicide agent in traditional medicinal practices. The intense bitterness of the plant bark can stimulate digestive secretions and improve appetite. When administered in specific doses, it acts as a purgative to help clear the intestinal tract. Additionally, its chemical constituents allow it to function as a vermicide to expel intestinal parasites. These biological actions make it a significant subject in ethnobotanical studies.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause skin irritation, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and dizziness if ingested or touched. Contact with the leaves or bark often results in painful dermatitis or localized skin irritation. If the plant is consumed, it frequently triggers gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Some individuals may also experience neurological symptoms like dizziness following exposure. These adverse reactions make the species a significant concern for both human health and domestic animals.
