What is Indigofera deccanensis?

Indigofera deccanensis is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae, native to India.

It is an erect shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 2 meters in height, with a woody stem and branches. Leaves are simple, alternate, and trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets that are ovate or elliptical in shape, with a length of 2-8 cm and a width of 1-4 cm. The inflorescence is a spike-like raceme, bearing 2-6 purple flowers with a calyx of five sepals and a corolla of five petals.

Flowers are followed by flat, linear seed pods, each containing 2-6 seeds, which are 6-8 mm long and 3-4 mm wide.
