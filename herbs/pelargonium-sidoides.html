Pelargonium sidoides, commonly known as South African geranium, is a medicinal herb that has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of upper respiratory tract infections, such as the common cold and flu.

The herb's main health benefits include reducing inflammation, soothing sore throats, and relieving coughs. Traditionally, the roots of the herb are used to make a herbal preparation called Umckaloabo, which is often used to treat respiratory infections and other health conditions.
