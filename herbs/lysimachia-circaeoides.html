Lysimachia circaeoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia circaeoides?

Lysimachia circaeoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Ericales. It is a member of the family Primulaceae, falling under the genus Lysimachia, and is specifically identified by its species name, circaeoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia circaeoides has erect, glabrous stems that reach heights of 10 to 60 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins.

The foliage is typically bright green and tapers to a sharp point at the apex. Small, yellow, five-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils in a terminal cyme. Each bloom features a central cluster of stamens that provide a subtle contrast to the bright corolla.

The plant's overall structure is slender and upright, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
