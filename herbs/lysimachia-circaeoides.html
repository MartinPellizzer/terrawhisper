Lysimachia circaeoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia circaeoides?

Lysimachia circaeoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Ericales. It is a member of the family Primulaceae, falling under the genus Lysimachia, and is specifically identified by its species name, circaeoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia circaeoides has erect, glabrous stems that reach heights of 10 to 60 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically bright green and tapers to a sharp point at the apex. Small, yellow, five-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils in a terminal cyme. Each bloom features a central cluster of stamens that provide a subtle contrast to the bright corolla. The plant's overall structure is slender and upright, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds kaempferol, kaempferol-3-O-beta-D-glucoside, luteolin, quercetin, and rutin. These specific phytochemicals belong to the flavonoid family, which is known for providing significant antioxidant properties. The presence of quercetin and rutin suggests that the plant may assist in reducing oxidative stress within biological systems. Additionally, the inclusion of luteolin and kaempferol derivatives contributes to its potential pharmacological profile. Together, these diverse compounds form the chemical basis for the plant's medicinal characteristics.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", eight compounds were isolated and identified from the extract of Lysimachia circaeoides. These eight compounds were identified as kaempferol (1), beta-daucosterol (2), beta-sitosterol (3), quercetin (4), luteolin (5), quercetin-3-O-beta-D-galactoside (6), kaempferol-3-O-beta-D-glucoside (7) and rutin (8). The researchers used column chromatographic techniques to isolate these substances and utilized EI-MS and NMR to identify their structures. Compounds 1 through 8 were isolated from this plant for the first time.
