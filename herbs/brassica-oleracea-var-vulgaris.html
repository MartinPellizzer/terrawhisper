Brassica oleracea var. vulgaris

Brassica oleracea var. vulgaris, commonly known as wild cabbage or common kale, is a plant species belonging to the Brassica genus and is closely related to broccoli, cauliflower, and cabbage.

The medicinal properties of this plant are attributed to its high content of glucosinolates, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Consuming wild cabbage has been linked to various health benefits, including reducing the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease and certain types of cancer, as well as supporting digestive health and immune function.

Wild cabbage can be prepared in various herbal forms, including teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be used to support overall health and well-being.
