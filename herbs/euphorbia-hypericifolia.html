Euphorbia hypericifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Euphorbia hypericifolia?

Euphorbia hypericifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is part of the order Malpighiales and the family Euphorbiaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Euphorbia, with its specific identification as the species hypericifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euphorbia hypericifolia has erect, branching stems that reach heights of up to 40 centimeters and feature small, lanceolate leaves arranged in opposite pairs along the length of the plant. The leaves are typically green, sometimes displaying reddish tints, and possess smooth margins with pointed tips. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish cyathia clustered in terminal cymes. The plant also produces a milky white latex when the stems or leaves are damaged. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in rocky or disturbed habitats.
