Crataegus shensiensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crataegus shensiensis has small, serrated leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape and possess a slightly hairy surface. The plant features woody stems that often develop sharp thorns as a defensive mechanism. Its inflorescences consist of small, delicate white flowers that emerge during the blooming season. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy pomes that serve as its fruit. The overall structure is that of a compact shrub or small tree with a dense branching pattern.
