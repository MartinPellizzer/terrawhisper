boneset

Eupatorium perfoliatum

Boneset (Eupatorium perfoliatum) is a member of the Asteraceae family, traditionally used for treatment of fever, relief of inflammation, and as a digestive aid. Native to North America, it has been historically employed in various cultural and regional practices, with the leaves and stems typically harvested for its medicinal properties.

Botanical Identification

Eupatorium perfoliatum, commonly known as Boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America. Belonging to the Asteraceae family, it is also called Cup-plant and St. John's Wort in some regions. The plant has opposite leaves with hollow stems, and its showy white or pale purple flowers appear in clusters. Synonyms in herbal literature include Boneset and St. John's Wort.

Active Compounds

Boneset contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, coumarins, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work together to support immune function and reduce inflammation. Their synergistic effects enhance the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Tincture: Used for treating insect bites and skin irritations.

Safety Profile

Boneset is contraindicated during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulation. It should be avoided in individuals with bleeding disorders or those taking anticoagulants. Always consult a healthcare provider before use to ensure safety.
