Side Effects of eupatorium perfoliatum

Eupatorium perfoliatum causes skin irritation, which may manifest as redness, itching, or a burning sensation upon contact with the plant's leaves or oils.

This herb can also trigger allergic reactions in sensitive individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, swelling, or even anaphylaxis in severe cases. Additionally, it may induce skin rash, particularly in those with pre-existing dermatological conditions or heightened sensitivity to plant-based compounds.

Some users report nausea and dizziness after ingesting or applying the herb, suggesting potential gastrointestinal and neurological side effects. Furthermore, Eupatorium perfoliatum can cause eye and throat irritation, dry mouth, blurred vision, and even nosebleeds, highlighting the need for caution when using this herb, especially in its raw or concentrated forms.

These health effects underscore the importance of proper preparation and consultation with a healthcare professional before using Eupatorium perfoliatum for medicinal purposes.
