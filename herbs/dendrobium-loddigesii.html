Dendrobium loddigesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium loddigesii has elongated, pendulous pseudobulbs that are distinctly compressed and can reach lengths of up to 60 centimeters. These fleshy stems serve as primary water storage organs and are often densely clustered at the base of the plant. The foliage consists of relatively small, leathery leaves that emerge from the apex of each pseudobulb. Its inflorescence produces numerous small, fragrant flowers that typically display a pale yellow or cream coloration with subtle markings. The overall growth habit is epiphytic, allowing the plant to cling to tree branches in its natural habitat.
