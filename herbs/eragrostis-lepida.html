What is Eragrostis lepida?

Eragrostis lepida is a perennial grass native to southwestern Australia, belonging to the family Poaceae.

The plant grows up to 100 cm in height, with a slender culm bearing linear, flat leaves that are 2-10 mm wide and 10-30 cm long. The inflorescence is a panicle, typically 10-30 cm long, bearing spikelets with two to four florets, each containing a single floret and a reduced floret. Each spikelet is 7-10 mm long, with a glume and two to four florets, the uppermost of which is fertile and produces a 3-4 mm long, yellowish or purple flower.

The plant produces seeds with a silky or hairy pericarp, which disperse the seeds.
