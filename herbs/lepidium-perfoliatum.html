Lepidium perfoliatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lepidium perfoliatum?

Lepidium perfoliatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Brassicales and is a member of the family Brassicaceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Lepidium, with its specific designation being the species perfoliatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lepidium perfoliatum has an erect, slender stem that typically reaches heights of 5 to 25 centimeters and supports small, alternate, linear-to-lanceolate leaves. The foliage is characterized by its sparse distribution and narrow shape, often appearing somewhat fleshy or succulent. Its inflorescence consists of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in compact racemes at the stem tips. The fruit is a distinctive small, flattened silicle that is often slightly notched at the apex. These morphological traits collectively define the plant's compact and delicate structural profile.

According to a study published by "Advanced biomedical research", the hydroalcoholic extract of Lepidium perfoliatum was investigated for its activity against the Toxoplasma gondii parasite. The extract was tested at concentrations ranging from 5-600 μg/mL to determine its effects on infected Vero cells. This plant demonstrated a 50% inhibitory concentration value of 14.67 μg/mL. Furthermore, the study recorded a selectivity index of 2.59 for Lepidium perfoliatum. These results indicate that the extract has promising anti-Toxoplasma activity by inhibiting the growth of T. gondii tachyzoites.
