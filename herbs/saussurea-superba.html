Saussurea superba

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea superba?

Saussurea superba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. Its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Saussurea, with its unique designation being the species superba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea superba has a massive rosette of thick, leathery leaves that are lanceolate in shape and often feature dense, white woolly hairs on their undersides. These basal leaves are arranged in a tight circular pattern to protect the plant's core from harsh alpine environments. From the center of this rosette, a tall, sturdy flowering stalk emerges, reaching significant heights to support heavy inflorescences. The flowers are clustered in dense, yellowish-white heads that sit atop the elongated stem. This specialized morphology allows the plant to conserve heat and moisture in high-altitude rocky habitats.
