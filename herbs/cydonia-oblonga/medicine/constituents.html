10 Medicinal Constituents Of Cydonia Oblonga (Quince)

Cydonia oblonga has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and ascorbic acid.

These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. They have been traditionally used to treat digestive issues, reduce pain and inflammation, and soothe skin irritations.

For instance, the antioxidant properties of quince can help protect against cell damage, potentially reducing the risk of chronic diseases such as cancer and heart disease.

1. Quercetin

Cydonia oblonga quercetin is a type of flavonoid compound found in its fruit and leaves.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Quercetin has been shown to have anti-cancer properties, and may help protect against cardiovascular disease, allergies, and other conditions.

Its antioxidant activity also suggests it could help reduce oxidative stress and promote overall health.

2. Gallic acid

Cydonia oblonga gallic acid is a phenolic compound found in its fruit and leaves.

This polyphenol has been extensively studied for its antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties. Gallic acid has been reported to exhibit cytotoxic effects on various cancer cell lines and may have potential therapeutic applications in the treatment of certain diseases.

Additionally, it has also been used as a natural preservative and flavoring agent in food products.

3. Flavonoids

Cydonia oblonga flavonoids is a class of bioactive compounds that have been identified in its fruit and leaves.

These potent antioxidants have been found to possess numerous pharmacological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antitumor properties.

Flavonoids from Cydonia oblonga have been shown to exhibit protective effects against oxidative stress and inflammation, making them a potential natural remedy for various diseases and disorders.

4. Catechins

Cydonia oblonga catechins is a type of polyphenolic compound found in its fruit, leaves, and seeds.

Catechins are powerful antioxidants that have been linked to various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The quince catechins exhibit unique biochemical profiles compared to those found in green tea, making them attractive for further research and potential applications in pharmaceuticals, food, and cosmetics.

5. Epicatechin

Cydonia oblonga epicatechin is a powerful flavonoid compound found in its fruit and leaves.

This antioxidant-rich molecule has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer properties.

Epicatechin has also been shown to improve cardiovascular health by reducing blood pressure and LDL cholesterol levels, making it a promising natural therapeutic agent for various diseases.

6. Kaempferol

Cydonia oblonga kaempferol is a flavonoid compound found in the fruit of this ancient tree species.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Kaempferol has been shown to have protective effects against certain diseases, such as cardiovascular disease and cancer, and may also play a role in improving cognitive function and reducing the risk of neurodegenerative disorders.

7. Chlorogenic acid

Cydonia oblonga chlorogenic acid is a type of phenolic compound that has been isolated from its fruit.

This antioxidant-rich compound has been found to exhibit numerous biological activities, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties. It has also been shown to possess anticancer potential and may play a role in protecting against cardiovascular disease.

Further research is needed to fully understand the extent of its benefits and applications.

8. Ellagic acid

Cydonia oblonga ellagic acid is a polyphenolic compound found in its fruit and leaves.

This bioactive compound has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. Ellagic acid has also been shown to exhibit anticancer activity by inhibiting the growth of cancer cells and inducing apoptosis (cell death).

Additionally, it may help protect against cardiovascular disease by reducing LDL cholesterol levels and improving blood lipid profiles.

9. Naringenin

Cydonia oblonga naringenin is a flavonoid compound derived from its fruit and seeds.

It has been extensively studied for its numerous biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Naringenin has also been shown to exhibit potential therapeutic benefits in the treatment of various diseases, such as cardiovascular disorders, cancer, and metabolic syndrome.

Its unique chemical structure and bioavailability make it a promising candidate for further research and development into novel pharmaceutical applications.

10. Hesperidin

Cydonia oblonga hesperidin is a flavonoid compound found in the fruit and leaves of this plant.

It is a powerful antioxidant that has been studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Hesperidin has been shown to have a positive effect on cardiovascular health, improving blood vessel function and reducing the risk of heart disease.

Additionally, it may also have anti-cancer properties, although more research is needed to confirm this.
