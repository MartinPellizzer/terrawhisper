Vaccaria hispanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vaccaria hispanica has an annual herbaceous habit characterized by erect, branched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are small, linear to lanceolate, and arranged oppositely along the slender stems. Its flowers are small, white, and possess five petals that are fused at the base. The fruit is a distinctive, woody, kidney-shaped capsule that contains several small seeds. This plant's compact structure allows it to thrive in the arid, sandy environments it typically inhabits.
