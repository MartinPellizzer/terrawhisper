What to know about Tabebuia rosea (pink trumpet tree) before using it medicinally

Pink trumpet tree, scientifically known as Tabebuia rosea, is a treasured herb renowned for its impressive health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving respiratory function.

In the kitchen, pink trumpet tree's delicate flowers and tender leaves are used to add flavor and freshness to salads, soups, and teas, while also making a popular garnish for cocktails and desserts. From an horticultural standpoint, the pink trumpet tree is a low-maintenance shrub that thrives in warm climates and can be grown as an ornamental plant or pruned for its medicinal bark. Botanically, Tabebuia rosea belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range from pink to white.

With a rich history dating back thousands of years to ancient Mayan and Aztec civilizations, the pink trumpet tree has long been revered for its spiritual and medicinal properties, earning it a place in many traditional folk remedies and rituals.
