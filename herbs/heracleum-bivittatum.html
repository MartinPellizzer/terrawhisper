Heracleum bivittatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Heracleum bivittatum?

Heracleum bivittatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Apiales and falls within the family Apiaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Heracleum, leading to its specific designation as the species bivittatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its flowering heads consist of multiple secondary umbels that branch out from a central point to form a broad, flat canopy.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe skin irritation, photosensitivity, blistering, dermatitis, nausea, vomiting, and dizziness. Contact with the sap often triggers a phototoxic reaction where sunlight intensifies the damage to the tissue. This process frequently results in painful blistering and various forms of dermatitis on the affected areas.
