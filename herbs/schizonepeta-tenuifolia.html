Chinese catnip

Schizonepeta tenuifolia

Chinese Catnip (Schizonepeta tenuifolia) is a perennial herb native to China and parts of East Asia, widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported ability to improve circulation, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of respiratory and skin conditions.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Catnip, or Schizonepeta tenuifolia, is a perennial herb in the Lamiaceae family. It is also known as Chinese nepeta or fragrant catnip. Native to East Asia, it has slender stems, opposite leaves, and small purple flowers. It is sometimes referred to as Schizonepeta chinensis in older herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Schizonepeta
  • Species: Schizonepeta tenuifolia

Active Compounds

Chinese Catnip contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, essential oils, and coumarin. These compounds work synergistically to exert therapeutic effects, with flavonoids providing antioxidant support and essential oils contributing to its aromatic and medicinal properties.

  • Flavonoid
  • Essential oil
  • Coumarin

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and as a diaphoretic to promote sweating.

Infusion: Used for colds, flu, and to relieve nasal congestion.

Tincture: Used for respiratory infections and to improve circulation.

Safety Profile

Chinese Catnip is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause mild side effects such as digestive upset or drowsiness. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by individuals with known allergies to the Lamiaceae family.
