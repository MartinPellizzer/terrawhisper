Delphinium biternatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium biternatum has pinnately compound leaves that are divided into two distinct pairs of leaflets arranged along a central axis. These leaflets are typically deeply lobed or serrated, providing a complex architectural structure to the foliage. The plant features elongated, upright stems that support clusters of zygomorphic flowers. Each individual bloom is characterized by a prominent nectar spur located at the back of the petal structure. The overall morphology is defined by this vertical growth habit and the intricate, segmented patterns of its vegetative parts.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Delphinium biternatum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potentially fatal respiratory failure if ingested due to its high concentration of toxic alkaloids. These poisonous compounds act as potent neurotoxins that disrupt the central nervous system. Once consumed, symptoms often include intense vomiting, abdominal pain, and muscle spasms. In more advanced stages of poisoning, an individual may experience convulsions, paralysis, or a complete cessation of breathing. Immediate medical intervention is required to manage these life-threatening physiological reactions.
