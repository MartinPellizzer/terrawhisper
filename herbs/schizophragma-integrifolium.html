Schizophragma integrifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schizophragma integrifolium has pinnately compound leaves characterized by several pairs of entire, lanceolate leaflets that lack serrations along their margins. The leaf stalks and petioles are typically slender and serve to support the complex arrangement of the leaflets. These leaflets exhibit a smooth texture and a prominent central midrib that extends to the tip of each segment. The plant's overall structure is defined by these spreading leaf patterns which help maximize light absorption. Small, inconspicuous flowers and specialized fruit structures further define the morphological profile of this species.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Schizophragma integrifolium can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may trigger redness, itching, or localized swelling in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of any part of the plant is not recommended due to the potential for severe stomach pain or nausea. Because specific toxicological studies on this species are limited, the exact chemical compounds responsible for these reactions remain largely uncharacterized. It is advisable to avoid direct handling without gloves to prevent unpredictable dermatological responses.
