Amygdalus ferganensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Amygdalus ferganensis?

Amygdalus ferganensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Rosales and falls within the family Rosaceae. At the most specific taxonomic levels, it is identified by the genus Amygdalus and the species ferganensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amygdalus ferganensis has a deciduous, woody shrub or small tree structure characterized by slender, spreading branches and lanceolate to narrowly elliptical leaves. Its flowers are solitary or arranged in small clusters, emerging from the woody stems during the early spring season. The fruit develops into a small, drupaceous nutlet enclosed within a hard, woody endocarp.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe cyanide poisoning if ingested, leading to critical physiological complications. Initial symptoms of toxicity often include nausea, vomiting, headache, and dizziness. As the condition progresses, an individual may experience mental confusion and significant respiratory distress. More extreme neurological and cardiovascular reactions can manifest as seizures and tachycardia.
