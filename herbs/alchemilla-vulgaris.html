Alchemilla Vulgaris Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Alchemilla vulgaris, commonly known as lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

It is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its soothing and healing properties, particularly for women's health issues and skin conditions.

The herb contains bioactive constituents such as tannins, flavonoids, and mucilage, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and wound-healing effects.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and topical salves can be made from Alchemilla vulgaris to harness its therapeutic benefits.
