Lady's Mantle

Alchemilla vulgaris

Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its distinctive shield-shaped leaves and medicinal properties that have been used for centuries. It is primarily used to support women's health, particularly for conditions such as menstrual irregularities, menopause symptoms, and vaginal dryness.

Taxonomy

Lady's Mantle, or Alchemilla vulgaris, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, classified under the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, genus Alchemilla, and species Alchemilla vulgaris.

Morphology

Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) is a low-growing perennial herb with a basal rosette of shield-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it produces upright flowering stems bearing small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer.

Distribution

Alchemilla vulgaris, commonly known as Lady's Mantle, is native to temperate regions of Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and is widely distributed across moist meadows, woodlands, and rocky soils in these areas.

Traditional Uses

Traditionally, Lady's Mantle has been used in herbal medicine to support women's health, particularly for menstrual issues, uterine bleeding, and as a mild tonic for the reproductive system.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and European herbalism, Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) has been historically used for its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and hemostatic properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) includes a variety of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, triterpenes, and polyphenols, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties.

Pharmacology

Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including astringent, anti-inflammatory, hemostatic, and mild antimicrobial actions, which support its traditional use in treating wounds, menstrual irregularities, and gastrointestinal issues.

Botanical Identification

Alchemilla vulgaris, commonly known as Lady's Mantle, belongs to the Rosaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features broad, shield-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers, with synonyms including Alchemilla xanthochlora and Alchemilla mollis in historical herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Alchemilla vulgaris is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and by individuals with kidney disorders. It may interact with certain medications, so consulting a healthcare provider before use is advisable.
