Lady's Mantle

Alchemilla vulgaris

Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) is a member of the Rosaceae family and has been traditionally used for treatment of wounds, digestive issues, and skin conditions. The plant parts commonly utilized include leaves, flowers, and roots, and it has a long history of cultural and regional use in herbal medicine across Europe.

Botanical Identification

Alchemilla vulgaris, commonly known as Lady's Mantle, belongs to the Rosaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features broad, shield-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers, with synonyms including Alchemilla xanthochlora and Alchemilla mollis in historical herbal texts.

Lady's Mantle Identification.

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of Lady's Mantle include flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. They work synergistically to support its traditional uses in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a mild diuretic.

Decoction: Used for treating skin conditions and inflammation.

Powder: Used for topical application to reduce inflammation and irritation.

Safety Profile

Alchemilla vulgaris is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and by individuals with kidney disorders. It may interact with certain medications, so consulting a healthcare provider before use is advisable.
