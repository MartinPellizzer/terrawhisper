What to know about Alchemilla vulgaris (lady's mantle) before using it medicinally

Lady's mantle, scientifically known as Alchemilla vulgaris, is a herb that has been prized for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to soothe digestive issues and reduce inflammation.

In the culinary world, lady's mantle is often used to add a tangy flavor to salads, soups, and sauces, while its leaves can be steeped into a tea to make a refreshing brew. As a horticultural gem, lady's mantle is relatively low-maintenance and can thrive in a variety of conditions, making it an ideal addition to any garden or indoor space. From a botanical perspective, lady's mantle boasts small yellow flowers and distinctive scalloped leaves that are said to resemble a cloak or "mantle", hence its name.

With references dating back to ancient Greek and Roman times, where it was used as a treatment for menstrual cramps and other women's health issues, lady's mantle has been a revered herb in many cultures throughout history.
