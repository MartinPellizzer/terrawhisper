What's the taxonomical classification of Osmanthus delavayi?

Osmanthus delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It is further categorized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Oleaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Osmanthus, and its unique species designation, delavayi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Osmanthus delavayi has evergreen leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length with entire, slightly leathery margins and a glossy dark green upper surface. The foliage is characterized by a dense arrangement on woody stems, providing a thick texture to the shrub or small tree. Small, white, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically blooming in late autumn or winter. These delicate blossoms are highly fragrant and possess four spreading lobes at the mouth of the corolla. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark color upon ripening.
