Ficus Religiosa Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Ficus religiosa, commonly known as the sacred fig, is a tree native to South and Southeast Asia, valued for its medicinal properties.

This herb is widely used in traditional medicine for its diverse health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects.

The therapeutic actions of Ficus religiosa are attributed to its bioactive constituents such as alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins.

Herbal preparations like infusions, decoctions, and poultices can be made from its leaves, bark, or fruits to harness its healing properties.

Ficus religiosa kaempferol is another flavonoid that possesses antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antitumor activities, contributing to the plant's therapeutic potential in treating various ailments.

Ficus religiosa ellagic acid is a phenolic acid with strong antioxidant properties, known to inhibit the growth of cancer cells and support detoxification processes within the body.

Side Effects of ficus religiosa

The herb causes skin irritation because its sap contains substances that can provoke an inflammatory response in the skin upon contact.

Ficus religiosa results in eye redness as the irritant compounds may lead to increased blood flow and inflammation in the conjunctiva.

The herb leads to allergic reactions because some individuals may have hypersensitivity to the proteins or other allergens present in the plant's sap.
