Ficus Religiosa: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Ficus religiosa, commonly known as the sacred fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South and Southeast Asia, valued for its medicinal properties.

This herb is renowned for its leaves, bark, and fruit, which have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The health benefits of Ficus religiosa include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, which support immune function and digestive health.

Its therapeutic actions encompass reducing fever, treating skin conditions, and promoting wound healing, while its bioactive constituents include flavonoids, tannins, and alkaloids.
