What is Aristida glauca?

Aristida glauca is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is classified within the tribe Aristideae. The plant grows up to 1 meter in height, with a slender stem and a panicle inflorescence that is 10-30 cm long, bearing spikelets with two to four florets. The glumes are one-nerved and keeled, while the lemmas are three-nerved and glabrous.

The species is characterized by its pale blue-green leaves and its habit of growing in dry, sandy soils.
