Impatiens balansae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens balansae has succulent, erect stems that reach heights of up to 1 meter and are topped by terminal clusters of zygomorphic, pink to purple flowers with yellow nectar guides. The leaves are lanceolate to ovate, featuring serrated margins and an opposite arrangement along the fleshy green stems. Its floral structure is characterized by a prominent spurred sepal and a complex arrangement of petals that attract specific pollinators. The plant typically features a shallow root system suited for moist, shaded environments. Small, capsule-like seed pods develop after flowering and possess a ballistic mechanism for seed dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically occurring in areas such as Thailand, Vietnam, and parts of southern China. It primarily thrives in the humid, shaded environments of moist forest floors and montane regions. The distribution is largely restricted to these specific microclimates where consistent moisture is available. Some populations may also be found in isolated pockets across other subtropical Asian territories. These localized habitats ensure the species remains concentrated within specific ecological zones.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic reaction, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach cramps if handled or ingested. Contact with the foliage or stems may lead to redness or itching on the skin. Some individuals might experience a more severe allergic reaction upon exposure to the plant's juices.
