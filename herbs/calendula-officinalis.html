Marigold

Calendula officinalis

Marigold (Calendula officinalis) is a flowering plant known for its bright orange or yellow petals and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions and promote wound healing.

Marigold, or Calendula officinalis, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and widespread use in herbal medicine.

Morphology

Marigold, or Calendula officinalis, is an annual herb characterized by its stout, hairy stems, lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that consist of a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Distribution

Marigold (Calendula officinalis) is native to southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, with its natural range extending from the Mediterranean region to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

Traditional Uses

Marigold (Calendula officinalis) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antifungal, and wound-healing properties, often applied topically to treat skin conditions, burns, and minor cuts.

Medicinal Systems

Marigold, or Calendula officinalis, has been utilized in traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Chinese medicine, and European herbalism for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and skin-healing properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Marigold (Calendula officinalis) is characterized by a rich composition of flavonoids, triterpenoids, carotenoids, and essential oils, with alpha-tocopherol, beta-carotene, and flavonol glycosides such as quercetin and apigenin being the most prominent bioactive compounds.

Pharmacology

Marigold (Calendula officinalis) exhibits anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing properties, making it effective for treating skin conditions, reducing inflammation, and promoting tissue repair.

Botanical Identification

Marigold, scientifically known as Calendula officinalis, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is also called pot marigold in some regions. This annual plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It has yellow-orange flowers, hairy leaves, and a taproot. Historically, it was also referred to as common marigold or garden marigold in herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Marigold, or Calendula officinalis, is generally safe when used appropriately. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by those with known allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family.
