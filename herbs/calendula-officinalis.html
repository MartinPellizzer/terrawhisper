What to know about Calendula officinalis before using it medicinally

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as pot marigold, is a medicinal herb that has been used to treat various health conditions, including wounds, skin irritations, and inflammatory disorders such as eczema and acne.

The herb is known for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which provide several health benefits, including reducing inflammation, promoting wound healing, and protecting against infection. The therapeutic properties of Calendula officinalis include its ability to soothe and calm irritated skin, reduce redness and swelling, and promote tissue repair. The herb contains bioactive compounds such as triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and carotenoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Calendula officinalis is often used to make topical creams, ointments, and salves, as well as teas and infusions, which are used to promote skin health and reduce inflammation.
