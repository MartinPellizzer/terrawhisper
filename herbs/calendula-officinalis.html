Calendula Officinalis Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Calendula officinalis, commonly known as the pot marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is widely used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antifungal, and wound-healing properties.

The bioactive constituents in calendula, such as flavonoids, triterpenes, and carotenoids, contribute to its therapeutic actions.

This herb can be prepared as an infusion, tincture, salve, or ointment to address skin irritations, digestive issues, and respiratory ailments.

Medicinal Parts

Calendula officinalis flower is the primary medicinal part used for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and wound-healing properties.

The flower is commonly used to make infusions, tinctures, and salves, which are applied topically to treat skin irritations, eczema, and minor burns.

Calendula officinalis leaf, while less commonly used than the flower, also possesses mild anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The leaf can be used to prepare herbal teas or poultices, though its medicinal applications are generally more limited compared to the flower.
