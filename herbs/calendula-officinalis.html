Marigold

Calendula officinalis

Marigold (Calendula officinalis) is a flowering plant known for its bright orange or yellow petals and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions and promote wound healing.

Botanical Identification

Marigold, scientifically known as Calendula officinalis, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is also called pot marigold in some regions. This annual plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It has yellow-orange flowers, hairy leaves, and a taproot. Historically, it was also referred to as common marigold or garden marigold in herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Asterales
  • Family: Asteraceae
  • Genus: Calendula
  • Species: Calendula officinalis

Safety Profile

Marigold, or Calendula officinalis, is generally safe when used appropriately. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by those with known allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family.
