What is Legousia julianii?

Legousia julianii is a species of flowering plant in the family Campanulaceae, native to the Mediterranean region.

It is an annual or biennial herb, 10-50 cm tall, with simple, linear-lanceolate leaves and a raceme of bell-shaped flowers, usually blue or purple, 10-15 mm long, with five lobes and a distinct calyx. The flowers are hermaphroditic, with a corolla of five lobes and a calyx of five sepals, and are arranged in a terminal inflorescence. The plant has a fibrous root system and a woody base, and it grows in dry, rocky areas and along roadsides.

The species is characterized by its small size, simple leaves, and showy, bell-shaped flowers, which are similar to those of other Legousia species.
