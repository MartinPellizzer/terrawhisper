Cassia mannii

What's the taxonomical classification of Cassia mannii?

Cassia mannii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage through the order Fabales, it is placed in the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Cassia and is specifically designated as the species mannii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cassia mannii contains bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and glycosides that contribute to its pharmacological properties. These specific secondary metabolites are responsible for the plant's documented antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers have focused on these compounds to explore their potential in treating various infections and reducing swelling in biological tissues. The presence of tannins and saponins particularly suggests a capacity for wound healing and antioxidant defense. Such chemical constituents make the plant a significant subject for ethnobotanical and pharmaceutical study.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cassia mannii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach aches, fever, and various types of inflammation. The bark and leaves are often prepared into decoctions or poultices to address these specific ailments. Local healers frequently apply the crushed plant matter directly to wounds to prevent infection and promote healing. When ingested, the extracts are believed to help soothe gastrointestinal distress and regulate body temperature. These medicinal applications are central to the traditional healing practices involving the species.
