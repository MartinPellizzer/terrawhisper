White Lily (Lilium candidum)

White Lily (Lilium candidum) is a member of the Liliaceae family, native to Central Asia, Southern Europe, and Mediterranean Region. Traditionally, its bulbs, flowers, and leaves have been used for infusions, decoctions, and oil infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and sedative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 40 to 100 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface white to pale green and lower surface pale green to silvery white, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of solitary flowers with six white tepals, actinomorphic symmetry, and a prominent central stamen cluster surrounded by a cupular nectary.
Stems
Cylindrical, hollow, terete, glabrous, herbaceous, with nodes and internodes, exhibiting a sympodial growth habit, and bearing opposite, sheathing, leaf bases.
