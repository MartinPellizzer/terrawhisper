Gossypium arboreum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gossypium arboreuum has a woody, tree-like structure characterized by upright stems, palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and creamy white to pale yellow flowers. The plant grows into a small tree reaching heights of up to five meters, distinguishing it from its shrubby relatives. Its leaves are typically dark green and possess a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs on the surface. During the flowering stage, the blossoms feature a prominent central column of stamens that surround the pistil. Once pollination occurs, the plant develops distinctive green fruit known as capsules which eventually burst to reveal soft, white linty fibers and dark seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America, spanning from Mexico through much of the Caribbean and into the northern parts of South America. It thrives in warm, humid environments where consistent moisture and high temperatures are available. Throughout its history, cultivation has expanded to various tropical zones worldwide due to its importance in fiber production. In many regions, its presence is heavily influenced by human agricultural practices rather than purely natural spread. Consequently, its distribution is closely linked to areas that support intensive cotton farming.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Gossypium arboreuum targets specific proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various inflammatory cytokines to exert its pharmacological effects. The plant contains bioactive polyphenols and terpenoids that inhibit the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway, thereby reducing the production of pro-inflammatory mediators. These compounds also interact with antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase to mitigate oxidative stress in cellular environments. Furthermore, certain extracts demonstrate an ability to modulate nuclear factor kappa B signaling, which helps regulate immune responses. Through these diverse protein-ligand interactions, the plant exhibits significant anti-inflammatory and cytoprotective properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Gossypium arboreum?

Gossypium arboreuum has therapeutic actions that include being an astringent, hemostatic, anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and emmenagogue. Its astringent properties help to constrict body tissues, while its hemostatic qualities allow it to effectively stop bleeding. The plant also serves as an anti-inflammatory agent to reduce swelling and an antiseptic to prevent infection in wounds. Additionally, it acts as an emmenagogue to stimulate or increase menstrual flow. These diverse biological activities make it a versatile component in traditional medicinal practices.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe physiological harm due to its high concentration of the toxic polyphenolic compound known as gossypol. Ingesting parts of the plant may lead to acute gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Individuals often experience significant abdominal pain following consumption. Some people may also suffer from neurological symptoms such as dizziness. These side effects can escalate if the toxic components interfere with normal metabolic functions.

The main side effects this plant can have are shown in the list below.

  • Toxicity (gossypol)
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Abdominal pain
  • Dizziness
