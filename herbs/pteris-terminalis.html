Pteris terminalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Pteris terminalis?

Pteris terminalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Pteridaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Pteris, where it is identified as the species terminalis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris terminalis has pinnate fronds that typically measure between 20 and 60 centimeters in length, featuring a dark green color and a distinctively textured surface. The individual pinnae are lanceolate in shape and are arranged symmetrically along a central rachis that often displays a reddish or brownish hue. The margins of the leaflets are frequently serrated or lobed, which contributes to the overall delicate appearance of the foliage. Small, kidney-shaped sori are located on the underside of the mature leaves to facilitate spore production. These morphological features allow the fern to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
