Tussilago (Tussilago Alba)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Tussilago alba.

Tussilago, scientifically known as Tussilago alba, is a medicinal herb commonly referred to as the "foam flower" due to its frothy appearance when the leaves are crushed. This plant is renowned for its primary benefits in respiratory health, particularly as a natural expectorant and demulcent that helps soothe coughs and ease the removal of mucus from the airways. Traditionally used in various cultures, including European and Native American traditions, Tussilago has been valued for its ability to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis, asthma, and other respiratory ailments. In modern wellness practices, it is often incorporated into herbal remedies, cough syrups, and lozenges to support lung function and reduce inflammation. One of its unique features is the presence of a rare active compound called tussilagine, which contributes to its soothing properties, and it has a mild, slightly bitter flavor that is often masked in formulations.
