10 Possible Side Effects Of Rauvolfia Vomitoria

Rauvolfia vomitoria has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, dizziness, and fatigue.

These side effects occur due to the herb's high content of reserpine, a compound that can disrupt the body's neurotransmitter balance. If left unchecked, these side effects can worsen people's lives by impairing daily functioning, social interactions, and overall quality of life.

In severe cases, they may even lead to anxiety disorders and depression.

1. Causes hypertension

Rauvolfia vomitoria causes hypertension due to its ability to slow down the heart rate and reduce blood pressure.

This is because it contains a compound called reserpine, which acts on the body's sympathetic nervous system, causing blood vessels to constrict and increasing cardiac output.

As a result, people who use Rauvolfia vomitoria may experience an increase in their blood pressure, leading to hypertension.

2. Triggers respiratory depression

Rauvolfia vomitoria triggers respiratory depression due to its ability to slow down the central nervous system and depress breathing.

This occurs because the plant contains a number of alkaloids, including reserpine and rescinnamine, which bind to serotonin receptors in the brain, leading to decreased respiratory drive and potentially life-threatening respiratory failure.

As a result, individuals taking Rauvolfia vomitoria may experience shallow or rapid breathing, blue discoloration of the skin and mucous membranes due to poor oxygenation, and even loss of consciousness.

3. Produces hallucinations

Rauvolfia vomitoria produces hallucinations due to its ability to alter the levels of serotonin and dopamine in the brain.

The plant contains alkaloids that bind to specific receptors, leading to an overload of neurotransmitters. This can cause visual and auditory disturbances, often accompanied by altered perception, disorientation, and distorted senses of time and space.

As a result, individuals may experience vivid hallucinations, further complicating their mental state.

4. Induces sedation

Rauvolfia vomitoria induces sedation due to its ability to increase the levels of serotonin and GABA in the brain.

As a result, users may experience feelings of relaxation and reduced anxiety, but may also feel drowsy or lethargic.

The plant's active compounds, including reserpine, have been shown to affect neurotransmitters, leading to sedative effects that can impact daily activities.

5. Leads to bradycardia

Rauvolfia vomitoria leads to bradycardia due to its ability to stimulate the vagus nerve, which regulates heart rate.

The plant's alkaloids, particularly reserpine and rescinnamine, interact with serotonin and other neurotransmitters in the nervous system, causing a decrease in heart rate.

This can lead to symptoms such as dizziness, fatigue, and shortness of breath, potentially worsening underlying cardiovascular conditions.

6. Causes urinary retention

Rauvolfia vomitoria causes urinary retention due to its ability to relax smooth muscle, including the muscles in the bladder neck and urethra.

This relaxation can lead to increased pressure within the bladder, making it difficult for urine to flow out of the body. Additionally, Rauvolfia vomitoria's anticholinergic properties can further exacerbate this issue by reducing the contractions that help propel urine through the urinary tract.

As a result, individuals using Rauvolfia vomitoria may experience painful urination, frequent urination, and in severe cases, complete inability to void the bladder.

7. Triggers seizures

Rauvolfia vomitoria triggers seizures due to its ability to affect the central nervous system.

The plant contains alkaloids such as reserpine and rescinnamine, which can alter serotonin levels in the brain, leading to convulsions and seizures.

Additionally, the plant's interaction with other neurotransmitters like dopamine and norepinephrine may also contribute to this side effect.

8. Increases serotonin levels

Rauvolfia vomitoria increases serotonin levels by inhibiting the breakdown of this neurotransmitter in the brain.

This is due to the presence of a chemical compound called reserpine, which blocks the action of monoamine oxidase (MAO), an enzyme responsible for degrading serotonin.

As a result, serotonin levels accumulate and can lead to various side effects, including changes in mood, appetite, and sleep patterns.

9. Disrupts heart rhythm

Rauvolfia vomitoria disrupts heart rhythm due to its ability to affect the autonomic nervous system and alter the normal balance of parasympathetic and sympathetic activity.

The alkaloids present in the plant, particularly reserpine, can bind to specific receptors in the heart, leading to changes in cardiac conduction and rhythm.

This disruption can result in an irregular heartbeat or palpitations, which may be a common side effect of using Rauvolfia vomitoria.

10. Causes dry mouth

Rauvolfia vomitoria causes dry mouth due to its anticholinergic properties, which can block the action of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter responsible for controlling salivation.

As the body's natural response to reduce saliva production is disrupted, individuals may experience persistent dryness in their mouths, making it uncomfortable and potentially affecting daily activities.
