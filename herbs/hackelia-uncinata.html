Hackelia uncinata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hackelia uncinata has erect, hairy stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 80 centimeters and bear leaves that are primarily basal or arranged in a rosette. The basal leaves are often long, lanceolate, and possess serrated margins, while the upper stem leaves are smaller and more reduced in size. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cyme containing small, five-lobed blue or purple flowers. The most distinctive morphological feature is the fruit, which is a nutlet covered in stiff, hooked bristles designed for animal dispersal. These bristly nutlets are often found in clusters and serve as the primary identification marker for the species.
