What's the taxonomical classification of Hackelia uncinata?

Hackelia uncinata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Boraginales and the family Boraginaceae, eventually reaching its specific identification within the genus Hackelia, where it is designated as the species uncinata.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Hackelia uncinata can cause painful skin irritation, redness, and localized inflammation upon direct contact with its hooked hairs. These microscopic bristles act like tiny needles that embed themselves into the skin, leading to persistent itching and discomfort. Some individuals may experience a more severe allergic reaction characterized by swelling or a spreading rash. If the hairs are not carefully removed, the irritation can last for several days. Avoiding contact with the plant is the best way to prevent these dermatological issues.
