10 Possible Side Effects Of Cirsium Lanceolatum

Cirsium lanceolatum has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, skin irritation, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption or incorrect preparation of the herb.

For instance, an individual taking high doses may experience nausea, diarrhea, and stomach cramps, leading to malnutrition, dehydration, and even life-threatening conditions.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Cirsium lanceolatum causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high content of sesquiterpene lactones and other bioactive compounds.

These substances can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as skin rash, itching, and respiratory issues.

The plant's allergenic properties are thought to be linked to the presence of specific proteins and carbohydrates that stimulate the production of IgE antibodies, resulting in an overactive immune response.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Cirsium lanceolatum triggers asthma attacks due to its allergenic properties.

The plant's pollen and other particles can become airborne and cause an allergic reaction in susceptible individuals, leading to constriction of the airways and increased mucus production.

This reaction can trigger symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath, which may worsen over time if left untreated.

3. Induces dizziness

Cirsium lanceolatum induces dizziness due to its sedative properties and potential interaction with central nervous system receptors.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including lactones and polyacetylenes, can affect the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to feelings of lightheadedness or spinning sensation.

This side effect may be more pronounced when ingesting large doses or combining Cirsium lanceolatum with other sedative herbs or substances.

4. Produces nausea and vomiting

Cirsium lanceolatum produces nausea and vomiting.

The plant's bitter compounds, including sesquiterpenes and phenolic acids, are thought to cause gastrointestinal distress in some individuals. These compounds may irritate the stomach lining and stimulate the vomiting center in the brain, leading to unpleasant symptoms.

Additionally, the plant's high fiber content can slow digestion and contribute to feelings of nausea.

5. Increases blood pressure

Cirsium lanceolatum increases blood pressure.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's high concentration of inulin and saponins, which can stimulate the cardiovascular system and cause an increase in blood pressure.

Additionally, Cirsium lanceolatum contains alkaloids that may have a vasoconstrictive effect, further contributing to this side effect.

6. Reduces heart rate

Cirsium lanceolatum reduces heart rate due to its ability to dilate blood vessels and lower blood pressure.

The cardiac depressant properties of the plant's constituents, such as saponins and flavonoids, contribute to this effect by slowing down the heart's contractions.

This can lead to a decrease in heart rate, potentially resulting in symptoms like dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting in individuals who consume Cirsium lanceolatum.

7. Worsens kidney function

Cirsium lanceolatum worsens kidney function due to its high concentration of saponins, which can cause increased urine production and diuretic effects.

Additionally, the plant's ability to inhibit P-glycoprotein, a protein responsible for removing toxins from the body, may contribute to the exacerbation of kidney issues.

As a result, individuals with pre-existing kidney problems or compromised renal function may experience worsening symptoms when using Cirsium lanceolatum.

8. Disrupts liver enzymes

Cirsium lanceolatum disrupts liver enzymes, which can cause an imbalance in the body's detoxification processes.

This is due to the plant's ability to alter the activity of certain enzymes responsible for metabolizing drugs and toxins.

Specifically, Cirsium lanceolatum contains bioactive compounds such as sesquiterpenes and polyacetylenes that may inhibit or induce the production of liver enzymes, leading to changes in the body's normal detoxification mechanisms.

9. Affects fertility rates

Cirsium lanceolatum affects fertility rates.

This is due to its ability to suppress the production of testosterone and estrogen in the body, leading to reduced sperm count and abnormal ovulation. Additionally, its phytoestrogenic properties can mimic the effects of estrogen, disrupting hormone balances and potentially impacting reproductive health.

As a result, regular use of Cirsium lanceolatum may lead to difficulties conceiving or maintaining pregnancy.

10. Lowers white blood cell count

Cirsium lanceolatum lowers white blood cell count due to its potential to cause bone marrow suppression.

This is believed to be a result of the herb's ability to inhibit the production of new cells in the bone marrow, leading to a decrease in the number of white blood cells produced.

Additionally, the herb's high concentration of saponins may contribute to this effect by altering the activity of certain enzymes involved in cell division and differentiation.
