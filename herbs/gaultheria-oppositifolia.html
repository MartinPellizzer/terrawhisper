Red Alder (Gaultheria Oppositifolia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Gaultheria oppositifolia.

Red alder, scientifically known as Gaultheria oppositifolia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, known for its medicinal properties and use as a traditional herb. It contains high levels of methyl salicylate, a compound with analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, making it a natural remedy for pain relief and reducing inflammation. Traditionally, Indigenous peoples such as the Coast Salish and Tlingit used red alder to treat headaches, muscle pain, and skin conditions, often applying its leaves or oils topically. In modern wellness practices, red alder is used in aromatherapy, topical salves, and as a natural alternative to synthetic pain relievers. Its distinctive peppery, eucalyptus-like flavor and historical significance in traditional medicine make it a unique and valued plant in both cultural and therapeutic contexts.
