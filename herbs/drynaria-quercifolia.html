Drynaria quercifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Drynaria quercifolia has pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of oblong to elliptical leaflets that resemble the shape of oak foliage. The plant features a woody, climbing habit with stems that utilize specialized structures for support. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in dense axillary clusters. The leaflets are characterized by entire or slightly wavy margins and a texture that can range from smooth to somewhat leathery. These morphological traits help distinguish the species within its specific taxonomic group.

Drynaria quercifolia is prepared as decoctions, infusions, and topical poultices derived from its leaves and roots. What side-effects this plant can have?

Drynaria quercifolia can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested in improper quantities. Because the plant contains various bioactive alkaloids, it may trigger unexpected allergic responses in sensitive individuals. Contact with the sap or crushed leaves can lead to localized redness or itching on the skin. There is also limited scientific documentation regarding its long-term systemic effects on internal organs. Users should exercise extreme caution and avoid self-medication with this species due to these unknown risks.
