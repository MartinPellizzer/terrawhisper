Drynaria quercifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Drynaria quercifolia?

Drynaria quercifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Polypodiaceae, specifically within the genus Drynaria, with its unique identity defined by the species name quercifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Drynaria quercifolia has pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of oblong to elliptical leaflets that resemble the shape of oak foliage. The plant features a woody, climbing habit with stems that utilize specialized structures for support. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in dense axillary clusters. The leaflets are characterized by entire or slightly wavy margins and a texture that can range from smooth to somewhat leathery. These morphological traits help distinguish the species within its specific taxonomic group.

What are the pharmacological activities of Drynaria quercifolia?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its anti-implantation, anti-fertility, abortifacient, uterotonic, and anti-inflammatory activities. The phytochemical constituents within Drynaria quercifolia contribute to its ability to prevent pregnancy by disrupting the implantation process or inducing abortion. Furthermore, its uterotonic properties suggest a capacity to stimulate uterine contractions, which supports its reported reproductive effects. Beyond its impact on the reproductive system, the plant also exhibits notable anti-inflammatory actions that help reduce swelling and pain. These diverse medicinal properties make it a subject of intense interest for pharmacological research.

As discussed in a study, the methanol extract of Drynaria quercifolia (L.) J. Smith rhizome showed 87% effectiveness on uterotonic activity, while the aqueous extract showed 68% effectiveness. The methanolic extract demonstrated higher efficacy for both abortifaicient (p<0.01) and anti-implantation performance (p<0.01). This methanolic extract also effected hormone release levels at a significance of p<0.01. The extracts showed a relatively non-toxic effect during the acute toxicity study. Ultimately, the methanolic extract of the Drynaria quercifolia (L.) J. Smith rhizome was proved to have significant anti-fertility activity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds friedelin, beta-amyrin, beta-sitosterol, eriocitrin, and naringenin. These specific bioactive molecules contribute to various therapeutic properties within the organism. For instance, the triterpenoids friedelin and beta-amyrin are often studied for their anti-inflammatory potential. Meanwhile, the sterol beta-sitosterol is recognized for its ability to support cardiovascular health. The flavonoids eriocitrin and naringenin provide significant antioxidant activities that protect cells from oxidative stress. Together, these diverse chemical constituents form a complex profile of pharmacological interest.

As discussed in a study, the dried rhizome of Drynaria quercifolia contains several medicinal compounds including friedelin, epifriedelinol, beta-amyrin, beta-sitosterol, beta-sitosterol 3-beta-D-glucopyranoside, and naringin. These specific substances were isolated from the plant during the research. Furthermore, the methanol extract of Drynaria quercifolia demonstrated broad and concentration-dependent antibacterial activity. This study focused on characterizing the phytochemical properties of the species.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating Neisseria gonorrhoeae bacterial infections, chronic jaundice, liver fibrosis, and rheumatism. Practitioners utilize its medicinal properties to target various systemic ailments affecting the reproductive and hepatic systems. It serves as a natural remedy for managing the inflammation associated with chronic joint pain and rheumatism. Furthermore, the plant is applied to address serious liver complications such as fibrosis and jaundice. By addressing these diverse bacterial and inflammatory conditions, it plays a significant role in traditional healing practices.
