Celtis sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis sinensis?

Celtis sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Rosales and falls within the family Cannabaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Celtis, with its specific identification being the species sinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Celtis sinensis contains bioactive compounds including alkaloids, flavonoids, triterpenoids, and various phenolic acids that provide its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents work together to demonstrate significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities in medicinal applications. Specifically, the bark and leaves are often utilized in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as coughs, skin inflammation, and digestive issues. Researchers have identified specific glycosides within the plant that may contribute to its ability to regulate physiological functions. Ongoing pharmacological studies continue to investigate how these extracted molecules can be utilized for modern drug development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celtis sinensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin ailments, digestive issues, and respiratory ailments. The bark is often employed in traditional medicine to reduce swelling and soothe skin irritations such as eczema or rashes. Decoctions made from its components are frequently consumed to alleviate symptoms of coughs, bronchitis, and other lung-related discomforts. Additionally, certain preparations are used to address gastrointestinal distress and promote overall digestive health. These therapeutic applications rely on the plant's diverse chemical properties to target specific bodily systems.
