Celtis sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis sinensis?

Celtis sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Rosales and falls within the family Cannabaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Celtis, with its specific identification being the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celtis sinensis has alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively asymmetrical base, growing on branches characterized by prominent, corky ridges. The tree typically reaches heights of 10 to 25 meters and features a gray-brown bark that becomes increasingly textured with age. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in clusters, eventually developing into fleshy, drupaceous fruits that turn from green to red and finally to black upon ripening. The leaf venation is prominent, with pinnate veins extending toward the finely toothed edges of the lamina. This combination of textured bark and distinctive leaf shape makes the species easily identifiable in forest environments.
