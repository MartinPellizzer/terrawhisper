Aquilegia yabeana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aquilegia yabeana has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are compound, ternately divided, and feature lobed or coarsely toothed leaflets. The plant produces upright flowering stems that emerge from a central rosette to support its distinctively shaped blossoms. These flowers exhibit a complex structure with five nectar-producing spurs extending backward from the petals. The petals are typically colored in shades of blue or violet, often accented by lighter tones near the center. Small, capsule-like fruits develop following pollination to house the plant's seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of old-growth forests and riparian zones. Within these habitats, the species often colonizes areas with deep organic soil and heavy canopy cover.
