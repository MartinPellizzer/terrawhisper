Dolichos trilobus

What's the taxonomical classification of Dolichos trilobus?

Dolichos trilobus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Fabales. It further falls under the family Fabaceae, which is a large group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is specifically categorized under the genus Dolichos, with its unique identity defined by the species name trilobus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

The plant's growth habit is characterized by elongated, flexible vines that allow it to scramble over surrounding vegetation. What are the pharmacological activities of Dolichos trilobus?

This plant has potent pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory and α-glucosidase inhibitory activities. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively reduce inflammation by modulating various biochemical pathways in the body. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates significant potential in managing blood glucose levels by inhibiting the α-glucosidase enzyme. These enzymatic interactions help slow down carbohydrate digestion in the digestive tract. Such diverse therapeutic effects suggest that Dolicho trilobus could be a valuable source for developing new medicinal treatments.

As discussed in a study, four new coumestans dolichoins A-D (1-4) and four known compounds isosojagol (5), phaseol (6), psoralidin (7), and 4″,5″-dehydroisopsoralidin (8) were isolated from the roots of Dolicho trifolobus. The anti-inflammatory activity of compounds 1-8 was evaluated via the inhibition of nitric oxide production in RAW 264.7 cells. Within this evaluation, compounds 1 and 6 displayed moderate inhibitory activity and no cytotoxic effects. In an α-glucosidase inhibitory assay, compounds 1 and 5-8 exhibited appreciable inhibition. Specifically, compounds 1, 7, and 8 showed IC50 values lower than 20.0 µM.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the bioactive medicinal compound doliroside B along with various other secondary metabolites. These specific chemical structures are primarily utilized in pharmacological research to understand potential therapeutic applications. Scientists study these compounds to evaluate their ability to interact with biological targets in the human body. The presence of such diverse chemical constituents makes the species a significant subject for natural product chemistry. Ongoing investigations aim to isolate these molecules for future drug development.

As discussed in a study, Dolichos trilobus Linn is used in Yi ethnic medicine to treat pain, fracture, and rheumatism. The plant contains doliroside B (DB) and its disodium salt (DBDS). In studies using Kunming mice, DB and DBDS were administered at doses of 0.31, 0.62, 1.25, 2.5, and 5 mg/kg for the writhing test. For the hot plate and formalin tests, doses of 2.5, 5, 10, and 20 mg/kg were used. DBDS at 5 mg/kg inhibited the writhing number by 80.2% and reduced the production of NO, iNOS, and IL-6 by 55.8%, 69.0%, and 49.9% inhibition, respectively.
