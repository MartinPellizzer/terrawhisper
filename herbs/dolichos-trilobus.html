What's the taxonomical classification of Dolichos trilobus?

Dolichos trilobus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Fabales. It further falls under the family Fabaceae, which is a large group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is specifically categorized under the genus Dolichos, with its unique identity defined by the species name trilobus.

What are the pharmacological activities of Dolichos trilobus?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory and alpha-glucosidase inhibitory properties. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds effectively reduce swelling and oxidative stress in biological systems. Additionally, the plant demonstrates a significant capacity to inhibit alpha-glucosidase enzymes, which helps regulate blood glucose levels after meals. These dual mechanisms suggest that Dolichos trilobus holds therapeutic potential for managing metabolic disorders and inflammatory conditions. Such diverse medicinal actions make it a subject of interest for developing new natural treatments.

According to a study published by "Planta medica", four new coumestans called dolichoins A-D (1-4) were isolated from the roots of Dolicho trifobus along with four known compounds, isosojagol (5), phaseol (6), psoralidin (7), and 4″,5″-dehydroisopsoralidin (8). In an evaluation of anti-inflammatory activity through the inhibition of nitric oxide production in RAW 264.7 cells, compounds 1 and 6 displayed moderate inhibitory activity with no cytotoxic effects. Regarding α-glucosidase inhibition, compounds 1 and 5-8 exhibited appreciable inhibition. Specifically, compounds 1, 7, and 8 showed IC50 values lower than 20.0 µM.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a variety of bioactive medicinal compounds, most notably the unique glycoside known as doliroside B. According to a study published by "Pharmaceuctical biology", Dolichos trilobus Linn (Leguminosae) is used in Yi ethnic medicine to treat pain, fracture, and rheumatism. The plant contains doliroside B (DB) and its disodium salt (DBDS). In the writhing test, mice were orally treated with DB and DBDS at doses of 0.31, 0.62, 1.25, 2.5, and 5 mg/kg. In the hot plate and formalin tests, doses of 2.5, 5, 10, and 20 mg/kg were used. DBDS at 5 mg/kg inhibited the writhing number by 80.2% and reduced the production of NO, iNOS, and IL-6 by 55.8%, 69.0%, and 49.9% inhibition, respectively.
