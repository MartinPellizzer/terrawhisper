Dolichos trilobus

What's the taxonomical classification of Dolichos trilobus?

Dolichos trilobus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Fabales. It further falls under the family Fabaceae, which is a large group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is specifically categorized under the genus Dolichos, with its unique identity defined by the species name trilobus.

    Dolichos trilobus has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties that primarily function by inhibiting oxidative stress and reducing pro-inflammatory cytokines in biological tissues.     What are the herbal preparations of Dolichos trilobus?

    Dolichos trilobus is prepared as a liquid decoction, a concentrated infusion, or a dried powder for topical applications. Practitioners often simmer the roots and leaves in water to extract active phytochemicals for oral consumption. The dried plant material can also be finely ground to create powders that are encapsulated or mixed into food. Some traditional methods involve steeping the herb in alcohol to produce a potent tincture. These various forms allow for different absorption rates and specific therapeutic uses depending on the intended treatment.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Dolichos trilobus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, abdominal cramping, and diarrhea if ingested. These adverse reactions typically occur because the plant contains bioactive compounds that irritate the digestive lining. Some individuals may also experience localized skin irritation or allergic dermatitis upon direct contact with the foliage. Prolonged exposure to the toxins within the plant can lead to more systemic issues such as dizziness or headaches. It is crucial to avoid all contact with this species to prevent these physical complications.
