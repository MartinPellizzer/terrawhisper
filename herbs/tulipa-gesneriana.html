Tulipa gesneriana

What's the taxonomical classification of Tulipa gesneriana?

Tulipa gesneriana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Liliaceae, falling under the genus Tulipa, and is specifically identified by its species name, gesneriana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tulipa gesneriana has a bulbous root system, basal leaves that are lanceolate and often glaucous, and upright stems supporting single or multiple cup-shaped flowers. The foliage typically features a distinct midrib and can vary in width depending on the specific environmental conditions. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors from pale yellow to deep orange, frequently displaying a darker base within the tepals. The perianth consists of six tepals that form a classic goblet shape during peak bloom. These reproductive structures are positioned atop slender, smooth stems that rise directly from the bulb.
