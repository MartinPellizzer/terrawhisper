What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tulipa gesneriana has a bulbous root system, basal leaves that are lanceolate and often glaucous, and upright stems supporting single or multiple cup-shaped flowers. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors from pale yellow to deep orange, frequently displaying a darker base within the tepals. The perianth consists of six tepals that form a classic goblet shape during peak bloom. These reproductive structures are positioned atop slender, smooth stems that rise directly from the bulb.
