Asplenium varians

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium varians has pinnate fronds characterized by an even number of pinnae that are lanceolate in shape and possess slightly undulating margins. The stipe is typically dark and relatively slender, supporting a lamina that can vary in size depending on the maturity of the plant. Each individual leaflet is arranged oppositely along the central rachis, creating a symmetrical appearance. Small sori are located on the underside of the blades, often protected by a thin membrane called an indusium. The texture of the foliage is generally thin and herbaceous, contributing to its delicate overall structure.
