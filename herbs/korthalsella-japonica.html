Korthalsella japonica has a creeping rhizomatous habit with small, simple, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant typically produces tiny seeds that facilitate its dispersal within its specific ecological niche.     Korthalsella japonica can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. It is essential to avoid direct skin contact and prevent any accidental ingestion to mitigate these risks.
