Korthalsella japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Santalales and is a member of the family Santalaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Korthalsella, with the specific epithet japonica.

Korthalsella japonica is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant material in water to release its medicinal compounds. Some practitioners also create topical washes or poultices using the crushed plant to apply directly to the skin. These various preparations allow for different methods of absorption and therapeutic application depending on the intended use.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Korthalsella japonica can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may trigger redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. If a person consumes any part of the plant, they might experience nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting. Some individuals may also exhibit more severe systemic reactions such as dizziness or headaches. It is essential to avoid direct skin contact and prevent any accidental ingestion to mitigate these risks.
