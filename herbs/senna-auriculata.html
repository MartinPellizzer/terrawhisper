Golden Shower Tree (Senna auriculata)

Golden Shower Tree (Senna auriculata) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Southern Asia, Northern Australia, and Pacific Islands. Traditionally, its leaves, seeds, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with acute apex, entire margin, and acute base, arranged oppositely, with upper surface yellow-green and lower surface pale green, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are racemose clusters of yellow flowers with five ovate petals, actinomorphic symmetry, and a prominent yellow ovary covered in fine pubescence.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit, opposite branching, smooth, glabrous surface, persistent stipules.
