Golden Shower Tree

Senna auriculata

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Senna auriculata, commonly known as the Golden Shower Tree, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family. It is an annual shrub native to tropical regions of Asia. The plant has yellow flowers, palmate leaves, and is sometimes referred to as Kuth or Koth in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The Golden Shower Tree contains key bioactive compounds like flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, skin irritation, inflammatory skin disorders Antimicrobial, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antipyretic
Digestive Gastrointestinal disorders, constipation, diarrhea Laxative, antidiarrheal, anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, carminative
Immune Inflammatory conditions, fever, allergies, allergic reactions Anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, antihistaminic

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and skin conditions.

Infusion: Used for respiratory infections and fever.

Powder: Used for constipation and as a laxative.

Safety Profile

The Golden Shower Tree is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or with existing medical conditions.
