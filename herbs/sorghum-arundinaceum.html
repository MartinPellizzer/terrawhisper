Sorghum arundinaceum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sorghum arundinaceuum has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of up to one meter. The leaves are linear and lanceolate with prominent midribs and parallel venation typical of the Poaceae family. Its inflorescence consists of compact, terminal panicles that hold numerous small, spikelet-like flowers. The root system is fibrous and well-developed to support its upright structure in various soil types. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in disturbed or grassy habitats.

      What are the herbal preparations of Sorghum arundinaceum?

      Sorghum arundinaceum is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using the dried roots or rhizomes of the plant.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Sorghum arundinaceuum can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of the plant may lead to symptoms such as nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. Direct contact with the leaves or stems can trigger contact dermatitis, manifesting as redness or itching on the skin. Some people might experience respiratory irritation if the pollen or fine dust from the plant is inhaled. It is important to handle the specimen with care to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
