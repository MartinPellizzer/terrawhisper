Pedicularis decorissima

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis decorissima?

Pedicularis decorissima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Orobanchaceae and is specifically identified by its genus, Pedicularis, and its species designation, decorissima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis decorissima has upright, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and bear densely packed, tubular flowers colored in shades of pink, purple, or red.

The leaves are primarily basal and deeply pinnatifid, featuring narrow, linear lobes that create a feathery appearance. Moving up the stem, the leaves become smaller and more sparse, often transitioning into bracts that surround the flowering spikes. The corollas are characterized by a prominent upper lip that is hooded or galea-shaped, a classic trait of the genus.

This structure serves to protect the reproductive organs and facilitate specific pollination mechanisms.
