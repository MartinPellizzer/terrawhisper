What's the taxonomical classification of Eschenbachia japonica?

Eschenbachia japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, this plant is specifically categorized under the genus Eschenbachia, with the species designation being japonica.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Eschenbachia japonica can cause localized skin irritation, redness, and swelling upon direct contact with its foliage or stems. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience a persistent itchy rash or dermatitis following exposure. Some reports suggest that accidental ingestion could lead to mild gastrointestinal distress, such as nausea or stomach cramping. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, caution is advised to prevent any adverse dermatological reactions. Always use protective gloves when handling this species to minimize the risk of physical irritation.
