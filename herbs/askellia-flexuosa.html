Askellia flexuosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Askellia flexuosa?

Askellia flexuosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is further categorized into the genus Askellia, ultimately defining its specific biological identity as the species flexuosa.

What are the herbal preparations of Askellia flexuosa?

Askellia flexuosa is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical wash using the dried aerial parts of the plant. These liquid extracts are often consumed orally to address internal ailments or digestive issues. The plant material can also be macerated in oil to create a medicinal ointment for skin applications. Some traditional practices involve using the crushed leaves directly as a poultice for localized treatment. These various forms allow for both systemic and localized therapeutic use of the herb.
