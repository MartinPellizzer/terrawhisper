Cistanche salsa

What's the taxonomical classification of Cistanche salsa?

Cistanche salsa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cistanche, and its species name, salsa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cistanche salsa has an entire root system that lacks chlorophyll and consists of thick, fleshy, yellowish-to-brownish structures adapted for parasitism. The stems are erect, succulent, and typically reach heights between 10 and 40 centimeters. These stems are often somewhat branched and possess small, scale-like leaves that do not perform photosynthesis. The flowers are tubular, yellowish, and emerge from the stem in a dense cluster. Mature plants eventually produce capsules containing numerous small, brown seeds.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cistanche salsa?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-oxidative stress, anti-proliferative, antiapoptotic, antioxidative, and neuroprotective properties. Its potent antioxidative activity helps neutralize harmful free radicals within biological systems. By exerting anti-proliferative effects, the plant can inhibit the uncontrolled growth of certain abnormal cells. Additionally, its antiapoptotic capabilities play a role in regulating programmed cell death pathways. These combined mechanisms contribute significantly to its overall neuroprotective potential in protecting nerve cells from damage.

As discussed in a study, tubuloside B is a phenylethanoid isolated from the stems of Cistanche salsa that exhibits neuroprotective capacity. When SH-SY5Y cells are treated with TNFalpha 100 microg/L for 36 h, apoptotic cells can accumulate to a highest percentage of 37.5 %. In these conditions, caspase-3 activity increases by about five-fold compared with controls. Pretreatment with tubuloside B at concentrations of 1, 10, or 100 mg/L for 2 h attenuates this TNFalpha-mediated apoptosis. This antiapoptotic action involves the inhibition of caspase-3 activity, maintenance of mitochondria function, and a decrease in the concentration of free intracellular calcium.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several medicinal phenylethanoid glycosides including acteoside, cistanoside A, echinacoside, and isoacteoside. These specific compounds are known for their diverse biological activities and potential therapeutic properties. Phenylethanoid glycosides represent a significant class of secondary metabolites found within the plant's chemical structure. Researchers often study these molecules to understand their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. The presence of these specialized compounds makes the plant a subject of interest in pharmacological studies.

As discussed in a study, the antioxidant activity of 6 phenylethanoid glycosides from plants of Cistanche salsa was determined using the assay systems of DPPH. The antioxidative activity of these phenylethanoid glycosides showed a variant with a dose-dependent effect. The strength of the antioxidative activity of the 6 components followed a specific sequence: 2'-Acetylacteoside > Acteoside > or = Tubuloside B > or = Isoacteoside > Echinacoside > Cistanoside A. This activity is related to the number of phenolic hydroxyl, steric hindrance, 2-acetyl on the middle glucopyranose, and the location of phenolic hydroxyl. It may also be related to the alpha, beta-unsaturated ketone of phenyl-2-propenoyl.
