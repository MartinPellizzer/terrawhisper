What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Urceola rosea has a woody, perennial shrubby habit characterized by erect, cylindrical stems that typically reach heights of one to two meters. The leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptic to oblong in shape with entire margins and acute tips. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the zygomorphic, tubular pink flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms possess a prominent, elongated corolla tube that curves slightly to facilitate pollination by long-tongued insects. The plant also produces small, fleshy berries that serve as the primary fruit structure.
