What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia eriostachya?

Elsholtzia eriostachya belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Lamiales, it is further classified under the family Lamiaceae. Finally, this specific plant is identified by the genus Elsholtzia, with its unique designation being the species eriostachya.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia eriostachyra has a deciduous tree structure characterized by pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets and small, densely hairy, spike-like inflorescences.

The leaflets are typically elliptic to lanceolate in shape and possess serrated margins. Its bark is generally smooth when young but becomes fissured and grayish with age. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters that give way to woody, dehiscent fruit capsules.

These capsules eventually release small, winged samaras designed for wind dispersal.
