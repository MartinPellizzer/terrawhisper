Elsholtzia eriostachya

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia eriostachyra has a deciduous tree structure characterized by pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets and small, densely hairy, spike-like inflorescences. The leaflets are typically elliptic to lanceolate in shape and possess serrated margins. Its bark is generally smooth when young but becomes fissured and grayish with age. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters that give way to woody, dehiscent fruit capsules. These capsules eventually release small, winged samaras designed for wind dispersal.
