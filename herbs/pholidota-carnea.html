Pholidota Carnea

Pholidota carnea, commonly known as the hairy-tailed gerbil, has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

The plant is believed to possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, making it useful in treating skin infections and inflammatory conditions. Its leaves and roots are often prepared as decoctions or poultices to alleviate symptoms of wounds, eczema, and respiratory ailments. However, scientific research on its medicinal efficacy remains limited, and its use is largely based on indigenous knowledge.

As a result, further studies are needed to validate its potential applications in modern pharmacology.
