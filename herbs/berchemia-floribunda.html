Berchemia floribunda

What's the taxonomical classification of Berchemia floribunda?

Berchemia floribunda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Rosales. This plant is a representative of the family Rhamnaceae, specifically situated within the genus Berchemia, which culminates in its unique designation as the species floribunda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berchemia floribunda has deciduous, lanceolate to elliptical leaves that measure between 3 and 10 centimeters in length and are arranged alternately along its woody, spreading branches. The leaf margins are typically finely serrated, while the upper surfaces exhibit a dark green hue and the undersides appear lighter. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and emerge in clusters from the leaf axils during the spring season. Following pollination, the plant produces small, globose drupes that transition from green to a dark purple or black color when fully ripe. These fleshy fruits are often utilized by local wildlife as a seasonal food source.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of Southern Africa, specifically occurring within South Africa, Zimbabwe, Botswana, and Mozambique. It is commonly found inhabiting various landscapes ranging from rocky hillsides to dry savanna woodlands. The species typically thrives in well-drained soils throughout these semi-arid environments. Its distribution is closely tied to specific climatic conditions that support its shrubby growth habit. What are the pharmacological activities of Berchemia floribunda?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and whitening pharmacological activities that make it a valuable subject for dermatological research. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, which protects skin cells from oxidative stress and premature aging. The anti-inflammatory effects allow the plant to reduce redness and soothe irritated tissues effectively. Additionally, the whitening capabilities work by inhibiting melanin production to help lighten hyperpigmentation and even out skin tone. These combined therapeutic actions suggest significant potential for developing advanced skincare formulations.

As discussed in a study, Berchemia floribunda (BF) has been reported to have antioxidant and whitening effects. The study evaluated the anti-inflamatory effect of leaves (BR-L), branches (BR-B), and fruit (BR-F) extracted with 70% ethanol. BR-L showed a strong anti-inflamatory activity through the inhibition of NO production. This leaf extract significantly suppressed the production of pro-inflamatory mediators such as iNOS, COX-2, IL-1β, IL-6, and TNF-α in LPS-stimulated RAW264.7 cells. Furthermore, BR-L suppressed the degradation and phosphorylation of IκB-α and obstructed the phosphorylation of MAPKs, specifically ERK1/2, p38, and JNK.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of irritating sap and secondary metabolites. Contact with the milky latex can lead to contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If any part of the plant is swallowed, it may trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Some individuals might also experience allergic reactions or localized swelling upon touching the foliage. Careful handling is necessary to avoid these unpleasant physiological responses.
