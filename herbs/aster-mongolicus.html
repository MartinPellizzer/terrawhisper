Aster mongolicus

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster mongolicus?

Aster mongolicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling under the genus Aster, with its specific designation being the species mongolicus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster mongolicus has lanceolate to linear leaves that are typically sessile, varying in color from bright green to a purplish hue, and grow from a rhizomatous root system. The plant produces terminal flower heads characterized by bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of darker florets. These inflorescences are supported by stiff, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. The foliage often becomes more sparse toward the base of the plant as it matures through the growing season. This perennial herb is distinguished by its compact growth habit and prolific blooming period.
