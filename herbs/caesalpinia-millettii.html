Caesalpinia millettii

What's the taxonomical classification of Caesalpinia millettii?

Caesalpinia millettii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized into the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae and is specifically identified under the genus Caesalpinia, with its unique designation being the species millettii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caesalpinia millettii has a woody shrub or small tree structure characterized by bipinnate leaves, small yellow flowers, and elongated, flattened legume pods. Its bright yellow blossoms are typically borne in axillary racemes and feature prominent stamens. Once fertilized, the plant produces distinctive brown pods that contain several small, hard seeds. The bark is generally rough and provides structural support for its spreading branch architecture.
