Caesalpinia millettii

What's the taxonomical classification of Caesalpinia millettii?

Caesalpinia millettii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized into the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae and is specifically identified under the genus Caesalpinia, with its unique designation being the species millettii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caesalpinia millettii has a woody shrub or small tree structure characterized by bipinnate leaves, small yellow flowers, and elongated, flattened legume pods. The foliage consists of numerous small, oblong leaflets that provide a feathery texture to the canopy. Its bright yellow blossoms are typically borne in axillary racemes and feature prominent stamens. Once fertilized, the plant produces distinctive brown pods that contain several small, hard seeds. The bark is generally rough and provides structural support for its spreading branch architecture.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several distinct medicinal compounds including 8-methoxybonducelliin, bonducelliin, eucomin, intricatinol, and tamarixetin 3-O-(6''-O-E-caffeoyl)-beta-D-galactopyranoside. These chemical constituents are primarily isolated from the various parts of the Caesalpinia millettii species. Research into these specific molecules helps scientists understand the plant's potential pharmacological properties. Each of these flavonoids and glycosides contributes to the overall complex chemical profile of the specimen. Studying these substances provides insight into how the plant interacts with biological systems.

According to a study published by "Chemical & pharmaceutical bulletin", chemical examination of the stems of Caesalpinia millettii HOOK. et ARN. led to the isolation of a new flavonol glycoside caffeiate ester (1) and a new homoisoflavone (2). The structure of compound 1 was established as tamarixeetin 3-O-(6''-O-E-caffeoyl)-beta-D-galactopyranoside. The structure of compound 2 was identified as (Z)-7-hydroxy-8-methoxy-3-(4-methoxybenzyl) chroman-4-one. Additionally, four known homoisoflavones were identified: eucomin (3), bonducellin (4), 8-methoxybonducellin (5), and intricatinol (6). Compounds 3, 4, 5, and 6 were isolated from this plant for the first time.
