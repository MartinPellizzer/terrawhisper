Caesalpinia millettii

What's the taxonomical classification of Caesalpinia millettii?

Caesalpinia millettii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized into the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae and is specifically identified under the genus Caesalpinia, with its unique designation being the species millettii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caesalpinia millettii has a woody shrub or small tree structure characterized by bipinnate leaves, small yellow flowers, and elongated, flattened legume pods. The foliage consists of numerous small, oblong leaflets that provide a feathery texture to the canopy. Its bright yellow blossoms are typically borne in axillary racemes and feature prominent stamens. Once fertilized, the plant produces distinctive brown pods that contain several small, hard seeds. The bark is generally rough and provides structural support for its spreading branch architecture.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 8-methoxybonducelliin, bonducelliin, eucomin, intricatinol, and tamarixetin 3-O-(6''-O-E-caffeoyl)-beta-D-galactopyranoside. These specific phytochemicals are distributed throughout the plant's tissues and contribute to its unique chemical profile. Research into these molecules helps scientists understand the potential therapeutic applications of the species. Each individual compound possesses a distinct molecular structure that may interact with different biological pathways. Studying these constituents is essential for characterizing the plant's overall pharmacological properties.

As discussed in a study, chemical examination of the stems of Caesalpinia millettii HOOK. et ARN. led to the isolation of a new flavonol glycoside caffeiate ester (1) and a homoisoflavone (2). The structure of compound 1 was established as tamarixeetin 3-O-(6''-O-E-caffeoyl)-beta-D-galactopyranoside. Additionally, the study identified the structure of compound 2 as (Z)-7-hydroxy-8-methoxy-3-(4-methoxybenzyl) chroman-4-one. The plant also contained four known homoisoflavones, which were eucomin (3), bonducelliin (4), 8-methoxybonducelliin (5), and intricatinol (6). Compounds 3, 4, 5, and 6 were isolated from this plant for the first time.
