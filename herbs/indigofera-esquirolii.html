What's the taxonomical classification of Indigofera esquirolii?

Indigofera esquirolii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific placement within the genus Indigofera, reaching its unique biological designation at the species level as esquirolii.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Indigofera esquirolii can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if consumed in improper quantities. While some traditional practices use the plant, its chemical constituents may trigger allergic contact dermatitis when applied topically to sensitive skin. Ingestion of concentrated extracts can lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain in certain individuals. There is also a lack of extensive clinical data regarding its long-term systemic effects on human organ function. Consequently, caution is necessary to avoid unintended physiological complications during use.
