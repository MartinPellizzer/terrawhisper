Indigofera esquirolii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera esquirolii has pinnately compound leaves composed of several pairs of small, oblong leaflets and erect, woody stems that bear small, fragrant, papilionaceous flowers arranged in axillary racemes. The leaflets typically exhibit a smooth texture with entire margins and a greenish hue. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters that emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying white or pale pink petals. The fruit is a small, indehiscent pod that contains a limited number of seeds. This species is characterized by its slender growth habit and fine, branching structure.
