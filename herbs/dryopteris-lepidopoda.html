Dryopteris lepidopoda

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris lepidopoda has large, tripinnate fronds that typically reach heights of 50 to 100 centimeters and feature a dark brown, scaly stipe. The pinnae are broadly lanceolate in shape and possess deeply incised lobes with serrated margins. A defining characteristic is the presence of dense, brownish scales covering the rachis and the underside of the veins. The sori are arranged in two rows on the underside of the pinnules and are protected by kidney-shaped indusia. These morphological traits distinguish the species from other members of the Dryopteris genus within its native habitat.
