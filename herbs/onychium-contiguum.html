Onychium contiguum

What's the taxonomical classification of Onychium contiguum?

Onychium contiguum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, falling under the order Polypodiales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Pteridaceae, specifically within the genus Onychium, ultimately reaching its binomial designation as the species Onychium contiiguum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Onychium contiguum has a rosette of fleshy, lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically bright green and characterized by a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The leaf texture is often somewhat succulent, allowing the plant to retain moisture in its natural environment. These leaves emerge from a central base and spread outwards to form a dense, ground-hugging cluster. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are held on slender, upright stems.

The overall structure is compact, making it a distinctively low-growing perennial.
