Dunbaria circinalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dunbaria circinalis?

Dunbaria circinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. This plant is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, a group known for its diverse legumes. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Dunbaria, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, circinalis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dunbaria circinalis has pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of small, oval leaflets and small, yellow, papilionaceous flowers held in axillary racemes. The plant typically exhibits a woody, scrambling shrub habit with slender, slightly hairy stems. Its leaflets are arranged oppositely along the rachis and feature smooth margins with rounded tips. The inflorescence consists of small, pea-like blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils. Each flower possesses the characteristic structure of the Fabaceae family, including a standard, wings, and a keel.
