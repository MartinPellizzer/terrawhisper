Haematoxylum campechianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Haematoxylum campechianum?

Haematoxylum campechianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Fabales, it falls into the family Fabaceae, which is commonly known as the legume family. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Haematoxylum and is distinguished as the species campechianum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Haematoxylum campechianum?

This plant has astringent and spasmolytic pharmacological activities that can be utilized for various therapeutic purposes. The astringent properties allow the plant extracts to contract or constrict body tissues, which is often helpful in treating inflammatory conditions. Additionally, its spasmolytic effects work by reducing muscle contractions and relieving cramps throughout the body. These combined chemical actions make the species a significant subject for studying natural medicinal compounds. Research into these specific biological functions continues to expand our understanding of its potential clinical applications.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Haematoxylum campechianum is a medicinal plant used as an astringent to purify the blood and treat stomach problems like diarrhea and dysentery. A bio-guided chemical fractionation of its methanolic extract led to the isolation of five compounds: two chalcones (1 and 2) and three homoisoflavonoids (3, 4, and 5). The plant demonstrates spasmolytic activity in an in vitro model, with the methanolic extract showing an EC50 of 62.11 ± 3.23. Specific fractions also exhibited relaxing activity, including HcF7 with an EC50 of 61.75 ± 3.55 and HcF9 with an EC50 of 125.5 ± 10.65. Furthermore, compounds 1 and 2 presented significant relaxing activity with EC50 values of 16.06 ± 2.15 and 25.37 ± 3.47, respectively.
