Haematoxylum campechianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Haematoxylum campechianum has a woody, deciduous shrub or small tree structure characterized by slender, grayish branches and simple, alternate leaves. The foliage typically consists of ovate to elliptic leaflets with entire or slightly serrated margins and prominent venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters, often appearing during the growing season to facilitate reproduction. The plant develops small, fleshy drupes that serve as the primary fruit type for seed dispersal. These morphological traits distinguish it within its specific botanical family and ecological niche.

Haematoxylum campechianum has medicinal properties that function primarily through its high tannin content to act as an astringent, hemostatic, anti-inflammatory, and antidiarheal agent.
