What to know about Saussurea lappa (costus) before using it medicinally

Saussurea lappa, commonly known as costus, is a medicinal herb that has been prized for its diverse health benefits, which include reducing inflammation, relieving digestive issues, and alleviating pain.

As a hardy perennial, this herb requires well-draining soil and partial shade to thrive, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates. Saussurea lappa has a distinctive botanical profile, with large, sword-shaped leaves and a tall, branching stem adorned with small, white or purple flowers.

Historically, this herb has been mentioned in traditional Ayurvedic medicine and has been used for centuries to treat a range of ailments, including arthritis and skin conditions.

This article explains the medicinal, horticultural, botanical, and historical aspects of Saussurea lappa.

What are the medicinal properties of Saussurea lappa?

Saussurea lappa helps with various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, due to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antispasmodic properties.

The active constituents of Saussurea lappa include safflor yellow, costunolide, and costic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties, such as reducing inflammation and killing bacteria.

The rhizome and roots of Saussurea lappa are primarily used for medicinal purposes, as they contain the highest concentration of active constituents, and are often dried and processed into powder or extracts.

Improper use of Saussurea lappa can cause side effects, such as allergic reactions, nausea, and diarrhea, especially when used in large doses or for extended periods.

Precautions when using Saussurea lappa medicinally include pregnant women avoiding its use, individuals with bleeding disorders being cautious, and those with kidney or liver issues monitoring their condition closely, as the plant may interact with medications.

What are the horticulural aspects of Saussurea lappa?

Saussurea lappa grow best in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. The optimal temperature range for its growth is between 15-30°C (59-86°F). Water requirement is moderate, but it can tolerate some drought.

Planting tips for Saussurea lappa include selecting a spot with good air circulation to prevent fungal diseases. Plant rhizomes 10-15 cm deep and 30-60 cm apart. Mulch around the base to retain moisture and suppress weeds. Soil pH should be slightly acidic to neutral.

Harvesting tips for Saussurea lappa include digging up rhizomes in autumn after the foliage has died back. Cut off the foliage, leaving 2-3 cm of stem attached to the rhizome. Clean and dry the rhizomes to prevent moisture from causing decay.

Pests and diseases commonly affecting Saussurea lappa include root rot caused by overwatering, aphids, and spider mites. Regular monitoring and maintenance can help prevent these issues.
