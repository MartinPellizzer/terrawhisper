Medicinal Parts

Saussurea lappa root, also known as *Gao Ben*, is a highly valued medicinal part of the plant, widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its diverse therapeutic properties.

The root is typically harvested after the plant has matured for several years, ensuring the accumulation of active compounds such as alkaloids, essential oils, and sesquiterpene lactones. These compounds contribute to the root's ability to regulate blood pressure, improve circulation, and alleviate symptoms of respiratory and digestive disorders.

In traditional formulations, the root is often processed into powders, decoctions, or pills to enhance its bioavailability and efficacy. Its warming properties make it particularly effective in treating conditions associated with cold and dampness in the body, such as chronic cough, asthma, and gastrointestinal discomfort. The root's medicinal value has also been supported by modern pharmacological studies, which have explored its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and neuroprotective effects.

Overall, Saussurea lappa root remains a cornerstone in both traditional and contemporary herbal medicine due to its broad spectrum of health benefits.
