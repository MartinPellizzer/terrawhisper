What's the taxonomical classification of Phoebe lanceolata?

Phoebe lanceolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Phoebe, leading to its unique species designation, Phoebe lanceolata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phoebe lanceolata has simple, alternate, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and are characterized by entire margins and acute apices. The leaf blades are typically coriaceous in texture and possess an acute or acuminate tip. These leaves are arranged spirally along the woody stems of the evergreen tree. The plant also features small, inconspicuous flowers and produces ovoid to ellipsoidal drupes that turn dark when ripe. The bark is generally smooth and grayish, providing a structural contrast to the dense foliage.
