Celastrus rugosus

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus rugosus?

Celastrus rugosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Celastrus and the specific species designation rugosus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus rugosus has opposite, elliptical leaves with a distinctly wrinkled or rugose upper surface and finely serrated margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy berries that are characteristic of the Celastraceae family.
