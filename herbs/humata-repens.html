Humata repens

What's the taxonomical classification of Humata repens?

Humata repens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Humata and the species repens.

What are the therapeutic actions of Humata repens?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including respiratory issues, skin infections, digestive problems, and inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed to alleviate symptoms of coughs and bronchial discomfort. Local healers may also apply extracts topically to address wounds or skin irritations. Additionally, certain preparations are believed to aid in settling stomach disturbances. These therapeutic applications vary significantly depending on the specific part of the plant utilized.

What are the herbal preparations of Humata repens?

Humata repens is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice to utilize its medicinal properties. The plant material is typically boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal use. Alternatively, steeping the leaves in hot water allows for a milder infusion that preserves delicate volatile components. For external ailments, the crushed herb is applied directly to the skin as a soothing poultice. These various methods ensure that the active compounds are effectively extracted for therapeutic application.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Humata repens can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if its sap or leaves are ingested by humans or pets. While many indoor plants are relatively benign, the sap of this species may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Contact with the skin can result in redness, itching, or a localized rash. If a person or animal accidentally consumes any part of the plant, they might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. It is always advisable to handle the plant with care and keep it out of reach of curious children and domestic animals.
