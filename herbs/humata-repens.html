Humata repens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Humata repens has a creeping, prostrate growth habit characterized by slender, creeping stems that produce small, oval to lanceolate leaves along their length. These leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern and possess a somewhat succulent texture with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage often displays a green to reddish hue depending on environmental exposure and light intensity. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils to facilitate reproduction. This compact morphological structure allows the plant to spread effectively across soil surfaces.

      Humata repens can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if its sap or leaves are ingested by humans or pets.
