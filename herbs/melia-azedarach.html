Melia azedarach

What's the taxonomical classification of Melia azedarach?

Melia azedarach belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Sapindales. This plant is a member of the family Meliaceae, specifically placed within the genus Melia, and is identified as the species azedarach.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Melia azedarach contains medicinal compounds found in its bark, leaves, flowers, and fruits. The bark is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions and various parasitic infections. Extracts from the leaves are often applied topically to manage inflammatory issues or used in decoctions for digestive ailments. The flowers and fruits also possess bioactive properties that contribute to the plant's overall therapeutic profile. These different components are carefully processed to harness specific phytochemicals for healing purposes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Melia azedarach contains limonoids such as azadirachtin, meliantechin, and salannin which serve as its primary bioactive compounds. These complex triterpenoids are responsible for the plant's significant insecticidal and antiparasitic properties. Research also indicates that the leaves and bark possess various alkaloids and flavonoids that contribute to anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. Some traditional medicinal practices utilize these extracts to treat skin conditions and certain fungal infections. However, the concentration of these compounds requires careful handling due to their potential toxicity in high doses.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Melia azedarach targets several key proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, various inflammatory cytokines, and specific enzymes involved in oxidative stress pathways. The presence of bioactive limonoids such as azadirachtin and meliante facilitates the inhibition of pro-inflammatory mediators like nitric oxide and tumor necrosis factor-alpha. These phytochemicals also interact with antioxidant enzymes to mitigate cellular damage caused by reactive oxygen species. Furthermore, the plant's compounds show potential for modulating protein kinases that regulate cell cycle progression and apoptosis. This multifaceted interaction with enzymatic and signaling proteins underlies its documented anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Melia azedarach?

Melia azedarach has demonstrated medicinal potential through its ability to exhibit antibacterial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties due to the presence of limonoids and other bioactive compounds. These chemical constituents work by inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms and reducing localized swelling or pain. Research suggests that extracts from the plant can be used to combat skin infections and certain parasitic activities. Some studies also investigate its potential role in modulating immune responses and managing metabolic issues. However, the plant contains toxic substances that necessitate cautious application to avoid systemic poisoning.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as scabies, ringworm, and various fungal infections through its topical application. The leaves and bark contain bioactive compounds that exhibit significant antimicrobial and insecticidal properties. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize decoctions of the plant to address parasitic infestations on the skin. Some cultures also employ the plant to manage gastrointestinal ailments and certain inflammatory responses. However, caution is necessary because the plant contains toxic substances that can be harmful if ingested in large quantities.

What are the herbal preparations of Melia azedarach?

Melia azedarach is prepared as a topical paste, a decoction, an infusion, or an oil extraction for various traditional uses. The bark and leaves are often boiled in water to create a decoction used for skin conditions or external washes. Infusions are made by steeping the plant material in hot water to extract its active compounds for milder applications. Additionally, the seeds and leaves can be infused into oils to facilitate topical applications for insecticidal or medicinal purposes. These preparations must be handled with extreme caution because the plant contains toxic compounds that can be harmful if ingested.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Melia azedarach can cause severe poisoning characterized by vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, dizziness, and potential neurological impairment if ingested. The plant contains toxic compounds like meliadrin and azadirachtin that affect the central nervous system. In children or pets, even small amounts of the seeds or bark can lead to life-threatening symptoms. High doses may result in seizures, coma, or death due to systemic toxicity. Contact with the sap may also trigger skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.
