Maackia amurensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Maackia amurensis?

Maackia amurensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is part of the order Fabales and falls within the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Maackia, and its unique species designation, amurensis.

What are the herbal preparations of Maackia amurensis?

Maackia amurensis is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice for various medicinal uses. The bark and roots are most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid tea used for internal ailments. Leaves can also be steeped in hot water to form a milder infusion for digestive support. For external skin irritations, the plant material is often crushed into a paste and applied directly to the affected area. These traditional methods allow the active compounds within the woody tissues to be effectively extracted.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Maackia amurensis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential digestive distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's foliage or stems may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals, resulting in redness or itching. Some users have reported mild gastrointestinal upset when consuming parts of the plant accidentally. While widespread toxicity is not extensively documented, caution is advised when handling the species to prevent dermatological issues. Always use gloves when gardening with this plant to minimize the risk of an adverse reaction.
