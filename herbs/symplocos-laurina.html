Symplocos laurina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Symplocos laurina has simple, alternate, elliptic to oblong leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins and a coriaceous texture. The leaf surfaces are typically glossy green on the upper side while the undersides may exhibit a more matte appearance. The plant features small, inconspicuous white or cream-colored flowers that are arranged in axillary racemes or clusters. These flowers eventually develop into small, globose drupes that transition from green to a dark purplish or black color upon maturity. The woody stem structure supports a shrubby or small tree habit characterized by smooth to slightly fissured bark.
