Lappula spinocarpos belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lappula spinocarpos has an erect or ascending annual herbaceous habit characterized by slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically lanceolate or linear in shape. Small, funnel-shaped flowers emerge from the axils, displaying a delicate blue or violet coloration. The most distinctive morphological feature is the fruit, which consists of a nutlet covered in stiff, hooked spines designed for seed dispersal via animals.
