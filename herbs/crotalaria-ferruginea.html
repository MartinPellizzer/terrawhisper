Crotalaria ferruginea

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria ferruginea?

Crotalaria ferruginea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, a group well-known for its diverse range of legumes. Specifically, it is categorized within the genus Crotalaria, and its unique scientific designation is completed by the species name ferruginea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria ferruginea has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, woody stems that are densely covered in ferruginous, or rusty-colored, stellate hairs. The leaves are typically pinnate with several pairs of oblong to elliptical leaflets that also exhibit a velvety texture due to their indumentum. Small, bright yellow flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, featuring a distinct papilionaceous structure typical of the Fabaceae family. The fruit is a compressed, bristly legume pod that contains multiple seeds protected by a dense layer of brownish pubescence. These morphological traits, particularly the rusty hairiness, serve as key diagnostic features for distinguishing this species from related taxa.

What are the pharmacological activities of Crotalaria ferruginea?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including potent anti-inflammatory properties and phosphodiesterase-4 (PDE4) inhibitory activity. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds work to suppress the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines in various biological models. Specifically, the inhibition of the PDE4 enzyme plays a crucial role in regulating cyclic adenosine monophosphate levels within cells. This molecular mechanism helps to reduce swelling and alleviate inflammatory responses in tissues. These combined effects suggest that the species holds substantial potential for the development of new therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", bioassay-guided fractionation of the ethanol extract of Crotalaria ferruginea led to the isolation of 1 new isoflavonoid, 4'-hydroxy-2'-methylalpinum-isoflavone (1), and 8 known analogs (2-9). Compounds 1, 2, 5, and 8 showed inhibitory activities against phosphodiesterase-4 (PDE4). These specific compounds demonstrated IC50 values ranging from 2.57 to 8.94 μM. This study may explain the anti-inflammatory function of this plant in Chinese folk medicine.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe toxic reactions in animals and humans due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids which lead to irreversible liver damage and hepatic veno-occlusive disease. These toxic compounds are processed by the body into highly reactive metabolites that destroy the endothelial cells of the liver sinusoids. Prolonged exposure or ingestion often results in progressive liver failure, jaundice, and abdominal swelling. In many cases, the damage to the hepatic system is permanent and can be fatal if not treated immediately. Symptoms may not appear instantly, making the plant particularly dangerous because the internal damage occurs before outward signs are visible.
