Crotalaria ferruginea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria ferruginea has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, woody stems that are densely covered in ferruginous, or rusty-colored, stellate hairs. The leaves are typically pinnate with several pairs of oblong to elliptical leaflets that also exhibit a velvety texture due to their indumentum. Small, bright yellow flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, featuring a distinct papilionaceous structure typical of the Fabaceae family. The fruit is a compressed, bristly legume pod that contains multiple seeds protected by a dense layer of brownish pubescence. These morphological traits, particularly the rusty hairiness, serve as key diagnostic features for distinguishing this species from related taxa.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe toxic reactions in animals and humans due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids which lead to irreversible liver damage and hepatic veno-occlusive disease. These toxic compounds are processed by the body into highly reactive metabolites that destroy the endothelial cells of the liver sinusoids. Prolonged exposure or ingestion often results in progressive liver failure, jaundice, and abdominal swelling. In many cases, the damage to the hepatic system is permanent and can be fatal if not treated immediately. Symptoms may not appear instantly, making the plant particularly dangerous because the internal damage occurs before outward signs are visible.
