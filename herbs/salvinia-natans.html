Salvinia natans

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvinia natans?

Salvinia natans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, its lineage continues through the subclass Polypodiinae. It is situated within the order Salviniales and falls under the family Salviniaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, it is categorized under the genus Salvinia, with the specific epithet natans identifying the individual species.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Salvinia natans contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and tannins that exhibit potential therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents contribute to significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities within biological systems. Research suggests that the extract can inhibit the growth of certain pathogenic bacteria and fungi. Additionally, the presence of specific polyphenols may offer anti-inflammatory benefits in medicinal applications. Further investigation into these specific compounds is necessary to fully understand their pharmacological potential for human health.

What are the therapeutic actions of Salvinia natans?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and minor wounds due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The floating aquatic fern contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and soothe irritated tissue. When applied topically, its extracts can assist in the healing process of superficial skin lesions. Some traditional medicinal practices also utilize the plant to address digestive ailments and internal inflammations. Research continues to explore the specific chemical constituents that contribute to these therapeutic effects.
