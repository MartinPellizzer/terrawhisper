Cupressus duclouxiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales. Its taxonomic progression continues through the family Cupressaceae and the genus Cupressus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species duclouxiana.

Its bark typically appears fibrous and develops deep longitudinal fissures as the tree matures. The reproductive structures are small, globose cones that transition from green to a brownish hue upon ripening.
