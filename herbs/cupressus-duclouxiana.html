Cupressus duclouxiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales. Its taxonomic progression continues through the family Cupressaceae and the genus Cupressus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species duclouxiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cupressus duclouxiana has a columnar to narrowly pyramidal growth habit characterized by dense, scale-like foliage and small, woody cones. Its bark typically appears fibrous and develops deep longitudinal fissures as the tree matures. The reproductive structures are small, globose cones that transition from green to a brownish hue upon ripening. This species maintains a relatively tight branching pattern that contributes to its structural uniformity.
