Adonis sutchuenensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Adonis sutchuenensis?

Adonis sutchuenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Adonis, with its specific identification being the species sutchuenenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adonis sutchuenensis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The leaves are basal and divided into many fine, linear segments that create a delicate, feathery appearance. Each stem terminates in a single, solitary flower featuring bright yellow petals that surround a central cluster of numerous stamens. The floral structure is actinomorphic, displaying a radial symmetry common to the Ranunculaceae family.

Small, inconspicuous bracts often subtend the flowering stalks during the blooming period.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Adonis sutchuenensis contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its aerial parts, which include the leaves, stems, and flowers. These above-ground components are harvested and used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments. The plant is valued for its glycosides that exert effects on the cardiovascular system. Practitioners typically utilize the dried whole plant to prepare infusions or decoctions. Careful dosage is essential because the chemical constituents can significantly influence heart rate and rhythm.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains cardiac glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The cardiac glycosides are the primary bioactive components responsible for influencing heart muscle contraction and regulating rhythm. Flavonoids present in the plant provide antioxidant benefits by neutralizing free radicals in the body. Additionally, the phenolic acids offer anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities.

Together, these diverse chemical compounds define the therapeutic potential of Adonis sutchuenensis.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Cardiac glycosides
  • Flavonoids
  • Phenolic acids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Adonis sutchuenensis targets phosphodiesterase enzymes, specifically phosphodiesterase 3, to increase intracellular cyclic adenosine monophosphate levels. This inhibition leads to a cascade of biochemical events that enhance calcium influx in cardiac myocytes. By elevating these second messenger levels, the plant exerts positive inotropic effects that strengthen the force of heart contractions. These molecular interactions primarily contribute to its traditional use in treating congestive heart failure and other cardiovascular ailments. The potency of these glycoside-driven mechanisms makes the plant a significant subject in cardiovascular pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Adonis sutchuenensis?

Adonis sutchuenensis has cardiotonic properties that strengthen heart contractions and regulate cardiac rhythm through its glycoside content.

This plant acts as a sedative to help calm the central nervous system and reduce overall excitability. Its antispasmodic effects allow it to alleviate involuntary muscle contractions and smooth muscle tension. Because it possesses a bitter taste, it stimulates digestive secretions to improve appetite and metabolic function.

These combined pharmacological actions make it a significant component in traditional herbal medicine for heart and digestive health.

The primary therapeutic actions of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Cardiotonic
  • Sedative
  • Antispasmodic
  • Bitter

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat dysmenorrhea, menstrual irregularities, amenorrhea, and various menopause-related symptoms.

It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to help regulate the female reproductive cycle. Practitioners often prescribe it to alleviate painful cramping during menstruation. Additionally, it serves as a remedy for those experiencing a total absence of menstrual periods.

The plant also helps manage the physical and emotional discomforts associated with the menopausal transition.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Dysmenorrhea

  • Amenorrhea
  • Menopause-related symptoms

What are the herbal preparations of Adonis sutchuenensis?

Adonis sutchuenensis is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried aerial parts of the plant in water to extract its bioactive glycosides. This traditional method ensures that the medicinal compounds are effectively released into a concentrated liquid form for consumption. In some clinical or laboratory settings, the plant material may also be processed into alcoholic tinctures to enhance the solubility of specific alkaloids. These various preparations are used to harness the plant's cardiovascular effects under controlled conditions. Careful dosing is essential because the strength of the liquid extract can vary significantly based on the preparation technique used.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Adonis sutchuenensis can cause severe cardiac arrhythmias, significant hypotension, and potentially fatal heart failure due to its high concentration of cardiac glycosides. These potent compounds act similarly to digitalis, meaning they can lead to dangerous irregularities in heart rate and rhythm. Overexposure may also trigger nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and neurological disturbances such as confusion or blurred vision. Because the therapeutic window is extremely narrow, even a slight overdose can result in toxic effects that require urgent medical intervention. Monitoring blood pressure and heart function is essential when dealing with plants containing these specific glycosides.
