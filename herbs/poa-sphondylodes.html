Poa sphondylodes

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Poa sphondylodes has a perennial growth habit characterized by tufted, caespitose clumps of slender, bright green leaves and dense, pyramidal panicles. The leaf blades are typically narrow and flat, often featuring a prominent midrib and a slightly scabrid texture along the margins. Its flowering stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters, supporting numerous small, spikelet-bearing branches. Each spikelet is relatively small and contains several florets that give the inflorescence a soft, airy appearance. The root system consists of sturdy, creeping rhizomes that facilitate its spreading through grassy habitats.
