Meliosma yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Meliosma yunnanensis has opposite, simple, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute to acuminate apices. The leaf texture is typically coriaceous, while the venation pattern features prominent lateral veins that arch toward the leaf tip. Its woody stems exhibit a smooth to slightly fissured bark texture depending on the age of the specimen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary panicles. Following pollination, it develops small, fleshy drupes that serve as its primary reproductive structures.
