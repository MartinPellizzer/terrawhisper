What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corylopsis henryi has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by oppositely arranged, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically 3 to 7 centimeters in length with serrated margins and distinct pinnate venation. Its stems are slender and branched, providing a structural framework for the dense canopy of leaves. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the growing season.
