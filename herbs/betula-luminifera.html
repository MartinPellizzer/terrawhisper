What's the taxonomical classification of Betula luminifera?

Betula luminifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Fagales and the family Betulaceae, placing it within the genus Betula. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species designation, luminifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Betula luminifera has a deciduous growth habit characterized by a single trunk that reaches heights of up to 25 meters, featuring distinctive silvery-white bark with horizontal lenticels. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate to elliptical in shape, possessing serrated margins and acute tips. During the spring and summer, the foliage presents a vibrant green hue, which transitions to bright yellow during the autumn season. The reproductive structures consist of pendulous catkins that emerge from woody twigs. Small, winged samaras serve as the primary method for seed dispersal via wind.
