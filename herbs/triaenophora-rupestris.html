Triaenophora rupestris

What's the taxonomical classification of Triaenophora rupestris?

Triaenophora rupestris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is positioned under Equisetopsida, falling into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further organized within the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed through its specific classification within the genus Triaenophora, resulting in its scientific name, Triaenophora rupestris.

      What are the herbal preparations of Triaenophora rupestris?

      Triaenophora rupestris is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using the dried aerial parts of the plant. These liquid preparations are often consumed to harness the specific bioactive compounds found within the foliage and stems. Some traditional practitioners may also incorporate the plant material into topical poultices for external application.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Triaenophora rupestris can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant's sap comes into contact with human tissue. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience intense itching or redness that lasts for several days. If the juices are accidentally ingested, they might lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or abdominal pain. There is also a risk of systemic reactions in people who possess a specific hypersensitivity to its chemical compounds. Proper precautions should be taken whenever handling this species to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
