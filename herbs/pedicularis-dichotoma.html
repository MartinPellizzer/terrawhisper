Pedicularis dichotoma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis dichotoma has a basal rosette of leaves that transition into stem leaves with deeply pinnatifid or lobed margins and a dense, terminal inflorescence of bilabiate, tubular flowers ranging in color from pink to reddish-purple. The plant typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and possesses a woody caudex from which the stems arise. Each individual flower features a prominent, hooded upper lip and a smaller, three-lobed lower lip characteristic of the genus. The leaves are often arranged oppositely along the stem, though they may appear alternate near the top. This species is distinguished by its specific branching pattern and the delicate, hairy texture of its floral structures.

    What are the herbal preparations of Pedicularis dichotoma?

    Pedicularis dichotoma is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried roots, a concentrated infusion of the aerial parts, or a tincture made by soaking the plant material in alcohol.
