What to know about Lespedeza capitata (bush clover) before using it medicinally

Bush clover, scientifically known as Lespedeza capitata, is a nutrient-rich herb that has been touted for its health benefits, which include improving digestion, reducing inflammation, and boosting immunity.

In addition to its medicinal uses, bush clover is also prized in the culinary world for its delicate flavor and versatility in soups, salads, and teas. As a low-maintenance plant, bush clover is also a popular choice among gardeners, who appreciate its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and attract beneficial insects. From a botanical standpoint, bush clover belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

With a history dating back thousands of years, bush clover has been referenced in ancient Chinese medicine and has since become a staple herb in many Eastern cuisines.
