Ardisia filiformis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ardisia filiformis?

Ardisia filiformis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Ericales. Further specialization occurs at the family level under Primulaceae, where it is categorized under the genus Ardisia. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific species name, filiformis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia filiformis has slender, thread-like leaves that are characteristically narrow, elongated, and arranged alternately along its woody stems.

The foliage displays a deep green color on the upper surface, often appearing somewhat leathery in texture. Its stems are typically thin and flexible, reflecting the filiform nature suggested by its specific epithet. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves to facilitate reproduction.

The plant often develops small, fleshy fruits that serve as a key identification feature in its natural habitat.
