Exochorda racemosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Exochorda racemosa has deciduous, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that typically measure two to four inches in length and feature finely serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a smooth, glossy texture and a bright green color that transitions as the seasons change. During the flowering period, the plant produces small, four-petaled white blossoms that emerge in clusters along elongated racemes. These delicate flowers often appear in late spring, sometimes preceding the full development of the leaves. The stems are woody and can grow in a spreading, shrubby habit, reaching heights of several feet.

What are the herbal preparations of Exochorda racemosa?

Exochorda racemosa is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice derived from its bark, leaves, or flowers. What side-effects this plant can have?

Exochorda racemosa can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive upset if ingested or handled improperly. While the plant is primarily grown for ornamental purposes, some individuals may experience contact dermatitis after touching its foliage or flowers. Ingestion of any part of the shrub could lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort in sensitive people. There is limited clinical research regarding its systemic toxicity, so caution is advised when using it in herbal preparations. It is best to avoid direct contact if you have a history of plant allergies.
