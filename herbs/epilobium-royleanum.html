Epilobium royleanum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Epilobium royleanum has erect, woody stems that reach heights of up to 2 meters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by an ovate-lanceolate shape and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically displaying a pale pink to white coloration. These blossoms feature four spreading petals and delicate stamens that protrude slightly from the corolla. The plant's overall structure is upright and shrub-like, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Within these mountainous ecosystems, it is frequently found in moist, shaded areas such as forest edges and near mountain streams. The species is specifically adapted to the temperate climates and high humidity levels characteristic of these Eastern Himalayan slopes.
