    What is Cacalia aurea?

    Cacalia aurea is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

    It grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that terminates in a corymbiform inflorescence. The leaves are lanceolate, serrulate, and pubescent, measuring 5-15 cm in length and 1-3 cm in width. The flowers are small, yellow, and borne in a capitulum, with five-merous corollas and a disc of 20-30 florets.

    The fruit is an achene, approximately 3-4 mm in length, with a pappus of 2-3 cm long filaments.
