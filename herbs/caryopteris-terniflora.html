Caryopteris terniflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Caryopteris terniflora?

Caryopteris terniflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls into the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement within the genus Caryopteris, specifically designated as the species terniflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caryopteris terniflora has an upright, woody, deciduous shrub habit characterized by trifoliate leaves and terminal clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers. Its stems are slender and multi-branched, often reaching a height and spread of approximately three feet. During the late summer, the plant produces dense panicles of flowers that attract various pollinators. The overall structure provides a compact, mounding appearance that is highly ornamental in garden settings.
