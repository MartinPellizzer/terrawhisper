Eulophia graminea

What's the taxonomical classification of Eulophia graminea?

Eulophia graminea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Eulophia, and its unique species designation, graminea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Eulophia graminea contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's underground tubers. Research suggests that these compounds may exhibit significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Some traditional medicinal practices utilize these extracts to address inflammatory conditions and digestive ailments. The specific synergy between these molecules provides the basis for its pharmacological activity in ethnobotanical applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Eulophia graminea?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including respiratory issues, digestive disorders, and skin infections. The tubers are often processed into a powder or decoction to alleviate symptoms of coughs and bronchitis. It is also applied topically to soothe inflammations and promote healing in wounds or rashes. Some traditional practices utilize the plant to manage gastrointestinal distress by regulating bowel movements. These medicinal properties are primarily derived from the chemical compounds found within its underground structures.
