Eulophia graminea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eulophia graminea has elongated, linear leaves that resemble grass, a subterranean tuberous root system, and small flowers typically colored in shades of pink or white. The leaves are narrow and slender, growing directly from the base of the plant to form a dense cluster. Its flowering stems are relatively short and produce multiple small blossoms arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. The underground tubers serve as storage organs, allowing the plant to survive in various soil conditions.
