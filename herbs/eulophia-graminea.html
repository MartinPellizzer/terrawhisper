Eulophia graminea

What's the taxonomical classification of Eulophia graminea?

Eulophia graminea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Eulophia, and its unique species designation, graminea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eulophia graminea has elongated, linear leaves that resemble grass, a subterranean tuberous root system, and small flowers typically colored in shades of pink or white.

The leaves are narrow and slender, growing directly from the base of the plant to form a dense cluster. Its flowering stems are relatively short and produce multiple small blossoms arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. The underground tubers serve as storage organs, allowing the plant to survive in various soil conditions.
