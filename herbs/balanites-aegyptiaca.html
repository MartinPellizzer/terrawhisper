What is Balanites (Balanites Aegyptiaca)?

Balanites aegyptiaca, commonly known as the desert date or balanites, is a small, spiny shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East, characterized by its thick, fleshy fruit and medicinal properties used in traditional herbal medicine.

Balanites aegyptiaca, commonly known as the desert date, is also referred to by various regional names such as khallak, balan, and tamar, and is widely recognized in traditional herbal medicine across North Africa, the Middle East, and parts of South Asia.

Balanites aegyptiaca is a small, thorny shrub characterized by its dense, branching structure, yellow-green leaves with a waxy coating, and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into hard, spherical, woody fruits containing a single large seed.

Balanites aegyptiaca is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of southern Asia, including countries such as Egypt, Sudan, India, and Pakistan.
