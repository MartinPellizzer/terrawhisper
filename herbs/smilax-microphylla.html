Smilax microphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Smilax microphylla?

Smilax microphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Liliales. This climbing plant is a member of the family Smilacaceae, falling under the genus Smilax, and is specifically identified by its species name, microphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax microphylla has woody, climbing stems equipped with axillary thorns and small, leathery, elliptical leaves that typically measure between 2 and 5 centimeters in length. The foliage often displays a glossy texture and can be arranged alternately along the vine. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually giving way to clusters of small, fleshy berries. These berries transition in color from green to a dark red or black hue as they reach maturity. The plant's overall structure is defined by its ability to use its thorns to scramble through dense forest undergrowth.
