Reinwardtia indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Reinwardtia indica?

Reinwardtia indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's taxonomic path continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Linaeaecae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Reinwardtia, and its species designation, indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Reinwardtia indica has a woody shrub habit characterized by erect, slender branches and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically displays a dull green color and a somewhat rugose texture on the upper surface. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils in dense, terminal clusters, often showcasing a distinct pink or purplish hue. These blossoms are supported by delicate pedicels and contribute to the plant's ornamental appearance. The overall structure is compact yet can expand into a bushy form as the plant matures.

What are the pharmacological activities of Reinwardtia indica?

This plant has potent analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-microbial, antioxidant, and wound healing pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to mitigate pain and reduce swelling by inhibiting specific inflammatory mediators in the body. The presence of phytochemicals also provides significant defense against various bacterial and fungal pathogens. Additionally, the plant's antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, thereby preventing oxidative stress and cellular damage. These combined mechanisms significantly accelerate the tissue regeneration process required for effective wound healing.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Reinwardtia indica Dumort. protects against ponatinib-induced cerebral ischemia in adult zebrafish by attenuating oxidative stress, neuroinflammation, mitochondrial dysfunction, and neurotransmitter dysregulation. In the study, zebrafish subjected to a 24 h ischemic insult were treated with the hydroalcoholic leaf extract of R. indica for 7 consecutive days. The treatment significantly reduced lipid peroxidation and increased the antioxidant enzyme activity of SOD and catalase. Furthermore, the extract reduced levels of inflammatory cytokines, specifically TNF-α and IL-1β, while restoring levels of glutamate, GABA, acetylcholine, serotonin, dopamine, and noradrenaline. These results indicate that R. indica may be a promising therapeutic candidate for mitigating the effects of cerebral ischemia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a high concentration of bioactive medicinal compounds including total phenol, flavonoid, phenol, rutin, and tannins. These phytochemicals contribute to the diverse therapeutic potential and antioxidant properties of the species. Specifically, the presence of flavonoids and rutin supports cellular protection against oxidative stress. The total phenol and phenol content play a critical role in the plant's biological activities. Additionally, the tannins found within its tissues provide significant astringent and antimicrobial benefits.

According to a study published by "Microbial pathogenesis", the hydro alcoholic extract of Reinwardtia indica leaf contained a maximum total phenol content of 540.37 mg per g of gallic acid equivalents, while the stem contained 330.51 mg per g of gallic acid equivalents. The ethanolic extract of the leaf showed a maximum flavonoid content of 305 mg per gram of rutin equivalents, whereas the ethyl acetate extract of the stem contained 170.6 mg per gram of rutin equivalents. The ethanolic extract of the leaf exhibited the maximum zone of inhibition against E. coli at 11.00 ± 1.73 mm, P. aeurogenosa at 11.67 ± 0.58 mm, S. aureus at 10.33 ± 1.53 mm, and C. albicans at 11.33 ± 1.10 mm. Additionally, the ethyl acetate extracts of the leaf and stem demonstrated the minimum inhibitory concentration against all tested microorganisms.

This plant is used for treating cerebral ischemia, ischemic brain injuries, neuroinflammation, stroke, and various health complications. The therapeutic potential of Reinwardtia indica lies in its ability to mitigate damage caused by a lack of blood flow to the brain. Researchers have focused on its capacity to reduce neuroinflammation, which is a critical factor in the progression of neurological damage. By addressing these specific pathways, the plant offers a biological mechanism to combat the effects of a stroke. Ultimately, these properties make it a subject of interest for managing a wide range of various health complications related to brain health.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Reinwardtia indica Dumort. is used in traditional medicine across Asian countries like India, Nepal, and China for treating neurological disorders, particularly paralysis. This plant is utilized for managing wound care and various skin conditions, as well as providing pain relief for headaches and backaches.
