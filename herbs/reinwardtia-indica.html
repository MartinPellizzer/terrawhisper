Reinwardtia indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Reinwardtia indica?

Reinwardtia indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's taxonomic path continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Linaeaecae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Reinwardtia, and its species designation, indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Reinwardtia indica has a woody shrub habit characterized by erect, slender branches and oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically displays a dull green color and a somewhat rugose texture on the upper surface. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils in dense, terminal clusters, often showcasing a distinct pink or purplish hue. These blossoms are supported by delicate pedicels and contribute to the plant's ornamental appearance. The overall structure is compact yet can expand into a bushy form as the plant matures.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across India, Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Thailand, and parts of the Malay Peninsula. It thrives primarily in the dry, deciduous forests and scrublands found within these warmer climates. The species is frequently observed in rocky terrains and open woodland environments where sunlight is abundant. Its distribution follows a pattern typical of monsoon-influenced ecosystems. Localized populations are often found in various hilly and coastal areas across its primary range.

What are the pharmacological activities of Reinwardtia indica?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-microbial, antioxidant, and anti-tumor pharmacological activities. The bioactive compounds within Reinwardtia indica work to reduce pain and mitigate swelling by inhibiting inflammatory mediators. Its natural chemical constituents also demonstrate significant ability to combat various bacterial and fungal pathogens. Additionally, the plant provides protective antioxidant effects that neutralize harmful free radicals in the body. Research also indicates that certain extracts possess potent anti-tumor properties that may help inhibit the growth of cancer cells.

As discussed in a study, Reinwardtia indica Dumort. is used in traditional medicine to treat neurological disorders like paralysis, skin conditions, headaches, backaches, and gastrointestinal issues such as indigestion. In a study involving adult zebrafish subjected to a 24 h ponatinib-induced ischemic insult, the plant was administered via hydroalcoholic leaf extract for 7 consecutive days. The treatment significantly attenuated oxidative stress by reducing lipid peroxidation and increasing SOD and catalase antioxidant enzyme activity. It also reduced neuroinflammation by decreasing levels of TNF-α and IL-1β, while restoring mitochondrial function through increased levels of mitochondrial complexes. Furthermore, the plant normalized levels of glutamate, GABA, acetylcholine, serotonin, dopamine, and noradrenaline, which improved locomotion and reduced depressive-like behaviours.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of medicinal compounds including total phenol, flavonoid, phenol, rutin, and tannins. The presence of total phenol and various phenol derivatives provides significant antioxidant activity to the plant's chemical makeup. Within these categories, flavonoids serve as essential bioactive agents that help reduce inflammation in biological systems. The specific compound rutin contributes further to the plant's ability to strengthen capillary walls and protect cells from oxidative stress. Additionally, the concentration of tannins offers astringent properties that can assist in wound healing and antimicrobial defense.

As discussed in a study, Reinwardtia indica contains medicinal compounds such as phenols and flavonoids. The hydro alcoholic extract of the leaf contained a maximum total phenol content of 540.37 mg per g of gallic acid equivalents, while the stem contained 330.51 mg per g of gallic acid equivalents. Regarding flavonoid content, the ethanolic extract of the leaf reached 305 mg per gram of rutin equivalents and the ethyl acetate extract of the stem reached 170.6 mg per gram of rutin equivalents. The ethanolic extract of the leaf also showed maximum zones of inhibition against E. coli at 11.00 ± 1.73 mm, P. aeurogenosa at 11.67 ± 0.58 mm, S. aureus at 10.33 ± 1.53 mm, and C. albicans at 11.33 ± 1.10 mm. These extracts serve as potent natural antioxidants with antibacterial and antifungal actions.
