What's the taxonomical classification of Axyris prostrata?

Axyris prostrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Caryophyllales and the family Amaranthaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Axyris, with the specific epithet prostrata defining its species designation.

      What are the herbal preparations of Axyris prostrata?

      Axyris prostrata is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the specific medicinal application intended. Practitioners often boil the leaves and stems in water to create a potent liquid used for internal consumption. For external ailments such as skin irritations or wounds, the plant material is crushed into a thick paste to be applied directly to the affected area. Some traditional methods also involve drying the plant parts to facilitate the creation of powders or tea blends. These diverse preparation methods allow for the extraction of various bioactive compounds necessary for treatment.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Axyris prostrata can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential digestive distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may lead to localized redness, itching, or a rash on sensitive skin. Some individuals might experience more severe dermatological responses such as swelling or blistering depending on their personal sensitivity. If consumed accidentally, the plant could trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort. Due to these risks, it is important to handle the species with caution and avoid any direct contact with mucous membranes.
