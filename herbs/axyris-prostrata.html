What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Axyris prostrata has a prostrate, spreading growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems that typically reach heights of only a few centimeters above the ground. The leaves are small, linear to narrowly lanceolate, and arranged alternately along the stems. These plants produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in the axils of the leaves. The fruit is a small, dry schizocarp that splits into individual mericarps at maturity. This low-growing structure allows the plant to thrive in disturbed or sandy environments where competition from taller vegetation is minimal.
