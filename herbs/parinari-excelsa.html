Parinari excelsa

What's the taxonomical classification of Parinari excelsa?

Parinari excelsa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, the plant is placed in the order Malpighiales and belongs to the family Chrysobalanaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Parinarri, with the specific epithet excelsa.

Parinari excelsa has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties derived from its bark, leaves, and roots. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, gastrointestinal issues, fever, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to address dermatological problems like eczema and fungal infections. In traditional medicine, extracts are also utilized to manage digestive ailments and promote gut health. Additionally, the plant serves as an agent to reduce body temperature during febrile episodes. What are the herbal preparations of Parinari excelsa?

Parinari excelsa is prepared as decoctions, infusions, bark powders, and topical poultices derived from its various parts. The bark is frequently boiled in water to create medicinal teas used for treating diverse ailments. Extracts from the leaves and roots are also processed into liquid preparations for internal consumption. For external applications, the plant material is often crushed into a paste to be applied directly to the skin. These diverse forms allow the botanical properties of the tree to be utilized for both systemic and localized healing.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Parinari excelsa can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and allergic responses if ingested or applied incorrectly. While many traditional uses suggest therapeutic benefits, the lack of extensive clinical human trials makes the full spectrum of toxicity difficult to determine. Some users may experience localized itching or rashes when using extracts topically on sensitive skin. Ingestion of concentrated forms might also lead to nausea or stomach discomfort in certain individuals. It is essential to exercise caution and consult a medical professional before incorporating this plant into any health regimen.
