Parinari excelsa

What's the taxonomical classification of Parinari excelsa?

Parinari excelsa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, the plant is placed in the order Malpighiales and belongs to the family Chrysobalanaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Parinarri, with the specific epithet excelsa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Parinari excelsa contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including tannins, saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated in the bark and leaves of the tree. Scientific studies suggest that these compounds possess significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Such medicinal qualities are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat inflammation and various infections. The presence of these diverse phytochemicals supports the plant's role in diverse pharmacological applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Parinari excelsa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, gastrointestinal issues, fever, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to address dermatological problems like eczema and fungal infections. In traditional medicine, extracts are also utilized to manage digestive ailments and promote gut health. Additionally, the plant serves as an agent to reduce body temperature during febrile episodes. Its diverse chemical composition allows it to act as a natural remedy for multiple systemic ailments.
