Pimpinella yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pimpinnella yunnanensis has herbaceous stems that reach heights of approximately 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring compound leaves with serrated leaflets and small, white, umbel-shaped flower clusters. The foliage is characterized by deeply divided segments that provide a delicate, feathery texture to the plant's overall structure. Each inflorescence consists of numerous tiny individual flowers arranged in a flat-topped pattern typical of the Apiaceae family. The roots are typically fleshy and serve as a primary storage organ for the species. Small, dry fruits develop following successful pollination to facilitate seed dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Yunnan province of southwestern China. It grows specifically within high-altitude montane regions where the climate is temperate and moist. Its distribution is closely tied to the unique topographical features of the Hengduan Mountains. Within these mountainous habitats, it thrives in shaded forest understories and rocky slopes. Localized populations are often found at elevations that provide consistent seasonal rainfall.
