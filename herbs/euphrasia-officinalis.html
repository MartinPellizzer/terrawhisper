Euphrasia

Euphrasia officinalis

Euphrasia (Euphrasia officinalis) is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, commonly known as the eyebright plant, which has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, particularly in improving eye health and reducing symptoms of eye conditions such as conjunctivitis and blepharitis.

Botanical Identification

Euphrasia officinalis, commonly known as Eyebright, is a perennial herb in the Scrophulariaceae family. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it features small yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. It is also referred to as Euphrasia roboarta in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Scrophulariales
  • Family: Scrophulariaceae
  • Genus: Euphrasia
  • Species: Euphrasia officinalis

Active Compounds

Euphrasia officinalis contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to support eye health and reduce inflammation. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Euphrasia Actions.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and eye conditions due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Infusion: Used for eye ailments and as a mild stimulant due to its high vitamin c content.

Tincture: Used for eye infections and inflammation due to its concentrated active compounds.

Safety Profile

Euphrasia officinalis is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Individuals with allergies to plants in the Plantain family should also avoid its use.
