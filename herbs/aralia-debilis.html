Aralia debilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia debilis has a prostrate to decumbent growth habit characterized by slender, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant typically features creeping stems that spread along the ground before ascending slightly. Each leaf consists of three distinct leaflets that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in umbels, which eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in shaded, forest floor environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across southern China, Taiwan, Japan, and parts of Southeast Asia. It typically thrives in the shaded understory of broad-leaved forests where humidity remains consistently high. The species is often found growing in moist, well-drained soils along forest edges or near stream banks. Its distribution is largely determined by the availability of consistent moisture and protection from direct, intense sunlight. Consequently, its presence is most concentrated in temperate to subtropical maritime climates.
