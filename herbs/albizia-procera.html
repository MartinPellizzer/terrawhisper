Albizia procera

What's the taxonomical classification of Albizia procera?

Albizia procera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is ordered within the Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Albizia and is specifically designated as the species procera.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Albizia procera contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and terpenoids that exhibit diverse pharmacological activities. These chemical constituents are primarily utilized in traditional medicine to address inflammatory conditions and promote wound healing. Specifically, the presence of tannins and flavonoids provides significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties to the plant extracts. Research suggests that the various alkaloids within the species may also contribute to sedative or analgesic effects. These medicinal compounds work synergistically to provide therapeutic benefits in treating various ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Albizia procera?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, gastrointestinal disorders, inflammation, and various respiratory ailments. The bark is frequently employed in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of stomach aches and dysentery. Topical applications of the leaves or bark extracts are often utilized to soothe wounds and manage dermatological issues. Additionally, decoctions made from the plant parts may help reduce fever and calm persistent coughs. These medicinal properties are highly valued in various regional folk healing practices.
