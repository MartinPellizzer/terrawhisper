What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum fuchsiifolium?

Jasminum fuchsiifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales. This species is a member of the family Oleaceae and falls under the genus Jasminum, with its specific designation being fuchsiifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum fuchsiifoliuum has opposite, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that measure between 3 and 7 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with acute tips. The foliage is typically a deep, lustrous green and grows on woody, climbing stems that can reach significant lengths through twining. Its flowers are tubular and salverform, featuring five white lobes that bloom in clusters from the leaf axils. During the flowering season, these fragrant blossoms emerge from small, green calyces. The plant also produces small, fleshy drupes that turn dark upon maturity.
