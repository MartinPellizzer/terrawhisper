Jasminum fuchsiifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum fuchsiifolium?

Jasminum fuchsiifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales. This species is a member of the family Oleaceae and falls under the genus Jasminum, with its specific designation being fuchsiifolium.

What are the therapeutic actions of Jasminum fuchsiifolium?

Jasminum fuchsiifoliuum has therapeutic actions centered on its sedative, anxiolytic, and antispasmodic properties which help alleviate nervous tension and physical discomfort.     What are the herbal preparations of Jasminum fuchsiifolium?

    Jasminum fuchsiifoliuum is prepared as dried flowers for herbal teas, concentrated liquid extracts, topical oils, and infused tinctures. The dried blossoms are frequently steeped in hot water to create aromatic infusions used for their calming properties. In traditional practices, the plant is often processed into concentrated extracts that allow for precise dosing in liquid form. For external applications, the flowers are infused into carrier oils to produce soothing salves or massage oils. These various preparations utilize different parts of the plant to target both internal wellness and external skin health.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Jasminum fuchsiifoliuum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or a rash upon direct contact with the plant's leaves or stems. There is also a risk of developing hypersensitivity reactions in people who are frequently exposed to its oils. If consumed accidentally, it may lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramping. While much of the plant is used decoratively, cautious handling is recommended to avoid these adverse reactions.
