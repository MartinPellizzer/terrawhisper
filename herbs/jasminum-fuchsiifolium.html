Jasminum fuchsiifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum fuchsiifolium?

Jasminum fuchsiifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales. This species is a member of the family Oleaceae and falls under the genus Jasminum, with its specific designation being fuchsiifolium.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Jasminum fuchsiifoliuum contains high concentrations of bioactive phytochemicals including triterpenoids, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils such as linalool. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented pharmacological properties, particularly its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers have identified various sesquiterpenes within the leaf extracts that may offer potential antimicrobial benefits. Additionally, the presence of diverse terpenoid compounds suggests the plant could be utilized in developing natural therapeutic agents. These medicinal components work synergistically to provide the biological effects observed in traditional herbal applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Jasminum fuchsiifolium?

Jasminum fuchsiifoliuum has therapeutic actions centered on its sedative, anxiolytic, and antispasmodic properties which help alleviate nervous tension and physical discomfort. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that interact with the central nervous system to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality. When used in traditional practices, its extracts can help soothe digestive upsets and reduce muscle spasms. The aromatic components also serve to calm the mind and reduce feelings of stress or anxiety. These calming effects make it a valuable botanical resource for managing mild emotional disturbances.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, inflammation, fever, and digestive issues. Its medicinal properties are primarily derived from its ability to act as an anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial agent. Extracts from the leaves or flowers are often applied topically to soothe skin irritation or wounds. In some traditional practices, preparations are ingested to help reduce fever or stabilize the digestive system. The specific chemical compounds within the plant contribute to its broad therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

What are the herbal preparations of Jasminum fuchsiifolium?

Jasminum fuchsiifoliuum is prepared as dried flowers for herbal teas, concentrated liquid extracts, topical oils, and infused tinctures. The dried blossoms are frequently steeped in hot water to create aromatic infusions used for their calming properties. In traditional practices, the plant is often processed into concentrated extracts that allow for precise dosing in liquid form. For external applications, the flowers are infused into carrier oils to produce soothing salves or massage oils. These various preparations utilize different parts of the plant to target both internal wellness and external skin health.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Jasminum fuchsiifoliuum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or a rash upon direct contact with the plant's leaves or stems. There is also a risk of developing hypersensitivity reactions in people who are frequently exposed to its oils. If consumed accidentally, it may lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramping. While much of the plant is used decoratively, cautious handling is recommended to avoid these adverse reactions.
