Astragalus polycladus

What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus polycladus?

Astragalus polycladus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Astragalus, and its specific designation is given by the species name polycladus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Astragalus polycladus contains high concentrations of triterpenoid saponins, specifically polycladins, which serve as its primary bioactive constituents. These unique saponins are recognized for their potential immunomodulatory and anti-inflammatory properties in pharmacological studies. Beyond these specific compounds, the plant also possesses various polysaccharides that may support cellular defense mechanisms. Researchers are currently investigating how these chemical profiles contribute to the plant's traditional use in medicinal applications. The structural complexity of these triterpenoids makes them a significant subject of interest in natural product chemistry.

What are the therapeutic actions of Astragalus polycladus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin ailments, and certain types of infections. It is frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and manage localized swelling. Some traditional practices utilize its properties to address internal imbalances that contribute to systemic inflammation. Research suggests its bioactive compounds may play a role in managing symptoms related to immune responses. These medicinal applications highlight its significance in diverse herbal traditions.
