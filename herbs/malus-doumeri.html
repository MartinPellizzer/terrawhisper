Malus doumeri

What's the taxonomical classification of Malus doumeri?

Malus doumeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Rosales. It is a member of the family Rosaceae, falling under the genus Malus, and is specifically identified by the species name doumeri.

What are the herbal preparations of Malus doumeri?

Malus doumeri is prepared as a dried fruit infusion, a concentrated syrup, or a distilled tincture derived from its fleshy pomes. The dried fruit pieces are often steeped in hot water to create a mild, aromatic tea rich in tannins. For more potent applications, the sugars in the fruit are boiled down with honey or cane sugar to produce a medicinal syrup. Tinctures are also crafted by macerating the fruit in high-proof alcohol to extract specific bioactive compounds. These various forms allow for different dosages and methods of administration depending on the desired therapeutic effect.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Malus doumeri can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if any part of the plant is ingested or touched improperly. While the fruit is generally considered edible, consuming large quantities of unripe fruit may lead to stomach upset due to high acidity or tannins. Contact with the leaves or bark can trigger contact dermatitis in individuals with sensitive skin. Some people may experience swelling or itching if they encounter the pollen during the flowering season. It is important to exercise caution when handling the plant to avoid these potential adverse reactions.
