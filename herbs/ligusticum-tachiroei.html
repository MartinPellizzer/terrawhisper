Ligusticum tachiroei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligusticum tachiroei has hollow, striated stems that support large, compound leaves characterized by multiple pinnate leaflets with serrated margins. The foliage often exhibits a deep green color and a delicate, feathery texture due to the fine division of its segments. Small, white flowers are arranged in dense, flat-topped umbels typical of the Apiaceae family. These inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils or terminal positions on the flowering stalks. The plant also produces small, oblong fruits that develop following successful pollination.
