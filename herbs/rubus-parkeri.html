Rubus parkeri

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus parkeri?

Rubus parkeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical lineage further, it falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its genus, Rubus, and its specific species designation, parkeri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus parkeri has arching, perennial stems known as canes that are covered in fine, reddish-brown hairs and sharp, recurved prickles. These stems typically reach heights of one to two meters and can either grow upright or sprawl along the ground. The leaves are compound with three to five distinct, serrated leaflets that possess a dark green color and a slightly wrinkled texture. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the flowering season. Following pollination, the plant produces aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep, glossy red when mature.
