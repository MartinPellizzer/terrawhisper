Bioactive Constituents

Prunus virginiana ursolic acid is a triterpene compound known for its potent anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antitumor properties, which contribute to the herb's traditional use in treating skin conditions and inflammatory disorders.

Prunus virginiana prunasin is a cyanogenic glycoside that, when hydrolyzed, releases hydrogen cyanide, acting as a natural defense mechanism in the plant and potentially contributing to its medicinal effects in small therapeutic doses.

Prunus virginiana oleanolic acid is another triterpene with significant hepatoprotective and cholesterol-lowering effects, supporting liver function and cardiovascular health.

Prunus virginiana proanthocyanidins are a class of polyphenolic compounds with strong antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body.
