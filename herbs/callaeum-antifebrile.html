What is Callaeum antifebrile?

Callaeum antifebrile is a plant species in the family Malpighiaceae, native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub with hairy stems and leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, typically 10-20 cm in length, and have 4-6 pairs of lateral veins. The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a short petiole. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow in color, and arranged in axillary clusters.

The plant produces globose fruits that are 2-3 cm in diameter, containing 2-3 seeds.

The combination of these properties makes Callaeum antifebrile a valuable resource in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.
