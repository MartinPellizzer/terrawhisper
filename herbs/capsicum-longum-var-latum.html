Long Pepper (Capsicum Longum Var. Latum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Capsicum longum var. latum.

Long pepper, scientifically known as Capsicum longum var. latum, is a medicinal herb and spice that has been valued for centuries for its therapeutic properties. It is an adaptogen, meaning it helps the body resist stress and maintain balance, and is also known for its pungent, peppery flavor that distinguishes it from black pepper. Traditionally used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine, long pepper was believed to enhance digestion, stimulate circulation, and support respiratory health. In modern wellness practices, it is used as a natural remedy for colds, inflammation, and digestive issues due to its high concentration of capsaicinoids, which have analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects. Historically, long pepper was so prized that it was once referred to as "black gold" and played a significant role in ancient trade routes, making it a rare and valuable spice in global commerce.
