What is Cistanthe trigona?

Cistanthe trigona is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Montiaceae, native to South America.

It has a woody caudex and a rosette of narrow, linear to lanceolate leaves, measuring 2-10 cm in length and 0.5-2 mm in width. The leaves are glabrous or sparsely pubescent, with entire margins and a ciliate base. The plant produces a solitary, axillary inflorescence with 2-5 pink or white flowers, each measuring 10-15 mm in diameter and having a 5-lobed corolla.

The fruit is a schizocarp, breaking into 4-6 mericarps, each containing a single seed.
