Carya cathayensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Carya cathayensis?

Carya cathayensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Fagales and is a member of the family Juglandaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Carya, with its specific designation being the species cathayensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Carya cathayensis contains various secondary metabolites including polyphenols, flavonoids, and tannins that contribute to its potential pharmacological properties. These bioactive compounds are primarily studied for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory capabilities within traditional medicinal contexts. Research suggests that the extracts may possess antimicrobial effects against certain bacterial strains. Specific phenolic acids found in the plant tissues are believed to play a crucial role in neutralizing free radicals. Further investigation is required to fully map the precise chemical profiles and clinical applications of these specific constituents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach ailments, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are often prepared as decoctions to soothe digestive disturbances. Local traditional practitioners also apply topical preparations to manage wounds and fungal skin issues. These medicinal properties are utilized within specific regional folk medicine practices to promote healing. Such applications highlight the plant's role in managing common physical discomforts.
