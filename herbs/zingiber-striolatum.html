Zingiber striolatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Zingiber striolatum?

Zingiber striolatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Zingiberales. This plant is further categorized under the family Zingiberaceae, which places it among the ginger family, and it is specifically identified by the genus Zingiber and the species striolatum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Zingiber striolatum?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities, most notably demonstrating potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These biological effects are primarily attributed to the presence of bioactive secondary metabolites such as phenolic compounds and gingerols. The antioxidant capacity helps neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress within cellular structures. Simultaneously, its anti-inflammatory actions work by modulating specific biochemical pathways to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators. These combined therapeutic attributes make Zingiber striolatum a subject of interest for developing natural medicinal agents.

According to a study published by "Antioxidants (Basel, Switzerland)", essential oil from Zingiber striolatum (EOZS) extracted from 7 regions in China contained 116 identified compounds. Samples 2, 4, and 5 demonstrated the best capability regarding DPPH radical scavenging and NO inhibition. These specific samples significantly reduced the production of ROS, pro-inflammatory cytokines, macrophage morphological changes, migration, and phagocytic capability. In TPAs-induced mice, EOZS reduced the degree of ear swelling and local immune cell infiltration by blocking the activation of MAPK and NF-κB signaling pathways. The research unveils the antioxidant capability and potential molecular mechanism of EOZS in regulating inflammatory response.
