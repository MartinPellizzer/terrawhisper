What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera pseudotinctoria has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of small, oblong leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink or purple. The plant features a woody or semi-woody stem structure that can reach various heights depending on its growing environment. Its inflorescences are arranged in axillary racemes, often appearing in clusters along the branches. The fruit develops into small, flattened legumes that contain individual seeds. These morphological traits are characteristic of many species within the Fabaceae family.

      What are the herbal preparations of Indigofera pseudotinctoria?

      Indigofera pseudotinctoria is prepared as a fermented liquid dye, a topical paste, or a decoction through the processing of its fresh or dried leaves. The most common method involves soaking the plant material in water to initiate a fermentation process that releases the indigotin pigment. This liquid can then be used as a bath for textiles or applied to surfaces as a concentrated pigment. For traditional topical applications, the leaves are often crushed into a thick paste to serve as a coloring agent. These various preparations allow the plant's chemical properties to be utilized effectively for both artistic and practical purposes.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Indigofera pseudotinctoria can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling when the plant comes into direct contact with human tissue. The presence of certain alkaloids and flavonoids within its leaves and stems may trigger hypersensitivity reactions in sensitive individuals. When handled without protection, the sap can lead to redness or itching on the hands and arms. Some users might also experience systemic sensitivities if the plant material is ingested or processed improperly. Proper precautions like wearing gloves are recommended to mitigate these dermatological risks.
