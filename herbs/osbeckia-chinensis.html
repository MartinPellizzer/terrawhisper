Osbeckia chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Osbeckia chinensis?

Osbeckia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is organized under the order Myrtales and falls within the family Melastomataceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Osbeckia, with its specific designation being the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Osbeckia chinensis has opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves characterized by prominent longitudinal veins and densely hairy surfaces. The stems are woody and often display a reddish tint with fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, featuring five petals and numerous protruding stamens that create a fuzzy appearance. The fruit consists of a small, globose capsule that contains multiple tiny seeds. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species within the Melastomataceae family.
