Rehmannia chingii

What's the taxonomical classification of Rehmannia chingii?

Rehmannia chingii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Orobanchaceae. At the most specific levels of its biological classification, it is identified by the genus Rehmannia and the species chingii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rehmannia chingii has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by thick, fleshy, tuberous roots and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems that support its dense foliage. Its flowers are tubular or funnel-shaped and usually appear in shades of purple or violet. During the flowering season, these blossoms emerge from the upper leaf axils to form small clusters. The leaves possess a soft, slightly pubescent texture on both the upper and lower surfaces.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 6-o-p-coumaroylajugol, aucubin, catalpol, daphnin, and virexilacton. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse biological activities observed in the species. The iridoid glycosides aucubin and catalpol are particularly noted for their therapeutic potential. Additionally, the presence of daphnin and virexilactone adds to the complex chemical profile of the plant. Researchers study these substances to understand how they interact with various physiological systems.

According to a study published by "PeerJ", 1,420 metabolites were identified within Rehmannia chingii. Among these, four differential biomarkers were found: p-coumaroylcadaveriine, protocatechuic acid-4-o-glucoside, 5, 6-dimethyl-2-benzofuran-1, 3-dione, and daphnin. The study prioritized 31 potential bioactive ingredients via TCMSP screening. Roots accumulated high levels of tangeretin, 6-o-p-coumaroylajugol, guanosine, virexilactone, and aucubin, while multiple flavonoids were enriched in the leaves. Key potential bioactive marker ingredients included coniferin and tangeretin, which possessed oral bioavailability values ≥30% and drug-likeness values ≥0.18.
