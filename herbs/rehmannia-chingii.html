Rehmannia chingii

What's the taxonomical classification of Rehmannia chingii?

Rehmannia chingii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Orobanchaceae. At the most specific levels of its biological classification, it is identified by the genus Rehmannia and the species chingii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rehmannia chingii has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by thick, fleshy, tuberous roots and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems that support its dense foliage. Its flowers are tubular or funnel-shaped and usually appear in shades of purple or violet. During the flowering season, these blossoms emerge from the upper leaf axils to form small clusters. The leaves possess a soft, slightly pubescent texture on both the upper and lower surfaces.

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of southern China, specifically occurring in provinces such as Guangxi, Guangdong, Hunan, and Yunnan. It typically thrives in hilly areas and mountainous landscapes where the terrain provides necessary drainage. These plants are most frequently found growing in the understory of evergreen broad-leaved forests. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds aucubin, catalpol, iridoid glycosides, daphnin, and coniferiin. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the various therapeutic properties associated with the species. The iridoid glycosides, which include both aucubin and catalpol, often play a significant role in the plant's biological activity. Additionally, the presence of daphnin and coniferiin adds to its complex phytochemical profile. Understanding these substances is essential for studying the plant's potential pharmacological applications.

As discussed in a study, Rehmannia chingii contains 1,420 identified metabolites with specific medicinal compounds found in its leaves and roots. Four differential biomarkers were identified, including p-coumaroylcadaverine, protocatechuic acid-4-o-glucoside, 5, 6-dimethyl-2-benzofuran-1, 3-dione, and daphnin. Through TCMSP screening, 31 potential bioactive ingredients were prioritized for the plant. Roots accumulate higher levels of tangeretin, 6-o-p-coumaroylajugol, guanosine, virexilactone, and aucubin. Coniferin and tangeretin were identified as key bioactive marker ingredients with oral bioavailability values ≥30% and drug-likeness values ≥0.18.
