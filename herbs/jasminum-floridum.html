What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum floridum has woody, scrambling stems that produce opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and pinnate venation. The foliage typically reaches lengths of two to four inches and maintains a glossy, deep green appearance throughout the growing season. Small, tubular white flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils, characterized by five spreading lobes. These blossoms are accompanied by small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to dark purple or black upon maturity. The overall structure of the plant allows it to function as a climbing vine or a dense, spreading shrub.
