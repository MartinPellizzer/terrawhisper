Diplazium zeylanicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Diplazium zeylanicum?

Diplazium zeylanicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it resides in the order Polypodiales and is a member of the family Aspleniaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Diplazium, and its specific species designation, zeylanicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diplazium zeylanicum has large, bipinnate to tripinnate fronds characterized by lanceolate pinnae with serrated margins and a distinctively membranous texture. The stipe is typically long and dark, often appearing somewhat glossy or brownish in color. The rachis remains relatively slender as it extends toward the tip of the leaf. On the underside of the pinnules, the sori are arranged in neat rows between the midvein and the margin. These reproductive structures are protected by a thin, kidney-shaped indusium.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Within its range, it is often found in low-elevation montane forests and coastal areas characterized by high rainfall. The distribution is largely dictated by the availability of consistent humidity and specific soil compositions found in these equatorial zones.
