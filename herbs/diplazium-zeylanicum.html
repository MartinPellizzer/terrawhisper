Diplazium zeylanicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Diplazium zeylanicum?

Diplazium zeylanicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it resides in the order Polypodiales and is a member of the family Aspleniaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Diplazium, and its specific species designation, zeylanicum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Diplazium zeylanicum contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and sterols that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory capabilities. Research indicates that the presence of specific phenolic compounds helps in neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. Furthermore, the alkaloids and glycosides found in the rhizomes are often studied for their potential antimicrobial and diuretic effects. These diverse medicinal compounds collectively support the traditional use of the fern in various herbal remedies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Diplazium zeylanicum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, digestive issues, wounds, and respiratory ailments. The fern is frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of external injuries. In some traditional practices, decoctions made from its parts are ingested to address stomach discomfort and various gastrointestinal problems. It is also believed to possess properties that can help alleviate symptoms related to the respiratory system. These medicinal uses highlight the plant's importance in various regional folk medicine traditions.

What are the herbal preparations of Diplazium zeylanicum?

Diplazium zeylanicum is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried rhizomes in water to extract its medicinal compounds. This aqueous extract is the most common method used in traditional herbal medicine to treat various ailments. Sometimes the dried plant material is also incorporated into powder forms for easier measurement and administration. These powders can be mixed with honey or warm water to facilitate consumption. In certain regional practices, topical applications using plant infusions are utilized for external healing purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Diplazium zeylanicum can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. While specific clinical studies on humans are limited, many fern species contain compounds that may trigger sensitivity in certain individuals. Contact with the fronds might lead to redness or itching for those with sensitive skin. If the plant is consumed, it could potentially cause nausea or stomach discomfort. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid direct contact or ingestion to prevent these adverse reactions.
