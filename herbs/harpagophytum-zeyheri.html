Feverfew

Harpagophytum zeyheri

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) is a medicinal plant native to South Africa, known for its use in treating inflammatory conditions such as arthritis and muscle pain.

Botanical Identification

Feverfew, scientifically known as Harpagophytum zeyheri, belongs to the family Asteraceae. It is also called common feverfew and is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. The plant has finely divided leaves and yellow flowers, with roots used in traditional medicine. It is sometimes referred to as "devil's backbone" in herbal literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Harpagophytum
  • Species: Harpagophytum zeyheri

Active Compounds

Feverfew contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to reduce inflammation and pain. Their combined action supports feverfew's traditional use in treating headaches and inflammatory conditions.

  • Flavonoid
  • Terpenoid
  • Phenolic acid

Feverfew Uses.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for joint pain and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Extract: Used for muscle pain and arthritis due to its analgesic effects.

Safety Profile

Feverfew is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset, headaches, or allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential adverse effects on the fetus or infant.
