Feverfew

Harpagophytum zeyheri

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) is a medicinal plant native to South Africa, known for its use in treating inflammatory conditions such as arthritis and muscle pain.

Taxonomy

Feverfew, or Harpagophytum zeyheri, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae, belonging to the genus Harpagophytum within the order Fabales.

Morphology

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody, branched stem, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, with the plant typically growing to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

Distribution

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) is native to southern Africa, specifically occurring in countries such as South Africa, Namibia, Botswana, and parts of Zimbabwe and Mozambique, where it grows in arid and semi-arid regions.

Traditional Uses

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of arthritis, reduce inflammation, and ease pain, particularly in conditions such as rheumatoid arthritis and osteoarthritis.

Medicinal Systems

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) has been traditionally used in African herbal medicine, particularly among the Zulu and Xhosa communities, for its pain-relieving and anti-inflammatory properties.

Phytochemistry

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) contains bioactive compounds such as sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and polyacetylenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic effects.

Pharmacology

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antispasmodic effects, making it effective in the management of conditions such as osteoarthritis, muscle pain, and digestive disorders.

Botanical Identification

Feverfew, scientifically known as Harpagophytum zeyheri, belongs to the family Asteraceae. It is also called common feverfew and is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. The plant has finely divided leaves and yellow flowers, with roots used in traditional medicine. It is sometimes referred to as "devil's backbone" in herbal literature.

Safety Profile

Feverfew is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset, headaches, or allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential adverse effects on the fetus or infant.
