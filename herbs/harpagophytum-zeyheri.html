Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri)

Feverfew (Harpagophytum zeyheri) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Southern Africa, Namibia, and South Africa. Traditionally, its roots, stems, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and tibetan medicine.
