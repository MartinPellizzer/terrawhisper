Phlegmariurus mingcheensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlegmariurus mingcheensis has pendulous, slender, unbranched or sparsely branched stems covered in small, imbricated, lanceolate microphylls that are closely appressed to the axis. These leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern and possess a distinctively narrow, acute shape. The plant exhibits a creeping or hanging growth habit, often found colonizing mossy substrates in montane environments. The color of the vegetative body varies from bright green to a darker brownish-green depending on light exposure. Reproductive structures are located on specialized fertile leaves that differ significantly in shape from the sterile vegetative leaves.

      Phlegmariurus mingcheensis is prepared primarily as a decoction where the dried aerial parts of the plant are boiled in water to extract active medicinal compounds.
