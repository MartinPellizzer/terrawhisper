Sanguisorba minor

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Sanguisorba minor, commonly known as lesser burnet, is a perennial herb in the Rosaceae family. It is native to Europe and western Asia, featuring upright stems, toothed leaves, and pinkish-red flowers. It is also referred to as burnet or redroot in various regional contexts.

Active Compounds

Sanguisorba contains key active compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their synergistic action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Cough, respiratory infections, bronchitis, inflamed lungs Expectorant, antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, astringent
Integumentary Skin inflammation, wound healing, skin ulcers, skin infections, inflamed skin Anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, soother
Digestive Gastrointestinal inflammation, diarrhea, gastrointestinal spasms Anti-inflammatory, astringent, spasmolytic, anti-ulcer, carminative

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive discomfort and as a diuretic.

Extract: Used for skin conditions and as an anti-inflammatory.

Safety Profile

Sanguisorba minor is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Individuals with sensitive stomachs should use it with caution. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
