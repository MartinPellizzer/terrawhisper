Sanguisorba minor

Sanguisorba (Sanguisorba minor) is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, known for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Identification

Sanguisorba minor, commonly known as lesser burnet, is a perennial herb in the Rosaceae family. It is native to Europe and western Asia, featuring upright stems, toothed leaves, and pinkish-red flowers. It is also referred to as burnet or redroot in various regional contexts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rosales
  • Family: Rosaceae
  • Genus: Sanguisorba
  • Species: Sanguisorba minor

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Safety Profile

Sanguisorba minor is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Individuals with sensitive stomachs should use it with caution. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
