Rhamnus purshiana var. annonifolia

Rhamnus purshiana var. annonifolia, also known as cascara bark, is a plant species native to the western coast of North America, specifically in the Pacific Northwest region.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly as a natural laxative due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides, which stimulate bowel movements and aid in the elimination of waste.

The health benefits of cascara bark include promoting regular bowel movements, relieving constipation, and soothing digestive issues.

Cascara bark can be prepared as a tea, tincture, or powder, and is often used in herbal medicine to support digestive health and maintain a balanced gut microbiome.
