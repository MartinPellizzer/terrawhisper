Ribes meyeri

What's the taxonomical classification of Ribes meyeri?

Ribes meyeri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned in the order Saxifragales. It is a member of the family Grossulariaceae, which falls under the genus Ribes, and is specifically identified by the species name meyeri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ribes meyeri has woody, deciduous stems with small, ovate to heart-shaped leaves that feature serrated margins and a pubescent underside.

The leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches. During the flowering period, the plant produces small, bell-shaped blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers are often pale in color and serve to attract specific pollinators within its habitat.

The plant eventually develops small, fleshy berries that are characteristic of the genus.

The bioactive compounds found in this plant, particularly its polyphenols and flavonoids, act by inhibiting the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway.
