10 Medicinal Constituents Of Citrus Oto (Kaffir Lime)

Citrus oto has active constituents such as limonoids, flavonoids, and terpenes.

These constituents are responsible for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. The limonoids, in particular, have been shown to have anti-cancer and anti-HIV properties, improving lives by potentially preventing or treating these diseases.

Additionally, the flavonoids and terpenes can reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and stress.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Citrus oto.

1. Limonene

Citrus oto limonene is a compound found in its peel and leaves.

This essential oil has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it useful in aromatherapy and natural remedies. Its citrusy scent is often used to uplift mood and reduce stress.

Limonene has also been studied for its potential anticancer properties and as a natural insect repellent.

2. Citral

Citrus oto citral is a naturally occurring chemical compound found in its essential oil.

Citral is a volatile, highly unsaturated aldehyde that is responsible for the characteristic citrus fragrance of the kaffir lime.

It has a strong, citrusy aroma and is often used as a flavoring agent in food and beverages, as well as a fragrant component in perfumes and aromatherapy products.

3. Β-pinene

Citrus oto β-pinene is a naturally occurring compound found in the essential oil of the fruit.

It is a monoterpene hydrocarbon, which is a type of organic compound found in the oils of many plants. β-pinene is a key component of many perfumes and fragrances, and is also used as a solvent and as an active ingredient in some pharmaceuticals.

It has a fresh, citrusy aroma.

4. Α-pinene

Citrus oto α-pinene is a natural compound found in its peel oil.

It is a terpene with various applications in aromatherapy and perfumery. α-pinene is known for its refreshing and decongestant properties, and it can help alleviate respiratory issues such as asthma and bronchitis.

The scent of α-pinene is often used to uplift and invigorate the senses, promoting mental clarity and focus.

5. Furocoumarins

Citrus oto furocoumarins is a group of natural compounds found in its leaves.

Furocoumarins are known to possess phototoxic and photocarcinogenic properties, which can cause skin irritation, burns, and mutations when exposed to sunlight. They are also known to exhibit antifungal, antibacterial, and antiviral activities, making them of interest for their potential applications in medicine and cosmetics.

However, their toxicity requires careful handling and use.

6. Aurantiol

Citrus oto aurantiol is a naturally occurring compound found in the peel of the kaffir lime fruit.

It has been traditionally used in various cultures for its culinary and medicinal properties.

Aurantiol has been reported to possess antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant activities, making it a promising ingredient in the development of natural products and cosmetics.

7. Limonin

Citrus oto limonin is a naturally occurring compound found in the oil of certain citrus fruits.

It is known for its bitter flavor and has been used as a natural preservative in foods and beverages. Limonin has also been found to have various health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

It is often extracted and used in aromatherapy and natural remedies.

8. Hesperidin

Citrus oto hesperidin is a naturally occurring flavonoid glycoside found in the leaves and peels of citrus fruits, particularly in kaffir lime.

Hesperidin has been found to possess various health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and cardio-protective properties.

Research has also suggested its potential in improving blood lipid profiles and lowering blood pressure, making it a subject of interest in the field of nutritional science.

9. Naringenin

Citrus oto naringenin is a natural compound found in various citrus fruits, including lime, orange, and grapefruit.

It is a flavonoid, a type of plant compound that is known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Naringenin has been shown to have potential health benefits, including reducing cholesterol and blood sugar levels, and may also have anti-cancer and anti-inflammatory effects.

10. Naringin

Citrus oto naringin is a naturally occurring flavonoid glycoside compound found in its leaves.

It is widely used in traditional medicine due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Studies have also shown its potential in antioxidant, antihypertensive, and anticancer activities.

Naringin is obtained from Citrus oto leaves through a series of extraction and purification processes.
