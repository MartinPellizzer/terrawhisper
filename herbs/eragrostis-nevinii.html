What's the taxonomical classification of Eragrostis nevinii?

Eragrostis nevinii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the taxonomic order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its classification within the genus Eragrostis, resulting in its unique species designation, nevinii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eragrostis nevinii has an annual growth habit characterized by slender, creeping culms that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves with ligules that are often membranous and slightly lacerate. Its inflorescence is a compact, spike-like panicle composed of numerous small spikelets arranged tightly along the branches. Each spikelet contains a single floret protected by lemma and palea, which are often somewhat scabrous in texture.

The seeds are small, ovoid, and typically exhibit a slightly compressed shape.

        What are the herbal preparations of Eragrostis nevinii?

        Eragrostis nevinii is prepared as an infusion, decoction, or topical poultice for various traditional medicinal applications. The dried leaves and stems are often steeped in hot water to create a medicinal tea used for internal consumption. For skin irritations or wounds, the crushed plant material can be applied directly to the affected area as a moist poultice.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Eragrostis nevinii can cause skin irritation and allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon direct physical contact. The plant contains chemical compounds that may trigger redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. While there is limited clinical data on systemic toxicity, ingestion could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress in livestock or humans. Caution is advised when handling the foliage to prevent accidental dermatological reactions. Maintaining a safe distance from the plant helps mitigate these potential adverse effects.
