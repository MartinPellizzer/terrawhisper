Botanical Identification of Roundleaf Sundew (Drosera rotundifolia)

To identify Roundleaf Sundew (Drosera rotundifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Roundleaf Sundew, Drosera rotundifolia, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Sarraceniaceae, and it is part of the genus Drosera. The species name rotundifolia refers to its round, leaf-like structures that capture prey.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Roundleaf Sundew is Drosera rotundifolia, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It belongs to the Droseraceae family and is also known by various synonyms, common names, and regional names reflecting its widespread distribution and historical use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Roundleaf Sundew is a low-growing plant with a rosette of fleshy, round leaves. It has a short, fibrous root system and a short, erect stem. Its leaves are covered in sticky glands that trap insects, and it produces a single spike of small, white flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Roundleaf Sundew can be distinguished by its rosette of rounded, reddish leaves with dense glandular hairs. It lacks the spiny leaves of Drosera binata and has a more compact growth form. The leaves are typically smaller and more rounded compared to other sundews, and the plant produces small, white flowers in clusters.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Roundleaf Sundew is native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including Europe, Asia, and North America. It thrives in cool, temperate climates with high humidity. It prefers acidic, nutrient-poor soils such as peat or heathland. It typically grows at altitudes between 0 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Roundleaf Sundew blooms from June to August, producing pink to magenta flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, it is best harvested in early spring before flowering begins.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Roundleaf Sundew (Drosera rotundifolia) belongs to the Droseraceae family, closely related to other sundew species within the genus Drosera. It shares key traits like sticky, glandular leaves with digestive secretions, typical of its family. Its closest relatives include other Drosera species, such as D. intermedia and D. anglica, which also exhibit similar carnivorous adaptations.
