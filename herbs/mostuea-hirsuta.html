Mostuea hirsuta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mostuea hirsuta has woody, shrubby stems characterized by a dense covering of fine, spreading hairs that give the plant its hirsute appearance. The leaves are small, lanceolate to narrowly elliptical in shape, and possess serrated margins. These leaves are arranged alternately along the branches and are often quite stiff to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in the leaf axils. The overall structure is compact and adapted to the rugged, often wind-exposed environments where it grows.
