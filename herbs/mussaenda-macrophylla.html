Mussaenda macrophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Mussaenda macrophylla?

Mussaenda macrophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Its taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its placement in the genus Mussaenda, specifically identifying it as the species macrophylla.

    What are the herbal preparations of Mussaenda macrophylla?

    Mussaenda macrophylla is prepared as a decoction of the leaves, a poultice of the crushed foliage, or a topical wash derived from the plant material. These various herbal preparations are primarily utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions and inflammation. The leaves are often boiled in water to create a liquid infusion used for washing affected areas. Alternatively, the fresh leaves can be macerated into a paste and applied directly to wounds or swellings. This versatility in preparation allows the plant to address both internal and external ailments through different methods of administration.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Mussaenda macrophylla can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if ingested or handled improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, the plant contains various phytochemicals that may act as irritants to sensitive tissues. Contact with the leaves or colorful bracts might lead to contact dermatitis in some individuals. Ingestion of any part of the plant could potentially result in nausea or stomach discomfort. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid direct contact if you have known sensitivities to botanical substances.
