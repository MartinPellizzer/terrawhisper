Lithocarpus chrysocomus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lithocarpus chrysocomus?

Lithocarpus chrysocomus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, it falls into the order Fagales and the family Fagaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Lithocarpus, and its specific species name, chrysocomus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lithocarpus chrysocomus contains high concentrations of hydrolyzable tannins, flavonoids, and triterpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological activities. These polyphenolic compounds are known for providing potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects within biological systems. Specific triterpene acids found in the plant bark and leaves may exhibit antimicrobial properties against various pathogens. Research suggests that these chemical constituents can assist in regulating cellular oxidative stress. The complex mixture of secondary metabolites makes the plant a subject of interest for developing natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lithocarpus chrysocomus?

Lithocarpus chrysocomus has medicinal properties characterized by its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities derived from its concentrated polyphenolic compounds. The plant contains significant levels of tannins and flavonoids which help neutralize free radicals in the body. These bioactive components work together to reduce oxidative stress and suppress inflammatory responses in various tissues. Additionally, the extracts demonstrate an ability to inhibit the growth of certain pathogenic bacteria and fungi. This chemical profile makes the species a subject of interest for developing natural therapeutic agents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin conditions such as eczema, sores, and various types of dermatological inflammation. The leaves and bark are often prepared into medicinal decoctions or topical applications to soothe irritated tissues. These preparations contain bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and promote faster healing of minor wounds. Some traditional practitioners also utilize extracts to manage internal digestive issues and general malaise. Regular application of these plant derivatives provides a natural method for managing localized skin ailments.
