Lithocarpus chrysocomus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lithocarpus chrysocomus has evergreen leaves that are typically elliptical to oblong in shape, featuring coriaceous textures and distinctively yellowish-green surfaces. The leaf margins are often entire or slightly revolute, while the venation pattern remains prominent on the underside. Its woody stems develop into sturdy branches that support dense, spreading canopies characteristic of the Fagaceae family. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters, eventually giving rise to nut-like fruits encased in specialized woody cups. These cups are often covered in fine hairs or small scales that protect the developing seed.

      Lithocarpus chrysocomus is prepared as a decoction by boiling the dried leaves, bark, or acorns in water to extract its bioactive compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lithocarpus chrysocomus can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic responses if ingested or applied topically.
