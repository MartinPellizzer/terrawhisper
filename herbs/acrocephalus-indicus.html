Acrocephalus indicus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acrocephalus indicus has a slender, herbaceous stem that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The plant possesses an erect growth habit often found in moist or marshy environments. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a compact spike or spike-like cluster. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and exhibit a smooth, slightly shiny texture. Tiny, pale flowers emerge from the leaf axils or the terminal end of the stalk.
