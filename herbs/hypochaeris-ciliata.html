Hypochaeris ciliata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypochaeris ciliata has a perennial growth habit characterized by tufted, caespitose clumps of slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal, forming narrow, linear to lanceolate blades that are often quite thin. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters known as glomerules. These flowering heads are supported by delicate, branching structures that give the plant a fine, airy appearance. The seeds are tiny and smooth, facilitating dispersal within its natural habitat.
