Eurya tetragonoclada

What's the taxonomical classification of Eurya tetragonoclada?

Eurya tetragonoclada belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Ericales. This species is a part of the family Pentaphylacaceae, falling under the genus Eurya, and is specifically identified by its species name, tetragonoclada.

What are the herbal preparations of Eurya tetragonoclada?

Eurya tetragonoclada is prepared as a decoction of its dried leaves or as a topical paste made from crushed plant matter. Practitioners typically boil the harvested leaves in water to extract bioactive compounds for oral consumption. This liquid extract is often consumed in small doses to address various internal ailments. Additionally, the plant material can be ground into a fine consistency and applied directly to the skin as a poultice. Such topical applications are used to treat localized inflammation or skin irritations.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Eurya tetragonoclada can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if ingested or applied incorrectly. While the plant is used in traditional medicine, its chemical compounds may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Direct skin contact with the leaves or stems can result in redness, itching, or localized swelling. If consumed in uncontrolled amounts, it might lead to nausea or abdominal discomfort. Detailed clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, so caution is necessary when handling this species.
