Elsholtzia flava

What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia flava?

Elsholtzia flava belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Elsholtzia. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, flava.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia flava has pinnately compound leaves typically consisting of 7 to 15 leaflets that are ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins.

The tree reaches a medium height and develops a spreading crown with fine, feathery foliage. Its bark is smooth and gray when young, transitioning to a deeply furrowed texture as the specimen matures. Small, yellowish flowers emerge in clusters during the spring, eventually developing into winged samaras used for seed dispersal.

The overall structure provides a delicate, airy appearance to the woodland landscape.
