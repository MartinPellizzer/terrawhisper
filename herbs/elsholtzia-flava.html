Elsholtzia flava

What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia flava?

Elsholtzia flava belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Elsholtzia. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, flava.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Elsholtzia flava contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and various triterpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological potential. These secondary metabolites are primarily concentrated in the bark and leaves of the tree. Research suggests that the presence of these polyphenolic compounds may provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies also investigate the plant's extracts for potential antimicrobial and hepatoprotective activities. These chemical constituents serve as the basis for exploring the species in traditional and modern medicinal applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Elsholtzia flava?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive issues, skin inflammation, and various respiratory ailments. The bark and leaves contain medicinal properties that help alleviate stomach pains and indigestion. It is also frequently applied topically to reduce swelling and soothe irritated skin conditions. Some traditional practices utilize the plant to help manage symptoms of coughs and mild fevers. These therapeutic applications rely on the chemical compounds found within its botanical structures.
