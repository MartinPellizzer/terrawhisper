Elsholtzia flava

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia flava has pinnately compound leaves typically consisting of 7 to 15 leaflets that are ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins. The tree reaches a medium height and develops a spreading crown with fine, feathery foliage. Its bark is smooth and gray when young, transitioning to a deeply furrowed texture as the specimen matures. Small, yellowish flowers emerge in clusters during the spring, eventually developing into winged samaras used for seed dispersal. The overall structure provides a delicate, airy appearance to the woodland landscape.
