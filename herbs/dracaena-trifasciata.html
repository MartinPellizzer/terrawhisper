Dracaena trifasciata

What's the taxonomical classification of Dracaena trifasciata?

Dracaena trifasciata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Asparagales and the family Asparagaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Dracaena, and its specific species designation, trifasciata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracaena trifasciata has stiff, sword-shaped leaves that grow vertically from a central rosette and typically reach lengths of up to 3 feet. These leaves are characterized by a thick, succulent texture and a variegated pattern of dark green horizontal bands across a lighter green base. Some cultivars feature creamy white or pale yellow margins running along the edges of each leaf. The foliage is arranged in a dense, upright cluster that remains compact throughout its growth cycle. This plant lacks a traditional woody stem, instead emerging directly from a rhizomatous root system.
