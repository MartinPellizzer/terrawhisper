What's the taxonomical classification of Pueraria alopecuroides?

Pueraria alopecuroides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, it is categorized under the genus Pueraria, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species alopecuroides.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Pueraria alopecuroides can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin eruptions, and potential hormonal imbalances due to its phytoestrogenic properties. Individuals consuming this plant may experience nausea, stomach cramps, or diarrhea as the body reacts to its chemical constituents. Some users might develop localized itching or rashes if they have a sensitivity to the plant's alkaloids. Because it can mimic estrogen in the body, it may also interfere with regular endocrine function or medication efficacy. Careful monitoring is necessary to avoid these complications during use.
