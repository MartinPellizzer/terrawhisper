What's the taxonomical classification of Pueraria alopecuroides?

Pueraria alopecuroides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, it is categorized under the genus Pueraria, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species alopecuroides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pueraria alopecuroides has a climbing or trailing habit characterized by trifoliate leaves with leaflets that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and possess acute or obtuse apices. Its flowers are arranged in axillary racemes and display a characteristic pea-like structure typical of the Fabaceae family. The legume pods are relatively small and oblong, containing a limited number of seeds. Fine pubescence often covers the stems and leaf surfaces, distinguishing it from closely related species.
