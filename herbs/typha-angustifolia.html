Narrowleaf Cattail (Typha angustifolia): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Narrowleaf Cattail (Typha angustifolia) is a native wetland plant known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and diuretic effects, and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

What is Narrowleaf Cattail (Typha Angustifolia)?

Narrowleaf Cattail (Typha angustifolia) is a perennial wetland plant characterized by its narrow, sword-like leaves and a cylindrical flowering stem that produces fluffy, seed-bearing structures.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Narrowleaf Cattail is Typha angustifolia. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Poales, family Typhaceae, genus Typha, and species Typha angustifolia. Common names for this plant include Narrowleaf Cattail in North America and Common Cattail in Europe. It is also known as Narrowleaf Cattail in the United Kingdom and Typha angustifolia in botanical contexts. Synonyms for this plant include Typha angustifolia var. angustifolia and Typha angustifolia subsp. angustifolia.

Plant Description and Morphology

Narrowleaf Cattail (Typha angustifolia) is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically 20-40 cm long, and its cylindrical, brownish flower spike that contains a dense cluster of small, pale yellow flowers.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Native to temperate regions of North America, the Narrowleaf Cattail (Typha angustifolia) is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of freshwater lakes and streams, particularly in the eastern and central United States.

Pharmacological Properties

Narrowleaf Cattail (Typha angustifolia) exhibits pharmacological properties that include antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects, primarily due to the presence of flavonoids, tannins, and volatile oils in its rhizomes and leaves.
