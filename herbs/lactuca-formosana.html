Lactuca formosana

What's the taxonomical classification of Lactuca formosana?

Lactuca formosana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. It is further defined by its genus, Lactuca, and is specifically identified by its species name, formosanas.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lactuca formosana has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and features deeply lobed, pinnatifid leaves arranged in a basal rosette. The leaf margins are irregular and often display serrated edges, while the leaf texture can vary from smooth to slightly pubescent. The inflorescence is characterized by a terminal head of numerous small, yellow ligulate flowers that bloom in a clustered arrangement. These flower heads are supported by involucral bracts that surround the base of the developing seeds. The plant eventually produces small, cylindrical achenes that are dispersed by the wind.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It is commonly found growing in mountainous terrain where cooler temperatures and specific moisture levels are maintained. The distribution is often restricted to certain elevations that provide its required environmental conditions. Populations are typically scattered across various mountain ranges throughout the central part of the island. What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Lactuca formosana contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its leaves, stems, and the milky latex sap that exudes when the plant is damaged. The bitter components found in the leaves are often utilized to stimulate digestion and improve appetite. Many traditional practices involve harvesting the stems to extract the lactucarium, a substance known for its sedative and analgesic properties. This milky sap is frequently applied or ingested to help alleviate pain and promote relaxation. Additionally, the entire aerial portion of the herb can be decocted into a tea to address various inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lactuca formosana?

Lactuca formosana has therapeutic properties that function as a bitter tonic, a sedative, and a nervine to support the nervous system.
