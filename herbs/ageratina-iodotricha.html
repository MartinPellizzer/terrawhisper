Ageratina Iodotricha

Ageratina iodotricha, commonly known as the iodotricha bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

Traditionally, it has been used in folk medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and terpenoids, which may contribute to its medicinal effects. In recent studies, extracts from Ageratina iodotricha have shown promise in treating skin conditions and reducing pain associated with inflammation.

Due to its rich phytochemical profile, it is being explored as a potential source of natural remedies for various health issues.
