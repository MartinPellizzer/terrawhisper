Panicum notatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Panicum notatum has an upright growth habit characterized by tufted, perennial culms that typically reach heights between 30 and 100 centimeters. The leaves are linear and narrow, featuring a prominent midrib and a slightly scabrous texture along the margins. Its inflorescence consists of a contracted, spike-like panicle that appears dense and somewhat cylindrical. Each spikelet is small and contains several florets protected by delicate glumes. The plant often develops a fibrous root system that helps stabilize its clumped form in moist environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Panicum notatum can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and respiratory discomfort if handled or inhaled improperly. It is generally recommended to wear protective clothing when clearing or managing these grasses to minimize direct exposure.
