Mariscus cyperinus

What's the taxonomical classification of Mariscus cyperinus?

Mariscus cyperinus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Poales. This plant is a member of the family Cyperaceae, which contains the genus Mariscus, and is specifically identified as the species cyperinus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mariscus cyperinus has a central woody stem that supports lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a cluster of small, actinomorphic flowers. The leaves exhibit a distinct venation pattern that extends from the midrib to the leaf edges. Each flower is characterized by five fused petals and a prominent stamen arrangement. The root system is fibrous and shallowly distributed within the topsoil. Small, spherical fruits develop from the ovary following successful pollination.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Its presence is most documented along the coastlines of Madagascar and various island territories in the Indian Ocean.
