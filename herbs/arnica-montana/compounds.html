Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana) Compounds

The primary compounds in Mountain Arnica include terpenoids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant properties. Tannins and saponins provide astringent and detoxifying actions, while essential oils offer antiseptic benefits. Phenolic acids and glycosides support wound healing, and coumarin has mild anticoagulant properties.

Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana) Monograph

1. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Specific terpenoids like helenalin and flavonoids are responsible for its therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine. These compounds are often extracted for use in topical treatments.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana) that contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. They help reduce swelling and pain, making them key components in the plant's traditional use for treating bruises and muscle aches.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Tannins help reduce swelling and promote healing in topical applications. They also support the plant's traditional use in treating bruises and skin irritations.

4. Essential oil

The essential oil of Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana) contains bioactive compounds like sesquiterpene alcohols and flavonoids. It is used topically for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is a key component in many herbal preparations for muscle pain and skin conditions.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. This compound helps reduce pain and swelling, making it valuable in herbal treatments for musculoskeletal conditions.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Glycosides in Arnica are responsible for its traditional use in treating bruises and muscle pain. They work by stimulating blood circulation and reducing tissue swelling.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Saponins help reduce swelling and pain, making them valuable in treating conditions like bruises and muscle aches. They are often used in topical preparations for their therapeutic effects.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Coumarin may also aid in blood circulation and has been studied for its potential role in reducing swelling and pain. Its presence supports the traditional use of Arnica in treating musculoskeletal issues.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. While not the primary active constituents, they contribute to the plant's therapeutic effects, particularly in reducing pain and inflammation. These alkaloids are often found alongside sesquiterpene lactones, which are the main bioactive components of the plant.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Mountain Arnica (Arnica montana). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in treating bruises and inflammatory conditions.
