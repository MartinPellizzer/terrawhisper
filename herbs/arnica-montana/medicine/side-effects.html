10 Possible Side Effects Of Arnica Montana (Mountain Arnica)

Arnica montana has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, nausea, and vomiting.

These side effects can be caused by ingesting too much of the herb, applying it to broken skin, or using it undiluted.

If left unaddressed, these side effects can worsen, leading to digestive problems, skin rashes, and even allergic reactions that may impact daily activities and work performance.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Arnica montana if used imporperly.

When applied topically, these compounds can stimulate the skin's natural defense mechanisms, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation.

Additionally, Arnica montana's oil can also disrupt the skin's natural barrier function, allowing irritants to penetrate more easily and triggering a response from the immune system.
