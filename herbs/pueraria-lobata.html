Kudzu (Pueraria lobata)

Kudzu (Pueraria lobata) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to China, Japan, and Korea. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface dull green and lower surface lighter green, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescences are racemose clusters of white zygomorphic flowers with five petals, one of which is prominently lobed and adorned with a violet-colored blotch.
Stems
Woody, cylindrical, and somewhat hollow, the stems are often covered with a dense layer of pubescence, and exhibit a climbing growth habit with tendrils arising from the nodes.
