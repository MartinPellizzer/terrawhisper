Adenocaulon himalaicum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenocauloon himalaicum has a perennial growth habit characterized by a woody base, hairy stems, and small, ovate, serrated leaves that are densely covered in fine white pubescence. The foliage often forms a basal rosette, with leaves displaying a slightly thickened texture and a distinctively felt-like surface. Its inflorescence consists of small, capitate flower heads arranged in terminal clusters, typically featuring yellowish or white florets. These flower heads are surrounded by involucral bracts that are also heavily coated in trichomes to protect the reproductive structures. The overall morphology is highly adapted to alpine environments, utilizing dense hair to retain moisture and resist temperature fluctuations.
