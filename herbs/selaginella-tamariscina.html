Selaginella tamariscina

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella tamariscina?

Selaginella tamarisciina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae, following the taxonomic path through the order Selaginellales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Selaginellaceae and falls under the genus Selaginella, specifically identified by the species epithet tamarisciina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella tamariscina has a creeping rhizomatous habit with small, scale-like microphylls that are arranged in four ranks along slender, branched stems. These diminutive leaves are often imbricated, meaning they overlap closely like shingles on a roof. The plant frequently develops a bushy appearance due to the dense, spreading nature of its many fine branches. During periods of desiccation, the stems can curl inward to protect the delicate tissues from water loss. This morphological adaptation allows the species to survive in environments with fluctuating moisture levels.

What are the pharmacological activities of Selaginella tamariscina?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-cancer, anti-diabetic, anti-oxidant, anti-tumor, and anti-proliferative properties. Its high concentration of bioactive compounds allows it to effectively neutralize free radicals through its potent anti-oxidant mechanisms. Research indicates that these chemical constituents can inhibit the growth of malignant cells by exerting strong anti-proliferative and anti-tumor effects. Furthermore, the extract demonstrates significant potential in managing blood glucose levels due to its anti-diabetic characteristics. These combined therapeutic actions highlight its importance in modern drug discovery and medicinal studies.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Selaginella tamariscina extract (STE) and its major compound, amentoflavone, exhibit potent inhibitory activity against human aldo-keto reductase family 1B10 (AKR1B10). This detoxification enzyme is involved in drug resistance, and the extract was found to synergistically increase the anti-proliferative effect of doxorubicin in A549 and NCI-H460 human lung cancer cells that express high levels of AKR1B10 mRNA and protein. Furthermore, STE demonstrated the ability to significantly inhibit A549 tumor growth in a nude mouse xenograft model. These findings suggest that STE could serve as a potential anti-cancer agent and a candidate adjuvant agent to boost the efficacy of doxorubicin cancer chemotherapy.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a variety of bioactive medicinal compounds including selaginellins, selaginellin derivatives, selaphenin A, selaphenin B, and selaphenin C. These specialized metabolites are often classified as lycopodium alkaloids which contribute to the plant's unique chemical profile. Researchers have focused on these specific substances to understand their potential pharmacological activities in biological systems. The structural diversity found among the selaphenin variants provides a basis for studying different therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents represent the primary interest for scientists investigating the medicinal value of Selaginella tamariscina.

According to a study published by "Bioorganic & medicinal chemistry letters", twelve polyphenol compounds were isolated from the plant Selaginella tamariscina. These compounds include three novel phenols named selaphinins A-C (1-3), two known selaginelliin derivatives (4 and 5), and seven known biflavonoids (6-12). Notably, polyphenol compound 10 suppressed the migration of A549 cells by primarily targeting MMP-9. The antitumor activity of compound 10 was possibly due to the induction of cell apoptosis through intrinsic apoptosis pathways. This process was accompanied by increasing the expression of Bax and caspase-3 in a dose-dependent manner.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating UVB-induced epidermal photoaging, general skin photoaging, skin elasticity loss, skin hydration loss, and oxidative stress. The bioactive compounds within Selaginella tamaryssinia help protect the skin from the damaging effects of ultraviolet radiation. By neutralizing free radicals, the extract effectively mitigates oxidative stress that leads to cellular damage. Furthermore, its properties assist in maintaining moisture levels to prevent skin hydration loss. These combined benefits work to restore skin elasticity and combat the visible signs of aging.

According to a study published by "Free radical biology & medicine", the principal biflavonoid of the resurrection plant Selaginella tamarisciina, amentoflavone (AF), is used for anti-photoaging dermotherapy. Using doses of 0.1-0.2 mg g-1, AF was shown to restore skin hydration and elasticity while reducing melanin deposition. The plant's component also reversed UVB-induced epidermal hyperplasia and collagen/elastic-fiber disorganisation. Furthermore, AF suppressed the UVB-induced upregulation of senescence-associated markers p16, p21, and GLB1. These findings are supported by a high-affinity AF-PAR2 interaction with a LibDockScore of 125.596.
