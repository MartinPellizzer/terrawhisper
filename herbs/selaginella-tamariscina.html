Selaginella tamariscina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella tamariscina has a creeping rhizomatous habit with small, scale-like microphylls that are arranged in four ranks along slender, branched stems. These diminutive leaves are often imbricated, meaning they overlap closely like shingles on a roof. The plant frequently develops a bushy appearance due to the dense, spreading nature of its many fine branches. During periods of desiccation, the stems can curl inward to protect the delicate tissues from water loss. This morphological adaptation allows the species to survive in environments with fluctuating moisture levels.

What are the therapeutic actions of Selaginella tamariscina?

Selaginella tamarisciina has hemostatic, astringent, antipyretic, and diaporetic properties used to manage bleeding, skin irritation, fever, and perspiration.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Selaginella tamariscina can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, redness, or itching if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience respiratory irritation if they inhale fine spores or dust released from the plant's fronds. While systemic toxicity is not extensively documented in humans, ingesting any part of the plant could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress or nausea. It is important to exercise caution when handling the species to avoid these localized physical reactions. Always consult a medical professional if any adverse symptoms occur after exposure.
