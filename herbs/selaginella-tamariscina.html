Selaginella tamariscina

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella tamariscina?

Selaginella tamarisciina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae, following the taxonomic path through the order Selaginellales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Selaginellaceae and falls under the genus Selaginella, specifically identified by the species epithet tamarisciina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella tamariscina has a creeping rhizomatous habit with small, scale-like microphylls that are arranged in four ranks along slender, branched stems. These diminutive leaves are often imbricated, meaning they overlap closely like shingles on a roof. The plant frequently develops a bushy appearance due to the dense, spreading nature of its many fine branches. During periods of desiccation, the stems can curl inward to protect the delicate tissues from water loss. This morphological adaptation allows the species to survive in environments with fluctuating moisture levels.

What are the pharmacological activities of Selaginella tamariscina?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-cancer, anti-tumor, anti-proliferative, anti-diabetic, and antioxidant properties. Its ability to inhibit cell growth makes it a significant subject for anti-proliferative and anti-tumor research. The presence of bioactive compounds also provides potent antioxidant effects that help mitigate oxidative stress in cells. Additionally, studies suggest its potential in managing blood glucose levels through anti-diabetic mechanisms. These multifaceted therapeutic effects highlight its importance in modern drug discovery and natural medicine.

As discussed in a study, Selaginella tamariscina extract (STE) and its major compound, amentoflavone, possess potent inhibitory activity against human aldo-keto reductase family 1B10 (AKR1B10). These components synergistically increase the anti-proliferative effect of doxorubicin in A549 and NCI-H460 human lung cancer cells that express high levels of AKR1B10 mRNA and protein. Furthermore, STE has been shown to significantly inhibit A549 tumor growth in a nude mouse xenograft model. Due to these effects, STE and amentoflavone are considered potential anti-cancer agents that target AKR1B10. They may also serve as candidate adjuvant agents to boost the anti-cancer effect of doxorubicin.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains selaginellin derivatives, selaginellins, selaphenin A, selaphenin B, and selaphenin C. These specific medicinal compounds are unique chemical constituents found within the plant's biological structure. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential therapeutic applications in pharmacology. The varying chemical properties of selaphenin A, B, and C allow for diverse biological interactions. Consequently, these selaginellin derivatives represent a significant area of interest for natural product chemistry.

As discussed in a study, twelve polyphenol compounds were isolated from the plant Selaginella tamariscina. These include three novel phenols named selaphinins A-C (1-3), two known selaginelliin derivatives (4 and 5), and seven known biflavonoids (6-12). Notably, polyphenol compound 10 suppressed the migration of A549 cells by primarily targeting MMP-9. The antitumor activity of compound 10 was possibly due to the induction of cell apoptosis through intrinsic apoptosis pathways. This process was accompanied by increasing the expression of Bax and caspase-3 in a dose-dependent manner.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating cancer, human promyelocytic leukemia, and advanced cancer by inhibiting tumor cell growth. Research indicates that specific compounds within Selaginella tamarisciina possess potent anti-tumor properties. These biological activities are particularly effective at targeting the rapid multiplication of leukemia cells. Scientists study these extracts to understand how they can halt the progression of various malignant growths. By disrupting the mechanisms of tumor cell growth, the plant offers potential therapeutic pathways for managing complex oncological conditions.

As discussed in a study, Selaginella tamariscina is a traditional medicinal plant used for the treatment of advanced cancer in the Orient. The anticancer effect of Selaginella tamariscina was investigated by analyzing its potential to induce apoptosis in human HL-60 leukemia cells. This plant was found to be cytotoxic to HL-60 cells in a dose-dependent manner. Selaginella tamariscina caused DNA fragmentation and nuclear condensation, which are characteristics of apoptosis. The induction of apoptosis by Selaginella tamariscina is accompanied by the activation of caspase-3 and the specific proteolytic cleavage of PARP. Additionally, Selaginella tamariscina treatments led to an increase in proapoptotic Bax levels and a decrease in Bcl-2 expression.
