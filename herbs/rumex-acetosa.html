What to know about Rumex acetosa (sorrel) before using it medicinally

Sorrel, scientifically known as Rumex acetosa, is a leafy green herb that has been touted for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to improve digestion, reduce inflammation, and boost immune function.

In the culinary world, sorrel is often used as an ingredient in soups, salads, and sauces, where it adds a tangy, slightly sour flavor. Horticulturally, sorrel is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners. Botanically speaking, sorrel belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its pointed, heart-shaped leaves and slender stems.

With ancient references dating back to medieval European cooking and even being mentioned in classical Greek literature, sorrel has a rich history as a flavorful and versatile herb.
