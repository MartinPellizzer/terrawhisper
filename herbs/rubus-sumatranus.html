What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus sumatranus?

Rubus sumatranus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Rosales, it is a member of the family Rosaceae, which places it in the genus Rubus. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species designation, sumatranus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus sumatranus has woody, arching stems that are densely covered in sharp, recurved prickles and feature pinnate leaves with multiple serrated leaflets. The foliage typically consists of dark green, ovate leaflets that possess distinctively textured surfaces and prominent venation. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the axils of the branches, characterized by numerous delicate petals and central stamens. These blossoms eventually give way to aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep red or black color when fully ripe. The overall architecture of the plant allows it to form thick, scrambling thickets within its natural habitat.
