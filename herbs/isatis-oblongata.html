Isatis oblongata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Isatis oblongata has lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a somewhat fleshy texture and a distinct green coloration. Its stems are relatively slender and upright, supporting small, clustered flowers that emerge from the axils. These flowers consist of tiny petals that contribute to a compact inflorescence. The overall growth habit remains low to the ground, reflecting its adapted morphological structure.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes Mountains, specifically occurring within parts of Peru and Bolivia. It thrives in specialized alpine environments known as puna ecosystems. These habitats are typically characterized by rocky slopes and montane grasslands located at significant elevations. The species is restricted to these specific montane zones rather than being widely distributed across lower tropical lowlands. Consequently, its range is geographically fragmented by the complex topography of the mountain range.
