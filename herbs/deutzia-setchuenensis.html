Deutzia setchuenensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Deutzia setchuenensis?

Deutzia setchuenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Cornales and the family Hydrangeaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Deutzia, and its specific species epithet, setchuenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Deutzia setchuenensis has opposite, elliptical to ovate leaves that are typically three to seven centimeters long with finely serrated margins and a slightly pubescent texture.

The shrubby plant produces clusters of five-petaled, white flowers that emerge from terminal panicles during the blooming season. Its stems are woody and can exhibit a somewhat spreading growth habit as the plant matures. The reproductive structures include small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Overall, the foliage and floral arrangement characterize this species as a delicate deciduous shrub.
