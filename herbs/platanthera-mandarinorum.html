Platanthera mandarinorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Platanthera mandarinorum has an erect, slender stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and supports a terminal spike of numerous small, white, fragrant flowers. The leaves are lanceolate to narrowly elliptic, arranged in a basal rosette or alternately along the lower portion of the stem. Each flower features a long, nectar-producing spur that extends backward from the base of the lip. The labellum is relatively small and often possesses subtle lobes or teeth. These orchids are characterized by their delicate structure and a reproductive morphology adapted for specific insect pollinators.
