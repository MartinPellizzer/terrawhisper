Cyperus diffusus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyperus diffusus?

Cyperus diffusus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchy of its biological classification, it falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Cyperaceae. Ultimately, this plant is defined by its specific genus, Cyperus, and its unique species designation, diffusus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus diffusus has a creeping rhizomatous growth habit characterized by triangular stems that reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are linear, grass-like, and arranged in basal clusters or along the stem. Inflorescences consist of small, brownish spikelets arranged in umbel-like clusters at the top of the stem. These spikelets are densely packed and lack distinct floral parts visible to the naked eye. The entire plant exhibits the characteristic three-sided stem structure common to the Cyperaceae family.
