Rheum rhabarbarum

What's the taxonomical classification of Rheum rhabarbarum?

Rheum rhabarbaruum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Polygonaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Rheum and is specifically designated as the species rhabarbarum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Rheum rhabarbaruum contains anthraquinone glycosides such as emodin, rhein, and aloe-emodin which primarily function as stimulant laxatives. These specific chemical compounds work by increasing intestinal motility and stimulating the secretion of water into the bowel. In addition to these laxative properties, the plant possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory qualities attributed to its diverse phenolic compounds. Some studies also suggest that these medicinal elements may exhibit antimicrobial effects against certain pathogens. The concentration of these active constituents can vary significantly depending on the specific part of the plant harvested and its growing conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Rheum rhabarbarum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat constipation, digestive irregularities, and various gastrointestinal disorders through its potent laxative properties. The presence of anthraquinone glycosides in the roots stimulates intestinal contractions to promote regular bowel movements. It is also frequently employed to alleviate symptoms of indigestion and bloating by aiding the digestive process. Some traditional applications include managing mild liver ailments due to its potential choleretic effects. However, its use must be carefully monitored to avoid electrolyte imbalances or dependency.
