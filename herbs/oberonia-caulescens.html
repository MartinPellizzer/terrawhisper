Oberonia caulescens

What's the taxonomical classification of Oberonia caulescens?

Oberonia caulescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Asparagales. It is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling specifically under the genus Oberonia, with its unique identity defined by the species name caulescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oberonia caulescens has a miniature, epiphytic growth habit characterized by small, distichous leaves arranged along a slender, creeping stem. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, elongated raceme that emerges from the apex of the stem to bear numerous tiny, delicate flowers. These individual flowers exhibit complex structures typical of the genus, including small, fleshy petals and a specialized lip.
