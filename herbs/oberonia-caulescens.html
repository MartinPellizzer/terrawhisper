Oberonia caulescens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oberonia caulescens has a miniature, epiphytic growth habit characterized by small, distichous leaves arranged along a slender, creeping stem. The leaves are typically lanceolate to linear in shape and possess a thin, somewhat translucent texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, elongated raceme that emerges from the apex of the stem to bear numerous tiny, delicate flowers. These individual flowers exhibit complex structures typical of the genus, including small, fleshy petals and a specialized lip. The overall morphology is adapted for life in humid, shaded environments where it clings to host surfaces.
