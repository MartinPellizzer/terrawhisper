What's the taxonomical classification of Abroma augustum?

Abroma augustum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression through the order Malvales, it is placed within the family Malvaceae. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Abroma, and its unique species designation, augustum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Abroma augustum?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including potent antioxidant and hepatoprotective properties. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds effectively neutralize free radicals to prevent cellular oxidative damage. These antioxidant capabilities contribute directly to its ability to safeguard liver cells from toxic insults. By mitigating oxidative stress, the plant provides a protective mechanism that maintains hepatic function and prevents injury. Such multifaceted biological actions highlight its potential utility in medicinal applications related to liver health.

According to a study published by "Antioxidants (Basel, Switzerland)", Abroma augustum (L.) L. f. contains 50 distinct phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenes, phenolics, and coumarins. The extract demonstrated a total phenolic content of 12.32 ± 0.01 mg/g and a total flavonoid content of 42.14 ± 3.5 mg/g. Antioxidant activity was measured through DPPH, FRAP, and ABTS with IC50 values of 214.007 µg/mL, 132.307 µg/mL, and 45.455 µg/mL, respectively. The methanolic extract also exhibited significant hepatoprotective properties by reducing lipid peroxidation and decreasing concentrations of liver damage biomarkers such as ALT, AST, and LDH. These effects were observed in both HepG2 cells and goat liver homogenate.
