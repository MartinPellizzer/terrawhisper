Selaginella vaginata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella vaginaata has a creeping rhizome that produces small, dichotomously branched stems with microphylls arranged in four rows along the axis. The plant typically grows in dense, low-lying mats where the scale-like leaves are densely overlapping. These small leaves are characterized by their narrow, lanceolate shape and often feature a distinct central vein. The overall structure allows the plant to spread efficiently across moist substrates in its natural habitat. Such morphological adaptations are essential for its survival in shaded, humid environments.
