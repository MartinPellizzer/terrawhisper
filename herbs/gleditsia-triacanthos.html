Gleditsia triacanthos belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Gleditsia, and its specific species designation, triacanthos.

What are the pharmacological activities of Gleditsia triacanthos?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its notable cytotoxic activity against various cancer cell lines. Research indicates that bioactive compounds extracted from its tissues can inhibit the proliferation of malignant cells. These chemical constituents often act by disrupting cellular processes necessary for tumor growth and survival. Beyond its anti-tumor properties, the plant is studied for other therapeutic effects related to its secondary metabolites. Such investigations aim to identify specific molecules that could serve as leads for developing new medicinal drugs.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", methanolic extracts of Gleditsia triacanthoos L. fruits yielded two mono-carbonyl curcumin analogues, (1E,4E)-1,5-bis(4-hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)penta-1,4-dien-3-one [1] and (1E,4E)-1,5-bis(4-hydroxyphenyl)penta-1,4-dien-3-one [2]. These extracts also contained beta-sitosterol [3], apigenin [4], quercetin-3-O-methyl ether [5], rutin [6], quercetin [7], naringenin [8], eriodictyol [9], vitexin [10], isovitexin [11], and gleditsin A [12]. The curcumin analogues [1] and [2] were assayed in 2 hepatic cancer cells, specifically HepG-2 and Huh-7. Compound [1] demonstrated high cytotoxic activity against HepG-2 cells with an IC50 value of 14.39 µM. This activity was compared with a standard that had an IC50 value of 12.44 µM.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compound galactomannan which serves as its primary bioactive component. This specific polysaccharide is frequently studied for its potential applications in regulating blood glucose levels and improving digestive health. Researchers have also identified various secondary metabolites within the species that may contribute to anti-inflammatory properties. These complex carbohydrates work by interacting with biological pathways to support systemic wellness. The unique chemical profile of the plant makes it a subject of interest for pharmaceutical development.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", extracts of the cotyledons of germinated honey locust (Gleditsia triacanthos) seeds contain galactomanan as a reserve polysaccharide in the endosperm. The study examined fractions for a specific manno-6-kinase, but no evidence for such a kinase was obtained. Instead, research identified one particulate hexokinase as the major hexose-6-kinase fraction and two soluble hexokinase fractions as the minor portion. Additionally, a soluble fructo-6-kinase fraction was identified, which was further separated into 3 fractions. Kinetic studies on 1 of these fractions indicated specific structural requirements for the monosaccharide substrate regarding the beta-D-anomeric 2-OH, a 4-OH, and a -CH2OH substituent on C2.
