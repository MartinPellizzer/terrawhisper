Gleditsia triacanthos

What's the taxonomical classification of Gleditsia triacanthos?

Gleditsia triacanthos belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Fabales, it falls under the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Gleditsia, and its specific species designation, triacanthos.

What are the pharmacological activities of Gleditsia triacanthos?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including potent cytotoxic activity, antioxidant properties, and anti-inflammatory potential. Researchers have identified specific bioactive compounds within its extracts that can inhibit the growth of various cancer cell lines. These phytochemicals work by inducing apoptosis or interfering with the cell cycle of malignant cells. Additionally, the plant shows promise in reducing oxidative stress through its high concentration of polyphenols. Such multifaceted biological actions make it a subject of intense interest for drug discovery and natural product development.

As discussed in a study, methanolic extracts of Gleditsia triacanthos L. fruits yielded two mono-carbonyl curcumin analogues, (1E,4E)-1,5-bis(4-hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)penta-1,4-dien-3-one [1] and (1E,4E)-1,5-bis(4-hydroxyphenyl)penta-1,4-dien-3-one [2]. These extracts also contained beta-sitosterol [3], apigenin [4], quercetin-3-O-methyl ether [5], rutin [6], quercetin [7], naringenin [8], eriodictyol [9], vitexin [10], isovitexin [11], and gleditsin A [12]. The curcumin analogues [1] and [2] were assayed in two hepatic cancer cells, HepG-2 and Huh-7. Compound [1] demonstrated cytotoxic activity against HepG-2 with an IC50 value of 14.39 µM. This activity was compared against a standard with an IC50 of 12.44 μM.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compound galactomannan, which functions as a powerful immunomodulator and prebiotic. This specific polysaccharide helps regulate the immune system by stimulating the production of antibodies. Additionally, it supports digestive health by promoting the growth of beneficial gut bacteria. Researchers also study its potential to lower cholesterol levels and improve overall metabolic function. These diverse chemical properties make the plant a significant subject in pharmacological studies.

As discussed in a study, the cotyledons of germinated honey locust (Gleditsia triacanthos) seeds contain galactomannan as a reserve polysaccharide in the endosperm. These extracts contain one particulate hexokinase, which serves as the major hexose-6-kinase fraction. Additionally, the seeds possess two soluble hexokinase fractions that represent the minor portion. A soluble fructo-6-kinase fraction is also present and was separated into three distinct fractions. Kinetic studies on one of these fractions identified specific structural requirements for the monosaccharide substrate involving a beta-D-anomeric 2-OH and a 4-OH trans to the D-5-CH2OH.
