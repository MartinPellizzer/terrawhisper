10 Possible Side Effects Of Aristolochia Macrophylla (Dutchman'S Pipe)

Aristolochia macrophylla has some side effects when used improperly, such as kidney damage, interstitial nephritis, and renal failure.

These side effects are caused by the presence of aristolochic acids, toxic compounds that can be released when the herb is not properly processed or consumed in large quantities. If left untreated, these conditions can worsen, leading to anemia, bone fractures, and even kidney transplantation.

In extreme cases, they can even be life-threatening.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Aristolochia macrophylla if used imporperly.

1. Triggers seizures in rare cases

Aristolochia macrophylla causes kidney failure due to its high concentration of aristolochic acid.

This toxic compound is absorbed into the bloodstream and damages the renal tubules, leading to irreversible kidney damage and potentially life-threatening complications.

The exact mechanism by which it occurs is not fully understood, but it is believed that aristolochic acid's nephrotoxic properties are responsible for the destruction of kidney cells and tissue.

Prolonged exposure to aristolochic acid has been linked to the development of urothelial cell carcinoma, a type of bladder cancer.

Aristolochia macrophylla leads to renal damage due to its toxic compounds, specifically aristolochic acid.

This acid is metabolized in the kidneys, causing oxidative stress and inflammation that can lead to tubular necrosis and interstitial fibrosis, ultimately resulting in chronic kidney disease or even acute kidney failure.

The severity of the damage depends on the dose, duration, and individual susceptibility to the toxin.

This leads to an accumulation of waste products in the bloodstream, causing symptoms such as proteinuria, edema, and fatigue.

The plant's toxic compounds, including aristolochic acid, damage the kidney tubules, disrupting normal filtration functions and leading to nephrotic syndrome, a potentially life-threatening condition characterized by severe impairment of kidney function.
