Epilobium amurense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Epiloium amurense has perennial rhizomatous stems with opposite, lanceolate to oblong leaves that feature serrated margins and entire bases. The foliage is typically characterized by a bright green color and a texture that can range from smooth to slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of small, four-petaled flowers that are usually pink or white in color. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and are often supported by slender pedicels. The plant also produces elongated seed capsules that split open to release numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
