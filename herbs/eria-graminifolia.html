Eria graminifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eria graminifolia has linear, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that grow in dense, terminal racemes. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated foliage which lacks the broad surface area seen in many other orchid species. Its stems are relatively thin and support a compact arrangement of leaves that emerge from the base. The inflorescence consists of multiple small blossoms clustered at the tip of the stem to attract specific pollinators. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in its specific ecological niche within forest environments.
