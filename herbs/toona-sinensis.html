Toona sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Toona sinensis?

Toona sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Sapindales, the plant is a member of the family Meliaceae. Its specific taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement in the genus Toona, with its unique designation being the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Toona sinensis has large, pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of 7 to 15 leaflets arranged along a central rachis. Each leaflet is elliptical or lanceolate in shape with an entire margin and a pointed apex. The tree develops a straight, cylindrical trunk covered in greyish-brown bark that often features longitudinal fissures as the specimen matures. Small, fragrant flowers emerge in clusters, eventually giving way to woody capsules that split open to reveal winged seeds. These seeds are designed for wind dispersal, a common characteristic among members of the Meliaceae family.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains limonene, caryophyllene, flavonoids, naringenin, and quercetin, which provide various therapeutic benefits. The terpene limonene is known for its antioxidant properties, while caryophyllene acts as a potent anti-inflammatory agent. Within the flavonoid group, naringenin offers significant neuroprotective and cardiovascular support. Additionally, quercetin serves as a powerful bioactive compound that helps stabilize immune responses. Together, these diverse chemical constituents contribute to the overall medicinal profile of the plant.

As discussed in a study, the essential oil from Toona sinensis leaves contains a high amount of sesquiterpenes at 84.64%. This composition includes 8.27% copaene, 10.16% β-caryophyllene, 13.18% caryophyllene, and 5.06% β-eudesmene. The essential oil demonstrated antimicrobial activity against 20 clinically isolated strains of Staphylococcus aureus, including MSSA and MRSA, with lowest MIC values of 0.125 and 1mg/ml. These medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, antidoting, and worm-killing effects used for treating enteritis, dysentery, carbuncles, boils, and abdominal tumors.
