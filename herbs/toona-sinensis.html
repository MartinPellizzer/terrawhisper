Toona sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Toona sinensis?

Toona sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Sapindales, the plant is a member of the family Meliaceae. Its specific taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement in the genus Toona, with its unique designation being the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Toona sinensis has large, pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of 7 to 15 leaflets arranged along a central rachis. Each leaflet is elliptical or lanceolate in shape with an entire margin and a pointed apex. The tree develops a straight, cylindrical trunk covered in greyish-brown bark that often features longitudinal fissures as the specimen matures. Small, fragrant flowers emerge in clusters, eventually giving way to woody capsules that split open to reveal winged seeds. These seeds are designed for wind dispersal, a common characteristic among members of the Meliaceae family.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains limonene, caryophyllene, flavonoids, naringenin, and quercetin, which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. The terpene limonene provides antioxidant properties, while caryophyllene acts as a potent anti-inflammatory agent. Within the flavonoid group, the specific molecules naringenin and quercetin offer significant protection against oxidative stress. These various phytochemicals work synergistically to support overall cellular health and immune function. Together, these substances contribute to the complex therapeutic profile of the plant.

As discussed in a study, the essential oil from Toona sinensis leaves contains a high amount of sesquiterpenes at 84.64%. This composition includes 8.27% copaene, 10.16% β-caryophyllene, 13.18% caryophyllene, and 5.06% β-eudesmene. The essential oil demonstrated antibacterial activity against twenty clinically isolated strains of Staphylococcus aureus, including two standard strains, ATCCC 25923 and ATCCC 43300. It inhibited the growth of both MSSA and MRSA with the lowest MIC values of 0.125 and 1mg/ml, respectively. Furthermore, treatment for 24h suppressed the viability of three different cancer cell lines with a significance of P<0.05.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating dysentery, diabetes, bacterial infection, inflammation, and carbuncles. Its medicinal properties are often utilized to manage blood sugar levels in patients with diabetes. The plant also serves as a potent agent to combat various forms of bacterial infection. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects help reduce swelling and pain throughout the body. It is also frequently applied topically to soothe and heal painful carbuncles on the skin.

As discussed in a study, Toona sinensis has long been known for its medicinal efficacy for treating enteritis, dysentery, itch and some cancers.
