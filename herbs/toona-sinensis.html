Toona sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Toona sinensis?

Toona sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Sapindales, the plant is a member of the family Meliaceae. Its specific taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement in the genus Toona, with its unique designation being the species sinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Toona sinensis contains high concentrations of bioactive limonoids, specifically gedunin, which exhibits potent antimalarial, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer properties. These complex triterpenoids are primarily concentrated in the bark and leaves of the tree. Research indicates that these compounds can inhibit the growth of various pathogen strains and modulate immune responses. Beyond limonoids, the plant also possesses essential oils and tannins that contribute to its antimicrobial efficacy. These diverse chemical constituents make the species a significant subject in pharmacological studies focused on natural drug development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Toona sinensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea, dysentery, and stomach pain, as well as skin conditions like sores and inflammation. Its medicinal properties are often utilized to alleviate various types of digestive distress through its astringent qualities. In traditional medicine, the bark and leaves are frequently prepared as decoctions to manage internal ailments. Beyond digestion, it serves as a topical remedy for wound healing and reducing swelling. These diverse applications make it a significant resource in regional herbal practices.
