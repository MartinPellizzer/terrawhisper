Common Eyebright (Euphrasia Pectinata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Euphrasia pectinata.

Common eyebright, scientifically known as Euphrasia pectinata, is a medicinal herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its historical use in traditional medicine. This small flowering plant is valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and antimicrobial properties, making it particularly beneficial for eye health and respiratory conditions. Traditionally, it has been used to treat ailments such as conjunctivitis, cataracts, and respiratory infections, with herbalists often incorporating it into eye washes and tinctures. In modern wellness practices, Common eyebright is used in natural remedies for allergies, skin irritations, and as a supportive supplement for immune function. Its unique feature is the presence of flavonoids and tannins, which contribute to its potent healing properties, and it has a long history of being used in European herbal medicine for over a thousand years.
