Arundinella hirta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arundinella hirta has a woody, perennial habit characterized by erect stems that are densely covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are lanceolate in shape with entire margins and prominent longitudinal venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in compact clusters along the axils of the upper leaves. The plant typically produces small, fleshy fruits that facilitate seed dispersal. These morphological traits distinguish it from closely related species within its genus.
