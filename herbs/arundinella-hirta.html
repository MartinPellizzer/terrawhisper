What's the taxonomical classification of Arundinella hirta?

Arundinella hirta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down through its hierarchical structure, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Poales, it is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific biological identity as a member of the genus Arundinella, with its unique designation being the species hirta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arundinella hirta has a woody, perennial habit characterized by erect stems that are densely covered in fine, spreading hairs.

The leaves are lanceolate in shape with entire margins and prominent longitudinal venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in compact clusters along the axils of the upper leaves. The plant typically produces small, fleshy fruits that facilitate seed dispersal.

These morphological traits distinguish it from closely related species within its genus.
