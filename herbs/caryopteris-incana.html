What's the taxonomical classification of Caryopteris incana?

Caryopteris incana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, falling under the genus Caryopteris, with its specific designation being the species incana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caryopteris incana has an upright, deciduous shrubby growth habit characterized by woody stems and oppositely arranged, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length. The foliage is typically grayish-green due to a fine layer of pubescence on both the upper and lower surfaces. During the flowering season, the plant produces dense, terminal panicles of small, tubular, blue to violet flowers. These blooms emerge from axillary positions along the stems and are highly attractive to pollinators. The overall texture of the plant is soft and slightly fuzzy because of the trichomes covering the leaves and stems.

As discussed in a study, two new compounds named caryocanolide (1) and caryocanoside A (2) were isolated from the whole plant of Caryopteris incana along with nine known compounds (3-11). The structures of these compounds were determined using 2D nuclear magnetic resonance and high resolution fast atom bombardment mass spectroscopy data. The isolated compounds (1, 2, 4-11) were specifically tested for their antibacterial activity. Compound 1 exhibited moderate antimicrobial activity against Bacillus subtilis. This activity was recorded with a minimum inhibitory concentration value of 35.7 μM.

As discussed in a study, the whole plant of Caryopteris incana (Thunb.) Miq. contains one new phenylpropanoid glycoside called incanoside. This plant also contains 4 known phenylpropanoid glycosides, which are verbascoside, isoverbascoside, phlinoside A, and 6-O-caffeoyl-beta-D-glucose. The structure of incanoside was determined to be 1-O-(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)ethyl-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl (1-->2)-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl(1-->3)-6-O-caffeoyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside. These 4 phenylpropanoid glycosides exhibited potent radical scavenging activity against DPPH, hydroxyl (.OH), and superoxide anion (O2-.).
