Kola Nut (Cola acuminata)

Kola Nut (Cola acuminata) is a member of the Sterculiaceae family, native to West Africa, Central Africa, and Southern Africa. Traditionally, its leaves, seeds, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its stimulant, bitter, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves

Flowers
Calyx with 5 sepals, 5 petals arranged in a spiral, zygomorphic symmetry, flowers in clusters of 2 to 3, pinkish-white color with a yellowish throat.
Stems
Cylindrical, woody, branched, with smooth, pale brown surface, and nodes bearing adventitious root scars.
