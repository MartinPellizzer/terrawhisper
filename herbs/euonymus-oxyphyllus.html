Euonymus oxyphyllus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euonymus oxyphyllus has an erect, deciduous shrub habit characterized by slender, gray-brown stems and opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves that measure 3 to 8 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are typically entire or finely serrated, with a texture that ranges from smooth to slightly leathery. Small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring months. Following pollination, the plant produces distinctive four-lobed capsules that split open to reveal bright orange or red arils surrounding the seeds. These colorful structures serve as a primary identifying feature during the late summer and autumn seasons.
