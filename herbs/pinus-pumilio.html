Medicinal Parts of pinus pumilio

Pinus pumilio has many medicinal parts, such as its resin, needles, and bark, which are used to treat various ailments.

The resin of Pinus pumilio is rich in terpenes and diterpenes, including pinosane and pinobanksin, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The needles of the plant contain flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, including taxifolin and pinostrobin, which exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

The bark of Pinus pumilio is rich in phenolic compounds, including vanillin and syringic acid, which have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

Possible Side Effects of pinus pumilio

Pinus pumilio can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, nausea, and headaches in some individuals.

In rare cases, excessive consumption of Pinus pumilio may lead to gastrointestinal issues, including diarrhea and stomach pain.

Prolonged use or high doses of the herb may also interact with medications, such as blood thinners, and exacerbate conditions like high blood pressure.

Additionally, improper preparation or contamination of Pinus pumilio may cause respiratory issues or skin irritation in sensitive individuals.
