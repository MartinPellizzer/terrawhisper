Heather

Calluna vulgaris

Heather (Calluna vulgaris) is a low-growing shrub native to the northern hemisphere, commonly found in heathlands and acidic soils, known for its small, bell-shaped flowers and its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Morphology

Heather (Calluna vulgaris) is a low-growing evergreen shrub with dense, compact clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Distribution

Heather (Calluna vulgaris) is native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, with a widespread distribution across temperate regions, particularly in heathlands, moors, and mountainous areas.

Traditional Uses

Heather has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its soothing effects on respiratory conditions, as a diuretic to support kidney function, and to promote skin health through its antiseptic and astringent properties.

Medicinal Systems

Heather (Calluna vulgaris) has been utilized in traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and Native American healing practices for its purported anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and diuretic properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Heather (Calluna vulgaris) involves the presence of flavonoids, phenolic acids, tannins, and volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties and traditional use in herbal remedies.

Pharmacology

Heather (Calluna vulgaris) possesses antimicrobial, astringent, and mild diuretic properties, making it useful in treating respiratory infections, skin conditions, and urinary tract inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Heather, or Calluna vulgaris, is a flowering plant in the Ericaceae family. Common names include heath and cross-leaved heath, though regional variations exist. It is a perennial shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant has small, needle-like leaves and pink or white flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include "heather" and "common heath."

Safety Profile

Heather (Calluna vulgaris) is generally considered safe when used in moderation. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
