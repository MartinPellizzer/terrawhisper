Calluna Vulgaris: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Calluna vulgaris, commonly known as heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North America, known for its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.

This herb is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and antioxidant effects.

Health benefits include supporting respiratory health, reducing inflammation, and promoting skin healing.

The therapeutic actions of Calluna vulgaris include antimicrobial, antispasmodic, and mild diuretic effects, while its bioactive constituents include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins.
