Heather (Calluna vulgaris)

Heather (Calluna vulgaris) is a member of the Ericaceae family, native to Europe, and Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters.
Leaves
Lateral, needle-like leaves with pale green upper surfaces and darker green lower surfaces, exhibiting stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence composed of dense spikes of small, urn-shaped flowers with five lobes, typically pink to red in color, arranged in a compact, branched cluster, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry with no distinct markings.
Stems
Erect, woody, evergreen growth habit with a simple branching pattern, covered in short, glandular hairs, and featuring four-sided, angular stems with persistent, scaly leaf bases.
