Rosa × hibernica f. wilsonii

Rosa × hibernica f. wilsonii, also known as Wilson's Irish rose, is a specific form of the hybrid species Rosa × hibernica, a cross between Rosa centifolia and Rosa gallica.

This plant is a type of flowering shrub that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine due to its medicinal properties, particularly its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

The health benefits of Rosa × hibernica f. wilsonii include its ability to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and skin irritations.

In herbal preparations, the plant's flowers and leaves can be used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are often used to treat respiratory issues and skin conditions.
