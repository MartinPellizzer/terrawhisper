Michelia × alba

What's the taxonomical classification of Michelia × alba?

Michelia × alba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Magnoliales. The plant is a member of the family Magnoliaceae, which contains the genus Michelia, and is identified specifically by the species alba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Michelia × alba has evergreen, woody leaves that are elliptical or oblong in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with entire margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The foliage typically grows in an alternate arrangement along the stems, providing a dense canopy for the plant. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the large, fragrant, creamy white flowers that emerge from terminal or axillary buds. These blossoms possess multiple layers of waxy petals and numerous stamens clustered around a central gynoecium. The plant's overall structure is that of a medium-sized shrub or small tree with a spreading habit.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is a natural hybrid primarily found in the subtropical and tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically within parts of China, India, and Myanmar. Because it is a hybrid resulting from the cross between Magnolia grandiflora and Magnolia alba, its distribution is often linked to areas where these parent species overlap. It thrives in moist, warm climates that provide consistent humidity and well-drained soil. Many populations are located in coastal or low-altitude forest environments.
