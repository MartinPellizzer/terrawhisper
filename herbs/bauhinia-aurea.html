Bauhinia aurea

What's the taxonomical classification of Bauhinia aurea?

Bauhinia aurea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, falling into the genus Bauhinia, and is specifically identified by its species name, aurea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (+)-catechin, (-)-epicatechin, (-)-epicatechin 3-O-gallate, gallic acid, and lupeol. These specific polyphenols and triterpenoids are recognized for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of various flavan-3-ols like catechin and epicatechin contributes to the plant's ability to mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Furthermore, the inclusion of gallic acid and lupeol provides additional bioactive potential for cellular protection. Together, these chemical constituents form a complex profile that supports various therapeutic applications.

As discussed in a study, eight compounds were isolated from the ethyl acetate soluble fraction of the ethanolic extract of Bauhinia aurea. These medicinal compounds include isoengeletin (1), astilbin (2), neoastilbin (3), isoastilbin (4), neoisoastilbin (5), (+)-catechin (6), (-)-epicatechin (7) and (-)-epicatechin 3-O-gallate (8). Out of these eight isolated substances, five compounds were isolated from this genus for the first time. This total includes the new isolation of compounds 1, 3, 4, 5 and 7, while compounds 2, 6 and 8 had been previously identified. The structures of these purified compounds were determined using MS and NMR spectroscopic methods following chromatography over silica gel, reversed-phase RP-18, and Sephadex LH-20.
