What is Gomphrena paniculata?

Gomphrena paniculata is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae, native to tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America.

It is an annual herb, typically growing to a height of 30-100 cm, with a spreading or erect habit and simple, alternate leaves that are 5-10 cm long and 2-4 cm wide. The plant produces small, greenish-white or pinkish flowers in a terminal inflorescence, with 3-5 mm long, ovate calyx lobes and 5-7 mm long, tubular corolla. The fruit is a small, dry, and one-seeded capsule, approximately 3-5 mm in length.

The plant is often found in sandy or rocky areas, along roadsides and trails, and in disturbed areas.

The main therapeutic properties of Gomphrena paniculata are anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities.
