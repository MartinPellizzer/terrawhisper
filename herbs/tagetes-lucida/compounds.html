Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) Compounds

Black Sage contains essential oil as its primary volatile compound, contributing to its aromatic properties. Flavonoids and terpenoids provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin and phenolic acids offer antimicrobial benefits, while tannins and glycosides support digestive health. Saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins contribute to its diverse therapeutic properties.

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) contains bioactive compounds like camphor and thujone. These compounds contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is often used in aromatherapy and topical applications for its therapeutic benefits. It is a key component in the plant's traditional medicinal use.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in the leaves and are often used in herbal preparations for their therapeutic benefits. Flavonoids may enhance the plant's traditional uses in herbal medicine.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Black Sage (Tagetes lucida). They contribute to its aromatic profile and may have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Black Sage (Tagetes lucida). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin may support respiratory and digestive health, aligning with traditional uses of Black Sage in herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Black Sage (Tagetes lucida). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and skin conditions. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Black Sage.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Black Sage (Tagetes lucida). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antimicrobial effects, which enhance the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Black Sage (Tagetes lucida). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting respiratory health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in Black Sage are often responsible for its expectorant and antimicrobial effects.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Black Sage (Tagetes lucida). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins help support digestive health and may enhance the immune system. Their presence makes Black Sage a valuable herb in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Black Sage (Tagetes lucida). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific effects and mechanisms are not as well-studied as other compounds in the plant. Alkaloids are known for their potential therapeutic and pharmacological activities in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Black Sage (Tagetes lucida). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting overall health and potentially reducing inflammation. Anthocyanin may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.
