Botanical Identification of Black Sage (Tagetes lucida)

To identify Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Black Sage, or Tagetes lucida, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Tagetes, and species Tagetes lucida. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the daisy family, which includes many aromatic herbs used in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Black Sage is Tagetes lucida, a member of the Asteraceae family. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. Common names include Black Sage, Mexican tarragon, and Spanish tarragon. It is also known as Hierba de San Juan in some regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Black Sage, or Tagetes lucida, is an upright perennial with a fibrous root system. Its stems are woody at the base and hairy, growing up to 2-3 feet tall. Leaves are lance-shaped, gray-green with silver undersides, and have a strong sage scent. The plant produces yellow-orange flower clusters, and its fruits are small, dry capsules containing numerous seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Black Sage, or Tagetes lucida, can be distinguished by its aromatic, sage-like leaves with a grayish underside, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive red-pink center, and a strong, pungent scent. It is often confused with other members of the sunflower family, but its unique leaf texture and fragrance help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Black Sage, or Tagetes lucida, is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at low to moderate elevations, typically below 300 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Black Sage (Tagetes lucida) blooms from late spring to early fall, typically between May and September. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to October. For identification purposes, the most accurate harvest timing is during the flowering period, when the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Black Sage, or Tagetes lucida, belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae). It is closely related to other species within the Tagetes genus, such as marigolds. Its phylogenetic relationships highlight shared traits like composite flower heads and aromatic foliage, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.
