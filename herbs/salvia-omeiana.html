What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia omeiana?

Salvia omeiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which falls under the genus Salvia, and is specifically identified by its species name, omeiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia omeiana has an upright, perennial growth habit characterized by square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically rugose and covered in fine pubescence, providing a textured appearance to the plant. During its flowering period, it produces elongated terminal racemes packed with bilabiate flowers that transition from pale blue to deep purple. These floral structures are supported by small bracts and emerge from a robust root system capable of anchoring the plant in rocky terrain. The overall morphology reflects its adaptation to montane environments through its dense, protective hairiness.
