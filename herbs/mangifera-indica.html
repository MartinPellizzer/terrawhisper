Mango (Mangifera indica)

Mango (Mangifera indica) is a member of the Anacardiaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, South Asia, and Indo-Malay Region. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with upper surface dark green and lower surface pale green, exhibiting prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, with five white petals and five yellow sepals, arranged in a solitary inflorescence with a terminal flower cluster, exhibiting a distinct floral tube and staminodes.
Stems
Elongated, herbaceous, erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth surface, and presence of stipular remnants.
