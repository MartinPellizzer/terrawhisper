Mango

Mangifera indica

Mango (Mangifera indica) is a fruit-bearing tree native to South and Southeast Asia, belonging to the Anacardiaceae family. Traditionally, various parts of the mango plant have been used to address digestive issues, provide relief from cough and cold symptoms, and aid in the healing of skin wounds. The fruit, leaves, and bark are commonly utilized in traditional medicine practices across different regions.

Botanical Identification

Mango (Mangifera indica) is a tropical fruit tree belonging to the Anacardiaceae family. It is native to South Asia and has numerous regional names like "aloo" in Hindi and "mango" in English. The plant is a perennial with large, broad leaves and fragrant flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include "kodumbu" in Malayalam and "amla" in some contexts.

Active Compounds

Mango contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Safety Profile

Mango (Mangifera indica) is generally safe when consumed in normal food amounts. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated for those with mango allergy or latex-fruit syndrome. Always consult a healthcare provider before using mango for medicinal purposes.
