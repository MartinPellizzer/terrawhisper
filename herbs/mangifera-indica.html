Mangifera indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Mangifera indica?

Mangifera indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Sapindales and is a member of the family Anacardiaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Mangifera, with its specific designation being the species indica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Mangifera indica contains a diverse array of bioactive compounds including mangiferin, polyphenols, flavonoids, tannins, and various terpenoids. These phytochemicals are primarily concentrated in the leaves, bark, and fruit pulp of the plant. Mangiferin, a xanthone derivative, is particularly noted for its significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research suggests these chemical constituents may offer protective effects against oxidative stress and certain microbial infections. Additionally, the presence of organic acids and vitamins contributes to the plant's overall therapeutic potential in traditional medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Mangifera indica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, diarrhea, inflammation, diabetes, and various respiratory ailments. The leaves and bark contain bioactive compounds like polyphenols and flavonoids that possess strong antimicrobial properties. These medicinal components help reduce intestinal inflammation and manage blood glucose levels in diabetic patients. Additionally, topical applications of the plant extracts can soothe skin irritations and combat bacterial growth. Traditional medicine also utilizes its extracts to alleviate coughs and asthma symptoms.
