Hypericum japonicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Hypericum japonicum?

Hypericum japonicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Malpighiales, it is categorized under the family Hypericaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Hypericum and the species name japonicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypericum japonicum has creeping, woody rhizomes that produce stems reaching up to 30 centimeters in height with small, opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The foliage is characterized by a glossy texture and prominent glandular dots that appear dark when held against the light. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five distinct petals and numerous protruding stamens. These blossoms typically emerge from the leaf axils and form in small clusters. The plant's overall structure remains low-growing, allowing it to spread effectively as a groundcover.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

What are the therapeutic actions of Hypericum japonicum?

Hypericum japonicum has astringent, vulnerary, hemostatic, antiseptic, and diaphoretic therapeutic actions used to treat skin wounds and systemic ailments. Its astringent properties allow it to contract tissues, which helps to tighten skin and reduce inflammation. As a potent vulnerary, the plant effectively promotes the healing of damaged skin and various external lesions. It also functions as a hemostatic agent by helping to stop bleeding from minor cuts or abrasions. Furthermore, its antiseptic qualities prevent infection in open wounds, while its diaphoretic action helps induce sweating to manage fevers.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains flavonoids, hyperjaponicols A-D, japopyrones A, japopyrones B, and various xanthone derivatives. These specific medicinal compounds are often studied for their diverse biological activities and potential therapeutic applications. The hyperjaponicols and japopyrones serve as key chemical markers within the plant's complex secondary metabolite profile. Furthermore, the presence of xanthone derivatives and flavonoids contributes to the overall chemical complexity of the species. Researchers analyze these molecules to better understand how they interact with human biological systems.

As discussed in a study, Hypericum japonicum contains 9 undescribed compounds, including 5 xanthone derivatives, 2 flavonoids, 1 2-pyrone derivative, and 1 undescribed naturally occurring compound. The plant also contains 30 known phenolic compounds and includes hyperjaponols A and B identified as racemates. Regarding cytotoxic effects, 18 compounds showed good inhibitory activities against the HEL cell line with IC50 values of 3.53-18.7 μM. Additionally, 9 compounds exhibited moderate cytotoxicity against the MDA-MB-231 cancer cell line with IC50 values ranging from 4.92 to 10.75 μM.
