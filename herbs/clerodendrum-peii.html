Clerodendrum peii

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum peii?

Clerodendrum peii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and falls under the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Clerodendrum, and its unique species designation, peii.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Clerodendrum peii targets various enzymes and signaling proteins, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and certain proinflammatory cytokines to mediate its anti-inflammatory actions. Its bioactive phytochemicals, including flavonoids and terpenoids, interact with these proteins to disrupt the arachidonic acid cascade. By modulating these enzymatic pathways, the plant effectively reduces the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes. These molecular interactions are also linked to its potential antioxidant properties through the regulation of oxidative stress-related proteins. Such targeted biochemical mechanisms provide the underlying basis for its traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions.

      What are the herbal preparations of Clerodendrum peii?

      Clerodendrum peii is prepared as a decoction, a tea, or a topical poultice depending on the specific ailment being treated.
