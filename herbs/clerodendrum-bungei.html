Clerodendrum bungei

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum bungei?

Clerodendrum bungei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Lamiaceae and falls within the genus Clerodendrum, specifically identified by the species name bungei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum bungei has woody, erect stems characterized by square-shaped cross-sections and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and provides a dense texture to the shrub's overall structure. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils in clusters, often displaying contrasting colors that attract pollinators. These inflorescences eventually develop into fleshy, globose berries that change color as they mature. The plant's architecture is defined by its branching habit and the distinct arrangement of its leaf venation.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 4'-O-methylscutelarein, beta-sitosterol, myristicin, taraxerol, and trichotomoside. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse therapeutic potential observed in the species. For instance, the presence of sterols like beta-sitosterol and taraxerol often supports anti-inflammatory processes. Additionally, the alkaloid myristicin and the flavonoid 4'-O-methylscutelarein may offer antioxidant benefits to the body. The complex synergy between trichotomoside and these other elements defines the plant's chemical profile.

As discussed in a study, ten compounds were isolated from the 95% aqueous EtOH extract of Clerodendrum bungei using various chromatographic techniques. The identified structures include 11,12,16S-trihydroxy-7-oxo-17(15→16),18(4→3)-diabeo-abieta-3,8,11,13-tetraen-18-oic acid (1) and 12S,13R-dihydroxy-9-oxo-octadeca-10(E)-enoic acid (2), which are new natural products. Additionally, compounds 3, 5, and 6, which are clerodenoside A (3), glycosmisic acid (5), and 4'-O-methylscutellarein (6), were isolated from this plant for the first time. The extraction also yielded trichotomoside (4), neroplomacrol (7), butylitaconic acid (8), hexylitaconic acid (9), and p-hydroxybenzonic acid (10). Among these, compounds 7, 8, 9, and 10 were obtained from the genus Clerodendrum for the first time.
