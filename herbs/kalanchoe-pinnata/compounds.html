Kalanchoe (Kalanchoe pinnata) Compounds

Kalanchoe pinnata contains several key active compounds, including flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Saponins and tannins provide astringent effects, while glycosides and coumarin may support anti-inflammatory actions. Alkaloids and essential oils add to its diverse therapeutic potential. Anthocyanins contribute to antioxidant activity.

Kalanchoe (Kalanchoe pinnata) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Kalanchoe pinnata are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and skin conditions. They help reduce oxidative stress and support tissue repair, making them valuable in herbal medicine.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds found in Kalanchoe pinnata. These compounds contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and wound-healing effects. They are often responsible for the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and infections.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Kalanchoe pinnata. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This compound is often extracted for use in traditional herbal remedies. Its presence supports the plant's role in treating skin conditions and infections.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Kalanchoe pinnata. It exhibits anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and infections. Saponins also support immune function and may help in managing skin conditions. Their presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Kalanchoe pinnata in herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Kalanchoe pinnata, known for its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and skin conditions. Tannins in Kalanchoe help reduce inflammation and promote healing by binding to proteins and reducing tissue swelling.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Kalanchoe pinnata, known for its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and infections. Glycosides in Kalanchoe help reduce inflammation and promote tissue regeneration, making it a valuable component in herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Kalanchoe pinnata. It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and inflammatory conditions. Coumarin's presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine for its therapeutic effects.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Kalanchoe pinnata. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and infections. Alkaloids in Kalanchoe have shown antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Kalanchoe pinnata contains bioactive compounds like sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids. These compounds exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They are commonly used in traditional medicine for wound healing and treating skin infections. The essential oil is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and flowers.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Kalanchoe pinnata, known for its antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and may support skin health and wound healing. This compound is often studied for its potential therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.
