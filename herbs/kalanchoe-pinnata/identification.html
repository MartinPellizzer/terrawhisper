Botanical Identification of Kalanchoe (Kalanchoe pinnata)

To identify Kalanchoe (Kalanchoe pinnata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Kalanchoe pinnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Crassulaceae, genus Kalanchoe, and species Kalanchoe pinnata. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the family known for its succulent characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Kalanchoe pinnata is the scientific name of the plant, first described by the botanist K.S. Willemse in 1925. It is also known by several synonyms, including Kalanchoe daigremontiana and Kalanchoe pinnata var. daigremontiana. Common names include "Mother of Millions" and "Live Forever," while regional names vary across cultures. Historically, it has been referred to by names reflecting its medicinal uses in various traditional systems.

Morphological Characteristics

Kalanchoe pinnata is a succulent shrub with a branching growth habit. It has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions. The stem is upright and somewhat woody, while the leaves are fleshy, oppositely arranged, and have a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a raceme of clustered flowers, typically pink or red, and the fruit is a capsule containing numerous small seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Kalanchoe pinnata can be distinguished by its rosette-shaped leaves with wavy margins and translucent veins. It produces clusters of pink or red flowers on tall stems. The leaves have a unique ability to regenerate from their edges, a trait not commonly found in other Kalanchoe species. This combination of physical characteristics aids in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Kalanchoe pinnata is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly Madagascar. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It commonly grows at low altitudes, typically below 1,000 meters, in open, sunny environments.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Kalanchoe pinnata blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on regional climate. Seed formation occurs after flowering, usually in late summer to early fall. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during the flowering stage to ensure accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Kalanchoe pinnata is closely related to species within the Kalanchoe genus. It shares common traits with other members of the Crassulaceae family, such as succulent leaves and thick stems. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other rosette-forming plants in the family. These relationships help in understanding its botanical classification and medicinal uses.
