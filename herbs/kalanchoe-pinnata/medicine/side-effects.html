10 Possible Side Effects Of Kalanchoe Pinnata (Paddle Plant)

Kalanchoe pinnata has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, vomiting, and headaches.

These side effects can occur if the herb is ingested in large quantities or taken in combination with other medications.

In severe cases, prolonged use can lead to liver and kidney damage, exacerbating underlying health conditions and significantly impacting daily life.

When the sap comes into contact with the skin, it can cause redness, itching, and swelling, while oral consumption may lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

This is because the latex in Kalanchoe pinnata's sap contains saponins, which are naturally occurring compounds that can elicit an allergic response in some individuals.
