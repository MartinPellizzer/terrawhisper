Galinsoga parviflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Galinsoga parviflora has an erect or ascending herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a soft, slightly hairy texture and a bright green coloration. Small, white flower heads are arranged in terminal clusters, each consisting of multiple tiny florets. These inconspicuous blooms possess yellow centers composed of disc florets surrounded by white ray florets. The plant also produces small, achene-type fruits that facilitate its rapid dispersal in disturbed soils.

Galinsoga parviflora is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the specific medicinal application required. What side-effects this plant can have?

Galinsoga parviflora can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested in significant quantities. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or a rash upon direct contact with the plant's sap or foliage. When consumed, the plant may lead to stomach aches or nausea due to its chemical composition. There is also a lack of extensive clinical data regarding its long-term effects on human health or potential toxicity levels. Because of these unknown risks, caution is advised when handling or using this species for medicinal purposes.
