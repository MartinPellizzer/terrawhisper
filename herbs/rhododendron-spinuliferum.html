Rhododendron spinuliferum

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron spinuliferum?

Rhododendron spinuliferum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ericales. As a member of the family Ericaceae, it falls under the genus Rhododendron, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species spinuliferum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron spinuliferum has small, coriaceous leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess distinctive prickles or spinules along their margins. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and may feature a lighter underside. The plant exhibits woody stems that support clusters of flowers, which vary in color depending on the specific subspecies. These inflorescences are characterized by their funnel-shaped corollas and prominent stamens. The overall structure of the shrub is compact, reflecting its adaptation to specific montane environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of southwest China, specifically within the mountainous provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan. It thrives primarily in temperate montane environments where misty conditions and well-drained soils are prevalent. These populations are often found nestled in subalpine shrublands or along the edges of dense coniferous forests.
