White Willow (Salix alba) Compounds

The primary compounds in White Willow include tannins, which have astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties, while coumarin has anticoagulant effects. Glycosides, such as salicin, are responsible for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory actions.

White Willow (Salix alba) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in White Willow (Salix alba). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has historical use in reducing inflammation and soothing digestive issues. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal qualities by binding to proteins and other molecules.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in White Willow (Salix alba). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating pain and inflammation. These compounds are part of the broader therapeutic profile of White Willow in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in White Willow (Salix alba). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, similar to aspirin. This compound is responsible for some of the therapeutic effects associated with willow bark in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in White Willow (Salix alba). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in reducing inflammation and pain. Coumarin has anticoagulant properties and may support cardiovascular health. Its presence in White Willow highlights the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

The glycoside salicin is a key active compound in White Willow (Salix alba). It is responsible for the plant's analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, similar to aspirin. Salicin is converted in the body to salicylic acid, which reduces pain and fever. This compound has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its therapeutic properties.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in White Willow (Salix alba). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and mild analgesic properties. Saponins may support the plant's traditional use in treating minor ailments like headaches and joint pain. They also enhance the overall efficacy of White Willow in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in White Willow (Salix alba). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties and are involved in its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving effects. These compounds are part of the complex chemical profile that supports the traditional use of White Willow in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of White Willow (Salix alba) contains volatile compounds like limonene and pinene, which contribute to its aromatic profile. While not the primary medicinal component, the essential oil may support topical applications and complement the plant's anti-inflammatory properties.

9. Alkaloid

White Willow (Salix alba) contains the alkaloid salicin, which is chemically similar to aspirin. Salicin is known for its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties, making it a key component in traditional herbal medicine for treating conditions like arthritis and headaches.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in White Willow (Salix alba). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support anti-inflammatory effects. While not the primary medicinal component, anthocyanin enhances the overall therapeutic potential of White Willow.
