Common Dandelion (Taraxacum Latilobum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Taraxacum latilobum.

Common dandelion, scientifically known as Taraxacum latilobum, is a widely recognized medicinal herb known for its adaptogenic properties and historical use in traditional medicine. It is valued for its ability to support liver function, aid digestion, and promote detoxification due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds such as taraxacin and inulin. Traditionally, it has been used in European and Asian cultures to treat ailments like digestive issues, skin conditions, and as a natural diuretic. In modern wellness practices, it is often consumed as a tea or supplement to boost energy, enhance nutrient absorption, and support overall metabolic health. Its unique features include a distinctive bitter taste, a long history of use dating back to ancient times, and the presence of a rare active compound called sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.
