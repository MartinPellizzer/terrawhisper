1. Causes allergic reactions

Achillea ageratum causes allergic reactions due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids in its essential oil.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, and redness. In some cases, more severe reactions may occur, including hives, swelling, and difficulty breathing.

The exact mechanisms behind these allergic reactions are not fully understood, but it is thought that the body's immune system mistakes the plant's chemical compounds for a threat, leading to an overactive response.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Achillea ageratum triggers asthma attacks due to its potent allergenic properties.

The plant's tiny flowers and leaves contain a high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones, which can cause an allergic reaction in sensitive individuals, leading to bronchospasms and airway constriction.

This can lead to sudden and severe symptoms of asthma, including wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

3. Induces gastrointestinal bleeding

Achillea ageratum induces gastrointestinal bleeding due to its ability to thin the blood and increase its fluidity.

This property, known as anticoagulant activity, can lead to increased risk of bleeding in the digestive tract, potentially causing symptoms such as black or tarry stools, abdominal pain, and nausea.

The saponins present in Achillea ageratum are responsible for this effect, which may be exacerbated by concomitant use of blood thinners or other anticoagulant medications.

4. Increases blood pressure

Achillea ageratum increases blood pressure due to its stimulatory effect on the cardiovascular system.

The herb contains flavonoids and phenolic acids that can cause vasoconstriction, leading to increased blood pressure. Additionally, Achillea ageratum's beta-sitosterol content can stimulate the production of aldosterone, a hormone that regulates electrolyte balance in the body and can contribute to hypertension.

These physiological responses may result in elevated blood pressure levels when consuming Achillea ageratum.

5. Lowers blood sugar

Achillea ageratum lowers blood sugar levels due to its ability to stimulate insulin production and enhance glucose uptake in the body.

Its active compounds, such as coumarins and flavonoids, have been shown to improve insulin sensitivity, increasing the efficiency of glucose metabolism.

This may be beneficial for individuals with type 2 diabetes, but further research is needed to fully understand its effects on blood sugar regulation.

6. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Achillea ageratum disrupts menstrual cycles due to its ability to stimulate uterine contractions and affect hormone regulation.

The plant's flavonoids and volatile oils may cause an imbalance in estrogen levels, leading to irregular or absent periods.

Additionally, Achillea ageratum contains coumarins, which have been shown to increase the risk of miscarriage and premature labor when used during pregnancy.

7. Stimulates uterine contractions

Achillea ageratum stimulates uterine contractions due to its ability to increase prostaglandins and oxytocin in the body.

These hormonal changes can lead to increased muscle tone in the uterus, which may cause contractions.

This action is attributed to the plant's bioactive compounds, including flavonoids, terpenes, and sesquiterpenes, which have been shown to stimulate uterine smooth muscle cells.

8. Causes kidney damage

Achillea ageratum causes kidney damage due to its high concentration of coumarin and other pyranocoumarins.

These compounds can accumulate in the kidneys and disrupt their normal functioning, leading to nephrotoxicity.

As a result, long-term use or high doses of Achillea ageratum may cause scarring and fibrosis, ultimately impairing kidney function and potentially leading to chronic kidney disease.

9. Inflames skin lesions

Achillea ageratum inflames skin lesions due to its potential allergenic and irritating properties.

The plant's essential oils and other compounds may trigger an allergic reaction or irritation in some individuals, leading to inflammation of existing skin lesions.

This can cause redness, swelling, itching, and discomfort, making it important for users to be aware of this possible adverse effect when using Achillea ageratum.

10. Irritates mucous membranes

Achillea ageratum irritates mucous membranes.

This is due to its ability to stimulate the release of histamine from mast cells, leading to increased mucus production and inflammation in the respiratory tract.

The plant's volatile oils, particularly sesquiterpenes and phenolic compounds, are thought to be responsible for this effect, causing discomfort and potentially exacerbating conditions such as allergies or asthma.
