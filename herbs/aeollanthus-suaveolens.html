Aeollanthus suaveolens

What's the taxonomical classification of Aeollanthus suaveolens?

Aeollanthus suaveolens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Lamiales and belongs to the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Aeollanthus, and its species designation, suaveolens.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aeollanthus suaveolens contains a diverse profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's significant pharmacological potential in traditional medicine. Specifically, the presence of phenolic compounds provides potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies also suggest that its alkaloid content may exhibit antimicrobial and analgesic activities against various pathogens. Researchers continue to investigate these specific compounds to isolate pure substances for modern pharmaceutical development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aeollanthus suaveolens?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including malaria, intestinal worms, skin infections, and various inflammatory conditions. It contains bioactive compounds that exhibit significant antimicrobial and antimalarial properties. Traditional healers often prepare decoctions from the leaves to combat gastrointestinal parasites and stomach upsets. The plant also shows promise in managing wound healing and reducing swelling due to its medicinal phytochemicals. Its diverse chemical profile allows it to address both bacterial and parasitic infections in several cultures.
