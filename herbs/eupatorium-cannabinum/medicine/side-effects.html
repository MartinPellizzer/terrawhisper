10 Possible Side Effects Of Eupatorium Cannabinum

Eupatorium cannabinum has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, skin irritation, and allergic reactions.

Causes include excessive dosage, individual sensitivities, and interactions with other medications. Examples of how these side effects can worsen people's lives include debilitating stomach pain, prolonged recovery from treatment, and compromised immune systems.

In severe cases, these side effects can be life-threatening.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Eupatorium cannabinum causes allergic reactions due to its chemical composition and structure.

The plant contains sesquiterpene lactones and phenolic acids, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin rash, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the presence of allergenic proteins may also contribute to the development of allergies in susceptible people.

2. Induces respiratory distress

Eupatorium cannabinum induces respiratory distress due to its ability to stimulate the mucous membranes in the lungs.

This can lead to an increase in mucus production, causing wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

The plant's high concentration of flavonoids and sesquiterpenes may contribute to this effect by activating the body's natural defense mechanisms, resulting in increased bronchial secretions and airway constriction.

3. Triggers cardiac arrhythmia

Eupatorium cannabinum triggers cardiac arrhythmia due to its ability to interact with the heart's electrical activity.

Its bioactive compounds, particularly lignans and flavonoids, may affect the potassium channels in the heart muscle, leading to irregular heartbeat rhythms and palpitations.

Additionally, Eupatorium cannabinum may also influence the autonomic nervous system, causing changes in blood pressure and heart rate, which can further contribute to cardiac arrhythmia.

4. Increases blood pressure

Eupatorium cannabinum increases blood pressure due to its stimulant properties.

The herb contains flavonoids and triterpenes that can cause a rise in blood pressure by stimulating the heart rate and constricting blood vessels.

This increase in blood pressure may be more pronounced in individuals who are already experiencing hypertension or have pre-existing cardiovascular conditions.

5. Decreases liver function

Eupatorium cannabinum decreases liver function.

This is attributed to its potential interaction with certain medications metabolized by the liver, such as anticonvulsants and antidepressants. Additionally, the herb's phytochemicals may induce enzymes that promote liver metabolism, leading to a decrease in liver function over time.

As a result, individuals using Eupatorium cannabinum may experience altered liver enzyme levels or impaired liver function.

6. Interferes with kidney function

Eupatorium cannabinum interferes with kidney function due to its potential to increase blood pressure and reduce urine output.

This is attributed to the herb's diuretic properties, which can cause a decrease in fluid levels within the body, leading to dehydration if not adequately addressed.

As a result, individuals may experience decreased glomerular filtration rate and altered kidney function, potentially exacerbating pre-existing kidney conditions.

7. Disrupts hormonal balance

Eupatorium cannabinum disrupts hormonal balance due to its phytoestrogenic properties, which mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

As a result, it can lead to an imbalance of estrogen levels, potentially affecting menstrual cycles and fertility in women.

Additionally, its ability to bind to estrogen receptors may also impact the production of other hormones, such as testosterone and thyroid hormones, causing various hormonal imbalances.

8. Stimulates gastric secretions

Eupatorium cannabinum stimulates gastric secretions due to its ability to increase the production of stomach acid and digestive enzymes.

This is likely caused by the herb's bitter compounds, which can stimulate the appetite and digestive system, leading to increased acid secretion in the stomach.

As a result, individuals using Eupatorium cannabinum may experience symptoms such as heartburn, nausea, or stomach discomfort.

9. Causes urinary retention

Eupatorium cannabinum causes urinary retention due to its ability to increase smooth muscle tone in the urethra and bladder neck.

This increased tone can lead to a narrowing of the urine stream and difficulty urinating, resulting in urinary retention.

The plant's phytochemicals, such as flavonoids and saponins, are thought to be responsible for this effect, although the exact mechanism is not fully understood.

10. Produces gastrointestinal irritation

Eupatorium cannabinum produces gastrointestinal irritation due to its volatile oils and flavonoids, which can stimulate the digestive system and cause symptoms such as nausea, stomach pain, and diarrhea.

The saponins present in the plant may also contribute to this effect by altering the gut flora and disrupting normal digestion patterns.
