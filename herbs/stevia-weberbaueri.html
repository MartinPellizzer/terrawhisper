Stevia (Stevia Weberbaueri)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Stevia weberbaueri.

Stevia, scientifically known as Stevia weberbaueri, is a medicinal herb native to South America, renowned for its intense natural sweetness and various health benefits. It is commonly used as a sugar substitute and is considered a natural sweetener, adaptogen, and herbal remedy. The primary benefits of Stevia include its ability to support blood sugar regulation, reduce calorie intake, and promote oral health due to its antimicrobial properties. Traditionally, indigenous cultures in Paraguay and Brazil have used Stevia for centuries to treat ailments such as diabetes, hypertension, and inflammation. In modern wellness practices, Stevia is widely embraced for its zero-calorie content, making it a popular choice for those seeking to manage weight or follow a low-carb diet, and its unique, sweet, and slightly floral flavor sets it apart from other natural sweeteners.
