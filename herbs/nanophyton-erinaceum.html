Nanophyton erinaceum

What's the taxonomical classification of Nanophyton erinaceum?

Nanophyton erinaceum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Caryophyllales and falls within the family Amaranthaceae. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified by its unique genus, Nanophyton, and its specific species designation, erinaceum.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin ailments, and gastrointestinal issues through its medicinal properties. Local practitioners often utilize its extracts to alleviate symptoms of rheumatism and joint pain. It is also frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote healing in minor wounds. Some traditional applications involve using the plant to address stomach discomfort and digestive irregularities. These uses stem from the specific bioactive compounds found within its leaves and stems.
