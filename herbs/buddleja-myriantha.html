Buddleja myriantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Buddleja myriantha?

Buddleja myriantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical descent, it is placed in the order Lamiales and belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Buddlejia, and its unique species designation, myriantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buddlejia myriantha has opposite, lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, featuring finely serrated margins and a soft, pubescent texture on both surfaces. The foliage is characterized by a distinct grayish-green hue resulting from the dense covering of fine hairs. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal panicles composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. These individual blossoms exhibit a pale lilac to white coloration and are arranged in compact clusters along the flowering spikes. The overall structure of the plant is a deciduous shrub that can reach several meters in height.
