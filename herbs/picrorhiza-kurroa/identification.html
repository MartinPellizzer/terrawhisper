Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Picrorhiza kurroa is the scientific name of Kuroa, first described by the botanist Robert Wight in 1825. It belongs to the family Labiatae and is also known by several synonyms, including P. scrophularifolia and P. glabra. Common names such as Kuroa, Kurcha, and Kuroo are used in various regions, reflecting its traditional significance in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Kuroa is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system. It has a short, erect stem that is hairy and branched. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture. The plant produces a spike-like inflorescence with small, greenish-yellow flowers. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Kuroa can be distinguished by its unique purple or violet rhizomes with a strong, bitter aroma. It has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy undersides and produces small, pale blue flowers. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Picrorhiza scrophularifolia. Its rhizome morphology and scent are key diagnostic traits.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Kuroa is native to the western Himalayas, primarily in India and Nepal. It thrives in temperate climates with cool summers and cold winters. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Kuroa blooms between June and July, producing small, violet flowers. Seed formation occurs from August to September. Harvesting is typically done in late autumn, around October to November, when the roots are most developed and contain the highest concentration of active compounds.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Kuroa belongs to the genus Picrorhiza, closely related to species like Picrorhiza scrophulariiflora.
