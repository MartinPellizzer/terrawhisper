What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Torenia colorata has soft, herbaceous stems that support oppositely arranged, heart-shaped leaves with crenate margins and a textured, pubescent surface. The foliage typically presents a deep green color, often featuring lighter veins that create a patterned appearance. Its most striking feature is the funnel-shaped flowers, which display vibrant hues like yellow, orange, or salmon with distinct dark nectar guides on the throat. These blooms are produced on slender stalks and emerge from the leaf axils throughout the growing season. The plant remains relatively compact, making it an ideal choice for hanging baskets or shaded garden borders.
