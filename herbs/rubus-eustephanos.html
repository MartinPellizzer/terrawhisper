Rubus eustephanos

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus eustephanos has arching, woody stems that are densely covered in sharp, recurved prickles and small, lanceolate leaves arranged in an alternate pattern. The foliage consists of leaflets that are typically serrated along the margins and possess a slightly textured surface. During the flowering stage, the plant produces small, delicate white flowers that emerge from the axils of the stems. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy aggregate fruits known as drupelets. The overall structure of the plant allows it to form thick, scrambling thickets in its natural habitat.
