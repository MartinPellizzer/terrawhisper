Pedicularis kansuensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis kansuensis has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and is characterized by small, linear to lanceolate leaves that are oppositely arranged. The foliage consists of narrow, simple leaves that lack large lobes, distinguishing it from many other species in the genus. Its inflorescence is a terminal spike containing numerous small, tubular flowers that exhibit a subtle pink to purplish coloration. The corollas are zygomorphic and feature a distinct upper lip that is often slightly hooded. These botanical structures are adapted to the specific alpine or subalpine environments where the plant typically grows.
