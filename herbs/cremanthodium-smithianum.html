Cremanthodium smithianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cremanthodium smithianum?

Cremanthodium smithianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Asterales and places it within the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cremanthodium, and its species designation, smithianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cremanthodium smithianum has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that reach heights of up to 100 centimeters.

The leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern and consist of thin, lanceolate blades with entire margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves in terminal or subterminal clusters. The inflorescence is typically composed of many tiny, actinomorphic florets that are often white or pale in color.

The root system consists of a relatively shallow, fibrous network that anchors the plant in moist environments.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Cremanthodium smithianum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential neurotoxicity if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger allergic reactions such as contact dermatitis upon skin exposure. Ingestion of its parts can lead to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain due to its chemical composition. Some studies suggest that concentrated extracts might interfere with neurological functions if systemic absorption occurs. Caution should be exercised when handling this species to avoid unintended physiological harm.
