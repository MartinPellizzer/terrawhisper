Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) Compounds

Selfheal contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to its astringent effects. Phenolic acids and coumarin also play roles in its medicinal properties. Glycosides and saponins may support its anti-inflammatory actions, while terpenoids and essential oils contribute to its aromatic and therapeutic qualities. Alkaloids and anthocyanins add to its complex pharmacological profile.

Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce swelling and support immune function. They are found in the plant's leaves and flowers, making it useful in herbal remedies for respiratory and skin conditions.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) contributes to its astringent properties, helping reduce inflammation and soothe mucous membranes. It is found in the plant's leaves and flowers, supporting its traditional use in treating sore throats and skin conditions. Tannins also aid in wound healing and may have antimicrobial effects.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Found in various parts of the plant, phenolic acid supports its traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. This compound plays a vital role in the therapeutic effects of Selfheal.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's mild antispasmodic and sedative effects. Coumarin also has anticoagulant properties, though it should be used cautiously in individuals on blood-thinning medications. Its presence supports Selfheal's traditional use in treating respiratory and nervous system conditions.

5. Glycoside

Glycosides in Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds, such as prunellin and prunellianin, have anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects. They are often responsible for the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Glycosides are extracted and used in herbal formulations.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may also support immune function and help in reducing swelling. These effects make saponin a valuable component in herbal treatments.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds help reduce swelling and combat infections, making the plant useful in traditional herbal remedies for respiratory and skin conditions. Specific terpenoids like limonene and myrtenol are known for their therapeutic effects.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) contains volatile compounds like limonene and cineole, which contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These components support its traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation from the plant's flowers and leaves.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key compound found in Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in reducing inflammation and supporting respiratory health. Alkaloids in Selfheal are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Anthocyanin helps support immune function and may aid in reducing oxidative stress. Its presence in Selfheal highlights the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.
