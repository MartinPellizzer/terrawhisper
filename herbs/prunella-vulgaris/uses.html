Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) Uses

Selfheal is commonly used to treat sore throat, cough, and throat inflammation. It helps reduce symptoms of tonsillitis and pharyngitis by soothing irritation and fighting infection. The herb is also effective for respiratory tract infections, sinusitis, and skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) Monograph

1. Sore throat

Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) is used to soothe sore throats due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce irritation and swelling in the throat, making it easier to swallow. Often prepared as a tea, it can be consumed warm to provide relief from coughing and discomfort.

2. Cough

Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) is used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Often prepared as a tea, it supports respiratory health during colds or bronchitis. Its mild nature makes it suitable for frequent or persistent coughs.

3. Tonsillitis

Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) has been traditionally used to support the treatment of tonsillitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It may help reduce throat inflammation and ease symptoms such as pain and swelling. Typically, it is prepared as a tea or tincture and used as a supportive remedy in herbal medicine practices.

4. Pharyngitis

Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) is used to treat pharyngitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce throat irritation and soothe inflammation. A tea made from its leaves can be consumed to relieve symptoms. It is often combined with other herbs in traditional herbal formulations for enhanced efficacy.

5. Throat inflammation

Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) is used to alleviate throat inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling and soothe irritation, making it beneficial for conditions like sore throat and pharyngitis. Typically prepared as a tea, it supports healing and provides relief from symptoms.

6. Respiratory tract infections

Selfheal is used to support the respiratory system by reducing inflammation and soothing irritation in the throat and airways. It may help alleviate symptoms of coughs, bronchitis, and other respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Traditionally, it has been prepared as a tea or tincture to ease congestion and promote healing.

7. Inflamed tonsils

Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) is used to soothe inflamed tonsils due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling and discomfort, often prepared as a gargle or infusion. Its historical use in herbal medicine supports its role in supporting throat health during infections.

8. Sinusitis

Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) is used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of sinusitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce nasal congestion and combat infection. It may be prepared as a tea or tincture to ease symptoms and promote healing.

9. Respiratory infections

Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. It may help alleviate symptoms of coughs, sore throats, and mild respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Its use is common in herbal medicine for its soothing and cleansing effects on the respiratory system.

10. Skin infections

Selfheal (Prunella vulgaris) is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce symptoms of bacterial infections, such as boils and abscesses, and promotes healing. A poultice or infused oil made from the plant can be applied directly to affected areas for effective relief.
