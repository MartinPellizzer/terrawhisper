Pueraria phaseoloides

What's the taxonomical classification of Pueraria phaseoloides?

Pueraria phaseoloides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is a member of the order Fabales and falls under the family Fabaceae, which is commonly known as the legume family. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Pueraria, and its specific identity is defined by the species name phaseoloides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pueraria phaseoloides has a perennial climbing habit characterized by woody stems, trifoliate leaves with ovate leaflets, and papilionaceous flowers that are typically pale purple or white. The plant utilizes twining stems to ascend through surrounding vegetation in its natural habitat. Its leaves are composed of three leaflets that possess serrated margins and a slightly pubescent texture on the underside. The inflorescences are arranged in racemes that emerge from the leaf axils. Small, oblong pods follow flowering and contain several seeds which aid in the species' dispersal.
