Pleurolobus salicifolius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pleurolobus salicifolius has small, creeping, perennial rhizomes that produce narrow, lanceolate leaves measuring approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in width and up to 5 centimeters in length. The foliage is characterized by smooth margins and a willow-like appearance, often featuring a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowering structure consists of tiny, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils on slender pedicels. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing mats across moist, shaded substrates. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in specialized riparian or rocky microhabitats.
