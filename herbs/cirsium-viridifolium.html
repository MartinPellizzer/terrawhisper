Cirsium viridifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Cirsium viridifolium?

Cirsium viridifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is further categorized into the genus Cirsium, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species viridifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium viridifolium has deeply lobed, green leaves with prominent white veins and prickly margins, upright stems, and composite flower heads composed of pink to purple tubular florets. The foliage is characterized by a spreading basal rosette that transitions into smaller, serrated leaves along the ascending stem. These leaves are distinct for their bright green color and lack of the heavy white woolly pubescence often seen in related species. The inflorescence consists of several flower heads arranged in a cluster, each surrounded by a series of overlapping bracts. Each head produces numerous individual disk florets that bloom sequentially to provide a lasting display.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the western United States, specifically ranging from southern British Columbia through Washington, Oregon, Idaho, Montana, Wyoming, Colorado, and into New Mexico. It primarily inhabits montane and subalpine environments across the Rocky Mountains and the Cascade Range. Individual populations are frequently found in open meadows, mountain slopes, and along rocky ridges at various elevations. The species tends to cluster in regions with sufficient moisture and well-drained soils typical of high-altitude ecosystems.
