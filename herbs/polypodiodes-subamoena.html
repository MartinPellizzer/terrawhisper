What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polypodioides subamoena has small, thin, lanceolate to narrowly elliptic fronds that are typically single-pinnate with entire or slightly wavy margins. The texture of the leaves is often somewhat thin and membranous, reflecting its delicate growth habit. The stipe and rachis are generally slender and can vary in color from green to brownish tones. Sori are located on the underside of the blades, often arranged in rows near the margins. These reproductive structures are protected by a small indusium that helps shield the spores.
