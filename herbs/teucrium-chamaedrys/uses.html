Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys) Uses

Germander is used to treat respiratory infections, coughs, and cold symptoms due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps alleviate sore throat and sinusitis by reducing inflammation. The herb may also support asthma management and promote wound healing. It is applied topically for skin infections, inflammation, and mouth ulcers.

Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Germander has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly for conditions like bronchitis and coughs. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways. It is often prepared as a tea to ease congestion and promote clearer breathing.

2. Cough

Germander is used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. It acts as an expectorant, helping to loosen mucus and ease bronchial congestion. Its antiseptic properties may reduce inflammation and infection in the respiratory tract. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for this purpose.

3. Cold symptoms

Germander is traditionally used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce fever, soothe sore throats, and ease congestion. Often prepared as a tea, it supports the immune system during respiratory infections. Its aromatic compounds also act as a mild decongestant.

4. Sore throat

Germander has been traditionally used to soothe sore throats due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling and irritation in the throat, making it easier to swallow. Often prepared as a tea, it provides gentle relief and is considered safe for regular use in herbal remedies.

5. Sinusitis

Germander has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly for sinusitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help reduce congestion and combat infection. It is often used in steam inhalations or as a tincture. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.

6. Asthma

Germander has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. It is believed to help reduce bronchial spasms and ease breathing. Its expectorant properties may assist in clearing mucus, while its anti-inflammatory effects can soothe airway irritation. However, it should be used under professional guidance due to potential interactions and side effects.

7. Skin infections

Germander has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce inflammation and promote healing in conditions like eczema and fungal infections. Its essential oils contain compounds that inhibit bacterial and fungal growth. External application of germander preparations is commonly recommended for these purposes.

8. Wound healing

Germander has been traditionally used to support wound healing due to its antimicrobial and astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and prevent infection, promoting faster tissue repair. Its active compounds may stimulate cellular regeneration, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for treating minor cuts and skin injuries.

9. Mouth ulcers

Germander has been traditionally used to treat mouth ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce pain and inflammation, promoting faster healing. A decoction of the leaves can be used as a gargle or applied directly to the affected area. It is often recommended for its soothing effects on oral mucosa.

10. Skin inflammation

Germander is used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation associated with conditions like eczema or minor skin infections. Topical applications of its distilled water or infused oil can soothe inflamed skin and promote healing.
