Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys) Compounds

The primary compounds in Germander include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and saponins contribute to its astringent qualities, while essential oils provide aromatic and antimicrobial benefits. Coumarin and terpenoids support its traditional use in digestive and respiratory support.

Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are present in various parts of the plant, including leaves and flowers. Flavonoids may enhance the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Found in various parts of the plant, phenolic acid supports its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Its presence enhances the overall efficacy of Germander in herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support wound healing and digestive health. Tannins in Germander are typically found in its leaves and stems. They play a role in the plant's traditional use for respiratory and skin conditions.

4. Essential oil

The essential oil of Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys) contains volatile compounds like thujone and geraniol. These components contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is often used in herbal medicine for its soothing effects on the respiratory and digestive systems. It is typically extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and flowers.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys). It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and sedative effects. Coumarin is also known for its mild anticoagulant properties. Its presence supports Germander's traditional use in treating digestive and nervous system disorders.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds are responsible for many of the therapeutic effects attributed to Germander in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin helps support respiratory and digestive health. It is often used in herbal formulations for its cleansing and immune-supporting effects.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Alkaloids in Germander are known for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. They are often used in herbal formulations to support immune and gastrointestinal health.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Germander (Teucrium chamaedrys). It contributes to the plant's vibrant blue and purple pigmentation and may possess antioxidant properties. These compounds are often studied for their potential health benefits in herbal medicine.
