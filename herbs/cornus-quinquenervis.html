Cornus quinquenervis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cornus quinquenervis?

Cornus quinquenervis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Cornales and the family Cornaceae, eventually reaching the genus Cornus. Finally, it is identified at the species level as quinquenervis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cornus quinquenervis has opposite, decussate leaves that typically feature five prominent longitudinal veins branching from the base to the tip. These elliptical to ovate leaves often develop a characteristic reddish or purplish hue during the autumn season. The plant produces small, creamy white flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped cymes throughout the late spring. Following flowering, it develops small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a deep blue or black color when ripe. The woody stems and branches exhibit a grayish bark that provides a structural contrast to the foliage.
