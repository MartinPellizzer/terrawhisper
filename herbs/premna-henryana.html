Premna henryana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Premna henryana has oppositely arranged, elliptical to oblong leaves that typically measure between five and twelve centimeters in length with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage often displays a lustrous green upper surface and can exhibit a lighter underside depending on the specific specimen. Its woody stems are characterized by a branching habit that forms a compact shrub or small tree structure. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, eventually developing into fleshy drupes used for reproduction. The overall architecture of the plant is defined by these dense leaf arrangements and sturdy, lignified branches.
