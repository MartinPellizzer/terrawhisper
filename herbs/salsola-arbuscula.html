Salsola arbuscula

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salsola arbuscula has a woody, shrubby habit characterized by dense, branching stems that often form rounded or spreading mounds. The leaves are small, succulent, and scale-like, frequently being fused to the stem to reduce surface area for water conservation. These leaves are typically arranged in an alternate or spiral pattern and can appear green or slightly reddish depending on environmental stress. The plant produces inconspicuous flowers that are often tucked within the leaf axils. Its overall structure is adapted to survive in arid, saline environments through its compact and tough physical form.
