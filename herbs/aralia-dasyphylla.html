What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia dasyphylla has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by large, compound leaves that are pinnately arranged on long, slender petioles. These leaves are often arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems, which can become somewhat woody as the plant matures. The inflorescence consists of large, branched panicles that produce clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers.
