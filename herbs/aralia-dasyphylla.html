What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the treatment of CCl4-induced liver injury and alloxan-induced diabetes. Researchers have studied its bioactive compounds to determine how they protect liver cells from chemical damage. The plant also demonstrates significant potential in managing blood glucose levels in diabetic models. These medicinal properties are attributed to its high concentration of antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agents. Such studies suggest that the species could serve as a natural therapeutic agent for metabolic and hepatic disorders.

According to a study published by "Yao xue xue bao = Acta pharmaceutica Sinica", Aralia dasyphylla Miq contains two triterpenoids and their glycosides, specifically oleanoic acid(I), 16 beta-hydroxy-18 beta-H-oleanoic acid(II), oleanoic acid-28-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside(III) and 16 beta-hydroxy-18 beta-H-oleanoic acid-28-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside(IV). Pharmacological experiments demonstrated that the total saponins from this plant exerted a preventative effect on CCl4-induced liver injury of male mice. Additionally, these saponins showed a hypoglycemic effect on a model of alloxan-induced diabetes in rats. The structures were identified through various data including UV, IR, MS, 1H and 13CNMR, DEPT, HMQC and HMBC spectra.
