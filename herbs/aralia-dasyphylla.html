Aralia dasyphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Aralia dasyphylla?

Aralia dasyphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Apiales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Araliaceae and is categorized under the genus Aralia. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species designation, Aralia dasyphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia dasyphylla has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by large, compound leaves that are pinnately arranged on long, slender petioles.

The leaflets are typically lanceolate or elliptical in shape with serrated margins and a leathery texture. These leaves are often arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems, which can become somewhat woody as the plant matures. The inflorescence consists of large, branched panicles that produce clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers.

Following flowering, the plant may develop small fleshy fruits that serve as a key identification feature.
