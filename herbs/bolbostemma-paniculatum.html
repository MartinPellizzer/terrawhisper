Chinese bellflower

Bolbostemma paniculatum

Chinese Bellflower (Bolbostemma paniculatum) is a perennial climbing plant native to China, known for its traditional use in treating respiratory and cardiovascular conditions.

Botanical Identification

Bolbostemma paniculatum, also known as Chinese Bellflower, belongs to the family Campanulaceae. It is a perennial herb native to China, characterized by its tuberous roots, heart-shaped leaves, and bell-shaped blue flowers. Common names include Hua Geng and Geng Shen, and it has been referenced in traditional Chinese herbal texts under various synonyms.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments due to its warming properties.

Infusion: Used for mild ailments like colds and coughs due to its gentle nature.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

The Chinese Bellflower is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
