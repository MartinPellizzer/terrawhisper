What's the taxonomical classification of Bolbostemma paniculatum?

Bolbostemma paniculatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cucurbitales. This plant is further categorized into the family Cucurbitaceae, which contains the genus Bolbostemma, and is specifically identified by the species name paniculatum.

As discussed in a study, tubeimosides I, II, and III were isolated from the tubers of Bolbostemma paniculatum to evaluate their anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, and anti-tumorigenic activities. All 3 tubeimosides are natural analogues of oleanane type triterepene saponins that demonstrate anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, and anti-tumor-promoting effects. The anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, and anti-tumorigenic activities of tubeimoside II are stronger than tubeimoside I, while tubeimoside II also has lower acute toxicity than tubeimoside I. Furthermore, the anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, and anti-tumorigenic activities of tubeimoside III are stronger than tubeimoside II, although the acute toxicity of tubeimoside III is also stronger than that of tubeimoside II. Among the 3 isolated compounds, tubeimoside II may be the most promising agent for cancer chemoprevention and chemotherapy.

The tubeimoside series is frequently studied for its potential anti-tumor and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the presence of cucurbitacin B(III) contributes to the overall therapeutic profile of the plant's chemical makeup. Together, these compounds form a complex chemical landscape used to investigate various medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, Bolbostemma paniculatum (Maxim.) Franquet (Cucurbitaceae) contains the triterpenoid saponin Tubeimoside-1 (TBMS1) extracted from its tubers. This plant is widely prescribed in traditional Chinese medicine for its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. In research involving human lung cancer A549 cells, TBMS1 inhibited cell proliferation in a dose- and time-dependent manner. The compound induced apoptosis by increasing the Bax to Bcl-2 ratio and decreasing COX-2 expression. These effects on A549 cells were confirmed through flow cytometry, fluorescence microscopy, and transmission electron microscopy.
