Microtoena pauciflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microtoena pauciflora has a prostrate growth habit characterized by creeping stems, small opposite leaves, and solitary white flower heads. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and possess entire margins. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head held atop a short peduncle. The capitula feature white ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular florets. These small, herbaceous plants are adapted to spreading across open, sandy habitats.
