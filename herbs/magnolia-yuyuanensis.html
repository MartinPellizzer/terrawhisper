What is Magnolia yuyuanensis?

Magnolia yuyuanensis is a species of flowering plant in the family Magnoliaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that grows up to 3 meters tall, with elliptic to ovate leaves that are 6-14 centimeters long and 2-6 centimeters wide. The leaves have a leathery texture, entire margin, and a prominent midrib. The plant produces white flowers with 9-12 petals, 2-4 centimeters long, in late winter to early spring, which are followed by red to purple fruit.

The fruit is a berry, 2-3 centimeters long, containing 2-3 seeds.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Magnolia yuyuanensis are magnolol and honokiol, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant and antimicrobial activities by inhibiting the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and scavenging free radicals, thereby protecting against tissue damage and infection.

Oleanolic acid and ursolic acid, triterpenoid saponins present in the plant, have been found to possess anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects by suppressing the activity of pro-inflammatory cytokines and enhancing the production of anti-inflammatory cytokines. Additionally, lignans, a class of polyphenolic compounds, in Magnolia yuyuanensis have been reported to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory and antiviral activities, contributing to the plant's overall therapeutic potential. These constituents have been isolated and characterized through various chromatographic and spectroscopic techniques, providing evidence for their presence and potential therapeutic effects.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Magnolia yuyuanensis are yuyuanensis tincture, decoction, magnolia bark tea, yuyuanensis infusion, and yuyuanensis tea.

Yuyuanensis tincture is a concentrated liquid extract obtained through the process of maceration of the bark in a solvent such as ethanol or glycerin. Decoction is a preparation method that involves boiling the bark in water to release its bioactive compounds, often used to treat respiratory issues and fever. Magnolia bark tea is a more diluted form of decoction, typically consumed as a warm beverage to alleviate stress and anxiety.

Yuyuanensis infusion and tea are similar to each other, with the former referring to the steeping of the dried bark in hot water, while the latter is often consumed as a medicinal beverage to promote relaxation and immune function.
