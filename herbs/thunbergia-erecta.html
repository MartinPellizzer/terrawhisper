Thunbergia erecta

What's the taxonomical classification of Thunbergia erecta?

Thunbergia erecta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls into the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is part of the order Lamiales and the family Acanthaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Thunbergia, with its specific designation being the species erecta.

What are the herbal preparations of Thunbergia erecta?

Thunbergia erecta is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using its leaves and stems for medicinal purposes. These liquid preparations are often consumed to address inflammatory conditions or to serve as a traditional tonic. Some practitioners also utilize the crushed plant material as a topical poultice applied directly to the skin. The dried foliage can be processed into powdered forms for more concentrated applications. These various methods allow for different levels of potency depending on the intended therapeutic use.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Thunbergia erecta can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or digestive upset if ingested by humans or animals. While there is limited clinical data on its toxicity, some individuals may experience redness or itching upon direct contact with the foliage. Ingestion of any part of the plant might lead to nausea or stomach discomfort in sensitive subjects. It is always advisable to wear gloves when pruning the shrub to prevent potential skin reactions. Maintaining distance from the plant during consumption can mitigate most risks associated with accidental exposure.
