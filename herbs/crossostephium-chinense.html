Crossostephium chinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Crossostephium chinense?

Crossostephium chinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. The botanical identity of this plant is defined by its genus, Crossostephium, and its specific species, chinense.

Some regional variations involve using the herb in infused teas where it is steeped rather than boiled. While often used in traditional medicine, the specific pharmacological profile and long-term safety of its chemical constituents remain insufficiently documented in modern clinical studies. There is also a lack of comprehensive data regarding how the plant interacts with pharmaceutical medications or its effects on pregnant and lactating women. Consequently, caution is advised when using this species due to the unpredictable nature of its bioactive compounds.
