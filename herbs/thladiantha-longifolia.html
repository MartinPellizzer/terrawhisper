Thladiantha longifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Thladiantha longifolia?

Thladiantha longifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Cucurbitales. It is a member of the family Cucurbitaceae, which places it within the genus Thladiantha, ultimately defining its specific identity as the species Thladiantha longifolia.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, intestinal parasites, and respiratory issues. Practitioners often apply its leaves topically to soothe inflammatory skin conditions and heal wounds. When ingested in specific preparations, it is believed to help expel worms from the digestive tract. Some traditional medicinal practices also utilize the plant to alleviate symptoms of common coughs and congestion. These applications rely on the specific chemical properties found within its foliage.
