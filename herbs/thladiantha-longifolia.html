      What are the herbal preparations of Thladiantha longifolia?

      Thladiantha longifolia is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using the plant's leaves and stems. Practitioners often boil the dried plant material in water to extract its active chemical constituents for oral consumption. Infusions are also common, where hot water is poured over fresh leaves to create a milder tea.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Thladiantha longifolia can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or touched. Direct contact with the plant's sap or leaves may lead to contact dermatitis characterized by redness and itching. If swallowed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Some people may also display systemic symptoms such as dizziness or localized swelling. These reactions vary depending on the individual's sensitivity to the plant's chemical compounds.
