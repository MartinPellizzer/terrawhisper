        What are the herbal preparations of Chrysosplenium serreanum?

        Chrysosplenium serreanum is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid infusion made by boiling the dried roots or stems in water to extract active compounds. Practitioners may also transform the dried plant material into a fine powder for use in medicinal capsules or topical applications. These various forms allow for different methods of absorption and dosage depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Chrysosplenium serreanum can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or touched. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to localized dermatitis characterized by redness and swelling. If the leaves are consumed, individuals might experience nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. Some people may also develop respiratory discomfort if the pollen becomes airborne in enclosed spaces. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when working near this species.
