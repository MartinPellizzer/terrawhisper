Lotus corniculatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lotus corniculatus?

Lotus corniculatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Fabales. The plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, falling under the genus Lotus, and is specifically identified by its species name, corniculatus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lotus corniculatus contains high concentrations of triterpenoid saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that provide significant therapeutic potential. These bioactive compounds contribute to the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research suggests that the specific saponins found in the species may exhibit antimicrobial activity against various pathogens. Additionally, the presence of flavonoids supports its traditional use in soothing skin irritations and managing oxidative stress. These chemical constituents work synergistically to promote cellular protection and systemic wellness.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lotus corniculatus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive disorders, urinary tract infections, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are primarily attributed to the presence of tannins and flavonoids which aid in soothing internal membranes. It is often applied in traditional remedies to alleviate symptoms of diarrhea and intestinal discomfort. Additionally, the plant may assist in managing mild skin irritations and promoting wound healing through its antiseptic qualities. These therapeutic effects make it a valuable component in several herbal formulations.
