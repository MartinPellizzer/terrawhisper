Tilia nobilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Tilia nobilis?

Tilia nobilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Tilia, and its unique species designation, nobilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tilia nobilis has deciduous, heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins and a base that is distinctly asymmetrical. The foliage is characterized by a lustrous, dark green upper surface and a paler, somewhat pubescent underside. Its woody stems develop into a robust tree structure with a spreading crown and textured bark. Small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers emerge from axillary clusters during the blooming season. These blossoms are accompanied by a specialized leaf-like bract that serves to facilitate seed dispersal.
