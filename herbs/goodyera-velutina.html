Goodyera velutina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Goodyera velutina has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are characterized by a dense covering of soft, velvety hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. These leaves are arranged in a basal rosette and feature prominent longitudinal veins that extend toward the margins. The plant produces a slender, upright flowering spike that emerges from the center of the rosette. Small, inconspicuous white or pale flowers are clustered along this spike, typically appearing during the warmer months. The root system consists of underground rhizomes that allow the plant to spread slowly through its habitat.
