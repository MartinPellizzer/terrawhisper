Elatostema involucratum

What's the taxonomical classification of Elatostema involucratum?

Elatostema involucratum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. This plant is further categorized under the family Urticaceae, which places it among a diverse group of herbaceous plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Elatostema, specifically designated as the species involucratum.

      What are the herbal preparations of Elatostema involucratum?

      Elatostema involucratum is prepared as a decoction or a tea by boiling the plant parts in water to extract its medicinal properties. This method is commonly used to utilize the plant's bioactive compounds for therapeutic purposes. Practitioners may also utilize the plant in the form of a topical paste applied directly to the skin.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Elatostema involucratum can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. There is currently limited toxicological data available to define the exact chemical compounds responsible for these reactions. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to localized redness, itching, or swelling on sensitive areas of the skin. If consumed, individuals might experience nausea or abdominal discomfort. Because specific studies on human reactions are sparse, extreme caution should be exercised when interacting with this species.
