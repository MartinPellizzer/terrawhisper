Drosera indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Caryophyllales and the family Droseraceae, which characterizes its specialized biological traits. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Drosera, and its specific species designation, indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Drosera indica has a rosette of small, spatulate leaves that are covered in glandular trichomes secreting a sticky, mucilaginous substance. These leaves are typically bright green or reddish and grow close to the soil surface on a short rhizome. Each leaf features specialized tentacles tipped with glistening droplets to trap and digest small insects. The plant also produces slender, upright flower stalks that terminate in small, delicate blooms. The overall structure is compact and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil environments.
