What is Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia Frutescens)?

Cancer Bush, or Sutherlandia frutescens, is a small, shrub-like plant native to southern Africa, known for its traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Cancer Bush, or Sutherlandia frutescens, is a small, shrub-like plant native to southern Africa, belonging to the family Rhamnaceae and characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and small, velvety leaves.

Common Names and Synonyms

The Cancer Bush, also known as Sutherlandia frutescens, is a shrub native to southern Africa and is commonly referred to by other names such as "Cancer weed," "Lion's tail," and "Mole's tail" in various regional dialects.

Plant Description and Morphology

The Cancer Bush, or Sutherlandia frutescens, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial plant with woody stems, small grey-green leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically found in the semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

Native Habitat and Distribution

The Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia frutescens) is native to southern Africa, specifically found in countries such as South Africa, Lesotho, Namibia, and parts of Zimbabwe, where it grows in dry, sandy soils and grassland habitats.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The roots, leaves, and flowers of the Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia frutescens) are used medicinally in traditional African herbal medicine.

Phytochemical Composition

The phytochemical composition of Sutherlandia frutescens includes bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, sutherlandin, and various polysaccharides, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Pharmacological Properties

The pharmacological properties of Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia frutescens) include immunomodulatory, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory effects, with preliminary research suggesting potential therapeutic applications in cancer treatment.

Mechanisms of Action

The mechanisms of action of Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia frutescens) involve its bioactive compounds, such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins, which may exhibit immunomodulatory, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, potentially supporting the body's natural defenses against cancer.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Cancer bush is used to treat cancer, particularly in supportive care for patients with advanced stages, as well as for managing symptoms such as pain, fatigue, and nausea associated with cancer and its treatments.

Preparation Methods and Forms

The preparation methods and forms of Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia frutescens) typically involve drying the plant material and using it to make teas, tinctures, or capsules, with the most common form being a herbal tea prepared by steeping the dried leaves and stems in hot water.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

Drug Interactions

While Sutherlandia frutescens is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals, and it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before use, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or who are pregnant or breastfeeding.

Cultivation of Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia frutescens) requires well-drained soil, full to partial sunlight, and a warm, dry climate, with careful attention to watering to avoid root rot.

Processing and Extraction

Storage and Shelf Life

Cancer Bush (Sutherlandia frutescens) should be stored in an airtight container in a cool, dry, and dark place to preserve its potency and extend its shelf life, which is typically up to two years when properly stored.
