Potentilla cryptotaeniae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella cryptotaeniae has a low-growing, caespitose habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves with five to seven narrow, serrated leaflets and small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs that provide a soft texture to the foliage. Its stems are typically short and woody at the base, helping the plant form dense mats. The floral structures include multiple stamens and numerous carpels centered within a cup-like receptacle. Once pollination occurs, the plant develops small, aggregate dry fruits known as achenes.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the island of Crete in Greece. It is specifically found within the rugged limestone habitats of the White Mountains and other mountain ranges across the island. These populations are typically restricted to subalpine or alpine zones where rocky crevices provide necessary shelter. Because its range is confined to these specific Mediterranean montane environments, the species has a very limited global footprint. This localized distribution makes the plant a significant subject for studying island biogeography and endemic evolution.
