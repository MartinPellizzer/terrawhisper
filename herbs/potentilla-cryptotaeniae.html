What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla cryptotaeniae?

Potentiella cryptotaeniae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae, eventually reaching its specific classification within the genus Potentiella. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, cryptotaeniae.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella cryptotaeniae has a low-growing, caespitose habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves with five to seven narrow, serrated leaflets and small, five-petaled yellow flowers.

The leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs that provide a soft texture to the foliage. Its stems are typically short and woody at the base, helping the plant form dense mats. The floral structures include multiple stamens and numerous carpels centered within a cup-like receptacle.

Once pollination occurs, the plant develops small, aggregate dry fruits known as achenes.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Potentiella cryptotaeniae can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, redness, and localized itching upon direct physical contact. Individuals with hypersensitivity to the Rosaceae family may experience more severe inflammatory responses. Some users might report mild gastrointestinal discomfort if any part of the plant is accidentally ingested. Because there is limited clinical research on this specific species, the full range of toxicological effects remains largely uncharacterized. It is advisable to exercise caution and avoid skin contact until further safety studies are completed.
