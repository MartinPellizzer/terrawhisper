Lindernia brevipedunculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Lindernia brevipedunculata?

Lindernia brevipedunculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Linderniaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Lindernia, with its specific designation being the species brevipedunculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia brevipedunculata has an erect to ascending herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins and a short peduncle.

The stems are typically slender and may exhibit varying degrees of pubescence depending on the specific environmental conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in the axils of the leaves, often appearing in small clusters. The fruit is a small capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds essential for its reproductive cycle.

This plant's overall structure is adapted for growth in moist or semi-aquatic habitats.
