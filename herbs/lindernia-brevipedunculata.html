Lindernia brevipedunculata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia brevipedunculata has an erect to ascending herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins and a short peduncle. The stems are typically slender and may exhibit varying degrees of pubescence depending on the specific environmental conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in the axils of the leaves, often appearing in small clusters. The fruit is a small capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds essential for its reproductive cycle. This plant's overall structure is adapted for growth in moist or semi-aquatic habitats.
