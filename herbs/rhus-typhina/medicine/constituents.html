10 Medicinal Constituents Of Rhus Typhina (Stag'S Horn Sumach)

Rhus typhina has active constituents such as tannins, flavonoids, and gallic acid.

These constituents impart the plant's astringent, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The astringent property can help in soothing digestive issues, while the anti-inflammatory property can alleviate pain and reduce swelling. The antimicrobial property can help in preventing infections and promoting wound healing.

Regular consumption can improve digestive health, boost immunity, and reduce the risk of chronic diseases.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Rhus typhina.

1. Flavonoids

Rhus typhina flavonoids is a group of plant compounds that are abundant in its leaves, stems, and roots.

2. Tannins

Rhus typhina tannins is a type of compound found in its leaves and stems.

Tannins are known for their astringent properties, which can cause a puckering or drying sensation on the tongue when ingested. These compounds play a crucial role in protecting the plant from pathogens and insects, as well as helping to preserve its tissues.

They are also responsible for the characteristic bitter flavor of the plant's leaves and stems.

3. Saponins

Rhus typhina saponins is a type of bioactive compound extracted from its leaves and stems.
