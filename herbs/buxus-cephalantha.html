Buxus cephalantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Buxus cephalantha?

Buxus cephalantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Buxales and the family Buxaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Buxus, with its specific designation being the species cephalantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buxus cephalantha has small, opposite, elliptical leaves that are evergreen, leathery in texture, and typically measure between 1 and 2 centimeters in length. The plant exhibits a low-growing, creeping growth habit that allows it to form dense, mat-like carpets over the ground. Its tiny, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters and lack prominent petals or sepals. After pollination, the plant develops small, globose drupes that transition from green to a dark color upon maturity. The stems are woody and slender, contributing to the overall compact architecture of this species.
