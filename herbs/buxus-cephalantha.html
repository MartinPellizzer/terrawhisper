Buxus cephalantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buxus cephalantha has small, opposite, elliptical leaves that are evergreen, leathery in texture, and typically measure between 1 and 2 centimeters in length. The plant exhibits a low-growing, creeping growth habit that allows it to form dense, mat-like carpets over the ground. Its tiny, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters and lack prominent petals or sepals. After pollination, the plant develops small, globose drupes that transition from green to a dark color upon maturity. The stems are woody and slender, contributing to the overall compact architecture of this species.

      What are the herbal preparations of Buxus cephalantha?

      Buxus cephalantha is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a liquid extract derived from its dried leaves and stems. Practitioners often boil the plant material in water to create a concentrated medicinal tea used in traditional remedies. The dried parts can also be finely ground into a powder to be mixed with other substances or consumed directly. In some instances, the plant is processed into a tincture by soaking it in alcohol to enhance its potency.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Buxus cephalantha can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if ingested. The plant contains toxic alkaloids that may lead to nausea, vomiting, or more severe gastrointestinal issues upon consumption. Contact with the foliage or stems can result in contact dermatitis, manifesting as redness or itching on the skin. Some individuals might experience hypersensitivity to the plant's chemical compounds during handling. Because of these potential hazards, caution should always be exercised when interacting with this species.
