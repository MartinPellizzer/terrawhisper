Bidens bipinnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Bidens bipinnata?

Bidens bipinnaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic placement continues through the order Asterales, placing it within the widely recognized family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Bidens, and its unique species designation, bipinnata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bidens bipinnaata has bipinnate leaves characterized by two levels of division where primary leaflets are further subdivided into smaller, secondary pinnules. The foliage typically exhibits a bright green coloration and a delicate, feathery texture due to the intricate arrangement of these small segments. Each leaf structure is supported by a central rachis that branches out to facilitate the distribution of the leaflets. The margins of the individual pinnules are often slightly serrated or smooth depending on the specific developmental stage of the plant. This complex architectural pattern helps maximize surface area for light absorption while maintaining structural efficiency.

What are the pharmacological activities of Bidens bipinnata?

This plant has antibacterial, antimicrobial, anti-diarrheal, and antihyperlipidemic pharmacological activities that demonstrate its diverse therapeutic potential. Its antimicrobial properties allow it to combat various pathogenic microorganisms effectively. The plant also exhibits significant antibacterial capabilities against specific bacterial strains. Furthermore, its anti-diarrheal effects make it useful for managing gastrointestinal distress. Finally, the antihyperlipidemic activity suggests it may play a role in regulating lipid levels within the body.

According to a study published by "Saudi journal of biological sciences", Bidens bipinnaata is a medicinal plant used for the treatment of diseases such as malaria, sore throat, acute nephritis, and dysentery. The hexane extract of its leaves was found to be the most active, leading to the isolation of 16-Pregnenolone and 9-Octadecanoic acid (Z)-, methyl ester. Through a broth dilution procedure, 16-Pregnenolone revealed a strong antimicrobial activity with an MIC50 of 72 μg/mL. In comparison, 9-Octadecanoic acid (Z)-, methyl ester displayed an MIC50 of >250 μg/mL. This study represents the first report on the isolation of these specific compounds from Bidens bipinnaata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive constituents including bippinatapolyacetylpside, bippinately polyacetylenicloside, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins. These specific chemical profiles contribute to the diverse pharmacological properties observed in the species. The presence of flavonoids often provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Meanwhile, the saponins and tannins are known to interact with biological membranes and offer antimicrobial effects. These complex medicinal compounds work together to support the traditional therapeutic uses of the plant.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", the vegetative parts of Bidens bipinnata are rich in tannins, saponins, oxalates, cyanogenic glycoside and lipids, while the roots contain high percentages of alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenols. The hexane extract of Bidens bipinnata leaves showed significant anticandiida activity, inhibiting C. albicans by 42.54%, C. glabrata by 46.98%, C. tropicalis by 50.89%, C. krusei by 40.56%, and C. orthopsiulosis by 50.24%. After obtaining 220 fractions through silica gel chromatography, the extract was analyzed using HPLC-DAD and GC-MS/MS. This analysis identified two anticandiida compounds, dehydroabietic and linoleic acid, which demonstrated inhibition rates of 85% and 92% respectively.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating diarrhoea, dysentery, malaria, sore throat, and acute nephritis. The medicinal properties of its various parts help alleviate gastrointestinal distress caused by infections. It is also frequently applied to reduce the symptoms of fever associated with malaria. For respiratory discomfort, the plant provides relief for an aching sore throat. Additionally, it plays a role in managing kidney-related issues such as acute nephritis.

According to a study published by "Phytotherapy research : PTR", Bidens bipinnata L. (BB) was evaluated for its antidiarrhoeal activity. A significant antidiarrhoeal effect against castor oil-induced diarrhoea in rats was achieved by doses of 200 and 400 mg/kg. The plant extract decreased gastrointestinal movement, as indicated by a significantly decreased distance travelled by the charcoal meal with p<0.05 to 0.001. The large dose was found to be slightly more effective than the small one. This antidiarrhoeal effect was further confirmed by the reported dose dependent inhibition of the motility of duodenum isolated from freshly slaughtered rabbits.
