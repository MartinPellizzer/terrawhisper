Bidens bipinnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Bidens bipinnata?

Bidens bipinnaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic placement continues through the order Asterales, placing it within the widely recognized family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Bidens, and its unique species designation, bipinnata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bidens bipinnaata has bipinnate leaves characterized by two levels of division where primary leaflets are further subdivided into smaller, secondary pinnules. The foliage typically exhibits a bright green coloration and a delicate, feathery texture due to the intricate arrangement of these small segments. Each leaf structure is supported by a central rachis that branches out to facilitate the distribution of the leaflets. The margins of the individual pinnules are often slightly serrated or smooth depending on the specific developmental stage of the plant. This complex architectural pattern helps maximize surface area for light absorption while maintaining structural efficiency.

The species is often found growing in shaded understory environments or along the edges of riverbanks. What are the pharmacological activities of Bidens bipinnata?

This plant has antibacterial, antimicrobial, anti-diarrheal, and antihyperlipidemic pharmacological activities. Its antimicrobial properties allow it to combat various pathogenic microorganisms effectively. The plant also demonstrates significant antibacterial capabilities against specific bacterial strains. Furthermore, its anti-diarrheal effects help manage gastrointestinal distress and stabilize bowel function. Finally, the antihyperlipidemic action assists in regulating lipid levels within the body.

As discussed in a study, Bidens bipinnaata is a widely utilized medicinal plant for the treatment of diseases such as malaria, sore throat, acute nephritis, and dysentery. The hexane extract of its leaves was found to be the most active for antimicrobial investigation. Through purification processes, two compounds were isolated: 16-Pregnenolone and 9-Octadecenoic acid (Z)-, methyl ester. Testing via a broth dilution procedure revealed that 16-Pregnenolone showed strong antimicrobial activity with an MIC50 of 72 μg/mL. In comparison, 9-Octadecenoic acid (Z)-, methyl ester displayed an MIC50 of >250 μg/mL.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal flavonoids including rutin, quercetin, hyperoside, and isoquercitrin. These specific polyphenolic compounds are primarily recognized for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Rutin and quercetin serve as fundamental building blocks within the plant's chemical profile to help mitigate oxidative stress. Additionally, the presence of hyperoside and isoquercitrin contributes to the complex therapeutic potential of the species. Together, these bioactive substances work synergistically to support various biological functions in medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, the vegetative parts of Bidens bipinnata are rich in tannins, saponins, oxalates, cyanogenic glycoside and lipids, while the roots contain high percentages of alkaloids, flavonoids, and phenols. The hexane extract of its leaves showed significant anticandiida activity by inhibiting C. albicans at 42.54%, C. glabrata at 46.98%, C. tropicalis at 50.89%, C. krusei at 40.56%, and C. orthopsilosis at 50.24%. After obtaining 220 fractions through silica gel chromatography, analysis identified two specific anticandiida compounds. These identified compounds include dehydroabietic and linoleic acid, which exhibited an inhibition of 85% and 92%, respectively.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic reactions, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramps, and dizziness in sensitive individuals. Some people may experience skin irritation or respiratory distress if they come into direct contact with its components. Digestive issues like intense stomach cramps and frequent vomiting often occur shortly after ingestion. Severe cases might lead to persistent diarrhea and significant dehydration. Additionally, some users report feeling lightheaded or experiencing bouts of dizziness following exposure.
