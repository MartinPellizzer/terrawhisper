Camellia

Camellia sinensis

Camellia (Camellia sinensis) is a flowering plant native to East Asia, best known for being the source of tea, and it has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to improve digestion, reduce stress, and support overall health.

Botanical Identification

Camellia sinensis is the scientific name for the plant commonly known as the tea plant. It belongs to the family Theaceae. Common names include cha in Chinese and camellia in some regions. It is a perennial evergreen shrub native to East Asia. The plant has deep roots, leathery leaves, and yellow flowers. It is also known as tea plant in herbal literature.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Theales
  • Family: Theaceae
  • Genus: Camellia
  • Species: Camellia sinensis

Active Compounds

Camellia sinensis contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a calming beverage.

Decoction: Used for gastrointestinal issues and as a detoxifying agent.

Culinary use: Used for its flavor and as a source of antioxidants in food.

Safety Profile

Camellia sinensis, the plant source of tea, is generally safe when consumed in typical amounts. However, it may cause side effects such as insomnia, nausea, or anxiety in sensitive individuals. High doses or prolonged use can lead to more severe effects, including heart palpitations and gastrointestinal issues.
