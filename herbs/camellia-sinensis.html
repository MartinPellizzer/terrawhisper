What to know about Camellia sinensis before using it medicinally

Camellia sinensis, commonly known as tea plant, is a medicinal herb with a long history of use in treating various health conditions, including fever, diarrhea, and inflammation.

The main health benefits of Camellia sinensis include its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which have been shown to protect against oxidative stress, improve cardiovascular health, and boost the immune system. The herb's therapeutic properties are attributed to its ability to modulate immune responses, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antibacterial and antiviral activities. The bioactive compounds present in Camellia sinensis include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and caffeine, which have been identified as the primary contributors to its medicinal properties.

The most common herbal preparation made with Camellia sinensis is a tea infusion, which is consumed globally and is a rich source of antioxidants and other beneficial compounds.
