What is Cabbage Palm (Sabal Serrulata)?

The Cabbage Palm (Sabal serrulata) is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its rosette of fleshy, cabbage-like leaves and its use in traditional herbal medicine for its purported respiratory and anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanical Identity and Classification

The Cabbage Palm (Sabal serrulata) is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family, native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast, and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and edible, starchy fruits.

Common Names and Synonyms

The Cabbage Palm (Sabal serrulata) is commonly known by several names including Cabbage Tree, Cabbage Palm, and Sabal, with regional variations such as Florida Cabbage Palm and Slender Cabbage Palm.

Plant Description and Morphology

The Cabbage Palm (Sabal serrulata) is a monocot with a stout, unbranched stem that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, bearing a dense crown of long, narrow, dark green leaves that are typically 1.5 to 3 meters in length.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Cabbage Palm (Sabal serrulata) is native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States, specifically Florida and the lower Mississippi Valley, where it thrives in wetland and swampy environments.
