Jasminum multiflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum multiflorum?

Jasminum multiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Lamiales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Oleaceae, which contains the genus Jasminum, and is specifically identified by the species name multiflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum multiflorum has woody, climbing stems that produce opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and an entire or slightly wavy texture. The foliage typically consists of leaflets that are smooth and glossy on the upper surface. During its blooming period, the plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These fragrant blossoms feature five petals that spread outward to form a star shape. The overall growth habit is characterized by sprawling, twining vines that can cover significant areas of support.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning from India through much of the Indochinese peninsula. It thrives in the warm, humid climates found in countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. Because it prefers these specific environmental conditions, its natural range is largely restricted to low-altitude areas with consistent warmth. However, due to its fragrant flowers, it has been widely introduced as an ornamental species in various temperate and subtropical gardens around the world. This global dispersal has allowed it to establish presence in many regions far beyond its original biological origins.

What are the pharmacological activities of Jasminum multiflorum?

This plant has anti-oncogenic and antiviral pharmacological activities that stem from its diverse bioactive secondary metabolites. Research indicates that its extracts can inhibit the proliferation of various cancer cell lines, demonstrating significant potential in tumor suppression. Furthermore, the plant contains compounds capable of interfering with viral replication processes to combat infectious agents. These medicinal properties are largely attributed to the presence of specific alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids within its chemical profile. Continued studies aim to isolate these active constituents for more targeted therapeutic applications.

As discussed in a study, Jasminum multiflorum is an ornamental plant with traditional medicinal importance. The flower extract demonstrated a significant antiviral potential by reducing active viral replication by more than 90%. This reduction in replication is attributed to the specific reduction of viral NS5A and cellular EphA2 protein levels. Molecular docking analysis identified the roles of isolated compounds, specifically multifloroside, jasfloroside A, and jasfloroside B, as possible anti HCV molecules. These compounds were evaluated against hepatocellular carcinoma cells infected with hepatitis C virus in vitro.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating hepatitis C virus infection and hepatocellular carcinoma through its bioactive medicinal properties. Research indicates that extracts from Jasminum multiflorum possess antiviral activities that can help combat the hepatitis C virus. Additionally, the plant shows significant potential in inhibiting the progression of hepatocellular carcinoma, which is a primary form of liver cancer. These therapeutic effects are often attributed to the presence of specific phytochemicals that target damaged or infected liver cells. Consequently, it serves as a subject of interest for developing natural treatments for severe hepatic diseases.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive upset if ingested improperly. While the flowers are often used for fragrance, direct contact with the plant material may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience redness, itching, or swelling upon touching the leaves or stems. If consumed in large quantities, the plant could lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. It is essential to exercise caution and consult a professional before using any part of this species for medicinal purposes.
