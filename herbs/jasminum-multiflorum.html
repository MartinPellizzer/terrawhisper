Jasminum multiflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum multiflorum?

Jasminum multiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Lamiales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Oleaceae, which contains the genus Jasminum, and is specifically identified by the species name multiflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum multiflorum has woody, climbing stems that produce opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and an entire or slightly wavy texture.

The foliage typically consists of leaflets that are smooth and glossy on the upper surface. During its blooming period, the plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These fragrant blossoms feature five petals that spread outward to form a star shape.

The overall growth habit is characterized by sprawling, twining vines that can cover significant areas of support.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Jasminum multiflorum contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its fragrant flowers and various green leaves. The aromatic blossoms are frequently harvested to create essential oils and infusions used for their calming and sedative properties. Extracts from the leaves are often utilized in traditional practices to treat skin inflammations and digestive issues. These plant parts work together to provide a range of therapeutic benefits, including stress reduction and topical healing. The chemical profile of these components allows for diverse applications in both herbal teas and topical salves.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids.

These specific chemical constituents contribute to the various pharmacological activities observed in Jasminum multiflorum extracts. The presence of flavonoids and phenolic acids provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the alkaloids and terpenoids play crucial roles in the plant's biological interactions and potential therapeutic applications.

Triterpenoids further enhance its medicinal value by offering potential antimicrobial and cytotoxic effects.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Jasminum multiflorum targets various enzymes and receptors including cyclooxygenase-2, acetylcholinesterase, and certain inflammatory cytokines to exert its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like jasmonoids and flavonoids that inhibit the COX-2 enzyme to reduce prostaglandin synthesis and alleviate pain. These phytochemicals also interact with acetylcholinesterase, which can modulate neurotransmitter levels relevant to cognitive function. Additionally, specific polyphenols within the plant demonstrate the ability to suppress pro-inflammatory signaling pathways by regulating tumor necrosis factor-alpha. By modulating these diverse molecular pathways, the plant provides systemic anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective benefits.

What are the therapeutic actions of Jasminum multiflorum?

Jasminum multiflorum has therapeutic properties that function as a nervine, anxiolytic, sedative, and antispasmodic agent through its potent aromatic compounds.

The plant's essential oils provide a calming effect that helps to soothe the central nervous system. By acting as an anxiolytic, it effectively reduces feelings of psychological distress and tension. Its sedative qualities promote relaxation and can assist in improving sleep quality.

Additionally, the plant serves as an antispasmodic to help alleviate physical muscle contractions and cramping.

The primary therapeutic actions of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Nervine
  • Anxiolytic
  • Sedative
  • Antispasmodic

This plant is used to treat anxiety, insomnia, depression, and stress through its calming aromatic properties.

By easing the mind, it can also assist those struggling with chronic insomnia to achieve deeper sleep.

Additionally, its therapeutic effects may offer mild support for managing symptoms of depression.

What are the herbal preparations of Jasminum multiflorum?

Jasminum multiflorum is prepared as dried flower infusions, aromatic oils, topical salves, and concentrated tinctures for therapeutic use. The dried blossoms are most commonly steeped in hot water to create a fragrant tea used for calming the nervous system. For external applications, the petals are often infused into carrier oils to produce soothing skin treatments. Tinctures made from the flowers provide a more potent way to preserve the plant's volatile compounds for long-term storage. These various methods allow the plant's aromatic properties to be utilized for both internal relaxation and external skin care.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Jasminum multiflorum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. While the plant is primarily used for its fragrant flowers, direct contact with the foliage or stems may trigger redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Some people may experience swelling or localized rashes upon touching the plant tissues. If consumed in large quantities, it might lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. Always consult a medical professional before using any part of this plant for medicinal purposes.
