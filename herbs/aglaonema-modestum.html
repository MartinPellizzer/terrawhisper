Aglaonema modestum

What's the taxonomical classification of Aglaonema modestum?

Aglaonema modestum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Aliasmaetales and is a member of the family Araceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Aglaonema and the species modestum.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Aglaonema modestum contains no documented parts used for medicinal purposes in human or animal pharmacology. While some species in the Aglaonemum genus are studied for potential bioactive compounds, this specific plant is primarily grown as an ornamental houseplant. It is important to note that the plant contains calcium oxalate crystals which can cause irritation to the mouth and throat if ingested. Because of these toxic properties, it is not considered a safe source for herbal remedies. Medical use of the plant is not supported by current botanical or pharmacological literature.

    What are the herbal preparations of Aglaonema modestum?

    Aglaonema modestum is prepared primarily as a topical poultice or a diluted liquid wash for external dermatological applications. Because the plant contains calcium oxalate crystals, it is not used as an oral infusion or tincture due to potential toxicity. The leaves or stems are crushed into a paste and applied directly to skin irritations to utilize their chemical properties. Careful dilution is necessary when using the plant in liquid form to prevent severe irritation to the mucous membranes. Practitioners must exercise extreme caution to ensure that no part of the preparation is ingested during medicinal use.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Aglaonema modestum can cause skin irritation, mouth swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if the plant's sap comes into contact with the body or is ingested. The plant contains insoluble calcium oxalate crystals that act like tiny needles when touched or eaten. These crystals can pierce the delicate tissues of the mouth and throat, leading to intense burning sensations and swelling. Contact with the leaves can also result in dermatitis or itchy rashes on sensitive skin. It is essential to keep this plant away from pets and small children to prevent accidental poisoning.
