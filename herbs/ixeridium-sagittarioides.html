What is Ixeridium sagittarioides?

Ixeridium sagittarioides is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is an annual herb with a prostrate to decumbent growth habit, reaching up to 50 cm in height and 100 cm in width. The leaves are lanceolate to ovate, 2-10 cm in length, and 0.5-2.5 cm in width, with a ciliate margin and a sessile base. The inflorescence is a capitulum, bearing 8-20 ligulate florets and a few discoid florets, with the ligulate florets being 8-15 mm in length and 1.5-3 mm in width.

The plant is native to the Andes mountains in Argentina and Chile.
