Pilea peploides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea peploides has small, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically 1 to 3 centimeters long, featuring serrated margins and a slightly fleshy texture. The foliage displays a vibrant green color and is arranged in an opposite pattern along slender, delicate stems. These stems exhibit a creeping or decumbent growth habit, allowing the plant to spread across the soil surface. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often tucked away near the leaf axils. The overall structure creates a dense, low-growing mat of vegetation.
