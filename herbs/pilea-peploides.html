Pilea peploides

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea peploides?

Pilea peploides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Rosales. It is further categorized into the family Urticaceae, which characterizes its botanical lineage. Within this family, the plant is situated in the genus Pilea, and its specific taxonomic designation is completed by the species name peploides.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, fungal conditions, and various inflammatory ailments through its topical application. The leaves are often crushed into a paste to soothe localized irritations and promote healing of the skin. In traditional medicinal practices, extracts may be used to manage symptoms associated with minor wounds or rashes. Some practitioners also utilize the plant to address internal issues related to digestive discomfort. Its chemical properties are believed to provide antibacterial and antifungal benefits when used correctly.
