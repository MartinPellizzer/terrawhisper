10 Possible Side Effects Of Bellis Perennis (Daisy)

Bellis perennis has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritation.

These side effects can occur if the herb is consumed in excess or if individuals have sensitivities to its active compounds.

For example, if a person with asthma consumes large amounts of daisy, their respiratory problems can worsen, causing difficulty breathing and disrupting daily life.

Bellis perennis causes allergic reactions due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones and polyacetylenes, which are responsible for triggering an immune response in some individuals.

These compounds can stimulate the release of histamine, leading to symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling at the site of contact or upon oral ingestion.

In rare cases, severe allergic reactions may occur, requiring prompt medical attention.
