English Daisy (Bellis perennis) Uses

English Daisy is used to treat various skin conditions such as inflammation, bruises, wounds, and minor burns. It aids in wound healing and can alleviate skin irritations and insect bites. The plant is also applied for skin infections, ulcers, and mild irritations due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

English Daisy (Bellis perennis) Monograph

1. Skin inflammation

English Daisy has been traditionally used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce redness, irritation, and swelling, making it useful for conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The plant's compounds may inhibit inflammatory responses in the skin, offering a natural remedy for mild to moderate skin issues.

2. Bruises

English Daisy has been traditionally used to treat bruises due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. The plant contains compounds that help reduce swelling and promote healing. It can be applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to soothe bruised tissues. Its mild nature makes it suitable for external use in first aid situations.

3. Wound healing

English Daisy, or Bellis perennis, has been traditionally used to support wound healing. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce infection risk and promote tissue repair. The leaves and flowers can be applied topically as a poultice or infused into salves. It is valued in herbal medicine for its soothing and healing effects on minor cuts and skin irritations.

4. Minor burns

English Daisy can be applied topically to minor burns to soothe inflammation and promote healing. Its anti-inflammatory properties help reduce redness and pain. A poultice made from fresh leaves or a cooled infusion can be used as a compress. It is best suited for slight burns and should not replace professional medical care for severe injuries.

5. Skin irritations

English Daisy has been traditionally used to treat skin irritations due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. Its leaves and flowers can be made into a poultice or infused oil to relieve rashes, eczema, and minor burns. The plant's mild astringent qualities help reduce redness and promote skin healing. It is often recommended in herbal medicine for its gentle yet effective approach to skin care.

6. Insect bites

English Daisy can be applied topically to reduce swelling and pain from insect bites. Its anti-inflammatory properties help soothe the skin, while its mild astringent action may prevent further irritation. A poultice or infused oil made from fresh leaves is commonly used for this purpose.

7. Mild skin irritations

English Daisy can be applied topically to soothe mild skin irritations. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties help reduce redness and swelling. A poultice or infused oil made from the fresh flowers is commonly used. It is gentle enough for sensitive skin but should be tested on a small area first.

8. Skin infections

English Daisy has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant contains compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil. Its gentle nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin conditions.

9. Wounds

English Daisy has been traditionally used to treat wounds due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps in reducing swelling and promoting tissue repair. The plant's ability to stimulate healing makes it a valuable component in herbal first-aid remedies. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil.

10. Skin ulcers

English Daisy has been traditionally used to treat skin ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. The plant contains compounds that help reduce infection and promote tissue repair. It can be applied topically as a poultice or infused oil. This use highlights its role in traditional herbal medicine for skin conditions.
