English Daisy (Bellis perennis) Compounds

The primary compounds of English Daisy include flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and coumarin, known for its anticoagulant effects. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to antioxidant activity, while terpenoids and glycosides support immune function. Essential oils and saponins aid in digestion, and anthocyanins provide protective benefits to cells. Alkaloids also play a role in the plant's medicinal properties.

English Daisy (Bellis perennis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in English Daisy (Bellis perennis) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are commonly found in the plant's flowers and leaves. Flavonoids may also aid in skin health and wound healing.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in English Daisy (Bellis perennis). It contributes to the plant's anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Found in the roots and leaves, coumarin is used in herbal medicine to support circulation and reduce inflammation. Its presence highlights the plant's role in traditional healing practices.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in English Daisy (Bellis perennis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Found in the flowers and leaves, it supports its traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. This compound plays a vital role in the medicinal profile of Bellis perennis.

4. Tannin

Tannin in English Daisy (Bellis perennis) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and supporting wound healing. Tannins also aid in the plant's ability to retain moisture and protect against pathogens.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in English Daisy (Bellis perennis) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. These compounds, including mono- and sesquiterpenes, help reduce pain and swelling, making the plant useful in herbal treatments for minor injuries and inflammatory conditions.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in English Daisy (Bellis perennis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and mild analgesic properties. Glycosides in English Daisy are often used in traditional herbal medicine to support respiratory and digestive health. They are typically extracted and used in tinctures or teas.

7. Essential oil

The essential oil of English Daisy (Bellis perennis) contains bioactive compounds like cineole and geraniol. These contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is used in herbal medicine for respiratory support and skin care. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the flowers and leaves.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in English Daisy (Bellis perennis). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support skin health and immune function when used as part of herbal preparations.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in English Daisy (Bellis perennis). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though specific alkaloids and their effects require further research. Alkaloids are known for their potential therapeutic uses in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in English Daisy (Bellis perennis). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower colors and possesses antioxidant properties. In herbal medicine, anthocyanins may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation. Their presence in English Daisy highlights the plant's potential therapeutic benefits.
