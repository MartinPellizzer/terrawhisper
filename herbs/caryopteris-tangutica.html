Caryopteris tangutica

What's the taxonomical classification of Caryopteris tangutica?

Caryopteris tangutica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Lamiales. It is further categorized under the family Lamiaceae, which places it among a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Caryopteris, with its specific designation being the species tangutica.

Caryopteris tangutica is prepared as an infusion, decoction, or topical poultice to utilize its bioactive compounds. What side-effects this plant can have?

Caryopteris tangutica can cause mild skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon direct contact with its foliage or stems. While it is generally considered safe for ornamental landscaping, some people may experience redness or itching if they touch the plant without protection. There is limited clinical documentation regarding systemic toxicity, but ingestion should be avoided to prevent potential gastrointestinal discomfort. It is also advisable to monitor pets for any adverse reactions if they happen to chew on the leaves. Always practice caution when handling unknown vegetation to minimize the risk of an unexpected reaction.
