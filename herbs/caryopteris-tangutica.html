Caryopteris tangutica

What's the taxonomical classification of Caryopteris tangutica?

Caryopteris tangutica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Lamiales. It is further categorized under the family Lamiaceae, which places it among a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Caryopteris, with its specific designation being the species tangutica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caryopteris tangutica has deciduous, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length and possess finely serrated margins. The foliage displays a distinctively rugose texture with prominent veins that create a wrinkled appearance on the upper surface. Stems are woody at the base and become more herbaceous and flexible toward the growing tips. During the blooming season, the plant produces small, tubular, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense terminal panicles. These inflorescences emerge from the leafy branches to create a striking visual contrast against the green foliage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?
