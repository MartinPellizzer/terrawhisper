What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula sieboldii has a basal rosette of crinkled, oblong leaves that are bright green and feature prominent, reticulate venation. The plant produces upright, leafless stalks known as scapes that can reach heights of up to thirty centimeters. At the apex of these scapes, clusters of nodding, salverform flowers emerge in a terminal umbel. Each individual blossom typically displays five petals with a distinct yellow eye at the center of the corolla tube. The petal colors range from deep violet to pale lavender, often exhibiting delicate shading. The entire structure is supported by a fleshy rhizome that facilitates its perennial growth habit.
