Astragalus khasianus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus khasianus has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by erect stems, pinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets, and papilionaceous flowers that typically appear in dense inflorescences. The plant often develops a woody base or caudex from which the leafy shoots emerge. Its leaves are composed of several pairs of leaflets that are generally oblong or elliptical in shape. The flowers are small and can vary in color from white to pale pink or violet. This species is specifically adapted to high-altitude environments, often presenting a compact growth habit to withstand harsh conditions.
