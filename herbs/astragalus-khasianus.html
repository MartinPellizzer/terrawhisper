Astragalus khasianus

What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus khasianus?

Astragalus khasianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is placed under the order Fabales. It is a distinguished representative of the family Fabaceae, falling under the genus Astragalus, and is specifically identified by the species name khasianus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Astragalus khasianus contains a complex profile of bioactive compounds including polysaccharides, saponins, flavonoids, and various amino acids. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's significant immunomodulatory and antioxidant properties. The saponins within the root structure are particularly noted for their ability to influence cellular signaling pathways. Furthermore, the presence of flavonoids helps in neutralizing free radicals and reducing oxidative stress in biological systems. Researchers often investigate these phytochemicals to understand their potential role in supporting immune function and overall metabolic health.

What are the therapeutic actions of Astragalus khasianus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory infections, skin inflammation, gastrointestinal disorders, and various autoimmune conditions. Its medicinal properties are primarily attributed to its ability to modulate the immune system and reduce systemic inflammation. In traditional practices, it is often applied to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis and chronic coughs. Some studies suggest its compounds may also help manage digestive issues and support skin healing processes. The specific bioactive constituents allow it to act as a potent adaptogen for strengthening the body's natural defenses.

What are the herbal preparations of Astragalus khasianus?

Astragalus khasianus is prepared primarily as a decoction using the dried root, though it can also be processed into powders, tinctures, or topical salves. The dried root pieces are typically simmered in water for an extended period to extract its bioactive polysaccharides and saponins. Once the liquid is concentrated, it is often consumed as a medicinal tea to support immune function. In some traditional practices, the finely ground root powder is mixed with honey to create a palatable paste. These various forms allow for different rates of absorption and specific applications in herbal medicine.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Astragalus khasianus can potentially cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, or interactions with immunosuppressant medications due to its bioactive compounds. While specific clinical studies on this exact species are limited, members of the Astragalus genus often contain saponins that may irritate the digestive tract if consumed in excess. Some individuals might experience hypersensitivity symptoms such as itching, redness, or localized swelling upon contact with the plant. It is also important to note that its immunomodulatory properties could interfere with drugs designed to suppress the immune system. Consequently, caution is advised for pregnant women and individuals with autoimmune disorders.
