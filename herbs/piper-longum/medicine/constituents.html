10 Medicinal Constituents Of Piper Longum (Long Pepper)

Piper longum has active constituents such as pipelidine, piperine, and myristicin.

These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antibacterial properties, which contribute to its medicinal uses. The constituents can alleviate pain, reduce inflammation, and improve digestion. For instance, piperine can enhance the bioavailability of certain medications and nutrients, while myristicin has shown potential in treating respiratory issues and cognitive impairment.

Overall, the constituents of Piper longum make it a valuable herb in traditional medicine.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Piper longum.

1. Piperic acid

Piper longum piperic acid is a bioactive compound derived from the fruit of the Piper longum plant.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including fever, cough, and rheumatism. Piperic acid exhibits potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential natural remedy for modern health issues such as infections and chronic pain.

Its unique chemical structure also contributes to its bioavailability and potential therapeutic applications.

2. Piperine

Piper longum piperine is a bioactive compound extracted from the fruit of the Piper longum plant.

Research suggests that piperine may also have potential benefits in improving cognitive function and enhancing the bioavailability of certain nutrients.
