Long Pepper (Piper longum) Uses

Long pepper is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, asthma, bronchitis, and sinusitis. It helps reduce nasal congestion and inflammation in the throat and lungs. It is also used for gastrointestinal inflammation and to alleviate symptoms of the common cold and tuberculosis.

Long Pepper (Piper longum) Monograph

1. Cough

Long pepper is used to alleviate cough by stimulating mucus expulsion and soothing respiratory irritation. It acts as an expectorant, helping to clear congestion and reduce inflammation in the airways. Its warming properties also help to loosen phlegm and ease breathing in cases of persistent cough.

2. Sinusitis

Long pepper is used in traditional medicine to alleviate sinusitis symptoms by reducing inflammation and mucus production. It contains piperine, which has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. When applied as a nasal powder or inhalant, it helps clear nasal passages and relieve congestion. Its warming action also supports overall respiratory health.

3. Asthma

Long pepper is used in traditional herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It helps reduce inflammation in the airways and may ease symptoms of asthma by promoting bronchodilation. Its warming properties aid in clearing mucus and improving lung function. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

4. Bronchitis

Long pepper is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis by acting as an expectorant and bronchodilator. It helps reduce mucus congestion and soothe inflamed airways, supporting respiratory health. Its warming properties may also help clear congestion and ease breathing in cases of chronic bronchial inflammation.

5. Respiratory infections

Long pepper is used in herbal medicine to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing, bronchitis, and congestion by supporting expectoration and reducing mucus buildup. Its warming effects also help clear nasal passages and soothe irritated airways.

6. Tuberculosis

Long pepper has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It is believed to help alleviate symptoms of tuberculosis by enhancing lung function and reducing inflammation. Its warming properties may assist in clearing mucus and improving circulation, making it a valued remedy in Ayurvedic treatments for chronic respiratory conditions.

7. Common cold

Long pepper is used to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its warming properties and ability to stimulate mucus production. It helps clear nasal congestion and soothe respiratory tract inflammation. Its pungent compounds also have antimicrobial effects, supporting the body's defense against viral infections.

8. Nasal congestion

Long pepper is used to alleviate nasal congestion by promoting mucus clearance and reducing inflammation in the nasal passages. Its warming properties help open up airways, making it a common remedy in herbal medicine for respiratory issues. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its decongestant effects.

9. Inflamed tonsils

Long pepper is used to reduce inflammation in the tonsils due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps alleviate pain and swelling associated with tonsillitis. Traditionally, it is prepared as a paste or decoction to soothe throat irritation. Its warming effects also support overall immune function in respiratory infections.

10. Gastrointestinal inflammation

Long pepper has anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce gastrointestinal inflammation by inhibiting pro-inflammatory cytokines. It supports mucosal healing and protects the digestive tract lining. Its pungent compounds promote healthy digestion and alleviate symptoms like bloating and discomfort. Regular use may enhance gut immunity and reduce inflammatory conditions like gastritis.
