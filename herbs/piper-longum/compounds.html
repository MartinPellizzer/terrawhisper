Long Pepper (Piper longum) Compounds

The primary compounds of Long Pepper include alkaloids, which have stimulant properties, and essential oils, contributing to its aromatic profile. Flavonoids and terpenoids provide antioxidant benefits, while phenolic acids and tannins support anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin, glycosides, saponins, and anthocyanins further enhance its therapeutic potential.

Long Pepper (Piper longum) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Long Pepper (Piper longum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by influencing various physiological processes. Alkaloids in Long Pepper are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. They play a significant role in the therapeutic use of this herb in traditional medicine.

2. Essential oil

The essential oil of Long Pepper (Piper longum) contains volatile compounds like piperine and limonene. These oils contribute to the plant's pungent aroma and are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and digestive properties. They are often extracted through steam distillation and utilized in formulations for respiratory and gastrointestinal support.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Long Pepper (Piper longum). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds may support digestive health and immune function. Flavonoids are also associated with potential cardiovascular benefits.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Long Pepper (Piper longum). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. These compounds are responsible for the plant's distinctive aroma and are commonly used in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Long Pepper (Piper longum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports digestive health and may help in managing oxidative stress. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Long Pepper in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound found in Long Pepper (Piper longum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to aid in digestive health. Tannins may also have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting traditional uses of Long Pepper in herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is an active compound found in Long Pepper (Piper longum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has potential anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant effects. Coumarin is used in traditional herbal medicine to support digestive health and may aid in reducing blood clotting. Its presence in Long Pepper highlights the plant's therapeutic value in herbal formulations.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Long Pepper (Piper longum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in Long Pepper are known for their potential therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Long Pepper (Piper longum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Saponin also aids in improving digestion and may support respiratory health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Long Pepper in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Long Pepper (Piper longum). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties. These compounds may support overall health by neutralizing free radicals. Anthocyanin is also studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and cardioprotective effects.
