Senecio graciliflorus

What's the taxonomical classification of Senecio graciliflorus?

Seneciio graciliflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Senecio, with its unique designation being the species graciliflorus.

    Senecio graciliflorus is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the specific therapeutic application intended. Liquid extracts are frequently derived by boiling the plant parts in water to concentrate its active constituents. These aqueous solutions can also be administered as tinctures through alcohol-based extraction methods for long-term storage. For localized skin treatments, the fresh leaves are often crushed into a fine pulp to form a direct poultice. Each method is carefully selected to balance the potency of the plant's phytochemical properties with the desired delivery mechanism.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Senecio graciliflorus can cause severe liver damage, gastrointestinal distress, and potential death if ingested due to the presence of toxic pyrrolizidine alkaloids. These chemical compounds are known to accumulate in the liver over time, leading to progressive hepatic necrosis or cirrhosis. Symptoms of toxicity often include vomiting, loss of appetite, and significant weakness. Prolonged exposure can result in irreversible organ failure even if the initial dose seems small. Because these toxins are systemic, they pose a serious risk to both humans and animals who consume any part of the plant.
