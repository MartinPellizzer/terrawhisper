Carum ajowan

Carum ajowan, commonly known as bishop's weed or ajwain, is a small annual herb belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

This plant has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, particularly in Ayurvedic and Unani practices, and is valued for its numerous health benefits.

The health benefits of Carum ajowan include its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which can help alleviate digestive issues, reduce pain and inflammation, and boost the immune system.

The bioactive constituents of Carum ajowan include carvone, limonene, and thymol, which are responsible for its therapeutic actions, including its ability to stimulate digestion, reduce anxiety, and exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption of carum ajowan may also interact with other medications, exacerbating their side effects or reducing their efficacy.
