Symphytum

Symphytum orientale

Symphytum (Symphytum orientale) is a perennial herb known for its healing properties, particularly in the treatment of bone and joint conditions.

Botanical Identification

Symphytum orientale, also known as oriental comfrey, belongs to the Boraginaceae family. It is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Europe. The plant has thick, fleshy roots, broad heart-shaped leaves, and violet-blue flowers. Common synonyms include Symphytum officinale and Russian comfrey.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Scrophulariales
  • Family: Scrophulariaceae
  • Genus: Symphytum
  • Species: Symphytum orientale

Active Compounds

Symphytum orientale contains key active compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties by reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair. Preparation Methods

Powder: Used for digestive issues and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments due to its soothing and antimicrobial properties.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and inflammation due to its concentrated active compounds.

Safety Profile

Symphytum orientale contains toxic alkaloids and should not be used during pregnancy or by individuals with hypersensitivity to the plant. It may cause gastrointestinal upset and skin irritation. Use under professional supervision to avoid adverse effects.
