What's the taxonomical classification of Fagopyrum urophyllum?

Fagopyrum urophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Polygonaceae. Finally, its taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its placement within the genus Fagopyrum, specifically identifying it as the species urophyllum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fagopyrum urophyllum has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by ovate to lanceolate leaves with distinctively serrated margins and a somewhat wilted or drooping appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. The plant produces small, angular achenes that serve as its primary seed type. These morphological features distinguish it from other members of the Polygonaceae family.
