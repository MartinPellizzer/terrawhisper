Delphinium forrestii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium forrestii has a perennial growth habit characterized by upright, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights between 30 and 60 centimeters. The foliage consists of deeply lobed, palmate leaves that are dark green in color and provide a lush base for the flowering stalks. Its inflorescence features clusters of zygomorphic flowers that exhibit a striking deep blue to violet hue. Each individual blossom is marked by a prominent nectar spur at the rear, which is a defining morphological trait of the genus. The plant's structural arrangement allows it to thrive in moist, high-altitude environments where its sturdy stems can support heavy blooms.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Delphinium forrestii can cause severe poisoning characterized by cardiac arrhythmias, respiratory failure, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains potent neurotoxins and alkaloids that disrupt the central nervous system and heart rhythm. Symptoms often include nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain shortly after consumption. Immediate medical intervention is required following any accidental exposure to the plant's toxic components.
