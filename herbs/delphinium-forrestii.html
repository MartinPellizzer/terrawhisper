What's the taxonomical classification of Delphinium forrestii?

Delphinium forrestii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Delphinium, and its species designation, forrestii.

    What are the herbal preparations of Delphinium forrestii?

    Delphinium forrestii is prepared as a decoction, a tincture, or a topical poultice depending on the specific medicinal application intended. These preparations typically utilize the dried roots or leaves to extract active alkaloids. When making a decoction, the plant material is boiled in water to create a potent liquid extract. Tinctures are created by soaking the plant in high-proof alcohol to preserve the chemical constituents for long-term use. Topical poultices involve crushing the fresh plant matter into a paste to be applied directly to the skin.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Delphinium forrestii can cause severe poisoning characterized by cardiac arrhythmias, respiratory failure, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains potent neurotoxins and alkaloids that disrupt the central nervous system and heart rhythm. Symptoms often include nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain shortly after consumption. In more critical cases, a person may experience muscle tremors, seizures, or a dangerous drop in blood pressure. Immediate medical intervention is required following any accidental exposure to the plant's toxic components.
