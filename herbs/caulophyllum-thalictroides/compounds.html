Caulophyllum Cohosh (Caulophyllum thalictroides) Compounds

Caulophyllum cohosh contains several active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Coumarin contributes to its mild sedative effects. Tannins and phenolic acids provide astringent and anti-inflammatory actions. Glycosides and saponins support digestive health, while alkaloids and terpenoids contribute to its overall therapeutic profile.

Caulophyllum Cohosh (Caulophyllum thalictroides) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of compounds found in Caulophyllum thalictroides. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in women's health, particularly for menstrual support. They possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which may help alleviate symptoms associated with hormonal imbalances.

2. Coumarin

Caulophyllum thalictroides contains coumarin, a key active compound known for its antispasmodic and sedative properties. Coumarin contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating menstrual cramps and nervous system disorders. It also exhibits mild anticoagulant effects, though its therapeutic applications require careful consideration due to potential side effects.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Caulophyllum thalictroides. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support menstrual health. Tannins also help reduce inflammation and can aid in digestive issues. These effects make tannin an important component in herbal formulations using Caulophyllum Cohosh.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Caulophyllum thalictroides. It contributes to the plant's estrogenic properties and may support hormonal balance. This compound is associated with the plant's traditional use in women's health. It plays a role in the plant's therapeutic effects.

5. Glycoside

Caulophyllum thalictroides contains the glycoside actein, which is responsible for its uterine toning effects. Actein supports hormonal balance and may alleviate menstrual discomfort. It is a key constituent in traditional herbal formulations for women's health.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Caulophyllum thalictroides. It contributes to the plant's traditional use as a uterine tonic and mild sedative. Saponins may support hormonal balance and have mild anti-inflammatory properties. They are responsible for some of the plant's therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Caulophyllum thalictroides contains the terpenoid compound called cauloside. This terpenoid contributes to the plant's traditional use in women's health, particularly for menstrual support and menopausal symptoms. It is one of the key constituents responsible for the herb's therapeutic effects.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Caulophyllum thalictroides. It contributes to the plant's traditional use for menstrual disorders and menopausal symptoms. The oil contains volatile compounds like methyl salicylate, which may have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. It is typically extracted through steam distillation from the plant's roots and rhizomes.

9. Alkaloid

Caulophyllum thalictroides contains the alkaloid protopine, which contributes to its medicinal properties. Protopine is known for its antispasmodic and sedative effects, making it relevant in herbal formulations for menstrual disorders and nervous system support. This compound is a key component in the traditional use of Caulophyllum Cohosh.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are flavonoid compounds found in Caulophyllum thalictroides. They contribute to the plant's deep blue color and may support cardiovascular and circulatory health. These compounds are believed to enhance the plant's traditional use in women's health formulas.
