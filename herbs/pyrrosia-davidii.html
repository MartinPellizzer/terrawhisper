Pyrrosia davidii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia davidii has perennial, creeping rhizomes that produce small, opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and a slightly pubescent texture. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear inconspicuous, actinomorphic flowers. These flowers typically exhibit a tubular corolla with five lobes and are often pale or whitish in color. The inflorescence is usually arranged in small clusters or solitary terminal positions. Mature seed capsules are small and dehisce to release minute seeds for dispersal.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pyrrosia davidii can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested.
