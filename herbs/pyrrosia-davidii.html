What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrrosia davidii?

Pyrrosia davidii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is a representative of the family Polypodiaceae, situated within the genus Pyrrosia, and is specifically identified by its species name, davidii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrrosia davidii has perennial, creeping rhizomes that produce small, opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and a slightly pubescent texture.

The plant features slender, upright stems that bear inconspicuous, actinomorphic flowers. These flowers typically exhibit a tubular corolla with five lobes and are often pale or whitish in color. The inflorescence is usually arranged in small clusters or solitary terminal positions.

Mature seed capsules are small and dehisce to release minute seeds for dispersal.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Pyrrosia davidii can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap often leads to contact dermatitis, characterized by redness and itching. Some individuals may experience allergic reactions that manifest as rashes or hives upon touching the leaves. If the plant is consumed accidentally, it can result in nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when handling or being near the species.
