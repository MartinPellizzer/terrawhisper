Astragalus balfourianus

What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus balfourianus?

Astragalus balfourianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary path continues through the order Fabales, placing it firmly within the family Fabaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by the genus Astragalus and is specifically designated as the species balfourianus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus balfourianus has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by spreading, woody rhizomes and pinnately compound leaves consisting of seven to fifteen small, oval leaflets. The foliage is typically covered in fine, silvery hairs that provide a soft texture to the plant. Its inflorescence features dense clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink or lavender. These flowers emerge from short stalks and are supported by stems that can reach heights of up to thirty centimeters. The seed pods are small, inflated, and oblong, eventually turning dark as they mature.
