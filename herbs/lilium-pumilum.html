What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium pumilum?

Lilium pumilum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Liliales and is a member of the family Liliaceae. Ultimately, its scientific designation is defined by the genus Lilium and the specific species pumilum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium pumilum has slender, upright stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and bear several lanceolate, bright green leaves arranged in whorls or spirals. The flowers are relatively small and nodding, featuring six tepals that exhibit a pale yellowish to creamy white coloration often marked with small reddish-brown spots. These blooms possess delicate, protruding stamens and a central pistil that are characteristic of the genus. The bulb serves as the underground storage organ, consisting of fleshy scales that allow the plant to survive dormant periods.
