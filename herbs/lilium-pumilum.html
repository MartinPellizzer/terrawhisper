Little Lily (Lilium pumilum)

Little Lily (Lilium pumilum) is a member of the Liliaceae family, native to North America, Canada, and United States. Traditionally, its bulbs, flowers, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and sedative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface pale green and lower surface dark green, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Radial symmetry, white to pale yellow flowers with six tepals, arranged in solitary umbels, featuring a prominent central stamen cluster and three lobed stigmatic surfaces.
Stems
Erect, herbaceous growth habit with opposite branching, smooth, glabrous surface, and nodes bearing adventitious root scars.
