Water Mint (Mentha Aquatica Subsp. Lanigera)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Mentha aquatica subsp. lanigera.

Water mint, scientifically known as Mentha aquatica subsp. lanigera, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, prized for its medicinal and aromatic properties. This adaptogen and medicinal herb is known for its refreshing, minty flavor and its rich content of essential oils, particularly menthol and menthone, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. Traditionally used in folk medicine to soothe digestive issues, relieve headaches, and promote respiratory health, water mint has also been incorporated into culinary practices as a spice to enhance the flavor of teas, salads, and sauces. In modern wellness, it is utilized in aromatherapy, herbal teas, and topical applications to ease muscle tension and reduce stress. Its unique combination of historical significance and contemporary relevance makes water mint a versatile plant with enduring value in both traditional and modern health practices.
