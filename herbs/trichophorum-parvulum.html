Trichophorum Parvulum

Trichophorum parvulum, commonly known as the small hairgrass, is a small, tufted grass species that has been traditionally used in various medicinal systems for its potential therapeutic properties.

It is primarily valued for its ability to promote wound healing and reduce inflammation due to the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids. In traditional herbal medicine, Trichophorum parvulum has been employed to treat skin conditions, including eczema and minor wounds, owing to its antimicrobial and astringent effects. Recent scientific studies suggest that it may also possess antioxidant properties that contribute to its overall health benefits.
