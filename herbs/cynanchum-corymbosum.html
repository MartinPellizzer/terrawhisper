Cynanchum corymbosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum corymbosum has climbing, twining stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves and small, actinomorphic white flowers arranged in dense, terminal corymbose cymes. The foliage is characterized by smooth margins and a somewhat fleshy texture typical of many milkweeds. Its inflorescences consist of numerous tiny blossoms clustered tightly at the ends of the branches. The plant develops specialized structures known as pollinia to facilitate its reproductive process.
