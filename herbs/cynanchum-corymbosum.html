Cynanchum corymbosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum corymbosum?

Cynanchum corymbosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae, falling under the genus Cynanchum, and is specifically identified by the species name corymbosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum corymbosum has climbing, twining stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves and small, actinomorphic white flowers arranged in dense, terminal corymbose cymes.

The foliage is characterized by smooth margins and a somewhat fleshy texture typical of many milkweeds. Its inflorescences consist of numerous tiny blossoms clustered tightly at the ends of the branches. The plant develops specialized structures known as pollinia to facilitate its reproductive process.
