Mikania cordata

What's the taxonomical classification of Mikania cordata?

Mikania cordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, which is one of the most diverse families of flowering plants. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Mikania, and its specific biological designation is completed by the species name cordata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mikaia cordata has climbing, herbaceous stems that produce heart-shaped, opposite leaves with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage typically measures several centimeters in length and features a smooth, slightly pubescent texture on the surface. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular, yellowish-white flowers that emerge from axillary clusters. The plant relies on these vine-like structures to scramble over surrounding vegetation in tropical environments. These morphological traits allow the species to effectively colonize forest edges and disturbed habitats.

What are the therapeutic actions of Mikania cordata?

Mikania cordata has therapeutic properties that include being an astringent, hemostatic, vulnerary, and cicatrisant. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including an alkaloidal fraction and the specific diterpene known as scandenolide. These chemical constituents are responsible for the biological activities observed in various pharmacological studies. The alkaloidal fraction provides a complex mixture of nitrogenous substances that interact with different physiological receptors. Within this chemical profile, scandenolide serves as a key marker for the plant's unique therapeutic potential. Researchers study these specific molecules to understand how they might be used in developing new pharmaceutical treatments.

As discussed in a study, Mikania cordata contains an alkaloidal fraction that demonstrates anti-ulcerogenic effects. In experimental groups, the administration of 50 mg kg(-1) of this alkaloidal fraction significantly increased pH values in the stomach to 3.09 +/- 0.0429 compared to 2.07 +/- 0.0339 and in the duodenum to 6.79 +/- 0.1162 compared to 6.19 +/- 0.1273. The ulcer index for the stomach in the alkaloidal-administered group was 0.268 +/- 0.0346, which was lower than the 0.691 +/- 0.0184 seen in the diclofenac group. Similarly, the duodenal ulcer index was 0.050 +/- 0.0129 compared to 0.0933 +/- 0.0138. Histological grading showed 0 lesions in the alkaloidal-administered group compared to 5 lesions in the diclofenac-only group.
