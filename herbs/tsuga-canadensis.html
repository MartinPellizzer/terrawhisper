One of the most significant pests affecting T. canadensis is the hemlock woolly adelgid (Adelges tsugae), an aphid-like insect that feeds on the tree's sap, causing yellowing or bronzing of the needles and eventually defoliation and dieback. Other common pests include spider mites, scales, and borers, which can cause damage to the bark, leaves, and roots. Additionally, leaf spots and rusts caused by pathogens like Septoria and Puccinia spp. can cause discoloration and defoliation of the needles, while canker diseases like Phomopsis spp. can infect the trunk and branches, leading to deadwood formation and reduced growth rates.

Effective management strategies involve integrated pest management techniques, including regular monitoring, pruning, and application of fungicides or insecticides as needed.
