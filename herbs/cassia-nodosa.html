Cassia nodosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Cassia nodosa?

Cassia nodosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Cassia and the species nodosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cassia nodosa has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small, oblong leaflets and bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that grow in large terminal racemes. The tree typically reaches heights of up to 15 meters, characterized by a spreading crown and a relatively short trunk. Its bark is generally smooth and light gray, often becoming slightly fissured as the specimen matures. The fruit consists of long, cylindrical, woody pods that hang from the branches and turn dark brown upon ripening. These pods eventually dehisce to release small, dark seeds into the surrounding environment.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of warm, frost-free climates typical of the Neotropical realm. Because it prefers subtropical and tropical moisture, it rarely extends into more arid or temperate latitudes.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause diarrhea, abdominal cramps, nausea, and gastrointestinal irritation when consumed. These adverse reactions typically occur because the plant contains potent anthraquinone glycosides that act as stimulant laxatives. As these compounds irritate the lining of the intestines, they trigger increased peristalsis and water secretion. Such intense stimulation of the digestive tract often leads to painful cramping and urgent bowel movements. Users should exercise caution to avoid these unpleasant systemic effects.
