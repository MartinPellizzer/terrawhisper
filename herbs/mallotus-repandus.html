Mallotus repandus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mallotus repandus has prostrate or climbing stems with simple or branched leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and possess entire or slightly wavy margins. The leaves are characterized by an acute or acuminate apex and a cuneate base, often displaying a somewhat leathery texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, leading to the development of small, globose capsules. These capsules eventually release tiny seeds that are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. The overall growth habit allows the plant to spread across the forest floor or climb over surrounding vegetation.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of the Philippines. It is primarily found growing in lowland rainforest environments and secondary forests where humidity remains high. Within these ecosystems, the species tends to occupy various strata of the vegetation, often clinging to trees or forest edges. Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of warm, moist climates characteristic of the equatorial belt.
