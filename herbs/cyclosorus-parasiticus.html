Cyclosorus parasiticus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyclosorus parasiticus?

Cyclosorus parasiticus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This specific plant is a representative of the family Aspleniaceae, falling under the genus Cyclosorus, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, parasiticus.

What are the herbal preparations of Cyclosorus parasiticus?

Cyclosorus parasiticus is prepared as a concentrated decoction, a dried powder for topical application, or an infused tincture. The decoction method involves boiling the plant material in water to extract its potent bioactive compounds. When used as a powder, the plant is often mixed with oils or fats to create a healing salve for skin ailments. Tinctures are made by soaking the plant parts in high-proof alcohol to ensure long-term stability and ease of dosing. These diverse forms allow for both internal consumption and external therapeutic use depending on the specific ailment being treated.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cyclosorus parasiticus can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and acute nausea if any part of the plant is ingested. Contact with the leaves often triggers an immediate inflammatory response characterized by red, itchy rashes. Some individuals may experience more serious systemic reactions such as dizziness or mild hallucinations following accidental exposure. Long-term contact with the sap is known to cause persistent dermatitis that requires medical intervention. These adverse effects are typically most intense in sensitive populations or during high concentrations of the plant.
