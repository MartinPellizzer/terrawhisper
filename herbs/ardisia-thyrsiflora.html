What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia thyrsiflora has elliptical to lanceolate leaves with entire margins, small white tubular flowers arranged in dense axillary clusters, and fleshy drupes that turn red or black upon maturity. The foliage is typically evergreen, displaying a glossy texture and prominent pinnate venation on the upper surface. Its inflorescence consists of numerous tiny blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils to form compact cymes. The fruit develops from these flowers into rounded berries that serve as a primary dispersal mechanism. These morphological traits are characteristic of the Rubiaceae family to which this species belongs.
