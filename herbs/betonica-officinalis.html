Betonica Officinalis (Betonica officinalis)

Betonica Officinalis (Betonica officinalis) is a member of the Labiatae family, native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, bitter, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 80 centimeters.
Leaves
Opposite, elliptic leaves with dark green upper surface and lighter green lower surface, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescence consists of solitary flowers with blue-violet petals arranged in a whorled pattern, each flower exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry and possessing five lobed petals with dark purple markings along the midvein.
Stems
Erect growth habit with opposite branching, glabrous surface, and presence of longitudinal ridges.
