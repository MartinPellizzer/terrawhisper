Pinellia pedatisecta

What's the taxonomical classification of Pinellia pedatisecta?

Pinellia pedatisecta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Alismatales. This species is a member of the family Araceae and falls under the genus Pinellia, specifically identified by its species name, pedatisecta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pinellia pedatisecta has deeply lobed leaves that are typically divided into five to seven distinct segments with serrated margins. The leaf arrangement is alternate along a slender, upright stem that can reach varying heights depending on the environment. Each lobe is lanceolate in shape and tapers to a sharp point at the apex. The texture of the foliage is somewhat leathery, providing a durable surface for the plant. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically spanning across parts of northern India, Nepal, Bhutan, and southwestern China. Its range is primarily restricted to alpine and subalpine zones where rocky terrain and sparse vegetation are common. Populations are often found growing at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters above sea level. These specific mountain corridors provide the cool, well-drained environments necessary for its survival. The distribution follows the rugged contours of the Himalayan mountain chain throughout its southern and eastern reaches.

What are the pharmacological activities of Pinellia pedatisecta?

This plant has antibacterial and phytotoxic pharmacological activities that stem from its unique chemical composition. The antibacterial properties allow the plant to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms through specific biochemical pathways. Its phytotoxic effects are characterized by the ability to interfere with the physiological processes of target plant species. Researchers study these biological actions to understand how the plant's secondary metabolites interact with living cells. These multifaceted activities make the species a significant subject for further pharmacological investigation.

According to a study published by "BMC microbiology", endophytic fungi were isolated from the roots, stems, leaves, and tubers of Pinellia pedatisecta. From the fungus Aspergillus floccosus PP39 of P. pedatisecta, three compounds were isolated, including terreic acid (5), terremutin (6), and citrinin (7). Compound 5 exhibited strong antibacterial activities against Escherichia coli, Micrococcus tetragenus, Staphylococcus aureus, and Pseudomonas syringae pv. actinidiae with inhibition zone diameters of 36.0, 31.0, 33.7, and 40.2 mm. The minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) values for these bacteria were 1.56, 3.13, 1.56, and 1.56 μg/mL, respectively. Additionally, compound 7 showed strong antibacterial activity against P. syringae pv. actinidiae with an inhibition zone diameter of 26.0 mm and a MIC value of 6.25 μg/mL. Compound 7 also demonstrated potent phytotoxic activity against E. crusgalli with a 73.4% inhibition rate at a concentration of 100 μg/mL.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds pedatisectine F, pedatisectine G, citriniin, terreic acid, and terremutin. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for much of the biological activity observed in the species. Researchers often study these substances to understand their potential pharmacological properties. The presence of such diverse secondary metabolites characterizes the unique chemical profile of the plant. Such findings contribute to the ongoing scientific investigation of natural products derived from this flora.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", five compounds were isolated from the alkaloid extract of Rhizoma Pinelliae Pedatisecta. These identified structures include pedatisectine F(I), hypoxanthine(II), erythritol(III), uridine(IV) and pedatisectine G(V). Among these, compounds I and V are identified as new compounds. Furthermore, compounds II, III and IV were found in this plant for the first time.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating skin irritation, inflammation, and various digestive ailments. Its medicinal properties are often utilized to soothe localized discomfort and reduce redness on the surface of the skin. Additionally, practitioners may use it to calm internal swelling and promote overall gastrointestinal health. The bioactive compounds within the plant work to alleviate these specific symptoms effectively.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", the study investigated whether the raphide of calcium oxalate in Pinelliia pedatisecta Schott caused irritation. Researchers compared unprocessed Pinelliia pedatisecta to samples processed for 36 h, 72 h, and 120 h to observe structural modifications. The time course study observed great modifications in the structure of raphide, specifically for those with barbs in the processed samples. An animal experimental study demonstrated that the rate of change for raphide with barbs existed in a dose-reponse relationship to irritation. These investigations showed that the raphide of calcium oxalate is at least in part responsible for the irritation of Pinelliia pedatisecta.
