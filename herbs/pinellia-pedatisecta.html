What's the taxonomical classification of Pinellia pedatisecta?

Pinellia pedatisecta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Alismatales. This species is a member of the family Araceae and falls under the genus Pinellia, specifically identified by its species name, pedatisecta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pinellia pedatisecta has deeply lobed leaves that are typically divided into five to seven distinct segments with serrated margins. The leaf arrangement is alternate along a slender, upright stem that can reach varying heights depending on the environment. Each lobe is lanceolate in shape and tapers to a sharp point at the apex. The texture of the foliage is somewhat leathery, providing a durable surface for the plant. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season.
