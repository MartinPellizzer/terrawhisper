Botanical Identification of Pfaffia (Pfaffia paniculata)

To identify Pfaffia (Pfaffia paniculata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Pfaffia paniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Araliaceae, genus Pfaffia, and species Pfaffia paniculata. These classifications place it within the flowering plants and the broader group of eudicots.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Pfaffia paniculata is the accepted scientific name for this species. It was first described by the botanist Johann Friedrich Pfaff, hence the genus name Pfaffia. The species name paniculata refers to the paniculate inflorescence characteristic of the plant. This name is widely recognized in botanical literature and is used to distinguish it from related species.

Morphological Characteristics

Pfaffia paniculata is a perennial herb with a rosette growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it to the soil. The stems are upright and slightly hairy, while the leaves are lance-shaped with a pointed tip. The inflorescence is a branched cluster of small white flowers. The fruit is a dry capsule containing several small, dark seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Pfaffia paniculata can be distinguished by its distinctive yellowish-green stems with sparse hairs, lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and yellow flowers arranged in terminal panicles. It differs from similar species through its leaf shape, flowering pattern, and specific habitat preferences. Key features include the presence of stipules and the overall plant structure.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Pfaffia paniculata is native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and is typically found at low to mid-altitude regions, usually between 500 and 1,500 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Pfaffia paniculata blooms from late summer to early autumn, typically between July and September in its native range. Seed formation occurs in the following spring, around April to May. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during its flowering stage, which is critical for accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Pfaffia paniculata belongs to the Asteraceae family, sharing traits like composite flowers and fibrous roots with other members. It is closely related to species in the genus Heterotheca, differing mainly in leaf morphology and flower structure. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary links to other sunflower relatives.
