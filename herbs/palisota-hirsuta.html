Palisota hirsuta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Palisota hirsuta has a perennial, climbing habit characterized by its large, ovate, or elliptical leaves that possess a distinctly hairy or hirsute texture on both surfaces. The leaves are arranged alternately along a flexible, twining stem that allows the plant to ascend through surrounding vegetation. Its inflorescence is typically a spike-like raceme that emerges from the leaf axils, bearing small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant also develops specialized adventitious roots that assist in anchoring the climbing structure to its host. These morphological features collectively enable the species to thrive in the shaded, humid environments of tropical forests.
