Adina pilulifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Adina pilulifera?

Adina pilulifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Adina, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Adina pilulifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adiina pilulifera has pinnately compound leaves typically consisting of 7 to 15 leaflets, small yellowish-white flowers arranged in axillary panicles, and small drupaceous fruits covered in fine, bristly hairs. The leaflets are generally lanceolate or ovate in shape with serrated margins and acute tips. The stems exhibit a woody structure and are often covered in fine pubescence depending on the age of the plant. Each flowering panicle serves as a cluster for numerous tiny individual blossoms that eventually transition into the characteristic hairy fruit. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species within its specific taxonomic group.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds morroniside, sarracenin, 3beta-O-beta-D-xylopyranosyl (1 --> 3)-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-pyrocincholic acid 28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl (1 --> 6)-beta-D-glucopyranosyl ester, and 3beta-O-beta-D-xylopyranosyl(1 --> 3)-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-cincholic acid 28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl ester. These specific chemical constituents are often studied for their unique biological activities and potential therapeutic applications. The complex glycosylated structures of the cincholic acid derivatives represent highly specialized secondary metabolites. Such diverse compounds contribute to the overall chemical profile of the plant species. Researchers analyze these molecules to understand how they interact with human biological systems.

As discussed in a study, the chemical constituents of Adina pilulifera were isolated and characterized. Two compounds were obtained from the ethyl acetate fraction of the ethanol extract of Adina pilulifera, while one compound was obtained from the n-butanol fraction of the ethanol extract. These compounds were identified through spectroscopic analysis as sarracenin (1), 2-methyl-5, 7-dihydroxychromone (2), and morroniside (3). Except for compound 2, the other two compounds were isolated from this plant for the first time. Additionally, these two compounds were also obtained from the Adina genus for the first time.
