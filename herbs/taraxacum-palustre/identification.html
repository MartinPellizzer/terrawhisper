Botanical Identification of Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre)

To identify Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Water Dandelion, Taraxacum palustre, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Compositae, and it is part of the genus Taraxacum. The species name palustre refers to its habitat in wet or marshy areas. The other classification levels—division, class, and order—are not specified in the provided data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Water Dandelion is Taraxacum palustre, first described by Carl von Linné. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is part of the genus Taraxacum. This species is also known by several synonyms, including Leontopodium palustre and Taraxacum aquaticum, reflecting historical taxonomic revisions.

Morphological Characteristics

Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre) is a perennial herb with a rosette growth habit. It has a taproot system and a cylindrical, hairy stem that rises from the base. Its leaves are spoon-shaped, with a waxy coating and a serrated edge. The inflorescence is a single flower head surrounded by bracts. The flowers are yellow with a central disk, and the fruit is a spiky achene with a pappus for wind dispersal.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Water Dandelion can be distinguished by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering stem with yellow disk flowers. It lacks the fuzzy leaves of common dandelions and has a more robust, upright growth habit. It is typically found in wetter habitats, which helps differentiate it from similar species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre) is native to northern regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. It thrives in temperate climates with cool, moist conditions. It prefers wet, clay-rich soils and is commonly found in marshes and along waterways. This plant typically grows at low to moderate altitudes, often near freshwater habitats.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Water Dandelion blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the blooming period when the plant is most recognizable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre) belongs to the genus Taraxacum in the family Asteraceae. It is closely related to other species within the same genus, sharing similar morphological and genetic traits. Its phylogenetic position reflects its close ties to other dandelion-like plants in the Asteraceae family.
