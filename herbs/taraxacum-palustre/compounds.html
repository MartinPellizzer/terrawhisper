Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre) Compounds

Water Dandelion contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarins, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Tannins and saponins provide astringent effects, while essential oils and terpenoids support its aromatic and digestive benefits. Glycosides, anthocyanins, and alkaloids further enhance its medicinal profile.

Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in the leaves and roots, enhancing the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. Flavonoids may also aid in digestive health and skin conditions.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce oxidative stress and supports the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and skin conditions. It is one of several bioactive components responsible for the plant's therapeutic effects.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre). It contributes to the plant's diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin also acts as a mild sedative and may support digestive health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Water Dandelion in herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin in Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing. It also supports digestive health by binding to proteins and aiding in the elimination of toxins from the body.

5. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre). It contains volatile compounds like sesquiterpenes and monoterpenes, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This oil is often used in herbal medicine for its ability to support digestive health and skin conditions.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in digestive and respiratory health. They also enhance the plant's overall therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties. Saponins help support liver function and may aid in the elimination of toxins from the body. They are also believed to enhance the immune system.

8. Glycoside

Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre) contains the glycoside taraxacine, which contributes to its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting urinary health and reducing swelling.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre), is a pigment with strong antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's potential health benefits by neutralizing free radicals and supporting cellular health. This compound may aid in reducing inflammation and promoting overall wellness.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Water Dandelion (Taraxacum palustre). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and may have mild stimulant properties. While research on its specific medicinal effects is limited, it is part of the complex array of bioactive compounds in this herb.
