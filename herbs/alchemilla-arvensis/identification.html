Botanical Identification of Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla arvensis)

To identify Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla arvensis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Lady's Mantle, or Alchemilla arvensis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Alchemilla, and species Alchemilla arvensis. Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Lady's Mantle is Alchemilla arvensis, first described by the botanist Carl von Linné. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known by several synonyms, including Alchemilla vulgaris and Alchemilla mollis. Common names such as Lady's Mantle, Alchemilla, and Buck's Horn reflect its historical and regional significance in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla arvensis) is a low-growing perennial with a compact, mat-like growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that spreads horizontally, anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients. The stems are short and often lie close to the ground, supporting the foliage. The leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in dense umbels. The fruit is a three-lobed achene, with a pappus that aids in wind dispersal.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Lady's Mantle can be distinguished by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It has a distinctive leaf arrangement and lacks the fuzzy texture found in similar species like Alchemilla mollis. The stems are smooth and often reddish, and the plant produces numerous stamens in its flower clusters.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla arvensis) is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at low to mid-altitude regions, typically between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla arvensis) blooms from June to August, producing yellowish-green flowers. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested in late spring to early summer when the leaves are fully developed and the flowers are in bloom.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla arvensis) belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing traits with other genera like Rubus and Rosa. It is closely related to species such as Alchemilla mollis and Alchemilla vulgaris. These relationships are reflected in its morphology and genetic makeup, which align with broader phylogenetic groupings within the Rosaceae.
