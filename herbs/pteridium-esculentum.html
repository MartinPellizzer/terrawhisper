Pteridium esculentum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pteridium esculentum?

Pteridium esculentum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This plant is a representative of the family Dennstaedtiaceae, falling under the genus Pteridium, with its specific designation being the species esculentum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteridium esculentum has a creeping rhizome that produces large, triangular, deciduous fronds which can reach up to one meter in length. The leaves are typically bipinnate or tripinnate, featuring deeply lobed pinnules with serrated margins. A distinguishing characteristic is the presence of small, dark scales covering the stipe and rachis. The underside of the fertile fronds contains clusters of sporangia organized into circular sori. These structures allow the plant to reproduce via spores released from the specialized underside of the foliage.
