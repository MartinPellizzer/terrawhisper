Dalbergia pinnata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia pinnata has a medium-sized tree habit characterized by a spreading crown, pinnately compound leaves, and small, fragrant, cream-colored flowers. The leaves typically consist of several pairs of leaflets that are ovate or elliptical in shape with entire margins. Its bark is usually greyish or brownish and can become fissured as the specimen matures. The plant produces small, flat, indehiscent pods that contain a single seed. This species is often distinguished by its distinctive woody structure and fine-textured foliage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically covering territories in India, Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Thailand, and parts of Indonesia. It thrives primarily in seasonally dry deciduous forests and scrublands where moisture levels fluctuate throughout the year. The species is frequently found in lowland areas and along riverbanks where the soil remains well-drained yet fertile. Its distribution is closely linked to specific climate patterns that support the growth of woody legumes in warm environments. Consequently, its presence is most concentrated in areas characterized by monsoon weather cycles.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Dalbergia pinnata targets cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) enzymes, lipoxygenase (LOX) proteins, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines such as tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) to exert its therapeutic effects. The secondary metabolites present in the plant, including flavonoids and polyphenols, act as potent inhibitors of these specific inflammatory mediators. By suppressing the activity of COX-2, the plant reduces the production of prostaglandins, which helps alleviate pain and swelling. Additionally, its ability to modulate oxidative stress through the inhibition of NADPH oxidase contributes to its antioxidant properties. These interactions at the protein level explain the plant's documented efficacy in treating inflammatory conditions and skin disorders.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dalbergia pinnata?

Dalbergia pinnata has therapeutic properties including astringent, hemostatic, anti-inflammatory, antidiarheal, vulnerary, and antipyretic actions. Its astringent and hemostatic qualities make it effective for controlling bleeding and tightening tissues. The plant functions as an antidiarheal agent to manage gastrointestinal distress by reducing fluid loss. It also acts as an anti-inflammatory and antipyretic to lower fevers and soothe swelling throughout the body. Furthermore, its vulnerary properties support the healing of wounds and damaged skin.

What are the herbal preparations of Dalbergia pinnata?

Dalbergia pinnata is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, and pastes using its bark, leaves, and flowers to treat various ailments. The bark is often boiled in water to create a potent liquid extract used for its astringent properties. Leaves can be crushed into a fresh paste for topical application on skin inflammations or wounds. Many traditional practitioners also dry the plant components to grind them into fine medicinal powders for oral consumption. These diverse preparations allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be utilized for both internal and external therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dalbergia pinnata can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling upon direct contact with its sap or crushed leaves. Some individuals may experience redness or a burning sensation where the plant touches the skin. If the plant material is ingested in large quantities, it might lead to digestive discomfort or gastrointestinal distress. While systemic toxicity is not extensively documented in humans, cautious handling is necessary to avoid hypersensitivity reactions. Always test a small area of skin before widespread exposure to ensure no adverse reaction occurs.
