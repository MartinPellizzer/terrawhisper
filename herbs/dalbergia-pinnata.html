Dalbergia pinnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Dalbergia pinnata?

Dalbergia pinnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its distinct legume-bearing properties. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Dalbergia and is specifically designated by the species name pinnata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia pinnata has a medium-sized tree habit characterized by a spreading crown, pinnately compound leaves, and small, fragrant, cream-colored flowers. The leaves typically consist of several pairs of leaflets that are ovate or elliptical in shape with entire margins. Its bark is usually greyish or brownish and can become fissured as the specimen matures. The plant produces small, flat, indehiscent pods that contain a single seed. This species is often distinguished by its distinctive woody structure and fine-textured foliage.

What are the pharmacological activities of Dalbergia pinnata?

This plant has pharmacological activities that include anxiety reduction, relaxation, and acting as a sleep aid. Its bioactive compounds interact with the central nervous system to help soothe an overactive mind. By promoting a sense of calm, the plant assists in lowering physiological tension throughout the body. These sedative properties make it an effective natural option for managing insomnia and restlessness. Consequently, regular use can help regulate sleep patterns and improve overall mental tranquility.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", the essential oil of Dalbergia pinnata was investigated for its effects on the electroencephalographic activity of 30 healthy volunteers consisting of 15 men and 15 women. Using 30 electrodes placed on the scalp, researchers found that delta waves increased by 20% in noiseless and white noise environments during the inhalation of the essential oil. In the presence of pink noise and traffic noise, alpha and delta wave activity in the frontal pole and frontal lobe increased markedly when inhaling the essential oil. These changes in brain activity may aid sleep, relaxation, and the reduction of anxiety. The study also noted that women are more likely to relax and get sleepy than men when inhaling the essential oil with different auditory stimuli.
