Gentiana filistyla

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana filistyla?

Gentiana filistyla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. The plant is further categorized into the family Gentianaceae and is specifically identified under the genus Gentiana, with its unique species designation being filistyla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana filistyla has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along a stem and funnel-shaped, pale blue to white flowers with a distinctively long, slender style. The foliage is typically small and slender, reflecting its adaptation to specific rocky or alpine habitats. Its corolla is characterized by a tubular structure that opens into lobes, often displaying subtle veining. The reproductive organs are notable for the elongated style that extends significantly from the floral tube.
