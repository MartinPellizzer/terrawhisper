Water Pennywort (Hydrocotyle asiatica): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Water Pennywort (Hydrocotyle asiatica) is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia, widely used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing properties.

What is Water Pennywort (Hydrocotyle Asiatica)?

Water Pennywort (Hydrocotyle asiatica) is a creeping, herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia, characterized by its small, shiny, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Water Pennywort is Hydrocotyle asiatica. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Araliaceae, genus Hydrocotyle, and species Hydrocotyle asiatica. It is commonly known as Water Pennywort in North America and Pennywort in Europe. The plant is also referred to as Asiatic Pennywort in some botanical references and as Gokulakka in parts of India.

Plant Description and Morphology

Water Pennywort is a perennial aquatic or semi-aquatic herb that typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm, with a sprawling or floating growth habit.

Its leaves are heart-shaped, broad, and often have a waxy texture, with a smooth or slightly lobed margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, usually appearing in late summer to early autumn. Its root system consists of rhizomes that help it spread and anchor in wet soil or submerged substrates.

The leaves and stems are usually green, with a mild, slightly bitter smell, and the texture is soft to the touch.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Water Pennywort (Hydrocotyle asiatica) is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries such as India, China, Japan, and Indonesia, and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves and stems of Water Pennywort (Hydrocotyle asiatica) are primarily used medicinally, with the leaves being most commonly harvested for their therapeutic properties.

Phytochemical Composition

Pharmacological Properties

Water Pennywort (Hydrocotyle asiatica) is traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat skin conditions, respiratory infections, and digestive issues due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antispasmodic properties.
