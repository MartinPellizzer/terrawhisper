The therapeutic actions of Hydrocotyle asiatica are attributed to its bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and tannins.

Side Effects of hydrocotyle asiatica

Hydrocotyle asiatica triggers allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as sneezing, runny nose, and even difficulty breathing in severe cases.

The herb can also trigger skin rash, characterized by redness, itching, and bumps, particularly in those with sensitive skin. Prolonged contact with the plant may cause skin irritation, resulting in dryness, flaking, or a burning sensation. Ingesting Hydrocotyle asiatica may result in nausea, accompanied by a loss of appetite and a feeling of fullness in the stomach.

Additionally, it can cause dry mouth, eye irritation, and throat irritation, which may lead to discomfort and a persistent cough. The herb is also known to lead to stomach pain and causes digestive issues such as bloating, gas, and indigestion. These side effects highlight the importance of caution when using Hydrocotyle asiatica, especially for individuals with known allergies or sensitive systems.

It is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating this herb into one's routine to minimize potential adverse effects.
