What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium cariniferum?

Dendrobium cariniferum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asparagales, it falls under the diverse family Orchidaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by its genus, Dendrobium, and is distinguished as the species cariniferum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium cariniferum has sympodial pseudobulbs that are clavate or fusiform in shape, characterized by a distinct longitudinal ridge along their surface. These thickened stems serve as primary water storage organs and are typically clothed in several dried, papery sheaths. The leaves are relatively small, coriaceous, and arranged distichously toward the apex of the pseudobulbs. Its inflorescence emerges from the upper nodes, producing small, fragrant flowers that often feature a specialized labellum. The floral structure is defined by a fleshy texture and a specific keel-like formation that gives the species its name.
