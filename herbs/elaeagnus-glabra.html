Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Elaeagnus, and its specific species designation, glabra.

What are the herbal preparations of Elaeagnus glabra?

Elaeagnus glabra is prepared as a decoction from the bark, a tea from the leaves, or a topical poultice using crushed plant material. Leaf preparations are commonly brewed into mild infusions to support digestive health or skin soothing. In some traditional practices, the berries are consumed directly or processed into syrups and jams. These various forms allow for the utilization of different chemical constituents present throughout the plant.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Elaeagnus glabra can cause skin irritation and allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals through direct contact with its foliage or bark. While the plant is not classified as highly toxic, some people may experience redness, itching, or a localized rash upon touching the leaves. There is also a potential for gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is accidentally ingested by humans or pets. Most adverse reactions are superficial and typically resolve once the irritant is removed from the skin. Monitoring for these signs is recommended when working closely with the shrub in a garden setting.
