Burasaia madagascariensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Burasaia madagascariensis has woody, branched stems and elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely or in whorls along the branches. The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture with prominent pinnate venation on the undersides. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often appearing in shades of white or cream. These blossoms eventually give rise to fleshy, indehiscent fruits that serve as a primary characteristic of the genus. The overall structure of the plant reflects its adaptation to the specific environmental conditions found within its native Malagasy habitats.
