Burasaia madagascariensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Burasaia madagascariensis?

Burasaia madagascariensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. More specifically, this plant is situated in the family Menispermaceae, which contains the genus Burasaia. The taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its specific designation as the species madagascariensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Burasaia madagascariensis has woody, branched stems and elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely or in whorls along the branches. The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture with prominent pinnate venation on the undersides. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often appearing in shades of white or cream. These blossoms eventually give rise to fleshy, indehiscent fruits that serve as a primary characteristic of the genus.
