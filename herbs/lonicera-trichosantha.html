Lonicera trichosantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera trichosantha has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and a glabrous surface. The stems of this deciduous shrub are typically woody and can grow in a sprawling or arching habit. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in pairs or small clusters. These blossoms are characterized by their long, slender corolla tubes and pale yellowish or white coloration. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that turn a dark color when ripe.
