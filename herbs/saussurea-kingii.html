What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea kingii?

Saussurea kingii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the order Asterales. It is further categorized under the family Asteraceae, a large group of flowering plants, and is specifically placed within the genus Saussurea. Ultimately, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species kingii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea kingii has a perennial habit characterized by basal rosette-forming leaves and erect, unbranched flowering stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, terminal capitulum that is surrounded by several layers of overlapping, involucral bracts. The individual florets within the head are tubular and exhibit a distinct yellow or yellowish-green coloration.
