What is Magnolia rufibarbata?

Magnolia rufibarbata is a species of flowering plant in the family Magnoliaceae.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that grows up to 10 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and a straight, cylindrical trunk. The leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate, measuring 6-12 centimeters in length, with a leathery texture and a reddish-brown color when young, turning dark green above and pale green below. The flowers are white, fragrant, and solitary, measuring 10-15 centimeters in diameter, with six to eight stamens and a superior ovary.

The fruit is a red, woody capsule, 5-6 centimeters in length, containing several seeds.
