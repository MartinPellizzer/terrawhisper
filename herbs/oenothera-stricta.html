Oenothera stricta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oenothera stricta has erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 60 centimeters and are covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, while the upper cauline leaves are much smaller and more narrowly elliptical. Its flowers consist of four large, bright yellow petals that surround a central cluster of numerous stamens and a prominent stigma. The fruit is a long, slender capsule that dehisces to release small, dark seeds. This plant often forms dense colonies through the use of creeping rhizomes.

      What are the herbal preparations of Oenothera stricta?

      Oenothera stricta is prepared as an infusion, decoction, tincture, or topical poultice depending on the intended medicinal application. The dried aerial parts are commonly steeped in hot water to create an infusion for internal consumption. For more robust extractions, the roots or woody stems can be simmered into a concentrated decoction. Tinctures made with alcohol are also utilized to preserve the plant's bioactive compounds for long-term use. When treating skin irritations, the plant material is often crushed into a poultice for direct topical application.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Oenothera stricta can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, redness, and itching if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. Individuals with known sensitivities to the Onagraceae family may experience localized swelling or irritation upon touching the foliage or flowers. While there is limited clinical data regarding internal toxicity, ingestion should be avoided to prevent potential gastrointestinal distress or unforeseen systemic reactions. Dermatological irritation is the most documented concern associated with handling this specific species of evening primrose. Always use caution and protective barriers when working closely with this plant in its natural habitat.
