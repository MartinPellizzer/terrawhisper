Primula malacoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Primula malacoides?

Primula malacoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the evolutionary hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Ericales and the family Primulaceae, which places it within the well-known genus Primula. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its designation as the species malacoides.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Primula malacoides can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant belongs to a genus known to contain primin, a substance that often triggers sensitive skin reactions upon contact. Individuals may experience redness, itching, or even blistering if the sap touches unprotected areas of the body. While specific toxicological studies on this exact species are limited, related primulas are frequently cited as potential allergens. Careful handling is recommended to avoid accidental exposure to its chemical compounds.
