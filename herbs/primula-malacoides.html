Primula malacoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula malacoides has a basal rosette of long-petioled, obovate to elliptical leaves with entire or slightly dentate margins and a slightly wrinkled surface. The foliage is typically green and grows densely from a central point above the ground. Its inflorescence consists of an umbel of several flowers held aloft on a central, leafless scape. Each flower features five spreading petals that are often pale yellow or cream in color with a darker center. The stamens and pistil are centrally positioned within the tubular corolla to facilitate pollination.
