Erigeron annuus

What's the taxonomical classification of Erigeron annuus?

Erigeron annuus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is a prominent representative of the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. Within this family, it is specifically categorized under the genus Erigeron, with its unique designation being the species annuus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erigeron annuus has an upright, branched stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 100 centimeters and is covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, while the stem leaves are smaller, alternate, and often sessile. Its flower heads consist of numerous white ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular florets. Each flowering stem can support several of these composite heads, which are held atop long, slender peduncles. The plant often displays a somewhat bushy appearance due to its tendency to branch multiple times near the top.

What are the pharmacological activities of Erigeron annuus?

This plant has antioxidant, antimicrobial, antifungal, aldose reductase inhibitory, and protein glycation inhibitory activities. These diverse pharmacological properties suggest that Erigeron annuus contains potent bioactive compounds capable of combating oxidative stress and various pathogens. The ability to inhibit aldose reductase and protein glycation makes it particularly relevant for managing complications related to diabetes. Furthermore, its antimicrobial and antifungal actions provide a basis for potential therapeutic uses against infectious agents. Such multifaceted biological effects highlight the medicinal potential of this species in pharmaceutical research.

As discussed in a study, the flowers of Erigeron annuus contain an ethyl acetate-soluble extract from which one novel 2,3-dioxygenaated flavanone, named erigeroflavanone (1), was isolated. This plant also yielded eight known flavonoids and two known gamma-pyrone derivatives. The isolates were subjected to in vitro bioassays to evaluate their inhibitory activity. Specifically, erigeroflavanone exhibits protein glycation and aldose reductase inhibitory activity. These tests focused on the inhibition of advanced glycation end products formation and rat lens aldose reductase.
