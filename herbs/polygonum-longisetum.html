Polygonum longisetum

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum longisetum?

Polygonum longisetum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Caryophyllales and the family Polygonaceae. Specifically, this plant is categorized under the genus Polygonum, ultimately reaching its unique identification at the species level as Polygonum longisetum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum longisetum has erect, slender stems that reach heights of approximately 20 to 50 centimeters and are characterized by distinct nodes and ocreae. The leaves are lanceolate to narrowly elliptical in shape, featuring smooth margins and an acute apex. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense terminal clusters or axillary groups. These blossoms possess long, protruding stamens that give the inflorescence a feathery appearance. The plant typically develops a branched structure that supports its delicate, spreading growth habit.
