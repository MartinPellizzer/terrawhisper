Callistemon rigidus

What's the taxonomical classification of Callistemon rigidus?

Callistemon rigidus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Myrtales and the family Myrtaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Callistemon, and its specific species designation, rigidus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callistemon rigidus has stiff, spreading branches with small, leathery, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 10 to 20 millimeters in length.

The foliage is characterized by its rigid texture and a somewhat dull green coloration. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the bright red, cylindrical flower spikes composed of numerous long, protruding stamens. These inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils and are supported by woody stems.

The plant typically develops a compact, shrubby growth habit suited to its natural environment.
