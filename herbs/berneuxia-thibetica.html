Berneuxia thibetica

What's the taxonomical classification of Berneuxia thibetica?

Berneuxia thibetica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Diapensiaceae and is uniquely represented by the genus Berneuxia, specifically within the species thibetica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berneuxia thibetica has an erect, perennial habit characterized by slender, branched stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 40 centimeters.

The leaves are alternate and linear to narrowly lanceolate, often appearing somewhat fleshy or leathery in texture. Small, solitary flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring five white petals and distinctively long, protruding stamens. The fruit is a small, globose capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

These morphological traits distinguish the species within its genus and reflect its adaptation to specific montane environments.

Contact with the plant's sap may lead to redness, itching, or swelling in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of unknown quantities could potentially trigger nausea or stomach discomfort due to its chemical composition. Users should exercise extreme caution and avoid direct skin contact to prevent unexpected adverse reactions.
