Berneuxia thibetica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berneuxia thibetica has an erect, perennial habit characterized by slender, branched stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are alternate and linear to narrowly lanceolate, often appearing somewhat fleshy or leathery in texture. Small, solitary flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring five white petals and distinctively long, protruding stamens. The fruit is a small, globose capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its genus and reflect its adaptation to specific montane environments.

It is typically found growing in subalpine and afroalpine vegetation zones. The species thrives in the unique environmental conditions provided by these elevated terrains. Its distribution is strictly limited to these specific African montane habitats. Consequently, it is not found naturally in any other part of the world.
