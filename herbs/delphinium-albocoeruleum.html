Delphinium albocoeruleum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium albocoeruleum has erect, herbaceous stems that support terminal racemes of zygomorphic flowers ranging in color from white to deep blue. The leaves are palmately lobed or divided into several narrow, linear segments that create a dense foliage base. Each flower is characterized by a prominent nectar-producing spur extending from the rear of the calyx. The sepals are often larger and more colorful than the true petals, which are clustered in the center of the bloom. These plants typically reach heights that reflect their robust, upright growth habit within their native habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Gansu. It typically inhabits high-altitude environments such as alpine meadows, rocky slopes, and forest margins. These habitats are characterized by cool temperatures and well-drained soils that support its specialized growth requirements. The species is often found at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters above sea level. Its distribution remains concentrated within these specific temperate montane ecosystems.

Delphinium albocoeruleum has therapeutic actions that include acting as a cardiac stimulant and an emetic, though the plant is highly toxic if misused. Because of its potent and unpredictable nature, it must be handled with extreme caution to avoid fatal poisoning.
