Euphorbia royleana

What's the taxonomical classification of Euphorbia royleana?

Euphorbia royleana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Euphorbiaceae and the genus Euphorbia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species royleana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euphorbia royleana has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by erect, succulent stems that often develop a slightly glaucous or waxy appearance. These stems are typically branched and can reach significant heights depending on the environmental conditions. The leaves are arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems and possess an elliptical to lanceolate shape with smooth margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in specialized structures known as cyathia, which are typical for the Euphorbiaceae family. Upon injury, the plant exudes a milky white latex from its tissues.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically concentrated in parts of India, Myanmar, and Thailand. It thrives primarily in the moist, shaded environments found within lowland evergreen forests and riverine habitats. Within these ecosystems, it is often discovered growing in well-drained soils along forest edges or near water sources. The species maintains a relatively localized distribution compared to more widespread members of the Euphorbia genus. Climatic conditions characterized by high humidity and consistent warmth are essential for its natural occurrence.

What are the therapeutic actions of Euphorbia royleana?

Euphorbia royleana has therapeutic actions primarily characterized by its potent purgative, emetic, and anthelmintic properties derived from its milky latex.     What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains flavonoids, phenols, saponins, steroids, and ent-abietane-type diterpenoids that contribute to its diverse medicinal properties. These chemical constituents work together to provide various biological activities such as antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. The presence of phenols and flavonoids specifically aids in neutralizing free radicals within the body. Additionally, the saponins and steroids can influence cellular membranes and hormonal pathways. The specialized ent-abietane-type diterpenoid further distinguishes its unique pharmacological profile.

    As discussed in a study, the ethanolic extracts of Euphorbia royleana possess high anti-proliferative activity against breast cancer cells such as MDA-MB-231 and MCF-7. This plant has an IC50 value of 80 µg/mL. Qualitative phytochemical analysis confirmed that the extract contains phenols, flavonoids, and steroids, while saponins were specifically found in Euphorbia royleana. The extract induces cell cycle arrest at the G1/S phase through the down-regulation of CDK4 and Cyclin D1 expression. Additionally, Euphorbia royleana triggers the intrinsic pathways of apoptosis via a caspase-dependent mechanism.

        This plant can cause severe skin irritation characterized by redness, a burning sensation, and blistering upon contact with its milky sap. If the sap enters the eyes, it may lead to intense eye irritation and temporary blindness.
