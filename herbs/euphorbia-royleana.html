Euphorbia royleana

What's the taxonomical classification of Euphorbia royleana?

Euphorbia royleana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Euphorbiaceae and the genus Euphorbia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species royleana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Euphorbia royleana contains various bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, terpenoids, steroids, saponins, and flavonoids. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's milky white latex. Researchers have identified specific diterpene compounds that contribute to the plant's documented pharmacological activities. Such substances are often studied for their potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and cytotoxic properties. Local traditional medicine frequently utilizes these medicinal compounds to treat various skin ailments and inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Euphorbia royleana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin infections, wounds, and inflammatory conditions through its medicinal latex. Local healers often apply the sap to manage dermatological issues like scabies or fungal irritations. Some traditional practices involve using the plant to address digestive ailments or topical swellings. However, the milky substance is highly caustic and can cause severe irritation if handled improperly. It remains a significant component in certain folk medicine traditions for its potent chemical properties.
