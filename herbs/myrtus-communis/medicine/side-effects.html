10 Possible Side Effects Of Myrtus Communis (Myrtle)

Myrtus communis has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can occur due to the herb's high essential oil content and interactions with certain medications. For example, excessive consumption can lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, causing discomfort and affecting daily activities, such as work and social engagements.

Additionally, severe allergic reactions can even lead to respiratory problems or anaphylaxis, posing a significant threat to one's life.
