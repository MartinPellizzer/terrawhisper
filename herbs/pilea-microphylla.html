Pilea microphylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea microphylla has small, ovate to elliptical leaves that typically measure between 5 and 15 millimeters in length and feature finely serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a soft, slightly fleshy texture and a vibrant green color that can vary depending on light exposure. These diminutive leaves are arranged oppositely along slender, creeping stems that allow the plant to form dense, moss-like mats. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils rather than large, showy blooms. This compact growth habit and fine leaf structure make it an ideal specimen for terrariums and miniature landscapes.

      What are the herbal preparations of Pilea microphylla?

      Pilea microphylla is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice to utilize its medicinal properties. Practitioners often boil the plant material in water to extract active compounds for oral consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pilea microphylla can cause skin irritation or allergic reactions such as redness, itching, and dermatitis if the sap comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. While there is limited toxicological data available for humans, handling the plant without protection may trigger discomfort in some individuals. Pets may also experience mild gastrointestinal upset if they ingest any part of the foliage. It is generally considered safe for most people when handled with caution. Always wash your hands thoroughly after gardening with this species to prevent any potential irritation.
