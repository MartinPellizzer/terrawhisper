Cypripedium micranthum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cypripedium micranthum?

Cypripedium micranthum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling under the genus Cypripedium, and is specifically identified by its species name, micranthum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cypripedium micranthum has small, yellowish-green flowers characterized by a pouch-like labellum, a prominent dorsal sepal, and lateral petals that are narrow and spreading.

The plant features slender, upright stems that typically reach heights of ten to thirty centimeters. Its leaves are broadly elliptical or lanceolate, arranged in a basal rosette, and possess a smooth texture with prominent longitudinal veins. The inflorescence consists of one to several flowers that bloom during the late spring or early summer months.

This orchid species is distinguished from its relatives by its diminutive floral size and subtle coloration.
