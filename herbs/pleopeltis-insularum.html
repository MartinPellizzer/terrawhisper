Pleopeltis insularum

Pleopeltis insularum, also known as the island resurrection fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is known for its unique ability to dry out and revive itself in response to changes in humidity.

The plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Herbal preparations of Pleopeltis insularum are often made by infusing the dried plant in hot water, and it is also used in traditional medicine to promote overall health and well-being.
