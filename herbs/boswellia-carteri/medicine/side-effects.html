10 Possible Side Effects Of Boswellia Carteri

Boswellia carteri has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, diarrhea, and stomach cramps.

These side effects can occur due to the herb's ability to increase urine production, which can cause dehydration if not balanced with sufficient water intake.

Excessive use of Boswellia carteri can also exacerbate pre-existing conditions like gastritis, ulcers, and inflammatory bowel disease, significantly worsening a person's quality of life, affecting their ability to work and participate in daily activities.

1. Causes stomach upset

Boswellia carteri causes stomach upset.

This is likely due to its ability to increase the production of digestive enzymes and acids, which can irritate the stomach lining. Additionally, Boswellia's anti-inflammatory properties may cause a decrease in stomach motility, leading to slowed digestion and discomfort.

As a result, some individuals may experience symptoms such as heartburn, bloating, and nausea when using Boswellia carteri.

2. Induces nausea and vomiting

Boswellia carteri induces nausea and vomiting due to its potential interactions with certain medications, such as anti-inflammatory drugs and antacids.

The herb's boswellic acids may also irritate the stomach lining, causing digestive discomfort and leading to adverse reactions like vomiting.

Additionally, Boswellia carteri may exacerbate existing gastrointestinal conditions, such as irritable bowel syndrome, further contributing to nausea and vomiting.

3. Triggers allergic reactions

Boswellia carteri triggers allergic reactions due to its chemical composition, which contains terpenes and boswellic acids.

These compounds can cause an immune response, leading to the release of histamine, a chemical that stimulates allergic symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

In some individuals, these chemicals may also stimulate the production of IgE antibodies, further exacerbating the allergic reaction.

4. Increases bleeding risk

Boswellia carteri increases bleeding risk due to its ability to thin the blood and interfere with platelet function.

This is because boswellic acids in the herb inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory compounds that are involved in blood clotting, thereby increasing the likelihood of bleeding complications.

Additionally, Boswellia carteri may also interact with anticoagulant medications, further exacerbating this risk.

5. Enhances liver toxicity

Boswellia carteri enhances liver toxicity due to its ability to alter the metabolism of certain medications and substances in the body.

As a result, individuals using boswellia may experience elevated liver enzymes, inflammation, or even acute liver damage, particularly when combined with other compounds that can already affect liver function.

6. Reduces blood clotting

Boswellia carteri reduces blood clotting due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and decrease thromboxane production.

This is thought to be a result of the herb's anti-inflammatory properties, which may affect the body's natural mechanisms for forming clots.

As Boswellia carteri interacts with platelets and blood vessels, it can potentially alter the balance of coagulation factors, leading to reduced clotting abilities.

7. Activates digestive enzymes

Boswellia carteri activates digestive enzymes when consumed, which can lead to stomach upset and diarrhea.

This is because boswellic acids, a primary component of the herb, inhibit the activity of 5-lipoxygenase, an enzyme that regulates inflammation in the gut, leading to increased production of digestive enzymes and potentially altering normal digestion processes.

As a result, users may experience gastrointestinal disturbances when using Boswellia carteri.

8. Inhibits platelet aggregation

Boswellia carteri inhibits platelet aggregation, which may lead to a potential side effect of increased bleeding risk.

This is due to its ability to suppress the activity of platelets, which are blood cells that play a crucial role in blood clotting.

As Boswellia carteri interferes with platelet function, it may increase the likelihood of bruising or bleeding, particularly when combined with anticoagulant medications or other herbs that also affect blood clotting.

9. Produces respiratory distress

Boswellia carteri produces respiratory distress due to its ability to irritate the mucous membranes in the lungs.

The resin's volatile compounds can cause inflammation and bronchospasm, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, boswellic acids may stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical that constricts airways and worsens respiratory symptoms.

10. Increases serum creatinine

Boswellia carteri increases serum creatinine due to its potential to interact with kidney function.

The compound boswellic acid, present in frankincense, may impede the excretion of creatinine by the kidneys, leading to elevated levels in the blood.

This interaction may be attributed to boswellic acid's ability to alter protein expression and signaling pathways involved in kidney function regulation.
