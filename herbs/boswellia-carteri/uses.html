Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) Uses

Olibanum is used to treat osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It also helps with gout and inflammatory bowel diseases like colitis. It may reduce gastrointestinal inflammation, gastritis, and gastric ulcers by soothing the digestive tract.

Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) Monograph

1. Osteoarthritis

Olibanum, derived from Boswellia carteri, has been traditionally used to support joint health. It contains anti-inflammatory compounds that may help reduce pain and swelling in osteoarthritis. Studies suggest it can alleviate symptoms by inhibiting inflammatory enzymes. It is often used as a complementary therapy in herbal medicine for arthritis management.

2. Rheumatoid arthritis

Olibanum has been traditionally used to support joint health and reduce inflammation in rheumatoid arthritis. Its active compounds, including boswellic acids, inhibit inflammatory pathways, helping to alleviate pain and swelling. It is often used as a complementary therapy alongside conventional treatments. Clinical studies suggest it may improve mobility and reduce reliance on anti-inflammatory drugs.
