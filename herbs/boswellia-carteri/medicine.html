What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Boswellia Carteri (Frankincense)?

Boswellia carteri, also known as frankincense, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and relieving pain.

The herb's medicinal constituents include boswellic acids, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These active ingredients are commonly used in traditional medicine to prepare ointments, tinctures, and essential oils. However, excessive consumption may cause gastrointestinal upset, diarrhea, and stomach irritation.

Precautions include pregnant and breastfeeding women limiting their intake, while also consulting a healthcare professional before using frankincense for medicinal purposes.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Boswellia carteri.

What are the health benefits of Boswellia carteri?

Boswellia carteri has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and alleviating symptoms of arthritis, including joint pain and swelling.

It also exhibits anti-anxiety and antidepressant properties, promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality. Additionally, its antimicrobial properties can help combat infections and boost the immune system.

Overall, frankincense has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to promote overall well-being and longevity.

What are the active constituents of Boswellia carteri?

The health benefits of Boswellia carteri comes from its active constituents, such as boswellic acids, which have potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Frankincense oil is rich in sesquiterpenes like incensole and boswellene, which are responsible for its sedative and anti-anxiety effects. Additionally, it contains terpenoids like alpha-pinene and beta-pinene, known for their antiseptic and antimicrobial properties.

These bioactive compounds work together to provide the plant's numerous therapeutic benefits.

What are the medicinal preparations of Boswellia carteri?

Boswellia carteri has different medicinal preparations, such as essential oils, resin extracts, and capsules.

The essential oil is often used in aromatherapy to alleviate stress, anxiety, and insomnia. Resin extracts are used topically to treat skin conditions like acne, wounds, and psoriasis. Capsules, on the other hand, contain standardized extracts that help reduce inflammation and pain in conditions like arthritis, fibromyalgia, and inflammatory bowel disease.

These preparations can be administered orally or applied topically, depending on the specific condition being treated.

What are the possible side effect of using Boswellia carteri improperly?

Improper use of Boswellia carteri increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain, and dizziness.

It may also cause allergic reactions like hives, itching, and difficulty breathing. In rare cases, frankincense can lead to liver damage, kidney problems, or even an increased risk of bleeding due to its ability to thin the blood.

Furthermore, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and blood pressure medication, leading to adverse reactions.

What precautions to take when using Boswellia carteri medicinally?

Before using Boswellia carteri for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or are pregnant/breastfeeding.

It's also important to monitor your blood pressure and liver function regularly while taking boswellic acids, as they may interact with certain medications.

Additionally, start with low doses and gradually increase as needed to avoid potential side effects such as stomach upset or allergic reactions.
