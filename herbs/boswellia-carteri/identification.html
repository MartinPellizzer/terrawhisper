Botanical Identification of Olibanum (Boswellia carteri)

To identify Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Olibanum, or Boswellia carteri, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Burserales, family Burseraceae, genus Boswellia, and species Boswellia carteri. This classification places it within the group of flowering plants and highlights its botanical relationship to other resin-producing trees.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Boswellia carteri is the scientific name for Olibanum. It was first described by the botanist Richard E. Carter. This species is also known by several synonyms, including Boswellia sacra and Boswellia frereana. Common names such as frankincense and olibanum are widely used, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Olibanum, or Boswellia carteri, is a small tree or shrub with a deep taproot system. Its stems are straight and woody, often branching near the base. Leaves are pinnately compound, with small, lance-shaped leaflets. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing yellow-green flowers that are small and clustered. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Olibanum, or Boswellia carteri, can be distinguished by its pale yellow resin, which solidifies into a hard, fragrant gum. It is typically harvested from the bark of the tree, and the resin has a distinct, balsamic scent. Unlike similar species, it lacks the reddish coloration found in other Boswellia resins. Its unique aroma and physical characteristics aid in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Olibanum, or Boswellia carteri, is native to regions of eastern Africa, including countries like Somalia and Ethiopia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates with well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. This tree typically grows at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) blooms from March to May, producing yellowish flowers. Seed formation occurs from June to August. Harvesting for resin typically happens during the dry season, from September to November, when the resin flow is most abundant.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Olibanum, or Boswellia carteri, belongs to the genus Boswellia in the family Burseraceae. It is closely related to Boswellia sacra and Boswellia frereana. These species share similar resinous properties and are often grouped together due to their botanical and chemical similarities.
