Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) Compounds

Olibanum contains several primary compounds, including terpenoids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids also play roles in its therapeutic effects. Coumarin and tannins provide additional antioxidant benefits, while essential oils and saponins support its overall medicinal profile.

Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) Monograph

1. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key group of active compounds found in Olibanum (Boswellia carteri). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Specific terpenoids, such as boswellic acids, inhibit inflammatory enzymes and support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Olibanum (Boswellia carteri). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for conditions like arthritis and digestive issues. These compounds help modulate inflammatory responses and protect cellular structures.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Olibanum (Boswellia carteri). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. This compound is responsible for some of the therapeutic effects observed in traditional herbal medicine. It supports the use of Olibanum in treating conditions like arthritis and inflammatory disorders.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Olibanum (Boswellia carteri). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural anticoagulant, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for conditions involving inflammation and pain.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Olibanum (Boswellia carteri). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Tannins help reduce swelling and support wound healing. They are found in the resinous sap of the Boswellia carteri tree.

6. Essential oil

The essential oil of Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) is a volatile compound extracted from its resin. It contains aromatic compounds like alpha-pinene and limonene, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The oil is used in traditional medicine to support respiratory and digestive health.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Olibanum (Boswellia carteri). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Saponins help in reducing swelling and pain, making Olibanum effective for treating conditions like arthritis and inflammatory disorders.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Olibanum (Boswellia carteri). It contributes to the resin's anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Glycosides in Boswellia carteri are known to support joint health and reduce inflammation in conditions like osteoarthritis.

9. Alkaloid

Olibanum (Boswellia carteri) contains alkaloids, which are nitrogen-containing compounds. These alkaloids contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in reducing inflammation and pain. They are part of the complex mixture of bioactive substances found in Olibanum resin. Alkaloids in Boswellia carteri are often studied for their potential therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Olibanum (Boswellia carteri). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. While not the primary active component, anthocyanin supports the overall therapeutic effects of Olibanum in traditional herbal medicine.
