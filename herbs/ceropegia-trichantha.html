Ceropegia trichantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ceropegia trichantha has slender, cylindrical, climbing stems that are covered in dense, fine, white hairs and feature small, scale-like leaves. These stems are often quite thin and can reach significant lengths as they scramble through surrounding vegetation. The plant produces small, tubular, greenish-white flowers that typically emerge from the upper nodes. These blooms are characterized by their delicate structure and often possess a subtle fragrance to attract specific pollinators. The overall texture of the plant is distinctly fuzzy due to the pervasive trichomes covering its surface.

      What are the herbal preparations of Ceropegia trichantha?

      Ceropegia trichantha is prepared as a concentrated aqueous decoction, a dried powdered extract, or a topical infused oil.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ceropegia trichantha can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, or localized allergic reactions if the plant's sap or spines come into direct contact with human tissue. The presence of fine spines on the cactus body can lead to painful puncture wounds or microscopic skin abrasions. Once these physical injuries occur, the area may experience redness, swelling, or itching as an inflammatory response. Some individuals might also experience more systemic discomfort if the plant is ingested, although its toxicity levels are not extensively documented. It is essential to handle this succulent with protective gloves to avoid these dermatological complications.
