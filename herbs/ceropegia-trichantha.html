What's the taxonomical classification of Ceropegia trichantha?

Ceropegia trichantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is further categorized into the family Apocynaceae, which defines its botanical relationships. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Ceropegia, and its specific identity is designated by the species name trichantha.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires a well-draining succulent substrate consisting of organic matter mixed with pumice or perlite, bright indirect light, and infrequent watering that allows the medium to dry completely between applications.

Because it is a caudiciform species, it is essential to prevent moisture from sitting around the base of the swollen stem to avoid rot. During the active growing season, providing consistent warmth and moderate humidity will encourage healthy foliage development. Once the plant enters its dormant period, reduce watering significantly and keep it in a cooler environment.
