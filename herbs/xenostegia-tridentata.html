Xenostegia tridentata

What's the taxonomical classification of Xenostegia tridentata?

Xenostegia tridentata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned in the order Solanales. It is further categorized into the family Convolvulaceae, falling under the genus Xenostegia, and is specifically identified by the species name tridentata.

    Xenostegia tridentata is prepared primarily as an aqueous infusion, a decoction, or a dried powder for medicinal use.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Xenostegia tridentata can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and mild gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's foliage may trigger redness, itching, or a burning sensation on sensitive areas of the skin. Individuals with known hypersensitivity to similar botanical species might experience more severe swelling or hives upon exposure. If any part of the plant is consumed, it may lead to nausea or stomach cramping in some users. Monitoring for these physiological reactions is essential when handling the plant in an uncontrolled environment.
