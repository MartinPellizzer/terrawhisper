Xenostegia tridentata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Xenostegia tridentata has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems and feature an ovate to lanceolate shape with distinctly serrated or tridentate margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying a pale lavender or white coloration. The floral structure is zygomorphic, providing a specialized landing platform for specific insect pollinators. These botanical features collectively allow the plant to thrive in its specific ecological niche.
