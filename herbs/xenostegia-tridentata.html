Xenostegia tridentata

What's the taxonomical classification of Xenostegia tridentata?

Xenostegia tridentata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned in the order Solanales. It is further categorized into the family Convolvulaceae, falling under the genus Xenostegia, and is specifically identified by the species name tridentata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Xenostegia tridentata has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems and feature an ovate to lanceolate shape with distinctly serrated or tridentate margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying a pale lavender or white coloration. The floral structure is zygomorphic, providing a specialized landing platform for specific insect pollinators.
