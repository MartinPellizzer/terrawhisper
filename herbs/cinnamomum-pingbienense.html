Cinnamomum pingbienense

What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum pingbienense?

Cinnamomum pingbienense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a lineage characterized by its significant evolutionary position. This plant is further categorized under the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae. Within this botanical framework, it is situated in the genus Cinnamomum, specifically identified as the species pingbienense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cinnamomum pingbienense has simple, evergreen leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with an entire or slightly wavy margin. The leaf texture is coriaceous, meaning it feels leathery to the touch, and the upper surface often displays a dark green hue while the underside is lighter. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary panicles, which eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes. These fruits are characterized by their ovoid shape and typically transition from green to a dark color upon ripening. The bark of the tree is relatively smooth and grayish, providing a protective layer for the woody stem.
