What's the taxonomical classification of Cassia javanica?

Cassia javanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of plant classification, it falls under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cassia, and its unique species designation, javanica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

During the flowering stage, the plant produces numerous clusters of vivid blossoms that stand out against its green canopy. As the plant matures, it develops thick, brown pods that eventually split to release small, dark seeds. What are the therapeutic actions of Cassia javanica?

Cassia javanica has therapeutic properties that function primarily as a bitter stomachic and a mild laxative to support digestive health. These combined actions make it a useful botanical tool for addressing various gastrointestinal discomforts.
