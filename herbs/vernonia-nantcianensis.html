Vernonia nantcianensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia nantcianensis?

Vernonia nantcianensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is further categorized under the family Asteraceae, a group known for its complex flower heads. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Vernonia, with its specific identity defined by the species epithet nantcianensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia nantcianensis has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and feature lanceolate to narrowly elliptic shapes with finely serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of small, discoid flower heads that are clustered in terminal panicles. The florets are predominantly purple or violet in color, providing a distinct contrast to the green foliage. Small, linear bracts surround the base of each individual flower head.
