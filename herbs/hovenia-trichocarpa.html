Hovenia trichocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hovenia trichocarpa has a small, herbacious growth habit characterized by slender, upright stems and densely hairy, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The foliage is covered in fine, multicellular trichomes that give the plant a textured, pubescent appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The most distinctive morphological feature is the production of small, capsule-like fruits that are heavily coated in fine hairs. These hairy structures are essential for the plant's identification within its specific habitat.
