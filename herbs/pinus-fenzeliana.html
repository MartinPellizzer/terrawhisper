Pinus fenzeliana

What's the taxonomical classification of Pinus fenzeliana?

Pinus fenzeliana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae. Within the order Pinales, it falls under the family Pinaceae. Finally, this plant is categorized under the genus Pinus, specifically identified by the species name fenzeliana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Pinus fenzeliiana contains bioactive terpenoids, specifically alpha-pinene and beta-pinene, which possess significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's resin and essential oils. Researchers have identified that these specific monoterpenes can inhibit the growth of various bacterial strains. Additionally, the presence of certain diterpenes may contribute to its potential use in soothing skin irritations. These medicinal compounds are often studied for their ability to modulate immune responses in biological systems.

What are the therapeutic actions of Pinus fenzeliana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments including bronchitis, asthma, coughs, and various inflammatory conditions of the lungs. The essential oils extracted from its needles possess potent antimicrobial and expectorant properties that help clear mucus from the airways. These therapeutic compounds also work to reduce inflammation in the bronchial tubes, providing relief from persistent chest congestion. Additionally, the plant's extracts are sometimes applied topically to soothe skin irritations and minor wounds. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize these properties to support overall immune function during seasonal illnesses.
