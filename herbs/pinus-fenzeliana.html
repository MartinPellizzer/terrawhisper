Pinus fenzeliana

What's the taxonomical classification of Pinus fenzeliana?

Pinus fenzeliana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae. Within the order Pinales, it falls under the family Pinaceae. Finally, this plant is categorized under the genus Pinus, specifically identified by the species name fenzeliana.

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Sierra Madre Occidental mountain range in northwestern Mexico. It grows primarily within the states of Chihuahua, Durango, and Sinaloa. These pine trees are typically found in temperate coniferous forests located at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,800 meters above sea level.
