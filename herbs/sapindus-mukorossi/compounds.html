Chinese Soapberry (Sapindus mukorossi) Compounds

Sapindus mukorossi contains several active compounds, including saponin, which has cleansing properties. Tannins contribute to astringency and antimicrobial effects. Flavonoids and phenolic acids provide antioxidant benefits. Coumarin and terpenoids may support anti-inflammatory actions. Glycosides, essential oils, alkaloids, and anthocyanins also contribute to its therapeutic potential.

Chinese Soapberry (Sapindus mukorossi) Monograph

1. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Soapberry (Sapindus mukorossi). It exhibits surfactant properties, helping to clean and detoxify the skin. Saponin also has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and infections.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Soapberry (Sapindus mukorossi). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins help in treating skin conditions and digestive issues.
