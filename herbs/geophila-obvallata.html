Geophila obvallata

What's the taxonomical classification of Geophila obvallata?

Geophila obvallata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Gentianaales. It is further categorized under the family Rubiaceae, which is a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Geophila, and its specific identity is defined by its species designation, obvallata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Geophila obvallata contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and various terpenoids. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's significant therapeutic potential in traditional medicine systems. Specifically, the presence of phenolic compounds provides potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The alkaloids within the plant are often studied for their potential psychoactive or antimicrobial effects. Furthermore, the various glycosides and sterols support its use in treating various systemic ailments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, gastrointestinal disorders, and various types of wounds. The extracts from its roots and leaves possess antimicrobial properties that help combat bacterial and fungal ailments. It is also frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of cuts. Furthermore, traditional medicinal practices utilize it to alleviate digestive discomfort and internal swelling.
