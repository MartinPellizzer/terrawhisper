Aconitum sungpanense

What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum sungpanense?

Aconitum sungpanense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Aconitum, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as sungpanense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aconitum sungpanense has perennial herbaceous stems that support pinnately compound leaves with deeply lobed segments and terminal clusters of helmet-shaped, purplish-blue flowers. The leaves are divided into multiple narrow, serrated leaflets that emerge from a basal rosette. Each flower is characterized by a prominent, hooded upper sepal that protects the internal reproductive organs. The inflorescence typically forms a dense raceme atop the upright flowering stalks. Small, dark roots form a tuberous rhizome system that supports the plant's growth in moist, mountain habitats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several toxic diterpene alkaloids, specifically aconitine, mesaconitine, and hypaconitine, which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These bioactive molecules act as potent neurotoxins by opening voltage-gated sodium channels in nerve and muscle cells. While these substances are dangerous in high doses, they are utilized in traditional pharmacology to treat pain and inflammatory conditions. Precise extraction and processing are required to manage the extreme toxicity of these alkaloids during therapeutic use.     What side effects this plant can have?

The toxins then attack the cardiovascular system, potentially triggering arrhythmia and fatal cardiac arrest. Neurological damage may follow, resulting in convulsions or total paralysis. Finally, the body may experience critical respiratory depression as the central nervous system fails.
