Chrozophora plicata

What's the taxonomical classification of Chrozophora plicata?

Chrozophora plicata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae. Finally, its specific identification is determined by its placement in the genus Chrozophora, with the individual species being designated as plicata.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Chrozophora plicata can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested due to its chemical composition. Contact with the sap or leaves may lead to localized redness, itching, or swelling on sensitive skin surfaces. If the plant parts are consumed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. These adverse reactions occur because the plant contains bioactive compounds that act as irritants to biological tissues. It is essential to handle this species with care to avoid accidental exposure to these harmful effects.
