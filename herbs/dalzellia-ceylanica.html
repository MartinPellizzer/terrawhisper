What is Dalzellia ceylanica?

Dalzellia ceylanica is a species of plant that belongs to the family Podostemaceae, commonly referred to as the riverweed family.

It is an aquatic plant, typically found in shallow, fast-flowing streams and rivers in Sri Lanka. Botanically, Dalzellia ceylanica is characterized by its submerged, branching stems and leaves that are elliptical in shape, 2-7 cm long, and 1-4 cm wide, with a leathery texture and entire margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in axillary clusters, which are 3-4 mm in diameter and have 5-7 petals.

The fruit of Dalzellia ceylanica is a capsule that splits into two valves, releasing seeds that are 1-2 mm long.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Dalzellia ceylanica are made from its leaves, roots and stems which are traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine.

Infusion is prepared by steeping dried leaves in hot water to make a tea-like beverage used to treat fever and inflammation. Decoction involves boiling the roots and stems in water to create a concentrated liquid used to alleviate digestive issues and as a purgative. Tincture is a concentrated liquid preparation made by soaking the plant's parts in a solvent like ethanol or glycerin to create a medicinal extract used for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

A salve is prepared by mixing the plant's extracts with a carrier oil and wax to create a topical application used for wound healing and skin conditions.

Furthermore, using Dalzellia ceylanica without proper guidance can induce nausea, exacerbating stomach discomfort and potentially leading to dehydration. In some cases, individuals may experience allergic reactions, such as hives, itching, or swelling, due to sensitivity to the plant's compounds.

These side effects can be severe and are often preventable by proper usage and dosage of Dalzellia ceylanica under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
