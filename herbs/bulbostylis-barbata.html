Bulbostylis barbata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbostylis barbata has a tufted growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical leaves and small, solitary spikelets that feature long, white, hair-like bristles. The plant typically forms dense clumps of fine, wiry foliage that arise from a short rhizome. Each spikelet is somewhat ovoid in shape and is surrounded by several brownish or whitish involucral bracts. These bracts are most distinctive for their elongated, scabrid appendages that give the plant its bearded appearance. The tiny, inconspicuous flowers are embedded within these bristly structures to facilitate wind pollination.
