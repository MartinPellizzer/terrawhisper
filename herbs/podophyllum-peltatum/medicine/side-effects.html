10 Possible Side Effects Of Podophyllum Peltatum

Podophyllum peltatum has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can be caused by ingesting excessive amounts of the herb, mixing it with other medications, or having pre-existing medical conditions.

In severe cases, these side effects can worsen a person's quality of life by causing prolonged nausea, fatigue, and decreased productivity, ultimately affecting their work, relationships, and overall well-being.

1. Causes vomiting

Podophyllum peltatum causes vomiting due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase gastric acid secretion.

This stimulation can lead to nausea and vomiting in some individuals who use the herb.

Additionally, Podophyllum peltatum contains a toxic compound called podophyllotoxin, which can irritate the stomach lining and exacerbate gastrointestinal symptoms, resulting in vomiting.

2. Induces diarrhea

Podophyllum peltatum induces diarrhea due to its ability to slow down digestion and absorption of nutrients in the gut.

The saponins present in the plant can irritate the mucous membranes, leading to increased peristalsis and watery stools.

Additionally, the flavonoids and terpenoids in Podophyllum peltatum may also contribute to this side effect by altering the normal balance of gut flora, further exacerbating diarrhea symptoms.

3. Triggers abdominal pain

Podophyllum peltatum triggers abdominal pain due to its ability to interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and antiplatelet agents.

Additionally, the compound podophyllotoxin found in the plant can cause gastrointestinal irritation, leading to stomach discomfort, nausea, and cramping.

4. Produces nausea

Podophyllum peltatum produces nausea due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system, causing an increase in serotonin levels.

This can lead to feelings of dizziness and stomach discomfort, often accompanied by vomiting and diarrhea.

The compound's alkaloids, specifically podophyllotoxin, are responsible for this side effect, as they interact with the body's neurotransmitters, leading to these unpleasant symptoms.

5. Stimulates rapid heart rate

Podophyllum peltatum stimulates rapid heart rate due to its ability to increase cardiac contractility and stroke volume.

This is attributed to the presence of podophyllotoxin, a lignan compound that affects the autonomic nervous system, leading to increased sympathetic activity and decreased parasympathetic activity.

As a result, the heart rate increases, often accompanied by palpitations, tachycardia, or even arrhythmias in some cases.

6. Lowers blood pressure

Podophyllum peltatum lowers blood pressure due to its ability to dilate blood vessels and reduce peripheral vascular resistance.

This is primarily attributed to the presence of podophyllotoxin, a lignan that acts as a vasodilator, causing a decrease in systemic vascular resistance and subsequently lowering blood pressure.

Additionally, mayapple's flavonoids and phenolic acids also contribute to its hypotensive effects by inhibiting calcium channels and reducing cardiac contractility, ultimately leading to decreased blood pressure.

7. Increases risk of bleeding

Podophyllum peltatum increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to interfere with platelet function and coagulation, leading to a decrease in blood clotting factors.

This is attributed to the plant's presence of podophyllotoxin, a lignan that inhibits platelet aggregation and prolongs bleeding time.

Additionally, Podophyllum peltatum may also cause gastrointestinal irritation, further contributing to its proclivity for bleeding complications.

8. Aggravates allergic reactions

Podophyllum peltatum aggravates allergic reactions due to its high content of saponins and alkaloids.

These compounds can trigger an overactive immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and inflammation.

Additionally, the plant's sap has been known to cause skin irritation and contact dermatitis in those who are sensitive to it.

9. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Podophyllum peltatum disrupts menstrual cycles by affecting the production and balance of hormones in the body.

Its active compounds, including podophyllotoxin, have been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes responsible for estrogen metabolism, leading to changes in hormone levels that can impact menstruation.

Additionally, mayapple's anti-inflammatory properties may contribute to its ability to alter menstrual cycles by reducing inflammation and promoting hormonal imbalance.

10. Causes kidney damage

Podophyllum peltatum causes kidney damage due to its ability to increase urine production and decrease the excretion of waste products in the kidneys.

This can lead to a buildup of toxins and eventually cause damage to the kidneys over time.

The compound podophyllotoxin, found in the plant's roots and leaves, is responsible for this toxic effect on the kidneys.
