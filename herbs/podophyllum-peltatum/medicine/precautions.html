10 Precautions To Take When Using Podophyllum Peltatum (Mayapple)

Podophyllum peltatum has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as proper dosing and monitoring of toxicity levels.

It's essential to adhere to these guidelines to avoid liver and kidney damage. Ingesting excessive amounts or using it for extended periods can cause nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.

1. Consult before giving to others

When using Podophyllum peltatum medicinally, it's important to take under expert supervision because of its potential toxic and carcinogenic properties.

The plant contains podophyllotoxin, which can cause severe gastrointestinal symptoms, neurological damage, and even death if not used properly.

Without proper guidance from an experienced healthcare professional, incorrect dosage or preparation can lead to serious adverse effects, making it crucial to seek expert supervision to minimize risks and ensure safe use.

5. Consult before giving to others

When using Podophyllum peltatum medicinally, it's important to avoid in pregnancy or breastfeeding.

This is due to the potential teratogenic and embryotoxic effects of the plant's toxic compounds, specifically podophyllotoxin, which can harm the developing fetus and newborn baby. Additionally, the risk of adverse effects on lactation and infant development poses a significant concern.

As a result, it is crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before using Podophyllum peltatum during these sensitive periods to ensure safe and effective treatment.

When using Podophyllum peltatum medicinally, it's important to be cautious with kidney disease.

As a result, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Podophyllum peltatum if you have a pre-existing kidney condition.
