What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone shikokiana has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal leaves that are deeply divided into three to five lobes with serrated margins and solitary, actinomorphic flowers with five to seven white petals. Its flowers feature a central cluster of numerous yellow stamens that contrast sharply against the pale tepals. The pedicels supporting these blooms are slender and upright, elevating the flowers above the surrounding greenery.
