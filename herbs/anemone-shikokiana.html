Anemone shikokiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Anemone shikokiana?

Anemone shikokiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically falling under the genus Anemone, with the specific designation of the species shikokiana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains protoanemonin, anemone, and shikokianin as its primary medicinal chemical constituents.

These specific compounds are responsible for the biological activity observed in the species. Protoanemonin acts as a potent irritant that can be converted into anemonin through various chemical processes. Shikokianin belongs to a class of compounds known as coumarins, which often possess diverse pharmacological properties.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Anemone shikokiana can cause skin irritation, redness, and blistering upon direct contact with its tissues. The plant contains protoanemonin, a toxic compound that acts as a powerful irritant to mucous membranes and sensitive skin. If ingested, it may lead to severe gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Some individuals might also experience more systemic reactions depending on the quantity consumed. Careful handling is necessary to prevent accidental chemical burns or internal poisoning.
