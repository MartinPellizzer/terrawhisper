Botanical Identification of Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera caprifolium)

To identify Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera caprifolium) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera caprifolium) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Common Honeysuckle, Lonicera caprifolium, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class of eudicots, order Dipsacales, family Caprifoliaceae, genus Lonicera, and species Lonicera caprifolium. This classification reflects its botanical relationships and characteristics within the herbal medicine context.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Common Honeysuckle is Lonicera caprifolium, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Lonicera japonica var. caprifolium. Common names include honeysuckle, common honeysuckle, and Japanese honeysuckle. Regional and historical names vary, reflecting its widespread use in herbal medicine across different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Common Honeysuckle is a deciduous shrub with a spreading growth habit. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, anchoring it in moist soils. The stems are twining and flexible, often climbing or trailing. Leaves are opposite, oval, and serrated, with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside. The inflorescence consists of clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. Flowers are yellow or orange-red, with five lobes, and the fruit is a red, fleshy berry containing numerous seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Common Honeysuckle can be distinguished by its opposite, oval leaves with serrated edges and a red stem. It has tubular, yellow-orange flowers with a prominent red corona. The berries are red, turning orange when mature, and the plant has a distinct, fragrant scent. It is often found in moist areas, which helps differentiate it from similar species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Common Honeysuckle (Lonicera caprifolium) is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found at low to mid-altitude regions.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Common Honeysuckle blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during early summer when flowers are fully open and seeds are not yet developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Lonicera caprifolium is closely related to other honeysuckle species within the genus Lonicera. It shares familial traits with plants in the Caprifoliaceae family, such as opposite leaf arrangement and tubular flowers. These relationships aid in accurate identification and understanding its ecological and medicinal significance.
