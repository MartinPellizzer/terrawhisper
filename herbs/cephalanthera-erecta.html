Cephalanthera erecta

What's the taxonomical classification of Cephalanthera erecta?

Cephalanthera erecta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Asparagales and the family Orchidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cephalanthera, and its unique species designation, erecta.

Cephalanthera erecta has potent antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties derived from its bioactive secondary metabolites like alkaloids and flavonoids. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including malaria, fever, inflammation, and gastrointestinal issues. Its medicinal properties are primarily derived from the alkaloids found within its roots and stems. Local healers often prepare decoctions from the plant to reduce high body temperatures and combat parasitic infections. Additionally, its topical applications are believed to soothe skin irritations and reduce swelling in affected areas. These traditional uses highlight the plant's role in managing both systemic and localized inflammatory conditions.
