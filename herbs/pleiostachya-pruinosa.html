What is Pleiostachya pruinosa?

Pleiostachya pruinosa is a species of flowering plant in the family Marantaceae, native to tropical Africa.

It is a perennial herb with rhizomatous stems, growing up to 1.5 meters tall, and has linear-lanceolate leaves that are 10-40 centimeters long and 3-10 centimeters wide, with a pale green color and a hairy surface. The plant produces inflorescences that are 10-30 centimeters long, bearing white or pinkish flowers that are 5-7 millimeters in diameter, with 3 stamens and a 3-parted calyx. The fruit is a capsule, 1-2 centimeters long, containing 3-6 seeds.

The plant flowers from March to May and reproduces via seeds and vegetative propagation.
