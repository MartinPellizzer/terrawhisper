Thunbergia coccinea

What's the taxonomical classification of Thunbergia coccinea?

Thunbergia coccinea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. The plant is a member of the family Acanthaceae, specifically within the genus Thunbergia, and is identified by its species name, coccinea.

Thunbergia coccinea has medicinal properties primarily utilized for its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities. What are the herbal preparations of Thunbergia coccinea?

Thunbergia coccinea is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using its leaves and stems. The plant material is often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. In some traditional practices, the crushed leaves are applied directly to the skin as a warm compress to treat localized inflammation. These preparations are frequently used to address digestive issues or to soothe skin irritations. The specific method of preparation depends on whether the goal is systemic relief or direct topical application.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Thunbergia coccinea can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or mild digestive upset if ingested due to its unknown chemical properties in humans. While primarily grown as an ornamental vine, contact with the foliage may trigger redness or itching in individuals with sensitive skin. There is currently a lack of comprehensive clinical studies regarding its long-term toxicity or systemic effects on human health. Because the plant is not widely documented for medicinal use, consuming any part of it carries an unpredictable risk of adverse reactions. Therefore, caution is advised when handling or introducing this species into a domestic environment.
