Thunbergia coccinea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thunbergia coccinea has a woody, perennial climbing habit characterized by twining stems that produce large, trumpet-shaped scarlet flowers with prominent yellow throats. The foliage consists of dark green, ovate to heart-shaped leaves with slightly serrated margins and a velvety texture. Its inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a tubular structure that transitions into five spreading lobes. The plant utilizes its flexible stems to scramble over supports, creating a dense curtain of vibrant red blooms. Robust root systems allow this climber to establish itself firmly in tropical or subtropical environments.

This plant is native to the eastern regions of South Africa, specifically within the provinces of KwaZulu-Natal and Mpumalanga. Within its natural range, it is often found growing in rocky areas or along forest edges where sunlight is abundant. Although it is indigenous to these specific African territories, it has been introduced to other tropical and subtropical regions worldwide as an ornamental species.
