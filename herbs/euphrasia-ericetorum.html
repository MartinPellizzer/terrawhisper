Purple Eyebright (Euphrasia Ericetorum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Euphrasia ericetorum.

Purple eyebright, scientifically known as Euphrasia ericetorum, is a small, flowering medicinal herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, prized for its purported eye health benefits. Traditionally used in herbal medicine for centuries, it is believed to support vision, reduce eye strain, and alleviate symptoms of eye conditions such as conjunctivitis and cataracts. Its primary benefits include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and mild stimulant properties, which may contribute to its historical use as an adaptogen to enhance mental clarity and physical endurance. In modern wellness practices, it is often incorporated into eye drops, herbal teas, and supplements aimed at promoting ocular health and reducing fatigue. Notably, it possesses a distinct, slightly bitter flavor and contains unique compounds like flavonoids and iridoid glycosides, which have been studied for their potential therapeutic effects.
