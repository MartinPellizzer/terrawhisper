What's the taxonomical classification of Vaccinium dunalianum?

Vaccinium dunalianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary path continues through the order Ericales and the family Ericaceae, where it is placed in the genus Vaccinium. Ultimately, this plant is identified at the species level as Vaccinium dunalianum.

According to a study published by "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", Vaccinium dunalianum contains a major bioactive compound known as 6'-O-caffeoylarbutin (CA) at approximately 20 %. This plant has been traditionally consumed to improve vascular health and shows potential in treating ischemic stroke. In studies, CA effectively reduced nitric oxide (NO) release and the transcript-level expression of inflammatory factors such as IL-6, IL-1β, and TNF-α. The compound exerts neuroprotective effects by increasing brain superoxide dismutase, catalase, and glutathione peroxidase levels. These pharmacological activities occur through the inhibition of inflammation and oxidative stress via the PI3K/AKT/NF-κB pathway.
