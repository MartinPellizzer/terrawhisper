Vaccinium dunalianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vaccinium dunalianum has woody, perennial stems that branch into small shrubs with elliptical to lanceolate leaves characterized by serrated margins and a somewhat leathery texture. The foliage typically displays a bright green color on the upper surface and may appear slightly paler underneath. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in clusters. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy berries that serve as the primary fruit of the species. The overall structure of the plant is compact, allowing it to thrive in specific montane or forest environments.
