Lomatogonium rotatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lomatogonium rotatum has small, cylindrical, bright green gametophytes that are typically branched and measure only a few millimeters in length. The individual branches are characterized by a spiral arrangement of densely packed, overlapping leaves. These leaves are relatively short and possess a distinctively rounded or blunt apex. The plant often forms compact, tufted mats or cushions on moist substrates such as rocks or damp soil.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lomatogonium rotatum can cause severe skin irritation, localized dermatitis, and chemical burns upon direct contact with its sap. The plant contains irritating compounds that may trigger redness, swelling, or intense itching in sensitive individuals. If the juices come into contact with the eyes, they can lead to significant inflammation and potential corneal damage. Some people might also experience allergic reactions if the plant is ingested or if the sap enters small cuts in the skin. Proper protective gear should always be worn when handling this species to avoid these adverse dermatological effects.
