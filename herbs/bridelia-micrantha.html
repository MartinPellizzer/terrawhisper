Bridelia micrantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Bridelia micrantha?

Bridelia micrantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae. Specifically, it is identified within the genus Bridelia, with the specific epithet micrantha completing its scientific designation.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bridelia micrantha has woody stems with grayish bark and oppositely arranged, elliptical leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length. The foliage typically features an acute apex and a slightly serrated margin with prominent pinnate venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in dense, axillary clusters, which eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes. These fruits transition from green to a dark reddish or black color as they reach maturity. The overall structure of the plant allows it to form dense shrubs or small trees in tropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of sub-Saharan Africa, ranging from Senegal in the west to Ethiopia in the east and extending south to South Africa. It is most commonly found in diverse habitats such as riverine forests, gallery forests, and thickets near water sources. The species thrives in moist environments where it can establish itself along stream banks and in swampy areas. Because it prefers humid conditions, its distribution is closely tied to the presence of permanent or seasonal water bodies across the continent. It is frequently encountered in both undisturbed wild landscapes and disturbed forest edges.

What are the pharmacological activities of Bridelia micrantha?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including antimicrobial, antibacterial, antimalarial, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective properties. Its antibacterial capabilities allow it to combat various pathogenic bacteria, while its antimicrobial actions provide broad protection against diverse microorganisms. The plant also demonstrates potent antimalarial effects, making it a subject of interest for treating parasitic infections. Furthermore, its antioxidant capacity helps neutralize harmful free radicals within the body. Finally, the species exhibits hepatoprotective qualities that assist in shielding liver cells from chemical or oxidative damage.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the methanolic extract of B. micrantha (Bark) demonstrated weak inhibitory activities against S. flexneri and S. plesiomonas. Based on results in experimental rat models, the plant extract caused significant reductions in faecal output and frequency of droppings compared with castor-oil treated rats. The extract also significantly retarded the propulsion of charcoal meal. Additionally, the extract significantly inhibited the PGE(2)-induced enteropooling. These results confirmed the effectiveness of the plant as an anti-diarrhoeal agent.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

The presence of flavonoids and triterpenes often provides antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties to the species. The complex chemical structure represented by N(b)-benzyl-14-(carboxymethyl) further illustrates the botanical richness of Bridelia micrantha.

According to a study published by "Acta poloniae pharmaceutica", two flavonoids and two triterpene compounds were isolated from B. micrantha. These specific compounds had not been previously isolated from this plant species. The antioxidant capacity of the isolated flavonoids was found to be comparable to that of the known antioxidant ascorbic acid. Gas chromatography-mass spectrometry profiling of the fruit extracts showed different classes of compounds.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating dysentery, diarrhoea, intestinal infections, stomach ache, and gastric ulcers. The various parts of the plant, including the leaves and bark, contain bioactive compounds that help soothe the digestive tract. These medicinal properties allow it to combat harmful pathogens that cause severe intestinal infections. Furthermore, its soothing effects can provide significant relief for individuals suffering from persistent stomach ache or gastric ulcers. Regular use in traditional medicine is often aimed at restoring balance to the digestive system.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Bridelia micrantha is used as herbal medicine in 57.3% of the countries in tropical Africa where it is indigenous. A total of 54 ethnomedicinal uses of the plant have been recorded. There is a high degree of consensus regarding its use for conditions such as burns, wounds, conjunctivitis, painful eyes, constipation, gastric ulcers, cough, headache, rheumatism, and painful joints. Additionally, the plant is used to treat dysentery, malaria, sexually transmitted infections, stomach ache, tape worms, and diarrhoea.
