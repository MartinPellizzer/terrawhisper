Bridelia micrantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Bridelia micrantha?

Bridelia micrantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae. Specifically, it is identified within the genus Bridelia, with the specific epithet micrantha completing its scientific designation.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bridelia micrantha has woody stems with grayish bark and oppositely arranged, elliptical leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length. The foliage typically features an acute apex and a slightly serrated margin with prominent pinnate venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in dense, axillary clusters, which eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes. These fruits transition from green to a dark reddish or black color as they reach maturity. The overall structure of the plant allows it to form dense shrubs or small trees in tropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of sub-Saharan Africa, ranging from Senegal in the west to Ethiopia in the east and extending south to South Africa. It is most commonly found in diverse habitats such as riverine forests, gallery forests, and thickets near water sources. The species thrives in moist environments where it can establish itself along stream banks and in swampy areas. Because it prefers humid conditions, its distribution is closely tied to the presence of permanent or seasonal water bodies across the continent. It is frequently encountered in both undisturbed wild landscapes and disturbed forest edges.

What are the pharmacological activities of Bridelia micrantha?

This plant has antimicrobial, antimalarial, antioxidant, antibacterial, and anti-diarrhoeal pharmacological activities. Its potent antioxidant properties help neutralize harmful free radicals within the body. The plant exhibits significant antibacterial and antimicrobial actions against various pathogenic microorganisms. Research also demonstrates its effectiveness in combating malaria parasites through its antimalarial components. Additionally, the extracts show promise in treating intestinal distress due to their anti-diarrhoeal effects.

As discussed in a study, the ethyl acetate extract of the stem bark of Bridelia micrantha contains potent bioactive phytocompounds that may be developed into new antimicrobials. Bioautography on BEA plates revealed the presence of three bioactive compounds with Rf values of 0.12, 0.20, and 0.42. These compounds were designated as fractions 3, 7, and 8. Fraction 3 exhibited MIC50 values ranging from 0.0048mg/mL to 1.25mg/mL. Fraction 7 showed MIC50 values from 0.0024mg/mL to 5 mg/mL, while fraction 8 had MIC50 values between 0.0024mg/mL and 2.5mg/mL.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 2-pinen-4-one, flavonoids, linalool, triterpenes, and N(b)-benzyl-14-(carboxymethyl). These diverse chemical constituents contribute to the various therapeutic properties observed in the species. The presence of flavonoids and triterpenes often provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Additionally, the terpene profiles including linalool and 2-pinen-4-one can offer calming or antimicrobial benefits. The specific nitrogenous compound N(b)-benzyl-14-(carboxymethyl) further adds to the complex phytochemical makeup of the plant.

As discussed in a study, the n-Hexane fraction of Bridelia micrantha bark contains twenty-four constituents representing 60.5% of the fraction. Detected compounds include 1.3-bis (3-phenoxyphenoxy (13.51%), 2-pinen-4-one (10.03%), and N(b)-benzyl-14-(carboxymethyl) (6.35%), while the least detected compound was linalool (0.2%). This fraction showed 20% inhibition of MTB H37Ra and almost 35% inhibition of an MTB isolate resistant to all five first-line drugs at 10 μg/mL. The study utilized 100% n-Hexane and Chloroform/Methanol (99:1) as solvents during bioassay-guided fractionation.
