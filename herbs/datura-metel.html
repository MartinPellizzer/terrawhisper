Datura metel

What's the taxonomical classification of Datura metel?

Datura metei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Solanales and is a member of the family Solanaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Datura and the specific species metel.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Datura metel has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by hollow, branched stems and large, ovate to lanceolate leaves with wavy or coarsely toothed margins. The foliage is typically bright green and can grow quite large depending on the available nutrients. Its most striking feature is the large, trumpet-shaped flowers that are white or pale lavender and bloom primarily at night. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and possess a long, tubular corolla. Following pollination, the plant produces distinctive, spiny, egg-shaped seed capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, spanning from the southern United States through Central America and down into South America. It has since become naturalized in various warm climates across the globe, including parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia. Because it thrives in disturbed soils and sunny environments, it often appears in waste areas and along roadsides in many different countries. What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Datura metel contains medicinal alkaloids found primarily in its seeds, leaves, roots, and flowers. These components are utilized in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as asthma, coughs, and various skin conditions. The leaves are often applied topically to reduce inflammation or used in decoctions to alleviate respiratory distress. What are the pharmacological activities of Datura metel?

This plant has anticholinergic, hallucinogenic, neurotoxic, antispasmodic, and analgesic pharmacological activities derived primarily from its tropane alkaloids. The presence of hyoscyamine and scopolamine allows it to function as an effective antispasmodic for treating smooth muscle spasms and as an analgesic for pain relief. However, its potent anticholinergic effects can trigger intense hallucinogenic experiences that alter perception and consciousness. Excessive consumption is dangerous because these same chemical compounds exhibit neurotoxic properties that can cause severe cognitive impairment or physical harm. Careful monitoring is essential due to the narrow margin between these medicinal uses and systemic toxicity.

As discussed in a study, Datura metel exerts anticholinergic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects, and provides protection from organophosphate poisoning. Its medicinal potencies for Parkinson's disease include the treatment of bradycardia and motor sickness. Chronic administration of 50, 100, and 200 mg/kg of Datura metel extract improved the 14-s latency time induced by haloperidol to 54, 54, and 57 s, respectively. The descending times for haloperidol-induced cataleptic mice were significantly reduced from 110 s to 17.7, 17.7, and 12.5 s by these respective chronic doses. These effects are suggested to be due to the synergistic inhibition of alpha-synuclein and dopa decarboxylase by phytoconstituents like atropine and scopoalamine.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the tropane alkaloids atropine, hyoscine, hyoscyamine, and scopolamine. These specific medicinal compounds function as anticholinergic agents by blocking acetylcholine receptors in the nervous system. They are frequently utilized in pharmaceutical applications to treat various gastrointestinal spasms and motion sickness. The precise concentrations of these alkaloids can vary significantly depending on the environmental conditions of the plant's growth. Careful extraction is required because these potent substances can be toxic if administered in incorrect dosages.

As discussed in a study, Datura metel is a medicinal plant containing active phytoconstituents with neuropharmacological potentials. The methanolic extract of Datura metel contains phytochemicals such as atropine and scopolamine that exhibit synergistic inhibition of ASN and DDC. Chronic administration of 50, 100, and 200 mg/kg of Datura metel extract improved the 14-s latency time induced by haloperidol to 54, 54, and 57 s respectively. These doses also reduced descending times from 110 s to 17.7, 17.7, and 12.5 s, compared to 17.5 s for mice administered 30 mg/kg of levodopa on a 30 cm pole. The identified phytochemicals in Datura metel interact more potently with dual targets than levodopa and carbidopa.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating bronchial asthma, motor sickness, neurological ailments, pains, and chronic bronchitis. Its alkaloids act as antispasmodics to help relax the airways in patients suffering from respiratory distress. The medicinal properties of the plant also provide relief from the discomfort of various pains. It is frequently utilized to manage symptoms associated with neurological ailments through its sedative effects. Additionally, its ability to suppress nausea makes it an effective remedy for motor sickness.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe anticholinergic toxicity characterized by nausea, vomiting, dizziness, hallucinations, confusion, tachycardia, dry mouth, blurred vision, mydriasis, urinary retention, delirium, seizures, hyperthermia, coma, and death. The consumption of its alkaloids leads to profound neurological disturbances and significant physiological distress. Users often experience intense visual and auditory hallucinations alongside a complete loss of reality through delirium. Physical symptoms include an increased heart rate and an inability to urinate due to muscle dysfunction. In extreme cases, the body's temperature rises dangerously high, which can lead to seizures or a fatal outcome.
