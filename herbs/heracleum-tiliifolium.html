What's the taxonomical classification of Heracleum tiliifolium?

Heracleum tiliifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Apiales and is a member of the family Apiaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Heracleum, with its unique identity established as the species tiliifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Heracleum tiliifolium has large, compound leaves that are deeply divided into three distinct leaflets, each displaying a shape similar to those of a linden tree. The individual leaflets are broadly ovate or cordate with serrated margins and prominent venation. These leaves are arranged alternately along a thick, hollow stem that often features reddish spots or striations. The plant produces large, flat-topped umbels composed of numerous tiny white flowers. The overall structure is robust and herbaceous, reaching significant heights during its flowering period.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe phototoxicity and skin irritation upon contact, as well as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and dizziness if ingested. The skin reactions often involve painful blistering that becomes significantly worse when the affected areas are exposed to sunlight. If consumed, the botanical toxins frequently trigger acute gastrointestinal distress such as stomach cramps and repeated vomiting. Some individuals may also experience neurological symptoms like dizziness following accidental exposure or ingestion. These varied side effects make the plant highly dangerous to handle without protection.
