Eriosema glomeratum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eriosema glomeratum has prostrate or decumbent herbaceous stems that produce trifoliate leaves characterized by densely pubescent, often tomentose undersides. The foliage typically features leaflets that are ovate to elliptical in shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. Small, bright yellow flowers emerge from the leaf axils in compact, clustered groups known as glomerules. These blossoms possess a distinct papilionaceous structure typical of the Fabaceae family, featuring a prominent standard petal. The plant's overall morphology is defined by this heavy hairiness and the clustered arrangement of its reproductive organs.
