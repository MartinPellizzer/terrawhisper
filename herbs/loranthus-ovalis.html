Loranthus Mistletoe (Loranthus Ovalis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Loranthus ovalis.

Loranthus mistletoe, scientifically known as Loranthus ovalis, is a parasitic plant commonly used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. This medicinal herb is valued for its adaptogenic properties, which are believed to help the body resist stress and maintain balance. Traditionally, it has been used in various cultures to treat ailments such as respiratory issues, fatigue, and digestive problems, often prepared as a tea or tincture. In modern wellness practices, Loranthus mistletoe is gaining attention for its potential immune-boosting effects and its unique compound, loranthol, which may have anti-inflammatory properties. Its rare and distinctive flavor, often described as slightly bitter and earthy, adds to its appeal in both traditional and contemporary herbal applications.
