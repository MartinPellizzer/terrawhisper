Miscanthus sacchariflorus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Miscanthus sacchariflorus has long, narrow leaves that reach up to 3 feet in length and are characterized by a distinctively prominent midrib and a somewhat arching growth habit. The plant typically grows in dense clumps with slender, upright stems that can reach heights of 5 to 8 feet. During the late summer and fall, it produces large, airy panicles of silvery-white flowers that emerge from the upper leaf axils. These inflorescences are highly branched and create a feathery texture throughout the foliage. The root system consists of vigorous rhizomes that allow the grass to spread and form stable colonies in moist environments.
