Trilepisium madagascariense

What's the taxonomical classification of Trilepisium madagascariense?

Trilepisium madagascariense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the biological hierarchy further, it is categorized under the order Rosales and falls within the family Moraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Trilepisium, with its unique identity established by the species name madagascariense.

Trilepisium madagascariense contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and terpenoids. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the plant's diverse biological activities in traditional medicine. What are the therapeutic actions of Trilepisium madagascariense?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including malaria, respiratory infections, and digestive issues. The medicinal properties of its leaves and stems are frequently applied to combat fevers associated with parasitic infections. Local practitioners often use decoctions derived from the plant to alleviate symptoms of coughs and bronchitis. Additionally, its extracts are utilized to manage intestinal discomfort and other gastrointestinal disorders. This species serves as a vital component in traditional Malagasy medicine for managing these specific health conditions.
