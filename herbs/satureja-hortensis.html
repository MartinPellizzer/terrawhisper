Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis)

Greek Oregano (Satureja hortensis) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Mediterranean Region, Southern Europe, and Middle East. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and oil infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its carminative, antispasmodic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and japanese kampo medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface gray-green and lower surface white-woolly, bearing distinct stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are zygomorphic with two lips, the upper lip consisting of two lobes and the lower lip divided into three lobes, featuring a prominent nectar gland at the base of the throat, and displaying a pale purple to violet coloration with a darker hue at the base of the corolla.
Stems
Cylindrical, somewhat square in cross-section, hairy, with opposite branching, and bearing terminal and axillary buds.
