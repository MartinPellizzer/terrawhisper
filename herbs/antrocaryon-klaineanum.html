Antrocaryon klaineanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Antrocaryon klaineanum?

Antrocaryon klaineanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. This species is nested within the family Anacardiaceae, falling under the genus Antrocaryon, and is specifically identified by its species name, klaineanum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Antrocaryon klaineanum?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its documented anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antileishmanial, antiparasitic, and antiplasmodial activities. Research into its chemical constituents suggests that these properties may stem from specific secondary metabolites found within its tissues. The antibacterial effects provide a basis for addressing various microbial infections, while its antiparasitic and antileishmanial capabilities highlight its utility against protozoan threats. Furthermore, its antiplasmodial activity indicates a specific effectiveness against malaria-causing organisms. These diverse biological actions position the species as a valuable subject for future drug development and medicinal studies.

As discussed in a study, Antrocaryon klaineanum Pierre extracts were evaluated for toxic, antibacterial, and anti-inflammatory effects. The lethal concentration LC50 increased from 110.03 ± 1.25 to 15.86 ± 2.21 μg/mL after 24 and 96 hours of exposure. In tadpoles exposed to 7.81 μg/mL extract, the first mortalities of 12.5% were observed on the fifth day. The Ak F2, Ak F3, and Ak F4 fractions from the water-ethanol crude extract demonstrated the greatest antibacterial activities. Additionally, the water, water-acetone, and water-ethanol extracts showed good inhibition of denaturation, and the extracts exhibited good anti-inflammatory activities according to the haemolysis test. Phytochemical characterization revealed four major compounds and a molecular network showing five main clusters.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 20S-hydroxy-24(28)-ergosten-3-one, Antrocarines A-F, antroklaicerebroside, hydroxy-ergostadian, and monogallate epicatechin. These specific chemical constituents are identified through rigorous phytochemical analysis to determine their therapeutic potential. The presence of Antrocarines A-F and antroklaicerebroside suggests unique bioactive properties within the plant's profile. Furthermore, the steroidal structures like 20S-hydroxy-24(28)-ergosten-3-one and hydroxy-ergostadian contribute to its complex chemical makeup. The inclusion of monogallate epicatechin adds further complexity to the plant's medicinal value.

As discussed in a study, the stem bark of Antrocaryon klaineanum contains antiplasmodial ergostane steroids known as Antrocarines A-F. Six new sterols were characterized, which include 4,24(28)-ergostadiene-6α,7α-diol (1), 6α-methoxy-4,24(28)-ergostadiene-7α,20S-diol (2), 6α-methoxy-4,24(28)-ergostadien-7α-ol (3), 20S-hydroxy-24(28)-ergosten-3-one (4), 7α-hydroxy-4,24(28)-ergostadien-3-one (5), and 24(28)-ergostene-3β,6α-diol (6). The study also isolated known steroids 7 and 8. These compounds and the crude extract were evaluated for their ability to inhibit the 3D7 strain of Plasmodium falciparum. Compounds 2, 3, and 8 demonstrated potent activity, whereas the crude extract showed moderate activity.
