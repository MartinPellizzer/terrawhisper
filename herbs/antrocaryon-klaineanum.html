Antrocaryon klaineanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Antrocaryon klaineanum?

Antrocaryon klaineanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. This species is nested within the family Anacardiaceae, falling under the genus Antrocaryon, and is specifically identified by its species name, klaineanum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Antrocaryon klaineanum contains various secondary metabolites including alkaloids, saponins, tannins, and terpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological potential. These bioactive compounds are primarily concentrated within the bark and leaf extracts of the plant. Research suggests that the presence of these phytochemicals may offer antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Local traditional medicine often utilizes these chemical constituents to treat various ailments and skin conditions. Further investigation into these specific molecular structures is necessary to fully understand their therapeutic applications in modern medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Antrocaryon klaineanum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are often prepared into decoctions or pastes to address these ailments. Specifically, the medicinal properties are frequently applied to soothe skin irritation and promote the healing of open sores. When ingested, the extracts are believed to alleviate digestive discomfort and abdominal pain. Local traditional practices rely on these components to manage a range of minor physical maladies.
