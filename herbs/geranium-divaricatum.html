Geranium divaricatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium divaricatum?

Geranium divaricatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Geraniales. This plant is a member of the family Geraniaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Geranium, and is identified by its unique species name, divaricatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium divaricatum has branched stems that are typically hairy and bear palmately lobed leaves with deeply divided segments.

The foliage is characterized by a spreading habit where the petioles connect the leaves to the spreading axes. Its flowers consist of five distinct petals that are often pink or lavender in color and arranged symmetrically around a central reproductive column. The fruit develops into a specialized schizocarp that splits to disperse individual seeds.

These morphological features help the plant thrive in its specific rocky or montane habitats.

Because scientific studies on human toxicity are limited, the exact physiological impacts remain partially undocumented.
