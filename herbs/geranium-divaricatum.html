Geranium divaricatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Geranium divaricatum?

Geranium divaricatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Geraniales. This plant is a member of the family Geraniaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Geranium, and is identified by its unique species name, divaricatum.

What are the herbal preparations of Geranium divaricatum?

Geranium divaricatum is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical wash using the dried or fresh aerial parts of the plant. Practitioners often boil the plant material in water to extract its active compounds for internal consumption to address inflammatory conditions. For external applications, the liquid is cooled and applied directly to the skin to soothe irritations or wounds. Some traditional methods also involve using the plant in tincture form, where alcohol is used to preserve the medicinal properties for long-term storage. These various preparation methods allow the plant's chemical constituents to be delivered effectively based on the specific ailment being treated.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Geranium divaricatum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or applied improperly. Some individuals may experience contact dermatitis characterized by redness, itching, or swelling upon touching the plant. There is also a risk of stomach upset or more severe internal complications if the plant is consumed accidentally. Because scientific studies on human toxicity are limited, the exact physiological impacts remain partially undocumented. Users should exercise extreme caution and avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse reactions.
