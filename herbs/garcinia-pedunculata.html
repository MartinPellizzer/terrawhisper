Garcinia pedunculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Garcinia pedunculata?

Garcinia pedunculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Clusiaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Garcinia and is known by the species name pedunculata.

Its fruit is a small, globose berry that transitions from green to a bright yellow or orange color when ripe. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific medicinal xanthones α-mangostin, β-mangostin, and γ-mangostin which serve as its primary bioactive compounds. These polyphenolic substances are concentrated within the fruit pericarp and exhibit significant antioxidant properties. Researchers have investigated these molecules for their ability to combat oxidative stress and reduce inflammation in cellular models. The unique chemical structures of α-mangostin, β-mangostin, and γ-mangostin contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile of the species. Ongoing studies continue to explore how these distinct compounds might be utilized in developing new therapeutic agents.

As discussed in a study, the fruit rinds of Garcinia pedunculata contain five biologically important medicinal compounds. These five compounds include three xanthones, which are alpha-mangostin, beta-mangostin, and gamma-mangostin. Additionally, the plant contains two polyisoprenylated benzophenones known as xanthochymol and isoxanthochymol. A validated UHPLC-PDA method was utilized to identify and quantify these substances with a total run time of 9 min. The research highlights that there is a wide variation in the concentration of these five compounds within the extracts.
