Garcinia pedunculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Garcinia pedunculata?

Garcinia pedunculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Clusiaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Garcinia and is known by the species name pedunculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Garcinia pedunculata has a small tree or shrub structure characterized by simple, opposite, oblong-elliptic leaves that are glossy green with an entire margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that emerge from short peduncles arranged in axillary clusters. Its fruit is a small, globose berry that transitions from green to a bright yellow or orange color when ripe. The woody stems often exhibit a dense branching pattern which supports the lush, evergreen foliage. These morphological features are essential for identifying the species within its tropical forest habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Thailand, and Malaysia. It is frequently found growing in coastal areas and along the edges of mangrove forests where humidity is high. Within these habitats, the species thrives in sandy soils and seasonally flooded environments. Its distribution is closely tied to warm, monsoon-influenced climates that provide consistent moisture. This specific range allows the species to occupy unique ecological niches in low-lying maritime landscapes.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant grows best in tropical, humid environments with well-draining, acidic soil and requires partial shade to thrive.

It is typically cultivated through seeds or stem cuttings in warm climates where temperatures remain consistently high. Regular watering is essential to maintain moisture levels, although the roots must not sit in stagnant water to prevent rot. Once established, the plant needs organic mulch to help retain soil nutrients and moisture during dry spells.

Careful pruning helps manage its bushy growth habit and encourages the development of its characteristic small, fleshy fruits.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive xanthones, specifically alpha-mangostin, beta-mangostin, and gamma-mangostin, which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These specific phytochemicals are recognized for their significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that these xanthones can assist in combating various oxidative stresses within biological systems. The presence of these unique chemical structures contributes to the plant's overall therapeutic potential in traditional medicine. Scientists continue to study these individual compounds to better understand their roles in pharmacological applications.

According to a study published by "Journal of AOAC International", the fruit rinds of Garcinias pedunculata contain five biologically important compounds. These medicinal compounds include 3 xanthones, specifically α-mangostin, β-mangostin, and γ-mangostin. Additionally, the plant contains 2 polyisoprenylated benzophenones known as xanthochymol and isoxanthochymol. A validated UHPLC-PDA method with a total run time of 9 min was used to analyze these extracts. The research indicates that there is a wide variation in the concentration of these 5 compounds within the species.

These findings contribute to the traditional remedies used for treating diabetic ailments in the rural Dhemaji district of Assam, Northeast India.
