Garcinia pedunculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Garcinia pedunculata?

Garcinia pedunculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Clusiaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Garcinia and is known by the species name pedunculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Garcinia pedunculata has a small tree or shrub structure characterized by simple, opposite, oblong-elliptic leaves that are glossy green with an entire margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that emerge from short peduncles arranged in axillary clusters. Its fruit is a small, globose berry that transitions from green to a bright yellow or orange color when ripe. The woody stems often exhibit a dense branching pattern which supports the lush, evergreen foliage. These morphological features are essential for identifying the species within its tropical forest habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Thailand, and Malaysia. Within these habitats, the species thrives in sandy soils and seasonally flooded environments. Its distribution is closely tied to warm, monsoon-influenced climates that provide consistent moisture. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These polyphenolic substances are concentrated within the fruit pericarp and exhibit significant antioxidant properties. Researchers have investigated these molecules for their ability to combat oxidative stress and reduce inflammation in cellular models. The unique chemical structures of α-mangostin, β-mangostin, and γ-mangostin contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile of the species. Ongoing studies continue to explore how these distinct compounds might be utilized in developing new therapeutic agents.

What side effects this plant can have?

There is also limited clinical data regarding its long-term effects on liver or kidney function. Due to these risks, caution should be exercised when using the plant for medicinal purposes.
