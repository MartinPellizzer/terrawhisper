Garcinia pedunculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Garcinia pedunculata?

Garcinia pedunculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Clusiaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Garcinia and is known by the species name pedunculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Garcinia pedunculata has a small tree or shrub structure characterized by simple, opposite, oblong-elliptic leaves that are glossy green with an entire margin. Its fruit is a small, globose berry that transitions from green to a bright yellow or orange color when ripe. The woody stems often exhibit a dense branching pattern which supports the lush, evergreen foliage. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Garcinia pedunculata targets inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and lipoxygenase (LOX), alongside various pro-inflammatory cytokines and oxidative stress markers. By modulating these signaling pathways, the plant's compounds effectively reduce the production of nitric oxide and prostaglandin E2. Additionally, its chemical constituents interact with various protein kinases to mitigate cellular damage caused by reactive oxygen species. These diverse molecular interactions underpin the plant's documented antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.
