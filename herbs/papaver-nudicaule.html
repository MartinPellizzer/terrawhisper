Papaver nudicaule

What's the taxonomical classification of Papaver nudicaule?

Papaver nudicaule belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Papaveraceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Papaver and the specific species nudicaule.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Papaver nudicaule has a basal rosette of pinnatifid or deeply lobed leaves and a leafless, erect flowering stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are green, often with a slightly hairy texture, and are concentrated near the ground rather than along the stalk. Its flowers are solitary and terminal, featuring four delicate, pale yellow or white petals that surround a central cluster of numerous stamens. The fruit develops into a small, capsule-shaped seedpod that stands upright atop the stem after the petals fall. Each capsule contains many tiny, dark seeds which are dispersed once the structure dries and opens at the top.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining, sandy loam soil and a full sun exposure of at least six to eight hours daily to thrive.

Seeds should be sown directly into the garden during early spring because the species has a sensitive taproot that does not tolerate transplanting well. Maintaining consistent moisture during the germination phase is critical, although the soil must not remain waterlogged to prevent root rot. Once established, the plant is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers a temperate climate with cool nights.

Careful thinning of seedlings ensures adequate airflow and space for the mature stems to develop properly.
