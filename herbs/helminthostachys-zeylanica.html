What's the taxonomical classification of Helminthostachys zeylanica?

Helminthostachys zeylanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Ophioglossidae. Following its placement in the order Ophioglossales, the plant is further organized into the family Ophioglossaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Helminthostachys, and its unique species designation, zeylanica.

      What are the herbal preparations of Helminthostachys zeylanica?

      Helminthostachys zeylanica is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant parts in water. This traditional method is used to release the medicinal compounds from the tough plant tissues into a drinkable form. In some regional practices, the leaves are also crushed to create a fresh paste applied topically to the skin. Some practitioners may incorporate the dried plant material into infusions, which involves steeping the herb in hot water rather than boiling it. These various preparations allow the plant's properties to be utilized for both internal and external therapeutic purposes.
