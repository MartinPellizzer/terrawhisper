Helminthostachys zeylanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Helminthostachys zeylanica?

Helminthostachys zeylanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Ophioglossidae. Following its placement in the order Ophioglossales, the plant is further organized into the family Ophioglossaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Helminthostachys, and its unique species designation, zeylanica.

According to a study published by "Molecular neurobiology", the roots of the fern Helminthostachys zeylanica (L.) Hook. (Ophioglossaceae) possess an array of medicinal properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. The extracts E0, E1, and E2 significantly reduce MMP-9 induced by BK in brain astrocytes (RBA-1 cells). These H. zeylanica extracts can inhibit BK-stimulated phosphorylation of c-Src, Pyk2, and PKC(α/δ). Additionally, BK-stimulated NADPH oxidase (Nox)-derived reactive oxygen species (ROS) generation is attenuated by pretreatment with these extracts. The extracts also blocked activation of MAPK pathways, specifically ERK1/2 and p38 MAPK, by BK. This indicates that the plant may have anti-inflammatory activity by reducing BK-induced ROS-dependent MMP-9 expression.
