Hypericum bellum

What's the taxonomical classification of Hypericum bellum?

Hypericum bellum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Malpighiales and falls under the family Hypericaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Hypericum, and its specific species designation, bellum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hypericum bellum has opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins and terminal clusters of bright yellow, five-petaled flowers. The stems are typically woody at the base and exhibit a square or slightly angular cross-section. Small, translucent glandular dots are scattered across the leaf surfaces, which is a characteristic trait of the genus. The floral structure features numerous prominent stamens that create a fuzzy appearance in the center of the bloom. Once the flowering period concludes, the plant produces small, dehiscent capsules containing numerous tiny seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude mountainous regions of the Himalayas, spanning across parts of India, Nepal, Bhutan, and Tibet. It thrives specifically in alpine and subalpine zones where rocky terrain and cold climates are prevalent. These populations are often found at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters above sea level. The species occupies narrow ecological niches characterized by well-drained soils and intense sunlight. Its distribution is largely constrained by the extreme environmental conditions of these high-elevation habitats.
