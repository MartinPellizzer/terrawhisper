What's the taxonomical classification of Trivalvaria costata?

Trivalvaria costata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Magnoliales and the family Annonaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Trivalvaria, and its species name, costata.

The plant develops small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. These specific alkaloids serve as the primary chemical constituents responsible for the plant's unique biological activities. Researchers often study these molecules to understand their potential therapeutic applications in pharmacology. The presence of heteropsine and urabaine suggests complex interactions within various physiological systems. Understanding the concentration of trivalcostaisoquinoline and the Bidebilines is essential for determining the overall potency of the plant.

As discussed in a study, a phytochemical investigation of the twig extract of Trivalvaria costata identified ten undescribed dimeric apoRPINE alkaloids named trivalcostatines A-J, one undescribed isoquinoline alkaloid named trivalcostaisoquinoline, and four known apoRPINE alkaloids. Three of the dimeric apoRPINE structures were confirmed through single crystal X-ray diffraction analysis. Bidebilines A and B, heteropsine, and urabaine demonstrated α-glucosidase inhibitory activities. These specific compounds showed IC50 values within the range of 4.1-11 μM.
