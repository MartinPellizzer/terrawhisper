Trivalvaria costata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trivalvaria costata has a creeping rhizomatous stem with narrow, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a distichous pattern. The leaves are characterized by their parallel venation and acute tips. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. The plant develops small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. This species typically thrives in moist, shaded forest environments where its low-growing structure allows it to spread across the ground cover.
