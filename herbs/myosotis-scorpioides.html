Side Effects

Myosotis scorpioides can have some side effects, such as skin irritation or allergic reactions when the plant is handled or ingested.

The sap of the plant contains compounds that may cause contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals, leading to redness, itching, and blisters. Ingestion of any part of the plant can result in gastrointestinal discomfort, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. While there is limited research on long-term effects, some studies suggest that the plant may have toxic properties in higher doses.

It is advisable to avoid direct contact with the plant and to seek medical attention if any adverse reactions occur.
