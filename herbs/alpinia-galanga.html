Java Ginger (Alpinia galanga)

Java Ginger (Alpinia galanga) is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, Indonesia, and India. Traditionally, its rhizomes, leaves, and stems have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, carminative, and stimulant actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface pale green and lower surface darker green, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are racemose with pale yellow to white flowers, actinomorphic, having three sepals and three petals, each with a distinct midvein, and three stamens with filaments shorter than the anthers.
Stems
Cylindrical, herbaceous, glabrous, terete, with opposite branching, and possessing a pungent aroma.
