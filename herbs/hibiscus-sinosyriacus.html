Hibiscus sinosyriacus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hibiscus sinosyriacus has woody, deciduous stems that support large, palmately lobed leaves with serrated margins and a dark green color. The plant produces large, funnel-shaped flowers that typically feature five spreading petals and a prominent central staminal column. These blossoms can vary in color from deep red to pale pink or white, often displaying a contrasting colored throat. The foliage remains relatively coarse and can reach heights of ten to fifteen feet in mature specimens. Small, woody capsules form following pollination to house the developing seeds.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause allergic skin reactions, respiratory distress, or digestive upset in sensitive individuals if the flowers or leaves are ingested or touched. While generally considered non-toxic to humans, some people may experience contact dermatitis characterized by redness, itching, or swelling upon skin contact. Ingestion of the plant material could potentially lead to nausea or stomach discomfort in certain cases. There is also limited clinical research regarding its long-term systemic effects on human health.
