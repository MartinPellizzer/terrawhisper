Hibiscus sinosyriacus

What's the taxonomical classification of Hibiscus sinosyriacus?

Hibiscus syriacus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it falls under the order Malvales and belongs to the family Malvaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Hibiscus and is specifically designated as the species sinoysriacus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hibiscus sinosyriacus has woody, deciduous stems that support large, palmately lobed leaves with serrated margins and a dark green color. The plant produces large, funnel-shaped flowers that typically feature five spreading petals and a prominent central staminal column. These blossoms can vary in color from deep red to pale pink or white, often displaying a contrasting colored throat. The foliage remains relatively coarse and can reach heights of ten to fifteen feet in mature specimens. Small, woody capsules form following pollination to house the developing seeds.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant thrives in full sun and well-draining, fertile soil with a neutral to slightly acidic pH.

It requires regular watering during dry spells to maintain consistent moisture without allowing the roots to sit in standing water. Pruning is most effective in late winter or early spring to encourage vigorous new growth and prolific flowering. Once established, the shrub is quite hardy and can tolerate various soil types provided they are not overly compacted.

Periodic mulching helps retain necessary soil moisture and suppresses competing weeds around the base.
