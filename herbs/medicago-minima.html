Medicago minima

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Medicago minima has prostrate, trailing stems that form dense mats, trifoliate leaves with small leaflets, and bright yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically features highly branched, spreading stems that stay close to the ground to maximize coverage. Each leaf consists of three oval to oblong leaflets that are often slightly hairy on the undersides. The small, fragrant flowers are borne in compact clusters and belong to the Fabaceae family. Its seed pods are small, cylindrical, and slightly curved, containing a few tiny seeds.
