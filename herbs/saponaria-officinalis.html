Saponaria officinalis, commonly known as soapwort, is a perennial herb that has been traditionally used to treat a variety of health conditions, including skin irritations, wounds, and respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma.

The herb is also rich in antioxidants, which contribute to its numerous health benefits, including reducing oxidative stress and improving overall well-being. The main therapeutic properties of Saponaria officinalis include its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and emollient properties, which are attributed to its therapeutic effectiveness.

The herb is rich in bioactive compounds such as saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties, and is often prepared as a decoction or infusion to treat various health conditions.
