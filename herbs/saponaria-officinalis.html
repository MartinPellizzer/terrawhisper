Soaproot (Saponaria officinalis)

Soaproot (Saponaria officinalis) is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, antispasmodic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.
