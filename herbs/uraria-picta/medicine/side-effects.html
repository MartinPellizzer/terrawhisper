10 Possible Side Effects Of Uraria Picta

Uraria picta has some side effects when used improperly, such as urinary retention and kidney stones.

These side effects can occur due to the herb's diuretic properties and the presence of certain alkaloids. If left untreated, urinary retention can lead to kidney damage and urinary tract infections, severely impacting daily life and causing debilitating pain and discomfort.

In severe cases, kidney stones can cause permanent damage.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Uraria picta causes allergic reactions due to its chemical composition which includes alkaloids and glycosides.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

The exact mechanism of the allergic reaction is not fully understood, but it is thought that the body's immune system mistakenly identifies these chemicals as foreign substances, resulting in an inflammatory response.

2. Triggers anxiety attacks

Uraria picta triggers anxiety attacks in some individuals.

The herb's stimulatory properties can cause an increase in cortisol levels, leading to feelings of restlessness and heightened awareness, which can culminate in panic and anxiety episodes.

Additionally, the plant's bitter taste and pungent aroma may stimulate the nervous system, further exacerbating anxious sensations.

3. Induces vomiting episodes

Uraria picta induces vomiting episodes when consumed due to the presence of alkaloids such as urarianine and urariene.

These compounds stimulate the nervous system, causing a sudden contraction of the muscles in the stomach, leading to nausea and subsequent vomiting.

The vomiting episodes can be intense and may lead to dehydration if not managed properly.

4. Lowers blood pressure

Uraria picta lowers blood pressure by inhibiting the enzyme angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), which plays a crucial role in regulating blood pressure.

This inhibition leads to a decrease in the production of angiotensin II, a potent vasoconstrictor, resulting in vasodilation and lowered blood pressure.

Additionally, Uraria picta's antioxidant properties may also contribute to its blood-pressure-lowering effect by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation in blood vessels, further exacerbating the hypotensive response.

5. Interferes with sleep patterns

Uraria picta interferes with sleep patterns due to its stimulating effects on the central nervous system.

The plant contains alkaloids that can cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or remain asleep throughout the night.

As a result, users may experience insomnia or disrupted sleep patterns, leading to feelings of fatigue, irritability, and decreased cognitive function.

6. Increases heart rate

Uraria picta increases heart rate due to its stimulant properties.

The plant contains alkaloids such as urarine and pictamine, which are responsible for this effect. When ingested, these compounds can stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

This reaction is a result of the plant's ability to increase sympathetic activity, causing the body to release more adrenaline.

7. Provokes skin rashes

Uraria picta provokes skin rashes due to its content of toxic alkaloids and glycosides.

The plant's extracts can cause an allergic reaction or irritation on the skin, leading to the development of redness, itching, and swelling. This adverse effect is often seen in individuals who have sensitive skin or are prone to allergic reactions.

As a result, some people may experience discomfort, annoyance, or even pain due to these unwanted reactions.

8. Disrupts digestive habits

Uraria picta disrupts digestive habits by influencing the gut flora and altering the normal functioning of the gastrointestinal system.

The compound beraprost, present in Uraria picta, has been shown to stimulate bowel movements and reduce constipation, leading to changes in digestive patterns.

Additionally, the herb's saponin content may contribute to its effects on digestion, potentially causing temporary disruptions in bowel habits.

9. Aggravates respiratory issues

Uraria picta aggravates respiratory issues due to its ability to cause bronchospasms and constrict airways.

The herb's volatile oils can irritate the mucous membranes of the lungs and throat, leading to increased mucus production and coughing fits. Additionally, Uraria picta may stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical that can exacerbate asthma symptoms and worsen respiratory conditions such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

As a result, individuals with pre-existing respiratory issues may experience worsening symptoms when using Uraria picta.

10. Affects liver function

Uraria picta affects liver function due to its ability to inhibit the activity of certain enzymes involved in liver metabolism.

This may lead to an accumulation of toxic substances in the body, causing adverse reactions. Additionally, Uraria picta's potential to interact with other medications may further exacerbate liver dysfunction.

As a result, users may experience symptoms such as jaundice, fatigue, and abdominal pain.
