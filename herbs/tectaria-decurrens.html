Tectaria decurrens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tectaria decurrens has pinnately compound leaves that typically feature 2 to 4 pairs of oblong to lanceolate leaflets arranged along a central rachis. The leaflets are characterized by entire margins and an acute or obtuse apex, often displaying a smooth or slightly textured surface. These compound structures are supported by slender petioles that emerge from a creeping or climbing rhizomatous stem. The plant often exhibits a pendulous growth habit, allowing the foliage to drape gracefully from its substrate. Small, inconspicuous flowers may emerge from the axils, contributing to the overall delicate morphology of the species.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Tectaria decurrens can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, or allergic reactions if the plant material comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or a localized rash after handling the foliage. While extensive clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, caution is advised when using the plant in herbal preparations. Ingestion of unknown quantities could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress or other systemic sensitivities. It is important to avoid contact with eyes and mucous membranes to prevent further inflammation.
