Tectaria decurrens

What's the taxonomical classification of Tectaria decurrens?

Tecartia decurrens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is further categorized into the family Polypodiaceae, specifically within the genus Tecartia, and is identified by its unique species name, decurrens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tectaria decurrens has pinnately compound leaves that typically feature 2 to 4 pairs of oblong to lanceolate leaflets arranged along a central rachis. The leaflets are characterized by entire margins and an acute or obtuse apex, often displaying a smooth or slightly textured surface. These compound structures are supported by slender petioles that emerge from a creeping or climbing rhizomatous stem. The plant often exhibits a pendulous growth habit, allowing the foliage to drape gracefully from its substrate.
