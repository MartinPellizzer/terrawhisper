What's the taxonomical classification of Centaurium pulchellum?

Centaurium pulchellum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianales. As a member of the family Gentianaecae, it is placed within the genus Centaurium, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as Centaurium pulchellum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

These blossoms are predominantly pink or pale rose in hue and emerge from the leaf axils. Small, yellowish centers distinguish the floral structure, while the plant often produces small, capsule-like fruits upon maturity. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds centauriol and centaurione, which are classified as secoiridoid glycosides. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for many of the plant's biological activities. Research indicates that these compounds contribute to the bitter taste characteristic of the species. They are often studied for their potential roles in various therapeutic applications. The presence of these unique molecules distinguishes Centaurium pulchellum from other related botanical species.
