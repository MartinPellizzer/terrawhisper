Huperzia copelandiana

Huperzia copelandiana, commonly known as the "New Caledonian liverwort," is a species of liverwort that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

This plant has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various health benefits, including its potential to improve memory and cognitive function, as well as its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The therapeutic actions of Huperzia copelandiana are attributed to its bioactive constituents, including huperzine A, which is known to inhibit acetylcholinesterase and thus increase acetylcholine levels in the brain, leading to improved memory and cognitive function.

The bioactive constituents of Huperzia copelandiana also include alkaloids, phenolic compounds, and flavonoids, which contribute to its overall health benefits and therapeutic actions.
