Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Aristolochia, with its specific designation being the species tubiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aristolochia tubiflora has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and tubular, yellowish-green flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Its unique inflorescences are characterized by elongated, pipe-like corollas that often exhibit subtle longitudinal striations.       What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe nephrotoxicity, progressive uropathy, acute renal failure, and potent carcinogenicity in humans. The presence of aristolochic acid leads to direct damage to the kidney tissues and the urinary tract. This damage often manifests as urothelium injury, which can cause permanent renal failure. Furthermore, the plant's high level of carcinogenicity is linked to specific mutations in DNA. These genetic changes frequently lead to uropathy-induced urothelial carcinoma in the bladder and kidneys.
