What's the taxonomical classification of Lonicera chrysantha?

Lonicera chrysantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Dipsacales, it is placed within the family Caprifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Lonicera, with its specific designation being the species chrysantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera chrysantha has decumbent to ascending woody stems that reach heights of 1 to 2 feet, featuring opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins.

The foliage is typically bright green and can vary in size depending on the specific habitat. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, tubular, yellow flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are often accompanied by small, berry-like fruits that transition from green to a dark blue or black color upon ripening.

The overall structure of the shrub is spreading and somewhat bushy, making it effective for ground cover.
