Lonicera chrysantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lonicera chrysantha has decumbent to ascending woody stems that reach heights of 1 to 2 feet, featuring opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically bright green and can vary in size depending on the specific habitat. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, tubular, yellow flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are often accompanied by small, berry-like fruits that transition from green to a dark blue or black color upon ripening. The overall structure of the shrub is spreading and somewhat bushy, making it effective for ground cover.
