Sagina ciliata

Sagina ciliata, also known as Marsh Pepperwort or Tufted Pearlwort, is a species of flowering plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis and coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The health benefits of Sagina ciliata include its potential to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of respiratory conditions, as well as its possible antioxidant and antimicrobial properties.

In herbal preparations, Sagina ciliata is often used as a tea or tincture to harness its medicinal properties, although more research is needed to confirm its effectiveness and safety for human consumption.
