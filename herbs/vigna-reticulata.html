Black Gram (Vigna Reticulata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Vigna reticulata.

Black gram, scientifically known as Vigna reticulata, is a leguminous plant widely recognized as both a medicinal herb and a nutrient-rich spice. It has been valued for centuries in traditional medicine for its adaptogenic properties, which help the body manage stress and promote resilience. The primary benefits of black gram include its high protein content, rich supply of dietary fiber, and presence of essential minerals like iron and magnesium, which support overall health and energy levels. In Ayurveda and traditional Indian medicine, black gram has been used to treat digestive disorders, anemia, and skin conditions, while modern wellness practices incorporate it into diets for its cholesterol-lowering and antioxidant effects. Its unique flavor, combining a slight nuttiness with a subtle earthiness, along with its rare bioactive compounds such as alkaloids and polyphenols, make it a valuable ingredient in both culinary and therapeutic applications.
