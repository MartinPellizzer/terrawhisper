Cola heterophyllla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Cola. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, heterophylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cola heterophylla has heterophyllous leaves that transition from entire, lanceolate shapes in juvenile stages to deeply pinnatifid or lobed forms as the plant matures.

The leaf blades are typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the stem. These leaves possess prominent pinnate venation and a smooth, entire margin on the younger leaflets. The plant also develops woody stems that support a dense, shrubby or small tree-like growth habit.

Small, inconspicuous flowers often emerge from the axils of these varying leaf structures.
