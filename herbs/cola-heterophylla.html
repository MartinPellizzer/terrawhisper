Cola heterophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cola heterophylla has heterophyllous leaves that transition from entire, lanceolate shapes in juvenile stages to deeply pinnatifid or lobed forms as the plant matures. The leaf blades are typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the stem. These leaves possess prominent pinnate venation and a smooth, entire margin on the younger leaflets. The plant also develops woody stems that support a dense, shrubby or small tree-like growth habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers often emerge from the axils of these varying leaf structures.
