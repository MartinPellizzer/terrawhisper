Impatiens holocentra

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens holocentra?

Impatiens holocentra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is part of the order Ericales and the family Balsaminaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Impatiens, and its unique species designation, holocentra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens holocentra has succulent, herbaceous stems that branch extensively and bear serrated, elliptical leaves arranged in an opposite pattern. The foliage typically features bright green surfaces with prominent venation and slightly pointed tips. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the zygomorphic flower, which possesses a spurred nectar tube and vibrant pink or reddish petals. These blooms are often accompanied by small, capsule-like fruit that dehisces explosively to disperse seeds. The overall structure of the plant is compact and lush, characteristic of many species within the Balsaminaceae family.
