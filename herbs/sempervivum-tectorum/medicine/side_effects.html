1. Causes allergic reactions

Sempervivum tectorum causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, which are chemical compounds that can trigger an immune response in sensitive people.

These compounds are responsible for the plant's characteristic bitter taste and aroma, but they can also cause skin irritation, itching, and redness in those who are allergic to them.

2. Induces nausea

Sempervivum tectorum induces nausea due to its chemical composition, which includes saponins and flavonoids.

These compounds can cause stomach upset and trigger nausea in some individuals.

The plant's high concentration of these chemicals may also lead to gastrointestinal irritation, contributing to feelings of queasiness or vomiting.

3. Triggers headaches

Sempervivum tectorum triggers headaches due to its ability to interact with certain medications and alter their effectiveness.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as saponins and flavonoids, can bind to proteins in the body and disrupt normal physiological processes, leading to adverse reactions like headaches.

Additionally, Sempervivum tectorum may also cause increased blood pressure and heart rate, further contributing to headache development.

4. Produces stomach upset

Sempervivum tectorum produces stomach upset in some individuals due to its saponin content.

These natural compounds can cause gastrointestinal disturbances such as nausea and stomach discomfort when consumed in large quantities or without proper preparation.

The saponins may bind to stomach acid, leading to an imbalance of gut flora and resulting in stomach upset.

5. Causes dizziness

Sempervivum tectorum causes dizziness due to its high concentration of saponins and flavonoids, which can alter the normal functioning of the brain and nervous system.

These compounds may affect the balance and equilibrium centers in the inner ear, leading to feelings of lightheadedness or dizziness.

Additionally, Sempervivum tectorum's potential interactions with other medications may also contribute to this side effect.

6. Elicits vomiting

Sempervivum tectorum elicits vomiting due to its bitter and unpleasant taste, which can stimulate the gag reflex and induce nausea.

The plant's high concentration of saponins and anthraquinones may also contribute to this side effect by irritating the digestive system and causing stomach upset.

Additionally, the sap of Sempervivum tectorum contains a compound that has been shown to have emetic properties, further increasing the likelihood of vomiting.

7. Inflames skin

Sempervivum tectorum inflames skin due to its sap containing saponins and sesquiterpenes, which can cause allergic reactions or irritate the skin.

When coming into contact with Sempervivum tectorum, these compounds can trigger an inflammatory response, leading to redness, itching, and swelling.

This reaction is often more pronounced in individuals with sensitive skin or those who have previously shown allergies to similar plant species.

8. Irritates eyes

Sempervivum tectorum irritates eyes due to its sap and latex content.

When the plant's juices come into contact with the eyes, they can cause discomfort, redness, and itching.

The allergic reaction occurs when the body's immune system reacts to the proteins present in the plant's sap, leading to an inflammatory response that manifests as eye irritation.

9. Aggravates asthma

Sempervivum tectorum aggravates asthma due to its allergenic properties.

The plant's sap and extracts contain various compounds that can trigger allergic reactions in susceptible individuals, leading to wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

This is because the immune system mistakenly perceives these compounds as harmful and releases histamine, a chemical mediator responsible for the characteristic symptoms of an asthma attack.

10. Increases blood pressure

Sempervivum tectorum increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of glycosides and saponins.

These compounds can cause the body's blood vessels to constrict, leading to an increase in blood pressure.

Additionally, the plant's active ingredients may stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, further contributing to a rise in blood pressure.
