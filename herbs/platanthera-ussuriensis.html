What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Platanthera ussuriensis has an herbaceous perennial structure characterized by slender, erect stems that reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and bear several lanceolate, opposite leaves. At the apex of the stem, the plant produces a dense, terminal raceme of small, white, or pale greenish flowers. Each flower features a long, narrow spur at the base of the labellum, which is an adaptation for specific pollinators. The roots are typically composed of fleshy, tuberous rhizomes that facilitate nutrient storage.
