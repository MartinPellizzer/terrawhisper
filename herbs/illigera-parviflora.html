What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illigera parviflora has woody, climbing vines characterized by opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical with entire margins and acute tips. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying a yellowish or greenish hue depending on the stage of development. The fruit is typically a small, fleshy drupe that develops from the fertilized ovary.
