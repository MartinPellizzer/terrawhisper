Illigera parviflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Illigera parviflora?

Illigera parviflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Laurales and belongs to the family Hernandiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Illigera, and its specific species name, parviflora.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and various gastrointestinal ailments. The leaves and stems are often prepared into decoctions to alleviate symptoms of stomach aches and indigestion. Topical applications of its extracts are frequently utilized to manage skin irritations and localized swelling. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to address respiratory issues and minor feverish conditions. This botanical species serves as a significant component in several regional medicinal traditions for managing these specific health concerns.
