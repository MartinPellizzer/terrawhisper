Syzygium tetragonum

What's the taxonomical classification of Syzygium tetragonum?

Syzygium tetragonum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Myrtales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Myrtaceae, placing it within the genus Syzygium, with the specific designation of the species tetragonum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its woody stems are characterized by a smooth bark that may eventually become slightly textured as the tree matures. The plant produces small, white or cream-colored flowers that are clustered together in terminal panicles.
