Syzygium tetragonum

What's the taxonomical classification of Syzygium tetragonum?

Syzygium tetragonum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Myrtales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Myrtaceae, placing it within the genus Syzygium, with the specific designation of the species tetragonum.

      What are the herbal preparations of Syzygium tetragonum?

      Syzygium tetragonum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using its leaves, bark, or fruits. The plant's aromatic properties allow it to be processed into liquid extracts that are often used in traditional medicinal practices. When making a decoction, the harder woody parts are boiled in water to extract concentrated bioactive compounds. Conversely, leaves are frequently steeped as a milder infusion to preserve delicate volatile oils. Local healers may also grind the fresh material into a fine paste for direct application to skin irritations.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Syzygium tetragonum can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, digestive upset, or allergic dermatitis if handled or ingested improperly. While many species in the Syzygium genus are studied for medicinal properties, the specific toxicological profile of this particular plant remains insufficiently documented in mainstream literature. Potential contact with the leaves or sap might trigger localized redness or itching in sensitive individuals. Because scientific data on its safety is limited, any direct consumption or topical application carries an unpredictable risk of harm. Users should exercise extreme caution and consult a professional before any exposure to the plant.
