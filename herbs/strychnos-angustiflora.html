Strychnos angustiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strychnos angustiflora has woody climbing vines characterized by slender, elongated stems and oppositely arranged, elliptical leaves with narrow bases. The foliage is typically leathery in texture and features prominent midribs on the underside. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. These blossoms transition into fleshy, globose fruits that contain several hard, woody seeds. The overall structure of the plant allows it to scramble through forest undergrowth using its twining habit.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

In traditional medicinal practices, extracts from the plant are utilized for their potential antimalarial and antimicrobial properties. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Strychnos angustiflora targets various neurotransmitter receptors and enzymes, primarily through the interaction of indole alkaloids with nicotinic acetylcholine receptors and GABA receptors. The bioactive alkaloids contained within the plant act as competitive antagonists or agonists depending on their specific chemical structure. By modulating these ion channels, the compounds influence neuromuscular transmission and central nervous system excitability. These interactions can lead to physiological effects such as muscle relaxation or altered sensory perception. Such molecular mechanisms underpin the plant's traditional use in treating neurological and inflammatory conditions.

    Strychnos angustiflora is prepared as a decoction from the bark or roots, a liquid infusion used primarily for medicinal purposes.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Strychnos angustiflora can cause severe neurological toxicity, muscle spasms, and gastrointestinal distress due to the presence of potent indole alkaloids. Ingestion of the plant may lead to involuntary muscular contractions and heightened sensitivity to external stimuli. These alkaloids can interfere with spinal reflexes, potentially resulting in painful convulsions or seizures if consumed in high doses. Furthermore, patients might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain shortly after contact with the plant material. Careful monitoring is essential because the toxicological profile of this species poses significant risks to the central nervous system.
