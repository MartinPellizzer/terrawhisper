Trachyphrynium violaceum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trachyphrynium violaceuum has opposite, petiolate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The stems are woody and can develop a somewhat scrambling or climbing growth habit as the plant matures. These morphological features collectively define its structural presence within its native habitat.
