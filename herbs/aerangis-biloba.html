Aerangis biloba

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aerangiis biloba has pendulous, fleshy, cylindrical roots and leaves that are narrowly elliptical in shape. The plant is an epiphytic orchid characterized by its creeping rhizome which allows it to cling to tree bark. Its foliage is typically leathery and arranged in a way that supports its climbing habit. The inflorescence produces fragrant, white flowers that often feature distinct lobes on the labellum. These blooms are typically held on long, slender peduncles that dangle from the main body of the plant.

      Aerangis biloba is prepared as a concentrated decoction, a fine powdered extract, or a topical infused oil to utilize its active compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Aerangis biloba can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if the plant material is ingested or touched without protection. While specific toxicological data is limited, many orchid species contain alkaloids or organic compounds that may trigger hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. Contact with the sap or leaves can result in redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. If swallowed, the plant might lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain. It is generally recommended to wear gloves when handling the specimen to prevent these adverse reactions.
