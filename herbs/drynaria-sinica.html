Drynaria sinica

What's the taxonomical classification of Drynaria sinica?

Drynaria sinica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, following the order Polypodiales. Within this lineage, it is a member of the family Polypodiaceae and falls under the genus Drynaria, specifically identified as the species sinica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Drynaria sinica contains bioactive alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids that contribute to its diverse pharmacological properties. The specific alkaloid profiles within the plant are primarily responsible for its potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Research indicates that its chemical constituents also exhibit significant antioxidant activity, which helps protect cells from oxidative stress. Furthermore, certain compounds found in the roots and stems demonstrate antimicrobial properties capable of inhibiting various pathogens. These complex medicinal compounds make the plant a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents in traditional and modern medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Drynaria sinica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, skin infections, gastrointestinal issues, and various types of pain. The extracts are frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote healing of wounds. In traditional medicine, oral preparations are often used to manage stomach discomfort and digestive ailments. Some studies also suggest its potential effectiveness in reducing systemic inflammation within the body. These therapeutic applications rely on the specific bioactive compounds found within the plant's leaves and roots.
