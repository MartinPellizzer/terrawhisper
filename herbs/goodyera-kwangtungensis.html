Goodyera kwangtungensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Goodyera kwangtungensis?

Goodyera kwangtungensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Goodyera and is designated by the species name kwangtungensis.

What are the herbal preparations of Goodyera kwangtungensis?

Goodyera kwangtungensis is prepared primarily as a decoction of the roots, a leaf infusion, or a crushed paste for topical application. These various medicinal preparations are used to treat a wide range of ailments including respiratory issues and digestive discomfort. When the roots are boiled in water, the resulting liquid is often consumed to address internal inflammations. The leaves can also be steeped to create a tea that serves as a mild sedative or tonic. Additionally, applying the plant in a macerated form directly to the skin helps soothe local swellings and pains.
