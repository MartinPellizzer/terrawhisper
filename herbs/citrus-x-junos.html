Citrus × junos

What's the taxonomical classification of Citrus × junos?

Citrus × junos belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Sapindales. This plant is a part of the family Rutaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Citrus, with the species designation junos.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Citrus × junos has an evergreen growth habit characterized by spreading, bushy branches, dark green elliptical leaves with slightly serrated margins, and small white fragrant flowers. The foliage often displays a glossy texture that helps the plant retain moisture. Its fruit is a medium-sized citrus that transitions from green to a deep yellow or orange when fully ripe. Each fruit features a thick, pulpy rind and contains juicy segments filled with acidic juice. The overall structure of the plant provides dense canopy cover suitable for subtropical environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical regions of southern China and Taiwan. It primarily thrives in the warm, humid climates found throughout the coastal areas of these territories. While its natural range is concentrated in East Asia, it has been widely introduced to other tropical and subtropical zones globally through cultivation. Many gardeners and farmers now grow it in various parts of the world where frost is minimal. Its specific environmental requirements limit its successful naturalization to similar maritime or subtropical landscapes.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, commonly known as the sudachi, requires a subtropical climate with well-draining, slightly acidic soil and consistent moisture to thrive.

Growers typically plant these trees in full sun to ensure the development of flavorful, high-acid fruit. Because they are susceptible to frost, protecting the root systems during winter months is essential for long-term health. Regular pruning helps maintain manageable tree sizes and promotes better airflow through the canopy to prevent fungal diseases.

Proper fertilization with nitrogen-rich organic matter supports the vigorous growth needed for a productive citrus harvest.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as an essential oil, a tincture, a decoction or infusion of the dried peel, or a concentrated extract. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, diarrhea, stomach upset, and allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Consuming parts of the citrus plant may lead to significant gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or diarrhea. Additionally, a person may suffer an allergic reaction characterized by skin irritation or respiratory issues. It is important to approach the use of this species with caution to avoid these adverse effects.
