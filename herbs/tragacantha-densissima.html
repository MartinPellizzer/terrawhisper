Persian Gum (Tragacantha Densissima)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Tragacantha densissima.

Persian gum, scientifically known as Tragacantha densissima, is a valuable medicinal herb native to the Middle East and Central Asia, traditionally used for its therapeutic properties. This plant is valued for its high mucilage content, which provides soothing and anti-inflammatory benefits, making it an effective remedy for digestive and respiratory ailments. Historically, Persian gum has been used in traditional medicine to treat ulcers, coughs, and skin conditions, and it holds cultural significance in Persian and Arab herbal traditions. In modern wellness practices, it is used as a natural thickener in food and as a supplement to support gut health and immune function. Its unique feature is the presence of tragacanthin, a rare polysaccharide with potent antioxidant properties, and its historical use dates back over 2,000 years in ancient healing practices.
