What to know about Torilis japonica (hedge parsley) before using it medicinally

Hedge parsley, scientifically known as Torilis japonica, is a versatile and highly beneficial herb that has been touted for its numerous health properties.

Rich in antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds, it has been shown to improve overall well-being by reducing the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, cancer, and diabetes. In culinary applications, hedge parsley adds a delicate anise flavor and aroma to soups, salads, and sauces, making it a popular ingredient among chefs and home cooks alike. From a horticultural perspective, hedge parsley is a relatively low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it an attractive addition to any garden or herb patch.

With its ancient origins dating back to the Middle Ages, hedge parsley has been referenced in historical texts such as the 13th-century herbal "Liber de Simplicibus", solidifying its position as a timeless and revered herb in many cultures around the world.
