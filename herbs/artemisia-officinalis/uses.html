Wormwood (Artemisia officinalis) Uses

Wormwood is used to treat respiratory infections, including cough, bronchitis, and asthma. It supports the immune system and may help with influenza. It also addresses skin infections, eczema, and other inflammatory skin conditions due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Wormwood (Artemisia officinalis) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Wormwood is used to support respiratory health by helping to clear mucus and reduce inflammation in the airways. It may assist in relieving symptoms of coughs, colds, and bronchitis. Its antimicrobial properties can help combat respiratory infections, though it should be used under guidance to ensure safety and effectiveness.

2. Cough

Wormwood is used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. It helps reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways. Often prepared as a tea, it is valued for its expectorant properties. Regular use may ease persistent coughing and promote clearer breathing.

3. Bronchitis

Wormwood is used to support respiratory health in bronchitis by acting as an expectorant and anti-inflammatory. It helps loosen mucus and reduce bronchial irritation, easing coughing and breathing difficulties. Its bitter compounds stimulate digestion and may enhance immune response, aiding recovery from respiratory infections.

4. Allergic rhinitis

Wormwood (Artemisia officinalis) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis. It contains compounds that may reduce inflammation and histamine release. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture. Its antihistaminic properties support its role in managing seasonal allergies.

5. Tuberculosis

Wormwood has been historically used in traditional medicine for its potential respiratory benefits. It contains compounds that may help alleviate symptoms of tuberculosis by supporting lung function and reducing inflammation. However, it is not a primary treatment and should be used under professional guidance alongside conventional therapies.

6. Asthma

Wormwood (Artemisia officinalis) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. It may help alleviate asthma symptoms by acting as an expectorant and bronchodilator. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance due to its potent nature.

7. Influenza

Wormwood (Artemisia officinalis) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health and may help alleviate symptoms of influenza due to its antiviral and immune-stimulating properties. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to help reduce fever, soothe coughs, and support the body's recovery process during flu outbreaks.

8. Skin infections

Wormwood is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin. Often applied as a poultice or infused oil, it is effective for conditions like eczema and fungal infections. Its active compounds support natural healing and prevent further infection.

9. Eczema

Wormwood is used externally for eczema due to its antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties. It can be made into a poultice or infused oil to soothe irritated skin. Regular application may help reduce redness and itching associated with eczema. Its astringent qualities also aid in drying excess moisture on the skin.

10. Inflammatory skin conditions

Wormwood contains compounds like thujone and cineole that have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is traditionally used to treat inflammatory skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis. Topical applications may help reduce redness, itching, and infection risk. However, it should be used with caution due to potential toxicity.
