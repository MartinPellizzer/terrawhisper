Desmodium lasiocarpum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium lasiocarpum has trifoliate leaves with elliptical to oblong leaflets, hairy stems, and distinctive fuzzy, hairy seed pods. The leaflets are typically covered in fine, spreading hairs that give the foliage a soft texture. Its stems are erect and can reach significant heights, often exhibiting a dense covering of pubescence. The most identifying feature is the legume pod, which is densely covered in long, soft hairs that facilitate dispersal by clinging to animal fur. These pods eventually turn dark and contain small, hard seeds.
