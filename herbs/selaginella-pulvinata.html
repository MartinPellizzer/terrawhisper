Selaginella pulvinata

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella pulvinata?

Selaginella pulvinata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. The plant is further organized into the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Selaginella, with its specific designation being the species pulvinaata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selagineella pulvinaata has a creeping rhizome that supports small, densely arranged, scale-like leaves arranged in four distinct rows along each stem. These leaves are triangular in shape and feature a characteristic cushion-like appearance due to their compact growth habit. The plant displays a highly specialized ability to undergo desiccation, where the leaves curl inward tightly around the stem to protect the delicate tissues during dry periods. When moisture returns, the leaves unfurl and expand to resume photosynthesis. This morphological adaptation allows the species to survive in fluctuating environments where water availability is inconsistent.

What are the pharmacological activities of Selaginella pulvinata?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including potent α-glucosidase inhibitory activity, antioxidant properties, and anti-inflammatory effects. The inhibition of the α-glucosidase enzyme suggests that extracts from Selaginella pulvinata may play a role in managing postprandial blood glucose levels in diabetic models. Beyond glucose regulation, the plant contains bioactive compounds like flavonoids and terpenoids that combat oxidative stress within cellular environments. These secondary metabolites contribute to its ability to reduce systemic inflammation and protect tissues from damage. Research into these diverse biological functions highlights the medicinal potential of this species in modern drug development.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", three new selaginelliin derivatives named selaginpulvilins V-X (1-3) were isolated from whole plants of Selaginella pulvinata. These three new derivatives, along with seven known analogs (4-10), were identified through spectroscopic methods. Biological evaluation of these compounds revealed specific pharmacological activities against α-glucosidase. Specifically, compounds 2, 6 and 8 displayed moderate inhibition with IC50 values of 3.71, 2.04 and 4.00 μM, respectively.
