Selaginella pulvinata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selagineella pulvinaata has a creeping rhizome that supports small, densely arranged, scale-like leaves arranged in four distinct rows along each stem. These leaves are triangular in shape and feature a characteristic cushion-like appearance due to their compact growth habit. The plant displays a highly specialized ability to undergo desiccation, where the leaves curl inward tightly around the stem to protect the delicate tissues during dry periods. When moisture returns, the leaves unfurl and expand to resume photosynthesis. This morphological adaptation allows the species to survive in fluctuating environments where water availability is inconsistent.

      What are the herbal preparations of Selaginella pulvinata?

      Selaginella pulvinaata is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste for various medicinal applications.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Selaginella pulvinaata can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or respiratory distress if its spores are inhaled in large quantities. While it is primarily grown as an ornamental houseplant, some individuals may experience itching or redness upon touching the foliage. The fine spores produced by the plant can act as airborne irritants for people with sensitive lungs or asthma. There is limited clinical research regarding the systemic toxicity of this species, so caution is advised during handling. Maintaining proper ventilation and wearing gloves can help mitigate these potential reactions.
