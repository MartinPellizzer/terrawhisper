Selaginella pulvinata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selagineella pulvinaata has a creeping rhizome that supports small, densely arranged, scale-like leaves arranged in four distinct rows along each stem. These leaves are triangular in shape and feature a characteristic cushion-like appearance due to their compact growth habit. The plant displays a highly specialized ability to undergo desiccation, where the leaves curl inward tightly around the stem to protect the delicate tissues during dry periods. When moisture returns, the leaves unfurl and expand to resume photosynthesis. This morphological adaptation allows the species to survive in fluctuating environments where water availability is inconsistent.
