Psychotria vogeliana

What's the taxonomical classification of Psychotria vogeliana?

Psychotria vogeliana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. This species is a representative of the family Rubiaceae, situated within the genus Psychotria, and is specifically identified by the species name vogeliana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Psychotria vogeliana contains various indole alkaloids and secondary metabolites that exhibit significant pharmacological potential. These bioactive compounds are primarily derived from the plant's complex chemical structure within the Rubiaceae family. Researchers often investigate these substances for their potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The specific profile of alkaloids can vary depending on the plant's geographic origin and environmental conditions. Understanding these chemical constituents is essential for developing future therapeutic applications in traditional and modern medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Psychotria vogeliana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and inflammatory conditions. The leaves and roots are often prepared as decoctions or poultices to soothe localized swelling and irritation. Some traditional practices also utilize its extracts to address digestive disturbances and gastrointestinal discomfort. Local healers rely on its medicinal properties to manage minor external injuries and promote healing of the skin. Its application serves as a vital component in regional herbal medicine for managing these specific health concerns.

What are the herbal preparations of Psychotria vogeliana?

Psychotria vogeliana is prepared primarily as a decoction or infusion of its leaves and roots to extract bioactive alkaloids for medicinal use. These liquid preparations are often consumed orally to address various traditional ailments. Some practitioners may also utilize the plant material in the form of topical poultices applied directly to the skin. In certain regional traditions, the crushed plant matter is incorporated into oils for external applications. The specific method of preparation typically depends on which part of the plant is being harvested and the intended therapeutic effect.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Psychotria vogeliana can cause significant neurological impairment, including intense hallucinations, disorientation, and severe cardiovascular distress. These adverse reactions are primarily due to the presence of potent psychoactive alkaloids found within its tissues. Users may experience profound changes in perception that lead to a loss of physical coordination and cognitive function. Prolonged exposure or high doses can potentially trigger acute psychological crises or long-term mental disturbances. Because the chemical composition is highly unpredictable, the physiological impact varies greatly between individuals.
