Turnera ulmifolia

Turnera Ulmifolia (Turnera ulmifolia) is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating skin conditions and inflammatory disorders.

Morphology

Turnera ulmifolia is a low-growing shrub characterized by its branching stems, ovate to elliptic leaves with a rough texture, and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically blooming in the spring and summer months.

Distribution

Turnera ulmifolia is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America, including countries such as Mexico, Guatemala, Belize, Honduras, and parts of the Amazon Basin.

Traditional Uses

Turnera ulmifolia has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial properties, often applied to treat respiratory infections, skin conditions, and digestive disorders.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Chinese medicine, and indigenous South American practices, Turnera ulmifolia has been utilized for its purported healing properties, including treating respiratory ailments, skin conditions, and digestive issues.

Phytochemistry

Turnera ulmifolia contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

Turnera ulmifolia exhibits pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic effects, which support its traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions.

Botanical Identification

Turnera ulmifolia, also known as turnera or turner's leaf, is a member of the Rutaceae family. It is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves and yellow flowers, and is sometimes referred to by regional names like "yerba de san juan" in some areas.

Safety Profile

Turnera ulmifolia is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult a healthcare provider before use.
