What is Turnera Ulmifolia (Turnera Ulmifolia)?

Turnera ulmifolia, also known as turnera or Mexican turnera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Turnera ulmifolia is Turnera ulmifolia. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Turnera, and species Turnera ulmifolia. It is commonly known as the "Mexican turnera" in Mexico and "wild cherry tree" in parts of Central America. The plant is also referred to as "Turnera ulmifolia" in botanical contexts and has regional synonyms such as "cherry tree" in some South American regions.

Plant Description and Morphology

Turnera ulmifolia is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters, with a spreading or upright growth habit depending on the environment.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a slightly pointed tip. The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in terminal cymes, emitting a subtle, sweet scent that attracts pollinators. The root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing the plant to thrive in well-drained soils.

The leaves are glossy green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, while the stems are woody and slightly hairy.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Turnera ulmifolia is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America, including countries such as Mexico, Guatemala, Belize, Honduras, and parts of the Amazon Basin.

It is commonly found in lowland areas, particularly in regions with moist, well-drained soils. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with abundant sunlight, typically in areas with distinct wet and dry seasons. It prefers shaded to partially shaded environments within forest understories or near water sources.

Ecological conditions favoring its growth include moderate to high rainfall and a lack of extreme temperature fluctuations.
