Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Common Hawthorn (Crataegus Oxyacantha)?

Common Hawthorn, or Crataegus oxyacantha, is a thorny shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, known for its small white flowers, red berries, and traditional use in herbal medicine for cardiovascular and digestive support.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) is a thorny shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its dark green oval leaves, showy white or pink flowers, and red to orange berries, and is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia.

Common Names and Synonyms

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) is also known by various common names such as Maythorn, Hawthorn, Thorned Hawthorn, and White Hawthorn, with regional variations including English Hawthorn and Common Thorn.

Plant Description and Morphology

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2-6 meters, featuring thorny branches, oval to lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that develop into red, orange, or yellow berries in the fall.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) is native to Europe, western Asia, and parts of North Africa, growing in a variety of habitats including woodlands, hedgerows, and rocky slopes.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The dried flowers, berries, and leaves of Common Hawthorn are primarily used medicinally in herbal medicine.

Phytochemical Composition

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) contains a variety of bioactive compounds including flavonoids, oligomeric proanthocyanidins, triterpenes, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacological Properties

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) exhibits pharmacological properties primarily attributed to its flavonoids, proanthocyanidins, and cardiac glycosides, which contribute to its effects on cardiovascular health, antioxidant activity, and potential anti-inflammatory and sedative actions.

Mechanisms of Action

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) exerts its therapeutic effects primarily through its influence on the cardiovascular system, including vasodilation, improvement of cardiac output, and modulation of neurotransmitters such as serotonin and acetylcholine, which contribute to its anxiolytic and sedative properties.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Common Hawthorn is used to treat cardiovascular conditions, including hypertension, angina, and arrhythmias, as well as digestive issues like indigestion and bloating, and to support mental health by alleviating symptoms of anxiety and insomnia.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) is typically prepared as a tincture, infusion, or dried herb for use in herbal remedies, with the most common forms being standardized extracts, dried berries, and decoctions.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Common Hawthorn for herbal use ranges from 60 to 240 mg of standardized extract containing 0.3% to 0.5% flavonoids, taken two to three times daily, though specific formulations and dosages may vary depending on the product and intended therapeutic use.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) may cause side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, dizziness, and fatigue, particularly when taken in high doses or combined with certain medications.

Drug Interactions

Common Hawthorn may interact with medications such as antidepressants, blood thinners, and heart medications due to its effects on the cardiovascular system and potential to enhance or reduce their effects.

Toxicity and Precautions

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, but it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, dizziness, or allergic reactions in some individuals, and its safety during pregnancy, breastfeeding, and in children has not been fully established.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) is typically cultivated in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, requiring minimal care once established.

Processing and Extraction

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) is typically processed by drying the flowers, berries, and leaves, often used in tinctures, teas, and decoctions for its medicinal properties.

Storage and Shelf Life

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture, and when properly stored, dried fruits and herbal preparations can retain their potency for up to 2 to 3 years.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) has primarily focused on its potential cardiovascular benefits, with studies indicating its ability to support heart health by improving blood pressure, reducing arrhythmias, and enhancing coronary circulation.

Traditional and Historical Uses

Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for centuries to support cardiovascular health, alleviate anxiety, and promote digestive function through its astringent, antispasmodic, and mild sedative properties.

Regulatory Status

The Common Hawthorn (Crataegus oxyacantha) is not approved as a drug by the FDA, but its use in herbal medicine is regulated under the Dietary Supplement and Herbal Act, requiring manufacturers to comply with good manufacturing practices and label requirements.
