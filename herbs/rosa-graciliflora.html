Rosa graciliflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa graciliflora has slender, arching stems that are often armed with small, sharp prickles and bear pinnately compound leaves typically consisting of five to seven small, ovate leaflets. The foliage is characterized by finely serrated margins and a soft, somewhat pubescent texture on the undersides. Its most distinguishing feature is the solitary, pale pink to white flowers that possess five delicate petals and numerous long stamens. These blooms are often held on long, graceful pedicels that give the plant an airy appearance. The resulting fruit is a small, fleshy red hip that develops after successful pollination.
