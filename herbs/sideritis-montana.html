What is Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis Montana)?

Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis montana) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean, primarily used in traditional herbal medicine for its adaptogenic and calming properties.

Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis montana) is a flowering plant species belonging to the Lamiaceae family, commonly known as the mint family, and is native to the mountainous regions of Southern Europe.

Common Names and Synonyms

Common names for Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis montana) include Alpine Sideritis, Mountain Sideris, and Rock Sideritis, reflecting its geographic distribution and habitat preferences across mountainous regions in Europe and parts of the Middle East.

The Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis montana) is a perennial herb with upright, branched stems that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Native Habitat and Distribution: Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis montana) is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in countries such as Greece, Albania, and parts of the Balkans, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 500 to 2000 meters.

Pharmacological Properties
