Mountain Sideritis

Sideritis montana

Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis montana) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, known for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

Botanical Identification

Sideritis montana, commonly known as Mountain Sideritis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean. Belonging to the Lamiaceae family, it is also known as Greek Sideritis in some regions. The plant has square stems, hairy leaves, and pinkish-purple flowers, and has been referenced in herbal texts under various synonyms.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Sideritis
  • Species: Sideritis montana

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of Mountain Sideritis include flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments due to its mild antispasmodic and expectorant properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic due to its warming and digestive properties.

Tincture: Used for respiratory conditions and as a stimulant due to its concentrated active compounds.

Safety Profile

Sideritis montana is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Individuals with allergies to plants in the Lamiaceae family should exercise caution.
