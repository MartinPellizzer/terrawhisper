Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis montana)

Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis montana) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Greece, Balkans, and Eastern Europe. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, bitter, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and tibetan medicine.
