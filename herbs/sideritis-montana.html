Mountain Sideritis

Sideritis montana

Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis montana) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, known for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

Morphology

The Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis montana) is a perennial herb with upright, branched stems that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Distribution

Native to the mountainous regions of the Balkan Peninsula, including countries such as Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Greece, Kosovo, Montenegro, North Macedonia, and Serbia, Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis montana) thrives in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats.

Traditional Uses

In traditional herbal medicine, Mountain Sideritis (Sideritis montana) has been used for its calming and antispasmodic properties, particularly to alleviate symptoms of anxiety, nervous tension, and digestive disorders.

Medicinal Systems
