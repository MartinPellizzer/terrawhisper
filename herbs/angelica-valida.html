What's the taxonomical classification of Angelica valida?

Angelica valida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Apiales and is a member of the family Apiaceae. Specifically, it is situated within the genus Angelica, and its unique biological identity is defined by its species name, valida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angelica valida has a robust, perennial herbaceous structure characterized by thick, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to two meters and large, compound leaves with serrated leaflets. The foliage is deeply divided into multiple pinnae, often displaying a bright green color and a somewhat coarse texture. Its inflorescence consists of large, terminal umbels composed of numerous small, white, five-petaled flowers arranged in a dense cluster. The root system is typically stout and fleshy, providing the plant with significant stability and nutrient storage.
