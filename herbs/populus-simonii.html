Populus simonii

What's the taxonomical classification of Populus simonii?

Populus simonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malpighiales. Its taxonomic progression continues through the family Salicaceae and the genus Populus, ultimately identifying it as the specific species simonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Populus simonii has woody stems with grayish bark, ovate leaves with serrated margins, and small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers. The leaf structure features prominent venation and a slightly asymmetrical base that distinguishes it from related species. Its branching pattern is typically irregular, allowing the plant to form a dense, shrub-like canopy. The root system is extensive and fibrous, providing stability in various soil types. These morphological characteristics collectively define its growth habit and ecological niche.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is found exclusively in the high-altitude regions of the Ethiopian Highlands. It thrives within specific montane ecosystems that provide the necessary cool and moist conditions for its survival. Its distribution is highly fragmented due to the rugged terrain and isolated mountain peaks of East Africa.
