Populus simonii

What's the taxonomical classification of Populus simonii?

Populus simonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malpighiales. Its taxonomic progression continues through the family Salicaceae and the genus Populus, ultimately identifying it as the specific species simonii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Populus simonii contains salicylic acid, flavonoids, and various phenolic compounds that serve as its primary medicinal constituents. These specific chemical agents are recognized for their potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The presence of tannins in the bark also contributes to its ability to act as an astringent for treating skin irritations. Researchers have noted that the antioxidant capacity of these compounds helps mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Through these diverse phytochemical profiles, the plant offers significant therapeutic potential for managing pain and swelling.

What are the therapeutic actions of Populus simonii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and certain types of digestive disorders. The medicinal properties of its components are often utilized to soothe irritated tissues and reduce swelling. Local traditional practices frequently involve applying extracts to wounds to prevent bacterial growth. Furthermore, some preparations are ingested in controlled doses to alleviate stomach discomfort. These applications highlight the plant's role in managing both external and internal health concerns.
