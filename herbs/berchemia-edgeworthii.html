Berchemia edgeworthii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berchemia edgeworthii has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically 3 to 7 centimeters long with entire or slightly serrated margins and a coriaceous texture. The plant grows as a woody shrub or small tree characterized by slender, somewhat spreading branches. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in axillary clusters and possess a yellowish-green hue. Following pollination, the species produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a bright red or orange color when mature. These morphological traits distinguish it from other members of the Rhamnaceae family within its native habitat.

Its range is largely restricted to these specific Neotropical ecosystems where moisture levels remain consistently high.
