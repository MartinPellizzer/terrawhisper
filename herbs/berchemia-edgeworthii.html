What's the taxonomical classification of Berchemia edgeworthii?

Berchemia edgeworthii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's taxonomic progression continues through the order Rosales and the family Rhamnaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Berchemia, with its specific identification being the species edgeworthii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berchemia edgeworthii has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically 3 to 7 centimeters long with entire or slightly serrated margins and a coriaceous texture.

The plant grows as a woody shrub or small tree characterized by slender, somewhat spreading branches. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in axillary clusters and possess a yellowish-green hue. Following pollination, the species produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a bright red or orange color when mature.

These morphological traits distinguish it from other members of the Rhamnaceae family within its native habitat.
