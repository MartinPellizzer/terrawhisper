Ehretia cymosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Ehretia cymosa?

Ehretia cymosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Boraginales and the family Boraginaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Ehretia, and its unique species name, cymosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ehretia cymosa contains various bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, saponins, tannins, and flavonoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily utilized in traditional medicine to address inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. The presence of specific glycosides within the plant leaves suggests potential antimicrobial and antioxidant activities. Researchers often study these compounds to understand how they might assist in treating gastrointestinal disturbances. Through the extraction of these secondary metabolites, the plant serves as a significant source of natural healing agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ehretia cymosa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, intestinal worms, digestive disorders, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed by applying leaf extracts to wounds or sores to prevent infection. In some traditional practices, decoctions made from the plant are ingested to expel parasites from the digestive tract. It is also frequently utilized to alleviate stomach aches and general abdominal discomfort. These therapeutic applications rely on the specific phytochemical compounds found within its leaves and bark.
