Blumea clarkei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea clarkei has perennial herbaceous plants characterized by erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters and are densely covered in white tomentose hairs. The leaves are large, broadly ovate to lanceolate, and possess serrated margins with a distinctively rugose surface texture. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and often exhibit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed. The inflorescence consists of numerous small, yellowish heads arranged in dense, corymbose panicles. Each flower head is surrounded by several layers of overlapping, hairy phyllaries that protect the developing florets.
