Asteropyrum cavaleriei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asteropyrum cavaleriei has a woody, perennial shrub habit characterized by lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and somewhat leathery in texture. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, terminal capitula that often exhibit a yellowish or pale hue. Its stems are relatively slender but sturdy, providing a structural framework for the spreading foliage. The leaf margins are generally entire or slightly serrated, contributing to the plant's distinct silhouette. This species is morphologically adapted to its specific ecological niche through its compact growth form and durable leaf surface.
