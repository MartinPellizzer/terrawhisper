Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Kola Nut is Cola nitida, a classification established by the botanist A. DC. in 1832. This name is widely recognized in botanical literature and is used to distinguish it from other cola species. It is also known by various common names, including kola nut, cola acuminata, and cola africana, reflecting its regional and cultural significance.

Morphological Characteristics

Kola Nut (Cola nitida) is a small evergreen tree with a straight, unbranched stem. It has a fibrous root system and simple, alternate, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, white, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to reveal two kidney-shaped seeds, which are the characteristic kola nuts.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Kola nut (Cola nitida) can be distinguished by its elliptical leaves with slightly serrated edges and a smooth, dark green upper surface. The fruit is a capsule with a distinctive white aril covering the seed. It differs from similar species like Cola acuminata by its smaller size and the presence of a more prominent calyx.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Kola Nut (Cola nitida) is native to western and central Africa. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and rainfall. It prefers well-drained, sandy loam soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 200 and 800 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Kola Nut (Cola nitida) blooms during the dry season, typically between January and April. Seed formation occurs from May to September. Harvesting is done when the fruit turns from green to yellowish-brown, usually around September to November.
