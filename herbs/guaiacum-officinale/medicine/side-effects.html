10 Possible Side Effects Of Guaiacum Officinale (Lignum Vitae)

Guaiacum officinale has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.

These side effects are caused by the herb's high saponin and tannin content, which can irritate the digestive system.

In severe cases, these side effects can worsen people's lives by leading to dehydration and malnutrition, particularly in vulnerable populations such as children and the elderly.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Guaiacum officinale if used imporperly.

Guaiacum officinale causes allergic reactions due to its high concentration of terpenoids and flavonoids.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the essential oils present in the plant may cause skin irritation or gastrointestinal upset in those who are sensitive to them.

Furthermore, the oil's aromatic compounds can stimulate bronchial muscles, worsening breathing difficulties and triggering severe asthmatic episodes.
