Botanical Identification of Guaiacum (Guaiacum officinale)

To identify Guaiacum (Guaiacum officinale) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Guaiacum officinale belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Zygophyllaceae, genus Guaiacum, and species Guaiacum officinale. It is a member of the legume order and is known for its medicinal properties in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Guaiacum officinale, a species within the Burseraceae family. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1742. Common synonyms include Guaiacum sanctum and Guaiacum buxifolium. It is also known by regional names such as "gumbo" in the Caribbean and "sacred wood" in traditional contexts.

Morphological Characteristics

Guaiacum officinale is a small, spiny shrub with a dense, branching root system. Its stems are woody and often thorny, while its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic with a pointed tip. The plant produces yellowish-green inflorescences with small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody shell.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Guaiacum officinale can be distinguished by its smooth, dark green leaves with a slightly rough texture, and its yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The bark is thick and deeply fissured, and the wood has a distinct aromatic scent. These features help differentiate it from other Guaiacum species and similar plants.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Guaiacum officinale is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It is typically found at low to mid-altitudes, often near coastal areas.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Guaiacum officinale blooms from April to June, producing yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the late summer to early autumn when the plant is most mature and the characteristic features are most apparent.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Guaiacum officinale belongs to the family Zingiberaceae, closely related to other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with its family members, such as aromatic oils and rhizomatous growth. Its closest relatives include species like Curcuma and Kaempferia, which are also valued for their medicinal properties.
