Eurya ciliata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eurya ciliata has small, elliptical leaves that are typically 1 to 3 centimeters long, featuring finely serrated margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The leaf arrangement is opposite along the woody stems, which are characterized by a dense, shrubby growth habit. Small, white, or cream-colored flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. These flowers possess tiny petals and are followed by small, fleshy berries that change color as they mature. The overall structure of the plant is compact, making it a common feature in shaded understory environments.
