What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhynchospora rubra has upright, woody stems that typically reach heights of 15 to 50 centimeters and feature small, opposite, ovate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a dark green color and a smooth texture, often appearing somewhat leathery to the touch. During the flowering period, the plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge in clusters from the leaf axils. These blooms eventually transition into small, fleshy, reddish berries that serve as the primary fruit of the species.
