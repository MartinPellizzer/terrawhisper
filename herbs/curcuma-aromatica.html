Turmeric (Curcuma aromatica)

Turmeric (Curcuma aromatica) is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, Indonesia, and India. Traditionally, its rhizomes, leaves, and flowers have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, carminative, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.
