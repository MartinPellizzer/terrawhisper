Gentiana flavomaculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana flavomaculata?

Gentiana flavomaculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and placed in the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Gentianaceae, falling under the genus Gentiana, and is specifically identified by its species name, flavomaculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana flavomaculata has upright, unbranched stems that reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and are characterized by opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically bright green and possesses a smooth texture along the leaf blades. Its flowers are funnel-shaped, featuring a deep blue or violet corolla that is distinguished by prominent yellow spots located within the throat. These blossoms are usually solitary and emerge from the leaf axils during the summer months. The plant often develops a branched base as it matures, contributing to its overall structural density.
