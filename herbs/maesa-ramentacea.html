Maesa ramentacea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Maesa ramentacea has lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, a woody stem approximately two centimeters in diameter, and small pentamerous white flowers. The foliage is arranged in an opposite pattern along the primary branches to maximize sunlight absorption. Its root system consists of a deep taproot that provides stability in rocky soil. The bark exhibits a rough, grayish texture that peels slightly in thin strips during the growing season. Tiny, globular fruit capsules emerge from the stem nodes following successful pollination.
