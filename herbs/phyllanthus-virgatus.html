Phyllanthus virgatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllanthus virgatus?

Phyllanthus virgatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Malpighiales. Specifically, this plant is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae, falling under the genus Phyllanthus, and is identified at the species level as Phyllanthus virgatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllanthus virgatus has an erect, branched herbaceous structure characterized by slender stems and small, elliptical leaves arranged in a distichous fashion along the branches. The leaves are typically opposite or sub-opposite, displaying a bright green color and a smooth texture. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters or solitary along the stem. The plant produces small, rounded fruits that eventually split open to reveal seeds. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in various tropical and subtropical environments.
