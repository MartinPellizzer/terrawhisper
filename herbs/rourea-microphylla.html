Rourea microphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Rourea microphylla?

Rourea microphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Oxalidales. The plant is a member of the family Connaraceae, falling within the genus Rourea, and is specifically identified by its species name, microphylla.

    Rourea microphylla has medicinal properties primarily used to treat gastrointestinal ailments, skin inflammations, and various febrile conditions.     What are the herbal preparations of Rourea microphylla?

    Rourea microphylla is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the leaves and stems in water to extract its bioactive compounds. This liquid infusion is the most common method used in traditional medicinal practices to address various ailments. Some practitioners also utilize a powder form derived from dried plant material for topical applications or specific oral dosages. In certain regional traditions, the plant parts are macerated in alcohol to create a concentrated tincture. These different preparation styles allow for varied absorption rates and therapeutic uses depending on the intended treatment.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Rourea microphylla can cause localized skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, or allergic reactions if ingested or applied improperly. While the plant is used in traditional medicine, the specific toxicity levels for humans remain insufficiently documented in modern clinical studies. Potential users may experience nausea or stomach cramping if the plant parts are consumed without proper preparation. Contact with the leaves or stems might trigger redness or itching in individuals with sensitive skin. Due to this lack of comprehensive safety data, caution is necessary when exploring its medicinal properties.
