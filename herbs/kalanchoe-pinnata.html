Kalanchoe (Kalanchoe pinnata)

Kalanchoe (Kalanchoe pinnata) is a member of the Crassulaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, Indian Subcontinent, and Tropical Africa. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for decoctions, poultices, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.
