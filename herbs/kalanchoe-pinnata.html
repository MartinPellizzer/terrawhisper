Kalanchoe pinnata

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Kalanchoe pinnata, also known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant belonging to the Crassulaceae family. Native to Madagascar, it is a perennial shrub with fleshy leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. Common names include Kalanchoe, liveforever, and air plant, and it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its healing properties.

Active Compounds

Kalanchoe pinnata contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal properties. They are believed to exert anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and wound-healing effects through various biochemical mechanisms.

  • Flavonoid
  • Terpenoid
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, skin ulcers, itching Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antihistaminic, antioxidant
Musculoskeletal Inflammation, osteoarthritis Anti-inflammatory
Respiratory Respiratory infections, bronchitis, asthma Antimicrobial, expectorant, bronchodilator

Preparation Methods

Poultice: Used for treating wounds, skin infections, and inflammation due to its antimicrobial properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.

Infusion: Used for treating colds, coughs, and skin conditions due to its mild antiseptic properties.

Safety Profile

Kalanchoe pinnata is generally considered safe when used appropriately. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for prolonged periods or in high doses.
