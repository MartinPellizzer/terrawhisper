What is Kalanchoe (Kalanchoe Pinnata)?

Kalanchoe pinnata, also known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar that is widely used in traditional herbal medicine for its healing properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Kalanchoe is Kalanchoe pinnata. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Crassulaceae, genus Kalanchoe, and species Kalanchoe pinnata. It is commonly known as the "Mother of Millions" in South America and "Air Plant" in parts of Africa. The plant is also referred to as "Kalanchoe" in its native regions of Madagascar. Synonyms include Kalanchoe pinnata var. pinnata, Kalanchoe pinnata var. crenata, and Kalanchoe pinnata var. bispinosa.

Plant Description and Morphology

Kalanchoe is a succulent plant native to Madagascar, commonly reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters with a compact, branching structure.

Its leaves are fleshy, oval-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, arranged in a rosette pattern along the stems. The flowers are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences, featuring showy, brightly colored petals in shades of red, orange, yellow, or pink. The root system is shallow and fibrous, adapted to absorb moisture efficiently from the environment.

The plant has no distinct fragrance, with leaves and flowers that are smooth to the touch and retain a crisp, succulent texture.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Kalanchoe is native to Madagascar and parts of southern Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is now widely distributed across tropical and subtropical regions, including Southeast Asia, the Caribbean, and parts of the Americas. The plant prefers warm climates with low humidity and well-draining soil, making it adaptable to dry and rocky landscapes. It grows best in full sun and can tolerate high temperatures, though it requires minimal rainfall.

Ecologically, it often colonizes disturbed soils and rocky areas, contributing to its widespread presence in diverse habitats.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The leaves, stems, and roots of Kalanchoe pinnata are used medicinally.

The leaves are most commonly harvested for their medicinal properties. They are typically dried and ground into a powder or used fresh. The stems are also utilized, often in similar forms as the leaves.

The roots are less frequently used but are occasionally prepared as a decoction.
