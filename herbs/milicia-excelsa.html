Milicia excelsa

What's the taxonomical classification of Milicia excelsa?

Milicia excelsa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and falls under the family Moraeaecae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Milicia, with the specific epithet excelsa denoting its unique species status.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Milicia excelsa contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, tannins, saponins, and flavonoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as skin infections and inflammation. The presence of tannins specifically provides astringent qualities that aid in wound healing. Furthermore, the alkaloids found within the plant bark are studied for their potential antimicrobial and analgesic effects. Researchers continue to investigate these specific compounds to understand their precise biochemical mechanisms in human health.

What are the therapeutic actions of Milicia excelsa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat malaria, skin infections, diarrhea, stomach aches, and various types of inflammation. The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that serve as traditional medicinal remedies in several African regions. Practitioners often prepare decoctions or poultices from these parts to manage febrile illnesses and digestive distress. Additionally, its antimicrobial properties make it a common choice for healing wounds and skin irritations. These applications demonstrate the plant's significant role in local ethnobotanical practices.
