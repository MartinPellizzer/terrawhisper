Fleeceflower

Tripterygium wilfordii

Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) is a flowering plant native to China, known for its use in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as rheumatoid arthritis and inflammatory disorders.

Taxonomy

Fleeceflower, or Tripterygium wilfordii, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Celastraceae, order Celastrales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) is a perennial herb characterized by its branched, woody stems, opposite leaves with a serrated margin, and small yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes.

Distribution

Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) is native to China, particularly growing in the mountainous regions of Sichuan, Hubei, and Yunnan provinces, as well as parts of northern Vietnam and northern India.

Traditional Uses

Fleeceflower has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat inflammatory conditions such as rheumatoid arthritis, lupus, and other autoimmune disorders due to its potent anti-inflammatory and immunosuppressive properties.

Medicinal Systems

Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine, Ayurveda, and indigenous herbal practices across Asia for its purported anti-inflammatory, immunosuppressive, and antitumor properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) is characterized by the presence of a diverse array of bioactive compounds, including diterpenoids, alkaloids, triterpenoids, and flavonoids, with tripterygium glycosides being the most notable and extensively studied class of compounds.

Pharmacology

Fleeceflower exhibits anti-inflammatory, immunosuppressive, and antitumor activities, making it effective in treating autoimmune diseases, inflammatory conditions, and certain types of cancer.

Botanical Identification

Tripterygium wilfordii, commonly known as Fleeceflower, is a perennial shrub native to China. Belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae, it is also referred to as Leavlet or Thunder God's Bell in various regions. The plant features a woody root system, alternate leaves, and small yellow flowers. Safety Profile

Fleeceflower is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential teratogenic effects. It may also interact with immunosuppressants and anticoagulants, increasing the risk of bleeding. Patients should consult a healthcare provider before use.
