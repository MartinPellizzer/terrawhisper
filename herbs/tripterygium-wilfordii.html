What is Fleeceflower (Tripterygium Wilfordii)?

Fleeceflower, or Tripterygium wilfordii, is a flowering plant native to China, known for its medicinal properties and used in traditional Chinese medicine for its anti-inflammatory, immunosuppressive, and antitumor effects.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Fleeceflower is Tripterygium wilfordii. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Celastrales, family Celastraceae, genus Tripterygium, and species Tripterygium wilfordii. It is commonly known as Fleeceflower in China, and as Thunder of God in India. The synonyms for this plant include Tripterygium wilfordii Hook. f., Tripterygium nanum, and regional names such as Hua Tuo in China and Kali Muhur in India.

Plant Description and Morphology

Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its branched stems, alternate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green underside, and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, and Hubei, and is commonly found in moist, shaded environments like forests and rocky slopes.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The roots, leaves, and fruiting bodies of Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) are the primary plant parts used medicinally.

Phytochemical Composition

Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including diterpenoids, alkaloids, flavonoids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its pharmacological effects.

Pharmacological Properties

Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) exhibits potent anti-inflammatory, immunosuppressive, and antitumor activities, primarily attributed to its active compounds such as tripterygium glycosides, which modulate immune responses and inhibit the proliferation of inflammatory cells.

Mechanisms of Action

Fleeceflower exerts its pharmacological effects primarily through the inhibition of protein tyrosine kinases, modulation of immune responses, and suppression of inflammatory cytokines, with its active compounds such as triptolide and celastrol playing key roles in these mechanisms.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) is traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat autoimmune disorders, inflammation, and certain skin conditions due to its potent immunosuppressive and anti-inflammatory properties.
