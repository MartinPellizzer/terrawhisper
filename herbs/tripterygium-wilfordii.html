Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Fleeceflower (Tripterygium Wilfordii)?

Fleeceflower, or Tripterygium wilfordii, is a flowering plant native to China, known for its medicinal properties and used in traditional Chinese medicine for its anti-inflammatory, immunosuppressive, and antitumor effects.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Fleeceflower is Tripterygium wilfordii. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Celastrales, family Celastraceae, genus Tripterygium, and species Tripterygium wilfordii. It is commonly known as Fleeceflower in China, and as Thunder of God in India. The synonyms for this plant include Tripterygium wilfordii Hook. f., Tripterygium nanum, and regional names such as Hua Tuo in China and Kali Muhur in India.

Plant Description and Morphology

Fleeceflower is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with a branched, upright stem covered in fine, white hairs.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a hairy surface. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, branched inflorescences, giving the plant its common name. The root system consists of a thick, fleshy taproot with lateral branches, which is used in traditional medicine.

The plant has a mild, slightly bitter odor, with a rough, fibrous texture to its stems and leaves.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Fleeceflower is native to the mountainous regions of southern and eastern China, particularly in provinces such as Sichuan, Hubei, and Yunnan.

It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates, often growing at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters. The plant prefers well-drained soils and partial to full shade, commonly found in forested areas or rocky slopes. Its natural habitat includes moist, shaded environments with high humidity and moderate temperatures.

Fleeceflower is typically found in regions with a monsoonal climate, where seasonal rainfall supports its growth and survival.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The primary plant parts used medicinally in Fleeceflower are the roots and the leaves.

The roots are most commonly harvested and used due to their higher concentration of active compounds. Leaves are also utilized, particularly in traditional preparations. Both parts are typically dried and ground into powder or decocted in water.

The roots are often preferred for their stronger therapeutic effects.

Pharmacological Properties

Fleeceflower (Tripterygium wilfordii) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties that contribute to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Its therapeutic actions include anti-inflammatory, immunosuppressive, and antitumor effects, making it valuable for treating autoimmune diseases and inflammatory conditions. The biological activity of Fleeceflower is attributed to its bioactive compounds, such as tripterygium glycosides and tanshinone derivatives, which modulate cellular signaling pathways. Pharmacodynamically, it inhibits the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and suppresses T-cell activity, reducing immune system overactivity.

System-level effects include reductions in joint inflammation, improvements in skin conditions, and potential benefits in managing certain types of cancer.

Biochemically, it targets enzymes such as phosphodiesterase and protein tyrosine kinases, which are involved in inflammatory and immune signaling. Therapeutic Uses

Fleeceflower, or Tripterygium wilfordii, is a medicinal plant traditionally used in Chinese herbal medicine for its potent therapeutic properties.

It is primarily employed to treat inflammatory conditions and autoimmune disorders due to its strong anti-inflammatory and immunosuppressive effects.
