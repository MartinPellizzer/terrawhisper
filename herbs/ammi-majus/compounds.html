Ammi Majus (Ammi majus) Compounds

Ammi majus contains several primary active compounds, including coumarin, which has anticoagulant properties, and essential oils that contribute to its aromatic profile. Flavonoids and phenolic acids provide antioxidant benefits, while terpenoids and glycosides may support anti-inflammatory effects. Saponins, tannins, and alkaloids also contribute to its medicinal properties.

Ammi Majus (Ammi majus) Monograph

1. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Ammi majus, known for its antispasmodic and analgesic properties. It helps relieve muscle spasms and pain, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating conditions like menstrual cramps and gastrointestinal issues. Its presence in Ammi majus supports its traditional use in Ayurvedic and European herbal practices.

2. Essential oil

The essential oil of Ammi Majus contains compounds like anethole and apiol, which have antispasmodic and carminative properties. It is used in herbal medicine to relieve gastrointestinal discomfort and promote digestion. The oil's aromatic profile also supports its traditional use in respiratory and digestive remedies.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Ammi majus. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds are believed to support the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Flavonoids may enhance the therapeutic effects of Ammi majus in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Ammi majus. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Found in significant amounts in the seeds and roots, it supports the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Its biochemical activity enhances the therapeutic potential of Ammi majus in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Ammi majus contains the terpenoid compound apiol. Apiol is known for its antispasmodic and carminative properties, making it useful in treating digestive issues and muscle spasms. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its therapeutic effects.

6. Glycoside

Ammi majus contains the glycoside aristoflavan. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Glycosides like aristoflavan are known for their anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties, supporting the plant's role in herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Ammi majus. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Saponins have surfactant properties and may enhance the absorption of other compounds. They are also believed to have anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects.

8. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Ammi majus. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects. Tannins in Ammi majus may support digestive health and help in treating skin conditions.

9. Alkaloid

Ammi majus contains the alkaloid lappa alkaloid, which contributes to its medicinal properties. This compound is known for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. It plays a key role in the plant's traditional use for treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Lappa alkaloid is one of the primary active components responsible for the therapeutic actions of Ammi majus.

10. Anthocyanin

Ammi majus contains anthocyanins, which are flavonoid pigments responsible for the plant's vibrant colors. These compounds act as antioxidants, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. Anthocyanins may support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine.
