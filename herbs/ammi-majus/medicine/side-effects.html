10 Possible Side Effects Of Ammi Majus

Ammi majus has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can occur if the herb is consumed in large quantities, if individual is allergic, or if skin contact is prolonged.

For instance, excessive consumption can lead to diarrhea, vomiting, and stomach cramps, which can cause dehydration and disrupt daily activities, while skin irritation can result in itching, redness, and inflammation, potentially leading to infections or scarring.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Ammi majus causes allergic reactions.

This occurs due to the presence of furanocoumarins and other volatile compounds in its essential oils, which can trigger an immune response in susceptible individuals.

As a result, people may experience symptoms such as skin rash, itching, hives, or respiratory issues like congestion and shortness of breath when they come into contact with Ammi majus or its extracts.

2. Induces vomiting frequently

Ammi majus induces vomiting frequently due to its ability to stimulate the vagus nerve and increase salivation.

This property, combined with its bitter taste, can trigger a strong gag reflex in some individuals, leading to repeated episodes of vomiting.

Additionally, Ammi majus' expectorant properties may also contribute to this side effect by stimulating the digestive system and increasing mucus production in the stomach.

3. Increases blood pressure rapidly

Ammi majus increases blood pressure rapidly due to its high content of alkaloids, such as falcarinol and falcarindiol.

These compounds can cause the blood vessels to constrict, leading to a rapid increase in blood pressure.

Additionally, Ammi majus has been shown to stimulate the heart rate, further contributing to elevated blood pressure levels.

4. Stimulates rapid heartbeat often

Ammi majus stimulates rapid heartbeat often due to its ability to increase blood pressure and cardiac output.

The herb's stimulatory effects on the cardiovascular system can lead to an elevated heart rate, which may be uncomfortable for some individuals. This is because Ammi majus contains compounds that dilate blood vessels and increase sympathetic nervous system activity, leading to increased cardiac workload.

As a result, users of this herb may experience palpitations or tachycardia.

5. Activates uterine contractions strongly

Ammi majus activates uterine contractions strongly due to its high content of coumarins and furanocoumarins.

These compounds are capable of stimulating the uterus, which can lead to increased muscle tone and frequency of contractions. This property makes Ammi majus a commonly used herb in traditional medicine for inducing labor and promoting parturition.

As a result, people may experience uterine contractions when using Ammi majus, especially during pregnancy or childbirth.

6. Disrupts menstrual cycles severely

Ammi majus disrupts menstrual cycles severely due to its ability to stimulate uterine contractions and alter hormone levels in the body.

The plant's estrogenic properties can mimic the effects of estrogen, leading to an imbalance in hormonal regulation.

This imbalance can cause irregular or absent periods, making it challenging for women who use Ammi majus to maintain a regular menstrual cycle.

7. Triggers severe headaches commonly

Ammi majus triggers severe headaches commonly due to its high concentration of alkaloids such as apiol andasarone.

These compounds can cause a sudden surge in blood pressure, leading to pain and discomfort in the head and neck region. Additionally, Ammi majus may stimulate the nervous system, causing a feeling of anxiety or panic that can exacerbate headache symptoms.

This reaction is thought to be particularly common among individuals who are sensitive to the plant's active compounds.

8. Produces stomach cramps intensely

Ammi majus produces stomach cramps intensely when used due to its high concentration of furanocoumarins.

These bioactive compounds can irritate the digestive tract, leading to severe cramping and discomfort.

As Ammi majus is often used in traditional medicine for various ailments, including respiratory issues and skin conditions, it's essential to be aware of this potential adverse effect when using this herb.

9. Inhibits kidney function significantly

Ammi majus inhibits kidney function significantly.

This is due to its high concentration of furanocoumarins, which are toxic compounds that can damage the kidneys by reducing their ability to filter waste and excess fluids from the blood.

Additionally, Ammi majus may increase the risk of kidney damage by causing dehydration, which further compromises kidney function.

10. Affects vision negatively sharply

Ammi majus affects vision negatively sharply due to its ability to lower blood pressure and increase blood flow to the eyes.

The sudden drop in blood pressure can cause blurred vision, double vision, or even temporary loss of sight.

Additionally, the increased blood flow may lead to eye strain and discomfort, potentially causing headaches and eye fatigue.
