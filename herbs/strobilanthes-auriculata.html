Strobilanthes auriculata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strobilanthes auriculata has an erect, perennial shrubby habit characterized by opposite, elliptical leaves that feature distinct, ear-like lobes at the base of the leaf blade. The leaf margins are typically entire, and the foliage often displays a deep green color with prominent venation. Its stems are quadrangular in cross-section, which is a common trait among members of the Acanthaceae family. The inflorescence consists of dense, terminal racemes that bear tubular, bilabiate flowers. These flowers are typically blue or violet and emerge from specialized bracts that surround the floral structure.
