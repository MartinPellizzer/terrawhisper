Carpesium macrocephalum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carpesium macrocephalum has a robust, woody stem characterized by thick cortical layers and a highly branched architectural structure. The leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern and feature large, elliptical blades with prominent reticulate venation. Its most defining feature is the large, spherical inflorescence that emerges from the terminal nodes of the branches. These flower clusters are supported by sturdy peduncles capable of bearing significant weight. The overall morphology suggests an adaptation to dense forest environments where structural stability is essential.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Carpesium macrocephalum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense abdominal cramping, nausea, and projectile vomiting if ingested in significant quantities. Prolonged contact with the plant's sap has been known to induce localized inflammation and dermatitis on sensitive skin. Careful avoidance of all parts of this plant is necessary to prevent these potentially dangerous physiological responses.
