Carpesium macrocephalum

What's the taxonomical classification of Carpesium macrocephalum?

Carpesium macrocephalum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Asterales. It is a part of the diverse family Asteraceae, specifically falling under the genus Carpesium, with its unique biological identity defined by its specific designation as the species macrocephalum.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Carpesium macrocephalum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense abdominal cramping, nausea, and projectile vomiting if ingested in significant quantities. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may also trigger acute allergic reactions such as skin rashes or respiratory irritation. Some individuals might experience neurological symptoms like dizziness or lightheadedness following exposure to its concentrated extracts. Prolonged contact with the plant's sap has been known to induce localized inflammation and dermatitis on sensitive skin. Careful avoidance of all parts of this plant is necessary to prevent these potentially dangerous physiological responses.
