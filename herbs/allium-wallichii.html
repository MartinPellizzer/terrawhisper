Allium wallichii

What's the taxonomical classification of Allium wallichii?

Allium wallichii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the biological hierarchy further, it falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Allium and is designated by the species epithet wallichii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium wallichii has a bulbous base supporting narrow, linear leaves that emerge from the ground and rise toward a terminal, globe-shaped umbel of small, white or pale pink flowers. The individual florets are characterized by six tepals that create a delicate, airy appearance within the inflorescence. This plant typically features slender, hollow stems that provide structural support for the heavy flowering head. The foliage remains relatively thin and grass-like throughout its growing season. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in rocky or alpine environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Allium wallichii?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-cancer, anti-microbial, and anti-oxidant properties. Its anti-cancer potential involves inhibiting the growth and proliferation of various malignant cell lines. The anti-microbial effects allow it to combat diverse bacterial and fungal pathogens effectively. Furthermore, its high antioxidant capacity helps neutralize harmful free radicals within the body. These diverse therapeutic actions make Alliun wallichii a subject of interest for medicinal research.

As discussed in a study, Allium wallichii is a plant native to Nepal growing at elevations of 2300-4800 m that possesses moderate anti-microbial and anti-oxidant activity. Phytochemical screening of the aqueous ethanol extract revealed the presence of steroids, terpenoids, flavonoids, reducing sugars, and glycosides. The plant showed significant anti-cancer potential with IC50 values of 69.69 μg/ml for the Prostate cancer (PC3) cell line, 55.29 μg/ml for the Breast Cancer (MCF-7) cell line, and 46.51 μg/ml for the cervical cancer (HeLa) cell line. Additionally, the cell viability assay using FACS determined the IC50 value for the Burkitt's lymphoma (B-Lymphoma) cell line was 3.817 ± 1.99 mg/ml. These results suggest that the crude extract of Allium wallichii is a promising candidate for use as an anti-cancer agent.
