Iberis Amara (Iberis amara) Compounds

Iberis amara contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin and glycosides may support its use in digestive health. Saponins, terpenoids, and alkaloids also play roles in its medicinal profile. Essential oils and anthocyanins add to its complex pharmacological activity.

Iberis Amara (Iberis amara) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Iberis amara are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. They support the plant's role in herbal medicine by enhancing immune function and reducing oxidative stress.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Iberis amara contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe mucous membranes. It is particularly valued in herbal medicine for its ability to support digestive health and aid in wound healing. This compound plays a key role in the plant's traditional use for treating respiratory and gastrointestinal conditions.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Iberis amara, known for its antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's ability to reduce inflammation and support digestive health. This compound is found in significant amounts in the above-ground parts of the plant. Its presence enhances the traditional use of Iberis amara in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Iberis amara. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly its antispasmodic and analgesic effects. Coumarin is often used in herbal medicine to support digestive health and relieve muscle spasms. Its presence in Iberis amara makes it a valuable component in traditional herbal formulations.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Iberis amara, a plant used in herbal medicine. It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and may have potential effects on digestive and inflammatory processes. Glycosides in Iberis amara are often studied for their traditional uses in supporting gut health and reducing inflammation.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Iberis amara, known for its detergent-like properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Saponin may help reduce inflammation and support immune function. Its presence enhances the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Iberis amara contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are responsible for the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments. Terpenoids enhance the overall therapeutic potential of Iberis amara.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound in Iberis amara, known for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and respiratory conditions. Research suggests it may support gut health and alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome. Its presence highlights the plant's value in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Iberis amara contains volatile compounds like monoterpene hydrocarbons and oxygenated terpenes. These compounds contribute to its aromatic profile and may support its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory support. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's aerial parts.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Iberis amara, contributing to its antioxidant properties. Found in the plant's flowers and leaves, it helps neutralize free radicals and may support immune function. This compound is also associated with anti-inflammatory effects, making Iberis amara a valuable herb in herbal medicine.
