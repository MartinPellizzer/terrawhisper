What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Teucrium Polium (Wood Sage)?

Teucrium polium has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and alleviating respiratory issues.

Its medicinal constituents include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The herb can be prepared into teas, tinctures, or infusions for topical or internal use. While generally considered safe, possible side effects may include allergic reactions or interactions with medications.

It is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using wood sage, especially if you have any underlying health conditions or are pregnant/breastfeeding.

What are the health benefits of Teucrium polium?

Teucrium polium has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving respiratory issues due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's essential oils have been shown to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis, asthma, and coughs, making it a natural remedy for respiratory problems.

Additionally, Teucrium polium has antioxidant properties that may help protect against cell damage and oxidative stress, potentially contributing to overall health and well-being.

What are the active constituents of Teucrium polium?

The health benefits of Teucrium polium comes from its active constituents, such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes.

These compounds have been found to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils have been shown to exhibit spasmolytic effects, making it a potential treatment for digestive issues like irritable bowel syndrome.

What are the medicinal preparations of Teucrium polium?

Teucrium polium has different medicinal preparations, such as teas, infusions, tinctures, and capsules.

The tea is used to treat digestive issues like bloating and indigestion, while the infusion is employed for its anti-inflammatory properties in treating skin conditions like acne and eczema.

The tincture is used internally to treat respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma, whereas the capsules are utilized as a supplement to support cardiovascular health by reducing blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

What are the possible side effect of using Teucrium polium improperly?
