Clavate Lobelia (Lobelia Clavata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Lobelia clavata.

Clavate lobelia, scientifically known as Lobelia clavata, is a medicinal herb native to North America, valued for its adaptogenic properties and historical use in traditional healing practices. This small, flowering plant is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and a unique, slightly bitter taste, which has contributed to its traditional use as a stimulant and respiratory tonic. Historically, Indigenous peoples of North America used Lobelia clavata to treat respiratory ailments such as asthma and bronchitis, as well as to aid in digestion and reduce inflammation. In modern wellness applications, it is sometimes used in herbal formulations to support respiratory health and as a mild stimulant to enhance mental clarity and energy levels. Despite its limited commercial availability, Lobelia clavata remains a subject of interest in herbal medicine due to its potential active compounds, though its use requires caution due to its potency and possible toxicity if misused.
