What to know about Barosma betulina (buchu) before using it medicinally

Buchu, scientifically known as Barosma betulina, is a plant that has been used for centuries to promote overall health and wellness due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

In addition to its medicinal benefits, buchu is also prized in the culinary world for its use in traditional South African dishes such as stews, soups, and braais. Horticulturally, buchu is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners and landscapers. From a botanical standpoint, buchu belongs to the Rutaceae family and has small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive bitter flavor.

The use of buchu dates back centuries, with references to its medicinal properties appearing in ancient African folklore and traditional medicine practices.
