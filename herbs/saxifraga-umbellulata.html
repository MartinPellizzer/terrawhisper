Saxifraga umbellulata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga umbellulata has a low-growing, caespitose habit characterized by dense basal rosettes of spoon-shaped, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red. These leaves possess entire margins and a smooth texture, clustering tightly to form a compact cushion. From the center of these rosettes, a slender, leafless scape emerges to support a terminal umbel of small flowers. The blossoms typically feature five white petals and are supported by a small, cup-like calyx. This structural arrangement allows the plant to thrive in rocky, alpine crevices.
