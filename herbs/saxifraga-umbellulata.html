Saxifraga umbellulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. This plant is a representative of the family Saxifragaceae, falling under the genus Saxifraga, and is specifically identified by its unique species name, umbellulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga umbellulata has a low-growing, caespitose habit characterized by dense basal rosettes of spoon-shaped, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red. From the center of these rosettes, a slender, leafless scape emerges to support a terminal umbel of small flowers. The blossoms typically feature five white petals and are supported by a small, cup-like calyx. This structural arrangement allows the plant to thrive in rocky, alpine crevices.
