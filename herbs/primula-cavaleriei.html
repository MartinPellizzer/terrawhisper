Primula cavaleriei

What's the taxonomical classification of Primula cavaleriei?

Primula cavaleriei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is part of the order Ericales and falls under the family Primulaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Primula, specifically identified by the species name cavaleriei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula cavaleriei has a basal rosette of long-petioled, obovate to elliptical leaves that feature crenate or dentate margins and a slightly pubescent texture. The leaves are typically dark green and grow densely together to form a compact ground cover. From the center of this rosette, several upright flowering stalks emerge, bearing clusters of salverform flowers. These blossoms display a distinct color palette, often ranging from pale pink to deeper shades of lavender or violet. The corolla consists of five fused petals that expand into a flat limb, centered around a small, pale eye.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Yunnan province in southwestern China. It thrives specifically within the limestone crevices and rocky slopes of the Hengduan Mountains. These populations are typically found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 3,500 meters above sea level. The distribution is highly localized, meaning it exists only in very specific microclimates within this mountain range. Such a restricted range makes the species sensitive to changes in its immediate montane habitat.

How is this plant cultivated?
