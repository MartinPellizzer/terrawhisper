Bothriospermum secundum has a woody, climbing habit characterized by twining stems, lanceolate to elliptical leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary clusters. Its stems can become quite woody at the base as the plant matures and climbs through forest canopies. The reproductive structures consist of tiny, often yellowish or greenish flowers that produce small, fleshy fruits.
