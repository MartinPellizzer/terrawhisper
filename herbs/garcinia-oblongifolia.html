Garcinia oblongifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Garcinia oblongifolia?

Garcina oblongifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Clusiaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Garcinia, with its specific designation being the species oblongifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Garcinia oblongifolia has an evergreen shrub or small tree habit characterized by opposite, oblong to elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture and possess entire margins. The foliage typically displays a glossy dark green upper surface with a paler underside, often featuring prominent pinnate venation. Its stems are woody and can become somewhat branched as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually giving rise to fleshy, rounded fruits. These fruits transition in color from green to yellow or orange upon ripening and contain several seeds.

What are the pharmacological activities of Garcinia oblongifolia?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antimicrobial, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. These biological properties are primarily attributed to the presence of bioactive secondary metabolites like xanthones and phenolic compounds. The antioxidant capacity helps neutralize free radicals, thereby protecting cells from oxidative stress and damage. Additionally, its antibacterial and antimicrobial effects allow it to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. These combined actions also provide significant relief from pain and reduce swelling through its anti-inflammatory and analgesic mechanisms.

According to a study published by "BMC genomics", Garcinia oblongifolia exhibits notable anti-inflamatory, analgesic, antimyatrophy, antibacterial, and antitumor properties. Metabolite profiling of the plant identified 402 metabolites, including 215 that accumulated differentially across 4 tissues. Among these, 16 xanthones were predominantly localized in the bark and branches. The study utilized 70,401 full-length transcripts and identified 8,842 differentially expressed transcripts to understand its chemical makeup. A reconstructed xanthone biosynthetic pathway involves 32 full-length transcripts encoding 7 key enzymes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several specific medicinal compounds including Oblongifolin C, Oblongifolins I, obligonifolins E-G, obligonixanthones A-C, and various other xanthones. These bioactive substances are primarily classified as xanthones, which are known for their diverse pharmacological properties. The presence of these distinct molecules like Oblongifolin C and Oblongifolins I suggests significant potential for therapeutic applications. Furthermore, the specialized obligonixanthones A-C contribute to the plant's unique chemical profile. Such a complex array of secondary metabolites defines the medicinal value of Garciniat oblongifolia.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", the acetone extract of the bark of Garcinia oblongifolia resulted in the isolation of 3 new xanthones known as oblongixanthones A-C (1-3). The process also yielded 3 new polyprenylated benzoylphloroglucinol compounds called oblongifolins E-G (4-6) along with 12 known compounds. Among these, oblongifoins I (5) and J (6) represent the first natural products with structural features similar to 2 known oxidation products of garcinol. All isolates were tested for apoptosis-inducing effects against HeLa-C3 cells. Oblongifolin C (16) was identified as the most potent apoptotic inducer of the compounds evaluated.
