Garcinia oblongifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Garcinia oblongifolia?

Garcina oblongifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Clusiaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Garcinia, with its specific designation being the species oblongifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Garcinia oblongifolia has an evergreen shrub or small tree habit characterized by opposite, oblong to elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture and possess entire margins. The foliage typically displays a glossy dark green upper surface with a paler underside, often featuring prominent pinnate venation. Its stems are woody and can become somewhat branched as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually giving rise to fleshy, rounded fruits. These fruits transition in color from green to yellow or orange upon ripening and contain several seeds.

As discussed in a study, Garcinia oblongifolia exhibits notable anti-inflamatory, analgesic, antimyatrophy, antibacterial, and antitumor properties. Metabolite profiling of the plant identified 402 metabolites, including 215 that accumulated differentially across four tissues. Within these findings, 16 xanthones were predominantly localized in the bark and branches. The study utilized integrated sequencing to yield 70,401 full-length transcripts to better understand these bioactive compounds. Analysis revealed 8,842 differentially expressed transcripts and a biosynthetic pathway involving 32 full-length transcripts encoding seven key enzymes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Oblongifolin C, Oblongifolins I, cirsimarin, kaempferol, and marucoflavone. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the complex chemical profile used in various pharmacological studies. Oblongifolin C and the related Oblongifolins I are notable components within its structure. The presence of cirsimarin and marucoflavone further enhances its biological significance. Additionally, the flavonoid kaempferol is found within the plant, often providing antioxidant properties.

As discussed in a study, the acetone extract of the bark of Garcinia oblongifolia yielded 3 new xanthones named oblongixanthones A-C (1-3) and 3 new polyprenylated benzoylphloroglucinols named oblongifolins E-G (4-6). The fractionation process also resulted in the isolation of 12 known compounds. Obliginolins I (5) and J (6) represent the first natural products with structural features similar to 2 known oxidation products of garcinol. All isolates were tested for apoptosis-inducing effects against HeLa-C3 cells. Among the compounds evaluated, oblongifolin C (16) was identified as the most potent apoptotic inducer.
