Garcina oblongifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Clusiaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Garcinia, with its specific designation being the species oblongifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Garcinia oblongifolia has an evergreen shrub or small tree habit characterized by opposite, oblong to elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture and possess entire margins. The foliage typically displays a glossy dark green upper surface with a paler underside, often featuring prominent pinnate venation. Its stems are woody and can become somewhat branched as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually giving rise to fleshy, rounded fruits.
