What's the taxonomical classification of Quercus fabri?

Quercus fabri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Fagales and the family Fagaceae, eventually reaching the genus Quercus. Finally, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species designation, fabri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Quercus fabri has a woody, perennial structure characterized by thick, furrowed bark, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and small, nut-like acorns held in shallow cups. The leaves are typically leathery in texture and arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches. Its branching habit often forms a spreading canopy that provides dense shade. The reproductive structures consist of catkins that appear before the foliage fully develops in the spring. These morphological features collectively allow the species to thrive in its specific ecological niche.
