What's the taxonomical classification of Cirsium souliei?

Cirsium souliei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asterales, it falls under the family Asteraceae, a group known for its diverse composite flowers. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Cirsium, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species souliei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium souliei has large, deeply lobed leaves with spiny margins and terminal flower heads composed of numerous pink to purple tubular florets.

The leaves are typically lanceolate or oblong in shape and feature prominent veins that provide structural support. Along the stems, the plant often displays stiff, bristly hairs that serve as a defensive mechanism against herbivores. The inflorescence consists of dense, rounded clusters that emerge from the leaf axils or the stem apex.

These flower heads are supported by thick, involucrate bracts that protect the developing seeds.
