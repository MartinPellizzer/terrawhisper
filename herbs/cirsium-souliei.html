Cirsium souliei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium souliei has large, deeply lobed leaves with spiny margins and terminal flower heads composed of numerous pink to purple tubular florets. The leaves are typically lanceolate or oblong in shape and feature prominent veins that provide structural support. Along the stems, the plant often displays stiff, bristly hairs that serve as a defensive mechanism against herbivores. The inflorescence consists of dense, rounded clusters that emerge from the leaf axils or the stem apex. These flower heads are supported by thick, involucrate bracts that protect the developing seeds.
