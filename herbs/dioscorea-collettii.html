Dioscorea collettii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Dioscoreales. This plant is a representative of the family Dioscoreaceae and is specifically situated within the genus Dioscorea, where it is identified by the species name collettii.

What are the pharmacological activities of Dioscorea collettii?

According to a study published by "Journal of aquatic animal health", Dioscorea collettii exhibited 100% activity against Dactylogyrus intermedius. The findings indicate that extracts of this plant can be developed as a preferred natural antiparasitic for the control of D. intermedius.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the steroidal sapogenin diosgenin, along with various other bioactive alkaloids and flavonoids. Diosgenin serves as a primary chemical precursor for the industrial synthesis of steroid hormones like progesterone and cortisone. These medicinal compounds are often extracted from the tuberous roots to be utilized in pharmaceutical applications. The presence of these specific phytochemicals contributes to the plant's traditional use in various medicinal practices. Researchers continue to study these substances to better understand their therapeutic potential in human medicine.

According to a study published by "BMC biochemistry", the plant Dioscorea collettii was identified as one of the species containing the steroidal sapogenin diosgenin. This finding resulted from an analysis using an ultra-performance liquid chromatography coupled with diode array detection and electrospray ionization mass spectrometry method. To screen potential resources, the developed method was used to analyze 24 batches of plant samples. Among the four Dioscorea species tested, Dioscorea collettii was one of the three species in which diosgenin was found.
