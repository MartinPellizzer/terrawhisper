What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea collettii?

Dioscorea collettii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Dioscoreales. This plant is a representative of the family Dioscoreaceae and is specifically situated within the genus Dioscorea, where it is identified by the species name collettii.

What are the pharmacological activities of Dioscorea collettii?

This plant has potent anthelmintic and antiparasitic pharmacological activities used to combat various internal infections. Research indicates that the chemical constituents within its tubers effectively target and eliminate parasitic worms from the host organism. These bioactive compounds work by disrupting the physiological processes of the parasites, leading to their destruction. Furthermore, its traditional medicinal use supports its reputation as a natural remedy for treating helminth infestations. The specific secondary metabolites present in the plant contribute to its broad spectrum of antimicrobial properties.

As discussed in a study, Dioscorea collettii exhibited 100% activity against Dactylogyrus intermedius. The ethyl-acetate, chloroform, and methanol extracts of Dioscorea collettii demonstrated 100% efficacy at 80, 80, and 120 mg/L. Following 48 h of exposure, these extracts had EC50 values of 19.7, 27.1, and 37.8 mg/L, respectively. These findings suggest that extracts of the plant can be developed as a preferred natural antiparasitic for the control of D. intermedius.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the steroidal sapogenin diosgenin, along with various flavonoids and polyphenolic compounds that contribute to its therapeutic potential. Diosgenin serves as a critical chemical precursor used extensively in the pharmaceutical industry for the synthesis of steroid hormones. Beyond its role in hormone production, the plant's secondary metabolites often exhibit significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers study these specific chemical constituents to understand how they might mitigate oxidative stress in human cells. The complex synergy between these various medicinal compounds makes the plant a subject of interest in natural product chemistry.

As discussed in a study, Dioscorea collettii is one of the species where diosgenin was found. The research utilized an ultra-performance liquid chromatography (UPLC) coupled with diode array detection (DAD) and electrospray ionization mass spectrometry (ESI-MS) method for identification and determination. A total of 24 batches of plant samples were analyzed using this developed method. This study included four Dioscorea species among the various plants screened. The method used to identify the compounds in Dioscorea collettii presented good sensitivity, precision, and accuracy.
