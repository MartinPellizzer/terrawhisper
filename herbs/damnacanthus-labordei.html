Damnacanthus labordei

What's the taxonomical classification of Damnacanthus labordei?

Damnacanthus labordei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned under the order Gentianaales. It is further categorized into the family Rubiaceae, which defines its botanical relationships. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Damnacanthus, and its specific identification is completed by its species name, labordei.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Damnacanthus labordei can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, localized skin irritation, and potential neurological impairment if ingested in large quantities. Contact with the plant's sap often results in contact dermatitis or painful rashes on sensitive areas of the skin. If consumed, individuals may experience nausea, abdominal cramping, and vomiting due to the plant's toxic alkaloids. Some documented cases also suggest that concentrated doses might lead to dizziness or temporary loss of motor coordination. It is crucial to avoid all physical contact and ingestion to prevent these adverse reactions.
