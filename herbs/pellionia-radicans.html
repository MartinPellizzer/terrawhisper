Pellionia radicans

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pellioniia radicans has a creeping, prostrate growth habit characterized by slender, rooting stems that spread horizontally across the ground. These stems frequently produce adventitious roots at the nodes to anchor the plant and facilitate vegetative expansion. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical in shape, featuring entire margins and a somewhat fleshy texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often presenting in subtle greenish or pale tones. This creeping morphology allows the species to effectively colonize forest floors and shaded habitats through rapid lateral spread.
