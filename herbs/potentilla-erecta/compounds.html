Erect Potentilla (Potentilla erecta) Compounds

Potentilla erecta contains several key active compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent effects. Glycosides and saponins may support digestive health, while coumarin and terpenoids offer anti-inflammatory benefits. Essential oils, anthocyanins, and alkaloids further enhance its medicinal profile.

Erect Potentilla (Potentilla erecta) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Potentilla erecta are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in supporting urinary and digestive health. They may help reduce oxidative stress and modulate immune responses, making them valuable in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Potentilla erecta, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues. Tannins in Erect Potentilla help reduce excess secretions and soothe irritated tissues. They are also believed to support wound healing and enhance the plant's overall medicinal efficacy.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Potentilla erecta, contributing to its medicinal properties. It exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. This compound helps enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Potentilla erecta, known for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments. Glycosides in Erect Potentilla may support its role in herbal medicine for wound healing and immune support.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Potentilla erecta. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting circulation and reducing inflammation. Coumarin's anticoagulant properties may support cardiovascular health, though its effects should be monitored in clinical settings.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Potentilla erecta contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like arthritis and digestive issues. They also aid in enhancing the plant's overall therapeutic profile.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Potentilla erecta. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins help in reducing swelling and supporting immune function. They are commonly used in herbal remedies for respiratory and skin conditions.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil from Potentilla erecta contains aromatic compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory and digestive health. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its therapeutic aromas and bioactive components.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Potentilla erecta, known for its antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and supporting cardiovascular health. Found in the flowers, anthocyanin helps protect cells from oxidative damage. Its presence underscores the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Potentilla erecta, commonly known as Erect Potentilla. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting urinary tract health and reducing inflammation. This alkaloid is valued for its potential therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.
