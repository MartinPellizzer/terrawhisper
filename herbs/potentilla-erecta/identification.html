Botanical Identification of Erect Potentilla (Potentilla erecta)

To identify Erect Potentilla (Potentilla erecta) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Erect Potentilla (Potentilla erecta) belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Potentilla, and species Potentilla erecta. This classification places it within the rose family, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Erect Potentilla is Potentilla erecta, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It has several synonyms, including Spiraea erecta and Potentilla vulgaris, reflecting historical classification shifts. Common names include Evergreen Cinquefoil and Scotch Cinquefoil, while regional names vary, such as "Bog Cinquefoil" in Scotland. Historical names often reflect its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Erect Potentilla (Potentilla erecta) is a perennial herb with a taproot system. It has upright, branching stems that grow up to 30 cm tall. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with 5-7 pairs of oval leaflets. The inflorescence is a dense cluster of yellow flowers, each with five petals and numerous stamens. The fruit is a dry capsule containing small, flattened seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Erect Potentilla can be distinguished by its upright growth habit, smooth stems, and small, yellow flowers. It has pinnately lobed leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red-brown fruits. It is often confused with other Potentilla species but is easily identified by its distinctive leaf shape and flower color.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Potentilla erecta is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Erect Potentilla blooms from June to August, producing yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification, harvest the plant in early spring before flowering for the most accurate botanical characteristics.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Potentilla erecta is closely related to other species within the Potentilla genus. It shares key characteristics with related genera in the Rosaceae family, such as rose and bramble. Its phylogenetic placement reflects shared traits like leaf structure and flower morphology, aiding in its identification within the family.
