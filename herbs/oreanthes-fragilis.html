What is Oreanthes fragilis?

Oreanthes fragilis is an evergreen shrub native to the tropical forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in terminal clusters, and its leathery, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and a cuneate base. The plant grows 0.5-1 meter tall and 1-2 meters wide, with a woody base and a branching system that becomes more sparse with age. The flowers are borne on a peduncle 1-2 mm long, and the corolla is campanulate, 4-5 mm long, and 3-4 mm wide.

The fruit is a red or purple berry, 5-7 mm in diameter, containing 3-5 seeds.

The main herbal preparations of Oreanthes fragilis are derived from its leaves and flowers.
