Davallia sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Davallia sinensis?

Davalliia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This plant is a representative of the family Polypodiaceae, falling under the genus Davallia, with its specific designation being the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Davalliia sinensis has a creeping rhizome that is covered in dense, brownish-to-white scales and bears pinnately divided, triangular fronds. These leaves are typically bright green and feature a delicate, lace-like appearance due to their deeply lobed structure. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit where the fronds emerge from the central rhizome in a rosette pattern. Small, round sori are located on the underside of the pinnules to facilitate spore production. This morphology allows the fern to effectively cling to surfaces and spread across its substrate.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight and consistently moist, well-draining substrate to thrive in a humid environment.

Because it is an epiphytic fern, it performs best when grown in a bark-based mix that allows air to reach the roots. Frequent misting or the use of a pebble tray helps maintain the high moisture levels necessary to prevent the delicate fronds from drying out. It is also important to avoid exposing the plant to cold drafts or direct midday sun, which can scorch the foliage.

Regular watering should ensure the medium stays damp but never waterlogged to prevent root rot.
