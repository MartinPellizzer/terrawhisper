Davallia sinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Davalliia sinensis has a creeping rhizome that is covered in dense, brownish-to-white scales and bears pinnately divided, triangular fronds. These leaves are typically bright green and feature a delicate, lace-like appearance due to their deeply lobed structure. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit where the fronds emerge from the central rhizome in a rosette pattern. Small, round sori are located on the underside of the pinnules to facilitate spore production. This morphology allows the fern to effectively cling to surfaces and spread across its substrate.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of southern China, specifically occurring in provinces such as Guangdong, Guangxi, Hainan, Fujian, and Yunnan. It thrives primarily in humid, shaded forest environments where moisture levels remain consistently high. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific climatic conditions found across the southeastern Asian mainland. Within these habitats, the species is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks as an epiphyte. This localized range makes it a distinctive component of the regional flora in these specific Asian territories.
