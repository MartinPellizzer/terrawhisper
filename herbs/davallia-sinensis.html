What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Davalliia sinensis has a creeping rhizome that is covered in dense, brownish-to-white scales and bears pinnately divided, triangular fronds. Small, round sori are located on the underside of the pinnules to facilitate spore production. This morphology allows the fern to effectively cling to surfaces and spread across its substrate.
