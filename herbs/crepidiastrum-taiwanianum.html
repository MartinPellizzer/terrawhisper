Crepidiastrum taiwanianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crepidiastrum taiwanianum has woody stems, elliptic to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins, and small, yellowish-white actinomorphic flowers arranged in terminal cymes. The leaves are typically arranged oppositely along the branches and possess a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. Its inflorescences consist of multiple small blossoms that emerge from the ends of the stems during the blooming season. The floral structure features five distinct petals and a central cluster of stamens characteristic of the Rubiaceae family. These morphological traits distinguish the species from other climbing woody vines within its genus.
