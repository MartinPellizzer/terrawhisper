What is Hemionitis angustifrondosa?

Hemionitis angustifrondosa is a species of fern in the family Pteridaceae.

It has a rhizome with a dimorphic pattern of frond and root production, and its fronds are monomorphic, pinnate, and leathery, measuring up to 40 cm in length. The rachis is glabrous, with a submarginal vein present in each pinna, and the pinnae are ovate-lanceolate in shape with an entire margin. The frond base is a stipitate sorus with a dense cluster of spores, and the sporophyte is dioecious.

Hemionitis angustifrondosa is endemic to Papua New Guinea.
