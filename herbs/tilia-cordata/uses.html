Tilia (Tilia cordata) Uses

Tilia cordata is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, cough, and respiratory infections. It helps alleviate symptoms of colds, hay fever, and allergic rhinitis. It may also reduce inflammation in the tonsils and ease headaches and insomnia. Its soothing properties support overall respiratory and nervous system health.

Tilia (Tilia cordata) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Tilia cordata has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties help reduce bronchial irritation and ease coughing. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate symptoms and promote easier breathing.

2. Cold symptoms

Tilia cordata is used to alleviate cold symptoms such as congestion, coughing, and sore throat. Its anti-inflammatory and mild sedative properties help reduce irritation and soothe respiratory discomfort. Often prepared as a tea, it supports the body's natural recovery process during mild colds.

3. Hay fever

Tilia cordata has been traditionally used to alleviate hay fever symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and allergic reactions, making it a valued remedy in herbal medicine for seasonal allergies. Its use is supported by historical practices and modern herbal formulations.

4. Cough

Tilia cordata is used to soothe coughs due to its mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce bronchial irritation and ease respiratory discomfort. Often prepared as a tea, it is valued for its calming effect on the throat and lungs. It is particularly effective for dry, persistent coughs.

5. Inflamed tonsils

Tilia cordata has been traditionally used to soothe inflamed tonsils due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is often prepared as a gargle or infusion to reduce swelling and alleviate throat pain. Its mild sedative effects may also help ease associated discomfort and promote healing.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Tilia cordata has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose by soothing mucous membranes. Often prepared as a tea or tincture, it supports respiratory health and is a common remedy in herbal medicine for seasonal allergies.

7. Influenza

Tilia cordata has been traditionally used to support respiratory health during influenza. Its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties help reduce symptoms like coughing and congestion. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe the throat and ease breathing. Historically, it was a key herb in herbal remedies for flu-related respiratory discomfort.

8. Respiratory infections

Tilia cordata has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections such as coughs and bronchitis. It may reduce mucus congestion and soothe irritated airways, making it a valued remedy in herbal medicine for respiratory support.

9. Headache

Tilia cordata is used to relieve headaches, particularly tension-type headaches, due to its mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce muscle tension in the scalp and neck, promoting relaxation. Often combined with other herbs like valerian or chamomile, it supports overall nervous system balance. Its calming effects make it a gentle option for regular headache management.

10. Insomnia

Tilia cordata has been traditionally used to support sleep and alleviate insomnia. Its calming effects are attributed to compounds like tiliroside and flavonoids, which may reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture and is considered safe for long-term use in herbal medicine.
