Tilia (Tilia cordata) Compounds

The primary compounds in Tilia include flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and coumarin, known for its anticoagulant effects. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to antioxidant and astringent actions. Glycosides and saponins support cardiovascular health, while essential oils and terpenoids provide aromatic and mild sedative effects. Anthocyanins and alkaloids add to the plant's diverse therapeutic potential.

Tilia (Tilia cordata) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Tilia cordata, commonly known as small leaf lime. These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making them valuable in herbal medicine for treating conditions like respiratory and skin disorders.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Tilia cordata, commonly known as small-leaved lime. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its mild sedative and antispasmodic properties, aiding in the treatment of nervous system disorders and digestive issues.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Tilia cordata, commonly known as small-leaf linden. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating respiratory and inflammatory conditions.

4. Tannin

Tannin in Tilia cordata is a key active compound known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in reducing inflammation and treating skin conditions. Tannins also support digestive health by binding to proteins and aiding in the elimination of toxins. This compound plays a significant role in the therapeutic applications of lime tree in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Tilia cordata, commonly known as small leaf lime. It contributes to the plant's calming and anti-inflammatory effects, making it valuable in herbal medicine for respiratory and nervous system support.

6. Essential oil

The essential oil of Tilia cordata contains compounds like limonene and methyl salicylate. It is known for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties. This oil is commonly used in aromatherapy and topical applications. It supports respiratory and digestive health.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Tilia cordata, commonly known as smallleaf linden. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in soothing respiratory conditions and reducing inflammation. Saponins in Tilia cordata may help support immune function and have mild antiviral properties.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Tilia cordata, commonly known as smallleaf linden. These compounds contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and may have anti-inflammatory and calming effects, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine for respiratory and nervous system support.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Tilia cordata, commonly known as small-leaved linden. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory conditions and inflammation due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Anthocyanin is responsible for the flower's deep color and is often extracted for medicinal applications.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Tilia cordata, commonly known as small-leaved lime. It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic properties, often used in herbal medicine for its calming effects on the nervous system.
