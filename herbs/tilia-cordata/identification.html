Botanical Identification of Tilia (Tilia cordata)

To identify Tilia (Tilia cordata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Tilia cordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Tiliaceae. The genus is Tilia, and the species is Tilia cordata. The division, class, and order are not provided in the data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name for small-leaved lime is Tilia cordata, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Tilia europaea and Tilia lata. Common names include small-leaved lime, small-leaved linden, and European lime. Regional names vary, with some areas calling it linden or lime tree.

Morphological Characteristics

Tilia cordata, commonly known as small-leaf lime, is a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded crown. It has a deep taproot system that anchors it firmly. The stem is smooth, gray-brown, and becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are heart-shaped, dark green above, and lighter below, with a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a spherical cluster of small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a small, winged nut enclosed in a capsule.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Tilia cordata can be distinguished by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, pale yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, and smooth, gray bark. It differs from Tilia europaea by smaller leaves and less showy flowers, and from Tilia americana by its narrower leaves and different fruit structure.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Tilia cordata, or small-leaf linden, is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is commonly found at low to mid-altitudes, typically below 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Tilia cordata blooms in early to mid-summer, typically between June and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, leaves are best harvested in late spring to early summer when they are fully developed and characteristic.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Tilia cordata is closely related to other Tilia species, sharing common traits within the Tiliaceae family. It belongs to the genus Tilia, which includes species like Tilia europaea. These species exhibit similar morphological and genetic characteristics, making them easily identifiable within the genus.
