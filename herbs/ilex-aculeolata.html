Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Ilex, and its specific species designation, aculeolata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex aculeolata has evergreen, sclerophyllous leaves that are lanceolate to narrowly elliptical in shape, characterized by sharply serrated margins with stiff, spine-tipped teeth. The leaf surfaces exhibit a coriaceous texture, often appearing dark green and lustrous on the upper side while remaining slightly paler underneath. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody, grayish-brown stems that form a dense, shrubby architecture. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. The plant eventually produces small, globose drupes that transition in color as they mature.
