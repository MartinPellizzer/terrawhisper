Cyclea polypetala

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyclea polypetala?

Cyclea polypetala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae, specifically falling under the genus Cyclea, with the specific epithet polypeatala defining its unique species identity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cyclea polypetala contains diverse secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and various glycosides that contribute to its pharmacological potential. Researchers have identified specific bioactive components such as cyclopeptides and lignans within its chemical profile. These compounds are primarily studied for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Some studies also suggest that extracts from the plant may possess neuroprotective effects due to these specific molecular structures. The therapeutic application of these substances is currently being explored through various pharmacological assays to determine their full medicinal value.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, digestive issues, and respiratory ailments. It contains bioactive compounds that help soothe skin irritation and heal wounds through topical application. In traditional medicine, decoctions of the leaves are often consumed to manage stomach pains and gastrointestinal disturbances. The plant also possesses properties that can alleviate symptoms of coughs and congestion. These medicinal uses are common in various ethnobotanical practices across tropical regions.
