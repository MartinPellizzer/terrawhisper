Cyclea polypetala

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyclea polypetala?

Cyclea polypetala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Menispermaceae, specifically falling under the genus Cyclea, with the specific epithet polypeatala defining its unique species identity.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Cyclea polypetala contains bioactive compounds in its leaves, stems, and roots that are utilized for various medicinal purposes. The leaves are frequently employed in traditional practices to treat skin inflammations and minor wounds due to their soothing properties. Some regional medicinal traditions also use the roots to address digestive issues or internal discomfort. Extracts from the entire plant are sometimes applied topically to reduce swelling or manage localized pain. These different components work together to provide a broad range of therapeutic applications in folk medicine.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

These compounds interact with the NF-kappaB signaling pathway to suppress the production of pro-inflammatory molecules. Furthermore, certain constituents exhibit inhibitory effects on specific protein kinases that regulate cell proliferation and survival. By disrupting these molecular interactions, the plant demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory and potentially anti-tumor properties.

    What are the herbal preparations of Cyclea polypetala?

    Cyclea polypetala is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant parts in water, or as a crushed paste applied topically to the skin. The leaves and stems are often boiled to create medicinal teas used for internal consumption to address various ailments. In some traditional practices, the fresh juice is squeezed directly from the foliage for immediate application to wounds or skin irritations. Additionally, the dried plant material can be processed into a powder that is mixed with liquids or honey. These diverse preparation methods allow the active compounds within the plant to be utilized for both systemic and localized treatments.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Cyclea polypetala can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic reactions if consumed or applied improperly. The plant contains various bioactive alkaloids that may trigger adverse biological responses in sensitive individuals. Some users have reported nausea or abdominal discomfort following ingestion of its derivatives. Topical application of certain extracts might lead to contact dermatitis or localized redness on the skin. Because the specific toxicological profile is not fully documented in humans, caution is necessary when using this species.
