Ludwigia adscendens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ludwigia adscendens has a creeping or climbing growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The leaves are typically arranged in an opposite pattern and possess an elliptical to lanceolate shape with entire margins. Small, yellowish flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring four distinct petals that are characteristic of the Onagraceae family. The plant often develops adventitious roots at the nodes where the stems make contact with the substrate. This morphological structure allows the species to effectively colonize moist environments and spread across surfaces.

      What are the herbal preparations of Ludwigia adscendens?

      Ludwigia adscendens is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using the dried aerial parts of the plant to extract its bioactive compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ludwigia adscendens can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive human tissue. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, the presence of certain phytochemicals may trigger inflammatory responses in some individuals. Contact with the sap or crushed leaves can result in redness or a stinging sensation on the epidermis. People with known hypersensitivity to similar aquatic flora should exercise extreme caution when handling this species. Avoiding skin contact is the most effective way to prevent these adverse physical reactions.
