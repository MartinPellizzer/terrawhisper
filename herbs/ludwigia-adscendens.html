Ludwigia adscendens

What's the taxonomical classification of Ludwigia adscendens?

Ludwigia adscendens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Myrtales and the family Onagraceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Ludwigia, with its specific identification as the species adscecedens.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various inflammatory conditions. It is frequently applied topically as a poultice to help heal sores and minor injuries through its antimicrobial properties. In traditional medicinal practices, decoctions of the plant are sometimes ingested to soothe digestive distress. The species contains bioactive compounds that may assist in reducing swelling and managing systemic discomfort. These applications vary significantly depending on the specific cultural traditions and preparation methods used by local practitioners.
