What's the taxonomical classification of Bostrychanthera deflexa?

Bostrychanthera deflexa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Bostrychanthera, with the specific epithet deflexa.

      What are the herbal preparations of Bostrychanthera deflexa?

      Bostrychanthera deflexa is prepared as a decoction of the leaves and stems, a powder made from dried plant parts, or an infusion for medicinal use. These different preparations allow practitioners to extract varying concentrations of the plant's active compounds. When used as a decoction, the material is boiled to release tougher chemical constituents into the liquid. Alternatively, the dried parts can be ground into a fine powder to be mixed with water or honey. Infusions provide a gentler method of extraction by steeping the plant in hot water without boiling.

Due to these unpredictable biological reactions, caution is necessary when interacting with this species.
