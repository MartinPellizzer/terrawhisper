Sugarcane (Saccharum officinarum)

Sugarcane (Saccharum officinarum) is a member of the Poaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, Pacific Islands, and Indian Subcontinent. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and sap have been used for culinary uses, decoctions, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface pale green, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are compound, paniculate, with spikelets arranged in two rows, each spikelet bearing two bracts and two to three bisexual florets with three stamens and a single pistil, flowers are white to pale green, actinomorphic, with three sepals and three petals of similar length, and a prominent palea and lemma.
Stems
Woody, cylindrical, terete, with nodes and internodes, glabrous, and exhibiting a sympodial growth habit.
