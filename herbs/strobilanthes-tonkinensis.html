Strobilanthes tonkinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Strobilanthes tonkinensis?

Strobilanthes tonkinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Lamiales, it is further nested within the family Acanthaceae. This specific plant is identified by its genus, Strobilanthes, and is uniquely distinguished by its species name, tonkinensis.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin infections, digestive issues, and respiratory ailments. It is commonly employed in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of stomach aches and general gastrointestinal discomfort. The leaves can also be applied topically to help heal wounds or soothe skin irritations. Additionally, some practitioners use preparations of the plant to manage coughs and other lung-related disturbances. These diverse medicinal applications highlight its importance in local herbal practices.
