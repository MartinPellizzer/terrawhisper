Strobilanthes tonkinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strobilanthes tonkinensis has opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins and acute apices that emerge from woody, erect stems. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes that bear small, tubular flowers characteristic of the Acanthaceae family. These flowers are often purplish or white and are arranged in a way that attracts specific pollinators.
