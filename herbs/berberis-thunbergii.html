As for diseases, Berberis thunbergii is prone to root rot caused by overwatering, which can lead to the death of the plant. Additionally, fungal infections such as botrytis (Botrytis cinerea) and septoria leaf spot (Septoria berberidis) can infect the leaves and stems of the plant, causing black spots or lesions that can spread quickly if not treated promptly. Furthermore, powdery mildew (Erysiphe polygoni) can also affect Berberis thunbergii, causing a white, powdery coating to form on the leaves.

To prevent these problems, it is essential to provide good air circulation, maintain proper watering habits, and use integrated pest management techniques whenever possible.
