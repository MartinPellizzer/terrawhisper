What is Quercus meihuashanensis?

Quercus meihuashanensis is a species of tree in the family Fagaceae.

It is endemic to China and Taiwan, with a distribution limited to mountainous regions. This deciduous tree has a broad, rounded crown and a straight, columnar trunk, typically growing to 20-30 meters in height. The leaves are alternate, with 7-9 lobes, 4-7 cm in length, and a wavy margin, while the petiole is 1-2 cm long and glabrous.

The fruits are acorns, 2-3 cm long, with a cap covering 1/3 to 1/2 of the nut.
