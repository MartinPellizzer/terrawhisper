What is Angelica cyclocarpa?

Angelica cyclocarpa is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is a perennial herb with a stout rhizome, and erect, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 150 cm tall. The plant has compound, bipinnate leaves with 2-3 pairs of pinnae, and a terminal leaflet, with the leaf margins being lobed or serrated. The inflorescence is a compound umbel with numerous small white flowers, which are arranged in a circular pattern around the center.

The fruit is a small, two-sided schizocarp with two seeds, each containing one seed.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Angelica cyclocarpa are coumarins, ligustilide, butylphthalide, angelan, and phthalides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Coumarins have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anticancer activities, contributing to the plant's potential in reducing oxidative stress and cancer cell growth. The phthalides and angelan, a lignan, in Angelica cyclocarpa have also been reported to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, further emphasizing the plant's potential in the management of inflammation and oxidative stress.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Angelica cyclocarpa are tincture, infusion, tea, decoction and salve.

Tinctures are made by steeping the roots in a solvent, typically ethanol or glycerol, to extract its bioactive compounds such as polysaccharides and phenolic compounds with antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. Infusions and teas are prepared by steeping the roots in hot water to extract flavonoids and alkaloids that may have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Decoctions are made by boiling the roots in water to extract its lignans and phenolic acids that may have antimicrobial and antiviral properties.

Salves are prepared by infusing the roots in a carrier oil, such as olive or coconut oil, to create topical preparations for wound healing and skin issues.

What are the possible side effects of using this herb improperly?

The possible side effects of using Angelica cyclocarpa improperly include experiencing vomiting and nausea due to its emetic properties, which can cause irritation to the digestive system.

Allergic reactions may occur, particularly in individuals with sensitivities or allergies to plants in the same genus, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing. Additionally, Angelica cyclocarpa may interact with medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, by altering their metabolism and efficacy. Prolonged or excessive use of Angelica cyclocarpa has also been linked to the development of liver damage, as its compounds may cause oxidative stress and inflammation in the liver cells.

Furthermore, the improper use of Angelica cyclocarpa can exacerbate pre-existing liver conditions, such as hepatitis or cirrhosis, making it essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using this herb.
