Adenophora liliifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Adenophora liliifolia?

Adenophora liliifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is further categorized under the family Campanulaceae, which defines its broader botanical characteristics. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Adenophora, and its specific taxonomic identity is completed by its species name, liliifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora liliifolia has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are arranged oppositely or in whorls along a stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The foliage is characterized by smooth margins and a texture that varies from slightly pubescent to glabrous depending on the specific specimen. Its inflorescence consists of small, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from terminal or axillary clusters. These blossoms feature a purplish or pale lavender corolla and are supported by slender pedicels. The plant also develops a woody caudex that serves as a persistent underground base.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of central and western China, specifically found in provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, and Gansu. It typically thrives in mountainous habitats where it grows among rocky crevices or forest margins. The species is most frequently located at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 3,500 meters above sea level. These specific environmental conditions provide the cool, moist climates necessary for its survival.
