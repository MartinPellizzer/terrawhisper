Aoranthe cladantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aoranthe cladantha has an erect, perennial herb structure characterized by slender, brownish stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters. The leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along the stem and possess an ovate or lanceolate shape with entire margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a delicate pink to purple coloration. These blossoms are often clustered in small groups, which provides a subtle visual contrast against the green foliage. The plant's overall morphology is defined by its delicate, wiry habit and compact growth form.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Aoranthe cladantha can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and localized swelling upon direct contact with the flesh. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger inflammatory responses in sensitive individuals. If the sap or crushed leaves touch the eyes, it can lead to intense stinging and temporary blurred vision. Some reports suggest that accidental ingestion could result in gastrointestinal distress or nausea. Careful handling and the use of gloves are recommended when working with this species to avoid these adverse reactions.
