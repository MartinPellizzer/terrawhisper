Ipecac

Cephaelis ipecacuanha

Ipecac (Cephaelis ipecacuanha) is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, known for its use in traditional medicine to treat respiratory and digestive ailments, particularly coughs and nausea.

Botanical Identification

Ipecac, or Cephaelis ipecacuanha, is a flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family. Commonly known as Brazilian ipecac or quebra-matã, it is a perennial shrub native to South America. It has opposite leaves, yellow flowers, and a taproot, and has been referenced under various synonyms in traditional herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rubiales
  • Family: Rubiaceae
  • Genus: Cephaelis
  • Species: Cephaelis ipecacuanha

Preparation Methods

Powder: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Infusion: Used for treating coughs and respiratory infections.

Tincture: Used for treating coughs and bronchial issues.

Safety Profile

Ipecac is contraindicated in cases of poisoning or overdose due to its emetic properties. It should not be used by individuals with severe gastrointestinal disorders. Prolonged use may lead to dependency and reduced effectiveness. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
