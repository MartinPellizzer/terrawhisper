Cephaelis ipecacuanha

Cephaelis ipecacuanha, also known as ipecac, is a perennial plant native to Brazil and other parts of South America, belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

The medicinal properties of Cephaelis ipecacuanha lie in its roots, which contain alkaloids that have emetic and expectorant properties, making it traditionally used to induce vomiting and relieve respiratory issues.

The health benefits of ipecac include its ability to treat nausea, indigestion, and coughs, as well as its use in traditional medicine to treat fever and diarrhea.

Ipecac is often used in herbal preparations such as tinctures, infusions, and syrups, although its use is largely limited due to the availability of safer alternatives.
