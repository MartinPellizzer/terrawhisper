Seseli yunnanense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Seseli yunnanense has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by basal leaves that are pinnately divided into narrow, linear segments and terminal inflorescences composed of dense compound umbels. The stems are typically erect and can reach varying heights depending on the specific habitat conditions. Its leaves feature finely serrated margins and a distinctively textured surface. The small, white flowers are arranged in numerous tiny florets that create a broad, flat-topped flowering structure. This morphological arrangement is typical of the Apiaceae family, aiding in effective pollination.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Seseli yunnanense can cause severe skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and phototoxicity upon direct contact with its tissues. This plant contains furanocoumarins, which are chemical compounds that react with sunlight to damage skin cells. When the sap touches the skin and is subsequently exposed to ultraviolet light, it can trigger redness, blistering, and painful inflammation. Individuals with sensitive skin or pre-existing allergies may experience more intense swelling or itching. It is important to avoid handling the plant without protective gloves to prevent these dermatological reactions.
