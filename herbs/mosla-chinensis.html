Mosla chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Mosla chinensis?

Mosla chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Lamiales. It falls within the family Lamiaceae, which defines its closer botanical relatives. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Mosla, and its unique species designation, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mosla chinensis has a woody, perennial structure characterized by upright stems, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Its stems exhibit a branching pattern that supports a dense, bushy growth habit. The root system is fibrous and well-developed to ensure stability in its native soil. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to thrive in varied subtropical environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Mosla chinensis?

This plant has antibacterial, antioxidant, and spleen strengthening pharmacological activities. The antibacterial properties allow it to combat various pathogenic microorganisms effectively. Its antioxidant capacity helps neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within the body. Furthermore, the plant is traditionally used for its ability to support spleen strengthening and improve digestive health. These combined medicinal effects make Mosla chinensis a valuable resource in natural therapeutics.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Mosla chinensis exhibited considerable antibacterial activity against both serotypes of Streptococcus mutans. The minimal inhibitory concentration for this plant was measured at 7.8-23.4 mg/ml. This activity was demonstrated against both serotypes of the cariogenic bacterium. The study included a total of 79 aqueous extracts in its preliminary antibacterial screening.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds apiagenin, chrysoeriol, luteolin, negluetin, and quercetin. These specific flavonoids contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile found within the species. Each compound plays a unique role in the plant's chemical defense and potential therapeutic properties. Researchers often study these substances for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory characteristics. The presence of such a complex mixture of phytochemicals makes Mosla chinensis a subject of significant botanical interest.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", five compounds have been isolated from Mosla chinensis 'jiangxiangru'. These chemical components were isolated and purified using silica gel and Sephadex LH-20 column chromatography. The identified compounds include negletein (I), luteolin (II), quercetin (III), chrysoeriol (IV), and apiigenin (V). These 5 specific compounds were obtained from the genus for the first time. Structures for these components were elucidated through spectral data and physicochemical properties.
