Mosla chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Mosla chinensis?

Mosla chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Lamiales. It falls within the family Lamiaceae, which defines its closer botanical relatives. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Mosla, and its unique species designation, chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mosla chinensis has a woody, perennial structure characterized by upright stems, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Its stems exhibit a branching pattern that supports a dense, bushy growth habit. The root system is fibrous and well-developed to ensure stability in its native soil. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to thrive in varied subtropical environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Mosla chinensis?

This plant has antibacterial, antioxidant, and spleen strengthening pharmacological activities that contribute to its therapeutic potential in traditional medicine. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within the body's cells. The antibacterial components allow it to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Additionally, it is utilized for its ability to promote spleen strengthening and improve digestive functions. These combined effects make Mosla chinensis a significant subject for pharmacological research.

As discussed in a study, Mosla chinensis exhibited considerable antibacterial activity against both serotypes of Streptococcus mutans. The minimal inhibitory concentration for this plant was found to be between 7.8 mg/ml and 23.4 mg/ml. These measurements were expressed in terms of dry starting material. This activity was observed against both serotypes c and d of the bacterium.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds apigenin, carvacrol, luteolins, quercetin, and thymol. These specific phytochemicals work together to provide various therapeutic benefits to the human body. For instance, the presence of carvacrol and thymol often contributes to strong antimicrobial properties. Additionally, the flavonoids apigenin, luteolins, and quercetin are recognized for their significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Such a diverse chemical profile makes the plant a valuable subject for pharmacological research.

As discussed in a study, five compounds have been isolated from Mosla chinensis 'jiangxiangru'. These medicinal compounds were isolated and purified by silica gel and Sephadex LH-20 column chromatography. The specific compounds identified through spectral analysis and physicochemical properties are negletein (I), luteolin (II), quercetin (III), chrysoeriol (IV) and apiigenin (V). This study represents the first time that compounds I-V are obtained from this genus.

What side effects this plant can have?
