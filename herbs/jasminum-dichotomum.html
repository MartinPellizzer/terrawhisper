Jasminum dichotomum

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum dichotomum?

Jasminum dichotomum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Oleaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Jasminum and the species dichotomum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum dichotomum has woody, climbing stems that feature opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with entire margins and acute apices. The foliage typically grows in pairs along the branches, creating a dense canopy of green. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying a delicate white or pale cream coloration. These blossoms are characterized by their fragrant scent and five-lobed corolla structure. The plant's growth habit relies on its twining stems to ascend surrounding vegetation or structures.

According to a study published by "Evidence-based complementary and alternative medicine : eCAM", Jasminum dichotomum was one of 17 medicinal plants investigated for its wound healing potentials. The study utilized a scratch assay with NIH 3T3 fibroblasts from mice to evaluate how these plant extracts affect cell proliferation and/or migration in vitro. After 21 hours of incubation, Jasminum dichotomum was identified as one of 5 plant species that demonstrated wound healing activity in vitro.
