Tripterospermum taiwanense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tripterospermum taiwanense has woody stems, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and unique three-winged achenes. The plant typically presents as a small shrub or subshrub with oppositely arranged leaves that feature prominent venation. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into the characteristic winged fruits. These wings are a key diagnostic feature used to distinguish the species from other members of the Euphorbiaceae family. The overall structure is compact, adapted to the specific environmental conditions of its native habitat.
