Juncus allioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juncus alliios has slender, cylindrical stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and are characterized by a smooth, greenish texture. The leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures located at the base of the plant. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in compact clusters near the tips of the stems. Each flower features six brownish tepals and produces small, three-valved capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. The overall growth habit is tufted, forming dense clumps in its natural wetland habitat.
