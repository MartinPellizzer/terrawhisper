What's the taxonomical classification of Juncus allioides?

Juncus allioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Poales and into the family Juncacae, where it is situated within the genus Juncus. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species designation, alliodes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juncus alliios has slender, cylindrical stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and are characterized by a smooth, greenish texture.

The leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures located at the base of the plant. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in compact clusters near the tips of the stems. Each flower features six brownish tepals and produces small, three-valved capsules containing numerous tiny seeds.

The overall growth habit is tufted, forming dense clumps in its natural wetland habitat.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Juncius allioides can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if handled or ingested improperly. Individuals with sensitive skin might experience redness, itching, or a burning sensation in the affected areas. If consumed, it could lead to nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting due to its inherent toxicity. It is essential to avoid direct contact and ensure the plant is not used in any medicinal capacity without professional guidance.
