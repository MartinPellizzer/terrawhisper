Castanopsis argyrophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Castanopsis argyrophylla has large, leathery, elliptic to oblong leaves that measure up to 30 centimeters in length and feature a distinctive silvery-white underside due to a dense layer of fine hairs. The upper surfaces of the foliage are typically a dark, glossy green, providing a sharp contrast to the pale, pubescent undersides. Its branches are sturdy and woody, supporting a dense canopy characteristic of an evergreen tree. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually giving way to woody, globose fruits. The overall structure of the plant reflects its adaptation to humid, tropical environments through its robust and thick-textured foliage.

Because it prefers Mediterranean climates, its distribution is strictly limited to areas with significant fog influence and mild temperatures.
