Ligularia sibirica

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia sibirica?

Ligularia sibirica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asterales, it falls under the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Ligularia, with its specific identity defined by the species name sibiriica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia sibirica has large, coarsely serrated, ovate to lanceolate leaves that grow from a basal rosette and upright flowering stems. These dark green leaves possess a distinctively rugose texture with prominent venation. The plant produces tall, erect stalks that can reach heights of up to one meter. At the top of these stems, bright yellow composite flower heads emerge in dense, terminal clusters. Each flower head consists of numerous small ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular florets.
