Petrocosmea nervosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Petrocosmea nervosa?

Petrocosmea nervosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the taxonomic order Lamiales. As a member of the family Gesneriaceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Petrocosmea, with its specific designation being the species nervosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Petrocosmea nervosa has succulent, orbicular to reniform leaves that feature deeply impressed, prominent venation creating a rugose surface texture.

The leaf margins are often slightly crenate or irregularly lobed, helping to distinguish it from similar species. These leaves grow in a basal rosette pattern, spreading outward from a central stem to maximize light absorption in shaded environments. The undersides of the foliage may exhibit a different coloration or texture compared to the upper surfaces.

Small, delicate flowers emerge from the center of the rosette, typically displaying a tubular structure characteristic of the Gesneriaceae family.
