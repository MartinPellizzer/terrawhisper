Illigera cordata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illigera cordata has woody climbing vines characterized by heart-shaped, entire leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The foliage typically features a glossy texture with prominent venation patterns on the upper surface. Its stems are slender and capable of twining around surrounding vegetation to reach sunlight in forest understories. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that often transition in color as they mature. These morphological traits are essential for its identification within its tropical habitat.
