Ainsliaea latifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ainsliaea latifolia has broad, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively textured, rugose surface. The foliage typically features three to five prominent veins originating from the base of the leaf blade. These leaves are arranged alternately along woody, somewhat spreading stems. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in terminal clusters, often displaying a yellowish or greenish hue. The plant structure is characterized by a low-growing, shrubby habit that thrives in shaded forest environments.
