In addition to its health benefits, yellow gentian is also used in cooking, particularly in traditional European dishes such as stews and soups, where it adds a tangy flavor. As a cultivated plant, yellow gentian requires specific horticultural conditions, including well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice among gardeners. The plant's botanical properties include its unique tubular flowers and distinctive bitter taste, which has been used to create teas and infusions for centuries.

Historically, yellow gentian has been mentioned in ancient Greek and Roman texts, where it was valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
