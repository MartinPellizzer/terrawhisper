What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Cyclopia Intermedia (Suba)?

Cyclopia intermedia, also known as suba, has health benefits such as lowering cholesterol and improving heart health.

The herb contains medicinal constituents such as flavonoids, xanthines, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties. Suba is often prepared as a tea or infused in hot water to make a medicinal concoction. However, excessive consumption may cause stomach upset, diarrhea, or allergic reactions.

It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using suba to treat any medical condition.

What are the medicinal preparations of Cyclopia intermedia?

Cyclopia intermedia has different medicinal preparations, such as infusions, decoctions, and syrups.

The infusion is used to treat coughs and colds, while the decoction is employed for its anti-inflammatory properties in the treatment of skin conditions and wounds. Syrup preparations are used to soothe sore throats and relieve digestive issues.

Additionally, suba's dried leaves can be made into a tea that helps alleviate menstrual cramps and supports lactation in nursing mothers.

What are the possible side effect of using Cyclopia intermedia improperly?

Improper use of Cyclopia intermedia increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as dizziness, headaches, and nausea.

It may also lead to respiratory issues like asthma attacks, bronchitis, and lung inflammation. Other potential side effects include stomach ulcers, diarrhea, and vomiting. Furthermore, it can cause skin problems like rashes, itching, and eczema, as well as hair loss and brittle nails.

Additionally, improper use of suba may result in increased heart rate, blood pressure, and anxiety, potentially leading to cardiovascular complications.
