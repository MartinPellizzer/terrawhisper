Cyclopia (Cyclopia intermedia) Uses

Cyclopia intermedia is used to treat respiratory infections, cough, asthma, and common cold due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps alleviate symptoms of bronchitis, allergic rhinitis, and sinusitis by reducing congestion and irritation. The herb also supports recovery from otitis media and influenza by boosting immune function and reducing throat inflammation.

Cyclopia (Cyclopia intermedia) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Cyclopia intermedia is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing irritation in the throat and airways. It may help alleviate symptoms of coughs, colds, and bronchitis due to its antimicrobial and expectorant properties. Traditional use highlights its role in clearing mucus and promoting easier breathing during respiratory infections.

2. Cough

Cyclopia intermedia is used to alleviate coughs due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce irritation in the respiratory tract and promotes the removal of mucus, making it effective for both dry and productive coughs. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture to support respiratory health.

3. Asthma

Cyclopia intermedia, also known as African resthoort, is used in traditional herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It is believed to help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing inflammation and promoting bronchial clearance. Its expectorant properties aid in loosening mucus, making it easier to breathe. This plant is often prepared as a tea or infusion for its therapeutic effects.

4. Common cold

Cyclopia intermedia, also known as Cape chamomile, is used traditionally to alleviate symptoms of the common cold. It helps reduce fever, soothe sore throats, and ease nasal congestion. Its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties support respiratory health during colds. It is often prepared as a tea to promote comfort and recovery.

5. Bronchitis

Cyclopia intermedia is used in traditional herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It may help alleviate symptoms of bronchitis by reducing inflammation and easing coughing. Its expectorant properties assist in clearing mucus from the airways, promoting easier breathing. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for these purposes.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Cyclopia intermedia, also known as African violet, is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties help reduce nasal congestion and sneezing. It is typically prepared as a tea or tincture and is valued for its mild, natural approach to managing seasonal allergies.

7. Otitis media

Cyclopia intermedia is used in herbal medicine to support the treatment of otitis media due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling and infection in the middle ear, often used alongside conventional treatments. Its bitter compounds may stimulate immune response and promote drainage.

8. Influenza

Cyclopia intermedia is used in traditional herbal medicine to support respiratory health during influenza. It contains anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting compounds that may help alleviate symptoms and shorten the duration of the illness. Its expectorant properties aid in clearing mucus, making it a valued remedy in treating respiratory infections.

9. Sinusitis

Cyclopia intermedia is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health, particularly for sinusitis. It helps reduce inflammation and mucus production in the sinuses, offering relief from congestion and pain. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties make it a valuable remedy for chronic or recurrent sinus infections.

10. Sore throat

Cyclopia intermedia is used to soothe sore throats due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce irritation and swelling in the throat lining. Often prepared as a tea, it provides quick relief and supports the body's natural healing process. Its mild taste makes it suitable for regular use during throat infections.
