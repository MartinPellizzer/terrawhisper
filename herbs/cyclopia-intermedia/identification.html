Botanical Identification of Cyclopia (Cyclopia intermedia)

To identify Cyclopia (Cyclopia intermedia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Cyclopia intermedia belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Cyclopia, and species Cyclopia intermedia. It is a flowering plant commonly known as the "sugarbush" and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its bitter properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Cyclopia intermedia, first described by the botanist Carl Peter Thunberg in 1811. It is also known by several synonyms, including Cyclopia sessilis and Cyclopia corymbosa, reflecting historical taxonomic revisions. Common names include "cyclopia" and "cape chamomile," while regional and historical names vary across different cultures and regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Cyclopia intermedia is a small, erect shrub with a compact growth habit. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, adapting to arid environments. The stem is woody and branched, with a rough texture. Leaves are small, linear, and slightly hairy, arranged alternately along the stem. The inflorescence is a dense cluster of yellow flowers, typically arranged in umbels. The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cyclopia intermedia can be distinguished by its distinctive yellowish-brown stem with longitudinal ridges, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic, and its unique yellow flowers with five narrow lobes. It is often confused with other Cyclopia species, but its leaf shape and floral structure provide key differentiating features.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cyclopia intermedia is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at elevations between 300 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cyclopia intermedia blooms from September to November, producing small yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from December to March. Harvesting is typically done during the flowering period for accurate identification and to ensure potency.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cyclopia intermedia is closely related to species within the same genus, Cyclopia. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with other Cyclopia species. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its close relationship within the Asteraceae family, characterized by composite flower heads and specific leaf structures.
