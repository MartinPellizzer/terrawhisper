What's the taxonomical classification of Vitex quinata?

Vitex quinata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Lamiales and belongs to the family Lamiaceae. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Vitex, specifically identified by the species name quinata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitex quinata has palmately compound leaves typically consisting of five leaflets that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape with serrated margins. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaf arrangement. Each leaflet possesses a pointed apex and a distinctively textured surface. The inflorescences are composed of small, tubular flowers that grow in terminal clusters. These flowers are often lavender or blue and eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes.
