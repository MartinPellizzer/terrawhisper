What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bletilla formosana has an underground tuberous rhizome from which emerges a rosette of long, lanceolate leaves that are typically bright green with slightly wavy margins. The plant produces upright flowering stalks that bear clusters of bell-shaped, zygomorphic flowers. Each bloom is characterized by intricate patterns of purple, pink, or white markings on the tepals.
