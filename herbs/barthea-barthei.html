Barthea barthei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Barthea barthei has a perennial, woody, rhizomatous habit characterized by upright, slender stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. The leaves are small, linear to lanceolate in shape, and arranged oppositely along the stems. Flowers are typically small and solitary, emerging from the leaf axils with a delicate structure. The plant features a fine, somewhat pubescent texture on its vegetative parts. Its root system relies on a creeping rhizome to facilitate vegetative spread within its habitat.
