Alopecurus aequalis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alopecurus aequalis has a perennial growth habit characterized by narrow, linear leaves and a terminal panicle inflorescence consisting of numerous small, spikelet-like branches. The leaves are typically basal or cauline, featuring parallel venation and a slightly scabrous texture along the margins. Its flowering heads are relatively compact and cylindrical, often displaying a greenish to purplish hue depending on the stage of maturity. The spikelets are arranged in a way that creates a dense, somewhat bristly appearance. The plant's root system is fibrous, providing stability within its preferred grassland habitats.
