What's the taxonomical classification of Strophanthus wallichii?

Strophanthus wallichii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Gentianaales and falls under the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Strophanthus, and its specific species name, wallichii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Strophantus wallichii contains cardiac glycosides, specifically strophanthin and related compounds, which act by inhibiting the sodium-potassium ATPase pump in heart muscle cells. These bioactive molecules are primarily concentrated in the seeds of the plant. Due to their potent effect on cardiac contraction, they have historically been investigated for their ability to regulate heart rhythm and increase contractile force. However, the therapeutic window is extremely narrow, making the compounds highly toxic if not administered with precision. Careful extraction is required to isolate these alkaloids for potential medicinal applications in treating congestive heart failure.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Strophantus wallichii targets cardiac glycosides like strothophin and wallichin to inhibit the sodium-potassium ATPase pump in myocardial cells. This specific inhibition leads to an increase in intracellular sodium, which subsequently enhances calcium influx through the sodium-calcium exchanger. The resulting rise in cytosolic calcium levels increases the force of myocardial contractions, providing a positive inotropic effect. These biochemical interactions are primarily responsible for the plant's potent cardiotonic properties used in treating congestive heart failure. However, the high affinity for these protein targets also necessitates precise dosing to avoid life-threatening cardiac arrhythmias.

What side effects this plant can have?

Its potent cardiac glycosides may lead to life-threatening cardiovascular issues such as arrhythmia, bradycardia, and hypotension. Patients experiencing toxicity often report significant dizziness alongside these physical symptoms. These side effects occur because the plant's chemical compounds directly impact heart rate and blood pressure regulation. Careful monitoring is essential due to the potential for these systemic reactions to escalate quickly.
