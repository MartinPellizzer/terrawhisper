Strophanthus wallichii

What's the taxonomical classification of Strophanthus wallichii?

Strophanthus wallichii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Gentianaales and falls under the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Strophanthus, and its specific species name, wallichii.

What are the pharmacological activities of Strophanthus wallichii?

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the highly toxic cardiac glycoside known as wallichine, along with other bioactive compounds such as strophantin and various alkaloids. These specific medicinal compounds function by inhibiting the sodium-potassium pump in cardiac muscle cells, which can increase the force of heart contractions. In controlled pharmacological settings, these substances have been researched for their potential to treat congestive heart failure and certain arrhythmias. However, the potency of these chemicals requires extreme precision because even minor dosage errors can lead to fatal cardiac toxicity. Detailed chemical analysis of the seeds and leaves is essential for isolating these compounds safely for therapeutic use.
