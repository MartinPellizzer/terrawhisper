Strophanthus wallichii

What's the taxonomical classification of Strophanthus wallichii?

Strophanthus wallichii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Gentianaales and falls under the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Strophanthus, and its specific species name, wallichii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Strophantus wallichii contains cardiac glycosides, specifically strophanthin and related compounds, which act by inhibiting the sodium-potassium ATPase pump in heart muscle cells. These bioactive molecules are primarily concentrated in the seeds of the plant. Due to their potent effect on cardiac contraction, they have historically been investigated for their ability to regulate heart rhythm and increase contractile force. However, the therapeutic window is extremely narrow, making the compounds highly toxic if not administered with precision. Careful extraction is required to isolate these alkaloids for potential medicinal applications in treating congestive heart failure.

What are the therapeutic actions of Strophanthus wallichii?

Strophantus wallichii has potent cardiac glycoside properties that allow it to increase the force of myocardial contractions and regulate irregular heart rhythms. These therapeutic actions are primarily driven by compounds that inhibit the sodium-potassium pump within cardiac cells. By increasing intracellular calcium levels, the plant helps improve the efficiency of the heart muscle during systole. It is traditionally used in various medicinal practices to treat conditions related to congestive heart failure and palpitations. However, its high toxicity requires extremely precise dosing to prevent life-threatening arrhythmias.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various conditions including cardiac issues, skin ailments, and parasitic infestations. The seeds contain potent glycosides that have historically been utilized to manage heart failure and regulate irregular heartbeats. In traditional medicine, topical applications of the plant are sometimes applied to address skin infections or fungal growths. Additionally, certain preparations are used as anthelmintics to expel intestinal worms and other parasites from the body. Due to its high toxicity, these medicinal uses must be strictly controlled to prevent dangerous side effects.

What are the herbal preparations of Strophanthus wallichii?

Strophanthus wallichii is prepared as a concentrated decoction, a fine powder, or an infusion derived from its seeds and leaves for medicinal use. The seeds are the most potent part of the plant and are frequently processed into a powder to be administered in controlled doses. Because the plant contains highly toxic cardiac glycosides, preparations are strictly regulated to avoid fatal toxicity. In traditional systems, the extracts are sometimes used in topical applications or as part of complex herbal formulations. These various forms allow practitioners to manage the potency of the active compounds during treatment.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Strophantus wallichii can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, cardiac arrhythmias, and potential death if ingested due to its toxic cardiac glycosides. These potent compounds interfere with the sodium-potassium pump in heart cells, leading to irregular heartbeats and palpitations. Symptoms of poisoning often include intense vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea shortly after consumption. In higher doses, the toxicity can progress to dangerous fluctuations in heart rate or complete cardiac arrest. Because of these significant risks, the plant should be handled with extreme caution and never consumed.
