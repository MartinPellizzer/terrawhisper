What's the taxonomical classification of Craibiodendron stellatum?

Craibiodendron stellatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is positioned in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of its biological classification, it falls under the order Ericales and the family Ericaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Craibiodendron, and its unique species designation, stellatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Craibiodendron stellatum has woody stems characterized by a deeply fissured, corky bark and serrated, lanceolate leaves that measure between five and eight centimeters in length. The foliage is arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches, displaying a glossy texture on the upper surface and a pale, pubescent underside. Its most distinctive feature is the star-shaped, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from axillary clusters. These blossoms possess five creamy white petals and a central cluster of prominent, golden anthers. The plant also produces small, drupaceous fruits that transition from green to a deep indigo upon maturation.
