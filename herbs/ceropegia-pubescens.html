What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ceropegia pubescens has slender, cylindrical, succulent stems that are covered in fine, soft, white hairs and feature distinct longitudinal ribs.

These stems are often branched and can reach significant heights in their natural habitat. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that typically emerge from the upper portions of the stems. These blossoms are often pale or creamy in color and exhibit a delicate, waxy texture.

      What are the herbal preparations of Ceropegia pubescens?

      Ceropegia pubescens is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice to utilize its medicinal properties. The decoction is made by boiling the dried plant material in water to extract potent alkaloids for internal consumption. For milder therapeutic effects, an infusion allows the delicate components to steep in hot water without intense heat.
