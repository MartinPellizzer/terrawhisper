What are the pharmacological activities of Altingia chinensis?

These medicinal properties are often attributed to the presence of specific flavonoids and alkaloids that interfere with cancer cell growth cycles. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates potential in suppressing tumor development through targeted biochemical pathways. These findings suggest that Altingia chinensis may serve as a valuable source for developing new therapeutic agents against various malignancies.

As discussed in a study, the petroleum ether extract (APE) of Altingiia chinensis suppresses non-small-cell lung cancer (NSCLC) via the induction of apoptosis, attenuation of EMT, and downregulation of the PI3K/Akt pathway. In vitro, APE at doses of 31.25, 62.5, and 125 μg/ml dose-dependently decreased cell viability and induced apoptosis in LLC and H1299 cells by increasing cleaved-caspase 3 levels and the Bax/Bcl-2 ratio. The extract also suppressed cell migration and invasion by increasing E-cadherin expression and downregulating vimentin to inhibit metastasis. In vivo, the intragastric administration of 100 mg/kg APE significantly inhibited tumor growth in a C57BL/6J mice model without apparent side effects. Furthermore, APE directly bound to PI3Kα to inhibit its activity, thereby suppressing the PI3K-Akt pathway.
