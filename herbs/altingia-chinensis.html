Altingia chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Altingia chinensis?

Altingia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic progression, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Saxifragales, this plant is uniquely situated in the family Altingiaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Altingia and the species chinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Altingia chinensis contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including triterpenoids, flavonoids, and various phenolic compounds. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented pharmacological activities. Specifically, the triterpenoids within the species often contribute to significant anti-inflammatory and immunosuppressive effects. The presence of flavonoids provides potent antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals in biological systems. Researchers study these specific compounds to explore their potential applications in developing new antimicrobial and anticancer treatments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Altingia chinensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammation, skin infections, digestive disorders, and various types of tumors. It is frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of wounds. Some traditional practices utilize its components to alleviate internal ailments related to the gastrointestinal tract. Researchers are also investigating its potential bioactive compounds for their ability to combat abnormal cell growth. This plant remains a significant subject of interest in the study of natural medicinal properties.
