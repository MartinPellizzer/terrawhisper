Euonymus myrianthus

What's the taxonomical classification of Euonymus myrianthus?

Euonymus myrianthus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Celastrales. This plant is a member of the family Celastraceae, specifically situated within the genus Euonymus, and is identified by the species epithet myrianthus.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Euonymus myrianthus can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may lead to dermatitis or localized rashes in sensitive individuals. If the berries or leaves are eaten, symptoms such as vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea are likely to occur. Severe poisoning could potentially lead to more serious neurological complications or organ distress. Because the exact chemical composition of its toxins is not fully documented, caution is necessary when handling any part of this species.
