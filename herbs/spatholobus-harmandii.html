Spatholobus harmandii

What's the taxonomical classification of Spatholobus harmandii?

Spatholobus harmandii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fabales. The plant is a member of the family Fabaceae and is further distinguished by its placement within the genus Spatholobus, ultimately identified as the species harmandii.

Spatholobus harmandii has demonstrated significant therapeutic potential through its ability to act as an anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant agent in traditional medicinal practices. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various conditions including skin diseases, wounds, stomach aches, and fever. Local communities often apply the plant parts topically to soothe inflammatory skin irritations and promote the healing of open cuts. When ingested, it is believed to alleviate internal discomfort related to gastrointestinal issues. It also serves as a traditional remedy for reducing body temperature during febrile episodes. These medicinal applications are rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical practices within its native regions.
