Cautleya gracilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cautleya gracilis has large, elliptical leaves that emerge from a pseudobulb and produce bright, wide-spreading flowers on a tall, erect inflorescence. The pseudobulbs are typically compressed and elongated, serving as the primary storage organs for the plant. Each leaf features a prominent midrib and a smooth texture, often reaching significant lengths to maximize light absorption. The inflorescence can grow several feet high, bearing multiple showy blossoms that exhibit distinct color patterns. These flowers are characterized by their complex structure and often feature a large, colorful labellum.
