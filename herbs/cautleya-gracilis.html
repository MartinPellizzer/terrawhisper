Cattleya gracilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is further categorized under the family Zingiberaceae, which places it among the ginger plants. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Cattleya and its designation as the species gracilis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated in the rhizomes and leaves of the plant. Research suggests that these compounds possess significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Some studies also indicate that the plant extracts may exhibit cytotoxic effects against specific cancer cell lines. These chemical profiles provide a scientific basis for its traditional use in treating various ailments.
