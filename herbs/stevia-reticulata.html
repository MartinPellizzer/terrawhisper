Stevia (Stevia Reticulata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Stevia reticulata.

Stevia, scientifically known as Stevia reticulata, is a medicinal herb native to South America, widely recognized for its intense sweet flavor and numerous health benefits. It contains stevioside, a rare active compound that provides a natural, calorie-free sweetening alternative without affecting blood sugar levels, making it particularly beneficial for individuals with diabetes. Traditionally used by indigenous communities in Paraguay and Brazil for its calming and appetite-suppressing properties, stevia has also been employed to treat hypertension and as a digestive aid. In modern wellness practices, it is increasingly popular as a natural sweetener and for its potential to support metabolic health and weight management. Its unique combination of a sweet taste, medicinal properties, and historical significance has made it a versatile and valuable plant in both traditional and contemporary health systems.
