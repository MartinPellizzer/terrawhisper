What's the taxonomical classification of Oresitrophe rupifraga?

Oresitrophe rupifraga belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Saxifragales and is a member of the family Saxifragaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific taxonomic placement within the genus Oresitrophe and the species rupifraga.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Oresitrophe rupifraga can cause acute gastrointestinal distress, severe skin irritation upon contact, and potential respiratory inflammation if inhaled. These reactions typically manifest as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping shortly after ingestion. Direct physical contact with the plant's foliage often results in contact dermatitis characterized by redness and itching. Some individuals may also experience swelling of the throat or coughing if microscopic particles enter the airways. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when handling the species in its natural habitat.
