Fraxinus stylosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Fraxinus stylosa?

Fraxinus stylosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Oleaceae, falling under the genus Fraxinus, and is specifically identified by the species name stylosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fraxinus stylosa has pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 13 leaflets that feature serrated margins and acute tips. The leaflets are typically elliptic or lanceolate in shape and are arranged oppositely along the rachis. The stems exhibit a woody texture with a characteristic bark that becomes fissured as the tree matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters, eventually developing into winged samaras for seed dispersal. These reproductive structures are designed to be carried effectively by the wind.
