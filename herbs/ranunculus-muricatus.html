Ranunculus muricatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Ranunculus muricatus?

Ranunculus muricatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae and is further categorized under the genus Ranunculus, specifically identified as the species muricatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ranunculus muricatus has a low-growing, creeping habit characterized by slender, prostrate stems that form dense mats through the use of stolons. Its leaves are typically small, deeply lobed, and possess serrated margins that give them a distinctively irregular appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that emerge from the foliage on short stalks. These blossoms feature multiple bright petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens. The fruit consists of small, dry achenes that are often slightly textured or bumpy on the surface.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining, loamy soil and full sun exposure to successfully produce its characteristic blooms.

Growers should plant the tubers in early spring once the danger of frost has passed and the soil has warmed significantly. Regular watering is essential during the growing season, but it is vital to avoid waterlogged conditions that can lead to tuber rot. Providing consistent organic mulch helps maintain moisture levels and regulates soil temperature around the root zone.

Once the flowering period concludes, the foliage should be removed to allow the plant to enter its necessary dormant phase.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Ranunculus muricatus contains medicinal compounds primarily located within its entire vegetative structure, including the roots, stems, and leaves. These parts house ranunculin, a glycoside that can be converted into protoanemonin when the plant tissue is crushed or damaged. Historically, various species in this genus have been utilized in traditional practices to treat skin conditions or as topical applications for inflammation. However, the chemical transformation of these components can lead to significant skin irritation or blistering if used improperly. Due to the presence of these potent irritants, modern medicinal use is strictly regulated and often cautioned against.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds muracatanes A, muracatanes B, muracatanes C, 4-benzyloxylonchocarpin, and 4-methoxylonchocarpin. These specific chemical constituents are classified as unique secondary metabolites found within the Ranunculus muricatus species. The muracatane derivatives represent a distinct group of compounds identified through phytochemical analysis of the plant's tissues. Additionally, the presence of the lonchoarpin-related molecules highlights the complex chemical profile of this botanical specimen. These substances are of significant interest to researchers studying the bioactive properties of the Ranunculus genus.

According to a study published by "Biomolecules", Ranunculus muricatus L. contains the new chalcone 4-benzyloxyonchocharpin (1), the new anthraquinones muracatanes A (2), muracatanes B (3), and the new naphthlene analog muracatanes C (4). The plant also yields 4-methoxylonchocharpin (5), β-sitosterol (6), and β-sitosterol β-D-glucopyranoside (7). Chalcone 1 exhibited potent acetylcholinesterase inhibitory effects with Ki of 5.39 µM and Ki' of 3.54 µM. Anthraquinone 3 showed α-glucosidase inhibitory effects with IC50-values of 164.46 ± 83.04 µM. Compound 5 displayed moderate cytotoxic activity against ovarian carcinoma (A2780, IC50 = 25.4 µM), colorectal adenocarcinoma (HT29, IC50 = 20.2 µM), breast cancer (MCF7, IC50 = 23.7 µM), and thyroid carcinoma (SW1736, IC50 = 26.2 µM).

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing asthma, treating immune system disorders, reducing intermittent fevers, combating oxidative stress, and fighting bacteria. Its chemical properties help stabilize the body's response during respiratory distress and periodic temperature spikes. The plant also functions as a potent antioxidant to neutralize harmful effects from oxidative stress. Additionally, its bioactive compounds provide defense against various bacteria and support the regulation of the immune system.

According to a study published by "Pakistan journal of pharmaceuatical sciences", Ranunculus muricatus is commonly used in the treatment of intermittent fevers, gout and asthma. Qualitative analysis indicated the presence of saponins, tannins, phenols, flavonoids and alkaloids, with saponins present in a high amount compared to other chemicals. Heavy metals estimation showed that iron was present in a high amount followed by zinc, though the concentration of these metals remains below the acceptable limit. The study also determined the physical parameters, antioxidant and antimicrobial activities of the extracts. The acetone extract fraction showed optimal antioxidant activity when compared to the ethanol and chloroform fractions of the candidate plant.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

Because the plant contains protoanemonin, all herbal preparations must undergo careful processing to neutralize these toxic compounds. Some historical accounts suggest using highly diluted tinctures, though this is rarely practiced due to the risk of severe blistering.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, blistering, mucous membrane irritation, nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain if handled or ingested. Contact with the plant's sap frequently leads to localized redness and the formation of painful blisters on the skin. If the tissue comes into contact with the eyes or mouth, it results in severe mucous membrane irritation. Ingestion of any part of the plant triggers gastrointestinal distress, specifically nausea and vomiting. These digestive issues are often accompanied by intense abdominal pain.
