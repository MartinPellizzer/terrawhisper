Oxalis acetosella

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxalis acetosella?

Oxalis acetosella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Oxalidales. As a member of the family Oxalidaceae, it is further organized into the genus Oxalis, specifically identified by the species name acetosella.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxalis acetosella has delicate, trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets that emerge from a creeping rhizome. These soft, green leaves are known for their ability to fold inward when touched or during darkness. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that feature fine purple veins and grow from short stalks. Its reproductive structures include small, green, capsule-like seed pods that eventually split to release tiny seeds. A thin, underground rhizome allows the plant to spread horizontally through the forest floor.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, stomach cramps, kidney stones, and calcium deficiency due to its high concentration of oxalic acid. Consuming large amounts of the leaves can lead to acute gastrointestinal distress such as nausea and vomiting. The acid also tends to irritate the digestive tract, frequently resulting in painful stomach cramps. If ingested regularly, the oxalates may bind with minerals in the body to form kidney stones. Furthermore, this chemical process can interfere with mineral absorption and eventually lead to a systemic calcium deficiency.
