Paraderris elliptica

What's the taxonomical classification of Paraderris elliptica?

Paraderris elliptica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is situated in the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, the specific classification is defined by its placement in the genus Paraderris, with its unique identity designated as the species elliptica.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Paraderris elliptica can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap may result in redness, itching, or localized swelling on sensitive areas of the skin. Some individuals might experience more severe inflammatory reactions depending on their specific sensitivity to the plant's chemical compounds. If swallowed, the plant can lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain. It is important to handle the vegetation with care to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
