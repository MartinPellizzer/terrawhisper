Paraderris elliptica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paraderris elliptica has elliptic to oblong leaves that typically measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length with entire margins and a smooth, leathery texture. The foliage is characterized by a deep green color and prominent venation that radiates from the midrib. Its stems are woody and climbing, allowing the plant to sprawl or ascend through various structures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in clusters. The plant produces fleshy fruits that contribute to its overall structural complexity.
