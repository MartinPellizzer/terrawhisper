Paraderris elliptica

What's the taxonomical classification of Paraderris elliptica?

Paraderris elliptica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is situated in the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, the specific classification is defined by its placement in the genus Paraderris, with its unique identity designated as the species elliptica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paraderris elliptica has elliptic to oblong leaves that typically measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length with entire margins and a smooth, leathery texture.

The foliage is characterized by a deep green color and prominent venation that radiates from the midrib. Its stems are woody and climbing, allowing the plant to sprawl or ascend through various structures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in clusters.

The plant produces fleshy fruits that contribute to its overall structural complexity.
