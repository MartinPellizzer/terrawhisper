Stellaria palustris

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria palustris has an annual growth habit characterized by erect or ascending stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are small, opposite, and ovate to lanceolate in shape, often measuring only a few millimeters in length. Its flowers are white and diminutive, featuring five petals that are frequently split or deeply notched at the tips. The plant produces small, ovoid capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds as its primary method of reproduction. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in the moist, marshy environments where it is commonly found.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate wetland regions of North America, specifically ranging from the southeastern United States through the mid-Atlantic coastal plains. It is most frequently documented in low-lying areas such as marshes, bogs, and the edges of slow-moving freshwater streams. Because it thrives in saturated soil, its presence is often tied to specific hydrological patterns within these ecosystems. Populations tend to be patchy rather than continuous, following the availability of acidic, nutrient-poor peat or mud.
