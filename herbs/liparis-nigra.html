Liparis nigra

What's the taxonomical classification of Liparis nigra?

Liparis nigra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Asparagales. As a member of the family Orchidaceae, it is placed within the genus Liparis, specifically identified by its species designation, nigra.

    What are the herbal preparations of Liparis nigra?

    Liparis nigra is prepared as a concentrated decoction, a topical poultice, or a diluted tincture for medicinal applications. Tinctures created through alcohol extraction allow for a more stable and long-lasting preservation of the plant's essence. These various methods ensure that the specific properties of the species are effectively delivered to the user.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Liparis nigra can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, or swelling upon direct contact with the plant's tissues. Some people might react to its chemical compounds by developing a rash or hives in the affected area. There is also a risk of nausea or stomach cramping if any part of the orchid is consumed accidentally. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when handling or being in close proximity to the plant.
