Lygodium circinnatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lygodium circinnatum?

Lygodium circinnatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and is further categorized within the order Schizaeales. Its taxonomic placement continues through the family Schizaeaceae, which houses the genus Lygodium. Finally, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species name, circinnatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lygodium circinnaum has thin, wiry, and branched rhizomes that support deeply divided, bipinnate to tripinnate fronds measuring between 10 and 40 centimeters in length. The individual pinnules are small, oblong to lanceolate in shape, and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. These delicate leaves are typically bright green and arranged in a spreading or ascending manner from the base. The plant lacks true stipes, as the leaf segments emerge directly from the branching axes. Spore-producing structures called sori are located on the undersides of the fertile segments.
