Lygodium circinnatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lygodium circinnaum has thin, wiry, and branched rhizomes that support deeply divided, bipinnate to tripinnate fronds measuring between 10 and 40 centimeters in length. The individual pinnules are small, oblong to lanceolate in shape, and possess entire or slightly serrated margins. These delicate leaves are typically bright green and arranged in a spreading or ascending manner from the base. The plant lacks true stipes, as the leaf segments emerge directly from the branching axes. Spore-producing structures called sori are located on the undersides of the fertile segments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, and parts of the Philippines. Its distribution is primarily concentrated within humid, forested environments where moisture levels remain consistently high. It can often be found growing in shaded areas like forest floors or along rocky outcrops in damp ravines. The species follows a range that extends through much of the Indo-Malayan botanical province. This specific geographic pattern reflects its dependency on warm, wet climates for survival.

      Lygodium circinnatum is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste to utilize its medicinal properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lygodium circinnaatum can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and localized allergic reactions upon direct physical contact with its tissues. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may act as irritants to sensitive human skin. Individuals handling the fern without protection might experience redness, itching, or swelling at the site of touch. There is also a potential risk of gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is accidentally ingested. Proper precautions should be taken to avoid unnecessary exposure to this species.
