Lygodium circinnatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Lygodium circinnatum?

Lygodium circinnatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and is further categorized within the order Schizaeales. Its taxonomic placement continues through the family Schizaeaceae, which houses the genus Lygodium. Finally, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species name, circinnatum.

Lygodium circinnatum has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties used primarily in traditional medicine to treat skin infections and respiratory ailments. What are the herbal preparations of Lygodium circinnatum?

Lygodium circinnatum is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste to utilize its medicinal properties. Practitioners often boil the dried aerial parts in water to create a concentrated liquid for internal consumption. In some traditional practices, the crushed plant material is mixed with a carrier to form a poultice applied directly to the skin. These various methods allow the active constituents of the fern to be effectively extracted and administered. The choice of preparation depends specifically on whether the ailment requires systemic treatment or localized relief.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Lygodium circinnaatum can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and localized allergic reactions upon direct physical contact with its tissues. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may act as irritants to sensitive human skin. Individuals handling the fern without protection might experience redness, itching, or swelling at the site of touch. There is also a potential risk of gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is accidentally ingested. Proper precautions should be taken to avoid unnecessary exposure to this species.
