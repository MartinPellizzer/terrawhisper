Prunus pseudocerasus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Prunus pseudocerasus has an upright, deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by slender, grayish-brown branches and elliptical, serrated leaves with finely toothed margins. The foliage typically grows in an alternate pattern along the stems, providing a dense canopy during the growing season. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in clusters from the axils to signal the onset of spring. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a deep red or black color when ripe. These fruits are often surrounded by a thin skin and contain a single hard stone at the center.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Prunus pseudocerasus can cause gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain if the seeds or unripe fruits are consumed in large quantities. The seeds of this plant contain cyanogenic glycosides which can release toxic hydrogen cyanide into the bloodstream upon ingestion. Symptoms of cyanide poisoning include dizziness, headaches, and rapid heart rate. In severe cases, high doses may lead to more critical complications like respiratory failure or seizures. Careful handling and proper identification are essential to avoid these potential adverse reactions.
