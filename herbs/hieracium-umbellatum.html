Hieracium umbellatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Hieracium umbellatum?

Hieracium umbellatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its progression through the order Asterales, the plant is placed into the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Hieracium and the species umbellatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hieracium umbellatum has a perennial growth habit characterized by a basal rosette of leaves and an umbel-like inflorescence of yellow flower heads. The flowering stalks are erect and can vary in height, supporting several composite heads arranged in a clustered manner. Each flower head consists of numerous small, bright yellow ligulate florets typical of the Asteraceae family.
