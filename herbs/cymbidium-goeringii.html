Cymbidium goeringii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium goeringii has narrow, linear, grass-like leaves that emerge from a creeping rhizome and produce fragrant, star-shaped flowers featuring a prominent, fleshy lip. The leaves are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 40 centimeters. During the flowering season, the plant produces upright, slender scapes that bear several delicate blooms. These flowers are characterized by their subtle colors, often ranging from pale green to soft pink or white. The labellum of each flower provides a distinct structural focal point for pollinators.

      Cymbidium goeringii is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the plant parts in water or as a concentrated tincture infused in alcohol.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cymbidium goeringii can cause allergic skin reactions or respiratory irritation if its pollen or sap comes into direct contact with sensitive individuals. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic to humans in typical indoor settings, accidental ingestion of its parts might lead to mild gastrointestinal discomfort. Some people may experience redness, itching, or swelling on the skin after handling the leaves or stems. There is limited scientific data regarding its systemic toxicity, so caution is advised for those with known botanical allergies. Monitoring for any unexpected physical responses is recommended when introducing this orchid to a new environment.
