Scirpus karuisawensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scirpus karuisawensis has slender, cylindrical, and solid stems that emerge from a creeping rhizome to form dense tufts. The leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures located primarily at the base of the plant. The inflorescence consists of small, brownish flower clusters arranged in a compact spike or panicle. Each flower is protected by a delicate, brownish bract that often extends beyond the length of the spike. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in the saturated, marshy environments it inhabits.
