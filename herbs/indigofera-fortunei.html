Indigofera fortunei

What's the taxonomical classification of Indigofera fortunei?

Indigofera fortunei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Fabales and belongs to the family Fabaceae, which is known for its significant role in nitrogen fixation. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed within the genus Indigofera, specifically identified as the species fortunei.

    What are the herbal preparations of Indigofera fortunei?

    Indigofera fortunei is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste depending on the specific therapeutic application required. The dried leaves and stems are most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. For dermatological issues, the plant material is often ground into a fine powder or mixed with water to form a thick paste for direct skin application. These preparations allow the medicinal compounds within the plant to be effectively absorbed by the body. Traditional practitioners select the specific form based on whether they aim to treat systemic ailments or localized skin conditions.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Indigofera fortunei can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's leaves or stems may trigger redness, itching, or swelling in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience an inflammatory response known as contact dermatitis upon touching the foliage. If the plant is consumed accidentally, it could lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. It is important to handle this species with care to avoid these adverse reactions.
