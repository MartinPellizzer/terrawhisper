Actinidia callosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Actinidia callosa?

Actinidia callosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Actinidiaceae, specifically belonging to the genus Actinidia, with the distinct species designation callosa.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Actinidia callosa targets various inflammatory mediators and enzymatic pathways, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and lipoxygenase (LOX) to suppress pro-inflammatory prostaglandin and leukotriene production. These interactions modulate the activity of nuclear factor kappa B (NF-κB), a key protein regulating the immune response and cellular inflammation. The plant also contains bioactive compounds that influence antioxidant enzymes such as superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase to mitigate oxidative stress. By interacting with these specific protein systems, the plant achieves its documented pharmacological effects in reducing tissue swelling and protecting cells from damage. These molecular mechanisms collectively contribute to its potential utility in treating inflammatory and oxidative-related conditions.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Actinidia callosa can cause allergic reactions such as skin rashes, itching, swelling, or irritation upon direct contact with the plant. Some individuals may experience localized dermatitis if they handle the stems or leaves without protection. If the plant is ingested, it might lead to digestive discomfort or gastrointestinal distress. The specific severity of these side effects often depends on an individual's unique sensitivity to the plant's chemical compounds. Monitoring for any signs of hypersensitivity is recommended when being in close proximity to this species.
