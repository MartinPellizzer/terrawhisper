Actinidia callosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Actinidia callosa?

Actinidia callosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Actinidiaceae, specifically belonging to the genus Actinidia, with the distinct species designation callosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Actinidia callosa has woody, climbing vines characterized by trifoliate leaves with ovate or broadly elliptical leaflets and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers.

The foliage typically features serrated margins and a soft, pubescent texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Its reproductive structures consist of small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a yellowish or brownish hue upon ripening. The stem structure is flexible, allowing the plant to scramble over other vegetation using adventitious roots for support.

These morphological traits are essential for its survival in dense, shaded forest environments.
