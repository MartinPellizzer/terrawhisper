Burmese Yam (Dioscorea Birmanica)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Dioscorea birmanica.

Burmese yam, scientifically known as Dioscorea birmanica, is a medicinal herb native to Southeast Asia, valued for its adaptogenic properties and traditional use in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine. It is rich in starch, saponins, and other bioactive compounds that contribute to its reputation as a natural energy booster and immune support supplement. Traditionally, Burmese yam has been used to treat digestive disorders, fatigue, and respiratory conditions, and it holds cultural significance in Myanmar and Thailand as a staple in both culinary and medicinal practices. In modern wellness, it is increasingly recognized for its potential to enhance vitality, support metabolic health, and aid in weight management due to its high nutrient density and low calorie content. One unique feature of this yam is its distinctive nutty flavor and the presence of diosgenin, a rare steroidal saponin with potential hormonal and anti-inflammatory benefits.
