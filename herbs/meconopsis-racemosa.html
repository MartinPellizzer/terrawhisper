What's the taxonomical classification of Meconopsis racemosa?

Meconopsis racemosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the biological lineage of the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Papaveraceae. At the most specific taxonomic levels, it is categorized under the genus Meconopsis, with its unique scientific designation being the species racemosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Meconopsis racemosa has large, deeply lobed, bluish-green leaves that reach up to 30 centimeters in length and a tall, upright stem that supports drooping clusters of bright blue, cup-shaped flowers. The foliage is characterized by a distinct glaucous coating that provides a silvery appearance to the textured, palmate blades. Each flowering stalk can grow significantly, often reaching heights between one and two meters, and produces multiple blossoms on long, pendulous racemes. The plant also features a robust root system that supports its substantial vegetative growth during the growing season.
