Ophiopogon heterandrus

Ophiopogon heterandrus has narrow, linear, dark green leaves that grow in dense tufts from a creeping rhizome. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often presenting in varying numbers per stalk as suggested by its specific epithet. These blossoms are typically white or pale blue and transition into small, dark blue or blackish berries upon maturation.
