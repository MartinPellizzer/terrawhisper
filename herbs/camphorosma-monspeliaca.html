What's the taxonomical classification of Camphorosma monspeliaca?

Camphorosma monspeliaca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, it falls under the order Caryophyllales and the family Amaranthaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Camphorosma, and its species name, monspeliaca.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Camphorosma monspeliaca can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and digestive distress if ingested. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may trigger localized inflammatory responses upon contact with sensitive skin. Individuals with known allergies to similar botanical species should exercise extreme caution when handling the foliage. Ingestion of the plant parts can lead to gastrointestinal upset such as nausea or abdominal discomfort. There is limited clinical data regarding long-term systemic toxicity, so avoidance is recommended for pregnant or nursing individuals.
