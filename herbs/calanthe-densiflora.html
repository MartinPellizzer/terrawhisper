Calanthe densiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calanthe densiflora has upright, fleshy stems that terminate in densely clustered, egg-shaped inflorescences composed of numerous small, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are elliptical to lanceolate in shape, featuring smooth margins and a deep green coloration that grows from the base of the plant. Each individual flower possesses a complex structure with a prominent, spurred lip characteristic of the orchid family. The roots are thick and succulent, allowing the plant to absorb moisture efficiently from its forest floor habitat. These morphological features combine to create a compact and robust growth habit within its natural environment.
