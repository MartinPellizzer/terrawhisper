Indigofera cassioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Indigofera cassioides?

Indigofera cassioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which is further refined into the genus Indigofera. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, cassioides.

What are the pharmacological activities of Indigofera cassioides?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-tubercular, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that extracts from Indigofera cassioides demonstrate the ability to inhibit the growth of Mycobacterium tuberculosis. These bioactive compounds also work to combat various bacterial and fungal pathogens found in different environments. Additionally, the plant exhibits potential in reducing inflammation through its unique chemical constituents. Such diverse medicinal qualities make it a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic treatments.

As discussed in a study, the leaves of Indigofera cassioides Rottl.ex DC. contain secondary metabolites including saponins, steroids, anthro-quinones, terpinods, flavonoids and phlabotanins. Non-polar and polar solvent extracts of these leaves were tested against M. tuberculosis H37RV to determine minimum inhibitory concentrations (MICs) using an agar based proportion assay. The crude leaf extracts showed a MIC value of 50 μg/ml compared to the standard drug Isoniazid value of 0.025 μg/ml. These results indicate that the crude extracts of the legume leaves exhibit potential anti-tubercular activity against M. tuberculosis. Further work is required to identify the active molecule to develop a novel anti-tubercular drug.
