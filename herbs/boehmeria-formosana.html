Boehmeria formosana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Boehmeria formosana has an upright, herbaceous habit characterized by oppositely arranged, ovate leaves with serrated margins and long petioles. The stems are typically slender and may exhibit fine pubescence depending on the specific environmental conditions. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary or terminal clusters. The plant often features a branched structure that supports its spreading foliage. These morphological traits are characteristic of its classification within the Urticaceae family.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Boehmeria formosana can cause skin irritation, redness, and localized itching due to the presence of stinging hairs on its foliage. These tiny trichomes act like needles that inject irritating substances directly into the epidermis upon contact. Once triggered, the skin may exhibit small bumps or a burning sensation that lasts for a short period. Individuals with sensitive skin or known allergies to similar plant species may experience more intense inflammatory responses. It is important to avoid touching the leaves to prevent these uncomfortable dermatological reactions.
