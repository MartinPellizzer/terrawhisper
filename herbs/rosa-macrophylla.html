Rosa macrophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa macrophylla?

Rosa macrophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is a member of the order Rosales. This plant is further categorized into the family Rosaceae, within the genus Rosa, and is specifically identified by its species name, macrophyllas.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Rosa macrophylla contains high concentrations of polyphenols, flavonoids, vitamin C, and essential oils within its petals and hips. These bioactive compounds act as powerful antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals in the body. The presence of organic acids and tannins contributes to its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. When processed into extracts or teas, these chemical constituents may support immune function and skin health. Researchers often study these specific phytochemical profiles to understand their therapeutic value in traditional medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Rosa macrophylla?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin inflammation, wounds, gastrointestinal issues, and various digestive ailments. The leaves and petals contain bioactive compounds that possess antiseptic and soothing properties for topical application. When prepared as a tea or decoction, it can help alleviate symptoms of stomach discomfort and indigestion. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to reduce fever and manage minor infections. Its medicinal versatility makes it a significant component in various herbal remedies.
