Rosa macrophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa macrophylla?

Rosa macrophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is a member of the order Rosales. This plant is further categorized into the family Rosaceae, within the genus Rosa, and is specifically identified by its species name, macrophyllas.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa macrophylla has large, broadly ovate to elliptical leaflets that typically measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and possess serrated margins. The compound leaves are generally pinnate with an odd number of leaflets arranged along a central petiole. Each leaf stalk is often armed with small prickles or glandular hairs that provide a distinct texture. The plant produces showy, solitary flowers that feature five spreading petals and a central cluster of numerous stamens. Its stems are woody and frequently covered in sharp, curved prickles to protect the climbing or arching growth habit.

The species is often found in moist, well-drained soils where sunlight is filtered through a forest canopy.
