What is Podocarpus excelsus?

Podocarpus excelsus is a species of evergreen tree belonging to the family Podocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical forests of southern Africa, specifically in South Africa, Lesotho, and Swaziland. Botanically, it is characterized by its conical shape, straight trunk, and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The tree grows up to 30 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and needle-like leaves that are 1-3 centimeters long and arranged in two rows along the branch.

The leaves have a distinctive blue-green color and are highly aromatic, with a distinctive scent when crushed.

The main herbal preparations of Podocarpus excelsus are infusion, tincture, tea, salve and decoction.
