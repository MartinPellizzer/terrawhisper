Zanthoxylum dimorphophyllum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zanthoxylum dimorphophyllum has leaves characterized by a distinct dimorphism where some leaflets are deeply lobed while others remain entire or only slightly serrated. The plant typically features pinnately compound leaves that vary in shape depending on their position on the stem. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the branches and possess a leathery texture. The leaflets often exhibit an acute apex and a finely serrated margin along the edges. Small, inconspicuous flowers eventually give rise to drupaceous fruits that contribute to the overall morphological profile of the species.
