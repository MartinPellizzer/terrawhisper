Zanthoxylum dimorphophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum dimorphophyllum?

Zanthoxylum dimorphophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical classification through the order Sapindales, it is placed within the family Rutaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Zanthoxylum and is specifically distinguished as the species dimorphophyllum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Zanthoxylum dimorphophyllum contains various bioactive alkaloids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its traditional medicinal properties. These chemical constituents are primarily utilized for their antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic effects in folk medicine. Research into the plant's secondary metabolites suggests potential applications in treating skin infections and digestive ailments. Specific isolated compounds may also interact with biological pathways to mitigate oxidative stress within cellular structures. Further pharmacological investigation is necessary to fully characterize the therapeutic potential of these complex chemical profiles.

What are the therapeutic actions of Zanthoxylum dimorphophyllum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat stomach aches, indigestion, diarrhea, and various types of intestinal worms. Local healers often prepare decoctions from the bark or leaves to soothe gastrointestinal distress and promote digestion. In some traditional practices, the plant is also applied to address skin conditions or minor wounds. The therapeutic properties are primarily derived from the bioactive compounds found within its woody parts. These applications reflect its significant role in folk medicine for managing digestive and dermatological ailments.
