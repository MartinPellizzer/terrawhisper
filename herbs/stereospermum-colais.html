Stereospermum colais

What's the taxonomical classification of Stereospermum colais?

Stereosperma colais belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage that leads to the order Lamiales. Specifically, this plant is situated within the family Bignoniaceae, which falls under the genus Stereosperma, and is identified at the species level as Stereosperma colais.

    What are the herbal preparations of Stereospermum colais?

    Stereospermum colais is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a powder derived from its dried bark and fruits. The bark is frequently boiled in water to extract active compounds for medicinal use. Practitioners often grind the dried plant parts into a fine powder to be mixed with honey or water. Some traditional applications involve creating a topical paste using the crushed leaves for external treatments. These diverse preparation methods allow for different routes of administration depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Stereospermum colais can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically without proper preparation. While used in traditional medicine, its bioactive compounds may trigger nausea or stomach cramps in sensitive individuals. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves might result in localized redness or itching on the skin. There is also a potential for systemic toxicity if the dosage is not strictly controlled. Detailed clinical studies on human side effects remain limited, so caution is necessary when using this species.
