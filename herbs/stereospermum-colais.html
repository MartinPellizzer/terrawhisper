Stereospermum colais

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stereospermum colais has pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of leaflets that exhibit an elliptical to oblong shape and an entire margin. The woody stems are characterized by a rough texture and a distinct grayish bark that protects the inner tissues. Its flowers are large, tubular, and zygomorphic, often displaying pale colors with delicate spotting on the inner lobes. The fruit develops into a long, slender, woody capsule that eventually splits open to release winged seeds. These seeds are specifically adapted for wind dispersal through their thin, papery extensions.

    What are the herbal preparations of Stereospermum colais?

    Stereospermum colais is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a powder derived from its dried bark and fruits. The bark is frequently boiled in water to extract active compounds for medicinal use. Practitioners often grind the dried plant parts into a fine powder to be mixed with honey or water.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Stereospermum colais can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically without proper preparation. While used in traditional medicine, its bioactive compounds may trigger nausea or stomach cramps in sensitive individuals. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves might result in localized redness or itching on the skin. There is also a potential for systemic toxicity if the dosage is not strictly controlled. Detailed clinical studies on human side effects remain limited, so caution is necessary when using this species.
