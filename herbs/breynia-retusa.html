Breynia retusa

What's the taxonomical classification of Breynia retusa?

Breynia retusa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The species is further categorized under the order Malpighiales and within the family Phyllanthaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Breynia, and its specific species epithet, retusa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Breynia retusa has small, opposite, obovate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 millimeters long with obtuse or slightly emarginate apices and entire margins. The plant is a shrubby species characterized by slender, woody stems that often exhibit a branching habit. Its inflorescences are small and inconspicuous, bearing tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically unisexual. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that develops from the fertilized female flowers. These morphological features collectively distinguish it from other members of the Phyllanthaceae family.

What are the pharmacological activities of Breynia retusa?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-proliferative, anticancer, antioxidant, and cytotoxic pharmacological activities. Its phytochemical constituents work together to neutralize harmful free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. The bioactive compounds also demonstrate significant cytotoxic effects by inducing programmed cell death in various malignant cell lines. Furthermore, the plant exhibits anti-proliferative properties that help inhibit the rapid growth and spread of abnormal cells. These combined mechanisms make Breynia retusa a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents against inflammation and cancer.

As discussed in a study, Breynia retusa methanolic extract demonstrates exuberant antioxidant activity with an 81.91% scavenging ability of DPPH radicals and an antioxidant capacity of 22.98 mg ascorbic acid equivalents/g extract. This extract acts as an important anti-inflammatory agent through a membrane stabilization inhibition of 83%. Regarding anti-proliferative potential, the cytotoxicity study against the SKOV-3 cell line indicated an IC50 value of 34.01 µg/mL, while showing very negligible neurotoxicity in SH-SY5Y cell lines. Molecular docking revealed that secalciferol molecules interact with IGF1 at -6.758 kcal/mol and c-ERBB2 at -4.281 kcal/mol. Additionally, the bio-accessibility of phenolic compounds and their antioxidant activity ranged above 90%, indicating promising bioavailability.
