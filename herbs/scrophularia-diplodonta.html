Scrophularia diplodonta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scrophularia diplodonta has upright, square-shaped stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by distinct venation and a slightly textured surface. Its flowers are tubular and bilabiate, typically displaying a yellowish or brownish hue with intricate spotting. These blossoms emerge from axillary clusters and are supported by short pedicels. The plant produces small, dry capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds upon maturity.

      What are the herbal preparations of Scrophularia diplodonta?

      Scrophularia diplodonta is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice derived from its dried aerial parts. These liquid preparations are often used in traditional medicinal practices to extract specific alkaloids and flavonoids.
