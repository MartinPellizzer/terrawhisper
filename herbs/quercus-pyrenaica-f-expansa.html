Quercus pyrenaica f. expansa

Quercus pyrenaica f. expansa, also known as the Pyrenean oak, is a subspecies of the Pyrenean white oak tree, characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and sturdy branches.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which can be attributed to its rich content of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

The health benefits of Quercus pyrenaica f. expansa include reducing inflammation, improving cardiovascular health, and supporting the immune system, making it a valuable herbal remedy for various health conditions.

Herbal preparations of this plant can be made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, and can be consumed to promote overall well-being and alleviate symptoms of various diseases.
