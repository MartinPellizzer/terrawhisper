Clinopodium urticifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clinopodium urticifolium has opposite, ovate-lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a square-shaped stem characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a slightly textured surface due to fine pubescence. Its flowering structure consists of small, tubular flowers that emerge from axillary clusters. These blossoms often exhibit a pale lavender or white coloration depending on the specific variety. The plant maintains a compact, herbaceous growth habit that reaches moderate heights.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountain regions of Central and Southern Europe, ranging from the Alps to the Carpathians and the Balkans. It typically thrives in subalpine and alpine zones where rocky terrain and high elevations are present. Its distribution is often concentrated in specific habitats such as limestone screes and grassy slopes. Many populations are found at altitudes between 1,500 and 2,500 meters above sea level. The species exhibits a fragmented pattern due to its preference for specialized mountain microclimates.
