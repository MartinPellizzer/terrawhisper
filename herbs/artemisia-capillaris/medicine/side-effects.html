10 Possible Side Effects Of Artemisia Capillaris

Artemisia capillaris has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, sensitivity to the herb's compounds, or combined use with other herbs or medications.

In severe cases, these side effects can worsen people's lives by causing chronic pain, digestive problems, and anxiety.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Artemisia capillaris causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The compound nepetalactone, responsible for its insect-repelling properties, is thought to trigger these reactions. This allergenic response can manifest as skin irritation, respiratory issues, or even anaphylaxis.

Additionally, the volatile oils present in Artemisia capillaris may also contribute to allergic symptoms, leading to an adverse reaction in susceptible individuals.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Artemisia capillaris triggers asthma attacks due to its inherent compounds, particularly sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids.

These bioactive molecules can stimulate the immune system, leading to increased production of cytokines and prostaglandins, which in turn exacerbate bronchial constriction and inflammation characteristic of asthma.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may also cause allergic reactions, further contributing to asthma symptoms.

3. Induces stomach ulcers

Artemisia capillaris induces stomach ulcers due to its high concentration of thujone, a toxic compound that can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract.

Additionally, Artemisia capillaris contains volatile oils that may exacerbate acid reflux and further contribute to the development of stomach ulcers.

4. Increases bleeding risk

Artemisia capillaris increases bleeding risk due to its ability to interact with blood clotting medications and thin the blood.

Its high content of vitamin K and flavonoids can enhance the effects of anticoagulant drugs, making it more likely for users to experience excessive bleeding.

Additionally, wormwood's anti-inflammatory properties may also contribute to increased bleeding risk by reducing platelet aggregation and promoting a more fluid blood state.

5. Causes kidney damage

Artemisia capillaris causes kidney damage due to its high concentration of sesquiterpenoids and flavonoids, which can increase urine production and alter the kidneys' ability to concentrate and filter waste.

The plant's antioxidant properties may also exacerbate existing kidney issues by reducing the effectiveness of natural defense mechanisms in the kidneys.

As a result, long-term consumption of Artemisia capillaris may lead to chronic kidney damage or worsening of pre-existing conditions.

6. Produces hallucinations

Artemisia capillaris produces hallucinations when used.

This occurs due to its high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones, particularly artemisinin and artabsin, which can affect the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to altered perceptions, distorted thoughts, and hallucinations.

The plant's volatile oils may also play a role in this effect, as they can interact with the brain's chemistry to produce changes in cognitive function and sensory perception.

7. Enhances sedative effect

Artemisia capillaris enhances sedative effect due to its active compounds, including sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, which can interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, such as GABA, serotonin, and dopamine.

These interactions can lead to feelings of relaxation and calmness, potentially causing excessive drowsiness or sleepiness.

As a result, users may experience a heightened sedative effect, impacting their daily activities and overall functionality.

8. Interferes with medications

Artemisia capillaris interferes with medications due to its ability to alter the metabolism of certain drugs.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, may inhibit the activity of liver enzymes responsible for breaking down medications, leading to increased levels of these substances in the blood.

This interaction can result in unpredictable effects or adverse reactions when taking Artemisia capillaris in conjunction with prescribed medications.

9. Affects blood sugar levels

Artemisia capillaris affects blood sugar levels due to its potential to lower insulin sensitivity and reduce glucose absorption in the gut.

The herb's flavonoids and terpenes may interact with diabetes medications, altering their effectiveness and causing fluctuations in blood sugar levels.

Additionally, Artemisia capillaris has been shown to stimulate the release of glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1), a hormone that regulates glucose metabolism, further contributing to its impact on blood sugar levels.

10. Lowers blood pressure

Artemisia capillaris lowers blood pressure due to its ability to cause a decrease in peripheral vascular resistance and cardiac output.

This is achieved through the herb's effect on the autonomic nervous system, specifically by activating alpha-adrenergic receptors that constrict blood vessels and slow heart rate.

Additionally, Artemisia capillaris may also inhibit the release of certain neurotransmitters that regulate blood pressure, leading to a further decline in blood pressure levels.
