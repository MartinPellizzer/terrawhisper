Poinsettia cyathophora var. graminifolia has many herbal preparations, such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are used to treat various health conditions.

Tinctures, on the other hand, are concentrated extracts that can be used to treat pain, inflammation, and fever, and are often added to water or taken sublingually.

Possible Side Effects of Poinsettia cyathophora var. graminifolia

Poinsettia cyathophora var. graminifolia can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive problems due to its sap.

Ingesting the plant's leaves or flowers may cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea in some individuals.

The sap of the plant can also cause skin redness, itching, and swelling, particularly for those with sensitive skin.

If ingested or applied topically in large quantities, the plant may lead to more severe side effects, including respiratory problems and cardiovascular issues.
