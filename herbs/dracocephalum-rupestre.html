Dracocephalum rupestre

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum rupestre has a perennial growth habit characterized by upright, square-shaped stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate, while the upper cauline leaves are smaller, opposite, and often possess serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal verticillasters that bear small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The calyx is tubular and features distinct teeth that protect the developing seeds. This plant often develops a woody base over time, allowing it to anchor firmly in rocky substrates.
