Dracocephalum rupestre

What's the taxonomical classification of Dracocephalum rupestre?

Dracocephalum rupestre belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Lamiales and falls within the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Dracocephalum and is specifically designated as the species rupestre.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum rupestre has a perennial growth habit characterized by upright, square-shaped stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate, while the upper cauline leaves are smaller, opposite, and often possess serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal verticillasters that bear small, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The calyx is tubular and features distinct teeth that protect the developing seeds.

This plant often develops a woody base over time, allowing it to anchor firmly in rocky substrates.
