Pteris henryi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris henryi has pinnate fronds that measure up to 60 centimeters in length and feature light green, oblong pinnae with slightly wavy margins. The stipe is typically dark brown or blackish and possesses a distinctively smooth texture. Each pinna is arranged symmetrically along the central rachis, which remains relatively slender throughout the growth cycle. Small, kidney-shaped sori are located on the underside of the leaflets near the margins to facilitate spore production. The overall texture of the foliage is somewhat thin and herbaceous rather than leathery.
