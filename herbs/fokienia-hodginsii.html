Fokienia hodginsii

What's the taxonomical classification of Fokienia hodginsii?

Fokienia hodginsii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales. This coniferous species is a member of the family Cupressaceae, falling under the genus Fokienia, and is specifically identified by its species name, hodginsii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fokienia hodginsii has scale-like, overlapping leaves that are arranged in four distinct ranks along its spreading, woody branches.

These small, green foliage scales are sessile and tightly pressed against the stems to create a compact appearance. The plant typically exhibits a low-growing, creeping growth habit with branches that may trail along the ground or ascend slightly. Its stems are characterized by a textured bark that develops as the plant matures into a perennial shrub.

The overall morphology is adapted for moist, shaded forest environments where its dense structure helps retain humidity.
