What is Cactus gracilis?

Cactus gracilis is a species of cactus in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to Mexico and Guatemala, typically growing in the wild on limestone and rocky outcrops. This cactus has a cylindrical to columnar stem with 7 to 11 prominent ribs, bearing short, dense, and yellowish spines. Its flowers are yellow with red tips and produce red to yellow fruits, containing numerous seeds.

Cactus gracilis grows up to 1 meter in height and 10 centimeters in diameter.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Cactus gracilis are cactus juice, cactus tincture, cactus tea, infusion, and cactus salve, which are derived from its stems and roots.

Cactus juice is obtained by squeezing the juice from the cactus stems, rich in vitamins and minerals, and is used to treat digestive issues and skin problems. Cactus tincture is a concentrated liquid extract of the cactus, often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties and to treat wounds and skin conditions. Cactus tea and infusion are prepared by steeping the dried cactus stems in hot water, and are traditionally used to treat fever, sore throats, and respiratory issues.

Cactus salve is a topical preparation made from the cactus extract, often used to treat skin irritations, wounds, and burns, due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
