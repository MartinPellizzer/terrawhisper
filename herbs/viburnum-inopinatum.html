Viburnum inopinatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum inopinatum?

Viburnum inopinatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Dipsacales and is a member of the family Viburnaceae. Finally, this plant is categorized under the genus Viburnum, with its specific designation being the species inopinatum.

What are the therapeutic actions of Viburnum inopinatum?

Viburnum inopinatum has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that contribute to its potential therapeutic uses in treating skin irritations and promoting cellular health. The plant contains various bioactive compounds such as polyphenols and flavonoids that help neutralize free radicals in the body. These chemical constituents work together to reduce oxidative stress and mitigate inflammatory responses in damaged tissues. Additionally, its natural antimicrobial effects can assist in inhibiting the growth of certain pathogens on the skin surface. Such multifaceted actions make it a subject of interest for developing natural topical treatments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various gastrointestinal ailments, specifically diarrhea, dysentery, and abdominal pain. The medicinal properties are primarily derived from the bark and roots of the specimen. These parts contain tannins that act as astringents to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. Traditional practitioners often prepare decoctions to soothe stomach cramps and stabilize bowel movements. This botanical application has been documented in several historical herbalist practices.
