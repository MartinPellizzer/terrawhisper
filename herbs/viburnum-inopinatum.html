Viburnum inopinatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum inopinatum has oppositely arranged, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately five to ten centimeters in length and feature serrated margins with prominent pinnate venation. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color during the growing season, often transitioning to warmer hues in autumn. Its woody stems are characterized by a smooth texture in younger growth that becomes increasingly furrowed as the shrub matures. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in dense, flat-topped cymes during the late spring or early summer. These floral clusters eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that serve as a primary feature of the plant's reproductive morphology.
