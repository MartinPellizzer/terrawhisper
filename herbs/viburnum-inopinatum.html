Viburnum inopinatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum inopinatum?

Viburnum inopinatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Dipsacales and is a member of the family Viburnaceae. Finally, this plant is categorized under the genus Viburnum, with its specific designation being the species inopinatum.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Viburnum inopinatum can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and allergic reactions if the plant is ingested or touched. While there is limited clinical data regarding human toxicity, many members of the Viburnum genus contain glycosides that may induce nausea or vomiting. Direct contact with the leaves or berries might trigger contact dermatitis in individuals with sensitive skin. It is important to avoid consuming any part of the plant to prevent potential poisoning. Always consult a medical professional if accidental ingestion occurs.
