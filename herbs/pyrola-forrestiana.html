Pyrola forrestiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrola forrestiana?

Pyrola forrestiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Ericales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Ericaceae, falling under the genus Pyrola, and is specifically identified by its species name, forrestiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrola forrestiana has an erect, perennial herb morphology characterized by slender, reddish stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are oppositely arranged along the stem and possess an elliptical to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring delicate white or pale pink petals that are often marked with subtle spots. The reproductive structures include a superior ovary and a thin pedicel that supports the developing fruit. This plant's overall structure is adapted to the moist, shaded forest environments it inhabits.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating hypoxia, sepsis, ventricular fibrillation, and weak cardiac muscular contraction. Its medicinal properties help restore oxygen levels in tissues when breathing is impaired. The plant also demonstrates significant potential in managing systemic infections and septic conditions. Furthermore, it plays a critical role in stabilizing irregular heart rhythms to prevent fatal cardiac events. By strengthening the heart, it effectively addresses instances of diminished cardiac muscle strength.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", the water extracts of P. forrestiana demonstrate specific pharmacological actions. The results show that the actions of P. forrestiana on antiventricular fibrillation, strengthening cardiac muscular contraction, and antihypoxia ability are more stronger.
