Possible Side Effects of hamamelis virginiana

Hamamelis virginiana can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with other medications.

Taking excessive amounts of the herb can cause stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea.

Additionally, individuals with bleeding disorders or those taking anticoagulant medications should avoid using hamamelis virginiana due to its potential to increase bleeding risk.

Furthermore, applying hamamelis virginiana topically to the skin may cause irritation, redness, or an allergic contact dermatitis in some individuals.
