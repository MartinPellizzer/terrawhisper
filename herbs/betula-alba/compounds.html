White Birch (Betula alba) Compounds

The primary compounds of White Birch include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to its astringent effects. Phenolic acids and coumarin provide anti-inflammatory benefits, while essential oils and saponins support its traditional use in herbal medicine. Glycosides, terpenoids, and alkaloids also play roles in its therapeutic applications. Anthocyanins add to its antioxidant profile and contribute to its natural color.

White Birch (Betula alba) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in White Birch (Betula alba) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function. They are found in the bark and sap of the plant. Flavonoids may enhance the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and skin conditions.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in White Birch (Betula alba). It contributes to the bark's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Tannins help in treating skin conditions and digestive issues, making them an important component of White Birch's therapeutic profile.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in White Birch (Betula alba). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Found in the bark and sap, it supports traditional uses for skin conditions and respiratory health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of White Birch in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in White Birch (Betula alba). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. Coumarin is found in the bark and sap of the tree. It has been used traditionally to support circulation and reduce swelling.

5. Essential oil

The essential oil of White Birch (Betula alba) contains volatile compounds like limonene and terpenes. These contribute to its aromatic properties and may support respiratory and skin health. It is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in White Birch (Betula alba). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may support immune function and skin health, making them valuable in herbal formulations.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in White Birch (Betula alba). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Glycosides in White Birch may have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. They are often extracted from the bark and used in herbal formulations.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in White Birch (Betula alba) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory infections. They are also involved in the plant's defense mechanisms and may enhance its therapeutic potential.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in White Birch (Betula alba). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its specific role and concentration may vary. 10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in White Birch (Betula alba), is a flavonoid responsible for the tree's vibrant autumn colors. It exhibits strong antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports overall health by enhancing immune function and protecting cells from damage. Its presence in White Birch highlights the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.
