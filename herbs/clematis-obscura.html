Clematis obscura

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis obscura?

Clematis obscura belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, it is placed in the genus Clematis, with its specific designation being the species obscura.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis obscura has woody, climbing stems that utilize petioles to twine around supporting vegetation, accompanied by opposite, trifoliate leaves with ovate leaflets that possess entire or slightly wavy margins. The foliage typically displays a dark green upper surface and a paler underside, providing a dense textural appearance to the climbing vine. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge in clusters, characterized by inconspicuous, pale greenish-white or yellowish tepals rather than showy petals. These flowers are followed by distinctive achenes that feature long, feathery styles, allowing the seeds to be easily dispersed by the wind. The overall growth habit is that of a vigorous perennial herb that can form extensive mats or climb significantly in shaded forest environments.
