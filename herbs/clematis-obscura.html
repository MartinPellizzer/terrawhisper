Clematis obscura

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis obscura?

Clematis obscura belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, it is placed in the genus Clematis, with its specific designation being the species obscura.

      What are the herbal preparations of Clematis obscura?

      Clematis obscura is prepared primarily as a liquid tincture or a decoction of the aerial parts to extract its active compounds. Traditional herbalists often use the dried stems and leaves to create infused oils for topical applications. These decoctions are typically simmered in water to concentrate the plant's essence for medicinal use. Tinctures offer a more shelf-stable method of delivery by utilizing alcohol as a solvent. Practitioners may also incorporate the plant into tea preparations, though careful dosage is essential due to its potency.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis obscura can cause severe skin irritation, vomiting, and abdominal pain if ingested or touched. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that acts as a potent irritant to mucous membranes and skin. Contact with the leaves or stems often leads to redness, itching, or blistering on the affected area. If any part of the plant is swallowed, it can provoke intense gastrointestinal distress and swelling of the throat. Such reactions can be particularly dangerous if the person has a heightened sensitivity to the species.
