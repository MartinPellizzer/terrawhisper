Rinorea subintegrifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rinorea subintegrifolia has simple, elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically subentire along the margins, meaning the edges are nearly smooth but may possess very slight indentations. The leaf arrangement is opposite, with each petiole supporting a blade that features prominent pinnate venation. The plant exhibits a woody structure characteristic of its genus, often presenting as a shrub or small tree. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced, which are typically borne in clusters or along the stems. The overall morphology is defined by these compact, leathery foliage characteristics and a streamlined growth habit.
