Meehania fargesii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Meehania fargesii has decumbent to erect stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters and bear opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a slightly pubescent texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular, violet to purplish flowers that are arranged in terminal cymes. Each flower features a distinct two-lipped corolla structure characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. These delicate blooms emerge from the axils of the upper leaves to attract specific pollinators during the growing season.

      What are the herbal preparations of Meehania fargesii?

      Meehania fargesii is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried leaves and stems in water to extract its medicinal compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Meehania fargesii can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, itching, and redness if the plant material touches sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience localized swelling or irritation following physical contact with the leaves or stems. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented in human literature, ingestion should be avoided to prevent unknown gastrointestinal distress. Sensitivities can vary significantly depending on a person's individual immune response to the plant's chemical compounds. Always use caution and wear gloves when handling this species to minimize the risk of an adverse reaction.
