Sakhalin Angelica (Angelica Sachalinensis F. Saninensis)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Angelica sachalinensis f. saninensis.

Sakhalin angelica, scientifically known as Angelica sachalinensis f. saninensis, is a rare medicinal herb native to the Russian Far East, particularly the island of Sakhalin. This adaptogen is valued for its rich content of bioactive compounds, including volatile oils, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its wide range of therapeutic properties. Traditionally used in Siberian and Far Eastern medicine, it was employed to treat digestive issues, respiratory ailments, and as a general tonic to enhance vitality and immunity. In modern wellness practices, it is increasingly recognized for its potential to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and aid in stress management. Its unique feature is the presence of a rare active compound called saninene, which gives it a distinct aroma and enhances its medicinal potency, making it a valuable herb in both traditional and contemporary herbal medicine.
