Neocinnamomum mekongense

What's the taxonomical classification of Neocinnamomum mekongense?

Neocinnamomum mekongense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and follows the order Laurales. This plant is a member of the family Lauraceae, falling under the specific genus Neocinnamomum, and is identified by its species name, mekongense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Neocinnamomum mekongense has woody stems, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles.

The leaves are typically arranged in an opposite pattern and exhibit a coriaceous texture. Its reproductive structures consist of small, fleshy fruits that develop from the flowering panicles. The plant's overall architecture reflects its classification within the Lauraceae family through its characteristic leaf venation and bark structure.

These morphological traits are essential for distinguishing the species from its close relatives in the Mekong region.

        Neocinnamomum mekongense is prepared as a decoction, a topical paste, or an infusion depending on the specific therapeutic application intended.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Neocinnamomum mekongense can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Although specific clinical trials on humans are limited, the chemical constituents found in the Lauraceae family often trigger hypersensitivity reactions in sensitive individuals. Contact with the plant's oils or sap may lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on the dermis. If consumed, the bioactive compounds could potentially disrupt digestive processes or cause nausea. Individuals with known allergies to cinnamon or related botanical species should exercise extreme caution when handling this plant.
