Liparis petiolata

What's the taxonomical classification of Liparis petiolata?

Liparis petiolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Asparagales and the family Orchidaceae, which characterizes it as a member of the orchid family. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Liparis, and its unique species designation, petiolata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Liparis petiolata has an upright, fleshy growth habit characterized by several thin, lanceolate leaves arranged in a distichous pattern along a slender stem. The leaves are typically bright green and possess a smooth, somewhat succulent texture. Small, greenish-yellow flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in loose, elongated racemes. Each individual bloom features a prominent, fleshy lip that is slightly larger than the other tepals.
