Liparis petiolata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Liparis petiolata has an upright, fleshy growth habit characterized by several thin, lanceolate leaves arranged in a distichous pattern along a slender stem. The leaves are typically bright green and possess a smooth, somewhat succulent texture. Small, greenish-yellow flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in loose, elongated racemes. Each individual bloom features a prominent, fleshy lip that is slightly larger than the other tepals. The plant's overall structure is delicate and adapted to the humid, shaded environments of its natural forest habitat.
