Ophiopogon platyphyllus

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiopogon platyphyllus?

Ophiopogon platyphyllus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Asparagales and the family Asparagaceae, eventually narrowing down to the genus Ophiopogon. Finally, it is identified at the species level as platyphyllus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophiopogon platyphyllus has narrow, linear leaves that form dense tufts and small, globose fruits that transition from green to black upon maturity. The foliage typically measures between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and possesses a dark green color with a slightly leathery texture. Its flowering stems arise from the base, bearing small, inconspicuous white or pale lilac flowers. These flowers eventually give way to fleshy berries that are often held on short pedicels. The overall growth habit is clump-forming, creating a low-growing groundcover effect through its fibrous root system.
