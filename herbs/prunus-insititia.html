What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause significant gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, if the seeds or pits are ingested due to their cyanide-containing compounds. The presence of cyanogenic glycosides in the seeds allows for the release of hydrogen cyanide during digestion. Consuming even small amounts of these pits may lead to symptoms such as dizziness, headaches, or rapid heartbeat. In severe cases of poisoning, an individual might experience respiratory failure or loss of consciousness. It is important to avoid consuming any part of the fruit that contains the hard stone.
