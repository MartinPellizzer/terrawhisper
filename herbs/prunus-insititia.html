What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Prunus insititia has spreading or erect woody stems characterized by greyish bark and small, simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical with finely serrated margins. The foliage is often dark green on the upper surface and slightly paler underneath, providing a dense canopy. During the flowering season, the plant produces clusters of white or pale pink blossoms that emerge from short spurs. These flowers possess five petals and a central cluster of numerous stamens, which are typical of the Rosaceae family. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns dark purple or black when fully ripe.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause significant gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, if the seeds or pits are ingested due to their cyanide-containing compounds. The presence of cyanogenic glycosides in the seeds allows for the release of hydrogen cyanide during digestion. Consuming even small amounts of these pits may lead to symptoms such as dizziness, headaches, or rapid heartbeat. In severe cases of poisoning, an individual might experience respiratory failure or loss of consciousness. It is important to avoid consuming any part of the fruit that contains the hard stone.
