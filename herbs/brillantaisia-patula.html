Brillantaisia patula

What's the taxonomical classification of Brillantaisia patula?

Brillantaisia patula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Acanthaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Brillantaisia, with the specific species identified as patula.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Brillantaisia patula has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a slightly pubescent texture on both surfaces. The stems are typically erect and can reach heights of up to one meter in length. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils and are characterized by their bright yellow or pale cream coloration. The plant produces small, globose fruit capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. These morphological features help distinguish the species within the Acanthaceae family.

What are the pharmacological activities of Brillantaisia patula?

This plant has antibacterial, antifungal, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. The antibacterial properties allow it to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria, making it a subject of interest in medicinal research. Its antioxidant capacity helps neutralize harmful free radicals within biological systems. Additionally, the plant exhibits significant anti-inflammatory effects that can reduce swelling and pain. These diverse biological actions highlight its potential therapeutic utility in treating multiple conditions.

According to a study published by "Heliyon", silver nanoparticles were synthesized using aqueous leaf extracts from Brillantaisia patula. These synthesized nanoparticles were spherical shaped and well-dispersed with average sizes ranging from 45 to 110 nm. The resulting AgNPs derived from Brillantaisia patula exhibited higher antibacterial activity against 3 bacterial pathogens of the human skin compared to the crude extract and AgNO3. This indicates that the biomolecules covering the nanoparticles may enhance the biological activity of metal nanoparticles. Such findings support that the biogenic synthesis of AgNPs from this plant constitutes a potential area of interest for the therapeutic management of microbial diseases such as infectious skin diseases.
