Botanical Identification of Lithospermum (Lithospermum officinale)

To identify Lithospermum (Lithospermum officinale) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Lithospermum officinale belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Boraginaceae, genus Lithospermum, and species Lithospermum officinale. This classification places it within the flowering plants and highlights its botanical relationships within the Boraginaceae family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Lithospermum officinale is the scientific name for the plant commonly known as Lithospermum. It was first described by the botanist Linnaeus in 1753. The species has several synonyms, including Lithospermum roseum and Lithospermum erythrorhizon, which reflect historical taxonomic changes.

Morphological Characteristics

Lithospermum officinale is an upright perennial herb with a taproot system. It has hairy, branched stems that grow up to 60 cm tall. The leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and hairy, with serrated edges. The plant produces spikes of small, pink to red flowers, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Lithospermum officinale can be distinguished by its lanceolate, dark green leaves with prominent veins, showy red-orange flowers in terminal clusters, and dark purple to black elliptical berries. It differs from similar species like Lithospermum erythrorhizon by its leaf shape and flower color. The presence of a distinct, waxy bloom on the fruit also helps in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Lithospermum officinale is native to regions in Europe, western Asia, and parts of North Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 500 and 2000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Lithospermum officinale blooms from June to August. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during the flowering period for accurate species recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Lithospermum officinale is closely related to species like Lithospermum erythrorhizon. It shares characteristics with other members of the Boraginaceae family, such as hairy stems and blue or purple flowers. Its genus relations highlight its position within the broader context of medicinal plants. Family traits include a distinctive root system and specific leaf morphology.
