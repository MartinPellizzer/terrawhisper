Lithospermum (Lithospermum officinale) Compounds

The primary compounds of Lithospermum officinale include tannins, which have astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties, while glycosides and alkaloids support its medicinal uses. Coumarin, terpenoids, saponins, essential oils, and anthocyanins also play roles in its therapeutic effects.

Lithospermum (Lithospermum officinale) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Lithospermum officinale. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins in Lithospermum are often used in traditional medicine to treat skin conditions and wounds. Their ability to bind proteins makes them effective in reducing bleeding and promoting tissue healing.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Lithospermum officinale. They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for skin healing and wound repair. These compounds help modulate cellular responses and may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Lithospermum officinale. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which are important in traditional herbal medicine. This compound supports the plant's use in treating skin conditions and promoting wound healing.

4. Glycoside

Lithospermum officinale contains the glycoside lithospermic acid. This compound is known for its potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin infections and inflammatory conditions. Lithospermic acid is also a key component in the preparation of traditional herbal remedies.

5. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key compound found in Lithospermum officinale. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine. Alkaloids in this plant are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are often used in formulations for skin conditions and wound healing.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Lithospermum officinale. It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, particularly in promoting skin health and wound healing. Coumarin's antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Lithospermum officinale contains the terpenoid lithospermic acid, which contributes to its medicinal properties. This compound is known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for skin conditions and wound healing.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a bioactive compound found in Lithospermum officinale. It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin helps in the treatment of skin conditions and supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Lithospermum officinale. It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has antimicrobial properties. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Lithospermum officinale. It contributes to the plant's vivid purple color and has antioxidant properties. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
