Oxytropis hirta

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxytropis hirta?

Oxytropis hirta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Fabales, it is situated within the family Fabaceae, which is commonly known as the legume family. The plant is further categorized under the genus Oxytropis, with its specific identification being the species hirta.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as coughs, asthma, and bronchitis, as well as various inflammatory skin conditions. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of pulmonary distress through its expectorant properties. Some practitioners also apply preparations of the plant to soothe skin irritations and minor wounds. The chemical constituents within the plant are believed to provide these therapeutic effects. Detailed studies on its specific medicinal applications continue to explore its potential in managing systemic inflammation.
