Hyparrhenia diplandra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hyparrheniia diplandra has a perennial, tufted growth habit characterized by densely packed, caespitose culms that rise from a fibrous root system. The leaves are linear and often involute, featuring a prominent midrib and a somewhat scabrous texture along the margins. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, spike-like panicle that is typically reddish or purplish in color. The spikelets are arranged in pairs, with one fertile floret and one often sterile or reduced floret, which is a defining trait of the species. This grass is specifically adapted to arid environments through its tough, sclerophyllous leaf structure.
