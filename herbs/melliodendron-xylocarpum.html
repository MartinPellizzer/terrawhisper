Melliodendron xylocarpum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Melliodendron xylocarpum has a woody structure characterized by a straight trunk, spreading branches, and elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically leathery in texture. The foliage is often arranged in a way that creates a dense, rounded canopy. Its flowers are small and cream-colored, blooming in large, showy clusters that attract various pollinators. The fruit is a distinctive woody capsule that is quite hard and contains several seeds. These physical traits collectively define the tree's robust and hardy appearance in its natural habitat.
