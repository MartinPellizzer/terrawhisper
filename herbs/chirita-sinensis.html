Chirita sinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chirita sinensis has ovate to lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along a stem and feature entire margins with a slightly pubescent texture. The foliage typically displays a bright green color and can grow to a moderate size depending on the environmental conditions. Its inflorescences consist of tubular, zygomorphic flowers that are often purplish or pinkish in hue. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and possess a distinct bilabiate structure characteristic of the Gesneriaceae family. The overall growth habit is that of a small, herbaceous perennial plant.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the limestone regions of Xinzheng in Henan Province, China. It is specifically adapted to the unique karst landscapes found within this particular locality. Because of this specialized habitat requirement, its natural range is extremely restricted and localized. The species typically grows in rocky crevices where moisture and shade are consistently available. These specific environmental conditions prevent it from spreading to broader geographic areas outside of its native province.

      Chirita sinensis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or digestive upset if ingested due to the presence of uncharacterized secondary metabolites. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many members of the Gesneriaceae family contain compounds that may trigger contact sensitivity in sensitive individuals.
