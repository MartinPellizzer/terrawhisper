Chirita sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Chirita sinensis?

Chirita sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and falls under the family Gesneriaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Chirita and the species name sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chirita sinensis has ovate to lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along a stem and feature entire margins with a slightly pubescent texture.

The foliage typically displays a bright green color and can grow to a moderate size depending on the environmental conditions. Its inflorescences consist of tubular, zygomorphic flowers that are often purplish or pinkish in hue. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and possess a distinct bilabiate structure characteristic of the Gesneriaceae family.

The overall growth habit is that of a small, herbaceous perennial plant.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Chirita sinensis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or digestive upset if ingested due to the presence of uncharacterized secondary metabolites. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many members of the Gesneriaceae family contain compounds that may trigger contact sensitivity in sensitive individuals. Direct contact with the sap or foliage might lead to localized redness, itching, or swelling on the skin. If the plant is accidentally consumed, it could potentially cause nausea or abdominal discomfort. It is generally advisable to handle this species with gloves and keep it away from pets and small children to avoid accidental exposure.
