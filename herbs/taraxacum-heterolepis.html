Taraxacum heterolepis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Taraxacum heterolepis has a basal rosette of leaves that vary in shape from spatulate to oblong, featuring dentate margins and a distinctively hairy texture. Each head consists of numerous individual florets surrounded by multiple layers of involucral bracts. The seeds are equipped with a pappus of fine white bristles to facilitate wind dispersal.
