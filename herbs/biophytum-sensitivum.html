Biophytum sensitivum

What's the taxonomical classification of Biophytum sensitivum?

Biophytum sensitivum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Oxalidales. This plant is further categorized into the family Oxalidaceae, within the genus Biophytum, and is specifically identified by the species name sensitivum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Biophytum sensitivum contains various bioactive alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its pharmacological properties. These specific phytochemicals work together to provide significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects within the plant tissues. Research indicates that the presence of these compounds allows the plant to be used traditionally for treating skin irritations and wound healing. Furthermore, the medicinal value is often derived from the aqueous extracts of the leaves and roots. Such chemical constituents make the plant a subject of interest for developing new natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Biophytum sensitivum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, boils, wounds, inflammation, and various types of swelling. The leaves are often crushed into a paste and applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote healing. In some traditional medicinal practices, it is also utilized to alleviate digestive issues and minor internal ailments. The plant contains bioactive compounds that contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Practitioners often rely on its cooling effects to reduce redness and pain associated with localized injuries.
