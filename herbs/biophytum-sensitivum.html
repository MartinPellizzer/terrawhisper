Biophytum sensitivum

What's the taxonomical classification of Biophytum sensitivum?

Biophytum sensitivum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Oxalidales. This plant is further categorized into the family Oxalidaceae, within the genus Biophytum, and is specifically identified by the species name sensitivum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Biophytum sensitivum has a creeping rhizomatous stem, large heart-shaped leaves that exhibit rapid nyctinastic movement, and solitary flowers with five petals. The leaves are characterized by their sensitive nature, folding inward quickly when touched or during the night to protect themselves. These leaves emerge from a thick, fleshy rootstock that allows the plant to spread across the forest floor. The flowers are typically pale yellow or white and appear on long stalks rising above the foliage. Small, fleshy fruits develop from the flowers after successful pollination.
