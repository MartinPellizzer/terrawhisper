Biophytum sensitivum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Biophytum sensitivum has a creeping rhizomatous stem, large heart-shaped leaves that exhibit rapid nyctinastic movement, and solitary flowers with five petals. The leaves are characterized by their sensitive nature, folding inward quickly when touched or during the night to protect themselves. These leaves emerge from a thick, fleshy rootstock that allows the plant to spread across the forest floor. The flowers are typically pale yellow or white and appear on long stalks rising above the foliage. Small, fleshy fruits develop from the flowers after successful pollination.

What are the herbal preparations of Biophytum sensitivum?

Biophytum sensitivum is prepared as a topical paste, a decoction, or a juice extracted directly from the fresh plant parts. What side-effects this plant can have?

Biophytum sensitivum can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. While often used in traditional medicine, the specific concentration of its active compounds remains insufficiently studied in modern clinical settings. Contact with the plant's sap may trigger redness or itching in individuals with sensitive skin. There is also a potential for systemic reactions if the plant is consumed in large quantities without professional guidance. Users should exercise caution and consult a medical expert before applying or consuming any part of this species.
