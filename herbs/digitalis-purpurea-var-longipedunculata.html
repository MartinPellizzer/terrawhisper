Purple Foxglove (Digitalis Purpurea Var. Longipedunculata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Digitalis purpurea var. longipedunculata.

Digitalis purpurea var. longipedunculata, a variant of the common foxglove, has been historically valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its cardiac glycosides such as digoxin and digitoxin. These compounds are used to treat heart conditions like congestive heart failure and atrial fibrillation by enhancing cardiac contractility and regulating heart rhythm. Although the plant is less commonly cultivated than the standard Digitalis purpurea, its medicinal potential remains significant in pharmaceutical applications. Due to its toxicity, it must be handled with care and used only under medical supervision. Despite its risks, the plant continues to play a role in modern cardiology due to the efficacy of its active compounds.
