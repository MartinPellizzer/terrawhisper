Convolvulus ammannii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Convolvulus ammannii has a perennial, herbaceous growth habit characterized by twining stems that often form dense mats or climb over surrounding vegetation. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, featuring entire margins and a smooth, somewhat fleshy texture. Its flowers are funnel-shaped and solitary, emerging from the leaf axils with delicate, pale petals that create a striking contrast against the foliage. The reproductive structures include a central ovary that develops into small, rounded capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. This morphological structure allows the plant to efficiently occupy ground space and compete for light in its native habitats.

Convolvulus ammannii has therapeutic actions that include acting as a bitter, digestive stimulant, carminative, stomachic, and aperient.
