Alysicarpus rugosus

What's the taxonomical classification of Alysicarpus rugosus?

Alysicarpus rugosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Alysicarpus, with its specific identification being the species rugosus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alysicarpus rugosus has a prostrate or ascending herbaceous growth habit characterized by woody stems, pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and small, pale purple to white papilionaceous flowers.

The leaflets are typically oblong to elliptic in shape and possess a distinctively wrinkled or rugose texture on their surfaces. Its inflorescences are arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant produces small, indehiscent pods that are slightly curved and contain individual seeds.

These structural features collectively define its identity within the Fabaceae family.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alysicarpus rugosus?

Alysicarpus rugosus has medicinal properties that function as an astringent, hemostatic, wound healing, and diuretic agent.

The plant's astringent qualities help to shrink or constrict body tissues, which aids in reducing inflammation. Due to its hemostatic actions, it is effective at slowing or stopping bleeding from minor cuts and injuries. These combined effects significantly accelerate the wound healing process by protecting the damaged area.

Additionally, the plant serves as a diuretic to help increase urine production and support kidney function.
