What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alysicarpus rugosus has a prostrate or ascending herbaceous growth habit characterized by woody stems, pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and small, pale purple to white papilionaceous flowers. Its inflorescences are arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant produces small, indehiscent pods that are slightly curved and contain individual seeds. These structural features collectively define its identity within the Fabaceae family.
