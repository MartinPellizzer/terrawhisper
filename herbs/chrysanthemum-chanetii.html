What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysanthemum chanetii?

Chrysanthemum chanetii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Asterales. The plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads, and it is further categorized under the genus Chrysanthemum. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, chanetii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysanthemum chanetii has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and bear deeply lobed, serrated leaves. Its inflorescences are composed of composite flower heads featuring ray florets that often display shades of yellow or white surrounding central disc florets. The stems are sturdy and can be slightly pubescent depending on the specific environmental conditions of its habitat.
