Urtica thunbergiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Urtica thunbergiana?

Urtica thunbergiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. It is further categorized under the family Urticaceae, placing it within the genus Urtica, and is specifically identified by its species name, thunbergiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Urtica thunbergiana has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a dense covering of stinging trichomes. The plant features long, slender stems that are typically covered in fine hairs. The leaf bases are often slightly cordate, and the venation is prominent on the undersides. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary clusters or spikes. These morphological traits are typical of the Urticaceae family and serve as primary defensive mechanisms.
