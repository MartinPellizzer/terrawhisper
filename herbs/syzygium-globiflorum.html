Syzygium globiflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Syzygium globiflorum?

Syzygium globiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Myrtales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Myrtaceae, placing it within the genus Syzygium, and it is specifically identified by the species name globiflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syzygium globiflorum has opposite, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length and possess an entire margin with a distinctively coriaceous texture. The foliage is characterized by a glossy upper surface and a paler underside, often displaying prominent pinnate venation. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, white flowers arranged in dense, globose clusters that emerge from the axils of the branches. These flowers feature numerous protruding stamens that give the clusters a fuzzy or brush-like appearance. The plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they reach maturity.
