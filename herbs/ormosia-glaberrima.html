Ormosia glaberrima

What's the taxonomical classification of Ormosia glaberrima?

Ormosia glaberrima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, specifically falling under the genus Ormosia, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name glaberrima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ormosia glaberrima has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of smooth, lanceolate leaflets and produces distinctive bright red seeds encased in hard black shells.

The foliage is characterized by its glabrous texture, meaning the leaf surfaces lack any hair or pubescence. Its stems exhibit a woody structure typical of tropical legumes, often branching to form a dense canopy. The flowers are small and arranged in clusters, providing a contrast to the striking coloration of the mature fruit.

These botanical features collectively define the plant's visual identity within its natural habitat.

        What are the herbal preparations of Ormosia glaberrima?

        Ormosia glaberrima is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a tincture from the seeds, or a topical paste made from crushed leaves. The bark is typically boiled in water to create a medicinal liquid used for various internal ailments. For skin conditions, the leaves are ground into a thick substance and applied directly to the affected area. Seed extracts are often processed with alcohol to concentrate their bioactive components for more potent medicinal use. These diverse forms allow practitioners to tailor the delivery method to the specific therapeutic need of the patient.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Ormosia glaberrima can cause severe poisoning if the seeds are ingested due to the presence of toxic proteins known as toxalbumins. These substances act similarly to ricin and can lead to debilitating gastrointestinal distress, including intense vomiting and diarrhea. If significant quantities are consumed, the toxins may cause systemic organ damage or even death. While the bark and leaves are less concentrated, handling the plant can still cause skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. It is crucial to avoid all contact with the seeds to prevent these hazardous biological effects.
