10 Possible Side Effects Of Mentha Pulegium

Mentha pulegium has some side effects when used improperly, such as kidney or liver damage, due to its high concentration of pulegone and menthone.

These compounds can cause severe vomiting, seizures, and even coma, especially when ingested in large quantities.

In severe cases, people may experience kidney or liver failure, leading to prolonged hospital stays, increased medical bills, and a reduced quality of life.

1. Irritates skin surfaces

Mentha pulegium irritates skin surfaces due to its essential oil content, which is rich in thymol and menthol.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation on the skin. The high concentration of these oils in Mentha pulegium extracts can also disrupt the skin's natural barrier function, making it more susceptible to irritation.

As a result, individuals may experience skin discomfort when using products containing this herb.

2. Causes allergic reactions

Mentha pulegium causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The herb contains a compound called menthone, which is responsible for its allergenic properties. Menthone can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the high concentration of volatile oils in pennyroyal can also contribute to its potential to induce allergic reactions, making it essential to be aware of these possible effects when using this herb.

3. Triggers asthma attacks

Mentha pulegium triggers asthma attacks due to its potent allergic properties.

The herb's essential oils, particularly menthone and isopulegol, can stimulate the airways, leading to inflammation, constriction, and increased sensitivity in individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions such as asthma.

As a result, exposure to Mentha pulegium may exacerbate symptoms of asthma attacks, causing wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

4. Produces excessive sweating

Mentha pulegium produces excessive sweating due to its potent menthol content.

When ingested or applied topically, menthol stimulates sweat glands, causing an increase in perspiration. This can lead to uncomfortable and inconvenient situations, especially in social or professional settings.

The intense cooling sensation caused by menthol can also amplify this effect, resulting in increased sweating as the body attempts to regulate its temperature.

5. Disrupts digestive rhythms

Mentha pulegium disrupts digestive rhythms due to its essential oil's ability to stimulate the gut and alter the balance of gut bacteria.

This can lead to changes in bowel movements, such as diarrhea or constipation, and potentially cause discomfort or pain in the stomach area.

The volatile compounds present in pennyroyal oil may also irritate the mucous membranes, exacerbating digestive issues.

6. Induces dizziness frequently

Mentha pulegium induces dizziness frequently due to its ability to alter blood pressure and heart rate.

The menthol content in pennyroyal oil can cause a sudden drop in blood pressure, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness.

Additionally, the essential oil's ability to stimulate the nervous system can also contribute to this side effect, resulting in an increased sense of unease or disorientation.

7. Weakens immune responses

Mentha pulegium weakens immune responses by suppressing the activity of natural killer cells and macrophages, which are essential for fighting off infections.

This is due to the presence of menthone and pulegone, two primary components of the herb that have been shown to inhibit the production of cytokines and chemokines, leading to a decline in immune function.

As a result, individuals using Mentha pulegium may experience increased susceptibility to illnesses and infections.

8. Increases blood pressure

Mentha pulegium increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system and release adrenaline.

The essential oil contained in Mentha pulegium, pulegone, acts as a vasoconstrictor, causing blood vessels to narrow and constrict, which in turn increases blood pressure.

Additionally, Mentha pulegium has been shown to increase the activity of the enzyme cytochrome P450, which plays a role in regulating blood pressure.

9. Slows down heart rate

Mentha pulegium slows down heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the parasympathetic nervous system.

This is caused by the presence of menthol and other essential oils, which can increase the production of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that promotes relaxation and decreases heart rate.

As a result, people may experience a decrease in blood pressure and heart rate when using Mentha pulegium, leading to a calming effect on the body.

10. Interferes with fertility
