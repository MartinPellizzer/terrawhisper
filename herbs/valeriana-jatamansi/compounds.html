Jatamansi (Valeriana jatamansi) Compounds

The primary compounds of Jatamansi include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and terpenoids, known for their sedative effects. Alkaloids contribute to its calming properties, while coumarin and phenolic acids offer anti-inflammatory benefits. Glycosides and essential oils support its traditional use in treating anxiety and insomnia.

Jatamansi (Valeriana jatamansi) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Jatamansi (Valeriana jatamansi). They contribute to the plant's sedative and anxiolytic properties. These compounds help regulate neurotransmitters, supporting mental calmness and sleep. Flavonoids are also known for their antioxidant and neuroprotective effects.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Valeriana jatamansi, known for their calming and sedative effects. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating anxiety and insomnia. They work by modulating neurotransmitter activity in the brain, enhancing the plant's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

3. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound in Jatamansi (Valeriana jatamansi). It contributes to the plant's calming and sedative effects, often used in traditional herbal medicine for anxiety and sleep disorders. This compound is extracted from the roots and is valued for its bioactive properties.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Jatamansi (Valeriana jatamansi). It contributes to the plant's sedative and calming effects by influencing the central nervous system. Coumarin also has antispasmodic properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating anxiety and insomnia.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Jatamansi (Valeriana jatamansi). It contributes to the plant's sedative and calming effects by modulating neurotransmitter activity. This compound is believed to support mental clarity and reduce anxiety. Its presence enhances the traditional uses of Jatamansi in herbal medicine.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Valeriana jatamansi. It contributes to the plant's sedative and calming effects. Glycosides in Jatamansi are known to influence the central nervous system, aiding in reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation.

7. Essential oil

The essential oil of Jatamansi (Valeriana jatamansi) is a key active compound, extracted through steam distillation. It contains volatile components like valerenic acid and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its calming effects. This oil is used in traditional medicine for its anxiolytic and sedative properties.

8. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound found in Jatamansi (Valeriana jatamansi). It contributes to the herb's astringent properties and plays a role in its traditional use for calming the nervous system and promoting sleep. Tannins also help in reducing inflammation and supporting digestive health.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Jatamansi (Valeriana jatamansi). It contributes to the plant's calming and sedative effects by interacting with the central nervous system. Saponins also exhibit antimicrobial properties, enhancing the plant's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Jatamansi (Valeriana jatamansi). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting mental clarity and stress relief. This pigment also enhances the plant's therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.
