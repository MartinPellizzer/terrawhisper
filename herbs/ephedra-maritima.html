Sea Ephedra (Ephedra Maritima)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Ephedra maritima.

Sea ephedra, scientifically known as Ephedra maritima, is a medicinal herb native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East, valued for its adaptogenic properties and historical use in traditional medicine. This plant contains ephedrine and pseudoephedrine, rare alkaloids that act as bronchodilators and stimulants, making it beneficial for respiratory conditions like asthma and nasal congestion. Historically, it has been used in ancient Greek and Arabic medicine to treat cold symptoms, respiratory ailments, and as a stimulant for energy and endurance. In modern wellness practices, it is occasionally used in herbal remedies for sinus support and as a natural alternative to over-the-counter decongestants. Its unique feature is its ability to thrive in saline environments, and its historical significance in ancient healing traditions adds to its cultural and medicinal appeal.
