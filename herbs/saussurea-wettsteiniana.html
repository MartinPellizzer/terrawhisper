Saussurea wettsteiniana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea wettsteiniana has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by basal rosette-forming leaves that are lanceolate to ovate with serrated margins. The stems are typically erect and can reach moderate heights, supporting a terminal inflorescence of densely packed capitula. Each flower head consists of numerous small, tubular florets that are often surrounded by specialized bracts. The foliage exhibits a somewhat leathery texture, which is a common adaptation for high-altitude environments. These morphological features collectively facilitate the plant's survival in specific alpine ecological niches.
