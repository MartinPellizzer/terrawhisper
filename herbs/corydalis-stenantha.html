Corydalis stenantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis stenantha has slender, upright stems and deeply lobed, pinnate leaves that exhibit a delicate, fern-like structure. The flowers are characteristically zygomorphic with a long, narrow nectar spur that distinguishes the species from its relatives. Its petals are typically pale in color, often appearing white or light yellow, and are arranged in a specialized shape to facilitate pollination. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that develop from the ovary after successful fertilization. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to thrive in specific temperate forest environments.

Research suggests these substances possess analgesic and sedative properties that can help alleviate pain and promote relaxation.
