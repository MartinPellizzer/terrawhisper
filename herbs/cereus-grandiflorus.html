Possible Side Effects of cereus grandiflorus

Cereus grandiflorus can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high concentration of alkaloids and saponins.

Prolonged use of the herb may also lead to cardiovascular problems, including hypertension and arrhythmia, as it can interact with blood pressure medications.

Additionally, improper use of cereus grandiflorus may cause allergic reactions, such as skin rashes, itching, and respiratory issues, in some individuals.

In severe cases, excessive consumption of the herb can lead to kidney damage and liver toxicity, highlighting the importance of proper dosage and medical supervision.
