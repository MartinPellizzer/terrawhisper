1. Causes allergic reactions

Ferula gummosa causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of terpenes and sesquiterpenes in its resin.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling. In severe cases, anaphylaxis may occur, characterized by difficulty breathing and rapid heartbeat.

The exact mechanism behind this reaction is not fully understood, but it is believed that the body's immune system mistakenly identifies these natural substances as harmful, resulting in an overactive response.

2. Triggers stomach ulcers

Ferula gummosa triggers stomach ulcers due to its high concentration of saponins and phenolic compounds.

These bioactive compounds can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract, leading to inflammation and erosion of the gastric lining, which may result in stomach ulcers.

The prolonged use of Ferula gummosa products may increase the risk of developing this adverse effect, potentially causing discomfort, pain, and bleeding in some individuals.

3. Increases blood pressure

Ferula gummosa increases blood pressure due to its high content of saponins and alkaloids.

These compounds can cause blood vessels to constrict, leading to an increase in blood pressure. Additionally, the gum's ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system may also contribute to this effect.

As a result, individuals using Ferula gummosa may experience elevated blood pressure levels, potentially posing a risk to cardiovascular health.

4. Stimulates excessive sweating

Ferula gummosa stimulates excessive sweating.

This is due to its high concentration of volatile oils, particularly ferulic acid, which can stimulate the sweat glands and increase perspiration.

Additionally, the herb's diaphoretic properties allow it to promote blood flow and release toxins through sweat, further contributing to excessive sweating.

5. Interferes with medication

Ferula gummosa interferes with medication due to its ability to interact with certain enzymes in the body.

The presence of terpenoids and phenolic compounds in Ferula gummosa can slow down or accelerate the metabolism of prescription medications, leading to altered blood levels and potentially adverse effects.

This interaction can occur even at low doses, making it crucial for individuals taking medication to be aware of potential contraindications when using this natural substance.

6. Worsens diarrhea

Ferula gummosa worsens diarrhea due to its high concentration of sennosides, a group of glycosides that stimulate bowel movements and increase stool frequency.

This can lead to increased fluid loss and electrolyte imbalance in the body, resulting in worsening of diarrhea symptoms.

7. Enhances anxiety attacks

Ferula gummosa enhances anxiety attacks by interacting with the brain's neurotransmitters, specifically serotonin and GABA.

This interaction can disrupt the normal balance of these chemicals, leading to feelings of heightened stress and anxiety. The gum's bitter compounds may also contribute to this effect, as they can stimulate the nervous system and cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

As a result, individuals using Ferula gummosa may experience intense worry, fear, or apprehension.

8. Reduces appetite significantly

Ferula gummosa reduces appetite significantly.

This side effect is attributed to the presence of certain bioactive compounds like coumarins and terpenes, which have been shown to suppress appetite by affecting gut hormones responsible for regulating food intake.

Additionally, the gum's anti-inflammatory properties may contribute to its appetite-suppressing effects by reducing inflammation in the digestive system, leading to a decrease in hunger pangs.

9. Provokes kidney damage

Ferula gummosa provokes kidney damage due to its high concentration of coumarins and furanocoumarins, which can cause nephrotoxicity.

These compounds are metabolized in the liver and excreted through the kidneys, potentially leading to inflammation, scarring, and eventually chronic kidney disease.

The repeated use of Ferula gummosa may increase the risk of cumulative damage to the kidneys, making it essential for individuals to be aware of this potential side effect when using this herb.

10. Disrupts menstrual cycle

Ferula gummosa disrupts menstrual cycle due to its phytoestrogenic properties, which can mimic the effects of estrogen in the body.

This may lead to irregularities and alterations in the menstrual cycle, potentially causing changes in frequency or duration of periods.

The presence of estrogen-like compounds in Ferula gummosa can also influence hormone production and balance, contributing to disruptions in the menstrual cycle.
