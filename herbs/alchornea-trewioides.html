Alchornea trewioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Alchornea trewioides?

Alchornea trewioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Malpighiales. The plant is further categorized under the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically within the genus Alchornea, and is identified by its unique species name, trewioides.

Alchornea trewioides contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that serve its medicinal purposes. What are the therapeutic actions of Alchornea trewioides?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, stomach aches, and various respiratory ailments. The leaves are frequently prepared as a decoction or poultice to soothe irritated skin and promote healing. When ingested, the plant's extracts serve as a remedy for gastrointestinal distress and abdominal discomfort. It is also utilized in traditional practices to alleviate symptoms associated with coughs and congestion. These medicinal applications highlight its significance in local herbal traditions.
