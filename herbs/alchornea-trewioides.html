Alchornea trewioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Alchornea trewioides?

Alchornea trewioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Malpighiales. The plant is further categorized under the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically within the genus Alchornea, and is identified by its unique species name, trewioides.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 1-O-galloyl-6-O-vanilloyl-beta-glucose, erigesiide C, ethyl gallate, gallic acid, and syringic acid. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse biological activities found within the species. Gallic acid and syringic acid are known for providing significant antioxidant properties to the plant's chemical profile. Furthermore, the presence of ethyl gallate and erigesiide C highlights the complex phenolic composition of the leaves and stems. Together, these substances represent the primary therapeutic constituents identified in Alc hornea trewioides.

According to a study published by "Yao xue xue bao = Acta pharmaceutica Sinica", the 95% EtOH extract of the root of Alchornea trewioides was analyzed using several chromatography and spectroscopic methods. This process led to the identification of 8 phenolic acids. These specific compounds are 1-O-galloyl-6-O-vanilloyl-beta-glucose (1), gallic acid (2), ethyl gallate (3), syringic acid (4), glucosyringic acid (5), erigeside C (6), 3, 4-dimethoxyphenyl-(6'-O-alpha-L-rhamnosyl)-beta-D-glucopyranoside (7) and 3, 4, 5-trimethoxyphenyl-(6'-O-galloyl)-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (8). Compound 1 is a new compound, while compounds 4-8 are being isolated from the genus Alchornea for the first time. Additionally, the other compounds were isolated from this plant for the first time.
