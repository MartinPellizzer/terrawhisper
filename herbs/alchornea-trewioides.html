Alchornea trewioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Alchornea trewioides?

Alchornea trewioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Malpighiales. The plant is further categorized under the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically within the genus Alchornea, and is identified by its unique species name, trewioides.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 1-O-galloyl-6-O-vanilloyl-beta-glucose, ethyl gallate, gallic acid, glucosyringic acid, and syringic acid. These specific phenolic compounds are known for their diverse biological activities within organic systems. Gallic acid and its derivatives often provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the presence of syringic acid and glucosyringic acid contributes to the complex chemical profile of the species. These substances collectively support the plant's medicinal potential in various pharmacological applications.

As discussed in a study, the 95% EtOH extract of the root of Alchornea trewioides was analyzed to identify its chemical constituents. Through various chromatography methods, eight phenolic acids were obtained from the plant. These identified compounds include 1-O-galloyl-6-O-vanilloyl-beta-glucose (1), gallic acid (2), ethyl gallate (3), syringic acid (4), glucosyringic acid (5), erigesiode C (6), 3, 4-dimethoxyphenyl-(6'-O-alpha-L-rhamnosyl)-beta-D-glucopyranoside (7), and 3, 4, 5-trimethoxyphenyl-(6'-O-galloyl)-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (8). Among these, compound 1 is a new compound, while compounds 4-8 are isolated from the genus Alchornea for the first time. Additionally, the other identified compounds were isolated from the plant for the first time.
