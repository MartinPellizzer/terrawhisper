Alchornea trewioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Alchornea trewioides?

Alchornea trewioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Malpighiales. The plant is further categorized under the family Euphorbiaceae, specifically within the genus Alchornea, and is identified by its unique species name, trewioides.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alchornea trewioides?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, stomach aches, and various respiratory ailments. The leaves are frequently prepared as a decoction or poultice to soothe irritated skin and promote healing. When ingested, the plant's extracts serve as a remedy for gastrointestinal distress and abdominal discomfort. It is also utilized in traditional practices to alleviate symptoms associated with coughs and congestion. These medicinal applications highlight its significance in local herbal traditions.

What are the herbal preparations of Alchornea trewioides?

Alchornea trewioides is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion made from its leaves and roots to treat various ailments. These liquid preparations are commonly consumed orally to address digestive issues or to reduce inflammation. In some traditional practices, the plant material is also used in topical applications such as poultices or washes. These external methods help soothe skin irritations or localized pain. The specific method of preparation often depends on the targeted symptom and the part of the plant being utilized.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Alchornea trewioides can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested incorrectly. The plant contains secondary metabolites that may trigger contact dermatitis in individuals with sensitive skin upon direct touch. When consumed, these chemical compounds can lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps. Some users might also experience dizziness or headaches following exposure to its concentrated extracts. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse physiological responses.
