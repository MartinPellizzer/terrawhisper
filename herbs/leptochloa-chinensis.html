Leptochloa chinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leptochloa chinensis has an erect, tufted growth habit characterized by slender, creeping rhizomes and smooth, cylindrical stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are linear with narrow, acute tips and possess a prominent midrib that distinguishes them from similar grasses. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, spreading panicle containing numerous small, spikelet-bearing branches. Each spikelet is relatively small and contains a single fertile floret encased by several thin glumes. The plant often produces a dense root system that allows it to stabilize in various soil types.

What are the therapeutic actions of Leptochloa chinensis?

Leptochloa chinensis has diuretic, astringent, hemostatic, and anti-inflammatory therapeutic actions used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, itching, or redness when it comes into direct contact with sensitive human skin. Because it is a common grass, individuals with general hay fever or seasonal allergies may experience respiratory irritation or sneezing if they inhale its pollen. Some people might also develop localized swelling or hives if they have a specific hypersensitivity to its components. There is limited clinical data regarding the internal toxicity of this species, so ingestion should be avoided to prevent unknown gastrointestinal distress. Monitoring for any physical irritation is recommended when working in areas where this grass is prevalent.
