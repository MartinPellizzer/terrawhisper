Actinidia chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Actinidia chinensis?

Actinidia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is situated in Equisetopsida, further descending into the subclass Magnoliidae. It follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ericales and is a member of the family Actinidiaceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Actinidia, with its specific designation being the species chinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Actinidia chinensis contains high concentrations of ascorbic acid, polyphenols, flavonoids, and various bioactive carotenoids. These medicinal compounds work together to provide potent antioxidant properties that combat oxidative stress in human cells. The presence of dietary fiber and specific enzymes also aids in digestive health and metabolic regulation. Furthermore, certain phytochemicals within the plant exhibit anti-inflammatory effects that may support immune system function. Research suggests these organic substances contribute significantly to the plant's overall therapeutic potential.

What are the therapeutic actions of Actinidia chinensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat constipation, digestive disorders, hypertension, and certain types of skin inflammation. The high fiber content in the fruit acts as a natural laxative to regulate bowel movements and alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort. Its rich antioxidant profile helps combat oxidative stress which may support cardiovascular health and blood pressure management. Additionally, topical applications or dietary intake can assist in soothing inflammatory skin conditions. These medicinal properties stem from the plant's complex nutritional composition.
