Dog Rose (Rosa Canina Subsp. Senticosa)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Rosa canina subsp. senticosa.

Rosa canina subsp. senticosa, commonly known as the sweet-briar rose, has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties due to its high content of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and vitamin C. The plant's hips, which are the fruit of the rose, are particularly valued for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, making them beneficial in supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. In herbal medicine, Rosa canina subsp. senticosa has been employed to treat conditions such as colds, respiratory infections, and digestive issues, often in the form of teas or tinctures. Its astringent properties also make it useful for skin health, aiding in the treatment of wounds and reducing inflammation. Due to its rich nutrient profile and historical use, it remains a popular ingredient in natural remedies for promoting overall wellness.
