Oldenlandia lancifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Oldenlandia lancifolia?

Oldenlandia lancifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Gentianaales. This plant is further nested within the family Rubiaceae, a diverse group of flowering plants. Its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Oldenlandia, with the unique species designation lancifolia.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, digestive issues, and inflammatory conditions. Local traditional practices often employ its leaves to soothe wounds and heal skin irritations through topical application. Some cultures also utilize the plant to mitigate stomach pains and other gastrointestinal discomforts. Its chemical properties are believed to assist in reducing swelling and managing localized pain in different parts of the body. These medicinal applications demonstrate the significant role the species plays in regional herbal medicine.
