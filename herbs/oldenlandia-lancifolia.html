Oldenlandia lancifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oldenlandia lancifolia has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along its stems and possess a distinctively elongated, pointed shape. The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture and a deep green coloration to facilitate moisture retention. Its stems are woody at the base, providing a sturdy structure for the plant's growth habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often appearing in clusters. The overall architecture of the plant is characterized by its slender, upright growth pattern.
