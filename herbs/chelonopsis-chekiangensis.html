Chelonopsis chekiangensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chelonopsis chekiangensis has a woody, perennial structure characterized by a specialized rhizomatous root system and lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage typically emerges in dense clusters, displaying a distinct texture that aids in moisture retention. Its stems are relatively slender but provide necessary support for the spreading leaf canopy. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axillary regions during its blooming period. The overall architectural form of the plant is compact and adapted to its specific ecological niche.
