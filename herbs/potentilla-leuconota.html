Potentilla leuconota

What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla leuconota?

Potentiella leuconota belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Potentiella, and its unique species designation, leuconoeta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella leuconota has a perennial growth habit characterized by creeping stolons, pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and small five-petaled white flowers.

The foliage typically consists of leaflets that are oblong or elliptical in shape with serrated margins. These leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs that give the plant a slightly pubescent texture. The inflorescences consist of several individual flowers held on slender pedicels.

Each flower features a central cluster of numerous stamens surrounded by a ring of pale petals.
