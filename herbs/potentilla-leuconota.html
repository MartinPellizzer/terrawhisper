Potentilla leuconota

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella leuconota has a perennial growth habit characterized by creeping stolons, pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and small five-petaled white flowers. The foliage typically consists of leaflets that are oblong or elliptical in shape with serrated margins. These leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs that give the plant a slightly pubescent texture. The inflorescences consist of several individual flowers held on slender pedicels. Each flower features a central cluster of numerous stamens surrounded by a ring of pale petals.
