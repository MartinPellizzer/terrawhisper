What's the taxonomical classification of Elaeagnus lanceolata?

Elaeagnuus lanceolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Rosales and belongs to the family Elaeaagnaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Elaeagnuus, and its species name, lanceolata.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Elaeagnuus lanceolata can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is ingested. While the plant is not documented as highly toxic, direct contact with its foliage or bark may trigger redness and itching in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience swelling or a rash if they handle the plant without protective clothing. Ingestion of the berries or leaves can lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain. It is generally advisable to avoid direct contact and keep the plant away from small children and pets to prevent accidental consumption.
