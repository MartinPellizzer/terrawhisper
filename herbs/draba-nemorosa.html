What's the taxonomical classification of Draba nemorosa?

Draba nemorosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Brassicales and is a member of the family Brassicaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Draba and is distinguished as the species nemorosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Draba nemorosa has small, opposite, grayish-green leaves that are typically spatulate or oblong in shape and possess a slightly hairy texture.

The plant features a low-growing rosette structure from which slender, upright flowering stalks emerge. These stems bear small, four-petaled white flowers that bloom in clusters known as racemes. After flowering, the plant produces tiny, rounded seed pods that are characteristic of the mustard family.

The overall stature of the plant is compact, making it well-adapted to rocky or alpine environments.
