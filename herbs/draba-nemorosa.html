Draba nemorosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Draba nemorosa?

Draba nemorosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Brassicales and is a member of the family Brassicaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Draba and is distinguished as the species nemorosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These sulfur-containing compounds are characteristic of the Brassicaceae family and often exhibit antimicrobial properties. The presence of flavonoids suggests potential antioxidant effects that help neutralize free radicals within organic systems. While specific clinical applications are limited in modern pharmacology, these secondary metabolites provide the chemical basis for its traditional medicinal interest.
