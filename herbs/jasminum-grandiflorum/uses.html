Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) Uses

Jasmine is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, tuberculosis, asthma, and respiratory infections. It helps alleviate symptoms of cough and common cold. It also supports skin health by treating infections, irritation, and aiding in wound healing.

Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing symptoms of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce coughing and mucus production. Essential oil inhalation or steam therapy with jasmine can soothe irritated airways and ease breathing in bronchitis cases.

2. Tuberculosis

Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its essential oil is believed to help alleviate symptoms of tuberculosis by reducing inflammation and enhancing lung function. While not a substitute for modern medical treatment, jasmine may complement conventional therapies in managing respiratory conditions.

3. Asthma

Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its essential oil, known for its calming and bronchodilating properties, may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing airway inflammation and easing breathing. However, it should be used under professional guidance to ensure safety and effectiveness.

4. Respiratory infections

Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) has been used traditionally to support respiratory health. Its essential oil possesses antiseptic and decongestant properties that may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. Steam inhalation with jasmine oil can ease congestion and soothe irritated airways. It is often used in herbal formulations for coughs and colds.

5. Cough

Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) is used in herbal medicine to soothe coughs due to its expectorant and antispasmodic properties. The essential oil, often diluted, can be inhaled to relieve bronchial congestion and reduce coughing fits. It may also help calm the nervous system, easing the discomfort associated with persistent coughing.

6. Respiratory tract infections

Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its essential oils possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties that may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections. Inhalation of jasmine steam or use of its extracts can soothe congestion and reduce inflammation in the airways. It is often used in herbal remedies for colds, bronchitis, and other respiratory conditions.

7. Common cold

Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) is used in traditional herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of the common cold. Its essential oil has antiviral and decongestant properties that help reduce nasal congestion and soothe respiratory discomfort. It is often used in steam inhalations or as a topical application to ease breathing and promote recovery.

8. Skin infections

Jasmine essential oil has antimicrobial properties that help treat skin infections. It can reduce bacterial growth and soothe inflamed areas. Often diluted in a carrier oil, it is applied topically to combat conditions like eczema or fungal infections. Its soothing aroma also promotes relaxation during treatment.

9. Skin irritation

Jasmine essential oil is used to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It can help reduce redness, itching, and inflammation associated with conditions like eczema or minor burns. Diluted jasmine oil may be applied topically to affected areas for relief. It is important to perform a patch test before use to avoid allergic reactions.

10. Wound healing

Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) has been traditionally used to promote wound healing due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oil and flower extracts help reduce infection risk and accelerate tissue repair. It is often applied topically to clean wounds, burns, and skin abrasions to enhance recovery and prevent scarring.
