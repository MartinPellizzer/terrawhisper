Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) Compounds

The primary compounds of Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) include essential oil, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarin. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and potential therapeutic effects, such as antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory actions. Terpenoids and glycosides also play roles in its medicinal value.

Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) is a key active compound, rich in compounds like linalool and benzyl acetate. It is widely used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for its calming and soothing properties. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its aromatic and therapeutic benefits.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, which may support immune function and reduce inflammation. These compounds are often extracted from jasmine flowers and used in herbal preparations for their therapeutic potential.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, which may support skin health and reduce inflammation. This compound is commonly extracted from jasmine flowers for use in herbal remedies and aromatherapy.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has mild sedative and antispasmodic effects. Found in the essential oils of jasmine flowers, coumarin is used in herbal medicine for its calming and relaxing properties.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of organic compounds found in Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have been studied for their potential therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. These compounds are often extracted from the flowers and used in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin in Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) contributes to its astringent properties and may support wound healing and inflammation reduction. It is found in the plant's flowers and leaves, and while it is not the primary active compound, it plays a role in the plant's traditional medicinal uses.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has been studied for its potential calming and antispasmodic effects. Glycosides in jasmine may support respiratory and nervous system health, making them relevant in herbal medicine practices.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties by exhibiting antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin is also used in traditional medicine to support respiratory and digestive health.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum) are naturally occurring nitrogen-containing compounds with potential pharmacological effects. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for their possible anti-inflammatory and sedative properties. However, research on their specific therapeutic applications remains limited.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Jasmine (Jasminum grandiflorum). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower color and possesses antioxidant properties. In herbal medicine, anthocyanin may support skin health and reduce inflammation. Its presence in jasmine highlights the plant's potential therapeutic applications.
