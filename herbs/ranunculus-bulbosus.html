What are the pest and diseases tips of Ranunculus bulbosus?

Ranunculus bulbosus, also known as creeping buttercup, is a perennial species that can be affected by several pests and diseases.

One common issue is crown rot, caused by fungal pathogens such as Sclerotinia sclerotiorum and Botrytis cinerea, which can lead to the decay of the plant's crown and roots, ultimately causing plant death. Slugs and snails are also major pests that target Ranunculus bulbosus, feeding on leaves and flowers, and leaving behind characteristic holes and scars. Aphids, whiteflies, and spider mites may also infest the plants, sucking sap and spreading disease.

Additionally, powdery mildew, a fungal disease caused by Erysiphe polygoni, can infect Ranunculus bulbosus, covering leaves with a white powdery coating that reduces photosynthesis and plant growth.
