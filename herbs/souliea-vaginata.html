Souliea vaginata

What's the taxonomical classification of Souliea vaginata?

Souliea vaginata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically situated within the genus Souliea, with its unique designation as the species vaginata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Souliea vaginata has a woody climbing habit characterized by twining stems that produce lanceolate, leathery leaves with entire margins and distinct pinnate venation.

The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color on the upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Its inflorescences consist of solitary flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, often featuring delicate, pale petals. The reproductive structures are notable for their specific shape, which gives the species its unique botanical name.

These morphological features help distinguish the plant within its specific ecological niche.

      What are the herbal preparations of Souliea vaginata?

      Souliea vaginata is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the intended medicinal use.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Souliea vaginata can cause severe skin irritation, localized allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger contact dermatitis upon touching the leaves or stems. Some individuals might experience redness, itching, or swelling at the site of exposure. If the plant is consumed, it could lead to nausea or stomach cramps in sensitive users. Careful handling is necessary to prevent these adverse physiological responses.
