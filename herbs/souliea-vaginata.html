Souliea vaginata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Souliea vaginata has a woody climbing habit characterized by twining stems that produce lanceolate, leathery leaves with entire margins and distinct pinnate venation. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color on the upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Its inflorescences consist of solitary flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, often featuring delicate, pale petals. The reproductive structures are notable for their specific shape, which gives the species its unique botanical name.
