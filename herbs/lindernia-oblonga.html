Lindernia oblonga

What's the taxonomical classification of Lindernia oblonga?

Lindernia oblonga belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is classified under the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Linderniaceae and is specifically situated within the genus Lindernia, ultimately identified by its species name, oblonga.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia oblonga has an erect to ascending herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a mucronate tip. The stems are typically slender and may be branched towards the upper portions of the plant. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils and are typically white or pale lavender in color. The fruit is a small capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds necessary for reproduction. This plant's structure is adapted to its preference for moist or semi-aquatic environments.
