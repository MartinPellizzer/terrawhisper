Lindernia oblonga

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia oblonga has an erect to ascending herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a mucronate tip. The stems are typically slender and may be branched towards the upper portions of the plant. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils and are typically white or pale lavender in color. The fruit is a small capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds necessary for reproduction. This plant's structure is adapted to its preference for moist or semi-aquatic environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, spanning from India and Sri Lanka through Myanmar and Thailand to parts of Indonesia and China. It typically flourishes in wetland environments such as marshes, rice paddies, and the edges of shallow ponds. Because it prefers saturated soils, its distribution is closely tied to areas with consistent water availability throughout the growing season. The species is most commonly encountered in low-lying coastal plains and river basins where moisture is abundant. This specific ecological preference limits its presence to humid, lowland habitats within its broad geographic range.

Lindernia oblonga is prepared as a decoction, a juice, or a paste for topical application.
