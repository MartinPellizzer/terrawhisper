What's the taxonomical classification of Davallia formosana?

Davalliia formosana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae, eventually placing it within the genus Davallia. Finally, the specific identification of this plant is completed by its species name, formosana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Davalliia formosana has a creeping rhizome covered in dense, light brown to tan scales and produces pinnate fronds that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length.

The texture of the plant is defined by these hairy, somewhat fleshy rhizomes which allow it to cling to various substrates. Each frond consists of multiple small, rounded to ovate leaflets arranged along a central rachis. The underside of the foliage often displays characteristic sori, which are the spore-producing structures typical of this genus.

These morphological traits collectively enable the plant to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

        What are the herbal preparations of Davallia formosana?

        Davalliia formosana is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical poultice to utilize its medicinal properties. Practitioners often boil the plant parts in water to create a concentrated liquid tea for internal consumption. The dried material can also be ground into a fine powder that is either ingested or mixed into other substances.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Davalliia formosana can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals due to the presence of irritants in its fronds. While it is not considered highly toxic, touching the plant may result in redness, itching, or a rash on the hands. Some people might also experience mild respiratory discomfort if they inhale fine spores released by the fern during reproduction. It is generally advisable to wash your hands thoroughly after handling the foliage to prevent direct contact with any potential allergens. Owners of curious pets should also monitor for signs of gastrointestinal upset if the plant is accidentally ingested.
