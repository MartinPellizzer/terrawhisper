Chamaesyce Centralis

Chamaesyce centralis, commonly known as the prickly goosefoot, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast.

Traditionally used by Native American tribes for its medicinal properties, it has been valued for its ability to treat various ailments, including skin conditions and respiratory issues. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and alkaloids, which may contribute to its therapeutic effects. In modern herbal practices, it is sometimes used topically to reduce inflammation and as a remedy for minor wounds.

However, due to its toxic properties, it should be used with caution and under the guidance of a qualified herbalist.
