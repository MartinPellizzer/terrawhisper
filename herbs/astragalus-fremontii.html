Fremont'S Milkvetch (Astragalus Fremontii)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Astragalus fremontii.

Fremont's milkvetch, scientifically known as Astragalus fremontii, is a medicinal herb native to the arid regions of the western United States, particularly in the Great Basin and California. This plant is recognized for its adaptogenic properties, which help the body resist stress and maintain balance, making it a valuable component in traditional and modern wellness practices. Traditionally used by Native American tribes such as the Shoshone and Paiute, Fremont's milkvetch was valued for its ability to support immune function and promote vitality, often consumed as a tea or incorporated into herbal remedies. In modern wellness, it is utilized in supplements and herbal formulations to enhance immune response, reduce inflammation, and support overall health, particularly in individuals with chronic fatigue or weakened immune systems. One of its unique features is the presence of a rare active compound called cycloastragenol, which has shown potential in promoting cellular health, along with its distinctive, slightly sweet and earthy flavor that makes it a sought-after ingredient in both traditional and contemporary herbal medicine.
