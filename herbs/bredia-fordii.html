Bredia fordii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bredia fordii has a prostrate or ascending growth habit with slender, woody stems and small, opposite, ovate-to-elliptic leaves that feature entire margins and short petioles. The foliage is typically characterized by a smooth surface and a somewhat succulent texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary clusters and often possess a delicate structure. These plants frequently develop from a woody base that allows them to spread across the ground. The overall morphology is adapted for survival in rocky or sandy substrates within its native habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of West and Central Africa, spanning from Guinea and Sierra Leone eastward through Nigeria to the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It thrives primarily in the humid environments of lowland rainforests and moist secondary forests. Within these ecosystems, it is often found growing in shaded areas where soil moisture remains consistently high. Its distribution follows the equatorial belt where heavy rainfall and warm temperatures are characteristic.
