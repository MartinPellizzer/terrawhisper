Caryopteris nepetifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Caryopteris nepetifolia?

Caryopteris nepetifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It is further classified into the family Lamiaceae, which places it within the genus Caryopteris, and is specifically identified by its species name, nepetifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caryopteris nepetifolia has woody, mounding stems that produce small, lanceolate, grayish-green leaves with serrated edges. The foliage typically reaches a height and spread of approximately two to three feet. During the summer months, the plant produces dense terminal clusters of small, tubular, blue to violet flowers. These blooms emerge from terminal panicles that contrast sharply against the textured, matte leaf surface. The overall structure remains compact and shrubby, making it ideal for garden borders.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to central and southern China, where it grows naturally in various habitats. It is commonly found in mountainous regions and rocky areas throughout its primary range. Due to its attractive blue flowers and hardiness, it has been widely introduced to temperate gardens across Europe and North America. While it thrives in these cultivated settings, it does not form invasive populations in most non-native territories. The species prefers well-drained soils and sunny locations consistent with its original East Asian environment.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant grows best in well-drained soil within full sun to partial shade environments and requires USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.

You should plant it in a location that avoids soggy feet, as it is highly susceptible to root rot in overly wet conditions. Regular watering is necessary during the first growing season to establish the root system, but the mature shrub is quite drought-tolerant. Pruning the stems back by half in early spring promotes vigorous new growth and a profusion of blue flowers.

Providing adequate air circulation around the foliage helps to prevent fungal issues throughout the summer.
