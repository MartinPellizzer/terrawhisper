Betula pendula

What's the taxonomical classification of Betula pendula?

Betula pendula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Fagales and is a member of the family Betulaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its designation within the genus Betula and its specific species name, pendula.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Betula pendula contains high concentrations of triterpenes, specifically betulin and betulinic acid, alongside flavonoids, tannins, and saponins. These specific triterpenoids are primarily located in the bark and are recognized for their significant anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The presence of salicylic acid derivatives within the plant also contributes to its traditional use in managing pain and fever. Additionally, the leaves and buds provide antioxidant support through various phenolic compounds. These combined chemical constituents make the silver birch a versatile subject in phytotherapy and pharmacological research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Betula pendula?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat urinary tract infections, kidney stones, bladder inflammation, and various skin conditions such as eczema or psoriasis. The birch bark and leaves contain betulin and flavonoids that possess significant diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. These components help flush the kidneys and promote the elimination of toxins from the body. Additionally, topical applications of birch extracts are often utilized to soothe irritated skin and reduce swelling. Regular consumption of birch tea is a traditional method for managing water retention and supporting renal health.
