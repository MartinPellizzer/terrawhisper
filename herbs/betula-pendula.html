Side Effects of betula pendula

Betula pendula triggers allergic reaction in individuals who are sensitive to the plant, leading to a range of symptoms that can affect multiple systems of the body.

One of the most common side effects is breathing difficulty, as the pollen from this herb can cause asthma-like symptoms in those with respiratory conditions. Additionally, Betula pendula can induce skin itching and irritation, often resulting in a red, itchy rash that may spread across exposed skin areas.

Eye irritation is another potential effect, with symptoms such as redness, watering, and a burning sensation in the eyes. In more severe cases, the herb may cause throat swelling, which can be life-threatening if it obstructs the airway.

These side effects highlight the importance of awareness and caution when handling or being exposed to Betula pendula, especially for individuals with known allergies or respiratory sensitivities.
