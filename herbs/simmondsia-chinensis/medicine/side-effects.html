10 Possible Side Effects Of Simmondsia Chinensis

Simmondsia chinensis has some side effects when used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and eye problems.

These side effects occur when the skin is sensitive or when the herb is applied in high concentrations. For instance, prolonged use of jojoba oil can cause skin dryness, leading to irritation and discomfort.

In severe cases, it can worsen skin conditions like eczema and acne, affecting the quality of life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Simmondsia chinensis causes allergic reactions.

This is often due to the presence of certain proteins and fatty acids in the oil extracted from its seeds, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

Additionally, cross-reactivity with other common allergens may occur, making it difficult for the body to distinguish between harmless substances and the jojoba extract, leading to allergic reactions.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Simmondsia chinensis triggers asthma attacks due to its potential allergenic properties.

The plant's seeds and oil contain various compounds that can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to asthmatic symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

This reaction is often unpredictable and may occur even after prolonged exposure to the substance.

3. Induces skin irritation

Simmondsia chinensis induces skin irritation in some individuals due to its high concentration of fatty acids and saponins.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction or exacerbate existing skin conditions such as eczema or acne, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation.

Additionally, the oil's strong moisturizing properties may also trigger skin reactions in people with sensitive skin types.

4. Increases blood pressure

Simmondsia chinensis increases blood pressure due to its potential to cause vasoconstriction, which is a constriction of blood vessels.

This may occur because the oil's chemical composition, including its high levels of saturated fats and omega-9 fatty acids, can stimulate the release of vasopressor hormones such as angiotensin II.

Additionally, jojoba oil has been shown to increase peripheral resistance, further contributing to elevated blood pressure in some individuals.

5. Decreases fertility rate

Simmondsia chinensis decreases fertility rate in some individuals due to its potential to disrupt hormonal balance.

The seed oil extracted from this plant contains compounds that may interfere with the body's natural production of estrogen and testosterone, leading to altered reproductive function.

Additionally, jojoba oil has been shown to have anti-progesterone activity, which can further compromise fertility by disrupting the delicate balance of hormones necessary for conception.

6. Affects thyroid function

Simmondsia chinensis affects thyroid function by interfering with the absorption of levothyroxine, a common medication used to treat hypothyroidism.

This is due to the high levels of omega-9 fatty acids present in jojoba oil, which can bind to levothyroxine and reduce its effectiveness.

As a result, individuals taking jojoba supplements or using jojoba products topically may experience changes in thyroid hormone levels, leading to potential side effects such as fatigue, weight gain, and depression.

7. Interacts with medications

Simmondsia chinensis interacts with medications such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and blood pressure medications due to its potential to alter their levels in the body.

This interaction can increase the risk of bleeding complications, hypoglycemia, or hypertension, respectively.

The bioactive compounds present in jojoba oil, particularly alpha-hydroxy acids and fatty acid esters, are thought to contribute to these interactions by affecting liver enzymes responsible for medication metabolism.

8. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Simmondsia chinensis disrupts menstrual cycles due to its high levels of omega-9 fatty acids and antioxidants.

The consumption of this plant-based oil can alter the body's hormonal balance, leading to irregular periods, changes in bleeding patterns, or even complete cessation of menstruation.

9. Worsens acne symptoms

Simmondsia chinensis worsens acne symptoms due to its comedogenic properties.

The oil's high linoleic acid content can clog pores and cause blackheads and whiteheads, exacerbating existing acne issues.

Additionally, jojoba oil can stimulate the sebaceous glands, leading to increased sebum production, which may trigger acne breakouts in individuals with sensitive skin or pre-existing acne conditions.

10. Raises cholesterol levels

Simmondsia chinensis raises cholesterol levels by containing a high amount of saturated fatty acids.

These fatty acids are primarily composed of triglycerides, which can increase the production of low-density lipoprotein (LDL) or "bad" cholesterol in the body.

As a result, regular consumption of Simmondsia chinensis may lead to an imbalance in lipid profiles and potentially contribute to cardiovascular disease risk factors.
