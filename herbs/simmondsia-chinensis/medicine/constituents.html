10 Medicinal Constituents Of Simmondsia Chinensis (Jojoba)

Simmondsia chinensis has active constituents such as triterpenoid and antioxidant compounds.

These constituents exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and regenerative properties. They can improve skin conditions, wounds, and hair loss. For example, the antioxidants in jojoba can protect the skin from premature aging and damage, while its antimicrobial properties can help prevent infections.

These properties can lead to improved skin health, reduced inflammation, and enhanced overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Simmondsia chinensis.

1. Oleic acid

Simmondsia chinensis oleic acid is a natural moisturizing compound found in its seed oil.

It is a non-ionic surfactant that effectively cleanses and nourishes the skin without stripping it of its natural oils. Oleic acid has anti-inflammatory properties, which can help soothe and calm irritated skin, making it an ideal ingredient in skincare products for acne-prone or sensitive skin types.

Its hydrating benefits also make it suitable for use as a leave-on moisturizer or in hair care products to nourish and condition the scalp.

2. Linoleic acid

Simmondsia chinensis linoleic acid is an essential fatty acid found in the seeds of this desert plant.

It is a polyunsaturated fatty acid, particularly abundant in the unsaponifiable fraction of jojoba oil, which is widely used in cosmetics and pharmaceuticals due to its moisturizing, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

Linoleic acid plays a crucial role in maintaining healthy skin and hair, as well as supporting heart health and immune function.

3. Palmitic acid

Simmondsia chinensis palmitic acid is a naturally occurring fatty acid that comprises approximately 10-15% of jojoba oil.

It is an unusual fatty acid because it has a unique double bond at carbon atom six (C6) and C7, which is not found in most other plant-based oils.

This distinctive structure gives jojoba oil its exceptional stability and makes it an excellent ingredient for skincare products, as it can effectively moisturize and soothe the skin without clogging pores or leaving a greasy residue.

4. Stearic acid

Simmondsia chinensis stearic acid is a saturated fatty acid naturally occurring in its seeds.

It has been extracted and used extensively in various industries due to its unique properties. Jojoba stearic acid is non-comedogenic, meaning it won't clog pores, and has emollient and humectant properties, making it an excellent ingredient for skincare and haircare products.

Additionally, it is also used as a lubricant and in the production of food additives.

5. Triterpenoids

Simmondsia chinensis triterpenoids is a type of compound found in the seeds and leaves of this desert shrub.

These triterpenoids have been shown to exhibit a range of biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

They are believed to contribute to the plant's natural resistance to pests and diseases, as well as its ability to tolerate extreme environmental conditions.

6. Sterols

Simmondsia chinensis sterols is a type of phytosterol derived from the seeds of the Simmondsia chinensis plant.

These sterols are structurally similar to cholesterol and have been shown to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Additionally, jojoba sterols have been found to improve skin elasticity, reduce wrinkles, and soothe irritated skin conditions, making them a popular ingredient in cosmetic products.

7. Squalene

Simmondsia chinensis squalene is a natural moisturizing agent extracted from the seeds of the plant.

This non-comedogenic and hypoallergenic compound has been widely used in cosmetic and pharmaceutical products due to its ability to hydrate and protect the skin and hair without leaving any residue or causing irritation.

Squalene has antioxidant properties, making it an effective anti-aging ingredient that helps to reduce fine lines and wrinkles.

8. Campesterol

Simmondsia chinensis campesterol is a type of plant sterol found in high concentrations in jojoba oil.

This naturally occurring compound has been studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Campesterol has also been shown to have moisturizing and regenerative effects on the skin, making it a popular ingredient in skincare products.

9. Beta-sitosterol

Simmondsia chinensis beta-sitosterol is a phytosterol that is found in its seeds.

This compound has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, particularly in relation to cardiovascular disease and cholesterol levels. Beta-sitosterol has been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties and may help to reduce the risk of certain cancers.

It is also used as an ingredient in cosmetic products due to its moisturizing and emollient properties.

10. Oleanolic acid

Simmondsia chinensis oleanolic acid is a type of triterpenoid compound extracted from the seeds of the jojoba plant.

It has been studied for its potential therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities.

Oleanolic acid has been shown to have protective effects on liver and kidney function, as well as anti-tumor and anti-diabetic properties, making it a promising compound for the treatment of various diseases.
