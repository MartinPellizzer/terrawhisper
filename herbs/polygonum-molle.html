Polygonum molle

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum molle?

Polygonum molle belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Caryophyllales. It is further categorized into the family Polygonaceae, falling under the genus Polygonum, and is specifically identified by the species name molle.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum molle has slender, erect stems that reach heights of 10 to 50 centimeters and feature smooth, reddish-tinted surfaces with occasional fine hairs. The leaves are simple, lanceolate to narrowly ovate in shape, and arranged alternately along the stem. Each leaf possesses a blunt or slightly pointed apex and an entire margin, often accompanied by a small, ocreate stipule at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pinkish flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. These blooms eventually develop into small, dry achenes that facilitate the species' reproduction.
