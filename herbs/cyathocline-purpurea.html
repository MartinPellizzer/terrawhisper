Cyathocline purpurea

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyathocline purpurea?

Cyathocline purpurea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling under the genus Cyathocline, and is specifically identified by its species name, purpurea.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Cyathocline purpurea targets inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and various lipoxygenases to suppress the production of pro-inflammatory eicosanoids. These bioactive compounds inhibit the signaling pathways of nuclear factor kappa B (NF-κB), which prevents the transcription of cytokines that drive systemic inflammation. By modulating these specific protein interactions, the plant helps reduce cellular swelling and oxidative stress in damaged tissues. The mechanism also involves the regulation of nitric oxide synthase (NOS) to control vasodilation and immune responses. This multi-target approach allows the plant to exert its medicinal effects across several biological systems.

      What are the herbal preparations of Cyathocline purpurea?

      Cyathocline purpurea is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic application. The dried roots are frequently boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction used for internal consumption. For milder effects, the flowering tops are steeped in hot liquid to produce a soothing infusion. Additionally, the crushed fresh leaves are applied directly to the skin as a poultice to treat localized inflammation. These various methods allow practitioners to extract the specific phytochemicals required for different medicinal needs.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cyathocline purpurea can cause skin irritation, localized redness, and mild swelling upon direct contact with the epidermis. These dermatological reactions typically occur when the plant's sap touches sensitive areas of the body. Some individuals may also experience respiratory discomfort if they inhale pollen or airborne particles from the blossoms. Allergic sensitivities can vary significantly between different people depending on their specific immune responses. It is advisable to wear protective clothing when handling this species to avoid these adverse effects.
