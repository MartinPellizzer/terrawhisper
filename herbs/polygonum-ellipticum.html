Polygonum ellipticum

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum ellipticum?

Polygonum ellipticum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Caryophyllales and the family Polygonaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its genus, Polygonum, and its specific species designation, ellipticum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum ellipticum has prostrate, creeping stems that branch from the base and produce small, elliptical leaves with entire margins. The leaves are typically opposite and range in shape from narrowly lanceolate to broadly elliptic. Small, inconspicuous white or pinkish flowers emerge from the leaf axils in small clusters. These flowers feature five petals and are often surrounded by a membranous ocrea at the nodes. The plant develops small, dry achenes that serve as its primary fruit.
