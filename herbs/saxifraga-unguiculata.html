Saxifraga unguiculata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga unguiculata has a basal rosette of fleshy, spatulate leaves characterized by thick, succulent margins and a slightly hairy texture. These leaves are typically dark green and grow close to the ground to conserve moisture in its rocky habitat. From the center of this rosette, a slender, upright flowering stem emerges to support small clusters of blossoms. Each flower features five white petals that are distinctively marked with fine reddish or purplish veins. The plant also develops a shallow, fibrous root system that helps it anchor itself within crevices and thin soil layers.

Its distribution is closely tied to moist, shaded environments found at elevations ranging from several thousand feet up to the timberline.       Saxifraga unguiculata is prepared primarily as a decoction, a tincture, or a topical poultice to utilize its bioactive compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Saxifraga unguiculata can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon direct physical contact. While there is limited clinical research regarding its systemic toxicity, handling the plant may lead to redness or itching on the hands. Some people might experience localized swelling if the sap comes into contact with broken skin. It is generally advised to avoid ingesting any part of the plant because its internal chemical composition is not fully documented for human consumption.
