Dalbergia millettii

What's the taxonomical classification of Dalbergia millettii?

Dalbergia millettii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, it is identified as belonging to the genus Dalbergia, with the specific epithet millettii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dalbergia millettii contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, terpenoids, and stilbenoids that provide significant therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's recognized antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant activities. Research indicates that extracts from the bark and leaves can inhibit the growth of certain pathogenic bacteria and fungi. Additionally, the presence of specific phenolic compounds helps in neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. Such pharmacological potential makes the species a subject of interest for developing new medicinal treatments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dalbergia millettii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, diarrhea, dysentery, and various inflammatory conditions. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that serve as potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory agents. In traditional medicine, extracts are often applied topically to accelerate the healing process of sores and dermatological issues. Oral preparations are sometimes utilized to soothe gastrointestinal distress and manage intestinal ailments. These diverse medicinal applications highlight the plant's significant therapeutic potential in herbal pharmacology.
