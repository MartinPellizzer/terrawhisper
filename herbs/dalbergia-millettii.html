Dalbergia millettii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia millettii has a medium-sized deciduous tree habit characterized by a straight bole, spreading crown, and pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of leaflets. The bark is typically greyish and becomes fissured or flaky as the tree matures. Its compound leaves feature ovate to elliptic leaflets that are often slightly asymmetrical at the base. Small, fragrant flowers appear in axillary panicles, which eventually develop into flattened, hairy pods. These woody legumes contain several hard seeds that facilitate the plant's reproductive cycle.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It is primarily found in deciduous and semi-evergreen forests located within these subtropical zones. The species typically thrives in low to medium altitude areas where seasonal rainfall is consistent. Its distribution follows the humid climates characteristic of the Indo-Malayan botanical realm. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Dalbergia millettii targets various enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase (COX), lipoxygenase (LOX), and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), to exert its anti-inflammatory effects. The plant contains bioactive phytochemicals such as flavonoids and terpenoids that inhibit these specific proteins to reduce prostaglandin and nitric oxide production. These molecular interactions contribute to the plant's ability to modulate the inflammatory cascade within cellular pathways. What are the therapeutic actions of Dalbergia millettii?

Dalbergia millettii has therapeutic actions that include being an astringent, hemostatic, anti-inflammatory, vulnerary, and antipyretic agent. Its astringent and hemostatic properties allow it to effectively constrict tissues and control bleeding when applied to wounds. As a vulnerary, the plant promotes the healing of damaged skin and helps repair various cutaneous lesions. The anti-inflammatory qualities work to reduce swelling and alleviate pain in affected areas of the body. Additionally, its antipyretic effects make it useful for reducing high body temperatures during a fever.

Dalbergia millettii is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, or pastes derived from its bark, leaves, and heartwood. What side-effects this plant can have?

Dalbergia millettii can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling when the wood or extracts come into direct contact with sensitive areas. Some individuals may experience itching or redness due to the presence of specific chemical compounds found within the plant's bark and heartwood. Inhalation of fine dust particles during woodworking can also lead to respiratory discomfort or allergic reactions in the lungs. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented in humans, cautious handling is recommended to avoid these dermatological and respiratory issues. Regular use of protective gear is advised to minimize the risk of developing a heightened sensitivity to the plant.
