Dalbergia millettii

What's the taxonomical classification of Dalbergia millettii?

Dalbergia millettii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, it is identified as belonging to the genus Dalbergia, with the specific epithet millettii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dalbergia millettii has a medium-sized deciduous tree habit characterized by a straight bole, spreading crown, and pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of leaflets.

The bark is typically greyish and becomes fissured or flaky as the tree matures. Its compound leaves feature ovate to elliptic leaflets that are often slightly asymmetrical at the base. Small, fragrant flowers appear in axillary panicles, which eventually develop into flattened, hairy pods.

These woody legumes contain several hard seeds that facilitate the plant's reproductive cycle.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Dalbergia millettii contains medicinal properties primarily derived from its bark, leaves, and heartwood. The bark is frequently harvested to prepare decoctions used in treating various inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. Extracts from the leaves are often utilized for their potential antimicrobial and antioxidant activities in traditional remedies. Additionally, the dense heartwood is valued in certain cultures for its specific therapeutic applications. These various plant components are processed into different forms depending on the intended medicinal use.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including flavonoids, terpenoids, phenolics, alkaloids, and steroids.

These bioactive molecules contribute to the various therapeutic properties observed in its traditional uses. The presence of phenolics and flavonoids provides significant antioxidant activity to protect cells from damage. Additionally, the alkaloids and terpenoids may offer antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

Steroids within the plant structure further support its complex pharmacological profile.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Dalbergia millettii targets various enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase (COX), lipoxygenase (LOX), and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), to exert its anti-inflammatory effects. The plant contains bioactive phytochemicals such as flavonoids and terpenoids that inhibit these specific proteins to reduce prostaglandin and nitric oxide production. These molecular interactions contribute to the plant's ability to modulate the inflammatory cascade within cellular pathways. Additionally, certain compounds from the species may interact with antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase to combat oxidative stress. By targeting these specific proteins, the plant provides therapeutic relief for inflammatory and oxidative conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dalbergia millettii?

Dalbergia millettii has therapeutic actions that include being an astringent, hemostatic, anti-inflammatory, vulnerary, and antipyretic agent.

Its astringent and hemostatic properties allow it to effectively constrict tissues and control bleeding when applied to wounds. As a vulnerary, the plant promotes the healing of damaged skin and helps repair various cutaneous lesions. The anti-inflammatory qualities work to reduce swelling and alleviate pain in affected areas of the body.

Additionally, its antipyretic effects make it useful for reducing high body temperatures during a fever.

The primary therapeutic actions of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Astringent
  • Hemostatic
  • Anti-inflammatory
  • Vulnerary
  • Antipyretic

What are the herbal preparations of Dalbergia millettii?

Dalbergia millettii is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, or pastes derived from its bark, leaves, and heartwood. Traditional practitioners often boil the bark in water to extract bioactive compounds for medicinal teas. The dried leaves can be crushed into a fine powder to be mixed with honey or other vehicles for consumption. Topical applications frequently involve making a paste from the wood to treat skin ailments. These various forms allow the plant's properties to be administered both internally and externally depending on the specific ailment.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dalbergia millettii can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling when the wood or extracts come into direct contact with sensitive areas. Some individuals may experience itching or redness due to the presence of specific chemical compounds found within the plant's bark and heartwood. Inhalation of fine dust particles during woodworking can also lead to respiratory discomfort or allergic reactions in the lungs. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented in humans, cautious handling is recommended to avoid these dermatological and respiratory issues. Regular use of protective gear is advised to minimize the risk of developing a heightened sensitivity to the plant.
