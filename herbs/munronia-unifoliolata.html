Munronia unifoliolata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Munroia unifoliolata has simple, single-veined leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem and are characterized by their distinctly unilateral arrangement. The leaf blades are typically oblong or lanceolate in shape with entire margins. These leaves are relatively small and possess a texture that can range from thin to slightly coriaceous. The stems are woody and provide a sturdy framework for the foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils to complete the plant's structural profile.
