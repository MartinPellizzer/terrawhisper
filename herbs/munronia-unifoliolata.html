Munronia unifoliolata

What's the taxonomical classification of Munronia unifoliolata?

Munroonia unifoliolata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Sapindales and the family Meliaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Munroonia, with the specific designation of the species unifoliolatas.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Munroia unifoliolata has simple, single-veined leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem and are characterized by their distinctly unilateral arrangement.

The leaf blades are typically oblong or lanceolate in shape with entire margins. These leaves are relatively small and possess a texture that can range from thin to slightly coriaceous. The stems are woody and provide a sturdy framework for the foliage.

Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils to complete the plant's structural profile.
