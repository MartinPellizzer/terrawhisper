Common Teucrium

Teucrium marum

Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its aromatic leaves and small pink or purple flowers, and it has been traditionally used for its therapeutic properties, particularly in treating respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis.

Taxonomy

Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) is a species belonging to the genus Teucrium in the family Labiatae, within the kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) is a perennial herb with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are hairy on the underside, and small, pink to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

Distribution

Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) is native to southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, with a range extending into parts of the Middle East and the Mediterranean region.

Traditional Uses

Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to alleviate digestive issues, reduce inflammation, and support respiratory health.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Native American herbalism, Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) has been used for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and digestive properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) is characterized by the presence of iridoid glycosides, particularly aucubin, along with essential oils containing monoterpene derivatives and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Common Teucrium (Teucrium marum) exhibits antispasmodic, anti-inflammatory, and mild sedative properties, making it useful in traditional herbal medicine for digestive disorders, respiratory conditions, and nervous system support.

Botanical Identification

Teucrium marum, commonly known as Common Teucrium, is a perennial herb in the Lamiaceae family. Native to Europe and western Asia, it has regional names like Creeping Germander. It features square stems, aromatic leaves, and small blue or purple flowers. Synonyms include Teucrium polium and Germander, often used in herbal texts.
