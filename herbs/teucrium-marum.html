Teucrium Marum: What To Know Before Using It For Medicinal Purposes

Teucrium marum, commonly known as creeping teucrium or caraway weed, has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties in various herbal remedies.

It contains essential oils, tannins, and flavonoids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects. Historically, it has been employed to treat digestive ailments such as indigestion and gastritis due to its carminative properties. The plant is also used in herbal medicine to alleviate respiratory conditions like coughs and sore throats.

However, its use should be approached with caution, as it may cause skin irritation in some individuals.
