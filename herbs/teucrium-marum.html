What is Common Teucrium (Teucrium Marum)?

Common Teucrium, or Teucrium marum, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal medicine.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Common Teucrium is Teucrium marum. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, family Labiatae, genus Teucrium, and species Teucrium marum. It is known as Teucrium marum in Europe and as Teucrium marum in North America. Common names include "Common Teucrium," "Herb of the Mountain," and "St. John's Wort." Synonyms for this plant include Teucrium polium and Teucrium fruticosum.

Plant Description and Morphology

Common Teucrium is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, with a spreading, compact structure.

Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated along the edges, and have a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls along the stems, displaying shades of violet to purple. The root system consists of fibrous roots that anchor the plant and aid in nutrient absorption.

The plant has a mild, aromatic scent, with leaves that are rough to the touch and have a slightly bitter taste.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Common Teucrium is native to southern Europe, North Africa, and parts of the Middle East, with a notable presence in regions such as Italy, Spain, and the Mediterranean Basin.

It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich areas. The plant prefers full sun to partial shade and is commonly associated with dry, open woodlands and grasslands. It is well adapted to Mediterranean climates, where it can withstand mild winters and hot, dry summers.
