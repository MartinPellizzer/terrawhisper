Desmodium

Desmodium adscendens

Desmodium (Desmodium adscendens) is a member of the Fabaceae family, traditionally used in various regions for its anti-inflammatory properties, as a tonic for digestive health, and as a treatment for malaria. The leaves and stems are commonly harvested and prepared for use in traditional medicine.

Botanical Identification

Desmodium adscendens is a species of legume in the Fabaceae family. Commonly known as climbing desmodium, it is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant has trifoliate leaves, yellow flowers, and a climbing habit, often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits. Synonyms include Desmodium polystachyum and Desmodium incanum in some regional texts.

Active Compounds

Desmodium adscendens contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for treating fever and inflammation.

Powder: Used for digestive disorders and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

Desmodium adscendens is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Individuals with gastrointestinal issues should use it with caution.
