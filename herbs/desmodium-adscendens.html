Desmodium adscendens

Desmodium (Desmodium adscendens) is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical regions, known for its traditional use in treating various health conditions, particularly inflammation and digestive disorders.

Botanical Identification

Desmodium adscendens is a species of legume in the Fabaceae family. Commonly known as climbing desmodium, it is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant has trifoliate leaves, yellow flowers, and a climbing habit, often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits. Synonyms include Desmodium polystachyum and Desmodium incanum in some regional texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Desmodium
  • Species: Desmodium adscendens

Safety Profile

Desmodium adscendens is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Individuals with gastrointestinal issues should use it with caution.
