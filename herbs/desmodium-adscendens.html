Scientific and Botanical Profile

Climbing Desmodium, with botanical name Desmodium adscendens, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Native to Eastern Africa, Southern Africa, Madagascar, Seychelles, Comoros, Tanzania, Kenya, Uganda, and South Africa, it is also referred to as Velvet Bean, Babylonia Grass, Coral Bean, Common Desmodium, Sicklepod, Whiteweed, Whistling Chickpea, Babylonian Trefoil, Bababoon Pea, Bermuda Clover, Climbing Sicklepod, Babylonia Bean, Coral Clover, Desmodium Vine, Spike Desmodium, Climbing Desmodium, Red Bean, Desmodium, and Velvet Bean. Morphologically, it is a perennial herb with trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape, and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruits are slender, curved pods that contain several elliptical seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.

Forms, Preparation and Usage

Desmodium adscendens has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits, and it is available in various forms including fresh leaves, dried tincture, powder, essential oil, and capsule. To prepare the plant, it can be made into a tea by steeping dried leaves in hot water, or a decoction by boiling the plant material for a longer period. An infusion can also be made by soaking the dried leaves in cold water, and the plant is sometimes applied topically as a poultice or ointment. For adults, the recommended dosage is typically 1-2 grams of dried leaves per cup of water, taken up to three times daily, while children should only use it under medical supervision. Due to limited scientific evidence, it is advisable to use desmodium adscendens sparingly and for short durations, consulting a healthcare professional before prolonged use.

Safety, Side Effects and Contraindications

Desmodium adscendens can be used as a medicinal plant with potential benefits for various health conditions, but its safety profile requires careful consideration. While generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, it may cause mild side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, nausea, or allergic reactions in some individuals. It may interact with certain medications, including anticoagulants and diuretics, potentially increasing the risk of adverse effects. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid its use due to insufficient data on its safety during these periods, and individuals with chronic illnesses should consult a healthcare provider before use. To ensure safe use, it is advisable to consult a qualified herbalist or healthcare professional, start with low doses, and monitor for any adverse reactions.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Desmodium adscendens grows best in well-drained, loamy soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5, in full sun to partial shade, and requires regular watering to maintain consistent moisture without waterlogging. It thrives in warm, tropical to subtropical climates and benefits from organic mulch to retain soil moisture and suppress weeds. For optimal growth, regular weeding, pruning of lower branches to encourage upward growth, and periodic fertilization with a balanced NPK fertilizer are recommended. The plant is typically harvested when the leaves are fully developed but before flowering, using shears to cut the stems just above the node to ensure regrowth; this is usually done in the early morning to minimize stress on the plant. After harvesting, the leaves and stems should be thoroughly dried in a shaded, well-ventilated area to preserve their medicinal potency, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dry place or refrigerated to extend shelf life.
