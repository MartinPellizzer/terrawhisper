Elaeagnus oxycarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Elaeagnus oxycarpa?

Elaeagnus oxycarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. Within this order, it is a member of the family Elaeagnaceae. Its specific taxonomic identity is further defined by its placement in the genus Elaeagnus, ultimately identifying it as the species oxycarpa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Elaeagnus oxycarpa contains bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins that provide therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's leaves, bark, and fruits. Specifically, the presence of flavonoids and tannins suggests significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory capabilities. Researchers often study these compounds for their potential to combat oxidative stress and microbial infections. Such medicinal value makes the plant a subject of interest in ethnobotanical studies and pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Elaeagnus oxycarpa?

Elaeagnus oxycarpa has medicinal properties that primarily involve its use as an antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant agent to treat various ailments. The plant contains bioactive compounds that help reduce oxidative stress and protect cells from damage. Extracts from the leaves and bark are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to manage infections and soothe skin irritations. Furthermore, certain parts of the plant are believed to aid in digestive health and support immune function. These diverse therapeutic actions make it a valuable resource in ethnobotanical studies.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat malaria, intestinal worms, dysentery, and various types of skin infections. The leaves and bark are often prepared into decoctions or infusions to combat parasitic infections in the digestive tract. Its medicinal properties are also frequently applied topically to soothe inflammatory skin conditions and promote healing. In some traditional practices, the plant serves as a remedy for managing feverish symptoms associated with malarial infections. This versatile botanical resource plays a significant role in community-based herbal medicine.
