Dalbergia yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dalbergia yunnanensis?

Dalbergia yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Fabales. The plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, falling under the genus Dalbergia, and is specifically identified by the species name yunnanensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dalbergia yunnanensis contains bioactive flavonoids, neoflavonoids, and various polyphenolic compounds that serve as its primary medicinal constituents. These phytochemicals are often extracted from the plant's heartwood and bark to utilize their potent antioxidant properties. Research suggests that these specific compounds may exhibit significant anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities in biological systems. Furthermore, the presence of specific tannins contributes to its traditional use in treating various skin ailments and promoting wound healing. Ongoing studies aim to further isolate these molecules to understand their potential role in modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dalbergia yunnanensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, digestive disorders, skin ailments, and respiratory issues. It is frequently employed in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of rheumatism and joint pain. The plant's components are also utilized to soothe stomach aches and promote healthy digestion. Additionally, topical applications of the plant can help manage skin infections and minor wounds. Its properties are sometimes leveraged to calm coughs and ease congestion in the lungs.
