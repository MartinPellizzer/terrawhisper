What's the taxonomical classification of Hymenocallis littoralis?

Hymenocallis littoralis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic order Asparagales. This plant is further categorized under the family Amaryllidaceae, falling into the genus Hymenocallis, with the specific species designation littoralis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hymenocallis littoralis has long, linear, strap-shaped leaves that grow in a basal rosette and large, funnel-shaped white flowers characterized by six delicate, petal-like tepals and a prominent central staminal cup. The foliage typically reaches heights of twelve to eighteen inches and maintains a deep green color throughout the growing season. Each inflorescence consists of a terminal umbel that can hold several fragrant blossoms simultaneously. The tubular base of each flower leads into the central structure which serves as a primary identifying feature of the species. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in moist, coastal environments.
