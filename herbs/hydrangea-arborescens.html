Smooth hydrangea

Hydrangea arborescens

Smooth Hydrangea (Hydrangea arborescens) is a flowering plant native to North America, known for its large, showy white or pink flower clusters and its traditional use in herbal medicine to support urinary tract health and alleviate symptoms of urinary tract infections.

Botanical Identification

Smooth Hydrangea, scientifically known as Hydrangea arborescens, belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family. It is also called Panicled Hydrangea and is a deciduous perennial native to eastern North America. The plant features large, rounded white or pink flower clusters, with opposite, ovate leaves and fibrous, shallow roots. It is commonly referenced in herbal texts under various regional names.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Saxifragales
  • Family: Hydrangeaceae
  • Genus: Hydrangea
  • Species: Hydrangea arborescens

Safety Profile

Smooth Hydrangea (Hydrangea arborescens) is generally considered safe when used as a herbal remedy. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
