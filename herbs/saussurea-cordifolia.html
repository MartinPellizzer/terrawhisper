Saussurea cordifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea cordifolia?

Saussurea cordifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. At the most specific taxonomic levels, it is identified under the genus Saussurea, with its unique designation being the species cordifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea cordifolia has a perennial life cycle characterized by a thick, fleshy taproot and basal leaves that are cordate in shape with dentate margins. The foliage typically grows in a rosette pattern, featuring leathery textures that help the plant survive high-altitude environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, many-flowered capitulum supported by a long, erect scape. The individual florets are small and tucked within a complex arrangement of involucral bracts. These specialized morphological traits allow the species to withstand the extreme cold and intense solar radiation of its alpine habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine zones of the Himalayas, spanning across several countries including India, Nepal, Bhutan, Tibet, and parts of China. It typically flourishes in rocky crevices and scree slopes at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters above sea level. The species is specifically adapted to the harsh, cold environments found in these mountainous regions. Its distribution is strictly limited by these extreme climatic conditions and specific soil requirements.
