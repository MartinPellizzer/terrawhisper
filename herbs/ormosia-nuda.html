Ormosia nuda

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ormosia nuda has a woody structure characterized by a small to medium-sized tree habit, glabrous stems, and pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of leaflets. The leaves are typically arranged alternately and feature elliptical to oblong leaflets with entire margins. Its inflorescences are often small and axillary, bearing flowers that lead to the development of distinctive seed pods. These pods are dehiscent and contain seeds that are famously bright red or orange with a black hilum. The overall vegetative morphology is defined by its smooth bark and a lack of dense pubescence on most surfaces.

Its range is strictly limited to these specific Neotropical biomes, making it an endemic element of the South American flora.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ormosia nuda can cause severe poisoning through the ingestion of its toxic seeds, which contain high concentrations of the lectin known as abrin. This potent toxin disrupts cellular protein synthesis, potentially leading to intense abdominal pain, vomiting, and bloody diarrhea. If a significant amount is consumed, the symptoms can escalate to organ failure, seizures, and even death. Because the toxins are so lethal, there is no specific antidote available to treat an overdose. Medical intervention focuses primarily on supportive care to manage the systemic effects of the poisoning.
