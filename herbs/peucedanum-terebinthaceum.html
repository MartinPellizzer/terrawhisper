Peucedanum terebinthaceum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Peucedanum terebinthaceum has a perennial rootstock that produces erect, hollow stems reaching heights of up to 100 centimeters with leaves that are pinnately divided into narrow, linear segments. The foliage is characterized by a distinct bluish-green hue and a fine, dissected texture. Its inflorescence consists of large, compound umbels composed of numerous small, white flowers. Each individual flower features five petals and is held within a structure of spreading bracts. The plant's overall structure is upright and herbaceous, typical of many members of the Apiaceae family.
