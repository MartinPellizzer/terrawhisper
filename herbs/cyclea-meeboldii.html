Cyclea meeboldii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyclea meeboldii has woody climbing vines characterized by cordate or ovate leaves with entire margins and prominent venation. The foliage typically displays a glossy texture and can grow in dense clusters along its supporting structures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often featuring a subtle yellowish or greenish hue. The reproductive structures include small fruits that develop from the fertilized blossoms. These morphological traits are essential for identifying the species within its tropical forest habitat.
