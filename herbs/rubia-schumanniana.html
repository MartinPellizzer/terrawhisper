Rubia schumanniana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubia schumanniana has a perennial, climbing habit characterized by slender, quadrangular stems that utilize small, opposite leaves and axillary bristles to scramble over surrounding vegetation. The leaves are typically arranged in whorls of four, appearing ovate to lanceolate with entire margins and acute tips. Small, tubular flowers emerge in dense cymes, often presenting a pale or white coloration during their blooming period. The fruit develops into a small, fleshy drupe that transitions in color as it matures. This morphological structure allows the plant to effectively navigate through complex forest undergrowth.
