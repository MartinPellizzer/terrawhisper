Polystichum acutidens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polystichum acutidens has pinnate fronds featuring narrow, lanceolate pinnae with serrated margins and distinctively pointed tips. The texture of the foliage is leathery and robust, providing a dense appearance to the overall fern structure. Each leaflet displays a prominent central vein that extends toward the apex of the blade. The underside of the fronds is characterized by small, circular sori arranged in neat rows beneath the protective indusia. These reproductive structures are clustered to facilitate efficient spore dispersal in its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the eastern Himalayan range, specifically found within the moist temperate forests of Nepal and Bhutan. It thrives in narrow ecological niches characterized by high humidity and constant mist. Its populations are often restricted to specific elevations between 2,500 and 3,500 meters above sea level. Because it requires such specialized environmental conditions, its distribution remains fragmented across different mountain ridges. Localized weather patterns and soil composition significantly influence where individual specimens can survive.
