Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia) Compounds

Tongkat Ali contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids. These compounds contribute to its pharmacological properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Other notable components are saponins, phenolic acids, and glycosides, which support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in enhancing vitality and immune function. These compounds may also play a role in modulating cellular processes related to stress and metabolic health.

2. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound found in Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in enhancing vitality and supporting male health. Alkaloids in Tongkat Ali are believed to interact with hormonal pathways, though research continues to clarify their exact mechanisms.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia). These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in enhancing vitality and supporting overall health. They are known for their potential adaptogenic properties and are among the key constituents responsible for the plant's medicinal effects.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia). It contributes to the plant's traditional use for enhancing stamina and sexual health. Saponins also possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support hormonal balance. This compound is known to enhance physical performance and reduce stress in users. Its presence plays a crucial role in the therapeutic effects of Tongkat Ali.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in enhancing vitality and supporting hormonal balance. Glycosides are known for their potential adaptogenic properties, which help the body manage stress and promote overall well-being.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in enhancing vitality and reducing stress. Coumarin exhibits antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

8. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia) and contributes to its medicinal properties. It has astringent effects and may support immune function and wound healing. Tannins in Tongkat Ali are also believed to enhance the plant's traditional uses in boosting vitality and reducing inflammation.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia). It contains volatile compounds like sesquiterpenes and monoterpenes, which contribute to its stimulating and adaptogenic properties. The essential oil is extracted through steam distillation and is used in traditional medicine for its purported energy-boosting effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports overall health by enhancing cellular protection and potentially improving immune function.
