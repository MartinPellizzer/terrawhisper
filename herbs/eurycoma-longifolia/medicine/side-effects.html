10 Possible Side Effects Of Eurycoma Longifolia

Eurycoma longifolia has some side effects when used improperly, such as insomnia, anxiety, and jitteriness due to excessive caffeine-like properties.

These side effects can worsen lives by disrupting daily routines, affecting work or school performance, and straining relationships.

Consuming high doses or combining it with stimulants can exacerbate these issues, making it essential to use this herb in moderation and under proper guidance.

1. Causes nausea

Eurycoma longifolia causes nausea due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and alkaloids.

The excessive production of these compounds in the body can lead to stomach upset and nausea. Additionally, Eurycoma longifolia's ability to stimulate digestive enzymes and alter gut motility may also contribute to this side effect.

As a result, some individuals may experience mild to moderate nausea after consuming the extract or supplements containing Eurycoma longifolia.

2. Increases anxiety

Eurycoma longifolia increases anxiety in some individuals due to its potential interaction with the brain's neurotransmitter systems.

The plant's active compounds, such as eurypeptides and quassinoids, may affect the levels of serotonin, dopamine, and GABA, leading to increased stress and anxiety responses.

This may be exacerbated by the stimulatory effects of Eurycoma longifolia on the central nervous system, potentially causing individuals to feel more jittery and agitated.

3. Enhances irritability

Eurycoma longifolia enhances irritability in some individuals due to its stimulating effects on the nervous system.

The herb's active compounds, including eurycomanone and eurypephrin, can increase adrenaline levels and stimulate the release of neurotransmitters such as dopamine and serotonin, leading to feelings of anxiety and agitation.

Additionally, Eurycoma longifolia may interact with other medications that also affect the brain's neurotransmitter balance, potentially exacerbating irritability.

4. Triggers insomnia

Eurycoma longifolia triggers insomnia due to its stimulating properties, which can interfere with a person's ability to fall asleep.

The herb contains compounds that increase alertness and energy levels, making it challenging for individuals to relax and wind down before bedtime.

Additionally, the stimulating effects of Eurycoma longifolia may disrupt the body's natural sleep-wake cycle, leading to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep throughout the night.

5. Raises blood pressure

Eurycoma longifolia raises blood pressure by increasing the levels of catecholamines such as adrenaline and noradrenaline in the body.

This is due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, which can cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, Eurycoma longifolia may also affect the vasodilation and vasoconstriction processes, leading to a rise in blood pressure.

6. Lowers blood sugar

Eurycoma longifolia lowers blood sugar by inhibiting the activity of alpha-glucosidase enzymes in the gut, which slows down carbohydrate digestion and absorption.

This can lead to reduced glucose uptake in the bloodstream, causing a decrease in blood sugar levels. Additionally, Eurycoma longifolia's flavonoid compounds may also contribute to its hypoglycemic effect by enhancing insulin sensitivity and increasing glucose transport into cells.

As a result, individuals with diabetes or those taking blood-sugar lowering medications should monitor their blood sugar levels closely when using Eurycoma longifolia supplements.

7. Induces headaches

Eurycoma longifolia induces headaches due to its ability to increase adrenaline levels and stimulate the nervous system.

The herb's high concentration of alkaloids, particularly eurypephrine and eurycomanone, can cause blood vessels to constrict, leading to a surge in blood pressure and subsequent headache pain.

Additionally, Eurycoma longifolia's stimulatory effects on the brain may contribute to its headaches-inducing properties, particularly when taken in large doses or without proper tolerance.

8. Creates stomach upset

Eurycoma longifolia creates stomach upset due to its high concentration of alkaloids and other bioactive compounds.

These substances can irritate the digestive tract and disrupt normal gut function, leading to symptoms such as nausea, bloating, and abdominal discomfort.

The root cause is believed to be the plant's natural ability to stimulate digestion and increase gut motility, which can overwhelm sensitive stomachs and cause adverse reactions.

9. Produces allergic reactions

Eurycoma longifolia produces allergic reactions in some individuals.

The allergenic properties are attributed to its unique chemical composition, which includes alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenes. These compounds can stimulate the immune system, leading to an overactive response that manifests as allergic symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

In severe cases, anaphylaxis may occur, requiring immediate attention.

10. Reduces testosterone levels

Eurycoma longifolia reduces testosterone levels by interacting with the 5-alpha-reductase enzyme in the body.

This enzyme plays a crucial role in the production and regulation of testosterone. Eurycoma longifolia's flavonoids and phenolic acids bind to the enzyme, thereby inhibiting its activity and ultimately leading to decreased testosterone levels.

This may be problematic for individuals relying on testosterone supplements or those who are sensitive to changes in hormone levels.
