Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia) Uses

Tongkat Ali is used to treat respiratory infections, asthma, tuberculosis, and common colds. It may help reduce inflammation in conditions like bronchitis and chronic bronchitis. The herb is also believed to alleviate symptoms of cough and influenza due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia) Monograph

1. Respiratory infection

Tongkat Ali has been traditionally used to support respiratory health by enhancing immune function and reducing inflammation. It may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections such as coughing and congestion. Its antimicrobial properties are believed to aid in fighting bacterial and viral pathogens. Studies suggest it may shorten the duration of common colds and other upper respiratory tract infections.

2. Asthma

Tongkat Ali has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It may help reduce inflammation in the airways and improve lung function in asthma patients. Some studies suggest it could enhance bronchodilation, though more research is needed to confirm its effectiveness and safety for asthma treatment.

3. Respiratory infections

Tongkat Ali has been traditionally used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and boosting immune function. It may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections such as coughs and congestion. Its antimicrobial properties are believed to inhibit pathogen growth in the respiratory tract. Studies suggest it may enhance the body's ability to fight off infections more effectively.

4. Tuberculosis

Tongkat Ali has been traditionally used in some Southeast Asian countries to support respiratory health. While not a primary treatment for tuberculosis, it may help reduce symptoms and improve immune function. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties are believed to contribute to its potential role in managing tuberculosis.

5. Common cold

Tongkat Ali is traditionally used to support immune function and may help alleviate symptoms of the common cold by reducing inflammation and enhancing the body's natural defenses. It is often consumed as a tonic during cold seasons to promote recovery and prevent illness. Its effects are believed to stem from its rich content of alkaloids and antioxidants.

6. Bronchitis

Tongkat Ali has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, including in the management of bronchitis. It may help reduce inflammation and ease breathing by acting as an expectorant and bronchodilator. Its alkaloid content, such as quassin, is believed to contribute to these effects, though more research is needed for confirmation.

7. Cough

Tongkat Ali is traditionally used to alleviate cough symptoms by supporting respiratory health and reducing inflammation. It may help loosen mucus and soothe throat irritation, though its efficacy for specific cough types requires further scientific validation. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for chronic or persistent coughs.

8. Chronic bronchitis

Tongkat Ali has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, including in the management of chronic bronchitis. It may help reduce inflammation and mucus production, easing breathing difficulties. Its antioxidant properties may also protect lung tissue from oxidative stress. Clinical studies suggest it could be a complementary therapy for chronic bronchitis when used alongside conventional treatments.

9. Inflammation

Tongkat Ali contains compounds with anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and pain. It may inhibit inflammatory pathways in the body, making it useful for conditions like arthritis and skin inflammation. Research suggests it could support immune regulation and reduce chronic inflammation. Its effects are supported by both traditional use and preliminary scientific studies.

10. Influenza

Tongkat Ali has been studied for its potential to support immune function, which may help in managing symptoms of influenza. It contains compounds that may enhance the body's natural defenses, though more research is needed to confirm its efficacy specifically for influenza treatment.
