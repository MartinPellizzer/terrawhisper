Botanical Identification of Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia)

To identify Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Tongkat Ali, or Eurycoma longifolia, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Simaroubaceae, genus Eurycoma, and species Eurycoma longifolia. This classification places it within the flowering plants and highlights its botanical relationships within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Tongkat Ali is Eurycoma longifolia, first described by the botanist Robert Brown in 1830. It has several synonyms, including Eurycoma macrophylla and Eurycoma utilis. Common names include Tongkat Ali, Pasak Bumi, and Long Leaf. It is also known by regional and historical names such as Kacang Bumi and Jengger Ayam.

Morphological Characteristics

Tongkat Ali is a shrub or small tree with a straight, cylindrical stem. Its root system consists of a thick, fleshy taproot. Leaves are simple, opposite, and have a lanceolate shape with a smooth margin. The inflorescence is a raceme, bearing small yellow flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Tongkat Ali can be distinguished by its unique combination of features, including its woody, branching stems with longitudinal ridges, opposite leaf arrangement, and distinctive yellow flowers in clusters. The fruit is a capsule with three longitudinal ridges, and the roots are thick, fleshy, and often branched, setting it apart from similar species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Tongkat Ali is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia, Indonesia, and Thailand. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at altitudes between 0 and 1,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Tongkat Ali blooms between March and May, producing yellow-orange flowers. Seed formation occurs from June to August. Harvesting typically happens when the roots are mature, usually between September and November, to ensure optimal chemical composition for identification and use in herbal medicine.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Tongkat Ali, or Eurycoma longifolia, is closely related to species within the genus Eurycoma. It belongs to the family Simaroubaceae, sharing characteristics such as compound leaves and medicinal properties with other members of the family. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary links to related tropical plants.
