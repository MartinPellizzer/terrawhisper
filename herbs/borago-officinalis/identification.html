Botanical Identification of Borage (Borago officinalis)

To identify Borage (Borago officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Borage (Borago officinalis) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Borage, or Borago officinalis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Borago, and species Borago officinalis. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the mint family, which is characteristic of many aromatic herbs.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Borage is Borago officinalis, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It has several synonyms, including Borago hirta and Borago maritima. Common names include borage, starflower, and honey flower. It is also known by regional and historical names such as "blueweed" and "devil's herb."

Morphological Characteristics

Borage (Borago officinalis) is an annual herb with a taproot system. It has hairy, erect stems that can grow up to 60 cm tall. Leaves are opposite, ovate, and hairy, with a serrated edge. The inflorescence is a raceme of blue or violet star-shaped flowers. The fruit is a small capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Borage can be distinguished by its distinctive star-shaped blue or purple flowers with five petals, hairy stems, and heart-shaped leaves. It also has a strong, aromatic scent and produces small, spiny fruits. These features help differentiate it from similar plants like comfrey or hemp-nettle.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Borage is native to southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with full sun. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It grows best between 0 and 1,500 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Borage blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during the flowering period when the leaves and flowers are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Borage belongs to the Boraginaceae family, closely related to species like forget-me-nots and hound's tongue. It shares morphological traits such as hairy leaves and blue flowers with its relatives. Its closest species include other Borago species, and it is often grouped within the Boraginaceae genus due to shared genetic and structural features.
