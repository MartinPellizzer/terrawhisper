Borage (Borago officinalis) Uses

Borage is used to treat various skin conditions such as irritation, infections, inflammation, and wounds. It supports wound healing and may help with eczema and burns. Its anti-inflammatory properties make it useful for managing respiratory infections and other inflammatory skin disorders.

Borage (Borago officinalis) Monograph

1. Skin irritation

Borage is used topically to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It contains gamma-linolenic acid, which helps reduce redness and inflammation. Applications include eczema, psoriasis, and insect bites. It is often prepared as a salve or infused oil for direct application.

2. Wound healing

Borage has been traditionally used to promote wound healing due to its high content of gamma-linolenic acid, which reduces inflammation and supports tissue repair. It also contains antioxidants that help prevent infection and accelerate the healing process. Historically, borage oil was applied topically to soothe burns and skin irritations.

3. Skin infections

Borage has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Its leaves and oils contain compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil for conditions like eczema and minor wounds.

4. Skin inflammation

Borage is used topically to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It contains gamma-linolenic acid, which helps reduce redness and irritation. The leaves can be made into a poultice or infused oil for direct application on affected areas. This application is commonly used in herbal medicine for eczema and other inflammatory skin conditions.

5. Skin wounds

Borage has been traditionally used to treat skin wounds due to its anti-inflammatory and mild analgesic properties. The oil from its seeds contains gamma-linolenic acid, which may aid in tissue repair. It is often applied topically as a salve or infused oil to promote healing and reduce inflammation in minor cuts and abrasions.

6. Eczema

Borage (Borago officinalis) has been traditionally used to treat eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and skin-soothing properties. The plant contains gamma-linolenic acid, which may help reduce skin irritation and inflammation. It is often prepared as a topical oil or infused in creams for localized application. Its use in herbal medicine highlights its potential in managing skin conditions like eczema.

7. Skin eczema

Borage (Borago officinalis) has been traditionally used to treat skin eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. The leaves contain gamma-linolenic acid, which helps reduce skin inflammation and irritation. It is often prepared as a poultice or infusion to alleviate symptoms and promote healing in eczema-affected skin.

8. Inflammatory skin conditions

Borage (Borago officinalis) is used topically for inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. The leaves contain gamma-linolenic acid, which helps reduce redness and irritation. It is often applied as a poultice or infused oil to treat eczema, psoriasis, and other skin irritations.

9. Burns

Borage is used topically for burns due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. The leaves and oils contain gamma-linolenic acid, which helps reduce inflammation and promote skin healing. It is often applied as a poultice or infused oil to alleviate pain and speed recovery from minor burns.

10. Respiratory infections

Borage is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing irritation in the airways. Its anti-inflammatory properties may help alleviate symptoms of conditions like bronchitis and coughs. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease breathing and promote healing in respiratory infections.
