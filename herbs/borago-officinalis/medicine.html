What Are The Medicinal Properties of borage (Borago officinalis)?

What are the health benefits of borage?

Borage has health benefits such as providing essential fatty acids, reducing inflammation, and supporting the immune system.

It is a rich source of gamma-linolenic acid (GLA), an omega-6 fatty acid that helps reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of conditions like rheumatoid arthritis. Additionally, borage contains antioxidants that support the immune system and protect the body from damage caused by free radicals.

Furthermore, it has been shown to lower blood pressure and cholesterol levels, promoting cardiovascular health. Borage can be consumed as a tea, supplement, or added to salads and other dishes for its cucumber-like flavor.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before taking borage as a supplement, especially for those who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or taking blood thinners.

What are the active constituents of borage?

The health benefits of borage comes from its active constituents, such as alkaloids, saponins, and phenolic acids.

It also contains essential fatty acids, particularly gamma-linolenic acid (GLA), which is known for its anti-inflammatory properties. Borage is also rich in mucilage, tannins, and resins, making it useful in soothing mucous membranes. Its flavonoid content, including rutin and quercetin, contributes to its antioxidant properties.

These constituents work together to provide various health benefits, such as reducing inflammation, supporting the immune system, and promoting skin health.

What are the medicinal preparations of borage?

Borage has different medicinal preparations, such as teas, capsules, and oils.

The aerial parts of the plant are often used to make tea, which is believed to alleviate symptoms of cold and flu, as well as reduce inflammation. Capsules containing dried borage leaves or seeds are also available and are used to treat respiratory conditions, such as bronchitis and asthma.

Moreover, borage seed oil is rich in gamma-linolenic acid (GLA), an essential fatty acid that has anti-inflammatory properties. As such, borage seed oil is often used to treat skin conditions, like eczema and dermatitis, as well as rheumatoid arthritis.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before using borage as a medicinal preparation, as it can have side effects and interact with certain medications.

What are the possible side effect of using borage improperly?

Improper use of borage increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as stomach discomfort, nausea, and diarrhea.

In some cases, it may also cause allergic reactions, leading to symptoms such as rashes, itching, and swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat. Additionally, borage may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and potentially cause adverse effects.

Long-term use of borage may also lead to liver damage, although this is rare. Pregnant women should avoid using borage, as it may stimulate menstruation and potentially lead to miscarriage.

It is important to consult with a healthcare provider before using borage, especially for those with pre-existing medical conditions or those taking medications.

What precautions to take when using borage medicinally?

Before using borage for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional first, as it can interact with certain medications and have side effects.

Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using borage, as its safety has not been established. People with liver or kidney problems should also avoid using borage, as it may worsen these conditions. Additionally, borage contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic in large amounts, so it is important to use it in moderation and only under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

It is also recommended to use borage that has been processed to remove or reduce the levels of pyrrolizidine alkaloids.
