Botanical Identification of Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium)

To identify Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Willow Herb, or Epilobium angustifolium, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Onagraceae, genus Epilobium, and species angustifolium. This classification reflects its botanical relationships and helps in understanding its ecological and medicinal significance.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Willow Herb is Epilobium angustifolium, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It has several synonyms, including Chenopodium angustifolium and Lepidium angustifolium. Common names include Willow Herb, Narrowleaf Willow Herb, and Narrow-leaved Willow Herb. It is also known by regional and historical names such as Redstem and St. John's Wort in some areas.

Morphological Characteristics

Willow Herb is an upright, herbaceous perennial with a fibrous root system. Its stems are slender, often branched, and covered in fine hairs. Leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface. The inflorescence is a dense spike of flowers, each with five pink or red petals and stamen. The fruit is a small capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Willow Herb can be distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside, bright yellow flowers in dense spikes, and reddish stems. It lacks the sticky hairs found on some similar species and has a distinct, slightly woody base. These features help differentiate it from other Epilobium species and similar plants.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Willow Herb is native to North America, particularly the western United States and Canada. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained soils, often found in moist, open areas. It typically grows at altitudes between 1,000 and 3,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Willow Herb blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the most reliable time to collect the plant is during its flowering period, when key features are most visible.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Willow Herb, Epilobium angustifolium, belongs to the genus Epilobium in the family Onagraceae. It is closely related to other Epilobium species and shares family traits such as opposite leaves and showy flowers. Its phylogenetic position reflects its close ties to other willow herbs within the Onagraceae family.
