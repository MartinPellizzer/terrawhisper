Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium) Compounds

The primary compounds in Willow Herb include flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant activity, while glycosides and saponins support overall plant defenses. Coumarin and terpenoids also play roles in its medicinal profile.

Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe mucous membranes. It is found in the leaves and stems, supporting traditional uses for digestive and skin conditions. Tannins also aid in preserving the plant's medicinal qualities during preparation.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments. This compound helps modulate immune responses and may aid in wound healing.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Glycosides in Willow Herb are often responsible for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin may also support cardiovascular health by promoting blood flow. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of Willow Herb in herbal medicine.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds help reduce swelling and support wound healing. They are commonly found in the plant's leaves and flowers, making them valuable in herbal formulations.

7. Saponin

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium) contains volatile compounds like terpenes and phenols, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is often used in herbal medicine to support respiratory and skin health.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, particularly in reducing inflammation and supporting respiratory health. Alkaloids in this species are known for their mild sedative and antispasmodic effects. They are often used in herbal preparations for their calming and healing properties.

10. Anthocyanin
