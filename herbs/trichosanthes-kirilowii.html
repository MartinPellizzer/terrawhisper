Chinese lantern

Trichosanthes kirilowii

Chinese Lantern (Trichosanthes kirilowii) is a flowering plant native to China, known for its distinctive orange-red, lantern-shaped fruits and its use in traditional Chinese medicine for its therapeutic properties, particularly in improving respiratory conditions and promoting digestion.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Lantern, scientifically known as Trichosanthes kirilowii, belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae. It is also called "Guan Guo" in Chinese and is a perennial vine native to East Asia. The plant has trailing stems, heart-shaped leaves, and bright orange, lantern-like flowers. It is sometimes referred to as "Kiku" in traditional herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Cucurbitales
  • Family: Cucurbitaceae
  • Genus: Trichosanthes
  • Species: Trichosanthes kirilowii

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments due to its expectorant and carminative properties.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic due to its mild laxative and anti-inflammatory effects.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions like coughs and colds due to its antipyretic and expectorant properties.

Safety Profile

Chinese Lantern is contraindicated during pregnancy due to its potential to cause uterine contractions. It may also induce vomiting and should be used with caution in individuals with gastrointestinal sensitivity. Always consult a qualified practitioner before use.
