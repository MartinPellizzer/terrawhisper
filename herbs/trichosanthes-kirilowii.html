Chinese Lantern (Trichosanthes kirilowii)

Chinese Lantern (Trichosanthes kirilowii) is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, native to China, Korea, and Japan. Traditionally, its fruits, seeds, and roots have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, bitter, and expectorant actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, korean traditional medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Flowers
Inflorescences are solitary, terminal, orange-red to yellow, actinomorphic, with 5 lobed calyces and 5 free petals, each petal bearing a prominent yellow midrib.
Stems
Smooth, cylindrical, woody stems with opposite branching, covered in persistent, glandular hairs, exhibiting a climbing growth habit with tendrils arising from leaf axils.
