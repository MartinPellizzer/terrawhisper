Selaginella kouytcheensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selagineella kouytcheensis has a creeping rhizome with small, scale-like leaves arranged in four distinct rows along the stems. The microphylls are lanceolate in shape and possess a characteristic dark green coloration that contrasts with the stem. These tiny leaves are arranged in a strictly quadrangular pattern, which is a defining feature of this lycopod species. The plant exhibits a delicate, branching structure that allows it to spread efficiently across moist substrates. Its morphological complexity is further defined by the arrangement of sporangia located in specialized strobili at the tips of the branches.
