Selaginella kouytcheensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella kouytcheensis?

Selaginella kouytcheensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Following its lineage further, it falls under the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Selaginella and is specifically designated as the species kouytcheensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Selaginella kouytcheensis contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, alkaloids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its pharmacological potential. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that specific extracts from the species may exhibit significant neuroprotective effects by mitigating oxidative stress in cellular models. Additionally, some studies suggest that these medicinal compounds possess antimicrobial activities against certain pathogens. The complex synergy of these phytochemicals makes the plant a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.
