Alisma plantago-aquatica

What's the taxonomical classification of Alisma plantago-aquatica?

Alistma plantago-aquatica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Alismatales and the family Alismataceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Alistma, and its specific species name, plantago-aquatica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Alisma plantago-aquatica contains bioactive secondary metabolites including iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, tannins, alkaloids, and various phenolic compounds. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's recognized anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Specifically, the presence of aucubin and catalpol provides significant medicinal potential in treating various ailments. These compounds also demonstrate antimicrobial activities that can help combat certain pathogens. Such biochemical diversity supports the traditional use of the plant in managing respiratory and digestive issues.

What are the therapeutic actions of Alisma plantago-aquatica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments such as coughs, asthma, and bronchitis, as well as skin irritations including inflammation, wounds, and insect bites. The leaves contain mucilage that acts as a soothing agent for irritated mucous membranes in the throat and lungs. When applied topically, the plant possesses astringent and anti-inflammatory properties that help reduce swelling and speed up healing. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to soothe digestive upsets by calming the lining of the gastrointestinal tract. These diverse medicinal applications make it a versatile remedy for both internal and external conditions.
