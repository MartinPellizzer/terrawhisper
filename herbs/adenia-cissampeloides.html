Adenia cissampeloides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenia cissampeloides has a large, tuberous caudex and climbing stems that bear trifoliate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets. The foliage typically consists of three leaflets that are relatively small and somewhat fleshy in texture. Its stems function as twining vines that allow the plant to scramble over surrounding vegetation. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, often tucked away within the leaf axils. This species is characterized by its specialized succulent structure designed for water storage in arid environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Adenia cissampeloides can cause severe toxicity and potential death if ingested due to the presence of highly toxic cyanogenic glycosides within its tissues. These compounds release hydrogen cyanide when the plant material is processed by the body, leading to rapid cellular suffocation. Symptoms of poisoning may include dizziness, headache, nausea, and extreme confusion. In advanced cases, the toxins can lead to respiratory failure, seizures, or cardiac arrest. Because of these lethal risks, any part of this plant should be handled with extreme caution and never consumed.
