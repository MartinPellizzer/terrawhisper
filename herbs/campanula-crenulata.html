Campanula crenulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Asterales. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Campanula crenulata has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate with crenate or serrated margins and erect, unbranched or sparsely branched stems supporting bell-shaped flowers. Each flower possesses a five-lobed corolla that varies in color from pale blue to deep violet.
