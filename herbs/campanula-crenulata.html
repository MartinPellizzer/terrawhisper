Campanula crenulata

What's the taxonomical classification of Campanula crenulata?

Campanula crenulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Campanulaceae, which falls under the genus Campanula, and is specifically identified by the species name crenulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Campanula crenulata has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate with crenate or serrated margins and erect, unbranched or sparsely branched stems supporting bell-shaped flowers. The foliage typically displays a hairy texture, which serves as a protective layer for the plant's surface. Each flower possesses a five-lobed corolla that varies in color from pale blue to deep violet. The reproductive structures consist of a central pistil surrounded by several stamens tucked within the floral tube. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in rocky, alpine environments.
