10 Possible Side Effects Of Bidens Pilosa

Bidens pilosa has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal problems.

These reactions can be caused by excessive consumption or individual sensitivity.

In severe cases, these side effects can worsen people's lives by leading to anaphylaxis, digestive issues that disrupt daily routines, and skin conditions that affect self-confidence.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Bidens pilosa, also known as Spanish Needle, causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpenes and flavonoids in the plant, which can trigger an immune response and lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

The allergenic compounds in Bidens pilosa can also cause respiratory issues like coughing and sneezing, making it a potential irritant for people with pre-existing allergies or sensitivities.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Bidens pilosa, also known as Spanish Needle, triggers asthma attacks due to its inherent allergenic properties.

The plant's pollen and other particles can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to constriction of airways and increased mucus production, resulting in symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

This reaction is often exacerbated by the release of histamine, a chemical mediator that amplifies the inflammatory response, further worsening respiratory distress.

3. Increases blood pressure

Bidens pilosa, also known as Spanish Needle, increases blood pressure due to its natural ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase adrenaline production.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and saponins, can cause a rapid heartbeat and elevated blood pressure in some individuals.

This effect is thought to be related to the plant's traditional use as a stimulant and energizer in folk medicine, where it has been used to treat fatigue and lethargy.

4. Stimulates heart rate

Bidens pilosa, also known as Spanish Needle, stimulates heart rate due to its ability to increase sympathetic nervous system activity.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as alkaloids and glycosides, can bind to adrenergic receptors in the heart, leading to an increased heart rate and cardiac output.

This effect is thought to be mediated by the plant's stimulatory action on the autonomic nervous system, which regulates various bodily functions including heart rate, blood pressure, and respiration.

5. Produces kidney damage

Bidens pilosa produces kidney damage due to its high concentration of oxalate and uric acid content.

These compounds can accumulate in the kidneys and cause inflammation, leading to potential damage to the delicate filtration units within the kidneys.

Repeated exposure to these toxic substances may eventually result in scarring and permanent loss of renal function, a serious and debilitating condition.

6. Inflames liver tissues

Bidens pilosa, also known as Spanish Needle, inflames liver tissues due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds like alkaloids and flavonoids.

These compounds can cause oxidative stress and inflammation in the liver, leading to cellular damage and potential long-term consequences.

The plant's ability to alter liver metabolism and function may also contribute to this adverse effect, highlighting the importance of understanding the potential risks associated with using Bidens pilosa.

7. Induces seizures

Bidens pilosa, also known as Spanish Needle, induces seizures in some individuals due to its high content of sesquiterpene lactones, which are a type of natural compound that can affect the central nervous system.

These compounds can cause excitotoxicity, leading to an overstimulation of nerve cells and potentially resulting in seizures.

8. Worsens insomnia

Bidens pilosa, also known as Spanish Needle, worsens insomnia due to its high content of alkaloids and flavonoids, which can affect the body's sleep-wake cycles.

The herb has been found to increase alertness and stimulation, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep.

Additionally, the plant's ability to alter heart rate and blood pressure may also contribute to insomnia in some cases.

9. Aggravates anxiety

Bidens pilosa, also known as Spanish Needle, aggravates anxiety due to its potential interactions with neurotransmitters in the brain.

The plant's active compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, may alter serotonin and dopamine levels, leading to increased feelings of unease and apprehension. This can exacerbate existing anxiety disorders or cause new ones to develop in sensitive individuals.

As a result, users may experience heightened states of worry, fear, or restlessness when consuming Bidens pilosa.

10. Lowers white blood count

Bidens pilosa, also known as Spanish Needle, lowers white blood count.

This side effect is likely caused by the plant's ability to stimulate the production of cytokines, which can lead to a suppression of immune function and a decrease in the number of white blood cells.

Additionally, Bidens pilosa contains compounds that may interfere with the bone marrow's ability to produce new white blood cells, further contributing to this side effect.
