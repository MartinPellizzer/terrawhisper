Caryopteris glutinosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Caryopteris glutinosa?

Caryopteris glutinosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by its genus, Caryopteris, and its unique species designation, glutinosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caryopteris glutinosa has woody, multi-stemmed shrub habit with opposite, ovate leaves that are characterized by a distinctively sticky, glandular texture on both surfaces. The foliage typically measures between two and five centimeters in length and possesses serrated margins. During its flowering period, the plant produces terminal clusters of small, tubular, blue to violet flowers. These blossoms emerge from a dense, panicle-like inflorescence that rises above the sticky green foliage. The overall structure is compact, often reaching heights of approximately one meter in mature specimens.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China, specifically within provinces like Hubei, Sichuan, and Yunnan. It typically thrives in higher elevations where the terrain is rocky or well-drained. These populations are often found growing along slopes and in forest margins. While its natural range is restricted to these East Asian highlands, it is occasionally found in cultivation across other temperate parts of the world. Its specific habitat requirements limit its spontaneous spread outside of these primary Asian territories.
