Caryopteris glutinosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Caryopteris glutinosa?

Caryopteris glutinosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by its genus, Caryopteris, and its unique species designation, glutinosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caryopteris glutinosa has woody, multi-stemmed shrub habit with opposite, ovate leaves that are characterized by a distinctively sticky, glandular texture on both surfaces. During its flowering period, the plant produces terminal clusters of small, tubular, blue to violet flowers. These blossoms emerge from a dense, panicle-like inflorescence that rises above the sticky green foliage. The overall structure is compact, often reaching heights of approximately one meter in mature specimens.
