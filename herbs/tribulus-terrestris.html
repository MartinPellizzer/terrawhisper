Tribulus terrestris

What's the taxonomical classification of Tribulus terrestris?

Tribulus terrestris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Zygophyllales and the family Zygophyllaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Tribulus, and its specific species designation, terrestris.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Tribulus terrestris contains saponins, flavonoids, alkaloids, and steroidal glycosides such as protodioscin. These primary bioactive compounds are responsible for much of the plant's pharmacological activity in various studies. Saponins are particularly noted for their potential role in supporting reproductive health and hormonal balance. The presence of flavonoids provides antioxidant properties that help combat oxidative stress in cells. Additionally, the steroidal glycosides are frequently investigated for their impact on physical performance and libido.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tribulus terrestris?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat erectile dysfunction, low libido, infertility, urinary tract infections, and symptoms of benign prostatic hyperplasia. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to support reproductive health and enhance hormonal balance. Some studies suggest it may assist in managing blood pressure and improving cholesterol levels. Practitioners often prescribe it to help alleviate symptoms associated with kidney stones and bladder irregularities. This botanical is widely sought after for its potential to boost physical performance and stamina.
