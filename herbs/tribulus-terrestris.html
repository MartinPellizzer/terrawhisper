Puncture Vine (Tribulus terrestris)

Puncture Vine (Tribulus terrestris) is a member of the Zygophyllaceae family, native to Southern Europe, Middle East, and North Africa. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and flowers have been used for decoctions, powders, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, stimulant, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Annual herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with upper surface glabrous and pale green, lower surface pubescent and darker green, bearing prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are yellow with five stamens and five sepals arranged in a solitary inflorescence, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry and having a five-lobed calyx with recurved lobes.
Stems
Elongated, prostrate stems with opposite branching, covered in fine pubescence, possessing spiny tubercles along the nodes.
