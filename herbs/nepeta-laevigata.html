What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nepeta laevigata has decumbent to ascending square stems that are typically covered in fine, spreading hairs and terminate in dense, verticillate clusters of small, bilabiate flowers. These foliage elements are often characterized by a grayish-green hue due to a coating of trichomes. The floral structure consists of small, tubular corollas that are usually pale lavender or white in color. Small, nutlet-like fruits develop within the calyx following successful pollination.
