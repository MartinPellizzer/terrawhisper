Boehmeria spicata

What's the taxonomical classification of Boehmeria spicata?

Boehmeria spicata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and placed in the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae, falling under the genus Boehmeria, and is specifically identified by its species name, spicata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Boehmeria spicata has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and leaves that are oppositely arranged with ovate to lanceolate blades and serrated margins. The foliage often features long, tapering tips and prominent venation that extends toward the edges. Its reproductive structures consist of dense, spike-like inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils or the stem tips. These spikes are composed of numerous small, inconspicuous flowers that lack showy petals. The plant's overall structure is defined by its slender, upright growth and the clustered arrangement of its flowering spikes.
