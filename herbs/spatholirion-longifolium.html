Spatholirion longifolium

Spatholirioon longifoiliun belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Commelinaales and the family Commelinaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Spatholirioon, and its species designation, longifoiliun.

Spatholirion longifoliuum is prepared as a decoction, a topical poultice, or an infused oil to treat various ailments. These different methods allow the plant's active properties to be delivered according to the specific healing need.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Spatholirion longifoliuum can cause skin irritation, eye inflammation, and gastrointestinal distress if the plant is ingested or handled without protection. The foliage contains calcium oxalate crystals that act as tiny needles when they come into contact with sensitive tissues. If these crystals touch the skin, they often trigger redness, itching, or a burning sensation. Swelling of the mouth and throat is a serious risk if a person accidentally eats any part of the plant. Contact with the eyes can lead to intense pain and temporary swelling of the conjunctiva.
