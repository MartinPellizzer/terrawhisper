Botanical Identification of Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia)

To identify Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Russian Silver Bush, Elaeagnus angustifolia, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Elaeagnaceae, genus Elaeagnus, and species Elaeagnus angustifolia. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the Elaeagnaceae family, known for its shrubs with silver foliage.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Russian Silver Bush is Elaeagnus angustifolia, first described by the botanist Carl von Linné. It is also known by several synonyms, including Elaeagnus xanthocarpa and Elaeagnus angustifolia subsp. angustifolia. Common names include silver bush, Russian silver bush, and American silverberry. It is widely recognized in herbal medicine for its therapeutic properties.

Morphological Characteristics

Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia) is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a bushy growth habit. It has a deep, spreading root system that helps it thrive in poor soils. The stems are upright, often multi-branched, and covered in silvery-white hairs. Leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets and a distinctive silvery underside. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small yellow flowers that are highly fragrant. The fruit is a small, reddish-orange drupe containing one or two seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Elaeagnus angustifolia can be distinguished by its silvery-gray leaves with glandular dots, fragrant golden-yellow flowers, and small, reddish-purple fruits. It differs from similar species like Elaeagnus umbellata by its narrower leaves and less branched inflorescences. The bark is also more reddish and has a distinct, aromatic scent.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Elaeagnus angustifolia is native to temperate regions of Asia, including Russia, Central Asia, and parts of China. It thrives in dry, warm climates and prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soils. This shrub typically grows at low altitudes, often in steppe or semi-desert environments.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Russian Silver Bush blooms in early summer, typically between June and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to observe key features is during the flowering period, which is crucial for accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Elaeagnus angustifolia belongs to the Elaeagnus genus within the Elaeagnaceae family. Its closest relatives include species like Elaeagnus umbellata and Elaeagnus ebbingei. It shares traits such as compound leaves and yellow flowers with other members of the family. These relationships help in understanding its ecological and medicinal significance.
