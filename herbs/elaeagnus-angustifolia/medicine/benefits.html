10 Health Benefits Of Elaeagnus Angustifolia (Silverberry)

Elaeagnus angustifolia has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that can improve overall well-being.

The plant's medicinal properties include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes, which have been shown to reduce inflammation and protect against oxidative stress.

1. Reduces inflammation

Elaeagnus angustifolia reduces inflammation because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds.

Its leaves and stems contain flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which have potent anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, thereby reducing inflammation and oxidative stress in the body.

This makes silverberry a valuable natural remedy for the treatment of various inflammatory diseases.

2. Alleviates stomach issues

Elaeagnus angustifolia alleviates stomach issues because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds.

The plant's leaves and seeds contain flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes that have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

Elaeagnus angustifolia boosts immune system because it is rich in antioxidants and flavonoids.

Elaeagnus angustifolia improves cardiovascular health because it is rich in antioxidants and flavonoids that help to reduce inflammation and improve blood vessel function.

Elaeagnus angustifolia reduces blood sugar levels because it contains a unique compound called oleuropein.

This bioactive molecule has been shown to inhibit the activity of alpha-glucosidase enzymes in the small intestine, which slows down the digestion and absorption of carbohydrates, resulting in lower glucose levels.

Additionally, oleuropein has antioxidant properties that help protect against insulin resistance and inflammation, further contributing to its hypoglycemic effects.

10. Relieves respiratory issues

Elaeagnus angustifolia relieves respiratory issues because of its rich source of flavonoids and terpenes.
