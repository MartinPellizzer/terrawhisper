What's the taxonomical classification of Rumex maritimus?

Rumex maritimus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Caryophyllales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Polygonaceae and falls under the genus Rumex, specifically identified by the species epithet maritimus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rumex maritimus has erect, perennial stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and feature basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate with entire or slightly wavy margins. The plant's leaves are often succulent and can exhibit a reddish or purplish tint, especially when exposed to salt spray. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal panicles composed of numerous small, greenish flowers. The fruit is a three-sided achene enclosed within winged valves that facilitate dispersal by water or wind. These specialized structures allow the species to thrive in harsh coastal and saline environments.
