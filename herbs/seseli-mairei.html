Seseli mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Seseli mairei?

Seseli mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Seseli, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species mairei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Seseli mairei has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are broadly ovate to cordate with coarsely serrated margins and an erect, branched flowering stem. Its inflorescence consists of compound umbels that carry numerous small, white flowers clustered together. The stems are relatively stout and can reach significant heights during the blooming season. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds apiin, chrysoeriol, umbelliferone, rutin, and ferulic acid. These specific phytochemicals are responsible for many of the biological activities observed in the species. The presence of flavonoids like rutin and chrysoeriol often contributes to the antioxidant properties of the plant. Additionally, the coumarins umbelliferone and ferulic acid play significant roles in its chemical profile. Such diverse compounds suggest that Seseli mairei may possess various therapeutic potential in pharmacological studies.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", thirteen compounds were isolated and identified from the aerial parts of Seseli mairei Wolf. These chemical constituents include sphondin (I), hergapten (II), isopimpinellin (III), umbelliferone (IV), chrysosptertin B (V), apiin (VI), rutin (VII), quercetin (VIII), ferulic acid (IX), falcarindiol (X), docosanol (XI), beta-sitosterol (XII), and daucosterol (XIII). All 13 of these specific compounds were isolated from the aerial parts of Seseli mairei Wolf for the first time. The structures of these constituents were determined by spectral analysis following separation via manifold chromatography methods.
