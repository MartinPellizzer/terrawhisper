Seseli mairei

What's the taxonomical classification of Seseli mairei?

Seseli mairei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Seseli, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species mairei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Seseli mairei has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are broadly ovate to cordate with coarsely serrated margins and an erect, branched flowering stem.

The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant, providing a lush green foundation. Its inflorescence consists of compound umbels that carry numerous small, white flowers clustered together. The stems are relatively stout and can reach significant heights during the blooming season.

Fine hairs may be present on the stems and leaf surfaces, contributing to the plant's specific texture.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Seseli mairei can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and potential allergic reactions due to the presence of furanocoumarins in its tissues. These chemical compounds are known to make human skin hypersensitive to ultraviolet light. If the plant sap touches the skin and is subsequently exposed to sunlight, it may result in painful blistering or redness. Such a reaction is formally known as phytophotodermatitis and can cause lingering skin discoloration. Individuals with sensitive skin or pre-existing dermatological conditions should exercise extreme caution when handling this species.
