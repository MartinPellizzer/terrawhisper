Clematis chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis chinensis?

Clematis chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Clematis, and is specifically identified by the species name chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis chinensis has woody, climbing stems with opposite, trifoliate leaves that consist of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins. The plant produces large, bell-shaped flowers that typically feature bright yellow or orange tepals with a central cluster of prominent stamens. These blooms emerge in late summer and can reach several inches in diameter during their peak flowering period. The foliage remains lush and green throughout the growing season to support the climbing habit of the vines. As the season progresses, the plant develops feathery, silver-colored seed heads known as achenes which are attached to long, persistent styles.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds clemaphenol A, clematichinenoside A, clematichinenoside B, clematichinenoside C, and hederagenin. These specific chemical constituents are often studied for their various biological activities and therapeutic potential. The clematichinenosides belong to a class of glycosides that are characteristic of this species. Additionally, the presence of hederagenin, a triterpenoid saponin, contributes to the complex chemical profile of the plant. Researchers analyze these substances to better understand how the plant might interact with human physiological systems.

As discussed in a study, the root of Clematis chinensis contains specific chemical components. Researchers used various chromatographic techniques to separate and purify the components of the plant. Two compounds were isolated and elucidated from the root. These were identified as clemaphenol A (I) and dihydro-4-hydroxy-5-hyroxymethy-2(3H)- furanone (II). Both compound I and compound II are new compounds.
