Polystichum omeiense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polystichum omeiense has large, leathery, dark green fronds that are bipinnate to tripinnate in structure with lanceolate leaflets featuring serrated margins. The pinnae are arranged symmetrically along a central rachis that is often covered in fine, brownish scales. Each individual pinnule possesses a distinct midvein and slightly revolute edges that contribute to the plant's dense, textured appearance. The overall texture of the foliage is relatively stiff, allowing the fronds to maintain an upright or spreading habit. These morphological traits are characteristic of ferns adapted to shaded, moist forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Omeishan mountain range in Sichuan Province, China. It grows specifically within high-altitude montane environments characterized by cool and moist climates. Its distribution is highly localized, meaning it is found only in certain pockets of this specific mountainous region.
