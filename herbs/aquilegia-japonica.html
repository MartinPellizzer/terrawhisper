Aquilegia japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Aquilegia japonica?

Aquilegia japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Aquilegia, and is specifically identified by the species epithet japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aquilegia japonica has a herbaceous perennial habit characterized by compound, ternately divided leaves with lobed leaflets and terminal clusters of nodding, spurred flowers that feature blue to violet-colored petals and yellow centers. The foliage is delicate and divided into multiple segments that create a lacy texture throughout the plant. Its stems are typically upright and can reach varying heights depending on the specific environmental conditions. The distinctive nectar spurs extend backward from the base of each petal to facilitate specialized pollination. These flowering structures are held on slender pedicels that allow the blooms to dangle gracefully.
