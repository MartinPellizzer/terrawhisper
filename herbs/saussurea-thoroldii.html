Saussurea thoroldii

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea thoroldii?

Saussurea thoroldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asterales, it is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Saussurea, with its specific designation being the species thoroldii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea thoroldii has a perennial growth habit characterized by a rosette of lanceolate to oblong leaves that feature serrated margins and distinct pinnatifid lobes.

The foliage is typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs that serve to protect the plant from intense high-altitude ultraviolet radiation. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, head-like capitulum located atop a sturdy, hairy scape. The individual florets within the flower head are tubular and often exhibit a pale yellowish or white coloration.

These morphological adaptations allow the species to thrive in the harsh, rocky environments of the alpine zones.
