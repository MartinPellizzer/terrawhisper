Saussurea thoroldii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea thoroldii has a perennial growth habit characterized by a rosette of lanceolate to oblong leaves that feature serrated margins and distinct pinnatifid lobes. The foliage is typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs that serve to protect the plant from intense high-altitude ultraviolet radiation. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, head-like capitulum located atop a sturdy, hairy scape. The individual florets within the flower head are tubular and often exhibit a pale yellowish or white coloration. These morphological adaptations allow the species to thrive in the harsh, rocky environments of the alpine zones.
