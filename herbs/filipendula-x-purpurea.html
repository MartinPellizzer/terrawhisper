Filipendula × purpurea

What's the taxonomical classification of Filipendula × purpurea?

Filipendula × purpurea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Rosales. This plant is a part of the family Rosaceae, which is a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Filipendula, with the specific designation of the species purpurea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Filipendula × purpurea has erect, rhizomatous stems that produce pinnate, compound leaves with serrated leaflets and clusters of small, deep pink to purplish flowers. The foliage consists of multiple leaflets arranged along a central axis, providing a lush, textured appearance to the plant. Its inflorescences are typically dense, terminal panicles that bloom in mid-to-late summer. The stems can reach significant heights, often supporting heavy floral displays that sway in the wind. Each individual flower possesses numerous stamens that add to the overall delicate texture of the bloom.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, Filipendula purpurea, requires moist, well-drained soil and partial shade to thrive in temperate garden environments.

It grows best in humus-rich ground that maintains consistent moisture levels throughout the growing season. Because it prefers cooler temperatures, it should be planted away from intense, direct afternoon sunlight to prevent leaf scorch. Regular watering is essential to prevent the soil from drying out completely, especially during summer months.

Once established, this perennial is relatively hardy and can be propagated through division in early spring or autumn.
