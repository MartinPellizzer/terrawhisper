Cayratia ciliifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Cayratia ciliifera?

Cayratiia ciliifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Vitales and the family Vitaceae, eventually reaching its specific classification within the genus Cayratiia. Finally, the plant is identified at the species level as ciliifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cayratiia ciliifera has a climbing or trailing herbaceous habit characterized by hollow, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

Its leaves are typically cordate or ovate in shape, featuring serrated margins and a distinctively hairy texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. The inflorescence consists of small, greenish-white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. These flowers possess five petals and are followed by small, globose fruits that eventually turn dark as they mature.

The entire plant structure is adapted for spreading across forest floors or climbing through dense vegetation.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Cayratia ciliifera contains bioactive compounds primarily located in its aerial parts, specifically the leaves and roots, which are utilized for their therapeutic properties. Additionally, the roots are harvested to extract chemical constituents used in traditional remedies for digestive issues and fever reduction. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cayratia ciliifera targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines including tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. The plant's bioactive compounds exert their medicinal effects by modulating these specific protein pathways to reduce oxidative stress and systemic inflammation. Phytochemical constituents within the species interact with signal transduction pathways to inhibit the activation of nuclear factor kappa B. These molecular interactions contribute significantly to the plant's observed analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties in pharmacological studies. Furthermore, the modulation of specific enzyme activities helps mitigate cellular damage caused by excessive reactive oxygen species.

Cayratia ciliifera has potent therapeutic properties including diuretic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities.

Furthermore, its antimicrobial effects allow it to combat various pathogenic microorganisms effectively.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal issues like diarrhea and stomach pain, skin infections, inflammation, and respiratory ailments such as coughs.

Indigenous healers often prepare decoctions from the leaves to soothe digestive distress and cleanse the gut. Topical applications of the plant are frequently employed to reduce swelling and heal minor wounds or fungal infections. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to alleviate symptoms of fever and common colds.

These diverse medicinal uses highlight its significant role in regional ethnobotanical traditions.

What are the herbal preparations of Cayratia ciliifera?

Cayratia ciliifera is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice made from its leaves and roots. The leaves are often boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for internal consumption. When used externally, the plant material can be crushed into a paste to be applied directly to the skin. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant in concentrated extracts or macerated oils. These various forms allow the bioactive compounds to be administered for different therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cayratia ciliifera can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if ingested in uncontrolled amounts due to the presence of unidentified bioactive compounds. While some traditional uses suggest medicinal benefits, the lack of standardized clinical data means that side effects are not fully documented for human consumption. Individuals may experience nausea, abdominal pain, or vomiting after consuming preparations of the plant. Topical application might lead to localized allergic reactions or contact dermatitis in sensitive users. Because its chemical composition is not entirely understood, accidental overdose could lead to more severe systemic complications.
