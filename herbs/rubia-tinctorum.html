Madder

Rubia tinctorum

Madder (Rubia tinctorum) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its deep red roots that have been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including skin conditions and digestive issues.

Botanical Identification

Madder is scientifically known as Rubia tinctorum, a member of the Rubiaceae family. Common names include madder root and red root, with regional variations. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has rhizomes, palmately lobed leaves, and small pinkish flowers. It has been referred to by various synonyms in historical herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rubiales
  • Family: Rubiaceae
  • Genus: Rubia
  • Species: Rubia tinctorum

Madder contains key active compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide its therapeutic effects, influencing inflammation, oxidative stress, and skin health through various biochemical pathways.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a mild sedative.

Decoction: Used for treating skin conditions and inflammation.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and as an antiseptic.

Safety Profile

Madder (Rubia tinctorum) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated in pregnant and breastfeeding women due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for long-term or high-dose applications.
