Rubia Tinctorum Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Side Effects of rubia tinctorum

Rubia tinctorum triggers allergic reaction due to the presence of certain compounds such as alkaloids and glycosides that can act as allergens, prompting the immune system to respond with symptoms like hives, itching, or even anaphylaxis in severe cases.

Rubia tinctorum causes skin irritation because its chemical constituents, including tannins and other irritants, can provoke inflammation and redness upon direct contact with the skin.

Rubia tinctorum causes eye irritation as its fine particles or volatile compounds may enter the eyes, leading to redness, tearing, and a burning sensation.

Rubia tinctorum triggers respiratory issues when its airborne particles or pollen are inhaled, potentially causing bronchial constriction, coughing, or asthma-like symptoms in sensitive individuals.
