Pinus canadensis

Pinus canadensis, commonly known as the Eastern White Pine, is a species of evergreen coniferous tree native to eastern North America.

The health benefits of Pinus canadensis include its anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to reduce swelling and ease pain, as well as its antimicrobial properties, which can aid in the treatment of infections.

The therapeutic actions of this plant include its ability to act as an expectorant, helping to relieve respiratory issues such as coughs and bronchitis, and its use as an antiseptic to promote wound healing.

Pinus canadensis contains bioactive constituents such as alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, and terpinen-4-ol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
