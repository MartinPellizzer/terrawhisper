What is Phymatopteris longisquamata?

Phymatopteris longisquamata is a species of fern in the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a terrestrial epiphyte with a rhizome that is creeping, and its fronds are simple, leathery, and lanceolate, with pinnate or pinnatifid pinnae. The frond rachis is glabrous, and the veins are free, with an adaxial vein that is more prominent than the abaxial one. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, and it grows in humid environments.

The specific epithet "longisquamata" refers to the presence of long scales on the rachis and the petiole of the frond.
