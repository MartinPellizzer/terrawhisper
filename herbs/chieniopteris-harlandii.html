What's the taxonomical classification of Chieniopteris harlandii?

Chieniopteris harlandii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, it is situated under the order Polypodiales. This fern is specifically categorized under the family Aspleniaceae, falling into the genus Chieniopteris, with the unique species designation harlandii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chieniopteris harlandii has bipinnate fronds characterized by elongated, lanceolate pinnules that feature distinctively revolute margins and a slightly thickened texture.

The rachis is relatively robust and often exhibits a subtle brownish or reddish tint depending on the maturity of the specimen. Each individual segment displays a pinnatifid architecture, where the lobes are deeply incised but remain connected to the central vein. Small, inconspicuous sori are typically located on the undersides of the pinnules, arranged in a regular pattern along the veins.

The overall structure of the foliage provides a delicate, fern-like appearance while maintaining a sturdy physical presence.
