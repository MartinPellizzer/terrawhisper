What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chieniopteris harlandii has bipinnate fronds characterized by elongated, lanceolate pinnules that feature distinctively revolute margins and a slightly thickened texture. Each individual segment displays a pinnatifid architecture, where the lobes are deeply incised but remain connected to the central vein. Small, inconspicuous sori are typically located on the undersides of the pinnules, arranged in a regular pattern along the veins. The overall structure of the foliage provides a delicate, fern-like appearance while maintaining a sturdy physical presence.
