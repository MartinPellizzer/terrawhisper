    Reineckea carnea can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense abdominal pain, vomiting, and diarrhea, if consumed improperly. These chemical compounds can lead to systemic toxicity if large quantities are ingested by humans or animals. Because its medicinal use is not standardized, there is a significant risk of accidental poisoning. Careful avoidance of all parts of the plant is necessary to prevent these harmful physiological reactions.
