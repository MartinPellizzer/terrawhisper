Reineckea carnea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Reineckea carnea has woody, perennial stems that form a dense, scrambling shrub characterized by thick, coriaceous leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The foliage consists of elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins and a distinctively leathery texture. These leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches. The flowering structures are relatively small and often tucked within the leaf axils. The overall growth habit allows the plant to form complex, intertwined thickets in its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of West and Central Africa, specifically ranging from Sierra Leone and Liberia through to the Congo Basin. The species is frequently documented in countries such as Gabon, Cameroon, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. Its distribution is strictly limited by the necessity for specific soil compositions and high annual rainfall found in these equatorial zones.
