What's the taxonomical classification of Reineckea carnea?

Reineckea carnea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asparagales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Asparagaceae, specifically within the genus Reineckea, which identifies its unique biological characteristics. Ultimately, the specific designation for this organism is the species carnea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds beta-sitosterol, isorrhoeadsapogenin, kitigenin-5-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, and nicotianoside B. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the complex pharmacological profile of the species. Research suggests that these molecules may play significant roles in various biological activities. Scientists study these individual substances to better understand their therapeutic potential in medicine. Identifying these compounds helps clarify the plant's traditional uses in herbal remedies.

As discussed in a study, six compounds were isolated and identified from the ethyl acetate extraction of Reineckea carnea. These identified medicinal compounds include beta-sitosterol (1), isorhodeasapogenin (2), isorhodeasapogenin (3), (25S)-1beta,3beta,4beta-trihydroxyspirotan-5beta-yl-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (4), kitigenin-5-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (5), and nicotianoside B (6). The research notes that compounds 4 and 6 are obtained from this plant for the first time. Identification of these substances was achieved through various spectroscopic methods such as 1D and 2D-NMR spectrum, MS, and IR. Isolation was performed using extraction, silica gel, and reversed phase silica gel column chromatography.
