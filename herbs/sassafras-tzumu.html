Sassafras tzumu

What's the taxonomical classification of Sassafras tzumu?

Sassafras tzumu belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Laurales. The plant is further categorized under the family Lauraceae, which places it within the genus Sassafras, ultimately identifying it by its specific species name, tzumu.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sassafras tzumu has an upright, woody stem structure characterized by deeply fissured, grayish-brown bark and a branching pattern that forms a dense, rounded canopy. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the branches, typically appearing in clusters during the spring season. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that serve as the primary fruit of the plant. The root system is extensive and fibrous, providing necessary stability and nutrient absorption for its growth.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe liver toxicity, demonstrate carcinogenicity, and induce gastrointestinal distress such as nausea and vomiting. The primary danger arises from the presence of safrole, a compound found within the root bark. Research has indicated that consuming safrole can lead to the development of cancerous tumors in various animal studies. This chemical exposure is also linked to significant damage to hepatic cells and function. Consequently, the ingestion of this species poses serious long-term health risks.
