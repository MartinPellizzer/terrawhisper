Ulmus carpinifolia f. propendens

Ulmus carpinifolia f. propendens, also known as the rock elm, is a variety of the Wych Elm tree, characterized by its broad, flat branches and small leaves.

The rock elm has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which have been shown to have medicinal value in treating various health conditions.

The health benefits of Ulmus carpinifolia f. propendens include its ability to reduce fever, alleviate pain, and soothe digestive issues, making it a popular herbal remedy in folk medicine.

The plant can be prepared into herbal teas, infusions, or tinctures, and is often used in combination with other herbs to enhance its medicinal properties.
