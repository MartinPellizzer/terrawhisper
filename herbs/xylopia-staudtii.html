Xylopia staudtii

What's the taxonomical classification of Xylopia staudtii?

Xylopia staudtii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Magnoliales and is a member of the family Annonaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Xylopia, with its unique designation being the species staudtii.

The fruit develops into a woody follicle that contains numerous seeds. These morphological features are typical of many species within the Annonaceae family.

What health problems are treated using this plant?

This plant is used for treating bacillary dysentery, shigellosis, and various forms of dysentery. Extracts from the plant serve as a traditional remedy to combat these severe gastrointestinal infections. It is frequently administered to alleviate the intense abdominal cramp that accompanies these conditions. By addressing the underlying causes of dysentery, it helps restore digestive stability. This medicinal application is vital for managing acute intestinal distress in local communities.

As discussed in a study, the hydroethanolic extract from Xylopia staudtii bark was tested for efficacy in immunodepressed mice infected with Shigella flexneri. Infected mice were treated with the extract at doses of 100, 200 and 400 mg/kg during a 9 day treatment period. The extract demonstrated a bactericidal effect against Shigella and reduced the rate of deaths to zero (0) in animals treated at 400 mg/kg. Furthermore, the bacteria load in faeces was reduced by 100% in animals treated at 400 mg/kg. These findings support the use of Xylopia staudtii as an antimicrobial against bacillary dysentery.
