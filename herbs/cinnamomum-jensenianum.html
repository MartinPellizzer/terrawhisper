What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum jensenianum?

Cinnamomum jensenianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it falls under the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Cinnamomum, and its specific species epithet, jensenianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cinnamomum jennsenianum has lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 12 centimeters long, featuring an entire margin and an entire or slightly wavy edge. The foliage is characterized by a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, often displaying distinct venation patterns. Its stems exhibit a woody texture with a somewhat rough bark as the plant matures. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into small, globose fruits. These morphological traits are consistent with other members of the Lauraceae family.
