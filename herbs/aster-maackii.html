Aster maackii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster maackii has perennial, herbaceous stems that typically grow to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and feature leaves that are primarily linear to lanceolate in shape. The foliage is often characterized by a somewhat fleshy texture and can be smooth or slightly pubescent depending on the specific specimen. Its floral heads are relatively small and consist of numerous tiny ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular florets. These flower heads are usually arranged in terminal clusters, displaying colors that range from pale lavender to white. The root system is composed of rhizomatous structures that allow the plant to spread through its native rocky habitats.
