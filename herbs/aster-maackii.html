Aster maackii

What's the taxonomical classification of Aster maackii?

Aster maackii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, the plant falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Aster and is specifically distinguished as the species maackii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aster maackii has perennial, herbaceous stems that typically grow to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and feature leaves that are primarily linear to lanceolate in shape.

The foliage is often characterized by a somewhat fleshy texture and can be smooth or slightly pubescent depending on the specific specimen. Its floral heads are relatively small and consist of numerous tiny ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular florets. These flower heads are usually arranged in terminal clusters, displaying colors that range from pale lavender to white.

The root system is composed of rhizomatous structures that allow the plant to spread through its native rocky habitats.
