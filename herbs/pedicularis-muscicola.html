Pedicularis muscicola

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis muscicola?

Pedicularis muscicola belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further organized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Pedicularis and the specific species epithet muscicola.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis muscicola has a perennial life cycle characterized by a basal rosette of leaves and a terminal spike of zygomorphic, bilabiate flowers that are typically pink to purplish. The leaves are deeply pinnatifid or even bipinnatifid, featuring narrow, linear segments that give the foliage a delicate appearance. Its flowering stems are slender and upright, bearing numerous individual blossoms arranged closely along the upper portion of the axis. The corolla is distinctively shaped with a prominent upper lip that protects the reproductive organs. This plant often grows in mossy habitats where its low-growing structure allows it to integrate seamlessly with the surrounding ground cover.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds caryoptoside, phloyoside II, sesamoside, and syringaresinol-4"-O-beta-D-glucoside. These specific chemical constituents are categorized as bioactive glycosides found within the plant's tissues. Researchers study these compounds to understand their potential therapeutic properties and biological activities. The presence of these unique molecules contributes to the overall chemical profile of Pedicularis muscicola. Detailed analysis of these substances helps clarify the plant's role in traditional medicinal practices.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", four glycosidic compounds were isolated from an ethanolic extract of whole plants of Pedicularis muscicola. These include one lignan glycoside and three iridoid glycosides identified as syringaresinol-4"-O-beta-D-glucoside (1), sesamoside (2), phloyoside II (3), and caryoptoside (4). All 4 of these compounds were isolated from Pedicularis muscicola for the first time. Additionally, the compounds 2 and 3 were obtained for the first time from the Pedicularis genus.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating snakebite, skin ailments, and various internal inflammations. Indigenous practitioners often apply prepared extracts of the plant directly to wounds to mitigate the effects of venom. Beyond topical uses, it is sometimes ingested in controlled doses to address digestive discomfort. The medicinal properties of the species are primarily centered on its ability to soothe irritation and reduce swelling. This traditional knowledge forms a key part of the botanical heritage associated with the plant.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", the herb Pedicularis muscicola is used in a secret recipe for the treatment of snakebite. This recipe is located in Mabian, an autonomous county of the Yi nationality in Sichuan Province. The herb is used in a ratio of one to one within the composition of the recipe. The study also investigated the histological characteristics of the herb microscopically.
