Stephania hainanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Stephania hainanensis?

Stephania hainanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned under the order Ranunculales. Further taxonomic refinement places it in the family Menispermaceae, within the genus Stephania, and is specifically identified by its species name, hainanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephania hainanensis has a tuberous root system, pinnate leaves, and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically features a climbing habit supported by its delicate, twining stems. Each leaf is divided into multiple leaflets that create a fine, textured appearance. The reproductive structures are relatively small compared to the overall foliage. These morphological traits are characteristic of many species within the Menispermaceae family.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of medicinal compounds including benzylisoquinoline-type alkaloids, bisbenzylisoquinoline-type alkaloids, aporphinine-type alkaloids, proaporphine-type alkaloids, and oxocrebanine. These chemical constituents are primarily categorized by their complex carbon skeletons and varying degrees of oxidation. The benzylisoquinoline-type alkaloids often serve as the fundamental building blocks for more intricate structures within the species. Within this chemical landscape, the bisbenzylisoquinoline-type alkaloids and oxocrebanine represent highly specialized derivatives. Furthermore, the presence of both apo- and proaporphine-type alkaloids highlights the structural diversity essential for its biological activity.

As discussed in a study, only 10 alkaloids have been previously reported in the medicinal plant Stephania hainanensis. Using an LC-QTOF-MS/MS method, a total of 37 tetrahydroprotoberberine-, quaternary protoberberine-, apoerphine-, proaporphine-, benzylisoquinoline-, or bisbenzylisoquinoline-type alkaloids were identified or tentatively identified in a single LC run. Within this group, 27 of these alkaloids, including the benzylisoquinoline-type, have not been previously reported in Stephania hainanensis. The study also observed characteristic losses of CH3 N = CH2 for the benzylisoquinoline and apoerphine alkaloids with 2 methyl groups on the nitrogen. These findings regarding the diverse alkaloids may help in evaluating the quality of this plant.

    What side effects this plant can have?
