Cydonia oblonga

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cydonia oblonga has elongated, elliptical leaves with smooth margins and a distinctively leathery texture. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the woody stems. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils to support the reproductive cycle. The plant develops small, fleshy fruits that vary in shape depending on the maturity of the specimen. These morphological features collectively define its structural identity within its specific habitat.
