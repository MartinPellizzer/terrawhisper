Cydonia oblonga

What's the taxonomical classification of Cydonia oblonga?

Cydonia oblonga belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Cydonia and the specific species epithet oblonga.

As discussed in a study, Cydonia oblonga leaves are used as a folk remedy for the treatment of diabetes. The antidiaabetic activities of the ethanol extracts were studied in normal and streptozotocin-induced diabetic rats for 5 days. Extracts were administered orally to rats at the doses of 250 and 500 mg/kg. Oral administration of Cydonia oblonga extract at 500 mg/kg for 5 days in diabetic rats caused a decrease in blood glucose levels by 33.8%. Moreover, the Cydonia oblonga extract induced a significant alleviation on heart tissue TBARS levels by 45.7%. None of the extracts restored GSH levels in kidney, liver, and heart tissues of diabetic rats.
