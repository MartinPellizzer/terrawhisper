Chinese Milkvetch (Polygala tenuifolia)

Chinese Milkvetch (Polygala tenuifolia) is a member of the Polygalaceae family, native to China, Korea, and Japan. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, korean traditional medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Leaves
Ovate leaves with smooth margins, upper surface glabrous and pale green, lower surface pubescent and darker green, bearing two distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are zygomorphic with five petals arranged in a spathaceous structure, featuring a prominent spurred lip and a reduced calyx with five sepals.
Stems
Erect, branching habit with opposite phyllotaxis, glabrous surface, and nodes bearing stipitate, sessile, or clustered leaf scars.
