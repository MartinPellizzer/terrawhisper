Chinese milkvetch

Polygala tenuifolia

Chinese Milkvetch (Polygala tenuifolia) is a traditional Chinese medicinal plant known for its use in improving cognitive function and treating conditions such as memory loss and mental fatigue.

Taxonomy

Chinese Milkvetch, or Polygala tenuifolia, is a species belonging to the genus Polygala in the family Polygalaceae, classified under the order Fabales, class Fabales, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Chinese Milkvetch (Polygala tenuifolia) is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems, trifoliate leaves with small, lanceolate leaflets, and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing elongated, yellowish seeds.

Distribution

Chinese Milkvetch (Polygala tenuifolia) is native to China, specifically growing in the northern and northwestern regions including provinces such as Hebei, Shanxi, Shaanxi, Gansu, and Ningxia, as well as parts of the Korean Peninsula and southern Siberia.

Traditional Uses

Chinese Milkvetch has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to support respiratory health, alleviate coughs, and promote the resolution of phlegm, often prepared as a decoction or powder.

Medicinal Systems

Chinese Milkvetch (Polygala tenuifolia) has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine, Tibetan medicine, and traditional Korean medicine for its purported health benefits.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Chinese Milkvetch (Polygala tenuifolia) is characterized by the presence of alkaloids, saponins, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, with alkaloids such as polygaline and polygalacon being the most prominent and pharmacologically significant constituents.

Pharmacology

Chinese Milkvetch exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including neuroprotective, anti-inflammatory, immunomodulatory, and antitumor effects, which are attributed to its traditional use in enhancing cognitive function, reducing oxidative stress, and supporting immune system regulation.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Milkvetch, scientifically known as Polygala tenuifolia, belongs to the family Polygalaceae. It is also called Milkvetch or Chinese Milkvetch, and is a perennial herb native to China. The plant has fibrous roots, lance-shaped leaves, and small, clustered flowers. It is sometimes referred to as Polygala japonica in older herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for respiratory and neurological conditions due to its bioactive compounds.

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments due to its mild soothing properties.

Decoction: Used for muscle pain and inflammation due to its higher concentration of active compounds.

Safety Profile

Chinese Milkvetch is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. There are no well-documented serious adverse effects, but caution is advised for individuals with known allergies to plants in the Polygala family.
