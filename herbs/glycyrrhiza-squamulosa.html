What's the taxonomical classification of Glycyrrhiza squamulosa?

Glycyrrhiza squamuosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. It is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its legume-bearing plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Glycyrrhiza, and its specific identification is defined by its species name, squamulosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glycyrrhiza squamuosa has a perennial herbaceous growth habit characterized by a woody taproot, pinnately compound leaves, and small, pea-like flowers. The leaves typically consist of several pairs of oblong to lanceolate leaflets that are arranged along a central rachis. Its inflorescence is a raceme containing numerous small, pale purple or white blossoms that belong to the Fabaceae family. The stems are erect and can reach a height of approximately one meter depending on environmental conditions. Small, indehiscent legumes develop from the flowers to facilitate seed dispersal.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause serious physiological complications such as hypertension, hypokalemia, edema, muscle weakness, nausea, dizziness, heart palpitations, and weight gain. These adverse effects typically stem from the excessive consumption of glycyrrhizin, which interferes with electrolyte balance in the body. As potassium levels drop due to hypokalemia, individuals may experience significant muscle weakness and dangerous heart palpitations. This chemical imbalance also frequently leads to increased blood pressure and fluid retention known as edema. Consequently, patients might notice sudden weight gain along with secondary symptoms like nausea and dizziness.
