What is Stevia eupatoria?

Stevia eupatoria is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula, belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It grows to 60-100 cm in height, with a woody base and a leafy stem bearing ovate or lanceolate leaves 2-10 cm in length and 1-4 cm in width. The leaves are alternate, simple, and entire, with a pubescent underside. Stevia eupatoria produces small white or yellowish flowers in a capitulum, with a daisy-like appearance, from June to September.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Stevia eupatoria are rebaudioside A, steviol, rebaudioside C, stevioside, and dulcoside A. Rebaudioside A is a glycoside responsible for the sweetness of Stevia and has been shown to exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Steviol is a compound that is converted to stevioside, a potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory, which may help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress. Rebaudioside C is another glycoside that has been found to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, suggesting it may contribute to the plant's potential health benefits.

Dulcoside A, a minor constituent, has been reported to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, although its role in the plant's healing effects is less well-documented.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Stevia eupatoria are derived from its leaves, which contain bioactive compounds such as stevioside and rebaudioside.

Infusion and decoction are prepared by steeping the leaves in hot water to extract these compounds, which can be used to treat various health conditions, including diabetes and hypertension. Tincture is made by soaking the leaves in a solvent such as ethanol or glycerin to create a concentrated extract, which can be used topically or internally. Tea is prepared similarly to infusion but is typically made with a smaller amount of leaves and is often used as a calming agent.

A salve is prepared by infusing the leaves in a carrier oil such as coconut or olive oil, which can be used topically to treat skin conditions.

What are the possible side effects of using this herb improperly?

The possible side effects of using Stevia eupatoria improperly include experiencing nausea, which is often due to its intense sweetness that can be overwhelming to the digestive system.

Additionally, Stevia eupatoria may cause hypoglycemia, a drop in blood sugar levels, as it is known to inhibit the alpha-glucosidase enzyme, which can interfere with carbohydrate digestion and absorption. Furthermore, consuming Stevia eupatoria excessively can lead to diarrhea and other gastrointestinal issues, possibly due to its prebiotic properties that can alter gut bacteria balance. Some individuals may also develop allergic reactions, including skin rashes, itching, and swelling, as Stevia eupatoria is known to be a potential allergen.

These side effects are often associated with the consumption of large doses of Stevia eupatoria or prolonged use.
