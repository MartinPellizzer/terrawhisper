Saxifraga pasumensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga pasumensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by a compact rosette of leaves and terminal inflorescences. The leaves are generally small, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and possess slightly serrated margins. These basal leaves often feature a fleshy texture and may have distinctively shaped tips. The flowering stalks arise from the center of the rosette, bearing small, five-petaled flowers that typically display a pale coloration. This morphological arrangement allows the plant to thrive in the specific alpine or rocky niches it inhabits.
