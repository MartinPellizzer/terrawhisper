Morinda parvifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Morinda parvifolia?

Morinda parvifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is placed in the order Gentianales and the family Rubiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Morinda, and its unique species designation, parvifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Morinda parvifolia has small, elliptical to oblong leaves with an entire margin, woody stems, and small white flowers that cluster into a dense, fleshy syncarp fruit. The foliage is typically characterized by a glossy texture and a dark green color on the upper surface. Its stems are often branched and possess a somewhat cylindrical structure common to the Rubiaceae family. The inflorescence consists of multiple tiny, tubular blossoms that eventually fuse together to form a single aggregate fruit. This fruit is typically globose or ovoid and contains several small seeds embedded within the pulp.
