Trigonotis mollis

What's the taxonomical classification of Trigonotis mollis?

Trigonotis mollis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Boraginales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Boraginaceae. Finally, its specific biological identification is defined by the genus Trigonotis and the species epithet mollis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trigonotis mollis has an upright to ascending herbaceous habit characterized by slender, hairy stems and small, opposite, ovate leaves that taper to a point. The leaves feature entire margins and a soft, pubescent texture on both surfaces. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a tubular corolla that is typically white or pale lavender. These blooms are relatively small in scale compared to the overall leaf size. The plant completes its life cycle with a delicate structure that allows it to thrive in disturbed or shaded soil environments.
