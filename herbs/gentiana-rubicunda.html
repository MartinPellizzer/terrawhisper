Gentiana rubicunda

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana rubicunda?

Gentiana rubicunda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Gentianales. It is further nested within the family Gentianaceae, specifically falling under the genus Gentiana, which leads to its unique designation as the species rubicunda.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana rubicunda has erect, perennial stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a vibrant green color, often appearing somewhat fleshy. Its most striking feature is the trumpet-shaped flowers, which are usually a deep blue or violet and possess five distinct lobes. These blossoms are often marked by subtle white or pale spotting within the throat of the corolla. Small, rounded seed capsules develop following pollination to facilitate the plant's reproduction.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of the European Alps, spanning countries such as France, Switzerland, Italy, Austria, and Germany. It typically inhabits subalpine and alpine meadows located at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters above sea level. Within these mountainous zones, the species thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils often found near snowmelt areas. Its presence is frequently restricted to specific microclimates where consistent moisture and cool temperatures are maintained.
