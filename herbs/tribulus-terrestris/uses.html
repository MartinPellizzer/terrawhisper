Puncture Vine (Tribulus terrestris) Uses

Puncture Vine is used to treat prostatitis, erectile dysfunction, and premature ejaculation. It supports male fertility and may help with benign prostatic hyperplasia. It's also used for kidney stones, urinary tract infections, and wound healing. Its effects are primarily related to urinary and reproductive health.

Puncture Vine (Tribulus terrestris) Monograph

1. Prostatitis

Tribulus terrestris has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support prostate health. It may help reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of prostatitis by promoting urinary function and reducing bacterial growth. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties make it a valuable complementary therapy in managing this condition.

2. Erectile dysfunction

Tribulus terrestris has been traditionally used to support erectile function by enhancing libido and improving blood flow. Its active compounds, including saponins, may stimulate hormone production and improve vascular health. It is commonly used in herbal formulations for men's health, though results can vary. Always consult a qualified practitioner before use.

3. Premature ejaculation

Tribulus terrestris is traditionally used to support sexual health, including addressing premature ejaculation. It is believed to enhance libido and nerve function, possibly through its phytochemical compounds. However, its effectiveness varies, and it should be used under professional guidance.

4. Low libido

Tribulus terrestris is traditionally used to support libido, particularly in men. It is believed to enhance testosterone levels, which can improve sexual desire and performance. However, results may vary, and it is best used under professional guidance to ensure safety and effectiveness.

5. Male infertility

Tribulus terrestris has been traditionally used to support male fertility by enhancing libido and sperm production. Its active compounds, including saponins, may improve hormonal balance and reproductive function. Studies suggest it may increase sperm count and motility, though results vary. It is often used as a complementary therapy in herbal medicine for fertility support.

6. Fertility issues

Tribulus terrestris is traditionally used to support fertility by enhancing reproductive function. It is believed to increase libido and improve sperm quality in men. In women, it may help regulate menstrual cycles and support hormonal balance. Its effects are attributed to compounds like saponins and flavonoids.

7. Benign prostatic hyperplasia

Tribulus terrestris has been traditionally used to support urinary function in cases of benign prostatic hyperplasia. It may help reduce bladder obstruction by promoting prostate health and improving urinary flow. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties may contribute to its efficacy in managing symptoms associated with this condition.

8. Kidney stones

Tribulus terrestris is used in traditional medicine to support kidney health and may help prevent or dissolve kidney stones by promoting urinary flow and reducing mineral buildup. Its diuretic properties aid in flushing out toxins and excess minerals, potentially reducing stone formation. However, it should be used under professional guidance to avoid adverse effects.

9. Urinary tract infections

Tribulus terrestris may support urinary tract health by promoting diuretic effects and reducing bacterial adhesion. It is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of urinary tract infections, though scientific evidence remains limited. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for persistent or severe infections.

10. Wound healing

Tribulus terrestris supports wound healing by promoting tissue repair and reducing inflammation. Its compounds enhance cellular regeneration and improve blood circulation, accelerating the healing process. Used externally as a poultice or internally in herbal formulations, it is valued for its antiseptic and regenerative properties.
