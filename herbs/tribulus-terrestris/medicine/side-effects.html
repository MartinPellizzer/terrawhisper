10 Possible Side Effects Of Tribulus Terrestris

Tribulus terrestris has some side effects when used improperly, such as increased blood pressure, dizziness, and stomach discomfort.

These side effects can occur due to excessive dosage, interactions with other medications, and individual sensitivities. For instance, people with high blood pressure may experience severe hypertension, which can lead to cardiovascular problems, stroke, or even kidney damage.

Others may experience debilitating dizziness that hampers daily activities, affecting their work and social lives.

1. Increases blood pressure rises

Tribulus terrestris causes kidney damage occurs in some individuals due to its high saponin content.

Tribulus terrestris lowers sperm quality drops.

5. Induces anxiety attacks triggers

Tribulus terrestris induces anxiety attacks triggers due to its potential interaction with certain neurotransmitters in the brain.

The herb may alter the balance of serotonin and dopamine levels, leading to increased feelings of stress and apprehension.

6. Increases heart rate beats

Tribulus terrestris increases heart rate beats.

This is attributed to its ability to stimulate the production of adrenaline, a hormone that regulates heart rate and blood pressure. As a result, users may experience an elevated pulse, which can be a side effect of supplementing with Tribulus terrestris.

Tribulus terrestris causes stomach upset develops in some individuals due to its high saponin content.

As a result, the body's natural production of testosterone is suppressed, leading to decreased levels.

Additionally, Tribulus terrestris can also stimulate the production of follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) and luteinizing hormone (LH), which may further contribute to the reduction in testosterone levels.

Tribulus terrestris hinder's mental performance impairs.

10. Increases blood sugar spikes

Tribulus terrestris increases blood sugar spikes due to its ability to stimulate insulin secretion and enhance glucose absorption in the gut.

This may lead to rapid rises in blood sugar levels, potentially affecting individuals with diabetes or those who are sensitive to changes in blood sugar control.

Additionally, Tribulus terrestris may interact with certain medications used to regulate blood sugar, further exacerbating this side effect.
