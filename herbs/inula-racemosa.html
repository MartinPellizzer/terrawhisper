Inula racemosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Inula racemosa?

Inula racemosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized into the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, this plant is specifically identified by the genus Inula and the species racemosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Inula racemosa contains high concentrations of sesquiterpene lactones, specifically helenalin and alantolactone, which serve as its primary bioactive constituents. These compounds are responsible for the plant's significant anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Additionally, the plant contains various essential oils and phenolic compounds that contribute to its therapeutic effects. These secondary metabolites work together to support respiratory health and alleviate digestive discomfort. Research suggests that these chemical profiles make the plant a potent subject for studying natural anti-tumor activities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Inula racemosa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat diabetes, inflammatory conditions, urinary tract infections, and various digestive disorders. Its bioactive compounds help regulate blood glucose levels by improving insulin sensitivity. The plant also possesses potent anti-inflammatory properties that assist in managing joint pain and swelling. Additionally, its antimicrobial effects make it useful for addressing infections within the urinary system. Traditional medicine practitioners frequently utilize its roots and leaves to alleviate gastrointestinal distress.
