Racemose Inula

Inula racemosa

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Inula racemosa, commonly known as Racemose Inula, belongs to the family Asteraceae. It is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and Central Asia. The plant has yellow ray florets, hairy stems, and fibrous roots. It is also referred to as Indian Buttercup or Himalayan Inula in some regional texts.

Active Compounds

Racemose Inula contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and tannins, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in herbal medicine applications.

  • Flavonoid
  • Terpenoid
  • Tannin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Cough, bronchitis, asthma, respiratory tract infections, allergic rhinitis Expectorant, anti-inflammatory, bronchodilator
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, fungal infections Antimicrobial, antiseptic, antifungal
Immune Allergies, inflammation, allergic reactions Antihistaminic, anti-inflammatory

Preparation Methods

Powder: Used for respiratory issues and digestive disorders.

Infusion: Used for treating coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for gastrointestinal problems and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Inula racemosa is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for individuals with known allergies or chronic health conditions.
