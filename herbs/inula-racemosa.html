What to know about Inula racemosa (golden samson) before using it medicinally

Golden Samson, scientifically known as Inula racemosa, is a herb that has been prized for its impressive array of health properties, which have been shown to improve respiratory function and reduce inflammation.

In culinary circles, golden samson is often used to add flavor and depth to soups, stews, and sauces, with its mild, slightly bitter taste and crunchy texture making it a popular addition to many dishes. As a horticultural crop, golden samson requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive, making it a great choice for gardeners looking to add some unique interest to their landscapes. Botanically speaking, golden samson is an asteraceae family member, characterized by its yellow-orange daisy-like flowers and feathery leaves.

With historical references dating back to ancient Greece and Rome, where it was used to treat a range of ailments from fever to indigestion, the allure of this versatile herb has endured for centuries.
