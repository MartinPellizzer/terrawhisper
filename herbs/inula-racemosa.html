Racemose Inula

Inula racemosa

Racemose Inula (Inula racemosa) is a medicinal plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia, known for its use in traditional medicine to improve respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma.

Botanical Identification

Inula racemosa, commonly known as Racemose Inula, belongs to the family Asteraceae. It is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and Central Asia. The plant has yellow ray florets, hairy stems, and fibrous roots. It is also referred to as Indian Buttercup or Himalayan Inula in some regional texts.

Safety Profile

Inula racemosa is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for individuals with known allergies or chronic health conditions.
