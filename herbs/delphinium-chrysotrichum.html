Delphinium chrysotrichum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium chrysotrichum has a perennial growth habit characterized by upright, erect stems that typically reach heights of up to one meter. The foliage consists of deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves that possess a distinctively hairy or pubescent texture. Small, bright yellow flowers emerge in terminal racemes, featuring five sepals and a central cluster of petals. A defining morphological feature is the presence of dense, golden-colored hairs covering the stems and the underside of the leaves. These fine trichomes provide a characteristic shimmering appearance to the entire plant structure.

      What are the herbal preparations of Delphinium chrysotrichum?

      Delphinium chrysotrichum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended medicinal application. The dried roots and leaves are often boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction used for internal treatments. For milder therapeutic effects, a hot water infusion is made by steeping the plant material to extract soluble components. When treating skin conditions, the crushed plant matter is applied directly as a poultice to the affected area. These various methods allow for the targeted delivery of the plant's bioactive compounds.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Delphinium chrysotrichum can cause severe poisoning if ingested, leading to symptoms such as vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, and potential cardiac complications. The plant contains toxic alkaloids that affect the central nervous system and internal organs. Contact with the plant may also trigger skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Because of these risks, accidental consumption can be life-threatening for both humans and pets. Careful handling is necessary to prevent any direct exposure to the foliage or flowers.
