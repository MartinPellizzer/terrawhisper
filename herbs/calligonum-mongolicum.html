What's the taxonomical classification of Calligonum mongolicum?

Calligonum mongolicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Caryophyllales. This plant is further categorized under the family Polygonaceae, which places it among a diverse group of herbaceous and woody plants. Within this family, it is specifically identified by the genus Calligonum, with its unique biological identity defined by the species mongolicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calligonum mongolicum has a perennial, woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by slender, photosynthetic, broom-like branches that lack true leaves. Its branches are often reddish or yellowish in color and possess a highly branched, spreading architecture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters along the stems, eventually developing into dry, winged fruits designed for wind dispersal.
