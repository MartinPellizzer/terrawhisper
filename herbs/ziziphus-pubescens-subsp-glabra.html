White Thorn (Ziziphus Pubescens Subsp. Glabra)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Ziziphus pubescens subsp. glabra.

White thorn, scientifically known as Ziziphus pubescens subsp. glabra, is a medicinal herb native to arid and semi-arid regions, valued for its therapeutic properties and adaptogenic qualities. It is traditionally used to support nervous system health, promote restful sleep, and enhance mental clarity due to its rich content of compounds like saponins and flavonoids. In traditional medicine systems, such as Ayurveda and Chinese herbalism, it has been employed to treat insomnia, anxiety, and digestive disorders, often prepared as a tonic or infusion. Modern wellness practices increasingly incorporate white thorn for its potential to reduce stress and improve cognitive function, making it a popular ingredient in adaptogenic supplements. This plant is also noted for its unique, slightly bitter flavor and historical use in folk remedies, reflecting its enduring significance in both cultural and medicinal contexts.
