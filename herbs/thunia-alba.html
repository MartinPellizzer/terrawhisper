Thunia alba

What's the taxonomical classification of Thunia alba?

Thunia alba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized into the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, placing it among the diverse orchids, specifically within the genus Thunia, and is identified by the species name alba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thunia alba has large, fleshy, oblong leaves that are bright green and arranged in an alternate pattern along its thick, upright stems. The plant typically reaches heights of several feet, characterized by a robust structure capable of supporting heavy floral displays. Its most striking morphological feature is the large, fragrant, white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms possess thick, waxy petals and a complex internal structure designed to attract specific pollinators. The root system is composed of thick, tuberous rhizomes that allow the plant to store water and nutrients efficiently.
