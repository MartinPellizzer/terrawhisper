Further classification places the plant in the order Ranunculales and the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Clematis, and its specific species designation, grandiidentata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis grandidentata has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that typically measure 5 to 15 centimeters in length and possess serrated margins. The plant grows as a woody climbing vine that uses its twisting petioles to scale surrounding vegetation. Its flowers are large and bell-shaped, featuring creamy white to pale yellowish petals that can reach up to 5 centimeters across. These blooms are supported by long, slender pedicels and emerge from the leaf axils. Once the flowering period ends, the plant develops distinctive feathery or hairy seed heads known as achenes.
