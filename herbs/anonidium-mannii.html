Anonidium mannii

What's the taxonomical classification of Anonidium mannii?

Anonidium mannii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Magnoliales. This plant is a member of the family Annonaceae, falling under the genus Anonidium, and is specifically identified by the species name mannii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anonidium mannii has small, circular, brownish-to-blackish fruiting bodies known as scale insects that attach firmly to the stems or leaves of its host plant. These structures consist of a protective waxy covering that shields the underlying insect from environmental stressors. The morphology of these scales often appears as a dense, crust-like layer that can obscure the surface of the plant tissue. Individual scales vary slightly in size and shape, forming irregular clusters across the affected area. This specialized body form allows the organism to remain stationary while extracting nutrients from the host.

What are the pharmacological activities of Anonidium mannii?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, anticancer, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. Its chemical constituents allow it to effectively reduce pain and suppress inflammatory responses in biological systems. The plant also demonstrates significant potency in inhibiting the growth of various bacterial strains. Furthermore, research indicates that its extracts possess the ability to combat oxidative stress and inhibit the progression of certain cancer cells. These diverse properties make it a subject of great interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Journal of basic and clinical physiology and pharmacology", the ethanol extracts of Anonidium mannii possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic properties. These extracts showed high phenolic contents of 15.93-64.45 mgCAE/g and flavonoid contents of 336.03-1053.48 mgCAE/g, with a ferric reducing power of 288.75-364.91 mgCAE/g. Radical scavenging activities were demonstrated via DPPH (IC50 = 0.30-1.65 μg/mL), ABTS (IC50 = 0.52-1.90 μg/mL), and H2O2 (IC50 = 1.40-3.55 μg/mL) assays. The plant inhibited protein denaturation by 6.79-82.27% and protected erythrocyte membranes from lysis by 18.90-88.00%. Furthermore, the extracts significantly reduced paw edema (p < 0.05), fever, and abdominal writhing (p < 0.001), particularly at a dose of 400 mg/kg.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive medicinal compounds including caffeoylquinic acids, various flavonoids, phenolic acids, and specific esters. These phytochemical constituents contribute significantly to the plant's documented pharmacological activities. The presence of phenolic compounds provides essential antioxidant properties to the species. Additionally, the concentration of flavonoids plays a crucial role in its medicinal potential. Researchers continue to study how these esters and other secondary metabolites interact within biological systems.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", the plant Anonidium mannii contains bioactive metabolites including flavonoids and caffeoylquinic acids, which are reported for the first time in this species. The butanol-water extract of the plant exhibited an antioxidant IC50 of 0.44 ± 0.05 µg/mL and antibacterial effects with MIC values ranging from 0.62 to 5.0 mg/mL against six pathogenic bacteria. Additionally, the hexane extract showed a selectivity index (SI) of 1.99 toward HepG2 cells. GC-MS also tentatively identified several bioactive hydrocarbons and esters with known anticancer properties within the plant.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating cancer, fevers, infections, and inflammation. Researchers have studied its bioactive compounds for their potential to combat various types of cancers. Its medicinal properties also include the ability to reduce high fever and manage systemic infections. Additionally, the plant is frequently utilized to alleviate symptoms associated with inflammation. These therapeutic applications make it a significant subject in ethnobotanical studies.

According to a study published by "Evidence-based complementary and alternative medicine : eCAM", Anonidium mannii is studied for its potential as a source for new anticancer drugs. At 40 g/mL, the extract from Anonidium mannii leaves (AML) showed a growth of CCRF-CEM leukemia cells by 31.58%. This plant obtained an IC50 value below 30 μg/mL against 8/9 tested cancer cell lines. The lowest IC50 value for AML was 9.14 μg/mL against U87MG.ΔEGFR cells. Furthermore, the extract induced cell cycle arrest in the G0/G1 phase and enhanced the production of ROS.
