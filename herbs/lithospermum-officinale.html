What to know about Lithospermum officinale (gromwell) before using it medicinally

Gromwell, scientifically known as Lithospermum officinale, is a herb that has been touted for its impressive health properties, which include reducing inflammation and improving respiratory function.

In the kitchen, gromwell is often used to add depth and complexity to soups, stews, and braises, while its leaves can be used as a peppery garnish. As a hardy perennial, gromwell is relatively easy to cultivate and can thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a popular choice among gardeners. Botanically speaking, gromwell belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its purple flowers and hairy leaves.

With records dating back to ancient Greece and Rome, gromwell has been a staple in herbal medicine for centuries, with references to its uses appearing in the writings of Hippocrates and Dioscorides.
