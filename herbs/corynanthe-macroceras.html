Corynanthe macroceras

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corynanthe macroceras has a woody, shrubby habit characterized by opposite leaves and tubular, long-exserted flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical to oblong in shape with entire margins and prominent venation. Its inflorescences consist of small, densely clustered flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The most distinguishing feature is the elongated corolla tube, which gives the plant its specific epithet. These floral structures are often pale or yellowish in color to attract specific pollinators.
