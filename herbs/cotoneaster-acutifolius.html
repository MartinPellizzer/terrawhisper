What's the taxonomical classification of Cotoneaster acutifolius?

Cotoneaster acutifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Rosales and falls within the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Cotoneaster and is specifically designated as the species acutifolius.

    What are the herbal preparations of Cotoneaster acutifolius?

    Cotoneaster acutifolius is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical tincture derived from its berries and leaves. The berries are often simmered in water to create a concentrated decoction used for internal medicinal purposes. Leaves can be steeped in hot water to form a mild infusion suitable for calming various ailments. Additionally, the plant material can be processed into a tincture using alcohol to extract specific bioactive compounds for long-term storage. These various preparations allow for different methods of administration depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Cotoneaster acutifolius can cause gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea if any part of the plant is ingested. The berries contain cyanogenic glycosides which can release toxic cyanide into the body when consumed. Ingestion of these compounds may lead to more severe symptoms such as dizziness, headaches, or even more critical neurological issues. While skin contact is generally not considered highly toxic, some individuals might experience mild irritation or allergic reactions from the foliage. It is essential to keep this shrub away from children and pets to prevent accidental poisoning.
