Cotoneaster acutifolius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cotoneaster acuifolius has small, lanceolate to narrowly elliptical leaves that are characterized by sharply pointed tips and finely serrated margins. These leaves are typically dark green and leathery in texture, often appearing densely arranged along the woody stems. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that emerge from inconspicuous bracts during the blooming season. Following pollination, it develops small, globose pomes that transition from green to a bright red color when mature. The overall growth habit is that of a low-growing, spreading shrub with multiple branching stems.
