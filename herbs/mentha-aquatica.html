Water Mint (Mentha aquatica)

Water Mint (Mentha aquatica) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North America. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its antispasmodic, carminative, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Opposite, oval-shaped leaves with smooth upper surface and slightly hairy lower surface, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Bilaterally symmetrical flowers with two-lipped corolla, blue to violet coloration, arranged in whorled clusters, featuring four stamens and a superior ovary.
Stems
Erect, somewhat quadrangular, hairy, opposite branching, with longitudinal ridges and a pith that is white and spongy.
