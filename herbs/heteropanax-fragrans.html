Heteropanax fragrans

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Heteropanax fragrans has a woody shrub or small tree habit characterized by simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a distinct, fragrant aroma when crushed. Its stems are slender and develop a textured bark as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils to eventually produce fleshy drupes. These fruits serve as a primary morphological feature for identification within its genus.
