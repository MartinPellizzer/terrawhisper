Cyclea gracillima

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyclea gracillima has woody climbing vines characterized by slender stems and trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct leaflets. Each leaflet is typically ovate or elliptical in shape with smooth or slightly wavy margins and a pointed tip. The foliage is arranged alternately along the climbing stems to maximize sunlight absorption in forest undergrowth. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils to facilitate reproduction. The plant's structure is adapted for climbing through surrounding vegetation using its flexible stems.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. Its distribution is closely tied to areas that maintain high levels of moisture and warm temperatures year-round.       What are the herbal preparations of Cyclea gracillima?

      Cyclea gracillima is prepared as a decoction by boiling the fresh leaves in water, as a topical poultice applied directly to the skin, or as a juice extracted through crushing the plant material.
