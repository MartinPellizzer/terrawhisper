Cyclea gracillima

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyclea gracillima?

Cyclea gracillima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Menispermaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Cyclea, with the binomial designation of the species gracillima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyclea gracillima has woody climbing vines characterized by slender stems and trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct leaflets. Each leaflet is typically ovate or elliptical in shape with smooth or slightly wavy margins and a pointed tip. The foliage is arranged alternately along the climbing stems to maximize sunlight absorption in forest undergrowth. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils to facilitate reproduction. The plant's structure is adapted for climbing through surrounding vegetation using its flexible stems.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cyclea gracillima?

This plant has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antitumor, and anticonvulsant pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. The anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects suggest potential uses in managing swelling and fighting various infectious pathogens. Additionally, research indicates that its antitumor capabilities may assist in inhibiting the growth of malignant cells. Finally, the anticonvulsant activity points toward its possible role in stabilizing neurological functions and preventing seizures.

According to a study published by "Journal of food and drug analysis", Cyclea gracillima Diels is a Taiwanese native medicinal herb with antioxidant attributes assayed through several in vitro models. The research evaluated its efficacy using 2,2-diphenyl-1-(2,4,6-trinitrophenyl)-hydrazyl radical scavenging, Trolox equivalent antioxidant capacity, reducing power, and chelating ferrous ions. These extracts also demonstrated activity in the inhibition of human low density lipoprotein oxidation, inhibition of human erythrocyte hemolysis, and scavenging oxygen radicals in human blood. The extracts contained 6 phenolic acids, primarily ferulic acid, sinapic acid, and syringic acid, along with 12 flavonoids, mainly naringenin, myricetin, naringin, and apigenin. High polyphenol levels in these extracts, particularly those extracted with ethanol, resulted in remarkable antioxidant effects across all assays.
