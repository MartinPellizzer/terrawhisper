Cyclea gracillima

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyclea gracillima has woody climbing vines characterized by slender stems and trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct leaflets. Each leaflet is typically ovate or elliptical in shape with smooth or slightly wavy margins and a pointed tip. The foliage is arranged alternately along the climbing stems to maximize sunlight absorption in forest undergrowth. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils to facilitate reproduction. The plant's structure is adapted for climbing through surrounding vegetation using its flexible stems.
