Hura crepitans

What's the taxonomical classification of Hura crepitans?

Hura crepitans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Malpighiales. The plant is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae, which contains the genus Hura, ultimately identifying the specific organism as the species crepitans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hura crepitans has a woody, deciduous habit characterized by a trunk that reaches heights of up to 30 meters and branches that bear large, pinnately compound leaves. The leaves consist of several pairs of leaflets that are typically elliptic or oblong in shape with entire margins. The flowers are relatively small and appear in axillary clusters, eventually developing into distinctive woody capsules. These capsules are known for their explosive dehiscence, which forcefully ejects the seeds away from the parent plant. The bark of the tree is generally smooth and greyish, providing a stark contrast to the dense green foliage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

What are the pharmacological activities of Hura crepitans?

This plant has antibacterial, antimalarial, antiplasmodial, and curative properties against leprosy. Researchers have identified specific bioactive compounds within its tissues that effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria. Its potent antimalarial and antiplasmodial activities make it a significant subject of study for treating parasitic blood infections. Furthermore, traditional medicinal practices and preliminary studies suggest its potential to help cure leprosy. These diverse pharmacological profiles highlight the plant's importance in natural medicine.

As discussed in a study, Hura crepitans was identified as one of the 12 plants that showed relevant antibacterial activity. This specific activity was characterized by a Minimal Inhibitory Concentration (MIC) of ≤0.15 mg/mL for one or several of the 36 microorganisms tested. The plant was among 59 species and 81 selected extracts evaluated for antimicrobial properties against a panel of 36 sensitive and multi-resistant bacteria or yeast. The research utilized an agar dilution method on 35 bacteria strains and 1 yeast to determine these MIC values.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains alkaloids, flavonoids, phenolic compounds, quinones, and triterpenoids that contribute to its diverse pharmacological profile. The presence of alkaloids provides potent biological activities often targeting the nervous system. Flavonoids and phenolic compounds act as significant antioxidants to combat oxidative stress in cells. Quinones offer unique chemical properties that can influence various enzymatic pathways. Additionally, triterpenoids provide anti-inflammatory benefits and contribute to the overall medicinal potency of the species.

As discussed in a study, Hura crepitans L. showed good antiplasmodial activity with a mean inhibitory concentration of 5,7 microg/mL. This plant extract demonstrated excellent selectivity with a selectivity index of 18,8. A preliminary phytochemical screening of the extract revealed the presence of phenolic compounds, triterpenoids, alkaloids, quinones, flavonoids and anthocyanidins. This potent antimalarial activity in vitro was obtained from the hydroalcoholic extract.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating Plasmodium falciparum malaria, leprosy, and complications arising from excessive alcohol consumption. Its medicinal properties are often utilized in traditional practices to combat the parasitic infection that causes severe malaria. Additionally, extracts from the plant are applied to manage the symptoms and progression of leprosy. Researchers also study its potential to mitigate health issues related to high levels of alcohol consumption in the body. These diverse applications demonstrate the plant's significance in various folk medicine systems.

As discussed in a study, Hura crepitans L. was evaluated in vitro against Plasmodium falciparum to determine its antiplasmodial activity. This plant extract was the only one that showed good antiplasmodial activity with a mean inhibitory concentration of 5,7 microg/mL. It also demonstrated excellent selectivity with a selectivity index of 18,8. Phytochemical screening of this extract revealed the presence of phenolic compounds, triterpenoids, alkaloids, quinones, flavonoids and anthocyanidins. The results provide a potent antimalarial activity in vitro for this vegetal preparation.

    What side effects this plant can have?

If the sap accidentally touches the eyes, it may lead to significant inflammation or even temporary blindness. Ingesting any part of the organism can result in serious internal damage and digestive distress. Therefore, extreme caution should be exercised whenever handling this species in its natural habitat.
