What's the taxonomical classification of Crepis elongata?

Crepis elongata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the hierarchy of biological classification, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic ranks, this plant is part of the order Asterales and is a member of the widely known family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Crepis, resulting in its unique scientific designation as the species elongata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crepis elongata has an erect, slender stem that typically grows between 10 and 40 centimeters tall and features sparsely distributed, lanceolate leaves. The basal leaves are often larger and more continuous, while the cauline leaves decrease in size as they ascend the stem. Its inflorescence consists of a few heads arranged in a loose, terminal corymbiform cyme. Each flower head contains numerous yellow ligulate florets and is topped by a pappus of fine, white bristles. The plant also develops a taproot system that anchors it firmly in its habitat.
