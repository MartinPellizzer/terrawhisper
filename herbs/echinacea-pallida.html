Pale Coneflower

Echinacea pallida

Pale Coneflower (Echinacea pallida) is a North American native plant known for its bright pink to purple flowers and its traditional use in supporting immune health, particularly in the treatment of colds and respiratory infections.

Botanical Identification

Echinacea pallida, commonly known as the pale coneflower, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb native to North America, particularly the eastern and central United States. The plant has a fibrous root system, lance-shaped leaves, and a distinctive yellow-orange flower head with purple bracts. It is also referred to as swamp coneflower in some herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The Pale Coneflower contains flavonoids, glycosides, and tannins as its main active compounds. These bioactive components work together to support immune function and reduce inflammation. Their synergistic effects enhance the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Safety Profile

The Pale Coneflower is generally safe when used as directed. It is contraindicated in individuals with allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family. No significant adverse effects are reported when used appropriately.
