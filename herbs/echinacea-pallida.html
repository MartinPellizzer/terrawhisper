Side Effects of echinacea pallida

Echinacea pallida leads to allergic reaction in some individuals, particularly those with a history of allergies or sensitivities to plants in the Asteraceae family, such as ragweed, chrysanthemums, or daisies.

This can manifest as symptoms like hives, itching, or even anaphylaxis in severe cases. Additionally, the herb can cause stomach upset, bringing about nausea, vomiting, and gastrointestinal discomfort, which may be more pronounced in higher doses or when taken on an empty stomach.

Some users may experience a dry mouth, which can be uncomfortable but is generally not serious. Furthermore, echinacea pallida can result in diarrhea or other forms of gastrointestinal upset, especially if the individual has a sensitive digestive system.

These side effects highlight the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before using echinacea, particularly for those with pre-existing conditions or who are taking other medications.
