Passion Fruit (Passiflora edulis) Compounds

Passion fruit contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant properties as well. Anthocyanins give the fruit its vibrant color and also have antioxidant benefits. Coumarin is present in small amounts and may have mild sedative effects.

Passion Fruit (Passiflora edulis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Passion Fruit (Passiflora edulis). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds support overall health and may aid in managing conditions like anxiety and inflammation.

2. Phenolic acid

Passion Fruit (Passiflora edulis) contains phenolic acid, which contributes to its antioxidant properties. This compound helps neutralize free radicals, supporting overall cellular health. Phenolic acid in passion fruit may also aid in reducing inflammation and promoting skin health. Its presence enhances the plant's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

3. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Passion Fruit (Passiflora edulis). It contributes to the fruit's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound supports overall health by neutralizing free radicals and may aid in reducing inflammation. Anthocyanin is also believed to enhance cognitive function and promote cardiovascular health.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Passion Fruit (Passiflora edulis). It contributes to the plant's sedative and anxiolytic effects, often used in herbal medicine for stress relief and sleep support. Coumarin also has antispasmodic properties, making it valuable in treating nervous system disorders. Its presence enhances the plant's therapeutic profile in traditional and modern herbal formulations.

5. Glycoside

Passion fruit (Passiflora edulis) contains the glycoside passiflorine. This compound contributes to the plant's calming effects and is studied for its potential anxiolytic properties. Glycosides like passiflorine are often responsible for the medicinal actions of herbal remedies.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Passion Fruit (Passiflora edulis). It contributes to the fruit's astringent taste and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins may support digestive health and help in managing conditions like diarrhea. They are also believed to aid in reducing oxidative stress.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Passion Fruit (Passiflora edulis) contribute to its calming effects. These compounds, including flavonoids and alkaloids, help reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. They also possess antioxidant properties that support overall health. Terpenoids are key to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Passion Fruit (Passiflora edulis). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting mild antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin may support digestive health and immune function when consumed as part of the fruit or its herbal preparations.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Passion Fruit (Passiflora edulis) contains volatile compounds like limonene and geraniol. These compounds contribute to the plant's calming properties and are used in herbal medicine for their potential anxiolytic and sedative effects.

10. Alkaloid

Passion fruit (Passiflora edulis) contains alkaloids, which are nitrogen-containing compounds with potential pharmacological effects. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's sedative and anxiolytic properties, though research on their specific roles in herbal medicine is still emerging.
