10 Precautions To Take When Using Passiflora Edulis (Passion Fruit)

Passiflora edulis has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as pregnant or breastfeeding women being cautious due to potential estrogenic effects.

Overconsumption may lead to drowsiness, dizziness, or stomach upset. Without proper caution, passion fruit may interact with certain medications, such as benzodiazepines or blood thinners, exacerbating side effects like sedation, nausea, or bleeding complications.

It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using passion fruit medicinally.

1. Follow proper preparation methods

When using Passiflora edulis medicinally, it's important to take under medical supervision due to its potential interactions with certain medications and the risk of adverse effects.

The herb can cause sedation and drowsiness, which may amplify the effects of anxiolytics, hypnotics, and anticonvulsants.

Furthermore, passion flower may also interact with blood thinners and diabetes medications, increasing the risk of bleeding or hypoglycemia.
