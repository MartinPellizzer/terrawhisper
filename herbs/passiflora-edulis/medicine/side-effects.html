10 Possible Side Effects Of Passiflora Edulis

Passiflora edulis has some side effects when used improperly, such as drowsiness, dizziness, and stomach upset, due to its sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.

These side effects can worsen people's lives by causing accidents, missed work or school, and strained relationships.

Furthermore, excessive use can lead to physical dependence and withdrawal symptoms, such as anxiety and insomnia, making everyday activities challenging.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Passiflora edulis causes allergic reactions.

This is likely due to the presence of proteins and allergenic compounds in the plant, such as flavonoids and alkaloids, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

The specific components responsible for these reactions are not fully understood, but it is thought that the unique combination of bioactive molecules in Passiflora edulis contributes to its potential to cause allergic symptoms in certain people.

2. Triggers anaphylaxis

Passiflora edulis triggers anaphylaxis in some individuals due to its high allergenic potential.

The seeds and pulp of the fruit contain a significant amount of latex, which is responsible for triggering allergic reactions in susceptible people. Additionally, the flavonoids and alkaloids present in the fruit may also contribute to the development of anaphylactic symptoms.

As a result, those who are prone to allergies or have a history of allergic reactions may experience severe anaphylaxis upon consuming Passiflora edulis.

3. Induces respiratory problems

Passiflora edulis induces respiratory problems.

This can occur due to its ability to cause sedation and relaxation, which can lead to shallow breathing and decreased lung function.

The flavonoids and alkaloids present in the plant may also contribute to bronchial constriction, exacerbating existing respiratory conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

4. Affects blood pressure

Passiflora edulis affects blood pressure due to its ability to relax smooth muscle and dilate blood vessels.

This is attributed to the presence of flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins in the plant's extract, which can cause a decrease in systolic blood pressure.

As a result, people may experience a drop in blood pressure upon consuming passion fruit or its supplements, leading to dizziness, lightheadedness, or even fainting in some cases.

5. Increases sedation

Passiflora edulis increases sedation due to its ability to interact with GABA receptors in the brain, leading to a decrease in anxiety and restlessness.

The flavonoids present in the herb, particularly apigenin, kaempferol, and luteolin, contribute to this sedative effect by enhancing the binding of GABA to its receptors, resulting in a calming and relaxing state.

As the herb is known for its anxiolytic and hypnotic properties, it is not uncommon for individuals to experience increased sedation when using Passiflora edulis.

6. Disrupts sleep patterns

Passiflora edulis disrupts sleep patterns due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system.

The plant's flavonoids and alkaloids can increase alertness and energy levels, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

This effect is often attributed to the way these compounds interact with serotonin and other neurotransmitters in the brain, leading to a sense of restlessness and wakefulness that can interfere with normal sleep patterns.

7. Exhibits anticholinergic effects

Passiflora edulis exhibits anticholinergic effects, which can manifest as dry mouth, blurred vision, and urinary retention.

This occurs due to the herb's ability to bind with cholinergic receptors in the brain and body, thereby antagonizing the actions of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that regulates various physiological processes, including saliva production, pupil size, and bladder contraction.

8. Impairs cognitive function

Passiflora edulis impairs cognitive function due to its ability to interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, such as GABA and serotonin.

This interaction can lead to feelings of drowsiness, confusion, and disorientation, making it difficult for individuals to concentrate or remember information.

The herb's sedative properties may also contribute to these effects, causing a decrease in mental clarity and reaction time.

9. Activates gastrointestinal issues

Passiflora edulis activates gastrointestinal issues due to its high content of flavonoids and alkaloids, which can cause stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea.

The fruit's seeds, pulp, and juice contain compounds that may irritate the digestive tract, leading to symptoms such as bloating, cramps, and gas.

Additionally, some people may be sensitive to Passiflora edulis' saponins, which are known to stimulate bowel movements and increase gut motility, further contributing to gastrointestinal disturbances.

10. Causes drowsiness

Passiflora edulis causes drowsiness due to its sedative properties.

The herb contains flavonoids and alkaloids that interact with the body's GABA receptors, leading to a decrease in anxiety and stress levels. This can result in feelings of relaxation and calmness, which may progress to drowsiness in some individuals.

Additionally, passion fruit's sedating effects are enhanced by its ability to slow down the nervous system, further contributing to the drowsy sensation.
