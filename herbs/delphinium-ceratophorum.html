Delphinium ceratophorum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Delphinium ceratophorum has upright, lanceolate leaves that are deeply lobed and dark green in color. The plant features terminal racemes of striking, horn-shaped flowers that typically display deep blue or violet hues. These unique floral structures are characterized by an elongated nectar spur that gives the species its specific epithet. The stems are sturdy and erect, supporting the heavy weight of the blooming clusters. Smaller, bract-like leaves often emerge along the length of the flowering stalk to provide additional foliage.

      Delphinium ceratophorum is prepared as a decoction of the roots, a paste from crushed leaves, or a topical poultice for external applications.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Delphinium ceratophorum can cause severe poisoning characterized by respiratory failure, cardiac arrhythmia, and intense gastrointestinal distress upon ingestion. The plant contains potent alkaloids that act as neurotoxins, disrupting the central nervous system of humans and animals. Symptoms often begin with nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain shortly after contact or consumption. As toxicity progresses, individuals may experience muscle tremors, seizures, and a dangerous slowing of the heart rate. In extreme cases, the chemical compounds can lead to a complete cessation of breathing and subsequent death.
