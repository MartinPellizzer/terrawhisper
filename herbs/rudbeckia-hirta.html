Black-Eyed Susan

Rudbeckia hirta

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) is a common flowering plant native to North America, known for its bright yellow petals and dark brown central cone, and it has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, particularly in supporting immune function and reducing inflammation.

Taxonomy

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, belonging to the genus Rudbeckia within the order Asterales.

Morphology

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) is an herbaceous perennial with upright, branching stems that are hairy and typically grow 1 to 3 feet tall, bearing yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of dark brown or black tubular flowers.

Distribution

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) is native to eastern North America, ranging from Canada through the United States, and is commonly found in meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils in these regions.

Traditional Uses

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to reduce inflammation, support digestive health, and alleviate symptoms of respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and coughs.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Native American medicine, Chinese herbalism, and European folk remedies, Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) has been used for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and wound-healing properties.

Phytochemistry

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

Pharmacology

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) exhibits anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and mild sedative properties, making it traditionally used to support digestive health, alleviate menstrual discomfort, and promote relaxation.

Botanical Identification

Black-Eyed Susan, scientifically known as Rudbeckia hirta, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is a hardy perennial native to North America, commonly found in prairies and open woodlands. The plant features hairy stems, dark green leaves, and bright yellow petals surrounding a dark brown center. It is also known as Coneflower or Brown-eyed Susan in some regions.

Safety Profile

Black-Eyed Susan, or Rudbeckia hirta, is generally considered safe when used in culinary or herbal contexts. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated for pregnant or breastfeeding women due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
