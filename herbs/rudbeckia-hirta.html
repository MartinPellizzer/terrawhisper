Horticulturally, black-eyed Susan is a low-maintenance perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners of all skill levels. Botanically, the plant boasts daisy-like flowers with bright yellow petals and dark centers, which are rich in antioxidants and other beneficial compounds.

With its ancient roots dating back to Native American and European folklore, black-eyed Susan has been a beloved herb for centuries, used in traditional remedies and rituals for everything from protection to purification.

Rudbeckia hirta, commonly known as black-eyed Susan, is a perennial flowering plant that exhibits several distinctive botanical characteristics.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with dark centers, which are actually composed of hundreds of tiny florets that resemble a miniature sun. Each flower head is up to 2 inches in diameter and has a prominent, conical center surrounded by ray florets that curve outward from the base.

The inflorescence is typically a corymbiform (flat-topped) cluster of several to many flower heads, giving rise to its common name "black-eyed Susan.".
