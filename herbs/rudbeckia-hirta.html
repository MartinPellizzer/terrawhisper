Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta)

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to Eastern North America, United States, and Canada. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and roots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with smooth margins, upper surface glabrous and green, lower surface pubescent and pale green, bearing stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Radially symmetrical flowers with yellow disk and dark brown to black central disc, surrounded by 12 to 20 yellow ray florets arranged in a single plane, with 5 to 10 lobes each, and 5 to 10 stamens arranged in two whorls.
Stems
Elongated, upright growth habit, opposite branching pattern, glabrous surface, pith chamber present, leaf scars arranged in distinct whorls.
