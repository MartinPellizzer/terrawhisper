Acer tetramerum

What's the taxonomical classification of Acer tetramerum?

Acer tetramerum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Sapindales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Sapindaceae, falling under the genus Acer, and is specifically identified by its species name, tetramerum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Acer tetramerum contains bioactive phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and tannins that exhibit significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers have identified specific phytochemicals within its bark and leaves that help mitigate oxidative stress in cellular models. These natural constituents contribute to the plant's traditional use in managing various inflammatory conditions. Studies suggest that the concentration of these medicinal compounds varies depending on the specific part of the plant harvested. Further pharmacological investigations continue to explore how these extracts might be utilized in developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Acer tetramerum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammatory conditions, and digestive ailments. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed through the use of its bark or leaves in traditional remedies. Local practitioners frequently apply preparations of the plant to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of external injuries. Additionally, internal decoctions may be utilized to address gastrointestinal discomfort or systemic inflammation. These applications reflect the diverse therapeutic roles the species plays in folk medicine.
