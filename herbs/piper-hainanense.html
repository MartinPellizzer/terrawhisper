Piper hainanense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper hainanense has ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along its woody stems. The leaf margins are often entire or slightly wavy, while the venation displays a distinct pinnate structure. Its inflorescences consist of slender spikes that emerge from the leaf axils to facilitate pollination. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy berries. These morphological characteristics help distinguish the species within its specific tropical habitat.
