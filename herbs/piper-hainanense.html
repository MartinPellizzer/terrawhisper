Piper hainanense

What's the taxonomical classification of Piper hainanense?

Piper hainanense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Piperales and the family Piperaceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Piper, specifically identified as the species hainanense.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Piper hainanense targets key enzymes and receptors including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and various inflammatory cytokines to exert its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive alkaloids and phenylpropanoids that modulate the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. These compounds specifically inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like prostaglandins and leukotrienes. By interacting with these molecular signaling pathways, the plant demonstrates significant analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties. Furthermore, certain extracts appear to influence nitric oxide synthase activity to regulate vascular and immune responses.

While many species in the Piper genus are studied for medicinal properties, the specific toxicological profile of this plant remains insufficiently documented in clinical literature. Due to the lack of extensive human trials, it is prudent to avoid direct exposure until more comprehensive safety data is established.
