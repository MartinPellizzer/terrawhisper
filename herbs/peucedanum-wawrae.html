Peucedanum wawrae

What's the taxonomical classification of Peucedanum wawrae?

Peucedanum wawrae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, it is specifically categorized within the genus Peucedanum, with its unique designation being the species wawrae.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Peucedanum wawrae can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and phototoxicity when the plant's sap comes into contact with human skin. This reaction often occurs because the plant contains furanocoumarins, which make the skin hypersensitive to ultraviolet light. Exposure to sunlight following contact can lead to painful redness, swelling, and even chemical burns. If the plant is ingested, it may result in gastrointestinal distress or more serious systemic toxicity depending on the dose. Always use protective gear when handling this species to avoid these dermatological complications.
