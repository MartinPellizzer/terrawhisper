Impatiens uliginosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens uliginosa?

Impatiens uliginosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ericales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Balsaminaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Impatiens, with its unique identity defined by the species name uliginosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens uligonosa has succulent, herbaceous stems that reach heights of up to one meter and leaves that are primarily ovate to elliptical with distinctively rounded, or uliginate, bases. The leaf margins are typically serrated, and the venation is prominent across the blade surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often presenting in shades of pale pink or white. The plant is characterized by its fleshy texture, which allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its overall structure is somewhat spreading, forming dense clumps in its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of East and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning from China and Japan through Taiwan and into parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives primarily in humid, shaded forest environments across these diverse territories. Within these regions, its presence is most concentrated in moist, low-to-mid elevation woodlands. While its natural range is centered in the Eastern Palearctic and Indomalayan realms, it remains largely confined to these specific climates. Localized populations are often found near stream banks or in damp, shaded understories where moisture is consistent.
