Blue Lobelia (Lobelia siphilitica) Compounds

Blue Lobelia contains several primary compounds, including alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and respiratory effects. Other notable components include phenolic acids, terpenoids, and tannins, which support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Blue Lobelia (Lobelia siphilitica) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key active compound in Blue Lobelia (Lobelia siphilitica). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in respiratory and nervous system support. Alkaloids in Blue Lobelia may help alleviate symptoms like asthma and anxiety, though safety and dosage require careful consideration.

2. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Blue Lobelia (Lobelia siphilitica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including its use in respiratory and nervous system support. Glycosides in Lobelia may help with calming effects and respiratory relief, though safety and dosage should be carefully considered.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Blue Lobelia (Lobelia siphilitica) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support respiratory health and may aid in reducing mucus production. They are part of the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory conditions.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Blue Lobelia (Lobelia siphilitica). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive health.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Blue Lobelia (Lobelia siphilitica) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects, supporting respiratory health. They are also believed to enhance the plant's traditional use in treating coughs and bronchial congestion.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Blue Lobelia (Lobelia siphilitica). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support respiratory health by reducing mucus production. Tannins also have antimicrobial effects, enhancing the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Blue Lobelia (Lobelia siphilitica). It contributes to the plant's sedative and respiratory effects, often used in herbal medicine for calming and bronchodilating purposes. Its presence supports the plant's traditional use in treating anxiety and respiratory conditions.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Blue Lobelia (Lobelia siphilitica). It contributes to the plant's expectorant properties, helping to loosen mucus in the respiratory tract. Saponins also exhibit mild sedative effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and calming purposes.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Blue Lobelia (Lobelia siphilitica) contains volatile compounds like lobeline and other alkaloids. It is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its respiratory and calming effects. The oil's bioactive components contribute to its therapeutic applications in treating respiratory conditions and promoting mental clarity.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key compound found in Blue Lobelia (Lobelia siphilitica). It contributes to the plant's vibrant blue and purple pigmentation and may support cardiovascular health by enhancing blood vessel function and reducing oxidative stress.
