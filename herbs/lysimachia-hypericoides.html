Lysimachia hypericoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia hypericoides has creeping, rhizomatous stems that form dense mats and produces small, five-petaled bright yellow flowers arranged in terminal clusters. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves that grow in an opposite arrangement along the stems. These leaves are typically smooth and possess a slightly hairy texture on the undersides. The slender, spreading stems allow the plant to colonize ground cover effectively through vegetative spreading. Tiny, inconspicuous bracts often accompany the flower clusters during the blooming season.
