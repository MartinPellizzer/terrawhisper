Athyrium iseanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Athyrium iseanum?

Athyrium iseanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, it falls under the order Polypodiales. This fern is further categorized into the family Aspleniaceae, within the genus Athyrium, and is specifically identified by the species name iseanum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Athyrium iseanum has pinnate fronds characterized by bipinnate to tripinnate divisions, lanceolate pinnae with serrated margins, and a distinctively dark, sometimes purplish, stipe. The rhizomes are typically creeping and covered with pale scales that protect the emerging fronds. Each leaflet features prominent venation that extends toward the finely notched edges. The texture of the foliage is relatively thin and herbaceous, allowing the plant to thrive in moist, shaded forest environments.
