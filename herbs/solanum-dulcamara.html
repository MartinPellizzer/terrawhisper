Solanum Dulcamara

Solanum dulcamara, commonly known as common nightshade, is a perennial herb belonging to the Solanaceae family.

This plant has been used in traditional medicine for its health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, as well as its ability to lower blood pressure and improve cardiovascular health.

The therapeutic actions of Solanum dulcamara are attributed to its bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, glycosides, and saponins, which contribute to its analgesic, antispasmodic, and vasodilatory effects.

Possible Side Effects of solanum dulcamara

Solanum dulcamara can have side effects if used improperly, such as inducing nausea, vomiting, and stomach cramps in some individuals due to the presence of toxic compounds like solanine.

Furthermore, Solanum dulcamara may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, causing adverse reactions.

Prolonged exposure to the herb's toxic compounds can also exacerbate existing health conditions, including kidney and liver problems.
