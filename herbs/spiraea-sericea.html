What's the taxonomical classification of Spiraea sericea?

Spiraea sericea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. It is further categorized under the family Rosaceae, which places it among a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, the plant is identified by the genus Spiraea and is specifically designated by the species name sericea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spiraea sericea has slender, upright stems that reach heights of one to two meters and are characterized by a fine, silky pubescence on both the leaves and the flowering stalks. The leaves are small, lanceolate to narrowly elliptical, and possess finely serrated margins. During the blooming season, the plant produces numerous tiny, white, five-petaled flowers arranged in dense, terminal corymb-like clusters. These delicate blossoms emerge from the upper portions of the stems, creating a light, airy appearance. The overall texture of the foliage and stems is soft due to the presence of minute, silvery hairs.
