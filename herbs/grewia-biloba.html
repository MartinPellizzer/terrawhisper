Grewia biloba

What's the taxonomical classification of Grewia biloba?

Grewia biloba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Malvales and is a member of the family Malvaceae. Ultimately, this plant is defined by its specific genus, Grewia, and its species designation, biloba.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds beta-sitosterol, epi-friedelan-3-ol, friedelin, eatechin, and heneicosanoic acid. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse biological activities observed in Grewia biloba extracts. Research suggests that the presence of these triterpenoids and fatty acids may support various therapeutic properties. The unique combination of these chemical constituents defines the plant's pharmacological profile. Scientists continue to investigate how these molecules interact to produce health benefits.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", six compounds were isolated and identified from Grewia biloba. These identified compounds are friedelin (1), epi-friedelan-3-ol (2), heneicosanoic acid (3), beta-sitosterol (4), Propyl palmitate (5), and eatechin (6). While compounds 1-3, 5, and 6 were isolated from the Grewia genus for the first time, compound 4 was isolated from Grewia biloba for the first time. The isolation process involved silica gel, MCI, Sephadex LH-20, and Rp-18 column chromatography.
