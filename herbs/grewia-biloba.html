Grewia biloba

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Grewia biloba has a woody, deciduous shrub or small tree structure characterized by spreading branches and simple, alternate leaves that are lanceolate or elliptical in shape. The foliage typically features entire or slightly serrated margins and a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often appearing in clusters to facilitate pollination. The fruit develops into a dry, somewhat woody capsule that contains small, angular seeds. This botanical architecture allows the species to thrive in its native arid and semi-arid environments.

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central and Western Asia, specifically spanning from the Levant through Iran to parts of northern India. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Grewia biloba targets inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2 and lipoxygenase, as well as various pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. These bioactive compounds inhibit the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway, which effectively reduces the production of prostaglandins responsible for pain and swelling. The plant's polyphenolic constituents also interact with antioxidant enzymes to mitigate oxidative stress within cellular environments. By modulating these protein pathways, the extract demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties. Furthermore, its interaction with specific signaling proteins helps regulate the body's immune response to prevent chronic inflammation.

Grewia biloba has therapeutic actions that include astringent, hemostatic, emollient, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic properties.
