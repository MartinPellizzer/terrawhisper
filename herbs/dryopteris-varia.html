Dryopteris varia

What's the taxonomical classification of Dryopteris varia?

Dryopteris varia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Polypodiaceae, placing it within the genus Dryopteris, with its specific designation being the species varia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dryopteris varia has a rhizome that is short and scaly, producing bipinnate to tripinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 60 centimeters. The stipe is typically brown and covered in pale, hair-like scales. Each pinna is divided into smaller segments, creating a delicate and lace-like appearance. The underside of the fertile fronds features circular sori protected by a kidney-shaped indusium. These morphological features help distinguish this species from other similar ferns in its habitat.
