Sterculia lanceifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Sterculia lanceifolia?

Sterculia lanceifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Malvales. As a member of the family Malvaceae, it is situated within the genus Sterculia, ultimately identifying it by its specific species name, lanceifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Sterculia lanceifolia contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents are often extracted from the bark, leaves, or roots to be used in traditional medicinal practices. Specifically, the presence of tannins provides significant astringent and antimicrobial properties useful for treating skin ailments. Flavonoids within the plant act as potent antioxidants that help mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Researchers continue to study these specific compounds to better understand their pharmacological mechanisms and potential applications in modern medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Sterculia lanceifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, wounds, stomach aches, and fever. The bark and leaves are often prepared as decoctions to soothe various inflammatory conditions of the body. In traditional medicinal practices, it is frequently applied topically to accelerate the healing of cuts and skin infections. Some practitioners also utilize its extracts to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and regulate digestive functions. These therapeutic properties make it a significant component in various herbal remedies for common ailments.
