Sterculia lanceifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Sterculia lanceifolia?

Sterculia lanceifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Malvales. As a member of the family Malvaceae, it is situated within the genus Sterculia, ultimately identifying it by its specific species name, lanceifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sterculia lanceifolia has lanceolate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, featuring entire margins and a distinctively pointed apex. The foliage is arranged alternately along woody stems that support a medium-sized tree habit. Its flowers are often small and clustered, emerging from the axils or terminal positions of the branches. The plant produces woody follicles that split open to reveal seeds, which are characteristic of the Sterculiaceae family. These morphological traits distinguish it from other species within the same genus.
