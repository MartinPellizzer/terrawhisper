Amomum subulatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Amomum subulatum?

Amomum subulatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Zingiberales. As a member of the family Zingiberaceae, this plant is specifically identified under the genus Amomum, with its unique designation being the species subulatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amomum subulatum has lanceolate, parallel-veined leaves that emerge from a rhizomatous base and produce terminal inflorescences featuring small, pale flowers. The plant structure is characterized by long, slender petioles that support the foliage. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, serving as the primary underground storage organ. The flowers are typically tucked within the leaf sheaths and possess delicate, tubular corollas. This morphology is adapted for growth in moist, shaded tropical environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of medicinal compounds including ascaridole, carvacrol, pinocarvonne, and alpha-thujene within its essential oils. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's potent biological activities and therapeutic potential. The presence of carvacrol often provides significant antimicrobial and antioxidant properties to the organism. Additionally, compounds like ascaridole and alpha-thujene play critical roles in the plant's unique chemical signature. Together, these essential oils form a complex mixture that defines the plant's medicinal efficacy.

As discussed in a study, Amomum subulatum contains bioactive components such as flavonoids, phenols, vitamins, steroids, and essential oils. The total protein contents for Amomum subulatum were 235.03 ± 21.49 and 227.49 ± 25.82 mg/g dry weight, though the fruit contains more lipids than proteins. Carvacrol levels in Amomum subulatum are 20 times higher than in other species, while lower levels of α-Thujene, Phyllanderenes, Ascaridole, and Pinocarvone were also observed. According to the DPPH assay, the seed extract of Amomum subulatum exhibited the highest antioxidant activity at 78.26±9.27 %. Furthermore, the FRAP assay showed antioxidant activity of 20.14±1.11 µmol trolox g-1 DW for Amomum subulatum, and the fruit extract showed an anti-lipid peroxidation value of 6.08±0.35.
