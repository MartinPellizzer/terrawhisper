Kopsia arborea

What's the taxonomical classification of Kopsia arborea?

Kopsia arborea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Gentianales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Kopsia, and its unique species designation, arborea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kopsia arborea has a woody, arborescent habit characterized by a single trunk and a spreading crown of pinnately compound leaves. The foliage exhibits a lush, green appearance that provides a dense canopy for the tree. Its flowering structures emerge from the axils of the leaves, displaying specialized floral morphology typical of the family. The bark and stem structures support the significant height and biomass of this tropical woody species.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 19(R)-methoxytubotaiwine, 19(S)-methoxytubotaiwine, 19-OH-(-)-eburnamonine, valparicine, and apparicine. These specific alkaloids are often studied for their complex chemical structures and potential biological activities. Research into these substances frequently focuses on their pharmacological properties within various therapeutic models. The presence of such diverse indole alkaloids makes the species a subject of significant interest in phytochemical investigations. Understanding the concentration of these molecules helps scientists map the plant's chemical profile.

As discussed in a study, the stem-bark extract of Kopsia arborea contains nine new indole alkaloids including rhazinoline (1), 19(S)-methoxytubotaiwine (2), 19(R)-methoxytubotaiwine (3), kopsamidine A (4), kopsamidine B (5), kopsinidine A (6), kopsinidine B (7), paucidactine C (8), and pericine N-oxide (9). The plant also yielded several recently reported novel indoles and 34 other known ones. Among these compounds, valparicine (12) exhibited pronounced cytotoxic effects. This specific compound showed IC(50) values of 13.0 microM against KB cells and 0.91 microM against Jurkat cells.
