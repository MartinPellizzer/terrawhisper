Kopsia arborea

What's the taxonomical classification of Kopsia arborea?

Kopsia arborea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Gentianales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Kopsia, and its unique species designation, arborea.

The foliage exhibits a lush, green appearance that provides a dense canopy for the tree. Its flowering structures emerge from the axils of the leaves, displaying specialized floral morphology typical of the family. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several unique medicinal alkaloids including arboflorine, arboriidinine, arborisidine, arbornaamine, and kopsamine. These specific bioactive compounds are primarily isolated from the bark and stem of the species. Researchers study these chemical structures to understand their potential pharmacological properties in treating various ailments. Each of these alkaloids possesses distinct molecular configurations that contribute to the plant's complex chemical profile. The identification of these substances provides a foundation for future drug development and natural product chemistry.

According to a study published by "Organic letters", the Malayan Kopsia arborea contains a new indole alkaloid known as arboflorine. This medicinal compound is described as an unusual pentacyclic monoterpenoid indole alkaloid. The structure of arboflorine incorporates a third nitrogen atom within its novel pentacyclic carbon skeleton. Spectroscopic analysis was utilized to establish the structure of this compound. A possible biogenetic pathway from a preakuammicine-type precursor is also presented for this specific alkaloid.
