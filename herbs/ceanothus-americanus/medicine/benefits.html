10 Health Benefits Of Ceanothus Americanus (New Jersey Tea)

Ceanothus americanus has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties due to its high concentration of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These medicinal properties can help alleviate respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma by reducing inflammation in the lungs and airways.

Additionally, its antimicrobial compounds can combat infections and promote wound healing, potentially improving quality of life for individuals with chronic conditions or those undergoing surgery.

1. Reduces inflammation

Ceanothus americanus, also known as New Jersey tea, reduces inflammation because of its rich antioxidant and phenolic content.

The plant's extracts have been shown to inhibit pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, thereby decreasing the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes that contribute to inflammation.

Additionally, Ceanothus americanus contains flavonoids and polyphenols, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help alleviate symptoms associated with conditions such as arthritis, asthma, and other inflammatory disorders.

2. Supports digestive health

Ceanothus americanus supports digestive health because of its rich antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's leaves and flowers contain flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes that have been shown to soothe the digestive tract, reducing inflammation and discomfort associated with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), and other gastrointestinal disorders.

Its bitter compounds also stimulate digestion and improve gut motility, promoting regularity and overall digestive well-being.

3. Boosts immune system

Ceanothus americanus boosts immune system because of its rich concentration of antioxidants and flavonoids.

Ceanothus americanus, also known as New Jersey tea, regulates blood sugar levels because of its unique composition of bioactive compounds.

Ceanothus americanus improves cardiovascular health because of its rich antioxidant and flavonoid content.

Ceanothus americanus, also known as New Jersey tea, treats skin conditions because of its impressive array of bioactive compounds.
