10 Possible Side Effects Of Ceanothus Americanus (New Jersey Tea)

Ceanothus americanus has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

These side effects can occur if the herb is taken in large doses or if it interacts with other medications.

For instance, excessive consumption can lead to dehydration, which can worsen conditions like kidney disease or exacerbate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome, significantly affecting a person's quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Ceanothus americanus if used imporperly.

Ceanothus americanus causes allergic reactions when some individuals come into contact with its leaves, stems, or flowers.

The allergenic compounds present in the plant's sap can trigger an immune response, resulting in symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling of the skin, respiratory issues like congestion and coughing, and potentially life-threatening reactions like anaphylaxis.

Ceanothus americanus triggers skin rashes in some individuals due to its allergic potential.

The plant contains compounds such as saponins and terpenoids, which can cause an allergic reaction when ingested or applied topically.

These compounds can bind to skin proteins, triggering an immune response and leading to inflammation and irritation, resulting in a rash.
