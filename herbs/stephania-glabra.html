Stephania glabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Stephania glabra?

Stephania glabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned within the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Menispermaceae, falling under the genus Stephania, and is specifically identified by the species epithet glabra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephania glabra has a tuberous, woody caudex that serves as a primary storage organ, supporting slender, climbing stems with smooth, glabrous surfaces. The plant's growth habit is determined by these flexible vines that can sprawl across substrates or climb upward. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including 11-hydroxypalmatine, berberines, bisbenzylisoquinolines, palmatine, and stearphanine. These specific alkaloids are known for their significant biological activities and therapeutic potential in various pharmacological studies. The presence of berberines and palmatine often contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the complex structures of the bisbenzylisoquinolines and stearphanine play critical roles in the plant's chemical profile. Such a concentration of specialized metabolites makes the plant a subject of interest for developing new pharmaceutical treatments.

As discussed in a study, the tubers of Stephania glabra contain the palmaitne derivative 11-hydroxypalmatine (4) alongside three known alkaloids including palmatine (1), dehydrocorydalmine (2), and stepharanine (3). The hypoglycemic activity of compound 4 was evaluated in mice following an alloxan injection of 60 mg/kg, i.v. The test compound was administered orally at doses of 25, 50, and 100 mg/kg, p.o. after 36 h. Treatment with the test compound resulted in a 52% reduction in blood glucose. This effect was compared to a 54% reduction in the glibenclamide positive control and a 27% reduction in the diabetic control.
