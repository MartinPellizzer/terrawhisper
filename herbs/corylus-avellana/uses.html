Hazelnut (Corylus avellana) Uses

Hazelnuts are used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and cough due to their anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. They may also support recovery from influenza and respiratory infections. Additionally, hazelnuts can aid in wound healing and may help with skin infections and allergic rhinitis.

Hazelnut (Corylus avellana) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Hazelnuts have been used traditionally to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Their anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce bronchial irritation and ease coughing. Rich in vitamin E and antioxidants, they support immune function and may aid in clearing mucus. Regular consumption of hazelnuts can complement other bronchitis treatments in herbal medicine.

2. Asthma

Hazelnuts have been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. They contain anti-inflammatory compounds that may help reduce airway inflammation in asthma. However, they are not a primary treatment and should be used alongside conventional therapies under professional guidance.

3. Influenza

Hazelnuts contain compounds that may support immune function and reduce inflammation, potentially aiding in the management of influenza symptoms. They provide essential nutrients like vitamin E and zinc, which are important for immune health. However, they should not replace conventional medical treatments for influenza.

4. Respiratory infection

Hazelnuts have been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of mild respiratory infections. Their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties may help reduce coughing and soothe irritated airways. They are often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease symptoms and promote healing. Regular consumption may enhance the body's natural defenses against respiratory ailments.

5. Cough

Hazelnuts are used in herbal medicine to soothe coughs due to their anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. They help loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. A tea made from roasted hazelnuts can provide relief for persistent or dry coughs. Regular consumption may support overall respiratory health.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Hazelnut has been used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and sneezing by supporting immune balance. Its essential oils and extracts are often incorporated into formulations for respiratory support. Regular use may help manage seasonal allergies when combined with other herbal remedies.

7. Respiratory infections

Hazelnuts have been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Their high vitamin C content helps boost the immune system, aiding in the prevention and recovery from respiratory infections. The anti-inflammatory properties of hazelnuts may also help reduce symptoms of conditions like bronchitis and coughing. Regular consumption can contribute to maintaining clear airways and overall respiratory wellness.

8. Respiratory allergies

Hazelnuts contain compounds that may help alleviate respiratory allergy symptoms by reducing inflammation and supporting immune function. They are often used in herbal preparations to ease nasal congestion and seasonal allergies. Regular consumption of hazelnuts can contribute to overall respiratory health when incorporated into a balanced diet.

9. Skin infections

Hazelnut has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil extracted from its nuts contains compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and soothe inflamed skin. It is often applied topically to address conditions like eczema, fungal infections, and minor wounds. Its natural healing properties make it a valuable ingredient in herbal skincare formulations.

10. Wound healing

Hazelnuts support wound healing through their high content of vitamin E, which promotes skin repair and reduces inflammation. They also contain essential fatty acids that enhance tissue regeneration and prevent infection, making them a valuable component in traditional herbal medicine for treating minor cuts and abrasions.
