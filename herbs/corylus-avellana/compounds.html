Hazelnut (Corylus avellana) Compounds

The primary compounds in Hazelnut include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins contribute to astringency, while coumarin has anticoagulant effects. Saponins and terpenoids support immune function, and glycosides may aid in detoxification. Essential oils and alkaloids provide additional therapeutic benefits, and anthocyanins offer protective effects against oxidative stress.

Hazelnut (Corylus avellana) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Hazelnut (Corylus avellana). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing inflammation. These compounds are also linked to improved cardiovascular health and neuroprotection.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Hazelnut (Corylus avellana). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the therapeutic use of Hazelnut in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Hazelnut (Corylus avellana). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antioxidant effects, helping to protect cells from oxidative stress. These properties make tannin an important component in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a naturally occurring compound found in Hazelnut (Corylus avellana). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has mild anticoagulant effects. Coumarin is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential benefits in supporting cardiovascular health.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Hazelnut (Corylus avellana). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting mild cleansing and anti-inflammatory effects. Saponin may support digestive health and immune function when used as part of traditional herbal preparations.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Hazelnut (Corylus avellana). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are often extracted for use in herbal formulations. Their presence supports the therapeutic potential of Hazelnut in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Hazelnut (Corylus avellana). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in Hazelnut are often used in herbal medicine for their mild laxative and antispasmodic effects.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Hazelnut (Corylus avellana). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like limonene and cineole, which contribute to its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This oil is often used in herbal medicine for its soothing effects on the respiratory and digestive systems.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Hazelnut (Corylus avellana). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though specific alkaloids in hazelnut are less well-studied compared to other herbal sources. Its presence may support the plant's traditional use in mild medicinal applications.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Hazelnut (Corylus avellana). It contributes to the nut's antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. Found in the skin of the nut, anthocyanin may also aid in anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits.
