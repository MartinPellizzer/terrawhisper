10 Possible Side Effects Of Corylus Avellana (Hazel)

Corylus avellana has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, stomach cramps, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can occur due to excessive consumption or individual sensitivities to its flavonoids and tannins.

In severe cases, they can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, and respiratory issues, significantly affecting one's quality of life by limiting daily activities, sleep, and overall well-being.

Corylus avellana increases blood pressure due to its high content of alpha-linolenic acid (ALA), a polyunsaturated fatty acid that can raise blood pressure levels in some individuals.

Additionally, the natural compounds present in hazelnuts, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, may also contribute to this effect by stimulating vasoconstriction and increasing peripheral resistance.

As a result, people with hypertension or cardiovascular conditions may experience a rise in their blood pressure when consuming large amounts of hazelnuts or using Corylus avellana supplements regularly.
