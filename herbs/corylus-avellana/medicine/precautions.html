10 Precautions To Take When Using Corylus Avellana (Hazel)

Corylus avellana has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as allergic reactions and interactions with medications.

It's crucial to take these precautions to avoid side effects like hives, itching, or difficulty breathing. For instance, not considering a hazel allergy may lead to a severe reaction, while ignoring interactions with blood thinners may cause excessive bleeding.

Always consult a healthcare professional before using hazel medicinally.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Corylus avellana medicinally.

1. Seek professional advice immediately

When using Corylus avellana medicinally, it's important to take correct dosage regularly because the plant's extracts can have potent effects on the body.

Taking incorrect doses or irregularly can lead to adverse reactions, including stomach upset, dizziness, and allergic responses. Moreover, inconsistent dosing may reduce the herb's efficacy in treating the intended condition.

To ensure safe and effective use, it's crucial to follow a doctor's guidance or adhere to established dosage standards for any medicinal product containing Corylus avellana.

2. Seek professional advice immediately

When using Corylus avellana medicinally, it's important to consult a doctor before using.

Hazelnuts and hazel leaves have been traditionally used to treat various health issues, but their interactions with certain medications can be unpredictable. The active compounds in hazelnuts may alter blood clotting levels or interact with anticoagulant medications, while excessive consumption of hazelnuts can lead to an imbalance of nutrients.

A healthcare professional's guidance is essential to ensure safe and effective treatment, especially for individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or taking medication.

3. Seek professional advice immediately

When using Corylus avellana medicinally, it's important to monitor blood pressure levels.

As Corylus avellana can interact with blood thinners and antihypertensive medications, monitoring blood pressure is crucial to prevent potential complications such as hypotension or hypertension episodes.

Additionally, people with pre-existing hypertension should consult a healthcare professional before using hazel medicinally, as it may exacerbate blood pressure issues if not monitored properly.

4. Seek professional advice immediately

When using Corylus avellana medicinally, it's important to be aware of allergic reactions.

This precaution is crucial because the plant contains compounds that can cause skin irritation, itching, and rashes in some individuals. Furthermore, allergic reactions can range from mild to severe, and if left untreated, may even lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition.

Therefore, it's essential to conduct patch tests or small-scale trials before using hazel medicinally to identify any potential allergies and take necessary measures to avoid adverse reactions.

5. Seek professional advice immediately

When using Corylus avellana medicinally, it's important to avoid exceeding recommended amounts.

This precaution is crucial because consuming excessive amounts of hazelnut oil or extracts can lead to gastrointestinal issues, such as stomach upset and diarrhea, due to its high fatty acid content.

Additionally, overuse may also interact with certain medications, including blood thinners, diabetes medications, and thyroid hormones, potentially compromising their effectiveness.

6. Seek professional advice immediately

When using Corylus avellana medicinally, it's important to store safely away from children.

This precaution is crucial because the seeds of the plant contain a toxic compound called amygdalin, which can release cyanide upon ingestion. Children are more likely to ingest small quantities of the seed accidentally, potentially leading to poisoning.

Storing the medication out of reach of children ensures their safety and prevents any potential harm or discomfort caused by accidental exposure.

7. Seek professional advice immediately

When using Corylus avellana medicinally, it's important to follow proper preparation instructions.

This is crucial because the plant contains compounds that can be irritating or even toxic if not processed correctly. Improper preparation can lead to allergic reactions, gastrointestinal issues, and skin problems.

By following established guidelines, individuals can ensure the safe and effective use of Corylus avellana, minimizing the risk of adverse effects and maximizing its potential therapeutic benefits.

8. Seek professional advice immediately

When using Corylus avellana medicinally, it's important to discard expired or spoiled product.

This is crucial because expired or spoiled hazelnuts may lose their potency and efficacy, potentially leading to reduced therapeutic benefits or even adverse reactions.

Moreover, expired or spoiled products can harbor harmful contaminants that can compromise the integrity of the medicinal preparation, posing a risk to human health.

9. Seek professional advice immediately

When using Corylus avellana medicinally, it's important to keep out of direct sunlight.

This is crucial because the extract can be sensitive to UV light, which can cause degradation and loss of potency.

Furthermore, direct sunlight can also trigger allergic reactions or skin irritation in some individuals, making it essential to store the product in a shaded area to ensure its efficacy and safety.

10. Seek professional advice immediately

When using Corylus avellana medicinally, it's important to watch for interactions with medications.

As a rich source of antioxidants and flavonoids, hazel may enhance the effects of certain medications, such as blood thinners, and increase the risk of bleeding. Additionally, its stimulatory properties may amplify the actions of antidepressants, leading to excessive nervousness or anxiety.

Closely monitoring the combination and dosage is crucial to ensure safe and effective use.
