10 Health Benefits Of Corylus Avellana (Hazel)

Corylus avellana, also known as hazel, has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The leaves and bark contain flavonoids and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These can help alleviate symptoms of arthritis, improve digestion, and boost the immune system.

For example, a tea made from hazel leaves can provide relief for people suffering from eczema and other skin conditions, while its berries can be used to treat digestive issues and support eye health.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Corylus avellana.

1. Reduces risk of heart disease

Corylus avellana reduces risk of heart disease because it is rich in antioxidants and omega-3 fatty acids.

These nutrients help to lower cholesterol levels and prevent the formation of blood clots, which can reduce the risk of heart attacks and strokes.

Additionally, hazelnuts have been shown to improve blood vessel function, reducing inflammation and improving overall cardiovascular health.

2. Improves digestion

Corylus avellana improves digestion because of its rich composition of fiber, vitamins, and minerals.

The high water content in hazelnuts helps to soften stool, reducing constipation and alleviating symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

Additionally, the antioxidant properties of hazelnuts may help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract, promoting a healthy gut microbiome and overall digestion.

3. Boosts brain function

Corylus avellana boosts brain function because of its rich antioxidant and flavonoid content.

The antioxidants present in hazelnuts help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the brain, which can lead to improved cognitive function and memory.

Additionally, the flavonoids found in hazelnuts have been shown to improve blood flow to the brain, enhancing focus, concentration, and overall mental clarity.

4. Enhances skin health

Corylus avellana enhances skin health because of its rich antioxidant and vitamin E content.

The oil extracted from its nuts has been shown to soothe and calm irritated skin, reducing inflammation and promoting collagen production.

Additionally, the antioxidants present in hazelnut oil help protect the skin from environmental stressors and damage caused by free radicals, resulting in a more radiant and youthful complexion.

5. Strengthens bones

Corylus avellana strengthens bones because it is rich in calcium and vitamin K. Calcium is essential for building and maintaining strong bone density, while vitamin K helps to regulate blood clotting and bone metabolism.

The combination of these nutrients in hazelnuts can help to improve bone mineralization and reduce the risk of fractures.

6. Reduces inflammation

Corylus avellana reduces inflammation because of its rich antioxidant and polyphenol content.

The seeds and leaves of the hazelnut plant contain quercetin, a powerful flavonoid that has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes, thereby reducing oxidative stress and inflammation in the body.

This natural anti-inflammatory effect may help alleviate symptoms associated with chronic conditions such as arthritis, cardiovascular disease, and neurodegenerative disorders.

7. Regulates blood sugar

Corylus avellana regulates blood sugar because it contains a unique combination of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds have been shown to exhibit anti-diabetic properties by improving insulin sensitivity, inhibiting alpha-glucosidase enzyme activity, and reducing oxidative stress in the body.

By regulating blood sugar levels, hazel extract may help prevent or manage conditions like type 2 diabetes.

8. Lowers bad cholesterol

Corylus avellana lowers bad cholesterol because of its unique combination of soluble fibers, antioxidants, and polyphenols.

The soluble fibers in hazelnuts help bind to bile acids in the gut, which reduces the amount of cholesterol produced in the liver, leading to lower LDL (bad) cholesterol levels.

Additionally, the antioxidants and polyphenols present in hazelnuts have been shown to reduce inflammation and improve blood lipid profiles, further contributing to the lowering of bad cholesterol.

9. Increases good cholesterol

Corylus avellana increases good cholesterol because it is rich in alpha-linolenic acid (ALA), a type of omega-3 fatty acid.

ALA helps to increase the levels of high-density lipoprotein (HDL) or "good" cholesterol in the blood, which can help to remove excess cholesterol from the bloodstream and reduce the risk of heart disease.

Additionally, hazel also contains other beneficial compounds such as polyphenols and flavonoids that may contribute to its potential cardiovascular benefits.

10. Prevents cell damage

Corylus avellana prevents cell damage because of its rich antioxidant properties.

The nut and leaf extracts contain high levels of phenolic compounds, flavonoids, and saponins, which scavenge free radicals and neutralize oxidative stress.

This helps to protect cellular structures from damage caused by inflammation, radiation, and other environmental factors, ultimately leading to a reduced risk of chronic diseases such as cancer, cardiovascular disease, and neurodegenerative disorders.
