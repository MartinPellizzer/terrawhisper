10 Medicinal Constituents Of Corylus Avellana (Hazel)

Corylus avellana has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins.

These constituents possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The flavonoids, specifically quercetin and kaempferol, have been shown to improve cardiovascular health by reducing inflammation and improving blood lipid profiles.

This can lead to a reduced risk of heart disease and stroke, ultimately improving the quality of life for individuals with cardiovascular conditions.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Corylus avellana.

1. Quercetin

Corylus avellana quercetin is a type of flavonoid glycoside found in its nuts and leaves.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Quercetin is believed to have potential health benefits, including reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Its presence in hazelnuts also contributes to their distinctive flavor and aroma.

2. Isoquercitrin

Corylus avellana isoquercitrin is a bioflavonoid compound derived from its leaves and twigs.

This antioxidant-rich compound has been found to exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it potentially useful in the treatment of various diseases such as cardiovascular disorders, cancer, and microbial infections.

Isoquercitrin has also been shown to have protective effects against oxidative stress and cell damage.

3. Avicularin

Corylus avellana avicularin is a type of flavonoid glycoside that can be found in the bark and leaves of the plant.

It has been traditionally used in traditional medicine for its antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

Avicularin has also been shown to have potential applications in the prevention and treatment of various diseases, including cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

4. Betulinic acid

Corylus avellana betulinic acid is a type of phenolic compound found in the bark and leaves of the European hazelnut tree.

It has been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, making it a potential therapeutic agent for various diseases.

Additionally, betulinic acid has been reported to have antitumor and immunomodulatory effects, suggesting its potential as a natural cancer treatment or immune system enhancer.

5. Ursolic acid

Corylus avellana ursolic acid is a triterpenoid compound found in the bark and leaves of the species.

It has been extensively studied for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral effects. Ursolic acid has been shown to inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells and may have applications in the treatment of diseases such as arthritis and cardiovascular disease.

Its unique chemical structure also makes it a promising candidate for use in pharmaceuticals.

6. Gallic acid

Corylus avellana gallic acid is a phenolic compound found in the leaves of the European hazelnut tree.

It has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits and antioxidant properties. Gallic acid has been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral activities, making it a promising compound in the development of new pharmaceuticals and nutraceuticals.

Its presence is believed to contribute to the nut's distinct flavor and aroma.

7. Ellagic acid

Corylus avellana ellagic acid is a polyphenolic compound found in high concentrations in its nuts, leaves, and bark.

This potent antioxidant has been extensively studied for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and anticancer properties. Ellagic acid has been shown to exhibit cytotoxic activity against various cancer cells, inhibiting their growth and inducing apoptosis (cell death).

Its potential health benefits make it an attractive natural compound for the prevention and treatment of chronic diseases.

8. Kaempferol

Corylus avellana kaempferol is a flavonoid glycoside that has been isolated from the fruit of the European hazelnut tree.

It exhibits various biological activities, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Kaempferol has also been reported to have potential therapeutic effects on cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Its chemical structure is characterized by a flavonoid backbone with a disaccharide chain attached to the 3-hydroxy group of kaempferol.

9. Rhamnetin

Corylus avellana rhamnetin is a type of flavonoid compound found in the bark and leaves of the European Hazel tree.

This yellow pigment has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticancer properties.

Rhamnetin has been shown to exhibit significant bioactivity against various diseases, such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders, making it an area of ongoing research in the fields of pharmacy and medicine.

10. Isorhapontigenin

Corylus avellana isorhapontigenin is a flavonoid compound isolated from the leaves of the hazelnut tree.

It has been shown to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, as well as potential therapeutic properties for cardiovascular disease and cancer prevention.

Isorhapontigenin has also been found to have neuroprotective and antimicrobial activities, making it a promising compound for further research and development.
