What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Corylus Avellana (Hazel)?

Corylus avellana, also known as hazel, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation, improving cognitive function, and lowering cholesterol levels.

The medicinal constituents of Corylus avellana include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and betulinic acid, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The herb can be prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, which are often used to treat cardiovascular and neurological disorders.

Side effects may include gastrointestinal upset or allergic reactions, and users should consult a healthcare professional before using the herb, particularly if taking medications or with pre-existing conditions.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Corylus avellana.

What are the health benefits of Corylus avellana?

Corylus avellana has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health due to its high content of antioxidants and omega-3 fatty acids.

The nuts are also rich in vitamin E and copper, which can help protect against cell damage and promote healthy skin, hair, and eyesight.

Additionally, hazelnuts have been shown to support cognitive function and may even help alleviate symptoms of depression.

What are the active constituents of Corylus avellana?

The health benefits of Corylus avellana comes from its active constituents, such as flavonoids (quercetin and kaempferol), phenolic acids, and terpenes.

These compounds possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which are believed to contribute to the plant's traditional uses in treating various health conditions, including cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and respiratory issues.

Additionally, hazel is also rich in vitamins, minerals, and omega-3 fatty acids, making it a valuable addition to a healthy diet.

What are the medicinal preparations of Corylus avellana?

Corylus avellana has different medicinal preparations, such as leaves, bark, nuts, and oil.

The leaves are used in teas to treat digestive issues and respiratory problems. The bark is employed in infusions or tinctures to alleviate fever, inflammation, and skin conditions.

Hazelnut oil, extracted from the nuts, is applied topically to soothe skin irritations and wounds, while also being consumed as a dietary supplement for heart health and antioxidant properties.

What are the possible side effect of using Corylus avellana improperly?

Improper use of Corylus avellana increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions like hives, itching, and swelling, digestive issues including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and stomach cramps, respiratory problems like coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath, increased heart rate and blood pressure, anxiety, insomnia, headaches, dizziness, and skin rashes.

In rare cases, it may also cause more severe reactions such as anaphylaxis or liver damage.

What precautions to take when using Corylus avellana medicinally?

Before using Corylus avellana for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or are taking medications that may interact with its compounds.

Additionally, be cautious when consuming hazelnut oil or capsules, as high doses can cause gastrointestinal side effects, and avoid using hazel products during pregnancy or breastfeeding without proper guidance.
