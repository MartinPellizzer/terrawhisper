What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Corylus Avellana (Hazel)?

Corylus avellana, also known as hazel, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

The herb is rich in flavonoids, phenolic acids, and fatty acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. Hazel can be prepared as a tea, infusion, or extract, and is often used to treat conditions such as digestive issues and skin irritations. Side effects of consuming hazel may include gastrointestinal upset and allergic reactions.

Precautions should be taken when using hazel, particularly for individuals with allergies or sensitive skin, and in large doses.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Corylus avellana.

What are the health benefits of Corylus avellana?

Corylus avellana, also known as hazel, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

The nuts of the hazel tree are rich in healthy fats, protein, and fiber, making them an excellent snack for weight management and digestion.

Additionally, the leaves and bark of the tree have been traditionally used to treat fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, showcasing the broad-spectrum health benefits of this versatile plant.

What are the active constituents of Corylus avellana?

Corylus avellana, also known as hazel, has active constituents such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

The leaves contain corilagin, a unique tannin with potential cardiovascular and anti-cancer effects. The bark and leaves also contain betulin, a triterpenoid with antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These constituents contribute to the plant's traditional uses in treating digestive issues, skin conditions, and wounds.

What are the medicinal preparations of Corylus avellana?

Corylus avellana, also known as hazel, has medicinal preparations such as extracts, tinctures, and infusions that are used to treat various health conditions.

The nut and leaf extracts are valued for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. They are often used to treat digestive issues, skin problems, and respiratory infections.

Hazel extracts are also used in traditional medicine to lower cholesterol and blood pressure levels, as well as to alleviate menstrual cramps and menopausal symptoms.

What are the possible side effect of using Corylus avellana improperly?

Improper use of Corylus avellana, also known as hazel, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as gastrointestinal disturbances, including diarrhea and nausea.

Allergic reactions like skin rashes and itching can also occur, particularly in individuals with nut allergies. Additionally, excessive consumption of hazelnut oil may cause cardiovascular problems due to its high fat content.

These side effects can be mitigated by consuming hazel in moderation and under medical supervision.

What precautions to take when using Corylus avellana medicinally?

Before using Corylus avellana, also known as hazel, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the correct dosage and ensure safe use, particularly if you have allergies or are pregnant/breastfeeding.

Also, be aware of potential interactions with other medications, and avoid using hazel preparations that have not been properly processed and dried, as this can lead to contamination and adverse effects.
