Chirita pinnatifida

What's the taxonomical classification of Chirita pinnatifida?

Chirita pinnatifida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Gesneriaceae, falling under the genus Chirita, and is specifically identified by its species name, pinnatifida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chirita pinnatifida has deeply lobed or pinnatifid leaves that are arranged oppositely along a stem with a distinctively textured surface.

The leaf margins are irregular and cut significantly toward the central vein, giving the foliage a feathered appearance. These leaves typically possess a somewhat fleshy or succulent quality depending on environmental moisture levels. The plant also produces tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils to facilitate specific pollinators.

Small hairs may be present on the stems or leaf undersides to provide protection or moisture retention.
