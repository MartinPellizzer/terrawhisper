Chirita pinnatifida

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chirita pinnatifida has deeply lobed or pinnatifid leaves that are arranged oppositely along a stem with a distinctively textured surface. The leaf margins are irregular and cut significantly toward the central vein, giving the foliage a feathered appearance. These leaves typically possess a somewhat fleshy or succulent quality depending on environmental moisture levels. The plant also produces tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils to facilitate specific pollinators. Small hairs may be present on the stems or leaf undersides to provide protection or moisture retention.
