Microtropis tetragona

What's the taxonomical classification of Microtropis tetragona?

Microtropis tetragona belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Celastrales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Celastraceae, which contains the genus Microtropis. Ultimately, the specific identification of this organism is defined by its species name, tetragona.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Microtropis tetragona targets the Na+/K+-ATPase enzyme through the action of cardiac glycosides like microtropine and related steroid compounds. These molecules bind to the alpha subunit of the sodium-potassium pump, effectively inhibiting its ability to transport ions across the cell membrane. This inhibition leads to an increase in intracellular sodium concentration, which subsequently modulates calcium levels via the sodium-calcium exchanger. Such biochemical changes result in increased cardiac contractility and potential cytotoxic effects in cancer cells. These specific interactions underpin the plant's traditional use in treating certain ailments while also contributing to its significant toxicity.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Microtropis tetragona can cause severe toxicity including vomiting, diarrhea, and potential cardiac complications due to the presence of toxic glycosides in its tissues. These chemical compounds interfere with normal heart rhythms and can lead to life-threatening arrhythmias if ingested. Exposure to the sap or leaves may also trigger localized skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Because the specific concentration of toxins varies between plants, the severity of these side effects can be unpredictable. Careful avoidance of all parts of the plant is necessary to prevent accidental poisoning.
