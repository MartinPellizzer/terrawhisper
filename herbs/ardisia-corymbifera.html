Ardisia corymbifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Ardisia corymbifera?

Ardisia corymbifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Ericales. It is positioned within the family Primulaceae, specifically falling under the genus Ardisia, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name corymbifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia corymbifera has simple, elliptical to oblong leaves that are dark green, glossy, and arranged oppositely along its woody stems.

The foliage typically features entire margins and acute or acuminate tips, providing a dense appearance to the shrub or small tree. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense, flat-topped corymbose structures. These flowers eventually give rise to small, fleshy drupes that serve as the plant's characteristic fruit.

The overall structure is characterized by a compact branching habit and a leathery leaf texture.
