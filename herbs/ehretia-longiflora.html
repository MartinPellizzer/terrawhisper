Ehretia longiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ehretia longiflora has simple, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins and a slightly coriaceous texture. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the woody stems. Its most distinctive feature is the production of long, tubular, white flowers that emerge in dense clusters. These blossoms possess a prominent corolla tube and are characterized by their delicate, elongated structure. The plant also develops small, fleshy fruits that follow the flowering stage.

      Ehretia longiflora is prepared as decoctions, infusions, pastes, and poultices to utilize its various therapeutic properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ehretia longiflora can cause skin irritation, localized allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to redness, itching, or dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If the plant is swallowed, it can trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Some users might also experience more systemic responses depending on the dosage and individual sensitivity. Always use caution and avoid direct contact with the leaves and stems to minimize these risks.
