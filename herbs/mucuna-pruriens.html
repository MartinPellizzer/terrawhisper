What to know about Mucuna pruriens before using it medicinally

Mucuna pruriens, commonly known as cowhage, is a tropical plant with a long history of traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine.

The main medicinal uses of Mucuna pruriens are in the treatment of various health conditions, including anxiety, depression, Parkinson's disease, and erectile dysfunction. Its main health benefits include improved mood, reduced stress and anxiety, enhanced cognitive function, and improved sleep quality. The plant's therapeutic properties are attributed to its dopaminergic and antioxidant activities, which are mediated by its main bioactive compounds, including L-DOPA, flavonoids, and alkaloids.

In traditional herbal medicine, Mucuna pruriens is often prepared as a decoction or infusion, typically made by boiling the seeds in water to extract their medicinal properties.

Featured Study:

According to a study published by JPMA, The Journal of the Pakistan Medical Association, Mucuna pruriens has been found to decrease blood glucose levels in both normal and diabetic rabbits. This decrease in blood glucose levels suggests that Mucuna pruriens may help lower blood sugar levels, which can be beneficial for individuals with diabetes. The study also found high levels of minerals like manganese and zinc in Mucuna pruriens seeds, which may contribute to its hypoglycaemic effects.
