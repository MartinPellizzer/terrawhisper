Deutzia hypoglauca

What's the taxonomical classification of Deutzia hypoglauca?

Deutzia hypoglauca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cornales. This plant is further categorized within the family Hydrangeaceae and is identified by the genus Deutzia, with its specific designation being the species hypoglauca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Deutzia hypoglauca has opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are distinctly glaucous, meaning they possess a pale, waxy, bluish-green coating on their undersides.

The plant grows as a deciduous shrub with slender, arching woody stems. Small, white, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These blossoms typically feature five petals and are supported by delicate pedicels.

The overall structure of the foliage remains compact and dense throughout the growing period.
