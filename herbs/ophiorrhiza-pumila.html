Ophiorrhiza pumila

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiorrhiza pumila?

Ophiorrhiza pumila belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Ophiorrhiza, specifically identifying it as the species pumila.

Such traditional applications highlight the biological importance of the plant's subterranean anatomy.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Ophiorrhiza pumila targets various enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase enzymes and inflammatory mediators, to exert its medicinal effects. The plant contains bioactive secondary metabolites like alkaloids and terpenoids that interfere with the arachidonic acid pathway to reduce inflammation. These compounds often interact with prostaglandin synthesis to alleviate pain and swelling in biological systems. Furthermore, certain extracts show an ability to modulate antioxidant enzymes, helping to mitigate oxidative stress at a cellular level. Such multi-target interactions contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and skin ailments.
