Ophiorrhiza pumila

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiorrhiza pumila?

Ophiorrhiza pumila belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Ophiorrhiza, specifically identifying it as the species pumila.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophiorrhiza pumila has creeping rhizomes that produce small, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged in a basal rosette or along the stem.

The foliage is typically bright green with a smooth texture and prominent venation. Its flowering structures consist of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are often pale in color and feature a delicate, elongated shape.

The plant's overall growth habit is compact and low-growing, characteristic of many forest floor species.
