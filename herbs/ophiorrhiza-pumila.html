Ophiorrhiza pumila

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophiorrhiza pumila has creeping rhizomes that produce small, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged in a basal rosette or along the stem. The foliage is typically bright green with a smooth texture and prominent venation. Its flowering structures consist of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are often pale in color and feature a delicate, elongated shape. The plant's overall growth habit is compact and low-growing, characteristic of many forest floor species.
