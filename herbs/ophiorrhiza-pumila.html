What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiorrhiza pumila?

Ophiorrhiza pumila belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Ophiorrhiza, specifically identifying it as the species pumila.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the bioactive alkaloids camptothecin, camptothecin-related alkaloids, loganin, secologanin, and strictosamide. These chemical constituents are primarily classified as indole alkaloids that contribute to the plant's diverse pharmacological profile. Loganin and secologanin serve as essential precursors in the biosynthetic pathways of more complex iridoid and indole alkaloids. The presence of camptothecin and its related derivatives is particularly significant due to their potent cytotoxic properties in cancer research.
