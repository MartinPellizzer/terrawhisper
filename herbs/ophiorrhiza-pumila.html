Ophiorrhiza pumila

What's the taxonomical classification of Ophiorrhiza pumila?

Ophiorrhiza pumila belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Ophiorrhiza, specifically identifying it as the species pumila.

These blossoms are often pale in color and feature a delicate, elongated shape. The plant's overall growth habit is compact and low-growing, characteristic of many forest floor species.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ophiorrhiza pumila?

This plant has potent anti-neoplastic, anti-tumor, anticancer, and chemotherapeutic activities that make it a significant subject in pharmacological research. Studies indicate that the bioactive compounds found within the plant can effectively inhibit the growth and proliferation of malignant cells. These diverse properties allow it to function as a natural agent in the development of new anticancer treatments. By targeting specific cellular mechanisms, the plant demonstrates a strong potential for use in various chemotherapeutic strategies. Researchers continue to investigate these multifaceted biological effects to better understand their clinical applications.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", the methanol extracts of Ophiorrhiza pumila were profiled and identified using reverse-phase high performance liquid chromatography coupled with on-line photodiode array detection and electrospray-ionization ion-trap mass spectrometry. A natural 10-glycosyloxy camptothecin, known as chaboside, was accumulated in the tissues of O. pumila. Anthraquinones were present in the extracts of the hairy roots and calli of O. pumila, though they were not found in the differentiated plants. Furthermore, the activity of strictosidine synthase, which is a key enzyme of camptothecin biosynthesis, was detected in the protein extracts of the stems and roots of O. pumila. This enzymatic activity was correlated with the pattern of strictosidine synthase mRNA expression.

According to a study published by "Journal of integrative plant biology", Ophiorrhiza pumila contains the anticancer pentacyclic quinoline alkaloid camptothecin. This herbaceous plant from the Rubiaceae family has a high-quality reference genome with an estimated size of ~456.90 Mb. The accumulation level of camptothecin in the roots of Ophiorrhiza pumila was found to be higher than in its stems and leaves. Research identified two loganic acid O-methyltransferase genes, OpLAMT1 and OpLAMT2, within this plant. Specifically, OpLAMT1 was shown to convert loganic acid into loganin, while the knockout of OpLAMT1 expression led to the elimination of both loganin and camptothecin accumulation in Ophiorrhiza pumila hairy roots. Four key residues in the OpLAMT1 protein were also identified as crucial for this catalytic activity.
