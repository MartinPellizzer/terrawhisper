Field Horsetail (Equisetum Arvense Var. Duffortianum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Equisetum arvense var. duffortianum.

Field horsetail, scientifically known as Equisetum arvense var. duffortianum, is a medicinal herb native to temperate regions, prized for its historical and contemporary use in wellness practices. This ancient plant is recognized for its high concentration of silica, which supports joint health, skin integrity, and hair strength, along with its adaptogenic properties that help the body manage stress. Traditionally, it has been used in European folk medicine to treat urinary tract infections, respiratory ailments, and as a diuretic, while its distinctive, slightly bitter flavor made it a popular ingredient in herbal teas and culinary preparations. In modern wellness, field horsetail is increasingly incorporated into supplements and natural remedies for its potential anti-inflammatory and detoxifying effects, making it a unique and versatile botanical with a rich cultural heritage.
