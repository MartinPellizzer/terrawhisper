Alpine Pasqueflower (Pulsatilla alpina)

Alpine Pasqueflower (Pulsatilla alpina) is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, native to Alps, Central Europe, and European Mountain Ranges. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its sedative, anti-inflammatory, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, tibetan medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.
