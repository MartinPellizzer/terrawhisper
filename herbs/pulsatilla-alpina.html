Medicinal Parts

Pulsatilla alpina root is one of the most valuable medicinal parts of this plant, known for its rich content of alkaloids, saponins, and other bioactive compounds.

Traditionally, the root has been used in herbal medicine to treat respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It is also believed to have antispasmodic effects, making it useful in alleviating symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders. The root's ability to stimulate the immune system has led to its use in supporting the body's defense mechanisms against infections.

In addition to its root, the flower of Pulsatilla alpina is also utilized in some traditional remedies, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, though its use is less common compared to the root.
