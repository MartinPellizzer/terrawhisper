Dichocarpum fargesii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dichocarpum fargesii has a prostrate growth habit characterized by thin, creeping stems that typically measure 0.5 to 1 millimeter in diameter. The leaves are small, ovate to elliptic in shape, and possess a single central costa that extends nearly to the apex. These leaves are arranged in an alternate or somewhat irregular pattern along the stem and often appear somewhat translucent. The plant features small, inconspicuous sporophytes that emerge from the stem to facilitate reproduction. Overall, the morphology of this species reflects an adaptation to moist, shaded microhabitats where its delicate structure can thrive.
