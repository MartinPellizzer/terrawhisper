Dichocarpum fargesii

What's the taxonomical classification of Dichocarpum fargesii?

Dichocarpum fargesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This species is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically situated within the genus Dichocarpum. Ultimately, its unique biological characteristics are defined by its specific designation as the species fargesii.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Dichocarpum fargesii targets inflammatory signaling proteins such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha). These interactions are primarily driven by bioactive compounds like terpenoids and flavonoids present in the plant tissues. By inhibiting the COX-2 enzyme, the plant effectively reduces the production of prostaglandins that trigger pain and swelling. Furthermore, its ability to modulate the NF-kappaB signaling pathway helps suppress the broader immune response responsible for chronic inflammation. These specific molecular interactions provide the biochemical basis for its traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and fever.
