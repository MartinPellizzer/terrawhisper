Boehmeria japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Boehmeria japonica?

Boehmeria japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Boehmeria, resulting in its scientific name, species japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Boehmeria japonica has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender, somewhat woody stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 80 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and feature an ovate to broadly elliptic shape with serrated margins and pointed tips. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, appearing primarily in dense, greenish panicles. The reproductive structures include tiny, inconspicuous fruits that develop from these clustered flowers. These morphological traits help distinguish the species within its native forest understory habitats.
