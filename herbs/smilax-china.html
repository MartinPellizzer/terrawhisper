Smilax china

What's the taxonomical classification of Smilax china?

Smilax china belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Smilacaceae, which contains the genus Smilax, and is specifically identified by the species name china.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax china has woody climbing stems equipped with axillary thorns and leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate with entire or slightly wavy margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in small clusters. Following pollination, it develops small, fleshy berries that transition from green to red or black as they mature. Its root system consists of thick, tuberous rhizomes that help the vine anchor itself and store nutrients. The overall structure allows the plant to scramble over other vegetation using its specialized climbing mechanisms.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to a wide geographical range spanning across East Asia, specifically including China, Japan, Korea, Taiwan, and parts of Southeast Asia. It thrives primarily in temperate and subtropical climates where moist, shaded environments are available. Within these regions, the species is often found growing in forest edges, thickets, and along riverbanks. Its distribution is closely linked to the presence of suitable soil conditions and moderate humidity levels found in these habitats. Because it is a climbing vine, it frequently occupies the understory layer of various woodland ecosystems.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Smilax china contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its tuberous rhizomes and roots. These underground structures are harvested and processed to extract saponins and other bioactive elements used in traditional remedies. The rhizomes are frequently employed to treat inflammatory conditions, skin ailments, and joint pain. Some practitioners also utilize the stems and leaves in decoctions to support blood purification. Because the concentration of active ingredients varies, the roots remain the most valued part of the plant for therapeutic applications.

What are the pharmacological activities of Smilax china?

This plant has documented pharmacological activities including anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and α-glucosidase inhibition properties. The bioactive compounds within Smilax china work to neutralize free radicals, which provides its significant antioxidant effect. Researchers have observed that its extracts can suppress tumor cell growth and reduce inflammation in various biological models. Additionally, the plant demonstrates the ability to inhibit certain microbes and regulate blood sugar levels through α-glucosidase inhibition. These diverse therapeutic potentials suggest that the plant could serve as a valuable source for developing new medicinal agents.

As discussed in a study, Smilax china L. is a traditional medicinal plant used as an anti-cancer drug that possesses potential lipid-lowering, anti-inflammatory, and anti-ovarian cancer effects. Research focused on scopolin obtained from Smilax china L. to explore its mechanism against hepatocellular carcinoma. Scopolin regulates the expression of three specific glycolysis-related proteins: glucose-6-phosphate isomerase (GPI), glycerol-3-phosphate dehydrogenase, mitochondrial (GPD2), and phosphoglycerate kinase 2 (PGK2). The study found that scopolin can affect the interaction between Heat shock protein HSP 90-alpha (Hsp90α) and GPD2. By regulating GPI, GPD2, and PGK2, scopolin inhibits protein-protein interactions and reduces the energy metabolism of tumor tissue to inhibit tumor growth.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds baicalin, dihydrokaempferol, kaempferol, rutin, and smilasides A-F. These specific flavonoids and steroidal saponins are recognized for their various biological activities within the human body. For example, the presence of kaempferol and rutin contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties. The smilasides A-F group represents a unique set of saponins often studied for their potential health benefits. Together, these chemical constituents define the therapeutic profile of the species.

As discussed in a study, the major medical components detected in Smilax china include baicalin and astibin. Researchers analyzed samples from 8 counties of Xiangxi Autonomous Prefecture to determine how climatic factors affect these components. The study investigated relationships involving the annual average temperature, Jan average temp, Jul average temp, and valid accumulative temp of ≥ 10 degrees C. It also examined the annual maximum temp, annual minimum temp, annual precipitation, annual sunshine amount, non-frost period, and relative humidity. Findings showed that annual minimum temp, annual average temp, and valid accumulative temp were significantly correlated to the content of the major medical components. Additionally, relative humidity, annual precipitation, and the non-frost period were found to be negatively related to these medicinal contents.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the treatment of inflammation, inflammatory disorders, prostate enlargement, prosthetic hyperplasia, and benign prostatic hyperplasia. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to reduce swelling and manage various systemic inflammatory conditions. Practitioners often utilize its components to alleviate the symptoms associated with an enlarged prostate. This includes managing the discomfort and urinary issues caused by prosthetic hyperplasia. By addressing these specific ailments, the plant serves as a traditional remedy for both general and localized swelling.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

What side effects this plant can have?
