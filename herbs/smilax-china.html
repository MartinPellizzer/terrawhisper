Smilax china

What's the taxonomical classification of Smilax china?

Smilax china belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Smilacaceae, which contains the genus Smilax, and is specifically identified by the species name china.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax china has woody climbing stems equipped with axillary thorns and leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate with entire or slightly wavy margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in small clusters. Following pollination, it develops small, fleshy berries that transition from green to red or black as they mature. Its root system consists of thick, tuberous rhizomes that help the vine anchor itself and store nutrients. The overall structure allows the plant to scramble over other vegetation using its specialized climbing mechanisms.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds baicalin, dihydrokaempferol, kaempferol, rutin, and smilasides A-F. These specific flavonoids and steroidal saponins contribute to the plant's diverse therapeutic profile. Research indicates that the presence of these bioactive molecules supports various antioxidant and anti-inflammatory processes within the body. The smilasides A-F group is particularly notable for its unique chemical structure among the plant's constituents. Together, these substances form a complex chemical matrix that drives the plant's biological activity.

As discussed in a study, the major medical components in Smilax china include baicalin and astibin. Researchers detected these components in Smilax china from 8 counties of Xiangxi Autonomous Prefecture. The study examined relationships between these components and factors such as annual average temperature, Jan average temp, Jul average temp, valid accumulative temp of ≥ 10 degrees C, annual maximum temp, annual minimum temp, annual precipitation, annual sunshine amount, non-frost period and relative humidity. Relative humidity, annual minimum temp and annual precipitation were identified as the dominant factors. Annual minimum temp, annual average temp and valid accumulative temp were significantly correlated to the content of these major medical components. Additionally, relative humidity, annual precipitation and non-frost period were negatively related to them.
