Pilea peltata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea peltata?

Pilea peltata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Rosales, the plant is a member of the family Urticaceae. It is further defined by its membership in the genus Pilea, specifically identified by its species name, peltata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea peltata has fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are attached to the stem by a long petiole originating from the center of the leaf blade. These leaves are typically dark green with a slightly textured surface and often feature prominent venation. The plant grows in a low-creeping or spreading habit with stems that can be somewhat succulent in nature. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, usually appearing along the leaf axils. The overall structure is compact, making it suitable for ground cover in moist environments.
