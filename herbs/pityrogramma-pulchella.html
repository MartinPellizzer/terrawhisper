What is Pityrogramma pulchella?

Pityrogramma pulchella is a species of fern in the family Pteridaceae, native to tropical Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized, terrestrial fern with a rhizome that is covered with scales. The fronds are bipinnate, with two distinct rows of pinnae, and have a dark brown to black rachis. The pinnae are lanceolate in shape and have a pale green to yellow-green color with a prominent midrib.

The fertile fronds are smaller than the sterile fronds and have sporangia that are borne on the underside of the pinnae.
