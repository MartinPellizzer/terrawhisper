Caryota mitis

What's the taxonomical classification of Caryota mitis?

Caryota mitis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Arecales. This plant is a member of the family Arecaceae and falls under the genus Caryota, ultimately being identified by the specific species name mitis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caryota mitis has a single, slender trunk that can reach heights of up to 20 meters and is characterized by prominent, ring-like leaf scars. The plant features large, bipinnate leaves that create a feathery appearance with long, drooping leaflets. Its unique inflorescence is pendulous and produces numerous small, fleshy fruits that transition from green to a bright orange or red color when ripe. The stem is typically solitary but can occasionally form small clumps under specific environmental conditions. This species is easily identified by its distinctively shaped leaflets and the cascading nature of its flowering clusters.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and painful rashes if the sap or broken plant parts come into direct contact with human skin. The presence of calcium oxalate crystals within the plant tissue acts as a primary irritant to the body. If these microscopic needles penetrate the skin, they trigger an inflammatory response characterized by itching and redness. Accidental ingestion of any part of the plant can lead to intense burning sensations in the mouth and throat. Such exposure may also result in significant swelling of the mucous membranes, making it difficult to swallow.
