What's the taxonomical classification of Ormosia indurata?

Ormosia indurata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Fabales. It is further categorized under the family Fabaceae, a group known for its diverse range of legumes. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Ormosia, specifically identified by its unique species name, indurata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ormosia indurata has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of opposite, elliptic to oblong leaflets that terminate in a single apical leaflet. The leaflets feature entire margins and acute to acuminate tips, often exhibiting a smooth or slightly coriaceous texture. The plant produces woody, indehiscent pods that contain distinctive, brightly colored seeds typically ranging from red to orange. These seeds are characteristically smooth and possess a hard, lustrous testa. The overall growth habit is that of a woody shrub or small tree with branching patterns that support the dense foliage.
