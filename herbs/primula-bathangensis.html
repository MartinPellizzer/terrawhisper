Primula bathangensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula bathangensis has a basal rosette of leaves that are typically oblong to lanceolate with crenate or serrated margins. The leaves are often covered in fine hairs and possess distinct venation patterns. From the center of this rosette, an elongated scape emerges to support the inflorescence. This scape bears a cluster of flowers, which are characterized by their specific corolla shape and color variations. The reproductive structures include a central pistil surrounded by several stamens.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically found within the Tibet Autonomous Region of China. It primarily grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes situated at elevations ranging from 3,500 to 4,500 meters. The distribution is closely tied to the moist, cool environments characteristic of the Bathang mountain range. Within these specific mountain ecosystems, the species occupies niche habitats that provide consistent moisture and protection from extreme winds.
