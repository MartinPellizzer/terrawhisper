Lindernia procumbens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia procumbens has a prostrate or ascending growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems that typically reach a height of 5 to 15 centimeters. The leaves are small, opposite, and ovate to lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Its flowers are minute and white with distinct purple spots on the lower petal, appearing in the axils of the leaves. The plant produces small, globose capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds upon maturity. This species is often found in damp or muddy environments where its creeping stems can spread across the substrate.
