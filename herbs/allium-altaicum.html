Allium altaicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Allium altaicum?

Allium altaicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is a prominent representative of the family Amaryllidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Allium, and is identified by its unique specific epithet, altaicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium altaicum has narrow, linear leaves that are typically grayish-green and reach lengths of up to 20 centimeters. The plant produces an umbel-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, star-shaped flowers that transition from a pale pink to a deep purple color. Each individual flower features six tepals and prominent stamens that extend slightly beyond the perianth. The scape, or flowering stem, is erect and can grow quite tall to support the heavy cluster of blossoms. Underlying the aerial structures is a bulb characterized by a fibrous tunic that protects the growing tissue.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, specifically spanning across Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Mongolia, and parts of western China. It typically thrives in high-altitude environments such as rocky slopes, meadows, and steppes. These habitats are often characterized by cold climates and well-drained soils found in the Altai and Tian Shan mountain systems. Its distribution is largely limited to these specific continental interior ranges. Because it favors these unique topographical features, the species is geographically restricted to these specific Eurasian territories.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.
