What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anisochilus carnosus has succulent, cylindrical, or slightly compressed stems that are typically bright green and can reach several centimeters in length. The leaves are minimal or transient, often appearing as small, scale-like structures that fall off early in the plant's development. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the stem surfaces, usually appearing in clusters or solitary arrangements.
