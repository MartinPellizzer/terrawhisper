What is Ficus roxburghii?

Ficus roxburghii is an evergreen tree species belonging to the family Moraceae, native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It grows up to 15 meters in height, with a broad, spreading crown and a straight, columnar trunk. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical to ovate in shape, measuring 10-30 cm in length, with a leathery texture and an entire margin. The inflorescence is a syconium, a type of false fruit that contains numerous tiny flowers, and is green or yellowish in color.

Ficus roxburghii is a member of the Ficus genus, which comprises over 800 species of trees and shrubs, many of which are used as ornamentals or have cultural and economic significance.
