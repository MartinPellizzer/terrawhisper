Polygala polifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygala polifolia?

Polygala polifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Fabales. It is a representative of the family Polygalaceae, falling within the genus Polygala, and is specifically identified by its species name, polifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygala polifolia has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are small and zygomorphic, featuring a distinct keel and colorful wings that are characteristic of the Polygalaceae family. The inflorescence is usually terminal or axillary, presenting delicate blossoms that transition from green to subtle hues. The root system is generally fibrous, helping the plant anchor itself in its preferred sandy or rocky habitats.
