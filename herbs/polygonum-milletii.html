Polygonum milletii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum milletii has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 70 centimeters and features smooth, slightly angled surfaces. The leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, displaying a delicate pink to white coloration. The plant is characterized by its slender, fibrous root system which serves to anchor it within its native habitats. Its reproductive structures eventually develop into small, dry achenes that facilitate seed dispersal.
