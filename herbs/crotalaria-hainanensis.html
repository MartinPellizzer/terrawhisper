Crotalaria hainanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria hainanensis?

Crotalaria hainanenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Fabales, it falls under the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive legume structure. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Crotalaria, and its unique species designation, hainanenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria hainanensis has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by branched stems and pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of ovate to elliptical leaflets. The leaves possess entire margins and acute apices, often displaying a slightly pubescent texture on their surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in a raceme structure. The plant produces distinctive legume pods that are typically compressed and contain several small seeds. These structural features are key identifiers for distinguishing this species within the Fabaceae family.
