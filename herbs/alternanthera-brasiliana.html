What is Alternanthera brasiliana?

Alternanthera brasiliana is an herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a perennial species native to the tropical regions of South America, typically growing to a height of 30-60 cm. The plant features elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are oppositely arranged, with a length of 2-6 cm and a width of 1-2 cm. The leaves are dark green in color, glabrous, and have a cordate or cuneate base.

The plant produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers in a terminal inflorescence.

The main herbal preparations of Alternanthera brasiliana are tea, infusion, tincture, salve, and decoction.
