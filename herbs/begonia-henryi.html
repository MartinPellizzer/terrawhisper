Begonia henryi

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia henryi?

Begonia henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cucurbitales. This plant is a representative of the family Begoniaceae, specifically placed within the genus Begonia, and is identified by the unique species name henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia henryi has fleshy rhizomes that support large, asymmetrical, ovate leaves featuring bright red undersides and serrated margins.

The foliage is characterized by a glossy texture and prominent venation that contributes to its striking visual appearance. From these leaves emerge pendulous clusters of delicate, four-petaled flowers that transition from white to soft pink hues. These blooms are typically supported by slender, reddish stems that contrast with the deep green of the upper leaf surfaces.

The overall structure of the plant creates a lush, spreading habit suitable for shaded environments.

      What are the herbal preparations of Begonia henryi?

      Begonia henryi is prepared as a decoction or an infusion made from the fresh or dried leaves and stems. This plant is often boiled in water to extract its bioactive compounds for medicinal use. Some practitioners may also utilize the plant in the form of a topical paste applied directly to the skin. These preparations are typically used to address localized inflammation or skin irritations.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Begonia henryi can cause skin irritation, mouth swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched. The plant contains calcium oxalate crystals that act as tiny needles when they come into contact with sensitive tissues. This irritation often manifests as redness, itching, or a burning sensation on the skin or lips. If a person swallows the plant, they may experience intense throat pain, nausea, or vomiting. It is important to handle this species with care and keep it out of reach of pets and children.
