Bidens parviflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Bidens parviflora?

Bidens parviflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a diverse group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Bidens. Within this genus, it is identified by its unique species name, parviflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bidens parviflora has a herbaceous growth habit characterized by erect stems, pinnately lobed leaves, and small, yellowish-white flower heads. The leaves are typically deeply divided into narrow segments and feature a slightly hairy texture on their surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of small heads where the ray florets are inconspicuous and the disc florets are the primary reproductive structures. The plant often develops a branched structure that supports multiple flower clusters throughout its blooming period. These morphological traits distinguish it from other members of the Asteraceae family.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains (6-O-(E)-p-coumaroyl)-beta-D-fructofuranosyl-(2-->1)-(6-O-(E)-p-coumaroyl)-alpha-D-glucopyranoside II and (6-O-(E)-p-coumaroyl)-beta-D-fructofuranosyl-(2-->1)-alpha-D-glucopyranoside as its medicinal compounds. These specific molecules are characterized by their complex glycoside structures and the presence of p-coumaroyl groups. Such compounds often contribute to the biological activity and antioxidant properties of the plant. Researchers study these chemical constituents to understand their potential therapeutic applications in medicine. The structural complexity of these molecules makes them significant subjects for phytochemical analysis.

As discussed in a study, an investigation on Bidens parviflora led to the isolation of three sucrose esters and a substituted truxillate. The identified structures include (6-O-(E)-p-coumaroyl)-beta-D-fructofuranosyl-(2-->1)-alpha-D-glucopyranoside and (6-O-(E)-p-coumaroyl)-beta-D-fructofuranosyl-(2-->1)-(6-O-(E)-p-coumaroyl)-alpha-D-glucopyranoside II. Other compounds found were the 6,6'-sucrose ester of (1alpha,2alpha,3beta,4beta)-3,4-bis(4-hydroxyphenyl)-1,2-cyclobutanedicarboxylic acid and the dimethyl ester of (1alpha,2alpha,3alpha,4alpha)-2,4-bis(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)-1,3-cyclobutanedicarboxylic acid. These medicinal compounds were subjected to bioassays involving the histamine release inhibition of rat mast cells induced by antigen-antibody reaction. Additionally, the study examined the inhibitory activity of PGE(2) production by macrophages.
