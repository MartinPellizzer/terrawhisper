Anemone howellii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone howellii has a basal rosette of deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are characterized by their palmate shape and serrated margins. The plant typically reaches a height of approximately 10 to 20 centimeters above the ground. Its flowers are solitary and emerge on slender, hairy stalks from the center of the leaf cluster. Each blossom features several delicate, white to pale pink petals surrounding a cluster of bright yellow stamens. The overall structure is compact, adapted for growing in the shaded, moist environments of forest floors.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Sierra Nevada mountains in California, specifically occurring within the central and southern portions of the range. It thrives in rocky, subalpine environments and often grows in the shaded crevices of granite outcrops. These populations are typically found at elevations ranging from several thousand to over ten thousand feet above sea level.
