Cynanchum mooreanum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum mooreanum has slender, climbing stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that possess entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically arranged in pairs along the twining vines, providing a dense green texture to the plant's structure. Small, tubular flowers emerge in clusters, often displaying delicate colors characteristic of the Apocynaceae family. These blossoms are supported by thin pedicels that allow them to dangle from the climbing stems. The overall morphology is adapted for a sprawling or climbing growth habit in its natural habitat.
