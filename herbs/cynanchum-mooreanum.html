What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum mooreanum?

Cynanchum mooreanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Gentianales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Cynanchum, specifically as the species mooreanum.

      What are the herbal preparations of Cynanchum mooreanum?

      Cynanchum mooreanum is prepared as a decoction of its roots, a powder made from dried plant parts, or a topical paste for medicinal use. These various forms allow the plant's active compounds to be administered either orally or externally depending on the specific ailment. When used as a decoction, the roots are boiled in water to extract soluble constituents for internal consumption. The powdered form is often mixed with honey or water to facilitate swallowing and controlled dosing. Additionally, the plant material can be crushed into a fine paste to treat skin conditions through direct application.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cynanchum mooreanum can cause severe skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential systemic toxicity if ingested due to the presence of unidentified alkaloids. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to localized dermatitis characterized by redness, itching, or blistering. If consumed, individuals might experience gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. There is also a risk of more serious neurological effects depending on the concentration of toxins present in the specific specimen. Because the chemical composition of this species is not fully documented, medical professionals advise extreme caution when handling or approaching it.
