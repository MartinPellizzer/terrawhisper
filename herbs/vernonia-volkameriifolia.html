What's the taxonomical classification of Vernonia volkameriifolia?

Vernonia volkameriifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. It is further categorized under the family Asteraceae, a group known for its diverse flowering structures. Within this family, the plant is placed in the genus Vernonia, and its specific taxonomic designation is identified as the species volkameriifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vernonia volkameriifolia has lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along a woody, erect stem. The leaf margins are typically serrated or finely toothed, while the surfaces can exhibit varying degrees of pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular disc florets clustered into dense heads. These flower heads are often accompanied by involucral bracts that provide structural support to the bloom. The plant generally maintains a shrubby growth habit with a branching architecture.
