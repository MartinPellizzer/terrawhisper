Dill (Anethum graveolens) Uses

Dill is commonly used to treat respiratory issues like coughs and infections. It also helps with digestive problems such as gas, flatulence, indigestion, and stomach cramps. It may alleviate symptoms of gastroenteritis and nausea by soothing the gastrointestinal tract.

Dill (Anethum graveolens) Monograph

1. Cough

Dill is used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant properties, which help loosen mucus in the respiratory tract. It may also reduce inflammation in the throat and ease irritation. Traditionally, dill tea or essential oil has been applied to relieve persistent or dry coughs. Its mild nature makes it suitable for use in children and adults alike.

2. Respiratory infections

Dill is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and easing congestion. Its essential oils have antimicrobial properties that may help combat infections. It is often brewed as a tea to soothe coughs and ease breathing. Dill's expectorant qualities aid in clearing mucus from the airways.

3. Digestive gas

Dill aids in relieving digestive gas by stimulating the release of digestive enzymes and relaxing gastrointestinal muscles. It is commonly used to reduce bloating and discomfort associated with indigestion. Its carminative properties help expel trapped gases from the stomach and intestines. Dill can be consumed as a tea, spice, or tincture for digestive support.

4. Flatulence

Dill is traditionally used to relieve flatulence due to its carminative properties. It helps expel gas from the digestive tract, reducing bloating and discomfort. The essential oils in dill, such as limonene and carvone, contribute to its effectiveness in easing gas-related symptoms. It is often consumed as a tea or added to meals to support digestion.

5. Indigestion

Dill aids in relieving indigestion by stimulating digestive enzymes and reducing bloating. It soothes the stomach lining and promotes the movement of food through the digestive tract. Often used in teas or as a spice, dill can ease discomfort and improve digestion in individuals with mild gastrointestinal issues.

6. Gastroenteritis

Dill is used in herbal medicine to support digestion and alleviate symptoms of gastroenteritis. Its carminative properties help reduce gas and bloating, while its anti-inflammatory effects may ease intestinal irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to soothe digestive discomfort associated with inflammation or infection.

7. Stomach cramps

Dill is used to relieve stomach cramps due to its carminative and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce gas and relaxes the muscles of the gastrointestinal tract. Often consumed as a tea or added to meals, it provides gentle relief from bloating and discomfort. Its mild nature makes it suitable for regular use in mild digestive issues.

8. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Dill has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease due to its carminative and antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce stomach acidity and soothe digestive discomfort. Dill seed tea or oil may provide relief by promoting digestion and relaxing gastrointestinal muscles.

9. Nausea

Dill is used to alleviate nausea due to its carminative and antispasmodic properties. It helps soothe digestive discomfort and reduce vomiting. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or tincture. It is particularly effective for motion sickness and morning sickness.

10. Gastrointestinal gas

Dill aids in reducing gastrointestinal gas by promoting digestion and relaxing smooth muscle in the digestive tract. Its carminative properties help expel excess gas, relieving bloating and discomfort. It is often used in herbal remedies for digestive support and gas relief.
