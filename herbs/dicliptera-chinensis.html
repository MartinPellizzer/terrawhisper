What's the taxonomical classification of Dicliptera chinensis?

Dicliptera chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is a representative of the family Acanthaceae, specifically situated within the genus Dicliptera, and is identified by the unique species name chinensis.

Dicliptera chinensis has an erect herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins and a woody base. The inflorescence often features a distinct bilabiate corolla that transitions from white to light purple or blue hues. After pollination, the plant develops small, rounded capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

What are the pharmacological activities of Dicliptera chinensis?

This plant has anti-fibrotic pharmacological activity that helps prevent the excessive accumulation of connective tissue in organs. Researchers have investigated its potential to inhibit the processes that lead to tissue scarring and organ dysfunction. By targeting specific cellular pathways, the extracts from Dicliptera chinensis may reduce the buildup of extracellular matrix components. This biological action suggests that the plant could play a role in managing various fibrotic diseases. Further studies are necessary to fully understand its therapeutic mechanisms in human health.

According to a study published by "Cellular and molecular biology (Noisy-le-Grand, France)", Dicliptera chinensis polysaccharide (DCP) alleviates dimethylnitrosamine-induced liver fibrosis in rats through a dose-dependent mechanism. In vivo testing involved administering DCP at concentrations of 0, 100, and 300 mg/kg via intraperitoneal injection. The treatment significantly down-regulated TGF-β1 expression, pSmad2/3, and α-SMA expression in liver tissue. In vitro, DCP inhibited the proliferation and activation of HSC-T6 cells stimulated by 1 ng/mL of recombinant rat TGF-β1. This effect was observed using DCP concentrations of 0, 25, 50, 100, 200, and 400 µg/ml. Ultimately, DCP attenuates the progression of liver fibrosis by suppressing the TGF-β/Smad pathway.
