Doryopteris concolor

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Doryopteris concolor has pinnate fronds characterized by a distinct lack of color differentiation between the upper and lower surfaces of the leaves. The blades are typically composed of multiple pinnae that are arranged symmetrically along a central rachis. Each individual leaflet possesses fine, serrated margins and a delicate, membranous texture. The stipe is generally slender and can vary in color from light green to a brownish hue depending on the specimen. These morphological features collectively define the plant's visual identity within its specific ecological niche.
