Saussurea obvallata

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea obvallata?

Saussurea obvallata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which places it among the diverse group of composite flowers. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Saussurea, and its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, making its full scientific name Saussurea obvallata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea obvallata has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by a rosette of leaves and large, translucent, bladder-like bracts that surround the inflorescence. These distinctive bracts are membranous and enclose the flower head to protect it from extreme high-altitude cold and intense ultraviolet radiation. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 40 centimeters, featuring a stem that rises from a basal rosette of lanceolate or oblong leaves. The inflorescence consists of a dense, yellowish-white capitulum that is eventually revealed once the protective bracts expand or age. Its root system is robust and rhizomatous, allowing it to anchor firmly in the rocky, alpine soils of the Himalayas.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of the Himalayas, specifically found within the mountain ranges of India, Nepal, and Tibet. It typically grows in rocky crevices and alpine meadows at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters. These populations are scattered across diverse terrains such as the Ladakh region, Uttarakhand, and parts of Bhutan. The species thrives in environments characterized by extreme cold and intense solar radiation.
