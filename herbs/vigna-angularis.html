Possible Side Effects of vigna angularis

Vigna angularis can have side effects if used improperly, such as digestive issues, including diarrhea, nausea, and stomach pain, due to its potential to interact with medications or exacerbate pre-existing conditions like irritable bowel syndrome.

High doses of the herb may also cause allergic reactions, such as hives, itching, and swelling, particularly in individuals with sensitive skin or allergies.

Furthermore, improper use of Vigna angularis may lead to an imbalance of blood sugar levels, potentially causing hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia in people with diabetes.

In rare cases, prolonged or excessive consumption of the herb may contribute to kidney damage or liver toxicity, especially when combined with other medications or substances.
