Rhododendron campylocarpum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron campylocarpum has coriaceous, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and characterized by revolute margins. The plant exhibits a woody structure with stems that support these dense, leathery foliage clusters. Its flowers are notable for their distinct shape and are often held in terminal racemes. The species is specifically identified by its curved, fleshy fruit which gives the plant its specific epithet. These morphological traits collectively distinguish it from other members of the genus within its native habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Rhododendron campylocarpum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential cardiac issues if ingested due to the presence of toxic grayanotoxins. These toxins act by binding to sodium channels in cell membranes, which disrupts normal nerve and muscle function. Ingesting even small amounts may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and dizziness. More serious reactions include low blood pressure, an irregular heartbeat, and muscle weakness. Because of these dangerous physiological effects, this plant should be handled with extreme caution and never consumed.
