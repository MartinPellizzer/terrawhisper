Rhododendron campylocarpum

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron campylocarpum?

Rhododendron campylocarpum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Ericales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Ericaceae, which places it within the genus Rhododendron, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species campylocarpum.

      What are the herbal preparations of Rhododendron campylocarpum?

      Rhododendron campylocarpum is prepared as a decoction, a tea, or a medicinal infusion using its leaves and stems. Local practitioners often boil the plant material in water to extract its active compounds for internal consumption. Some traditional methods involve macerating the plant parts in alcohol to create a potent tincture. These preparations are frequently used to address specific ailments within its native habitat. The choice of preparation method depends on the desired strength and the specific medicinal purpose of the treatment.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Rhododendron campylocarpum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential cardiac issues if ingested due to the presence of toxic grayanotoxins. These toxins act by binding to sodium channels in cell membranes, which disrupts normal nerve and muscle function. Ingesting even small amounts may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and dizziness. More serious reactions include low blood pressure, an irregular heartbeat, and muscle weakness. Because of these dangerous physiological effects, this plant should be handled with extreme caution and never consumed.
