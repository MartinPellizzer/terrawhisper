Eragrostis perennans

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eragrostis perennans has an annual life cycle characterized by slender, tufted stems that typically grow between 10 and 40 centimeters in height. The leaves are linear and narrow, often featuring a prominent midrib and a somewhat scabrous texture along the margins. Its inflorescence consists of several dense, spike-like panicles that are arranged in a way that makes them appear somewhat compact. Each spikelet contains a single floret protected by lemma and palea structures that are often slightly hairy. The overall structure allows the plant to thrive in moist, disturbed soils where its fine-textured foliage can spread quickly.
