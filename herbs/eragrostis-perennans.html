Eragrostis perennans

What's the taxonomical classification of Eragrostis perennans?

Eragrostis perennans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Poales and the family Poaceae, which places it among the true grasses. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Eragrostis, and its unique species designation, perennans.

      What are the herbal preparations of Eragrostis perennans?

      Eragrostis perennans is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion by boiling or steeping the plant material in hot water. These aqueous extracts are the most common methods used to draw out the soluble components of the grass. Some practitioners may also use the dried plant to create topical poultices for localized applications. In certain traditional uses, the plant material is incorporated into tinctures using alcohol as a solvent to increase shelf life and potency.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Eragrostis perennans can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals through direct physical contact. The plant possesses sharp, stiff hairs along its leaves and stems that can prick the skin and cause mechanical discomfort. These tiny structures often lead to localized redness, itching, or small bumps if a person brushes against the foliage. Some people may experience more persistent inflammatory responses depending on their specific level of sensitivity to the plant's surface texture. While not typically considered highly toxic, the physical irritation remains the primary concern for those handling the species.
