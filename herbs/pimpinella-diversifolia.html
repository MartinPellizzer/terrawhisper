Pimpinella diversifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pimpineilla diversifolia has highly variable leaf morphology characterized by deeply pinnatisect or bipinnatisect blades that range from narrow to broadly lanceolate in shape. The foliage exhibits a distinct heterophylly where basal leaves are often larger and more complex than the upper leaves. Each leaflet is typically serrated along the margins and possesses a fine, delicate texture. The plant also features small, white, umbel-shaped flower clusters supported by slender green peduncles. The stems are erect and can become somewhat woody at the base as the plant matures.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Mexico, where it primarily inhabits high-altitude environments. Its distribution is largely concentrated within the Sierra Madre Oriental and Sierra Madre del Sur mountain ranges. Within these areas, it typically grows in temperate forest habitats or rocky outcrops at significant elevations. The species occupies specific ecological niches that provide the cool, moist conditions necessary for its survival. Localized populations may be found across various states in the Mexican highlands depending on soil composition and microclimates.
