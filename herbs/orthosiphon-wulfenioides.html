What's the taxonomical classification of Orthosiphon wulfenioides?

Orthosiphon wulfenioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Lamiaceae, which contains the genus Orthosiphon, and is specifically identified by its species name, wulfenioides.

What are the pharmacological activities of Orthosiphon wulfenioides?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, NLRP3 inflammasome inhibitory, mitochondria damage inhibitory, and anti-Zika virus activities. Its pharmacological profile is characterized by a significant ability to suppress inflammatory responses through the inhibition of the NLRP3 inflammasome pathway. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates protective effects by preventing damage to mitochondria during cellular stress. These bioactive properties extend to antiviral potential, specifically exhibiting inhibitory effects against the Zika virus. Such multifaceted mechanisms suggest that Orthosiphon wulfenioides serves as a potent source of therapeutic compounds.

As discussed in a study, the whole plant of Orthosiphon wulfenioides contains abietane diterpenoids known as Wulfenioidones A-K (1-11). Bioactivity screening showed that compounds 1-4, 6 and 8 exhibited lactate dehydrogenase inhibition with IC50 values ranging from 0.23 to 3.43 μM. These compounds prevented mononuclear macrophage cell pyroptosis induced by double signal stimulation of LPS and nigericin. Specifically, compound 1 was shown to selectively inhibit the NLRP3 inflammasome through the down-regulation of Caspase-1 and IL-1β. Cell morphological observation further supported that compound 1 prevented macrophage cell pyroptosis.
