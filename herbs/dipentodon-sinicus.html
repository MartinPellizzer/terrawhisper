What's the taxonomical classification of Dipentodon sinicus?

Dipentodon sinicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Huerteales. This plant is the type specimen for its family, Dipentodontaceae, and is categorized under the genus Dipentodon, with the specific epithet sinicus identifying its unique species designation.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Dipentodon sinicus can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense itching upon direct contact with the flesh. These dermatological reactions often manifest as bright red rashes or small blisters that may persist for several days. If the sap or crushed leaves enter an open wound, it can trigger heightened inflammation and painful stinging sensations. Some individuals may also experience systemic symptoms such as mild nausea or headaches if the plant material is accidentally ingested. It is essential to wash the affected area immediately with soap and water to minimize these adverse effects.
