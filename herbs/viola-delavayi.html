Viola delavayi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola delavayi has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are cordate or reniform in shape with crenate margins and a creeping rhizomatous rootstock. The foliage typically features long petioles that support thin, green leaf blades. Its inflorescence consists of solitary flowers emerging from the leaf axils, displaying a delicate violet or purplish coloration. Each flower possesses five petals, with the lower petals often marked by distinct nectar guides or darker veining. The reproductive structures are housed within a small, capsule-like ovary that eventually splits to release numerous tiny seeds.
