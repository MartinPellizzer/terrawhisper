    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Viola delavayi can cause skin irritation, digestive upset, and allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically. While specific clinical studies on this exact species are limited, most plants in the Viola genus contain saponins that may trigger nausea or vomiting upon consumption. Some individuals might experience contact dermatitis or redness if the plant touches sensitive skin. It is also possible for people with known allergies to Asteraceae or similar families to react to its pollen. Always consult a medical professional before using any wild plant for medicinal purposes.
