What to know about Curcuma wenyujin (turmeric) before using it medicinally

Turmeric, scientifically known as Curcuma wenyujin, is a highly revered herb that has been touted for its numerous health benefits.

Its powerful anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties have been shown to improve overall health by reducing the risk of chronic diseases such as arthritis, heart disease, and even certain types of cancer. In culinary contexts, turmeric is commonly used as a spice to add flavor and color to various dishes, particularly in Indian and Middle Eastern cuisine, where it is often paired with coriander and cumin. Horticulturally, turmeric requires specific growing conditions, including warm temperatures and high humidity, making it a challenging crop to cultivate.

Botanically, turmeric belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, with historical references dating back to ancient India, where it was considered a sacred plant and used in rituals and ceremonies.
