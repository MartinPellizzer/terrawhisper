Artocarpus heterophyllus

What's the taxonomical classification of Artocarpus heterophyllus?

Artocarpus heterophyllus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Rosales and the family Moraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Artocarpus, and its specific species name, heterophyllus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artocarpus heterophyllus has large, alternate, leathery leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape with entire margins and a glossy dark green upper surface. The tree produces massive, singular fruits known as jackfruits that grow directly from the trunk or thick branches in a cauliflorous manner. These fruits possess a thick, bumpy green rind that protects a fleshy interior filled with numerous yellow bulbs. Each bulb is connected to the core by a fibrous structure and contains a single large, smooth seed. The overall growth habit of the plant is characterized by a dense, spreading canopy with rugged, greyish-brown bark.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning from India and Sri Lanka through the Malay Peninsula to Indonesia. It thrives in humid, lowland tropical environments characterized by high rainfall and warm temperatures. Due to human cultivation, its range has expanded significantly into other tropical zones across Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of Latin America. In these new habitats, it is often found in home gardens and small-scale orchards rather than wild forests. The species continues to spread through various tropical territories as a major fruit-bearing crop.

What are the pharmacological activities of Artocarpus heterophyllus?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, anticancer, antimicrobial, and hypoglycaemic activities derived from its diverse bioactive compounds. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, while its anti-inflammatory effects reduce swelling and pain in biological systems. The presence of phytochemicals contributes to its anticancer potential by inhibiting the growth of malignant cells. Additionally, the plant exhibits antimicrobial action against various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. Finally, its hypoglycaemic activity assists in regulating blood glucose levels, making it useful for managing metabolic health.

As discussed in a study, the stems and leaves of Artocarpus heterophyllus contain two new prenylated chromones, artoheterophines A (1) and B (2), five known prenylated chromones (3-7), and five known biogegetically related prenylated flavonoids (8-12). These compounds 1-12 demonstrate notable antiproliferative effects against various human cancer cell lines with IC50 values ranging from 0.36 ± 0.02 to 22.09 ± 0.16 μM. Additionally, the isolated compounds 1-12 exhibit significant anti-inflammatory activities by inhibiting nitric oxide production. This inhibitory activity on nitric oxide production was measured with IC50 values in the range of 0.48 ± 0.05-19.87 ± 0.21 µM. These results suggest that the 7 prenylated chromones and 5 flavonoids from Artocarpus heterophyllus could be significant for the development of new drugs.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites, specifically flavonoids, saponins, tannins, and steroids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. The presence of flavonoids provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the human body. Saponins within the plant matter are known for their ability to interact with cell membranes and offer antimicrobial effects. Tannins function as astringents that can assist in wound healing and digestive health. Finally, the steroids present in the chemical profile help regulate various physiological processes and hormonal functions.

As discussed in a study, phytochemical screening of Artocarpus heterophyllus seed crude extracts revealed the presence of tannins, flavonoids, reducing sugars, cardiac glycosides, saponins, and steroids. The ethanolic and hexaolic extracts showed antibacterial activity against multidrug-resistant Pseudomonas aeruginosa and both methicillin-resistant and methicillin-susceptible Staphylococcus aureus. These extracts produced a mean and standard error zone of inhibition ranging from 8.5 ± 0.5 to 16.5 ± 0.25 mm. Regarding concentration levels, the ethanolic crude extract had a minimum inhibitory concentration of 31.25 mg/ml and a minimum bactericidal concentration of 125 mg/ml. In comparison, the hexane extract exhibited a minimum inhibitory concentration of 62.50 mg/ml and a minimum bactericidal concentration of 250 mg/ml.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic reactions, nausea, and digestive upset in sensitive individuals. Some people may experience skin irritation or respiratory issues if they come into contact with its latex or pollen. Consuming too much of the fruit can lead to stomach discomfort and bloating. Nausea often occurs if the fruit is eaten in excessive quantities or if an individual has a specific intolerance. Monitoring your body's response to the plant is essential to avoid these adverse effects.
