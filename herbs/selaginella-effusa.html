Selaginella effusa

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella effusa?

Selaginella effusa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. The plant further falls into the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Selaginella, specifically as the species effusa.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Selaginella effusa targets various proteins involved in inflammation and oxidative stress, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 and various pro-inflammatory cytokines. Its bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and terpenoids, interact with the NF-kappaB signaling pathway to downregulate the expression of inflammatory mediators. These molecules also show an affinity for antioxidant enzymes like superoxide dismutase and catalase, helping to mitigate cellular damage. Additionally, certain phytochemicals in the plant can modulate the activity of kinases that regulate cell proliferation and apoptosis. These diverse protein-ligand interactions collectively contribute to its potential therapeutic applications in treating inflammatory diseases.
