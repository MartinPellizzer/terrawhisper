What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selagineella effusa has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in four distinct rows along thin, branching stems to form flat, spreading fronds. These diminutive leaves are typically dark green and possess a characteristic overlapping structure that gives the plant a textured appearance. The stems exhibit a delicate, creeping growth habit that allows the plant to colonize moist substrates effectively. Microscopic examination reveals that the leaves contain small sporangia located in specialized clusters called strobili. This morphological complexity allows the species to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
