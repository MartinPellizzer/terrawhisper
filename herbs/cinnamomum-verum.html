Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum)

Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum) is a member of the Lauraceae family, native to Sri Lanka, India, and Southern Asia. Traditionally, its bark, leaves, and resin have been used for infusions, decoctions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 5 to 15 meters.
Leaves
Lateral, narrow, elongate leaves with a dark brown upper surface and a lighter green lower surface, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Bilaterally symmetrical flowers with two opposing lobes, yellowish-green to pale brown coloration, solitary arrangement, and two longitudinal wings extending from the base of the flower.
Stems
Erect, woody, aromatic, with a smooth, glabrous surface, and a branched structure exhibiting opposite leaf scars.
