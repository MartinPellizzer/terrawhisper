Cinnamomum verum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cinnamomum verum?

Cinnamomum verum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, the plant is part of the order Laurales and the family Lauraceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Cinnamomum, and its unique species name, verum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cinnamomum verum contains high concentrations of cinnamaldehyde, eugenol, and various polyphenolic compounds like proanthocyanidins. The primary component, cinnamaldehyde, provides the characteristic aroma and serves as a potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory agent. Eugenol acts as a natural analgesic and antiseptic, contributing to the plant's traditional use in dental care. Additionally, the presence of antioxidant flavonoids helps neutralize free radicals within the body. These combined chemical properties support the plant's widespread application in both culinary and therapeutic practices.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cinnamomum verum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal issues like indigestion and bloating, respiratory ailments such as asthma and coughs, and inflammatory conditions including arthritis. Its bioactive compounds possess antimicrobial and antifungal properties that help combat various infections. Some traditional practices also utilize its warming effects to manage menstrual cramps and regulate blood sugar levels. Additionally, the essential oils found in its bark are often applied to soothe skin irritations and minor wounds.
