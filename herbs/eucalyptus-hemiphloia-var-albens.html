Eucalyptus hemiphloia var. albens

Eucalyptus hemiphloia var. albens, also known as White Ironbark, is a subspecies of the Eucalyptus hemiphloia plant, native to eastern Australia.

This plant has been used for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities, making it beneficial for treating wounds, colds, and respiratory issues.

The health benefits of White Ironbark include its ability to aid in the relief of symptoms associated with bronchitis, coughs, and sore throats, as well as its potential to reduce fever and alleviate pain.

Herbal preparations of White Ironbark can be made into teas, essential oils, or salves, and are often used in traditional medicine to promote overall wellness and recovery.
