Hymenocardia ulmoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Hymenocardia ulmoides?

Hymenocardia ulmoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Malpighiales. It is further categorized within the family Phyllanthaceae, falling under the genus Hymenocardia, with its specific designation being the species ulmoides.

The plant often exhibits a spreading growth habit with branching patterns that support its dense leaf arrangement.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Hymenocardia ulmoides can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized swelling if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with human tissue. The chemical compounds within its milky latex are known to act as irritants to the mucous membranes and sensitive skin surfaces. Individuals with preexisting sensitivities may experience heightened inflammatory responses or redness upon exposure. While extensive clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, traditional knowledge suggests caution regarding its topical application. Careful avoidance of the sap is recommended to prevent unpredictable dermatological reactions.
