Cayratias corniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Vitales. This plant is a member of the family Vitaceae, specifically situated within the genus Cayratiat and identified by the species name corniculata.

Cayratiia corniculata has a perennial climbing habit characterized by twining stems, cordate to ovate leaves with entire margins, and small, pale yellow, five-petaled flowers arranged in axillary cymes. Its stems are slender and flexible, allowing the plant to scramble over surrounding vegetation for sunlight. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous blooms that emerge from the leaf axils.
