Botanical Identification of Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense)

To identify Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Bupleurum chinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Apiales, family Apiaceae, genus Bupleurum, and species Bupleurum chinense. This classification places it within the carrot family, commonly known for its medicinal and aromatic plants.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chinese Bupleurum is Bupleurum chinense, a classification established to standardize its identification in botanical studies. This name reflects its origin and is widely recognized in herbal medicine literature. It is part of the genus Bupleurum, which includes several species used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Bupleurum chinense is a herbaceous plant with a taproot system. It has upright, branched stems that are hairy. The leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have serrated edges. The inflorescence is a raceme of small, yellowish flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, flat seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Bupleurum chinense can be distinguished by its cylindrical, slightly grooved stems and dark green, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, while the roots are yellowish and fibrous. It differs from similar species like Bupleurum scorzonerifolium by its leaf structure and stem characteristics.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Bupleurum chinense is native to China, particularly in regions like Sichuan, Hubei, and Guizhou. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Bupleurum chinense blooms from April to June, with seed formation occurring between July and September. Harvesting for identification purposes is typically conducted in late summer to early autumn when the plant has matured and the seeds are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Bupleurum chinense is closely related to species within the same genus, Bupleurum. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with other members of the Apiaceae family, such as compound leaves and umbellate flowers. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other aromatic herbs in the family.
