Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense) Compounds

The primary compounds in Chinese Bupleurum include flavonoids, saponins, glycosides, and terpenoids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Coumarins and phenolic acids also play roles in its therapeutic applications. Alkaloids, tannins, and essential oils further support its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds are commonly found in the roots and are used in traditional herbal medicine for their therapeutic effects. Flavonoids help regulate immune responses and may support liver function.

2. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense). It contributes to the herb's traditional use in treating inflammatory and digestive conditions. Saponins have demonstrated antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties in research studies. They are commonly extracted and used in herbal formulations.

3. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic effects, particularly in regulating immune responses and reducing inflammation. This compound is often extracted and used in traditional herbal formulations for its medicinal properties.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Bupleurum chinense. They contribute to the plant's therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. These compounds are essential in traditional Chinese medicine for their role in treating conditions like liver dysfunction and fever.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense). It contributes to the plant's traditional uses in promoting circulation and reducing inflammation. Coumarin's pharmacological properties support its role in herbal medicine for treating conditions like liver disorders and respiratory ailments.

6. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating conditions like liver dysfunction and fever. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Bupleurum in herbal medicine.

7. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a key compound found in Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological effects, including anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. Alkaloids from this plant are often studied for their potential therapeutic applications in traditional and modern medicine.

8. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. Tannins are found in various parts of the plant and are believed to support digestive health and immune function. They play a significant role in the traditional uses of Bupleurum in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Bupleurum chinense contains volatile compounds like cineole and limonene. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and are believed to support immune function and reduce inflammation. Essential oil is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's roots and leaves. It is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its therapeutic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Chinese Bupleurum (Bupleurum chinense). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. This compound supports the traditional use of Bupleurum in treating conditions related to liver function and immune response.
