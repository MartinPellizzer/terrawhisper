Chinese Pasqueflower (Pulsatilla chinensis)

Chinese Pasqueflower (Pulsatilla chinensis) is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, native to China, and Central Asia. Traditionally, its roots, rhizomes, and bulbs have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and sedative actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.
