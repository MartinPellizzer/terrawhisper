What is Castilleja racemosa?

Castilleja racemosa is a perennial hemiparasitic plant native to western North America, belonging to the family Orobanchaceae.

It grows 10-30 cm tall with a leafy stem and racemes of flowers, typically pink or purple, that are arranged in an umbrella-like shape. The leaves are oval-shaped, pointed at the tip, and arranged oppositely on the stem. Castilleja racemosa obtains its nutrients by tapping into the roots of other plants, specifically those in the genus Lupinus, through its haustorium, a modified root structure.

Its flowers bloom from April to August and are pollinated by bees and butterflies.
