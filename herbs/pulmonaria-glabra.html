Pulmonaria glabra

Pulmonaria glabra, commonly known as Lungwort, is a perennial plant species native to Europe and Western Asia, characterized by its unique, spotted leaves and blue or pink flowers.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, due to its health benefits, which include soothing and calming the respiratory system.

The therapeutic actions of Pulmonaria glabra are attributed to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, which help to reduce inflammation and combat infections in the lungs.

The bioactive constituents of the plant include saponins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties and make it a valuable natural remedy for respiratory health issues.
