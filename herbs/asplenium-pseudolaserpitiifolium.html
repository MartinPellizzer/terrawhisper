What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium pseudolaserpitiifolium?

Asplenium pseudolaserpitiifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This specific fern is a representative of the family Aspleniaceae, situated within the genus Asplenium, and is identified at the species level as pseudolaserpitiifolium.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Asplenium pseudolaserpitiifolium can cause severe contact dermatitis, gastrointestinal distress, and potential respiratory irritation if the plant matter is inhaled or ingested. Skin contact with the sap often results in redness, itching, and the formation of painful blisters. If consumed, individuals may experience nausea, abdominal cramping, and vomiting as the body reacts to its toxins. Inhalation of airborne particles from the dried leaves can lead to coughing or shortness of breath in sensitive subjects. It is crucial to avoid all direct contact with this species to prevent these adverse physiological reactions.
