Lactuca denticulata

What's the taxonomical classification of Lactuca denticulata?

Lactuca denticulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Asterales and falls within the family Asteraceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Lactuca, and its specific species name, denticulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lactuca denticulata has a basal rosette of deeply lobed, serrated leaves and an erect, branching flowering stem topped with small yellow composite heads. The foliage features irregular, tooth-like margins that give the species its specific epithet. As the plant matures, the central stem elongates to support multiple inflorescences. Each flower head consists of numerous tiny florets clustered together on a common receptacle. The overall structure allows the plant to transition from a compact ground cover to a tall, spreading reproductive form.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across China, Japan, Korea, and parts of the Russian Far East. Within these territories, it is frequently found growing in mountainous environments and along forest edges. The species thrives in areas characterized by moderate moisture and well-drained soils. Its distribution is largely influenced by the seasonal climate variations typical of the northern Pacific rim. Because it prefers cooler, montane habitats, it is rarely encountered in low-lying tropical or arid zones.
