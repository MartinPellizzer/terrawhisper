What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microtoena patchoulii has a prostrate, creeping growth habit characterized by small, glandular-pubescent stems and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flower heads arranged in axillary clusters that appear near the leaf bases. These heads contain tiny, tubular florets that are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.
