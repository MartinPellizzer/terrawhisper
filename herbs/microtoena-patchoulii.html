Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Microtoena, with the unique species designation of patchoulii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microtoena patchoulii has a prostrate, creeping growth habit characterized by small, glandular-pubescent stems and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage typically measures only a few centimeters in length and possesses a distinctively aromatic quality due to its glandular hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flower heads arranged in axillary clusters that appear near the leaf bases. These heads contain tiny, tubular florets that are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.
