Triticum turgidum

What's the taxonomical classification of Triticum turgidum?

Triticum turgidum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical order, it falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Triticum, with its unique designation being the species turgidum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Triticum turgidum has a slender, erect growth habit characterized by narrow, linear leaves and a dense terminal spike composed of numerous spikelets arranged along a central rachis. The spikelets are typically arranged in two rows and contain several fertile florets protected by stiff, pointed glumes. The lemmas are often slightly pubescent and feature a distinct awn that extends from the apex. The overall architecture of the plant reflects its classification as a dwarf or semi-dwarf wheat variety. These morphological traits facilitate efficient grain production within its specific ecological niche.
