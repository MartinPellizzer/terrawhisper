Viola yedoensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Viola yedoensis?

Viola yedoensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Violaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Viola, specifically identified by the species epithet yedoensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola yedoensis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by long-petioled leaves and small, five-petaled violet flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to cordate in shape with crenate or serrate margins and are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowering structures feature a zygomorphic symmetry with a prominent spur at the base of the lower petals. The plant often produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds for dispersal. These morphological traits help distinguish it from other members of the violet genus found in similar habitats.

As discussed in a study, Viola yedoensis Makiho is a medicinal herb traditionally used to treat inflammation-related disorders including allergic skin reactions. Researchers evaluated the effects of Viola yedoensis Makiho ethanol extract (VYE) over a period of 3 weeks in ICR mice with DNCB-induced skin lesions. The administration of VYE suppressed levels of IL-1β, IL-6, and TNF-α in serum and reduced the ratio of CD4+/CD8+ T lymphocytes in the spleen. Furthermore, VYE treatment resulted in the down-regulation of iNOS and COX-2 expression in the dorsal skin. These results indicate that VYE alleviates symptoms such as hypertrophy and hyperkeratosis by inhibiting the T cell-mediated allergic immune response.
