Viola yedoensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola yedoensis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by long-petioled leaves and small, five-petaled violet flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to cordate in shape with crenate or serrate margins and are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowering structures feature a zygomorphic symmetry with a prominent spur at the base of the lower petals. The plant often produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds for dispersal. These morphological traits help distinguish it from other members of the violet genus found in similar habitats.
