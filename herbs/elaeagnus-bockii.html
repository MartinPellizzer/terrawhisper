What's the taxonomical classification of Elaeagnus bockii?

Elaeagnus bockii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Elaeagnaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic position is defined by the genus Elaeagnus and the species bockii.

Elaeagnus bockii has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape, measuring roughly 2 to 5 centimeters in length with entire or slightly serrated margins.

Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters, characterized by their delicate structure and subtle fragrance. The plant produces small, fleshy drupes that serve as its primary fruit type, which often turn a distinct color as they ripen.

      What are the herbal preparations of Elaeagnus bockii?

      Elaeagnus bockii is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical wash using the dried leaves, bark, or fruit of the plant. These liquid extractions are commonly used in traditional medicine to harness the plant's bioactive compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Elaeagnus bockii can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may trigger localized redness or itching upon contact with sensitive skin. While specific toxicity levels are not extensively documented in humans, accidental consumption of the berries or foliage could lead to nausea or stomach upset. Some individuals may also experience respiratory discomfort if they inhale pollen released during flowering periods. Careful avoidance is recommended for those with known sensitivities to similar woody shrub species.
