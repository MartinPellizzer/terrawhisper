Rubus wallichianus

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus wallichianus?

Rubus wallichianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. At the most specific taxonomic levels, it is identified by the genus Rubus and the unique species name wallichianus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus wallichianus has arching, woody stems covered in sharp, recurved prickles and bearing pinnately compound leaves with five to seven serrated leaflets. The leaflets are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with acute tips and a slightly pubescent texture on the underside. These plants produce small, fragrant white flowers that emerge from terminal clusters during the blooming season. Following pollination, the plant develops aggregate fruits known as blackberries which transition from green to a deep reddish-black color when ripe. The overall growth habit is that of a sprawling, thorny shrub adapted to mountain forest understories.
