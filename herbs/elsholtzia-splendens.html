Elsholtzia splendens

What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia splendens?

Elsholtzia splendens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its specific genus, Elsholtzia, and its unique species designation, splendens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia splendens has a deciduous habit characterized by a spreading crown, pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets, and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers.

The tree typically develops a rugged, fissured bark that matures into a grayish-brown color. Its leaflets are elliptic to oblong in shape and feature finely serrated margins. During the fruiting season, the plant produces small, winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal.

The overall structure of the plant is designed to support a dense canopy that provides significant shade.
