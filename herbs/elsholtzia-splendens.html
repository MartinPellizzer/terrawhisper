Elsholtzia splendens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its specific genus, Elsholtzia, and its unique species designation, splendens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia splendens has a deciduous habit characterized by a spreading crown, pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets, and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers. During the fruiting season, the plant produces small, winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal.     As discussed in a study, Elsholtzia splendens Nakai has been used in North-East Asia as an ingredient of folk medicines for treating cough, headache and inflammation. The 75% ethanol extract of the aerial part of E. splendens significantly inhibited mouse croton oil-induced ear edema with a 44.6% inhibition at 400 mg/kg. This plant material also showed significant inhibitory activity against mouse ear edema induced by multiple treatment of phorbol ester for 3 days. E. splendens exhibited significant analgesic activity against mouse acetic acid-induced writhing at 50% inhibition at 400 mg/kg, whereas indomethacin at 5 mg/kg demonstrated 95% inhibition.
