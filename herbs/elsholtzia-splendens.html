What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia splendens?

Elsholtzia splendens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by its specific genus, Elsholtzia, and its unique species designation, splendens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia splendens has a deciduous habit characterized by a spreading crown, pinnately compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets, and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers. The tree typically develops a rugged, fissured bark that matures into a grayish-brown color. Its leaflets are elliptic to oblong in shape and feature finely serrated margins. During the fruiting season, the plant produces small, winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal. The overall structure of the plant is designed to support a dense canopy that provides significant shade.

What are the pharmacological activities of Elsholtzia splendens?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antithrombotic, and vasorelaxant properties. The presence of specific bioactive compounds allows the extracts to effectively reduce pain and suppress inflammatory responses in biological systems. Its antibacterial capabilities enable it to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Additionally, the plant exhibits antithrombotic effects that help prevent blood clot formation. These actions are further complemented by its vasorelaxant activity, which assists in the dilation of blood vessels.

According to a study published by "Archives of pharmacal research", the 75% ethanol extract of the aerial part of Elsholtzia splendens showed significant inhibition of mouse croton oil-induced ear edema at 44.6% with an oral administration of 400 mg/kg. This plant material also demonstrated significant inhibitory activity against mouse ear edema induced by multiple treatments of phorbol ester for 3 days. Regarding analgesic activity, Elsholtzia splendens achieved 50% inhibition of mouse acetic acid-induced writhing at 400 mg/kg, whereas indomethacin at 5 mg/kg demonstrated 95% inhibition. Furthermore, Elsholtzia splendens at concentrations of 5-100 microg/mL significantly inhibited PGE2 production by pre-induced cyclooxygenase-2 of lipopolysaccharide-treated RAW 264.7 cells.

According to a study published by "Environmental toxicology and pharmacology", Elsholtzia splendens is rich in flavonoids and used as folk medicine to benefit cardiovascular systems. The total flavonoids of Elsholtzia splendens (TFES) at concentrations of 5-200mg/L caused a concentration-dependent vasorelaxation in endothelium-intact rings. The endothelium-dependent vasorelaxation of TFES was significantly blocked by the NOS inhibitor N(ω)-nitro-l-arginine methyl ester at 100μM and the guanylate cyclase inhibitor 1H-[1,2,4]oxadiazolo[4,2-α]quinoxalin-1-one at 30μM. Additionally, the indo-methacin at 10μM did not affect the vasorelaxation induced by TFES. The endothelium-independent vasorelaxation was significantly attenuated by the KATP channel blocker glibenclamide.
