Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra)

Japanese Persimmon (Myrica rubra) is a member of the Myricaceae family, native to East Asia, China, and Japan. Traditionally, its fruits, bark, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial shrub.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 3 to 8 meters.
Leaves
Lateral, ovate, acuminate leaves with a smooth upper surface that is glabrous and pale green, and a lower surface that is glaucous and slightly paler.
Flowers
Inflorescences are clustered, flowers are actinomorphic, with five white petals and five yellow sepals, and have five stamens and a superior ovary.
Stems
Erect, woody, branched, with smooth, glabrous surface, and nodes that are slightly swollen.
