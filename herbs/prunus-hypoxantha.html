Possible Side Effects of Prunus hypoxantha

Prunus hypoxantha can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea and diarrhea, due to its high concentration of amygdalin, a compound that can release cyanide when ingested.

Ingesting large quantities of the plant can also lead to respiratory problems, including shortness of breath and difficulty breathing, as the cyanide can damage the respiratory system.

Additionally, improper use of Prunus hypoxantha can cause allergic reactions, including skin irritation and anaphylaxis, in some individuals.

In severe cases, ingesting the plant can lead to cyanide poisoning, which can be fatal if left untreated.
