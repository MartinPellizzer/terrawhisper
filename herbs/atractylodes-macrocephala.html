What is Chinese Atractylodes (Atractylodes Macrocephala)?

Chinese Atractylodes, also known as Atractylodes macrocephala, is a perennial herb native to China, widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported ability to strengthen the spleen, dry dampness, and dispel wind-damp.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Chinese Atractylodes is Atractylodes macrocephala. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, family Compositae, genus Atractylodes, and species Atractylodes macrocephala. It is commonly known as Atractylodes in China and as Chinese Atractylodes in other regions. The plant is also referred to as Bupleurum in some botanical contexts and as "Bai Zhu" in traditional Chinese medicine. Its synonyms include Atractylodes chinensis and Atractylodes lancea, though these are sometimes considered regional or outdated names.

Plant Description and Morphology

Chinese Atractylodes is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm, with a thick, short rhizome that gives rise to multiple upright, hairy stems.

Its leaves are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with a prominent midrib and a wavy, serrated margin, arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces dense, terminal clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with tubular corollas and prominent stamens, blooming in late summer. The root system consists of a fleshy, branched rhizome with numerous fibrous roots, which are the primary medicinal parts.

The roots are typically pale yellow to brown, have a slightly sweet and earthy smell, and are rough in texture with a fibrous interior.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Chinese Atractylodes (Atractylodes macrocephala) is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded forest environments.

The roots and rhizomes of Chinese Atractylodes are primarily used medicinally.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Chinese Atractylodes (Atractylodes macrocephala) is traditionally used in Chinese medicine to strengthen the spleen, resolve dampness, and harmonize the middle jiao, making it effective for conditions such as diarrhea, poor digestion, and edema.
