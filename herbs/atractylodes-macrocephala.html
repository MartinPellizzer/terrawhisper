Chinese Atractylodes

Atractylodes macrocephala

Chinese Atractylodes (atractylodes macrocephala) is a member of the Compositae family and has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat dampness and promote digestion. It is also known for relieving swelling and pain, and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for respiratory issues. The roots of the plant are typically harvested and dried for medicinal preparation.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Atractylodes, scientifically known as Atractylodes macrocephala, belongs to the family Asteraceae. It is also called Bai Zhu in Chinese and is a perennial herb native to China. The plant has a thick, irregularly branched root, broad heart-shaped leaves, and yellow flowers. It is sometimes referred to as "large-headed Atractylodes" in herbal literature.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Powder: Used for gastrointestinal complaints and as a general tonic.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and to relieve coughs.

Safety Profile

Chinese Atractylodes is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy due to potential risks. Long-term use may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, so it is important to follow professional guidance.
