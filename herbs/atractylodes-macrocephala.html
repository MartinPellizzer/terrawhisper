Atractylodes macrocephala

What's the taxonomical classification of Atractylodes macrocephala?

Atractylodes macrocephala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Asterales. It is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which places it among the sunflower and daisy relatives. Within this family, it is specifically identified by the genus Atractylodes and the species macrocephala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Atractylodes macrocephala has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a thick, fleshy, and branched rhizome that serves as a primary storage organ. The plant produces large, basal leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins and a textured, rugose surface. Its flowering stems are erect and can reach significant heights, terminating in dense, terminal capitula composed of numerous small florets. These flower heads are surrounded by multiple layers of involucral bracts that exhibit a distinctive shape and arrangement. The resulting inflorescence presents a robust structure that is central to its taxonomic identification.

What are the pharmacological activities of Atractylodes macrocephala?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, gastroprotective, hepatoprotective, and immunomodulatory pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to suppress systemic inflammation and inhibit the proliferation of malignant cells in various cancer models. The plant also demonstrates a significant ability to protect the gastric mucosa from ulceration and shield liver cells from chemical or oxidative damage. Furthermore, its immunomodulatory properties help regulate the body's immune response to maintain physiological balance. These diverse therapeutic effects make it a valuable subject for pharmacological research and traditional medicine.

As discussed in a study, a homogeneous polysaccharide known as AMP was isolated from Atractylodes macrocephala Koidz. This AMP has a molecular weight of 3.56 kDa and consists primarily of 92.4 % fructose and 7.6 % glucose. In a DSS-induced mouse model of ulcerative colitis, AMP showed efficacy by increasing body weight, lengthening colon length, and elevating goblet cell counts. The polysaccharide also reduced the disease activity index and histological damage by restoring intestinal barrier integrity. These pharmacological effects were mediated via the PI3K/Akt pathway and were entirely dependent on the homeostasis of the gut microbiota.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Atractylenolide I, Atractylenolide III, Atractylon, Atramacronins A-P, and Alisol A. These specific sesquiterpene lactones and steroids contribute to the plant's diverse pharmacological profile. Atractylenolides are frequently studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and anti-tumor activities. The presence of Atractylon and the Atramacronins group further enhances its chemical complexity. Additionally, Alisol A serves as a significant bioactive component within its biological makeup.

As discussed in a study, Atractylodes macrocephala contains Atractylenolide I (8,9-dehhydroasterolide, B), 4,15-epoxy-8 beta-hydroxyasterolide (C) and atractylenolide III (8 beta-hydroasterolide, D). These compounds at concentrations of 28.0 and 56.0 mumol/L inhibited the spontaneous movement of rat isolated ileum with a contractile force decrease (P < 0.001) while the rest force was not affected (P > 0.05). The compounds B, C and D at 28.0 or 56.0 mumol/L significantly antagonized CaCl2-induced contraction of rat isolated ileum when used as a pretreatment for Ver 0.28 mumol/L. Co bathing of B, C or D at 56.0 mumol/L with Atr 0.19 mumol/L weakened the response of ileum to Ach, and at 56.0 mumol/L they restrained the spasm mediated by Neo 2.15 mumol/L. Additionally, B, C or D in a dose of 28.0 mg/kg inhibited the gastrointestinal prochoresis of mice in the modified Phenol Red Paste Model.
