Grewia polygama

What's the taxonomical classification of Grewia polygama?

Grewia polygama belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized under the order Malvales. This plant is a representative of the family Malvaceae, falling under the genus Grewia, and is specifically identified by the species name polygama.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Grewia polygama contains various bioactive compounds including alkaloids, saponins, flavonoids, and tannins that contribute to its medicinal properties. These secondary metabolites are primarily responsible for the plant's antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Research suggests that the specific chemical profile of the plant may offer neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory effects. Some studies also investigate the potential of its saponin content for regulating physiological processes. The concentration of these medicinal constituents can vary depending on the plant's geographic origin and environmental conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Grewia polygama?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin diseases, wounds, inflammation, and certain types of internal infections. In traditional medicinal practices, the bark and leaves are often applied topically to soothe skin irritations or promote the healing of cuts. Some practitioners also utilize extracts from the plant to address digestive issues or systemic inflammation. The chemical properties of the plant are believed to provide antimicrobial and antioxidant benefits during treatment. Research continues into how these specific components interact with different pathological conditions.
