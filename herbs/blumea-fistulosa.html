Blumea fistulosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea fistulosa?

Blumea fistulosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the evolutionary hierarchy, it falls under the order Asterales and is a prominent member of the family Asteraceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Blumea, resulting in its unique scientific designation as the species fistulosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea fistulosa has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by stems that are typically hairy and reach heights of up to one meter. The leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern, featuring an ovate to lanceolate shape with serrated margins and a distinctly pubescent surface. Its inflorescence consists of dense, discoid flower heads that are clustered in terminal corymbose cymes. These small, yellowish flower heads lack ray florets and are supported by involucral bracts that often possess glandular hairs. The overall morphology is defined by its sticky, glandular texture and a robust root system.
