Lepisorus oligolepidus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lepisorus oligolepidus?

Lepisorus oligolepidus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Polypodiinae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae, placing it within the genus Lepisorus. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species designation, oligolepidus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lepisorus oligolepidus has small, triangular to lanceolate fronds that are typically sub-erect and characterized by pinnate division into narrow, linear segments.

The texture of the leaf tissue is relatively thin and somewhat membranous compared to other species in its genus. Its indusia, which cover the sporangia, are notably small and composed of a reduced number of scales. The stipes are generally slender and may feature slight scales or hairs at the base.
