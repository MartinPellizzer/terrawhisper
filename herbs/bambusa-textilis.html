Bambusa textilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Poales, the plant is placed within the family Poaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Bambusa, with its unique designation being the species textilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bambusa textilis has erect, woody culms that reach heights of up to 15 meters and feature a diameter typically ranging between 5 and 10 centimeters. The surface of these stems is characterized by a smooth, yellowish-green texture that may develop small lenticels as the plant matures. Branching occurs at the nodes, with clusters of smaller branches extending from the main stem to support the foliage. The leaves are lanceolate in shape, possessing a fine texture and a bright green color that provides a dense canopy. This bamboo species is distinguished by its compact growth habit and the distinct arrangement of its internodes.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and physical abrasions due to its sharp, lignified outer culm surface. Individuals handling the stalks may experience redness, itching, or swelling if their skin comes into direct contact with the fibrous material. The presence of microscopic splinters or silica can lead to painful puncture wounds if the plant is not managed properly. Some people might also exhibit hypersensitivity reactions to the organic compounds found within the bamboo tissue. Careful use of protective gear is recommended to avoid these dermatological and mechanical issues.
