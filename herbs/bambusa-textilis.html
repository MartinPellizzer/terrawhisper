Bambusa textilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Bambusa textilis?

Bambusa textilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Poales, the plant is placed within the family Poaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Bambusa, with its unique designation being the species textilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bambusa textilis has erect, woody culms that reach heights of up to 15 meters and feature a diameter typically ranging between 5 and 10 centimeters. The surface of these stems is characterized by a smooth, yellowish-green texture that may develop small lenticels as the plant matures. Branching occurs at the nodes, with clusters of smaller branches extending from the main stem to support the foliage. The leaves are lanceolate in shape, possessing a fine texture and a bright green color that provides a dense canopy. This bamboo species is distinguished by its compact growth habit and the distinct arrangement of its internodes.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, Bambusa textilis, requires well-draining, loamy soil and consistent moisture levels to thrive in tropical or subtropical climates with temperatures staying above 15 degrees Celsius.

Researchers continue to investigate these biological effects to better understand their specific molecular pathways.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Bambusa textilis McClure is a traditional Chinese medicinal plant used to treat chronic fever and infectious diseases. A validated chromatographic fingerprint method using LC-Q-TOF-MS detected twenty compounds in the bamboo leaves. Of these twenty detected compounds, fifteen were tentatively identified through literature reviews or by comparing retention time and mass spectrometry data with reference compounds. Three potential bioactive compounds, including (E)-pcoumaric acid, (Z)-p-coumaric acid, and apiigenin-8-C-beta-D-(2"-O-alpha-L-rhamnosyl)-glucopyranoside, were detected in both the leaves and rabbit plasma. The apiigenin-8-C-beta-D-(2"-O-alpha-L-rhamnosyl)glucopyranoside was specifically identified using its UV, MS, and NMR spectra.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating rheumatoid arthritis, chronic fever, and various infectious diseases. In traditional medicinal practices, the components of Bambusa textilis are applied to reduce the painful inflammation associated with joint disorders. Its properties are also utilized to help regulate body temperature during prolonged periods of fever. Additionally, the plant serves as a natural remedy to combat different types of infectious diseases. These applications highlight its importance in folk medicine for managing both inflammatory and systemic ailments.

According to a study published by "Immunopharmacology and immunotoxicology", Bambusae Caulis In Taeniam, or BC, was investigated for its suitability to treat rheumatoid arthritis (RA). In the study, fibroblast-like synoviocytes (FLSs) were adjusted to 2 × 10(6) cells/mL and treated with distilled water extracts of the plant at 1, 10, and 100 µg/mL. The plant showed no toxicity and significantly reduced the IL-1β-induced MMP-3 level by >50%. This reduction was dose-dependent, specifically achieving a P < 0.001 significance at 100 µg/mL. This research represents the first study on the MMP-3 inhibitory effect of the examined plants in FLSs isolated from RA patients.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and physical abrasions due to its sharp, lignified outer culm surface. Individuals handling the stalks may experience redness, itching, or swelling if their skin comes into direct contact with the fibrous material. The presence of microscopic splinters or silica can lead to painful puncture wounds if the plant is not managed properly. Some people might also exhibit hypersensitivity reactions to the organic compounds found within the bamboo tissue. Careful use of protective gear is recommended to avoid these dermatological and mechanical issues.
