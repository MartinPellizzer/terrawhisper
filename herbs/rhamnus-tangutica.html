Rhamnus tangutica

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhamnus tangutica?

Rhamnus tangutica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Rosales. It is a member of the family Rhamnaceae and is specifically placed within the genus Rhamnus, with the specific epithet tangutica defining its unique species identity.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhamnus tangutica has woody, deciduous shrubs characterized by slender, arching branches and small, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The fruit consists of small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark color upon maturation.
