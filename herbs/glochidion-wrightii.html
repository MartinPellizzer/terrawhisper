What's the taxonomical classification of Glochidion wrightii?

Glochidion wrrightii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant's evolutionary path continues through the order Malpighiales and falls within the family Phyllanthaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Glochidion and the specific species designation wrightii.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Glochidion wrrightii can cause intense skin irritation, redness, and painful stinging sensations due to the presence of microscopic, irritating hairs on its foliage. These tiny structures act as a mechanical irritant when they come into contact with human skin. Once embedded, they can lead to localized inflammation or even small, itchy bumps. If the hairs are accidentally inhaled or if they touch the eyes, they may cause respiratory discomfort or ocular irritation. It is essential to avoid direct contact with the leaves to prevent these dermatological reactions.
