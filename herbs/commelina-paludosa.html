What's the taxonomical classification of Commelina paludosa?

Commelina paludosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is positioned in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Commelinales and the family Commelinaceae, which uniquely characterizes its evolutionary placement. Ultimately, the plant is categorized under the genus Commelina, with its specific designation being the species paludosa.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude wetlands and peatlands of the eastern Andes in South America, specifically spanning regions in Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, and Bolivia. It thrives in specialized alpine environments known as paramo and puna ecosystems. These habitats are characterized by high moisture levels and cold temperatures at elevations typically exceeding 3,000 meters. The species is often found in saturated soils or near the edges of mountain lakes and bogs. Its distribution is strictly limited to these specific montane niches across the tropical Andes.
