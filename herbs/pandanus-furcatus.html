Pandanus furcatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Pandanus furcatus?

Pandanus furcatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Pandanales. This specific plant is a representative of the family Pandanaceae and falls under the genus Pandanus, ultimately being identified by its unique species name, furcatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pandanus furcatus has a woody, arborescent growth habit characterized by a branched stem and long, spirally arranged leaves that terminate in sharp spines. The leaves are linear and elongated, featuring serrated margins along the edges to protect the plant. This species often develops a system of stilt roots at the base of the stem to provide structural stability in its environment. The inflorescence is typically inconspicuous, bearing small flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, segmented drupes.
