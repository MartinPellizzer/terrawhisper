What is Saraca minor?

Saraca minor is an evergreen tree species in the family Fabaceae.

It has a straight and columnar stem with a greyish-brown bark, growing up to 15 meters in height. The leaves are bipinnate with 2-4 pairs of pinnae, each pinna bearing 3-7 pairs of leaflets that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape. The inflorescence is a axillary raceme of 2-6 white flowers, with 5 petals and 10 stamens, and a calyx of 5 sepals.

The fruit is a linear pod, 5-7 centimeters long, containing 2-4 seeds.
