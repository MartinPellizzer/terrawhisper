Begonia circumlobata

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia circumlobata?

Begonia circumlobata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is part of the order Cucurbitales and falls within the family Begoniaceae. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Begonia, and its unique species designation, circumlobata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia circumlobata has asymmetrical, cordate leaves characterized by distinctively rounded, lobed margins that encompass the leaf edges. The leaf blades exhibit a fleshy texture and a vibrant green color with prominent venation patterns. These leaves are arranged alternately along succulent, often reddish stems. The margins are notably continuous and smooth, which distinguishes this species from many other members of the genus. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the leaf axils, contributing to the plant's intricate structural complexity.
