What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea leontodontoides has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are lanceolate to oblong in shape with entire or slightly dentate margins. The plant produces erect, unbranched flowering stems that reach various heights depending on the specific alpine environment. Its inflorescence consists of dense, yellow flower heads arranged in a compact terminal cluster. Each individual floret is small and tubular, typical of the Asteraceae family. The root system is robust and woody, allowing the species to anchor firmly in rocky, high-altitude substrates.
