Polygonum campanulatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum campanulatum?

Polygonum campanulatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Caryophyllales and the family Polygonaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Polygonum, and its unique species designation, campanulatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum campanulatum has slender, erect stems that reach heights of up to 50 centimeters and are characterized by distinct, membranous ochreae at each node. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils in terminal or axillary clusters. These blossoms typically exhibit a pale pink to white coloration and are supported by delicate pedicels.
