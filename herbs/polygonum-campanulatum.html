Polygonum campanulatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum campanulatum has slender, erect stems that reach heights of up to 50 centimeters and are characterized by distinct, membranous ochreae at each node. The leaves are simple, lanceolate to narrowly elliptical in shape, and possess entire margins with smooth textures. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils in terminal or axillary clusters. These blossoms typically exhibit a pale pink to white coloration and are supported by delicate pedicels. The plant often develops a sprawling growth habit when environmental conditions lack sufficient structural support.
