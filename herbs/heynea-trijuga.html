Heynea trijuga

What's the taxonomical classification of Heynea trijuga?

Heynea trijuga belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a taxonomic lineage that places it under the order Sapindales. Further classification identifies the plant as a member of the family Meliaceae, specifically within the genus Heynea, with its unique designation as the species trijuga.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Heynea trijuga contains diverse secondary metabolites including various triterpenoids, flavonoids, and specific alkaloids that contribute to its biological activity. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the plant's leaves and bark extracts. Research suggests that these bioactive compounds may possess significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies also indicate potential antimicrobial effects against certain bacterial strains. The specific structural diversity of these molecules provides a basis for investigating their use in traditional pharmacological applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Heynea trijuga?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, fever, cough, and digestive ailments. Traditional practitioners often apply its extracts to soothe various dermatological conditions and irritations. The plant's properties are also utilized to manage respiratory issues like persistent coughing. Furthermore, it serves as a remedy for regulating bodily heat and addressing stomach-related discomforts. These therapeutic applications are common in regions where the species is native.
