Cremaspora triflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Cremaspora triflora?

Cremaspora triflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Gentianaales. This plant is further categorized under the family Rubiaceae, a group known for its diverse botanical characteristics. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Cremaspora, and its specific identity is defined by its species name, triflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cremaspora triflora has small, white, three-petaled flowers that grow from a fleshy, brownish-to-tan underground rhizome. This plant lacks chlorophyll and green leaves, meaning it does not perform photosynthesis and relies entirely on fungal associations for nutrients. The stem is relatively short and emerges directly from the soil to support the floral structure. Each individual flower typically displays a delicate, star-like shape due to its specific petal arrangement. What are the pharmacological activities of Cremaspora triflora?

This plant has antibacterial properties that allow it to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Researchers have identified specific phytochemical compounds within its leaves and stems that disrupt bacterial cell membranes. These bioactive constituents demonstrate effectiveness against both Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria in controlled laboratory settings. Beyond its antimicrobial capabilities, the plant is also being investigated for potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Such diverse pharmacological activities suggest that Cremaspora triflora could serve as a valuable source for developing new therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "BMC complementary and alternative medicine", Cremaspora triflora leaf extracts demonstrated high antibacterial activity against both Gram-positive and Gram-negative pathogens. This plant had a mean MIC of 0.07 mg/ml. The extract showed relatively low cytotoxicity with an LC50 of 20 μg/ml. Additionally, Cremaspora triflora possessed a selectivity index (SI) against S. aureus of 2.87 and an SI against E. coli of 1.15. These results suggest that the plant has promising activities and potential safety for therapeutic use.
