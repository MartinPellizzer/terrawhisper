Cremaspora triflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Cremaspora triflora?

Cremaspora triflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Gentianaales. This plant is further categorized under the family Rubiaceae, a group known for its diverse botanical characteristics. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Cremaspora, and its specific identity is defined by its species name, triflora.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin diseases, wounds, inflammation, and stomach-related issues. The extracts from its leaves and bark possess medicinal properties that assist in healing topical injuries and infections. It is often utilized in traditional practices to soothe skin irritation and promote the regeneration of damaged tissue. Furthermore, its components are believed to alleviate internal discomfort by regulating digestive processes. This botanical species serves as a versatile resource for addressing both dermatological and gastrointestinal concerns.
