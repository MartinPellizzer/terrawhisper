Cremaspora triflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cremaspora triflora has small, white, three-petaled flowers that grow from a fleshy, brownish-to-tan underground rhizome. This plant lacks chlorophyll and green leaves, meaning it does not perform photosynthesis and relies entirely on fungal associations for nutrients. The stem is relatively short and emerges directly from the soil to support the floral structure. Each individual flower typically displays a delicate, star-like shape due to its specific petal arrangement. The overall structure is highly specialized for a parasitic or mycoheterotrophic lifestyle.
