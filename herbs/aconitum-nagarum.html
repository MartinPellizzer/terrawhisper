What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum nagarum?

Aconitum nagarum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Aconitum, specifically identifying as the species nagarum.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Aconitum nagarum targets voltage-gated sodium channels by delivering diterpene alkaloids that bind to and inhibit the influx of sodium ions across nerve membranes. These alkaloids, primarily aconitine and its derivatives, modulate the electrical activity of excitable cells to exert analgesic and anti-arrhythmic effects. By altering the kinetics of sodium channel opening and inactivation, the plant components can suppress pain signaling in peripheral nerves. This interaction with ion channels is also responsible for the profound cardiotoxicity observed at higher concentrations. Such precise protein modulation allows the plant's compounds to serve as potent pharmacological tools for managing inflammation and pain.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe toxicity including nausea, vomiting, dizziness, arrhythmia, bradycardia, hypotension, paralysis, convulsions, respiratory failure, paresthesia, and cardiac arrest. As the poisoning progresses, patients may experience violent convulsions and eventual respiratory failure. Ultimately, the intense physiological strain can culminate in fatal cardiac arrest.
