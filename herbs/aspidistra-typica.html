What's the taxonomical classification of Aspidistra typica?

Aspidistra typica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Asparagaceae. Specifically, it is categorized within the genus Aspidistra, with its taxonomic designation completed by the species epithet typica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aspidistra typica has thick, leathery, dark green leaves that grow in a basal rosette pattern and exhibit a distinctive longitudinal ribbed texture on their surface. The plant lacks a visible stem, with the foliage emerging directly from a creeping rhizome located underground. Its flowering structure is unique, producing small, inconspicuous, brownish flowers that are typically tucked close to the soil surface.
