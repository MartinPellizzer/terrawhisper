Aspidistra typica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aspidistra typica has thick, leathery, dark green leaves that grow in a basal rosette pattern and exhibit a distinctive longitudinal ribbed texture on their surface. These leaves are lanceolate to elliptical in shape and possess a heavy, almost succulent-like consistency. The plant lacks a visible stem, with the foliage emerging directly from a creeping rhizome located underground. Its flowering structure is unique, producing small, inconspicuous, brownish flowers that are typically tucked close to the soil surface. This specialized morphology allows the species to thrive in the dim, humid conditions of the forest floor.
