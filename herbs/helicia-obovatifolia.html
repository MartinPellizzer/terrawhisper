Helicia obovatifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Helicia obovatifolia?

Helicia obovatifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Proteales. It is a member of the family Proteaceae, specifically falling under the genus Helicia, with the unique designation of the species obovatifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Helicia obovatifolia has obovate leaves that are typically 5 to 12 centimeters long, characterized by a wider apex and a gradually narrowing base. The leaf margins are entire, and the texture can range from glabrous to slightly pubescent depending on the specific specimen. Its stems are woody and often feature a slightly roughened bark texture as the plant matures. The flowers are generally small and emerge from the axils, displaying a structure consistent with the Sterculiaceae family.
