Golden Shower Tree

Senna alata

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Senna alata, commonly known as the Golden Shower Tree, belongs to the Fabaceae family. It is an annual shrub native to tropical regions. The plant has feathery leaves, yellow flowers, and a woody root system. It is also known by various regional names such as Kachnar and Lata in India.

Active Compounds

The Golden Shower Tree contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, coumarins, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin
  • Tannin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Digestive Constipation, gastrointestinal inflammation, diarrhea, gastrointestinal ulcers, gastrointestinal spasms Laxative, anti-inflammatory, astringent, anti-ulcer
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, burns Antimicrobial, antiseptic, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory
Immune Inflammation, fever, malaria, allergic reactions, bacterial infections Anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, antimalarial, antihistaminic

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for treating fever, cough, and inflammation.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and skin infections.

Powder: Used for topical application to treat wounds and skin conditions.

Safety Profile

The Golden Shower Tree contains toxic compounds that can cause serious side effects, including gastrointestinal distress and skin irritation. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential harm to the fetus or infant. Use under professional supervision is strongly recommended.
