Sorbus tapashana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sorbus tapashana has pinnate leaves composed of seven to nine elliptical leaflets with serrated margins and a woody structure characterized by small, clustered red pomes. The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and slightly paler underneath, often displaying a fine pubescence on the undersides. Its branches are slender and can develop a rough texture as the tree matures. The fruit clusters emerge in late summer, providing a distinct visual contrast against the foliage. These morphological traits are essential for distinguishing this specific species from other members of the Sorbus genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Caucasus Mountains, specifically found within the high-altitude regions of Georgia. Its range is restricted to certain montane habitats where the terrain provides suitable rocky or forest-edge conditions. Within these mountainous areas, it typically grows at elevations that support specialized alpine or subalpine flora. The distribution remains quite localized due to the unique climatic requirements of its native mountain range. Consequently, the species does not occur naturally in any other part of the world.

    Sorbus tapashana is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or syrups using the dried berries and leaves of the plant.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Sorbus tapashana can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, or mild toxicity if the seeds and core are consumed in large quantities. While the fruit is often used for culinary purposes, the presence of cyanogenic glycosides in the seeds poses a risk of chemical irritation. Some individuals may experience nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting after ingesting significant amounts of the raw fruit. Skin contact with the plant parts might also trigger localized itching or dermatitis in sensitive people. Monitoring consumption levels is essential to avoid these potential adverse physiological responses.
