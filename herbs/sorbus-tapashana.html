What's the taxonomical classification of Sorbus tapashana?

Sorbus tapashana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Rosales. The plant is a member of the family Rosaceae, which places it within the genus Sorbus, ultimately defining its specific identity as the species tapashana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sorbus tapashana has pinnate leaves composed of seven to nine elliptical leaflets with serrated margins and a woody structure characterized by small, clustered red pomes. The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and slightly paler underneath, often displaying a fine pubescence on the undersides. Its branches are slender and can develop a rough texture as the tree matures. The fruit clusters emerge in late summer, providing a distinct visual contrast against the foliage.
