Pteris deltodon

What's the taxonomical classification of Pteris deltodon?

Pteris deltodon belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. The plant is a representative of the family Pteridaceae, falling under the genus Pteris, and is specifically identified by its species name, deltodon.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

The bioactive compounds within the plant, such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, act as inhibitors against the COX-2 protein to reduce prostaglandin synthesis. These phytochemicals also interact with tumor necrosis factor-alpha and other pro-inflammatory signaling pathways to suppress systemic inflammation. Additionally, the plant's constituents modulate the activity of superoxide dismutase and catalase to mitigate oxidative stress at a cellular level. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant demonstrates significant potential for treating inflammatory disorders and oxidative damage.

    What are the herbal preparations of Pteris deltodon?

    Pteris deltodon is prepared as a decoction of the dried rhizomes or as a topical poultice made from crushed fresh leaves. These herbal preparations are traditionally used to address various inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. When processed as a decoction, the liquid extract is often ingested to treat internal discomfort. Alternatively, the leaf paste is applied directly to the surface of the skin to soothe localized swelling. The specific method of preparation depends heavily on the desired therapeutic outcome for the patient.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pteris deltodon can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of certain chemical compounds. Contact with the fronds may lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on sensitive areas of the skin. Some individuals may experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions depending on their individual biological makeup. If the plant is consumed, it can disrupt digestive processes or interfere with internal physiological functions. Careful handling and avoidance of ingestion are essential to mitigate these biological risks.
