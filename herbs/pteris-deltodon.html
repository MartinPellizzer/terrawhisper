Pteris deltodon

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris deltodon has pinnate fronds consisting of large, triangular-shaped pinnules that are deeply lobed and possess distinctively serrated margins. The individual segments are arranged along a central rachis that is typically dark in color and somewhat woody at the base. Each pinna displays a spreading architecture that gives the entire leaf a broad, fan-like appearance. On the underside of the leaflets, small, rounded sori are clustered within protected indusia. These morphological characteristics allow the plant to maximize light capture within its shaded forest habitat.
