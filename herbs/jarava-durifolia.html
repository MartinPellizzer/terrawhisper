Jarava durifolia

Jarava durifolia, commonly known as "Q'ente" in the Quechua language, is a type of plant native to the Andean region in South America.

The bioactive constituents present in Jarava durifolia include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic actions, including antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

Possible Side Effects of Jarava durifolia

Jarava durifolia can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and photosensitivity.

In severe cases, prolonged exposure to the plant's oils can cause contact dermatitis, burns, and blisters.

Improper use of Jarava durifolia can also lead to gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, diarrhea, and stomach ulcers.

Furthermore, excessive consumption of the plant's extracts can cause kidney damage and interact with certain medications, exacerbating pre-existing health conditions.
