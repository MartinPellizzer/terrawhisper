Cynanchum wallichii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Gentianaales, the plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Its specific taxonomic identity is further defined by its placement within the genus Cynanchum, ultimately reaching its species designation as Cynanchum wallichii.

It is also utilized to restore sensation in areas suffering from muscular or skeletal numbness. These diverse therapeutic applications make it a versatile component in traditional medicinal practices.

As discussed in a study, Cynanchum wallichii Wight (CW) is a component of the classic compound formulation Huli San (HLS) used to treat rheumatic pain. This formulation is utilized by ethnic minorities such as the Yi and Miao peoples in Southwest China to alleviate pain and numbness of muscles and bones. The HLS ethanol extract (HLS-EE) containing CW was analyzed and found to have 156 kinds of chemicals. The LD50 value for HLS-EE is 2.962 g/kg. Histopathological examination showed no major abnormalities in the heart, liver, spleen, lungs, and kidneys at 15 times the clinical dose (CLD), though inflammatory cell infiltrations occurred at 90 and 120 times the CLD.
