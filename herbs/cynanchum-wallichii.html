Cynanchum wallichii

What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum wallichii?

Cynanchum wallichii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Gentianaales, the plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Its specific taxonomic identity is further defined by its placement within the genus Cynanchum, ultimately reaching its species designation as Cynanchum wallichii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cynanchum wallichii has slender, twining stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that possess entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically arranged in pairs along the climbing vines, creating a dense green appearance. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying pale colors with complex internal structures characteristic of the Apocynaceae family. These delicate blossoms are accompanied by specialized seed pods known as follicles that split to release wind-dispersed seeds. The overall morphology reflects a specialized adaptation for a climbing, herbaceous lifestyle in forest understories.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cynanchum wallichii?

This plant has significant analgesic and anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities that make it a subject of medicinal interest. Research indicates that its chemical constituents work to reduce pain perception and suppress various inflammatory responses in biological systems. These properties are often attributed to the presence of specific bioactive compounds like alkaloids and glycosides found within the species. Such therapeutic effects highlight its value in traditional medicine and ongoing pharmacological studies.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Cynanchum wallichii Wight (CW) is a component of the classic compound formulation Huli San (HLS). This formulation is utilized to dispel wind and dampness, invigorate blood, and alleviate pain. It is traditionally used to treat rheumatic pain, numbness of muscles and bones, and injuries from falls and bruises. The study analyzed the HLS ethanol extract (HLS-EE), which contains 156 kinds of chemicals. The LD50 value of the HLS-EE is 2.962 g/kg.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for rheumatic disorders, rheumatic pain, musculoskeletal numbness, numbness of muscles and bones, and injuries from falls and bruises. It serves as a traditional remedy specifically targeted at alleviating various forms of physical discomfort. Practitioners utilize it to address the sensation of numbness that affects both the muscles and the bones. Additionally, the plant is applied to treat the localized pain resulting from physical trauma. This includes soothing the body after sustaining bruises or injuries from falls.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Cynanchum wallichii Wight (CW) is one of the medicinal herbs used in the Hulisan formulation to treat rheumatic pain, numbness of muscles and bones, and injuries from falls and bruises. This formulation is utilized by ethnic minorities such as the Yi and Miao peoples to address wind-damp conditions and promote blood circulation. While the study provides data on the HLS-EE ethanol extract, which contains 156 kinds of chemicals and has an LD50 value of 2.962 g/kg, it specifically notes the clinical efficacy of the compound containing Cynanchum wallichii for alleviating pain. The extract was tested against models involving 3 classical types of inflammation and evaluated via multiple analgesic tests. At 15 times the clinical dose (CLD), no major abnormalities were found in major organs, though infiltration occurred at 90 and 120 times the CLD.
