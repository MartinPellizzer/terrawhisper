Crotalaria yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Crotalaria yunnanensis?

Crotalaria yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Fabales. It is further categorized under the family Fabaceae, which places it among the legumes. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Crotalaria, and its specific biological designation is the species yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crotalaria yunnanensis has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of oblong leaflets. The foliage features smooth margins and a soft texture, often displaying a slightly pubescent surface. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellow, papilionaceous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. The fruit develops into a distinctive, inflated, and somewhat bladder-like legume that contains multiple seeds. These specialized pods are essential for the plant's reproductive success and dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe systemic toxicity due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids within its tissues. Ingestion often leads to significant liver damage, which may manifest as acute or chronic hepatic injury. A serious complication of this poisoning is the development of hepatic veno-occlusive disease, also known as venous obstruction syndrome. Patients suffering from these toxic effects frequently experience gastrointestinal distress, including nausea and vomiting. Additionally, intense abdominal pain is a common symptom reported during the onset of poisoning.

  • Toxicity due to pyrrolizidine alkaloids
  • Liver damage
  • Venous obstruction syndrome
