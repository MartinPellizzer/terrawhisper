What is Anredera cordifolia?

Anredera cordifolia is a perennial, twining vine native to South America, belonging to the family Basellaceae.

It has heart-shaped (cordiform) leaves, 2-15 cm in length, with wavy margins and a petiole 1-2 cm long. The leaves are glabrous, alternate, and arranged oppositely along the stem. This species produces white, fragrant flowers in terminal cymes, followed by edible, starchy tubers (corms) 5-10 cm long.

The tubers are used as a food source, particularly in times of famine, and have been introduced to other parts of the world as a potential crop.
