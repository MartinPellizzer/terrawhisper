What's the taxonomical classification of Platanthera clavigera?

Platanthera clavigera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Asparagales and the family Orchidaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific placement within the genus Platanthera and is identified by the species name clavigera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Platanthera clavigera has a slender, greenish stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and supports a single, terminal spike of small, white, club-shaped flowers.

The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, arising primarily from the base of the plant or lower along the stem. Each individual flower features a prominent, elongated nectar spur that is characteristic of the orchid family. The labellum is relatively small and lacks the intense fragrance found in some of its close relatives.

These floral structures are designed to attract specific insect pollinators through their unique shape and color.
