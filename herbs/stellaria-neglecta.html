Stellaria neglecta

What's the taxonomical classification of Stellaria neglecta?

Stellaria neglecta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is further categorized into the family Caryophyllaceae, specifically within the genus Stellaria, ultimately reaching its scientific designation at the species level as Stellaria neglecta.

    What are the herbal preparations of Stellaria neglecta?

    Stellaria neglecta is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice for therapeutic use. The plant material is often simmered in water to create a concentrated liquid medicine. This liquid can also be steeped in hot water to produce a milder tea. For external applications, the crushed leaves are applied directly to the skin to soothe irritation. These various forms allow for both internal consumption and localized treatment.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Stellaria neglecta can cause severe contact dermatitis, localized swelling, and intense skin irritation upon direct exposure to its sap. The plant contains specific irritants that trigger inflammatory responses in the epidermis of sensitive individuals. Once the oils touch the skin, redness and itching may persist for several days. If the sap accidentally enters the eyes, it can cause significant stinging and temporary blurred vision. Prolonged contact without immediate washing may lead to blistering or long-term skin sensitivity.
