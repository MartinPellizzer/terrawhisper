What to know about Eriodictyon californicum (yerba santa) before using it medicinally

Yerba santa, scientifically known as Eriodictyon californicum, is a herb renowned for its impressive health benefits, boasting anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that can help alleviate respiratory issues such as asthma and bronchitis.

Beyond its medicinal uses, yerba santa is also prized in the culinary world for its unique flavor profile, often used to add depth and complexity to traditional Mexican dishes like soups, stews, and sauces. In horticultural circles, yerba santa is recognized for its ease of cultivation and adaptability to a variety of environments, making it a popular choice among gardeners and herbalists alike. From a botanical standpoint, yerba santa belongs to the Boraginaceae family and features distinctive, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly bitter taste.

With roots tracing back to ancient Mesoamerican civilizations, yerba santa has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and spiritual practices, earning its place as a revered and versatile herb in many cultures around the world.
