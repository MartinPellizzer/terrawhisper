Pinellia ternata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pinellia ternata has a growth habit characterized by trifoliate leaves, where each leaf is composed of three distinct, elliptical leaflets arranged around a central petiole. These leaflets typically exhibit smooth margins and a subtle, fine pubescence on their undersides. The plant features a woody stem structure that supports its relatively low-lying foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during its blooming period. The overall morphological structure is adapted for stability in its specific ecological niche.

      What are the herbal preparations of Pinellia ternata?

      Pinellia ternata is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical ointment derived from its dried leaves and stems. The dried plant material is often boiled in water to extract its active constituents for oral consumption. In some traditional practices, a concentrated tincture is made by soaking the herb in alcohol to ensure long-term preservation. For external applications, the plant is frequently crushed into a paste or infused into oils to soothe skin irritations. These various methods allow for the effective delivery of the plant's medicinal properties depending on the specific ailment being addressed.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pinellia ternata can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and intense itching upon direct physical contact with the plant's foliage. These dermatological reactions often manifest as red, inflamed patches or small blisters that may persist for several days. If the sap accidentally enters the eyes, it can lead to significant stinging sensations and temporary blurred vision. Some sensitive individuals might also experience mild gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is ingested. It is essential to wear protective gloves when handling this species to avoid these adverse physical effects.
