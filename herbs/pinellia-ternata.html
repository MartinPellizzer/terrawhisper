Pinellia

Pinellia ternata

Pinellia (Pinellia ternata) is a perennial herb native to East Asia, known for its tuberous rhizomes that are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for their ability to relieve nausea, vomiting, and digestive discomfort.

Botanical Identification

Pinellia ternata, commonly known as pinellia, is a perennial herb belonging to the family Araceae. Native to East Asia, it has various regional names such as banxia in Chinese. The plant features a rhizomatous root system, heart-shaped leaves, and a spadix flower structure. It is also referred to by synonyms like tiannan星 in traditional texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Poales
  • Family: Poaceae
  • Genus: Pinellia
  • Species: Pinellia ternata

Pinellia Actions.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Extract: Used for treating nausea and vomiting.

Powder: Used for coughs and bronchitis.

Safety Profile

Pinellia is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to the plant. Always consult a qualified practitioner before use, especially during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
