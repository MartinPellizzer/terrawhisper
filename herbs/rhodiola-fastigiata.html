Rhodiola fastigiata

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhodiola fastigiata?

Rhodiola fastigiata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Saxifragales. This plant is further categorized into the family Crassulaceae, which contains the genus Rhodiola, ultimately placing it within the specific species Rhodiola fastigiata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhodiola fastigiata has a perennial rhizomatous rootstock that produces erect, unbranched stems reaching heights of up to 15 centimeters. The leaves are arranged in dense, alternating patterns along the stem and typically possess an ovate or elliptical shape with entire margins. These leaves are succulent in texture, helping the plant retain moisture in its high-altitude rocky habitats. Small, yellowish-white flowers emerge from the terminal inflorescences in compact clusters. The resulting fruit consists of small, dry capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds for dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of the Himalayas, spanning across parts of China, India, Nepal, Bhutan, and Tibet. It typically thrives in rocky crevices and subalpine meadows at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters above sea level. Its distribution is strictly limited to cold, moist environments found within these specific mountain ranges. The species prefers well-drained, nutrient-poor soils characteristic of high-mountain scree slopes. Because it is adapted to such extreme elevations, it is rarely found in lowland or temperate forest ecosystems.
