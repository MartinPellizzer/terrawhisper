Thalictrum cirrhosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum cirrhosum?

Thalictrum cirrhosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. It is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Thalictrum, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species cirrhosum.

Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters, displaying four greenish-yellow petals and numerous prominent stamens. These blooms are often accompanied by winged achenes that facilitate wind dispersal once the seeds mature. What are the pharmacological activities of Thalictrum cirrhosum?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including immune-inhibitive and immunosuppressive properties. These biological effects are primarily attributed to the presence of specific alkaloids found within its chemical profile. By modulating the body's defense mechanisms, the extracts can effectively reduce inflammatory responses. Such characteristics make the species a subject of interest for potential applications in treating autoimmune conditions. Further research into these pathways continues to clarify how the plant interacts with various cellular components of the immune system.

As discussed in a study, five new phenyl-C1 substituent apoarpine alkaloids, identified as 6aR-2'-methoxycarbonyl-thaliadin (1), 6aR-2'-carboxyl-thaliadin (2), 6aR-3-methoxy-hernandalinol (3), 6aS-1,3,10-trimethoxy-natalamine (4), and 3-methoxy-2'-methoxycarbonyl-oxohernandalincin (5), were isolated from the whole herb of Thalictrum cirrhosum (Levl.). Along with these, sixteen known isoquinoline alkaloids (6-21) were also obtained from the plant. Six of these isoquinoline alkaloids demonstrated significant inhibitory activity on the proliferation of concanavalin A-stimulated splenocytes. This inhibitory effect was measured through an immunosuppressive bioassay, resulting in IC50 values ranging from 36-44 μM.
