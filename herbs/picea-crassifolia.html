Picea crassifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Picea crassifolia?

Picea crassifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Pinaceae and the genus Picea, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species crassifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picea crassifolia has a conical growth habit characterized by thick, fleshy needles and woody, multi-branched stems. The foliage consists of stiff, dark green needles that are arranged spirally around the twigs. These needles exhibit a distinctively robust texture compared to other spruce species within its genus. Small, woody cones develop from the branch tips and serve as the primary reproductive structures. The bark of the mature tree becomes deeply fissured and develops a rough, scaly appearance over time.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the eastern Himalayas, specifically distributed across parts of southwestern China, Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India. It typically inhabits subalpine zones where it thrives in moist, cool environments. These coniferous trees are most commonly found growing on steep mountain slopes at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters. Their presence is often restricted to specific microclimates that provide consistent moisture and protection from extreme temperature shifts.
