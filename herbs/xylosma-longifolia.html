Xylosma longifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Xylosma longifolia?

Xylosma longifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Salicaceae, falling specifically within the genus Xylosma, and is identified by its unique species name, longifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Xylosma longifolia contains bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and various terpenoids that provide its medicinal properties. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers often study these compounds to understand their potential role in treating oxidative stress and cellular damage. The presence of tannins specifically suggests antimicrobial capabilities that may be useful in traditional medicine. Furthermore, the diverse profile of polyphenols in the leaves supports its use in various ethnobotanical applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Xylosma longifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and various gastrointestinal ailments. The leaves are often prepared as a decoction or poultice to apply directly to cutaneous lesions. In traditional medicinal practices, its extracts are also utilized to alleviate internal digestive discomfort. Some practitioners employ the plant to reduce swelling and manage localized pain through topical administration. These applications rely on the bioactive compounds found within its foliage.
