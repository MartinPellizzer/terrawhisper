What's the taxonomical classification of Carex meyeriana?

Carex meyeriana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Poales, it is further placed into the family Cyperaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Carex, and its specific species designation, meyerianana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex meyeriana has a perennial growth habit characterized by tufted, cespitose clumps of narrow, linear leaves that typically measure 2 to 5 millimeters in width. The foliage is predominantly bright green and lacks prominent midveins, which helps distinguish it from similar sedges. Its inflorescence consists of several distinct spikes, with male spikes located at the top and female spikes situated lower on the culm. The perigynia are relatively small and oval in shape, often possessing a short beak. These reproductive structures are clustered tightly along the central stem to facilitate pollination and seed dispersal.
