Ficus altissima

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus altissima has large, elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture, glossy dark green on the upper surface, and pale green underneath. The plant grows as a woody tree or shrub with a thick, upright trunk and spreading branches. Its leaves feature prominent veins that create a textured appearance on the foliage. The margins of the leaves are entire, meaning they are smooth and lack serrations. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced within specialized structures called syconia.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across countries such as India, China, Thailand, and Indonesia. It thrives primarily in warm, humid climates where consistent moisture and high temperatures are available. Within its natural range, it is frequently found in lowland forests and coastal areas. Many populations have been introduced to other tropical zones worldwide for use in landscaping and ornamental gardens. This wide dispersal is due to its popularity in urban environments across various tropical latitudes.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause skin irritation, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and dizziness if its sap or parts are ingested or touched. The milky latex found within the leaves and stems is a known irritant that may lead to redness or rashes upon contact with the skin. If a person consumes any part of the plant, they may experience gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Some individuals might also report feeling dizziness following exposure or ingestion. These symptoms generally occur due to the plant's natural chemical compounds acting as a defense mechanism.
