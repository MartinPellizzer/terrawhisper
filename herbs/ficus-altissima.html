Ficus altissima

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus altissima?

Ficus altissima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its classification through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Rosales. The plant is a member of the family Moraceae and is further identified by its genus, Ficus, and its specific species, altissima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus altissima has large, elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture, glossy dark green on the upper surface, and pale green underneath. The plant grows as a woody tree or shrub with a thick, upright trunk and spreading branches. Its leaves feature prominent veins that create a textured appearance on the foliage. The margins of the leaves are entire, meaning they are smooth and lack serrations. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced within specialized structures called syconia.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight and consistently moist but well-draining soil to thrive in indoor environments.

You should maintain a stable room temperature between 60 and 80 degrees Fahrenheit to prevent leaf drop. Avoid placing it in drafty areas or near air conditioning vents, as sudden temperature shifts can stress the foliage. Regular fertilization during the spring and summer growing seasons will support robust leaf production.

Finally, wipe the large leaves with a damp cloth occasionally to remove dust and ensure efficient photosynthesis.
