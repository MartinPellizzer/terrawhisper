Gomphrena globosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Gomphrena globosa?

Gomphrena globosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Amaranthaceae, specifically falling within the genus Gomphrena, and is identified by its unique species name, globosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gomphrena globosa has erect, branched stems that support globose, papery flower heads composed of small, tubular florets surrounded by colorful, bract-like structures. The leaves are typically opposite, lanceolate to ovate in shape, and possess a smooth or slightly hairy texture. These plants are characterized by their vibrant, spherical inflorescences that can range in color from bright pink to deep purple. The bracts provide a long-lasting ornamental quality because they remain colorful even after the plant has dried. Small, inconspicuous flowers are nestled within these showy bracts to facilitate pollination.
