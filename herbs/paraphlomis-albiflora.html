Paraphlomis albiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Paraphlomis albiflora?

Paraphlomis albiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Lamiales. This species is a member of the family Lamiaceae, specifically falling within the genus Paraphlomis, with its unique identity defined by the species name albiflora.

Paraphlomis albiflora has pharmacological properties that include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and analgesic effects used primarily in traditional medicine to treat various ailments. Researchers often investigate its potential for managing chronic inflammatory conditions and protecting against tissue damage. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammation, skin infections, respiratory ailments, and various digestive disorders. It contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and soothe irritation in the body. Practitioners often apply its extracts topically to combat fungal or bacterial issues on the skin. When taken internally, it may assist in calming lung irritation or relieving stomach discomfort. These therapeutic properties make it a significant component in traditional herbal medicine.
