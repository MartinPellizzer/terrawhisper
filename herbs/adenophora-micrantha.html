What's the taxonomical classification of Adenophora micrantha?

Adenophora micrantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Campanulaceae, which places it within the genus Adenophora. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species designation, micrantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora micrantha has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems that reach heights of approximately 10 to 40 centimeters and bear small, tubular, violet-colored flowers. Each flower is supported by a slender pedicel and possesses a five-lobed corolla that is typical of the Campanulaceae family. The inflorescence is usually a terminal cyme, where multiple small blossoms cluster together at the top of the plant.
