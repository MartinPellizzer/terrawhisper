Micromelum falcatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Micromelum falcatum?

Micromelum falcatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Micromelum. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific epithet, resulting in the full scientific name Micromelum falcatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Micromelum falcatum has woody, climbing stems with small, oppositely arranged, falcate leaves that are characterized by their sickle-shaped margins and acute tips. The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a bright green color, often appearing somewhat fleshy or leathery. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in a characteristic arrangement. According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Micromelum falcatum is a medicinal plant of the Rutaceae family used in Traditional Chinese Medicine mainly against colds and rheumatoid arthritis. The study developed a rapid UPLC-ESI(+)-HRMS method for the profiling of M. falcatum leaf extracts and the targeted isolation of coumarin constituents. After targeted isolation, 8 coumarins were purified using semi-preparative HPLC, which included 3 new natural products named microfalcrin, microcoumaririn, and micromelosidester. Several 7-oxygenated coumarin derivatives were detected through chromatographic, spectroscopic, and spectrometric features. The anti-inflammatory properties of the extracts and representative compounds were evaluated by measuring the inhibition of the pro-inflammatory mediator NF-κB induction and nitric oxide (NO) production.
