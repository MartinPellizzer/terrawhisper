Micromelum falcatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Micromelum falcatum has woody, climbing stems with small, oppositely arranged, falcate leaves that are characterized by their sickle-shaped margins and acute tips. The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a bright green color, often appearing somewhat fleshy or leathery. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in a characteristic arrangement. These plants frequently produce tiny, fleshy fruits that serve as a key identifying feature of the species. The overall growth habit allows the plant to scramble through dense vegetation using its slender vines.
