Rosa kokanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa kokanica?

Rosa kokanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. It is further categorized under the family Rosaceae, which places it within the well-known genus Rosa. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, kokanica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Rosa kokanica contains high concentrations of vitamin C, polyphenols, anthocyanins, and various flavonoids that contribute to its medicinal profile. These bioactive compounds act as potent antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals within the body. The presence of organic acids and tannins provides the plant with astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. When utilized in traditional remedies, these chemical constituents support immune function and promote skin health. Research suggests that these specific phytochemicals may also offer protective effects against oxidative stress at a cellular level.

What are the therapeutic actions of Rosa kokanica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, digestive issues, and respiratory ailments through its medicinal properties. The rose hips and petals contain significant amounts of Vitamin C and antioxidants that help boost the immune system against common illnesses. In traditional medicine, extracts from the plant are applied topically to soothe minor burns, wounds, and skin irritations. Some herbal preparations are also utilized to alleviate symptoms of the common cold or seasonal allergies. These therapeutic uses stem from the plant's natural antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory compounds.
