Callicarpa kwangtungensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa kwangtungensis has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a pubescence that covers the undersides. The woody stems are typically slender and develop a somewhat rough texture as the plant matures. Small, axillary clusters of flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually producing distinctive spherical drupes. These berries transition from green to a vibrant purple or reddish color when fully ripe. The overall structure of the shrub is characterized by a spreading habit and dense foliage.
