Callicarpa kwangtungensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa kwangtungensis?

Callicarpa kwangtungensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Callicarpa, with the unique species designation kwangtungensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa kwangtungensis has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a pubescence that covers the undersides.

The woody stems are typically slender and develop a somewhat rough texture as the plant matures. Small, axillary clusters of flowers emerge from the leaf axils, eventually producing distinctive spherical drupes. These berries transition from green to a vibrant purple or reddish color when fully ripe.

The overall structure of the shrub is characterized by a spreading habit and dense foliage.

Because specific toxicological studies on this exact species are limited, cautious handling is recommended to avoid unexpected reactions.
