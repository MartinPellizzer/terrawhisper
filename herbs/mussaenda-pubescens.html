What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mussaenda pubescens has a woody shrub architecture characterized by spreading branches, elliptical leaves with entire margins, and large, showy, petal-like colorful sepals. Its most striking morphological feature is the enlarged, brightly colored calyx that serves to attract pollinators to the smaller, tubular white flowers. The stems are often slightly hairy, contributing to the overall pubescent quality suggested by its specific epithet.
