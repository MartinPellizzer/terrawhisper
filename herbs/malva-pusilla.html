Malva pusilla

What's the taxonomical classification of Malva pusilla?

Malva pusilla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Malvales and is a member of the family Malvaceae. Taxonomically, it is placed within the genus Malva, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species pusilla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Malva pusilla has an annual, erect, and branched habit characterized by small, simple, or irregularly lobed leaves and tiny, five-petaled pink to white flowers. The stems are typically slender and can grow to a height of approximately 10 to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often cordate or reniform in shape with crenate margins and are arranged alternately along the stem. The flowers are borne in the leaf axils and are much smaller than those of related species in the Malvaceae family. Following pollination, the plant produces small, schizocarpic fruits that are somewhat rounded and contain several seeds.

What are the pharmacological activities of Malva pusilla?

This plant has pharmacological activities that include inducing abortion, treating joint sprains, and treating postpartum hemorrhage. The emmenagogue properties of Malva pusilla allow it to stimulate uterine contractions for the purpose of terminating a pregnancy. Its topical application is also used in traditional medicine to alleviate the pain and inflammation associated with joint sprains. Furthermore, the plant acts as a uterotonic to help control and treat postpartum hemorrhage by encouraging the uterus to contract after childbirth. These diverse medicinal uses demonstrate the plant's significant impact on reproductive and musculoskeletal health.
