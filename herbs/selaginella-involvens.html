Selaginella involvens

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella involvens?

Selaginella involvens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is part of the class Equisetopsida and falls under the subclass Lycopodiidae. Within this taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is organized into the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Selaginella, with its specific designation being the species involvens.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Selaginella involvens can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if handled improperly or ingested. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many lycopods contain alkaloids that may trigger hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. Contact with the plant's surface can lead to redness or itching on the skin. If accidentally swallowed, it may result in nausea or abdominal discomfort. It is essential to use caution and avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse reactions.
