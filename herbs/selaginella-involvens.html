Selaginella involvens

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella involvens?

Selaginella involvens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is part of the class Equisetopsida and falls under the subclass Lycopodiidae. Within this taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is organized into the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Selaginella, with its specific designation being the species involvens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella involvens has a creeping rhizome from which small, scale-like leaves emerge in four distinct rows along the stems.

These microphylls are arranged in a highly organized pattern, giving the plant a characteristic feathery or fern-like appearance. The leaves are small and triangular, often overlapping to protect the delicate photosynthetic tissues underneath. Each stem is branched and can form dense, spreading mats across its substrate.

This specific morphology allows the plant to efficiently colonize moist environments through vegetative spreading.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Selaginella involvens can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if handled improperly or ingested. It is essential to use caution and avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse reactions.
