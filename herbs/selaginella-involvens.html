Selaginella involvens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella involvens has a creeping rhizome from which small, scale-like leaves emerge in four distinct rows along the stems. These microphylls are arranged in a highly organized pattern, giving the plant a characteristic feathery or fern-like appearance. The leaves are small and triangular, often overlapping to protect the delicate photosynthetic tissues underneath. Each stem is branched and can form dense, spreading mats across its substrate. This specific morphology allows the plant to efficiently colonize moist environments through vegetative spreading.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia, specifically occurring within the subtropical and tropical montane forests of China, Vietnam, Thailand, and Myanmar. It is typically found growing in moist, shaded environments at elevations ranging from several hundred to over two thousand meters above sea level. The species favors rocky crevices and damp soil where humidity remains consistently high throughout the year. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific microclimates provided by mountain ranges in these specific geographic corridors.
