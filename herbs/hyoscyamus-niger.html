Hyoscyamus niger

What's the taxonomical classification of Hyoscyamus niger?

Hyoscyamus niger belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Solanales and the family Solanaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Hyoscyamus, and its species name, niger.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Hyoscyamus niger contains the tropane alkaloids hyoscyamine, scopolamine, and atropine which act as potent anticholinergic agents. These alkaloids function by competitively inhibiting acetylcholine at muscarinic receptor sites within the nervous system. In controlled medical applications, these compounds are utilized to treat gastrointestinal spasms and reduce excessive secretions. They also serve as essential components for treating certain types of bradycardia and organophosphate poisoning. Because of their high toxicity, these chemical constituents must be strictly regulated during any therapeutic use.

What are the therapeutic actions of Hyoscyamus niger?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat gastrointestinal spasms, excessive secretions, Parkinson's disease symptoms, and certain types of ocular conditions. The alkaloids found within the plant act as anticholinergic agents to relax smooth muscle tissue. Doctors often utilize its derivatives to alleviate cramping caused by irritable bowel syndrome or peptic ulcers. Additionally, the substances help manage tremors and rigidity associated with neurological disorders. These medicinal properties are carefully controlled due to the high toxicity of the raw plant material.
