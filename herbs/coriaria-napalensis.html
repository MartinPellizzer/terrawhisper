Coriaria napalensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Coriaria napalensis?

Coriaria napalensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, it is placed in the order Cucurbitaleales and falls under the family Coriariaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Coriaria and the species napalenensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Coriaria napalensis contains various bioactive compounds including tannins, flavonoids, and specific phenolic acids that contribute to its medicinal properties. These secondary metabolites provide significant antioxidant activity which helps neutralize free radicals in biological systems. The plant also possesses antimicrobial qualities that can inhibit the growth of certain pathogenic microorganisms. Researchers study these chemical constituents to understand their potential applications in developing new pharmacological treatments. The complex chemical profile of the species suggests a diverse range of therapeutic possibilities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Coriaria napalensis?

Coriaria napalensis has anthelmintic properties that allow it to effectively expel intestinal parasites from a host. The plant contains specific chemical compounds that act on the nervous systems of worms to paralyze them. Once the parasites are immobilized, they are unable to maintain their grip on the intestinal wall and are expelled through the digestive tract. Historically, these medicinal qualities have been utilized in traditional practices to treat various parasitic infections. Careful dosage is essential because the active constituents can be toxic to the host if administered in excess.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments, skin conditions, and inflammatory symptoms. Its medicinal properties are primarily utilized to soothe stomach discomfort and gastrointestinal issues. Additionally, topical applications of the plant help alleviate various skin irritations and infections. The chemical components within the species serve to reduce inflammation throughout the body. Local traditional practices often rely on these specific healing capabilities for holistic wellness.

What are the herbal preparations of Coriaria napalensis?

Coriaria napalensis is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the roots, leaves, or stems in water to extract its active constituents. This liquid extract is often used in traditional medicinal practices to address specific ailments through oral consumption. In some regional traditions, the plant material may also be processed into a topical paste for external application on the skin. These various preparations allow for the controlled delivery of the plant's phytochemicals depending on the intended therapeutic use. The method of preparation is crucial because different parts of the plant contain varying concentrations of chemical compounds.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Coriaria napalensis can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea, due to the presence of toxic compounds like coriamyrtin. These poisonous substances act as potent toxins that can lead to significant physiological damage if ingested. In many cases, the consumption of the plant's fruit or bark results in rapid onset of symptoms shortly after ingestion. If the dose is high enough, the toxicity may escalate to more serious systemic issues. Therefore, extreme caution is necessary because even small amounts can lead to acute illness.
