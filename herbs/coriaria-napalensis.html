Coriaria napalensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coriaria napalensis has small, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a leathery texture. These leaves are typically dark green and can grow up to several centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that cluster into dense inflorescences. Its stems are woody and often feature a sprawling or climbing growth habit. The fruit consists of small, fleshy drupes that change color as they ripen.

    What are the herbal preparations of Coriaria napalensis?

    Coriaria napalensis is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the roots, leaves, or stems in water to extract its active constituents. This liquid extract is often used in traditional medicinal practices to address specific ailments through oral consumption. In some regional traditions, the plant material may also be processed into a topical paste for external application on the skin.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Coriaria napalensis can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including intense vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea, due to the presence of toxic compounds like coriamyrtin. These poisonous substances act as potent toxins that can lead to significant physiological damage if ingested. In many cases, the consumption of the plant's fruit or bark results in rapid onset of symptoms shortly after ingestion. If the dose is high enough, the toxicity may escalate to more serious systemic issues. Therefore, extreme caution is necessary because even small amounts can lead to acute illness.
