Athyrium brevifrons

What's the taxonomical classification of Athyrium brevifrons?

Athyrium brevifrons belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Polypodiales and is a member of the family Aspleniaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Athyrium, with its specific designation being the species brevifrons.

Athyrium brevifrons is prepared as a decoction, a dried powder, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic application. When addressing skin irritations or localized swelling, the fresh or dried leaves are crushed into a paste and applied directly to the affected area. These various methods allow for the targeted delivery of the plant's phytochemical properties.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Athyrium brevifrons can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and digestive distress if consumed or touched without protection. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many members of the Athyrium genus contain compounds that may trigger hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. Contact with the fronds often results in localized redness, itching, or a rash on the skin. If ingested, the plant might lead to nausea or abdominal discomfort due to its chemical composition. It is essential to use caution and avoid direct contact to prevent these adverse reactions.
