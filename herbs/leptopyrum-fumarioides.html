According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the crude methanol extract of Leptopyrum fumarioides was separated into 3 different sub-extracts consisting of dichloromethane, n-butanol, and water. The crude methanol extract, the dichloromethane sub-extract, and a mixture of isolated compounds increased the level of DNA damage after 3 hours of exposure. In contrast, no increase in DNA damage was observed in cells exposed to the n-butanol and water sub-extracts or the pure flavone. When tested with the DNA damaging agent catechol, all sub-extracts were found to reduce the induced DNA damage. The n-butanol sub-extract and the flavone demonstrated the most prominent antioxidative effects.
