Aframomum angustifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aframomum angustifoiliun has narrow, lanceolate leaves that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length and possess parallel venation. The plant grows from a creeping rhizome that serves as a sturdy underground stem. Its foliage is characterized by a deep green color and a smooth, glabrous texture on the leaf surfaces. The inflorescence emerges from the center of the leaf stalks, producing small, delicate flowers. These botanical structures collectively define the species' distinct appearance within its tropical habitat.

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of West and Central Africa, specifically spanning from Sierra Leone to Uganda. Within these habitats, it is often found in densely vegetated areas characterized by rich, organic soils. The species follows the distribution of the Guineo-Congolian phytogeographical region.
