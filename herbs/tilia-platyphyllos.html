Native to regions including Eastern Europe, Central Europe, Southeastern Europe, Southwest Asia, Eastern Mediterranean, Caucasus Region, Middle East, Southern Russia, and the Balkans, this tree is well adapted to temperate climates. Morphologically, it is a deciduous tree with smooth, grey bark, heart-shaped leaves that are broad and have a flat base, and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that appear in early summer.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Tilia platyphyllos grows in well-drained, loamy soils that are rich in organic matter, preferring full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall, requiring regular watering during dry periods but avoiding waterlogging. To ensure healthy growth, regular pruning of dead or diseased branches and mulching around the base of the plant help maintain soil moisture and prevent weed competition. The best time to harvest its leaves and flowers is in early summer, when the plant is in full bloom, using sharp pruning shears to cut the stems just below the flower clusters.

For maximum potency, the harvested parts should be dried in a cool, dark, and well-ventilated area, then stored in airtight containers away from light and moisture to preserve their medicinal properties.
