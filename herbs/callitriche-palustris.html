Callitriche palustris

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callitriche palustris has submerged, whorled leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, typically measuring between 10 and 30 millimeters in length. These delicate leaves emerge in clusters of varying numbers around thin, cylindrical stems that can grow several decimeters long. The plant lacks a distinct root system, instead utilizing small rhizomes to anchor itself within soft aquatic substrates. Its reproductive structures consist of tiny, inconspicuous flowers that develop directly on the submerged stems. The overall morphology is adapted for life in stagnant or slow-moving freshwater environments where its fine foliage can efficiently capture nutrients.
