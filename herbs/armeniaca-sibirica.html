Armeniaca sibirica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Armeniaca sibirica has a woody perennial structure characterized by spreading branches, serrated ovate leaves, and small white flowers with five petals. The shrubby growth habit typically reaches heights of one to two meters depending on its environmental conditions. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stems and feature finely toothed margins with prominent veins. During the flowering season, the blossoms emerge from the buds to signal the plant's reproductive cycle. Small, fleshy fruits develop following successful pollination to aid in seed dispersal.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Armeniaca sibirica targets inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and lipoxygenase (LOX), as well as various pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). Its bioactive compounds, particularly phenolic acids and flavonoids, work by inhibiting the signaling pathways that trigger these protein responses. By modulating the activity of these specific enzymes, the plant effectively reduces the production of prostaglandins and leukotrienes responsible for pain and swelling. Additionally, its constituents may interact with antioxidant enzymes to mitigate oxidative stress at the cellular level. These multifaceted protein interactions underpin the plant's demonstrated anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
