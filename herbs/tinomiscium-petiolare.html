Tinomiscium petiolare

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tinomiscium petiolare has woody vines characterized by trifoliate leaves with long petioles, small greenish flowers, and small fleshy fruits. The leaves consist of three distinct leaflets that are typically elliptic or ovate in shape. These vines often climb through forest canopies using specialized structures to anchor themselves to host trees. The small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in clusters and serve as the reproductive centers of the plant. Once fertilized, the plant produces berries that play a role in its dispersal within the ecosystem.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Tinomiscium petiolare can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if handled or ingested improperly. Some individuals might experience swelling or redness if the sap touches an open wound. There is also a risk of nausea or abdominal pain if any part of the vine is consumed accidentally. Scientific studies on its specific toxicity levels in humans remain limited, so caution is necessary.
