10 Precautions To Take When Using Euphorbia Peplus (Radium Weed)

Euphorbia peplus has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as topical application in a well-ventilated area and avoiding contact with the eyes, nose, and mouth.

This is crucial to prevent irritation and potential phototoxicity, which can manifest as skin redness and blistering if exposed to sunlight.

For instance, neglecting this precaution may lead to severe skin damage or even burns.

1. Store safely out reach

When using Euphorbia peplus medicinally, it's important to avoid contact with skin because the sap of this plant contains a milky latex that can cause severe dermatitis and irritation.

The latex can also be absorbed through open wounds or mucous membranes, leading to systemic toxicity. Prolonged exposure can result in respiratory issues, allergic reactions, and even anaphylaxis.

Taking this precaution ensures a safe and effective treatment experience.
