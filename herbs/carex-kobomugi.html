Carex kobomugi

What's the taxonomical classification of Carex kobomugi?

Carex kobomugi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is categorized under the order Poales. As a member of the family Cyperaceae, it is further organized into the genus Carex, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species kobomugi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex kobomugi has densely tufted, perennial growth habits characterized by narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green, somewhat rigid, and reach lengths of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaf blades possess a slightly channeled or folded structure and often feature a subtle luster. During its flowering season, the plant produces inconspicuous, brownish-green spikes that emerge from the center of the foliage clumps. These spikes are composed of small, scale-like perigynia that contain the seeds. The overall form of the plant remains compact and mounded, making it a distinctive groundcover in many landscapes.
