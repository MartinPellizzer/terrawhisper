What's the taxonomical classification of Raphia gracilis?

Raphia gracilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Arecales, the plant is placed within the family Arecaceae. Finally, its specific identification is defined by the genus Raphia and the species gracilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Raphia gracilis has a single unbranched stem that reaches heights of up to 10 meters and bears large, pinnately compound leaves arranged in a terminal rosette. The leaf rachis is slender and elongated, supporting numerous linear leaflets that extend outward from the central axis. These leaflets exhibit a parallel venation pattern characteristic of monocots and possess a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaf bases. Its root system consists of a stout, fibrous structure designed to anchor the tall stem in tropical environments.
