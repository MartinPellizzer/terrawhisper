Kerria japonica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Kerria japonica has arching, woody stems that produce bright yellow, five-petaled flowers or double-flowered varieties with numerous petals. The foliage consists of ovate to elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. These leaves typically feature finely serrated margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. During the blooming season, the plant can reach a height and spread of several feet, forming a dense, shrubby habit. The overall structure is characterized by its graceful, weeping branches that often bend toward the ground under the weight of the flowers.
