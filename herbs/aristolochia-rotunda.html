What to know about Aristolochia rotunda (birthwort) before using it medicinally

Birthwort, scientifically known as Aristolochia rotunda, is a herb that has been revered for its numerous health benefits, including its ability to soothe digestive issues and alleviate respiratory problems.

In addition to its medicinal properties, birthwort is also used in various culinary dishes, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine where it is added to soups and stews for its believed ability to promote healthy blood flow and digestion. As a horticultural interest, birthwort is relatively easy to grow and can thrive in a variety of environments, making it a popular choice among gardeners. From a botanical perspective, birthwort belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, curved fruit capsules that resemble horns or trumpets.

Throughout history, birthwort has been referenced in ancient texts, including those from Greece and Rome, where it was used for its medicinal and spiritual properties.

What are the side effects of Aristolochia rotunda when used improperly?

When used improperly, Aristolochia rotunda increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as severe gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Moreover, the herb can cause kidney damage and even lead to a condition known as aristolochic acid nephropathy (AAN), which is a progressive and potentially life-threatening disease characterized by inflammation, fibrosis, and scarring of the kidneys. In some cases, AAN can also result in bladder cancer and urothelial carcinoma.

Furthermore, Aristolochia rotunda may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and exacerbate bleeding disorders or worsen hypertension. Additionally, using the herb improperly may also lead to allergic reactions, including skin rashes, itching, and hives.

It is essential to consult a qualified healthcare professional before using Aristolochia rotunda for medicinal purposes to minimize the risk of adverse effects and ensure safe and effective treatment.
