Astragalus mongholicus

What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus mongholicus?

Astragalus mongholicus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, it falls within the genus Astragalus, eventually reaching its specific designation at the species level as monghoolicus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus mongholicus has a perennial herbaceous rootstock that produces erect stems reaching up to 30 centimeters in height, characterized by trifoliate leaves and dense, papilionaceous flower clusters. The leaves consist of three leaflets that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with finely serrated margins. Its flowers are arranged in umbel-like racemes and exhibit a range of colors from pale yellow to creamy white. The legume-style pods are small and compressed, housing several tiny seeds essential for its reproduction. Fine hairs often cover the stems and foliage, providing a protective texture to the plant's structure.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid steppe regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across Mongolia, northern China, and parts of southern Siberia. It thrives predominantly in temperate grasslands and desert-steppe ecosystems characterized by low precipitation. The species is frequently found growing in sandy or loamy soils within these vast, open landscapes. Its distribution follows the specific climatic patterns of the Central Asian interior where seasonal temperature extremes are common. Localized populations are often concentrated in areas with high sun exposure and minimal competition from dense forest cover.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Astragalus mongholiicus contains medicinal compounds primarily concentrated in its woody, perennial root. Practitioners often harvest and dry these roots to create decoctions, powders, or tinctures for therapeutic use. What are the pharmacological activities of Astragalus mongholicus?

This plant has immunomodulatory, anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, anti-apoptotic, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to regulate the immune system and suppress excessive inflammatory responses in various tissues. Research indicates that its components can inhibit tumor progression by modulating cellular signaling pathways. Additionally, the plant exerts anti-apoptotic effects that influence programmed cell death processes within specific cell types. Finally, its potent antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress throughout the body.

As discussed in a study, Astragalus mongholicus contains Astragaloside III (AS-III), which is one of its most active components with immunomodulatory effects. Network pharmacology and molecular docking identified 105 common targets related to AS-III and immunosuppression. In CTX-induced immunosuppressive mouse models, AS-III provided significant protection regarding body weight, immune organ index, and hematological indices. Using a macrophage RAW264.7 cell model induced by hypoxia/lipopolysaccharide (LPS), AS-III improved immune function by reducing the release of NO, TNF-α, IL-1β, PDHK-1, LDH, lactate, HK, PK, and GLUT-1. The study confirmed that AS-III attenuates the immunosuppressive state through the HIF-1α/PDHK-1 pathway and restores decreased ATP levels caused by hypoxia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Astragaloside IV, Astragaloside III, Formononetin, Calycosin, and Astragalus saponin IV. These bioactive molecules are primarily categorized as saponins and phytoestrogens that contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties. Astragaloside IV and Astragaloside III are studied extensively for their potential to support cardiovascular health and immune function. The presence of Formononetin and Calycosin provides additional antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the organism. Together, these diverse chemical constituents drive the pharmacological activity observed in traditional herbal medicine.

As discussed in a study, the roots of Astragalus mongholicus contain triterpenoid saponins known as astragalosides. The IC50 values calculated for astragalosides II, III and IV were 5.9 uM, 4.2 uM, and 4.0 uM, respectively. These compounds exhibit binding energies that vary from approximately -55 to -51 kJ/mol. A positive correlation was observed between the experimentally-determined IC50 values and the theoretically-predicted binding energies with a correlation coefficient value equal to 0.956. What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating liver fibrosis, pulmonary fibrosis, renal fibrosis, kidney injury, and immunosuppression. It functions by modulating the body's immune response and reducing excessive inflammation in various organs. The medicinal properties of the plant help mitigate the progressive scarring associated with fibrosis in the liver, lungs, and kidneys. Additionally, its bioactive compounds provide protective effects that can assist in recovering from acute kidney injury. By regulating cellular pathways, it can also address issues related to immunosuppression to restore balance to the immune system.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?
