Eurya chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Eurya chinensis?

Eurya chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Ericales, the plant is placed within the family Pentaphylacaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Eurya and the species chinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Eurya chinensis contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites, primarily including triterpenoids, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and various alkaloids. These chemical constituents are distributed throughout the leaves, stems, and roots of the plant. Research indicates that the triterpene compounds contribute significantly to the plant's demonstrated anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Additionally, the presence of specific flavonoids supports its traditional use in treating various ailments through cellular protection. These combined medicinal compounds offer a complex pharmacological profile that is currently being studied for potential therapeutic applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Eurya chinensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, skin diseases, respiratory ailments, and various types of infections. The leaves of Eurya chinensis contain bioactive compounds that possess significant antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. Traditionally, decoctions made from the plant are applied to manage skin irritations and promote wound healing. Some medicinal practices also utilize the plant to alleviate symptoms associated with coughs and other lung-related issues. These therapeutic applications are primarily rooted in its potential to regulate immune responses and combat pathogens.
