Blumea megacephala

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea megacephala?

Blumea megacephala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling into the genus Blumea, and is specifically identified by its species name, megacephala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea megacephala has a perennial herb structure characterized by large, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a densely tomentose, hairy underside. The plant typically reaches heights of up to two meters and features thick, woody stems that support its expansive foliage. Its inflorescence consists of large, discoid heads that are clustered into dense, terminal capitula. These flower heads are covered in a thick layer of white, woolly hairs that give the plant a distinctive velvety texture. The leaves often possess a strong, aromatic scent when crushed due to their specialized glandular structures.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds caffeic acid, chlorogenic acid, and protocatechuic acid. These specific phenolic acids contribute to the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Research indicates that these bioactive substances help mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. The presence of these compounds makes the plant a subject of interest for developing natural therapeutic agents. Such chemical profiles are essential for understanding its traditional medicinal uses.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", Blumea megacephala contains the medicinal compounds protocatechuic acid, chlorogenic acid, and caffeic acid. The concentration of protocatechuic acid shows a linear relationship in the range of 1.7-17 mg x L(-1). Chloroic acid content is found in the range of 15.6-156 mg x L(-1), while caffeic acid is present in the range of 3.96-39.6 mg x L(-1). The average recoveries for these three components were 103.4%, 102.2%, and 98.5% respectively. This study utilized a detection wavelength set at 258 and 327nm to evaluate these constituents.
