Basil (Ocimum basilicum) Compounds

Basil contains several primary compounds, including essential oil, which gives it its aromatic flavor. Flavonoids and terpenoids contribute to its antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids and tannins provide anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin and glycosides may support digestive health, while saponins and alkaloids have various medicinal benefits.

Basil (Ocimum basilicum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Basil (Ocimum basilicum). It contains volatile aromatic compounds like eugenol and methyl chavicol, which contribute to its medicinal properties. This oil is widely used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and calming effects.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Basil (Ocimum basilicum) contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative stress and support immune function. They are naturally occurring in the plant and are responsible for some of its therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of compounds found in Basil (Ocimum basilicum). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have various pharmacological effects, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory actions. These compounds are responsible for many of Basil's therapeutic uses in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Basil (Ocimum basilicum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound supports overall health by reducing oxidative stress and may aid in inflammatory conditions. Its presence enhances Basil's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Basil (Ocimum basilicum) that contributes to its astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and may support digestive health by tightening tissues. Tannins are also believed to have antimicrobial effects, enhancing Basil's traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Basil (Ocimum basilicum). It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has mild anticoagulant properties. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and is often used in traditional herbal formulations. Its presence enhances Basil's therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Basil (Ocimum basilicum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has mild antimicrobial effects. Glycosides in Basil may support digestive health and help reduce inflammation. They are part of the plant's broader therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Basil (Ocimum basilicum) that contributes to its medicinal properties. It has mild cleansing and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting digestive health and immune function. Saponin also aids in the absorption of nutrients and may help in the removal of toxins from the body.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Basil (Ocimum basilicum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. While not the primary active component, alkaloids may support Basil's traditional use in herbal medicine. Their presence adds to the complex pharmacological profile of the plant.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Basil (Ocimum basilicum) that contributes to its vibrant color. This compound acts as an antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. It supports overall health by reducing oxidative stress and may aid in preventing cellular damage. Anthocyanin also enhances the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in anti-inflammatory and immune-supporting effects.
