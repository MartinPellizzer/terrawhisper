10 Health Benefits Of Valerian

1. Improves sleep quality

Valerian improves sleep quality because of its calming and sedative properties.

The plant contains various compounds, including valerenic acid, that may increase the amount of a neurotransmitter called gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain. GABA helps regulate nerve cells and has a relaxing effect that can promote sleep. Valerian has been shown to help people fall asleep faster and improve sleep quality, making it a popular natural remedy for insomnia and other sleep disorders.

However, it is important to note that valerian can interact with certain medications and may cause side effects, so it is always best to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplement regimen.

2. Reduces sleep latency

Valerian reduces sleep latency because it contains numerous compounds that influence the sleep-regulating system in the brain.

Its primary active constituents are valerenic acid, isovaleric acid, and valepotriates, which have been shown to increase the amount of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain. GABA is a neurotransmitter that promotes relaxation and reduces excitability in the central nervous system, thereby facilitating the onset of sleep. Valerian also has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that may contribute to its sleep-promoting effects.

Clinical studies have demonstrated that valerian extracts can significantly decrease sleep latency, making it a popular natural remedy for insomnia and other sleep disturbances.

3. Alleviates anxiety

Valerian alleviates anxiety because of its active compounds, including valerenic acid and isovaleric acid.

These constituents influence gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) receptors in the brain, promoting GABA binding and inhibitory effects on neurotransmission. This modulation results in reduced neuronal excitability, which is associated with anxiety relief.

Additionally, valerian has been found to decrease the breakdown of GABA, further enhancing its anxiolytic properties. Moreover, it may interact with serotonin and adenosine receptors, contributing to its calming effects.

Valerian's ability to improve sleep quality and reduce sleep latency may also contribute to its anxiety-reducing capabilities.

4. Soothes restlessness

Valerian soothes restlessness because of its unique chemical composition.

This herb contains volatile oils, alkaloids, and a group of compounds called valepotriates, which are believed to have sedative and anxiolytic properties. Valerian has been shown to increase the amount of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain, a neurotransmitter that helps regulate nerve cells and has a calming effect. Additionally, it may affect other neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, contributing to its soothing effects.

Valerian's ability to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, and improve sleep quality makes it a popular natural remedy for individuals experiencing restlessness.

5. Eases nervous tension

Valerian eases nervous tension due to its calming properties on the central nervous system.

This herb contains a variety of compounds, including valerenic acid, which has been found to inhibit the breakdown of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), a neurotransmitter that helps regulate nerve impulses in the brain. By increasing GABA levels, valerian can help promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, and improve sleep quality. Additionally, valerian may also affect other neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, contributing to its anxiolytic effects.

Its soothing qualities have been utilized for centuries to alleviate symptoms of stress and anxiety, making it a popular natural remedy for managing nervous tension.

6. Helps treat depression

Valerian helps treat depression because of its natural sedative and anxiolytic properties.

It contains various compounds, including valerenic acid and isovaleric acid, which may influence the neurotransmitter GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid) in the brain, leading to reduced anxiety and improved mood. Valerian has been found to alleviate symptoms of depression by promoting relaxation, enhancing sleep quality, and regulating the body's stress response.

However, it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before incorporating valerian into a depression treatment plan, as individual responses may vary, and it may interact with other medications.

7. Relieves menstrual cramps

Valerian relieves menstrual cramps because of its natural anti-spasmodic and analgesic properties.

The root of the valerian plant contains active compounds, including valepotriates and volatile oils, that help relax the smooth muscles of the uterus, reducing menstrual cramp pain. Valerian also has a calming effect on the nervous system, which may help alleviate the emotional symptoms associated with menstruation, such as anxiety and irritability. Additionally, valerian has been shown to have anti-inflammatory effects, which can further reduce menstrual pain and discomfort.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before taking valerian or any other herbal supplement, especially if you are taking other medications or have a medical condition.

8. Reduces headache frequency

Valerian reduces headache frequency because of its natural sedative and analgesic properties.

The plant contains various compounds, including valepotriates and volatile oils, that are believed to interact with the neurotransmitters in the brain, helping to regulate nerve impulses and reduce muscle tension. Valerian has been shown to have a calming effect on the central nervous system, which can help alleviate the symptoms of tension headaches and migraines. Furthermore, valerian's anti-inflammatory properties may also contribute to its ability to reduce headache frequency.

By promoting relaxation, reducing muscle tension, and alleviating inflammation, valerian can provide relief for those who suffer from chronic headaches.

9. Lowers blood pressure

Valerian lowers blood pressure due to its natural sedative properties.

It contains a variety of compounds, including valerenic acid, which can have a relaxing effect on the body. This relaxation can lead to a decrease in heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, valerian may increase the production of a chemical called gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain. GABA helps regulate nerve cells and can have a calming effect, further contributing to the reduction of blood pressure.

However, it is important to note that valerian should not be used as a substitute for prescribed medication and should be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

10. Improves stomach discomfort

Valerian improves stomach discomfort because of its natural sedative and antispasmodic properties.

It contains volatile oils, including valerenic acid, which helps to regulate the neurotransmitter GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid) in the brain, reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation. This, in turn, can help to alleviate stress-related stomach discomfort such as indigestion, bloating, and cramps.

Valerian also has antispasmodic effects on the smooth muscles of the gastrointestinal tract, helping to reduce spasms and relieve pain. Furthermore, it has been shown to increase gastric juice production, aiding in digestion and preventing stomach discomfort.

Valerian's soothing properties make it a popular natural remedy for stomach discomfort and other digestive issues.
