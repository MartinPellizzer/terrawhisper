What to know about Illicium verum (star anise) before using it medicinally

Illicium verum, commonly known as star anise, is a versatile herb that has been used for centuries to promote digestive health, reduce inflammation, and boost the immune system.

As a popular garden herb, Illicium verum is relatively easy to cultivate and can thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a great addition to home gardens and botanical collections. Botanically, the herb belongs to the Lauraceae family and is known for its distinctive star-shaped seeds, which give it its name and aroma.

Historically, Illicium verum has been referenced in traditional Chinese medicine and Indian Ayurvedic practices, where it is valued for its medicinal and culinary properties.

This article explains the medicinal, horticultural, botanical, and historical aspects of Illicium verum.

What are the medicinal properties of Illicium verum?

Illicium verum helps with respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and coughs, due to its expectorant properties. It also has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, making it useful for sore throats and skin conditions. Additionally, it has been used to alleviate menstrual cramps and digestive problems.

The active constituents of Illicium verum include volatile oils, particularly anethole, which is responsible for its medicinal properties. These oils are also responsible for its characteristic aroma and flavor. Other constituents include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its pharmacological effects.

The most commonly used parts of the Illicium verum plant for medicinal purposes are its seeds and oil. The seeds are used to make extracts and decoctions, while the oil is used topically for skin conditions and internally for digestive issues. The leaves and bark are also used in some traditional medicine practices.

When used improperly, Illicium verum can cause side effects such as nausea, vomiting, and dizziness. Prolonged use can also lead to anemia, decreased platelet count, and allergic reactions. In some cases, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications.

To use Illicium verum medicinally, it is essential to follow proper guidelines and precautions. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using the plant, as well as individuals with bleeding disorders or allergies. The plant should also be used in moderation, as excessive consumption can lead to adverse effects.

What are the horticulural aspects of Illicium verum?

Illicium verum grow best in well-drained acidic soils with a pH range of 6.0-7.0. It thrives in partial shade to full sun, requiring temperatures between 64°F and 75°F (18°C and 24°C) for optimal growth.

Planting tips for Illicium verum include choosing a location with filtered sunlight and well-draining soil. Dig a hole that is 1-2 times the width and depth of the plant's root ball, adding organic matter such as compost or manure to improve soil fertility.

Harvesting star anise involves pinching off individual flowers as they appear, or cutting back the entire plant to promote new growth. The seeds can be collected in the fall, but it's essential to allow the pods to dry completely before harvesting to avoid moisture retention.

Illicium verum is susceptible to pests such as aphids, spider mites, and scale insects, which can be managed through integrated pest management techniques. Fungal diseases like root rot and leaf spot can be caused by overwatering or poor air circulation, and are typically treated with fungicides or by improving growing conditions.

Botanically, it exhibits several distinctive characteristics. The aromatic flowers are small, white, and clustered on a peduncle, producing a star-shaped inflorescence that resembles a star anise pod. The plant's growth habit is erect, reaching a height of up to 5 meters, with a spread of around 3-4 meters.

Illicium verum is native to southern China and Southeast Asia, where it thrives in warm, humid climates with rich soil and partial shade.

What is the taxonomy of Illicium verum?

Illicium verum belongs to the family Illiciaceae, which is a group of flowering plants commonly known as star anise or Chinese star anise.

Within this family, I. verum is classified in the genus Illicium, which consists of about 40 species of evergreen shrubs and small trees native to eastern Asia.

The specific epithet "verum" means "true" or "genuine", referring to the plant's authenticity as a culinary spice.
