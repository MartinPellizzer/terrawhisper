Star anise

Illicium verum

Star Anise (Illicium verum) is a spice and medicinal plant native to southern China, known for its distinct licorice-like flavor and its use in traditional medicine to support respiratory and digestive health.

Botanical Identification

Star Anise, scientifically known as Illicium verum, belongs to the family Schisandraceae. It is also called Chinese star anise and is a evergreen shrub native to southern China. The plant has pinnate leaves, yellow flowers, and distinctive star-shaped fruits. It is sometimes referred to as Chinese star anise or Japanese star anise in herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Schisandraceae
  • Family: Schisandraceae
  • Genus: Illicium
  • Species: Illicium verum

Active Compounds

Star Anise contains essential oils, flavonoids, and terpenoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to exert anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Flavonoids and terpenoids may synergistically enhance the herb's therapeutic properties.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Terpenoid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and digestive issues.

Decoction: Used for digestive ailments and respiratory infections.

Essential oil: Used for respiratory support and as a natural insect repellent.

Safety Profile

Star Anise is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and by individuals with known allergies to the plant. High doses may cause gastrointestinal upset, and improper use can lead to toxicity.
