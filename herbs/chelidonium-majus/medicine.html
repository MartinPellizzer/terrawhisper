What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Chelidonium Majus (Celandine)?

Chelidonium majus, also known as celandine, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and promoting wound healing.

The medicinal constituents of this herb include chelidonic acid, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. Chelidonium majus can be prepared as a tincture, tea, or ointment, and is traditionally used to treat various conditions, including eczema and arthritis. However, it may cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals.

Precautions should be taken when using Chelidonium majus, especially in cases of pregnancy or breastfeeding.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Chelidonium majus.

What are the health benefits of Chelidonium majus?

Chelidonium majus, also known as celandine, has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

It has been traditionally used to treat various conditions including digestive issues, arthritis, and skin problems. The plant's root and leaves contain berberine, which has been shown to have antimicrobial and antiviral effects, helping to combat infections and promote wound healing.

This may also contribute to its anti-cancer properties.

What are the active constituents of Chelidonium majus?

Chelidonium majus, also known as celandine, has active constituents such as berberine, palmatine, and chelidonine, which have shown antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and pain-relieving properties.

Chelidonium contains a range of flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, which are also found in other medicinal herbs. These compounds are thought to contribute to its traditional uses, including skin conditions, digestive problems, and as a diuretic.

Further research is needed to fully understand its medicinal properties.

What are the medicinal preparations of Chelidonium majus?

Chelidonium majus, also known as celandine, has medicinal preparations such as tinctures, ointments, and infusions.

The root and rhizome are the primary sources of active compounds, including chelidonic acid and berberine. Tinctures and infusions are often used to treat digestive issues, fever, and skin conditions, while ointments are applied topically for inflammatory and rheumatic conditions.

Preparations can be purchased over-the-counter or obtained through herbal medicine practitioners.

What are the possible side effect of using Chelidonium majus improperly?

Improper use of Chelidonium majus, also known as celandine, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

It can also cause allergic reactions, including skin irritation and hives, especially when applied topically. Furthermore, excessive consumption of celandine can lead to headaches, dizziness, and fatigue.

Pregnant and breastfeeding women should exercise caution, as there is limited research on its safety for these groups.

What precautions to take when using Chelidonium majus medicinally?

Before using Chelidonium majus, also known as celandine, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a qualified healthcare practitioner, as it can interact with certain medications and exacerbate health conditions like kidney or liver disease.

Additionally, celandine's toxicity increases with prolonged or excessive use, so careful dosing and monitoring are essential.

Pregnant or breastfeeding women should also exercise caution.
