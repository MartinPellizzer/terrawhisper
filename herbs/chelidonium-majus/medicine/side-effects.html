10 Possible Side Effects Of Chelidonium Majus

Chelidonium majus has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal disturbances, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

These side effects are typically caused by the herb's bitter compounds, which can irritate the stomach lining. For those with sensitive stomachs, these symptoms can worsen, leading to malnutrition and dehydration.

In extreme cases, prolonged exposure to these side effects can result in chronic digestive issues, further affecting daily life and overall well-being.

1. Causes liver damage

Chelidonium majus causes liver damage due to its high content of alkaloids, particularly chelerythrine and sanguinarine.

These compounds have been shown to be toxic to the liver cells, leading to inflammation and potentially irreversible damage.

Long-term use or excessive intake of Chelidonium majus has been linked to increased risk of liver injury, including hepatitis, fibrosis, and even cirrhosis.

2. Produces vomiting

Chelidonium majus produces vomiting.

This side effect is likely due to the alkaloids present in the plant, particularly chelerythrine and sanguinarine, which are known to have emetic properties. When ingested, these compounds can stimulate the vomit center in the brain, leading to nausea and vomiting.

The intense bitter taste of the herb may also contribute to this side effect, as it can irritate the stomach lining and trigger a gag reflex.

3. Triggers allergic reactions

Chelidonium majus triggers allergic reactions due to its high content of alkaloids, specifically protoanemonin and chelerythrine.

These compounds can stimulate the immune system, leading to an overactive response in susceptible individuals. This may result in symptoms such as hives, itching, swelling, and even anaphylaxis in severe cases.

The allergenic properties of Chelidonium majus are thought to be responsible for these adverse reactions.

4. Increases blood pressure

Chelidonium majus increases blood pressure due to its stimulatory effect on the heart and blood vessels.

The alkaloids present in this plant, such as chelerythrine and sanguinarine, can cause vasoconstriction, leading to increased blood pressure. Additionally, Chelidonium majus may also increase adrenaline production, further contributing to elevated blood pressure levels.

This cardiovascular stimulation can be a significant side effect for individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions.

5. Induces hallucinations

Chelidonium majus induces hallucinations due to its ability to affect the nervous system.

The active compound, chelerythrine, has been shown to interact with neurotransmitters such as serotonin and dopamine, leading to altered states of consciousness and distorted perceptions.

This can result in visual and auditory hallucinations, as well as changes in mood and cognitive function.

6. Affects vision poorly

Chelidonium majus affects vision poorly due to its ability to alter the sensitivity of the eye's retina.

The bioactive compounds present in the herb, such as chelerythrine and berberine, can bind to opioid receptors in the visual pathway, disrupting normal transmission of signals from the eye to the brain.

This disruption can result in blurred vision, double vision, or even temporary loss of vision, making it essential for individuals using Chelidonium majus to monitor their visual acuity closely.

7. Worsens asthma attacks

Chelidonium majus worsens asthma attacks due to its expectorant properties, which can stimulate the lungs and increase mucus production.

This may exacerbate existing respiratory conditions such as asthma, leading to severe coughing fits, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, Chelidonium majus contains alkaloids that can cause bronchial constriction, further irritating the airways and worsening symptoms in individuals with asthma.

8. Increases urine production

Chelidonium majus increases urine production due to its diuretic properties.

The plant's alkaloid content, particularly chelerythrine and sanguinarine, stimulates the kidneys to excrete more water, resulting in an increased volume of urine produced. This diuretic effect is a common response to the ingestion of Chelidonium majus, as it allows the body to eliminate excess fluids and toxins.

As a result, individuals using Chelidonium majus may experience a noticeable increase in their urine output.

9. Causes stomach cramps

Chelidonium majus causes stomach cramps due to its bitter and potentially irritating properties.

The compound chelerythrine, present in the plant, can stimulate the digestive system, leading to increased peristalsis and spasms, which may result in cramping sensations.

Additionally, the alkaloids in Chelidonium majus can also irritate the mucous membranes of the stomach and intestines, further contributing to stomach discomfort.

10. Triggers anxiety attacks

Chelidonium majus triggers anxiety attacks due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system and increase the production of adrenaline.

The herb's alkaloids, particularly chelerythrine, can cause a rapid heart rate, palpitations, and feelings of restlessness and jitteriness, which may lead to episodes of panic or anxiety.

Additionally, Chelidonium majus has been known to interact with certain medications, including antidepressants, and may exacerbate pre-existing anxiety conditions.
