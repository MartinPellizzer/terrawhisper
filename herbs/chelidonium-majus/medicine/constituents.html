10 Medicinal Constituents Of Chelidonium Majus (Celandine)

Chelidonium majus has active constituents such as berberine, protopine, and chelidonine.

These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and expectorant properties. They help alleviate symptoms of digestive issues, such as irritable bowel syndrome, and respiratory conditions like bronchitis and asthma.

By reducing inflammation and promoting healthy gut function, these constituents can improve the quality of life for individuals with digestive issues, allowing them to lead more active and productive lives.

1. Berberine

Chelidonium majus berberine is a naturally occurring compound derived from the root of the plant.

Berberine has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including infections, wounds, and digestive issues.

It exhibits potent antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties, making it effective against bacteria, viruses, and parasites.

2. Palmatine

Chelidonium majus palmatine is a bioactive alkaloid extracted from the leaves of the Greater Celandine plant (Chelidonium majus).

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Palmatine has also been shown to exhibit antioxidant, antimicrobial, and antiviral activities, making it a promising compound for various therapeutic applications.

Its potential benefits include reducing pain and inflammation, as well as treating respiratory and cardiovascular disorders.

3. Sanguinarine

Chelidonium majus sanguinarine is a bioactive compound derived from the plant's rhizomes and roots.

It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and skin conditions. Sanguinarine exhibits anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it a potential natural remedy for wound healing, pain relief, and antiseptic applications.

Its unique chemical structure allows it to bind to specific proteins, making it an attractive candidate for further research in pharmaceutical development.

4. Chelerythrine

Chelidonium majus chelerythrine is a bioactive compound extracted from the roots of the plant.

This yellow alkaloid has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions.

Chelerythrine has also been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties, making it a promising natural remedy for modern healthcare.

5. Cryptopalmatine

Chelidonium majus cryptopalmatine is a bioactive alkaloid extracted from the plant's dried rhizomes and seeds.

It has been traditionally used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. Cryptopalmatine exhibits potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a potential candidate for treating diseases like tuberculosis and cancer.

Additionally, its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation may also contribute to its use in cardiovascular disease prevention.

6. Berbamine

Chelidonium majus berbamine is a bioactive alkaloid extracted from the leaves of the Greater Celandine plant (Chelidonium majus).

Berbamine has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine for centuries to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin diseases.

Modern research has confirmed its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties, making it a promising natural compound with potential therapeutic applications.

7. Coptisine

Chelidonium majus coptisine is a bioactive alkaloid compound extracted from the dried rhizome and stem of the Celandine poppy plant (Chelidonium majus).

It has been traditionally used in medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antibacterial properties.

Research has shown that coptisine exhibits potential therapeutic effects, including improving cognitive function, reducing anxiety and depression, and possessing anticancer activities.

8. Epiberberine

Chelidonium majus epiberberine is a bioactive alkaloid compound extracted from the plant's leaves and stems.

It has been found to possess various pharmacological properties, including antiviral, antibacterial, and anti-inflammatory activities. Epiberberine has also shown potential in treating cardiovascular diseases by inhibiting the uptake of excess potassium into cardiac cells, which may help regulate heart rhythm.

Further research is needed to fully understand its effects on human health.

9. Isoboldine

Chelidonium majus isoboldine is a chemical compound isolated from the leaves and stems of the plant.

It is a minor alkaloid found in small amounts alongside other active compounds such as chelerythrine and sanguinarine.

Isoboldine has been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties, making it a potential candidate for the treatment of various diseases, including viral infections and inflammatory disorders.

10. Columbamine

Chelidonium majus columbamine is a bioactive alkaloid compound isolated from the plant.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health issues, such as liver and gallbladder disorders.

Columbamine has been reported to possess anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties, making it a potential candidate for the development of novel therapeutic agents against infectious diseases and inflammatory conditions.
