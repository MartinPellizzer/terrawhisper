Cynanchum atratum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cynanchum atratum?

Cynanchum atratum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it is placed in the order Gentianaales and further nested within the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Cynanchum, and its unique species designation, atratum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cynanchum atratum contains a diverse profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including indole alkaloids, triterpenoids, steroids, and various glycosides. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's observed pharmacological activities in traditional medicine. Researchers have identified that certain indole alkaloids within the species exhibit significant anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Additionally, the presence of triterpenoids suggests potential applications in modulating immune responses and protecting cells from oxidative stress. Ongoing studies continue to investigate how these complex molecular structures can be utilized for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cynanchum atratum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including stomach aches, respiratory issues, and skin infections. In traditional medicinal practices, its extracts are applied to soothe intestinal discomfort and digestive distress. It is also utilized in certain cultures to address coughs and other breathing difficulties. Some preparations of the plant serve as topical treatments for localized skin irritations or wounds. Research continues into how its specific chemical compounds might influence these therapeutic outcomes.
