Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum) Compounds

Tulsi contains essential oil, which includes compounds like eugenol and linalool. Flavonoids such as apigenin and luteolin have antioxidant properties. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides, saponins, and alkaloids also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum). It contains aromatic compounds like eugenol and linalool, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. The oil is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and adaptogenic effects. It plays a significant role in the holistic benefits of Tulsi.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-modulating properties. These compounds support Tulsi's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive disorders. Their presence enhances the overall therapeutic value of the herb.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum) and contributes to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps in reducing swelling and strengthening tissues, making Tulsi effective for treating respiratory and digestive disorders. Tannins also support immune function and are commonly used in traditional herbal remedies.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of active compounds in Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are responsible for many of the medicinal benefits attributed to Tulsi in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in promoting respiratory health and reducing stress. This compound helps in neutralizing free radicals and modulating immune responses within the body.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum). It contributes to the plant's adaptogenic and anti-stress properties by modulating the body's stress response. Glycosides in Tulsi also support respiratory and immune health, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum). It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to Tulsi's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Coumarin also supports cardiovascular health by preventing blood clot formation. Its presence enhances the plant's therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum) that contributes to its therapeutic properties. It exhibits antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and immune-modulating effects, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating respiratory and digestive disorders. Saponin's ability to enhance the absorption of other bioactive compounds further supports Tulsi's efficacy in traditional formulations.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic effects, particularly in reducing stress and inflammation. Alkaloids in Tulsi are known for their adaptogenic properties, supporting the body's ability to cope with stress. They are among the key bioactive components responsible for the plant's medicinal value.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Tulsi (Ocimum sanctum) that contributes to its medicinal properties. It acts as an antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound supports immune function and may reduce inflammation, making it a key component in Tulsi's traditional use for health promotion.
