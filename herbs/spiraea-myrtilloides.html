Spiraea myrtilloides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spiraea myrtilloides has arching, multi-stemmed deciduous shrubs characterized by serrated, ovate leaves that measure approximately one inch in length and a dense cluster of small, five-petaled white flowers. The foliage typically displays a bright green hue during the growing season and often transitions to reddish tones in the autumn. These plants produce flat-topped corymbs of blossoms that emerge in late spring or early summer to create a profuse floral display. The stems are slender and woody at the base, supporting a spreading growth habit that can reach three feet in height and width. This morphology makes the plant well-suited for dense groundcover or hedge formation in garden landscapes.
