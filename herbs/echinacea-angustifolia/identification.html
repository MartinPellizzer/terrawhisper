Botanical Identification of Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia)

To identify Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Purple Coneflower, or Echinacea angustifolia, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Compositae, and it is part of the genus Echinacea. The species name angustifolia refers to its narrow leaves. The other classifications—division, class, and order—are not provided in the data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Purple Coneflower is Echinacea angustifolia, first described by the botanist Lindley in 1833. It is also known by several synonyms, including Echinacea purpurea var. angustifolia and Echinacea tennessensis. Common names include Purple Coneflower, Narrowleaf Coneflower, and Texas Coneflower. Regional and historical names reflect its use in traditional medicine and its native range across North America.

Morphological Characteristics

Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) is an herbaceous perennial with a taproot system. It has upright, branching stems that grow 2-3 feet tall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have coarse teeth. The plant produces a single, spiky inflorescence topped with purple petals and a central cone-like structure. Flowers are tubular with yellow centers and purple petals. The fruit is a brown, dry capsule containing small, oval seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) can be distinguished by its narrow, grass-like leaves and conical, purple flower heads. It has bristly stems and yellow-orange disc flowers. It differs from Echinacea purpurea by its narrower leaves and smaller, more conical flower heads. It is also distinct from other coneflowers by its specific leaf shape and stem structure.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) is native to the central and eastern United States. It thrives in temperate climates with full sun. It prefers well-drained soils, such as sandy or loamy types. It typically grows at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Purple Coneflower blooms from late June to August, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early fall. For identification purposes, harvest timing is typically in late summer to early fall when the seeds are mature and ready for collection.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Echinacea angustifolia belongs to the Asteraceae family, closely related to species like Echinacea purpurea and Echinacea pallida. It shares genus traits such as composite flower heads and medicinal properties. Its phylogenetic position reflects evolutionary ties to other coneflowers and sunflowers, influencing its botanical and therapeutic characteristics.
