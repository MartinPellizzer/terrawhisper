Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) Compounds

The primary compounds in Echinacea angustifolia include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides and terpenoids contribute to immune support, while saponins and tannins may enhance absorption and have antimicrobial properties. Coumarin and alkaloids add to the plant's complex pharmacological profile. Essential oils and anthocyanins provide additional antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits.

Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help modulate the immune system and may support the body's response to infections. They are found in the roots and flowers of the plant. Flavonoids are a key group of phytochemicals in Echinacea.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in boosting immune response and treating respiratory infections.

3. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's immune-modulating properties by supporting the body's natural defenses. Glycosides in Echinacea are believed to stimulate white blood cell activity and enhance respiratory health. These compounds are primarily found in the roots and flowers of the plant.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Echinacea angustifolia, including alkamides and glycosides. These compounds contribute to the plant's immune-modulating properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for boosting the immune system.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's immune-boosting properties by enhancing white blood cell activity and modulating inflammatory responses. Saponins also exhibit antimicrobial effects, supporting Echinacea's traditional use in treating colds and infections.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, supporting immune function. Tannins also help in reducing irritation and promoting wound healing in traditional herbal applications.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Coumarin may support immune function and has been studied for its potential role in reducing symptoms of respiratory infections.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia). While alkaloids are more commonly associated with other medicinal plants, Echinacea contains minimal amounts of alkaloids, which may contribute to its immune-supporting properties. Their role in the plant's medicinal effects is not as well understood as other constituents like polysaccharides.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia) contains volatile compounds like sesquiterpenes and aromatic hydrocarbons. It is traditionally used to support immune function and may have antimicrobial properties. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation from the plant's flowers and roots.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Purple Coneflower (Echinacea angustifolia). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties that may support immune function and reduce inflammation.
