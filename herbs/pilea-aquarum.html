Pilea aquarum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea aquarum?

Pilea aquarum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Rosales. It is a member of the family Urticaceae, which contains the genus Pilea, and is specifically identified by its species name, aquarum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea aquarum has fleshy, bright green leaves that are roughly circular or kidney-shaped with distinctively crenate or scalloped margins. The leaf blades possess a slightly textured surface and are arranged in an opposite pattern along a succulent, upright stem. These leaves are supported by short petioles that emerge from the central growth point of the plant. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically bloom from the leaf axils during the growing season. The overall structure of the plant remains compact and herbaceous, making it well-suited for moist environments.
