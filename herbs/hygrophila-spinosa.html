Side Effects of hygrophila spinosa

Hygrophila spinosa triggers respiratory issues in individuals who are sensitive to its pollen or airborne particles, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

It can also trigger allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and in severe cases, anaphylaxis, particularly in those with existing allergies. The herb may cause skin irritation when handled, resulting in redness, rashes, or blisters due to its chemical composition.

Exposure to Hygrophila spinosa can lead to nausea, especially when ingested or inhaled in large quantities, as it contains compounds that may irritate the gastrointestinal tract. Additionally, it can cause eye irritation and nasal congestion, with symptoms like redness, tearing, and a runny nose, making it a concern for both indoor and outdoor environments.

These health side effects highlight the importance of caution when handling or using this herb, particularly for individuals with pre-existing conditions or allergies.
