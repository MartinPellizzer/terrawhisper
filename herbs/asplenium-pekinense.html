What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium pekinense?

Asplenium pekinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Polypodiales. This fern is further categorized under the family Aspleniaceae and the genus Asplenium, ultimately reaching its specific taxonomic designation at the species level as pekinense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium pekinense has pinnate fronds that reach 20 to 50 centimeters in length, featuring a dark brown or blackish stipe and bright green pinnae. The individual leaflets are oblong to lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. These leaflets are arranged oppositely along the central rachis, which is typically smooth and somewhat fleshy. Small, inconspicuous sori are located on the underside of the blades, protected by thin, kidney-shaped indusia. The overall growth habit is characterized by a compact tuft of foliage emerging from a short rhizome.
