10 Possible Side Effects Of Rhamnus Alaternus

Rhamnus alaternus, also known as Italian buckthorn, has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, skin irritation, and allergic reactions.

These issues are often caused by excessive consumption, allergic sensitivity, or contaminated extracts. For instance, gastrointestinal problems may lead to digestive discomfort, nausea, or even vomiting, severely impacting daily life and productivity.

In severe cases, allergic reactions may cause anaphylaxis, which can be life-threatening.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Rhamnus alaternus, also known as Italian buckthorn, causes allergic reactions, which can be attributed to its unique composition of flavonoids and saponins.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in sensitive individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, itching, and respiratory issues.

The sap of the plant also contains a chemical called urushiol, a known allergen responsible for causing reactions.

2. Triggers respiratory distress

Rhamnus alaternus, also known as Italian buckthorn, triggers respiratory distress due to the presence of saponins and flavonoids in its extracts.

These compounds can cause irritation to the respiratory system, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

The saponins, in particular, can induce an allergic response, causing the airways to constrict and exacerbating respiratory conditions.

3. Induces gastrointestinal issues

Rhamnus alaternus, also known as Italian buckthorn, induces gastrointestinal issues in some individuals due to its anthraquinone content, specifically emodin.

This compound can cause irritation and inflammation in the digestive tract, leading to symptoms such as abdominal cramps, diarrhea, and nausea.

The anthraquinones in Rhamnus alaternus can also alter gut bacteria, further contributing to gastrointestinal problems.

4. Promotes cardiovascular problems

Rhamnus alaternus, also known as Italian buckthorn, promotes cardiovascular problems.

The plant's high content of tannins, flavonoids, and other polyphenolic compounds can interact with cardiovascular medications and increase blood pressure. Additionally, the presence of oleuropein, a potent vasodilator, may also exacerbate existing cardiovascular conditions.

The combination of these bioactive compounds can lead to cardiovascular issues such as arrhythmias, atherosclerosis, and cardiac hypertrophy.

5. Increases blood pressure

Rhamnus alaternus, also known as Italian buckthorn, increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of alkaloids, such as rutin and quercetin.

These compounds can stimulate the heart and blood vessels, causing a rise in blood pressure.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids may also contribute to this effect by constricting blood vessels and increasing cardiac output, further exacerbating blood pressure issues.

6. Affects liver function tests

Rhamnus alaternus, also known as Italian buckthorn, affects liver function tests due to its potential to increase the levels of certain liver enzymes.

This is thought to be caused by the presence of anthraquinones, a class of compounds found in the plant, which can have a toxic effect on the liver.

The exact mechanisms by which Rhamnus alaternus exerts this effect are not fully understood, but it is believed to involve the inhibition of certain enzymes involved in liver function.

7. Reduces kidney function

Rhamnus alaternus, also known as Italian buckthorn, reduces kidney function due to its high content of anthraquinones, particularly emodin.

This compound has been shown to have a direct toxic effect on renal cells, leading to decreased glomerular filtration rate and increased levels of serum creatinine.

The flavonoids present in the plant, such as quercetin, may also contribute to its nephrotoxic effects.

8. Interacts with medications

Rhamnus alaternus, also known as Italian buckthorn, interacts with medications in a way that can lead to increased levels of certain drugs in the body.

This is due to its ability to inhibit the activity of enzymes responsible for breaking down these medications, such as the cytochrome P450 system.

As a result, individuals taking Rhamnus alaternus may experience elevated levels of medications, potentially causing adverse effects or toxicity.

9. Produces skin irritation

Rhamnus alaternus, also known as Italian buckthorn, produces skin irritation due to its sap, which contains alkaloids that can cause inflammation and irritation upon contact with the skin.

The sap's acidity and presence of glycosides, such as rhamnocitrin, contribute to its irritating properties.

Prolonged exposure to the plant's sap can lead to redness, itching, and burning sensations.

10. Elicits neurotoxic symptoms

Rhamnus alaternus, also known as Italian buckthorn, elicits neurotoxic symptoms in some individuals due to its toxic compounds, including alkaloids and glycosides.

These chemicals can interfere with the normal functioning of the nervous system, causing symptoms such as tremors, muscle weakness, and cognitive impairment.

The toxic properties of Rhamnus alaternus are thought to be a defense mechanism to deter herbivores, but for humans, these compounds can have unintended consequences.
