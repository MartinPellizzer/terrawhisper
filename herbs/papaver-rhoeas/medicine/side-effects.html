1. Causes allergic reactions

Papaver rhoeas causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its chemical composition.

The plant contains alkaloids such as papaverine and morphine, which are responsible for triggering an immune response in sensitive people. These chemicals can bind to IgE antibodies, leading to the release of histamine and other inflammatory mediators that cause symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

As a result, some individuals may experience an allergic reaction when exposed to Papaver rhoeas.

Papaver rhoeas triggers respiratory issues due to its containing alkaloids such as morphine and codeine.
