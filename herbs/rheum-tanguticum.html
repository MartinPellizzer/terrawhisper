Rheum Tanguticum

Rheum tanguticum, commonly known as Tibetan rhubarb or Tangut rhubarb, is a type of medicinal herb belonging to the Rhubarb genus.

The health benefits of Rheum tanguticum include its ability to aid in digestion, reduce inflammation, and act as a natural laxative.

Therapeutically, Rheum tanguticum is known for its anthraquinone and tannin content, which exert purgative, anti-inflammatory, and astringent actions.

The bioactive constituents of Rheum tanguticum include anthraquinones, such as rhein and emodin, as well as tannins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.
