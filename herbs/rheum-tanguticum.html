Chinese rhubarb

Rheum tanguticum

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Chinese Rhubarb, Rheum tanguticum, is a perennial plant in the Polygonaceae family. Native to the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent regions, it is known by various regional names such as Dangshen and Tangut rhubarb. The plant features large, broad leaves and a thick, medicinal root used in traditional herbal medicine. Synonyms include Rheum palmatum and Rheum officinale in historical texts.

Active Compounds

Chinese Rhubarb contains key active compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties by influencing gut health and inflammation. They work synergistically to enhance therapeutic effects in herbal formulations.

  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action

Integumentary Skin inflammation, skin eruptions, skin infections, acne Anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial
Respiratory Respiratory infection, respiratory infections Antimicrobial

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and constipation due to its laxative properties.

Powder: Used for constipation and as a general tonic.

Extract: Used for inflammatory conditions and as a laxative.

Safety Profile

Chinese Rhubarb (Rheum tanguticum) is generally safe when used in recommended doses but may cause gastrointestinal irritation. It is contraindicated in cases of ulcers, hemorrhoids, or during pregnancy. Long-term use should be avoided due to potential toxicity.
