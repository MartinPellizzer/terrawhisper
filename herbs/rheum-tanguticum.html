Chinese rhubarb

Rheum tanguticum

Chinese Rhubarb (Rheum tanguticum) is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau and is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its strong laxative properties and ability to treat digestive disorders.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Rhubarb, Rheum tanguticum, is a perennial plant in the Polygonaceae family. Native to the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent regions, it is known by various regional names such as Dangshen and Tangut rhubarb. The plant features large, broad leaves and a thick, medicinal root used in traditional herbal medicine. Synonyms include Rheum palmatum and Rheum officinale in historical texts.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and constipation due to its laxative properties.

Powder: Used for constipation and as a general tonic.

Extract: Used for inflammatory conditions and as a laxative.

Safety Profile

Chinese Rhubarb (Rheum tanguticum) is generally safe when used in recommended doses but may cause gastrointestinal irritation. It is contraindicated in cases of ulcers, hemorrhoids, or during pregnancy. Long-term use should be avoided due to potential toxicity.
