10 Health Benefits Of Malvaviscus Arboreus (Turkey Tallow)

Malvaviscus arboreus, also known as turkey tallow, has health benefits such as treating fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions due to its antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties.

Its leaves and roots have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The plant's benefits can improve people's lives by reducing inflammation, relieving pain, and promoting wound healing, which can enhance overall quality of life and even help manage chronic diseases.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Malvaviscus arboreus.

1. Treats anxiety

Malvaviscus arboreus treats anxiety because of its rich antioxidant and flavonoid content.

The plant's extracts have been shown to modulate the body's stress response by increasing the production of neurotransmitters like GABA and serotonin, which regulate mood and emotional state. Additionally, turkey tallow's anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce cortisol levels, leading to a sense of calm and relaxation.

These mechanisms combined may contribute to the plant's anxiolytic effects, making it a promising natural remedy for anxiety management.

2. Relieves depression

Malvaviscus arboreus relieves depression because it contains a unique combination of alkaloids, flavonoids, and saponins.

These compounds work synergistically to modulate the body's neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, which are often imbalanced in individuals experiencing depression.

By normalizing these neurotransmitters, turkey tallow helps to alleviate symptoms of depression, including mood swings, anxiety, and insomnia.

3. Soothes insomnia

Malvaviscus arboreus, also known as Mexican buckeye, soothes insomnia because its roots and stems contain flavonoids and alkaloids that have a calming effect on the nervous system.

These compounds may help regulate sleep patterns and promote a sense of relaxation, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep.

The plant's natural sedative properties have been used in traditional medicine to treat various sleep disorders.

4. Reduces inflammation

Malvaviscus arboreus reduces inflammation because it contains a unique combination of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

These compounds possess potent anti-inflammatory properties, which have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and enzymes, thereby reducing the inflammatory response.

Additionally, turkey tallow has been found to possess antioxidant properties, which help to neutralize free radicals and further contribute to its anti-inflammatory effects.

5. Lowers cholesterol

Malvaviscus arboreus lowers cholesterol because of its unique combination of flavonoids, saponins, and alkaloids.

The polyphenolic compounds in the plant have been shown to inhibit the production of low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol, also known as bad cholesterol, while increasing the levels of high-density lipoprotein (HDL) cholesterol, or good cholesterol.

This reduction in LDL and increase in HDL helps to maintain healthy blood lipid profiles, reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease.

6. Combats cancer

Malvaviscus arboreus combats cancer because it possesses unique bioactive compounds that exhibit potent anti-cancer properties.

The plant contains a rich source of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and glycosides that have been shown to inhibit the growth of cancer cells, induce apoptosis, and reduce angiogenesis.

Additionally, its extracts have been found to exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, which can help protect against DNA damage and oxidative stress, ultimately contributing to the prevention and treatment of various types of cancer.

7. Protects liver

Malvaviscus arboreus protects liver because of its rich antioxidant properties.

The plant's extract has been shown to scavenge free radicals, thus preventing oxidative stress and liver damage. Additionally, the flavonoids present in turkey tallow have been found to inhibit the activation of pro-inflammatory enzymes, reducing the risk of liver disease and damage.

As a result, consumption of Malvaviscus arboreus has been associated with improved liver health and a reduced risk of liver-related complications.

8. Enhances digestion

Malvaviscus arboreus enhances digestion because of its unique composition of fatty acids and glycosides.

The fatty acids in turkey tallow help to stimulate the digestive process, increasing the production of digestive enzymes and bile salts. This, in turn, helps to break down fats and proteins more efficiently, making it easier for the body to absorb the nutrients it needs.

Additionally, the glycosides in turkey tallow have anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe the digestive tract and reduce the risk of digestive disorders.

9. Relaxes muscles

Malvaviscus arboreus relaxes muscles because it contains a unique combination of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds have been shown to possess potent vasodilatory and muscle-relaxing properties, which help to reduce muscle spasms and tension.

By increasing blood flow and relaxing muscle fibers, turkey tallow may provide relief from muscle cramps, spasms, and inflammation, making it a valuable natural remedy for muscle-related disorders.

10. Reduces stress

Malvaviscus arboreus reduces stress because its unique compounds have a profound impact on the body's response to anxiety.

The plant's flavonoids and alkaloids have been shown to modulate the activity of neurotransmitters like GABA, serotonin, and dopamine, which are critical for regulating emotional arousal.

By promoting relaxation and calming the nervous system, turkey tallow can help alleviate symptoms of stress and anxiety, leading to a greater sense of well-being and overall calm.
