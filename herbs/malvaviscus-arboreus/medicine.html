What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Malvaviscus Arboreus (Turkey Tallow)?

Malvaviscus arboreus, also known as turkey tallow, has health benefits such as treating fever, skin conditions, and respiratory issues.

Its medicinal constituents include flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Various preparations of the herb are used to treat these conditions, including teas, infusions, and poultices. However, possible side effects include allergic reactions and interactions with certain medications.

Precautions should be taken to avoid overuse, especially in children, pregnant women, and individuals with sensitive skin.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Malvaviscus arboreus.

What are the active constituents of Malvaviscus arboreus?

The health benefits of Malvaviscus arboreus comes from its active constituents, such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and alkaloids.

These compounds have been shown to possess antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, making them useful in treating various health conditions. Flavonoids like quercetin and kaempferol are known for their ability to reduce inflammation and improve cardiovascular health, while phenolic acids have been linked to antimicrobial and antioxidant activities.

What are the medicinal preparations of Malvaviscus arboreus?

Malvaviscus arboreus has different medicinal preparations, such as infusions, decoctions, and ointments.

The infusion is used to treat digestive issues like constipation and diarrhea, while the decoction is employed to relieve respiratory problems like bronchitis and asthma.

The ointment, rich in antioxidants and fatty acids, is applied topically to soothe skin irritations, wounds, and burns.

What are the possible side effect of using Malvaviscus arboreus improperly?

Improper use of Malvaviscus arboreus increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and gastrointestinal issues like diarrhea, nausea, and stomach cramps.

Prolonged use may lead to headaches, fatigue, and dizziness. In some cases, it can cause blood pressure fluctuations, irregular heartbeat, and even respiratory problems.

Additionally, excessive consumption has been linked to kidney and liver damage, as well as an increased risk of cancer due to the presence of impurities and contaminants.
