Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) Compounds

The primary active compounds in Mexican Mallow include tannins, which have astringent properties, and flavonoids, known for their antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids contribute to anti-inflammatory actions, while glycosides and saponins support digestive health. Coumarin and terpenoids offer antimicrobial benefits, and essential oils provide aromatic and therapeutic properties.

Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin in Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in reducing inflammation and soothing irritated tissues. It is commonly used in traditional herbal remedies for digestive issues and skin conditions. Tannins also help in preserving the plant's medicinal properties during preparation.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. They help reduce oxidative stress and modulate immune responses, enhancing the plant's therapeutic potential.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce oxidative stress and supports the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. It is also believed to enhance the plant's overall therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating skin irritations and respiratory conditions. Its presence supports the plant's traditional use in natural remedies.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties, making it useful in treating sore throats and digestive discomfort. Saponins also enhance the plant's overall therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating conditions like muscle spasms and digestive issues. Its presence also supports the plant's traditional use in soothing respiratory and gastrointestinal discomfort.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's use in traditional remedies for respiratory and skin conditions. They are responsible for some of the plant's therapeutic actions in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) contains volatile compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine to support respiratory and skin health. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and flowers.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid in Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) refers to a class of nitrogen-containing compounds. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, though research on their specific effects and mechanisms is limited. They are part of the broader biochemical profile that supports the traditional use of this plant in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are pigments found in Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) that contribute to its vibrant colors. These compounds have antioxidant properties and may support cardiovascular health. They are commonly found in the flowers and leaves of the plant. Anthocyanins are also being studied for their potential anti-inflammatory effects.
