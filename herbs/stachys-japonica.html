Stachys japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Stachys japonica?

Stachys japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is a member of the order Lamiales. This plant is further categorized within the family Lamiaceae, specifically belonging to the genus Stachys, with the specific epithet japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stachys japonica has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal rosettes of ovate, serrated leaves and upright, square-shaped flowering stalks. The leaves are typically soft and hairy, featuring a texture that feels slightly velvety to the touch. As the plant matures, it produces inflorescences consisting of dense, compact clusters of small, tubular flowers. These blossoms are often purplish or pinkish in color and emerge from the upper nodes of the stem. The root system is rhizomatous, allowing the plant to spread effectively through the soil.
