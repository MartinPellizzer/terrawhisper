Cilician Nigella (Nigella Cilicica)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Nigella cilicica.

Cilician nigella, scientifically known as Nigella cilicica, is a medicinal herb native to the Cilicia region of southern Turkey, prized for its therapeutic properties and historical significance. This plant is valued not only as a spice but also as an adaptogen and traditional remedy, offering a range of health benefits including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and digestive support. Historically, it has been used in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cultures to treat ailments such as respiratory issues, skin conditions, and menstrual discomfort, often incorporated into teas and poultices. In modern wellness practices, it is gaining recognition for its potential in supporting gut health and reducing oxidative stress, with its unique flavor combining earthy and peppery notes. Additionally, its rare active compound, nigellone, has sparked interest in its possible role in neuroprotective and anti-cancer research, highlighting its enduring relevance in both traditional and contemporary medicine.
