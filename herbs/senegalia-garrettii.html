What is Senegalia garrettii?

Senegalia garrettii is a species of shrub or small tree in the family Fabaceae.

It is endemic to the Philippines and is characterized by its branches that are armed with small, straight spines, and its leaves that are bipinnate with 6-12 pairs of pinnae, each pinna having 8-20 pairs of leaflets. The leaflets are linear to oblanceolate in shape, 3-12 mm long, and have a pointed tip. The flowers are yellow and arranged in axillary clusters, while the pods are straight, flat, and 6-12 cm long, containing 2-4 seeds.

The species typically grows 3-6 meters tall and is found in open areas and along trails at elevations of 300-800 meters above sea level.
