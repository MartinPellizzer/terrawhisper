Veronica serpyllifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Veronica serpyllifolia?

Veronica serpyllifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Plantaginaceae, which is further refined into the genus Veronica, ultimately leading to its specific identification as the species serpyllifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Veronica serpyllifolia has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with crenate margins and a hairy surface, growing on slender, creeping stems that form mats. The plant typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters above the ground. Its flowers are small and blue with pale white centers and four distinct lobes. Long, thin pedicels support the blossoms, which often appear in dense clusters. The calyx consists of four sepals that are noticeably hairy, matching the texture of the foliage.
