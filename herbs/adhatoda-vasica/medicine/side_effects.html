1. Causes allergic reactions

Adhatoda vasica causes allergic reactions due to its high content of alkaloids, particularly vasicinone and vasicoline.

These compounds can stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical mediator that triggers an allergic response in some individuals.

This may manifest as symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling, which can range from mild to severe.

2. Triggers bronchospasm attacks

Adhatoda vasica triggers bronchospasm attacks.

The herb contains a compound called vasicinone, which can stimulate the vagus nerve and cause constriction of the airways. Additionally, Adhatoda vasica can increase mucus production, further exacerbating breathing difficulties.

These effects are likely due to the herb's ability to bind to beta-2 receptors in the lungs, leading to increased bronchial smooth muscle tone and reduced lung function.

3. Induces respiratory distress

Adhatoda vasica induces respiratory distress due to its ability to stimulate the vagus nerve and relax bronchial smooth muscle.

This can lead to increased mucus production and constriction of airways, causing symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, Adhatoda vasica's saponins may also cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, further exacerbating respiratory distress.

4. Increases heart rate rapidly

Adhatoda vasica increases heart rate rapidly due to its ability to stimulate the cardiac system and increase blood pressure.

This is attributed to the presence of alkaloids such as vasicinone and vasicine, which are known to have a direct effect on the autonomic nervous system, leading to an increased heart rate and cardiac output.

As Adhatoda vasica works to dilate blood vessels and improve circulation, it can also cause palpitations and tachycardia in some individuals.

5. Lowers blood pressure drastically

Adhatoda vasica lowers blood pressure drastically.

This side effect is attributed to the presence of certain compounds in the herb, such as vasin and adhatodine, which have been shown to relax blood vessels and reduce cardiac output, thereby leading to a significant drop in blood pressure.

Additionally, Adhatoda vasica may also inhibit the production of angiotensin-converting enzyme, a key player in regulating blood pressure, further contributing to this adverse effect.

6. Stimulates uterine contractions

Adhatoda vasica stimulates uterine contractions due to its oxytocic properties.

This is because the plant's extracts contain alkaloids that mimic the effects of the hormone oxytocin, which plays a crucial role in uterine contractions during childbirth and lactation.

As a result, Adhatoda vasica can potentially induce premature labor or enhance existing uterine contractions, leading to complications in pregnant women.

7. Provokes menstrual irregularities

Adhatoda vasica provokes menstrual irregularities due to its ability to stimulate uterine contractions and alter hormone levels.

The herb contains compounds that have been shown to mimic the effects of estrogen and progesterone in the body, which can disrupt normal menstrual cycles and lead to irregularities such as changes in bleeding patterns, delayed or skipped periods, and hormonal imbalances.

8. Enhances sedative effects

Adhatoda vasica enhances sedative effects by its ability to increase the activity of GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid), a neurotransmitter that helps regulate sleep and relaxation.

This effect is due to the presence of compounds like alkaloids and phenolic acids in Adhatoda vasica, which interact with GABA receptors in the brain, leading to increased sedation and potentially causing drowsiness or lethargy.

9. Interacts with anticoagulant drugs

Adhatoda vasica interacts with anticoagulant drugs.

This interaction can lead to bleeding or bruising easily due to its ability to thin the blood and increase the risk of bleeding complications when combined with warfarin, heparin, and other anticoagulants.

The compound vasicine in Adhatoda vasica is responsible for this effect, as it inhibits platelet aggregation and prolongs bleeding time, further exacerbating the anticoagulant's blood-thinning properties.

10. Reduces glucose levels significantly

Adhatoda vasica reduces glucose levels significantly.

This is because it contains a compound called vasicinone, which has been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in carbohydrate metabolism, leading to decreased glucose absorption and increased glucose excretion.

Additionally, Adhatoda vasica may also stimulate the release of insulin from pancreatic beta cells, further contributing to its hypoglycemic effects.
