10 Possible Side Effects Of Adhatoda Vasica (Malabar Nut)

Adhatoda vasica has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, dizziness, and insomnia.

These side effects are caused by the excessive consumption of the herb's leaves and flowers, which can lead to an overload of vasaka alkaloids. If left unmanaged, these side effects can worsen and interfere with daily activities, impacting work performance and social relationships.

Furthermore, frequent bouts of dizziness may lead to accidents, straining personal and financial stability.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Adhatoda vasica if used imporperly.

Adhatoda vasica causes allergic reactions due to its high content of alkaloids, particularly vasicinone and vasicoline.

These compounds can stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical mediator that triggers an allergic response in some individuals.

This may manifest as symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling, which can range from mild to severe.

The herb contains a compound called vasicinone, which can stimulate the vagus nerve and cause constriction of the airways. Additionally, Adhatoda vasica can increase mucus production, further exacerbating breathing difficulties.

These effects are likely due to the herb's ability to bind to beta-2 receptors in the lungs, leading to increased bronchial smooth muscle tone and reduced lung function.
