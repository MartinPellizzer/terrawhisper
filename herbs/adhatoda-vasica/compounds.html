Kalmegh (Adhatoda vasica) Compounds

The primary compounds of Kalmegh include alkaloids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Flavonoids and glycosides contribute to its antioxidant and antimicrobial activities, while saponins and terpenoids support its overall therapeutic profile.

Kalmegh (Adhatoda vasica) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Adhatoda vasica contains the alkaloid vasicine, which is known for its bronchodilatory and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound helps in relieving respiratory conditions by relaxing airway muscles and reducing mucus secretion. Vasicine is a key contributor to the plant's traditional use in treating coughs and asthma.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Kalmegh (Adhatoda vasica), known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory conditions and inflammation by reducing secretions and tightening mucous membranes. Tannins also support the plant's antimicrobial and antioxidant effects.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Adhatoda vasica. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in respiratory and digestive health. Flavonoids enhance the overall therapeutic potential of Kalmegh in herbal medicine.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Kalmegh (Adhatoda vasica). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including its role in respiratory and digestive health. Glycosides in Kalmegh are known for their anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory effects. These compounds are essential for the plant's traditional and modern medicinal applications.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Kalmegh (Adhatoda vasica). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in respiratory and digestive health. Saponins exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, supporting Kalmegh's role in herbal medicine. They are responsible for some of the plant's therapeutic effects.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Kalmegh (Adhatoda vasica) that contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and bronchodilatory effects, making them valuable in treating respiratory and skin conditions. 7. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Kalmegh (Adhatoda vasica), known for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. 8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Kalmegh (Adhatoda vasica). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin helps in treating respiratory conditions and digestive issues. Its presence supports the traditional use of Kalmegh in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Kalmegh (Adhatoda vasica). It is extracted from the leaves and possesses antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and bronchodilatory properties. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory conditions and infections.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Kalmegh (Adhatoda vasica). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting respiratory health and reducing inflammation. This compound helps enhance the plant's therapeutic effects in treating conditions like cough and bronchitis.
