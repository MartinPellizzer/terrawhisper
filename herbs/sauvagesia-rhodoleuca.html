Sauvagesia rhodoleuca

Sauvagesia rhodoleuca has opposite, simple leaves with serrated margins and prominent venation, pink to reddish flowers with five petals, and a woody stem structure. The foliage is typically arranged in pairs along the length of the plant, providing a dense green canopy. Its inflorescence consists of bright, colorful blossoms that attract various pollinators to the plant. The reproductive organs are centered within the petal arrangement, leading to the development of small fruits. These morphological features collectively define the species within its specific botanical family.
