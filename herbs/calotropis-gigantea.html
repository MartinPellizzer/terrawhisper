Calotropis gigantea

What's the taxonomical classification of Calotropis gigantea?

Calotropis gigantea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, it is defined by the genus Calotropis and is specifically identified by the species name gigantea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calotropis gigantea has large, thick, elliptical leaves that are opposite or whorled and possess a waxy, grayish-green surface with prominent pale veins.

The stems are woody at the base and characterized by a milky white latex that exudes when the plant is damaged. Its flowers are large, pentamerous, and star-shaped, displaying a complex structure with a prominent corona in shades of white, purple, or pink. The plant produces long, slender follicles that develop into woody fruit capsules containing numerous seeds.

Each seed is equipped with a tuft of silky hairs called coma to facilitate wind dispersal.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Calotropis gigantea contains bioactive compounds in its leaves, stems, roots, flowers, and milky latex that are utilized for diverse therapeutic purposes. The milky sap is frequently applied topically to treat skin diseases, warts, and inflammatory conditions. Medicinal preparations often involve the dried leaves to alleviate respiratory issues like asthma or to reduce swelling through poultices. The roots are sometimes processed to treat digestive ailments and fever, though they must be used with caution due to their potency. Additionally, the flowers are incorporated into traditional remedies to manage various systemic disorders.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including cardiac glycosides, flavonoids, alkaloids, terpenoids, steroids, phenolics, triterpenoids, and tannins.

The presence of cardiac glycosides is particularly significant due to their potent effects on heart muscle activity. Alkaloids and steroids within the plant contribute to its various pharmacological activities and potential therapeutic applications. Furthermore, the combination of phenolics, flavonoids, and tannins provides substantial antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These complex terpenoids and triterpenoids further enhance the medicinal profile of the species in traditional treatments.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Calotropis gigantea targets several key proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, various inflammatory cytokines like TNF-alpha, and specific heat shock proteins to exert its pharmacological effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as cardenolides and flavonoids that inhibit the cyclooxygenase pathway to reduce pain and swelling. These phytochemicals also modulate signaling proteins involved in cell proliferation and apoptosis, which contributes to its potential anticancer properties. Furthermore, its interaction with various protease enzymes and antioxidant enzymes helps mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Through these diverse protein-ligand interactions, the plant demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and cytotoxic activities.

What are the therapeutic actions of Calotropis gigantea?

Calotropis gigantea has a diverse range of therapeutic actions including emetic, purgative, anti-inflammatory, analgesic, astringent, wound healing, diuretic, anti-parasitic, and immunomodulatory properties.

The plant's milky latex is frequently utilized for its wound healing and astringent effects when applied topically to damaged skin. Internally, its compounds can act as an emetic or purgative to induce vomiting or bowel movements in specific medicinal preparations. The species also exhibits systemic benefits by functioning as an analgesic to relieve pain and an anti-inflammatory agent to reduce swelling. Furthermore, it serves as a diuretic to increase urine production and possesses anti-parasitic qualities to combat various infections.

Finally, its immunomodulatory capabilities help regulate and support the body's natural immune responses.

What are the herbal preparations of Calotropis gigantea?

Calotropis gigantea is prepared as a juice extracted from fresh leaves, a paste made from crushed stems, or a decoction obtained by boiling the roots. The milky latex is frequently applied topically as a poultice to treat skin ailments and swellings. In traditional Ayurvedic practices, the dried leaves are often used in decoctions to address various internal inflammatory conditions. Some preparations involve smoking the dried plant material or consuming finely powdered dried roots in controlled medicinal doses. These diverse forms allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be administered for both external and internal therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Calotropis gigantea can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential blindness due to the highly toxic cardiac glycosides present in its milky sap. Contact with the plant's latex often results in contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, and painful blistering on the skin. If the sap accidentally touches the eyes, it can lead to intense inflammation and permanent damage to the cornea. Ingesting any part of the plant can trigger nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Furthermore, the toxic compounds can affect heart rhythm, potentially leading to serious cardiac complications if consumed in significant amounts.
