Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) Compounds

Black Cohosh contains several primary compounds, including alkaloids, glycosides, and flavonoids. Alkaloids like actein contribute to its medicinal properties. Glycosides such as acteine and 2,4-dihydroxy-6-methoxybenzoyl-β-D-glucopyranoside are key in its estrogenic effects. Flavonoids provide antioxidant benefits.

Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) contains alkaloids, which are nitrogen-containing compounds with potential pharmacological effects. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in managing menopausal symptoms and other hormonal imbalances. However, their exact role and mechanisms of action are still under investigation.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also help preserve the plant's active components and enhance its therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Glycoside

Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) contains the glycoside actein, which is believed to contribute to its traditional use in treating menopausal symptoms. Actein may influence hormone-like effects and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties.

4. Flavonoid

Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) contains flavonoids, which are plant-based compounds known for their antioxidant properties. These flavonoids contribute to the herb's traditional use in supporting women's health, particularly in managing menopausal symptoms. They work synergistically with other active constituents in the plant to provide its therapeutic effects.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating menopausal symptoms by influencing hormonal balance and reducing hot flashes. Saponins also have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that support the plant's overall medicinal profile.

6. Phenolic acid

Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) contains phenolic acid, which contributes to its traditional use in women's health. This compound exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its role in alleviating menopausal symptoms and hormonal imbalances. Phenolic acid is part of the complex phytochemical profile that gives Black Cohosh its therapeutic effects.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating menopausal symptoms and has mild antispasmodic properties. Coumarin's presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine for hormonal balance and uterine support.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including potential effects on hormonal balance and menopausal symptoms. Specific terpenoids in Black Cohosh may interact with estrogen receptors, though research continues to clarify their mechanisms and efficacy.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by acting as an antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in treating menstrual disorders and menopausal symptoms.
