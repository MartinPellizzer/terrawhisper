Botanical Identification of Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa)

To identify Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Black Cohosh, or Actaea racemosa, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Berberidales, family Berberidaceae, genus Actaea, and species racemosa. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Black Cohosh is Actaea racemosa, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is also known by several synonyms, including Cimicifuga racemosa, reflecting historical taxonomic changes.

Morphological Characteristics

Black Cohosh is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system. It features a tall, unbranched stem that is green and hairy. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged with serrated edges. The plant produces an erect, branched inflorescence of small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a follicle containing multiple seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Black Cohosh can be distinguished by its tall, unbranched stem with smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of white, raceme-like flowers. It has a distinctive red or purple rootstock and lacks the red berries of similar species like Cimicifuga. Its leaf arrangement and flower structure are key differentiators from other Actaea species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) is native to eastern North America, particularly the eastern United States and southeastern Canada. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in woodlands and shaded areas. It grows at elevations between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Black Cohosh blooms in late summer to early fall, typically between July and September, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs in late fall, with seeds developing from September to November. For identification purposes, the plant is most identifiable during its flowering period, which is key for accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Black Cohosh (Actaea racemosa) is closely related to species within the same genus, Actaea, and shares key family traits with the Ranunculaceae family. It is most closely related to Actaea simplex and Actaea pachypoda, differing mainly in floral structure and habitat preferences. These relationships help in distinguishing it from similar species in both morphology and genetics.
