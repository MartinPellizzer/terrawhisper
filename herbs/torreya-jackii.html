Torreya jackii

What's the taxonomical classification of Torreya jackii?

Torreya jackii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Taxaceae, which contains the genus Torreya, and it is specifically identified by the species name jackii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Torreya jackii has linear, dark green leaves that are arranged in two ranks along the branches and possess a leathery texture with a distinctively pointed tip. The foliage grows in a dense, pyramidal shape, often appearing somewhat stiff to the touch. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous male cones that produce pollen and larger, fleshy female cones that mature into reddish-brown seeds. These seeds are typically held within a thick, pulpy integument that helps facilitate dispersal. The bark of the tree is generally smooth and greyish, becoming more textured as the specimen matures.

Through these multifaceted protein interactions, the plant exerts significant anti-tumor, anti-inflammatory, and neuroprotective properties.
