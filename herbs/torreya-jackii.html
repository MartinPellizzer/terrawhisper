Torreya jackii

What's the taxonomical classification of Torreya jackii?

Torreya jackii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Taxaceae, which contains the genus Torreya, and it is specifically identified by the species name jackii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Torreya jackii has linear, dark green leaves that are arranged in two ranks along the branches and possess a leathery texture with a distinctively pointed tip. The foliage grows in a dense, pyramidal shape, often appearing somewhat stiff to the touch. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous male cones that produce pollen and larger, fleshy female cones that mature into reddish-brown seeds. These seeds are typically held within a thick, pulpy integument that helps facilitate dispersal. The bark of the tree is generally smooth and greyish, becoming more textured as the specimen matures.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the medicinal compounds 7-oxo-dehydroabietic acid, cyrtophyllone B, torreyins A-C, torreyins A-D, and torreyins E and F. These specific chemical constituents are often studied for their unique biological activities and potential therapeutic properties. The presence of various torreyin isomers suggests a complex profile of secondary metabolites within the plant's tissue. Researchers frequently analyze these diterpenoids to understand how they interact with cellular pathways. Such detailed chemical mapping helps in identifying the primary active ingredients responsible for the plant's medicinal effects.

    As discussed in a study, a phytochemical investigation on the MeOH extract of the leaves and twigs of Torreya jackii led to the isolation and characterization of 21 structurally diverse diterpenoids. Among these, six are previously undescribed, which include four abiettane-type torreyins A-D and two labdane-type diterpenoids, torreyins E and F. Specifically, torreyins A-C are identified as rare 11,12-seco-abiettane type diterpenoids possessing a dilactone moiety. Within the isolates, 7-oxo-dehydroabietic acid showed an inhibitory effect against acetyl-coenzyme A carboxylase 1 (ACC1) with an IC50 value of 3.1 μM. Additionally, 15-methoxy-7,13-abietadien-18-oiic acid exhibited inhibitory effects against protein tyrosine phosphatase 1B (PTP1B) with an IC50 value of 6.8 μM.
