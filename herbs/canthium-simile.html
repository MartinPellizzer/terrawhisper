These flowers possess tiny petals and stamens that are adapted for specific pollination methods.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Canthium simile can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Some individuals might experience more severe dermatological responses such as blistering or swelling depending on their sensitivity. If the plant is consumed accidentally, it may lead to nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting. It is important to exercise caution when handling this species to avoid these adverse effects.
