Tricyrtis pilosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tricyrtis pilosa has erect, herbaceous stems that reach heights of approximately 30 to 60 centimeters and are covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are oppositely arranged, lanceolate to ovate in shape, and possess distinctively pubescent surfaces on both sides. Its flowers are bell-shaped and typically display a mottled pattern of purple or pink spots on a lighter background. These blossoms emerge from the upper leaf axils during the late summer months. The plant's overall structure is characterized by its soft, hairy texture and delicate floral ornamentation.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and western China, specifically found in provinces such as Sichuan, Guizhou, Hubei, and Yunnan. It typically inhabits moist, shaded environments within temperate broadleaf and mixed forests. These populations are often located at moderate elevations where the soil remains consistently damp and cool. The specific microclimates of these high-altitude areas provide the necessary humidity for its survival. Consequently, its range is restricted to these specific ecological niches within the East Asian mainland.

Tricyrts pilosa has therapeutic actions as a diaphoric, antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, astringent, and hemostatic agent.
