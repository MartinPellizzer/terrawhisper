Tricyrtis pilosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Tricyrtis pilosa?

Tricyrtis pilosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Liliales. This plant is a representative of the family Liliaceae, situated within the genus Tricyrtis, and is specifically identified by the species name pilosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tricyrtis pilosa has erect, herbaceous stems that reach heights of approximately 30 to 60 centimeters and are covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are oppositely arranged, lanceolate to ovate in shape, and possess distinctively pubescent surfaces on both sides. Its flowers are bell-shaped and typically display a mottled pattern of purple or pink spots on a lighter background. These blossoms emerge from the upper leaf axils during the late summer months. The plant's overall structure is characterized by its soft, hairy texture and delicate floral ornamentation.

These populations are often located at moderate elevations where the soil remains consistently damp and cool.
