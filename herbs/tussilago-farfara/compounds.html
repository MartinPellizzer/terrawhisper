Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara) Compounds

The primary compounds in Coltsfoot include flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and glycosides, which may soothe respiratory tissues. Terpenoids and essential oils contribute to its expectorant effects, while coumarin and saponins support its traditional use in treating respiratory conditions.

Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce airway inflammation and may support respiratory health. They are present in the plant's leaves and are often used in traditional remedies for coughs and bronchitis. Flavonoids enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

2. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara). It contributes to the plant's expectorant properties, aiding in the removal of mucus from the respiratory tract. Glycosides in Coltsfoot may also have anti-inflammatory effects, supporting its traditional use for respiratory conditions.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara). They contribute to the plant's expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, aiding in the treatment of respiratory conditions. Specific terpenoids like farfaresol and tussilagine are believed to enhance mucous clearance and reduce airway irritation.

4. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara). It contributes to the plant's expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties, aiding in respiratory health. The oil contains volatile compounds like cineole and alpha-pinene, which support its traditional use for coughs and bronchial issues.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara). It contributes to the plant's expectorant properties, helping to loosen mucus in the respiratory tract. Coumarin also has mild antispasmodic effects, supporting its traditional use in treating coughs and bronchial congestion.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara). It contributes to the plant's expectorant properties by reducing mucus viscosity and promoting respiratory clearance. Saponin also exhibits mild anti-inflammatory effects, supporting Coltsfoot's traditional use in treating coughs and respiratory ailments.

7. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce respiratory inflammation and supports mucous membrane health. It is one of several bioactive components responsible for the plant's traditional use in treating coughs and bronchial issues.

8. Tannin

Tannin in Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce mucus production and soothe respiratory irritation. Tannins also support the healing of mucous membranes, making Coltsfoot useful for respiratory conditions.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara) are secondary plant compounds with potential bronchodilatory and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory conditions like coughs and bronchitis. However, their exact role and mechanisms in the plant's therapeutic effects remain under investigation.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Coltsfoot (Tussilago farfara). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by acting as an antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent. Anthocyanin supports respiratory health and may help reduce inflammation in the airways, making it a key component in traditional uses of Coltsfoot.
