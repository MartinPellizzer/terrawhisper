Duboscia polyantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Duboscia polyantha?

Duboscia polyantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malvales. The plant is further categorized into the family Malvaceae and is specifically identified by the genus Duboscia, with its unique designation being the species polyantha.

Duboscia polyantha is prepared primarily as a liquid extract or an aqueous infusion using the dried leaves and bark of the plant. What side-effects this plant can have?

Duboscia polyantha can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and localized inflammation upon direct contact with its sap or foliage. These dermatological reactions often manifest as redness, itching, or swelling in sensitive individuals. Because the plant is not widely studied in clinical settings, the full extent of its systemic toxicity remains largely unknown to medical professionals. Ingesting any part of the plant may lead to gastrointestinal distress or more severe internal complications. Caution should always be exercised when handling this species to prevent accidental exposure.
