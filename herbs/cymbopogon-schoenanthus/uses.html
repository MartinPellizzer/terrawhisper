Cymbopogon Grass (Cymbopogon schoenanthus) Uses

Cymbopogon schoenanthus is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and cough. It helps alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections and allergic rhinitis. The grass also supports the treatment of skin and fungal infections, including tuberculosis and the common cold. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties make it effective for these ailments.

Cymbopogon Grass (Cymbopogon schoenanthus) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Cymbopogon schoenanthus is used in herbal medicine to treat bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe respiratory tract irritation. Its essential oils are often steam-distilled and used in inhalation or topical applications to alleviate symptoms.

2. Asthma

Cymbopogon schoenanthus, also known as wild lemon grass, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its essential oils contain compounds like citral and geraniol, which may help ease bronchial spasms and reduce inflammation, making it a potential aid for managing asthma symptoms.

Cymbopogon schoenanthus is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and easing symptoms of infections. Its antimicrobial properties help combat pathogens, while its expectorant effects aid in clearing mucus. It is commonly prepared as a tea to relieve coughs and congestion.
