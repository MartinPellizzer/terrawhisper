Botanical Identification of Cymbopogon Grass (Cymbopogon schoenanthus)

To identify Cymbopogon Grass (Cymbopogon schoenanthus) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Cymbopogon schoenanthus belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Poales, family Poaceae, genus Cymbopogon, and species Cymbopogon schoenanthus. This classification places it within the grass family, commonly known as bamboo or reed grass, and is native to tropical regions.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the grass is Cymbopogon schoenanthus, first described by the botanist Étienne Pierre Jussieu in 1789. It has several synonyms, including Cymbopogon galacticus and Cymbopogon hirtus, reflecting historical taxonomic revisions. Common names include citronella grass and lemon grass, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Cymbopogon schoenanthus is a perennial grass with a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. Its stems are erect and cylindrical, often growing up to 1 meter tall. Leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a strong aromatic scent. The inflorescence is a panicle, with small, spikelet-like flowers arranged in clusters. The fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule containing minute seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cymbopogon schoenanthus can be distinguished by its narrow, grass-like leaves with a slight sheen, and its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence. It has a distinct lemony aroma due to its high citral content, and its leaves are typically longer than wide. It differs from similar species by its leaf structure and aromatic properties.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Cymbopogon schoenanthus is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa. It thrives in warm, temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Cymbopogon schoenanthus blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs from mid-summer to early autumn, around July to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering stage, which is most reliable between June and August.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cymbopogon schoenanthus is closely related to other Cymbopogon species, particularly those in the grass family Poaceae. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with its genus, including narrow leaves and inflorescences. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties to other aromatic grasses used in herbal medicine.
