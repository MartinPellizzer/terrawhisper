10 Possible Side Effects Of Cymbopogon Schoenanthus

Cymbopogon schoenanthus has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects are often caused by consuming excessive amounts or applying the oil topically without proper dilution.

For instance, digestive issues can lead to stomach pain, nausea, and diarrhea, making daily activities challenging and affecting a person's quality of life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Cymbopogon schoenanthus, also known as lemongrass, causes allergic reactions.

This is due to the presence of citral, a compound that can trigger an immune response in some individuals. The allergenic potential of citral is exacerbated by the high concentration found in Cymbopogon schoenanthus, which can lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the oil's potent aroma can also cause respiratory issues in sensitive individuals.

2. Induces skin irritation

Cymbopogon schoenanthus, also known as lemongrass, induces skin irritation in some individuals due to its high concentration of citral and geraniol compounds.

These potent essential oils can cause allergic reactions, such as redness, itching, and rashes, particularly when the skin comes into contact with the plant's oils or extracts.

Additionally, the irritating properties of lemongrass may exacerbate existing skin conditions like eczema or acne, leading to increased discomfort and inflammation.

3. Triggers headaches frequently

Cymbopogon schoenanthus, also known as lemongrass, triggers headaches frequently.

The essential oil extracted from its leaves and stalks contains citral, a compound that can cause phototoxicity in some individuals. When applied topically or consumed orally, citral can increase the skin's sensitivity to light, leading to headache-inducing effects such as inflammation, pain, and discomfort.

Additionally, lemongrass may also interact with certain medications, further exacerbating the risk of headaches.

4. Produces stomach upset often

Cymbopogon schoenanthus, also known as lemongrass, produces stomach upset often.

This is due to its volatile oils, which can irritate the digestive tract and cause discomfort, nausea, and abdominal pain. The essential oil's high citral content may also contribute to this side effect, leading to stomach irritation and inflammation.

As a result, users of Cymbopogon schoenanthus may experience stomach upset as their body processes its bioactive compounds.

5. Causes dizziness occasionally

Cymbopogon schoenanthus causes dizziness occasionally.

This is attributed to its potent essential oils, particularly citral and geraniol, which can affect the nervous system and alter blood flow to the brain.

Additionally, the herb's stimulating properties may cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure, further contributing to feelings of dizziness.

6. Affects blood pressure negatively

The herb's active compounds, such as citral and geraniol, can cause a significant increase in heart rate and cardiac output, leading to a decrease in blood pressure.

Additionally, the stimulant properties of lemongrass may also interact with medications used to treat hypertension, further exacerbating the negative effects on blood pressure.

8. Worsens anxiety symptoms

Cymbopogon schoenanthus, also known as lemongrass, worsens anxiety symptoms due to its high content of citral and geraniol.

These essential oils can have a stimulating effect on the nervous system, leading to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure, which can exacerbate feelings of anxiety and unease.

Additionally, the plant's aromatic compounds may also interfere with the body's natural ability to regulate stress response systems, further contributing to worsened anxiety symptoms.

Cymbopogon schoenanthus lowers blood sugar levels due to its active compounds, citral and geraniol.

These natural chemicals have been found to stimulate insulin secretion and increase glucose uptake in the body's cells, potentially leading to a decrease in blood sugar levels.
