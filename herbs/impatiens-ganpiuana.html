What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens ganpiuana?

Impatiens ganpiuana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Ericales, the plant is a member of the family Balsaminaceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Impatiens, specifically as the species Impatiens ganpiuana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens ganpiuana has succulent, translucent stems and elliptical, serrated leaves that grow in an opposite arrangement along the plant body. The foliage typically exhibits a bright green hue with distinct venation patterns visible on the leaf surfaces. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the axils, often featuring a unique spurred structure characteristic of the genus. The root system is relatively shallow, supporting the moisture-retentive nature of its fleshy stems. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat.
