What's the taxonomical classification of Sparganium emersum?

Sparganium emersum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Typhaceae. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Sparganium, with its specific designation being the species emersum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sparganium emersum has a morphology characterized by a small, globose to subglobose spore sac that typically measures between 150 and 300 micrometers in diameter and is supported by a short, slender stalk. The peridium, or outer skin of the spore sac, is often thin and can be smooth, finely granular, or slightly textured. Inside the sac, the spores are contained within a capillitium that consists of branched, septate threads.
