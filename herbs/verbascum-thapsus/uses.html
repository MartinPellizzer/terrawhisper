Velvetleaf (Verbascum thapsus) Uses

Velvetleaf is used to treat respiratory infections, coughs, and bronchitis. It may help alleviate asthma symptoms and reduce respiratory congestion. It is also applied for sinusitis and upper respiratory tract infections, as well as respiratory allergies. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties support respiratory health.

Velvetleaf (Verbascum thapsus) Monograph

1. Respiratory infection

Velvetleaf has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly for mild infections like coughs and bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help clear mucus and reduce irritation in the airways. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease symptoms and promote healing.

2. Cough

Velvetleaf, or Verbascum thapsus, is used to help alleviate cough symptoms due to its mucilage content, which soothes irritated airways. It is often prepared as a tea to reduce inflammation and ease respiratory discomfort. Its mild expectorant properties may aid in clearing mucus, making it a traditional remedy for dry or productive coughs.

3. Bronchitis

Velvetleaf has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties may help reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated airways. It is often prepared as a tea to ease coughing and promote easier breathing. However, it should be used under the guidance of a qualified herbalist.

4. Asthma symptoms

Velvetleaf has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. It is believed to help reduce inflammation and ease bronchial constriction. Its expectorant properties may assist in clearing mucus, while its mild sedative effects can help calm breathing. However, it should be used under professional guidance.

5. Upper respiratory tract infection

Velvetleaf has been traditionally used to support the treatment of upper respiratory tract infections. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help reduce symptoms like sore throat and congestion. It is often prepared as a tea to soothe the throat and ease breathing. Regular use may aid in clearing mucus and supporting immune function during respiratory illnesses.

6. Respiratory infections

Velvetleaf has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of coughs and bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help clear mucus and soothe irritated airways. It may also enhance immune function, making it a valuable herb for managing respiratory infections.

7. Sinusitis

Velvetleaf has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly for sinusitis. Its anti-inflammatory and mucolytic properties help reduce nasal congestion and mucus buildup. It may aid in clearing sinuses and alleviating symptoms associated with chronic or acute sinus infections.

8. Respiratory congestion

Velvetleaf is used to alleviate respiratory congestion due to its demulcent and expectorant properties. It helps soothe irritated airways and loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. Often prepared as a tea, it is valued for its mild yet effective action on the respiratory system.

9. Upper respiratory tract infections

Velvetleaf has been traditionally used to support the treatment of upper respiratory tract infections. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties may help alleviate symptoms such as congestion and coughing. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to promote respiratory comfort. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.

10. Respiratory allergies

Velvetleaf (Verbascum thapsus) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate respiratory allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce symptoms like sneezing, itching, and nasal congestion by supporting immune regulation and reducing allergic reactions. Its mucilage content also soothes irritated airways, making it a valuable remedy for seasonal allergy relief.
