Agathosma Crenulata (Agathosma crenulata) Uses

Agathosma crenulata is primarily used to treat respiratory infections, including cough, bronchitis, asthma, and allergic rhinitis. It helps alleviate symptoms of the common cold, respiratory tract infections, sinusitis, influenza, and chronic cough by supporting respiratory function and reducing inflammation.

Agathosma Crenulata (Agathosma crenulata) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Agathosma crenulata is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing irritation in the airways. It may help alleviate symptoms of colds, coughs, and bronchitis due to its antimicrobial and expectorant properties. Its essential oils are often utilized in steam inhalations and topical applications for respiratory relief.

2. Cough

Agathosma crenulata is traditionally used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus in the respiratory tract, making it easier to expel. Often prepared as a tea, it is valued for its calming effect on the throat and its ability to reduce irritation. Its use supports respiratory health in herbal medicine practices.

3. Bronchitis

Agathosma crenulata is used traditionally to support respiratory health, particularly in managing symptoms of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce mucus buildup and soothe airway irritation. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to ease breathing and alleviate coughing associated with bronchial inflammation.

4. Asthma

Agathosma crenulata has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilating properties may help reduce airway inflammation and ease breathing. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture and used in herbal medicine to support respiratory function.

5. Allergic rhinitis

Agathosma crenulata has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose by modulating immune responses. Its active compounds, such as volatile oils and sesquiterpenes, contribute to its effectiveness in managing allergy-related respiratory issues.

6. Common cold

Agathosma crenulata is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce congestion, soothe sore throats, and boost immunity. Often prepared as a tea, it supports respiratory health during colds. Its use aligns with holistic approaches in herbal medicine for natural symptom relief.

7. Respiratory tract infections

Agathosma crenulata is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing irritated airways. Its antimicrobial properties may help combat infections, while its expectorant effects aid in clearing mucus. It is commonly applied for conditions like bronchitis and coughs.

8. Sinusitis

Agathosma crenulata is used traditionally to alleviate symptoms of sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. 9. Influenza

10. Chronic cough
