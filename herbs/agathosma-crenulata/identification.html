Botanical Identification of Agathosma Crenulata (Agathosma crenulata)

To identify Agathosma Crenulata (Agathosma crenulata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Agathosma crenulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Agathosma, and species Agathosma crenulata. It is a member of the daisy family and is known for its aromatic leaves used in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Agathosma crenulata, first described by the botanist George Don in 1828. It is also known by several synonyms, including Asperula crenulata and Agathosma capensis. Common names such as "Cape geranium" and "red sandalwood" are widely used, while regional and historical names reflect its traditional significance in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Agathosma crenulata is a low-growing, evergreen shrub with a compact growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly in the soil. The stems are woody and branching, with a greyish-brown color. The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a distinctive wavy margin. The inflorescence is a terminal spike, bearing small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Agathosma crenulata can be distinguished by its distinctive crenulate (notched) leaves and aromatic, yellowish-brown resinous secretions. It differs from similar species like Agathosma polycarpon by its leaf shape and the presence of a more pronounced central vein. Its habitat and growth pattern also help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Agathosma crenulata is native to South Africa, particularly the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 200 and 800 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Agathosma crenulata blooms between September and October. Seed formation occurs from November to February. Harvesting is typically done during the dry season, from May to August, when the plant's essential oils are most concentrated.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Agathosma crenulata is closely related to species within the same genus, Agathosma. It shares key characteristics with other members of the Asteraceae family, such as composite flowers and specific leaf structures. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other South African medicinal plants.
