Agathosma Crenulata (Agathosma crenulata) Compounds

Agathosma crenulata contains essential oils, terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds contribute to its aromatic profile and potential medicinal properties. Coumarin and tannins offer anti-inflammatory effects, while glycosides and saponins may support digestive health. Alkaloids and anthocyanins add to its complex chemical makeup and therapeutic potential.

Agathosma Crenulata (Agathosma crenulata) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Agathosma crenulata contains bioactive compounds like limonene and caryophyllene. These compounds contribute to its traditional use in treating respiratory and inflammatory conditions. The essential oil is extracted through steam distillation, preserving its therapeutic properties. It is a key component in the plant's medicinal applications.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Agathosma crenulata, commonly known as buchu. These compounds contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating respiratory and urinary tract infections.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Agathosma crenulata are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. They help modulate immune responses and may support overall health. Flavonoids are a key component of the plant's therapeutic profile.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Agathosma crenulata, contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps support the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. This compound is abundant in the leaves and roots of the plant. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Agathosma crenulata in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Agathosma crenulata, known for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating pain and inflammation. Coumarin also exhibits antimicrobial activity, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin in Agathosma crenulata contributes to its astringent properties and may support digestive health. This compound is known for its ability to bind proteins and reduce inflammation. It plays a role in the plant's traditional use for treating gastrointestinal issues. Tannins also contribute to the plant's overall medicinal profile.

7. Glycoside

Agathosma crenulata contains the glycoside agathosmin. This compound is known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Agathosmin is a key component in the plant's therapeutic profile.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Agathosma crenulata. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. Saponin helps support the plant's reputation as a valuable remedy in natural healthcare practices.

9. Alkaloid

Agathosma crenulata contains the alkaloid agathosmin. This compound is known for its stimulating and antidepressant effects. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating depression and fatigue. Agathosmin is one of the key active components responsible for the plant's therapeutic properties.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Agathosma crenulata, contributing to its antioxidant properties. Found in the plant's leaves, it helps neutralize free radicals and may support overall health. Its presence highlights the plant's potential in herbal medicine for promoting cellular protection and wellness.
