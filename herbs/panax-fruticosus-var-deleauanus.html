Panax fruticosus var. deleauanus

Panax fruticosus var. deleauanus, also known as the Delevay's ginseng or Delevay's Chinese ginseng, is a rare and endangered species of the Panax genus, which is a group of slow-growing, perennial plants native to the mountains of Asia.

The medicinal properties of Panax fruticosus var. deleauanus include its ability to stimulate the immune system, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antioxidant and adaptogenic activities.

The health benefits of this plant are attributed to its ability to improve cognitive function, boost energy, and alleviate symptoms of fatigue and stress, making it a potential natural remedy for various health conditions.

Panax fruticosus var. deleauanus can be prepared as a herbal supplement in the form of capsules, tablets, or teas, or used as an extract in traditional medicine, although its availability is limited due to its rarity and endangered status.
