Euodia lepta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euodia lepta has small, opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage is characterized by a glossy texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. Its stems are woody and slender, often exhibiting a branching habit that supports a compact shrub structure. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters throughout the growing season. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that facilitate the plant's reproduction.
