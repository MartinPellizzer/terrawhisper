Synotis triligulata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Synotis triligulata has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with prominent three-veined bases and finely serrated margins. The leaf blades are typically green and possess a texture that varies between herbaceous and slightly leathery. Its stems are erect and can become woody at the base as the plant matures. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These floral structures are characterized by their delicate arrangement along the stem.
