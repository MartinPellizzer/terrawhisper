Thalictrum glandulosissimum

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum glandulosissimum?

Thalictrum glandulosissimum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, which contains the genus Thalictrum, and is specifically identified by the species name glandulosissimum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum glandulosissimum has pinnately compound leaves characterized by numerous small, glandular-tipped leaflets and stems densely covered in sticky, glandular hairs. The foliage displays a delicate texture due to the fine segmentation of the leaf blades. These glandular structures are distributed across the entire plant surface, providing a distinctive tactile quality. During the flowering stage, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The overall morphology is defined by this widespread glandularity which distinguishes it from related species.
