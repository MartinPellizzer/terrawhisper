Thalictrum glandulosissimum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum glandulosissimum has pinnately compound leaves characterized by numerous small, glandular-tipped leaflets and stems densely covered in sticky, glandular hairs. The foliage displays a delicate texture due to the fine segmentation of the leaf blades. These glandular structures are distributed across the entire plant surface, providing a distinctive tactile quality. During the flowering stage, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. The overall morphology is defined by this widespread glandularity which distinguishes it from related species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Gansu. It typically thrives in high-altitude environments located within subalpine meadows and forest margins. These habitats are characterized by cool, moist conditions provided by mountain slopes and stream banks. The species is primarily restricted to these specific East Asian montane ecosystems. Its distribution remains concentrated in these high-elevation niches across the Hengduan Mountains.

      What are the herbal preparations of Thalictrum glandulosissimum?

      Thalictrum glandulosissimum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical tincture for various medicinal applications.
