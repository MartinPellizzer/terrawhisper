Celtis julianae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celtis julianae has simple, alternate leaves with ovate to elliptical shapes, serrated margins, and prominent pinnate venation. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy texture on the upper surface and a paler green color underneath. Its woody stems feature characteristic lenticels and a bark texture that may become somewhat furrowed with age. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy drupes used for dispersal. These morphological traits are essential for distinguishing this specific species within the Celtis genus.

      What are the herbal preparations of Celtis julianae?

      Celtis julianae is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a medicinal infusion of the leaves, or a topical poultice made from crushed plant material.       Celtis julianae can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if the sap or bark comes into direct contact with sensitive human tissue. There is also a potential for gastrointestinal distress if any part of the tree is accidentally ingested. While specific toxicological studies on this species are limited, caution is advised when handling the plant to prevent unexpected reactions.
