Rosa indica var. longifolia

Rosa indica var. longifolia, commonly known as Damask rose, is a fragrant and ornamental plant native to the Middle East and parts of Europe.

This plant is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antispasmodic properties, which can help to soothe digestive issues, reduce stress, and promote relaxation.

The therapeutic actions of Rosa indica var. longifolia include its ability to reduce anxiety, improve sleep quality, and alleviate symptoms of menopause, while its bioactive constituents include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils such as geraniol and linalool, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Overall, the Rosa indica var. longifolia plant has been valued for centuries for its fragrance, beauty, and healing properties, making it a valuable resource in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

The roots of Rosa indica var. longifolia are not commonly used medicinally, but they do contain sesquiterpenes and other compounds that have been shown to have antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.
