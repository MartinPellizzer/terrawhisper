Clematis connata

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis connata?

Clematis connata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Ranunculales and the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Clematis, and its unique species designation, connaata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis connata has woody, climbing stems that utilize opposite, trifoliate leaves composed of three distinct, ovate leaflets with entire margins. The foliage is typically a vibrant green color and serves to support the plant as it scales surrounding structures. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that lack showy petals but feature prominent, hairy stamens. These floral components are arranged in clusters that emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. The plant's overall structure is characterized by its ability to spread through the use of twisting petioles that act as tendrils.
