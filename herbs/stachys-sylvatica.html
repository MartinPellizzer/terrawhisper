Wood Betony (Stachys sylvatica)

Wood Betony (Stachys sylvatica) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Europe, and Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and native american herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 80 centimeters.
Leaves
Ovate leaves with dark green upper surface and lighter green lower surface, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences consist of dense spikes of actinomorphic flowers with two-lipped corollas, each having five lobes, and four stamens arranged in two pairs, with calyces that are tubular and pubescent.
Stems
Erect, unbranched, woody, hairy, with opposite leaf scars and a smooth, cylindrical surface.
