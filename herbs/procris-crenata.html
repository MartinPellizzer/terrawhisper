What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Procris crenata has linear, dark green leaves with crenate or wavy margins and a leathery texture. The plant is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and relatively small stature. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stems and often feature a distinct midrib. The flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters that emerge from the leaf axils. The overall structure of the plant reflects its adaptation to coastal or rocky environments where it often grows.
