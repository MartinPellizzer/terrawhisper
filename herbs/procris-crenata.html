What's the taxonomical classification of Procris crenata?

Procris crenata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Rosales and into the family Urticaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Procris, and its unique species designation, crenata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Procris crenata has linear, dark green leaves with crenate or wavy margins and a leathery texture.

The plant is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and relatively small stature. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stems and often feature a distinct midrib. The flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters that emerge from the leaf axils.

The overall structure of the plant reflects its adaptation to coastal or rocky environments where it often grows.
