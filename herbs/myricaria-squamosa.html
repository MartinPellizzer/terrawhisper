Myricaria squamosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Myricaria squamosa?

Myricaria squamosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Tamaricaceae and is identified by the genus Myricaria, specifically within the species squamosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Myricaria squamosa has an erect, perennial herb structure characterized by hollow, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The leaves are arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem and possess a lanceolate shape with entire margins. Each leaf features a prominent midrib and a smooth, somewhat fleshy texture typical of aquatic vegetation. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in the leaf axils. These plants frequently develop a spreading rhizomatous root system that allows them to anchor firmly in muddy or submerged substrates.
