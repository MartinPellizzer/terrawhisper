Vitex pachyphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitex pachyphylla?

Vitex pachyphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Lamiales and falls under the family Lamiaceae. This specific plant is identified by the genus Vitex and is distinguished as the species pachyphylla.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Vitex pachyphylla contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including iridoids, flavonoids, triterpenes, and various phenolic compounds. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Specific studies have identified unique diterpenoids that may contribute to its potential pharmacological activities. The presence of these compounds suggests therapeutic value in managing oxidative stress within biological systems. Researchers continue to investigate how these specific molecular structures interact with human cellular pathways.

What are the therapeutic actions of Vitex pachyphylla?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various hormonal imbalances, menstrual irregularities, such as dysmenorrhea and oligomenorrhea, and symptoms associated with premenstrual syndrome. It functions primarily by influencing the endocrine system to regulate the secretion of luteinizing hormone. Practitioners often utilize its properties to manage symptoms of menopause and stabilize reproductive cycles in women. The botanical extracts are believed to support the luteal phase and improve overall hormonal equilibrium. This traditional application focuses on restoring physiological balance through its bioactive compounds.
