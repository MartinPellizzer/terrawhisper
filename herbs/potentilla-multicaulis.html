Potentilla multicaulis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella multicaulis has a perennial growth habit characterized by multiple ascending or decumbent stems arising from a woody rhizome. The leaves are typically trifoliate or pinnately divided with serrated margins and a fine coating of pubescence on the undersides. Small, five-petaled flowers emerge on slender pedicels, displaying bright yellow petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens and carpels. The plant often develops a low-growing, spreading mound shape due to its multi-stemmed structure. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in rocky or alpine environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine and subalpine regions of western North America, ranging from Alaska and the Yukon through much of Canada and into the western United States. It typically thrives in rocky tundra, mountain meadows, and fellfields where soil is thin and exposure is high. Its presence is most concentrated in the mountainous areas of the Rocky Mountains and the Pacific Northwest. Because it prefers cold, montane environments, its distribution is strictly limited to specific elevational belts. These habitats provide the necessary moisture and sunlight required for its survival in harsh northern climates.
