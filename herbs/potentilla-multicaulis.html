What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla multicaulis?

Potentiella multicaulis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Rosales. It is a member of the family Rosaceae, which is further refined into the genus Potentiella, ultimately identifying the specific species as Potentiella multicaulis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella multicaulis has a perennial growth habit characterized by multiple ascending or decumbent stems arising from a woody rhizome. Small, five-petaled flowers emerge on slender pedicels, displaying bright yellow petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens and carpels. The plant often develops a low-growing, spreading mound shape due to its multi-stemmed structure. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in rocky or alpine environments.
