Coula edulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Coula edulis?

Coula eduledis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Santalales and is a member of the family Olacaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Coulia and the species edulis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Coula eduilis contains a diverse profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, saponins, tannins, flavonoids, and polyphenolic compounds. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. The presence of saponins is particularly notable for their potential role in managing cholesterol and inflammatory responses. Additionally, the tannins and flavonoids within the leaves and seeds act as natural agents to combat oxidative stress in biological systems. Research continues to investigate how these specific molecular structures can be utilized for developing new therapeutic treatments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Coula edulis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various gastrointestinal ailments including diarrhea, stomach aches, and intestinal parasites. Its leaves and stems contain bioactive compounds that help soothe digestive inflammation and regulate bowel movements. In many traditional medicinal practices, extracts are applied to combat minor infections and promote healing. The plant also serves as a remedy for managing symptoms related to respiratory discomfort and general malaise. These therapeutic properties make it a significant component in several regional herbal medicine systems.
