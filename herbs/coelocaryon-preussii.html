Coelocaryon preussii

What's the taxonomical classification of Coelocaryon preussii?

Coelocaryon preussii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, which falls under the subclass Magnoliidae. It is further organized into the order Magnoliales and is a member of the family Myristicaceae. Within this family, the plant is identified by the genus Coelocaryon, with the specific designation of the species preussii.

Local traditional practices utilize the plant to soothe irritation and reduce swelling on the skin's surface. What are the herbal preparations of Coelocaryon preussii?

Coelocaryon preussii is prepared primarily as a decoction or a tea made from its leaves and bark to address various ailments. Indigenous groups in New Guinea often use these aqueous extracts to treat stomach pains and digestive issues. Some practitioners may also crush the plant material to create a topical poultice for skin irritations. The infusion is frequently administered orally to help manage internal inflammation. These traditional methods rely on the solubility of the plant's active compounds in hot water.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Coelocaryon preussii can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or touched. The plant contains chemical compounds that may act as irritants to the delicate tissues of the digestive tract. Contact with the sap or foliage can lead to redness, itching, or localized inflammation on the skin. Some individuals may experience more severe symptoms such as swelling or nausea following accidental exposure. Monitoring for these reactions is essential when handling any part of this species.
