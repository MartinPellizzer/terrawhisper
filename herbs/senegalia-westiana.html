What is Senegalia westiana?

Senegalia westiana is a tree species belonging to the family Fabaceae, subfamily Faboideae.

It is endemic to Western Australia, specifically in the southwestern region. Botanically, Senegalia westiana is characterized by its glabrous or puberulent stems and branches, with a height range of 1-5 meters. The leaves are bipinnate, with 2-4 pairs of pinnae, and 10-20 pairs of leaflets, each 2-5 mm long.

Flowers are yellow or greenish, arranged in axillary racemes, and are pollinated by bees.
