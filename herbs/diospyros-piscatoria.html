Diospyros piscatoria

What's the taxonomical classification of Diospyros piscatoria?

Diospyros piscatoria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Ebenaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Diospyros, with its specific designation being the species piscatoria.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diospyros piscatoria has large, evergreen, leathery leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape and arranged oppositely on thick, woody stems.

The foliage typically displays a dark green upper surface with a lighter green underside. Its flowers are relatively small and white, emerging from the axils of the leaves. The plant produces fleshy, globose fruits that contain several large, stony seeds.

These woody structures are characteristic of its growth habit within tropical forest ecosystems.

While the plant is used in traditional medicine, the chemical composition of its various parts is not fully documented for human safety.
