Diospyros piscatoria

What's the taxonomical classification of Diospyros piscatoria?

Diospyros piscatoria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ericales and is a member of the family Ebenaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Diospyros, with its specific designation being the species piscatoria.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Diospyros piscatoria contains bioactive compounds including triterpenoids, steroids, and various polyphenolic substances found within its bark, leaves, and fruit. These chemical constituents are studied for their potential pharmacological properties such as antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. Some research suggests that extracts from this species may exhibit anti-inflammatory effects due to the presence of specific terpenoid structures. The presence of these secondary metabolites makes the plant a subject of interest in ethnobotanical studies regarding traditional medicine. Understanding the precise concentration of these molecules is essential for evaluating their therapeutic potential in modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Diospyros piscatoria?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, intestinal worms, and inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are often prepared as decoctions or poultices to address these specific health concerns. In some traditional practices, extracts from the plant are applied topically to soothe irritated skin or fungal growths. Additionally, the medicinal properties are utilized to help manage internal discomforts related to digestive issues. Local healers rely on these botanical components to provide relief from a range of common physical maladies.

What are the herbal preparations of Diospyros piscatoria?

Diospyros piscatoria is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using the various parts of the plant including the bark, leaves, and roots. These aqueous extractions are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to address inflammatory conditions or digestive issues. The bark is often boiled to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. Additionally, the leaves can be crushed into a poultice to treat skin ailments through direct application. These diverse preparations allow practitioners to extract the specific bioactive compounds required for different therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Diospyros piscatoria can cause gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and stomach cramps, if the fruit or its components are consumed in improper quantities or preparations. While the plant is used in traditional medicine, the chemical composition of its various parts is not fully documented for human safety. Potential toxicity may arise from specific secondary metabolites found within the genus Diospyros. There is also a risk of allergic reactions in individuals sensitive to the plant's compounds. Careful consideration must be given to the dosage and the specific part of the plant being utilized to avoid adverse effects.
