Diospyros piscatoria

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Diospyros piscatoria has large, evergreen, leathery leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape and arranged oppositely on thick, woody stems. The foliage typically displays a dark green upper surface with a lighter green underside. Its flowers are relatively small and white, emerging from the axils of the leaves. The plant produces fleshy, globose fruits that contain several large, stony seeds. These woody structures are characteristic of its growth habit within tropical forest ecosystems.
