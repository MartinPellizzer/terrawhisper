What's the taxonomical classification of Fritillaria maximowiczii?

Fritillaria maximowiczii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy through the order Liliales, it is placed in the family Liliaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Fritillaria, with its specific designation being the species maximowiczii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Friitillaria maximowiczii has erect, hollow stems that reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and support several nodding, bell-shaped flowers characterized by a brownish-purple or yellowish hue and distinct dark spots. The leaves are lanceolate or linear in shape, arranged alternately along the stem, and possess a smooth texture. At the base of the plant, a bulb composed of several fleshy scales serves as the primary storage organ. Each flower typically features six tepals that curve gracefully outward at the tips. This morphological structure allows the plant to thrive in temperate meadow environments.
