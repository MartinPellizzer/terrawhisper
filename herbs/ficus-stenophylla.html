Ficus stenophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus stenophylla?

Ficus stenophyllas belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Rosales and is a member of the family Moraceae. Ultimately, its taxonomic position is defined by its placement within the genus Ficus, specifically identified as the species stenophyllas.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus stenophylla has narrow, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and possess smooth, entire margins. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color with a leathery texture and prominent pinnate venation. Its growth habit often manifests as a woody shrub or small tree with slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous figs that develop directly from the leaf axils. These botanical features allow the species to adapt effectively to its native tropical environments.
