Selaginella biformis

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella biformis?

Selaginella biformis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Lycopodiidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Selaginella, with its specific designation being the species biformis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selagineella biformis has a creeping rhizome that produces small, scale-like leaves arranged in four rows along slender, branching stems.

These leaves are diminutive and triangular, overlapping closely to cover the stem surface. The plant exhibits a distinct dimorphism in its sporophylls, where specialized leaves bear sporangia for reproduction. Each sporangium is typically located in the axils of these modified leaves, often appearing in clusters.

This morphological structure allows the plant to thrive in moist, shaded environments through efficient water retention and spore dispersal.
