Parnassia farreri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Parnassia farreri has a basal rosette of fleshy, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a distinctive long-petioled structure. The plant typically produces a single upright flowering stem that rises from the center of the leaf cluster. Its flowers are characterized by five white petals that feature intricate, colored veins and dark spots near the base. Small, nectar-producing structures are located within the center of the bloom to attract specific pollinators. The fruit develops into a small capsule that eventually disperses seeds from the dried floral remains.
