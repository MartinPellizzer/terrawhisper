Clematis nannophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis nannophylla?

Clematis nannophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Clematis, and is specifically identified by its species name, nannophylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis nannophylla has opposite, trifoliate leaves characterized by small, ovate leaflets that are typically less than 3 centimeters in length. The foliage is relatively sparse compared to other species in the genus, giving the plant a delicate appearance. Its woody stems act as climbing vines that use petioles to grasp surrounding structures for support. The flowers are small and often feature pale or cream-colored tepals that bloom during the warmer seasons. This plant is specifically adapted to rocky or mountainous environments where its compact growth habit is advantageous.
