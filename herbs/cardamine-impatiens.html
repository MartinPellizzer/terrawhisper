Cardamine impatiens

What's the taxonomical classification of Cardamine impatiens?

Cardamine impatiens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Brassicales. It further falls into the family Brassicaceae, which defines its botanical relationships. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is established within the genus Cardamine, with the specific epithet impatiens designating it as the species Cardamine impatiens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cardamine impatiens has opposite, pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of four to eight leaflets with serrated margins and a base that is often cordate or rounded. The stems are slender, erect, and may exhibit a reddish tint depending on the environmental conditions. Small, four-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a white or pale pink coloration. These blossoms are arranged in loose racemes that develop atop the main stem during the flowering period. The plant's reproductive structures eventually form small, elongated siliques that contain numerous tiny seeds.
