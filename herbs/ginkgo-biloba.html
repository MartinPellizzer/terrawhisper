Ginkgo biloba

What's the taxonomical classification of Ginkgo biloba?

Ginkgo biloba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Ginkgoidae. The plant's taxonomic progression continues through the order Ginkgoales and the family Ginkgoaceae, which leads to its specific designation within the genus Ginkgo. Ultimately, its scientific identity is completed by its species name, biloba.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ginkgo biloba contains terpene lactones, specifically ginkgolides and bilobalide, along with various flavonoid glycosides that serve as its primary active medicinal components. The terpene lactones are known for improving blood circulation by reducing platelet aggregation and enhancing microcirculation. Flavonoids act as potent antioxidants that protect cells from oxidative stress and free radical damage. These compounds work synergistically to support cognitive function and neuroprotection in the brain. Together, these phytochemicals make the plant a significant subject in studies regarding memory enhancement and vascular health.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ginkgo biloba?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat cognitive impairments such as Alzheimer's disease, dementia, and age-related memory loss by enhancing blood flow to the brain. It is also frequently utilized to alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression through its effects on neurotransmitter regulation. Some patients use the extract to manage symptoms of peripheral artery disease, which involves poor circulation in the limbs. Additionally, it may help reduce the frequency of vertigo and tinnitus caused by vascular issues. Clinical research continues to explore its potential role in stabilizing mood and supporting overall neurological health.
