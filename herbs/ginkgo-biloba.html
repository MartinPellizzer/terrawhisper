What to know about Ginkgo biloba before using it medicinally

Ginkgo biloba, commonly known as maidenhair tree, has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to address a range of health conditions, including cognitive decline, memory loss, and tinnitus, as well as to improve blood circulation and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression.

The main health benefits associated with Ginkgo biloba include enhanced cognitive function, improved blood flow to the brain, and antioxidant properties that protect against cell damage. Its main therapeutic properties are attributed to its ability to improve blood circulation, reduce inflammation, and modulate platelet aggregation. The primary bioactive compounds found in Ginkgo biloba include flavonoids, bilobalide, and ginkgolides, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

In herbal preparations, Ginkgo biloba is often made into a standardized extract or tea, which is used to support overall health and well-being.

What are the possible side effects of Ginkgo biloba if used improperly?

Furthermore, Ginkgo biloba can cause suffering from bleeding, including nosebleeds and bruising, and experiencing liver damage, which may be exacerbated by pre-existing liver conditions.
