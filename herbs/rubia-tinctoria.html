Madder (Rubia tinctoria)

Madder (Rubia tinctoria) is a member of the Rubiaceae family, native to Southern Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its roots, stems, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and ointments.

This herb is particularly valued for its sedative, anti-inflammatory, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with upper surface reddish-brown and lower surface pale green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are spikes bearing actinomorphic flowers with five stamen, five sepals, and two petals, each petal with a distinct midvein and a red to orange coloration.
Stems
Woody, erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth, glabrous surface, and presence of longitudinal ridges and scattered stomata.
