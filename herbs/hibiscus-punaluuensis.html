Possible Side Effects of Hibiscus punaluuensis

Hibiscus punaluuensis can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing gastrointestinal issues, including diarrhea and stomach cramps, due to its high fiber and tannin content.

Additionally, excessive consumption may lead to allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and difficulty breathing in some individuals.

In rare cases, prolonged use can also lower blood pressure to a potentially hazardous level, especially for people with pre-existing hypotension.

Furthermore, it may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, resulting in adverse health effects.
