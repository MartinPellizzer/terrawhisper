Cassandra calyculata

Cassandra calyculata, commonly known as Bearberry, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to heathlands in Europe and Asia.

It has been traditionally used for its potential health benefits, including the treatment of urinary tract infections, diarrhea, and as a diuretic to reduce blood pressure.

The therapeutic actions of Bearberry are attributed to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, which help to soothe and protect the mucous membranes in the urinary tract.

The bioactive constituents of Bearberry include arbutin, a glycoside responsible for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects, as well as other phenolic compounds with antioxidant properties.
