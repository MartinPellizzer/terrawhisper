Justicia procumbens

What's the taxonomical classification of Justicia procumbens?

Justicia procumbens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Justicia, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, procumbens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Justicia procumbens has a prostrate or decumbent growth habit characterized by slender, square-shaped stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are oppositely arranged, simple, and typically possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils and are usually white or pale pink with distinct purple spots on the lower lip. The plant produces small, dehiscent capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds following successful pollination. This herbaceous species generally remains low to the ground, forming spreading mats in shaded or moist environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Justicia procumbens?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-cancer, antioxidant, and antipyretic pharmacological activities that make it a significant subject of ethnopharmacological research. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, thereby protecting cells from oxidative stress and damage. The plant also demonstrates potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects, which can be utilized to manage pain and reduce swelling. Additionally, its antipyretic capabilities allow it to effectively reduce fever in various biological models. Emerging studies are further investigating its potential anti-cancer activities to determine how it might inhibit tumor growth.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Justicia procumbens is a traditional herbal remedy used to treat fever, pain, and cancer. A new compound isolated from the plant, 6'-hydroxy justicidin A (JR6), shows obvious anti-cancer effects by inhibiting growth in human bladder cancer EJ cells. This compound reduces SOD activity, increases the content of reactive oxygen species (ROS), and disrupts the mitochondrial membrane potential (Δψm). The study found that JR6 induces apoptosis through a caspase-dependent pathway involving the activation of caspase-3, caspase-8, and caspase-9. Furthermore, the treatment unregulates the expressions of Bax and p53 in EJ cells.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds justiciidin A, justiciidin B, justiciidin C, procumbenoside A, and procumbenoside C. These specific phytochemicals are responsible for many of the biological activities attributed to the species. Researchers have identified these alkaloids and glycosides as key components in its traditional therapeutic uses. The presence of these diverse chemical structures contributes to the plant's complex pharmacological profile. Studying these individual molecules helps scientists understand how the plant interacts with human physiological systems.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", 12 compounds were isolated from the whole plant of Justicia procumbens. These included a new lignan glycoside named procumbenoside A, which is identified as 4-O-alpha-L-arabinopyranosyl-(1' "-->2' ')-beta-D-apiofuranosyldiphylliin (2), and 11 known compounds. Among the known compounds were justicidin A (1), diphyllin (3), and tuberculatin (4). The compounds justicidin A (1), diphyllin (3), and tuberculatin (4) showed potent cytotoxic effects against a number of cancer cells in vitro. Additionally, compounds 1 and 4 strongly enhanced tumor-necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) generation from mouse macrophage-like RAW 264.7 cells stimulated with lipopolysaccharide (LPS).

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating blood stasis syndrome, colds with fever, fever, jaundice, and swollen and sore throat. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to promote circulation and clear stagnation in the blood. When patients suffer from febrile illnesses, the herb helps to reduce body temperature and alleviate symptoms. Its properties are also applied to address hepatic issues related to jaundice. Additionally, it provides relief for inflammatory conditions such as a swollen and sore throat.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Justicia procumbens has been traditionally used to treat colds with fever, swollen and sore throat, jaundice, malaria and eczema. Systematic phytochemical investigations of the plant identified a total of 132 compounds. These constituents include 54 lignans, 19 flavonoids, 31 organic acids, 18 alkaloids, and 10 other types of constituents. Within this group, 77 compounds are reported for the first time in Justicia procumbens, including 14 potential novel compounds.
