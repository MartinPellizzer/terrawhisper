Justicia procumbens

What's the taxonomical classification of Justicia procumbens?

Justicia procumbens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Justicia, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, procumbens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Justicia procumbens has a prostrate or decumbent growth habit characterized by slender, square-shaped stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are oppositely arranged, simple, and typically possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils and are usually white or pale pink with distinct purple spots on the lower lip. The plant produces small, dehiscent capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds following successful pollination. This herbaceous species generally remains low to the ground, forming spreading mats in shaded or moist environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds justiciidin A, justiciidin B, justiciidin C, procumbenoside A, and taiwaniin C. These specific secondary metabolites are responsible for many of the biological activities observed in the species. Researchers often study these molecules to understand their potential therapeutic applications in pharmacology. The presence of these diverse chemical structures highlights the complex chemical profile of the plant. Detailed analysis of these constituents helps scientists identify new pathways for drug development.

As discussed in a study, a new lignan glycoside named procumbenoside A, which is identified as 4-O-alpha-L-arabinopyranosyl-(1' "-->2' ')-beta-D-apiofuranosyldiphyllin (2), was isolated from the whole plant of Justicia procumbens. Along with this new compound, 11 known compounds were also isolated from the plant. Among these, justicidin A (1), diphyllin (3), and tuberculatin (4) demonstrated potent cytotoxic effects against various cancer cells in vitro. Furthermore, compounds 1 and 4 strongly enhanced tumor-necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) generation from mouse macrophage-like RAW 264.7 cells that were stimulated with lipopolysaccharide (LPS).
