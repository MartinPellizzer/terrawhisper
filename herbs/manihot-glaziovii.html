Manihot glaziovii has woody stems with palmate leaves characterized by three to five deeply lobed segments and a milky latex secretion throughout its tissues.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Manihot glaziovii can cause severe cyanide poisoning if the plant tissues are ingested due to the presence of cyanogenic glycosides. These compounds release hydrogen cyanide during digestion, which interferes with the body's ability to utilize oxygen at a cellular level. Symptoms of exposure typically include dizziness, headache, nausea, and rapid breathing. In cases of significant consumption, the toxicity can lead to convulsions, coma, or even death. Proper processing is essential to neutralize these chemical defenses before any part of the plant is consumed.
