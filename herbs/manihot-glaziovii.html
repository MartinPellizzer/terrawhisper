What's the taxonomical classification of Manihot glaziovii?

Manihot glaziovii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Malpighiales, it is a member of the family Euphorbiaceae. Specifically, the plant is identified by the genus Manihot and is distinguished as the species glaziovii.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe neurological damage, acute cyanide poisoning, and gastrointestinal distress if its toxic cyanogenic glycosides are ingested. These chemical compounds release hydrogen cyanide when the plant tissue is crushed or processed incorrectly. Symptoms of toxicity often include dizziness, rapid heartbeat, and vomiting shortly after consumption. Prolongical exposure or high doses can lead to respiratory failure and permanent damage to the central nervous system. Proper detoxification through extensive soaking and heating is required to minimize these life-threatening risks.
