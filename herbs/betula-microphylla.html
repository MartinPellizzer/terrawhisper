Betula microphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Betula microphylla?

Betula microphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the order Fagales and belongs to the family Betulaceae. Finally, it is identified within the genus Betula, specifically as the species microphylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Betula microphylla has small, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and a characteristic pointed apex. The leaf texture is typically thin and somewhat papery, often displaying a slightly lustrous surface. Its branches are relatively slender and frequently exhibit a light gray or silvery bark texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into tiny, winged nutlets for wind dispersal. This species is generally characterized by its compact growth habit and fine-textured foliage.
