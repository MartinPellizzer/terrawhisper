Campanula cana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Campanula cana has perennial, rhizomatous stems that produce basal leaves shaped like narrow, lanceolate blades and smaller, cauline leaves along the stem. The foliage is characterized by a grayish-green hue and a slightly pubescent texture that provides a soft appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically range from pale blue to violet in color. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils on slender pedicels and feature five distinct lobes at the mouth of the corolla. The plant often forms low-growing mats or clumps, making its morphological structure well-suited for rocky or alpine environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-elevation alpine regions of the western United States, specifically within the Rocky Mountains and the Sierra Nevada ranges. It grows primarily in rocky crevices and mountain meadows located at subalpine to alpine altitudes. Its range extends through various states including Colorado, Wyoming, Utah, and California. These populations are typically found in well-drained, stony soils characteristic of montane environments.
