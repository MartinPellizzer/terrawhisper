What is Ostrya virginiana?

Ostrya virginiana is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, belonging to the family Betulaceae.

It grows 10-20 meters tall, with a straight, smooth trunk, and a broad, rounded crown. The bark is grayish-brown, peeling off in horizontal strips, and the leaves are alternate, 5-10 cm long, with 5-9 lobes, and a 1-2 cm long petiole. The male catkins are 2-4 cm long, and the female catkins are 2-4 cm long, with 1-2 seeds per cone, each about 1 cm long.

The wood is valued for its strength and durability, often used in furniture-making and woodworking.
