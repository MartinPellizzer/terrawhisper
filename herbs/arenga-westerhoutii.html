What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arenga westerhoutii has a solitary, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, topped by a large crown of pinnate leaves measuring 3 to 4 meters in length.

Its inflorescence is branched and emerges from the leaf axils, bearing small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into woody fruits. These fruits are typically ovoid or ellipsoidal in shape and are characterized by a fibrous mesocarp.
