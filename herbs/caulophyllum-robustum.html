Caulophyllum robustum

What's the taxonomical classification of Caulophyllum robustum?

Caulophyllum robustum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Berberidaceae and is categorized under the genus Caulophyllum, specifically identified as the species robustum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caulophyllum robustum has large, pinnately compound leaves with numerous lanceolate leaflets and a sturdy, upright stem that can reach heights of up to one meter. During the flowering stage, the plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms eventually give way to distinctive blue or purple berries that grow in clusters along the stem. What are the therapeutic actions of Caulophyllum robustum?

Caulophyllum robustum has therapeutic properties as a potent uterotonic and emmenagogue used to stimulate uterine contractions and regulate menstrual cycles. This plant contains methyltryprostyltrimethylindolyl alkaloids which act directly on the smooth muscle of the uterus. By increasing blood flow to the pelvic region, it helps induce menses in cases of amenorrhea or delayed menstruation. Its emmenagogue actions make it a traditional remedy for addressing various gynecological irregularities. Physicians historically utilized its effects to assist in the management of difficult labors and postpartum recovery.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains malonyl-triterpene saponins, taspine, total alkaloids, triterpene saponins, and TG_s as its primary medicinal compounds. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the overall therapeutic profile of the botanical specimen. The presence of total alkaloids often indicates significant pharmacological activity within the plant. Furthermore, the triterpene saponins and their malonyl derivatives play crucial roles in its biological functions. TG_s also represent a distinct component of its complex chemical makeup.

As discussed in a study, the hair roots of Caulophyllum robustum contain malonyl-triterpene saponins (MTSs) that possess valuable bioactivities. Researchers utilized an energy-resolved technique involving in-source collision induced dissociation at 0, 25, 50 and 100 eV to analyze these compounds. This method collected precursor and fragment ion information from 4 energy-resolved MS spectra during a single run of 18 min. Through a flowchart involving a stepwise procedure from ESI-/ESI+-FT-MS(1, 1) to MS(1, 4) spectra, a total of 23 MTSs were discovered and tentatively identified. All of these 23 MTSs were potentially new compounds that had not been previously reported from Caulophyllum species.

What side effects this plant can have?
