What's the taxonomical classification of Phlomis pratensis?

Phlomis pratensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a representative of the family Lamiaceae. More specifically, this plant is categorized under the genus Phlomis, with its unique identity defined by the species epithet pratensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis pratensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, square-shaped stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and are covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are large, ovate to oblong, and possess a distinctly rugose or wrinkled texture with crenate margins. Toward the top of the plant, the leaves are arranged in dense, opposite whorls that serve as bracts for the flowers. The inflorescence consists of several verticillasters, which are ring-like clusters of tubular, yellowish-green flowers. Each individual blossom features a prominent two-lipped corolla, a defining trait of the Lamiaceae family.
