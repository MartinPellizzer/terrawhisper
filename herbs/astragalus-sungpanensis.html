Astragalus sungpanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus sungpanensis?

Astragalus sungpanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, it is placed within the genus Astragalus, with its specific identity defined by the species designation sungpanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus sungpanensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by a woody caudex, pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of leaflets, and pea-like flowers arranged in dense, capitate inflorescences. The leaflets are typically small and oblong, often displaying a slightly hairy texture on both surfaces. The calyx is covered in fine pubescence, which protects the developing floral structures. Its flowers exhibit the classic papilionaceous structure, featuring a prominent banner, two wings, and a keel. The legume pods are relatively small and dehisce to release numerous tiny seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Songpan County area within the Sichuan Province of China. It specifically inhabits subalpine meadow ecosystems located within the eastern edge of the Qinghai-Tibetan Plateau. These populations are restricted to specialized microhabitats characterized by cool temperatures and well-drained mountainous soils.
