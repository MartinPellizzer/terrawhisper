What's the taxonomical classification of Coleus esquirolii?

Coleus esquirolii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized within the family Lamiaceae, a group known for its diverse herbaceous plants. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its classification under the genus Coleus and the specific species epithet esquirolii.

Additionally, certain polyphenolic constituents may influence antioxidant enzymes to mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. This multifaceted interaction with molecular signaling proteins supports the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and pain.

      What are the herbal preparations of Coleus esquirolii?

      Coleus esquirolii is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using its leaves and stems. These traditional methods involve boiling the plant parts in water to extract concentrated medicinal compounds. For internal consumption, the liquid extract is often administered in small, measured doses. When applied externally, the crushed plant material is formed into a paste to treat skin irritations or localized inflammation. The specific preparation method chosen depends heavily on the intended therapeutic application.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Coleus esquirolii can cause skin irritation, redness, or allergic dermatitis upon direct contact with its foliage or stems. The plant contains phytochemicals that may trigger a localized inflammatory response in sensitive individuals. Contact with the sap should be avoided to prevent potential stinging or itching sensations on the skin. Some people might also experience eye irritation if the plant's juices accidentally touch the ocular area. While specific toxicity data is limited, standard precautions for ornamental coleus species suggest cautious handling to minimize exposure.
