Coleus esquirolii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coleus esquirolii has ovate to lanceolate leaves that typically measure two to four inches in length and feature deeply serrated margins with prominent, light-colored veins. The foliage displays a striking variegated pattern, often showcasing bright lime green or chartreuse hues contrasted against darker emerald tones. These leaves grow in opposite pairs along square, succulent stems that provide structural support for the herbaceous plant. The texture of the leaf surface is slightly velvety due to the presence of fine hairs. Small, tubular flowers may occasionally emerge from the terminal ends of the stems during peak growing conditions.
