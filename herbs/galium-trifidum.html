Galium trifidum

What's the taxonomical classification of Galium trifidum?

Galium trifidum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae and falls under the genus Galium, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species trifidum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Galium trifidum has whorled leaves consisting of four to eight narrow, linear leaflets that measure between 1 and 4 centimeters in length. The stems are typically square in cross-section and can grow in an upright or sprawling manner depending on the habitat. Small, inconspicuous white flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters during the summer months. These flowers transition into tiny, dry fruits that are characterized by having small hooked hairs to aid in seed dispersal. The overall plant structure is delicate and often forms dense mats in wetland environments.
