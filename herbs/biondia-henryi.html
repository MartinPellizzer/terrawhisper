Biondia henryi

What's the taxonomical classification of Biondia henryi?

Biondia henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Gentianales. This plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae, which is further refined into the genus Biondiia, with the specific designation of the species henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Biondia henryi has a woody, perennial habit characterized by slender stems, opposite leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary clusters.

The foliage typically presents an elliptical to oblong shape with acute tips and a smooth surface texture. Its floral structure consists of tiny petals that are often pale in color, emerging from nodes along the stem. The root system is adapted to its specific tropical habitat, providing stability for its delicate upright growth.

These morphological traits distinguish the species within its botanical family.

Biondia henryi is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using its various parts such as leaves, roots, and stems. What side-effects this plant can have?

Biondia henryi can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential toxicity if consumed in excessive or improper dosages. While some use the plant for medicinal purposes, unregulated consumption may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Individuals with sensitive skin might experience localized redness or itching upon contact with the plant's extracts. There is also a risk of systemic toxicity if the chemical compounds interact poorly with existing medications. Due to the lack of extensive human clinical trials, the long-term safety profile of this species remains largely unknown.
