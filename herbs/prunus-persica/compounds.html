Peach Tree (Prunus persica) Compounds

The primary compounds of the Peach Tree include flavonoids, which are antioxidants that support cardiovascular health. Phenolic acids contribute to anti-inflammatory properties, while tannins offer astringent and antimicrobial effects. Coumarin has anticoagulant properties, and glycosides may support heart function. Saponins, anthocyanins, terpenoids, essential oils, and alkaloids each contribute unique therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

Peach Tree (Prunus persica) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in the Peach Tree (Prunus persica). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cardiovascular and inflammatory health. These compounds are present in various parts of the peach tree, including leaves, bark, and fruit. Their presence enhances the therapeutic potential of peach-based herbal remedies.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in the Peach Tree (Prunus persica). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound is commonly used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and protective effects on cellular health.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Peach Tree (Prunus persica). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins are found in the bark, leaves, and fruit of the peach tree, making it useful in traditional herbal medicine for treating infections and skin conditions.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in the Peach Tree (Prunus persica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential to support circulatory health and reduce inflammation.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in the Peach Tree (Prunus persica). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Glycosides are commonly used in herbal medicine to support digestive and respiratory health. They are often extracted from the fruit and bark of the Peach Tree.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Peach Tree (Prunus persica). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. Saponins help in detoxification and may support digestive health. Their presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Peach Tree in herbal formulations.

7. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in the Peach Tree (Prunus persica). It contributes to the fruit's vibrant color and possesses antioxidant properties that support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation. These benefits make anthocyanin a valuable component in traditional herbal medicine derived from peach.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in the Peach Tree (Prunus persica). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of the Peach Tree (Prunus persica) contains volatile compounds like limonene and linalool. These components contribute to its aromatic properties and may have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. It is used in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in the Peach Tree (Prunus persica). While alkaloids are more commonly associated with other medicinal plants, some alkaloids may be present in trace amounts in peach tree extracts. Their role in the therapeutic properties of peach tree is not well established, but they may contribute to the plant's overall pharmacological profile.
