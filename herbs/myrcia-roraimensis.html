Myrcia roraimensis

Myrcia roraimensis, also known as the Roraima myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, native to the Guiana Shield.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The health benefits of Myrcia roraimensis include its ability to aid in the treatment of respiratory issues, such as bronchitis and asthma, as well as its potential to help lower blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

Herbal preparations of Myrcia roraimensis can be made into teas, infusions, and tinctures, and are often used in traditional medicine to promote overall well-being and immune function.
