Coptis omeiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Coptis omeiensis?

Coptis omeiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically situated within the genus Coptis, and is identified by the unique species name omeiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coptis omeiensis has a rhizomatous perennial structure characterized by basal leaves that are typically divided into three distinct leaflets, each further subdivided into numerous smaller, lanceolate lobes. The leaf margins are often serrated or dentate, providing a textured appearance to the foliage. From the center of these leaf clusters, slender flowering stalks emerge to support small, solitary flowers. These flowers possess bright yellow petals and are surrounded by green sepals. The plant's overall morphology is adapted for growth in moist, shaded mountain environments.
