Banana (Musa acuminata)

Banana (Musa acuminata) is a member of the Musaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, Pacific Islands, and Indo-Malayan Region. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and african traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 4 to 6 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface ranging from pale green to dark green and lower surface lighter in color, featuring prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, with a solitary arrangement, having six tepals that are usually white to yellowish in color, and a prominently exposed stamen cluster.
Stems
Cylindrical, herbaceous, erect growth habit with a sympodial branching pattern, smooth, glabrous surface, and a distinctive leaf sheath at the base.
