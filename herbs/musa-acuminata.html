Botanical Identification

Banana (Musa acuminata) is a species of flowering plant in the family Musaceae. It is commonly known as the dwarf banana or wild banana in various regions. This perennial plant is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by large, broad leaves and a clustered flower structure. It is also referred to by several synonyms in traditional herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The banana (Musa acuminata) contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds work synergistically to support overall health, though their exact mechanisms of action are still under research.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments and digestive issues.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and skin conditions.

Powder: Used for digestive ailments and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

Banana (Musa acuminata) is generally safe when consumed in normal dietary amounts. However, it should be avoided by individuals with known allergies to bananas or related fruits. Large quantities may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in sensitive individuals.
