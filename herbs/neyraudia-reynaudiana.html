Neyraudia reynaudiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Neyraudia reynaudiana?

Neyraudia reynaudiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Poales and the family Poaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Neyraudia. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, reynaudiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Neyraudia reynaudiana has an evergreen shrub morphology characterized by upright, spreading branches and lanceolate, leathery leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length.

The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and may appear slightly paler underneath. Small, white, tubular flowers emerge in dense, terminal panicles during the blooming season. These inflorescences are followed by small, fleshy drupes that serve as the plant's fruit.

The overall structure of the plant allows it to form dense, thicket-like masses in its natural habitat.

These compounds also interact with various cytokines to modulate the body's immune response during inflammation. Neyraudia reynaudiana has potent therapeutic properties acting as a nervine, antispasmodic, sedative, and anxiolytic agent to treat various neurological and muscular conditions.

    Neyraudia reynaudiana is prepared primarily as a decoction of its leaves or as a topical infusion for dermatological applications.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Neyraudia reynaudiana can cause severe skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and potential systemic toxicity if ingested or absorbed in large quantities. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger allergic reactions when the sap or foliage touches sensitive skin. Contact with the plant often results in redness, swelling, or painful rashes in susceptible individuals. If accidentally consumed, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress or more serious neurological effects depending on the dosage. Careful handling is essential to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
