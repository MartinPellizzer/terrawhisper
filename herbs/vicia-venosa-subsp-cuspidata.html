Vicia venosa subsp. cuspidata

Vicia venosa subsp.

cuspidata is a subspecies of the vetch plant, a legume native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating respiratory issues such as coughs and bronchitis, due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The health benefits of Vicia venosa subsp.

cuspidata include reducing inflammation, relieving congestion, and soothing sore throats, making it a popular herbal remedy for respiratory conditions.

In herbal preparations, the plant is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which can be consumed to harness its medicinal properties and alleviate respiratory symptoms.
