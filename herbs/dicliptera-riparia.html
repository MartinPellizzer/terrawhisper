Dicliptera riparia

What's the taxonomical classification of Dicliptera riparia?

Dicliptera riparia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Dicliptera, with its unique designation being the species riparia.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, inflammation, and various gastrointestinal ailments. The leaves are frequently prepared as a poultice to accelerate the healing of external sores and minor cuts. For internal issues, decoctions made from the plant material are traditionally administered to alleviate stomach discomfort. Some practitioners also utilize its extracts to manage symptoms associated with systemic inflammation. These medicinal applications are rooted in various traditional healing practices.
