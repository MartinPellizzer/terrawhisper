Securinega suffruticosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Securinega suffruticosa has woody, branched stems that form a suffruticose shrub reaching up to 1 meter in height, characterized by small, simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape. The leaf margins are typically entire, and the texture often appears slightly leathery or coriaceous. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, which eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes. These fruits transition in color from green to red or black upon ripening. The overall architecture of the plant reflects its adaptation to shrubby, understory environments.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Securinega suffruticosa targets phosphodiesterase enzymes, specifically inhibiting phosphodiesterase 4 (PDE4) and phosphodiesterase 3 (PDE3) to regulate intracellular cyclic nucleotide levels. This interaction increases the concentration of cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP) and cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP), which mediates its potent anti-inflammatory and vasodilatory effects. The plant's bioactive alkaloids, such as securinine, also interact with nicotinic acetylcholine receptors and various ion channels within the central nervous system. These molecular engagements contribute to its observed neuroprotective and anticonvulsant activities. Furthermore, secondary metabolites in the species have shown the ability to modulate inflammatory signaling pathways by inhibiting protein kinases.
