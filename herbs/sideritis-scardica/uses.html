Scardic Sideritis (Sideritis scardica) Uses

Scardic Sideritis is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, bronchitis, and inflamed throat. It helps alleviate symptoms of colds, asthma, and allergic rhinitis. Its anti-inflammatory properties support recovery from respiratory infections and reduce sore throat discomfort.

Scardic Sideritis (Sideritis scardica) Monograph

1. Cough

Sideritis scardica is used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps clear mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Traditionally prepared as a tea, it is valued for its calming effect on the throat and its ability to alleviate persistent coughing.

2. Asthma

Sideritis scardica has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties help reduce airway swelling and improve breathing. Often prepared as a tea, it is valued for its calming effect on the respiratory system. It is commonly used in herbal medicine to alleviate persistent coughing and shortness of breath.

3. Inflammation

Sideritis scardica is used to reduce inflammation due to its high content of flavonoids and phenolic compounds, which possess strong anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms of inflammatory conditions such as arthritis and respiratory infections when consumed as a herbal tea or extract. Its bioactive components modulate inflammatory pathways in the body, supporting natural healing processes.

4. Cold symptoms

Sideritis scardica is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its antispasmodic and expectorant properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, soothe sore throats, and ease coughing. Often prepared as a tea, it supports the body's natural defenses during colds and respiratory infections. Its mild warming effect also helps relieve chills and body aches.

5. Bronchitis

Sideritis scardica is traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing symptoms of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated airways. 6. Allergic rhinitis

8. Respiratory infections
