Carob (Ceratonia siliqua)

Carob (Ceratonia siliqua) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Mediterranean Region, Middle East, and North Africa. Traditionally, its fruits, seeds, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in mediterranean herbal traditions, european herbal medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 5 to 10 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with a smooth upper surface that is dark green and a lower surface that is pale green, featuring a prominent stomatal band along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are white to pale yellow with five petals arranged in a raceme, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry and bearing a distinctive swollen ovary at the base of each flower.
Stems
Woody, erect, branched, with smooth, ridged, and longitudinally fissured bark, and opposite, simple, and slightly pubescent leaf scars.
