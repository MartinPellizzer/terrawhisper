Pteridrys australis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteridrys australis has erect, woody rhizomes that produce large, pinnate fronds measuring between 30 and 100 centimeters in length. Each frond consists of multiple pinnae that are deeply lobed and taper toward the tip. The underside of the leaflets contains distinct sori arranged in rows, which are often protected by small, kidney-shaped indusia. These fronds emerge from a central crown and typically exhibit a dark green color with a slightly leathery texture. The overall structure of the plant allows it to form dense, spreading colonies in its natural habitat.
