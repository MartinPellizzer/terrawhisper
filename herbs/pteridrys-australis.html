What's the taxonomical classification of Pteridrys australis?

Pteridrys australis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic placement continues through the family Polypodiaceae, within which it is categorized under the genus Pteridrys. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, australis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteridrys australis has erect, woody rhizomes that produce large, pinnate fronds measuring between 30 and 100 centimeters in length. Each frond consists of multiple pinnae that are deeply lobed and taper toward the tip. The underside of the leaflets contains distinct sori arranged in rows, which are often protected by small, kidney-shaped indusia. These fronds emerge from a central crown and typically exhibit a dark green color with a slightly leathery texture.
