Coniogramme affinis

What's the taxonomical classification of Coniogramme affinis?

Coniogramme affinis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic placement continues within the family Pteridaceae, where it is categorized under the genus Coniogramme, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species affinis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coniogramme affinis has large, triangular to broadly ovate fronds characterized by a delicate, membranous texture and deeply pinnatifid to pinnate division patterns. The stipes are typically slender and dark, often possessing a distinct brownish or reddish hue at the base. Each pinna is further subdivided into narrow, lanceolate segments that create a fine, lace-like appearance when viewed closely. The sori are arranged in a characteristic pattern along the margins of the segments, often protected by a specialized reflexed margin known as a false indusium. This combination of thin lamina and marginal spore structures defines its distinct morphological profile within the genus.
