Terminalia Belerica Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Terminalia belerica, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or bishtak, is a medicinal plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is valued for its rich content of bioactive compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and polyphenols, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

The herb is known to support digestive health, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antioxidant and antimicrobial effects.

It can be prepared as an infusion, decoction, or powder for use in traditional remedies.

Bioactive Constituents

Terminalia belerica cardiac glycosides have been identified as one of the key medicinal constituents of the herb, known for their potential cardiovascular benefits.

These compounds are believed to exert positive inotropic effects, enhancing the force of heart muscle contractions, which may aid in the treatment of heart failure and other cardiac disorders. In addition to cardiac glycosides, Terminalia belerica contains ellagic acid, a polyphenolic compound with strong antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body.

Tannins present in the herb are known for their astringent properties, which can help in the treatment of gastrointestinal issues and inflammation. Furthermore, gallic acid, another important constituent, is recognized for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant activities, contributing to the overall therapeutic value of Terminalia belerica.

Together, these compounds make Terminalia belerica a valuable herb in traditional and modern medicinal practices.

Medicinal Parts

Terminalia belerica leaf is widely used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties, particularly in Ayurveda and Unani systems.

The leaves are known for their astringent, antiseptic, and anti-inflammatory effects, making them useful in treating skin conditions, wounds, and digestive disorders. They contain various bioactive compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to their medicinal value. In some regions, the leaves are also used to make herbal teas that help in managing diabetes and reducing cholesterol levels.

Additionally, the leaves are sometimes applied topically to reduce inflammation and promote healing in cases of infections or ulcers.
