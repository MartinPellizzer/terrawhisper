What's the taxonomical classification of Lepisorus thunbergianus?

Lepisorus thunbergianus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is organized into the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Polypodiaceae, within the genus Lepisorus, and ultimately identifies it as the specific species thunbergianus.

      What are the herbal preparations of Lepisorus thunbergianus?

      Lepisorus thunbergianus is prepared primarily as a decoction or an aqueous infusion where the plant material is boiled or steeped in water to extract its bioactive compounds. This liquid extraction method is the most common way to utilize the fern in traditional medicinal practices. Some practitioners may also utilize the crushed fresh leaves to create a topical poultice for direct application to the skin. In certain regional applications, the dried plant matter is ground into a fine powder to be incorporated into other medicinal mixtures. These various forms allow for both internal consumption and external healing treatments depending on the specific ailment being addressed.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lepisorus thunbergianus can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized swelling if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive tissue. While specific toxicological studies on this particular fern species are limited, many similar ferns contain irritants that trigger redness or itching. Individuals with known sensitivities to tropical vegetation should exercise caution when handling the foliage. Contact with the sap may lead to a delayed hypersensitivity reaction in some people. Always wash your hands thoroughly after touching the plant to minimize any risk of accidental exposure to the eyes or mouth.
