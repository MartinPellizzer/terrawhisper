Anemone delavayi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anemone delavayi has perennial herbaceous stems that reach heights of approximately 10 to 30 centimeters, supporting compound leaves with deeply lobed or divided leaflets and solitary terminal flowers. The foliage is characterized by a basal rosette of leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. Each flower features several spreading petals that are usually white or pale pink in color, often accompanied by numerous central stamens. The plant produces small, aggregate achenes that form from the central carpels after successful pollination. This species is structurally adapted to thrive in rocky or mountainous terrains where its low-growing habit provides stability.
