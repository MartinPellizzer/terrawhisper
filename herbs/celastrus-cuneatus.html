Celastrus cuneatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus cuneatus?

Celastrus cuneatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Celastrales and is a member of the family Celastraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Celastrus and the species cuneatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus cuneatus has opposite, simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a distinctively wedge-shaped base.

The foliage typically displays a smooth texture and a lustrous green surface that becomes more prominent during maturity. Its woody stems are characterized by a somewhat irregular branching pattern and a grayish bark texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters, eventually giving way to distinctive capsule-like fruits.

These fruits often split open to reveal bright, fleshy seeds that serve as a key identification feature.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Celastrus cuneatus targets cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) enzymes, various proinflammatory cytokines, and specific inflammatory mediators to produce its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive compounds like alkaloids and flavonoids that inhibit the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. By suppressing COX-2 activity, these phytochemicals effectively reduce the synthesis of prostaglandins responsible for pain and swelling. Additionally, the extracts interfere with the expression of tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6 in cellular models. These multi-target interactions allow the plant to exhibit significant anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celastrus cuneatus?

Celastrus cuneatus has therapeutic properties that act as a bitter, stomachic, aperient, and emetic agent to treat various digestive ailments.

The plant contains bitter compounds that stimulate the secretion of gastric juices to improve digestion. When taken in larger doses, it functions as a stomachic to restore appetite and regulate gastrointestinal function. It also serves as an aperient to provide a gentle laxative effect for relieving constipation.

In certain concentrations, its powerful action can act as an emetic to induce vomiting for clearing the stomach.

What are the herbal preparations of Celastrus cuneatus?

Celastrus cuneatus is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste using the dried bark or leaves of the plant. These liquid preparations are often used to extract bioactive compounds for internal consumption to address various ailments. When applied externally, the crushed plant material acts as a poultice to treat skin conditions or localized inflammation. Some traditional practitioners also utilize the plant in the form of a tincture by soaking it in alcohol to ensure long-term stability. The choice of preparation method depends entirely on whether the intended therapeutic effect is systemic or localized.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Celastrus cuneatus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea if any part of the plant is ingested. The presence of toxic alkaloids in the tissues makes consumption highly dangerous for humans and animals alike. Contact with the plant may also lead to skin irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Symptoms of poisoning can escalate quickly depending on the amount consumed. It is vital to avoid all contact and ingestion to prevent these adverse effects.
