What's the taxonomical classification of Schima noronhae?

Schima noronhae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Ericales, it falls under the family Theaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Schima and is specifically designated as the species noronhae.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schima noronhae has elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are dark green on the upper surface, paler beneath, and arranged alternately along its woody branches. The leaf margins are entire and often exhibit a slightly revolute edge. Its flowers are characterized by prominent white petals and numerous long, spreading stamens that create a brush-like appearance. The plant typically develops small, woody capsules that contain seeds used for reproduction. These structural features are consistent with its classification within the Theaceae family.
