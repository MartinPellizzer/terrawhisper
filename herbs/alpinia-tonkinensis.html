Alpinia tonkinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Alpinia tonkinensis?

Alpinia tonkinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned within the order Zingiberales. It further falls under the family Zingiberaceae, which characterizes its botanical lineage. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Alpinia, and its unique species designation, tonkinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia tonkinensis has large, oblong leaves that grow from thick, creeping rhizomes and produce upright, terminal inflorescences containing white or pale pink flowers. The foliage is characterized by prominent parallel venation and a glossy texture that covers the broad, lanceolate blades. These plants develop sturdy, fleshy stems that emerge from an underground network of roots. The flowers are typically clustered in spikes and feature delicate petals arranged around a central stamen. During the flowering stage, the plant displays a striking contrast between its deep green leaves and its light-colored floral structures.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South America, specifically found within the mountainous terrains of Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. It thrives primarily in high-altitude cloud forests and moist montane environments where humidity is consistently high. The species is typically distributed along the eastern slopes of the Andes mountains. Within these specific ecosystems, it occupies shaded understories that provide the necessary moisture for its growth. According to a study published by "Journal of Asian natural products research", two new compounds, 2 alpha-(p-hydroxycinnamoyl) cineole (2) and (E)-15-nor-16-oxo-8(17),12-labdadiene (4), were isolated from Alpinia tonkinesis Gangep.
