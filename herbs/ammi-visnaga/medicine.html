What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Ammi Visnaga (Khella)?

Ammi visnaga has health benefits such as treating respiratory issues, relieving eye strain, and alleviating menstrual cramps.

Its medicinal constituents include furanochromones, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which are responsible for its pharmacological activities. The herb is prepared in various forms, including capsules, tablets, and tinctures, and can also be consumed as a tea or infusion. However, khella may cause gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, or interact with certain medications when taken in excess.

To ensure safe use, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using this herb, especially if you have any underlying medical conditions or are taking prescribed medications.

What are the health benefits of Ammi visnaga?

Ammi visnaga has health benefits such as relieving digestive issues like constipation and diarrhea, as well as reducing inflammation in the respiratory tract.

Its anti-inflammatory properties also make it effective in treating conditions like asthma, bronchitis, and other breathing disorders.

Additionally, khella is used to treat urinary problems, including kidney stones and gallstones.

What are the active constituents of Ammi visnaga?

The health benefits of Ammi visnaga comes from its active constituents, such as alkaloids like visnagin and khellin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Visnagin has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects, while khellin exhibits vasodilatory and bronchodilatory properties. Additionally, the plant contains other bioactive compounds like flavonoids and coumarins, which contribute to its overall therapeutic profile.

These constituents work together to provide relief from various health conditions, including asthma, hypertension, and menstrual cramps.

What are the medicinal preparations of Ammi visnaga?

Ammi visnaga has different medicinal preparations, such as dried roots, leaves, and fruit, which are used to treat various ailments.

The dried root is used in the form of capsules or powder to alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), while the leaf extract is used topically to soothe skin irritations and wounds. The fruit pulp is used to prepare an infusion that helps relieve menstrual cramps, bloating, and other symptoms associated with PMS.

Additionally, khella oil is used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and reduce stress.

What are the possible side effect of using Ammi visnaga improperly?

Improper use of Ammi visnaga increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as nausea and vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramps, and abdominal pain.

Additionally, users may experience dizziness, headaches, fatigue, insomnia, and allergic reactions like hives and itching.

In rare cases, it can cause blood clotting disorders, liver damage, and kidney problems, leading to serious complications if left untreated or monitored improperly.

What precautions to take when using Ammi visnaga medicinally?

Before using Ammi visnaga for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and duration of treatment.

Additionally, it is essential to monitor blood pressure regularly, as excessive use can lead to hypertension.

Furthermore, patients with kidney or liver disease should exercise caution due to potential interactions with medications and decreased elimination rates.
