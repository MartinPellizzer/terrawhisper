What is Adenocarpus cincinnatus?

Adenocarpus cincinnatus is a shrub or small tree in the family Fabaceae, endemic to the Cape Provinces of South Africa.

It grows up to 2 meters tall, with a woody stem and a leafy crown. The leaves are compound, pinnate, and alternate, consisting of 3-7 pairs of leaflets that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape and measure 1-3 cm long. The plant produces yellow flowers in a terminal raceme, each flower with five petals and a yellow banner, keel, and wings.

The fruits are papilionaceous, containing one seed per pod.
