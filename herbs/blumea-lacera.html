Blumea lacera

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea lacera?

Blumea lacera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the biological lineage of the order Asterales. It is further categorized under the family Asteraceae, a large group of flowering plants, and specifically falls within the genus Blumea. Ultimately, its unique taxonomic identity is defined by its specific designation as the species lacera.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Blumea lacera contains a diverse profile of bioactive secondary metabolites including sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, triterpenoids, and various essential oils. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. Research indicates that the diterpenoid compounds found within the leaves play a significant role in its traditional medicinal applications. The presence of specific terpenoids also contributes to the plant's ability to combat various fungal and bacterial pathogens. These complex organic molecules work synergistically to provide therapeutic benefits in ethnobotanical practices.

What are the therapeutic actions of Blumea lacera?

Blumea lacera has potent anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties derived from its rich concentration of bioactive compounds like terpenoids and flavonoids. These chemical constituents allow the plant to effectively combat various bacterial infections and reduce swelling in inflamed tissues. It is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin ailments, wounds, and gastrointestinal disturbances. The antioxidant activity helps protect cells from oxidative stress, potentially preventing cellular damage. Additionally, its medicinal extracts may assist in regulating metabolic processes and supporting the immune system.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, fever, stomach aches, and various respiratory ailments. Its leaves are frequently applied topically as a poultice to soothe inflammation and accelerate the healing of cuts or sores. When prepared as a decoction, the plant helps reduce body temperature during febrile episodes. It is also traditionally consumed to alleviate digestive distress and gastrointestinal discomfort. Additionally, the botanical extracts are utilized to clear congestion and manage symptoms associated with coughs and colds.
