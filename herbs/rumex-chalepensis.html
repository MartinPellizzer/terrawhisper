What's the taxonomical classification of Rumex chalepensis?

Rumex chalepensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls into the order Caryophyllales and the family Polygonaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Rumex, with its specific designation being the species chalepensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rumex chalepensis has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by smooth, reddish stems that can reach heights of up to 100 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and ovate to lanceolate in shape, featuring entire or slightly wavy margins and a distinctively long petiole. As the plant matures, the cauline leaves become progressively smaller and more lanceolate in form. The inflorescence consists of dense, terminal panicles composed of numerous small, greenish flowers.
