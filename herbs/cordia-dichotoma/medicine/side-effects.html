10 Possible Side Effects Of Cordia Dichotoma

Cordia dichotoma has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can be caused by consuming excessive amounts or combining it with other medications.

For example, a person taking Cordia dichotoma for its purported anti-inflammatory properties may experience worsening symptoms of stomach ulcers or interact with their blood thinners, leading to excessive bleeding.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Cordia dichotoma causes allergic reactions due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains various allergenic compounds, including proteins and glycoproteins, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals. This reaction may manifest as symptoms such as hives, itching, swelling, and respiratory distress.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils and alkaloids can also contribute to allergic reactions, making it essential to exercise caution when handling or consuming Cordia dichotoma products.

2. Induces digestive issues

Cordia dichotoma induces digestive issues due to its potential interaction with gut flora and enzymes.

The plant's phytochemicals may alter the balance of beneficial bacteria in the gut, leading to changes in digestion, absorption, and nutrient utilization.

Additionally, Cordia dichotoma's saponins and tannins can inhibit the activity of digestive enzymes, further contributing to digestive issues such as bloating, cramps, and diarrhea.

3. Triggers skin irritation

Cordia dichotoma triggers skin irritation due to its high concentration of latex and saponins.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction, leading to redness, itching, and rashes on the skin. The latex in Cordia dichotoma is particularly potent, releasing irritant chemicals that can penetrate the skin and trigger an inflammatory response.

As a result, some individuals may experience discomfort or irritation after using this plant-based remedy.

4. Enhances blood pressure

Cordia dichotoma enhances blood pressure due to its rich concentration of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and alkaloids.

These phytochemicals stimulate the production of nitric oxide in the body, which relaxes blood vessels and increases blood flow. This, in turn, leads to an elevation in blood pressure.

Additionally, the herb's ability to regulate cortisol levels and improve cardiovascular function can further contribute to this increase in blood pressure.

5. Increases heart rate

Cordia dichotoma increases heart rate due to its stimulatory properties.

The compound cordifol, present in the plant's leaves and stems, acts on the cardiovascular system, enhancing sympathetic nervous system activity and increasing cardiac output. This results in an increase in heart rate, which can be attributed to the direct stimulation of beta-adrenergic receptors by cordifol.

As a result, individuals may experience palpitations or rapid heartbeat when using Cordia dichotoma.

6. Stimulates kidney stones

Cordia dichotoma stimulates kidney stones due to its high concentration of oxalates.

The plant's leaves and fruit are rich in oxalic acid, a compound that can increase the risk of developing kidney stones by increasing the amount of calcium and other minerals in the urine.

Additionally, the plant's diuretic properties may also contribute to this side effect, as increased urine production can lead to an imbalance of electrolytes and mineral levels in the body.

7. Causes dizziness often

Cordia dichotoma causes dizziness often due to its ability to affect the nervous system.

The herb is believed to contain compounds that alter the balance of neurotransmitters in the brain, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and disorientation. Additionally, Cordia dichotoma may cause changes in blood pressure, which can also contribute to dizziness in some individuals.

As a result, users may experience episodes of dizziness when taking this herb.

8. Produces stomach upset

Cordia dichotoma produces stomach upset.

This is due to its bitter and astringent properties, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract and disrupt normal digestion.

Additionally, the plant's tannins may also contribute to this side effect by reducing the absorption of nutrients and altering the natural balance of gut flora.

9. Relaxes muscles excessively

Cordia dichotoma relaxes muscles excessively due to its containing compounds that interact with the body's nervous system.

The alkaloids and saponins present in the plant can stimulate the release of neurotransmitters such as GABA, which has a calming effect on muscle contractions.

This excessive relaxation can lead to feelings of lethargy, weakness, or even sedation in some individuals.

10. Slows down breathing

Cordia dichotoma slows down breathing due to its ability to induce sedation and relaxation.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and glycosides, interact with the body's nervous system, causing a decrease in respiratory rate and depth.

This reduction in breathing frequency can lead to feelings of drowsiness and lethargy, as the body responds to the plant's calming effects.
