Daucus Carota: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Daucus carota, commonly known as Queen Anne's lace, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is valued for its edible roots and delicate, lace-like flowers, and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

The herb offers various health benefits, including supporting digestion, reducing inflammation, and promoting respiratory health.

Its therapeutic actions include antispasmodic, diuretic, and expectorant effects, while its bioactive constituents include flavonoids, coumarins, and essential oils.
