Daucus Carota Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Daucus carota, commonly known as Queen Anne's lace, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its delicate, lace-like white flowers and edible roots.

This herb is known for its potential health benefits, including its antioxidant properties, support for digestive health, and possible anti-inflammatory effects.

The bioactive constituents in Daucus carota include flavonoids, coumarins, and volatile oils, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and decoctions can be made from its roots or leaves to harness its medicinal properties.
