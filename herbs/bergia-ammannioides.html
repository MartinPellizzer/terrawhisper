Bergia ammannioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bergia ammannioides has a small, creeping, branched rhizomatous structure with tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. The plant typically grows in submerged or semi-aquatic environments, forming dense green mats. Its leaves are characterized by a single layer of cells, often appearing translucent or dark green depending on light exposure. Small, inconspicuous reproductive structures called archegonia and antheridia are produced on the underside of the leaves. This bryophyte lacks true roots, instead utilizing rhizoids for attachment to the substrate.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across India, Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Thailand, and parts of Malaysia. It thrives primarily in freshwater habitats such as shallow ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams where it can form dense submerged mats. The species is frequently found in areas with soft substrates and high nutrient levels that support its aquatic lifestyle. Its presence is often tied to seasonal water levels in low-lying wetland ecosystems throughout these specific geographic zones. Due to its specific environmental requirements, its distribution remains restricted to these warm, aquatic environments.

Bergia ammannioides is prepared as a decoction, a paste, or a juice extracted from the fresh plant parts for various medicinal applications.
