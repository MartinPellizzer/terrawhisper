Eggplant (Solanum melongena) Compounds

Eggplant contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which are antioxidants that support heart health. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins and anthocyanins provide astringent effects and color. Glycosides and saponins have detoxifying and immune-supporting roles. Coumarin, terpenoids, essential oils, and alkaloids also contribute to its medicinal profile.

Eggplant (Solanum melongena) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Eggplant (Solanum melongena). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting cardiovascular and immune health. These compounds help neutralize free radicals and may reduce the risk of chronic diseases.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Eggplant (Solanum melongena). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body. This compound supports various health benefits associated with consuming eggplant, including anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular protective effects.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Eggplant (Solanum melongena). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Eggplant (Solanum melongena), is a powerful antioxidant that contributes to the plant's vibrant color. It helps combat oxidative stress and inflammation, supporting cardiovascular and immune health. This compound is particularly abundant in the skin and flesh of the eggplant.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Eggplant (Solanum melongena). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in eggplant may support cardiovascular health and help manage conditions like hypertension. These compounds are part of the plant's natural defense mechanisms and are beneficial in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Eggplant (Solanum melongena). It exhibits mild anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponin contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and immune support. It is also known to enhance the absorption of certain nutrients.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Eggplant (Solanum melongena). It contributes to the plant's potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant effects. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and aid in reducing oxidative stress within the body.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Eggplant (Solanum melongena) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. They are found in various parts of the plant, including the skin and seeds, and are studied for their potential therapeutic benefits in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Eggplant (Solanum melongena) contains volatile compounds like methyl eugenol and limonene. These compounds exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They are used in herbal medicine to support respiratory and digestive health. The essential oil is often extracted through steam distillation from the leaves and flowers.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Eggplant (Solanum melongena) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds with potential pharmacological effects. These compounds may contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though research on their specific therapeutic roles is limited. They are found in various parts of the plant and are part of the broader biochemical profile of Eggplant.
