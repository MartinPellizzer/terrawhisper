10 Possible Side Effects Of Solanum Melongena

Solanum melongena has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with medications.

These issues arise from consuming excessive amounts, having a pre-existing allergy, or combining it with other herbs.

For instance, an individual may experience severe itching, swelling, or anaphylaxis after consuming eggplant, which can hinder daily activities, lead to missed work or school, and cause financial strain due to medical expenses.

1. Causes stomach upset

Solanum melongena causes stomach upset in some individuals due to its high content of solanine and chaconine.

These glycoalkaloids can stimulate the production of gastric acid, leading to heartburn, bloating, and discomfort.

Additionally, the presence of tannins in eggplant may slow down digestion, causing stomach upset and potentially exacerbate existing gastrointestinal issues.

2. Produces allergic reactions

Solanum melongena produces allergic reactions due to the presence of a protein called solanine, which is found in its leaves, stems, and unripe fruit.

This allergen can cause symptoms such as itching, hives, and swelling when it comes into contact with sensitive individuals through skin exposure or ingestion.

Additionally, some people may experience respiratory issues like asthma or congestion due to inhaling eggplant particles or consuming the fruit's seeds.

3. Triggers blood pressure increase

Solanum melongena triggers blood pressure increase due to its high content of solanine and choline.

These compounds can cause a sudden spike in blood pressure levels, potentially leading to hypertension. Additionally, the flavonoids and phenolic acids present in eggplant may further contribute to this effect by increasing peripheral resistance and cardiac output.

This increased pressure can lead to cardiovascular issues if left unchecked.

4. Induces skin rashes

Solanum melongena induces skin rashes due to its glycoalkaloids and saponins content.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction or irritation on the skin, leading to the development of rashes, itching, and redness.

The exact mechanism is not fully understood, but it's believed that the breakdown of these compounds in the body triggers an immune response, resulting in an inflammatory response that manifests as a skin rash.

5. Affects digestion slowly

Solanum melongena affects digestion slowly due to its high fiber and starch content.

The fiber can slow down the movement of food through the digestive system, leading to feelings of bloating and discomfort, while the starch can cause an increase in blood sugar levels.

Additionally, some people may experience difficulty absorbing nutrients from the protein and fat present in eggplant, further affecting digestion speed.

6. Causes dizziness suddenly

Solanum melongena causes dizziness suddenly due to its high levels of glycoalkaloids, particularly solanine.

This compound can disrupt the balance of electrolytes in the body, leading to a sudden drop in blood pressure and subsequent dizziness.

Additionally, the potassium content in eggplant may also play a role in causing dizziness by interfering with the normal functioning of the nervous system.

7. Increases heart rate rapidly

Solanum melongena increases heart rate rapidly due to its high concentration of solanine and chaconine, two glycoalkaloids that can stimulate the cardiovascular system.

These compounds can cause an increase in sympathetic nervous activity, leading to tachycardia or rapid heartbeat, as the body's "fight or flight" response is triggered.

8. Activates respiratory issues

Solanum melongena activates respiratory issues due to its high content of solanine and chaconine, which are toxic glycoalkaloids.

These compounds can cause inflammation and constriction of the airways, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

The allergenic properties of these chemicals can also trigger asthma attacks or exacerbate existing respiratory conditions in susceptible individuals.

9. Disrupts sleep patterns

Solanum melongena disrupts sleep patterns due to its high content of nicotine and other alkaloids, which can stimulate the nervous system and interfere with normal sleep-wake cycles.

The plant's active compounds, such as solanine and chaconine, can also cause increased alertness and energy, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

10. Interacts with medications poorly

Solanum melongena interacts with medications poorly due to its high content of glycosides and anthocyanins.

These compounds can bind to medications in the gut, reducing their absorption and effectiveness. Additionally, Solanum melongena's flavonoids may inhibit certain enzymes involved in drug metabolism, further compromising medication efficacy.

This interaction can lead to reduced therapeutic outcomes or increased risk of adverse reactions when taking multiple medications simultaneously.
