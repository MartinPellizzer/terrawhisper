10 Possible Side Effects Of Solanum Melongena (Eggplant)

Solanum melongena has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with medications.

These issues arise from consuming excessive amounts, having a pre-existing allergy, or combining it with other herbs.

For instance, an individual may experience severe itching, swelling, or anaphylaxis after consuming eggplant, which can hinder daily activities, lead to missed work or school, and cause financial strain due to medical expenses.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Solanum melongena if used imporperly.

1. Induces vomiting frequently

Solanum melongena causes stomach upset in some individuals due to its high content of solanine and chaconine.

These glycoalkaloids can stimulate the production of gastric acid, leading to heartburn, bloating, and discomfort.

Additionally, the presence of tannins in eggplant may slow down digestion, causing stomach upset and potentially exacerbate existing gastrointestinal issues.

Solanum melongena produces allergic reactions due to the presence of a protein called solanine, which is found in its leaves, stems, and unripe fruit.

This allergen can cause symptoms such as itching, hives, and swelling when it comes into contact with sensitive individuals through skin exposure or ingestion.

Additionally, some people may experience respiratory issues like asthma or congestion due to inhaling eggplant particles or consuming the fruit's seeds.
