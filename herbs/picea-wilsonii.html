Picea wilsonii

What's the taxonomical classification of Picea wilsonii?

Picea wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida, the subclass Pinidae, and the order Pinales. As a member of the family Pinaceae, it is situated within the genus Picea, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as wilsonii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Picea wilsonii contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including terpenoids, resin acids, and phenolic compounds that serve as its primary medicinal constituents. These chemical components are largely concentrated within the tree's resin and essential oils. The terpenoids found in the species often exhibit significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the presence of specific diterpenes may contribute to its traditional uses in soothing respiratory ailments. Research continues to explore how these complex organic molecules interact with biological systems for therapeutic potential.

What are the therapeutic actions of Picea wilsonii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various respiratory ailments including coughs, asthma, and bronchitis through its medicinal properties. The extracts from the needles and bark are often employed to alleviate inflammation within the lungs and airways. Its bioactive compounds help reduce congestion and soothe irritated mucous membranes during seasonal illnesses. Traditional applications also focus on its potential to combat minor infections and promote general respiratory health. Such uses stem from the plant's natural chemical composition which supports the body's healing processes.
