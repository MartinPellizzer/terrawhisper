Picea wilsonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Picea wilsonii has a conical to narrowly pyramidal growth habit characterized by spreading branches and short, stiff, needle-like leaves. The foliage consists of dark green, acerose needles that are typically four-angled and measure approximately 10 to 20 millimeters in length. These needles are arranged spirally around the twigs but often appear somewhat two-ranked due to the spreading nature of the branches. The bark on mature specimens becomes scaly and brownish-gray in color. Small, ovoid cones emerge from the branches, eventually turning woody as they mature.
