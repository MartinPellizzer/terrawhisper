Picea wilsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida, the subclass Pinidae, and the order Pinales.     What are the herbal preparations of Picea wilsonii?

    Picea wilsonii is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or tinctures using the needles, bark, or resinous parts of the tree. The needles are often steeped in hot water to create a tea that captures the plant's aromatic volatile oils. Extracts derived from the bark or resin can also be processed into concentrated tinctures for more potent medicinal applications. These various liquid forms allow for the efficient extraction of bioactive compounds like terpenes and flavonoids. Practitioners may also use the resin in topical applications or as a component in specialized herbal blends.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Picea wilsonii can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, redness, and itching if an individual comes into direct contact with its needles or bark. While the plant is primarily used for ornamental purposes, inhaling high concentrations of pollen may trigger respiratory irritation or seasonal allergy symptoms in sensitive people. Some individuals might also experience mild eye irritation if airborne particles from the tree drift into their vision. There is currently limited clinical research regarding the internal toxicity of this species, so ingestion should be strictly avoided. Always exercise caution when handling coniferous plants to prevent unexpected physical discomfort.
