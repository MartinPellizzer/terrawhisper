Clerodendrum serratum

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum serratum?

Clerodendrum serratum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Lamiaceae, falling under the genus Clerodendrum, and is specifically identified by the species name serratum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum serratum has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape and characterized by prominent serrated margins and a dull green surface. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub or small tree reaching heights of up to three meters. Its stems are woody at the base and often feature a slightly hairy texture. The inflorescences consist of terminal panicles bearing small, white, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually give way to small, fleshy, black drupes that serve as the plant's fruit.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across India, Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Thailand, and parts of Indonesia. It thrives predominantly in moist, low-altitude environments such as forest edges, riverbanks, and disturbed roadside areas. While its primary range is centered in the Indian subcontinent, certain populations have expanded into other tropical zones due to favorable climatic conditions. The species is frequently found in regions characterized by high humidity and seasonal rainfall patterns. Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of warm, tropical climates that support its rapid growth.

What are the pharmacological activities of Clerodendrum serratum?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, anti-arthritic, anti-edematous, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties. Its chemical constituents work to reduce swelling and mitigate the painful symptoms associated with joint inflammation and various rheumatic conditions. The plant also demonstrates a strong ability to inhibit the growth of diverse pathogenic microorganisms and combat viral infections. These diverse biological actions suggest that the species holds considerable potential for developing new therapeutic agents. Such multifaceted medicinal capabilities make it a subject of intense interest in ethnopharmacological research.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in pharmacology", Clerodendrum serratum possesses unique anti-inflammatoy and anti-arthritic properties. HPTLC analysis revealed the presence of ursolic acid with an Rf value of 0.38 and a quantified amount of 0.03% w/w. Further HPLC analysis showed an assay content for ursolic acid of 0.059% with a relative standard deviation of less than 2.5% and spike recovery between 95-110%. The aqueous extract exhibited COX-2 and TNF-alpha inhibition with significant results for both parameters where p < 0.0001. This plant extract showed reclamation of arthritis regarding paw edema, arthritic score, joint diameter, and body weight. Additionally, the plant extract exhibited in-vitro anti-inflammatoy activity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds hispidulin, luteolin, salvigenin, uncinaone, and saponins. These diverse phytochemicals contribute to the significant therapeutic potential observed in Clerodendrum serratum extracts. Specifically, the flavones hispidulin and luteolin are known for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of salvigenin and uncinaone further enhances the bioactive profile of the species. Additionally, the saponins found within the plant contribute to its various biological activities and traditional medicinal uses.

According to a study published by "Microbial pathogenesis", Clerodendrum serratum leaf extract contains five major phytoconstituent compounds including Hispidulin, Luteolin, Salvigenin, Pectolinarigenin, and Uncinaone. These bioactive compounds were analyzed through in silico molecular docking with critical biofilm-associated targets such as FadD32, InhA, and MmpL3. The analysis showed that these key phytoconstituents demonstrated strong binding affinities with a docking score of up to -11.0 kcal/mol. This research highlights the anti-biofilm potential of Clerodendrum serratum in targeting resilient mycobacterial biofilms.
