Clerodendrum serratum

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum serratum?

Clerodendrum serratum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Lamiales. It is a member of the family Lamiaceae, falling under the genus Clerodendrum, and is specifically identified by the species name serratum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum serratum has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape and characterized by prominent serrated margins and a dull green surface. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub or small tree reaching heights of up to three meters. Its stems are woody at the base and often feature a slightly hairy texture. The inflorescences consist of terminal panicles bearing small, white, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually give way to small, fleshy, black drupes that serve as the plant's fruit.
