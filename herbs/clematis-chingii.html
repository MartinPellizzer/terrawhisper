Clematis chingii

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis chingii?

Clematis chingii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Clematis. Ultimately, its specific scientific designation is defined by its species name, chingii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis chingii has oppositely arranged, trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to elliptical leaflets with entire margins and acute tips. The plant features woody, climbing stems that utilize petioles to scramble over surrounding vegetation. Its flowers are characterized by four to six large, petaloid sepals that typically exhibit a pale violet or bluish-purple hue. These blossoms are supported by long pedicels and often emerge in small clusters or solitary arrangements. The reproductive structures include central clusters of numerous stamens that surround the carpels.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It primarily inhabits temperate climates located at moderate elevations. These specimens are typically found growing in rocky crevices or along the edges of mountain forests. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-drained, humus-rich soil and a location that provides full sun to partial shade with cool, moist roots and warm foliage.

It thrives when planted at the base of a sturdy support or trellis to allow its climbing vines to ascend. Regular watering is essential to keep the soil consistently damp without becoming waterlogged.
