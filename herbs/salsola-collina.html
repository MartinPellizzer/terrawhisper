Salsola collina

What's the taxonomical classification of Salsola collina?

Salsola collina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually placing it in the order Caryophyllales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Amaranthaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Salsola, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name collina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salsola collina has succulent, cylindrical to subcylindrical branches that are typically branched from the base and possess small, scale-like leaves. These leaves are fleshy and imbricate, meaning they overlap like shingles to minimize water loss. The plant often exhibits a compact, shrubby growth habit with branches that may be slightly woody at the base. Small, inconspicuous flowers are tucked within the axils of the scale-like leaves. The fruit is a dry, many-seeded utricle enclosed by persistent, bladder-like perianth segments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Salsola collina?

This plant has documented analgesic, antihypertensive, antioxidant, carminative, and sedative pharmacological activities that contribute to its medicinal value. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, potentially protecting cells from oxidative damage. The plant demonstrates analgesic effects by reducing pain sensation and exhibits sedative qualities that can assist in calming the nervous system. Furthermore, it serves as an antihypertensive agent to help regulate blood pressure levels. It also acts as a carminative to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort and gas.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Salsola collina Pall has a long history of being used as a traditional medicine to treat hypertension, headache, insomnia, constipation and vertigo. A total of 637 putative metabolites were identified from the shoots and roots of the plant. These metabolites were mainly classified into 10 different categories. Analysis indicated that flavonoids were the major putative antioxidant components. Most of these putative antioxidant components exhibited higher relative concentrations in the L-Sc than the R-Sc.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive medicinal compounds including beta-sitosterol, ferulic acid, p-coumaric acid, tricin, and vanillic acid. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile observed in Salsola collina species. The presence of phenolic acids like ferulic, p-coumaric, and vanillic acid provides significant antioxidant properties. Additionally, the phytosterol beta-sitosterol and the flavone tricin support various biological activities within the plant's chemical structure. These combined constituents make the plant a subject of interest for therapeutic research.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", seven compounds were isolated from Salsola collina through column chromatography. These identified compounds include n-lignoceric acid (1), n-dotriacontanic acid (2), beta-sitosterol (3), 5, 2'-dihydroxy-6, 7-methylenedioxy-isoflavone (4), vanillic acid (5), (-)syringaresinol 4,4'-bis-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (6), and salicylic acid (7). The study notes that compounds 1, 2, 4, 5, and 6 were isolated from the plant for the first time. Structures for these constituents were identified using physical and chemical properties along with spectral data.
