Salsola collina

What's the taxonomical classification of Salsola collina?

Salsola collina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually placing it in the order Caryophyllales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Amaranthaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Salsola, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name collina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salsola collina has succulent, cylindrical to subcylindrical branches that are typically branched from the base and possess small, scale-like leaves. These leaves are fleshy and imbricate, meaning they overlap like shingles to minimize water loss. The plant often exhibits a compact, shrubby growth habit with branches that may be slightly woody at the base. Small, inconspicuous flowers are tucked within the axils of the scale-like leaves. The fruit is a dry, many-seeded utricle enclosed by persistent, bladder-like perianth segments.
