Salsola collina

What's the taxonomical classification of Salsola collina?

Salsola collina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually placing it in the order Caryophyllales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Amaranthaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Salsola, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name collina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salsola collina has succulent, cylindrical to subcylindrical branches that are typically branched from the base and possess small, scale-like leaves. These leaves are fleshy and imbricate, meaning they overlap like shingles to minimize water loss. The plant often exhibits a compact, shrubby growth habit with branches that may be slightly woody at the base. Small, inconspicuous flowers are tucked within the axils of the scale-like leaves. The fruit is a dry, many-seeded utricle enclosed by persistent, bladder-like perianth segments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Central Asia. It primarily inhabits sandy or saline soils within desert ecosystems and coastal dunes. Its distribution extends through countries such as Egypt, Libya, and Israel, where it thrives in harsh, dry climates. The species is frequently found in salt marshes and disturbed sandy areas where competition from other vegetation is minimal. This wide range reflects its ability to adapt to extreme temperatures and low moisture availability.

What are the pharmacological activities of Salsola collina?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including analgesic, antihypertensive, antioxidant, carminative, and sedative properties. Its antioxidant capacity helps neutralize free radicals and prevent cellular damage within the body. The plant also demonstrates analgesic effects by reducing pain perception and sedative qualities that promote relaxation. Additionally, its antihypertensive actions assist in managing elevated blood pressure levels. Finally, the carminative properties support digestive health by helping to relieve intestinal gas.

As discussed in a study, Salsola collina Pall has a long history of being used as a traditional medicine to treat hypertension, headache, insomnia, constipation and vertigo. The shoots and roots, known as L-Sc and R-Sc, were investigated using UPLC-ESI-MS/MS. A total of 637 putative metabolites were identified and these metabolites were mainly classified into 10 different categories. Flavonoids were identified as the major putative antioxidant components. Most of these putative antioxidant components exhibited higher relative concentrations in the L-Sc than the R-Sc.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds acanthoside D, ferulic acid, p-coumaric acid, salicylic acid, and tricin. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's diverse biological activities and therapeutic potential. Ferulic acid and p-coumaric acid are known for their strong antioxidant properties that protect cells from damage. The presence of salicylic acid provides significant anti-inflammatory effects similar to those found in traditional medicines. Additionally, acanthoside D and tricin further enhance the phytochemical profile of the species. Together, these compounds form a complex chemical matrix used in various pharmacological studies.

As discussed in a study, the EtOH extract from the whole plant of Salsola collina was used to isolate and purify medicinal compounds via column chromatography. A total of 11 compounds were identified from the plant through physical properties and spectroscopic data. These constituents include ferulic acid (1), p-coumaric acid (2), salicylic acid (3), tricin (4), selagin (5), acanthoside D (6), tricin-7-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (7), tricin-4'-O-beta-D-apioside (8), isorrhamnetin-7-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (9), isorrhamnetin-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (10), and isorrhamnetin-3-O-alpha-L-arabino-pyranosyl (1-->6)-beta-D-glucopyranoside (11). Notably, compounds 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, and 9 were isolated from Salsola for the first time.
