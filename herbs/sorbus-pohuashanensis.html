What's the taxonomical classification of Sorbus pohuashanensis?

Sorbus pohuashanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific genus, Sorbus, and its unique species designation, pohuashanensis.

What are the pharmacological activities of Sorbus pohuashanensis?

Such diverse biological properties suggest that Sorbus pohuashanehensis holds potential for future therapeutic development.

As discussed in a study, triterpenoids consisting mainly of ursolic acid and oleanolic acid were isolated from the fruits of Sorbus pohuashanensis. These triterpenoids significantly restrained the increase of serum AST, ALT, TNF-alpha, IL-1beta, IL-6, and hepatic MDA levels. The treatment also evidently reversed the decrease of hepatic SOD, GSH, and CAT levels induced by acetaminophen. Furthermore, the triterpenoids inhibited the over-expressions of iNOS and COX-2 while effectively restraining the phosphorylation of MAPK family signals including p38, JNK, and ERK. These pharmacological activities demonstrate that the liver-protection effects of Sorbus pohuashanensis are related to anti-oxidation and anti-inflammation properties.
