What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Peristylus goodyeroides has a creeping rhizome that produces basal leaves with ovate to cordate blades and a single, terminal, non-resupinate flower.

The leaves are typically dark green with prominent venation and are arranged in a basal rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of a small number of white or cream-colored flowers that emerge on a slender, upright scape. Each flower features three spreading sepals and three petals that are often similar in appearance to the sepals.

The reproductive structures include a central ovary that develops into a small, fleshy capsule upon successful pollination.
