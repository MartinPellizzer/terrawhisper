Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica) Compounds

Tannin is a major compound with astringent properties. Flavonoids contribute to antioxidant effects. Phenolic acids provide anti-inflammatory benefits. Saponins have mild laxative and antimicrobial actions. Glycosides may support cardiovascular health. Coumarin has anticoagulant properties. Terpenoids offer anti-inflammatory and antifungal effects. Essential oils contribute to digestive and antimicrobial properties. Anthocyanins are powerful antioxidants. Alkaloids may have various pharmacological activities.

Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Tannins help in treating digestive issues and skin conditions by reducing irritation and promoting healing.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative stress and support overall immune function. Flavonoids are responsible for many of the therapeutic benefits associated with Terminalia bellirica in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and support immune function. This compound is also involved in the plant's traditional use for digestive and respiratory health.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in Ayurveda for digestive and anti-inflammatory support. 5. Glycoside

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Coumarin is found in the bark and fruit of the plant. It is used in traditional medicine to support digestive and respiratory health.

7. Terpenoid

9. Anthocyanin

10. Alkaloid
