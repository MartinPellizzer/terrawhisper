Botanical Identification of Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica)

To identify Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Belleric Myrobalan, or Terminalia bellirica, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Combretaceae, genus Terminalia, and species Terminalia bellirica. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the broader group of eudicots, reflecting its evolutionary relationships and structural features.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Terminalia bellirica is the scientific name of Belleric Myrobalan. It was first described by the botanist Robert Brown in 1814. The plant is also known by various synonyms, including Combretum belliricum and Terminalia chebula subsp. bellirica. It is widely recognized by its common name, Belleric Myrobalan, and has regional and historical names used in traditional medicine systems.

Morphological Characteristics

Belleric Myrobalan is a deciduous tree with a spreading growth habit. It has a deep taproot system and a strong, straight trunk. The leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a drupe, turning red when mature, containing a single seed.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Belleric Myrobalan can be distinguished by its elliptic leaves with smooth margins, rough bark, and oval, yellowish fruits. It differs from Terminalia catappa by having smaller, less fleshy fruits and from Terminalia chebula by its distinct leaf shape and bark texture. Its unique combination of features aids in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Belleric Myrobalan is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly India, Nepal, and Bangladesh. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at altitudes between 300 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Belleric Myrobalan blooms from March to May, producing small yellowish-green flowers. Seed formation occurs between June and August. Harvesting takes place in November to December, when the fruit is fully mature and the outer layer turns dark brown, making it easier to identify for medicinal use.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Belleric Myrobalan (Terminalia bellirica) is closely related to other Terminalia species, sharing characteristics such as similar fruit morphology and wood structure. It belongs to the Combretaceae family, which includes other tropical trees with similar ecological and medicinal properties. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to related genera within the family.
