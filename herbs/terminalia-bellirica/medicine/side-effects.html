10 Possible Side Effects Of Terminalia Bellirica (Bahera)

Terminalia bellirica has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with certain medications.

These side effects can occur due to excessive consumption, allergies, or interactions with other substances.

In severe cases, they can lead to stomach ulcers, anaphylaxis, or worsen conditions like diabetes, thyroid disorders, and epilepsy, significantly impacting one's quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Terminalia bellirica if used imporperly.

1. Enhances sedative effects

Terminalia bellirica causes stomach upset.

This is due to its bitter and astringent properties, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract and disrupt digestion.

The bark of the tree, commonly used in traditional medicine, contains tannins that can bind to proteins and reduce the absorption of nutrients, leading to symptoms such as bloating, cramps, and diarrhea.

The bark of the tree contains a high concentration of tannins, which can irritate the mucous membranes in the digestive tract and stimulate bowel movements.

Additionally, the fruit pulp of Terminalia bellirica has been known to contain anthraquinones, which are responsible for its laxative properties, leading to loose stools and frequent diarrhea in some individuals.

Terminalia bellirica triggers allergic reactions when consumed by some individuals.

This is due to the presence of certain compounds in the fruit, bark, and leaves that can cause an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing.
