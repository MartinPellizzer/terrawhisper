What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Terminalia Bellirica (Bahera)?

Terminalia bellirica, also known as bahera, has health benefits such as lowering cholesterol and triglyceride levels, as well as exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its medicinal constituents include flavonoids, terpenoids, and tannins, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects. Various medicinal preparations, including decoctions, infusions, and powder, are made from the fruit and bark of the tree. Possible side effects include gastrointestinal irritation and allergic reactions.

Precautions include consulting with a healthcare professional before using bahera, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Terminalia bellirica.

What are the health benefits of Terminalia bellirica?

Terminalia bellirica, also known as bahera, has health benefits such as antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help protect against cell damage and chronic diseases.

Its extracts have been shown to exhibit antimicrobial, anti-cancer, and cardio-protective effects.

Additionally, Terminalia bellirica is traditionally used to treat various health issues, including fever, cough, and digestive problems, due to its potential anti-microbial, anti-oxidative, and anti-inflammatory activities.

What are the active constituents of Terminalia bellirica?

Terminalia bellirica, also known as bahera, has active constituents such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

The bark, leaves, and fruit of the plant are rich in gallic acid, ellagic acid, and quercetin, which exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities.

These constituents contribute to the plant's traditional uses, including treating digestive issues, fever, and respiratory problems, as well as promoting oral health.

What are the medicinal preparations of Terminalia bellirica?

Terminalia bellirica, also known as bahera, has medicinal preparations such as its bark, which is used to treat various ailments.

The bark is used to treat fever, rheumatism, and diarrhea. Its seeds are used in the treatment of skin diseases and ulcers, while its root is used to cure coughs and respiratory problems.

In Ayurvedic medicine, the fruit is used as a laxative and to treat fever and cough.

What are the possible side effect of using Terminalia bellirica improperly?

Improper use of Terminalia bellirica, also known as bahera, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as gastrointestinal disturbances, nausea, and vomiting.

Long-term consumption may lead to stomach ulcers and inflammation, as well as interact with certain medications like blood thinners and diabetes medications.

Additionally, bahera may exacerbate existing conditions like kidney stones and high blood pressure.

What precautions to take when using Terminalia bellirica medicinally?

Before using Terminalia bellirica, also known as bahera, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional, especially if you have any underlying health conditions, are pregnant or breastfeeding.

Inform your doctor about any medications you are taking to avoid potential interactions. Use standardized extracts and follow recommended dosages.

Monitor your body's response and discontinue use if you experience any adverse effects.
