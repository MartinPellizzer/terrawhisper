What is Cyclobalanopsis lamelloides?

Cyclobalanopsis lamelloides is a species of tree in the family Fagaceae.

It is a deciduous tree with a rounded crown, growing up to 20 meters tall, with a straight or slightly curved trunk, and a smooth, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, measuring 5-15 cm long, with 3-5 pairs of lateral veins, and a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a catkin, with male and female flowers borne separately, with the male flowers producing 2-3 mm long, yellowish-green, apetalous flowers, and the female flowers producing 2-3 mm long, apetalous, 3-celled ovary.

The fruit is a samara, measuring 1-2 cm long, with a flat, winged seed, and a leathery, 2-lobed calyx.
