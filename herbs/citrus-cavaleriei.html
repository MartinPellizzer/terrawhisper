What's the taxonomical classification of Citrus cavaleriei?

Citrus cavaleriei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression through the order Sapindales, it is placed within the family Rutaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Citrus, identifying its specific biological designation as the species cavaleriei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Citrus cavaleriei has small, elliptical leaves with serrated margins, woody stems, and globose fruit that typically measure between two and four centimeters in diameter. Its flowers are white and fragrant, emerging in small clusters from the axils of the leaves. The fruit develops a relatively thin rind and contains a juicy, acidic pulp. These structural features are characteristic of its classification within the Rutaceae family.
