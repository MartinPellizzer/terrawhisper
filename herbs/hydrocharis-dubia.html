Hydrocharis dubia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydrocharis dubia has floating, heart-shaped leaves that are bright green, glossy, and possess long, slender petioles that attach them to submerged rhizomes. These leaves typically measure between 3 and 10 centimeters in width and feature a smooth margin. The plant produces small, white, three-petaled flowers that emerge from the foliage on short stalks. Below the water surface, a creeping rhizome system allows the plant to spread across the aquatic environment. This morphology enables the species to form dense mats that cover the surface of slow-moving or stagnant waters.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically occurring in countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of India. Its presence is often tied to lowland areas that experience significant seasonal rainfall and humidity. Within these habitats, the species prefers shallow, stagnant, or low-velocity waters that allow its delicate leaves to remain buoyant.
