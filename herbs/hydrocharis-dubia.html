Hydrocharis dubia

What's the taxonomical classification of Hydrocharis dubia?

Hydrocharis dubia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Alismatales. Specifically, this plant is a member of the family Hydrocharitaceae, falling under the genus Hydrocharis, and is identified by its specific species name, dubia.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, digestive ailments, and respiratory issues. It is frequently applied topically to soothe irritation or manage minor skin infections. When used internally, it is believed to aid in settling stomach discomfort and improving overall gastrointestinal health. Some traditional practices also suggest its efficacy in calming coughs or related breathing difficulties. These medicinal uses are often rooted in long-standing regional herbal traditions.
