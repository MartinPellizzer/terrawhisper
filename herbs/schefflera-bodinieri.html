What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds bodinitiin A, bodinoen, bodinoen glycoside, bodirin A, and stigmasterol 3-O-glucoside. These specific chemical constituents are characteristic of the Schefflerera bodinieri species and contribute to its unique biochemical profile. Research into these substances helps scientists understand the potential therapeutic applications of the plant. Each of these isolated molecules plays a distinct role in the plant's overall chemical makeup. Studying these specific compounds is essential for identifying the biological activities inherent to the species.

According to a study published by "Planta medica", fourteen plant ingredients were obtained from the roots and leaves of Schefflera bodinieri. Nine of these isolated compounds were tested by fifteen receptor binding assays for their central nervous system activities. Three specific compounds, bodinone, bodinone glycoside, and D-sorbitol, were able to selectively bind to muscarine receptors, a trisaccharide bound to Ca2+ channel and 5HT-2 receptors, and stigmasterol 3-O-glucosides bound to 5HT-2 receptors. Additionally, bodirin A bound to dopamine-2 receptors with IC50 values at the microM level. In drug-interaction studies, bodinone, bodinone glycoside, bodirin A, bodinitin A, and the trisaccharide were found to affect the binding affinity of certain specific binding agents to the 5HT1C, 5HT2, opiate, beta-adrenergic, and histamine 1 receptors.
