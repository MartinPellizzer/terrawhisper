What side-effects this plant can have?

Schefflera bodinieri can cause skin irritation, mouth swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched. The plant contains sap that acts as a localized irritant to the skin of many people. Contact with this sap often results in redness, itching, or a burning sensation. If a person accidentally eats parts of the plant, they may experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Pets are also susceptible to these adverse reactions if they consume the foliage.
