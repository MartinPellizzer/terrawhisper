Bioactive Constituents

Lavandula officinalis linalool is a primary monoterpene alcohol that contributes significantly to the herb's characteristic floral and spicy aroma, and it is known for its calming and sedative effects, making it a key component in the treatment of anxiety and insomnia.

Lavandula officinalis linalyl acetate is another major constituent, responsible for the herb's sweet and woody scent, and it exhibits anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, supporting its use in pain relief and skin healing.

Lavandula officinalis beta-caryophyllene is a sesquiterpene that acts as a selective agonist for the CB2 receptor, offering anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits, which contribute to its role in managing conditions like arthritis and inflammation.

Lavandula officinalis geraniol is a monoterpene alcohol with antimicrobial and antifungal properties, often used in aromatherapy and topical applications for its soothing and protective effects on the skin.

Side Effects of lavandula officinalis

Lavandula officinalis causes skin irritation due to the presence of certain chemical compounds, such as linalool and linalyl acetate, which can trigger a reaction in sensitive individuals.

Lavandula officinalis induces allergic reactions because it contains volatile oils that may act as allergens, leading to symptoms like hives or respiratory issues in those with sensitivities.

Lavandula officinalis leads to nausea as its essential oils can stimulate the digestive system and, in some cases, cause gastrointestinal discomfort.

Lavandula officinalis results in dry mouth because the aromatic compounds in the herb can have a dehydrating effect on the oral mucosa, reducing saliva production.
