English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis)

English Lavender (Lavandula officinalis) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Mediterranean Region, Southern Europe, and Northern Africa. Traditionally, its flowers, leaves, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and oil infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its antispasmodic, sedative, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 0.3 to 0.8 meters.
Leaves
Opposite, linear leaves with white to pale green upper surfaces and gray-green lower surfaces, bearing distinct stomatal bands.
Flowers
Bilaterally symmetrical flowers with violet-blue corollas, arranged in dense spike-like clusters, featuring five fused lobes with two showy upper lobes and three smaller lower lobes, and prominent glandular dots on the corolla.
Stems
Cylindrical, woody, aromatic, branched, with opposite, linear leaves and terminal spikes of purple flowers.
