Symplocos sumuntia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Symplocos sumuntia has an evergreen woody habit characterized by lanceolate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and an alternate arrangement along the stems. The foliage typically displays a coriaceous texture and a glossy dark green upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often presenting a yellowish or whitish hue. These flowers eventually give rise to fleshy drupes that serve as the primary fruit type for the species. The overall structure of the plant is defined by its dense branching and sturdy, bark-covered stems.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Symplocos sumuntia can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically without proper preparation.
