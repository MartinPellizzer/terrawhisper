What's the taxonomical classification of Cicerbita azurea?

Cicerbita azurea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Asterales. It is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Cicerbita, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name azurea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cicerbita azurea has erect, herbaceous stems that reach heights of up to 1.5 meters and bear deeply lobed, pinnatisect leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and displays a somewhat coarse texture throughout the plant. Its inflorescences consist of numerous small, bright blue flower heads arranged in large, flat-topped corymbose clusters. Each individual flower head features prominent ray florets surrounding a center of tubular disc florets. The overall structure of the plant is robust and upright, characteristic of many members of the Asteraceae family.
