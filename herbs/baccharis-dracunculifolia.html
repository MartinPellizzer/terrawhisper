Brazilian feverfew

Baccharis dracunculifolia

Brazilian Feverfew (Baccharis dracunculifolia) is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, known for its traditional use in treating fever and inflammatory conditions.

Morphology

Brazilian Feverfew (Baccharis dracunculifolia) is a shrub characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage, small white to yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and a branching structure that typically grows between 1 to 3 meters in height.

Distribution

Brazilian Feverfew (Baccharis dracunculifolia) is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Brazil, particularly in the southeastern and southern parts of the country, including the states of São Paulo, Paraná, and Santa Catarina.

Traditional Uses

Traditionally used in Brazilian herbal medicine to treat inflammation, headaches, and digestive issues, Brazilian Feverfew (Baccharis dracunculifolia) has been valued for its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antispasmodic properties.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Brazilian folk medicine, Ayurveda, and traditional Chinese medicine, Brazilian Feverfew (Baccharis dracunculifolia) has been used for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and analgesic properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Brazilian Feverfew (Baccharis dracunculifolia) is characterized by the presence of various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids, triterpenoids, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Brazilian Feverfew (Baccharis dracunculifolia) exhibits pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, analgesic, and antispasmodic effects, which support its traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions, infections, pain, and digestive disorders.

Botanical Identification

Brazilian Feverfew, scientifically known as Baccharis dracunculifolia, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is also called Erva-de-Santa-Maria in Portuguese and is a perennial shrub native to Brazil. Safety Profile

Brazilian feverfew is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild side effects such as stomach upset or allergic reactions. It is contraindicated for pregnant women and those with bleeding disorders due to its potential to increase bleeding risk.
