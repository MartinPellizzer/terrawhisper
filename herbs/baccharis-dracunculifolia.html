Brazilian feverfew

Baccharis dracunculifolia

Brazilian Feverfew (Baccharis dracunculifolia) is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, known for its traditional use in treating fever and inflammatory conditions.

Botanical Identification

Brazilian Feverfew, scientifically known as Baccharis dracunculifolia, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is also called Erva-de-Santa-Maria in Portuguese and is a perennial shrub native to Brazil. The plant has aromatic leaves, small yellow flowers, and a fibrous root system. It is sometimes referred to by other names in traditional herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Sapindales
  • Family: Rutaceae
  • Genus: Baccharis
  • Species: Baccharis dracunculifolia

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and digestive issues.

Extract: Used for its antioxidant properties and to support immune function.

Safety Profile

Brazilian feverfew is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild side effects such as stomach upset or allergic reactions. It is contraindicated for pregnant women and those with bleeding disorders due to its potential to increase bleeding risk.
