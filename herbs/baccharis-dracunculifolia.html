Brazilian Feverfew (Baccharis dracunculifolia)

Brazilian Feverfew (Baccharis dracunculifolia) is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and resin have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, african traditional medicine, and native american herbal medicine.
