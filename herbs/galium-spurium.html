Galium spurium

What's the taxonomical classification of Galium spurium?

Galium spurium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, its specific identity is defined by the genus Galium and the species epithet spurium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Galium spurium has whorled leaves arranged in circles of six to eight, square-shaped stems that are covered in fine bristly hairs, and small, four-petaled white flowers. The plant typically grows in a creeping or decumbent manner, allowing it to spread across the ground through its rooting stems. Its leaves are lanceolate or linear in shape and possess a distinct texture due to the presence of small hairs along the margins and surfaces. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. These structural features collectively allow the species to thrive in disturbed habitats and shaded areas.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to Europe and parts of Western and Central Asia, ranging from the British Isles through much of the European continent to the Caucasus. It is commonly found inhabiting temperate regions where it thrives in various habitats such as woodland edges, hedgerows, and meadows. Due to human activity and trade, certain populations have been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America. Its distribution is largely dictated by its preference for moist, shaded environments within these temperate zones. The species remains most concentrated in the undisturbed deciduous forests of its primary native range.
