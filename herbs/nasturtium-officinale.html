Possible Side Effects of nasturtium officinale

Nasturtium officinale can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high concentration of glucosinolates.

Consuming large amounts of this herb can also lead to kidney damage and an increased risk of bleeding, particularly in individuals taking anticoagulant medications.

Additionally, improper use of nasturtium officinale may trigger allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Furthermore, pregnant or breastfeeding women should exercise caution when using this herb, as it may stimulate uterine contractions and interfere with milk production.
