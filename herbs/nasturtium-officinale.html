Nasturtium Officinale

Nasturtium officinale, commonly known as watercress, is a type of herb that belongs to the Brassicaceae family.

This herb is rich in nutrients and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its numerous health benefits, which include its ability to act as an antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial agent.

The therapeutic actions of Nasturtium officinale include its use in treating various conditions such as digestive issues, respiratory problems, and skin conditions, due to its bioactive constituents, which include vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like potassium and iron.

Possible Side Effects of nasturtium officinale

Nasturtium officinale can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing gastrointestinal irritation, diarrhea, and stomach cramps due to its high content of isothiocyanates.

Additionally, the herb may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and decrease their effectiveness or increase the risk of bleeding.

In severe cases, excessive consumption can cause kidney damage and failure, emphasizing the importance of proper dosing and medical supervision.
