Garden Nasturtium

Nasturtium officinale

Garden Nasturtium (nasturtium officinale) is a member of the Tropiceae family, traditionally used for its potential benefits in supporting digestive health, treating skin infections, and alleviating symptoms of colds and flu. The plant parts commonly utilized include the leaves, stems, and flowers, which have been historically valued in various cultural and regional practices for their medicinal properties.

Botanical Identification

Nasturtium officinale is a member of the Brassicaceae family. Common names include garden nasturtium and Indian cress. It is an annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves and yellow or orange flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include Tropaeolum majus and Tropaeolum speciosum.

Active Compounds

The Garden Nasturtium contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, essential oils, and tannins. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal properties, with flavonoids offering anti-inflammatory benefits and essential oils contributing to antimicrobial effects.

  • Essential oil
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory infections and digestive issues.

Tincture: Used for skin infections and as an antimicrobial agent.

Culinary use: Used to enhance flavor and aid digestion.

Safety Profile

Garden Nasturtium is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks. Large quantities may cause gastrointestinal upset, so use with caution and consult a healthcare provider if necessary.
