Herbal Preparations

Ammi majus teas are traditionally prepared by steeping the dried herb in hot water, allowing the volatile oils and mild compounds to dissolve into the liquid.

These teas are often consumed for their purported calming effects and digestive benefits, though scientific evidence supporting these claims is limited. The preparation of Ammi majus decoctions involves simmering the herb for an extended period, which extracts more robust compounds and is commonly used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Tinctures made from Ammi majus are concentrated liquid extracts, typically alcohol-based, and are used for their purported ability to support respiratory health and reduce congestion. Mucillages, which are the gel-like substances derived from the herb, are used in poultices or as a soothing agent for irritated mucous membranes.

Each preparation method offers different therapeutic potentials, but their efficacy and safety should be approached with caution, as Ammi majus contains compounds that may be toxic in high doses.
