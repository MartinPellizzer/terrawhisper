Medicinal Constituents of ammi majus

Ammi majus flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to the herb's therapeutic potential in treating various ailments.

Ammi majus terpinen-4-ol, a monoterpene alcohol, is renowned for its potent antimicrobial and antifungal activities, often used in natural remedies for skin and respiratory conditions.

Ammi majus leaf is commonly used in herbal medicine for its aromatic and mild stimulant properties, often employed to aid digestion and relieve respiratory congestion.

The root of Ammi majus is known for its strong aromatic compounds and is primarily used in the preparation of essential oils and tinctures due to its potency.

The flower, though less commonly utilized, can be incorporated into herbal infusions or used as a spice in culinary applications.
