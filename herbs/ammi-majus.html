The herb is relatively easy to cultivate and requires minimal maintenance, making it a favorite among gardeners and horticulturists. Botanically speaking, bishop's weed belongs to the Apiaceae family, which also includes carrots, celery, and parsley, and its distinctive seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural remedy for various ailments.

Throughout history, bishop's weed has been referenced in ancient texts such as those of the Greek physician Dioscorides and the Indian Ayurvedic texts, solidifying its place as a valued herb with a rich cultural heritage.
