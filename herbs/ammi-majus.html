Ammi majus

Ammi Majus (Ammi majus) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, commonly known as cow parsley or wild angelica. It is primarily used in traditional medicine to treat respiratory conditions such as asthma and bronchitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Morphology

Ammi Majus, also known as greater celandine, is a flowering plant with a distinctive morphology characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in yellowish hairs, large heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture, and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Distribution

Ammi majus is native to regions of southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, with a particular concentration in the Mediterranean basin.

Traditional Uses

Ammi Majus has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat respiratory conditions, digestive issues, and as a vermifuge to expel intestinal parasites.

Medicinal Systems

Ammi Majus has been traditionally used in Ayurveda, Unani, and Traditional Chinese Medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Ammi majus involves the presence of compounds such as psoralens, which are known for their phototoxic properties and potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

Ammi majus exhibits antispasmodic, carminative, and anti-inflammatory properties, making it effective in treating digestive disorders, menstrual cramps, and respiratory conditions.

Botanical Identification

Ammi majus, also known as greater hogweed, is a biennial plant in the Apiaceae family.
