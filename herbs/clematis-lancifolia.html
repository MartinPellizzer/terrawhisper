Clematis lancifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis lancifolia?

Clematis lancifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls into the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Specifically, it is identified by its genus, Clematis, and its unique species designation, lancifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis lancifolia has slender, climbing stems and lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the vine. The foliage consists of small, green leaflets that taper to a sharp point, reflecting its specific epithet. Its flowers are characterized by delicate, star-shaped petals that often feature subtle color variations. The plant utilizes specialized petioles to wrap around surrounding vegetation for structural support as it grows. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in climbing niches within its natural habitat.
