Possible Side Effects of Prunus × lannesiana f. kirin

Prunus × lannesiana f. kirin can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing gastrointestinal issues, including nausea and diarrhea, due to its high concentration of amygdalin, a compound that releases cyanide when ingested.

Ingestion of large amounts can also lead to cyanide poisoning, which may result in symptoms like headaches, dizziness, and even respiratory failure.

Additionally, improper handling of the plant can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions in some individuals, manifesting as redness, itching, and rashes.

Furthermore, excessive exposure to the plant's sap may cause photodermatitis, a skin reaction triggered by sunlight.
