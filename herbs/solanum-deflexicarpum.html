Solanum deflexicarpum

What's the taxonomical classification of Solanum deflexicarpum?

Solanum deflexicarpum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Solanales and is a member of the family Solanaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Solanum and the species name deflexicarpum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Solanum deflexicarpum contains a variety of bioactive alkaloids, steroidal saponins, and phenolic compounds that serve as its primary medicinal components. These chemical constituents are often studied for their potential to exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of specific glycoalkaloids within the plant species may also contribute to its biological activity in traditional medicinal practices. Researchers analyze these complex molecules to understand how they interact with cellular targets. Further investigation into these compounds could reveal new therapeutic applications for managing various ailments.
