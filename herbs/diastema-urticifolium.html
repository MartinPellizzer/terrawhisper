What is Diastema urticifolium?

Diastema urticifolium is a species of flowering plant in the family Gesneriaceae.

It is a perennial herb with erect stems, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and a woody base. Leaves are dark green, elliptical in shape, and measure 10-20 centimeters in length, with entire margins and a pointed apex. The plant produces white or yellowish flowers in a terminal raceme, with a calyx of four sepals and a corolla of four petals, each 3-5 centimeters in length.

Flowers are fragrant, and the fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Diastema urticifolium are infusion, decoction, tincture, salve, and tea.

Tincture is made by steeping the roots in a solvent like ethanol or glycerin to create a concentrated extract, used for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Salve is prepared by infusing the leaves in oil to create a topical application, used to treat skin conditions and wounds.
