1. Cures malaria

Malaria is a parasitic infection that affects the bloodstream and can be life-threatening.

3. Cures malaria relapse

Malaria Relapse.

A recurring manifestation of malaria, often referred to as a secondary attack or a re-emergence of the disease, occurs when the parasite, which was previously dormant in the liver, becomes active again.

This condition is typically caused by the Plasmodium vivax or Plasmodium ovale species, which can remain latent in the liver for weeks or even months before reactivating.

It commonly affects individuals who have previously recovered from malaria but whose immune systems are weakened or who have not fully completed their treatment.

Artemisia annua, known for its active compound artemisinin, has been shown to effectively cure malaria relapse by targeting the dormant parasite stages in the liver.

How this herb helps with cures malaria relapse?

Artemisia annua cures malaria relapse because it contains bioactive constituents such as artemisinin, alkaloids, and flavonoids.

These compounds work synergistically to target the Plasmodium parasite, particularly its dormant forms that cause relapses. Artemisinin, the most well-known compound, disrupts the parasite's cellular processes and prevents its replication. Alkaloids contribute to the plant's antimalarial activity by interfering with the parasite's metabolic pathways.

Flavonoids enhance the overall efficacy of the treatment by reducing inflammation and supporting the immune response.

According to "Phytomedicine : international journal of phytotherapy and phytopharmacology", Artemisia annua infusions showed a cure rate of 100% for adults and 82% for pediatrics against malaria, and were associated with faster fever clearance, elimination of gametocytes, and fewer adverse effects compared to the standard treatment ASAQ.
