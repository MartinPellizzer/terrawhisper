Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua) Compounds

The primary compounds of Sweet Wormwood include terpenoids, which are volatile oils responsible for its aromatic properties. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant effects. Essential oils, glycosides, and alkaloids also play roles in its medicinal properties. Coumarin, saponins, tannins, and anthocyanins further enhance its therapeutic potential.

Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua) Monograph

1. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a major class of compounds found in Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, with artemisinin being a key example. These compounds exhibit antimalarial and anti-inflammatory effects, making them vital to the plant's therapeutic use in herbal medicine.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine. These compounds may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects when combined with other active components like artemisinin.

3. Essential oil

The essential oil of Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua) contains volatile compounds like thujone and camphor. These components contribute to its aromatic properties and may support antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. The essential oil is often used in aromatherapy and traditional herbal preparations.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine. This compound plays a role in the plant's ability to combat infections and reduce oxidative stress in the body.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic effects, particularly in the treatment of malaria. Glycosides in Artemisia annua are known to have antimalarial properties, making the plant a significant source in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

6. Alkaloid

Artemisinin is the key alkaloid in Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua). It is a sesquiterpene lactone with potent antimalarial properties. Artemisinin works by disrupting the malaria parasite's cellular structures and is widely used in modern antimalarial treatments.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and may have mild anticoagulant effects. While not the primary medicinal component, coumarin supports the overall therapeutic profile of the herb.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and has mild detoxifying properties. While not the primary active component for antimalarial effects, saponins support the plant's overall therapeutic profile.

9. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also act as antioxidants and can help reduce inflammation. They are present in various parts of the plant, including the leaves and flowers.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid compound found in Sweet Wormwood (Artemisia annua). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. While not the primary active component, anthocyanin enhances the overall therapeutic potential of the herb.
