What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia clethroides?

Lysimachia clethroides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Primulaceae, specifically within the genus Lysimachia, leading to its unique scientific designation as the species clethroides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia clethroides has upright, unbranched stems that reach heights of up to one meter and bear whorls of lanceolate, bright green leaves. These individual blossoms possess five delicate petals and are arranged in airy, plume-like clusters. The overall structure creates a graceful, fountain-like appearance in garden settings.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Lysimachia clethroides Duby is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb used to treat edema, jaundice diseases, hepatitis, tumor and inflammations. Researchers investigated total flavonoids from Lysimachia clethroides Duby, referred to as ZE4, which contained 15 isolated and identified flavonoids. The study found that ZE4 could inhibit the growth of human chronic myeloid leukemia K562 cells significantly by induction of apoptosis. During this process, Bcl-2 expression was down-regulated remarkably, while Fas, Trail and DR5 were up-regulated. These results suggest that the total flavonoids of Lysimachia clethroides Duby exert potential anti-cancer activity through growth inhibition and apoptosis in K562 cells.
