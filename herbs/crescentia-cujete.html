Crescentia cujete

What's the taxonomical classification of Crescentia cujete?

Crescentia cujete belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is part of the order Lamiales and the family Bignoniaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Crescentia, and its species name, cujete.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Crescentia cujete contains high concentrations of iridoids, flavonoids, tannins, and saponins within its fruit pulp and bark. These bioactive compounds contribute to the plant's significant antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of specific glycosides allows the plant to be used traditionally in treating respiratory ailments like coughs and asthma. Additionally, its antioxidant components help mitigate oxidative stress in various biological systems. Researchers continue to study these chemical constituents to isolate potential therapeutic agents for modern medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Crescentia cujete?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat respiratory ailments like coughs and asthma, digestive issues such as stomach aches, and various skin inflammations. The pulp from the fruit is often processed into a syrup or decoction to soothe throat irritation and congestion. Additionally, local healers utilize the fruit to address gastrointestinal distress and intestinal parasites. Some traditional practices also involve applying the fruit extract topically to heal wounds or skin eruptions. These medicinal applications rely heavily on the fruit's bioactive compounds to provide relief from systemic and localized discomfort.
