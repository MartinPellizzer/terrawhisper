Rhynchosia hirta

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhynchosia hirta?

Rhynchosia hirta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Fabales and falls within the family Fabaceae, commonly known as the legume family. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Rhynchosia and the species hirta.

What are the therapeutic actions of Rhynchosia hirta?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin ailments such as wounds, sores, and various types of inflammation. Traditional medicinal practices often involve applying the plant's crushed parts or extracts directly to affected areas to promote healing. It is believed to possess antimicrobial properties that help prevent infections in open lesions. Some local healers also utilize it to soothe itching caused by insect bites or minor dermatological irritations. The application is typically localized to ensure the active compounds interact directly with the skin surface.
