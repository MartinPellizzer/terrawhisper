Rhynchosia hirta has a low-growing, spreading habit characterized by densely hairy stems and small, pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, spreading trichomes. The plant often forms compact mats or cushions as it spreads across open, sandy, or disturbed habitats. This dense pubescence serves as a protective layer against moisture loss and intense sunlight.
