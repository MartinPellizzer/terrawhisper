Aralia echinocaulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Aralia echinocaulis?

Aralia echinocaulis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Apiales. This plant is a specific representative of the family Araliaceae, situated within the genus Aralia, and is identified by its unique species name, echinocaulis.

According to a study published by "International journal of biological macromolecules", Aralia echinocaulis is a medicinal food plant used mainly to prevent or treat rheumatoid arthritis in China. The study reported the isolation of a polysaccharide named HSM-1-1 from Aralia echinocaulis, which is a new 4-O-methylglucuronoxylan with a molecular weight of 1.6 × 10^4 Da. This polysaccharide demonstrated potent anti-inflammatory and anti-tumor activities in vitro. Specifically, HSM-1-1 showed a proliferation inhibition activity on colon cancer cell SW480 with an inhibition rate of 17.57 ± 1.03 % at a concentration of 600 μg/mL. This represents the first report of a polysaccharide structure obtained from Aralia echinocaulis and its bioactivities.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Aralia echinocaulis is rich in polysaccharides and glycosides. The total polysaccharide and glycoside (TPG) from Aralia echinocaulis can inhibit arthritis and reduce serum IL-1β and TNF-α levels in an RA rat model. Through 16S rRNA sequencing, it was found that TPG modulates the gut microbiota community structure. A cooccurrence analysis of the 50 most abundant genera showed that 19 were in a cooccurrence network, where 16 showed a positive correlation and 3 showed a negative correlation. These medicinal effects of TPG from Aralia echinocaulis are related to the modulation of the gut microbiota.

In a collagen-induced arthritis (CIA) rat model, TPG from Aralia echinocaulis could inhibit arthritis and reduce serum IL-1β and TNF-α levels.
