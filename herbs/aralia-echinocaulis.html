Aralia echinocaulis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia echiinocaulis has a woody stem characterized by a dense covering of sharp, thorn-like spines and a branched, shrubby growth habit. The leaves are typically compound and arranged in an alternate pattern along the prickly branches. Each leaflet is relatively small with serrated margins and a distinctively textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that eventually develop into fleshy berries. These morphological features serve as protective adaptations against herbivory in its natural habitat.
