Micromeria barosma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Micromeria barosma has opposite, elliptic to obovate leaves that are typically 1 to 3 centimeters long with entire margins and acute or obtuse apices. The stems are woody at the base and often exhibit a prostrate or spreading growth habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary cymes and display a range of colors from white to pale pink. The fruit is a small, globose berry that matures into a dark color, facilitating seed dispersal. This species is characterized by its relatively small size and delicate floral structures.
