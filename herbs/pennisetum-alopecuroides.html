Pennisetum alopecuroides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pennisetum alopecuroides has slender, upright culms that typically reach heights between 30 and 100 centimeters, topped by narrow, contracted panicles featuring soft, bristly spikelets. The leaves are linear and often exhibit a slight fold or involute shape along the midrib. Each spikelet consists of several florets enclosed by tightly packed, fine hairs that give the inflorescence a fuzzy appearance. The plant also features a fibrous root system that supports its perennial growth habit. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in various grassland environments.
