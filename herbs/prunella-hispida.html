What's the taxonomical classification of Prunella hispida?

Prunella hispida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Lamiaceae, which contains the genus Prunella, ultimately leading to its specific identification as the species hispida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Prunella hispida has a decumbent to erect growth habit characterized by square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal spikes composed of small, tubular flowers that emerge from protective bracts. These flowers are generally pale or white and possess a distinct bilabiate structure common to the Lamiaceae family.
