Bromus japonicus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bromus japonicus has an annual growth habit characterized by slender, erect culms that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and feature spreading, linear leaves with prominent parallel venation. The inflorescence is a loose, open panicle containing numerous small, spikelet-like branches that give it a delicate appearance. Each spikelet consists of several florets enclosed by unequal, often hairy lemmas that feature a distinct awn. These awns are typically short and straight, emerging from the apex of the lemma to assist in seed dispersal. The overall structure is lightweight and flexible, allowing the plant to sway easily in windy environments.
