Tabernaemonthe iboga belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. This plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae, specifically falling within the genus Tabernaemonthe, with the specific designation of the species iboga.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tabernanthe iboga has an evergreen shrub or small tree morphology characterized by opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that emerge in clusters from the leaf axils. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a ripe red color. These botanical features allow the species to thrive in the shaded understory of tropical rainforest environments.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Tabernanthe iboga contains high concentrations of ibogaine and other indole alkaloids primarily within its woody root bark. This specific part of the plant is harvested because it serves as the most potent source of the psychoactive and therapeutic compounds sought by practitioners. While the leaves and stems contain secondary alkaloids, they lack the density of active substances found in the subterranean structures. Traditional healers and modern researchers focus almost exclusively on the root bark to prepare infusions or powders for medicinal use. The extraction process typically targets these root tissues to ensure the maximum efficacy of the resulting treatment.

What are the pharmacological activities of Tabernanthe iboga?

This plant has psychoactive, stimulant, anti-addictive, pro-antioxidant, and alcohol-reducing pharmacological activities primarily driven by its alkaloid ibogaine. Its psychoactive properties induce profound alterations in consciousness and perception when ingested. As a stimulant, it can increase alertness and physiological arousal in the central nervous system. Research suggests the plant possesses potent anti-addictive qualities that assist in managing substance use disorders. Furthermore, it has demonstrated efficacy in reducing alcohol consumption and providing pro-antioxidant benefits to mitigate cellular oxidative stress.

As discussed in a study, the West African Tabernanthe iboga plant provides ibogaine, a naturally occurring alkaloid with psychoactive, metabotropic, and anti-addictive effects. When human erythrocyte samples were incubated with ibogaine at 10 and 20 μM at 37°C for 1h, the treatment increased ATP concentration in the blood plasma. Ibogaine also increased SOD1 activity in erythrocytes at both applied doses, including the 20 μM treatment which elevated GR activity. These pharmacological activities suggest that ibogaine acts as a pro-antioxidant by increasing the activity of antioxidative enzymes and as an adaptagene in oxidative distress.
