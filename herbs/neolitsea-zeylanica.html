Neolitsea zeylanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Neolitsea zeylanica has lanceolate to oblong leaves that are leathery, dark green on the upper surface, and possess entire margins with pinnate venation. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the woody stems of the shrub or small tree. These leaves often exhibit a slightly revolute margin and a distinctively shiny texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in panicles, which eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen canopy and compact branching habit.
