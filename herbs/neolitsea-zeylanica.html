Neolitsea zeylanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Neolitsea zeylanica?

Neolitsea zeylanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Laurales and is a member of the family Lauraceae. More specifically, it is situated within the genus Neolitsea, with its specific identification being the species zeylanica.

Neolitsea zeylanica is prepared as an aqueous decoction or an infusion of its leaves and bark to treat various ailments. Additionally, the essential oils extracted from the plant can be used for their aromatic and therapeutic properties. These various methods allow for the effective administration of the plant's natural constituents.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Neolitsea zeylanica can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The essential oils contained within its leaves are known to act as potent allergens for sensitive individuals. When the plant comes into direct contact with the skin, it may trigger redness, itching, or a localized rash. Ingesting any part of the plant without proper medicinal preparation could lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. Because specific toxicological studies are limited, caution is necessary when handling this species.
