Bambusa ventricosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bambusa ventricosa has distinctive swollen internodes that create a bulbous, segmented appearance along its culms. These characteristic expansions occur between the nodes, giving the plant its common name, Buddha Belly bamboo. The foliage consists of lanceolate, bright green leaves that grow in dense clusters from the branches. Its culms are typically thick and sturdy to support the weight of the enlarged sections. The overall growth habit is bushy and upright, reaching significant heights in favorable tropical conditions.
