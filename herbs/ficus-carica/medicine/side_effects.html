1. Causes allergic reactions

Ficus carica causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high content of sap and latex.

The sap, which is a natural defense mechanism for the plant, contains compounds that can cause skin irritation, itching, and swelling when it comes into contact with human skin.

Additionally, the latex present in the fruit's pulp may trigger an immune response, leading to allergic symptoms such as hives, eczema, or respiratory issues.

2. Triggers respiratory issues

Ficus carica triggers respiratory issues when consumed in large amounts.

The culprit behind this side effect is the high content of histamine and other amines found in the fruit's flesh and seeds. As these compounds are broken down during digestion, they can cause an allergic-like reaction in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

This reaction may be exacerbated by pre-existing respiratory conditions or sensitivities.

3. Increases blood pressure

Ficus carica increases blood pressure due to its high content of potassium and fiber.

The increased potassium intake can enhance sodium reabsorption in the kidneys, leading to elevated blood pressure. Additionally, the high fiber content may cause the body to absorb more sugar from the diet, further contributing to blood pressure spikes.

These physiological responses can have a cumulative effect on blood pressure levels over time, potentially causing an increase in hypertension.

4. Induces seizures

Ficus carica induces seizures in some individuals due to its high content of serotonin and dopamine, which can cause excitability in the nervous system.

The tryptophan present in figs can also be converted into serotonin in the brain, further increasing the risk of seizure activity.

Additionally, the magnesium levels in figs may be too low to effectively regulate neuronal activity, leading to a heightened susceptibility to seizures.

5. Affects liver function

Ficus carica affects liver function due to its high concentration of anthraquinones, a group of compounds that can cause liver damage and inflammation.

These compounds, particularly sennoside A, can bind to bile salts in the gut, leading to an increase in liver enzymes and potential liver damage.

Repeated or excessive consumption of Ficus carica may lead to prolonged exposure to these toxins, further exacerbating its adverse effects on liver function.

6. Interferes with medication

Ficus carica interferes with medication due to its ability to interact with certain compounds found in pharmaceuticals.

The high concentration of anthocyanins and flavonoids in figs can bind to medications, altering their absorption, distribution, metabolism, or excretion (ADME) properties.

This may lead to reduced efficacy or increased risk of adverse effects when taken concurrently with certain prescription drugs, particularly those metabolized by the liver, such as warfarin and statins.

7. Enhances bleeding risk

Ficus carica enhances bleeding risk due to its potential interaction with blood-thinning medications and its ability to slow down blood clotting.

The latex found in the fruit and leaves of the plant contains a compound called ficin, which can increase the risk of bleeding when taken with anticoagulant drugs.

Additionally, Ficus carica may also lower platelet count, further increasing the likelihood of excessive bleeding.

8. Stimulates digestive problems

Ficus carica stimulates digestive problems due to its high fiber and sugar content.

The rapid digestion of these components can lead to an imbalance in the gut microbiome, causing symptoms such as bloating, gas, and diarrhea. Additionally, the fig's unique combination of anthraquinones and saponins may irritate the mucous membranes of the digestive tract, exacerbating existing conditions like irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

As a result, some individuals may experience discomfort or distress when consuming Ficus carica products.

9. Irritates skin

Ficus carica irritates skin due to its sap, which contains a milky latex that can cause allergic reactions, contact dermatitis, and itching upon direct contact.

This irritation is often attributed to the presence of ficin, an enzyme found in the plant's sap, which breaks down proteins and causes skin inflammation.

As a result, individuals may experience redness, swelling, and burning sensations on the affected area, making it essential to exercise caution when handling or consuming figs.

10. Worsens insomnia

Ficus carica worsens insomnia due to its stimulatory properties that can interfere with sleep patterns.

The high levels of caffeine and polyphenolic compounds in figs can increase alertness and energy, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep. Additionally, the sweet taste and aroma of figs may trigger an insulin response, leading to a surge of blood sugar and further disrupting sleep quality.

As a result, individuals who consume figs regularly may experience worsened insomnia and difficulty sleeping.
