What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Osmundastrum cinnamomeum has large, bipinnate-pinnatifid fronds that typically measure between 30 and 100 centimeters in length and possess a distinct cinnamon-colored stipe. These leaflets are arranged in a delicate, lace-like pattern along the rachis, creating a highly textured appearance. The fertile fronds are structurally similar to the sterile ones but feature specialized sporangia concentrated in dense, brownish clusters.
