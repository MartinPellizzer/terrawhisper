10 Possible Side Effects Of Galipea Officinalis (Angelica)

Galipea officinalis has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal problems, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

The causes of these side effects are often attributed to excessive consumption, interactions with other medications, and individual sensitivities.

For example, gastrointestinal issues can lead to discomfort, digestive problems, and nutrient deficiencies, which can worsen the lives of individuals with pre-existing health conditions or those with compromised immune systems.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Galipea officinalis if used imporperly.

Galipea officinalis causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its potential cross-reactivity with other members of the Apiaceae family, such as celery and carrots.

The allergenic proteins present in Galipea officinalis can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may also contribute to allergic reactions, causing respiratory issues and skin irritation in susceptible individuals.
