Berberis atrocarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Berberis atrocarpa?

Berberis atrocarppa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is part of the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Berberidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Berberis, and is identified by its unique species name, atrocarpa.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Gansu. It typically thrives in high-altitude environments located between 2000 and 3500 meters above sea level. These populations are often found nestled in forest margins or along rocky slopes where drainage is sufficient. The specific distribution is limited to these temperate, montane ecosystems of East Asia. This localized range makes the species a characteristic element of its specific regional habitat.
