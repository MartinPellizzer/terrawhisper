Stinkweed (Paederia foetida)

Stinkweed (Paederia foetida) is a member of the Sarmentaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, Indo-Pacific, and Tropical Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, bitter, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and european herbal medicine.
