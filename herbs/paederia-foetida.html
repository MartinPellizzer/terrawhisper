Stinkweed

Paederia foetida

Stinkweed (Paederia foetida) is a flowering plant native to tropical regions, known for its strong odor and traditional use in treating various health conditions, including inflammation and digestive disorders.

Botanical Identification

Stinkweed, scientifically known as Paederia foetida, belongs to the Rubiaceae family. Commonly called stinkweed or pigweed, it is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and small white flowers, with a distinctive strong odor. It is also referred to by various regional names in herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for treating colds and flu symptoms.

Poultice: Used for skin infections and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Stinkweed is contraindicated during pregnancy due to potential risks to fetal development. It may cause gastrointestinal irritation and should be used with caution in individuals with kidney or liver disorders. Always consult a qualified practitioner before use.
