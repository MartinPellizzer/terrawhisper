What's the taxonomical classification of Iris × germanica?

Iris × germanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Iridaeceae and is situated within the genus Iris, specifically identified as the species germanica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris × germanica has rhizomatous rootstocks that produce erect, sword-shaped leaves with parallel venation and a distinct ensiform structure. These foliage blades grow in fan-like clumps and typically reach heights of several dozen centimeters. The plant produces large, complex flowers characterized by three upright standards and three drooping falls. The petals often feature intricate color patterns, veining, and contrasting signals near the center. Each flower is supported by a sturdy stem that emerges from the central rhizome during the blooming season.
