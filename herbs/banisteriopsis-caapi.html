Horticulturally, ayahuasca is a climbing vine that requires specific growing conditions, making it challenging to cultivate outside of its native Amazonian region. From a botanical perspective, the plant contains a complex mix of alkaloids and phenolic compounds that are responsible for its medicinal properties.

With roots dating back thousands of years to ancient shamanic rituals in Peru and Brazil, ayahuasca has been an integral part of indigenous cultures, with its use documented by early Spanish conquistadors and later by modern researchers and healers.
