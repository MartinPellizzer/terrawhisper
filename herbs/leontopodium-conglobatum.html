Leontopodium conglobatum has a perennial, woody habit characterized by compact, tufted growth forms and small, scale-like leaves that are closely appressed to the stems. Its foliage is often densely packed, providing a protective layer over the stems. The inflorescences emerge from these compact clusters, often presenting small, inconspicuous flowers.
