10 Possible Side Effects Of Geranium Robertianum

Geranium robertianum has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal irritation, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can be caused by ingesting large quantities or using it without proper preparation.

For instance, a person who uses it as an abortifacient may experience severe stomach cramps and bleeding, leading to hospitalization and long-term health issues.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Geranium robertianum causes allergic reactions.

The plant contains sesquiterpene lactones and flavonoids, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin irritation, hives, and respiratory issues.

These allergenic compounds can also cause anaphylaxis in severe cases, making it essential for people with a history of allergies or sensitivity to exercise caution when using Geranium robertianum.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Geranium robertianum triggers asthma attacks due to its potential to exacerbate respiratory issues.

The herb contains rosmarinic acid, a compound that can cause allergic reactions and inflammation in the airways, leading to bronchospasm and increased sensitivity.

Additionally, Geranium robertianum has been known to stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical involved in allergic responses, which can further aggravate asthma symptoms.

3. Produces excessive sweating

Geranium robertianum produces excessive sweating due to its high concentration of volatile oils, particularly geraniol and linalool.

These compounds are responsible for stimulating the sweat glands, leading to an increase in perspiration. Additionally, Geranium robertianum is known to have a diaphoretic effect, meaning it can cause a person's body to release heat through sweating.

As a result, individuals using this herb may experience increased sweating, especially when taken in large quantities or for extended periods of time.

4. Induces vomiting episodes

Geranium robertianum induces vomiting episodes when used.

The plant's volatile oils, particularly geraniol and linalool, are responsible for this side effect. These oils can stimulate the stomach and intestines, triggering a reflexive response to expel their contents.

As a result, individuals may experience frequent vomiting, often accompanied by nausea, dizziness, or stomach cramps, which can be uncomfortable and even debilitating.

5. Lowers blood pressure suddenly

Geranium robertianum lowers blood pressure suddenly.

This is due to its ability to relax the smooth muscles in the blood vessels, which can lead to a sudden and significant drop in blood pressure. Additionally, the herb's vasodilatory properties may also contribute to this effect, causing blood vessels to dilate and reduce overall blood pressure.

As a result, users may experience dizziness or lightheadedness as their blood pressure drops.

6. Increases heart rate rapidly

Geranium robertianum increases heart rate rapidly due to its stimulant properties, which can cause a sudden and intense rise in blood pressure.

This is attributed to the presence of rosmarinic acid, a bioactive compound that has been shown to increase cardiac activity and enhance sympathetic nervous system stimulation.

As a result, users may experience palpitations, tachycardia, or even arrhythmias.

7. Causes kidney damage slowly

Geranium robertianum causes kidney damage slowly due to its high concentration of geraniin and other tannins.

These compounds can lead to the accumulation of toxic substances in the kidneys, gradually damaging their function over time.

As a result, prolonged use or excessive intake of Geranium robertianum may increase the risk of kidney problems, particularly in individuals with pre-existing kidney issues.

8. Triggers seizures occasionally

Geranium robertianum triggers seizures occasionally.

This is likely due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system, which can be particularly problematic for individuals with a history of epilepsy or other seizure disorders.

The herb's active compounds, such as rosmarinic acid and geraniol, may contribute to this effect by altering neurotransmitter activity and increasing excitability in brain regions.

10. Worsens angina symptoms

Geranium robertianum worsens angina symptoms by constricting blood vessels and increasing blood pressure.

This herb is known to have a vasoconstrictive effect on the cardiovascular system, which can exacerbate conditions such as angina in individuals who are already experiencing cardiac difficulties.

Additionally, Geranium robertianum may also increase heart rate, further contributing to worsened angina symptoms.
