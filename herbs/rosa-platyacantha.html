What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa platyacantha?

Rosa platyacantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Rosalales, it falls into the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Rosa, and its specific species designation, platyacantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa platyacantha has spreading, arching stems that are densely covered in sharp, recurved prickles and feature pinnately compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets. The leaflets possess serrated margins and a smooth, somewhat leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Its flowers are typically solitary and display five distinct petals that range in color from pale pink to white. These blooms are characterized by numerous stamens that surround a central cluster of pistils. Following pollination, the plant develops small, fleshy hip structures that house the seeds.
