Impatiens crassiloba

What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens crassiloba?

Impatiens crassiloba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually falling under the order Ericales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Balsaminaceae, which defines its specific botanical characteristics. Finally, its scientific identity is completed at the genus level as Impatiens and at the species level as crassiloba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Impatiens crassiloba has thick, succulent stems, opposite lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and zygomorphic flowers characterized by a prominent nectar spur. The leaves possess distinct venation and a fleshy texture that helps the plant retain moisture in its habitat. Its floral structure features a unique arrangement of petals and sepals designed to facilitate specific pollination mechanisms. Small, capsule-like fruits develop after flowering, which typically burst to disperse seeds. These morphological features collectively define its identity within the Balsaminaceae family.
