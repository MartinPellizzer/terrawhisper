Schisandra micrantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Schisandra micrantha?

Schisandra micrantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Austrobaileyales and the family Schisandraceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Schisandra, reaching its specific designation as the species micrantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schisandra micrantha has woody climbing vines characterized by opposite, ovate leaves and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that grow in clusters. The leaves possess smooth margins and a glossy texture, typically reaching several centimeters in length. During the flowering stage, the plant produces tiny, fragrant blossoms that eventually transition into small, fleshy red drupes. These fruits are arranged in aggregate clusters and serve as a primary distinguishing feature of the species. The overall structure allows the plant to scramble through forest undergrowth using its flexible stems.

What are the pharmacological activities of Schisandra micrantha?

This plant has anti-HIV, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can inhibit the replication of the human immunodeficiency virus in cellular models. The presence of lignans within the plant contributes significantly to its ability to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. Furthermore, its natural constituents demonstrate a capacity to suppress inflammatory responses and combat various bacterial pathogens. These diverse properties make Schisandra micrantha a subject of significant interest for pharmaceutical development.

As discussed in a study, Schisandra micrantha A. C. Smith provides the dibenzocyclooctadiene lignan gomisin M2. This specific substance serves as the basis for the synthesis of the anti-HIV bioactive compound SJP-L-5. While the study focuses on the derivative, the plant is linked to the production of this nitrogen-containing, biphenyl compound. The resulting compound SJP-L-5 displays 50% cytoxicity concentrations greater than 200 μg/ml. It also shows high antiviral activity with 50% effective concentrations against HIV-1 laboratory-adapted strains ranging from 0.16-0.97 μg/ml and primary isolates ranging from 1.96-5.33 μg/ml.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (+) -Anwulignan, Micrandilactone A, deoxyschizandrin, and gomisin M2. These specific chemical constituents are categorized as lignans and are often studied for their unique biological activities. Researchers investigate these molecules to understand their potential roles in pharmacological applications. Each of these compounds possesses a distinct molecular structure that contributes to the plant's overall chemical profile. Such detailed analysis helps scientists identify the specific properties that make these natural products valuable for drug development.

As discussed in a study, the plant Schisandra micrantha contains various lignans, including a high content of deoxyschizandrin in its fruits. The stems of Schisandra micrantha contain the lignan (+)-anwulignan. While the study notes that the percent contents of 9 lignans in the fruits of 5 species range from 0.52% to 1.96%, it also specifies that the contents in the stems of 11 species range from 0.02% to 1.51%. Overall, the lignan contents in the fruits of Schisandra were found to be higher than those in the roots and stems.
