10 Medicinal Constituents Of Prunus Amygdalus (Almond)

Prunus amygdalus has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and essential oils.

These constituents possess medicinal properties like anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities. The flavonoids, in particular, have been shown to improve cardiovascular health by reducing cholesterol levels and triglycerides.

This can lead to a reduced risk of heart disease and improved overall well-being for individuals with high blood pressure or high cholesterol.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Prunus amygdalus.

1. Amygdalin

Prunus amygdalus amygdalin is a naturally occurring cyanogenic glycoside found in the pits and kernels of apricot, peach, cherry, and plum seeds.

It has been reported to have potential therapeutic properties, including anti-tumor and anti-inflammatory effects, although its efficacy and safety for human consumption are still debated among scientists.

2. Oleic acid

Prunus amygdalus oleic acid is a natural fatty acid found in high concentrations in almonds and other related tree nuts.

It has been widely studied for its potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular risk factors.

Additionally, oleic acid has been shown to have antimicrobial properties and may help protect against certain diseases.

3. Linoleic acid

Prunus amygdalus linoleic acid is an essential fatty acid that is present in the oil extracted from its seeds.

It is an omega-6 fatty acid, which plays a crucial role in various biological processes including inflammation regulation, skin health, and brain function.

Linoleic acid has been linked to several health benefits, including reducing cardiovascular risk factors, improving insulin sensitivity, and exhibiting anti-inflammatory properties.

4. Tannins

Prunus amygdalus tannins is a type of polyphenolic compound naturally present in the fruit, bark, and leaves of the tree.

These tannins are responsible for the characteristic astringent taste and bitter flavor of almonds, as well as their ability to precipitate proteins and reduce bitterness in foods and beverages.

Almond tannins have also been reported to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

5. B-sitosterol

Prunus amygdalus b-sitosterol is a naturally occurring phytosterol that is found in high concentrations in the oil of almonds.

It has been shown to have various health benefits, including lowering cholesterol levels by inhibiting the absorption of dietary cholesterol and improving blood lipid profiles.

Additionally, b-sitosterol may help to reduce inflammation and improve cardiovascular risk factors.

6. Vitamins

Prunus amygdalus vitamins is a rich source of several essential nutrients.

It is an excellent source of vitamin E, a powerful antioxidant that protects cells from damage and supports skin health. Almonds are also a good source of magnesium, copper, and potassium, which support heart health, bone health, and muscle function respectively.

Additionally, they contain healthy fats, protein, and fiber, making them a nutritious snack or addition to a balanced diet.

7. Minerals

Prunus amygdalus minerals is a rich source of essential nutrients.

Almonds are an excellent source of potassium, magnesium, and copper, making them a great choice for heart health. They are also rich in phosphorus, manganese, and zinc, which support bone health and immune function.

Additionally, almonds contain fiber, vitamins E and B2, and antioxidants that promote overall well-being and longevity.

8. Phenolic acids

Prunus amygdalus phenolic acids is a type of bioactive compound found in high concentrations in the seeds and hulls of almonds.

These compounds have been shown to possess antioxidant and antimicrobial properties, making them valuable for protecting against oxidative stress and microorganisms that can cause spoilage.

Phenolic acids in almonds may also contribute to their potential health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health.

9. Flavonoids

Prunus amygdalus flavonoids is a type of bioactive compound that has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits.

These flavonoids, particularly cyanidin-3-glucoside and epicatechin, have been found to exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

They may help protect against chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative disorders by inhibiting oxidative stress and improving vascular function.

10. Alphamurisic acid

Prunus amygdalus alphamurisic acid is a type of flavonoid compound that has been isolated from the hulls of almonds.

This bioactive compound exhibits potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a potential natural remedy for various health conditions. Studies have shown that alphamurisic acid can inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells, as well as reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in animal models.

Further research is needed to fully understand its therapeutic applications.
