10 Possible Side Effects Of Prunus Amygdalus (Almond)

Prunus amygdalus has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, allergies, and skin irritation.

These side effects can occur due to individual sensitivities or incorrect dosages. For instance, consuming excessive amounts of almond oil or kernel can lead to gastrointestinal problems and allergic reactions, exacerbating conditions like eczema, asthma, or even life-threatening anaphylaxis.

This can significantly impact daily life, causing discomfort, fatigue, and decreased productivity.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Prunus amygdalus if used imporperly.

1. Increases risk of cancer

Prunus amygdalus causes allergic reactions.

The allergenic proteins in almonds, such as vicilin and conglutin, can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, and swelling. Additionally, the tree's pollen can cause respiratory issues, including congestion, coughing, and sneezing.

Furthermore, cross-reactivity with other nuts may also occur, exacerbating allergic reactions.

2. Increases risk of cancer

Prunus amygdalus triggers anaphylaxis attacks in some individuals due to its high levels of proteins such as Ara h 1, Ara h 2, and Ara h 3.

These proteins can cause the immune system to overreact, leading to a severe allergic reaction characterized by symptoms such as difficulty breathing, rapid heartbeat, and a drop in blood pressure.

This reaction can be life-threatening if not promptly treated.
