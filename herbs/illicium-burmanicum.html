What's the taxonomical classification of Illicium burmanicum?

Illicium burmanicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Austrobaileyales and is a member of the family Schisandraceae. Specifically, its taxonomic hierarchy places it within the genus Illicium, with its unique identity defined by the species burmanicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illicium burmanium has evergreen leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape, typically measuring 5 to 12 centimeters in length with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage exhibits a leathery texture and a deep green color on the upper surface, while the underside may appear slightly paler. Its woody stems are characterized by a branching habit that supports a dense canopy of foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into distinctive star-shaped, woody follicles. These mature fruits contain aromatic seeds which are the hallmark of the Illiciaceae family.
