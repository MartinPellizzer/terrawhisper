Illicium burmanicum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illicium burmanium has evergreen leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape, typically measuring 5 to 12 centimeters in length with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage exhibits a leathery texture and a deep green color on the upper surface, while the underside may appear slightly paler. Its woody stems are characterized by a branching habit that supports a dense canopy of foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into distinctive star-shaped, woody follicles. These mature fruits contain aromatic seeds which are the hallmark of the Illiciaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the tropical regions of southern China, specifically concentrated within the provinces of Yunnan and Guangxi. It thrives primarily in the moist, subtropical evergreen broad-leaved forests found in these mountainous areas. The species occupies specific ecological niches where high humidity and shaded understories are prevalent. Its distribution is relatively restricted compared to other members of the Illicium genus due to these precise environmental requirements. What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Illicium burmanium contains bioactive compounds primarily located in its dried fruit, which is harvested for its potent aromatic properties. The ripe star-shaped follicles are the most significant part used in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues and respiratory ailments.
