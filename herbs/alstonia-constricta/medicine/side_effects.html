1. Causes allergic reactions

Alstonia constricta causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains compounds like alkaloids and glycosides, which can trigger an immune response leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may also contribute to the allergic reaction, causing respiratory issues and skin irritation in susceptible people.

2. Induces stomach upset

Alstonia constricta induces stomach upset due to its bitter and astringent properties, which can cause irritation and inflammation in the digestive tract.

The plant contains alkaloids that may interfere with digestion, leading to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramps.

Additionally, its tannins can bind to stomach acids, reducing the production of digestive enzymes and further exacerbating discomfort.

3. Increases blood pressure

Alstonia constricta increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as alkaloids and flavonoids, interact with the body's natural systems, causing a rise in blood pressure levels. This increased blood pressure can lead to a range of symptoms, including headaches, dizziness, and anxiety.

The exact mechanism by which Alstonia constricta exerts its pressor effect is not fully understood, but it is thought to involve the activation of certain neurotransmitters and hormones that regulate blood vessel constriction.

4. Triggers anxiety attacks

Alstonia constricta triggers anxiety attacks due to its potential to interact with the body's neurotransmitters, particularly serotonin and GABA.

This interaction can lead to an imbalance of these chemicals, resulting in feelings of unease, nervousness, and intense fear, characteristic of anxiety attacks.

Additionally, Alstonia constricta's ability to affect the brain's reward system may also contribute to its anxiety-inducing effects.

5. Produces dizziness spells

Alstonia constricta produces dizziness spells.

The active compounds present in the plant, such as alkaloids and glycosides, can interact with the central nervous system, leading to feelings of vertigo, lightheadedness, and disorientation.

This is because Alstonia constricta contains substances that affect neurotransmitters and blood pressure, causing a sudden drop in blood flow to the brain, which may result in dizziness spells.

6. Provokes insomnia problems

Alstonia constricta provokes insomnia problems due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system, causing increased alertness and restlessness.

The plant's alkaloids, including vobtusine, can also disrupt normal sleep patterns by affecting the brain's chemistry and leading to a racing mind, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep.

This stimulation of the CNS and disruption of neurotransmitters may result in persistent wakefulness and insomnia issues.

7. Increases heart rate

Alstonia constricta increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system.

The plant contains various alkaloids and glycosides that can cause an increase in blood pressure and heart rate by activating alpha-adrenergic receptors.

This stimulation leads to a surge in adrenaline release, resulting in rapid heartbeats, palpitations, and other cardiovascular symptoms.

8. Causes nausea episodes

Alstonia constricta causes nausea episodes due to its inherent toxicity and ability to bind with certain neurotransmitters in the brain.

The plant contains alkaloids such as vinblastine and vinblastine-like compounds that can disrupt the normal functioning of the digestive system, leading to nausea and vomiting. Additionally, Alstonia constricta has been shown to cause stimulation of the vagus nerve, which can also contribute to nausea episodes.

These mechanisms are thought to be responsible for the gastrointestinal disturbances associated with the use of Alstonia constricta.

9. Triggers respiratory issues

Alstonia constricta triggers respiratory issues due to its high concentration of alkaloids, which can cause constriction of the airways and lungs.

The plant's ability to stimulate the nervous system may also contribute to increased breathing rates and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the potential for bronchospasms and inflammation in the lungs may exacerbate existing respiratory conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

10. Increases sweating excessively

Alstonia constricta increases sweating excessively.

This is due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, causing an overactivation of sweat glands. The alkaloids present in the plant, such as vobasine and vobasinine, are believed to play a key role in this excessive sweating response, leading to an increase in body temperature regulation.

As a result, individuals using Alstonia constricta may experience increased perspiration as their body attempts to cool itself down.
