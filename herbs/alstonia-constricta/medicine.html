What are the active constituents of Alstonia constricta ?

Alstonia constricta, also known as devil's breath, has active constituents such as flavonoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds.

These compounds are responsible for its medicinal properties. The alkaloids, including vobasine, vobtusine, and yohimbine, exhibit antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and cardiovascular stabilizing effects. The flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, show antioxidant and free radical scavenging activities.

These compounds contribute to the plant's purported uses in traditional medicine, including treatment of fever, malaria, and various infections.

What are the medicinal preparations of Alstonia constricta ?

Alstonia constricta, also known as devil's breath, has medicinal preparations such as extracts of its roots and stems, which are used to treat various ailments.

The extracts are used in traditional medicine to cure fever, skin infections, and respiratory issues. They also possess antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which contribute to their therapeutic effectiveness.

Additionally, the plant has been studied for its potential anti-cancer and antioxidant properties.

What are the possible side effect of using Alstonia constricta improperly?

Improper use of Alstonia constricta, also known as devil's breath, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as dizziness, headaches, and nausea.

Long-term consumption can lead to gastrointestinal issues, including vomiting and diarrhea. Additionally, high doses may cause tremors, anxiety, and insomnia due to the plant's potential to disrupt normal neurotransmitter function.

Overuse can also result in kidney damage, cardiovascular problems, and other severe health complications.

What precautions to take when using Alstonia constricta medicinally?

Before using Alstonia constricta, also known as devil's breath, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the safe dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Additionally, be aware of the plant's toxic alkaloids, such as alstonine and alstonidine, which can cause adverse effects when ingested in excessive amounts, including dizziness, nausea, and respiratory problems.

Proper handling and storage of the plant are also crucial.
