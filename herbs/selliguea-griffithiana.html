Selliguea griffithiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selliguea griffithiana has woody climbing stems that develop into thick lianas with large, simple, elliptical leaves featuring entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. The foliage is typically leathery in texture and arranged oppositely along the climbing vines. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually produce woody, dehiscent capsules. These capsules split open to reveal seeds that are often equipped with specialized appendages for dispersal. The overall architecture of the plant is adapted for navigating forest canopies through rapid vertical growth.
