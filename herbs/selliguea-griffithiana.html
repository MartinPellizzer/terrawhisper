Selliguea griffithiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Selliguea griffithiana?

Selligea griffithiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Taxonomically, this plant is situated within the family Polypodiaceae, specifically belonging to the genus Selliguea, with its unique identification as the species griffithiana.

      What are the herbal preparations of Selliguea griffithiana?

      Selliguea griffithiana is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a concentrated infusion of the leaves, or a topical paste made from crushed plant materials. These various preparations are primarily utilized in traditional medicine to treat specific ailments such as skin infections or internal discomfort. The bark is often boiled in water for extended periods to extract active compounds for medicinal use. Leaf infusions are frequently consumed as teas to address systemic health issues. Additionally, the raw plant matter can be ground into a thick consistency to serve as a medicinal poultice for external application.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Selliguea griffithiana can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and mucosal inflammation due to the presence of bioactive alkaloids and potentially toxic secondary metabolites. Contact with the plant's sap or crushed leaves may trigger localized redness, itching, or blistering on sensitive skin areas. If ingested, the plant could lead to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea or vomiting, depending on the concentration of its chemical constituents. While specific clinical data on human toxicity is limited, the presence of alkaloids suggests a need for extreme caution during handling. Users should always wear protective gear to avoid accidental exposure to these bioactive compounds.
