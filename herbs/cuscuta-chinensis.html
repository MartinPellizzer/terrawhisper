Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis)

Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis) is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, native to China, East Asia, and Southeast Asia. Traditionally, its seeds, stems, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, astringent, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.
