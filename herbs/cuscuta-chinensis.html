Chinese dodder

Cuscuta chinensis

Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis) is a parasitic flowering plant commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported ability to improve reproductive health and treat conditions such as infertility and impotence.

Taxonomy

Chinese Dodder, or Cuscuta chinensis, is a parasitic flowering plant belonging to the family Convolvulaceae, classified within the order Solanales, class Caryophyllopsida, division Spermatophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis) is a parasitic herb characterized by its yellow or orange-red, serpentine stems that lack leaves, flowers, and roots, relying entirely on its host plant for nutrients and water.

Distribution

Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis) is native to East Asia, including China, Japan, Korea, and parts of Southeast Asia, and is commonly found in moist, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, forests, and agricultural areas.

Traditional Uses

Chinese Dodder has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to clear heat, detoxify the body, and promote the circulation of qi and blood, often employed in formulations for treating conditions such as fever, inflammation, and skin disorders.

Medicinal Systems

Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis) has been traditionally used in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), Ayurveda, and Native American herbal practices for its purported tonic and restorative properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis) is characterized by the presence of a variety of bioactive compounds, including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications in traditional Chinese medicine.

Pharmacology

Chinese Dodder (Cuscuta chinensis) is known for its ability to invigorate the kidney, tonify the liver and kidney, and promote the production of essence and marrow, thereby supporting reproductive health, bone strength, and overall vitality in traditional Chinese medicine.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Dodder, scientifically known as Cuscuta chinensis, is an annual parasitic plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family. It is also called Chinese yellow dodder and is commonly found in Asia. The plant lacks roots, has yellowish stems, and produces small, greenish flowers. It is often referred to by various regional names in traditional herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for treating respiratory infections and digestive issues.

Poultice: Applied externally for skin infections and wounds.

Extract: Used for managing diabetes and hypertension.

Safety Profile

Chinese Dodder is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be used with caution in individuals with hypersensitivity to plants in the Convolvulaceae family.
