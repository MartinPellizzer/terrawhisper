    What is Salacia weberbaueri?

    Salacia weberbaueri is a species of flowering plant in the family Celastraceae.

    It is an evergreen shrub or small tree that grows up to 15 meters tall, with a straight or slightly curved stem and a brown or grayish bark. The leaves are alternate, elliptic or lanceolate, and have a length of 4-13 centimeters and a width of 2-5 centimeters, with a leathery texture and a glabrous upper surface and a pubescent lower surface. The inflorescence is a corymb, with 2-4 small, greenish or yellowish flowers, each 3-4 millimeters in diameter, and a 4-lobed calyx.

    The plant produces a yellowish or red fruit, a berry-like drupe, with a length of 6-8 millimeters and a width of 5-6 millimeters.
