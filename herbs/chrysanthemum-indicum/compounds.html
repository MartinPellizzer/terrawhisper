Chrysanthemum (Chrysanthemum indicum) Compounds

The primary active compounds in Chrysanthemum indicum include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and terpenoids contribute to its astringent and antimicrobial properties, while glycosides and saponins support its therapeutic uses in traditional medicine.

Chrysanthemum (Chrysanthemum indicum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Chrysanthemum indicum. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds are commonly used in traditional medicine to support respiratory and immune health. Flavonoids help reduce oxidative stress and may enhance the therapeutic effects of Chrysanthemum-based remedies.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chrysanthemum indicum. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports the therapeutic use of Chrysanthemum in traditional herbal medicine. It helps in treating conditions like fever and skin inflammation.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chrysanthemum indicum. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins help in reducing fever and supporting digestive health. They are found in various parts of the plant, including the leaves and flowers.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are key active compounds in Chrysanthemum indicum, contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. They are responsible for the plant's therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine. Terpenoids like chrysanthemum lactone and flavonoids are widely studied for their health benefits. These compounds support the plant's use in treating respiratory and skin conditions.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Chrysanthemum indicum. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. Glycosides in chrysanthemum are often responsible for its traditional use in treating fevers and respiratory conditions. These compounds are extracted and used in herbal formulations for their therapeutic effects.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Chrysanthemum indicum. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory and digestive conditions. Coumarin exhibits antispasmodic and anticoagulant properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for its therapeutic effects.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chrysanthemum indicum. It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. Saponin also aids in enhancing the bioavailability of other phytochemicals in the plant.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Chrysanthemum indicum. It contains volatile aromatic compounds like chamazulene and bisabolol, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. This oil is commonly used in herbal medicine for respiratory and digestive ailments.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Chrysanthemum indicum. It contributes to the plant's pharmacological effects, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Alkaloids in chrysanthemum are often used in traditional herbal medicine to treat respiratory and digestive disorders. Their presence enhances the therapeutic potential of the plant.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Chrysanthemum indicum. It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower colors and has antioxidant properties. In herbal medicine, anthocyanin supports skin health and may aid in reducing inflammation. Its presence highlights the plant's therapeutic potential in traditional remedies.
