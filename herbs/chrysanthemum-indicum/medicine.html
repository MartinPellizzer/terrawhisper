What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Chrysanthemum Indicum (Yellow Chrysanthemum)?

Chrysanthemum indicum, also known as yellow chrysanthemum, has health benefits such as reducing fever, alleviating headaches, and treating respiratory issues.

Its medicinal constituents include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenes, which are responsible for its pharmacological properties. The herb is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine to make teas, infusions, and decoctions. However, excessive consumption may cause stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea in some individuals.

To ensure safe use, it is recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before using Chrysanthemum indicum, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Chrysanthemum indicum.

What are the health benefits of Chrysanthemum indicum?

Chrysanthemum indicum, also known as yellow chrysanthemum, has health benefits such as reducing fever, alleviating headache and rheumatic pains, and treating skin problems like eczema and acne.

Its antioxidant properties also help protect the body against free radicals, which can cause cell damage and contribute to diseases.

Additionally, it may help improve blood circulation and lower blood pressure, making it a popular herbal remedy in traditional Chinese medicine.

What are the active constituents of Chrysanthemum indicum?

Chrysanthemum indicum, also known as yellow chrysanthemum, has active constituents such as flavonoids, triterpenoid saponins, and volatile oils.

The flavonoids, particularly kaempferol and quercetin, exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, while triterpenoid saponins display immunomodulatory and antiviral activities.

Volatile oils, rich in linalool and geraniol, contribute to the herb's anxiolytic and sedative effects, making it a valuable traditional remedy for various health conditions.

What are the medicinal preparations of Chrysanthemum indicum?

Chrysanthemum indicum, also known as yellow chrysanthemum, has medicinal preparations such as infusions, decoctions, and salves.

The flowers and leaves are used to make teas that help to relieve fever, cough, and cold symptoms. In traditional Chinese medicine, it is used to treat various ailments, including inflammation, skin conditions, and respiratory problems.

The antiviral and antibacterial properties of Chrysanthemum indicum make it a popular ingredient in many herbal remedies.

What are the possible side effect of using Chrysanthemum indicum improperly?

Improper use of Chrysanthemum indicum, also known as yellow chrysanthemum, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as diarrhea, nausea, and dizziness.

It can also cause allergic reactions in some individuals, including skin rashes and itching. Furthermore, excessive consumption may lead to liver and kidney damage.

Pregnant and breastfeeding women should use caution when consuming Chrysanthemum indicum, as it may interfere with fetal development or affect milk production.

What precautions to take when using Chrysanthemum indicum medicinally?

Before using Chrysanthemum indicum, also known as yellow chrysanthemum, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women, as it may interact with certain medications or exacerbate underlying health conditions.

Additionally, individuals with allergies or sensitivities to plants in the daisy family should exercise caution, and dosage should be carefully monitored to avoid adverse effects.
