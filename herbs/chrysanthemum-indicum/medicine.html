What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Chrysanthemum Indicum (Yellow Chrysanthemum)?

Chrysanthemum indicum, also known as yellow chrysanthemum, has health benefits such as lowering fever and relieving headaches.

The medicinal constituents of this herb include flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. Chrysanthemum indicum is often used to make teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are taken internally to promote respiratory health and alleviate symptoms of allergies. However, excessive consumption can cause stomach upset and allergic reactions in some individuals.

Precautions should be taken when using Chrysanthemum indicum, particularly for pregnant or breastfeeding women, as it may interact with other medications.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Chrysanthemum indicum.

What are the health benefits of Chrysanthemum indicum?

Chrysanthemum indicum has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and alleviating respiratory issues like bronchitis and asthma.

Its flavonoids and polyphenols have antioxidant properties that help protect against cell damage and oxidative stress.

Additionally, yellow chrysanthemum tea has been used to treat fever, headache, and skin conditions like eczema and acne, making it a popular herbal remedy in traditional medicine.

What are the active constituents of Chrysanthemum indicum?

The health benefits of Chrysanthemum indicum comes from its active constituents, such as flavonoids, triterpenoids, and phenolic acids.

The plant is rich in antioxidants like quercetin, kaempferol, and luteolin, which have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antiviral properties. Additionally, it contains saponins, sesquiterpenes, and terpenoids that contribute to its sedative, analgesic, and anti-allergic activities.

These medicinal constituents work together to provide a range of health benefits, including relieving fever, respiratory issues, and skin conditions.

What are the medicinal preparations of Chrysanthemum indicum?

Chrysanthemum indicum has different medicinal preparations, such as flowers, leaves, stems, and roots.

The flower infusion is used to treat fever, headache, and sinusitis, while the leaf and stem infusion helps alleviate diarrhea, dysentery, and respiratory issues. The root decoction is employed in traditional Chinese medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, treating conditions like arthritis, rheumatism, and skin diseases.

Additionally, the whole plant extract is used as a topical application to relieve pain and reduce inflammation.

What are the possible side effect of using Chrysanthemum indicum improperly?

Improper use of Chrysanthemum indicum increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as dizziness and nausea, allergic reactions including skin rashes and itching, stomach discomfort and diarrhea, rapid heartbeat and palpitations, blood pressure fluctuations, fatigue and insomnia, headaches and migraines, respiratory issues like asthma and bronchitis, kidney damage and increased risk of urinary tract infections.

What precautions to take when using Chrysanthemum indicum medicinally?

Before using Chrysanthemum indicum for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and ensure it is safe for your individual needs.

Additionally, be aware of potential allergic reactions or interactions with other medications, and start with small doses to monitor tolerance.

Proper identification and authentication of the plant are also crucial to avoid adulteration or misidentification.
