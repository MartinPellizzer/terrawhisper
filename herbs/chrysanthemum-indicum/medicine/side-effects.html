10 Possible Side Effects Of Chrysanthemum Indicum (Yellow Chrysanthemum)

Chrysanthemum indicum has some side effects when used improperly, such as nausea, dizziness, and stomach discomfort.

These side effects are usually caused by overdosing on the herb, allergic reactions, or interactions with other medications.

If not addressed, they can worsen daily life, causing people to struggle with fatigue, loss of appetite, and difficulty concentrating, ultimately affecting their productivity and overall well-being.

These compounds can stimulate the skin's natural response, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation.

Additionally, the plant's sap may contain allergenic proteins that can cause an immune response, resulting in skin irritation and discomfort for some individuals who use it.

The flavonoids and sesquiterpenes present in the plant may cause stomach upset, nausea, and vomiting in some individuals.

Additionally, the saponins found in Chrysanthemum indicum may also contribute to the discomfort by irritating the mucous membranes of the gut, leading to inflammation and digestive issues.
