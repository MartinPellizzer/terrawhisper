Botanical Identification of Chrysanthemum (Chrysanthemum indicum)

To identify Chrysanthemum (Chrysanthemum indicum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Chrysanthemum indicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Chrysanthemum, and species Chrysanthemum indicum. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the daisy family, highlighting its botanical significance in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Chrysanthemum indicum, first described by Linnaeus. It has several synonyms, including Chrysanthemum morifolium and Chrysanthemum zawadskii. Common names include chrysanthemum, garden chrysanthemum, and Chinese chrysanthemum. It is also known by regional and historical names such as Chrysanthemum chinense and Chrysanthemum japonicum.

Morphological Characteristics

Chrysanthemum indicum is an herbaceous plant with a fibrous root system. Its stems are upright and branching, often hairy. Leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture. The inflorescence is a flat cluster of flowers, typically yellow or white. Flowers are composite, with ray florets and disc florets. The fruit is a dry capsule containing small, oval seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chrysanthemum indicum can be distinguished by its alternate, lanceolate leaves with serrated edges and its clustered, daisy-like yellow flowers. It differs from similar species like Chrysanthemum morifolium by its smaller flower size and more robust, hairy stems. The presence of white, hairy glands on the leaves also aids in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chrysanthemum indicum is native to Asia, particularly China, India, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chrysanthemum indicum typically blooms from late summer to early autumn, around August to October, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs after flowering, usually in late autumn. For identification purposes, harvest timing is crucial, as the plant's appearance changes throughout its growth cycle.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Chrysanthemum indicum is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family, particularly within the genus Chrysanthemum. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with species like Chrysanthemum morifolium and Chrysanthemum zawadskii. These relationships are supported by molecular data, highlighting its position within the tribe Astereae.
