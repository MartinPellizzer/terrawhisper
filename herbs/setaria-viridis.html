Setaria viridis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Setaria viridis has an erect, annual growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 100 centimeters. Its reproductive structures are arranged in dense, bristly panicles that feature long, spreading hairs surrounding the spikelets. These spikelets are small and ovate, often containing a single grain that is adapted for efficient seed dispersal. The root system is fibrous, providing stability and nutrient uptake for its rapid lifecycle.
