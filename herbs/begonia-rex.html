Begonia rex

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia rex?

Begonia rex belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its lineage further, it is placed in the order Cucurbitaleales and the family Begoniaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by the genus Begonia and is specifically designated as the species rex.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia rex has asymmetrical, heart-shaped leaves characterized by prominent venation and diverse patterns of metallic colors, such as silver, pink, or deep burgundy. These leaves often feature serrated edges and a textured, sometimes waxy surface that enhances their visual complexity. The plant grows from a fleshy, rhizomatous stem that remains low to the ground. Small, delicate flowers typically emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters of white or pale pink. The overall structure is compact, focusing its morphological energy on the striking foliage rather than tall stems.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight, consistently moist but well-draining soil, and high humidity levels to thrive.

You should use a peat-based potting mix to ensure proper aeration for the sensitive root system. Watering should be frequent enough to keep the soil damp, but you must avoid getting moisture on the leaves to prevent fungal diseases. Maintaining a warm room temperature between 60 and 75 degrees Fahrenheit is ideal for its growth.

Regular fertilization during the spring and summer months will help enhance the vibrant colors of its foliage.
