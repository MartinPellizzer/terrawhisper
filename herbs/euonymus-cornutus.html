What's the taxonomical classification of Euonymus cornutus?

Euonymus cornutus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Celastrales. It further falls under the family Celastraceae, which contains the genus Euonymus, and is specifically identified as the species cornutus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euonymus cornutus has an upright, deciduous shrub habit characterized by slender, arching branches and small, ovate leaves that measure approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length. The foliage is typically dark green with finely serrated margins and a smooth texture. During the flowering period, small, inconspicuous greenish flowers emerge from the leaf axils. The plant produces distinctive fruit in the form of small, four-lobed capsules that transition from green to a bright pink or red color upon maturity. These capsules eventually split open to reveal bright orange or red seeds nestled within the lobes.
