Euonymus cornutus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euonymus cornutus has an upright, deciduous shrub habit characterized by slender, arching branches and small, ovate leaves that measure approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length. The foliage is typically dark green with finely serrated margins and a smooth texture. During the flowering period, small, inconspicuous greenish flowers emerge from the leaf axils. The plant produces distinctive fruit in the form of small, four-lobed capsules that transition from green to a bright pink or red color upon maturity. These capsules eventually split open to reveal bright orange or red seeds nestled within the lobes.

      What are the herbal preparations of Euonymus cornutus?

      Euonymus cornutus is prepared primarily as a decoction of the dried bark or as a concentrated tincture extracted with alcohol. The bark is typically boiled in water for an extended period to release its active alkaloids and glycosides. Some practitioners also use a simple infusion if the plant material is processed more gently. These liquid preparations are the most common methods used to deliver the plant's medicinal properties. Tinctures allow for a more shelf-stable and potent concentration of the botanical constituents.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Euonymus cornutus can cause skin irritation, redness, and itching if the plant comes into contact with human skin. This reaction is typically triggered by the sap contained within the stems and leaves of the shrub. Some individuals may also experience gastrointestinal distress, such as vomiting or stomach pain, if any part of the plant is ingested. While these side effects are generally localized or mild, they can be more severe in people with sensitive skin or allergies. It is important to wear gloves when pruning this species to prevent contact dermatitis.
