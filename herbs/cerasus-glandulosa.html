Cerasus glandulosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cerasus glandulosa has a woody, shrubby habit characterized by erect stems that often feature small, glandular hairs along their surface. The leaves are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape, possessing serrated margins and a leathery texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the foliage, usually appearing in small clusters. The fruit develops into small, fleshy drupes that change color as they reach maturity. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species within its specific ecological niche.
