Cerasus glandulosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Cerasus glandulosa?

Cerasus glandulosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its higher-level classification, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific biological designation within the genus Cerasus and the species glandulosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cerasus glandulosa has a woody, shrubby habit characterized by erect stems that often feature small, glandular hairs along their surface.

The leaves are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape, possessing serrated margins and a leathery texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the foliage, usually appearing in small clusters. The fruit develops into small, fleshy drupes that change color as they reach maturity.

These morphological traits collectively distinguish the species within its specific ecological niche.

      What are the herbal preparations of Cerasus glandulosa?

      Cerasus glandulosa is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to utilize its bioactive compounds. Some individuals might also experience dizziness or mild neurological changes following ingestion.
