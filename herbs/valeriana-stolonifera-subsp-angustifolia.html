Common Valerian (Valeriana Stolonifera Subsp. Angustifolia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Valeriana stolonifera subsp. angustifolia.

Common valerian, scientifically known as Valeriana stolonifera subsp. angustifolia, is a medicinal herb widely recognized for its calming and sleep-inducing properties. It is primarily valued for its ability to promote relaxation, reduce anxiety, and improve sleep quality, making it a popular adaptogen in traditional and modern wellness practices. Historically, it has been used in European and Asian traditional medicine to treat insomnia, nervousness, and digestive issues, with roots dating back centuries. In contemporary wellness, it is commonly found in herbal supplements and teas aimed at stress relief and sleep support. One of its unique features is its distinctive, earthy aroma and the presence of valerenic acid, a rare active compound that contributes to its sedative effects.
