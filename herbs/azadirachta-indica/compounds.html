Indian Lilac (Azadirachta indica) Compounds

The primary compounds in Indian Lilac include saponins, which have insecticidal properties, and terpenoids, known for their antifungal and antibacterial effects. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant activity, while tannins and alkaloids provide additional antimicrobial benefits. Essential oils and coumarin also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Indian Lilac (Azadirachta indica) Monograph

1. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Indian Lilac (Azadirachta indica). It exhibits antifungal, antibacterial, and antiviral properties, contributing to the plant's traditional use in treating skin infections and respiratory ailments. Saponin also aids in enhancing the immune response and reducing inflammation.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Azadirachta indica, commonly known as Indian Lilac. These compounds contribute to the plant's insecticidal and antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable resource in herbal medicine for treating skin infections and repelling pests.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Indian Lilac (Azadirachta indica) contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory issues. They also enhance the plant's overall therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin, found in Azadirachta indica, contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support wound healing and anti-inflammatory effects. It works synergistically with other bioactive compounds in the Indian Lilac to enhance its traditional medicinal applications.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Indian Lilac (Azadirachta indica). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and infections. Its presence enhances the overall therapeutic potential of Indian Lilac in herbal medicine.

6. Alkaloid

Azadirachtin is an alkaloid found in the Indian Lilac (Azadirachta indica). It is a key active compound known for its potent insecticidal properties, particularly effective against larvae and adult insects. This compound contributes significantly to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for pest control and skin disorders.

7. Essential oil

The essential oil of Azadirachta indica contains bioactive compounds like azadirachtin and nimbin. It exhibits insecticidal, antifungal, and antimicrobial properties. This oil is widely used in herbal medicine for its therapeutic and protective effects against pests and infections.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Azadirachta indica, commonly known as Indian Lilac. It contributes to the plant's insecticidal properties and has potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Coumarin is one of several key constituents responsible for the traditional medicinal uses of this plant.

9. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Indian Lilac (Azadirachta indica). It contributes to the plant's pesticidal and anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides in Indian Lilac are often responsible for its traditional use in treating skin infections and respiratory ailments. These compounds are extracted and used in herbal formulations for their therapeutic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Azadirachta indica, contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties. This compound helps neutralize free radicals, supporting overall health. It is part of the broader bioactive profile of Indian Lilac, enhancing its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.
