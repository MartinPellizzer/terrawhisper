Botanical Identification of Indian Lilac (Azadirachta indica)

To identify Indian Lilac (Azadirachta indica) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Indian Lilac, Azadirachta indica, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Meliaceae, genus Azadirachta, and species Azadirachta indica. This classification places it within the flowering plants and highlights its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Azadirachta indica is the scientific name for Indian Lilac, also known as neem. It was first described by the botanist Robert Sweet in 1786. The plant has numerous common names, including neem, margosa, and Indian lilac, and is widely recognized across South and Southeast Asia.

Morphological Characteristics

Azadirachta indica, commonly known as Indian Lilac, is a small to medium-sized tree with a dense, rounded crown. It has a robust root system that supports its growth in various soil types. The stem is straight and smooth, with a grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The leaves are simple, alternate, and pinnately compound, consisting of 4-6 pairs of elliptical leaflets. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, fragrant flowers that are yellowish-white with a red stigma. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds coated in a sticky, aromatic substance.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Azadirachta indica can be distinguished by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, and fruit that turns from green to red when mature. It is often confused with other mahogany species, but its distinct leaf shape and flowering pattern help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Azadirachta indica, or Indian Lilac, is native to the Indian subcontinent, including countries like India, Bangladesh, and Sri Lanka. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high temperatures and moderate rainfall. The tree prefers well-drained soils, such as sandy or loamy types, and is commonly found at altitudes ranging from sea level up to 900 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Azadirachta indica blooms from March to May, producing small, fragrant yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs between June and August, with seeds developing within the capsule fruits. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested during the dry season, from November to February, when the leaves and bark are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Azadirachta indica belongs to the genus Azadirachta within the family Meliaceae. Its closest relatives include species like Azadirachta excelsa and Azadirachta lancescens. It shares key family traits such as stipulate leaves and capsule fruits, which help distinguish it from other genera in the Meliaceae family.
