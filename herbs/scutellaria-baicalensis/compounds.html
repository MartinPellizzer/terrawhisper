Chinese Skullcap (Scutellaria baicalensis) Compounds

Scutellaria baicalensis contains several key compounds, including flavonoids like baicalin and wogonin, which have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Alkaloids such as baicalein contribute to its medicinal effects, while glycosides and phenolic acids support its therapeutic uses in traditional Chinese medicine.

Chinese Skullcap (Scutellaria baicalensis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Scutellaria baicalensis. They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds are known to support immune function and may help in treating conditions like inflammation and infections.

2. Alkaloid

Scutellaria baicalensis contains the alkaloid baicalein, which exhibits anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating infections and inflammatory conditions. Baicalein is one of the key active constituents responsible for the therapeutic effects of Chinese Skullcap.

3. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Scutellaria baicalensis. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Glycosides in Chinese Skullcap are often responsible for its traditional use in treating infections and fever. These compounds are extracted and used in various herbal formulations.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Scutellaria baicalensis, known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating infections and inflammation. This compound is widely studied for its potential therapeutic applications in modern medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Scutellaria baicalensis, commonly known as Chinese Skullcap. These compounds contribute to the plant's pharmacological effects, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Specific terpenoids like wogonin and baicalin are well-studied for their therapeutic potential in traditional and modern medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Scutellaria baicalensis. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin is also known for its potential role in reducing oxidative stress and supporting cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of Chinese Skullcap in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a key compound found in Scutellaria baicalensis. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also act as antioxidants, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound plays a role in the traditional use of Chinese Skullcap for its therapeutic effects.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Scutellaria baicalensis. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Saponin helps modulate immune responses and may support liver function. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Chinese Skullcap in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Scutellaria baicalensis. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Anthocyanin helps support immune function and may aid in reducing oxidative stress in the body. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Chinese Skullcap in herbal medicine.

10. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Scutellaria baicalensis. It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and may enhance the bioavailability of active constituents. The essential oil is often extracted through steam distillation and is used in traditional herbal formulations. It supports the plant's therapeutic properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
