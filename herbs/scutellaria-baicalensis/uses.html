Chinese Skullcap (Scutellaria baicalensis) Uses

Scutellaria baicalensis is used to treat influenza, pneumonia, and bronchitis due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It also helps with allergic rhinitis and gastrointestinal infections by reducing inflammation and supporting immune function. Additionally, it has shown potential in managing lung cancer and inflammatory bowel disease through its antioxidant and antitumor effects.

Chinese Skullcap (Scutellaria baicalensis) Monograph

1. Influenza

Scutellaria baicalensis is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat influenza due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce fever, relieve sore throat, and alleviate respiratory symptoms. The herb is often combined with other herbs in formulas to enhance its effectiveness against viral infections.

2. Pneumonia

Scutellaria baicalensis is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat pneumonia due to its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. It helps reduce lung inflammation and supports immune function, aiding recovery. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in clearing lung heat and resolving phlegm.

3. Allergic rhinitis

Scutellaria baicalensis has shown potential in alleviating symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. The active compound baicalein may inhibit histamine release and reduce nasal congestion. It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine to support respiratory health during allergy seasons. Clinical studies suggest it may complement conventional treatments for persistent allergic rhinitis.

4. Bronchitis

Scutellaria baicalensis is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. It helps reduce mucus production and soothe respiratory irritation. Often combined with other herbs, it supports clearing lung congestion and easing coughing symptoms.

5. Lung cancer

Scutellaria baicalensis has shown potential in the treatment of lung cancer due to its anti-cancer compounds like baicalin and wogonin, which inhibit tumor growth and induce apoptosis. Studies suggest it may enhance the effectiveness of conventional therapies and reduce side effects, though more research is needed for clinical application.

6. Acute bronchitis

Scutellaria baicalensis is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat acute bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce coughing, mucus production, and respiratory inflammation, supporting recovery. Often combined with other herbs, it is prescribed to alleviate symptoms and enhance lung function during acute episodes.

7. Gastrointestinal infection

Scutellaria baicalensis exhibits antimicrobial properties that make it effective against gastrointestinal infections caused by bacteria and viruses. Its active compounds, such as baicalin and wogonin, help reduce inflammation and inhibit pathogen growth in the digestive tract. It is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine to support recovery from stomach and intestinal infections.

8. Inflammatory bowel disease

Scutellaria baicalensis has shown potential in managing inflammatory bowel disease due to its anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. Its active compounds, such as baicalin and wogonin, help reduce intestinal inflammation and oxidative stress. Clinical studies suggest it may support symptom relief and mucosal healing in conditions like ulcerative colitis and Crohn's disease.

9. Gastroenteritis

Scutellaria baicalensis is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat gastroenteritis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce intestinal inflammation and soothe digestive discomfort. The herb is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in managing symptoms like diarrhea and abdominal pain.

10. Gastritis

Scutellaria baicalensis is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat gastritis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce stomach inflammation and soothe irritation, often in formulations aimed at balancing digestive function and reducing symptoms like pain and indigestion. Its active compounds, such as baicalin and wogonin, contribute to its therapeutic effects on the gastrointestinal tract.
