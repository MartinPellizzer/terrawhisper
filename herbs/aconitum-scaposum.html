Aconitum scaposum

What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum scaposum?

Aconium scaposum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, falling specifically within the genus Aconitum, and is identified by its unique species designation, scaposum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aconium scaposum has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems that reach heights of up to 150 centimeters and bear large, palmately lobed leaves with deeply divided segments. The foliage is typically dark green and displays a distinctively dissected structure typical of the Ranunculaceae family. Its inflorescence consists of numerous zygomorphic, helmet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep blue to violet. These blossoms are arranged in loose racemes and feature a prominent upper sepal that forms a protective hood over the reproductive organs. The root system is composed of a thick, tuberous rhizome that allows the plant to persist through dormant periods.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe toxicity leading to nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, dizziness, arrhythmia, bradycardia, hypotension, paresthesia, convulsions, respiratory failure, and cardiac arrest. The alkaloids within the plant act on the nervous and cardiovascular systems to produce intense gastrointestinal distress. Neurological symptoms often manifest as tingling sensations or paresthesia followed by violent convulsions. Cardiovascular complications are particularly dangerous, as the toxin can induce a slowed heart rate known as bradycardia and a significant drop in blood pressure. Ultimately, the progression of poisoning may lead to life-threatening respiratory failure or sudden cardiac arrest.
