Dracocephalum moldavica

What's the taxonomical classification of Dracocephalum moldavica?

Dracocephalum moldavica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Lamiaceae, a group well-known for its aromatic properties. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Dracocephalum, specifically identified by its species name, moldavica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dracocephalum moldavica has square, decussate stems that reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a grayish-green hue and a slightly textured surface. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes of small, bilabiate flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. Each flower features a prominent upper lip and a smaller, three-lobed lower lip. The plant also develops small, nut-like fruits that are typical of the Lamiaceae family.

What are the pharmacological activities of Dracocephalum moldavica?

This plant has antibacterial, antimicrobial, antioxidant, neuroprotective, and spasmolytic pharmacological activities. Its rich composition of bioactive compounds allows it to effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. The presence of secondary metabolites provides significant antioxidant properties that combat oxidative stress in biological systems. As discussed in a study, the essential oil of Dracocephalum moldavica demonstrated antibacterial effects on Enterococcus faecalis. The research utilized micro-broth dilution and disc diffusion methods to evaluate the antimicrobial activity through minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) and minimum bactericidal concentration (MBC) parameters. While the essential oils of the mentioned plants showed significant antibacterial effects on E. faecalis, the study found that the greatest antimicrobial activity belonged to ZT. The results indicate that Dracocephalum moldavica is one of the medicinal plants evaluated for its potential use in a clinical setting.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds rosmarinic acid, chlorogenic acid, caffeic acid, kaempferol, and apihenin. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Rosmarinic and caffeic acids are known for their ability to scavenge free radicals within biological systems. Additionally, the presence of chlorogenic acid provides potent metabolic benefits. The flavonoids kaempferol and apihenin further enhance the plant's therapeutic potential by supporting cellular health.

As discussed in a study, Dracocephalum moldavica contains various phenolic compounds including chlorogenic acid, rosmarinic acid, gallic acid, cinnamic acid, caffeic acid, rutin, p-Coumaric acid, apiigenin, and quercetin. The major abundant phenolic compound detected in the extracts of the plant is p-Coumaric acid. Specific amounts of gallic acid, rosmarinic acid, caffeic acid, quercetin, and rutin were found to be highest when using a 75:25 ratio of NH4+:NO3-. The essential oil composition includes geraenial, geranyl acetate, geraniol, and nera로, which reached highest levels at the 75:25, 25:75, and 50:50 ratios of NH4+:NO3- respectively. The plant also contains total carotenoids, chlorophyll a, and chlorophyll b.
