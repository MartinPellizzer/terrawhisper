What's the taxonomical classification of Ixora henryi?

Ixora henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the Rubiaceae family, specifically placed within the genus Ixora, and is identified by the species name henryi.

      What are the herbal preparations of Ixora henryi?

      Ixora henryi is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the intended medicinal application. The leaves and stems are often boiled in water to create a liquid extract used for internal consumption. In some traditional practices, the plant material is crushed into a fine pulp to form a poultice for skin ailments. This paste is applied directly to affected areas to utilize its topical properties. These various methods allow practitioners to tailor the plant's potency to specific health needs.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ixora henryi can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or pets. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may lead to localized redness, itching, or contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. While toxicity levels are not extensively documented in humans, consuming any part of the plant could result in nausea or stomach upset. Pets that chew on the foliage might experience similar digestive issues or mild swelling of the mouth. It is always best to handle the plant with gloves to prevent unexpected dermatological responses.
