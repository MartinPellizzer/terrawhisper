Ixora henryi

What's the taxonomical classification of Ixora henryi?

Ixora henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the Rubiaceae family, specifically placed within the genus Ixora, and is identified by the species name henryi.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, digestive issues, wounds, and inflammation. The leaves and stems are frequently prepared in traditional medicine to manage various dermatological ailments. Practitioners often utilize extracts from the plant to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort and promote healing in open cuts. It is also valued for its ability to reduce swelling and systemic inflammation within the body. This botanical application remains a significant part of local medicinal practices.
