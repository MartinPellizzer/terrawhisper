Celastrus paniculatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Celastrus paniculatus?

Celastrus paniculatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Celastrales and is a member of the family Celastraceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Celastrus, specifically identified by its species name, paniculatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus paniculatus has an upright, deciduous shrub form characterized by opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical with entire margins. The stems are woody and can become somewhat spreading as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in terminal panicles and emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers transition into distinctive fruit capsules that eventually split open to reveal bright orange or red arils. These colorful structures serve to attract birds for seed dispersal throughout the growing season.
