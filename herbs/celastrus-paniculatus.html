Celastrus paniculatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celastrus paniculatus has an upright, deciduous shrub form characterized by opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical with entire margins. The stems are woody and can become somewhat spreading as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in terminal panicles and emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers transition into distinctive fruit capsules that eventually split open to reveal bright orange or red arils. These colorful structures serve to attract birds for seed dispersal throughout the growing season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the eastern and central United States, spanning from southern New England through the Appalachian Mountains and extending west toward the Great Plains. It commonly inhabits open woodlands, thickets, and forest edges throughout various states including New York, Pennsylvania, and Ohio. Its range often follows river valleys and moist slopes where it can thrive in partial shade. Populations can be found as far south as the Gulf Coast states and as far north as southern Ontario in Canada.
