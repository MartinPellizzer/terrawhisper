      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Arceuthobium pini can cause severe structural damage and eventual death to pine trees by acting as a parasitic dwarf mistletoe that drains vital nutrients and water. This parasite enters the host through small wounds and develops complex, branched structures within the tree's tissue. As the mistletoe grows, it induces the formation of abnormal swellings known as galls on the branches. These galls redirect the tree's resources away from healthy growth and toward the parasite's own development. Over time, the combined stress of nutrient depletion and structural deformity weakens the host significantly. This process often leads to branch dieback and can compromise the entire tree's ability to survive environmental stressors.
