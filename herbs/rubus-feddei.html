Rubus feddei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus feddei has arching or trailing stems that are covered in dense, fine prickles and possess small, simple leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically features three to five leaflets that are ovate in shape and exhibit a slightly pubescent texture on both surfaces. Its flowers are relatively small and emerge from the leaf axils, displaying white petals and numerous stamens. The resulting fruit is a small, aggregate drupelet structure that transitions in color as it matures. These morphological traits are characteristic of the Rubus genus and help distinguish this specific species within its habitat.
