Coelogyne flaccida

What's the taxonomical classification of Coelogyne flaccida?

Coelogyne flacciida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asparagales, the plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae. More specifically, it is situated within the genus Coelogyne, and its unique biological identity is defined by its species name, flaccida.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, respiratory ailments, and skin infections in traditional medicinal practices. It is frequently applied to alleviate symptoms of coughs, bronchitis, and other lung-related issues. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may help reduce swelling and pain in various parts of the body. Additionally, its extracts are sometimes utilized to address dermatological concerns such as irritation or minor wounds. Local practitioners rely on its properties to manage general malaise and febrile conditions.
