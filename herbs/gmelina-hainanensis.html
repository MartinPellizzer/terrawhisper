What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gmelina hainanensis has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by slender stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite pattern. The foliage features fine pubescence on both the upper and lower surfaces, giving the plant a soft texture. Its inflorescences consist of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The reproductive structures eventually produce small, woody capsules containing the seeds. These morphological traits are adaptations that allow the species to thrive within its specific ecological niche.
