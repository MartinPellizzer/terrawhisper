What's the taxonomical classification of Staphylea bumalda?

Staphylea bumalda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Crossosomatales. This plant is uniquely positioned within the family Staphyleaceae, falling under the genus Staphylea, and is specifically identified by its species name, bumalda.

      What are the herbal preparations of Staphylea bumalda?

      Staphylae bumalda is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the intended medicinal use. Infusions are often created by steeping the softer plant parts in hot water for a milder therapeutic effect.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Staphylia bumalda can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if ingested or applied incorrectly. Topical application might lead to redness, itching, or swelling at the site of contact. If the plant parts are consumed, they could potentially cause nausea, stomach cramps, or vomiting. It is essential to exercise caution and consult a professional before using this plant for any medicinal purpose.
