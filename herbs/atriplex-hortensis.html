What's the taxonomical classification of Atriplex hortensis?

Atriplex hortensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Amaranthaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Atriplex, with its specific designation being the species hortensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Atriplex hortensis has ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long, characterized by a fleshy texture and distinctively mealy or scurfy surfaces due to salt-secreting bladder hairs. The plant structure consists of an erect, branched stem that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its inflorescences are small and inconspicuous, appearing as clusters of greenish flowers located in the leaf axils or at the terminal ends of the branches. The reproductive parts include tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that lack showy petals. The root system is a sturdy taproot that allows the species to anchor firmly in saline or disturbed soils.
