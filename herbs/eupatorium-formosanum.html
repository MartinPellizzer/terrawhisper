Eupatorium formosanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Eupatorium formosanum?

Eupatorium formosanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, placing it among the sunflowers and their relatives. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Eupatorium, with its specific identification being the species formosanum.

      What are the herbal preparations of Eupatorium formosanum?

      Eupatorium formosanum is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion made from its dried roots and leaves to extract its medicinal compounds. Traditional practitioners often simmer the plant material in water for an extended period to ensure the active constituents are fully released. In some regional practices, the plant is also processed into a concentrated liquid extract or a tincture using alcohol as a solvent. These liquid preparations are frequently consumed orally to address specific ailments related to its traditional uses. Some local preparations may even involve using the plant in topical applications such as poultices.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Eupatorium formosanum can cause severe skin irritation, localized swelling, and allergic dermatitis upon direct contact with the epidermis. The plant contains potent chemical compounds that may trigger redness and itching if the sap touches the skin. In some sensitive individuals, accidental ingestion could lead to gastrointestinal distress or nausea. Prolonged exposure to the foliage might result in blistering or a persistent rash that requires medical attention. Always wear protective gloves when handling this species to mitigate these physiological reactions.
