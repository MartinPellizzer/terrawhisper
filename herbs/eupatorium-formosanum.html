Eupatorium formosanum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eupatorium formosanum has a perennial growth habit characterized by upright, woody stems and deeply lobed, serrated leaves that typically emerge in an alternate pattern. The foliage displays a distinct texture with prominent venation and a dark green coloration on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters along the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. The reproductive structures eventually develop into small, fleshy fruits that serve as a primary dispersal mechanism. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species within its specific ecological niche.
