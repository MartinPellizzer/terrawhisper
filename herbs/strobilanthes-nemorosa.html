Strobilanthes nemorosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Strobilanthes nemorosa has erect, woody stems and oppositely arranged, elliptical leaves that are characterized by prominent longitudinal veins and slightly serrated margins. The foliage is typically a deep green color with a smooth texture on the upper surface. During its blooming period, the plant produces dense, terminal racemes of tubular flowers that exhibit a striking purplish-blue hue. These blossoms are often accompanied by colorful bracts that enhance the visual impact of the inflorescence. The overall structure of the plant allows it to grow in shaded understory environments where its lush greenery thrives.
