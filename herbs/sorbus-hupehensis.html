Sorbus hupehensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Sorbus hupehensis?

Sorbus hupehensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. At the most specific levels of its biological classification, it is identified by the genus Sorbus and the unique species epithet hupehensis.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Sorbus hupehensis can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, or potential toxicity if the seeds or unripe fruits are consumed in large quantities. While many species within the Sorbus genus are used for culinary purposes, certain parts may contain cyanogenic glycosides that release toxic hydrogen cyanide during digestion. Individuals with known sensitivities to the Rosaceae family should exercise extreme caution to avoid contact with the plant. Skin irritation or contact dermatitis can occur if the sap or foliage touches sensitive areas of the body. Monitoring for symptoms like nausea, dizziness, or localized swelling is recommended when interacting with this species.
