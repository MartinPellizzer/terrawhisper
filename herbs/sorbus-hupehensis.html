Sorbus hupehensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sorbus hupehensis has pinnately compound leaves characterized by numerous small, serrated leaflets and a woody structure with a distinctive bark pattern. The leaf blades are typically elliptic or lanceolate in shape, tapering towards both the tip and the base. These compound leaves are arranged alternately along the stems, which support dense clusters of small, white flowers during the blooming season. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy pomes that often turn reddish or orange as they ripen. The overall growth habit is that of a small tree or large shrub, adapted to specific mountainous forest environments.
