Symplocos dolichotricha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Symplocos dolichotricha has lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves characterized by long, spreading hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. The leaf margins are typically entire, while the venation is distinct and provides structural support to the blade. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are often arranged in axillary clusters. Its woody stems are covered in a fine pubescence that matches the texture of the foliage. The fruit develops into small drupes that serve as a key identifying feature of the species.
