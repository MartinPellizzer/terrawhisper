Duguetia staudtii

What's the taxonomical classification of Duguetia staudtii?

Duguetia staudtii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Magnoliales, it falls under the family Annonaceae. Within this family, the plant is identified by the genus Duguetia, and its specific taxonomic designation is completed by the species name staudtii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Duguetia staudtii contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, and terpenoids. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's potential pharmacological activities, such as antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Specific studies on members of the Euphorbiaceae family suggest that these compounds may play a role in protecting cells against oxidative stress. Researchers continue to investigate how the unique profile of these phytochemicals can be utilized for therapeutic purposes. The presence of these diverse molecules makes the species a subject of interest for ethnobotanical and pharmacological research.

What are the therapeutic actions of Duguetia staudtii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach aches, intestinal parasites, and various types of inflammation. Traditional healers often prepare decoctions from the leaves or bark to address these specific ailments. For digestive issues, the plant serves as a remedy to soothe pain and expel worms from the gut. It is also frequently applied topically to help clear up dermatological conditions and reduce swelling. These medicinal properties make it a significant resource in local traditional medicine.
