Duguetia staudtii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Within this family, the plant is identified by the genus Duguetia, and its specific taxonomic designation is completed by the species name staudtii.

    As discussed in a study, the Cameroonian medicinal plant Duguetia staudtii (Engl. and Diels) Chatrou naturally harbours the endophytic fungus Simplicillium subtropicum (SPC3). From the broth-culture of this fungus, two new tetracyclic polyketides known as simplicilones A (1) and B (2) were isolated. These compounds demonstrated weak cytotoxic effects against the KB3.1 cell line. Specifically, the in vitro cytotoxicity (IC50) was measured at 25 µg/mL for simplicolone A (1) and 29 µg/mL for simplicolone B (2). These substances remained inactive against the tested Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria as well as fungi.
