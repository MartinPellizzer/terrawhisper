Litsea veitchiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Litsea veitchiana has elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically 5 to 12 centimeters long, featuring entire margins and prominent pinnate venation with distinctively curved secondary veins. The foliage is characterized by a coriaceous texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. The stems are woody and often possess a slightly roughened bark texture as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary panicles, which eventually give rise to small, fleshy drupes. These morphological traits are characteristic of many species within the Lauraceae family.
