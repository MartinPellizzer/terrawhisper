    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, kidney stones, and electrolyte imbalance due to its high concentration of oxalic acid. When ingested, the acid reacts with calcium in the body to form insoluble calcium oxalate crystals. These crystals can irritate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to immediate stomach distress and digestive upset. Over time, the accumulation of these crystals in the urinary system significantly increases the risk of developing painful kidney stones. Furthermore, the depletion of calcium levels through this process can result in a dangerous electrolyte imbalance throughout the body.
