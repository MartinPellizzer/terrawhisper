What's the taxonomical classification of Oxalis griffithii?

Oxalis griffithii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the evolutionary hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant falls under the order Oxalidales and is a member of the family Oxalidaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic designation is defined by the genus Oxalis and the species griffithii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxalis griffithii has a perennial herbaceous structure characterized by trifoliate leaves with obovate leaflets and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pale yellow to creamy white. The leaves are arranged on long, slender petioles that emerge from an underground bulbous rhizome. Each leaf consists of three distinct leaflets that exhibit a delicate, clover-like appearance. The inflorescence typically presents single or small clusters of flowers atop thin stalks. These reproductive structures are supported by a delicate stem system that allows the plant to spread through its specialized rhizomatous growth.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, kidney stones, and electrolyte imbalance due to its high concentration of oxalic acid. When ingested, the acid reacts with calcium in the body to form insoluble calcium oxalate crystals. These crystals can irritate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to immediate stomach distress and digestive upset. Over time, the accumulation of these crystals in the urinary system significantly increases the risk of developing painful kidney stones. Furthermore, the depletion of calcium levels through this process can result in a dangerous electrolyte imbalance throughout the body.
