What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caragana turfanensis has a woody, perennial growth habit characterized by erect stems and pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of leaflets. The foliage typically presents a lush green color and provides a dense texture to the plant's structure. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during its blooming period. The root system is well-developed to support its upright posture in various soil conditions. Its overall morphology allows it to adapt effectively to its specific ecological niche.

      What are the herbal preparations of Caragana turfanensis?

      Caragana turfanensis is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice using the dried bark and leaves of the plant. These herbal forms are traditionally used to extract specific phytochemicals that may possess medicinal properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Caragana turfanensis can cause severe skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and localized swelling upon direct physical contact with the plant's sap or foliage. The chemical compounds within the plant act as irritants that trigger inflammatory responses in sensitive individuals. If the juices come into contact with the eyes, they may cause stinging, redness, or temporary blurred vision. Ingestion of any part of the specimen could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or abdominal cramping. It is essential to wear protective gloves when handling this species to avoid these adverse reactions.
