Polygonum kawagoeanum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum kawagoeanum has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by slender, reddish stems and lanceolate leaves with acute tips. The foliage is typically arranged alternately along the stem and features smooth margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, often displaying a delicate white or pale pink hue. The plant develops distinctive swollen nodes at intervals along its length. Its root system consists of a rhizomatous structure that facilitates spreading through soil.
