Whorled Eupatorium (Eupatorium Verticillatum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Eupatorium verticillatum.

Whorled eupatorium, scientifically known as Eupatorium verticillatum, is a perennial herb native to North America, valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional healing practices. This plant, often referred to as "whorled hemp" or "blueweed," contains compounds that support immune function and have adaptogenic qualities, helping the body manage stress. Traditionally, Indigenous peoples used it to treat respiratory conditions, fever, and as a calming tonic, while early settlers incorporated it into herbal remedies for digestive and nervous system support. In modern wellness, it is used in herbal teas and supplements to promote respiratory health and reduce inflammation, with its distinctive blue-green flowers adding a unique visual element. Notably, its historical use dates back to colonial times, and its rare active compounds continue to intrigue researchers in the field of herbal medicine.
