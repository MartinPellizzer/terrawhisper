To identify Dragon Herb (Houttuynia cordata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Dragon Herb, or Houttuynia cordata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Sarmentaceae, and it is part of the genus Houttuynia. The species is Houttuynia cordata, a commonly used herb in traditional medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Houttuynia cordata is the scientific name for Dragon Herb. It was first described by the botanist Georg von Houttuyn in 1776. The plant is also known by several synonyms, including Houttuynia cordata Thunb. and Houttuynia cordata Thunb. ex Juss. It is commonly referred to by its English name, Dragon Herb, and has various regional and historical names in different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Houttuynia cordata is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. It has square, ascending stems that are often hairy. The leaves are heart-shaped, opposite, and have a glossy green surface with a distinct, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in terminal clusters, followed by dark red, berry-like fruits containing tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Houttuynia cordata can be distinguished by its heart-shaped leaves with a light green underside, a strong aromatic scent when crushed, and small white flowers with yellow centers. It differs from similar species like Pilea peperomioides by its leaf shape and the presence of a distinct, spicy fragrance.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Houttuynia cordata is native to Southeast Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers moist, well-drained soils. It is commonly found at low to mid-altitude regions, typically between 200 and 1,500 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Houttuynia cordata blooms from June to August, producing small white or yellowish flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the herb is typically harvested in early summer when the leaves are young and the plant is most aromatic.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Houttuynia cordata is closely related to species within the Saururaceae family. It shares key characteristics with other members of its genus, such as similar leaf morphology and reproductive structures. Its phylogenetic position reflects its close ties to other aquatic and semi-aquatic plants in the family. These relationships aid in understanding its ecological and medicinal properties.
