10 Possible Side Effects Of Houttuynia Cordata

Houttuynia cordata has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

These side effects are often caused by excessive dosage, poor preparation, or individual sensitivities.

For example, digestive problems can worsen irritable bowel syndrome symptoms, leading to increased frequency of bowel movements, bloating, and discomfort, severely impacting daily life and work productivity.

1. Causes stomach upset

Houttuynia cordata causes stomach upset due to its ability to stimulate digestive processes, increasing gastric acid secretion and contractions in the gastrointestinal tract.

This can lead to symptoms such as bloating, nausea, and discomfort.

Additionally, Houttuynia cordata's saponins may interact with certain medications or exacerbate existing digestive issues, further contributing to stomach upset.

2. Induces allergic reactions

Houttuynia cordata induces allergic reactions due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant contains a number of bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and terpenoids, which can cause an immune response in some individuals. This may lead to symptoms such as skin rash, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the plant's high levels of histamine and other biogenic amines have been shown to exacerbate allergic reactions, making it a potential allergen for those with sensitivities.

3. Produces dizziness

Houttuynia cordata produces dizziness due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system and affect blood pressure.

The herb's bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and alkaloids, may cause a temporary disruption in balance and equilibrium, leading to feelings of dizziness or lightheadedness.

Additionally, Houttuynia cordata has been known to increase heart rate and cardiac output, which can further contribute to these side effects.

4. Triggers nausea

Houttuynia cordata triggers nausea due to its ability to interact with certain medications and alter their absorption rates in the body.

Specifically, its high concentration of alkaloids can slow down gastric emptying, leading to discomfort and nausea. Additionally, the herb's potential to stimulate digestive secretions may further exacerbate these symptoms.

As a result, some individuals may experience gastrointestinal upset when using Houttuynia cordata.

5. Increases heart rate

Houttuynia cordata increases heart rate due to its stimulatory effects on the cardiovascular system.

The herb contains compounds that can increase blood pressure and stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, leading to an elevation in heart rate. This is thought to be related to the herb's ability to enhance adrenaline release and increase cardiac output.

As a result, individuals using Houttuynia cordata may experience palpitations, tachycardia, or other cardiovascular-related symptoms.

6. Lowers blood pressure

Houttuynia cordata lowers blood pressure due to its ability to dilate blood vessels and improve cardiovascular function.

This is attributed to the presence of compounds such as stigmasterol and beta-sitosterol, which have been shown to relax blood vessel walls, allowing for increased blood flow and reduced blood pressure.

Additionally, Houttuynia cordata's antioxidant properties may help protect against oxidative stress and inflammation, further contributing to its vasodilatory effects on the cardiovascular system.

7. Causes respiratory distress

Houttuynia cordata causes respiratory distress due to its high concentration of saponins and flavonoids, which can trigger an allergic response in some individuals.

The saponins may stimulate the release of histamine, a chemical mediator involved in allergic reactions, leading to constriction of the airways and bronchospasm.

Additionally, the flavonoids may exacerbate inflammation in the lungs, further contributing to respiratory distress symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

8. Stimulates excessive sweating

Houttuynia cordata stimulates excessive sweating due to its ability to increase sweat production and release toxins through the skin.

Its diuretic properties can lead to an increase in urine output, resulting in dehydration and a subsequent increase in sweat rate.

Additionally, Houttuynia cordata's stimulatory effect on the nervous system may also contribute to excessive sweating by triggering sympathetic nerve responses that promote sweat gland activity.

9. Inhibits liver function

Houttuynia cordata inhibits liver function due to its ability to interact with certain enzymes in the body.

Specifically, it has been shown to inhibit the activity of cytochrome P450, a group of enzymes that play a crucial role in the metabolism and detoxification of medications and other foreign substances.

This inhibition can lead to decreased liver function, potentially resulting in a range of adverse effects including nausea, fatigue, and abdominal pain.

10. Interferes with thyroid hormones

Houttuynia cordata interferes with thyroid hormones by suppressing the production of thyroid-stimulating hormone and reducing the levels of thyroxine in the body.

This is likely due to the presence of certain compounds in the plant, such as flavonoids and alkaloids, which have been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in thyroid hormone synthesis.

As a result, individuals using Houttuynia cordata may experience symptoms such as fatigue, weight gain, and depression, similar to hypothyroidism.
