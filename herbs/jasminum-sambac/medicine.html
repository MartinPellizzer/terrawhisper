What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Jasminum Sambac (Arabian Jasmine)?

Jasminum sambac, also known as arabian jasmine, has health benefits such as reducing anxiety and stress due to its calming effects.

The herb contains medicinal constituents like flavonoids, saponins, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. Traditional medicine utilizes the herb to prepare infusions, decoctions, and essential oils, which are used to treat various ailments. However, excessive consumption may lead to side effects like nausea, dizziness, and skin irritation.

Pregnant women and individuals with allergies should exercise caution when using this herb, and it is recommended to consult a healthcare professional before consumption.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Jasminum sambac.

What are the health benefits of Jasminum sambac?

Jasminum sambac, also known as arabian jasmine, has health benefits such as reducing stress and anxiety due to its calming effects.

It has antioxidant properties that can protect against oxidative stress and inflammation, which may help alleviate symptoms of conditions like arthritis and diabetes. Additionally, its essential oils have antimicrobial properties, making it potentially effective against infections and promoting skin health.

Its therapeutic potential has been explored in traditional medicine.

What are the active constituents of Jasminum sambac?

Jasminum sambac, also known as arabian jasmine, has active constituents such as flavonoids, including apigenin, luteolin, and kaempferol, which exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Saponins, triterpenoids, and coumarins are also present in the plant, contributing to its therapeutic effects.

These bioactive compounds have been traditionally used to manage stress, anxiety, and sleep disorders, as well as to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress.

What are the medicinal preparations of Jasminum sambac?

Jasminum sambac, also known as arabian jasmine, has medicinal preparations such as essential oils, extracts, and tinctures.

The oil is used in aromatherapy to reduce stress and promote relaxation. In traditional medicine, it is used to treat skin conditions, anxiety, and insomnia. The extract is also used to treat digestive issues, fever, and respiratory problems.

Furthermore, it is used as a natural antiseptic and anti-inflammatory agent.

What are the possible side effect of using Jasminum sambac improperly?

Improper use of Jasminum sambac, also known as arabian jasmine, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and digestive issues.

High doses can also lead to interactions with medications and exacerbate pre-existing conditions, particularly for people with cardiovascular problems or those using blood thinners.

Moderate use under medical guidance is recommended to minimize the risk of adverse effects.

What precautions to take when using Jasminum sambac medicinally?

Before using Jasminum sambac, also known as arabian jasmine, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional, especially if you have allergies or are pregnant/breastfeeding.

Additionally, the plant's essential oil may interact with certain medications, including sedatives, and cause drowsiness.

Proper identification and dosing are also crucial to avoid adverse effects, as the plant's constituents can be toxic in excessive quantities.
