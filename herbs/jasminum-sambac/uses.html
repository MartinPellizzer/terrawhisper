Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) Uses

Common Jasmine is used to treat skin infections, irritation, and inflammation, as well as promote wound healing and reduce signs of skin aging. It also helps with respiratory issues like bronchitis, asthma, and mucous congestion by reducing inflammation and clearing airways.

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) Monograph

1. Skin infections

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oil extracted from its flowers has been shown to inhibit bacterial growth, making it effective for conditions like fungal infections and minor wounds. Its soothing qualities also help reduce inflammation and promote healing.

2. Skin irritation

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is used topically to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. The essential oil can be diluted and applied to affected areas to reduce redness, itching, and inflammation. It is particularly effective for minor burns, rashes, and eczema. Its calming aroma also helps ease the discomfort associated with skin conditions.

3. Wound healing

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is used in traditional herbal medicine to promote wound healing due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant's essential oils and extracts help reduce infection risk and accelerate tissue repair. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to support the healing process.

4. Skin aging

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is used in traditional herbal medicine to combat skin aging due to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oils and extracts help reduce wrinkles, improve skin elasticity, and protect against free radical damage, making it a valuable ingredient in anti-aging skincare formulations.

5. Skin inflammation

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is used topically to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The essential oil and flowers can be applied directly to affected areas to reduce redness, swelling, and infection. It is particularly effective for conditions like eczema and minor skin infections.

6. Respiratory infections

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is used in traditional medicine to support respiratory health. Its essential oil has antimicrobial properties that may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. It is often inhaled or applied externally to reduce congestion and soothe irritated airways.

7. Inflammation

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is used to reduce inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory compounds like flavonoids and essential oils. It helps alleviate symptoms in conditions such as arthritis and skin inflammation. Topical applications are common, while internal use may involve teas or extracts. Its soothing properties make it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for managing inflammatory responses.

8. Bronchitis

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. The essential oil is often inhaled to ease breathing and reduce mucus buildup. It may also help soothe coughing and inflammation in the airways.

9. Mucous congestion

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is used to alleviate mucous congestion due to its expectorant properties. The essential oil helps loosen mucus in the respiratory tract, making it easier to expel. It is often used in steam inhalations or as a topical application to reduce congestion and soothe irritated airways.

10. Asthma

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms. Its essential oil, rich in compounds like linalool and benzyl acetate, helps relax airway muscles and reduce inflammation. It is often used in aromatherapy and steam inhalation to ease breathing and soothe respiratory discomfort.
