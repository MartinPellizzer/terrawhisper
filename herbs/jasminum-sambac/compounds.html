Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) Compounds

The primary compounds of Common Jasmine include essential oil, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and potential health benefits. Coumarin and terpenoids also play roles in its fragrance and pharmacological activity.

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) Monograph

1. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound of Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac). It is extracted through steam distillation and contains aromatic compounds like linalool and benzyl acetate. This oil is used in herbal medicine for its calming properties and as a natural fragrance. It also has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory benefits.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) contribute to its medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help protect cells from damage and may support immune function, making them valuable in herbal remedies for various health conditions.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in promoting relaxation and aiding sleep. Coumarin also has mild antispasmodic effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have shown potential in anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. These compounds are often extracted for use in herbal medicine and aromatherapy.

6. Tannin

Tannin in Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) contributes to its astringent properties, aiding in wound healing and reducing inflammation. It also helps in the preservation of the plant's essential oils, enhancing its medicinal efficacy in traditional herbal practices.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Glycosides in jasmine are often used in herbal formulations to support respiratory and skin health.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, potentially aiding in respiratory and digestive health due to its anti-inflammatory and mild cleansing properties.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though its specific therapeutic effects require further research. Alkaloids in jasmine may influence nervous system functions and have potential applications in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower color and has antioxidant properties. These compounds may support skin health and reduce inflammation, making them relevant in herbal medicine applications.
