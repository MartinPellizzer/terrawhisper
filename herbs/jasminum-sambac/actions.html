Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) Actions

Common Jasmine exhibits several therapeutic actions including anti-inflammatory, sedative, and tonic properties. It also acts as an antispasmodic, carminative, and diuretic. Additionally, it has astringent, bitter, expectorant, and stimulant effects, making it versatile in herbal medicine applications.

Common Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Common Jasmine has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to the presence of compounds like flavonoids and essential oils. These components help reduce inflammation by inhibiting pro-inflammatory enzymes and modulating immune responses. It is commonly used in herbal formulations to alleviate conditions like arthritis and skin irritations.

2. Sedative

Common Jasmine has a sedative action, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety. It works by calming the nervous system and easing mental tension. This makes it useful for managing stress and insomnia. Its mild calming effect supports restful sleep without causing drowsiness.

3. Tonic

Common Jasmine has a tonic action, which helps to strengthen and invigorate the body. It supports overall vitality and can be used to combat fatigue and weakness. This action makes it beneficial for those needing energy restoration and general health support.

4. Antispasmodic

Common Jasmine has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps by relaxing smooth muscles. It is used in herbal medicine to ease symptoms associated with gastrointestinal issues and menstrual pain. Its calming effect on muscle tissue makes it a valuable remedy in traditional healing practices.

5. Carminative

Common Jasmine has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating by stimulating digestion and promoting the expulsion of trapped air from the gastrointestinal tract. This effect supports digestive comfort and reduces discomfort associated with gas-related issues.

6. Diuretic

Common Jasmine has a diuretic action, promoting the elimination of excess fluids through increased urine production. This effect supports the body's natural detoxification processes and can help manage fluid retention. It is typically used in herbal formulations for its mild and supportive diuretic properties.

7. Astringent

Common Jasmine has an astringent action, which helps reduce secretion and tighten tissues. It is particularly effective for conditions involving excessive mucus, inflammation, or bleeding. This property makes it useful in respiratory and digestive health. It supports healing by promoting tissue contraction and reducing fluid loss.

8. Bitter

Common Jasmine has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and helps clear heat from the body. It is used to reduce inflammation and support liver function. This action makes it effective for conditions like indigestion and skin irritations. Its bitter properties also help balance internal dampness and heat.

9. Expectorant

Common Jasmine has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It is effective in alleviating symptoms of coughs and bronchitis by stimulating the production of bronchial secretions. This property makes it useful in supporting respiratory health during conditions involving excess mucus buildup.

10. Stimulant

Common Jasmine has a stimulant action, enhancing mental alertness and physical energy. It increases circulation and metabolic activity, making it useful for fatigue and low vitality. This effect is due to its essential oils and bioactive compounds. It supports overall vitality and can aid in overcoming lethargy.
