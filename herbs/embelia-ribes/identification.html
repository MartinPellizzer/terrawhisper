Botanical Identification of Embelia (Embelia ribes)

To identify Embelia (Embelia ribes) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Embelia ribes belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Myrtaceae, genus Embelia, and species Embelia ribes. This classification places it within the flowering plants and highlights its evolutionary relationships within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Embelia ribes, which was first described by the botanist Robert Sweet in 1780. This name is widely accepted in botanical literature. It has several synonyms, including Embelia officinalis and Embelia corymbosa, reflecting its historical classification. The plant is also known by various common and regional names, which are used in traditional herbal medicine practices.

Morphological Characteristics

Embelia ribes is a small to medium shrub with a compact growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly in the soil. The stem is slender, branching, and often covered with fine hairs. Leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, leathery texture with serrated edges. The inflorescence consists of small clusters of greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody pulp.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Embelia ribes can be distinguished by its small, dark red berries with a whitish bloom, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and a strong, aromatic odor. It is often confused with other embelias, but its unique fruit color and scent help in accurate identification. The presence of stipules at the base of leaves is another key differentiating feature.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Embelia ribes is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained, loamy soils. The plant typically grows at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Embelia ribes typically blooms from April to June, producing small white flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during the early stages of fruit development, usually in late summer to early autumn.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Embelia ribes belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is closely related to species like Syzgium and Eugenia. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with these genera, such as aromatic leaves and flower structures. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties within the Myrtaceae family, aiding in accurate botanical classification.
