Corydalis thyrsiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis thyrsiflora?

Corydalis thyrsiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Ranunculales and the family Papaveraceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Corydalis, and its unique species designation, thyrsiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis thyrsiflora has slender, upright stems that support terminal clusters of zygomorphic, spurred flowers characterized by a pale lavender or white coloration. The plant typically grows in low-lying herbaceous clumps, displaying a spreading habit through its root system.
