Lysimachia filipes

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia filipes?

Lysimachia filipes belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Ericales and falls within the family Primulaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement in the genus Lysimachia, with the specific epithet filipes.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia filipes has slender, creeping rhizomes that produce long, trailing stems with small, opposite, lanceolate leaves measuring approximately 1 to 2 centimeters in length.

The plant often forms dense mats through these prostrate stems which can root at the nodes upon contact with the soil. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically featuring five distinct petals that form a star-like shape. These blooms emerge from the leaf axils and are supported by delicate pedicels.

The overall structure of the plant is characterized by its fine, thread-like appearance in both stem and foliage.
