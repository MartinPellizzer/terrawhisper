What's the taxonomical classification of Anisodus tanguticus?

Anisodus tanguticus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Solanales. As a member of the family Solanaceae, this plant is further categorized under the genus Anisodus, with its specific identity defined by the species name tanguticus.

What are the pharmacological activities of Anisodus tanguticus?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-oxygenation, anti-shock, calcium antagonist, cerebral protective, and cholinolytic effects. Its ability to act as a calcium antagonist helps in regulating vascular functions and blood pressure. The anti-oxygenation and anti-shock properties suggest it may help mitigate cellular damage and stabilize physiological responses during acute stress. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates significant cerebral protective effects that could assist in preserving neurological integrity. Finally, its cholinolytic effect indicates an ability to interfere with acetylcholine signaling in the nervous system.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", anisodamine is an alkaloid extracted from the roots of the Anisodus tanguticus Maxim. As an antagonist to M-choline receptors, it exhibits diverse pharmacological effects including cholinolytic, calcium antagonist, and anti-oxygenation effects. This prominent constituent of the tropine alkaloid family displays pharmacological effects akin to those of atropine and scopolamine. Due to its low toxicity and moderate efficacy in clinical use, it has wide applications, especially for varieties of shock treatment.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains various medicinal tropane alkaloids, most notably the high concentrations of anisodaamine, hyoscyamine, and scopolamine. These specific chemical constituents are primarily utilized for their potent anticholinergic and antispasmodic properties. Anisodaamine serves as a key component in treating various circulatory and inflammatory conditions. The presence of hyoscyamine and scopolamine further enhances its pharmacological profile for managing smooth muscle spasms. Together, these compounds make the plant a significant source of alkaloids used in modern medicine.

What side effects this plant can have?
