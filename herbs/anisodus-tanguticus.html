Anisodus tanguticus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains various tropane alkaloids, specifically anisodaamine, hyoscyamine, scopolamine, and atropine, which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These alkaloids act on the parasympathetic nervous system to produce significant physiological effects. Anisodaamine is particularly notable for its ability to improve microcirculation and treat various types of shock. The presence of hyoscyamine and scopolamine allows the plant to function as an effective antispasmodic agent. Atropine further contributes to its pharmacological profile by modulating heart rate and pupil dilation. Together, these chemical components make the species a valuable subject in traditional and modern pharmacology.
