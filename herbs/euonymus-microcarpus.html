Euonymus microcarpus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euonymus microcarpus has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape with finely serrated margins and a dull green upper surface. The stems are woody and often exhibit a somewhat quadrangular form during early growth stages. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. The plant is most distinctive for its small, four-lobed capsules that split open to reveal bright orange or red seeds enclosed in fleshy arils. These botanical features collectively define its structural identity within the Caprifoliaceae family.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Euonymus microcarpus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested. The berries and leaves contain alkaloids and glycosides that act as irritants to the digestive tract. Ingestion may lead to symptoms such as vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea. Topical contact with the plant can also result in dermatitis or redness on sensitive skin. Because of these risks, the plant should be handled with caution and kept away from children and pets.
