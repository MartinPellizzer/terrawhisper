What is Ocotea commutata?

Ocotea commutata is a species of plant in the family Lauraceae, native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is an evergreen tree that typically grows up to 15 meters in height, with a straight trunk and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, measuring 8-12 cm in length and 3-4 cm in width, with a leathery texture and a wavy margin. The flowers are small and yellowish-green in color, arranged in clusters at the end of the branches, while the fruit is a drupe, consisting of a single seed surrounded by a fleshy covering.

The wood of Ocotea commutata is highly valued for its timber, which is used for furniture-making and construction.
