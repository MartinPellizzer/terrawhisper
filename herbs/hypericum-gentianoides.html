What is Hypericum gentianoides?

Hypericum gentianoides is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Hypericaceae.

It is characterized by its ovate to lance-shaped leaves, typically 2.5-7.5 cm long and 1-3 cm wide, with cuneate or rounded bases and entire or serrulate margins. The leaves are opposite or subopposite, and the plant produces white to yellowish flowers in a corymb, with five-lobed sepals and five petals, the latter 1-2 cm long and 4-6 mm wide. The flowers are axillary, solitary or in small clusters, and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

Hypericum gentianoides typically grows 20-50 cm tall, and is found in wetlands and along streams in eastern North America.
