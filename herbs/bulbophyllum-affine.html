Bulbophyllum affine

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum affine has creeping rhizomes that produce pseudobulbs ranging from ovate to cylindrical in shape, which are topped by a single, fleshy, ovate-lanceolate leaf. The inflorescence emerges from the base of the pseudobulb and features a raceme of numerous small, yellowish to brownish flowers. Each individual flower possesses a highly modified, mobile labellum that is characteristic of the genus. The sepals are typically longer than the petals and often exhibit a somewhat fleshy texture.
