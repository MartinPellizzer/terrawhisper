Bulbophyllum affine

What's the taxonomical classification of Bulbophyllum affine?

Bulbophyllum affine belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the biological order Asparagales and is a member of the diverse family Orchidaceae. Specifically, it is placed within the genus Bulbophyllum, with its unique identity defined by the species name affine.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum affine has creeping rhizomes that produce pseudobulbs ranging from ovate to cylindrical in shape, which are topped by a single, fleshy, ovate-lanceolate leaf.

The inflorescence emerges from the base of the pseudobulb and features a raceme of numerous small, yellowish to brownish flowers. Each individual flower possesses a highly modified, mobile labellum that is characteristic of the genus. The sepals are typically longer than the petals and often exhibit a somewhat fleshy texture.

These botanical structures work together to support the plant's specialized pollination mechanisms within its natural habitat.
