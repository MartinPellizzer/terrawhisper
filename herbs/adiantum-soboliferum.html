Adiantum soboliferum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adiantum soboliferum has delicate, bipinnate to tripinnate fronds characterized by thin, dark, wire-like stipes and small, fan-shaped pinnules with entire or slightly lobed margins. The plant typically exhibits a creeping rhizome that facilitates its spreading habit across moist substrates. Its leaves are often a vibrant green, providing a feathery texture to the overall foliage. Small, kidney-shaped sori are located on the underside of the pinnules, which is a defining feature of this fern species. The fine structure of the leaf segments allows for efficient light capture in shaded environments.
