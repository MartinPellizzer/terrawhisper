What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea basicordata?

Pilea basicordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Rosales and is a member of the family Urticaceae. Ultimately, its scientific identity is defined by its placement within the genus Pilea, specifically as the species Pilea basicordata.

      What are the herbal preparations of Pilea basicordata?

      Pilea basicordata is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice depending on the intended therapeutic application. The dried leaves and stems are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid extract for internal consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pilea basicordata can cause mild skin irritation or allergic reactions if the sap comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. While there is limited clinical research regarding its toxicity, cautious handling is recommended for those with known plant sensitivities. Always wash your hands thoroughly after pruning or repotting this species to prevent accidental contact with your eyes or mouth.
