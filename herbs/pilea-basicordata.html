Pilea basicordata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea basicordata has ovate to elliptical leaves characterized by a distinctively cordate or heart-shaped base and serrated margins. The leaf texture is typically thin and herbaceous, often displaying a slightly fleshy quality. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, which is a common trait among members of the Urticaceae family. The stems are slender and succulent, providing structural support for the spreading foliage. These morphological features help distinguish the species within its specific ecological niche.
