10 Possible Side Effects Of Lonicera Japonica (Japanese Honeysuckle)

Lonicera japonica, also known as Japanese honeysuckle, has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with medications.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, individual sensitivity, or combination with other herbs. For example, severe allergic reactions can lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical attention.

Additionally, digestive issues can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, severely impacting daily life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Lonicera japonica if used imporperly.
