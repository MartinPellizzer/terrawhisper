Photinia kwangsiensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Photinia kwangsiensis has evergreen leaves that are typically elliptical or oblong in shape, measuring approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length with finely serrated margins and a glossy upper surface. The foliage often displays a reddish tint when young before maturing into a deep, lustrous green. This woody shrub features slender, branching stems that support a dense canopy of leaves. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in terminal clusters during the blooming season. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy drupes that serve as a characteristic feature of its reproductive morphology.
