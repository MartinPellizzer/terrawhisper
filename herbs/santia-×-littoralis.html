Santia × littoralis

Santia × littoralis, commonly known as Santolina, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sunflower family, native to the Mediterranean region.

This plant is valued for its numerous health benefits, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it useful in treating various conditions such as wounds, skin issues, and respiratory problems.

The therapeutic actions of Santia × littoralis include its ability to soothe and calm the nervous system, reduce anxiety and stress, and promote relaxation.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include sesquiterpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Possible Side Effects of Santia × littoralis

Santia × littoralis can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal issues.

Prolonged exposure to the plant's oils may lead to symptoms like headaches, dizziness, and nausea.

In severe cases, improper use of Santia × littoralis can cause more serious health issues, including respiratory problems, liver damage, and increased risk of bleeding.

It is essential to consult with a medical professional before using the plant for medicinal purposes to avoid these potential side effects.
