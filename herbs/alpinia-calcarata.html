What's the taxonomical classification of Alpinia calcarata?

Alpinia calcarata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Zingiberales. It is a member of the family Zingiberaceae, which places it within the genus Alpinia, and is specifically identified as the species calcarata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia calcarata has lanceolate, bright green leaves that emerge from thick, fleshy rhizomes and grow on erect, upright stems reaching significant heights. The foliage is characterized by prominent parallel venation and a smooth, waxy texture typical of the Zingiberaceae family. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes that bear vibrant, tubular flowers often displaying contrasting colors. These flowers are supported by sturdy bracts that protect the developing buds throughout their lifecycle. The plant's overall structure is robust and herbaceous, allowing it to thrive in moist, tropical environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (E)-methyl cinnamate, Calcaratarin D, calcaratarin E, alpha-terpineol, and eucalyptol. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the diverse therapeutic profile of the species. The presence of alpha-terpineol and eucalyptol often provides significant antimicrobial and aromatic properties. Additionally, the unique calcaratarin compounds serve as specialized metabolites within its biological structure. These various substances work together to define the plant's overall pharmacological potential.
