What's the taxonomical classification of Maesa montana?

Maesa montana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Ericales, placing it within the family Primulaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Maesa, and its species designation, montana.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude montane forests of Northern Thailand, specifically concentrated within the Mae Sa valley region. It thrives in the moist, shaded understories found at elevations typically ranging from 1,000 to 1,500 meters above sea level. Its presence is closely tied to the specific microclimates provided by the temperate mountain slopes of the Chiang Mai province. Due to this highly localized habitat, the species does not occur naturally in any other geographical territories.
