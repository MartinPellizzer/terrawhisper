Maesa montana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Maesa montana has woody stems, lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and small, actinomorphic flowers arranged in dense terminal inflorescences. The foliage typically displays a leathery texture and a deep green coloration on the upper surface. Small, inconspicuous petals characterize the floral structure, often accompanied by prominent stamens. The plant's root system is adapted to stabilize its growth within montane environments. These morphological features collectively define its physical presence in its native habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude montane forests of Northern Thailand, specifically concentrated within the Mae Sa valley region. It thrives in the moist, shaded understories found at elevations typically ranging from 1,000 to 1,500 meters above sea level. Its presence is closely tied to the specific microclimates provided by the temperate mountain slopes of the Chiang Mai province. Due to this highly localized habitat, the species does not occur naturally in any other geographical territories. This restricted range makes the plant a significant subject for studying regional biodiversity and conservation efforts.

      Maesa montana is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the plant's leaves and stems in water or as a topical paste applied directly to the skin.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Maesa montana can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or touched without protection. Some individuals may experience localized redness or itching upon direct contact with the plant's surfaces. If consumed accidentally, it may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping. There is also a risk of systemic sensitivity in people with pre-existing plant allergies. Careful handling is recommended to avoid these adverse physiological responses.
