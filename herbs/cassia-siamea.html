Cassia siamea

What's the taxonomical classification of Cassia siamea?

Cassia siamea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, the plant is placed in the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Cassia and the species siamea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cassia siamea contains anthraquinones, flavonoids, tannins, and various alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. The presence of anthraquinones makes the plant an effective natural laxative by stimulating intestinal movements. Flavonoids within the leaves and seeds provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the body. Tannins are also found in the plant, which assist in treating skin irritations and wound healing due to their astringent qualities. These diverse chemical constituents allow the plant to be used in traditional medicine for digestive and dermatological ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cassia siamea?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat constipation, skin infections, intestinal parasites, and various inflammatory conditions. Its potent laxative properties are primarily derived from its anthraquinone content, which stimulates bowel movements. In traditional medicine, topical applications of the seeds or leaves are employed to manage wounds and fungal infections. Some studies also suggest its effectiveness in mitigating symptoms of diabetes and reducing swelling in the body. These diverse medicinal uses stem from the plant's complex chemical composition.
