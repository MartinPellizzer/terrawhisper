    What is Achyranthes lindenii?

    Achyranthes lindenii is a species of plant that belongs to the family Amaranthaceae, a family of flowering plants commonly known as the pigweed or amaranth family.

    The plant is a shrub or vine that grows up to 1 meter in height, with a woody stem and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical or ovate in shape, measuring 4-10 cm in length and 2-4 cm in width. The leaves are dark green on the upper surface and lighter green on the lower surface, with a pointed tip and a smooth margin. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in a terminal spike or panicle, which are arranged in a cymose or umbel-like fashion.

    The flowers are dioecious, meaning that they are either male or female, and they are arranged in a single spike or panicle at the end of the stem.
