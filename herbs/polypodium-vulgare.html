What is Polypody (Polypodium Vulgare)?

Polypody (Polypodium vulgare) is a fern commonly used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to support kidney function, reduce inflammation, and aid in the treatment of urinary tract disorders.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Polypody is Polypodium vulgare. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Pteridophyta, class Leptosporangiata, order Polypodiales, family Polypodiaceae, genus Polypodium, and species Polypodium vulgare. It is commonly known as Polypody in Europe and Polypodium in North America. The plant is also referred to by synonyms such as Polypodium vulgare subsp. vulgare and Polypodium vulgare var. vulgare in botanical contexts.

Plant Description and Morphology

Polypody is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm, with a clumping growth habit formed by rhizomatous roots.

Its leaves are pinnate, with lanceolate to ovate leaflets that are glossy and serrated along the edges. Polypody does not produce flowers; instead, it reproduces via spores released from sporangia located on the underside of its fronds. The root system consists of a network of rhizomes that spread horizontally beneath the soil, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption.

The fronds are dark green and leathery, with a faint, earthy aroma, and the texture is firm yet flexible.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Polypody is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is commonly found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors, rocky crevices, and along stream banks. The plant thrives in humid climates with mild temperatures, typically between 10°C and 25°C. It prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil that remains consistently moist.

Polypody grows best in areas with high humidity and partial to full shade, often forming dense colonies in undisturbed habitats.
