Ailanthus giraldii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ailanthus giraldii?

Ailanthus girauldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Sapindales and falls within the family Simaroubaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Ailanthus, with its specific designation being the species girauldii.

These different parts are processed to isolate specific alkaloids and terpenoids for therapeutic use.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Ailantus giraordi targets several key proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, tumor necrosis factor-alpha, and various matrix metalloproteinases to exert its medicinal effects. The plant's bioactive limonoids and phenolic compounds work by inhibiting the cyclooxygenase-2 enzyme to reduce inflammatory cytokine production. These secondary metabolites also interact with signaling pathways that regulate apoptosis in malignant cells, suggesting potential anticancer mechanisms. Furthermore, the modulation of matrix metalloproteinases helps prevent tissue degradation and promotes wound healing processes. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ailanthus giraldii?

Ailantus giraldii has medicinal properties primarily characterized by its bitter principles that act as an emetic, purgative, and antiseptic agent in traditional applications. The bark and leaves contain various alkaloids and terpenoids that contribute to its biological activity. These compounds are often studied for their potential to inhibit certain bacterial growth through their chemical structure. Some traditional practices utilize extracts from the plant to address skin ailments or digestive irregularities. However, the high toxicity of certain components necessitates extreme caution when considering its pharmacological use.

    What are the herbal preparations of Ailanthus giraldii?

    Ailantus giraldii is prepared primarily as a decoction of the bark, an infusion of the leaves, or a topical tincture derived from the root. These traditional preparations utilize different parts of the plant to extract specific bioactive compounds for medicinal use. When used as a decoction, the bark is boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid. Infusions involving the leaves offer a gentler method of extraction suitable for milder applications. Topical tinctures are often applied directly to the skin to address localized issues. These various forms allow for diverse methods of administration depending on the intended therapeutic goal.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Ailantus giraldii can cause severe skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains quassinoids that act as toxins to many living organisms. Direct contact with the sap or leaves may result in dermatitis or painful rashes on sensitive skin. If the plant material is consumed, it can lead to nausea, vomiting, or more serious internal complications. Careful handling is necessary to avoid accidental exposure to these harmful chemical compounds.
