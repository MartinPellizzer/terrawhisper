What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ailantus giraldii has pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 60 centimeters in length, consisting of numerous lanceolate leaflets arranged in pairs along a central rachis. Each individual leaflet is typically 5 to 15 centimeters long and possesses serrated margins with an acute apex. The plant's structure is characterized by a woody stem that develops a rough, fissured bark as the tree matures. Small, yellowish-green flowers emerge in dense, terminal panicles during the summer months. These flowers eventually give way to small, winged samaras that are dispersed by the wind.
