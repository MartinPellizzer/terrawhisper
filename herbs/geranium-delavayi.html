What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Geranium delavayi targets various inflammatory enzymes and oxidative stress markers including cyclooxygenase-2, lipoxygenase, and superoxide dismutase. The phytochemical constituents of this plant, particularly its polyphenols and flavonoids, act as potent inhibitors of the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. By modulating these specific proteins, the plant extract effectively reduces the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like prostaglandins and leukotrienes. Additionally, its ability to interact with antioxidant enzymes helps neutralize reactive oxygen species within cellular environments. This multi-target mechanism explains its traditional use in treating inflammation and oxidative damage.

What are the therapeutic actions of Geranium delavayi?

Geranium delavayi has mild antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and astringent properties that are traditionally utilized to treat minor skin irritations and digestive upsets. The essential oils and tannins present in the plant help to reduce swelling and soothe inflamed tissues when applied topically. Some herbal practices suggest using its extracts to promote wound healing through its ability to contract skin tissues. Additionally, the plant is believed to possess subtle calming effects that may assist in reducing mild physical discomfort. These therapeutic actions are primarily attributed to its specific chemical constituents found within its leaves and stems.

What are the herbal preparations of Geranium delavayi?

Geranium delavayi is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice to utilize its medicinal properties. The dried leaves and stems are often simmered in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. For external applications, a tea can be applied directly to the skin or used as a wash to soothe irritation. Some practitioners also crush the fresh plant material into a paste to form a poultice for localized treatment. These various methods allow for the extraction of active compounds depending on the specific therapeutic need.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Geranium delaavayi can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's foliage or stems may lead to localized redness, itching, or dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If the plant is consumed, it might trigger nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps due to its chemical composition. There is also a potential risk of more severe systemic reactions if a large amount is swallowed. Always exercise caution and avoid direct skin contact or ingestion to prevent these adverse effects.
