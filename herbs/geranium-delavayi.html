Geranium delavayi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Geranium delavayi has a perennial growth habit characterized by deeply lobed, palmate leaves and five-petaled flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose. The foliage is often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance to the plant's low-growing mounds. Its stems are relatively slender and can become somewhat woody at the base as the plant matures. The flowers are borne on long pedicels that elevate them above the dense canopy of leaves. Each blossom features prominent stamens that contrast with the delicate petal coloration.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and western Asia, specifically spanning across Turkey, Iran, Iraq, and parts of Afghanistan. It typically thrives in rocky terrains and high-altitude meadows where drainage is excellent. Within these regions, the species is often found growing in limestone-rich soils among sparse vegetation. Its distribution follows the specific climatic conditions of the Irano-Turanian phytogeographical region. This specialized habitat allows the species to persist in environments characterized by seasonal moisture and significant temperature fluctuations.

      What are the herbal preparations of Geranium delavayi?

      Geranium delavayi is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice to utilize its medicinal properties. The dried leaves and stems are often simmered in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. For external applications, a tea can be applied directly to the skin or used as a wash to soothe irritation.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Geranium delaavayi can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's foliage or stems may lead to localized redness, itching, or dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If the plant is consumed, it might trigger nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps due to its chemical composition. There is also a potential risk of more severe systemic reactions if a large amount is swallowed. Always exercise caution and avoid direct skin contact or ingestion to prevent these adverse effects.
