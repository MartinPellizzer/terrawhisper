Giant Dianthus (Dianthus superbus) Compounds

The primary compounds of Giant Dianthus include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins and glycosides provide astringent effects, while coumarin and saponins offer potential antimicrobial benefits. Essential oils and terpenoids contribute to its aromatic profile, and anthocyanins give it vibrant color and antioxidant activity. Alkaloids may also play a role in its medicinal effects.

Giant Dianthus (Dianthus superbus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoid is a key active compound in Giant Dianthus (Dianthus superbus). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and circulatory health. Flavonoids help reduce oxidative stress and may enhance immune function.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Giant Dianthus (Dianthus superbus), contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The essential oil of Giant Dianthus (Dianthus superbus) contains bioactive compounds like geraniol and linalool, which contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Terpenoids in Giant Dianthus (Dianthus superbus) are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.
