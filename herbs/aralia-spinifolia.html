Aralia spinifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Aralia spinifolia?

Aralia spinifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it is part of the order Apiales and falls under the family Araliaceae. Ultimately, its biological identity is defined by its membership in the genus Aralia, specifically as the species spinifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aralia spinifolia has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves and sharp, spine-like teeth along the margins of its leaflets. The stems are often armed with small prickles that provide a defensive structure to the plant. Each leaf consists of several pairs of leaflets arranged symmetrically along a central rachis. The foliage typically displays a bright green color with a somewhat leathery texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers eventually develop into clusters of fleshy fruits.
