What is Jumellea walleri?

Jumellea walleri is an epiphytic orchid species belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to Madagascar, where it grows in humid forests at elevations of 700-1,200 meters. Jumellea walleri has a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and several pseudobulbs that are 2-5 cm long and 1-2 cm wide. The leaves are elliptical to lanceolate, 10-20 cm long, and 2-4 cm wide, and are arranged in a distichous pattern.

The inflorescence is a long, slender spike that bears 5-20 white, fragrant flowers with a yellowish-green lip and a prominent spur.
