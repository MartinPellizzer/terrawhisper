Aconitum cavaleriei

What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum cavaleriei?

Aconitum cavaleriei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Aconitum, specifically identified by its species name, cavaleriei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aconitum cavaleriei has herbaceous stems that reach heights of approximately 20 to 50 centimeters and bear deeply palmately lobed leaves with narrow, serrated segments. The plant produces distinctive zygomorphic flowers that are typically deep blue or violet in color. Each flower features a prominent, helmet-shaped uppermost sepal that protects the internal reproductive organs. The floral structure also includes lateral petals that are often smaller and more inconspicuous. These botanical features are characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family and help distinguish the species within its genus.
