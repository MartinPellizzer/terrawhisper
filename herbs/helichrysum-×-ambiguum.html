What is Helichrysum × ambiguum?

Helichrysum × ambiguum is a hybrid species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial plant that grows up to 1 meter in height, with a woody base and an erect stem, bearing narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are 2-5 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow or white flowers at the end of its stem, each flower consisting of five petals and numerous tiny florets. The flowers are arranged in a capitulum, a type of inflorescence typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is native to South Africa and has been cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in traditional medicine.

Pain is relieved by its analgesic properties, and infections are prevented by its antibacterial properties. Additionally, Helichrysum × ambiguum has been used to treat anxiety, burns, bruises, rheumatism, asthma, cancer, ulcers, diabetes, sprains, gout, and wounds, by leveraging its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antibacterial properties.

What are the therapeutic properties this herb?

The main therapeutic properties of Helichrysum × ambiguum are its ability to act as an antioxidant, which is primarily due to the presence of flavonoids and phenolic acids in this plant.

Additionally, this plant exhibits antimicrobial properties, attributed to the presence of volatile oils, which allow it to combat a wide range of pathogens. The anti-inflammatory properties of Helichrysum × ambiguum are also noteworthy, as they are largely responsible for its ability to reduce inflammation and alleviate pain. Furthermore, the antifungal and antibacterial properties of this plant are significant, as they enable it to combat fungal and bacterial infections effectively.

The plant's medicinal properties are also attributed to the presence of saponins, which contribute to its ability to fight infections and reduce inflammation.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Helichrysum × ambiguum are α-pinene, caryophyllene, β-pinene, apigenin, and quercetin.

α-Pinene and β-pinene exhibit anti-inflammatory properties, whereas caryophyllene shows anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities. Apigenin has been found to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, while quercetin exhibits anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral activities. These compounds contribute to the plant's therapeutic potential in treating various conditions such as inflammation, pain, and oxidative stress.

The presence of these bioactive compounds in Helichrysum × ambiguum underscores its potential as a valuable medicinal plant.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Helichrysum × ambiguum are derived from its flowers and leaves, which contain sesquiterpene lactones, flavonoids, and volatile oils.

Helichrysum × ambiguum tincture is prepared by macerating its flowers in a solvent, typically ethanol or glycerin, to create a concentrated liquid extract used to treat wounds, reduce inflammation, and alleviate pain. Essential oil of Helichrysum × ambiguum is obtained through steam distillation of its flowers, which is used topically to reduce inflammation, improve skin health, and relieve anxiety. Helichrysum × ambiguum salve is a topical cream or ointment prepared by infusing its flowers in a carrier oil, often combined with beeswax, to create a protective barrier for wounds and skin irritations.

Additionally, tea and infused oil preparations of Helichrysum × ambiguum are used to promote relaxation, reduce stress, and alleviate digestive issues due to its bioactive compounds.

Interacting with medications such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and certain antidepressants due to its ability to affect the cytochrome P450 enzyme system. Triggering allergic reactions, asthma attacks, and other respiratory issues in individuals sensitive to pollen and plant allergens. Helichrysum × ambiguum contains sesquiterpene lactones, which can induce nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea in some individuals.

Ingesting large quantities can lead to adverse effects due to the plant's high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones and other bioactive compounds.
