Bredia esquirolii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bredia esquirolii has a prostrate, creeping growth habit characterized by slender, rooting stems and small, opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves. The foliage is typically thin and membranous with entire margins and acute or obtuse tips. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils in a simplified arrangement. These plants often form dense mats over moist substrates due to their stoloniferous nature. The overall structure is delicate and adapted for spreading across ground cover.
