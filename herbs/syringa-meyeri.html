Syringa meyeri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syringa meyeri has oppositely arranged, elliptical to oblong leaves that typically measure between 5 and 10 centimeters in length and possess entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a smooth, glabrous texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal panicles that produce small, tubular flowers. These blossoms are usually pale lilac or white and emerge during the late spring or early summer months. The woody stems of this shrub exhibit a spreading growth habit that contributes to its overall structural form.

Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of dense vegetation and stable equatorial climates. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger localized redness or itching upon contact with sensitive skin. While specific toxicological data is limited, caution should always be exercised when handling its foliage or flowers.
