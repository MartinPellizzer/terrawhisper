Starfruit (Averrhoa carambola) Compounds

Starfruit contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, which act as antioxidants. Phenolic acids contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins provide astringent effects, while anthocyanins give it its vibrant color and antioxidant benefits. Coumarin and saponins also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Starfruit (Averrhoa carambola) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Starfruit (Averrhoa carambola) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress. They are found in the fruit's pulp and skin, enhancing the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Starfruit (Averrhoa carambola). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound may support immune function and has potential anti-inflammatory effects. Its presence in Starfruit highlights the plant's medicinal value in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Starfruit (Averrhoa carambola) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and may support digestive health. Tannins are also known for their antioxidant effects, which can aid in protecting cells from damage. This compound is commonly found in the fruit's skin and pulp.

4. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Starfruit (Averrhoa carambola). It acts as a powerful antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound supports cardiovascular health and may reduce inflammation, contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Starfruit (Averrhoa carambola). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin is also responsible for the characteristic aroma of Starfruit. Its presence supports the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Starfruit (Averrhoa carambola). It exhibits mild laxative properties and may support digestive health. Found in the fruit's pulp, saponin contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for gastrointestinal support.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Starfruit (Averrhoa carambola). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in Starfruit are known for their antioxidant and antimicrobial effects, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Starfruit (Averrhoa carambola) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. They also play a role in the plant's natural defense mechanisms.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil from Starfruit (Averrhoa carambola) contains volatile compounds like limonene and geraniol. These oils are extracted through steam distillation and are used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They contribute to the plant's therapeutic potential in treating infections and skin conditions.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Starfruit (Averrhoa carambola). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its specific role and therapeutic potential require further research. Alkaloids in Starfruit may influence its traditional use in herbal medicine.
