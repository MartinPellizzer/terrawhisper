Mentha × rotundifolia var. glabrata

Mentha × rotundifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as Apple Mint, is a hybrid species of mint, derived from the cross between Mentha suaveolens and Mentha × rotundifolia.

This plant has been used in herbal medicine for its various health benefits, including its ability to alleviate digestive issues, reduce nausea, and provide relief from headaches and fever.

The therapeutic actions of Apple Mint are attributed to its bioactive constituents, including menthol, menthone, and limonene, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

By incorporating Apple Mint into one's diet or using it as a topical application, individuals can experience the numerous health benefits and therapeutic actions of this versatile and fragrant herb.
