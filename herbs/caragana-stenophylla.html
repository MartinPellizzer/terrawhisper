Caragana stenophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Caragana stenophylla?

Caragana stenophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows a lineage that leads into the order Fabales. It is further categorized under the family Fabaceae, a group well-known for its diverse botanical characteristics. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Caragana, with the unique designation of the species stenophylla.

What are the herbal preparations of Caragana stenophylla?

Caragana stenophylla is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice derived from its dried bark or leaves. The bark is most commonly boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption. This decoction method allows for the extraction of specific tannins and bioactive compounds present in the woody tissue. For external applications, the plant material is often crushed and applied directly to the skin as a warm paste. These various preparations are traditionally utilized to address localized inflammation or digestive concerns.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Caragana stenophylla can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may lead to localized dermatitis or redness on sensitive skin areas. If consumed, individuals might experience symptoms such as nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. While specific toxicity levels are not extensively documented in all human populations, caution is advised when handling the specimen. Always perform a patch test or consult a professional before any direct exposure to this species.
