Buxus rugulosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Buxus rugulosa?

Buxus rugulosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Buxales and the family Buxaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by the genus Buxus and is specifically designated as the species rugulosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buxus rugulosa has small, elliptic to obovate leaves that are leathery in texture and characterized by a distinctly wrinkled or rugose upper surface. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or a miniature tree with dense, woody branches. Its leaf margins are entire, and the foliage often displays a deep green color that provides a thick canopy. The stems are slender and may develop a slightly rough bark as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, leading to the development of tiny, globose fruits.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of southern China, specifically within provinces such as Guangdong, Guangxi, Hainan, Fujian, and Yunnan. It primarily thrives in the understory of evergreen broad-leaved forests where moisture is abundant. The species is also documented in certain parts of Southeast Asia, extending its range into neighboring mountainous areas. These specific habitats provide the shaded, humid conditions necessary for its survival. Its distribution is largely dictated by the presence of well-drained soils and specific altitudinal requirements found in these warm climates.
