What to know about Cassia angustifolia before using it medicinally

Cassia angustifolia, commonly known as senna, is a medicinal herb that has been used to treat various health conditions, including constipation, irritable bowel syndrome, and inflammatory bowel diseases such as Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis.

The main health benefits of Cassia angustifolia include its ability to promote bowel movements, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antioxidant properties. The herb is known to possess main therapeutic properties such as laxative, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. The bioactive compounds present in Cassia angustifolia include anthraquinones, such as aloe-emodin and rhein, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

In traditional herbal medicine, Cassia angustifolia is often prepared as a tea or infusion, which is commonly known as senna tea, to stimulate digestion and relieve constipation.
