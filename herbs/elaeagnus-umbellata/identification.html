Botanical Identification of Silver Bush (Elaeagnus umbellata)

To identify Silver Bush (Elaeagnus umbellata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Silver Bush, or Elaeagnus umbellata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Elaeagnaceae, genus Elaeagnus, and species Elaeagnus umbellata. This classification places it within the flowering plants and highlights its relationship to other members of the Elaeagnaceae family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Silver Bush is Elaeagnus umbellata, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Elaeagnus sinensis and Elaeagnus monogyna. Common names such as silver bush, silverleaf, and Chinese silverberry are widely used. Regional and historical names vary, reflecting its use in traditional herbal medicine across different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Silver Bush (Elaeagnus umbellata) is a deciduous shrub with a dense, multi-branched growth habit. It has a deep, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. The stems are woody and covered with silvery, glandular hairs. Leaves are opposite, oval, and have a silvery-green upper surface with a rough texture. The inflorescence is a flat-topped cluster of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The fruit is a small, reddish-purple drupe containing one seed.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Silver Bush (Elaeagnus umbellata) can be distinguished by its silvery-gray foliage, opposite leaf arrangement, and fragrant yellow flowers. It lacks the thorny stems of Elaeagnus umbellata var. macrophylla and has smaller, more rounded leaves compared to Elaeagnus angustifolia. The fruit is also smaller and more clustered in umbels, setting it apart from similar species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Silver Bush (Elaeagnus umbellata) is native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. It prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Silver Bush (Elaeagnus umbellata) blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during late summer when the leaves are fully developed and the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Silver Bush (Elaeagnus umbellata) is closely related to other Elaeagnus species, sharing characteristics with the genus Elaeagnus. It belongs to the Elaeagnaceae family, which includes shrubs and small trees. Its phylogenetic position reflects shared traits such as compound leaves and floral structures with related species.
