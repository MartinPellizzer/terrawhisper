Forsythia likiangensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Forsythia likiangensis?

Forsythia likiangensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Oleaceae and falls under the genus Forsythia, with its specific designation being the species likiangensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Forsythia likiangensis has arching, deciduous woody stems that produce clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers before the leaves emerge. The foliage consists of simple, oppositely arranged leaves with serrated margins and an ovate to elliptical shape. Each leaf typically possesses a pointed tip and a smooth, green surface that provides a lush backdrop during the summer months. The plant's overall structure is characterized by a spreading habit with slender, whip-like branches. These morphological traits allow the species to transition effectively from its vibrant spring blooming period to its dense green vegetative state.
