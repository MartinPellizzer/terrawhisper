The therapeutic actions of Asparagus racemosus include its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and adaptogenic properties, which help to protect against cell damage and promote overall well-being.

The bioactive constituents of Asparagus racemosus include saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties and health benefits.

Possible Side Effects of asparagus racemosus

Asparagus racemosus can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing nausea, dizziness, and allergic reactions in some individuals.

It may also interact with certain medications, including blood thinners and diabetes medications, leading to complications like bleeding or blood sugar imbalances.

In rare cases, excessive consumption of the herb can cause digestive issues, such as stomach pain and diarrhea.

Additionally, Asparagus racemosus may exacerbate pre-existing conditions like high blood pressure, asthma, or kidney problems if not used under medical supervision.
