Lespedeza bicolor

What's the taxonomical classification of Lespedeza bicolor?

Lespedeza bicolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Lespedeza, and its unique species designation, bicolor.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lespedeza bicolor has trifoliate leaves consisting of three elliptical to oblong leaflets that are typically 1 to 3 centimeters long and exhibit a distinct bicolor appearance with dark green upper surfaces and paler undersides. The plant grows as a perennial herb or subshrub, often reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters with slender, ascending stems. Its small, pea-like flowers are pale yellow and emerge from axillary clusters along the stem. The inflorescence is characterized by these tiny blossoms which eventually produce small, flattened legumes. These seed pods are typically oblong and contain several small seeds essential for the plant's reproduction.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the medicinal compounds genistein and quercetin which provide various therapeutic benefits to the human body. Genistein acts as a potent isoflavone that can help regulate hormone levels and support bone health. Quercetin serves as a powerful antioxidant that helps reduce inflammation and protect cells from oxidative stress. Together, these bioactive molecules contribute to the plant's overall reputation in traditional and modern medicine. These specific phytochemicals are often studied for their potential role in preventing chronic diseases.

    As discussed in a study, Lespedeza bicolor 1 (LB1) was identified as the most potent species among 26 different Lespedeza species investigated for its protective effects against methylglyoxal-induced toxicity. Using HPLC-Q-TOF-MS/MS, researchers tentatively identified 17 chemical constituents within the LB1 extract. Among these identified components, specific compounds such as genistein and quercetin significantly reduced the formation of advanced glycation end products (AGEs) and increased AGEs-breaking activity. These medicinal properties allow LB1 to reduce intracellular reactive oxidative species and strongly inhibit all the mitogen-activated protein kinase signals. Ultimately, the LB1 extract has shown to be effective in preventing or treating methylglyoxal-induced endothelial dysfunction.
