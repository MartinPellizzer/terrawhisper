Lespedeza bicolor

What's the taxonomical classification of Lespedeza bicolor?

Lespedeza bicolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Lespedeza, and its unique species designation, bicolor.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lespedeza bicolor has trifoliate leaves consisting of three elliptical to oblong leaflets that are typically 1 to 3 centimeters long and exhibit a distinct bicolor appearance with dark green upper surfaces and paler undersides. The plant grows as a perennial herb or subshrub, often reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters with slender, ascending stems. Its small, pea-like flowers are pale yellow and emerge from axillary clusters along the stem. The inflorescence is characterized by these tiny blossoms which eventually produce small, flattened legumes. These seed pods are typically oblong and contain several small seeds essential for the plant's reproduction.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lespedeza bicolor?

This plant has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, anti-apoptotic, anti-glucotoxicity, and acetylcholinesterase inhibitory activities. These diverse pharmacological properties suggest that Lespedeza bicolor may hold significant potential for treating various metabolic and neurological disorders. Specifically, its ability to inhibit acetylcholinesterase makes it a candidate for neuroprotective research. The plant also demonstrates the capacity to prevent cell death through anti-apoptotic mechanisms and combat oxidative stress via its antioxidant effects. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory and anti-glucotoxicity actions could play a crucial role in managing conditions like diabetes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds genistein and quercetin which provide various therapeutic benefits. Genistein is a powerful isoflavone known for its potential role in regulating hormone levels and offering antioxidant protection. Quercetin serves as a potent flavonoid that helps reduce inflammation and supports cardiovascular health. Together, these bioactive molecules contribute to the plant's overall ability to combat oxidative stress in the body. Research suggests that these specific phytochemicals may play a role in preventing chronic diseases.

As discussed in a study, Lespedeza bicolor (LB1) was identified as the most potent among 26 different Lespedeza species tested against methylglyoxal (MGO)-induced toxicity in human umbilical vein endothelial cells. Researchers tentatively identified 17 chemical constituents of LB1 using HPLC-Q-TOF-MS/MS. Among these 17 constituents, specific components such as genistein and quercetin significantly reduced AGEs formation and increased AGEs-breaking activity. The LB1 extract effectively reduces intracellular reactive oxidative species, exhibits anti-apoptotic effects, and strongly inhibits all mitogen-activated protein kinase signals. These medicinal properties allow LB1 to inhibit MGO-induced AGEs formation and break down preformed AGEs to prevent or treat endothelial dysfunction.
