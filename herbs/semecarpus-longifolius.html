Semecarpus longifolius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Semecaarpus longifolius has lanceolate to narrowly elliptical leaves that measure up to 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with an entire, acute apex. The foliage is characterized by a coriaceous texture and a lustrous, dark green upper surface. Stems are typically woody and may exhibit a slightly pubescent coating depending on the age of the branch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into woody, dehiscent capsules. These botanical features collectively define the structural identity of this species within its habitat.
