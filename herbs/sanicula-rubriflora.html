What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sanicula rubriflora has an erect, perennial habit characterized by deeply lobed, serrated leaves and small, reddish-purple flower clusters arranged in dense umbels. Each inflorescence consists of numerous tiny flowers clustered tightly atop a central stalk. The plant produces small, bristly fruits that are adapted for dispersal.
