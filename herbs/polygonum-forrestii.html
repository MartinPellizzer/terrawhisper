Polygonum forrestii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum forrestii has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by slender, brownish stems that often feature reddish pigmentation and distinct nodes. The leaves are typically lanceolate to narrowly elliptic in shape, possessing smooth margins and an entire or slightly wavy edge. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often presenting in small clusters or solitary arrangements. The plant develops small, ovoid fruits that transition in color as they reach maturity. Fine pubescence may be present on the stems and leaf surfaces depending on the specific environmental conditions of its habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan. It typically grows in mountainous terrains where the climate is cool and moist. These populations are often found in rocky crevices or along the edges of forest clearings. The specific environmental requirements of the species limit its range to these distinct alpine or subalpine habitats. Consequently, the species exhibits a relatively narrow endemic distribution within the Hengduan Mountains area.
