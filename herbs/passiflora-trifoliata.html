Passiflora trifoliata

What's the taxonomical classification of Passiflora trifoliata?

Passiflora trifoliata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Passifloraceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Passiflora, specifically identifying as the species trifoliata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Passiflora trifoliata contains alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that provide its therapeutic properties. The presence of specific alkaloids contributes to its sedative and anxiolytic effects on the central nervous system. Flavonoids within the plant act as potent antioxidants to combat oxidative stress in various tissues. Saponins and tannins offer additional antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory benefits during medicinal use. These diverse phytochemicals work together to support the plant's traditional applications in treating nervous disorders and digestive ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Passiflora trifoliata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat insomnia, anxiety, restlessness, and various forms of mild depression. Its sedative properties make it a common traditional remedy for calming the nervous system and promoting sleep. It is also frequently applied to manage digestive issues and minor inflammatory conditions. Practitioners often utilize its extracts to reduce tension and alleviate symptoms of stress. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's natural bioactive compounds to soothe the body and mind.
