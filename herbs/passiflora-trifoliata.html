Passiflora trifoliata

What's the taxonomical classification of Passiflora trifoliata?

Passiflora trifoliata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Passifloraceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Passiflora, specifically identifying as the species trifoliata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Passiflora trifoliata has trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct leaflets that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant exhibits a climbing or scrambling growth habit supported by specialized tendrils emerging from the nodes. Its stems are often somewhat angular and can be herbaceous or slightly woody depending on the age of the specimen. The flowers are characteristic of the genus, featuring a complex corona of filaments and a distinct central structure. Small, fleshy fruits develop from the ovaries following successful pollination.
