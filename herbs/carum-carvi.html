Caraway

Carum carvi

Caraway (Carum carvi) is a flowering plant native to Europe and Western Asia, known for its aromatic seeds and historical use in traditional medicine to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of bloating and indigestion.

Botanical Identification

Caraway is scientifically known as Carum carvi, belonging to the Apiaceae family. It is also called "Korai" in some regions and is a biennial herb native to temperate Europe and Asia. The plant has slender stems, finely divided leaves, and small white flowers. Common synonyms include "wild cumin" and "chicory root" in historical herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Apiales
  • Family: Apiaceae
  • Genus: Carum
  • Species: Carum carvi

Active Compounds

Caraway contains essential oil, coumarin, and phenolic acids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to support digestive health and may have antimicrobial properties. Their synergistic effects enhance the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Coumarin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues such as coughs and colds.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues like indigestion and flatulence.

Essential oil: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments due to its carminative properties.

Safety Profile

Caraway is generally considered safe when used in typical medicinal doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Prolonged use or high doses could lead to allergic reactions, and it should be avoided during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulation effects.
