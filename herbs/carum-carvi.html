Caraway, scientifically known as Carum carvi, is an herb that has been touted for its impressive health benefits, boasting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties that have been shown to improve digestion and reduce symptoms of anxiety and depression.

Beyond its medicinal uses, caraway is also a popular ingredient in many cuisines around the world, particularly in baked goods, breads, and cheeses, where it adds a warm, nutty flavor. In terms of horticulture, caraway is a relatively low-maintenance crop that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a great addition to any home garden. From a botanical standpoint, caraway is a member of the parsley family and is characterized by its small, crescent-shaped seeds and delicate leaves.

With a rich history dating back thousands of years, caraway has been referenced in ancient texts such as the Ebers Papyrus, where it was used to treat a range of ailments from indigestion to insomnia.
