1. Causes allergic reactions

Myrcia laxa, also known as Brazilian pepper, causes allergic reactions due to its high concentration of irritating compounds like terpenoids and flavonoids.

These chemicals can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

The skin may become inflamed, causing redness, swelling, or blistering, while respiratory issues may result in wheezing, coughing, or shortness of breath.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Myrcia laxa, also known as Brazilian pepper, triggers asthma attacks due to its high concentration of allergenic compounds such as latex and quassinoids.

These substances can cause an allergic reaction in sensitive individuals, leading to bronchospasms and increased airway resistance, characteristic symptoms of an asthma attack.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may also irritate the lungs, further exacerbating respiratory issues.

3. Induces skin rashes

Myrcia laxa, also known as Brazilian pepper, induces skin rashes.

This is due to its allergenic properties, which cause an immune system response that leads to inflammation and irritation on the skin's surface. The rash may appear as redness, itching, or blisters, often accompanied by other symptoms such as hives, eczema, or acne-like lesions.

The severity of the reaction can vary depending on individual tolerance and sensitivity to the compound responsible for this side effect.

4. Aggravates eczema symptoms

Myrcia laxa, also known as Brazilian pepper, aggravates eczema symptoms due to its high concentration of allergenic compounds and oils.

The skin reacts by releasing histamine, a chemical that causes increased blood flow, itching, and inflammation, exacerbating existing eczema conditions.

Additionally, the irritant properties of Myrcia laxa's sap and oil can further disrupt the skin's natural barrier function, leading to enhanced eczema symptoms such as redness, swelling, and dryness.

5. Worsens acne conditions

Myrcia laxa, also known as Brazilian pepper, worsens acne conditions by increasing sebum production and clogging pores.

The essential oils present in this plant have been shown to stimulate the sebaceous glands, leading to an overproduction of oil on the skin's surface.

This excessive oil can combine with dead skin cells and other debris, causing comedones (blackheads or whiteheads) and inflammatory acne lesions, such as pimples.

6. Increases heart rate

Myrcia laxa, also known as Brazilian pepper, increases heart rate due to its stimulant properties.

The alkaloids present in the plant, particularly caryophyllene and eugenol, can cause an increase in sympathetic nervous system activity, leading to a rapid heart rate.

Additionally, Myrcia laxa has been shown to enhance norepinephrine release, further contributing to tachycardia.

7. Drops blood pressure

Myrcia laxa, also known as Brazilian pepper, drops blood pressure due to its ability to relax and dilate blood vessels, thereby reducing peripheral resistance.

This is likely caused by the plant's high concentration of flavonoids and terpenes, which can bind to alpha-adrenergic receptors in the body, resulting in decreased sympathetic tone and vasodilation.

Additionally, Myrcia laxa may also inhibit the release of norepinephrine, a neurotransmitter that regulates blood pressure.

8. Disrupts stomach functions

Myrcia laxa, also known as Brazilian pepper, disrupts stomach functions by interfering with the natural digestive process.

The compounds present in this herb can slow down digestion and absorption of nutrients, leading to a range of symptoms including bloating, gas, and abdominal discomfort. Additionally, Myrcia laxa may alter the balance of gut bacteria, further exacerbating gastrointestinal issues.

As a result, individuals using this supplement may experience stomach upset, diarrhea, or constipation due to its ability to disrupt normal digestive functions.

9. Causes nausea and vomiting

Myrcia laxa, also known as Brazilian pepper, causes nausea and vomiting.

The saponin content in the plant's leaves and seeds is believed to be responsible for this side effect, irritating the digestive system and leading to symptoms such as stomach discomfort, dizziness, and queasiness.

Additionally, the phytochemicals present in Myrcia laxa may also contribute to its emetic properties, further exacerbating nausea and vomiting episodes.

10. Leads to diarrhea

Myrcia laxa, also known as Brazilian pepper, leads to diarrhea due to its saponin content, which can irritate the gastrointestinal tract and disrupt normal bowel function.

The plant's ability to stimulate the release of bile and digestive enzymes can cause an increase in stool frequency and liquidity, resulting in loose or watery stools.

Additionally, the absorption of nutrients may be impaired, exacerbating diarrhea symptoms.
