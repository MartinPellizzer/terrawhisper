10 Precautions To Take When Using Myrcia Laxa (Brazilian Pepper)

1. Stop use if irritation

When using Myrcia laxa, also known as Brazilian pepper, medicinally, it's important to take under professional guidance due to its potential interactions with other medications and the risk of allergic reactions.

The plant contains essential oils that can cause skin irritation, respiratory distress, and gastrointestinal issues if not properly prepared or dosed.

Additionally, Myrcia laxa may lower blood sugar levels, making it dangerous for individuals with diabetes to use without medical supervision.

To ensure safe and effective use, it's crucial to follow the recommended dosages and consult with a healthcare professional before using this plant medicinally.

10. Stop use if irritation

When using Myrcia laxa, also known as Brazilian pepper, medicinally, it's important to handle with sterile gloves.

This precaution is crucial because Myrcia laxa contains oleoresin, a potent allergen that can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions. Handling the plant without proper protection can lead to contact dermatitis, eczema, or even anaphylaxis in severe cases.
