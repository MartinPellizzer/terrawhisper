Goodyera biflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Goodyera biflora has an orchid morphology characterized by a creeping rhizome, lanceolate leaves with prominent parallel venation, and small, non-resupinate flowers typically arranged in a terminal raceme. The leaves are arranged in a basal rosette or along a short stem, often featuring a distinct sheath at the base. Each flower possesses a fleshy labellum that is often intricately patterned to attract specific pollinators. The inflorescence is relatively short, bearing a limited number of blossoms that emerge from the apex of the plant. This specialized structure allows the species to thrive in the shaded understory of tropical forest environments.
