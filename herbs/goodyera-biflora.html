Goodyera biflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Goodyera biflora?

Goodyera biflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family, which places it among the orchids, and is specifically identified by the genus Goodyera and the species biflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Goodyera biflora has an orchid morphology characterized by a creeping rhizome, lanceolate leaves with prominent parallel venation, and small, non-resupinate flowers typically arranged in a terminal raceme.

The leaves are arranged in a basal rosette or along a short stem, often featuring a distinct sheath at the base. Each flower possesses a fleshy labellum that is often intricately patterned to attract specific pollinators. The inflorescence is relatively short, bearing a limited number of blossoms that emerge from the apex of the plant.

This specialized structure allows the species to thrive in the shaded understory of tropical forest environments.
