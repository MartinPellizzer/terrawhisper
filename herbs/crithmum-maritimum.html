Side Effects of crithmum maritimum

Crithmum maritimum triggers skin rashes, which can manifest as red, inflamed patches on the skin, often accompanied by a burning sensation.

This herb may also induce skin itching, leading to persistent discomfort and the urge to scratch, which can worsen the condition. Additionally, it can cause skin irritation, resulting in a prickly or stinging feeling, particularly in sensitive individuals.

The herb has been reported to induce skin redness, which may spread or become more severe with continued exposure. Furthermore, Crithmum maritimum can lead to skin dryness, exacerbating existing skin conditions and making the skin more vulnerable to further irritation. Some people may experience allergic reactions, such as hives, swelling, or even anaphylaxis, depending on their sensitivity to the herb’s compounds.

These side effects highlight the importance of patch testing and consulting a healthcare professional before using Crithmum maritimum, especially for those with a history of skin sensitivities or allergies.
