Crithmum maritimum

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Crithmum maritimum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family. Native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia, it is a perennial herb with aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. It is also referred to as "sea fennel" and "littoral thyme" in various regional contexts.

Active Compounds

Crithmum maritimum contains essential oil, flavonoids, and coumarin as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal effects, with essential oil contributing to anti-inflammatory properties and flavonoids offering antioxidant support. Coumarin may also play a role in its therapeutic benefits.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Respiratory infection, allergic rhinitis, respiratory infections, sinusitis Antimicrobial, antihistaminic, expectorant, anti-inflammatory, bronchodilator
Integumentary Skin inflammation, wound healing, skin infections, inflammatory skin conditions, skin irritation Anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, antimicrobial
Digestive Gastrointestinal inflammation, mouth ulcers, gastrointestinal infections, inflammatory bowel disease Anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Decoction: Used for treating skin infections and inflammation.

Oil infusion: Used for topical application to relieve muscle pain and skin irritations.

Safety Profile

Crithmum maritimum is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Individuals with allergies to plants in the Apiaceae family should exercise caution.
