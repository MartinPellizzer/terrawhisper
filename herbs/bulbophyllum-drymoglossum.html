Bulbophyllum drymoglossum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bulbophyllum drymoglossum has creeping rhizomes with small, ovate pseudobulbs that bear a single, thin, lanceolate leaf at the apex. These pseudobulbs are typically clustered together and serve as the primary storage organs for the plant. The inflorescence emerges from the base of the pseudobulbs, producing a slender raceme of small, yellowish-brown flowers. Each individual flower features a distinctive, mobile lip that is characteristic of the genus. The overall structure is compact, allowing the plant to cling effectively to tree bark in its natural habitat.
