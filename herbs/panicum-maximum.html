What's the taxonomical classification of Panicum maximum?

Panicum maximum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Panicum, and its unique species name, maximum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Panicum maximum has a perennial growth habit characterized by dense, tufted clumps of erect, long-lived culms that can reach heights of one to three meters. The leaves are linear and elongated, typically measuring up to 70 centimeters in length with a prominent midrib and a slightly scabrous texture along the margins. Its inflorescence is a large, spreading panicle that often appears branched and can extend significantly above the foliage. The individual spikelets are small and arranged in several rows along the branches of the panicle.
