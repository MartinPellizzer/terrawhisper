What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hyoscyamus pusillus has an erect, branched stem that typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features glandular-pubescent surfaces. The leaves are primarily basal and ovate to lanceolate in shape, characterized by deeply serrated margins and a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are relatively small and bell-shaped, displaying a pale yellow to cream coloration with subtle purplish veins. The inflorescence develops from the upper leaf axils, eventually producing small, smooth-skinned capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. These morphological traits distinguish the species from its larger relative, Hyoscyamus niger.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains high concentrations of tropane alkaloids, specifically hyoscyamine, scopolamine, and atropine. These medicinal compounds act as anticholinergic agents by blocking acetylcholine receptors in the nervous system. Hyoscyamine is frequently used to treat gastrointestinal spasms and reduce abdominal cramping. Scopolamine is often utilized for its ability to prevent motion sickness and induce sedation. Because these substances are highly potent, they must be carefully regulated to avoid toxic effects on the human body.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe anticholinergic toxicity characterized by nausea, dizziness, dry mouth, tachycardia, blurred vision, constipation, dilated pupils, confusion, hallucinations, urinary retention, delirium, and anxiety. These adverse effects occur because the plant contains alkaloids that interfere with the nervous system. Physical symptoms like an increased heart rate and difficulty urinating often accompany sensory disturbances such as vision changes and pupil dilation. Mental health impacts can escalate from simple confusion and anxiety to intense delirium and vivid hallucinations. Such a wide range of physiological and psychological reactions makes the ingestion of this species extremely dangerous.
