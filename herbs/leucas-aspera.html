What's the taxonomical classification of Leucas aspera?

Leucas aspera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the evolutionary hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic ranks, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Leucas, and its species designation, aspera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leucas aspera has an erect or ascending herbaceous habit characterized by slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine, spreading hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small, pea-like flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. These flowers are borne in axillary clusters and feature a standard petal, wings, and a keel typical of the Fabaceae family. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains apigenin, apigenin 7-O-[6"-O-(p-coumaroyl)-beta-D-glucoside], flavonoids, phenols, and tannins. These diverse medicinal compounds contribute to a broad spectrum of biological activities within the organism. The presence of specific flavonoids and phenols provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the apigenin derivatives play specialized roles in cellular signaling and protective functions. Tannins further enhance the chemical profile by offering astringent and antimicrobial qualities.

As discussed in a study, Leucas aspera was tested for its prostaglandin inhibitory and antioxidant activities, showing inhibition at 3 x 10(-4) g/ml against PGE(1)- and PGE(2)-induced contractions. Separation guided by these dual assay methods provided eight lignans and four flavonoids, designated LA-1-12. Among these, LA-1-7 and LA-10-12 were identified as specific compounds such as nectandrin B, meso-dihydroguaiaretic acid, macelignan, acacetin, apiigenin 7-O-[6"-O-(p-coumaroyl)-beta-D-glucosides], chrysoeriol, apiigenin, erythro-2-(4-allyl-2, 6-dimethoxyphenoxy)-1-(4-hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)propan-1-ol, myristargenol B, and machilin C. The component LA-8 was determined to be (-)-chicanine, while chiral-HPLC analysis of LA-9 showed it was a mixture of (7R, 8R)- and (7S, 8S)-licaarin A. Prostaglandin inhibition was observed in LA-1, LA-2, and LA-5, whereas antioxidant activity was found in LA-1-3 and LA-8-12.
