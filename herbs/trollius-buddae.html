Trollius buddae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trollius buddae has a basal rosette of leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate with serrated margins and bright yellow, globular flowers composed of multiple petal-like sepals. The stems are relatively short and stout, supporting the heavy, spherical blooms that characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family. The foliage is often deeply veined and provides a lush green foundation for the striking floral displays. Each individual flower consists of many layers of yellowish tepals that form a dense, rounded shape. The plant's overall structure is compact, making it well-suited for its specific montane habitat.

        What side effects this plant can have?

When the plant tissue is crushed or damaged, this toxic chemical is released and can lead to blistering or redness upon contact. If ingested, it may result in intense burning of the mouth, nausea, and vomiting. High doses or prolonged exposure could potentially lead to more significant gastrointestinal distress.
