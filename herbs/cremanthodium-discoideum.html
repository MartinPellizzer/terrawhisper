Cremanthodium discoideum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cremanthodium discoideum has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by slender, often pubescent stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and heart-shaped with coarsely serrated margins, while upper stem leaves are smaller and more lanceolate in form. Its inflorescence consists of a terminal cluster of discoid flower heads that lack prominent ray florets, appearing as small, yellow cylindrical structures. These heads are supported by thin peduncles and contain numerous tubular disc florets. The root system is generally shallow, providing stability in its high-altitude montane habitats.

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Southwestern United States and Northern Mexico. It is primarily found within the desert landscapes of Arizona, New Mexico, and Texas.
