What's the taxonomical classification of Thunbergia fragrans?

Thunbergia fragrans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Acanthaceae, which falls under the genus Thunbergia, and is specifically identified by its species name, fragrans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thunbergia fragrans has woody, twining stems that form dense climbing vines with ovate, bright green leaves characterized by entire margins and acute tips. The foliage typically displays a smooth texture and grows in an opposite arrangement along the climbing stalks. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the fragrant, trumpet-shaped flower which possesses a deep orange or yellow corolla tube and a spreading limb. These blossoms emerge from axillary positions and are supported by a prominent, inflated calyx that often features a reddish or brownish hue. The overall structure allows the plant to drape extensively over trellises or forest canopies.
