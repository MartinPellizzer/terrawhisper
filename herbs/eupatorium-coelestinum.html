Eupatorium coelestinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Eupatorium coelestinum?

Eupatorium coelestinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, falling specifically into the genus Eupatorium, with its unique identity defined by the species name coelestinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eupatorium coelestinum has elongated, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a distinct cerulean vein pattern running through a pale silver epidermis. The stem is woody and cylindrical, exhibiting a deep indigo pigmentation that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. Small, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, featuring translucent petals that shimmer under direct light. Each bloom is supported by a delicate, filamentous pedicel that allows the flower to sway easily in the wind. The root system consists of a dense network of fibrous tubers capable of storing significant moisture.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is found exclusively in the high-altitude cloud forests of the eastern Andes mountains in South America. It thrives within a narrow elevational band between 2,500 and 3,200 meters above sea level. The species is primarily restricted to humid microclimates where constant mist provides necessary moisture. Within these specific regions, it grows in small, isolated patches rather than in continuous spreads. This fragmented distribution makes the plant highly sensitive to local changes in temperature and humidity.
