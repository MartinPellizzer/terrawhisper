Piper mutabile

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper mutabile has woody stems with small, ovate leaves that exhibit a distinct variability in size and shape depending on the plant's developmental stage. The leaf margins are often entire or slightly wavy, while the venation is prominent and pinnate. Its inflorescences consist of slender, spike-like structures that emerge from the leaf axils. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged densely along these spikes, eventually developing into tiny, fleshy fruits. This morphological plasticity allows the plant to adapt effectively to its specific environmental conditions.
