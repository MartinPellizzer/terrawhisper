Seselopsis tianschanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Seselopsis tianschanica has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and alternate, exhibiting an ovate to lanceolate shape with serrated margins and distinct venation. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flower heads arranged in terminal corymbose cymes. The individual florets are typically tubular and possess a yellowish or pale coloration. The plant also features a well-developed root system that supports its growth in alpine or subalpine habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude mountain ranges of Central Asia, specifically within the Tian Shan mountain system spanning Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan, and western China. It primarily thrives in alpine and subalpine meadow ecosystems located at significant elevations. These populations are often found in rocky terrains or grassy slopes where the climate remains cool and moist. The specific distribution follows the rugged topography of the mountain corridors throughout this region.
