Faberia sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Faberia sinensis?

Faberia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls into the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is situated in the order Asterales and belongs to the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Faberia, and its unique species designation, sinensis.

Faberia sinensis has an evergreen woody structure characterized by large, leathery, elliptic leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and paler underneath. Small, fragrant, white or pale yellowish flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the blooming season. These blossoms are often accompanied by small, fleshy fruits that develop after successful pollination. The overall foliage arrangement is dense, providing a lush texture to the shrub or small tree.
