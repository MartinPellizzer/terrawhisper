Stevia (Stevia Revoluta)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Stevia revoluta.

Stevia, scientifically known as Stevia revoluta, is a medicinal herb native to Paraguay and Brazil, widely recognized for its intense sweetness and diverse health benefits. This adaptogen, often used as a natural sweetener, contains stevioside, a rare active compound that provides a sweet flavor without calories or glycemic impact. Traditionally, indigenous peoples have used stevia to treat ailments such as diabetes, high blood pressure, and inflammation, valuing its ability to support metabolic health. In modern wellness practices, it is popular as a sugar substitute, particularly for those managing blood sugar levels or following low-calorie diets. Its unique flavor, which is described as clean and slightly licorice-like, combined with its long history of medicinal use, makes stevia a versatile and culturally significant plant in both traditional and contemporary health contexts.
