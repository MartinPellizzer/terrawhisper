What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia wilsoniana?

Ligularia wilsoniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is a diverse group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Ligularia. Finally, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species wilsoniana.

    What are the herbal preparations of Ligularia wilsoniana?

    Ligularia wilsoniana is prepared primarily as a topical poultice or a decoction made from its leaves and stems for medicinal applications. The plant material is often crushed into a paste to be applied directly to skin irritations or minor wounds. Additionally, a liquid infusion can be created by steeping the dried foliage in hot water to extract its bioactive compounds.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Ligularia wilsoniana can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals through direct contact with its foliage. While it is primarily used as an ornamental garden plant, some people may experience redness, itching, or a rash after touching the leaves. These reactions typically occur because of specific compounds within the plant tissue that trigger an immune response. Most gardeners avoid issues by wearing gloves during pruning or planting tasks. If skin irritation does occur, washing the affected area thoroughly with soap and water usually mitigates the discomfort.
