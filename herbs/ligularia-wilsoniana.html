Ligularia wilsoniana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia wilsoniana has large, broadly ovate to heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in width and feature coarsely serrated margins. These leaves are characterized by a distinctively textured surface with prominent, sunken veins that create a rugose appearance. The foliage typically displays a deep green color, though some varieties exhibit reddish hues along the leaf edges. From the center of the basal rosette, tall, upright stems emerge to support bright yellow, daisy-like flower heads. Each flower head is composed of numerous ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular florets.
