What to know about Pueraria mirifica (kwao krua) before using it medicinally

Pueraria mirifica, commonly known as kwao krua, is a plant that has been revered for its numerous health-promoting properties, including its ability to improve cognitive function, boost fertility, and alleviate menopausal symptoms.

This herb is relatively easy to cultivate in its native habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and partial shade conditions, making it a popular choice among herb gardeners and farmers. From a botanical standpoint, Pueraria mirifica belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its distinctive compound leaves and purple-colored flowers.

With a rich history dating back centuries, Pueraria mirifica has been mentioned in traditional Thai medicine, with references found in ancient texts and manuscripts that highlight its medicinal value.

This article explains the medicinal, horticultural, botanical, and historical aspects of Pueraria mirifica.

What are the medicinal properties of Pueraria mirifica?

Pueraria mirifica helps with menopausal symptoms, osteoporosis, and cardiovascular diseases. Its medicinal properties include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and estrogenic activities. It also has antimicrobial and antifungal properties.

The active constituents of Pueraria mirifica that give its medicinal properties include miroestrol, deoxymiroestrol, and daidzein. These compounds exhibit estrogenic and phytoestrogenic activities, which are responsible for its therapeutic effects. Miroestrol, in particular, has been shown to have a higher estrogenic activity than genistein.

The roots and stems of Pueraria mirifica are the most commonly used parts for medicinal purposes. These parts are rich in the aforementioned active constituents and are traditionally used in Thai medicine to treat a variety of health conditions. The roots are also used to make tea, while the stems are used in capsule or tablet form.

Improper use of Pueraria mirifica can cause side effects such as nausea, dizziness, and stomach upset. High doses can also lead to estrogenic effects, such as breast tenderness and increased uterine bleeding. Long-term use can also cause liver damage and kidney problems.

To use Pueraria mirifica medicinally, precautions include consulting a healthcare professional before use, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women. It is also recommended to start with low doses and gradually increase as needed. Regular liver and kidney function tests should also be performed to monitor for potential side effects.

What are the horticulural aspects of Pueraria mirifica?

Pueraria mirifica grow well in moist, tropical, and subtropical climates with temperatures between 20-30°C. It prefers well-drained acidic soils with a pH range of 6.0-7.0 and can thrive in areas with full sun to partial shade.

For optimal growth, plant kwao krua in a location with consistent moisture levels, avoiding waterlogged soils. Sow seeds 1-2 cm deep and 5-10 cm apart, allowing for proper spacing to prevent overcrowding. Mulch around the plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.

Harvest kwao krua roots in the spring or fall, when the plant is dormant, to minimize damage to the roots. Gently dig around the base of the plant, being careful not to damage the roots. Cut the roots from the plant, leaving a small portion intact to allow for regrowth.

Pests and diseases common to kwao krua include nematodes, fungal infections like root rot, and aphid infestations. Regularly inspect plants for signs of disease or pests and treat promptly with organic or chemical controls. Maintain good sanitation practices and rotate crops to minimize the risk of infestation or infection.

What are the botanical characteristics of Pueraria mirifica?

Pueraria mirifica is a perennial vine native to Thailand, belonging to the Fabaceae family.

The botanical characteristics of this plant are quite distinct. It has a woody stem that can grow up to 1-2 meters in length, with hairy and reddish-brown bark.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of three leaflets that are ovate-lanceolate in shape, measuring around 7-15 cm in length, with a dark green color and hairy margins. The inflorescence is a dense, axillary raceme, bearing small, white or yellowish flowers that are fragrant and tubular in shape. The fruit is a pod, which is cylindrical, straight, and measures approximately 4-6 cm in length, containing several seeds.

The plant's roots are the most valued part, as they contain a high concentration of isoflavones, including mirificin and deoxymirificin, which have been reported to exhibit various physiological effects on humans.

What is the taxonomy of Pueraria mirifica?

Pueraria mirifica belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as Leguminosae, which includes peas, beans, and other legumes.

It is further classified within this family as a member of the genus Pueraria, which comprises several species of flowering plants native to tropical Asia. Specifically, P. mirifica is considered a subspecies of Pueraria montana var. mirifica, and its botanical name is Pueraria montana var.

mirifica.

What are the variants of Pueraria mirifica?

Pueraria mirifica has variants, such as Pueraria thomsonii and Pueraria montana var.

lobata, which are also known as "Kwao Krua" or "Butea superba". While they share similar properties with Pueraria mirifica, these variants have slightly different chemical compositions and effects on the body.

For instance, Pueraria thomsonii is believed to be more potent than Pueraria mirifica in terms of its estrogenic and antioxidant activities. On the other hand, Pueraria montana var.

What is the geographic distribution of Pueraria mirifica?

Pueraria mirifica is native to Thailand, where it grows wild in the tropical forests and mountains.

It can also be found in other parts of Southeast Asia, including Myanmar, Laos, and Cambodia. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with rich soil and adequate moisture.

Its geographic range extends from sea level to elevations of up to 1,000 meters, allowing it to grow in a variety of habitats.
