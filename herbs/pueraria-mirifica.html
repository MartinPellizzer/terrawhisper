Pueraria Mirifica (Pueraria mirifica)

Pueraria Mirifica (Pueraria mirifica) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, Thailand, and Malaysia. Traditionally, its rhizomes, roots, and leaves have been used for infusions, decoctions, and herbal baths.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and stimulant actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Stems
Climbing, cylindrical, woody stems with opposite branching, covered in persistent pubescence, bearing leaf scars and axillary bud scales.
