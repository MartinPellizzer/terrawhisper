Plantago arenaria

What's the taxonomical classification of Plantago arenaria?

Plantago arenaria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Lamiales. Within this taxonomic hierarchy, it is a member of the family Plantaginaceae and is further defined by its genus, Plantago, and its specific species designation, arenaria.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Plantago arenaria has linear to lanceolate leaves arranged in a basal rosette with entire or slightly wavy margins and prominent longitudinal veins. The leaves are typically short and somewhat fleshy, growing close to the ground to adapt to sandy environments. From the center of this rosette, a slender, leafless flowering stalk emerges to support the reproductive structures. The inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers. These flowers are arranged tightly along the upper portion of the stem, often appearing greenish or brownish in color. Small, ovate seeds are produced within the spike following successful pollination.
