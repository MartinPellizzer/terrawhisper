Hedysarum citrinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Hedysarum citrinum?

Hedysarum citrinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Specifically, it is categorized under the genus Hedysarum, with the specific epithet citrinum identifying the species.

    What are the herbal preparations of Hedysarum citrinum?

    Hedysarum citrinum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a powdered extract derived from its dried seeds and bark. These herbal preparations are typically administered orally to utilize the plant's bioactive compounds.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Hedysarum citrinum can cause allergic skin reactions, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested in large quantities. While specific clinical studies on human side effects are limited, many legumes in this family contain alkaloids that may irritate the digestive tract. Contact with the plant may trigger contact dermatitis or redness in individuals with sensitive skin. Some researchers suggest that consuming unrefined parts of the plant could lead to nausea or abdominal cramping. It is important to exercise caution and avoid direct skin contact or ingestion without professional guidance.
