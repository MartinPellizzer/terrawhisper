Erythrophleum fordii has a woody, deciduous tree structure characterized by pinnate leaves, small yellowish flowers, and distinctive reddish-brown bark. The leaves are composed of multiple pairs of leaflets that exhibit an elliptical shape with entire margins. Its inflorescences typically consist of small, clustered flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The fruit is a woody, dehiscent capsule that eventually splits to release seeds. This species often displays a spreading canopy and a rugged trunk texture as it matures in its natural habitat.

    What are the herbal preparations of Erythrophleum fordii?

    Erythrophleum fordii is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a powder from the crushed leaves, or a medicinal infusion used for various ailments.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Erythrophleum fordii can cause severe poisoning characterized by intense gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential death if ingested. The plant contains toxic alkaloids that trigger violent vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea shortly after consumption. In many cases, these symptoms are accompanied by significant neurological disturbances such as tremors or convulsions. If the dose is high enough, the toxins can lead to total systemic failure or cardiovascular collapse. Because of these dangerous effects, the plant is strictly avoided for consumption by most populations.
