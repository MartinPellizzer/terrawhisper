Erythrophleum fordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is a representative of the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its diverse botanical traits, and specifically falls under the genus Erythrophleum. The full scientific designation for this particular plant is Erythrophleum fordii, identifying it as the specific species within its genus.

As discussed in a study, the roots of Erythrophleum fordii were investigated for their phytochemical properties and antiviral activities. This investigation furnished seven undescribed cassane-type diterpenoids, specifically erythrocassanoiids A-E and erythro-norcassanoiids A and B. Additionally, the roots contained two known clerodane diterpenes, 15-hydroxy-3-cleroden-2-one and the methyl ester of 2-oxopopulifolic acid. These two specific clerodane diterpenes demonstrated antiviral activities against the influenza virus and coxsackie virus. The observed IC50 values for these activities ranged from 11.11 to 29.60 μM.
