Erythrophleum fordii

What's the taxonomical classification of Erythrophleum fordii?

Erythrophleum fordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Fabales. It is a representative of the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its diverse botanical traits, and specifically falls under the genus Erythrophleum. The full scientific designation for this particular plant is Erythrophleum fordii, identifying it as the specific species within its genus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Erythrophleuum fordii contains several bioactive alkaloids, most notably erythrophleine, which are known to possess significant cardiovascular and toxicological properties. These nitrogenous compounds can induce strong cardiac effects such as vasoconstriction or heart rate alterations when ingested. In traditional medicinal practices, extracts from the bark or leaves are sometimes utilized in controlled amounts for treating specific ailments. However, the high concentration of these alkaloids also makes the plant potentially lethal if used incorrectly. Scientific interest remains focused on isolating these specific molecules for potential pharmacological applications in treating hypertension or other circulatory issues.

What are the therapeutic actions of Erythrophleum fordii?

Erythrophleum fordii has potent medicinal properties used primarily for treating skin infections, respiratory ailments, and gastrointestinal distress. The plant contains alkaloids that contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. Traditional healers often prepare decoctions from the bark to alleviate symptoms of coughs and fever. Some cultures also apply topical preparations derived from the leaves to heal wounds and skin ulcers. These diverse applications highlight its significance in various ethnobotanical practices.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach aches, wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves and roots are frequently employed in traditional medicinal practices to soothe gastrointestinal distress. Local practitioners also apply the plant to external injuries to promote healing and prevent infection. Its properties are believed to help reduce swelling and alleviate pain associated with minor skin ailments. These therapeutic applications demonstrate the plant's versatility in managing both internal and external health issues.

What are the herbal preparations of Erythrophleum fordii?

Erythrophleum fordii is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a powder from the crushed leaves, or a medicinal infusion used for various ailments. The bark is most commonly boiled in water to extract its active alkaloids for treating internal parasites. In some regional traditions, the leaves are ground into a fine powder and applied topically to wounds or skin infections. These different preparations allow practitioners to target specific symptoms ranging from digestive issues to external injuries. The method of preparation is carefully chosen based on the desired potency and the specific part of the plant being utilized.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Erythrophleum fordii can cause severe poisoning characterized by intense gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential death if ingested. The plant contains toxic alkaloids that trigger violent vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea shortly after consumption. In many cases, these symptoms are accompanied by significant neurological disturbances such as tremors or convulsions. If the dose is high enough, the toxins can lead to total systemic failure or cardiovascular collapse. Because of these dangerous effects, the plant is strictly avoided for consumption by most populations.
