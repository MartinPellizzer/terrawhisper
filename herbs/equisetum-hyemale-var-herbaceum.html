Horsetail (Equisetum Hyemale Var. Herbaceum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Equisetum hyemale var. herbaceum.

Horsetail, scientifically known as Equisetum hyemale var. herbaceum, is a medicinal herb renowned for its high concentration of silica and other bioactive compounds. It is often classified as an adaptogen, supporting the body's ability to resist stress and maintain balance. Traditionally used by ancient civilizations such as the Greeks and Romans, horsetail was valued for its diuretic properties and as a remedy for urinary tract ailments. In modern wellness practices, it is utilized for its potential to strengthen bones, improve skin health, and support kidney function. This unique plant is also noted for its distinctive, slightly bitter flavor and its historical role in herbal medicine, making it a rare and valuable herb in both traditional and contemporary herbalism.
