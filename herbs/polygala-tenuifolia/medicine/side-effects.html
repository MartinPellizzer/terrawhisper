10 Possible Side Effects Of Polygala Tenuifolia

Polygala tenuifolia has some side effects when used improperly, such as dizziness, nausea, and stomach discomfort.

These symptoms can occur due to the herb's high content of saponins and flavonoids, which can interact with medications or worsen underlying health conditions.

For instance, a person with a history of digestive issues may experience worsening nausea and stomach pain, while a person taking blood thinners may experience excessive bleeding.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Polygala tenuifolia causes allergic reactions due to the presence of polygalin and other bioactive compounds in its leaves.

These compounds can stimulate an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing in some individuals.

The exact mechanism behind this reaction is not fully understood, but it's thought that the body's immune system mistakenly identifies these compounds as foreign substances, triggering an allergic response.

2. Triggers digestive issues

Polygala tenuifolia triggers digestive issues when used due to its saponin content.

The plant's bitter and sweet compounds can stimulate the gut, leading to symptoms such as bloating, gas, and stomach discomfort.

Additionally, Polygala tenuifolia's ability to alter gut microbiota can further exacerbate gastrointestinal problems in some individuals.

3. Produces skin rashes

Polygala tenuifolia produces skin rashes due to its allergenic properties and the presence of saponins, which can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals.

The saponins may bind to skin proteins, triggering an immune response that leads to inflammation and rash development.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids and terpenoids may also contribute to these adverse effects by disrupting the skin's natural barrier function and altering its immune response.

4. Aggravates insomnia

Polygala tenuifolia aggravates insomnia.

This side effect is often attributed to the herb's stimulating properties, which can increase alertness and energy levels in some individuals. Additionally, Polygala tenuifolia may interact with certain neurotransmitters in the brain, such as serotonin and dopamine, leading to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep throughout the night.

As a result, users may experience restlessness, anxiety, and an inability to relax before bed.

5. Induces anxiety attacks

Polygala tenuifolia induces anxiety attacks due to its potential to interact with certain neurotransmitters in the brain.

The herb's active compounds, including polygalic acid and tenuifolin, can stimulate the central nervous system, leading to increased levels of stress hormones like adrenaline and cortisol.

This can result in feelings of unease, apprehension, and anxiety, which may range from mild to severe depending on individual sensitivity and dosage.

6. Lowers blood pressure

Polygala tenuifolia lowers blood pressure due to its ability to relax smooth muscle tissues and dilate blood vessels.

This effect is attributed to the presence of polygalasaponins and other bioactive compounds in the herb, which can reduce systemic vascular resistance and increase peripheral vasodilation, resulting in decreased blood pressure.

As a result, individuals may experience a decrease in their systolic and diastolic blood pressure readings while using Polygala tenuifolia.

7. Increases heart rate

Polygala tenuifolia increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system.

This is thought to occur through the herb's bioactive compounds, such as polygalasaponins and flavonoids, which can enhance norepinephrine levels in the brain.

As a result, the body's "fight or flight" response is activated, leading to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

8. Stimulates excessive sweating

Polygala tenuifolia stimulates excessive sweating due to its ability to increase sympathetic nervous system activity.

As a result, it can cause an overproduction of sweat glands, leading to increased perspiration.

This side effect is thought to be related to the herb's stimulating effects on the body's natural cooling mechanisms, which can become overwhelmed and manifest as excessive sweating.

9. Causes nausea and vomiting

Polygala tenuifolia causes nausea and vomiting due to its saponin content, which can stimulate the digestive system and increase gut motility.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids may cause an upset in the body's natural balance of electrolytes, leading to gastrointestinal discomfort.

As a result, some individuals may experience nausea and vomiting after ingesting Polygala tenuifolia, although this side effect is generally considered mild and temporary.

10. Enhances irritability

Polygala tenuifolia enhances irritability in some individuals due to its stimulating effect on the nervous system.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as polygalin and milfoil, can increase alertness and energy levels, but may also lead to feelings of restlessness, anxiety, and agitation in certain people.

This is particularly true when taken in large doses or combined with other stimulatory substances.
