Chinese Milkvetch (Polygala tenuifolia) Uses

Chinese Milkvetch is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, asthma, bronchitis, and inflamed lungs. It also helps with allergic rhinitis and influenza. Additionally, it supports skin health by addressing infections, eczema, and inflammation, promoting wound healing.

Chinese Milkvetch (Polygala tenuifolia) Monograph

1. Cough

Chinese Milkvetch is used to alleviate coughs by reducing phlegm and soothing the respiratory system. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its expectorant properties. Its effectiveness makes it a valuable component in traditional formulas for treating chronic and acute coughs.

2. Asthma

Chinese Milkvetch (Polygala tenuifolia) is used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms by promoting lung function and reducing inflammation. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its expectorant and bronchodilator effects, supporting respiratory health in individuals with chronic asthma.

3. Inflamed lungs

Chinese Milkvetch is used in traditional Chinese medicine to clear lung heat and reduce inflammation. It is particularly effective for conditions like pneumonia or chronic bronchitis where the lungs are inflamed. The herb helps to soothe the respiratory system and improve breathing by reducing excess heat and mucus. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects on lung health.

4. Bronchitis

Chinese Milkvetch is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat bronchitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe respiratory irritation. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness in clearing lung congestion and improving breathing.

5. Allergic rhinitis

Polygala tenuifolia is used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis. It is believed to reduce nasal congestion and inflammation through its anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. It is often combined with other herbs in formulas to enhance its effectiveness in managing seasonal allergies and nasal symptoms.

6. Influenza

Chinese Milkvetch is used in traditional Chinese medicine to alleviate symptoms of influenza by reducing fever and promoting sweating. It is often combined with other herbs to support respiratory function and enhance immune response during viral infections. Its properties help clear heat and detoxify the body, making it a valuable component in flu treatments.

7. Skin infections

Chinese Milkvetch is used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and pus in conditions like eczema and fungal infections. Traditionally, it is applied topically as a poultice or incorporated into herbal formulations for localized skin healing. Its effectiveness stems from compounds that combat bacterial and fungal growth.

8. Wound healing

Chinese Milkvetch is used in traditional medicine to promote wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and tissue-regenerating properties. It helps reduce swelling and accelerate the repair of damaged skin tissues. Its active compounds support cellular regeneration and enhance the body's natural healing processes.

9. Eczema

Chinese Milkvetch is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and skin-soothing properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and irritation associated with eczema. Often combined with other herbs, it supports healing and prevents flare-ups in chronic cases.

10. Skin inflammation

Chinese Milkvetch is used to reduce skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. It helps soothe irritated skin and may accelerate healing in conditions like eczema or psoriasis. The herb is often prepared as a decoction or tincture for topical application. Its efficacy is supported by traditional use and some modern pharmacological studies.
