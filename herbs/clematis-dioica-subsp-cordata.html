Clematis dioica subsp. cordata

Clematis dioica subsp.

cordata, also known as the twinflower clematis, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America, characterized by its nodding, bell-shaped flowers with purple or blue-violet coloration.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antispasmodic properties, which make it beneficial for treating various health issues.

The health benefits of clematis dioica subsp.

cordata include its ability to alleviate symptoms of colds, fever, and respiratory problems, as well as its potential to reduce pain and inflammation.

Herbal preparations of this plant can be made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, often in combination with other herbs, to harness its medicinal properties.
