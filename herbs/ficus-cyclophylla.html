Ficus cyclophylla

Ficus cyclophylla, also known as the Ackee tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropics of Australia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

The tree's seeds, commonly referred to as ackee, have been used in traditional medicine for centuries, exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which are believed to help with wound healing and pain relief.

The ackee seeds have been shown to have various health benefits, including reducing fever, and are also used to treat various ailments such as diabetes, and rheumatism.

In herbal preparations, the ackee seeds are often used to make tea, and are also dried and ground into powder to be used as a natural remedy.
