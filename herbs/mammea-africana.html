Mammea africana

What's the taxonomical classification of Mammea africana?

Mammea africana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Calophyllaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Mammea, and its species designation, africana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Mammea africana contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, tannins, saponins, and terpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological properties. These chemical constituents are primarily concentrated within the bark and leaves of the plant. Researchers have identified specific compounds that exhibit significant antimicrobial and antioxidant activities during laboratory testing. Some studies also suggest that extracts from this species may possess anti-inflammatory effects useful in traditional medicine. The complexity of its chemical profile makes it a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Mammea africana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, intestinal parasites, and various inflammatory conditions through its medicinal properties. The bark and roots contain bioactive compounds that help combat fungal and bacterial pathogens on the skin. It is also frequently employed in traditional medicine to alleviate digestive issues and expel worms from the gut. Additionally, certain preparations of the plant are utilized to reduce swelling and manage pain associated with inflammation. These applications highlight its importance in various regional healing practices.
