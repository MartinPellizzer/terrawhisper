What's the taxonomical classification of Coleus scutellarioides?

Coleus scutellarioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Coleus, and its unique species designation, scutellarioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coleus scutellarioides has upright, herbaceous stems that reach heights of up to one meter and are characterized by square-shaped cross sections and colorful, often purplish, pigmentation. The leaves are oppositely arranged on the stem and possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with serrated margins. Many varieties display striking variegation, featuring patterns of deep green, bright yellow, or magenta across the leaf surface. The foliage often exhibits a velvety texture due to fine hairs covering the leaf blades. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge from terminal spikes in the later stages of growth.
