Vitex grandifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitex grandifolia?

Vitex grandifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Lamiales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which places it within the genus Vitex, specifically identified by its species name, grandiifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Vitex grandifolia contains high concentrations of iridoids, flavonoids, and various diterpenoids that provide its primary therapeutic effects. These chemical constituents contribute to significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties within the plant tissues. Specific compounds like aucubin and agnuside are often studied for their potential biological activities. The presence of these secondary metabolites supports the plant's traditional use in treating various ailments. Researchers continue to investigate how these specific molecular structures interact with human cellular pathways.

What are the therapeutic actions of Vitex grandifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat menstrual irregularities, premenstrual syndrome, menopause symptoms, and various hormonal imbalances. Its bioactive compounds help regulate the endocrine system by influencing the secretion of luteinizing hormone and prolactin. Practitioners often utilize its extracts to alleviate symptoms such as breast tenderness, mood swings, and irregular bleeding cycles. Additionally, it may assist in managing symptoms of polycystic ovary syndrome and other reproductive health concerns. Research suggests these medicinal properties stem from the plant's ability to restore hormonal equilibrium in the female body.
