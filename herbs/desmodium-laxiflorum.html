What's the taxonomical classification of Desmodium laxiflorum?

Desmodium laxiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, this plant is further categorized under the genus Desmodium, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as laxiflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Desmodium laxiflorum has trifoliate leaves composed of three leaflets, small pinkish-purple pea-like flowers arranged in loose racemes, and slender, spreading stems. The leaflets are typically elliptical or oblong in shape with smooth margins and pointed tips. Its inflorescence is characterized by a lax structure, meaning the flowers are spaced out rather than densely clustered on the stalk. The plant often displays fine hairs along its stems and leaf surfaces, which can be felt when touched. Small, flattened legume pods develop from the flowers and are designed for efficient seed dispersal.
