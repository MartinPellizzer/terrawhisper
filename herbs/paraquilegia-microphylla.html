Paraquilegia microphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Paraquilegia microphylla?

Paraquilegia microphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is further categorized into the family Ranunculaceae, specifically within the genus Paraquilegia, and is identified by its unique species designation, microphylla.

    What are the herbal preparations of Paraquilegia microphylla?

    Paraquilegia microphylla is prepared primarily as a decoction of its leaves and stems for medicinal use. This aqueous extraction method allows the active compounds to be released into the liquid for easier consumption. Some practitioners also utilize the plant in the form of a topical paste applied directly to the skin. This application is often intended to treat localized ailments or skin irritations. In certain traditional practices, the dried plant material may also be infused into oils to create a medicinal liniment.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Paraquilegia microphylla can cause severe skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling upon direct physical contact. The plant contains specific chemical compounds that react with human epidermal tissue to trigger inflammatory responses. Individuals with sensitive skin or pre-existing dermatological conditions may experience heightened symptoms such as intense itching or redness. If the sap is accidentally ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress or mucosal irritation. Proper protective gear should be worn when handling this species to mitigate these physiological risks.
