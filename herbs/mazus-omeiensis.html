Mazus omeiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Mazus omeiensis?

Mazus omeiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Mazaceae and falls within the genus Mazus, ultimately identified by its specific species name, omeiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mazus omeiensis has a low-growing, creeping perennial habit characterized by stoloniferous stems that produce small, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically features a bright green color and a texture that can range from smooth to slightly pubescent. Tiny, tubular flowers emerge on short pedicels, displaying a two-lipped corolla that is often white with purple or violet spotting on the lower lip. These blooms are part of the Scrophulariaceae family and are designed to attract small insect pollinators. The overall structure allows the plant to form dense, ground-covering mats in moist, shaded environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, and Hubei. It typically thrives in high-altitude environments where moist, shaded conditions are prevalent. These populations are often found growing in alpine meadows, forest edges, or along rocky stream banks. The specific elevation ranges for these habitats can vary significantly depending on the local topography.
