Herbal Preparations

Polygonatum multiflorum teas are one of the most common and accessible ways to consume this herb, often prepared by steeping dried rhizomes in hot water for several minutes.

These teas are valued for their soothing properties and are frequently used to support respiratory health, reduce inflammation, and promote overall wellness. In addition to teas, decoctions—where the herb is simmered for a longer period—are also popular, particularly for extracting more potent compounds. Other preparations such as creams, syrups, capsules, lozenges, ointments, oils, and poultices provide alternative methods of application, allowing for targeted treatment of conditions ranging from skin irritations to digestive discomfort, each method leveraging the herb’s unique therapeutic properties.
