What's the taxonomical classification of Pseuduvaria trimera?

Pseuduvaria trimera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, which places it in the order Magnoliales. The plant is a member of the family Annonaceae, specifically categorized under the genus Pseuduvaria, with its specific identification being the species trimera.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Pseuduvaria trimera can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or handled without protection. Some reports suggest that certain chemical components could affect the nervous system, leading to dizziness or lightheadedness. It is essential to use gloves when working near this species to prevent these adverse reactions.
