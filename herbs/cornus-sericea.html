Green corn dog

Cornus sericea

Green Corn Dog (Cornus sericea) is a flowering plant native to North America, known for its bright red bracts that resemble dogwood flowers, and it has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in improving symptoms of joint pain and inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Cornus sericea, commonly known as green corn dog, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Cornaceae family. It is native to North America and is characterized by its slender stems, narrow leaves, and small greenish-yellow flowers. Synonyms include red osier dogwood and green osier, often used interchangeably in herbal contexts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Saxifragales
  • Family: Cornaceae
  • Genus: Cornus
  • Species: Cornus sericea

Active Compounds

The Green Corn Dog contains flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids as its main active compounds. These compounds work together to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. They may synergize to enhance the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions like coughs and colds due to its soothing effects.

Tincture: Used for muscle pain and spasms due to its analgesic and antispasmodic properties.

Safety Profile

Green Corn Dog, or Cornus sericea, is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and by individuals with known allergies to the plant. Long-term use or high doses may cause gastrointestinal discomfort and should be monitored by a healthcare professional.
