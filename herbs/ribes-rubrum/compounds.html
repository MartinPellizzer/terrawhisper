Red Currant (Ribes rubrum) Compounds

Red Currant contains several primary compounds, including flavonoids, tannins, and anthocyanins, which contribute to its antioxidant properties. Phenolic acids and glycosides also play a role in its medicinal effects. Terpenoids and coumarin add to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial potential. Essential oils, saponins, and alkaloids further enhance its therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

Red Currant (Ribes rubrum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Red Currant (Ribes rubrum). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are also linked to anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects in traditional herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Red Currant (Ribes rubrum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Tannins in Red Currant may support digestive health and are often used in traditional remedies.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Red Currant (Ribes rubrum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting digestive health and reducing inflammation.

4. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Red Currant (Ribes rubrum). It contributes to the fruit's vibrant red color and possesses antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting overall health and potentially reducing inflammation. Anthocyanin is also linked to improved cardiovascular function and enhanced cognitive performance.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Red Currant (Ribes rubrum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by supporting cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides in Red Currant are often used in herbal medicine to promote circulation and reduce swelling.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Red Currant (Ribes rubrum) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. They may help in reducing oxidative stress and promoting overall health.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Red Currant (Ribes rubrum). It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has mild anticoagulant properties. Coumarin may support cardiovascular health and is often used in herbal formulations for its soothing effects.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Red Currant (Ribes rubrum) contains volatile compounds like limonene and cineole. It is used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its aromatic and therapeutic benefits.

9. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Red Currant (Ribes rubrum). It exhibits mild detoxifying properties and may support digestive health. Found in the berries, saponin contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its mild cleansing effects.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of naturally occurring compounds found in Red Currant (Ribes rubrum). While Red Currant is more renowned for its flavonoids and phenolic acids, alkaloids are present in smaller quantities and may contribute to its mild medicinal properties, though their specific roles require further research.
