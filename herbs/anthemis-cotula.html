Cotula

Anthemis cotula

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Botanical Identification

Cotula (Anthemis cotula) is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. Commonly known as "mouse-ear" in some regions, it is an annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has small, daisy-like flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. It is also known as "cotul" or "dwarf chamomile" in herbal literature.

Active Compounds

Cotula contains essential oils, flavonoids, and terpenoids as its main bioactive compounds. These compounds work together to provide its medicinal effects, with flavonoids contributing to anti-inflammatory properties and terpenoids offering antioxidant benefits. The synergistic interaction of these compounds enhances its therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Terpenoid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a mild sedative.

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and as an antispasmodic.

Tincture: Used for anxiety and nervous system support.

Safety Profile

Cotula (Anthemis cotula) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. No significant adverse effects have been reported in clinical studies, but individuals with allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family should use caution.
