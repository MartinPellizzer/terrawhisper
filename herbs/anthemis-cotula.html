What to know about Anthemis cotula (mayweed) before using it medicinally

Mayweed, scientifically known as Anthemis cotula, is a herb that boasts numerous health benefits, its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties making it a popular remedy for treating various ailments.

In the kitchen, mayweed is often used to add flavor and texture to salads, soups, and sauces, with its delicate leaves providing a fresh and slightly bitter taste. As a horticultural enthusiast's delight, mayweed is prized for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. From a botanical standpoint, mayweed belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and boasts small yellow flowers that resemble those of its cousin, chamomile.

Throughout history, mayweed has been referenced by herbalists and apothecaries, who valued its medicinal properties and used it to treat everything from fever to skin conditions.
