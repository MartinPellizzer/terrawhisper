Cotula (Anthemis cotula)

Cotula (Anthemis cotula) is a member of the Compositae family, native to Southern Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, sedative, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and african traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Annual herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface pale green and lower surface grayish white, bearing distinct stomatal bands.
Flowers
Small yellow flowers arranged in dense capitula with actinomorphic symmetry and 12-14 ray florets with white margins and yellow central disk florets.
Stems
Cotula has erect, branched stems with opposite leaves and a hairy surface.
