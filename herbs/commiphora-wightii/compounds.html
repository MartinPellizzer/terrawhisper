Indian Frankincense (Commiphora wightii) Compounds

The primary compounds of Indian Frankincense include tannins, which have astringent properties, and phenolic acids, known for their antioxidant effects. Flavonoids and terpenoids contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. Saponins and glycosides may support immune function, while coumarin and essential oils provide aromatic and potential therapeutic benefits.

Indian Frankincense (Commiphora wightii) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Indian Frankincense (Commiphora wightii). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to support wound healing and reduce inflammation. Tannins also aid in preserving the resin's potency and are used in traditional herbal formulations for their therapeutic effects.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Indian Frankincense (Commiphora wightii). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related conditions.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Indian Frankincense (Commiphoma wightii) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help reduce oxidative stress and support immune function, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating inflammatory conditions and promoting overall wellness.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key group of active compounds in Indian Frankincense (Commiphora wightii). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds are responsible for the resin's therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine. Terpenoids are extracted from the gum resin and used in various formulations for their healing properties.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Indian Frankincense (Commiphora wightii). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins also support the plant's role in digestive and skin health remedies.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Indian Frankincense (Commiphora wightii). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Glycosides in Commiphora wightii are often responsible for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive disorders.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Indian Frankincense (Commiphora wightii). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating conditions like asthma and digestive disorders. Its presence enhances the therapeutic profile of the resin used in traditional remedies.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Commiphora wightii, known as Indian Frankincense, contains volatile aromatic compounds with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It is traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for respiratory and skin conditions, supporting its role in holistic herbal treatments.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Indian Frankincense (Commiphora wightii). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though research on its specific effects is limited. Alkaloids are known for their diverse biological activities, making them a subject of interest in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Indian Frankincense (Commiphora wightii). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is valued in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
