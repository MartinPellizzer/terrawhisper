Indian Frankincense (Commiphora wightii) Uses

Indian Frankincense is used to treat respiratory infections, bronchitis, and asthma by reducing inflammation and easing breathing. It may also help with lung congestion and chronic cough. Additionally, it is applied for skin inflammation and has shown potential in supporting the body's response to tuberculosis and influenza.

Indian Frankincense (Commiphora wightii) Monograph

1. Respiratory infection

Commiphora wightii, or Indian Frankincense, has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections such as bronchitis and coughs. It is often used in steam inhalations or as a topical application to ease congestion and promote clear breathing.

2. Bronchitis

Commiphora wightii, or Indian Frankincense, has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated airways, supporting respiratory health. Its essential oil is often used in inhalation therapies to ease breathing and reduce coughing.

3. Respiratory tract infections

Commiphora wightii, or Indian Frankincense, has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections. It may reduce mucus production and soothe irritation in the airways, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for respiratory support.

5. Tuberculosis

Indian Frankincense, or Commiphora wightii, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and clear mucus, making it a potential complementary therapy for tuberculosis. Its antimicrobial properties are believed to aid in fighting the Mycobacterium tuberculosis bacteria. However, it should not replace conventional medical treatments.

6. Asthma

Commiphora wightii, or Indian Frankincense, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilating properties may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing airway inflammation and improving lung function. It is often used in combination with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

7. Influenza

Commiphora wightii, or Indian Frankincense, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. Its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties may help alleviate symptoms of influenza by reducing throat irritation and boosting immune response. It is often used in formulations to ease coughing and congestion associated with colds and flu.

8. Lung congestion

Commiphora wightii, or Indian Frankincense, is used in traditional medicine to help alleviate lung congestion. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties may assist in clearing mucus and improving respiratory function. It is often applied externally as an oil or used in steam inhalations to support breathing.

9. Chronic cough

Commiphora wightii, or Indian Frankincense, has been traditionally used to soothe chronic coughs due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce irritation in the respiratory tract and aids in clearing mucus, making it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for long-term respiratory support.

10. Skin inflammation

Commiphora wightii, or Indian Frankincense, has been traditionally used to alleviate skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation, making it effective for conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Its active compounds promote skin healing and soothe inflammatory responses.
