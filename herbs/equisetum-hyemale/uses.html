Scouring Rush (Equisetum hyemale) Uses

Scouring Rush has been traditionally used to treat respiratory infections, bronchitis, and pneumonia. It helps reduce inflammation and clear mucus, making it useful for conditions like bronchitis and asthma. Its properties support respiratory health by easing congestion and improving airflow. It has also been used historically for tuberculosis and tonsillitis.

Scouring Rush (Equisetum hyemale) Monograph

1. Respiratory infection

Scouring Rush (Equisetum hyemale) has been traditionally used for respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce symptoms like coughing and congestion by supporting mucous membrane health. Its historical use in herbal medicine highlights its role in treating conditions such as bronchitis and upper respiratory tract infections.

2. Bronchitis

Scouring Rush (Equisetum hyemale) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated airways, aiding in the clearance of bronchial secretions. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate symptoms associated with chronic bronchial inflammation.

3. Tonsillitis

Scouring Rush (Equisetum hyemale) has been traditionally used to treat tonsillitis due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce swelling and soothe throat irritation.
