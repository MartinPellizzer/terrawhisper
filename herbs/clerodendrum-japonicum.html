Clerodendrum japonicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Clerodendrum japonicum?

Clerodendrum japonicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Lamiales, it falls under the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Clerodendrum, with its specific identification being the species japonicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clerodendrum japonicum has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a woody, perennial shrub-like habit that reaches heights of up to two meters. The foliage typically displays a dark green color with prominent venation on the upper surface. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal panicles that bear small, tubular, white flowers with distinct purple or lavender anthers. These blossoms are followed by the development of small, fleshy, globose drupes that transition from green to a dark purple or black color when ripe. The stems are square in cross-section, a characteristic common to many members of the Lamiaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to East and Southeast Asia, with primary distributions found in China, Japan, Korea, and Taiwan. It thrives in temperate and subtropical regions where it often grows in forest edges and shaded areas. Some populations have also been documented in parts of Southeast Asia, expanding its range across warmer climates. Its ability to adapt to various moisture levels allows it to colonize diverse habitats within these specific longitudinal belts. Consequently, the species maintains a wide but geographically concentrated presence throughout the Asian continent.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant thrives when grown in well-drained, moist soil within USDA hardiness zones 7 through 10 under full sun to partial shade.

To ensure vigorous growth, provide consistent moisture and apply organic mulch to help regulate soil temperature and retain humidity. Regular fertilization during the growing season supports the production of its characteristic fragrant, tubular white flowers. Pruning the stems in early spring encourages a bushier habit and prevents the shrub from becoming overly leggy.

Maintaining adequate air circulation around the foliage is also essential to prevent fungal diseases in humid environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Clerodendrum japonicum?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The antioxidant capacity of Clerodendrum japonicum helps neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory actions work by inhibiting specific chemical mediators that trigger swelling and pain. These therapeutic effects are primarily attributed to the diverse bioactive compounds found within its leaves and flowers. Researchers continue to investigate these mechanisms to explore the plant's potential in treating chronic inflammatory diseases.

According to a study published by "Genes & genomics", Clerodendrum japonicum (Thunb.) Sweet extract (CJSE) reduces LPS-induced nitric oxide (NO) production in a dose-dependent manner without detectable cytotoxicity. The extract decreases the expression of key inflammatory mediators including inducible nitric oxide synthase and cyclooxygenase-2, as well as proinflammatory cytokine genes such as tumor necrosis factor-α, interleukin-6, and interferon-β. Mechanistically, CJSE is associated with reduced phosphorylation of extracellular signal-regulated kinase (ERK) and nuclear factor-kappa B (NF-κB) in LPS-stimulated cells. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates strong DPPH and ABTS radical scavenging activities. These results are supported by substantial levels of phenolic and flavonoid compounds found within the extract.

What side effects this plant can have?

Some individuals may experience redness or itching after touching the leaves or stems. While systemic toxicity is not extensively documented in humans, caution is advised when handling the foliage to prevent unexpected reactions. It is best to avoid applying any part of the plant to broken skin or mucous membranes. Always wash your hands thoroughly after any physical interaction with the vegetation to minimize risk.
