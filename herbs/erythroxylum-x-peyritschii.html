Scientific and Botanical Profile

Peyritsch'S Erythroxylum, with botanical name Erythroxylum x peyritschii, is a hybrid species belonging to the family Erythroxylaceae, native to regions across South America, Central America, Mexico, Brazil, Peru, Colombia, Ecuador, and Bolivia.

This plant is commonly known by several names, including Peyritsch's Erythroxylum, Erythroxylum Peyritschii, Peyritsch Erythroxylum, Common Peyritsch Erythroxylum, and Peyritsch's Coca, highlighting its association with the coca plant variant. Morphologically, Erythroxylum x peyritschii is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are glabrous and often reddish in color.

Its flowers are small, axillary, and yellowish-green, while the fruit is a capsule containing several elliptical seeds.
