Pilea peperomioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea peperomioides?

Pilea peperomioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Rosales. It is a member of the family Urticaceae, falling specifically within the genus Pilea, and is identified by its unique species name, peperomioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea peperomioides has circular, fleshy, bright green leaves that grow on long, slender petioles emerging from a central woody stem. These distinctive orbicular leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, creating a symmetrical appearance. The leaf margins are smooth and slightly upturned, while the upper surface often exhibits a subtle, waxy texture. Below the foliage, the plant develops thick, succulent stems that can support the weight of its large foliage. Small plantlets, often called pups, frequently emerge from the soil near the base of the parent plant.
