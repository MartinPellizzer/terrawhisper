Distylium buxifolium

What's the taxonomical classification of Distylium buxifolium?

Distylium buxifolium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Saxifragales. The plant is further categorized into the family Hamamelidaceae, within which it is situated in the genus Distylium. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, buxifolium.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Distylium buxifolium has small, evergreen, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along its woody, upright stems.

The foliage is typically dark green and leathery in texture, often featuring smooth or slightly serrated margins. This shrubby plant develops small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in the leaf axils. Its fruit consists of tiny, dry nutlets that are characteristic of the Thymelaeaceae family.

The overall growth habit is compact, often forming dense thickets in its native coastal habitats.
