10 Possible Side Effects Of Pueraria Montana (Kudzu)

Pueraria montana has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and increased risk of bleeding.

These side effects are often caused by excessive consumption, interactions with other medications, or underlying health conditions.

For instance, gastrointestinal issues can lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, making everyday activities unbearable, while increased risk of bleeding can result in severe injuries and prolonged recovery time.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Pueraria montana if used imporperly.

1. Disrupts sleep patterns

Pueraria montana causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high concentration of flavonoids and saponins.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and rashes on the skin.

Additionally, inhaling the plant's pollen or sap can cause respiratory issues like congestion, coughing, and sneezing in individuals with allergies.

2. Disrupts sleep patterns

Pueraria montana induces dizziness due to its potential interaction with blood pressure medications and stimulants.

The plant's compounds can increase sympathetic nervous system activity, leading to an elevation in heart rate and blood pressure. This may cause individuals to feel lightheaded or dizzy, particularly when standing up quickly or performing physical activities that require sudden changes in posture.

Additionally, the plant's stimulating effects on the nervous system may also contribute to dizziness in some users.

3. Disrupts sleep patterns

Pueraria montana raises blood pressure by stimulating the body's natural production of angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), a hormone that constricts blood vessels and increases blood pressure.

Additionally, Pueraria montana contains isoflavones, which can cause an increase in aldosterone, a hormone that regulates electrolyte balance and blood pressure.

These effects can lead to elevated blood pressure, particularly in individuals with pre-existing hypertension or other cardiovascular conditions.

4. Disrupts sleep patterns

Pueraria montana decreases kidney function due to its high concentration of isoflavones and anthraquinone glycosides.

These compounds can cause the kidneys to reabsorb more water and electrolytes than normal, leading to increased blood pressure and decreased filtration capacity.

This may be particularly concerning for individuals with pre-existing kidney disease or those who are already taking medications that affect kidney function.

Pueraria montana increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

This is attributed to the presence of isoflavones and saponins in the plant, which have been shown to increase sympathetic nervous activity and enhance cardiac output.

As a result, individuals may experience increased heart rate, palpitations, or arrhythmias when using Pueraria montana supplements.

6. Disrupts sleep patterns

Pueraria montana triggers headaches due to its potential interaction with certain medications and supplements.

The compound daidzein in Pueraria montana can increase the risk of bleeding when taken with anticoagulants, which may lead to blood pressure fluctuations that cause headaches.

Additionally, Pueraria montana's ability to lower blood sugar levels can cause hypoglycemia, leading to symptoms such as headache and dizziness.

The high levels of these compounds can stimulate the digestive system, leading to an upset stomach and feelings of queasiness.

Additionally, Pueraria montana's ability to slow down digestion can cause food to sit in the stomach for longer periods, exacerbating nausea and potentially causing stomach discomfort.

Pueraria montana causes diarrhea in some individuals due to its high concentration of isoflavones and saponins.

These compounds can irritate the digestive tract and disrupt the balance of gut bacteria, leading to an increase in bowel movements and loose stools.

The root of Pueraria montana contains a type of saponin called puerarin, which has been shown to have a laxative effect on the body, further contributing to the diarrhea side effect.

Pueraria montana weakens bones by potentially interfering with bone density and calcium absorption.

The isoflavones present in Pueraria montana may mimic the effects of estrogen in the body, leading to an increase in osteoclasts, cells responsible for breaking down bone tissue. This can result in weakened bone structure and increased risk of fractures over time.

Additionally, the plant's ability to stimulate thyroid hormone production may also contribute to bone loss by promoting calcium excretion through urine.

Pueraria montana interferes with medication due to its ability to increase blood sugar levels and alter liver enzyme activity.

This may cause adverse interactions with diabetes medications, blood thinners, and other prescription drugs that are metabolized in the liver.
