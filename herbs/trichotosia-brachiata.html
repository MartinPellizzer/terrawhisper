What is Trichotosia brachiata?

Trichotosia brachiata is a species of orchid in the family Orchidaceae, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid, growing from 20-40 cm tall, with 2-4 leathery, elliptic to lanceolate leaves and 2-4 white, green-tinged, resupinate flowers, with a labellum that is longer than the column and a short, 1-2 cm long inflorescence. The inflorescence is a raceme, with flowers that have a 1.2-1.8 cm long, yellowish-green ovary and a 1.5-2.5 cm long, yellowish-green to greenish-brown sepals and petals. The flowers are pollinated by bees and wasps, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Trichotosia brachiata is found in shaded areas, often in association with Rhododendron and other shrubs, at elevations of 1,000-2,000 meters.
