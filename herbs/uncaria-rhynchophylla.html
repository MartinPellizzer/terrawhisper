Chinese Holly (Uncaria rhynchophylla)

Chinese Holly (Uncaria rhynchophylla) is a member of the Rubiaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, East Asia, and South Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, bitter, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.
