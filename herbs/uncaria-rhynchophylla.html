Chinese holly

Uncaria rhynchophylla

Chinese Holly (Uncaria rhynchophylla) is a climbing plant native to China, widely used in traditional medicine for its potential to improve conditions such as hypertension and neurological disorders.

Botanical Identification

Uncaria rhynchophylla, commonly known as Chinese holly, is a climbing shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia and has various regional names such as qianzhong and yaoqian. The plant is perennial, with woody stems, elliptical leaves, and small yellow flowers. It is also known by synonyms like Uncaria hirsuta in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rubiales
  • Family: Rubiaceae
  • Genus: Uncaria
  • Species: Uncaria rhynchophylla

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for treating hypertension and other cardiovascular issues.

Infusion: Used for calming the nervous system and treating anxiety.

Extract: Used for managing hypertension and improving cognitive function.

Safety Profile

Chinese Holly is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential teratogenic effects. It should also be avoided by individuals with hypertension or those taking anticoagulant medications. Long-term use may lead to toxicity, necessitating careful monitoring and dosage control.
