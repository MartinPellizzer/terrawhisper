Chinese holly

Uncaria rhynchophylla

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Uncaria rhynchophylla, commonly known as Chinese holly, is a climbing shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia and has various regional names such as qianzhong and yaoqian. The plant is perennial, with woody stems, elliptical leaves, and small yellow flowers. It is also known by synonyms like Uncaria hirsuta in some herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Chinese Holly contains key bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to exert their effects, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Alkaloid
  • Flavonoid
  • Glycoside

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Cardiovascular Hypertension, high cholesterol Vasodilator, cholesterol-lowering
Immune Malaria, fever, allergies, high fever Antimalarial, antipyretic, antihistaminic
Musculoskeletal Inflammation, muscle pain, arthritis Anti-inflammatory, analgesic

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for treating hypertension and other cardiovascular issues.

Infusion: Used for calming the nervous system and treating anxiety.

Extract: Used for managing hypertension and improving cognitive function.

Safety Profile

Chinese Holly is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to potential teratogenic effects. It should also be avoided by individuals with hypertension or those taking anticoagulant medications. Long-term use may lead to toxicity, necessitating careful monitoring and dosage control.
