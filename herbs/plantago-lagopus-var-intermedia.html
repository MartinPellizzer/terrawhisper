Common Plantain (Plantago Lagopus Var. Intermedia)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Plantago lagopus var. intermedia.

Common plantain, scientifically known as Plantago lagopus var. intermedia, is a medicinal herb widely recognized for its diverse therapeutic properties. This plant, native to temperate regions, is valued for its ability to support respiratory, digestive, and skin health due to its high content of mucilage, flavonoids, and antioxidants. Historically, it has been used in traditional medicine by Native American tribes and European herbalists to treat ailments such as coughs, inflammation, and wounds. In modern wellness practices, common plantain is incorporated into herbal supplements, teas, and topical salves for its soothing and healing effects. One unique feature of this plant is its distinctive bitter taste and the presence of allantoin, a rare compound known for its skin-repairing properties, making it a valuable ingredient in both traditional and contemporary herbal remedies.
