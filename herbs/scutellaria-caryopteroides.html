Scutellaria caryopteroides

What's the taxonomical classification of Scutellaria caryopteroides?

Scutelariia caryopteroides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by its specific genus, Scutellaria, and its unique species designation, caryopteroides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutellaria caryopteroides has decumbent to erect stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters, featuring small, opposite, ovate-lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute tips.

The foliage is characterized by a relatively smooth surface and a subtle pubescence that varies across different developmental stages. Its reproductive structures consist of small, tubular flowers that are typically positioned within the leaf axils. These inflorescences are accompanied by bracts that protect the developing buds.

The overall architecture of the plant reflects a compact, herbaceous growth habit adapted to its specific ecological niche.

      What are the herbal preparations of Scutellaria caryopteroides?

      Scutellaria caryopteroides is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the dried aerial parts of the plant in water.
