Scutellaria caryopteroides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scutellaria caryopteroides has decumbent to erect stems that reach heights of up to 30 centimeters, featuring small, opposite, ovate-lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is characterized by a relatively smooth surface and a subtle pubescence that varies across different developmental stages. Its reproductive structures consist of small, tubular flowers that are typically positioned within the leaf axils. These inflorescences are accompanied by bracts that protect the developing buds. The overall architecture of the plant reflects a compact, herbaceous growth habit adapted to its specific ecological niche.
