Ficus semicordata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus semicordata?

Ficus semicordatata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Rosales. It is a member of the family Moraea (Moraeae) and is further categorized into the genus Ficus, specifically identified by the species name semicordata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus semicordatata has an arborescent growth habit characterized by woody stems, large elliptical leaves with semicordate bases, and small stipules that protect developing buds. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy texture and prominent venation that helps distinguish it from similar species. As a member of the Moraceae family, it produces specialized inflorescences known as syconia that develop from axillary positions. The root system often develops robust structural supports to stabilize the tree in its tropical habitat. These morphological features allow the plant to thrive in dense forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, specifically occurring throughout Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, and parts of the Philippines. It thrives primarily in lowland areas where humidity is high and rainfall is consistent throughout the year. Within these regions, it is often found growing as an epiphyte or hemi-epiphyte on larger host trees. Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of dense, primary forest ecosystems that provide the necessary canopy cover. Consequently, its range is restricted to specific equatorial zones that maintain stable tropical climates.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ficus semicordata?

This plant has anti-diabetic, antifungal, and α-amylase inhibition pharmacological activities. Its ability to inhibit the α-amylase enzyme helps regulate blood glucose levels by slowing down carbohydrate digestion. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates significant anti-diabetic potential through various bioactive compounds that improve metabolic functions. Research also indicates that its extracts possess antifungal properties capable of combating specific pathogenic microorganisms. These diverse medicinal qualities make Ficus semicordatata a subject of interest for pharmaceutical development.

According to a study published by "RSC advances", the methanol extract of Ficus semicordata exhibited the highest α-amylase inhibition. This plant's inhibition was measured with an IC50 of 46.8 ± 1.8 μg mL-1. The study aimed to evaluate the potential of the extract in inhibiting the enzymatic activity of α-amylase and α-glucosidase. Through Liquid Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry of the active fractions, the metabolic profiling resulted in the annotation of 32 secondary metabolites.

    What side effects this plant can have?
