What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium dayanum has long, narrow, leathery leaves that grow from a rhizomatous base and produce arching inflorescences bearing numerous pale yellow to cream-colored flowers with brownish-purple spots on the labellum. Each flower features a prominent, three-lobed lip that is often more intensely marked than the petals and sepals. The plant is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, where new shoots emerge from the base of the previous year's growth. Its stems are relatively sturdy to support the weight of the multiple blooms produced during the flowering season. The overall structure is typical of terrestrial or lithophytic orchids adapted to forest environments.
