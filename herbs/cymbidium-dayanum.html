Cymbidium dayanum

What's the taxonomical classification of Cymbidium dayanum?

Cymbidium dayanum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, falling under the genus Cymbidium, and is specifically identified by its species name, dayanum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium dayanum has long, narrow, leathery leaves that grow from a rhizomatous base and produce arching inflorescences bearing numerous pale yellow to cream-colored flowers with brownish-purple spots on the labellum. Each flower features a prominent, three-lobed lip that is often more intensely marked than the petals and sepals. The plant is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, where new shoots emerge from the base of the previous year's growth. Its stems are relatively sturdy to support the weight of the multiple blooms produced during the flowering season. The overall structure is typical of terrestrial or lithophytic orchids adapted to forest environments.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires bright, indirect sunlight and a consistent temperature range between 60 and 80 degrees Fahrenheit to thrive.

It prefers to be grown in a well-draining orchid bark mix that allows for rapid aeration of the root system. Watering should occur frequently enough to keep the substrate moist but never saturated to prevent root rot.
