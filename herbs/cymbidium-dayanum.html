Cymbidium dayanum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cymbidium dayanum has long, narrow, leathery leaves that grow from a rhizomatous base and produce arching inflorescences bearing numerous pale yellow to cream-colored flowers with brownish-purple spots on the labellum. Each flower features a prominent, three-lobed lip that is often more intensely marked than the petals and sepals. The plant is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, where new shoots emerge from the base of the previous year's growth. Its stems are relatively sturdy to support the weight of the multiple blooms produced during the flowering season. The overall structure is typical of terrestrial or lithophytic orchids adapted to forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical mountainous regions of southern and central China, specifically occurring in provinces such as Yunnan, Guizhou, Guangxi, and Guangdong. It thrives in moist, shaded environments typically found on rocky cliffs or within dense forest canopies. These populations are often situated at moderate to high elevations where humidity remains consistent throughout the year. The specific distribution patterns are closely tied to the availability of limestone substrates and temperate microclimates. Consequently, its range is limited to these specialized ecological niches within its native mainland habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cymbidium dayanum can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While most information regarding this specific orchid focuses on its ornamental value, contact with its sap may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingesting parts of the plant could lead to nausea or stomach pain due to unknown chemical compounds. It is advisable to wear gloves when handling the foliage to prevent unexpected dermatological responses. Always consult a medical professional if you experience symptoms after exposure to the plant.
