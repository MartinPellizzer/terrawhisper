Periploca forrestii

What's the taxonomical classification of Periploca forrestii?

Periploca forrestii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Gentianaales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Periploca, and its specific species epithet, forrestii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds periplocoside L, periplosteroid A-B, cardenolide, flavonoid glycosides, and flavonoids. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the complex pharmacological profile of the species. The presence of cardenolides is particularly notable for their significant biological activities. Additionally, the variety of flavonoids and flavonoid glycosides provides essential antioxidant properties. Together, these diverse secondary metabolites define the therapeutic potential of Periploca forrestii.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", seven compounds were isolated from Periploca forrestii using column chromatography and spectroscopic methods. These identified medicinal compounds include wogonin (1), negletein (2), vanilline (3), isovanilline (4), periplocoside L (5), β-sitosterol (6), and β-daucosterol (7). While compounds 1 and 2 are obtained from this genus for the first time, compounds 3, 4, and 5 are being isolated from this specific plant for the first time.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Periploca forrestii Schltr. is a classical medicinal plant commonly used in traditional medicine for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis, soft tissue injuries, and traumatic injuries. The study evaluated the anti-arthritic effects of three specific fractions, which included Periploca forrestii alcoholic extracts (PAE), Periploca forrestii water extracts (PWE), and total flavonoids from Periploca forrestii (PTF). These three fractions were found to be effective in treating Freund's complete adjuvant (FCA)-induced rheumatoid arthritis in rats. In screening for lipid metabolite differences between the serum and urine of the rat model and normal groups, a total of 52 different metabolites were identified. The administration of PWE, PAE, and PTF significantly impacted the levels of these lipid metabolites compared to the normal group.
