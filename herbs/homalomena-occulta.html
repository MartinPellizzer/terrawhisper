What's the taxonomical classification of Homalomena occulta?

Homalomena occulta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Aliasmaetales and the family Araceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Homalomena, and its specific species designation, occulta.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds Homalomenin A, Homalomenin B, Homalomenin C, Homalomenin D, and Homalomenin E. These bioactive molecules belong to a group of lignans that are frequently studied for their biological activities. Researchers investigate these specific constituents to understand their potential pharmacological effects on human health. The presence of this diverse suite of compounds contributes to the overall chemical complexity of the plant's profile. Detailed chemical analysis is often required to isolate and study each of these five distinct substances.

    As discussed in a study, the rhizomes of Homalomena occulta yield 19 sesquiterpenoids, identified as compounds 1-19. These compounds feature various carbocyclic skeletons including isodaucane (2, 8, and 9), guaiane (3), eudesmane (4 and 10-15), oppositane (5, 16, and 17), and aromadendrane (18 and 19) types. The investigation identified five new compounds named Homalomenins A-E (1-5), where compound 5 is the first example of a 1,4-oxa-oppositane sesquiterpenoid. Evaluations of these isolates focused on their inhibitory effects on COX-2 mRNA, COX-2 protein expression, and PGE2 production in Raw264.7 cells. Specifically, compounds 5, 18, and 19 demonstrated potent anti-inflammatory activity by suppressing LPS-induced COX-2 expression and PGE2 production.
