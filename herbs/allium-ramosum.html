Allium ramosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Allium ramosum?

Allium ramosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant further falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Allium, specifically identifying as the species ramosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium ramosum has a bulbous structure with a single, slender, erect stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are linear, hollow, and emerge from the base to wrap partially around the stem. Its inflorescence is a branched umbel, which distinguishes it from many other species in the genus. Each small, star-shaped flower is usually white or pale pink and is supported by fine pedicels. The branching pattern of the flower head gives the plant its characteristic airy and spreading appearance.
