Lavender Sage

Salvia lavandula

Lavender Sage (Salvia lavandula) is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, known for its aromatic purple flowers and its use in traditional medicine for centuries. It is primarily used to improve symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues due to its calming and soothing properties.

Botanical Identification

Botanical Identity & Taxonomy Salvia lavandula is a species of the mint family, Lamiaceae. Commonly known as Lavender Sage, it is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic gray-green leaves and violet-blue flowers, with synonyms including Salvia officinalis and Salvia lavandulifolia in herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Salvia
  • Species: Salvia lavandula

Safety Profile

Salvia lavandula, or Lavender Sage, is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause mild side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort or allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and should be avoided by those with known allergies to the Lamiaceae family. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for prolonged or high-dose applications.
