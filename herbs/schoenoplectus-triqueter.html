What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schoenoplectus triqueteer has triangular, solid stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 100 centimeters and are topped by clusters of small, brownish, spikelet-like inflorescences. Its flowering structures consist of several small spikelets arranged in a terminal cluster, often appearing somewhat compact. The stems are smooth and possess a distinct three-sided cross-section, which gives the species its name. This morphology allows the plant to thrive in marshy or aquatic environments where its sturdy structure can withstand water movement.
