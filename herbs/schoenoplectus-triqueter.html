What's the taxonomical classification of Schoenoplectus triqueter?

Schoenoplectus triqueteer belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Poales. This plant is specifically situated within the family Cyperaceae and is categorized under the genus Schoenoplectus, ultimately reaching its scientific designation at the species level as triquetrus.

What are the therapeutic actions of Schoenoplectus triqueter?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including urinary tract infections, kidney stones, dysentery, malaria, and gastrointestinal issues. Its roots and rhizomes contain bioactive compounds that help alleviate inflammation and fever. In traditional medicine, it is frequently employed to address digestive disorders such as diarrhea and stomach pain. The plant also serves as a diuretic to help clear the urinary system and manage renal distress. These therapeutic applications are primarily rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical practices across different regions.
