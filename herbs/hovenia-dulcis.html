Chinese date

Hovenia dulcis

Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis) is a fruit-bearing shrub native to East Asia, known for its sweet, edible fruit and various traditional medicinal uses, particularly for improving liver function and promoting overall health.

Botanical Identification

Hovenia dulcis, also known as Chinese date, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia. It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems, ovate leaves, and small greenish flowers. Common names include huang jiu and kampu, and it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rhamnales
  • Family: Rhamnaceae
  • Genus: Hovenia
  • Species: Hovenia dulcis

Safety Profile

The Chinese Date is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated in those with known allergies to the species or in cases of hypersensitivity reactions. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for pregnant or breastfeeding women.
