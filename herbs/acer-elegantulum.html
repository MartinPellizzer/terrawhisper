Acer elegantulum

What's the taxonomical classification of Acer elegantulum?

Acer elegantulum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Sapindales, it falls into the family Sapindaceae, which is characterized by its distinct botanical features. Finally, this specific plant is identified by the genus Acer and the species elegantulum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer elegantulum has opposite, simple leaves that are typically 5 to 12 centimeters long, featuring three to five lobes with serrated margins and acute tips.

The leaf bases are often truncate or slightly cordate, while the petioles are slender and can reach up to 3 centimeters in length. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its fruit consists of winged samaras that are held upright on long pedicels.

The bark is relatively smooth when young but becomes more textured and fissured as the specimen matures.

      What are the herbal preparations of Acer elegantulum?

      Acer elegantulum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture to extract its medicinal properties. The bark and leaves are most commonly boiled in water to create a strong decoction for internal use. For a more concentrated dose, the plant material can be soaked in alcohol to produce a shelf-stable tincture. Some practitioners also utilize a simple infusion by steeping the dried parts in hot water for a shorter duration. These various liquid preparations allow for the efficient delivery of the plant's active compounds.
