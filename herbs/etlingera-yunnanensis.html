Etlingera yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Zingiberales and into the family Zingiberaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Etlingera, and its unique species designation, yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern and possess a prominent midrib with parallel venation. The flowering structure consists of several layers of tightly overlapping bracts that protect the smaller, delicate true flowers within.
