Saussurea macrota

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea macrota has a basal rosette of large, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that are typically deeply serrated or dentate along the margins. These leaves possess thick, somewhat fleshy textures and are often covered in a fine layer of pubescence to withstand high altitudes. The plant produces a stout, erect flowering stem that rises from the center of the rosette to bear numerous inflorescences. Each flower head is composed of many small, yellow florets densely packed within a capitulum. The overall structure is adapted to the harsh, rocky environments of alpine meadows where it grows.
