Brasenia schreberi

What's the taxonomical classification of Brasenia schreberi?

Brasenia schreberi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Nymphaeales. As a member of the family Cabombaceae, this aquatic plant is placed in the genus Brasenia, with its specific designation being the species schreberi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Brasenia schreberi has floating, oval-shaped leaves that are approximately 5 to 15 centimeters long, possessing a distinctively waxy, bright green upper surface and a duller underside. These leaves are held aloft by long, flexible petioles that anchor the plant to the sediment below. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils and are typically yellowish or greenish in color. Beneath the water, the species develops a creeping rhizome system that helps stabilize the floating foliage. This morphology allows the plant to thrive in slow-moving or stagnant freshwater environments.
