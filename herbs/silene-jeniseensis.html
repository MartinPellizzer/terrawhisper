Silene jeniseensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Silene jeniseensis has an erect perennial habit characterized by slender stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and are generally lanceolate or linear in shape with smooth margins. Its flowers feature a tubular calyx that expands into five distinct, spreading petals, often displaying a pale pink or white coloration. The inflorescence is usually composed of a few terminal flowers supported by delicate pedicels. Small glandular hairs are often present on the calyx and stems, providing a protective texture to the plant's surface.
