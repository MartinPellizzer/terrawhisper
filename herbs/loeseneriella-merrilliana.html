Loeseneriella merrilliana

What's the taxonomical classification of Loeseneriella merrilliana?

Loeseneriella merrilliana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Celastrales and the family Celastraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Loeseneriella, and its species designation, merrilliana.

    Loeseneriella merrilliana can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or applied topically. If consumed, individuals may experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping as the body reacts to its chemical components. Some reports suggest that concentrated doses could lead to dizziness or altered sensory perception. Because its precise toxicological profile is not fully documented, caution is advised when handling any part of the specimen.
