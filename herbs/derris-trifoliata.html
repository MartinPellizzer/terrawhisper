Derris trifoliata

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris trifoliata?

Derris trifoliata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Derris and is specifically designated as the species trifoliata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Derris trifoliata has a climbing habit characterized by woody stems that use tendrils to ascend surrounding vegetation.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, meaning they are composed of three distinct leaflets that are often ovate or elliptical in shape. These leaflets possess smooth margins and can vary in size depending on the age and environment of the plant. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually arranged in dense clusters or racemes.

Its root system is adapted to provide stability for its sprawling, climbing growth pattern.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Derris trifoliata contains roten, a bioactive compound primarily extracted from the plant's crushed roots for use as a medicinal insecticide and anthelmintic. These roots are the most significant part of the plant used to treat parasitic infections in various organisms. When processed, the root extracts serve as a potent agent to eliminate intestinal worms and other parasites. Historically, the plant has also been utilized in traditional practices to manage certain skin conditions. The chemical properties found within the root structure make it a valuable source for developing natural pest control solutions.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains rotenoids, flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids which provide its diverse medicinal and bioactive properties.

The presence of rotenoids is particularly significant due to their potent insecticidal and piscicidal qualities used in traditional applications. Flavonoids within the plant contribute antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects that help protect cells from oxidative stress. Alkaloids offer various pharmacological activities that can influence the central nervous system or provide antimicrobial benefits.

Additionally, terpenoids play a role in the plant's chemical defense mechanisms and possess potential therapeutic value in treating diverse ailments.

  • Rotenoids
  • Flavonoids
  • Alkaloids
  • Terpenoids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Derris trifoliata targets specific protein mediators of inflammation and oxidative stress, most notably cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines. The bioactive compounds within the plant act as inhibitors for these proteins to reduce the production of prostaglandins and nitric oxide in biological tissues. By modulating these molecular pathways, the plant extracts can suppress the signaling cascades that lead to chronic inflammatory responses. Additionally, some components interact with antioxidant enzymes to mitigate cellular damage caused by reactive oxygen species. These protein-ligand interactions underpin the plant's pharmacological potential in treating inflammatory disorders and oxidative stress-related conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Derris trifoliata?

Derris trifoliata has potent insecticidal, acaricidal, and vermicide properties due to the high concentration of rotenone found in its roots.

This botanical compound acts by inhibiting cellular respiration in target organisms, effectively paralyzing their nervous systems. When applied to crops, it serves as a natural insecticidal agent to control various pest populations. The plant also functions as an acaricidal treatment to eliminate mites and other small arachnids.

Furthermore, its vermicide qualities make it useful for treating parasitic worm infestations in certain animals.

The primary therapeutic actions of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Insecticidal
  • Acaricidal
  • Vermicide

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat infestations of lice, fleas, and bedbugs through the application of its natural insecticidal properties.

The roots of the plant contain rotenone, which acts as a potent toxin against various small arthropods. People often prepare a decoction or extract from the plant material to eliminate these pests from the body or living spaces. This traditional remedy is particularly effective at disrupting the respiratory systems of the insects.

By utilizing these botanical compounds, individuals can manage parasitic outbreaks without relying solely on synthetic chemicals.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Lice
  • Fleas
  • Bedbugs

What are the herbal preparations of Derris trifoliata?

Derris trifoliata is prepared primarily as a concentrated liquid extract or a fine powder derived from its dried roots for use as a natural insecticide and pesticide. The roots are often crushed or processed into a botanical solution that can be sprayed directly onto plants to control various insect pests. In some traditional applications, the plant material is boiled to create a decoction used for topical treatments or pest management. These preparations leverage the potent rotenone content found naturally within the root system. Careful measurement is required when utilizing these forms to ensure effective pest control without damaging the target environment.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Derris trifoliata can cause skin irritation, numbness, and localized swelling if the plant or its extracted rotenone comes into direct contact with human tissue. The primary toxic component, rotenone, acts as a potent mitochondrial inhibitor that can disrupt cellular energy production. If ingested, the plant may lead to severe gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. High levels of exposure can potentially impact the central nervous system, resulting in dizziness or coordination issues. Because of these risks, handling the plant requires careful precautions to avoid accidental poisoning or dermatological reactions.
