Derris trifoliata

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris trifoliata?

Derris trifoliata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Derris and is specifically designated as the species trifoliata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Derris trifoliata contains rotenone, a potent natural insecticide and piscicide derived from its roots. This compound acts as a mitochondrial inhibitor by disrupting the electron transport chain in cells. Because of this biochemical mechanism, extracts are frequently used in traditional medicine to treat parasitic infections and skin conditions. Beyond its insecticidal properties, the plant is historically utilized in fishing practices to stun aquatic life. Researchers continue to study these bioactive molecules for their potential applications in modern pharmacology and pest control.

What are the therapeutic actions of Derris trifoliata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat infestations of ectoparasites including lice, fleas, bedbugs, and various types of mites. The primary active ingredient, rotenone, acts as a potent neurotoxin that disrupts the nervous systems of these small organisms. Historically, it has been utilized in traditional medicine to manage skin conditions caused by parasitic discomfort. Because it effectively kills insects upon contact, it has served as a foundational component in many early insecticidal formulations. However, its application is carefully controlled due to its toxicity to other living creatures.
