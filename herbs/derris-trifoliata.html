Derris trifoliata

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris trifoliata?

Derris trifoliata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Derris and is specifically designated as the species trifoliata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Derris trifoliata has a climbing habit characterized by woody stems that use tendrils to ascend surrounding vegetation. The leaves are typically trifoliate, meaning they are composed of three distinct leaflets that are often ovate or elliptical in shape. These leaflets possess smooth margins and can vary in size depending on the age and environment of the plant. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually arranged in dense clusters or racemes. Its root system is adapted to provide stability for its sprawling, climbing growth pattern.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Derris trifoliata contains roten, a bioactive compound primarily extracted from the plant's crushed roots for use as a medicinal insecticide and anthelmintic. These roots are the most significant part of the plant used to treat parasitic infections in various organisms. When processed, the root extracts serve as a potent agent to eliminate intestinal worms and other parasites. Historically, the plant has also been utilized in traditional practices to manage certain skin conditions. The chemical properties found within the root structure make it a valuable source for developing natural pest control solutions.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a variety of bioactive phytochemicals including 12a-hydroxy rotenone, 12alphabeta-hydroxyrot-2'-enoic acid, dehurodeguelin, dehydrorotenone, and tephrosin. These specific compounds are often studied for their potent biological activities and potential pharmacological applications. The rotenoid derivatives within the plant are particularly noted for their insecticidal and piscicidal properties. Additionally, substances like tephrosin contribute to the complex chemical profile used in traditional medicine. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Derris trifoliata targets specific protein mediators of inflammation and oxidative stress, most notably cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines. The bioactive compounds within the plant act as inhibitors for these proteins to reduce the production of prostaglandins and nitric oxide in biological tissues. By modulating these molecular pathways, the plant extracts can suppress the signaling cascades that lead to chronic inflammatory responses. Additionally, some components interact with antioxidant enzymes to mitigate cellular damage caused by reactive oxygen species. These protein-ligand interactions underpin the plant's pharmacological potential in treating inflammatory disorders and oxidative stress-related conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Derris trifoliata?

Derris trifoliata has potent insecticidal, acaricidal, and vermicide properties due to the high concentration of rotenone found in its roots. This botanical compound acts by inhibiting cellular respiration in target organisms, effectively paralyzing their nervous systems. When applied to crops, it serves as a natural insecticidal agent to control various pest populations. The plant also functions as an acaricidal treatment to eliminate mites and other small arachnids. Furthermore, its vermicide qualities make it useful for treating parasitic worm infestations in certain animals.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, dizziness, and severe neurotoxicity due to the presence of rotenone. Direct contact with the plant's parts may lead to localized redness or rashes on the skin. If ingested, individuals often experience gastrointestinal distress characterized by nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Exposure can also trigger neurological symptoms such as dizziness. Most critically, the substance is known for its potent neurotoxicity which can impact the central nervous system.
