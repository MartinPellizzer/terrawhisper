Indian Sarsaparilla (Hemidesmus indicus) Compounds

The primary compounds in Indian Sarsaparilla include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to astringent effects. Glycosides and phenolic acids also play roles in its medicinal properties, while coumarin and saponins support anti-inflammatory actions. Terpenoids, alkaloids, essential oils, and anthocyanins further enhance its therapeutic potential.

Indian Sarsaparilla (Hemidesmus indicus) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Hemidesmus indicus, known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and promoting overall wellness. They help in reducing oxidative stress and supporting immune function.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Indian Sarsaparilla (Hemidesmus indicus). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to support digestive health and reduce inflammation. Tannins in Hemidesmus indicus are also associated with its traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.
