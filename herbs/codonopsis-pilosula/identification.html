Botanical Identification of Chinese Ginseng (Codonopsis pilosula)

To identify Chinese Ginseng (Codonopsis pilosula) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Chinese Ginseng (Codonopsis pilosula) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Chinese Ginseng, or Codonopsis pilosula, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Campanulales, family Campanulaceae, genus Codonopsis, and species Codonopsis pilosula. This classification places it within the flowering plants and the grass-like group, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chinese Ginseng is Codonopsis pilosula, first described by the botanist Carl von Linné. It is also known as Dendropanax pedunculatus and has been referred to by various regional and historical names, including "Ren Shen" in Chinese. This nomenclature reflects its traditional use and botanical classification in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Ginseng (Codonopsis pilosula) is a perennial herb with a taproot system. It has a slender, branched stem that grows upright. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated edges. The plant produces a terminal umbel of small, yellow-green flowers. The fruit is a dry capsule containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chinese Ginseng (Codonopsis pilosula) can be distinguished by its rough, cylindrical roots with a brownish exterior, reddish-brown internal bark, and a sweet, slightly bitter aroma. It differs from similar species like Dendrocodonopsis by its shorter, more robust roots and the presence of a distinct central column in the cross-section.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chinese Ginseng, or Codonopsis pilosula, is native to China, particularly in the provinces of Sichuan, Hubei, and Yunnan. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall and well-drained, loamy soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 1,000 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chinese Ginseng typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. Seed formation occurs in the following spring, with seeds maturing by late summer. Harvesting for identification purposes is usually done in autumn when the plant has fully matured and the roots are most developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Codonopsis pilosula belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is closely related to other Codonopsis species. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with its genus relatives, such as similar leaf structures and flowering patterns. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties within the family, distinguishing it from unrelated genera.
