Common Clubmoss (Scolopendrium vulgare)

Common Clubmoss (Scolopendrium vulgare) is a member of the Polypodiaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its stems, leaves, and rhizomes have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, bitter, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and native american herbal medicine.
