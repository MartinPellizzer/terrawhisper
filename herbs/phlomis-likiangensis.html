Phlomis likiangensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Phlomis likiangensis?

Phlomis likiangensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its progression through the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Phlomis, and its unique species designation, likiangensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis likiangensis has a woody, perennial structure characterized by erect, square-shaped stems and large, rugose, ovate leaves that are covered in dense, velvety hairs.

The foliage is typically grayish-green and possesses a prominent reticulate venation pattern on the surface. During its flowering period, the plant produces dense, verticillaster inflorescences that form distinct whorls along the upper portions of the stems. These tubular, bilabiate flowers are often yellowish or pale in color and emerge from protective, leafy bracts.

The overall habit of the plant is bushy and robust, allowing it to thrive in rocky, Mediterranean-style habitats.
