Phlomis likiangensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis likiangensis has a woody, perennial structure characterized by erect, square-shaped stems and large, rugose, ovate leaves that are covered in dense, velvety hairs. During its flowering period, the plant produces dense, verticillaster inflorescences that form distinct whorls along the upper portions of the stems. These tubular, bilabiate flowers are often yellowish or pale in color and emerge from protective, leafy bracts.
