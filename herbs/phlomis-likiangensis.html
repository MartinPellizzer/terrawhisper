Phlomis likiangensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Phlomis likiangensis?

Phlomis likiangensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its progression through the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Phlomis, and its unique species designation, likiangensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phlomis likiangensis has a woody, perennial structure characterized by erect, square-shaped stems and large, rugose, ovate leaves that are covered in dense, velvety hairs. The foliage is typically grayish-green and possesses a prominent reticulate venation pattern on the surface. During its flowering period, the plant produces dense, verticillaster inflorescences that form distinct whorls along the upper portions of the stems. These tubular, bilabiate flowers are often yellowish or pale in color and emerge from protective, leafy bracts. The overall habit of the plant is bushy and robust, allowing it to thrive in rocky, Mediterranean-style habitats.

What are the pharmacological activities of Phlomis likiangensis?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including vasodilatory, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The vasodilatory effects of Phlomis likiangensis contribute to its potential in managing cardiovascular conditions by relaxing smooth muscles in blood vessels. Research suggests that its bioactive compounds also play a role in reducing systemic inflammation and oxidative stress within the body. These diverse therapeutic actions make the species a subject of interest for developing new medicinal treatments. Such multifaceted biological responses highlight the medicinal value of its chemical constituents.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", Phlomis likiangensis is traditionally used in China to activate collaterals and protect the cardiovascular system. Twelve known iridoid glucosides, labeled 1-12, were isolated from Phlomis likiangensis to investigate their pharmacological properties. These 12 iridoid glucosides were found to significantly increase vasodilatation in rat aortic rings. This vasodilatory effect was abolished by removing the endothelium of the vessels or by eliminating the generation of nitric oxide. These findings provide the first evidence that the iridoid glucosides of Phlomis likiangensis may serve as the pharmacodynamic basis for its traditional efficacy.
