Blessed Thistle (Cnicus benedictus)

Blessed Thistle (Cnicus benedictus) is a member of the Compositae family, native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and seeds have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, bitter, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface white woolly covering, bearing stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescences are solitary capitula with yellow tubular flowers surrounded by white to grayish cobalt-blue bracts, actinomorphic, with five lobed corollas and five stamens.
Stems
Cylindrical, woody, branched, with opposite, sparsely hairy, persistent stipules.
