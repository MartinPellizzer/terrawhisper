Pentapanax yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pentapanax yunnanensis has large, palmately compound leaves with five to seven distinct leaflets that exhibit serrated margins and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant grows as a woody shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of several meters in temperate forest environments. Its stems are sturdy and can develop a bark texture that becomes slightly fissured with age. The leaves are arranged alternately along the branches, providing a dense canopy of foliage. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically appear in clusters, eventually developing into fleshy fruits that facilitate seed dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan. It primarily inhabits montane environments where the terrain is rugged and the climate is temperate to cool. These botanical specimens are often found growing in mountain forests or along rocky slopes at significant elevations. The specific ecological niches it occupies are defined by the unique moisture and soil conditions of the Hengduan Mountains. Consequently, its range is restricted to these particular subtropical and temperate highland ecosystems.
