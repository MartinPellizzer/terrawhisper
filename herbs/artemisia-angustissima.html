What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia angustissima?

Artemisia angustissima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants, and it is further defined by its placement in the genus Artemisia. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is established at the species level as angustissima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia angustissima has narrow, linear leaves that are typically 2 to 10 millimeters wide and possess a silvery-gray or grayish-green coloration due to fine, silky hairs. The plant grows as a perennial herb with a woody caudex and often forms dense clumps or colonies. Its stems are slender, erect, and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. The foliage is divided into many small, narrow segments, giving the plant a finely textured appearance. Small, inconspicuous flower heads are arranged in terminal panicles and bloom during the summer months.
