Paramignya confertifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Paramignya confertifolia?

Paramignya confertifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Sapindales. Specifically, this plant is situated in the family Rutaceae and is categorized under the genus Paramignya, ultimately defined by its specific epithet, confertifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paramignya confertifolia has opposite, simple, elliptical to oblong leaves that measure approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length and are characterized by entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface with a slightly paler underside. Its stems are woody and climbing, often utilizing small aerial roots to attach itself to supporting structures. The plant produces clusters of tubular, salverform flowers that transition from white to a deep pink or red as they age. These blooms are supported by sturdy pedicels and emerge from the axils of the leaves.
