Paspalum scrobiculatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paspalum scrobiculatum has a perennial growth habit characterized by tufted, stoloniferous clumps with slender, erect culms that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are linear and narrow, featuring a prominent midrib and scabrous margins that feel rough to the touch. Its inflorescence consists of several distinct, spike-like panicles that are arranged in a somewhat contracted or spreading manner. Each spikelet is small and contains a single fertile floret, often displaying a subtle purplish hue during maturity. The overall structure allows the plant to spread effectively across grassy environments through its creeping runners.
