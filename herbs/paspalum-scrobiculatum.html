What's the taxonomical classification of Paspalum scrobiculatum?

Paspalum scrobiculatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Poales into the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Paspalum, with its specific identification designated as the species scrobiculatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paspalum scrobiculatum has a perennial growth habit characterized by tufted, stoloniferous clumps with slender, erect culms that typically reach heights of 20 to 60 centimeters.

The leaves are linear and narrow, featuring a prominent midrib and scabrous margins that feel rough to the touch. Its inflorescence consists of several distinct, spike-like panicles that are arranged in a somewhat contracted or spreading manner. Each spikelet is small and contains a single fertile floret, often displaying a subtle purplish hue during maturity.

The overall structure allows the plant to spread effectively across grassy environments through its creeping runners.
