What's the taxonomical classification of Paspalum scrobiculatum?

Paspalum scrobiculatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Poales into the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Paspalum, with its specific identification designated as the species scrobiculatum.

      Paspalum scrobiculatum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice for various traditional uses. The plant material is typically boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid used for internal consumption.       Paspalum scrobiculatum can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or respiratory discomfort if individuals with specific sensitivities come into direct contact with its foliage. Monitoring for these mild reactions is recommended when working in areas where this grass is prevalent.
