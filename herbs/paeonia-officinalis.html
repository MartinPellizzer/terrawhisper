Common Peony

Paeonia officinalis

Botanical Identification

Paeonia officinalis is a perennial herbaceous plant in the family Ranunculaceae. Commonly known as the common peony, it is native to Europe and western Asia. It features thick, fleshy roots, large compound leaves, and showy, pinkish-red flowers. It has also been referred to as the peony or wild peony in historical herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The Common Peony contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, glycosides, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Glycoside
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for inflammation and pain relief due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Tincture: Used for digestive issues and nervous system disorders due to its calming and antispasmodic effects.

Decoction: Used for respiratory infections and as a general tonic due to its warming and strengthening properties.

Safety Profile

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulant effects. Long-term use may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, and individuals with allergies to plants in the Ranunculaceae family should exercise caution.
