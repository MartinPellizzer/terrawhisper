Common Peony

Paeonia officinalis

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia, known for its showy pink or white petals and its long history of use in traditional medicine. It is primarily used to improve symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and mood swings, and has also been employed in the treatment of inflammatory conditions and digestive disorders.

Taxonomy

The Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Paeoniales, family Paeoniaceae, genus Paeonia, and species Paeonia officinalis.

Morphology

The Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, broad palmately lobed leaves with serrated margins, and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to deep pink, typically with a distinctive red or purple center.

Distribution

The Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is native to southern Europe and western Asia, with historical cultivation extending to regions such as the Mediterranean, the Middle East, and parts of Eastern Europe.

Traditional Uses

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and analgesic properties, often applied to treat conditions such as menstrual disorders, digestive issues, and skin ailments.

Medicinal Systems

The Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) has been utilized in traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and Native American herbal practices for its therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) involves the presence of various bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, essential oils, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications.

Pharmacology

The Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) exhibits anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antispasmodic properties, making it effective in treating conditions such as menstrual disorders, gastrointestinal spasms, and inflammatory diseases.

Botanical Identification

Paeonia officinalis is a perennial herbaceous plant in the family Ranunculaceae. Commonly known as the common peony, it is native to Europe and western Asia. It features thick, fleshy roots, large compound leaves, and showy, pinkish-red flowers. It has also been referred to as the peony or wild peony in historical herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for inflammation and pain relief due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Safety Profile

Common Peony (Paeonia officinalis) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulant effects. Long-term use may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, and individuals with allergies to plants in the Ranunculaceae family should exercise caution.
