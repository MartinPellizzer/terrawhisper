What's the taxonomical classification of Liriope kansuensis?

Liriope kansuensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this botanical hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Asparagales and the family Asparagaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by its specific genus, Lioriope, and its unique species designation, kansuensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Liriope kansuensis has linear, grass-like leaves that are dark green, strap-shaped, and typically reach heights of 20 to 40 centimeters. These leaves grow in dense, clumping tufts and possess a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, pale lavender to white bell-shaped flowers on slender stalks. Following the blooms, it develops small, dark blue or blackish berries that serve as its fruit. The root system consists of thick, creeping rhizomes that allow the plant to spread steadily through the soil.
