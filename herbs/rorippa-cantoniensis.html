Rorippa cantoniensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rorippa cantoniensis has an erect or ascending herbaceous habit with stems that are typically branched, cylindrical, and sometimes slightly pubescent. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and feature an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in dense, terminal, or axillary clusters that often appear yellowish or greenish in color. The reproductive structures consist of small florets that mature into cylindrical achenes. These achenes are frequently equipped with a pappus that assists in wind dispersal.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and potential allergic reactions when the sap or leaves come into direct contact with human tissue. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or localized swelling at the site of exposure. If the plant is accidentally ingested, it could lead to gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or stomach pain. While direct toxicity levels are not extensively documented, caution should always be exercised when handling any wild vegetation. It is advisable to wear gloves to prevent any unwanted dermatological responses during interaction with the species.
