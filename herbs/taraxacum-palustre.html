What to know about Taraxacum palustre (marsh dandelion) before using it medicinally

Marsh dandelion, scientifically known as Taraxacum palustre, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to improve overall well-being by reducing inflammation and fighting off infections.

In the culinary world, marsh dandelion is often used as a flavorful addition to soups, salads, and teas, adding a unique bitter taste and depth of flavor. As an ornamental plant, marsh dandelion is prized for its bright yellow flowers and feathery foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. From a botanical standpoint, marsh dandelion belongs to the Asteraceae family and can be found growing wild in wet meadows and marshy areas throughout Europe and Asia.

With historical references dating back to ancient Greek and Roman medicine, marsh dandelion has long been recognized for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional folk remedies.
