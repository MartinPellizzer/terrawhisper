Gymnopetalum chinense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gymnopyetalum chinense has woody stems with opposite, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface, while the underside may appear slightly paler. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. These flowers are characterized by a lack of petals, which is a defining feature of the genus. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy fruits that serve as a primary reproductive structure.

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of southern China, including provinces such as Guangdong, Guangxi, Hainan, and Yunnan. The species typically thrives in moist, shaded environments characteristic of these warm climate zones. Its distribution is closely tied to the availability of humid forest ecosystems.
