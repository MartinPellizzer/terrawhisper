Gymnopetalum chinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Gymnopetalum chinense?

Gymnopetalum chinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a lineage that leads to the order Cucurbitales. This plant is a distinguished member of the family Cucurbitaceae, a group known for its diverse flowering members. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Gymnopetalum, and its specific identification is defined by its species name, chinense.

Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters. These flowers are characterized by a lack of petals, which is a defining feature of the genus. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, known as the China root, requires well-draining, sandy loam soil and consistent moisture levels to thrive in subtropical or tropical environments.
