Cissus pteroclada

What's the taxonomical classification of Cissus pteroclada?

Cissus pteroclada belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Cissus and is specifically known by its species name, pteroclada.

    This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and various types of inflammation. Traditional medicinal practices involve applying the crushed leaves directly to affected areas of the skin to promote healing. Some cultures also prepare decoctions from the plant to alleviate internal digestive discomfort. The bioactive compounds within the foliage are believed to possess antimicrobial and analgesic properties. These applications serve as a vital resource for local communities managing minor ailments and injuries.
