Ligularia hodgsonii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia hodgsonii?

Ligularia hodgsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, which falls under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Ligularia, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species hodgsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia hodgsonii has massive, cordate leaves that can reach up to 50 centimeters in length and 30 centimeters in width, characterized by deeply serrated margins and prominent venation. These large, bright green foliage surfaces are held on thick, sturdy petioles that emerge from a clumping base. The plant produces tall, upright flowering stalks that rise significantly above the leaf canopy to display golden-yellow composite flower heads. Each inflorescence consists of multiple ray and disc florets arranged in a dense, terminal cluster. The overall structure is robust and architectural, making it a dominant feature in moist, shaded environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan, Sichuan, and Guizhou. It thrives primarily in moist, mountainous habitats where it grows along the edges of streams and in shaded forest clearings. These populations are typically found at elevations ranging from moderate to high mountain levels. The specific environmental conditions of these subtropical highland zones provide the necessary humidity for its large leaves to flourish. Because its range is concentrated in these specific provinces, it remains a characteristic species of the regional montane flora.
