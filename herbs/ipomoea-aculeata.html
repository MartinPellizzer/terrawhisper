Ipomoea aculeata

Ipomoea aculeata, also known as the thorny morning-glory, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, characterized by its thorny stems and trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its various medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which are believed to aid in the treatment of wounds, skin conditions, and respiratory issues.

The health benefits of Ipomoea aculeata include its potential to reduce fever, alleviate pain, and support the immune system.

The plant can be prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used in traditional remedies in the form of poultices and salves.
