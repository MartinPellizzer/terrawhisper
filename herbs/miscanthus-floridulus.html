What's the taxonomical classification of Miscanthus floridulus?

Miscanthus floridulus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is ordered under Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Finally, it is categorized within the genus Miscanthus, with its specific designation being the species floridulus.

What are the pharmacological activities of Miscanthus floridulus?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its potent alpha-amylase inhibitory activity and alpha-glucosidase inhibitory activity. These specific enzymatic inhibitions suggest that extracts from Miscanthus floridulus may play a role in managing postprandial blood glucose levels. By slowing down the breakdown of complex carbohydrates into simple sugars, the plant acts as a natural modulator of glycemic responses. Further research into these biochemical pathways could reveal its therapeutic utility in treating type 2 diabetes. Such medicinal properties highlight the importance of exploring this species for developing new antidiabetic agents.

As discussed in a study, the total phenolic content (TPC) of the hydromethanolic extracts of Miscanthus floridulus was found to be in the range of 0.020 ± 0.009 to 0.009 ± 0.01 mM CE. The hydromethanolic extracts of Miscanthus floridulus significantly inhibited α-glucosidase compared to acarbose. This inhibition was evidenced by an IC50 value for Miscanthus floridulus of 1.58 ± 0.03 ng/µl, whereas the IC50 of acarbose was 3.34 ± 0.15 ng/µl. These results suggest that this plant could be a potential source of natural inhibitors of enzymes involved in carbohydrate digestion.
