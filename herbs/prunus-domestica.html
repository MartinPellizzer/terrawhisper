Botanical Identification

European Plum (Prunus domestica) is a species in the Rosaceae family. Common names include damson and European prune, though these refer to related species. It is a deciduous perennial shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. The plant has a taproot, alternate simple leaves, and white or pink flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include "plum" and "prune," though these are often used interchangeably with other Prunus species.

Active Compounds

The European plum contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Decoction: Used for treating colds, coughs, and digestive disorders.

Poultice: Used for topical application to soothe skin irritations and inflammation.

Safety Profile

European Plum (Prunus domestica) is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild digestive upset in some individuals. It is contraindicated in people with known allergies to related plants and should be used with caution during pregnancy and breastfeeding.
