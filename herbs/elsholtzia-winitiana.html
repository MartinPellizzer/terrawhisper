Elsholtzia winitiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia winitiana has pinnately compound leaves typically consisting of 7 to 15 leaflets that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The leaflets are arranged oppositely along the rachis and feature fine pubescence on their undersides. The tree develops a spreading crown with bark that becomes textured and fissured as the specimen matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, eventually giving rise to winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal. These fruits are characterized by a distinctively shaped wing that aids in their aerodynamic flight.
