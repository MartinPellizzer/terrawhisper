    What is Myrcia minoriflora?

    Myrcia minoriflora is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

    It is a shrub that grows up to 2 meters in height, with a woody stem and dark green, opposite, elliptical leaves that are 6-12 millimeters long. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters at the end of its branches, and red, fleshy fruits that are 5-7 millimeters in diameter. The species is endemic to Brazil, where it is found in the Atlantic Forest biome.

    Myrcia minoriflora is a member of the genus Myrcia, which includes approximately 1,200 species of trees and shrubs.
