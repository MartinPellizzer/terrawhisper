What to know about Aloe vera before using it medicinally

Aloe vera, commonly known as aloe vera, is a medicinal herb that has been used to treat various health conditions, including skin wounds, burns, eczema, and psoriasis.

It is also used to alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), reduce inflammation, and lower blood sugar levels in people with diabetes. The main health benefits of aloe vera include its ability to promote skin hydration, reduce oxidative stress, and exhibit antimicrobial properties. The main therapeutic properties of aloe vera include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory effects, which contribute to its healing and protective properties.

Aloe vera contains bioactive compounds such as aloin, aloe-emodin, and vitamins A, C, and E, and is often used to make herbal preparations like gel, juice, and powder, which are used topically or taken internally to harness its medicinal properties.
