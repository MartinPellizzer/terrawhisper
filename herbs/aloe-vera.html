Aloe vera

What's the taxonomical classification of Aloe vera?

Aloe vera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Asparagales and the family Asphodelaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Aloe and the specific species vera.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aloe vera contains polysaccharides like acemannan, anthraquinones such as aloin, and various vitamins, minerals, and amino acids. The complex sugars in the gel act as soothing agents that promote skin hydration and accelerate the healing of burns. Anthraquinones provide significant antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties that help combat topical infections. Additionally, the presence of salicylic acid allows the plant to serve as a natural remedy for reducing redness and swelling. These combined bioactive compounds make the plant highly effective for both dermatological and digestive health applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aloe vera?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat minor skin conditions such as sunburns, abrasions, small wounds, and inflammatory skin diseases like psoriasis or eczema. Its gel contains bioactive compounds that provide soothing relief and accelerate the healing process of damaged tissues. When applied topically, the substance acts as an anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial agent to prevent infection in minor cuts. Some studies also suggest that oral consumption may assist with digestive issues such as constipation or acid reflux. However, medical supervision is often necessary when using the plant for internal medicinal purposes.
