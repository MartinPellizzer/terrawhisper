Family

Native Region

Part Used

Botanical Identification

Aloe Vera, scientifically known as Aloe barbadensis, belongs to the Asphodelaceae family. Common names include "true aloe" and "burn plant," with regional variants like "kumari" in India. It is a perennial succulent native to the Arabian Peninsula. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves with a spiky margin and produces yellow flowers. Synonyms in herbal texts often include Aloe vera and Aloe barbadensis.

Active Compounds

Aloe Vera contains key active compounds such as terpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids. These bioactives work synergistically to reduce inflammation and support skin healing. Their combined effects enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Terpenoid
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Poultice: Used for skin irritations and wounds

Extract: Used for digestive issues and constipation

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and constipation

Safety Profile

Aloe vera is generally safe when used topically or orally in recommended amounts. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. Long-term or excessive internal use can lead to gastrointestinal issues and electrolyte imbalances. Always consult a healthcare provider before using aloe vera for medicinal purposes.
