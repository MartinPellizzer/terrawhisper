What to know about Bupleurum falcatum (chinese thoroughwort) before using it medicinally

Chinese thoroughwort, scientifically known as Bupleurum falcatum, is a highly prized herb in traditional Chinese medicine, renowned for its impressive health benefits that include reducing inflammation, alleviating fever and improving digestion.

In the kitchen, Chinese thoroughwort is often used to add flavor and aroma to a variety of dishes, particularly in soups and stir-fries. As a popular ornamental plant, Chinese thoroughwort is highly valued for its beautiful yellow flowers and slender leaves, which make it a sought-after addition to gardens and bouquets. Botanically speaking, Chinese thoroughwort belongs to the carrot family, with its distinctive foliage and stems bearing small, delicate umbels of tiny yellow flowers.

With a rich history dating back thousands of years, Chinese thoroughwort has been mentioned in ancient texts such as the Shen Nong Ben Cao Jing, solidifying its status as an esteemed herb in traditional Chinese medicine and cuisine.
