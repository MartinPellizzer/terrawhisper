What is Aster albus?

Aster albus is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It has a taproot, unbranched stem up to 30-60 cm tall, with simple alternate leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, 5-20 cm long and 0.5-2.5 cm wide. The plant produces a capitulum (inflorescence) with 20-30 white, ray florets and 40-60 yellow or white, disc florets. The inflorescence is 3-6 cm in diameter and is surrounded by a leafy bract.

Aster albus is native to Europe and Western Asia, and is commonly found in wet meadows and along streams.
