Machilus rufipes

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Machilus rufipes has simple, alternate, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that feature entire margins and a distinctively reddish-brown pubescence on the undersides. The leaf apex is typically acute or acuminate, while the base varies from cuneate to obtuse. The petioles are notably hairy, contributing to the specific epithet of the species. Its bark is generally smooth, and the plant often exhibits a woody structure characteristic of the Lauraceae family. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in panicles, eventually developing into fleshy drupes.
