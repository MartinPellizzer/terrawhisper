Flax (Linum usitatissimum)

Flax (Linum usitatissimum) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Middle East, Central Asia, and Southern Europe. Traditionally, its seeds, flowers, and leaves have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.

Cylindrical, erect, hollow, fibrous, four-angled, branching at nodes, glabrous, leafy at base, with distinct ridges and nodes.

Anti-inflammatory

Diuretic
