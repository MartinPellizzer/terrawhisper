Psychotria prainii

What's the taxonomical classification of Psychotria prainii?

Psychotria prainii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the evolutionary path of the order Gentianaales, this plant is a member of the Rubiaceae family. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Psychotria and the species prainii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Psychotria prainii contains high concentrations of indole alkaloids, specifically those belonging to the tryptamine class such as dimethyltryptamine and various related derivatives. These psychoactive compounds function as agonists at serotonin receptors within the central nervous system. Beyond their hallucinogenic properties, these alkaloids are studied for their potential neuroprotective and antidepressant effects. The plant also possesses secondary metabolites including flavonoids and tannins that may contribute to its overall pharmacological profile. Researchers continue to investigate how these specific chemical constituents interact with human physiology to develop new therapeutic applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Psychotria prainii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, skin infections, stomach ailments, and various respiratory issues. Local healers often apply its crushed leaves to wounds or rashes to promote healing and reduce swelling. It is also prepared as an infusion to soothe gastrointestinal discomfort and digestive disturbances. Some traditional practices involve using the plant to alleviate symptoms associated with coughs and bronchial irritation. These medicinal applications demonstrate the plant's role in addressing both external skin ailments and internal systemic problems.
