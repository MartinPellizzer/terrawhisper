Boenninghausenia sessilicarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Boenninghausenia sessilicarpa?

Boenninghausenia sessilicarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Sapindales. It is a part of the family Rutaceae, falling specifically under the genus Boenninghausenia, with the unique specific epithet sessilicarpa defining its species designation.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Boenninghausenia sessilicarpa contains bioactive alkaloids, saponins, and various terpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological profile. These chemical constituents are primarily extracted from the leaves and roots of the plant. Researchers have studied these compounds for their potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies also suggest that the plant extracts may exhibit antioxidant activity within biological systems. Detailed phytochemical screening is ongoing to further isolate specific molecules responsible for these medicinal effects.

What are the therapeutic actions of Boenninghausenia sessilicarpa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat diarrhea, stomach aches, and various forms of intestinal inflammation. The leaves and roots are often prepared as decoctions or infusions to soothe digestive distress. Local communities rely on these extracts to alleviate symptoms associated with stomach cramps and abdominal discomfort. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to combat general malaise related to gastrointestinal infections. This medicinal application highlights its importance in ethnobotanical studies of the region.
