Orostachys malacophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Orostachys malacophylla?

Orostachys malacophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Saxifragales. It is a specific representative of the family Crassulaceae, falling under the genus Orostachys, and is uniquely identified by its species name, malacophylla.

      What are the herbal preparations of Orostachys malacophylla?

      Orostachys malacophylla is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the dried aerial parts of the plant in water to extract its active compounds. This liquid preparation is often consumed as a medicinal tea to address specific physiological conditions. In some traditional practices, the plant material may also be processed into a concentrated powder for easier dosage. This powder can be mixed with warm water or honey to facilitate ingestion. Some regional applications involve using a topical wash derived from the plant infusion for external skin treatments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Orostachys malacophylla can cause severe dermatological irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and localized swelling if the plant's juices come into contact with skin or mucous membranes. The chemical compounds within its sap are known to trigger allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of any part of the plant may lead to nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. Some users have also reported mild neurological sensations such as tingling or numbness near the site of exposure. It is essential to handle this species with protective gear to avoid these adverse reactions.
