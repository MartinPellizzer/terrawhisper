Orostachys malacophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Orostachys malacophylla?

Orostachys malacophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized under the order Saxifragales. It is a specific representative of the family Crassulaceae, falling under the genus Orostachys, and is uniquely identified by its species name, malacophylla.

What are the pharmacological activities of Orostachys malacophylla?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including acting as an effective anti-Toxoplasma gondii agent. Research into its bioactive compounds reveals a significant capacity to inhibit the growth of this specific protozoan parasite. Beyond its antiparasitic properties, the plant exhibits diverse medicinal potential through its various phytochemical constituents. These chemical profiles contribute to a broad spectrum of biological responses observed in laboratory settings. Such findings suggest that the plant serves as a valuable source for developing new therapeutic treatments.

As discussed in a study, Orostachys malacophylla (Pallas) Fischer yields isolated compounds including nipagic acid, gallic acid, ethyl gallate, phloretic acid, protocatechuic acid, methyl p-coumarate, arbutin, and homoprotocatechuic acid. Among these, arbutin can effectively prolong the survival time of mice acutely infected with T. gondii. This component exhibits a curative effect similar to the Spiramycin (Spi) group regarding glutathione (GSH) and malondialdehyde (MDA) content. Furthermore, arbutin helps alleviate hepatomegaly and splenomegaly. Based on structure-activity relationship and molecular docking, the phenolic hydroxyl group is preferred for improving activity, marking arbutin as a potential anti-T. gondii candidate.
