      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lathyros komarovii can cause severe neurological damage, including permanent paralysis or death, if its seeds are ingested due to the presence of toxic amino acids. These specific toxins, often classified as neurotoxins, interfere with the central nervous system and can lead to a condition known as lathyrism. Symptoms typically manifest as muscle weakness and progressive spasticity in the lower limbs. In extreme cases, the damage to motor neurons becomes irreversible, making walking difficult or impossible. Because the toxicity is concentrated in the seeds, accidental consumption by humans or livestock poses a significant health risk.
