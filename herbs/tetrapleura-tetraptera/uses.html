Tetrapleura Tetraptera (Tetrapleura tetraptera) Uses

Tetrapleura tetraptera is used to treat various respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and pneumonia. It helps alleviate symptoms of lung inflammation and infections. The plant is also employed for coughs and respiratory infections, including influenza and tuberculosis. It shows potential in managing allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Tetrapleura Tetraptera (Tetrapleura tetraptera) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Tetrapleura tetraptera is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and soothe respiratory irritation, making it a valuable remedy for managing bronchial inflammation and coughing.

2. Influenza

Tetrapleura tetraptera is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat influenza due to its antipyretic and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce fever and alleviate symptoms such as coughing and body aches. The leaves and bark are commonly prepared as decoctions or infusions for therapeutic use.

3. Tuberculosis

Tetrapleura tetraptera has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support treatment of tuberculosis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is believed to help alleviate symptoms and boost the immune system, though more research is needed to confirm its efficacy in modern medical practice.

4. Allergic rhinitis

Tetrapleura tetraptera has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties help reduce nasal congestion and allergic reactions. It is often prepared as a decoction or infusion to support respiratory health in cases of seasonal allergies.

5. Lung inflammation

Tetrapleura tetraptera is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat lung inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling and mucus production in the respiratory tract, providing relief from symptoms associated with conditions like bronchitis and pneumonia. The leaves and bark are commonly prepared as decoctions or infusions for this purpose.

6. Asthma

Tetrapleura tetraptera has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms. Its leaves contain compounds with bronchodilatory effects, helping to relax airway muscles. It is often prepared as a decoction and used to reduce inflammation and ease breathing in asthmatic patients.

7. Pneumonia

Tetrapleura tetraptera is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat pneumonia due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce lung inflammation and facilitates the removal of mucus, easing breathing. The leaves and bark are commonly prepared as decoctions or infusions for this purpose.

8. Lung infections

Tetrapleura tetraptera is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat lung infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce symptoms like coughing and fever, and is often prepared as a decoction or poultice. The plant's active compounds support respiratory health by combating pathogens and soothing irritated airways.

9. Cough

Tetrapleura tetraptera is used to treat cough by soothing the respiratory tract and reducing inflammation. Its leaves and bark contain compounds that help loosen mucus and ease breathing. It is commonly prepared as a decoction or infusion for this purpose. This traditional remedy is valued for its effectiveness in managing persistent cough symptoms.

10. Respiratory infections

Tetrapleura tetraptera is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms such as coughing, bronchitis, and throat infections. The plant's leaves and bark are commonly prepared as decoctions or infusions for respiratory support. Its active compounds exhibit broad-spectrum activity against pathogens causing respiratory ailments.
