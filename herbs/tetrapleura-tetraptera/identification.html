Tetrapleura tetraptera belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Sapindaceae, genus Tetrapleura, and species Tetrapleura tetraptera. The scientific name of the plant is Tetrapleura tetraptera, first described by the botanist A. DC. in 1832. It is also known by several synonyms, including Pentapleura tetraptera and Pentapleura tetraptera var. tetraptera. Tetrapleura tetraptera is a small to medium-sized tree with a straight trunk and a dense, rounded crown. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, adapting to moist environments. The inflorescence is a terminal panicle, bearing small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule splitting into four valves, releasing flat, winged seeds.

Tetrapleura tetraptera can be distinguished by its distinctive leaf morphology, with opposite, pinnately compound leaves having 5-7 pairs of leaflets. Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Tetrapleura tetraptera is native to tropical Africa, particularly Nigeria and Cameroon. It thrives in warm, humid climates with high rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found at low to mid-altitude regions, typically between 200 and 1,000 meters above sea level.

Tetrapleura tetraptera blooms from May to July, producing yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from August to October. For herbal use, the leaves are harvested during the rainy season, while the bark is collected year-round, depending on the specific medicinal requirement.
