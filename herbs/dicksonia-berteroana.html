What is Dicksonia berteroana?

Dicksonia berteroana is a species of tree fern native to South America, specifically in the Andes mountains.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae, a group of extinct and extant ferns commonly known as the scaly tree ferns. Botanically, Dicksonia berteroana has large, simple leaves with a deeply pinnate or bipinnate structure, and a scaly rhizome that produces short, stout stipes supporting the fronds. The plant typically grows to a height of 3-5 meters, with a trunk diameter of up to 30 centimeters.

Its fertile fronds are usually smaller than the sterile fronds and are often less divided, with sporangia forming on the underside of the leaflets.
