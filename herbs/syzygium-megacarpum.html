Syzygium megacarpum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syzygium megacarpum has a large woody structure characterized by spreading crowns, smooth greyish bark, and elliptical leaves that are oppositely arranged on the stems. The foliage is typically glossy and dark green, providing a dense canopy for the tree. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters that attract various pollinators. The most distinctive morphological feature is the large, fleshy fruit which can reach significant sizes compared to related species. These fruits often contain multiple seeds and serve as a vital food source for local fauna.
