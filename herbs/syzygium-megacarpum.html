Syzygium megacarpum

What's the taxonomical classification of Syzygium megacarpum?

Syzygium megacarpum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Myrtales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Myrtaceae and is categorized under the genus Syzygium, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species Syzygium megacarpum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syzygium megacarpum has a large woody structure characterized by spreading crowns, smooth greyish bark, and elliptical leaves that are oppositely arranged on the stems.

The foliage is typically glossy and dark green, providing a dense canopy for the tree. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters that attract various pollinators. The most distinctive morphological feature is the large, fleshy fruit which can reach significant sizes compared to related species.

These fruits often contain multiple seeds and serve as a vital food source for local fauna.

Contact with the leaves or bark might trigger contact dermatitis in people with specific plant sensitivities. There is currently limited clinical data regarding the long-term toxicological effects of this species on humans.
