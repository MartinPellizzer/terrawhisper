Radermachera pentandra

What's the taxonomical classification of Radermachera pentandra?

Radermachera pentandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Bignoniaceae, specifically within the genus Radermachera, and is identified by its unique species name, pentandra.

      What are the herbal preparations of Radermachera pentandra?

      Radermachera pentandra is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the specific ailment being treated. The leaves are commonly boiled in water to create a liquid extract used for internal consumption. In some traditional practices, the crushed plant material is applied directly to the skin as a poultice to soothe local inflammation. These various preparations allow the medicinal properties of the plant to be delivered effectively to different parts of the body. The choice of form is dictated by whether the goal is to treat systemic issues or localized skin conditions.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Radermachera pentandra can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the sap or leaves may trigger redness, itching, or a localized rash on sensitive skin. If a person accidentally consumes parts of the plant, they might experience nausea, vomiting, or stomach pain. While human toxicity is not extensively documented, caution is advised when handling the foliage. It is best to wear gloves and keep the plant out of reach of pets and children to prevent accidental exposure.
