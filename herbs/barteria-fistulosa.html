Barteria fistulosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Barteria fistulosa?

Barteria fistulosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Malpighiales, this plant is a member of the family Passifloraceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Barteria, with its unique designation being the species fistulosa.

Barteria fistulosa contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that exhibit pharmacological potential. These chemical constituents are primarily studied for their antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. What are the therapeutic actions of Barteria fistulosa?

Barteria fistulosa has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that allow it to combat various infections and reduce cellular oxidative stress. Its chemical composition includes bioactive secondary metabolites that inhibit the growth of specific pathogenic microorganisms. These compounds also help mitigate inflammatory responses within biological tissues, potentially soothing swelling and irritation. Furthermore, the plant's ability to neutralize free radicals supports overall cellular health and protects against oxidative damage. Such diverse therapeutic actions suggest significant potential for developing new pharmacological treatments.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, gastrointestinal issues, and various inflammatory conditions. The botanical extracts are frequently applied topically to soothe wounds and clear up fungal or bacterial skin irritations. When ingested in specific preparations, it is believed to aid in settling digestive disturbances and reducing abdominal discomfort. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant to manage symptoms of fever and general systemic inflammation. This medicinal utility stems from the unique bioactive compounds found within its tissues.
