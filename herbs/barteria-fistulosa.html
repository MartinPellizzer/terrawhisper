Barteria fistulosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Barteria fistulosa has a woody, perennial structure characterized by erect stems, simple or occasionally branched leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate to narrowly elliptical in shape with entire margins and an alternate arrangement along the stem. Its reproductive structures consist of small, fleshy fruits that develop from the tiny, yellowish or greenish flowers. The bark is generally smooth to slightly textured, providing protection for the plant's internal vascular tissues. These morphological features collectively define its growth habit within its specific ecological niche.
