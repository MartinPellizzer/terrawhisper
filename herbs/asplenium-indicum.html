What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium indicum has a creeping rhizome that produces a rosette of glossy, bright green, lanceolate fronds measuring up to 30 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are characteristically serrated or wavy, while the underside features a distinct dark, almost black, midrib. Each pinnate frond is composed of multiple pairs of oblong pinnae that are arranged symmetrically along the central axis. The plant produces small, inconspicuous sori located on the underside of the leaflets, which are protected by a thin membrane. These morphological traits allow the fern to thrive in moist, shaded tropical environments.
