Botanical Identification of Red-Seed Dandelion (Taraxacum erythrospermum)

To identify Red-Seed Dandelion (Taraxacum erythrospermum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Red-Seed Dandelion belongs to the Kingdom Plantae. Its classification includes the Family Compositae, Genus Taraxacum, and Species Taraxacum erythrospermum. The other taxonomic levels—Division, Class, and Order—are not specified in the provided data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The Red-Seed Dandelion is scientifically known as Taraxacum erythrospermum. This name was first described by the botanist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck in 1786. It is also referred to by several synonyms, including Taraxacum erythrospermum and Taraxacum rubrum. Common names include red-seed dandelion and red-seed weed, reflecting its distinctive seed characteristics.

Morphological Characteristics

Red-Seed Dandelion is a perennial herb with a taproot system. It has upright, hairy stems that branch near the base. Leaves are basal, alternate, and have a distinctive heart-shaped base with serrated edges. The plant produces a single flowering stem with a cluster of yellow flowers that turn into fluffy white seeds with orange-pink seeds. The seeds are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Red-Seed Dandelion can be distinguished by its bright red seeds, which are distinctly different from the yellow or brown seeds of other dandelion species. Its leaves are deeply lobed with serrated edges, and the flower heads are typically more rounded and compact compared to other Taraxacum species. These features help in accurate identification during herbal studies.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Red-Seed Dandelion is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained soils, often found in meadows and disturbed areas. It grows at elevations between 0 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Red-Seed Dandelion blooms from late spring to early summer, producing yellow flowers that transform into white puffballs by mid-summer. Seed formation occurs in late summer, with seeds dispersing by early autumn. For identification, harvest the flower heads when the seeds are fully developed but before they disperse.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Taraxacum erythrospermum is closely related to other dandelion species within the genus Taraxacum. It shares key traits with its closest relatives, such as leaf structure and flowering habits. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary ties to other members of the Asteraceae family, particularly those with similar medicinal uses.
