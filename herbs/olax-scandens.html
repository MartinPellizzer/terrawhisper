What's the taxonomical classification of Olax scandens?

Olax scandens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic journey continues through the order Santalales and the family Olacaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Olax, and its specific identity is defined by the species name scandens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Olax scandens has woody stems that function as a climbing shrub or scandent tree, characterized by opposite, elliptical, or lanceolate leaves with entire margins and an acute apex. The foliage is typically leathery in texture and possesses a glossy upper surface with distinct pinnate venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary panicles, often appearing in clusters along the stem. The fruit is a fleshy drupe that matures into a dark color, containing a single hard seed. These morphological features allow the plant to effectively navigate through forest canopies to access light.
