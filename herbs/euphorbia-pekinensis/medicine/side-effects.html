1. Causes skin irritation

Euphorbia pekinensis, also known as Chinese milkweed, causes skin irritation due to its latex sap containing irritating compounds that can cause contact dermatitis.

The saponins and alkaloids present in the plant's sap can trigger allergic reactions or even phototoxicity when exposed to sunlight.

This leads to symptoms such as redness, itching, and blistering on the skin, making it essential to handle the plant with care to avoid exposure.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Euphorbia pekinensis, also known as Chinese milkweed, triggers allergic reactions due to its high concentration of latex and milky sap.

The sap contains allergenic compounds that can cause an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as itching, redness, and swelling at the site of contact or inhalation.

Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may exacerbate respiratory issues like asthma in sensitive individuals.

3. Induces vomiting

Euphorbia pekinensis, also known as Chinese Milkweed, induces vomiting.

This side effect is attributed to the presence of sapotoxins and saponins in the plant's latex, which can cause gastrointestinal irritation and stimulate the vomiting reflex when ingested or come into contact with the mucous membranes.

The irritating properties of these compounds can trigger a strong response from the body, resulting in nausea and vomiting.

4. Produces severe diarrhea

Euphorbia pekinensis, also known as Chinese Milkweed, produces severe diarrhea when used.

This is due to the presence of saponins and glycosides in its plant parts, which can irritate the digestive system and cause a laxative effect.

The excessive water loss from the body can lead to dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, and discomfort.

5. Increases heart rate rapidly

Euphorbia pekinensis, also known as Chinese Milkweed, increases heart rate rapidly due to its stimulant properties.

The plant contains a number of bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and glycosides, which can stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to an increase in heart rate. Additionally, Euphorbia pekinensis has been shown to have vasoconstrictive effects, causing blood vessels to constrict or narrow, which can further contribute to a rapid heart rate.

This rapid heart rate can be a significant side effect of using Euphorbia pekinensis.

6. Lowers blood pressure suddenly

Euphorbia pekinensis, also known as Chinese milkweed, lowers blood pressure suddenly due to its ability to stimulate the body's natural vasodilators, causing blood vessels to widen and blood pressure to decrease.

Additionally, it contains a compound called euphorbain, which is believed to contribute to its hypotensive effects by blocking the action of certain neurotransmitters that regulate blood pressure.

7. Affects liver function seriously

Euphorbia pekinensis, also known as Chinese milkweed, affects liver function seriously.

This is due to the presence of saponins and phorbol esters in its composition, which are known to cause hepatotoxicity.

The plant's ability to induce oxidative stress and inflammation in the liver tissue contributes to this adverse effect, leading to potential damage or even failure of the organ.

8. Interferes with kidney function

Euphorbia pekinensis, also known as Chinese milkweed, interferes with kidney function due to its potential to cause nephrotoxicity.

The toxic compound present in the plant, euphorbol, can damage renal tubular cells and disrupt normal kidney functioning, leading to impairment of urine production and electrolyte imbalance.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption of Euphorbia pekinensis may exacerbate these effects, potentially resulting in chronic kidney disease.

9. Reduces vision temporarily

Euphorbia pekinensis, also known as Chinese milkweed, reduces vision temporarily.

This is due to its ability to cause irritation and inflammation of the eyes, potentially leading to blurred vision or sensitivity to light.

The sap of the plant contains a compound called euphol, which can irritate the mucous membranes and conjunctiva in the eye, resulting in temporary visual impairment.

10. Causes respiratory distress

Euphorbia pekinensis, also known as Chinese milkweed, causes respiratory distress due to its sap and latex content, which can release irritating compounds when ingested or inhaled.

The sap contains a toxic substance called euphorbin, which can stimulate the mucous membranes in the lungs, leading to coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the plant's spines and thorns can cause accidental inhalation or irritation of the skin and eyes when handling the plant.
