Saussurea oligantha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea oligantha has a rosette of lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves that are typically basal and characterized by a sparsely hairy surface. The plant produces upright flowering stems that are relatively slender and often covered in fine pubescence. Its inflorescences consist of small, few-flowered heads that emerge from the terminal ends of the stems. The individual florets are relatively inconspicuous, which aligns with the specific epithet denoting a low number of flowers. These morphological features are adapted to the high-altitude alpine environments where the species is typically found.
