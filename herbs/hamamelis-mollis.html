Hamamelis mollis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hamamelis mollis?

Hamamelis mollis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, the plant is part of the order Saxifragales and the family Hamamelidaceae. Finally, it is identified under the genus Hamamelis, specifically as the species mollis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hamamelis mollis has deciduous, elliptical to ovate leaves that measure up to 15 centimeters in length and feature finely serrated margins and a soft, pubescent texture. The plant typically reaches a height of 3 to 4 meters, forming a spreading, multi-stemmed shrub. During late winter, it produces fragrant, strap-shaped yellow flowers that emerge from axillary clusters before the foliage appears. These blooms are characterized by long, ribbon-like petals that provide a distinct visual texture. The bark is smooth on young stems but becomes deeply fissured and grayish as the shrub matures.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Hamamelis mollis targets cyclooxygenase enzymes, lipoxygenase, and various inflammatory cytokines to exert its primary medicinal effects.
