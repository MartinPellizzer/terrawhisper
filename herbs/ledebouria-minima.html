What is Ledebouria minima?

Ledebouria minima is a small, bulbous, perennial plant native to southern Africa, belonging to the family Asparagaceae.

It has a subterranean, globular bulb with a diameter of approximately 5-10 mm, and two to four, strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape and measure up to 10 cm in length. The leaves are fleshy, glabrous, and have a glossy texture. Inflorescences are terminal, with a scape measuring up to 10-20 cm in height, bearing a raceme of small, tubular flowers with a white or greenish-white color.

The flowers are borne in the summer months and are pollinated by flies and other small insects.
