Oxystelma esculentum

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxystelma esculentum?

Oxystelma esculentum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Gentianaales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Oxystelma, and its species name, esculentum.

According to a study published by "Scientific reports", Oxystelma esculentum (L.f.) Sm. extract was used for the green synthesis of zinc oxide nanoparticles which exhibited various biological activities. These nanoparticles showed antifungal inhibition rates of 72.3% against Aspergillus niger, 72.2% against Penicillium notatum, and 69.3% against Fusarium oxysporum at 4 mg/mL. The antibacterial activity peaked at a 13.9 mm inhibition zone at 8 mg/mL, while the highest antileishmania l activity was 57.33% at 160 µg/mL. They demonstrated 79% anti-inflammatory activity at 800 µg/mL and 63.11 ± 0.22% alpha-amylase inhibition at 400 µg/mL. Furthermore, the particles showed antioxidant activities including 51.7 ± 1.2% TAC, 42.4 ± 0.02% TRP, and 55.6 ± 1.4% FRSA at 200 µg/mL.
