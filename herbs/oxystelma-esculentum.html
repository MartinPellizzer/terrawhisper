Oxystelma esculentum

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxystelma esculentum?

Oxystelma esculentum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Gentianaales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Oxystelma, and its species name, esculentum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxystelma esculentum has a climbing, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves consisting of three ovate to elliptical leaflets. The foliage features smooth margins and a bright green color, often providing dense ground cover or climbing structures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, typically displaying a yellowish or greenish hue. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruit pods that contain numerous small seeds necessary for reproduction. These morphological features allow the species to effectively colonize moist, shaded environments through rapid vegetative spreading.

What are the pharmacological activities of Oxystelma esculentum?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, anti-diabetic, antimicrobial, and antioxidant pharmacological activities. These biological properties suggest that the plant can effectively combat various pathogens and oxidative stress within a biological system. The presence of bioactive compounds allows it to inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria and fungi. Furthermore, its ability to regulate blood glucose levels demonstrates significant therapeutic potential for managing metabolic disorders. These combined effects highlight the medicinal importance of the species in traditional and modern pharmacology.

As discussed in a study, the Oxystelma esculentum extract was used for the green synthesis of zinc oxide nanoparticles that exhibited various biological activities. These nanoparticles showed antifungal inhibition rates of 72.3% against Aspergillus niger, 72.2% against Penicillium notatum, and 69.3% against Fusarium oxysporum at 4 mg/mL. They demonstrated an antibacterial inhibition zone of 13.9 mm at 8 mg/mL and an antileishmanial activity of 57.33% at 160 µg/mL. The nanoparticles displayed 79% anti-inflammatory activity at 800 µg/mL, 63.11 ± 0.22% alpha-amylase inhibition at 400 µg/mL, and 17.66 ± 0.55% α-glucosidase inhibition at 400 µg/mL. Antioxidant activities included 51.7 ± 1.2% TAC, 42.4 ± 0.02% TRP, and 55.6 ± 1.4% FRSA at 200 µg/mL. Additionally, the hemolytic activity remained low at 1.41 ± 0.22% at 200 µg/mL.
