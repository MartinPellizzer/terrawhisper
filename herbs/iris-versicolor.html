What are the botanical characteristics of Iris versicolor?

Iris versicolor, also known as the blue flag or harlequin blue flag, is a species of iris that exhibits several distinctive botanical characteristics.

The flowers, which bloom from May to July, are borne on stems that can grow up to 60 cm (24 in) tall and feature three drooping petals of a vibrant blue color with darker veining, as well as a three-lobed central column called a perianth tube.

Each flower also has a prominent spur or horn-like structure that is often longer than the flower itself, which contains nectar and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Additionally, Iris versicolor has a rhizomatous growth habit, with the underground stems producing offsets or "pups" that can be separated to create new plants.

Overall, this species of iris is known for its striking blue flowers and distinctive foliage.
