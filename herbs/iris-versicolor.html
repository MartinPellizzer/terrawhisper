Variable Iris

Iris versicolor

Variable Iris (iris versicolor) is a member of the Iridaceae family, traditionally used in various cultural and regional practices. Its plant parts, including leaves and roots, have been historically employed for skin wound healing, treatment of respiratory issues, and as a digestive disorder remedy.

Botanical Identification

Variable Iris, scientifically known as Iris versicolor, belongs to the Iridaceae family. Commonly called Blue Flag or Variable Iris, it is a perennial herb native to wetlands in North America. The plant has rhizomes, sword-shaped leaves, and blue-violet flowers. It is also referred to as Iris florentina in some herbal texts.

Variable Iris Identification.

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of Variable Iris include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins. These compounds work together to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their synergistic interaction enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Phenolic acid
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive and respiratory issues.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Variable Iris (Iris versicolor) should not be used by individuals with known allergies to the iris family. It may cause gastrointestinal upset and is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for prolonged periods.
