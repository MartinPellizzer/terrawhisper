Variable Iris (Iris versicolor)

Variable Iris (Iris versicolor) is a member of the Iridaceae family, native to North America, Central America, and South America. Traditionally, its rhizomes, leaves, and roots have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and native american herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 80 centimeters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface dull green and lower surface pale white, featuring distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Bilaterally symmetrical flowers with six tepals arranged in three pairs, three outer tepals blue to violet with white markings, three inner tepals white with blue veins, clustered in a raceme.
Stems
Elongated, upright growth habit, opposite branching pattern, glabrous surface, presence of distinct nodes and leaf scars.
