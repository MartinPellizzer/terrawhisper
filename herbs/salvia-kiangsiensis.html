Salvia kiangsiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia kiangsiensis?

Salvia kiangsiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Lamiales. This species is a member of the family Lamiaceae, situated within the genus Salvia, and is specifically identified by the species name kiangsiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia kiangsiensis has an erect, perennial herb structure characterized by square stems and opposite, oblong-lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically covered in fine hairs that provide a textured surface. Its inflorescence consists of dense verticillasters arranged in elongated racemes extending from the upper nodes. The flowers are bilabiate in shape, featuring a prominent upper lip and a larger, spreading lower lip.
