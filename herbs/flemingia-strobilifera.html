Flemingia strobilifera

What's the taxonomical classification of Flemingia strobilifera?

Flemingia strobilifera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Flemingia, ultimately reaching its taxonomic designation at the species level as strobilifera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Flemingia strobilifera has erect, woody stems that typically reach a height of 1 to 2 meters and are characterized by pinnately compound leaves. Each leaf consists of several pairs of oval to elliptical leaflets with entire margins and acute tips. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils and contain numerous small, yellowish flowers. These floral structures are often densely packed, giving the spikes a distinct cylindrical appearance. The root system is robust and capable of forming symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in the soil.
