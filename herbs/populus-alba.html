Populus alba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Salicaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Populus and the species alba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Populus alba has deltoid to lanceolate leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and possess a distinct white, felt-like pubescence on the underside. The margins of these leaves are typically irregularly serrated or dentate. Its bark is characterized by a smooth, white to light gray color that becomes deeply fissured and rugged as the tree matures. The branches are often pendulous, giving the canopy a weeping appearance. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in clusters on the catkins during the spring season.
