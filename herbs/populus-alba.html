Populus alba

What's the taxonomical classification of Populus alba?

Populus alba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Salicaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Populus and the species alba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Populus alba has deltoid to lanceolate leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and possess a distinct white, felt-like pubescence on the underside. The margins of these leaves are typically irregularly serrated or dentate. Its bark is characterized by a smooth, white to light gray color that becomes deeply fissured and rugged as the tree matures. The branches are often pendulous, giving the canopy a weeping appearance. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in clusters on the catkins during the spring season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the river valleys and riparian zones of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It typically flourishes in areas where soil moisture is consistent, such as along the banks of streams and floodplains. Its range extends from the Colorado River basin through parts of Texas and into the arid landscapes of the Sonoran Desert. While primarily found in these specific regions, it can also be introduced to other temperate climates for ornamental use. The distribution of this species is strictly limited by its biological dependence on access to groundwater.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Populus alba contains therapeutic compounds found primarily in its bark and leaves that are used to treat inflammatory conditions and fevers. The bark is particularly valued for its high concentration of salicin, which serves as a natural precursor to salicylic acid. Herbalists often prepare decoctions from this bark to alleviate pain and reduce swelling in various parts of the body. The leaves are sometimes utilized in infusions or poultices to soothe skin irritations and promote healing.
