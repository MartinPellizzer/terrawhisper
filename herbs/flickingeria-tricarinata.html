What's the taxonomical classification of Flickingeria tricarinata?

Flickingeria tricarinaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. This specific plant is a representative of the family Orchidaceae, situated within the genus Flickingeria, and is identified by its unique species name, tricarinata.

    What are the herbal preparations of Flickingeria tricarinata?

    Flickingeria tricarinaata is prepared as a decoction, infusion, or topical poultice depending on the intended medicinal application. Alternatively, steeping the leaves in hot water produces a milder infusion suitable for more frequent consumption. For skin conditions, the plant material can be crushed into a thick paste and applied directly to the affected area as a poultice.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Flickingeria tricarinata can cause skin irritation, localized allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various phytochemicals that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon physical touch. When the foliage or stems are handled, the released compounds can lead to redness, itching, or swelling on the skin surface. If accidentally swallowed, it might cause nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain depending on the amount consumed. There is currently limited clinical data regarding its long-term toxicity in humans.
