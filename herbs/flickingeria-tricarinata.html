Flickingeria tricarinaata has an erect, annual herbaceous habit characterized by stems that are often branched and leaves that possess three distinct longitudinal veins. Small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in axillary clusters, presenting a delicate structure typical of the family.       What are the herbal preparations of Flickingeria tricarinata?

      Flickingeria tricarinaata is prepared as a decoction, infusion, or topical poultice depending on the intended medicinal application. The dried aerial parts of the plant are typically boiled in water to create a concentrated decoction for internal use.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Flickingeria tricarinata can cause skin irritation, localized allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various phytochemicals that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon physical touch. When the foliage or stems are handled, the released compounds can lead to redness, itching, or swelling on the skin surface. If accidentally swallowed, it might cause nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain depending on the amount consumed. There is currently limited clinical data regarding its long-term toxicity in humans.
