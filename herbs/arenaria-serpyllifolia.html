Arenaria serpyllifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Arenaria serpyllifolia?

Arenaria serpyllifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Caryophyllales, it is further categorized into the family Caryophyllaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Arenaria, and its specific species name, serpyllifolia.

What are the therapeutic actions of Arenaria serpyllifolia?

What side-effects this plant can have?

Arenaria serpyllifolia can cause adverse reactions such as skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested. While specific clinical data on human toxicity is limited, many plants in the Caryophyllaceae family contain saponins that may cause stomach upset. Contact with the leaves or stems might trigger redness or itching in individuals with sensitive skin. It is essential to avoid consuming any part of the plant without professional guidance to prevent unknown systemic effects. Monitoring for unexpected rashes or digestive changes is recommended if accidental exposure occurs.
