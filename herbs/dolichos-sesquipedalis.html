Dolichos sesquipedalis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dolichos sesquipedalis has a climbing stem structure characterized by elongated, slender vines and trifoliate leaves consisting of three distinct leaflets. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, typically displaying a pale or yellowish hue. The reproductive organs are centered within the floral structure, supporting the development of small, elongated seed pods. These pods eventually mature to contain the seeds essential for the plant's propagation.
