What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salviva bowleyana has a rhizomatous perennial habit featuring basal rosettes of deeply lobed, rugose, and hairy leaves that typically measure several centimeters in length. From these basal clusters, upright flowering stems emerge to support terminal verticillasters of tubular, bilabiate flowers. These blossoms are characteristically blue to violet and are surrounded by protective calyces.
