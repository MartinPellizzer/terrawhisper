What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia bowleyana?

Salvia bowleyaana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Lamiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Lamiaceae, placing it within the genus Salvia, and it is specifically identified by the species name bowlheyana.

What are the therapeutic actions of Salvia bowleyana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including respiratory issues, digestive disturbances, and inflammatory skin conditions. Historically, its medicinal properties have been applied to soothe coughing and congestion in the lungs. It is also frequently utilized to calm stomach upset and support overall gut health. Furthermore, the plant possesses antimicrobial qualities that can aid in the healing of minor skin irritations. These applications stem from the natural bioactive compounds found within its leaves.
