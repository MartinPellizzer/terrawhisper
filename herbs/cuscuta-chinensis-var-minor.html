Dodder (Cuscuta Chinensis Var. Minor)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Cuscuta chinensis var. minor.

Dodder, scientifically known as Cuscuta chinensis var. minor, is a parasitic flowering plant commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine as a medicinal herb and adaptogen. It is believed to support overall vitality and balance within the body, offering primary benefits such as enhancing immunity, reducing inflammation, and promoting liver health. Traditionally, it has been used in Chinese herbal formulations to treat ailments like fever, cough, and digestive issues, and it holds cultural significance in Ayurvedic and indigenous healing practices as well. In modern wellness contexts, Dodder is increasingly incorporated into supplements and herbal teas for its purported adaptogenic properties and stress-relieving effects. One of its unique features is the presence of bioactive compounds like alkaloids and flavonoids, which contribute to its therapeutic potential, and it has a long history dating back thousands of years in traditional healing systems.
