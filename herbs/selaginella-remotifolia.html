Selaginella remotifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Selaginella remotifolia has small, herbaceous, creeping rhizomes that produce narrow, linear, and sparsely arranged microphylls with distinctively remote spacing between the leaf bases. The plant exhibits a delicate, branched structure where the stems are relatively slender and often grow in a spreading manner across the substrate. Each individual leaf is characterized by its reduced size and a simple, non-overlapping arrangement that distinguishes it from more densely foliated species. These minute leaves are typically arranged in multiple rows along the stem, contributing to the plant's open and airy appearance. The overall morphological habit is adapted for colonizing moist, shaded environments through its creeping growth form.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Selaginella remotifolia can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many species in the Selaginella genus contain alkaloids that may trigger localized dermatitis upon contact. Ingesting parts of the plant could potentially lead to nausea or stomach cramps due to these chemical compounds. Individuals with sensitive skin or known allergies to fern-like vegetation should exercise extreme caution. It is best to avoid direct skin contact and ensure the plant is not consumed in any form.
