Solanum surattense

What's the taxonomical classification of Solanum surattense?

Solanum surattense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its lineage through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Solanales. This species is a member of the family Solanaceae, falling under the widely recognized genus Solanum, and is specifically identified by its unique species name, surattense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Solanum surattense has an erect, perennial shrubby habit characterized by woody stems and densely pubescent foliage. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate with entire or slightly wavy margins and a velvety texture caused by fine hairs. Small, actinomorphic flowers emerge in clusters, featuring five distinct petals and prominent yellow anthers. The fruit develops as a fleshy berry that transitions in color as it matures. These morphological traits distinguish it within its specific taxonomic group in tropical regions.

What are the pharmacological activities of Solanum surattense?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its documented anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidative, antibacterial, antimicrobial, and antifungal activities. Scientific investigations reveal that its bioactive compounds effectively combat various pathogenic microorganisms and fungal strains. These chemical constituents also work to neutralize free radicals, providing a robust anti-oxidative defense for cellular structures. Furthermore, the plant's ability to reduce swelling and pain highlights its therapeutic value as an anti-inflammatory agent. Such diverse biological properties suggest that Solanum surattense serves as a promising candidate for developing new medicinal treatments.

According to a study published by "Pharmaceuticaals (Basel, Switzerland)", mixtures of different parts of Solanum surattense in water-ethanol have shown in vitro and/or in vivo antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, anti-tumoral, hepatoprotective, and larvicidal activities. Among 338 isolated metabolites, 51 were identified and biologically tested, with antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antitumoral being the most reported activities. The plant's metabolites include 137 (40.53%) terpenoids, 56 (16.56%) phenol derivatives, and 52 (15.38%) lipids. Clinical trials in humans using the whole plant extract demonstrated its efficacy as an anti-asthmatic agent. High potency is reflected in biologically active metabolites such as solamargine, solanidine, solasodine, solasonine, tomatidine, xanthosaponin A-B, dioscin, lupeol, and stigmasterol.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive steroidal glycoalkaloids including solamargine, solanidine, solasodine, solasonine, and tomatidine. These specific medicinal compounds serve as potent defensive secondary metabolites that exhibit various pharmacological properties. The chemical structures of solanidine and solasodine act as aglycone bases for many of the other complex molecules found within the species. Research suggests that these alkaloids may possess significant antimicrobial and antiparasitic activities. Understanding the concentration of solasonine and tomatidine is essential for evaluating the plant's overall therapeutic potential.

According to a study published by "Pharmaceuticals (Basel, Switzerland)", a total of 338 metabolites of various chemical classes were isolated from Solanum surattense. These include 137 (40.53%) terpenoids, 56 (16.56%) phenol derivatives, and 52 (15.38%) lipids. Among these metabolites, 51 were identified and biologically tested, with antioxidant, anti-inflammarotory, and antitumoal activities being the most reported. The most biologically active metabolites with high potency are mostly steroidal alkaloids and triterpenoids, such as solamargine, solanidine, solasodine, solasonine, tomatidine, xanthosaponin A-B, dioscin, lupeol, and stigmasterol. According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Solanum surattense is an herbal plant used in many regions of Asia to treat many disorders including various types of cancer. Previous in vitro studies revealed the medicinal importance of Solanum surattense against hepatocellular carcinoma, which is one of the most common malignant liver tumors. The plant appears to act on hepatocellular carcinoma by modulating different molecular functions, many biological processes, and potential targets implicated in 11 different pathways. Through network pharmacology, the study explored the multi-targeted mechanisms of Solanum surattense against this condition. The active ingredients of Solanum surattense were found to influence potential genes such as TNF, EGFR, mTOR, BCL2L1, ER, HRAS, HIF1-alpha, and AKT1.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe physiological and neurological reactions including nausea, vomiting, dizziness, abdominal pain, diarrhea, hallucinations, confusion, and tachycardia. Ingestion of the toxic components often leads to acute gastrointestinal distress characterized by intense abdominal pain and frequent diarrhea. The chemical compounds may also trigger central nervous system disturbances such as disorientation and vivid hallucinations. Furthermore, the heart rate can increase significantly, resulting in dangerous episodes of tachycardia. These combined symptoms indicate a serious risk of poisoning if any part of the plant is consumed.
