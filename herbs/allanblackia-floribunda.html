Allanblackia floribunda

What's the taxonomical classification of Allanblackia floribunda?

Allanblackia floribunda belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Malpighiales. It is further categorized into the family Clusiaceae, falling under the genus Allanblackia, and is specifically identified by its species name, floribunda.

What are the pharmacological activities of Allanblackia floribunda?

These combined biological effects highlight the therapeutic importance of Allanblackia floribuanda in medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, the root bark extract of Allanblackia floribunda exhibits moderate tumor reducing activity and demonstrated antimycobacterial and antitumor activities reported for the first time. In the DPPH radical scavenging test, all studied samples scavenged more than 50% of the free radical, with compound 3 showing the best inhibitory activity with an IC50 of 49.08 µg/mL. The extract and compound 2 were active on M. tuberculosis, and compounds 1 to 3 prevented the growth of Mycobacterium smegmatis. For antimicrobial investigations, the lowest MIC value for the extract was 9.76 μg/mL against Enterobacter aerogenes. Additionally, a corresponding value of 4.88 µg/mL was obtained with compound 2 on Trichophyton rubrum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds morelloflavone, 7'-O-glucoside of morelloflavone, volkensiflavonone, fukugiside, and stigmasterol. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's complex biochemical profile and potential therapeutic properties. The presence of various flavones and glucosides suggests significant bioactive potential within its tissues. Additionally, the inclusion of sterols like stigmasterol plays a role in the plant's structural and chemical makeup. Together, these diverse compounds form the basis for studying the plant's pharmacological applications.

As discussed in a study, the stem bark ethanol extract of Allanblackia floribunda contains medicinal compounds that exhibit vasorelaxant and aphrodisiac properties. The crude ethanolic extract induced relaxation on aorta rings with an EC50 of 11±2μg/mL, while Morelloflavone displayed a similar activity with an EC50 of 42±6μg/mL. At the maximum assessable concentration of 90μg/mL, the vasodilatory percentages were 30±8 for Fukugiside, 24±6 for Spicataside, 33±4 for the combination of Morelloflavone and Volkensiflavone, and 47±1 for Volkensiflavone. Among the 9 parameters evaluated regarding male sexual behaviour, only the crude ethanolic extract and Fukugiside showed activity. These results involving the identified secondary metabolites Fukugiside, Morelloflavone, and Volkensiflavone support the traditional use of the plant.
