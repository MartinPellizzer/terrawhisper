Rubus komarovii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus komarovii has arching, woody stems that are densely covered in sharp, recurved prickles and fine pubescence. The leaves are typically trifoliate or pentafoliate, featuring serrated margins and a textured, rugose surface. Small, white or pale pink flowers emerge from the axils, characterized by numerous stamens surrounding a central cluster of carpels. The plant produces aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep red or black color upon ripening. These morphological traits help distinguish the species within its specific regional habitat.
