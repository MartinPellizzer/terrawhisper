10 Possible Side Effects Of Maranta Arundinacea

Maranta arundinacea has some side effects when used improperly, such as digestive issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can occur due to excessive consumption, individual sensitivities, or poor preparation methods.

For instance, consuming large quantities may lead to stomach pain and diarrhea, potentially disrupting daily routines and work schedules.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Maranta arundinacea causes allergic reactions.

The allergenic properties of this plant are attributed to its saponins and glycosides, which can trigger an immune response in sensitive individuals. When exposed to these compounds, the body's defense mechanisms may overreact, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

In severe cases, anaphylaxis or asthma attacks may occur.

2. Triggers diarrhea

Maranta arundinacea triggers diarrhea due to its high concentration of iridoid glycosides and flavonoids.

These compounds can stimulate bowel movements and increase gut motility, leading to loose stools and frequent bowel movements. As the body adjusts to these compounds, it may take time for the digestive system to normalize, resulting in diarrhea.

The severity and duration of this side effect vary depending on individual tolerance and dosage.

3. Induces stomach cramps

Maranta arundinacea induces stomach cramps due to its high content of anthraquinones and saponins.

These compounds can cause irritation in the digestive tract, leading to abdominal pain, cramping, and discomfort.

As Maranta arundinacea is used traditionally for various medicinal purposes, it is essential to be aware of these potential side effects, especially for those who are new to using this herb.

4. Produces nausea and vomiting

Maranta arundinacea produces nausea and vomiting due to its high concentration of saponins, which can cause gastrointestinal irritation.

The bitter taste and chemical composition of the plant's extracts may stimulate the vomiting center in the brain, leading to unwanted symptoms.

Additionally, Maranta arundinacea may interact with other medications or substances, exacerbating nausea and vomiting.

5. Affects blood pressure

Maranta arundinacea affects blood pressure due to its potential interaction with certain medications that regulate blood pressure.

The herb's flavonoids and saponins may alter the body's response to these drugs, causing blood pressure fluctuations.

Additionally, Maranta arundinacea can increase heart rate and cardiac output, further contributing to changes in blood pressure levels.

6. Interferes with thyroid function

Maranta arundinacea interferes with thyroid function due to its potential to disrupt the balance of thyroid hormones in the body.

This may be attributed to the presence of certain compounds such as saponins and flavonoids, which have been shown to inhibit thyroid peroxidase activity and potentially lead to decreased production of thyroxine (T4) and triiodothyronine (T3).

As a result, individuals using Maranta arundinacea may experience changes in their thyroid function, potentially leading to hormonal imbalances.

7. Inhibits bone growth

Maranta arundinacea inhibits bone growth due to its high content of saponins and flavonoids.

These compounds can disrupt the normal functioning of osteoblasts, leading to impaired bone formation and density.

Additionally, Maranta arundinacea may also interfere with the regulation of calcium metabolism, further exacerbating the inhibition of bone growth.

8. Increases risk of bleeding

Maranta arundinacea increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and anticoagulant properties.

These effects are attributed to the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins in the plant's extract.

As a result, users may experience an increased susceptibility to bruising, hemorrhage, or other bleeding-related issues.

9. Disrupts menstrual cycles

Maranta arundinacea disrupts menstrual cycles due to its ability to alter hormonal balance in the body.

The herb is believed to interact with estrogen receptors, potentially leading to changes in ovulation and menstruation patterns.

Additionally, Maranta arundinacea contains compounds that may stimulate or inhibit the release of hormones responsible for regulating the menstrual cycle, resulting in irregularities and disruptions.

10. Causes skin rashes

Maranta arundinacea causes skin rashes due to its natural compounds and oils, which can irritate the skin and cause an allergic reaction.

The essential fatty acids and antioxidants present in Maranta arundinacea may trigger a hypersensitive response in some individuals, leading to redness, itching, and swelling on the affected area.

Additionally, the plant's saponins and phenolic compounds can further exacerbate skin irritation, resulting in uncomfortable skin rashes.
