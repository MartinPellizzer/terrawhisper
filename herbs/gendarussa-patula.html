Gendarussa (Gendarussa Patula)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Gendarussa patula.

Gendarussa patula, commonly known as the Java bean or *Strychnos nux-vomica*, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is widely used in traditional medicine for its potent medicinal properties. The plant contains various alkaloids, including strychnine and brucine, which have been historically utilized for their stimulant and analgesic effects, though they are also highly toxic in larger doses. In traditional Indonesian medicine, Gendarussa patula is employed to treat ailments such as inflammation, pain, and parasitic infections, often in the form of tinctures or decoctions. However, due to its toxic nature, the use of Gendarussa patula requires careful preparation and administration to avoid adverse effects. Despite its risks, ongoing research continues to explore its potential therapeutic applications in modern pharmacology.
