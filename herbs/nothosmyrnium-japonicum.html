What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nothosmyrnium japonicum has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in terminal panicles. The stem is slender and can reach heights of approximately 10 to 30 centimeters depending on the environment. Its inflorescence features multiple small, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the upper nodes of the plant.
