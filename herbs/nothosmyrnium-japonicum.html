Nothosmyrnium japonicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Nothosmyrnium japonicum?

Nothosmyrnium japonicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Apiales. This plant is further categorized into the family Apiaceae, which defines its botanical relationships. Within this group, it is identified by the genus Nothosmyrnium and is specifically designated by the species epithet japonicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nothosmyrnium japonicum has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in terminal panicles.

The leaves typically consist of several pairs of leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape with serrated margins. The stem is slender and can reach heights of approximately 10 to 30 centimeters depending on the environment. Its inflorescence features multiple small, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the upper nodes of the plant.

The root system is composed of slender rhizomes that allow the species to spread through woodland habitats.
