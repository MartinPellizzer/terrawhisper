Holy Basil

Ocimum tenuiflorum

Holy Basil (Ocimum tenuiflorum) is a revered medicinal plant native to the Indian subcontinent, commonly known as Tulsi, which is widely used in Ayurvedic medicine for its adaptogenic properties and ability to support overall health, particularly in reducing stress and improving respiratory conditions.

Botanical Identification

Holy Basil, scientifically known as Ocimum tenuiflorum, belongs to the Lamiaceae family. Commonly called tulsi in India, it is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent. It features aromatic leaves, white flowers, and a fibrous root system. Synonyms include Ocimum sanctum and Basilica, reflecting its revered status in traditional medicine.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Lamiales
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Ocimum
  • Species: Ocimum tenuiflorum

Holy Basil Identification.

Active Compounds

Holy Basil contains essential oil, flavonoids, and tannins as its primary active compounds. These components work synergistically to modulate stress responses, enhance cognitive function, and exhibit anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oil provides aromatic and antimicrobial benefits, while flavonoids and tannins contribute to its adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin

Holy Basil Actions.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions like colds and coughs.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and to treat inflammation.

Essential oil: Used for aromatherapy and to relieve stress and anxiety.

Safety Profile

Holy Basil is generally considered safe when used in typical medicinal doses. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding women due to potential risks to the fetus or infant.
