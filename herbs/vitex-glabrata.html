Vitex glabrata

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitex glabrata?

Vitex glabrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Lamiales, it is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Vitex and the species glabrata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitex glabrata has opposite, decussate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, featuring a lanceolate to ovate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and a glabrous, light green surface. The plant is a woody shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. Its inflorescences consist of terminal panicles containing small, tubular flowers that transition in color from pale blue to deep violet. The fruit is a small, fleshy, globose drupe that turns dark purple or black when fully ripe. These botanical characteristics distinguish it from other species within the Lamiaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Caribbean islands, specifically found throughout the Bahamas, Cuba, Jamaica, and Hispaniola. It typically occupies coastal habitats where it can thrive in sandy soils and salty environments. Within these tropical regions, the species is often located near shorelines or in low-lying scrublands. Its distribution is closely tied to maritime climates that provide consistent warmth and humidity. Some populations may also be found in small, scattered pockets across adjacent coastal areas of the southeastern United States.

What are the pharmacological activities of Vitex glabrata?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including HIV-1 RT inhibition, antiestrogenic, antiproliferative, antipsoriatic, and α-amylase inhibitory activity. Its ability to inhibit HIV-1 reverse transcriptase suggests potential utility in managing viral replication processes. The extract also demonstrates antiestrogenic properties and antiproliferative effects that may impact hormone-sensitive cell growth. Furthermore, its antipsoriatic qualities offer therapeutic possibilities for managing inflammatory skin conditions. Finally, the observed α-amylase inhibitory activity indicates a role in regulating carbohydrate digestion and managing blood glucose levels.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the water extract of the Vitex glabrata branch demonstrated HIV-1 RT inhibition activity. This specific extract showed an HIV-1 RT inhibition ratio (% IR) higher than 90% when tested at a concentration of 200 microg/ml. The study focused on testing water and 80% ethanol extracts of 20 Thai medicinal plants used to treat AIDS. Among the tested plants, Vitex glabrata was identified as having significant inhibitory effects at the specified concentration.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for lactation suppression, insufficient breastfeeding, sexual enhancement, diabetes mellitus, and HIV-1. It is traditionally employed to help women stop the production of breast milk or manage issues related to low milk supply. Some studies suggest it may also play a role in improving sexual function and libido. Additionally, research has explored its potential impact on regulating blood sugar levels in patients with diabetes mellitus. There is also scientific interest regarding its possible effects on the replication of HIV-1 in certain biological contexts.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", the leaves of Vitex glabrata are investigated for their potential antiestrogenic properties. This plant belongs to the same genus as the chaste tree, which is traditionally used to support lactation. The leaves of Vitex glabrata afforded the new compounds khainaoside A (14), khainaoside B (15), and khainaoside C (16), together with 6 known compounds. Among the isolates tested for biological activity using the estrogen-responsive human breast cancer cell lines MCF-7 and T47D, khainaoside A (14) showed one of the most potent inhibitory effects on estrogen-enhanced cell proliferation. These findings highlight the possibility of using Vitex glabrata for its antiestrogenic properties.
