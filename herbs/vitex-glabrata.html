Vitex glabrata

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitex glabrata?

Vitex glabrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Lamiales, it is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Vitex and the species glabrata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitex glabrata has opposite, decussate leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, featuring a lanceolate to ovate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and a glabrous, light green surface. The plant is a woody shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. Its inflorescences consist of terminal panicles containing small, tubular flowers that transition in color from pale blue to deep violet. The fruit is a small, fleshy, globose drupe that turns dark purple or black when fully ripe. These botanical characteristics distinguish it from other species within the Lamiaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Caribbean islands, specifically found throughout the Bahamas, Cuba, Jamaica, and Hispaniola. It typically occupies coastal habitats where it can thrive in sandy soils and salty environments. Within these tropical regions, the species is often located near shorelines or in low-lying scrublands. Its distribution is closely tied to maritime climates that provide consistent warmth and humidity. Some populations may also be found in small, scattered pockets across adjacent coastal areas of the southeastern United States.

What are the pharmacological activities of Vitex glabrata?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including HIV-1 RT inhibition, antiestrogenic, antiproliferative, antipsoriatic, and galactogogue properties. Its ability to inhibit HIV-1 reverse transcriptase suggests potential applications in managing viral replication. The extract also demonstrates antiestrogenic effects that may influence hormone-sensitive processes. Furthermore, its antiproliferative and antipsoriatic actions provide a basis for treating skin conditions and abnormal cell growth. Finally, the plant serves as a galactogogue to support lactation in nursing mothers.

As discussed in a study, the water extract of the Vitex glabrata branch demonstrated HIV-1 RT inhibition. This extract showed an HIV-1 RT inhibition ratio higher than 90% at a concentration of 200 microg/ml. These results were obtained through testing water and 80% ethanol extracts of 20 Thai medicinal plants. The study focused on the inhibitory activities of these extracts against HIV type 1 reverse transcriptase.

What health problems is this plant used for?

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause nausea, dizziness, headache, gastrointestinal upset, and menstrual irregularities in some individuals. These adverse reactions may manifest as varying degrees of physical discomfort following ingestion. Some users might experience mild gastrointestinal upset or persistent nausea during treatment. Neurological symptoms such as dizziness and headache are also reported possibilities. Furthermore, because of its hormonal influence, users should monitor for unexpected menstrual irregularities.
