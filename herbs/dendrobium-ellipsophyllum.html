What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium ellipsophyllum?

Dendrobium ellipsophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further classified under the order Asparagales. It is a member of the family Orchidaceae, which places it within the genus Dendrobium, and it is specifically identified by its species name, ellipsophyllum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium ellipsophyllum has pendulous, sympodial stems that grow as elongated, slender pseudobulbs reaching lengths of up to 40 centimeters. These stems are characterized by a thin, fleshy texture and bear small, widely spaced leaves. The leaves are distinctly elliptical in shape, possessing a coriaceous consistency and a pointed apex. Its inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers that typically emerge from the upper nodes of the stem. The floral structure features petals and sepals that are often pale in color with subtle markings.
