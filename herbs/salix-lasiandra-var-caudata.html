Salix lasiandra var. caudata

Salix lasiandra var. caudata, also known as Caudata willow, is a variety of the Pacific willow tree (Salix lasiandra), which is native to the western United States and Canada.

This plant has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly in Native American medicine, due to its bark containing salicin, a compound similar to aspirin, which provides pain-relieving and anti-inflammatory effects.

The health benefits of Caudata willow include relieving headaches, fever, and rheumatism, as well as acting as a natural anti-inflammatory and antiseptic.

The herbal preparations of Salix lasiandra var. caudata typically involve drying the bark and making it into a tea, tincture, or salve, which can be used to treat various ailments.
