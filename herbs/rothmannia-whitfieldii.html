What's the taxonomical classification of Rothmannia whitfieldii?

Rothmannia whitfieldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Gentianales and falls under the family Rubiaceae. At the most specific levels of its biological classification, it is identified by the genus Rothmannia and the species whitfieldii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rothmannia whitfieldii has woody, evergreen shrub or small tree structures characterized by oppositely arranged, elliptical leaves with smooth margins and a glossy texture.

The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color on the upper surface and a slightly paler hue underneath. Its flowers are large, trumpet-shaped, and creamy white, often featuring delicate longitudinal stripes that guide pollinators. These blooms emerge from terminal clusters and possess long, slender tubes that transition into flared lobes.

      Rothmannia whitfieldii can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or digestive distress if ingested. Careful handling is recommended to avoid these potential adverse reactions.
