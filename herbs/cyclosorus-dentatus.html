Cyclosorus dentatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyclosorus dentatus has a distinctive morphology characterized by deeply serrated leaf margins and a robust, woody stem structure. The foliage consists of thick, lanceolate leaves that feature prominent, tooth-like projections along their edges. These teeth are arranged in a regular, rhythmic pattern that defines the plant's silhouette. Fine trichomes often cover the underside of the leaves, providing a textured surface. The vascular system is highly visible through the translucent layers of the leaf tissue.
