Medicinal Parts of pinus sylvestris

Pinus sylvestris has many medicinal parts, such as the resin, needles, and cones.

The cones contain terpene acids like abietic acid, which has been traditionally used for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Possible Side Effects of pinus sylvestris

Pinus sylvestris can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing allergic reactions, skin irritation, and respiratory issues in individuals sensitive to pine or coniferous trees.

Ingesting large quantities can lead to gastrointestinal problems, including nausea, diarrhea, and stomach pain.

Excessive consumption may also interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, resulting in adverse health effects.

Furthermore, using Pinus sylvestris improperly can also cause dizziness, headaches, and fatigue due to its potential impact on the central nervous system.
