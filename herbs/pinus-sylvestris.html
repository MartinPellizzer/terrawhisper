Pinus Sylvestris

Pinus sylvestris, commonly known as the Scots pine, has been traditionally used for its various medicinal properties.

The resin and needles of the tree contain compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects, making them useful in treating respiratory infections and skin conditions. In traditional medicine, Scots pine is often employed to alleviate symptoms of asthma, bronchitis, and other respiratory ailments due to its expectorant properties. The essential oils extracted from the pine are also used in aromatherapy to promote relaxation and ease stress.

Additionally, recent studies suggest that certain compounds from Pinus sylvestris may have potential in supporting immune function and reducing oxidative stress in the body.
