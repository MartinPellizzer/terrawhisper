Scleria racemosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Scleria racemosa?

Scleria racemosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Cyperaceae. Finally, its taxonomic position is completed within the genus Scleria, identifying it specifically as the species racemosa.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Scleria racemosa contains high concentrations of essential oils primarily composed of sesquiterpenes such as patchouli alcohol, alpha-patchoulene, and beta-patchoulene. These chemical constituents provide the plant with its distinct earthy aroma and significant biological activity. The presence of these terpenoids contributes to the plant's known antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Research suggests that these compounds can also exhibit sedative effects when utilized in traditional medicinal practices. Consequently, the specific ratio of these volatile oils determines the therapeutic efficacy of the plant extract.

What are the therapeutic actions of Scleria racemosa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, skin infections, respiratory ailments, and various bacterial or fungal infections. Its resin contains high concentrations of sesquiterpenes that provide potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Historically, practitioners have utilized the extract to alleviate symptoms of eczema, psoriasis, and other dermatological irritations. It is also frequently applied in traditional medicine to soothe sore throats and manage coughs. These therapeutic applications stem from the plant's ability to inhibit the growth of specific pathogens.
