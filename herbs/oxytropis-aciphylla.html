Oxytropis aciphylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxytropis aciphylla?

Oxytropis aciphylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is classified under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, falling specifically into the genus Oxytropis, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name aciphylla.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Oxytropis aciphylla can cause severe poisoning in livestock including respiratory distress, neurological impairment, and death due to its high concentration of toxic alkaloids. These chemical compounds primarily affect the central nervous system of grazing animals such as horses, cattle, and sheep. Symptoms often manifest as muscle tremors, staggering, or a complete inability to stand. In some cases, animals may experience sudden death following the ingestion of even small amounts of the plant. Because the toxicity is potent, careful management of grazing lands is necessary to prevent fatal accidents.
