What is Eleocharis fuscopurpurea?

Eleocharis fuscopurpurea is a perennial rhizomatous sedge belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dark brown to purple-colored culms and leaves, typically 1-6 mm long, which are often shorter than the 3-20 mm long spikelets. The inflorescence is a solitary spike, usually 5-25 cm long, with the lower spikelets often sterile and the upper ones fertile. The plant grows in wet environments, including marshes, fens, and shallow water, and is native to North America.

The species can be distinguished from other Eleocharis species by its unique combination of leaf and inflorescence morphology.
