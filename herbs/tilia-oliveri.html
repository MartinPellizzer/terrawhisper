What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tilia oliveri has ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins and an acuminate apex, growing on alternate arrangement along its woody stems. Its flowers are small, cream-colored, and clustered in axillary cymes, often accompanied by a characteristic leafy bract. The fruit consists of small nutlets contained within a dry, indehiscent capsule. This species is further distinguished by its relatively short internodes and sturdy, brownish bark.
