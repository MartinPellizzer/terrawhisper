Corydalis delavayi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis delaavayi has a herbaceous perennial structure characterized by deeply lobed, pinnatifid leaves and zygomorphic flowers that feature a distinct, elongated nectar spur. The foliage typically presents a soft, bluish-green hue and is arranged in a basal rosette pattern during the early stages of growth. Its flowering stems bear delicate blooms that transition from pale yellow to a soft lavender or cream color. The reproductive organs are tucked within the specialized petal structure, which serves to facilitate specific pollination mechanisms. Each plant eventually develops a small, capsule-like fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Corydalis delavayi contains a diverse array of isoquinoline alkaloids, most notably protopine, corydaline, and dehydrocorydaline. These bioactive compounds are primarily concentrated within the plant's rhizomes and serve as the basis for its traditional pharmacological applications. Many of these alkaloids exhibit significant analgesic, sedative, and antitussive properties when processed correctly. Research indicates that these chemical constituents may also influence the central nervous system to alleviate pain and promote relaxation. The complex chemical profile of the plant makes it a subject of interest for developing new treatments for neurological and respiratory conditions.

What side effects this plant can have?

The most dangerous complication involves the suppression of breathing patterns, which requires immediate medical attention.
