Clematis subumbellata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis subumbellata has slender, climbing stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are typically arranged in groups of three or more. The foliage often exhibits a slightly hairy texture on the underside of the blades. Its flowers are characterized by small, inconspicuous petals that emerge from terminal clusters. These blooms are often followed by decorative, feathery seed heads known as achenes. The plant utilizes twisting petioles to scramble over surrounding vegetation for structural support.
