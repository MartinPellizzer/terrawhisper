Clematis subumbellata

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis subumbellata?

Clematis subumbellata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a representative of the family Ranunculaceae, specifically situated within the genus Clematis, and is identified by the unique species designation subumbellata.

      What are the herbal preparations of Clematis subumbellata?

      Clematis subumbellata is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant parts in water to extract active compounds. This method is preferred for the tougher, woody structures of the plant to ensure maximum potency. Some practitioners also utilize dried powdered forms of the plant for topical applications or inclusion in capsules. Infusions can be made for the softer leaves, though decoctions remain the most common traditional technique. These various preparations allow for different methods of administration depending on the intended therapeutic use.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis subumbellaata can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that acts as a potent irritant to the mucous membranes and skin. Contact with the foliage or stems may lead to redness, itching, or blistering in sensitive individuals. If swallowed, the toxins can trigger nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. These physiological reactions make handling the plant with gloves and avoiding consumption essential for safety.
