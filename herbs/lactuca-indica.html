Lactuca indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Lactuca indica?

Lactuca indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Asterales. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is part of the genus Lactuca, and is specifically identified by the species name indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lactuca indica has an erect, annual herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and features a milky white latex sap. The leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, displaying a lanceolate to oblong shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Its inflorescence consists of small, bright yellow flower heads arranged in a corymbose cyme. The individual florets are discoid, leading to small, cylindrical achenes that are often topped with a pappus of fine hairs. These morphological characteristics allow the plant to thrive in disturbed soils and open habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to a vast range spanning across much of tropical and subtropical Asia, including countries like India, China, Thailand, and Vietnam. It thrives primarily in warm climates where moisture is abundant during the growing season. Beyond its native Asian territories, it has been introduced to various other regions through human activity and natural seed dispersal. It is frequently found growing in disturbed soils, roadsides, and agricultural fields throughout these diverse landscapes. This wide distribution allows it to establish itself in many different ecological niches across the continent.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Lactuca indica contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its entire leafy green structure, the stems containing milky latex, and its roots. The bitter milky sap extracted from the stems is frequently used to treat inflammation and digestive issues. The leaves are often prepared as a decoction or poultice to reduce fever and soothe skin irritations. Additionally, the roots are harvested for their specific properties in addressing various internal ailments. What are the pharmacological activities of Lactuca indica?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antidiabetic, antioxidant, and hepatoprotective properties. Its antioxidant capacity helps neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress within various biological systems. The anti-inflammatory effects assist in managing swelling and pain by modulating specific immune responses. Furthermore, its antidiabetic qualities aid in regulating blood glucose levels, while its hepatoprotective nature provides essential defense for liver cells against toxic damage.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds apiagenin, kaempferol, lactucain A, lactucaide, and luteolins. These specific chemical constituents are known for providing various therapeutic properties to the organism. Apiagenin and kaempferol are flavonoids that often contribute to anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. The presence of lactucain A and lactucaide suggests unique sesquiterpene lactone activities within the plant's profile. Furthermore, the luteolins present in the specimen assist in a range of biological functions.

As discussed in a study, Lactuca indica contains three novel sesquiterpene lactones identified as lactucain A (1), lactucain B (2), and lactucain C (3). The plant also yields a new furofuran lignan known as lactucaside (4). In addition to these, nine known compounds were isolated, including 11beta,13-dihydrolactucin, cichoriosides B, quercetin, quercetin 3-O-glucoside, rutin, apiigenin, luteolin, luteolin 7-O-glucuronide, and chlorogenic acid. Among these specific constituents, lactucain C (3) and lactucaside (4) demonstrated significant antidiabetic activity.
