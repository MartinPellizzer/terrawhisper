What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hedyotis effusa has a sprawling or decumbent growth habit characterized by slender, procumbent stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are typically simple, arranged oppositely along the stems, and possess an elliptical to lanceolate shape with entire margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often presenting in small clusters or solitary arrangements. The reproductive structures include tiny capsules that contain numerous minute seeds for dispersal. This morphological structure allows the plant to effectively colonize disturbed ground through vegetative spreading.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Hedyotis effusa can cause severe skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and localized swelling if the plant's sap comes into contact with human tissue. Individuals sensitive to its chemical compounds may experience intense redness and itching at the site of exposure. If the sap accidentally enters the eyes, it can lead to painful inflammation and temporary vision impairment. Ingestion of any part of this plant is considered toxic and may result in gastrointestinal distress or more systemic complications. Always use protective gloves when handling this species to mitigate these dermatological risks.
