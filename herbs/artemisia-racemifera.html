Russian Wormwood (Artemisia Racemifera)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Artemisia racemifera.

Russian wormwood, scientifically known as Artemisia racemifera, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia, valued for its medicinal properties and historical significance. It is commonly used as an adaptogen, helping the body resist stress and maintain balance, and is also recognized for its distinctive bitter taste and aromatic profile. Traditionally, Russian wormwood has been employed in herbal medicine to aid digestion, treat respiratory ailments, and support liver function, with roots in Slavic and traditional Russian healing practices. In modern wellness, it is used in natural remedies for detoxification, mood support, and as a flavoring in teas and tinctures. One of its unique features is the presence of thujone, a compound that gives it a sharp, camphor-like aroma and has contributed to its historical use in both medicine and as a stimulant.
