What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis meyeniana?

Clematis meyeniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Ranunculales, the plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Clematis, specifically reaching the species designation of meyenianana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis meyeniana has woody, climbing stems that produce opposite, trifoliate leaves characterized by small, ovate leaflets with entire margins. The foliage typically displays a bright green color and serves as the primary vegetative structure for the vine. Its inflorescence consists of small, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms are usually pale or cream-colored and feature a delicate, tubular structure. The plant eventually produces small, dry achenes that are attached to feathery styles for wind dispersal.
