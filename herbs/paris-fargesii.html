Paris fargesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific medicinal compounds parisfargosides A-E, which are bioactive triterpenoid saponins. These unique chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the pharmacological activities observed in the species. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential therapeutic applications in various medical contexts. The structural complexity of parisfargosides A through E distinguishes them from other similar plant secondary metabolites. Detailed analysis of these compounds helps clarify the relationship between the plant's chemical profile and its traditional medicinal uses.

As discussed in a study, five new cholestane glycosides named parisfargosides A-E (1-5) were isolated from the rhizomes of Paris fargesii. The structures of all 5 compounds contained an alpha, beta-unsaturated ketone unit. Compounds 3-5 possessed a 16,23-cyclocholest skeleton with 6/6/6/5/5 condensed rings. The platelet aggregation activity and cytotoxic activity of compounds 1-5 were evaluated against 5 human cancer cell lines, specifically HL-60, A549, SMMC-7721, MDA-MB-231, and SW480.
