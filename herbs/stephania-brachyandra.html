Stephania brachyandra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stephania brachyandra has a tuberous stem, pinnate leaves with multiple leaflets, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters. The plant features a prominent, woody tuber that serves as a storage organ for nutrients and water. Its foliage consists of compound leaves that provide a lush, green appearance to the climbing vine. The flowers are typically pale or greenish and lack the showy petals found in many other tropical species. This morphology allows the plant to thrive in shaded forest environments by utilizing its tuber for survival during dry periods.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

      What are the herbal preparations of Stephania brachyandra?

      Stephania brachyandra is prepared as a decoction of the dried rhizomes or as a concentrated aqueous extract for medicinal use. The tuberous roots are typically sliced and boiled in water to release their bioactive alkaloids.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Stephania brachyandra can cause severe toxicity, including neurological impairment, gastrointestinal distress, and potential organ damage due to the presence of potent isoquinoline alkaloids. These chemical compounds can interfere with the central nervous system, leading to symptoms such as dizziness or tremors. Ingestion of the plant often results in nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. Because the concentration of these alkaloids can vary significantly between individual plants, the severity of the reaction is unpredictable. Medical attention is necessary if any part of this plant is consumed accidentally.
