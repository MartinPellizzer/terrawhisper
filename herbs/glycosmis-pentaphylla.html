Glycosmis pentaphylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glycosmis pentaphyllla has a woody shrub or small tree morphology characterized by simple, alternate, coriaceous leaves that typically occur in clusters of five at the branch tips. The leaves are elliptic to oblong in shape, featuring an entire margin and an acute to acuminate apex. Its stems are often slightly angular and can exhibit a reddish tint when young. Small, fragrant, white flowers emerge from the axils, leading to the production of small, fleshy berries that transition from green to red or black upon maturity. This plant's structure allows it to thrive in forest understories and scrublands.

What are the herbal preparations of Glycosmis pentaphylla?

Glycosmis pentaphylla is prepared as decoctions, infusions, and powders derived from its leaves, roots, and bark for various medicinal applications. What side-effects this plant can have?

Glycosmis pentaphylla can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic responses if ingested or applied topically without proper supervision. While many traditional uses focus on its medicinal properties, the concentration of bioactive alkaloids may trigger nausea or stomach cramping in sensitive individuals. Some users have reported localized redness or itching when the plant makes contact with the skin. Because clinical trials on human toxicity are limited, the full range of potential long-term side effects remains largely unknown to modern science. Therefore, cautious administration is necessary to avoid unexpected physiological complications.
