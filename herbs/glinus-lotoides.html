Glinus lotoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Glinus lotoides?

Glinus lotoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Caryophyllales. This specific plant is a representative of the family Molluginaceae, falling under the genus Glinus, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, lotoides.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains flavonoids, glinusides F, glinusides G, hopane-type saponins, and oleanane-type saponins. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the biological activity and therapeutic potential of the species. The presence of various flavonoids often assists in providing antioxidant properties to the plant. Furthermore, the specific glinusides F and G serve as important markers for its chemical profile. The structural diversity of the hopane-type and oleanane-type saponins also plays a significant role in its medicinal characteristics.

    As discussed in a study, an extract of Glinus lotoides was fractionated using reverse phase solid-phase extraction and a stepwise gradient elution of methanol in water to produce 4 fractions. These fractions were analyzed for DNA damaging effects in mouse lymphoma cells using an alkaline version of the comet assay. Liquid chromatography coupled to mass spectrometry, LC-MS/MS, along with 1D and 2D nuclear magnetic resonance analyses, were used to identify specific compounds. While fractions containing a mixture of flavonoids and oleanane-type saponins or oleanane-type saponins alone produced no DNA damage, those containing hopane-type saponins exhibited a pronounced DNA damaging effect. Further studies are still required to determine if individual saponins are responsible for the reported genotoxicity of the crude extract of G. lotoides.
