Glinus lotoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Glinus lotoides?

Glinus lotoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Caryophyllales. This specific plant is a representative of the family Molluginaceae, falling under the genus Glinus, with its unique biological identity defined by its species name, lotoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glinus lotoides has a perennial growth habit characterized by creeping rhizomes and leaves that are broadly ovate to cordate in shape. The foliage typically features prominent venation and a smooth or slightly textured margin. Its flowering structure consists of small, inconspicuous blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant often develops a dense cluster of stems as it spreads through its natural habitat. These morphological traits allow the species to colonize moist, shaded environments effectively.

What are the pharmacological activities of Glinus lotoides?

This plant has significant pharmacological potential through its demonstrated anticancer, antioxidant, antitumour, and chemopreventive activities. The antioxidant properties of Glinus lotoides help neutralize harmful free radicals that cause cellular damage. By mitigating this oxidative stress, the plant functions as a chemopreventive agent to stop the development of disease. Furthermore, its specific antitumour effects allow it to inhibit the growth of malignant cells. These combined mechanisms contribute to its overall effectiveness as a natural anticancer candidate.

As discussed in a study, the methanol and n-hexane extracts of Glinus lotoides seeds showed differential growth inhibitory responses in carcinoma cell lines with Calu-3 IC(50) values of 29.7 and 79.8 µg/mL and Caco-2 IC(50) values of 69.7 and 74.6 µg/mL. These extracts were compared to normal cell lines where MDCK IC(50) was 106.1 and 131.1 µg/mL and IEC-6 IC(50) was 134.0 and 128.5 µg/mL. The extracts induced significant apoptosis in the cancer cells at 100 µg/mL with a p<0.05 significance level. Furthermore, the plant exhibited antioxidant activity with a hydrophilic oxygen radical absorption capacity (ORAC) value of 123 µM Trolox Equiv./g. The seeds also contain nutritional compounds with chemopreventive activity such as vitamin E, folic acid, selenium and calcium.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains flavonoids, glinusides F, glinusides G, hopane-type saponins, and oleanane-type saponins. These diverse chemical constituents contribute to the complex pharmacological profile of the species. The specific presence of glinusides F and G suggests unique biochemical activities within the plant tissue. Furthermore, the combination of hopane-type and oleanane-type saponins provides a structural variety often associated with medicinal properties. Such a rich array of secondary metabolites makes the plant a significant subject for phytochemical study.

As discussed in a study, Glinus lotoides contains medicinal compounds including flavonoids and saponins. The plant extract was processed through reverse phase solid-phase extraction to collect 4 fractions. Analysis identified that some of these components are oleanane-type saponins and hopane-type saponins. While fractions with a mixture of flavonoids and oleanane-type saponins or oleanane-type saponins alone produced no DNA damage, the hopane-type saponins exhibited a pronounced DNA damaging effect. The study utilized 1D and 2D nuclear magnetic resonance analyses to confirm the identity of some saponins within the plant.
