Glinus lotoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glinus lotoides has a perennial growth habit characterized by creeping rhizomes and leaves that are broadly ovate to cordate in shape. The foliage typically features prominent venation and a smooth or slightly textured margin. Its flowering structure consists of small, inconspicuous blossoms that emerge from the leaf axils. The plant often develops a dense cluster of stems as it spreads through its natural habitat. These morphological traits allow the species to colonize moist, shaded environments effectively.
