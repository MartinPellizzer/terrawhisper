Zingiber cochleariforme has spoon-shaped or cochlear-shaped rhizomes that are fleshy and yellowish-white in color. Above the soil, the plant produces slender, elongated leaves that emerge from the rhizome to facilitate photosynthesis. The flowering structure is typically inconspicuous, often appearing near the base of the foliage.
