Zelkova sinica

What's the taxonomical classification of Zelkova sinica?

Zelkova sinica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Ulmaceae. More specifically, it is categorized within the genus Zelkova, with its unique designation being the species sinica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Zelkova sinica contains high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids, tannins, and triterpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These phytochemical constituents are primarily utilized in traditional medicine for their potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities. Specifically, the extracts from the bark and leaves are studied for their ability to inhibit oxidative stress in cellular models. Some research also suggests that the plant's chemical profile may offer antimicrobial benefits against certain pathogens. These medicinal compounds work together to support various physiological healing processes.

What are the therapeutic actions of Zelkova sinica?

Zelkova sinica has medicinal properties derived primarily from its bark and leaves which act as an anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial agent. The plant contains various bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and tannins that help mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. These chemical constituents work to reduce swelling and alleviate pain associated with inflammatory conditions. Additionally, its extract can assist in managing skin irritations due to its ability to inhibit microbial growth. Regular application or consumption of its derivatives may support overall immune function through these diverse mechanisms.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin conditions, including eczema, dermatitis, and inflammatory skin rashes. The bark contains bioactive compounds that provide soothing and anti-inflammatory effects when applied topically. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize extracts from the tree to manage localized swellings and irritation. Some historical uses also suggest its application for calming minor wound inflammations. These therapeutic properties are primarily attributed to the presence of specific tannins and phytochemicals found within the plant material.

What are the herbal preparations of Zelkova sinica?

Zelkova sinica is prepared as a decoction by boiling the bark, leaves, or twigs in water to extract its medicinal compounds. These herbal preparations are most commonly consumed as liquid teas or concentrated extracts for internal use. In some traditional practices, the bark may also be processed into a fine powder that is mixed with honey or warm water. External applications sometimes utilize a topical wash made from the boiled plant material to treat skin conditions. The choice of preparation form depends heavily on whether the desired therapeutic effect is systemic or localized.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Zelkova sinica can cause allergic contact dermatitis, skin irritation, or respiratory discomfort in sensitive individuals through contact with its leaves, bark, or pollen. While the tree is generally considered non-toxic to humans in terms of ingestion, direct skin contact with certain plant components may trigger redness or itching. People with existing pollen allergies might experience seasonal hay fever or asthma-like symptoms when the tree releases pollen into the air. Additionally, some individuals might notice minor swelling or rashes if they handle the wood or foliage without protection. It is best to exercise caution if you have a history of hypersensitivity to various tree species.
