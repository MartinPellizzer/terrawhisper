Zelkova sinica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zelkova sinica has small, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins, an alternate arrangement on the branches, and a scabrous texture. The tree typically reaches a significant height with a spreading crown and bark that becomes grayish and fissured as it matures. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into tiny, winged samaras used for wind dispersal. The twigs are often slender and slightly zigzagged in their growth pattern. This species overall exhibits a sturdy woody structure characteristic of the Ulmaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate forests of northeastern China, the Korean Peninsula, and the Russian Far East. Its range extends across specific provinces in China such as Liaoning and Jilin. In Korea, it is widely distributed throughout the northern and central regions of the country. The species also inhabits the Amur and Primorsky regions of Russia where the climate supports its growth. These areas generally provide the moist, deciduous forest habitats required for the species to thrive.

      What are the herbal preparations of Zelkova sinica?

      Zelkova sinica is prepared as a decoction by boiling the bark, leaves, or twigs in water to extract its medicinal compounds. These herbal preparations are most commonly consumed as liquid teas or concentrated extracts for internal use.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Zelkova sinica can cause allergic contact dermatitis, skin irritation, or respiratory discomfort in sensitive individuals through contact with its leaves, bark, or pollen. While the tree is generally considered non-toxic to humans in terms of ingestion, direct skin contact with certain plant components may trigger redness or itching. People with existing pollen allergies might experience seasonal hay fever or asthma-like symptoms when the tree releases pollen into the air. Additionally, some individuals might notice minor swelling or rashes if they handle the wood or foliage without protection. It is best to exercise caution if you have a history of hypersensitivity to various tree species.
