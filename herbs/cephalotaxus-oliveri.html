Cephalotaxus oliveri

What's the taxonomical classification of Cephalotaxus oliveri?

Cephalotaxus oliveri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Pinidae and falls under the order Pinales. This plant is a member of the family Cephalotaxaceae and is categorized within the genus Cephalotaxus, ultimately reaching its specific identification at the species level as Cephalotaxus oliveri.

Cephalotaxus oliveiri contains medicinal alkaloids primarily extracted from its leaves and bark. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Cephalotaxus oliveri targets tubulin proteins to inhibit microtubule polymerization, a mechanism primarily driven by its high concentration of vinca alkaloids. These alkaloids bind specifically to tubulin subunits, preventing the assembly of the mitotic spindle necessary for cell division. By disrupting this structural process, the plant's compounds induce cell cycle arrest during the metaphase stage. This targeted interference leads to programmed cell death in rapidly proliferating cancer cells. Consequently, these molecular interactions provide the foundation for the plant's significant potential in developing chemotherapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cephalotaxus oliveri?

Cephalotaxus oliveiri has potent antineoplastic properties derived from its indole alkaloids which inhibit microtubule assembly during cell division. These bioactive compounds work by binding to tubulin, effectively preventing the polymerization necessary for mitosis in cancer cells. This mechanism leads to cell cycle arrest and eventually triggers programmed cell death in various malignant lineages. Researchers focus on these specific alkaloids to develop more effective chemotherapy agents for treating solid tumors. The plant serves as a critical natural source for the isolation of complex molecules used in oncology research.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Cephalotaxus oliveiri can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potential organ toxicity if ingested due to its high concentration of potent alkaloids. These toxic compounds may trigger intense nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain shortly after consumption. If large quantities are taken, individuals might experience dizziness, tremors, or more significant central nervous system depression. Long-term or high-dose exposure could lead to serious damage to the liver or kidneys. Because the plant contains complex chemical structures similar to pharmaceutical agents, even small amounts pose a significant health risk.
