Pedicularis verticillata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis verticillata?

Pedicularis verticillata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Orobanchaceae, falling under the genus Pedicularis, and is specifically identified by its species name, verticillata.

    What are the herbal preparations of Pedicularis verticillata?

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Pedicularis verticillata can cause adverse physiological reactions such as nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort if ingested due to the presence of potentially toxic alkaloids. Many species within the Pedicularis genus contain compounds that may act as central nervous system depressants or irritants to the digestive tract. While specific toxicological data for this exact species is limited, caution is necessary because related plants are known to induce dizziness or lethargy. Allergic skin reactions like redness or itching may also occur if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. Therefore, avoiding ingestion and minimizing handling is recommended for those unfamiliar with its chemical properties.
