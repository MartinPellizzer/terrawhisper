Pedicularis verticillata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis verticillata?

Pedicularis verticillata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Orobanchaceae, falling under the genus Pedicularis, and is specifically identified by its species name, verticillata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis verticillata has an erect, unbranched stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and is characterized by whorled, lanceolate leaves arranged in distinct tiers. The foliage consists of sessile, deeply serrated leaves that wrap slightly around the stem at each node. Its inflorescence is a dense, terminal spike featuring tubular, two-lipped flowers that vary in color from pink to purplish-red. The upper lip of the corolla is notably hooded or galea-shaped, which is a defining trait of the genus. Each flower is supported by a small bract that is often similar in shape to the leaves.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It is commonly found growing in subalpine and alpine environments across the Rocky Mountains and the Pacific Northwest. Specific habitats include moist meadows, tundra, and rocky slopes where moisture is consistent. Its distribution is closely tied to high-elevation ecosystems that provide the cool, damp conditions necessary for its survival.
