Corydalis cava

Corydalis (Corydalis cava) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China, known for its use in traditional medicine to alleviate pain and improve circulation.

Botanical Identification

Corydalis cava, commonly known as the lesser corydalis, is a perennial herb in the family Fumariaceae. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, it features delicate pink or white flowers and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its analgesic and sedative properties. Synonyms include "Corydalis crocata" and "Corydalis tenera" in older botanical references.

Safety Profile

Corydalis cava is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses may lead to liver toxicity, so caution is advised, especially for those with pre-existing liver conditions.
