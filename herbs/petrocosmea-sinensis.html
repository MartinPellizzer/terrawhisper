Petrocosmea sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Petrocosmea sinensis?

Petrocosmea sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Gesneriaceae, falling under the genus Petrocosmea, with its specific designation being the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Petrocosmea sinensis has a rosette of fleshy, ovate to elliptical leaves that typically measure several centimeters in length and feature slightly serrated or wavy margins.

The leaf surfaces often exhibit a subtle pubescence or a somewhat succulent texture, which helps the plant retain moisture in its natural habitat. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the center of the rosette on slender peduncles, displaying a tubular corolla structure. These blossoms are typically characterized by their pale color and fine hairs along the exterior of the petals.

The plant's overall structure is compact and low-growing, reflecting its adaptation to rocky or shaded environments.
