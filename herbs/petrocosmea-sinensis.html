Petrocosmea sinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Petrocosmea sinensis has a rosette of fleshy, ovate to elliptical leaves that typically measure several centimeters in length and feature slightly serrated or wavy margins. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a subtle pubescence or a somewhat succulent texture, which helps the plant retain moisture in its natural habitat. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the center of the rosette on slender peduncles, displaying a tubular corolla structure. These blossoms are typically characterized by their pale color and fine hairs along the exterior of the petals. The plant's overall structure is compact and low-growing, reflecting its adaptation to rocky or shaded environments.
