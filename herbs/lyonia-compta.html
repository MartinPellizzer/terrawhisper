Lyonia compta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lyonia compta has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, branching stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems and possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly wavy margins. Small, inconspicuous white flowers emerge from the leaf axils, featuring four petals that are much shorter than the protruding stamens. Following pollination, the plant produces small, globose berries that transition from green to a dark, fleshy color when ripe. These morphological features distinguish the species within its wetland habitat.
