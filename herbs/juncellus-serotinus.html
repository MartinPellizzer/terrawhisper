Juncellus serotinus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juncellus serotinus has a woody, perennial structure characterized by erect, slender stems that typically reach heights of thirty to sixty centimeters. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and possess an elliptical shape with entire margins and acute tips. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the late growing season, giving the species its specific epithet. These blossoms are predominantly pale in color and feature a simplified floral anatomy consisting of five fused petals. The plant also develops small, dry fruits that facilitate seed dispersal across its native habitat.
