Herbal Preparations of Magnolia aromatica

Magnolia aromatica has many herbal preparations, such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, which are derived from its bark, flowers, and leaves.

The tincture, on the other hand, is often used to alleviate stress and anxiety due to its sedative effects, while the infusion of its flowers is used to treat respiratory issues such as bronchitis and asthma.

Possible Side Effects of Magnolia aromatica

Magnolia aromatica can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing allergic reactions, including skin irritation, hives, and itching.

Ingesting large quantities of the plant's bark or leaves can lead to stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea.

Additionally, using Magnolia aromatica essential oil without proper dilution can cause respiratory issues, such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Overuse of the plant's extracts may also interact with certain medications, exacerbating conditions like high blood pressure, diabetes, and anxiety disorders.
