Deutzia ningpoensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Deutzia ningpoensis has deciduous, shrubby growth habits characterized by arching branches and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately two to five centimeters in length. The foliage is typically bright green with finely serrated margins and prominent venation. During the blooming period, the plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms often feature a yellowish center and are held on slender pedicels. The overall structure creates a graceful, spreading canopy suitable for ornamental landscaping.
