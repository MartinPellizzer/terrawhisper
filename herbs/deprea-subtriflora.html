What is Deprea subtriflora?

Deprea subtriflora is an annual or perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with entire margins, 2-7 cm long, and its small, white to purple flowers in axillary clusters of 2-4. The flowers have 5 petals and are 5-7 mm in diameter, with 5 stamens and a bilocular ovary. The plant produces small, globular fruits, 5-7 mm in diameter, containing 1-2 seeds.

Its stems are green, hairy, and 30-60 cm tall, with a woody base at the base.

What are the medicinal constituents this herb?

The most important healing constituents of Deprea subtriflora are alkaloids, specifically tropane derivatives, which include scopolamine, hyoscyamine, tropacocaine, and tropine.

These compounds are known to have anticholinergic properties, meaning they inhibit the action of the neurotransmitter acetylcholine, which can help to reduce spasms, smooth muscle contractions, and secretions. Atropine, another tropane alkaloid present in Deprea subtriflora, acts as a muscarinic receptor antagonist, further contributing to its anticholinergic effects. The anticholinergic properties of these constituents are believed to be responsible for the plant's potential therapeutic uses in treating conditions such as spasms, asthma, and gastrointestinal disorders.

The precise mechanisms of action and therapeutic effects of these compounds are still being researched and studied.
