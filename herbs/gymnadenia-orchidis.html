Gymnadenia orchidis

What's the taxonomical classification of Gymnadenia orchidis?

Gymnadenia orchidis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Finally, it is defined by the genus Gymnadenia and the specific species epithet orchidis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gymnadenia orchidis has an erect stem reaching up to 40 centimeters in height, bearing several ovate-lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stalk.

The inflorescence consists of a dense, terminal spike containing numerous small, fragrant, pink to pale lilac flowers. Each individual flower features a long, slender nectar spur that extends significantly from the base of the lip. The labellum is deeply three-lobed, with a central lobe that is often narrower than the lateral ones.

The overall structure is delicate and optimized for pollination by long-tongued insects.
