Gymnadenia orchidis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Finally, it is defined by the genus Gymnadenia and the specific species epithet orchidis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Each individual flower features a long, slender nectar spur that extends significantly from the base of the lip. The labellum is deeply three-lobed, with a central lobe that is often narrower than the lateral ones. The overall structure is delicate and optimized for pollination by long-tongued insects.
