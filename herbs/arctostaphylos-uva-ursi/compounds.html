Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi) Compounds

The primary compounds in Bearberry include tannins, which have astringent properties, and phenolic acids, known for their antioxidant effects. Flavonoids and glycosides contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. Terpenoids and coumarin support its traditional use in urinary tract health, while saponins and essential oils add to its overall therapeutic profile. Alkaloids and anthocyanins also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi) Monograph

1. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to support urinary tract health by reducing inflammation and microbial growth. Tannins in Bearberry are primarily found in the leaves and are responsible for many of its traditional medicinal uses.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial properties, supporting its traditional use in urinary tract health. This compound is found in the leaves and is responsible for some of the plant's therapeutic effects.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support urinary tract health by reducing inflammation and oxidative stress. They work synergistically with other active components in the plant to enhance its therapeutic effects.

4. Glycoside

The glycoside arbutin is a key active compound in Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi). It is primarily responsible for the plant's antimicrobial and urinary tract health benefits. Arbutin is converted in the body to hydroquinone, which has antibacterial properties. This compound is commonly used in herbal remedies for urinary infections.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi). The primary terpenoid in Bearberry is arbutin, which is known for its antibacterial properties. Arbutin is responsible for many of the plant's therapeutic effects, particularly in urinary tract health.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and astringent properties, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for urinary tract support and skin conditions.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi). It contributes to the plant's antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for treating urinary tract infections and other inflammatory conditions.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi) contains aromatic compounds like methyl salicylate and arctiin. It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial properties and is used in formulations for urinary tract support. The oil's volatile components enhance the overall efficacy of Bearberry in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

The alkaloid known as arbutin is found in Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi). It is the primary active compound responsible for the plant's antimicrobial and urinary tract health benefits. Arbutin is metabolized in the body to produce hydroquinone, which has antibacterial properties.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key compound found in Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in urinary tract support due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Anthocyanin also aids in protecting cells from oxidative stress, enhancing the plant's therapeutic potential.
