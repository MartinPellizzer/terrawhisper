Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi) Uses

Bearberry is primarily used to treat urinary tract infections, bladder inflammation, and prostatitis. It helps reduce urinary tract irritation and discomfort, making it useful for conditions like interstitial cystitis and bladder infections. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties support healing in urinary tract inflammation and kidney stones.

Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi) Monograph

1. Urinary tract infection

Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi) is used traditionally for urinary tract infections due to its antibacterial properties, particularly from arbutin, which is converted to a mild antibiotic in the body. It supports bladder health and may help alleviate symptoms of UTIs when used as part of a holistic treatment approach.

2. Bladder inflammation

Bearberry leaves contain arctiin, which has antibacterial properties that help reduce urinary tract infections and inflammation. It is traditionally used to support bladder health and alleviate symptoms of cystitis. Modern studies suggest its compounds may inhibit bacterial growth in the urinary tract.

3. Kidney stones

Bearberry is traditionally used to support kidney health and may help prevent or dissolve kidney stones due to its high concentration of arbutin, which has antimicrobial and diuretic properties. It is often used in herbal formulations to promote urinary tract health and reduce the risk of stone formation.

4. Prostatitis

Bearberry has been traditionally used to support urinary tract health, particularly in cases of prostatitis. Its active compound, arbutin, is converted in the body to hydroquinone, which has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce inflammation and infection in the prostate gland, making it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for managing prostatitis symptoms.

5. Urinary tract irritation

Bearberry has been traditionally used to treat urinary tract irritation due to its active compound, arctiin, which has antimicrobial and astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and soothe bladder lining, making it useful for conditions like cystitis. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance due to potential side effects.

6. Urinary tract infections

Bearberry has been traditionally used for urinary tract infections due to its active compound, arbutin, which acts as a urinary antiseptic. It helps reduce inflammation and combat bacterial growth in the urinary system. However, it should be used under professional guidance due to potential side effects and interactions.

7. Urinary tract discomfort

Bearberry has been traditionally used to support urinary tract health. Its active compound, arbutin, is metabolized into hydroquinone, which has antibacterial properties. This makes it effective for addressing urinary tract discomfort and mild infections. It is often used in herbal formulas for urinary support.

8. Interstitial cystitis

Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi) is used in herbal medicine for interstitial cystitis due to its active compound, arbutin, which has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bladder irritation and infection risk, supporting urinary tract health. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture under professional guidance.

9. Bladder infection

Bearberry has been traditionally used to treat bladder infections due to its high concentration of arbutin, which is converted in the body to compounds that have antibacterial properties. It helps reduce inflammation and supports urinary tract health, making it a common herb in herbal medicine for urinary infections.

10. Urinary tract inflammation

Its astringent effects also help reduce inflammation and soothe irritated urinary tissues.
