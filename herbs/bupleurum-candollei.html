Bupleurum candollei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bupleurum candollei has upright, perennial stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and support alternate, lanceolate to linear leaves with entire margins. The foliage is characterized by a somewhat leathery texture and a dull green color that provides a subtle backdrop for the reproductive structures. Small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in dense, terminal umbels that form a distinctive flat-topped cluster. These inflorescences are surrounded by specialized bracts that protect the developing florets during the early stages of growth. The plant's root system is sturdy and fibrous, helping it anchor effectively in its natural rocky or montane habitats.
