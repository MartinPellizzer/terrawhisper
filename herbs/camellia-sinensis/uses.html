Camellia (Camellia sinensis) Uses

Camellia sinensis, or green tea, is used to treat gastritis, constipation, and gastroesophageal reflux disease by reducing inflammation and soothing the digestive tract. It also supports pancreatic health and may help manage type 2 diabetes and hypertension through antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Its compounds can aid in ulcer healing and improve overall gastrointestinal function.

Camellia (Camellia sinensis) Monograph

1. Gastritis

Camellia sinensis, or green tea, is used in herbal medicine to soothe gastritis due to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It helps reduce stomach inflammation and neutralize harmful bacteria, promoting healing. Regular consumption may alleviate symptoms like pain and indigestion associated with gastritis.

2. Constipation

Camellia sinensis, or green tea, can help alleviate constipation due to its mild laxative effects and high fiber content. It stimulates bowel movements and promotes digestive health. Regular consumption may improve gut motility and support regularity in individuals experiencing mild constipation.

3. Gastroesophageal reflux disease

Camellia sinensis, or green tea, has been used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of gastroesophageal reflux disease. Its polyphenols, particularly catechins, reduce gastric acid secretion and enhance lower esophageal sphincter function, offering a natural approach to managing reflux.

4. Inflammatory bowel disease

Camellia sinensis, or green tea, contains compounds like catechins and polyphenols that exhibit anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds may help reduce intestinal inflammation and modulate gut microbiota, showing potential in managing symptoms of inflammatory bowel disease. Clinical studies suggest green tea extract may support intestinal health and reduce inflammation in conditions like ulcerative colitis and Crohn's disease.

5. Ulcer

Camellia sinensis, or green tea, has been used traditionally to support digestive health. Its polyphenols, particularly catechins, may help reduce stomach acid and protect the stomach lining. It is often used in herbal medicine to manage symptoms of ulcers and promote healing. However, it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.

6. Peptic ulcer

Camellia sinensis, or green tea, has been used traditionally to support digestive health. Its polyphenols, particularly catechins, may help reduce stomach acid and protect the gastric lining. Studies suggest it can aid in the healing of peptic ulcers by inhibiting harmful bacteria and promoting tissue repair.

7. Hypertension

Camellia sinensis, or green tea, contains compounds like caffeine and catechins that may help lower blood pressure. These substances can improve vascular function and reduce oxidative stress, contributing to better cardiovascular health. Regular consumption may support blood pressure regulation as part of a holistic approach to managing hypertension.

8. High blood pressure

Camellia sinensis, or green tea, contains compounds like epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG) that may help lower blood pressure by improving endothelial function and reducing oxidative stress. Regular consumption has been associated with modest but consistent reductions in both systolic and diastolic blood pressure in some studies.

9. Type 2 diabetes

Camellia sinensis, or green tea, has been studied for its potential role in managing type 2 diabetes. Its polyphenols, particularly epigallocatechin gallate, may improve insulin sensitivity and reduce glucose absorption. Regular consumption has shown promise in lowering blood sugar levels and supporting metabolic health.

10. Pancreatic health

Camellia sinensis, or green tea, supports pancreatic health by reducing inflammation and improving insulin sensitivity. Its polyphenols, particularly EGCG, may protect pancreatic cells from oxidative stress and enhance beta-cell function, contributing to better glucose regulation and reduced risk of diabetes-related complications.
