Camellia (Camellia sinensis) Compounds

The primary compounds in Camellia sinensis include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and theanine, known for its calming effects. Tannins and polyphenols contribute to its astringent taste and health benefits. Catechins, a type of flavonoid, are linked to metabolic and cardiovascular support.

Camellia (Camellia sinensis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Camellia sinensis, the plant used to make tea. They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support cardiovascular health. These compounds are also linked to anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects in traditional herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Camellia sinensis, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the bitter taste of tea and has antimicrobial and antioxidant effects. Tannins may aid in digestion and support gut health, making them important in herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Camellia sinensis. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound is also associated with potential health benefits such as improved cardiovascular function and reduced inflammation.

4. Glycoside

Glycosides are naturally occurring compounds found in Camellia sinensis. They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties by interacting with biological systems to support health. These compounds are often associated with the plant's ability to promote digestion and reduce inflammation.

5. Alkaloid

Alkaloids are a class of naturally occurring chemical compounds found in Camellia sinensis. These compounds contribute to the plant's pharmacological properties, including potential effects on the central nervous system and cardiovascular system. They are also involved in the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Camellia sinensis. It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has mild anticoagulant properties. While not the primary medicinal component, coumarin may support cardiovascular health when consumed in moderation as part of tea.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Camellia sinensis, the plant used to make tea. These compounds contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. They are also responsible for some of the aroma and flavor profiles in tea.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Camellia sinensis. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Saponins may also support cardiovascular health by reducing cholesterol levels. These compounds are naturally present in tea leaves and are extracted during processing.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Camellia sinensis. It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound supports overall health by neutralizing free radicals and may aid in reducing inflammation. Its presence in tea leaves enhances the medicinal value of Camellia sinensis.

10. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Camellia sinensis. It contributes to the plant's aromatic profile and has antimicrobial properties. Extracted through distillation, it is used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for its calming and digestive benefits.

11. Polyphenol

Polyphenols are key active compounds in Camellia sinensis, the plant used to make tea. These compounds contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties and are associated with various health benefits, including reduced inflammation and improved cardiovascular function. They are also responsible for the bitter taste and dark color of many tea varieties.

12. Catechin

Catechin is a key active compound found in Camellia sinensis. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and is associated with potential health benefits such as supporting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation. Catechins are abundant in tea leaves and are responsible for some of the health-promoting effects linked to regular tea consumption.

13. Theanine

Theanine is a unique amino acid found in Camellia sinensis, the plant used to make green and black tea. It contributes to the calming effects of tea by influencing neurotransmitter activity, promoting relaxation without causing drowsiness. Theanine is also associated with improved cognitive function and reduced stress levels.
