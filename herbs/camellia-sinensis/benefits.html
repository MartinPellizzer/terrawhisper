1. Eat antioxidants

Oxidative stress is a condition characterized by an imbalance between free radicals and the body's ability to neutralize them, leading to cellular damage.

This issue, also known as oxidative damage or free radical-induced harm, occurs when there is an excess of reactive oxygen species that the body cannot effectively manage.

It is commonly experienced by individuals exposed to environmental toxins, smokers, and those with poor dietary habits.

Camellia sinensis, the plant from which green tea is derived, provides antioxidants that help combat oxidative stress by neutralizing free radicals.

Regular consumption of antioxidants from Camellia sinensis can support the body's natural defenses and reduce the risk of related health issues.

How this herb helps with eat antioxidants?

Camellia sinensis eat antioxidants because it contains bioactive constituents such as alkaloids and flavonoids.

These compounds help neutralize free radicals in the body, reducing oxidative stress. Antioxidants from Camellia sinensis are known to support overall cellular health. Regular consumption of these antioxidants may lower the risk of chronic diseases.

The presence of these natural compounds makes Camellia sinensis a valuable source of health-promoting nutrients.
