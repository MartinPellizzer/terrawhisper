Ailanthus triphysa

What's the taxonomical classification of Ailanthus triphysa?

Ailanthus triphyza belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant falls under the order Sapindales and is a member of the family Simaroubaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Ailanthus, and its unique species designation, triphyza.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ailanthus triphyza contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its pharmacological properties. These chemical constituents provide significant antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory activities when utilized in traditional medicine. Specific alkaloids within the plant are often studied for their potential to combat pathogenic bacteria and fungal infections. The presence of terpenoids also suggests a role in managing oxidative stress and regulating cellular responses. Researchers continue to investigate these complex organic compounds to identify precise therapeutic applications for human health.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ailanthus triphysa?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin diseases such as scabies, eczema, and fungal infections. It is frequently applied topically to address dermatological issues and inflammatory conditions. Beyond skin ailments, the plant is utilized in traditional medicine to manage symptoms of digestive disorders and fever. Some practitioners also use extracts from its bark or leaves to help alleviate certain types of inflammation throughout the body. These medicinal properties are primarily attributed to the presence of specific alkaloids within the plant.
