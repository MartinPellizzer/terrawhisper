Ailanthus triphysa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ailantus triphysa has pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of three leaflets arranged in an opposite fashion. The leaflets are broadly ovate or elliptical in shape and possess serrated margins along their edges. These leaves are supported by long petioles that connect the foliage to the woody stem of the tree. The plant also features small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into winged samaras for seed dispersal. The overall structure of the foliage gives the canopy a light and airy appearance.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ailanthus triphysa?

What are the herbal preparations of Ailanthus triphysa?

Ailanthus triphylla is prepared as decoctions, infusions, and topical pastes using its leaves, bark, and roots to treat various ailments. What side-effects this plant can have?

Ailantus triphylla can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that act as irritants when they come into contact with human tissue. Individuals may experience redness, itching, or blistering after touching the leaves or stems. If consumed, the plant may lead to nausea, vomiting, or more significant digestive complications. Careful handling is necessary to avoid accidental exposure to its chemical compounds.
