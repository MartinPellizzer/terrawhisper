Coriandrum sativum

What's the taxonomical classification of Coriandrum sativum?

Coriandrrum sativum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Apiales and the family Apiaceae, which is characterized by its distinct floral structures. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Coriandrum and its specific species name, sativum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coriandrum sativum has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and features a branched structure with pinnately divided leaves. The plant exhibits heterophylly, meaning it produces two distinct leaf shapes: feathery, thread-like leaves on the lower part of the stem and broader, lobed leaves near the flowering top. Small, white or pale pink flowers grow in compound umbels, which are characteristic of the Apiaceae family. As the plant matures, it produces small, spherical, yellowish-brown schizocarps that contain the aromatic seeds. The entire morphology is designed to support rapid growth and efficient seed dispersal through its branching architecture.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Western Asia, though it is now cultivated globally in diverse climates. Its primary centers of origin include the southern European coastlines and the Middle East. Due to its widespread use in culinary traditions, farmers grow it extensively in warm, temperate zones across India and North Africa. It also thrives in many parts of the Americas where irrigation and soil conditions support its growth. This global distribution is driven by the high demand for its seeds and leaves in international spice markets.

As discussed in a study, Coriandrum sativum is a notable medicinal plant known for its diverse properties, including anti-inflammatoty, antioxidant, anticancer, analgesic, and anti-diabetic effects. An aqueous extract derived from Coriandrum sativum leaves can mitigate kidney malformations induced by aristolochic acid. Pretreatment with coriander extract reduces the accumulation of red blood cells in the glomerular region and partially mitigates renal function impairment. The extract likely alleviates acute renal failure by reducing the expression of proinflammatoty and proapoptotic genes induced by aristolochic acid. This suggests the plant has potential to attenuate renal failure by reducing inflammation and apoptosis.

What side effects this plant can have?
