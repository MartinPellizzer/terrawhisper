What to know about Coriandrum sativum before using it medicinally

Coriandrum sativum, commonly known as coriander, is a herb with a long history of medicinal use, primarily utilized in the treatment of digestive disorders such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), dyspepsia, and nausea.

The main health benefits associated with coriander include its anti-inflammatory properties, its ability to regulate blood sugar levels, and its potential to reduce cholesterol levels. The therapeutic properties of coriander are attributed to its carminative, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which make it effective in alleviating symptoms of digestive issues and protecting against oxidative stress. Coriander contains a range of bioactive compounds, including alkaloids, flavonoids, and essential oils, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities.

Coriander is commonly used to make teas, infusions, and digestive tonics, with its essential oils also being extracted for use in aromatherapy and topical applications.
