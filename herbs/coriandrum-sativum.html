Coriandrum Sativum

Coriandrum sativum, commonly known as coriander, is a plant-based herb belonging to the parsley family (Apiaceae).

It has been used for centuries in traditional medicine, offering various health benefits such as aiding digestion, reducing nausea and vomiting, and exhibiting anti-inflammatory properties.

The therapeutic actions of coriander are attributed to its ability to soothe the stomach, relax muscles, and exhibit antioxidant properties, making it a popular remedy for various ailments.

The bioactive constituents of coriander include limonene, linalool, and borneol, among others, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Possible Side Effects of coriandrum sativum

In some cases, coriandrum sativum may interact with medications, such as blood thinners, and decrease their effectiveness.
