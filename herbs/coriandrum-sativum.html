Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum)

Cilantro (Coriandrum sativum) is a member of the Apiaceae family, native to Middle East, Southwest Asia, and Southern Europe. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and fruits have been used for culinary uses, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its carminative, antispasmodic, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.
