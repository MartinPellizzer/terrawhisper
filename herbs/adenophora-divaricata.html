Adenophora divaricata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora divaricata has erect, perennial stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and feature opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is often characterized by a slightly pubescent texture and prominent venation on the leaf surfaces. Small, tubular flowers emerge from terminal or axillary inflorescences, displaying a pale lavender to bluish hue. These blossoms possess a five-lobed corolla that transitions into a slender tube. The plant's root system consists of a woody caudex from which the annual stems arise.

What are the therapeutic actions of Adenophora divaricata?

Adenophora divaricata has therapeutic properties that function as an expectorant, antitussive, diaphoretic, and bitter tonic to treat respiratory ailments and digestive issues. By acting as a diaphoretic, it helps induce sweating to reduce fever and promote detoxification. Additionally, its bitter components stimulate digestion and improve appetite through its tonic effects.
