Adenophora divaricata

What's the taxonomical classification of Adenophora divaricata?

Adenophora divaricata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Campanulaceae, falling into the genus Adenophora, and is specifically identified by its species name, divaricata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora divaricata has erect, perennial stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and feature opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is often characterized by a slightly pubescent texture and prominent venation on the leaf surfaces. Small, tubular flowers emerge from terminal or axillary inflorescences, displaying a pale lavender to bluish hue. These blossoms possess a five-lobed corolla that transitions into a slender tube. The plant's root system consists of a woody caudex from which the annual stems arise.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating pulmonary inflammation and various respiratory diseases. It is traditionally utilized to alleviate symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath. The bioactive compounds within the herb help to reduce swelling and irritation in the lung tissues. By addressing these inflammatory responses, it supports the overall function of the respiratory system. This medicinal application has been documented in various traditional healing practices.

According to a study published by "Cells", Adenophora divaricata is investigated for its ability to attenuate particulate matter-induced inflammatory responses and pulmonary inflammation. The study utilized PM-stimulated RAW264.7 cells and PM-exposed mice to evaluate the anti-inflammatory properties of the methanolic extract of the plant. In the experimental model, PM was administered intranasally to mice for 7 days, while Adenophora divaricata was orally administered for the same 7 day duration. Treatment with the plant significantly attenuated pulmonary inflammation by reducing inflammatory cell counts and decreasing cytokine levels in bronchoalveolar lavage fluid. Furthermore, the plant decreased oxidative stress markers and suppressed TXNIP/NLRP3 inflammasome expression, suggesting it may serve as a potential therapeutic agent for pulmonary inflammation.
