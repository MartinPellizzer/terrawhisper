Bambusa beecheyana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bambusa beecheyana has erect, woody culms that reach heights of up to 20 meters, characterized by a diameter of 5 to 10 centimeters and a smooth, yellowish-green surface. The plant exhibits a climbing or scrambling growth habit due to its slender branches and flexible structure. Dense clusters of fine, branching twigs emerge from the nodes to support the foliage.
