What's the taxonomical classification of Thyrocarpus glochidiatus?

Thyrocarpus glochidiatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its specific taxonomic ranks, the plant is part of the order Boraginales and falls within the family Boraginaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Thyrocarpus, with its specific designation being the species glochidiatus.

        What are the herbal preparations of Thyrocarpus glochidiatus?

        Thyrocarpus glochidiatus is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the tough, fibrous fungal tissues in water to release its bioactive compounds. This method is necessary because the structure of the fruiting body requires prolonged heat to break down its complex cell walls. Once the liquid is strained, the resulting decoction can be consumed directly or used as a base for other medicinal formulations. Some practitioners may also incorporate the dried material into powders for easier measurement in standardized doses.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Thyrocarpus glochidiatus can cause localized skin irritation, redness, and itching upon direct contact with its tissues. Some people might experience swelling if the plant material is handled frequently without protection. It is generally recommended to avoid touching the plant to prevent these dermatological issues.
