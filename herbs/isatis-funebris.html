Black Mustard (Isatis Funebris)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Isatis funebris.

Black mustard, scientifically known as Isatis funebris, is a medicinal herb with a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It is valued for its potential to support immune function, reduce inflammation, and aid in respiratory health, making it a popular remedy in both traditional and modern wellness practices. Historically, it has been used in Chinese and Native American traditions to treat ailments such as coughs, colds, and skin infections, often in the form of teas or topical applications. In modern wellness, black mustard is increasingly being explored for its potential anti-cancer properties and as a natural immune booster, particularly due to its high concentration of glucosinolates. Its distinctive bitter taste and rare active compounds, such as isothiocyanates, set it apart from other mustard species and contribute to its unique medicinal profile.
