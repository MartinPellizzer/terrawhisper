Indigofera amblyantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Indigofera amblyantha?

Indigofera amblyantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. The plant is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its legume-bearing nature. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Indigofera, and its specific classification is completed by its species name, amblyantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Indigofera amblyantha has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of small, oblong leaflets and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary racemes. The plant typically presents as a perennial shrub or subshrub characterized by woody stems that can grow quite robustly. Its leaflets are often arranged in an opposite pattern along the rachis and possess a smooth or slightly pubescent texture. The inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils, bearing tiny, pea-like blossoms that are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. These floral structures eventually develop into small, flattened legumes that contain the seeds.
