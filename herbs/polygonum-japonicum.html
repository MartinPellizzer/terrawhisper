Polygonum japonicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum japonicum?

Polygonum japonicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Polygonaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Polygonum and is specifically designated by the species name japonicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum japonicum has erect, herbaceous stems that are typically cylindrical, glabrous, and marked by distinct swollen nodes. The leaves are simple, alternate, and possess an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire margins and short petioles. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary clusters and generally feature five tepals that are pink or white in color. The plant develops a creeping rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread through underground networks. During maturation, the stems may develop a reddish hue and become somewhat woody at the base.
