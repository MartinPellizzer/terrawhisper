What's the taxonomical classification of Syzygium araiocladum?

Syzygium araiocladum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its lineage through the order Myrtales, it is placed in the family Myrtaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Syzygium, and its specific species designation, araiocladum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syzygium araiocladum has elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, characterized by an entire margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface.

The leaf arrangement is opposite, with distinct pinnate venation visible on the underside. The plant features woody stems that develop a rugged bark as it matures. Its inflorescences are produced in small clusters, eventually giving rise to fleshy, globose fruits.

These fruits often transition in color from green to a deeper hue upon ripening.
