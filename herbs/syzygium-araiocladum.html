Syzygium araiocladum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Syzygium araiocladum has elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, characterized by an entire margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface. The leaf arrangement is opposite, with distinct pinnate venation visible on the underside. The plant features woody stems that develop a rugged bark as it matures. Its inflorescences are produced in small clusters, eventually giving rise to fleshy, globose fruits. These fruits often transition in color from green to a deeper hue upon ripening.
