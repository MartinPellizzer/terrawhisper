Botanical Identification of Ladys' Mantle (Digitalis lutea)

To identify Ladys' Mantle (Digitalis lutea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Ladys' Mantle, or Digitalis lutea, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Plantaginales, family Plantaginaceae, genus Digitalis, and species Digitalis lutea. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the group of monocots.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name for Ladys' Mantle is Digitalis lutea, a member of the Plantaginaceae family. It was first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. Common names include Lady's Mantle and Cuckoo's Mantle, while regional names vary across Europe. Historically, it was also known as the "Witch's Mantle" in some areas.

Morphological Characteristics

Ladys' Mantle (Digitalis lutea) is a biennial plant with a compact, upright growth habit. It has a short, fibrous root system. The stems are hairy and branching, typically growing up to 60 cm tall. Its leaves are broadly ovate with notched edges and a hairy surface. The inflorescence is a tall, upright spike bearing yellow flowers with spurred petals. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Ladys' Mantle (Digitalis lutea) can be distinguished by its yellow flowers, upright growth habit, and distinctive basal leaves with notched margins. It differs from Digitalis purpurea by its yellow rather than purple flowers and shorter, more compact stems. The leaves are broader and more heart-shaped compared to other similar species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Ladys' Mantle (Digitalis lutea) is native to Europe and parts of Western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found in meadows and woodland edges. It grows at elevations between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Ladys' Mantle blooms from June to August, producing vibrant red to orange flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification, harvest timing is critical during the flowering period to ensure accurate recognition of the plant.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Ladys' Mantle (Digitalis lutea) belongs to the genus Digitalis in the family Plantaginaceae. It is closely related to other species like Digitalis purpurea and Digitalis ferganiensis. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, reflecting their close phylogenetic relationships within the genus.
