Digitalis lutea treats heart failure because it contains bioactive constituents such as digoxin and digitoxin, which enhance cardiac contractility and regulate heart rhythm.

These compounds work by inhibiting the sodium-potassium ATPase pump, increasing intracellular calcium levels, and improving the heart's pumping efficiency.

This benefit can relieve conditions such as congestive heart failure and arrhythmias, supporting better cardiac function in patients with weakened heart muscles.

Additionally, it may help reduce symptoms like shortness of breath and fluid retention associated with heart failure.
