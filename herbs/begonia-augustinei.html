Begonia augustinei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia augustinei has asymmetrical, ovate leaves that are characterized by a deep reddish-brown or bronze coloration and a noticeably puckered, bullate texture. The leaf margins are irregularly serrated, adding to the complex visual profile of the foliage. These leaves emerge from a rhizomatous stem that stays relatively compact near the soil surface. Small, delicate flowers typically appear in clusters, contrasting with the heavy, textured appearance of the leaves. The plant's overall structure reflects a specialized adaptation to shaded, humid forest environments.

      What are the herbal preparations of Begonia augustinei?

      Begonia augustinei is prepared as a decoction, a tea, or a topical poultice depending on the specific ailment being treated.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Begonia augustinei can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or pets. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to dermatitis or localized redness on sensitive skin areas. If the leaves or stems are eaten, the presence of oxalic acid can cause painful burning in the mouth and throat. Swallowing even small amounts may result in nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps. Proper care should be taken to wash hands thoroughly after handling this species to avoid accidental exposure.
