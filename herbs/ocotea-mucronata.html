What is Ocotea mucronata?

Ocotea mucronata is a species of plant that belongs to the family Lauraceae.

It is a tree that grows up to 25 meters in height, with a straight and cylindrical trunk, a smooth grey bark, and a rounded crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, elliptical, and acute at the apex, measuring 10-15 centimeters in length and 5-7 centimeters in width. The inflorescence is a raceme of 5-10 flowers, yellowish-green in color, and fragrant, while the fruit is a drupe, 2-3 centimeters in diameter, red when mature.

The plant is native to the tropical forests of Central and South America, including Costa Rica, Panama, and Colombia.
