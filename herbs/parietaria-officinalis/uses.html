Parietary (Parietaria officinalis) Uses

Parietaria officinalis is used to treat asthma, allergic rhinitis, and respiratory allergies due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It helps alleviate symptoms of bronchitis, sinusitis, and mucous membrane inflammation. The plant is also applied for skin conditions like eczema and inflammation, though it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals.

Parietary (Parietaria officinalis) Monograph

1. Asthma

Parietaria officinalis has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. It is believed to help reduce inflammation and mucus production in the airways. Its anti-inflammatory properties may contribute to easing breathing difficulties associated with asthma. However, it should be used under professional guidance due to potential allergic reactions.

2. Allergic rhinitis

Parietaria officinalis has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose by modulating immune responses. Its use is often combined with other herbs in herbal formulations for enhanced relief.

3. Respiratory infections

Parietaria officinalis is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and easing symptoms of respiratory infections. Its antihistamine properties may help alleviate coughing and congestion. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to address mild to moderate respiratory issues.

4. Bronchitis

Parietaria officinalis has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce bronchial swelling and ease breathing. It is often prepared as a tincture or infusion to address symptoms like coughing and mucus buildup. Long-term use should be monitored due to potential allergenic effects.

5. Sinusitis

Parietaria officinalis has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of sinusitis due to its anti-inflammatory and mucolytic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and mucus production, supporting respiratory comfort. Often prepared as a tincture or infusion, it is used in herbal medicine to address chronic or acute sinus inflammation. Its action supports clearing sinus passages and reducing irritation.

6. Allergic conjunctivitis

Parietaria officinalis has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic conjunctivitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce eye redness, itching, and swelling associated with allergic reactions. The plant's extracts are often prepared as infusions or tinctures for local application. Its use is supported by historical herbal practices and some modern studies on its pharmacological effects.

7. Respiratory allergies

Parietaria officinalis is used in herbal medicine to alleviate respiratory allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce symptoms like sneezing, itching, and congestion by modulating immune responses. Traditionally, it has been employed to support respiratory health in allergic conditions. Its use is often combined with other herbs for enhanced efficacy.

8. Mucous membrane inflammation

Parietaria officinalis is used to soothe mucous membrane inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It is often applied externally as a poultice or infused oil to reduce swelling and irritation in conditions like sore throats or inflamed skin. Its mucilage content helps to coat and protect sensitive tissues, promoting healing.

9. Skin inflammation

Parietaria officinalis is used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and swelling associated with conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Topical applications are common, though internal use may also be recommended under professional guidance.

10. Eczema

Parietaria officinalis has been traditionally used to treat eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce skin irritation and redness, promoting healing. External applications of its extracts or infusions can alleviate symptoms, though internal use should be cautious and guided by a practitioner.
