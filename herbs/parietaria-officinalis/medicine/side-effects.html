10 Possible Side Effects Of Parietaria Officinalis (Pellitory)

Parietaria officinalis has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can occur due to allergic sensitivity, misuse, or excessive consumption.

For example, gastrointestinal issues can worsen in individuals with pre-existing conditions, leading to malnutrition, weight loss, and fatigue, affecting daily life and productivity.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Parietaria officinalis if used imporperly.

1. Interferes with medication efficacy

Parietaria officinalis causes allergic reactions due to its high content of sesquiterpenes and flavonoids.

These compounds can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and redness of the skin.

The allergenic properties may also cause respiratory issues like coughing, sneezing, and congestion, especially in individuals with pre-existing allergies or sensitivities.

2. Interferes with medication efficacy

Parietaria officinalis triggers anaphylaxis attacks due to its allergenic properties.

The plant contains allergens such as proteins and glycoproteins that can stimulate the immune system to release histamine, leading to severe symptoms like hives, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing.

In rare cases, this reaction can escalate into a life-threatening anaphylactic shock.

4. Interferes with medication efficacy

Parietaria officinalis induces asthma symptoms in some individuals.

This is because its pollen contains allergens that can trigger an immune response, leading to the production of IgE antibodies and subsequent release of histamine, which causes the characteristic symptoms of asthma, including wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the plant's pollen can also increase mucus production in the airways, further exacerbating asthmatic episodes.

5. Interferes with medication efficacy

Parietaria officinalis affects liver function due to its ability to stimulate the production of bile and alter liver enzyme activity.

This may cause mild elevations in liver enzymes, potentially leading to liver damage or even jaundice in severe cases.

The herb's saponin content, responsible for its medicinal properties, is believed to be the primary contributor to these hepatic effects.

6. Interferes with medication efficacy

Parietaria officinalis interferes with blood clotting by inhibiting platelet aggregation and increasing the risk of bleeding due to its ability to suppress thromboxane A2 production.

This is thought to be attributed to the plant's flavonoid content, which has been shown to have anticoagulant properties.

The disruption in normal blood coagulation processes can lead to increased bleeding time and potential hemorrhagic complications.

7. Interferes with medication efficacy

Parietaria officinalis increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to thin the blood and interfere with platelet function.

The herb's coumarin content is responsible for this effect, which can lead to increased bleeding in individuals taking anticoagulant medications or suffering from bleeding disorders.

Additionally, Parietaria officinalis may interact with other herbs that also have antiplatelet properties, further enhancing its blood-thinning effects.

8. Interferes with medication efficacy

Parietaria officinalis suppresses immune system by interacting with the body's natural defenses and reducing its ability to fight off infections.

This is likely due to the herb's anti-inflammatory properties, which can inhibit the production of immune cells and cytokines that normally help to combat disease.

As a result, individuals using Parietaria officinalis may be more susceptible to illnesses and infections.

9. Interferes with medication efficacy

Parietaria officinalis exacerbates autoimmune diseases.

This is because the herb contains a number of bioactive compounds that can stimulate the immune system and lead to an overactive response. In individuals with pre-existing autoimmune conditions, this stimulation can cause their condition to worsen, leading to increased symptoms and reduced quality of life.

The specific compounds responsible for this effect are believed to be the sesquiterpenoids and phenolic acids present in the herb, which have been shown to enhance immune cell activity and increase inflammation.
