What to know about Lepidium virginicum (wild cress) before using it medicinally

Wild cress, scientifically known as Lepidium virginicum, is a nutrient-rich herb that has been revered for its numerous health benefits.

Packed with vitamins A, C, and K, calcium, and iron, wild cress is believed to boost immune function, reduce inflammation, and even support cardiovascular health. In the culinary world, wild cress is often used as a peppery addition to salads, sandwiches, and sauces, adding a burst of flavor and texture. As a horticultural specimen, wild cress is an easy-to-grow annual that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice among gardeners.

Botanically speaking, wild cress belongs to the Brassicaceae family, sharing similarities with other cruciferous plants like broccoli and cauliflower, while its historical significance dates back to ancient Roman times when it was used as a medicinal remedy.
