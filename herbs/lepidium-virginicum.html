Side Effects of lepidium virginicum

Lepidium virginicum induces vomiting, which can be particularly distressing and potentially dangerous if ingested in large quantities.

Individuals may experience headaches or respiratory issues as a result of inhaling its airborne particles or consuming it improperly. Furthermore, Lepidium virginicum can trigger allergic reactions, including throat swelling and nasal congestion, which may require immediate medical attention. In more severe cases, it may lead to coughing, stomach pain, or even heart palpitations, highlighting the importance of caution when handling or using this herb.

Due to its potential for causing a range of adverse effects, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using Lepidium virginicum for any medicinal or therapeutic purpose.
