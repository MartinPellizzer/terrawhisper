Gleditsia sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Gleditsia sinensis?

Gleditsia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized within the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, it is specifically identified by the genus Gleditsia and the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gleditsia sinensis has pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, featuring numerous small, ovate leaflets arranged along a central rachis. The tree is characterized by its deciduous nature and a bark texture that becomes deeply furrowed as the specimen matures. Its branches are often armed with large, branched thorns that serve as a defensive mechanism against herbivores. Small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers emerge in clusters during the spring season to facilitate pollination. The resulting legume-like pods are flattened and contain several seeds essential for the plant's reproductive cycle.

What are the pharmacological activities of Gleditsia sinensis?

This plant has anti-hyperlipidemic, anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidant, anti-cancer, and anti-tumor pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize harmful free radicals to prevent cellular damage within the body. The anti-inflammatory effects suggest its potential for reducing systemic swelling and chronic irritation. Research into its anti-hyperlipidemic capabilities indicates it may assist in managing high cholesterol levels. Furthermore, the plant shows significant promise in anti-cancer and anti-tumor studies by inhibiting the growth and spread of malignant cells.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Gleditscia sinensis Lam. has been used in traditional Chinese medicine as a chief ingredient for treating obesity and thrombosis. The fruit aqueous extract (GAE) was tested on Japanese white rabbits fed a high-fat diet and supplemented with 6 or 12 mg/kg/day (p.o.). Serum lipid profiles including triglycerides (TG), total cholesterol (TC), and low density lipoprotein cholesterol (LDL-C) were determined on the 0th, 4th, 8th, and 14th week. Prophylactic treatment with GAE significantly decreased lipid levels of the serum, aorta, and liver while attenuating aortic atherosclerosis and improving aortic remodeling. The study also measured activities of aspartate aminotransferase (AST), alanine aminotransferase (ALT), and creatine kinase (CK) and found no significant liver or muscle toxicity. These findings suggest GAE has therapeutic potential in treating hyperlipidemia-related cardiovascular diseases through anti-hyperlipidemic activity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive medicinal compounds including gleditsiaponin C, gleditsiaponin E, gleditsioside H, gleditsioside I, and gleditsioside J. These specific triterpenoid saponins and glycosides are the primary chemical constituents identified within the species. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential pharmacological activities in various biological systems. The unique structural configurations of these saponins contribute to the plant's traditional medicinal uses. Ongoing chemical analysis focuses on how these specific glycosides interact with cellular targets.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", six bisdesmosidic triterpenoidal saponins were isolated from the anomalous fruits of Gleditsia sinensis. These medicinal compounds include gleditsioside H, gleditsioside K, gleditsioside I, gleditsioside J, gleditsia saponin C', and gleditsia saponin E'. The specific structures of these 6 compounds were established using a combination of chemical degradation and extensive NMR studies. These NMR methods included DEPT, DQF-COSY, HETCOR, HOHAHA, HMBC, and ROESY.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating cancer, hyperlipidemia, inflammation, malignancies, and tumor growth. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds possess potent antioxidant properties that may help inhibit the progression of various malignancies. These chemical constituents are also studied for their ability to reduce systemic inflammation within the body. Additionally, the plant shows promise in managing hyperlipidemia by helping to regulate lipid levels in the blood. Such therapeutic applications highlight its potential role in addressing both metabolic and oncological health conditions.

According to a study published by "Phytotherapy research : PTR", aqueous extracts of Gleditsia sinensis were evaluated for antiproliferative activity. This research tested the extract on 8 cancer cell lines, which included 5 human and 3 murine cell lines representing different tissues such as breast, lung, pancreas, and prostate. The study found that all crude aqueous extracts demonstrated growth inhibitory activity on some or all of the cancer cell lines. These results indicate the potential use of the herb as an antineoplastic agent. Further studies are warranted to evaluate its mechanism of action and the isolation of active antitumoer compounds.
