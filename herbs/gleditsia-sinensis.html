Gleditsia sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Gleditsia sinensis?

Gleditsia sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized within the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, it is specifically identified by the genus Gleditsia and the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gleditsia sinensis has pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, featuring numerous small, ovate leaflets arranged along a central rachis. The tree is characterized by its deciduous nature and a bark texture that becomes deeply furrowed as the specimen matures. Its branches are often armed with large, branched thorns that serve as a defensive mechanism against herbivores. Small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers emerge in clusters during the spring season to facilitate pollination. The resulting legume-like pods are flattened and contain several seeds essential for the plant's reproductive cycle.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds gleditsiia saponin C, gleditsiia saponin E, gleditsioside H, gleditsioside I, and gleditsioside J. These specific triterpenoid saponins are primary bioactive constituents identified within the plant's chemical profile. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential pharmacological activities and therapeutic properties. Each compound possesses a unique chemical structure that contributes to the plant's overall medicinal value. Understanding the distribution of these glycosides is essential for advancing natural product chemistry.

As discussed in a study, six bisdesmosidic triterpenoidal saponins were isolated from the anomalous fruits of Gleditsia sinensis. These medicinal compounds include gleditsioside H, gleditsioside I, gleditsioside K, gleditsia saponin C', and gleditsia saponin E'. The specific structures of these 6 compounds were established through a combination of extensive NMR studies, including DEPT, DQF-COSY, HETCOR, HOHAHA, HMBC, and ROESY. Chemical degradation was also utilized to establish their structures.
