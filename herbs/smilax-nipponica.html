What's the taxonomical classification of Smilax nipponica?

Smilax nipponica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression through the order Liliales, it is placed within the family Smilacaceae. Finally, the plant is identified under the genus Smilax, with its specific designation being the species nipponica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax nipponica has woody climbing stems equipped with axillary thorns, trifoliate leaves with ovate leaflets, and small greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries. The leaves are typically arranged in groups of three, featuring prominent veins and smooth or slightly serrated margins. Its stems utilize these sharp spines to scramble through surrounding vegetation to reach sunlight. During the flowering season, the plant produces small clusters of inconspicuous blossoms. These flowers eventually transform into fleshy, globose fruits that serve as a food source for various animals.
