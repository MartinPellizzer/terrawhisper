Aeschynomene elaphroxylon

What's the taxonomical classification of Aeschynomene elaphroxylon?

Aeschynomene elaphroxylon belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the lineage of the order Fabales. It is further categorized under the family Fabaceae, a group known for its significant ecological importance. Within this family, the plant is situated in the genus Aeschynomene, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species elaphroxylon.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Aeschynomene elaphroxylon contains diverse phytochemicals including flavonoids, alkaloids, tannins, and saponins that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These bioactive compounds are primarily concentrated in the roots and leaves of the plant. Research indicates that these substances possess significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Some studies also suggest that the plant extracts exhibit anti-inflammatory effects in various biological models. These chemical constituents form the basis for its traditional use in treating diverse ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Aeschynomene elaphroxylon?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach pains, and various inflammatory conditions. Local healers often prepare decoctions or poultices from the leaves and stems to apply directly to affected skin areas. When ingested in controlled amounts, the plant material is believed to alleviate gastrointestinal distress and internal aches. Its medicinal properties are frequently utilized in traditional practices to manage localized swelling and minor abrasions. These applications vary depending on the specific preparation method used by practitioners.
