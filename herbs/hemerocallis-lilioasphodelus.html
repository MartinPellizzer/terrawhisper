What's the taxonomical classification of Hemerocallis lilioasphodelus?

Hemerocallis lilioasphodeluus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchy through its order, Asparagales, the plant is placed within the family Asphodelaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Hemerocallis and the species lilioasphodeluus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemerocallis lilioasphodelus has long, linear, strap-like leaves that grow in dense basal clumps and emerge from thick, fleshy rhizomes. These leaves are typically bright green and possess a smooth, parallel venation characteristic of monocots. The plant produces large, trumpet-shaped flowers that vary in color from yellow to orange and feature prominent stamens. Each bloom is held aloft by a sturdy, leafless scape that rises directly from the center of the foliage. The overall structure is robust, allowing the plant to form sprawling colonies through its underground spreading root systems.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, commonly known as the daylily, thrives in well-drained soil with a neutral to slightly acidic pH and requires full sun to six hours of direct light daily for optimal blooming.

Regular watering is necessary during dry spells, but the plant is notably drought-tolerant once its root system is established.
