Shorea multiflora

Shorea multiflora, commonly known as the "Borneo Ironwood", is a type of large tree species native to the tropical forests of Borneo.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin infections, due to its numerous health benefits.

The plant's therapeutic actions are attributed to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antimicrobial properties, which help to reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote wound healing.

The Shorea multiflora contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and make it a valuable resource in traditional medicine.
