Cyperus malaccensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyperus malaccensis?

Cyperus malaccensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this biological classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical descent, the plant is organized under the order Poales and falls within the family Cyperaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by its specific genus, Cyperus, and its unique species designation, malaccensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus malaccensis has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by triangular stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces long, slender leaves that emerge from the base and are typically linear in shape. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal clusters of spikelets that appear yellowish-green in color. These spikelets are arranged in a compact manner at the apex of the culm. The root system consists of a creeping rhizome that allows the plant to spread through wetland environments.
