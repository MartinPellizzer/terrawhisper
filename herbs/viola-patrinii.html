Viola patrinii

What's the taxonomical classification of Viola patrinii?

Viola patrinii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Violaceae. Finally, it is identified within the genus Viola, specifically as the species patriini.

    What are the herbal preparations of Viola patrinii?

    Viola patrinii is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical tincture for medicinal use. The dried aerial parts are simmered in water to extract soluble compounds for internal consumption. Alternatively, soaking the plant material in alcohol creates a concentrated tincture suitable for more potent dosing. These liquid preparations are often applied externally to soothe skin irritations or localized inflammation. Each form serves a specific therapeutic purpose depending on the desired strength and method of administration.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Viola patrinii can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically. While specific toxicological studies on this particular species are limited, most members of the Viola genus contain saponins that may trigger nausea or vomiting. Contact with the plant can lead to contact dermatitis characterized by redness, itching, or small rashes on sensitive skin. Individuals with known allergies to the violet family should exercise extreme caution to avoid respiratory or dermal hypersensitivity. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when encountering unknown wild flora.
