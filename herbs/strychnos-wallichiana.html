Strychnos wallichiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Strychnos wallichiana?

Strychnos wallichiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this botanical lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Gentianales and the family Loganiaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific classification within the genus Strychnos, culminating in its unique species designation, Strychnos wallichiana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Strychnos wallichiana contains a variety of bioactive alkaloids, most notably strychnine, brucine, and various indole alkaloids that exhibit significant pharmacological properties. These chemical constituents are primarily studied for their potent central nervous system effects and potential antimicrobial activities. Some researchers focus on the plant's ability to produce specific glycosides which may influence its traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions. The complex chemical profile of the plant also includes diverse secondary metabolites that serve as precursors for pharmaceutical synthesis. Understanding these precise molecular structures is essential for isolating compounds that can be used in future medicinal developments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Strychnos wallichiana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, wounds, digestive disorders, and various types of inflammation. The bark of the species is often employed in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal distress. It also serves as a topical remedy for healing cuts and managing skin infections. Some practitioners utilize extracts from the plant to reduce swelling and ease localized pain. These medicinal applications rely on the specific chemical properties found within its woody parts.
