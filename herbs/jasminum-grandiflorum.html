Medicinal Parts of jasminum grandiflorum

Jasminum grandiflorum has many medicinal parts, such as its flowers, leaves, and essential oil, which are used for their therapeutic properties.

The flowers of Jasminum grandiflorum are rich in flavonoids, including jasmolin, jasmolinic acid, and beta-sitosterol, which are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

The leaves of the plant contain various alkaloids, including jasminine, jasminine oxide, and grandiflorine, which have been reported to exhibit antimicrobial and antispasmodic activities.

The essential oil of Jasminum grandiflorum, obtained from the flowers, contains linalool, benzaldehyde, and geraniol, which are used in aromatherapy for their calming and relaxing effects.

Possible Side Effects of jasminum grandiflorum

Jasminum grandiflorum can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and respiratory problems.

Prolonged use of the herb may also lead to dizziness, headaches, and nausea, particularly in individuals with pre-existing health conditions.

In some cases, excessive consumption of Jasminum grandiflorum may interact with medications, such as blood thinners, and reduce their effectiveness.

It is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using the herb to avoid these potential side effects.
