Possible Side Effects of jasminum grandiflorum

Jasminum grandiflorum can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing allergic reactions in some individuals, including skin rashes, itching, and swelling.

It may also interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, leading to increased bleeding risks and blood sugar fluctuations.

Ingesting large quantities of the herb can result in stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea.

Additionally, pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers should exercise caution when using Jasminum grandiflorum, as it may stimulate uterine contractions and affect milk production.
