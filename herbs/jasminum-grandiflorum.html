Jasminum grandiflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum grandiflorum?

Jasminum grandiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Lamiales, it falls under the family Oleaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Jasminum, specifically identified by its species name, grandiflorum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Jasminum grandiflorum contains high concentrations of jasmonoids, terpenoids, and various phenolic compounds that contribute to its bioactive properties. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers have identified specific volatile oils within the flowers that possess antimicrobial qualities against certain pathogens. Additionally, the presence of glycosides suggests that the plant may have sedative or calming effects on the nervous system. Further studies are often required to isolate these specific molecules for pharmaceutical applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Jasminum grandiflorum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, digestive disorders, and various types of fever. The flowers and leaves are frequently prepared into topical applications to soothe irritation and minor wounds. In some traditional practices, extracts are administered orally to help manage symptoms of internal discomfort. These medicinal properties are often attributed to the plant's specific phytochemical components. Scientific investigation into its exact therapeutic mechanisms is still ongoing in many regions.
