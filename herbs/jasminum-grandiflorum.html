Side Effects of jasminum grandiflorum

Jasminum grandiflorum leads to allergic reaction in some individuals, particularly those with sensitivities to its essential oils or pollen.

The herb can cause skin irritation, manifesting as redness, burning, or a tingling sensation upon contact with the skin. Exposure to its pollen or extracts may also induce eye irritation, resulting in redness, watering, and a gritty feeling.

Additionally, it can trigger respiratory issues, such as throat irritation, coughing, and sneezing, especially in people with asthma or allergies. Prolonged exposure may lead to skin rash, itching, and dryness, making it important for those with sensitive skin or respiratory conditions to use the herb with caution.

While Jasminum grandiflorum is commonly used in aromatherapy and cosmetics, its potential to cause these adverse effects underscores the need for proper handling and awareness of individual sensitivities.
