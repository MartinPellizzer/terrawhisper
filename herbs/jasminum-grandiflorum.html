Jasminum grandiflorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum grandiflorum?

Jasminum grandiflorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Lamiales, it falls under the family Oleaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized within the genus Jasminum, specifically identified by its species name, grandiflorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum grandiflorum has woody, climbing stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that measure approximately five to ten centimeters in length. The foliage is typically evergreen, displaying a glossy texture and a deep green hue on the upper surface. Its most distinguishing feature is the large, fragrant, white flowers that emerge in clusters from the axils of the leaves. Each blossom consists of five to nine petals that form a star-like shape, often reaching several centimeters in diameter. The plant develops a dense, sprawling habit as the vines weave through supporting structures.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and parts of Malaysia. It thrives primarily in the dense, humid undergrowth of evergreen forests where it can climb towards sunlight. Within these habitats, it prefers areas with well-drained soil and consistent moisture to support its vigorous climbing habit. Its distribution is closely tied to warm climates that prevent frost damage to its delicate foliage. Because it favors these specific environmental conditions, it is rarely found in more temperate or arid zones.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-draining, fertile soil, consistent moisture without waterlogging, and full sun to partial shade to thrive.

It flourishes in warm, humid climates where temperatures remain stable throughout the growing season. Regular fertilization during the spring and summer months helps encourage the production of its large, highly fragrant white blossoms. Providing support such as a trellis or fence is necessary as it is a vigorous climbing vine.

Pruning after the blooming period ensures the plant maintains a healthy shape and promotes new growth.

What are the pharmacological activities of Jasminum grandiflorum?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, cytotoxic, gastroprotective, and renoprotective pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help mitigate oxidative stress by neutralizing harmful free radicals in the body. The anti-inflammatory actions contribute to reducing swelling and pain associated with various physiological conditions. Through its cytotoxic effects, the plant shows potential in targeting and destroying specific abnormal cells. Furthermore, its gastroprotective and renoprotective qualities offer defense mechanisms for the stomach lining and kidney tissues, respectively.

According to a study published by "Biomedicine & pharmacotherapy = Biomedecine & pharmacotherapie", Jasminum grandiflorum L. is a medicinal plant used to treat hepatitis and gastritis. The study analyzed the effects of 4 different extracts and 2 compounds from the flower of J. grandiflorum in a mouse model of HCl/EtOH-induced gastric ulcer. These flower extracts alleviated gastric mucosal ulceration by increasing PGE2 production and the activity of antioxidant enzymes. Furthermore, the extracts worked by suppressing reactive oxygen species (ROS) generation, lipid peroxidation, apoptosis-related proteins, pro-inflammatoty cytokines and nitric oxide (NO) production.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of medicinal compounds including (Z)-jasmone, (Z)-methyl jasmonoate, isoquercitrin, ursolic acid, and various types of phenolic content. The presence of (Z)-jasmone and (Z)-methyl jasmonoate suggests potential roles in regulating plant defense mechanisms and biological signaling. Isoquercitrin contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties through its flavonoid structure. Additionally, ursolic acid is recognized for its significant anti-inflammatory and anti-tumor activities. The overall high phenolic content further enhances the therapeutic potential of Jasminum grandiflorum by providing broad-spectrum bioactive benefits.

According to a study published by "Natural product communications", the flowers of Jasminum grandiflorum L. can produce a concrete at 0.35% and an absolute at 0.27% through specific extraction processes. Direct extraction of these flowers using liquid CO2 results in a fat-free product with a 0.26% yield. This liquid CO2 extract is enriched with terpenoids and benzenoids, including major compounds such as benzyl acetate, (E,E)-alpha-farnesene, and (Z)-3-hexenyl benzoate. Other medicinal compounds present include indole, methyl anthranilate, (Z)-jasmone, (Z)-methyl jasmonoate, and (Z)-methyl epi-jasmonoate. While these substances contribute to the fragrance, the yield of the essential oil was recorded as a poor 0.05%.

    What side effects this plant can have?

Some people report redness or itching upon touching the plant. If parts of the jasmine are consumed, it might lead to nausea or stomach upset. Always exercise caution and consult a professional before using any part of the species for topical or medicinal purposes.
