Viola variegata

What's the taxonomical classification of Viola variegata?

Viola variegata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is part of the order Malpighiales. It is a member of the family Violaceae, which contains the genus Viola, and is specifically identified as the species variegata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola variegata has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that exhibit distinct variegated patterns of light and dark green shades. The foliage typically grows in a basal rosette pattern, spreading close to the ground. Its flowers are small and zygomorphic, featuring five petals that often display delicate violet or purplish hues with lighter centers. Thin, creeping stems can extend from the base to facilitate vegetative spreading across the substrate. The reproductive structures are centered within the corolla, supported by a slender pedicel.
