Buddleja macrostachya

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buddlejia macrostachya has lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, featuring serrated margins and a densely pubescent underside. The plant is characterized by its large, elongated terminal panicles which hold numerous small, tubular flowers. These blossoms are often pale lilac or white in color and possess a fragrant scent to attract pollinators. The woody stems are covered in fine hairs that provide a textured surface throughout the growth cycle. This species displays a robust shrubby habit with a spreading crown.
