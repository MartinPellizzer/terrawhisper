Oxytropis glabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxytropis glabra?

Oxytropis glabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. It is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which is known for its diverse range of legumes. Within this family, the plant is placed in the genus Oxytropis, and its specific identity is defined by the species epithet glabra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

The legume-style pods are small, compressed, and often slightly hairy compared to the foliage. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal alkaloids N-methylcytisine, baptifoline, dictamnine, sparteine, and thermopsine. These chemical compounds are primarily classified as quinolizidine alkaloids which exhibit various pharmacological properties. Researchers study these substances for their potential biological activities, including neurotoxic and sedative effects. The presence of these specific molecules characterizes the unique phytochemical profile of the species. Understanding these constituents is essential for exploring the plant's traditional and modern medicinal applications.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", 8 alkaloids were isolated from Oxytropis glabra. These compounds were identified as anagirine, thermopsinine, N-methylcytisine, sparteine, baptifoline, adenine, dictamnine and ethyl allophanate. All 8 of these alkaloids were isolated from this genus for the 1st time. Additionally, ethyl allophanate was found in nature for the 1st time.
