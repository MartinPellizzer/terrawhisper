Oxytropis glabra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxytropis glabra has a perennial herbaceous growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with several pairs of narrow, linear to lanceolate leaflets that are notably smooth and hairless. Its inflorescence consists of small, pea-like flowers that display a range of colors from pale yellow to deep purple. The legume-style pods are small, compressed, and often slightly hairy compared to the foliage. These distinct morphological traits allow the species to be identified within its high-elevation montane habitats.
