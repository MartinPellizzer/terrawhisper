Trichosanthes ovigera

What's the taxonomical classification of Trichosanthes ovigera?

Trichosanthes ovigera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cucurbitales. This plant is a representative of the family Cucurbitaceae, which is characterized by its distinct botanical traits. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Trichosanthes, and its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the species name ovigera.

Trichosanthes ovigera is prepared as decoctions, infusions, powders, or topical pastes depending on the specific therapeutic application. In some traditional practices, the fresh plant material is crushed into a fine powder or mixed with oils to form a medicinal paste for external application. These varied methods allow practitioners to tailor the concentration and delivery of the plant's bioactive constituents.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Individuals should approach its use with caution to avoid these potential physiological complications.
