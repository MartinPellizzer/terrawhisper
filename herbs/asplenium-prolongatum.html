Asplenium prolongatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium prolongatum?

Asplenium prolongatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is positioned in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the hierarchical structure of its lineage, it falls under the order Polypodiales and is a member of the family Aspleniaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Asplenium, specifically as the species prolongatum.

The plant contains bioactive secondary metabolites that inhibit the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway, thereby reducing the production of prostaglandins. Additionally, its antioxidant compounds neutralize reactive oxygen species by modulating enzymatic systems such as superoxide dismutase. Through these specific protein interactions, the plant demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

If the plant is consumed, it might lead to nausea, abdominal cramping, or more severe digestive disruptions. Monitoring for these reactions is essential when handling or studying this specific botanical specimen.
