Cocculus orbiculatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cocculus orbiculatus?

Cocculus orbiculatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Menispermaceae. At the most specific levels of its classification, it is identified under the genus Cocculus, with the specific epithet orbiculatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cocculus orbiculatus has woody, climbing stems with opposite, orbicular to cordate leaves that feature entire or slightly wavy margins and a dull green surface. The foliage is characterized by its rounded shape and distinctively blunt or rounded tips. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, typically appearing in shades of white or pale cream. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark, often blackish color upon ripening. The overall structure of the plant allows it to scramble through surrounding vegetation using its flexible vines.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cocculus orbiculatus?

These diverse biological functions make Cocculus orbiculatus a subject of interest for drug discovery.

According to a study published by "Analytical methods : advancing methods and applications", Cocculus orbiculatus is a medicinal herb valued for its dried roots. These roots possess anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and diuretic therapeutic properties. The study identified a total of 113 components across the root, stem, leaf, flower, and fruit. Through PLS-DA and OPLS-DA, researchers annotated 10 differentiating components with VIP > 1.5, P < 0.05, FC > 2 or FC < 0.67. Among these, 5 components were unique to the root and stem and exhibited lower mass spectrometric responses.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including the specific alkaloids (+)-coccuorbiculatin A and (-)-sinococuline, alongside various flavonoids and organic acids. These bioactive alkaloids are often studied for their unique pharmacological properties and potential therapeutic applications. In addition to these nitrogenous substances, the plant is rich in flavonoids which contribute to its antioxidant profile. The presence of various organic acids further complicates its complex chemical composition. Together, these various chemical constituents define the medicinal potential of Cocculus orbiculatus.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", the stems of Taiwanese Cocculus orbiculatus contain several medicinal compounds. These include two new bisbenzylisoquinoline alkaloids, (+)-coccuorbiculatine A (2) and (+)-10-hydroxyisotrilobine (3). The plant also provides two new amidic aporphes, specifically a mixture of (+)-laurelliptinhexadecan-1-one (6) and (+)-laurelliptinoctadecan-1-one (7). Additionally, one new protoberberiine, (-)-4-methoxy-13,14-dihydrooxypalmatine (8), was isolated. The cytotoxicity of these compounds was tested against HepG2, Hep3B, MCF-7, and MDA-MB-231 cancer cell lines. Among them, alkaloids 1 and (-)-sinococuline (9) demonstrated significant inhibitory activity against the target cell lines.
