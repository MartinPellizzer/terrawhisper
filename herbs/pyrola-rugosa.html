Pyrola rugosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Pyrola rugosa?

Pyrola rugosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is positioned within the order Ericales. The plant is further categorized under the family Ericaceae, falling into the genus Pyrola, with its specific designation being the species rugosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pyrola rugosa has a perennial rhizomatous habit characterized by opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with distinctively rugose or wrinkled surfaces and serrated margins. The stems are typically erect and can reach heights of several decimeters, often supporting small, terminal clusters of flowers. These flowers are bell-shaped, typically white or pinkish, and possess a prominent, hooded upper sepal. The plant also features a specialized root system designed for nutrient absorption in moist, often acidic soil environments.
