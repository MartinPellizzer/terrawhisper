Pandanus candelabrum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pandanus candelabrum has a distinctive woody architecture characterized by multiple branching stems that radiate from a central point to form a candelabra-like shape. The plant features long, sword-shaped leaves with serrated edges that spiral around the branches in dense clusters. To support its weight in sandy or unstable coastal soils, it develops prominent stilt roots that emerge from the lower trunk. These aerial prop roots provide both stability and nutrient uptake for the sprawling canopy. The overall structure creates a wide, spreading silhouette that is highly adapted to tropical island environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of the Indian Ocean, specifically distributed across the Mascarene Islands including Mauritius and Réunion. It grows primarily within coastal and lowland habitats where moisture levels are consistent. The species is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline or within moist forest margins. While its natural range is limited to these specific island groups, its presence is highly dependent on the unique volcanic soil compositions of the region. Its distribution remains relatively localized due to these specific ecological requirements.
