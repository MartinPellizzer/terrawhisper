Pandanus candelabrum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pandanus candelabrum?

Pandanus candelabrum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further categorized under the order Pandanales and the family Pandanaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Pandanus, and its specific species, candelabrum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pandanus candelabrum has a distinctive woody architecture characterized by multiple branching stems that radiate from a central point to form a candelabra-like shape. The plant features long, sword-shaped leaves with serrated edges that spiral around the branches in dense clusters. To support its weight in sandy or unstable coastal soils, it develops prominent stilt roots that emerge from the lower trunk. These aerial prop roots provide both stability and nutrient uptake for the sprawling canopy. The overall structure creates a wide, spreading silhouette that is highly adapted to tropical island environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of the Indian Ocean, specifically distributed across the Mascarene Islands including Mauritius and Réunion. It grows primarily within coastal and lowland habitats where moisture levels are consistent. The species is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline or within moist forest margins. While its natural range is limited to these specific island groups, its presence is highly dependent on the unique volcanic soil compositions of the region. How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires a tropical environment with high humidity, consistent warmth between 70 and 85 degrees Fahrenheit, and well-draining sandy or loamy soil to thrive.

It prefers bright, indirect sunlight but can tolerate some direct morning sun if the air remains moist. Regular watering is essential to keep the substrate damp, though the roots must never sit in stagnant water to avoid rot. Providing adequate space is important because the woody stems expand significantly as the plant matures into its characteristic candelabra shape.

Periodic fertilization during the growing season will support its vigorous structural development.
