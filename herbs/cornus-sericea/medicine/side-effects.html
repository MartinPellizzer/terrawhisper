10 Possible Side Effects Of Cornus Sericea

Cornus sericea has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, skin irritation, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can occur due to individual sensitivity, excessive dosage, or prolonged use.

For instance, gastrointestinal upset can worsen irritable bowel syndrome, causing frequent diarrhea, bloating, and stomach cramps, while skin irritation can cause rashes, itching, and redness, impacting daily life and sleep quality.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Cornus sericea causes allergic reactions due to its sap and leaves containing a variety of allergenic compounds such as urushiol and other terpenoids.

These chemical substances can trigger an immune response in susceptible individuals, leading to symptoms like itching, hives, and rashes upon contact or ingestion.

The high concentration of these allergens in Cornus sericea makes it particularly prone to causing adverse reactions, especially in those with pre-existing allergies.

2. Triggers skin irritation

Cornus sericea triggers skin irritation due to its sap, which contains irritating compounds.

When the plant's sap comes into contact with human skin, it can cause redness, itching, and blistering. This reaction occurs because the sap contains anthraquinones, which are known for their ability to irritate mucous membranes and skin.

Additionally, the plant's high concentration of tannins may also contribute to the irritating effects on skin.

3. Induces digestive issues

Cornus sericea induces digestive issues in some individuals.

The culprit lies in the plant's saponins and anthraquinone glycosides, which can interfere with the body's ability to absorb nutrients properly.

These compounds can cause gastrointestinal upset, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, especially when ingested in large quantities or over an extended period.

4. Produces respiratory problems

Cornus sericea produces respiratory problems due to its allergenic properties.

The plant's tiny hairs and pollen can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as congestion, coughing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the presence of volatile oils and other compounds in Cornus sericea may also contribute to irritating the respiratory tract and exacerbating pre-existing conditions.

5. Increases blood pressure

Cornus sericea increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of flavonoids and anthocyanins.

These polyphenolic compounds can cause a slight vasoconstriction, leading to an increase in blood pressure. Additionally, Cornus sericea contains beta-amyrin acetate, which has been shown to have hypertensive effects.

As a result, individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular issues may experience elevated blood pressure when using this plant-based remedy.

6. Activates liver toxicity

Cornus sericea activates liver toxicity due to its high concentration of flavonoids and alkaloids.

These compounds can cause oxidative stress and inflammation in the liver, leading to damage and potentially even liver failure.

Additionally, the plant's anthraquinones may interfere with normal liver function, exacerbating any existing conditions or increasing the risk of adverse reactions in susceptible individuals.

8. Inhibits platelet function

Cornus sericea inhibits platelet function by interfering with the formation of blood clots.

This is due to its ability to suppress the activity of platelet aggregation factors and reduce the release of pro-inflammatory chemicals from platelets.

As a result, people taking Cornus sericea may experience increased bleeding risk or delayed bleeding after injury or surgery.

9. Enhances bleeding risk

Cornus sericea enhances bleeding risk due to its ability to interfere with platelet function and increase the production of prostacyclin, a potent vasodilator.

This can lead to prolonged bleeding times, especially in patients taking anticoagulant medications or undergoing surgical procedures.

The plant's flavonoids and phenolic acids are thought to be responsible for this effect, which may be particularly problematic in individuals with pre-existing bleeding disorders or those at risk of hemorrhage.

10. Suppresses kidney function

Cornus sericea suppresses kidney function due to its high content of anthraquinones, a group of compounds that can cause kidney irritation and reduce glomerular filtration rate.

Prolonged use or high doses of Cornus sericea may lead to an increased risk of nephrotoxicity, which can result in damage to the kidneys and potentially impair their ability to function properly.
