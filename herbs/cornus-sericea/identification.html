Botanical Identification of Green Corn Dog (Cornus sericea)

To identify Green Corn Dog (Cornus sericea) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Green Corn Dog (Cornus sericea) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Green Corn Dog, scientifically known as Cornus sericea, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Cornaceae, genus Cornus, and species Cornus sericea. This classification places it within the broader group of flowering plants and highlights its relationship to other members of the Cornaceae family.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Green Corn Dog is Cornus sericea, a member of the Cornaceae family. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. Commonly known as green corn dog, it is also referred to as red osier dogwood in some regions. This name reflects its distinctive red stems and small green fruits.

Morphological Characteristics

Green Corn Dog, or Cornus sericea, is a shrub that grows in moist environments. It has a fibrous root system that helps anchor it in wet soil. The stems are slender and often reddish, with a smooth bark. Leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a serrated edge. The inflorescence is a green, bracted cluster that turns red as it matures. Flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a red drupe containing a single seed.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Cornus sericea, or Green Corn Dog, can be distinguished by its bright yellow to greenish-yellow bracts that resemble corn on the cob, its smooth, ribbed stems, and its small white flowers. It lacks the red or purple bracts of Cornus species like Cornus florida, and its leaves are opposite with a waxy coating. These features help differentiate it from similar plants in the Cornus genus.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Green Corn Dog, or Cornus sericea, is native to North America, particularly Canada and the northern United States. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers moist, well-drained soils. This species is commonly found at low to mid-elevation areas, often near water sources such as streams and wetlands.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Green Corn Dog, or Cornus sericea, blooms in early to mid-summer, typically from June to July. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the plant is most easily recognized in late summer when the bright red berries are fully developed and visible.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cornus sericea, or Green Corn Dog, is closely related to other Cornus species within the Cornaceae family. It shares key characteristics with species like Cornus sanguiflora and Cornus alba, including similar leaf structure and flowering patterns. Its placement within the genus Cornus highlights its evolutionary ties to other dogwood species.
