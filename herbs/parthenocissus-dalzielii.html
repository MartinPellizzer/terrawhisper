What's the taxonomical classification of Parthenocissus dalzielii?

Parthenocissus dalzielii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy through the order Vitales and the family Vitaeaceae, the plant is placed into the genus Parthenocissus, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species dalzielii.

      What are the herbal preparations of Parthenocissus dalzielii?

      Parthenocissus dalzielii is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste depending on the intended therapeutic application. The leaves and stems are most commonly boiled in water to extract active compounds for oral consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Parthenocissus dalzielii can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if handled incorrectly or ingested. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If the plant is accidentally consumed, it may trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain in sensitive individuals. There is also a potential for respiratory irritation if the pollen or dust from the plant is inhaled in large quantities. Always use protective gloves when handling this species to minimize the risk of adverse physical reactions.
