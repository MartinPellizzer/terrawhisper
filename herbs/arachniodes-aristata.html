Arachniodes aristata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arachniodes aristata has a rhizomatous growth habit characterized by long-creeping rhizomes and large, deeply divided, multi-pinnate fronds that can reach significant lengths. The individual pinnae are narrow and linear, often featuring prominent, hair-like extensions known as awns at their tips. These distinctively bristly margins give the foliage a feathery or spider-like appearance. The texture of the blades is relatively thin, and the stipes are typically dark or brownish in color. This complex leaf structure allows the plant to effectively capture light within its shaded forest habitat.
