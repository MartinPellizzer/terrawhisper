Pittosporum podocarpum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pittosporum podocarpum has an upright, woody shrub habit reaching up to 3 meters in height with elliptical, leathery leaves that measure approximately 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The foliage is characterized by an entire margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface with a paler underside. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils, typically displaying a pale yellowish or cream coloration. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark reddish or black hue upon maturity. The branching pattern is dense, creating a compact structure often found in forest understories.
