Pittosporum podocarpum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pittosporum podocarpum?

Pittosporum podocarpum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is a member of the order Apiales. More specifically, this plant is situated within the family Pittosporaceae and the genus Pittosporum, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species podocarpum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pittosporum podocarpum has an upright, woody shrub habit reaching up to 3 meters in height with elliptical, leathery leaves that measure approximately 5 to 10 centimeters in length.

The foliage is characterized by an entire margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface with a paler underside. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils, typically displaying a pale yellowish or cream coloration. These blossoms eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark reddish or black hue upon maturity.

The branching pattern is dense, creating a compact structure often found in forest understories.
