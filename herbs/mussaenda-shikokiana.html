Mussaenda shikokiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Mussaenda shikokiana?

Mussaenda shikokiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Gentianaales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, specifically placed within the genus Mussaenda, with its unique designation as the species shikokiana.

These phytochemical constituents modulate the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway to reduce the production of pro-inflammatory mediators like prostaglandins and leukotrienes. The plant's bioactive compounds also interact with antioxidant enzymes such as superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase (CAT) to mitigate cellular damage from reactive oxygen species. Through the regulation of these protein pathways, the extracts demonstrate significant anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and neuroprotective activities. This multi-target mechanism allows the plant to address complex physiological processes involved in pain and swelling.
