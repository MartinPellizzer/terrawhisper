Mussaenda shikokiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mussaenda shikokiana has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated margins and a glossy texture. The plant is most distinguished by its large, showy, bright red or orange calyx lobes that function as petal-like structures to attract pollinators. These enlarged sepals surround much smaller, inconspicuous true flowers located at the center of the cluster. The foliage is arranged oppositely along the stems, providing a lush backdrop for the vibrant floral displays. This combination of dramatic bract-like structures and dense greenery makes the species a striking ornamental specimen.
