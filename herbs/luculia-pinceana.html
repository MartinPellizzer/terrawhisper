Luculia pinceana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Luculia pinceana has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are dark green, leathery, and possess slightly serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a glossy texture and prominent venation that adds depth to the canopy. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, typically displaying a pale yellow or creamy white coloration. These blooms are often solitary or in small clusters, providing a delicate contrast to the dense greenery. The plant develops a woody structure that supports its spreading habit as it matures.
