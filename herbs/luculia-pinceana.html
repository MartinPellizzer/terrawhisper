Luculia pinceana

What's the taxonomical classification of Luculia pinceana?

Luculia pinceana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Luculia, and is identified by its unique species name, pinceana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Luculia pinceana has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are dark green, leathery, and possess slightly serrated margins.

The foliage is characterized by a glossy texture and prominent venation that adds depth to the canopy. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, typically displaying a pale yellow or creamy white coloration. These blooms are often solitary or in small clusters, providing a delicate contrast to the dense greenery.

The plant develops a woody structure that supports its spreading habit as it matures.
