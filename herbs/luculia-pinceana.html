Luculia pinceana

What's the taxonomical classification of Luculia pinceana?

Luculia pinceana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Luculia, and is identified by its unique species name, pinceana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Luculia pinceana has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are dark green, leathery, and possess slightly serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a glossy texture and prominent venation that adds depth to the canopy. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, typically displaying a pale yellow or creamy white coloration. These blooms are often solitary or in small clusters, providing a delicate contrast to the dense greenery. The plant develops a woody structure that supports its spreading habit as it matures.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds cincholic acid 28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl ester, cincholic acid-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, loganin, loganoside, and vogeloside. These specific chemical constituents are primary components identified within the phytochemical profile of Luculia pinceana. The presence of these iridoids and cincholic acid derivatives suggests significant pharmacological potential. Researchers often study these molecules to understand their biological activities in various therapeutic applications. Such detailed chemical analysis helps characterize the unique medicinal properties of this botanical species.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", seven compounds were isolated from the n-BuOH fraction of the ethanolic extract from the stem of Luculia pinceana. These isolated compounds include vogeloside (1), epi-vogeloside (2), loganoside (3), and loganin (4). The study also identified cincholic acid 28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl ester (5), cincholic acid-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, 28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl ester (6), and cincholic acid-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (7). These specific compounds 1-7 were isolated from the genus for the first time.
