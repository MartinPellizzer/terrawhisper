Luculia pinceana

What's the taxonomical classification of Luculia pinceana?

Luculia pinceana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Luculia, and is identified by its unique species name, pinceana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Luculia pinceana has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that are dark green, leathery, and possess slightly serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a glossy texture and prominent venation that adds depth to the canopy. Small, fragrant flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, typically displaying a pale yellow or creamy white coloration. These blooms are often solitary or in small clusters, providing a delicate contrast to the dense greenery. The plant develops a woody structure that supports its spreading habit as it matures.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds cincholic acid 28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl ester, cincholic acid-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, loganin, loganoside, and paeonol. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the overall pharmacological profile of the species. The presence of loganin and loganoside is often linked to various biological activities within the plant's system. Additionally, the compounds cincholic acid 28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl ester and cincholic acid-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside represent important glycosylated derivatives. The inclusion of paeonol further enhances the complex chemical makeup of the organism.

As discussed in a study, the n-BuOH portion of the ethanolic extract from the stem of Luculia pinceana was analyzed to study its chemical constituents. Using column chromatographic techniques and various spectroscopic methods, 7 compounds were isolated from the n-BuOH fraction. These isolated structures include vogeloside (1), epi-vogeloside (2), loganoside (3), loganin (4), cincholic acid 28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl ester (5), cincholic acid-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, 28-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl ester (6), and cincholic acid-3-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside (7). The identification of these compounds was achieved through a combination of IR, FAB-MS, NMR, and 2D NMR spectroscopy. Ultimately, compounds 1-7 were isolated from this genus for the first time.
