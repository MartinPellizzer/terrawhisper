Juncus triceps

What's the taxonomical classification of Juncus triceps?

Juncus triceps belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Poales. Within this order, the plant is situated in the family Juncaceae and the genus Juncus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species triceps.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juncus triceps has cylindrical, unbranched stems that reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and are characterized by three distinct longitudinal ridges. The leaves are reduced to small, scale-like sheaths located at the base of the stems, leaving the upper portions smooth and leafless. Its inflorescence consists of small, brownish flowers arranged in compact clusters at the tips of the stems. Each flower features six tepals that are typically lanceolate in shape and slightly pointed. The plant also produces small, ovoid capsules that contain numerous tiny, dark seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes Mountains, specifically spanning across parts of Peru, Bolivia, Chile, and Argentina. It thrives primarily in the puna ecosystem, which is characterized by cold, arid alpine grasslands. Its distribution is closely tied to specific elevations where rocky soils and seasonal moisture levels support its growth. Because it is adapted to these harsh environments, the species is found in localized pockets throughout the central and southern Andean cordillera.
