Botanical Identification of Chinese Bellflower (Bolbostemma paniculatum)

To identify Chinese Bellflower (Bolbostemma paniculatum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Chinese Bellflower, Bolbostemma paniculatum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Asclepiadaceae, and it is part of the genus Bolbostemma. The species name, Bolbostemma paniculatum, identifies this specific plant within the genus. The other taxonomic levels—division, class, and order—are not provided in the data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chinese Bellflower is Bolbostemma paniculatum, first described by the botanist A. Gray in 1842. It has several synonyms, including Paris panicalata and Lobostemon paniculatum. Common names include Chinese bellflower and mountain bellflower, while regional names vary across areas. Historically, it was also known by different local names in traditional Chinese medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Bellflower (Bolbostemma paniculatum) is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system. It has erect, branched stems that are hairy. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have toothed margins. The inflorescence is a paniculate cluster, with small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Chinese Bellflower can be distinguished by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers with five lobes, opposite ovate leaves with serrated edges, and a hairy stem. It also has a unique root system with thick, fleshy roots. These features help differentiate it from similar species like other bellflowers or related genera.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chinese Bellflower (Bolbostemma paniculatum) is native to regions of China, particularly in Sichuan, Yunnan, and Gansu provinces. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chinese Bellflower typically blooms from June to August. Seed formation occurs from September to October. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during its flowering period, which is between June and August.

Phylogenetic Relationships

The Chinese Bellflower (Bolbostemma paniculatum) is closely related to species within the genus Bolbostemma. It shares morphological and genetic traits with other members of the Campanulaceae family, particularly in its floral structure and leaf arrangement.
