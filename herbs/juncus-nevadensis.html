What is Juncus nevadensis?

Juncus nevadensis is a perennial plant that belongs to the family Juncaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous habit, with leafless stems that grow up to 10-40 cm in height, and linear, grass-like leaves that are 1-3 mm in width. The inflorescence is a spike or panicle, bearing brown, 3-4 mm long flowers that are arranged in a spiral pattern, and are monoecious, with male flowers located below the female flowers. The plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, typically growing in wetlands, riparian areas, and mountain meadows.

Juncus nevadensis is typically found at elevations between 1,500-3,000 meters above sea level.
