Ficus baccata

Ficus baccata, commonly known as the Banyan Fig, is a plant species belonging to the Moraceae family, native to tropical Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It has been traditionally used in various cultures for its medicinal properties, which include health benefits such as treating fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues.

The therapeutic actions of Ficus baccata are attributed to its ability to reduce inflammation, improve wound healing, and exhibit antimicrobial properties.

The bioactive constituents present in the plant include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and other secondary metabolites, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.
