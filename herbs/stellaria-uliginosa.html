Stellaria uliginosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Stellaria uliginosa?

Stellaria uliginosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the hierarchy of biological classification, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic ranks, it is organized under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Stellaria, and its unique species designation, uliginosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria uliginosa has an annual herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems that typically reach heights of 5 to 20 centimeters. The leaves are small, linear to narrowly lanceolate, and arranged oppositely along the stem. Each leaf is typically 5 to 15 millimeters in length and possesses an entire margin with a rounded or slightly acute apex. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that feature five petals and are borne in terminal or axillary clusters. Its root system is relatively shallow, allowing it to colonize moist, disturbed soils effectively.
