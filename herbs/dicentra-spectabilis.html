Dicementra spectabilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ranunculales. This plant is a prominent representative of the family Papaveraceae, which is characterized by its distinct floral structures. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Dicentra, and its specific identification is given by the species epithet spectabilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dicentra spectabilis has deeply lobed, fern-like foliage consisting of light green, compound leaves that emerge from a clump-forming rhizomatous base.

The plant produces arching stems that bear numerous heart-shaped, bladder-like flowers hanging from delicate pedicels. These blossoms typically feature a distinct two-part structure with long, protruding spurs that give them a unique bleeding-heart appearance. The foliage provides a lush, textured backdrop that remains attractive throughout the growing season.

As the plant matures, the delicate canopy of leaves creates a soft, ornamental mound.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Dicentra spectabilis can cause skin irritation, stomach upset, and vomiting if ingested by humans or pets. While the plant is generally not considered highly toxic, the alkaloids present in its tissues may trigger adverse reactions in sensitive individuals. Contact with the foliage or stems can sometimes lead to localized dermatitis or itching on the skin. If swallowed in significant quantities, it may disrupt the digestive system and cause nausea. It is best to wear gloves when handling the plant to avoid potential contact sensitivities.
