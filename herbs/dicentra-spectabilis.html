What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dicentra spectabilis has deeply lobed, fern-like foliage consisting of light green, compound leaves that emerge from a clump-forming rhizomatous base. The plant produces arching stems that bear numerous heart-shaped, bladder-like flowers hanging from delicate pedicels. These blossoms typically feature a distinct two-part structure with long, protruding spurs that give them a unique bleeding-heart appearance. The foliage provides a lush, textured backdrop that remains attractive throughout the growing season. As the plant matures, the delicate canopy of leaves creates a soft, ornamental mound.
