Ostryopsis davidiana has an upright, deciduous shrub or small tree habit characterized by slender, grayish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical with serrated margins. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The fruit consists of small, nut-like structures contained within specialized wing-like bracts that facilitate wind dispersal. These morphological traits distinguish the species within its specific ecological niche.
