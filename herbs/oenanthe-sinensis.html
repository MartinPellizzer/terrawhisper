Oenanthe sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Oenanthe sinensis?

Oenanthe sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Apiales, it falls into the family Apiaceae, a group known for its distinct floral structures. The taxonomic hierarchy concludes with its specific placement in the genus Oenanthe, identifying the plant by its unique species name, sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oenanthe sinensis has an erect, herbaceous perennial growth habit characterized by smooth, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and feature an ovate to lanceolate shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge in clusters, typically displaying a white or pale pink coloration. The root system consists of creeping rhizomes that allow the plant to spread through various wetland habitats. These structural features collectively enable the species to thrive in moist, semi-aquatic environments.
