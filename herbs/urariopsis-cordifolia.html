Urariopsis cordifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Urariopsis cordifolia has cordate to ovate leaves with entire margins and an acute apex, supported by woody stems and a branching habit. The foliage typically displays a smooth surface and a distinctively heart-shaped base as suggested by its specific epithet. Its flowers are small and arranged in compact inflorescences characteristic of the Asteraceae family. The plant's overall structure consists of a perennial shrubby form with specialized reproductive units. These morphological traits allow the species to adapt to its specific ecological niche within tropical environments.
