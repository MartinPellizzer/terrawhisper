Urophysa henryi has a perennial rhizomatous habit characterized by upright, erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are basal and lanceolate in shape, possessing smooth margins and parallel venation typical of monocots. Small, delicate flowers emerge from the stem, often featuring specialized structures adapted for specific pollination syndromes.
