Urophysa henryi

What's the taxonomical classification of Urophysa henryi?

Urophysa henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Ranunculales. This plant is a specific representative of the family Ranunculaceae, situated within the genus Urophysa, and is ultimately identified by its unique species name, henryi.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Urophysa henryi can cause skin irritation, digestive distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to its chemical constituents. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that may trigger allergic reactions upon direct contact with the skin. If swallowed, individuals might experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. There is limited clinical data regarding the long-term effects of its consumption in humans. Therefore, cautious handling and avoidance of ingestion are highly recommended to prevent adverse health outcomes.
