What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia chlamydophylla?

Aristolochia chlamydophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Piperales. This plant is a representative of the family Aristolochiaceae, specifically situated within the genus Aristolochia, with its unique biological identity defined by the species chlamydophylla.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe nephrototoxicity leading to kidney damage, carcinogenicities such as kidney cancer, hepatotoxicity resulting in liver damage, and significant gastrointestinal distress. The presence of aristolochic acids makes these physiological complications a high risk for anyone consuming it. These toxic compounds are known to trigger progressive renal failure in many documented cases. Furthermore, the chemical properties of the plant can lead to life-threatening liver dysfunction. Chronic exposure is also linked to the development of malignant tumors within the urinary tract.
