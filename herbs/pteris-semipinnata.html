Pteris semipinnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pteris semipinnata?

Pteris semipinnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Pteridaceae, which contains the genus Pteris, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Pteris semipinnata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris semipinnata has bipinnate to tripinnate fronds characterized by small, oblong-shaped leaflets that are often irregularly lobed or dentate along the margins. The pinnae are arranged alternately along a central rachis that typically exhibits a reddish or brownish hue. Each individual pinnule is relatively small and possesses a somewhat leathery texture with a distinctively wavy edge. The overall foliage structure creates a delicate, lacy appearance that is characteristic of many members of the Pteridaceae family. These complex leaf arrangements allow the plant to maximize light interception in shaded forest understories.
