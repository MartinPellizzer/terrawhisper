Pteris semipinnata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pteris semipinnata?

Pteris semipinnata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the family Pteridaceae, which contains the genus Pteris, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Pteris semipinnata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pteris semipinnata has bipinnate to tripinnate fronds characterized by small, oblong-shaped leaflets that are often irregularly lobed or dentate along the margins. The pinnae are arranged alternately along a central rachis that typically exhibits a reddish or brownish hue. Each individual pinnule is relatively small and possesses a somewhat leathery texture with a distinctively wavy edge. The overall foliage structure creates a delicate, lacy appearance that is characteristic of many members of the Pteridaceae family. These complex leaf arrangements allow the plant to maximize light interception in shaded forest understories.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, spanning across countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, and parts of the Philippines. It primarily inhabits humid, shaded environments like rainforest understories and moist forest margins where moisture levels remain consistent. Within these habitats, it often grows in rocky crevices or along the edges of streams where it can access filtered sunlight. The species shows a wide latitudinal range across the Indo-Malayan realm, following the distribution of warm, wet climates. Its presence is closely tied to the specific microclimates found in dense, evergreen tropical forests.

What are the pharmacological activities of Pteris semipinnata?

This plant has anti-cancer, anti-inflammation, anti-proliferation, and skin whitening pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to inhibit the uncontrolled growth of malignant cells through potent anti-proliferation mechanisms. The plant also demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory properties by reducing various mediators of swelling and pain. Furthermore, its specific phytochemical profile assists in skin whitening by inhibiting melanin production. These diverse biological effects make Pteris semipinnata a subject of interest for medicinal and cosmetic research.

According to a study published by "Foods (Basel, Switzerland)", Pteris semipinnata contains the multifunctional biochemical Ent-11α-hydroxy-15-oxo-kaur-16-en-19-oiic acid (11αOH-KA). Proposed therapeutic applications for 11αOH-KA include anti-cancer, anti-inflammation, and skin whitening properties. Although these applications are suggested, the content of 11αOH-KA in Pteris semipinnata is not high.
