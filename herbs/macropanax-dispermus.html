Macropanax dispermus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Macropanax dispermus has large, palmately compound leaves consisting of five to seven leaflets that are broadly ovate or elliptical in shape with serrated margins. The leaflets are typically dark green on the upper surface and may appear slightly paler underneath. These leaves are supported by long, stout petioles that emerge from a woody, branched stem. The plant develops small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in dense umbels. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they ripen.
