Litsea elongata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Litsea elongata has lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, arranged alternately along slender, elongated woody stems. The leaf margins are entire and the venation is prominent, often featuring a distinct midrib with secondary veins that curve toward the apex. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The plant produces small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature. This species is characterized by its fine, elongated growth habit and smooth bark texture.
