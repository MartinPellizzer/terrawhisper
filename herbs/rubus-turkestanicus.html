What is Rubus turkestanicus?

Rubus turkestanicus is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae, native to Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub with erect to spreading habit, growing up to 2 meters tall, with a woody base and a mass of thorny stems. The leaves are pinnate with 3-5 pairs of leaflets, and the inflorescence is a corymb of 5-6 flowers, with a white corolla and 5 stamens. The fruit is a red or purple aggregate of drupelets, 10-15 mm in diameter.

The plant flowers in June and July, and the fruit ripens in August.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Rubus turkestanicus are infusion, tincture, tea, salve, and decoction.

Tincture is a concentrated liquid solution made by steeping the plant material in a solvent, commonly ethanol, and is used for its medicinal properties. Rubus turkestanicus tea is a liquid solution made by steeping the dried plant material in hot water and is traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Decoction is a liquid solution made by boiling the plant material in water, often used for its anthelmintic and anti-inflammatory properties.
