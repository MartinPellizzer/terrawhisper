What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis chenocephala has a dense, terminal spike of hooded, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purplish and characterized by a prominent upper lip. The leaves along the stem are oppositely arranged and deeply lobed or pinnately divided into linear segments. Each individual flower possesses a galea, which is the enlarged upper petal that arches over the reproductive organs.
