Pedicularis chenocephala

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis chenocephala?

Pedicularis chenocephala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Orobanchaceae, falling specifically into the genus Pedicularis, and is identified by its unique species name, chenocephala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis chenocephala has a dense, terminal spike of hooded, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purplish and characterized by a prominent upper lip.

The plant features an erect stem that can reach several decimeters in height, often accompanied by basal leaves that are long and narrow. The leaves along the stem are oppositely arranged and deeply lobed or pinnately divided into linear segments. Each individual flower possesses a galea, which is the enlarged upper petal that arches over the reproductive organs.

The overall structure is compact, giving the inflorescence a head-like appearance that defines its species name.
