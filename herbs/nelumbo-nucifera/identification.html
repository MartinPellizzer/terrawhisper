Botanical Identification of Sacred Lotus (Nelumbo nucifera)

To identify Sacred Lotus (Nelumbo nucifera) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Sacred Lotus, Nelumbo nucifera, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Nelumbophyta, class Nelumbiflorae, order Nelumbonales, family Nelumbonaceae, genus Nelumbo, and species Nelumbo nucifera. These classifications help identify its botanical placement within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The Sacred Lotus is scientifically known as Nelumbo nucifera, a name derived from the Latin words "nucifera," meaning "nut-bearing," and "nelumbo," a term used in ancient texts. This name reflects its historical significance and botanical characteristics. It is also known by various synonyms, common names, and regional names that highlight its cultural and medicinal importance across different regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Sacred Lotus is an aquatic herb with a rhizomatous root system. It has a thick, fleshy stem that supports its large leaves and flowers. The leaves are round, floating, and have a waxy surface. The plant produces a single inflorescence with flowers that open sequentially, starting with the male flowers, followed by the female flower. The fruit is a fibrous, spiky capsule containing seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Sacred Lotus can be distinguished by its large, showy pink or white flowers with a distinct yellow center, palmately divided leaves with a thick, waxy texture, and a fibrous, hollow stem. It is commonly found in still or slow-moving water, unlike similar species with different leaf shapes or flower structures.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

The Sacred Lotus is native to southern and eastern Asia, including regions like India, China, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with warm temperatures and high humidity. It prefers shallow, nutrient-rich freshwater soils and is commonly found in wetlands, ponds, and along riverbanks. It grows best at low to mid-altitudes, typically between 0 and 1,000 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Sacred Lotus blooms from June to September, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is when the seed pods are mature and begin to split open.

Phylogenetic Relationships

The Sacred Lotus, Nelumbo nucifera, is closely related to Nelumbo lutea, a species found in South America. It belongs to the genus Nelumbo within the family Nelumbonaceae. This family is characterized by its aquatic habitat, showy flowers, and distinctive seed structures.
