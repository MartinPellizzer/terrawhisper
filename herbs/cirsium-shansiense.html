What's the taxonomical classification of Cirsium shansiense?

Cirsium shansiense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. The plant is a specific representative of the family Asteraceae, characterized by its distinct floral structure. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Cirsium, with its unique identity defined by the specific epithet shansiense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cirsium shansiense has erect stems that reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and are covered in spreading white hairs. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, featuring deeply lobed margins and prominent white pubescence on the undersides. Flowering heads are solitary and terminal, characterized by pink to purple involucral bracts that surround the dense flower clusters. The phyllaries are typically unequal in length and contribute to the distinct texture of the flower head. Each individual flower within the head is tubular and possesses fine hairs that add to the plant's overall tactile complexity.
