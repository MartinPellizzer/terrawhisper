Tinctures, another popular preparation, involve soaking the herb in alcohol to create a concentrated liquid that can be taken orally or applied topically for its healing effects.

Side Effects of panax notoginseng

Panax notoginseng may cause bleeding because it contains compounds that act as anticoagulants, which can interfere with blood clotting and increase the risk of hemorrhage, especially when combined with other blood-thinning medications.

Panax notoginseng causes stomach upset due to its potential to irritate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to symptoms such as indigestion or discomfort. Panax notoginseng induces nausea because it may affect the digestive system and stimulate the vomiting center in the brain, particularly in higher doses.
