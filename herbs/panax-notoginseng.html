Notoginseng

Panax notoginseng

Notoginseng (Panax notoginseng) is a perennial herb native to China, particularly found in the Yunnan province, known for its traditional medicinal properties and use in treating various health conditions.

Botanical Identification

Panax notoginseng, also known as notoginseng, is a species of the Araliaceae family. Native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China, it is a perennial plant characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical root and trifoliate leaves. Commonly referred to as tian qi or panax, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Apiales
  • Family: Araliaceae
  • Genus: Panax
  • Species: Panax notoginseng

Active Compounds

Notoginseng contains key active compounds such as saponins, flavonoids, and glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and cardiovascular benefits. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

  • Saponin
  • Flavonoid
  • Glycoside

Notoginseng Actions.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for internal ailments such as cardiovascular issues and inflammation.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions and to improve circulation.

Extract: Used for treating wounds and enhancing physical performance.

Safety Profile

Notoginseng is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may interact with blood-thinning medications due to its antiplatelet effects. Long-term use or high doses could increase the risk of bleeding, so caution is advised for individuals with bleeding disorders or those on anticoagulant therapy.
