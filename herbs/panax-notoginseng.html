Panax notoginseng

What's the taxonomical classification of Panax notoginseng?

Panax notoginseng belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Apiales. This plant is further categorized under the family Araliaceae, which places it within the genus Panax, ultimately identifying it by its specific species name, notoginseng.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Panax notoginseng has a fleshy, spindle-shaped taproot that is typically light yellow to white in color and possesses a characteristic pungent aroma.

The aerial portion consists of a perennial herbaceous structure featuring large, palmate leaves that often grow in clusters of three to five leaflets. These leaves are dark green and possess serrated margins along their edges. During the flowering stage, the plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers arranged in umbels on a central stalk.

After pollination, the plant develops small, round berries that transition from green to a bright red color when fully ripe.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Panax notoginseng contains bioactive saponins and polysaccharides primarily concentrated in its fleshy taproot. This underground root is harvested and processed to extract the medicinal compounds used in traditional and modern pharmacology. While the root is the principal part used for therapeutic purposes, some studies also investigate the potential benefits of the rhizomes. The specific ginsenosides found within these root tissues are credited with supporting cardiovascular health and cognitive function. Consequently, the entire root structure is carefully cultivated to ensure a high concentration of these valuable medicinal elements.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains ginsenosides, which are a specific class of triterpene saponins, along with polysaccharides, flavonoids, phenolic acids, sterols, and alkaloids.

The ginsenosides serve as the primary bioactive components responsible for the adaptogenic and neuroprotective effects observed in clinical studies. Polysaccharides within the roots contribute significantly to immune system modulation and antioxidant activity. Furthermore, the presence of flavonoids and phenolic acids provides a robust defense against oxidative stress in cellular environments.

Sterols and alkaloids further diversify the chemical profile, assisting in various physiological regulatory processes.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Ginsenosides (triterpene saponins)
  • Polysaccharides
  • Flavonoids
  • Phenolic acids
  • Sterols
  • Alkaloids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Panax notoginseng targets key proteins including cyclooxygenase-2, matrix metalloproteinases, and various nitric oxide synthase isoforms to mediate its anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular effects. Its active ginsenosides interact with adenosine receptors and various voltage-gated ion channels to regulate neurological and muscular functions. The plant also modulates the PI3K/Akt and MAPK signaling pathways, which are crucial for controlling cell survival and apoptosis. By inhibiting various inflammatory cytokines and modulating proteases, it helps reduce tissue damage and swelling. These diverse molecular interactions underpin its widespread use in treating circulatory disorders and inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Panax notoginseng?

Panax notoginseng has diverse therapeutic actions including acting as an adaptogen, a tonic, an immunomodulator, an anti-fatigue agent, a nootropic, an anti-inflammatory, and a circulatory stimulant.

This potent herb helps the body maintain homeostasis by regulating its response to various physical and mental stressors. As a nootropic and anti-fatigue agent, it enhances cognitive function and combats exhaustion to improve mental clarity. Its role as an immunomodulator supports the natural defense mechanisms of the immune system through balanced regulation.

Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory properties and function as a circulatory stimulant promote healthy blood flow and reduce systemic irritation.

What are the herbal preparations of Panax notoginseng?

Panax notoginseng is prepared as decoctions, powders, granules, tinctures, pills, or topical applications such as poultices and ointments. In traditional Chinese medicine, the dried root is most frequently simmered in water to create a concentrated tea or decoction. Many manufacturers process the root into a fine powder or small granules to improve solubility and ease of consumption in modern supplements. For targeted external use, the plant is often crushed and mixed with oils or bases to form medicinal pastes for skin treatments. These various forms allow for different absorption rates and specific therapeutic applications depending on the intended use.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Panax notoginseng can cause side effects such as headaches, dizziness, insomnia, digestive upset, and increased blood pressure or heart palpitations. These adverse reactions often occur if the herb is consumed in excessive amounts or by individuals sensitive to its stimulating properties. Some users may also experience skin rashes or mild gastrointestinal discomfort like nausea and diarrhea. Because it can influence blood clotting and glucose levels, it may interact negatively with anticoagulant or diabetes medications. It is important to consult a medical professional before use to minimize these potential risks.
