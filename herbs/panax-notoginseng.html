What to know about Panax notoginseng (tienchi) before using it medicinally

Tienchi, scientifically known as Panax notoginseng, is a revered herb that has been prized for its remarkable health properties, which have been shown to improve cardiovascular function, boost immunity, and even reduce stress levels.

While it's often used in traditional Chinese medicine, tienchi's culinary versatility also makes it a popular ingredient in many Asian dishes, where it adds depth and warmth to stir-fries, soups, and stews. As an ornamental plant, tienchi is highly prized for its beautiful foliage and striking flowers, making it a sought-after addition to gardens and green spaces. Botanically speaking, tienchi belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small white flowers.

With roots dating back thousands of years, tienchi has been mentioned in ancient Chinese medical texts, including the revered Compendium of Materia Medica, solidifying its status as a treasured herb with a rich cultural heritage.

What are the medicinal properties of Panax notoginseng?

Panax notoginseng has several medicinal properties, such as antithrombotic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and vasodilatory effects, which have been extensively studied and validated.

One of the most important medicinal properties of Panax notoginseng is its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and reduce blood clotting time, making it a natural anticoagulant that can help prevent cardiovascular diseases such as heart attacks and strokes. Additionally, its anti-inflammatory activity has been shown to alleviate symptoms of conditions like rheumatoid arthritis, asthma, and other inflammatory disorders. Furthermore, Panax notoginseng's antioxidant properties have been found to protect against oxidative stress and cellular damage, potentially reducing the risk of chronic diseases like cancer and neurodegenerative disorders.

Finally, its vasodilatory effects can help improve blood flow and oxygenation to the tissues, which may be beneficial in conditions such as hypertension, angina pectoris, and peripheral artery disease.

What parts of Panax notoginseng are used for medicinal purposes?

The most commonly used parts of Panax notoginseng for medicinal purposes are its roots, rhizomes, and stems.

These parts contain a variety of bioactive compounds, including ginsenosides, notoginsenosides, and other triterpenoid saponins, which have been found to possess numerous therapeutic properties. The roots, in particular, are rich in saponins such as Rb1, Rb2, Rc, and Rd, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anticoagulant activities, making them useful for treating conditions such as cardiovascular disease, stroke, and chronic pain.

The rhizomes, on the other hand, contain higher concentrations of notoginsenosides, which are believed to play a key role in reducing blood pressure, improving blood flow, and promoting wound healing. The stems have also been found to possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, making them useful for treating respiratory disorders such as bronchitis and asthma.

Overall, the combination of bioactive compounds present in these parts of Panax notoginseng has led to its widespread use in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries, with many modern studies confirming its efficacy and safety for a range of therapeutic applications.

What are the side effects of Panax notoginseng when used improperly?

When used improperly, Panax notoginseng increases the chances of experiencing side effects, such as gastrointestinal disturbances, including nausea, vomiting, and stomach pain.

This is often caused by the unprepared or raw form of the herb being consumed, which can lead to an imbalance of saponins in the body. Additionally, improper preparation methods, such as drying or processing at high temperatures, can further exacerbate these side effects.

Moreover, combining Panax notoginseng with other medications, including blood thinners and anticoagulants, can increase the risk of bleeding due to its ability to enhance clotting function. Other potential side effects include dizziness, fatigue, and insomnia, which may be more pronounced in individuals who are already experiencing underlying health conditions or taking certain medications.

Furthermore, prolonged use of Panax notoginseng without proper guidance from a healthcare professional can lead to an over-reliance on the herb for medicinal purposes, potentially masking underlying health issues that require attention.

What precautions to take before using Panax notoginseng medicinally?

The precautions to take before using Panax notoginseng medicinally are crucial to ensure safe and effective treatment.

First and foremost, it is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any supplementation regimen, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions or are taking medications that may interact with notoginseng. Additionally, individuals with high blood pressure, bleeding disorders, or those prone to bleeding should exercise caution when using Panax notoginseng, as it may increase the risk of bleeding and hypotension.

Furthermore, notoginseng can potentiate the effects of anticoagulant and antiplatelet medications, making regular blood tests and monitoring vital signs necessary during treatment. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should also consult with their healthcare provider before using notoginseng, as its safety has not been established in these populations. Finally, it is recommended to start with a low dose and gradually increase as needed and under medical supervision, to minimize the risk of adverse effects such as dizziness, stomach upset, and allergic reactions.

By taking these precautions, individuals can ensure safe and effective use of Panax notoginseng for medicinal purposes.

What are the harvesting tips of Panax notoginseng?

Panax notoginseng, also known as San Qi or Tien Chi, is a highly prized medicinal herb that requires careful handling during harvesting to ensure its quality and efficacy.

What are the pest and diseases tips of Panax notoginseng?

Panax notoginseng, also known as Sanqi or Tienchi, is a species of ginseng native to China and Southeast Asia.

Like many crops, it is susceptible to various pests and diseases that can significantly impact its growth, yield, and quality. One of the most common pests affecting Panax notoginseng is the root-knot nematode (Meloidogyne incognita), which causes galls or knots on the roots, leading to reduced plant growth and increased susceptibility to other pathogens.

Other common pests include aphids, whiteflies, and spider mites, which can spread plant viruses and cause damage through feeding activities. In terms of diseases, Panax notoginseng is prone to fungal infections such as fusarium wilt (Fusarium oxysporum) and rhizome rot (Fusarium solani), which can lead to root decay and plant death. Bacterial leaf blight caused by Pseudomonas syringae and Xanthomonas campestris, as well as fungal leaf spots caused by Colletotrichum spp., are also common issues affecting the plant's leaves and stems.

Effective integrated pest management (IPM) practices, including cultural controls, biological control agents, and chemical pesticides, are crucial for minimizing the impact of these pests and diseases on Panax notoginseng cultivation.
