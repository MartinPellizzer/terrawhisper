Panax notoginseng

What's the taxonomical classification of Panax notoginseng?

Panax notoginseng belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Apiales. This plant is further categorized under the family Araliaceae, which places it within the genus Panax, ultimately identifying it by its specific species name, notoginseng.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Panax notoginseng has a fleshy, spindle-shaped taproot that is typically light yellow to white in color and possesses a characteristic pungent aroma. The aerial portion consists of a perennial herbaceous structure featuring large, palmate leaves that often grow in clusters of three to five leaflets. These leaves are dark green and possess serrated margins along their edges. During the flowering stage, the plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers arranged in umbels on a central stalk. After pollination, the plant develops small, round berries that transition from green to a bright red color when fully ripe.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of the Korean Peninsula and parts of northeastern China. It thrives primarily in temperate forest ecosystems characterized by high humidity and cool climates. Its natural habitat is typically restricted to shaded areas with well-drained, organic-rich soil found at specific elevations. Due to its specialized environmental requirements, the species is often confined to protected woodland zones. Wild populations are increasingly rare as natural habitats face pressure from human activity and climate shifts.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Panax notoginseng contains bioactive saponins and polysaccharides primarily concentrated in its fleshy taproot. This underground root is harvested and processed to extract the medicinal compounds used in traditional and modern pharmacology. While the root is the principal part used for therapeutic purposes, some studies also investigate the potential benefits of the rhizomes. The specific ginsenosides found within these root tissues are credited with supporting cardiovascular health and cognitive function. Consequently, the entire root structure is carefully cultivated to ensure a high concentration of these valuable medicinal elements.

What are the pharmacological activities of Panax notoginseng?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, blood circulation promoting, neuroprotective, and blood stasis clearing properties. Its active ginsenosides work to neutralize free radicals and reduce systemic oxidative stress within the body. These compounds also effectively mitigate inflammation and help protect neural tissues from degenerative damage. Furthermore, the plant plays a crucial role in improving vascular function by promoting healthy blood circulation and clearing blood stasis. Such multifaceted biological actions make it a significant subject in medicinal research.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several potent medicinal ginsenosides, specifically Ginsenoside Rg1, Ginsenoside Rb1, Ginsenoside Rd, Ginsenoside Re, and Ginsenoside Rg3. These bioactive compounds are primary components of its chemical profile and contribute to its well-known therapeutic effects. Each specific saponin plays a unique role in modulating various physiological processes within the body. Researchers study these individual molecules to understand their impact on neurological health and immune function. The concentration and balance of these compounds determine the overall medicinal quality of the plant.

As discussed in a study, Panax notoginseng contains the natural active ingredient ginsenoside Rg1, which is a saponin derived from its roots or stems. This compound exerts neurotrophic and neuroprotective effects in the central nervous system through 4 sets of mechanisms. These mechanisms include anti-oxidant activity via the Akt, Nrf2/HO-1, PPARγ/HO-1, ERK, p38, and c-Jun N-terminal kinase (JNk) pathways, or the caspase-3/ROCK1/MLC pathway. Anti-inflammatory activities involve MAPK pathways such as JNk1/2 + ERK1/2 and PPARγ/HO-1, as well as HMGB1-induced TLR2/4/9 and RAGE pathways. Furthermore, the plant's compound influences neurological cell cycle and regeneration via pathways including JNk1/2 + ERK1/2, PI3K-Akt/mTOR, PKB/Akt, and HIF-1α/VEGF. Finally, it regulates energy metabolism and ATP levels through N-methyl-D-aspartic acid (NMDA) receptors, ERS, and AMP/AMPK-GLUT pathways.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing Alzheimer's disease, cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, cancer, and stroke through its diverse bioactive compounds. Research suggests that its ginsenosides may help reduce neuroinflammation and oxidative stress associated with cognitive decline. The plant also shows potential in regulating blood glucose levels to assist with diabetic complications. Furthermore, its antioxidant properties are being studied for their ability to protect against cell damage linked to cancer and improve circulatory health. These medicinal qualities contribute to its role in preventing and mitigating the effects of stroke and other vascular issues.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be processed into various medicinal forms including decoction, tincture, powder, extract, and alcoholic preparation. A decoction is typically made by simmering the dried roots in water to draw out active ginsenosides. For more concentrated applications, a tincture or an alcoholic preparation is created by soaking the plant material in ethanol. The dried root can also be finely ground into a powder for direct consumption or mixed into food. Additionally, a standardized extract provides a potent and measured dose of the plant's primary therapeutic compounds.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause a range of adverse reactions including insomnia, nausea, diarrhea, dizziness, headache, hypertension, tachycardia, palpitations, agitation, irritability, and changes in sleep pattern. Some individuals may experience gastrointestinal distress such as nausea or diarrhea after consumption. Stimulant-like effects can lead to cardiovascular issues like hypertension, tachycardia, and palpitations. Neurological and psychological symptoms often manifest as headache, dizziness, agitation, or irritability. Additionally, the herb is known to cause significant changes in sleep pattern and persistent insomnia.
