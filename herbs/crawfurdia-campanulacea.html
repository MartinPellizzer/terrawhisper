Crawfurdia campanulacea

What's the taxonomical classification of Crawfurdia campanulacea?

Crawfurdia campanulacea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Gentianaales. This plant is a member of the family Gentianaceae, specifically falling under the genus Crawfurdia, with the species designation campanulacea.

This plant is used to treat various skin conditions including eczema, psoriasis, and minor wounds. Its medicinal properties are derived from specific chemical compounds that help reduce inflammation on the skin's surface. Local communities often apply the plant in the form of poultices or infusions to soothe irritation. It is also believed to possess antiseptic qualities that assist in the healing of small cuts. These applications focus primarily on external ailments rather than internal infections.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Crawfurdia campanulacea can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if ingested or handled improperly. When consumed, the chemical constituents might trigger nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramping. Detailed toxicological studies on this specific species are limited, making its exact physiological impact difficult to predict.
