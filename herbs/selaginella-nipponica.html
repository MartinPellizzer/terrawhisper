Selaginella nipponica

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella nipponica?

Selaginella nipponica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Selaginellales and the family Selaginellaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Selaginella, with its specific designation being the species nipponica.

What are the herbal preparations of Selaginella nipponica?

Selaginella nipponica is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried plant material in water, though it can also be processed into powders, tinctures, or topical ointments. The most common traditional method involves simmering the whole plant or its rhizomes to extract active bioactive compounds for oral consumption. Some practitioners utilize concentrated extracts to create liquid tinctures that allow for more precise dosing and longer shelf life. In certain clinical applications, the plant material is finely ground into a powder to be encapsulated or mixed into medicinal broths. These various preparation styles are chosen based on the specific therapeutic goal and the desired absorption rate of the plant's phytochemicals.
