10 Possible Side Effects Of Chamaemelum Nobilis

Chamaemelum nobilis has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and interactions with medications.

These side effects are caused by individual sensitivities, high doses, or prolonged use.

For example, an allergic reaction can worsen respiratory conditions like asthma, while digestive issues can lead to nutrient deficiencies and malabsorption, ultimately impacting overall health and quality of life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Chamaemelum nobilis causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is often due to the presence of sesquiterpenes and flavonoids in the plant, which can trigger an immune response in sensitive people.

In rare cases, these compounds can cause contact dermatitis, hives, or even anaphylaxis.

2. Produces respiratory issues

Chamaemelum nobilis produces respiratory issues such as asthma-like symptoms, coughing, and wheezing in some individuals.

This is due to the herb's allergenic compounds, which can trigger an immune response that causes inflammation and constriction of the airways.

Additionally, chamomile contains a flavonoid called apigenin, which has been shown to increase the sensitivity of airway smooth muscle to bronchospasm-inducing agents, exacerbating respiratory problems.

3. Increases drowsiness

Chamaemelum nobilis increases drowsiness due to its sedative properties, which can affect the brain's neurotransmitters, such as GABA and serotonin.

The herb's apigenin content binds to benzodiazepine receptors, promoting relaxation and reducing anxiety, leading to a sense of calmness and somnolence.

Additionally, chamomile's flavonoids, particularly apigetrin, have been shown to enhance the activity of GABA, further contributing to its sleep-inducing effects.

4. Lowers blood pressure

Chamaemelum nobilis lowers blood pressure by its ability to relax and calm the body.

As a natural sedative, it slows down heart rate and reduces blood vessel tension, leading to a decrease in blood pressure. The flavonoids present in chamomile, such as apigenin and luteolin, are thought to contribute to this effect by binding to alpha-adrenergic receptors, thereby reducing sympathetic nervous system activity.

This results in a decrease in blood pressure, which may be beneficial for individuals with hypertension or cardiovascular disease.

5. Induces liver damage

Chamaemelum nobilis induces liver damage due to its ability to cause a buildup of bilirubin in the blood.

This occurs when the herb's flavonoids and terpenoids interfere with the body's normal metabolism of bilirubin, leading to an accumulation of this waste product in the liver.

Additionally, the saponins present in chamomile may also contribute to liver damage by causing inflammation and cell death.

6. Interferes with thyroid function

Chamaemelum nobilis interferes with thyroid function by suppressing the production of thyroid-stimulating hormones and reducing the activity of thyroid enzymes.

This may be due to its ability to bind to thyroid receptors, altering their normal functioning.

The exact mechanisms are not fully understood, but it is thought that chamomile's flavonoids and terpenoids play a role in this interference.

7. Affects sleep quality negatively

Chamaemelum nobilis affects sleep quality negatively.

The herb's stimulating properties can cause an increase in alertness and energy levels, making it difficult for individuals to wind down and fall asleep. Additionally, the flavonoids present in chamomile may interfere with the body's natural sleep-wake cycle, leading to restless nights and early morning wakefulness.

As a result, some people may experience disturbed sleep patterns or find it challenging to get a good night's rest after consuming Chamaemelum nobilis.

8. Triggers stomach upset

Chamaemelum nobilis triggers stomach upset due to its ability to increase digestive enzyme production and stimulate the muscles in the stomach, which can lead to cramping, bloating, and discomfort.

Additionally, chamomile's apigenin content may interact with gut bacteria, altering the balance of the gut microbiome and potentially causing nausea and stomach pain.

These effects are typically mild and temporary, but they may be exacerbated by high doses or prolonged use.

9. Causes headaches frequently

Chamaemelum nobilis causes headaches frequently due to its stimulating effects on the nervous system.

The apigenin and other flavonoids present in the herb may interact with the brain's serotonin receptors, leading to increased activity and potentially causing migraines or tension headaches.

Additionally, the essential oils contained in chamomile can cause sensitivity reactions or allergic responses, which may also contribute to headache development.

10. Enhances sedative effects

Chamaemelum nobilis enhances sedative effects by increasing the levels of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain.

GABA is a neurotransmitter that helps to regulate sleep and relaxation, leading to feelings of calmness and reduced anxiety.

This increased GABA activity can amplify the effects of other sedatives, such as benzodiazepines, and may also cause drowsiness or lethargy when taken alone.
