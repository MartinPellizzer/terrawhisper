Serissa japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Serissa japonica?

Serissa japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is further categorized into the family Rubiaceae, which is the group to which its genus, Serissa, is assigned. Ultimately, the specific identity of this organism is defined by its species name, japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Serissa japonica has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length and are arranged oppositely along its woody, branching stems. These leaves exhibit a glossy texture and a deep green color on the upper surface, while the undersides often appear slightly lighter. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the axils of the foliage. Following the blooming period, the plant develops small, globose drupes that transition from green to a bright red color when mature. Its overall growth habit is typically shrubby, allowing it to form dense, rounded thickets.
