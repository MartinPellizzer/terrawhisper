Tilia paucicostata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tilia paucicostata has cordate leaves with serrated margins, elliptical to ovate shapes, and relatively few flowers per inflorescence. The foliage typically exhibits a smooth texture and a prominent midrib that extends toward the apex. Its flowers are small and cream-colored, clustered in a distinctive bract that facilitates wind-assisted seed dispersal. The woody stems are characterized by a rugged bark texture that develops significantly as the tree matures. This species is distinguished from other lindens by the specific density and arrangement of its floral structures.
