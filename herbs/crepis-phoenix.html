Crepis phoenix

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crepis phoenix has a perennial herbaceous growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are typically lanceolate or oblong with entire or slightly dentate margins. The plant develops an erect, unbranched or sparingly branched flowering stem that can reach various heights depending on environmental conditions. Its leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, often featuring long petioles that transition into smaller, alternate leaves along the stem. The inflorescence consists of several heads arranged in a corymbose manner, each containing numerous yellow ligulate florets. These flower heads are supported by involucres composed of multiple rows of phyllaries that protect the developing seeds.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the central Andes mountains in South America, specifically within parts of Peru and Bolivia. It grows primarily in alpine tundra environments where rocky terrain and extreme temperatures are common. Its distribution is restricted to specific elevational zones that provide the unique moisture and light conditions it requires. Because it is a specialist of these montane ecosystems, it is not found in lowland forests or coastal plains. This narrow range makes the species highly dependent on the stability of high-altitude mountain habitats.

      What are the herbal preparations of Crepis phoenix?

      Crepis phoenix is prepared as a decoction, a concentrated infusion, or a topical salve depending on the intended therapeutic use. For internal administration, the dried leaves and flowering tops are simmered in water to extract active phytochemicals.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Crepis phoenix can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on sensitive skin. Individuals with known sensitivities to the Asteraceae family might experience more severe dermatological responses. If consumed, the plant could trigger nausea, vomiting, or abdominal discomfort in certain people. Thoroughly washing hands after contact is recommended to mitigate these potential adverse effects.
