Crepis phoenix

What's the taxonomical classification of Crepis phoenix?

Crepis phoenix belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asterales, it is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Crepis and the species phoenix.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crepis phoenix has a perennial herbaceous growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are typically lanceolate or oblong with entire or slightly dentate margins. The plant develops an erect, unbranched or sparingly branched flowering stem that can reach various heights depending on environmental conditions. Its leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, often featuring long petioles that transition into smaller, alternate leaves along the stem. The inflorescence consists of several heads arranged in a corymbose manner, each containing numerous yellow ligulate florets. These flower heads are supported by involucres composed of multiple rows of phyllaries that protect the developing seeds.
