What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stachyurus retusus has small, elliptical to ovate leaves with obtuse or slightly emarginate tips and finely serrated margins. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the woody stems. Its flowers are relatively small and emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in clusters. The plant exhibits a shrubby growth habit with slender, brownish branches. These morphological traits help distinguish the species within its specific botanical family.
