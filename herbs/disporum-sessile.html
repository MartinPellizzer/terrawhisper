Disporum sessile

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Disporum sessile has a rhizomatous root system with lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along a slender, erect stem. The leaves are typically unicostate and possess entire margins with acute tips. Small, white, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. These blossoms eventually develop into fleshy, oblong berries that transition from green to a dark reddish-brown color upon ripening. The overall plant structure remains relatively diminutive, typically reaching heights of only a few decimeters.
