Disporum sessile

What's the taxonomical classification of Disporum sessile?

Disporum sessile belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Colchicaceae, falling within the genus Disporum, and is specifically identified by the species name sessile.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Disporum sessile has a rhizomatous root system with lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along a slender, erect stem.

The leaves are typically unicostate and possess entire margins with acute tips. Small, white, bell-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. These blossoms eventually develop into fleshy, oblong berries that transition from green to a dark reddish-brown color upon ripening.

The overall plant structure remains relatively diminutive, typically reaching heights of only a few decimeters.
