What's the taxonomical classification of Millettia pachycarpa?

Millettia pachycarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Millettia, and its unique species designation, pachycarpa.

What are the pharmacological activities of Millettia pachycarpa?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its documented anthelmintic, anti-cancer, and antiestrogenic activities. The anthelmintic properties allow the plant extracts to effectively combat parasitic worm infections in various biological models. Regarding its medicinal value in oncology, certain compounds derived from the species exhibit anti-cancer capabilities by inhibiting tumor cell growth. Furthermore, the plant displays antiestrogenic activity, which can assist in modulating hormone-sensitive processes within the body. These diverse biological actions suggest that the phytochemical constituents of Millettia pachycarpa hold promise for future drug development.

As discussed in a study, the flavonoid compound Furowanin A (Fur A) isolated from the medicinal plant Millettia pachycarpa Benth exhibits anti-cancer activities against colorectal cancer (CRC). In CRC cells, Fur A demonstrated anti-proliferative effects, blocked cell cycle progression, and promoted apoptotic cell death. The compound suppressed migration, invasion, and epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT) in vitro, while also reducing tumor growth and pulmonary metastasis in vivo without causing obvious toxicity. Through i-TRAQ analysis, Pfn1 was identified as a gene up-regulated by Fur A. Furthermore, Fur A exhibited anti-cancer activities in both in vitro and in vivo CRC models by up-regulating Pfn1.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds furowanin B, millewaniins G, and millewaniins H, which are typically isolated through a methanol extract. These specific phytochemicals are the primary bioactive constituents responsible for the plant's therapeutic potential. Researchers often utilize a methanol extract to concentrate these molecules for further pharmacological study. The presence of millewaniins G and H suggests unique chemical properties that distinguish this species from others. Detailed analysis of furowanin B provides additional insight into its potential biological activities.

As discussed in a study, three new isoflavonoids were isolated from the leaves of Millettia pachycarpa. These specific medicinal compounds are named millewaniins G (1), millewaniins H (2), and furowaniin B (3). The structures of these 3 compounds were elucidated using spectroscopic analyses. In a yeast two-hybrid assay, the antiestrogenic activity of these isoflavonoids was examined. This activity was shown to be comparable with that of 4-hydroxytamoxifen.
