10 Medicinal Constituents Of Calendula Hybr (Pot Marigold)

Calendula hybr has active constituents such as triterpenoids, flavonoids, and carotenoids.

These constituents possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which are beneficial for wound healing, skin irritations, and digestive issues.

The triterpenoids, in particular, have been shown to inhibit the growth of bacteria and viruses, while the carotenoids have been found to reduce inflammation and improve skin health, thus reducing the severity of conditions like eczema and acne.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Calendula hybr.

1. Lutein

Calendula hybr lutein is an extract obtained from the flowers of the plant Calendula officinalis.

It is a rich source of antioxidants and carotenoids, particularly beta-carotene and lutein, which have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Lutein is also known for its benefits to eye health, including reducing the risk of age-related macular degeneration and cataracts.

2. Zeaxanthin

Calendula hybr zeaxanthin is a carotenoid pigment that gives it its bright orange color.

This pigment has potent antioxidant properties, which can help to reduce inflammation and protect against cell damage.

Zeaxanthin is found in the petals of the Calendula flower and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for centuries to promote wound healing and skin health.

3. Β-carotene

Calendula hybr β-carotene is a pigment that gives the plant its vibrant yellow and orange hues.

It is a precursor to vitamin A, playing a key role in maintaining healthy skin, vision, and immune function. This pigment is rich in antioxidants, helping to protect the body against oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals.

Its benefits are also utilized in cosmetic and skincare products.

4. Oleic acid

Calendula hybr oleic acid is one of its key components.

This naturally occurring fatty acid is extracted from the plant's petals. Oleic acid has been traditionally used in skincare products for its emollient and soothing properties, helping to calm irritated skin and reduce inflammation.

Its moisturizing effects make it a popular ingredient in cosmetics, particularly in creams, lotions, and balms.

5. Linoleic acid

Calendula hybr linoleic acid is a fatty acid found in the leaves and flowers of the plant.

It is an essential omega-6 fatty acid that has anti-inflammatory properties, making it beneficial for skin and wound healing.

Linoleic acid also helps to maintain the integrity of cell membranes and can aid in the reduction of inflammation and redness in the skin.

6. Α-linolenic acid

Calendula hybr α-linolenic acid is a fatty acid found in the seeds of the plant.

It is an omega-3 fatty acid, a type of essential fatty acid that the human body cannot produce on its own. This compound has been reported to have anti-inflammatory properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies to soothe skin irritations and wounds.

Its benefits extend to supporting skin health.

7. Palmitic acid

Calendula hybr palmitic acid is a key component in its oil extraction process.

This fatty acid is derived from the seed of the Calendula plant, which is widely used in skincare and cosmetic products due to its soothing and moisturizing properties.

Palmitic acid is valued for its ability to lock in moisture, reduce inflammation, and promote skin elasticity, making it a popular ingredient in natural skincare formulations.

8. Stearic acid

Calendula hybr stearic acid is a naturally occurring compound derived from the plant Calendula officinalis.

This gentle and non-comedogenic acid is commonly used in skincare products due to its ability to moisturize and soothe the skin. It helps to lock in moisture, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing, making it an ideal ingredient for sensitive skin and skin irritations.

Its soothing properties make it a popular choice in herbal remedies.

9. Calendulin

Calendula hybr calendulin is a plant-derived compound that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

It is a glycoprotein complex that is rich in carbohydrates and other nutrients, making it beneficial for skin and wound healing. Calendulin has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which aid in promoting tissue repair and preventing infection.

It is commonly used in creams, ointments, and gels for topical application.

10. Quercetin

Calendula hybr quercetin is a powerful flavonoid antioxidant derived from the flowers of the Calendula officinalis plant.

It is known for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, making it a popular natural remedy for soothing skin irritations, wounds, and sunburns.

Quercetin has also been shown to have anti-cancer and anti-aging effects, and is commonly used in skincare products and supplements to promote healthy aging and protect against environmental stressors.
