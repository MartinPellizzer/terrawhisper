Hedyotis vestita

What's the taxonomical classification of Hedyotis vestita?

Hedyotis vestita belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, which falls under the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, the plant is placed in the order Gentianales and the family Rubiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Hedyotis, with its specific designation being the species vestita.

Hedyotis vestita contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic potential. What are the therapeutic actions of Hedyotis vestita?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and fever. Its medicinal properties are primarily utilized by indigenous communities to soothe various topical irritations. When applied to the skin, the plant acts as a natural remedy for healing cuts and preventing infections. It is also frequently consumed in decoctions to alleviate digestive discomfort and reduce body temperature during illness. These diverse applications highlight the plant's significance in traditional ethnobotanical practices.
