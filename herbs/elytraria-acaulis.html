Elytraria acaulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Elytraria acaulis?

Elytraria acaulis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Acanthaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Elytraria, with its scientific designation completed by the species name acaulis.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, digestive issues, and inflammatory conditions. The leaves are frequently applied topically to manage dermatological ailments and promote the healing of cutaneous lesions. When consumed as a decoction, it can help alleviate gastrointestinal distress and internal inflammation. Traditional healers also utilize its properties to address various systemic ailments through specific preparations. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's bioactive compounds to combat pathogens and soothe discomfort.
