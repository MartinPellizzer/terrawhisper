What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana loureiroi?

Gentiana loureiroi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Gentianaales, it is a member of the family Gentianaceae. Finally, this plant is situated in the genus Gentiana, specifically identified as the species loureiroi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana loureiroi has lanceolate to elliptical leaves that are oppositely arranged on an erect, herbaceous stem. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color with smooth margins and distinct venation. Its flowers are characterized by a tubular or funnel-shaped corolla that emerges from the leaf axils. These blossoms often feature a vibrant blue or pale blue pigmentation typical of the Gentianaceae family. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the high-altitude regions of the Sierra Madre Oriental in Mexico, specifically within the states of San Luis Potosi and Querétaro. It grows primarily in specialized montane habitats characterized by rocky outcrops and limestone substrates. These populations are typically found at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 3,000 meters above sea level. The species is restricted to these specific mountain corridors, which limits its overall range to a relatively small geographic footprint.
