Botanical Identification of Copaiba (Copaifera officinalis)

To identify Copaiba (Copaifera officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Copaiba officinalis belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Copaifera, and species Copaifera officinalis. This classification highlights its botanical relationships and placement within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Copaiba is Copaifera officinalis, first described by Carl von Linne in 1753. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is also known by several synonyms, including Copaifera reticulata and Copaifera cupulifera. Common names vary by region, with "Copaiba" being widely recognized, while other names include "Copaíba" and "Copaíba do Brasil." Historically, it has also been referred to as "Copaíba do Pau."

Morphological Characteristics

Copaifera officinalis is a tall, straight tree with a dense crown. Its root system is deep and fibrous, anchoring it firmly in the soil. The stem is cylindrical, smooth, and often cracked with age. Leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy dark green upper surface. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small yellow flowers. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Copaifera officinalis can be distinguished by its elliptical, dark green leaves with prominent veins, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters, and bark that exudes a fragrant, pale yellow resin. It is often confused with other Copaifera species, but its leaf shape and resin color are key differentiators. The plant also has long, slender stems and is typically found in tropical rainforests.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Copaiba (Copaifera officinalis) is native to the Amazon Basin and parts of Central and South America. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and rainfall. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is commonly found at low to mid-altitudes in dense rainforests.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Copaiba (Copaifera officinalis) blooms from September to November. Seed formation occurs between December and March. Harvesting is typically done during the dry season, from April to June, when resin production is highest and the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Copaifera officinalis belongs to the Fabaceae family and is closely related to other species within the Copaifera genus. Its phylogenetic relationships show it is most closely related to Copaifera reticulata and Copaifera langsdorffii. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, which help in distinguishing Copaiba from other tropical hardwoods.
