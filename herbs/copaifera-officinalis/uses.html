Copaiba (Copaifera officinalis) Uses

Copaiba is used to treat respiratory conditions such as bronchitis, asthma, and respiratory infections. It helps alleviate symptoms of cough and respiratory allergies. Its anti-inflammatory properties support recovery from tuberculosis and influenza. It is also applied for chronic cough and allergic rhinitis.

Copaiba (Copaifera officinalis) Monograph

1. Bronchitis

Copaiba has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties help reduce bronchial inflammation and clear mucus. It may ease coughing and improve breathing in individuals with chronic bronchial irritation. Copaiba essential oil is often used in inhalations or diluted topical applications for this purpose.

2. Respiratory infections

Copaiba has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in treating symptoms of respiratory infections. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help reduce inflammation and combat pathogens in the respiratory tract. It is often used in inhalation therapies and topical applications to alleviate congestion and support healing.

3. Cough

Copaiba is used to alleviate coughs due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce airway irritation, making it beneficial for respiratory conditions. Traditionally, Copaiba oil is applied topically or inhaled to ease breathing and soothe coughing fits.

4. Respiratory allergies

Copaiba is used to alleviate respiratory allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and allergic reactions by calming the immune response. Often used in inhalations or as a tincture, it supports respiratory comfort in allergy sufferers. Its effectiveness stems from its rich content of bioactive compounds like alpha-pinene and limonene.

5. Respiratory tract infections

Copaiba has been traditionally used to support respiratory health due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory tract infections by reducing mucous production and soothing irritated airways. Its essential oils are often used in inhalation therapies to promote clear breathing and ease congestion.

6. Asthma

Copaiba resin has been traditionally used to support respiratory health in asthma management. Its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties may help reduce airway inflammation and ease breathing. It is often used in herbal formulations to complement conventional treatments. However, it should not replace prescribed medical care.

7. Influenza

Copaiba resin has been used traditionally to support respiratory health during influenza. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce symptoms and shorten the duration of illness. It is often prepared as a tincture or steam inhalation to ease congestion and soothe the throat.

8. Tuberculosis

Copaiba has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health, including in the treatment of tuberculosis. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help reduce lung inflammation and inhibit Mycobacterium tuberculosis. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects. However, it should not replace conventional medical treatment for tuberculosis.

9. Allergic rhinitis

Copaiba essential oil has shown efficacy in reducing symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps alleviate nasal congestion, sneezing, and runny nose by inhibiting inflammatory pathways. Clinical studies suggest it may be a natural alternative for managing seasonal allergies. Its active compounds, such as alpha-pinene and beta-caryophyllene, contribute to its therapeutic effects.

10. Chronic cough

Copaiba resin has been traditionally used to soothe chronic cough due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce throat irritation and loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. Often prepared as a tincture or essential oil, it is applied topically or inhaled for respiratory relief. Its effectiveness is supported by historical use in herbal medicine.
