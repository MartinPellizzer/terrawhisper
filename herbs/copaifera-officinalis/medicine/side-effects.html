10 Possible Side Effects Of Copaifera Officinalis

Copaifera officinalis has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and digestive issues.

These side effects are often caused by excessive consumption or sensitivity to the herb's volatile oils. For instance, individuals with sensitive skin may experience redness, itching, and swelling, leading to discomfort during daily activities.

Furthermore, gastrointestinal issues may worsen conditions like irritable bowel syndrome, affecting daily life and overall well-being.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Copaifera officinalis causes allergic reactions due to its chemical composition, which includes terpenes and sesquiterpenes.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, redness, and swelling. Additionally, the resinous sap of Copaifera officinalis may contain allergenic proteins that can cause an allergic reaction in sensitive people.

As a result, exposure to this plant or its essential oil may elicit an adverse response in those prone to allergies.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Copaifera officinalis triggers asthma attacks due to its potent volatile oils, particularly beta-pinene and alpha-phellandrene, which can cause bronchospasm and airway constriction.

These compounds can stimulate the respiratory tract, leading to increased mucus production and inflammation in the lungs, further exacerbating asthmatic symptoms.

3. Induces gastrointestinal upset

Copaifera officinalis induces gastrointestinal upset due to its volatile compounds, such as limonene and beta-pinene, which can stimulate the digestive system and cause irritation.

This may lead to symptoms like nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, and diarrhea in some individuals.

The oil's ability to increase gastric acid production and relax the lower esophageal sphincter can further contribute to this side effect.

4. Leads to skin irritation

Copaifera officinalis leads to skin irritation due to its essential oil containing a high concentration of sesquiterpenes, which can cause allergic reactions and phototoxicity.

When applied topically, these compounds may react with the skin's natural oils and proteins, leading to redness, itching, and inflammation.

The sesquiterpenes may also increase the skin's sensitivity to sunlight, exacerbating symptoms in individuals who are already prone to sunburn or photoallergic reactions.

5. Elicits respiratory distress

Copaifera officinalis elicits respiratory distress due to its irritating and stimulating effects on the mucous membranes of the airways.

The essential oils present in Copaifera officinalis can cause inflammation and constriction of the bronchial tubes, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, its expectorant properties may also exacerbate respiratory issues, triggering episodes of asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

6. Produces headache

Copaifera officinalis produces headache due to its volatile oils and terpenoids, which can stimulate the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to increased blood flow and pressure.

This may cause a throbbing or pulsating sensation in the head, potentially accompanied by feelings of anxiety and restlessness.

The essential oil's aromatic properties can also irritate the nervous system, further contributing to this side effect.

7. Increases heart rate

Copaifera officinalis increases heart rate due to its ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system.

This is attributed to the presence of beta-pinene and other volatile oils in the plant, which can cause increased blood pressure and heart palpitations.

The stimulatory effect on the nervous system may also contribute to this side effect, leading to an accelerated heart rate.

8. Lowers blood pressure

Copaifera officinalis lowers blood pressure due to its ability to relax and dilate blood vessels.

This occurs because of the presence of sesquiterpenes, which are a type of essential oil that has vasodilatory properties. As a result, the heart does not have to work as hard to pump blood throughout the body, leading to a decrease in blood pressure.

This reduction in blood pressure can be beneficial for individuals with hypertension, but it may also pose a risk for those who already take medications for low blood pressure.

9. Stimulates excessive sweating

Copaifera officinalis stimulates excessive sweating due to its diaphoretic properties, which cause an increase in perspiration.

This is attributed to the essential oils present in the plant, particularly beta-eudesmol and sesquiterpenes, that stimulate the sweat glands, leading to a sudden and intense release of sweat.

As a result, individuals may experience an excessive sweating response, characterized by profuse sweating and a sensation of heat.

10. Causes dizziness

Copaifera officinalis causes dizziness due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system and affect blood pressure.

The essential oil contained in the plant has been shown to increase heart rate and cardiac output, leading to a temporary sense of lightheadedness or dizziness.

Additionally, the aromatic compounds present in Copaifera officinalis can also cause a mild sedative effect, further contributing to feelings of dizziness and disorientation.
