Madhuca pasquieri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Madhuca pasquieri has a woody structure characterized by an erect habit, elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins, and small, creamy-white fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches and possess a coriaceous texture. Its inflorescences are often clustered in the leaf axils, bearing small petals that eventually give way to fleshy drupes. These fruits are ovoid in shape and serve as a primary morphological feature for identifying the species. The overall vegetative morphology reflects its adaptation to tropical forest environments.
