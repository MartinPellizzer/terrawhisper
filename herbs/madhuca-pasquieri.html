Madhuca pasquieri

What's the taxonomical classification of Madhuca pasquieri?

Madhuca pasquieri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Ericales. This plant is further categorized under the family Sapotaceae, placing it within a group of diverse woody plants. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Madhuca, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name pasquieri.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Madhuca pasquieri has a woody structure characterized by an erect habit, elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins, and small, creamy-white fragrant flowers.

The leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches and possess a coriaceous texture. Its inflorescences are often clustered in the leaf axils, bearing small petals that eventually give way to fleshy drupes. These fruits are ovoid in shape and serve as a primary morphological feature for identifying the species.

The overall vegetative morphology reflects its adaptation to tropical forest environments.

    What are the molecular targets of this plant?

    Madhuca pasquieri targets several key enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase enzymes, alpha-glucosidase, and various proinflammatory cytokines. The bioactive triterpenoids and polyphenols found in the plant act as potent inhibitors of cyclooxygenase-2, which helps reduce inflammation and pain. Additionally, its compounds interact with alpha-glucosidase to slow down carbohydrate absorption, offering potential benefits for blood glucose management. These interactions also extend to the modulation of nitric oxide synthase and various interleukins involved in the immune response. By binding to these specific molecular sites, the plant extracts exert their observed antioxidant and anti-inflammatory medicinal effects.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Madhuca pasquieri can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic responses if ingested or applied improperly. While specific clinical trials on human toxicity are limited, many species within the Maduca genus contain bioactive alkaloids that may impact metabolic processes. Excessive consumption might lead to nausea or stomach cramping in sensitive individuals. Users should also be aware of potential dermatological sensitivities when using topical extracts derived from the plant. Consulting a medical professional is recommended to avoid unpredictable interactions with existing medications.
