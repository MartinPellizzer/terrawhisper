Madhuca pasquieri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Madhuca pasquieri has a woody structure characterized by an erect habit, elliptic to oblong leaves with entire margins, and small, creamy-white fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches and possess a coriaceous texture. Its inflorescences are often clustered in the leaf axils, bearing small petals that eventually give way to fleshy drupes. These fruits are ovoid in shape and serve as a primary morphological feature for identifying the species. The overall vegetative morphology reflects its adaptation to tropical forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of Madagascar. Its specific range is confined to the island's unique ecosystems, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Within these habitats, the species is often found in lowland forests that maintain high humidity levels throughout the year. The distribution of this species is largely dictated by the localized soil compositions and microclimates found across the Madagascan landscape. Because it is endemic to this single island, its survival is closely tied to the preservation of Madagascar's remaining primary forest tracts.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Madhuca pasquieri targets several key enzymes and signaling proteins, most notably cyclooxygenase enzymes, alpha-glucosidase, and various proinflammatory cytokines. The bioactive triterpenoids and polyphenols found in the plant act as potent inhibitors of cyclooxygenase-2, which helps reduce inflammation and pain. Additionally, its compounds interact with alpha-glucosidase to slow down carbohydrate absorption, offering potential benefits for blood glucose management. These interactions also extend to the modulation of nitric oxide synthase and various interleukins involved in the immune response.
