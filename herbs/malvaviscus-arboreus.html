Mexican mallow

Malvaviscus arboreus

Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) is a flowering plant native to the Americas, known for its medicinal properties and use in traditional remedies to support respiratory and digestive health.

Taxonomy

Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) is a species belonging to the family Malvaceae, genus Malvaviscus, within the order Malvales, class Magnoliopsida, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) is a flowering plant characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with woody stems, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, pale yellow to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the growing season.

Distribution

Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) is native to the southern United States, particularly Texas, and extends into parts of Mexico, including the states of Tamaulipas, Veracruz, and Oaxaca, where it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Traditional Uses

Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its mucilage-rich leaves, which are applied topically to soothe skin irritations, reduce inflammation, and promote wound healing.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Mexican folk medicine, indigenous practices of the Americas, and traditional herbal remedies used by Native American communities, Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) has been utilized for its healing properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) includes the presence of mucilage, flavonoids, and triterpenoids, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for soothing inflammation and respiratory conditions.

Pharmacology

Mexican Mallow exhibits anti-inflammatory, demulcent, and mild laxative properties, making it useful in the treatment of respiratory and gastrointestinal conditions.

Botanical Identification

Mexican Mallow is scientifically known as Malvaviscus arboreus and belongs to the Malvaceae family. It is also called Redroot Mallow and is a perennial shrub native to Central America. The plant has hairy stems, palmate leaves, and showy red-orange flowers. It is sometimes referred to as "Mexican Mallow" in herbal texts.

Safety Profile

Mexican Mallow is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or if taking other medications.
