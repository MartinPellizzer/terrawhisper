Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus)

Mexican Mallow (Malvaviscus arboreus) is a member of the Malvaceae family, native to Central America, Northern South America, and Caribbean. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its astringent, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, native american herbal medicine, and european herbal medicine.
