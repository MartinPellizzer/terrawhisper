Aristolochia ovatifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Aristolochia ovatifolia?

Aristolochia ovatifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its descent through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, the plant is placed in the order Piperales. It is a member of the family Aristolochiaceae, falling under the genus Aristolochia, and is specifically identified by its species name, ovatifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe kidney damage, cancer, and urinary tract issues due to the presence of toxic aristolochic acids. These compounds are known for their significant nephrotoxicity, which can lead to permanent destruction of the renal tissues. Long-term exposure is closely linked to carcinogenicity, specifically increasing the risk of developing upper urinary tract cancers. Patients may also experience uropathy, a condition characterized by structural or functional abnormalities of the urinary system. In many cases, the accumulation of these toxins results in progressive renal failure.
