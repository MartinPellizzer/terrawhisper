Acanthopanax senticosus

Acanthopanax Senticosus (Acanthopanax senticosus) is a woody shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, known for its traditional use in enhancing vitality and supporting immune function, particularly in conditions such as fatigue and chronic inflammation.

Botanical Identification

Acanthopanax senticosus, also known as Siberian ginseng, is a plant in the Araliaceae family. It is native to eastern Asia and is a deciduous shrub with compound leaves and yellowish flowers. Common names include eleuthero and tien kwa, and it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for centuries.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Apiales
  • Family: Araliaceae
  • Genus: Acanthopanax
  • Species: Acanthopanax senticosus

Safety Profile

Acanthopanax senticosus is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause mild side effects such as insomnia or digestive issues. Long-term use or high doses could lead to more serious adverse effects, and it should be avoided by pregnant or breastfeeding women.
