What is Cheirolophus sempervirens?

Cheirolophus sempervirens is a perennial shrub, native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is also known as the 'evergreen cheirolophus'. The plant grows 1-2 meters in height, with grey-green leaves that are ovate or lanceolate in shape and have a wavy margin. The flowers are small, blue-purple in color, and arranged in a corymb; they bloom in summer and are hermaphroditic.

The plant produces achenes with a white or yellowish pappus, which are dispersed by wind or water.
