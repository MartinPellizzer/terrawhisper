What is Pentacalia magnusii?

Pentacalia magnusii is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is an endemic species to Ecuador and is found in the Andes mountain range. The plant is an herbaceous perennial, typically growing to a height of 100-200 cm, with a woody base and a branching habit. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical in shape, and have a length of 5-12 cm and a width of 2-4 cm, with a glossy green color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a capitulum, consisting of tubular florets and disc florets.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Pentacalia magnusii are tincture, infusion, tea, salve, and decoction.

Tincture is a concentrated liquid extract typically made with a solvent such as ethanol or glycerin. Infusion and tea are similar preparations made by steeping the plant material in hot water, but infusion uses cold water, while tea uses boiling water. Salve is a topical preparation made by infusing the plant material in oil, often with added beeswax to create a solid balm.

Decoction is a preparation made by boiling the plant material in water for an extended period to extract its active compounds.
