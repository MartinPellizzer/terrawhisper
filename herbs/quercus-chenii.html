Quercus chenii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Quercus chenii has a spreading, irregular crown with rough, deeply fissured bark and small, leathery leaves that are oblong to elliptical in shape. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly wavy, often featuring a thick texture suited for dry environments. Its flowers are inconspicuous and borne in catkins, which are characteristic of the oak genus. The plant produces small, nut-like acorns that sit atop a shallow, cup-shaped receptacle. These morphological traits help the species adapt to its specific ecological niche within the scrublands.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the southern regions of China, specifically occurring within the provinces of Guangdong, Guangxi, Fujian, and Hainan. It typically thrives in subtropical evergreen broad-leaved forests located at various elevations. The species is often found in hilly or mountainous terrain where the climate remains warm and humid throughout the year. Within these specific habitats, it contributes to the complex structure of the regional forest canopy. Its distribution is strictly limited to these East Asian subtropical zones.

If consumed, the tannins and other chemical compounds present in the plant can lead to nausea, vomiting, or stomach cramps.
