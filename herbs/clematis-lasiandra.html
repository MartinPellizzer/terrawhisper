What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis lasiandra?

Clematis lasiandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is further categorized under the genus Clematis, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species lasiandra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis lasiandra has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically 2 to 5 centimeters long and possess entire margins. The plant features a woody climbing habit facilitated by its specialized petioles that act as tendrils for support. Its flowers are relatively small and characterized by four to five cream-colored or yellowish tepals. These blossoms are accompanied by numerous prominent stamens that contribute to the flower's delicate appearance. The fruit consists of many small, dry achenes, each topped with a long, silky style that aids in wind dispersal.
