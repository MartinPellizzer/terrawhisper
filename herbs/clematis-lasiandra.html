What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis lasiandra?

Clematis lasiandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary lineage of the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, this plant is further categorized under the genus Clematis, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species lasiandra.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Clematis lasiandra can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that acts as a potent irritant to mucous membranes and skin upon contact. People handling the plant may experience redness, itching, or blistering in areas where the sap touches the body. If any part of the plant is eaten, it can lead to severe stomach pain, vomiting, or diarrhea. These adverse reactions occur because the toxic components react aggressively with biological tissues.
