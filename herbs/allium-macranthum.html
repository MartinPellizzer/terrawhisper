Allium macranthum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium macranthum has linear, basal leaves that are typically green and grow directly from the bulb. The plant features a central, leafless stem known as a scape that supports a large, spherical umbel of flowers. Each individual blossom is characterized by its prominent, large petals which give the species its name. These flowers transition through various shades of pink or white depending on the specific variety. The overall structure is robust and upright, reaching heights that vary based on its environmental conditions.
