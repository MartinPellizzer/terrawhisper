Dontostemon dentatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dontostemon dentatus has upright stems reaching up to 40 centimeters in height with opposite, lanceolate leaves that feature sharply serrated margins. The foliage is primarily basal, forming a rosette of leaves that transition into smaller, narrower leaves along the flowering stem. Small, white to pale lavender flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by four petals that are slightly unequal in size. These blooms are supported by slender pedicels that rise above the dense, green vegetative structure. The plant typically develops a woody base as it matures, providing stability to its delicate flowering stalks.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the eastern United States, specifically ranging from southern New England through the Mid-Atlantic and down into the Southeast. Populations are most commonly found in states like New York, Pennsylvania, Virginia, and North Carolina.
