Dontostemon dentatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Dontostemon dentatus?

Dontostemon dentatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary path continues through the order Brassicales and the family Brassicaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Dontostemon, and its species name, dentatus.

Dontostemon dentatus is prepared as an infusion, decoction, or tincture to utilize its medicinal properties. These different preparations allow for varied methods of administration depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dontostemon dentatus can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis or localized rashes when handled by sensitive individuals. While clinical data on human toxicity is limited, many members of the Saxifragaceae family may trigger hypersensitivity in certain populations. Some people might experience mild irritation or redness if the plant comes into direct contact with the skin or mucous membranes. It is important to use caution and avoid ingestion since the full pharmacological profile and potential systemic effects remain understudied.
