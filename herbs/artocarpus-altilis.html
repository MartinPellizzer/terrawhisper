Artocarpus altilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Artocarpus altilis?

Artocarpus altiilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Rosales. It is a member of the family Moraceae and is specifically identified within the genus Artocarpus, with the specific epithet altilis.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Artocarpus altiilis contains medicinal properties found in its leaves, bark, fruit, and roots. The leaves are frequently prepared as a decoction to treat skin inflammations and digestive issues. Bark extracts are often utilized in traditional practices to manage fever and certain types of swelling. The fruit itself provides essential nutrients and antioxidants that support general immune health. Additionally, the roots can be processed into medicinal preparations to address various internal ailments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive constituents including flavonoids, terpenoids, phenolic compounds, alkaloids, steroids, tannins, and saponins.

These chemical groups contribute to the significant pharmacological potential observed in various parts of the tree. Specifically, the presence of phenolic compounds and flavonoids provides strong antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The alkaloids and steroids are often linked to various physiological activities within biological systems.

Furthermore, the combination of tannins and saponins contributes to the antimicrobial and therapeutic capabilities of the species.

  • Flavonoids
  • Terpenoids
  • Phenolic compounds
  • Alkaloids
  • Steroids
  • Tannins
  • Saponins

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Artocarpus altiilis targets enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2, alpha-glucosidase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its therapeutic effects. The presence of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and acetogenins allows the plant to inhibit the COX-2 enzyme, which reduces pain and inflammation in the body. Additionally, its constituents act on alpha-glucosidase to slow down carbohydrate absorption, making it a potential agent for managing blood glucose levels. These phytochemicals also interact with nitric oxide synthase and various interleukins to modulate the immune response. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant demonstrates significant antioxidant, anti-diabetic, and anti-inflammatory properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Artocarpus altilis?

Artocarpus altilis has significant therapeutic potential due to its high concentrations of bioactive compounds like flavonoids, polyphenols, and sterols that provide antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. The fruit and leaves contain phytochemicals that help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within the body. These natural constituents assist in managing systemic inflammation and may support cardiovascular health by improving lipid profiles. Additionally, certain extracts from the plant exhibit antibacterial activity that can aid in combating various pathogens. Regular consumption or topical application of its derivatives can contribute to overall metabolic regulation and immune support.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat diabetes, high blood pressure, skin infections, and various inflammatory conditions. The leaves are often prepared as a decoction to help regulate blood glucose levels in diabetic patients. Some traditional practices also utilize the bark and fruit to soothe skin irritations and promote healing. Additionally, extracts from the plant are believed to possess antioxidant properties that help combat oxidative stress in the body. These medicinal applications rely on the diverse bioactive compounds found throughout the various parts of the tree.

What are the herbal preparations of Artocarpus altilis?

Artocarpus altilis is prepared as decoctions from the bark, infusions from the leaves, and topical poultices from the crushed fruit or roots. The bark is commonly boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used to treat various internal ailments. Leaves are often steeped in hot water to produce a soothing liquid for topical applications or consumption. In some traditional practices, the raw sap or crushed fruit flesh is applied directly to wounds to facilitate healing. These diverse methods allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be utilized for both systemic and localized therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Artocarpus altiilis can cause allergic skin reactions, such as contact dermatitis, in individuals sensitive to its milky white latex. This sticky sap can lead to redness, itching, or swelling if it comes into direct contact with the skin. Some people may also experience gastrointestinal discomfort if they consume large quantities of the fruit or its derivatives. While generally safe for most, those with existing latex allergies should exercise extreme caution. Monitoring for any signs of an adverse reaction is recommended when introducing this plant into a diet.
