Artocarpus altilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artocarpus altiilis has large, leathery, evergreen leaves that are broadly elliptical or obovate in shape, measuring up to 60 centimeters in length with prominent, deeply impressed pinnate venation. The plant typically develops into a massive tree reaching heights of up to 30 meters with a spreading crown and a sturdy, straight trunk. Its bark is greyish-brown and often exudes a milky white latex when injured. The most distinctive morphological feature is the large, compound fruit, which is covered in numerous small, fleshy protuberances and contains a sweet, starchy pulp surrounding large seeds. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in separate male and female inflorescences on the same tree.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the islands of the Malay Archipelago. It has since been widely introduced to various tropical maritime environments across the globe through human cultivation. Today, significant populations are found throughout the Pacific Islands, the Caribbean, and parts of Central and South America. Its distribution is strictly limited to warm, humid climates where frost is absent. Many coastal areas in Africa and India also support extensive plantations of this species.

Artocarpus altiilis contains medicinal properties found in its leaves, bark, fruit, and roots. The leaves are frequently prepared as a decoction to treat skin inflammations and digestive issues. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Artocarpus altiilis contains bioactive compounds including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, alkaloids, and terpenoids that provide various therapeutic benefits. These chemical constituents exhibit significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties when utilized in traditional medicine. Specifically, the extracts from the bark and leaves are often studied for their potential to regulate blood glucose levels and reduce oxidative stress. Some studies also suggest that certain phytochemicals in the fruit and stem can assist in managing gastrointestinal issues and skin irritations. This diverse profile of secondary metabolites makes the plant a subject of interest for developing natural pharmaceutical treatments.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Artocarpus altiilis targets enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2, alpha-glucosidase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its therapeutic effects. The presence of bioactive compounds like flavonoids and acetogenins allows the plant to inhibit the COX-2 enzyme, which reduces pain and inflammation in the body. Additionally, its constituents act on alpha-glucosidase to slow down carbohydrate absorption, making it a potential agent for managing blood glucose levels. These phytochemicals also interact with nitric oxide synthase and various interleukins to modulate the immune response. Through these diverse protein interactions, the plant demonstrates significant antioxidant, anti-diabetic, and anti-inflammatory properties.

What are the herbal preparations of Artocarpus altilis?

Artocarpus altilis is prepared as decoctions from the bark, infusions from the leaves, and topical poultices from the crushed fruit or roots. The bark is commonly boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used to treat various internal ailments. Leaves are often steeped in hot water to produce a soothing liquid for topical applications or consumption. In some traditional practices, the raw sap or crushed fruit flesh is applied directly to wounds to facilitate healing. These diverse methods allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be utilized for both systemic and localized therapeutic purposes.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Artocarpus altiilis can cause allergic skin reactions, such as contact dermatitis, in individuals sensitive to its milky white latex. This sticky sap can lead to redness, itching, or swelling if it comes into direct contact with the skin. Some people may also experience gastrointestinal discomfort if they consume large quantities of the fruit or its derivatives. While generally safe for most, those with existing latex allergies should exercise extreme caution. Monitoring for any signs of an adverse reaction is recommended when introducing this plant into a diet.
