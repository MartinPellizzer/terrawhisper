Sapindus delaavay belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its specific classification within the genus Sapindus as the species delaayi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sapinduus delavayi has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of closely spaced, elliptic to oblong leaflets that are glossy green on the upper surface and slightly paler beneath. The leaflets feature entire margins and acute to acuminate apices, often appearing somewhat leathery in texture. The plant develops small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a fleshy, globose drupe that transitions from green to a bright red or orange color when mature. The woody stems and branches are characterized by a smooth to slightly fissured bark depending on the age of the tree.
