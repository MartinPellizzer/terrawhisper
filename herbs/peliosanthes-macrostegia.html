Peliosanthes macrostegia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Peliosanthes macrostegia has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by large, ovate, thin-textured leaves with prominent venation and specialized, large, bell-shaped flowers. The foliage typically features long petioles that elevate the broad blades above the forest floor substrate. Its most distinctive morphological feature is the oversized, fleshy perianth that serves to attract specific pollinators through scent and visual cues. The reproductive structures are centered within this expanded floral chamber, which provides a protected environment for pollination events. This plant's structural complexity is specifically adapted to the humid, low-light conditions of its tropical understory habitat.
