Xylosma congesta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Xylosma congesta has opposite, elliptic to oblong leaves that measure approximately 5 to 15 centimeters in length and possess entire margins with acute tips. The foliage is typically dark green and leathery in texture, often displaying a slightly glossy surface. Its stems are woody and can develop a somewhat spreading or shrubby growth habit depending on environmental conditions. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often appearing in dense arrangements near the leaf nodes. The plant eventually develops small, fleshy fruits that serve as a primary reproductive feature.
