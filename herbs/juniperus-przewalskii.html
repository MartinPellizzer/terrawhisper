What's the taxonomical classification of Juniperus przewalskii?

Juniperus przewalskii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida, the subclass Pinidae, and the order Pinales. As a member of the family Cupressaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Juniperus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species przewalskii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juniperus przewalskii has an upright, spreading habit with scale-like leaves that are arranged in opposite or whorled decussate patterns on short shoots. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a somewhat stiff, prickly texture due to the pointed tips of the leaves. Its branches are woody and often twist slightly as the tree matures into a large shrub or small tree. The plant produces small, globose, fleshy cones that transition from a blue-green to a deep glaucous blue when ripe. These cones are typically around six to nine millimeters in diameter and contain the seeds for reproduction.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of central and western China, specifically within the provinces of Qinghai, Gansu, Sichuan, and Tibet. It typically thrives in alpine and subalpine zones situated along the edges of mountain ridges and rocky slopes. These populations are often found at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters above sea level. The species prefers harsh, cold environments where it can withstand significant seasonal temperature fluctuations.
