Podocarpus macrophyllus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Podocarpus macrophyllus has an evergreen habit characterized by linear, leathery leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long and dark green in color. The foliage grows in a dense, spirally arranged pattern along woody, branching stems. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous male cones and fleshy, berry-like female receptacles that turn reddish or purple when mature. The tree develops a sturdy, upright trunk with fissured gray-brown bark as it reaches maturity. The overall structure is often used for ornamental hedging due to its compact and resilient growth form.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across southern China, Taiwan, and Japan. It thrives primarily in coastal areas and mountainous terrains where humidity remains relatively high. Beyond its natural range, it has been widely introduced to Mediterranean climates and warm temperate zones around the world. Many species have established themselves in cultivation within parts of Europe and the United States due to their ornamental value.
