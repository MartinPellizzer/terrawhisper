What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichosanthes laceribractea has prostrate, herbaceous stems that feature serrated or lacerate bracts and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its reproductive structures are small and often tucked within the leaf axils, reflecting its climbing habit. The plant develops specialized tendrils that allow it to anchor itself to surrounding vegetation.
