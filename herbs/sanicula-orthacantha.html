What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sanicula orthacantha has upright, woody stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and bear leaves with three to five distinct lobes and sharply serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a deep green color and a texture that can appear somewhat rough or hairy depending on the specimen. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense, head-like clusters at the ends of the stems. These floral structures eventually transition into small, nut-like fruits that are often equipped with tiny, hooked bristles to aid in seed dispersal. The overall architecture of the plant is compact and bushy, reflecting its adaptation to specific forest floor habitats.
