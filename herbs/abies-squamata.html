What's the taxonomical classification of Abies squamata?

Abies squamata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae. Following its placement in the order Pinales and the family Pinaceae, the plant is further defined by its genus, Abies, and its specific species, squamata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Abies squamata has a small, shrubby growth habit characterized by short branches, scale-like leaves, and relatively small, ovoid cones. The foliage consists of tiny, overlapping bracts that resemble scales rather than the typical needle-like leaves found in many other fir species. Its branches are often spreading or ascending, giving the plant a compact and dense appearance. The cones are typically held upright on the branches and are much smaller in size compared to those of larger fir trees.
