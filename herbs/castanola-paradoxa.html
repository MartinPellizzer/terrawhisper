Castanola paradoxa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Castanola paradoxa has woody, erect stems with small, simple, lanceolate leaves that are arranged oppositely along the branches. The foliage typically displays a leathery texture with entire margins and prominent venation. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in small clusters. These flowers eventually give way to small, woody drupes that serve as the plant's fruit. The overall structure of the plant is characteristic of many members within the Myrtaceae family.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Castanola paradoxa can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves may lead to contact dermatitis characterized by redness and itching. Some individuals might experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions depending on their biological sensitivity to its chemical constituents. Ingestion of any part of the plant poses a risk of nausea or stomach pain. It is important to exercise caution and avoid direct skin contact to prevent these adverse effects.
