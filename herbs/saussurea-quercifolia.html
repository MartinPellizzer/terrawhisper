Saussurea quercifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea quercifolia?

Saussurea quercifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. It is further categorized into the genus Saussurea, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species quercifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea quercifolia has deeply lobed, oak-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette and feature serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by its leathery texture and a distinctively dark green color on the upper surface. Stems arising from the base are often hairy and support small, inconspicuous flowering heads. These inflorescences consist of dense clusters of small, tubular florets tucked within protective bracts.
