Saussurea quercifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea quercifolia has deeply lobed, oak-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette and feature serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by its leathery texture and a distinctively dark green color on the upper surface. Stems arising from the base are often hairy and support small, inconspicuous flowering heads. These inflorescences consist of dense clusters of small, tubular florets tucked within protective bracts. The overall structure of the plant is compact, reflecting its adaptation to high-altitude environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically ranging across parts of northern India, Nepal, Bhutan, and Tibet. It thrives in alpine and subalpine zones where rocky terrain and scree slopes are common. These populations are typically found at elevations between 3,000 and 4,500 meters above sea level. The distribution is dictated by the specific microclimates of mountain ridges and meadows.
