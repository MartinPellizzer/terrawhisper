Viburnum sempervirens

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum sempervirens?

Viburnum sempervirens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized into the order Dipsacales. This plant is a member of the family Viburnaceae and is situated within the genus Viburnum, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species sempervirens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum sempervirens has an evergreen, multi-stemmed shrubby growth habit characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are typically serrated, while the upper surfaces display a deep green luster and the undersides appear paler. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, creamy white blossoms arranged in dense, flat-topped cymes. These inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils and are followed by small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to red or black upon ripening. The bark of the mature stems is relatively smooth, providing a sturdy structural framework for the dense foliage.
