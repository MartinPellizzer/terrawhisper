Viburnum sempervirens

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum sempervirens?

Viburnum sempervirens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized into the order Dipsacales. This plant is a member of the family Viburnaceae and is situated within the genus Viburnum, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species sempervirens.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Viburnum sempervirens contains medicinal properties primarily concentrated in its bark and roots. Traditional healers have historically utilized these specific woody components to address various ailments. The extracts from the bark are often studied for their potential to alleviate inflammatory conditions. Some folk medicine practices also involve using the roots to treat digestive discomfort or fever. Careful preparation of these plant parts is essential to harness their bioactive compounds effectively.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive substances including flavonoids, terpenoids, and various phenolic compounds that contribute to its medicinal potential.

These specific chemical constituents are known to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties when utilized in therapeutic contexts. The presence of flavonoids helps to neutralize free radicals and protect cellular structures from oxidative stress. Additionally, the terpenoids and phenolic compounds may offer antimicrobial effects that support the plant's overall biological activity.

Together, these compounds form a complex chemical profile that serves as the basis for its pharmacological interest.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Flavonoids
  • Terpenoids
  • Phenolic compounds

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Viburnum sempervirens targets inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) and various lipoxygenases to mitigate systemic inflammation. The plant contains bioactive phytochemicals like flavonoids and phenolic acids that modulate the activity of pro-inflammatory cytokines, specifically inhibiting the release of tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. These secondary metabolites also interact with the nuclear factor kappa B (NF-κB) signaling pathway to suppress the transcriptional activation of inflammatory genes. Furthermore, certain compounds in the plant exhibit antioxidant properties by scavenging reactive oxygen species and regulating superoxide dismutase activity. This multi-target approach allows the plant to exert its therapeutic effects on oxidative stress and immune regulation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Viburnum sempervirens?

Viburnum sempervirens has therapeutic properties primarily utilized in traditional medicine to address inflammation, digestive issues, and various skin conditions. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may act as mild analgesics to soothe localized pain or discomfort. Some practitioners use decoctions of the bark or leaves to help regulate bowel movements and alleviate gastrointestinal distress. Topical applications of processed plant material are often employed to reduce swelling and promote the healing of minor cutaneous irritations. These medicinal actions are rooted in the plant's historical use within specific regional herbal traditions.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including respiratory issues, fever, skin infections, and digestive disturbances. In traditional medicinal practices, its leaves and bark are often prepared as decoctions or poultices to soothe inflammation. The plant possesses properties that are believed to assist in lowering high body temperatures and calming persistent coughs. Some herbalists also utilize its components to address minor wounds or topical irritations on the skin. These applications are rooted in long-standing ethnobotanical knowledge regarding its bioactive compounds.

What are the herbal preparations of Viburnum sempervirens?

Viburnum sempervirens is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a tincture using the dried leaves, bark, or berries of the plant. The leaves and bark are often boiled in water for an extended period to extract the medicinal constituents for a decoction. For more delicate parts or to capture volatile compounds, a hot water infusion is frequently used. Tinctures are created by soaking the plant material in alcohol to produce a concentrated liquid extract for long-term storage. These various methods allow practitioners to tailor the potency and delivery of the plant's active properties.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Viburnum sempervirens can cause skin irritation, localized swelling, and allergic reactions in sensitive individuals through direct contact with its sap or leaves. While it is generally considered non-toxic to humans when not ingested, the plant contains compounds that may trigger dermatitis. Contact dermatitis is the most common side effect experienced by gardeners handling the shrub. Some people may also experience mild gastrointestinal distress if parts of the plant are accidentally consumed. Monitoring for redness or itching is recommended for those with known plant sensitivities.
