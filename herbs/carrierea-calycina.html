What's the taxonomical classification of Carrierea calycina?

Carrierea calycina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Malpighiales, this plant is a member of the family Salicaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Carrierea and the species epithet calycina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carrierea calycina has opposite, elliptic to oblong leaves that are typically 3 to 8 centimeters long with entire margins and acute or obtuse apices.

The foliage is characterized by a smooth texture and a dark green color on the upper surface. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in axillary clusters. The calyx is notably prominent, which serves as the basis for its specific epithet.

These botanical features are characteristic of its growth habit within its native tropical habitat.
