Parkinsonia aculeata

What's the taxonomical classification of Parkinsonia aculeata?

Parkinsonia aculeata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Fabales. It is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which is a prominent group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Parkinsonia, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name aculeaata.

Its flowers are small and pale, often appearing in clusters that emerge from the axils of the leaves. What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for the treatment of fungal infections, specifically targeting various species of Candida spp. Researchers have identified that the bioactive compounds within the plant possess significant antifungal properties. These constituents work by inhibiting the growth and replication of yeast cells within the body. By disrupting the cellular integrity of the fungi, the plant helps alleviate symptoms associated with these infections. Consequently, it serves as a traditional remedy for managing diverse dermatological and systemic fungal issues.

According to a study published by "Pakistan journal of pharmaceuctical sciences", the methanol extracts of Parkinsonia aculeata were evaluated for antifungal activity. The study used the micro dilution assay described in CLSI M27-A protocol to test clinical isolates of 5 different species of Candida, including C. albicans, C. parapsilosis, C. tropicalis, C. krusei and C. glabrata. All extracts, including those from Parkinsonia aculeata, showed MIC values < 31.25μg/mL against at least one of the strains used. The research focused on addressing severe fungal infections caused by Candida spp which are associated with extremely high rates of morbidity and mortality.
