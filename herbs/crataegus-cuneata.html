Crataegus cuneata

What's the taxonomical classification of Crataegus cuneata?

Crataegus cuneata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Rosales. The plant is a member of the family Rosaceae and falls within the genus Crataegus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species cuneata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Crataegus cuneata contains high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids, procyanidins, triterpenoids, and organic acids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These polyphenolic compounds function primarily as potent antioxidants that neutralize free radicals within the body. Many of these constituents also demonstrate significant cardioprotective effects by improving blood flow and regulating vascular tension. The presence of specific tannins and phenolic acids further supports its traditional use in treating digestive and inflammatory conditions. Research suggests these chemical profiles work synergistically to enhance overall cardiovascular health and cellular protection.

What are the therapeutic actions of Crataegus cuneata?

Crataegus cuneata has cardioprotective properties that primarily work by increasing coronary blood flow, improving myocardial contractility, and exerting mild vasodilatory effects on the coronary arteries. These therapeutic actions are largely driven by high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids and oligomeric procyanidins found within the plant tissues. By reducing oxidative stress and inhibiting certain inflammatory pathways, the plant helps to protect cardiac muscle cells from damage. It is frequently utilized in traditional and modern herbal medicine to support overall cardiovascular health and manage symptoms of mild heart insufficiency. This multifaceted approach makes it a significant botanical agent for maintaining circulatory efficiency.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat congestive heart failure, cardiac arrhythmias, and symptoms associated with coronary artery disease. Its active compounds, specifically procyanidins and flavonoids, help improve blood flow and strengthen the contractions of the heart muscle. Patients often utilize extracts from this species to manage mild hypertension and stabilize irregular heartbeats. These medicinal properties are believed to work by increasing coronary blood flow and providing antioxidant protection to the cardiovascular system. Regular use in traditional medicine aims to reduce the overall workload on the heart during physical activity.
