Crataegus cuneata

What's the taxonomical classification of Crataegus cuneata?

Crataegus cuneata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Rosales. The plant is a member of the family Rosaceae and falls within the genus Crataegus, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species cuneata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crataegus cuneata has small, ovate leaves with serrated margins, woody stems protected by sharp thorns, and small white flowers that transition into fleshy red or black drupes. The foliage typically displays a dark green color on the upper surface with a slightly paler underside. Its branching structure is dense and irregular, often forming a shrubby or small tree habit depending on the environment. The fruit develops from a single ovary and serves as a significant morphological feature during the fruiting season. These characteristic structures help distinguish the species from other members of the Rosaceae family.
