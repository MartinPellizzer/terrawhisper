Scorodonia

Teucrium scorodonia

Scorodonia (Teucrium scorodonia) is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, known for its aromatic leaves and traditional use in herbal medicine to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.

Morphology

Scorodonia, or Teucrium scorodonia, is a perennial herb with square, ascending stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs, bearing opposite, oval to lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a fragrant, aromatic scent.

Distribution

Scorodonia (Teucrium scorodonia) is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, and is commonly found in rocky soils and dry habitats.

Traditional Uses

Scorodonia, also known as Teucrium scorodonia, has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its digestive properties, particularly to treat symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and flatulence.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Unani, and traditional Chinese medicine, Scorodonia (Teucrium scorodonia) has been utilized for its purported therapeutic properties.

Scorodonia (Teucrium scorodonia) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including essential oils, flavonoids, tannins, and iridoid glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

Pharmacology

Scorodonia (Teucrium scorodonia) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and mild sedative effects, making it useful in traditional herbal medicine for treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

Botanical Identification

Scorodonia, also known as Teucrium scorodonia, is a perennial herb in the Lamiaceae family. Native to the Mediterranean, it is commonly called "Greek tea" or "Scorodonia." The plant has square stems, aromatic leaves, and small blue or purple flowers. It is sometimes referred to by older names like "Scorodona" in herbal texts.
