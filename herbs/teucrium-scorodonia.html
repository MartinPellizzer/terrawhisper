Scientific and Botanical Profile

Scorodonia, with botanical name Teucrium scorodonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family, commonly known as the mint family.

It is widely recognized by various common names such as Blueweed, Scorodonia Germander, Greek Valerian, and Blue Germander, among others, reflecting its widespread use and regional significance. Native to Southern Europe, Western Asia, North Africa, and the Canary Islands, Sardinia, Corsica, Turkey, Lebanon, and other Mediterranean regions, this plant thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. Morphologically, Scorodonia features upright, branched stems that are typically reddish or purple in color, with opposite, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and the plant is sometimes referred to as Teucrium Blueweed or Turk's Cap Thyme due to its distinctive appearance and aromatic properties.

Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Teucrium scorodonia grows best in well-drained, loamy soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH, thriving in full sun to partial shade and requiring regular watering during dry periods without becoming waterlogged.

It is relatively low maintenance, benefiting from occasional weeding and pruning to promote air circulation and prevent disease. The best time to harvest Teucrium scorodonia is during the flowering stage, typically in late summer, using sharp scissors or shears to cut the top 10-15 cm of the plant to encourage regrowth. After harvesting, the leaves and flowers should be dried in a cool, dark, well-ventilated area to preserve their potency, and stored in airtight containers away from light and moisture.

Proper storage ensures the medicinal properties remain intact for several months, making it a valuable herb for traditional and herbal medicine.
