What is Scorodonia (Teucrium Scorodonia)?

Scorodonia, also known as Teucrium scorodonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, commonly used in herbal medicine for its digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Common Names and Synonyms

Scorodonia, also known as Teucrium scorodonia, is commonly referred to by various names including Armenian germander, mountain germander, and scorzonera, reflecting its traditional use in herbal medicine across different regions.

Scorodonia, or Teucrium scorodonia, is a perennial herb with square, ascending stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs, bearing opposite, oval to lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a fragrant, aromatic scent.
