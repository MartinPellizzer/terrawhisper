Erigeron bonariensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Erigeron bonariensis?

Erigeron bonariensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a diverse group of flowering plants, and is specifically categorized under the genus Erigeron. Within this genus, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific designation as the species bonariensis.

Some practitioners may also utilize the plant in the form of a topical tincture applied directly to the skin for external use. Additionally, the dried material can be incorporated into salves or ointments to facilitate local absorption. These various methods allow for the versatile administration of the plant's properties depending on the intended therapeutic goal.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Erigeron bonariensis can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or localized swelling if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive tissue. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, members of the Asteraceae family often contain sesquiterpene lactones that trigger contact hypersensitivity. Individuals with existing allergies to ragweed or daisies may experience more severe reactions such as itching or redness. It is generally advisable to avoid handling the plant without gloves to prevent these dermatological issues. Monitoring for any signs of respiratory distress or systemic swelling is recommended if accidental ingestion occurs.
