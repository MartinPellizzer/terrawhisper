Carpinus oblongifolia

Carpinus oblongifolia, commonly known as Korean hornbeam, is a deciduous tree species native to East Asia, characterized by its distinctive oblong leaves and robust growth habit.

This tree is valued for its medicinal properties, which include a range of health benefits such as antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects, contributing to the improvement of cardiovascular health, immune function, and skin conditions.

The therapeutic actions of Carpinus oblongifolia are attributed to its bioactive constituents, including flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which have been shown to exhibit anti-cancer, anti-diabetic, and anti-hypertensive properties.

Furthermore, the plant's extracts have been reported to exhibit anti-aging and anti-inflammatory effects, making it a valuable natural resource for traditional medicine and modern pharmaceutical research.

The roots of Carpinus oblongifolia contain saponins, including oleanolic acid and ursolic acid, which have been traditionally used to treat fever and respiratory issues.

Possible Side Effects of Carpinus oblongifolia

Carpinus oblongifolia can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and digestive issues.

Ingesting large amounts of the plant can lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, as well as increased heart rate and blood pressure.

If used topically, Carpinus oblongifolia can cause skin redness, itching, and inflammation, particularly if left on the skin for extended periods.

Prolonged exposure to the plant's sap or extracts can also lead to respiratory problems, such as bronchitis and asthma exacerbation.
