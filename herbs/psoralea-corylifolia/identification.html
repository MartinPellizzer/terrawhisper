Botanical Identification of Corylifolia Psoralea (Psoralea corylifolia)

To identify Corylifolia Psoralea (Psoralea corylifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Corylifolia Psoralea, scientifically known as Psoralea corylifolia, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Psoralea, and species Psoralea corylifolia. It is a member of the legume family and is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Psoralea corylifolia, first described by the botanist Jussieu in 1789. It is also known by several synonyms, including Mucuna corylifolia and Dolichoris corylifolia. Common names such as "Babool" and "Khesari" are widely used in various regions. This name reflects its traditional use in herbal medicine across different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Psoralea corylifolia is an erect shrub with a taproot system. Its stems are branched and covered in short, soft hairs. The leaves are pinnately compound, with 7-13 pairs of elliptical leaflets. The inflorescence is a raceme, bearing small yellow flowers. The fruit is a flattened pod containing two kidney-shaped seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Corylifolia Psoralea can be distinguished by its compound leaves with 5-11 pairs of oval leaflets, each with a short stalk. The flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in axillary spikes. The fruit is a flat, oblong pod containing several kidney-shaped seeds. These features help differentiate it from other Psoralea species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Psoralea corylifolia is native to regions of India, Southeast Asia, and parts of China. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes ranging from 300 to 1,500 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Corylifolia Psoralea blooms from April to June, producing small yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs between July and September. Harvesting for identification purposes is typically done in late autumn when the seeds are fully mature and the plant has completed its annual growth cycle.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Psoralea corylifolia belongs to the genus Psoralea within the legume family Fabaceae. It shares close phylogenetic relationships with other Psoralea species, characterized by similar morphological and genetic traits. Its family traits include compound leaves and pod-like fruits, typical of the Fabaceae family.
