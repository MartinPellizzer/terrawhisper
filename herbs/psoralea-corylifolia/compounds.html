Corylifolia Psoralea (Psoralea corylifolia) Compounds

The primary compounds of Corylifolia Psoralea include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and coumarin, known for its anticoagulant effects. Tannins and phenolic acids contribute to its astringent and anti-inflammatory actions. Saponins and glycosides support immune function, while terpenoids and essential oils provide antimicrobial benefits. Alkaloids and anthocyanins also play roles in medicinal effects.

Corylifolia Psoralea (Psoralea corylifolia) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Psoralea corylifolia. They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for treating various ailments.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Psoralea corylifolia. It exhibits anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties. Tannin is a key active compound in Psoralea corylifolia. It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to support skin health and wound healing. Tannins in this plant may also aid in reducing inflammation and promoting tissue repair.
