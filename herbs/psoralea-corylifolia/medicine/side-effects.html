10 Possible Side Effects Of Psoralea Corylifolia

Psoralea corylifolia has some side effects when used improperly, such as photosensitivity and skin irritation, which can be caused by its active compounds, lignans and coumarins.

Prolonged exposure to sunlight can worsen these effects, leading to blisters, burns, and scarring, further affecting people's self-esteem and daily activities.

1. Causes phototoxicity

Psoralea corylifolia causes phototoxicity in some individuals.

This is due to the presence of furanocoumarins, a class of compounds that can increase the skin's sensitivity to sunlight and ultraviolet (UV) radiation. When taken with exposure to UV light, these compounds can cause damage to DNA and lead to an increased risk of skin reactions, such as redness, itching, blistering, and burning.

This reaction can be severe in some cases, making it essential to avoid prolonged sun exposure after taking Psoralea corylifolia.

2. Induces allergic reactions

Psoralea corylifolia induces allergic reactions.

This is due to the presence of sesquiterpenes and flavonoids in its extract, which can cause an immune response and trigger symptoms such as hives, itching, and respiratory issues in some individuals. Additionally, the herb's ability to stimulate histamine release can exacerbate allergies and asthma-like conditions.

As a result, people who are prone to allergic reactions may experience adverse effects when using Psoralea corylifolia.

3. Increases risk of bleeding

Psoralea corylifolia increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

The flavonoids present in the plant's extracts can interact with blood clotting factors, leading to a decrease in the production of thromboxane A2, a prostaglandin that plays a crucial role in blood clotting.

This interaction may result in excessive bleeding or prolonged bleeding after an injury.

4. Affects liver function

Psoralea corylifolia affects liver function.

This is due to its ability to alter the metabolism of certain enzymes and compounds in the body, which can lead to changes in liver enzyme levels. Additionally, Psoralea corylifolia contains furanocoumarins, a group of chemicals that have been shown to inhibit liver function, potentially resulting in damage or inflammation.

As a result, people may experience altered liver function, including increased liver enzymes, when using this herbal supplement.

5. Interacts with warfarin

Psoralea corylifolia interacts with warfarin by inhibiting its metabolism in the liver.

This can lead to increased levels of warfarin in the blood, resulting in excessive bleeding and bruising. The interaction may occur due to Psoralea corylifolia's ability to suppress cytochrome P450 enzymes, which are responsible for breaking down warfarin.

As a result, patients taking both substances concurrently may experience enhanced anticoagulant effects, increasing the risk of adverse reactions.

6. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Psoralea corylifolia produces gastrointestinal upset due to its ability to stimulate digestive secretions and alter gut flora.

The bioactive compounds present in the plant, such as psoralen and bakuchiol, can irritate the mucous membranes of the stomach and intestines, leading to symptoms like nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.

These effects are often dose-dependent and may be more pronounced in individuals with pre-existing digestive issues or who consume high amounts of Psoralea corylifolia.

7. Triggers skin irritation

Psoralea corylifolia triggers skin irritation due to its high concentration of furanocoumarins and psoralen compounds.

These phytochemicals can cause phototoxic reactions when exposed to sunlight, leading to inflammation, redness, and discomfort on the skin. The risk is higher for individuals with fair skin or those who are more sensitive to UV light.

As a result, some users may experience adverse effects such as itching, burning, and blistering.

8. Enhances risk of kidney damage

Psoralea corylifolia enhances risk of kidney damage due to its toxic compounds, such as psoralen and bergapten.

These chemicals can accumulate in the body and cause kidney injury, potentially leading to chronic kidney disease or even kidney failure.

The prolonged use of Psoralea corylifolia supplements may exacerbate this risk, especially in individuals with pre-existing kidney issues.

9. Inhibits blood clotting

Psoralea corylifolia inhibits blood clotting by interfering with platelet aggregation and thromboxane production.

This is due to its ability to block the action of platelet-derived growth factor, which plays a crucial role in platelet activation and clot formation.

Additionally, Psoralea corylifolia's flavonoids have been shown to inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in coagulation pathways, further contributing to its anticoagulant effects.

10. Stimulates seizures

Psoralea corylifolia stimulates seizures due to its ability to increase the activity of certain neurotransmitters in the brain.

This can lead to an imbalance of electrical impulses, resulting in a seizure.

Additionally, Psoralea corylifolia has been found to affect the levels of serotonin and dopamine, neurotransmitters that play a crucial role in regulating mood, appetite, and sleep, which may also contribute to the increased risk of seizures.
