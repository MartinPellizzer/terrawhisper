10 Health Benefits Of Psoralea Corylifolia (Babchi)

Psoralea corylifolia, also known as babchi, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation, promoting skin rejuvenation, and treating various skin conditions.

Its medicinal properties include antibacterial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory compounds that contribute to its therapeutic effects.

By alleviating symptoms of psoriasis, eczema, and other dermatological issues, babchi can improve people's lives by enhancing skin health, reducing discomfort, and promoting overall well-being.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Psoralea corylifolia .

1. Lowers cholesterol levels

Psoralea corylifolia lowers cholesterol levels because its active compounds, particularly psoralen and isopsoralen, exhibit hypocholesterolemic properties.

These compounds work by inhibiting the absorption of dietary cholesterol in the intestine, thereby reducing the amount of cholesterol entering the bloodstream.

Additionally, babchi's flavonoids and saponins have been shown to improve lipid profiles and enhance antioxidant activity, contributing to its cholesterol-lowering effects.

2. Reduces blood sugar levels

Psoralea corylifolia reduces blood sugar levels because of its lignan and coumarin compounds, which have been shown to inhibit alpha-glucosidase, an enzyme involved in carbohydrate digestion and glucose absorption.

By slowing down the digestion of carbohydrates, babchi delays the increase in blood sugar levels, making it a potential natural remedy for managing blood sugar levels in individuals with type 2 diabetes.

This action helps regulate blood sugar levels.

3. Helps regulate blood pressure

Psoralea corylifolia helps regulate blood pressure because it has a direct effect on the blood vessels, causing them to relax and dilate.

This reduces resistance and allows blood to flow more freely, thereby lowering blood pressure. Additionally, babchi's antioxidant properties help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, both of which contribute to high blood pressure.

Regular consumption of babchi may lead to improved cardiovascular health.

4. Combats hair loss

Psoralea corylifolia combats hair loss because it contains coumarins and alkaloids that stimulate hair growth, improve scalp health, and reduce oxidative stress.

The plant's active compounds also have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to soothe an itchy scalp and reduce dandruff.

By promoting a healthy scalp environment, babchi helps to prevent hair loss and stimulate new hair growth, making it a popular natural remedy for hair restoration.

5. Prevents premature aging

Psoralea corylifolia prevents premature aging because of its rich antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's lignans and coumarins help to neutralize free radicals, protecting skin cells from oxidative stress and damage. Additionally, babchi's bioactive compounds improve skin elasticity and firmness, reducing the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles, and promoting overall skin rejuvenation and renewal.

This results in a more youthful and radiant complexion.

6. Heals skin disorders

Psoralea corylifolia heals skin disorders because of its rich concentration of furanocoumarins and lignans.

These compounds have potent phototoxic and antioxidant properties that help to break down and remove damaged skin cells and scars. Regular use of babchi oil or extracts is believed to alleviate conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, and vitiligo, promoting skin regeneration and renewal.

Its natural anti-inflammatory and antibacterial effects further support skin healing and soothe irritation.

7. Relieves respiratory issues

Psoralea corylifolia relieves respiratory issues because it possesses anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.

The seeds of this plant contain a compound called isopsoralen, which helps to relax bronchial muscles and increase mucus secretion, making it easier to cough up phlegm and relieve congestion.

Additionally, the plant's antioxidant properties help to protect the lungs from oxidative stress and inflammation, promoting overall respiratory health and well-being.

8. Soothes digestive problems

Psoralea corylifolia soothes digestive problems because it has anti-inflammatory properties that reduce inflammation and irritation in the digestive tract.

Its lignans and coumarins have been shown to have a protective effect on the gut, promoting healthy digestion and absorption of nutrients. Additionally, babchi's demulcent properties create a protective barrier on the gut lining, shielding it from damage and discomfort.

This makes babchi a valuable natural remedy for digestive issues.

9. Reduces inflammation

Psoralea corylifolia reduces inflammation because of its unique combination of bioactive compounds, including psoralen and isopsoralen.

These compounds have been shown to inhibit pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, thereby reducing swelling and pain. Additionally, babchi's antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals that contribute to inflammation.

Its anti-inflammatory properties make it a popular ingredient in traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine for treating various inflammatory conditions.

10. Boosts immunity

Psoralea corylifolia boosts immunity because of its rich content of isopsoralen and sporalen, which activate vitamin D3 receptors.

This enhances the body's natural ability to fight infections. Additionally, babchi's anti-inflammatory properties help reduce oxidative stress, promoting a balanced immune response.

By stimulating the body's natural defense mechanisms, babchi supports overall health and resilience against diseases.
