Corylifolia Psoralea (Psoralea corylifolia) Uses

Corylifolia Psoralea is used to treat psoriasis, eczema, and skin infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps manage vitiligo and skin ulcers by promoting skin regeneration. The herb also addresses rheumatoid arthritis, osteoporosis, gout, and osteoarthritis through its ability to reduce inflammation and support bone health.

Corylifolia Psoralea (Psoralea corylifolia) Monograph

1. Psoriasis

Psoralea corylifolia is used in traditional medicine to treat psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. It helps reduce skin inflammation and scaling by regulating immune responses. The herb is often applied topically or taken internally as a supplement in herbal formulations. Its active compounds, such as psoralen, enhance its therapeutic effects on psoriatic lesions.

2. Eczema

Psoralea corylifolia is used in traditional medicine to treat eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and skin-soothing properties. The seeds contain compounds that help reduce skin irritation and promote healing. It is often prepared as a topical application or internal remedy under professional guidance.

3. Skin infections

Psoralea corylifolia is used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on the skin. The herb is often applied topically in the form of poultices or oils. It is particularly effective against fungal and bacterial skin conditions.

4. Vitiligo

Psoralea corylifolia is used in traditional medicine to treat vitiligo due to its skin-repairing and pigmentation properties. It helps restore skin color by stimulating melanocyte activity and reducing oxidative stress. Typically prepared as a decoction or topical application, it is valued for its ability to promote repigmentation in affected areas.

5. Skin ulcers

Psoralea corylifolia is used topically to treat skin ulcers due to its antimicrobial and wound-healing properties. The leaves and seeds contain compounds that reduce inflammation and promote tissue regeneration. It is often applied as a poultice or ointment to accelerate ulcer healing and prevent infection.

6. Fungal infections

Psoralea corylifolia has been used in traditional medicine to treat fungal infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds like psoralen and scopoletin, which exhibit antifungal activity. It is often applied topically to skin infections and may support systemic treatment when used in conjunction with other remedies.

7. Rheumatoid arthritis

Psoralea corylifolia is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of rheumatoid arthritis. Its active compounds, including psoralen and scopoletin, possess anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. These effects help reduce joint inflammation and pain, supporting overall joint health in patients with the condition.

8. Osteoporosis

Psoralea corylifolia, also known as bishop's candlestick, is used in traditional medicine to support bone health. Its active compounds, including psoralens and flavonoids, may enhance calcium absorption and reduce bone resorption. Studies suggest it can help prevent osteoporosis by promoting bone density and reducing inflammation. It is often used in combination with other herbs in herbal formulations for this purpose.

9. Gout

Psoralea corylifolia is used in traditional medicine to manage gout due to its anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties. It helps reduce uric acid levels and alleviate joint pain associated with gout. The herb is often prepared as a decoction or powder for internal use. Its efficacy is supported by historical use in Chinese herbal practices.

10. Osteoarthritis

Psoralea corylifolia is used in traditional medicine to alleviate osteoarthritis symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties help reduce joint pain and stiffness. The seeds are often processed into powders or decoctions for internal use. Clinical studies suggest it may support cartilage health and slow degenerative joint changes.
