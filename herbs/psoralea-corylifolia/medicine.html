What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Psoralea Corylifolia (Babchi)?

Psoralea corylifolia, also known as babchi, has health benefits such as skin lightening and antifungal properties.

Its medicinal constituents include isopimpinellin, psoralen, and bergapten, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. Medicinal preparations of babchi include decoctions, ointments, and creams, which are used to treat skin conditions like eczema and vitiligo. While babchi can be beneficial, it may cause allergic reactions, skin irritation, or increased sensitivity to sunlight.

Precautions include using it under medical supervision and in moderate amounts, especially in sensitive individuals or those with skin conditions.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Psoralea corylifolia.

What are the health benefits of Psoralea corylifolia?

Psoralea corylifolia has health benefits such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which help to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the body.

It is also used to treat skin conditions like eczema, acne, and psoriasis due to its antiseptic and antibacterial properties.

Additionally, it may help to improve digestion, reduce fever, and even have anti-cancer properties.

What are the active constituents of Psoralea corylifolia?

The health benefits of Psoralea corylifolia comes from its active constituents, such as psoralen, isopsoralen, and bergapten.

These flavonoids have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antifungal properties, which may contribute to the herb's traditional uses in treating skin conditions like leprosy and vitiligo.

Additionally, babchi's constituents have been found to exhibit antioxidant and cytotoxic activities, making it a potentially useful agent for combating various diseases.

What are the medicinal preparations of Psoralea corylifolia?

Psoralea corylifolia has different medicinal preparations, such as capsules, tablets, powders, and teas.

Capsules and tablets are used to treat skin diseases like leucoderma, vitiligo, and psoriasis, by promoting pigmentation and improving skin texture.

The powder is used topically to treat various skin conditions, including acne, eczema, and wounds, while the tea is consumed orally to alleviate digestive issues, such as diarrhea and dysentery, and to relieve symptoms of menstrual disorders like amenorrhea and dysmenorrhea.

What are the possible side effect of using Psoralea corylifolia improperly?

Improper use of Psoralea corylifolia increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea.

It can also lead to skin rashes, allergic reactions, and even liver damage in severe cases. Additionally, high doses may cause dizziness, fatigue, and headaches. In some individuals, it can interact with other medications, reducing their effectiveness or increasing the risk of adverse reactions.

Moreover, improper use may also cause irregular heartbeat, respiratory problems, and increased sensitivity to light.

What precautions to take when using Psoralea corylifolia medicinally?

Before using Psoralea corylifolia for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a qualified healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and treatment duration.

Additionally, it is essential to purchase high-quality products from reputable sources to ensure purity and authenticity.

Furthermore, pregnant or breastfeeding women, children under 12 years old, and individuals with kidney or liver issues should avoid using babchi altogether due to potential adverse effects.
