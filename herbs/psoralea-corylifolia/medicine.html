What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Psoralea Corylifolia (Babchi)?

Psoralea corylifolia, also known as babchi, has health benefits such as enhancing skin health and treating various skin conditions like eczema and acne.

The herb contains medicinal constituents like coumarins, flavonoids, and alkaloids, which are responsible for its therapeutic properties. Babchi is traditionally prepared into decoctions, infusions, and creams, which are applied topically to affected areas. However, long-term use may cause allergic reactions and photosensitivity.

Precautions should be taken to avoid overexposure to sunlight when using babchi preparations to avoid potential complications.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Psoralea corylifolia .

What are the health benefits of Psoralea corylifolia ?

Psoralea corylifolia, also known as babchi, has health benefits such as treating skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

It also helps to reduce stress, anxiety, and insomnia, as well as alleviate symptoms of menopause. Traditionally, it has been used to promote hair growth and treat fungal infections, with its properties also having antioxidant and anti-cancer effects.

Its uses have been validated in Ayurvedic medicine.

What are the active constituents of Psoralea corylifolia ?

Psoralea corylifolia, also known as babchi, has active constituents such as psoralen, isopsoralen, and bergapten, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Psoralen is a coumarin derivative that inhibits the synthesis of melanin and DNA, exhibiting antiphototoxic and antipigmentary effects. Bergapten has anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and antiviral properties, while isopsoralen has antioxidant and antipigmentary effects.

These constituents contribute to babchi's use in traditional medicine for skin conditions, such as vitiligo and leucoderma.

What are the medicinal preparations of Psoralea corylifolia ?

Psoralea corylifolia, also known as babchi, has medicinal preparations such as oil, powder, and decoction.

Babchi oil is used to treat skin diseases like leprosy, eczema, and psoriasis, while babchi powder is used to treat hair loss, premature graying, and skin conditions. The decoction of babchi seeds is used to treat conditions like anemia and gout.

The plant's roots and seeds are also used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various health issues.

What are the possible side effect of using Psoralea corylifolia improperly?

Improper use of Psoralea corylifolia, also known as babchi, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as skin irritation, rashes, and itching.

Internal consumption can lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. High doses may cause kidney damage, liver toxicity, and anemia. Additionally, allergic reactions like hives and swelling can occur.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using babchi to prevent such adverse effects and ensure safe usage.

What precautions to take when using Psoralea corylifolia medicinally?

Before using Psoralea corylifolia, also known as babchi, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional and carefully monitoring your skin for any adverse reactions.

Babchi oil can cause skin irritation, photosensitivity, and allergic reactions in some individuals. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using it altogether, as its effects on the fetus or baby are unknown.

Use it in moderation and under proper guidance to minimize risks.
