The therapeutic actions of Melaleuca glabra include its ability to act as an antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial agent, which can help to soothe and protect the skin.

Possible Side Effects of Melaleuca glabra

Melaleuca glabra can have side effects if used improperly, such as skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal issues.

If ingested, it can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea due to its high tannin content.

Prolonged skin exposure to the plant's essential oil may also lead to phototoxicity, causing redness, itching, and blistering in sunlight-exposed areas.

Additionally, improper use of melaleuca glabra can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and exacerbate conditions like high blood pressure and kidney disease.
