Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum) Compounds

Salvia divinorum contains several key compounds, including alkaloids, terpenoids, and flavonoids. These compounds contribute to its pharmacological properties and are responsible for its psychoactive effects. Essential oils and glycosides also play a role in its traditional use. Other components like phenolic acids and coumarin add to its complex chemical profile.

Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Salvia divinorum contains the alkaloid tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), which is responsible for its psychoactive effects. This compound interacts with the brain's cannabinoid receptors, influencing mood, perception, and consciousness. While THC is the primary active compound, it is important to note that Salvia divinorum also contains other alkaloids with varying effects.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of organic compounds found in Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum). They contribute to the plant's unique properties and are believed to be responsible for its psychoactive and medicinal effects. These compounds play a key role in the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of compounds found in Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum). These bioactive molecules contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. They may support the plant's traditional use in healing and spiritual practices. Flavonoids are part of the complex phytochemical profile of Salvia divinorum.

4. Essential oil

The essential oil of Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum) contains bioactive compounds like tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) and other terpenoids. These compounds contribute to its psychoactive and potential therapeutic properties, though research on their medical applications is ongoing.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum). It contributes to the plant's psychoactive and potential therapeutic properties. Glycosides in Salvia divinorum may interact with the body's opioid receptors, influencing mood and perception. Their structure and function are central to understanding the plant's medicinal and ethnobotanical significance.

6. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a compound found in Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support its traditional use in herbal medicine. This compound is part of the broader group of bioactive substances in Salvia divinorum that are studied for their potential therapeutic effects.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum). It contributes to the plant's psychoactive and therapeutic effects, influencing mood, perception, and physical sensations. Coumarin's presence highlights the complex pharmacological profile of this traditional herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though its specific medicinal effects are less well-documented compared to other compounds like tetrahydrocannabinol. Saponins are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

9. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins also help preserve the plant's active compounds and can influence its traditional uses in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are pigments found in Mexican Sage (Salvia divinorum) that contribute to its vibrant color. While not the primary psychoactive component, they may support overall plant health and have antioxidant properties. Their role in the plant's medicinal use is still being studied.
