Albizia julibrissin

What's the taxonomical classification of Albizia julibrissin?

Albizia juliibrissin belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Albizia and is known by its species name juliibrissin.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Albizia julibrissin contains various bioactive alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These chemical constituents are responsible for the plant's diverse pharmacological activities, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Specific alkaloids within the species have been studied for their potential neuroprotective and sedative effects. Additionally, the presence of polyphenolic compounds contributes to the mitigation of oxidative stress in biological systems. Researchers often investigate these phytochemicals to develop new treatments for metabolic and inflammatory disorders.

What are the therapeutic actions of Albizia julibrissin?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, respiratory infections, inflammation, and various types of pain. The bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that help alleviate symptoms of chronic inflammation in the body. It is frequently employed in traditional medicine to soothe skin irritations and promote healing for external wounds. Additionally, its properties are utilized to calm coughs and manage discomfort associated with respiratory distress. These medicinal applications rely on the plant's natural ability to reduce swelling and fight microbial activity.
