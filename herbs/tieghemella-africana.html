Tieghemella africana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tieghemella africana has a woody, perennial shrub structure characterized by opposite, simple leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape with entire margins. The leaves possess a distinctively glossy texture and acute apices, often appearing dark green on the upper surface. Its inflorescences are typically small and arranged in axillary clusters, bearing inconspicuous flowers. The plant develops small, fleshy fruits that serve as a key identifier for the species. These morphological features are essential for distinguishing it from other members of the Rubiaceae family.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of West and Central Africa, spanning from Guinea and Sierra Leone through to Cameroon and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It is primarily found within the Guineo-Congolian regional center of endemism where humidity and rainfall are consistently high. The species inhabits the understory of dense, evergreen lowland forests that provide the necessary shade and moisture for its growth. Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of undisturbed primary forest ecosystems. Some individuals may experience nausea or stomach cramping shortly after ingestion. Furthermore, the chemical constituents of the plant may trigger hypersensitivity reactions in people with specific allergies. Due to these uncertainties, careful monitoring is necessary when using this species for any therapeutic application.
