Ixeriom gracile belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ixeriidum gracile has slender, erect stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and bear small, opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins.

The overall structure is lightweight and airy, allowing it to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Ixeridium gracile can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage often results in redness, itching, or swelling at the site of touch. Some individuals may experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions, including hives or respiratory discomfort upon exposure. If consumed accidentally, the plant may trigger nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. There is currently limited clinical data regarding long-term systemic toxicity in humans.
