Ixeridium gracile

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ixeriidum gracile has slender, erect stems that reach heights of up to 60 centimeters and bear small, opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically bright green and possesses a delicate, thin texture characteristic of the species. Tiny, inconspicuous white flowers emerge in axillary clusters from the upper nodes of the plant. These blooms are followed by small, dry fruits that facilitate the plant's reproduction. The overall structure is lightweight and airy, allowing it to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Ixeridium gracile can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage often results in redness, itching, or swelling at the site of touch. Some individuals may experience more severe hypersensitivity reactions, including hives or respiratory discomfort upon exposure. If consumed accidentally, the plant may trigger nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. There is currently limited clinical data regarding long-term systemic toxicity in humans.
