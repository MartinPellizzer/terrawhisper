What is Pseudoclausia tschimganica?

Pseudoclausia tschimganica is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It has a slender stem, 20-50 cm high, with glabrous or pubescent, alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves, 2-5 cm long, and 0.5-2 cm wide, and a racemose inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellowish or white, 4-petaled flowers, 6-10 mm in diameter, with 6 stamens, and a globose or ovoid siliqua, 3-6 cm long, and 2-4 cm wide. The siliqua splits into two valves, releasing seeds 2-3 mm long.

Pseudoclausia tschimganica is native to the Central Asian region.
