Erythroxylum novogranatense

What's the taxonomical classification of Erythroxylum novogranatense?

Erythroxylum novogranatense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Erythroxylaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Erythroxylum and the species epithet novogranatense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Erythroxylum novogranatense has woody stems, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary clusters.

The leaves are typically characterized by a prominent midrib and acute or obtuse apices. Its branching pattern forms a dense shrub or small tree structure depending on environmental conditions. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that transitions in color as it reaches maturity.

These morphological features distinguish the species from other members of the Erythroxylaceae family within its native habitat.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Erythroxylum novogranatense targets the dopamine transporter (DAT), the serotonin transporter (SERT), and the norepinephrine transporter (NET) to induce its physiological effects. These alkaloids act as competitive inhibitors at the binding sites of these monoamine transporters, effectively increasing the concentration of neurotransmitters in the synaptic cleft. By modulating these protein channels, the plant's secondary metabolites influence central nervous system signaling and reward pathways. The specific interaction with these transmembrane proteins results in the observed stimulant and analgesic properties of the species. Such molecular binding mechanisms are characteristic of the tropane alkaloid class found within the Erythroxylaceae family.

What are the therapeutic actions of Erythroxylum novogranatense?

Erythroxylum novogranatense has therapeutic properties that function as a bitter stomachic and a tonic to improve overall digestive health.

The plant contains chemical constituents that trigger the secretion of gastric juices to aid in nutrient breakdown. This action serves as a potent digestive stimulant for individuals experiencing sluggish metabolism or low appetite. Regular use of its extracts can help tone the gastrointestinal tract and support steady metabolic function.

These combined effects make it a useful traditional remedy for promoting efficient digestion.

    What are the herbal preparations of Erythroxylum novogranatense?

    Erythroxylum novogranatense is prepared as a decoction by boiling the dried leaves in water or as a concentrated infusion to extract its medicinal compounds. The plant material is often steeped in hot liquids to facilitate the release of alkaloids and other bioactive constituents. In some traditional practices, the powdered leaves may be incorporated into topical pastes for external applications. Some preparations involve macerating the plant parts in alcohol to create tinctures for more stable long-term use. These various forms allow for different methods of administration depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Erythroxylum novogranatense can cause physiological side effects including increased heart rate, elevated blood pressure, anxiety, and potential neurological disturbances. These symptoms often stem from the stimulant properties inherent in its alkaloids. Users may also experience digestive issues such as nausea or loss of appetite following consumption. Prolonged or excessive use might lead to more severe complications like insomnia or cardiac palpitations. Monitoring the intensity of these reactions is essential for anyone interacting with the plant.
