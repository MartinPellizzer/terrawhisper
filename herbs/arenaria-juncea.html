Arenaria juncea

What's the taxonomical classification of Arenaria juncea?

Arenaria juncea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is part of the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae, specifically situated within the genus Arenaria, and is identified by the species epithet juncea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arenaria juncea has slender, cylindrical, unbranched stems that reach heights of 5 to 15 centimeters and are densely covered in small, linear leaves.

These leaves are typically bright green, sessile, and arranged in a tightly packed, somewhat whorled fashion along the stem. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during its blooming period. Its root system is characterized by a delicate, fibrous structure suited for rocky or sandy substrates.

The overall growth habit is compact and tufted, giving the plant a wiry appearance.
