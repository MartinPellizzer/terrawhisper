Sanicula rugulosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sanicula rugulosa has opposite, petiolate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and feature deeply serrated margins with a distinctively rugose, wrinkled upper surface. The leaf blades often possess three to five prominent veins originating from the base, which contribute to the textured appearance of the lamina. This perennial herb produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, head-like clusters atop slender, upright stems. The resulting fruits are small, nut-like drupes that are covered in hooked bristles to facilitate dispersal by clinging to passing animals. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to thrive in shaded, woodland environments.
