What's the taxonomical classification of Smithia sensitiva?

Smithia sensitiva belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Smithia, and its species designation, sensitiva.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smithia sensitiva has prostrate, creeping stems that feature small, trifoliate leaves and bright yellow pea-like flowers. The leaves consist of three leaflets that are capable of folding inward when touched, exhibiting a sensitive response to physical stimuli. These stems grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats across the soil surface. The floral structure is typical of the Fabaceae family, characterized by a distinct keel, wings, and a standard petal. Small, cylindrical legume pods develop from the flowers after successful pollination.
