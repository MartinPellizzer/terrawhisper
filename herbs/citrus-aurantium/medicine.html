What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Citrus Aurantium (Bitter Orange)?

Citrus aurantium, also known as bitter orange, has health benefits such as reducing anxiety and improving digestion.

It contains bioflavonoids, particularly naringenin, hesperidin, and synephrine, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. Various preparations, including capsules, teas, and essential oils, are available for medicinal use. However, potential side effects include increased heart rate and blood pressure.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Citrus aurantium, especially for individuals with cardiovascular conditions or high blood pressure.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Citrus aurantium .

What are the health benefits of Citrus aurantium ?

Citrus aurantium, also known as bitter orange, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health due to its high content of flavonoids and limonoids.

It may also help lower cholesterol levels and reduce the risk of heart disease. Additionally, its antioxidant properties can aid in protecting against cell damage and oxidative stress, potentially supporting overall well-being.

Research is ongoing to fully explore its medicinal potential.

What are the active constituents of Citrus aurantium ?

Citrus aurantium, also known as bitter orange, has active constituents such as limonene, aurantiamide acetate, and naringenin, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Limonene and aurantiamide acetate have been found to possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, respectively. Naringenin, a flavonoid compound, exhibits antioxidant and anti-platelet aggregation activities.

These compounds may help to explain the traditional uses of bitter orange in folk medicine for various health conditions, including digestive issues and fever reduction.

What are the medicinal preparations of Citrus aurantium ?

Citrus aurantium, also known as bitter orange, has medicinal preparations such as essential oils, extracts, and bioflavonoids that are used to treat various health conditions.

The peel of the fruit is rich in bergamottin and synephrine, compounds that have been shown to have anti-inflammatory and anti-anxiety properties.

These compounds are often used to alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression, and to promote relaxation.

What are the possible side effect of using Citrus aurantium improperly?

Improper use of Citrus aurantium, also known as bitter orange, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as hypertension, cardiac arrhythmias, and stroke due to its stimulant properties.

High doses can also cause nausea, dizziness, and headaches.

Additionally, bitter orange can interact with other medications, such as MAOIs, and exacerbate underlying medical conditions like high blood pressure and cardiovascular disease.

What precautions to take when using Citrus aurantium medicinally?

Before using Citrus aurantium, also known as bitter orange, for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting a healthcare professional, as it can interact with certain medications and exacerbate health conditions.

Additionally, be cautious when consuming high concentrations, as it may cause adverse effects like anxiety, insomnia, and increased heart rate.

Proper dosing and individual tolerance should also be monitored.
