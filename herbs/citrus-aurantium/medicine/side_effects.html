1. Raises blood pressure suddenly

Citrus aurantium raises blood pressure suddenly due to its high levels of synephrine and octopamine.

These natural compounds mimic the effects of adrenaline, causing blood vessels to constrict and heart rate to increase.

This can lead to a sudden surge in blood pressure, potentially posing risks for individuals with pre-existing hypertension or cardiovascular conditions.

2. Causes insomnia in some people

Citrus aurantium causes insomnia in some people due to its high concentration of synephrine and other alkaloids.

These compounds can stimulate the central nervous system, leading to increased alertness and energy. However, this stimulation can also disrupt sleep patterns and make it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep.

As a result, users may experience insomnia, making it essential to be aware of these potential side effects when using Citrus aurantium supplements.

3. Stimulates heart rate excessively

Citrus aurantium stimulates heart rate excessively.

This is due to its primary active compound, synephrine, which is a natural sympathomimetic amine that mimics the effects of epinephrine in the body.

As a result, it can increase heart rate and blood pressure, leading to an excessive stimulation of the cardiovascular system.

4. Produces rapid heartbeat noticeably

Citrus aurantium produces rapid heartbeat noticeably due to its high concentration of synephrine, a compound similar to ephedrine.

This stimulant-like effect is caused by the increased release of norepinephrine in the brain and body, leading to an enhancement of sympathetic nervous system activity.

As a result, the heart beats faster and more forcefully, causing palpitations or rapid heartbeat.

5. Increases anxiety levels rapidly

Citrus aurantium increases anxiety levels rapidly due to its high concentration of synephrine, a compound similar to ephedrine.

This stimulant can cause an intense rush of adrenaline in the body, leading to feelings of jitteriness and heightened alertness, which may manifest as increased anxiety.

Additionally, Citrus aurantium's ability to stimulate the sympathetic nervous system can further exacerbate anxious symptoms, making it essential for individuals to be aware of this potential side effect when considering its use.

6. Triggers severe headaches occasionally

Citrus aurantium triggers severe headaches occasionally.

This is due to the presence of synephrine, a naturally occurring compound that can stimulate the central nervous system and cause blood vessel constriction, leading to increased pressure on the brain.

Additionally, Citrus aurantium's ability to enhance the effects of norepinephrine, a neurotransmitter involved in pain processing, may also contribute to the development of headaches.

7. Enhances adrenaline release excessively

Citrus aurantium enhances adrenaline release excessively due to its high concentration of synephrine, a compound similar to ephedrine.

Synephrine stimulates the body's natural fight-or-flight response, causing an excessive increase in adrenaline levels.

This can lead to symptoms such as jitteriness, anxiety, insomnia, and increased heart rate and blood pressure.

8. Lowers blood sugar dramatically

Citrus aurantium lowers blood sugar dramatically.

This side effect is primarily attributed to the high concentration of synephrine, a chemical that stimulates the body's sympathetic nervous system, mimicking the effects of epinephrine. Synephrine increases glucose uptake in muscles and liver cells, leading to rapid glucose consumption and decreased blood sugar levels.

Additionally, Citrus aurantium may also enhance insulin sensitivity, further contributing to its hypoglycemic effects.

9. Causes stomach upset frequently

Citrus aurantium causes stomach upset frequently due to its high concentration of flavonoids and limonoids.

These compounds can irritate the digestive tract, leading to symptoms such as nausea, bloating, and abdominal pain.

Additionally, Citrus aurantium contains synephrine, a sympathomimetic amine that can stimulate the release of stomach acid, further exacerbating stomach upset in some individuals.

10. Induces jitteriness immediately

Citrus aurantium induces jitteriness immediately.

This side effect is attributed to its high concentration of synephrine, a compound similar to ephedrine and epinephrine.

Synephrine stimulates the central nervous system, causing an increase in heart rate and blood pressure, leading to feelings of restlessness, anxiety, and jitteriness.
