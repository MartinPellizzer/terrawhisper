Fragaria orientalis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fragaria orientalis has a perennial growth habit characterized by stolons that produce small, trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and shallowly lobed leaflets. The foliage typically features a bright green color and a slightly hairy texture on the undersides. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These blossoms transition into aggregate fruits that serve as the plant's fleshy red berries. The root system is relatively shallow and spreads via the aforementioned runners to colonize surrounding soil.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

What are the therapeutic actions of Fragaria orientalis?

Fragaria orientalis has astringent, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that are primarily utilized to treat gastrointestinal issues and skin irritations. The plant contains high concentrations of tannins which help to shrink swollen tissues and reduce diarrhea by constricting mucous membranes. Its rich supply of vitamin C and various polyphenols works to neutralize free radicals and support the immune system. When applied topically, the extracts can soothe minor abrasions and reduce redness through their calming effect on the skin. These therapeutic actions make it a valuable component in traditional herbal remedies for digestive and dermatological health.

      What are the herbal preparations of Fragaria orientalis?

      Fragaria orientalis is prepared as a decoction of the dried roots, a tea from the leaves, or a topical tincture applied to the skin. The roots are often simmered for an extended period to extract their medicinal compounds for internal use. Leaf preparations are frequently consumed as mild infusions to support digestive health. Additionally, the plant can be processed into a concentrated tincture for more potent dosing. These various forms allow for flexible administration depending on whether the desired effect is systemic or localized.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Fragaria orientalis can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, localized swelling, and itching upon direct contact with its leaves or stems. Some individuals may experience gastrointestinal distress, including nausea or stomach cramps, if the plant parts are accidentally ingested. While specific toxicity levels are not extensively documented, skin sensitivity can escalate into redness or hives in hypersensitive people. It is important to exercise caution when handling the plant to avoid these dermatological issues. Always monitor for any unexpected physiological responses after exposure.
