Fragaria orientalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Fragaria orientalis?

Fragaria orientalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and belongs to the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Fragaria, and its specific species name, orientalis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fragaria orientalis has a perennial growth habit characterized by stolons that produce small, trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and shallowly lobed leaflets. The foliage typically features a bright green color and a slightly hairy texture on the undersides. Small, white, five-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These blossoms transition into aggregate fruits that serve as the plant's fleshy red berries. The root system is relatively shallow and spreads via the aforementioned runners to colonize surrounding soil.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-drained, acidic soil with a pH between 5.5 and 6.5 and consistent moisture to thrive.

Growers should plant it in a location that receives full sun to ensure maximum berry production and sweetness. Mulching with organic matter helps maintain even soil temperature and suppresses competing weeds around the runners. Regular irrigation is necessary to prevent fruit cracking and to support the vigorous growth of new stolons.
