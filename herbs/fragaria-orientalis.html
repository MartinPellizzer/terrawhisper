Fragaria orientalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Fragaria orientalis?

Fragaria orientalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and belongs to the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Fragaria, and its specific species name, orientalis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Fragaria orientalis contains high concentrations of ellagitannins, ascorbic acid, quercetin, and various phenolic acids that provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These bioactive compounds work together to neutralize free radicals and mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. The presence of anthocyanins contributes to the plant's ability to support cardiovascular health by improving vascular function. Additionally, the specific profile of organic acids in the fruit may assist in regulating metabolic processes. Such chemical constituents make the plant a subject of interest for developing natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Fragaria orientalis?

Fragaria orientalis has astringent, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties that are primarily utilized to treat digestive issues and skin irritations. The plant contains high concentrations of tannins which help to constrict body tissues and soothe inflamed mucous membranes. These chemical compounds work effectively to reduce diarrhea and stabilize gastrointestinal distress. Additionally, the extract can be applied topically to accelerate the healing of minor wounds and skin abrasions. Its rich profile of polyphenols also provides systemic protection against oxidative stress.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments, inflammatory skin conditions, and various urinary tract infections. Its chemical properties allow it to act as an astringent that can soothe irritated mucosal membranes. Practitioners often apply its extracts to reduce swelling and redness on the surface of the skin. Additionally, the plant's compounds may help manage symptoms related to bladder discomfort. These therapeutic applications rely on the specific bioactive constituents found within its leaves and roots.
