Aesculus Hippocastanum

It has been used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including the reduction of swelling and inflammation in the legs and veins, relief from varicose veins and hemorrhoids, and the improvement of cardiovascular health.

The therapeutic actions of horse chestnut herb are attributed to its ability to inhibit the breakdown of connective tissue, improve blood circulation, and exhibit anti-inflammatory properties.

The bioactive constituents of horse chestnut herb include aescin, a saponin glycoside that is responsible for its therapeutic effects, as well as flavonoids and other polyphenolic compounds.
