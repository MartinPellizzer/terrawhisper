What to know about Aesculus hippocastanum before using it medicinally

Aesculus hippocastanum, commonly known as horse chestnut, is a plant with a rich history of traditional medicine, primarily used to treat conditions such as varicose veins, hemorrhoids, and swelling associated with injuries and surgery.

The main health benefits of this herb include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and vascular protective properties, which contribute to its therapeutic efficacy. The therapeutic properties of Aesculus hippocastanum are attributed to its ability to inhibit the activity of pro-inflammatory enzymes, improve blood vessel function, and enhance the integrity of the venous system. The main bioactive compounds responsible for these effects are flavonoids, particularly aesculin, and triterpenoid saponins, which have been demonstrated to possess potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities.

A popular herbal preparation made from Aesculus hippocastanum is horse chestnut extract, often used in the form of capsules, tablets, or topical creams to support venous health and alleviate symptoms of varicose veins and related conditions.
