Aesculus Hippocastanum: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Aesculus hippocastanum, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan region, known for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

This herb is derived from the seeds and bark of the horse chestnut tree, containing various bioactive constituents such as saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids.

It is valued for its health benefits, including improving circulation and reducing inflammation, and is often used to alleviate symptoms of varicose veins and hemorrhoids.

Therapeutically, it acts as a venotonic, promoting the health of blood vessels and enhancing blood flow.
