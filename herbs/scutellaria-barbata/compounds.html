Scutellaria Barbata (Scutellaria barbata) Compounds

Scutellaria barbata contains several primary compounds, including alkaloids, flavonoids, and glycosides. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Terpenoids and phenolic acids also play a role in its therapeutic applications.

Scutellaria Barbata (Scutellaria barbata) Monograph

1. Alkaloid

Scutellaria barbata contains the alkaloid baicalin, which is known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating fever and respiratory conditions. Baicalin is also being studied for its potential in modern medicine, including neuroprotective effects.

2. Flavonoid

Scutellaria barbata contains the flavonoid wogonin, known for its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating infections and inflammatory conditions. Wogonin also shows promise in cancer research due to its ability to inhibit tumor growth.

3. Glycoside

Scutellaria barbata contains the glycoside scutellarein, which exhibits anti-inflammatory and antitumor properties. This compound is known for its ability to inhibit certain enzymes involved in inflammation and cancer progression. It is a key active constituent contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

4. Terpenoid

Scutellaria barbata contains the terpenoid wogonin, which exhibits anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating infections and digestive disorders. Wogonin is also being studied for its potential in cancer therapy. Its presence highlights the plant's therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Scutellaria barbata contains phenolic acid, which contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps in reducing oxidative stress and supporting immune function, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Scutellaria barbata, known for its astringent properties and ability to reduce inflammation. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. Tannins also help preserve the plant's medicinal properties during preparation.

7. Saponin

Scutellaria barbata contains saponins, which are known for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Saponins also support immune function and may help in managing conditions like inflammation and infections.

8. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Scutellaria barbata. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Coumarin also supports the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory ailments.

9. Essential oil

Scutellaria barbata contains essential oil as one of its active compounds. This oil is extracted from the plant's aerial parts and is known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for treating infections and skin conditions.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Scutellaria barbata, known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Its presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine for its therapeutic benefits.
