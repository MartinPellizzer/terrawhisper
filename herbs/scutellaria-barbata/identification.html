Botanical Identification of Scutellaria Barbata (Scutellaria barbata)

To identify Scutellaria Barbata (Scutellaria barbata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Scutellaria barbata belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Scutellaria, and species Scutellaria barbata. It is a member of the mint family, known for its medicinal properties and distinctive appearance.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Scutellaria barbata is the scientific name of the plant, first described by the botanist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck in 1785. It is also known by several synonyms, including Scutellaria barbata subsp. barbata and Scutellaria barbata var. barbata. Common names include purple skullcap and bearded skullcap, while regional names vary across different areas. Historically, it has been referred to by various names in traditional herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Scutellaria barbata is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system. It has erect, branched stems that are hairless. The leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and hairy. The inflorescence is a spike of flowers. Each flower has two lips, with the upper lip being shorter. The fruit is a capsule containing small, black seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Scutellaria barbata can be distinguished by its distinctive barbed calyx lobes, which are longer and more pronounced than in similar species. The leaves are opposite and ovate, with a serrated margin. The flowers are blue to purple, and the plant has a square stem with hairy nodes. These features help differentiate it from closely related Scutellaria species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Scutellaria barbata is native to the Mediterranean region, including southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It thrives in temperate climates with mild summers and cool winters. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Scutellaria barbata blooms from May to July, producing blue to purple flowers. Seed formation occurs from August to September. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in late summer to early autumn when the seeds are mature and the leaves are still identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Scutellaria barbata is most closely related to species within the Scutellaria genus, particularly those sharing similar morphological and genetic traits. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems, opposite leaves, and aromatic properties. Its phylogenetic position reflects evolutionary relationships with other members of the genus and the broader Lamiaceae family.
