Rosa longicuspis

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa longicuspis?

Rosa longicuspis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Rosales and into the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Rosa, where it is specifically identified by the species name longicuspis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa longicuspis has long, slender, arching stems that are densely covered in sharp, recurved prickles. The foliage consists of odd-pinnate leaves with several pairs of ovate leaflets that possess serrated margins. Each flower typically displays five delicate petals in shades of pale pink or white surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens. These blossoms often emerge in small clusters or singly from the leaf axils. The plant's climbing habit is supported by its rigid, thorny branches which allow it to scramble over other vegetation.

The species is typically found growing in thickets or along the edges of forests within these temperate zones.
