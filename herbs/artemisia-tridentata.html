Big Sagebrush (Artemisia tridentata)

Big Sagebrush (Artemisia tridentata) is a member of the Asteraceae family, native to North America, United States, and Western United States. Traditionally, its leaves, resin, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.
