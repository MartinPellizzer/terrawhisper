Quercus aquifolioides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Quercus aquifolioides has elliptical to ovate leaves with serrated margins, a woody trunk, and small, acorn-shaped fruits. The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture and a dark green color on the upper surface. These leaves often feature small teeth along the edges that distinguish them from other oak species. The bark is generally rugged and develops deep fissures as the tree matures. Small clusters of inconspicuous flowers appear during the spring growing season.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of southern China, specifically found in provinces such as Guangdong, Guangxi, Hainan, and Fujian. It typically thrives in low-altitude environments ranging from coastal areas to hilly terrains. These oaks are often located within evergreen broad-leaved forests where humidity and temperature remain relatively stable. The species prefers well-drained soils and sheltered landscapes common to its southeastern distribution. Consequently, its range is strictly limited to these specific East Asian subtropical climatic zones.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

The tannins present in the plant extract are known for their astringent qualities, making them useful for treating skin inflammations and minor wounds.
