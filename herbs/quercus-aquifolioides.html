What's the taxonomical classification of Quercus aquifolioides?

Quercus aquifolioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Fagales and is a member of the family Fagaceae. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its specific classification within the genus Quercus, with its unique biological identity established at the species level as aquifolioides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Quercus aquifolioides has elliptical to ovate leaves with serrated margins, a woody trunk, and small, acorn-shaped fruits. The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture and a dark green color on the upper surface. These leaves often feature small teeth along the edges that distinguish them from other oak species. The bark is generally rugged and develops deep fissures as the tree matures. Small clusters of inconspicuous flowers appear during the spring growing season.
