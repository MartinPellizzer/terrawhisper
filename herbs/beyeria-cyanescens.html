What is Beyeria cyanescens?

Beyeria cyanescens is a species of shrub in the family Euphorbiaceae, native to Western Australia.

It grows up to 1 metre tall, with a spread of 1 metre, and has a woody stem with a greyish-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, measuring 3-8 cm in length, with a pale green upper surface and a pale yellow-green lower surface. The inflorescence is a cyathium, consisting of a cup-shaped structure with 2-4 yellow-green flowers, surrounded by 3-4 green bracts.

The fruit is a 3-angled capsule, 3-6 mm in diameter, containing 3-4 seeds.
