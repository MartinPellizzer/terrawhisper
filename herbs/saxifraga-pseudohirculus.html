Saxifraga pseudohirculus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga pseudohirculus has a low-growing, rosette-forming habit characterized by thick, fleshy leaves that are often rounded or spatulate in shape and possess slightly serrated or crenate margins. The leaves are typically densely covered in fine hairs, which helps the plant retain moisture in its rocky alpine environment. From the center of this basal rosette, slender, upright flowering stalks emerge to bear small, star-shaped blossoms. These flowers usually feature five distinct petals that range in color from white to pale pink. The overall structure is compact and adapted for survival in harsh, high-altitude crevices.
