Polygonatum zanlanscianense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonatum zanlanscianense has rhizomes that are thick, fleshy, and somewhat compressed with a white or pale yellowish surface. The leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, featuring an elliptical to lanceolate shape with entire margins and acute tips. Each leaf typically possesses parallel venation and a smooth, somewhat waxy texture. The inflorescence consists of small, pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale green in color. These delicate blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and eventually develop into small, fleshy berries.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and western China, specifically occurring in provinces such as Gansu, Hubei, Shaanxi, Sichuan, and Yunnan. It typically inhabits moist, shaded environments found within temperate deciduous or coniferous forests. These plants often grow in well-drained soil at mid-to-high elevations along mountain slopes. The species occupies specific ecological niches where humidity and light levels remain relatively consistent throughout the growing season. Its range is largely defined by the rugged topography and climatic variations of the Chinese mainland.
