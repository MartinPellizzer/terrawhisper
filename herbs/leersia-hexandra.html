Leersia hexandra

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leersia hexandra has woody branches with simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical or oblong in shape and possess entire margins. The leaves are typically coriaceous in texture and feature a prominent midrib with inconspicuous secondary veins. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, characterized specifically by having six stamens. These floral structures eventually develop into small, fleshy fruits that contain distinct seeds. The overall vegetative habit reflects a tree-like growth pattern typical of the family Phyllanthaceae.
