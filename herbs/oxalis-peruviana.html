Oxalis peruviana

Oxalis peruviana, also known as wood sorrel or purple wood sorrel, is a perennial plant in the wood sorrel family that is native to South America.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, including its ability to act as a diuretic and to treat urinary tract infections.

The health benefits of oxalis peruviana include its potential to reduce inflammation and to have antioxidant properties.

In herbal preparations, the plant's leaves can be dried and used to make teas or infusions, and its roots can be used to make tinctures and other extracts.
