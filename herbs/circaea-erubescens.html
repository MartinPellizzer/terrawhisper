Circaea erubescens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Circaea erubescens has an upright, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, ovate-lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by prominent venation and a slightly hairy texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Small, white, four-petaled flowers emerge in terminal, many-flowered racemes that often appear reddish or purplish before fully opening. Following pollination, the plant produces distinctive two-seeded fruits equipped with long, hooked bristles designed for dispersal by animals. These specialized structures ensure the seeds can easily cling to passing fur or clothing.
