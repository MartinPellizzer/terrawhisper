What's the taxonomical classification of Circaea erubescens?

Circaea erubescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Myrtyleales and into the family Onagraceae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Circaea erubescens has an upright, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, ovate-lanceolate leaves with serrated margins.

The foliage is characterized by prominent venation and a slightly hairy texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. Small, white, four-petaled flowers emerge in terminal, many-flowered racemes that often appear reddish or purplish before fully opening. Following pollination, the plant produces distinctive two-seeded fruits equipped with long, hooked bristles designed for dispersal by animals.

These specialized structures ensure the seeds can easily cling to passing fur or clothing.

        What are the herbal preparations of Circaea erubescens?

        Circaea erubescens is prepared as an infusion, decoction, or tincture using the aerial parts of the plant such as the leaves and stems.
