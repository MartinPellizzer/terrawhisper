Callerya championii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callerya championii has large, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, creamy-white flowers with distinctively large, spreading labella.

Each flower features a prominent, fleshy callus structure at the base of the lip which is characteristic of the genus. The plant produces long, arching pseudobulbs that serve as the primary storage organs for water and nutrients. Its leaves are coriaceous, lanceolate, and grow in pairs at the apex of each pseudobulb.

The overall growth habit is sympodial, allowing the plant to spread through a creeping rhizome system.
