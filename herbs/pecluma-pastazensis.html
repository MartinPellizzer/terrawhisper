What is Pecluma pastazensis?

Pecluma pastazensis is a species of epiphytic fern in the family Polypodiaceae.

It has a rhizome with dark brown, hairy scales, and sterile fronds that are pinnatifid, lanceolate, and 2-4 times pinnate. The fertile fronds are pinnate to bipinnate, bearing sori under the leaves. The sori are linear and bear 64-128 spores per sorus.

The species is found in Ecuador and Peru, in humid, tropical forests at elevations up to 2,000 meters.
