Potentilla betonicifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla betonicifolia?

Potentiella betonicifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression through the order Rosales, it is placed in the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Potentiella, with its specific designation being the species betonicifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella betonicifolia has palmately lobed leaves that are typically divided into five to seven distinct, serrated leaflets with a hairy underside.

The stems are woody at the base and often spread horizontally along the ground to form low mats. Small, yellow, five-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils on short stalks. These blossoms feature a central cluster of numerous stamens and pistils surrounded by a ring of petals.

The plant overall exhibits a compact, herbaceous growth habit adapted to rocky or mountainous terrain.
