Potentilla betonicifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella betonicifolia has palmately lobed leaves that are typically divided into five to seven distinct, serrated leaflets with a hairy underside. The stems are woody at the base and often spread horizontally along the ground to form low mats. Small, yellow, five-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils on short stalks. These blossoms feature a central cluster of numerous stamens and pistils surrounded by a ring of petals. The plant overall exhibits a compact, herbaceous growth habit adapted to rocky or mountainous terrain.
