Ocimum gratissimum

What's the taxonomical classification of Ocimum gratissimum?

Ocimum gratissimum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Ocimum and is specifically designated as the species gratisimum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ocimum gratissimum has an erect, perennial woody subshrub structure characterized by quadrangular stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage typically presents a bright green color and a slightly rugose texture on the leaf surface. Its inflorescence consists of terminal racemes containing small, tubular flowers that can vary in color from white to pale purple. These flowers emerge from clusters of bracts and eventually develop into small, nutlet-like seeds. The entire plant possesses a distinct aromatic quality due to the presence of essential oil glands embedded within the leaf tissue.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and parts of the Pacific Islands. It thrives in warm climates where humidity is relatively high and temperatures remain consistent throughout the year. Its distribution spans from the vast landscapes of sub-Saharan Africa to the various archipelagoes in Southeast Asia. Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, humans have introduced it to many other tropical zones across the globe. It can often be found growing in disturbed soils or along roadsides in these diverse geographical areas.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Ocimum gratissimum contains bioactive compounds primarily concentrated in its leaves, seeds, and essential oils extracted from the entire aerial portion of the plant. The leaves are the most frequently utilized part in traditional medicine due to their high concentration of essential oils like eugenol. These leaves are often prepared as decoctions or infusions to treat digestive issues, respiratory ailments, and skin infections. The seeds are also valued for their mucilaginous properties, which can help soothe sore throats and gastrointestinal discomfort. Additionally, the volatile oils derived from the plant's stems and foliage provide potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ocimum gratissimum?

This plant has antibacterial, antimicrobial, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic pharmacological activities that make it a valuable medicinal resource. Its antimicrobial and antibacterial properties allow it to effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and microorganisms. The presence of bioactive compounds also provides potent antioxidant effects that help neutralize harmful free radicals in the body. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory capabilities by reducing swelling and tissue irritation. Finally, its analgesic properties contribute to the relief of pain through natural chemical interactions.

As discussed in a study, the methanolic extract of Ocimum gratissimum was evaluated for its antibacterial and wound healing properties. The extract did not inhibit the growth of any of the 11 wound isolates tested, which included four strains of Staphylococcus aureus, two strains of E. coli, one strain of Pseudomonas aeruginosa, three strains of Proteus spp., and one strain of Shigella spp. Regarding its wound healing properties, the extract was tested using the excision wound model. More than 90% wound healing was recorded in the extract treated group by 14 days post surgery. This healing rate was higher than the 72% healing observed in the distilled water-treated group.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds eugenol, rosmarinic acid, chicoric acid, luteolin, and kaempferol. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the plant's diverse therapeutic profile through various biological activities. Eugenol and rosmarinic acid are frequently studied for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of chicoric acid further enhances the plant's ability to combat oxidative stress within cells. Additionally, the flavonoids luteolin and kaempferol provide significant neuroprotective and anticancer potential. Together, these substances form a complex chemical synergy that supports many health benefits.

As discussed in a study, Ocimum gratissimum essential oil contains active components including eugenol and myrcene. The essential oil was tested at doses of 10, 20, or 40 mg/kg, while eugenol and myrcene were administered at doses of 1, 5, or 10 mg/kg for 14 days. Treatment with 20 and 40 mg/kg of the Ocimum gratissimum essential oil and at doses of 5 and 10 mg/kg of the active components promoted antihypernociception. Additionally, the essential oil of the plant or eugenol was effective in reducing levels of interleukin-1β in the sciatic nerve.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating malaria, diabetes, diarrhea, inflammation, and skin infections. Its medicinal properties allow it to serve as an antimalarial agent to reduce fever and combat parasites. The leaves also help regulate blood sugar levels in patients managing diabetes. When used to treat diarrhea, the plant acts as an effective gastrointestinal soothing agent. Additionally, its natural compounds help reduce inflammation throughout the body and provide relief from various skin infections.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as an infusion or a decoction by steeping or boiling the leaves in water to create medicinal teas. Practitioners often extract its potent aromatic compounds through steam distillation to produce a concentrated essential oil. For more intense applications, the plant is macerated in alcohol to create a tincture or crushed into a topical poultice for skin ailments. Steam inhalation of the plant's vapors is frequently used to alleviate respiratory congestion. These diverse methods allow the various active constituents to be utilized for both internal and external therapeutic purposes.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic reactions, nausea, dizziness, gastrointestinal upset, hypoglycemia, and blood thinning effects in some individuals. Users may experience digestive issues such as nausea or general gastrointestinal upset after consumption. Some people might encounter neurological symptoms like dizziness or skin-related allergic reactions. The plant may also influence blood sugar levels leading to hypoglycemia. Additionally, its properties can contribute to blood thinning effects in the body.

The main side effects this plant can have are shown in the list below.

  • Nausea
  • Dizziness
  • Gastrointestinal upset
  • Hypoglycemia
  • Blood thinning effects
