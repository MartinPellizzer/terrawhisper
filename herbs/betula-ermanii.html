Betula ermanii

What's the taxonomical classification of Betula ermanii?

Betula ermanii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Fagales. The plant is a member of the family Betulaceae, falling under the genus Betula, and is specifically identified as the species ermanii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Betula ermanii contains various bioactive compounds including betulin, betulinic acid, lupeol, and various phenolic compounds such as quercetin and kaempferol. These triterpenoids are known for their significant anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and potential anticancer properties. The presence of flavonoids contributes to the plant's antioxidant capacity, helping to neutralize free radicals in biological systems. Additionally, certain extracts from the bark and leaves have demonstrated antimicrobial activity against specific bacterial strains. These chemical constituents make the species a subject of interest for pharmacological research into natural therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Betula ermanii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin ailments, and urinary tract issues through its medicinal properties. The bark and leaves often contain compounds that help reduce swelling and soothe irritation in the body. In traditional medicine, extracts from the species are applied topically to address eczema and other dermatological problems. It is also utilized in some practices to support kidney function and alleviate symptoms of bladder infections. These therapeutic applications rely on the plant's natural diuretic and antiseptic qualities.
