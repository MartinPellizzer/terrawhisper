Achillea asiatica

What's the taxonomical classification of Achillea asiatica?

Achillea asiatica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is placed within the genus Achillea, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species asiatica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Achillea asiatica has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by finely dissected, pinnatifid leaves and terminal corymbose inflorescences composed of small, yellowish-white flower heads. The foliage is deeply lobed and feathery, creating a delicate texture throughout the plant's base. Its stems are erect and often branched toward the top to support multiple flower clusters. Each individual flower head contains several tubular disc florets surrounded by inconspicuous ray florets. The overall structure is compact and bushy, typical of many plants within the Asteraceae family.
