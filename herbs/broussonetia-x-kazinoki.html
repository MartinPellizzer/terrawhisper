Broussonetia × kazinoki

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Broussonetiia × kazinoki has large, ovate leaves characterized by deeply lobed margins and a coarse, sandpaper-like texture on the upper surface. The foliage typically exhibits a striking variegated pattern consisting of creamy white or pale yellow patches against a deep green background. These leaves are arranged oppositely along woody, upright stems that form a dense, shrubby habit. The plant features prominent venation that creates a textured appearance across the leaf blade. Its overall structure provides a lush, ornamental presence in garden settings.
