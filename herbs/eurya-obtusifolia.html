Eurya obtusifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Eurya obtusifolia?

Eurya obtusifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ericales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Pentaphylacaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Eurya, with its specific identity defined by the species name obtusifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Eurya obtusifolia contains high concentrations of triterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic compounds that provide its therapeutic properties. These specific chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's documented antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. Researchers have identified various ursane-type triterpenes within the leaf extracts that may contribute to antimicrobial effects. Additionally, the presence of polyphenols suggests the plant could play a role in protecting cells against oxidative stress. These medicinal components are frequently studied for their potential applications in developing new pharmacological treatments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Eurya obtusifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and various types of wounds. Its leaves contain bioactive compounds that possess significant antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. These medicinal qualities allow the plant to combat bacterial pathogens that cause localized skin irritations. Additionally, extracts from the foliage are often applied topically to reduce swelling and promote the healing of damaged tissue. Local traditional practices frequently rely on these extracts for managing minor ailments and dermatological issues.
