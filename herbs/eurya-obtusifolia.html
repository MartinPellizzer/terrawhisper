Eurya obtusifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Eurya obtusifolia?

Eurya obtusifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ericales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Pentaphylacaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Eurya, with its specific identity defined by the species name obtusifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eurya obtusifolia has small, evergreen, elliptical leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length and possess blunt, obtuse tips with finely serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface, while the undersides may exhibit a slightly lighter hue. Its stems are woody and branched, providing a sturdy structure for the dense canopy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. These flowers eventually give way to small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they mature.
