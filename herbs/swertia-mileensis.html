Swertia mileensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Swertia mileensis?

Swertia mileensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianales. This plant is a part of the family Gentianaceae, which is characterized by its distinct floral structures. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Swertia, with its specific identity defined by the species name mileensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Swertia mileensis has opposite, decussate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape with entire margins and a distinctively acute apex. The leaf surfaces are typically glabrous and exhibit a deep green coloration on the upper side. Its stems are slender and woody, providing a structural framework for the spreading habit of the shrub. The flowers are small, tubular, and emerge from the axils of the leaves in small clusters. These blossoms are generally white or pale in color and possess a delicate structure characteristic of the Gentianaceae family.

What are the pharmacological activities of Swertia mileensis?

This plant has anti-hepatitis virus activity and the ability to inhibit the secretion of the cytokines IL-6 and TNF-alpha. These pharmacological properties suggest that the species possesses significant medicinal potential in treating viral infections and managing systemic inflammation. By suppressing the production of IL-6, the plant helps modulate the body's immune response to prevent excessive cellular signaling. Similarly, its capacity to inhibit TNF-alpha secretion can reduce the inflammatory damage caused by overactive immune pathways. These combined effects make Swertiia mileensis a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Pharmacognosy magazine", Swertiia mileensis is a medicinal plant used for hepatitis in the system of traditional Chinese medicine. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal sesquiterpene lactones (±)-swermilactones A, (±)-swermilactones B, Swerimlactones A, and Swerimlactones B. These specific chemical constituents are secondary metabolites responsible for the biological activity of the species. Researchers isolate these compounds to study their potential pharmacological properties in various therapeutic applications. The structural diversity of these lactones contributes to the unique chemical profile of the plant. Understanding these molecules is essential for developing new treatments derived from natural sources.

According to a study published by "Organic & biomolecular chemistry", two pairs of racemic xanthone-secoiridoid heterodimers, (±)-swermilactones A (1) and B (2), were isolated from the whole plants of Swertia mileensis. These medicinal compounds feature an unusual C-C bond linkage between the xanthone and secoiridoid moieties. The compounds (+)-1, (-)-1, (+)-2, and (-)-2 were found to moderately inhibit the secretion of cytokines TNF-α and IL-6 in LPS-induced RAW 264.7 macrophages. These inhibitory effects were measured with IC50 values ranging from 12.01 to 39.06 μM.
