Tabernaemontana divaricata

What's the taxonomical classification of Tabernaemontana divaricata?

Tabernaemontana divaricata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Tabernaemontana, and its unique species designation, divaricata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tabernaemontana divaricata has an evergreen, woody shrub morphology characterized by opposite, oblong, glossy green leaves with entire margins and an erect growth habit. The foliage typically features a leathery texture and prominent pinnate venation. Its inflorescences consist of fragrant, white, pinwheel-shaped flowers that emerge from the terminal ends of the branches. These blossoms possess five to eight spreading petals that create a distinct star-like appearance. The plant often produces small, fleshy, green drupes that eventually turn black upon maturity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, spanning from India through much of the Indomalayan realm. It flourishes in warm climates characterized by high humidity and consistent rainfall. Due to its ornamental value, it has been widely introduced and naturalized in other tropical zones, including parts of Africa and the Pacific Islands. It is commonly cultivated in gardens across the Caribbean and various coastal areas worldwide. This widespread distribution is largely a result of human intervention for landscaping purposes.

What are the pharmacological activities of Tabernaemontana divaricata?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-nociceptive, antibacterial, and antioxidant properties. Scientific studies indicate that its various extracts can effectively reduce pain perception and mitigate inflammatory responses in biological models. The plant also exhibits a strong ability to combat bacterial infections by inhibiting the growth of specific pathogens. Furthermore, its potent antioxidant components help neutralize free radicals and prevent oxidative stress within cells. These diverse therapeutic capabilities suggest that Tabernaemontana divaricata holds great potential for medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, ethanolic extracts of the roots, stems, leaves, and flowers of Tabernaemontana divaricata caused sedation, decreased respiration, and decreased skeletal muscle tone. These extracts caused vasodilatation of the ear vasculature, with the exception of the T. pandacaqui leaf extract. Analgesic activity was observed in all extracts of T. divaricata except for the flower extract. Lethal doses of these extracts caused animals to die from respiratory paralysis. The intensity of these pharmacological activities was greater when using the root and stem extracts.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several potent medicinal isoquinoline alkaloids including Catharanthine, Voacamine, Voacangine, Vobasinyl-iboga bisindole alkaloids, and tabernaelegantine A. These specific chemical compounds are primarily studied for their complex structural frameworks and potential pharmacological activities. Researchers often investigate these molecules due to their significant impact on the central nervous system. The presence of these various bisindole alkaloids provides a unique biochemical profile for the species. Understanding these substances is essential for developing new therapeutic applications in medicinal chemistry.

As discussed in a study, the flowers of Tabernaemontana divaricata contain three indole alkaloids which are Voacangine, Catharanthine, and O-acetyl Vallesamine. The methanolic extract of these flowers, known as TDFME, was tested in doses of 125, 250, and 500 mg/kg via oral administration. This extract produces anti-nociception through opioid, nitric oxide, K+-ATP, and glutamate mechanisms. The anti-nociceptive effect of TDFME was significantly attenuated at a level of p< 0.001 by the intraperitoneal pretreatment of naloxone, L-Arginine, and glibenclamide. Additionally, TDFME inhibited nociception produced by glutamate and enhanced the anti-nociceptive activity of L-NAME when given in combination.
