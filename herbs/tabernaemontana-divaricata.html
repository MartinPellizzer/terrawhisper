Tabernaemontana divaricata

What's the taxonomical classification of Tabernaemontana divaricata?

Tabernaemontana divaricata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Tabernaemontana, and its unique species designation, divaricata.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Tabernaemontana divaricata contains medicinal compounds located primarily in its leaves, latex, bark, and roots. The milky white latex is frequently extracted to treat skin infections, wounds, and inflammatory conditions. Leaf extracts are often applied topically or ingested in controlled doses to manage fever and various ailments. The bark and roots are also utilized in traditional practices to address digestive issues and certain types of pain. These various plant parts work together to provide a diverse range of therapeutic properties in ethnobotanical applications.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, steroids, phenolics, and saponins that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The presence of various alkaloids is particularly significant for their potential pharmacological activities in the central nervous system. Flavonoids and phenolics within the plant tissue provide essential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits. Additionally, the chemical profile is enriched by terpenoids and steroids which often demonstrate antimicrobial and cytotoxic effects.

Saponins also play a role in the plant's complex chemical makeup and biological interactions.

  • Steroids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Tabernaemontana divaricata targets various biological pathways by interacting with enzymes and receptors such as acetylcholinesterase, cyclooxygenase-2, and various opioid receptors. The plant contains bioactive indole alkaloids that can inhibit acetylcholinesterase, which plays a crucial role in regulating neurotransmitter levels for cognitive enhancement. Additionally, its chemical constituents act as inhibitors of cyclooxygenase-2 to reduce inflammatory mediators in the body. Some alkaloids within the plant also exhibit affinity for mu-opioid receptors, contributing to its documented analgesic properties. These multifaceted molecular interactions allow the plant to exert significant anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and neuroprotective effects.

What are the therapeutic actions of Tabernaemontana divaricata?

Tabernaemontana divaricata has anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial properties derived from its diverse phytochemical constituents such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids. The plant's latex is frequently applied topically to treat skin infections and reduce swelling in wounded areas. Extracts from the leaves and roots are often utilized in traditional medicine to manage pain and alleviate fever. Some studies also suggest that its bioactive compounds may possess neuroprotective and anticonvulsant activities. These therapeutic actions make it a significant subject for pharmacological research into treating various inflammatory and infectious conditions.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, wounds, inflammation, fever, and gastrointestinal disorders. The milky latex extracted from the stem is frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations and promote the healing of minor cuts. Additionally, decoctions made from the leaves are often consumed to help reduce bodily fever and manage stomach-related ailments. Some traditional practices also utilize the roots to address various inflammatory conditions throughout the body. These medicinal applications highlight the plant's versatility in folk medicine.

What are the herbal preparations of Tabernaemontana divaricata?

Tabernaemontana divaricata is prepared as a topical paste from crushed leaves, a decoction from the bark and roots, or a juice extracted from fresh plant parts. The leaf paste is frequently applied directly to the skin to treat wounds, swellings, and inflammatory conditions. Practitioners often boil the bark or roots in water to create a medicinal tea used for internal ailments. Additionally, the milky latex extracted from the stem can be used as a topical agent for specific skin irritations. These various methods allow different parts of the plant to target specific physiological needs.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Tabernaemontana divaricata can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The milky white latex produced by the plant contains alkaloids that may lead to contact dermatitis or redness when touched. If the sap comes into contact with the eyes, it can cause significant irritation or temporary blurred vision. Ingesting any part of the plant may result in nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its chemical composition. While not typically considered highly lethal, these physiological responses necessitate careful handling and avoidance of oral consumption.
