The parts of the plant most used for medicinal purposes are the roots and stems, which are rich in alkaloids and glycosides. The roots are typically dried and powdered for use in traditional medicine. The stems, on the other hand, are used to make infusions and decoctions.

Improper use of Marsdenia condurango can lead to side effects, including nausea, vomiting, and stomach cramps. High doses may also cause dizziness, headaches, and allergic reactions. These symptoms typically subside once the dosage is reduced or the plant is discontinued.

When using Marsdenia condurango medicinally, it is essential to follow standard precautions. These include consulting with a healthcare professional, monitoring dosages, and avoiding use during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Additionally, individuals with pre-existing medical conditions, such as kidney or liver disease, should exercise caution when using this plant.
