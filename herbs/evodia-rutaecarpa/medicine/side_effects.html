1. Causes stomach upset

Evodia rutaecarpa, also known as Chinese Rue, causes stomach upset.

This side effect is likely due to the plant's high concentration of alkaloids, which can irritate the digestive system and disrupt normal gut function. The volatile oils present in Evodia rutaecarpa may also contribute to this discomfort, causing nausea, bloating, and abdominal pain.

These compounds can interact with the body's natural digestive processes, leading to an unpleasant gastrointestinal response.

2. Produces allergic reactions

Evodia rutaecarpa, also known as Chinese rue, produces allergic reactions due to its high concentration of limonoids and flavonoids, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals.

The allergenic compounds in Evodia rutaecarpa may bind to IgE antibodies in the bloodstream, causing the release of histamine and other chemicals that lead to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

3. Induces vomiting

Evodia rutaecarpa, also known as Chinese Rue, induces vomiting.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's unique properties, which contain a high concentration of alkaloids and flavonoids. These compounds can stimulate the stomach lining, causing nausea and vomiting in some individuals. Additionally, the volatile oils present in Evodia rutaecarpa can irritate the digestive system, exacerbating these symptoms.

As a result, some people may experience episodes of vomiting after using this plant.

4. Increases heart rate

Evodia rutaecarpa, also known as Chinese rue, increases heart rate due to its high concentration of alkaloids and volatile oils.

These compounds stimulate the sympathetic nervous system, causing an increase in heart rate and blood pressure. Additionally, Evodia rutaecarpa's ability to inhibit the activity of certain enzymes in the body can further contribute to this side effect.

As a result, individuals using Evodia rutaecarpa may experience palpitations, anxiety, or jitteriness as their heart rate increases.

5. Lowers blood pressure

Evodia rutaecarpa, also known as Chinese rue, lowers blood pressure due to its ability to dilate blood vessels and reduce peripheral resistance.

This is caused by the presence of alkaloids such as evodiamine and rutaecarpine, which have been shown to relax smooth muscle tissue and decrease blood pressure.

As a result, Evodia rutaecarpa may cause individuals to experience decreased blood flow to vital organs and potentially lead to symptoms such as dizziness or fainting.

6. Stimulates respiratory system

Evodia rutaecarpa, also known as Chinese rue, stimulates respiratory system by increasing heart rate and blood pressure, which can lead to rapid breathing, palpitations, and increased sweating.

The alkaloids present in the plant, particularly rutaecarpine, are responsible for this stimulation, as they interact with the sympathetic nervous system and stimulate the release of catecholamines.

This can result in a range of symptoms, including anxiety, tremors, and an increase in blood flow to the lungs.

7. Causes anxiety attacks

Evodia rutaecarpa, also known as Chinese Rue, causes anxiety attacks due to its stimulating effects on the central nervous system.

The plant's active compounds, such as evodiamine and rutaecarpine, can increase heart rate, blood pressure, and respiratory rate, leading to feelings of panic and fear.

Additionally, Evodia rutaecarpa may interact with other substances in the body, exacerbating anxiety symptoms and potentially triggering full-blown anxiety attacks.

8. Triggers insomnia

Evodia rutaecarpa, also known as Chinese Rue, triggers insomnia due to its high concentration of alkaloids, particularly rutacridine, which can stimulate the central nervous system and disrupt normal sleep patterns.

The herb's ability to increase heart rate and blood pressure may also contribute to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep, leading to feelings of fatigue and restlessness during the day.

9. Leads to headaches

Evodia rutaecarpa, also known as Chinese Rue, leads to headaches due to its ability to stimulate the nervous system and increase blood pressure.

The herb's active compounds, such as rutaecarpine and evodiamine, can cause a rapid increase in heart rate and blood flow, leading to migraines or tension headaches.

Additionally, the herb's bitter properties may also contribute to stomach upset, which can exacerbate headache symptoms.

10. Develops diarrhea

Evodia rutaecarpa, also known as Chinese Rue, develops diarrhea in some individuals.

This side effect is attributed to its high concentration of alkaloids, particularly rutaecarpine and evodiamine, which can stimulate the gut and increase bowel movements. Additionally, the plant's bitter compounds may irritate the stomach lining, leading to increased water secretion and loose stools.

As a result, users may experience frequent bowel movements and changes in stool consistency.
