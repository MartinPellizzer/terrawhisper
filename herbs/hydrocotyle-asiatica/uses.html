Water Pennywort (Hydrocotyle asiatica) Uses

Water Pennywort is used to treat various skin conditions such as infections, inflammation, rashes, and insect bites. It helps reduce itching and irritation, promotes wound healing, and may aid in managing skin ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Water Pennywort (Hydrocotyle asiatica) Monograph

1. Skin infection

Water Pennywort is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce inflammation and promotes healing. The plant's leaves can be crushed and applied directly to the affected area. It is particularly effective for minor bacterial and fungal infections.

2. Inflamed skin

Water Pennywort is used externally to treat inflamed skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce redness, irritation, and swelling when applied topically. The herb's ability to cool and calm the skin makes it effective for eczema, rashes, and minor burns. It is often prepared as a poultice or infused oil for direct application.

3. Skin rash

Water Pennywort is used topically to treat skin rashes due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce redness, irritation, and itching associated with various skin conditions. The leaves are often crushed and applied directly to the affected area for localized relief.

4. Wound healing

Water Pennywort is used topically to promote wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce inflammation, prevent infection, and accelerate tissue repair. The plant's active compounds support cellular regeneration, making it a valued herb in traditional wound care practices.

5. Skin irritation

Water Pennywort is used to alleviate skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It can be applied topically as a poultice or infusion to reduce redness, itching, and inflammation. Its mild astringent action helps calm sensitive skin and promote healing. It is particularly effective for conditions like eczema and minor burns.

6. Skin infections

Water Pennywort is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on infected skin. The plant's leaves are often crushed and applied directly or brewed into a poultice for localized treatment.

7. Itching

Water Pennywort is traditionally used to relieve itching caused by skin conditions such as eczema or fungal infections. Its anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties help reduce irritation and soothe the skin. It is often prepared as a topical application or infusion for localized relief.

8. Insect bites

Water Pennywort is used to alleviate symptoms of insect bites due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It can be applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to reduce swelling, itching, and irritation. Its cooling effect helps ease discomfort and promote healing.

9. Skin inflammation

Water Pennywort is used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation associated with conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The plant's extracts are often applied topically to promote skin healing and alleviate discomfort.

10. Skin ulcers

Water Pennywort is used topically to treat skin ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. It helps reduce infection and promotes tissue regeneration. The leaves are often crushed and applied directly to the affected area. This traditional remedy supports faster healing and skin recovery.
