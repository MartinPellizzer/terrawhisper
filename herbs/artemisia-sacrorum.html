Artemisia sacrorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia sacrorum?

Artemisia sacrorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is a member of the order Asterales and falls within the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Artemisia and is distinguished as the species sacrorum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia sacrorum has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, woody stems that reach heights of up to 1 meter and are covered in fine, silvery pubescence. The leaves are deeply pinnatisect or bipinnatisect, featuring narrow, linear lobes that provide a feathery appearance to the foliage. Both the upper and lower surfaces of the leaves exhibit a grayish-green coloration due to a dense covering of trichomes. These hairs serve to protect the plant from excessive transpiration and intense sunlight. The flowering structures consist of small, inconspicuous heads arranged in complex panicles at the ends of the branches.
