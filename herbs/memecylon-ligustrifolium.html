Memecylon ligustrifolium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Memecylon ligustrifolium has elliptical to oblong leaves that are glossy, coriaceous, and typically measuring between five and twelve centimeters in length. The leaf margins are entire and the venation is characterized by fine, closely parallel secondary veins. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that emerge from the axils of the branches. These flowers are followed by small, globose berries that transition from green to a deep purple or black color when ripe. The plant overall exhibits a woody, shrubby growth habit with smooth, greyish bark.
