Brazzeia soyauxii

What's the taxonomical classification of Brazzeia soyauxii?

Brazzeia soyauxii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Ericales. It is a member of the family Lecythidaceae, which falls under the genus Brazzeia, and is specifically identified by its species name, soyauxii.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and intestinal parasites. The bark and roots are often prepared as decoctions or infusions to alleviate gastrointestinal distress. Local communities frequently apply the plant material topically to promote the healing of cuts and sores. It also serves as a traditional remedy for addressing various dermatological issues and parasitic infestations. These medicinal applications highlight the plant's role in regional ethnomedicine.
