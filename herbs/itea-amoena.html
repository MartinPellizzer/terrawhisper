Itea amoena

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Itea amoena has an erect, deciduous shrub habit characterized by slender, arching branches and finely textured, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length. The foliage displays a bright green color during the growing season and transitions to deep shades of red or bronze in the autumn. In late spring, the plant produces numerous small, fragrant, four-petaled white flowers arranged in drooping, terminal racemes. These floral clusters can reach several inches in length and serve as a primary attraction for pollinators. After blooming, the plant develops small, globose, brownish-black drupes that persist on the stems.
