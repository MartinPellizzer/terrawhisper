What's the taxonomical classification of Itea amoena?

Itea amoena belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually reaching the order Saxifragales. This plant is a member of the family Iteaceae and is situated within the genus Itea, specifically identified by the species name amoena.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Itea amoena has an erect, deciduous shrub habit characterized by slender, arching branches and finely textured, lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 2 to 5 centimeters in length.

The foliage displays a bright green color during the growing season and transitions to deep shades of red or bronze in the autumn. In late spring, the plant produces numerous small, fragrant, four-petaled white flowers arranged in drooping, terminal racemes. These floral clusters can reach several inches in length and serve as a primary attraction for pollinators.

After blooming, the plant develops small, globose, brownish-black drupes that persist on the stems.
