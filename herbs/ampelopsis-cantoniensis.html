Ampelopsis cantoniensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ampelopsis cantoniensis?

Ampelopsis cantoniensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Vitales and the family Vitaceae, eventually reaching its specific classification within the genus Ampelopsis. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species designation, cantoniensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ampelopsis cantoniensis has woody, climbing vines characterized by trifoliate leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and small, greenish-white flowers. The foliage typically features serrated margins and a somewhat leathery texture that aids in its climbing habit. Its stems are slender and capable of twining around surrounding vegetation to reach sunlight. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that change color as they mature. These morphological features collectively distinguish it from other members of the Vitaceae family.
