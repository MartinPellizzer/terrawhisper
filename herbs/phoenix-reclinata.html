Phoenix reclinata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phoenix reclinata has a slender, solitary trunk that typically reaches heights of up to 10 meters and is characterized by a dense crown of arching, pinnate leaves. These large fronds can measure up to 3 meters in length and consist of numerous stiff, linear leaflets that emerge from a central rachis. The plant often exhibits a leaning or reclining growth habit, which distinguishes it from more upright palm species. Its reproductive structures include small, yellowish flowers produced in clusters, followed by the development of small, fleshy fruits. The root system is fibrous, providing stability for the trunk within its natural subtropical habitat.
