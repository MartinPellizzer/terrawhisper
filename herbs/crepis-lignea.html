Crepis lignea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crepis lignea has a basal rosette of lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically entire or slightly dentate, supporting a slender, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. The leaves transition from larger, broader forms at the base to smaller, sessile bracts along the flowering stem. Its inflorescence consists of several yellow ligulate flower heads arranged in a loose corymbose manner. Each individual flower head is supported by a cylindrical involucre composed of numerous overlapping phyllaries. The plant's overall structure is characterized by a delicate, herbaceous habit adapted to open habitats.
