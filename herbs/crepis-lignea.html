What's the taxonomical classification of Crepis lignea?

Crepis lignea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its hierarchical descent, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asterales, it falls into the widely recognized family Asteraceae. Within this family, the plant is situated in the genus Crepis, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species linea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crepis lignea has a basal rosette of lanceolate to oblong leaves that are typically entire or slightly dentate, supporting a slender, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. The leaves transition from larger, broader forms at the base to smaller, sessile bracts along the flowering stem. Its inflorescence consists of several yellow ligulate flower heads arranged in a loose corymbose manner. Each individual flower head is supported by a cylindrical involucre composed of numerous overlapping phyllaries. The plant's overall structure is characterized by a delicate, herbaceous habit adapted to open habitats.

According to a study published by "Bioorganic chemistry", the water-soluble α-D-glucan CLP80-1 isolated from the roots of Crepis lignea possesses a molecular weight of 8.17 × 10^5 Da. Treatment with 1% CLP80-1 significantly improved survival rates and attenuated gut atrophy in models of ulcerative colitis. This plant-derived substance reduced intestinal permeability, enhanced intestinal barrier function, and inhibited the death of intestinal epithelial cells. The pharmacological effects of CLP80-1 included suppressing the excessive proliferation of intestinal stem cells and enteroblasts while reducing reactive oxygen species levels. Additionally, CLP80-1 downregulated the JAK-STAT signaling pathway to mitigate oxidative stress and inflammatory responses.
