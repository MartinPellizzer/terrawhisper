What is Gardenia (Gardenia Jasminoides)?

Gardenia jasminoides, commonly known as Gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Asia and the Pacific Islands, widely used in herbal medicine for its calming properties and traditional applications in treating digestive and respiratory ailments.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Gardenia is Gardenia jasminoides. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rubiales, family Rubiaceae, genus Gardenia, and species Gardenia jasminoides. It is commonly known as the gardenia in the United States and as the kōkō in Hawaii. The synonyms for this plant include Gardenia jasminoides var. asiatica and Gardenia asiatica, which are botanically recognized and used in different regional contexts.
