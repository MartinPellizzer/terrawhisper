What is Gardenia (Gardenia Jasminoides)?

Gardenia jasminoides, commonly known as Gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Asia and the Pacific Islands, widely used in herbal medicine for its calming properties and traditional applications in treating digestive and respiratory ailments.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Gardenia is Gardenia jasminoides. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rubiales, family Rubiaceae, genus Gardenia, and species Gardenia jasminoides. It is commonly known as the gardenia in the United States and as the kōkō in Hawaii. The synonyms for this plant include Gardenia jasminoides var. asiatica and Gardenia asiatica, which are botanically recognized and used in different regional contexts.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

When used appropriately, Gardenia jasminoides is generally considered safe for short-term use, but it may cause side effects such as gastrointestinal discomfort, nausea, and headaches in some individuals.

Prolonged or excessive use can lead to more serious issues, including liver damage and hormonal imbalances, due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds. Individuals with known allergies to plants in the Rubiaceae family should avoid Gardenia, as it may trigger allergic reactions ranging from skin irritation to respiratory symptoms. It is contraindicated in people with liver disease or those taking medications that affect liver function, as it may exacerbate existing conditions.

During pregnancy, Gardenia should be avoided due to limited safety data and potential risks to fetal development.

Gardenia jasminoides may interact with medications that affect the central nervous system, such as sedatives, antidepressants, and antipsychotics, potentially enhancing their effects and increasing the risk of adverse reactions.
