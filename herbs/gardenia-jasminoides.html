Gardenia

Gardenia jasminoides

Gardenia (Gardenia jasminoides) is a flowering plant native to Asia, known for its use in traditional medicine to treat conditions such as insomnia, depression, and digestive disorders.

Botanical Identification

Gardenia jasminoides is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family. Commonly known as gardenia or white ginger, it is a evergreen shrub native to southern Asia. It has glossy leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. Synonyms include Gardenia asiatica and G. jasminoides var. flavida in some herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rubiales
  • Family: Rubiaceae
  • Genus: Gardenia
  • Species: Gardenia jasminoides

Safety Profile

Gardenia jasminoides is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Prolonged use or high doses can lead to toxicity, so it should be used with caution and under professional guidance.
