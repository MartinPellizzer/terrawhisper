What's the taxonomical classification of Synotis cappa?

Synotis cappa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic lineage through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is part of the genus Synotis, and is specifically identified by the species name cappa.

        What are the herbal preparations of Synotis cappa?

        Synotis cappa is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the dried leaves and stems in water to extract its active chemical compounds. This liquid infusion is often consumed warm to soothe digestive discomfort or respiratory issues. Some practitioners also grind the dried plant material into a fine powder to be mixed with honey or warm water. Additionally, the crushed fresh leaves can be applied topically as a poultice for skin irritations. These various methods allow for both internal ingestion and external application of the plant's properties.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Synotis cappa can cause severe skin irritation, nausea, dizziness, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically. Individuals may experience redness or itching where the plant makes direct contact with the epidermis. Some users might also report gastrointestinal distress if the plant is consumed accidentally. Monitoring for swelling or respiratory issues is recommended for those with known sensitivities. Consulting a medical professional is necessary if any adverse symptoms persist after exposure.
