Laggera crispata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Laggera crispata has woody, perennial stems that produce deeply lobed, crispate leaves characterized by wavy, undulating margins and a somewhat succulent texture. The foliage is typically dark green and arranged alternately along the branches. During its flowering period, the plant produces small, tubular flowers that often feature bright yellow or orange coloration. These blossoms are clustered in terminal inflorescences that rise above the dense greenery. The overall structure of the plant is bushy and compact, making it well-suited for various garden environments.
