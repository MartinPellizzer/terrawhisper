Laggera crispata

What's the taxonomical classification of Laggera crispata?

Laggera crispata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. It is a specific member of the family Asteraceae, a large and diverse group of flowering plants, falling under the genus Laggera. Finally, its unique biological identity is defined by its specific classification as the species crispata.

Laggera crispata contains bioactive secondary metabolites including flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds that contribute to its pharmacological potential. What are the therapeutic actions of Laggera crispata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, stomach aches, and inflammatory conditions. The leaves are often prepared into a poultice or decoction to accelerate the healing of minor cuts and abrasions. For digestive issues, the plant extracts are applied to soothe gastrointestinal discomfort and pain. Its antimicrobial properties also make it effective in managing various topical fungal or bacterial ailments. Traditional practitioners rely on these botanical compounds to reduce swelling and localized inflammation.
