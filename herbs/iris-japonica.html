Iris japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris japonica has sword-shaped, dark green leaves that grow in dense, fan-like clumps from thick, creeping rhizomes. The foliage typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features a prominent central vein running through each blade. During the flowering season, the plant produces elegant, funnel-shaped blossoms that are primarily purple or violet with yellow markings on the falls. These flowers emerge from a central stalk and are supported by protective, papery spathes. The overall structure of the plant creates a low-growing, textured appearance suitable for ground cover.
