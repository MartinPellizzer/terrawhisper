Erect Potentilla

Potentilla erecta

Erect Potentilla (Potentilla erecta) is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its yellow flowers and historical use in traditional medicine to support digestive health and alleviate symptoms of gastrointestinal disorders.

Botanical Identification

Erect Potentilla, scientifically known as Potentilla erecta, belongs to the Rosaceae family. It is also called upright cinquefoil and is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has upright stems, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers. It is sometimes referred to as Spiraea in older herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rosales
  • Family: Rosaceae
  • Genus: Potentilla
  • Species: Potentilla erecta

Active Compounds

Erect Potentilla contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These bioactive compounds work synergistically to provide its medicinal properties, primarily through antioxidant and anti-inflammatory mechanisms. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in herbal medicine applications.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Safety Profile

Erect Potentilla is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Individuals with known allergies to plants in the Rosaceae family should also exercise caution.
