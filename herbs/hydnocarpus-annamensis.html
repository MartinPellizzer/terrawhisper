Hydnocarpus annamensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hydnocarpus annamensis?

Hydnocarpus annamensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Achariaceae, which serves as the broader group for this specific organism. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Hydnocarpus, with the specific epithet annamensis identifying the individual species.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hydnocarpus annamensis has a medium to large tree habit characterized by an elliptical to obovate leaf shape, an entire leaf margin, and a coriaceous texture. The foliage typically displays a glossy green upper surface with a paler underside. Its reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into woody, globose fruits. These fruits contain oily seeds which are a distinguishing feature of the genus. The bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured depending on the age of the specimen.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of Northeast India, specifically within the state of Assam. Its natural range is restricted to the moist deciduous and evergreen forest ecosystems of the Indo-Burma biodiversity hotspot. Within these habitats, it typically grows in the understory of dense, humid woodlands. The species is closely associated with the specific soil compositions and high rainfall patterns characteristic of this subtropical zone. Consequently, its distribution is highly localized to these specific ecological niches in South Asia.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Hydnocarpus annamensis contains medicinal compounds primarily extracted from its seeds and oil. The seeds are harvested to produce a valuable fixed oil that is utilized for its antifungal and antiparasitic properties. This oil is often applied topically to treat various skin conditions, including scabies and fungal infections. Additionally, extracts from the bark and leaves have been studied for their potential bioactive effects in traditional medicine. What are the pharmacological activities of Hydnocarpus annamensis?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidation, antimicrobial, and cytotoxic properties. Its chemical constituents work to reduce swelling and inhibit the production of inflammatory mediators in biological systems. The presence of specific bioactive compounds also allows it to neutralize free radicals, thereby providing significant anti-oxidation benefits to cells. These multifaceted medicinal effects suggest that the species holds considerable potential for developing new therapeutic agents. Researchers continue to investigate how these various mechanisms can be harnessed for clinical applications in treating chronic diseases.

As discussed in a study, two new phenolic glycosides 1 and 2 were isolated from the barks of Hydnocarpus annamensis along with eighteen known compounds. Among these isolates, compounds 1, 5, 15 and 17 exhibited COX-2 inhibitory activities with an inhibitory rate of 60.3 - 63.4 % at 10 microM. Furthermore, compounds 7, 12, 13 and 20 demonstrated anti-oxidation capacities in the DPPH free-radical assay. These specific compounds showed a scavenging effect of 60.9 - 69.4 % at 50 microM and 18.5 - 34.6 % at 10 microM.

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as chaulmoogra oil, an essential fixed oil extracted from its seeds, seed powder, and an essential oil. The chaulmoogra oil is primarily obtained through the cold pressing of the seeds and is widely used in traditional medicine for treating skin ailments. Seed powder is often utilized in topical applications or as a component in various medicinal formulations. The essential oil provides a concentrated aromatic extract that contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties. These different preparations allow for diverse uses in both dermatological and holistic wellness practices.
