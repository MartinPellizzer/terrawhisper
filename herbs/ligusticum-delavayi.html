Ligusticum delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligusticum delavayi?

Ligusticum delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Apiales and the family Apiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Ligusticum and is specifically designated as the species delavayi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligusticum delavayi has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by large, compound, tripinnate leaves that can reach up to 50 centimeters in length. The foliage is bright green and features serrated leaflets that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its stems are hollow, erect, and often develop purplish pigmentation towards the base. During the flowering season, the plant produces numerous small, white flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped umbels. These inflorescences are supported by sturdy peduncles that elevate the blooms above the dense leafy canopy. The root system is typically robust and fleshy, providing the plant with significant storage capacity.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude mountainous regions of southwestern China, specifically within the provinces of Yunnan, Sichuan, and Guizhou. It primarily inhabits temperate forest ecosystems located at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 3,500 meters above sea level. These populations are often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain slopes and forest margins where soil moisture is consistent. The species is restricted to these specific montane habitats due to its reliance on cool, humid climates.
