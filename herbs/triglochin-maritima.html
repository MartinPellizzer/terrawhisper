Triglochin maritima

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Triglochin maritima has succulent, linear to lanceolate leaves that are fleshy, bright green, and typically measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length. These leaves arise from a creeping rhizome and are arranged in a basal rosette or scattered along the stem. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are greenish or yellowish and borne in dense, spike-like racemes. Its reproductive structures consist of three distinct carpels that develop into small, fleshy, berry-like fruits. This specialized anatomy allows the plant to thrive in saline coastal environments and salt marshes.
