Possible Side Effects of Pachycarpus robustus

Pachycarpus robustus can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal irritation, nausea, and vomiting, which can be triggered by consuming large amounts of the plant's leaves or roots.

Additionally, it may cause allergic reactions, including skin rashes and respiratory issues, in some individuals.

Overuse or prolonged exposure to the plant's extracts can also lead to liver and kidney damage, which can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.

Furthermore, pregnant women and people with pre-existing medical conditions should exercise extreme caution when handling or using pachycarpus robustus, as it may exacerbate underlying health issues.
