1. Aristolochic acid

Aristolochia littoralis, also known as Dutchman's pipe, aristolochic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from its roots and leaves.

This highly toxic and nephrotoxic substance has been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various ailments, including gout and rheumatism. However, studies have shown that prolonged use of aristolochic acid can cause severe kidney damage, potentially leading to chronic kidney disease or even kidney failure.

Its use is thus strongly discouraged.

2. Flavonoids

Aristolochia littoralis flavonoids is a class of plant compounds that have been isolated from its aerial parts.

These flavonoids exhibit significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, making them potential agents in the prevention and treatment of various diseases. Specifically, aristolochic acid A (AAA) has been shown to inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells and induce apoptosis, highlighting its promising role as a therapeutic agent.

Further research is needed to fully elucidate the biological effects of these flavonoids.

3. Phenolic acids

These compounds have been shown to exhibit antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties, which can contribute to the plant's traditional uses in folk medicine.

Specifically, aristolochic acid I, a major phenolic acid component, has been reported to inhibit the growth of certain bacteria and fungi, highlighting its potential as a natural antibiotic and antifungal agent.

9. Quercetin

Aristolochia littoralis, also known as Dutchman's pipe, quercetin is a flavonoid compound extracted from its roots.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various ailments such as fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues. Quercetin exhibits anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiviral properties, making it a potential natural remedy for several diseases, including cancer, cardiovascular disorders, and neurodegenerative conditions.

Its unique chemical structure also provides antimicrobial and antifungal activities.

10. Kaempferol

Aristolochia littoralis, also known as Dutchman's pipe, kaempferol is a bioflavonoid compound extracted from the plant Aristolochia littoralis.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine to treat various diseases, including cancer, cardiovascular and respiratory disorders. Kaempferol exhibits strong antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a promising agent for the prevention of chronic diseases.

Its molecular structure also suggests potential therapeutic applications as an anticancer and antimicrobial agent.
