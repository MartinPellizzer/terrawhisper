What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Eclipta Prostrata (False Daisy)?

Eclipta prostrata, also known as false daisy, has health benefits such as promoting hair growth and treating skin conditions.

Its medicinal constituents include flavonoids, terpenoids, and triterpenes, which are responsible for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Eclipta prostrata is often used to prepare topical creams and ointments for skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Possible side effects include allergic reactions and skin irritation.

Precautions include consulting a healthcare professional before using Eclipta prostrata, especially if pregnant or breastfeeding.

What are the health benefits of Eclipta prostrata?

Eclipta prostrata, also known as false daisy, has health benefits such as promoting hair growth, strengthening hair follicles, and reducing dandruff.

What are the active constituents of Eclipta prostrata?

Eclipta prostrata, also known as false daisy, has active constituents such as flavonoids, triterpenoid saponins, and alkaloids that possess medicinal properties.

The flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, while the triterpenoid saponins have been shown to possess immunomodulatory and antifungal effects.

Additionally, the plant's extracts have been found to have antidiabetic and hepatoprotective properties, making it a potential candidate for the development of natural therapeutics.

What are the possible side effect of using Eclipta prostrata improperly?

Improper use of Eclipta prostrata, also known as false daisy, increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and digestive issues.

Consuming large quantities may lead to stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea. Additionally, some individuals may experience headaches, dizziness, or fatigue.

Pregnant women, breastfeeding mothers, and individuals with certain medical conditions should consult a healthcare professional before using Eclipta prostrata due to its potential interactions with medications.
