10 Health Benefits Of Eclipta Prostrata (False Daisy)

Eclipta prostrata, also known as false daisy, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and improving hair growth due to its medicinal properties of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

These compounds have been shown to inhibit the activity of 5-alpha-reductase, an enzyme responsible for hair loss. As a result, individuals with androgenetic alopecia can benefit from consuming false daisy, leading to improved self-confidence and overall quality of life.

It may also help with skin conditions, such as eczema and acne.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Eclipta prostrata.

1. Improves liver function

Eclipta prostrata improves liver function because it contains a unique compound called madecassoside, which has been shown to possess hepatoprotective properties.

Madecassoside helps to reduce liver inflammation and oxidative stress, while also enhancing antioxidant defenses within the liver cells.

This natural compound has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat various liver-related disorders, including jaundice and hepatitis.

2. Stimulates hair growth

Eclipta prostrata stimulates hair growth because of its unique properties and bioactive compounds.

The plant contains a compound called apigenin, which has been shown to promote the growth and proliferation of hair follicles, while also improving blood circulation to the scalp.

Additionally, Eclipta prostrata has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to protect the hair from damage caused by free radicals and oxidative stress.

3. Reduces inflammation

Eclipta prostrata reduces inflammation because of its rich antioxidant and flavonoid content.

The herb's compounds, including ecdysterone and eupatoriopicrin, have been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory enzymes and cytokines, thereby reducing inflammation in the body.

Additionally, false daisy has been found to exhibit potent anti-nociceptive properties, making it a promising natural remedy for alleviating pain and discomfort associated with inflammation.

4. Treats skin diseases

Eclipta prostrata treats skin diseases because of its potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The herb contains a compound called ecliptin, which has been shown to inhibit the growth of harmful bacteria and fungi that can cause infections.

Additionally, its antioxidant properties help to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the skin, making it an effective remedy for conditions such as eczema, acne, and psoriasis.

5. Prevents premature graying

Eclipta prostrata prevents premature graying because of its unique antioxidant and melanin-boosting properties.

The plant's extracts have been shown to stimulate the production of melanocytes, the cells responsible for hair pigmentation, thereby increasing melanin levels in the scalp and preventing the formation of gray hairs.

Additionally, Eclipta prostrata's antioxidant compounds help protect the hair follicles from oxidative damage, further promoting healthy hair growth and color retention.

6. Lowers cholesterol levels

Eclipta prostrata lowers cholesterol levels because it contains a variety of bioactive compounds that have been shown to have hypolipidemic properties.

The plant's extracts have been found to inhibit the production of triglycerides and cholesterol in the liver, while also increasing the excretion of these substances from the body through the bile ducts.

This natural mechanism helps to reduce total cholesterol levels and LDL ("bad") cholesterol, thereby reducing the risk of cardiovascular disease.

7. Fights diabetes

Eclipta prostrata fights diabetes because it possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties that help to regulate blood sugar levels.

The plant's flavonoids and alkaloids have been shown to improve insulin sensitivity and glucose uptake in the body, reducing the risk of developing type 2 diabetes.

Additionally, its anti-inflammatory effects may also help to protect against diabetic complications such as nephropathy and neuropathy.

8. Reduces fever

Eclipta prostrata reduces fever because of its unique composition.

The plant contains a compound called ecdysone, which has been shown to have anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties. When taken orally or applied topically, ecdysone helps to reduce body temperature by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that contribute to fever.

Additionally, false daisy's flavonoids and saponins may also contribute to its febrifugal effects, making it a traditional remedy for treating fever and other inflammatory conditions.

9. Treats respiratory disorders

Eclipta prostrata treats respiratory disorders because of its remarkable phytoconstituents.

The herb contains flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenes that exhibit potent anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and bronchodilatory properties.

These bioactive compounds help to reduce inflammation in the lungs, relax airway muscles, and enhance mucociliary clearance, thus alleviating symptoms of respiratory disorders such as asthma, bronchitis, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

10. Improves digestion

Eclipta prostrata improves digestion because it contains bioactive compounds that help stimulate digestive enzymes and boost gut motility.

The plant's leaves have been traditionally used to treat various gastrointestinal disorders, including constipation, bloating, and indigestion.

Studies have shown that Eclipta prostrata extracts exhibit anti-inflammatory properties, which can help alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) and other digestive issues.
