10 Possible Side Effects Of Eclipta Prostrata

Eclipta prostrata has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and interact with medications.

Causes of these side effects include excessive consumption, individual sensitivities, or concurrent use with other herbal remedies or medications.

For instance, people who already have digestive issues may experience worsened symptoms like nausea, diarrhea, or stomach cramps, affecting daily activities, social life, and overall well-being.

1. Increases blood pressure

Eclipta prostrata increases blood pressure by stimulating the cardiovascular system and increasing heart rate.

This is due to its ability to increase peripheral resistance, leading to an elevation in systolic blood pressure. Additionally, Eclipta prostrata may cause vasoconstriction, which further contributes to increased blood pressure.

As a result, individuals taking this herb may experience elevated blood pressure readings, potentially putting them at risk for cardiovascular complications if left untreated.

2. Causes allergic reactions

Eclipta prostrata causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is likely due to the presence of sesquiterpenoids and flavonoids, two types of bioactive compounds found in the plant, which can stimulate an immune response and lead to symptoms such as itching, hives, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the allergenic potential of Eclipta prostrata may be enhanced by its ability to release histamine, a chemical mediator involved in allergic reactions, from mast cells.

3. Provokes nausea and vomiting

Eclipta prostrata provokes nausea and vomiting due to its ability to stimulate the digestive system and increase stomach acid secretion.

The herb's bitter compounds, such as ecliptin and sesquiterpenes, can irritate the stomach lining, leading to feelings of discomfort, queasiness, and subsequent vomiting.

Additionally, Eclipta prostrata's stimulating properties may also disrupt normal digestion patterns, further exacerbating nausea and vomiting symptoms.

4. Lowers white blood cell count

Eclipta prostrata lowers white blood cell count.

This herb contains a bioactive compound called ecliptin, which has been shown to suppress the production of white blood cells in the bone marrow, leading to a decrease in their count.

Additionally, Eclipta prostrata may interfere with the body's natural ability to fight off infections by reducing the activity of immune cells, making individuals more susceptible to illness and disease.

5. Triggers diarrhea

Eclipta prostrata triggers diarrhea due to its high concentration of iridoid glycosides and phenolic compounds.

These bioactive compounds have been shown to stimulate the intestinal tract and increase bowel motility, leading to rapid emptying of the stomach and intestines, which can result in diarrhea.

Additionally, the plant's saponins may also contribute to this side effect by altering the balance of gut flora and increasing water absorption in the colon.

6. Interacts with medications

Eclipta prostrata interacts with medications that lower blood pressure, leading to a significant drop in blood pressure.

This is because Eclipta prostrata can potentiate the effects of these medications, causing an excessive decrease in blood pressure.

Additionally, it may also interact with warfarin and other anticoagulant medications, increasing the risk of bleeding complications.

7. Affects thyroid function

Eclipta prostrata affects thyroid function due to its potential to interfere with the production and regulation of thyroxine (T4) and triiodothyronine (T3).

This is because Eclipta prostrata contains bioactive compounds that may bind to thyroid receptors, disrupting normal thyroid hormone metabolism.

Additionally, it has been suggested that Eclipta prostrata may alter the conversion of T4 to T3, leading to changes in thyroid function and potentially affecting overall health.

8. Enhances bleeding risk

Eclipta prostrata enhances bleeding risk due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and increase the risk of bleeding complications.

This is attributed to the presence of sesquiterpenoids and phenolic acids in the plant, which have been shown to reduce blood clotting factors and increase the likelihood of excessive bleeding.

As a result, individuals using Eclipta prostrata may experience increased bleeding during surgical procedures or with concurrent use of anticoagulant medications.

9. Worsens kidney function

Eclipta prostrata worsens kidney function by causing a decline in glomerular filtration rate and increasing tubular damage.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including eclalbin and ecliptin, may interfere with the normal functioning of the kidneys, leading to reduced elimination of waste products and electrolytes from the body.

This can result in an accumulation of toxins and metabolic waste, further exacerbating kidney dysfunction.

10. Aggravates respiratory issues

Eclipta prostrata aggravates respiratory issues due to its ability to stimulate the central nervous system and increase sympathetic activity.

This can lead to an increase in bronchial secretions, airway constriction, and heightened sensitivity to allergens, exacerbating conditions such as asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).
