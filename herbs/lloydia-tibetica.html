What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Llloydia tibetica has an erect perennial herb structure characterized by a basal rosette of leaves and a single, slender flowering stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The inflorescence consists of a solitary, terminal flower that is often nodding or pendulous. Each flower features five delicate, pale pink to white petals that surround a central cluster of numerous stamens.
