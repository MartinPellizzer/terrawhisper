Gentiana rhodantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana rhodantha?

Gentiana rhodantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Gentianales. This plant is a member of the family Gentianaceae and is further categorized under the genus Gentiana, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species rhodantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana rhodantha has opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins and a prominent central vein, supporting upright stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The flowers are bell-shaped and characterized by a deep pink to rose-colored corolla with five distinct lobes. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and often feature subtle dark spotting or striations on the interior surfaces. The calyx consists of small, green, pointed lobes that protect the developing bud. Small, globose capsules develop from the ovaries to disperse seeds after the flowering period concludes.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains ursolic acid, mangiferin, salicylic acid, quercetin, and ferulic acid. These specific medicinal compounds work together to provide diverse therapeutic benefits to the human body. For instance, ursolic acid and quercetin are known for their potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Mangiferin and ferulic acid contribute to cellular protection by neutralizing free radicals. Additionally, the presence of salicylic acid offers natural analgesic and antimicrobial effects.
