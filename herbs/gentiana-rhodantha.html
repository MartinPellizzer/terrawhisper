Gentiana rhodantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana rhodantha?

Gentiana rhodantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Gentianales. This plant is a member of the family Gentianaceae and is further categorized under the genus Gentiana, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species rhodantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gentiana rhodantha has opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves with entire margins and a prominent central vein, supporting upright stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The flowers are bell-shaped and characterized by a deep pink to rose-colored corolla with five distinct lobes. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and often feature subtle dark spotting or striations on the interior surfaces. The calyx consists of small, green, pointed lobes that protect the developing bud. Small, globose capsules develop from the ovaries to disperse seeds after the flowering period concludes.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-drained, sandy or gravelly soil and a cool, temperate climate to thrive during its growing season.

It should be sown in late summer or early autumn to allow for natural stratification through a period of cold. Once established, the species prefers full sun to partial shade and must avoid waterlogged conditions to prevent root rot. Regular watering is necessary during dry spells, but the substrate must dry out slightly between applications.

Because it is a short-lived perennial, it is best managed in locations where it can colonize small gaps in rocky terrain.

What are the pharmacological activities of Gentiana rhodantha?

This plant has anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities that can help reduce swelling and pain in biological tissues. These medicinal properties are often attributed to the presence of specific bitter compounds found within its roots. Researchers study these chemical constituents to understand how they modulate the body's immune response to irritation. By inhibiting certain inflammatory pathways, the plant may offer therapeutic potential for various inflammatory conditions. This specific action makes Gentiana rhodantha a subject of interest in natural product pharmacology.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", Gentiana rhodantha is used medicinally for the treatment of inflammation, cholecystitis, and tuberculosis. Investigations into its components showed that rhodanthenone D (4) and mangiferin (12) exhibited 18.4% and 13.4% of acetylcholinesterase (AChE) inhibitory effects at a concentration of 10(-4) M, respectively. Additionally, compounds 1-5 along with the known xanthones lancerin (11), mangiferin (12), and neomangiferin (13) displayed no cytotoxicity at a concentration of 40 μM. These results were observed against five human cancer cell lines.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds gentiopicroside, loganic acid, rhodanthenone A, rhodanthenone B, and sweroside. These specific iridoids and secoiridoids are responsible for the distinct bitter taste characteristic of the Gentiana genus. Research indicates that these bioactive molecules play a significant role in the plant's pharmacological properties. The presence of rhodanthenone A and rhodanthenone B distinguishes this species from other gentians. Together, these chemical constituents contribute to the diverse therapeutic potential found within the plant's extracts.

According to a study published by "Biomedical chromatography : BMC", the plant Gentiana rhodantha Franch. ex Hemsl. contains six index constituents including gentiopicroside, swertiamarin, loganic acid, sweroside, mangiferin, and ferulic acid. The determination of these compounds was performed using a UPPLC-UV-MS method on a Shim-Pack XR-ODS III column measuring 150 × 2.0 mm with a 2.2 µm particle size. Analysis utilized a wavelength of 242 nm for the four iridoids and mangiferin, while 320 nm was set for ferulic acid. All regression equations for the identification of these constituents revealed a linear relationship where R(2) was greater than 0.9993. The similarity value for Gentiana rhodantha Franch. ex Hemsl. was found to be less than 0.788 when compared to the other species.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", Gentiana rhodantha Franch. ex Hemsl. is an annual herb used medicinally by local people for the treatment of inflammation, cholecystitis, and tuberculosis. Chemical investigation on the whole plants led to the identification of 8 new phenolic compounds, specifically rhodanthenones A-D (1-4, resp.), apigenin 7-O-glucopyranosyl-(1→3)-glucopyranosyl-(1→3)-glucopyranoside (5), 1,2-dihydroxy-4-methoxybenzene 1-O-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1→6)-β-D-glucopyranoside (6), 1,2-dihydroxy-4,6-dimethoxybenzene 1-O-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1→6)-β-D-glucopyranoside (7), and methyl 2-O-β-D-glucopyranosyl-2,4,6-trihydroxybenzoate (8). These were found alongside 11 known compounds, numbered 9-19. Regarding inhibitory effects, rhodanthenone D (4) and mangiferin (12) exhibited 18.4% and 13.4% of AChE inhibitory effects at a concentration of 10(-4) M. Additionally, compounds 1-5 and known compounds 11, 12, and 13 displayed no cytotoxicity at a concentration of 40 μM against 5 human cancer cell lines.
