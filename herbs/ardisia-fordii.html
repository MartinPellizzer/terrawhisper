Ardisia fordii

What's the taxonomical classification of Ardisia fordii?

Ardisia fordii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, falling under the genus Ardisia, and is specifically identified by its species name, foridii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ardisia fordii has opposite, simple, elliptical to oblong leaves that are glossy dark green on the upper surface and slightly paler underneath.

The leaf margins are entire and the venation is pinnate with prominent secondary veins. This evergreen shrub or small tree features woody stems that support a dense canopy of foliage. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically white or cream-colored and emerge from the leaf axils.

The plant eventually produces fleshy, globose fruits that transition in color as they ripen.

    What are the herbal preparations of Ardisia fordii?

These diverse methods allow practitioners to tailor the delivery of the plant's medicinal properties to specific health needs.
