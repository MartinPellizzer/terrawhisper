Cremanthodium lineare

What's the taxonomical classification of Cremanthodium lineare?

Cremanthodium lineare belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is further categorized into the family Asteraceae, which is the characteristic group for many composite flowers. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Cremanthodium, with its specific classification being the species lineare.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cremanthodium lineare has linear, undivided leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled or subopposite pattern along its slender, upright stems.

These leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance, often possessing entire margins and acute tips. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in terminal heads or panicles. Its stem structure is relatively delicate and can vary in height depending on the local environmental conditions.

The overall morphology is characterized by a fine, wiry texture that distinguishes it from more broad-leaved species in the Asteraceae family.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cremanthodium lineare can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting, diarrhea, and intense abdominal pain, if ingested due to its toxic chemical constituents. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may irritate the digestive tract or lead to systemic toxicity. Potential side effects might also include neurological symptoms or dehydration resulting from prolonged bouts of emesis. Because its exact toxicity profile is not fully documented in humans, ingestion should be treated as a medical emergency. Careful avoidance of all parts of this plant is necessary to prevent accidental poisoning.
