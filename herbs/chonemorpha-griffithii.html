Chonemorpha griffithii

What's the taxonomical classification of Chonemorpha griffithii?

Chonemorpha griffithii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Gentianaales. It is further categorized into the family Apocynaceae, which defines its broader botanical relationships. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Chonemorpha, specifically identifying it as the species griffithii.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Chonemorpha griffithii can cause localized skin irritation, redness, and swelling upon direct contact with its foliage or stems. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience a more intense inflammatory response or an itchy rash. There is also a risk of allergic dermatitis if the plant's sap touches broken skin. While systemic toxicity is less documented, caution should be exercised to prevent accidental ingestion. Proper protective clothing should be worn when handling this species to minimize these dermatological risks.
