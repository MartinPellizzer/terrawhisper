Brassica Napus: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Brassica napus, commonly known as rapeseed, is a flowering plant in the mustard family that is cultivated for its seeds, which are used to produce oil.

This herb is rich in bioactive constituents such as glucosinolates, flavonoids, and omega-3 fatty acids, which contribute to its health benefits.

It is known to support cardiovascular health, reduce inflammation, and enhance immune function due to its therapeutic actions.

Additionally, Brassica napus may aid in detoxification processes and has shown potential in cancer prevention.
