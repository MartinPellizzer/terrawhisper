Rapeseed (Brassica napus)

Rapeseed (Brassica napus) is a member of the Brassicaceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North America. Traditionally, its seeds, fruits, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and culinary uses.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and expectorant actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Annual herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface pale green, bearing prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are yellow with four petals arranged in a cross shape, displaying bilateral symmetry, and have four stamens and a single pistil.
Stems
Cylindrical, herbaceous, glabrous, erect growth habit with opposite branching pattern and smooth, ridged surface.

Medicinal Actions

Diuretic
Commonly referenced as a gentle diuretic, for moisture-related balance.
Anti-inflammatory

Expectorant
Traditionally described as a calming expectorant, in respiratory system contexts.
Carminative
In herbal literature, noted as a cooling carminative, in stomach-related herbal uses.
