Hydnocarpus kurzii

What's the taxonomical classification of Hydnocarpus kurzii?

Hydnocarpus kurzii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Malpighiales. The plant is further categorized into the family Achariaceae, within which it is placed in the genus Hydnocarpus. Finally, its specific identification is completed by its species name, kurzii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Hydnocarpus kurzii contains unique fatty acids known as chaulmoogric, hydnocarpic, and sophorolipid which serve as the primary medicinal components. These specific compounds are extracted from the seeds to produce oils used extensively in dermatological treatments. The oil is particularly effective for managing skin conditions such as leprosy, psoriasis, and various types of eczema. Scientists value these lipids for their ability to penetrate the skin and exert antimicrobial effects against certain pathogens. Research into these chemical constituents continues to explore their potential applications in modern pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Hydnocarpus kurzii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various types of fungal skin infections, specifically those caused by dermatophytes such as ringworm, athlete's foot, and favus. The seeds of the plant contain chaulmoogric acid, which possesses potent antifungal properties. This chemical compound works by disrupting the cellular structure of the invading fungi to stop their growth. Historically, extracts from the plant have also been researched for their potential effectiveness against leprosy. Modern applications focus on using the oil derived from the seeds in topical dermatological preparations.
