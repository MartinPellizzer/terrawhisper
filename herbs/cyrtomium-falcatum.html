Cyrtomium falcatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyrtomium falcatum has large, deeply divided pinnate fronds that typically measure 20 to 50 centimeters in length with distinctively sickle-shaped or falcate segments. The individual pinnules are narrow and taper toward the tips, giving the foliage a delicate, lace-like appearance. These fern blades emerge from a creeping rhizome and are supported by sturdy, upright stipes. The texture of the foliage is relatively thin, allowing the plant to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Small sori are located on the underside of the leaflets to facilitate reproduction through spores.
