What's the taxonomical classification of Cyrtomium falcatum?

Cyrtomium falcatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Polypodiaceae, placing it within the genus Cyrtomium, and it is specifically identified as the species falcatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyrtomium falcatum has large, deeply divided pinnate fronds that typically measure 20 to 50 centimeters in length with distinctively sickle-shaped or falcate segments. These fern blades emerge from a creeping rhizome and are supported by sturdy, upright stipes. The texture of the foliage is relatively thin, allowing the plant to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
