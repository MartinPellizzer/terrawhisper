Botanical Identification of Golden Shower Tree (Senna alata)

To identify Golden Shower Tree (Senna alata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Golden Shower Tree, Senna alata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Senna, and species Senna alata. This classification places it within the legume family, known for its nitrogen-fixing properties and medicinal uses.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The Golden Shower Tree is scientifically known as Senna alata. This name was first described by the botanist Carl von Linné in 1753. It has several synonyms, including Cassia alata and Senna tora, which reflect its classification within the Fabaceae family. Common names vary widely, including flame tree and crown of thorns, depending on the region.

Morphological Characteristics

The Golden Shower Tree is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a short, thick root system. Its stem is upright, often multi-branched, and covered in fine hairs. Leaves are alternate, pinnately compound with 12-20 pairs of elliptical leaflets. The inflorescence is a raceme of yellow flowers, which are fragrant and attract pollinators. The fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Golden Shower Tree (Senna alata) can be distinguished by its trifoliate leaves with a prominent midrib, yellow flowers in racemes, and oblong pods with a fibrous texture. It differs from similar species like Senna tora by its leaf shape and the presence of a stipule at the base of each leaflet. The plant also has a distinctive, aromatic odor when crushed.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

The Golden Shower Tree, Senna alata, is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. It thrives in warm climates with high humidity and well-drained, loamy soils. It commonly grows at altitudes between 0 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Golden Shower Tree (Senna alata) blooms from March to May, producing bright yellow flowers in clusters. Seed formation occurs from June to August, with pods developing and maturing by late summer. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during the flowering period for accurate recognition of the plant's key features.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Senna alata belongs to the genus Senna, which is closely related to species like Senna tora and Senna occidentalis. It shares familial traits with other Fabaceae members, such as compound leaves and pod fruits, but differs in leaf shape and flower structure. Its phylogenetic placement highlights its evolutionary ties within the legume family.
