Golden Shower Tree (Senna alata) Compounds

The primary compounds in Golden Shower Tree include flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and coumarin, known for its anticoagulant effects. Tannins and saponins contribute to astringency and potential antimicrobial activity. Phenolic acids and glycosides offer antioxidant benefits, while terpenoids and essential oils may support immune function. Alkaloids and anthocyanins also play roles in the plant's medicinal properties.

Golden Shower Tree (Senna alata) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Senna alata, known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. They also support overall immune function and may aid in wound healing.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Senna alata. It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural anticoagulant, aiding in blood circulation. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Senna alata in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Senna alata, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating diarrhea and skin conditions. Tannins also help in reducing inflammation and may support wound healing. This compound plays a significant role in the therapeutic applications of Senna alata.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Golden Shower Tree (Senna alata). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic effects by exhibiting antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and larvicidal properties. Saponin's ability to disrupt cell membranes makes it valuable in herbal medicine for treating infections and parasitic infestations.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Golden Shower Tree (Senna alata). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps in reducing oxidative stress and supporting immune function. Phenolic acid is widely studied for its potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in the Golden Shower Tree (Senna alata). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in treating skin conditions and as a laxative. Glycosides in Senna alata are responsible for its therapeutic effects due to their ability to stimulate bowel movements and reduce inflammation.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Senna alata contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are commonly used in traditional medicine for treating skin infections and digestive issues. Terpenoids enhance the plant's therapeutic value in herbal formulations.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Senna alata contains bioactive compounds like sennosides and flavonoids. It is traditionally used for its laxative properties and anti-inflammatory effects. The oil is extracted from the leaves and has been studied for its potential in treating digestive disorders. Its therapeutic use is supported by traditional herbal medicine practices.

9. Alkaloid

Senna alata contains the alkaloid sennoside, which is known for its laxative properties. This compound stimulates bowel movements by increasing intestinal motility. It is commonly used in herbal medicine to treat constipation and other digestive issues. Sennoside is one of the primary active compounds responsible for the plant's therapeutic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Senna alata, known for its antioxidant properties. It contributes to the plant's medicinal uses by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation. This compound is also responsible for the plant's vibrant flower colors. Anthocyanin supports overall health and is a key component in traditional herbal remedies.
