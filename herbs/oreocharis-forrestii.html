Oreocharis forrestii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oreocharis forrestii has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and ciliate edges that grow from a perennial herbaceous habit.

Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in a cyme formation. The corolla is characterized by a specific color pattern and a sympetalous structure typical of the Gesneriaceae family.
