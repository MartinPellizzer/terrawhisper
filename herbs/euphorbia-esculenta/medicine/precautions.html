10 Precautions To Take When Using Euphorbia Esculenta (Wild Potato)

Euphorbia esculenta has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as proper identification to avoid confusion with toxic lookalikes.

It's also essential to note potential allergic reactions, especially in individuals with latex allergies, as the sap of Euphorbia esculenta contains allergenic compounds.

Failure to identify the plant correctly or ignoring allergic sensitivities can lead to side effects like skin irritation, digestive issues, or, in severe cases, anaphylaxis.

This article explains in details the 10 most important precautions to take when using Euphorbia esculenta medicinally.

5. Avoid using during pregnancy

When using Euphorbia esculenta medicinally, it's important to monitor blood pressure closely.

This is because the plant contains saponins and anthraquinones, which can cause a significant drop in blood pressure when consumed. If left unchecked, this could lead to dizziness, lightheadedness, or even fainting.

Additionally, individuals with pre-existing low blood pressure or cardiovascular conditions should be particularly cautious, as Euphorbia esculenta may exacerbate their condition.
