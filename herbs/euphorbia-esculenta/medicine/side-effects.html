10 Possible Side Effects Of Euphorbia Esculenta

Euphorbia esculenta has some side effects when used improperly, such as diarrhea, stomach cramps, and nausea.

These issues arise from its high levels of toxic compounds, such as latex sap, which can cause irritation and allergic reactions.

If left untreated, these symptoms can worsen, leading to dehydration, fatigue, and malnutrition, ultimately affecting one's daily activities, work, and relationships.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Euphorbia esculenta causes allergic reactions when consumed due to the presence of allergenic proteins in its sap and seeds.

The sap contains a chemical called saponin, which can trigger an immune response in sensitive individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the seeds contain a protein called globulin, which is also known to cause allergic reactions.

2. Triggers skin irritation

Euphorbia esculenta triggers skin irritation due to its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds such as euphorsin and isocrotonol.

When the sap comes into contact with skin, it can cause redness, itching, and inflammation.

Additionally, the plant's latex-like substance may also contribute to skin irritation, leading to discomfort and potentially even allergic reactions in some individuals.

3. Induces gastrointestinal upset

Euphorbia esculenta induces gastrointestinal upset due to its high concentration of saponins and alkaloids.

These compounds can irritate the mucous membranes in the stomach and intestines, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal cramps.

The exact mechanism of this action is not fully understood, but it is believed that the irritant properties of these compounds disrupt the normal functioning of the digestive system, causing adverse effects on the gut.

4. Provokes respiratory distress

Euphorbia esculenta provokes respiratory distress due to the presence of toxic compounds such as euphorbol and 12-ketonejatrophone.

These potent sapotoxins can cause severe inflammation in the lungs, leading to symptoms like coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the high levels of calcium oxalate crystals present in the plant can exacerbate respiratory issues by irritating airways and causing constriction of airway passages.

5. Leads to headache formation

Euphorbia esculenta leads to headache formation due to its potential allergenic and irritant properties.

The milky sap of the plant contains toxic compounds like latex and alkaloids, which can cause skin irritation and allergic reactions when ingested or come into contact with mucous membranes.

This may trigger a series of chemical reactions in the body, ultimately leading to the development of headaches as a symptom.

6. Increases blood pressure

Euphorbia esculenta increases blood pressure due to its high content of saponins and alkaloids.

These compounds can cause a rise in blood pressure by stimulating the heart to beat faster and stronger, thereby increasing cardiac output. Additionally, the saponins may also constrict blood vessels, further contributing to the increase in blood pressure.

This effect is likely due to the plant's natural defense mechanisms, which are intended to deter predators and compete with other plants for resources.

7. Enhances cardiac arrhythmia

Euphorbia esculenta enhances cardiac arrhythmia due to its ability to interact with certain medications and alter heart rhythms.

The saponins present in the plant can stimulate the heart muscle, increasing the frequency of contractions and potentially leading to abnormal heartbeats.

Additionally, Euphorbia esculenta's ability to lower blood pressure may also contribute to cardiac arrhythmia by reducing the heart's workload and altering its natural rhythm.

8. Reduces kidney function

Euphorbia esculenta reduces kidney function.

This occurs due to its high concentration of saponins and alkaloids, which can impair the kidneys' ability to filter waste and excess fluids from the blood.

The toxic compounds in Euphorbia esculenta may also cause inflammation and damage to the renal tubules, leading to a decline in kidney function over time.

9. Disrupts thyroid hormones

Euphorbia esculenta disrupts thyroid hormones by interfering with the body's natural production of triiodothyronine (T3) and thyroxine (T4).

Its bioactive compounds, such as saponins and alkaloids, have been shown to suppress thyroid function, potentially leading to changes in metabolism, energy levels, and weight regulation.

This disruption can also cause fluctuations in body temperature, heart rate, and menstrual cycles.

10. Affects liver enzymes

Euphorbia esculenta affects liver enzymes when used.

This is due to the presence of toxic compounds such as saponins and resins in its tubers. These compounds can interact with liver enzymes, potentially leading to changes in their activity and function.

As a result, individuals may experience alterations in their liver's ability to process and metabolize certain substances, which can have various effects on overall health.
