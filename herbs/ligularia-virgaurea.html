Ligularia virgaurea

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia virgaurea?

Ligularia virgaurea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually falling under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, this plant is categorized under the genus Ligularia, with its specific designation being the species virgaurea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia virgaurea has large, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure up to 20 centimeters in length and feature coarsely serrated margins with a distinctively rugose, textured surface. The foliage is typically bright green and grows on long, sturdy petioles emerging from a rhizomatous base. During the flowering season, the plant produces upright, terminal panicles of bright yellow ray florets. These composite flower heads are surrounded by smaller disc florets and sit atop thick, hairy stems. The overall structure of the plant is robust, often reaching heights of one meter in favorable conditions.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 1-methyl-3-(1-methylethyl)-benzene, 4-methyl-1-(1-methylethyl)-3-cyclohexen-1-ol, alpha-cadinol, crotonic acid, and trans-1-methyl-4-(1-methylethyl)-2-cyclohexen-1-ol. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile of Ligularia virgata. Research into these substances often focuses on their potential therapeutic properties and biological activities. The presence of these diverse organic molecules highlights the complex chemical makeup of the species. Understanding these compounds is essential for exploring the plant's traditional uses in medicine.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", the essential oils of Ligularia virgaurea were analyzed using steam distillation and GC capillary columns. A total of 41 components were identified through GC-MS, which accounted for 72.73% of the essential oils. The main component identified was 4-methyl-1-(1-methylethyl)-3-cyclohexen-1-ol at 14.369%. Other major components included crotonic acid, 2,2-dimethyl-butanoic acid, 1-methyl-3-(1-methylethyl)-benzene, (1s-endo)-1,7,7-trimethyl-bicyclo[2,2,1]heptan-2-ol, trans-1-methyl-4-(1-methylethyl)-2-cyclohexen-1-ol, alpha-cadinol, and alpha,alpha,4-trimethyl-3-cyclohexene-1-methanol.
