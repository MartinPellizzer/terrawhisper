What's the taxonomical classification of Caragana franchetiana?

Caragana franchetiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, which is further categorized into the genus Caragana, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species franchetiana.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Caragana franchetiana can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Some individuals may experience redness, itching, or swelling upon direct contact with the plant's foliage or sap. Ingesting parts of the plant can lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain depending on the quantity consumed. While widespread toxicity is not extensively documented in human medical literature, caution is necessary when handling its tissues. Always use gloves to minimize the risk of a localized dermatological reaction.
