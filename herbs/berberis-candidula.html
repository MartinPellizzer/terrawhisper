Berberis candidula

What's the taxonomical classification of Berberis candidula?

Berberis candidula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic placement continues within the order Ranunculales and the family Berberidaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Berberis, and its specific species epithet, candidula.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berberis candidula has erect or ascending woody shrubs that typically reach heights of 0.5 to 1.5 meters and feature small, simple, entire-margined leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The foliage is characterized by a bright green color and a texture that can vary from smooth to slightly leathery. Sharp, single spines are located at the base of each leaf, serving as a primary defensive mechanism for the plant. Its flowers are small and pale, often appearing in clusters along the stems during the blooming season. The plant eventually produces small, fleshy berries that contribute to its overall reproductive morphology.
