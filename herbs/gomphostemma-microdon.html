What's the taxonomical classification of Gomphostemma microdon?

Gomphostemma microdon belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae, eventually placing the plant in the genus Gomphostemma. Its specific biological identity is finalized as the species microdon.

These interactions occur primarily through its rich content of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and phenylethanoid glycosides. By modulating the COX-2 pathway, the plant effectively reduces the production of prostaglandins that drive pain and swelling. Furthermore, its ability to suppress iNOS expression helps decrease nitric oxide levels, which mitigates oxidative stress and cellular damage. These multi-target molecular mechanisms collectively underpin the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and febrile conditions.
