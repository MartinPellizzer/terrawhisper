Melampyrum roseum

What's the taxonomical classification of Melampyrum roseum?

Melampyrum roseum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Melampyrum, with the specific designation of the species roseum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Melampyrum roseum has an erect, branched stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and features small, opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins.

These floral structures are arranged in terminal racemes, often appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems. The leaves are generally sessile and can vary in size depending on their position along the stem.
