What's the taxonomical classification of Ribes burejense?

Ribes burejense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Saxifragales. This plant is a member of the family Grossulariaceae, which falls under the genus Ribes. Finally, the specific identification of this organism is completed by its species designation, burejense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ribes burejense has deciduous, opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically three to five-lobed with serrated margins and a pubescent texture.

The plant grows as a small shrub characterized by woody stems that may exhibit slight pubescence in younger growth. Its inflorescence consists of small, pendulous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These flowers are often characterized by a tubular shape and a specific color palette typical of the Ribes genus.

The fruit develops into small berries that hang from the branches following successful pollination.
