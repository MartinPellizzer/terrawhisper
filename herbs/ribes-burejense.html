Ribes burejense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ribes burejense has deciduous, opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically three to five-lobed with serrated margins and a pubescent texture. The plant grows as a small shrub characterized by woody stems that may exhibit slight pubescence in younger growth. Its inflorescence consists of small, pendulous flowers that emerge from the axils of the leaves. These flowers are often characterized by a tubular shape and a specific color palette typical of the Ribes genus. The fruit develops into small berries that hang from the branches following successful pollination.
