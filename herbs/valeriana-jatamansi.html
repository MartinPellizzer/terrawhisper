Jatamansi

Valeriana jatamansi

Jatamansi (Valeriana jatamansi) is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent, known for its calming effects and traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine to treat anxiety, insomnia, and nervous disorders.

Botanical Identification

Jatamansi, scientifically known as Valeriana jatamansi, belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family. It is also called Jatamansi or Jatamansi root in regional contexts. A perennial herb native to the Himalayas, it features aromatic roots, ovate leaves, and small white flowers. Its synonyms include Valeriana officinalis and Valeriana jatamansi.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Dipsacales
  • Family: Caprifoliaceae
  • Genus: Valeriana
  • Species: Valeriana jatamansi

Active Compounds

Jatamansi contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, terpenoids, and alkaloids. These compounds work together to exert calming and sedative effects by influencing neurotransmitter activity. Their synergistic action enhances the herb's traditional use in treating anxiety and insomnia.

  • Terpenoid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for anxiety and insomnia due to its calming properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and nervous disorders.

Tincture: Used for sleep disorders and stress-related ailments.

Safety Profile

Jatamansi is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to potential risks. Individuals with a known allergy to valerian or related plants should avoid its use.
