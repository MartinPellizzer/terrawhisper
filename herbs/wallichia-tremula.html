    What is Wallichia tremula?

    Wallichia tremula is a species of palm tree in the family Arecaceae.

    It has a solitary stem, up to 20 meters tall, with a greyish-brown trunk that is smooth or slightly scaly. The leaves are pinnate, 2-3 meters long, with 50-70 leaflets that are lanceolate to linear in shape. The inflorescence is a large panicle, up to 2 meters long, bearing numerous small flowers.

    The fruit is a syncarp, composed of multiple follicles, each containing a single seed.
