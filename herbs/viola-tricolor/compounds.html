Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor) Compounds

The primary compounds in Three-Color Violet include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and anthocyanins, responsible for its vibrant colors. Phenolic acids and tannins contribute to its astringent taste and potential anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin and glycosides may support its traditional use in herbal remedies.

Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds support skin health and may aid in reducing oxidative stress. Flavonoids are commonly used in herbal medicine for their therapeutic benefits.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, which may support immune function and reduce inflammation. This compound is commonly found in the petals and leaves, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal remedies.

3. Tannin

Tannin in Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe digestive issues. It also supports wound healing by promoting tissue repair and reducing microbial growth. Tannins are abundant in the leaves and flowers, making them a key component in traditional herbal preparations.

4. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor). It contributes to the plant's vibrant colors and possesses antioxidant properties. This compound supports skin health and may aid in reducing inflammation. Anthocyanin is often used in herbal remedies for its therapeutic benefits.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor). It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and mild sedative properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural anticoagulant, supporting cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and nervous system support.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Glycosides in Viola tricolor are often responsible for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. They are typically metabolized into simpler sugars for biological activity.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. 8. Essential oil

Essential oil from Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor) contains volatile compounds like limonene and geraniol. These oils are extracted through steam distillation and contribute to the plant's aromatic properties. They are used in herbal medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

9. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. They also play a role in the plant's aroma and may enhance the efficacy of other active components in herbal formulations.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Three-Color Violet (Viola tricolor). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. Alkaloids in Viola tricolor are often used in traditional herbal medicine to support respiratory and skin health.
