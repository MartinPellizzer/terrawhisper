Lablab purpureus

Lablab (Lablab purpureus) is a leguminous plant native to Africa and widely cultivated for its edible seeds and medicinal properties, traditionally used to improve conditions such as hypertension, diabetes, and digestive disorders.

Morphology

Lablab purpureus is a leguminous plant characterized by its erect, branching stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, trifoliate leaves with stipules, and clusters of purple or pink pea-like flowers that develop into elongated, fibrous pods containing 4-6 angular, flat seeds.

Distribution

Lablab purpureus is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly southern Africa, and is also found in parts of southern Asia, including India, Nepal, and Sri Lanka.

Identification

Lablab purpureus, commonly known as hyacinth bean or urad dal, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and edible, round, green seeds.

Lablab purpureus is closely related to other legumes in the Fabaceae family, including species such as Vigna angularis (adzuki bean), Vigna radiata (mung bean), and Phaseolus vulgaris (common bean), which share similar botanical characteristics and are often used in traditional medicine for their nutritional and therapeutic properties.
