Lablab purpureus

Lablab (Lablab purpureus) is a leguminous plant native to Africa and widely cultivated for its edible seeds and medicinal properties, traditionally used to improve conditions such as hypertension, diabetes, and digestive disorders.

Lablab purpureus is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and showy purple flowers.

Morphology

Lablab purpureus is a leguminous plant characterized by its erect, branching stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, trifoliate leaves with stipules, and clusters of purple or pink pea-like flowers that develop into elongated, fibrous pods containing 4-6 angular, flat seeds.

Distribution

Lablab purpureus is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly southern Africa, and is also found in parts of southern Asia, including India, Nepal, and Sri Lanka.

Traditional Uses

Lablab (Lablab purpureus) has been traditionally used in various herbal systems to support digestion, reduce inflammation, and promote overall vitality.

Medicinal Systems

Lablab purpureus has been utilized in traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and African traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

Lablab (Lablab purpureus) contains bioactive compounds such as alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and polysaccharides, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic effects.

Pharmacology

Lablab purpureus exhibits various pharmacological properties including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, antidiabetic, and immunomodulatory effects, which contribute to its traditional use in treating conditions such as diabetes, inflammation, and respiratory disorders.

Botanical Identification

Lablab purpureus, commonly known as hyacinth bean or lablab, belongs to the Fabaceae family. It is an annual legume native to Africa and southern Asia. The plant has compound leaves, purple flowers, and edible pods. It is also referred to as "khesari" in some regions and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Safety Profile

Lablab purpureus is generally safe when consumed in moderate amounts as a food or herbal remedy. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised for those with kidney conditions or during pregnancy.
