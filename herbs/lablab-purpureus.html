Lablab purpureus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lablab purpureus?

Lablab purpureus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it among the legumes, and is specifically identified within the genus Lablab as the species purpureus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lablab purpureus has a climbing or trailing habit with trifoliate leaves consisting of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets and papilionaceous flowers that are typically purple or white. The stems are herbaceous and can reach significant lengths through the use of twining mechanisms. Each leaf features a small stipule at the base of the petiole and a fine pubescence on the surface. The inflorescences are arranged in racemes that emerge from the leaf axils. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds characterized by a hard, smooth coat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lablab purpureus?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, hypoglycemic, hypolipidemic, and hepatoprotective pharmacological activities that contribute to its therapeutic potential. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress within the body's cells. The plant demonstrates hypoglycemic effects by assisting in the regulation of blood glucose levels, which is crucial for managing diabetes. Furthermore, its hypolipidemic action supports cardiovascular health by helping to lower lipid concentrations in the bloodstream. Finally, its hepatoprotective qualities serve to shield the liver from damage caused by toxins or disease.

According to a study published by "Scientific reports", the crude methanolic extract of Lablab purpureus seeds exhibited potent antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and cytotoxic activities. Antioxidant activity was demonstrated using the DPPH, hydroxyl radical scavenging, ferric oxide reducing, and ABTS radical reducing assays. Anti-inflammatory activity was confirmed through RBC membrane stabilization and protein denaturation assays. The extract exhibited significant in vitro cytotoxicity towards MCF-7 and A549 cell lines, causing apoptosis and cell cycle arrests at the S and G2/M phases. Furthermore, the extract showed minimal cytotoxicity towards normal HEK-293 cell lines. Molecular docking using Schrodinger v14.1 identified ligands like N-Acetyl-D-glucosamine forming stable complexes with proteins to indicate potent anti-breast and anti-lung cancer agents.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating amoebiasis, diarrhea, loose stools, malnourishment, and stomach weakness. The seeds and leaves provide essential nutrients that help combat the effects of malnourishment in various populations. Its medicinal properties help stabilize the digestive system to reduce the frequency of loose stools. Furthermore, the plant acts as a remedy to soothe stomach weakness and restore intestinal health. These combined benefits make it a valuable resource for managing gastrointestinal distress and amoebiasis.

According to a study published by "Parasitology research", extracts from Lablab purpureus L. showed bioefficacy against specific causal agents. The research focused on the plant's ability to target health conditions including malaria, dracunculiasis, and amoebiasis. Six different rotenoids were isolated and identified from the plant, specifically deguelin, dehydrodeguelin, rotenol, rotenone, tephrosin, and sumatrol. These six compounds were quantified both in vivo and in vitro to determine their effectiveness. The study analyzed various plant parts, finding the maximum rotenoid content in the roots and the minimum in the seeds.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe cyanide poisoning if the seeds are consumed raw or improperly processed. This toxic reaction often manifests through immediate gastrointestinal distress such as nausea and vomiting. Individuals may also suffer from significant abdominal pain during the onset of symptoms. Chronic or acute exposure can lead to debilitating diarrhea as the body reacts to the toxins. Proper cooking and soaking are essential steps to neutralize these dangerous compounds.

The main side effects this plant can have are shown in the list below.

  • Cyanide poisoning
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Abdominal pain
  • Diarrhea
