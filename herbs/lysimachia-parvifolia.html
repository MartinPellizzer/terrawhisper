Lysimachia parvifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia parvifolia has small, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically one to two centimeters long and possess entire margins. The stems are slender and can grow in an erect or ascending habit depending on the specific habitat. Its flowers are characterized by five bright yellow petals that form a star-like shape when fully bloomed. The inflorescence often emerges from the leaf axils, presenting small clusters of blossoms. This plant is distinguished by its diminutive stature and delicate foliage compared to other members of the primrose family.
