Sanicula serrata

What's the taxonomical classification of Sanicula serrata?

Sanicula serrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, eventually placing it in the order Apiales. As a member of the family Apiaceae, it is further categorized under the genus Sanicula, with its specific identification being the species serrata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sanicula serrata has opposite, petiolate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape with sharply serrated margins and prominent venation. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a rugose texture with deep veins that create a wrinkled appearance. These plants feature slender, upright stems that support small, inconspicuous flower clusters known as umbels. Each individual flower is tiny and typically possesses five petals. The plant also produces small, bristly fruits that are characteristic of the genus.
