Salvia maximowicziana

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia maximowicziana?

Salvia maximowicziana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by its distinct morphological traits. Within this family, the plant is placed in the genus Salvia, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, maximowicziana.

    What are the herbal preparations of Salvia maximowicziana?

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Salvia maximowicziana can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if ingested or handled improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many members of the Salvia genus contain bioactive compounds like terpenoids that may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. If the plant is consumed, it could potentially lead to nausea or stomach upset due to its chemical composition. People with known allergies to the Lamiaceae family should exercise extreme caution when coming into contact with this species. Always consult a medical professional or toxicologist if an adverse reaction occurs after exposure.
