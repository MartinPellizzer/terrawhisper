Salvia maximowicziana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvoria maximowicziana has upright, square-shaped stems that reach heights of approximately 40 to 80 centimeters and support opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a slightly rugose texture and a grayish-green hue that provides a backdrop for the flowering spikes. Inflorescences consist of dense terminal racemes containing numerous two-lipped flowers that typically display shades of violet or light blue. These flowers feature a prominent lower lip and are supported by persistent, often slightly hairy calyces. The overall structure of the plant is herbaceous and robust, suited for its native woodland environments.
