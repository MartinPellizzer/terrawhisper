Deparia pycnosora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Deparia pycnosora has a creeping rhizome and pinnately compound fronds that typically reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The pinnae are arranged oppositely along the rachis and exhibit a distinctively narrow, lanceolate shape. Each leaflet features finely serrated margins and acute tips that contribute to its delicate appearance. The sori are positioned on the underside of the pinnules, protected by relatively small, rounded indusia. These morphological characteristics distinguish the species from other members of the Deparia genus found in similar habitats.
