Pertya discolor

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pertya discolor has large, elliptical leaves that are characterized by a dark green upper surface and a distinctively reddish or copper-colored underside. These leaves are arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems of this perennial herb. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Its stems are fleshy and can grow quite robustly in tropical environments. The overall structure is lush and dense, creating a striking visual contrast due to the bicolor foliage.
