Tamarix hohenackeri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tamarix hohenackeri has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in an opposite or whorled pattern along slender, woody stems. These tiny leaves function to minimize water loss in arid environments by reducing surface area. The plant typically exhibits a multi-stemmed, shrubby growth habit with branches that can become quite dense. Its flowers are minute and pinkish to white, clustered in dense spikes along the branchlets. The overall structure is characterized by a fine, feathery texture caused by the intricate branching and reduced foliage.
