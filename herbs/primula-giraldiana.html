Primula giraldiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Primula giraldiana?

Primula giraldiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the hierarchy of biological classification, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further into its taxonomic lineage, the plant is part of the order Ericales and falls under the family Primulaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by the genus Primula and is specifically designated as the species giraldiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula giraldiana has a basal rosette of long-handled, oblanceolate leaves that are dark green, wrinkled, and possess serrated margins. The flowering stems emerge from the center of this foliage, typically reaching heights of fifteen to twenty-five centimeters. Each scape supports a terminal umbel containing several fragrant, salverform flowers. These blossoms display a distinctive coloration, often featuring pale pink or lavender petals with a bright yellow eye at the center. The overall structure of the plant is compact yet robust, suited for its natural alpine habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude limestone regions of the Yunnan Province in southwestern China. It thrives specifically within the subalpine zones of the Hengduan Mountains. These populations are often found growing in rocky crevices and moist alpine meadows at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 3,500 meters. The localized nature of its habitat means it is restricted to specific mountain ranges rather than being widely dispersed across the continent. Consequently, its range is quite narrow and tied to the unique microclimates of this mountainous terrain.
