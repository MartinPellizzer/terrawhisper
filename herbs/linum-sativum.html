Flax (Linum Sativum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Linum sativum.

Flax, scientifically known as Linum sativum, is a versatile medicinal herb cultivated for its nutrient-rich seeds and fiber. It is widely recognized for its primary benefits, including high omega-3 fatty acid content, fiber, and lignans, which support heart health, digestive wellness, and hormonal balance. Traditionally used in ancient Egypt, Greece, and China for its healing properties, flax has been employed to treat ailments such as inflammation, constipation, and skin conditions. In modern wellness practices, it is valued as a natural source of plant-based protein and as a component in herbal remedies for stress relief and immune support. One of its unique features is the presence of alpha-linolenic acid (ALA), a rare essential fatty acid, and its historical significance as one of the earliest domesticated crops.
