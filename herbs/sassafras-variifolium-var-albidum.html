White Sassafras (Sassafras Variifolium Var. Albidum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Sassafras variifolium var. albidum.

*Sassafras variifolium* var. *albidum*, commonly known as white sassafras, has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties due to its rich content of bioactive compounds such as safrole and essential oils. Historically, it has been employed in herbal medicine to treat ailments like digestive issues, skin conditions, and respiratory infections, owing to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. However, the presence of safrole, which is classified as a potential carcinogen, has raised concerns about its safety for long-term use. Despite these risks, some indigenous communities and alternative medicine practitioners still utilize it in controlled preparations for specific therapeutic purposes. Research continues to explore its potential benefits while emphasizing the need for caution and further scientific validation.
