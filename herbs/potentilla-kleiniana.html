Potentilla kleiniana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella kleiniana has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are palmately divided into five to seven deeply lobed, serrated leaflets with a hairy underside. The plant produces erect, slender stems that bear smaller, less complex leaves at various intervals along their length. Its flowers are typically bright yellow, featuring five distinct petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens and pistils. The fruit consists of small, dry achenes that are tightly packed within a fleshy receptacle. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in moist, montane meadow environments.
