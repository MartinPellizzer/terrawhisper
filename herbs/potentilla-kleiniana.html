What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla kleiniana?

Potentiilla kleiniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its lineage further, it falls under the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Potentiella, and its species name, kleiniana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella kleiniana has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are palmately divided into five to seven deeply lobed, serrated leaflets with a hairy underside. Its flowers are typically bright yellow, featuring five distinct petals surrounding a central cluster of numerous stamens and pistils. The fruit consists of small, dry achenes that are tightly packed within a fleshy receptacle. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in moist, montane meadow environments.
