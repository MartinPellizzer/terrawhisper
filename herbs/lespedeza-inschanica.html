Lespedeza inschanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lespedeza inschanica has trifoliate leaves with small, ovate to elliptical leaflets that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The stems are slender and often exhibit a somewhat woody texture as they mature. Its flowers are small and pea-like, usually appearing in clusters along the upper parts of the branches. The plant also produces small, flat seed pods that follow the flowering stage. These morphological features help distinguish the species from other members of the Fabaceae family.
