What's the taxonomical classification of Lespedeza inschanica?

Lespedeza inschanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Fabales, it falls within the prominent family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its diverse group of flowering plants. Specifically, this plant is identified by its genus, Lespedeza, and is further distinguished by its unique species epithet, inschanica.

What side-effects this plant can have?

While specific toxicological data on humans is limited, many species within the Lespedezetrum genus contain chemical compounds that may trigger hypersensitivity in sensitive individuals. Ingesting parts of the plant might lead to gastrointestinal distress, including nausea or stomach cramping. It is important to exercise caution when handling the foliage to avoid potential irritation to the eyes or mucous membranes. Always consult a medical professional if any adverse reaction occurs after exposure to this species.
