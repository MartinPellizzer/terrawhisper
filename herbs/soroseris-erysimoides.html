Soroseris erysimoides

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Soroseris erysimoides has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal rosettes of pinnatifid leaves and erect, branched flowering stems that produce small, yellowish-white heads. The leaves are deeply lobed with narrow segments that create a fine, feathery texture throughout the foliage. Its inflorescence consists of numerous compact capitula arranged in terminal clusters. Each flower head is relatively small and possesses a discoid structure typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant typically reaches a modest height, maintaining a compact form suitable for its alpine or subalpine habitats.
