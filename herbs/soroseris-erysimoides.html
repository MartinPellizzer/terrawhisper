Soroseris erysimoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Soroseris, specifically identified as the species erysimoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Soroseris erysimoides has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal rosettes of pinnatifid leaves and erect, branched flowering stems that produce small, yellowish-white heads. Each flower head is relatively small and possesses a discoid structure typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant typically reaches a modest height, maintaining a compact form suitable for its alpine or subalpine habitats.
