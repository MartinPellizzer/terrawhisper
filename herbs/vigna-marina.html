Vigna marina

What's the taxonomical classification of Vigna marina?

Vigna marina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression through the order Fabales, it is placed in the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Vigna, and its unique species designation, marina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vigna marina has procumbent to ascending stems that form dense mats, trifoliate leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets, and small, pale yellow pea-like flowers. The foliage typically features smooth, entire margins and a slightly fleshy texture adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stems, often presenting a dull green color on the upper surface. The inflorescence consists of small axillary racemes that produce delicate, zygomorphic blossoms. These plants frequently develop a robust root system to stabilize themselves in sandy or saline substrates.
