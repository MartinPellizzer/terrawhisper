Vigna marina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vigna marina has procumbent to ascending stems that form dense mats, trifoliate leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets, and small, pale yellow pea-like flowers. The foliage typically features smooth, entire margins and a slightly fleshy texture adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stems, often presenting a dull green color on the upper surface. The inflorescence consists of small axillary racemes that produce delicate, zygomorphic blossoms. These plants frequently develop a robust root system to stabilize themselves in sandy or saline substrates.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the coastal regions of the Indian Ocean, ranging from the shores of East Africa to Southeast Asia and Australia. It specifically inhabits sandy beaches and salt marshes where it can tolerate high salinity levels. This species is frequently found in tropical and subtropical maritime environments across the Indo-Pacific realm. Its distribution is closely tied to low-lying coastal habitats that receive seasonal tidal influences. Through its unique adaptations, it thrives in these niche saline ecosystems along much of the warm coastline.
