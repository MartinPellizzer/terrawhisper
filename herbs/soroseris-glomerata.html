Soroseris glomerata

What's the taxonomical classification of Soroseris glomerata?

Soroseris glomerata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is part of the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Soroseris, with its specific designation being the species glomerata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Soroseris glomerata has a perennial growth habit characterized by a woody caudex and leaves that are pinnately divided into numerous narrow, linear segments. The foliage typically exhibits a fine, feathery texture due to the deeply dissected nature of the leaflets. Inflorescences consist of small, clustered heads arranged in dense, terminal corymbose structures. The flower heads feature small, yellowish to white disc florets that lack prominent ray florets. These compact clusters are supported by slender, branched peduncles that arise from the base of the plant.
