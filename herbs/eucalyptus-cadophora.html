Eucalyptus cadophora

Eucalyptus cadophora, commonly known as the cadaga or narrow-leaved peppermint gum, is a species of tree in the family Myrtaceae native to Australia.

This plant is valued for its essential oil, which is rich in bioactive constituents that provide numerous health benefits, including relief from respiratory issues, such as congestion and coughs, due to its expectorant and decongestant properties.

The therapeutic actions of E. cadophora include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it useful in treating skin conditions, wounds, and other health issues.

The bioactive constituents of E. cadophora include eucalyptol, alpha-pinene, and beta-pinene, among others, which contribute to its medicinal and therapeutic properties.
