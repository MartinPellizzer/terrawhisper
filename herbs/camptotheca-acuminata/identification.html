Botanical Identification of Chinese Fringe Tree (Camptotheca acuminata)

To identify Chinese Fringe Tree (Camptotheca acuminata) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Chinese Fringe Tree, Camptotheca acuminata, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Fagales, family Nyssaceae, genus Camptotheca, and species Camptotheca acuminata. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of the Chinese Fringe Tree is Camptotheca acuminata, first described by the botanist Henry Francis Baker in 1873. This name is widely accepted in modern botanical literature, though it has several historical synonyms and common names used in different regions.

Morphological Characteristics

The Chinese Fringe Tree is a deciduous tree with a spreading growth habit. Its root system is deep and fibrous, providing stability. The stem is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing fissures with age. Leaves are alternate, ovate with serrated margins, and have a dark green upper surface. The inflorescence consists of clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, winged seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Camptotheca acuminata can be distinguished by its distinctive pendulous, bottle-shaped fruits that turn red when mature, and its opposite, ovate leaves with smooth margins. It also has a unique, aromatic bark that peels in thin layers, setting it apart from similar species like Fraxinus.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Camptotheca acuminata is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. The tree prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Camptotheca acuminata blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, seeds are best harvested in late fall when they are fully mature and easily separated from the plant.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Camptotheca acuminata belongs to the family Nyssaceae, closely related to species like Fraxinus and Nyssa. It shares morphological and genetic traits with these genera, including compound leaves and drupes. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary link to other temperate hardwoods.
