Chinese Fringe Tree (Camptotheca acuminata) Uses

Camptotheca acuminata is used to treat various cancers, including lung cancer and leukemia, due to its anti-tumor properties. It also helps reduce inflammation and is applied in managing respiratory infections like tuberculosis, asthma, and chronic bronchitis. Its effects support immune function and may aid in treating influenza.

Chinese Fringe Tree (Camptotheca acuminata) Monograph

1. Cancer

Camptotheca acuminata, also known as Chinese Fringe Tree, contains potent anticancer compounds like camptothecin. It is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various cancers, including leukemia and lymphoma. Its alkaloids inhibit tumor growth by interfering with DNA replication. Clinical studies suggest it may enhance the effectiveness of conventional cancer therapies.

2. Malignant tumors

Camptotheca acuminata contains potent anticancer compounds like camptothecin, which inhibit tumor cell growth by interfering with DNA replication. It has been used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat various malignant tumors, including lung and liver cancers, though clinical use remains limited due to toxicity concerns.

3. Leukemia

Camptotheca acuminata contains compounds like camptothecin, which inhibit topoisomerase I, a key enzyme in DNA replication. This action disrupts cancer cell division, making it a potential treatment for leukemia. Research suggests it may reduce tumor growth and enhance the effectiveness of chemotherapy in leukemia patients.

4. Inflammation

The Chinese Fringe Tree is used in traditional herbal medicine to treat inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory compounds, such as camptothecin. It is often applied topically for skin inflammation or brewed into tea to reduce internal swelling and pain. Its properties make it a valued remedy in addressing various inflammatory conditions.

5. Respiratory infections

Camptotheca acuminata has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat respiratory infections due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce symptoms like coughing and congestion by supporting immune function and clearing lung congestion. The active compound, camptothecin, contributes to its efficacy in combating viral and bacterial respiratory pathogens.

6. Lung cancer

Camptotheca acuminata contains active compounds like camptothecin, which inhibit cancer cell growth. It has shown potential in treating lung cancer by targeting DNA replication. Traditional Chinese medicine uses it to support cancer therapy. Clinical studies suggest it may enhance the effectiveness of chemotherapy.

7. Chronic bronchitis

Camptotheca acuminata is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat chronic bronchitis due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus production and soothe respiratory irritation, supporting clearer breathing and easing symptoms in long-term bronchial conditions.

8. Tuberculosis

Camptotheca acuminata has been traditionally used in Chinese herbal medicine to treat tuberculosis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bark contains compounds that may inhibit Mycobacterium tuberculosis, though clinical evidence remains limited. It is often combined with other herbs to support respiratory health and reduce symptoms associated with the disease.

9. Asthma

Camptotheca acuminata has been traditionally used in Chinese herbal medicine to treat asthma due to its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties. It helps reduce airway inflammation and improve respiratory function, making it a valued remedy for managing asthma symptoms in traditional practice.

10. Influenza

Camptotheca acuminata has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat influenza due to its antiviral and immune-boosting properties. It helps reduce fever, alleviate coughing, and support respiratory health during viral infections. The plant's active compounds are believed to enhance the body's natural defenses against the flu.
