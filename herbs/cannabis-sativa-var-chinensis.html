Cannabis sativa var. chinensis

Cannabis sativa var. chinensis, also known as Chinese hemp, is a subspecies of the cannabis sativa plant, native to China and other parts of Asia.

It has been used for centuries in traditional Chinese medicine, valued for its medicinal properties, including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties, which have been shown to relieve pain, reduce inflammation, and prevent infection.

The plant is also rich in essential oils, flavonoids, and terpenes, which contribute to its health benefits, such as improving sleep quality, reducing anxiety, and boosting the immune system.

In herbal preparations, the leaves and flowers of Cannabis sativa var. chinensis are often used to make teas, tinctures, and salves, which are used to treat a range of health conditions, including arthritis, insomnia, and skin conditions.
