Polyalthia suberosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polyalthia suberosa has an evergreen habit characterized by woody stems, elliptical to lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The foliage typically exhibits a coriaceous texture and a glossy surface on the upper side, while the underside remains slightly paler. Its branching pattern is often spreading, contributing to a dense and bushy canopy structure. The fruit develops into small, fleshy drupes that change color as they reach maturity. These morphological traits are consistent with many members of the Annonaceae family.

      What are the herbal preparations of Polyalthia suberosa?

      Polyalthia suberosa is prepared primarily as a decoction of its bark, a powder from the dried leaves, or an infusion of the flowers to access its medicinal properties. The decoction method involves boiling the hard bark in water to extract concentrated alkaloids and tannins. For more delicate components, the leaves are often finely ground into a powder that can be ingested or applied topically. Some traditional practices also utilize the plant in the form of a juice extracted from fresh stem segments. These various preparations allow for the systematic delivery of the plant's bioactive compounds for different therapeutic uses.
