Allium chrysanthum

What's the taxonomical classification of Allium chrysanthum?

Allium chrysanthum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asparagales, it falls under the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Allium, with its specific identification being the species chrysanthum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium chrysanthum has upright, slender green stems that reach heights of approximately 30 to 50 centimeters and terminate in dense, spherical umbels composed of numerous bright yellow flowers. The basal leaves are linear, hollow, and grow directly from the bulb to provide a structural foundation for the plant. Each individual flower within the globe-shaped cluster features six spreading tepals that create a vibrant, star-like appearance. The underground bulb is relatively small and serves as the primary storage organ for nutrients. This morphology allows the plant to effectively display its colorful inflorescence to pollinators in its natural habitat.
