Lemon Balm

Melissa officinalis

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Botanical Identification

Lemon balm, scientifically known as Melissa officinalis, belongs to the Lamiaceae family. Common names include lemon grass and bee balm, though these are often confused with other plants. It is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant features aromatic leaves, small yellow flowers, and a spreading growth habit. Synonyms in herbal literature include Melissa aromatica and Melissa viridiflora.

Active Compounds

Lemon balm contains essential oils, flavonoids, and coumarin as its primary active compounds. These work synergistically to modulate neurotransmitters and reduce anxiety. The essential oils provide calming effects, while flavonoids and coumarin support anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and anxiety due to its calming and digestive properties.

Essential oil: Used for stress relief and respiratory support due to its aromatic and antiseptic properties.

Tincture: Used for nervous system disorders and digestive ailments due to its concentrated active compounds.

Safety Profile

Lemon balm is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks. Individuals with known allergies to the Lamiaceae family should also exercise caution.
