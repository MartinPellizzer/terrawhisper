What to know about Melissa officinalis before using it medicinally

Melissa officinalis, commonly known as sweet melissa, is a herb that has been traditionally used to treat a range of health conditions, including anxiety, insomnia, and digestive issues such as irritable bowel syndrome (IBS) and stomach ulcers.

The main health benefits associated with Melissa officinalis include its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties, which contribute to its therapeutic potential. The herb is also known for its calming and relaxing effects, which have been attributed to its anxiolytic and sedative properties. The main bioactive compounds found in Melissa officinalis include limonene, linalool, and rosmarinic acid, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

People often prepare Melissa officinalis as a tea or tincture to take advantage of its therapeutic benefits, which can be used to soothe the nervous system and promote relaxation.
