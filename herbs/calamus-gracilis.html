Calamus gracilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Calamus gracilis?

Calamus gracilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Arecales. This plant is a member of the family Arecaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Calamus, and is identified by its species name, gracilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calamus gracilis has slender, climbing stems that reach heights of up to 10 meters and are covered in dense, sharp spines. The leaves are large and pinnately compound, featuring numerous narrow leaflets arranged along a central axis. At the base of the leaf petioles, specialized climbing organs known as flagella emerge to help the plant anchor itself to surrounding vegetation. The plant also produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into bright red or orange drupes. These fruits are typically arranged in clusters and contain a single seed protected by a fleshy outer layer.
