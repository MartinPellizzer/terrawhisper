Marsdenia officinalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Marsdenia officinalis?

Marsdenia officiinalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent, the plant is placed in the order Gentianales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its membership in the genus Marsdenia, with its distinct designation being the species officiinalis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Marsdenia officinaliis has woody, climbing vines characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and a glossy surface. The stems are typically twining and can reach significant lengths as they ascend through surrounding vegetation. Small, tubular flowers emerge in clusters, often displaying a pale or creamy coloration typical of the Apocynaceae family. The plant produces elongated, somewhat woody follicles that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds equipped with tufts of hair. These morphological features collectively assist the plant in both structural support and effective reproduction within its native tropical habitats.
