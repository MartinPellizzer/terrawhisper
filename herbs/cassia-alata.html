Candle Bush

Cassia alata

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Candle Bush, or Cassia alata, is a shrub in the Fabaceae family. Known also as candlewood or yellow candle bush, it is a perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas. It has yellow flowers, compound leaves, and is often referred to by various regional names in herbal traditions.

Active Compounds

Cassia alata contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, coumarins, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to exert anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Coumarin
  • Tannin

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties.

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions like coughs and colds due to its expectorant properties.

Poultice: Used for topical application on wounds and skin irritations due to its anti-inflammatory effects.

Safety Profile

Candle bush is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause skin irritation in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or if you have existing health conditions.
