Maca

Lepidium meyenii

Maca (Lepidium meyenii) is a root vegetable native to the high altitude regions of Peru, traditionally used for its purported ability to enhance energy, stamina, and sexual function.

Botanical Identification

Maca (Lepidium meyenii) is a perennial plant native to the Peruvian Andes. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is also known as Peruvian ginseng. The plant has thick, fleshy roots, dark green leaves, and small yellow flowers. Common synonyms include "maca root" and "Lepidium meyenii var. meyenii."

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Spermatophyta
  • Class: caryophyllopsida
  • Order: Brassicales
  • Family: Brassicaceae
  • Genus: Lepidium
  • Species: Lepidium meyenii

Safety Profile

Maca is generally considered safe for most adults when taken as directed. However, it may cause mild side effects such as digestive discomfort or insomnia in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use if you have thyroid issues or are on medication.
