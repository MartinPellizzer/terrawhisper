Maca (Lepidium meyenii)

Maca (Lepidium meyenii) is a member of the Brassicaceae family, native to Andes Mountains, Peru, Peru, and South America. Traditionally, its roots, shoots, and leaves have been used for decoctions, culinary uses, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.
