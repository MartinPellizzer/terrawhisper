What to know about Lepidium meyenii (maca) before using it medicinally

Maca, scientifically known as Lepidium meyenii, is a Peruvian herb that has been touted for its numerous health benefits, improving overall wellness and vitality by addressing hormonal imbalances, energy deficiencies, and menopausal symptoms.

While it's often consumed as a culinary ingredient, adding depth and umami flavor to dishes such as soups and stir-fries, maca is also cultivated and harvested for its ornamental value, with its vibrant yellow flowers and edible stalks being prized in Andean gardens. Horticulturally speaking, maca thrives in high-altitude environments, requiring specific soil and climate conditions that make it a challenging crop to cultivate. Botanically, maca belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes plants such as broccoli and cauliflower, sharing similarities in its nutritional profile and potential health benefits.

With a history dating back thousands of years, maca has been revered by indigenous cultures for its medicinal and spiritual properties, with ancient records of its use found in Incan and Aztec texts.
