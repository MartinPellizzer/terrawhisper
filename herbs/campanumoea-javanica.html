Campanumoea javanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Campanumoea javanica has large, cordate to ovate leaves with entire margins and a glossy, dark green surface. The plant exhibits a woody, climbing habit characterized by slender stems that use adventitious roots to cling to host structures. Its flowers are relatively small and tubular, typically appearing in axillary clusters. The foliage is densely arranged along the vines, providing significant shade coverage. These morphological features allow the species to effectively colonize forest canopies in tropical environments.
