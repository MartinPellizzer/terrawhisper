Stellaria yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Stellaria yunnanensis?

Stellaria yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the taxonomic order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific genus, Stellaria, and its unique species designation, yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria yunnanensis has slender, herbaceous stems that branch frequently and bear small, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the length of the plant. The foliage is typically smooth with entire margins and a delicate, green appearance. Small, white flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by five distinct petals that are often slightly notched at the tips. These blossoms are supported by thin pedicels that allow them to sway easily in the wind. The root system consists of fine, fibrous structures that anchor the plant within its moist, mountainous habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Yunnan province of southwestern China. It grows primarily within the high-altitude montane regions of this specific territory. The species is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest margins or rocky crevices. Its distribution is largely restricted by the complex topography and climate of the Hengduan Mountains. Localized populations are often influenced by the unique soil compositions found in these mountainous zones.

What are the pharmacological activities of Stellaria yunnanensis?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its potent anti-renal fibrosis and cytotoxic activities. Research indicates that its extracts can mitigate the progression of kidney scarring by regulating inflammatory pathways and reducing collagen accumulation. Furthermore, the plant exhibits strong cytotoxic properties that allow it to inhibit the proliferation of various cancer cell lines. These specific biological effects suggest that its chemical constituents could serve as valuable leads for developing new therapeutic agents. Ongoing studies continue to investigate the precise molecular mechanisms driving these diverse medicinal responses.

As discussed in a study, an undescribed cyclic nonapeptide identified as cyclo-(Tyr-Ala-Thr-Leu-Thr-Ala-Phe-Ala-Thr) was isolated from the crude root extract of Stellaria yunnanensis Franch. This cyclic nonapeptide, referred to as compound 1, underwent structural elucidation through high-resolution mass spectrometry and one- and two-dimensional nuclear magnetic resonance spectroscopy. The study also utilized experimental and calculated circular dichroism spectroscopy to determine its structure. Evaluation of the anti-renal fibrosis activity of compounds 1-3 demonstrated potential for reducing the expression of the fibrotic protein collagen I. This effect was specifically observed in NRK-49F cells that were induced by TGF-β1.

As discussed in a study, a novel cytotoxic cyclic heptapeptide named yunnanin C (1) was isolated from the roots of Stellaria yunnanensis. The chemical structure of this compound is identified as cyclo(-Gly-Ile-Gly-Phe-Tyr-Ser-Pro-). This specific cyclic heptapeptide was elucidated through spectroscopic evidence and chemical degradation. The study identifies this singular compound as a medicinal component found within the plant.
