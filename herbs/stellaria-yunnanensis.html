Stellaria yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Stellaria yunnanensis?

Stellaria yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the taxonomic order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific genus, Stellaria, and its unique species designation, yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria yunnanensis has slender, herbaceous stems that branch frequently and bear small, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the length of the plant. The foliage is typically smooth with entire margins and a delicate, green appearance. Small, white flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by five distinct petals that are often slightly notched at the tips. These blossoms are supported by thin pedicels that allow them to sway easily in the wind. The root system consists of fine, fibrous structures that anchor the plant within its moist, mountainous habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Yunnan province of southwestern China. It grows primarily within the high-altitude montane regions of this specific territory. The species is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest margins or rocky crevices. Its distribution is largely restricted by the complex topography and climate of the Hengduan Mountains. What are the pharmacological activities of Stellaria yunnanensis?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-renal fibrosis and cytotoxic properties. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively inhibit the progression of kidney scarring by modulating inflammatory pathways. Furthermore, the plant exhibits potent cytotoxic effects that can target and induce death in specific cancer cell lines. These medicinal qualities suggest that its chemical constituents hold great potential for future drug development in nephrology and oncology. Scientists continue to investigate the specific mechanisms through which these extracts interact with cellular targets.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", an undescribed cyclic nonapeptide identified as cyclo-(Tyr-Ala-Thr-Leu-Thr-Ala-Phe-Ala-Thr) was isolated from the crude root extract of Stellaria yunnanensis Franch. This specific compound, known as Stellarin I or compound 1, was evaluated for its anti-renal fibrosis activity. The study showed that compounds 1-3 have potential for anti-renal fibrosis by reducing the expression of the fibrotic protein collagen I. This reduction occurred in NRK-49F cells that were induced by TGF-β1.
