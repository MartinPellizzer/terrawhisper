Stellaria yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Stellaria yunnanensis?

Stellaria yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant follows the taxonomic order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae. Finally, it is defined by its specific genus, Stellaria, and its unique species designation, yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria yunnanensis has slender, herbaceous stems that branch frequently and bear small, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the length of the plant. The foliage is typically smooth with entire margins and a delicate, green appearance. Small, white flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by five distinct petals that are often slightly notched at the tips. These blossoms are supported by thin pedicels that allow them to sway easily in the wind. The root system consists of fine, fibrous structures that anchor the plant within its moist, mountainous habitat.

The species is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest margins or rocky crevices. Its distribution is largely restricted by the complex topography and climate of the Hengduan Mountains.
