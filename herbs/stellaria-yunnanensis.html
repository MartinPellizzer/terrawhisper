Stellaria yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria yunnanensis has slender, herbaceous stems that branch frequently and bear small, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the length of the plant. The foliage is typically smooth with entire margins and a delicate, green appearance. Small, white flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by five distinct petals that are often slightly notched at the tips. These blossoms are supported by thin pedicels that allow them to sway easily in the wind. The root system consists of fine, fibrous structures that anchor the plant within its moist, mountainous habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Yunnan province of southwestern China. It grows primarily within the high-altitude montane regions of this specific territory. The species is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest margins or rocky crevices. Its distribution is largely restricted by the complex topography and climate of the Hengduan Mountains. Localized populations are often influenced by the unique soil compositions found in these mountainous zones.
