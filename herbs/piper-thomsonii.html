What's the taxonomical classification of Piper thomsonii?

Piper thomsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Piperales, it falls under the family Piperaceae. Specifically, this plant is categorized within the genus Piper, and its unique scientific designation is defined by its species name, thomsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Piper thomsonii has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with acuminate tips and entire or slightly wavy margins that grow from woody, climbing stems.

The leaf surfaces often exhibit a smooth texture and a deep green coloration, sometimes featuring prominent venation patterns. Its inflorescences consist of slender, spike-like structures that emerge from the leaf axils. These spikes bear small, inconspicuous flowers arranged densely along a central axis.

The plant typically develops a scrambling or climbing habit, using its stems to ascend through surrounding vegetation.
