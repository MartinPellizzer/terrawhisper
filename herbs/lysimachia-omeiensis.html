Lysimachia omeiensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia omeiensis has upright, herbaceous stems that reach heights of approximately 20 to 60 centimeters and bear lanceolate to elliptic leaves arranged in an opposite pattern. The foliage features smooth margins and a distinct green color, often with slightly pointed tips. Small, yellow, funnel-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically appearing in small clusters. Each bloom consists of five delicate petals that surround a central cluster of stamens. The plant's root system is composed of slender rhizomes that allow it to spread gradually through its natural habitat.
