Lysimachia omeiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia omeiensis?

Lysimachia omeiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ericales and the family Primulaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Lysimachia, with its specific designation being the species omeiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia omeiensis has upright, herbaceous stems that reach heights of approximately 20 to 60 centimeters and bear lanceolate to elliptic leaves arranged in an opposite pattern.

The foliage features smooth margins and a distinct green color, often with slightly pointed tips. Small, yellow, funnel-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils, typically appearing in small clusters. Each bloom consists of five delicate petals that surround a central cluster of stamens.

The plant's root system is composed of slender rhizomes that allow it to spread gradually through its natural habitat.

        Lysimachia omeiensis is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried aerial parts in water to extract its medicinal components.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Lysimachia omeiensis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or digestive distress if ingested or touched. There is limited clinical data available regarding its long-term toxicological profile in humans. Some users may experience localized redness or itching upon direct contact with the plant's tissues. If consumed accidentally, it might lead to nausea or stomach discomfort in sensitive individuals. Caution is advised when handling this species due to these potential physiological responses.
