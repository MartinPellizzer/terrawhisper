What is Maerua deckenii?

Maerua deckenii is a shrub or small tree species that belongs to the family Capparaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa, specifically in the regions of Ethiopia and Kenya. Botanically, it is characterized by its deciduous or evergreen leaves, which are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, measuring 2-10 cm in length. The leaves are also glabrous or pubescent, with a petiole that is up to 1.5 cm long.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in axillary corymbose inflorescences, which are followed by a capsule, containing numerous seeds.

The main herbal preparations of Maerua deckenii are infusion, tincture, decoction, tea, and Maerua decoction.

Maerua decoction is a traditional preparation method used in African herbal medicine, where the plant's roots and leaves are boiled together to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and skin diseases.
