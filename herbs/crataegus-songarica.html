What's the taxonomical classification of Crataegus songarica?

Crataegus songarica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, the plant is ordered within the Rosales and falls under the family Rosaecae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Crataegus and the species songarica.

What are the pharmacological activities of Crataegus songarica?

This plant has potent antioxidant, cardioprotective, hypotensive, anticancer, and cytotoxic pharmacological activities. Its high concentration of bioactive compounds allows it to neutralize free radicals, providing significant antioxidant benefits to the body. These properties extend to cardiovascular health by offering cardioprotective effects and acting as a hypotensive agent to help regulate blood pressure. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates anticancer potential through its ability to induce cytotoxic effects on various malignant cell lines. These diverse mechanisms make Crataegus songarica a subject of significant interest for therapeutic development.

According to a study published by "Pharmaceuatical biology", Crataegus songarica methanol extract protects kidney and heart tissue against CCl4-induced toxicity and shows potential antioxidant effects. When administered at 300 mg/kg b wt, the extract significantly decreases serum creatinine by 59.74%, urea by 40.23%, and cholesterol by 54 mg/dL. It also reduces MDA levels to 0.007 nmol/mg protein in kidney and 0.025 nmol/mg protein in heart tissue. In the kidney, the extract affects GSH (209.79 ± 54.6), GR (111.45 ± 2.84), GPx (94.01 ± 14.80), and GST (201.71), while in the heart, it influences GSH (51.47 ± 1.47), GR (45.42 ± 6.69), GPx (77.19 ± 10.94), and GST (49.89). Furthermore, methanol, ethanol, and ethyl acetate extracts demonstrate potent anticancer activity on six cancer cell lines with IC50 values ranging from 28.57 to 85.106 µg/mL.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating cardiovascular disorders, including heart failure, heart tissue damage, hypertension, and vascular insufficiency. Its bioactive compounds work to support the structural integrity of the cardiac muscle during periods of stress. By regulating blood pressure, it helps manage the symptoms of hypertension and reduces the strain on the circulatory system. These therapeutic properties also aid in improving blood flow to mitigate the effects of vascular insufficiency. Consequently, the plant serves as a functional aid in stabilizing various forms of heart-related complications.

According to a study published by "Journal of medicinal food", Crataegus songarica fruits are locally consumed for the treatment of various cardiovascular disorders, including hypertension, heart failure, and vascular insufficiency. The study evaluated the cardioprotective effects of an aqueous-methanol extract (AMECS) on glucose-induced hypertensive rats after 21 days of treatment. This treatment involved a 500 mg/kg single-daily oral dose of the extract. Results showed that AMECS prevented the increase in systolic blood pressure, oxidative stress, endothelial dysfunction, and metabolic alterations induced by glucose. Ultimately, the fruit extract of Crataegus songarica presents a significant cardioprotective effect in these glucose-induced hypertensive rats.
