What is Phoebe amplexicaulis?

Phoebe amplexicaulis is a species of plant in the family Lauraceae, native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 5 meters in height, with a woody stem and a broad, ovate to elliptic leaf shape, measuring 5-10 centimeters in length, with a rounded base and a cuneate apex. The leaves are dark green, glabrous, and have a leathery texture, with a prominent midrib and 4-6 pairs of secondary veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in axillary panicles, measuring 1-2 centimeters in diameter, and is followed by purple, ellipsoidal fruits, 1-1.5 centimeters in length.

The plant's bark is greyish-brown, with longitudinal lenticels, and the wood is yellowish-brown and dense.
