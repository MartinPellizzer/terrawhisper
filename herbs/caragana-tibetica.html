Caragana tibetica

What's the taxonomical classification of Caragana tibetica?

Caragana tibetica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it among the legumes, and it is specifically identified within the genus Caragana as the species tibetica.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These specific chemical constituents are classified as secondary metabolites that contribute to the overall biological activity of the species. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential roles in pharmacological applications. The presence of such diverse compounds suggests complex biochemical properties within the plant's tissues. Detailed analysis of these molecules helps determine the therapeutic value of the organism.

    As discussed in a study, the 70% acetone extract of the stems of Caragana tibetica was found to have a potent superoxide anion scavenging activity. The study isolated a new piceatannol dimer called Tibeticanol (1) which possesses antioxidant activity. Along with this new compound, eleven known aromatic compounds were isolated from the plant. Through the enzyme oxidation of the monomeric stilbene piceatanol (3) using horseradish peroxidase and hydrogen peroxide, cassigarol E (5) and G (6) were yielded as major products. Most of the isolated compounds from Caragana tibetica exhibited superoxide anion scavenging activity.
