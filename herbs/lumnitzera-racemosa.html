What are the pharmacological activities of Lumnitzera racemosa?

This plant has potent antibacterial and antimicrobial pharmacological activities derived from its diverse secondary metabolites. Research indicates that extracts from Lumnitzera racemosa can effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. These bioactive compounds target specific cellular processes in bacteria to prevent infection and proliferation. Studies suggest that the plant's chemical profile contributes to its potential use in developing new therapeutic agents. Such properties make it a significant subject of interest for natural product pharmacology.

According to a study published by "Indian journal of pharmaceuatical sciences", Lumnitzera racemosa showed promising antibacterial activity against both Staphylococcus aureus and Proteus sp. when using Soxhleth extracts of petroleum ether, ethyl acetate, ethanol and water. This plant was selected for further investigation because it gave the best inhibition for both bacterial species. Charcoal treated plant extracts of L. racemosa were able to inhibit both bacterial strains more than those of untreated plant extracts. Phytochemical screening of the mature leaf and bark of L. racemosa revealed the presence of alkaloids, steroids, triterpenoids and flavonoids. However, none of these extracts indicated the presence of saponins and cardiac glycosides. Separated bands of extracts by TLC analysis demonstrated antibacterial activity against S. aureus.
