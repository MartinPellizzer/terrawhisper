Arak

Salvadora persica

Arak (Salvadora persica) is a plant belonging to the Salicaceae family, traditionally used in various regions for dental care, such as toothbrushing, and for skin wound healing. Its roots and stems are commonly harvested and prepared for use, reflecting its significance in traditional practices across parts of Asia and Africa.

Botanical Identification

Arak, scientifically known as Salvadora persica, belongs to the family Salicaceae. It is commonly referred to as toothbrush tree in various regions. A small, deciduous shrub, it is native to southern Asia. The plant has small, yellow flowers and is often used in traditional herbal medicine. Synonyms include "safed musali" and "dhoop patra" in Indian literature.

Arak Identification.

Active Compounds

Arak contains key bioactive compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for oral administration to treat oral health issues like gingivitis and tooth decay

Infusion: Used for oral consumption to address digestive issues and as a mild stimulant

Extract: Used for topical application to treat skin infections and wounds

Safety Profile

Arak, or Salvadora persica, is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, excessive consumption may lead to gastrointestinal discomfort. It is contraindicated for pregnant women and those with sensitive stomach conditions due to its potential irritant effects. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
