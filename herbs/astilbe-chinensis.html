Astilbe chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Astilbe chinensis?

Astilbe chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Saxifragales, it is positioned within the family Saxifragaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Astilbe and the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astilbe chinensis has basal, pinnately compound leaves that are deeply serrated and typically measure several inches in length. These foliage structures emerge from a thick, spreading rhizome that anchors the plant in moist soil. During the blooming period, the plant produces upright, pyramidal panicles of small, densely packed flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The stems are sturdy and erect, supporting the heavy floral displays throughout the growing season. As the season progresses, the textures of the fine-textured leaves and plume-like inflorescences provide a complex visual structure to the plant.

What are the pharmacological activities of Astilbe chinensis?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-irritation, antiplatelet, and cough relieving pharmacological activities. Its medicinal properties allow it to reduce pain and decrease swelling through various biological pathways. The substance also serves to soothe sensitive tissues by providing significant anti-irritation effects. Furthermore, it functions as an antiplatelet agent to assist in regulating blood flow. These combined actions make the species a versatile subject for therapeutic research.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", rhizoma Astilbe chinensis demonstrates effects in abiirritation, blood activation, cough relieving, and sputum elimination. The decoction is significantly effective in reducing the frequency of licking behavior of mice on hot-plate and reducing the writhed response induced by acetic acid. It also improves the hemarhology of acute blood-stasis model rats and reduces the frequency of cough induced by ammonia. Furthermore, the plant increases the quantity of phenol red output from the trachea in mice. The maximum tolerance dose of gastrogavage in mice was determined to be 400 g x kg(-1), which is 666.7 times the dose clinically used for humans.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several specific medicinal compounds including bergenin, 11-O-galloylbergenin, 4-O-galloylbergenin, 11-O-(3'-O-methylgalloyl)-bergenin, and bergenin IV. These bioactive molecules belong to a group of C-glycosylphenolics known for their diverse pharmacological properties. Research indicates that these constituents may exhibit significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects within biological systems. The structural variations among these galloylated derivatives influence how they interact with various cellular targets. Identifying these specific chemical markers is essential for understanding the therapeutic potential of Astilbe chinensis.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", four compounds were isolated and purified from Astilbe chinensis using extraction and column chromatography on silica gel. The chemical structures of these compounds were determined through physical-chemical evidence and spectral analysis including HR-EIMS, EIMS, 13C-NMR, 1H-NMR, DEPT, 1H-1H-COSY, HMQC, and HMBC. The identified constituents were elucidated as beta-sitosterol palmitate I, daucosterol II, beta-sitosterol III, and Bergenin IV. Notably, compound I and compound II were isolated from A. chinensis for the first time.
