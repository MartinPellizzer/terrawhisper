Astilbe chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Astilbe chinensis?

Astilbe chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Saxifragales, it is positioned within the family Saxifragaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Astilbe and the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astilbe chinensis has basal, pinnately compound leaves that are deeply serrated and typically measure several inches in length. These foliage structures emerge from a thick, spreading rhizome that anchors the plant in moist soil. During the blooming period, the plant produces upright, pyramidal panicles of small, densely packed flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The stems are sturdy and erect, supporting the heavy floral displays throughout the growing season. As the season progresses, the textures of the fine-textured leaves and plume-like inflorescences provide a complex visual structure to the plant.

What are the pharmacological activities of Astilbe chinensis?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-irritation, antiplatelet, and cough relieving pharmacological activities. Its components work to reduce pain and swelling by modulating various inflammatory pathways within the body. The anti-irritation properties help soothe sensitive tissues, while its antiplatelet effects can assist in managing blood flow and preventing unwanted clotting. Additionally, the plant serves as an effective agent for cough relieving by easing respiratory discomfort. These diverse medicinal qualities make Astilbe chinensis a significant subject in pharmacological research.

As discussed in a study, the decoction of rhizoma Astilbe chinensis is significantly effective in reducing the frequency of licking behavior of mice on hot-plate and the writhing response induced by acetic acid. It improves the hemarhology of acute blood-stasis model rats and is capable of prolonging the latent period. The plant also reduces the frequency of cough induced by ammonia and increases the quantity of phenol red output from the trachea in mice. Regarding safety, the maximum tolerance dose of gastrogavage in mice was 400 g x kg(-1). This amount was 666.7 times of that clinically used for human.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 11-O-galloylbergenin, 4-O-galloylbergenin, bergenin, catechin, and various triterpenoids. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the diverse pharmacological activities observed in the plant's extracts. Bergenin and its galloylated derivatives are often studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The presence of catechin further enhances the biological profile through its well-known scavenging abilities. Additionally, the triterpenoids present in the tissue may offer various therapeutic benefits depending on the specific structure of the molecules.

As discussed in a study, the chemical constituents in the rhizome of Astilbe chinensis were isolated using column chromatographies and identified through various spectral analyses including UV, IR, MS, 1H-NMR, 13C-NMR, and 2D-NMR. The specific structures identified in the plant were bergenin (1), 11-O-galloylbergenin (2), 4-O-galloylbergenin (3), and catechin (4). Notably, compound 3 was isolated from Astilbe chinensis for the first time. These four medicinal compounds represent the primary constituents identified in the rhizome.
