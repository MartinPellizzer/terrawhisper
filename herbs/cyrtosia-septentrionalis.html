Cyrtosia septentrionalis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyrtosia septentrionalis?

Cyrtosia septentrionalis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the biological lineage of the order Asparagales. It is further nested within the family Orchidaceae, which characterizes its botanical relationship to other orchids. Specifically, it is identified under the genus Cyrtosia, with its unique designation being the species septentrionalis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyrtosia septentrionalis has a mycoheterotrophic morphology characterized by a fleshy, non-green stem that lacks chlorophyll and arises from a subterranean rhizome.

The plant typically measures between 10 and 30 centimeters in height and features a scale-like structure instead of functional leaves. Its flowers are tubular or bell-shaped, often displaying creamy white to yellowish hues with subtle reddish markings. The inflorescence is usually solitary or arranged in a small cluster at the apex of the stem.

Because it does not photosynthesize, the entire structure appears somewhat translucent or brownish in color.
