        What are the herbal preparations of Neocheiropteris palmatopedata?

        Neocheiropteris palmatopedata is prepared as a concentrated decoction, a topical poultice made from crushed leaves, and a fine powder for oral consumption.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Neocheiropteris palmatopedata can cause severe dermal irritation, respiratory distress, and acute gastrointestinal upset if handled or ingested. Direct contact with the plant's sap often results in redness and painful blistering on the skin. If the airborne spores are inhaled, individuals may experience significant coughing fits and shortness of breath. Ingesting any part of the foliage leads to rapid onset nausea and abdominal cramping. These reactions are most intense when the plant is crushed or processed in enclosed spaces.
