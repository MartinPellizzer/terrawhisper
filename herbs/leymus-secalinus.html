Leymus secalinus

What's the taxonomical classification of Leymus secalinus?

Leymus secalinus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Poales, it falls under the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Leymus and the species name secalinus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leymus secalinus has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical culms that can reach heights of up to 100 centimeters.

The leaves are long and narrow with a prominent midrib and a slightly scabrous texture along the margins. Its inflorescence is a dense, spike-like panicle containing multiple spikelets arranged along a central axis. Each spikelet contains several florets that possess long awns, which are crucial for identification.

The root system is fibrous and robust, allowing the plant to stabilize itself in various soil types.
