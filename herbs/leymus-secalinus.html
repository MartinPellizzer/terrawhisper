What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leymus secalinus has an erect, perennial growth habit characterized by slender, cylindrical culms that can reach heights of up to 100 centimeters. Its inflorescence is a dense, spike-like panicle containing multiple spikelets arranged along a central axis. Each spikelet contains several florets that possess long awns, which are crucial for identification. The root system is fibrous and robust, allowing the plant to stabilize itself in various soil types.
