Saccharum arundinaceum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saccharum arundinacium has perennial, rhizomatous growth habits characterized by tall, erect, cane-like stems that can reach several meters in height. These stems are segmented by distinct nodes and internodes, typically possessing a fibrous, cylindrical structure. The leaves are linear and lanceolate, emerging from the nodes with prominent parallel venation and a papery texture. At the apex of the stalks, the plant produces large, plumose inflorescences known as panicles. These flowering structures consist of numerous small spikelets arranged in branching clusters.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, including countries such as India, China, and Thailand. It thrives primarily in warm, humid climates where seasonal rainfall is abundant. The species is frequently found growing along the edges of rivers, streams, and marshes within lowland areas. Its preference for moist soil allows it to colonize riparian zones and seasonally flooded plains. Consequently, its distribution is closely tied to freshwater ecosystems throughout its native range.
