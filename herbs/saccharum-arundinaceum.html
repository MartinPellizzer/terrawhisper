Saccharum arundinacaeum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Poales. It is further categorized into the family Poaceae, specifically within the genus Saccharum, and is identified by the species name arundinaceum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saccharum arundinacium has perennial, rhizomatous growth habits characterized by tall, erect, cane-like stems that can reach several meters in height. These stems are segmented by distinct nodes and internodes, typically possessing a fibrous, cylindrical structure. The leaves are linear and lanceolate, emerging from the nodes with prominent parallel venation and a papery texture. At the apex of the stalks, the plant produces large, plumose inflorescences known as panicles. These flowering structures consist of numerous small spikelets arranged in branching clusters.
