Rehmannia glutinosa supports eye health because it contains bioactive constituents such as phenolic acids, flavonoids, and polysaccharides, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These constituents help protect the eyes from oxidative stress and inflammation, thereby reducing the risk of age-related macular degeneration and cataracts.
