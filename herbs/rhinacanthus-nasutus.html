Rhinacanthus nasutus

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhinacanthus nasutus?

Rhinacanthus nasutus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Acanthaceae, specifically categorized within the genus Rhinacanthus, with the species designation nasutus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhinacanthus nasutus has small, opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and a distinctively elongated, snout-like corolla tube. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and emerge from the leaf axils. These blossoms feature five spreading lobes and a prominent, curved tube that gives the species its name. The plant grows as a woody shrub or small tree with slender branches and a somewhat irregular branching pattern. Its fruit is a small, globose capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.
