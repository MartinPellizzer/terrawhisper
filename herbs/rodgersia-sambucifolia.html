Rodgersia sambucifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rodgersia sambucifolia has large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 60 centimeters in length and are characterized by deeply serrated, lanceolate leaflets. These compound leaves emerge from thick, fleshy rhizomes and form a dense, spreading mound of foliage. The leaflets possess a distinct texture with prominent veins and a slightly rugose surface. During the blooming period, the plant produces upright, pyramidal panicles of creamy white flowers that rise above the lush greenery. The overall structure is robust and architectural, making it a prominent feature in shade gardens.
