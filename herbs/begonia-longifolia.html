Begonia longifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia longifolia?

Begonia longifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Cucurbitaleales. This plant is a member of the family Begoniaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Begonia, falling under the unique species designation longifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Begonia longifolia has elongated, lanceolate leaves that can reach up to 40 centimeters in length and are characterized by asymmetrical bases and serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a deep green color with distinct venation patterns that extend toward the leaf edges. Its stems are succulent and often feature reddish tinting or fine pubescence depending on the specific growth stage. The inflorescence consists of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in a clustered arrangement. These plants generally maintain a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing them to spread across moist forest floors.
