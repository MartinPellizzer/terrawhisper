What is Cyperus aggregatus?

Cyperus aggregatus is a species of sedge in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb with a short stem that produces a cluster of brown scales and a single spike of yellow or brown flowers, each consisting of three stamens and a single pistil. The leaves are flat, linear, and often rolled inwards, typically 5-15 cm long, and 1-3 mm wide. The inflorescence is a dense, ovoid or elliptical spike, 2-5 cm long, composed of crowded, sessile flowers.

The species is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the world, including Australia, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.
