Burmannia disticha

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Burmannia disticha has a diminutive, annual habit characterized by a slender, erect stem that typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters. The leaves are small, linear to lanceolate, and arranged in a densely whorled pattern around the stem. Its reproductive structures consist of small, white, or translucent flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers are actinomorphic and possess a distinct tubular calyx with four to five lobes. The plant often develops a specialized flowering spike that is distinctly two-ranked or distichous in its arrangement.
