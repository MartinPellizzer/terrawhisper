Saraca indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Saraca indica?

Saraca indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Saraca, with the specific epithet indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saraca indica has a medium-sized tree habit characterized by its spreading canopy, simple alternate leaves, and bright orange to yellow flowers. The leaves are typically elliptic or obovate in shape with an entire margin and a distinctively blunt or rounded apex. The flowers are prominent and emerge from the axils, often appearing in small clusters that provide a striking visual contrast to the green foliage. Its bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured depending on the maturity of the specimen. The plant also produces small, fleshy fruits that follow the flowering stage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, spanning across countries such as India, Sri Lanka, Thailand, and parts of Indonesia. It thrives primarily in moist, lowland evergreen forests and along the edges of riverine ecosystems. The distribution is concentrated in areas characterized by high humidity and consistent seasonal rainfall. Specific populations are often found in the hilly terrains of the Western Ghats and other forested tracts of the Indian subcontinent. What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Saraca indica contains medicinal compounds found primarily in its bark, leaves, flowers, and seeds. The bark is frequently utilized in traditional Ayurvedic practices to treat inflammation, skin diseases, and various digestive disorders. Extracts from the leaves are often applied topically to soothe skin irritations or consumed to manage fever. The flowers possess cooling properties that can be used to balance bodily heat and treat respiratory issues. What are the pharmacological activities of Saraca indica?

This plant has antioxidant, estrogenic, cardioprotective, antibreast cancer, and DNA topoisomerase IB inhibitory activities. Its potent antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals to prevent cellular oxidative damage. The presence of estrogenic activity allows the plant to interact with hormonal pathways in the body. Furthermore, its cardioprotective effects contribute to maintaining heart health and preventing cardiovascular dysfunction. The plant also exhibits antibreast cancer activity, which is linked to its ability to function as a DNA topoisomerase IB inhibitor.

As discussed in a study, Saraca indica bark extract shows in vitro antioxidant and antibreast cancer activity. The research focused on the antioxidant, antibreast cancer activity, and toxicological evaluation of Saraca indica bark extract (SIE). These results indicated that this herbal preparation possesses both antioxidant and antibreast cancer activity. Toxicological studies suggest that SIE is safer to use. Consequently, it may have a potential to be used as complementary and alternative medicine for breast cancer therapy.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 5'-methoxyisolarciresinol-9'-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, icariside E3, lyoniside, nudiposide, and saracoside. These specific chemical constituents are categorized as lignans and glycosides found within the plant's biological structure. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential bioactive properties and therapeutic applications. Each compound possesses a unique molecular configuration that may contribute to the plant's overall medicinal profile. Further investigation into these substances helps clarify their specific roles in pharmacological development.

As discussed in a study, chemical investigation of the stem bark of Saraca indica resulted in the isolation of a new lignan glycoside named saracoside. This species also contains 4 known lignan glycosides including lyoniside, icariside E3, (+)5'-methoxyisolarciresinol-9'-O-beta-D-glucopyranoside, and nudiposide. What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as a decoction of the bark to treat various ailments, a fine powder used for internal administration, an infusion of the leaves for soothing properties, a topical paste or poultice for skin inflammation, and an oil extracted for massage. The bark is most commonly boiled in water to create a potent decoction used in traditional medicine. Dried parts of the plant are often ground into a powder to be mixed with honey or water. For milder therapeutic effects, a gentle infusion of the foliage is frequently prepared. Additionally, the plant material can be crushed into a thick paste or poultice to apply directly to wounds or swellings. Finally, botanical oils are produced from the plant to serve as a base for medicinal applications.
