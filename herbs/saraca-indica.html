Saraca indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Saraca indica?

Saraca indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Saraca, with the specific epithet indica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Saraca indica contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including tannins, flavonoids, alkaloids, saponins, and glycosides. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's significant pharmacological activities such as antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Specifically, the presence of polyphenolic compounds assists in neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. The alkaloids and tannins are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat skin ailments and promote wound healing. Research suggests that these diverse compounds work synergistically to provide antimicrobial and hepatoprotective effects.

What are the therapeutic actions of Saraca indica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, respiratory ailments, and gastrointestinal issues such as diarrhea. Its leaves and bark contain bioactive compounds that act as effective antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory agents. In traditional medicine, extracts are often applied topically to accelerate the healing of cuts and various dermatological conditions. Some preparations are also ingested to soothe stomach discomfort or alleviate symptoms of bronchial congestion. The diverse chemical profile of the plant makes it a versatile tool for managing both external and internal maladies.
