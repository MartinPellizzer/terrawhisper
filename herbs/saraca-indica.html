Saraca indica

What's the taxonomical classification of Saraca indica?

Saraca indica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Saraca, with the specific epithet indica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saraca indica has a medium-sized tree habit characterized by its spreading canopy, simple alternate leaves, and bright orange to yellow flowers. The leaves are typically elliptic or obovate in shape with an entire margin and a distinctively blunt or rounded apex. The flowers are prominent and emerge from the axils, often appearing in small clusters that provide a striking visual contrast to the green foliage. Its bark is generally smooth or slightly fissured depending on the maturity of the specimen. The plant also produces small, fleshy fruits that follow the flowering stage.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, spanning across countries such as India, Sri Lanka, Thailand, and parts of Indonesia. It thrives primarily in moist, lowland evergreen forests and along the edges of riverine ecosystems. The distribution is concentrated in areas characterized by high humidity and consistent seasonal rainfall. Specific populations are often found in the hilly terrains of the Western Ghats and other forested tracts of the Indian subcontinent. What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Saraca indica contains medicinal compounds found primarily in its bark, leaves, flowers, and seeds. The bark is frequently utilized in traditional Ayurvedic practices to treat inflammation, skin diseases, and various digestive disorders. Extracts from the leaves are often applied topically to soothe skin irritations or consumed to manage fever. The flowers possess cooling properties that can be used to balance bodily heat and treat respiratory issues. What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Saraca indica targets various inflammatory mediators, including cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes and lipoxygenase (LOX), as well as pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). These interactions occur primarily through the presence of bioactive phytochemicals such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and tannins. By inhibiting COX enzymes, the plant reduces the production of prostaglandins, which helps alleviate pain and swelling. Furthermore, the modulation of cytokine signaling pathways assists in suppressing systemic inflammatory responses. These specific protein-ligand interactions underpin the plant's traditional use in treating skin infections and inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Saraca indica?

What are the herbal preparations of this plant?

This plant can be prepared as a decoction of the bark to treat various ailments, a fine powder used for internal administration, an infusion of the leaves for soothing properties, a topical paste or poultice for skin inflammation, and an oil extracted for massage. Finally, botanical oils are produced from the plant to serve as a base for medicinal applications.
