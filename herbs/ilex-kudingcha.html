Ilex kudingcha

What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex kudingcha?

Ilex kudingcha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, it is identified under the genus Ilex and is specifically designated as the species kudingcha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex kudingcha has evergreen, lanceolate leaves that are dark green, leathery, and possess serrated margins with a glossy upper surface. The plant typically grows as a woody shrub or small tree reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stems and feature prominent venation patterns. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. The fruit develops into small, dark berries that serve as a key characteristic of its reproductive morphology.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and temperate forests of southern China, specifically spanning provinces such as Fujian, Guangdong, Guangxi, Hunan, Jiangxi, and Zhejiang. It thrives primarily in mountainous regions where it can find suitable shade and moisture. The species is often found growing in the understory of evergreen broad-leaved forests. Its distribution is closely tied to specific elevation ranges and humidity levels common in these East Asian habitats. Due to its specific environmental needs, it remains localized to these distinct ecological zones.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds beta-sitosterol, daucosterol, lupeol, ursolic acid, and 3 beta-hydroxyl-lup-20(29)-ene-24-methyl ester. These specific phytosterols and triterpenoids are known for their diverse biological activities in human health. For instance, beta-sitosterol and daucosterol are frequently studied for their ability to support cholesterol management. The presence of lupeol and ursolic acid suggests potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties within the plant's chemical profile. Additionally, the specialized compound 3 beta-hydroxyl-lup-20(29)-ene-24-methyl ester contributes to the complex chemical identity of the species. Together, these molecules form a potent therapeutic toolkit found within the plant's tissues.

As discussed in a study, seven compounds were isolated from the leaves of Ilex kudingcha using a chromatographic method and spectral analysis. These medicinal compounds include lupeol (I), 3 beta-hydroxyl-lup-20(29)-ene-24-methyl ester (II), lup-20(29)-ene-3 beta-, 24-diol (III), beta-sitosterol (IV), ursolic acid (V), daucosterol (VI), and mannitol (VII). The study identifies that compounds I, III, VI, and VII were obtained from the genus Ilex for the first time. Through this research, all 7 identified constituents were characterized by their specific structures.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause hypoglycemia, nausea, diarrhea, hypotension, dizziness, and abdominal discomfort in some users. Because it may lower blood sugar levels, individuals must monitor for signs of hypoglycemia. The plant is also known to impact blood pressure, potentially leading to hypotension and subsequent dizziness. Gastrointestinal issues such as nausea, diarrhea, and abdominal discomfort are common side effects reported by consumers. Careful consideration should be given to these physiological responses when using this botanical.
