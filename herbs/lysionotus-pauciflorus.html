Lysionotus pauciflorus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysionotus pauciflorus?

Lysionotus pauciflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Lamiales and the family Gesneriaceae. Finally, the specific classification of this organism is defined by its genus, Lysionotus, and its species designation, pauciflorus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysionotus pauciflorus has woody stems, elliptical leaves with serrated margins, and sparse, small white flowers. The leaves are arranged in an opposite pattern along the branches and possess a leathery texture. Its stems exhibit a reddish-brown bark that becomes increasingly textured as the plant matures. The floral structures are characterized by a limited number of petals per inflorescence, which gives the species its specific name. Small, fleshy fruits develop following pollination to aid in seed dispersal.

What are the pharmacological activities of Lysionotus pauciflorus?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including significant anti-ferroptosis and anti-food allergic properties. The anti-ferroptosis mechanism helps protect cells from iron-dependent lipid peroxidation, which is a critical process in various degenerative diseases. Additionally, its anti-food allergic activity provides a biological way to mitigate adverse immune responses following the ingestion of specific allergens. These combined therapeutic effects suggest that the plant could serve as a valuable source for developing new treatments for inflammatory and metabolic disorders. Future research remains essential to fully characterize the molecular pathways involved in these medicinal actions.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural medicines", phytochemical investigation on the aerial parts of Lysionotus pauciflorus Maxim. resulted in the isolation of 13 secondary metabolites. These metabolites included 2 methoxyflavones, 6 flavonoid glycosides, and 5 caffeoyl phenylethanoiid glycosides. Among these, 3 previously undescribed metabolites were identified as nevadensin 7-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1 to 2)-beta-D-glucopyranoside (1), nevadensin 7-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1 to 6)-beta-D-glucopyranoside (2), and 2-(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)ethyl-1-O-beta-D-apiofuranosyl-(1 to 4)-alpha-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1 to 3)-beta-D-(6'-O-E-caffeoyl)glucopyranoside (3). The study evaluated the inhibitory activity of the isolated compounds against RSL3-induced ferroptosis on human hepatoma Hep3B cells.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating allergic diseases, allergic responses, food hypersensitivity, passive cutaneous anaphylaxis reactions, and diarrhea. It functions by modulating the body's immune system to reduce the intensity of various hypersensitivity states. The medicinal properties help stabilize cellular responses during acute allergic episodes. Furthermore, its effects can assist in calming gastrointestinal distress associated with food intolerances. By addressing these specific symptoms, the plant serves as a versatile remedy for diverse immunological issues.

According to a study published by "Food & function", Lysionotus pauciflorus Maxim is the source of the natural flavonoid compound dihydromyricetin (DMY). This compound is used to address food hypersensitivity or allergic diseases, specifically ovalbumin-induced allergic responses. In studies using ovalbumin-allergic mice, DMY worked to upregulate rectal temperature and suppress the development of diarrhea. It also decreased levels of serum specific IgE, histamine, and mouse mast cell protease-1 while promoting interleukin-10 production. Furthermore, DMY inhibited the release of beta-hexosaminidase and histamine in rat basophilic leukemia-2H3 cells.
