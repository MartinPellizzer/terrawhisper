What is Distephanus forrestii?

Distephanus forrestii is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that grows up to 6 meters tall, with a single stem (cauline) and a branching system (ramification) that is dichotomous, meaning the branches divide into two equal parts. The leaves are opposite, elliptical in shape, and have a serrated margin, measuring up to 15 cm in length. The inflorescence is a capitulum, a type of composite flower head found in the Asteraceae family, consisting of small, tubular flowers (florets) that are arranged in a spiral pattern.

Distephanus forrestii produces yellow or orange flowers, which are hermaphroditic, meaning they contain both male and female reproductive organs.
