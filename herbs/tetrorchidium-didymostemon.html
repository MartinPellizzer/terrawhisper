Tetrorchidium didymostemon

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tetrorchidium didymostemon has a rhizomatous habit with creeping, slender stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a distichous manner. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear to lanceolate blades with parallel venation characteristic of many monocots. The root system is fibrous and serves to anchor the creeping rhizomes within its moist habitat. These morphological traits allow the species to effectively colonize shaded, humid environments.
