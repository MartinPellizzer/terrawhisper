White Dead-Nettle (Lamium album) Uses

White Dead-Nettle is used to treat respiratory infections, coughs, and cold symptoms by soothing mucous membrane irritation. It also supports wound healing and reduces skin inflammation. The herb may help with fungal infections and skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

White Dead-Nettle (Lamium album) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

White Dead-Nettle is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and easing congestion. Its antimicrobial properties may help combat bacterial and viral infections. It is often prepared as a tea to soothe coughs and sore throats. Regular use can aid in clearing respiratory tract infections.

2. Cough

White Dead-Nettle is used to soothe coughs due to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Traditionally, it has been prepared as a tea to ease persistent or dry coughs. Its mild nature makes it suitable for long-term use in cough management.

3. Mucous membrane irritation

White Dead-Nettle can irritate mucous membranes due to its high content of iridoid glycosides, which may cause burning or stinging sensations when applied topically. This effect is more pronounced on sensitive areas like the eyes or mucous membranes of the respiratory tract. It is important to use caution and dilute preparations when applying to such areas.

4. Cold symptoms

White Dead-Nettle is traditionally used to alleviate cold symptoms such as congestion and sore throat. Its anti-inflammatory and mild antiseptic properties help reduce throat irritation and ease respiratory discomfort. It is often prepared as a tea to support the body's natural healing process during colds.

5. Mucosal irritation

White Dead-Nettle can cause mucosal irritation due to its mild irritant properties. This is most commonly observed in the mouth and throat when used in oral preparations. It is important to use it cautiously and dilute appropriately to minimize discomfort. Always consult a qualified herbalist for safe usage.

6. Respiratory tract infections

White Dead-Nettle is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and easing symptoms of coughs and congestion. It may help alleviate sore throats and improve mucus flow, making it a valuable herb for respiratory tract infections. Its mild antiseptic properties support the body's natural healing process.

7. Skin infections

White Dead-Nettle is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on affected areas. Often applied as a poultice or infused oil, it supports the healing process of minor skin infections like boils and eczema.

8. Wound healing

White Dead-Nettle has been traditionally used to promote wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce swelling, prevent infection, and accelerate tissue repair. The plant's leaves can be applied topically as a poultice or infused into oils for direct application on wounds.

9. Skin inflammation

White Dead-Nettle is used topically to reduce skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps soothe irritated skin and may alleviate conditions like eczema or minor rashes. A poultice or infusion can be applied directly to the affected area for localized relief.

10. Fungal infections

White Dead-Nettle has been traditionally used to treat fungal infections due to its antifungal properties. It contains compounds that inhibit the growth of fungi, making it effective for conditions like athlete's foot and ringworm. Its topical application helps reduce symptoms and promote healing. It is often used in herbal preparations for localized fungal infections.
