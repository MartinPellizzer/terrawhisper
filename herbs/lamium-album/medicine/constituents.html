10 Medicinal Constituents Of Lamium Album (White Deadnettle)

Lamium album has active constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids.

These compounds confer anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can soothe skin irritations and reduce oxidative stress.

The constituents may also help alleviate symptoms of allergies and eczema, improving the quality of life for individuals suffering from these conditions by providing relief from discomfort and itchiness.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Lamium album.

1. Rosmarinic acid

Lamium album rosmarinic acid is a naturally occurring compound found in its leaves and stems.

This phenolic acid has been extensively studied for its potential therapeutic benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties.

It has been shown to exhibit significant activity against various pathogens and may have applications in the development of novel treatments for infectious diseases and chronic disorders.

2. Apigenin

Lamium album apigenin is a flavonoid compound found in the plant's leaves and flowers.

This bioactive compound has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Apigenin has been shown to have therapeutic effects on various diseases such as cancer, cardiovascular disorders, and neurodegenerative conditions, making it a promising candidate for the development of new medicines.

3. Luteolin

Lamium album luteolin is a type of flavonoid glycoside found in the plant.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various health issues, including respiratory problems and inflammation.

Luteolin has been shown to have potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a promising compound for the prevention and treatment of chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disease and cancer.

4. Naringenin

Lamium album naringenin is a flavonoid compound isolated from its leaves and stems.

This bioactive substance has been reported to possess various biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Studies have shown that naringenin exhibits potential benefits for human health, such as cardiovascular protection, cancer prevention, and improved cognitive function.

Its therapeutic applications are currently being researched and explored.

5. Quercetin

Lamium album quercetin is a flavonoid glycoside compound found in the leaves and stems of this herb.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Quercetin has been shown to have potent effects on immune system function, exhibiting immunomodulatory and antiviral activities, making it a promising natural therapeutic agent for various diseases.

6. Kaempferol

Lamium album kaempferol is a flavonoid compound that has been isolated from its leaves and flowers.

It exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, making it a potential therapeutic agent for various diseases. Kaempferol has been shown to scavenge free radicals, protect against DNA damage, and inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, among other beneficial effects.

Its bioactive properties have garnered significant attention in recent years, sparking further research into its medicinal applications.

7. Chlorogenic acid

Lamium album chlorogenic acid is a phenolic compound that has been isolated from its leaves and stems.

It possesses antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been shown to provide protection against oxidative stress and inflammation in various biological systems.

This bioactive compound may exhibit potential therapeutic applications in the prevention of chronic diseases such as cancer, cardiovascular disease, and neurodegenerative disorders.

8. Caffeic acid

Lamium album caffeic acid is a compound extracted from its leaves.

This bioactive molecule has been extensively studied for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

It has been shown to exhibit potent scavenging activity against free radicals, making it a potential therapeutic agent in the prevention of chronic diseases such as cancer, cardiovascular disease, and neurodegenerative disorders.

9. Furocoumarins

Lamium album furocoumarins is a type of bioactive compound found in its leaves and stems.

These compounds exhibit phototoxic properties, meaning they can cause damage to skin and mucous membranes when combined with sunlight exposure. Furocoumarins have been shown to possess antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antitumor activities, making them of interest for potential therapeutic applications.

Further research is needed to fully understand the effects and benefits of Lamium album furocoumarins.

10. Sesquiterpenes

Lamium album sesquiterpenes is a type of bioactive compound found in its leaves and stems.

These sesquiterpenes have been shown to exhibit significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, making them potential candidates for the treatment of various diseases.

They have also been found to inhibit the growth of certain microorganisms, highlighting their antimicrobial properties.
