Cineraria

Tanacetum cinerariifolium

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Cineraria, scientifically known as Tanacetum cinerariifolium, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is also called "cinerary" or "blueweed" in some regions. A perennial herb native to Europe, it has finely divided leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is sometimes referred to by older herbal names like "wormwood" in historical texts.

Active Compounds

Cineraria contains essential oil, terpenoids, and coumarin as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to support its therapeutic effects, likely through anti-inflammatory and antioxidant mechanisms. The presence of multiple active constituents enhances its potential in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Terpenoid
  • Coumarin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Respiratory infections, bronchial congestion, asthmatic symptoms, mucous membrane irritation Antimicrobial, expectorant, bronchodilator, antiseptic, decongestant
Integumentary Skin inflammation, wound healing, insect bites, skin infections Anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, antimicrobial
Immune Allergic reactions, inflammatory conditions, allergies, influenza Antihistaminic, anti-inflammatory, immunostimulant

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and digestive disorders.

Extract: Used for inflammation and skin conditions.

Capsule: Used for general health support and immune system enhancement.

Safety Profile

Cineraria, or Tanacetum cinerariifolium, is generally considered safe when used appropriately. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy due to potential risks. Individuals with allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family should also exercise caution.
