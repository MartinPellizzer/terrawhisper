Astragalus tibetanus

What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus tibetanus?

Astragalus tibetanus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, it falls under the genus Astragalus, with its specific identification defined by the species epithet tibetanus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus tibetanus has a perennial growth habit characterized by woody taproots, pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and dense clusters of pea-like flowers. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stems and consist of multiple pairs of leaflets that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its inflorescence is composed of several small, papilionaceous blossoms that range in color from pale purple to white. The stems are often decumbent or ascending, showing a degree of pubescence that protects the plant from harsh alpine environments. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in the rocky, high-altitude habitats of the Tibetan Plateau.
