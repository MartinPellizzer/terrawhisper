Adenia heterophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenia heterophyllla has a large, tuberous woody caudex, pinnate leaves that vary in shape, and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in cymes. The plant is characterized by heterophylly, meaning its foliage can transition from simple, entire margins to deeply lobed or compound structures depending on the growth stage. Its massive underground tuber serves as a specialized water storage organ to sustain the plant during dry periods. The stems are relatively slender and often climbing or sprawling as they emerge from the central caudex. Small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered together to facilitate pollination.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Adenia heterophylla can cause severe irritation to the skin, eyes, and mucous membranes due to the presence of toxic alkaloids and caustic substances in its tissues. Contact with the plant's sap may lead to painful dermatitis, redness, or blistering upon the skin. If the plant parts are ingested, they can induce significant gastrointestinal distress, including nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. There is also a risk of systemic toxicity if the chemical compounds are absorbed into the bloodstream. Because of these potential reactions, extreme caution should be taken when handling any part of this species.
