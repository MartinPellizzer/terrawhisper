What's the taxonomical classification of Abelmoschus manihot?

Abelmoschus manihot belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae, eventually narrowing down to the genus Abelmoschus. Finally, the specific identification of this plant is completed at the species level as Abelmoschus manihot.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Abelmoschus manihot has an erect, perennial herb morphology characterized by woody stems, palmately lobed leaves, and funnel-shaped flowers. The foliage consists of broad, deeply divided blades with serrated margins and long petioles. Its stems are typically robust and can become somewhat lignified at the base as the plant matures. The inflorescence produces large, solitary flowers that exhibit a pale yellow or cream coloration with a distinct darker center. Small, capsule-like fruits develop following pollination, containing numerous small seeds.

What are the pharmacological activities of Abelmoschus manihot?

This plant has anti-diabetic nephropathy, anti-renal fibrosis, anti-inflammatory, anti-oxidative, and renoprotective pharmacological activities. These diverse therapeutic properties work together to shield the kidneys from damage caused by high blood glucose levels. By reducing oxidative stress and suppressing inflammatory responses, the plant helps maintain cellular integrity within the renal tissues. Furthermore, its ability to mitigate the progression of renal fibrosis suggests a potential role in preventing long-term kidney scarring. Consequently, Abelmoschus manihot serves as a significant natural candidate for managing complex kidney disorders.

As discussed in a study, Abelmoschus manihot (L.) medic is a natural medicinal plant used for the treatment of chronic kidney disease in China. An extract from this plant, known as Huangkui capsule (HKC), has been clinically effective in improving renal inflammation and glomerular injury. In a study using a DN rat model, HKC was administered at three different doses consisting of a high dose of 300mg/kg/d, a middle dose of 175mg/kg/d, and a low dose of 75mg/kg/d. After 12 weeks of treatment, HKC was shown to enhance the transcriptional activity of PPARα and PPARγ in cultured cells, livers, and kidneys of DN rats. The extract also reduced serum triglyceride and cholesterol levels, reduced the expression of inflammatory genes, and attenuated ER stress and c-Jun NH2-terminal kinase activation. These results suggest that HKC has potential as an anti-DN agent for the treatment of DN in humans.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse range of medicinal compounds including flavonoids, flavones, hyperoside, isoquercitrin, and quercetin. These bioactive constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Specifically, the presence of various flavones contributes to its ability to protect cells from oxidative stress. The glycosides hyperoside and isoquercitrin further enhance the therapeutic potential of the plant's chemical profile. Additionally, the foundational compound quercetin serves as a key component in its overall medicinal efficacy.

As discussed in a study, flavonoids are the main active components of Abelmoschus manihot and can be transformed into glucuronide/sulfate conjugates in vivo. A flavonoid fraction of Abelmoschus manihot was extracted from the flower with ethanol and administered at 400 mg/kg to rats. In normal rats, the AUC of quercetin-glucuronide conjugates, isorhamnetin-glucuronide conjugates, quercetin-sulfate conjugates, and isorhamnetin-sulfate conjugates were 459.45 ± 192.70, 1153.01 ± 697.04, 417.81 ± 220.31, and 2475.19 ± 1085.22 μmol h/L, respectively. The AUC of quercetin and isorhamnetin were 5.47 ± 2.54 and 30.73 ± 25.95 μmol h/L. The AUC of the glucuronide-sulfate conjugates of these components is 125-times higher than that of the aglycone. In CKD rats, the ratio of AUCglucuronide/sulfate/AUCaglcyone decreased from 125 to 104.
