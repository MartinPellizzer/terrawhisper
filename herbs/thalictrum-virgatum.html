What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum virgatum?

Thalictrum virgatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It further falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Thalictrum and the species virgatum.

      What are the herbal preparations of Thalictrum virgatum?

      Thalictrum virgatum is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using the dried aerial parts of the plant to extract its medicinal compounds. These liquid preparations are often consumed to utilize the alkaloids contained within the stems and leaves. Some herbalists also utilize the plant in the form of a tincture by soaking the dried material in alcohol for several weeks. This method allows for a more concentrated dose that is shelf-stable for long periods. Additionally, topical applications such as poultices may be made from the fresh plant material for local use.
