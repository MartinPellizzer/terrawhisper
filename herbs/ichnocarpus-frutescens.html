Ichnocarpus frutescens

What's the taxonomical classification of Ichnocarpus frutescens?

Ichnocarpus frutescens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Gentianales and the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Ichnocarpus, and its species designation, frutescens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ichnocarpus frutescens has a woody, shrubby growth habit characterized by erect, branched stems and small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the branches. Its flowers are relatively inconspicuous and emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that serve as a primary reproductive structure. These morphological traits collectively define its structure as a perennial shrub within its specific habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Ichnocarpus frutescens?

This plant has anti-adipogenesis, anti-diabetic, anticancer, antihyperglycemic, and antihyperlipidemic pharmacological activities. Its biological properties allow it to effectively regulate blood glucose levels by acting as an antihyperglycemic and anti-diabetic agent. Additionally, the plant demonstrates the ability to manage metabolic disorders through its antihyperlipidemic and anti-adipogenesis effects. Research also indicates that its bioactive compounds possess potent anticancer potential. These diverse medicinal functions make it a significant subject for therapeutic drug development.

As discussed in a study, the hexane extract of Ichnocarpus frutescens inhibited cell viability in a time- and dose-related manner. An increased release of LDH was observed at a dose of 200 μg/mL, while DNA fragmentation confirmed an apoptotic effect. Morphological observations showed a decrease in cellular lipid content at the tested concentrations compared to control cells. In experiments of lipolytic activity, treatment with the extract enhanced glycerol secretion with rates of approximately 28%, 55%, and 46% at the concentrations of 100, 200, and 300 μg/mL, respectively. These properties reveal the medicinal property of Ichnocarpus frutescens in the treatment of obesity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds ursolic acid, various triterpenes, and α-amyrin. These specific phytochemicals are known for contributing to the plant's diverse biological activities. Ursolic acid often plays a role in supporting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant processes within the body. The presence of triterpenes further enhances the therapeutic potential of the plant's chemical profile. Additionally, α-amyrin serves as another significant component that contributes to its overall medicinal value.

As discussed in a study, the methanolic extract of roots of Ichnocarpus frutescens (MIF) demonstrated significant anticancer activity against four cancer cell lines with IC50 values of 163.5±3.58, 156.3±2.95, 142.6±2.60, and 112.4±1.85. Isolated triterpene ursolic acid also showed anticancer activity on these four cell lines with IC50 values of 8.5±0.29, 9.9±0.12, 8.1±0.40, and 6.2±0.23. Additionally, the compound α-amyrin exhibited activity on the four cancer cell lines with IC50 values of 7.2±0.12, 8.2±0.29, 7.6±0.06, and 5.0±0.12. These medicinal compounds were evaluated using the 3-(4, 5-dimethyl thiazol-2-yl)-2, 5-diphenyltetrazolium bromide (MTT) assay.
