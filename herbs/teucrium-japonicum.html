Teucrium japonicum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Teucrium japonicum has a perennial, creeping growth habit characterized by woody stems that produce small, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and slightly hairy, providing a dense groundcover texture. Its reproductive structures consist of small, tubular, bilabiate flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in late summer. These blossoms are often pale pink or white and are arranged in compact clusters. The plant's root system allows it to spread effectively through rhizomes to establish thick mats.

Teucrium japonicum has therapeutic actions that include being bitter, astringent, hemostatic, diaphoretic, and antispasmodic. What are the herbal preparations of Teucrium japonicum?

Teucrium japonicum is prepared primarily as a decoction or infusion where the dried aerial parts of the plant are steeped in hot water to extract its bioactive compounds. This aqueous extraction is the most traditional method used in folk medicine to facilitate oral consumption. Some practitioners also utilize the plant in the form of a tincture, which involves soaking the herb in alcohol to create a concentrated liquid extract. Additionally, the powdered dried leaves can be incorporated into topical applications or swallowed as a fine powder. These various methods allow for different levels of potency and absorption depending on the intended medicinal use.

Teucrium japonicum can cause potential adverse reactions such as skin irritation, digestive upset, or allergic responses in sensitive individuals. Because the specific toxicity profile of this plant is not extensively documented in human clinical trials, users should exercise caution when handling it.
