Caesalpinia vernalis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Caesalpinia vernalis has bipinnate leaves consisting of numerous small, oblong leaflets and bright yellow flowers that emerge from woody, spreading shrubs. The plant typically develops a multi-stemmed structure with grayish bark and a somewhat irregular canopy. Its small, ovate leaflets are arranged symmetrically along the secondary rachis to maximize light absorption. The inflorescences are characterized by prominent stamens that extend beyond the petals during blooming. During maturity, the plant may produce small, flattened woody pods that contain several seeds.
