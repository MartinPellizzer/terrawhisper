Allium tuberosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Allium tuberosum?

Allium tuberosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asparagales, the plant is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae. Its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Allium and the species tuberosum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Allium tuberosum contains organosulfur compounds, flavonoids, and essential oils that provide its primary medicinal properties. These sulfur-based constituents are known to exhibit significant antibacterial, antifungal, and antioxidant activities within the body. The presence of various phenolic compounds helps in neutralizing free radicals and reducing systemic inflammation. Additionally, certain alkaloids found in the plant may support cardiovascular health by influencing blood pressure and lipid profiles. These bioactive elements collectively contribute to its traditional use in treating digestive ailments and skin infections.

What are the therapeutic actions of Allium tuberosum?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including hypertension, high cholesterol, and various inflammatory conditions. Its medicinal properties are often attributed to the presence of organosulfur compounds that promote cardiovascular health. These bioactive constituents assist in lowering blood pressure and reducing the buildup of plaque in the arteries. Additionally, the plant exhibits antimicrobial and antioxidant effects that help combat localized infections. Regular consumption in traditional practices is believed to support overall immune function and metabolic regulation.
