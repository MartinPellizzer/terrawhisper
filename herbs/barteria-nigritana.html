Barteria nigritana

What's the taxonomical classification of Barteria nigritana?

Barteria nigritana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and into the family Passifloraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Barteria, and its species designation, nigritana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Barteria nigritana contains high concentrations of bioactive alkaloids and terpenoids that serve as its primary medicinal components. These chemical constituents are known to exhibit significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties in various biological studies. Researchers have identified specific secondary metabolites within the plant that may assist in regulating oxidative stress in cellular models. The unique molecular structure of its phenolic compounds also suggests potential applications in developing new antioxidant therapies. Continued pharmacological analysis aims to isolate these active molecules for targeted medical use.

What are the therapeutic actions of Barteria nigritana?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, stomach pains, headaches, and respiratory issues. The medicinal extracts are often applied topically to soothe inflammatory skin conditions and promote healing. When ingested in controlled preparations, it is believed to alleviate gastrointestinal discomfort and digestive distress. It also serves as a traditional remedy for reducing the intensity of headaches. Additionally, its properties are utilized to address symptoms associated with various respiratory ailments.
