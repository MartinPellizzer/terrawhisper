Barteria nigritana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Barteria nigritana has small, woody, evergreen leaves that are typically elliptic or lanceolate in shape and possess a coriaceous texture. The foliage is characterized by a dark green upper surface and often displays a slightly paler underside. Its stems are slender and branched, forming a compact shrubby habit. The plant produces inconspicuous flowers that are generally solitary or in small clusters among the leaves. Small, fleshy fruits develop from these flowers, serving as a key identifying feature of the species.
