Arundo donax

What's the taxonomical classification of Arundo donax?

Arundo donax belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls under the subclass Magnoliidae and is categorized under the order Poales. This plant is a member of the family Poaceae, which places it within the genus Arundo, and it is specifically identified by its species name, donaax.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arundo donax has tall, perennial, rhizomatous stalks that reach heights of up to 6 meters and feature thick, woody stems with prominent nodes. These stems are hollow and cylindrical, often reaching diameters of several centimeters as they grow in dense clumps. The leaves are long, linear, and lanceolate, measuring up to 1 meter in length with a distinct midrib and parallel venation. Large, feathery panicles emerge at the top of the culms to produce numerous small, cream-colored flowers. The entire plant is characterized by a robust, grass-like appearance supported by a vigorous underground spreading system.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Mediterranean Basin, including regions across Southern Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia. While its natural range is centered around these warm climates, it has become widely established in many other parts of the world. It frequently colonizes riparian zones and wetlands in subtropical and temperate areas of the Americas, Australia, and South Africa. These invasive populations often thrive in disturbed habitats near water sources where they can spread rapidly. Consequently, its distribution has expanded globally due to human activity and its ability to adapt to various moist environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Arundo donax?

This plant has various pharmacological activities including anthelmintic, antimicrobial, antipyretic, antitussive, and carminative properties. Its anthelmintic action allows it to be used in treating intestinal worm infestations. The presence of antimicrobial compounds helps the plant combat diverse bacterial and fungal infections. It also functions as an antipyretic to reduce fever and as an antitussive to suppress coughing. Additionally, its carminative effects assist in relieving gastrointestinal discomfort and gas.

According to a study published by "Revista brasileira de parasitologia veterinaria = Brazilian journal of veterinary parasitology : Orgao Oficial do Colegio Brasileiro de Parasitolologia Veterinaria", the anthelmintic activities of Arundo donax L. were determined using in vitro and in vivo tests. The research utilized an egg hatch assay (EHA), an adult motility assay (AMA), and a faecal egg count reduction test (FECRT) against the Haemoncchus contortus model. In the EHA, Arundo donax L. was identified as an ovicide, following the potency of Areca catechu and Ferula assa-foetida. Results from the FECRT indicated that the extract of Arundo donax L. was less effective than the extract of Ferula assa-foetida L. against gastrointestinal parasitic nematodes. Additionally, the crude aqueous methanol extracts (CAME) of the plant were better than its crude powders in vivo.
