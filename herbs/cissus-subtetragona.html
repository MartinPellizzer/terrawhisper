Cissus subtetragona

What's the taxonomical classification of Cissus subtetragona?

Cissus subtetragona belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Cissus and is specifically designated as the species subtetragona.

    Cissus subtetragona has medicinal properties that are primarily utilized for their anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and wound-healing capabilities in traditional medicine.       What are the herbal preparations of Cissus subtetragona?

      Ciissis subtetragona is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the plant material in water to extract its active compounds. This method is preferred because the fibrous structure of the vine requires sustained heat to release its medicinal properties. In some traditional practices, the plant may also be processed into a fine powder for easier consumption. This powdered form can be mixed with warm water or honey to mask its natural taste. Additionally, topical applications such as poultices may be crafted from the crushed leaves for localized use.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Cissus subtetragona can cause localized skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or mild digestive upset if ingested. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many members of the Vitaceae family contain compounds that may trigger sensitivity in certain individuals. Contact with the sap or leaves might lead to redness, itching, or a rash on the skin. Internal consumption should be avoided unless under professional guidance due to the potential for gastrointestinal discomfort. Always perform a patch test before using any part of this plant topically.
