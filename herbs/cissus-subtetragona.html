Cissus subtetragona

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cissus subtetragona has woody, climbing vines characterized by quadrangular stems that possess distinct longitudinal ridges. The leaves are typically palmately lobed with three to five lobes and feature prominent veins extending from the base. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often appearing in axillary positions along the stem. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that change color as they mature. Its growth habit relies on these specialized stems to navigate through surrounding vegetation in its natural habitat.
