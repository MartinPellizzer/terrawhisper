Cissus subtetragona

What's the taxonomical classification of Cissus subtetragona?

Cissus subtetragona belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Cissus and is specifically designated as the species subtetragona.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cissus subtetragona has woody, climbing vines characterized by quadrangular stems that possess distinct longitudinal ridges. The leaves are typically palmately lobed with three to five lobes and feature prominent veins extending from the base. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often appearing in axillary positions along the stem. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that change color as they mature. Its growth habit relies on these specialized stems to navigate through surrounding vegetation in its natural habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Cissus subtetragona?

Researchers continue to investigate how these biological effects can be applied to various health conditions.

As discussed in a study, Cissus subtetragona ethanol extract (Cs-EE) demonstrates potential anti-inflammatory effects in LPS-induced RAW264.7 cells and primary macrophages. The Cs-EE concentration-dependently reduced the secretion of NO and PGE2 while inhibiting inflammation-related cytokines. Additionally, the extract decreased NF-κB- and AP-1-luciferase activity and the phosphorylation events of NF-κB and AP-1 pathways. Treatment with Cs-EE significantly ameliorated inflammatory symptoms in HCl/EtOH-induced acute gastritis and LPS-induced ALI mouse models. These inhibitory inflammatory responses result from the attenuation of Src and TAK1 activation.
