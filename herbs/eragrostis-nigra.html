Eragrostis nigra

What's the taxonomical classification of Eragrostis nigra?

Eragrostis nigra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Poales. It is a member of the family Poaceae, which characterizes its botanical group. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Eragrostis and is defined by its specific species name, nigra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eragrostis nigra has an annual growth habit characterized by slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes and produce narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and features a branched inflorescence composed of several spike-like clusters. These panicles are arranged in a way that makes them appear somewhat condensed or somewhat spreading depending on the maturity of the plant. Small, compressed spikelets are densely packed along the branches and contain single florets with inconspicuous glumes. The overall appearance of the foliage and flowering structures allows it to blend easily into moist, disturbed soil environments.
