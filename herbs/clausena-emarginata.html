Clausena emarginata

What's the taxonomical classification of Clausena emarginata?

Clausena emarginata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. It is further categorized under the family Rutaceae, specifically falling into the genus Clausena, and is identified by its unique species name, emarginata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clausena emarginata has small, opposite leaves that are characteristically notched or emarginate at the apex, meaning the tips are slightly indented rather than pointed. These leaves are typically elliptical or ovate in shape and possess a smooth, entire margin. The plant features a woody stem structure that supports its growth in specific forest habitats. Its reproductive parts consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the foliage.     What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains the medicinal compounds chalepin, clausemargine A, murrastanine A, murrayacine, and murrayafoline A. These specific bioactive molecules are secondary metabolites that contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential pharmacological activities in human health. The presence of these various chemical constituents highlights the complex chemical profile of the species. Each compound may play a unique role in the biological interactions of the plant.

    As discussed in a study, sixteen compounds were isolated from the 90% ethanol extract of the stems and leaves of Clausena emarginata. These compounds include siamenol (1), murrastanine A (2), 3-formyl-1,6-dimethoxycarbazole (3), 3-methoxymethylcarbazole (4), 3-methylcarbazole (5), murrayafoline A (6), 3-formylcarbazole (7), 3-formyl-1-hydroxycarbazole (8), 3-formyl-6-methoxycarbazole (9), murrayanine (10), murrayacine (11), girinimbine (12), nordentatin (13), chalepin (14), 8-hydroxy-6-methoxy-3-pentylisocoumarin (15) and ethyl orsellinate (16). Compounds 1-4 and 14-16 were isolated from Clausena emarginata for the first time, while compounds 1, 2, 15 and 16 were isolated from the genus Clausena for the first time. All isolated compounds were evaluated for cytotoxic activities against five human cancer cell lines: HL-60, SMMC-7721, A-549, MCF-7 and SW480. Compounds 12 and 14 showed significant inhibitory effects against various human cancer cell lines with IC50 values comparable to those of doxorubicin.
