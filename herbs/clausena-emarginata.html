Clausena emarginata

What's the taxonomical classification of Clausena emarginata?

Clausena emarginata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. It is further categorized under the family Rutaceae, specifically falling into the genus Clausena, and is identified by its unique species name, emarginata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clausena emarginata has small, opposite leaves that are characteristically notched or emarginate at the apex, meaning the tips are slightly indented rather than pointed. Its reproductive parts consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the foliage. What are the pharmacological activities of Clausena emarginata?

This plant has potent antiproliferative and cytotoxic pharmacological activities that target various cancer cell lines. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively inhibit the growth and multiplication of malignant cells. These medicinal properties are primarily attributed to the presence of specific phytochemicals like flavonoids and tannins. By inducing apoptosis, the plant's extracts demonstrate a significant ability to reduce tumor cell viability in laboratory settings. Such findings highlight its potential importance in developing new therapeutic strategies for cancer treatment.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", a phytochemical investigation on the stems and leaves of Clausena emarginata led to the isolation of one previously undescribed carbazole alkaloid, clausemargine A (1), alongside 11 known analogues (2-12). All 11 known compounds (2-12) were isolated from Clausena emarginata for the first time. These 12 isolated compounds (1-12) were evaluated for their antiproliferative activities against 5 human cancer cell lines, which included HL-60, SMMC-7721, A-549, MCF-7, and SW480. The compounds exhibited significant antiproliferative effects with IC50 values ranging from 0.28 to 15.18 μM.
