Didymocarpus niveolanosus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Didymocarpus niveolanosus has a small, perennial, cushion-forming habit characterized by densely packed, imbricate leaves that are covered in a thick layer of white, mealy trichomes. These fine hairs give the plant its namesake snowy appearance and serve to protect the photosynthetic tissues from intense light. The leaves are typically scale-like and tightly pressed against the stem to minimize water loss in its specialized habitat. This compact growth form allows the plant to create a microclimate within its own structure. The overall morphology is highly adapted for survival in rocky, high-altitude environments.
