Didymocarpus niveolanosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Didymocarpus niveolanosus has a small, perennial, cushion-forming habit characterized by densely packed, imbricate leaves that are covered in a thick layer of white, mealy trichomes. The leaves are typically scale-like and tightly pressed against the stem to minimize water loss in its specialized habitat. This compact growth form allows the plant to create a microclimate within its own structure. The overall morphology is highly adapted for survival in rocky, high-altitude environments.
