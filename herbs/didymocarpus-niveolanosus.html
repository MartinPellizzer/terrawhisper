What's the taxonomical classification of Didymocarpus niveolanosus?

Didymocarpus niveolanosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Lamiales. It is further categorized into the family Gesneriaceae, specifically within the genus Didymocarpus, and is identified by its unique species name, niveolanosus.

    Didymocarpus niveolanosus can cause moderate skin irritation, respiratory discomfort when spores are inhaled, and mild gastrointestinal distress if accidentally ingested. Contact with the plant's surface often results in localized redness or an itchy sensation on the hands and arms. These reactions are generally temporary and subside once the person is removed from contact with the plant.
