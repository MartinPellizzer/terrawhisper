Deutzia grandiflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Deutzia grandiflora has deciduous, oppositely arranged, ovate to elliptical leaves that measure approximately 2 to 7 centimeters in length with finely serrated margins. The plant typically grows as a spreading shrub reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its most distinctive feature is the terminal clusters of large, five-petaled white flowers that emerge in late spring. These blossoms possess long, protruding stamens that give the inflorescence a delicate, airy appearance. The woody stems are often slightly arching, contributing to the shrub's dense and rounded growth habit.
