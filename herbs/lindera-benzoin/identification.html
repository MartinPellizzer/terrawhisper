Botanical Identification of Lindera Benzoin (Lindera benzoin)

To identify Lindera Benzoin (Lindera benzoin) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Lindera benzoin belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Liliopsida, order Liliales, family Monimiaceae, genus Lindera, and species Lindera benzoin. This classification places it within the flowering plants, specifically in the order of Liliales, and the family Monimiaceae, which includes aromatic shrubs.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Lindera benzoin, first described by the botanist Carl von Linne in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Anisodendron benzoin and Cinnamomum benzoin. Common names include wild cinnamon and spicebush, while regional and historical names vary across different cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Lindera benzoin is a deciduous shrub with a compact growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that spreads horizontally. The stems are slender and reddish-brown, often branching from the base. Leaves are opposite, elliptic to ovate, with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside. The inflorescence is a terminal panicle, bearing small, yellow-green flowers. The fruit is a dark purple drupe containing a single seed.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Lindera benzoin can be distinguished by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a distinct aromatic scent. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a red, fleshy drupe. It is often confused with Lindera glabra, but it has a more pronounced aroma and smoother bark.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Lindera benzoin is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like China, Japan, and Indonesia. It thrives in warm, humid climates with mild winters. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is commonly found at low to mid-altitude ranges, typically between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Lindera benzoin blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to September. For identification purposes, the plant is most easily recognized during its flowering period, which is critical for accurate botanical identification.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Lindera benzoin belongs to the Lauraceae family, sharing characteristics with other members like Magnolia and Cinnamomum. It is closely related to species such as Lindera glabra and Lindera mahagoni. These relationships are reflected in its morphological and genetic traits, which help in accurate identification and classification within the genus.
