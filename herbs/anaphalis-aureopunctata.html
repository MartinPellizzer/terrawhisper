What's the taxonomical classification of Anaphalis aureopunctata?

Anaphalis aureopuncatata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Anaphalis, with the specific designation of the species aureopuncatata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anaphalis aureopuncata has small, perennial herbaceous structures characterized by lanceolate to elliptical leaves with finely serrated margins and distinctively bright yellow discoid flower heads. The foliage is typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs that provide a silvery-green appearance to the stems and blades. Its inflorescence consists of compact capitula where the bright yellow disc florets contrast sharply against the surrounding bracts. The plant develops a compact, bushy growth habit that allows it to thrive in rocky or montane environments. These morphological traits collectively assist the species in moisture retention and visibility to pollinators.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds alpha-Bisabolol, Caryophyllene oxide, and alpha-Selinene. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the therapeutic profile of the species through various biological mechanisms. Alpha-Bisabolol is frequently recognized for its potent anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. Caryophyllene oxide may play a role in modulating cellular responses within the body. Additionally, the presence of alpha-Selinene adds to the complex chemical synergy found in the plant's essential oils.

As discussed in a study, the chemical components of essential oil from Anaphalis aureopunctata were analyzed by GC-MS. The essential oil was extracted using the stem distillation (SD) method. The main components identified in the oil include Eudesma-4(14), 11-diene, alpha-Bisabolol, 6,10,14-trimethyl-2-pentadecanone, Caryophyllene oxide, and alpha-Selinene. This study marks the first time GC-MS has been adopted to analyze the chemical components of the volatile oil of Anaphalis aureopunctata.
