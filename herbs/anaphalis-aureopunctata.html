What's the taxonomical classification of Anaphalis aureopunctata?

Anaphalis aureopuncatata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Anaphalis, with the specific designation of the species aureopuncatata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anaphalis aureopuncata has small, perennial herbaceous structures characterized by lanceolate to elliptical leaves with finely serrated margins and distinctively bright yellow discoid flower heads. Its inflorescence consists of compact capitula where the bright yellow disc florets contrast sharply against the surrounding bracts. The plant develops a compact, bushy growth habit that allows it to thrive in rocky or montane environments. These morphological traits collectively assist the species in moisture retention and visibility to pollinators.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds caryophyllene oxide, alpha-bisabolol, and alpha-selinene. These specific sesquiterpenes contribute to the complex chemical profile that defines the plant's therapeutic potential. The presence of alpha-bisabolol is often linked to soothing and anti-inflammatory properties within botanical extracts. Additionally, caryophyllene oxide and alpha-selinene play significant roles in the overall bioactive activity of the species. Researchers study these constituents to better understand how the plant interacts with biological systems.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", the chemical components of essential oil from Anaphalis aureopunctatata were analyzed by GC-MS. The essential oil was extracted through stem distillation (SD). The main components identified in the oil were Eudesma-4(14), 11-diene, alpha-Bisabolol, 6,10,14-trimethyl-2-Pentadecanone, Caryophyllene oxide, and alpha-Selinene. This research marks the first time GC-MS was adopted to analyze the chemical components of the volatile oil of Anaphalis aureopunctatata.
