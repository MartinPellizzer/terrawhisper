Lonicera japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Lonicera japonica?

Lonicera japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Dipsacales, it is a member of the family Caprifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Lonicera, with its specific designation being the species japonica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lonicera japonica contains high concentrations of bioactive compounds including chlorogenic acid, luteolin, rutin, and various flavonoids such as quercetin. These phytochemicals are primarily responsible for the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Studies suggest that these constituents can help inhibit the growth of certain pathogens and reduce oxidative stress in cells. In traditional medicine, extracts from the flowers and stems are often utilized to treat fevers, coughs, and skin inflammations. The synergy between these diverse chemical profiles makes the plant a subject of interest for developing new antimicrobial agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lonicera japonica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat inflammatory conditions, fever, respiratory ailments, and various skin infections. Its medicinal properties are primarily derived from the flowers and stems which contain bioactive compounds. Practitioners often utilize extracts from the plant to alleviate symptoms of coughs and sore throats. Additionally, topical applications are frequently applied to soothe skin irritation or manage localized swelling. These traditional uses are widely documented in various herbal medicine practices across East Asia.
