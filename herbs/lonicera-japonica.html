Japanese Honeysuckle (Lonicera japonica)

Japanese Honeysuckle (Lonicera japonica) is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family, native to East Asia, China, and Japan. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and stems have been used for infusions, decoctions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and korean traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous vine.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 2 to 5 meters.
Leaves
Opposite, ovate leaves with glossy dark green upper surface and lighter green lower surface, featuring distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, yellow and white, arranged in pairs along a twining stem, with five ovate petals and four stamens.
Stems
Erect, twining, herbaceous stems with opposite branching, hairy surface, and presence of adventitious roots.
