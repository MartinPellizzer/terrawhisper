1. Causes allergic reactions

Syringa reticulata, also known as Japanese Lilac, causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of allergenic compounds such as proteins, glycoproteins, and terpenoids.

These substances are naturally found in the plant's sap, leaves, and flowers, which can be released into the air or skin contact, triggering an immune response that manifests as symptoms like itching, redness, and swelling.

2. Triggers respiratory distress

Syringa reticulata, also known as Japanese Lilac, triggers respiratory distress due to its allergenic properties, releasing pollen and volatile organic compounds that can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals.

The allergens may bind to mast cells, triggering the release of histamine, a potent mediator of inflammation, which can lead to increased mucus production, airway constriction, and difficulty breathing.

3. Induces nausea and vomiting

Syringa reticulata, also known as Japanese lilac, induces nausea and vomiting.

The volatile oils present in the plant's leaves and flowers are responsible for this side effect. When ingested or inhaled, these oils can stimulate the vagus nerve, leading to symptoms of nausea and vomiting. Additionally, the plant's toxic compounds can irritate the stomach lining, further exacerbating gastrointestinal distress.

As a result, individuals who come into contact with Syringa reticulata may experience unpleasant gastrointestinal symptoms.

4. Lowers blood pressure significantly

Syringa reticulata, also known as Japanese lilac, lowers blood pressure significantly.

This is due to the presence of flavonoids and terpenoids in its extracts, which have been shown to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow. Additionally, Syringa reticulata contains cardiac glycosides that can slow heart rate and reduce cardiac output, further contributing to decreased blood pressure.

As a result, users may experience symptoms such as dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting when taking Syringa reticulata supplements.

5. Increases heart rate rapidly

Syringa reticulata, also known as Japanese lilac, increases heart rate rapidly due to its stimulating properties.

The plant's bark and leaves contain a unique combination of compounds that can stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to a rapid increase in heart rate. This is because these compounds mimic the effects of certain neurotransmitters, such as adrenaline, which are responsible for regulating heart rate and blood pressure.

As a result, individuals using Syringa reticulata may experience an increased pulse and potentially even palpitations or arrhythmias.

6. Produces dizziness and lightheadedness

Syringa reticulata, also known as Japanese Lilac, produces dizziness and lighheadedness due to its high concentration of essential oils, particularly linalool and anethole.

These compounds can cause a decrease in blood pressure, leading to a temporary drop in circulation that affects the brain's ability to receive adequate oxygen and nutrients, resulting in feelings of dizziness and lightheadedness.

7. Enhances anxiety symptoms

Syringa reticulata, also known as Japanese lilac, enhances anxiety symptoms due to its stimulating properties.

The plant's essential oils contain high levels of linalool and other volatile compounds that can increase alertness and energy, which may exacerbate existing anxiety in some individuals.

Additionally, the plant's sweet, floral scent can trigger an overproduction of cortisol, a hormone associated with stress and anxiety, leading to heightened feelings of unease and apprehension.

8. Worsens depression

Syringa reticulata, also known as Japanese lilac, worsens depression due to its stimulant properties, which can increase cortisol levels and disrupt serotonin production in the brain.

The plant's essential oils, including limonene and beta-pinene, may also have a negative impact on mood regulation by altering GABA receptors.

As a result, individuals with pre-existing depression or anxiety may experience increased symptoms when using Syringa reticulata, leading to worsening of their condition.

9. Interferes with sleep patterns

Syringa reticulata, also known as Japanese lilac, interferes with sleep patterns due to its fragrance releasing properties.

The strong scent of the plant can stimulate the senses, making it difficult for individuals to relax and fall asleep. Additionally, the sweet, floral aroma may cause vivid dreams or restless sleep, disrupting normal sleep patterns.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's natural ability to release volatile organic compounds (VOCs) into the air, which can impact an individual's ability to wind down at night.

10. Causes stomach upset

Syringa reticulata, also known as Japanese lilac, causes stomach upset due to its potential to interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medication.

The consumption of Syringa reticulata may disrupt the normal functioning of these medications, leading to stomach discomfort, nausea, and vomiting.

Additionally, the plant's high concentration of volatile oils can irritate the digestive system, further exacerbating stomach upset symptoms.
