Corydalis spatulatha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ranunculales and the family Papaveraceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific placement within the genus Corydalis, resulting in its unique species designation, Corydalis spatulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis spathulata has glaucous, spatulate leaves that are deeply lobed and a zygomorphic flower featuring a long, curved nectar spur. The foliage typically consists of basal leaves with a fleshy texture and a pale greenish-blue hue. Its inflorescence displays delicate, tubular blossoms that transition from light shades to more intense colors as they mature. The characteristic spur of the flower serves as a specialized structure for pollinator interaction. Stems are relatively slender and support the spreading arrangement of the leaf blades.

What are the therapeutic actions of Corydalis spathulata?

Corydalis spathulata has potent sedative, analgesic, antispasmodic, nervine, and anxiolytic properties derived from its high concentration of isoquinoline alkaloids. The plant functions as an effective nervine to stabilize irregular nerve activity and calm emotional distress. Its anxiolytic effects help reduce feelings of panic and restlessness in patients. Additionally, the plant provides relief from physical tension through its strong antispasmodic actions on smooth muscle.
