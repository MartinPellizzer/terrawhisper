Citrus × nobilis subf. reticulata

Citrus × nobilis subf.

reticulata, also known as mandarin, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Rutaceae family and is derived from the cross between Citrus reticulata and either Citrus maxima or Citrus medica.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including its antiviral, antibacterial, and anti-inflammatory effects, which are attributed to its high content of limonoids and flavonoids.

The health benefits of Citrus × nobilis subf.

reticulata include its ability to boost the immune system, reduce fever, and alleviate symptoms of colds and flu.

The plant is often used in herbal preparations, such as teas, tinctures, and essential oils, which can be consumed or applied topically to harness its medicinal properties.
