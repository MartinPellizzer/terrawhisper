Polygonum lichiangense

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum lichiangense?

Polygonum lichiangense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is further categorized into the family Polygonaceae, which places it within the genus Polygonum, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Polygonum lichiangense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum lichiangense has slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and are characterized by distinct, swollen nodes. The leaves are small, lanceolate to linear in shape, and arranged alternately along the stem. These leaves feature entire margins and lack petioles, attaching directly to the stem surface. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters. The plant also develops small, ovoid fruits that contain a single seed upon maturity.
