Artemisia qinlingensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia qinlingensis?

Artemisia qinlingensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is further categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, this plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is a large and diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Artemisia, and its specific classification is completed by its species designation, qinlingenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia qinlingenuis has a perennial herb growth habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 20 to 50 centimeters. The leaves are deeply divided into narrow, linear lobes and exhibit a distinct silvery-gray color due to dense pubescence on both surfaces. Small, yellowish flower heads are arranged in compact terminal clusters known as panicles. These inflorescences are composed of numerous small capitula that lack ray florets and consist primarily of tubular disc florets. The plant often develops a woody base as it matures over several growing seasons.
