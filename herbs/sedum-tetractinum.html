Sedum tetractinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Sedum tetractinum?

Sedum tetractinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Saxifragales and belongs to the family Crassulaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its genus, Sedum, and its specific species designation, tetractinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sedum tetractinum has succulent, cylindrical to slightly flattened leaves that are typically light green and arranged in a dense rosette or along trailing stems. These fleshy leaves are often covered in a thin, waxy cuticle that helps the plant retain moisture in arid environments. The stems are slender and can become somewhat woody at the base as the plant matures. Small, star-shaped flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying four distinct petals that are usually white or pale pink. This morphology allows the plant to thrive in rocky crevices and shallow soils where water availability is inconsistent.
