Dendrophthoe pentandra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. This plant is a member of the family Loranthaceae, which places it within the genus Dendrophthoe, and its specific classification is completed by its species name, pentandra.

According to a study published by "F1000Research", flavonoid compounds obtained from the crude methanol extract of Dendrophthoe pentandra L. Miq leaves include quercetin-like compounds (QLCs). These QLCs were obtained by extracting 5 kg of BD leaves through a maceration method and separating them into hexane, chloroform, ethyl acetate, and methanol fractions. Using semi-preparative high-performance liquid chromatography, the QLCs were identified and tested in vitro against the Newcastle disease virus at a virulence titer of 10-5 Tissue Culture Infectious Dose 50% (TCID50). Solutions of 0.05% (w/v) QLCs demonstrated antiviral activity against NDVs with an average cytopathogenic effect antigenicity at a 10-5 dilution (p<0.05). These QLCs from the leaves of BD may have the potential to be developed as medicinal compounds for the treatment of other human or animal viral infections.
