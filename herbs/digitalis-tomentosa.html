Fuzzy Foxglove (Digitalis Tomentosa)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Digitalis tomentosa.

Fuzzy foxglove, scientifically known as Digitalis tomentosa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, valued for its medicinal properties. This herb, often referred to as a cardiac tonic, contains cardenolides, which have been historically used to support heart health by regulating heart rate and improving cardiac function. In traditional medicine, it has been employed to treat ailments such as heart failure, arrhythmias, and respiratory conditions, with some cultures also using it as an adaptogen to enhance endurance and reduce stress. Modern wellness applications include its use in herbal formulations aimed at supporting cardiovascular health and as a natural supplement for those seeking to manage stress and fatigue. Notably, its unique fuzzy leaves and striking violet flowers make it a distinctive plant, and its historical use dates back to ancient herbal practices, highlighting its enduring role in both traditional and contemporary medicine.
