Smilax stans

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax stans has woody climbing stems equipped with axillary spines, lanceolate leaves with entire margins, and small greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles. The plant features a climbing habit facilitated by these sharp thorns located at the nodes. Its leaves are typically evergreen or semi-evergreen, exhibiting a glossy texture and prominent venation. Small, fleshy berries develop from the flowers and serve as the primary fruit type for the species. The root system is composed of thick, creeping rhizomes that allow the plant to spread through the undergrowth.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the southeastern United States, specifically spanning from eastern Texas through the Gulf Coast states to Florida and up into the Atlantic coastal plain. It thrives primarily in the humid, subtropical environments characteristic of these southern regions. Within these areas, it is commonly found in moist habitats such as bottomland forests, thickets, and swamp margins. The species prefers the shaded or partially shaded understories of deciduous woodlands. Its range is closely tied to the presence of consistent moisture and warm seasonal temperatures.
