Stylidium uliginosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Stylidium uliginosum?

Stylidium uliginosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Asterales. It is specifically categorized within the family Stylidiaceae, which places it in the genus Stylidium, and is ultimately identified by its unique species name, uliginosum.

These bioactive compounds are produced by the plant as defense mechanisms against herbivores and environmental stressors. Research into the chemical profile suggests that these substances may possess antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. While specific clinical applications for this exact species are still being documented, its chemical constituents follow patterns seen in other members of the Stylidiaceae family.
