What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stylidium uliginosum has a perennial growth habit characterized by small, tufted leaves that are typically linear to narrowly lanceolate and often feature slightly revolute margins. The foliage is densely clustered at the base of the plant, forming low-growing mats or small clumps. Its inflorescence consists of a slender scape that elevates several small, zygomorphic flowers above the foliage. These flowers are distinctive for their specialized column, which acts as a sensitive trigger mechanism for pollination.
