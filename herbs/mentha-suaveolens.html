Sweet Mint (Mentha suaveolens)

Sweet Mint (Mentha suaveolens) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to Southern Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its carminative, antispasmodic, and expectorant actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and ayurvedic medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height

Leaves
Ovate leaves with acute tips, upper surface glabrous and dark green, lower surface pale green with prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Bilaterally symmetric flowers with two-lipped corolla, white to pale blue petals, arranged in whorled clusters, with four stamens and a two-lobed stigma.
Stems
Erect, herbaceous, quadrangular, glabrous, with opposite branching and aromatic glandular structures.

Medicinal Actions

Carminative
In herbal texts, considered a mild carminative, within digestive system contexts.
Antispasmodic
Traditionally described as a moderate antispasmodic, in smooth muscle contexts.
Expectorant
Historically regarded as a warming expectorant, in chest-related herbal contexts.
Stimulant
As described in traditional systems, a calming stimulant, in energy-related contexts.
