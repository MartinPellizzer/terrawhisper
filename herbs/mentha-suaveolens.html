What to know about Mentha suaveolens (apple mint) before using it medicinally

Apple mint, scientifically known as Mentha suaveolens, is a herb that boasts numerous health benefits, possessing antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties that can aid in alleviating digestive issues and reducing stress.

In the culinary world, apple mint is a versatile ingredient used to add flavor and freshness to various dishes, from salads to cocktails, and is often paired with sweet ingredients like apples and honey to create a harmonious balance of flavors. Horticulturally speaking, apple mint is an easy-to-grow perennial herb that thrives in moist environments and requires minimal maintenance, making it a popular choice among gardeners and cooks alike. From a botanical standpoint, apple mint belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture and its distinctive apple-like aroma.

With ancient references dating back to Greek mythology, where it was associated with the goddess of love Aphrodite, apple mint has been a cherished herb for centuries, used not only for its culinary and medicinal properties but also as a symbol of fertility and abundance.
