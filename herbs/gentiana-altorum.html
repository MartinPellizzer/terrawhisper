Gentiana altorum

What's the taxonomical classification of Gentiana altorum?

Gentiana altorum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianales. The plant is a representative of the family Gentianaceae, situated within the genus Gentiana, and is specifically identified by its species name, altorum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Gentiana altorum contains high concentrations of secoiridoid glycosides, specifically amarogentin, xanthogen, and gentiopicroside, which serve as its primary bitter principles. These chemical compounds act as powerful digestive stimulants by triggering bitter taste receptors in the mouth and throat. The presence of these glycosides helps to increase the secretion of gastric juices and bile, thereby improving overall gastrointestinal motility. Beyond digestive support, the plant also features various phenolic acids and flavonoids that contribute to its antioxidant properties. These diverse secondary metabolites make the plant a significant subject for studying natural bitter agents in pharmacology.

What are the therapeutic actions of Gentiana altorum?

Gentiana altorum has bitter principles such as amarogentin and gentiopicrosin that stimulate the secretion of digestive enzymes and bile to improve gastrointestinal function. These intense bitter compounds act on the gustatory receptors to trigger a reflex increase in saliva and gastric juice production. This physiological response helps to alleviate symptoms of indigestion, bloating, and loss of appetite. Additionally, the plant possesses mild antimicrobial properties that may support gut health by regulating intestinal flora. Regular use in small doses is traditionally intended to enhance overall metabolic efficiency and digestive comfort.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive disorders including dyspepsia, loss of appetite, gastritis, and various forms of intestinal discomfort. Its bitter compounds stimulate the secretion of gastric juices to improve digestion and metabolic processes. The plant is frequently employed in herbal remedies to alleviate nausea and promote healthy bowel function. Additionally, its properties are utilized to support liver health and stimulate bile production. These therapeutic effects make it a common component in traditional bitters and stomach tonics.
