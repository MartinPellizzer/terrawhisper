Pedicularis longicaulis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis longicaulis has an erect, unbranched stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 50 centimeters and is adorned with lanceolate to linear leaves. The foliage is primarily basal, forming a rosette of leaves that are often deeply serrated or lobed along the margins. Moving up the stem, the leaves become smaller and more spaced out, eventually transitioning into bracts that surround the inflorescence. The flower spike is dense and terminal, featuring tubular, bilabiate corollas that vary in color from pink to deep red. These specialized flowers are characterized by an enlarged upper lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.
