Ficus laevis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus laevis?

Ficus laevis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Rosales and belongs to the family Moraceae. Finally, it is identified within the genus Ficus, with its specific designation being the species laevis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ficus laevis has elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically three to ten centimeters long, featuring a smooth, leathery texture and an entire margin. The foliage is characterized by a glossy, dark green upper surface and a paler underside. Its stems are woody and can become quite robust as the plant matures into a shrub or small tree. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in specialized structures called syconia, which are typical of the Moraceae family. These fruits eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that aid in seed dispersal.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforests of West and Central Africa, spanning from Guinea and Sierra Leone through to the Congo Basin and Uganda. It thrives primarily in lowland areas where high humidity and consistent rainfall are abundant. The species is commonly found within the understory or as an epiphyte in dense, moist forest ecosystems. Its presence is often tied to specific microclimates that support the growth of large tropical trees. Consequently, its range is strictly limited to these equatorial forest belts.

How is this plant cultivated?
