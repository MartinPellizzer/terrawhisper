What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lygodium conforme has small, triangular to lanceolate pinnules that are typically deeply lobed or irregularly shaped along the margins. Spore-producing structures called sori are located on the underside of these segments, often appearing as small, dark spots. The plant's texture is generally thin and membranous, reflecting its specialized adaptation to moist environments.
