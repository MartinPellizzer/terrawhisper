What is Lepidium gracile?

Lepidium gracile is an annual herbaceous plant that belongs to the family Brassicaceae.

It has a taproot and a slender stem, typically 10-40 cm tall, with a glabrous or slightly hairy texture. The leaves are lanceolate to ovate in shape, 1-5 cm long, and 0.5-1.5 cm wide, with a serrated margin and a glabrous or slightly hairy surface. The flowers are white, arranged in a racemose inflorescence, and have four-petaled sepals and four-petaled petals, with the stamens and styles exerted beyond the sepals.

Lepidium gracile produces seeds in a silique, which is a dehiscent fruit that splits open to release the seeds.
