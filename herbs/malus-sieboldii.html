What's the taxonomical classification of Malus sieboldii?

Malus sieboldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Rosales, it is placed into the family Rosaceae, which is characteristic of many flowering fruit plants. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Malus and the species sieboldii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Malus sieboldii has a deciduous woody structure characterized by small to medium-sized trees that typically reach heights of two to five meters with spreading, rounded crowns. The foliage consists of simple, ovate leaves with finely serrated margins and a dark green color that often turns reddish in autumn. During the flowering season, the plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled white or pale pink blossoms that emerge from terminal buds. Following the bloom, it develops small, globular pomaceous fruits that transition from green to a bright, attractive red as they ripen. The bark on mature specimens becomes textured and develops a grayish tone as the tree ages.
