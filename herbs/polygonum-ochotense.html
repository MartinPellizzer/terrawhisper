Polygonum ochotense

What's the taxonomical classification of Polygonum ochotense?

Polygonum ochotense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further categorized under the order Caryophyllales. It is a member of the family Polygonaceae, falling within the genus Polygonum, and is specifically identified by its species name, ochotense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygonum ochotense has an erect, annual herbaceous habit characterized by slender stems that often exhibit reddish pigmentation and nodes enclosed by membranous ochreae. The leaves are typically lanceolate to narrowly elliptic in shape with entire margins and acute tips. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary clusters or terminal racemes. These blossoms possess five tepals that are often pinkish or white in color. The plant produces small, dry achenes that serve as its primary means of seed dispersal.
