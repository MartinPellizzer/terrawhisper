Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Tetrapleura tetraptera grows best in well-drained, loamy soils with a slightly acidic to neutral pH, in full sun to partial shade, and requires regular watering to maintain consistent moisture without waterlogging.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, and proper spacing between plants ensures adequate air circulation and reduces disease risk. Regular weeding, mulching, and occasional pruning help maintain plant health and promote vigorous growth. Harvesting is best done during the dry season when the leaves and fruits are most potent, using clean shears to cut mature leaves or hand-picking ripe fruits for optimal yield.

After harvesting, the plant material should be dried in a shaded, well-ventilated area to preserve its medicinal properties, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dry place to maintain potency and prevent spoilage.
