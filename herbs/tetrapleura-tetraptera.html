Tetrapleura tetraptera

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Tetrapleura tetraptera, commonly known as African satinwood, belongs to the family Rubiaceae. It is a small evergreen tree native to West Africa. The plant has opposite leaves, yellow flowers, and a woody root system. It is also referred to as "Kokoro" in some regions and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of Tetrapleura tetraptera include flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties through antioxidant and anti-inflammatory mechanisms. They work synergistically to enhance the herb's therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action

Preparation Methods

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Decoction: Used for respiratory infections and fever reduction.

Infusion: Used for calming effects and treating anxiety.

Safety Profile

Tetrapleura tetraptera is generally considered safe when used in traditional herbal medicine. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised for pregnant or breastfeeding women due to limited research on its effects during these periods.
