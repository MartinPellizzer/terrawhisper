Tetrapleura tetraptera

Tetrapleura Tetraptera (tetrapleura tetraptera) is a plant native to Africa, belonging to the Sapindaceae family. Traditionally, various parts of the plant, including the roots and leaves, have been used in folk medicine for treating malaria, managing diabetes, and addressing skin infections. It holds cultural significance in regions where it is commonly harvested and utilized for its purported therapeutic properties.

Botanical Identification

Tetrapleura tetraptera, commonly known as African satinwood, belongs to the family Rubiaceae. It is a small evergreen tree native to West Africa. The plant has opposite leaves, yellow flowers, and a woody root system. It is also referred to as "Kokoro" in some regions and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

Tetrapleura tetraptera is generally considered safe when used in traditional herbal medicine. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised for pregnant or breastfeeding women due to limited research on its effects during these periods.
