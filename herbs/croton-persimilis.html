Croton persimilis

What's the taxonomical classification of Croton persimilis?

Croton persimilis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is a member of the order Malpighiales and falls within the family Euphorbiaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by its membership in the genus Croton, with its unique biological designation being the species persimilis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Croton persimilis has elliptical to oblong leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along its woody stems. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often appearing in axillary clusters characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant structure is defined by its shrubby growth habit and sturdy branching. Seed production occurs through small capsules that eventually dehisce to disperse the offspring.
