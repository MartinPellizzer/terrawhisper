Scleria terrestris

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scleria terrestris has a perennial, tufted growth habit characterized by long, narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a dense base. The foliage is typically linear and tough, often exhibiting a dark green color with a slightly channeled or folded structure. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal panicles that rise above the foliage. The plant produces small, dry fruits that are adapted for dispersal within its terrestrial habitat. This morphological structure allows the species to thrive in competitive, grassy environments.
