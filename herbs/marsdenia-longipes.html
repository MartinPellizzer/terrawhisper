Marsdenia longipes

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Marsdenia longipes has woody, climbing vines characterized by opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that possess smooth margins and entire shapes. The foliage is typically leathery in texture and displays a deep green color on the upper surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that emerge from the leaf axils on elongated peduncles. These flowers are often pale or creamy in hue and possess a tubular structure typical of the Apocynaceae family. The plant also produces distinctive woody follicles that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds equipped with silky hairs.

What are the therapeutic actions of Marsdenia longipes?

Marsdenia longipes has therapeutic actions that primarily function as a bitter, emetic, and astringent agent in traditional medicinal practices.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Marsdenia longipes can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if handled or consumed improperly. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many plants in the Apocynaceae family contain bioactive compounds that may trigger localized dermatitis upon contact. Ingesting parts of the plant could potentially lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping due to its complex chemical makeup. There is also a risk of systemic toxicity if the plant's alkaloids interact negatively with an individual's unique physiology. Careful avoidance is recommended for anyone with sensitive skin or known allergies to similar botanical species.
