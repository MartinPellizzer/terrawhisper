What's the taxonomical classification of Gongronema napalense?

Gongronema napalense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Gentianales. This species is a member of the family Apocynaceae, specifically belonging to the genus Gongronema, with its precise biological identification being the species napalense.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress, hypoglycemia, and potential liver toxicity if consumed in improper quantities or without medical supervision. While it is traditionally used to manage blood glucose levels, an excessive dose may lead to dangerously low blood sugar levels. Some users have reported experiencing nausea, abdominal pain, or diarrhea following ingestion. There is also a risk of adverse interactions with conventional diabetes medications, which can complicate existing treatment plans. Careful monitoring is necessary to avoid these systemic complications during herbal use.
