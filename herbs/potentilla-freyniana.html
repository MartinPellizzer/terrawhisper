What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla freyniana?

Potentiella freyniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, it is placed in the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Potentiella, and its unique species designation, freyniana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella freyniana has a woody, perennial habit characterized by erect to ascending stems that often exhibit a reddish tint and are covered in fine, spreading hairs.

The leaves are typically trifoliate or occasionally five-foliate, featuring serrated margins and a soft, pubescent texture on both surfaces. Small, five-petaled flowers emerge from the terminal ends of the branches, displaying bright yellow petals that contrast with the green foliage. The fruit consists of numerous small, dry achenes clustered tightly together at the center of the flower.

These morphological features collectively allow the plant to thrive within its specific montane or subalpine habitats.
