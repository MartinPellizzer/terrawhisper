Willow herb

Epilobium angustifolium

Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium) is a flowering plant native to North America and parts of Europe, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions such as rheumatism and skin disorders.

Taxonomy

Willow Herb, or Epilobium angustifolium, is a flowering plant in the family Onagraceae, belonging to the genus Epilobium within the order Saxifragales.

Morphology

Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium) is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems that are usually reddish-purple in color, linear to lance-shaped leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces, and small, clustered flowers that range from pink to red, often with a distinctive yellowish or greenish central spot.

Distribution

Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium) is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including North America, Europe, and parts of Asia, and is commonly found in moist, often disturbed habitats such as stream banks, wet meadows, and roadside areas.

Traditional Uses

Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and mild sedative properties, often prepared as a tea or tincture to support urinary tract health, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of menstrual cramps and nervous tension.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Native American medicine, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and European herbalism, Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium) has been used for its anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and wound-healing properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Willow Herb (Epilobium angustifolium) includes bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties and potential therapeutic applications.

Pharmacology

Willow herb exhibits anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and mild diuretic properties, making it effective in alleviating respiratory and digestive discomfort while supporting urinary health.

Botanical Identification

Epilobium angustifolium, commonly known as Willow Herb, belongs to the Onagraceae family. It is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. The plant has slender stems, narrow lance-shaped leaves, and showy pink or red flowers. It is also known as Narrow-leaf Willow Herb and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Safety Profile

Willow herb is generally safe when used as directed. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
