Lindernia nummulariifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lindernia nummulariifolia has small, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 15 millimeters long with entire or slightly serrated margins. The stems are slender and often prostrate or ascending, allowing the plant to spread across moist substrates. Its flowers are minute, zygomorphic, and usually white or pale lilac, emerging from the leaf axils. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds for reproduction. These morphological traits are characteristic of its growth in damp, shaded environments.
