Viburnum yunnanense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum yunnanense has woody, deciduous stems with opposite, elliptical leaves that measure 5 to 15 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and may exhibit a lighter shade underneath. During the flowering season, the plant produces dense, flat-topped cymes of small, creamy white flowers. These inflorescences emerge from the terminal ends of the branches and are followed by small, fleshy drupes. The fruit transitions from green to a deep red or black color upon maturation.
