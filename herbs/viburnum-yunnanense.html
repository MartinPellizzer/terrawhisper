Viburnum yunnanense

What's the taxonomical classification of Viburnum yunnanense?

Viburnum yunnanense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Dipsacales and is a member of the family Viburnaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Viburnum, and its specific species name, yunnanense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viburnum yunnanense has woody, deciduous stems with opposite, elliptical leaves that measure 5 to 15 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins. During the flowering season, the plant produces dense, flat-topped cymes of small, creamy white flowers. These inflorescences emerge from the terminal ends of the branches and are followed by small, fleshy drupes. The fruit transitions from green to a deep red or black color upon maturation.
