Ficus Umbellata

Ficus umbellata, commonly known as the small-leaved fig or Indian banyan, is a species of fig tree that is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

This herb is valued for its potential health benefits, which include reducing inflammation and improving digestion, as well as exhibiting antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.

The therapeutic actions of Ficus umbellata are attributed to its ability to modulate the immune system, lower blood sugar levels, and exhibit anti-cancer properties.

Possible Side Effects of ficus umbellata

Ficus umbellata can have side effects if used improperly, such as allergic reactions, skin irritation, and respiratory problems.

Prolonged use of the herb may also lead to stomach upset, diarrhea, and nausea, as well as interactions with other medications that can exacerbate underlying health conditions.

In rare cases, high doses of ficus umbellata can cause headaches, dizziness, and fatigue, particularly in individuals with pre-existing kidney or liver issues.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using ficus umbellata to avoid potential complications and ensure safe consumption.
