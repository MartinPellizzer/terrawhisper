Colysis Minor

Colysis minor, also known as minor colysis, is a medical procedure used to relieve intestinal obstruction by gently breaking down or loosening the blockage in the colon.

It typically involves the use of specialized instruments to carefully dissect and remove adhesions or other obstructions that are causing the blockage. This procedure is less invasive compared to major colonic surgery and is often performed under local or regional anesthesia. It is commonly used in cases of partial bowel obstruction where the bowel is still functional and the patient is stable.

Colysis minor can help restore normal bowel function and reduce the risk of complications such as bowel perforation or sepsis.
