What's the taxonomical classification of Heracleum henryi?

Heracleum henryi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Apiales. It is a member of the family Apiaceae, specifically falling under the genus Heracleum, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name henryi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Its stems are robust, hollow, and often exhibit purple spotting or staining near the base. The inflorescence consists of massive compound umbels featuring numerous small, white flowers arranged in dense clusters.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and intense phototoxicity upon contact with human skin. The plant contains furanocoumarins which react with ultraviolet light to damage skin cells. If the sap touches your skin and is subsequently exposed to sunlight, it can trigger painful chemical burns and blistering. Many individuals may also develop a delayed allergic reaction known as phytophotodermatitis. These physical symptoms often result in redness, swelling, and long-lasting pigmentation changes.
