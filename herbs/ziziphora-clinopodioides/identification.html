Botanical Identification of Summer Savory (Ziziphora clinopodioides)

To identify Summer Savory (Ziziphora clinopodioides) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Summer Savory, or Ziziphora clinopodioides, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Ziziphora, and species Ziziphora clinopodioides. It is a member of the mint family, known for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Summer Savory is Ziziphora clinopodioides, first described by the botanist Johann Friedrich von Gaertner in 1793. It has several synonyms, including Teucrium clinopodioides and Salvia clinopodioides, reflecting historical classification shifts. Common names include Summer Savory, Narrow-leafed Teucrium, and Wild Thyme, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Summer Savory is a low-growing perennial herb with a taproot system. It has square, hairy stems that branch near the base. Its opposite, oval leaves have a minty scent. The plant bears whorled inflorescences of small, pinkish-white flowers. The fruits are small, nutlet-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Summer Savory can be distinguished by its aromatic, opposite leaves with rounded tips and pink to purple flowers in whorls. It lacks the glandular hairs found on similar species like Thymus and has a distinct, spicy scent when crushed. Its small, oval-shaped fruits and branching structure also help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Summer Savory (Ziziphora clinopodioides) is native to the Middle East, North Africa, and parts of southern Europe. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained soils, often found in rocky or gravelly areas. It typically grows at elevations between 500 and 2,000 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Summer Savory blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on the climate. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering stage, usually in June to July, when the plant is most identifiable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Ziziphora clinopodioides belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members like Salvia and Thymus. It is closely related to species such as Z. acutifolia and Z. taurica. Its genus relations highlight similarities in morphology and chemical composition, aiding in its identification within the tribe Lamiideae.
