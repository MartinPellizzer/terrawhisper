What is Calamus smitinandii?

Calamus smitinandii is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae, native to Thailand.

It is a type of rattan palm characterized by its slender stems and long, thin leaves with a rounded apex, reaching up to 6 meters in length and 0.6-1.2 meters in width. The leaves are pinnately compound, composed of 30-60 leaflets that are lanceolate in shape and have a wavy or undulate margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in a spicate inflorescence, which are followed by red or purple fruit that is globose in shape and reaches up to 1.5 centimeters in diameter.

Calamus smitinandii is a climbing plant that uses its stems to twine around other vegetation for support.
