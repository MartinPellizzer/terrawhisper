Dennstaedtia scabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Dennstaedtia scabra?

Dennstaedtia scabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the order Polypodiales, this plant is a member of the family Dennstaedtiaceae. Its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Dennstaedtia and the species scabra.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dennstaedtia scabra has rhizomes that are creeping or ascending, covered in brownish scales, and bears pinnate fronds that can reach heights of up to one meter.

The stipes are typically dark brown or blackish and are densely covered in stiff, bristly hairs. Each frond consists of multiple pinnae that are further divided into smaller segments, giving the foliage a delicate, feathery appearance. The margins of these segments are often serrated or irregularly lobed.

These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in the shaded, moist environments of forest understories.
