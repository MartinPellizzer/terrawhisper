Dennstaedtia scabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Dennstaedtia scabra?

Dennstaedtia scabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the order Polypodiales, this plant is a member of the family Dennstaedtiaceae. Its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Dennstaedtia and the species scabra.

    Dennstaedtia scabra can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or localized dermatitis if a person comes into direct contact with its foliage or spores. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, many ferns in this family possess physical or chemical properties that trigger sensitivity. Additionally, inhaling dispersed spores could potentially lead to respiratory discomfort in sensitive individuals. Users should exercise caution and avoid touching the plant to prevent these adverse physical responses.
