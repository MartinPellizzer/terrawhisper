Dennstaedtia scabra

What's the taxonomical classification of Dennstaedtia scabra?

Dennstaedtia scabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the order Polypodiales, this plant is a member of the family Dennstaedtiaceae. Its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Dennstaedtia and the species scabra.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dennstaedtia scabra contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including polyphenols, flavonoids, and tannins that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents provide significant antioxidant properties by neutralizing free radicals within biological systems. Research into its chemical profile suggests these compounds may possess anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities. The specific concentrations of these phytochemicals vary depending on the environmental conditions of the plant's habitat. Such medicinal properties make the species a subject of interest for pharmacological studies involving natural extracts.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat stomachaches, diarrhea, and various types of intestinal pain. Local practitioners often prepare decoctions from the rhizomes to alleviate these gastrointestinal issues. Beyond digestive ailments, it is sometimes applied to soothe skin irritations or minor wounds. The medicinal properties are primarily concentrated within the underground parts of the fern. Knowledge of these specific uses is held by traditional communities in certain tropical regions.
