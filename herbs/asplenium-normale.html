Asplenium normale

What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium normale?

Asplenium normale belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, followed by the order Polypodiales. The plant is a member of the family Aspleniaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Asplenium, with its unique designation being the species normale.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

While many ferns are considered non-toxic, direct contact with the fronds may trigger a localized rash or redness in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience swelling or itching if the plant's sap touches their skin during handling. Ingestion of the plant material could potentially lead to nausea or abdominal discomfort. It is always best to avoid direct contact or consumption to prevent these adverse reactions.
