Asplenium normale

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium normale has large, pinnate fronds that typically measure up to 1 meter in length and consist of dark green, oblong pinnae arranged along a sturdy, dark brown rachis. The individual leaflets are characterized by their entire margins and a somewhat leathery texture that provides structural integrity. These fronds emerge from a compact, creeping rhizome that anchors the plant to its substrate. The underside of each leaflet features sori arranged in rows, which are a defining reproductive characteristic of the species. This morphological structure allows the fern to efficiently capture light in shaded, humid environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across parts of India, Myanmar, Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. Within these habitats, the species is often found clinging to damp rocks or growing in moist, organic debris. Its distribution is closely tied to areas with consistent rainfall and high levels of ambient moisture.       Asplenium normale is prepared primarily as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice for medicinal use.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Asplenium normale can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or digestive upset if ingested by humans or animals. While many ferns are considered non-toxic, direct contact with the fronds may trigger a localized rash or redness in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience swelling or itching if the plant's sap touches their skin during handling. Ingestion of the plant material could potentially lead to nausea or abdominal discomfort. It is always best to avoid direct contact or consumption to prevent these adverse reactions.
