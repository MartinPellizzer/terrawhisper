Heartleaf Hawthorn (Crataegus Cordata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus cordata.

Heartleaf hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus cordata, is a medicinal herb native to North America, prized for its adaptogenic and cardiovascular benefits. This shrub, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers, has been traditionally used by Native American tribes for its calming and heart-supporting properties. Its primary benefits include improving circulation, reducing stress, and supporting heart health, making it a valuable herb in both traditional and modern wellness practices. Historically, it was used to treat ailments like hypertension and anxiety, and today it is incorporated into herbal supplements and teas for its unique blend of antioxidants and flavonoids. Its distinctive bitter flavor and rare cardiac glycosides make it a unique and historically significant plant in the world of herbal medicine.
