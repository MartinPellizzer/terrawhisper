Urceola tournieri

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Urceola tournieri has opposite, elliptical leaves with entire margins and a distinctively coriaceous texture. The foliage is typically dark green and can reach several centimeters in length depending on the maturity of the specimen. Its woody stems exhibit a branching pattern characteristic of small shrubs or subshrubs within the Rubiaceae family. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often presenting in clusters or solitary arrangements. The plant's overall structure is compact, supporting a dense arrangement of leafage that protects the inner stem.
