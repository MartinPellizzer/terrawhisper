Melaleuca ciliata

Melaleuca ciliata, also known as the hairy honey myrtle, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to the southwestern region of Western Australia.

It has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, particularly its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which have been attributed to the presence of essential oils such as melaleuca ciliata oil.

The health benefits of Melaleuca ciliata include its ability to aid in wound healing, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory issues.

Melaleuca ciliata is often prepared into herbal remedies such as teas, tinctures, and salves, and is also used in aromatherapy due to its distinctive and sweet fragrance.
