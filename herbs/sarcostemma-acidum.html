What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds (+)-pinoresinol, 9alpha-hydroxypinoresinol, Sacidumlignan A, Sacidumlignan D, and perforatic acid. Research suggests that these chemical constituents may contribute to the plant's overall therapeutic potential. Scientists study these unique molecules to understand their interactions with human cellular pathways. Each compound plays a distinct role in the complex phytochemical profile of the organism.

As discussed in a study, the ethanolic extract of the whole plant of Sarcostemma acidum contains four lignans known as sacidumlignan A-D (1-4). Two degraded lignan derivatives, sacidumols A (5) and B (6), were also isolated from the plant. Additionally, the study identified four known compounds: (+) -pinoresinol, 9alpha-hydroxypinoresinol, perforatic acid, and peucenine-7-O-methyl ether. One specific compound, sacidumlignan D (4), was assigned as a rearranged tetrahydrofuran lignan with an unprecedented skeleton. Furthermore, sacidumlignan A (1) showed moderate antimicrobial activities against 2 Gram-positive bacteria in vitro.
