Orange Coneflower (Calendula Suffruticosa Subsp. Marginata)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Calendula suffruticosa subsp. marginata.

Orange coneflower, scientifically known as Calendula suffruticosa subsp. marginata, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States, prized for its medicinal properties and distinctive orange-yellow blooms. This perennial herb, often referred to as a medicinal herb and adaptogen, has been historically valued for its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and wound-healing qualities. Traditionally used by Native American tribes and early European settlers, it was applied topically to treat skin conditions, reduce inflammation, and promote healing of cuts and burns. In modern wellness practices, it is commonly used in topical salves, creams, and teas to support skin health and alleviate minor ailments. Notably, its unique orange hue and slightly spicy, honey-like aroma set it apart, while its bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids and triterpenes, contribute to its therapeutic reputation.
