What's the taxonomical classification of Panax zingiberensis?

Panax zingiberensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Apiales. This plant is a member of the family Araliaceae, falling under the genus Panax, and is specifically identified by the species name zingiberensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

These compounds also interact with peroxisome proliferator-activated receptors to regulate lipid metabolism and glucose homeostasis. Additionally, the plant's constituents influence various matrix metalloproteinases which can assist in managing tissue remodeling and wound healing. Through these multifaceted protein interactions, the plant demonstrates significant potential in treating metabolic and inflammatory disorders.
