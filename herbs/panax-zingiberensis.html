Panax zingiberensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Panax zingiberensis?

Panax zingiberensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Apiales. This plant is a member of the family Araliaceae, falling under the genus Panax, and is specifically identified by the species name zingiberensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds ginsenoside Rb1, ginsenoside Rg1, ginsenoside F3, ginsenoside Rf, and ginsenoside Ro. These specific saponins are bioactive molecules that contribute to the therapeutic properties of Panax zingiberensis. Researchers study these chemical constituents to understand how they influence various physiological processes in the human body. The presence of these diverse ginsenosides suggests that the plant may possess significant adaptogenic and pharmacological potential. Such complex chemical profiles are characteristic of high-quality medicinal ginseng species.

According to a study published by "Plant physiology and biochemistry : PPB", Panax zingiberensis is rich in oleanane-type ginsenosides and serves as a valuable traditional Chinese medicine. The plant contains accumulations of ginsenoside Rg1 (G-Rg1), ginsenoside Rb1 (G-Rb1), ginsenoside Ro (G-Ro), chikusetsusaponin IVa (C-IVa), and chikusetsusaponin IV (C-IV). These medicinal compounds were quantified in the different tissues of 2-year-old and 4-year-old Panax zingiberensis. The ginsenosides primarily accumulate in the underground part, with higher concentrations found in the biennial of the plant. In vitro enzymatic assays showed that PzUGT2 catalyzes the reaction of oleanolic acid 3-O-beta-D-glucuronide and C-IVa with UDP-Glc to form zingiberoside R1 and G-Ro.
