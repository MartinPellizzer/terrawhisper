African Cherry (Prunus africana)

African Cherry (Prunus africana) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to West Africa, Central Africa, and Eastern Africa. Traditionally, its bark, fruits, and leaves have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, astringent, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Perennial shrub.
Height

Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface dark green and lower surface pale green, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are solitary, zygomorphic, with five petals arranged in a bilabiate structure, and exhibit a bilaterally symmetric pattern with a distinct tubular corolla and two lips.
Stems
Erect growth habit with opposite branching, glabrous surface, and presence of stipular scars.
