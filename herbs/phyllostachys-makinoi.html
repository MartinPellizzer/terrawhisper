What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllostachys makinoi?

Phyllostachys makinoi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Poales and falls under the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Phyllostachys and the species makinoi.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Phyllostachys makinoi can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and respiratory distress if its dust or spores are inhaled. Contact with the plant's surface may trigger redness, itching, or swelling in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience an inflammatory response if the sap touches open wounds. Inhalation of airborne particles could lead to coughing or throat irritation. It is advisable to use protective gloves and masks when handling this species to minimize these risks.
