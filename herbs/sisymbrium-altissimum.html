Sisymbrium altissimum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sisymbrium altissimum has an erect, herbaceous growth habit characterized by branched stems that can reach heights of up to 100 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and pinnatifid, while the cauline leaves are smaller and more sparsely distributed along the upper stem. Tiny, white to yellowish flowers are arranged in terminal racemes, featuring four petals that are typically shorter than the sepals. The fruit consists of slender, elongated siliques that contain numerous small seeds. This plant's overall structure is adapted to open, disturbed habitats where its upright form allows for efficient seed dispersal.
