Rubus innominatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus innominatus?

Rubus innominatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this biological hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant further falls under the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic placement is defined by the genus Rubus and the species innominatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus innominatus has woody, arching stems that are densely covered in small, sharp prickles and feature trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage consists of three distinct leaflets that typically exhibit a slightly textured or rugose surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often presenting white or pale petals. The plant develops aggregate fruits that transition from green to a deep red or black hue upon ripening.
