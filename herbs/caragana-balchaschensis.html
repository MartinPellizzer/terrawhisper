What is Caragana balchaschensis?

Caragana balchaschensis is a shrub that belongs to the family Fabaceae.

Its botanical characteristics include deciduous, glabrous, and 1-2 m tall stems, with trifoliate leaves that have 3 ovate leaflets and a 1-2.5 cm long petiole. The plant produces pinkish-white flowers in axillary racemes, with 5-6 mm long corolla and 10-12 mm long calyx. Fruits are flat, 3-5 cm long pods that contain 2-3 seeds.

It is native to the Tian Shan Mountains in eastern Kazakhstan and western China.
