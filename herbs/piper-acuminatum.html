Piper Longum (Piper Acuminatum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Piper acuminatum.

Piper acuminatum, commonly known as the Indian pepper or long pepper, has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine for its various therapeutic properties. It contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including piperine, which is known to enhance the absorption of other nutrients and compounds in the body. In medicinal applications, Piper acuminatum is often used to aid digestion, reduce inflammation, and relieve respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma. Its warming properties make it beneficial for treating colds, coughs, and muscle pain. Additionally, it has shown potential in supporting metabolic health and may contribute to the management of diabetes and obesity when used as part of a holistic treatment approach.
