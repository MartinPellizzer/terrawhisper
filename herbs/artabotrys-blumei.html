Artabotrys blumei

What's the taxonomical classification of Artabotrys blumei?

Artabotrys blumei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Magnoliales. The plant is a member of the family Annonaceae, specifically categorized under the genus Artabotrys, and is identified by the species name bluemi.

What are the therapeutic actions of Artabotrys blumei?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin conditions, wounds, and stomach-related ailments in traditional medicinal practices. The leaves and bark are often applied topically to help heal superficial injuries and skin infections. When prepared as a decoction, it is sometimes ingested to alleviate digestive discomforts. Local healers utilize its bioactive properties to manage minor inflammatory responses in the body. Research into its specific chemical constituents continues to explore its potential therapeutic applications.

What are the herbal preparations of Artabotrys blumei?

Artabotrys blumei is prepared as a decoction using the dried leaves or a poultice made from the crushed plant parts. These various preparations are applied to address different traditional medicinal needs. The leaf decoction is often consumed orally to treat internal ailments. Meanwhile, the topical poultice serves to soothe localized skin irritations or inflammation. Practitioners select the specific form based on the severity and location of the symptoms being treated.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Artabotrys bluemi can cause severe skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and mucosal inflammation due to the presence of toxic compounds in its tissues. These chemical irritants often trigger redness, itching, and swelling upon direct contact with the skin. If the sap or foliage is accidentally ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as vomiting or abdominal pain. Some individuals might also experience allergic reactions if they inhale pollen or dust from the plant. Because of these potential hazards, careful handling is necessary to avoid physical injury or systemic discomfort.
