What's the taxonomical classification of Artabotrys blumei?

Artabotrys blumei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Magnoliales. The plant is a member of the family Annonaceae, specifically categorized under the genus Artabotrys, and is identified by the species name bluemi.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Artabotrys bluemi can cause severe skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and mucosal inflammation due to the presence of toxic compounds in its tissues. These chemical irritants often trigger redness, itching, and swelling upon direct contact with the skin. If the sap or foliage is accidentally ingested, it may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as vomiting or abdominal pain. Some individuals might also experience allergic reactions if they inhale pollen or dust from the plant. Because of these potential hazards, careful handling is necessary to avoid physical injury or systemic discomfort.
