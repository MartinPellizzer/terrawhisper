Eleutherococcus nodiflorus

What's the taxonomical classification of Eleutherococcus nodiflorus?

Eleutherococcus nodiflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Apiales. It is a prominent representative of the family Araliaceae, falling under the genus Eleutherococcus, and is specifically identified by its species name, nodiflorus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eleutherococcus nodiflorus has a deciduous, woody shrub habit characterized by arching stems and palmately compound leaves that typically feature five to seven leaflets. The leaflets are ovate to elliptical in shape, possessing serrated margins and a slightly leathery texture. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge in axillary clusters, eventually developing into small, globose drupes that turn black or dark purple when ripe. The bark of the mature stems is relatively smooth, though it may develop subtle lenticels as the plant ages. This species is distinguished from its relatives by its specific branching patterns and the arrangement of its leaf clusters.
