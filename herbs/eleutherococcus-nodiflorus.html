Eleutherococcus nodiflorus

What's the taxonomical classification of Eleutherococcus nodiflorus?

Eleutherococcus nodiflorus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Apiales. It is a prominent representative of the family Araliaceae, falling under the genus Eleutherococcus, and is specifically identified by its species name, nodiflorus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Eleutherococcus nodiflorus contains a complex mixture of bioactive compounds including eleutherosides, sesquiterpenes, polysaccharides, and various lignans. These specific eleutherosides function as adaptogens that help the body manage physiological stress and maintain homeostasis. The presence of polysaccharides supports immune system modulation and enhances cellular defense mechanisms. Additionally, the plant's unique lignan profile contributes to its potential metabolic and antioxidant activities. Together, these chemical constituents provide the foundation for its traditional use in promoting energy and physical stamina.

What are the therapeutic actions of Eleutherococcus nodiflorus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat symptoms of physical fatigue, mental exhaustion, and various stress-related ailments through its adaptogenic properties. It helps the body manage physiological stress by stabilizing the metabolic processes and supporting the immune system. Herbalists often prescribe it to combat general malaise and to improve performance during periods of intense physical or mental exertion. Additionally, it is sometimes utilized to bolster resistance against infections and to enhance overall vitality. This species is frequently selected for its ability to promote homeostasis in the human body.
