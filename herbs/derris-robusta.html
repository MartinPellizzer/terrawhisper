Derris robusta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Derris robusta has a woody climbing vine habit characterized by large, compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers. The foliage typically consists of trifoliate leaflets that are elliptic or ovate in shape with smooth margins. Its stems are robust and capable of twisting around host trees to reach higher light levels in the forest canopy. The inflorescence emerges in dense racemes, displaying colors that range from pale yellow to creamy white. Small, legume-like pods develop from the flowers once pollination is complete.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Derris robusta contains rotenone, a potent isoquinoline alkaloid that serves as a natural insecticide and piscicide. This specific compound acts by inhibiting the mitochondrial electron transport chain, effectively disrupting cellular respiration in target organisms. Researchers have studied its potential applications in controlling various insect pests and managing parasitic infestations. Beyond its insecticidal properties, the chemical structure of rotenone offers a foundation for developing new pharmaceutical agents. Scientific investigations continue to explore how these bioactive molecules might impact human health or provide therapeutic benefits in controlled settings.

Derris robusta has insecticidal, acaricidal, and piscicidal properties derived from its high concentration of the rotenoid compound rotenone. What are the herbal preparations of Derris robusta?

Derris robusta is prepared primarily as a crude powder or a concentrated liquid extract derived from its roots to utilize its insecticidal properties. The dried roots are often crushed into a fine dust that can be applied directly to surfaces or mixed into topical ointments. In some traditional applications, a decoction is made by boiling the root material in water to create a potent solution for pest control. This liquid form can also be formulated into sprays for agricultural or domestic use. These various preparations leverage the natural rotenone content found within the plant tissues.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Derris robusta can cause severe neurotoxicity, skin irritation, and potential respiratory distress if the rotenone it contains is ingested or inhaled. This plant is a primary source of rotenone, a potent chemical that interferes with cellular respiration by inhibiting mitochondrial function. When humans come into contact with high concentrations, it may lead to symptoms such as dizziness, nausea, or tremors. Topical exposure can result in localized dermatitis or allergic reactions on the skin. Because of these significant health risks, the plant must be handled with extreme caution to avoid accidental poisoning.
