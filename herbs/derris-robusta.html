Derris robusta

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris robusta?

Derris robusta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae and is specifically placed within the genus Derris, with its unique designation being the species robusta.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Derris robusta contains rotenone, a potent isoquinoline alkaloid that serves as a natural insecticide and piscicide. This specific compound acts by inhibiting the mitochondrial electron transport chain, effectively disrupting cellular respiration in target organisms. Researchers have studied its potential applications in controlling various insect pests and managing parasitic infestations. Beyond its insecticidal properties, the chemical structure of rotenone offers a foundation for developing new pharmaceutical agents. Scientific investigations continue to explore how these bioactive molecules might impact human health or provide therapeutic benefits in controlled settings.

What are the therapeutic actions of Derris robusta?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various insect infestations by acting as a potent natural insecticide against mosquitoes, lice, fleas, and ants. The active compound known as rotenone serves as a respiratory inhibitor that disrupts the metabolic processes of these pests. It is frequently applied in agricultural settings to control harmful beetles and caterpillars that damage crops. Beyond farming, the extracts are often utilized in household products to manage domestic insect populations. Its ability to target specific organisms makes it a valuable traditional remedy for managing pest-borne discomfort.
