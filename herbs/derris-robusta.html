Derris robusta

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris robusta?

Derris robusta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae and is specifically placed within the genus Derris, with its unique designation being the species robusta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Derris robusta has a woody climbing vine habit characterized by large, compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers. The foliage typically consists of trifoliate leaflets that are elliptic or ovate in shape with smooth margins. Its stems are robust and capable of twisting around host trees to reach higher light levels in the forest canopy. The inflorescence emerges in dense racemes, displaying colors that range from pale yellow to creamy white. Small, legume-like pods develop from the flowers once pollination is complete.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and parts of India. Its distribution is closely tied to the availability of high rainfall and consistent warm temperatures characteristic of these equatorial zones. What are the pharmacological activities of Derris robusta?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including potent α-glucosidase inhibitory activity, antioxidant properties, and antimicrobial effects. The inhibition of the α-glucosidase enzyme suggests that extracts from Derris robusta may play a role in managing postprandial blood glucose levels in diabetic models. Beyond glucose regulation, various phytochemical constituents within the plant contribute to its ability to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. These bioactive compounds also demonstrate the capacity to suppress the growth of several pathogenic bacterial strains. Such diverse medicinal properties highlight the potential of this species for developing new therapeutic agents in metabolic and infectious disease management.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating skin infections, parasitic infestations, and various insect-related ailments. It contains rotenone, a potent natural compound that functions as an effective insecticide and piscicide. Traditional applications often involve using extracts to manage lice, scabies, and other external parasites on the body. Some cultures also utilize the plant to control agricultural pests that damage essential food crops. Due to its high toxicity to aquatic life, it is frequently employed in fishing to stun fish for easy collection.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe rotenone toxicity characterized by symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, dizziness, headache, muscle weakness, hypotension, respiratory distress, and convulsions. These physiological reactions occur when the chemical compounds within the plant disrupt cellular energy production. Exposure can lead to a rapid decline in physical stability and neurological function. The severity of these side effects often depends on the level of contact or ingestion. Monitoring for these specific medical emergencies is essential if accidental exposure occurs.
