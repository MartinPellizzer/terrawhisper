Derris robusta

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris robusta?

Derris robusta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae and is specifically placed within the genus Derris, with its unique designation being the species robusta.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Derris robusta contains rotenone, a potent isoquinoline alkaloid that serves as a natural insecticide and piscicide. This specific compound acts by inhibiting the mitochondrial electron transport chain, effectively disrupting cellular respiration in target organisms. Researchers have studied its potential applications in controlling various insect pests and managing parasitic infestations. Beyond its insecticidal properties, the chemical structure of rotenone offers a foundation for developing new pharmaceutical agents. Scientific investigations continue to explore how these bioactive molecules might impact human health or provide therapeutic benefits in controlled settings.

What are the therapeutic actions of Derris robusta?

Derris robusta has potent insecticidal and antiparasitic properties primarily due to the presence of the rotenoid compound called rotenone. This natural chemical acts as a respiratory inhibitor by blocking the electron transport chain within the mitochondria of target organisms. In traditional medicine, extracts from the roots are applied topically to treat various skin conditions and parasitic infections. The substance is also utilized in agricultural settings as a botanical pesticide to control nematodes and various insect larvae. While effective against pests, its high toxicity necessitates careful handling to avoid adverse effects on non-target species.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various insect infestations by acting as a potent natural insecticide against mosquitoes, lice, fleas, and ants. The active compound known as rotenone serves as a respiratory inhibitor that disrupts the metabolic processes of these pests. It is frequently applied in agricultural settings to control harmful beetles and caterpillars that damage crops. Beyond farming, the extracts are often utilized in household products to manage domestic insect populations. Its ability to target specific organisms makes it a valuable traditional remedy for managing pest-borne discomfort.

What are the herbal preparations of Derris robusta?

Derris robusta is prepared primarily as a crude powder or a concentrated liquid extract derived from its roots to utilize its insecticidal properties. The dried roots are often crushed into a fine dust that can be applied directly to surfaces or mixed into topical ointments. In some traditional applications, a decoction is made by boiling the root material in water to create a potent solution for pest control. This liquid form can also be formulated into sprays for agricultural or domestic use. These various preparations leverage the natural rotenone content found within the plant tissues.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Derris robusta can cause severe neurotoxicity, skin irritation, and potential respiratory distress if the rotenone it contains is ingested or inhaled. This plant is a primary source of rotenone, a potent chemical that interferes with cellular respiration by inhibiting mitochondrial function. When humans come into contact with high concentrations, it may lead to symptoms such as dizziness, nausea, or tremors. Topical exposure can result in localized dermatitis or allergic reactions on the skin. Because of these significant health risks, the plant must be handled with extreme caution to avoid accidental poisoning.
