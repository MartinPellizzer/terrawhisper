Gymnosporia esquirolii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gymnosporia esquirolii has small, elliptic to obovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely or in clusters on woody, branched stems. The leaf margins are smooth and the texture can vary from glabrous to slightly pubescent depending on the specific specimen. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often borne in axillary clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy drupes that serve as its primary fruit type. These morphological traits are characteristic of its growth habit within its natural habitat.
