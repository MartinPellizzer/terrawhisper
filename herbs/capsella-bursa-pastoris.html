Capsella bursa-pastoris

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Capsella bursa-pastoris has a basal rosette of leaves characterized by pinnatifid margins and a small, upright flowering stem that produces heart-shaped silicles. The primary leaves are deeply lobed, while the upper leaves on the stem become smaller and more simplified. Tiny white flowers emerge in clusters from the axils, eventually developing into distinctive triangular seed pods. These pods are flat and contain numerous small seeds, giving the plant its common name of shepherd's purse. The overall structure remains compact and low to the ground during its early growth stages.

This plant is widely distributed across nearly every continent including North America, Europe, Asia, Africa, and parts of Australia and South America. You can typically find it growing in disturbed soils such as roadsides, agricultural fields, and waste areas. What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Capsella bursa-pastoris contains bioactive compounds in the entire herb, including the leaves, stems, flowers, seeds, and roots. The aerial parts of the plant are most commonly harvested for their astringent properties to treat excessive bleeding and uterine disturbances. Seeds are frequently utilized in traditional medicine due to their high concentration of essential oils and tannins. Root extracts are sometimes employed in specific herbal formulations to support circulatory health. These various components work together to provide the plant's characteristic hemostatic and anti-inflammatory effects.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Capsella bursa-pastoris contains bioactive compounds including flavonoids, tannins, triterpenoids, and various phenolic acids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents work together to provide significant astringent, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory effects. Specifically, the presence of tannins helps to constrict tissues, making the plant a traditional remedy for reducing excessive bleeding. Flavonoids within the plant also contribute to its ability to support cardiovascular health and reduce oxidative stress. Researchers continue to study these specific phytochemicals to better understand their potential in modern pharmacological applications.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Capsella bursa-pastoris targets various enzymes and inflammatory signaling proteins including cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, lipoxygenase (LOX), and certain cytokines. The plant contains high concentrations of flavonoids and phenolic acids that inhibit the COX-2 pathway to reduce prostaglandin synthesis. These bioactive compounds also modulate the production of nitric oxide by interacting with inducible nitric oxide synthase. Additionally, the presence of specific alkaloids and glucosinolates allows the plant to influence cellular metabolic pathways related to oxidative stress. This multi-target interaction provides the basis for its traditional use in treating inflammation and respiratory ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Capsella bursa-pastoris?

Capsella bursa-pastoris has diverse therapeutic actions including hemostatic, styptic, astringent, emmenagogue, and diaphoretic properties. Its powerful hemostatic and styptic effects make it an effective remedy for controlling excessive bleeding from wounds or internal hemorrhages. The plant's astringent qualities allow it to contract tissues, which helps reduce inflammation and secretions in various mucous membranes. As an emmenagogue, it can be used to stimulate menstrual flow and regulate irregular cycles. Additionally, its diaphoretic action promotes perspiration to assist in the body's natural detoxification and temperature regulation processes.
