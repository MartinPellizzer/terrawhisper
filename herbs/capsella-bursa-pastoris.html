Capsella bursa-pastoris

What's the taxonomical classification of Capsella bursa-pastoris?

Capsella bursa-pastoris belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the biological hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Brassicales and the family Brassicaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed at the genus level as Capsella and at the species level as bursa-pastoris.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Capsella bursa-pastoris contains bioactive compounds including flavonoids, tannins, triterpenoids, and various phenolic acids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents work together to provide significant astringent, diuretic, and anti-inflammatory effects. Specifically, the presence of tannins helps to constrict tissues, making the plant a traditional remedy for reducing excessive bleeding. Flavonoids within the plant also contribute to its ability to support cardiovascular health and reduce oxidative stress. Researchers continue to study these specific phytochemicals to better understand their potential in modern pharmacological applications.

What are the therapeutic actions of Capsella bursa-pastoris?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat excessive bleeding from wounds, postpartum hemorrhage, menstruation, and nosebleeds. Its primary therapeutic property lies in its ability to act as a powerful astringent and styptic to stop hemorrhaging. Traditionally, it has been applied to manage various inflammatory conditions of the mucous membranes. Some herbal practitioners also use it to help regulate irregular menstrual cycles. The plant contains specific alkaloids and tannins that facilitate the constriction of blood vessels.
