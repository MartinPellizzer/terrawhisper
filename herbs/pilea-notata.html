Pilea notata

What's the taxonomical classification of Pilea notata?

Pilea notata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is a member of the order Rosales and falls under the family Urticaceae. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Pilea, with its unique biological classification completed by the species name notata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea notata has ovate to elliptical leaves characterized by deep green surfaces marked with distinctive silvery-white patterns and serrated margins. The foliage texture is relatively thin and delicate, often exhibiting a slightly puckered or rugose appearance. These leaves grow from thin, succulent stems that branch out to create a dense, bushy habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge from the leaf axils in clusters. The overall structure of the plant is compact, making it a popular choice for decorative indoor containers.
