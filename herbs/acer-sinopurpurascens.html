Acer sinopurpurascens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer sinopurpurascens has a small to medium-sized deciduous habit characterized by spreading, rounded crowns and dark purplish-red foliage that transitions to green in summer. The leaves are palmately lobed with three to five distinct lobes and finely serrated margins. During the spring, the tree produces small, red, upright clusters of flowers that emerge alongside the new leaf growth. The bark typically becomes grayish and develops shallow fissures as the specimen reaches maturity. In the autumn, the foliage often shifts to vibrant shades of orange, red, or deep purple before falling.

      What are the herbal preparations of Acer sinopurpurascens?

      Acer sinopurpurascens is prepared as a liquid decoction, an infused tea, or a topical tincture derived from its bark and leaves. Practitioners often boil the bark to extract tannins and medicinal compounds for internal consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Acer sinopurpurascens can cause contact dermatitis, skin irritation, or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals who touch its leaves or bark. While the plant is not considered highly toxic to humans, its sap may trigger redness or itching upon skin contact. Some people might also experience respiratory irritation if they are exposed to large amounts of pollen during the blooming season. Pet owners should exercise caution because ingestion could potentially lead to mild gastrointestinal upset in dogs or cats. Monitoring for these specific reactions is recommended when handling the plant in a garden setting.
