From a botanical standpoint, watermelon is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which also includes cucumbers, squash, and melons.

With its origins dating back over 4,000 years to ancient Africa and Egypt, watermelon has been a staple in many cultures throughout history, with references to its cultivation appearing in ancient texts like the Egyptian Book of the Dead.

Another significant disease is powdery mildew, caused by the fungus Podosphaera xanthii, which covers the leaves with a white powder and impairs photosynthesis.

Additionally, watermelon is also susceptible to bacterial wilt, caused by the bacterium Xanthomonas citri, which infects the plant through natural openings and causes yellowing of leaves and stem collapse.
