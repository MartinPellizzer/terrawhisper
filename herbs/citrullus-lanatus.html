Watermelon (Citrullus lanatus)

Watermelon (Citrullus lanatus) is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, native to Southern Africa, Northern Africa, and Middle East. Traditionally, its fruits, seeds, and leaves have been used for decoctions, culinary uses, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.
