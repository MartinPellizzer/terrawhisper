Celtis timorensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis timorensis?

Celtis timorensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Rosales, the plant is further organized into the family Cannabaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Celtis, with its unique designation being the species timorensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Celtis timorensis contains diverse bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These specific chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Researchers have identified various glycosides within the bark and leaf extracts that may assist in regulating biological functions. The presence of these organic compounds suggests the plant could be utilized in traditional medicine for treating inflammatory conditions. Further biochemical analysis is required to isolate the exact molecules responsible for these medicinal effects.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celtis timorensis?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, digestive issues, and various types of wounds. The bark and leaves are often prepared into decoctions or poultices to address localized dermatological problems. These medicinal applications help soothe irritated skin and promote the healing of external injuries. Additionally, some traditional practices utilize the plant to mitigate internal discomfort related to the gastrointestinal tract. The therapeutic properties of the plant make it a significant resource in regional herbal medicine.
