Celtis timorensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis timorensis?

Celtis timorensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Rosales, the plant is further organized into the family Cannabaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Celtis, with its unique designation being the species timorensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celtis timorensis has an arborescent growth habit characterized by a woody trunk, elliptical to ovate leaves with serrated margins, and small drupaceous fruits.

The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches, featuring distinct venation and a somewhat leathery texture. Its bark often exhibits a textured surface that provides protection for the vascular tissues of the tree. The reproductive structures consist of inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy fruits used for seed dispersal.

These morphological traits collectively define its physical structure within its native tropical environment.

      What are the herbal preparations of Celtis timorensis?

      Celtis timorensis is prepared as a decoction of the bark, a juice extracted from the leaves, or a poultice made from crushed plant parts. While specific toxicological studies on humans are limited, many plants in the Celtis genus contain chemical compounds that may trigger dermatitis in susceptible individuals. It is important to exercise caution when handling the foliage or bark to avoid unexpected bodily responses.
