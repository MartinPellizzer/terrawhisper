Celtis timorensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celtis timorensis has an arborescent growth habit characterized by a woody trunk, elliptical to ovate leaves with serrated margins, and small drupaceous fruits. The foliage is typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the branches, featuring distinct venation and a somewhat leathery texture. Its bark often exhibits a textured surface that provides protection for the vascular tissues of the tree. The reproductive structures consist of inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into fleshy fruits used for seed dispersal. These morphological traits collectively define its physical structure within its native tropical environment.
