Rhodiola heterodonta

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhodiola heterodonta?

Rhodiola heterodonta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Saxifragales. This plant is a member of the family Crassulaceae and is categorized under the genus Rhodiola, specifically identified by its species name, heterodonta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhodiola heterodonta has a thick, fleshy rhizome and a basal rosette of leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. The leaves are typically green with smooth margins and are arranged densely at the base of the stem. During the flowering stage, the plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered on a compact inflorescence. These blossoms often feature a yellowish or pale hue and are supported by short pedicels. The overall structure is adapted for survival in rocky, high-altitude environments.
