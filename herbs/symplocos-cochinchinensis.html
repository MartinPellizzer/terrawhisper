Symplocos cochinchinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Symplocos cochinchinensis?

Symplocos cochinchinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Ericales and belongs to the family Symplocaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Symplocos, and its unique species designation, cochinchiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Symplocos cochinchinensis has evergreen leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape, featuring an entire margin and a distinctly coriaceous texture. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a lustrous appearance with finely serrated edges near the apex. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, typically displaying a yellowish or greenish hue. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that transition in color as they ripen. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by woody stems and a dense canopy of foliage.

What are the pharmacological activities of Symplocos cochinchinensis?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including anti-diabetic, anti-oxidant, anti-glycation, anti-hyperglycemic, and insulin sensitizing properties. Its antioxidant capacity helps mitigate oxidative stress caused by high glucose levels in the body. Through its anti-glycation effects, the plant prevents the harmful formation of advanced glycation end products. Additionally, it functions as an anti-hyperglycemic agent by effectively lowering elevated blood sugar concentrations. The plant also acts as an insulin sensitizing tool to improve the body's metabolic response to glucose.

As discussed in a study, Symplocos cochinchinensis ethanol extract (SCE) enhances insulin sensitivity via the down regulation of lipogenesis and insulin resistance in a high fructose and saturated fat (HFS) fed insulin resistant rat model. The study utilized experimental groups including ND, ND+SCE 500mg/kg bwd, HFS+vehicle, HFS+metformin 100mg/kg bwd, and HFS+SCE 250/500mg/kg bwd. After animals were kept under an HFS diet for 8 weeks, SCE administration was carried out for 3 weeks to analyze the effects. SCE administration attenuates insulin resistance in HFS rats by the down regulation of SCD1 gene expression, which modulates SREBP-1c dependent and independent hepatic lipid accumulation. This plant has been utilized in the Indian system of medicine as a main ingredient in the Ayurvedic preparation for diabetes known as Nisakathakadi Kashayam.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds beta-sitosterol, oleanolic acid, and phloretin 2'glucoside. These specific phytochemicals contribute to a wide range of therapeutic biological activities within the human body. Beta-sitosterol is frequently recognized for its ability to support healthy cholesterol levels and prostate function. Oleanolic acid acts as a potent anti-inflammatory agent that may protect liver cells from oxidative stress. Additionally, phloretin 2'glucoside provides antioxidant properties that help stabilize cellular structures. Together, these substances demonstrate the complex chemical profile used in traditional and modern medicine.

As discussed in a study, the ethanolic extract of the bark of Symplocos cochinchinensis exhibits an alpha glucosidase inhibition with an IC50 value of 82.07 ± 2.10 µg/mL. This extract produces a 3 fold increase in insulin dependent glucose uptake in L6 myotubes and a 3.5 fold increase in pancreatic beta cell regeneration in RIN-m5F. Additionally, the extract results in a 22% decrease in triglyceride accumulation in 3T3L1 cells and a 59.57% decrease in the generation of reactive oxygen species in HepG2 cells. Chemical characterization by HPLC identified bioactive compounds including beta-sitosterol, phloretin 2'glucoside, and oleanolic acid. The plant also contains minerals such as magnesium, calcium, potassium, sodium, zinc, and manganese.
