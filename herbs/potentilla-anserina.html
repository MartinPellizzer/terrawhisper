Potentilla anserina

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella anserina has a perennial growth habit characterized by a creeping rhizome and basal leaves that are palmately divided into five to seven deeply lobed, ovate leaflets with serrated margins. The stems are often prostrate or ascending and can be covered in fine, spreading hairs. Small, bright yellow flowers emerge from terminal clusters, each featuring five distinct petals that are typically broader than the sepals. The leaves often exhibit a distinctive reddish or purplish tint on their undersides, which helps distinguish it from similar species. These morphological traits allow the plant to form dense mats across moist, grassy habitats.

What are the therapeutic actions of Potentilla anserina?

Potentiella anserina has astringent and anti-inflammatory properties that are primarily utilized to treat gastrointestinal distress and skin irritations. The plant contains tannins which work to constrict body tissues and reduce swelling in inflamed areas. When applied topically, the extract can soothe minor wounds and reduce the redness associated with various dermatological conditions. Such therapeutic actions make it a traditional remedy for maintaining digestive and cutaneous health.

      What are the herbal preparations of Potentilla anserina?

      Potentilla anserina is prepared primarily as an infusion, decoction, or tincture to extract its bioactive compounds for medicinal use. The dried aerial parts are steeped in hot water to create a mild tea that is often consumed for its astringent properties. For more concentrated applications, the plant material is soaked in alcohol to produce a tincture suitable for long-term storage.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Potentiella anserina can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or animals. While it is not widely documented as a highly toxic species, any plant can trigger hypersensitivity reactions in sensitive individuals. Contact with the foliage may lead to redness or itching for those with specific botanical allergies. There is currently limited clinical research regarding its long-term systemic effects on human health. Consequently, caution should be exercised when handling the plant to avoid unexpected physical reactions.
