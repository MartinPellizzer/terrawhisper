Potentilla anserina

What's the taxonomical classification of Potentilla anserina?

Potentiella anserina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Rosales. It is further categorized under the family Rosaceae, which places it among a diverse group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is assigned to the genus Potentiella, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name anserina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Potentiella anserina has a perennial growth habit characterized by a creeping rhizome and basal leaves that are palmately divided into five to seven deeply lobed, ovate leaflets with serrated margins. The stems are often prostrate or ascending and can be covered in fine, spreading hairs. Small, bright yellow flowers emerge from terminal clusters, each featuring five distinct petals that are typically broader than the sepals. The leaves often exhibit a distinctive reddish or purplish tint on their undersides, which helps distinguish it from similar species. These morphological traits allow the plant to form dense mats across moist, grassy habitats.

What are the pharmacological activities of Potentilla anserina?

This plant has anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities that stem from its rich concentration of bioactive compounds like tannins and flavonoids. The presence of these phytochemicals allows the plant to effectively reduce swelling and inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory mediators in biological systems. Furthermore, its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, thereby protecting cells from oxidative stress and damage. Research also suggests that certain extracts can inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. These diverse therapeutic capabilities make Potentiella anserina a subject of interest for developing natural medicinal agents.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", the aqueous extracts from Potentiella anserina L. herb facilitate the formation of urolithin A, B and C by the human gut microbiota. These metabolites produced from the plant material demonstrate anti-inflammatory activity, specifically through the significant inhibition of TNF-α production. The most potent metabolite, urolithin A, showed inhibition at nanomolar concentrations of 0.625 μM with a level of 29.2±6.4% inhibition. Additionally, urolithin C was identified as the only compound capable of inhibiting IL-6 production at 0.625 μM with 13.9±2.2% of inhibition. These findings suggest that the bioactivity of these gut microbiota metabolites must be considered when evaluating the oral use of this plant.
