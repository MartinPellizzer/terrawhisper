Polygala subopposita

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Polygala subopposita has small, lanceolate leaves that are arranged in subopposite pairs along its slender, erect stems. The foliage is typically characterized by a smooth texture and a greenish hue that blends into the surrounding vegetation. Its flowers are delicate and feature a distinct zygomorphic structure with prominent keel petals. These blossoms often display subtle color variations ranging from pale violet to white. The plant's overall growth habit is compact and herbaceous, allowing it to thrive in specific niche habitats.
