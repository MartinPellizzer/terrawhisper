What's the taxonomical classification of Allium caeruleum?

Allium caeruleum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae, and its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Allium and the species caeruleum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium caeruleum has upright, cylindrical leaves that are hollow and linear, supporting a single, globe-shaped umbel of blue to violet flowers atop a leafless scape. The flowering stem is sturdy and smooth, typically reaching heights between 30 and 60 centimeters. Each individual flower is small and star-shaped, emerging from a common base to form a dense, spherical cluster. The plant features a subterranean bulb that serves as its primary storage organ and means of reproduction. These bulbs are often surrounded by narrow, papery tunics that protect the developing tissue.
