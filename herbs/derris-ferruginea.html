Derris ferruginea

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris ferruginea?

Derris ferruginea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Specifically, this plant is categorized within the genus Derris, and its unique biological identity is defined by its specific epithet, ferruginea.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Derris ferruginea contains medicinal compounds primarily extracted from its roots, which are utilized for their potent bioactive properties. These root extracts are often processed to isolate specific alkaloids and phytochemicals that exhibit significant biological activity. In various traditional applications, the plant parts are used to develop treatments for inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. Researchers also study these components for their potential antimicrobial and insecticidal qualities. The concentration of these medicinal elements varies depending on the specific part of the root being harvested.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Derris ferruginea contains high concentrations of rotenoids, which serve as potent natural insecticides and piscicides. These specific chemical compounds function by inhibiting cellular respiration in target organisms, leading to paralysis or death. Beyond these primary alkaloids, the plant also possesses various secondary metabolites including tannins and flavonoids. These additional substances contribute to its broader pharmacological potential, particularly in demonstrating antimicrobial and antioxidant activities. Researchers study these complex chemical profiles to develop new bioactive agents for medical and agricultural applications.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Derris ferruginea targets cyclooxygenase-2, inducible nitric oxide synthase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its medicinal effects. The plant contains bioactive phytochemicals that inhibit these specific enzymes to reduce systemic inflammation and pain. These interactions prevent the overproduction of prostaglandins and nitric oxide, which are key mediators in inflammatory pathways. By modulating these protein targets, the extracts demonstrate potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Such molecular mechanisms suggest the plant could be useful in managing chronic inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Derris ferruginea?

Derris ferruginea has potent insecticidal and antiparasitic properties primarily due to the presence of rotenone, a natural compound that acts as a mitochondrial inhibitor. This plant is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat various skin conditions and parasitic infestations. By disrupting the cellular respiration of target organisms, it effectively eliminates lice, mites, and other small invertebrates. The chemical constituents also exhibit mild sedative effects when applied in specific traditional preparations. These therapeutic actions make the plant a significant resource in both ethnobotanical studies and natural pest management strategies.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat infestations of various insect pests, particularly lice, bedbugs, and other small biting insects. The active ingredient, rotenone, acts as a potent insecticide by inhibiting cellular respiration in the target organisms. Because of this toxicity, it is often applied in traditional medicinal practices to manage skin parasites. In some regions, extracts are also used to protect stored crops from consuming beetles and moths. Careful application is required because the substance can be toxic to humans and other animals if ingested.

What are the herbal preparations of Derris ferruginea?

Derris ferruginea is prepared primarily as a fine powder or an aqueous decoction derived from its dried roots for use as a natural insecticide. This plant contains rotenone, which is extracted through crushing the root material to create a concentrated botanical solution. The resulting liquid can be applied directly to surfaces or diluted to treat various pests in agricultural settings. Some traditional applications involve mixing the powdered root into ointments or topical pastes. These various preparations leverage the chemical potency of the plant to manage insect populations effectively.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Derris ferruginea can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and potential neurotoxicity if the rotenone extracted from it is ingested or absorbed in high quantities. Exposure to the plant's active compounds often leads to redness, itching, or swelling upon contact with sensitive skin. If inhaled or swallowed, the toxins can disrupt cellular respiration, potentially leading to nausea, dizziness, or more serious neurological symptoms. Because the chemical properties are highly potent, accidental contact with eyes can cause significant inflammation and irritation. Caution is necessary when handling this species due to these unpredictable biological reactions.
