Dracaena letestui

What's the taxonomical classification of Dracaena letestui?

Dracaena letestui belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Asparagaceae. Within this family, it is identified under the genus Dracaena and is specifically designated by its species name, letestui.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Dracaena letestui can cause skin irritation, mouth swelling, vomiting, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested by humans or pets. The plant contains saponins, which are toxic compounds that react poorly with the digestive tract. Contact with the sap may lead to redness or itching on sensitive skin areas. If swallowed, animals often exhibit drooling and loss of appetite as a primary reaction. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential if accidental exposure occurs.
