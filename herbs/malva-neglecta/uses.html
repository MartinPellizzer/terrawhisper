Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) Uses

Little Mallow is used to treat various skin conditions such as irritation, inflammation, infections, and ulcers. It supports wound healing and may help with hemorrhoids and insect bites. Its anti-inflammatory properties make it useful for managing inflammatory skin conditions and respiratory infections.

Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) Monograph

1. Skin irritation

Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) has been used traditionally to soothe skin irritation. Its mucilage content provides a protective barrier, reducing inflammation and promoting healing. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to treat minor rashes, burns, and eczema. Its gentle nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin.

2. Wound healing

Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) has been traditionally used to promote wound healing due to its high mucilage content, which helps soothe and protect damaged skin. The plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties further support tissue repair and prevent infection in minor wounds and skin irritations.

3. Hemorrhoids

Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) has been traditionally used to treat hemorrhoids due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. The leaves can be brewed into a tea or applied topically as a poultice to reduce swelling and soothe irritation. It is valued for its ability to promote healing and alleviate discomfort associated with hemorrhoidal conditions.

4. Insect bites

Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) can be applied topically to insect bites to reduce inflammation and soothe irritation. Its cooling properties help alleviate itching and redness, while its anti-inflammatory compounds promote healing. A poultice or infused oil made from the leaves is commonly used for this purpose.

5. Skin inflammation

Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) has been used traditionally to treat skin inflammation due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant contains mucilage, which helps to calm irritated skin and reduce redness. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to alleviate conditions like eczema and rashes.

6. Skin infections

Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The plant contains compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin, making it effective for conditions like eczema and minor wounds. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil.

7. Skin ulcers

Malva neglecta has been traditionally used to treat skin ulcers due to its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. The plant's mucilage helps soothe irritated tissues and promotes regeneration. External applications of its leaves or extracts may reduce infection risk and speed up healing in chronic ulcers.

8. Inflammatory skin conditions

Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) has been traditionally used to treat inflammatory skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis. Its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties help reduce redness, irritation, and itching. External applications of its leaves or poultices can provide relief and promote skin healing.

9. Wounds

Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) has been traditionally used to treat wounds due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. The leaves can be applied topically to reduce swelling and promote healing. Its mucilage content helps soothe irritated tissues, making it a valuable remedy in herbal wound care.

10. Respiratory infections

Little Mallow (Malva neglecta) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its mucilage content helps soothe irritated airways and reduce inflammation, making it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis and coughs. It may also help loosen mucus, aiding in the clearance of respiratory tract congestion.
