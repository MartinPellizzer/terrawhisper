Curculigo crassifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Curculigo crassifolia?

Curculigo crassifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Hypoxidaceae, specifically falling under the genus Curculigo, with its unique biological identity defined by the species crassifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Curculigo crassifolia has thick, coriaceous leaves that are linear-lanceolate in shape and grow from a creeping rhizome. These leaves are arranged in a basal rosette and exhibit prominent parallel venation throughout their length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that typically emerge from the base of the foliage. The inflorescence is often solitary or arranged in a small cluster, featuring delicate white or pale petals. Its underground rhizomes are fleshy and serve as the primary storage organ for the species.
