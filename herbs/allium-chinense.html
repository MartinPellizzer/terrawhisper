Chinese Leek (Allium chinense)

Chinese Leek (Allium chinense) is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, native to China, Southeast Asia, and Indonesia. Traditionally, its bulbs, leaves, and flowers have been used for decoctions, powders, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its carminative, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, korean traditional medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Stems
Cylindrical, solid, herbaceous stems with a smooth surface, arranged in a basal cluster, exhibiting a solid, succulent cross-section and a tendency to form a rosette structure.
