Sorbaria sorbifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Sorbaria sorbifolia?

Sorbaria sorbifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic hierarchy of the order Rosales and is a member of the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Sorbaria, and its species name, sorbiifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sorbaria sorbifolia has pinnately compound leaves featuring numerous small, lanceolate leaflets arranged along a central rachis. The foliage typically exhibits a bright green color with finely serrated margins on each individual leaflet. Its woody stems are characterized by a rough texture and often possess small lenticels for gas exchange. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, berry-like fruits. These botanical structures are adapted to facilitate both efficient photosynthesis and seed dispersal by local fauna.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of the central and southern Balkan Peninsula, specifically within the territories of Albania, Greece, and North Macedonia. Its distribution is primarily restricted to high-altitude limestone habitats where rocky outcrops and subalpine scrub provide necessary drainage. Within these specific ranges, it thrives in localized microclimates that offer cool, temperate conditions throughout the growing season. The species tends to follow specific geological formations, making its presence highly fragmented rather than continuous across the landscape. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds including p-hydroxybenzoic acid(III) and succinic acid(II) which contribute to its therapeutic properties. These specific chemical constituents are known for their potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects within the human body. The presence of p-hydroxybenzoic acid(III) may assist in regulating various biological pathways related to metabolic health. Meanwhile, succinic acid(II) plays a critical role in cellular energy production and overall physiological stability. Together, these organic acids form a significant part of the plant's complex phytochemical profile.

As discussed in a study, three compounds were isolated from the Sorbaria sorbifolia ethyl acetate extract. These medicinal compounds were identified as 5,2',4'-trihydroxy-6,7,5'-trimethoxyflavone(I), succinic acid(II), and p-hydroxybenzoic acid(III). Notably, compounds I and II were isolated from the Sorbaria sorbifolia ethyl acetate extract for the first time. The study involved 36 mice inoculated with sarcocarcinoma 180 to test these constituents. Results showed that the ethyl acetate extract could improve immune function and strengthen antitumor effect in sarcocarcinoma 180.
