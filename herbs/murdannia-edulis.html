What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murdannia edulis has creeping, perennial rhizomes that produce lanceolate to ovate leaves arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The foliage is typically glabrous and features prominent venation on the leaf surfaces. Small, white or pale pinkish flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by a tubular base and delicate petals. These plants often develop fleshy, berry-like fruits that are used for seed dispersal. The overall growth habit allows it to form dense mats across moist forest floors.
