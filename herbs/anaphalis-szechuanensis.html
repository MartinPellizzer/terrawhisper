Anaphalis szechuanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anaphalis szechuanenensis has small, herbaceous plants characterized by pinnately divided leaves and composite flower heads composed of numerous white ray florets and yellow disc florets. The leaves are typically narrow and deeply lobed, providing a delicate texture to the foliage. Each flowering head is supported by a specialized structure known as a receptacle, which is surrounded by several rows of overlapping involucral bracts. The stems are relatively slender and can vary in height depending on the specific habitat of the plant. These morphological features collectively define its identity within the Asteraceae family.
