Primula poissonii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Primula poissonii has a basal rosette of oblong to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green, slightly textured, and possess finely serrated margins. These leaves grow in a tight cluster from a central stem, often featuring long petioles that attach to the base. The plant produces upright flowering stalks that emerge from the center of the foliage. Each stalk bears several nodding flowers characterized by five petals that vary in color from pale pink to deep violet. The floral structure includes a long tube that leads to a spreading corolla, often marked with small yellow eyes at the center.
