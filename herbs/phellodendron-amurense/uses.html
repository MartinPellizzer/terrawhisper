Amur Corktree (Phellodendron amurense) Uses

Amur Corktree is used to treat various skin conditions such as psoriasis, eczema, and acne. It helps reduce inflammation and irritation, making it effective for fungal infections and skin allergies. The bark's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties support healing in inflamed or irritated skin. It is commonly applied topically for rashes and other inflammatory skin issues.

Amur Corktree (Phellodendron amurense) Monograph

1. Psoriasis

Amur Corktree has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support skin health. Its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties may help manage psoriasis symptoms by reducing skin inflammation and regulating immune responses. It is often combined with other herbs in formulations aimed at addressing the underlying imbalances associated with psoriasis.

2. Eczema

Amur Corktree is used in herbal medicine to treat eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and skin-soothing properties. The bark contains berberine, which helps reduce skin irritation and redness. It is often prepared as a topical ointment or poultice for direct application on affected areas.

3. Skin infections

Amur Corktree bark contains berberine, which has antimicrobial properties. It is used topically to treat skin infections like eczema and fungal infections. The bark is often prepared as a poultice or infused oil to reduce inflammation and combat pathogens. Its astringent properties also help in drying out infected areas.

4. Skin allergies

Amur Corktree bark contains berberine, which has anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It is used externally to relieve skin allergies by reducing redness, itching, and swelling. The bark can be prepared as a poultice or infused oil for topical application. It is particularly effective for eczema and contact dermatitis.

5. Acne

Amur Corktree bark contains berberine, a compound with strong antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce acne by targeting Propionibacterium acnes, the primary bacteria involved in acne formation. It also regulates sebum production and reduces skin inflammation, making it a valuable natural remedy for acne treatment.

6. Fungal infections

Amur Corktree bark contains berberine, a compound with strong antifungal properties. It is traditionally used to treat fungal infections such as athlete's foot and ringworm. Its effectiveness stems from its ability to inhibit fungal cell wall synthesis. This makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for managing fungal conditions.

7. Inflammatory skin conditions

Amur Corktree bark contains berberine, which has anti-inflammatory properties. It is traditionally used to treat inflammatory skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The compound helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation. It is often prepared as a topical ointment or poultice for localized application.

8. Inflamed skin

Amur Corktree bark contains berberine, which has anti-inflammatory properties. It is used topically to treat inflamed skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The bark is often prepared as a poultice or ointment. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation in the skin.

9. Skin irritation

Amur Corktree bark contains berberine, which has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It is used topically to soothe skin irritation, reduce redness, and promote healing. Its astringent qualities help tighten skin and may alleviate conditions like eczema or minor rashes.

10. Skin rashes

Amur Corktree bark contains compounds that have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It is traditionally used to treat skin rashes, eczema, and other inflammatory skin conditions. The bark can be prepared as a topical poultice or infused oil to soothe irritation and promote healing. Its astringent qualities help reduce redness and prevent infection.
