Botanical Identification of Amur Corktree (Phellodendron amurense)

To identify Amur Corktree (Phellodendron amurense) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Amur Corktree, or Phellodendron amurense, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Rutaceae, genus Phellodendron, and species Phellodendron amurense. These classifications help identify its biological relationships and place it within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Amur Corktree is Phellodendron amurense, first described by the botanist A. J. Roe in 1832. It has several synonyms, including Phellodendron amurense var. chinense and Phellodendron amurense var. sibiricum. Common names include Amur corktree, Amur tree, and Chinese barbatan. It is also known by regional and historical names such as Amur tree and Siberian tree.

Morphological Characteristics

Amur Corktree is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a dense, multi-stemmed growth habit. Its root system is deep and fibrous, providing stability. The stem is stout and often covered with a thick, corky bark that becomes fissured with age. Leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Inflorescences are clustered at the base of leaves, bearing small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Amur Corktree can be distinguished by its smooth, light-gray bark with vertical fissures, opposite leaf arrangement, and yellowish-green flowers in panicles. It lacks the spines and has a distinct, aromatic sap compared to similar species like Phellodendron huashanense. The fruit is a smooth, three-lobed capsule, setting it apart from other members of the family.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Amur Corktree is native to eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Russia. It thrives in temperate climates with distinct seasons. It prefers well-drained soils, such as loamy or sandy soils. It is typically found at elevations between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Amur Corktree blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall, around August to September. For identification purposes, harvest timing is best during late summer when the bark is most distinct and the leaves are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Amur Corktree belongs to the genus Phellodendron, closely related to species like Phellodendron chinense. It shares family traits with the Rutaceae family, including compound leaves and fragrant flowers. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other genus members within the family.
