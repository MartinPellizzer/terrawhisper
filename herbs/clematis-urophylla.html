Clematis urophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis urophylla?

Clematis urophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Ranunculales, the plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Clematis and the species name urophylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis urophyllas has trifoliate leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape with distinctly serrated margins and an acute apex. The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a somewhat leathery texture. This climbing woody vine utilizes twisting petioles to scramble over surrounding vegetation for support. Its flowering stems produce small, creamy white to yellowish blossoms that emerge in late spring or early summer. The plant's growth habit is characterized by vigorous spreading through its perennial rootstock and twining stems.

How is this plant cultivated?

Regular watering is essential to maintain consistent moisture without allowing the roots to sit in stagnant water. Once established, this woody climber is relatively drought-tolerant but benefits from a thick layer of organic mulch to retain soil humidity.
