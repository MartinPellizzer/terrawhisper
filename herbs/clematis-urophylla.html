Clematis urophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis urophylla?

Clematis urophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Ranunculales, the plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Clematis and the species name urophylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis urophyllas has trifoliate leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape with distinctly serrated margins and an acute apex.

The foliage is typically dark green and possesses a somewhat leathery texture. This climbing woody vine utilizes twisting petioles to scramble over surrounding vegetation for support. Its flowering stems produce small, creamy white to yellowish blossoms that emerge in late spring or early summer.

The plant's growth habit is characterized by vigorous spreading through its perennial rootstock and twining stems.

      What are the herbal preparations of Clematis urophylla?

      Clematis urophylla is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid extract made by boiling the dried roots or stems in water. This traditional method is used to extract the active chemical constituents for medicinal or ritualistic applications. In some regional practices, the plant material may also be infused in alcohol to create a potent tincture. These liquid preparations are often the most common way to administer the herb's properties. Some practitioners may also use the dried plant matter as a powder or a topical poultice.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clematis urophylla can cause severe skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if handled or ingested improperly. The plant contains protoanemonin, a chemical compound that acts as a potent irritant to the skin and mucous membranes. Contact with the sap may result in redness, itching, or blistering upon the affected area. If swallowed, the toxins can lead to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. It is essential to wear gloves and avoid any accidental ingestion to prevent these adverse effects.
