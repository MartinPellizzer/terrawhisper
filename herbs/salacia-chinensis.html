Salacia chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls into the order Celastrales. Its taxonomic progression continues through the family Celastraeceae and the genus Salacia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species chinensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds salaquinone A, salasol A, salasones A, salasones B, and salasones C. These specific chemical constituents belong to the quinone class and are extracted from the woody vines of the species. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential pharmacological activities in various biological systems. The unique structural properties of these compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. Detailed chemical analysis is necessary to isolate each substance for therapeutic evaluation.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", an 80% aqueous methanolic extract of the stems of Salaacia chinensis collected in Thailand yielded several compounds. Three new friedelane-type triterpene compounds named salasones A (1), B (2), and C (3) were isolated from the plant. The study also identified a new norfriedelane-type triterpene called salaquinone A (4) and a new acylated eudesmane-type sesquitepene named salasol A (5). Furthermore, six constituents including 3beta,22beta-dihydroxyolean-12-en-29-oic acid, tingenone, tingenine B, regeol A, triptocalline A, and mangiferin were found to show an inhibitory effect on rat lens aldose reductase.
