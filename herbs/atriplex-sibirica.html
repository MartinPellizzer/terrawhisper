What's the taxonomical classification of Atriplex sibirica?

Atriplex sibirica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Caryophyllales. It is further categorized under the family Amaranthaceae, which contains the genus Atriplex, leading to its specific identification as the species Atriplex sibirica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Atriplex sibirica has erect, perennial stems that reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and bear alternate, ovate-oblong leaves measuring 2 to 7 centimeters in length. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly wavy, while the surfaces exhibit a distinct mealy or scurfy texture due to small bladder-like hairs. These leaves are often succulent in appearance and can range in color from bright green to a reddish hue. The inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters along the upper parts of the stems. The plant produces small, triangular fruits that are characteristic of the Amaranthaceae family.
