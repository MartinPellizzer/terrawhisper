    What is Leontodon crispatus?

    Leontodon crispatus is a perennial plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

    It has a rosette of leaves with lanceolate, serrated blades that are 10-30 cm long, and a short, stout stem that supports a solitary yellow flower head, 3-5 cm in diameter, with 10-20 ligulate florets and no ray florets. The leaves are glabrous to sparsely hairy, and the stem is usually leafless, sometimes with a single leaf at the base. The plant grows in dry, well-drained soils, often in woodland clearings, and is native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

    The flower heads are produced from June to September, and the plant reproduces by seed, with achenes that are 3-5 mm long.
