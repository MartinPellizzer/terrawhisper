Patrinia heterophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Patrinia heterophylla?

Patrinia heterophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Dipsacales and the family Caprifoliaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its genus, Patriinia, and its specific epithet, heterophylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Patrinia heterophylla has highly variable leaf morphology characterized by distinct heterophylly where leaf shape changes significantly throughout the plant's development.

The lower leaves are typically large and broadly ovate or cordate, while the upper leaves transition into narrower, more lanceolate forms. These leaves possess serrated margins and are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant also features terminal inflorescences composed of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the foliage.

The overall structure is complemented by slender, branching stems that support the diverse leaf types.
