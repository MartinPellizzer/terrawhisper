Patrinia heterophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Patrinia heterophylla has highly variable leaf morphology characterized by distinct heterophylly where leaf shape changes significantly throughout the plant's development. The lower leaves are typically large and broadly ovate or cordate, while the upper leaves transition into narrower, more lanceolate forms. These leaves possess serrated margins and are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant also features terminal inflorescences composed of small, delicate flowers that emerge from the foliage. The overall structure is complemented by slender, branching stems that support the diverse leaf types.
