Possible Side Effects of Prunus glabra

Prunus glabra can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high content of amygdalin, a compound that releases cyanide when ingested.

Ingesting large quantities can lead to cyanide poisoning, causing symptoms like headaches, dizziness, and even respiratory failure in severe cases.

Additionally, external use of Prunus glabra extracts may cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and photosensitivity, especially when applied in high concentrations or without proper dilution.
