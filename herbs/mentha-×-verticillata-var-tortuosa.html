Mentha × verticillata var. tortuosa

Mentha × verticillata var. tortuosa, commonly known as curled mint or tortuous mint, is a variety of mint plant known for its twisted and curled leaves.

This plant is valued for its various health benefits, including its ability to act as a digestive aid, alleviate symptoms of IBS (irritable bowel syndrome), and exhibit antimicrobial properties.

The therapeutic actions of Mentha × verticillata var. tortuosa are attributed to its bioactive constituents, which include menthone, limonene, and menthol, that display anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and analgesic properties.

The bioactive constituents of this plant also exhibit spasmolytic, diuretic, and expectorant actions, making it a promising natural remedy for various health conditions.

Possible Side Effects of Mentha × verticillata var. tortuosa

In rare cases, improper use of Mentha × verticillata var. tortuosa can interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and decrease their effectiveness or increase the risk of bleeding.

Additionally, topical application of the plant's oil can cause skin irritation and burns, especially if used in high concentrations or on sensitive areas.
