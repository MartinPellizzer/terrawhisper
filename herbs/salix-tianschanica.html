What's the taxonomical classification of Salix tianschanica?

Salix tianschanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the biological lineage of the order Malpighiales and is a member of the family Salicaceae. Finally, it is classified under the genus Salix, with its specific designation being the species tianschanica.

Its branches are characterized by a flexible structure that allows the plant to withstand mountain winds. The reproductive structures consist of small, drooping flowers arranged along a central axis. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountain ranges of Central Asia, specifically within the Tianshan and Altai mountain systems of China, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, and Uzbekistan. It typically inhabits high-altitude environments located along riverbanks and moist alpine meadows. The species thrives in rocky terrain and slopes where water is consistently available from snowmelt or mountain streams. Its distribution is largely restricted to these specific temperate montane zones across the Eurasian interior. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Salix tianschanica contains high concentrations of salicin, a phenolic glycoside that serves as a natural precursor to salicylic acid. This primary compound acts as a potent analgesic and antipyretic agent by inhibiting prostaglandin synthesis in the body. Additionally, the plant bark and leaves possess tannins and flavonoids that provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These secondary metabolites help reduce cellular oxidative stress and swelling in various biological tissues.
