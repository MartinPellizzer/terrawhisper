What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ludwigia perennis has perennial, erect stems that reach up to 1.5 meters in height and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves measuring 3 to 7 centimeters in length. The foliage is characterized by smooth margins and a vibrant green color, often appearing densely along the stem. Small, four-petaled yellow flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the summer months. These blossoms typically possess a central cluster of numerous stamens and a prominent ovary. The plant's root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist or aquatic substrates.
