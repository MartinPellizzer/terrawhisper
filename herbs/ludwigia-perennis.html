Ludwigia perennis

What's the taxonomical classification of Ludwigia perennis?

Ludwigia perennis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic progression continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Myrtales. Specifically, this plant is a member of the family Onagraceae, falling within the genus Ludwigia, and is identified by its specific epithet, perennis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ludwigia perennis has perennial, erect stems that reach up to 1.5 meters in height and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves measuring 3 to 7 centimeters in length.

The foliage is characterized by smooth margins and a vibrant green color, often appearing densely along the stem. Small, four-petaled yellow flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the summer months. These blossoms typically possess a central cluster of numerous stamens and a prominent ovary.

The plant's root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist or aquatic substrates.
