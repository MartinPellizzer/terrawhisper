Botanical Identification of Smooth Hydrangea (Hydrangea arborescens)

To identify Smooth Hydrangea (Hydrangea arborescens) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Smooth Hydrangea, or Hydrangea arborescens, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Hydrangeaceae, genus Hydrangea, and species H. arborescens. These classifications place it within the broader group of flowering plants and help identify its botanical relationships.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Smooth Hydrangea is Hydrangea arborescens, first described by André Michaux in 1789. It is also known by several synonyms, including Hydrangea arborescens var. mollis and Hydrangea arborescens subsp. mollis. Common names include Smooth Hydrangea, White Hydrangea, and Bigleaf Hydrangea. It is widely recognized across North America and has been used historically in herbal medicine for its soothing properties.

Morphological Characteristics

Smooth Hydrangea (Hydrangea arborescens) is a deciduous shrub with a rounded, bushy growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that spreads horizontally. The stems are upright, branching, and covered in rough, grayish bark. Leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. The inflorescence is a flat, round cluster of sterile flowers, known as a mophead, with white to pink blooms. The fruit is a small, dry capsule containing numerous tiny, brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Smooth Hydrangea can be distinguished by its large, rounded, white to pink flower clusters that remain smooth and not fuzzy. It has opposite, simple leaves with a serrated edge and a white, waxy underside. The stems are smooth and often reddish, unlike the hairy stems of similar species. It typically grows in moist, shady areas, which helps differentiate it from other hydrangeas.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Smooth Hydrangea (Hydrangea arborescens) is native to eastern North America, including the United States and Canada. It thrives in temperate climates with cool summers and mild winters. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found at low to mid-elevation areas.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Smooth Hydrangea blooms in late spring to early summer, typically June to July. It forms seeds in late summer to early fall, around August to September. For identification purposes, harvest leaves and flowers in early summer for accurate botanical analysis.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Smooth Hydrangea (Hydrangea arborescens) belongs to the Hydrangea genus within the Ericaceae family. It is closely related to other hydrangea species, sharing similar morphological and genetic traits. Its phylogenetic position reflects its close ties to related genera within the same family, highlighting its evolutionary connections.
