10 Possible Side Effects Of Hydrangea Arborescens (Treetop Hydrangea)

Hydrangea arborescens has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and interactions with medications.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption of the herb or by individuals with pre-existing conditions.

For instance, gastrointestinal upset can worsen conditions like ulcers, irritable bowel syndrome, and acid reflux, affecting daily life by causing persistent discomfort and affecting nutrient absorption.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Hydrangea arborescens if used imporperly.

1. Increases risk of overdose

Hydrangea arborescens causes allergic reactions due to its unique chemical composition.

The plant's leaves, stems, and flowers contain allergenic compounds like terpenoids and flavonoids, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

These reactions may be more common in people with pre-existing allergies or sensitivities.

2. Increases risk of overdose

Hydrangea arborescens triggers stomach upset due to its anthraquinone and polyacetylene compounds.

These bioactive molecules can irritate the gastrointestinal tract, causing symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or abdominal cramps in some individuals.

As a result, the smooth muscle contractions in the digestive system are altered, leading to discomfort and potentially disrupting normal bowel habits.

3. Increases risk of overdose

Hydrangea arborescens leads to diarrhea due to its ability to alter the gut flora.

The saponins and polyphenols present in this plant can cause an imbalance in the digestive system, leading to changes in bowel movements and frequency.

This can result in loose stools or watery diarrhea, which may be uncomfortable for some individuals.

4. Increases risk of overdose

Hydrangea arborescens induces vomiting in some individuals.

This side effect is thought to be caused by the plant's saponins and glycosides, which can irritate the digestive system and stimulate the vomiting center in the brain.

Additionally, the plant's flavonoids may contribute to its emetic properties by altering gut motility and increasing gastric contractions, leading to nausea and vomiting in some people who ingest it.

5. Increases risk of overdose

Hydrangea arborescens causes dizziness.

This side effect is thought to be related to the plant's ability to affect the nervous system and alter neurotransmitter activity. The plant contains compounds such as anthraquinones, which can influence serotonin levels in the brain, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness.

Additionally, Hydrangea arborescens may also interact with other herbs or supplements that have a similar effect on the nervous system, potentially exacerbating the symptoms.

6. Increases risk of overdose

Hydrangea arborescens increases blood pressure due to its ability to stimulate the heart and cardiovascular system.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and glycosides, have been shown to increase cardiac output and blood flow, leading to a rise in blood pressure.

This effect is thought to be related to the plant's traditional use in treating circulatory issues such as poor circulation and edema.

Hydrangea arborescens lowers blood sugar due to its active compounds, such as 2-hydroxy-4-methoxybenzoic acid and 3,5-di-O-caffeoylquinate.

These constituents have been shown to exhibit hypoglycemic properties by inhibiting alpha-glucosidase enzymes in the small intestine and increasing insulin sensitivity, ultimately reducing blood sugar levels.

As a result, individuals using Hydrangea arborescens may experience a decrease in their blood glucose levels.

8. Increases risk of overdose

Hydrangea arborescens worsens bleeding disorders by interfering with platelet function and increasing the risk of uncontrolled bleeding.

The plant's active compounds, particularly anthraquinones and flavonoids, may alter blood clotting mechanisms, potentially leading to prolonged bleeding times and increased risk of hemorrhage in individuals with pre-existing bleeding disorders.

9. Increases risk of overdose

Hydrangea arborescens interferes with clotting by inhibiting platelet aggregation and altering blood vessel tone.

This is likely due to its ability to stimulate the production of prostaglandins, which are hormones that relax blood vessels and reduce platelet stickiness.

Additionally, Hydrangea arborescens contains compounds that may interact with vitamin K-dependent clotting factors, further disrupting the body's natural blood clotting process.

Hydrangea arborescens stimulates uterus contractions.

This side effect is due to the plant's ability to induce uterine contractions, which can be attributed to its diterpenoid saponin compounds.

These compounds are believed to stimulate the uterine muscles, leading to increased contractility and potentially causing menstrual irregularities or even premature labor in pregnant women.
