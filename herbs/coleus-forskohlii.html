Coleus forskohlii

What's the taxonomical classification of Coleus forskohlii?

Coleus forskohlii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Taxonomically, it is identified by the genus Coleus, with its specific designation being the species forskohlii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coleus forskohlii has large, ovate to elliptical leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length, characterized by a deep green color and prominently serrated margins.

The leaf surfaces often display a slightly textured or rugose appearance due to a well-defined network of veins. This perennial herb typically grows in a bushy habit with square, succulent stems characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in terminal spikes.

Its overall structure is robust, allowing the foliage to form a dense and spreading mound.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Coleus forskohlii contains therapeutic compounds primarily extracted from its roots. The root is the specific part of the plant used in medicinal preparations to harness the benefits of forskolin. This active diterpene is concentrated in the root tissue, making it the sole focus for pharmaceutical and herbal applications. Various extracts derived from the root are used to support metabolic health and weight management. Some formulations also utilize the roots to aid in maintaining healthy cellular function and cardiovascular wellness.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the bioactive labdane diterpene known as forskolin, which serves as its primary medicinal constituent for regulating cellular signaling.

In addition to this key compound, the plant's chemical profile is enriched with a diverse range of terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic potential. It also possesses various flavonoids and phenolic compounds that provide antioxidant properties to the organism. Furthermore, the presence of alkaloids adds to the complex pharmacological activity found within its leaves.

These combined chemical classes work together to support various physiological functions in medicinal applications.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Forskolin (labdane diterpene)
  • Terpenoids
  • Flavonoids
  • Phenolic compounds
  • Alkaloids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Coleus forskhlii targets the enzyme adenylate cyclase to increase intracellular levels of cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP). This inhibition of adenylate cyclase occurs primarily through its active constituent, forskolin, which acts as a potent allosteric activator of the enzyme. By elevating cAMP concentrations, the plant modulates various downstream signaling pathways involving protein kinase A. This biochemical cascade influences diverse physiological processes such as smooth muscle relaxation and lipid metabolism. These interactions allow the plant to exert medicinal effects on cardiovascular, respiratory, and metabolic functions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Coleus forskohlii?

Coleus forskohlii has diverse therapeutic actions including acting as an adenosine monophosphate-activated protein kinase (AMPK) activator, a lipolytic agent, a bronchodilator, an anti-inflammatory, and a thyroidotropic stimulant.

By activating AMPK, the plant helps regulate cellular energy metabolism and can promote fat oxidation. Its lipolytic properties assist in breaking down stored fats, which supports weight management efforts. Additionally, its role as a bronchodilator helps relax the smooth muscles in the airways to aid respiration.

The plant also functions as an anti-inflammatory to reduce swelling and as a thyroidotropic to influence metabolic rates via thyroid hormone modulation.

The primary therapeutic actions of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Adenosine monophosphate-activated protein kinase (ampk) activator
  • Lipolytic
  • Bronchodilator
  • Anti-inflammatory
  • Thyroidotropic

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat obesity, hyperthyroidism, asthma, and glaucoma through its active compound known as forskolin.

In cases of obesity, the plant helps increase the rate of fat metabolism and lipolysis. Its effects on the respiratory system allow it to act as a bronchodilator to manage asthma symptoms. When addressing hyperthyroidism, it can influence hormone levels, though this requires careful monitoring.

Additionally, the plant is researched for its ability to reduce intraocular pressure in patients with glaucoma.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Obesity
  • Hyperthyroidism
  • Asthma
  • Glaucoma

What are the herbal preparations of Coleus forskohlii?

Coleus forskohlii is prepared as standardized extracts in capsule, tablet, or liquid form to ensure a consistent concentration of the active compound forskolin. Many commercial supplements utilize these concentrated formats to provide measurable doses for metabolic or hormonal support. While the dried leaves can be made into traditional teas, the extraction process is more common in modern herbalism to maximize potency. Some users also incorporate the plant into oil-based preparations for topical applications. These diverse forms allow individuals to tailor their intake based on specific therapeutic goals and convenience.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Coleus forskohlii can cause gastrointestinal issues such as nausea, diarrhea, and stomach pain in some users. Because it may lower blood pressure, it can lead to dizziness or lightheadedness if taken alongside antihypertensive medications. The plant can also influence blood sugar levels, which might pose risks for individuals on diabetes treatments. It is known to potentially interact with blood-thinning drugs by increasing the risk of bleeding. Due to these physiological effects, medical supervision is often recommended before starting supplementation.
