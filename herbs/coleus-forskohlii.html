Coleus forskohlii

What's the taxonomical classification of Coleus forskohlii?

Coleus forskohlii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Lamiaceae. Taxonomically, it is identified by the genus Coleus, with its specific designation being the species forskohlii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coleus forskohlii has large, ovate to elliptical leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length, characterized by a deep green color and prominently serrated margins. The leaf surfaces often display a slightly textured or rugose appearance due to a well-defined network of veins. This perennial herb typically grows in a bushy habit with square, succulent stems characteristic of the Lamiaceae family. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in terminal spikes. Its overall structure is robust, allowing the foliage to form a dense and spreading mound.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Africa, specifically found throughout the moist, lowland environments of sub-Saharan countries such as Ethiopia, Nigeria, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It thrives primarily in humid, shaded areas where soil moisture remains consistent. The species is also frequently documented in parts of South and Southeast Asia due to its ability to adapt to similar tropical climates. Its distribution is strictly limited to regions that maintain high temperatures and significant rainfall throughout the year. Because it is sensitive to frost, it does not naturally occur in temperate or arid zones.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Coleus forskohlii contains therapeutic compounds primarily extracted from its roots. The root is the specific part of the plant used in medicinal preparations to harness the benefits of forskolin. This active diterpene is concentrated in the root tissue, making it the sole focus for pharmaceutical and herbal applications. Various extracts derived from the root are used to support metabolic health and weight management. What are the pharmacological activities of Coleus forskohlii?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, anti-cancer, treatment of asthma, treatment of glaucoma, and treatment of congestive heart failure properties. Its primary active compound, forskolin, acts by increasing intracellular cyclic AMP levels to influence various cellular processes. These biochemical actions allow it to relax bronchial smooth muscles, making it useful in the treatment of asthma. Furthermore, its ability to modulate cell signaling contributes to its potential in anti-cancer therapies and the management of glaucoma. Finally, the plant's effect on cardiac contractility provides significant therapeutic benefits in the treatment of congestive heart failure.

As discussed in a study, Coleus forskohlii contains genkwanin, which is the only flavone reported from the plant. Genkwanin is known to possess anti-inflammatory activity. The plant utilizes two differentially expressed cytochrome P450 genes, CfCYP93B and CfCYP706C, which exhibit maximum expression in leaf tissues. These enzymes function at a metabolic junction to execute the biosynthesis of different sub-classes of flavonoids from a common precursor. Specifically, CfCYP93B may be involved in the conversion of naringenin to flavones, possibly genkwanin via apiigenin.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several potent medicinal compounds including forskolin, betulinic acid, lupeol, apiogenin, and beta-sitosterol. Forskolin is particularly noted for its ability to increase intracellular cAMP levels, which can help regulate various physiological processes. The triterpenoids betulinic acid and lupeol are frequently studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and anti-tumor properties. Additionally, the flavone apigenin offers antioxidant benefits, while beta-sitosterol serves as a plant sterol that may assist in managing cholesterol levels. Together, these bioactive molecules contribute to the plant's diverse pharmacological profile.

As discussed in a study, the dry roots of Coleus forskohlii were used to extract Forskolin using toluene as a solvent. The obtained extract of Coleus forskohlii was standardized to 30% for further studies. X-ray diffraction analysis of the root extract showed sulfur trioxide (SO3) and trimer of sulfur trioxiode (S3 O9). Elemental analysis identified carbon, oxygen, magnesium, aluminum, silicon, phosphorous, and sulfur within the plant. Carbon showed the highest weight of 75.49% in comparison to all other elements.

What health problems is this plant used for?

This plant is used for managing asthma, heart disease, hypertension, obesity, and diabetes through its active chemical compound known as forskolin. This compound acts as a vasodilator, which helps to lower blood pressure and improve cardiovascular function in patients with heart disease. It also promotes weight loss by increasing the rate of fat metabolism, making it a common subject of study for treating obesity. Furthermore, forskolin can influence insulin sensitivity and glucose levels, providing potential benefits for those managing diabetes. Its ability to relax smooth muscles also makes it a valuable traditional remedy for easing respiratory issues like asthma.

This plant can be prepared as a standardized extract, powdered root capsules, a tincture, or a decoction to utilize its active forskolin compounds. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause side effects including nausea, hypotension, dizziness, diarrhea, abdominal pain, electrolyte imbalance, and headache. Individuals consuming Coleus forskohlii may experience significant digestive distress such as nausea, diarrhea, or abdominal pain. The supplement's effect on blood pressure can lead to hypotension, which often results in feelings of dizziness. Furthermore, the loss of fluids from gastrointestinal issues may contribute to a dangerous electrolyte imbalance. Users also frequently report suffering from persistent headaches after ingestion.
