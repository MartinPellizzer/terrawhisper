What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa moyesii?

Rosa moyesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it resides in the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Rosa, and its specific species designation, moyesii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa moyesii has pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, prickles on the stems, and fragrant pink to deep rose-colored flowers. The leaflets are typically ovate or elliptical with serrated margins and a slightly pubescent texture. Its stems are woody and armed with sharp, downward-curving prickles that aid in climbing or scrambling through vegetation. The flowers are usually solitary or in small clusters, featuring five petals surrounding numerous prominent stamens. The fruit develops into a fleshy, red or orange hip that contains several small seeds.
