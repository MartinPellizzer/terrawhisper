Salvia japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia japonica?

Salvia japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic progression, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Lamiales and belongs to the family Lamiaceae. Within this family, it is identified under the genus Salvia, with its specific classification being the species japonica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Salvias japonica contains various bioactive compounds including tanshinones, phenolic acids, flavonoids, and diterpenoids that contribute to its pharmacological activity. These chemical constituents are primarily responsible for the plant's significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Specific diterpenoids found within the species are often studied for their potential to regulate cardiovascular health and improve blood circulation. Research also suggests that the various phenolic components may offer neuroprotective effects by reducing oxidative stress in cellular environments. Together, these medicinal compounds form the basis for its traditional use in managing inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Salvia japonica?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin infections, respiratory ailments, and digestive disorders. It contains bioactive compounds that help reduce swelling and soothe irritated tissues in the body. In traditional medicine, its leaves are often applied topically to heal wounds or manage skin rashes. It is also utilized to alleviate symptoms of coughs and other common bronchial issues. Additionally, the plant's properties may assist in stabilizing digestive processes and relieving stomach discomfort.
