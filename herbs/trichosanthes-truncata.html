Trichosanthes truncata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichosanthes truncata has a climbing, herbaceous habit characterized by slender, twining stems and palmately lobed leaves that feature deeply incised segments. The foliage typically displays a bright green color with distinct venation patterns visible on the surface. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often arranged in axillary clusters. The fruit develops into a small, fleshy berry which serves as a primary method for seed dispersal. These morphological features allow the plant to effectively navigate through dense vegetation to reach sunlight.
