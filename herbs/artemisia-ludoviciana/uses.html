White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) Uses

White Sage is used to treat respiratory infections, excess mucus, and coughs. It also addresses skin infections, inflammatory conditions, and fungal infections. It helps with insect bites and dermatitis by reducing inflammation and promoting healing. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties make it effective for these ailments.

White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

White Sage is traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly for infections like bronchitis and colds. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties help reduce symptoms such as congestion and coughing. It is often smoked or brewed as a tea to clear the airways and boost the immune system.

2. Excess mucus

White Sage is traditionally used to address excess mucus in the respiratory system. It acts as a mucolytic and expectorant, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the lungs and bronchial passages. This makes it valuable in supporting respiratory health during conditions like colds, coughs, or bronchitis.

3. Tuberculosis

White Sage has been traditionally used by Indigenous peoples to support respiratory health. It was believed to help clear congestion and ease symptoms of tuberculosis through its antimicrobial and expectorant properties. While modern research is limited, historical use suggests it may have played a role in supporting lung function during respiratory infections.

4. Cough

White Sage is used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. It may also soothe irritated airways and reduce inflammation. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or steam inhalation for these purposes.

5. Skin infections

White Sage has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. It can be applied topically as a poultice or infusion to reduce inflammation and combat bacterial and fungal infections. Its soothing effects help promote healing and prevent further skin damage.

6. Inflammatory skin conditions

White Sage is used topically to treat inflammatory skin conditions such as eczema and psoriasis. Its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties help reduce redness, itching, and infection. A poultice or infusion can be applied directly to the affected area for relief. It is often combined with other herbs for enhanced efficacy in skin care.

7. Insect bites

White Sage is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms from insect bites. Its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties help reduce swelling, itching, and infection risk. A poultice or infused oil made from the leaves can be applied directly to the affected area for relief.

8. Skin inflammation

White Sage is used topically to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation associated with conditions like eczema or psoriasis. A poultice or infusion can be applied directly to the affected area for localized relief. Its soothing effects make it a valuable herb in herbal skincare practices.

9. Dermatitis

White Sage is traditionally used to treat dermatitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps soothe skin irritation and reduce redness. Infusions or poultices made from the plant can be applied topically for relief. Its use aligns with traditional herbal medicine practices for skin conditions.

10. Fungal infections

White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) has been traditionally used to treat fungal infections due to its antifungal properties. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infusion to address conditions like athlete's foot and ringworm. Its active compounds inhibit fungal growth, making it a valued remedy in herbal medicine for skin infections.
