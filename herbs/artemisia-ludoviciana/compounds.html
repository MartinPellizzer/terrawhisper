White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) Compounds

White Sage contains essential oil as its primary volatile compound, rich in terpenoids. Flavonoids and phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant properties. Coumarin and tannins offer anti-inflammatory effects, while glycosides and saponins support immune function. Alkaloids and anthocyanins add to its diverse therapeutic profile.

White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) contains volatile aromatic compounds like camphor and cineole. These compounds contribute to its traditional use in respiratory support and emotional balance. The oil is often used in smudging practices for purification and spiritual clarity.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana). These compounds contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and are believed to have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects. They play a key role in the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) contribute to its medicinal properties by acting as antioxidants and anti-inflammatory agents. These compounds help support respiratory health and may enhance immune function, aligning with traditional uses of the plant in herbal medicine.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, which may support immune function and reduce inflammation. This compound is often found in high concentrations in the leaves and stems of the plant.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has mild anticoagulant effects. Coumarin may support respiratory and digestive health, though its role in traditional herbal medicine is less defined compared to other constituents.

6. Tannin

Tannin in White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe mucous membranes. It also supports the plant's traditional use in respiratory and digestive health by promoting tissue repair and antimicrobial activity.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Glycosides are often responsible for the plant's medicinal actions in traditional herbal practices.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana). It contributes to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for respiratory and skin conditions. Saponins also enhance the plant's ability to detoxify the body and support immune function.

9. Alkaloid

Active compounds of White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) include alkaloids, which are nitrogen-containing compounds often found in plants. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in respiratory and digestive health. They are believed to have mild antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. However, their specific roles and mechanisms in White Sage require further scientific study.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in White Sage (Artemisia ludoviciana) that contributes to its potential medicinal properties. It acts as an antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound may support overall health by reducing oxidative stress and inflammation.
