10 Medicinal Constituents Of Artemisia Ludoviciana (Mountain Sage)

Artemisia ludoviciana has active constituents such as essential oils, terpenes, and sesquiterpenes.

These compounds exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, making it a valuable plant for medicinal purposes. 1. Artemisinic acid

Artemisia ludoviciana artemisinic acid is a chemical compound found in the plant's essential oil.

It is a precursor to artemisinin, a potent antimalarial agent used to treat malaria. Artemisininic acid has been shown to possess medicinal properties, including antiparasitic and antioxidant effects.

It has been used in the production of artemisinin-based antimalarial drugs, providing a new avenue for the treatment of malaria and other diseases.

Its effects are being investigated for various applications.

3. Artemioid

Artemisia ludoviciana artemioid is a perennial herb native to North America.
