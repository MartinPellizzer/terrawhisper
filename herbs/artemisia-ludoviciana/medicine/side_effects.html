1. Causes allergic reactions

Artemisia ludoviciana causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is due to the plant's high concentration of sesquiterpene lactones, which can trigger an immune system response, leading to symptoms such as skin rashes, itching, and swelling. Additionally, the plant's volatile oils can also cause respiratory problems and increase mucus production, further exacerbating allergic reactions.

These reactions may be more common in people with pre-existing allergies or sensitivities.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Artemisia ludoviciana triggers asthma attacks in some individuals due to its high pollen content and volatile oils, particularly thujone and borneol.

These compounds can cause inflammation and irritation in the airways, leading to asthma-like symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

The plant's essential oils can also exacerbate existing respiratory conditions, causing more frequent and severe asthma attacks.

3. Induces coma in overdose

Artemisia ludoviciana induces coma in overdose, a serious consequence of its neurotoxic properties.

The sesquiterpene lactones present in the plant, particularly artabonoside, are responsible for this effect.

These compounds can disrupt the normal functioning of the nervous system, leading to impaired consciousness and ultimately coma, as they alter the normal activity of neurons and disrupt synaptic transmission.

4. Causes cardiovascular problems

Artemisia ludoviciana causes cardiovascular problems in some individuals due to its high content of sesquiterpene lactones, specifically artabsin, which can lead to vasodilation and decreased blood pressure, potentially triggering conditions like hypotension, dizziness, and fainting.

This effect is thought to be exacerbated by the plant's ability to stimulate the parasympathetic nervous system, further contributing to its cardiovascular impact.

5. Leads to kidney damage

Artemisia ludoviciana leads to kidney damage due to its high content of sesquiterpene lactones, which are toxic compounds that can cause oxidative stress and inflammation in the kidneys.

The repeated consumption of this herb may lead to an accumulation of these toxins, causing damage to the kidney cells and impairing their function.

This damage can lead to a range of complications, including acute kidney injury and chronic kidney disease.

6. Increases blood pressure

Artemisia ludoviciana increases blood pressure due to its stimulatory effect on the heart and blood vessels.

This is attributed to the presence of certain volatile oils, such as thujone and bornyl acetate, which can cause vasoconstriction and tachycardia.

The herb's ability to constrict blood vessels and increase heart rate can lead to elevated blood pressure levels, potentially exacerbating existing cardiovascular conditions.

7. Triggers seizures

Artemisia ludoviciana triggers seizures in some individuals.

This side effect is likely due to the plant's neuroactive compounds, which can affect the nervous system and alter brain activity. The presence of sesquiterpene lactones, specifically artemisinine and thujone, is thought to contribute to its neurotoxic effects.

These compounds can cause hyperexcitability and abnormal electrical activity in the brain, leading to seizures in susceptible individuals.

8. Causes respiratory distress

Artemisia ludoviciana causes respiratory distress in some individuals due to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones, particularly artemisinin, which can trigger allergic reactions and inflammation in the respiratory tract.

This leads to symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath. The essential oils in Artemisia ludoviciana may also irritate the lungs and airways, further contributing to respiratory discomfort.

This can be a significant issue for people with pre-existing respiratory conditions.

9. Provokes stomach upset

Artemisia ludoviciana provokes stomach upset due to its tannin and volatile oil content, which can irritate the mucous membranes of the stomach and digestive tract, leading to discomfort and nausea.

This is because the plant's bioactive compounds can alter the stomach's acid balance and stimulate its muscles, resulting in gastrointestinal disturbances.

The symptoms can be a consequence of the body's sensitivity to these chemical constituents.

10. Induces vomiting

Artemisia ludoviciana induces vomiting as a common side effect in individuals who use it.

This occurs due to the volatile oils present in the plant, particularly thujone, which can stimulate the digestive system and trigger nausea and vomiting.

The irritating effect of these oils on the stomach lining and the central nervous system can also contribute to this side effect.
