Saussurea malitiosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea malitiosa?

Saussurea malitiosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Saussurea, specifically identified by its species name, maliotiosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea malitiosa has a basal rosette of leaves that are typically lanceolate or oblong in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant features a sturdy, erect flowering stem that emerges from the center of the leaf cluster. Its inflorescence is composed of a dense, yellowish-to-brownish capitulum surrounded by numerous involucral bracts. The individual florets within the head are tubular and characteristic of the Asteraceae family. These morphological traits allow the plant to adapt to its specific high-altitude environment.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

It thrives in mountainous terrains where rocky crevices and scree slopes provide its necessary habitat. These populations are typically found at elevations ranging from 3,500 to 4,500 meters above sea level. The distribution is heavily influenced by the extreme climate and specific soil compositions found in these Himalayan foothills.
