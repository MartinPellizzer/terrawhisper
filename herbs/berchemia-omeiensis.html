Berchemia omeiensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Berchemia omeiensis has deciduous woody stems, oppositely arranged elliptical leaves with serrated margins, and small yellowish-green flowers arranged in axillary panicles. The leaves typically measure several centimeters in length and possess a smooth, entire base with a pointed apex. Its inflorescence consists of multiple small blossoms that eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes. These fruits are often characterized by their rounded shape and change color as they reach maturity. The overall structure of the plant reflects its adaptation to temperate forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the mountainous regions of southwestern China, specifically occurring within the Sichuan province. It is most frequently documented growing in the high-altitude forests of the Emei Shan mountain range. The species thrives in temperate montane environments where it occupies specific ecological niches among rocky outcrops or forest undergrowth. Its distribution is relatively narrow, reflecting a specialized adaptation to these localized climatic conditions. Because of this limited range, the plant is considered a regional specialist within its native habitat.
