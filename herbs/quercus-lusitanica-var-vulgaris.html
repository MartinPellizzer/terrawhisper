Quercus lusitanica var. vulgaris

Quercus lusitanica var. vulgaris, commonly known as the Portuguese oak, is a tree species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

This plant is valued for its leaves, which have been used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties, particularly in treating respiratory issues and as an anti-inflammatory agent.

The health benefits of Quercus lusitanica var. vulgaris include reducing inflammation, fighting off infections, and lowering blood pressure.

The plant's therapeutic actions are attributed to its bioactive constituents, such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which exhibit antioxidant, antiseptic, and anti-inflammatory properties.
