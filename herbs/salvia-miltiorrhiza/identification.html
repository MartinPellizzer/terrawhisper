Botanical Identification of Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza)

To identify Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Red Sage, or Salvia miltiorrhiza, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Salvia, and species Salvia miltiorrhiza. It is a member of the mint family and is widely used in traditional herbal medicine for its medicinal properties.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Salvia miltiorrhiza is the scientific name for Red Sage, also known as Chinese sage. It was first described by the botanist Carl von Linné in 1753. The plant has various synonyms, including Salvia cordifolia and Salvia miltiorrhiza var. purpurea. It is widely recognized by its common name Red Sage and is also referred to by regional and historical names in traditional Chinese medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Red Sage is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system. Its stems are square and erect, often branching. Leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated margins. The inflorescence is a dense spike of purple flowers. Each flower has two-lipped petals and stamens. The fruit is a small, dry capsule containing tiny, elliptical seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Red Sage can be distinguished by its distinctive purple-red flowers and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It has square stems and produces small, dark purple seeds. These features help differentiate it from similar species like Salvia splendens and Salvia leucantha.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is native to China, particularly in the provinces of Shandong, Hebei, and Henan. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Red Sage blooms from June to August, producing vibrant red flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer, typically around September. For identification purposes, the plant is harvested in early autumn when the leaves are fully developed but before the seeds mature.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) belongs to the genus Salvia in the Lamiaceae family. Its closest relatives include other Salvia species such as Salvia miltiorrhiza var. haenkei. The genus Salvia is characterized by square stems, opposite leaves, and bilaterally symmetric flowers. These traits are consistent with its family relations in the Lamiaceae, which also includes plants like rosemary and thyme.
