10 Possible Side Effects Of Salvia Miltiorrhiza

Salvia miltiorrhiza has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, dizziness, and nosebleeds.

These side effects can occur when the herb is used in high doses or for extended periods. Improper use can also lead to allergic reactions, interactions with medications, and worsen underlying medical conditions like bleeding disorders or ulcers.

For instance, excessive bleeding can lead to anemia, fatigue, and decreased quality of life.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Salvia miltiorrhiza causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its chemical composition.

The plant contains a number of bioactive compounds, including polyacetylenes and flavonoids, which can cause an immune response and trigger an allergic reaction in susceptible people.

This may manifest as hives, itching, or other skin symptoms, as well as respiratory issues such as coughing or congestion.

2. Triggers hypertension attacks

Salvia miltiorrhiza triggers hypertension attacks due to its ability to increase blood pressure and cardiac output.

This is attributed to the presence of active compounds such as lithospermic acid and salvianolic acid, which can stimulate the cardiovascular system and cause a significant rise in blood pressure.

As a result, individuals may experience symptoms of hypertension, including headaches, dizziness, and shortness of breath.

3. Induces seizures suddenly

Salvia miltiorrhiza induces seizures suddenly due to its ability to interact with the brain's neurotransmitters.

The compound danshensu present in Salvia miltiorrhiza can affect the levels of serotonin and dopamine, which are involved in regulating seizure activity. Additionally, the plant's potential to alter the blood-brain barrier may also contribute to its seizures-inducing effect.

As a result, some individuals may experience sudden onset of seizures after using Salvia miltiorrhiza.

4. Produces cardiac arrhythmias

Salvia miltiorrhiza produces cardiac arrhythmias due to its ability to inhibit the reabsorption of sodium ions in the heart, leading to changes in the electrical activity of the heart.

This can cause irregular heart rhythms and palpitations.

Additionally, Salvia miltiorrhiza's stimulatory effects on the sympathetic nervous system may contribute to increased heart rate and blood pressure, further exacerbating cardiac arrhythmias.

5. Increases heart rate dramatically

Salvia miltiorrhiza increases heart rate dramatically due to its stimulatory effects on the cardiovascular system.

This is attributed to the presence of tanshinones, a group of compounds that act as beta-adrenergic receptor agonists, mimicking the action of epinephrine and norepinephrine.

As a result, the body's natural response is to increase heart rate and blood pressure, which can lead to palpitations, anxiety, and even tachycardia in some individuals.

6. Lowers blood pressure sharply

Salvia miltiorrhiza lowers blood pressure sharply.

This side effect is attributed to the plant's ability to relax blood vessels and reduce peripheral resistance. The active compounds in Salvia miltiorrhiza, such as salvianolic acid and tanshinone, have been shown to stimulate nitric oxide production, which leads to vasodilation and a subsequent decrease in blood pressure.

This can be particularly pronounced in individuals who already have low blood pressure or take medications that lower blood pressure, such as beta blockers.

7. Causes stomach upset severely

Salvia miltiorhhiza causes stomach upset severely due to its high concentration of tanshinones and phenolic acids.

These bioactive compounds can irritate the digestive system, leading to nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain in some individuals. The alkaloids present in Salvia miltiorrhiza may also stimulate the production of stomach acid, further exacerbating gastric issues.

Additionally, danshen's bitter taste and astringent properties can disrupt normal gut flora, potentially causing inflammation and discomfort.

8. Induces liver damage slowly

Salvia miltiorhhiza induces liver damage slowly due to its bioactive compounds, particularly salvianolic acids and tanshinones.

These compounds can alter liver metabolism and increase oxidative stress, leading to inflammation and potential tissue damage over time.

As a result, prolonged or high-dose usage of Salvia miltiorrhiza may cause subtle yet cumulative harm to the liver, potentially manifesting as impaired function or other complications.

9. Raises creatinine levels

Salvia miltiorrhiza raises creatinine levels due to its ability to inhibit the activity of cytochrome P450 enzymes in the liver.

This inhibition can lead to a decrease in the clearance of creatinine from the blood, resulting in elevated serum creatinine levels.

Additionally, danshen's inhibition of renal tubular secretion may also contribute to increased creatinine levels by reducing the excretion of creatinine through the kidneys.

10. Activates respiratory depression

Salvia miltiorrhiza activates respiratory depression due to its ability to inhibit the activity of enzymes responsible for breaking down neurotransmitters in the brain.

This leads to an accumulation of serotonin and dopamine, which can slow down breathing rates and increase the risk of respiratory depression.

Additionally, danshen's sedative properties may further contribute to this side effect by reducing the body's natural respiratory drive.
