Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) Compounds

The primary compounds in Red Sage include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides and terpenoids contribute to its cardiovascular benefits, while tannins and saponins support digestive health. Coumarin and anthocyanin also play roles in its therapeutic actions.

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, playing a key role in its traditional use for cardiovascular and neurological health. Specific flavonoids like tectorigenin and salvianolic acid B are particularly notable for their therapeutic effects.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. This compound is involved in the regulation of blood lipid levels and has been studied for its potential cardiovascular benefits. Its presence supports the traditional use of Red Sage in treating circulatory disorders.

3. Glycoside

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) contains the glycoside salvianolic acid, which is a key active compound known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the herb's traditional use in promoting cardiovascular health and reducing oxidative stress in the body.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza). They contribute to the herb's pharmacological properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Specific terpenoids like tanshinone I and IIA are known for their role in improving blood circulation and reducing oxidative stress. These compounds are key to the therapeutic use of Red Sage in traditional and modern medicine.

5. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may aid in reducing inflammation and bleeding. Tannins are also believed to support the plant's traditional use in promoting cardiovascular health and wound healing.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antithrombotic properties. Coumarin helps improve blood circulation and may support cardiovascular health. It is one of several bioactive compounds responsible for the therapeutic effects of Red Sage.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting blood circulation and reducing inflammation. Saponin also aids in enhancing the bioavailability of other active constituents in Red Sage.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a minor component of Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza). It contains volatile compounds like labdane-type diterpenes. These contribute to the plant's aroma and may support its traditional uses in promoting circulation and reducing inflammation.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by influencing various physiological processes. However, alkaloids are not the primary active compounds in Red Sage; the main bioactive components are tanshinones and salvianolic acids.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the therapeutic effects of Red Sage in traditional herbal medicine.
