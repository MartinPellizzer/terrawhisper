Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) Uses

Red Sage is used to treat menstrual dyscrasia, menstrual disorders, and menstrual cramps due to its blood-activating properties. It also supports cardiovascular health by addressing atherosclerosis, ischemic heart disease, hypertension, and thrombosis, making it valuable for managing various cardiovascular conditions.

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) Monograph

1. Menstrual dyscrasia

Red Sage is used to treat menstrual dyscrasia, a condition involving irregular or painful menstruation. It helps regulate menstrual flow, reduce cramps, and alleviate symptoms of menorrhagia. Its active compounds, such as tanshinones, have antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties that support uterine health.

2. Menstrual disorders

Red Sage is used to treat menstrual disorders by regulating blood flow and reducing inflammation. It helps alleviate symptoms like heavy bleeding, painful cramps, and irregular cycles. Its active compounds, such as tanshinones, support hormonal balance and uterine health. It is often prescribed in traditional Chinese medicine for women's reproductive health.

3. Menstrual cramps

Red Sage is traditionally used to alleviate menstrual cramps by promoting blood circulation and reducing stagnation. It helps ease pain and discomfort associated with dysmenorrhea. Its active compounds, such as tanshinones, have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties that support uterine health. It is often combined with other herbs in traditional formulas for enhanced effectiveness.

4. Atherosclerosis

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is used in traditional Chinese medicine to support cardiovascular health, particularly in managing atherosclerosis. Its active compounds, such as tanshinone and salvianolic acid, help reduce inflammation, oxidative stress, and plaque buildup in arteries, improving blood flow and reducing risk factors associated with arterial disease.

5. Ischemic heart disease

Red Sage, or Salvia miltiorrhiza, is used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat ischemic heart disease due to its ability to improve blood circulation and reduce oxidative stress. It contains compounds like tanshinone and salvianolic acid that help enhance cardiac function and prevent arterial plaque buildup.

6. Hypertension

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is used in traditional Chinese medicine to help manage hypertension. It contains compounds like tanshinone that may improve blood flow and reduce oxidative stress. Studies suggest it can lower blood pressure by regulating vascular function and reducing inflammation. It is often combined with other herbs in formulas tailored for cardiovascular health.

7. Cardiovascular disease

Red Sage, or Salvia miltiorrhiza, is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine to support cardiovascular health. It helps improve blood circulation, reduce inflammation, and lower cholesterol levels. Its active compounds, such as tanshinones, contribute to its protective effects on the heart and blood vessels.

8. Thrombosis

Red Sage (Salvia miltiorrhiza) is used in traditional Chinese medicine to prevent and treat thrombosis due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and promote blood circulation. The active compound tanshinone reduces clot formation and improves vascular function, making it a valuable herb in managing cardiovascular conditions.

9. Ischemia

Red Sage, or Salvia miltiorrhiza, is used in traditional Chinese medicine to improve blood circulation and reduce ischemia. Its active compounds, such as tanshinone, help dilate blood vessels and enhance oxygen delivery to tissues. It is commonly prescribed for conditions involving poor blood flow, including coronary artery disease and peripheral vascular disorders.

10. Cardiovascular diseases

Red Sage is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine to support cardiovascular health. It contains compounds like tanshinone that may help improve blood circulation, reduce inflammation, and lower cholesterol. These properties make it a key herb in treating conditions such as atherosclerosis and hypertension. Its role in promoting heart health is well-documented in both historical and modern research.
