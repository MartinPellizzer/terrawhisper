Fagraea ceilanica

What's the taxonomical classification of Fagraea ceilanica?

Fagraea ceilanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Gentianales. It is a representative of the family Gentianaceae, specifically situated within the genus Fagraea, with the specific epithet ceilanica denoting its unique species identity.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Fagraea ceilanicica contains bioactive compounds in its leaves, bark, and roots that are utilized for various traditional medicinal purposes. The leaves are frequently prepared as infusions or poultices to treat skin ailments and reduce inflammation. Extracts from the bark are often employed in folk medicine to address digestive issues and systemic infections. Additionally, the roots are sometimes processed to create decoctions intended to alleviate pain or fever. These diverse components provide a wide array of therapeutic applications in local healing practices.

What are the therapeutic actions of Fagraea ceilanica?

Fagraea ceilanicica has antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties utilized primarily for treating skin infections and topical ailments. The plant contains various bioactive compounds that inhibit the growth of harmful pathogens on the skin. Extracts from its leaves and bark are often applied to accelerate the recovery of damaged tissues and soothe irritation. These therapeutic actions are supported by the presence of natural chemical constituents that reduce swelling and redness. Local traditional practices frequently employ these botanical elements to manage minor injuries and dermatological concerns.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin infections, inflammation, stomach pains, and respiratory ailments. Traditional healers often apply the leaves or decoctions topically to soothe wounds and skin irritations. When ingested, the plant extracts are believed to help alleviate digestive distress and gastrointestinal discomfort. It is also utilized in certain folk medicine practices to mitigate symptoms of coughs and other breathing issues. The medicinal properties are primarily concentrated in the leaves and stems of the species.

What are the herbal preparations of Fagraea ceilanica?

Fagraea ceilanica is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical paste depending on the intended therapeutic use. The bark and leaves are most commonly boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for internal ailments. For skin conditions or localized inflammation, the plant material is crushed into a thick poultice and applied directly to the affected area. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant extracts in oil-based preparations for topical applications. These various methods allow practitioners to utilize the different chemical properties of the plant effectively.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Fagraea ceilanicica can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may trigger hypersensitivity reactions in sensitive individuals upon direct contact. Contact with the sap or foliage often leads to redness, itching, or swelling at the site of exposure. If consumed, the chemical constituents may provoke nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping. Careful avoidance of contact and ingestion is advised to prevent these adverse physical responses.
