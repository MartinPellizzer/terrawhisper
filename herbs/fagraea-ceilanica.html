Fagraea ceilanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Fagraea ceilanicica has a woody structure characterized by spreading branches, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire margins, and small, actinomorphic flowers. The foliage is typically coriaceous and arranged in an opposite or sub-opposite pattern along the stems. Its flowers are often fragrant and possess a tubular calyx that transitions into a more open corolla during blooming. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that serve as a primary dispersal mechanism for the species. These morphological traits are consistent with its classification within the Clusiaceae family.
