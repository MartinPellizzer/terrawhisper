What's the taxonomical classification of Taxillus nigrans?

Taxillus nigrans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this botanical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Santalales and the family Loranthaceae, eventually narrowing down to its specific classification within the genus Taxillus and the species nigrans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Taxillus nigrans has dark, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a thick, waxy cuticle that covers its deep purple stems. The foliage grows in a dense, opposite pattern along the primary stalks to maximize light absorption. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the peak growing season. These blooms possess a distinctively dark pigmentation that blends seamlessly with the plant's structural coloring. The root system consists of a robust, fibrous network designed to anchor the plant firmly in loose soil.
