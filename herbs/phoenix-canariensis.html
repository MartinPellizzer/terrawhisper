Phoenix canariensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phoenix canariensis has a single, thick, woody trunk that can reach heights of up to 10 meters and is characterized by a dense pattern of diamond-shaped leaf scars. The crown consists of a massive rosette of pinnate leaves that extend up to 3 meters in length with stiff, dark green leaflets. These leaflets are arranged in multiple planes along the rachis, giving the fronds a lush, three-dimensional appearance. At the base of the leaf stalks, small spines are often present to protect the developing growth. The plant also produces large, heavy clusters of bright orange or yellow fruits that hang beneath the foliage.
