What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola lactiflora has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by long-petioled, cordate to reniform leaves that emerge from a creeping rhizome. The leaf blades are typically dark green with crenate margins and a prominent venation pattern. Its flowers are zygomorphic and feature five petals, often displaying a distinct white or pale lavender coloration with subtle nectar guides. The reproductive structure includes a superior ovary that develops into a capsule containing numerous small seeds.
