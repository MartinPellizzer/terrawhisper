Asplenium griffithianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium griffithianum has large, oblong-lanceolate fronds that typically measure between 30 and 80 centimeters in length and possess a distinct pinnate arrangement. The leaf margins are characterized by fine, serrated teeth that give the edges a delicate texture. Each pinna is attached to a central, dark-colored rachis that provides structural support to the entire fern. The underside of the blades contains conspicuous sori arranged in rows along the veins for reproduction. These morphological features collectively define the plant's robust and spreading growth habit.
