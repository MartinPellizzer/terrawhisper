Asplenium griffithianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Asplenium griffithianum?

Asplenium griffithianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, it falls under the order Polypodiales. Taxonomically, this plant is positioned within the family Aspleniaceae and is a specific representative of the genus Asplenium, with its unique identity defined by its species name, griffithianum.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases such as rashes, boils, and wounds, as well as internal ailments like stomach aches and fever. The leaves are often prepared as a poultice to soothe localized inflammation or applied topically to promote the healing of skin irritations. In some traditional medicinal practices, parts of the plant are processed to alleviate digestive discomfort. These applications rely on the plant's perceived antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The specific method of preparation can vary depending on the severity of the symptoms being addressed.
