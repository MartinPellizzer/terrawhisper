Capsicum Annuum

Capsicum annuum, commonly known as cayenne, is a herb that belongs to the nightshade family and is native to the Americas.

This herb is rich in health benefits, including reducing inflammation, improving circulation, and alleviating pain, which are attributed to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The therapeutic actions of Capsicum annuum include analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and diaphoretic effects, making it a popular remedy for conditions such as arthritis, headaches, and colds.

The bioactive constituents of Capsicum annuum include capsaicin, a potent compound responsible for its spiciness, as well as flavonoids and carotenoids, which contribute to its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities.

Possible Side Effects of capsicum annuum

Prolonged exposure to the capsaicin in capsicum annuum can also lead to a condition known as "oral ulcers" or mucosal irritation in some individuals.
