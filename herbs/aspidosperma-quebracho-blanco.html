Quebracho Blanco (Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco)

Quebracho Blanco (Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco) is a member of the Apocynaceae family, native to Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay. Traditionally, its bark, wood, and resin have been used for decoctions, infusions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, native american herbal medicine, and african traditional medicine.
