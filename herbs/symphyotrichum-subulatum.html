Symphyotrichum subulatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Symphyotrichum subulatum?

Symphyotrichum subulatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which is further categorized into the genus Symphyotrichum, ultimately reaching its specific identification as the species subulatum.

    Symphyotrichum subulatum is prepared primarily as a dried herbal tea, a tincture extracted in alcohol, or a topical poultice made from crushed plant matter. For more concentrated medicinal effects, the plant material is macerated in high-proof spirits to produce a tincture. Topically, the plant can be applied as a wash or a direct poultice to soothe skin irritations.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Symphyotrichum subulatum can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis or localized swelling if a person touches the plant. Some individuals may experience itchy rashes or redness upon contact with the foliage or flowers. Inhaling pollen from the blooms can also trigger respiratory symptoms in those with seasonal allergies. While ingestion is less common, consuming any part of the plant could potentially lead to gastrointestinal distress. Always exercise caution when handling this species to avoid these adverse physical reactions.
