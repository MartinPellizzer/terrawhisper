Olyra latifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Olyra latifolia has broad, linear-lanceolate leaves that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length and possess a distinctively wide blade compared to other species in its genus. The leaf blades are characterized by parallel venation and a prominent midrib that extends toward the apex. Its stems are slender, erect, and often reach heights of up to 1 meter, featuring nodes that are slightly swollen. The inflorescence consists of several spreading panicles that bear small, inconspicuous spikelets arranged along the branches. These spikelets are typically solitary or in small clusters and exhibit a pale green to straw-colored hue when mature.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically within countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. It primarily thrives in moist, lowland forest environments where humidity levels remain consistently high. The species is often found along riverbanks and in shaded undergrowth where soil moisture is abundant. While its core range is centered in mainland Southeast Asia, sporadic populations may exist across broader Indo-Malayan territories. These specific ecological niches dictate the limited but distinct geographical footprint of the species.

      What are the herbal preparations of Olyra latifolia?

      Olyra latifolia is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, a paste, or a juice depending on the specific therapeutic application. The leaves and stems are frequently boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for internal consumption. For topical treatments, the fresh plant material is often crushed into a fine paste to be applied directly to the skin. In some traditional practices, the juice is extracted from the crushed leaves to serve as a liquid remedy. These various methods allow practitioners to tailor the concentration and delivery of the plant's active components.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Olyra latifolia can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the leaves may trigger redness, itching, or swelling in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience an inflammatory response if the plant's oils touch broken skin. If consumed accidentally, it can lead to nausea or abdominal cramping. These reactions vary depending on the individual's sensitivity to the plant's chemical compounds.
