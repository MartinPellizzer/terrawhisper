Olyra latifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Olyra latifolia?

Olyra latifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is a member of the order Poales and falls under the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Olyra and the species latifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Olyra latifolia has broad, linear-lanceolate leaves that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length and possess a distinctively wide blade compared to other species in its genus. The leaf blades are characterized by parallel venation and a prominent midrib that extends toward the apex. Its stems are slender, erect, and often reach heights of up to 1 meter, featuring nodes that are slightly swollen. The inflorescence consists of several spreading panicles that bear small, inconspicuous spikelets arranged along the branches. These spikelets are typically solitary or in small clusters and exhibit a pale green to straw-colored hue when mature.
