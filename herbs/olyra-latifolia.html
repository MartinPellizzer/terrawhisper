Olyra latifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Olyra latifolia has broad, linear-lanceolate leaves that typically measure between 10 and 30 centimeters in length and possess a distinctively wide blade compared to other species in its genus. The leaf blades are characterized by parallel venation and a prominent midrib that extends toward the apex. Its stems are slender, erect, and often reach heights of up to 1 meter, featuring nodes that are slightly swollen. The inflorescence consists of several spreading panicles that bear small, inconspicuous spikelets arranged along the branches. These spikelets are typically solitary or in small clusters and exhibit a pale green to straw-colored hue when mature.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically within countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. It primarily thrives in moist, lowland forest environments where humidity levels remain consistently high. The species is often found along riverbanks and in shaded undergrowth where soil moisture is abundant. While its core range is centered in mainland Southeast Asia, sporadic populations may exist across broader Indo-Malayan territories. These specific ecological niches dictate the limited but distinct geographical footprint of the species.
