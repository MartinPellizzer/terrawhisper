Jasminum urophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum urophyllum?

Jasminum urophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Lamiales and the family Oleaceae. Finally, its specific identification is defined by the genus Jasminum and the unique species epithet urophyllum.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Jasminum urophyllum targets inflammatory mediators such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) and interleukin-6 (IL-6). The bioactive compounds within the plant, including various flavonoids and terpenoids, work by inhibiting the enzymatic activity of these proteins to reduce systemic inflammation. These phytochemicals also interact with the NF-kappaB signaling pathway, preventing the translocation of transcription factors that trigger the production of inflammatory proteins. Furthermore, certain extracts have shown the ability to modulate antioxidant enzymes, thereby protecting cells from oxidative stress-induced damage. This multi-target approach allows the plant to exert significant anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects at a cellular level.

Jasminum urophyllum has therapeutic properties that include anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities used primarily in traditional medicine to treat skin ailments and infections.     What are the herbal preparations of Jasminum urophyllum?

    Jasminum urophyllum is prepared primarily as a decoction or tea made by boiling the dried leaves and stems in water to extract their medicinal components. This herbal infusion is often consumed to address various ailments such as inflammation or digestive issues. Some practitioners may also use the plant to create topical poultices from crushed fresh leaves for skin applications. Additionally, concentrated extracts can be formulated into tinctures using alcohol as a solvent for long-term storage and potency. These diverse preparation methods allow for different modes of administration depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Jasminum urophyllum can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or applied improperly. While specific clinical trials on humans are limited, the presence of certain alkaloids and essential oils in the plant may trigger sensitivity in some individuals. Direct contact with the foliage or flowers might lead to redness, itching, or a localized rash on sensitive skin. If consumed, users might experience nausea, stomach cramps, or other digestive disturbances. It is essential to exercise caution and consult medical professionals before any use of this species.
