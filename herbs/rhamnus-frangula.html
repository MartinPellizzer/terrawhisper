Rhamnus frangula

What's the taxonomical classification of Rhamnus frangula?

Rhamnus frangula belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Rosales. More specifically, the plant is a member of the family Rhamnaceae, which falls under the genus Rhamnus, and is identified by the specific epithet franugula to denote its species, Rhamnus frangula.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhamnus frangula has an erect, deciduous shrub structure characterized by slender, greyish-brown branches and elliptical, serrated leaves that grow in an opposite arrangement. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface and paler underneath, often featuring distinct parallel veins. Small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers emerge in clusters during the late spring or early summer. These blossoms eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a dark purple or black color when fully ripe. What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Rhamnus frangula contains anthraquinone glycosides primarily located in its dried bark and roots which are utilized for their potent laxative properties. These specific chemical compounds act as stimulant purgatives by increasing intestinal motility and water secretion. Harvesting typically occurs after the plant has matured to ensure the concentration of active principles is sufficient for medicinal use. When processed correctly, the bark is often prepared as a decoction or infusion to treat acute constipation. Careful dosage is essential because the high concentration of glycosides can cause severe abdominal cramping if used improperly.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, and griping. These intense symptoms often arise from the plant's potent laxative properties. If the diarrhea is frequent and uncontrolled, it can quickly lead to dangerous dehydration. This loss of fluids may also result in a serious electrolyte imbalance within the body. Such complications require careful monitoring to prevent more significant health issues.
