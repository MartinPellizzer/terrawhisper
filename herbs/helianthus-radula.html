What is Helianthus radula?

Helianthus radula is a plant species belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is an annual or perennial herb, growing up to 1 meter tall, with a woody base and a branching stem. The leaves are alternate, simple, and linear, measuring up to 15 centimeters long and 1 centimeter wide, with a hairy texture and a serrated margin. The inflorescence is a large, yellow daisy-like flower head, composed of numerous small florets, with a diameter of up to 7 centimeters and a prominent center disc.

Helianthus radula is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

What are the main herbal preparations this herb?

The main herbal preparations of Helianthus radula are tincture, infusion, tea, salve, and decoction.

A tincture is a concentrated liquid solution made from the plant's flowers, roots, or leaves in a solvent such as ethanol or glycerin. Infusion is a liquid extract made by steeping the plant's parts in hot water, while tea is a more diluted form of infusion often consumed for its flavor and potential medicinal properties. A salve is a topical preparation made from the plant's extracts and oils, applied directly to the skin to treat various conditions.

Decoction is a liquid extract made by boiling the plant's harder parts, such as roots or bark, in water to release their medicinal compounds.
