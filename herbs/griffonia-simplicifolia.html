Griffonia simplicifolia

Griffonia simplicifolia

Griffonia Simplicifolia (Griffonia simplicifolia) is a tropical plant native to West Africa, known for its rich source of 5-hydroxytryptophan (5-HTP), which is used to support mood regulation and improve symptoms of depression and anxiety.

Botanical Identification

Griffonia simplicifolia is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family. Native to West Africa, it is an annual shrub with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. Commonly known as African bean or wild cowpea, it is also referred to as G. simplicifolia in herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Fabales
  • Order: Fabales
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Genus: Griffonia
  • Species: Griffonia simplicifolia

Active Compounds

Griffonia simplicifolia contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, glycosides, and coumarins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. These compounds work synergistically to support cognitive and neurological functions. Their combined action enhances the herb's efficacy in herbal medicine applications.

Preparation Methods

Extract: Used for conditions related to cognitive function and nerve health due to its high concentration of 5-htp.

Capsule: Used as a dietary supplement for mood regulation and sleep disorders.

Tincture: Used for supporting nervous system function and managing symptoms of anxiety.

Safety Profile

Griffonia simplicifolia is generally considered safe when used as directed. However, it should be avoided by individuals with a known allergy to legumes or soy. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
