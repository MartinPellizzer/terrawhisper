Cardamine hirsuta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cardamine hirsuta has a rosette of pinnately divided leaves that feature fine, spreading hairs and small, white, four-petaled flowers arranged in terminal racemes. The basal leaves are typically larger and more deeply lobed than the smaller, narrower leaves found along the upright stem. This plant produces slender, elongated seed pods called siliques that stand erect or spread outward as they mature. Its root system consists of a shallow taproot that allows it to establish quickly in disturbed soils. The overall structure is diminutive and herbaceous, often reaching only a few inches in height.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cardamine hirsuta contains glucosinolates such as sinigrin, which are sulfur-containing compounds that break down into bioactive isothiocyanates. These specific phytochemicals are known for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties in biological systems. The plant also provides significant amounts of vitamin C and various phenolic acids that contribute to cellular protection. Some studies suggest that these medicinal constituents may play a role in inhibiting the growth of certain cancer cells. What are the therapeutic actions of Cardamine hirsuta?

Cardamine hirsuta has therapeutic properties that include acting as a bitter, diuretic, astringent, alterative, and hemostatic agent.
