What's the taxonomical classification of Cardamine hirsuta?

Cardamine hirsuta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is situated in the order Brassicales and the family Brassicaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Cardamine, with the specific epithet hirsuta, completing its scientific designation as Cardamine hirsuta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cardamine hirsuta has a rosette of pinnately divided leaves that feature fine, spreading hairs and small, white, four-petaled flowers arranged in terminal racemes. The basal leaves are typically larger and more deeply lobed than the smaller, narrower leaves found along the upright stem. This plant produces slender, elongated seed pods called siliques that stand erect or spread outward as they mature. Its root system consists of a shallow taproot that allows it to establish quickly in disturbed soils. The overall structure is diminutive and herbaceous, often reaching only a few inches in height.
