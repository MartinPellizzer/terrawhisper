Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis) Compounds

The primary compounds in Chinese Date include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Coumarin contributes to the plant's aroma and may have anticoagulant properties. Tannins and glycosides provide astringent and medicinal benefits, while saponins and terpenoids support immune function. Alkaloids and anthocyanins add additional therapeutic potential, and essential oils contribute to the plant's overall medicinal profile.

Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoid is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support liver function and vascular health. Found in the fruit's pulp, flavonoid plays a role in the traditional medicinal uses of Hovenia dulcis.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and may support liver function and digestion. This compound is often studied for its potential therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Coumarin is responsible for some of the medicinal effects attributed to Chinese Date in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has been traditionally used to aid digestion and reduce inflammation. Tannins in Chinese Date are also believed to support liver function and promote overall digestive health.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in promoting liver function and enhancing digestion. This compound is often extracted and used in traditional herbal formulations for its therapeutic effects.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Saponin is also believed to support liver function and aid in detoxification processes. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Hovenia dulcis in traditional herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic benefits in traditional herbal medicine.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting liver health and enhancing vitality. Alkaloids in Hovenia dulcis are known for their potential to support metabolic functions and may aid in reducing fatigue.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis). It contributes to the fruit's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound supports liver health and may aid in detoxification, aligning with the traditional uses of Hovenia dulcis in herbal medicine.

10. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Chinese Date (Hovenia dulcis). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and may support digestive and anti-inflammatory effects. The essential oil is extracted through steam distillation and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its therapeutic benefits.
