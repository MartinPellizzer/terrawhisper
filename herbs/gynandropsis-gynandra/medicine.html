What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Gynandropsis Gynandra (Devil'S Claw)?

Gynandropsis gynandra, also known as devil's claw, has health benefits such as alleviating pain, reducing inflammation, and treating fever.

The herb's medicinal constituents include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties. The leaves and stems are typically used to prepare teas, infusions, or extracts, which are taken orally to harness its medicinal benefits.

Possible side effects include gastrointestinal upset and allergic reactions; it is essential to consult a healthcare professional before using devil's claw, especially for prolonged periods or in high doses.

What are the possible side effect of using Gynandropsis gynandra improperly?

Improper use of Gynandropsis gynandra increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as nausea, stomach upset, diarrhea, and vomiting.

It can also cause allergic reactions like hives, itching, and swelling. Furthermore, excessive consumption may lead to insomnia, anxiety, irritability, and mood swings.

Additionally, it can interact with blood thinners, anticoagulants, and diabetes medications, resulting in adverse effects on the cardiovascular system, blood sugar levels, and overall health.
