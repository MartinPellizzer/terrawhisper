African Leafy Vegetable (Gynandropsis gynandra) Uses

Gynandropsis gynandra is used to treat asthma, cough, and respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilator properties. It also helps with influenza and inflamed throat, while its antimicrobial qualities address skin infections and fungal conditions. The plant supports wound healing and is effective against gastrointestinal infections.

African Leafy Vegetable (Gynandropsis gynandra) Monograph

1. Asthma

Gynandropsis gynandra is used in traditional African medicine to alleviate asthma symptoms. Its leaves are brewed into a tea that helps reduce inflammation and relax airway muscles. The plant's anti-inflammatory and bronchodilating properties are believed to support respiratory function in asthma patients.

2. Cough

Gynandropsis gynandra is used to alleviate cough symptoms due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Traditionally prepared as a tea or decoction, it is valued for its soothing effect on the throat and lungs.

3. Respiratory infections

Gynandropsis gynandra is used to treat respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce symptoms like coughing and congestion. The leaves are often brewed into teas or decoctions. It is valued in traditional medicine for its soothing and healing effects on the respiratory system.

4. Influenza

Gynandropsis gynandra is used traditionally to alleviate influenza symptoms due to its antipyretic and immune-boosting properties. It helps reduce fever and supports the body's natural defenses against viral infections. The leaves are often prepared as a decoction or infusion to ease respiratory discomfort and promote recovery.

5. Inflamed throat

Gynandropsis gynandra is used to soothe inflamed throats due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The leaves are often brewed into a tea or applied topically as a poultice. It helps reduce swelling and relieve symptoms of sore throat and pharyngitis. This traditional remedy is valued for its accessibility and effectiveness in community-based herbal medicine.

6. Skin infections

Gynandropsis gynandra is used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
