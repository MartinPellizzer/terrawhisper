Astragalus drusorum f. maroniticus

Astragalus drusorum f. maroniticus, commonly known as Maronitic Astragalus, is a plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

This plant is used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including reducing inflammation and improving immune function, which is attributed to its therapeutic actions such as anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects.

The bioactive constituents of Astragalus drusorum f. maroniticus include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Possible Side Effects of Astragalus drusorum f. maroniticus

Astragalus drusorum f. maroniticus can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, due to its high content of saponins.

Prolonged use or excessive consumption can also lead to kidney damage and decreased kidney function in some individuals.

In rare cases, astragalus drusorum f. maroniticus may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, and exacerbate conditions like high blood pressure and autoimmune disorders.

Furthermore, its use during pregnancy or breastfeeding may pose risks to the fetus or infant, highlighting the need for proper guidance and caution when using this plant for medicinal purposes.
