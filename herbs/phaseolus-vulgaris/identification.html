Botanical Identification of Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris)

To identify Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The Common Bean, Phaseolus vulgaris, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Phaseolus, and species vulgaris. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Common Bean is Phaseolus vulgaris, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It belongs to the legume family and is widely recognized globally. Common synonyms include Phaseolus lunatus and Vigna radiata, though these refer to different species. Regional names vary, with examples like "haricot" in French and "pinto" in Spanish.

Morphological Characteristics

Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris) is an annual herb with a well-developed taproot system. The stem is erect, hairy, and branched. Leaves are alternate, heart-shaped with a prominent midrib. Inflorescences are axillary clusters, bearing small, yellow flowers. The fruit is a dry, dehiscent pod containing 2-6 oval seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris) can be distinguished by its upright growth habit, pinnately compound leaves with stipules, and pods that are usually hairy and contain 2-4 bean-like seeds. It differs from similar species like Phaseolus coccineus by its shorter, more robust pods and the presence of a distinct peduncle.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris) is native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America. It thrives in warm, temperate climates with well-drained soils. It grows best at altitudes between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris) blooms 45-60 days after planting, typically in late spring to early summer. Seed formation occurs 10-15 days after flowering, with pods developing from green to mature colors. Harvest timing is critical for identification, usually occurring 50-70 days post-planting when pods are fully developed and seeds are dry.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris) belongs to the genus Phaseolus in the Fabaceae family. Its closest relatives include species like Phaseolus coccineus and Phaseolus lunatus. These species share similar morphological and genetic traits, reflecting their close phylogenetic relationship within the genus.
