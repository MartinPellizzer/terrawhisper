Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris) Compounds

The primary compounds in Common Bean include flavonoids, which act as antioxidants, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory properties. Tannins contribute to astringency, while saponins have antimicrobial effects. Coumarin and glycosides are also present, offering various health benefits. Terpenoids, anthocyanins, alkaloids, and essential oils further contribute to the plant's medicinal profile.

Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris). They exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, contributing to the plant's potential therapeutic uses. These compounds may support cardiovascular health and immune function, making Common Bean a valuable resource in herbal medicine.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris). It exhibits antioxidant properties that help reduce oxidative stress and inflammation. This compound contributes to the plant's potential therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support digestive health. Tannins in Common Bean are known for their antimicrobial and antioxidant effects, making them valuable in traditional herbal medicine.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris). It exhibits antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine. Saponins also aid in reducing cholesterol and improving digestion, contributing to the plant's therapeutic potential.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is an active compound found in Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris). It exhibits anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties, making it relevant in herbal medicine for conditions involving blood clotting and inflammation. Its presence in Common Bean highlights the plant's potential therapeutic applications.

Glycosides in Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris) are natural compounds with potential medicinal properties. 7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris) contribute to its medicinal properties. 8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's vibrant colors and has antioxidant properties. 9. Alkaloid

These alkaloids may exhibit anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antimicrobial effects, making them relevant in herbal medicine for treating various ailments. Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Common Bean (Phaseolus vulgaris). It contains various bioactive components like phenolic acids and flavonoids. These compounds contribute to the plant's antimicrobial and antioxidant properties. Essential oil from Common Bean is used in herbal medicine to support digestive and immune health.
