What to know about Prunus mume (chinese plum) before using it medicinally

Prunus mume, commonly known as Chinese plum, is a herb that has been widely used for its numerous health benefits, which range from reducing inflammation and improving cardiovascular health to alleviating digestive issues.

From a horticultural perspective, Prunus mume is a deciduous shrub or small tree that is relatively easy to cultivate and maintain, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. Botanically speaking, Prunus mume is a member of the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other stone fruits, such as plums and cherries.

Historically, Prunus mume has been prized for its medicinal and culinary value for thousands of years, with ancient Chinese physicians and chefs highly regarded for their use of the herb.

This article explains the medicinal, horticultural, botanical, and historical aspects of Prunus mume.

What are the medicinal properties of Prunus mume?

Prunus mume, also known as Chinese plum, helps with relieving cough, fever, and sore throat, as well as treating bronchitis, pneumonia, and other respiratory infections. Its medicinal properties also extend to aiding in digestion, reducing inflammation, and regulating blood sugar levels.

The active constituents of Prunus mume include flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids, which give it its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antiviral properties. These compounds work together to provide relief from various health issues.

The most commonly used parts of the Prunus mume plant for medicinal purposes are the flowers, bark, and leaves. The flowers are often used to treat cough and fever, while the bark is used to treat digestive issues. The leaves are used to treat respiratory infections.

When used improperly, Prunus mume can cause side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea. Overdosing on the plant can also lead to kidney damage and increased heart rate. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using the plant.

To use Prunus mume medicinally, individuals should consult with a healthcare professional to determine the correct dosage and preparation method. Individuals with allergies to the plant or its constituents should avoid using it altogether.

What are the horticulural aspects of Prunus mume?

Prunus mume, also known as Chinese plum, grow best in well-drained acidic soil with a pH range of 5.5-6.5. It prefers full sun to partial shade and can tolerate temperatures from -10°C to 30°C. Mature plants require 1-2 meters of spacing.

For planting, choose a location with good air circulation and avoid low-lying areas prone to waterlogging. Plant bare-root or potted trees in early spring or fall, when the weather is cooler. Dig a hole 2-3 times the width of the root ball and mix in 1-2 kg of compost.

For harvesting, fruit is usually ready 2-3 months after flowering, depending on the cultivar. Select fruit with a red or pink skin and a sweet aroma. Harvest in the morning, when the fruit is firm but not hard. Avoid bruising or crushing the fruit.

Common pests affecting Prunus mume include Japanese beetle, aphids, and codling moth. Diseases include powdery mildew, brown rot, and leaf spot. Regular monitoring and maintenance can help prevent infestations and infections.

What are the botanical characteristics of Prunus mume?

Prunus mume, also known as Chinese plum or Japanese apricot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

Botanically speaking, P. mume is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5-10 meters in height, with a spreading canopy and thorny branches. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, measuring around 4-8 cm in length and 1.5-3 cm in width, with a pointed tip and serrated margins.

The flowers are white, fragrant, and arranged in clusters of 2-5, appearing in late winter to early spring. They have five petals, measuring around 10-15 mm in diameter, and contain numerous stamens. The fruit is a drupe, typically yellow or red when mature, with a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

P. mume is often grown for its ornamental value, as well as for its edible fruit, which is used to make traditional Chinese preserves and condiments.

What is the taxonomy of Prunus mume?

Prunus mume belongs to the kingdom Plantae, class Magnoliopsida, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, and genus Prunus.

Specifically, it is classified as Prunus mume (L.) Ten., a species within the stone fruit subfamily Amygdaloideae.

This classification reflects its close relationships with other stone fruits like plums, cherries, and peaches, as well as its shared characteristics such as having a pit or stone in the center of the fruit.

What is the geographic distribution of Prunus mume?

Prunus mume, also known as Chinese plum or Japanese apricot, is native to China and Japan.

What are the historical uses of Prunus mume?

Prunus mume, also known as Chinese plum or Ume, has a rich history dating back over 2,000 years.

Its fruits have been used in traditional medicine to treat digestive issues and reduce inflammation. In Japan, the fruit is pickled in salt to make umeboshi, a sour and salty condiment often served with rice and miso soup.

In China, the wood of Prunus mume has been used for making furniture, while its seeds have been used as a natural dye.
