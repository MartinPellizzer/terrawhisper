Corydalis yanhusuo

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis yanhusuo?

Corydalis yanhusuo belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Papaveraceae. Within this family, it is classified under the genus Corydalis, with the specific identifier being the species yanhusuo.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex profile of isoquinoline alkaloids including berberine, palmatine, protopine, glaucine, and corydaline. These specific medicinal compounds are primarily responsible for the plant's pharmacological actions in traditional medicine. Berberine and palmatine are frequently studied for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Protopine and glaucine contribute to the plant's ability to act on the central nervous system and influence smooth muscle relaxation. Finally, corydaline provides additional bioactive effects that support the overall therapeutic utility of the species.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Corydalis yanhusuo targets various neurotransmitter receptors and enzymes, specifically acting as a potent modulator of GABAergic signaling and opioid receptor pathways.
