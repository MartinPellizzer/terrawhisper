Corydalis yanhusuo

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis yanhusuo?

Corydalis yanhusuo belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Papaveraceae. Within this family, it is classified under the genus Corydalis, with the specific identifier being the species yanhusuo.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis yanhusuo has a fleshy, tuberous rhizome that serves as its primary storage organ, supporting a herbaceous plant structure with deeply pinnate or bipinnate leaves. The foliage features multiple leaflets that are often divided into smaller segments, giving the plant a delicate and lacy appearance. During its flowering stage, it produces terminal racemes of zygomorphic flowers that are typically yellow or pale in color. These flowers possess a distinctive nectar-producing spur that characterizes the genus. The plant's overall morphology is adapted for seasonal growth cycles within moist, shaded forest environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Corydalis yanhusuo?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, antioxidant, and gastroprotective pharmacological activities driven by its rich concentration of isoquinoline alkaloids. Its analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties make it effective in managing various types of pain and reducing systemic inflammation. The plant also demonstrates significant anti-tumor potential by inhibiting the growth and proliferation of certain cancer cell lines. Additionally, its antioxidant capacity helps neutralize free radicals and mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Finally, the gastroprotective effects of Corydalis yanhusuo provide a mechanism for protecting the stomach lining against various forms of mucosal injury.

As discussed in a study, Corydalis yanhusuo has therapeutic effects on cardiovascular diseases and an analgesic effect where l-tetrahydropalmatine is the main active principle. In a rat heart failure model, rats were orally administered with ethanolic extract of Corydalis yanhusuo at doses of 50, 100, or 200 mg kg(-1) daily starting from the 7th day after surgery. Following 8 weeks of treatment, administration led to a significant reduction in infarct size and improvement in cardiac function through lower left ventricular end diastolic pressure and elevated +/-dp/dt(max). The plant also significantly reduced the left ventricular/body weight ratio and the lung/body weight ratio. These findings indicate that Corydalis yanhusuo exerted salutary effects on heart failure induced by myocardial infarction in rats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal alkaloids berberine, glaucine, palmatine, coptisine, and columbamine. These specific chemical compounds are often studied for their diverse biological activities in pharmaceutical research. Berberine and palmatine are particularly noted for their potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Glaucine is frequently investigated for its effects on the central nervous system and respiratory functions. The presence of coptisine and columbamine further enhances the complex chemical profile of the species. Together, these substances contribute to the plant's traditional use in various medicinal applications.

As discussed in a study, Corydalis yanhusuo has been used for the treatment of digestive system diseases in China. The study examined extracts obtained through solid-liquid extraction with water (WECY), ethanol (EECY), and chloroform (CECY). Among the major alkaloids in CECY, berberine and dehydrocorydaline exhibited obvious anti-H. pylori activity. Specifically, berberine showed a minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) of 25 μg/mL, while dehydrocorydaline demonstrated an MIC of 12.5 μg/mL. Dehydrocorydaline is an active alkaloid compound isolated from Corydalis yanhusuo that warrants further investigation for its potential activity.
