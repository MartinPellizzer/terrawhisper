Corydalis Yanhusuo

Corydalis yanhusuo, also known as Chinese Corydalis, is a traditional Chinese medicinal herb widely used for its analgesic and antispasmodic properties.

It contains active compounds such as tetrahydroisoquinoline alkaloids, which contribute to its effectiveness in treating pain and inflammation. In traditional medicine, it is often prescribed for conditions like menstrual pain, neuralgia, and musculoskeletal disorders. The plant's rhizomes and roots are typically processed through methods like steaming or drying to enhance its medicinal potency.

Due to its therapeutic benefits, Corydalis yanhusuo remains a valuable component in both traditional and modern pharmacological applications.

Corydalis yanhusuo has several bioactive constituents, such as tetrahydroisoquinoline alkaloids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

These alkaloids, including tetrahydrocolumbamine and tetrahydrocorybuline, exhibit analgesic, antispasmodic, and anti-inflammatory effects. They work by interacting with various receptors in the nervous system, including opioid and serotonin receptors, thereby modulating pain perception and muscle contractions. The plant is traditionally used in Chinese medicine to treat conditions such as menstrual cramps, headaches, and cardiovascular disorders.
