Corydalis yanhusuo

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis yanhusuo?

Corydalis yanhusuo belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical classification, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Papaveraceae. Within this family, it is classified under the genus Corydalis, with the specific identifier being the species yanhusuo.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis yanhusuo has a fleshy, tuberous rhizome that serves as its primary storage organ, supporting a herbaceous plant structure with deeply pinnate or bipinnate leaves. The foliage features multiple leaflets that are often divided into smaller segments, giving the plant a delicate and lacy appearance. During its flowering stage, it produces terminal racemes of zygomorphic flowers that are typically yellow or pale in color. These flowers possess a distinctive nectar-producing spur that characterizes the genus. The plant's overall morphology is adapted for seasonal growth cycles within moist, shaded forest environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Corydalis yanhusuo?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, anti-tumor, antioxidant, and gastroprotective pharmacological activities. Its bioactive alkaloids work to reduce pain and swelling by modulating various inflammatory pathways in the body. Researchers have also observed its ability to combat oxidative stress through potent antioxidant mechanisms. Additionally, the plant demonstrates potential in inhibiting tumor cell growth and protecting the gastric mucosa from damage. These diverse properties make it a significant subject for pharmacological research and therapeutic development.

According to a study published by "The Journal of pharmacy and pharmacology", Corydalis yanhusuo has therapeutic effects on cardiovascular diseases and an analgesic effect with l-tetrahydropalmatine as the main active principle. In a rat model, the ethanolic extract was orally administered at doses of 50, 100, or 200 mg kg(-1) daily starting from the 7th day after surgery. Following 8 weeks of treatment, the plant led to a significant reduction in infarct size and improved cardiac function by lowering left ventricular end diastolic pressure (LVEDP) and elevating +/-dp/dt(max). Additionally, Corydalis yanhusuo significantly reduced the left ventricular (LV)/body weight ratio and the lung/body weight ratio while inhibiting neurohormonal activation. These results indicate that the plant exerts salutary effects on heart failure induced by myocardial infarction in rats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive isoquinoline alkaloids, specifically berberine, palmatine, corydaline, glaucine, and protopine. These medicinal compounds are primarily concentrated within the rhizomes of the species. Each constituent plays a distinct role in the plant's pharmacological profile, contributing to its traditional uses in various medicinal practices. Research suggests that the synergistic interaction of these alkaloids may provide significant therapeutic potential. Scientists continue to study these specific molecules to better understand their individual effects on human health.

According to a study published by "Journal of ethnopharmacology", Corydalis yanhusuo contains major alkaloids such as berberine and dehydrocorydaline. Among the extracts tested, the chloroform extract known as CECY showed the maximum in vitro antibacterial activity. Dehydrocorydaline is an active alkaloid compound isolated from Corydalus yanhusuo that warrants further investigation for its potential anti-H. pylori activity. Berberine and dehydrocorydaline exhibited obvious anti-H. pylori activity with a MIC of 25 and 12.5 μg/mL, respectively. The study evaluated three extracts including WECY, EECY, and CECY to identify these bioactive constituents.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

It contains various alkaloids that interact with the central nervous system to alleviate physical discomfort. These bioactive compounds help reduce the sensation of pain across different physiological mechanisms. Research suggests its efficacy in managing both sudden injuries and long-term suffering. By targeting specific pathways, it serves as a natural approach to addressing diverse painful conditions.

According to a study published by "Pharmaceuticaals (Basel, Switzerland)", Corydalis yanhusuo has been used as an analgesic in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries. The extract has been shown to promote analgesia in animal models of acute, inflammatory, and neuropathic pain. It may be advantageous to manage addiction by inhibiting the dopamine D2 receptor. When coadministered with morphine, YHS inhibits morphine tolerance, dependence, and addiction. Furthermore, in animals treated for several days with morphine, YHS can reverse morphine dependence and addiction.
