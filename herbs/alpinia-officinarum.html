Alpinia Ginger (Alpinia officinarum)

Alpinia Ginger (Alpinia officinarum) is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, Indonesia, and Philippines. Traditionally, its rhizomes, leaves, and flowers have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in traditional chinese medicine, japanese kampo medicine, and ayurvedic medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Broad leaves with upper surface dull green and lower surface pale white, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences composed of dense spike-like clusters of small white to pale yellow flowers with three sepals and three petals, actinomorphic, with a prominent three-lobed lip and a spur at the base of the ovary.
Stems
Culinary ginger with upright growth habit, alternate branching, and smooth, glabrous surface.
