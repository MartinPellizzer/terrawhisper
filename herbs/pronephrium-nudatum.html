Pronephrium nudatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Pronephrium nudatum?

Pronephrium nudatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. Within this lineage, it is placed in the family Aspleniaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Pronephrium, and its unique species designation, nudatum.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Pronephrium nudatum can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap often leads to redness, itching, or even chemical burns on sensitive areas of the body. Some individuals may experience swelling or localized hives as a direct response to the botanical compounds. If swallowed, the plant may trigger nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. Careful avoidance of skin contact and ingestion is necessary to prevent these adverse reactions.
