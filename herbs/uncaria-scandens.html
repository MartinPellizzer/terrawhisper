Uncaria scandens

What's the taxonomical classification of Uncaria scandens?

Uncaria scandens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is part of the order Gentianaales and falls under the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Uncaria, and its specific species name, scandens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Uncaria scandens has woody, climbing stems equipped with recurved, hook-like spines that facilitate its movement up supporting vegetation. The leaves are typically simple, opposite, and elliptical or oblong in shape with entire margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying shades of white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that serve as the primary method for seed dispersal. These structural adaptations allow the species to thrive within competitive tropical forest environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse range of bioactive medicinal compounds including cadambine-type alkaloids, corynanthein-type alkaloids, and corynoxine-type alkaloids. These specific chemical constituents are primarily classified as oxindole alkaloids, which are responsible for much of the species' pharmacological activity. Researchers have studied these substances extensively for their potential anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties. The complex interaction between these different alkaloid groups contributes to the plant's traditional use in various herbal remedies. Understanding the precise structure of these compounds allows scientists to better evaluate their therapeutic efficacy in modern medicine.

According to a study published by "Natural products and bioprospecting", a total of 57 compounds were characterized across seven Uncaria species, which included 35 indole alkaloids, ten flavonoids, five triterpenoids, five chlorogenic analogues, and two other compounds. Within this profile, cadambine-type and corynanthein-type alkaloids were exclusively present in U.rhynchophylla and U.scandens. Additionally, corynoxine-type alkaloids were commonly detected in all seven Uncaria plants. These findings were obtained through UFLC-IT/TOF-MS analysis integrated with agonistic assays on four neurotransmitter receptors, specifically MT1, MT2, 5-HT1A, and 5-HT2C.
