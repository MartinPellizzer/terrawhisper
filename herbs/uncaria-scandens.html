Uncaria scandens

What's the taxonomical classification of Uncaria scandens?

Uncaria scandens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is part of the order Gentianaales and falls under the family Rubiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Uncaria, and its specific species name, scandens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Uncaria scandens has woody, climbing stems equipped with recurved, hook-like spines that facilitate its movement up supporting vegetation. The leaves are typically simple, opposite, and elliptical or oblong in shape with entire margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, often displaying shades of white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that serve as the primary method for seed dispersal. These structural adaptations allow the species to thrive within competitive tropical forest environments.
