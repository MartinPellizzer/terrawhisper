What to know about Dioscorea villosa (wild yam) before using it medicinally

Wild yam, scientifically known as Dioscorea villosa, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to improve cardiovascular and digestive health, reduce inflammation, and even alleviate menstrual cramps.

Beyond its medicinal uses, wild yam is also used in various culinary preparations, particularly in Asian and Latin American cuisines, where it adds a subtle flavor and texture to soups, stews, and stir-fries. In horticulture, wild yam is prized for its striking, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, tuberous roots, which can be cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes. Botanically speaking, wild yam belongs to the Dioscoreaceae family and features a unique, hairy stem and leaves with distinctive ridges and veins.

For centuries, wild yam has been documented in traditional medicine practices around the world, including ancient Chinese and Native American remedies, where it was often used to treat various ailments and imbalances.
