1. Causes dizziness

Morinda officinalis causes dizziness in some individuals due to its potential interactions with medications and its ability to stimulate the nervous system.

The compound scopoletin, found in noni, has been shown to affect the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to feelings of lightheadedness or dizziness. Additionally, the herb's flavonoids may also contribute to this side effect by altering blood vessel tone and blood pressure regulation.

As a result, people who consume Morinda officinalis may experience episodes of dizziness due to these physiological changes.

2. Induces vomiting

Morinda officinalis induces vomiting due to its strong stimulating properties on the digestive system.

The fruit and leaves of this plant contain various alkaloids, glycosides, and other compounds that can cause irritation and stimulation of the stomach lining, leading to nausea and vomiting in some individuals.

Additionally, noni's ability to stimulate digestion and bowel movements can also contribute to this side effect, especially when taken in large doses or without proper preparation.

3. Produces diarrhea

Morinda officinalis produces diarrhea due to its high concentration of flavonoids and phenolic acids.

These compounds can stimulate the gut lining, leading to increased motility and water absorption in the intestines. As a result, individuals may experience frequent bowel movements, often accompanied by urgency, abdominal cramps, and discomfort.

The potency of noni's bioactive compounds is believed to be responsible for this gastrointestinal side effect, although further research is needed to fully understand its mechanisms.

4. Triggers allergic reactions

Morinda officinalis triggers allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high concentration of flavonoids and iridoid glycosides.

These compounds can cause an overactive immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, the fruit's mucilage content may also contribute to allergic reactions, as it can release histamine and other chemicals that exacerbate allergic responses.

5. Increases blood pressure

Morinda officinalis increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of alkaloids and saponins.

These bioactive compounds can stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to an increase in blood pressure. Additionally, noni's flavonoids may enhance vasoconstriction, further contributing to the rise in blood pressure.

As a result, individuals taking Morinda officinalis may experience elevated blood pressure levels, potentially affecting their overall health and wellbeing.

6. Decreases appetite

Morinda officinalis decreases appetite by suppressing the production of ghrelin, a hormone that stimulates hunger.

As a result, individuals may experience reduced food cravings and intake. Additionally, noni's saponins, which are responsible for its medicinal properties, have been shown to affect the gut-brain axis, leading to changes in appetite regulation.

This side effect is often reported by those taking Morinda officinalis supplements or consuming noni juice.

7. Affects liver function

Morinda officinalis affects liver function due to its high concentration of saponins and anthraquinones, which can cause inflammation and damage to liver cells.

The polyphenolic compounds present in the fruit may also interfere with the body's natural detoxification processes, leading to an increase in bilirubin levels and potentially causing jaundice or other liver-related symptoms.

8. Interacts with medications

Morinda officinalis interacts with medications by altering their metabolism in the body.

Its active compounds, including alkaloids and terpenoids, can increase the breakdown of certain drugs, such as anticoagulants, antiplatelet agents, and blood pressure medications, which may lead to reduced efficacy or increased risk of bleeding.

Additionally, noni may also decrease the absorption of medications taken orally, further compromising their effectiveness.

9. Provokes insomnia

Morinda officinalis provokes insomnia due to its potential interaction with certain neurotransmitters in the brain.

The plant's active compounds, such as scopoletin and iridoids, can alter the body's natural sleep-wake cycle by increasing alertness and stimulating the nervous system.

This can lead to difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep, resulting in fatigue, irritability, and other related symptoms.

10. Elicits respiratory issues

Morinda officinalis elicits respiratory issues due to its high concentration of alkaloids and saponins.

These compounds can stimulate the lungs, leading to symptoms such as coughing, shortness of breath, and wheezing in some individuals.

Additionally, the herb's flavonoids and antioxidants may exacerbate existing respiratory conditions like asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).
