Botanical Identification of Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis)

To identify Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

The botanical identification of Chinese Date, Morinda officinalis, places it in the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rubiales, family Rubiaceae, genus Morinda, and species Morinda officinalis. This classification reflects its position within the plant kingdom and its taxonomic relationships.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Chinese Date is Morinda officinalis, a species within the Rubiaceae family. It was first described by the botanist Carl Linnaeus in 1753. This name is widely accepted in botanical literature and is used to distinguish it from other similar species.

Morphological Characteristics

Chinese Date, or Morinda officinalis, is a shrub or small tree with a taproot system and multiple branches. Its stems are woody and cylindrical, often branching from the base. Leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with a dark green upper surface. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, clustered flowers that are pinkish-white with five petals. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Morinda officinalis can be distinguished by its smooth, reddish-brown fruit with a single seed, and its alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. It differs from similar species like Morinda citrifolia by its smaller, more rounded fruit and the absence of a strong, distinctive odor. The plant also has a unique combination of leaf shape and fruit structure that aids in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis) is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, India, and Indonesia. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained, loamy soils. It is typically found at altitudes between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Chinese Date flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, around May to June. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, from August to September. Harvesting is usually done in autumn when the fruits are fully mature, typically from September to October, depending on the region and climate.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Morinda officinalis belongs to the Rubiaceae family, sharing close relationships with species like Morinda citrifolia. It is closely related within the genus Morinda, characterized by similar morphological and genetic traits. These relationships help in understanding its evolutionary placement and medicinal properties.
