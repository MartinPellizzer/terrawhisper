Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis) Compounds

The primary compounds of Chinese Date include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties. Tannins contribute to its astringent taste and potential health benefits. Phenolic acids and glycosides are also present, offering anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Coumarin and saponins add to its medicinal profile, while terpenoids, alkaloids, essential oils, and anthocyanins provide additional therapeutic properties.

Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis). They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic benefits in traditional herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is believed to support digestive health and reduce inflammation. Tannins in Chinese Date are also associated with antioxidant and antimicrobial effects in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound supports the traditional uses of Chinese Date in promoting health and vitality.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis). It contributes to the plant's therapeutic effects, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Glycosides in Chinese Date are often used in traditional formulations to support immune function and treat conditions like rheumatism and kidney disorders.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Morinda officinalis. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and pain. Coumarin's pharmacological properties support its role in herbal medicine, enhancing the therapeutic value of Chinese Date.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting kidney health and enhancing vitality. Saponins also exhibit anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory effects. These compounds are widely studied for their potential in traditional and modern medicine.

8. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Morinda officinalis, a traditional Chinese medicinal plant. It contributes to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects. Alkaloids in Morinda officinalis are valued for their role in treating conditions like fatigue and nerve disorders.

9. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Morinda officinalis. It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its role in supporting overall health in herbal formulations.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Chinese Date (Morinda officinalis). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective effects.
