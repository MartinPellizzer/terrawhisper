Desert gumweed

Grindelia squarrosa

Desert Gumweed (Grindelia squarrosa) is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and cough.

Botanical Identification

Grindelia squarrosa, commonly known as Desert Gumweed, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features gray-green leaves and yellow flowers, with a deep taproot system. It is also known as Squawweed or Gumweed in some regional contexts.

Safety Profile

Desert Gumweed is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. There are no well-documented contraindications, but caution is advised for pregnant or breastfeeding women due to limited research on its effects during these periods.
