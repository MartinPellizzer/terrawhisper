What's the taxonomical classification of Phyllodium elegans?

Phyllodium elegans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage leading to the order Fabales. It is situated within the family Fabaceae, a diverse group of flowering plants, and is specifically identified under the genus Phyllodium. Within this genus, it is distinguished as the species elegans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllodium elegans has large, leaf-like phyllodia that are broadly ovate to elliptical, measuring up to 20 centimeters in length, which serve as the primary photosynthetic organs instead of true leaves. The actual leaves are much smaller, inconspicuous, and often fall off early in the plant's development. Its flowering stems bear small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are arranged in axillary racemes.
