Phyllodium elegans

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phyllodium elegans has large, leaf-like phyllodia that are broadly ovate to elliptical, measuring up to 20 centimeters in length, which serve as the primary photosynthetic organs instead of true leaves. These modified structures are typically bright green and possess a leathery texture with prominent pinnate venation. The actual leaves are much smaller, inconspicuous, and often fall off early in the plant's development. Its flowering stems bear small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are arranged in axillary racemes. The plant also features woody stems and a sprawling growth habit characteristic of its legume family.
