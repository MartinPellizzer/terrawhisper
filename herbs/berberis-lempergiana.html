Berberis lempergiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. This specific plant is a representative of the family Berberidaceae and is further categorized under the genus Berberis. Ultimately, its unique botanical identity is defined by its specific classification as the species lempergiana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Berberis lempergiana contains high concentrations of isoquinoline alkaloids, most notably berberine, palmatine, and jatrorrhizine. Berberine acts as a potent antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory agent through its ability to inhibit various bacterial enzymes. Additionally, these alkaloids exhibit significant antioxidant properties that help mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems.
