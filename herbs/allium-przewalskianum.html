What's the taxonomical classification of Allium przewalskianum?

Allium przewalskianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Asparagales. As a member of the family Amaryllidaceae, this plant is further categorized into the genus Allium, eventually reaching its specific identification as the species przewalskianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium przewalskianum has bulbous underground structures, narrow linear leaves that are typically dark green, and umbels of star-shaped flowers ranging from pale pink to deep rose. The scape is a leafless, upright stem that supports the flowering head. Each individual flower possesses six tepals and prominent stamens that extend outward from the center. The plant often grows in clumps, with the foliage forming a dense base from which the flowering stalks emerge. These morphological traits are characteristic of many perennial species within the Amaryllidaceae family.
