Hyptis suaveolens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hyptis suaveolens has an erect or ascending herbaceous habit characterized by opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a distinctively aromatic scent. The stems are typically slender and can exhibit varying degrees of pubescence depending on the specific environment. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellow capitula arranged in dense, terminal heads. Each individual flower within the head features tubular florets that are typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant also produces small, dry achenes that facilitate its reproductive cycle.

What are the herbal preparations of Hyptis suaveolens?

Hyptis suaveolenx is prepared primarily as an infusion, decoction, or topical poultice using the fresh or dried leaves of the plant. What side-effects this plant can have?

Hyptis suaveolens can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested or applied incorrectly. While the plant is often used in traditional medicine, its chemical compounds may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Contact with the leaves or stems can lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. If consumed, it might cause stomach upset or other internal discomfort depending on the dosage and preparation. Scientific research regarding its long-term toxicity in humans remains limited, necessitating cautious use.
