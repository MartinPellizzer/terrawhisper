Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile) Uses

Chamomile is commonly used to treat respiratory infections, inflammation, and allergic rhinitis. It may help alleviate symptoms of asthma, the common cold, and nasal congestion. Its anti-inflammatory properties also address skin infections, irritation, and acne by reducing redness and promoting healing.

Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile) is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and soothing irritation in the airways. Its antiseptic and expectorant properties help alleviate symptoms of coughs, sore throats, and mild respiratory infections. It is often prepared as a tea to ease congestion and promote easier breathing.

2. Inflammation

Chamomile contains anti-inflammatory compounds like flavonoids and bisabolol that reduce swelling and redness. It is commonly used for conditions such as skin irritations, arthritis, and digestive inflammation. Its mild nature makes it suitable for topical and internal applications. Studies support its effectiveness in managing mild inflammatory responses.

3. Allergic rhinitis

Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile) is used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion, sneezing, and itching by soothing the respiratory tract. Studies suggest it may offer a natural alternative for managing seasonal allergies with minimal side effects.

4. Asthma

Chamomile may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing airway inflammation and bronchial spasms. Its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties can ease breathing difficulties. However, it should be used as a complementary therapy under professional guidance, as it is not a substitute for conventional asthma treatments.

5. Common cold

Chamomile may help alleviate symptoms of the common cold due to its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties. It can soothe sore throats and reduce congestion when used as a tea. However, it is not a cure for the cold and should be used alongside other supportive care measures.

6. Nasal congestion

Chamomile can help alleviate nasal congestion by reducing inflammation and soothing irritated mucous membranes. Its antihistamine and anti-inflammatory properties may ease symptoms of allergic rhinitis and common colds. A steam inhalation with chamomile tea is a traditional method to clear nasal passages. It is often used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies for respiratory comfort.

7. Respiratory inflammation

Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile) is used to reduce respiratory inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like bronchitis and asthma by soothing airway irritation. Its essential oils contain compounds that inhibit inflammatory responses in the respiratory tract. Regular use may support clearer breathing and reduced coughing.

8. Skin infections

Chamomile has antimicrobial properties that make it effective for treating minor skin infections. Its active compounds, such as bisabolol and chamazulene, help reduce inflammation and fight bacteria. Chamomile can be applied topically as a compress or infused oil to soothe infected skin and promote healing. It is commonly used for conditions like eczema, fungal infections, and minor burns.

9. Skin irritation

Chamomile is effective for skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It soothes redness, itching, and minor burns when applied topically. Chamomile-infused creams or compresses can reduce swelling and promote healing. It is commonly used for eczema, psoriasis, and insect bites.

10. Acne

Chamomile contains compounds with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties that may help reduce acne symptoms. It can soothe skin irritation and prevent bacterial growth, making it a useful topical remedy for mild to moderate acne. However, it should be used cautiously to avoid potential allergic reactions.
