Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile) Compounds

Chamomile contains essential oils, flavonoids, and coumarin as key compounds. Essential oils provide its calming scent and effects, while flavonoids have antioxidant properties. Coumarin contributes to its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory actions.

Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile) is a key active compound, containing volatile compounds like chamazulene and bisabolol. It possesses anti-inflammatory, antispasmodic, and anxiolytic properties, making it widely used in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related ailments.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine for calming and soothing effects.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also exhibits mild sedative effects, supporting Chamomile's traditional use for calming and digestive support.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce irritation and supports skin health. It is commonly found in chamomile extracts used in herbal remedies.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and calming effects, making them essential for its traditional use in herbal medicine. Specific terpenoids like bisabolol and chamazulene are responsible for many of its therapeutic properties.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile). It contributes to the herb's anti-inflammatory and astringent properties, helping to soothe digestive issues and reduce irritation. Tannins also support the plant's traditional use in calming upset stomachs and promoting overall digestive health.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile). It contributes to the plant's calming and anti-inflammatory effects. Glycosides help in reducing stress and promoting relaxation, making Chamomile effective for digestive and skin conditions.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and mild sedative properties. Saponins may aid in reducing irritation and supporting digestive health. They are part of the broader active constituents that make chamomile effective in traditional herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile) contains alkaloids, though they are present in smaller amounts compared to other compounds. These alkaloids contribute to the plant's mild sedative and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting its traditional use in calming the nervous system and reducing irritation.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile). It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound may support anti-inflammatory and calming effects, aligning with Chamomile's traditional use in herbal medicine.
