Cornus mas

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Cornus mas, commonly known as Cornelian cherry or European dogwood, is a species in the family Cornaceae. It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia. The plant has yellow flowers, oval leaves, and red berries, and is also referred to as Cornus mas L. in botanical literature.

Active Compounds

Cornus mas contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Musculoskeletal Arthritis, gout, osteoporosis Anti-inflammatory, antioxidant
Digestive Diarrhea, dyspepsia, gastritis Antimicrobial, spasmolytic, anti-inflammatory
Integumentary Skin infections, wounds, skin inflammation, eczema Antimicrobial, antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory and digestive issues due to its anti-inflammatory properties.

Decoction: Used for digestive ailments and as a tonic due to its high concentration of bioactive compounds.

Capsule: Used for general health support and immune system enhancement.

Safety Profile

Cornus mas is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Long-term use may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with chronic conditions.
