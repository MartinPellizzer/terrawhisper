Cornus mas

Cornus Mas (Cornus mas) is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia, known for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional medicine. It is primarily used to improve conditions such as urinary tract infections, kidney disorders, and digestive issues due to its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Cornus mas, commonly known as Cornelian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems, oval to elliptic leaves with a serrated margin, and clusters of small yellowish-green flowers that appear before the leaves in early spring.

Distribution

Cornus mas is native to southern and central Europe, including regions such as France, Italy, Spain, and parts of Eastern Europe, and is also found in northwest Africa and the Middle East.

Traditional Uses

Cornus mas has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to support urinary tract health, alleviate symptoms of diabetes, and promote digestive function through its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Medicinal Systems

Cornus mas has been utilized in traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Native American herbal practices for its purported healing properties.

Pharmacology

Cornus mas exhibits pharmacological properties including antipyretic, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory effects, which contribute to its traditional use in treating fever, inflammation, and immune-related conditions.

Medicinal Uses
