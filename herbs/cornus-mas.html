Cornus Mas (Cornus mas): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Cornus Mas (Cornus mas) is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia, known for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional medicine. It is primarily used to improve conditions such as urinary tract infections, kidney disorders, and digestive issues due to its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.

What is Cornus Mas (Cornus Mas)?

Cornus mas, also known as cornelian cherry or dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its yellow flowers, red berries, and medicinal properties used in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Identity and Classification

Cornus mas, commonly known as the dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the family Cornaceae, characterized by its showy white or pink flowers and distinctive red berries.

Common Names and Synonyms

Common Names and Synonyms include Cornus mas, also known as European dogwood, cornel, cornel cherry, and sweet dogwood.

Cornus mas, commonly known as Cornelian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems, oval to elliptic leaves with a serrated margin, and clusters of small yellowish-green flowers that appear before the leaves in early spring.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Cornus mas, commonly known as the dogwood, is native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia, including parts of the Balkans, the Middle East, and the Caucasus, and has been historically cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices across these areas.

Plant Parts Used Medicinally

The roots, bark, leaves, and fruits of Cornus mas are used medicinally, with the roots and bark being the most commonly utilized parts for their therapeutic properties.

Phytochemical Composition

Cornus mas contains a variety of bioactive compounds including flavonoids, triterpenoids, tannins, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic potential.

Pharmacological Properties

Cornus mas exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antimicrobial, and immunomodulatory effects, supported by both traditional use and modern scientific research.

Mechanisms of Action

The mechanisms of action of Cornus mas involve its bioactive compounds, such as flavonoids, tannins, and iridoids, which exhibit antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory effects by scavenging free radicals, inhibiting pro-inflammatory cytokines, and modulating cellular signaling pathways.

Therapeutic Uses and Indications

Cornus mas is used to treat conditions such as digestive disorders, urinary tract infections, liver diseases, arthritis, skin conditions, and respiratory ailments.

Preparation Methods and Forms

Cornus Mas is typically prepared as a decoction, tincture, or powder, with the dried fruits or bark being the primary parts used, often harvested in late summer to early autumn for optimal potency.

Dosage and Administration

The typical dosage of Cornus mas for medicinal use ranges from 200 to 600 mg of dried fruit per day, usually divided into two to three doses, though specific formulations may vary based on the intended therapeutic effect.

Safety, Side Effects, and Contraindications

While Cornus mas is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses, some individuals may experience mild gastrointestinal discomfort, allergic reactions, or interactions with certain medications, particularly those affecting the liver or blood clotting.

Drug Interactions

Toxicity and Precautions

While Cornus mas is generally considered safe when used in moderate amounts, it may cause mild gastrointestinal upset in some individuals, and there is limited evidence of toxicity when consumed in typical medicinal doses.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Cornus mas is typically cultivated in temperate regions with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring regular watering during its establishment phase and minimal pruning to maintain its natural form.

Processing and Extraction

The processing of Cornus Mas involves harvesting the mature berries when they turn from green to red, typically in late summer to early autumn, followed by drying the berries in the sun or using low-heat methods to preserve their active compounds.

Storage and Shelf Life

Cornus Mas (Cornus mas) should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, and its shelf life is typically 1-2 years when properly preserved.

Scientific Research and Evidence

Scientific research on Cornus mas has shown potential benefits in supporting immune function, reducing inflammation, and exhibiting antioxidant properties, with some studies suggesting its use in traditional medicine for conditions such as arthritis and respiratory infections.

Cornus mas has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported ability to support kidney function, reduce inflammation, and alleviate symptoms of urinary tract disorders.
