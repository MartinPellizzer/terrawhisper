Cornus Mas (Cornus mas)

Cornus Mas (Cornus mas) is a member of the Cornaceae family, native to Southern Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, astringent, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and korean traditional medicine.

Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, solitary, with five white petals and five yellow stamens, and a cupular calyx.

Erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth surface, and presence of thorn-like structures.
