What precautions to take before using Cornus mas medicinally?

The precautions to take before using Cornus mas medicinally are crucial to ensure safe and effective administration.

First and foremost, Cornus mas should only be used under the guidance of a healthcare professional, as its active compounds can interact with certain medications, including blood thinners, anticoagulants, and diabetes medications. Additionally, individuals with bleeding disorders, recent surgeries or injuries, and those taking warfarin or other anticoagulant medications should avoid using Cornus mas, as it may increase the risk of bleeding complications.

Furthermore, pregnant and breastfeeding women should exercise caution when using Cornus mas, as its effects on fetal development and infant health are not well understood. It is also essential to monitor blood pressure and kidney function closely during treatment, as Cornus mas can cause hypotension (low blood pressure) and exacerbate kidney issues in susceptible individuals.

Finally, the recommended dosage should be followed precisely, as excessive consumption of Cornus mas may lead to adverse reactions, including dizziness, headaches, and gastrointestinal upset.
