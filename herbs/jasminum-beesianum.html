Jasminum beesianum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum beesianum has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that typically measure between 3 and 7 centimeters in length and feature entire margins with acute tips. The foliage is characterized by a smooth, glabrous texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. Its woody stems are often arching or climbing, allowing the shrub to form dense thickets. The plant produces fragrant, white, tubular flowers that emerge in small clusters from the leaf axils. These blossoms possess five spreading lobes that create a star-like appearance when fully open.

      What are the herbal preparations of Jasminum beesianum?

      Jasminum beesianum is prepared as an infusion, a decoction, or an aromatic oil for topical and internal use. The fragrant flowers are often steeped in hot water to create a soothing tea that helps relieve respiratory discomfort. In some traditional practices, the plant material is boiled for a longer duration to extract stronger medicinal compounds for decoctions.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Jasminum beesianum can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or localized swelling if the plant's oils come into direct contact with sensitive areas of the body. While the plant is primarily known for its fragrant flowers, some individuals may experience respiratory discomfort if they inhale concentrated amounts of its scent in enclosed spaces. Topical application of any part of the plant may lead to redness or itching in people with pre-existing sensitivities to the Jasminum genus. There is limited toxicological data regarding ingestion, but it should be avoided to prevent potential gastrointestinal distress. Always exercise caution when handling the foliage to minimize the risk of an adverse reaction.
