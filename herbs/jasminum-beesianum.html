Jasminum beesianum

What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum beesianum?

Jasminum beesianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Lamiales. It is a member of the Oleaceae family, falling specifically into the genus Jasminum, with the unique species designation beesianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum beesianum has opposite, elliptical to oblong leaves that typically measure between 3 and 7 centimeters in length and feature entire margins with acute tips. The foliage is characterized by a smooth, glabrous texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. Its woody stems are often arching or climbing, allowing the shrub to form dense thickets. The plant produces fragrant, white, tubular flowers that emerge in small clusters from the leaf axils. These blossoms possess five spreading lobes that create a star-like appearance when fully open.
