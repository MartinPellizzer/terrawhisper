10 Possible Side Effects Of Betula Pendula

Betula pendula has some side effects when used improperly, such as allergic reactions, digestive issues, and skin irritation.

These reactions are often caused by an excessive intake or prolonged use of the herb. For instance, an individual with a history of allergies may experience hives or difficulty breathing if they ingest large quantities of Betula pendula.

This can worsen their quality of life by limiting their daily activities and requiring medical intervention.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Betula pendula causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

The sap of this tree contains a compound called betulinic acid, which is believed to be responsible for triggering an allergic response in susceptible people. When ingested or contacted with the skin, this compound can cause symptoms such as hives, itching, and redness.

Additionally, the pollen of Betula pendula has been known to exacerbate respiratory allergies like asthma and rhinitis.

2. Triggers skin irritation

Betula pendula triggers skin irritation due to its high concentration of polyphenolic compounds and flavonoids.

These bioactive molecules can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to redness, itching, and even blistering upon contact with the skin.

Additionally, the sap of the Betula pendula tree contains salicylic acid, which may further exacerbate skin irritation and lead to discomfort for those who experience this adverse effect.

3. Induces nausea attacks

Betula pendula induces nausea attacks.

This is often attributed to the presence of compounds called betulinic acid and lupeol, which have been shown to have gastrointestinal stimulating properties. When taken orally, these compounds can irritate the stomach lining, leading to an increased production of stomach acids and a feeling of discomfort or nausea in some individuals.

The exact mechanism behind this effect is not yet fully understood, but it is believed that the stimulating properties of these compounds are responsible for the nausea experienced by some users of Betula pendula.

4. Elicits dizziness spells

Betula pendula elicits dizziness spells in some individuals due to its potent saponin content.

The saponins found in this herb can cause an imbalance of electrolytes and fluids in the body, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness.

Additionally, Betula pendula's vasodilatory properties may further exacerbate these symptoms by causing a rapid drop in blood pressure, resulting in a sensation of spinning or loss of balance.

5. Produces stomach cramps

Betula pendula produces stomach cramps due to its ability to stimulate digestion and increase bowel movements.

The saponins present in this plant can irritate the digestive tract, leading to cramping sensations and discomfort.

Additionally, the flavonoids found in Betula pendula may cause an increase in gut motility, further contributing to stomach cramps in some individuals who use it.

6. Initiates headaches

Betula pendula initiates headaches due to its potential allergenic and irritating properties.

The chemical compounds present in the plant, such as betulinic acid and betulonic acid, can cause sensitivity reactions in some individuals, leading to headache pain. Additionally, the plant's volatile oils may stimulate histamine release, which can further exacerbate headache symptoms.

As a result, people taking Betula pendula supplements or using it topically may experience headaches as an adverse reaction.

7. Leads to insomnia

Betula pendula leads to insomnia due to its potential to interact with certain medications and exacerbate underlying anxiety conditions.

The herb's stimulating properties may cause restlessness and difficulty falling asleep, particularly when taken in large doses or combined with other stimulating substances.

Additionally, the mental clarity and alertness promoted by Betula pendula can make it challenging for individuals to wind down and relax at night, further contributing to insomnia.

8. Brings on anxiety

Betula pendula brings on anxiety due to its high content of salicin, a compound similar to aspirin.

This can lead to an overload of the liver and kidneys, causing feelings of restlessness and unease.

Additionally, Betula pendula's ability to stimulate the nervous system may also contribute to anxiety, as it can increase heart rate and blood pressure, further exacerbating symptoms.

9. Increases heart rate

Betula pendula increases heart rate due to its active ingredient, betulinic acid.

This compound has been found to stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, the beta-amyrin present in Betula pendula can cause vasoconstriction, further contributing to this side effect.

10. Lowers blood pressure

Betula pendula lowers blood pressure by releasing isoflavones and flavonoids, which are natural compounds that have a vasodilatory effect.

These compounds help to relax the blood vessels, allowing for increased blood flow and reduced blood pressure.

Additionally, Betula pendula's saponins may also contribute to this effect by blocking the formation of angiotensin-converting enzyme, further reducing blood pressure.
