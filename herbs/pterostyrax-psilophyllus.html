Pterostyrax psilophyllus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pterostyrax psilophyllus has small, simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical to lanceolate in shape and possess entire margins. The foliage is characterized by its sparse arrangement, which gives the plant its name. These leaves are often leathery in texture and exhibit a dull green color on the upper surface. The stems are woody and can develop a somewhat rough bark as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves during the growing season.
