Sloanea leptocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sloanea leptocarpa has woody stems, elliptical to oblong leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into narrow, elongated capsules. The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture with entire margins and prominent venation. Its inflorescences are often arranged in axillary clusters, providing a platform for small, pale blossoms. The characteristic slender fruit, from which the species derives its name, contains seeds protected within a thin woody wall. These morphological traits collectively distinguish the plant within its tropical forest habitat.
