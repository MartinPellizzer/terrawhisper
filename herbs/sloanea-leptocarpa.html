Sloanea leptocarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Sloanea leptocarpa?

Sloanea leptocarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Oxalidales and is a member of the family Elaeocarpaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Sloanea, and its specific species designation, leptocarpa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sloanea leptocarpa has woody stems, elliptical to oblong leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into narrow, elongated capsules.

The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture with entire margins and prominent venation. Its inflorescences are often arranged in axillary clusters, providing a platform for small, pale blossoms. The characteristic slender fruit, from which the species derives its name, contains seeds protected within a thin woody wall.

These morphological traits collectively distinguish the plant within its tropical forest habitat.
