10 Medicinal Constituents Of Alchemilla Vulgaris (Lady'S Mantle)

Alchemilla vulgaris has active constituents such as flavonoids, triterpenoid saponins, and phenolic acids.

These constituents give the plant medicinal properties including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects. The plant can improve lives by alleviating menstrual cramps and other symptoms associated with PMS, as well as soothing skin irritations. Its antiseptic properties also make it useful for wound healing and preventing infection.

Overall, the medicinal properties of lady's mantle can provide relief and comfort to those who use it.

This article explains in details the 10 best active constituents of Alchemilla vulgaris.

1. Flavonoids

Alchemilla vulgaris flavonoids is a class of bioactive compounds found in its aerial parts.

These flavonoids are responsible for the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They have been shown to exhibit potent activities against oxidative stress, inflammation, and cancer cell growth.

Additionally, they may possess antimicrobial and antiviral properties, making Alchemilla vulgaris a valuable herb in traditional medicine and research.

2. Phenolic acids

Alchemilla vulgaris phenolic acids is a group of compounds responsible for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

These acidic molecules are derived from the plant's leaves and flowers, which have been used in traditional medicine for centuries to treat various ailments.

Phenolic acids in Alchemilla vulgaris have been shown to exhibit antimicrobial and antiviral activities, as well as protect against oxidative stress and DNA damage.

3. Tannins

Alchemilla vulgaris tannins is a type of polyphenolic compound found in its leaves and stems.

These tannins are responsible for the plant's astringent properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various health issues such as diarrhea, dysentery, and menstrual disorders.

The tannins also contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, making it a popular ingredient in herbal remedies and traditional medicine.

4. Carboxylic acids

Alchemilla vulgaris carboxylic acids is a class of compounds responsible for its medicinal properties.

These organic acids are found in the plant's leaves and stems and have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities.

The main carboxylic acids present in Alchemilla vulgaris include tartaric acid, citric acid, malic acid, and fumaric acid, which contribute to its traditional uses for treating wounds, burns, and skin irritations.

5. Terpenes

Alchemilla vulgaris terpenes is a complex mixture of volatile compounds responsible for its distinctive aroma and medicinal properties.

The plant contains over 100 different terpenes, including beta-caryophyllene, alpha-pinene, and limonene, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial activities.

These terpenes work together to provide the herb's traditional uses, such as treating wounds, soothing digestive issues, and supporting women's health.

6. Quercetin

Alchemilla vulgaris quercetin is a bioactive flavonoid compound extracted from the plant.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory problems. Quercetin exhibits potent antioxidant properties, scavenging free radicals and protecting against oxidative stress.

Its anti-inflammatory effects make it a popular ingredient in natural remedies for joint pain and allergies.

7. Kaempferol

Alchemilla vulgaris kaempferol is a flavonoid glycoside that has been isolated from the plant's leaves and flowers.

It has been studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Kaempferol has also been shown to have antimicrobial and antiviral activities, making it a promising compound for the development of new medicines.

8. Catechins

Alchemilla vulgaris catechins is a type of polyphenolic compound found in the plant.

These compounds have been shown to exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, making them potentially beneficial for various health conditions.

Catechins are also responsible for the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, where they are believed to soothe digestive issues and promote wound healing.

9. Gallic acid

Alchemilla vulgaris gallic acid is a phenolic compound extracted from its leaves and stems.

This bioactive compound has been extensively studied for its medicinal properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities.

Gallic acid has been shown to exhibit cytotoxic effects against cancer cells, making it a potential natural therapy for various diseases, particularly those related to inflammation and oxidative stress.

10. Ellagic acid

Alchemilla vulgaris ellagic acid is a polyphenolic compound found in its leaves and stems.

This phytochemical has been extensively studied for its potential health benefits, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Ellagic acid has also been shown to have anticancer effects by inhibiting the growth of cancer cells and inducing apoptosis (programmed cell death).

Additionally, it may help protect against neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's.
