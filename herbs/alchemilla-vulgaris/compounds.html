Lady'S Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) Compounds

Lady's Mantle contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to its astringent effects. Phenolic acids and coumarin provide anti-inflammatory benefits, while glycosides and saponins support digestive health. Terpenoids and essential oils contribute to its aromatic profile, and anthocyanins offer additional antioxidant support. Alkaloids are also present, though in smaller amounts.

Lady'S Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting skin health and reducing inflammation. These compounds are often responsible for the plant's therapeutic effects in traditional herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing effects. Tannins also help in the preparation of tinctures and salves from Lady's Mantle.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound supports its traditional use in promoting wound healing and digestive health. Phenolic acid is found in high concentrations in the leaves and stems of the plant.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's mild antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Found in the leaves and roots, coumarin supports digestive health and may aid in reducing menstrual discomfort. Its presence enhances the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting women's health and digestive function. Glycosides in Lady's Mantle are known for their mild astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. These compounds help reduce tissue damage and promote cellular repair. They are found in the plant's leaves and are often used in herbal formulations for skin conditions and digestive support.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and mild detoxifying properties. Saponins may support digestive health and skin healing, making them valuable in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) contains volatile compounds like limonene and cineole. It is used in herbal medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and flowers. It supports skin health and is valued in traditional remedies.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanins are pigments found in Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) that contribute to its antioxidant properties. These compounds help neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. They are also associated with anti-inflammatory and protective effects in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though it is not the primary active constituent. Alkaloids in this plant are associated with mild anti-inflammatory and astringent effects. They are typically present in lower concentrations compared to other bioactive compounds like flavonoids.
