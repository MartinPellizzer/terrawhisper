Botanical Identification of Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris)

To identify Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Lady's Mantle, or Alchemilla vulgaris, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Rosales, family Rosaceae, genus Alchemilla, and species Alchemilla vulgaris. These classifications help identify its botanical relationships and place it within the broader plant kingdom.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Lady's Mantle is Alchemilla vulgaris, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It has several synonyms, including Alchemilla virgata and Alchemilla mollis, reflecting historical taxonomic revisions. Common names such as "Lady's Mantle" and "Alchemilla" derive from its traditional uses and appearance. Regional and historical names vary, often tied to folk usage and local traditions.

Morphological Characteristics

Lady's Mantle is a low-growing perennial with a dense, fibrous root system. It has upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces upright, branched inflorescences with small, greenish-yellow flowers. The fruit is a three-lobed achene, and the seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Lady's Mantle can be distinguished by its distinctive shield-shaped leaves with wavy edges and a central notch, along with its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It often grows in moist, shaded areas and has a creeping root system. It is typically found in temperate regions and is easily confused with related species like Alchemilla mollis, but its leaf shape and growth habit are key differentiators.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Lady's Mantle is native to temperate regions of Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. It thrives in cool, moist climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is commonly found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas, typically growing between 200 and 1,500 meters in elevation.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Lady's Mantle blooms from June to August, producing yellow-green flowers in clusters. Seed formation occurs in late summer, with seeds dispersing by wind in autumn. For identification, the plant is most accurately recognized during flowering, which typically occurs in mid to late summer.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Lady's Mantle (Alchemilla vulgaris) is closely related to other members of the Rosaceae family, particularly species within the genus Alchemilla. It shares key family traits such as stipitate stipules and compound leaves. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its close ties to other alpine and herbaceous plants in the rose family.
