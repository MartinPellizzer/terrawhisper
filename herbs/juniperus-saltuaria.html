Juniperus saltuaria

What's the taxonomical classification of Juniperus saltuaria?

Juniperus saltuaria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae, falling under the order Pinales. The plant is a member of the family Cupressaceae and is further categorized into the genus Juniperus, specifically identified as the species saltuaria.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juniperus saltuaria has scale-like leaves arranged in opposite pairs and small, globose blue-gray berries that develop from fleshy cones. The foliage is densely packed along slender, woody stems that exhibit a spreading or shrubby growth habit. Each leaf scale is tightly appressed to the twig, creating a smooth texture on younger branches. The mature plant often features a multi-stemmed structure with reddish-brown bark. These reproductive cones transition from green to a deep indigo hue upon reaching full maturity.
