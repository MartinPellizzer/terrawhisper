What's the taxonomical classification of Phleum paniculatum?

Phleum paniculatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Poales. This plant is a member of the family Poaceae, which places it within the genus Phleum, ultimately identifying it at the species level as Phleum paniculatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phleum paniculatum has upright, slender, and unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, terminal panicle of small, inconspicuous flowers that transition from green to a brownish hue. The root system is composed of creeping rhizomes that allow the plant to form dense colonies in its natural habitat.
