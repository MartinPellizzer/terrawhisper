Artemisia halodendron

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia halodendroon has perennial, woody-based stems with deeply lobed, silvery-grey leaves that exhibit a fine, felt-like pubescence on both surfaces. The foliage consists of narrow, linear segments that create a delicate, feathery texture throughout the plant. These leaves are arranged alternately along the branches to maximize light absorption in its native habitat. Small, inconspicuous flower heads emerge in dense clusters during the blooming season. The overall structure of the plant remains compact and bushy, providing a low-growing profile.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Artemisia halodendroon can cause severe skin irritation, localized rashes, and potential allergic reactions upon direct physical contact. The plant contains volatile oils and chemical compounds that act as topical irritants to sensitive dermal tissue. Individuals exposed to the plant may experience redness, itching, or swelling at the site of contact. If the oils are accidentally ingested, they may lead to gastrointestinal distress or more serious systemic toxicity. Proper protective clothing should be worn to prevent accidental exposure during handling.
