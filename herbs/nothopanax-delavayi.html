Nothopanax delavayi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nothopanax delavayi has a woody shrub or small tree habit characterized by large, palmately compound leaves that typically consist of five to seven leaflets. Each leaflet is ovate to lanceolate in shape and features serrated margins with distinct venation. The plant produces terminal inflorescences that bear small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that serve as the primary fruit type for the species. The overall structure is defined by its branching woody stems and the prominent, spreading arrangement of its foliage.
