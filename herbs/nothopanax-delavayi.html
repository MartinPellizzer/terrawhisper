Nothopanax delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Nothopanax delavayi?

Nothopanax delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is further categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Apiales, it falls under the family Araliaceae. Within this family, the plant is assigned to the genus Nothopanax, with its specific designation being the species delavayi.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nothopanax delavayi has a woody shrub or small tree habit characterized by large, palmately compound leaves that typically consist of five to seven leaflets.

Each leaflet is ovate to lanceolate in shape and features serrated margins with distinct venation. The plant produces terminal inflorescences that bear small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes that serve as the primary fruit type for the species.

The overall structure is defined by its branching woody stems and the prominent, spreading arrangement of its foliage.
