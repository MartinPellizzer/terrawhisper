Purple Violet

Viola violacea

Purple Violet (Viola violacea) is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, known for its striking purple petals and historical use in traditional medicine to support respiratory and skin health.

Botanical Identification

Purple Violet (Viola violacea) is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is also known as the common violet. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and violet flowers with a distinctive purple hue. Its scientific name is widely recognized in herbal literature, though it has been referred to by various regional common names.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Violales
  • Family: Violaceae
  • Genus: Viola
  • Species: Viola violacea

Safety Profile

Purple Violet (Viola violacea) is generally safe when used in moderate amounts. However, it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for those with existing health conditions.
