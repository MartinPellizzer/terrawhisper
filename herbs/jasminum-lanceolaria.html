What's the taxonomical classification of Jasminum lanceolaria?

Jasminum lanceolaria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized under the order Lamiales and falls within the family Oleaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Jasminum, with its unique designation as the species lanceolaria.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Jasminum lanceolaria has woody, climbing stems characterized by lanceolate, entire-margined leaves that typically measure several centimeters in length and possess a glossy texture. The foliage is arranged in opposite pairs along the slender branches, creating a lush, verdant appearance. Its inflorescences consist of fragrant, tubular flowers that are often white or pale yellow in color. These blossoms emerge from the leaf axils and feature five distinct lobes at the corolla opening. The plant also produces small, fleshy berries that develop once the flowering period concludes.
