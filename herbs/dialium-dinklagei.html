Dialium dinklagei

What's the taxonomical classification of Dialium dinklagei?

Dialium dinklagei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Fabales. It is further categorized under the family Fabaceae, a group known for its diverse and economically significant legumes. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Dialium, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, dinklagei.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dialium dinklagei has a woody structure characterized by a medium to large tree habit with dark, fissured bark and pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of oblong leaflets.

The foliage is typically coriaceous and displays a distinctively glossy surface on the upper side. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually developing into hard, woody pods that contain edible seeds. These pods are often dark in color and possess a thick, protective pericarp.

The overall morphology reflects its adaptation to tropical woodland environments.
