Dialium dinklagei

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dialium dinklagei has a woody structure characterized by a medium to large tree habit with dark, fissured bark and pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of oblong leaflets. The foliage is typically coriaceous and displays a distinctively glossy surface on the upper side. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually developing into hard, woody pods that contain edible seeds. These pods are often dark in color and possess a thick, protective pericarp. The overall morphology reflects its adaptation to tropical woodland environments.
