What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia campbellii?

Artemisia campbellii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its progression through the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Artemisia, specifically as the species campbellii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau within China. It grows specifically in montane environments across provinces such as Tibet, Qinghai, and Sichuan. These populations are typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at significant elevations. The species is restricted to these specialized cold climates and does not occur in lowland territories.
