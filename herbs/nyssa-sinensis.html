Nyssa sinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Nyssa sinensis?

Nyssa sinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Cornales and the family Nyssaceae. Ultimately, its specific scientific classification is defined by the genus Nyssa and the species sinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nyssa sinensis has an upright, deciduous growth habit characterized by small, ovate leaves with entire margins and a glossy texture that typically turns vibrant red or orange in autumn. The plant features slender, grayish-brown branches that produce small, fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring. These blossoms eventually develop into fleshy, dark purple to black drupes that are roughly six millimeters in diameter. The foliage is arranged alternately along the stems, creating a dense canopy during the growing season. Its root system is typically well-developed to support its medium-sized tree structure.
