Wisteria villosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Wisteria villosa?

Wisteria villosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is categorized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, which places it among the legumes. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Wisteria and the species villosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Wisteria villosa has woody, twining vines characterized by stems covered in fine, soft hairs and pinnately compound leaves consisting of seven to eleven elliptical leaflets. The foliage features a soft texture due to the presence of pubescence on both the upper and lower leaf surfaces. During the flowering season, the plant produces drooping clusters of pea-like blossoms that range in color from pale blue to light violet. Each flower is attached to a slender pedicel and emerges from axillary positions along the climbing stem. The woody structure of the plant becomes increasingly robust as it matures, providing the necessary strength for its climbing habit.
