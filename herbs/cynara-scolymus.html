What to know about Cynara scolymus before using it medicinally

Cynara scolymus, commonly known as globe artichoke, has been traditionally used in the treatment of various health conditions, including liver and gallbladder disorders, digestive issues, and high cholesterol.

The herb is valued for its ability to promote overall health and wellness, with its main health benefits including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and hepatoprotective properties. The main therapeutic properties of Cynara scolymus include its ability to stimulate bile production, improve digestion, and protect against liver damage. The herb is rich in bioactive compounds such as cynarin, silymarin, and flavonoids, which have been shown to have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

A common herbal preparation made from Cynara scolymus is a leaf extract or a standardized cynarin-rich extract, which is often used to support liver health and improve digestion.

Featured Study:

According to a study published by "Immunopharmacology and immunotoxicology", Cynara scolymus has shown potential therapeutic effects on ulcerative colitis (UC) by alleviating clinical symptoms, including disease activity index and histological damage, in animal models. Furthermore, Cynara scolymus may mitigate UC partially through inhibiting the STAT3/NF-кB signaling pathways and macrophage polarization toward M1, suggesting its potential as an effective therapy for UC.
