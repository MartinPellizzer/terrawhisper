Side Effects of cynara scolymus

Cynara scolymus may cause nausea, which can be a common side effect when consumed in large quantities or by individuals with sensitive digestive systems.

This herb may lead to vomiting in some cases, particularly if it is not properly prepared or if it interacts with other medications. It can also cause stomach upset, resulting in discomfort and bloating, which may exacerbate existing gastrointestinal conditions.

Additionally, Cynara scolymus may trigger digestive issues such as diarrhea, which can be mild or severe depending on the dosage and individual tolerance. Some people may experience mouth irritation or heartburn due to the herb's bitter compounds, while others might develop skin rashes or allergic reactions, especially if they have a known sensitivity to plants in the Asteraceae family.

These side effects highlight the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before using Cynara scolymus, particularly for those with preexisting health conditions or allergies.
