Artichoke

Cynara scolymus

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Artichoke, scientifically known as Cynara scolymus, belongs to the Asteraceae family. Common names include globe artichoke and heart of palm, with regional variations. It is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant has spiny leaves, a thick stem, and purple flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include artichoke and cardoon.

Active Compounds

Artichoke contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and coumarins. These compounds work synergistically to support various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Coumarin

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action

Integumentary Skin infections, psoriasis, wounds Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant
Respiratory Respiratory infections, cough, asthma, allergic rhinitis, chronic bronchitis Antimicrobial, expectorant, antihistaminic

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Culinary use: Used for general nutrition and as a food source.

Extract: Used for immune support and as an antioxidant.

Safety Profile

Artichoke is generally safe when consumed in typical food amounts. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated for people with known allergies to plants in the Asteraceae family.
