Enkianthus serrulatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Enkianthus serrulatus?

Enkianthus serrulatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ericales and the family Ericaceae, ultimately reaching its specific designation within the genus Enkianthus. As the specific epithet defines its identity, the plant is known scientifically as the species Enkianthus serrulatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Enkianthus serrulatus has erect, woody stems that typically reach heights of 0.5 to 1.5 meters and feature leaves that are narrowly lanceolate to linear with distinctively serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a bright green color and a texture that often feels somewhat leathery to the touch. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a pale pink to white coloration that contrasts with the darker stems. These blooms are arranged in loose clusters and possess a delicate structure typical of the Rubiaceae family. The plant's overall morphology reflects an adaptation to semi-arid environments where its sturdy stem structure provides necessary support.
