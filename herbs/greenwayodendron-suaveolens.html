What's the taxonomical classification of Greenwayodendron suaveolens?

Greenwayodendron suaveolens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Magnoliales. The plant is further categorized under the family Annonaceae, which places it within a group of diverse tropical flowering plants. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Greenwayodendron, specifically reaching its unique taxonomic designation at the species level as Greenwayodendron suaveolens.

What are the pharmacological activities of Greenwayodendron suaveolens?

This plant has antibacterial, antimycobacterial, antiprotozoal, and cytotoxic pharmacological activities derived from its diverse bioactive secondary metabolites. Research indicates that its extracts demonstrate significant inhibitory effects against various pathogenic bacterial strains and mycobacteria. The presence of specific chemical constituents also allows the plant to combat protozoal infections effectively. Furthermore, certain compounds isolated from the species exhibit cytotoxic properties that are being studied for potential anti-cancer applications. These multi-faceted biological actions highlight the therapeutic potential of Greenwayodendron suaveolens in modern pharmacology.

As discussed in a study, methanolic extracts of isolated endophytic fungi from the stem barks and leaves of Greenwayodendron suaveolens possess antibacterial activity against Klebsiella pnumeoniae ATCC 43816, Haemophilus influenzae ATCC 49247, Pseudomonas aeruginosa ATCC 27853, and Escherichia coli ATCC 35218. These endophytic fungi produced inhibition zones ranging from 7.00 to 25.00 mm. Specifically, the endophytic fungus GS15 presented an inhibition zone of 20.33 mm, which was better than the results obtained for Levofloxacin. The extracts showed minimum inhibitory concentrations ranging from 6.25 x 10^-4 to 2 x 10^-2 g/L and minimum bactericidal concentrations ranging from 2.5 x 10^-3 to 2 x 10^-2 g/L. The GS15 extract was the most effective and showed bactericidal effects on the tested bacterial strains.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains acetogenins, alkaloids, flavonoids, glycosides, and saponosides that contribute to its diverse medicinal properties. The acetogenins act as potent cytotoxic agents, while the alkaloids often provide significant neurological or physiological effects. Flavonoids within the plant offer strong antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the body. Additionally, the glycosides can influence metabolic processes, and the saponosides contribute to its immune-modulating capabilities. These various chemical constituents work together to create a complex therapeutic profile.

As discussed in a study, Greenwayodendron suaveolens was evaluated for its in vitro activity against Mycobacterium smegmatis. The chemical investigation of its promising extracts revealed the presence of phenols, alkaloids, glycosides, triterpene, and acetogenins. Subfraction P12 from the Greenwayodendron suaveolens twig was found to be more potent with a minimum inhibitory concentration value of 6.25μg/mL. Additionally, subfraction P29 from the twig showed potency with a minimum inhibitory concentration value of 25μg/mL. These results support the traditional claims of Greenwayodendron suaveolens in the treatment of mycobacterial infections.
