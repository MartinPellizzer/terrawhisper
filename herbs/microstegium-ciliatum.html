Microstegium ciliatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microstegium ciliatum has a slender, tufted growth habit characterized by erect or ascending culms that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The leaves are narrow and linear, often possessing fine, hair-like margins that give the foliage a delicate texture. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, contracted panicles that appear spike-like and are densely packed with small spikelets. Each spikelet is composed of several florets enclosed by lemma and palea structures that may feature fine cilia. The overall morphology is adapted for efficient survival in disturbed or open habitats.

    Microstegium ciliatum can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, itching, and redness when the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. Monitoring for any adverse reactions is recommended when handling this species in a garden setting.
