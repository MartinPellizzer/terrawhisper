Microstegium ciliatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Microstegium ciliatum?

Microstegium ciliatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Poales and the family Poaceae, which ultimately places it within the genus Microstegium. As the specific identifier, the species name is ciliatum.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Microstegium ciliatum can cause allergic skin reactions such as contact dermatitis, itching, and redness when the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive skin. Some individuals may experience localized swelling or irritation if they touch the foliage or stems. Inhalation of pollen or dust from the plant could potentially trigger respiratory symptoms in people with known allergies or asthma. While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, caution is advised to avoid ingestion to prevent unpredictable gastrointestinal distress. Monitoring for any adverse reactions is recommended when handling this species in a garden setting.
