Microstegium ciliatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Microstegium ciliatum?

Microstegium ciliatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Poales and the family Poaceae, which ultimately places it within the genus Microstegium. As the specific identifier, the species name is ciliatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microstegium ciliatum has a slender, tufted growth habit characterized by erect or ascending culms that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often possessing fine, hair-like margins that give the foliage a delicate texture. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, contracted panicles that appear spike-like and are densely packed with small spikelets. Each spikelet is composed of several florets enclosed by lemma and palea structures that may feature fine cilia.

The overall morphology is adapted for efficient survival in disturbed or open habitats.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

While systemic toxicity is not widely documented, caution is advised to avoid ingestion to prevent unpredictable gastrointestinal distress. Monitoring for any adverse reactions is recommended when handling this species in a garden setting.
