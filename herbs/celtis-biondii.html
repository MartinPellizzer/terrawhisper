Celtis biondii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celtis biondii has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape with serrated margins and prominent pinnate venation. The leaf bases are often asymmetrical, a characteristic common to many species within the Celtidaceae family. The stems exhibit a rugged texture with lenticels that become more pronounced as the tree matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in panicles, eventually developing into small, fleshy drupes. These fruits serve as a vital food source for various avian species in its natural habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and West Africa, specifically found within the moist lowland forests of countries such as Cameroon, Gabon, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It typically thrives in dense, humid environments where high rainfall and consistent temperatures are present. Within these ecosystems, the species is often distributed along riverbanks or in shaded understory layers. Its range is closely tied to the availability of stable tropical climates and specific soil types found in the Guineo-Congolian forest region.     What are the herbal preparations of Celtis biondii?

    Celtis biondii is prepared as a decoction of the roots, a powder from the bark, or a topical paste made from crushed leaves. The root decoction is frequently consumed to treat various internal ailments such as stomach discomfort.
