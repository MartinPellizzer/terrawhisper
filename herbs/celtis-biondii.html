Celtis biondii

What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis biondii?

Celtis biondii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is ordered within the Rosales and placed in the family Cannabaceae. Ultimately, this plant is defined by its genus, Celtis, and its specific species designation, biondii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Celtis biondiii contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, saponins, and tannins that provide its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents work synergistically to produce significant antioxidant and antimicrobial activities within various medicinal preparations. Researchers have identified specific phytochemical profiles that suggest the plant may assist in treating inflammatory conditions and digestive ailments. Different parts of the tree, such as the bark and leaves, are processed to extract these compounds for traditional healing practices. The presence of these diverse molecules underpins the plant's recognized role in ethnobotanical medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celtis biondii?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat stomach aches, intestinal worms, skin infections, and various types of inflammation. The bark is often decocted in water to serve as a remedy for digestive issues and parasitic infestations. When applied topically, the plant material helps to soothe skin irritations and clear up minor wounds. It is also utilized in traditional medicine to reduce swelling and manage pain in different parts of the body. These therapeutic applications demonstrate the diverse medicinal properties found within its various parts.
