Celtis biondii

What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis biondii?

Celtis biondii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is ordered within the Rosales and placed in the family Cannabaceae. Ultimately, this plant is defined by its genus, Celtis, and its specific species designation, biondii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celtis biondii has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape with serrated margins and prominent pinnate venation.

The leaf bases are often asymmetrical, a characteristic common to many species within the Celtidaceae family. The stems exhibit a rugged texture with lenticels that become more pronounced as the tree matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in panicles, eventually developing into small, fleshy drupes.

These fruits serve as a vital food source for various avian species in its natural habitat.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Celtis biondii targets cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, lipoxygenase (LOX) proteins, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to produce its medicinal effects. The presence of bioactive secondary metabolites like flavonoids and polyphenols allows these compounds to inhibit the arachidonic acid metabolism pathway. By suppressing COX-2 activity, the plant effectively reduces the synthesis of prostaglandins, which are key mediators of pain and inflammation. Additionally, these molecular interactions extend to the modulation of nitric oxide synthase, helping to regulate oxidative stress within cellular environments. This multi-targeted approach to protein inhibition explains the plant's diverse pharmacological profile in traditional medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Celtis biondii?

Celtis biondii has medicinal properties that include astringent, mucilaginous, anti-inflammatory, and emollient actions used to treat various ailments.

The plant's mucilaginous components provide a soothing layer that protects irritated membranes during treatment. Its astringent qualities help to contract tissues and reduce excessive secretions in affected areas. Furthermore, the anti-inflammatory effects work to decrease swelling and redness within the body.

These combined attributes also allow the plant to act as an emollient, softening the skin and easing discomfort.

    What are the herbal preparations of Celtis biondii?

    Celtis biondii is prepared as a decoction of the roots, a powder from the bark, or a topical paste made from crushed leaves. The root decoction is frequently consumed to treat various internal ailments such as stomach discomfort. Bark is often processed into a fine powder that can be mixed with water or other liquids for ingestion. Leaves are crushed into a thick paste and applied directly to the skin to soothe localized inflammation or wounds. These different preparation methods allow practitioners to target specific symptoms using various parts of the plant.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Celtis biondii can cause allergic skin reactions such as dermatitis, gastrointestinal distress, or unexpected toxicity if ingested in improper quantities. While specific clinical data on human subjects remains limited, many species within the Celtis genus contain bioactive compounds that may trigger sensitivity in certain individuals. Contact with the sap or raw plant material might lead to localized redness, itching, or swelling on the skin. Careful observation is necessary when using this plant for medicinal purposes to avoid adverse biological responses. It is always advisable to consult a professional before any direct application or consumption.
