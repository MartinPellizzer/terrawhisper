Ficus auriculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Ficus auriculata?

Ficus auriculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Rosales and the family Moraceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Ficus, with the specific epithet auriculata, identifying it as the distinct species Ficus auriculata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Ficus auriculata contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These specific chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and antimicrobial capabilities. Research suggests that extracts from the leaves and bark can be utilized to treat inflammatory conditions and various skin ailments. Some traditional medicinal practices also employ these compounds to address digestive issues and fever. The presence of these diverse phytochemicals makes the plant a valuable subject for pharmacological studies.

What are the therapeutic actions of Ficus auriculata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including diarrhea, dysentery, skin infections, inflammation, and menstrual irregularities. The leaves and bark are frequently prepared into decoctions or pastes to address gastrointestinal distress and digestive issues. Topical applications of the plant material are often utilized to soothe skin irritations and promote healing in wounded areas. Additionally, traditional medicinal practices employ parts of the plant to manage swelling and regulate reproductive health. These therapeutic properties make it a significant resource in various ethnobotanical traditions.
