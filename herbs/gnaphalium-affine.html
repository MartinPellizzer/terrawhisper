Gnaphalium affine

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gnaphaliuum affine has a perennial, woody-based herb characterized by silver-grey, woolly foliage and small, discoid flower heads arranged in terminal clusters. The leaves are typically linear to lanceolate in shape and are densely covered in fine, white tomentose hairs that provide a felt-like texture. These hairs serve to protect the plant from moisture loss and intense sunlight in its natural habitat. The stems are often branched and can become somewhat woody at the base as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flower heads lack ray florets and are composed entirely of tubular disc florets.
