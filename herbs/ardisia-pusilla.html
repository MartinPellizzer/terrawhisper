What's the taxonomical classification of Ardisia pusilla?

Ardisia pusilla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Ericales and into the family Primulaceae. Finally, it is identified by its genus, Ardisia, and its specific epithet, pusilla.

As discussed in a study, ardipu$$illoside I is extracted from Ardisia pusilla A.DC and effectively inhibits the progression of several cancers in animal models. This substance is considered a potential anti-cancer drug candidate. After oral administration of ardipu$$illoside I, 4 possible metabolites, identified as M1, M2, M3, and M4, were detected in the content from the rat small intestine. In rat plasma, only 2 of these metabolites, specifically M3 and M4, were detected following oral administration. It was noted that none of the metabolites were detected in plasma samples after intravenous administration of ardipu$$illoside I to rats. These findings suggest that M3 and M4 may be responsible for the antitumor activity of orally administered ardipu$$illoside I in rat models of cancer.

As discussed in a study, the plant Ardisia pusilla contains bergengin at a level of 59% at least. The determination of this compound was performed using High Performance Liquid Chromatography with a mobile phase of methanol-water (20:80) and a flow rate of 0.600 ml/min. During the analysis, the detecting wavelength was set at 275 nm and the column temperature was 25 degrees C. Bergengin showed a good linearity in the range of 0.098-0.591 microg with an r = 0.9998. The average recovery for the study was 102.0% with an RSD of 1.4% (n=6).
