What's the taxonomical classification of Veronicastrum villosulum?

Veronicastrum villosulum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales and falls within the family Plantaginaceae. Ultimately, its specific identification is defined by the genus Veronicastrum and the species villosulum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Veronicastrum villosulum has small, ovate leaves covered in dense, soft, villous hairs and terminal clusters of tiny, tubular, pale blue to white flowers.

The stems are woody at the base and become increasingly pubescent toward the growing tips. Each leaf possesses a slightly serrated margin and a distinctively textured surface due to the thick coating of trichomes. The inflorescence is characterized by a compact arrangement of blossoms that emerge from the axils of the upper leaves.

These fine hairs serve as a protective layer against moisture loss and herbivory.
