10 Health Benefits Of Anthemis Cotula (Mayweed)

Anthemis cotula, also known as mayweed, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and promoting relaxation.

Its medicinal properties include flavonoids and terpenoids, which have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. These benefits can improve people's lives by alleviating symptoms of anxiety and insomnia, and soothing digestive issues.

For example, mayweed tea has been used to calm nervous stomachs and promote a restful night's sleep.

1. Reduces inflammation

Anthemis cotula reduces inflammation because it contains a unique compound called sesquiterpene lactone, which has potent anti-inflammatory properties.

This compound has been shown to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, thereby reducing the inflammatory response.

Additionally, mayweed's flavonoids and phenolic acids have been demonstrated to possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, further contributing to its anti-inflammatory capabilities.

2. Relieves pain

Anthemis cotula relieves pain because it contains compounds that have anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's flavonoids and terpenoids work together to reduce swelling and discomfort, making it a natural remedy for minor aches and pains.

Additionally, mayweed's camphor content has a soothing effect on the skin, further contributing to its pain-relieving abilities.

3. Lowers fever

Anthemis cotula lowers fever because of its inherent ability to reduce inflammation and discomfort associated with high body temperatures.

The plant's active compounds, including flavonoids and sesquiterpenes, possess potent anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties, which help to ease the symptoms of fever and alleviate discomfort.

This natural remedy has been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat fevers and other ailments, providing a safe and effective way to manage temperature and promote overall well-being.

4. Treats wounds

Anthemis cotula treats wounds because of its potent anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant's leaves and flowers contain flavonoids, terpenes, and other bioactive compounds that inhibit bacterial growth, reduce swelling, and promote tissue repair.

Traditionally, mayweed has been used to dress wounds, ulcers, and skin irritations, and its application is believed to accelerate the healing process and prevent infection.

5. Fights infections

Anthemis cotula fights infections because of its rich history of traditional use as a natural remedy.

The plant's bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpenes and flavonoids, have been shown to exhibit antimicrobial properties, inhibiting the growth of various microorganisms including bacteria, viruses, and fungi.

This may help to reduce the severity and duration of infections, making mayweed a promising candidate for further research and potential applications in medicine.

6. Relaxes muscles

Anthemis cotula relaxes muscles because it contains a unique compound called sesquiterpene lactone, which has been shown to have a calming effect on the nervous system.

This compound works by blocking the production of stress hormones, such as cortisol, and promoting the release of relaxing neurotransmitters, such as GABA and serotonin.

As a result, mayweed has been traditionally used to treat muscle spasms, anxiety, and insomnia, and has been found to be effective in reducing muscle tension and promoting relaxation.

Anthemis cotula calms anxiety because it contains compounds that have a sedative effect on the nervous system.

The plant's essential oils, particularly camomile and apigenin, bind to GABA receptors, promoting relaxation and reducing stress-induced anxiety. As a natural relaxant, mayweed soothes the mind and body, helping to calm the nervous system and alleviate symptoms of anxiety disorders.

Its calming properties make it a popular herbal remedy for anxiety relief.

8. Eases digestion

Anthemis cotula eases digestion because of its unique composition.

The plant contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids, terpenes, and sesquiterpenes, which work together to soothe the digestive system. Specifically, mayweed has been shown to reduce inflammation and alleviate symptoms of irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), such as bloating, cramps, and diarrhea.

By calming the gut and reducing discomfort, mayweed helps to promote a healthy digestive process and alleviate symptoms of digestive disorders.

9. Boosts immunity

Anthemis cotula boosts immunity because of its rich antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's essential oil, containing sesquiterpenes and terpenoids, has been shown to stimulate the production of white blood cells, thereby enhancing the body's natural defense against infections and diseases. Additionally, mayweed's flavonoids and phenolic acids help to neutralize free radicals, protecting cells from damage and supporting overall immune function.

This natural immunity-boosting effect makes mayweed a valuable component in herbal remedies and a popular ingredient in many traditional folk medicines.

10. Kills cancer cells

Anthemis cotula kills cancer cells because of its unique ability to induce apoptosis, or programmed cell death, in cancerous cells.

Mayweed contains a compound called anthemic acid, which has been shown to selectively target and destroy cancer cells, while leaving healthy cells intact.

This selectivity is due to the fact that anthemic acid binds to a specific protein on the surface of cancer cells, causing them to self-destruct.
