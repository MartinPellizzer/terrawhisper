Cotula (Anthemis cotula) Compounds

The primary compounds in Cotula include essential oil, which contributes to its aromatic properties. Flavonoids and terpenoids are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Phenolic acids and coumarin provide additional therapeutic benefits. Glycosides, tannins, saponins, and alkaloids also contribute to its medicinal profile. Anthocyanins add to its potential health benefits.

Cotula (Anthemis cotula) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Cotula (Anthemis cotula) contains bioactive compounds like chamazulene and bisabolol. These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The oil is commonly used in herbal medicine for its soothing effects on the respiratory and skin systems.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Cotula (Anthemis cotula). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory ailments. These compounds help modulate cellular responses and may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects.

3. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Cotula (Anthemis cotula). These compounds contribute to the plant's antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They are responsible for some of the therapeutic effects observed in traditional herbal medicine using Cotula.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Cotula (Anthemis cotula), contributing to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. It supports the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and skin conditions. This compound helps modulate immune responses and may aid in reducing oxidative stress in the body.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Cotula (Anthemis cotula). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for its antispasmodic and sedative properties. Coumarin also exhibits mild anticoagulant effects, supporting its role in treating digestive and nervous system ailments.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Cotula (Anthemis cotula). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting respiratory and digestive health. Glycosides in Cotula are known for their mild sedative and anti-inflammatory effects.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Cotula (Anthemis cotula), contributing to its astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and may support digestive health. Tannins in Cotula are also believed to aid in wound healing and have antimicrobial effects.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Cotula (Anthemis cotula). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Saponins may support digestive health and help reduce symptoms of respiratory conditions. Their presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Cotula in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Anthemis cotula contains the alkaloid cotuline. This compound contributes to the plant's sedative and antispasmodic properties. It is believed to support digestive health and may aid in reducing anxiety. Cotuline is one of several bioactive components responsible for the plant's traditional medicinal uses.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Cotula (Anthemis cotula) that contributes to its medicinal properties. It acts as an antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting respiratory and digestive health.
