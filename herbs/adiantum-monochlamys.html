Adiantum monochlamys

What's the taxonomical classification of Adiantum monochlamys?

Adiantum monochlamys belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae, this plant falls under the order Polypodiales. It is further categorized into the family Pteridaceae and is identified within the genus Adiantum, specifically as the species monochlamys.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various respiratory ailments, digestive disturbances, and inflammatory skin conditions. Its medicinal properties are often leveraged to soothe coughs and alleviate symptoms of asthma or bronchitis. In traditional practices, extracts from the plant may be applied topically to reduce swelling or irritation on the skin. Some practitioners also utilize it to address gastrointestinal issues such as indigestion or stomach cramps. The specific application of the plant depends heavily on the preparation method and the severity of the condition being addressed.
