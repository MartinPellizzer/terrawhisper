Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus Angustifolia)?

The Russian Silver Bush, or Elaeagnus angustifolia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, known for its rapid growth, nitrogen-fixing ability, and historical use in herbal medicine for its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and skin-healing properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Russian Silver Bush is Elaeagnus angustifolia. This plant belongs to the family Elaeagnaceae, genus Elaeagnus, and species Elaeagnus angustifolia. It is commonly known as Russian Silver Bush in Europe and as Silverthorn in North America. The plant is also known by the synonyms Elaeagnus monogyna and Elaeagnus x hybrida.

Plant Description and Morphology

Russian Silver Bush is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters with a spreading, multi-branched structure.

Its leaves are oval to elliptic in shape, with a finely serrated margin and a silvery-white underside that gives the plant its common name. The flowers are small, yellow-green, and clustered in dense catkins, appearing in early summer before the leaves fully emerge. The root system is extensive and fibrous, allowing the plant to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, while the leaves are thin, leathery, and emit a slight resinous odor when crushed.

Native Habitat and Distribution

Russian Silver Bush is native to Central Asia, the Middle East, and parts of southern Europe, with its origins in regions such as Russia, Turkey, and the Caucasus.

It is widely distributed across temperate regions, including the Mediterranean, parts of China, and the Indian subcontinent. The plant thrives in arid to semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is adaptable to various ecological conditions, including rocky soils and disturbed sites, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in regions with cold winters and hot, dry summers.
