Russian Silver Bush

Elaeagnus angustifolia

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Elaeagnus angustifolia, commonly known as Russian Silver Bush, belongs to the Elaeagnaceae family. It is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia and the Middle East. The plant has silvery leaves, yellow flowers, and a woody root system. It is also known as silver leaf elaeagnus in some herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The Russian Silver Bush contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid
  • Terpenoid

Digestive Digestive issues, gastrointestinal infections, diarrhea, gastric discomfort Antimicrobial, antispasmodic, antacid

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and digestive problems.

Decoction: Used for gastrointestinal disorders and inflammation.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a dietary supplement.

Safety Profile

The Russian Silver Bush is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential risks to fetal and infant development. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
