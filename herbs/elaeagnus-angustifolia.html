Russian Silver Bush

Elaeagnus angustifolia

Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia) is a shrub native to parts of Asia and Europe, known for its silver-gray leaves and fragrant yellow flowers, and it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support skin health and aid in the treatment of conditions like eczema and psoriasis.

Taxonomy

The Russian Silver Bush, or Elaeagnus angustifolia, is a species belonging to the genus Elaeagnus in the family Elaeagnaceae, classified within the order Saxifragales, class Magnoliopsida, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

The Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia) is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its silver-gray bark, slender elliptical leaves with a leathery texture, and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in early summer, followed by small, edible, reddish-brown fruits.

Distribution

The Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia) is native to temperate regions of Asia, including Central Asia, the Middle East, and parts of eastern Europe, and has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America and Australia, where it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Traditional Uses

In traditional herbal medicine, the Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia) has been used for its purported ability to support skin health, aid in the treatment of respiratory conditions, and promote wound healing due to its high content of essential oils and nutrients.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, and Persian medicine, the Russian Silver Bush has been utilized for its healing properties.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia) includes compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, triterpenes, and volatile oils, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for their antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

Pharmacology

Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia) exhibits pharmacological properties including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, immunostimulant, and antioxidant effects, which contribute to its traditional use in treating infections, respiratory conditions, and supporting immune function.

Botanical Identification

Elaeagnus angustifolia, commonly known as Russian Silver Bush, belongs to the Elaeagnaceae family. It is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia and the Middle East. The plant has silvery leaves, yellow flowers, and a woody root system. It is also known as silver leaf elaeagnus in some herbal texts.

Safety Profile

The Russian Silver Bush is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals.
