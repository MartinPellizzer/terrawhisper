Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia) is a shrub native to parts of Asia and Europe, known for its silver-gray leaves and fragrant yellow flowers, and it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to support skin health and aid in the treatment of conditions like eczema and psoriasis.

What is Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus Angustifolia)?

The Russian Silver Bush, or Elaeagnus angustifolia, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia, characterized by its silvery foliage, rapid growth, and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The Russian Silver Bush, also known as Elaeagnus angustifolia, is commonly referred to by various names including silverthorn, Russian tea, and Indian silver bush, and is widely used in traditional herbal medicine across different cultures.

The Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia) is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its silver-gray bark, slender elliptical leaves with a leathery texture, and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in early summer, followed by small, edible, reddish-brown fruits.

The Russian Silver Bush (Elaeagnus angustifolia) is native to Central Asia, particularly regions of Kazakhstan, southern Russia, and parts of northern China, and has been widely introduced and naturalized in temperate regions around the world.
