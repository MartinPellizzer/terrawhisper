Growing, Harvesting and Storage

Elaeagnus angustifolia grows best in well-drained sandy or loamy soils with full sun exposure, though it can tolerate partial shade.

It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and requires moderate watering during its establishment phase, though it is relatively drought-tolerant once mature. Regular pruning is recommended to maintain its shape and promote healthy growth, while mulching can help retain soil moisture and suppress weeds. The plant is typically harvested in late summer to early fall when the leaves and fruits are fully mature, using pruning shears or hand clippers to cut the branches.

For optimal potency, the harvested material should be dried in a well-ventilated area away from direct sunlight, then stored in airtight containers in a cool, dark place to preserve its medicinal properties.
