Saussurea pulchella

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea pulchella?

Saussurea pulchella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is placed within the genus Saussurea, with its specific designation being the species pulchella.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea pulchella has erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, often featuring deeply serrated or lobed margins. Smaller, alternate leaves are distributed along the stem, decreasing in size as they ascend toward the inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads arranged in a compact, terminal corymb-like cluster. These inflorescences are characterized by numerous tubular florets surrounded by inconspicuous bracts.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific alkaloid medicinal compounds pulchellamines A, pulchellamines B, pulchellamines C, pulchellamines D, and pulchellamines E. These bioactive molecules are primary secondary metabolites identified within the plant's chemical profile. Researchers study these specific derivatives to understand their potential pharmacological properties and biological activities. The presence of this distinct group of pulchellamines suggests significant therapeutic interest for future drug development. Detailed chemical analysis is often required to isolate each individual component for medical testing.

As discussed in a study, the methanol extract of the aerial parts of Saussurea pulchella contains two new guaiane sesquiterpene lactones (1 and 2) and seven new amino acid-sesquiterpene lactone conjugates (3-9). These seven new conjugates, known as pulchellamines A, B, C, D, E, F, and G (3-9), were identified alongside six known sesquiterpene lactones (10-15). The plant also contains 8alpha-O-(3'-hydroxy-3'-methylbutyryl)desacylcynaropicrin (1) and 8alpha-O-(2',3'-dihydroxyisobutyryl)11beta,13-dihydrodesacylcynaropicrin (2). Regarding cytotoxicity against human tumor cell lines, compounds 11 and 12 showed activity against SK-MEL-2 and SK-OV-3. Specifically, compounds 11 and 12 exhibited ED50 values of 1.53 and 4.07 microM for SK-MEL-2, and 2.49 and 7.42 microM for SK-OV-3.
