Saussurea pulchella

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea pulchella?

Saussurea pulchella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is placed within the genus Saussurea, with its specific designation being the species pulchella.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea pulchella has erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, often featuring deeply serrated or lobed margins. Smaller, alternate leaves are distributed along the stem, decreasing in size as they ascend toward the inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads arranged in a compact, terminal corymb-like cluster. These inflorescences are characterized by numerous tubular florets surrounded by inconspicuous bracts.

What are the pharmacological activities of Saussurea pulchella?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory and anti-ulcerative colitis properties that assist in managing various gastrointestinal disorders. Researchers have identified that its bioactive compounds work to reduce systemic inflammation and soothe intestinal tissue. These natural constituents help mitigate the damage caused by chronic inflammation within the colon. Furthermore, the plant's ability to protect the mucosal lining offers potential therapeutic benefits for patients suffering from colitis. Such medicinal qualities highlight its significant value in traditional and modern pharmacological studies.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Saussurea pulchella (SP) is a traditional medicinal plant used for its diverse biological activities, particularly anti-inflammatory effects. Qualitative and quantitative analysis of the SP 75% ethanol extract identified 149 compounds and determined 20 quantitatively. In a DSS model of ulcerative colitis mice, SP dose-dependently decreased the DAI score, spleen coefficient, and levels of TNF-α, IL-6, iNOS, MPO, and MDA. The extract also increased colon length, GSH level, and SOD activity while protecting the intestinal barrier. Furthermore, 10 metabolite biomarkers, 18 targets, and 5 metabolisms were found to play crucial roles in the treatment of ulcerative colitis with SP.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", the methanol extract of the aerial parts of Saussurea pulchella contains two new guaiane sesquiterpene lactones labeled 1 and 2, along with seven new amino acid-sesquiterpene lactone conjugates identified as pulchellamines A, B, C, D, E, F, and G (3-9). Additionally, six known sesquiterpene lactones (10-15) were isolated from the plant. The compound 8alpha- O-(3'-hydroxy-3'-methylbutyryl)desacylcynaropicrin is identified as 1, while 8alpha- O-(2', 3'-dihydroxyisobutyryl)11beta,13-dihydrodesacylcynaropicrin is 2. Compounds 11 and 12 showed cytotoxicity against SK-MEL-2 and SK-OV-3 human tumor cell lines with ED 50 values of 1.53 and 4.07 microM, and 2.49 and 7.42 microM, respectively.
