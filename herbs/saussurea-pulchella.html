Saussurea pulchella

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea pulchella?

Saussurea pulchella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it falls into the subclass Magnoliidae and is further categorized under the order Asterales. As a member of the family Asteraceae, it is placed within the genus Saussurea, with its specific designation being the species pulchella.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea pulchella has erect, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, often featuring deeply serrated or lobed margins. Smaller, alternate leaves are distributed along the stem, decreasing in size as they ascend toward the inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads arranged in a compact, terminal corymb-like cluster. These inflorescences are characterized by numerous tubular florets surrounded by inconspicuous bracts.

Its distribution is often limited to specific elevations where mountain meadows provide necessary moisture. What are the pharmacological activities of Saussurea pulchella?

This plant has potent pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory and anti-ulcerative colitis activities. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds work to reduce swelling and inhibit the release of pro-inflammatory mediators in the body. These chemical constituents also demonstrate a significant ability to protect the intestinal lining and mitigate the damage caused by ulcerative colitis. By modulating the immune response, the plant helps to stabilize the gastrointestinal tract and decrease mucosal injury. These therapeutic effects make it a subject of interest for treating various inflammatory conditions.

As discussed in a study, Saussurea pulchella (SP) 75% ethanol extract was analyzed using UPLC-Q/TOF-MS, which qualitatively identified 149 compounds and quantitatively determined 20 compounds. In mice with a dextran sodium sulfate model of ulcerative colitis, SP dose-dependently decreased the DAI score, spleen coefficient, and the levels of TNF-α, IL-6, iNOS, MP0, and MDA. The extract also increased colon length, GSH level, and SOD activity while protecting the intestinal barrier. Through the integration of network pharmacology and metabolomics, 10 metabolite biomarkers, 18 targets, and 5 metabolisms were identified as playing crucial roles in the treatment of ulcerative colitis with SP. Consequently, the SP 75% ethanol extract can be classified as a novel natural treatment for the progression of ulcerative colitis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the specific medicinal alkaloids pulchellamines A, pulchellamines B, pulchellamines C, pulchellamines D, and pulchellamines E. These unique chemical structures are primarily responsible for the biological activity observed in the species. Researchers study these compounds to understand their potential therapeutic applications in modern pharmacology. Each distinct pulchellamine variant may interact differently with biological targets within a living organism. Understanding the precise concentration of these molecules is essential for developing effective medicinal extracts.

As discussed in a study, the methanol extract of the aerial parts of Saussurea pulchella contains 2 new guaiane sesquiterpene lactones (1 and 2) and 7 new amino acid-sesquiterpene lactone conjugates (3-9). These specific conjugates, known as pulchellamines A, B, C, D, E, F, and G (3-9), were isolated along with 6 known sesquiterpene lactones (10-15). Among the isolated compounds, 11 and 12 demonstrated cytotoxic activity against 2 human tumor cell lines. Specifically, compounds 11 and 12 showed cytotoxicity against SK-MEL-2 and SK-OV-3 with ED 50 values of 1.53 and 4.07 microM and 2.49 and 7.42 microM, respectively.
