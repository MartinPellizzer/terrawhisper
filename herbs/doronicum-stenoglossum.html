Doronicum stenoglossum

What's the taxonomical classification of Doronicum stenoglossum?

Doronicum stenoglossum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Asterales and the family Asteraceae, which characterizes it as a member of the sunflower or daisy family. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Doronicum, and its unique species designation, stenoglossum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Doronicum stenoglossum has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette and possess serrated margins.

The stems are erect and can reach moderate heights, supporting a terminal cluster of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. Each flower head consists of several ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller tubular florets. The involucral bracts located beneath the flower head are green and provide structural support to the bloom.

This morphology is characteristic of many members within the Asteraceae family.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Doronicum stenoglossum can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids. These chemical compounds are known to cause damage to the liver upon consumption. Contact with the plant's sap may result in contact dermatitis or allergic skin reactions in sensitive individuals. It is important to avoid direct skin contact and ensure that no part of the plant is consumed by humans or animals.
