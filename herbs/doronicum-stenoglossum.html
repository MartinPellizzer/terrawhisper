Doronicum stenoglossum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Doronicum stenoglossum has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette and possess serrated margins. The stems are erect and can reach moderate heights, supporting a terminal cluster of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. Each flower head consists of several ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller tubular florets. The involucral bracts located beneath the flower head are green and provide structural support to the bloom. This morphology is characteristic of many members within the Asteraceae family.
