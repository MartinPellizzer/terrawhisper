Hygrophila auriculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Hygrophila auriculata?

Hygrophila auriculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Hygrophila and the species auriculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hygrophila auriculata has an erect, herbaceous stem that reaches heights of up to 1 meter, featuring opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically arranged in pairs along the stem, displaying a vibrant green color and a smooth texture. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, presenting a distinct white or pale lavender hue with yellowish markings. These blooms are characterized by a long corolla tube that tapers toward the base. The plant also develops a fibrous root system that helps stabilize it in wetland or moist soil environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, frequently inhabiting wetlands and riparian zones. It is widely distributed across the Indian subcontinent, where it thrives in seasonal ponds and marshes. Populations are also common throughout much of sub-Saharan Africa and various parts of Southeast Asia. Due to its ability to tolerate diverse water conditions, it has become naturalized in many other warm climates globally. This wide range is supported by its preference for disturbed habitats and moist soils.

What are the pharmacological activities of Hygrophila auriculata?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including antimycobacterial, antioxidant, antiproliferative, cytotoxicity, and hepatoprotective properties. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively inhibit the growth of mycobacteria, which is essential for treating infectious diseases. The plant also serves as a potent antioxidant by neutralizing free radicals to prevent oxidative stress within cells. Furthermore, its antiproliferative and cytotoxicity effects suggest a potential role in inhibiting the development and survival of cancer cells. Finally, the extract exhibits hepatoprotective capabilities by shielding liver tissues from chemical or toxin-induced damage.

According to a study published by "International journal of mycobacteriology", Hygrophila auriculata Heine is one of 15 medicinal plant species documented for use against tuberculosis or its signs and symptoms in the Greater Accra and Eastern parts of Ghana. This plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and the whole plant is utilized by Ghanaian communities. The study notes that these collected species could serve as a source for a new class of drugs against TB. Researchers recommend bioactivity guided fractionation to identify lead compounds for antimycobacterial activity. These findings provide a basis for further pharmacological, toxicological, and phytochemical studies to develop new plant-based antimycobacterial drugs.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive medicinal compounds including flavonoids, tannins, and a significant terpenoid fraction. These phytochemicals contribute to the diverse therapeutic properties often attributed to the species in traditional medicine. The presence of flavonoids provides strong antioxidant and anti-inflammatory capabilities to the plant extracts. Additionally, the tannins act as astringent agents that can assist in wound healing and microbial control. Finally, the terpenoid fraction plays a crucial role in the plant's overall pharmacological activity and defense mechanisms.

According to a study published by "Heliyon", Hygrophila auriculata was one of five common vegetables selected for the analysis of nutritional proximate, micronutrients, and antioxidant potential. The results for the collected plants demonstrated that they are rich in carbohydrate, fibre, proteins, moisture, and ash content, though they are low in lipid content. Mineral elements in the samples were high, with remarkable amounts of Na (19.9-21.5 mg/gm), K (7.9-13.5 mg/gm), and P (1.0-1.8 mg/gm). All samples were found to have a considerable amount of flavonoid (90.6-144.5 mg QE/gm) and tannin content (26.8-57.2 mg GAE/gm).

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating various liver diseases and liver ailments by demonstrating significant hepatoprotective properties. Scientific studies have shown its effectiveness in reversing rat liver damage caused by chemical stressors. It specifically helps mitigate the effects of thioacetamide intoxication in experimental models. Additionally, the plant provides protection against cellular injury resulting from paracetamol intoxication. These medicinal qualities make it a subject of interest for managing acute hepatic toxicity.

According to a study published by "Pharmaceutical biology", Hygrophila auriculata is used in the Indian System of Medicine as Rasayana for treating brain and liver diseases. The study specifically evaluated its neuroprotective effect against transient global cerebral ischemia in male Wistar rats using doses of 100 and 200 mg/kg. In the tGCI control group, the beam balance score was 5.1, the number of entries in the open field was 131, and the time spent in rotorod was 47 s. Following TF treatment, the beam balance score significantly decreased to 2.9, entries decreased to 67, and the time spent in rotorod increased to 63.25 s. The plant fraction also showed potential for protecting hippocampal CA1 neurons and restoring antioxidants against oxidative stress.

It is important to seek medical attention if these symptoms become severe or persistent.
