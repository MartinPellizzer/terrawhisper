Hygrophila auriculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Hygrophila auriculata?

Hygrophila auriculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Hygrophila and the species auriculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hygrophila auriculata has an erect, herbaceous stem that reaches heights of up to 1 meter, featuring opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically arranged in pairs along the stem, displaying a vibrant green color and a smooth texture. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, presenting a distinct white or pale lavender hue with yellowish markings. These blooms are characterized by a long corolla tube that tapers toward the base. The plant also develops a fibrous root system that helps stabilize it in wetland or moist soil environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

Populations are also common throughout much of sub-Saharan Africa and various parts of Southeast Asia.
