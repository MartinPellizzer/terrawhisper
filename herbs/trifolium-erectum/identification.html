Botanical Identification of White Clover (Trifolium erectum)

To identify White Clover (Trifolium erectum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

White Clover, Trifolium erectum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Trifolium, and species Trifolium erectum. It is a member of the legume family and is commonly found in temperate regions.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of White Clover is Trifolium erectum, first described by Linnaeus in 1753. It belongs to the genus Trifolium in the legume family. Common names include White Clover, Trefoil, and Cow's Parsley. It is also known as "Little Trefoil" in some regions.

Morphological Characteristics

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) is a low-growing perennial herb with a dense root system that forms a mat. Its stems are erect and branching, typically reaching up to 20 cm in height. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to heart-shaped leaflets. The inflorescence is a dense cluster of small white flowers, each with five petals. The fruit is a small legume pod containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) can be distinguished by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves with a basal leaflet, and small white flowers. It differs from red clover by having white instead of pink or red flowers and from birdsfoot trefoil by its three-leaf structure rather than five. The leaves are often hairy and have a distinct cloverhead shape.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers moist, well-drained soils. It commonly grows at low to mid-elevation areas, often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period when the leaves are fully developed and the flowers are in bloom.

Phylogenetic Relationships

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) belongs to the genus Trifolium within the family Fabaceae. Its closest species include other Trifolium species such as Trifolium pratense and Trifolium hybridum. It shares key family traits like compound leaves and legume fruits, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family.
