White Clover (Trifolium erectum) Uses

White Clover is used to treat respiratory infections, allergic rhinitis, and cold symptoms. It helps alleviate coughs and asthma by reducing inflammation. It also supports skin healing, treating inflammation, infections, rashes, and wounds due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. It may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The plant's compounds can soothe irritated airways and reduce mucus production, making it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for respiratory conditions.

2. Allergic rhinitis

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and inflammation, supporting respiratory comfort. Its mild nature makes it suitable for long-term use in managing seasonal allergies.

3. Cold symptoms

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) is traditionally used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its antimicrobial and expectorant properties. It helps reduce congestion, soothe sore throats, and support immune function. Often prepared as a tea, it is a gentle remedy for mild colds and respiratory discomfort.

4. Cough

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) is used to soothe coughs due to its mild expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce irritation in the respiratory tract. Often prepared as a tea, it is particularly effective for dry, persistent coughs and can be combined with other herbs for enhanced relief.

5. Asthma

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. It contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and ease bronchial constriction, making it a potential supportive herb for asthma management. However, it should be used under professional guidance as part of a comprehensive treatment plan.

6. Skin inflammation

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) has been traditionally used to address skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It contains compounds like flavonoids and tannins that help reduce redness, swelling, and irritation. Topical applications of its infused oils or decoctions can provide relief for conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Its gentle nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin.

7. Wound healing

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) has been traditionally used to support wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote tissue repair. Applications include poultices, salves, or teas to aid in the healing process of minor cuts and skin irritations.

8. Skin infections

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It can help reduce symptoms of conditions like eczema, fungal infections, and minor wounds when applied topically. Its active compounds, including flavonoids and tannins, contribute to its healing effects on the skin.

9. Skin rashes

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) has been traditionally used to treat skin rashes due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It contains compounds that help reduce irritation and redness, making it beneficial for conditions like eczema and contact dermatitis. External applications of its infused oil or poultice can provide relief and promote healing of inflamed skin.

10. Wounds

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) has been traditionally used to support wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote tissue repair. The leaves can be applied topically as a poultice or used in salves to aid in the healing process of minor cuts and abrasions.
