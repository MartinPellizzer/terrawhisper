White Clover (Trifolium erectum) Compounds

White Clover contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and tannins, which act as astringents. Phenolic acids contribute to its antioxidant effects, while coumarin may support digestive health. Glycosides and saponins are also present, offering additional therapeutic benefits.

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in White Clover (Trifolium erectum). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory support. These compounds help modulate cellular processes and may enhance the plant's therapeutic potential.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in White Clover (Trifolium erectum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in herbal medicine for its ability to reduce inflammation and support digestive health. Tannins in White Clover may also aid in wound healing and have antimicrobial effects.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in White Clover (Trifolium erectum). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects. This compound is particularly valued in herbal medicine for its potential to support digestive and respiratory health.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in White Clover (Trifolium erectum). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin may also support digestive health and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming effects.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in White Clover (Trifolium erectum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, particularly in supporting digestive health and reducing inflammation. Glycosides in White Clover are known for their mild tonic effects and potential to soothe gastrointestinal discomfort.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in White Clover (Trifolium erectum). It exhibits mild cleansing and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting digestive health and immune function. Saponins in White Clover may also contribute to its traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in White Clover (Trifolium erectum) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds help reduce swelling and combat infections, making White Clover useful in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. They are part of the plant's broader therapeutic profile in herbal medicine.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of White Clover (Trifolium erectum) contains volatile compounds like linalool and geraniol, which contribute to its aromatic profile. These compounds are valued for their potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making the oil a useful component in herbal formulations.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in White Clover (Trifolium erectum). It contributes to the plant's vibrant blue and purple hues and possesses antioxidant properties. These compounds help protect cells from oxidative stress and may support overall health. Anthocyanin is also associated with anti-inflammatory and cardiovascular benefits.

10. Alkaloid

White Clover (Trifolium erectum) contains the alkaloid trifolium alkaloid, which has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. This compound may contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
