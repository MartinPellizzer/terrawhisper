Red Currant (Ribes rubrum)

Red Currant (Ribes rubrum) is a member of the Grossulariaceae family, native to North America, Europe, and Asia. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and native american herbal medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters.
Leaves
Smooth upper surface with a single prominent stomatal band, lower surface glabrous and lighter in color, leaf type broadly ovate with acute apex and entire margin.
Flowers
Inflorescence composed of solitary flowers, each with five pink to red petals, five stamens, and a superior ovary with two styles, exhibiting actinomorphic symmetry.
Stems
Cylindrical, terete, glabrous, woody, with opposite branching, and bearing adventitious rootlets along the lower portion.
