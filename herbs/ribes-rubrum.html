Red currant

Ribes rubrum

Red Currant (Ribes rubrum) is a small, red berry-producing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.

Botanical Identification

Red Currant (Ribes rubrum) is a species of flowering plant in the family Berberidaceae. It is a perennial shrub native to Europe and western Asia. Known also as red gooseberry, it features red berries, alternate leaves, and yellow flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include "Rubi fructus rubri" and "Ribes rubrum."

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Saxifragales
  • Family: Grossulariaceae
  • Genus: Ribes
  • Species: Ribes rubrum

Active Compounds

Red Currant contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. These compounds work synergistically to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their combined action supports the herb's traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin health.

  • Flavonoid
  • Tannin
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory conditions.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive discomfort and cold symptoms.

Tincture: Used for skin irritations and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Red Currant is generally safe when used in typical culinary amounts. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated in those with known allergies to the Rosaceae family. Always consult a healthcare provider before use, especially for medicinal purposes.
