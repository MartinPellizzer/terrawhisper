Buxus myrica

What's the taxonomical classification of Buxus myrica?

Buxus myrica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. It is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical lineage, it falls under the order Buxales and the family Buxaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Buxus and the specific species name myrica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Buxus myrica has small, elliptical, leathery leaves that are arranged oppositely on woody, evergreen stems. The foliage typically displays a deep green color with a smooth or slightly coriaceous texture. Its flowers are inconspicuous and small, often appearing in clusters within the leaf axils. The plant develops small, globose drupes that change color as they reach maturity. This species maintains a dense, shrubby growth habit characterized by compact branching patterns.
