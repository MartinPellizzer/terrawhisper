What to know about Leonurus sibiricus (motherwort) before using it medicinally

Motherwort, scientifically known as Leonurus sibiricus, is a herb that has been touted for its impressive health benefits, including reducing anxiety and stress levels, alleviating symptoms of menopause, and promoting overall cardiovascular well-being.

In addition to its medicinal properties, motherwort is also used in various culinary applications, such as adding flavor to soups, stews, and salads. Horticulturally, motherwort is a relatively easy-to-grow perennial plant that thrives in temperate climates with full sun and well-drained soil. From a botanical standpoint, motherwort belongs to the Lamiaceae family and features heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers.

With its rich history dating back thousands of years, motherwort has been mentioned in traditional Chinese medicine and is said to have been used by ancient Greeks for its medicinal properties.
