Leonurus sibiricus

What's the taxonomical classification of Leonurus sibiricus?

Leonurus sibiricus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Lamiaceae, where it is represented by the genus Leonurus. Finally, the specific classification is completed by its species name, sibiricus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leonurus sibiricus has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by square stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 80 centimeters. The leaves are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem and possess a serrated margin with an ovate to lanceolate shape. Its small, bilabiate flowers are predominantly light purple or pink and emerge from verticillasters located in the leaf axils. The plant produces small, nut-like fruits that develop after the flowering period concludes. Dense hairs often cover the stems and leaf surfaces, contributing to its tactile texture.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the alkaloid leonurine, the diterpenoid quinones sibiricinones A-E, the phenylethanoid glycoside verbascoside, caffeic acid, and the flavonoid rutin. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile observed in the species. Leonurine is frequently studied for its potential cardiovascular and sedative effects. The presence of sibiricinones A-E provides unique structural properties that distinguish this plant from other members of the Lamiaceae family. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of the plant are largely driven by the synergistic action of caffeic acid, rutin, and verbascoside.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Leonurus sibiricus targets various neuroreceptors and enzyme systems, most notably acting as a modulator of GABAergic signaling and inhibiting acetylcholinesterase activity. Its bioactive alkaloids and flavonoids interact with gamma-aminobutyric acid receptors to produce sedative and anxiolytic effects within the central nervous system. Additionally, certain constituents demonstrate an ability to influence calcium channels, which contributes to its traditional use in regulating cardiovascular function. The plant also shows potential for interacting with inflammatory mediators, such as cyclooxygenase enzymes, to mitigate oxidative stress. These diverse molecular interactions collectively underpin its pharmacological profile in treating neurological and circulatory ailments.

Leonurus sibiricus has therapeutic properties as a bitter, nervine, emmenagogue, diaporetic, and antispasmodic agent used to treat various physiological imbalances.
