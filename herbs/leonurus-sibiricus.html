Leonurus Sibiricus (Leonurus sibiricus): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

Leonurus Sibiricus (Leonurus sibiricus) is a flowering plant native to Asia and Europe, commonly known as Siberian Motherwort, which has been traditionally used for its calming and circulatory benefits, particularly in improving conditions such as anxiety, insomnia, and cardiovascular health.

What is Leonurus Sibiricus (Leonurus Sibiricus)?

Leonurus sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian motherwort, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia, characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small pink or purple flowers.

Leonurus sibiricus is a perennial herb with a creeping rhizome, erect stems that are usually branched, and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, bearing small, pale blue or pink flowers in whorls along the stem.

Leonurus sibiricus is native to temperate regions of Asia, particularly widespread across eastern Europe and central Asia, including countries such as Russia, Mongolia, and China, with a historical presence in parts of northern Africa and the Middle East.
