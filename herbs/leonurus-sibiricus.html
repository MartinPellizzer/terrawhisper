Leonurus sibiricus

What's the taxonomical classification of Leonurus sibiricus?

Leonurus sibiricus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Lamiales. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the family Lamiaceae, where it is represented by the genus Leonurus. Finally, the specific classification is completed by its species name, sibiricus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leonurus sibiricus has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by square stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 80 centimeters. The leaves are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem and possess a serrated margin with an ovate to lanceolate shape. Its small, bilabiate flowers are predominantly light purple or pink and emerge from verticillasters located in the leaf axils. The plant produces small, nut-like fruits that develop after the flowering period concludes. Dense hairs often cover the stems and leaf surfaces, contributing to its tactile texture.

What are the pharmacological activities of Leonurus sibiricus?

This plant has anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antiproliferative, and antitumor pharmacological activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize harmful free radicals that cause cellular damage. The plant also demonstrates significant anti-inflammatory effects by modulating various inflammatory pathways in the body. Furthermore, its antiproliferative actions contribute to its potent antitumor potential by inhibiting the uncontrolled growth of abnormal cells. These combined biological functions make Leonurus sibiricus a subject of interest for various therapeutic applications.

As discussed in a study, Leonurus sibiricus L. shows several pharmacological effects, most notably its anti-cancer activity. Both transformed root and non-transformed root extracts of the plant demonstrate cytotoxic activity in glioma primary cells. Treatment with these extracts results in significant cell growth inhibition and S- and G2/M-phase cell cycle arrest. The extracts induce apoptosis in a dose-dependent fashion by causing about a 4-fold increase in the Bax/Bcl-2 ratio and a 5-fold increase in p53 activation. These findings indicate that the root extracts exhibit anti-cancer activity through the regulation of genes involved in apoptosis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds catechin, chlorogenic acid, ferulic acid, p-coumaric acid, and quercetin. These specific polyphenols and organic acids contribute to the biological activity and therapeutic potential of the species. Each compound plays a distinct role in providing antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties within the body. Scientific research often focuses on these substances to understand how they mitigate oxidative stress at a cellular level. The presence of this diverse chemical profile makes the plant a significant subject for pharmacological study.

As discussed in a study, Leonurus sibiricus contains nine phenolic compounds identified through LC-MS/MS and HPLC analyses. These include catechin, verbascoaside, 2 flavonoids consisting of quercetin and rutin, and 5 phenolic acids which are 4-hydroxybenzoic acid, chlorogenic acid, caffeic acid, p-coumaric acid, and ferulic acid. Preincubation of CHO cells with 0.5 mg/mL of these plant extracts resulted in increased expression levels of antioxidant genes SOD2, CAT, and GPx. The extracts demonstrate antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant activity through the reduction of intracellular reactive oxygen species. The highest antioxidant properties are found in the roots and aerial parts of the plant.
