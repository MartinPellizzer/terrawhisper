Side Effects of leonurus sibiricus

Leonurus sibiricus induces nausea, which can manifest as a persistent feeling of queasiness or an urge to vomit, particularly when consumed in higher doses or by individuals with heightened sensitivity.

This herb may also lead to headaches, possibly due to its effects on blood pressure or neurotransmitter activity in the brain. In some cases, it can cause diarrhea, characterized by frequent, loose stools, as it may irritate the gastrointestinal tract. Additionally, Leonurus sibiricus can cause dry mouth, a common side effect of its stimulating properties, and may lead to stomach pain due to increased digestive activity or irritation.

Skin rashes, anxiety, and drowsiness are also reported side effects, suggesting that the herb can have variable and potentially adverse effects on different individuals, emphasizing the importance of consulting a healthcare professional before use.
