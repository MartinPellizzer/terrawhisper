Populus davidiana

What's the taxonomical classification of Populus davidiana?

Populus davidiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Salicaceae, falling under the genus Populus, and is specifically identified by its species name, davidiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Populus davidiana has a woody, deciduous habit characterized by elongated, slender branches and ovate to lanceolate leaves with finely serrated margins. The bark typically exhibits a grayish to light brown coloration with shallow longitudinal fissures as the specimen matures. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and feature distinct petioles that allow for movement in the wind. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in catkins, which are produced in clusters during the reproductive season. The overall architecture of the plant reflects its adaptation to riparian environments through its flexible stem structure.

What are the pharmacological activities of Populus davidiana?

This plant has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial pharmacological activities that stem from its rich concentration of bioactive phytochemicals. These properties are primarily attributed to the presence of phenolic compounds and flavonoids found within its bark and leaves. The antioxidant capacity helps neutralize free radicals, thereby reducing oxidative stress in biological systems. Additionally, its ability to inhibit microbial growth makes it a subject of interest for potential therapeutic applications. Such diverse medicinal qualities suggest significant potential for developing new pharmaceutical agents derived from its natural extracts.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", a phytochemical study on the EtOAc-soluble fraction of the stem bark of Populus davidiana resulted in the isolation of 10 phenolic glycosides (1-10). Among these isolated compounds, three new compounds known as populosides A-C (1-3) were determined to be 2-coumaroylmethyl-4-hydroxyphenyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside, 2-coumaroylmethylphenyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside, and 2-feruoylmethylphenyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside. The compounds 1-10 were tested for their radical scavenging activity against an azo radical, ABTS+. Antioxidant activity in this assay was exhibited by populosides A-C (1-3), populoside (4), grandidentatin (8), salireposide (9), and coumaroyl-beta-D-glucoside (10).
