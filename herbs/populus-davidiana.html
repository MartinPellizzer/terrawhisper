Populus davidiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Populus davidiana has a woody, deciduous habit characterized by elongated, slender branches and ovate to lanceolate leaves with finely serrated margins. The bark typically exhibits a grayish to light brown coloration with shallow longitudinal fissures as the specimen matures. Its leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and feature distinct petioles that allow for movement in the wind. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in catkins, which are produced in clusters during the reproductive season. The overall architecture of the plant reflects its adaptation to riparian environments through its flexible stem structure.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous and hilly regions of central and southern China, specifically spanning provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, and Hunan. It typically occupies temperate forest environments ranging from low altitudes to mid-elevation slopes. Within these habitats, the species thrives in well-drained soils along riverbanks and moist valley bottoms. Its distribution is often closely linked to the availability of riparian corridors and shaded woodland areas. These specific ecological niches allow the species to maintain localized populations across its regional range.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Populus davidiana contains salicin within its bark and leaves, which is utilized for its analgesic and antipyretic properties. The bark is most frequently harvested to create decoctions used to treat inflammation, fever, and various types of pain. Some traditional applications also involve using the leaf extracts to soothe digestive ailments or skin irritations. These medicinal components act as precursors to salicylic acid, providing a natural method for managing discomfort. Practitioners often prepare these specific parts through boiling or soaking to extract the active chemical compounds.

Populus davidiana has therapeutic properties characterized by its ability to act as an astringent, anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic agent.       What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if the sap or bark comes into direct contact with sensitive human skin. While specific clinical studies on Populus davidiana are limited, members of the Populus genus often contain salicin, which may trigger reactions in individuals sensitive to aspirin. Potential users should be cautious of localized redness, itching, or swelling at the site of contact. Allergic reactions could also manifest as respiratory irritation if pollen from the plant is inhaled in high concentrations. Always conduct a small patch test before assuming any part of the plant is safe for topical application.
