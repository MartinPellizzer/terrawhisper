What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa candicans?

Callicarpa candicans belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized into the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the genus Callicarpa, ultimately identifying it as the specific species Callicarpa candicans.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa candicans has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and possess finely serrated margins and acute tips.

The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface with a paler underside, often displaying a slightly pubescent texture. Its woody stems are multi-branched and can grow into a large shrub or small tree reaching several meters in height. During the flowering season, small, fragrant, bell-shaped purple flowers emerge in dense axillary clusters.

These flowers eventually give way to distinctive, spherical clusters of bright metallic purple drupes that persist on the branches.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Callicarpa candicans can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals when their skin comes into direct contact with the plant's foliage or berries. Some people might experience localized redness or a mild rash if they handle the leaves without protection. There is limited clinical research regarding its long-term systemic effects, so cautious use is advised for those with known plant allergies.
