Callicarpa candicans

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa candicans has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and possess finely serrated margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface with a paler underside, often displaying a slightly pubescent texture. Its woody stems are multi-branched and can grow into a large shrub or small tree reaching several meters in height. During the flowering season, small, fragrant, bell-shaped purple flowers emerge in dense axillary clusters. These flowers eventually give way to distinctive, spherical clusters of bright metallic purple drupes that persist on the branches.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the southeastern United States, ranging from eastern Texas through the Gulf Coast states to Florida and northward into the Carolinas. It thrives primarily in the moist, acidic soils of subtropical forests and woodland edges. Populations are commonly found in states such as Georgia, Alabama, and Mississippi where humidity is high.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Callicarpa candicans can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals when their skin comes into direct contact with the plant's foliage or berries. There is limited clinical research regarding its long-term systemic effects, so cautious use is advised for those with known plant allergies.
