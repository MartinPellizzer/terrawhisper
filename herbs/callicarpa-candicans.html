What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa candicans has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length and possess finely serrated margins and acute tips. The foliage is typically dark green on the upper surface with a paler underside, often displaying a slightly pubescent texture. Its woody stems are multi-branched and can grow into a large shrub or small tree reaching several meters in height. During the flowering season, small, fragrant, bell-shaped purple flowers emerge in dense axillary clusters. These flowers eventually give way to distinctive, spherical clusters of bright metallic purple drupes that persist on the branches.
