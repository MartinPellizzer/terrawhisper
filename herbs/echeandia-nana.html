What is Echeandia nana?

Echeandia nana is a species of flowering plant in the family Asparagaceae.

It is an evergreen perennial with linear-lanceolate leaves, 3-6 cm long, and 0.8-1.2 cm wide, having a glabrous surface and a subapical acute apex. The plant produces a terminal inflorescence, 10-20 cm long, with a cylindrical spike of 2-5 cm long, bearing small white flowers. Each flower is 1-1.5 cm long, with three outer and inner tepals, the outer tepals being larger and elliptical in shape.

The plant grows in high-altitude subtropical regions, typically in humid shaded areas.
