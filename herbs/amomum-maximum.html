What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Amomaxins A, Amomaxins B, and isocoronaarin D. These specific bioactive molecules are primarily isolated from the chemical profile of the plant to study their therapeutic potential. Researchers often investigate these compounds for their unique pharmacological properties in various biological models. The presence of these substances contributes significantly to the plant's overall chemical complexity. Understanding these constituents helps scientists unlock new possibilities for drug development.

As discussed in a study, Amomum maximum contains two unprecedented rearranged labdane norditerpenoids known as Amomaxins A (1) and B (2). These compounds feature an unprecedented rearranged labdane norditerpene skeleton with a nine-membered ring. Amomaxins A (1) and B (2) were isolated from the roots of Amomum maximum along with the biosynthetic related known compound isocoronaarin D (3). Specifically, compound 2 showed an inhibitory effect on nitric oxide (NO) production in lipopolysaccharide-induced RAW264.7 macrophages.
