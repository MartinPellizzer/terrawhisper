Amomum maximum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Zingiberales and is a member of the family Zingiberaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Amomum and the species maximum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds amomaxins A, amomaxins B, and isocoronaarin D. These specific bioactive molecules are categorized as sesquiterpene lactones which are responsible for many of the plant's biological activities. Amomaxins A and B are particularly noted in scientific literature for their potential anti-tumor and anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, isocoronaarin D contributes to the complex chemical profile that defines the therapeutic value of Amomum maximum. Research continues to investigate how these individual constituents interact to produce medicinal effects.

According to a study published by "Organic letters", Amomum maximum contains Amomaxins A (1) and B (2), which are two unprecedented rearranged labdane norditerpenoids featuring a nine-membered ring. These compounds, along with the biosynthetic related known compound isocoronarin D (3), were isolated from the roots of Amomum maximum. The structures and absolute configurations of these compounds were determined using spectroscopic data, CD experimentation, and single-crystal X-ray diffraction. Furthermore, compound 2 showed an inhibitory effect on nitric oxide (NO) production in lipopolysaccharide-induced RAW264.7 macrophages.
