Amomum maximum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Amomum maximum has large, oblong to elliptical leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, paired with thick, fleshy rhizomes and terminal inflorescences bearing dense spikes of creamy white flowers. The plant's foliage is characterized by a deep green color and prominent parallel venation typical of the Zingiberaceae family. Its stems are pseudostems formed by tightly overlapping leaf sheaths, which provide structural support for the upright growth. The flowering spikes emerge from the center of the leafy clumps, often displaying small, fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators. This perennial herb reaches significant heights, creating a lush, tropical appearance in its natural habitat.
