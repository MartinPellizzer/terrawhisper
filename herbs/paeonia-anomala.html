What's the taxonomical classification of Paeonia anomala?

Paeonia anomala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Saxifragales. Specifically, this plant is a member of the family Paeoniaceae and falls under the genus Paeonia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species anomala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paeonia anomala has a robust, tuberous root system and deeply divided, pinnatifid leaves that are typically dark green and glabrous. The plant grows from a thick woody caudex that allows it to persist in rocky or mountainous habitats. Its large, solitary flowers feature numerous bright red or deep pink petals surrounding a dense center of golden stamens. The foliage is characterized by a complex, dissected structure that reduces surface area for water conservation. Following pollination, the plant produces large, follicle-like seed capsules that split open to reveal dark, shiny seeds.

What are the pharmacological activities of Paeonia anomala?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including anti-cancer, anticoagulant, and antiproliferative properties. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively inhibit the rapid growth of malignant cells through antiproliferative mechanisms. These specific phytochemicals also demonstrate potential in managing tumor progression by disrupting cancer cell cycles. Furthermore, the plant exhibits anticoagulant effects that may assist in regulating blood clotting processes. These diverse medicinal qualities make Paeonia anomala a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Medicinal chemistry (Shariqah (United Arab Emirates))", the ethanol extract from Paeonia anomala L. leaf showed the highest total phenolics content. The Paeonia anomala L. seed wallet demonstrated the most potent antiproliferative effects against the human liver cancer HepG2 cell line. From the Paeonia anomala L. seed wallet extract, the Gnetin-H compound was isolated and its molecular structure was determined by 1H and 13C NMR spectrum and IR spectroscopy methods. The major components found in the extract from the Paeonia anomala L. seed wallet were Gnetin-H, methyl gallate, and ethylgallate. This plant was among 15 Mongolian medicinal plants and 21 extracts screened for anti-oxidative effects.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 4-O-methyl-paeoniflorin and heparin-like fragments. These bioactive substances are primary components studied for their potential therapeutic properties in human health. The 4-O-methyl-paeoniflorin acts as a significant monoterpene glycoside within the species. Additionally, the presence of heparin-like fragments suggests that the plant may possess anticoagulant activities. Researchers continue to investigate how these specific chemical structures contribute to the plant's overall medicinal value.

According to a study published by "Izvestiia Akademii nauk. Seriia biologicheskaia", extracts prepared from the bark and wood parts of the Paeonia anomala root exhibit anticoagulant activity. This activity is attributed to the presence of heparin-like fragments within the preparations. Research on the functional state of the mast cell population and biochemical analysis supports this finding. Preparations derived from the Paeonia anomala root bark maintain their anticoagulant properties in the circulatory system for 1.5 h after intravenous administration. This duration of effect is similar to that of animal-derived heparin.
