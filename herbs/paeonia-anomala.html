Anomalous Peony

Paeonia anomala

Anomalous Peony (Paeonia anomala) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, known for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal properties. It is primarily used to improve symptoms of respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma, owing to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects.

Botanical Identification

Anomalous Peony (Paeonia anomala) is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Paeoniaceae and is also known as the "wild peony" in some regions. The plant features rhizomatous roots, palmate leaves, and large, showy flowers. It has been referenced in traditional herbal texts under various synonyms, though its scientific name is widely accepted in modern botany.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Paeoniales
  • Family: Paeoniaceae
  • Genus: Paeonia
  • Species: Paeonia anomala

Anomalous Peony is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. Consult a healthcare professional before use, especially if you have existing health conditions or are taking other medications.
