Paeonia anomala

What's the taxonomical classification of Paeonia anomala?

Paeonia anomala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Saxifragales. Specifically, this plant is a member of the family Paeoniaceae and falls under the genus Paeonia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species anomala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paeonia anomala has a robust, tuberous root system and deeply divided, pinnatifid leaves that are typically dark green and glabrous. The plant grows from a thick woody caudex that allows it to persist in rocky or mountainous habitats. Its large, solitary flowers feature numerous bright red or deep pink petals surrounding a dense center of golden stamens. The foliage is characterized by a complex, dissected structure that reduces surface area for water conservation. Following pollination, the plant produces large, follicle-like seed capsules that split open to reveal dark, shiny seeds.

As discussed in a study, ethanol extracts from the Paeonio anomala L. leaf showed the highest total phenolics content. The Paeonio anomala L. seed wallet showed the most potent antiproliferative effects against the human liver cancer HepG2 cell line. This seed wallet also mentioned the best anti-cancer effects among the extracts studied. The Gnetin-H compound was isolated from the Paeonio anomala L. seed wallet extract, where it was one of the major components alongside methyl gallate and ethylgallate. The molecular structure of this isolated Gnetin-H was determined using 1H and 13C NMR spectrum and IR spectroscopy methods.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains medicinal compounds including 4-O-methyl-paeoniflorin and various heparin-like fragments. These bioactive constituents contribute to the plant's diverse pharmacological profile. Specifically, 4-O-methyl-paeoniflorin is recognized for its significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The presence of heparin-like fragments suggests potential applications in anticoagulation and cardiovascular health. Researchers study these substances to better understand their therapeutic potential in treating systemic diseases.

As discussed in a study, the identification of 35 monoterpenoids in the LC-MS profiles of Paeonia species was enabled by the elucidation of MS/MS fragmentation patterns. Within the profiling analysis of 15 main monoterpenoids in 56 samples, 4-O-methyl-paeoniflorin was only detected in P. veiitchii and P. anomala. The total contents of the 15 monoterpenoids were found to be obviously lower in the roots of P. anomala compared to the roots of P. lactiflora and P. veiitchii. Principal component analysis based on the quantitative results allowed the samples to be classified into their respective groups, including the group for P. anomala.
