Paeonia anomala

What's the taxonomical classification of Paeonia anomala?

Paeonia anomala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it within the order Saxifragales. Specifically, this plant is a member of the family Paeoniaceae and falls under the genus Paeonia, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species anomala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paeonia anomala has a robust, tuberous root system and deeply divided, pinnatifid leaves that are typically dark green and glabrous. The plant grows from a thick woody caudex that allows it to persist in rocky or mountainous habitats. Its large, solitary flowers feature numerous bright red or deep pink petals surrounding a dense center of golden stamens. The foliage is characterized by a complex, dissected structure that reduces surface area for water conservation. Following pollination, the plant produces large, follicle-like seed capsules that split open to reveal dark, shiny seeds.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several significant medicinal compounds, most notably 4-O-methyl-paeoniflorin and various heparin-like fragments. The presence of 4-O-methyl-paeoniflorin is particularly important due to its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Additionally, the heparin-like fragments found within the plant tissue contribute to its unique pharmacological profile. These bioactive constituents work through different biological pathways to interact with human cellular processes. Researchers study these specific molecules to better understand how the plant can be utilized in modern therapeutic applications.

As discussed in a study, the identification of 35 monoterpenoids in the LC-MS profiles of Paeonia species provided clues regarding their chemical characteristics. Within these profiles, 4-O-methyl-paeoniflorin was specifically detected in Paeonia anomala. The study conducted a quantification of 15 main monoterpenoids across 56 samples to understand their distribution. It was observed that the total contents of these 15 monoterpenoids were obviously higher in the roots of P. lactiflora and P. veiitchii than in those of P. anomala. Principal component analysis allowed for the classification of the Paeonia anomala group based on these quantitative results.
