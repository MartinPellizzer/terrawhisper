Anomalous Peony

Paeonia anomala

Anomalous Peony (Paeonia anomala) is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, known for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal properties. It is primarily used to improve symptoms of respiratory conditions such as bronchitis and asthma, owing to its anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects.

The Anomalous Peony (Paeonia anomala) is a perennial herb characterized by its thick, rhizomatous root system, palmately compound leaves with 5-7 leaflets, and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

The Anomalous Peony (Paeonia anomala) is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China, specifically found in provinces such as Sichuan, Hubei, and Shaanxi, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
