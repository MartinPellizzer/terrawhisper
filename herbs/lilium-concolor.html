Lilium concolor

What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium concolor?

Lilium concolor belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the hierarchy of biological classification, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. This plant is further organized under the order Liliales and is a prominent member of the family Liliaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Lilium, with the unique species designation being concolor.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium concolor has upright, lanceolate leaves that grow in whorls or alternate patterns along a sturdy, erect stem reaching heights of up to one meter. The flowers are large, trumpet-shaped, and characterized by their striking bicolor appearance, featuring deep crimson or reddish petals with pale white or cream interiors. Each tepal often displays dark spots or speckles near the throat, adding intricate texture to the bloom. The plant produces large, globular bulbs that serve as its primary energy storage organ. These lilies typically bloom in mid-to-late summer, showcasing their vibrant colors to pollinators.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?
