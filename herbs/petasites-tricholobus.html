Petasites tricholobus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Petasiites tricholobus has large, cordate to reniform leaves characterized by deeply three-lobed margins and a densely pubescent surface. The leaf blades are expansive and possess prominent venation that radiates from the base toward the lobed edges. Long, sturdy petioles support these wide leaves, often extending from a basal rosette structure. The texture of the foliage is notably hairy, which provides a distinct tactile quality to the plant's morphology. These morphological traits allow the plant to effectively capture sunlight within its specific ecological niche.
