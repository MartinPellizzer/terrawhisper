    As discussed in a study, the free phenolic extract from Sphallerocarpus gracilis seeds contains predominant compounds such as chlorogenic acid, di-caffeoylquinic acid glucoside, and luteolin-7-O-glucoside. This extract exhibited significant protective activity against both ROO and OH radical-induced DNA damage, with more potent inhibitory activity against OH radical-induced damage than ROO radicals.
