What's the taxonomical classification of Allium senescens?

Allium seneceens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement within the genus Allium, with its unique designation being the species seneceens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium senescens has a bulbous root system, linear leaves that are typically basal, and a globose to hemispherical umbel composed of numerous small white or pale lilac flowers. The individual florets are characterized by six tepals and prominent stamens that emerge from the center of the bloom. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, often arching gracefully toward the ground as the plant matures. The flowering stalks, or scapes, are slender and erect, lifting the dense flower clusters well above the leaf base. This morphology allows the plant to transition efficiently from its vegetative state to its reproductive phase during the growing season.
