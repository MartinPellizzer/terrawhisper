What's the taxonomical classification of Adenophora himalayana?

Adenophora himalayana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. Its taxonomic journey continues through the family Campanulaceae, leading to its placement in the genus Adenophora. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species name, himalayana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenophora himalayaana has erect, perennial stems that reach heights of 10 to 50 centimeters and bear oppositely arranged, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils in terminal clusters, displaying colors that range from pale purple to deep violet. These blossoms feature a five-lobed corolla and are supported by slender pedicels. The plant eventually produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous tiny seeds for dispersal.
