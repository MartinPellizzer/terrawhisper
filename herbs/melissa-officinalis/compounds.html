Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis) Compounds

The primary compounds of Lemon Balm include essential oil, which contains monoterpenes like linalool. Flavonoids, such as apigenin, contribute to its antioxidant properties. Coumarin has mild sedative effects, while phenolic acids offer anti-inflammatory benefits. These compounds work together to support the plant's therapeutic uses in herbal medicine.

Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis) contains compounds like linalool and menthone. These contribute to its calming and antispasmodic properties. It is commonly used in herbal medicine for anxiety and digestive issues. The oil is extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and flowers.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis) contribute to its calming effects. These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. They support the plant's traditional use in reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation. Flavonoids also enhance the overall therapeutic profile of Lemon Balm.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis). It contributes to the plant's calming effects and is known for its mild sedative properties. Coumarin also has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits, supporting its use in herbal medicine for stress relief and digestive health.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis). It contributes to the plant's calming effects and is believed to support nervous system function. This compound is often found in high concentrations in the leaves, which are traditionally used in herbal remedies. Its antioxidant properties may also help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis). They contribute to its calming effects and are responsible for its characteristic aroma. These compounds have been studied for their potential role in reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis). It contributes to the herb's calming effects by influencing the central nervous system. Glycosides in Lemon Balm are known to reduce anxiety and promote relaxation. They are often responsible for the plant's sedative and antispasmodic properties.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a compound found in Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis) that contributes to its astringent properties. It helps reduce inflammation and can soothe mucous membranes, supporting its traditional use in calming digestive and respiratory issues. Tannins also aid in preserving the plant's active components during preparation.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative effects and may support digestive health. Saponins also have antimicrobial properties, enhancing the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports the plant's traditional use in promoting calmness and supporting cognitive function.

10. Alkaloid

Lemon Balm (Melissa officinalis) contains the alkaloid melissa. This compound contributes to the plant's calming effects, supporting its use in reducing anxiety and promoting relaxation. It works synergistically with other active components in the herb to enhance its therapeutic properties.
