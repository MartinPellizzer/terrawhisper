10 Health Benefits Of Lemon Balm

1. Reduces anxiety and stress

Lemon balm reduces anxiety and stress because of its calming properties.

It contains rosmarinic acid and eugenol, which are known to have anxiolytic effects. Additionally, lemon balm has been found to increase the activity of the neurotransmitter gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), which helps regulate anxiety in the brain.

Furthermore, studies have shown that lemon balm can reduce cortisol levels, a hormone released in response to stress. The soothing effects of lemon balm can also improve sleep quality, which can help alleviate anxiety and stress.

Overall, lemon balm's ability to modulate neurotransmitters, reduce cortisol levels, and promote relaxation make it a valuable herb for managing anxiety and stress.

2. Improves sleep quality

Lemon balm improves sleep quality because of its relaxing and calming properties.

It contains rosmarinic acid and citronellal, which have been found to have sedative effects. These compounds help to reduce anxiety and promote sleep.

Moreover, lemon balm has been shown to increase the levels of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) in the brain, a neurotransmitter that inhibits nerve transmission, thereby producing a calming effect. Additionally, it helps to regulate the body's circadian rhythm, promoting restful sleep.

Drinking lemon balm tea or taking it as a supplement can help improve sleep quality and reduce sleep disturbances, making it an ideal natural remedy for insomnia and other sleep disorders.

3. Alleviates symptoms of indigestion

Lemon balm alleviates symptoms of indigestion because it promotes the production of bile in the liver, which aids in the digestion of fats.

Additionally, it has antispasmodic properties that help to relax the gastrointestinal muscles, reducing discomfort and cramping. Lemon balm also contains rosmarinic acid, which has anti-inflammatory effects and can help to reduce inflammation in the digestive tract. Furthermore, it has been shown to have calming effects on the nervous system, which can help to reduce stress-related indigestion.

All of these factors make lemon balm a valuable herb for promoting healthy digestion and alleviating symptoms of indigestion.

4. Possesses antiviral properties

Lemon balm possesses antiviral properties due to the presence of certain compounds that inhibit viral replication.

These compounds include rosmarinic acid, caffeic acid, and phenolic acids, which have been shown to inhibit the replication of various viruses, such as the herpes simplex virus (HSV) and the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV). Additionally, lemon balm contains flavonoids and terpenes, which have been found to have antiviral effects against respiratory viruses, such as influenza and rhinovirus.

Furthermore, research suggests that lemon balm may enhance the immune system's response to viral infections, providing additional protection against viral pathogens.

5. Eases menstrual cramps

Lemon balm eases menstrual cramps because of its natural anti-spasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.

These properties help relax the smooth muscles of the uterus, reducing the intensity and frequency of menstrual cramps. Additionally, lemon balm contains rosmarinic acid, which has been shown to inhibit the production of prostaglandins, hormone-like substances that can cause inflammation and pain during menstruation. Furthermore, lemon balm has a calming effect on the nervous system, which can help alleviate the emotional and physical symptoms associated with menstruation.

Drinking lemon balm tea or taking it as a supplement may provide relief for women experiencing menstrual discomfort.

6. Boosts cognitive function

Lemon balm boosts cognitive function because it contains rosmarinic acid and antioxidants that protect the brain from damage.

It is believed to enhance memory and concentration by positively affecting the neurotransmitters in the brain, particularly acetylcholine. Lemon balm has been shown to reduce stress and anxiety, which can improve overall cognitive performance. Additionally, it may have potential benefits in protecting against age-related cognitive decline and neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's.

However, more research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms and extent of its cognitive-enhancing effects.

7. Helps lower cholesterol levels

Lemon balm helps lower cholesterol levels because it contains a compound called rosmarinic acid.

This acid has been shown to inhibit the absorption of cholesterol in the gut, thereby reducing the amount of cholesterol that enters the bloodstream. Additionally, lemon balm has been found to increase the activity of enzymes that break down cholesterol in the body. Furthermore, it has anti-inflammatory properties that can help protect the cardiovascular system from damage caused by high cholesterol levels.

Overall, lemon balm's cholesterol-lowering effects make it a promising natural remedy for maintaining heart health.

8. Soothes cold sore outbreaks

Lemon balm soothes cold sore outbreaks because of its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties.

It contains active compounds like rosmarinic acid and flavonoids that help inhibit the virus responsible for cold sores, called herpes simplex virus (HSV-1). Lemon balm works by blocking the ability of the virus to replicate, thus reducing the severity and duration of cold sore outbreaks. Moreover, its anti-inflammatory effects help alleviate pain, swelling, and redness associated with cold sores.

Applying lemon balm extract or ointment directly to the affected area can provide relief and promote faster healing.

9. Possesses antioxidant properties

Lemon balm possesses antioxidant properties because it contains several potent antioxidant compounds, including rosmarinic acid, caffeic acid, and luteolin.

These compounds work together to scavenge free radicals, unstable molecules that can cause cellular damage and contribute to the development of chronic diseases. Additionally, lemon balm has been shown to boost the activity of antioxidant enzymes in the body, such as superoxide dismutase and glutathione peroxidase. This dual action of scavenging free radicals and enhancing antioxidant enzymes makes lemon balm a valuable herb for promoting overall health and preventing oxidative stress-related diseases.

Furthermore, the antioxidant properties of lemon balm may also contribute to its potential benefits for skin health, cognitive function, and anxiety reduction.

10. Relieves headaches

Lemon balm relieves headaches because of its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The rosmarinic acid present in lemon balm reduces inflammation and swelling in the blood vessels, thereby alleviating headache pain. Additionally, the essential oils in lemon balm, such as citronellal and geraniol, have a relaxing effect on the nervous system, helping to reduce tension and ease headaches caused by stress or anxiety. Furthermore, lemon balm has been shown to have a calming effect on the mind, which can help to reduce the frequency and intensity of headaches caused by mental tension.

Drinking lemon balm tea or applying lemon balm oil to the temples and forehead can provide relief from headache symptoms.
