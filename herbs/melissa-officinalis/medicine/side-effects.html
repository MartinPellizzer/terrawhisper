10 Possible Side Effects Of Melissa Officinalis (Lemon Balm)

Melissa officinalis has some side effects when used improperly, such as dizziness, headaches, and stomach upset.

These side effects are often caused by consuming large amounts or using it in combination with other medications.

In severe cases, excessive use can lead to an increased heart rate, anxiety, and difficulty concentrating, which can worsen one's ability to work or interact with others, ultimately affecting daily life and productivity.

1. Increases risk of toxicity

Melissa officinalis causes allergic reactions.

The herb contains a compound called rosmarinic acid, which can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and inflammation.

Additionally, the essential oil present in Melissa officinalis may cause respiratory issues like congestion and difficulty breathing in people with sensitivities.

This sedative property is often attributed to the herb's traditional use in promoting sleep and reducing anxiety.
