Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum) Compounds

The primary compounds of Pyrethrum include essential oil, which contains pyrethrins and other volatile components responsible for its insecticidal properties. Flavonoids and coumarin contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Terpenoids and phenolic acids provide additional antimicrobial and antifungal benefits. Tannins, saponins, glycosides, alkaloids, and anthocyanins further enhance its therapeutic properties in herbal medicine.

Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum) contains potent insecticidal compounds like pyrethrins and piperidine derivatives. These compounds act on the nervous system of insects, causing paralysis and death. The oil is used in herbal medicine for its natural pest-repelling properties and as a safe alternative to synthetic insecticides.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a class of compounds found in Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related ailments. These compounds work synergistically with other active ingredients in the plant to enhance therapeutic effects.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also exhibits mild sedative effects and is used in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and nervous system support.

4. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are key active compounds in Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum), contributing to its insecticidal properties. These compounds, including pyrethrins and related derivatives, are responsible for the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine for pest control. They act as natural pesticides by targeting the nervous systems of insects.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum). It contributes to the plant's insecticidal properties by disrupting the nervous systems of pests. This compound is part of the broader group of bioactive substances responsible for the plant's therapeutic and pesticidal effects.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may support wound healing and digestive health. Tannins in Pyrethrum also enhance the plant's overall therapeutic effects in herbal medicine.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum). It contributes to the plant's natural insecticidal properties by disrupting the cell membranes of pests. Saponin also supports the plant's role in traditional herbal medicine for its cleansing and anti-inflammatory effects.

8. Glycoside

Glycoside is an active compound found in Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum). It contributes to the plant's insecticidal properties by disrupting the nervous system of pests. This compound is commonly used in herbal medicine for its natural pest control benefits.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, though pyrethrum is more renowned for its natural pesticides rather than alkaloids. Alkaloids in this plant may support its traditional use in herbal medicine for their potential therapeutic effects.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Pyrethrum (Anacyclus pyrethrum). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower color and may possess antioxidant properties. While not the primary active compound in Pyrethrum, anthocyanin supports the plant's overall medicinal profile by enhancing its therapeutic potential.
