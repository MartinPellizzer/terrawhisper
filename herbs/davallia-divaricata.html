Davallia divaricata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Davalliia divaricata has a creeping rhizome covered in dense, brown, hair-like scales and produces bipinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The pinnules are typically small, oblong, and possess slightly serrated margins that give the foliage a delicate appearance. These ferns are characterized by their ability to form compact tufts or mats as the rhizomes expand across their substrate. Small, round sori are arranged in rows on the undersides of the leaflets to facilitate spore production. This morphological structure allows the plant to effectively cling to tree bark or rocky surfaces in its natural habitat.
