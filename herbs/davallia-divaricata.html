Davallia divaricata

What's the taxonomical classification of Davallia divaricata?

Davalliia divaricata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized under the subclass Polypodiinae and the order Polypodiales. This fern is a representative of the family Polypodiaceae, falling under the genus Davalliia, and is specifically identified by its species name, divaricata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Davalliia divaricata contains various secondary metabolites including flavonoids, tannins, and terpenoids that serve as its primary medicinal constituents. These chemical compounds are believed to contribute to the plant's traditional uses in treating inflammatory conditions and skin ailments. Research into fern species often highlights the presence of phenolic compounds which act as potent antioxidants. Some studies suggest that these bioactive molecules may possess antimicrobial properties against certain pathogens. Further investigation is required to fully isolate and quantify the specific therapeutic potential of these extracts.

What are the therapeutic actions of Davallia divaricata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammation, and various types of wounds. It contains phytochemicals that possess significant antimicrobial and healing properties. These compounds help reduce swelling and soothe irritated tissues when applied topically. Some traditional practices also involve using extracts to manage minor ailments related to the respiratory system. Its medicinal value is primarily centered on its ability to combat bacterial and fungal growth.

What are the herbal preparations of Davallia divaricata?

Davalliia divaricata is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion using the dried rhizomes and fronds of the plant. The plant material is typically boiled in water to extract its active chemical constituents for medicinal use. Some traditional practitioners also utilize the plant in the form of a topical poultice applied directly to the skin. These preparations are often consumed to address specific internal ailments or used externally for their perceived properties. The specific method of preparation can vary depending on the intended therapeutic goal.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Davalliia divaricata can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals through direct physical contact with its fronds. The plant contains certain organic compounds that may trigger redness, itching, or a mild rash upon touching the foliage. While it is not considered highly toxic to humans, handling the plant without gloves can be uncomfortable for those with delicate skin. Pet owners should also be cautious as ingestion could potentially lead to mild gastrointestinal upset in cats or dogs. Always wash your hands thoroughly after gardening with this species to prevent any lingering irritation.
