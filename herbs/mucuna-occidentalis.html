What is Mucuna occidentalis?

Mucuna occidentalis is a species of plant that belongs to the family Fabaceae, commonly known as legumes.

It is a climbing vine that grows up to 15 meters in length, with woody stems and twining branches. The leaves are compound, consisting of 5-9 leaflets that are elliptical in shape, 2-12 cm long, and 1-3 cm wide. The flowers are small, yellowish, and fragrant, arranged in axillary racemes, while the fruit is a pod that is 10-20 cm long and contains 10-20 seeds.

The plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it grows in moist, shaded forests and woodlands.
