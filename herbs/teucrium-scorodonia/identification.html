Botanical Identification of Scorodonia (Teucrium scorodonia)

To identify Scorodonia (Teucrium scorodonia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Scorodonia, or Teucrium scorodonia, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Lamiales, order Lamiales, family Lamiaceae, genus Teucrium, and species Teucrium scorodonia. It is a member of the mint family, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Teucrium scorodonia is the scientific name for Scorodonia. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. Common synonyms include Scorodonia luteola and Teucrium luteum. It is also known by regional names such as "lamb's ear" and "scorodonia." Historically, it has been referred to by various names in different cultures.

Morphological Characteristics

Scorodonia, also known as Teucrium scorodonia, is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit. It has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in rocky or dry soils. The stems are upright and somewhat woody at the base, often branching near the base. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a grayish-white underside. The inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small blue or purple flowers. The fruit is a small, three-lobed nutlet containing one or two seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Scorodonia, or Teucrium scorodonia, can be distinguished by its distinctive blue to purple flowers, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and a strong, aromatic scent. It is often confused with other Teucrium species, but its unique leaf shape and flower color set it apart. The plant also has a characteristic bitter taste, which aids in identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Scorodonia, also known as Teucrium scorodonia, is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. The plant prefers well-drained, slightly alkaline soils and is commonly found at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Scorodonia, or Teucrium scorodonia, blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period, when the plant is most identifiable and the essential oils are concentrated.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Scorodonia, or Teucrium scorodonia, is closely related to other members of the genus Teucrium. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with species like Teucrium chamaedrys and Teucrium polium.
