Black cherry

Prunus serotina

Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) is a deciduous tree native to North America, known for its dark red berries and its use in traditional medicine for its potential benefits in treating respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis.

Botanical Identification

Black Cherry, scientifically known as Prunus serotina, belongs to the Rosaceae family. Common names include Wild Cherry and Sweet Cherry, though it is not a cultivated cherry. It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. The plant has a taproot system, pinnately compound leaves, and white flowers that bloom in spring. It has been referred to by various synonyms in historical herbal texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Eudicots
  • Order: Rosales
  • Family: Rosaceae
  • Genus: Prunus
  • Species: Prunus serotina

Active Compounds

Black Cherry contains key bioactive compounds such as coumarin, tannin, and flavonoid. These compounds work together to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Coumarin contributes to its sedative properties, while tannins and flavonoids support digestive and immune health.

  • Coumarin
  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Infusion: Used for mild digestive issues and cold symptoms.

Poultice: Used for skin conditions and inflammation.

Safety Profile

Black Cherry (Prunus serotina) may cause gastrointestinal upset or allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated in patients with known allergies to cherries or related plants. Caution is advised during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data.
