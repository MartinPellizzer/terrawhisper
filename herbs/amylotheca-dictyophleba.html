What is Amylotheca dictyophleba?

The leaves are simple, alternate, and leathery, with an elliptical or ovate shape, measuring 4-13 centimeters long and 2-7 centimeters wide, and are arranged in an opposite or whorled pattern on the stems. The plant produces small, white or cream-colored flowers, 3-4 millimeters in diameter, in clusters or panicles, and the fruit is a dark brown to black, globular berry, 5-7 millimeters in diameter.

The specific name "dictyophleba" refers to the net-veined or reticulated leaf pattern.
