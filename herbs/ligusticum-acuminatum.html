Ligusticum acuminatum

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligusticum acuminatum?

Ligusticum acuminatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Apiales. This plant is a member of the family Apiaceae, which places it within the genus Ligusticum, specifically identifying it as the species acuminatum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligusticum acuminatum has pinnately compound leaves with lanceolate to ovate leaflets that feature sharply serrated margins and acute tips. The plant typically displays a perennial herbaceous habit with hollow, striated stems that reach moderate heights. Its inflorescence consists of compound umbels bearing small, inconspicuous flowers that are often white or pale yellow in color. The root system is characterized by thick, fleshy rhizomes that facilitate its underground growth and survival. These morphological features collectively distinguish it from other species within the Apiaceae family.
