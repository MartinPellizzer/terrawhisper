Cerasus dictyoneura

What's the taxonomical classification of Cerasus dictyoneura?

Cerasus dictyoneura belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is further organized into the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is established at the genus level as Cerasus and at the species level as dictyoneura.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cerasus dictyoneura has leaves characterized by a distinct reticulate venation pattern where the secondary veins form a prominent, net-like network across the lamina.

The leaf blades are typically elliptical or oblong in shape with finely serrated margins. These leaves are arranged alternately along the woody stems of the plant. The texture of the foliage appears somewhat leathery, providing a sturdy structure to the canopy.

The intricate vein architecture is the most defining morphological feature used to identify this specific species.

Because the specific toxicological profile of this species is not extensively documented in clinical literature, caution is necessary.
