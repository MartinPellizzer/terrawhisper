Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium) Compounds

The primary compounds of Tansy include essential oil, coumarin, flavonoids, phenolic acids, terpenoids, glycosides, tannins, saponins, alkaloids, and anthocyanins. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. Each plays a role in the plant's therapeutic applications.

Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium) is a key active compound, containing compounds like thujone and camphor. It is used in herbal medicine for its antiparasitic and antifungal properties, though it must be handled with care due to its potent and potentially toxic nature.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium). It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and sedative properties. Coumarin also exhibits mild anticoagulant effects, though its use requires caution due to potential toxicity at high doses.

3. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory ailments. Flavonoids may also enhance the plant's overall therapeutic effects.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. This compound is responsible for some of the therapeutic effects attributed to Tansy in herbal medicine. Its presence supports the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and skin conditions.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium). These compounds contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including its use in treating digestive issues and nervous disorders. They are responsible for the plant's distinctive aroma and have demonstrated anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including its effects on the nervous system and as a sedative. Glycosides in Tansy are responsible for its calming and antispasmodic actions. They are often used in herbal formulations for anxiety and digestive issues.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and may aid in reducing inflammation and microbial growth. Tannins in Tansy are also believed to support digestive health and have antimicrobial effects.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium). It contributes to the plant's bitter taste and may have antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponin's role in Tansy supports its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and skin-related ailments.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds with potential pharmacological effects. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though their specific roles and mechanisms of action are still under investigation.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a pigment found in Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower color and may have antioxidant properties. While not the primary active compound, anthocyanin supports the overall therapeutic profile of Tansy in herbal medicine.
