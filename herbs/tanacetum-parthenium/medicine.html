What Are The Medicinal Properties Of Tanacetum Parthenium (Feverfew)?

Tanacetum parthenium, also known as feverfew, has health benefits such as reducing fever, relieving pain and inflammation, and acting as an antispasmodic.

The medicinal constituents of feverfew include parthenolide, a sesquiterpene lactone that is responsible for its pharmacological effects. Preparations of feverfew include teas, tinctures, and capsules, which can be used to treat various conditions, including migraines and arthritis. Side effects of feverfew may include stomach upset, dizziness, and allergic reactions.

Caution is advised for pregnant women, as feverfew may stimulate the uterus and induce bleeding.

This article explains the health benefits, active constituents, medicinal preparations, possible side effects, and precautions related to Tanacetum parthenium.

What are the health benefits of Tanacetum parthenium?

Tanacetum parthenium has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and alleviating headaches.

Its active compound, parthenolide, has anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help soothe pain and calm the nervous system. Feverfew is often used to treat migraines, arthritis, and other conditions characterized by pain and swelling.

Additionally, its antioxidant properties may help protect against chronic diseases like cancer and heart disease.

What are the active constituents of Tanacetum parthenium?

The health benefits of Tanacetum parthenium comes from its active constituents, such as parthenolide, camphor, and volatile oils.

Parthenolide is responsible for the plant's anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties, while camphor exhibits analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects.

The volatile oils present in feverfew possess antimicrobial and antioxidant activities, contributing to its overall therapeutic potential in relieving symptoms associated with headaches, arthritis, and other health conditions.

What are the medicinal preparations of Tanacetum parthenium?

Tanacetum parthenium has different medicinal preparations, such as dried leaves, tea, tincture, capsules, and topical creams.

The dried leaves are used to make a tea that helps in alleviating migraines, headaches, and arthritis symptoms. The tincture is used to treat anxiety, insomnia, and menstrual cramps, while capsules contain the herb's extract in standardized dosages for relieving pain and inflammation.

Topical creams or ointments are applied directly to the skin to reduce fever, inflammation, and joint pain.

What are the possible side effect of using Tanacetum parthenium improperly?

Improper use of Tanacetum parthenium increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.

It may also cause headaches, dizziness, fatigue, insomnia, anxiety, and depression in some individuals. In rare cases, high doses can lead to muscle weakness, joint pain, and allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

Additionally, feverfew may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners, diabetes medications, and sedatives, leading to adverse effects.

What precautions to take when using Tanacetum parthenium medicinally?

Before using Tanacetum parthenium for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare professional to determine the appropriate dosage and potential interactions with other medications.

Additionally, it is essential to avoid consuming feverfew if you have allergies or are pregnant/breastfeeding, as it may cause allergic reactions, stomach upset, and hormonal imbalances.
