What Are The Medicinal Properties of feverfew (Tanacetum parthenium)?

What are the health benefits of feverfew?

Feverfew has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and relieving headaches.

It contains parthenolide, a compound that helps prevent the production of substances that cause inflammation in the body. Feverfew has been traditionally used to treat migraines, menstrual cramps, and arthritis.

Some studies suggest that feverfew may help prevent the onset of migraines and reduce their severity. It may also alleviate symptoms of rheumatoid arthritis, such as joint stiffness and pain. Additionally, feverfew has been shown to reduce inflammation in the skin, making it a potential treatment for skin conditions like eczema and dermatitis.

However, it is important to consult with a healthcare provider before taking feverfew, as it can cause side effects such as stomach upset and mouth ulcers.

What are the active constituents of feverfew?

The health benefits of feverfew comes from its active constituents, such as parthenolide, tanacetin, and camphor.

Parthenolide is the most well-known compound in feverfew, which is believed to be responsible for its medicinal properties. It has anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and anti-fever effects.

Tanacetin, another compound found in feverfew, has been shown to have anti-spasmodic properties, which can help relieve menstrual cramps and migraines. Camphor, a volatile oil found in feverfew, has analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects, which can help relieve pain and reduce inflammation.

These constituents work together to make feverfew a valuable herb for treating a variety of ailments, including migraines, arthritis, and menstrual cramps.

What are the medicinal preparations of feverfew?

Feverfew has different medicinal preparations, such as dried leaves, tinctures, capsules, and teas.

The dried leaves can be used to make tea, which is often used to treat migraines, menstrual cramps, and arthritis. Tinctures, which are alcohol-based extracts of feverfew, can be taken orally or applied topically to the skin to relieve inflammation and pain. Capsules containing dried feverfew leaves or powdered parthenolide, the active compound in feverfew, are also available and are often used to prevent migraines.

The recommended dosage for feverfew varies depending on the preparation and the individual's age, health status, and other factors, so it is important to consult a healthcare provider before using feverfew for medicinal purposes.

What are the possible side effect of using feverfew improperly?

Improper use of feverfew increases the chances of experiencing side effects such as mouth ulcers, stomach discomfort, and digestive issues like nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.

It may also lead to allergic reactions, characterized by symptoms such as rashes, swelling, and difficulty breathing. Prolonged use of feverfew can result in joint stiffness, mouth sensitivity to certain foods, and increased risk of bleeding due to its blood-thinning properties. In some cases, it may cause restlessness, anxiety, and insomnia.

Additionally, it can lead to complications during pregnancy and breastfeeding, making it crucial to consult a healthcare professional before use.

What precautions to take when using feverfew medicinally?

Before using feverfew for medicinal purposes, you must take precautions such as consulting with a healthcare provider.

Feverfew can cause allergic reactions, mouth sores, and stomach upset in some individuals. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid using feverfew, as its safety has not been established.

Additionally, people who are taking blood thinners or nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) should avoid using feverfew, as it can increase the risk of bleeding. It is also essential to stop using feverfew at least two weeks before scheduled surgery due to the increased risk of bleeding. Furthermore, long-term use of feverfew can lead to withdrawal symptoms such as rebound headaches and anxiety.

Therefore, it is crucial to follow the recommended dosage and consult a healthcare provider before using feverfew for medicinal purposes.
