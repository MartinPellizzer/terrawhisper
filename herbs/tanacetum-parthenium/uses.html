Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium) Uses

Tansy is used to treat allergic rhinitis, asthma, and hay fever due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It may also help with coughs and skin conditions like eczema, hives, and insect bites by reducing inflammation and irritation. Its effects are attributed to compounds such as parthenin and flavonoids.

Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium) Monograph

1. Allergic rhinitis

Tansy is used in herbal medicine to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and sneezing by soothing the respiratory tract. Often prepared as a tincture or tea, it is typically used in combination with other herbs for enhanced effectiveness.

2. Asthma

Tansy has been historically used to support respiratory health, particularly in managing asthma symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties may help reduce airway swelling and improve breathing. However, it should be used cautiously and under professional guidance due to its potent nature and potential side effects.

3. Hay fever

Tansy is used in herbal medicine to alleviate hay fever symptoms due to its antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce inflammation and congestion by supporting the body's natural response to allergens. Often prepared as a tea or tincture, it can provide relief from sneezing, runny nose, and itchy eyes. Regular use may help manage seasonal allergy symptoms naturally.

4. Cough

Tansy is occasionally used in herbal medicine to alleviate coughs, particularly those associated with respiratory irritation. It contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and soothe mucous membranes. However, its use for coughs is less common compared to other herbs like thyme or eucalyptus, and it should be used with caution due to its potential toxicity.

5. Skin inflammation

Tansy is used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and infection in conditions like eczema and psoriasis. Topical applications of tansy preparations can soothe irritated skin and promote healing. Its active compounds target inflammatory responses and bacterial growth on the skin.

6. Eczema

Tansy is used topically for eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce skin irritation and prevent infection. A diluted tincture or infused oil can be applied to affected areas. However, it should be used cautiously as it may cause skin sensitization in some individuals.

7. Hives

Tansy is used to treat hives due to its antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce allergic reactions by inhibiting histamine release. Typically, it is prepared as a tincture or tea. It is important to consult a qualified herbalist for proper use and dosage.

8. Skin irritation

Tansy is used to treat skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It can soothe conditions like eczema and psoriasis when applied topically. However, it should be diluted to avoid skin sensitivity. Always consult a professional before use.

9. Insect bites

Tansy is used topically to alleviate the symptoms of insect bites. Its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties help reduce swelling, itching, and infection risk. A poultice or infused oil made from fresh Tansy leaves can be applied directly to the affected area for relief.

10. Inflammatory skin conditions

Tansy is used topically to treat inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and infection in conditions like eczema and psoriasis. A diluted tincture or infused oil is typically applied to affected areas for relief. Its effectiveness is supported by traditional use and some modern herbal studies.
