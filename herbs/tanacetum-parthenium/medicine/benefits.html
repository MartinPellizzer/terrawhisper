1. Reduces migraine frequency

Feverfew reduces migraine frequency because it contains compounds that help to prevent the widening of blood vessels in the brain.

These compounds, including parthenolide, inhibit the release of inflammatory substances, such as serotonin and prostaglandins, which can cause blood vessels to constrict and then dilate, leading to migraines. By reducing inflammation and stabilizing blood vessels, feverfew can help to alleviate the symptoms of migraines and reduce their frequency. Additionally, feverfew may also help to reduce the severity and duration of migraines by inhibiting the activation of platelets and the production of inflammatory chemicals.

Studies have shown that feverfew can be an effective preventative treatment for migraines, reducing their frequency and severity in some people.

2. Alleviates menstrual pain

Feverfew alleviates menstrual pain because of its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

It contains parthenolide, a compound that inhibits the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that cause inflammation and pain in the body. By reducing prostaglandin levels, feverfew can help alleviate menstrual cramps, bloating, and other symptoms associated with menstruation. Additionally, feverfew may also help relax the smooth muscles in the uterus, further reducing menstrual pain.

While more research is needed to fully understand the mechanisms behind feverfew's pain-relieving effects, it has been used traditionally for centuries as a natural remedy for menstrual discomfort.
