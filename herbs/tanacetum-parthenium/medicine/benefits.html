10 Health Benefits Of Tanacetum Parthenium (Feverfew)

Tanacetum parthenium, also known as feverfew, has health benefits such as reducing inflammation and relieving headache and migraine symptoms.

Its medicinal properties include the presence of parthenolide and volatile oils, which have anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects. For instance, studies show that feverfew can reduce the frequency and severity of migraines, allowing people to return to their daily activities with improved quality of life.

This plant also exhibits antipyretic properties, making it a natural remedy for fever relief.

This article explains in details the 10 best health benefits of Tanacetum parthenium.

1. Reduces migraine frequency

Tanacetum parthenium reduces migraine frequency because of its unique ability to inhibit the production of a chemical called 5-HT from platelets.

This reduction in serotonin release helps to prevent blood vessels from dilating excessively, which is a common trigger for migraines.

Additionally, feverfew's flavonoids and sesquiterpene lactones have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, further contributing to its migraine-reducing effects by reducing inflammation and oxidative stress.

2. Alleviates menstrual pain

Tanacetum parthenium alleviates menstrual pain because of its unique ability to reduce prostaglandins, hormone-like substances responsible for uterine cramping and contractions.

The plant's active compounds, such as parthenolide, have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties that help relax uterine muscles, thereby reducing menstrual cramps and discomfort.

Additionally, feverfew may also inhibit the production of prostaglandins, further contributing to its analgesic effects on menstruation-related pain.

3. Eases rheumatoid arthritis symptoms

Tanacetum parthenium eases rheumatoid arthritis symptoms because it contains compounds that reduce inflammation and relieve pain.

The leaves of the plant contain parthenolide, a sesquiterpene lactone that has been shown to inhibit the production of inflammatory chemicals in the body, thereby reducing joint swelling and tenderness.

Additionally, feverfew's anti-inflammatory properties may also help to slow down the progression of rheumatoid arthritis, making it a valuable natural remedy for managing symptoms associated with this chronic condition.

4. Lowers inflammation

Tanacetum parhenium lowers inflammation because it contains a unique compound called parthenolide.

This compound has been shown to inhibit the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that cause pain and swelling. Parthenolide also reduces the activity of enzymes involved in inflammation, thereby decreasing the levels of inflammatory mediators such as cytokines and chemokines.

As a result, feverfew has been traditionally used to treat various inflammatory disorders, including migraines, arthritis, and skin conditions.

5. Improves fever symptoms

Tanacetum parthenium improves fever symptoms because it contains a unique compound called parthenolide.

This natural antipyretic helps to reduce the production of prostaglandins, which are hormone-like substances that cause inflammation and increased body temperature.

By inhibiting prostaglandin synthesis, parthenolide effectively alleviates fever symptoms such as headache, fatigue, and sweating, making it a popular herbal remedy for treating mild fevers and other inflammatory conditions.

6. Relieves asthma symptoms

Tanacetum parthenium relieves asthma symptoms because it contains compounds that have a potent anti-inflammatory effect on the airways.

The plant's active ingredients, such as parthenolide and chamazulene, help to reduce inflammation in the lungs, making it easier for people with asthma to breathe.

Additionally, feverfew may also help to relax the airway muscles, allowing for improved airflow and reducing symptoms of wheezing and shortness of breath.

7. Reduces risk of blood clots

Tanacetum parthenium reduces risk of blood clots because it inhibits platelet aggregation and activity.

This herb contains compounds like parthenolide, which has been shown to decrease the release of serotonin, a potent stimulant for platelet activation.

As a result, feverfew supplementation can reduce the formation and growth of blood clots, thereby lowering the risk of thrombosis and related cardiovascular complications.

8. Soothes skin irritations

Tanacetum parthenium soothes skin irritations because of its unique composition.

The plant contains a group of sesquiterpene lactones, which have been shown to possess anti-inflammatory and antiallergic properties. These compounds help to reduce redness, swelling, and itching associated with skin irritations, while also preventing the release of histamine, a chemical that causes allergic reactions.

As a result, feverfew has been traditionally used in topical preparations to calm irritated skin conditions such as eczema, acne, and sunburn.

9. Helps with dermatitis

Tanacetum parthenium helps with dermatitis because it possesses potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The plant's bioactive compounds, such as parthenolide and camphor, have been shown to reduce inflammation and oxidative stress in the skin, thereby alleviating symptoms of dermatitis.

Additionally, feverfew has been found to inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, further supporting its therapeutic potential in managing this chronic skin condition.

10. Eases eczema symptoms

Tanacetum parthenium eases eczema symptoms because of its potent anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties.

The plant's active compounds, such as parthenolide, inhibit the production of pro-inflammatory chemicals, reducing redness, swelling, and itching associated with eczema.

Additionally, feverfew's flavonoids exhibit antihistaminic activity, which helps to relieve histamine-mediated itching and pruritus, providing relief from skin irritation and discomfort.
