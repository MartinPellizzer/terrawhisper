Botanical Identification of Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium)

To identify Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Tansy, or Tanacetum parthenium, belongs to the kingdom Plantae. Its family is Compositae, also known as Asteraceae. The genus is Tanacetum, and the species is Tanacetum parthenium. The other classifications—division, class, and order—are not provided in the data.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Tanacetum parthenium is the scientific name for Tansy. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. Synonyms include Chrysanthemum parthenium and T. vulgare. Common names include Tansy, goldenhead, and tansy ragwort. It is also known by regional names like tansy in England and tansy in Germany. Historical names include parthenium, derived from the Greek for "virgin," reflecting its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Tansy is a perennial herb with a robust root system that anchors it firmly in soil. Its stems are upright and hairy, growing up to 60 cm tall. Leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and a strong, bitter aroma. The plant produces a flat, round inflorescence of small yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The fruit is a small, dry achene with a pappus, aiding in seed dispersal.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Tansy can be distinguished by its yellow buttonhead flower heads surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. It has finely divided, fern-like leaves and a strong, camphor-like scent. It grows in moist soils and is often found in wet meadows or along waterways. It is commonly confused with other members of the Asteraceae family but can be identified by its distinctive foliage and flower structure.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Tansy is native to Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with full sun. It prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows and disturbed areas. It grows at elevations between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Tansy blooms from July to September, producing yellow button-like flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification, the best time to harvest is during the flowering period when the plant is most visually distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Tansy (Tanacetum parthenium) is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family, particularly within the genus Tanacetum. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with other species in this genus, such as finely divided leaves and composite flower heads. Its phylogenetic placement reflects its evolutionary relationships within the Compositae family, highlighting similarities in structure and chemistry.
