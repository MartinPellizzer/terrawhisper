Urera oblongifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Urera oblongifolia?

Urera oblongifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae, falling specifically within the genus Urera, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name oblongifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Urera oblongifolia has woody, perennial stems covered in stinging, hollow hairs that contain irritating chemical compounds. The leaves are typically arranged in an opposite pattern and feature an oblong to elliptic shape with serrated margins. A prominent characteristic of this species is the presence of fine, stinging trichomes distributed across the leaf surfaces and along the petioles. The venation of the foliage is quite distinct, with secondary veins arching toward the leaf tip. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in clusters, often appearing in the leaf axils.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause immediate skin irritation, painful stinging sensations, and localized swelling due to the presence of urticating hairs on its surface. When these microscopic needles penetrate the skin, they release chemical irritants that trigger an inflammatory response. Some individuals may experience redness or small bumps at the site of contact. In cases of sensitive skin, the reaction might persist for several hours or lead to itching. Avoiding direct physical contact is the most effective way to prevent these dermatological issues.
