Urera oblongifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Urera oblongifolia?

Urera oblongifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae, falling specifically within the genus Urera, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name oblongifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Urera oblongifolia contains a complex profile of bioactive compounds including flavonoids, tannins, saponins, and various alkaloids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These phytochemicals work together to provide significant anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activities within biological systems. Research indicates that the plant's chemical constituents may also possess antimicrobial and analgesic qualities. Some studies suggest these compounds can aid in the management of skin ailments and wound healing processes. The specific concentration of these secondary metabolites often varies depending on the plant's environmental growth conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Urera oblongifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, inflammatory conditions, digestive ailments, and respiratory issues. Its medicinal properties are frequently applied to soothe wounds and reduce the swelling associated with various dermatological problems. Some traditional practices utilize the plant to alleviate symptoms of gastritis and other gastrointestinal discomforts. Additionally, decoctions made from its leaves are sometimes employed to help manage symptoms of asthma or persistent coughs. These therapeutic applications rely on the specific bioactive compounds found within its foliage.

What are the herbal preparations of Urera oblongifolia?

Urera oblongifolia is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice to utilize its medicinal properties. The leaves and stems are often boiled in water to create a concentrated tea used for internal consumption. For external ailments such as skin irritations or wounds, the crushed plant material is applied directly to the affected area as a poultice. Some traditional practitioners also utilize the plant in the form of a wash to cleanse the skin. These various methods allow for the extraction of active compounds suitable for different therapeutic needs.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Urera oblongifolia can cause immediate skin irritation, redness, and localized swelling upon contact due to the presence of stinging hairs. These trichomes inject irritating substances that trigger a painful stinging or burning sensation on the epidermis. Some individuals may experience an allergic reaction characterized by hives or intense itching at the site of contact. If the plant is handled without protection, the discomfort can persist for several hours or even days. Careful avoidance of direct physical contact is necessary to prevent these dermatological symptoms.
