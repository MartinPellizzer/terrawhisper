Oxalis pes-caprae

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxalis pes-caprae?

Oxalis pes-caprae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary lineage of the order Oxalidales. At the family level, it is a member of the Oxalidaceae, which contains various species characterized by their distinctive leaf structures. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Oxalis, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species pes-caprae.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxalis pes-caprae has a perennial growth habit characterized by underground bulbils, trifoliate leaves with obcordate leaflets, and bright yellow actinomorphic flowers. The leaves consist of three heart-shaped leaflets that exhibit nyctinastic movements by closing at night. Its flowering stems emerge from the soil to support small, five-petaled blossoms that typically appear in clusters. The plant spreads aggressively through the production of small, dark bulbils located at the base of the stem or near the roots. These morphological features allow the species to thrive in various disturbed environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of South Africa, but it has become widely naturalized across much of the world's subtropical and temperate zones. It is frequently found in disturbed areas, gardens, and lawns throughout Australia, the southwestern United States, and various parts of South America. Because it spreads easily through underground bulbs, it often invades coastal regions and grassy habitats where climates remain mild. Its ability to thrive in diverse soil types has allowed it to establish itself on nearly every continent except Antarctica. This widespread dispersal makes it a common sight in many urban and agricultural landscapes globally.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant Oxalis pes-caprae is cultivated primarily for its ornamental qualities in subtropical climates and for its edible, sour-tasting leaves used in various culinary applications.

What are the pharmacological activities of Oxalis pes-caprae?

This plant has demonstrated significant antiabetic, antioxidant, hypoglycemic, alpha-amylase inhibitory, and alpha-glucosidase inhibitory activities. Its antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress within biological systems. The plant also exhibits potent hypoglycemic effects that assist in lowering elevated blood glucose levels. Specifically, it regulates glucose metabolism through its ability to inhibit alpha-amylase and alpha-glucosidase enzymes. These combined pharmacological actions suggest that Oxalis pes-caprae holds great potential for managing metabolic disorders like diabetes.

According to a study published by "Biomedicine & pharmacotherapy = Biomedecine & pharmacotherapie", Oxalis pes-caprae flowers extract demonstrates antioxidant, enzyme inhibitory, and antiglycation activities. The methanolic extract was found to be the richest in phenolic compounds, which relates to its remarkable biological effects. Oral administration of two doses, 150 mg/kg and 250 mg/kg daily for 3 weeks, resulted in a hypoglycemic effect compared to the reference drug glibenclamide at 10 mg/kg. The extract significantly increased the activities of antioxidants and glycolysis enzymes in the liver, kidney, and spleen of diabetic mice. These in-vitro and in-vivo studies confirm that Oxalis pes-caprae effectively exhibits hypoglycemic and antidiabetic effects against hyperglycemia and oxidative damage.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a cinnamic acid ester, a noroxyneolignan, various cinnamic ester derivatives, phenyl cinnamate derivatives, and two dihydrocinnamic acid esters. These specific phytochemicals are responsible for the plant's diverse pharmacological potential. The presence of these unique organic molecules makes Oxalis pes-caprae a subject of interest in biochemical research. Scientists study these structural components to understand their potential therapeutic applications in medicine. Such chemical complexity highlights the biological richness of the species.

According to a study published by "Chemistry & biodiversity", the extracts of fresh leaves and twigs of Oxalis pes-caprae contain a cinnamic acid ester, two dihydrocinnamic acid esters, a noroxyneolignan, and a dibenzyl ether derivative. These extracts also contain common aromatic compounds and flavonoids. The effects of these isolated aromatic compounds were studied at concentrations ranging from 10(-4) to 10(-7) M. The activities of some of these compounds were found to be higher than that of pendimethalin.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause kidney stones, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and calcium deficiency due to its high concentration of oxalic acid. When ingested, the acid interferes with the body's ability to absorb essential minerals. This process often leads to a calcium deficiency in the bloodstream. The accumulation of calcium oxalate crystals in the urinary tract is a primary cause of painful kidney stones. Consuming the plant may also trigger gastrointestinal distress such as nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal pain.
