Oxalis pes-caprae

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxalis pes-caprae?

Oxalis pes-caprae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the evolutionary lineage of the order Oxalidales. At the family level, it is a member of the Oxalidaceae, which contains various species characterized by their distinctive leaf structures. Within this family, the plant is categorized under the genus Oxalis, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species pes-caprae.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Oxalis pes-caprae contains oxalic acid, flavonoids, tannins, and various glycosides that serve as its primary bioactive compounds. The presence of oxalic acid is a defining chemical characteristic, though it can be toxic in high concentrations if ingested. Phytochemical studies have identified specific flavonoids and phenolic compounds that contribute to the plant's antioxidant properties. What are the therapeutic actions of Oxalis pes-caprae?

Oxalis pes-caprae has demonstrated medicinal properties through its use as a diuretic, a treatment for skin ailments, and an agent to soothe digestive disturbances. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including oxalic acid and flavonoids, which contribute to its biological activities. In traditional medicine, crushed leaves are often applied topically to manage inflammatory skin conditions or small wounds. Some regional practices also utilize decoctions of the plant to help stimulate kidney function and promote urination. However, its high oxalate content necessitates careful usage to avoid potential toxicity or kidney stone formation.

What are the herbal preparations of Oxalis pes-caprae?

Oxalis pes-caprae is prepared as a topical poultice, a decoction for external washes, or an infusion used to treat skin irritations. The fresh leaves and stems are often crushed directly onto the skin to soothe inflammation or minor wounds. When prepared as a decoction, the plant material is boiled in water to extract its active properties for use in cleansing washes. These liquid preparations can also be applied as a compress to reduce swelling in localized areas. Some traditional practices involve using the juice expressed from the plant to address specific dermatological concerns.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Oxalis pes-caprae can cause significant harm to humans and livestock through the ingestion of high concentrations of oxalic acid. This organic acid acts as a potent toxin by binding with calcium in the bloodstream to form insoluble calcium oxalate crystals. These crystals can precipitate in the kidneys, potentially leading to acute renal failure or permanent kidney damage. In animals, consumption often results in severe gastrointestinal distress, including vomiting and abdominal pain. Prolonged exposure or large doses can lead to systemic electrolyte imbalances and even death.
