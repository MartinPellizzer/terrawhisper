Sageretia lucida

What's the taxonomical classification of Sageretia lucida?

Sageretia lucida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its lineage through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, the plant is placed under the order Rosales. It is a member of the family Rhamnaceae, falling within the genus Sageretia, and is specifically identified by its species name, lucida.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Sageretia lucida contains high concentrations of bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, and phenolic compounds. These specific chemical constituents contribute to the plant's notable pharmacological properties, particularly its antioxidant and antimicrobial activities. The presence of flavonoids assists in neutralizing free radicals, which can help protect cells from oxidative stress. Additionally, the diverse profile of terpenoids suggests potential applications in treating inflammatory conditions. Research continues into how these individual compounds interact to provide therapeutic benefits in traditional medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Sageretia lucida?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments, respiratory infections, skin wounds, and various inflammatory conditions. It is frequently employed in traditional medicine to alleviate stomach pains and intestinal disturbances. The leaves are often applied topically to promote the healing of cuts and to reduce swelling in the skin. Additionally, decoctions made from the plant are used to soothe coughs and manage fever symptoms. Its diverse pharmacological properties make it a versatile remedy for both internal and external ailments.
