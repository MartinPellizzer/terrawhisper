Psychotria serpens

What's the taxonomical classification of Psychotria serpens?

Psychotria serpens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it resides in the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Psychotria, with its specific designation being the species serpens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Psychotria serpens has a prostrate, creeping growth habit characterized by slender, rooting stems that sprawl across the forest floor. The leaves are typically opposite, elliptic to oblong in shape, and possess an entire margin with an acute apex. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves, often appearing in small clusters. The inflorescence structure is characteristic of the Rubiaceae family, frequently featuring a reduced number of floral parts. This plant's morphology is adapted specifically for ground-level survival in shaded, tropical environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Psychotria serpens?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its potent active-oxygen scavenging activity and antiviral properties. The phytochemical constituents within the species work effectively to neutralize free radicals, which helps prevent oxidative stress in biological systems. Furthermore, studies have highlighted its capacity to inhibit the replication of certain viral pathogens. These combined biological actions suggest that the plant could serve as a valuable source for developing new therapeutic agents. Such diverse medicinal capabilities make it a subject of intense scientific interest in natural product research.

As discussed in a study, the water and methanol extracts of Psychotria serpens were evaluated for their active-oxygen scavenging activity. Out of 70 traditional herbal medicines tested, Psychotria serpens especially showed strong scavenging activity against the superoxide anion radical (O2-). These findings were obtained using the electron spin resonance (ESR) technique to evaluate effectiveness for anti-aging. The study focused on searching for new active-oxygen scavengers from natural resources among the 70 herbal medicines.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds isorhamnetin, tricin, taxifolin, chrysin, and euscaphic acid. These specific flavonoids and organic acids are recognized for their diverse biological activities in pharmacological studies. Isorhamnetin and tricin are frequently investigated for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Taxifolin and chrysin contribute to the plant's therapeutic profile through various protective mechanisms in cellular environments. Additionally, the presence of euscaphic acid provides unique chemical characteristics that distinguish the medicinal value of this species.

As discussed in a study, 18 compounds were isolated from the 90% ethanol extract of the stems and leaves of Psychotria serpens. These isolated compounds included chrysin(1), acacetin(2), genkwanin(3), chrysoeriol(4), rhamnocitrin(5), isorhamentin(6), tricin(7), jaceosidin(8), dillenetin(9), kumatakenin(10), ayanin(11), isosakuranetin(12), eriodictyol(13), homoeriodictyol(14), taxifolin(15), pomoic acid(16), fupenzic acid(17) and euscaphic acid(18). The separation and purification of these constituents were achieved using column chromatographies with silica gel, ODS, Sephadex LH-20 and PR-HPLC. All of these compounds were isolated from the genus Psychotria for the first time.
