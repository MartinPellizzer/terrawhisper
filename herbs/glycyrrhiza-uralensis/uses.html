Licorice (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) Uses

Licorice is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, bronchitis, and asthma by reducing inflammation and loosening mucus. It also helps with sore throat and mucous membrane inflammation. Additionally, it supports the immune system in managing influenza and other respiratory infections. Its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties make it valuable for allergic rhinitis and bronchial asthma.

Licorice (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) Monograph

1. Cough

Licorice is commonly used to soothe coughs due to its expectorant properties, which help loosen mucus in the respiratory tract. It also has anti-inflammatory effects that reduce irritation in the throat. In traditional Chinese medicine, it is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness for persistent or dry coughs.

2. Bronchitis

Licorice root is used in herbal medicine to treat bronchitis due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce airway inflammation, easing coughing and breathing difficulties. It is often combined with other herbs in traditional formulas to support respiratory health.

3. Influenza

Licorice is used in traditional Chinese medicine to support respiratory health during influenza. It helps reduce inflammation and soothe coughs. Its antiviral properties may aid in managing symptoms. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its effectiveness.

4. Respiratory tract infections

Licorice root is traditionally used to support respiratory health by soothing throat irritation and reducing inflammation. It contains glycyrrhizin, which may help suppress viral replication and ease symptoms of colds and coughs. Its expectorant properties aid in clearing mucus, making it a common remedy for respiratory tract infections.

5. Asthma

Licorice root has been traditionally used to support respiratory health in asthma. It helps reduce inflammation and bronchial spasms, easing breathing difficulties. Its expectorant properties assist in clearing mucus, while its antihistamine effects may alleviate allergic reactions contributing to asthma symptoms.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Licorice root has been traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and inflammation, supporting respiratory comfort. Its active compounds, such as glycyrrhizin, modulate immune responses, making it a valued herb in herbal medicine for managing seasonal allergies.

7. Respiratory infections

Licorice root is used in herbal medicine to support respiratory health. It helps reduce inflammation and soothe coughs by increasing mucus production. Its expectorant properties aid in clearing the airways, making it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis and sore throat. It is often combined with other herbs to enhance its therapeutic effects.

8. Bronchial asthma

Licorice root has been traditionally used to alleviate bronchial asthma symptoms by reducing inflammation and relaxing airway muscles. Its active compounds, such as glycyrrhizin, help suppress allergic reactions and ease breathing. It is often used in herbal formulations to support respiratory health. However, long-term use may cause side effects due to its potent effects on the body.

9. Mucous membrane inflammation

Licorice root is used to soothe mucous membrane inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and demulcent properties. It coats and protects irritated tissues in the throat and digestive tract. It is commonly used in herbal formulations for conditions like sore throat and gastritis. Its glycyrrhizin content helps reduce inflammation and enhance immune response.

10. Sore throat

Licorice root is commonly used to soothe sore throats due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It helps reduce swelling and irritation in the throat lining, while its mild expectorant action aids in clearing mucus. Traditionally, it is prepared as a tea or lozenge for direct application. Its effectiveness is supported by both historical use and modern scientific studies.
