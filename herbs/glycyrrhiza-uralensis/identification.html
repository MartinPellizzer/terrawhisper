Botanical Identification of Licorice (Glycyrrhiza uralensis)

To identify Licorice (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Licorice, or Glycyrrhiza uralensis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Glycyrrhiza, and species Glycyrrhiza uralensis. This classification places it within the legume family, which is characterized by specific floral and fruit structures.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Licorice is Glycyrrhiza uralensis, first described by the Russian botanist Peter Simon Pallas in 1763. It is part of the Fabaceae family and is also known by several synonyms, including Glycyrrhiza glabra and Glycyrrhiza echinata. Common names include licorice root, sweet root, and wild licorice, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and time periods.

Morphological Characteristics

Licorice (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) is a perennial herb with a taproot system. It has erect, branched stems that are hairy. The leaves are alternate, trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets. The inflorescence is a spike of small white to pale pink flowers. The fruit is a legume containing 1-3 oval, brown seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Licorice (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) can be distinguished by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, pinnately compound leaves with 11-23 leaflets. The flowers are pale pink to violet, clustered in umbels, and the root is thick, fleshy, and sweet-smelling. It is often confused with other Glycyrrhiza species, but its leaflet count and root characteristics are key identifiers.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Licorice (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) is native to regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia, including parts of Russia, Mongolia, and China. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained, loamy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 500 and 2,000 meters, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Licorice (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) blooms from June to July, producing pink to purple flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in late summer to early autumn when the roots are mature and contain the highest concentration of active compounds.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Licorice (Glycyrrhiza uralensis) belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is closely related to other members of the genus Glycyrrhiza. It shares key morphological and genetic traits with species like Glycyrrhiza inflata, and its phylogenetic position reflects its close relationship within the tribe Astragaleae. These relationships are supported by both molecular and morphological data.
