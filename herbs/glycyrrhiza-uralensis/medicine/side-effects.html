10 Possible Side Effects Of Glycyrrhiza Uralensis (Licorice)

Glycyrrhiza uralensis has some side effects when used improperly, such as increased blood pressure, water retention, and electrolyte imbalances.

These side effects can be caused by the herb's active compound, glycyrrhizin, which can interfere with the body's ability to regulate potassium levels.

In severe cases, this can lead to conditions like hypokalemia, which can cause muscle weakness, fatigue, and even heart palpitations, making everyday activities more challenging and potentially life-threatening.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Glycyrrhiza uralensis if used imporperly.

1. Enhances sensitivity to glucose

Glycyrrhiza uralensis increases blood pressure due to its ability to elevate cortisol levels and enhance aldosterone activity.

The compound glycyrrhetic acid in Glycyrrhiza uralensis inhibits the enzyme 11β-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase, which converts cortisol into its inactive form.

This leads to an increase in cortisol levels and subsequent stimulation of aldosterone production, resulting in increased sodium reabsorption and blood volume expansion.

Glycyrrhiza uralensis causes potassium loss.

This is due to its ability to inhibit the activity of the enzyme 11β-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase type 2 (11β-HSD2), which helps regulate potassium levels in the body. As a result, the kidneys retain more sodium and excrete more potassium, leading to a decrease in potassium levels.

This can lead to symptoms such as muscle weakness, fatigue, and heart palpitations.
