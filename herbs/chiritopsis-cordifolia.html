Chiritopsis cordifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Chiritopsis cordifolia?

Chiritopsis cordifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated within the order Lamiales. It is further categorized under the family Gesneriaceae, specifically falling into the genus Chiritopsis, with its unique identity defined by the species epithet cordifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chiritopsis cordifolia has cordate leaves that are roughly 5 to 15 centimeters wide, featuring prominent venation and serrated margins. These leaves are arranged oppositely along a sturdy, erect stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters. The plant produces small, white, actinomorphic flowers that grow in terminal clusters. Each flower consists of five distinct petals and several stamens characteristic of the Asteraceae family. The root system is composed of a persistent woody rhizome that allows the plant to spread through its woodland habitat.
