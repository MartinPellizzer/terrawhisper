Arctium tomentosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arctium tometosum has large, ovate, deeply veined leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, white tomentose hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. The plant typically develops a robust, erect stem that is also characterized by its fuzzy, woolly texture. Its inflorescences consist of globular flower heads enclosed by hooked bracts, which are specifically adapted for animal dispersal. These involucral bracts are heavily coated in fine hairs, giving the seed heads a distinctively velvety appearance. The overall structure of the plant reflects its adaptation to temperate environments through its thick, protective foliage.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic skin reactions, gastrointestinal distress, and potential complications for individuals with kidney issues or those who are pregnant. Contact with the leaves or stems may trigger dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some users report experiencing nausea, bloating, or stomach cramps after ingestion. Because of its diuretic properties, it might also interfere with certain medications or affect electrolyte balance. Consulting a healthcare professional is necessary to avoid these adverse reactions.
