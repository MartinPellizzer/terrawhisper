Arctium tomentosum

What's the taxonomical classification of Arctium tomentosum?

Arctium tomentosum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, falling under the genus Arctium, and is specifically identified by its species name, tomentosum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arctium tometosum has large, ovate, deeply veined leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, white tomentose hairs on both the upper and lower surfaces. The plant typically develops a robust, erect stem that is also characterized by its fuzzy, woolly texture. Its inflorescences consist of globular flower heads enclosed by hooked bracts, which are specifically adapted for animal dispersal. These involucral bracts are heavily coated in fine hairs, giving the seed heads a distinctively velvety appearance. The overall structure of the plant reflects its adaptation to temperate environments through its thick, protective foliage.
