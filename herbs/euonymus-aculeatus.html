Euonymus aculeatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Euonymus aculeatus has opposite, simple, entire leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape and characterized by acute tips and finely serrated margins. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy green surface on the upper side, while the underside remains slightly paler. Its woody stems are slender and can become somewhat angular as the plant matures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually give way to distinctive fleshy fruits. These fruits often split open to reveal brightly colored seeds nestled within a capsule.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Euonymus aculeatus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested. The berries and leaves contain alkaloids that act as irritants to the digestive tract, often leading to vomiting, diarrhea, or abdominal pain. Contact with the plant's sap may trigger contact dermatitis, resulting in redness, itching, or a localized rash on the skin. While direct poisoning is more common through ingestion, even small amounts can be dangerous to pets and children. Careful avoidance of all plant parts is necessary to prevent these adverse physiological reactions.
