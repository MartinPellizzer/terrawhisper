Limonium bicolor

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Limonium bicolor has a perennial growth habit characterized by erect, woody-based stems that produce terminal, branched inflorescences of small, two-toned flowers. The leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette of narrow, lanceolate, or linear shapes that are often grayish-green in color. These leaves possess a somewhat fleshy or leathery texture, which helps the plant retain moisture in its natural habitat. The flowering structures consist of many tiny, delicate blossoms clustered tightly atop thin, branching stalks. Each flower displays a distinct color contrast between its petals and calyx, giving the species its specific name. The overall architecture of the plant is airy and delicate, making it highly valued in floral arrangements.
