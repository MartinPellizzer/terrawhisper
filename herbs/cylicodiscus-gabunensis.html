Cylicodiscus gabunensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Cylicodiscus gabunensis?

Cylicodiscus gabunenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is organized under the order Fabales. It is a member of the family Fabaceae, falling within the genus Cylicodiscus, and is specifically identified by its species name, gabunenensis.

What are the herbal preparations of Cylicodiscus gabunensis?

Cylicodiscus gabunensis is prepared as a decoction made from the plant's leaves and bark to treat various medicinal conditions. Local practitioners typically boil these specific parts in water to extract active compounds for therapeutic use. Once the liquid is concentrated, it is often consumed orally to address internal ailments. In some traditional practices, the prepared liquid may also be applied topically to skin irritations. These diverse preparation methods ensure that the plant's properties are effectively delivered to the patient.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Cylicodiscus gabunensis can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurotoxicity if ingested or touched without protection. The plant contains bioactive secondary metabolites that may trigger localized dermatitis upon contact with human tissue. If consumed, these compounds can disrupt digestive processes, leading to nausea or abdominal cramping. There is also a risk that certain alkaloids within the specimen could interfere with neurological functions if absorbed into the bloodstream. Due to these unpredictable reactions, individuals should exercise extreme caution when handling the species.
