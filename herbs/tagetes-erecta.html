What is Tagetes (Tagetes Erecta)?

Tagetes erecta, commonly known as the African marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Americas, widely cultivated for its vibrant orange and yellow flowers and its use in traditional herbal medicine for its antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanical Classification and Taxonomy

The scientific name of Tagetes is Tagetes erecta. This plant belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Asterales, family Asteraceae, genus Tagetes, and species Tagetes erecta. It is commonly known as the Mexican marigold in North America and the African marigold in Africa. The plant is also referred to as the garden marigold in Europe. Synonyms for Tagetes erecta include Tagetes patula (botanical) and various regional names such as Aztec gold and winter marigold.

Plant Description and Morphology

Tagetes is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of 30-90 cm, featuring a sturdy, branched stem covered in fine hairs.

Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate to ovate in shape, with a rough, slightly serrated margin and a grayish-green upper surface. The flowers are bright and showy, with a radiate pattern of tubular central disc florets surrounded by yellow to orange ray florets, often with a prominent central tuft of bristles. The root system is fibrous and shallow, adapting to dry conditions by spreading horizontally near the soil surface.

The plant has a distinctive, pungent smell reminiscent of marigolds, with leaves and stems that are rough to the touch and have a waxy texture.

Cultivation and Harvesting

Tagetes erecta, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, thrives in well-drained soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5, making it ideal for garden beds or container planting.
