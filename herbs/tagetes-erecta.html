Botanical Identification

Tagetes erecta, commonly known as the African marigold, belongs to the Asteraceae family. It is an annual plant native to Mexico and Central America. Characterized by its bright yellow-orange flowers and feathery leaves, it is also known as Mexican marigold or Holy flower in various regional contexts.

Active Compounds

Tagetes contains essential oil, flavonoids, and phenolic acids as its primary bioactive compounds. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Essential oil
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Safety Profile

Tagetes erecta, commonly known as African marigold, is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
