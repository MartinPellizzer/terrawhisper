1. Causes digestive upset

Rhamnus cathartica causes digestive upset due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides and alkaloids.

These compounds can irritate the gastrointestinal tract, leading to symptoms such as diarrhea, abdominal cramps, and nausea.

Additionally, the laxative properties of Rhamnus cathartica may cause dehydration and electrolyte imbalances if used excessively or without proper hydration.

2. Triggers allergic reactions

Rhamnus cathartica triggers allergic reactions due to its high content of urushiol, a potent allergen responsible for causing skin irritation and anaphylaxis.

The plant's sap contains this allergenic compound, which can be released when the fruit or leaves are touched, inhaled, or ingested, leading to an immune system response characterized by symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

3. Induces vomiting frequently

Rhamnus cathartica induces vomiting frequently due to its strong laxative properties.

The plant's seeds and bark contain anthraquinone glycosides, which are quickly absorbed into the bloodstream and stimulate the muscles in the intestines, leading to a rapid increase in bowel movements and subsequent vomiting.

This can occur even at low doses, making it essential for individuals using Rhamnus cathartica to be aware of this potential side effect.

4. Leads to diarrhea episodes

Rhamnus cathartica leads to diarrhea episodes due to its anthraquinone glycosides and sennosides content.

These compounds stimulate the bowel movements, increasing peristalsis and water absorption in the intestine, resulting in loose stools or watery diarrhea.

The intense stimulation of the intestinal muscles can also cause abdominal cramps and discomfort, further exacerbating the gastrointestinal symptoms.

5. Produces excessive sweating

Rhamnus cathartica produces excessive sweating due to its high content of anthraquinone glycosides.

These compounds stimulate the sweat glands, causing an increase in perspiration. Additionally, the fruit and leaves of Rhamnus cathartica contain sennoside A and B, which are natural laxatives that can also contribute to the excessive sweating by stimulating the body's thermoregulatory response.

As a result, individuals using Rhamnus cathartica may experience an increased need for hydration and a higher risk of dehydration.

6. Causes skin irritation

Rhamnus cathartica causes skin irritation.

The plant's sap and leaves contain a high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides, which can cause allergic contact dermatitis in some individuals. When the skin comes into contact with these compounds, it may lead to redness, itching, and rashes.

Additionally, the plant's tannins and other phenolic compounds can also contribute to skin irritation by increasing the production of histamine and other chemical mediators that exacerbate allergic reactions.

7. Elicits anxiety symptoms

Rhamnus cathartica elicits anxiety symptoms due to its high concentration of alkaloids, such as berberine and anthraquinones.

These compounds can stimulate the nervous system, leading to increased heart rate, palpitations, and feelings of unease or panic.

Additionally, the plant's ability to alter gut motility and affect serotonin levels may also contribute to anxiety-like symptoms.

8. Provokes dizziness attacks

Rhamnus cathartica provokes dizziness attacks due to its ability to interact with certain medications, particularly those affecting the central nervous system.

The plant's active compounds, such as anthraquinones and flavonoids, can alter the body's electrolyte balance, leading to changes in heart rate and blood pressure, which may cause episodes of dizziness or lightheadedness.

Additionally, the plant's stimulant properties may also contribute to increased heart rate and palpitations, further exacerbating the risk of dizziness attacks.

9. Triggers headaches persistently

Rhamnus cathartica triggers headaches persistently due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides.

These compounds can irritate the gastrointestinal tract and cause an imbalance in the body's natural flora, leading to increased blood flow and pressure in the head and brain.

As a result, individuals may experience recurring headaches as their body attempts to compensate for the altered bodily functions.

10. Increases blood pressure

Rhamnus cathartica increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides, such as emodin and aloe-emodin.

These compounds stimulate the smooth muscle in blood vessels, leading to vasoconstriction and an increase in blood pressure.

Additionally, Rhamnus cathartica may also cause increased heart rate and cardiac output, further contributing to elevated blood pressure levels.
