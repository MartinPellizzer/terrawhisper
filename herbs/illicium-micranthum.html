Illicium micranthum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illicium micranthum has small, leathery, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along its woody stems. These leaves typically measure between one and three centimeters in length and possess entire margins with acute tips. The plant features tiny, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the foliage. Its woody structure is characterized by slender branches that support a dense, shrubby growth habit. The fruit develops into small, star-shaped follicles that are characteristic of the Illiciaceae family.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe neurological toxicity, including seizures, convulsions, and potential death, if ingested due to the presence of toxic compounds like anisatin. These neurotoxins act on the central nervous system to induce unpredictable and dangerous physical reactions. Symptoms often manifest as intense abdominal pain, vomiting, and dizziness shortly after consumption. Because the concentration of these toxins can vary, even small amounts may lead to life-threatening medical emergencies. It is critical to avoid consuming any part of this species to prevent serious poisoning.
