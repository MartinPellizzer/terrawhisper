Illicium micranthum

What's the taxonomical classification of Illicium micranthum?

Illicium micranthum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Austrobaileyales and the family Schisandraceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Illicium, with its specific designation being the species micranthum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Illicium micranthum has small, leathery, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along its woody stems.

These leaves typically measure between one and three centimeters in length and possess entire margins with acute tips. The plant features tiny, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the axils of the foliage. Its woody structure is characterized by slender branches that support a dense, shrubby growth habit.

The fruit develops into small, star-shaped follicles that are characteristic of the Illiciaceae family.

        What are the herbal preparations of Illicium micranthum?

        Illicium micranthum is prepared as a decoction, a powder, or a topical paste depending on the intended medicinal application. The dried parts of the plant are often boiled in water to create a concentrated liquid infusion used for internal consumption. Alternatively, the material can be ground into a fine powder to be mixed with honey or warm water. For external issues such as skin irritation, the plant is crushed into a paste and applied directly to the affected area. These various methods allow practitioners to tailor the dosage and delivery method to the specific ailment being treated.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Illicium micranthum can cause gastrointestinal distress, neurological impairment, and potentially fatal toxicity if ingested due to the presence of toxic compounds like shikimic acid derivatives or unknown alkaloids. While some species in the Illicium genus are used in traditional medicine, the specific chemical profile of this plant can trigger severe nausea and vomiting. Ingestion may lead to more serious central nervous system effects, including dizziness or confusion. Because the exact levels of toxicity vary between individual plants, consuming them poses a significant risk of poisoning. It is essential to avoid ingestion unless the plant has been professionally identified and cleared for consumption.
