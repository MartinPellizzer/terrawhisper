Senna Tree (Senna Aciphylla)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Senna aciphylla.

*Senna aciphylla*, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a small shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula, often found in arid and semi-arid regions. It has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its various therapeutic properties, particularly for its laxative effects due to the presence of saponins and anthraquinone compounds. The leaves and pods of the plant are typically harvested and prepared as decoctions or infusions to treat constipation and other digestive ailments. In traditional herbal practices, *Senna aciphylla* is also believed to have anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties, making it useful in treating gastrointestinal discomfort. However, prolonged use can lead to dependency and electrolyte imbalances, highlighting the need for caution and proper guidance when using it for medicinal purposes.
