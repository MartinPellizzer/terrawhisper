What's the taxonomical classification of Patrinia intermedia?

Patrinia intermedia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Dipsacales and belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Patriina, and its specific species designation, intermedia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Patrinia intermedia has an upright, herbaceous perennial habit characterized by slender, branching stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 100 centimeters. Small, white to cream-colored flowers emerge in terminal, branched cymes consisting of many individual blossoms. Each flower features five delicate petals and prominent stamens that extend beyond the corolla. The root system consists of a woody rhizome that allows the plant to persist through seasonal changes.
