What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa laevigata?

Rosa laevigata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Rosales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Rosaceae and the genus Rosa, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species laeviagata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa laevigatura has pinnately compound leaves composed of five to seven elliptical leaflets with smooth, entire margins and a distinctively glossy, glabrous upper surface. The plant features woody stems that are typically armed with sharp, curved prickles distributed along the length of the branches. Its flowers are actinomorphic and generally present in a pale pink to white coloration with five distinct petals. The fruit develops into a fleshy, globose hip that transitions from green to a bright red color upon maturity. Small, glandular structures may occasionally be observed on the pedicels or the base of the sepals.
