Gypsophila pacifica

What's the taxonomical classification of Gypsophila pacifica?

Gypsophila pacifica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated under the order Caryophyllales. It is further categorized into the family Caryophyllaceae, falling under the genus Gypsophila, and is specifically identified by the species name pacifica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gypsophila pacifica has slender, branching stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and support small, opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves. The foliage is generally light green and possesses a delicate, fine-textured appearance. At the terminal ends of these stems, the plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. These blossoms are often held in airy, cyme-like inflorescences that give the species its characteristic cloud-like look. The root system consists of a woody caudex that allows the plant to persist in its coastal habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to a small region of coastal California, specifically occurring in Monterey and San Luis Obispo counties. Its range is largely restricted to the maritime terraces and coastal bluffs along the Pacific shoreline. These populations typically thrive in sandy soils that are influenced by the immediate proximity of the ocean. Because it is native to these specific coastal microclimates, it is not found naturally in inland or mountainous areas. This limited distribution makes the species highly specialized to the unique environmental conditions of the central California coast.
