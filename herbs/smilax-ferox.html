Smilax ferox

What's the taxonomical classification of Smilax ferox?

Smilax ferox belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this botanical lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Liliales and the family Smilacaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Smilax, and its unique species designation, ferox.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax ferox has woody, climbing stems characterized by sharp, recurved prickles and trifoliate leaves that feature three distinct leaflets with serrated margins. These leaves are typically leathery in texture and can range from ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters that eventually develop into fleshy, red berries. Along the stem, the presence of axillary thorns assists the vine in scaling surrounding vegetation. The root system consists of a robust rhizome that allows the plant to spread through the soil.
