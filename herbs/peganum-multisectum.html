Peganum multisectum

What's the taxonomical classification of Peganum multisectum?

Peganum multisectum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specialized evolutionary lineage within the order Sapindales. This plant is further categorized under the family Nitrariaceae, which places it in the genus Peganum, specifically identified by its unique species epithet, multisectum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Peganum multisectum has deeply divided, pinnatisect leaves that are characterized by numerous narrow, linear segments arranged along a central axis.

These leaves are typically arranged in an alternate or basal rosette pattern depending on the stage of growth. The plant structure is herbaceous, often exhibiting a spreading or decumbent habit across its habitat. Small, inconspicuous flowers may emerge from the stem, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

The overall morphology is defined by the intricate, feathery texture provided by its highly dissected foliage.
