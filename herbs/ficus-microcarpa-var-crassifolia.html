Ficus microcarpa var. crassifolia

Ficus microcarpa var. crassifolia, commonly known as the Banyan fig or Chinese banyan, is a deciduous tree species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is prized for its medicinal properties, particularly its leaves, which contain compounds like flavonoids and alkaloids that exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant activities.

The plant has been traditionally used to treat various health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and respiratory issues, due to its potential health benefits in reducing inflammation, improving immune function, and promoting wound healing.

In herbal preparations, the leaves of Ficus microcarpa var. crassifolia are often used to make teas, infusions, and decoctions, which are believed to offer relief from various ailments and promote overall well-being.
