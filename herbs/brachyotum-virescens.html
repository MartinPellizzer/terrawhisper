What is Brachyotum virescens?

Brachyotum virescens is a species of plant in the family Melastomataceae, native to the Andes mountain range.

It is a shrub that grows up to 1 meter in height, with ovate leaves that are 6-12 centimeters long and 3-6 centimeters wide, having a petiole 1-2 centimeters long and a cuneate base. The inflorescence is a terminal panicle, with white or pale yellow flowers that are 1.5-2 centimeters in diameter, having 5-6 petals and 10-12 stamens. The flowers are hermaphroditic and have a calyx of 5-6 lobes.

The fruit is a small capsule, containing 5-6 seeds.
