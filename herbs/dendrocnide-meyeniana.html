What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrocnide meyeniana?

Dendrocnide meyeniana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the lineage, it is part of the order Rosales and falls under the family Urticaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Dendrocnide, and its species designation, meyeniana.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Dendrocnide meyeniana can cause severe skin irritation, blistering, and intense localized pain upon contact with its caustic sap. The plant contains stinging cells that inject toxins into the skin, leading to immediate redness and swelling. If the sap enters the eyes, it may cause significant inflammation or even permanent corneal damage. Ingestion of any part of the plant can result in gastrointestinal distress or more serious systemic toxicity. Users should always wear protective clothing to avoid accidental exposure to these hazardous chemical compounds.
