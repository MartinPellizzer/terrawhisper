What's the taxonomical classification of Streblus asper?

Streblus asper belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Rosales and belongs to the family Moraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Streblus, and its specific species epithet, asper.

As discussed in a study, the combined flowers, leaves, and twigs of Streblus asper contain one new non-cytotoxiic [(+)-17β-hydroxystrebloside (1)] and two known cytotoxic cardiac glycosides, specifically [(+)-3'-de-O-methylkamaloside (2) and (+)-strebloside (3)]. A new 14,21-epoxycardanolide (3a) was also synthesized from compound 3 through base treatment. The cytotoxic compound 3 docks in the Na+/K+-ATPase pocket and may form hydrogen bonds using its C-10 formyl and C-5, C-14, and C-4' hydroxy groups with the side-chains of Glu111, Glu117, Thr797, and Arg880. While compound 3 inhibits Na+/K+-ATPase, it does not affect glucose uptake in human lung cancer cells. This indicates that the cardiac glycoside 3 mediates its cytotoxicity by targeting Na+/K+-ATPase rather than interacting with glucose transporters.
