Black Cumin (Nigella sativa)

Black Cumin (Nigella sativa) is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, native to Southwest Asia, Middle East, and Central Asia. Traditionally, its seeds, leaves, and flowers have been used for powders, infusions, and decoctions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, carminative, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in unani medicine, ayurvedic medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.
