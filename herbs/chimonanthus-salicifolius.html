Chimonanthus salicifolius

What's the taxonomical classification of Chimonanthus salicifolius?

Chimonanthus salicifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy places it in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Laurales. Furthermore, it is a member of the family Calycanthaceae, specifically situated within the genus Chimonanthus, which concludes with its unique identification as the species salicifolius.

Chimonanthus salicifolius has lanceolate leaves with entire margins, a woody stem structure, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in terminal inflorescences. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds eucalyptol, isoquercitrin, kaempferol-7-O-rutinoside, nicotiflorin, and various terpenoids. Eucalyptol is a prominent monoterpenoid known for its significant antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties. The flavonoids isoquercitrin and kaempferol-7-O-rutinoside contribute to the plant's potent antioxidant activity. Additionally, the presence of nicotiflorin provides further bioactive benefits to the chemical profile. These combined terpenoids and phenolic compounds work together to support various therapeutic applications.

As discussed in a study, Chimonanthus salicifolius contains essential oils with various medicinal properties extracted from its flowers, stems, and leaves. The leaf essential oil of Chimonanthus salicifolius has the highest extraction rate at 2.22% and contains eucalyptol as the main component with a relative content of 54.65 ± 1.03%. Regarding antioxidant activities, the leaf essential oil exhibits a strong •OH scavenging activity with an IC50 of 39.47 ± 5.57 μL·mL-1, while the stem shows the highest DPPH scavenging activity with an IC50 of 20.78 ± 3.86. Additionally, the leaf essential oil has a minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) of 50 µL·mL-1 against Staphylococcus aureus, Bacillus pumilus, and Bacillus subtilis.
