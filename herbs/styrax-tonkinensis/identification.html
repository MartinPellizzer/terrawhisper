Botanical Identification of Tonkin Styrax (Styrax tonkinensis)

To identify Tonkin Styrax (Styrax tonkinensis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Tonkin Styrax, or Styrax tonkinensis, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Magnoliopsida, order Saxifragales, family Styracaceae, genus Styrax, and species Styrax tonkinensis. It is a flowering plant known for its aromatic resin and is used in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The botanical name of Tonkin Styrax is Styrax tonkinensis, first described by the French botanist Henri Ernest Baillot in 1876. It belongs to the family Styracaceae and is also known by several synonyms, including Styrax tonkinensis var. rotundifolia and Styrax rotundifolia. Common names include Tonkin storax and Vietnamese storax, while regional names vary across Southeast Asian countries.

Morphological Characteristics

Tonkin Styrax is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree. It has a fibrous root system that anchors it firmly. The stem is straight and often branches near the base. Leaves are alternate, elliptic, and glossy with a leathery texture. The inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small white or pinkish flowers. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Styrax tonkinensis can be distinguished by its elliptical, glossy leaves with a prominent midrib, pale yellow flowers in clusters, and a fragrant, resinous bark. It differs from similar species like Styrax japonicus by its smaller, more elongated leaves and the presence of a distinctive, aromatic resin. The bark is smoother and lighter in color compared to other styrax species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Styrax tonkinensis is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and southern China. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. It prefers well-drained, fertile soils and is typically found at altitudes between 300 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Tonkin Styrax blooms from April to June, producing small white flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the plant is best harvested during its flowering period for accurate botanical recognition.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Styrax tonkinensis is closely related to species like Styrax benzoin and Styrax japonicus. It belongs to the genus Styrax within the family Styracaceae. The family is characterized by its aromatic resins and simple, opposite leaves, traits shared with other members of the genus.
