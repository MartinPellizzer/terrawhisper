10 Medicinal Constituents Of Ballota Nigra (Black Horehound)

Ballota nigra has active constituents such as iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and triterpenic acids.

These compounds contribute to the plant's medicinal properties, including expectorant, anti-inflammatory, and antiseptic activities. The iridoid glycosides help loosen and remove mucus from the respiratory tract, while the flavonoids and triterpenic acids exhibit antimicrobial and antioxidant effects.

As a result, black horehound has been traditionally used to treat coughs, bronchitis, and other respiratory issues, helping to alleviate symptoms and improve breathing function in individuals suffering from chronic respiratory diseases.

1. Ursolic acid

Ballota nigra ursolic acid is a bioactive compound extracted from its leaves.

It has been extensively studied for its numerous pharmacological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties. Ursolic acid has also been shown to exhibit antitumor and anticancer effects by inhibiting the growth of cancer cells and inducing apoptosis.

Additionally, it has been reported to have neuroprotective potential against Alzheimer's disease and Parkinson's disease.

2. Oleanolic acid

Ballota nigra oleanolic acid is a triterpenoid compound that has been isolated from the plant's leaves and stems.

It exhibits a range of biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antitumor properties. Oleanolic acid has been shown to inhibit the growth of cancer cells and induce apoptosis (cell death) in various tumor cell lines.

Its potential therapeutic applications include treating inflammatory diseases, such as arthritis, and combating cancer.

3. Quercetin

Ballota nigra quercetin is a flavonoid compound derived from the plant.

Quercetin has been traditionally used in herbal medicine to treat various ailments such as inflammation, allergies, and respiratory issues. It is known for its antioxidant properties, which help protect against oxidative stress and damage caused by free radicals.

Quercetin also exhibits anti-inflammatory activity, making it a potential natural remedy for chronic diseases.
