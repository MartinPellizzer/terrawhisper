Side Effects of geranium maculatum

Geranium maculatum induces allergic reaction in some individuals, manifesting as sneezing, runny nose, and even difficulty breathing, particularly in those with a history of allergies or asthma.

Prolonged contact with the plant can result in eye irritation, causing redness, tearing, and a burning sensation, which may persist if not properly addressed. The herb also induces skin rash and causes skin irritation, often presenting as red, itchy patches or blisters upon direct contact with the leaves or stems.

In more severe cases, it may trigger respiratory issues such as coughing, wheezing, or shortness of breath, especially when the plant is crushed or inhaled as pollen. Additionally, Geranium maculatum can lead to stomach upset, nausea, and throat irritation when ingested, and some individuals may experience dry mouth or blurred vision as secondary effects.

These symptoms highlight the importance of handling the plant with care and avoiding ingestion, particularly for those with known sensitivities or allergies.
