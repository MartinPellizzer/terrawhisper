Cyperus iria

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyperus iria?

Cyperus iria belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Poales. This plant is classified under the family Cyperaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Cyperus, with its unique designation being the species iria.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus iria has a creeping rhizomatous root system that produces tufted, triangular stems reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and emerge from the base of the plant in a basal arrangement. At the top of each stem, a compact, globose inflorescence consists of numerous small, brownish flowers clustered into a head. These flower clusters are subtended by several leaf-like bracts that spread outwards from the base of the umbel. The overall structure is compact and adapted for growth in moist or saturated soil environments.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, commonly known as yellow flatsedge, thrives in saturated soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is a perennial sedge that spreads via underground rhizomes to form dense, grassy clumps. To cultivate it successfully, you must provide a consistently wet environment, such as a bog, pond margin, or shallow wetland area.
