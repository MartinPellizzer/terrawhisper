What's the taxonomical classification of Glochidion lanceolarium?

Glochidion lanceolarium belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Phyllanthaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Glochidion, and its unique species designation, lanceolarium.

What are the pharmacological activities of Glochidion lanceolarium?

This plant has demonstrated significant antibacterial and anti-biofilm pharmacological activities through the action of its bioactive secondary metabolites. These properties allow the plant extracts to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacterial strains by disrupting cellular functions. Furthermore, the extracts effectively prevent the formation of protective extracellular matrices that bacteria use to shield themselves. Such multifaceted actions highlight its therapeutic potential in combating drug-resistant microbial infections.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in microbiology", the leaf extracts of Glochidion lanceolarium contain phytoconstituents including flavonoids, phenolic compounds, phytosterols, and glycosides.
