Glochidion lanceolarium

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Glochidion lanceolarium has lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 15 centimeters long, featuring entire margins and acute apices. The foliage is characterized by a distinctively glossy upper surface and a paler, sometimes slightly hairy underside. Its woody stems exhibit a brownish to grayish bark texture that protects the internal vascular structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often borne in axillary clusters. These reproductive structures eventually develop into small, fleshy fruits that are essential for the species' dispersal.
