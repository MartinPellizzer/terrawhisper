Chrysanthemum mongolicum

What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysanthemum mongolicum?

Chrysanthemum mongolicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae and is further categorized under the genus Chrysanthemum, ultimately identifying as the species mongolicum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysanthemum mongolicum has an erect, perennial herbaceous structure characterized by stems reaching 20 to 60 centimeters in height with deeply lobed, serrated leaves and small, yellow, disc-shaped flower heads. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate and feature prominent veins along their surfaces. Its flowering heads consist of numerous tiny tubular florets that cluster together to form a dense, yellow center. The plant often develops a bushy appearance due to its branched stem system and dense foliage. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in various temperate grassland environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the temperate regions of East Asia, specifically spanning across northern China, Mongolia, Korea, and parts of the Russian Far East. It thrives primarily in mountainous areas and open grasslands where the soil is well-drained. You can often find populations growing in sunny meadows or along the edges of rocky slopes. The species is particularly well-adapted to the continental climates found in these northern latitudes. Its distribution is closely linked to the specific altitudinal zones of the Eurasian steppe and highland ecosystems.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant requires well-drained, sandy loam soil and full sun exposure to thrive in temperate climates.

It is typically propagated through seeds or division of the rootstock during the early spring. Growers must ensure the soil remains moist but never waterlogged to prevent root rot. Because it is a hardy perennial, it can tolerate cold temperatures once established in the ground.

Regular pruning helps maintain a compact shape and encourages vigorous flowering.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse profile of bioactive phytochemicals including acaacetin, chrysoeriol, genkwanin, kaempferol-3-O-gentiobioside, and linarin. These specific medicinal compounds are primarily classified as flavonoids and glycosides that contribute to the herb's therapeutic properties. The presence of these substances is often linked to the plant's ability to provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects in traditional medicine. Researchers study these individual molecules to understand how they interact with biological systems to promote healing. Together, these chemical constituents define the unique pharmacological identity of Chrysanthemum mongolicum.

According to a study published by "Phytochemical analysis : PCA", a comparative metabolomics analysis identified 477 metabolites in total. Among these, 72 metabolites showed significant differences in expression, primarily involving flavonoids, organic acids, and nucleotides. The study highlighted specific compounds such as genkwanin, trigonelline, diosmin, narcissoside, 3,4-dihydroxyphenylacetic acid, linarin, N',N'-p-coumarin, C-hexosyl-tricetin O-pentoside, chrysoeriol, acacetin, and kaempferol-3-O-gentiobioside. These specific contents represent potential biomarkers within the plant. The flavone and flavonol biosynthesis pathway showed significant enrichment among the differentially expressed metabolites.
