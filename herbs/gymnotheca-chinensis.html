Gymnotheca chinensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gymnotheca chinensis has an erect, perennial herbaceous habit characterized by stem diameters that reach up to 2 centimeters and leaves that are primarily ovate to lanceolate with serrated margins. The stems are typically glabrous or slightly pubescent, supporting alternate leaf arrangements along the length of the plant. Its flowers are small and often clustered in axillary inflorescences, displaying a structure consistent with the Euphorbiaceae family. The fruit is a characteristic capsule that splits upon maturity to release small, often shiny seeds. These morphological features collectively distinguish the species within its tropical and subtropical habitats.
