Lyonia villosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lyonia villosa has a woody, perennial habit characterized by erect stems that typically reach heights of 0.5 to 1 meter and are covered in fine, spreading hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical in shape, and possess serrated margins with distinct pubescence on both surfaces. Small, white, four-petaled flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters during the summer months. These blossoms give way to small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark color upon maturation. The root system is well-developed to support its growth in moist, shaded forest environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest, ranging from southern Alaska through British Columbia and down to northern California. It primarily occupies moist, shaded environments such as temperate rainforests, coastal thickets, and riparian zones. Within these habitats, it is often found growing in dense understory layers where moisture levels remain consistently high. Its distribution is closely tied to the maritime climate and the presence of well-drained, organic-rich soils. This specific ecological niche limits its widespread presence to the narrow strip of the Pacific coast.
