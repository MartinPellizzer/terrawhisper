Lyonia villosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Lyonia villosa?

Lyonia villosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Ericales, it falls into the family Ericaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Lyonia and the species epithet villosa.

    Lyonia villosa is prepared primarily as a decoction or infusion using the dried leaves and stems to extract its active constituents.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Lyonia villosa can cause skin irritation, redness, and allergic contact dermatitis upon direct physical contact with its foliage or stems. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience localized swelling or a burning sensation after touching the plant. Some users might also encounter respiratory discomfort if the plant's pollen or fine particles become airborne and inhaled. While systemic toxicity is not extensively documented, cautious handling is advised to prevent unpredictable dermatological reactions. Always use protective gloves when working with this species to minimize the risk of skin damage.
