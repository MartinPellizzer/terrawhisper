Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens)

Chili Pepper (Capsicum frutescens) is a member of the Solanaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, Australia, and Pacific Islands. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and seeds have been used for culinary uses, decoctions, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, stimulant, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, african traditional medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Leaves
Simple leaves with acute apices, upper surface glabrous and glossy green, lower surface duller and slightly pubescent with stomatal bands.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, with five white petals and five yellow sepals arranged in a solitary cluster, featuring a central ovary surrounded by stamens.
Stems
Erect, branched, woody, hairy, with opposite leaf arrangement and spiny ridges.
