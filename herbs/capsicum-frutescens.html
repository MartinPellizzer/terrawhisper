What to know about Capsicum frutescens before using it medicinally

Capsicum frutescens, commonly known as malagueta pepper, is a herb used for its medicinal properties in treating various health conditions, including pain management, inflammation, and infections.

It is valued for its health benefits, which include reducing fever, relieving headaches, and improving digestion. The herb is attributed to several therapeutic properties, including analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial effects. The bioactive compounds present in Capsicum frutescens include capsaicin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

In traditional medicine, Capsicum frutescens is often prepared as a paste or powder and used to make a condiment known as peri-peri sauce, which is used to add flavor and heat to various dishes.

Featured Study:

According to a study published by "Research communications in chemical pathology and pharmacology", Capsicum frutescens was found to have a mild effect on experimental animals when given orally for a relatively longer period. The study revealed that Capsicum frutescens reduced plasma levels of urea nitrogen, glucose, phospholipids, triglycerides, total cholesterol, free fatty acids, glutamic pyruvic transaminase, and alkaline phosphatase after one month of oral administration. Additionally, the study observed that Capsicum frutescens increased food intake in both experimental groups throughout the experimental periods.
