Capparis pubiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Capparis pubiflora?

Capparis pubiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Brassicales. The plant is further categorized into the family Capparaceae, placing it within the genus Capparis, and is specifically identified by its species name, pubiflora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Capparis pubiflora has woody stems, pinnate leaves with multiple leaflets, and small white flowers characterized by numerous long, protruding stamens. The foliage consists of elliptic or oblong leaflets that often exhibit a slightly hairy or pubescent texture on their surfaces. Its inflorescence typically features solitary or small clusters of flowers emerging from the leaf axils. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that develop from the fertilized ovaries of the blossoms.
