Cayratia geniculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Cayratia geniculata?

Cayratiia geniculaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Vitales and the family Vitaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Cayratia and the species geniculata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cayratiia geniculaata contains bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, saponins, flavonoids, and tannins that contribute to its pharmacological properties. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities used in traditional medicine. Researchers have identified specific glycosides within the plant that may assist in regulating physiological processes. Studies also suggest that the presence of polyphenolic compounds offers antioxidant protection against cellular damage. This complex chemical profile supports the plant's traditional use for treating various ailments such as skin infections and gastrointestinal issues.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cayratia geniculata?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, urinary tract problems, gastrointestinal issues, and inflammatory conditions. Its leaves are frequently applied topically to address wounds, sores, and various skin irritations. When prepared as a decoction, the plant serves as a remedy for stomach aches and intestinal disturbances. It is also utilized in traditional medicine to help manage symptoms of urinary ailments. The plant's pharmacological properties contribute to its widespread use in managing systemic inflammation.
