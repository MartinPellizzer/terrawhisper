Cayratiia geniculaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Vitales and the family Vitaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Cayratia and the species geniculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cayratiia geniculaata has climbing stems that are distinctly geniculate, meaning they possess sharp, knee-like bends at the nodes, and cordate leaves that are typically petiolate. The foliage features a heart-shaped blade with a smooth or slightly wavy margin and a base that is deeply notched. Its inflorescence consists of small, pale flowers arranged in axillary clusters, often appearing in shades of white or light cream. The plant utilizes its twining stems to ascend surrounding vegetation through its specialized growth habit. Small, fleshy berries develop from the flowers as the plant reaches maturity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds cayratioside, ent-labdane glucoside, and various glycoside derivatives. These bioactive substances are primary contributors to the plant's documented pharmacological activities. Researchers have identified these specific molecules through detailed phytochemical investigations of the species. The presence of these glycoside derivatives often correlates with the plant's traditional uses in herbal medicine. Such chemical profiles are essential for understanding how the plant interacts with biological systems.

According to a study published by "Journal of Asian natural products research", six glycoside derivatives, numbered 1-6, were isolated from the methanol extract of Cayratiya geniculaata stems and leaves. These compounds include one new ent-labdane glucoside named cayratioside, which is identified as compound 1. The structures of these 6 derivatives were elucidated using 1D, 2D NMR, and HRESIQTOF mass spectra. The inhibitory effect of compounds 1-6 on LPS-induced NO production in RAW264.7 cells was evaluated. Among these isolated substances, compound 1 exhibited moderate activity with an IC50 value of 59.65 ± 1.85 µM.
