What's the taxonomical classification of Stephania sutchuenensis?

Stephania sutchuenenis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Menispermaceae. Finally, this plant is identified by its specific genus, Stephania, and its unique species designation, sutchuenenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

The reproductive organs are specialized for specific pollinators, featuring delicate petals and a central reproductive column. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 1-nitroaknadinine, aknadiniine, and sinococuline. These specific alkaloids are recognized for their distinct biochemical properties within the plant's chemical profile. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential therapeutic applications in pharmacology. The presence of such complex molecules highlights the intricate chemical makeup of the species. Understanding these components is essential for developing new medicinal treatments.

As discussed in a study, three alkaloids isolated from Stephania sutchuenensis are aknadinine, 1-nitroaknadinine, and sinococuline. Sinococuline acts as an effective tumor cell growth inhibitor, while the toxicity of aknadinine and 1-nitroaknadinine is low towards all tested cells. When cells were grown in the presence of sinococuline for 24 h, a dose-dependent decrease in cell viability and in the uptake of tritidate-thymidine, -leucine, and -uridine was observed. The study used human leukaeemic HL60 cells and mouse fibroblast L929 cells to examine the mechanism of cytotoxicity. These findings suggest that apoptosis is the mode of death induced by sinococuline.
