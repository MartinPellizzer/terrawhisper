What's the taxonomical classification of Vanilla somae?

Vanilla somae belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asparagales. Within this order, it is situated in the family Orchidaceae, a group renowned for its diverse and specialized floral structures. Finally, the plant is identified under the genus Vanilla, with its specific designation being the species somae.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vanilla somae has succulent, climbing stems that can reach several meters in length and are characterized by thick, fleshy leaves arranged in an alternate pattern. The leaves are typically lanceolate or elliptic in shape with entire margins and a smooth, waxy texture. Small, aerial roots emerge from the stem nodes to assist the plant in anchoring itself to host trees for support. Its inflorescence consists of small, pale flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season. The reproductive structures eventually develop into elongated, fleshy green pods that contain numerous tiny seeds.
