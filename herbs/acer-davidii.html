Acer davidii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer davidii has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that typically feature three to five distinct lobes and serrated margins. The leaf blades are often ovate or broadly triangular in shape, measuring several centimeters in length. Its bark is characterized by a smooth, greyish texture that becomes more fissured as the tree matures. The plant produces small, winged samaras that develop in clusters from inconspicuous flowers. These fruit structures facilitate wind dispersal, which is a key trait of the maple genus.
