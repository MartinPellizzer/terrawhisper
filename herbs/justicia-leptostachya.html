What's the taxonomical classification of Justicia leptostachya?

Justicia leptostachya belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales and falls within the family Acanthaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Justicia, with its unique designation being the species leptostachyia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Justicia leptostachya has opposite, lanceolate leaves with entire margins and long, slender, spike-like inflorescences that emerge from the leaf axils.

The foliage typically displays a deep green color and a smooth texture on the upper surface. Its flowering structure consists of small, tubular flowers arranged densely along a thin, elongated rachis. The stems are herbaceous and square in cross-section, which is a characteristic trait of the Acanthaceae family.

These botanical features combine to create a distinctive upright growth habit.
