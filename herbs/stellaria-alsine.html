Stellaria alsine

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stellaria alsine has slender, creeping stems that branch frequently and bear small, opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins. The foliage is typically bright green and lacks significant pubescence, giving the plant a smooth appearance. Its flowers are white and possess five distinct petals that are often deeply notched at the apex. These blossoms are supported by thin pedicels and emerge from the leaf axils. Small, rounded capsules serve as the fruit, eventually releasing tiny seeds to facilitate reproduction.

What are the therapeutic actions of Stellaria alsine?

Stellaria alsine has diuretic, astringent, hemostatic, and anti-inflammatory properties that allow it to assist in various physiological processes. Its diuretic action promotes the excretion of excess fluids and salts through the kidneys to reduce swelling. The astringent qualities of the plant help to constrict body tissues, which is particularly useful for treating skin irritations or mucosal membranes. Additionally, its hemostatic properties enable it to effectively slow down or stop bleeding in minor wounds. These combined effects, along with its ability to act as an anti-inflammatory, make it a useful component in traditional herbal remedies.
