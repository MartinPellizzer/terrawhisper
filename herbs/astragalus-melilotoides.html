Astragalus melilotoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Astragalus melilotoides?

Astragalus melilotoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is positioned in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Fabales, the plant is a member of the family Fabaceae. It is further categorized into the genus Astragalus, and its specific designation is the species melilotoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Astragalus melilotoides has a perennial rootstock that produces erect or ascending stems reaching heights of 10 to 60 centimeters, characterized by trifoliate leaves and small, papilionaceous, purple-to-pink flowers. Each leaf consists of three leaflets that are typically oblong to elliptic in shape with smooth margins. The inflorescences are arranged in dense, elongated racemes that emerge from the leaf axils. The individual flowers possess a standard petal, two wing petals, and a keel that together form the characteristic legume structure. Small, flat, or slightly inflated pods develop from the flowers and contain several seeds.

    What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause severe neurological damage and respiratory failure in humans and animals due to the presence of the alkaloid coumarin, which converts into the toxic substance dicoumarol. When consumed, this compound acts as an anticoagulant that significantly interferes with the body's ability to clot blood. Such an effect can lead to spontaneous internal bleeding, bruising, and prolonged hemorrhaging from minor wounds. High doses or chronic exposure may result in life-threatening complications involving the brain or lungs. It is essential to avoid contact or ingestion to prevent these dangerous physiological reactions.
