Celtis soyauxii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Celtis soyauxii has alternate, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins and prominent pinnate venation. The leaf base is often asymmetrical, which is a characteristic feature of many species within the Celtidaceae family. Its branches are characterized by slender, woody stems that may exhibit lenticels on the bark surface. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into small, fleshy drupes. These fruits serve as a primary morphological identifier for the species during its fruiting season.

    What are the herbal preparations of Celtis soyauxii?

    Celtis soyauxii is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the bark, leaves, or roots in water to extract medicinal compounds. This aqueous extraction is the most traditional method used in folk medicine to treat various ailments. In some regional practices, the plant materials may also be processed into a topical paste by grinding the fresh leaves.     What side-effects this plant can have?

    Celtis soyauxii can cause adverse physiological reactions such as skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or digestive distress if consumed or applied improperly. While many species in the Celtis genus are used in traditional medicine, the specific chemical constituents of this plant may trigger hypersensitivity in certain individuals. Contact with the sap or bark can lead to redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. Ingesting parts of the plant without expert guidance poses a risk of nausea or stomach cramping. Always consult a medical professional or toxicologist before using any wild plant for medicinal purposes.
