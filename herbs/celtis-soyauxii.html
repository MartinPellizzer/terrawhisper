Celtis soyauxii

What's the taxonomical classification of Celtis soyauxii?

Celtis soyauxii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Rosales, the plant is a member of the family Cannabaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its membership in the genus Celtis, with its unique designation being the species soyauxii.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, gastrointestinal issues, respiratory ailments, and various inflammatory conditions. The bark and leaves are often prepared into decoctions or pastes to alleviate symptoms of stomach aches and diarrhea. When applied topically, the botanical extracts help soothe wounds and clear up persistent skin rashes. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to manage coughs and other breathing difficulties.
