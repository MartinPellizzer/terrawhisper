What's the taxonomical classification of Tainia penangiana?

Tainia penangiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asparagales, the plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Its taxonomic hierarchy is completed by its designation within the genus Tainia, specifically identifying it as the species penangiana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tainia penangiana has large, solitary, lanceolate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters and possess distinctively parallel venation. These leaves are characterized by a prominent midrib and a smooth, slightly wavy margin that extends from the base to the apex. The plant typically grows as a rhizomatous herb, allowing it to spread through underground stems. Its reproductive structures consist of large, fleshy inflorescences that emerge directly from the leaf bases. The overall structure is robust, designed to thrive in the shaded, moist understory of tropical forests.
