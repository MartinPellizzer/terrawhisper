Tainia penangiana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tainia penangiana has large, solitary, lanceolate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters and possess distinctively parallel venation. The plant typically grows as a rhizomatous herb, allowing it to spread through underground stems. Its reproductive structures consist of large, fleshy inflorescences that emerge directly from the leaf bases. The overall structure is robust, designed to thrive in the shaded, moist understory of tropical forests.
