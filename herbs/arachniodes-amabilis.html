Arachniodes amabilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Arachniodes amabilis has a creeping rhizome that produces small, dark green, ovate to lanceolate leaves arranged in a loose rosette. The leaf blades are thin and membranous with finely serrated margins and prominent venation. These plants typically grow in clusters, forming dense mats across moist forest floors. The reproductive structures consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the base of the foliage. This species maintains a delicate, low-growing profile suited for shaded, humid environments.
