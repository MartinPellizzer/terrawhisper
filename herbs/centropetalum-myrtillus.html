Centropetalum Myrtillus

Centropetalum myrtillus, commonly known as the American myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

Historically, it has been used by Native American tribes for its medicinal properties, particularly for treating respiratory and digestive ailments. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and tannins, which contribute to its therapeutic effects. In traditional medicine, its leaves and berries have been employed to alleviate symptoms of coughs, colds, and inflammation.

While modern scientific research on its medicinal uses is limited, there is ongoing interest in exploring its potential pharmacological benefits.
