Inula helianthus-aquatilis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Inula helianthus-aquatilis has lanceolate, serrated leaves that emerge from a perennial rootstock and produce bright yellow composite flower heads atop stiff, upright stems. The foliage is typically dark green with a slightly textured surface and prominent venation. Each flower head consists of numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. The stems are woody at the base and can reach significant heights depending on the water availability in its aquatic habitat. These structural adaptations allow the plant to stabilize itself in marshy environments while maximizing sunlight exposure.

      What are the herbal preparations of Inula helianthus-aquatilis?

      Inula helianthus-aquatilis is prepared as a concentrated aqueous decoction, a topical poultice made from crushed fresh leaves, or a distilled essential oil for aromatic use. The decoction method involves simmering the dried roots in water to extract potent bitter compounds for internal consumption. For skin ailments, the plant's foliage is macerated into a paste and applied directly to affected areas to utilize its anti-inflammatory properties. The essential oil is carefully extracted through steam distillation to preserve the volatile aromatic components of the flowering parts. These diverse methods allow practitioners to tailor the plant's therapeutic effects to specific medicinal needs.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Inula helianthus-aquatilis can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap often leads to redness, itching, or blistering on sensitive areas of the skin. Some individuals may experience swelling of the throat or difficulty breathing due to acute allergic reactions. If swallowed, the plant can trigger nausea, abdominal cramping, and persistent vomiting. It is essential to use protective gear when handling this species to prevent these adverse reactions.
