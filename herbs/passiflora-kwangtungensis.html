Passiflora kwangtungensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Passiflora kwangtungensis has climbing stems characterized by tendrils, trifoliate leaves with ovate leaflets, and large, complex flowers featuring distinct corona filaments and a prominent central structure. The foliage consists of three leaflets that typically exhibit a smooth or slightly serrated margin and a deep green color. Its inflorescence displays a sophisticated arrangement of petals and sepals that support a highly specialized corona designed to attract specific pollinators. The reproductive organs are housed within a central column, which includes both the ovary and the anthers. These morphological traits are essential for the plant's identification within the complex Passiflora genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the mountainous regions of Guangdong Province in southern China. It is specifically found growing within the subtropical broad-leaved forests that characterize this local landscape. The species thrives in high-altitude habitats where humidity and temperature remain relatively stable throughout the year. Its range is quite restricted compared to other members of the Passiflora genus. Because of this limited distribution, the plant is considered a regional specialist of its specific ecosystem.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Passiflora kwangtungensis targets gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) receptors, monoamine oxidase (MAO) enzymes, and various inflammatory signaling proteins to exert its medicinal effects. Its bioactive flavonoids and alkaloids primarily modulate the GABAergic system to produce sedative and anxiolytic outcomes. Additionally, the plant contains phytochemicals that inhibit cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, which helps reduce systemic inflammation. These compounds also interact with neurotransmitter transporters to influence mood regulation and central nervous system activity. Through these multifaceted protein interactions, the species provides significant pharmacological potential for treating anxiety and oxidative stress.

      What are the herbal preparations of Passiflora kwangtungensis?

      Passiflora kwangtungensis is prepared as a decoction of the leaves, a tea made from the flowers, or a topical paste applied directly to the skin.
