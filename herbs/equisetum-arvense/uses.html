Field Horsetail (Equisetum arvense) Uses

Field Horsetail is used to treat various respiratory infections, including bronchitis, tuberculosis, and respiratory allergies. It helps alleviate symptoms like cold symptoms, allergic rhinitis, and respiratory congestion. The plant may also support recovery from lung infections and other respiratory tract infections. Additionally, it has been applied for skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties.

Field Horsetail (Equisetum arvense) Monograph

1. Respiratory infections

Field Horsetail has been traditionally used for respiratory infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways. Its high silica content supports tissue repair, aiding recovery from conditions like bronchitis and pneumonia. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for internal use.

2. Bronchitis

Field Horsetail is used for bronchitis due to its high silica content, which supports respiratory health and tissue repair. It acts as a demulcent, soothing inflamed bronchial tissues. Its diuretic properties help reduce mucus buildup, easing breathing. It is often combined with other herbs like thyme or echinacea for enhanced respiratory support.

3. Tuberculosis

Field Horsetail has been historically used in herbal medicine for its potential supportive role in tuberculosis treatment. It is believed to aid in respiratory health and may help reduce inflammation in the lungs. Its high silica content and antimicrobial properties are thought to contribute to its traditional use in managing tuberculosis symptoms. However, it should not replace conventional medical treatment.

4. Respiratory allergies

Field Horsetail is used to support respiratory health in cases of allergies due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It helps reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways, making it a useful remedy for seasonal allergies and asthma symptoms. Its high silica content also strengthens respiratory tissues, enhancing overall lung function.

5. Cold symptoms

Field Horsetail is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its high silica content, which supports respiratory health. It may help reduce inflammation and mucus production in the respiratory tract. Often prepared as a tea, it is used to ease coughs and congestion. Its diuretic properties also help flush out toxins associated with colds.

6. Allergic rhinitis

Field Horsetail contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and mucus production, common symptoms of allergic rhinitis. Its astringent properties can soothe irritated nasal passages. Traditionally used in herbal medicine, it is often prepared as a tea or tincture to alleviate seasonal allergy symptoms.

7. Respiratory congestion

Field Horsetail is used to alleviate respiratory congestion due to its diuretic and expectorant properties. It helps reduce mucus buildup and clears the airways, making it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis and coughs. Its high silica content also supports lung tissue health. Regular use can ease breathing and promote clearer respiratory function.

8. Lung infections

Field Horsetail has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly for lung infections. Its high silica content may help strengthen lung tissue, while its antimicrobial properties can aid in fighting bacterial and fungal infections. It is often used in herbal preparations to promote expectoration and reduce inflammation in the respiratory tract.

9. Respiratory tract infections

Field Horsetail has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of coughs and bronchial congestion. Its high silica content may help strengthen respiratory tissues, while its antimicrobial properties may aid in reducing infection severity. It is often used in herbal formulations for its potential to soothe inflammation and promote mucus clearance.

10. Skin infections

Field Horsetail is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce bacterial growth and soothe irritated skin. Often applied as a poultice or infused oil, it supports healing in conditions like eczema and fungal infections. Its high silica content also strengthens skin tissue.
