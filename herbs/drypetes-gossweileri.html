What's the taxonomical classification of Drypetes gossweileri?

Drypetes gossweileri belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the order Malpighiales and belongs to the family Putranjivaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Drypetes, and its specific species designation, gossweileri.

      As discussed in a study, essential oils from Drypetes gossweileri stem bark showed activity against Candida albicans and Candida parapsilopsis with an MIC value of 62.5 µg/mL. These essential oils were fungicidal and demonstrated synergism on C. parapsilopsis and additivity on C. albicans, resulting in a 2 to 64-fold drop in MIC values. When combined with other essential oils, the MIC combination of 31.25/7.81 µg/mL and 1.95/31.25 µg/mL required 20 and 18 h of exposure to effectively kill 99.9% of the inoculum. This fungicidal effect was accompanied by the alteration of the cell walls and membranes of the yeasts.
