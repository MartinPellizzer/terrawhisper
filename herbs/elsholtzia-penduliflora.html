Elsholtzia penduliflora

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Elsholtzia penduliflora has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, ovate leaflets arranged along a central rachis with serrated margins. The tree typically exhibits a spreading or weeping canopy with a trunk characterized by fissured, grayish-brown bark as it matures. Its reproductive structures consist of small, yellowish-green flowers that hang in drooping clusters from the branches. These inflorescences eventually develop into small, winged samaras that facilitate wind dispersal. The overall architecture of the plant reflects its adaptation to temperate forest environments.
