What's the taxonomical classification of Elsholtzia penduliflora?

Elsholtzia penduliflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary placement continues through the order Lamiales and the family Lamiaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Elsholtzia, and its unique species designation, penduliflora.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Elsholtzia penduliflora targets cyclooxygenase enzymes, lipoxygenase proteins, and various proinflammatory cytokines to exert its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive polyphenols and flavonoids that inhibit the arachidonic acid cascade, effectively reducing the production of prostaglandins. By modulating these enzymatic pathways, the extracts help suppress systemic inflammation and alleviate pain. Furthermore, specific phytochemicals in the plant interact with nuclear factor kappa B to downregulate the expression of inflammatory genes. These multi-target interactions contribute to the plant's potential role in treating oxidative stress and inflammatory disorders.
