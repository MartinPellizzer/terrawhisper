10 Medicinal Constituents Of Betula Lenta (Yellow Birch)

Betula lenta has active constituents such as salicin, flavonoids, and phenolic acids.

These constituents contribute to its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antioxidant properties. They can help alleviate pain and reduce inflammation, improving conditions such as arthritis. Additionally, the antioxidants in yellow birch may also protect against chronic diseases like heart disease and cancer.

These medicinal properties can significantly enhance the quality of life for individuals suffering from these conditions.

1. Salicin

Betula lenta salicin is a compound found in its bark.

It is a glycoside that belongs to the salicylic acid family and has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties similar to aspirin. Salicin is responsible for the birch tree's ability to reduce inflammation and pain when ingested or used topically.

It also exhibits antimicrobial and antioxidant activities, making it a valuable component of traditional medicine in some cultures.
