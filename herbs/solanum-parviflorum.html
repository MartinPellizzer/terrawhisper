Solanum parviflorum

Solanum parviflorum, commonly known as small-flowered nightshade, is a plant species within the Solanaceae family, native to North America.

The therapeutic actions of Solanum parviflorum include its potential to reduce pain, fever, and inflammation, as well as its use in traditional medicine to treat various health issues.

Solanum parviflorum has many active constituents, such as alkaloids, glycosides, and phenolic compounds, which have been found to possess various medicinal properties.

Medicinal Parts of Solanum parviflorum

Solanum parviflorum has many medicinal parts, such as the leaves, roots, and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine.

The leaves of Solanum parviflorum are rich in flavonoids, including kaempferol and quercetin, which are known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.

The roots of the plant contain glycosides, like solasodine, which have been found to have antiviral and anti-inflammatory effects.

The fruits of Solanum parviflorum are rich in alkaloids, including solanine and solamargine, which have been used to treat various ailments, including fever and pain.
