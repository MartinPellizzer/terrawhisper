Wild Madder (Rubia peregrina) Compounds

Wild Madder contains several primary compounds, including tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its astringent and antioxidant properties. Coumarin and terpenoids provide anti-inflammatory effects, while anthocyanins add color and potential cardiovascular benefits. Glycosides, saponins, and alkaloids further support its traditional use in herbal medicine. Essential oils contribute to its aromatic profile and therapeutic applications.
