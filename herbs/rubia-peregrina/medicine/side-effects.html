10 Possible Side Effects Of Rubia Peregrina

Rubia peregrina has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal disturbances, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects are usually caused by the plant's anthraquinone content, which can cause stomach upset, diarrhea, and rash in some individuals.

For instance, people who suffer from digestive issues may experience worsening symptoms, while those with skin allergies may experience increased discomfort, making daily activities more challenging.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Rubia peregrina causes allergic reactions due to its chemical composition, specifically the presence of anthraquinone glycosides and alizarin.

These compounds can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as skin rashes, hives, and itching.

Additionally, people may experience respiratory issues like congestion, coughing, and shortness of breath.

2. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Rubia peregrina produces gastrointestinal upset due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides.

These compounds can irritate the mucous membranes in the stomach and intestines, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain.

The absorption of these glycosides can also cause changes in gut motility, resulting in constipation or loose stools, further exacerbating gastrointestinal discomfort.

3. Increases blood pressure

Rubia peregrina increases blood pressure by stimulating the sympathetic nervous system and releasing adrenaline.

This can lead to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output, resulting in elevated blood pressure levels.

Additionally, Rubia peregrina may enhance the body's natural stress response, further contributing to its hypertensive effects.

4. Triggers respiratory distress

Rubia peregrina triggers respiratory distress due to its alkaloid content, particularly anthraquinones.

These compounds can cause irritation and constriction of the airways, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, Rubia peregrina may stimulate the release of histamine, a potent chemical that can exacerbate respiratory issues and worsen breathing difficulties.

5. Induces skin rashes

Rubia peregrina induces skin rashes due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides and alkalioid rubiadin.

These compounds can cause an allergic reaction in some individuals, leading to the development of skin irritation and inflammation.

The rashes may range from mild to severe and can manifest as redness, itching, or blistering on various parts of the body.

6. Affects kidney function

Rubia peregrina affects kidney function.

The consumption of rubia peregrina can lead to an impairment of kidney function due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides, which are known to irritate the kidneys and potentially cause damage over time.

This is particularly concerning for individuals with pre-existing kidney issues or those who have a family history of kidney disease.

7. Interferes with digestion

Rubia peregrina interferes with digestion by affecting the normal functioning of the gastrointestinal tract.

The anthraquinones present in Rubia peregrina can irritate the digestive lining, leading to decreased appetite and altered bowel movements. Additionally, the plant's ability to stimulate the liver may increase bile production, causing stomach discomfort and diarrhea.

These effects can be attributed to the plant's pharmacological properties, which have been traditionally used to treat various gastrointestinal disorders.

8. Aggravates cardiovascular disease

Rubia peregrina aggravates cardiovascular disease due to its high concentration of anthraquinones and alkaloids, which can increase blood pressure and heart rate.

The plant's ability to stimulate the cardiovascular system can exacerbate existing conditions such as hypertension, atherosclerosis, and arrhythmias, potentially leading to more severe consequences.

9. Disrupts menstrual cycle

Rubia peregrina disrupts menstrual cycle due to its ability to affect hormone levels in the body.

The plant's compounds may alter the balance of estrogen and progesterone, leading to changes in menstruation patterns or even cessation of periods.

This disruption is thought to occur because Rubia peregrina contains anthraquinones, which are known to interact with the hormonal system and impact reproductive function.

10. Enhances sensitivity to light

Rubia peregrina enhances sensitivity to light due to its ability to stimulate the production of melanin in the eyes.

This increased sensitivity is a result of the herb's potent antioxidant and photoprotective properties, which can cause an overproduction of melanin in response to UV radiation.

As a result, users may experience temporary changes in their visual perception, such as increased light sensitivity or altered color vision.
