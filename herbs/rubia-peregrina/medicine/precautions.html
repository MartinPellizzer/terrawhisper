10 Precautions To Take When Using Rubia Peregrina (Madder)

Rubia peregrina has some precautions to consider before using it medicinally, such as proper dosing and monitoring of blood pressure.

It's crucial to take these precautions as excessive consumption can lead to hemolysis, a condition where red blood cells are destroyed.

For example, taking high doses without medical supervision may result in anemia, fatigue, and other adverse effects, which can be particularly concerning for individuals with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions.

3. Report any adverse effects

When using Rubia peregrina medicinally, it's important to consume in small doses.

This precaution is crucial because the plant contains anthraquinone glycosides, which can cause gastrointestinal upset, diarrhea, and even kidney damage if taken in excess. Additionally, large doses may lead to allergic reactions, skin irritation, and cardiovascular issues.

By consuming Rubia peregrina in moderation, individuals can harness its potential health benefits while minimizing the risk of adverse effects.

This is because the plant contains anthraquinones, which can cause a significant drop in blood pressure, potentially leading to dizziness, fainting, and other complications. Regular monitoring allows for early detection of any changes and enables prompt medical intervention if necessary.
