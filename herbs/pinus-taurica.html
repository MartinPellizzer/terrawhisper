Pinus taurica

Pinus taurica, commonly known as the Crimean pine, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Black Sea coastal regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

This tree is valued for its timber and resin, which is used in traditional medicine to treat various health conditions.

The resin of Pinus taurica is rich in bioactive constituents, including terpenes, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which have been shown to exhibit anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties.

The therapeutic actions of Pinus taurica resin include wound healing, pain relief, and the treatment of respiratory infections, thanks to its bioactive constituents and traditional use in folk medicine.
