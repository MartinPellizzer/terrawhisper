Gouania leptostachya

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gouania leptostachyia has a woody, scrambling shrub habit characterized by slender, often thorny branches and small, simple leaves. The foliage typically consists of elliptic to lanceolate leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its reproductive structures feature small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into distinctive, elongated seed pods. These pods are slender and somewhat woody, reflecting the plant's adaptation to its specific environmental niche. The overall morphology is defined by its ability to climb or spread through dense vegetation using its structural stems.

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of West and Central Africa, specifically ranging from Senegal in the west to Ethiopia in the east and down through much of the Congo Basin. The species thrives in diverse soil types across various African nations, including Nigeria, Cameroon, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo.
