What's the taxonomical classification of Gouania leptostachya?

Gouania leptostachya belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Rosales and the family Rhamnaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Gouania, reaching its most precise classification at the species level as Gouania leptostachya.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds gouanioside A, gouaniosides VII-IX, joazeiroside C, and resveratrol. These specific phytochemicals contribute to the complex chemical profile of the species. The gouanioside group consists of several distinct glycosides that are unique to this botanical source. Additionally, the presence of joazeiroside C and resveratrol provides the plant with significant bioactive properties. Researchers study these substances to understand their potential therapeutic applications in medicine.

As discussed in a study, the aerial parts of Gouania leptostachya contain four new triterpenoid saponins identified as gouaniasides VII-IX (1-3) and joazeiroside C (4), along with one known triterpenoid (5). The anti-inflammatory potential of compounds 1-3 was evaluated based on their ability to inhibit nitric oxide production by RAW 264.7 macrophages. All of these compounds significantly inhibited nitric oxide production by the macrophages at noncytotoxic concentrations. This inhibition occurred in a concentration-dependent manner.
