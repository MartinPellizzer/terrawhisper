What's the taxonomical classification of Gouania leptostachya?

Gouania leptostachya belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Rosales and the family Rhamnaceae. Ultimately, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Gouania, reaching its most precise classification at the species level as Gouania leptostachya.

What are the pharmacological activities of Gouania leptostachya?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. Researchers have identified various bioactive compounds within its chemical profile that contribute to these diverse biological effects. The anti-inflammatory action is particularly notable for its potential to reduce swelling and pain in biological models. Furthermore, its antioxidant capacity helps protect cells from oxidative stress caused by free radicals. These combined medicinal qualities make the species a subject of interest for natural product drug development.

According to a study published by "Natural product research", four new triterpenoid saponins identified as gouaniasides VII-IX (1-3) and joazeiroside C (4), along with one known triterpenoid (5), were isolated from the aerial parts of Gouania leptostachya. The anti-inflammatory potential of compounds 1-3 was evaluated based on their capacity to inhibit nitric oxide (NO) production by RAW 264.7 macrophages. All of these compounds significantly inhibited NO production by macrophages at noncytotoxic concentrations. This inhibitory effect occurred in a concentration-dependent manner.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating gastritis, gastritis lesions, inflammation, and various inflammatory symptoms. It acts as a natural remedy to soothe the stomach lining and promote healing of damaged tissues. The botanical properties help reduce the intensity of painful inflammatory symptoms experienced by patients. By addressing internal inflammation, it assists in the recovery of gastric lesions. This traditional application focuses on calming the digestive tract and restoring gastrointestinal health.

According to a study published by "Phytotherapy research : PTR", Gouania leptostachya is a traditional medicinal plant used in Thailand for treating various inflammatory symptoms. The methanol extract from its leaves and twigs (Gl-ME) was studied for its ability to attenuate gastritis lesions in mice treated with HCl/EtOH. Research showed that Gl-ME dose-dependently diminished the secretion of nitric oxide (NO) and prostaglandin E2 (PGE2) from LPS-stimulated RAW264.7 cells and peritoneal macrophages. Specifically, the extract at doses of 50 and 300 µg/mL reduced the mRNA expression of iNOS and COX-2. The study also identified resveratol as a major component that suppressed NO production and HCl/EtOH-induced gastric symptoms.
