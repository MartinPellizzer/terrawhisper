What is Dimetia prainiana?

Dimetia prainiana is a species of flowering plant in the family Rubiaceae, native to the Himalayas and China.

It is an evergreen shrub that grows up to 2 meters in height, with smooth, leathery leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape, 4-12 centimeters long, and 2-5 centimeters wide. The leaves are dark green on the upper surface and pale green on the lower surface, with a distinctive midrib and 10-15 pairs of secondary veins. The plant produces small, white flowers in axillary clusters, with a corolla that is 4-6 millimeters in diameter and has 4-5 lobes.

The flowers are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are 6-10 millimeters in diameter and contain 2-3 seeds.
