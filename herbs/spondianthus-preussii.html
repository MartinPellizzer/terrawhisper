What's the taxonomical classification of Spondianthus preussii?

Spondianthus preussii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Phyllanthaceae and is specifically identified within the genus Spondianthus, with its unique designation being the species preussii.

      What are the herbal preparations of Spondianthus preussii?

      Spondianthus preussii is prepared as a decoction of the bark or as a macerated infusion of the leaves to treat various ailments. Local practitioners typically boil the bark in water to extract its medicinal properties for internal consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Spondianthus preussii can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of unidentified secondary metabolites. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may trigger localized dermatitis or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. If consumed, the fruit or leaves might lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Because scientific research on the specific toxicological profile of this species is limited, caution is necessary when handling any part of the plant. Always avoid direct contact and ensure the plant is not consumed unless it has been verified as safe by an expert.
