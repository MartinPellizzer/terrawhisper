What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis davidii has a basal rosette of leaves that are typically pinnatifid or deeply lobed, combined with a terminal spike of zygomorphic flowers ranging in color from pink to purple. The inflorescence consists of densely packed, tubular corollas that feature a prominent upper lip and a smaller, often hooded lower lip. The stem is upright and can reach several decimeters in height, providing structural support for the elongated floral spike. Each flower is supported by a specialized bract that partially envelops the base of the corolla.
