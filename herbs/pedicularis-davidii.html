Pedicularis davidii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis davidii has a basal rosette of leaves that are typically pinnatifid or deeply lobed, combined with a terminal spike of zygomorphic flowers ranging in color from pink to purple. The inflorescence consists of densely packed, tubular corollas that feature a prominent upper lip and a smaller, often hooded lower lip. The stem is upright and can reach several decimeters in height, providing structural support for the elongated floral spike. Each flower is supported by a specialized bract that partially envelops the base of the corolla. These morphological traits are characteristic of the lousewort genus and facilitate specific pollination mechanisms.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of western China, specifically within the provinces of Sichuan, Gansu, Qinghai, Yunnan, and Tibet. It typically inhabits alpine meadows and rocky slopes where moisture is consistent. These populations are often found at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,000 meters above sea level. The distribution is largely dictated by the specific climatic conditions of the Qinghai-Tibetan Plateau.
