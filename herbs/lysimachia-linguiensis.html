Lysimachia linguiensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia linguiensis has lanceolate to linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along a stem often reaching heights of 20 to 50 centimeters. The foliage typically presents a smooth texture and a bright green coloration. Its small, yellowish flowers feature five distinct petals and emerge from the leaf axils during the summer months. The plant is characterized by a creeping rhizomatous growth habit that allows it to spread through moist soil. These structural features collectively help the species thrive in wetland habitats.
