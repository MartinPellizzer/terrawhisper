Lysimachia linguiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia linguiensis?

Lysimachia linguiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it falls into the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Ericales and belongs to the family Primulaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Lysimachia, with its specific identification being the species linguiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia linguiensis has lanceolate to linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along a stem often reaching heights of 20 to 50 centimeters.

The foliage typically presents a smooth texture and a bright green coloration. Its small, yellowish flowers feature five distinct petals and emerge from the leaf axils during the summer months. The plant is characterized by a creeping rhizomatous growth habit that allows it to spread through moist soil.

These structural features collectively help the species thrive in wetland habitats.
