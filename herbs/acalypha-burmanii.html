What is Acalypha burmanii?

Acalypha burmanii is a species of plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is a shrub-like herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, with a woody base and a branched stem. The leaves are simple, alternate, and lanceolate in shape, measuring 2-4 cm long and 0.5-1 cm wide, with a cuneate base and an acute apex. The inflorescence is a terminal corymb, bearing small, greenish-yellow flowers, 4-5 mm in diameter.

The plant produces small, winged seeds, 4-5 mm long, in a cylindrical fruit, 5-6 mm long.
