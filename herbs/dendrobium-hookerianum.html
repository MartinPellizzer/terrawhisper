What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium hookerianum?

Dendrobium hookerianum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is a representative of the family Orchidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Dendrobium, and is identified by the specific epithet hookerianum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium hookerianum has sympodial pseudobulbs that are elongated, cylindrical, and often slightly compressed with numerous nodes. These stems serve as primary storage organs for water and nutrients within the epiphytic structure. The leaves are arranged distichously along the upper portion of the pseudobulbs and possess an elliptical to lanceolate shape. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that typically emerge from the upper nodes of the mature stems.
