What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis labordei?

Pedicularis labordei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Orobanchaceae. Within this family, it is assigned to the genus Pedicularis, with its specific designation being the species labordei.

      What are the herbal preparations of Pedicularis labordei?

      Pedicularis labordei is prepared primarily as a decoction made from the dried aerial parts of the plant to extract its medicinal constituents. This method involves boiling the plant material in water to ensure the robust release of active compounds. Once the liquid has been concentrated through heating, it is often strained and consumed as a warm tea. In some traditional applications, the plant material may also be used in the form of a concentrated tincture by soaking it in alcohol. These various preparations allow for the controlled administration of the plant's properties in different medicinal contexts.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pedicularis labordei can cause adverse reactions such as nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and abdominal pain if ingested by humans or animals. Because many species within the Pedicularis genus contain bioactive compounds like lousewort toxins, consumption may lead to unpredictable neurological or gastrointestinal disturbances. There is also a potential for skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis if the plant comes into direct contact with sensitive dermal tissue. Detailed toxicological studies on this specific species are limited, which increases the risk of unknown systemic complications. Therefore, any accidental ingestion should be treated as a medical emergency.
