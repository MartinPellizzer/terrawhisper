Drosera Rotundifolia

Drosera rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved sundew, is a perennial herb found in bogs and marshy areas, characterized by its carnivorous nature and distinctive dew-like droplets on its leaves.

The health benefits of Drosera rotundifolia include its use in treating respiratory issues such as bronchitis, asthma, and coughs, as well as its ability to aid in digestion and relieve stress.

The therapeutic actions of this herb include its expectorant, anti-inflammatory, and antispasmodic properties, which contribute to its effectiveness in treating various health conditions.

The bioactive constituents of Drosera rotundifolia include iridoid glycosides, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.
