Roundleaf Sundew

Drosera rotundifolia

Roundleaf Sundew (Drosera rotundifolia) is a carnivorous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive conditions.

Taxonomy

The Roundleaf Sundew, scientifically classified as Drosera rotundifolia, belongs to the family Sarraceniaceae and is part of the genus Drosera, which includes various species of sundews known for their sticky, glandular leaves that trap insects.

Morphology

The Roundleaf Sundew (Drosera rotundifolia) is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette of rounded, waxy leaves that secrete a sticky, glandular substance to trap insects, with a height typically ranging from 5 to 30 centimeters.

Distribution

The Roundleaf Sundew (Drosera rotundifolia) is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including Europe, Asia, and North America, and is commonly found in moist, acidic environments such as bogs, fens, and heathlands.

Traditional Uses

In traditional herbal medicine, the Roundleaf Sundew has been used for its purported ability to treat respiratory ailments, digestive issues, and as a tonic to strengthen the body.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Native American, European herbal, and Traditional Chinese Medicine, the Roundleaf Sundew (Drosera rotundifolia) has been utilized for its purported healing properties, including treating wounds, respiratory infections, and digestive issues.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Roundleaf Sundew (Drosera rotundifolia) is characterized by the presence of various bioactive compounds, including mucilage, flavonoids, tannins, and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

The Roundleaf Sundew is known to exhibit mild diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties, which may support urinary tract health and reduce inflammation in mild cases.

Botanical Identification

Roundleaf Sundew, scientifically known as Drosera rotundifolia, belongs to the family Droseraceae. It is also called Rose Sundew and is a perennial herb native to northern regions. The plant has round, sticky leaves with hair-like structures and small, pinkish-white flowers. It is sometimes referred to by various regional names in herbal literature.
