Drosera Rotundifolia Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Drosera rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved sundew, is a carnivorous plant native to moist, acidic environments in the northern hemisphere.

This herb is known for its sticky, dew-like secretions that trap insects, which it then digests to obtain nutrients.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and detoxifying properties.

The bioactive constituents include flavonoids, tannins, and saponins, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and poultices can be made from its leaves to support respiratory and digestive health.
