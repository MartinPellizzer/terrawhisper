Roundleaf Sundew

Drosera rotundifolia

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Roundleaf Sundew, scientifically known as Drosera rotundifolia, belongs to the family Droseraceae. It is also called Rose Sundew and is a perennial herb native to northern regions. The plant has round, sticky leaves with hair-like structures and small, pinkish-white flowers. It is sometimes referred to by various regional names in herbal literature.

Active Compounds

The Roundleaf Sundew contains key bioactive compounds such as tannins, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Their presence enhances the herb's potential in herbal medicine applications.

  • Tannin
  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Therapeutic Indications

System Condidtion Action
Respiratory Bronchitis, respiratory tract infections, pulmonary congestion, excess mucus production Expectorant, antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory
Integumentary Skin infections, wound healing, insect bites Antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, antifungal
Digestive Gastrointestinal inflammation, gastrointestinal infections, mouth ulcers, dental caries, thrush Anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antifungal

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Tincture: Used for skin infections and wounds.

Poultice: Used for topical application on wounds and skin irritations.

Safety Profile

Roundleaf Sundew contains sticky, digestive enzymes that can irritate the mouth and throat if ingested. It is contraindicated for pregnant or breastfeeding women due to potential harm to the fetus or infant. Avoid use if allergic to any plant-based remedies.
