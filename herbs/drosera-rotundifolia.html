Roundleaf Sundew

Drosera rotundifolia

Roundleaf Sundew (Drosera rotundifolia) is a carnivorous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, known for its medicinal properties and traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive conditions.

Botanical Identification

Roundleaf Sundew, scientifically known as Drosera rotundifolia, belongs to the family Droseraceae. It is also called Rose Sundew and is a perennial herb native to northern regions. The plant has round, sticky leaves with hair-like structures and small, pinkish-white flowers. It is sometimes referred to by various regional names in herbal literature.

Safety Profile

Roundleaf Sundew contains sticky, digestive enzymes that can irritate the mouth and throat if ingested. It is contraindicated for pregnant or breastfeeding women due to potential harm to the fetus or infant. Avoid use if allergic to any plant-based remedies.
