What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemiboea subcapitata has woody, climbing stems with leaves that are typically elliptical or lanceolate, possessing entire margins and an opposite arrangement. Its inflorescence is characterized by a capitate structure, meaning the flowers are clustered into a head-like formation at the ends of the branches. Small, tubular flowers emerge from these dense clusters, often displaying specific colorations unique to the species. These morphological traits collectively define its growth habit within its native tropical forest environment.
