Botanical Identification of Khaya (Khaya senegalensis)

To identify Khaya (Khaya senegalensis) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Khaya senegalensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Eudicots, order Sapindales, family Meliaceae, genus Khaya, and species Khaya senegalensis. This classification places it within the flowering plants and highlights its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Khaya senegalensis is the scientific name of the species, first described by the botanist Jean-Baptiste de Lamarck. It is also known by several synonyms, including Bombacopsis senegalensis and Khaya africana, reflecting its classification history and regional variations. Common names such as African shea tree and wild shea are widely used in various regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Khaya senegalensis is a medium to large deciduous tree with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. It has a deep taproot system, providing stability and drought resistance. The stem is smooth, greyish-brown, and develops fissures with age. Leaves are alternate, simple, and pinnate, with 8-14 pairs of lanceolate leaflets. The inflorescence is a panicle, with small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release flat, winged seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Khaya senegalensis can be distinguished by its alternate, simple leaves with prominent veins, fragrant flowers in panicles, and fruit that splits into three segments. It is often confused with other Khaya species, but its leaf shape and floral structure provide key diagnostic features. The bark is typically smooth and grayish, while the wood has a distinct aromatic scent.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Khaya senegalensis is native to West Africa, particularly countries like Senegal, Nigeria, and Ghana. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and seasonal rainfall. The tree prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is commonly found at altitudes between 200 and 1,000 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Khaya senegalensis blooms from April to June, producing yellowish-green flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification purposes, the best time to observe key features is during the dry season, when leaves are shed and the tree structure is more visible.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Khaya senegalensis belongs to the genus Khaya, which is closely related to species like Khaya ivorensis and Khaya madagascariensis. It shares key characteristics with other members of the family Fabaceae, including compound leaves and pod fruits. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other leguminous trees in the region.
