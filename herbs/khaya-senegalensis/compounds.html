Khaya (Khaya senegalensis) Compounds

The primary compounds in Khaya senegalensis include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Phenolic acids contribute to its antimicrobial activity, while saponins exhibit anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds collectively support the plant's use in traditional herbal medicine.

Khaya (Khaya senegalensis) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Khaya senegalensis are bioactive compounds known for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like inflammation and digestive issues. They are often extracted from the bark and leaves, supporting their role in herbal medicine.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Khaya senegalensis contributes to its astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for treating wounds and diarrhea. It also helps in reducing inflammation and may support digestive health. This compound is commonly extracted from the bark and leaves of the plant.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Khaya senegalensis, known for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and digestive issues. This compound helps neutralize free radicals, supporting overall health and immune function. Its presence enhances the therapeutic value of Khaya senegalensis in herbal medicine.

4. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound found in Khaya senegalensis. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammatory conditions and skin disorders. Saponins exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making them valuable in herbal medicine.

5. Coumarin

Coumarin is a bioactive compound found in Khaya senegalensis. It exhibits antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine. This compound contributes to the plant's use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Its presence supports the therapeutic applications of Khaya senegalensis in herbal remedies.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Khaya senegalensis are bioactive compounds with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. They contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating infections and skin conditions. These compounds are extracted from the bark and leaves, supporting their role in herbal medicine. Terpenoids enhance the therapeutic value of Khaya senegalensis in natural remedies.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Khaya senegalensis. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues. Glycosides in Khaya senegalensis are known for their potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. These compounds are often extracted and used in herbal formulations.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Khaya senegalensis contains bioactive compounds like limonene and alpha-pinene. These compounds contribute to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. They are commonly extracted through steam distillation from the leaves and bark. The essential oil is used in traditional herbal medicine for treating infections and skin conditions.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Khaya senegalensis are bioactive compounds known for their pharmacological properties. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating ailments like inflammation and infections. Their presence supports the plant's role in herbal medicine. Further research is needed to fully understand their therapeutic potential.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Khaya senegalensis. It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties by acting as a powerful antioxidant. This compound helps neutralize free radicals, supporting cellular health and reducing oxidative stress. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Khaya senegalensis in traditional herbal medicine.
