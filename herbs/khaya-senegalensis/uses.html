Khaya (Khaya senegalensis) Uses

Khaya senegalensis is used to treat respiratory infections, including influenza and pulmonary inflammation. It helps alleviate cough and respiratory inflammation. The plant also addresses skin infections and fungal infections, supporting wound healing due to its antimicrobial properties.

Khaya (Khaya senegalensis) Monograph

1. Respiratory infection

Khaya senegalensis is used traditionally to treat respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The leaves and bark are often prepared as decoctions or poultices to alleviate symptoms such as coughing and congestion. It is valued for its ability to support the immune system during respiratory ailments.

2. Influenza

Khaya senegalensis is used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of influenza due to its antiviral and anti-inflammatory properties. The bark and leaves are often prepared as decoctions to reduce fever, cough, and body aches associated with the flu. Its active compounds help support the immune system during infection.

3. Respiratory infections

Khaya senegalensis is used traditionally to treat respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bark and leaves are often brewed into teas to alleviate symptoms like coughing and bronchitis. Its active compounds help reduce mucus production and soothe irritated airways. This makes it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for respiratory conditions.

4. Pulmonary inflammation

Khaya senegalensis is used to treat pulmonary inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory properties. It helps reduce swelling in the airways and alleviates symptoms like coughing and shortness of breath. The bark and leaves are commonly prepared as decoctions or poultices for respiratory relief.

5. Cough

Khaya senegalensis is used to treat coughs due to its expectorant and antitussive properties. The leaves and bark contain compounds that help loosen mucus and reduce coughing. It is often prepared as a decoction or poultice for respiratory relief. This traditional remedy is valued for its effectiveness in easing symptoms of persistent coughs.

6. Respiratory inflammation

Khaya senegalensis is used to reduce respiratory inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistaminic properties. It helps alleviate symptoms like coughing and bronchitis by soothing the airways. The bark and leaves are commonly prepared as decoctions or infusions for this purpose. It supports respiratory health in traditional herbal medicine practices.

7. Skin infection

Khaya senegalensis is used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The bark and leaves contain compounds that inhibit bacterial and fungal growth. It is applied topically as a poultice or infused oil. This traditional remedy helps reduce inflammation and accelerate healing.

8. Wound healing

Khaya senegalensis is used traditionally to promote wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The bark and leaves contain compounds that help reduce swelling, prevent infection, and accelerate tissue repair. It is often applied topically as a poultice or infused oil to treat cuts, burns, and skin infections.

9. Fungal infection

Khaya senegalensis is used traditionally to treat fungal infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The bark and leaves contain compounds that inhibit fungal growth. It is often prepared as a decoction or poultice for topical application. Its efficacy is supported by both traditional use and preliminary scientific studies.
