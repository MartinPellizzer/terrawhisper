Kokum

Garcinia indica

Kokum (Garcinia indica) is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to the Western Ghats of India, known for its sour fruit and a wide range of medicinal properties.

Taxonomy

Kokum (Garcinia indica) is a species belonging to the genus Garcinia in the family Guttiferae, within the kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Kokum (Garcinia indica) is a small evergreen tree characterized by its glossy dark green leaves, small yellow flowers, and oval-shaped yellow-orange fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a thick, acidic pulp.

Distribution

Kokum (Garcinia indica) is native to the Western Ghats of India, particularly the states of Karnataka, Kerala, and Goa, and is also found in parts of Sri Lanka and southern Nepal.

Traditional Uses

Kokum has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat digestive disorders, reduce fever, and alleviate respiratory conditions such as cough and bronchitis.

Medicinal Systems

Kokum (Garcinia indica) has been traditionally used in Ayurveda, Siddha, and Unani medicine systems for its therapeutic properties.

Phytochemistry

Kokum (Garcinia indica) contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid, flavonoids, tannins, and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Pharmacology

Kokum (Garcinia indica) exhibits a range of pharmacological properties including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antimicrobial, and hypoglycemic effects, which contribute to its traditional use in treating digestive disorders, skin conditions, and metabolic imbalances.

Botanical Identification

Kokum, scientifically known as Garcinia indica, belongs to the Clusiaceae family. It is also called kokum or kumara in various regions. A perennial tree native to India, it has smooth, elliptical leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine, and it is sometimes referred to as Garcinia kola or African bitter kola in herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and inflammation due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Extract: Used for weight loss and appetite suppression due to the presence of hydroxycitric acid.

Capsule: Used for weight management and metabolic support as a standardized form of the extract.

Safety Profile

Kokum is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare professional before use.
