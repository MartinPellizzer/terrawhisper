Kokum (Garcinia indica)

Kokum (Garcinia indica) is a member of the Guttiferae family, native to Southeast Asia, India, and Indonesia. Traditionally, its fruits, bark, and leaves have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, bitter, and anti-inflammatory actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 meters.
Leaves
Simple leaves with acute apex, entire margin, and pubescent lower surface, exhibiting a glossy green upper surface and a duller, lighter green lower surface, with prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, with five white to pale yellow petals, five sepals, and a superior ovary with two ovules, arranged in a cyme, and bearing a distinctive red calyx lobes.
Stems
Cylindrical, woody, with a smooth, glabrous surface, and exhibiting a sympodial growth habit.
