What's the taxonomical classification of Panax stipuleanatus?

Panax stipuleanatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Apiales. It is further categorized into the family Araliaceae, which defines its broader botanical grouping. Within this family, it is specifically identified by the genus Panax, with the unique species designation stipuleanatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Panax stipuleanatus has a perennial rhizomatous habit characterized by large, palmately compound leaves with five to seven distinct leaflets and prominent, large stipules at the base of the petioles. The leaflets exhibit serrated margins and an ovate to elliptical shape with acute tips. The plant features a terminal umbel of small, white, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the center of the leaf cluster. Following pollination, it develops small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a bright red color upon maturity. The root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients for the plant's seasonal growth cycles.
