What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Panax stipuleanatus has a perennial rhizomatous habit characterized by large, palmately compound leaves with five to seven distinct leaflets and prominent, large stipules at the base of the petioles. The plant features a terminal umbel of small, white, five-petaled flowers that emerge from the center of the leaf cluster. Following pollination, it develops small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a bright red color upon maturity.
