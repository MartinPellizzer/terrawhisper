What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Senecio wightii has pinnatifid leaves that are deeply lobed and a capitate inflorescence consisting of numerous yellow discoid flower heads. Each flower head is surrounded by several rows of overlapping involucral bracts that protect the developing florets. The stems are generally erect and may show slight branching as the plant matures.
