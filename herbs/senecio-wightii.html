What's the taxonomical classification of Senecio wightii?

Seneciio wightii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the biological hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. At the most specific taxonomic levels, this plant is identified by the genus Senecio and the species wightii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Senecio wightii has pinnatifid leaves that are deeply lobed and a capitate inflorescence consisting of numerous yellow discoid flower heads. The leaves are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem and exhibit a somewhat fleshy or succulent texture. Each flower head is surrounded by several rows of overlapping involucral bracts that protect the developing florets. The stems are generally erect and may show slight branching as the plant matures. The reproductive structures include small, tubular florets that lack prominent ray petals.
