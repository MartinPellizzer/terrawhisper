Soroseris rosularis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Soroseris rosularis has a low-growing, tufted habit characterized by basal rosettes of thick, fleshy, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically bright green with slightly serrated margins. These succulent leaves are arranged in dense clusters to conserve moisture in its native rocky environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flower heads that emerge on slender, upright stalks above the foliage. These inflorescences are composed of numerous tiny florets that are typical of the Asteraceae family. The overall structure is compact and adapted to survive in shallow, well-drained soils.

      Soroseris rosularis is prepared primarily as a decoction by boiling the dried stems and leaves in water to extract its bioactive compounds.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Soroseris rosularis can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals upon direct contact with its foliage. While it is generally considered non-toxic to humans when not ingested, touching the plant may trigger redness or itching. Some people may experience localized swelling if the plant's sap or surface textures come into contact with broken skin. There is limited scientific documentation regarding systemic toxicity, but caution is advised for those with known plant allergies. Always wash your hands thoroughly after handling the specimen to prevent unexpected dermatological reactions.
