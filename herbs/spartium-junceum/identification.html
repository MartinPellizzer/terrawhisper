Botanical Identification of Spartium (Spartium junceum)

To identify Spartium (Spartium junceum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Spartium (Spartium junceum) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Spartium junceum belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Spartium, and species Spartium junceum. It is a member of the legume family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. This classification helps in understanding its botanical relationships and ecological role.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Spartium junceum is the scientific name for the plant commonly known as Spanish thistle. It was first described by Linnaeus in 1753. The species has several synonyms, including Cirsium junceum and Cirsium spartum. It is also known by various common names such as Spanish thistle, red thistle, and thistle of the Pyrenees.

Morphological Characteristics

Spartium junceum is a shrub with a woody root system. Its stems are erect, often branched, and covered in fine hairs. Leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant bears a spike-like inflorescence with small, yellowish flowers. The fruit is a small, dry capsule containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Spartium junceum can be distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib, gray-green stems, and small, pale yellow flowers in dense spike-like clusters. It lacks the hairy stems and broader leaves of similar species like Spartium scoparium. Its distinctive appearance and growth habit help in accurate identification.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Spartium junceum is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in temperate climates with mild, wet winters and dry summers. It prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and is typically found at low to mid-elevation ranges, often in coastal areas or open woodlands.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Spartium junceum blooms from June to August, producing pink to purple flower clusters. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early autumn. For identification purposes, the most accurate harvest time is during the flowering period, as this is when the plant's distinguishing features are most apparent.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Spartium junceum is closely related to species within the same genus, Spartium. It shares family traits with the Leguminosae family, such as pod fruits and compound leaves. Its phylogenetic position reflects its evolutionary ties to other legumes, aiding in its identification and classification within herbal medicine.
