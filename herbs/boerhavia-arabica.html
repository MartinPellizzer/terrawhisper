Arabian Purslane (Boerhavia Arabica)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Boerhavia arabica.

Arabian purslane, scientifically known as Boerhavia arabica, is a small, leafy medicinal herb native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of Africa. It is traditionally valued as a nutrient-rich plant with adaptogenic properties, offering a range of health benefits such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and digestive support. In traditional Arabic and African medicine, it has been used for centuries to treat ailments like skin disorders, respiratory issues, and digestive discomfort, often prepared as a poultice or brewed into tea. Modern wellness applications include its potential role in supporting immune function and managing metabolic conditions, with research highlighting its high levels of omega-3 fatty acids and polyphenols. This unique plant is also noted for its distinctive salty, slightly peppery flavor and its historical use as a natural remedy in ancient trade routes, making it a rare and culturally significant herb.
