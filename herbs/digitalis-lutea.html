Digitalis Lutea Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Digitalis lutea, commonly known as the yellow foxglove, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its cardiovascular benefits.

The herb contains bioactive constituents such as cardiac glycosides, which help strengthen heart contractions and regulate heart rhythm.

Health benefits include supporting heart health, reducing arrhythmias, and improving circulation, though it must be used with caution due to its potency.

Herbal preparations such as infusions, tinctures, and decoctions can be made from its dried leaves and flowers.

Digitalis lutea treats arrhythmia by stabilizing the heart's electrical activity and regulating the rhythm of heartbeats through its influence on the sodium and potassium channels in cardiac cells.
