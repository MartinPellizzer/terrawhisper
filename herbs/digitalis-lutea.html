Ladys' Mantle

Digitalis lutea

Ladys' Mantle (Digitalis lutea) is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. It has been traditionally used to support cardiovascular health and is primarily valued for its potential to improve conditions such as heart failure and arrhythmia.

Botanical Identification

Digitalis lutea, commonly known as Ladys' Mantle, is a perennial plant in the Plantaginaceae family. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it features heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. It is also called Lady's Mantle and has been used in herbal medicine for its astringent properties. Synonyms include Alchemilla lutea and Alchemilla vulgaris in older texts.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Plantaginales
  • Family: Plantaginaceae
  • Genus: Digitalis
  • Species: Digitalis lutea

Active Compounds

Digitalis lutea contains key bioactive compounds such as glycosides, alkaloids, and flavonoids. These compounds work synergistically to support cardiovascular health by affecting heart rhythm and blood pressure. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for heart conditions and circulatory issues due to its cardiac glycoside content.

Tincture: Used for treating heart failure and arrhythmias due to its active cardiac compounds.

Extract: Used for managing heart rhythm disorders and improving cardiac function.

Safety Profile

Ladys' Mantle is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided during pregnancy due to potential uterine stimulation. Long-term use may lead to gastrointestinal upset, and individuals with known allergies should exercise caution.
