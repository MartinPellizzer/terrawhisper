Teucrium bidentatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Teucrium bidentatum has woody, perennial stems that produce small, oval-shaped leaves with distinctively two-toothed margins. The foliage is typically evergreen, displaying a leathery texture and a deep green color. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the branches, often appearing densely packed on the shrub. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the axils, showcasing a pale pink to white coloration. The overall growth habit forms a compact, rounded shrub that provides a structured appearance.
