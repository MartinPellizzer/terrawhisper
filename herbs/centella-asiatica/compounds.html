Centella (Centella asiatica) Compounds

Centella asiatica contains several primary active compounds, including flavonoids, terpenoids, and saponins. These compounds contribute to its medicinal properties, such as promoting wound healing and enhancing cognitive function. Other key components include tannins, phenolic acids, and glycosides, which support anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Centella (Centella asiatica) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of bioactive compounds found in Centella asiatica. They contribute to the plant's therapeutic effects by reducing inflammation and promoting skin healing. These compounds are known for their antioxidant properties, which support the plant's traditional use in treating wounds and neurological conditions.

2. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Centella asiatica, known for their role in the plant's medicinal properties. These compounds contribute to the plant's ability to promote skin healing and cognitive function. Specific terpenoids like asiatic acid and madecassic acid are key to its therapeutic effects. They are widely studied for their anti-inflammatory and wound-healing benefits.

3. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Centella asiatica, known for its skin-repairing and cognitive-enhancing properties. It promotes collagen synthesis and improves wound healing, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine for treating skin conditions and supporting mental clarity.

4. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Centella asiatica, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting skin healing and reducing inflammation. Tannins in Centella help strengthen connective tissues and may support wound repair. This compound is central to the plant's efficacy in herbal medicine.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Centella asiatica, known for its role in enhancing wound healing and cognitive function. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and improving memory. Its antioxidant properties support the plant's overall therapeutic effects.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Centella asiatica. It contributes to the plant's ability to promote skin healing and cognitive function. Glycosides in Centella are known for their wound-healing properties and may support nerve regeneration. These compounds are essential for the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Coumarin

Coumarin is a compound found in Centella asiatica. It contributes to the plant's ability to promote wound healing and improve circulation. Coumarin also has anticoagulant properties, which support its traditional use in herbal medicine for treating varicose veins and skin conditions.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Centella asiatica contains bioactive compounds like asiatic acid and madecassic acid, which contribute to its traditional use in promoting skin healing and cognitive function. It is extracted through steam distillation and is valued for its potential therapeutic properties in herbal medicine.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Centella asiatica, commonly known as gotu kola. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in promoting skin health and cognitive function. Alkaloids in Centella are believed to support circulation and reduce inflammation, making them valuable in herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in Centella asiatica. It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting skin health and wound healing. This pigment also helps protect cells from oxidative stress, enhancing the plant's therapeutic value in traditional herbal medicine.
