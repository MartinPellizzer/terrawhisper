Centella (Centella asiatica) Uses

Centella asiatica is commonly used for its wound healing properties. It helps treat skin infections, ulcers, and chronic wounds by promoting tissue repair. The herb is also effective for conditions like eczema, psoriasis, and skin inflammation. It may support healing in diabetic ulcers and improve circulation in varicose veins.

Centella (Centella asiatica) Monograph

1. Wound healing

Centella asiatica promotes wound healing by stimulating collagen production and improving skin elasticity. It reduces inflammation and enhances tissue regeneration, making it effective for treating burns, ulcers, and surgical wounds. Its active compounds, such as triterpenoids, support faster recovery and prevent scar formation.

2. Psoriasis

Centella asiatica has been used in traditional medicine to support skin health. It contains compounds that may reduce inflammation and promote skin healing, making it a potential aid for conditions like psoriasis. Its ability to enhance tissue repair and modulate immune responses contributes to its relevance in herbal treatments for chronic skin disorders.

3. Eczema

Centella asiatica is used topically to treat eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and skin-repairing properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and swelling associated with the condition. The herb promotes skin regeneration and strengthens the skin barrier, making it a valuable remedy in herbal medicine for managing eczema symptoms.

4. Skin infections

Centella asiatica has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and wound-healing properties. It helps reduce inflammation and promotes tissue repair, making it effective for conditions like fungal infections, eczema, and minor wounds. Its active compounds, such as asiatic acid and centellide, support skin regeneration and prevent infection.

5. Skin ulcers

Centella asiatica is used to treat skin ulcers due to its wound-healing properties. It promotes tissue regeneration and reduces inflammation, accelerating the healing process. The herb's active compounds support collagen synthesis and enhance skin repair. It is often applied topically in poultices or creams for effective relief.

6. Chronic wounds

Centella asiatica is used to treat chronic wounds due to its ability to stimulate collagen production and enhance tissue repair. It promotes wound healing by improving microcirculation and reducing inflammation. Its active compounds, such as asiatic acid and madecassic acid, support cellular regeneration and skin integrity. This makes it particularly effective for slow-healing ulcers and surgical wounds.

7. Skin inflammation

Centella asiatica is used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties. It reduces redness, swelling, and irritation, making it effective for conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The herb promotes skin regeneration and strengthens the skin's barrier function. It is commonly applied topically in creams or ointments.

8. Diabetic ulcers

Centella asiatica has shown potential in promoting wound healing in diabetic ulcers due to its ability to enhance collagen synthesis and improve microcirculation. Its active compounds, such as asiatic acid and madecassic acid, help reduce inflammation and increase tissue regeneration, making it a valuable complementary therapy for managing diabetic ulcers.

9. Poor wound healing

Centella asiatica promotes wound healing by enhancing collagen synthesis and improving tissue repair. It is particularly effective for slow-healing wounds, such as ulcers and burns. Its active compounds, including asiatic acid and madecassic acid, reduce inflammation and stimulate cell regeneration. This makes it a valuable herb in traditional and modern wound care practices.

10. Varicose veins

Centella asiatica is used traditionally to support vein health and reduce varicose veins. It contains compounds that strengthen blood vessel walls and improve circulation. Its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties may help alleviate symptoms associated with varicose veins. It is often applied topically or taken as a supplement under medical guidance.
