Murraya kwangsiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Murraya kwangsiensis?

Murraya kwangsiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Murraya, with its specific designation being the species kwangsiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murraya kwangsiensis has an evergreen, shrubby growth habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves that typically feature 7 to 15 pairs of elliptical, glossy green leaflets. The leaflets possess entire margins and acute apices, creating a dense, lush foliage texture. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the axillary positions, often appearing in clusters. These blooms eventually give rise to small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark reddish or black color upon ripening. The stems are woody and can become quite substantial as the plant matures into a large shrub or small tree.

What are the pharmacological activities of Murraya kwangsiensis?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological potential through its anti-inflammatory, anticancer, antitumor, and cytotoxic activities. Research indicates that its bioactive compounds can effectively inhibit inflammatory pathways to reduce swelling and pain. Furthermore, the plant exhibits potent cytotoxic properties that allow it to target and destroy harmful cells. These cytotoxic effects contribute to its broader anticancer and antitumor capabilities by preventing the growth and spread of malignant masses. Such multifaceted biological actions make it a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", the chemical investigation of Murraya kwangsiensis led to the isolation of 16 undescribed biscarbazole alkaloids known as kwangsines A-M, two undescribed natural products including (+/-)-bispyrayafoline C, and 19 known monomeric analogues. Within these findings, (±)-bispyrayafoline C and (±)-kwangsines A-C represent four pairs of biscarbazole atropisomers. One specific compound demonstrated significant inhibition on NO production in lipopolysaccharide-stimulated BV-2 microglial cells. Additionally, four compounds exhibited moderate cytotoxicities against HepG2 cells. These four compounds showed IC50 values that were less than 20 μM.
