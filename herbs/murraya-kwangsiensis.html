Murraya kwangsiensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Murraya kwangsiensis?

Murraya kwangsiensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Murraya, with its specific designation being the species kwangsiensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Murraya kwangsiensis has an evergreen, shrubby growth habit characterized by pinnately compound leaves that typically feature 7 to 15 pairs of elliptical, glossy green leaflets. The leaflets possess entire margins and acute apices, creating a dense, lush foliage texture. Small, fragrant white flowers emerge from the axillary positions, often appearing in clusters. These blooms eventually give rise to small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a dark reddish or black color upon ripening. The stems are woody and can become quite substantial as the plant matures into a large shrub or small tree.
