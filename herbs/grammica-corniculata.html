Grammica corniculata

Grammica corniculata, also known as False Pimpernel or Wormseed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, which include its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties, making it effective in treating various health conditions such as fever, digestive issues, and skin infections.

The health benefits of Grammica corniculata include its ability to boost the immune system, reduce inflammation, and exhibit antimicrobial properties, making it a valuable herb for maintaining overall well-being.
