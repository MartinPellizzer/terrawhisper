Daphne genkwa

What's the taxonomical classification of Daphne genkwa?

Daphne genkwa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Malvales. This plant is a member of the family Thymelaeaceae, falling under the genus Daphne, and is specifically identified by its species name, genkwa.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These specific constituents contribute significantly to the overall therapeutic profile of the species. The presence of total flavonoids plays a crucial role in providing antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the organism. Researchers often study these chemical markers to understand the plant's potential pharmacological applications. Each individual compound works in concert to define the complex biochemical makeup of the plant.

    As discussed in a study, the flower buds of Daphne genkwa were examined to isolate and purify chemical constituents from petroleum ether and ethyl acetate-soluble portions. Through chromatography and spectral analysis, 10 compounds were identified within the plant. These constituents include octacosane (1), dotriacontane (2), beta-sitosterol (3), 4', 7-dimethoxy-5-hydroxyflovon (4), aurantiamide acetate (5), genkwanin (6), luteolin (7), apigenin (8), 3'-hydroxygenkwanin (9), and daphnoretin (10). Notably, compound 1 and compound 2 are being isolated from this plant for the first time.
