Mentha × kerneri var. haynaldiana

Mentha × kerneri var. haynaldiana, commonly known as Hungarian mint or Bergamot mint, is a subspecies of hybrid mint, resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha aquatica.

This plant has been traditionally used for its health benefits, which include aiding in digestion, reducing nausea and vomiting, and exhibiting antimicrobial properties.

The therapeutic actions of Mentha × kerneri var. haynaldiana include anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antispasmodic effects, which are attributed to its bioactive constituents, such as menthone, limonene, and carvone.

The bioactive constituents of this plant contribute to its medicinal properties and have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
