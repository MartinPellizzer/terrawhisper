Bauhinia didyma

What's the taxonomical classification of Bauhinia didyma?

Bauhinia didyma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is placed in the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Ultimately, it is identified by its genus, Bauhinia, and its specific species designation, didyma.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bauhinia didyma has distinctive bilobed leaves that are shaped like a butterfly wing, featuring a deep notch at the apex and a smooth, entire margin. These leaves are typically arranged alternately along woody stems that exhibit a spreading, shrubby growth habit. The plant produces large, showy flowers that often transition from white to deep pink or purple as they age. Its reproductive structures include prominent stamens that emerge from the center of the petal arrangement. The overall structure is characterized by these large, cleft foliage elements and vibrant, wide-petaled blooms.
