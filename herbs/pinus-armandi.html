Pinus armandi

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pinus armandi has needles arranged in fascicles of three that are typically 10 to 15 centimeters long, a woody trunk with reddish-brown bark, and woody cones measuring 7 to 12 centimeters in length. The foliage is a deep green color and possesses a somewhat stiff texture. Its bark becomes deeply fissured and scaly as the tree reaches maturity. The cones are ovoid in shape and feature thick, thickened scales that protect the seeds. This species is characterized by its upright growth habit and spreading crown.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China, specifically spanning provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Guizhou, and Hubei. It typically thrives in high-altitude environments ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters above sea level. These populations are often found on rocky slopes and within temperate montane forests where moisture is consistent. The species occupies specific ecological niches that favor well-drained soils and cooler climates. Its distribution remains somewhat fragmented due to the rugged topography of its primary habitat.

What are the herbal preparations of Pinus armandi?

Pinus armandi is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical ointment derived from its needles, bark, and resin. The needles are often boiled in water to create a tea that is consumed for its aromatic and therapeutic properties. Resin extracted from the tree is frequently processed into salves or liniments to treat skin conditions and soothe muscular aches. Additionally, the bark can be steeped to produce medicinal liquids used in traditional practices. These various preparations allow for both internal consumption and external application of the plant's bioactive compounds.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Pinus armandi can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if ingested or handled improperly. Contact with its needles or resin may lead to contact dermatitis or redness in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience itching or swelling upon direct skin exposure. If consumed, the plant may trigger nausea or stomach discomfort. It is important to exercise caution when working with this species to avoid these adverse effects.
