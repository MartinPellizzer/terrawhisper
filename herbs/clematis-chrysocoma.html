What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis chrysocoma?

Clematis chrysocoma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Ranunculales and falls under the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement in the genus Clematis, specifically as the species chrysocoma.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clematis chrysocoma has woody, climbing vines that produce trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets with serrated margins. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that typically bloom in clusters from late spring through summer. These blossoms possess four to six petal-like sepals and a prominent central cluster of numerous stamens. Following pollination, the plant develops distinctive feathery, achene-bearing seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. The stems are twining and can reach significant lengths as they scramble over surrounding vegetation.
