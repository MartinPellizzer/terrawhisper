Clematis chrysocoma

What's the taxonomical classification of Clematis chrysocoma?

Clematis chrysocoma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Ranunculales and falls under the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed by its placement in the genus Clematis, specifically as the species chrysocoma.

What are the herbal preparations of Clematis chrysocoma?

Clematis chrysocoma is prepared as a decoction, tincture, or infused tea using the dried roots or stems of the plant. Practitioners typically simmer the hard woody parts in water to extract the active chemical compounds. Tinctures made with alcohol serve as a concentrated method for long-term storage and precise dosing. These liquid preparations are often used in traditional herbalism to address various systemic concerns. Each method varies in potency depending on the solvent used and the duration of the extraction process.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Clematis chrysocoma can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of protoanemonin. Contact with the plant's sap often leads to contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, and blistering on the skin. If the plant is eaten, it may cause symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain. These reactions occur because the plant contains irritant compounds that react with organic tissues. Individuals with sensitive skin or allergies should exercise extreme caution when handling this species.
