10 Possible Side Effects Of Myroxylon Pereirae

Myroxylon pereirae has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, contamination, or allergic sensitivity to the herb.

For example, severe gastrointestinal issues can lead to dehydration, fatigue, and even life-threatening conditions, making it difficult for individuals to perform daily tasks, work, and maintain relationships.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Myroxylon pereirae causes allergic reactions due to its high concentration of sesquiterpenes and lactones.

These compounds can stimulate an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and flushing.

Additionally, the tree resin's volatile oils may cause respiratory issues like coughing or shortness of breath in people with pre-existing allergies.

2. Triggers blood pressure increases

Myroxylon pereirae triggers blood pressure increases due to its high concentration of beta-caryophyllene, a sesquiterpene that can cause vasoconstriction and stimulate the sympathetic nervous system.

Additionally, the essential oils present in Myroxylon pereirae have been shown to increase heart rate and cardiac output, further contributing to elevated blood pressure levels.

3. Induces dizziness episodes

Myroxylon pereirae induces dizziness episodes due to its ability to interact with certain neurotransmitters in the brain, such as GABA and dopamine.

This interaction can lead to feelings of lightheadedness and disorientation, potentially causing individuals to feel dizzy or experience episodes of vertigo.

The exact mechanisms underlying this effect are not yet fully understood, but research suggests that it may be related to the compound's ability to affect the body's autonomic nervous system.

4. Promotes respiratory problems

Myroxylon pereirae promotes respiratory problems due to its ability to stimulate the lungs and increase mucus production.

The volatile oils present in copaiba oil can irritate the respiratory tract, leading to coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Additionally, the anti-inflammatory properties of Myroxylon pereirae may exacerbate existing respiratory conditions such as asthma or bronchitis, causing further discomfort and impairment.

5. Increases heart rate rapidly

Myroxylon pereirae increases heart rate rapidly due to its stimulating properties on the cardiovascular system.

The beta-amyrin and other sesquiterpenoids present in the essential oil can directly stimulate the heart, leading to an increase in heart rate and cardiac output.

This is likely a result of the oil's ability to dilate blood vessels and improve circulation, allowing more oxygenated blood to reach the body's tissues.

6. Produces skin irritation

Myroxylon pereirae produces skin irritation when used topically.

This is due to the presence of a chemical compound called caryophyllene oxide, which can cause skin redness and itching in some individuals. Additionally, the oil may also contain other compounds that can irritate the skin, leading to inflammation and discomfort.

The severity of these effects can vary depending on individual sensitivities and the concentration of the oil used.

7. Causes stomach upset frequently

Myroxylon pereirae causes stomach upset frequently.

This is due to the presence of limonoids, a type of compound that can irritate the mucous membranes in the stomach and intestines, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Additionally, the volatile oils present in Myroxylon pereirae may also contribute to this side effect by increasing gut motility and altering the normal digestion process.

8. Increases risk of bleeding

Myroxylon pereirae increases risk of bleeding due to its potent antiplatelet and anticoagulant properties.

The resin's bioactive compounds, such as limonoids and alkaloids, can disrupt platelet function and blood clotting mechanisms, leading to increased bleeding risk.

This effect may be particularly concerning for individuals with pre-existing bleeding disorders or taking medications that also affect blood coagulation.

9. Triggers anxiety attacks quickly

Myroxylon pereirae triggers anxiety attacks quickly due to its unique chemical composition.

The essential oil contained in the tree's resin, particularly the compound beta-caryophyllene, has been shown to interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, leading to an increase in stress hormones like adrenaline and cortisol. This can cause feelings of panic, unease, and apprehension, resulting in anxiety attacks.

The exact mechanisms by which this occurs are still not fully understood, but it is thought that beta-caryophyllene may be contributing to the stimulation of the brain's amygdala, a region involved in emotional processing and fear response.

10. Leads to insomnia cases

Myroxylon pereirae leads to insomnia cases due to its stimulating properties that can interfere with a person's ability to relax and fall asleep.

The beta-caryophyllene and other essential oils present in the plant can cause restlessness, anxiety, and agitation, making it difficult for individuals to wind down at night.

As a result, users of Myroxylon pereirae may experience disrupted sleep patterns, leading to fatigue, mood disturbances, and decreased productivity during the day.
