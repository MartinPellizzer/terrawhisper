Pedicularis superba

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis superba?

Pedicularis superba belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, the plant is situated in the order Lamiales and the family Orobanchaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Pedicularis, with its specific designation being the species superba.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis superba has a robust, erect stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and is adorned with lanceolate to ovate leaves that feature serrated margins. The foliage is primarily basal or located on the lower portions of the stem, often displaying a somewhat hairy or pubescent texture. Its most striking feature is the dense inflorescence consisting of tubular, bilabiate flowers that vary in color from pink to deep magenta. Each flower possesses a prominent upper lip that acts as a hood, protecting the reproductive organs within. The calyx is also deeply lobed and provides structural support to the colorful corolla during blooming.
