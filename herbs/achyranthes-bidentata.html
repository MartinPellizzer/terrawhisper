Achyranthes bidentata

What's the taxonomical classification of Achyranthes bidentata?

Achyranthes bidentaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Caryophyllales and is a member of the family Amaranthaceae. It is specifically identified within the genus Achyranthes, and its unique designation is given by its species name, bidentata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Achyranthes bidentaata has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by slender stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, terminal panicles that often transition from greenish to brownish hues. Its distinctive name refers to the two small teeth located at the base of the calyx lobes during fruiting. The resulting fruits are small, dry schizocarps that typically split into two single-seeded mericarps upon maturity. These morphological traits help distinguish the species within its taxonomic group.

What are the pharmacological activities of Achyranthes bidentata?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, immunomodulatory, neuroprotective, and anti-apoptotic pharmacological activities. Its bioactive compounds work to reduce pain perception and suppress systemic inflammatory responses. By modulating the immune system, the plant helps regulate various physiological defense mechanisms. Research also indicates its ability to shield neurons from damage and prevent programmed cell death. These diverse properties suggest significant potential for treating neurological disorders and chronic inflammatory conditions.

As discussed in a study, the herbal paste containing Achyranthis Bidentatae Radix, named DAEP, was applied topically to relieve osteoarthritic knee pain in rats. After 2 weeks of treatment, radiographic images showed less destruction at the posterior tibial plateau in the DAEP group compared to the Control. DAEP treatment significantly increased the Print Area and Maximum Intensity compared with the Control when measured via the CatWalk gait system. Furthermore, DAEP significantly suppressed the upregulation of the gene expression of interleukin (IL)-6, tumor necrosis factor (TNF)-α, and inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS). The effect of DAEP was exhibited via the nuclear factor (NF)-κB pathway by suppressing the phosphorylation of IκB kinase αβ (p-IKKαβ) and cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) protein expression.

As discussed in a study, Achyranthes bidentata Blume contains medicinal compounds including 25S-inokosterone (1), 25R-inokosterone (2), and ecdysterone (3). The research focused on the isolation and structure identification of C-25 epimers of inokosterone from Achyranthes bidentata Blume. An aqueous extract of Achyranthes bidentata was found to inhibit glutamate-induced neuronal damage in a dose- and time-dependent manner. This extract prevents glutamate-induced cell damage in primarily cultured hippocampal neurons by inhibiting an increase in [Ca(2+)](i).
