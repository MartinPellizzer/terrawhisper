What's the taxonomical classification of Angiopteris caudatiformis?

Angiopteris caudatiformis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida, it is situated under the subclass Marattiidae and falls within the order Marattiales. The plant is further categorized into the family Marattiaceae, which contains the genus Angiopteris, ultimately leading to its specific identification as the species caudatiformis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Angiopteris caudatiformis has a creeping rhizome structure with slender, dichotomously branching stems that terminate in specialized, tail-like fertile sporangiophores.

These fertile branches are characterized by a distinctively tapered or caudate shape, which distinguishes them from the more broadly expanded fertile structures of related species. The sterile vegetative branches are typically more delicate and provide the photosynthetic surface for the plant. Spores are produced within the sporangia located along these specialized stalks to facilitate reproduction.
