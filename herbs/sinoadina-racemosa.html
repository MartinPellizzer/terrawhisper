Sinoadina racemosa

What's the taxonomical classification of Sinoadina racemosa?

Sinoadina racemosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic lineage further, it falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Sinoadina, with its specific designation being the species racemosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sinoadina racemosa has erect, herbaceous stems that reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and feature opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically arranged in pairs along the stem, with the lower leaves being larger and more prominent than the upper ones. Its inflorescence consists of a terminal raceme bearing small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in a clustered fashion. The reproductive structures include five distinct petals and numerous stamens characteristic of the Papaveraceae family. These delicate floral components are supported by a slender pedicel that extends from the main axis.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of western China, specifically spanning provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Gansu, and Tibet. It typically thrives in high-altitude habitats ranging from alpine meadows to rocky slopes. The species is often found at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters above sea level. Its distribution is closely tied to the moist, temperate climates characteristic of the Hengduan Mountains. What are the pharmacological activities of Sinoadina racemosa?

Its chemical constituents act on the respiratory system to suppress coughing and help clear mucus from the airways. Beyond respiratory relief, the plant demonstrates significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects against various pathogens. Research also suggests that its alkaloids may possess cytotoxic properties useful in studying cancer cell inhibition. These diverse biological actions make it a valuable subject for both traditional medicine and modern drug development.
