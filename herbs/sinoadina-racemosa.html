What's the taxonomical classification of Sinoadina racemosa?

Sinoadina racemosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical structure, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic lineage further, it falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Sinoadina, with its specific designation being the species racemosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sinoadina racemosa has erect, herbaceous stems that reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and feature opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically arranged in pairs along the stem, with the lower leaves being larger and more prominent than the upper ones. Its inflorescence consists of a terminal raceme bearing small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in a clustered fashion. The reproductive structures include five distinct petals and numerous stamens characteristic of the Papaveraceae family.
