Sinoadina racemosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sinoadina racemosa has erect, herbaceous stems that reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and feature opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The foliage is typically arranged in pairs along the stem, with the lower leaves being larger and more prominent than the upper ones. Its inflorescence consists of a terminal raceme bearing small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in a clustered fashion. The reproductive structures include five distinct petals and numerous stamens characteristic of the Papaveraceae family. These delicate floral components are supported by a slender pedicel that extends from the main axis.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of western China, specifically spanning provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, Gansu, and Tibet. It typically thrives in high-altitude habitats ranging from alpine meadows to rocky slopes. The species is often found at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters above sea level. Its distribution is closely tied to the moist, temperate climates characteristic of the Hengduan Mountains. This localized range makes the plant a specialized component of the Eastern Himalayan botanical zone.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Sinoadina racemosa can cause serious side effects including severe nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, and potential neurological issues like dizziness or confusion. These adverse reactions often occur because the plant contains potent alkaloids that can act as toxins if consumed in improper amounts. Some individuals may also experience heart palpitations or changes in blood pressure due to the plant's stimulant properties. Because of these significant risks, the plant should be handled with extreme caution and never ingested without professional medical supervision. The toxicity levels can vary greatly depending on the specific part of the plant used and the dosage consumed.
