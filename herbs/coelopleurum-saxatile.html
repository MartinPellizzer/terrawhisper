Coelopleurum saxatile

What's the taxonomical classification of Coelopleurum saxatile?

Coelopleurum saxatile belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is further categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Apiales, this plant is a member of the family Apiaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Coelopleurum, with its unique species designation being saxatile.

Coelopleurum saxatile is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion by boiling or steeping the dried aerial parts of the plant in water. What side-effects this plant can have?

Coelopleurum saxatile can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap or foliage may trigger redness, itching, or localized swelling on sensitive skin areas. Some individuals might experience more severe allergic reactions including hives or rashes upon prolonged exposure. If accidentally consumed, it can lead to symptoms such as nausea, abdominal cramping, or vomiting. These adverse effects vary depending on the individual's sensitivity and the method of exposure.
