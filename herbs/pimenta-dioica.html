Allspice (Pimenta dioica)

Allspice (Pimenta dioica) is a member of the Myrtaceae family, native to Caribbean, Central America, and South America. Traditionally, its leaves, fruits, and bark have been used for decoctions, infusions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and european herbal medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.

Leaves
Bilaterally symmetrical leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter green lower surface, featuring prominent stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Inflorescences are spikes of small, white, zygomorphic flowers with five lobed corolla, each having two stamens and a single pistil, arranged in a dense, terminal cluster.
Stems
Woody, erect, unbranched stems with smooth, glabrous surfaces and persistent, suberized lenticels.
