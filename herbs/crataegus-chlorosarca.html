Crataegus chlorosarca

What's the taxonomical classification of Crataegus chlorosarca?

Crataegus chlorosarca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Rosales and the family Rosaecae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Crataegus, and its unique species designation, chlorosarca.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Crataegus chlorosarca contains high concentrations of bioactive flavonoids, proanthocyanidins, and organic acids that serve as its primary medicinal components. These polyphenolic compounds provide significant antioxidant properties that help mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. The presence of specific tannins further contributes to its potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. Additionally, the plant's unique chemical profile may support cardiovascular health through the regulation of vascular tone. Researchers continue to investigate how these specific phytochemicals interact with cellular pathways to treat chronic ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Crataegus chlorosarca?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat cardiovascular conditions including congestive heart failure, hypertension, and various types of arrhythmias. Its therapeutic properties are primarily derived from bioactive compounds that enhance coronary blood flow. These components help improve the contractile strength of the heart muscle while simultaneously relaxing blood vessels. By regulating heart rhythm, the plant assists in stabilizing irregular beats. This makes it a significant subject in the study of traditional herbal remedies for heart health.
