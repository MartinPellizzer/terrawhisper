What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Crataegus chlorosarca has small, elliptical leaves with serrated margins and woody stems characterized by sharp thorns. The plant features fleshy green drupes that distinguish it from many other species in the hawthorn genus. Its flowering structure typically consists of small, white blossoms arranged in clusters. The bark develops a rugged texture as the specimen matures into a small tree or shrub. These morphological traits collectively define its unique botanical identity within its habitat.
