10 Possible Side Effects Of Theobroma Cacao

Theobroma cacao has some side effects when used improperly, such as stomach upset, nausea, and diarrhea.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, caffeine sensitivity, or underlying health conditions. For instance, individuals with irritable bowel syndrome may experience worsening symptoms due to cacao's high polyphenol content.

Furthermore, individuals with anxiety disorders may experience increased heart palpitations or jitteriness due to the stimulatory effects of cacao's flavonoids.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Theobroma cacao causes allergic reactions in some individuals.

This is because it contains proteins and allergens that can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling.

Additionally, the flavonoids present in Theobroma cacao may exacerbate existing allergies or sensitivities.

2. Triggers anxiety attacks

Theobroma cacao triggers anxiety attacks in some individuals due to its high levels of phenylethylamine (PEA), a natural stimulant that can cause an increase in heart rate and blood pressure.

Additionally, the caffeine content in cacao can further exacerbate this effect, leading to feelings of jitteriness and unease.

As a result, people may experience anxiety attacks when consuming cacao products, particularly those with high levels of PEA and caffeine.

3. Increases blood pressure

Theobroma cacao increases blood pressure due to its high levels of flavonoids and methylxanthines.

These compounds can cause blood vessels to constrict, leading to an increase in blood pressure.

Additionally, the caffeine present in Theobroma cacao can also contribute to this effect, stimulating the nervous system and increasing heart rate and blood pressure.

4. Produces diarrhea

Theobroma cacao produces diarrhea in some individuals due to its high content of stimulants such as caffeine and theobromine.

These alkaloids can increase gut motility, leading to increased frequency and urgency of bowel movements.

Additionally, the flavonoids present in cacao may cause changes in gut bacteria composition, altering the balance of the microbiome and contributing to loose stools or diarrhea.

5. Induces drowsiness

Theobroma cacao induces drowsiness due to its high levels of phenylethylamine (PEA), a natural mood elevator that can cause relaxation and decreased alertness.

The flavonoids present in cacao, particularly epicatechin, can also contribute to this effect by interacting with the body's neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and dopamine, leading to feelings of calmness and fatigue.

Additionally, the tryptophan content in cacao can enhance its sedative properties by increasing the production of serotonin in the brain, further inducing drowsiness.

6. Exacerbates heartburn

Theobroma cacao exacerbates heartburn due to its high levels of caffeine and flavonoids, which can irritate the esophageal lining and relax the lower esophageal sphincter, allowing stomach acid to flow back up into the esophagus.

Additionally, the rich fatty content in cocoa products can also contribute to this side effect by stimulating the production of stomach acid and increasing its secretion, further exacerbating heartburn symptoms.

7. Interferes with medication

Theobroma cacao interferes with medication by altering the way the body absorbs and processes certain pharmaceuticals.

Its flavonoids can bind to proteins in the gut, changing the pH levels and altering the solubility of medications, leading to reduced absorption or altered efficacy.

This interference can affect blood pressure, antidepressant, and cholesterol-lowering medications, among others, potentially resulting in decreased therapeutic effects or even increased side effects.

8. Causes insomnia

Theobroma cacao causes insomnia due to its high caffeine content.

Caffeine is a stimulant that can disrupt normal sleep patterns by blocking adenosine receptors in the brain, making it difficult for individuals to fall asleep or stay asleep throughout the night.

Additionally, the release of other neurotransmitters such as dopamine and norepinephrine can also contribute to an increased sense of alertness and energy, further exacerbating insomnia symptoms.

9. Provokes mood swings

Theobroma cacao provokes mood swings in some individuals due to its high content of phenylethylamine (PEA), a natural mood elevator.

PEA can cause fluctuations in serotonin levels, leading to feelings of euphoria followed by crashes and irritability. Additionally, the presence of anandamide, a fatty acid neurotransmitter, may also contribute to mood swings as it binds to receptors in the brain, influencing emotional states.

These compounds can amplify existing emotional responses, resulting in unpredictable mood fluctuations.

10. Increases nausea

Theobroma cacao increases nausea.

This side effect is attributed to the high levels of polyphenols and flavonoids present in the plant, which can cause gastrointestinal irritation and stimulation of digestive enzymes, leading to nausea and potentially even vomiting.

Additionally, the stimulant properties of theobromine, a compound found in cacao, may also contribute to this adverse effect by increasing gut motility and sensitivity.
