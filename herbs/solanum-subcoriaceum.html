Solanum subcoriaceum

Solanum subcoriaceum, also known as the small-fruited bitter apple, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the Solanaceae family, native to tropical Australia and New Guinea.

It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its medicinal properties, which include anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities.

The plant is said to have various health benefits, including relieving symptoms of fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, as well as exhibiting antioxidant properties.

Solanum subcoriaceum is often used in herbal preparations such as teas, infusions, and tinctures, which are prepared from the plant's leaves and roots.
