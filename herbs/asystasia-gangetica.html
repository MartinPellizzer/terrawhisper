Asystasia gangetica

What's the taxonomical classification of Asystasia gangetica?

Asystasia gangetica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Asystasia, and its unique species name, gangetica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asystasia gangeetica has erect, herbaceous stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and feature opposite, ovate leaves with entire or slightly wavy margins. The foliage is characterized by a bright green color and a soft, sometimes pubescent texture on the leaf surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of funnel-shaped flowers that are predominantly white or pale lilac with distinct yellowish markings on the throat. These tubular blossoms are borne in axillary clusters and emerge from the leaf axils along the stem. The plant's reproductive structures include a small capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds for dispersal.
