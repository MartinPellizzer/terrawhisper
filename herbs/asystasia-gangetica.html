Asystasia gangetica

What's the taxonomical classification of Asystasia gangetica?

Asystasia gangetica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Asystasia, and its unique species name, gangetica.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Asystasia gangetica contains bioactive phytochemicals including flavonoids, terpenoids, alkaloids, and phenolic compounds that drive its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents contribute to the plant's documented antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities. Research indicates that the presence of specific glycosides and saponins may assist in regulating cellular processes. Traditional medicinal practices often utilize these compounds to treat skin ailments and various inflammatory conditions.
