Asystasia gangetica

What's the taxonomical classification of Asystasia gangetica?

Asystasia gangetica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Lamiales and is a member of the family Acanthaceae. Finally, it is identified by its specific genus, Asystasia, and its unique species name, gangetica.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Asystasia gangetica can cause adverse physiological reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic responses if ingested or applied topically without proper precaution. Because the plant contains various bioactive compounds, individuals may experience nausea, stomach cramps, or diarrhea upon consumption. Topical contact might trigger contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, or localized swelling on the skin. There is also a risk of systemic toxicity if the plant is misused or if concentrated extracts are absorbed into the bloodstream. Detailed clinical studies on the long-term human side effects of this specific species remain limited.
