Caraway (Carum carvi) Compounds

The primary compounds of Caraway include essential oil, which contains compounds like limonene and carvone. Coumarin is another key component, known for its aromatic properties. Phenolic acids and flavonoids contribute to its antioxidant effects. Terpenoids and glycosides also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Caraway (Carum carvi) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Caraway (Carum carvi) is the primary active compound, containing volatile substances like limonene and carvone. It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has carminative and antispasmodic properties, making it valuable in herbal medicine for digestive support.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Caraway (Carum carvi). It contributes to the plant's aromatic properties and has mild antispasmodic and carminative effects. Coumarin also acts as a natural anticoagulant, though it should be used cautiously in individuals on blood-thinning medications.

3. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Caraway (Carum carvi). It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has antioxidant properties. This compound supports digestive health and may help reduce inflammation. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Caraway in herbal medicine.

4. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Caraway (Carum carvi) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting the plant's use in digestive and respiratory health. They help reduce oxidative stress and may enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Caraway (Carum carvi). They contribute to the plant's aromatic properties and have various therapeutic effects, including antispasmodic and carminative actions. These compounds are responsible for many of the medicinal benefits associated with caraway in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin in Caraway (Carum carvi) contributes to its astringent properties, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe digestive issues. It also aids in protecting the plant's tissues and may support the plant's traditional use in treating gastrointestinal complaints.

7. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Caraway (Carum carvi). It contributes to the plant's characteristic aroma and has mild carminative properties, helping to relieve digestive discomfort. Glycosides in Caraway are often metabolized into active compounds like apiol and limonene, which support its traditional use in herbal medicine.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Caraway (Carum carvi). It contributes to the plant's digestive and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins help reduce bloating and support liver function. They also have mild antimicrobial effects.

9. Alkaloid

Caraway (Carum carvi) contains the alkaloid apiol. Apiol is responsible for the plant's characteristic aroma and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for digestive support and as a carminative. It also exhibits mild antispasmodic properties.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Caraway (Carum carvi). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, supporting overall health. While not the primary active compound in Caraway, anthocyanin may enhance the plant's therapeutic effects.
