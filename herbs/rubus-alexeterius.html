Rubus alexeterius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Rubus, with its specific designation being the species alexeterius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus alexeterius has erect, woody stems characterized by a dense covering of small, recurved prickles and a distinctly glandular-pubescent texture. Each leaflet often displays a dull green upper side and a lighter, sometimes slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition into aggregate fruits known as drupelets. These fruits eventually mature into dark, edible berries that hang from the stems.
