Rubus alexeterius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus alexeterius has erect, woody stems characterized by a dense covering of small, recurved prickles and a distinctly glandular-pubescent texture. The leaves are typically trifoliate or occasionally five-foliate, featuring serrated margins and a somewhat rugose surface. Each leaflet often displays a dull green upper side and a lighter, sometimes slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition into aggregate fruits known as drupelets. These fruits eventually mature into dark, edible berries that hang from the stems.

      What are the herbal preparations of Rubus alexeterius?

      Rubus alexeterius is prepared primarily as a decoction of its roots, an infusion of its leaves, or a tincture extracted using alcohol. The roots are often simmered for an extended period to extract the dense medicinal compounds found within the woody tissue. Leaves can be steeped in hot water to create a gentle tea suitable for daily consumption. Tinctures offer a more concentrated method of preservation and long-term storage.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Rubus alexeterius can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched. Contact with the plant's foliage or stems may lead to redness, itching, or localized rashes due to its physical structure or chemical composition. Some individuals might experience hypersensitivity reactions such as swelling or hives upon exposure to its sap. If consumed, the plant could potentially trigger nausea, stomach cramps, or more severe digestive upset. Careful handling is recommended to mitigate these risks during foraging or gardening.
