Ruellia venusta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ruelliia venusta has woody, climbing stems that produce tubular, orange-red flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves with entire margins. The plant exhibits a vining growth habit, using its flexible branches to ascend through surrounding vegetation. Each leaf is arranged oppositely along the stem and features a smooth texture with a prominent midrib. The corolla of the flower is elongated and bell-shaped, specifically adapted to attract long-tongued pollinators. Small, woody capsules serve as the fruit, containing the seeds necessary for the plant's reproduction.

        What side effects this plant can have?

        This plant can cause severe skin irritation, allergic reactions, and internal toxicity if ingested. Contact with the sap often leads to phytophotodermatitis, which results in painful blisters and redness when the skin is exposed to sunlight. Some individuals may experience significant swelling or itching if they touch the foliage without protection. If the plant is swallowed, it can trigger nausea, vomiting, and intense abdominal pain. These physiological responses vary depending on the amount of exposure and the individual's sensitivity to its chemical compounds.
