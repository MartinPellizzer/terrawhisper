Yellow Hawthorn (Crataegus Luteola)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Crataegus luteola.

Yellow hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus luteola, is a small, deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, valued for its medicinal properties and historical significance. As a medicinal herb, it is traditionally used to support cardiovascular health, with its primary benefits including improving heart function, reducing stress on the heart muscle, and enhancing circulation. Historically, it has been utilized in European folk medicine for centuries, often brewed into teas or tinctures to alleviate anxiety, promote relaxation, and support the nervous system. In modern wellness practices, Yellow hawthorn is increasingly incorporated into herbal supplements aimed at managing hypertension and enhancing emotional well-being. One of its unique features is its distinctive yellow flowers and the presence of flavonoids, which contribute to its adaptogenic properties and make it a rare yet potent plant in traditional and contemporary herbal medicine.
