What is Oxalis bakeriana?

Oxalis bakeriana is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is a perennial herb with a tufted habit, growing up to 30 cm tall, with leaves that are divided into 3-5 lobes, each lobe being triangular with a pointed tip. The leaves are dark green, simple, alternate, and 2-4 cm long, with a short petiole. The inflorescence is a corymb, consisting of 2-5 flowers, each being 4-5 cm in diameter, with 5 white, fragrant petals and 5 green sepals.

The plant blooms from April to July, producing red to purple berries in the fall.
