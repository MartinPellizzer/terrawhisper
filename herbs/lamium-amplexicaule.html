What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lamium amplexicaule has square stems that are densely covered in fine, white hairs and leaves that grow in opposite pairs with distinctly crenate margins. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a characteristic two-lipped structure with a pale pink or white coloration. These blossoms are often marked by subtle purple spots on the lower lip to attract pollinators. The plant's overall structure is spreading and herbaceous, allowing it to form dense ground cover in shaded environments.
