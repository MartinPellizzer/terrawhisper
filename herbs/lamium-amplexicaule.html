What's the taxonomical classification of Lamium amplexicaule?

Lamium amplexicaule belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage that leads to the order Lamiales. Within this order, it is situated in the family Lamiaceae, which is further narrowed down to the genus Lamium. Finally, the specific identity of this plant is established at the species level as Lamium amplexicaule.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lamium amplexicaule has square stems that are densely covered in fine, white hairs and leaves that grow in opposite pairs with distinctly crenate margins. The foliage is typically ovate or elliptical in shape and features a textured, rugose surface with prominent veining. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, displaying a characteristic two-lipped structure with a pale pink or white coloration. These blossoms are often marked by subtle purple spots on the lower lip to attract pollinators. The plant's overall structure is spreading and herbaceous, allowing it to form dense ground cover in shaded environments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lamium amplexicaule?

Lamium amplexicaule has therapeutic properties as an astringent, hemostatic, and vulnerary agent used to manage skin and mucosal tissues. Its role as a vulnerary supports the healing process of damaged skin through its ability to promote tissue repair. These combined actions make it a traditional remedy for treating superficial abrasions and minor lacerations.
