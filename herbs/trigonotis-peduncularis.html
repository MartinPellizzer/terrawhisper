Trigonotis peduncularis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trigonotis peduncularis has a decumbent or ascending growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems and small, opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically entire and acute. The foliage is often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance to the plant's structure. Its most distinctive feature is the long, slender peduncles that elevate the small, inconspicuous flowers above the leafy base. These flowers belong to the Convolvulaceae family and typically possess a funnel-shaped corolla. The overall morphology is quite delicate, making the plant appear low-growing and spreading in its natural habitat.
