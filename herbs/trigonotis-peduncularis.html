What's the taxonomical classification of Trigonotis peduncularis?

Trigonotis peduncularis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Boraginales and the family Boraginaceae. Finally, the plant is placed within the genus Trigonotis, identifying it specifically as the species peduncularis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trigonotis peduncularis has a decumbent or ascending growth habit characterized by slender, branched stems and small, opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically entire and acute. Its most distinctive feature is the long, slender peduncles that elevate the small, inconspicuous flowers above the leafy base. These flowers belong to the Convolvulaceae family and typically possess a funnel-shaped corolla.
