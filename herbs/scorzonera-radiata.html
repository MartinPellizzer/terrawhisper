Scorzonera radiata

What's the taxonomical classification of Scorzonera radiata?

Scorzonera radiata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a group known for its complex flower structures, and is specifically categorized under the genus Scorzonera. Within this genus, its unique identity is defined by its specific designation as the species radiaata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Scorzonera radiata has a basal rosette of elongated, lanceolate leaves that feature wavy or dentate margins and a slightly hairy texture. The foliage typically grows close to the ground, forming a compact cluster from which a single, slender flowering stalk eventually emerges. This stem carries several small, yellow composite flowers characterized by numerous ray florets. The roots are robust and fleshy, allowing the plant to anchor firmly in its substrate. Each leaf possesses a distinct midrib that provides structural support to the thin blade.

What are the pharmacological activities of Scorzonera radiata?

This plant has antioxidative, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antidiabetic pharmacological activities. Its rich profile of bioactive secondary metabolites enables it to neutralize free radicals and mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. Research indicates that its extracts can effectively inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and fungi. Additionally, the presence of specific phytochemicals helps regulate blood glucose levels and reduce systemic inflammation. These diverse therapeutic properties suggest significant potential for developing new medicinal treatments.

As discussed in a study, the chromatographic separation of a crude extract from the aerial parts of Scorzonera radiata yielded 5 new dihydrostilbenes named scorzodihydrostilbene A-E (1-5). These 5 compounds exhibited antioxidative activity when analyzed in the DPPH (2,2-diphenyl-1-picrylhydrazyl) assay. For compounds 1 and 5, the antioxidant activities were found to be stronger than that of the well-known naturally occurring stilbene antioxidant resveratrol. The structures of these 5 compounds were unambiguously elucidated using one- and two-dimensional NMR and mass spectrometric data.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Additionally, the quinic acid congeners and derivatives play important roles in the plant's metabolic pathways. The specific stilbene compounds, identified as scorzodihydrostilbenes A-E, represent unique structural components within its chemical makeup.

As discussed in a study, chromatographic separation of a crude extract obtained from the aerial parts of Scorzonera radiata yielded fifteen natural compounds. These include two new flavonoids and one new quinic acid congener. The extraction also identified four flavones and eight quinic acid derivatives. All of these identified substances are known natural compounds. The antioxidant activities of the quinic acid derivatives were evaluated using the DPPH assay.
