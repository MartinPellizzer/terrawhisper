Lagotis macrosiphon

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lagotis macrosiphon has a creeping rhizome that produces small, simple, opposite leaves with entire margins and a distinctively long, narrow petiole. The plant is characterized by its diminutive stature and delicate, herbaceous texture. Its leaves are typically lanceolate to linear in shape, often appearing somewhat fleshy. The stems are slender and tend to spread along the substrate to form low-growing mats. This morphology allows the species to effectively colonize moist, shaded environments.
