What's the taxonomical classification of Tripterospermum pallidum?

Tripterospermum pallidum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Gentianaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Tripterospermum and the species pallidum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tripterospermum pallidum has a slender, herbaceous growth habit characterized by pale green stems and small, lanceolate leaves arranged oppositely along the branches.

The leaves are typically thin and lack significant pubescence, contributing to the plant's delicate appearance. Its reproductive structures feature small, inconspicuous flowers that transition into distinctive three-winged seeds. These specialized seeds are designed for wind dispersal, which is a defining morphological trait of the genus.

The overall structure of the plant remains relatively compact and low to the ground.
