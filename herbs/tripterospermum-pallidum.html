What's the taxonomical classification of Tripterospermum pallidum?

Tripterospermum pallidum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Gentianaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Tripterospermum and the species pallidum.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Tripterospermum pallidum can cause severe skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. Contact with the plant's sap often results in redness, itching, or blistering on sensitive areas of the body. Individuals with existing skin conditions may experience more intense inflammatory responses upon exposure. Ingestion of the plant parts may lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to its chemical composition. Because specific toxicological studies are limited, any contact or consumption should be treated as potentially hazardous.
