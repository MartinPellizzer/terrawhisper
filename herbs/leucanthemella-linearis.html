What's the taxonomical classification of Leucanthemella linearis?

Leucanthemella linearis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Asterales. This plant is a member of the family Asteraceae, which places it within the genus Leucanthemella, ultimately reaching its specific taxonomic designation as the species linearis.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Leucanthemella linearis can cause severe skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or handled improperly. Some individuals may also experience dizziness or lightheadedness as a systemic reaction to its chemical constituents. Monitoring for these adverse effects is essential when working in environments where this species is present.
