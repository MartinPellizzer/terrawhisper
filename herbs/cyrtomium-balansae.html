Cyrtomium balansae

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyrtomium balansae has large, deeply pinnatifid fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length, featuring multiple pairs of narrow, oblong pinnae arranged along a central rachis. The stipe is typically robust and covered in scales, providing structural support for the expansive photosynthetic surface area. Each individual segment of the leaf displays a delicate, fine-textured appearance characteristic of many leptosporangiate ferns. The fertile fronds are often distinct in shape, bearing sori located on the underside of the pinnules. These reproductive structures are protected by indusia, which play a crucial role in spore dispersal within its humid habitat.
