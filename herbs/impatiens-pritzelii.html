What's the taxonomical classification of Impatiens pritzelii?

Impatiens pritzelii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the taxonomic order Ericales and is a member of the family Balsaminaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Impatiens and the species pritzelii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

The inflorescence is produced in the leaf axils, often appearing somewhat solitary or in small clusters. The root system is relatively shallow, adapted to the humus-rich soil of its native habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the montane forests of the Eastern Arc Mountains in Tanzania. It is specifically found within the high-altitude ecosystems of the Udzungwa Mountains. Because it is endemic to this specific region, its distribution is highly restricted and geographically isolated.
