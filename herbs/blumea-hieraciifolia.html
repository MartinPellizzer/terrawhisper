Blumea hieraciifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea hieraciifolia?

Blumea hieraciifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the biological hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, this plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. More specifically, it is identified by the genus Blumea, with its unique species designation being hieraciifolia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Blumea hieraciifolia contains bioactive secondary metabolites including terpenoids, flavonoids, tannins, and various essential oils that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These chemical constituents provide the plant with significant antioxidant and antimicrobial properties used in traditional medicine. Specifically, the presence of specific sesquiterpenes and phenolic compounds supports its application in treating inflammatory conditions. Research indicates that these compounds may also exhibit cytotoxic activities against certain cell lines. The complex synergy of these organic molecules makes the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological development.

What are the therapeutic actions of Blumea hieraciifolia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, wounds, digestive disorders, respiratory issues, and inflammatory conditions. Its leaves are frequently applied topically to soothe skin irritations, heal cuts, and reduce swelling. When used internally, decoctions of the plant can help alleviate stomach aches and manage gastrointestinal distress. It is also employed in traditional medicine to mitigate symptoms of coughs and other lung-related discomforts. The plant contains bioactive compounds that contribute to these diverse therapeutic applications.
