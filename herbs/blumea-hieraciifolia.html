Blumea hieraciifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Blumea hieraciifolia?

Blumea hieraciifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the biological hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, this plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. More specifically, it is identified by the genus Blumea, with its unique species designation being hieraciifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Blumea hieraciifolia has a perennial woody habit characterized by erect stems, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and small yellow flower heads arranged in terminal corymbose cymes. The foliage typically features a pubescent texture, often covered in fine hairs that can give the surface a soft or velvety appearance. Its leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem and taper toward the apex. The inflorescence consists of numerous small capitula that cluster together at the ends of the branches. This species is distinguished by its specific leaf shape and the dense, glandular hairs found on its vegetative parts.
