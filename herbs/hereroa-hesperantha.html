What is Hereroa hesperantha?

Hereroa hesperantha is a succulent plant species belonging to the family Aizoaceae, commonly known as the fig-marigold family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow petals and dark red to purple centers, which are borne in clusters at the tips of the stems. The plant has fleshy, greyish-green leaves that are narrow and pointed, arranged oppositely along the stems. Hereroa hesperantha grows up to 30 cm in height and 20 cm in width, with stems that are branching and woody at the base.

The plant reproduces by seed, producing small, brownish-black seeds in capsules that split open to release the seeds.
