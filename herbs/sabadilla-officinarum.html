Sabadilla (Sabadilla Officinarum)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Sabadilla officinarum.

Sabadilla officinarum, also known as the Spanish fly or sabadilla, is a plant native to South America, particularly cultivated in regions like Peru and Bolivia. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its antifungal and antiparasitic properties, making it a valuable remedy for treating skin infections and parasitic infestations. The plant's active compounds, primarily sabadilline and sabadilline derivatives, contribute to its medicinal effects by disrupting the cell membranes of fungi and parasites. Although it has been used historically for its medicinal benefits, its use today is limited due to its potential toxicity and the availability of more effective and safer alternatives. Despite its reduced contemporary use, Sabadilla officinarum remains an important plant in the study of natural remedies and traditional pharmacology.
