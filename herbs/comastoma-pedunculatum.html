Comastoma pedunculatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Comastoma pedunculatum has woody, climbing stems that form dense lianas with small, opposite, elliptical leaves. Its reproductive morphology is characterized by long-pedicelled flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers typically possess a tubular corolla which is a defining trait of the genus.
