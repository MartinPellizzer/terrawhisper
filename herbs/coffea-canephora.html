Coffea canephora

What's the taxonomical classification of Coffea canephora?

Coffea canephora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Gentianaales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Coffea and is specifically designated as the species canephora.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Coffea canephora has evergreen, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green, glossy, and measure between 10 and 25 centimeters in length.

These leaves grow in opposite pairs along woody, upright stems that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that emerge from the leaf axils during flowering periods. Following pollination, the plant develops small, spherical drupes that transition from green to bright red as they ripen.

The overall structure is a dense shrub or small tree characterized by a robust branching pattern.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Coffea canephora contains medicinal compounds primarily located in its seeds, leaves, and fruit pulp. The seeds, commonly known as coffee beans, are the most widely utilized part due to their high concentration of caffeine and chlorogenic acids. These bioactive components are studied for their potential to improve cognitive function, increase metabolic rates, and provide antioxidant effects. Additionally, the leaves can be processed into teas that offer various phytochemical benefits. The fruit pulp, or mucilage, also contains bioactive substances that are being investigated for their nutritional and therapeutic properties.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex array of bioactive phytochemicals including caffeine, trigonelline, chlorogenic acids, quinate esters, flavonoids, cafestol, and kahweol.

Caffeine acts as a potent central nervous system stimulant, while trigonelline contributes to various metabolic processes. The presence of chlorogenic acids and quinate esters provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Flavonoids within the plant further support cellular health through their ability to scavenge free radicals.

Finally, the diterpenes cafestol and kahweol are known to influence specific enzymatic pathways in the body.

The primary medicinal compounds of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Trigonelline
  • Caffeine
  • Chlorogenic acids
  • Cafestol
  • Kahweol
  • Quinate esters
  • Flavonoids

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Coffea canephora targets adenosine receptors, phosphodiesterase enzymes, and various antioxidant-related proteins to produce its physiological effects. The primary bioactive component, caffeine, acts as a competitive antagonist for adenosine A1 and A2A receptors, which prevents sleepiness and enhances alertness. By inhibiting phosphodiesterase enzymes, the plant increases intracellular cyclic AMP levels, leading to increased metabolic rates and lipolysis. Additionally, its high concentration of chlorogenic acids allows it to modulate glucose metabolism and reduce oxidative stress by targeting enzymes involved in inflammatory pathways. These interactions collectively contribute to the plant's stimulant, metabolic, and neuroprotective properties.

What are the therapeutic actions of Coffea canephora?

Coffea canephora has therapeutic properties that include acting as a potent stimulant, an analeptic, a diuretic, and a vasoconstrictor.

Its primary effect is stimulating the central nervous system to increase alertness and reduce fatigue. As an analeptic, it helps restore vigor and can aid in the recovery of vital functions during states of exhaustion. The plant also functions as a diuretic by increasing urine production to assist in fluid excretion.

Additionally, it acts as a vasoconstrictor, which can influence blood pressure and systemic circulation.

The primary therapeutic actions of this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Stimulant
  • Analeptic
  • Diuretic
  • Vasoconstrictor

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat coffee leaf rust (hemileiia vastatrix), coffee berry disease (colletotrichum kahawae), coffee leaf miner (leucoptera coffeella), cercospora leaf spot (cercospora coffeicola), and coffee anthracnose (colletotrichum gloeosporioides).

These various fungal, bacterial, and insect-driven ailments can significantly diminish the quality and quantity of the coffee harvest. Managing coffee leaf rust and cercospora leaf spot is essential to preventing widespread defoliation across plantations. Additionally, addressing coffee berry disease and coffee anthracnose helps protect the fruit from rot and decay.

Controlling the coffee leaf miner is also a vital part of maintaining the overall health of the plant.

The main common ailment treated using this plant are shown in the list below.

  • Coffee leaf rust (hemileia vastatrix)
  • Coffee berry disease (colletotrichum kahawae)
  • Coffee leaf miner (leucoptera coffeella)
  • Cercospora leaf spot (cercospora coffeicola)
  • Coffee anthracnose (colletotrichum gloeosporioides)

What are the herbal preparations of Coffea canephora?

Coffea canephora is prepared primarily as a roasted bean infusion, an extract, or a concentrated powder intended for oral consumption. The seeds undergo intense heat treatment to transform their chemical profile and develop characteristic flavor compounds. Once roasted, the beans are typically ground into varying degrees of fineness to facilitate brewing through methods like espresso or drip filtration. These preparations can also be processed into instant soluble powders or concentrated liquid extracts for rapid reconstitution. Additionally, specialized pharmaceutical or cosmetic preparations may utilize refined extracts from the plant for topical or systemic applications.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Coffea canephora can cause insomnia, increased heart rate, anxiety, and gastrointestinal distress due to its high concentration of caffeine. Because this species, commonly known as Robusta, contains nearly twice the caffeine of Arabica beans, the physiological stimulation is significantly more intense. Excessive consumption may lead to jitters, palpitations, and a feeling of nervousness in sensitive individuals. Some people also report headaches or digestive upset after consuming large amounts of its extract. Managing intake is essential to avoid these stimulant-driven side effects.
