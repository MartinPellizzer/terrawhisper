Anthogonium gracile

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anthogonium gracile has slender, upright stems that typically reach heights of ten to thirty centimeters and possess a delicate, wiry texture. The leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing alternately along the length of the stem to minimize wind resistance. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by their subtle coloration and minimal petal size. The reproductive structures are contained within compact capsules that eventually dry and release tiny seeds. These morphological traits collectively allow the plant to thrive in open, breezy habitats.
