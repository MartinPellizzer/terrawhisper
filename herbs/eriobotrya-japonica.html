Loquat

Eriobotrya japonica

Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) is a small evergreen tree native to China, known for its sweet, tangy fruit and its long history of use in traditional medicine. It is primarily used to treat respiratory conditions such as coughs, bronchitis, and asthma due to its expectorant and anti-inflammatory properties.

Taxonomy

Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) is a species belonging to the genus Eriobotrya in the family Rosaceae, which is part of the order Rosales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) is a deciduous tree characterized by its ovate to elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy surface, small white flowers that appear in early spring, and yellow-orange berries that ripen in late summer to early autumn.

Distribution

Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) is native to China and is primarily found in the southern regions, including provinces such as Guangdong, Guangxi, Sichuan, and Yunnan, with cultivation also extending to other parts of East Asia like Korea and Japan.

Traditional Uses

Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its expectorant, antitussive, and digestive properties, often employed to treat respiratory conditions such as coughs, bronchitis, and asthma, as well as to aid digestion and relieve gastrointestinal discomfort.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Traditional Chinese Medicine, Ayurveda, and Native American herbal practices, Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) has been used for its medicinal properties, particularly for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Phytochemistry

The phytochemistry of Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) involves the presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, triterpenoids, iridoids, and organic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic potential.

Pharmacology

Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by its ability to regulate respiratory function, reduce inflammation, and support digestive health through its traditional use in treating coughs, asthma, and gastrointestinal disorders.

Botanical Identification

Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) is a species in the Rosaceae family. Common names include Japanese loquat and wax apple. It is a deciduous tree native to China. The plant has smooth bark, elliptical leaves, and yellow-orange flowers. Synonyms in herbal literature include Chinese white plum and Japanese white plum.

Safety Profile

Loquat is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Consult a healthcare provider before use, especially if you have allergies or are on other medications.
