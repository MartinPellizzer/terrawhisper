What are the pest and diseases tips of Eriobotrya japonica?

Eriobotrya japonica, commonly known as loquat or Japanese plum, is a deciduous evergreen tree that is susceptible to several pests and diseases.

One of the most common pests affecting loquat trees is the loquat psyllid (Bactericera cockerelli), which feeds on the sap of the tree and can cause curled or distorted leaves, stunted growth, and reduced fruit production. Additionally, the loquat aphid (Toxoptera citricidus) can also infest loquat trees, causing curling and distortion of young leaves, as well as the formation of honeydew droplets that can attract other pests. As for diseases, loquat trees are susceptible to root rot caused by fungal pathogens such as Phytophthora spp., which can be spread through contaminated irrigation water or soil.

Leaf spot diseases caused by fungi like Colletotrichum spp. and Septoria spp. can also affect loquat trees, resulting in yellowing or browning of leaves, defoliation, and reduced tree vigor. Fungal cankers caused by species like Botryosphaeria dothidea and Diaporthe spp.

can also infect loquat trees, causing lesions on the trunk, branches, and twigs that can lead to branch dieback and eventually kill the tree if left untreated.
