Poa pratensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the hierarchical structure of botanical classification, it falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. At the most specific levels of its taxonomic identity, it is placed within the genus Poa, with its unique biological designation being the species pratensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Poa pratensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by a dense network of creeping rhizomes and fibrous roots that form a thick sod. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically bluish-green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowering stalks, or culms, grow upright and emerge from the base to reach heights between 30 and 90 centimeters. The inflorescence is a loose, open panicle composed of numerous small, spikelet-bearing branches. Each spikelet contains several florets that are relatively small and inconspicuous. These morphological traits allow the species to spread efficiently through vegetative reproduction while maintaining a competitive presence in turfgrass environments.
