Pilea angulata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pilea anguulat has fleshy, ovate leaves with distinctly angular, wavy margins and a bright green, textured surface. The foliage grows from a central stem and exhibits a slightly succulent quality due to its thickened leaf structure. These leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems, creating a dense and lush appearance. The plant's architecture is characterized by its compact, bushy habit and delicate branching patterns. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically emerge from the leaf axils during its blooming period.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Brazil, specifically within the Atlantic Forest biome. It thrives in the moist, shaded understories characteristic of these South American rainforests. Within these habitats, it is often found growing in high-humidity environments where organic matter is plentiful. Its distribution is largely restricted to these specific microclimates found along the Brazilian coast.
