Peucedanum ostruthium

Love-In-A-Mist (Peucedanum ostruthium) is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe, known for its delicate white or purple flowers and its traditional use in herbal medicine to support respiratory and digestive health.

Morphology

Love-In-A-Mist (Peucedanum ostruthium) is a biennial herb with slender, erect stems that are often branched, hairy, and range from 30 to 100 cm in height, bearing pale blue to white flowers in clustered umbels at the tips of the branches.

Distribution

Love-In-A-Mist (Peucedanum ostruthium) is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, particularly thriving in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, woodlands, and along riverbanks.

Traditional Uses

In traditional herbal medicine, Love-In-A-Mist has been used to treat respiratory conditions, digestive ailments, and as a mild sedative to calm the nervous system.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional medicinal systems such as Chinese medicine, Ayurveda, and European herbalism, Love-In-A-Mist has been utilized for its purported diuretic, expectorant, and digestive properties.

The phytochemistry of Love-In-A-Mist (Peucedanum ostruthium) includes a variety of bioactive compounds such as sesquiterpene lactones, alkaloids, and flavonoids, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory support.
