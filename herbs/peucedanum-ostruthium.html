Love-In-A-Mist (Peucedanum ostruthium): Benefits, Uses, Dosage, and Safety in Herbal Medicine

What is Love-In-A-Mist (Peucedanum Ostruthium)?

Love-In-A-Mist (Peucedanum ostruthium) is a biennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and pale blue or white flowers that resemble a mist, hence its common name.

Love-In-A-Mist (Peucedanum ostruthium) is a biennial herb with slender, erect stems that are often branched, hairy, and range from 30 to 100 cm in height, bearing pale blue to white flowers in clustered umbels at the tips of the branches.

Love-In-A-Mist (Peucedanum ostruthium) is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe, including parts of the Alps, Carpathians, and the Balkans, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and rocky slopes.
