What to know about Peucedanum ostruthium (masterwort) before using it medicinally

Masterwort, scientifically known as Peucedanum ostruthium, is a herb that has been revered for its impressive health properties, which have been shown to improve digestion, reduce inflammation, and even exhibit anti-cancerous qualities.

In the kitchen, masterwort is often used to add depth and complexity to soups, stews, and sauces, while its leaves and stems can be used fresh or dried as a flavorful garnish. As a hardy perennial plant, masterwort is relatively easy to cultivate and requires minimal maintenance, making it a popular choice among gardeners. From a botanical perspective, masterwort belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped flower clusters and pungent aroma.

With roots dating back to ancient Greek and Roman medicine, masterwort has been documented in historical texts as a treatment for various ailments, including fever, rheumatism, and even snake bites.
