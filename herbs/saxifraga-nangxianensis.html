Saxifraga nangxianensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saxifraga nangxianensis has a perennial growth habit characterized by a basal rosette of leaves and an erect flowering stem that typically reaches a height of 10 to 25 centimeters. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, featuring crenate or serrated margins and a texture that can range from glabrous to slightly pubescent. Its inflorescence is a terminal cyme that bears small, five-petaled flowers with a distinct color pattern often consisting of white petals with subtle markings. The scape is slender and upright, supporting the cluster of blossoms above the ground-level foliage. These morphological traits are adapted to its specific high-altitude rocky habitat.
