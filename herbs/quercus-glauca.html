Quercus glauca

What's the taxonomical classification of Quercus glauca?

Quercus glauca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Fagales. This plant is a member of the family Fagaceae, falling under the genus Quercus, and is specifically identified by its species name, glauca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Quercus glauca has evergreen leaves that are leathery, oblong, and characterized by a distinctive glaucous blue-gray color on the upper surface with a pale underside. The margins of these leaves are typically entire or slightly wavy, providing a smooth texture compared to many other oak species. Its branches are sturdy and often develop a rugged, grayish bark as the tree matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in catkins, followed by the production of small, nut-like acorns nestled in shallow cups. The overall growth habit forms a dense, spreading canopy that maintains its foliage throughout the year.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of bioactive medicinal compounds, most notably a significant concentration of ellagitannins. These specific polyphenolic compounds are known for their potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. In addition to these tannins, the species is rich in various flavonoids that contribute to its therapeutic potential. Such chemical constituents work together to provide protective effects against oxidative stress in biological systems. The presence of these specific secondary metabolites makes the plant a subject of interest for pharmacological research.

According to a study published by "Yakugaku zasshi : Journal of the Pharmaceutical Society of Japan", Quercus glauca contains ellagitanniins. These specific compounds are present in the young leaves of the plant. As the leaves of Quercus glauca mature, the ellagitanniins undergo a process of oxidative degradation. This dynamic change occurs as the plant develops over time.
