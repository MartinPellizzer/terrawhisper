Quercus glauca

What's the taxonomical classification of Quercus glauca?

Quercus glauca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Fagales. This plant is a member of the family Fagaceae, falling under the genus Quercus, and is specifically identified by its species name, glauca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Quercus glauca has evergreen leaves that are leathery, oblong, and characterized by a distinctive glaucous blue-gray color on the upper surface with a pale underside. The margins of these leaves are typically entire or slightly wavy, providing a smooth texture compared to many other oak species. Its branches are sturdy and often develop a rugged, grayish bark as the tree matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers appear in catkins, followed by the production of small, nut-like acorns nestled in shallow cups. The overall growth habit forms a dense, spreading canopy that maintains its foliage throughout the year.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

These extracts are often utilized in traditional practices to treat skin inflammations and promote wound healing. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Quercus glauca targets inflammatory enzymes such as cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and various pro-inflammatory cytokines like tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) to exert its therapeutic effects. These bioactive compounds also demonstrate an ability to inhibit matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) that are often involved in tissue degradation and cancer progression. Additionally, certain extracts interact with antioxidant enzymes to mitigate oxidative stress by scavenging free radicals and regulating superoxide dismutase (SOD) activity. This multifaceted interaction with diverse protein families accounts for the plant's reported anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and potential anti-tumor properties.
