Artocarpus styracifolius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artocarpus styracifolius has large, coriaceous leaves that are typically elliptic or oblong in shape, featuring entire margins and an acute or acuminate apex. The leaf texture is notably thick and leathery, often possessing a glossy upper surface and a paler underside. The venation pattern is prominent, with secondary veins extending toward the leaf edges in a pinnate arrangement. Its woody stem supports a spreading canopy, which is characteristic of many species within the Moraceae family. The reproductive structures consist of specialized inflorescences that eventually develop into fleshy, aggregate fruits.
