Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) Uses

Yarrow is used to treat skin inflammation, wound healing, insect bites, and skin infections. It helps with inflammatory skin conditions, dermatitis, and irritation. It may also support respiratory infections due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) Monograph

1. Skin inflammation

Yarrow is used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation. Topical applications of yarrow infusions or salves can soothe conditions like eczema or insect bites. Its soothing effects make it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for skin care.

2. Wound healing

Yarrow promotes wound healing through its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps reduce bleeding, prevent infection, and accelerate tissue repair. Traditionally applied as a poultice or infusion, it supports skin regeneration and is effective for minor cuts, abrasions, and bruises.

3. Insect bites

Yarrow is traditionally used to alleviate symptoms of insect bites. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties help reduce swelling, itching, and irritation. A poultice made from fresh yarrow leaves or a diluted tincture can be applied directly to the affected area for relief. It is often recommended as a first-aid remedy in herbal medicine.

4. Skin infection

Yarrow is used topically to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on the skin. A poultice or infused oil made from fresh yarrow can be applied directly to the affected area. It is particularly effective for minor wounds, boils, and fungal infections.

5. Skin infections

Yarrow is used to treat skin infections due to its antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. It can help reduce redness, swelling, and bacterial growth on the skin. Topical applications of yarrow infusions or salves are common in herbal medicine for minor cuts, rashes, and infections. It is often combined with other herbs for enhanced effectiveness.

6. Skin wounds

Yarrow is traditionally used to treat skin wounds due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce bleeding, promote healing, and prevent infection. Topical applications of yarrow preparations can soothe irritated skin and accelerate tissue repair. Its historical use in wound care supports its role in herbal medicine for minor cuts and abrasions.

7. Inflammatory skin conditions

Yarrow is used topically for inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation. A decoction or infused oil can be applied directly to the affected area. It is particularly effective for eczema, psoriasis, and minor burns.

8. Skin irritation

Yarrow is used to treat skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and itching associated with minor skin conditions. A poultice or infusion can be applied directly to the affected area for soothing relief. It is particularly effective for eczema, rashes, and insect bites.

9. Dermatitis

Yarrow is used topically for dermatitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and itching associated with skin irritations. A poultice or infused oil made from fresh yarrow can be applied directly to the affected area. It is particularly effective for eczema and contact dermatitis.

10. Respiratory infection

Yarrow is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and easing congestion. It may help alleviate symptoms of colds, bronchitis, and other respiratory infections. Its antiseptic and expectorant properties aid in clearing mucus and soothing irritated airways. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture for respiratory support.
