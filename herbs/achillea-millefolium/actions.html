Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) Actions

Yarrow exhibits several key therapeutic actions, including anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and antispasmodic properties. It also acts as a carminative, tonic, and sedative, helping to relieve digestive discomfort and promote relaxation. Its bitter and astringent qualities support digestive health, while its expectorant and stimulant effects aid respiratory and systemic function.

Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) Monograph

1. Anti-inflammatory

Yarrow has an anti-inflammatory action, primarily due to its active compounds like achilleine and azulene. These compounds help reduce inflammation by inhibiting inflammatory pathways in the body. It is commonly used for conditions like skin irritations and respiratory inflammation. Its effects are supported by traditional use and some scientific studies.

2. Diuretic

Yarrow has a diuretic action, promoting the elimination of excess fluids through urine. This helps reduce swelling and supports kidney function. It is particularly useful for conditions involving fluid retention, such as edema or hypertension. Its gentle nature makes it suitable for long-term use under professional guidance.

3. Antispasmodic

Yarrow has an antispasmodic action, helping to relieve muscle spasms and cramps. It works by relaxing smooth muscle tissue, making it useful for conditions like colic, menstrual cramps, and digestive issues. Its effects are supported by traditional use and some modern research.

4. Carminative

Yarrow has a carminative action, helping to relieve gas and bloating by stimulating the movement of the digestive tract. It reduces intestinal discomfort and supports the elimination of excess gas, making it useful for digestive ailments. Its gentle action makes it suitable for regular use in herbal formulations.

5. Tonic

Yarrow has a tonic action, supporting the body's overall vitality and function. It helps strengthen the digestive system and may improve energy levels. Its tonic properties are attributed to its ability to stimulate and balance bodily systems. This action makes it useful in cases of fatigue and weakened constitution.

6. Sedative

Yarrow has a sedative action, helping to calm the nervous system and reduce anxiety. It promotes relaxation and can aid in managing restlessness or insomnia when used appropriately. This effect is attributed to its compounds that influence neurotransmitter activity.

7. Bitter

Yarrow has a bitter action, which stimulates digestion and increases saliva and gastric secretions. This action helps to enhance nutrient absorption and support the liver and gallbladder functions. The bitterness also aids in clearing mucus and reducing inflammation in the respiratory and digestive systems.

8. Astringent

Yarrow has an astringent action, which means it helps to constrict tissues and reduce secretions. This property makes it useful for treating conditions like diarrhea, bleeding, and skin irritations. It works by tightening blood vessels and mucous membranes, promoting healing and reducing inflammation.

9. Expectorant

Yarrow has an expectorant action, helping to loosen and expel mucus from the respiratory tract. It is traditionally used for conditions like bronchitis and coughs where excessive mucus is present. Its effectiveness is attributed to its anti-inflammatory and mild stimulant properties. It supports clearing the airways and improving breathing.

10. Stimulant

Yarrow has a stimulant action, enhancing circulation and metabolic activity. It invigorates the body by increasing blood flow and supporting the cardiovascular system. This effect helps improve energy levels and overall vitality. It is commonly used to combat fatigue and promote a sense of well-being.
