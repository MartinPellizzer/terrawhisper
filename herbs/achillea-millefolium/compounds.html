Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) Compounds

Yarrow contains several key active compounds, including essential oils, flavonoids, and coumarin. These compounds contribute to its anti-inflammatory, antiseptic, and circulatory benefits. Other notable components are phenolic acids, terpenoids, and glycosides, which support its traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) Monograph

1. Essential oil

The essential oil of Yarrow (Achillea millefolium) contains compounds like chamazulene and bisabolol. These components contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties. The oil is commonly used in aromatherapy and topical applications for digestive and respiratory support.

2. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in Yarrow (Achillea millefolium). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. These compounds help support the immune system and may aid in reducing swelling and irritation. Flavonoids are commonly used in herbal medicine for their therapeutic effects.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Yarrow (Achillea millefolium). It contributes to the plant's antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural anticoagulant, supporting cardiovascular health. Its presence enhances Yarrow's traditional use in treating digestive and respiratory ailments.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Yarrow (Achillea millefolium). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Found in its leaves and flowers, phenolic acid supports digestive health and skin healing. It plays a vital role in the traditional uses of yarrow in herbal medicine.

5. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a key class of active compounds in Yarrow (Achillea millefolium). They contribute to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Terpenoids like aromadendrin and achilleine are found in Yarrow's essential oils. These compounds support its traditional use in treating wounds and digestive issues.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a compound found in Yarrow (Achillea millefolium). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Glycosides in Yarrow help support digestive health and reduce symptoms of inflammation. They are often used in herbal formulations for their therapeutic benefits.

7. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Yarrow (Achillea millefolium). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and helps reduce inflammation and bleeding. Tannins also support wound healing and are commonly used in traditional herbal remedies for digestive and skin conditions.

8. Saponin

Saponin is a compound found in Yarrow (Achillea millefolium). It contributes to the plant's mild diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. Saponins may support digestive health and help reduce swelling in the body. They are part of the complex mixture of active compounds in Yarrow that contribute to its traditional medicinal uses.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Yarrow (Achillea millefolium). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though Yarrow is more renowned for its sesquiterpene lactones. Alkaloids in Yarrow may support its traditional use in treating inflammatory and digestive conditions.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Yarrow (Achillea millefolium). It contributes to the plant's color and has antioxidant properties. Anthocyanin supports anti-inflammatory and protective effects in herbal medicine. It is one of several bioactive compounds in Yarrow used for its therapeutic benefits.
