Botanical Identification of Golden Shower Tree (Cassia fistula)

To identify Golden Shower Tree (Cassia fistula) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Golden Shower Tree (Cassia fistula) Monograph

Scientific Classification

The Golden Shower Tree, Cassia fistula, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Cassia, and species Cassia fistula. This classification highlights its relationship to other leguminous plants and its botanical significance in herbal medicine.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The Golden Shower Tree is scientifically known as Cassia fistula, a name that reflects its historical and medicinal significance. The author citation for this species is L., indicating it was first described by Carl von Linné. This name is widely recognized in botanical literature and is used as the standard reference for the species.

Morphological Characteristics

The Golden Shower Tree, Cassia fistula, is a deciduous tree with a straight, slender trunk and a spreading crown. It has a taproot system with lateral roots, providing stability. Its leaves are bipinnate, with many small leaflets arranged in clusters. The tree produces long, drooping inflorescences of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. The fruit is a flattened pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

The Golden Shower Tree can be distinguished by its opposite, pinnately compound leaves with 10-20 pairs of leaflets, yellow-orange inflorescences with clustered flowers, and pods that are cylindrical and slightly curved. It is also recognized by its bright yellow flowers that hang in drooping clusters, which are distinct from the more upright or different-colored inflorescences of similar species.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

The Golden Shower Tree is native to South and Southeast Asia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It typically grows at altitudes between 200 and 1,200 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

The Golden Shower Tree blooms from March to May, producing bright yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from September to November. For identification purposes, seeds are best harvested in late October to early November when they are fully mature and ready for collection.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Cassia fistula belongs to the genus Cassia in the family Fabaceae. Its closest relatives include species like Cassia alata and Cassia tora. It shares key family traits such as compound leaves and legume pods, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family.
