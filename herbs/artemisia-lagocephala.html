Artemisia lagocephala

What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia lagocephala?

Artemisia lagocephala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Asterales, the plant is a member of the widely recognized family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Artemisia and the species epithet lagocephala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia lagocephala has erect, perennial stems that reach heights of up to 2 meters and bear pinnately lobed leaves with deeply serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a distinct bicolor appearance, featuring dark green upper surfaces and silvery-white tomentose undersides. Small, discoid flower heads are arranged in dense, terminal panicles that emerge during the late summer or autumn. These inflorescences consist of numerous tiny, tubular florets that lack ray petals. The plant's overall structure is bushy and robust, adapted to various wetland and meadow habitats.
