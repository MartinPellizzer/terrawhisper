What's the taxonomical classification of Saccharum rufipilum?

Saccharum rufipilum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic lineage through the order Poales, it is placed in the family Poaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by the genus Saccharum and the species rufipilum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saccharum rufipilum has perennial, creeping rhizomes and erect, slender culms that reach heights of up to 2 meters with linear, lanceolate leaves featuring prominent midveins. Its inflorescence is a compact, terminal panicle consisting of numerous small, silvery-white spikelets. Each spikelet is characterized by long, reddish-brown or rufous awns that provide the species with its specific name.
