Botanical Identification of Tora Senna (Senna tora)

To identify Tora Senna (Senna tora) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Tora Senna, or Senna tora, belongs to the plant kingdom, specifically the division of flowering plants, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Senna, and species Senna tora. It is a member of the legume family known for its medicinal and ecological significance.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

Senna tora is the scientific name of Tora Senna. It was first described by the botanist Carl von Linné. The plant has several synonyms, including Cassia tora and Senna chionantha. It is known by various common names such as Tora, Indian Senna, and Kothala. Regional and historical names vary but often reflect its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Morphological Characteristics

Tora Senna is an annual herb with a branched root system. It has erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound with 5-9 pairs of oval leaflets. The inflorescence consists of racemes of yellow flowers. The fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are small and black.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Tora Senna can be distinguished by its palmately lobed leaves with five to seven pointed lobes, yellow flowers in clusters, and capsule fruits that split open to release small, flat seeds. It differs from similar species like Senna alexandrina by its leaf shape and the presence of a distinct, hairy calyx.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Tora Senna is native to regions across southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. It thrives in warm, temperate climates with well-drained soils. It commonly grows at low to moderate altitudes, typically between 0 and 1,000 meters above sea level.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Tora Senna blooms from April to June, producing yellow flowers. Seed formation occurs from July to September. For identification, harvest timing is critical between August and October when the plant is mature and seeds are fully developed.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Tora Senna (Senna tora) belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is closely related to other Senna species. It shares key characteristics with its genus, such as compound leaves and yellow flowers, and is phylogenetically linked to other legumes through shared morphological and genetic traits. Its closest relatives include other Senna species within the same genus.
