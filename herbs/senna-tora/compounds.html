Tora Senna (Senna tora) Compounds

The primary compounds in Tora Senna include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, known for their astringent effects. Coumarin contributes to its anti-inflammatory actions, while saponins may support digestive health. Phenolic acids and glycosides also play roles in its medicinal profile.

Tora Senna (Senna tora) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Tora Senna (Senna tora). They contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds support the plant's use in traditional medicine for digestive and skin-related ailments.

2. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Tora Senna (Senna tora). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antidiarrheal effects. Tannins in Tora Senna may also support digestive health by binding to proteins and reducing intestinal irritation.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Tora Senna (Senna tora). It contributes to the plant's laxative effects by stimulating bowel movements. Coumarin also exhibits anti-inflammatory and anticoagulant properties, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine. Its presence enhances the therapeutic potential of Tora Senna in treating digestive and inflammatory conditions.
