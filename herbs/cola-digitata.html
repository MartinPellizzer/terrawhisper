What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cola digitata has a single, unbranched trunk that reaches heights of up to 15 meters and is characterized by a ringed surface formed by old leaf scars. From the crown of the tree, these foliage clusters emerge to create a dense canopy. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that eventually develop into fleshy, globose fruits.
