Corydalis edulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis edulis?

Corydalis eduledis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Ranunculales. This plant is a member of the family Papaveraceae, specifically falling under the genus Corydalis, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name eduledis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis eduledis has a tuberous rhizome, pinnately compound leaves with rounded to ovate leaflets, and zygomorphic flowers characterized by a long, nectar-bearing spur. The foliage typically emerges in early spring, providing a dense green canopy before the plant enters dormancy. Each flower features a distinctively shaped perianth where the sepals form a tubular structure extending into a slender spur. These blooms are often held on upright stalks and can vary in color depending on the specific variety. The overall structure is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded forest environments.
