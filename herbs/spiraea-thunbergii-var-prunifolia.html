Spiraea thunbergii var. prunifolia

Spiraea thunbergii var. prunifolia, also known as Manchurian spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its clusters of small white flowers and reddish-brown branches.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties, which are attributed to the presence of flavonoids, phenolic acids, and other bioactive compounds.

In herbal medicine, Spiraea thunbergii var. prunifolia is used to treat a range of health conditions, including fever, rheumatism, and digestive issues, with potential benefits including reduced inflammation, improved immune function, and antioxidant activity.

Herbal preparations of the plant, such as teas, tinctures, and infusions, can be made from the flowers, leaves, and stems, although caution should be exercised due to potential interactions with medications and allergic reactions.
