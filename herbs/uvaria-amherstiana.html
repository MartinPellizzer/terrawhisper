What is Uvaria amherstiana?

Uvaria amherstiana is a plant species belonging to the family Annonaceae.

It is a climbing shrub with a woody stem, reaching up to 10 meters in height, and has simple, alternate, and leathery leaves that are 10-20 cm long and 5-10 cm wide, with a short petiole and a rounded base. The flowers are small, white, and arranged in axillary panicles, with 6-8 petals and a 6-8-merous calyx. The fruit is a berry, ellipsoidal in shape, 2-3 cm long, and 1.5-2.5 cm in diameter, with a greenish-yellow skin and a single seed.

Uvaria amherstiana is native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.
