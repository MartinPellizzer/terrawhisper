What is Brunellia pinnata?

Brunellia pinnata is a plant species belonging to the family Brunelliaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrubs or small trees that grow up to 10 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and a straight, often unbranched trunk. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, measuring 10-20 cm in length and 5-10 cm in width, with a leathery texture and entire margins. The leaves are also characterized by their petioles, which are up to 2 cm long and glabrous.

Flowers are axillary, clustered, and dioecious, with staminate flowers in clusters of 2-5 and pistillate flowers in clusters of 3-5.
