What's the taxonomical classification of Juncus thomsonii?

Juncus thomsonii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Juncaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by the genus Juncus and is specifically designated as the species thomsonii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Juncus thomsonii has cylindrical, unbranched stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 40 centimeters and terminate in small, inconspicuous flowers.

The leaves are reduced to dark brown or blackish sheaths that tightly encircle the base of each stem. These plants feature small, brownish-green flowers arranged in compact clusters called cymes. Each flower contains six tepals that are lanceolate in shape and provide a subtle texture to the inflorescence.

The overall structure is compact and wiry, allowing it to thrive in saturated wetland environments.
