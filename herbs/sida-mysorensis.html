Sida mysorensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Sida mysorensis?

Sida mysorenensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Sida. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its unique species name, mysorenensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Sida mysorenensis has an erect, perennial herb morphology characterized by opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape and possess serrated margins. The stems are typically slender and can grow in a woody or herbaceous manner depending on the age of the plant. Small, yellowish flowers emerge from the leaf axils and feature five distinct petals. The fruit is a characteristic schizocarp that splits into several small nutlets upon maturity. These structural features are essential for identifying the species within the Malvaceae family.
