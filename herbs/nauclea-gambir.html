Gambir (Nauclea Gambir)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Nauclea gambir.

Gambir, scientifically known as Nauclea gambir, is a medicinal herb native to Southeast Asia, widely recognized for its traditional and modern therapeutic applications. This plant is valued for its unique properties as a natural adaptogen and a source of a rare alkaloid called gambireine, which contributes to its medicinal significance. Traditionally, Gambir has been used in indigenous and traditional medicine systems to treat ailments such as digestive issues, inflammation, and fatigue, often prepared as a tea or tincture. In modern wellness practices, it is gaining attention for its potential anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-boosting properties, making it a popular ingredient in herbal supplements and natural health products. Its distinctive bitter flavor and historical use in both culinary and medicinal contexts highlight its cultural importance and unique place in traditional healing practices.
