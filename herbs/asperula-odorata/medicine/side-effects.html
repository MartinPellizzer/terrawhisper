10 Possible Side Effects Of Asperula Odorata (Woodruff)

Asperula odorata has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal disturbances, skin rashes, and allergic reactions.

These side effects can be caused by excessive consumption, individual tolerance levels, or interactions with other medications.

For instance, gastrointestinal issues can lead to dehydration, weight loss, and fatigue, while skin rashes can cause discomfort, itching, and embarrassment, potentially affecting a person's quality of life and social interactions.

This is attributed to its chemical composition, which includes volatile oils and flavonoids that can trigger an immune response in sensitive people. These compounds may bind to allergen receptors on skin cells, leading to symptoms such as itchiness, redness, and hives.

In rare cases, Asperula odorata allergy can also cause more severe reactions, including anaphylaxis.
