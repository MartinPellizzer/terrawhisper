Sweet Potato (Ipomoea batatas)

Sweet Potato (Ipomoea batatas) is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, native to Tropical Americas, Southeast Asia, and Pacific Islands. Traditionally, its tubers, leaves, and stems have been used for culinary uses, decoctions, and infusions.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, tonic, and diuretic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, african traditional medicine, and traditional chinese medicine.

Elongated, herbaceous stems with a cylindrical cross-section, exhibiting a scrambling growth habit, bearing opposite branching, and characterized by a smooth, glabrous surface.
