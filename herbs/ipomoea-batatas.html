What are the pest and diseases tips of Ipomoea batatas?

Ipomoea batatas, commonly known as sweet potato, is a versatile and nutritious crop that is susceptible to various pests and diseases.

One of the most significant pests affecting sweet potato is the Sweet Potato Weevil (Cylas formicarius), which attacks the tubers and causes damage to the plant's stems and leaves. The Weevil lays its eggs on the roots, and the larvae feed on the plant's sap, leading to stunted growth, reduced yields, and increased susceptibility to other diseases.

Another common pest is the Sweet Potato Vine Borer (Melittia cucurbitae), which damages the vines and tubers by laying its eggs inside the stems. As for diseases, Sweet Potato is vulnerable to fungal infections such as Root Rot (Fusarium oxysporum) and Leaf Spot (Cercospora solani), which can cause significant yield loss.

Additionally, Viral diseases like Sweet Potato Virus Disease (SPVD) and Sweet Potato Mosaic Virus (SPMV) can also affect the crop's productivity, causing stunted growth, mottling, and leaf distortion.
