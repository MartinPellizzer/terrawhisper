Dioscorea delavayi

What's the taxonomical classification of Dioscorea delavayi?

Dioscorea delavayi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the taxonomic order Dioscoreales. This plant is a representative of the family Dioscoreaceae and is nested within the genus Dioscorea, eventually reaching its specific identification at the species level as Dioscorea delavayi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Dioscorea delavayi contains high concentrations of steroidal saponins, specifically diosgenin, which serves as a primary precursor for the synthesis of various steroid hormones. These bioactive compounds are extracted from the tubers and are utilized in pharmacological research to develop medications for endocrine regulation. Beyond saponins, the plant exhibits various secondary metabolites that contribute to its traditional medicinal applications. Researchers study these chemical constituents to explore potential anti-inflammatory and anticonvulsant properties. The presence of these specific alkaloids and steroidal structures makes the species a significant subject in phytochemical analysis.

What are the therapeutic actions of Dioscorea delavayi?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, stomach ailments, and various types of internal inflammation. The tubers are often processed into medicinal preparations to address localized skin irritations and infections. When used for digestive issues, the plant helps soothe stomach discomfort and regulates bowel functions. Practitioners also rely on its properties to mitigate systemic inflammatory responses throughout the body. These traditional applications vary depending on the specific part of the plant utilized in the remedy.
