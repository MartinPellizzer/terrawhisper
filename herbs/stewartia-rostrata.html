Stewartia rostrata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Stewartia rostrata has a multi-stemmed, deciduous growth habit characterized by smooth, exfoliating bark that peels away in patches to reveal striking orange, pink, and gray tones. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves with serrated margins that transition from a vibrant green in summer to brilliant shades of red and orange during autumn. Its flowers are showy and solitary, consisting of five white petals surrounding a prominent cluster of yellow stamens. The fruit develops into small, ovoid capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. This ornamental tree is highly valued for its unique bark texture and seasonal color displays.
