Medicinal Parts

Uncaria tomentosa root, also known as the "sangre de drago" or dragon's blood, is a key medicinal part of the plant, valued for its rich concentration of alkaloids, particularly mitraphylline and theobromine.

These compounds are believed to have anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and immune-modulating properties, making the root a popular component in traditional medicine for treating infections and supporting immune health. The root is often used in the form of tinctures, powders, or decoctions to harness its therapeutic benefits.

In addition to its alkaloid content, the root contains tannins and other bioactive compounds that contribute to its medicinal profile. While the root is a primary focus, other parts of the plant, such as the stem and leaf, also contain valuable compounds, though the root remains the most extensively studied and utilized part in modern herbal formulations.

Its historical use by indigenous communities in South America for treating wounds, infections, and digestive issues further underscores its significance in traditional and complementary medicine.
