Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa)

Chinese Tea Vine (Uncaria tomentosa) is a member of the Rubiaceae family, native to Amazon Basin, South America, and Central America. Traditionally, its leaves, bark, and stems have been used for decoctions, infusions, and tinctures.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in ayurvedic medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and native american herbal medicine.

Botanical Description

Growth Habit
Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically reaches a height of 1 to 3 meters.
Leaves
Smooth, dorsiventral leaves with upper surface pale green and lower surface darker green, bearing prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescences are terminal racemes bearing small, yellowish-white flowers with five sepals and five petals, arranged in a zygomorphic pattern, each flower lacking a corona and having two stamens.
Stems
Erect, branched, woody, with persistent pubescence and opposite phyllotaxis.
