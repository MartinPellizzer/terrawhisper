What to know about Uncaria tomentosa (cat's claw) before using it medicinally

Cat's claw, scientifically known as Uncaria tomentosa, is a revered herb that has been used for centuries to improve overall health and wellbeing.

Not only does it possess anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which can help to alleviate symptoms of arthritis and reduce the risk of chronic diseases, but it also boasts culinary uses such as adding flavor and texture to soups, stews, and salads. As a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, cat's claw is also prized for its ornamental value in horticultural settings, where its delicate yellow flowers and striking stems can add visual interest to gardens. From a botanical perspective, cat's claw is characterized by its unique, hooked tendrils that resemble a cat's claw, from which it gets its name.

With its rich history dating back to ancient Mayan and Inca civilizations, cat's claw has been used not only for medicinal and culinary purposes but also as a symbol of protection and spiritual guidance in many indigenous cultures around the world.
