Calotropis procera

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calotropis procera has large, thick, grayish-green leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess a waxy cuticle to prevent water loss. The stems are woody at the base and produce a milky white latex when injured. Its flowers are pentamerous and waxy, typically displaying a combination of white and purple hues with a prominent crown. The plant produces long, slender follicles that eventually split open to release numerous seeds equipped with tufts of silky hairs for wind dispersal. These morphological features collectively allow the species to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and the Indian subcontinent. It thrives in various tropical and subtropical environments characterized by low rainfall and high temperatures. Its range has expanded significantly due to human activity and its ability to colonize disturbed soils. Many populations are now naturalized across parts of Southeast Asia and even some Mediterranean climates. The species is particularly successful in sandy deserts and dry scrublands where other vegetation struggles to survive.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Calotropis procera contains medicinal compounds found in its leaves, latex, roots, stems, and flowers. The milky latex is frequently applied topically to treat skin ailments, warts, and inflammatory swellings. Dried leaves are often used as poultices to relieve joint pain and swelling associated with rheumatism. Root extracts are sometimes employed in traditional medicine to manage fever or intestinal issues, though they must be used with extreme caution. The various parts of the plant are valued for their bioactive alkaloids and glycosides which provide diverse therapeutic properties.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Calotropis procera contains a diverse array of bioactive phytochemicals including cardiac glycosides such as calotropin and calotoxin, flavonoids, triterpenoids, and various alkaloids. These specific glycosides are primarily responsible for the plant's potent pharmacological effects on the cardiovascular system. Beyond these compounds, the presence of tannins and saponins contributes to its significant antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Many traditional medicinal practices utilize these chemical constituents to treat ailments ranging from skin diseases to respiratory issues. The complex synergy of these secondary metabolites makes the plant a subject of interest for modern drug development.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Calotropis procera targets various enzymes and receptors, most notably inhibiting acetylcholinesterase, cyclooxygenase-2, and lipoxygenase to exert its pharmacological effects. The presence of cardiac glycosides like calotropin and calotoxin allows the plant to specifically interact with the Na+/K+-ATPase enzyme, which modulates ion transport across cell membranes. These bioactive compounds also influence inflammatory pathways by suppressing the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and prostaglandins. Furthermore, its phytochemical constituents interact with various protein kinases to demonstrate anti-tumor and antioxidant activities. This complex interaction with multiple molecular signaling pathways accounts for its diverse medicinal properties in treating inflammation and infections.

What are the therapeutic actions of Calotropis procera?

Calotropis procera has diverse therapeutic actions including emetic, purgative, anti-inflammatory, astringent, diuretic, alterative, anti-asthmatic, antipyretic, and immunomodulator properties. The plant functions as an emetic to induce vomiting and a purgative to clear the bowels when used in specific dosages. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent qualities help reduce swelling and tighten tissues, while its diuretic effect promotes increased urine production. It serves as an alterative to restore healthy bodily functions and acts as an anti-asthmatic agent to alleviate respiratory distress. Additionally, the plant possesses antipyretic capabilities to reduce fever and works as an immunomodulator to regulate the immune system.

What are the herbal preparations of Calotropis procera?

Calotropis procera is prepared as a latex, a decoction, a paste, a juice, or an infusion for various medicinal applications. The milky white latex is frequently applied topically to treat skin diseases or used as a poultice for swellings. Oral administration often involves drinking the juice or a decoction made from the leaves and roots to address ailments like asthma or intestinal worms. Additionally, the leaves can be crushed into a fine paste to soothe inflammation or used in oil infusions for topical massage. These different forms allow practitioners to target both internal and external health concerns using specific parts of the plant.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Calotropis procera can cause severe skin irritation, temporary blindness, and gastrointestinal distress if the toxic milky latex is ingested or touches the eyes. The milky sap contains cardiac glycosides that can disrupt heart rhythms if consumed in significant quantities. Contact with the plant's fluids often leads to redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If the sap enters the eyes, it may cause intense pain and potentially permanent vision damage. Ingestion of the plant parts can also result in nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.
