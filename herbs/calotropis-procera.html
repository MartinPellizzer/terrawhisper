Calotropis procera

What's the taxonomical classification of Calotropis procera?

Calotropis procera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Gentianaales. The plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae, which places it within the genus Calotropis, and it is specifically identified as the species procera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calotropis procera has large, thick, grayish-green leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess a waxy cuticle to prevent water loss.

The stems are woody at the base and produce a milky white latex when injured. Its flowers are pentamerous and waxy, typically displaying a combination of white and purple hues with a prominent crown. The plant produces long, slender follicles that eventually split open to release numerous seeds equipped with tufts of silky hairs for wind dispersal.

These morphological features collectively allow the species to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Calotropis procera contains medicinal compounds found in its leaves, latex, roots, stems, and flowers. The milky latex is frequently applied topically to treat skin ailments, warts, and inflammatory swellings. Dried leaves are often used as poultices to relieve joint pain and swelling associated with rheumatism. Root extracts are sometimes employed in traditional medicine to manage fever or intestinal issues, though they must be used with extreme caution. The various parts of the plant are valued for their bioactive alkaloids and glycosides which provide diverse therapeutic properties.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex profile of medicinal compounds including cardiac glycosides, alkaloids, flavonoids, terpenoids, phenolics, and steroids.

Among its most significant bioactive constituents are specific cardiac glycosides such as calotropin, calotoxin, and uscariin. These diverse chemical groups contribute to the plant's wide range of pharmacological activities and therapeutic potential. The presence of various alkaloids and terpenoids further enhances its traditional use in treating various ailments.

Ultimately, the synergistic effect of these steroids and phenolic compounds defines its medicinal properties.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Calotropis procera targets various enzymes and receptors, most notably inhibiting acetylcholinesterase, cyclooxygenase-2, and lipoxygenase to exert its pharmacological effects. The presence of cardiac glycosides like calotropin and calotoxin allows the plant to specifically interact with the Na+/K+-ATPase enzyme, which modulates ion transport across cell membranes. These bioactive compounds also influence inflammatory pathways by suppressing the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines and prostaglandins. Furthermore, its phytochemical constituents interact with various protein kinases to demonstrate anti-tumor and antioxidant activities. This complex interaction with multiple molecular signaling pathways accounts for its diverse medicinal properties in treating inflammation and infections.

What are the therapeutic actions of Calotropis procera?

Calotropis procera has diverse therapeutic actions including emetic, purgative, anti-inflammatory, astringent, diuretic, alterative, anti-asthmatic, antipyretic, and immunomodulator properties.

The plant functions as an emetic to induce vomiting and a purgative to clear the bowels when used in specific dosages. Its anti-inflammatory and astringent qualities help reduce swelling and tighten tissues, while its diuretic effect promotes increased urine production. It serves as an alterative to restore healthy bodily functions and acts as an anti-asthmatic agent to alleviate respiratory distress.

Additionally, the plant possesses antipyretic capabilities to reduce fever and works as an immunomodulator to regulate the immune system.

What common ailments are treated using this plant?

This plant is used to treat skin diseases, respiratory issues, inflammation, intestinal worms, and various types of pain.

The milky latex extracted from the leaves is frequently applied topically to manage wounds, skin infections, and insect bites. Ingesting prepared extracts can help alleviate symptoms of asthma and bronchitis by acting as an expectorant. Additionally, the plant serves as a traditional remedy for soothing rheumatism and joint swelling.

Medicinal preparations of the root and bark are also utilized to combat parasitic infestations in the digestive tract.

What are the herbal preparations of Calotropis procera?

Calotropis procera is prepared as a latex, a decoction, a paste, a juice, or an infusion for various medicinal applications. The milky white latex is frequently applied topically to treat skin diseases or used as a poultice for swellings. Oral administration often involves drinking the juice or a decoction made from the leaves and roots to address ailments like asthma or intestinal worms. Additionally, the leaves can be crushed into a fine paste to soothe inflammation or used in oil infusions for topical massage. These different forms allow practitioners to target both internal and external health concerns using specific parts of the plant.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Calotropis procera can cause severe skin irritation, temporary blindness, and gastrointestinal distress if the toxic milky latex is ingested or touches the eyes. The milky sap contains cardiac glycosides that can disrupt heart rhythms if consumed in significant quantities. Contact with the plant's fluids often leads to redness, itching, or blistering on the skin. If the sap enters the eyes, it may cause intense pain and potentially permanent vision damage. Ingestion of the plant parts can also result in nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.
