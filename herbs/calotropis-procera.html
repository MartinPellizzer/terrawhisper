Calotropis procera

What's the taxonomical classification of Calotropis procera?

Calotropis procera belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Gentianaales. The plant is a member of the family Apocynaceae, which places it within the genus Calotropis, and it is specifically identified as the species procera.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Calotropis procera has large, thick, grayish-green leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape and possess a waxy cuticle to prevent water loss. The stems are woody at the base and produce a milky white latex when injured. Its flowers are pentamerous and waxy, typically displaying a combination of white and purple hues with a prominent crown. The plant produces long, slender follicles that eventually split open to release numerous seeds equipped with tufts of silky hairs for wind dispersal. These morphological features collectively allow the species to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and the Indian subcontinent. It thrives in various tropical and subtropical environments characterized by low rainfall and high temperatures. Its range has expanded significantly due to human activity and its ability to colonize disturbed soils. Many populations are now naturalized across parts of Southeast Asia and even some Mediterranean climates. The species is particularly successful in sandy deserts and dry scrublands where other vegetation struggles to survive.

What are the pharmacological activities of Calotropis procera?

This plant has antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and antiviral pharmacological activities that make it a significant subject of medicinal research. Its phytochemical constituents allow it to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic bacteria and combat diverse microbial infections. The plant also demonstrates the ability to neutralize free radicals through its potent antioxidant properties. Furthermore, its anti-inflammatory effects help reduce swelling and pain in biological tissues. Studies have also indicated that its bioactive compounds possess antiviral potential against certain infectious agents.

As discussed in a study, Calotropis procera was found to contain 118 metabolites identified through gas chromatography-mass spectrometry. The extract demonstrated stronger antibacterial activity against Escherichia coli and Proteus mirabilis compared to other studied plants. Regarding molluscicidal activity against Biomphalaria alexandrina, Calotropis procera exhibited an LC50 value of 135 mg/L. When snails were exposed to sub-lethal concentrations (LC25) of the Calotropis procera extract, the survival rates were 5%. Additionally, the study noted that palmitic acid showed binding with acid, alkaline, and alanine aminotransferases in treated digestive gland snails.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including cardenolides, flavonoids, steroids, triterpenes, and alkaloids. These chemical constituents work together to provide various therapeutic properties and biological activities. The presence of alkaloids often contributes to the plant's potent pharmacological effects on the nervous system. Additionally, flavonoids and triterpenes act as powerful antioxidants that support cellular health. Steroids and cardenolides further enhance its medicinal profile by influencing metabolic and cardiac functions.

As discussed in a study, column chromatography analysis of methanol and butanol extracts of the aerial parts and the methanol extract of the latex of Calotropis procera led to the isolation of 8 cardenolides. These compounds include calotropin and calotoxin, which demonstrated significant cytotoxicity against MDA-MB-231 and A549 cell lines ranging from 0.046 to 0.072 μM. In comparison, the methanol extract and the wax of the latex showed cytotoxicity ranging from 0.47 to 58.41 μM. The study also identified several triterpene and flavonoid glycoside within the plant. These results suggest that Calotropis procera contains a wide spectrum of cardenolides that are promising agents for targeted cancer phytotherapy.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain, alongside neurological issues like dizziness and seizures. Contact with the plant's milky latex may result in intense skin irritation or painful eye irritation. If the sap enters the eyes, it can even lead to temporary blindness. Furthermore, ingesting the plant can trigger dangerous cardiac arrhythmia due to its toxic compounds. These systemic effects highlight the significant danger posed by all parts of Calotropis procera.
