Afzelia bipindensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Afzelia bipindensis has a large woody trunk with grayish bark, pinnate leaves with several pairs of leaflets, and small yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles. The tree typically reaches significant heights in tropical forest canopies, supporting a dense crown of foliage. Each leaf is composed of multiple small, oval leaflets that are arranged symmetrically along a central rachis. The flowers are relatively inconspicuous but produce legume-type pods that eventually contain hard, woody seeds. These seeds are often winged or shaped in a way that aids in their eventual dispersal within the ecosystem.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of West and Central Africa, specifically spanning from Guinea and Sierra Leone in the west to the Democratic Republic of the Congo in the east. It thrives primarily within the humid lowland forests that characterize the Guineo-Congolian regional center of endemism. Within these ecosystems, the species is often found in moist, well-drained soils where canopy cover is significant. Its distribution follows the continuous belt of equatorial vegetation across the African continent.
