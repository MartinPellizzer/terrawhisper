Pittosporum pulchrum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pittosporum pulchrum has an erect, woody shrub habit characterized by smooth, greyish bark and densely arranged, leathery leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The foliage is evergreen, featuring a dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside with entire margins. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often appearing in clusters that attract pollinators. Following flowering, the plant produces distinctive woody capsules that split open to reveal bright orange or red seeds. These seeds are often coated in a sticky substance, which is a defining morphological trait of the genus.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the coastal and subcoastal regions of southeastern Australia, specifically spanning from southern Queensland through New South Wales to eastern Victoria. It primarily inhabits diverse ecosystems ranging from littoral rainforests and coastal scrubs to moist sclerophyll forests. Within these habitats, the species often thrives in well-drained soils along rocky outcrops or near the edges of forested areas. Its distribution is largely concentrated along the eastern seaboard where maritime influences provide necessary humidity. This specific range limits its occurrence to the temperate and subtropical zones of the Australian continent.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Pittosporum pulchrum can cause skin irritation, redness, and allergic contact dermatitis upon direct contact with its sap or foliage. Some individuals may experience itching or a burning sensation where the plant touches the skin. Ingestion of the plant is generally considered toxic and may lead to gastrointestinal distress such as vomiting or abdominal pain. While systemic toxicity is not extensively documented in humans, caution is advised to prevent accidental consumption by children or pets. Always wash hands thoroughly after handling the plant to mitigate these risks.
