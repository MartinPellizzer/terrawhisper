Trichophorum schansiense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trichophorum schansiense has perennial, tufted growth habits characterized by slender, erect, and cylindrical culms that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are reduced to basal sheaths, while the photosynthetic tissue is primarily concentrated within the green, photosynthetic stems. Inflorescences are terminal and consist of small, compact spikelets arranged in a head-like cluster. Each spikelet contains several tiny flowers with inconspicuous tepals and single stamens. The fruit is a small, nut-like achene often surrounded by a persistent, hairy perianth.
