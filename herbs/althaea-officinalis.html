Marshmallow

Althaea officinalis

Marshmallow (Althaea officinalis) is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, known for its mucilage-rich roots and leaves, which have been used for centuries in traditional medicine to soothe inflammation and irritation in the respiratory and digestive systems.

Botanical Identification

Althaea officinalis, commonly known as marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. Belonging to the Malvaceae family, it is recognized by its swollen roots, broad heart-shaped leaves, and pinkish-white flowers. It is also known as althea or mallow in various regions.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Malvales
  • Family: Malvaceae
  • Genus: Althaea
  • Species: Althaea officinalis

Marshmallow Identification.

Active Compounds

Marshmallow contains key active compounds such as tannins, glycosides, and flavonoids. These compounds work synergistically to soothe inflammation and protect mucous membranes. Tannins provide astringent properties, while glycosides and flavonoids contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

  • Tannin
  • Glycoside
  • Flavonoid

Marshmallow Actions.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory conditions such as coughs and bronchitis due to its mucilage content.

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic due to its high mucilage content.

Poultice: Used for skin irritations and wounds due to its soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

Safety Profile

Marshmallow is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided by individuals with known allergies to plants in the Malvaceae family. Prolonged use may cause gastrointestinal upset, and it is contraindicated in cases of severe asthma due to potential bronchial irritation.
