What to know about Althaea officinalis before using it medicinally

Althaea officinalis, commonly known as marsh mallow, is a medicinal herb that has been used to treat various health conditions, including gastrointestinal issues, such as irritable bowel syndrome and inflammatory bowel disease, as well as skin conditions like eczema and wounds.

The main health benefits associated with Althaea officinalis include its anti-inflammatory, soothing, and protective properties, which help to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. The herb's therapeutic properties are attributed to its ability to form a protective barrier on the skin's surface, reducing moisture loss and promoting a healthy environment for wound healing. The bioactive compounds present in Althaea officinalis include mucilages, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

People often prepare Althaea officinalis as a decoction or infusion, which is used topically to treat skin conditions and internally to soothe gastrointestinal issues.

Gastritis is another condition that benefits from Althaea officinalis, as its anti-inflammatory properties, which are attributed to the presence of triterpenoid saponins, help to reduce inflammation and protect the gastric mucosa.
