Oxytropis falcata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Oxytropis falcata has pinnately compound leaves consisting of several pairs of small, oval leaflets and a dense covering of fine, silvery hairs. The plant features curved, sickle-shaped pods that are characteristic of its specific epithet. Small, pea-like flowers typically emerge in shades of purple or blue from the hairy stems. These inflorescences are often held in clusters at the ends of the branches. The overall structure of the plant is low-growing and adapted to rocky or alpine environments.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Oxytropis falcata can cause severe poisoning in livestock through the ingestion of toxic alkaloids that primarily target the central nervous system. Once consumed, these compounds often lead to neurological symptoms such as tremors, loss of coordination, and muscle weakness. In many cases, animals may exhibit staggering movements or an inability to stand up properly. High doses of these toxins can result in permanent brain damage or sudden death in sheep and cattle. Because the plant is often palatable, animals may eat large quantities before the harmful effects become obvious.
