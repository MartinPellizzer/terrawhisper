Oxytropis falcata

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxytropis falcata?

Oxytropis falcata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Fabales. It is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which is commonly known as the legume or pea family. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Oxytropis, and its specific identification is completed by its species name, falcata.

These inflorescences are often held in clusters at the ends of the branches. The overall structure of the plant is low-growing and adapted to rocky or alpine environments.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 2'-hydroxy-4'-methoxy chalcone, 2'-methoxy-4'-hydroxy chalcone, and 7-hydroxy-4'-methoxy flavanones. These specific chemical constituents belong to the flavonoid and chalcone families, which are often studied for their biological activities. Research suggests that these molecules may contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Scientists investigate these specific structural variations to understand how they interact with various cellular targets. By studying these compounds, researchers aim to develop new therapeutic applications for plant-based medicine.

As discussed in a study, eight compounds were isolated and elucidated from Oxytropis falcata for the first time. These medicinal compounds were obtained using column chromatography with silica gel, Sephadex LH-20, and ODS as the adsorbents. The isolated substances include 2', 4'-dihydroxy-4-methoxy chalcone (1), 2', 4'-dihydroxy chalcone (2), and 5,7-dihydroxy-4'-methoxy flavonol (3). Additionally, the plant contains 7-hydroxy-4'-methoxy flavanones (4), 3', 7-dihydroxy-2',4'-dimethoxy isoflavan (5), 2'-hydroxy-4'-methoxy chalcone (6), 2'-methoxy-4'-hydroxy chalcone (7), and 2',4'-dihydroxy dihydrochalcone (8). Structures for these 8 compounds were elucidated through the use of NMR and MS spectroscopic data.
