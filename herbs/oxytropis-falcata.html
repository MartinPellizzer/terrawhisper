Oxytropis falcata

What's the taxonomical classification of Oxytropis falcata?

Oxytropis falcata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant falls under the order Fabales. It is further categorized into the family Fabaceae, which is commonly known as the legume or pea family. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Oxytropis, and its specific identification is completed by its species name, falcata.

As discussed in a study, eight compounds were isolated and elucidated from Oxytropis falcata for the first time. These medicinal compounds include 2', 4'-dihydroxy-4-methoxy chalcone (1), 2', 4'-dihydroxy chalcone (2), and 5,7-dihydroxy-4'-methoxy flavonol (3). The investigation also identified 7-hydroxy-4'-methoxy flavanones (4) and 3', 7-dihydroxy-2',4'-dimethoxy isoflavan (5). Additional substances found in the plant were 2'-hydroxy-4'-methoxy chalcone (6), 2'-methoxy-4'-hydroxy chalcone (7), and 2',4'-dihydroxy dihydrochalcone (8). All 8 compounds were obtained using column chromatography with silica gel, Sephadex LH-20, and ODS as adsorbents.
