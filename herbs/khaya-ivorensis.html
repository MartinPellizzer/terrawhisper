Khaya ivorensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Khaya ivorensis?

Khaya ivorensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Sapindales and falls within the family Meliaceae. Ultimately, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by its placement in the genus Khaya, with its unique designation being the species ivorensis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Khaya ivorensis contains various bioactive secondary metabolites including alkaloids, terpenoids, saponins, and tannins that serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These chemical constituents are frequently utilized in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as malaria, fever, and digestive issues. The bark and leaves are particularly valued for their potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. Researchers study these compounds to understand their efficacy in treating infectious diseases and managing pain. Such phytochemical profiles make the species a significant subject in ethnopharmacological studies.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including skin infections, stomach pains, and respiratory issues. Local communities often utilize extracts from the plant to manage symptoms of digestive distress and localized inflammation. Some traditional practices also involve using the plant to alleviate coughs and other related breathing difficulties.
