Conium Maculatum: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Conium maculatum, commonly known as poison hemlock, is a toxic herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It contains potent bioactive constituents such as cicutoxin, coniine, and other alkaloids that can be lethal in large doses.

Despite its toxicity, it has been historically used in small amounts for its therapeutic actions, including muscle relaxation and antispasmodic effects.

However, due to its high risk of poisoning, it is not recommended for use in modern herbal medicine.
