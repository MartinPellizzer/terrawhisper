Entandrophragma angolense

What's the taxonomical classification of Entandrophragma angolense?

Entandrophragma angolense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. This plant is further categorized under the family Meliaceae, which is known for its diverse woody species. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Entandrophragma, with its specific identification being the species angolense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a diverse array of medicinal compounds including limonoids, entandrophins A-C, entanolide, methyl angolensate, and stigmas-4-en-3-one. These bioactive molecules contribute significantly to the therapeutic potential of the species. The presence of specific entandrophins suggests unique biochemical activities that warrant further scientific investigation. Additionally, the combination of methyl angolensate and entanolide provides a complex chemical profile. Stigmas-4-en-3-one further adds to the structural variety found within the plant's secondary metabolites.

As discussed in a study, the stem bark of Entandrophragma angolense contains four new compounds identified as 1-4 and eleven known structures labeled 5-15. These medicinal compounds were isolated and their structures were elucidated using spectroscopic and HRM S data. The absolute configuration of these substances was established through electronic circular dichroism. Among these, compound 5 displayed moderate cytotoxicity against four specific cell lines, including MDA-MB-231, OVCAR3, MDA-MB-435, and HT29. For these cell lines, compound 5 showed IC50 values ranging from 2.0-5.9 μM.
