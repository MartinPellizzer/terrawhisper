Entandrophragma angolense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Entandrophragma angolense has a large, woody trunk with smooth, greyish bark and a spreading crown of dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are typically elliptic or oblong in shape, featuring entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils of the branches to facilitate pollination. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, winged seeds designed for wind dispersal. These morphological traits allow the species to thrive in the diverse ecological niches of African tropical forests.
