What's the taxonomical classification of Entandrophragma angolense?

Entandrophragma angolense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. This plant is further categorized under the family Meliaceae, which is known for its diverse woody species. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Entandrophragma, with its specific identification being the species angolense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several unique medicinal compounds including entandrophins A-C, entanolide, methyl angolensate, stigmas-4-en-3-one, and various limonoids. These bioactive substances are primarily extracted from the bark and leaves of the species. The entandrophins represent a specific group of specialized metabolites that distinguish this plant from others in its genus. Additionally, the presence of limonoids suggests that the plant possesses significant pharmacological potential for various therapeutic uses. Research into these specific chemical structures continues to reveal how they interact with biological systems.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", the bark of Entandrophragma angolense contains three new protolimonoids called entandrophins A-C (1-3) along with two known compounds, methyl angolensate (4) and stigmas-4-en-3-one (5). These isolated compounds were evaluated for cytotoxicity against HepG2 cells and α-glucosidase inhibitory activity. While compounds 1-4 displayed very weak activity against HepG2 cells, compounds 2 and 3 exhibited significant α-glucosidase inhibition. Specifically, compound 2 showed an IC50 value of 57.5 ± 1.5 μM. Compound 3 demonstrated an IC50 value of 32.5 ± 0.9 μM.
