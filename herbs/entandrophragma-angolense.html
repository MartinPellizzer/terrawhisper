Entandrophragma angolense

What's the taxonomical classification of Entandrophragma angolense?

Entandrophragma angolense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Sapindales. This plant is further categorized under the family Meliaceae, which is known for its diverse woody species. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Entandrophragma, with its specific identification being the species angolense.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Entandrophragma angolense contains various bioactive phytochemicals including alkaloids, saponins, tannins, and terpenoids that contribute to its therapeutic properties. These chemical constituents are primarily extracted from the bark and leaves of the tree for traditional medicinal use. The presence of these compounds allows the plant to be utilized in treating ailments such as skin infections, gastrointestinal issues, and inflammatory conditions. Research into its chemical profile suggests that the specific combination of secondary metabolites provides significant antimicrobial and antioxidant activities. These medicinal qualities make the species a subject of interest for pharmacological studies focused on natural drug development.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, gastrointestinal disorders, inflammatory conditions, and various types of pain. The bark and leaves are often prepared as decoctions or infusions to address stomach ailments and digestive discomfort. It also demonstrates potential in managing wound healing and localized skin irritations through its medicinal properties. Some traditional practices utilize the plant to alleviate symptoms associated with fever and general malaise. These applications highlight the ethnobotanical importance of the species in regional healthcare.
