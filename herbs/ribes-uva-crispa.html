Gooseberry (Ribes uva crispa)

Gooseberry (Ribes uva crispa) is a member of the Grossulariaceae family, native to Eastern North America, Central North America, and Northern Mexico. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and poultices.

This herb is particularly valued for its bitter, anti-inflammatory, and astringent actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and native american herbal medicine.

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height

Leaves
Opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins, upper surface dark green, lower surface pale green, bearing prominent stomatal bands along the midvein.
Flowers
Flowers are actinomorphic, solitary, with five white petals and five yellow sepals, bearing five stamens and a single pistil with two stigmas.
Stems
Erect, branched, woody stems with opposite branching, smooth, glabrous surfaces, and prominent lenticels.
