Uvaria calamistrata

What's the taxonomical classification of Uvaria calamistrata?

Uvaria calamistrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the order Magnoliales, the plant is a member of the family Annonaceae. More specifically, it is categorized under the genus Uvaria, ultimately reaching its scientific designation as the species calamistrata.

What are the pharmacological activities of Uvaria calamistrata?

These bioactive compounds work through various mechanisms to modulate cellular responses and reduce oxidative stress. Such pharmacological profiles suggest that Uvaria calamistrata holds promise for future drug development in treating inflammation and malignancy.

As discussed in a study, nine previously undescribed polyoxygenated cyclohexenes called uvacalols L-T (1-9) and five known analogues (10-14) were obtained from the stems of Uvaria calamistrata. Among these, uvacalols L-N (1-3) are identified as chlorinated analogues. Certain compounds were evaluated for anti-inflammatory activities by measuring the inhibition of NO generation in RAW 264.7 cells. Compound 8 demonstrated the best inhibitory activity during these tests. This specific compound recorded an IC50 value of 4.49 ± 0.38 μM.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

As discussed in a study, five new polyoxygenated cyclohexenes were isolated from the roots of Uvaria calamistrata. These compounds are named uvacalol A (1), B (2), C (3), D (4) and E (5). The specific structures established include (2R,3S,4R,5S)-2-acetoxyl-5-ethoxyl-1-benzoyloxymethylcyclohex-1(6)-ene3,4-diol-3-benzoate for compound 1 and (2R,3S,4R,5S)-2-acetoxyl-5-ethoxyl-1-benzoyloxymethylcyclohex-1(6)-ene-3,4-diol-4-benzoate for compound 2. Compound 3 was identified as (2R,3S,4R,5S)-5-ethoxyl-1-benzoyloxymethylcyclohex-1(6)-ene-2,3,4-triol-3-benzoate, while compound 4 is (2R,3S,4R,5S)-3-methoxyl-1-benzoyloxymethylcyclohex-1(6)-ene-2,3,5-triol. Finally, compound 5 was established as (2R,3S,4R,5S)-2-acetoxyl-1-benzoyloxymethylcyclohex-1(6)-ene-3,4,5-triol-5-benzoate.
