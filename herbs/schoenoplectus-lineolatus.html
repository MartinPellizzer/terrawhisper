Schoenoplectus lineolatus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schoenoplectus lineolatus has slender, cylindrical, green culms that typically reach heights of 10 to 60 centimeters and lack distinct nodes. The leaves are reduced to basal sheaths, making the plant appear primarily as a collection of smooth, leafless stalks. Each stem features a finely textured surface and may exhibit slight longitudinal striations. The inflorescence consists of small, brownish spikelets arranged in compact clusters at the tips of the stems. These plants often form dense tufts or mats due to their creeping rhizomatous growth habit.
