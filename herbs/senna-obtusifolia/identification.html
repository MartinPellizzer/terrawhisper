Botanical Identification of Senna (Senna obtusifolia)

To identify Senna (Senna obtusifolia) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Senna obtusifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Senna, and species Senna obtusifolia. These classifications reflect its position within the plant kingdom and its relationship to other legumes.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of the plant is Senna obtusifolia, which was first described by the botanist Carl Peter Thunberg. This name refers to its leaf shape, with "obtusifolia" meaning having broadly rounded leaves. It is also known by several common names, including sickle senna and broadleaf senna, reflecting its distinctive foliage.

Morphological Characteristics

Senna obtusifolia is an annual herb with a taproot system. It has erect, branching stems that are hairy. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, with oblique, ovate leaflets. The inflorescence is a dense, terminal spike. Flowers are yellow, with five petals and ten stamens. The fruit is a flat, dehiscent pod containing several kidney-shaped seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Senna obtusifolia can be distinguished by its alternate, ovate leaves with rounded tips, yellow flowers in raceme clusters, and cylindrical, dark brown pods. It differs from similar species like Senna tora by its more rounded leaf shape and the absence of glandular hairs on the stem and leaves.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Senna obtusifolia is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. It thrives in warm climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soils. It typically grows at low to mid-altitudes, often in disturbed areas like roadsides and fields.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Senna obtusifolia blooms from late spring to early autumn, typically between May and September, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs from late summer to early fall, around August to October. For identification purposes, the most accurate harvest timing is during the flowering period, when the plant's characteristics are most distinct.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Senna obtusifolia is closely related to species within the genus Senna, which belongs to the legume family Fabaceae. It shares key family traits such as pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. Its closest relatives include Senna tora and Senna alata, which also exhibit similar morphological and chemical characteristics.
