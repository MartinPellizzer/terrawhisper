10 Possible Side Effects Of Senna Obtusifolia (Cassia)

Senna obtusifolia has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, including diarrhea, abdominal pain, and cramping.

These side effects can be caused by consuming excessive amounts or using the herb for extended periods.

In severe cases, dehydration and electrolyte imbalances can occur, leading to fatigue, headaches, and dizziness, significantly impacting one's quality of life.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Senna obtusifolia if used imporperly.

Senna obtusifolia causes diarrhea due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides, particularly sennoside.

These compounds stimulate the intestines, increasing bowel movements and fluid secretion, leading to frequent watery stools.

The irritation caused by these glycosides can also lead to abdominal cramps, bloating, and discomfort, making diarrhea a common side effect associated with Senna obtusifolia use.

This intense stimulation can cause nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramps in some individuals, particularly when used in large doses or over an extended period.
