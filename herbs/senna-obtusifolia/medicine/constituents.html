10 Medicinal Constituents Of Senna Obtusifolia (Cassia)

Senna obtusifolia has active constituents such as anthraquinone glycosides (aloin and aloe-emodin), flavonoids (quercetin and kaempferol), and saponins.

These compounds give the plant medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and laxative effects. The anthraquinones can help treat constipation, hemorrhoids, and inflammatory bowel diseases by stimulating bowel movements. Quercetin has been shown to alleviate allergic reactions and reduce oxidative stress, while kaempferol may have antiviral and antibacterial properties.

Overall, the constituents of Senna obtusifolia offer natural relief for various health issues, improving the quality of life for individuals suffering from digestive disorders and other conditions.

Senna obtusifolia quercetin is a flavonoid compound extracted from its leaves.
