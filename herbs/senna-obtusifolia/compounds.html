Senna (Senna obtusifolia) Compounds

The primary compounds in Senna obtusifolia include flavonoids, which have anti-inflammatory properties, and coumarin, known for its anticoagulant effects. Saponins and glycosides contribute to its laxative action, while phenolic acids and tannins provide antioxidant benefits. Terpenoids, essential oils, and alkaloids also play roles in its therapeutic effects. Anthocyanins add to its antioxidant profile and contribute to its color.

Senna (Senna obtusifolia) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are bioactive compounds found in Senna obtusifolia. They contribute to the plant's medicinal properties by exhibiting anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. These compounds support the plant's traditional use in treating digestive and inflammatory conditions. Flavonoids enhance the therapeutic potential of Senna obtusifolia in herbal medicine.

2. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Senna obtusifolia. It contributes to the plant's mild laxative effects by stimulating bowel movements. This compound is also known for its antispasmodic properties, making it useful in treating digestive discomfort.

3. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Senna obtusifolia, known for its mild laxative effects. It works by increasing intestinal motility and reducing water absorption, aiding in bowel movements. Saponin also contributes to the plant's overall therapeutic value in herbal medicine.

4. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound found in Senna obtusifolia. It contributes to the plant's laxative effects by stimulating bowel movements. This compound is commonly used in herbal medicine for treating constipation and digestive issues. Its presence highlights the plant's traditional and modern therapeutic applications.

5. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound found in Senna obtusifolia. It contributes to the plant's laxative properties by stimulating bowel movements. This compound is also known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Phenolic acid plays a significant role in the therapeutic uses of Senna obtusifolia in herbal medicine.

6. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Senna obtusifolia, known for its astringent properties. It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. Tannins help reduce inflammation and may support wound healing. Their presence enhances the overall therapeutic profile of Senna obtusifolia in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of compounds found in Senna obtusifolia, a medicinal plant used in herbal medicine. These compounds contribute to the plant's therapeutic properties, including laxative effects, by interacting with the digestive system. They are also responsible for some of the plant's anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a volatile compound found in Senna obtusifolia. It contains aromatic compounds like limonene and pinene, which contribute to its medicinal properties. This oil is used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. It is often extracted through steam distillation from the plant's leaves and flowers.

9. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in Senna obtusifolia are naturally occurring nitrogen-containing compounds with pharmacological activity. These alkaloids contribute to the plant's laxative effects by stimulating bowel movements. They are responsible for some of the therapeutic properties attributed to Senna in traditional herbal medicine.

10. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a flavonoid found in Senna obtusifolia. It contributes to the plant's vibrant color and has antioxidant properties. This compound may support digestive health and reduce inflammation, making it a valuable component in traditional herbal medicine.
