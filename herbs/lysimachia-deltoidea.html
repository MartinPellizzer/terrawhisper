Lysimachia deltoidea

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia deltoidea?

Lysimachia deltoidea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further categorized into the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, situated within the genus Lysimachia, and is specifically identified by its species name, deltoidea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lysimachia deltoidea has lanceolate to deltoid leaves that are arranged oppositely along a slender, upright stem.

The foliage typically exhibits a smooth margin and a bright green coloration that can vary in intensity depending on light exposure. Its flowers are small and yellow, emerging from the leaf axils in a terminal or axillary fashion. Each bloom consists of five distinct petals that form a star-like shape when fully open.

The plant's overall structure is herbaceous and relatively delicate, often growing in moist or shaded environments.
