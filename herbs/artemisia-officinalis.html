Artemisia Officinalis Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Artemisia officinalis, commonly known as mugwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and Asia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers.

It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, including aiding digestion, reducing inflammation, and supporting respiratory health.

The herb contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, sesquiterpene lactones, and volatile oils, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Mugwort can be prepared as an infusion, tincture, or essential oil, and is often used in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
