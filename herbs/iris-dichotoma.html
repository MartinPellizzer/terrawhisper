Iris dichotoma

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Iris dichotoma has a rhizomatous root system, sword-shaped leaves with parallel venation, and flowers characterized by a distinct division into falls and standards. The leaves are typically narrow and erect, forming a dense basal clump that supports the flowering stalks. Its reproductive structures feature a complex perianth tube where the petals are often marked with intricate color patterns to attract pollinators. The stem is sturdy enough to elevate the blooms above the foliage during the peak growing season. Each flower displays a specialized arrangement of the style arms which helps facilitate the pollination process.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southwestern China, specifically spanning provinces such as Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou. It thrives primarily in high-altitude habitats where the terrain is rugged and rocky. These populations are often found nestled within forest margins or open alpine meadows. The species favors environments that provide well-drained soil and consistent moisture from seasonal rains. Consequently, its distribution is closely tied to the specific climatic conditions of these subtropical highland zones.
