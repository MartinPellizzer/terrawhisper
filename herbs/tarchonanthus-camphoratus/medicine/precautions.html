10 Precautions To Take When Using Tarchonanthus Camphoratus (Wild Sage)

1. Consult a doctor before usage

When using Tarchonanthus camphoratus medicinally, it's important to take with medical supervision.

This is crucial due to the herb's potential interactions with blood thinners and diabetes medications, which can lead to adverse effects when combined incorrectly. Additionally, wild sage may lower blood pressure, which can exacerbate pre-existing conditions if not monitored by a healthcare professional.

With proper guidance, however, wild sage can be a valuable addition to a treatment plan under medical supervision.

2. Consult a doctor before usage

When using Tarchonanthus camphoratus medicinally, it's important to avoid high doses orally because the herb can cause gastrointestinal upset, nausea, and vomiting at excessive levels.

High doses may also lead to an increase in heart rate and blood pressure, which can be problematic for individuals with pre-existing cardiovascular conditions.

Additionally, oral administration of large amounts of Tarchonanthus camphoratus can interact with other medications, such as sedatives and anticoagulants, potentially leading to adverse effects.
