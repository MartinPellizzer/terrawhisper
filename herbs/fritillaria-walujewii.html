Fritillaria walujewii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Friillaria walujewii has bulbous rhizomes that produce upright stems topped with nodding, bell-shaped flowers featuring dark purple to brownish-red markings on a lighter ground color. The leaves are lanceolate in shape and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. Each flower typically displays distinct tessellation or checkered patterns on its tepals. The plant often grows in clumps, emerging from its underground storage organ during its specific flowering season. These morphological traits help distinguish the species within its specific high-altitude habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Friillaria walujewii can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of toxic alkaloids. These chemical compounds often lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal cramping in humans and animals. Contact with the plant's tissues may also trigger contact dermatitis or localized rashes on sensitive skin. Because many species in the Fritillaria genus contain similar alkaloids, caution is necessary to avoid accidental poisoning. It is important to handle this plant with gloves to mitigate the risk of dermal absorption or irritation.
