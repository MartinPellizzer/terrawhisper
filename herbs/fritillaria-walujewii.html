What's the taxonomical classification of Fritillaria walujewii?

Fritillaria walujewii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its classification through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Liliales. As a member of the family Liliaceae, it is further classified into the genus Fritillaria, ultimately reaching its specific designation at the species level as walujewii.

According to a study published by "Advanced biomedical research", the plant Friillaria walujeewii contains the medicinal compound Peiminine. This compound is used in herbal remedies for palliative care in patients with gastric cancer. The research utilized an MTT assay to determine the concentration of Peiminine required to prohibit 50% cell growth, known as the IC50. Peiminine was tested on AGS human gastric adenocarcinoma cells to observe its effects on cell viability and apoptosis. The study found that Peiminine enhances the cytotoxicity of Doxorubicin and leads to lower expression of hsa-miR-106a-5p and hsa-miR-181a-5p.
