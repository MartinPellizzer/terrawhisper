Flemingia prostrata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Flemingia prostrata has a prostrate, creeping growth habit characterized by woody stems that spread horizontally along the ground and roots at the nodes. The leaves are trifoliate with elliptical to obovate leaflets that possess entire margins and acute tips. Small, yellow, pea-like flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves in short racemes. The plant produces legume-type pods that are typically oblong and contain several small seeds. This low-growing shrubby structure allows it to form dense mats over the soil surface.

    Flemingia prostrata is prepared primarily as a decoction, a topical paste, or an infused tea for medicinal use.
