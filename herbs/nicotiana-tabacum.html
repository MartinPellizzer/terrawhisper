Nicotiana Tabacum Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Nicotiana tabacum, commonly known as tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the Americas and widely cultivated for its leaves.

While it is best known for its use in cigarettes and other tobacco products, it also contains bioactive constituents such as alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

However, it is important to note that tobacco use is associated with significant health risks, including respiratory and cardiovascular diseases.

Herbal preparations such as infusions or tinctures can be made from its leaves, though they should be used with caution due to potential toxicity.
