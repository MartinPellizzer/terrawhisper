Indigofera kirilowii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Fabales and belongs to the family Fabaceae. Finally, its specific identification is defined by the genus Indigofera and the species kirilowii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the unique bioactive compounds kirilowin A and kirilowin B, which belong to the class of indole alkaloids. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the plant's distinct pharmacological properties. Researchers have identified these molecules as significant contributors to the medicinal potential of the Indigofera genus. Studies suggest that these compounds may possess various biological activities useful in drug development. Further investigation into their chemical structures aims to uncover deeper therapeutic applications.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", two unique glucose esters of 3-nitropropanoic acid with acryloyl moieties were identified in the roots of Indigofera kirilowii. These specific compounds, named kirilowin A and kirilowin B, were isolated from the plant. Detailed spectroscopic analysis was used to elucidate the structures of these 2 acrylate compounds. These findings represent new additions to the toxic glucose conjugates of 3-nitropropanoic acid found in angiosperms.

It also serves as a remedy to reduce various bodily swellings. Additionally, the plant helps in cooling the body to combat the effects of toxic heat. These applications demonstrate the plant's importance in traditional healing practices.

According to a study published by "Yao xue xue bao = Acta pharmaceutica Sinica", the plant Indigofera kirilowii Maxim. et Palibin is used as the Chinese drug Shan-Dou-Gen. This drug has been used since the ancient time for removing toxic heat, promoting the subsidence of swelling and soothing the sore throat. The study identifies Indigofera kirilowii Maxim. et Palibin as 1 of the 9 species used to produce this drug. Because the drug derived from different species has different actions and dosages, it is necessary to use the species correctly.
