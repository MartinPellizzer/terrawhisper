Albizia corniculata

What's the taxonomical classification of Albizia corniculata?

Albizia corniculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, it falls under the order Fabales and is a member of the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Albizia, and its specific species name, corniculata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Albizia corniculata has a small to medium-sized tree habit characterized by bipinnate leaves, fragrant creamy-white globose flower heads, and small oblong to ellipsoidal brown pods. The leaves are composed of numerous small, closely spaced leaflets that give the foliage a fine, feathery texture. Its inflorescences consist of dense clusters of flowers with long, protruding stamens that provide a soft appearance. The fruit develops into flat, dry pods that contain several small seeds. This species typically features a spreading crown and smooth to slightly fissured bark as it matures.
