Saussurea globosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea globosa has a rosette of thick, leathery leaves that are spatulate in shape and covered in dense, white woolly hairs. These leaves are arranged in a tight circular pattern around a central stem to protect the plant from extreme alpine temperatures. The inflorescence consists of a large, globular, yellowish-white flower head that sits atop a tall, sturdy scape. Each individual floret is surrounded by numerous translucent, papery bracts that create a distinctive cup-like structure. The entire plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and protective pubescence which helps it survive high-altitude environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of the Tibetan Plateau, specifically found in parts of China, Nepal, and Bhutan. It thrives in extremely harsh environments such as rocky crevices and scree slopes at elevations often exceeding 4,000 meters. The species is primarily distributed across the Himalayan mountain range where it can withstand intense solar radiation and freezing temperatures. Its specific habitat requirements limit its presence to localized patches within these mountainous ecosystems.
