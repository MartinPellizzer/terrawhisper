What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea globosa?

Saussurea globosa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, a large and diverse group of flowering plants. More specifically, it is categorized under the genus Saussurea, and its specific identity is defined by its species name, globosa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea globosa has a rosette of thick, leathery leaves that are spatulate in shape and covered in dense, white woolly hairs. The inflorescence consists of a large, globular, yellowish-white flower head that sits atop a tall, sturdy scape. Each individual floret is surrounded by numerous translucent, papery bracts that create a distinctive cup-like structure.
