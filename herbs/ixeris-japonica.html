Ixeris japonica

What's the taxonomical classification of Ixeris japonica?

Ixeris japonica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. As a member of the order Asterales, it falls into the family Asteraceae, a group known for its diverse floral structures. Specifically, this plant is identified by the genus Ixeris and is distinguished by its specific epithet, japonica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ixeris japonica has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish and support small, opposite leaves with lanceolate to linear shapes. The foliage is typically thin and lacks a prominent midrib, contributing to a delicate appearance. Tiny, white to pale pink flowers emerge from the leaf axils in small, inconspicuous clusters. These blooms are characterized by their minute size and lack of large, showy petals. The root system consists of thin rhizomes that allow the plant to spread slowly through its wetland habitat.
