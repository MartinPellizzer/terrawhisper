Megaphrynium macrostachyum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Megaphrynium macrostachyum has large, oblong-lanceolate leaves with prominent longitudinal veins and a distinctively thick, elongated rachis. The foliage is characterized by its robust texture and deep green pigmentation, which supports its growth in shaded forest environments. Its stems are sturdy and provide the necessary structural support for the heavy leaf canopy. Small, inconspicuous flowers are typically produced, often emerging from the leaf axils. The overall architecture of the plant is defined by these massive leaf structures and a sprawling growth habit.
