Suaeda glauca

What's the taxonomical classification of Suaeda glauca?

Suaeda glauca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Caryophyllales. The plant is further categorized into the family Amaranthaceae, specifically within the genus Suaeda, and is identified by the species name glauca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Suaeda glauca has succulent, cylindrical leaves that are typically blue-green or glaucous in color and measure between 1 and 3 centimeters in length. These leaves are arranged oppositely or in small clusters along fleshy, branched stems. The plant often exhibits a spreading or decumbent growth habit, forming low-lying mats or small shrubs. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, leading to the development of small, fleshy fruits. The overall structure is adapted to saline environments through its thick, water-storing tissue.

How is this plant cultivated?
