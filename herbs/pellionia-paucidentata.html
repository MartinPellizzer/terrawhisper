What's the taxonomical classification of Pellionia paucidentata?

Pellioniia paucidentaata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Rosales. This plant is a member of the family Urticaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Pellioniia, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name paucidentata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pellioniia paucidentata has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with irregularly serrated margins and a distinctively textured surface.

The plant features a creeping or scrambling growth habit with slender, somewhat succulent stems. The leaf venation is prominent, creating a slightly rugose appearance on the upper lamina. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils to facilitate reproduction.

This species is characterized by its relatively small leaf size compared to other members of its genus.
