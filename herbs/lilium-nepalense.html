Lilium nepalense

What's the taxonomical classification of Lilium nepalense?

Lilium nepalense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Liliales and is a member of the family Liliaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Lilium, and its specific species designation, nepalense.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lilium nepalense has erect, lanceolate leaves that are arranged in dense whorls or spirals along a sturdy, unbranched stem reaching heights of up to one meter. The flowers are large, nodding, and characterized by recurved tepals that often display a bright orange or reddish-orange hue with prominent dark spots. Each blossom typically features long, protruding stamens with large anthers that contrast sharply against the petal coloration. The plant grows from a bulbous underground structure that provides the necessary nutrients for its seasonal flowering cycle. These morphological traits distinguish it from other high-altitude lily species found in the Himalayan region.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

The species prefers shaded slopes and mountain meadows where the soil remains consistently damp. Its distribution follows the specific climatic patterns of the Eastern Himalayas, which provide the necessary cool temperatures and seasonal rainfall.     What side effects this plant can have?

    This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential toxicity if ingested by pets or humans. Contact with the foliage or petals may lead to redness, itching, or a rash on sensitive skin. If swallowed, the plant might induce gastrointestinal distress such as vomiting or stomach pain. Pet owners should be particularly cautious because lilies are notoriously toxic to cats and can lead to kidney failure. Always handle this species with care and keep it away from vulnerable individuals and animals.
