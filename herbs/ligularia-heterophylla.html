Ligularia heterophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Ligularia heterophylla?

Ligularia heterophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, placing it among the composite flowers, and is specifically categorized under the genus Ligularia. Its complete scientific designation is finalized at the species level as Ligularia heterophylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ligularia heterophylla has large, ovate to elliptical leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, featuring serrated margins and a distinctively heterophyllous arrangement where leaf shape varies along the stem. The foliage is typically a deep green with prominent venation that creates a textured surface. These leaves are borne on sturdy, upright stems that can grow quite tall in their natural habitat. During the flowering season, the plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like composite heads clustered at the tips of the stems. The overall structure is robust and herbaceous, allowing the plant to dominate moist, shaded environments.
