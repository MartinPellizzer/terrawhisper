What's the taxonomical classification of Allium rude?

Allium rude belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its placement in the order Asparagales, the plant is a member of the family Amaryllidaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Allium and the species rude.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Allium rude has a bulbous root system with narrow, linear leaves that typically measure between 2 and 6 millimeters in width. The plant produces a single, upright scape that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its inflorescence is a compact umbel containing numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. Each individual flower features six tepals and prominent stamens that protrude slightly from the perianth. The overall structure is slender and delicate, characteristic of many wild onion species.

How is this plant cultivated?
