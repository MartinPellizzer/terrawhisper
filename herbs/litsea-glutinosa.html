Litsea glutinosa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Litsea glutinosa has an evergreen tree habit characterized by a spreading crown, simple alternate leaves with entire margins, and small yellowish-green flowers arranged in axillary panicles. The leaves are typically elliptic to oblong in shape and feature a distinctively glossy, coriaceous texture on the upper surface. Young twigs and petioles are often covered in a fine, sticky pubescence that gives the plant its characteristic name. The inflorescences consist of numerous small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into small, globose drupes. These fruits transition from green to a dark color upon maturity and are a key identifying feature of the species.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, spanning from India and Sri Lanka through Myanmar, Thailand, and Malaysia to Indonesia and the Philippines. It thrives primarily in moist, lowland evergreen forests and hilly terrain throughout these humid zones. Within these habitats, the species is often found in well-drained soils along riverbanks or in dense jungle understories. Its presence is most concentrated in the Indo-Malayan botanical realm where seasonal rainfall is consistent. This widespread distribution reflects its adaptation to warm, moisture-rich ecosystems across the continent.

What are the herbal preparations of Litsea glutinosa?

Litsea glutinosa is prepared as a decoction, infusion, or topical paste using the dried leaves, bark, or roots of the plant. The leaves and bark are often boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for digestive or respiratory issues. For external applications, the plant material can be crushed into a fine paste to treat skin irritations or wounds. Some traditional practices also involve using the essential oils extracted from the plant for aromatic or topical purposes. These various methods allow the therapeutic compounds within the plant to be delivered effectively to the body.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Litsea glutinosa can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested improperly. While it is traditionally used in various medicinal practices, the essential oils derived from the plant may trigger sensitivity in individuals with delicate skin. Some users might experience redness, itching, or swelling upon topical application of the plant extracts. If consumed in large quantities, it could potentially lead to nausea or stomach discomfort. Always consult a medical professional before using this botanical for therapeutic purposes to avoid adverse reactions.
