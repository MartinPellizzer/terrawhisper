Spinifex littoreus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spinifex littoreus has a perennial, tufted growth habit characterized by stiff, wiry, and sharply pointed leaves that emerge from a woody base. The leaf blades are narrow and linear, often featuring serrated or spiny margins that provide a defensive texture. These plants typically produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are tucked within the dense, prickly foliage. The reproductive structures eventually develop into hard, woody fruits or achenes that assist in dispersal across coastal sandy environments. This rugged morphology allows the species to thrive in harsh, wind-swept maritime habitats.

Its range covers various habitats along the coastline, ranging from the Kimberley region in the west to parts of Queensland in the east.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Spinifex littoreus can cause physical skin irritation and minor abrasions due to its sharp, rigid, and needle-like foliage. The tough leaves often act like small blades that can scratch or puncture the skin upon contact. People handling the plant without protection may experience redness or localized discomfort from these mechanical injuries. Because of its structural toughness, the plant can also pose a puncture risk to bare feet or delicate areas. Protective clothing is generally recommended to avoid these direct physical side effects.
