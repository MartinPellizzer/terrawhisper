What's the taxonomical classification of Spinifex littoreus?

Spinifex littoreus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this biological hierarchy, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Poales and the family Poaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Spinifex, and its unique species designation, littoreus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Spinifex littoreus has a perennial, tufted growth habit characterized by stiff, wiry, and sharply pointed leaves that emerge from a woody base.

The leaf blades are narrow and linear, often featuring serrated or spiny margins that provide a defensive texture. These plants typically produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are tucked within the dense, prickly foliage. The reproductive structures eventually develop into hard, woody fruits or achenes that assist in dispersal across coastal sandy environments.

This rugged morphology allows the species to thrive in harsh, wind-swept maritime habitats.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Spinifex littoreus can cause physical skin irritation and minor abrasions due to its sharp, rigid, and needle-like foliage. The tough leaves often act like small blades that can scratch or puncture the skin upon contact. People handling the plant without protection may experience redness or localized discomfort from these mechanical injuries. Because of its structural toughness, the plant can also pose a puncture risk to bare feet or delicate areas. Protective clothing is generally recommended to avoid these direct physical side effects.
