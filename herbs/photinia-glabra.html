What's the taxonomical classification of Photinia glabra?

Photinia glabra belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it falls under the order Rosales and the family Rosaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Photinia, and its specific species name, glabra.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of cyanogenic glycosides in its tissues. Contact with the leaves or stems may result in contact dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, or a localized rash on sensitive skin. If any part of the shrub is eaten, individuals might experience symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, or dizziness. These chemical compounds can release hydrogen cyanide when processed by the body, which is a serious health concern. Because of these risks, it is important to handle the plant with gloves and keep it away from pets and small children.
