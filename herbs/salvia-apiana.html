Salvia Apiana Uses, Benefits, And Remedies

Salvia apiana, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is widely recognized for its aromatic leaves and traditional use in spiritual and medicinal practices.

The herb is known to offer various health benefits, including antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and immune-boosting properties.

Salvia apiana contains bioactive constituents such as flavonoids, phenolic compounds, and volatile oils, which contribute to its therapeutic actions.

This herb can be prepared in various forms, such as infusions, tinctures, and smudges, to harness its beneficial properties.
