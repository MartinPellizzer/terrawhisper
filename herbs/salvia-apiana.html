White Sage (Salvia apiana)

White Sage (Salvia apiana) is a member of the Lamiaceae family, native to California, Usa, Baja California, Mexico, and Sonoran Desert Region. Traditionally, its leaves, flowers, and resin have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, sedative, and carminative actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.
