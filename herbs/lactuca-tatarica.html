What's the taxonomical classification of Lactuca tatarica?

Lactuca tatarica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Finally, it is defined by the genus Lactuca and the specific species epithet tatarica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lactuca tatarica has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights between 30 and 100 centimeters and supports a basal rosette of leaves. The leaves are generally lanceolate or oblong in shape with slightly wavy or entire margins. These leaves possess a smooth texture and are arranged alternately along the stem once the plant matures. The inflorescence consists of numerous small, yellow flower heads arranged in a loose corymbose cyme. Each flower head contains multiple tubular florets that are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. The plant often develops a branched structure toward the top to support its flowering heads.
