Rosa spinosissima

What's the taxonomical classification of Rosa spinosissima?

Rosa spinosissima belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Rosales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Rosaceae, which falls under the genus Rosa. Finally, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species designation, spinosissima.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa spinosissima has highly branched, arching stems that are densely covered in large, sharp, recurved prickles. These woody stems support pinnately compound leaves that typically consist of five to seven small, serrated leaflets. The flowers are usually solitary and feature five delicate petals that vary in color from pale pink to deep rose. Small, fleshy red hips develop as fruit following the blooming period. The overall structure of the plant is characterized by a sprawling, shrubby habit due to the heavy armament of its stems.

How is this plant cultivated?
