Silver Lime (Tilia argentea)

Silver Lime (Tilia argentea) is a member of the Tiliaceae family, native to Central Asia, Eastern Europe, and Southern Russia. Traditionally, its leaves, bark, and flowers have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its anti-inflammatory, expectorant, and tonic actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, mediterranean herbal traditions, and traditional chinese medicine.

Flowers
Small white flowers arranged in terminal spikes, actinomorphic, with five ovate petals and five elongated sepals, lacking distinct markings or nectary structures.
Stems
Elongated, erect growth habit with opposite branching, smooth, glabrous surface, and persistent leaf scars along the axis.
