Silver Lime

Tilia argentea

Silver Lime (Tilia argentea) is a small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, known for its ornamental value and traditional use in herbal medicine for its calming and soothing properties, particularly in treating anxiety and sleep disorders.

Botanical Identification

Silver Lime, or Tilia argentea, is a species of tree in the Tiliaceae family. It is also known as Argentea Lime or Silver-leaf Lime in some regions. A deciduous perennial, it is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, and its small, fragrant flowers appear in clusters. It is sometimes referred to as Tilia argentea var. in herbal texts.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for respiratory ailments such as coughs and sore throats.

Extract: Used for digestive issues and to reduce inflammation.

Decoction: Used for gastrointestinal problems and to relieve pain.

Safety Profile

Silver Lime is generally safe when used appropriately, but it may cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some individuals. It is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation due to limited safety data. Always consult a healthcare professional before use, especially for prolonged periods or in high doses.
