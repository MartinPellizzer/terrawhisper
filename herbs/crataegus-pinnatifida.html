Chinese hawthorn

Crataegus pinnatifida

Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida) is a member of the Rosaceae family, traditionally used in Chinese medicine. The plant parts, including fruits, flowers, and leaves, have been historically employed for cardiovascular support, anxiety relief, and improving sleep quality. It has a long history of regional use in traditional herbal practices across East Asia.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus pinnatifida, belongs to the Rosaceae family. It is also called Chinese thornapple or hawthorn in some regions. A deciduous shrub, it is native to East Asia, particularly China. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces red berries in autumn. Synonyms include Crataegus rhipidophylla and Crataegus sinensis.

Active Compounds

Chinese Hawthorn contains key bioactive compounds such as flavonoids, glycosides, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to support cardiovascular health and reduce inflammation. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in traditional herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for cardiovascular issues and digestive disorders.

Infusion: Used for mild cardiovascular support and stress relief.

Extract: Used for heart health and circulatory disorders.

Safety Profile

Chinese Hawthorn is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided by individuals with known allergies to plants in the Rosaceae family. Prolonged use or high doses may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, and it is contraindicated in patients with severe hypotension or bradycardia.
