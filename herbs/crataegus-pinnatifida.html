Chinese hawthorn

Crataegus pinnatifida

Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida) is a deciduous shrub native to China, known for its small red berries and traditional use in herbal medicine to improve cardiovascular health and alleviate symptoms of heart-related conditions such as angina and arrhythmia.

Taxonomy

Chinese Hawthorn, or Crataegus pinnatifida, is a species belonging to the genus Crataegus in the family Rosaceae, classified under the order Rosales, class Eudicots, division Magnoliophyta, and kingdom Plantae.

Morphology

Chinese Hawthorn, or Crataegus pinnatifida, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2-5 meters, characterized by thorny branches, simple alternate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small white flowers that develop into red to orange berries.

Distribution

Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida) is native to China, primarily found in the southern and eastern regions, including provinces such as Hubei, Sichuan, Jiangxi, and Zhejiang, with a range extending into parts of Southeast Asia.

Traditional Uses

Chinese Hawthorn, or Crataegus pinnatifida, has been traditionally used in Chinese medicine to promote cardiovascular health, aid digestion, and alleviate symptoms of indigestion and bloating.

Medicinal Systems

In traditional Chinese medicine, Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida) has been widely used for its cardiovascular and digestive benefits, including the treatment of hypertension, indigestion, and liver disorders.

Phytochemistry

Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida) contains a variety of bioactive compounds including flavonoids, triterpenoids, phenolic acids, and iridoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties and therapeutic effects.

Pharmacology

Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida) exhibits pharmacological properties characterized by cardiovascular support, antioxidant activity, anti-inflammatory effects, and potential hepatoprotective actions, primarily through its traditional use in promoting circulation, reducing oxidative stress, and modulating immune responses.

Botanical Identification

Chinese Hawthorn, scientifically known as Crataegus pinnatifida, belongs to the Rosaceae family. It is also called Chinese thornapple or hawthorn in some regions. A deciduous shrub, it is native to East Asia, particularly China. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces red berries in autumn. Synonyms include Crataegus rhipidophylla and Crataegus sinensis.

Safety Profile

Chinese Hawthorn is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it should be avoided by individuals with known allergies to plants in the Rosaceae family. Prolonged use or high doses may cause gastrointestinal discomfort, and it is contraindicated in patients with severe hypotension or bradycardia.
