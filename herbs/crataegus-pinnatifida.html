Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida)

Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida) is a member of the Rosaceae family, native to China, East Asia, and Korean Peninsula. Traditionally, its fruits, leaves, and bark have been used for infusions, decoctions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and sedative actions, and has a long history of use in european herbal medicine, traditional chinese medicine, and japanese kampo medicine.

Leaves
Opposite, ovate, dark green upper surface with lighter green lower surface, bearing prominent stomatal bands.
Flowers
Inflorescences are racemes of small actinomorphic flowers with five white to pinkish sepals and five yellow stamens, each with two anthers, and a superior ovary with two styles.
Stems
Elongated, woody stems with opposite branching, smooth to slightly pubescent surfaces, and persistent stipular scars.
