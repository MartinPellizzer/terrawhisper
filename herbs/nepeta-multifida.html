Nepeta multifida

What's the taxonomical classification of Nepeta multifida?

Nepeta multifida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical classification continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which is further refined into the genus Nepeta. Ultimately, the specific taxonomic designation for this plant is the species multifida.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Nepeta multifida contains nepetalactone, nepetifolin, and various volatile essential oils that provide its primary chemical profile. These terpenoids are responsible for the plant's characteristic aromatic scent and its potential biological activity. The presence of flavones and phenolic compounds further contributes to its antioxidant properties. Research into these specific constituents suggests they may play a role in calming nervous system responses. These chemical compounds are characteristic of the Lamiaceae family and are often studied for their interaction with mammalian olfactory systems.

What are the therapeutic actions of Nepeta multifida?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including nervous system disorders, insomnia, and digestive issues. Its chemical properties are often utilized to soothe anxiety and promote relaxation in the central nervous system. Some traditional applications involve using its extracts to calm restlessness or mild agitation. Additionally, the plant may help alleviate stomach discomfort or gas through its calming effects on the body. These medicinal properties stem from the specific essential oils found within its leaves.
