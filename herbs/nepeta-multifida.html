Nepeta multifida

What's the taxonomical classification of Nepeta multifida?

Nepeta multifida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized within the phylum Streptophyta. Its hierarchical classification continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Lamiales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, which is further refined into the genus Nepeta. Ultimately, the specific taxonomic designation for this plant is the species multifida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nepeta multifida has deeply lobed, trifid leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and possess serrated margins. The foliage often exhibits a grayish-green hue due to a fine layer of pubescence covering the surface. Its stems are square in cross-section, which is a characteristic trait of the Lamiaceae family. Small, tubular flowers emerge from whorled clusters called verticillasters, usually appearing in shades of pale blue or lavender. These blossoms are supported by branching inflorescences that extend above the dense canopy of leaves.
