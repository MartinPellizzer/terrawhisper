1. Causes allergic reactions

Krameria triandra causes allergic reactions due to its high content of allergenic compounds such as Kramerin and Kramerol.

These substances can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, redness, and swelling at the site of contact or throughout the body.

The exact mechanism behind this reaction is still unclear, but it is believed that the body's hypersensitivity to these compounds is responsible for the allergic response.

2. Triggers anaphylaxis

Krameria triandra triggers anaphylaxis due to its allergenic properties.

The plant contains allergenic proteins that can cause an immune response, leading to the release of histamine and other chemicals in the body. This reaction can result in symptoms such as rapid heartbeat, difficulty breathing, and swelling of the face, throat, and hands.

In severe cases, anaphylaxis can be life-threatening if left untreated.

3. Increases blood pressure

Krameria triandra increases blood pressure due to its high concentration of glycosides and alkaloids.

These compounds cause blood vessels to constrict, leading to increased resistance and subsequently, elevated blood pressure.

Additionally, the plant's stimulation of the adrenals and thyroid gland may further contribute to this effect by releasing stress hormones that also influence blood pressure regulation.

4. Lowers blood sugar

Krameria triandra lowers blood sugar levels due to its ability to stimulate insulin secretion and improve glucose uptake in the body.

This effect is attributed to the presence of saponins, a type of glycoside found in the plant, which have been shown to enhance pancreatic function and increase glucose absorption in the intestines.

As a result, individuals using Krameria triandra may experience decreased blood sugar levels, potentially impacting their glycemic control.

5. Produces gastrointestinal upset

Krameria triandra produces gastrointestinal upset when consumed.

This is attributed to its saponin content, which can irritate the mucous membranes of the digestive tract, leading to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and stomach cramps. The plant's flavonoids may also contribute to this adverse effect by altering the gut microbiome and disrupting normal digestion processes.

As a result, some individuals may experience discomfort or pain in the abdomen when using Krameria triandra.

6. Induces kidney damage

Krameria triandra induces kidney damage due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides, which can cause irritation and inflammation in the kidneys.

These compounds are not fully absorbed by the body and are excreted in the urine, where they can concentrate and increase the risk of nephrotoxicity.

This may lead to damage to kidney tissues and potentially even permanent impairment of renal function.

7. Affects liver function

Krameria triandra affects liver function due to its ability to alter the body's natural detoxification processes.

The saponins and other compounds present in Krameria triandra may cause an increase in bilirubin levels in the blood, leading to jaundice or liver damage.

Additionally, the plant's alkaloids may also interfere with the liver's ability to metabolize certain substances, potentially causing a build-up of toxins in the body.

8. Interferes with digestion

Krameria triandra interferes with digestion, potentially causing gastrointestinal disturbances.

The saponin content in the plant is responsible for this effect, which can disrupt the normal functioning of the digestive system and lead to issues such as bloating, gas, and abdominal discomfort.

This interference may be due to the way saponins bind to cholesterol in food, altering its absorption rate and potentially causing an imbalance in gut bacteria.

9. Worsens respiratory issues

Krameria triandra worsens respiratory issues by irritating the mucous membranes and airways, leading to increased mucus production and constriction of the bronchial tubes.

This can cause symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath in individuals with pre-existing respiratory conditions like asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

The compound cinnamic acid present in Krameria triandra is thought to be responsible for this adverse effect, exacerbating respiratory issues and potentially triggering attacks.

10. Triggers headaches

Krameria triandra triggers headaches due to its high concentration of anthraquinone glycosides.

These compounds can stimulate the digestive system and increase blood flow, leading to increased pressure and irritation in the head and brain.

Additionally, the saponins present in Krameria triandra may cause vasodilation, further contributing to the occurrence of headaches in some individuals who use this herb.
