Krameria triandra causes allergic reactions due to its high content of allergenic compounds such as Kramerin and Kramerol.

These substances can trigger an immune response in some individuals, leading to symptoms like hives, itching, redness, and swelling at the site of contact or throughout the body.

The exact mechanism behind this reaction is still unclear, but it is believed that the body's hypersensitivity to these compounds is responsible for the allergic response.
