What is Phyllagathis velutina?

Phyllagathis velutina is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Melastomataceae, commonly known as the Memecyclaceae or the loganhurstia family.

It is native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia, specifically in countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like habit, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with elliptical to ovate leaves that are 8-20 centimeters long and 4-10 centimeters wide, having a velvety texture due to the dense pubescence. The leaves are oppositely arranged on the stem, with petioles 1-2 millimeters long.

The flowers of Phyllagathis velutina are small, white, and borne in axillary cymes, with a calyx that is 5-6-lobed.
