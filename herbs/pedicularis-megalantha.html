Pedicularis megalantha

What's the taxonomical classification of Pedicularis megalantha?

Pedicularis megalantha belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Orobanchaceae, falling into the genus Pedicularis, and is specifically identified by its species name, megalantha.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis megalantha has a robust, erect stem that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features large, tubular, pinkish-purple flowers with prominent upper lips.

The leaves are primarily basal and deeply pinnatifid, often divided into narrow, linear segments that give the foliage a feathery appearance. As the plant matures, the cauline leaves become smaller and more reduced in size along the length of the stem. The inflorescence is a dense spike composed of numerous zygomorphic flowers characterized by their enlarged, gaping corolla openings.

This distinct floral structure is specifically adapted to facilitate pollination by large insects.
