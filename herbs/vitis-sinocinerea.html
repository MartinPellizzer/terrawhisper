Vitis sinocinerea

What's the taxonomical classification of Vitis sinocinerea?

Vitis sinocinerea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Vitales and is a member of the family Vitaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Vitis, with its specific classification being the species sinocinerea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Vitis sinocinerea has woody climbing vines characterized by trifoliate or pentafoliate leaves with serrated margins and clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. The stems exhibit a twisting growth habit typical of lianas, often utilizing tendrils to anchor themselves to supporting structures. Leaf surfaces can vary in texture from smooth to slightly pubescent depending on the specific environmental conditions. The fruit consists of small, globose berries that transition in color as they reach maturity. These morphological features distinguish the species within its broader genus through specific leaf architecture and growth patterns.
