Botanical Identification

Lobelia inflata, commonly known as Indian Tobacco, is a perennial herb native to North America. Belonging to the Campanulaceae family, it is also referred to as Great Lobelia or Indian Tobacco. The plant features upright stems, heart-shaped leaves, and showy blue to purple flowers. It has a fibrous root system and is sometimes called "Blue Lobelia" in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

Indian Tobacco contains key active compounds such as alkaloids, glycosides, and essential oils. These compounds work synergistically to exert its medicinal effects, though the exact mechanism of action remains partially understood. The presence of multiple bioactive components contributes to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

  • Alkaloid
  • Glycoside
  • Essential oil

Preparation Methods

Tincture: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Infusion: Used for respiratory issues and cold symptoms.

Decoction: Used for digestive and respiratory conditions.

Safety Profile

Indian Tobacco (Lobelia inflata) should not be used by pregnant or breastfeeding women due to potential toxicity. It may cause nausea, vomiting, and respiratory irritation. Avoid long-term use and consult a healthcare provider before use.
