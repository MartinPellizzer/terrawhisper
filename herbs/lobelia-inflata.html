Indian Tobacco (Lobelia inflata)

Indian Tobacco (Lobelia inflata) is a member of the Campanulaceae family, native to Eastern North America, United States, and Canada. Traditionally, its leaves, stems, and roots have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its expectorant, bitter, and antispasmodic actions, and has a long history of use in native american herbal medicine, european herbal medicine, and african traditional medicine.

Botanical Description

Perennial herbaceous plant.
Height
It typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters.
Leaves
Smooth, dorsiventral leaves with upper surface glaucous green and lower surface pale green, bearing distinct stomatal bands along the midrib.
Flowers
Flowers are zygomorphic with five petals arranged in a bilabiate structure, exhibiting two lips with the upper lip consisting of two lobes and the lower lip divided into three lobes, and a distinctive spurred corolla tube.
Stems
Erect, branched, glabrous, with opposite, elongated, angular, and ribbed nodes.
