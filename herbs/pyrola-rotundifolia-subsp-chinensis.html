Pyrola rotundifolia subsp. chinensis

Pyrola rotundifolia subsp.

chinensis, also known as the Chinese wintergreen, is a flowering plant species in the heath family (Ericaceae) that is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties, including its anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antiviral properties, which are attributed to its high concentration of salicylic acid, a compound similar to aspirin.

The health benefits of Chinese wintergreen include reducing fever, relieving pain, and soothing sore throats, as well as its potential to lower blood pressure and improve digestion.

Chinese wintergreen can be prepared as a tea, tincture, or infused oil, and is often used in combination with other herbs to enhance its medicinal properties and promote overall well-being.
