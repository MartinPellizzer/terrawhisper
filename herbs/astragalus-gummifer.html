Gum Arabic Tree

Astragalus gummifer

Ashwagandha is a prominent medicinal herb in Ayurvedic medicine, classified as a rasayana (rejuvenator). It is primarily utilized as an adaptogen to help the body manage physical and chemical stress.

Family
Solanaceae (Nightshade)

Native Region
India, Middle East, Africa

Part Used
Root, Leaf, Berry

Botanical Identification

Gum Arabic Tree, scientifically known as Astragalus gummifer, belongs to the Fabaceae family. It is also called Acacia raifensis in some regions. A perennial shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula, it has a deep root system, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers. It is sometimes referred to as Arabic Gum in herbal texts.

Active Compounds

The Gum Arabic Tree contains key active compounds such as flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, enhancing the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

  • Flavonoid
  • Phenolic acid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Tincture: Used for immune support and chronic fatigue.

Powder: Used for general tonic and energy enhancement.

Safety Profile

Gum Arabic Tree is generally safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause gastrointestinal upset in some individuals. Long-term use or high doses should be avoided, and it is contraindicated in people with known allergies to legumes. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.
