Gum Arabic Tree (Astragalus gummifer)

Gum Arabic Tree (Astragalus gummifer) is a member of the Fabaceae family, native to Middle East, Central Asia, and Southern Europe. Traditionally, its roots, gum, and resin have been used for decoctions, infusions, and powders.

This herb is particularly valued for its tonic, anti-inflammatory, and bitter actions, and has a long history of use in african traditional medicine, european herbal medicine, and mediterranean herbal traditions.
