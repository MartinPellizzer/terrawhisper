What's the taxonomical classification of Smilax guianensis?

Smilax guianensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Smilacaceae, specifically within the genus Smilax, and is identified by the species name guianensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax guianenis has climbing woody stems equipped with recurved prickles, lanceolate leaves arranged in whorls of three, and small greenish-white flowers. The leaves typically feature prominent parallel venation and a distinctively pointed apex. These stems utilize their sharp thorns to scramble through dense tropical vegetation toward sunlight. The plant produces small fleshy berries that turn dark red or black when fully ripe. This morphology allows the species to thrive as a vigorous vine within its forest habitat.
