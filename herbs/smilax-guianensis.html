Smilax guianensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Smilax guianensis?

Smilax guianensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is situated in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Liliales. This plant is a member of the family Smilacaceae, specifically within the genus Smilax, and is identified by the species name guianensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Smilax guianenis has climbing woody stems equipped with recurved prickles, lanceolate leaves arranged in whorls of three, and small greenish-white flowers. The leaves typically feature prominent parallel venation and a distinctively pointed apex. These stems utilize their sharp thorns to scramble through dense tropical vegetation toward sunlight. The plant produces small fleshy berries that turn dark red or black when fully ripe. This morphology allows the species to thrive as a vigorous vine within its forest habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Smilax guianensis?

This plant has significant pharmacological activities including acetylcholinesterase inhibitory, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties. The inhibition of acetylcholinesterase makes it a potential candidate for managing neurodegenerative conditions like Alzheimer's disease. Additionally, various phytochemical constituents within the species contribute to its ability to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. These bioactive compounds also assist in modulating immune responses to mitigate inflammation in biological systems. Such diverse therapeutic potential highlights the medicinal importance of Smilax guianensis in pharmacological research.

According to a study published by "Evidence-based complementary and alternative medicine : eCAM", Smilax guianenensis (Smilacaceae) was found to show moderate AChE inhibition. The research recorded an IC50 value of 205 ± 31 µg/mL for this plant species. Although it showed this inhibitory effect, no significant BChE inhibitory effect was observed for extracts from this plant species.
