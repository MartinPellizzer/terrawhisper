What's the taxonomical classification of Beaumontia pitardii?

Beaumontia pitardii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further organized into the order Gentianales and falls under the family Apocynaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is defined by the genus Beaumontia and the species pitaardii.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Beaumontia pitaardii can cause skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and potential gastrointestinal distress if any part of the plant is ingested. The primary risk involves the plant's sap, which may act as a localized irritant upon contact with sensitive skin or mucous membranes. Individuals handling the foliage should be aware that prolonged exposure can lead to redness, itching, or swelling. Furthermore, while specific toxicity data for humans is limited, the ingestion of any part of this woody climber should be avoided to prevent nausea or abdominal discomfort. Proper protective measures such as gloves are recommended when pruning or managing this species.
