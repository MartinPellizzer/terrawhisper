Neocinnamomum caudatum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Neocinnamomum caudatum has woody stems, lanceolate to elliptical leaves with entire margins, and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in axillary clusters. The foliage typically exhibits a leathery texture and a deep green color on the upper surface. Its leaves are characterized by an attenuated base and a distinctively pointed apex. The plant often features a branched growth habit that supports its dense canopy. Small woody fruits develop following successful pollination of the axillary inflorescences.

      What are the herbal preparations of Neocinnamomum caudatum?

      Neocinnamomum caudatum is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a concentrated tincture to extract its bioactive compounds. The decoction method involves boiling the woody parts of the plant in water to release dense medicinal properties.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Neocinnamomum caudatum can cause skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress when ingested or applied topically. Individuals with sensitive skin may experience localized redness, itching, or swelling upon contact with its extracts. If consumed, the plant might trigger stomach cramps, nausea, or unexpected digestive discomfort in certain users. There is also a potential for systemic allergic reactions in people who have a pre-existing sensitivity to similar botanical compounds. Consulting a medical professional is advisable to mitigate these risks before use.
