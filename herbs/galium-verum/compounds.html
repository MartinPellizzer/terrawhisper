Lady'S Smock (Galium verum) Compounds

Lady's Smock contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and tannins, which contribute to its astringent effects. Coumarin is present and may have anti-inflammatory benefits, while phenolic acids support its antioxidant activity. Glycosides and terpenoids also play roles in its medicinal properties.

Lady'S Smock (Galium verum) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Lady's Smock (Galium verum) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds possess antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting skin health and wound healing. They are found in the plant's flowers and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their soothing and healing qualities.

2. Tannin

Tannin in Lady's Smock (Galium verum) contributes to its astringent properties. It helps in reducing inflammation and supporting wound healing. Tannins also aid in treating digestive issues by strengthening mucous membranes. This compound is a key component in the plant's traditional medicinal use.

3. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Lady's Smock (Galium verum). It contributes to the plant's mild sedative and antispasmodic properties. Coumarin also acts as a natural anticoagulant, supporting circulation. Its presence makes Lady's Smock useful in traditional herbal remedies for digestive and nervous system support.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid in Lady's Smock (Galium verum) contributes to its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. This compound helps reduce oxidative stress and may support skin health. It is part of the plant's broader profile of bioactive compounds used in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Lady's Smock (Galium verum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments. Glycosides in Galium verum may have anti-inflammatory and mild sedative properties. This compound supports the plant's role in herbal medicine for easing symptoms of coughs and indigestion.

6. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Lady's Smock (Galium verum) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. They are responsible for the plant's scent and may support skin healing. Terpenoids are part of the plant's complex chemical profile used in herbal remedies.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Lady's Smock (Galium verum). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory issues. Saponin's cleansing and anti-inflammatory properties support its role in herbal medicine. It is often extracted for its therapeutic benefits.

8. Essential oil

The essential oil of Lady's Smock (Galium verum) contains volatile compounds like cineole and geraniol. These compounds contribute to its aromatic properties and may support respiratory and digestive health. The oil is often used in aromatherapy and traditional herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Lady's Smock (Galium verum). It contributes to the plant's vibrant flower color and possesses antioxidant properties. These compounds support overall health by neutralizing free radicals. Anthocyanin may also aid in reducing inflammation and supporting cardiovascular health.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Lady's Smock (Galium verum). It contributes to the plant's pharmacological properties, though its specific role and mechanisms in traditional herbal medicine are not fully characterized. Research into its potential therapeutic applications is ongoing.
