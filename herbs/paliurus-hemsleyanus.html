Paliurus hemsleyanus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Paliurus hemsleyanus has deciduous, pinnately compound leaves consisting of 5 to 9 leaflets that are elliptic to lanceolate in shape with serrated margins. The plant typically grows as a spreading shrub or small tree with woody stems that often feature sharp, recurved thorns. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in axillary clusters and are generally greenish or yellowish in color. Following flowering, the species produces small, fleshy drupes that turn from green to a dark red or black color upon maturity. The overall structure is characterized by a dense, twiggy habit that provides significant texture to the landscape.
