What's the taxonomical classification of Rhododendron purdomii?

Rhododendron purdomii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. This plant is a member of the family Ericaceae and is specifically categorized within the genus Rhododendron, ultimately reaching its taxonomic designation at the species level as purdomii.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Rhododendron purdomii can cause severe poisoning if ingested because it contains grayanotoxins that disrupt the sodium channels in cell membranes. These toxins primarily affect the nervous system and the cardiovascular system, potentially leading to a dangerous drop in blood pressure. Common symptoms of consumption include nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and excessive salivation. In more serious cases, an individual may experience dizziness, confusion, or irregular heart rhythms known as arrhythmias. Because of these physiological risks, any part of the plant should be handled with extreme caution and kept away from children and pets.
