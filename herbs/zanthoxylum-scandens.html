Zanthoxylum scandens

What's the taxonomical classification of Zanthoxylum scandens?

Zanthoxylum scandens belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving through its higher taxonomic ranks, it is placed in the order Sapindales and the family Rutaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Zanthoxylum, and its unique species designation, scandens.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Zanthoxylum scandens has a climbing woody habit characterized by slender, twining stems and pinnately compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets. The leaflets are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape and possess serrated margins along their edges. Small, inconspicuous flowers are produced in axillary clusters, often leading to the development of small, fleshy drupes. These fruits are typically red or orange when mature and contain a single seed. The bark on older stems tends to become slightly textured as the plant climbs toward the forest canopy.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds magnoflorine, norchelerythrine, hesperidin, (E)-O-geranylconiferyl alcohol, and (-)(S)-O-methylbalfourodinium cation. These specific alkaloids and flavonoids contribute to the diverse pharmacological profile observed in the species. Research into these constituents often focuses on their potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of the (-)(S)-O-methylbalfourodinium cation is particularly notable within its unique chemical makeup. Each of these bioactive molecules plays a distinct role in the plant's overall medicinal utility.

According to a study published by "Zeitschrift fur Naturforschung. C, Journal of biosciences", the bark of Zanthoxylum scandens collected in Vietnam contains several medicinal compounds. The alkaloids norchelerythrine, magnoflorine and (-) (S)-O-methylbalfourodinium cation were isolated from the plant. Additionally, the bark contains the flavanone glycoside hesperidin and the phenylpropanoids (E)-O-geranylconiferyl alcohol and (E)-O-geranylconiferyl alcohol (9Z, 12Z)-linoleate. The latter is a novel compound identified through its spectral data and confirmed by chemical correlation.
