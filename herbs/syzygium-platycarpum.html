What is Syzygium platycarpum?

Syzygium platycarpum is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is an evergreen tree that grows up to 15 meters in height, with a broad, rounded crown and a straight, smooth trunk. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and glossy, measuring 4-10 cm in length and 1.5-3.5 cm in width, with a cuneate base and a rounded apex. The inflorescence is a corymbose panicle, bearing small, white flowers with a prominent, four-lobed calyx and a four-lobed corolla.

The fruit is a drupe, measuring 1-2 cm in length, with a green, leathery rind and a single seed.
