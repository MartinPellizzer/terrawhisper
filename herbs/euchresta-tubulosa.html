What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal alkaloids N-acetyl cytisine, N-formyl cytisine, matrine, and oxymatrine. These specific compounds are known for their diverse biological activities and potential therapeutic applications. Matrine and oxymatrine are particularly recognized for their anti-inflammatory and anti-tumor properties in various studies. Additionally, the cytisine derivatives contribute to the plant's complex chemical profile used in pharmacological research. The presence of these alkaloids makes Euchresta tubulosa a subject of interest for drug development.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", alkaloids were isolated and purified from the stem of Euchresta tubulosa Dunn. using high-speed counter-current chromatography with stepwise elution. Preliminary enrichment was performed using a liquid-liquid extraction with a methylene chloride-methanol-water ratio of 5:1:4, v/v. Two suitable solvent systems for the process were identified as carbon tetrachloride-methylene chloride-methanol-water at 2:3:3:2, v/v and methylene chloride-methanol-water at 5:3:2, v/v. This method allowed for the simultaneous elution of matrine (1), oxymatrine (2), N-formyl cytisine (3), and N-acetyl cytisine (4) with purities all higher than 93%. Through this study, cytisine-type alkaloids were isolated for the first time in this plant.
