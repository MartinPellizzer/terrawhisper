Specifically, this plant is categorized within the genus Dendrobium, and its unique biological identity is defined by its species designation, strongylanthuym.

Dendrobium strongylanthuum is prepared primarily as a decoction where the dried stems are simmered in water to extract its bioactive polysaccharides and alkaloids. Some practitioners also incorporate the processed plant into honey-based syrups to improve palatability and shelf life. Because specific toxicological studies on this exact species are limited, users should exercise extreme caution regarding potential toxicity. Always consult a medical professional or a toxicologist before attempting to use any wild orchid for medicinal purposes.
