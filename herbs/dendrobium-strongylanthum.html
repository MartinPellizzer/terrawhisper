Dendrobium strongylanthum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dendrobium strongylanthuum has sympodial pseudobulbs that are fusiform in shape, distinctly fleshy, and characterized by a smooth, yellowish-green surface. These thickened stems serve as primary water storage organs and are often densely clustered together. The foliage consists of relatively small, coriaceous leaves that emerge from the upper portion of the pseudobulbs. Its inflorescence features numerous small, fragrant flowers that typically exhibit a pale yellowish or creamy white coloration. The labellum is often more pronounced, providing a specialized structure for pollinators during the blooming period.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of New Guinea and surrounding islands in the Melanesian archipelago. It thrives primarily within the humid, lowland rainforest environments characteristic of this geographic zone. The species is typically found growing as an epiphyte on trees where moisture and filtered sunlight are abundant. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific climatic conditions of the Indo-Pacific tropical belt. These localized habitats ensure the plant remains restricted to these particular oceanic island ecosystems.
