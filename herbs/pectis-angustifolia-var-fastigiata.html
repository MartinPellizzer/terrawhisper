Pectis angustifolia var. fastigiata

Pectis angustifolia var. fastigiata, also known as Narrow-leaved Pectis or Mexican Tarragon, is a shrubby plant native to Mexico and South America, belonging to the Asteraceae family.

The medicinal properties of Pectis angustifolia var. fastigiata include its high content of essential oils, particularly limonene, and flavonoids, which exhibit anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial activities.

The health benefits of this plant are attributed to its ability to reduce inflammation, improve respiratory health, and exhibit antioxidant properties, making it a potential treatment for various conditions, including colds, flu, and digestive issues.

Pectis angustifolia var. fastigiata can be prepared as a tea, infusion, or tincture, and is also used as a culinary herb in traditional Mexican cooking.
