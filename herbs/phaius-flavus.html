Phaius flavus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phaius flavus has large, lanceolate green leaves and prominent yellow flowers characterized by a wide, spreading lip and a distinctively colored column. The plant grows from thick, fleshy rhizomes that anchor it within its natural forest floor habitat. Its inflorescences emerge on long, sturdy stalks that can support multiple blooms arranged in a loose spike. Each individual flower features delicate petals and sepals that frame a complex, fleshy labellum. These structural adaptations allow the orchid to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

      What are the herbal preparations of Phaius flavus?

      Phaius flavus is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the rhizomes or as a topical paste made from crushed plant material. These traditional methods allow for the extraction of active compounds used in various folk medicinal practices. When used as a tea, the liquid extract is consumed to address internal ailments.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Phaius flavus can cause severe gastrointestinal distress including nausea, abdominal cramping, and vomiting if ingested. The presence of unidentified alkaloids or irritants in the plant tissue may trigger localized skin irritation or allergic dermatitis upon direct contact. Some individuals might also experience systemic symptoms such as dizziness or mild headaches following accidental exposure. It is essential to avoid consuming any part of the plant to prevent these adverse physiological reactions. Consulting a medical professional is recommended if symptoms occur after handling or ingesting the species.
