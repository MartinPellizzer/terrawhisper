Phaius flavus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Phaius flavus has large, lanceolate green leaves and prominent yellow flowers characterized by a wide, spreading lip and a distinctively colored column. The plant grows from thick, fleshy rhizomes that anchor it within its natural forest floor habitat. Its inflorescences emerge on long, sturdy stalks that can support multiple blooms arranged in a loose spike. Each individual flower features delicate petals and sepals that frame a complex, fleshy labellum. These structural adaptations allow the orchid to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
