Leontopodium franchetii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leontopodium franchetii has a perennial growth habit characterized by a creeping rhizome and leaves that form dense, overlapping rosettes. The leaves are lanceolate to narrowly elliptical in shape and feature slightly serrated or entire margins depending on the specimen's maturity. These leaves are typically fleshy and exhibit a deep green coloration, often displaying fine longitudinal veins along their surface. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the center of the rosette on short stalks during the blooming season. The overall structure allows the plant to create thick mats that cover rocky or alpine substrates.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Hengduan Mountains within southwestern China, specifically spanning the provinces of Yunnan, Sichuan, and Tibet. It thrives primarily in montane environments characterized by cool, moist climates and rocky terrain. Within these mountainous corridors, the species is often found growing in forest margins or along stream banks at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 3,500 meters. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific ecological niches provided by the complex topography of the Sino-Himalayan region. These localized habitats allow the plant to exist in fragmented populations across different valley systems.
