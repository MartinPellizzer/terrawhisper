Leontopodium franchetii

What's the taxonomical classification of Leontopodium franchetii?

Leontopodium franchetii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Asterales. This plant is a specific member of the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower structures. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Leontopodium, and its specific identification is defined by the species epithet franchetii.

Leontopodium franchetii can cause gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and potential toxicity if ingested in improper quantities. Because its chemical composition is not fully documented in modern clinical literature, specific physiological reactions may vary between individuals. Some users might experience nausea or abdominal pain following consumption. Contact with the plant's sap or leaves could also trigger localized dermatitis or allergic rashes on the skin. It is essential to exercise extreme caution due to the lack of extensive human safety data.
