Bletilla ochracea

What's the taxonomical classification of Bletilla ochracea?

Blettilla ochracea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic path further, it is placed in the order Asparagales and belongs to the family Orchidaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Blettilla, with its specific designation being the species ochracea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bletilla ochracea has a basal rosette of linear, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and reach several inches in length. The plant produces several upright, unbranched flowering stems that emerge from a central crown. These stems bear numerous small, bell-shaped flowers which are characterized by their distinct yellowish to pale ochre coloration. The petals are delicate and often feature subtle markings near the throat of the corolla. Small, capsule-like seed pods develop following successful pollination to facilitate reproduction.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds bleochranol A, bleochranols A-D, bletillin A, bletillin B, and phenanthrene. These specific chemical constituents are primarily studied for their unique biological activities and potential therapeutic properties. The presence of the bleochranois group, including bleochranol A and its derivatives A through D, is a defining characteristic of its chemical profile. Additionally, the plant yields bletillin A and bletillin B, which contribute to its complex metabolic makeup. The inclusion of phenanthrene further diversifies the array of bioactive molecules found within the species.

As discussed in a study, the rhizomes of Bletilla ochracea contain 4 new dihydrophenanthrenofuran compounds called bleochranols A-D (1-4) and 21 known compounds including phenanthrenes (5-14) and bibenzyls (15-25). The main chemical constituents identified from the plant were phenanthrene and bibenzyl.
