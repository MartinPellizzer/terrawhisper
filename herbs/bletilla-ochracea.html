What's the taxonomical classification of Bletilla ochracea?

Blettilla ochracea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic path further, it is placed in the order Asparagales and belongs to the family Orchidaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Blettilla, with its specific designation being the species ochracea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bletilla ochracea has a basal rosette of linear, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and reach several inches in length.

The plant produces several upright, unbranched flowering stems that emerge from a central crown. These stems bear numerous small, bell-shaped flowers which are characterized by their distinct yellowish to pale ochre coloration. The petals are delicate and often feature subtle markings near the throat of the corolla.

Small, capsule-like seed pods develop following successful pollination to facilitate reproduction.
