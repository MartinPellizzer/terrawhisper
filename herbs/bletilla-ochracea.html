Bletilla ochracea

What's the taxonomical classification of Bletilla ochracea?

Blettilla ochracea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic path further, it is placed in the order Asparagales and belongs to the family Orchidaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Blettilla, with its specific designation being the species ochracea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bletilla ochracea has a basal rosette of linear, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and reach several inches in length. The plant produces several upright, unbranched flowering stems that emerge from a central crown. These stems bear numerous small, bell-shaped flowers which are characterized by their distinct yellowish to pale ochre coloration. The petals are delicate and often feature subtle markings near the throat of the corolla. Small, capsule-like seed pods develop following successful pollination to facilitate reproduction.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds bleochranois A through D, bletillin A, bletillin B, and militariniine. The specific chemical constituents identified within Blettella ochracea include the unique structural forms known as bleochranois A, B, C, and D. Additionally, the plant is characterized by the presence of the specific molecules bletillin A and bletillin B. Research into these substances often focuses on the presence of militariniine as well. These diverse secondary metabolites contribute to the complex chemical profile of the species.

According to a study published by "Fitoterapia", the rhizomes of Bletilla ochracea contain 4 new dihydrophenanthrenofran compounds named bleochranols A-D (1-4) and 21 known compounds including phenanthrenes (5-14) and bibenzyls (15-25). Among the 25 tested compounds, bleochranol A (1) demonstrated cytotoxic activity against HL-60, A-549, and MCF-7 human cancer cell lines with IC50 values of 0.24 ± 0.03, 3.51 ± 0.09, and 3.30 ± 0.99 μM respectively. Additionally, compound 12 exhibited the most potential anti-inflammatory activity against NO production in RAW 264.7 macrophages with an IC50 of 2.86 ± 0.17 μM. The main chemical constituents identified in the plant were phenanthrene and bibenzyl.

These applications help mitigate the severity of ulcerations throughout the gastrointestinal tract.
