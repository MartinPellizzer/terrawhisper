Bletilla ochracea

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Bletilla ochracea has a basal rosette of linear, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and reach several inches in length. The plant produces several upright, unbranched flowering stems that emerge from a central crown. These stems bear numerous small, bell-shaped flowers which are characterized by their distinct yellowish to pale ochre coloration. The petals are delicate and often feature subtle markings near the throat of the corolla. Small, capsule-like seed pods develop following successful pollination to facilitate reproduction.
