What is Isolepis fimbriata?

Isolepis fimbriata is a species of sedge that belongs to the family Cyperaceae, a large and widespread family of monocots.

The plant is characterized by its tufted habit, slender stems that reach up to 60 cm in height, and linear-lanceolate leaves that are 1-3 mm wide and 3-15 cm long. The leaves are usually bright green, often slightly darker at the base, and have a distinctive fringed or fimbriate margin. Isolepis fimbriata produces spikelets that are 1-3 mm long, with a linear-lanceolate glume and a narrowly elliptic sterile lemma, and are typically arranged in a dense, cylindrical spike.

The plant is commonly found in wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and water edges, where the water level is often fluctuating or seasonally flooded.
