Vepris madagascariensis

Vepris madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar Yellow Myrtle or Bois de Camphre, is a plant species in the Rutaceae family.

It is a tree native to Madagascar, valued for its medicinal properties and versatility in traditional medicine.

The health benefits of Vepris madagascariensis include its use in treating fever, rheumatism, and skin conditions, due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The bioactive constituents of this plant include limonoids, alkaloids, and coumarins, which exhibit therapeutic actions such as analgesia, antisepticism, and anti-inflammatory activity.

In severe cases, improper use of Vepris madagascariensis has been linked to cardiac arrest, seizures, and even death.

It is essential to consult a medical professional before using the plant for health purposes to avoid these potential side effects.
