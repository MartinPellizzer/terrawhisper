Dregea yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dregea yunnanensis has woody climbing vines characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and a smooth texture. The stems are often twining and can reach significant lengths as they ascend through forest canopies. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact cymes. The fruit is a distinctively winged schizocarp that facilitates wind dispersal. These morphological traits are typical of the Menispermaceae family to which this species belongs.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and tropical regions of southern China, specifically occurring within the provinces of Yunnan and Guangxi. The species is concentrated in the southeastern part of the Asian continent where humidity remains high throughout the year. Its range is restricted to these specific ecological zones that support its climbing growth habit.

      What are the herbal preparations of Dregea yunnanensis?

      Dregea yunnanensis is prepared as a decoction by boiling the dried stems and leaves in water to extract its medicinal properties. This liquid extract is commonly consumed orally to address various physiological ailments.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Dregea yunnanensis can cause severe skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and potential mucosal inflammation upon direct contact with its sap or foliage. The plant contains various secondary metabolites that may trigger localized swelling or redness if touched by sensitive individuals. Some users might experience itching or burning sensations at the site of contact. Because the specific toxicity levels for human consumption are not thoroughly documented, ingestion could lead to unpredictable gastrointestinal distress. It is essential to handle this species with caution to avoid unexpected dermatological reactions.
