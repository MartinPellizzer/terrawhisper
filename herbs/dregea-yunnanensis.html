Dregea yunnanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Dregea yunnanensis?

Dregea yunnanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is classified under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic progression, it falls under the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Apocynaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Dregea, and its species name, yunnanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Dregea yunnanensis has woody climbing vines characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and a smooth texture. The stems are often twining and can reach significant lengths as they ascend through forest canopies. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact cymes. The fruit is a distinctively winged schizocarp that facilitates wind dispersal. These morphological traits are typical of the Menispermaceae family to which this species belongs.
