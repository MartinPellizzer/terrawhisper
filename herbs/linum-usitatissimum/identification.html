Botanical Identification of Flax (Linum usitatissimum)

To identify Flax (Linum usitatissimum) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Flax (Linum usitatissimum) Monograph

Scientific Classification

Flax, Linum usitatissimum, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Linum, and species usitatissimum. This classification places it within the legume family, highlighting its botanical relationships and characteristics.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of flax is Linum usitatissimum, first described by Carl von Linné in 1753. It is also known by several synonyms, including Linum usitatissimum L. and Linum sativum. Common names such as flax, common flax, and linseed are widely used, while regional and historical names reflect its long-standing use in medicine and agriculture.

Morphological Characteristics

Flax is an annual herb with a taproot system and a fibrous root system. It has erect, slender stems that are smooth and slightly hairy. The leaves are alternate, linear, and have a prominent midrib. The inflorescence is a raceme of blue or white flowers with five petals. The fruit is a capsule containing small, oval seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Flax can be distinguished by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves with smooth margins. It produces blue or white flowers in loose clusters, and its capsules are slender, ribbed, and contain small, oval seeds. These features help differentiate it from similar plants like wild flax or other members of the Linaceae family.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Flax is native to temperate regions of Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. It thrives in cool climates with moderate rainfall. It prefers well-drained loamy soils and is commonly found at altitudes between 200 and 1,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Flax blooms from late spring to early summer, typically between May and July, depending on the region. Seed formation occurs in late summer, around August to September. For identification purposes, the plant is harvested when the seed capsules are mature and beginning to split, usually in late September to early October.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Flax belongs to the genus Linum within the family Plantaginaceae. Its closest relatives include species like Linum corniculatum and Linum lewisii. These species share similar morphological features, such as blue or purple flowers and capsule fruits, which are characteristic of the genus Linum.
