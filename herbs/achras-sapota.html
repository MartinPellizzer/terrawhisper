Achras sapota

What's the taxonomical classification of Achras sapota?

Achras sapota belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Further taxonomic refinement places the plant in the order Ericales and the family Sapotaeceae. Finally, its specific identity is defined by the genus Achras and the species sapota.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Achras sapota contains bioactive phytochemicals including tannins, saponins, flavonoids, and alkaloids that provide its therapeutic properties. The presence of tannins contributes to astringent effects, which are often utilized to treat gastrointestinal issues and wound healing. Flavonoids within the plant act as potent antioxidants, helping to reduce oxidative stress and inflammation in the body. Additionally, the alkaloids and saponins may possess antimicrobial and antispasmodic qualities. These diverse chemical components make the plant a subject of interest for various traditional and pharmacological applications.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including diarrhea, dysentery, malaria, and skin infections. The various parts of the tree, such as the bark and leaves, possess medicinal properties that are utilized in traditional healing practices. Specifically, decoctions made from the bark are often employed to manage digestive issues and feverish conditions. Some cultures also apply crushed leaves or extracts to the skin to soothe irritations and promote healing. These applications highlight the plant's historical importance in botanical medicine for managing systemic and localized symptoms.
