Arctium horrescens

Arctium horrescens, commonly known as the prickly thistle or devil's thistle, is a biennial plant species in the sunflower family native to Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa.

The plant is used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties, which help in treating digestive issues, wounds, and skin conditions.

The therapeutic actions of Arctium horrescens include its ability to act as a purgative, anti-diarrheal, and anti-spasmodic agent, while also possessing immunomodulatory and antioxidant properties.

The bioactive constituents of the plant include inulin, flavonoids, phenolic acids, and sesquiterpenes, which contribute to its medicinal properties and potential health benefits.
