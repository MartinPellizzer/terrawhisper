Selaginella davidi

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella davidi?

Selaginella davidi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Selaginellales. It is further categorized under the family Selaginellaceae, falling within the genus Selaginella, and is specifically identified by its species name, davidi.

What are the herbal preparations of Selaginella davidi?

Selaginella davidi is prepared primarily as a decoction through boiling the dried aerial parts of the plant in water to extract its bioactive compounds. This aqueous extract is frequently administered as a traditional tea or liquid medicine in various herbal formulations. Some practitioners also utilize the dried plant material in the form of powders that can be encapsulated or mixed into other medicinal substances. Additionally, concentrated extracts may be processed into tinctures using alcohol as a solvent to ensure long-term stability and potency. These diverse preparation methods allow for different concentrations of the plant's active constituents depending on the intended therapeutic use.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Selaginella davidi can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or respiratory discomfort if its spores or dust are inhaled or handled without protection. While this resurrection fern is primarily used for ornamental purposes, some individuals may experience contact dermatitis upon touching the foliage. Allergic responses like itching or redness are possible for those with sensitive skin. Additionally, if the plant is kept in a poorly ventilated area, the fine particles from its spores could potentially irritate the lungs. It is important to use gloves and avoid breathing in any airborne plant matter to minimize these risks.
