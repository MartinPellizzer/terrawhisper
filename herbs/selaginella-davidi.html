Selaginella davidi

What's the taxonomical classification of Selaginella davidi?

Selaginella davidi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Lycopodiidae, this plant follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Selaginellales. It is further categorized under the family Selaginellaceae, falling within the genus Selaginella, and is specifically identified by its species name, davidi.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Selaginella davidi contains bioactive flavonoids, alkaloids, and terpenoids that exhibit significant antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-tumor properties. Research indicates that these phytochemicals can inhibit the proliferation of various cancer cell lines by inducing apoptosis. The plant also demonstrates potential for neuroprotection due to its ability to mitigate oxidative stress in nervous tissues. Furthermore, the presence of specific polyphenolic compounds supports its use in traditional medicinal practices for treating inflammation. Ongoing pharmacological studies continue to investigate how these chemical constituents interact with biological pathways to treat chronic diseases.
