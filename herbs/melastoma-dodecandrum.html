What's the taxonomical classification of Melastoma dodecandrum?

Melastoma dodecandrum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Myrtales and the family Melastomataceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Melastoma. Finally, the plant is identified at the species level as dodecandrum.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds casuarictin, casuariinin, nobotannin B, pedunclagin, and various ellagitannins. These specific polyphenolic substances are classified as hydrolyzable tannins that contribute to the plant's biological activities. The presence of these compounds is often linked to the antioxidant and antimicrobial properties observed in the species. Researchers study these molecules to understand how they interact with cellular structures in medicinal applications. Such chemical profiles make the plant a subject of interest in pharmacological investigations.

According to a study published by "Biological & pharmaceutical bulletin", an extract of Melastoma dodecandrum Lour. obtained with 80% aqueous acetone (MDL) inhibits nitric oxide production in RAW264.7 cells. Further fractionation showed that the 50% methanol extracts contained hydrolyzable tannins, including casuariinin, casuarictin, pedunclagin, and nobotannin B. These specific tannins exhibited strong inhibitory activities toward nitric oxide production with ID50 values between 2.0 and 5.1 microM. Additionally, these tannins inhibited nitric oxide production in macrophages preactivated for 16 h with LPS and IFN-gamma, showing IC50 values around 30-130 microM. These findings suggest that MDL and its hydrolyzable tannins possess the pharmacological ability to suppress nitric oxide production.

This plant was among the 88 plant species investigated to see if they contain compounds with bacterial growth inhibition to prevent secondary infections.
