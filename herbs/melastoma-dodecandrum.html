Melastoma dodecandrum

What's the taxonomical classification of Melastoma dodecandrum?

Melastoma dodecandrum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Myrtales and the family Melastomataceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Melastoma. Finally, the plant is identified at the species level as dodecandrum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Melastoma dodecandrum has opposite, elliptical leaves with prominent longitudinal veins, bright yellow flowers with ten stamens, and small, globose berries. The foliage is typically dark green and slightly pubescent, providing a textured appearance to the shrub. Its flowers feature long, protruding stamens that are characteristic of the Melastomataceae family. The plant produces fleshy fruit that transitions in color as it matures. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains casuarictin, casuariinin, ellagitannins, gallic acid, and hydrolyzable tannins. These medicinal compounds are primarily concentrated within the plant's chemical structure and contribute to its biological properties. The presence of ellagitannins and hydrolyzable tannins provides significant antioxidant potential. Furthermore, the specific combination of casuarictin and casuariinin works alongside gallic acid to influence various physiological activities. These diverse phytochemicals collectively define the medicinal profile of the species.

As discussed in a study, an extract of Melastoma dodecandrum Lour. prepared with 80% aqueous acetone inhibits nitric oxide production. The majority of the inhibitory activity was found in 50% methanol extracts containing hydrolyzable tannins. Specifically, casuariinin, casuarictin, pedunclagin, and nobotannin B showed strong inhibitory activities toward nitric oxide production with ID50 values between 2.0 and 5.1 microM. These tannins also inhibited nitric oxide production in macrophages preactivated with LPS and IFN-gamma for 16 h, yielding IC50 values around 30-130 microM. These findings suggest that the hydrolyzable tannins within the plant possess major inhibitory activities.

As discussed in a study, Melastoma dodecandrum is a widely used traditional Chinese medicine rich in ellagitannins (ETs). Using MN-guided targeted profiling, a total of 70 ETs ranging from monomers to tetramers were tracked and characterized in the MD extract. Within this group, 59 ETs were reported for the first time in this species. The MD-ETs significantly improved memory impairment in AD mice through decreased escape latency and increased number of crossings and target quadrant distance in the Morris water maze test. These effects also included an increased rearing number in the open field test and an increased preference index in the novel object recognition test.
