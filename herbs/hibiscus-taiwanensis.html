Hibiscus taiwanensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Hibiscus taiwanensis?

Hibiscus taiwanensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further organized into the order Malvales and the family Malvaceae. Finally, its specific taxonomic identity is established within the genus Hibiscus, reaching its definitive classification at the species level as taiwanensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hibiscus taiwanensis has woody stems, elliptical to ovate leaves with serrated margins, and large, funnel-shaped flowers that typically feature five petals in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The leaves are arranged alternately along the branches and possess a leathery texture with prominent venation. Its floral structure is characterized by a central staminal column that is fused to the petal bases. The plant often displays a spreading or upright growth habit depending on its specific environmental conditions. Small, woody capsules form as fruit to protect the developing seeds.
