Gynostemma yixingense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gynostemma yixingense has climbing or creeping stems that produce opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute apices. The foliage typically features a smooth texture and a light green coloration on both surfaces. Small, white, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by five fused petals. The inflorescence is often solitary or arranged in small clusters along the stem. Its fruit develops into a small, globose capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.
