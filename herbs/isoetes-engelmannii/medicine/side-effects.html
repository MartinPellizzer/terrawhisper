1. Causes allergic reactions

Isoetes engelmannii causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to the presence of certain compounds such as isoetines and saponins.

These naturally occurring chemicals can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms like itching, redness, and swelling at the site of exposure.

Additionally, inhalation of airborne particles may cause respiratory issues, further exacerbating allergy-related discomfort.

2. Induces dizziness episodes

Isoetes engelmannii induces dizziness episodes due to its unique composition of sesquiterpenes and triterpenoids.

These compounds can affect the body's balance system by stimulating the vestibular apparatus in the inner ear, leading to feelings of lightheadedness and dizziness.

Additionally, Isoetes engelmannii contains alkaloids that can impact neurotransmitters in the brain, further contributing to the occurrence of these episodes.

3. Triggers respiratory problems

Isoetes engelmannii triggers respiratory problems due to its irritating properties that can cause inflammation and constriction of airways.

When inhaled, the plant's volatile oils may stimulate the respiratory system, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

The potent allergenic compounds present in the plant can also exacerbate existing respiratory conditions like asthma and bronchitis, further compromising lung function.

Isoetes engelmannii stimulates digestive issues due to its unique composition of volatile oils and saponins.

The consumption of this plant may cause an imbalance in the gut microbiome, leading to symptoms such as bloating, abdominal pain, and changes in bowel movements.

As Isoetes engelmannii binds to GABA receptors, it amplifies the calming effects of GABA, leading to increased feelings of drowsiness and reduced anxiety.
