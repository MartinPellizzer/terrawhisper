Typha minima

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Typha minima has slender, cylindrical leaves that reach up to 30 centimeters in length and a small, compact flowering spike that measures between 2 and 5 centimeters. The plant is characterized by its diminutive stature compared to other species in the genus. Its leaves are typically linear and can be either green or slightly brownish in hue. The inflorescence consists of a dense, brown spadix that is often quite short and lacks the prominent white fluff found in larger varieties. This species also features a creeping rhizomatous root system that helps it colonize wetland environments.
