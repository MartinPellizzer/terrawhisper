Leymus chinensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Leymus chinensis?

Leymus chinensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. It falls under the order Poales and is a member of the family Poaceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Leymus, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species chinensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Leymus chinensis has perennial, tufted growth habits characterized by erect, slender culms that typically reach heights of 60 to 120 centimeters. The leaves are narrow and linear, often featuring a prominent midrib and a slightly scabrous texture along the margins. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like panicles that are composed of numerous spikelets arranged along a central axis. Each spikelet contains multiple florets that are arranged in a compact, somewhat flattened structure. The root system is robust and fibrous, allowing the plant to anchor firmly in various soil types.
