Corydalis tibetoalpina

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis tibetoalpina?

Corydalis tibetoalpina belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Ranunculales and is a member of the family Papaveraceae. Specifically, it is identified under the genus Corydalis, with its unique species designation being tibetoalpina.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis tibetoalpina has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by dissected, pinnatifid leaves and zygomorphic, spurred flowers that typically feature a violet or purplish coloration. The plant develops from a tuberous rhizome that stores nutrients for its seasonal growth cycles in alpine environments. Its inflorescences consist of multiple flowers arranged along a central stem, each possessing a distinct nectar-producing spur. The leaf structure is deeply lobed, providing a high surface area for photosynthesis in thin mountain air. These morphological traits collectively allow the species to adapt to the specific climatic demands of its high-altitude habitat.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Corydalis tibetoalpina contains various isoquinoline alkaloids, including protopine, corydaline, and californidine, which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. Research indicates that these alkaloids possess potent analgesic and sedative properties useful for pain management. Additionally, certain constituents exhibit antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects within biological systems.
