Cimicifuga foetida

What's the taxonomical classification of Cimicifuga foetida?

Cimicifuga foetida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. Furthermore, this plant is a member of the family Ranunculaceae, falling under the genus Cimicifuga, and is specifically identified by the species epithet foetida.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds Cimifoeetiside III, cimicifugin, cimifoetiside VI, cimifoetiside VII, and cimimigenol-3-O-beta-D-xylopyranoside. These specific chemical constituents are primarily classified as triterpenoid saponins. They play a significant role in the plant's complex pharmacological profile. Researchers study these molecules to understand their potential therapeutic applications in various biological systems. The presence of such diverse compounds contributes to the overall chemical complexity of the species.

As discussed in a study, Cimicifuga foetida L. possesses inhibitory activity against RSV and contains cimicifugin as a major constituent. This compound dose-dependently inhibited RSV-induced plaque formation in both HEp-2 and A549 cell lines with a significance of p < 0.0001. The antiviral activity of cimicifugin was time-dependent (p < 0.0001) and was most effective when cells were treated before viral inoculation. Research demonstrated that cimicifugin could inhibit viral attachment (p < 0.0001) and viral internalization (p < 0.0001). Additionally, the compound could stimulate epithelial cells to secrete IFN-β to counteract the infection.
