Hippeastrum vittatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. This plant is a representative of the family Amaryllidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Hippeastrum, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name vittatum.

These specific alkaloids and flavonoids are often studied for their diverse biological activities in pharmacological research. Isoliquiritigenin and its methylated derivative are known for providing antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The presence of lycorine and ismine contributes to the complex chemical profile used in traditional medicine. Together, these substances represent the primary bioactive constituents responsible for the plant's therapeutic potential.

    As discussed in a study, sixteen chemically varied metabolites were isolated from the bulbs of Hippeastrum vittatum.
