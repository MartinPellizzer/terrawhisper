Tilia mandshurica

What's the taxonomical classification of Tilia mandshurica?

Tilia mandshurica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the taxonomic lineage of the order Malvales. It is further categorized under the family Malvaceae, specifically within the genus Tilia, and is identified by the species name mandshurica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tilia mandshurica has heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins, small yellowish-white fragrant flowers, and oval-shaped drupes attached to a specialized leaf-like bract. The leaves are typically arranged alternately on branches and possess a base that is often asymmetrical. During the flowering season, the inflorescences emerge from the leaf axils and are characterized by their subtle scent. The distinctive bract functions as a wing to aid in wind dispersal of the seeds. This deciduous tree displays a spreading crown with bark that becomes deeply fissured as the specimen matures.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?
