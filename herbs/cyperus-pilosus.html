Cyperus pilosus

What's the taxonomical classification of Cyperus pilosus?

Cyperus pilosus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is placed in the order Poales and belongs to the family Cyperaceae. Ultimately, it is defined by its genus, Cyperus, and its specific species designation, pilosus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cyperus pilosus has a perennial life cycle characterized by slender, triangular stems that emerge from a creeping rhizomatous base. The leaves are linear, grass-like, and typically grow in basal tufts with a fine, almost hair-like texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, brownish spikelets arranged in a terminal umbel-like cluster. Each spikelet contains numerous tiny flowers protected by scales that contribute to its compact appearance. This plant often develops a dense, tufted growth habit in its natural habitat.
