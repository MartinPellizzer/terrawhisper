Carex densifimbriata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Carex densifimbriata has a perennial growth habit characterized by dense, tufted clumps of linear, grass-like leaves that often appear folded or channeled. The leaves are typically dark green, relatively short, and possess a somewhat scabrous texture along the margins. Its reproductive structures consist of inconspicuous spikes arranged along a central stem, with scales that are often dark or brownish in color. The perigynia, which enclose the female flowers, are typically small, ovate, and feature a distinctly thickened or fringed beak. These morphological traits help distinguish this specific sedge from other similar species within its genus.
