Thalictrum cultratum

What's the taxonomical classification of Thalictrum cultratum?

Thalictrum cultratum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Ranunculales. As a member of the family Ranunculaceae, it is specifically categorized under the genus Thalictrum, with its unique identification completed at the species level as cultratum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Thalictrum cultratum has deeply lobed, ternately divided leaves that are characterized by their sharp, knife-like margins and a bright green coloration.

The plant features a sturdy stem that supports small, inconspicuous flowers typically arranged in loose, terminal panicles. These floral structures consist of delicate, pale petals and numerous stamens that contribute to a light, airy appearance. The overall growth habit is herbaceous, often emerging from rhizomatous root systems in moist forest environments.

Its morphological structure is specifically adapted to thrive in shaded, damp habitats.
