Phyllolobium chinense has small, pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of leaflets that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape with entire margins. These leaflets often exhibit a bright green color and a smooth texture on both the upper and lower surfaces. The plant features a woody stem structure that supports its spreading growth habit. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils during its blooming period. The overall morphology is characterized by its delicate foliage and compact branching pattern.

      What are the herbal preparations of Phyllolobium chinense?

      Phyllolobium chinense is prepared primarily as a decoction, a concentrated liquid made by boiling the dried leaves in water to extract active compounds. This traditional method is often supplemented by the creation of herbal teas where the plant material is steeped in hot water. Some practitioners also utilize the plant in powdered form, which can be mixed into warm liquids or encapsulated for easier consumption.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Phyllolobium chinense can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential interference with blood coagulation processes. Some individuals may experience nausea or stomach cramping after consuming the plant. Allergic sensitivities can manifest as itching, rashes, or swelling upon topical contact or ingestion. There is also a theoretical risk that the plant's active compounds might interact with anticoagulant medications. Monitoring for these symptoms is essential when using the plant for medicinal purposes.
