Acmella oleracea

What's the taxonomical classification of Acmella oleracea?

Acmella oleracea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is a part of the widely recognized family Asteraceae, which is further categorized into the genus Acmella, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species oleracea.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Acmella oleaceas contains spilanthol, which is a bioactive alkylamide responsible for its characteristic numbing and tingling sensations. This specific compound exhibits significant analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties when applied to tissues. The plant also possesses various flavonoids and phenolic acids that contribute to its antioxidant capacity. These phytochemicals work together to inhibit certain bacteria and reduce localized swelling. Researchers study these components to develop natural topical treatments for pain relief and skin health.

What are the therapeutic actions of Acmella oleracea?

Acmella oleacea has potent antimicrobial, analgesic, and anti-inflammatory properties primarily driven by the bioactive alkaloid spilanthol. This compound acts as a natural topical anesthetic by inducing a tingling sensation and temporarily numbing nerve endings in the skin or mucous membranes. Its ability to inhibit the growth of various bacteria and fungi makes it a useful component in treating skin infections and oral ailments. Additionally, the plant helps reduce swelling and pain by modulating inflammatory pathways in the body. These therapeutic actions are frequently utilized in dermatological preparations and natural oral care products.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, boils, abscesses, and various types of inflammatory dermatological conditions. Its medicinal properties are primarily harnessed through the application of its juice or crushed leaves to affected areas. The plant contains bioactive compounds that exhibit strong antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory activities. These properties make it particularly effective for accelerating the healing process of minor cuts and skin lesions. Local traditional medicine often relies on its ability to reduce swelling and combat bacterial pathogens.

What are the herbal preparations of Acmella oleracea?

Acmella oleacea is prepared as a topical paste, a decoction, or a fresh leaf infusion to utilize its numbing properties. The fresh leaves are often crushed directly onto the skin to treat localized pain or insect bites. When used internally, the plant is boiled in water to create a medicinal tea that addresses digestive issues. Some traditional practitioners also extract the essential oils for concentrated applications. These diverse methods allow for both immediate sensory relief and systemic herbal benefits.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Acmella oleracea can cause localized numbness, tingling, or a burning sensation on the skin and mucous membranes due to its high concentration of spilanthol. This bioactive compound acts as a natural anesthetic, which may lead to unexpected sensitivity or irritation if applied improperly. Some individuals might experience allergic reactions such as redness, itching, or swelling upon contact with the plant. Because the plant affects nerve endings, excessive topical use could result in temporary loss of sensation in the affected area. It is important to monitor for these reactions when using extracts derived from this species.
