Acmella oleracea

What's the taxonomical classification of Acmella oleracea?

Acmella oleracea belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is a part of the widely recognized family Asteraceae, which is further categorized into the genus Acmella, ultimately reaching its specific designation as the species oleracea.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acmella oleacea has a decumbent to erect herbaceous stem that typically reaches heights of up to 50 centimeters and features oppositely arranged, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire or slightly serrated margins. The foliage is characterized by a soft, pubescent texture that covers both the upper and lower surfaces of the blades. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flower heads arranged in dense, terminal clusters or capitula. These flower heads are supported by green involucral bracts and contain numerous tiny tubular florets. The plant's overall structure is compact, often forming low-growing mats in its natural habitat.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains 14 alkamides, spilanthol, lupeol (fagarasterol), quercetin, and (E)-caryophyllene. These specific medicinal compounds contribute to the complex chemical profile and therapeutic potential of the species. The presence of spilanthol is often associated with unique sensory and numbing effects. Additionally, the combination of quercetin and lupeol provides significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. The sesquiterpene (E)-caryophyllene further enhances the plant's bioactive versatility.

As discussed in a study, the inflorescences of Acmella oleracea contain 14 annotated alkamides. HPLC-DAD analysis of the ethanolic extract revealed a peak with a substantial relative area of approximately 85% that was identified as spilanthol. This specific compound was isolated from the extract through chromatographic fractionation. The plant is utilized for its local anesthetic, insecticidal, antiparasitic, and anthelmintic attributes. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause local irritation, allergic reactions, nausea, dizziness, and stomach upset in individuals who consume it or touch it. Some people may experience a systemic allergic reaction characterized by hives or respiratory distress. If ingested, the plant is known to lead to stomach upset and persistent nausea. Additionally, some users have reported feeling sudden dizziness following exposure.
