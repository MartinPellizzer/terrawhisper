Clitoria hanceana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clitoria hanceana has a herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, prostrate to ascending stems that often feature small, lanceolate leaves arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces zygomorphic flowers that exhibit a distinct structure typical of the Fabaceae family, including a prominent standard petal, two wing petals, and a keel. These floral structures are typically colored in shades of blue or violet, often accented by white or yellow markings near the center to guide pollinators. The inflorescence emerges from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters or solitary units. Small, legume-like pods develop following successful pollination, which eventually split to release tiny seeds.
