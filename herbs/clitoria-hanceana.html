Clitoria hanceana

What's the taxonomical classification of Clitoria hanceana?

Clitoria hanceana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its higher-level classification, it falls under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Clitoria, and its unique species designation, hanceanas.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clitoria hanceana has a herbaceous growth habit characterized by slender, prostrate to ascending stems that often feature small, lanceolate leaves arranged alternately along the branches.

The plant produces zygomorphic flowers that exhibit a distinct structure typical of the Fabaceae family, including a prominent standard petal, two wing petals, and a keel. These floral structures are typically colored in shades of blue or violet, often accented by white or yellow markings near the center to guide pollinators. The inflorescence emerges from the leaf axils, often appearing in small clusters or solitary units.

Small, legume-like pods develop following successful pollination, which eventually split to release tiny seeds.

        What are the herbal preparations of Clitoria hanceana?

        Clitoria hanceana is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion through the boiling or steeping of its dried plant parts in water. Practitioners often utilize the leaves and flowers to create medicinal teas intended for internal consumption. In some traditional applications, the plant material may be crushed to form a topical poultice applied directly to the skin.         What side-effects this plant can have?

        Clitoria hanceana can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, dizziness, and allergic skin irritations if consumed or applied improperly. While specific clinical data on human toxicity is limited, many plants in the Fabaceae family contain alkaloids that may trigger unexpected physiological responses. Individuals with known sensitivities to legumes should exercise extreme caution to avoid potential anaphylaxis or localized swelling. It is also possible for the plant to interact with certain medications, leading to unpredictable systemic effects. Consulting a medical professional is necessary before using any part of this species for medicinal purposes.
