Clitoria hanceana

What's the taxonomical classification of Clitoria hanceana?

Clitoria hanceana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its higher-level classification, it falls under the order Fabales and the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Clitoria, and its unique species designation, hanceanas.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wound healing, digestive issues, and inflammatory conditions. The leaves and flowers often possess antimicrobial properties that help combat various bacterial or fungal skin ailments. When prepared as a decoction, it can soothe internal inflammation and assist with gastrointestinal discomfort. Local practitioners frequently apply topical preparations to promote the rapid closing of cuts or abrasions. Its various bioactive compounds make it a versatile component in traditional herbal medicine for managing multiple ailments.

What are the herbal preparations of Clitoria hanceana?

Clitoria hanceana is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion through the boiling or steeping of its dried plant parts in water. Practitioners often utilize the leaves and flowers to create medicinal teas intended for internal consumption. In some traditional applications, the plant material may be crushed to form a topical poultice applied directly to the skin. Some variations involve macerating the botanical elements in alcohol to produce concentrated tinctures. These diverse methods allow for the extraction of different active compounds depending on the desired therapeutic effect.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Clitoria hanceana can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, dizziness, and allergic skin irritations if consumed or applied improperly. While specific clinical data on human toxicity is limited, many plants in the Fabaceae family contain alkaloids that may trigger unexpected physiological responses. Individuals with known sensitivities to legumes should exercise extreme caution to avoid potential anaphylaxis or localized swelling. It is also possible for the plant to interact with certain medications, leading to unpredictable systemic effects. Consulting a medical professional is necessary before using any part of this species for medicinal purposes.
