Veronica undulata

What's the taxonomical classification of Veronica undulata?

Veronica undulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is classified under the order Lamiales. This plant is a member of the family Plantaginaeae, falling under the genus Veronica, and is specifically identified by its species name, undulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Veronica undulata has narrow, linear leaves with distinctly wavy or undulate margins and a texture that is typically hairy on both surfaces. The plant is a perennial herb characterized by its creeping or ascending stems that can form dense mats. Its small, blue to violet flowers are arranged in terminal racemes and possess the characteristic four-lobed corolla typical of the genus. The calyx consists of four sepals that often enclose the developing fruit. This species is distinguished from similar plants primarily by the specific undulating shape of its leaf edges.
