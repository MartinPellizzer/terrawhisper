What are the pharmacological activities of Schisandra neglecta?

This plant has potent neuroprotective pharmacological activities that specifically provide protective activities against corticosterone-induced apoptosis in neural cells. These beneficial effects are primarily achieved through the modulation of cellular signaling pathways to prevent programmed cell death. By mitigating the damage caused by high levels of stress hormones, the plant helps maintain neuronal integrity and survival. Such mechanisms suggest that its bioactive compounds could play a significant role in managing stress-related neurological decline. Research indicates that these properties make it a promising candidate for neurotherapeutic development.

According to a study published by "Bioorganic chemistry", Schisdilactones L-S (2-8) and U (11) were isolated from the leaves and stems of Schisandra neglecta A. C. Smith. These 16,17-secopreschisanartane-type schinortriterpenoids demonstrated moderate protective activities against corticosterone-induced apoptosis of PC12 cells when used at 20 μM. The observed cell viability for these compounds was in the range of 62.95-66.97%. This study involved the isolation of a series of previously unreported schinortriterpenoids designated as Schisdilactones K-U (1-11).
