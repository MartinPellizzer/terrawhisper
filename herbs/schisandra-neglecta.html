Schisandra neglecta

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schisandra neglecta has woody, climbing vines characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. The stems are flexible and often woody at the base, allowing the plant to scramble through forest undergrowth. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically appear in clusters, followed by the development of distinctive aggregate fruits. These fruits are fleshy and contain multiple small seeds, often turning a deep red or brownish color when mature. The overall vegetative structure is designed for a climbing habit within shaded, humid environments.
