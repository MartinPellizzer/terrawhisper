What's the taxonomical classification of Schisandra neglecta?

Schisandra neglecta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant follows the order Austrobaileyales and is a member of the family Schisandraceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Schisandra, with its specific designation being the species neglecta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Schisandra neglecta has woody, climbing vines characterized by opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and prominent pinnate venation. The stems are flexible and often woody at the base, allowing the plant to scramble through forest undergrowth. Small, inconspicuous flowers typically appear in clusters, followed by the development of distinctive aggregate fruits. These fruits are fleshy and contain multiple small seeds, often turning a deep red or brownish color when mature. The overall vegetative structure is designed for a climbing habit within shaded, humid environments.

What are the pharmacological activities of Schisandra neglecta?

This plant has neuroprotective pharmacological activities and provides protective activities against corticosterone-induced apoptosis. These beneficial effects are primarily driven by the presence of unique lignans found within its chemical structure. Research indicates that these compounds help mitigate cellular damage caused by chronic stress hormones in the brain. By reducing programmed cell death, the extract maintains the structural integrity of neural tissues. Consequently, the plant serves as a significant subject for studying neuroprotection in stress-related models.

As discussed in a study, Schisdilactones L-S (2-8) and U (11) were identified from the leaves and stems of Schisandra neglecta A. C. Smith. These compounds exhibit moderate protective activities against corticosterone-induced apoptosis of PC12 cells when used at 20 μM. The observed cell viability during these activities was found to be in the range of 62.95-66.97%. These findings pertain to a series of 11 previously unreported 16,17-secopreschisanartane-type schinortriterpenoids known as Schisdilactones K-U (1-11).
