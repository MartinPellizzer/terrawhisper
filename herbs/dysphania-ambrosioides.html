Dysphania ambrosioides

What's the taxonomical classification of Dysphania ambrosioides?

Dysphania ambrosioides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is organized under the order Caryophyllales. This plant is a member of the family Amaranthaceae, falling specifically within the genus Dysphania, and is identified by the species name ambrosioides.

Dysphania ambrosioides contains medicinal properties primarily concentrated in its whole plant, including the leaves, stems, and roots. What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat skin infections, wounds, diarrhea, and inflammatory conditions. Its leaves and stems are often crushed to form a paste for topical application on sores or rashes. In traditional medicine, decoctions made from the plant are consumed to alleviate digestive distress and abdominal pain. The plant also possesses properties that help reduce swelling and soothe minor burns. These medicinal applications vary depending on the specific preparation method used by local practitioners.

What are the herbal preparations of Dysphania ambrosioides?

Dysphania ambrosioides is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical poultice for medicinal use. The dried leaves and stems are often boiled in water to create a strong liquid tea used for internal consumption. This aqueous extract can also be applied externally to soothe skin irritations or wounds. Some practitioners use the crushed fresh plant material to make a direct poultice for localized healing. These different methods allow the plant's bioactive compounds to be delivered according to the specific ailment being treated.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Dysphania ambrosioides can cause allergic skin reactions, respiratory distress, or gastrointestinal irritation if consumed or touched. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, its proximity to other members of the Asteraceae family suggests a risk of contact dermatitis for sensitive individuals. Some people may experience itching, redness, or swelling upon direct contact with the plant's foliage. Ingestion of various parts of the plant could potentially lead to stomach upset or nausea in certain populations. It is generally advised to avoid direct contact or consumption to prevent these unpredictable physiological responses.
