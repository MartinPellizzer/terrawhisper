Botanical Identification of Sweetroot (Glycyrrhiza lepidota)

To identify Sweetroot (Glycyrrhiza lepidota) accurately, examine its morphological characteristics, taxonomic classification, and diagnostic features.

Scientific Classification

Sweetroot, or Glycyrrhiza lepidota, belongs to the kingdom Plantae, division Magnoliophyta, class Fabales, order Fabales, family Fabaceae, genus Glycyrrhiza, and species Glycyrrhiza lepidota. This classification places it within the legume family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and medicinal uses.

Botanical Nomenclature and Synonyms

The scientific name of Sweetroot is Glycyrrhiza lepidota, first described by the botanist Carl Friedrich von Miquel in 1858. It has several synonyms, including Glycyrrhiza glabra var. lepidota and Purpurea lepidota. Common names include Sweetroot, Sweet Cicely, and Licorice Root, while regional and historical names vary across cultures and regions.

Morphological Characteristics

Sweetroot (Glycyrrhiza lepidota) is a perennial herb with a taproot system that can grow up to 1 meter deep. It has upright, branching stems that are hairy and often reddish-brown. Its leaves are alternate, compound, with 3-5 pairs of elliptic leaflets. The inflorescence is a raceme of small, pale pink flowers. The fruit is a legume containing several seeds.

Diagnostic Identification Features

Sweetroot can be distinguished by its thick, fleshy roots with a yellowish-brown exterior and a sweet, licorice-like scent. It has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with smooth edges and small, yellow flowers in clusters. It is often confused with licorice but has a more robust root system and a distinct, aromatic odor.

Natural Habitat and Geographic Distribution

Sweetroot (Glycyrrhiza lepidota) is native to the western United States, particularly California and Oregon. It thrives in temperate climates with mild, wet winters and dry summers. It prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is commonly found at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Phenology and Growth Cycle

Sweetroot blooms from June to August, producing pink to purple flowers. Seed formation occurs in late summer to early fall. For identification purposes, the plant is typically harvested in late spring to early summer when the roots are most developed and distinguishable.

Phylogenetic Relationships

Sweetroot (Glycyrrhiza lepidota) is closely related to other members of the genus Glycyrrhiza, such as licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra). It shares family traits with the legume family, Fabaceae, including compound leaves and pod fruits. Its phylogenetic position highlights its evolutionary ties to other sweetroot species and related legumes.
