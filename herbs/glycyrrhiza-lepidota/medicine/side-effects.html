10 Possible Side Effects Of Glycyrrhiza Lepidota

Glycyrrhiza lepidota has some side effects when used improperly, such as high blood pressure, water retention, and increased potassium levels.

These side effects are often caused by the herb's active compound, glycyrrhizin, which can disrupt the body's natural balance. For people who already have high blood pressure, these side effects can worsen their condition, leading to cardiovascular problems and increased risk of stroke.

In extreme cases, it can even cause heart failure.

1. Increases blood pressure

Glycyrrhiza lepidota increases blood pressure due to its ability to convert cortisol into a more potent form in the body.

This can lead to an imbalance of sodium and potassium levels, causing blood vessels to constrict and blood pressure to rise.

Additionally, glycyrrhizin, a compound found in Glycyrrhiza lepidota, can also contribute to increased blood pressure by activating the body's renin-angiotensin system, which regulates blood pressure.

2. Causes water retention

Glycyrrhiza lepidota causes water retention due to its ability to increase levels of aldosterone, a hormone that regulates electrolyte and water balance in the body.

This can lead to an imbalance of sodium and potassium, resulting in the accumulation of excess fluid in tissues and organs, causing swelling and bloating.

The saponins present in Glycyrrhiza lepidota also contribute to this effect by increasing blood pressure, further exacerbating water retention.

3. Affects electrolyte balance

Glycyrrhiza lepidota affects electrolyte balance.

The compound glycyrrhetic acid present in this herb can increase the levels of aldosterone, a hormone that regulates potassium and sodium balance in the body. This can lead to an imbalance of these essential minerals, causing symptoms such as muscle weakness, fatigue, and abnormal heart rhythms.

Additionally, the sweet-tasting saponins found in licorice root can also disrupt the body's natural electrolyte balance by altering the way it absorbs and excretes potassium and sodium.

4. Disrupts kidney function

Glycyrrhiza lepidota disrupts kidney function by altering the body's natural balance of electrolytes and hormones.

The compound glycyrrhetic acid, present in licorice, can increase aldosterone production, leading to an imbalance of potassium, sodium, and water levels in the body.

This disruption can cause the kidneys to retain excess fluid, increasing blood pressure and potentially straining kidney function over time.

5. Raises cortisol levels

Glycyrrhiza lepidota raises cortisol levels due to its ability to inhibit the action of 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase type 2 enzyme, which converts cortisol into a less active form.

This inhibition allows cortisol levels to remain elevated, potentially leading to adverse effects such as weight gain, insomnia, and anxiety.

Additionally, long-term use can lead to adrenal insufficiency in susceptible individuals.

6. Induces vomiting

Glycyrrhiza lepidota induces vomiting in some individuals.

This is due to its high content of glycyrrhetic acid, which can stimulate the vagus nerve and cause nausea and emesis.

Additionally, the herb's ability to relax the muscles of the digestive tract can lead to a slowing of gastric emptying, further contributing to the development of nausea and vomiting.

7. Causes diarrhea

Glycyrrhiza lepidota causes diarrhea due to its high concentration of glycyrrhizin, a compound that can alter the balance of gut bacteria and impair intestinal motility.

Additionally, licorice root's ability to inhibit the activity of enzymes involved in digestion can disrupt the normal functioning of the gastrointestinal system, leading to loose stools or diarrhea in some individuals.

8. Reduces potassium levels

Glycyrrhiza lepidota reduces potassium levels due to its ability to inhibit the activity of an enzyme that helps regulate potassium in the body.

This inhibition leads to a decrease in potassium levels, which can cause symptoms such as muscle weakness, fatigue, and heart palpitations.

Additionally, Glycyrrhiza lepidota's anti-inflammatory properties may also contribute to this side effect by interfering with the body's natural potassium balance.

9. Increases risk of bleeding

Glycyrrhiza lepidota increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to decrease blood clotting factors and platelet function.

The compound glycyrrhetinic acid, a key constituent of Glycyrrhiza lepidota, inhibits the production of thromboxane A2, a potent vasoconstrictor that plays a crucial role in hemostasis.

This inhibition can lead to reduced blood clotting and increased bleeding tendency, particularly in individuals who already have bleeding disorders or are taking anticoagulant medications.

10. Produces allergic reactions

Glycyrrhiza lepidota produces allergic reactions.

The compound glycyrrhetic acid in licorice can cause an allergic response in some individuals, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and swelling. Additionally, the flavonoids present in licorice can trigger an allergic reaction, releasing histamine into the bloodstream and causing blood vessels to dilate. This reaction can lead to symptoms like flushing, rapid heartbeat, and difficulty breathing.

The allergenic compounds in licorice may also cross-react with other plant-based substances, exacerbating the allergic response.
