Colysis digitata

What's the taxonomical classification of Colysis digitata?

Colysis digitata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Polypodiinae. Following the hierarchical structure of its classification, it falls under the order Polypodiales and the family Polypodiaceae. Ultimately, this specific plant is identified by its genus, Colysis, and its unique species designation, digitata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Colysis digitata has a rhizomatous growth habit featuring pinnate fronds that reach up to 40 centimeters in length with deeply divided, finger-like segments. The stipe is typically dark brown or black and possesses a characteristic scaly texture near the base. Each pinna is composed of multiple narrow, linear lobes that give the foliage its distinct digitated appearance. The sori are located on the underside of the pinnules and are arranged in a specific pattern characteristic of the genus. These morphological features allow the fern to thrive in moist, shaded forest environments.
