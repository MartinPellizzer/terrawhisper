What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex georgei?

Ilex georgei belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Aquifoliales and the family Aquifoliaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Ilex, and its unique species designation, georgei.

    This plant is used to treat various skin ailments, including inflammatory conditions, wounds, and minor infections. Local traditional practices often utilize extracts from the plant to promote the healing of cutaneous lesions.     What are the herbal preparations of Ilex georgei?

    Ilex georgei is prepared as a decoction or an infusion using the leaves and stems of the plant to extract its medicinal compounds. The leaves are typically steeped in hot water to create a tea that may be consumed for its purported properties. Some practitioners may also process the plant material into a concentrated tincture by soaking it in alcohol. This liquid extract allows for a more stable shelf life and easier dosing of the plant's constituents. Additionally, topical preparations like poultices can be fashioned from crushed plant matter to treat external ailments.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Ilex georgei can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential toxicity if any part of the plant is ingested. The sharp, spiny leaves are known to puncture the skin, often leading to localized redness or minor wounds upon contact. If the berries or foliage are eaten, individuals may experience nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, the plant's physical structure poses a direct mechanical risk to the eyes and mucous membranes. It is best to handle this species with gloves to avoid these unpleasant reactions.
