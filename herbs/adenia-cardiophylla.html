Adenia cardiophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Adenia cardiophylla?

Adenia cardiophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls within the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Passifloraceae, specifically situated within the genus Adenia, and is identified by its unique species name, cardiophylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenia cardiophylla has a woody, succulent stem characterized by a large, tuberous caudex that serves as a water storage organ.

The leaves are distinctly cordate in shape, meaning they possess a heart-like structure with a deep notch at the apex. These leaves are typically arranged in a way that provides a lush appearance to the climbing or scrambling vine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the foliage.

Its overall morphology is adapted for survival in seasonally dry environments through its specialized storage tissues.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Adenia cardiophylla can cause severe skin irritation, chemical burns, and localized tissue death if the plant's sap comes into contact with human skin or mucous membranes. The milky latex produced by this species contains complex alkaloids and potentially toxic compounds that act as potent irritants. Exposure to these substances often results in redness, swelling, and intense itching upon contact. If accidentally ingested, the plant may cause significant gastrointestinal distress or systemic toxicity. Because the exact chemical composition of its toxins is not fully documented, any contact should be treated with immediate caution.
