Adenia cardiophylla

What's the taxonomical classification of Adenia cardiophylla?

Adenia cardiophylla belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls within the order Malpighiales. This plant is a member of the family Passifloraceae, specifically situated within the genus Adenia, and is identified by its unique species name, cardiophylla.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Adenia cardiophylla has a woody, succulent stem characterized by a large, tuberous caudex that serves as a water storage organ. The leaves are distinctly cordate in shape, meaning they possess a heart-like structure with a deep notch at the apex. These leaves are typically arranged in a way that provides a lush appearance to the climbing or scrambling vine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the foliage. How is this plant cultivated?
