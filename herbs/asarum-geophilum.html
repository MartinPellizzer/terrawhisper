Asarum geophilum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asarum geophilum has kidney-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically cordate or reniform in form with entire margins and a somewhat leathery texture. The plant is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its creeping rhizomes that spread along the forest floor. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often appearing near the ground to facilitate pollination by ground-dwelling insects. The foliage is arranged in a basal rosette, which helps the plant shade its own root system in moist environments. These morphological traits collectively allow the species to thrive in the shaded understory of its native habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Asarum geophilum can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, liver toxicity, and potential neurological impairments if ingested. These adverse effects primarily stem from the presence of aristolochic acids, which are known to be highly nephrotoxic and carcinogenic. Consumption of plants within this genus may lead to kidney damage or even fatal outcomes due to these toxic compounds. Individuals should avoid any internal use of this species to prevent permanent organ damage. Careful identification is necessary because many species in this group share similar dangerous chemical profiles.
