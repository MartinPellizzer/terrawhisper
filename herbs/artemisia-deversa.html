Artemisia deversa

What's the taxonomical classification of Artemisia deversa?

Artemisia deversa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Asterales and is a member of the family Asteraceae. Ultimately, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Artemisia, and its unique species designation, deversa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Artemisia deversa has woody, spreading stems with small, grayish-green leaves that are typically deeply lobed or pinnatifid. The foliage often exhibits a fine, tomentose texture due to a dense covering of minute hairs. These leaves are arranged alternately along the branches and frequently feature silver or glaucous tones. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flower heads that appear in terminal clusters during its blooming season. Its overall growth habit is shrubby and compact, reflecting its adaptation to specific environmental conditions.
