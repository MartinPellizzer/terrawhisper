What is Primula × brueggeri?

Primula × brueggeri is an interspecific hybrid of the genus Primula, belonging to the family Primulaceae.

It has a compact growth habit, typically reaching 15-30 cm in height, with leaves that are elliptical to lanceolate in shape and have a glabrous or slightly hairy surface. The leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant produces white or pink flowers with four or five petals, which are arranged in a terminal cluster. The flowers are radially symmetrical and have a five-lobed calyx and a corolla with a slightly crenate margin.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer.
