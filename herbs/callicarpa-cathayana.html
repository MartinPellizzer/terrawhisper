Callicarpa cathayana

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa cathayana?

Callicarpa cathayana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Lamiales. Furthermore, the plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, situated within the genus Callicarpa, and is specifically identified by the species name cathayana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Callicarpa cathayana contains various bioactive compounds including triterpenoids, flavonoids, and polyphenols that exhibit significant pharmacological activities. The plant's leaves and stems are particularly rich in ursolic acid and oleanolic acid, which contribute to its anti-inflammatory properties. These chemical constituents also demonstrate potent antioxidant and antimicrobial effects in various biological studies. Additionally, certain extracts from the plant have shown potential in regulating blood sugar levels and protecting against oxidative stress. This complex chemical profile makes the species a subject of interest for developing new therapeutic agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Callicarpa cathayana?

Callicarpa cathayana has medicinal properties that include anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, and antioxidant effects primarily utilized to treat skin conditions and fever. The plant contains bioactive compounds like flavonoids and triterpenoids that help reduce swelling and soothe irritation. When applied topically, extracts from the leaves and stems can assist in healing wounds and managing dermatological infections. Some traditional practices also use decoctions of the plant to alleviate internal ailments such as gastrointestinal distress. These diverse chemical constituents allow the plant to act as a natural remedy for various physical stressors.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin ailments, and digestive disorders. Practitioners of traditional medicine often apply its extracts to soothe skin irritations and minor wounds. It is also utilized to alleviate symptoms associated with respiratory issues and certain types of fever. Some preparations are consumed to help manage abdominal pain or gastrointestinal distress. The plant's bioactive compounds are believed to contribute to these therapeutic effects.

What are the herbal preparations of Callicarpa cathayana?

Callicarpa cathayana is prepared as a decoction, a topical paste, or an infused liquid depending on the intended therapeutic application. The dried leaves and stems are most commonly boiled in water to create a medicinal tea for internal use. For skin conditions, the plant material is often ground into a fine powder and mixed with water or oil to form a soothing external paste. Some traditional practitioners also utilize alcohol-based tinctures to extract the active compounds for long-term storage. These diverse preparation methods allow for the versatile administration of the plant's phytochemical properties.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Callicarpa cathayana can cause localized skin irritation, allergic dermatitis, or gastrointestinal distress if ingested in significant amounts. While many species in this genus are used traditionally, direct contact with the leaves or stems may trigger redness and itching in sensitive individuals. Consumption of unverified quantities might lead to nausea or stomach discomfort due to various secondary metabolites present in the plant. Detailed toxicological studies on this specific species are limited, so caution is necessary when handling it. Always consult a medical professional if an adverse reaction occurs after exposure to the plant.
