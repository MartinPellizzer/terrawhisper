Callicarpa cathayana

What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa cathayana?

Callicarpa cathayana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Lamiales. Furthermore, the plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, situated within the genus Callicarpa, and is specifically identified by the species name cathayana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa cathayana has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and a woody, deciduous stem structure. The foliage typically displays a bright green color and can reach several inches in length depending on the specimen's maturity. Small, tubular flowers emerge in dense cymes from the leaf axils during the blooming season. These blossoms eventually give way to clusters of small, spherical drupes that turn a vibrant purple or magenta when ripe. The overall architecture of the shrub is characterized by an upright or spreading growth habit.

What are the pharmacological activities of Callicarpa cathayana?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, antilipid peroxidation, antioxidant, and liver protecting properties. Its chemical constituents work to neutralize free radicals, which directly contributes to its potent antioxidant capabilities. By inhibiting lipid peroxidation, the plant helps maintain cellular membrane integrity against oxidative stress. These combined mechanisms also allow it to reduce inflammation within various biological systems. Furthermore, its ability to mitigate oxidative damage plays a crucial role in its effectiveness for liver protecting applications.

According to a study published by "Chinese journal of natural medicines", phytochemical investigation of the leaves and twigs of Callicarpa cathayana led to the isolation of six new clerodane diterpenoids named cathayanalactones A-F (1-6) along with seven analogues (7-13). These compounds were studied for their potential anti-inflammatory activity. Specifically, compounds 1, 2, 3, 7 and 11 showed inhibitory activities on lipopolysaccharide-induced nitric oxide production in RAW264.7 cells. The structures of these isolated substances were established through extensive NMR analyses and experimental and calculated ECD spectra analyses.
