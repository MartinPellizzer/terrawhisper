What's the taxonomical classification of Callicarpa cathayana?

Callicarpa cathayana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is categorized under the order Lamiales. Furthermore, the plant is a member of the family Lamiaceae, situated within the genus Callicarpa, and is specifically identified by the species name cathayana.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains cathayanalactones A-F and various clerodane diterpenoids which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These specific chemical constituents are studied for their potential biological activities and pharmacological properties. The cathayanalactones represent a unique group of molecules that contribute to the plant's complex chemical profile. Additionally, the presence of clerodane diterpenoids provides a diverse range of structural frameworks for medicinal research. Together, these compounds define the therapeutic potential inherent in the plant's secondary metabolites.

As discussed in a study, the leaves and twigs of Callicarpa cathayana contain medicinal compounds including six new clerodane diterpenoids named cathayanalactones A-F (1-6) and seven analogues (7-13). These specific substances were isolated through phytochemical investigation. Among these, compounds 1, 2, 3, 7 and 11 demonstrated inhibitory activities on lipopolysaccharide-induced nitric oxide production in RAW264.7 cells. The structures of these compounds were established using extensive NMR analyses and both experimental and calculated ECD spectra analyses.
