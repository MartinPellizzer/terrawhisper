Dendrobium parishii

What's the taxonomical classification of Dendrobium parishii?

Dendrobium parishii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the family Orchidaceae, specifically situated within the genus Dendrobium, and is identified by the species name parishii.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Dendrobium parishii can cause skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, or localized swelling if the plant's sap comes into direct contact with sensitive human tissue. While specific toxicological studies on this exact species are limited, many members of the Orchidaceae family contain alkaloids that may trigger hypersensitivity in certain individuals. Symptoms typically manifest as redness, itching, or a mild burning sensation at the site of exposure. It is advisable to avoid touching the plant without gloves to prevent these unpredictable dermatological reactions. Individuals with known sensitivities to tropical flora should exercise extra caution when handling this specimen.
