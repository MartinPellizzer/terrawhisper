Pinus quadrifolia, commonly known as Chir Pine or Four-leaved Pine, is a species of evergreen tree that belongs to the pine family (Pinaceae).

The bioactive constituents of Pinus quadrifolia include sesquiterpenes, diterpenes, and phenolic compounds, which are responsible for its therapeutic actions such as anti-arthritic, anti-diabetic, and anti-cancer properties.

The therapeutic actions of Pinus quadrifolia also include its ability to improve cardiovascular health, enhance cognitive function, and boost the immune system.

Possible Side Effects of Pinus quadrifolia

Pinus quadrifolia can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and difficulty breathing in some individuals.

Additionally, improper use of Pinus quadrifolia may interact with certain medications, including blood thinners, and exacerbate conditions like high blood pressure and diabetes.

In rare cases, excessive consumption of the plant may cause more severe side effects, including liver damage and kidney problems.
