Streptopus simplex

What's the taxonomical classification of Streptopus simplex?

Streptopus simplex belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its progression through the order Liliales, it is placed in the family Liliaceae. Finally, its taxonomic identity is completed within the genus Streptopus, specifically as the species simplex.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Streptopus simplex has slender, arching stems that typically reach heights of ten to thirty centimeters and bear single, terminal, bell-shaped flowers.

The leaves are arranged alternately along the stem and consist of two to four elliptical, bright green blades with parallel venation. Each flower is suspended from a delicate pedicel and features six tepals that are often tinged with pink or reddish hues. Following pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy berries that transition from green to a deep red or purplish color when ripe.

The underground portion of the plant consists of a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread slowly through forest floor habitats.

Ingesting parts of the plant without proper identification or preparation could lead to nausea or stomach upset.
