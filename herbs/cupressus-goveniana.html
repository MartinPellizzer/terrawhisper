Cupressus goveniana

What's the taxonomical classification of Cupressus goveniana?

Cupressus govenianana belongs to the kingdom Plantae, placing it within the broad group of all multicellular plants. It is further classified under the phylum Streptophyta and the class Equisetopsida, moving it closer to the lineage of vascular plants. Within the subclass Pinidae and the order Pinales, its evolutionary lineage becomes more distinct, eventually leading to its placement in the family Cupressaceae. As a member of the genus Cupressus, it is specifically identified at the species level as govenianana, completing its unique taxonomic profile.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cupressus goveniana has a pyramidal growth habit characterized by a dense, dark green foliage of scale-like leaves that are tightly appressed to the small, cylindrical branchlets. The branches are often spreading or slightly drooping, creating a compact and conical silhouette. Its woody stems are covered in a rough, fissured bark that provides structural support for the heavy canopy. The reproductive structures consist of small, globose cones that transition from green to woody brown as they mature. These morphological traits allow the plant to thrive in its specific environmental niche.
