What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tripterospermum chinense has an erect, herbaceous habit characterized by slender, green stems and leaves that are typically opposite, lanceolate to ovate, and possess entire margins. The most distinctive morphological feature is the fruit, which is a capsule containing seeds that possess three prominent wing-like appendages. These wings facilitate wind dispersal, a key evolutionary adaptation for the species' reproductive success.
