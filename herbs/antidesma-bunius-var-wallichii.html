Indian Gooseberry (Antidesma Bunius Var. Wallichii)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Antidesma bunius var. wallichii.

Indian gooseberry, scientifically known as Antidesma bunius var. wallichii, is a medicinal herb native to the Indian subcontinent, revered for its rich nutritional profile and therapeutic properties. This plant is often referred to as a superfood due to its high vitamin C content, which acts as a powerful antioxidant and immune booster. Traditionally, it has been used in Ayurvedic medicine to treat ailments such as fever, inflammation, and digestive disorders, while also being incorporated into local cuisines as a tangy spice. In modern wellness, it is valued for its potential to support skin health, reduce oxidative stress, and enhance overall vitality. One of its unique features is the presence of rare bioactive compounds that contribute to its adaptogenic properties, making it a valuable herb in both traditional and contemporary health practices.
