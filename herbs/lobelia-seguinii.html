Lobelia seguinii

What's the taxonomical classification of Lobelia seguinii?

Lobelia seguinii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Asterales and the family Campanulaceae, eventually leading to its placement within the genus Lobelia. Ultimately, the specific identity of this plant is defined by its species designation, seguinii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lobelia seguinii has an erect, herbaceous stem that typically reaches a height of 20 to 50 centimeters and features opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The leaves are arranged in a decussate pattern, displaying prominent venation and a smooth or slightly pubescent surface texture. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, characterized by a zygomorphic structure and a distinct two-lipped corolla. These blooms often exhibit a blue or pale violet coloration, which is typical for many species within the Lobeliaceae family. The plant also develops small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, lightweight seeds for dispersal.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Lobelia seguinii targets nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (nAChRs) through its primary bioactive alkaloids, most notably lobeline. By binding to these specific protein receptors in the nervous system, the plant's compounds can modulate neurotransmitter release and influence muscle contraction. These interactions facilitate the plant's known pharmacological effects on the respiratory and gastrointestinal systems. Additionally, various secondary metabolites may interact with various enzyme systems and inflammatory signaling pathways to produce its therapeutic properties. This complex interplay with cellular proteins underlies its traditional use in treating asthma and digestive ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lobelia seguinii?

Lobelia seguinii has emetic, expectorant, diaphoretic, antispasmodic, and bitter therapeutic actions used to treat various respiratory and digestive ailments. The plant acts as a potent emetic when administered in higher doses to induce vomiting for detoxification. Its expectorant properties help loosen mucus in the airways, while its antispasmodic effects relax smooth muscles to soothe coughing fits. Additionally, it functions as a diaphoretic to promote sweating and assist in regulating body temperature. The bitter profile of the herb stimulates digestive secretions and improves appetite.
