Karela (Momordica Charantia Subsp. Macroloba)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Momordica charantia subsp. macroloba.

Karela, scientifically known as Momordica charantia subsp. macroloba, is a medicinal herb widely used in traditional and modern wellness practices. It is renowned for its unique bitter flavor and contains bioactive compounds such as charantin and momordicin, which contribute to its therapeutic properties. Karela has been traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine to manage diabetes, improve digestion, and support liver health. In modern wellness, it is often consumed as a supplement or in juice form to regulate blood sugar levels and promote metabolic health. Its historical significance dates back centuries, with ancient texts mentioning its use for treating various ailments, making it a valuable plant in both traditional and contemporary herbal medicine.
