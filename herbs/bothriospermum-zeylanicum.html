What's the taxonomical classification of Bothriospermum zeylanicum?

Bothriospermum zeylanicum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is positioned in the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Boraginales and the family Boraginaceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its genus, Bothriospermum, and its specific species designation, zeylanicum.

What are the herbal preparations of Bothriospermum zeylanicum?

Bothriospermum zeylanicum is prepared as decoctions, infusions, and pastes for various therapeutic applications. The roots and leaves are frequently boiled in water to create a liquid extract used in traditional medicine. These decoctions are often administered orally to address internal ailments. In some cases, the plant material is ground into a fine powder and mixed with water or oils to form a topical paste. This paste is applied directly to the skin to treat external inflammations or wounds.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Bothriospermum zeylanicum can cause skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and potential allergic reactions if ingested or applied topically without proper preparation. While parts of the plant are used in traditional medicine, the concentration of bioactive compounds may trigger adverse responses in sensitive individuals. Ingesting unrefined extracts can lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain due to the presence of unidentified alkaloids or saponins. Topical application might result in contact dermatitis or localized redness on the skin. Caution is necessary because clinical data regarding its long-term toxicity in humans remains limited.
