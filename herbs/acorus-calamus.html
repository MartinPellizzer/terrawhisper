Acorus Calamus: Medicinal Uses And Benefits

Acorus calamus, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

The herb is known for its cognitive-enhancing properties, aiding in memory and mental clarity, and it also supports digestive health.

Therapeutically, Acorus calamus exhibits anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and nervine actions, while its bioactive constituents include alkaloids, volatile oils, and flavonoids.
