Sweet Flag

Acorus calamus

Sweet Flag (Acorus calamus) is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas, known for its aromatic rhizomes that have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Botanical Identification

Acorus calamus, commonly known as sweet flag, belongs to the family Araceae. It is a perennial herb native to wetlands in Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves, a rhizomatous root system, and small greenish flowers. It is also known as calamus or sweet reed in various regional names.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Liliopsida
  • Order: Alismatales
  • Family: Araceae
  • Genus: Acorus
  • Species: Acorus calamus

Sweet Flag Identification.

Active Compounds

Sweet Flag contains essential oil, alkaloids, and flavonoids as its primary active compounds. These components work together to provide its therapeutic effects, with the essential oil contributing to its aromatic and anti-inflammatory properties. Alkaloids and flavonoids support its role in traditional medicine for digestive and respiratory health.

  • Essential oil
  • Alkaloid
  • Flavonoid

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for digestive issues and respiratory ailments.

Essential oil: Used for respiratory conditions and as a stimulant.

Tincture: Used for nervous system disorders and digestive issues.

Safety Profile

Sweet Flag (Acorus calamus) may cause gastrointestinal upset and allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated in pregnancy and should be used with caution in individuals with liver or kidney disease. Long-term use may lead to toxicity, so it is important to follow recommended dosages and durations.
