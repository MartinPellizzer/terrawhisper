Gerbera anandria

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Gerbera anaandria has a rosette-forming growth habit characterized by long-petioled leaves that are deeply lobed and feature serrated margins. The foliage is typically dark green and displays a textured surface due to fine pubescence. From the center of this basal rosette, erect flowering stems emerge to support large, composite flower heads. These inflorescences consist of numerous ray florets surrounding a central disc of smaller tubular florets. The overall structure is designed to maximize surface area for both light absorption and pollinator attraction.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

There is also a possibility of respiratory irritation for those with high sensitivities to its pollen. Careful handling is recommended to avoid these potential adverse reactions.
