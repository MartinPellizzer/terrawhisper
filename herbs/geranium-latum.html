Side Effects of geranium latum

Geranium latum induces allergic reactions in some individuals, manifesting as sneezing, runny nose, and even difficulty breathing in severe cases.

The herb can also cause skin irritation, leading to redness, itching, and a burning sensation upon contact with the skin. Additionally, it may induce skin rash, which can appear as raised, itchy bumps or patches on the skin.

Exposure to Geranium latum may result in throat irritation, causing a dry, scratchy feeling or even coughing. Furthermore, it can lead to stomach upset and nausea, particularly when ingested, as it contains compounds that may irritate the gastrointestinal tract. These side effects highlight the importance of caution when handling or using Geranium latum, especially for individuals with known allergies or sensitive skin.

It is advisable to consult a healthcare professional before using this herb, particularly for therapeutic purposes, to minimize the risk of adverse reactions.
