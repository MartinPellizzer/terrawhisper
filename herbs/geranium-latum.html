Medicinal Constituents of geranium latum

Geranium latum cardiac glycosides are a class of secondary metabolites that exhibit potent cardiovascular effects, primarily by inhibiting the sodium-potassium ATPase enzyme, thereby increasing intracellular sodium and calcium levels, which strengthen cardiac muscle contractions.

Geranium latum contains geraniol, a monoterpenoid alcohol known for its antimicrobial, antifungal, and antioxidant properties, which contributes to the plant's therapeutic potential in treating infections and inflammatory conditions.

The flavonoids present in Geranium latum, such as quercetin and kaempferol, are polyphenolic compounds with strong antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress in the body.

Quinones in Geranium latum are oxygenated derivatives of benzoquinone, which exhibit antitumor, antibacterial, and antiviral properties, making them valuable in the development of pharmaceutical agents.

Medicinal Parts of geranium latum

Geranium latum leaf and flower are the primary medicinal parts of this herb, known for their astringent, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial properties.

The leaves are typically harvested during the summer months, while the flowers are collected when fully open to ensure maximum potency.

The main herbal preparations made from these parts include tinctures, decoctions, and infusions, which are used to support digestive health, reduce inflammation, and treat skin conditions.

These preparations are valued for their ability to promote healing and balance within the body.

Herbal Preparations of geranium latum

Geranium latum teas are commonly prepared by steeping the dried leaves and flowers in hot water, and they are often used for their calming and digestive properties, with a mild, earthy flavor that is appreciated in herbal medicine.

Geranium latum decoctions involve simmering the tougher parts of the plant, such as the roots and stems, for a longer period to extract more potent compounds, making them effective for addressing respiratory and skin conditions.

Geranium latum tinctures are made by soaking the plant material in alcohol or glycerin, allowing for a concentrated form that is used topically or internally for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Geranium latum poultices involve crushing fresh plant material and applying it directly to the skin, often used to reduce swelling, soothe insect bites, and treat minor wounds due to its astringent and cooling properties.
