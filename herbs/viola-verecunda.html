Viola verecunda

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Viola verecunda has a perennial growth habit characterized by basal leaves that are reniform or cordate in shape with crenate margins and a distinctively long petiole. The foliage typically presents a bright green color and grows in a rosette formation close to the soil surface. Its reproductive structures consist of small, zygomorphic flowers that emerge on slender peduncles from the base of the plant. The corolla features five petals, often displaying subtle coloration patterns typical of the Viola genus. These flowers are supported by a delicate stem structure that allows them to be positioned effectively for pollinators.

      Viola verecunda is prepared primarily as a decoction or an infusion of its aerial parts to utilize its medicinal properties.
