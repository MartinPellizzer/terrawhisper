What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemsleya graciliflora has large, deeply pinnatifid or bipinnatifid leaves characterized by slender, delicate segments and a distinctively thin texture. The plant typically exhibits a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread across the forest floor. Its foliage often features a dark green color with a somewhat translucent appearance when held against light. Small, inconspicuous flowers are borne on specialized stalks, reflecting its specialized reproductive structure. The overall habit is that of a low-growing fern suited for shaded, moist environments.
