What's the taxonomical classification of Carpesium divaricatum?

Carpesium divaricatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Asterales and falls within the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Carpesium and the species name divaricatum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Carpesium divaricatum?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antiproliferative, antipyretic, and cytotoxic pharmacological activities. These therapeutic properties suggest that the chemical constituents of Carpesium divaricatum can effectively manage pain and reduce systemic inflammation. Furthermore, its antipyretic nature allows it to function as an agent for reducing fever in biological systems. The plant also demonstrates antiproliferative and cytotoxic effects, which indicate a potential role in inhibiting the growth of abnormal or cancerous cells. These diverse biological actions highlight the significant medicinal potential of the species for future drug development.

According to a study published by "Biochemical pharmacology", Carpesium divaricatum has been used in Korean traditional herbal medicine for its antipyretic, analgesic, vermifugic, and anti-inflammatory properties. A recently identified sesquiterpene lactone from the plant, 2beta,5-epoxy-5,10-dihydroxy-6alpha-angeloyloxy-9beta-isobutyloxy-germacran-8alpha,12-olide (C-1), decreases NO production in LPS/IFN-gamma-stimulated RAW 264.7 cells in a concentration-dependent manner. This compound C-1 demonstrated an IC50 of approximately 2.16 microM in suppressing NO production. The substance inhibits iNOS gene expression by decreasing iNOS protein and mRNA. These anti-inflammatory effects are linked to the inhibition of NF-kappaB activation through the stabilization of the NF-kappaB/I-kappaB complex.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds cardvin A, cardvin B, cardvin C, cardvin D, and germacrane-type sesquiterpene lactones. These specific bioactive constituents are primary components identified within the chemical profile of Carpesium divaricatum. The cardvin series of compounds represents a distinct group of molecules isolated from its tissues. Additionally, the presence of germacrane-type sesquiterpene lactones contributes to the plant's complex pharmacological properties. These diverse chemical elements are often studied for their potential therapeutic applications in medicinal research.

According to a study published by "Journal of natural products", four new sesquiterpenes of the germacranolide type were isolated from the aerial parts of Carpesium divaricatum. These four new cytotoxic germacranolides are named cardivin A (1), B (2), C (3), and D (4). The structures of compounds 1, 2, 3, and 4 were elucidated using spectroscopic techniques. These 4 compounds demonstrated cytotoxicity toward 5 different human tumor cells. The specific cell lines affected included A-549 (nonsmall cell lung), SK-OV-3 (ovary), SK-MEL-2 (skin), XF-498 (central nervous system), and HCT-15 (colon).

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating cancer, inflammation, pain, parasitic worms, and tumors. Its medicinal properties are often harnessed to reduce swelling and soothe various forms of bodily discomfort. Traditional applications include using its extracts to combat the growth of abnormal cells and tumors. Additionally, it serves as an effective remedy for eliminating parasitic worms from the digestive system. These diverse therapeutic uses demonstrate the significant pharmacological potential of the species.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", Carpesium divaricatum Sieb. & Zucc. is a traditional medicinal plant used as an inflammation-relieving remedy. This plant is a rich source of terpenoids, with at least 40 germacrane-type sesquiterpene lactones from 4 different structural groups being isolated. The biological activity of its compounds most frequently involves cytotoxicity against cancer cells in vitro. To assess anti-inflammatory effects, the study measured the release of 4 proinflammatory cytokines/chemokines, specifically IL-1β, IL-8, TNF-α, and CCL2. In sub-cytotoxic concentrations of 0.5-2.5 µM, the studied compounds significantly decreased this cytokine/chemokine release by lipopolysaccharide-stimulated human leukocytes.
