Carpesium divaricatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Carpesium and the species name divaricatum.

    What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

    This plant contains 11-methoxymethylgermacranolides, 12-oxo-phytodienoic acid (12-OPDA), chlorogenic acids, germacrane-type sesquiterpene lactones, and thymol derivatives. These specific medicinal compounds provide the plant with a diverse range of biological activities. The germacrane-type sesquiterpene lactones and 11-methoxymethylgermacranolides often contribute to anti-inflammatory properties. Additionally, the presence of chlorogenic acids offers significant antioxidant benefits to the organism. The inclusion of thymol derivatives and 12-oxo-phytodienoic acid further enhances its complex chemical profile.

    As discussed in a study, eight new 11-methoxymethylgermacranolides labeled 1-8 were isolated from the ethanol extract of the whole plant of Carpesium divaricatum. The absolute configuration of compound 1 was established by electronic circular dichroism spectrum and X-ray crystallographic analysis, while the stereochemistry of compounds 2-8 was determined using similar ECD data. Additionally, the absolute configurations of compounds 5 and 7 were further confirmed through quantum chemical electronic circular dichroism calculations. Regarding medicinal properties, compound 4 exhibited weak cytotoxicity against MCF-7 cells. Furthermore, compound 8 could potently decrease PGE2 productions in LPS-induced RAW 264.7 cells.
