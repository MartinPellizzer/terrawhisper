What's the taxonomical classification of Carpesium divaricatum?

Carpesium divaricatum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is further categorized under the order Asterales and falls within the family Asteraceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Carpesium and the species name divaricatum.

What are the pharmacological activities of Carpesium divaricatum?

This plant has analgesic, anti-inflammatory, antiproliferative, antipyretic, and vermifuge activities that contribute to its diverse medicinal profile. These pharmacological properties allow it to alleviate pain and reduce swelling in various biological systems. Its antiproliferative effects suggest potential utility in inhibiting the rapid growth of certain abnormal cells. Additionally, the plant acts as an antipyretic to help lower body temperatures during fever. Finally, its vermifuge action provides a means to expel parasitic worms from the digestive tract.

As discussed in a study, Carpesium divaricatum has been used in Korean traditional herbal medicine for its antipyretic, analgesic, vermifugic, and anti-inflammatoty properties. A new sesquiterpene lactone identified from the plant, named 2beta,5-epoxy-5,10-dihydroxy-6alpha-angeloyloxy-9beta-isobutyloxy-germacran-8alpha,12-olide (C-1), can suppress nitric oxide production in LPS/IFN-gamma-stimulated RAW 264.7 cells. This compound C-1 decreases nitric oxide production in a concentration-dependent manner with an IC50 of approximately 2.16 microM. The C-1 compound leads to a decrease in iNOS protein and mRNA, which may be responsible for its anti-inflammatoty effects. This inhibition occurs through the stabilization of the NF-kappaB/I-kappaB complex, which reduces the nuclear translocation of subunit p65.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains 11-methoxymethylgermacranolides, 12-oxo-phytodienoic acid (12-OPDA), chlorogenic acids, germacrane-type sesquiterpene lactones, and thymol derivatives. These diverse chemical constituents contribute to the complex therapeutic profile of the species. The germacrane-type sesquiterpene lactones and 11-methoxymethylgermacranolides are often studied for their specific biological activities. Additionally, the presence of chlorogenic acids provides significant antioxidant properties to the plant matter. Thymol derivatives and 12-oxo-phytodienoic acid further enhance its medicinal potential through various physiological pathways.

As discussed in a study, eight new 11-methoxymethylgermacranolides, labeled 1 through 8, were isolated from the ethanol extract of the whole plant of Carpesium divaricatum. The absolute configuration of compound 1 was established by electronic circular dichroism spectrum and X-ray crystallographic analysis, while the stereochemistry of compounds 2 through 8 was determined using similar ECD data. Furthermore, the absolute configurations of compounds 5 and 7 were confirmed through quantum chemical electronic circular dichroism calculations. Regarding biological activity, compound 4 exhibited weak cytotoxicity against MCF-7 cells. Additionally, compound 8 could potently decrease PGE2 productions in LPS-induced RAW 264.7 cells.
