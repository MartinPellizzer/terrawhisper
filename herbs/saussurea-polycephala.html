Saussurea polycephala

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea polycephala has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a thick, fleshy taproot and basal leaves that form a dense rosette. The leaves are typically lanceolate or oblong in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. From this rosette, a tall, erect flowering stem emerges, often reaching significant heights to support the inflorescence. The inflorescence consists of many small, tubular yellow florets clustered into compact, head-like capitula. These flower heads are often surrounded by specialized bracts that provide structural support and protection.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of the Tibetan Plateau and parts of southwestern China. It specifically inhabits rocky crevices and scree slopes located at elevations ranging from 3,500 to 5,000 meters above sea level. Its range is concentrated within the mountainous corridors of the Himalayas and the Hengduan Mountains. These specific environmental conditions provide the intense sunlight and well-drained soil necessary for its survival. Because it thrives in such specialized niches, its distribution remains relatively fragmented across these mountain ranges.
