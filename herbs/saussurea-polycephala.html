Saussurea polycephala

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea polycephala?

Saussurea polycephala belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the evolutionary lineage of the order Asterales. This plant is a part of the extensive family Asteraceae, a group widely known for its composite flower heads. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Saussurea, specifically identified by its unique species name, polycephala.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea polycephala has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by a thick, fleshy taproot and basal leaves that form a dense rosette. The leaves are typically lanceolate or oblong in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins. From this rosette, a tall, erect flowering stem emerges, often reaching significant heights to support the inflorescence. The inflorescence consists of many small, tubular yellow florets clustered into compact, head-like capitula. These flower heads are often surrounded by specialized bracts that provide structural support and protection.
