Trifolium fragiferum

What's the taxonomical classification of Trifolium fragiferum?

Trifolium fragiferum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Fabales, it is placed within the family Fabaceae. The plant is further defined by its genus, Trifolium, and is specifically identified by its species name, fragiferum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trifolium fragiferum has trifoliate leaves with obovate to elliptical leaflets, reddish-tinged petioles, and dense, white-pubescent stems. The leaflets often exhibit a distinct reddish or purplish tint, especially on the undersides. Its inflorescences consist of dense, globose heads containing numerous small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a low-lying stoloniferous herb, spreading through horizontal runners that root at the nodes. These creeping stems allow the species to form thick mats across its natural habitat.

What are the pharmacological activities of Trifolium fragiferum?

This plant has antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory pharmacological activities that contribute to its medicinal potential. The presence of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and polyphenols allows it to neutralize free radicals within biological systems. These chemical constituents also demonstrate the ability to inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, the plant extracts exhibit properties that help reduce swelling and immune responses in inflammatory models. Such diverse biological actions suggest that Trifolium fragiferum could serve as a valuable source for developing new therapeutic agents.

According to a study published by "Molecular and cellular biochemistry", Trifolium fragiferum L. was evaluated for its antioxidant effects using extracts at concentrations of 1.5-50.0 μg/ml. The plant's extracts contain three or four groups of (poly)phenolic compounds, including phenolic acids, clovamides, isoflavones, and other flavonoids. These extracts were tested for their ability to reduce oxidative and nitrative damage in blood platelets exposed to 100 μM peroxynitrite-induced oxidative stress in vitro. The antioxidant actions of the species were determined by biomarkers such as thiol groups, 3-nitrotyrosine, lipid hydroperoxides, and thiobarbituric acid-reactive substances (TBARS). The study noted that total phenolic concentrations for the extracts varied between 11.30 and 52.55 mg/g of dry mass.
