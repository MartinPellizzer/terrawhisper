Trifolium fragiferum

What's the taxonomical classification of Trifolium fragiferum?

Trifolium fragiferum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Fabales, it is placed within the family Fabaceae. The plant is further defined by its genus, Trifolium, and is specifically identified by its species name, fragiferum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Trifolium fragiferum has trifoliate leaves with obovate to elliptical leaflets, reddish-tinged petioles, and dense, white-pubescent stems. The leaflets often exhibit a distinct reddish or purplish tint, especially on the undersides. Its inflorescences consist of dense, globose heads containing numerous small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a low-lying stoloniferous herb, spreading through horizontal runners that root at the nodes. These creeping stems allow the species to form thick mats across its natural habitat.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains flavonoids, isoflavones, phenolic acids, and clovamides which serve as its primary medicinal compounds. These bioactive constituents work together to provide significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits to the human body. The presence of phenolic acids helps to neutralize free radicals and prevent cellular damage. Additionally, the isoflavones and flavonoids contribute to hormonal balance and cardiovascular health support. Clovamides further enhance the therapeutic profile by offering unique antimicrobial properties.

As discussed in a study, the extracts of Trifolium fragiferum contain three or four groups of (poly)phenolic compounds including phenolic acids, cloavamides, isoflavones, and other flavonoids. These extracts were evaluated at concentrations ranging from 1.5 to 50.0 μg/ml. The study assessed antioxidant properties by measuring the ability to reduce damage in blood platelets exposed to 100 μM peroxynitrite-induced oxidative stress. Total phenolic concentrations for the examined Trifolium extracts varied between 11.30 and 52.55 mg/g of dry mass. The antioxidant actions were determined using biomarkers such as thiol groups, 3-nitrotyrosine, lipid hydroperoxides, and thiobarbituric acid-reactive substances (TBARS).
