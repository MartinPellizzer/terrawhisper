Asplenium wrightii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Asplenium wrrightii has large, oblong to lanceolate fronds that can reach up to 100 centimeters in length and feature pinnate divisions with entire, slightly wavy margins. The texture of the foliage is typically leathery and glabrous, providing a sturdy structure to the fern. Each leaflet is arranged symmetrically along a central rachis that often displays a reddish or brownish pigmentation. The plant develops from a creeping rhizome that is covered in brownish scales. Small, kidney-shaped sori are positioned on the undersides of the mature leaves to facilitate reproduction.
