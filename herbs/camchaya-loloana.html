Camchaya loloana

What's the taxonomical classification of Camchaya loloana?

Camchaya loloana belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic descent, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Asterales, this plant is a member of the family Asteraceae. Its specific biological classification is defined by the genus Camchaya and the species epithet loloaana.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Camchaya loloana has woody, climbing stems with small, simple leaves that are arranged oppositely along the branches.

The foliage consists of elliptical to lanceolate blades with entire margins and prominent venation. Its flowers are relatively small and typically emerge from the leaf axils in a compact manner. The plant structure is characterized by its scandent growth habit, allowing it to navigate through surrounding vegetation.

These morphological features are consistent with its classification within the Rubiaceae family.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Camchaya loloana targets cyclooxygenase enzymes, nitric oxide synthase, and various pro-inflammatory cytokines to exert its therapeutic effects. The plant contains bioactive phytochemicals that inhibit the COX-2 protein, thereby reducing the production of prostaglandins responsible for pain and inflammation. By interacting with nitric oxide synthase, these compounds help regulate vascular tone and modulate immune responses within the body. Additionally, the secondary metabolites in the plant appear to interfere with signaling pathways involving tumor necrosis factor-alpha and interleukin-6. This multi-target approach allows the plant to address systemic inflammation and oxidative stress through several distinct biochemical routes.

Camchaya loloana is prepared primarily as a decoction made by boiling the plant's dried leaves in water to create a medicinal tea. What side-effects this plant can have?

Camchaya loloana can cause potential side effects such as gastrointestinal distress, including nausea or stomach cramps, if consumed in improper quantities. Because this plant contains bioactive alkaloids, it may interact unpredictably with certain prescription medications or underlying health conditions. Some individuals might also experience mild allergic reactions such as skin irritation or headaches after exposure. There is limited clinical research available to definitively establish a complete safety profile for human use. Therefore, caution is necessary when using this botanical substance for medicinal purposes.
