Eria szetschuanica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Asparagales. This plant is a member of the Orchidaceae family, falling specifically within the genus Eria, and is identified by its unique species name, szetschuanica.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eria szetschuanica has a monopodial growth habit characterized by creeping rhizomes, fleshy pseudobulbs, and large, broadly ovate, leathery leaves. The pseudobulbs are typically compressed and can reach significant sizes to facilitate water storage. Each leaf is attached to the apex of the pseudobulb by a short petiole. The inflorescence is a dense, many-flowered raceme that emerges from the upper portion of the stem.
