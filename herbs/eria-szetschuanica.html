Eria szetschuanica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eria szetschuanica has a monopodial growth habit characterized by creeping rhizomes, fleshy pseudobulbs, and large, broadly ovate, leathery leaves. The pseudobulbs are typically compressed and can reach significant sizes to facilitate water storage. Each leaf is attached to the apex of the pseudobulb by a short petiole. The inflorescence is a dense, many-flowered raceme that emerges from the upper portion of the stem. Flowers of this species feature prominent, often colorful labella and specialized structures adapted for specific pollinators.
