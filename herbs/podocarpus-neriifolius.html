Podocarpus neriifolius

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Podocarpus neriifolius has linear to lanceolate leaves that are coriaceous, typically measuring 5 to 15 centimeters in length and 1 to 3 centimeters in width. These evergreen leaves are arranged spirally along the branches and feature an acute apex with a slightly revolute margin. The foliage exhibits a deep green color on the upper surface, while the underside often appears a lighter shade. Its woody stems are sturdy and can support a tree or shrub growth habit depending on the environment. Small, fleshy receptacles appear as fruits, which change color as they mature to attract dispersers.

Within these habitats, it is often found growing in well-drained soils along creek lines or on sloping terrain.
