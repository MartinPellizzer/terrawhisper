Podocarpus neriifolius

What's the taxonomical classification of Podocarpus neriifolius?

Podocarpus neriifolius belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Pinidae, falling under the order Pinales. This plant is a member of the family Podocarpaceae, specifically placed within the genus Podocarpus, and is identified by its unique species name, neriifolius.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Podocarpus neriifolius has linear to lanceolate leaves that are coriaceous, typically measuring 5 to 15 centimeters in length and 1 to 3 centimeters in width.

These evergreen leaves are arranged spirally along the branches and feature an acute apex with a slightly revolute margin. The foliage exhibits a deep green color on the upper surface, while the underside often appears a lighter shade. Its woody stems are sturdy and can support a tree or shrub growth habit depending on the environment.

Small, fleshy receptacles appear as fruits, which change color as they mature to attract dispersers.

      What are the herbal preparations of Podocarpus neriifolius?

      Podocarpus neriifolius is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical wash depending on the intended therapeutic application.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Podocarpus neriifolius can cause skin irritation and gastrointestinal distress if its parts are ingested or handled improperly. While specific toxicity levels are not extensively documented for humans, the plant contains compounds that may trigger allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Ingestion of the foliage or fruit could lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. It is advisable to use gloves when pruning the shrub to prevent direct contact with any sap. Always keep this plant away from pets and small children to avoid accidental poisoning.
