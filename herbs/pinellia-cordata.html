Pinellia cordata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pinellia cordata has heart-shaped, cordate leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite or whorled pattern along a woody, perennial stem. The leaf margins are generally smooth or finely serrated, while the upper surface often exhibits a glossy, dark green texture. Its stems possess a slightly rough bark that becomes increasingly textured as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the peak growing season. The root system consists of a sturdy taproot designed to anchor the plant firmly in its native substrate.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of the southern Balkan Peninsula, specifically occurring in parts of Greece and Albania. It thrives within limestone-rich rocky habitats and scrublands situated at moderate elevations. These populations are often found in fragmented patches rather than continuous forests. The specific environmental requirements of the species limit its presence to these particular Mediterranean mountain ranges.
