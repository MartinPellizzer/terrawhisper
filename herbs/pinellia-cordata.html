What's the taxonomical classification of Pinellia cordata?

Pinellia cordata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Alismatales. As a member of the family Araceae, this plant is represented by the genus Pinellia, with the specific designation of the species cordata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pinellia cordata has heart-shaped, cordate leaves that are typically arranged in an opposite or whorled pattern along a woody, perennial stem. Its stems possess a slightly rough bark that becomes increasingly textured as the plant matures. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the peak growing season.
