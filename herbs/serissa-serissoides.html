Serissa serissoides

What's the taxonomical classification of Serissa serissoides?

Serissa serissoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Gentianales, this plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Serissa and the species serissoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Serissa serissoides has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length and are arranged oppositely on woody stems. The foliage is typically a glossy dark green on the upper surface while the undersides remain a lighter shade. Tiny, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils throughout the growing season. These blooms are often accompanied by small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a deep red or black color upon ripening. The plant generally maintains a compact, shrubby growth habit with multiple branching stems.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds beta-sitosterol, oleanolic acid, ursolic acid, corosolic acid, and caryophyllene. These specific phytochemicals are recognized for their diverse biological activities and therapeutic potential in human health. The triterpenoids such as oleanolic acid and ursolic acid often contribute to anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Additionally, the presence of corosolic acid may support metabolic functions, while caryophyllene serves as a significant sesquiterpene. Together, these bioactive constituents define the chemical profile and medicinal value of Serissa serissoides.

According to a study published by "Zhong yao cai = Zhongyaocai = Journal of Chinese medicinal materials", seven compounds were obtained from the 80% ethanol extract of Serissa serissoides roots using column chromatography over silica gel, Sephadex LH-20, and Polyamide. The seven compounds identified from the plant leaves are (+) -pinoresinol (1), (-) -syringaresinol (2), (+) -meduiresinol (3), (-) -olivil (4), beta-sitosterol (5), oleanolic acid (6), and daucosterol (7). Compounds 2 and 4 were obtained from this genus plants for the first time. Additionally, compounds 1, 3, and 7 were obtained from the root of the plant for the first time as known compounds.
