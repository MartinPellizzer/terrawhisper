What's the taxonomical classification of Serissa serissoides?

Serissa serissoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Gentianales, this plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Serissa and the species serissoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Serissa serissoides has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length and are arranged oppositely on woody stems. The foliage is typically a glossy dark green on the upper surface while the undersides remain a lighter shade. Tiny, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils throughout the growing season. These blooms are often accompanied by small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a deep red or black color upon ripening. The plant generally maintains a compact, shrubby growth habit with multiple branching stems.

These bioactive molecules contribute to a wide range of potential therapeutic benefits within the human body. For instance, ursolic acid and oleanolic acid are often studied for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. The presence of caryophyllene provides sesquiterpene benefits that may interact with endocannabinoid receptors. Additionally, corosolic acid and beta-sitosterol are recognized for their roles in supporting metabolic health and regulating glucose levels.

As discussed in a study, seven compounds were obtained from the 80% ethanol extract of Serissa serissoides roots using repeated column chromatography over silica gel, Sephadex LH-20 and Polyamide.
