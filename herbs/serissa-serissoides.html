What's the taxonomical classification of Serissa serissoides?

Serissa serissoides belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Within the order Gentianales, this plant is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Its specific taxonomic identification is defined by the genus Serissa and the species serissoides.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Serissa serissoides has small, elliptical to lanceolate leaves that measure approximately 1 to 3 centimeters in length and are arranged oppositely on woody stems.

The foliage is typically a glossy dark green on the upper surface while the undersides remain a lighter shade. Tiny, white, five-petaled flowers emerge in clusters from the leaf axils throughout the growing season. These blooms are often accompanied by small, fleshy drupes that transition from green to a deep red or black color upon ripening.

The plant generally maintains a compact, shrubby growth habit with multiple branching stems.

      What are the herbal preparations of Serissa serissoides?

      Serissa serissoides is prepared as a decoction, an infusion, or a topical wash using the leaves, stems, or whole plant parts.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Serissa serissoides can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, or mild toxicity if ingested by humans or pets. While the plant is generally considered non-toxic to humans in small amounts, direct contact with its sap may trigger contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals. Some people might experience redness, itching, or a localized rash upon touching the foliage. If consumed, it could lead to gastrointestinal discomfort or stomach upset. Monitoring for these symptoms is important when handling the plant or keeping it in living spaces.
