Anodendron formicinum

What's the taxonomical classification of Anodendron formicinum?

Anodendroon formicinum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Gentianaales. This plant is further categorized into the family Apocynaceae, which dictates its specific botanical characteristics. Within this family, it is identified by the genus Anodendron and is specifically designated by the species name formicinum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Anodendroon formicinum has slender, woody stems that possess a specialized morphology of hollow, bulbous nodes designed to house symbiotic ants. These nodes are often swollen and serve as protected chambers for the insect colony to reside within the plant structure. The leaves are typically small, elliptical or ovate, and arranged alternately along the climbing vines. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters that do not distract from the plant's unique stem structure. The overall growth habit is that of a climbing vine that relies on these structural adaptations for survival.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds formicinuoside A, formicinuoside B, formicinuoside C, and various prenylbenzoic acid derivatives. These specific chemical constituents are responsible for the unique biological activities observed in the species. Researchers focus on these glycosides and organic acids to understand their potential therapeutic applications. The presence of these diverse metabolites suggests a complex chemical profile capable of interacting with various biological targets. Ongoing studies aim to isolate these substances for further pharmacological testing.

As discussed in a study, a phytochemical investigation on the stems of Anodendron formicinum led to the isolation of eight prenylbenzoic acid derivatives. Three of these were new compounds designated as formicinuoside A (1), B (2), and C (3). Among the isolated compounds, compounds 4, 6 and 8 showed significant antibacterial activities against Providensia smartii with MIC values of 0.781 μg/mL. Additionally, compound 8 showed remarkable antibacterial activity against Eschericchia coli with an MIC value of 0.781 μg/mL.
