Phlegmariurus fargesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. This plant is a part of the family Lycopodiaceae, falling under the specific genus Phlegmariurus, and is identified by its unique species designation, fargesii.

What are the herbal preparations of Phlegmariurus fargesii?

Phlegmariurus fargesii is prepared primarily as a decoction through the boiling of its dried aerial parts in water to extract active alkaloids. In some regional practices, the plant may also be incorporated into tinctures using ethanol to increase the solubility of its chemical constituents. Powdered forms of the dried plant material are occasionally used as a secondary preparation for specific medicinal applications. These various methods aim to standardize the dosage of the plant's bioactive compounds for therapeutic use.
