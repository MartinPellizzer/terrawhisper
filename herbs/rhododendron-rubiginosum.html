What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron rubiginosum has small, leathery, elliptic to oblong leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and covered in rusty-colored, stellate scales on the underside. The plant typically grows as a low-spreading or mounding shrub with woody stems that exhibit similar ferruginous pubescence. Its inflorescences consist of small clusters of funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. Each individual bloom features five delicate lobes and is supported by a short pedicel. The overall structure is compact, allowing it to thrive in rocky, alpine environments.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe toxicity leading to nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, dizziness, confusion, arrhythmia, seizures, and respiratory depression. These symptoms occur because the plant contains grayanotoxins that interfere with sodium channels in the body. Once ingested, the toxins can disrupt the electrical signals in the heart, resulting in dangerous irregular heartbeats. Neurological impacts may manifest as disorientation or loss of consciousness following the initial gastrointestinal distress. In extreme cases, the systemic reaction can progress to life-threatening convulsions and a failure of the breathing process.
