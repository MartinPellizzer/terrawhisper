Rhododendron rubiginosum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rhododendron rubiginosum has small, leathery, elliptic to oblong leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and covered in rusty-colored, stellate scales on the underside. The plant typically grows as a low-spreading or mounding shrub with woody stems that exhibit similar ferruginous pubescence. Its inflorescences consist of small clusters of funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. Each individual bloom features five delicate lobes and is supported by a short pedicel. The overall structure is compact, allowing it to thrive in rocky, alpine environments.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of central and eastern Nepal, extending into parts of northern India and Bhutan. It is primarily found growing in the subalpine zone within the Himalayan mountain range. These rhododendrons typically inhabit moist, rocky slopes and forest margins at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 3,500 meters. The specific microclimates of these mountainous areas provide the cool, temperate conditions necessary for the species to thrive.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Rhododendron rubiginosum can cause severe poisoning if ingested because it contains grayanotoxins that disrupt the sodium channels in cell membranes. These toxins primarily affect the cardiovascular and nervous systems of humans and animals. Common symptoms of consumption include nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and dizziness. In more serious cases, an individual may experience low blood pressure, an irregular heartbeat, or even temporary paralysis. Medical intervention is often necessary to manage the dangerous physiological changes triggered by these compounds.
