What's the taxonomical classification of Mostuea batesii?

Mostuea batesii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Gentianales. Within this order, the plant is a member of the family Gelsemiaceae. Specifically, it is identified by the genus Mostuea and is distinguished as the species batesii.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mostuea batesii has woody, scrambling stems with small, opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and coriaceous.

The foliage is characterized by distinct pinnate venation and a somewhat leathery texture. Its inflorescences consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These flowers eventually develop into small, fleshy fruits that serve as a key identifying feature for the species.

The overall growth habit allows the plant to climb through dense vegetation in its native habitat.

        Mostuea batesii is prepared as a decoction of its bark, a concentrated infusion of its leaves, or a topical poultice made from crushed plant matter.
