Garden Horseradish (Armoracia armoracia) Uses

Garden horseradish is used to treat respiratory conditions such as cough, bronchitis, and sinusitis. It helps alleviate symptoms of influenza, allergic rhinitis, and colds by reducing mucus buildup and inflammation in the respiratory tract. Its antimicrobial properties support healing in respiratory infections and tract inflammation.

Garden Horseradish (Armoracia armoracia) Monograph

1. Cough

Garden horseradish is used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. Its pungent compounds help clear mucus and soothe irritated airways. It is often prepared as a gargle or tonic to reduce coughing and ease congestion. Regular use may help alleviate persistent or chronic coughs.

2. Bronchitis

Garden horseradish is used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help reduce mucus buildup and soothe irritated airways. It may be prepared as a tincture or syrup to ease symptoms and promote easier breathing.

3. Sinusitis

Garden horseradish is used to alleviate sinusitis due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and combat bacterial infections. Often prepared as a steam inhalation or nasal spray, it supports respiratory health. Its pungent compounds clear mucus and soothe sinus passages.

4. Influenza

Garden horseradish is used in herbal medicine to support immune function during influenza. Its high levels of sinigrin and other compounds may help reduce viral replication and alleviate symptoms. It is often prepared as a tincture or decoction to enhance its antiviral properties.

5. Allergic rhinitis

Garden horseradish is used to alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and decongestant properties. It helps reduce nasal congestion and inflammation, supporting respiratory health. Its active compounds may inhibit histamine release, providing relief from allergy-induced symptoms. Regular use can support long-term management of seasonal and perennial allergic rhinitis.

6. Cold symptoms

Garden horseradish is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its expectorant and decongestant properties. It helps loosen mucus and reduce nasal congestion. Its pungent compounds may also have antimicrobial effects, supporting the body's natural defenses during a cold. It is typically consumed as a syrup or tincture.

7. Respiratory infections

Garden horseradish is used to support respiratory health by helping to clear mucus and reduce inflammation in the airways. Its antimicrobial properties may assist in fighting bacterial and viral infections. It is often used in herbal teas or tinctures to alleviate symptoms of colds, coughs, and bronchitis. Its expectorant effects aid in loosening congestion and promoting easier breathing.

8. Respiratory tract inflammation

Garden horseradish contains compounds with anti-inflammatory properties that may help reduce swelling and irritation in the respiratory tract. It has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of inflammation, coughing, and mucus production. Its use is often combined with other herbs in respiratory formulas.

9. Mucus buildup

Garden horseradish is used to help alleviate mucus buildup due to its mucolytic properties. It contains compounds that break down mucus, making it easier to expel from the respiratory tract. This makes it beneficial for conditions like bronchitis or sinus congestion. It is often prepared as a tincture or infused in oil for internal use.

10. Respiratory tract infection

Garden horseradish is used to support respiratory health by reducing inflammation and clearing mucus. Its antimicrobial properties may help combat infections. It is often used in respiratory remedies to alleviate symptoms like coughing and congestion. Its expectorant action aids in removing pathogens from the respiratory tract.
