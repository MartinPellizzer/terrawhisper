Callicarpa yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Callicarpa yunnanensis has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that measure approximately 5 to 12 centimeters in length and feature serrated margins. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub with slender, woody stems that exhibit a brownish or grayish bark texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant white or pale lavender flowers arranged in dense axillary cymes. Following pollination, the plant produces small, spherical drupes that transition from green to a vibrant purple or deep blue color when ripe. These colorful berries are often clustered tightly along the branches, providing a distinct visual characteristic during the fruiting season.
