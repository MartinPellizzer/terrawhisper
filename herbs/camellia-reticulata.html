Camellia reticulata

What's the taxonomical classification of Camellia reticulata?

Camellia reticulata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is categorized under the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Ericales. It is a member of the family Theaceae, situated within the genus Camellia, and is specifically identified by its species name, reticulata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Camellia reticulata has large, elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green with serrated margins and a glossy surface. The plant is characterized by its impressive terminal flowers which can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. These blooms feature numerous overlapping petals and prominent central stamens that vary in color from deep red to soft pink. Its woody stems develop a sturdy structure as the shrub matures into a dense evergreen form. The overall morphology supports a robust growth habit suitable for shaded environments.
