Lithocarpus dealbatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lithocarpus dealbatus?

Lithocarpus dealbatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fagales. As a member of the family Fagaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Lithocarpus, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species dealbatus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Lithocarpus dealbatus contains high concentrations of polyphenolic compounds, specifically tannins, flavonoids, and triterpenoids. These bioactive constituents provide significant antioxidant properties that help neutralize free radicals in biological systems. The presence of specific tannins suggests potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory applications in traditional medicinal practices. Furthermore, the triterpene content may offer protective effects against cellular oxidative stress. Research into these specific chemical profiles continues to explore their utility in developing new pharmacological agents.

What are the therapeutic actions of Lithocarpus dealbatus?

Lithocarpus dealbatus has medicinal properties primarily characterized by its antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial activities derived from its high concentration of polyphenols and tannins. These bioactive compounds work by neutralizing free radicals to prevent cellular damage and oxidative stress within the body. The plant extracts are often studied for their ability to reduce swelling and inhibit the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Additionally, the tannins present in the bark and leaves may assist in treating digestive issues by acting as an astringent. This makes the species a significant subject in ethnobotanical research regarding natural therapeutic agents.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including diarrhea, dysentery, and skin inflammations. The leaves and bark of the species possess medicinal properties that help soothe gastrointestinal distress. Local practitioners often utilize decoctions made from these parts to combat infectious intestinal issues. Additionally, topical applications of the plant extracts are applied to wounds and skin irritations to promote healing. These traditional applications rely on the bioactive compounds found within the plant's tissues.
