Lithocarpus dealbatus

What's the taxonomical classification of Lithocarpus dealbatus?

Lithocarpus dealbatus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is placed in the subclass Magnoliidae and falls under the order Fagales. As a member of the family Fagaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Lithocarpus, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species dealbatus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Lithocarpus dealbatus has elliptical to oblong leaves that are characterized by a distinctively pale, white underside and a dark green, leathery upper surface. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly wavy, while the venation pattern is prominent on the lighter abaxial side. This evergreen tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into woody, cup-shaped acorns. These fruits are enclosed in a thick, scaly cupule that adheres closely to the nut. The bark of the mature tree is generally greyish and becomes deeply fissured with age.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the subtropical and temperate montane forests of Taiwan, where it grows at elevations ranging from 500 to 2,500 meters. It is primarily found across the central and northern mountain ranges of the island. Within these specific altitudinal zones, the species often dominates the canopy of evergreen broad-leaved forests. Its distribution is closely tied to the presence of humid, misty environments common in high-altitude regions. Localized populations vary depending on soil composition and the specific microclimates of the Taiwanese mountainsides.
