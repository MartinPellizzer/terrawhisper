Acer sinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Acer sinense?

Acer sinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Acer, specifically identified by the species name sinese.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Acer sinense contains high concentrations of triterpenoids, flavonoids, and phenolic acids that contribute to its therapeutic potential. These bioactive compounds are primarily extracted from the bark and leaves of the plant. Research indicates that these phytochemicals possess significant antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Some studies suggest that these substances may also exhibit antimicrobial activity against certain pathogens. Consequently, the plant is often investigated for its potential role in managing oxidative stress and inflammatory conditions.

What are the therapeutic actions of Acer sinense?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, skin disorders, and digestive ailments. Its medicinal properties are often applied to soothe skin irritation and manage topical infections. In traditional practices, extracts are sometimes utilized to address internal issues such as stomach discomfort or fever. The chemical components within the plant help regulate bodily responses to certain types of swelling. These applications rely on the plant's natural ability to act as an anti-inflammatory agent.
