Acer sinense

What's the taxonomical classification of Acer sinense?

Acer sinense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is classified in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its evolutionary lineage continues through the order Sapindales and the family Sapindaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Acer, specifically identified by the species name sinese.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Acer sinense has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that typically consist of five to seven distinct lobes with serrated margins. The foliage is deciduous and can transition to brilliant shades of red or orange during the autumn season. Its bark is relatively smooth when young but becomes grayish and fissured as the tree reaches maturity. The plant produces small, winged samaras that develop in clusters from inconspicuous flowers. These woody fruits are designed to spin through the air to facilitate seed dispersal via wind.
