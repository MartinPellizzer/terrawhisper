Opuntia stricta

What's the taxonomical classification of Opuntia stricta?

Opuntiia stricta belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Caryophyllales and the family Cactaceae, eventually reaching its specific designation within the genus Opuntia. Finally, its unique identity is established at the species level as stricta.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Opuntiia stricta has flattened, succulent, oval-shaped cladodes that are typically between 5 and 25 centimeters long and are covered in both large spines and tiny, hair-like glochids. These fleshy segments function as both photosynthetic organs and water storage tissues. The plant often grows in a branched, shrubby habit with segments that can become somewhat woody at the base as the plant matures. Small, nocturnal flowers emerge from the edges of the cladodes, usually displaying bright pink or magenta petals. After pollination, the plant produces small, fleshy fruits that transition from green to a reddish color.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the southeastern United States, ranging from the coastal plains of North Carolina and South Carolina down through Florida and west into Mississippi and Louisiana. It thrives primarily in sandy soils within coastal dunes, maritime forests, and pine flatwoods. Although its natural range is concentrated in the Atlantic and Gulf Coastal Plains, it has expanded into several other warm regions. Some populations have been introduced or naturalized in parts of the Caribbean and even as far north as some coastal areas of the Mid-Atlantic. The species prefers subtropical to temperate climates characterized by high humidity and mild winters.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant Opuntia stricta is cultivated by planting stem segments or cladodes in well-draining, sandy soils under full sunlight to ensure optimal growth.

Because it is a drought-tolerant cactus, it requires minimal supplemental watering once its root system is established. Growers must monitor for soil moisture levels to prevent root rot, which can easily damage the succulent tissues. This species thrives in warm climates and can be propagated easily through simple cuttings placed directly into the earth.

Regular maintenance involves removing competing weeds and ensuring the plant has sufficient space to spread its pads.

What traditional systems uses this plant?

This plant, Opuntia stricta, has been utilized by indigenous populations throughout the Caribbean and coastal Americas for centuries as a vital source of food, medicine, and dye.

People traditionally harvested the edible pads, known as nopales, to serve as a nutritional staple in various cooked dishes. The fruit was often consumed fresh or processed into syrups to provide hydration and vitamins. Medicinally, crushed plant material was applied topically to treat skin inflammations and wounds due to its soothing properties.

Additionally, certain varieties were used to produce natural pigments for coloring textiles.

What are the pharmacological activities of Opuntia stricta?

This plant has antioxidant, antibacterial, antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and cytotoxic pharmacological activities that contribute to its medicinal potential. The presence of bioactive compounds allows the species to neutralize free radicals and mitigate oxidative stress in biological systems. It demonstrates significant inhibitory effects against various bacterial strains, showcasing its antimicrobial strength. Furthermore, its ability to reduce inflammation makes it a candidate for treating swelling and immune-related responses. The observed cytotoxic properties also suggest that extracts from the plant may hold promise for specialized therapeutic applications in oncology research.

According to a study published by "PloS one", Opuntia stricta cladodes contain vitamins A, C and E in their dried weight. The phytochemical composition was evaluated using acetone, aqueous and ethanol extracts to determine antioxidant, anti-inflammaroty, and cytotoxic activities. Total phenolics were found to be higher than other phytochemicals in all of the extracts used. Antioxidant activities were determined through DPPH, nitric oxide, hydrogen peroxide scavenging activity and phosphomolybdenum activities. Anti-inflammaroty activity was evaluated using RAW 264.7 cells, while cytotoxicity was tested using U937 cells. The results indicate that the plant is rich in polyphenolic compounds and exhibits good antioxidant and anti-inflammaroty activities, though only one extract showed mild cytotoxicity.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains betalain derivatives such as betanin and isobetain, along with isorrhamnetin and various polyphenolic compounds. These bioactive molecules contribute to the complex chemical profile of Opuntia stricta. The betalains are particularly noted for providing the plant with its distinct pigmentation and antioxidant properties. Additionally, the presence of isorrhamnetin and other polyphenolic compounds supports various therapeutic potential in medicinal applications. Together, these substances facilitate the plant's diverse biological activities.

According to a study published by "Molecules (Basel, Switzerland)", the fruit pulp and seeds of Opuntia stricta contain various bioactive phytochemicals. Liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry analysis of the freeze-dried pulp and its hydroalcoholic extract revealed betalain derivatives, specifically isobetanin and betanin, along with piscidic acid, isorrhamnetin-3-O-glucoside, and isorrhamnetin-3-O-rutinoside. The seed's fixed oil contains 41.95% linoleic acid and 19.32% palmitic acid. In the ABTS test, the hydroalcoholic extract and freeze-dried fruit pulp showed significant half-maximal inhibitory concentrations (IC50) of 13.24 mg/mL and 14.82 mg/mL, respectively. Additionally, the freeze-dried fruit pulp exhibited a high effect against lipase with an IC50 of 33.54 μg/mL.

What health conditions is this plant used for?

This plant is used for treating diabetes, hyperglycemia, hypertension, oxidative stress, and blood purification. Additionally, the plant acts as a powerful antioxidant to neutralize oxidative stress within the body. These combined effects support the overall process of blood purification and metabolic health.

According to a study published by "African journal of traditional, complementary, and alternative medicines : AJTCAM", Opuntia stricta (Haw.) Haw. is utilized for the treatment of various diseases. These ailments include chest complaint, blood purification, asthma, hypertension, and infertility. The study involved 30 Bapedi traditional healers who were randomly selected via the snowball method. Within the medicinal plants identified, 57.6% of the parts used were leaves, while 33.3% were roots, and whole plant, seeds, and bark accounted for 3% each. Out of 10 invasive alien plants studied, 80% or 8 species are listed in both NEMBA and CARA legislation.
