Pandanus (Pandanus)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Pandanus.

Pandanus, scientifically known as Pandanus, is a genus of flowering plants commonly referred to as pandans, screw pines, or torch palms, widely recognized for its medicinal and culinary uses. This tropical plant, particularly the leaves and fruits, is valued as a medicinal herb and adaptogen, known for its ability to support the body's natural resilience. The primary benefits of Pandanus include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which contribute to digestive health, immune support, and skin care. Traditionally, Pandanus has been used in various cultures for making ceremonial items, as a flavoring in food, and for treating ailments such as digestive disorders and skin infections. In modern wellness practices, it is increasingly incorporated into natural remedies, herbal supplements, and aromatherapy, with its unique, fragrant leaves offering a distinctive flavor and potential therapeutic compounds that continue to intrigue researchers.
