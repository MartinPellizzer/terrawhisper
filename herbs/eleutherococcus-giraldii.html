Eleutherococcus giraldii

What's the taxonomical classification of Eleutherococcus giraldii?

Eleutherococcus giraldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the taxonomic order Apiales. The plant is a member of the family Araliaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Eleutherococcus, with its unique species designation being giraldii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Eleutherococcus giraldii contains a complex mixture of bioactive compounds including eleutherosides, saponins, polysaccharides, and various essential oils. These specific eleutherosides function as adaptogens that help the human body resist physical and environmental stressors. The presence of polysaccharides supports immune system modulation and enhances overall metabolic functions. Additionally, the plant's unique phenolic compounds contribute to its antioxidant properties. Together, these chemical constituents work synergistically to improve energy levels and mental clarity.

What are the therapeutic actions of Eleutherococcus giraldii?

Eleutherococcus giraldii has adaptogenic properties that help the body resist various physical, chemical, and biological stressors by modulating the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis. This plant functions by increasing non-specific resistance and enhancing the metabolic capacity of cells during periods of exhaustion. It is frequently used to improve mental performance, increase physical endurance, and stabilize the immune system. Regular consumption may assist in regulating blood pressure and improving lipid profiles in the blood. These therapeutic actions make it a valuable botanical tool for managing fatigue and promoting overall homeostasis.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat physical exhaustion, chronic fatigue syndrome, decreased immunity, and various stress-related disorders. It acts as an adaptogen by helping the body resist physiological and psychological stressors. Medicinal applications often include managing symptoms of mental fatigue and improving overall metabolic function. Some studies suggest it may assist in recovering from physical overexertion and supporting the immune system during illness. The plant is frequently utilized in traditional medicine to bolster stamina and vitality.
