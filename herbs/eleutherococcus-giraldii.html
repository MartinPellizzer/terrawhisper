What's the taxonomical classification of Eleutherococcus giraldii?

Eleutherococcus giraldii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It falls under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, following the taxonomic order Apiales. The plant is a member of the family Araliaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Eleutherococcus, with its unique species designation being giraldii.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

These bioactive substances function primarily as plant hormones that manage growth, development, and stress responses. The presence of ABA helps regulate water retention and seed dormancy, while GA3 and IAA drive essential elongation and cellular differentiation processes. Furthermore, the inclusion of iPA and ZR plays a critical role in regulating cytokinin-related activities such as cell division and nutrient mobilization. Understanding the synergy between these specific compounds offers insight into the plant's complex biochemical signaling pathways.
