Vicia unijuga

What's the taxonomical classification of Vicia unijuga?

Vicia unijuga belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, placing it under the order Fabales. As a member of the family Fabaceae, it is further categorized into the genus Vicia, with its specific designation being the species unijuga.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, respiratory ailments, and digestive disorders in traditional medicine. It is often employed to alleviate symptoms associated with skin irritations and wound healing. In some regional practices, it is also utilized to manage symptoms of fever and general malaise. The medicinal properties are typically attributed to the specific bioactive compounds found within its foliage. Practitioners rely on its various extracts to target internal imbalances and localized pain.
