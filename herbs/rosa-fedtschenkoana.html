Rosa fedtschenkoana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rosa fedtschenkoana has pinnate leaves composed of five to seven leaflets with serrated margins, stems armed with curved prickles, and solitary flowers featuring five petals. The leaflets are typically elliptic or ovate in shape and possess a distinctively textured surface. Its stems exhibit a woody structure that supports the sprawling or climbing growth habit characteristic of the species. The flowers are usually white or pale pink and contain numerous stamens arranged around a central receptacle. Small, fleshy hips develop as fruit after successful pollination occurs.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the mountain ranges of Central Asia, specifically distributed across Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan. It typically thrives in high-altitude environments located within rocky slopes and subalpine meadows. The species is often found at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters above sea level. These specific habitats provide the well-drained soil and cool climates necessary for its survival. Its presence is closely tied to the unique geological formations of the Tien Shan and Pamir-Alay mountain systems.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Rosa fedtschenkoana can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or handled improperly. Some individuals might experience dermatitis or localized redness if they are sensitive to the plant's chemical compounds. If consumed, the plant could trigger nausea, stomach cramps, or other digestive disturbances. It is important to exercise caution and avoid direct contact to prevent these potential adverse reactions.
