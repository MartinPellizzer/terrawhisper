White Clover (Trifolium vulgare) Compounds

White Clover contains several key compounds, including flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins and saponins contribute to its astringent and cleansing actions, while coumarin and essential oils provide antimicrobial benefits. Glycosides, terpenoids, and anthocyanins further support its role in herbal medicine. Alkaloids are also present, though in smaller amounts.

White Clover (Trifolium vulgare) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are a group of compounds found in White Clover (Trifolium vulgare). They contribute to the plant's antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting its traditional use in herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory health. These compounds help neutralize free radicals and may enhance immune function.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in White Clover (Trifolium vulgare). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports its traditional use in supporting immune function and digestive health.

3. Tannin

Tannin in White Clover (Trifolium vulgare) acts as an astringent, helping to reduce inflammation and soothe irritated tissues. It also contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and skin conditions. Tannins in White Clover are primarily found in the leaves and flowers, supporting its role in herbal medicine.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in White Clover (Trifolium vulgare). It contributes to the plant's mild anticoagulant and anti-inflammatory properties. Coumarin is also responsible for the characteristic sweet aroma of White Clover. Its presence supports the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

5. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in White Clover (Trifolium vulgare). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and mild sedative effects. Glycosides in White Clover are often responsible for its traditional use in easing respiratory and digestive discomfort. These compounds are typically found in the leaves and flowers of the plant.

6. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in White Clover (Trifolium vulgare). It exhibits mild detergent properties and may support digestive health by promoting bile secretion. Saponins also have potential anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects, contributing to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in White Clover (Trifolium vulgare) contribute to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. These compounds are found in the plant's flowers and leaves, supporting its use in traditional herbal medicine for digestive and respiratory ailments.

8. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a bioactive compound found in White Clover (Trifolium vulgare). It acts as a powerful antioxidant, helping to neutralize free radicals in the body. This compound contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting cardiovascular health and reducing inflammation.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of White Clover (Trifolium vulgare) contains volatile compounds like linalool and geraniol. These components contribute to its calming properties and are used in aromatherapy. The oil is also valued for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloids in White Clover (Trifolium vulgare) are a class of nitrogen-containing compounds with potential pharmacological effects. These alkaloids may contribute to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though research on their specific therapeutic applications remains limited.
