White Clover (Trifolium vulgare) Uses

White Clover is used to alleviate symptoms of hay fever, asthma, and respiratory infections. It supports the treatment of allergic rhinitis, cough, and cold symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory properties may help with bronchitis and skin rashes. It is commonly used to ease the common cold and related ailments.

White Clover (Trifolium vulgare) Monograph

1. Hay fever

White Clover is used in herbal medicine to alleviate hay fever symptoms due to its antihistamine properties. It contains compounds that help reduce allergic reactions, making it a natural remedy for seasonal allergies. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture to support respiratory health during pollen season.

2. Asthma

White Clover has been traditionally used to support respiratory health in herbal medicine. It may help alleviate asthma symptoms by reducing inflammation and improving bronchial function. Its mild expectorant properties can ease mucus buildup, while its antispasmodic effects may relieve bronchial constriction. It is often used as a supportive herb in asthma management.

3. Respiratory infections

White Clover (Trifolium vulgare) has been traditionally used to support respiratory health. Its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties may help alleviate symptoms of respiratory infections. It is often prepared as a tea to soothe coughs and reduce congestion. Its mild nature makes it suitable for long-term use in respiratory support.

4. Allergic rhinitis

White Clover (Trifolium vulgare) may help alleviate symptoms of allergic rhinitis due to its anti-inflammatory and antihistamine properties. It contains compounds like quercetin and rutin that can reduce nasal congestion and inflammation. Used in teas or tinctures, it supports respiratory health and may ease seasonal allergy symptoms.

5. Cough

White Clover has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly for coughs. It contains mucilage that soothes irritated airways and reduces inflammation. Its mild expectorant properties help loosen mucus, making it easier to expel. It is often used in herbal teas or tinctures for its calming and supportive effects on the respiratory system.

6. Cold symptoms

White Clover is used to alleviate cold symptoms due to its anti-inflammatory and mild antispasmodic properties. It helps reduce congestion and soothe sore throats when brewed as a tea. Its mild diaphoretic action supports sweating to reduce fever. It is often combined with other herbs for enhanced effectiveness in cold care.

7. Common cold

White Clover has been traditionally used to support immune function and alleviate symptoms of the common cold. Its anti-inflammatory and mild antiviral properties may help reduce congestion and soothe sore throats. It is often prepared as a tea and used internally, though it should not replace professional medical care for severe cold symptoms.

8. Colds

White Clover has been traditionally used to support the immune system and alleviate symptoms of colds. It contains compounds that may help reduce inflammation and support respiratory health. Its mild expectorant properties can assist in clearing mucus, making it a gentle remedy for mild colds.

9. Bronchitis

White Clover has been traditionally used to support respiratory health, particularly in cases of bronchitis. Its anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties help soothe irritated airways and ease coughing. It may also help reduce mucus buildup, making it a useful supportive herb in managing bronchial inflammation and congestion.

10. Skin rash

White Clover is used topically to treat skin rash due to its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. It helps reduce redness, itching, and irritation. A poultice or infused oil made from fresh leaves can be applied directly to the affected area for relief. Its mild nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin.
