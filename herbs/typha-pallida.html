Typha pallida

What's the taxonomical classification of Typha pallida?

Typha pallida belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is classified under the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Poales. Moving further down the taxonomic hierarchy, this plant is a member of the family Typhaceae and falls within the genus Typha, specifically identified by the species epithet pallida.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Typha pallida has long, linear, bright green leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length and a cylindrical flowering spike consisting of a dense brown female section atop a slender male section. The plant features a creeping rhizomatous root system that allows it to form dense, monospecific colonies in wetland habitats. Its leaves are sword-shaped with parallel venation and a slightly channeled structure. The inflorescence is characterized by a distinct separation between the upper staminate spike and the lower pistillate spike. These spikes are typically accompanied by small, inconspicuous flowers clustered tightly along the central axis.
