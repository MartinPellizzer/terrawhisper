Opisthopappus taihangensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Opisthopappus taihangensis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender stems, lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and small, yellow, discoid flower heads arranged in terminal corymbiform clusters. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem and possess distinct petioles. The flowering heads consist of numerous tubular florets that lack ray petals, a common trait within the Asteraceae family. The involucral bracts are arranged in several overlapping rows to protect the developing florets. Each plant develops a fibrous root system that supports its growth in rocky or mountainous habitats.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is endemic to the Taihang Mountains in north-central China. Its natural range is highly localized, primarily restricted to the specific mountain ranges within the Hebei and Shanxi provinces. Within these mountainous regions, the species typically occupies niche habitats characterized by specific elevations and soil compositions. Because its occurrence is tied to this unique terrain, it does not appear in any other global geographic locations. This restricted distribution makes the species a specialist of the temperate mountain ecosystems found in this part of East Asia.
