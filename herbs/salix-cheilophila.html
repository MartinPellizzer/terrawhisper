Salix cheilophila

What's the taxonomical classification of Salix cheilophila?

Salix cheilophila belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Malpighiales. It is a member of the family Salicaceae, which contains the genus Salix, and is specifically identified by its species name, cheilophilia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Salix cheilophila contains phenolic glycosides, specifically salicin, which serves as a primary precursor to salicylic acid. These bioactive compounds function by inhibiting the synthesis of prostaglandins, thereby providing analgesic and anti-inflammatory effects. The plant also possesses various flavonoids and tannins that contribute to its antioxidant properties. These phytochemicals work collectively to mitigate oxidative stress within biological systems. Such chemical profiles make the species a subject of interest for pharmacological research regarding pain management.

What are the therapeutic actions of Salix cheilophila?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various inflammatory conditions, such as fever, pain, and swelling. It contains chemical compounds that function similarly to modern salicylates. Local healers often utilize its properties to manage discomfort associated with headaches or muscle aches. The medicinal application focuses on reducing systemic inflammation within the body. These traditional uses rely on the plant's natural bioactive constituents to provide relief.
