What's the taxonomical classification of Salix cheilophila?

Salix cheilophila belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Malpighiales. It is a member of the family Salicaceae, which contains the genus Salix, and is specifically identified by its species name, cheilophilia.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

These phenolic glycosides act as precursors to salicylic acid, which inhibits the conversion of arachidonic acid into pro-inflammatory prostaglandins. By modulating these enzymatic pathways, the plant exerts significant analgesic and antipyretic effects on the biological systems of a consumer. Furthermore, the secondary metabolites in this species may interact with various inflammatory mediators and cytokine signaling pathways to reduce systemic swelling. This multi-targeted approach to biochemical regulation provides a foundation for its traditional use in treating pain and fever.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential interference with blood clotting processes due to its salicylate content. Furthermore, it may interact negatively with anticoagulant medications by increasing the risk of bleeding.
