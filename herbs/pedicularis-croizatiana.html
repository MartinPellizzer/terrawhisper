Pedicularis croizatiana

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis croizatiana has a perennial life cycle characterized by upright, leafy stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The leaves are primarily basal and lanceolate in shape, featuring deeply lobed or pinnatifid margins that give them a feathery appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, terminal spike composed of zygomorphic flowers with a distinctive tubular corolla. The petals are often brightly colored, ranging from pink to reddish hues, and are structured to facilitate pollination by specific insects. These flowering stalks emerge from a robust root system that allows the plant to anchor itself within its alpine habitat.
