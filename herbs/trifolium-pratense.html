What to know about Trifolium pratense before using it medicinally

Trifolium pratense, commonly known as red clover, is a perennial herb used in traditional medicine to alleviate symptoms of menopausal hot flashes, osteoporosis, and cardiovascular diseases.

This herb has been shown to exhibit several health benefits, including anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties. The therapeutic properties of red clover are attributed to its ability to balance estrogen levels, reduce inflammation, and improve cardiovascular health. The bioactive compounds responsible for these effects include isoflavones, particularly formononetin and genistein, which have been identified as the primary active constituents of the herb.

Red clover is commonly prepared as a tea, capsule, or supplement, and is also used in herbal remedies for its purported benefits in maintaining bone health and reducing the risk of osteoporosis.
