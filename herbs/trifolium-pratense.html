Red Clover

Trifolium pratense

Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) is a member of the Fabaceae family, traditionally used for its tonic properties related to blood and circulation, as well as for treating wounds and ulcers. It has also been historically employed as a diuretic and digestive aid, with the flowers, leaves, and stems commonly harvested for medicinal purposes.

Botanical Identification

Red Clover, scientifically known as Trifolium pratense, is a perennial herb in the Fabaceae family. Commonly called "Red Clover" across regions, it is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant has trifoliate leaves, pink to purple flowers, and a taproot system. It is also known as "Sweet Clover" in some herbal texts.

Red Clover Identification.

Active Compounds

Red Clover contains key active compounds such as flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work together to support various health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Their synergistic interactions enhance the herb's overall therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Infusion: Used for digestive issues and as a calming drink.

Decoction: Used for respiratory conditions and to reduce inflammation.

Tincture: Used for pain relief and as an antispasmodic.

Safety Profile

Red Clover (Trifolium pratense) is generally considered safe when used in recommended doses. However, it may cause allergic reactions in some individuals. It is contraindicated for use during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to potential hormonal effects.
