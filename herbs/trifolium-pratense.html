Trifolium pratense

Trifolium pratense, commonly known as Red Clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe, Western Asia, and Northwestern Africa.

It is valued for its potential health benefits, including reducing the risk of heart disease, menopausal symptoms, and osteoporosis, due to its rich content of isoflavones and other bioactive compounds.

The therapeutic actions of Red Clover are attributed to its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and estrogenic properties, which may help alleviate menopausal symptoms, improve bone density, and support cardiovascular health.

The bioactive constituents of Red Clover include isoflavones (genistein, formononetin, and biochanin A), coumarins, and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
