Mimusops elengi

What's the taxonomical classification of Mimusops elengi?

Mimusops elengi belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. Further taxonomic refinement places this plant in the family Sapotaceae, specifically within the genus Mimusops, with its unique identity defined by the species elengi.

What are the herbal preparations of Mimusops elengi?

Mimusops elengi is prepared as a decoction of its bark, leaves, or roots to treat various ailments including skin infections and digestive issues. The bark is frequently boiled in water to create a medicinal tea used for its antiseptic properties. Some traditional practitioners also crush the leaves to form a poultice that is applied directly to wounds or sores. In certain regions, the infusions are ingested to manage fever or stomach discomfort. These different methods allow for both internal and external therapeutic applications of the plant.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Mimusops elengi can cause severe skin irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested. The plant contains bioactive compounds that may trigger localized redness or swelling upon contact with sensitive skin. Ingestion of various parts of the plant could potentially lead to nausea, vomiting, or abdominal pain. Because its chemical profile is not fully documented for human safety, caution is necessary when handling its foliage or flowers. Users should avoid any direct exposure to prevent unexpected physiological reactions.
