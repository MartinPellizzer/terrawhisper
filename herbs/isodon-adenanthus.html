Isodon adenanthus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Isodon adenanthus has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that measure between 5 and 12 centimeters in length and feature entire margins with a slightly acuminate tip. The leaf surfaces are typically smooth and can range in color from a bright green to a darker shade depending on light exposure. Its stems are woody and square in cross-section during early growth stages before maturing into cylindrical branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge from axillary positions along the branches. These floral structures eventually give way to small, drupaceous fruits that facilitate the species' dispersal.
