10 Possible Side Effects Of Sideritis Montana (Mountain Tea)

Sideritis montana has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, and interactions with other medications.

Causes of these side effects include high doses, individual sensitivities, and concurrent use of medications.

Examples of how these side effects can worsen lives include severe nausea and vomiting that lead to dehydration, anaphylaxis that requires emergency medical attention, and interactions that exacerbate underlying medical conditions.

This article explains in details the 10 most common side effects of Sideritis montana if used imporperly.

1. Worsens underlying medical conditions

Sideritis montana causes allergic reactions in some individuals due to its rich content of flavonoids, terpenes, and phenolic compounds, which are known to stimulate the immune system and release histamine, leading to symptoms such as hives, itching, and difficulty breathing.

The high concentration of these compounds in mountain tea can also trigger an allergic response in people with sensitivities or allergies, making it a potential allergen to be aware of.

2. Worsens underlying medical conditions

Sideritis montana triggers digestive issues due to its high tannin content, which can irritate the stomach lining and reduce digestion.

The tea's active compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, may also affect the gut's natural pH balance and lead to an imbalance of gut bacteria.

As a result, individuals may experience symptoms such as nausea, bloating, and stomach cramps when consuming Sideritis montana.

3. Worsens underlying medical conditions

Sideritis montana induces excessive sweating due to its diaphoretic properties.

The herb contains compounds that stimulate the sweat glands, causing an increase in body temperature regulation. This can lead to profuse sweating, especially in individuals who are sensitive to its effects.

The increased perspiration can be attributed to the herb's ability to open up the pores and increase blood flow to the skin.

4. Worsens underlying medical conditions

Sideritis montana causes dizziness and lightheadedness due to its vasodilatory properties.

The herb's bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, can increase blood flow and reduce blood pressure, leading to a sensation of dizziness and lightheadedness.

This effect is more pronounced when consuming large quantities of the herb or in individuals who are already prone to blood pressure fluctuations.

5. Worsens underlying medical conditions

Sideritis montana interferes with blood clotting, a potential side effect associated with its consumption.

This is due to the presence of flavonoids and other bioactive compounds that affect platelet function and vitamin K-dependent coagulation pathways.

The polyphenolic compounds in mountain tea may enhance the activity of enzymes responsible for bleeding, thereby leading to a heightened risk of bleeding or bruising.

6. Worsens underlying medical conditions

Sideritis montana increases heart rate and pressure due to its stimulatory effects on the body.

The herb contains flavonoids and phenolic acids, which can stimulate the cardiovascular system, leading to a rise in heart rate and blood pressure.

This is particularly attributed to the presence of adrenergic compounds, which can interact with the body's natural sympathetic nervous system to produce a heightened cardiovascular response.

7. Worsens underlying medical conditions

Sideritis montana causes stomach upset and nausea.

This side effect occurs due to the high tannin content in the plant, which can irritate the stomach lining and disrupt digestive enzymes. Additionally, Sideritis montana's flavonoids may interact with stomach acid, leading to increased acidity and inflammation.

As a result, some individuals may experience stomach discomfort, nausea, and potentially, stomach ulcers.

8. Worsens underlying medical conditions

Sideritis montana disrupts menstrual cycles by exerting a phytoestrogenic effect on the body.

This means that the plant's bioactive compounds mimic the action of estrogen, a hormone that regulates menstrual cycles. As a result, some individuals may experience changes in menstrual frequency, duration, or heaviness.

The disruption of the delicate balance of hormones in the body may lead to irregular menstrual cycles.

9. Worsens underlying medical conditions

Sideritis montana inflames skin and mucous membranes due to the presence of sesquiterpenes and phenolic compounds.

These bioactive molecules can cause an increase in blood flow and permeability, leading to inflammation and irritation. Additionally, the high antioxidant activity of Sideritis montana can also contribute to this effect, as it can interact with cell membranes and trigger an inflammatory response in sensitive individuals.

This can manifest as skin redness, itching, and mucous membrane irritation.

10. Worsens underlying medical conditions

Sideritis montana aggravates anxiety and stress due to its high levels of volatile compounds, particularly thujone, which can have a stimulating effect on the nervous system.

Additionally, the plant contains flavonoids and phenolic acids that may interact with the brain's serotonin system, potentially exacerbating anxiety-like symptoms.

This can lead to increased heart rate, insomnia, and irritability in some individuals.
