Clausena dentata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Clausena dentata has small, elliptic to lanceolate leaves characterized by distinctively serrated or dentate margins and a leathery texture. The foliage is typically arranged in an opposite pattern along the slender, woody stems. These leaves feature a prominent midrib and a slightly glossy surface that can vary in shade from bright green to a deeper hue. The plant often displays fine pubescence on its younger growth or undersides. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, contributing to its overall delicate morphological profile.

      Clausena dentata is prepared primarily as a decoction of its leaves or as a concentrated aqueous extract used in traditional medicinal practices. These liquid preparations are typically boiled to extract bioactive compounds for treating various ailments.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Clausena dentata can cause severe skin irritation, contact dermatitis, and potential mucosal inflammation due to the presence of bioactive compounds like diterpenes. Direct contact with the sap or crushed leaves often results in redness, itching, and painful rashes on the skin. If these plant components accidentally enter the eyes, they may trigger intense stinging and localized swelling. Ingestion of the plant is also a significant concern as it may lead to gastrointestinal distress or systemic toxicity. Users should handle the plant with extreme caution to prevent accidental exposure to these reactive substances.
