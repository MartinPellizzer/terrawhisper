Mussaenda arcuata

What's the taxonomical classification of Mussaenda arcuata?

Mussaenda arcuata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it is placed in the order Gentianales and is a member of the family Rubiaceae. Finally, its specific biological identity is defined by its placement within the genus Mussaenda, with its unique designation being the species arcuata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Mussaenda arcuata has large, bright red, petal-like involucral bracts that surround small, cream-colored, five-lobed true flowers.

These showy bracts are the most prominent feature of the plant and serve to attract pollinators to the inconspicuous blooms. The foliage consists of dark green, elliptical leaves with smooth margins and prominent venation. Its woody stems form a shrubby or small tree-like structure that can reach several meters in height.

The plant is characterized by this striking contrast between its vibrant reproductive structures and its dense, verdant canopy.
