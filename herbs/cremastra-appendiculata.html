What's the taxonomical classification of Cremastra appendiculata?

Cremastra appendiculata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following the taxonomic hierarchy further, it falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Orchidaceae. Finally, it is identified by the genus Cremastra, with its specific designation being the species appendiculata.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause allergic reactions such as skin irritation, dermatitis, or swelling if it comes into direct contact with the body. Because it belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, it may contain aristolochic acids which are known to be highly toxic to the kidneys. These specific compounds can lead to severe renal damage or even kidney failure if ingested. There is also a significant risk of carcinogenic effects associated with the chemical constituents found within the plant. Therefore, cautious handling and avoiding any form of consumption are essential to prevent serious health complications.
