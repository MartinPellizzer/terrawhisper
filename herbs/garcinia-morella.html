What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Garcinia morella has an evergreen woody shrub or small tree habit characterized by oppositely arranged, elliptical, coriaceous leaves that possess an entire margin and an acute apex. The foliage typically exhibits a lustrous dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often appearing in clusters. The plant produces globose to subglobose fruits that transition from green to a deep purple or black color upon maturity. These fruits are fleshy and contain multiple seeds embedded within a pulp.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a complex profile of bioactive medicinal compounds including a Garcinol enriched fraction, isoxanthochymol, xanthochymol, α-mangostin, and β-mangostin. These specific phytochemicals are often studied for their significant pharmacological properties such as antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activities. The presence of xanthochymoide derivatives like isoxanthochymol and xanthochymol contributes to the plant's unique chemical signature. Furthermore, the mangostin isomers, specifically α-mangostin and β-mangostin, play critical roles in the plant's therapeutic potential. Together, these substances form a potent medicinal toolkit within the plant's organic structure.

As discussed in a study, the fruit rind of Garcinia morella contains a garcinol enriched fraction (GEF) that exhibits bioactive potential. In high-fat diet (HFD)-fed C57BL/6 mice, administration of this GEF significantly reduced body weight and serum lipids including triglyceride and total cholesterol levels. The treatment also lowered levels of enzymes responsible for liver dysfunction, specifically alkaline phosphatase and aspartate aminotransferase. GEF regulates signaling pathways of lipid metabolism by inhibiting the overexpression of genes involved in de novo lipogenesis. Furthermore, GEF treatment increases gut microbial diversity by reducing pathogenic bacteria and increasing Lactococcus and Streptococcaceae genera. This fraction also increases the expression of intestinal tight junction proteins to maintain gut barrier integrity.
