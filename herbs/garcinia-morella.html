Garciniia morella belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Clusiaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific classification within the genus Garcinia and the species morella.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Garcinia morella has an evergreen woody shrub or small tree habit characterized by oppositely arranged, elliptical, coriaceous leaves that possess an entire margin and an acute apex. The foliage typically exhibits a lustrous dark green upper surface and a slightly paler underside. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, often appearing in clusters. The plant produces globose to subglobose fruits that transition from green to a deep purple or black color upon maturity. These fruits are fleshy and contain multiple seeds embedded within a pulp.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically spanning across parts of India, Myanmar, Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It thrives primarily in humid, lowland rainforest environments where high moisture levels are consistent. Within these ecosystems, the species is often found growing in shaded understory layers or along riverbanks. Its distribution is closely linked to the presence of seasonal monsoon patterns that support its specific growth requirements. Because it requires stable tropical temperatures, it does not extend into temperate or arid zones.

What are the pharmacological activities of Garcinia morella?

This plant has demonstrated significant pharmacological activities including anti-inflammatory, anticancer, antimicrobial, antioxidant, and nonalcoholic fatty liver disease reducing properties. Its bioactive compounds work to neutralize free radicals, which provides a robust antioxidant defense for cellular structures. These constituents also exhibit potent antimicrobial effects by inhibiting the growth of various pathogenic microorganisms. Furthermore, the plant's ability to suppress inflammatory pathways and target malignant cell proliferation highlights its anticancer potential. Research specifically indicates that its extracts can effectively mitigate hepatic steatosis, making it useful for reducing nonalcoholic fatty liver disease.

According to a study published by "Frontiers in pharmacology", Garcinia morella is a medicinal plant used by traditional healers for the treatment of inflammatory disorders. Methanol extracts of the leaf, bark and fruit of Garcinia morella possessed in vitro antioxidant and anticancer properties, with the fruit extract showing the maximum activity. The anticancer activity was confirmed by the in vivo administration of 200 mg/kg of fruit extract for ten days to Dalton's lymphoma induced mice. This fruit extract significantly increased the mean survival time of the animals and decreased the tumor volume. The mechanism of action involves the induction of caspases and DNA fragmentation which ultimately leads to apoptosis.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains a variety of potent bioactive molecules including a Garcinol enriched fraction, isoxanthochymol, xanthochymol, α-mangostin, and β-mangostin. These specific polyphenolic compounds are recognized for their significant biological activities in medicinal research. The xanthochymol derivatives are frequently studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. Additionally, the mangostin isomers contribute to the complex chemical profile that characterizes the plant's therapeutic potential. Together, these substances form a diverse array of phytochemicals used to investigate various pharmacological applications.

According to a study published by "Food research international (Ottawa, Ont.)", the fruit rind of Garcinia morella contains a garcinol enriched fraction (GEF) with bioactive potential. This GEF treatment significantly reduces body weight and serum lipids, specifically triglyceride and total cholesterol levels, in high-fat diet-fed C57BL/6 mice. The compounds also reduce enzymes responsible for liver dysfunction, such as alkaline phosphatase and aspartate aminotransferase. Furthermore, GEF regulates lipid metabolism by inhibiting the overexpression of genes involved in de novo lipogenesis through the PCK1/ACC/SREBP1/FASN pathway. The treatment improves gut health by increasing the expression of intestinal tight junction proteins and increasing the Lactococcus and Streptococcaceae genera.
