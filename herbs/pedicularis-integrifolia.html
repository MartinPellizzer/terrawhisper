Pedicularis integrifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Pedicularis integrifolia has an erect stem reaching up to 40 centimeters in height, topped with a dense, spike-like inflorescence of pink to purple zygomorphic flowers and leaves that are primarily entire or only shallowly lobed. The leaves are typically basal and lanceolate, while the cauline leaves are smaller and sessile along the stem. Each flower features a prominent, hooded upper lip and a protruding lower lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators. The calyx is deeply divided into narrow, linear lobes that extend beyond the corolla. This morphological structure allows the plant to thrive in alpine and subalpine meadow environments.
