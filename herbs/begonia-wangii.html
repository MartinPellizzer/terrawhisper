What's the taxonomical classification of Begonia wangii?

Begonia wangii belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Cucurbitaleales. This plant is a representative of the family Begoniaceae, specifically situated within the genus Begonia, and is identified by the unique species epithet wangii.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Begonia wangii can cause skin irritation, mouth swelling, and gastrointestinal distress if ingested or touched. The plant contains oxalic acid, which acts as a primary irritant to mucous membranes and sensitive tissues. When skin comes into contact with the sap, it may result in redness, itching, or a burning sensation. If a person eats any part of the plant, they might experience intense burning in the mouth and throat. Severe cases of ingestion could lead to significant swelling that complicates swallowing or breathing. Always use gloves when handling this species to prevent accidental dermatological reactions.
