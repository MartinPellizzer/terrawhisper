Red sandwort, scientifically known as Arenaria rubra, is a herb that has been prized for its numerous health benefits, which have been attributed to its high concentration of antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds.

In the kitchen, red sandwort is often used as a flavorful addition to soups, salads, and sauces, while its leaves can be used fresh or dried to make herbal teas. As an ornamental plant, red sandwort is valued for its delicate, pink-tinged flowers and silvery-green foliage, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes. Botanically speaking, red sandwort belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and can be found growing in rocky outcroppings and sandy areas throughout Europe and Asia.

With a long history of use dating back to ancient Greek and Roman times, red sandwort has been documented as a medicinal herb by esteemed authorities such as Dioscorides and Galen.
