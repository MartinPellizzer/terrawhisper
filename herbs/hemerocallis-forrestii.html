Hemerocallis forrestii

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Hemerocallis forrestii has long, linear, bright green leaves that emerge from a basal clump and slender, upright flowering stalks that bear fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. These blossoms typically display a pale yellow or cream coloration with a distinctively darker throat. The floral structure consists of six tepals that taper into delicate, spreading segments. Its root system is composed of thick, fleshy rhizomes that allow the plant to store nutrients and spread through underground expansion. The overall growth habit is clumping, resulting in a dense arrangement of foliage and seasonal blooms.
