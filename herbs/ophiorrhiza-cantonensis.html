Ophiorrhiza cantonensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ophiorrhiza cantonensis has slender, creeping rhizomes that produce opposite, lanceolate to elliptical leaves with entire margins and distinct pinnate venation. The foliage typically exhibits a glossy texture and can reach several centimeters in length depending on the age of the plant. Small, tubular flowers emerge from the leaf axils, often displaying white or pale coloration. These blossoms are characterized by a delicate structure that attracts specific pollinators within its native forest habitat. The plant's overall growth habit is low-lying and spreading, allowing it to colonize the forest floor effectively.
