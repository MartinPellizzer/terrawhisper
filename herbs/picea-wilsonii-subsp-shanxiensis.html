Possible Side Effects of Picea wilsonii subsp. shanxiensis

Picea wilsonii subsp.

shanxiensis can have side effects if used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and potential interactions with medications.

Ingesting large amounts of the plant's extracts can lead to nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea, while skin contact may cause irritation and redness.

The plant's sap can also cause phototoxicity, increasing the risk of skin burns and blisters when exposed to sunlight.

Furthermore, improper use of Picea wilsonii subsp.

shanxiensis may exacerbate existing health conditions, such as kidney or liver disease, due to its potential to accumulate toxic compounds in the body.
