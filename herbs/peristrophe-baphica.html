Peristrophe baphica belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant is part of the order Lamiales and the family Acanthaceae. Finally, its specific classification is defined by the genus Peristrophe and the species baphica.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Peristrophe baphica can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, and neurological impairment if ingested or touched. The plant contains various toxic alkaloids that may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain upon consumption. Direct contact with the plant's sap or foliage can trigger localized dermatitis or allergic skin reactions in sensitive individuals. In higher doses, the chemical compounds may affect the central nervous system, potentially causing dizziness or disorientation. It is essential to handle this species with caution to avoid these adverse physiological effects.
