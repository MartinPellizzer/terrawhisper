Peristrophe baphica

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Peristrophe baphica has a climbing or trailing habit with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that possess entire margins and acute apices. The stems are herbaceous and can become somewhat woody at the base as the plant matures. Its flowers are funnelform and typically feature five fused petals that create a distinct tubular shape. The reproductive structures include a superior ovary that develops into a small, indehiscent fruit. These morphological characteristics allow the plant to effectively navigate through dense vegetation to reach sunlight.
