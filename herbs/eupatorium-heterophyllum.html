Eupatorium heterophyllum

What's the taxonomical classification of Eupatorium heterophyllum?

Eupatorium heterophyllum belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed under the order Asterales. This plant is a representative of the family Asteraceae, falling specifically under the genus Eupatorium, with its unique biological identity defined by the species name heterophyllum.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Eupatorium heterophyllum has leaves that vary significantly in shape and size along its stem, a characteristic known as heterophylly. The upper leaves are typically narrower and more lanceolate, whereas the lower leaves tend to be broader and more ovate. This morphological diversity allows the plant to adapt to changing light levels and water availability within its environment. The stems are sturdy enough to support the weight of these varying foliage types as they grow. Additionally, the arrangement of the leaves can shift from opposite to alternate patterns depending on the maturity of the branch.
