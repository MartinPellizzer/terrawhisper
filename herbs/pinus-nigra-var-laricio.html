Pinus nigra var. laricio

Pinus nigra var. laricio, also known as Corsican pine, is a variant of the black pine tree species native to the Mediterranean region.

The resin of this plant has been traditionally used in medicine due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, which can help to soothe skin irritations and wounds.

The essential oil extracted from the resin has been found to have potential health benefits, including reducing stress and promoting respiratory health.

Herbal preparations of Corsican pine resin include tinctures and essential oils, often used in aromatherapy and topical applications to harness its medicinal properties.
