Aeluropus pungens

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aeluropus pungens has narrow, linear leaves that terminate in sharp, spine-like tips and a stem structure characterized by stiff, upright growth. The foliage is typically dense and possesses a rigid texture that provides a defensive mechanism against herbivores. Its leaves are arranged closely along the stem, often appearing somewhat crowded or whorled in certain sections. The overall habit of the plant is compact and prickly, reflecting its adaptation to harsh or disturbed environments.
