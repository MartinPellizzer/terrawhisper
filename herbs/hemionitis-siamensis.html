What is Hemionitis siamensis?

Hemionitis siamensis is a species of fern in the family Pteridaceae.

It is a rhizomatous fern with a stout rhizome and erect or ascending fronds that are 30-100 cm long, with 3-5 pinnate pinnae and 10-20 pinnules on each pinna. The fronds are dark green on the upper surface and lighter green on the lower surface, with a rachis that is hairy on the lower side. The sorus is terminal or submarginal on the pinnules, with 2-4 sporangia per sorus, and the spores are yellow-brown when mature.

Hemionitis siamensis is native to Southeast Asia, specifically in Thailand, where it grows in shaded, humid forests.
