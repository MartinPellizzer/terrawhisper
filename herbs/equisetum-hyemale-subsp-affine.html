Equisetum hyemale subsp. affine

Equisetum hyemale subsp.

affine, also known as scouring rush, is a type of fern-like plant that belongs to the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hollow, and segmented stems.

Traditionally, the plant has been used in herbal medicine for its medicinal properties, which include antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and diuretic properties.

The health benefits of Equisetum hyemale subsp.

affine include treating urinary tract infections, kidney stones, and skin conditions such as eczema and acne, as well as being used as a natural remedy for arthritis and gout.

Herbal preparations of the plant can be made into teas, infusions, or tinctures, and it can also be dried and used in powdered form for topical applications.
