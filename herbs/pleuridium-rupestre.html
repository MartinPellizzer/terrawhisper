What is Pleuridium rupestre?

Pleuridium rupestre is a species of epiphytic fern in the family Polypodiaceae, native to the Americas.

It has a rhizome with scales and stipes that are brown to black, with lanceolate or ovate sterile leaves that are 0.5-1.5 cm long, and 2-4 pairs of pinnae that are oblong to lanceolate. The fertile leaves are similar in shape but have smaller pinnae and are 0.5-1 cm long, with a sorus on the underside of each pinna. The spores are 0.5-0.8 mm in diameter and have a reticulate perine.

Pleuridium rupestre typically grows on tree trunks and branches in shaded, humid environments.
