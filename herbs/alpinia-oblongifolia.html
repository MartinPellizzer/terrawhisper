Alpinia oblongifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Alpinia oblongifolia?

Alpinia oblongifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is further categorized under the order Zingiberales. As a member of the family Zingiberaceae, this plant is identified by the genus Alpinia, and its specific taxonomic designation is given by the species name oblongifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Alpinia oblongifolia has lanceolate to oblong leaves that are bright green, arranged alternately on upright, fleshy stems, and produce terminal inflorescences featuring white to pale pink flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in dense clumps with rhizomatous roots that allow it to spread through the soil. Each leaf possesses a smooth margin and a distinct midrib that runs the length of the blade. The flowering structures emerge from the top of the stems, often displaying delicate, petal-like bracts. These botanical features collectively define its appearance as a robust member of the Zingiberaceae family.
