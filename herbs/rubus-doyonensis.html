Rubus doyonensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubus doyonensis?

Rubus doyonensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Rosales, it falls into the family Rosaceae. Finally, it is categorized under the genus Rubus, with its specific designation being the species doyonenis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubus doyonensis has arching, deciduous stems that are densely covered in fine, spreading prickles and bear compound leaves typically consisting of three to five leaflets. The leaflets are generally ovate or elliptical with serrated margins and a texture that ranges from smooth to slightly pubescent on both surfaces. During the flowering season, the plant produces small, five-petaled white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils on short pedicels. These blossoms eventually develop into aggregate fruits known as drupelets, which transition from green to a deep red or black color when mature. The overall growth habit is that of a spreading shrub that can form dense thickets in its natural woodland habitat.
