What is Argyrotegium nitidulum?

Argyrotegium nitidulum is a shrub-like plant endemic to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is a member of the tribe Gnaphalieae. Botanically, Argyrotegium nitidulum has leaves that are linear, entire, and glabrous, typically measuring 10-30 mm in length and 0.5-1.5 mm in width. The leaves are arranged oppositely or in whorls on the stem, which is often hairy.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, with florets that are 6-8 mm in diameter.
