Microtoena urticifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Microtoena urticifolia?

Microtoena urticifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is positioned under the order Lamiales. It further falls into the family Lamiaceae, which is part of the broader group of flowering plants. Within this family, it is categorized under the genus Microtoena, and its specific biological identity is defined by its species name, urticifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Microtoena urticifolia has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins that resemble those of the nettle plant. The stems are typically slender and prostrate, allowing the herb to spread across the ground. Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense terminal heads or clusters. These floral structures feature tiny petals and are often characteristic of the Asteraceae family. The plant's overall stature is low-growing, making it a subtle component of its natural habitat.
