Cola lateritia

What's the taxonomical classification of Cola lateritia?

Cola lateritia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Malvales, it is placed within the family Malvaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Cola and is specifically designated as the species lateritia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Cola lateritia has a woody structure characterized by upright stems, elliptical to oblong leaves with entire or slightly wavy margins, and bright red, ovoid fruits. The foliage typically displays a deep green color and grows in an alternate arrangement along the branches. These plants are known for their distinctive reddish-colored seeds which are housed within a fleshy capsule. The branching pattern supports a dense canopy that provides shade in its native tropical habitats. What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforest regions of West and Central Africa, spanning from Guinea and Sierra Leone through Nigeria to the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It thrives primarily in the humid, shaded understory of lowland evergreen forests where moisture levels remain high. Its distribution is closely tied to the specific ecological conditions found within the Guineo-Congolian forest block. Local populations are often found in areas characterized by high annual rainfall and consistent equatorial temperatures. What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

The plant also possesses significant antioxidant properties due to its rich profile of flavonoids and tannins. What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause nausea, insomnia, dizziness, anxiety, tachycardia, and hypertension in individuals who consume it. These adverse reactions are primarily linked to the high concentration of caffeine and other stimulant compounds present in its seeds. Excessive intake may lead to a rapid heartbeat or elevated blood pressure, which can be dangerous for those with underlying cardiovascular issues. Users might also experience neurological symptoms such as restlessness or a persistent feeling of unease. Monitoring the dosage is essential to avoid these uncomfortable and potentially serious physiological responses.
