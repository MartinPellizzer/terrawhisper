Cola lateritia

What's the taxonomical classification of Cola lateritia?

Cola lateritia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its taxonomic descent through the order Malvales, it is placed within the family Malvaceae. Ultimately, this plant is identified by the genus Cola and is specifically designated as the species lateritia.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Cola lateritia contains high concentrations of caffeine, theobromine, and various polyphenolic compounds that serve as its primary medicinal constituents. These alkaloids act as central nervous system stimulants to increase alertness and reduce fatigue. The plant also possesses significant antioxidant properties due to its rich profile of flavonoids and tannins. These chemical components work together to provide mild antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects when utilized in traditional remedies. Such bioactive substances contribute to the plant's role in treating various digestive and neurological ailments.

What are the therapeutic actions of Cola lateritia?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat digestive ailments, respiratory infections, skin inflammations, and various forms of physical pain. The seeds are frequently employed as a traditional remedy to alleviate stomach discomfort and intestinal issues. In addition to digestive support, the plant possesses properties that help manage symptoms of coughs and congestion. It is also applied topically to soothe skin irritations and reduce localized swelling. Many practitioners utilize its components to mitigate headaches and general bodily aches.
