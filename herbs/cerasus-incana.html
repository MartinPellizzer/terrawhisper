Possible Side Effects of Cerasus incana

Cerasus incana can have side effects if used improperly, such as causing digestive issues, nausea, and vomiting due to its high concentration of amygdalin, which releases cyanide when ingested.

Prolonged consumption can also lead to cyanide poisoning, resulting in headaches, dizziness, and even seizures.

Additionally, Cerasus incana may interact with certain medications, such as blood thinners and diabetes medications, which can exacerbate underlying health conditions.

In severe cases, improper use of Cerasus incana can be fatal, highlighting the importance of proper identification and handling.
