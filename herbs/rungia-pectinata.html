What's the taxonomical classification of Rungia pectinata?

Rungia pectinata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic progression continues through the order Lamiales and into the family Acanthaceae. Ultimately, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Rungia, and its unique species designation, pectinata.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rungia pectinata has opposite, sessile, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a decussate pattern along its upright, herbaceous stems. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in a somewhat clustered fashion. These flowers are often pale in color and possess a delicate structure typical of the Rubiaceae family. According to a study published by "Journal of cellular and molecular medicine", Rungia pectinata L. is an ethnomedicinal herb that can effectively relieve the classic symptoms of inflammation. Through an integrated approach, seven inflammation-related active ingredients were identified from 38 candidates, revealing 22 intersecting genes associated with inflammation. The study suggests that the anti-inflammatory effects of Rungia pectinata may be mediated through key pathways involved in both inflammation and cancer.
