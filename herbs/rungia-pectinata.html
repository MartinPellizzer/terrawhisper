Rungia pectinata

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rungia pectinata has opposite, sessile, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a decussate pattern along its upright, herbaceous stems. The foliage is characterized by a smooth margin and a distinctively textured surface that can appear somewhat rugose. Its inflorescence consists of small, tubular flowers that emerge from the leaf axils in a somewhat clustered fashion. These flowers are often pale in color and possess a delicate structure typical of the Rubiaceae family. The overall growth habit is that of a compact, perennial herb suitable for shaded environments.
