Corydalis adunca

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis adunca?

Corydalis adunca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Ranunculales. It is a member of the family Papaveraceae, specifically falling under the genus Corydalis, with its unique scientific designation being the species adunca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis adunca has basal, deeply lobed leaves with several narrow, linear segments and a flowering stem topped by zygomorphic, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or pale violet. The foliage is primarily concentrated at the base of the plant, featuring finely divided margins that give the leaves a delicate, feathery appearance. Each flower possesses a distinct nectar spur extending from the back of the corolla, which serves as a specialized structure for pollinators. The stems are relatively slender and can support multiple blooms during the spring growing season. These plants often emerge from rhizomatous roots that allow them to colonize moist, shaded woodland habitats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal alkaloids coptisine, palmatine, dihydrosanguinarine, protopine, and tetrahydropalmatine. These specific chemical compounds are known for their diverse pharmacological activities in various biological systems. Researchers often study these alkaloids for their potential antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant properties. The presence of these substances contributes significantly to the traditional medicinal uses of the plant. Each compound interacts with different cellular pathways to produce unique therapeutic effects.

As discussed in a study, four alkaloid compounds were isolated from the alcohol extract of the herb of Corydalis adunca. These constituents were isolated using a silica gel column and their structures were elucidated through IR, NMR, and MS data. The four identified compounds are tetrahydropalmatine, 1, 2, 3, 4-tetrahydro-6-methoxy-1-(4'-methoxyphenyl) methyl-7-isoquinolinol, coptisine, and palmaitne. These 4 compounds were isolated from C. adunca for the first time.
