Corydalis adunca

What's the taxonomical classification of Corydalis adunca?

Corydalis adunca belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, this plant is situated in the order Ranunculales. It is a member of the family Papaveraceae, specifically falling under the genus Corydalis, with its unique scientific designation being the species adunca.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Corydalis adunca has basal, deeply lobed leaves with several narrow, linear segments and a flowering stem topped by zygomorphic, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or pale violet. The foliage is primarily concentrated at the base of the plant, featuring finely divided margins that give the leaves a delicate, feathery appearance. Each flower possesses a distinct nectar spur extending from the back of the corolla, which serves as a specialized structure for pollinators. The stems are relatively slender and can support multiple blooms during the spring growing season. These plants often emerge from rhizomatous roots that allow them to colonize moist, shaded woodland habitats.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains several bioactive isoquinoline alkaloids including coptisine, palmatine, protopine, tetrahydrocolumbaamine, and tetrahydropalmatine. These specific medicinal compounds are primarily concentrated within the rhizomes of the species. Researchers study these substances due to their diverse pharmacological potential in various therapeutic applications. The presence of these alkaloids contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine. Understanding the chemical profile of these compounds is essential for evaluating the plant's overall biological activity.

According to a study published by "Zhongguo Zhong yao za zhi = Zhongguo zhongyao zazhi = China journal of Chinese materia medica", four alkaloid compounds were isolated from the alcohol extract of the herb of Corydalis adunca. These isolated constituents were identified as tetrahydropalmatine, 1, 2, 3, 4-tetrahydro-6-methoxy-1-(4'-methoxyphenyl) methyl-7-isoquinolinol, coptisine, and palmatine. The structures of these substances were elucidated using IR, NMR, and MS data. A total of 4 compounds were isolated from Corydalis adunca for the first time.
