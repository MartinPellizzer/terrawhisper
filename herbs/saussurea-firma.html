Saussurea firma

What's the taxonomical classification of Saussurea firma?

Saussurea firma belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Moving through its hierarchical lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic placement continues through the order Asterales and into the family Asteraceae. Finally, the plant is identified by its specific genus, Saussurea, and its unique species designation, firma.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Saussurea firma has a perennial growth habit characterized by a thick, woody taproot and basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves that feature deeply dentate margins. The foliage is typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs which serve to protect the plant from intense high-altitude ultraviolet radiation. During its flowering stage, the plant produces a tall, erect inflorescence consisting of numerous small, yellow flower heads clustered in a compact capitulum. Each individual floret is surrounded by overlapping involucral bracts that create a structured, head-like appearance. The overall architecture of the plant is adapted for survival in rocky, alpine environments where wind and cold are prevalent.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the high-altitude alpine regions of the eastern Tibetan Plateau, specifically within the Sichuan and Yunnan provinces of China. It thrives in specialized microhabitats such as rocky crevices and scree slopes located at elevations typically ranging from 3,000 to 4,500 meters. The distribution is relatively localized due to its strict requirement for cold, montane environments and specific soil compositions. Because it is adapted to these extreme mountain conditions, its range is fragmented across various mountain ranges rather than being continuous. Consequently, the species remains restricted to these high-elevation pockets within its specific geographic territory.
