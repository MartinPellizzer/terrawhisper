Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida) Compounds

The primary active compounds in Chinese Hawthorn include flavonoids, which have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Glycosides contribute to cardiovascular benefits, while tannins and phenolic acids provide astringent and antimicrobial effects. Saponins and coumarins support digestive and anti-inflammatory actions, and terpenoids, along with essential oils, enhance its aromatic and therapeutic properties. Anthocyanins and alkaloids add to its antioxidant and physiological regulatory roles.

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids are key active compounds in Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida). They contribute to the plant's cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory benefits. These compounds help improve blood circulation and reduce oxidative stress. Flavonoids are widely studied for their role in supporting heart health and overall wellness.

2. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida). It contributes to the plant's cardiovascular benefits by improving heart function and regulating blood pressure. Glycosides are also associated with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties in traditional herbal medicine.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and has been studied for its potential effects on digestion and inflammation. Tannins in Chinese Hawthorn may support cardiovascular health and aid in reducing gastrointestinal bleeding.

4. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida). It contributes to the plant's cardiovascular benefits by improving blood flow and reducing oxidative stress. This compound is also associated with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties in traditional herbal medicine.

5. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida). It contributes to the plant's cardiovascular benefits by improving blood circulation and reducing cholesterol. Saponin also exhibits anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating heart conditions and digestive issues.

6. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound found in Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida). It contributes to the plant's cardiovascular and antispasmodic effects. Coumarin helps improve blood circulation and is used in traditional medicine to treat conditions like hypertension and arrhythmia.

7. Terpenoid

Terpenoids are a class of bioactive compounds found in Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida). They contribute to the plant's cardiovascular and anti-inflammatory effects. These compounds help regulate blood pressure and improve heart function, making them key components in traditional herbal formulations.

8. Essential oil

Essential oil is a key active compound in Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida). It is extracted from the flowers and fruits and contains volatile compounds like limonene and geraniol. This oil contributes to the plant's cardiovascular and antispasmodic effects. It is used in traditional medicine to support heart health and relieve stress.

9. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin is a key active compound in Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida). It contributes to the plant's antioxidant properties and is responsible for its characteristic red pigmentation. Found in the fruit and flowers, anthocyanin supports cardiovascular health and may aid in reducing inflammation.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a bioactive compound found in Chinese Hawthorn (Crataegus pinnatifida). It contributes to the plant's cardiovascular effects by influencing heart rate and blood pressure. Alkaloids are typically extracted along with other active components like flavonoids and triterpenoids. They play a key role in the therapeutic properties of this traditional medicinal plant.
