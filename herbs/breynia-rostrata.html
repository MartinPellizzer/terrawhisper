What's the taxonomical classification of Breynia rostrata?

Breynia rostrata belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is situated in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Malpighiales and the family Phyllanthaceae, eventually leading to its placement within the genus Breynia. As the specific identifier, it is formally recognized by the species name rostrata.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

This plant contains the medicinal compounds 1-O-galloyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside, 4"-phenolice-6-O-methylpropanoyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside, 6-O-methylpropanoyl-alpha-D-glucopyranoside, and arbutin. These specific glycosides and phenolic derivatives are characteristic of the chemical profile found within Breynia rostrata. The presence of arbutin is particularly notable for its documented biological activities. These various glucopyranoside derivatives contribute to the complex phytochemical makeup of the species. Such compounds are often studied for their potential therapeutic properties in pharmacological research.

According to a study published by "Yao xue xue bao = Acta pharmaceutica Sinica", chromatography was used to isolate and purify the chemical constituents of Breynia rostrata Merr. Spectral analysis identified 4 glycosides within the plant. These constituents include 6-O-methylpropanoyl-alpha-D-glucopyranoside (1), 4"-phenolica-6-O-methylpropanoyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside (2), 1-O-galloyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside (3), and arbutin (4). Compounds 1 and 2 are characterized as new compounds. Additionally, compounds 3 and 4 were isolated from Breynia rostrata Merr. for the 1st time.
