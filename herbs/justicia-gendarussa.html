Justicia gendarussa

What's the taxonomical classification of Justicia gendarussa?

Justicia gendarussa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is a member of the order Lamiales and is situated within the family Acanthaceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Justicia, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name gendarussa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Justicia gendarussa has opposite, decussate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and an acute apex. The stems are typically square in cross-section, a characteristic common to the Acanthaceae family, and often exhibit a slightly woody base as the plant matures. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes that bear small, zygomorphic flowers with a distinct two-lipped corolla. The floral structure often features a prominent lower lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators. These botanical features collectively define its growth habit as a perennial shrub.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, including countries such as India, Sri Lanka, and parts of China. It thrives primarily in humid environments where moisture is abundant throughout the year. The species is often found growing in the undergrowth of moist forests and along the edges of riverbanks. Because it prefers warm climates, its natural range is limited to areas that do not experience prolonged freezing temperatures. Many populations are concentrated in the hilly regions of the Indian subcontinent.

Justicia gendarussa contains medicinal compounds primarily concentrated in its leaves, though the roots and stems are also utilized in traditional remedies. What are the pharmacological activities of Justicia gendarussa?

This plant has anti-HIV, anti-HIV-1, anti-inflammatory, and antiproliferative pharmacological activities. Specifically, its bioactive compounds demonstrate potent anti-HIV-1 properties by achieving HIV-1 RT inhibition to prevent viral replication. The plant also functions as an anti-inflammatory agent by modulating various immune responses within the body. Furthermore, its antiproliferative effects suggest potential utility in inhibiting the growth of certain cancer cells. These diverse medicinal properties make Justicia gendarussa a significant subject for drug development research.

As discussed in a study, Justicia gendarussa is a medicinal plant used for the treatment of injury, arthritis, and rheumatism in Asia including China. Bioassay-guided fractionation of the methanol extract of the stems and barks of the plant led to the isolation of two anti-HIV compounds, justiprocumins A and B. These arylnaphthalide lignan glycosides are the chemical constituents that contribute to the anti-HIV activity of this plant. Justiprocumin B displayed potent activity against a broad spectrum of HIV strains with IC50 values in the range of 15-21 nM, compared to AZT which had IC50 values of 77-95 nM. The compound also displayed potent inhibitory activity against the NRTI-resistant isolate (HIV-1 1617-1) and the NNRTi-resistant isolate (HIV-1 N119) of the analogues AZT and nevaripine.
