Justicia gendarussa

What's the taxonomical classification of Justicia gendarussa?

Justicia gendarussa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. At the class level, it is categorized as Equisetopsida, falling under the subclass Magnoliidae. The plant is a member of the order Lamiales and is situated within the family Acanthaceae. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Justicia, and its specific biological identity is defined by the species name gendarussa.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Justicia gendarussa has opposite, decussate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape with entire or slightly serrated margins and an acute apex. The stems are typically square in cross-section, a characteristic common to the Acanthaceae family, and often exhibit a slightly woody base as the plant matures. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal spikes that bear small, zygomorphic flowers with a distinct two-lipped corolla. The floral structure often features a prominent lower lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators. These botanical features collectively define its growth habit as a perennial shrub.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, including countries such as India, Sri Lanka, and parts of China. It thrives primarily in humid environments where moisture is abundant throughout the year. The species is often found growing in the undergrowth of moist forests and along the edges of riverbanks. Because it prefers warm climates, its natural range is limited to areas that do not experience prolonged freezing temperatures. Many populations are concentrated in the hilly regions of the Indian subcontinent.

How is this plant cultivated?

This plant, Justicia gendarussa, thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-draining soil and consistent moisture.

It prefers partial shade to full sun and requires a temperature range between 60 and 85 degrees Fahrenheit for optimal growth. Propagation is most commonly achieved through stem cuttings rooted in moist soil or sand. Regular watering is essential to prevent wilting, though the roots should never sit in stagnant water to avoid rot.

Once established, the plant grows into a dense shrub that serves both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

What are the pharmacological activities of Justicia gendarussa?

This plant has potent pharmacological activities including anti-HIV, anti-HIV-1, HIV-1 RT inhibition, anti-inflammatory, and antiproliferative properties. Research indicates that its extracts can effectively interfere with the replication cycle of the virus by targeting specific viral components. Specifically, the plant demonstrates significant HIV-1 RT inhibition, which prevents the reverse transcriptase enzyme from converting viral RNA into DNA. Beyond its antiviral potential, the phytochemicals within the leaves provide strong anti-inflammatory effects to reduce swelling and pain. Furthermore, its antiproliferative activity suggests a capacity to inhibit the growth and spread of various cancer cell lines.

According to a study published by "Phytochemistry", Justicia gendarussa is a medicinal plant used for the treatment of injury, arthritis and rheumatism in Asia including China. Bioassay-guided fractionation of the methanol extract of the stems and barks of the plant led to the isolation of two anti-HIV compounds, justiprocumins A and B. These arylnaphthalide lignans (ANL) glycosides are the chemical constituents that contribute to the anti-HIV activity of the plant. Justiprocumin B displayed potent activity against a broad spectrum of HIV strains with IC50 values in the range of 15-21 nM, compared to AZT which had IC50 values of 77-95 nM. The compound also displayed potent inhibitory activity against the NRTI-resistant isolate (HIV-11617-1) of the analogue AZT and the NNRTi-resistant isolate (HIV-1N119) of the analogue nevaripine.
