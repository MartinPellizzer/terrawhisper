Queen Anne'S Lace (Filipendula ulmaria) Uses

Queen Anne's Lace is used to treat menstrual cramps and disorders, including irregularities. It also addresses skin issues like inflammation, wounds, infections, eczema, and irritation, particularly fungal infections. Its properties help reduce inflammation and support healing in various skin conditions.

Queen Anne'S Lace (Filipendula ulmaria) Monograph

1. Menstrual cramps

Queen Anne's Lace is used to alleviate menstrual cramps due to its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps relax uterine muscles and reduce pain associated with menstruation. Traditionally, it has been prepared as a tea or tincture to support menstrual flow and ease discomfort.

2. Menstrual disorders

Queen Anne's Lace is traditionally used to support menstrual health, addressing irregular cycles and cramping. Its compounds may help regulate hormonal balance and ease uterine tension. It is often prepared as a tea and is considered safe for short-term use in herbal medicine practices.

3. Menstrual irregularities

Queen Anne's Lace is used to support hormonal balance and regulate menstrual cycles. It may help alleviate symptoms of irregular periods, cramping, and premenstrual tension. Its phytoestrogenic properties are believed to influence menstrual flow and timing. It is often recommended as a gentle herbal support for women's reproductive health.

4. Skin inflammation

Queen Anne's Lace has been traditionally used to address skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation, making it useful for conditions like eczema and minor burns. External applications of its infused oils or decoctions can soothe inflamed skin and promote healing.

5. Skin wounds

Queen Anne's Lace has been traditionally used to support skin wound healing due to its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. It may help reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote tissue repair. External applications, such as poultices or infused oils, are commonly used for minor cuts, abrasions, and skin irritations.

6. Inflammatory skin conditions

Queen Anne's Lace has been traditionally used to treat inflammatory skin conditions due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation associated with eczema, psoriasis, and insect bites. Its soothing effects make it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for topical application.

7. Skin infections

Queen Anne's Lace has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial properties. The plant contains compounds that help reduce bacterial growth and inflammation. It can be applied topically as a poultice or infusion to soothe infected areas and promote healing. Its mild nature makes it suitable for sensitive skin.

8. Eczema

Queen Anne's Lace has been traditionally used to support skin health, particularly for eczema. Its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties may help reduce redness, itching, and irritation. External applications of its infused oil or tincture are commonly recommended. It is often combined with other herbs like chickweed or St. John's wort for enhanced effect.

9. Skin irritation

Queen Anne's Lace has been traditionally used to soothe skin irritation due to its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It can help reduce redness, itching, and swelling associated with minor skin conditions. A topical application of a prepared infusion or tincture may provide relief. It is often recommended for eczema, rashes, and insect bites.

10. Fungal infections

Queen Anne's Lace has shown antifungal properties, particularly effective against dermatophytes, which cause fungal infections like athlete's foot and ringworm. Its compounds may inhibit fungal growth, though more research is needed. It is often used topically in herbal preparations for skin infections.
