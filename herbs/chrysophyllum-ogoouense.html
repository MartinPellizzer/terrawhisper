Chrysophyllum ogoouense

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chrysophyllum ogoouense has large, coriaceous leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape, featuring an entire margin and a distinctively revolute edge. The leaf surfaces often exhibit a dark green upper side and a lighter, sometimes pubescent underside. The stems are characterized by a woody structure with bark that can vary in texture depending on the maturity of the tree. Small, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the axils, eventually developing into fleshy fruits typical of the Sapotaceae family. These morphological traits are key indicators used to identify the species within its tropical forest habitat.
