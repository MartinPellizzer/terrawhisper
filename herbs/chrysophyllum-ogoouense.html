Chrysophyllum ogoouense

What's the taxonomical classification of Chrysophyllum ogoouense?

Chrysophyllum ogoouense belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it falls under the order Ericales. This plant is further categorized into the family Sapotaceae, within the genus Chrysophyllum, and is specifically identified by the species name ogoouense.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Chrysophyllum ogoouense contains medicinal compounds primarily found in its bark, leaves, and fruit pulp. The bark is frequently harvested to prepare decoctions used for treating various digestive ailments and inflammatory conditions. Its leaves are often crushed or boiled to create extracts that serve as traditional remedies for skin infections and fever. Additionally, the nutrient-rich fruit pulp provides essential vitamins that support general immune health. These different botanical components are central to the traditional healing practices of communities where the tree grows naturally.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Chrysophyllum ogoouense targets various inflammatory enzymes and oxidative stress mediators, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase (COX) and lipoxygenase (LOX) proteins to reduce prostaglandin synthesis. Its bioactive secondary metabolites, such as polyphenols and flavonoids, also interact with protein kinases to modulate intracellular signaling pathways related to cell proliferation. These phytochemicals can interfere with the activity of certain bacterial enzymes, contributing to the plant's observed antimicrobial properties. Furthermore, the presence of antioxidant compounds allows the plant to interact with reactive oxygen species and related redox-sensitive proteins to mitigate cellular damage. This multi-target pharmacological profile underpins its traditional use in treating inflammation and infectious diseases.

What are the therapeutic actions of Chrysophyllum ogoouense?

Chrysophyllum ogoouense has demonstrated medicinal properties through its use in treating gastrointestinal issues, skin infections, and inflammatory conditions. Chrysophyllum ogoouenne is prepared as decoctions, infusions, or macerations using its various parts including the bark, leaves, and fruit. What side-effects this plant can have?

Chrysophyllum ogoouense can cause allergic reactions such as skin irritation, itching, or swelling if the plant components come into direct contact with sensitive skin. While specific clinical studies on human toxicity are limited, potential adverse effects may include gastrointestinal discomfort if any part of the plant is ingested improperly. Individuals with known sensitivities to the Sapotaceae family should exercise extreme caution when handling its leaves or fruit. Monitoring for unexpected dermatological or digestive responses is recommended during initial exposure. Always consult a medical professional if any symptoms arise after contact or consumption.
