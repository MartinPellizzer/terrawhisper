Dogwood (Cornus florida) Uses

Dogwood is used to treat arthritis and joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory properties. It may support bone health in osteoporosis and help manage diabetes by regulating blood sugar. It also aids in wound healing, reduces skin inflammation, and combats skin infections. Additionally, it is used for hypertension and to boost immune function in cases of immune deficiency.

Dogwood (Cornus florida) Monograph

1. Arthritis

Dogwood (Cornus florida) has been traditionally used to support joint health and alleviate arthritis symptoms. Its anti-inflammatory properties may help reduce pain and swelling. It is often prepared as a tincture or tea and is valued for its gentle action on the body.

2. Joint pain

Cornus florida has been traditionally used to alleviate joint pain due to its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. The bark and roots contain compounds that help reduce swelling and discomfort in conditions like arthritis. It is often prepared as a decoction or tincture for internal use. External applications may also provide relief when applied topically.

3. Osteoporosis

Cornus florida contains compounds that may support bone health by promoting collagen synthesis and reducing inflammation. Preliminary studies suggest it may help prevent bone loss, though more research is needed. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine to support joint and bone integrity.

4. Diabetes

Cornus florida has been traditionally used to support blood sugar regulation. Its berries contain compounds that may enhance insulin sensitivity and reduce glucose absorption. Studies suggest it may help manage symptoms of diabetes when used as part of a holistic treatment plan. It is often combined with other herbs to support metabolic health.

5. Skin infections

Dogwood (Cornus florida) has been traditionally used to treat skin infections due to its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties. The bark and leaves contain compounds that help reduce infection and soothe inflamed skin. It is often prepared as a poultice or decoction for topical application. This makes it a valuable herb in herbal medicine for addressing minor skin infections.

6. Skin inflammation

Cornus florida has been traditionally used to treat skin inflammation due to its anti-inflammatory and astringent properties. It helps reduce redness, swelling, and irritation, making it useful for conditions like eczema and psoriasis. The bark and leaves are often prepared as poultices or infusions to apply directly to affected areas.

7. Wound healing

Cornus florida supports wound healing through its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps reduce swelling, prevent infection, and promote tissue repair. Traditionally applied as a poultice or salve, it is valued for its ability to speed recovery and close wounds more effectively.

8. Hypertension

Cornus florida has been traditionally used to support cardiovascular health. Its bark contains compounds that may help regulate blood pressure. It is often prepared as a tea or tincture. Regular use may assist in managing hypertension when combined with a healthy lifestyle.

9. High blood pressure

Cornus florida, or American Dogwood, has been used traditionally to support cardiovascular health. Its bark contains compounds that may help regulate blood pressure by promoting relaxation of blood vessels. However, it should be used under professional guidance, as it may interact with medications and is not a substitute for medical treatment.

10. Immune deficiency

Cornus florida has been traditionally used to support immune function, particularly in cases of immune deficiency. Its compounds may enhance white blood cell activity and reduce inflammation, aiding the body's defense mechanisms. It is often used in herbal formulations to bolster resistance against infections and support overall immune health.
