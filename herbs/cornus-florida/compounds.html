Dogwood (Cornus florida) Compounds

The primary compounds in Dogwood (Cornus florida) include flavonoids, which have antioxidant properties, and phenolic acids, known for their anti-inflammatory effects. Tannins contribute to astringency, while coumarin has anticoagulant properties. Anthocyanins provide color and may support cardiovascular health. Glycosides, saponins, terpenoids, essential oils, and alkaloids also play roles in the plant's medicinal effects.

Dogwood (Cornus florida) Monograph

1. Flavonoid

Flavonoids in Dogwood (Cornus florida) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects, supporting skin health and wound healing. They also aid in reducing oxidative stress, making them valuable in herbal medicine for their therapeutic potential.

2. Phenolic acid

Phenolic acid is a key active compound in Dogwood (Cornus florida). It contributes to the plant's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, supporting its traditional use in treating ailments like arthritis and digestive issues. This compound is also believed to aid in wound healing and may help regulate blood sugar levels.

3. Tannin

Tannin is a key active compound in Dogwood (Cornus florida). It contributes to the plant's astringent properties and is used in traditional medicine for its ability to reduce inflammation and promote wound healing. Tannins also help in the preservation of the plant's medicinal qualities.

4. Coumarin

Coumarin is a key active compound in Dogwood (Cornus florida). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in treating digestive issues and as a mild sedative. Coumarin also has anticoagulant properties, though its use should be cautious due to potential interactions with medications.

5. Anthocyanin

Anthocyanin, found in Dogwood (Cornus florida), is a flavonoid responsible for the plant's vibrant red and blue pigments. It exhibits antioxidant properties, helping to neutralize free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. This compound supports cardiovascular health and may contribute to the plant's traditional use in treating inflammation and digestive issues.

6. Glycoside

Glycoside is a key active compound in Dogwood (Cornus florida). It contributes to the plant's medicinal properties, including its use in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory support. Glycosides in Dogwood are known for their potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects. These compounds are often extracted and used in herbal formulations.

7. Saponin

Saponin is a key active compound in Dogwood (Cornus florida). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in supporting urinary health and as a diuretic. Saponin's mild cleansing properties are believed to aid in detoxification and may help reduce inflammation.

8. Terpenoid

Terpenoids in Dogwood (Cornus florida) contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, supporting skin health and wound healing. They are often extracted from the bark and used in traditional herbal remedies. Terpenoids help enhance the plant's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

9. Essential oil

The essential oil of Dogwood (Cornus florida) contains volatile compounds like limonene and myrcene. It is used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. The oil is often extracted through steam distillation from the plant's flowers and leaves. It supports its traditional use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.

10. Alkaloid

Alkaloid is a compound found in Dogwood (Cornus florida). It contributes to the plant's traditional use in herbal medicine, though its specific therapeutic effects and mechanisms are not as well-documented as other compounds in the plant.
