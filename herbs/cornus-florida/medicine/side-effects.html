10 Possible Side Effects Of Cornus Florida

Cornus florida has some side effects when used improperly, such as gastrointestinal issues, allergic reactions, and skin irritation.

These side effects can occur due to the plant's high saponin content, which can cause digestive problems.

For example, eating large quantities of the fruit can lead to stomach pain, diarrhea, and vomiting, severely affecting daily life and causing discomfort.

1. Causes allergic reactions

Cornus florida causes allergic reactions when the plant's sap, pollen, or flowers come into contact with human skin.

The allergenic compounds present in Cornus florida can trigger an immune response, leading to symptoms such as redness, itching, swelling, and rashes on the skin.

Additionally, inhaling pollen from this plant may cause respiratory issues like congestion, sneezing, and runny nose in individuals prone to allergies.

2. Triggers asthma attacks

Cornus florida triggers asthma attacks when its pollen is inhaled by individuals allergic to it.

The pollen from this deciduous tree can cause an allergic reaction in some people, leading to inflammation and constriction of the airways, resulting in asthma symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath.

The allergenic properties of Cornus florida's pollen are due to the presence of specific proteins that stimulate an immune response in sensitive individuals.

3. Increases blood pressure

Cornus florida increases blood pressure.

The culprit behind this side effect is believed to be its high concentration of flavonoids and anthocyanins, which can cause vasodilation and stimulate the heart's activity, leading to an increase in blood pressure.

Additionally, Cornus florida may also interact with certain medications, such as beta-blockers, that are used to treat hypertension, further exacerbating the issue.

4. Reduces fertility

Cornus florida reduces fertility in some individuals due to its ability to mimic estrogenic hormones in the body.

The plant's flowers and leaves contain flavonoids and terpenoids, which can bind to estrogen receptors, potentially disrupting hormonal balance and affecting reproductive function.

This effect is more likely to occur when large quantities of the plant are consumed or its extracts are used for extended periods.

5. Stimulates rapid heartbeat

Cornus florida stimulates rapid heartbeat due to its high concentration of cardenolides, a group of compounds similar to digitalis.

These glycosides can increase the heart's contractility and conduction velocity, leading to an accelerated heart rate.

This side effect is more likely to occur when consuming large quantities or using the plant for extended periods, highlighting the importance of responsible use.

6. Causes stomach upset

Cornus florida causes stomach upset.

This is likely due to the presence of saponins and flavonoids in the plant, which are known to have anti-inflammatory properties but can also cause gastrointestinal disturbances when consumed.

Additionally, the seeds of Cornus florida contain a compound called cornin, which has been shown to induce vomiting and diarrhea in some individuals.

7. Induces seizures

Cornus florida induces seizures due to its alkaloids, including cornin and sweroside.

These compounds can interact with the brain's neurotransmitters, disrupting normal electrical activity and increasing the risk of seizure development.

The severity of this side effect may vary depending on individual factors, such as dosage and duration of use, but it is essential to be aware of this potential risk when using Cornus florida.

8. Lowers blood sugar levels

Cornus florida lowers blood sugar levels when consumed due to its ability to stimulate insulin secretion and improve glucose uptake in the body's cells.

The plant contains flavonoids, anthocyanins, and other bioactive compounds that may enhance insulin sensitivity and reduce glucose absorption from the gut.

As a result, individuals taking Cornus florida may experience hypoglycemia, requiring adjustments to their medication or dietary regimen.

9. Increases risk of bleeding

Cornus florida increases risk of bleeding due to its ability to inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.

The compound cornin, a flavonoid glycoside present in the plant's bark and leaves, is responsible for this effect.

When taken orally or used topically, Cornus florida may cause bruising, petechiae, or prolonged bleeding from wounds or surgical sites, particularly in individuals taking anticoagulant medications.

10. Affects liver function

Cornus florida affects liver function due to its potential interaction with certain medications.

The flavonoids and iridoids present in the plant may bind to liver enzymes, altering their activity and leading to changes in liver metabolism.

This can result in altered levels of medications being processed or eliminated from the body, potentially causing adverse effects on overall liver health.
