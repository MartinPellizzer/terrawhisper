What's the taxonomical classification of Didelotia minutiflora?

Didelotia minutiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is placed in the order Fabales. This plant is a member of the family Fabaceae, falling under the genus Didelotia, with its specific identification being the species minutiflora.

        What side-effects this plant can have?

        Didelotia minutiflora can cause severe gastrointestinal distress, allergic skin reactions, and potential neurological impairment if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains unidentified bioactive compounds that may trigger intense nausea, vomiting, or abdominal cramping in sensitive individuals. Contact with the leaves or stem can result in localized dermatitis, characterized by redness, itching, or blistering of the skin. Some users have reported dizziness and lightheadedness following accidental exposure to its concentrated extracts. Because clinical studies are limited, the full spectrum of its toxicity remains poorly understood by medical professionals.
