Derris scabricaulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris scabricaulis?

Derris scabricaulis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical progression, it is placed in the order Fabales and belongs to the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Derris, and its unique species designation, scabricaulis.

What parts of this plant are used medicinally?

Derris scabricaulis contains medicinal compounds primarily extracted from its roots and rhizomes. These underground structures are harvested to obtain various bioactive substances used in traditional healing practices. The plant is often utilized in folk medicine to treat skin ailments and inflammatory conditions. Some studies suggest that extracts from these parts possess insecticidal and antiparasitic properties. Researchers continue to investigate these specific components to better understand their therapeutic potential in modern pharmacology.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Derris scabricaulis targets various inflammatory signaling proteins, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) to exert its therapeutic effects. These bioactive compounds modulate the expression of these enzymes and cytokines to reduce oxidative stress and systemic inflammation. The plant's secondary metabolites also interact with the nuclear factor kappa B (NF-kB) signaling pathway to suppress the production of pro-inflammatory mediators. Such interactions contribute to its documented medicinal utility in treating pain and inflammatory disorders. By modulating these specific protein pathways, the plant provides a biochemical basis for its pharmacological activity.

What are the therapeutic actions of Derris scabricaulis?

Derris scabricaulis has potent insecticidal, anthelmintic, and antimicrobial properties primarily derived from its high concentration of rotenoids. These bioactive compounds act by inhibiting cellular respiration in pests and parasitic worms, making the plant an effective natural pesticide and deworming agent. In traditional medicine, extracts are often utilized to treat various skin infections and fungal growths due to their ability to disrupt microbial cell membranes. Some studies also suggest potential anti-inflammatory effects that may assist in managing localized swelling. However, the high toxicity of these alkaloids requires careful dosage to avoid harmful effects on humans and non-target organisms.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various skin infections, parasitic infestations, and intestinal ailments through its potent insecticidal and anthelmintic properties. The active compound rotenone found within the roots acts as a powerful agent to eliminate intestinal worms and other parasites. It has also been traditionally applied topically to address fungal skin diseases and various insect-related afflictions. Some cultures utilize decoctions of the plant to manage symptoms associated with internal parasitic loads. These medicinal applications rely heavily on the plant's ability to disrupt the biological processes of target organisms.

What are the herbal preparations of Derris scabricaulis?

Derris scabricaulis is prepared primarily as a decoction of its dried roots to extract active rotanones for medicinal use. This plant is frequently processed into concentrated extracts that are then used in topical applications to treat various skin conditions. Some traditional preparations involve soaking the root material in alcohol to create a tincture for more potent therapeutic effects. These liquid forms are often preferred because they allow for standardized dosing of the plant's chemical constituents. The various preparations must be carefully handled to ensure the stability of its insecticidal properties.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Derris scabricaulis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of rotenone. Contact with the raw plant material may lead to dermatitis or localized swelling in sensitive individuals. When consumed, the active compounds can act as a potent neurotoxin that affects the central nervous system. Symptoms of poisoning may include tremors, loss of coordination, or respiratory distress. It is essential to handle this plant with caution to avoid accidental exposure or ingestion.
