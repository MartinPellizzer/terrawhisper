Derris scabricaulis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Derris scabricaulis has a climbing woody vine structure characterized by pinnately compound leaves, trifoliate leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers. The leaves typically feature three leaflets that are elliptic or ovate in shape with smooth or slightly serrated margins. Its stems are flexible enough to scramble through forest undergrowth using tendrils for support. The inflorescence consists of small clusters of flowers that usually appear in shades of white, cream, or pale yellow. These botanical features distinguish the species within the Fabaceae family.

What are the molecular targets of this plant?

Derris scabricaulis targets various inflammatory signaling proteins, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) to exert its therapeutic effects. These bioactive compounds modulate the expression of these enzymes and cytokines to reduce oxidative stress and systemic inflammation. The plant's secondary metabolites also interact with the nuclear factor kappa B (NF-kB) signaling pathway to suppress the production of pro-inflammatory mediators. Such interactions contribute to its documented medicinal utility in treating pain and inflammatory disorders. By modulating these specific protein pathways, the plant provides a biochemical basis for its pharmacological activity.

What are the therapeutic actions of Derris scabricaulis?

Derris scabricaulis has potent anthelmintic, insecticidal, and acaricide properties primarily due to the presence of the bioactive compound rotenone. This plant is traditionally used to expel parasitic worms from the digestive tracts of livestock and humans. Its chemical constituents also serve as a powerful insecticide that can disrupt the nervous systems of various harmful pests. Additionally, the extract functions as an effective acaricide to combat infestations of mites and ticks. These diverse therapeutic actions make it a significant resource in both ethnobotanical practices and natural pest management.

      What side effects this plant can have?

      This plant can cause severe neurotoxicity, skin irritation, and potential respiratory distress if its active rotenone compounds are ingested or inhaled. The primary danger lies in the inhibition of mitochondrial respiration, which can lead to systemic cellular damage in humans. Contact with the plant material may result in localized redness, itching, or chemical burns on sensitive skin. If accidental ingestion occurs, symptoms might include nausea, vomiting, or more serious neurological impairments. Because it is a potent natural pesticide, even small doses can be hazardous to both humans and local wildlife.
