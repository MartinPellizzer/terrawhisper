Derris scabricaulis

What's the taxonomical classification of Derris scabricaulis?

Derris scabricaulis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Following its hierarchical progression, it is placed in the order Fabales and belongs to the family Fabaceae. Finally, the plant is defined by its specific genus, Derris, and its unique species designation, scabricaulis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Derris scabricaulis has a climbing woody vine structure characterized by pinnately compound leaves, trifoliate leaflets, and small, pea-like flowers.

The leaves typically feature three leaflets that are elliptic or ovate in shape with smooth or slightly serrated margins. Its stems are flexible enough to scramble through forest undergrowth using tendrils for support. The inflorescence consists of small clusters of flowers that usually appear in shades of white, cream, or pale yellow.

These botanical features distinguish the species within the Fabaceae family.

Derris scabricaulis targets various inflammatory signaling proteins, specifically inhibiting cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2), inducible nitric oxide synthase (iNOS), and tumor necrosis factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) to exert its therapeutic effects. What are the therapeutic actions of Derris scabricaulis?

Derris scabricaulis has potent anthelmintic, insecticidal, and acaricide properties primarily due to the presence of the bioactive compound rotenone.

This plant is traditionally used to expel parasitic worms from the digestive tracts of livestock and humans. Its chemical constituents also serve as a powerful insecticide that can disrupt the nervous systems of various harmful pests. Additionally, the extract functions as an effective acaricide to combat infestations of mites and ticks.

These diverse therapeutic actions make it a significant resource in both ethnobotanical practices and natural pest management.

    What are the herbal preparations of Derris scabricaulis?

    Derris scabricaulis is prepared primarily as a decoction of its dried roots to extract active rotanones for medicinal use. This plant is frequently processed into concentrated extracts that are then used in topical applications to treat various skin conditions. Some traditional preparations involve soaking the root material in alcohol to create a tincture for more potent therapeutic effects. These liquid forms are often preferred because they allow for standardized dosing of the plant's chemical constituents. The various preparations must be carefully handled to ensure the stability of its insecticidal properties.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Derris scabricaulis can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and potential toxicity if ingested due to the presence of rotenone. Contact with the raw plant material may lead to dermatitis or localized swelling in sensitive individuals. When consumed, the active compounds can act as a potent neurotoxin that affects the central nervous system. Symptoms of poisoning may include tremors, loss of coordination, or respiratory distress. It is essential to handle this plant with caution to avoid accidental exposure or ingestion.
