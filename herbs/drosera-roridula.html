Drosera Roridula

Drosera roridula, commonly known as the "bald lady," is a carnivorous plant native to South Africa, which has been traditionally used for its potential medicinal properties.

Historically, it has been employed in folk medicine to treat ailments such as inflammation, wounds, and digestive issues due to its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and tannins, which may contribute to its therapeutic benefits. However, scientific research on its medicinal applications remains limited, and its use is not widely supported by modern pharmacological studies.

Despite this, some traditional healers continue to utilize Drosera roridula for its purported healing properties, highlighting the need for further investigation into its potential health benefits.
