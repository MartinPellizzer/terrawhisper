Passiflora papilio

What's the taxonomical classification of Passiflora papilio?

Passiflora papiilio belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows a specific evolutionary lineage that leads it to the order Malpighiales. The plant is a representative of the family Passifloraceae, falling under the genus Passiflora, and is specifically identified by its species name, papiilio.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Passiflora papilio contains a complex mixture of alkaloids, flavonoids, and various phenolic compounds that serve as its primary bioactive agents. These chemical constituents are thought to interact with the central nervous system to provide sedative and anxiolytic effects. Specifically, the presence of certain flavones may help modulate GABA receptors in the brain to reduce anxiety. Additionally, the plant's antioxidant properties are attributed to its high concentration of polyphenols. Researchers continue to study these specific molecules to better understand their potential therapeutic applications in herbal medicine.

What are the therapeutic actions of Passiflora papilio?

Passiflora papilio has sedative, anxiolytic, and antispasmodic properties that primarily act on the central nervous system to reduce anxiety and promote sleep. These therapeutic effects are largely attributed to the presence of alkaloids and flavonoids which interact with GABA receptors in the brain. By increasing the inhibitory effects of gamma-aminobutyric acid, the plant helps to calm overactive neural signaling. This mechanism makes it useful for managing restlessness, insomnia, and mild nervous tension. Additionally, its antispasmodic nature can help relax smooth muscle tissues throughout the body.

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat anxiety, insomnia, nervousness, and various forms of mild depression. Its sedative properties help calm the central nervous system to facilitate better sleep patterns. People often consume preparations of the plant to manage stress and emotional agitation. It serves as a natural remedy for calming restlessness and soothing irritability. This botanical species is valued for its ability to reduce tension without causing severe lethargy.

What are the herbal preparations of Passiflora papilio?

Passiflora papilio is prepared as an aqueous infusion, a decoction of the dried aerial parts, or a concentrated tincture using ethanol. The infusion method involves steeping the leaves and flowers in hot water to extract soluble alkaloids and flavonoids. For stronger medicinal applications, a decoction is used to boil the more fibrous stems and roots to release denser compounds. Tinctures provide a shelf-stable option by utilizing alcohol to pull out bioactive constituents that water cannot dissolve alone. These various forms allow for different dosages and absorption rates depending on the intended therapeutic effect.

What side-effects this plant can have?

Passiflora papilio can cause drowsiness, dizziness, and potential confusion when consumed due to its sedative properties. These physiological effects occur because the plant contains alkaloids and flavonoids that influence the central nervous system. Individuals may experience lowered blood pressure or slowed heart rates if they use the plant in excessive amounts. It is also important to note that pregnant or breastfeeding women should avoid it to prevent potential uterine contractions. Combining this plant with other depressants like alcohol or sleeping medications can dangerously intensify these side effects.
