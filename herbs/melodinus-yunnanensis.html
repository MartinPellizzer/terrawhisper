Melodinus yunnanensis

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Melodinus yunnanensis has a perennial herbaceous habit characterized by slender, erect stems and pinnately compound leaves that typically feature several pairs of leaflets. The foliage consists of small, ovate to elliptic leaflets with entire or slightly serrated margins and distinct venation. Its inflorescence is composed of small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in compact clusters or racemes. The reproductive structures include delicate petals and functional stamens that are adapted to its specific ecological niche. The root system is typically fibrous, helping to anchor the plant within its montane habitat.

      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Melodinus yunnanensis can cause localized skin irritation, gastrointestinal distress, and mild neurological symptoms such as dizziness or nausea upon exposure. Contact with the plant's sap may result in redness, itching, or a burning sensation on the epidermis. If the plant is ingested, individuals might experience abdominal cramping, vomiting, or diarrhea. Some cases have also reported lightheadedness or a temporary loss of coordination following accidental consumption. Due to these unpredictable reactions, careful handling is essential to prevent adverse health outcomes.
