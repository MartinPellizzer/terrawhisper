Mexican Marigold (Tagetes Maxima)

Information Reliability Score: 2/10

This score reflects the overall reliability of the information presented in this article. It is based on the quality of scientific evidence, accuracy of sources, and the transparency of references related to Tagetes maxima.

Mexican marigold, scientifically known as Tagetes maxima, is a vibrant medicinal herb prized for its bright orange flowers and numerous health benefits. It is widely recognized as a powerful adaptogen and natural remedy, valued for its ability to support the body's resilience against stress and illness. The primary benefits of Mexican marigold include its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant properties, which contribute to its use in treating ailments like respiratory infections, skin conditions, and digestive issues. Traditionally, it has been used in Mexican and Central American cultures to make teas, tinctures, and poultices for healing wounds and easing cold symptoms. In modern wellness practices, it is incorporated into herbal supplements and natural skincare products, and its unique compound, such as its high content of flavonoids and essential oils, continues to attract scientific interest for potential therapeutic applications.
