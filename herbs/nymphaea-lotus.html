Nymphaea lotus

What's the taxonomical classification of Nymphaea lotus?

Nymphaea lotus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Nymphaeales and the family Nymphaeaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Nymphaea, specifically identified by its species name, lotus.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

Nymphaea lotus contains various bioactive alkaloids, flavonoids, and terpenoids that serve as the basis for its medicinal properties. These specific chemical constituents provide the plant with significant sedative and analgesic effects used in traditional healing. Research indicates that the presence of isoquinoline alkaloids can help soothe the central nervous system. Additionally, the antioxidant properties of its flavonoid content aid in reducing oxidative stress within biological tissues. These diverse compounds allow the lotus to be utilized in treating various ailments ranging from insomnia to inflammation.

What are the therapeutic actions of Nymphaea lotus?

What diseases this plant treats?

This plant is used to treat various ailments including inflammation, digestive issues, skin infections, and menstrual irregularities. The roots and rhizomes are often employed in traditional medicine to soothe stomach pains and improve overall gastrointestinal function. Its topical applications are frequently utilized to address skin irritations and wounds due to its soothing properties. Additionally, certain preparations are believed to help regulate hormonal balance and ease discomfort during menstruation. These medicinal uses are deeply rooted in historical herbal practices across several different cultures.
