Nymphaea lotus

What's the taxonomical classification of Nymphaea lotus?

Nymphaea lotus belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this lineage, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Its taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Nymphaeales and the family Nymphaeaceae. Finally, the plant is situated within the genus Nymphaea, specifically identified by its species name, lotus.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Nymphaea lotus has large, circular, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, featuring a deep notch where the petiole attaches. These leaves are typically bright green with a smooth, waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant produces showy, solitary flowers that rise slightly above the water level on long, sturdy stalks. These blossoms consist of numerous white petals arranged in multiple layers around a central cluster of yellow stamens. Below the surface, a thick, tuberous rhizome anchors the plant firmly into the muddy substrate of its aquatic habitat.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, ranging from Egypt and Sudan in the north down to South Africa. It is commonly found in slow-moving or stagnant freshwater habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of lakes. The species thrives in various climates throughout the Nile River basin and across much of the African continent. Its distribution is closely tied to the availability of shallow, warm water environments. Many populations also extend into parts of the Arabian Peninsula where suitable aquatic conditions exist.

What are the pharmacological activities of Nymphaea lotus?

This plant has diverse pharmacological activities including anti-aging, antioxidant, neuroprotective, protective role against tissue damages, and treatment of renal damage. Its potent antioxidant properties help neutralize free radicals, which directly contributes to its observed anti-aging effects. These chemical constituents also provide a neuroprotective shield that helps preserve brain function and cellular integrity. Furthermore, the plant demonstrates a significant protective role against tissue damages throughout various organ systems. These therapeutic mechanisms are particularly effective in the treatment of renal damage by mitigating oxidative stress in the kidneys.

As discussed in a study, the medicinal plant Nymphaea lotus contains two most prominent flavonoids, Quercetiin-3-O-rhamnoside (Que-3-Rha) and kaempferol-3-O-galactoside (Kae-3-Gal), found in its stamens and perianth extracts. These flavonoids demonstrate moderate to good inhibitory activity toward skin-aging enzymes including collagenase, elastase, and tyrosinase. Molecular docking analysis showed that both Que-3-Rha and Kae-3-Gal possessed superior binding affinity to these target enzymes when compared to positive controls. Computational predictions indicated favorable skin permeability and no severe toxicity for these two compounds. Furthermore, molecular dynamic simulations revealed that all complexes remained stable throughout a 200 ns MD simulation. These findings support the anti-aging potential of the flavonoids occurring in Nymphaea lotus for cosmeceutical or phytopharmaceutical applications.

What medicinal compounds this plant contains?

The inclusion of β-sitosterol further enhances the therapeutic potential of these plant extracts.

As discussed in a study, Nymphaea lotus is a potential plant in the Nymphaeaceae family that is recognized as an economic and traditional medicinal plant. The research investigated the flavonoid profiles and antioxidant potential of 13 populations collected from every floristic region. Flavonoids were identified as the main phytochemical class found in both the stamen and perianth extracts of the plant. The stamen offers higher flavonoids and richer antioxidant potential compared with the perianth. This study provided the first completed report at the population level to describe the significant correlation between phytochemical profiles in floral parts and the main antioxidant activity.

What side effects this plant can have?

This plant can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and digestive distress if consumed or handled improperly. Some individuals may experience redness or itching upon direct contact with the leaves or stems. Ingesting parts of the plant might lead to nausea or abdominal discomfort in sensitive people. There is also a risk of unexpected systemic reactions if the plant is used in unregulated medicinal preparations. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid contact with those who have known botanical sensitivities.
