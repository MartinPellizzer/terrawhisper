What's the taxonomical classification of Ilex bioritsensis?

Ilex bioritsensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is categorized under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further classified into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Aquifoliales. This species is a member of the family Aquifoliaceae and is positioned within the genus Ilex, ultimately reaching its specific taxonomic designation as the species bioritsensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Ilex bioritsensis has small, coriaceous, elliptical to oblong leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and noticeably paler underneath. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly serrated, providing a distinct texture to the foliage. These evergreen leaves are arranged oppositely or sub-oppositely along woody, slender stems. The plant often exhibits a shrubby growth habit with compact branching patterns. Tiny, inconspicuous flowers emerge from the leaf axils during the blooming season.
