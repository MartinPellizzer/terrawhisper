Damnacanthus macrophyllus

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Damnacanthus macrophyllus has large, lanceolate leaves that can reach lengths of up to thirty centimeters and feature prominent, parallel venation. The leaf margins are typically entire and possess a slightly leathery texture that aids in moisture retention. Its stems are robust and erect, providing structural support for the heavy foliage. The plant also produces clusters of small, actinomorphic flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. These floral structures are characterized by a distinct color pattern that contrasts with the dark green of the vegetative parts.
