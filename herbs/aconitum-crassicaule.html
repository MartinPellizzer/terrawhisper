Aconitum crassicaule

What's the taxonomical classification of Aconitum crassicaule?

Aconitum crassicaule belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. It is a member of the class Equisetopsida and falls under the subclass Magnoliidae. The taxonomic hierarchy continues through the order Ranunculales and the family Ranunculaceae. Finally, the plant is categorized under the genus Aconitum, specifically identified as the species crassicaule.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Aconitum crassicaule has thick, fleshy stems, deeply lobed basal leaves, and zygomorphic flowers characterized by a prominent helmet-shaped upper sepal. The plant's robust foliage consists of palmately divided leaflets that provide a dense ground cover. Its inflorescence features clusters of blue to violet flowers that emerge from the upper leaf axils. The sepals form a protective hood over the reproductive organs, a defining trait of the genus. Each flower is supported by a sturdy pedicel that helps maintain its upright position during blooming.

    What side effects this plant can have?

Ingestion of its toxic compounds leads to rapid neurological and cardiovascular distress. Initial symptoms often involve gastrointestinal upset and sensory disturbances such as numbness and tingling. As the toxins progress, they can trigger life-threatening heart rhythms like arrhythmia or a dangerous slowing of the heart known as bradycardia. Extreme cases may culminate in convulsions, total paralysis, or fatal respiratory failure and cardiac arrest.
