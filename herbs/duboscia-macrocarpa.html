Duboscia macrocarpa

What's the taxonomical classification of Duboscia macrocarpa?

Duboscia macrocarpa belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida, it is further organized into the subclass Magnoliidae and the order Malvales. Its taxonomic lineage continues through the family Malvaceae, where it is situated within the genus Duboscia, eventually reaching its specific designation as the species macrocarpa.

    What are the herbal preparations of Duboscia macrocarpa?

    Duboscia macrocarpa is prepared as a concentrated liquid extract, a decoction made from the bark, or a topical ointment derived from the crushed leaves. These various methods allow the active chemical constituents to be extracted efficiently for different therapeutic uses. When used as a decoction, the plant material is boiled in water to release its potent alkaloids and tannins. Liquid extracts are often more common for standardized dosing in medicinal applications. Topical preparations utilize the plant's properties to treat localized skin conditions through direct application.

    What side-effects this plant can have?

    Duboscia macrocarpa can cause adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal distress, skin irritation, or allergic responses if ingested or applied topically without proper preparation. Because the specific chemical composition of its secondary metabolites is not fully documented for human consumption, potential toxicity remains a concern. Some researchers suggest that concentrated extracts might interfere with metabolic processes if used in excessive amounts. Individuals with sensitive skin may also experience contact dermatitis upon touching the plant's tissues. It is essential to exercise caution and avoid self-medicating with this species until clinical safety profiles are established.
