Duboscia macrocarpa

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Duboscia macrocarpa has a woody tree structure characterized by large, leathery, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and possess prominent longitudinal veins. The foliage is arranged alternately along the branches, providing a dense canopy for the plant. Its most distinguishing feature is the production of large, woody, globose fruits that contain numerous seeds. These fruits are often heavy and persistent, hanging from the limbs after flowering. The bark is generally rough and textured, helping to protect the trunk from environmental stressors.

What is the geographical distribution of this plant?

This plant is native to the tropical rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia, specifically ranging from the Cook District to the Wet Tropics region. It is primarily found in lowland areas where high humidity and consistent rainfall support its growth. Within these ecosystems, it often inhabits the canopy layers of dense subtropical forests. The species' distribution is highly localized due to its specific reliance on these stable, moist environments. Consequently, it does not occur naturally in the drier or more temperate regions of the continent.
