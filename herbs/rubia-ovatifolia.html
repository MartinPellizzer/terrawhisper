Rubia ovatifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Rubia ovatifolia?

Rubia ovatifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. As a member of the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it follows the biological lineage of the order Gentianales. This plant is a representative of the family Rubiaceae, which is characterized by its distinct botanical features. Within this family, it is placed in the genus Rubia, and its specific identification is defined by its species name, ovatifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Rubia ovatifolia has creeping, rhizomatous stems that produce opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire margins and acute to acuminate tips. The foliage typically measures between 2 and 6 centimeters in length and features a smooth, somewhat leathery texture. Small, tubular, four-lobed flowers emerge from the leaf axils in clusters, presenting a white to pale pink coloration. These blossoms transition into small, fleshy drupes that change from green to red or dark purple as they mature. The plant's overall structure is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit which allows it to form dense mats across the forest floor.
