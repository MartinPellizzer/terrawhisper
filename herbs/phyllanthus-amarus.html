As a horticultural plant, phyllanthus is known for its adaptability to different environments and climates, making it an easy crop to cultivate. From a botanical standpoint, phyllanthus belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and distinctive fruit capsules.

With roots dating back thousands of years to ancient Indian and Chinese medicine, phyllanthus has been revered for its medicinal properties and continues to be an important herb in many traditional healing practices today.

What are the side effects of Phyllanthus amarus when used improperly?

Furthermore, Phyllanthus amarus may exacerbate pre-existing medical conditions, such as kidney disease, liver problems, or pregnancy complications. In severe cases, improper use can cause more serious adverse effects, including anemia, decreased white blood cell count, and even acute liver damage.

It is essential to consult with a healthcare professional before using Phyllanthus amarus for medicinal purposes to minimize the risk of these unwanted side effects.
