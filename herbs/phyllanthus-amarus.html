False Leaf

Phyllanthus amarus

False Leaf (Phyllanthus amarus) is a small, erect shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions, known for its traditional use in treating liver disorders and other health conditions.

Botanical Identification

False Leaf, scientifically known as Phyllanthus amarus, belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae. Commonly referred to as "False Leaf" in English, it is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant has alternate, opposite leaves and small yellow flowers. It is also known as "Khadir" in India and "Pangka" in Indonesia.

The following list summarize the taxonomy of plant:

  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Magnoliophyta
  • Class: Magnoliopsida
  • Order: Malpighiales
  • Family: Euphorbiaceae
  • Genus: Phyllanthus
  • Species: Phyllanthus amarus

Active Compounds

The main active compounds of False Leaf include flavonoids, phenolic acids, and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal properties. These compounds work synergistically to provide anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial effects. Their combined action enhances the herb's therapeutic potential in herbal medicine.

Preparation Methods

Decoction: Used for treating liver disorders and gastrointestinal issues.

Extract: Used for managing diabetes and liver health.

Powder: Used for digestive issues and as a general tonic.

Safety Profile

False Leaf (Phyllanthus amarus) should be used with caution during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to limited safety data. It may interact with medications metabolized by the liver, so consulting a healthcare provider is recommended. Always follow dosage guidelines to avoid potential adverse effects.
