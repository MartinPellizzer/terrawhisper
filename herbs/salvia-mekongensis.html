Salvia mekongensis

What's the taxonomical classification of Salvia mekongensis?

Salvia mekongensis belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, it is situated in the order Lamiales. This species is a member of the family Lamiaceae and is specifically categorized under the genus Salvia, with its unique designation being the species mekongensis.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Salvia mekongensis has erect, square-shaped stems that reach heights of up to one meter and bear opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a textured, rugose surface. The foliage is characterized by a distinct grayish-green hue and a somewhat pubescent texture that covers the leaf blades. Its inflorescences consist of dense, terminal spikes that feature tubular flowers ranging in color from pale blue to light violet. These floral structures are supported by bracts that often exhibit subtle pigmentation, aiding in the protection of the developing buds. The plant's overall architecture reflects a woody base that transitions into herbaceous upper growth.
