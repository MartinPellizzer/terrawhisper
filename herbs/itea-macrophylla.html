Itea macrophylla

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Itea macrophylla has an upright, deciduous shrub habit characterized by large, elliptical to oblong leaves that typically measure between 5 and 15 centimeters in length. The foliage features finely serrated margins and a glossy green upper surface that transitions to a lighter shade underneath. During the flowering season, the plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant blossoms that emerge from the axils of the leaves. The woody stems are slender and can grow several meters in height, forming a dense thicket as the plant matures. Following the bloom, small, berry-like fruits develop along the branches, contributing to its overall structural complexity.
