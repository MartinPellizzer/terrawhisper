What is Chloropetalum obtusiflorum?

Chloropetalum obtusiflorum is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, glabrous, and branching habit, with leaves that are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, measuring 1-3.5 cm in length and 0.5-1.5 cm in width. The leaves have a cordate base and an obtuse apex, with a leathery texture and a glabrous surface. Chloropetalum obtusiflorum produces white, fragrant flowers in an axillary panicle, with a tubular corolla and a 5-lobed limb, and a fruit that is a subglobose capsule containing 4-6 seeds.

The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Chile.
