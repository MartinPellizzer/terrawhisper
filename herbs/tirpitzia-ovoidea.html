      What side-effects this plant can have?

      Tirpitzia ovoidea can cause severe contact dermatitis, gastrointestinal distress, and respiratory irritation if ingested or handled improperly. The plant contains specialized alkaloids that trigger inflammatory responses upon skin contact. If small amounts are consumed, individuals may experience acute abdominal cramping and nausea. Inhaling airborne particles from the plant's pollen or crushed leaves can lead to coughing and shortness of breath. Precautionary measures such as wearing gloves and avoiding direct inhalation are necessary when working near this species.
