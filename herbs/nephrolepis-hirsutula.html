Nephrolepis hirsutula

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Neprolepis hirsutula has long, narrow, lanceolate fronds that are arranged in a loose rosette and feature pinnate divisions with distinctively hairy margins. The pinnules are typically oblong and taper toward the tip, often displaying a slightly wavy or undulating edge. Small, fine hairs are distributed along the edges of the leaflets, which gives the plant its specific species name. The stipe, or leaf stalk, is often brownish and can be covered in scales or fine pubescence near the base. These fronds emerge from a creeping rhizome that helps the fern spread through its natural habitat.
