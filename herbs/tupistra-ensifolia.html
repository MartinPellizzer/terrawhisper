Tupistra ensifolia

What's the taxonomical classification of Tupistra ensifolia?

Tupistra ensifolia belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified under the phylum Streptophyta. Within this group, it is categorized under the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae. Moving down the taxonomic hierarchy, the plant falls under the order Asparagales and is a member of the family Asparagaceae. Finally, it is placed within the genus Tupistra, specifically identified by the species name ensifolia.

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tupistra ensifolia has lanceolate, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and arranged in a basal rosette. These leaves possess prominent parallel venation and can reach lengths of up to thirty centimeters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from a short inflorescence. Its stems are relatively short, keeping the foliage close to the forest floor in its natural habitat. The overall structure is characteristic of an understory herb adapted to shaded environments.
