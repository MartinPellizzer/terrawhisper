Tupistra ensifolia

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Tupistra ensifolia has lanceolate, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and arranged in a basal rosette. These leaves possess prominent parallel venation and can reach lengths of up to thirty centimeters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from a short inflorescence. Its stems are relatively short, keeping the foliage close to the forest floor in its natural habitat. The overall structure is characteristic of an understory herb adapted to shaded environments.

This plant is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, specifically occurring in countries such as Malaysia, Thailand, and Indonesia.
