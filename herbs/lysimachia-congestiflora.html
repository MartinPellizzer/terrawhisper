Lysimachia congestiflora

What's the taxonomical classification of Lysimachia congestiflora?

Lysimachia congestiflora belongs to the kingdom Plantae and is classified within the phylum Streptophyta. Following its taxonomic hierarchy, it is placed in the class Equisetopsida and the subclass Magnoliidae, falling under the order Ericales. The plant is a member of the family Primulaceae, which contains the genus Lysimachia, and is specifically identified by its species name, congestiflora.

      What are the herbal preparations of Lysimachia congestiflora?

      Lysimachia congestiflora is prepared primarily as a liquid decoction or an infusion made from the dried aerial parts of the plant. These aqueous extracts are the most common methods for isolating the active constituents for medicinal or ritualistic use. Some practitioners may also utilize a tincture by soaking the plant material in high-proof alcohol to ensure long-term stability.       What side-effects this plant can have?

      Lysimachia congestiflora can cause skin irritation or allergic contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals who come into direct contact with the plant. While there is limited clinical research on systemic toxicity, any ingestion of the plant material should be avoided to prevent potential gastrointestinal distress. Some people may experience redness, itching, or a mild rash if the sap or foliage touches the skin. It is best to wear gloves when handling the plant to mitigate these dermatological risks. Monitoring for any unusual physical reactions is recommended if exposure occurs.
