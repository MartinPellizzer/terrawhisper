Chenopodium gracilispicum

What are the morphological characteristics of this plant?

Chenopodium gracilispicum has slender, erect stems characterized by a fine, mealy pubescence and small, lanceolate leaves that are often sparsely arranged along the branches. The leaf margins are typically entire or slightly wavy, with a texture that feels somewhat granular due to the presence of bladder-like hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous green flowers arranged in compact clusters along the upper portions of the stems. The reproductive structures are characterized by minute, single-seeded utricles that are often enclosed by a persistent perianth. These morphological traits collectively contribute to the plant's delicate and gracile appearance in its natural habitat.
